\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is great - I started playing when Shadowkeep launched and cross progression became a reality - I am now 1500 hours into the game and I have loved (almost) every bit of it.

It's got something for everyone; campaign stories, strikes (3 player missions with varying levels of difficulties called Nightfalls), PVP, PvEvP, Raids (6 player puzzles, encounters and bosses), open world activities, dungeons and more.

I've made a lot of friends by playing Destiny 2 and taking part in raids and dungeons as that is what I play the most, the game however has something for everyone.

It is important to note that when the new expansion Beyond Light is released, some of the older content will be removed in favour of the newer content. Bungie say they have their reasons, which I can empathise with - and I look forward to seeing how Bungie pulls it off - more information can be found on Bungie.net so you can make your own informed decision.

My main criticisms of the game are the lack of endgame loot incentive. The game is great in terms of goals to chase for through levelling, in game triumphs (challenges) and just wanting to play the game. In my last 500 or so hours however, I've not really had any loot to chase as I got it all fairly early on (this could be through RNG luck, but even the quest rewards were short to get) I fully acknowledge this is because I consider myself to be good at the game, however it would be nice for some more incentive at the top end in raids and dungeons.

My other criticism is the bugs that appear in the game. Whereas I don't experience them much myself, my friends have had their fair share of bugs. These will vary from player to player and every game has them, but it wouldn't be fair for me to not include them in this review.

Ultimately, D2 has been my favourite game for the last year and very few games have come close to toppling it - with the delay of the new expansion there is likely to be a content drought until November, but that makes it all the better for newer players to jump into the game and enjoy it without the worry of missing the new stuff. I recommend this game to casual and hardcore players alike, however my one message to the hardcore players is: you might run out of grinding at some point if currency grinding and armour stats aren't your cup of tea.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played this game since launch on Xbox and switched to steam when cross save was released. There is a lot to do in this game, and I totally recommend it for new players, especially since a lot is accessible for free. The story is good, there is a lot of lore, and there are tons of different activities to do.

There is a cycle that Destiny goes through every season, which is especially apparent to veteran or returning players. As veterans have done most things already, there is no incentive to return to the older content and keep playing it. Especially now, with the announcement of gear and weapon retirement, the older parts of the game have become obsolete.

For new players, I recommend checking it out. For returning or veteran players, it depends. Checking out the new stuff is always possible, or you could wait until the new big expansion drops for more new stuff. Personally, I am a bit burned out with the game after I’ve done everything. I am waiting for what the fall expansion brings, even though a lot of the game is going away, resulting in mixed feelings.\t\t\t\t\t
