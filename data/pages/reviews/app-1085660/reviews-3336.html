\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Just incase you dont have the time to read my very thoughtful review that I worked really hard at for the whole of 15 minutes, here's the over all score I give the game: 8.0/10. There, now git!

But to those of you who will be staying (and care enough to read my opinions on the internet), here's my slightly-more-indepth-but-also-not-really-indepth-review-of-the-game, or SMIBANRIROTG for short.

PREFACE: After the big split, Bungie has done a great job at addressing many of the issues that were plaguing the game in the past and have taken steps to fixing many of those as well. I will be honest and say there is still a LOT left to be fixed, but seeing what they are doing to at least ensure the game is fun and enjoyable. Take note that I am a person who has played a fair share amount of Warframe, and this is the first Destiny I have ever played, so take my review with a grain of salt, as I was not around when the game was in it's lowest. But anyways, on to the actual review:

Graphics: The game looks great, highly detailed and very very pleasing to look at visuals and biomes. I don't think I have had any issues with too many effects on the screen. (though the usual thing applies: if you get some dizzyness, make sure to tune those fx settings like motion blur, blah, blah, blah you know what to do if this applies to you.) easily 9.0/10

Gameplay: I love the gun play, the responsiveness, as well as the movement that you get unique to the class of character you play. One issue I do have however is that the physics engine (not fully the fault of Bungie, but mostly the engine they created the game with) can be wonky at times, making some things like parkour a pain in some very specific areas of the game (I'm looking at you Heroic Zero Hour) as well as hitting a wall with too much velocity is an instant OHKO. But other than that, I have no other things to complain about the mechanics themselves. Screw you physics, 7.5/10

PVE: fun. no really it's fun. Decent story, decently difficult content, a lotta grind. You know the jazz. Just make sure to look up some guides, cause with the new light update, your thown right into the whole thing no safety wheels at all. Just go to Amanda Holiday in the Tower to pick up any of the old story quests if you care for it. Just know that the rewards from them are no longer obtainable through the story missions, and they are now obtainable through other means. Good luck! Goodness, how do I get the Whisper of the Worm??? 7.5/10

PVP: Yes, this is also fun, gets you some decent loot and also is something you should try your hardest to actually play, cause it's simple, it's easy to get into, don't play gambit unless you hate yourself, you get me. Higher level pvp as of the writing of this review (10/28/2019) is fairly unbalanced as titans rule all and warlocks are only as relevant as the number of times they cast Nova Warp. But don't worry, they always will have things get nerfed eventually if things are too stong for too long... even if it means getting Breakneck nerfed in the process ;-;. But just know that Shaxx id that supportive dad who will always be happy that you at least tried, even if you lose 10 Control games in a row :v Gimme back me ol' Breakneck Bungo!!! 8.5/10

Bugs: Look, they just changed up how a lot of the game works, there's gonna be bugs. give it time and things will be fixed. They still got 2-3 years left with this baby and if they want this ship to sail, they will plug all the holes eventually. Erm... that sounded lewd sorry. MOVING ON. FFS BUNGIE GIVE ME THE IZANAGI REEEEEE 6.0/10

Should I play this game??? If you like Borderlands, Warframe, or literally any looter shooter ever, then yes, you should definitely pick the game up and give it try. Over all, I know they are gonna be fixing things as we go along, so be patient and just enjoy the game is whatever way you want. 8.0/10

p.s. Just letting you know, esp. for you vets out there. Remember, Emperor Calus is your sugar daddy, and that's the law. Goodbye.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played this game for hours when it was on battle.net, and it's still great. For a PC port, it's near flawless as ports go, with little stuttering or frame drops. It has a ton of content, great weapons and visual design, the pvp/pve activities are loads of fun (especially in groups), and the expansions are absolutely worth it if you enjoy the base game.

My biggest gripe is that it has a big learning curve for new players. Not because the moment-to-moment gameplay is complex, but everything around it is. Back then, players were sent to the tutorial no matter what, and it was very effective in teaching the basics. With Shadowkeep, it now throws you in after a short gameplay tutorial that's just the same from D1, which means you have to trigger the main campaign (The Red War) of D2 manually by talking to a specific npc in a hub. There's all the other things that need explaining too: Raids, nightfalls, heroic events, exotics and exotic quests, Lost sectors, class trees, pinnacle weapons, crucible playlists, iron banner, escalation protocol, etc. And that's just focusing on the base game experience, not including Forsaken or Shadowkeep

All in all, a great FPS, but I would recommend new players to ask a clan/patient friends to show them all the content when they jump in.\t\t\t\t\t
