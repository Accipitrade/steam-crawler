It just feels like the ups are *really* up and the downs are *really* down. Depending on the patch you may run into unplayable crashes and codes. If you want to play pvp either get really good or use meta weapons/cheese strats or some combination of both. Bringing back old raids has felt refreshing in the nostalgia department but doesnt bring any actual content to the game. You learn 1 or 2 mechanics per encounter and then its back to just being old content that feels re released. The grind each season for the *same weapon slightly different stats but also different/new perks* gets fairly old in my opinion and it can feel really dry because of it.
I dont recommend this game because if you arent comfortable having a grindfest of the same thing over and over then its not for you. The graphics are astounding, the music and backgrounds are absolutely gorgeous which you cant find in many modern titles. But the repetitive gameplay aspect gets old.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately, Destiny 2 is a game that I can't recommend after all the hours I've put into it. Look back at when you were younger, you got home from school and were excited to hop straight onto Destiny and grind/play with your friends. Those are memories I will forever treasure in my heart and wouldn't give up for anything. Until the announcement of Destiny 2, picture this, for the last x amount of years you played Destiny, you grew an emotional attachment to a lot of exotics you had earned and used throughout your play time, the raids and activities always there for you to play and enjoy with friends. Then they decided to release Destiny 2 with the promise that the game was going to be better and innovative, which was far from the case on launch. Destiny 2 has been a roller coaster since launch and while it had many good Seasons and Expansions, the game isn't doing enough with innovation and just replacing that with boring reskin 3-6 man activities that feel like you're doing the same thing every 3 months. My honest to god recommendation to Bungie would be to give players a 3 month break in between each season to give their players a break, instead they are worried about reaching a certain player count goal. Forcing your players to play seasons back to back is actually doing the opposite, currently as of posting this Destiny 2 has the lowest player count it has ever had. Now, you may be saying "oh but thats normal because the dlc releases soon", no thats not normal at all because usually the player count spikes once again when the new season drops. In this case, it has not raised as much as it has for a new season, players are tired, quite frankly even exhausted of playing the same reskin thing every single season. Bungie used to say that Activision was causing all of the problems but I would completely disagree, Destiny 2 had way better seasons and we got arguably one of the best DLCs, Forsaken, when they were under Activision. Now I will say that Activision wasn't all good, but, saying that Activision was the root of the problem is sorely ignoring the fact that Bungie has not shown any major improvement after leaving Activision. It's really a shame because I was looking forward to Lightfall but I don't think I will be buying it at all, I was holding out on saying that until I saw this season, but after seeing this season and viewing absolutely no improvement from last season, im very comfortable saying that I wont be buying Lightfall. At this stage of the game, I really don't recommend playing the game, especially if you are a new player.\t\t\t\t\t
