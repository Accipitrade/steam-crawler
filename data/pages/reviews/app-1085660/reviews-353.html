\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend destiny 2 wholeheartedly, provided you are a very specific person.

The gun-play and movement mechanics are second to none in my opinion, and it doesn't take long to master the basic mechanics. The campaigns are engaging, rewarding and challenging, ideal both for groups of friends, and solo players. In addition, there is an absolute cornucopia of content to explore, making it hard to see how anyone could ever run out of things to do.

And therein lies the problem for me. As something of a completionist, it drives me absolutely insane that I'd have to play for several hours every day to keep on top of all of the content, from raids to dungeons to season passes. This game is a grinder's paradise, and an absolute hell for anyone looking for a nice, casual experience to run through at your own pace. If you don't care about being "competitive", if you aren't going to feel compelled to try and obtain the best gear every season, you may have a relaxing experience. However, if you are someone that enjoys min-maxing, riding the meta wave and trying to stay at the top of the game, know that it will require dedicated time each day to keep up with the constant influx of new content, and the removal of the old.

So that's it. If you're a grinder, if you are looking for the "one game to rule them all", a place to hang your hat every day for a long time, Destiny has what you are looking for - perhaps better than anything else. However, If you are just a casual gamer just looking for a nice, relaxing experience, you need to be okay with the fact that you will rarely (if ever) be competitive in PvP, or PvE pinnacle content. If that isn't you, save yourself the pain and move on to something less voracious for your time.\t\t\t\t\t
