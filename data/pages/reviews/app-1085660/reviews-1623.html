\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played quite a bit before it was released on steam: played a bit at launch, then came back after Forsaken. I took a break right after Shadowkeep and came back right as the Season of Arrivals was starting. It is absolutely one of my favorite games of all time. I play both PvE and PvP, but the game is definitely centered around PvE more; however, there are plenty of PvP activities if that's what you're looking for. The gameplay is fantastic, each class feels unique and offers new ways to play, the armor system has just the right amount of complexity, the FPS mechanics are well polished and fun to use, and almost all the gameplay mechanics are accessible.

The thing about Destiny that's so great (to me), is you get out of it what you're willing to put in. If you want to grind into oblivion, this game has plenty of that,. If you're only here for the stories and lore (the lore in this game is top notch), or just grabbing some cool weapons and making a cool build, this game delivers.

Does it have it's flaws? Absolutely (I'm looking at you, Sunsetting). The base game is hard to understand, especially if you're a New Light (Free to Play) owner. I would recommend grabbing each expansion at a discount if you can, especially if you're only in it for the campaigns. This game shines when you dig deeper into each expansion, but that's not for everyone. The chronological order is: The Red War, Curse of Osiris, Warmind, Forsaken, and then Shadowkeep- Beyond Light after that. In my eyes, the positives far outweigh any negatives.

The endgame content is great as well, with dungeons, raids, and high level strikes, as well as missions. This will net you the best gear.

I would recommend this game in a heartbeat, especially since it's free. Try it out, and figure out what you like. 9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
