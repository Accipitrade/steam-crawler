\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSadly, nowadays empty shadow of former itself and mostly disappointment.

About game: Shared world looter-shooter which has identity crisis and tries to act too often as (bad) MMO or (badly) live service.
World is effectively grimdarkness lite. Ruins, Last City being repaired/rebuilt and hostile aliens everywhere.

In-game Activities:
Patrol zones: main maps of game. Just have fun, optional public events (can be done solo).
Strikes - Matchmake cooperative missions
Raids - 6-player large mission, no matchmaking
Dungeon - raid-like, can be soloed
Crucible - PvP Some modes are permanent and some are rotating.
Gambit - combination of PvE and PvP (often written as PvEvP or PvPvE) where objective is to kill enemies and collect motes to summon boss enemy. One can invade other team to slow them down.

Positives: +Good graphics
+Fun to run and shoot. Good for short sprints (about few hours at most)
+Great lore and usually pretty good stories in DLCs or in seasonal content
+Good weapons and visual of most armor with some appearance customization
+PvE is mostly enjoyable with pretty good variability (at least for a while), but beware one never feels really powerful.
+In PvP Mayhem is one of the most fun activity that is made available every three weeks. (All abilities recharge much faster)
+Microtransactions are only about cosmetics, but they are pretty expensive (most of older cosmetics are rewards now)

Negatives:
-Terminal amount of bugs, rate of fixes is very slow and there are frequent regressions and new bugs in patches.
-Half of game including base campaign was removed. Something like massive lobotomy by chainsaw. New content references a lot of removed content and there are remnants of old content all over the place referencing stuff new players can't experience. (one of alleged benefits was faster loading, on PC never happened)
-Often one gets connected to distant servers even for PvE and thus high latency resulting in teleporting enemies and other related fun.
-PvP is broken mess of bad hybrid P2P networking (one player hosts session like on consoles), bugs and badly balanced weapons and abilities and often terminal lag, because each match can have players from all over the world and thus unavoidable latencies. Also one can be forced to join match in progress and ten one which is almost finished (or finished).
-One can store only limited number of weapons, armor and items and there is no way to efficiently navigate vault without using apps or third-party websites.
-Rewards for activities are often a joke. (Not worth time invested)
-Expensive DLCs and seasonal content.
-Long load times for many activities be it PvE or PvP.
-Expensive upgrades for weapons and armor often using expensive materials (one has to invest a lot of time to gather them)

My recommendation, if you are really interested, check out demo (free version) and test drive it without buying anything for some time. Only after a while one gets to experience good and bad and ugly. Only then one is ready to make decision to buy. And if one is interested in DLCs and/or seasons, search on Internet for various deals, you can save quite a lot of money.
Also check out https://www.ishtar-collective.net/ and read lore from Destiny 1 and from removed content of D2, it will help somewhat to alleviate holes in story/lore of current content.

-buggy handling of mouse depending on its sensitivity, sensitivity setting ion Windows, in-game settings sand rounding. (Can be difference between getting fast headshot and missing by pixel)\t\t\t\t\t
