\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: It's getting better, slowly but surely.

---

Destiny has gone downhill since the transfer from battle.net to Steam. The practices that people were trying so hard to get rid of such as gun recycling is back and it makes playing the game unsatisfying. Before this game moved here, I used to play Destiny as it was filled with weeks worth of content. Time-gating activities does not make up for a lack of content. It seems that every hour I play, I feel driven away to go play another game. I only play this game to be with friends or breeze through new content.

On another topic, things that you put your hard earned work are being handed out like candy. With pinnacles such as Not Forgotten being available to any person, there is no reward to playing the Competitive PvP playlist unless you don't have Unbroken. It seems that all your hard earned work goes to waste after every patch.

Moving half of the content into the DCV killed the new player experience and starting off in the Cosmodrome makes no sense. That was a story to be told in Destiny 1 and half of the cinematics that play as soon as you launch the game will make no sense. With Beyond Light and Shadowkeep being the only campaigns left in the game, there is no free campaign available to the F2P audience. Destiny used to be an easy game to recommend as you would have access to three campaigns for free and can still explore the other sunset planets freely for more content. DCV optimizations should have been ready sooner if they were going to introduce it.

The transfer from Activision to Bungie has brought some unintended consequences. While Bungie has more direction in terms of decisions, the amount and quality of the content has reduced dramatically due to the lack of resources, We haven't seen a true expansion filled with as much content as the previous one did, Forsaken. That was beautiful to play and I had my money's run.\t\t\t\t\t
