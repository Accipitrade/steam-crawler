\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComing back to the game after a 6 months break I can't say I am overjoyed by the game's current state. After an extensive marketing campaign and hype we get a refurbished moon with a campaign that should put shame on the developers. I LOVED Destiny before and always in some sense understood the changes and addons they made. But this time I can't stand by it.

Believing to be able to pull back on the hardcore experience because of life was a serious wrongdoing. Pushing an Iron Banner (PvP with level advantages) tournament 2 weeks after launch only shows what kind of elitist game it has become. With a weekly rotation on how much Light i can go up each week, I have a hard time understanding how I am supposed to have a chance without no lifing the game to the extreme.

This is although no rage review coming from a bad experience in Iron Banner. Iron Banner is, in some way, supposed to show the Wolves among us Guardians and cull the weak. But the gameplay loop I have been presented with is not one that I can support.

From having been a mixed back of causal and hardcore with something to enjoy for everyone, this has gone far enough. Destiny is an immensive experience which most peeps sometime during their gaming experience should try out. But it is no longer a road for the faint hearted. If you want to at least try to ENJOY the game on a descent level, get ready to put your "normal" life on the line. At some point you will like me stand at the threshold and Destiny will ask for your soul to proceed.

If you want to just take a BITE out of the content this game has without paying a cent, you can do that. 20/10 for F2P.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's better than I would have imagined. It has the gameplay of Halo 3 with some token RPG elements thrown on top. It's pretty much perfect. It has tighter controls, better graphics, a more focussed endgame, and a more followable story than Warframe which I guess is the main competitor. It seems like almost an afterthought, but they hired the best voice actors for their dialogue.

The game isn't bogged down with minigames that distract from the core loop. And the core loop is great. You're always upgrading. You always feel powerful. The weapons and classes have been intentionally balanced. No build feels completely insane. Gear score matters but skill matters more, most of the time.

Also, the Bungie network engineers are wizards. I play lag free with my friends all over the world.\t\t\t\t\t
