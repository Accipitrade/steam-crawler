\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVeteran Destiny 1 and Destiny 2 Activ-Blizz days player here.

What is Destiny? Destiny borrows many elements of RPGs and shooters creating a shoot and loot experience integrating narrative and social elements in its multiplayer. You can consider it as a single player experience nested within a public online multiplayer experience. If you're potentially making a purchase or trying out the New Light product, you'll be greeted with content valued by many players as a 60+ USD experience before the additional expansions.

Gameplay for Destiny is hard to pin, but the flow of the game can be as follows: You open up to pre-rendered and beautifully voiced animated cinematics with a quick load into the game world and some introductory "missions" which plays in instances. (If you don't know what instances are, define it in google in the MMORPG context) Throughout gameplay you'll come across items dropped by enemies or gained as rewards. These "drops" increase your strength as you play, and eventually you'll be introduced to the greater world of Destiny where you'll cross paths with many other players who are at different points in other Campaigns and levels.

The action itself plays with the shooting gallery formula where critical points can be located elsewhere aside from the head, shields introduce a mechanic for energy and explosions, and different weapons will alter your approach to different situations. Do you like shotty snipers? Do you prefer autos? Are you a Battle Rifle guy? Do you just like to power trip? Once you get into the main game after a brief introduction, you'll be able to optimize for whatever your heart desires.

This is not a hardcore geared game for tank/dps/support roles until it gets to Raids. The roles will be clearly defined once you get into a no deaths playthrough. Due to the status bar defining shields+health and regeneration, healing becomes a non issue as survival will be a skill more than an ability as a player.

I don't regularly write reviews so forgive me for not being as all encompassing as I could be about this game, but it really does have a lot to offer. The new player experience is definitely a valid concern, but my opinion weighs in on the fact that the game integrates old and new players by placing everyone on the same light level (750) and opening all the activities to everyone. This way old players can guide new players while the dev team catches up.

The thing with this is that even though other games share the same shoot and loot core gameplay, Destiny bit off a lot in the beginning and have been incrementally realizing their vision one expansion cycle at a time. Right now, you'll get so many activities that you may even feel overwhelmed at times. But when you realize you have options to focus on what you want versus what you find, it becomes much more manageable.

Clans are a thing and there are regular raids and guided raid events where you can learn raids and gain assistance in Nightfall (significantly higher difficulty) strikes.

Can this be better? Yes. Will it be better? Not sure, but it has my support as it is this development team's first foray into independent publishing and development, so I want it to be better. Is it good? For a broad casual audience this is a lot to get just for free, and this gives you something to do all the time so yes. For hardcore players it should be taken in bites, and not consumed for days straight, otherwise you'll feel some repetition.

Now if they put out a low key easter egg in support of Hong Kong and help lead the way towards an industry that steers away from Chinese finances, I will gift any purchasable expansions to my friends. It's a solid game and, for now, deserves a gamble. At least in my opinion. Politics aside, give this a try, enjoy the game for what it is, and feel like a rock star.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been playing Destiny since 2014 and have seen many versions of the game come and go. I can confidently say that the game is currently (in October '22) in an excellent state.

The (4) elemental subclasses for the (3) main classes (12 total) have all been revised, allowing players to more freely tailor their subclass to their style, while also providing a more distinct and clearly defined role. Combine this new subclass system with some fantastic new weapon perks like Volt Shot or Incandescence, along with some complimentary armor mods, and guardians are able to synergize their playstyle more than ever.

Speaking of style, the transmog system has made it so that you can easily tailor your guardian's look without making compromises on your gear. Returning players would be interested to know that there is now a menu specifically for working with ornaments/shaders, shaders are no longer an inventory item but are instead able to be applied if unlocked at no cost (the same can be said for all mods), and primary ammo is now infinite.

Content-wise, the game is in a good state. The seasons tend to nudge the narrative along a little bit at a time - often times laying the foundation for future stories, picking up old threads, or giving us a closer look at a character. The expansions have been increasingly excellent, with The Witch Queen being no exception having added a Legendary difficulty which will pose a fun additional challenge (with additional rewards) for many. There are plenty of Dungeons and Raids to choose from (top-tier PVE activities, 3 & 6 man respectively), and Bungie has been going through playlists and giving them a bit of a refresher, with the trials receiving a tune-up and Iron Banner receiving a new game mode entirely.

And when it comes to getting the loot you want. Destiny now allows you to target specific drops with umbral engrams, and it also allows you to craft and upgrade weapons. These two features have been expanded on and improved steadily over time and will likely only get better.

It's not all sunshine and daisies though. Bungie tends to be excellent at communicating with their community, but lately has been less communicative and has let a couple of major changes slip in unnanounced - something which is justifiable due to certain margins of the community behaving in a toxic way, but it still bears mentioning. There are some complaints about Champions in the game - enemies which are roughly mini-boss strength unless you are specifically equipped to deal with them - and how this takes some of the fun and creativity out of building guardians for higher-level activities. And while Bungie has promised to never do this again - many players are still begrudged a significant portion of content being vaulted and gear being sunset only to then be trickle-released back into the game slightly different (but in many cases, improved over previous versions). It is worth mentioning that I am not personally bothered by this - and feel they are working towards a rolling narrative and content model, kind of like we're playing through a TV show.

It's definitely worth giving a shot if you like shooters. But I advise people to be mindful of the intense FOMO associated with this game. It's best enjoyed if you ignore the urge to collect and complete every little thing IMO.\t\t\t\t\t
