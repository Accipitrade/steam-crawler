\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have owned Destiny 2 since the beginning. As a looter-shooter, it's second to none. Borderlands, The Division 1 and 2, doesn't matter, it takes the cake. That being said, that was the quality of it back when it released. Charging a full $60+ depending on the version you bought, Destiny 2 had a full campaign, and a bit of end game content to keep people around.

Current monetary investment: $60

When Curse of Osiris dropped, which wasn't a lot of content but it was only $20, that started a bit of questions about the quality of what was to come. However, there was still plenty of content to play through.

Current monetary investment: $80

Then Warmind dropped and there was tonnes of content along with it, especially for the $20 price tag, and all of this dropped in year 1. Borderlands 3 still wasn't out, The division 2 wasn't out. Destiny 2 was, without question, the best looter-shooter still and an excellent value for what you paid.

Current monetary investment: $100

Forsaken dropped including a whole new campaign and area that lasted longer than an afternoon to get through. Initially available for $40 it was a steep ask but not unreasonable. For new players getting into the game this was enough content to last for months. For players like me who'd been keeping up, there was a new subclass for everyone to work with and learn as well as new exotics to farm and a whole new raid.

Current monetary investment: $140

Then came Shadowkeep and it was around here that the fatigue began to set in, there was new stuff to do between Forsaken and now including new pvp, new strikes, and stuff with the warmind but my time spent with the game had been going on for a while, but hey I had all the content to play whenever totaling around 300 hours and can be played on each class (titan, hunter, warlock) with 3 classes for each of those (9 classes total) with 3 branches for each class (that's 27 different ways to play through the 300+ hours of content!). But there's only so many times you can do a public event, raid, strike, or even pvp before you just get tired and nothing Shadowkeep brought to the table was fundamentally changing the formula enough for me. Shadowkeep was bought for $35.

Current Monetary Investment: $175

So I'm $25 short of having spent $200 on this game, if I'd bought warmind and black armory (I didn't my friend did for me), I'd be at $210. Keep this number in mind because it's vital for why I don't recommend this game.

Welcome to the current state of Destiny 2. Beyond Light is out, Destiny 2 went free to play, and most importantly, all the folks like me who paid close to or over $200 for the game's various content drops has lost access to most of it. The base game can no longer be played (-$60). Curse of Osiris can no longer be played (-$20). Warmind can no longer be played (-$20). The Forsaken campaign is being retired in February 2022 (-$40). The Shadowkeep campaign is being retired in February 2022 (-$35). So you're left with strikes at random from any of the game's content and a handful of the raids. Most of the exotic quests have been removed in exchange for just buying them to be farmed. And tonnes of Year 1 weapons and armor have been retired, meaning that you cannot level it up to keep it competitive. So loadouts that you got familiar with are now no longer useful, forcing you to go through the entry level experience of grinding for garbage for a while all over again (I know, I know, GaaS looter shooter shocked pikachu face and all that). Now Bungie has tonnes of content locked out unless you spend another $30 for the 30th anniversary pass, and other content locked out unless you spend $25 on the Beyond Light expansion and campaign with absolutely no promise that you'll get to keep what you get out of that expansion, all while trying to push you towards getting hyped for their upcoming expansion The Witch Queen. It reeks of f%@# you pay us style monetization that doesn't even seem to reward those of us who spend money on stuff. Just a constant drip of FOMO, cycling stuff in and out, and making things timed exclusive for the hell of it. If I wanted an MMO monetization platform I'd go play ESO\t\t\t\t\t
