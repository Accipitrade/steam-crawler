\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf ypu go to any game forum you'll quickly learn two things: (1) the primary occupation of most players is "grinding". That's all they talk about, and bungie responds by giving them new grinding challenges every week. They whine about the "grind" plenty, but that's what the play the game _for_ since it is most of their play-time. And any time some new trivial thing shows up -- a new shader, a weapon ornament -- they immediately share how best to grind it and get to work. They never ask _why_ this new title or color is worth the grind, because of course it isn't: these are ephemeral accomplishments. (2) The game is unplayable out of the box without dozens of third party applications which bungie is essentially collecting revenue from even tho their developers don't get paid. Even legit bungie devs don't play the game without them, so they know their game is unusable. (3) The third thing wrong with the game is not that uncommon: in opposition to spectator sports like NASCAR etc where the best players play with some sort of handicap that makes the competition more interesting and prizes are things like hockey's Stanley cup which you cannot play hockey with, this game caters to RAID/PvP crowd. Dedicated PvPers, as we all know, hate competition and only want to gank people (in general) and that probably explains the reward scheme where the best players are awarded coveted weapons which only make the best players even better. Like the RAID players (who also get great weapons) bungie believes that they need to be bribed to actually play well. Of course within the grind schema it might all make sense, but you cannot get weapons like tarrabah merely by grinding: you need to get six people together and grind together -- which places all kinds of limits on who can actually even try. They've successfully built a community around the concept of "sunk cost"; once you've spent a few hundred hours grinding out the basics its hard to admit the whole thing is stupid. I've played quite a bit because my spouse is really into it. But now I'm stuck holding her back when we're together because my job won't let me grind three or four hours a day which is kind of the minimum.\t\t\t\t\t
