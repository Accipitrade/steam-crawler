I started playing Destiny 2 back toward the end of October of last year (2019). I was luckily able to get Breytech Werewolf right before the event ended. There are some negative reviews out there but I just want everyone to keep in mind that they're giving you about 2 years worth of gameplay. It's true you won't be able to grab a few exotics items without the DLC but you can very much enjoy the campaign and most of the everything Destiny 2 has to offer other than the newest events. I personally have a bias against fps games but once you get past that initial phase of "not knowing what to do" this game starts to get really addicting. I'm a sucker for events that reward you with new materials/gear/weapons, and from time to time (usually around holidays) you'll get one. There is definitely some grinding you have to get by and if you spend at least a couple hours a week, you'll be able to obtain good stuff. I recommend watching some youtube videos for help with quite literally anything. You can by pass a lot of stuff and get straight into parts of the game you want to play after an initial introduction to specific gamemodes. I personally felt that the Forsaken and Shadowkeep DLC was worth it (definitely try to buy it during a sale if possible) and with the hours I racked up in this game, I can tell you there's plenty of stuff to explore. This really is the one game on steam that states that it's "free to play" and has quality content and gameplay that lasts for hours.\t\t\t\t\t
