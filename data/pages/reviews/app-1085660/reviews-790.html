\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played Destiny 2 since year one, never played Destiny 1. I'd like to say this game has changed a lot since then, its become a lot more like an MMO with gear and builds and mods and weapons. However, Bungie has also taken a lot of steps back that hold the game at weird impasses from being perfect.

1. New Player Experience - It's actually unbearable, you have no mods, no gear, no exotics, no way to get the new exotics, and if you're alone good ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ luck. It's basically a chaotic mess. The combat is solid enough to draw people in but I imagine a lot of people get turned off once they realize where everyone else is at. This also goes for stories, as stuff that has been setup or passed, important characters and plots have absolutely no context to people new to the franchise.

2. Weapon Archetype Balance - Destiny 2 has a lot of weapon archetypes, however, in the current meta with Ammo scavenging mods there really is absolutely no reason to use anything but double Special weapons in the kinetic and energy slot. The seasonal anti-champion mods are the only reason but there are exotics that deal damage and also deal with champions. This really just means that people who love Assault Rifles and Revolvers like myself are told to take a hike for End-game content. But even for standard content it's just far more efficient to run double slugs or double grenade launcher, since chaff is absolutely braindead and can be swept up with a single grenade often times. In higher end content, good luck using primaries, they may have plenty of ammo but they make up for it by doing ♥♥♥♥ all. The only places they're useful is PvP and... PvP is bad.

3. PvP is Awful - It's hard to go into depth about why PvP is awful, but the best way to explain it is by simply presenting you with the game. Destiny 2 is a PvE MMO with a Halo style PvP matchmaking. It's full of sweaty children, and boring metas. Sometimes they bring around a fun mode, but those rotate out so you can't always just choose to have a good time jumping around with a scorch cannon.

4. Exotic Balance is Silly - There are just some exotics, that are absolutely not worth the time equipping. Primaries already struggle and using up your single exotic weapon option for an auto-rifle that barely hits harder than other auto-rifles is like purposefully crippling yourself. And there are exotic armor options that range from MANDATORY to who cares? An exotic titan chest piece that can either, give you a second grenade, or make your super ABSOLUTELY DEMOLISH BOSS HEALTH BARS! An exotic that gives a second melee, vs one that makes your melee poison people, the poison spreading when the enemy dies, and it also synchronizing with a hand-cannon's poison effect. It's just silly how pointless some exotics are, not every bad exotic is bad because it doesn't do mad deeps, but often creative exotics that do unique things get sidelined because Destiny 2 isn't the Prey Remake, you don't beat Atheon by being creative. You beat him by doing the straightforward mechanic and damage.

I think the game has seriously missed out on creativity in the last few years. When year one as around and the Leviathan had non-boss encounters, it might have been good to have such diverse creative exotics to traverse problems in a unique way. But as it stands right now the primary end-game content is more about DPS and Survivability than any real problem solving mechanics. And I think Destiny could use a little more Doctor Who, less gun shooty and more creativity. The most recent 6 player public content, was literally entering a digital universe to un-hack the sun. And yet it was just shoot aliebs and then blow up boss!

I think also enemy diversity could really use an update. As it stands the last like, new enemy we got I think were Wyverns for the Vex, who are awful. There's an exotic mission where you go on a ship infested by weird mushroom stuff but instead of facing some zombified monsters or anything interesting its just Scorn, the Forsaken enemy which themselves are Fallen reskins.

Story-wise, Destiny needs to get away from the Darkness vs Light plot. It's an awful story that is dragging down the diversity and adventure aspects of the universe. It's also incredibly formulaic. Destiny 2 is at its best when you're just a guardian doing weird exploration in strange places because the universe is an odd mashup of interdimensional parasites and time travel alien magic. It's why I say it should become more Doctor Who-esque. I'm not saying guns and violence have no place in Destiny 2, some of my favorite things is mowing down hordes of red bars with an assault rifle. I just think that there should be more expansion on approaches of content. Not so much puzzles as just being able to use the player's own tools and creativity to solve the issue. Like maybe creating a stasis wall to block a wipe explosion. Sometimes those moments can happen but they rarely feel intended, and I think if the kind of experience can be tailored it would be amazing.

Besides that I just think Bungie needs to let go of some Bungisms, like focusing less on making good weapons bad and more on making bad weapons good. They seem constantly more interested in why weapons are being used more than why weapons are being used less, and don't seem to think very critically on why it is the way it is.

Some closing thoughts would be: Un-sunset Breakneck!\t\t\t\t\t
