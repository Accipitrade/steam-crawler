\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a long time Derpstiny player and even longer time Bungo fan, I can say without a doubt this game isn't worth playing. It's not that its not fun, but its stale. Generally you'd think the developers would release new content to counter act this, as most games do. However Destiny doesn't, instead they proactively release even less new content with every update, and now are going to begin cutting content in a game with basically none to speak of currently. This game has a great story, but unfortunately for most of it's life span you had to go to a website to read about it... Now its in a codex in the menu, which is an improvement, but it still isn't a playable story... It's just a book with extra steps. If that wasn't enough you'll probably have to go watch a Youtube video to explain what you just read, because its fairly vague and poorly written. Every enemy in the game is a slight variation of one of probably 9 base enemies. Same with guns they're all based on like 3 of each different type. Just basically every model in the was made by Sean Murray (No Man's Buy guy not the actor). While it is quite easy to ♥♥♥♥ on the game, it can be fun too. The Crucible (PVP) can be entertaining, but without prior knowledge of the current meta and decent guns you're likely going to be destroyed. Gambit (PVPVE) is a cool game mode (announcer voiced by Matthew McConaughey) that pits you versus another team attempting to collect 'motes' and then kill a boss who spawns after collecting enough. Which ever team kills their boss first wins. While collecting your 'motes' you can send enemies over to distract the other team or even go to their side yourself to kill them and delay them from spawning their boss fight. As cool as it all sounds it honestly gets tiring very quickly (not unlike the rest of the game). Doing a Raid (end game PVE) the first couple times can be fun. There's parkour and jumping puzzles, secrets, and cool mechanics. Unfortunately there's not as many Raids as I or basically anyone else would wish and like most the 'content' (if you could call this game's poor excuse content) some are going away soon, hopefully to add more (but I'm sure they'll take their sweet time and it'll be spread across as many $40+ DLCs as physically possible). The worst part of it all is, if you'd like to do those Raids you have to be a certain 'Light Level' (its just the games leveling system name), if you're not you'll do reduced damage and take more damage yourself (if you're too low you'll get one shot and the enemies are immune to you), so to do that you'll have to grind for hundreds of hours doing some of the most boring crap imaginable, then hit your weekly limit and be forced to wait till next Tuesday to do it all over again (that's real time, you actually have to wait a week to play again). You'll play all the stuff I mentioned above and a couple more, including Strikes (PVE) its basically a Raid with less people and way less effort. Or Nightfalls (PVE) its just another Strike, but they made it harder so you're stuck playing the game longer. Which is honestly what it comes down to, numbers. The whole game is just basically so on paper Bungo can say it did a good job. Stuff like the fact that after you spend hundreds of hundreds of hours to get a gun with the god roll or the perks you just want, just to find out its no longer usable (at least on current DLCs) in a few months. If you play the game enough you'll eventually come to realize it. I have probably 1500ish hours between this on Steam, Battle.net, and Xbox and then the original. If you have a console, I suggest buying the prequel and trying 'The Vault of Glass' and 'Wrath of the Machine' Raids (but only if you can find it for like basically nothing, sub $25 including DLC and that may be more than its really worth).

TLDR
For a F2P its pretty expensive
The game is ♥♥♥♥ all boring
Its not easy to pick up\t\t\t\t\t
