\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing this game since launch, from battle.net to steam now. Through its ups and downs and constant reworkings, and the game grew through the years, the developers listened to the community more and more. Guns got rebalanced, reworked, and so did classes. As a hunter main myself I feel that, the other classes are broken (looking at you titans), but from an outside view they all are pretty balanced for the most part. I miss the Red War campaign and the Menagerie, but new content has been added to fill the place of those. It just to bad that new players never get to experience the Red War. Lore wise and timeline wise, it makes sense to remove earlier expansions, but for new players not knowing the story or old players from D1 moving on. I wish it was there to add to the story building and lore. Destiny 2 is also seen as a predominantly pve focused game, in which at first glance it does seem to. But once you see more of the game, the pvp aspect comes into play. The reason I tend to play this game now, is for the pvp and sometimes its a grand experience, and sometimes its frustrating, but its a really enjoyable activity that I come back to. This game though wouldn't be what it was, if it wasn't for it's community. As said before, I've been playing this game since launch, and most activities require multiple people, thus where lfg comes into play. I've done raids, pve, and pvp content with random people through Bungie's system for lfg and outside sources as well. I've had a great experience meeting people and doing activities. I enjoy this game the most when I'm playing it with my friends and Destiny 2 has given me this enjoyment for hundreds of hours. Overall I'd give this game a 8/10 but, thank you Bungie and the people that are behind it, for making my time especially during COVID, a little less lonely.\t\t\t\t\t
