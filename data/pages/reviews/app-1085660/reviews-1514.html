\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA CATEGORY OF ITS OWN

**Review**
This is a game unlike any other. Many compare it to Warframe and Borderlands. But that doesn't really give an accurate descriptor other than to say that it is, in part, a looter shooter. Destiny is such a unique game and is a looter-shooter, but it's also very similar to an MMO. The raids are unlike anything in any shooter I've played. They're actually closer to WoW-styled raids with significant story relevance.

The state of the game has changed for the better since Bungie left Activision. Destiny 2's release was as bad as Destiny 1's initial release due to a contractual obligation for Bungie to produce a new game every other year, which is absurd. That kind of crunch was extremely hard on Bungie as a developer, so they've taken the game into their own hands.

There are still quite a few issues with this game that veterans of the community point out, and Bungie's been readily responsive with updates and "This Week at Bunige" posts (TWABs). They are constantly on top of community opinions and understand the necessity of comunication with their playerbase.

Destiny 2 has always been compared to it's predecessor. Since launch, Bungie had to reinvent the wheel from the first Destiny game, but are now beyond being able to compare the state of the game of D2 with D1. At this point Destiny 2 is one of, if not the greatest free-to-play MMO-Looter-Shooter that a person can engage with.

**For New Players**
Right now the new player experience is a bit overwhelming with so much to do. Bungie is aware of this and has spoken a few times to the community about their plan to fix this with the upcoming Fall DLC. If you do jump in, you'll be dropped off into the world of Destiny as it is now and have access to everything that the game has to offer except for DLCs beyond year 1 content and season pass content.

This game is more fun to play with other people, but also has a really interesting story that you can play by yourself. (However, this game is inherently online with a shared world instance.)

All that being said, there's still a significant amount of content available for free. If you wish to relive the main story events of Destiny 2's history, just visit Amanda Holiday in the Tower after the introductory story mission.

**What do you get in the game?**
All of year 1 content + raids.
- Red War (story)
- Warmind (story)
- Curse of Osiris (story)
- Leviathan (raid)
- Eater of Worlds (raid)
- Spire of Stars (raid)
- All planetary locations with activites
(including planets from the below DLCs)

**What you don't get (as of the time of writing).**
- Forsaken (story)
- Shadowkeep (story)
- Last Wish (raid)
- Scourge of the Past (raid)
- Crown of Sorrow (raid)
- Garden of Salvation (raid)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't let my "hours played" fool you; I have put thousands of hours into the Destiny universe across, well, every platform its ever been on. Destiny 2 Is the current product being presented. Make no mistake: Destiny 2 is only truly meant to be played with all of the expansion content. It is playable without, but it puts the player at a significant disadvantage.

Pros:
- Expansive Lore
- BEST GUN PLAY ON THE MARKET!
- Literal tons of loot to acquire
- A wide base of activities to cover almost any type of player (PVE, PVP, PVPVE, Casual, Endgame Grind, etc.)
- Visually captivating and gorgeous scenery
- Evolving world and economy

Cons:
- Not every activity has a matchmaking option
- Loot economy is way too saturated currently
- Many Quests can really only be completely with a team (Solo is an option but does not scale back difficulty)
- No in game LFG for non-matchmade games
- Current Season based model force players to either constantly be playing or miss out on content

Overall, Destiny 2 is a fantastic gaming experience. There is a lot of fun times to be had. However, it's also a dedication. You can pick it up, play for a bit, then forget but you would be doing yourself a disservice by not journeying further into the game. A player would have to sink hundreds of hours to experience the whole. I have, and I don't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played to max level when the game was on Battle.net, so my hours logged here are not accurate.

Pros:
* Massive universe with a ton of diverse worlds to visit
* Mostly satisfying combat, though after all these years Bungie really needs to introduce more enemy variety (new enemy types added to the roster for the existing enemy factions would be my preferred approach, but a new faction would be cool as well).
* Great guns, though not quite as good as some of the newer FPS games
* Seemingly endless stream of activities; exploring planets and doing things like patrols or adventures, multiple difficulties of Strikes (Destiny's dungeons), raids, quests, public events, seasonal events, and several multiplayer modes will all keep you busy.
* Lots of interesting loot and things to unlock
* Looks and sounds great, and generally runs pretty well
* Great visual art direction
* Co-op generally works well (ignoring that it's currently a bit buggier than most AAA co-op games I've played)
* What I've seen and heard of the worldbuilding is pretty cool and is the one part of the writing I actually enjoy.
* I really like the semi-persistent world - wandering around some of my favorite planets and just picking up random side activities which build my character is enjoyable.

Cons:
* Absolutely abysmal UX - definitely the worst I've ever personally seen (at least in a AAA title). * Completely forgettable writing which takes itself far too seriously

* Of course this is subjective, but all of the characters suck. They're so bland and completely uninteresting, often with tropey or so-typical-it's-annoying dialogue, and I'm shocked they managed to get people who have starred on shows as prestigious The Wire to voice act these things.
