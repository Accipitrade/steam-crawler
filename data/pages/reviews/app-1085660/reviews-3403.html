\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is the new looter-shooter/massively-multiplayer hybrid Steam release developed by Bungie, and previously published by Activision/Blizzard before the Bungie split.

I'd like to start by saying that as much as I enjoy this game, it has a plethora of problems I would suggest it fix before I'd say that it's wholeheartedly worth checking out for people who don't immediately take to the gameplay loop like a fly to ♥♥♥♥.

Firstly, the game's narrative is a disjointed mess that, while loosely follow-able, requires a lot of brainpower and time spent away from the game's main purpose to even discern what's happening with any level of precision beyond "I think xyz is happening". Secondly, there are a lot of things that happen out of order, with some events referencing things the player might not have experienced, or just the general fact that this game has a chronic fear of the word "linear experience". Third, the new update "New Light", while it did indeed make everything available from the get-go (no need to play the entirety of the Red War campaign in order to access the entirety of the game's content, which in my opinion, opening a player's options is always a good thing), new players immediately run into the issue that there's simply way too many options and not enough context to discern which option is most beneficial for what you want to accomplish, unless you're already familiar with the game.

There's quite a few game mechanics that aren't fully explained or even mentioned (such as the Triumphs system or the Collections menu, both of which I found very handy, though the game didn't mention them at all). Furthermore, the fact that the narrative doesn't help players understand things at all (or the fact that people even have trouble finding out what the main narrative even is) certainly makes D2 the equivalent of groping around a warehouse in the dark looking for a flashlight when all you can touch are things that aren't flashlights, unless you had already seen the warehouse with the lights on. Basically put, getting into it now for new players is borderline maddening.

I have a few recommendations for how this can be solved.

First, for new players, you need to limit their options and refine the process of integrating a new player into the game. Having a series of tutorial missions that both explain what the mechanics are, as well as what missions, adventures, triumphs, and collections are would be essential. What's present right now is wholly inadequate, and the tutorial needs a lifetimes' supply of steroids to ensure it's actually useful to new players and not just annoying.

Second, the main narrative of the game has to have obvious, explicit language about how it's the main quest beyond what's already there. Among the sea of bounties, secondary objectives, and account quests handed out, it's easy to lose it with everything else and forget. The UI for the quests menu needs to be further narrowed and specialized- Say, by the planet they take place on, or level of priority.

Third, and while this is a personal complaint, Forsaken should just be included in the base game without people having to pay additional bucks for it. Honestly, Warmind and Osiris are cool, but not worth writing home about in terms of dedicated time, while Shadowkeep and Forsaken are actually important. There needs to be much more effort in incorporating existing expansions with the base game and other expansions as well. And make it cheaper, too. Getting to the end of the base game and saying "pay $30 to keep playing with everyone else" is just a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ policy. Lower the max price to $20 or less after the base game. After all, that $100 million you got from Tencent should keep you floating for a long, long time, unless you go all Blizzard/Activision on us and turn into the very thing we applauded you for leaving.

Long story short, you should pick up this game if you understand what you're getting into and you enjoy the gameplay loop. Otherwise, it's not gonna be for you. I'm also suspicious as to how Bungie will monetize D2 in the future from now on, since I'll be routinely combing over the game with a microscope to check for Tencent/Chinese ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, as well as making sure Bungie's actually on the up-and-up and not just another one of those ♥♥♥♥♥♥ AAA teams who value money at the expense of the player base and basic human rights in general.

♥♥♥♥ Tencent, ♥♥♥♥ China, Hong Kong should remain free.

And if Bungie's wise, they'll share the same sentiment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is not for everyone, but it's worth at least trying out. You get a TON of content absolutely free. I mean like, $100+ initial retail price kind of free. No strings attached, only thing you're missing out on is a bit of the newer DLCs and some tiny season pass exclusive stuff. But seriously it's a fully complete game and then some, completely freaking free.

I've played a lot of Destiny over the years, and I won't lie, it's a tough game to get into because it's so overwhelming at first. I'd *highly* recommend having a player/friend help you if at all possible, or at least using a player-made guide online. It's a lot to take in, but once you get past the learning curve you might find that it's a pretty amazing piece of work.

It's very well optimized on PC, and in fact it runs better than any of my other games with similar graphics.Changing graphics settings also doesn't even require a restart, it's all real-time right in front of you. That's a huge plus for me.

Also I must note that Bungie is taking huge strides and making big changes since leaving the chains of Activision. Things are looking great, and it seems they're just going to keep getting better. Give it a go if you have the time, it just might suck you in.\t\t\t\t\t
