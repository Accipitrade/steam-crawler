\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA visual and conceptual feast for a sci-fi fan. The famous Arthur C. Clarke quote about technology and magic is very relevant. It's hard to know what order to do things in to experience the story chronologically, though, and with the original Destiny being a console exclusive, PC-only players can't go and experience that part for themselves. But, players have put a lot of cutscene compilations, timeline breakdowns, and lore overviews on YouTube. I caught myself up with Byf's four-hour "from origins to Shadowkeep" lore video and that helped a lot.

Having hundreds of hours in Warframe when I started playing Destiny 2, at first I missed Warframe's movement options, like bullet-jumping, aim-gliding, and wall-latching. I still miss wall-latching, and I sure wish jumping toward a ledge wouldn't (more often than not) bounce you backward and make you drift awkwardly down. However, with triple-jump selected in my subclasses and a few pieces of armor with high Mobility stats, I can actually jump longer distances in D2 than in Warframe, which has resulted in some embarrassing parkour fails in the latter when I hop between the two games.

Which game is better? Neither. They're both good. They're different. Play both.\t\t\t\t\t

I am going to finish this Season and we'll see if the next one will be worth it. If you are a new player to Destiny its a good option to try the game with New Light's release.\t\t\t\t\t
