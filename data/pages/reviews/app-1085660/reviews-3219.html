Putting them behind in game activities would incentivize participation in these activities. I feel that once you have the best items there's nothing to do and the endgame activities such as the raid I don't care about doing because I do not get any real reward from it, no item or cosmetic to chase. I would be much more interested in doing the raid and inviting other friends to the game to help me complete it if there was an actual ingame incentive to do so. As it stands, there is no real draw for me and my friends to even bother with the raids beyond perhaps doing them once to see it. I could just watch a youtube video and get about the same experience with a lot less hassle, it's not like I get anything worthwhile from doing it in person either way. Give me a reason to do endgame content and give me something I can use as incentive to draw friends into doing the raids with me.

I and two other friends literally quit the game at the release of black armory from being so burnt out being forced to do pvp so much to get strong pve weapons, as we wanted to have strong pve item options. They have not returned to the game since that experience and are not likely to return. Give the best pve items to pve players for doing long or difficult pve quests. Give the best pvp items to pvp players for doing pvp activities. Mixing the two and making players feel forced to do activities they dislike to feel like they can compete at the activities they actually like is not a fun gameplay experience. I am a pve player. Stop making me feel like I have to do things I hate to get more fun out of the things I enjoy. People stop doing things when they realize they hate doing them, and I play games to have fun.
