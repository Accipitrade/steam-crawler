\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I played the game on my Xbox originally when it first came out in '17, than played it on Battle.net in '19, than only recently started playing it on Steam early March looking for a new game to play.

I'll come out straight and say it's F2P, not P2W, and still has some of the magic the original D1 provided me back on le xbox. Of course, the power/light grind is still there, and there's a lot of content, but here's the main issue for me, and it's probably personal but I'll point it out.

I end up playing the game, pressing M to look at my map, seeing some blips on a map, and than exiting the game bored. Most games will put up hours of content just behind a blip on a map and expect you to chase after it. I miss the day content would chase after you, so you end up spending time bored, reluctant to playing the content that isn't layed out straight infront of you. When playing most games nowadays, I need to watch streams or youtube videos, see other people's progress in the game, and than take what I can from that to aspire to further enhance what I have to them.

I do think it's a better game than The Division, another looter-shooter im passionate about, and I say that because destiny has multiple classes, and you can't just transfer your progress between characters/classes. You have to make a new character if you want to experience the game as that class (titan/warlock/hunter). Whilst the content you do is visually identical amongst them, the way that you play the game can drastically change. Each class has their own apparel options, looking unidentical to eachother, and it adds triple the content of what you originally had. Each class has it's own type of super ability, grenades, class ability (dodge/wall/rifts), jump types, melee abilities, apparel like mentioned, and more. It's just kind of boring for me to play the some of the content and DLC over and over again, which I've done atleast 9-10 times now, 3 classes on xbox/original battlenet/and now steam which I'm working on.

Also, raids, super epic group activities that require you advanced coordination with your friends/clan-mates that can take hours to complete but reward exclusive and e p i c loot. :)

TLDR: Great game, even better with friends, better than The Division 1/2 in my opinion, but not as good as original Destiny, and that's just because what we come to expect in 2020 compared to 2014-17 when D1 was primarily played. It's free-to-play, and can be super addicting.\t\t\t\t\t
