\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATED REVIEW
Posted April, 15th 2022

Even with the release of Witch Queen and Season of The Risen, two releases I believe to be the single best additions to Destiny 2, I cannot recommend this game. The majority of the reasons are already listed below in my 2nd review update from December 5th 2021. The game has not given enough from what they have taken away, the world is stagnant as the story progresses and the endless grind just isnt rewarding because... nothing is. Destiny 2 holds an incredibly special place in my heart for many reasons, the Year 1 content and locations sucked me in and made me love the game and yet now its gone. It breaks my heart to see a game I have loved for years come to this... scraps of something that should be amazing.

There are... so many things that could be better, so many things that could be added, so many things that should be changed to reflect the content and story changes, and yet it has not been done. I am so sad to say this but even after everything the game is still a great example of lost and untapped potential.


UPDATED REVIEW
Posted December 5th, 2021

It is with a saddened heart that I can no longer recommend Destiny 2 in its current state. While I will continue to play D2 I no longer believe that it is truly worth playing, let alone paying for its various "expansions".

When I first got Destiny 2 I was enthralled by it; the gameplay, the movement, the story and the world. However since the vaulting of The Red War, Curse of Osiris, Warmind, various planets including Titan, Mercury, Mars, and Io it has drastically lacked anything new to compensate for the lost story and locations. With the vaulting of Forsaken and the Tangled Shore location in the near future things do not look good for the continued enjoyment of Destiny 2. I will update this review if The Witch Queen, which drops in February of 2022, does make up for the lost content. to circle back, when I first got D2 it offered F2P players enough story and gameplay to make players want to buy the rest of what was available and unfortunately that is no longer the case.

In addition to the lackluster F2P experience the world in and of itself has an abundance of untapped potential. From locations you can only access during strikes or missions you cannot repeat without making a new character. The EDZ alone has entire regions of the map that cannot be accessed and are leftover from story content that they removed and instead of putting these areas to use they block them off.

Make no mistake; there is a lot to love about this game like the combat and movement. However there is far more that makes me upset for this game. Even when buying the story stuff such as Forsaken, Shadowkeep and Beyond Light it still offers very little in the way of new gameplay; it is all just normal gameplay placed into the campaign as if it was something good. You do bounties, patrols and go to the same locations over and over doing tedious tasks for a cutscene and some information that pushes a story forward. But there is nothing NEW added truly; Beyond Light was a long grind to get a new subclass which ultimately was not rewarding.

I love Destiny's world and story, I love its gameplay and the things it has done for me. There is however a stark contrast between the story being told and the world and gameplay being presented, the world does not truly change to catch up with the story. Maybe a ship shows up in the air, maybe some Eliksni characters show up in the tower but there is no reflection in the gameplay. We are not a part of the world, the world is an afterthought. It feels as if Bungie has a story they want to tell and yet they will not listen to the people that are actually playing their game. I am not alone in a lot of what I am thinking, a large part of the community feels the same way and I used to disagree with them until I started looking below the surface level enjoyment of the game. When you do that the fascade of D2 fades away and you see a product of wasted potential and a complete divergence in the story's tone and the gameplay's.

I wont keep writing as I think I have made my view clear; D2 could be an amazing game but Bungie needs to start listening to the people who actually play their game.


ORIGINAL REVIEW
Posted July 10, 2020

Destiny 2 is an amazing game with an amazing world and evolving story, while the free version contains a well crafted story that will take hours to complete youll quickly find yourself wanting more as you explore the open world aspects as some things simply wont make sense without the rest of it.

I cant stress how fun this game is, while it can be grindy and annoying at first when finding gear you want to keep once you get a high power level youre able to keep and create a kit you enjoy and can master. In terms of the open world and things to do youll scarce find yourself bored with many missions, locations and events to participate in at any given time across many worlds.\t\t\t\t\t
