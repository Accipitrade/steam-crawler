\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to recommend this game so much, but I simply can't! This game has a lot going for it; beautiful visuals, great gunplay, and a good story. But what good is all of that if you don't want to stick around long after you've experienced it all? That was my experience, along with a large number of people I inquired regarding this game. After you've completed the majority of the story, as well a large number of high-level nightfalls, you look up to conquering the next big thing, raids. However, there's one big obstacle, the atrocious grind to get there! Allow me to explain my understanding of how the leveling system of Destiny 2 functions. Your Light Level is based on the average of the gear your character is wearing. To increase that, you need to get higher level gear. As of 10/27/2020; due to the introduction of new content, when you create a brand new character, you start at level 750, quickly working your way up to 950, as most of the drops you get from enemies are powerful drops, which are usually 2-3 levels higher than your current gear. This will continue until you reach the seasons "soft cap.", the point in which you can no longer level up with powerful gear drops, despite their name. Instead, you will need to participate in activities that will grant you pinnacle drops, allowing you to level up further until you reach the "hard cap," the point in which you can no longer level up your Light Level. The issue with this system is that to attain more power; you need to check in every week to complete mundane bounties over and over again every week. This can cause you to burnout out this game FAST, and I mean FAST. This is unfortunate as this game's universe is so fascinating, with meticulously crafted lore behind almost everything. This leads me to another flaw of this game, the amount of underwhelming boss fights in the game. This game is a looter shooter, so there are bound to be spongy bullet enemies, which I do not mind. However, what is disappointing is how the majority of bosses in this game feel like boring reskins of the primary fodder enemies in the game, sprinkled with gimmicky mechanics that sometimes hurt your experience. For example, the main story boss, Dominus Gaul, feels like a reskinned version of the basic Cabal centurion. Another is the final boss for the Garden of Salvation raid, The Sanctified Mind, looking like a massive gunked version of a vex minotaur, which was very underwhelming to face. Finally, I want to address the game's seasonal content. I applaud Bungie adding new content after splitting with Activision, but what is given very, very cookie-cutter every time. It consists of a new public activity, new gear, and a season pass, containing the new armor and two new exotic weapons. Unfortunately, the new exotics and seasonal activity are locked behind the passes' paywall. I understand that Bungie needs to make money on this game, but locking this boorish content and the exotics behind the paywall is quite laughable. Not far from Activision's practices for Call of Duty's season pass, this is an exploitation of FOMO (fear of missing out), locking gameplay changing items unless the player shells out some cash on silver, the in-game currency. The silver shop wouldn't be too bad if it weren't as grindy to obtain cosmetics for free. On top of that, most of the cosmetics aren't even worth spending your money. Overall, it seems that the scars that Activision left on this game still show today, as it still feels the same as when the game first came out, like it was meant to last a year until the next sequel. A damn shame.\t\t\t\t\t
