\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHIS REVIEW HAS BEEN EDITED TO REFLECT MY MOST APPLICABLE OPINION!

I don't usually recommend games or write these because I'm lazy but steam asked and so it shall receive. Destiny 2: Electric boogaloo (finally not on B.Net anymore so ♥♥♥♥ off Blizzard ya commies). You can fall in love with it even if you hate it originally, but make sure you experiences the game in both solo play and with friends before deciding. The graphics are good and game design is fun, and so is the climb to reach higher power rank. Did I mention it is free? The only thing being wasted is your time and you are spending that reading a review written by an idiot(me).

Destiny 2 does a good job of allowing the player to continue using whatever equipment they prefer rather than forcing them to use whatever is strongest like in most Looter Shooters(cough Borderlands 3)(still love BL3 tho). It may not always be as efficient, but the option is there and you can upgrade things to keep them with you throughout the journey. The struggle of completing raids with people is rarely found nowadays and it gives the entire group memories to share, and depending on everyone's mindset, some failures to laugh at. As a nut for old games and new ones that have the concept of teamwork and cooperation at the forefront, I cannot say how happy I am with Destiny 2 for giving me the satisfaction I haven't felt in awhile from any new games.

If you are really into these kinds of games, and are afraid of losing purpose once you play enough, then good for you! You can also relax. If someone tried to speedrun through all of the Campaigns(including DLC) in Destiny 2 it would not take long, but that is 100% not the mindset a player should have when playing this kind of game. There are daily bounties, weekly bounties, Strike playlists(imagine a playlist full of different songs that will randomly be cycled, but instead of songs its missions that are the equivalent of a boxed set with bosses and all sorts of varying gameplay).

Or so it was... (aprox. 900 hr mark edit begins here)

My original review back when I only had 75hrs on Steam was a good one(had played it on B.net before it was on Steam). I no longer recommend Destiny 2 due to it betraying all I had given it marks for in the above statements. Loot needs to be constantly changed out, everything is just a repeat of something else, and with Beyond Light's release, it officially became standard for Bungie to remove content in order to add more. Bungie has turned a game into a chore. You are constantly fed the idea that if you are not constantly playing then you will lose a once in a lifetime opportunity for something that is just ever so slightly a different shade of a color nobody can actually spot out of the handfull of other stuff being shoved out.\t\t\t\t\t
