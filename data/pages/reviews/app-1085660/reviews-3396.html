\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a Warframe player, I never went for Destiny because of the Activision-driven intense monetisation - that kills games, and is an automatic no from me.
But now it’s free to play (though I bought the expansions) it feels like the right things are being prioritised. Quality. Players. Creativity. Narrative.

This is an incredible game - and not just because of gameplay, which is of course some of the very best and around. What I wasn’t prepared for was the mindblowing visuals - the art is awe inspiring, vast locations, with towering structures, colossal planets looming above, insane, infinite inter-dimensional voids - it’s a sci-fi art masterpiece. AND the combat and epic challenges like Zero Hour are very, very fun.

It’s slightly daunting at first to know just where to start, like Warframe, but you get the hang of it.

I highly recommend joining a clan ASAP. Lots of the missions and quests do give plenty of direction, but there are many - and this is a very, very good thing - that are much more cryptic. For these you’ll want to be able to ask for advice, and recruit a fire team, either of more experienced players to guide you, or just as manpower. The results - finally beating a hard mission, having learned the route together, are extremely satisfying.

It can seem like a struggle when a quest only gives vague direction - but it it’s way better than every quest just telling you to follow a marker. Often it feels like video games of the olden days, when you’d have to talk to people to find out what to do. I’ve added more friends on steam since playing destiny than in my whole time playing games. The community involvement is fun but not at all essential for every part of the game. It’s definitely nice to have though.\t\t\t\t\t
