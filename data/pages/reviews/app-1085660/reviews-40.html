\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I am still playing and finding some enjoyment in the game, I think anyone who would enjoy Destiny 2 is already playing it. That being said, even I am starting to question how much I enjoy this game, as a veteran with well over 1000 hours played.

These reviews are for new players, and if you are a new player I cannot, in good conscience, recommend this game to you. The pricing structure on past content will require you to spend likely over 100 dollars just to get caught up, while the pricing of new expansions and season passes continue to rise. It is just not a good investment for someone on the fence of buying this game.

The gameplay feels great, and the world has so much promise, but over and over Bungie continues to make minute changes and additions that feel minuscule compared to the amount of revenue the game creates. I can't speak for others, but I always hoped the game would continue innovating and adding meaningful content or gameplay changes, new exotics, and ways to play. While the sandbox and buildcrafting by extension is in one of the best places it's ever been, the narrative, ritual playlist activities, pvp, and just meaningful mixups to content releases in general are NOT in the best place they've ever been. They have made some positive changes and mixups with things like Strand, but changes or additions on that scale feel few and far between. The game is not outdoing itself each update, and maybe that's unrealistic thinking, but when they raise pricing as if it DOES keep getting better, it causes me to come at it at a harsher angle.

This combined with the expansion pricing changes, seasonal pass pricing changes (makes you pay $15 instead of $10 if you buy with silver instead of getting the "deluxe" edition of the current expansion), the addition of a $10 event pass for extra cosmetics (for what have usually been 100% free events mind you), separation of dungeons from seasonal pricing (effectively pushing people to pay for the deluxe edition of the game to maintain a content-complete game or else pay more individually), and other increasingly prevalent monetization schemes and tactics to encourage spending at every corner. All this in a game that went "f2p" a few years ago but offers very little content for f2p players whilst seemingly using the f2p tagline as an excuse to increase the aggressiveness of the monetization elsewhere in the game.

Destiny 2 continues to be one of the best feeling PVE shooters out there, but built off the back of that model is a live-service game that refuses to push the envelope, and has poor pricing for new players and attempts to find new ways to monetize current ones. If you play this, you will no doubt find something to enjoy here, but be warned of the trajectory this game has been taking. This game feels like it does just enough to get by, to keep you on board for another year, and this isn't new to live service games. Foundationally this game is good, but there are enough questionable decisions and play-it-safe development choices (even with a few big changes like Strand, which is ultimately just a sub-class with a grapplehook which, in truth, is great) that makes this game feel like it lacks innovation and accessibility to new players.\t\t\t\t\t
