\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL: DR- This game is fun if you have time to sink into. It is also easy to pick up. There are three characters that you can max out to 1010 gear wise and even more than that. So many exotic quests and regular quests for unusual guns. Going through the journey to maxing out every character is really fun. I highly recommend all DLCs and bringing friends with you. (In-Depth Review Below)

I started playing Destiny 1 on the console and played it for about 60 days in-game time. That game was insanely fun, and I maxed out all my characters to 400 and had the best gear available. Grinding was so much fun, and that's how this game is as well. Since Activision took over Bungie, they ruined the game, but Bungie has rebounded and has made it like Destiny 1 again after they ended their deal with Activision. Year 1 of this game was fun to an extent, which made me feel burnt out of that grinding aspect that I had in Destiny 1, and I never touched D2 until last year, which I gave it another try and still refused to play it.

In the last 2 weeks, I have put 61 hours into Destiny 2, and I have been having so much fun it is crazy. Getting back on, I was extremely overwhelmed by all the vendors I had to visit and talk to and quests I had to complete. 61 hours in, and my quests page is still filled almost to the max. I grew back the love of grinding to make my character look cool, having the best gear available, and being able to do endgame content. Currently, I'm 977, and there isn't a current max, but 1010 is an excellent limit to go for. I have gotten some unique guns as well as some good exotics but nowhere near having the best gear as of yet.

Having 3 characters keeps the game more exciting and makes you grind even more. Sure, you can play one character, but having more than one will increase better loot drops and powerful gear if you do the weekly bounties/objectives on every character. Starting out a new character might seem overwhelming because it has made 0 progressions will be very rough. Still, if you save your high light-powered weapons in your vault, you'll be able to transfer the weapons and boost your characters to higher levels. Currently, I have both my warlock and hunter to 836, and I Haven't done anything on them except the Red War campaign, and the grind begins from there.

Endgame Content: This part of the game is where the real fun starts. Doing Exotic Quests that take days to maybe even a month to complete. Doing Nightfalls/Raids can be very challenging because it requires skill, coordination, and very good gear for high light level encounters. It's also satisfying to complete raids and getting exclusive, rare, amazing weapons from them. Crucible is the PvP version of this game, and its fun. I don't recommend it if you're a new player because you'll get decimated by people who have way better guns. They rotate the playlists weekly, which keeps t fresh and fun. But they also have competitive modes like Iron Banner and Trials. Both of these modes are different. Iron Banner is regular crucible, but light advantages are enabled, meaning if somebody is 1010 light and you're 950, they will shred you so fast. You have to be prepared, and its also fun getting great rewards and earning sweet gear. Trials is a 3v3 based mode kind of like search and rescue from CoD and requires a lot of Crucible skill but beware the hackers on there, The gear from there is excellent too, but you have to be very good to reach there and having a good squad with perfect coordination.
Last but not least, Strikes. A fun, high PvE mode where you get to kill all the enemies and progress to take down a boss with chances of getting exotic gear and also powerful equipment. Exotic speed is not hard to come by. Doing anything that requires higher light will give you greater chances, so like grandmaster raids and nightfalls have a higher chance. So many options and the game resets every Tuesdays to keep if fresh every week till you're maxed out on all 3 characters and gives you something to work towards and after all that a new year begins and the grind resumes.

In conclusion, this game is fantastic, and I appreciate Bungie for changing it and making me fall back in love with Destiny and grinding. I highly recommend grabbing the DLCs, or else you will be missing out on a lot of fun and content. I also recommend bringing friends with you since it is Free-To-Play. Even if you don't have any, there is a Destiny 2 Discord server you can meet people on. I've met a lot of people using LFG (Looking For Group) on Destiny 1, and I partially talk to them still today. This game is a must-have for people who love FPS open-world ended games.\t\t\t\t\t
