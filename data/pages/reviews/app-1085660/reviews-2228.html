\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, so - I actually purchased this game around this time last year originally, got the season pass and forsaken too. I think I spent around $80 USD altogether at the time.

I genuinely, really enjoyed the game. I played D1 when it first launched until level 20. And thoroughly enjoyed it. Never played the expansions, but never had anything bad to say.

With D2, i was really pleased. I enjoy the gun and shooting styles, the ability variance of each class, the lore and the captivating worlds and visual design elements!

However, I did take an extended break from Destiny 2 for while when Shadowkeep first came out. The seasons were great and offered a new twist on things I wasn't used to, and I played all through the Summer. This made sense and was enjoyable with no subscription based model. I had a good time with it.

But as far as end-game content goes, it's a grind. Literally. It feels like all I'm doing is grinding all the time. Which is find, but sometimes it feels a little intense compared to the rewards. Which I guess adds and element of pride to completion? But that is something I'd note to new players, as end-game feels completely different (at times even separate) from the main story-line. Which again, wasn't terrible - but just a note!

I just recently picked up Shadowkeep a couple hours ago, as it was on sale and I learned it included the season pass, so it made sense for me. I'm enjoying it so far, but I've been reading a lot of negative reviews in regards to weapon, ability and skill nerfing. Which, i know can be super frustrating; so I'm interested to see how my experience progresses!



Bottom line though, i'd say it's worth the money. I think now, if you buy Shadowkeep, you get D2 base + the previous expansions and season pass? That's a deal for the abundance of content if you want my opinion. I got my copy on sale for around $24 USD. Haven't seen or noticed any bugs yet! But I've only clocked a couple hours so far on Shadowkeep, so I'll be sure to update after I play more!\t\t\t\t\t
