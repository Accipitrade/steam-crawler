\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have put thousands of hours into this series. I loved this game. But it was because it allowed me to love it that it back difficult to break away from it when it grew to be toxic for me.

The game currently suffers from a lack of endgame incentives, highly predatory FOMO tactics, blatant priority of the MTX shop over the actual game, and the content is become more and more sparse with each season.

If you've never played Destiny or aren't very experience with the series. This game can be TONS of fun! There is a ludicrous amount of content just with the base version of the game. If you buy Forsaken that amount blows up and a bit more with Shadowkeep. It's a great game starting fresh, if a bit overwhelming.

The new player experience is pretty poor as Bungie has done an awful job at guiding new players or explaining mechanics in the game. But regardless, it can be a very fun game, especially if you have friends to play with.

The problems I have with the game arise the further into the game you get. So unless you really get hooked on the game you're unlikely to run into them.

I love the game, and there's a lot of fun to be had, but I can't recommend it to anyone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis constantly evolving looter shooter is among the best of its kind, I have over 4000 hours across PC and PS4/5 and I'm still playing after 7 years (across D1 and D2). Gunplay is a masterpiece, as you'd expect from Bungie, the world, OST, art design and sound design are always 11/10. The raids and dungeons are the the pinnacle of this design, and the world is easy to fall into while also serving as an explanation for that top end of content. As of late Bungie has made the best campaign for destiny that the franchise has ever seen, if you're looking to get into the game I recommend trying the Witch Queen campaign for a good experience.

The game strikes an interesting balance between fun looting and tedious grind that resonates really well with me but absolutely isn't for everyone. They keep it interesting by adding new modes and if you make it to endgame they have some really challenging content to keep you busy in an activity all week. Rewards from these activities range anywhere from downright insulting to "OH MY GOD I GOT IT" to chill grinding nights with good rolls by the end.

If you haven't played before:
This game can be hard to get into, a lot of things don't get explained and especially with the fragmented story now you get dropped in the deep end sometimes. Bungie has tools to let fans make apps to help manage items, triumphs, quests or whatever else, and the community puts together extremely detailed guides to get basically anything in the game at maximum efficiency, or archive and explain the story that isn't in the game anymore. If you know anyone playing the game it's really likely they will help you learn everything because that's just the kind of friendly community Destiny has created.

Seasonal content is quite weak most of the time being a very basic 3-6 player mode designed for people without a brain to be able to complete with some very meh loot offerings. The seasonal gamemodes tend to be quite weak outside of whatever new endgame stuff there is, while the story is a good side story that expands the world or characters some. This content is only $10 and gets deleted at the end of the year so it makes sense that it's usually reused assets, side stories and repetitive modes that players stop playing pretty quick.

I have to bring up as well that campaign content from years 1 & 2 are completely deleted as well as all seasonal content from anything besides the current year, making the story a little confusing if you don't know all the various little seasonal stories that expanded the characters we interact with. Overall doesn't make a massive impact but an in game way to see the cutscenes after the fact would be better

-------------

TL:DR this game goes through a lot of ups and downs and I don't know what exactly keeps me here during it's the droughts, but during it's new content releases this game feels like home with fluid gameplay and a fascinating universe I can't pull myself away from.\t\t\t\t\t
