\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUsed to play this thing a bunch, but ever since the Activision split, it's been one bad decision after another. The original game content was vaulted (removed) and the game made 'free to play'.

The free to play tag here is the biggest lie there is. Around 75% of the game is locked behind these DLCs that are ludicrously expensive. Not to mention the new paid event passes, battle passes and content keys.

For example:
Dungeons used to be season content, but now requires Special dlc editions or overpriced dungeon keys.
Seasonal content used to be improved with the season pass, now it's required to even access it.
Events used to offer a wide array of themed cosmetics for playing, but now provides the chance to purchase them separately for silver (Premium currency).
Many abilities and weapons are locked behind dlc, some of which are incredibly strong and often recommended to partake in higher difficulty activities.
This often results in Seasonal challenges being unobtainable as the dlc is required to access the content relating to the challenge.

It really is a shame as the story was good, I really enjoyed it when it was there, but now? You're jumping in mid-story with little context as to what happened prior, bar 6-7 sentences telling you the tl/dr version of those events.

Seasonal content usually carries on the story, and thus without it, you're left pretty unaware of some crucial plot points. This wouldn't be an issue, but every major dlc release, these seasonal activities and stories are also vaulted, leaving you with a few sentences summing up the bare minimum of that line of content.

The pvp scene is a mess with dlc weapons primarily dominating, the stasis class proving far superior to the others. With the dwindling player numbers too, it's hard to justify playing pvp knowing you'll most likely come across the same experienced players time and again.

Overall? I'd say this game needs some massive changes in order to justify giving you its time. The recent seasonal content has been dull, bland and repetitive. The stories mediocre. Asset recycling in abundance (Some of which is from the old removed content) and generally, is not worth the time or money. However, ironically enough, the paid cosmetic storefront has been receiving frequent and higher quality updates than most other things in the game. They don't care about the player-base, they care about their wallets.

TL/DR: This game is a money grab at this point. Low quality content pushed out slowly with a big focus on leeching as much as they can from your pockets. Heavy emphasis on FOMO with limited time seasonal rewards and content.

Can't recommend this in the state it's in.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI used to love this game. I started playing a few years ago and during that time I've made some of my best memories & met lots of amazing people. However, recently Bungie seems incredibly unable to provide the absolute bare minimum in up-keeping a live service game, despite their previous successes & current resources. The servers are INCREDIBLY inconsistent, going down for several hours on random days, extremely extended downtimes on patch release days, even after they allegedly "improved" their backend to allow for the weekly Tuesday reset/update. Almost every week, without fail, the game is down for several hours. And if it's not server problems plaguing the game, other basic functionalities won't be working, this week's issue is that you are entirely UNABLE TO MATCHMAKE IN ANY ACTIVITY, in a game where HALF OF THE ACTIVITIES ARE MATCHMADE.

What kind of content is there on a weekly basis that might make up for this? A single cutscene. The current season is going to last another 2 months and there is practically 0 content left. On top of that, we've yet again reached the cycle in Destiny where the 'loot' part of 'looter-shooter' does not exist. You are rewarded with a 54 base stat piece of armour (good armour is 65+) for finishing activities most of the time, and almost every weapon released with this season is entirely underwhelming. I understand that power creep is a problem, but they have literally already introduced a solution to it that was somehow horrible, AND they've refused to go back and iterate on.

Endgame pinnacle content has also fallen off tremendously in the last year or so. Sandbox reworks have made ability spam builds commonplace, almost all of which also have infinite health regeneration, both of these coupled with some of the easiest raid & dungeon activities we've gotten since the game first released have led to low levels of game mastery, and we've lost reasons to improve, continuously play, or build craft.

Next let's talk about the monetisation. They've increased prices for seasons, even though there is very little content actually included with seasons, maybe a couple hours for the first ~6 weeks of the multi-month seasons. They've introduced a "Dungeon Key" - the purchase of which allows you to do 2 medium length 3-player missions, releasing throughout the year. These used to come free with their respective seasons or DLCs, now each costs the same amount as AN ENTIRE season.

This is a company that was bought for $3.6 BILLION dollars. Destiny 2 at this point seems to only be a large cash cow for Bungie that is still running to allow them to work on Marathon & Matter.

I now only ever log on to this game if my friends are playing, or the weekly reset (gods willing the game is actually online) to check the current cosmetic store offerings (at least you can purchase SOME stuff there with free currency - very low bar they've managed to just barely clear) and see if there's a weekly hour of 'story' content to play.

I really miss the days where I'd have lots of repeatable content to play, weapons with rolls that excelled at different things to farm/chase, activities that were fun & secrets to explore, friends who would get on just to hang out and mess around. Now we're all playing different games (Diablo) that actually reward your time.

TL;DR
Don't buy all of this game, it's way too expensive. If you want to get into destiny, only buy the latest DLC, and make sure you really like the game before spending more money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImportant distinction to make before you decide to play Destiny 2: The free to play moniker is deceiving. Yes, you can play the game for free, but without the paid content that makes up for a majority of the soul that is Destiny 2, creating initial impressions from playing for free is difficult. Therefore, it is better to frame the game in light of one that is paid rather than one that is free.
With that being said, Destiny 2 provides some of the most satisfying gun-play and ability sandboxes there are without compromising too much in terms of quality of presentation. You'd be hard pressed to find an MMO that is like Destiny 2.
In addition to this, the developers have really improved at making Destiny 2 engaging from multiple perspectives and avenues. Want a relaxing game to just grind out and get awesome loot for? No worries. Want to fight against other players with many different guns and play-styles? Be my guest. Want a cooperative game to test the mettle of you and your friends? We've got you covered.
Are all of these avenues perfect? No. But the fact they exist in the first place in a state that isn't completely repulsive, broken, or mediocre is a testament to how much this game can give for you.
One final thing. If you are someone who has decided to venture into the game for the first time, try and find a friend. If not a friend, look to the community. The current new player experience is not very good, but I ensure you with someone to guide you it will all be worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
