\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeing a D1 player, I held off on this game on PC because there was no cross platform transfer system, until now. That being said, I will now list my opinions on this game.

Pros:
Very fun, there is a lot of grind, but 90% of the time it's very rewarding for what you had to have done. There is a lot of content as well, and a majority of it you can play with friends, with only a few things being solo only. The loot system is also nice, I am very glad that they brought back perk randomization for guns again too. The story is pass-able from base to great during Forsaken. They also brought back a lot of returning things from D1 into this one for the veterans and for new players to discover. Many more that I cannot think off the top of my head.

Cons:
There cannot be pros, without cons. This game may have a lot of content, but after a while they run out and you are left replaying repetitive game modes and tasks. The gun meta in PvE is good, but once you reach PvP, it gets very painful. There are some very busted weapons and some Exotic armour pieces that give you head aches. The story is good during some ages of the game, but terrible in others. The newest DLC gives you a crappy story and not enough content for the $35 you paid (or more if you got a better edition).

Overall I would recommend if you got friends to play with, or are charismatic enough to make easy friends from LFG, just don't spend money on DLC unless you are willing to take it to the next level.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played this game since day 1. I have been through the roughest of times with it. I actually stopped playing for about a year because I just wasn't feeling the content. Osiris and Warmind dlc's were had a very questionable price tag because they both felt too short. They also did not live up to my expectation of a "good" dlc pack. Forsaken brought the game back to life for me. Forsaken was an expensive dlc but it had a lot of content in it. But after awhile it got stale. This is when I took that break. Shadowkeep has introduced some new mechanics to the game and new content as well. But i feel as though the price tag was $35 dollars for the dlc and the season pass. The campaign felt as short as osiris ad warmind, but it brought a new raid, strikes, game mechanics. I overall feel mixed with the game because of how much money i put into it to finally feel as though it is a finished product. I recommend this game because: it is mindless fun alone or with friends, has challenging activities, and so much replayability. Another criticism I have for this game is that i feel that certain classes need certain items to feel useful in pve. You always hear someone asking if a hunter is running this, or a titan running that, and if a warlock is even needed. Crucible seems to be dominated by the recluse and titans with one eyed mask. I usually try to stay clear of pvp. Hope this helped anyone decide if they want to give this game a shot and if they would be willing to put money into it for future dlc.\t\t\t\t\t
