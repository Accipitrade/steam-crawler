\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I'm going to break this into a few pieces as the way this game is purchased is weird:

Free to play version (New Light): I'll be honest here, what you get to play for free is definitely a lot, but if you're a new player and don't have any friends that play co-op and explain things, you're going to be a little bored and likely annoyed at how bad the experience is as a new player. If you can make it through the absolutely tedious initial quests, you may find some value, but it'll be very frustrating, confusing, and annoying.

Foresaken Expansion: The best expansion, in my opinion. Solid amount of content, story is interesting, etc. This is really where the game shines and you'll get a lot of fun out of this.

Shadowkeep Expansion: This isn't very good--it's like a hodgepodge of bits of unrelated content. Unless you are a big Destiny fan, you'll likely find this a bit confusing and disjointed. The story piece of this expansion is basically replaying a watered down version of things you've already done in Destiny if you're an original player. If you're a new player, most of it will be lost on you. It's also incredibly short and ends abruptly without any real progression. As far as gameplay, there's a new dungeon style mission which is really cool. There's a new raid which is pretty neat, but again kind of disjointed from what you'd expect from the expansion based on the story that was told. Long story short, this expansion has a couple of decent adds for the game, but isn't even close to worth the price, in my opinion.

Multiplayer: Can be fun in very small doses, but no dedicated servers and pretty awful feeling from a connection stand point. On the plus, there's a lot of freedom to create different loadouts for your character and play in interesting ways. Some weapons and abilities can be annoying at times, but overall I'd say it's some good fun minus the terrible netcode.

Conclusion: Playing Destiny as a dedicated player can be an insulting, frustrating, and ultimately disappointing experience. With that said, if you take it for what it is, don't pay full price for any new content without first getting some info and reviews, and don't get expectations up based on marketing, you can have a TON of fun. With those very important caveats, I can recommend at least trying out New Light and see what you think.\t\t\t\t\t

As of the newest season (Season of Plunder), ALL new content bar a few weapons and a single exotic are locked behind a paywall. This means that if you want to play the newest content, or stay up to date with the story or equipment, you have no choice but to buy the season pass.

If you pick up this game for free, you are essentially picking up a demo for a subscription service game, with the occasional big DLC. I wouldn't actually have a problem with this, if they were up front about it. I do not recommend this game because of Bungie's opacity in regards to the reality of how they've chosen to monetise the game.\t\t\t\t\t
