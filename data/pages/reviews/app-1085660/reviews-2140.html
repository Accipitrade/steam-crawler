\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is a reason that the total reviews are consistently higher than recent reviews for the game and its additional content. This game is good, dont get me wrong, and free to play is a great thing to do but its clear that it wasnt made free to play to improve the game, grow the community, give back to its players, or anything real. The only reason it was made free to play is so that they could dig their hooks in on new people and it sucks because it is so clear that bungie is capable of so much more. So much of the game is paywalls and grind and yet every now and again you see a glimmer (pun intended) of real substance. Sadly however the most recent expansion, shadowkeep, shows a clear reversal of that perceived fortune. With complete honesty I havent even "played" most of it, I stopped when I was told I had to farm a full set of gear to even enter the main area which consisted of getting some arbitrary MacGuffin then doing some arbitrary MacTasks to get the arbitrary MacGear. Let me spell that out, they forced you to get a new set of gear, that you had to purify some essence to get, and made you farm most of the essence yourself; that is three levels of unnecessary farming. This is all done in an attempt to keep the players active for a longer period of time and it is very clear. Whereas forsaken for instance had repetition, literally just giving you a list of people to kill, each of those people had a basic story that fit them into a larger narrative, provided a little world building (although I do wish there was a tad bit more), and each and every one of them provided a new fighting style. This culminated in hours of play that remained enjoyable throughout which was only amplified by the emotional impact of a major characters death (one that for many players was very loved), and the foreshadowing of a light (pun intended) being shown onto the moral nuances of the actions our character takes, even being referenced in character dialog later. With that praise said, it should be very clear the distinction between past DLC stories (new fights, a deeper and more nuanced look into preexisting status quos, and ties to things we already knew and cared about), and the newest (none of that). This is all indicative of a larger issue I believe and one that is sadly becoming prevalent in today's gaming culture. Companies are trying very hard to create "Live service" games while not realizing that the core of such a game relies on the community. (I will be using Warframe of how to do this successfully since it has had a long period of success and growth even before the "Live Service" bandwagon started) With a traditional game sale the community may prompt a few extra sales but by a month in the company has made most of the profits and can, for the most part, forget about the game. In contrast, a "Live Service" game is one that relies on you keeping your community happy and them WANTING to give you money because you provide good content and have remained in good faith. It seems that these game companies fail to realize that the community's moral is integral to the game's success and is still trying to sell DLC and Microtransactions with straight marketing which wont work because the people in the game will hear it through word of mouth (as a relatively tight knit community makes information quick to spread, especially with big "influencers" who also play the game helping) and people outside of the game wont come in just to pay extra to get that new content which they cant even play yet. Warframe as an example has for the longest time remained grounded, open to the community, invested in their community, conservative on microtransactions (ill get into this later), and has hid absolutely ZERO in game content behind paywalls. (excepting a few skins and stuff which HAS to be paid for through steam but that seems to be more of a steam thing then a Digital extremes thing) Even the currency that they do have (you can work for everything on your own, using only in game materials except for cosmetics, but can buy virtually everything with Platinum, the in game currency) can be traded between players meaning that while every unit of platinum was payed for players CAN still get it without paying real money. While yes Destiny 2 and Warframe have VASTLY different scales, the work should already be put in, the engine is made, the story is setup, the development structure is present, etc, so their operating costs to income ratio shouldnt be that far off. So if Warframe can run for 7 years now (it came out march 25th 2013 on microsoft windows) and (I'm assuming) remain profitable for the majority of that time, Destiny 2 should be capable of the same thing, albeit with slight changes to account for the slightly different gameplay styles, scale, and demographic. With this established, the completely insanity present with the absurd level of monetization and the quantity of grind should be self evident. Yes there are clearly differences however if the game cannot remain profitable with its current setup changes need to be made for every party involved to remain content. With that said, that is the future of the game, I am only writing this in the hopes of inspiring a more critical eye in those who read it, before or after choosing to pick up the game as the state of the game as it is is passable, not great, but passable. So if you have made it this far, do not let the slow encroachment of monetization pass you by, it will only hurt everyone involved; the community will reach a breaking point eventually and they will stop putting up with it, and by that point it may be too far gone.
edit : exclusivity also doesnt help when your trying to let your community grow, I dont know how but I would recommend allowing some way for new players to get content that was only available in the past (I could be wrong here as I havent done research into if this specific group of items has another method of attainment but) such as Cayde's letters from his missions\t\t\t\t\t
