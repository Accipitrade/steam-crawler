\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is quite possibly the worst MMO I've ever played. This game twists your nards in unfathomably stupid ways that makes you want to stop playing but like an abusive partner knows you won't. Every bit of content is monetized, don't let that free to play tag fool you. All expansions are full price at launch, and no seasonal content remains after the year is over. In fact, the game has MISSING content from its first three expansions, as lukewarm as they were to begin with. Speaking objectively though, the gameplay is good. The powers feel nice, the guns have nice randomized perks that give unique options for playstyles, and overall the game manages to make you feel like a godlike space marine.

Unfortunately, all that is bogged down by the aforementioned monetization issues, and did I forget to mention they have a cash shop? Of course they do, why wouldn't they on top of their full price expansions, four annual season events, real life merch, and collectors' editions? If you're some new guy looking to give it a try because of the free to play tag, heed my words: Don't. It is free to play in the sense you may poke your head in. If you want to actually do anything, cough up the cash.

I hope this game dies one day. I can't say that for many, but I will make an exception here. I do NOT recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny was quite the ride. For almost 10 years I've been playing but this time it looks like I'm done. I love the game but with as high of a pay wall there is you just don't get enough quality content. I personally enjoy the PVP the most but it has received the most bare bones updates for several years and even as I began raiding and doing dungeons instead the content never really kept me invested for a full season since Beyond Light. That may be pretty recent compared to launch but that's still a lot of time that instead of only playing D2 like I used to, other games just became more favorable. Like I said, I like PVP. Trials of Osiris and the comp playlist are what I used to live in but cheating has become far too common in any list making it incredibly difficult to continue going through it unless if I have a full fireteam ready to go at all times. I don't think the cheaters is the deal breaker since it happens in all games of this nature. It's more distressing that the content is drip fed to us. Maybe if every season the changes were universally positive that would be ok but that's not what happened. Crucible like I said receives almost no support but let's shift our focus to Gambit. I remember on release everyone loved Gambit, the community was hyped, there was chase loot, and a special event to get Malfeasance. Nowadays almost everyone in all my discord servers and all of my friends in game despise it as a Pinnacle farm mainly. There are some awesome weapons that have rotated in but it feels like the mode was switched to Prime as a way to leave it as-is and be done with it. For what seems like 3 years the real focus of Bungie has been Dungeons as well as the Season pass. I think the season pass was a good idea to stay relevant against battle royal games but has only been mid as best. Cosmetics are always hit or miss in it and It feels like just another completion tab rather than super cool rewards for playing the game. The dungeons however have been pretty cool. Not quite my cup of tea but I respect the design and care that went into changing up the game. I think of them in the same vein as raids: when I like them I will run them all day with my team but if I don't then it's a bit of a chore getting the weapons/cosmetics I want from them. Now raids are where most people I play with love to spend their time and I can see why. They are really cool gameplay experiences that I think have always maintained Destiny's status either through new release hype, weapon chasing, mechanics, speed, or even aesthetic. It just feels a little disappointing that all these years later none of the newer raids have made as much of an impact for me as VoG or Kingsfall which ironically are back in the game. If there was one that I think ascends to the heights of Destiny greats it would be Last Wish but that was year 2 so in other words, a long time ago. I still think of Destiny as a great game at it's core but we aren't getting the quality we pay for. We're even being prompted to pay even more with how much the game has become an Eververse centric fashion show. Maybe it's rubbed me the wrong way for a personal reason but it's disheartening to see them advertise as hard as a Gacha game when I've already payed $100 every year per platform I wanted to be on. It very much is personal opinion but I can still remember early Destiny not needing to crutch on Eververse to have cool cosmetics or get me to log in every week. Hopefully I stay off Destiny for good this time and I hope everyone still having fun enjoys it through the end of the story. I just feel like I can't even recommend it to someone new considering this has been my favorite game for almost 10 years and I'm done.\t\t\t\t\t
