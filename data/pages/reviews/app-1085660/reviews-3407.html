\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSomething something no more Activision, something something don't usually write reviews.

When I first played Halo (now bear with me here) it was with a friend. Growing up I never had an Xbox, mainly because I was a Sony fanboy (I'm reformed, don't tell my PS4 it doesn't know I'm here). Growing up I played shooters like Medal of Honor and Call of Duty, back in the days of Big Red One. I had never played something like Halo, Marathon, or any of Bungie's other creations. To say that Halo surpassed any and all expectations of what a shooter could be for me is an understatement and the game remains one of my top 3 franchises here in the present. So fast forward to Destiny. The first one was a letdown. The story is told exclusively in grimoires that you read online on Bungie's website. The shooting was what I remembered but it didn't have the same feelings of grandeur in the place we found ourselves in and the wonder of what lay beneath the surface that Halo had. Halo also had story you could find in hidden places but those places were IN the game. I left the first one after Taken King, feeling let down by a once treasured developer. I resolved to not bother with Destiny 2 as I had no faith things could get any better.

Well obviously that didn't work out. Roughly around the release of Curse of Osiris I returned (on Xbox One) and I was pleased to find that there was more story and development in the first few minutes of Destiny 2 than Destiny had in it's entire experience. The old feelings returned and I began to crave more from a world that first felt so lifeless. It's the same solid gunplay that is to only be expected from Bungie, the classes have been expanded and you can do some pretty cool things with them now with the new abilities, the game has a wealth of content and I add my voice to the chorus of people saying this feels like what Destiny should have been. I still miss Halo, the likes of Big Team Battle and other multiplayer modes - which i consider to be a big part of the game's experience - are entirely absent from Destiny. But perhaps the future will see them return. For now, Destiny is an extremely enjoyable experience that fans of Sci-fi and first person shooters should take the time to check out.

Note for new players: I know a lot of people are balking at the less than welcoming entrance they're given if they're brand new to Destiny 2. The game does indeed NOT start you off at the very beginning but in this weird limbo of somehow before and after the main campaign of Destiny 2? I'm not kidding, you start in the exact same beginning sequence as you did in Destiny 1 and then the game throws you into a space that should only have been accessible after completing the Red War, Destiny 2's story. it's confusing, it's weird, and certainly a better job could be done to ingratiate new players to the experience. But here's what I want to say to those new players; don't give up. This community has a lot of great people in it and I know plenty of us are more than willing to help the new folks get set on their path. So ask questions. Talk to us. With any luck Bungie will learn from this mistake too and fix it.
