\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Its a good game that's worth trying, considering its free, and when it comes to buying dlc, you have to decide how attached you are to the game and your wallet.

I just recently came from ps4 where I have almost 1.5k hours in the game, so my review has a little more experience than just 40 hours. This is a game that causes me some immense anger and joy. Whilst there are a multitude of things that are wrong with the game when it comes to balancing, which bungie have been notorious for since d1, this is a game that (once I returned to my position on m&k to being onto of the leaderboard) I find always there for me to play and 'enjoy'. My current problem lies in the not too much of the balancing (surprisingly) but how they have added content for the recent seasons. I now do have all the dlc on pc and I have to say that this season, along with the previous one that I played on console, gave virtually no new content, especially if you haven't purchased the season passes. They were just grind-fests that gave no real reward and weren't all that fulfilling. Whilst some of the content is fun to be played with a friend. it really isn't all that worth the $15 for the pass or extra $50 if you don't have Shadowkeep. This being said due to the fact I've being playing the franchise since 2014, I find it almost impossible to turn down buying the latest dlc. I have formed so many good memories and pledged so much time into the game I have almost formed a dependency on it. Whilst this may sound like a bad thing, I don't quite see it the same way. I have formed this love/hate relationship with the game because even since the start of d1, I saw the game at its core as my dream game that was full of limitless potential, as I'm sure many others did. The hate part comes in when lack-lustre dlc rolls around and poor nerfs/buffs take place.

Although this review is long and convoluted and I doubt many people will read it, the take-away is as such:
I love this game and I doubt I will ever stop loving this franchise, but this only when I think about it on a fundamental level. The dlc's can be meh and the balancing is often dumb/unwarranted, but that being said it still has immense charm and an amazing primary gameplay loop. I'm going to grind the ♥♥♥♥ out of the rest of this season and probably many seasons to come, no matter how many times I say I'm going to quit. This game is amazing but it might not be right for everyone, that said, if you like a bit of looter-shooter action and/or some competitive pvp, I'm sure you can find at least something in this game. The dlc's however are just there to keep you somewhat interested with new weapons and armour along with a new to-do list. The purchasing of dlc's I would only recommend if you find yourself enjoy the primary gameplay loop of pve, as most of the content from the dlc's will be pve related. Get the base game (if you have 100GB of space free) and get the dlc's too if you find yourself enjoy the game and not very attached to your money.\t\t\t\t\t
