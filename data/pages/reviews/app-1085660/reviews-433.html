\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a fairly fantastic game when it comes to lore, music and gameplay. It's incredibly fun and genuinely addictive at times, and can provide solid experiences when you play solo or with friends. Their raids almost never miss and the dev team do a fairly solid job at keeping up with player feedback on a semi-regular basis. Sometimes there are misses, but they end up getting it right with time.

However, the game isn't without its flaws. The new player experience, is next to, if not entirely, non-existent. I've found myself having to write an entire beginner's guide to get my friends into the game with an idea of what they're doing. The game can feel fairly empty to free to play players, and everything that is meta, or actually interesting, or even new, is all behind the expansions. You find yourself in a loop of endlessly running strikes, or other playlist activites with nothing else to do. Sometimes, the more appealing version of strikes, "Nightfalls" Are locked because the origin strike is part of an expansion, so you end up waiting a whole week just to play it.

Be warned before jumping in, F2P only ends up with minor scraps of the game, almost like an endless free trial, and I'd recommend paying for expansions if you're really interested in playing.

Destiny 2 is not a free game, to anyone who actually wants to experience the game.

Still a good game though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is an interesting game. PvE side, it's Not free to play what so ever, despite being free to get. You get a lot of the intro missions to stuff, but are immediately locked out once the tutorial is over. Outside of this, the game is very much an MMO by all of its standards. If you do not like the MMO genre the game isn't for you. The grind is there, and the overwhelming sense of accomplishment is there once you clear stuff. The grind isn't half bad, and I'd even say the exotic quests can some times be engaging.

On the PvP side, the game tends to suffer a little bit. It's fun, sure. The combat is halo styled + your class abilities. However, like half of the time it's some unskilled dude running around with a shot gun that can one tap you from two and a half miles away from each other. There is a STRONG lack of balancing in the PvP. If it's not a shotgun plaguing your every waking existence in PvP, its some guy with a brokenly powerful exotic weapon of that season, or from trials. Usually a hand cannon. Or something that has a stupid gimmick to it.
Despite this however, PvP can still be fun (sometimes) if you decide to join the cesspool of people who think they're good at PvP after using a shotgun. Or a broken exotic/Legendary

TL;DR
PvE is great, PvP needs some work.

The Story is another thing entirely. I know it because I've been with this game since the beginning. However if you're a New Light, you're going to have a REALLY rough time understanding whats going on. Which is really unfortunate because the on going story to destiny is actually pretty good. And I genuinely recommend looking into it if you play this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have a major love/hate relationship with this game. I am sure that most others with more hours can agree. This game provides with fantastic world building and lore, a great combat system with multiple stat builds for whatever you like, and just overall good gameplay. However, Bungie has managed to give us very exciting DLC's that will get you super hyped to play, and then fizzle out to where you are doing maybe one or two activities a day and getting bored. I feel with the release of Witch Queen that things may change for the better, but there are still many things that Destiny needs to improve on in order to get the fanbase back on track. Overall great game in my opinion. If you do plan on getting this game be sure to be ready to put the hours in if you do want to get good.\t\t\t\t\t
