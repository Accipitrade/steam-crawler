\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdated Review as of 05/29/2022 (Season of the Haunted)

It's remarkable how much of a 180 turn this game has done in the last year or so. Bungie has perfected the seasonal model and has found a way to create meaningful and fun content on a great schedule.

My one complaint at this point, still remaining, is the piecemeal pricing. This is the one area that I think could use significant improvement. To own all the content as of the current DLC year, you will need to buy (1) The Witch Queen, (2) 4 Season Passses, (3) the Dungeon Pack, and (4) Optional Event Passes. The obvious solution is just to buy the Digital Deluxe every year, which includes everything but the event passes, but its really hard to justify shelling out $100+ at the start of a DLC to pay for content that won't unlock for months.

I would vastly prefer if they grouped the 'Dungeon Pack' in with the Seasons themselves and charge more for the individual seasons. Its really hard to get friends into this game once explaining the amount of money they have to fork over up-front.

Other than this though, the game is in a great place.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've just surpassed the 300 hour mark for D2 on Steam and I thought it was a good time to reflect upon my history with Destiny so far. I got in around the time the Taken King was released for D1 and started playing it on PS4, then switched to Xbox to play with family and friends... eventually played OG D2 on Xbox as well. At that point, friends had left Destiny altogether before I got in because of all the nerfs and patches. I guess a lot of people didn't like having their toys basically taken away from them, but I feel like everything's got to be changed up from time to time. So now 2500 hours of gameplay later (combined across both games... with an almost year long break as I didn't play much of D2 year 2), I'd say hands down this is an AWESOME FPS and the best it has become so far, especially if you have friends to do group activities and/or you like competitive matches. They've done an incredible job catering the game more to solo players. There's so much more variety in the activities and the weaponry. The fact that it's now free to play is pretty wild, but that just gives everyone a chance to try it out before they decide if they want to add on the expansions, which are DEFINITELY worth it. You could also wait and get them on sale (like I did since I already owned them on Xbox and the expansions aren't cross-buy). If you are a cross-save player, you'll notice a decent PC blows console performance completely out of the water with its smooth framerate and fast loading. I can never make excuses for missing any shots or losing 1v1 engagements in pvp now LOL. The story can be a bit forgettable, but the lore can go really deep, reminding me of ancient or zen/buddhist ideals which is really nice. The recent addition of momentum control is my absolute favorite as it completely changes how the game is played in pvp and it's a lot of fun in general.

If you enjoy semi-mmo FPS games, you have to at least give it a shot!\t\t\t\t\t
