\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 itself is very fun but it totally lacks any basic social features that are commonplace in most multiplayer games. The in-game chat is turned off by default and so it goes completely unused. No one talks in this game, there is no tower chat or active communication of any sort in any area of the game. There is no matchmaking for almost all the end game content and the game provides absolutely no way to search for a group or to find someone to play with. There is no indication in the game that it completely lacks these features so for new players coming from a game with some type of in-game community it will be confusing and very disappointing.

You will never join a raid or group for any end game content without having to leave the game and use a 3rd party app or website created to fill the social void left in this game. Even if you choose to use the 3rd party apps the process of queuing for lobbies, then exchanging steam ID's and having to add random people on steam as a friend so I can be invited to a fireteam just to run a 15 minute nightfall is ridiculous.

This also applies to clans, there is no way to search or apply to a clan and no way to advertise or recruit for your clan in game. Your best bet is again to find another 3rd party app or maybe just sit in the tower and hope one of the 10 people you loaded in with is sitting there scanning for people without a clan tag.

Its honestly mind boggling how they released the game on pc in its current form. With the lack of basic social features and almost no in-game communication, use of 3rd party apps required due to a lack of necessary in-game features (Social and QoL e.g. DIM, Ishtar), strange button holding mechanics to interact with anything, the inability of being able to move items in your inventory around, the lack of being able to bind certain abilities to different keys (aka lunging 10 yards for a melee attack when I just want to throw my ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ knife or vice versa....whyyy is this a thing!!), jerky screen movements in all the menus and keys to cycle through tabs like people aren't using a mouse, its the most console feeling game you will ever experience on pc.

I do enjoy the game, the gameplay itself is very fun but the game design forces a solo feeling experience in a multiplayer game which is very odd. Its difficult to wrap my head around how the glaring lack of features has persisted through so many seasons and expansions and Bungie just continues to leave it up to players to use 3rd party apps to make Destiny 2 function properly and feel whole.

While I do recommend the game, keep in mind the use of 3rd party apps and websites will be needed to meet people, make friends and find new people to play with. If you are not actively pursuing this through 3rd party apps the majority of your game experience will be as a solo player.\t\t\t\t\t
