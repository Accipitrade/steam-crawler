\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor perspective, I come from post launch, but pre-Forsaken. I did not play the original Destiny.

Destiny is a game that I ultimately recommend, now that I've put some substantial time into it. Despite what my review is going to highlight here, an overwhelming negative perspective, I still enjoy the game, these are just my observations that someone might find helpful who hasn't jumped in yet. They do not encompass the game as a whole, they are, again, just my observations that might give someone pause that hasn't played before.

When I first jumped in, I was very apathetic. I didn't care about the story (and it's still worth noting that it's still absolutely nothing to write home about), and the whole premise of the grind felt very uninspired (and take note that as a looter-shooter, it is entirely about grinding) New weapons that are slightly different than the one you just replaced it with, and largely with common, uncommon, and rare classed weapons, there is very little different between them. To an extent, legendaries suffer from the same issue, but there is an increase in diversity, and a need to find those that suit your playstyle. Exotics are the bread and butter of this game, as is to be expected, and they can and do vary widely in playstyle.

This game isn't without issue either, far from it. Multiple instances where there's fundamentally broken builds or items that, from time to time, have had to be completely disabled (by Bungie) for extended periods of time. With each new "season" (as they're now called), there seems to be yet some substantial flaw with something. Telesto is a recurring theme here, but is by no means exclusive.

This also isn't a game to play solo. It really isn't. A significant majority of the endgame content requires a team (with particular note to raids being unable to be completed solo because of certain progression mechanics) of up to six people. There isn't difficulty scaling for solo played content, meaning what you would face in a team of anywhere from two-six players, in a Strike for example, is the same fight you'll face solo. Some playlists have matchmaking, some don't, any content that needs a team that doesn't have matchmaking means you will need to assemble an appropriately sized fireteam. Modes that have matchmaking mean you run the risk of being pared with someone who, especially if you're new, will be moving way too quickly for you (speedrunning, skipping nonessential fights), players who generally don't have any clue what's going on, or players that are AFK (it's a somewhat viable farming method)

Although I participate in PVP, It's not my bread and butter, it's largely about slogging through it enough to complete a quest objective. I do not like PVP, and I feel that it's largely about meta builds. If you have a meta build, you will dominate those that don't, and be competitive with those that do. If you don't have a meta build (and can't build one because of gear limitations), you're going to be in for a bit of a rough time.

I like the content that the current content model brings, but I dislike the implementation. It uses a Fortnite style linear progression system; Unlock levels in a time-limited "battle pass" season. It encourages a very linear bounty grinding playstyle, as bounties provide significantly more exp than activities (raids, strikes, adventures, public events, ETC - though it's possible that this may change in future). If you don't have time to invest in this game to grind for those time-limited progression levels, you will miss out on content that you were unable to unlock.\t\t\t\t\t
