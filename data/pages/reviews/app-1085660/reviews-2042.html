\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny is a rollercoaster of a game. The highs are soaring and the lows are canyons.

The current state of Destiny 2 is like this: playing weekly & daily bounties can be a lot of fun. Repeatedly running Strikes and Crucible matches reward you regularly and the rewards are better now than I remember them being a few months ago. If you have an active clan you can easily do Raids and harder versions of the Nightfall. But if you have no luck in finding a good clan, like me, there is a limit to the amount of content with a meaningful rewards that you can do on any given day. Your ability to do Raids and increased Nightfall difficulty will be dependent on how long you want to spend in Destiny LFG.

There are some lingering problems that do deter my enjoyment of the game. As context I played Destiny 1 back in 2014 and played through every expansion (minus the Raids) up until Rise of Iron. I bought Destiny 2 at release on console and played with a small group of friends at that point, which was much more fun than solo play. I have played Destiny 2 on and off throughout the expansions, and I do think that in a lot of ways Forsaken & Shadowkeep were good steps forward for the game. The big problems, for me at least, are these:

1. No Strike specific loot
2. Poor Raid matchmaking system
3. Season Pass system
4. Eververse

Strike specific loot was a thing in the first game, but for some reason it is not in this game. I somewhat feel like this is a lingering effect of the advice Blizzard gave Bungie for making a 'looter' game. No Strike specific loot means that there is no reason to replay Strikes except for completing bounties, and it takes away from the satisfaction of acquiring a rare piece of gear.
There is a kind of matchmaking system for Raids, but it honestly sucks. On PS4 and Xbox One I have waited upwards of 45 minutes as a Seeker with no Guides ever matching with me. To be fair I have not tried on Windows yet so maybe it is better here.
Having content being split into seasons does work in some games. Battle Royales can use seasons to separate content and keep all players working towards the same stuff. This works in a Battle Royale because those games have things in common like one map, 1-3 game modes, and everyone is doing the same thing. There also usually is no story to pay attention to for most people. These games are designed to have players just constantly jumping in to match after match. An MMO-lite/FPS RPG like Destiny is not suitable for seasons, in my opinion. These kinds of games are usually suited to expansions. Large chunks of content immediately delivered, some small updates afterwards, and a gap between releases to allow new and veteran players to catch up and flatten the level curve. The way Bungie has implemented seasons means that players will be grinding about as much, if not more, than your average Battle Royale to complete a season and there often is no meaningful unique content you gain from it.
The grind in Destiny 2 is related to the Eververse. The Eververse if the premium currency store in the game, which means that you have to pay real-world money to acquire this premium currency. The season pass costs about $12-$14 CAD, which is on par with other games with a Season system. However, the more cool looking stuff you can buy in the Eververse often costs more than a comparable package in other games. For example if I want to buy a cool set of skins for my Warlock gear, that is ~$26. $26 for a set of skins for the head, hands, chest, legs, and class item. In Modern Warfare (2019) I can buy a $26 package and I get a cool skin for an operator, new legendary weapon blueprints, a voice line, a new player icon, a new player banner, and a new takedown kill. The amount of value I get from these packages in Modern Warfare, even if I don't use all of the items, is far greater than what I pay in Destiny 2.

With all of this said, I still have fun with Destiny 2. The game feels wonderful to play, the visual design is as good as it has ever been, and there are genuinely fun and great power-fantasy moments that keep me coming back.\t\t\t\t\t
