\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a surprisingly great, if particularly grindy game.

In the game, you take the role of a Guardian, a bearer of the Light and protector of humanity. As either a Titan, a Warlock, or a Hunter, you'll complete strike missions for the Vanguard, compete in the Crucible for honor and glory, or battle on the edge of Light and Darkness in Gambit. Playing these modes, alongside exploring the Solar System in general, will grant you loot in order to become more and more powerful. Once you start reaching the heights of power, you'll able to take on more difficult challenges, like Nightfall Strikes, or Raids and Dungeons.

However, the game has a bad habit of drip-feeding you more powerful gear, particularly once you reach a given season's soft cap. This means that in order to get to the game's best content, you'll need to grind, grind, GRIND. This can be a turn-off for some, particularly if you don't enjoy the core gameplay loop.

Overall, if the core gameplay is enjoyable to you, you'll likely be able to push past the grind and enjoy the excellent game hiding beneath all of it. However, if you can't, then I would advise against Destiny 2.\t\t\t\t\t
