\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWitch Queen Re-review time.

Having taken a break from the game at the very end of Shadowkeep due to the onset of Sunsetting I missed all of Beyond Light but decided to come back for the new expansion to see how the game is doing. Sunsetting it self has been sunset so that really helped in pushing me to give the game another go now that my gear doesn't have a use limit and admitably the death of Recluse and Revoker was a nice change.

Now, as for how the game plays now. I am hooked again cause the shooting is still the same great Destiny shooting. The void rework has been amazing so far as a Shield Titan causing huge groups of enemies to explode and buff myself is a great feel. The new Guardian Hive are definitely a mix up in the sandbox but generally feel good to fight, minus the fact that the Lightbearer wizard can heal itself like a warlock.

If you've been looking to either try Destiny or return to it after a break like I did I would recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy second most played game ever and by far one of my favorites. This game has introduced me to amazing people and an amazing community to interact with.

That being said I can't honestly recommend this game in its current state nor the direction it is heading. The decisions the dev team are making are illogical and do not benefit the casual playerbase or the veteran playerbase. Content has gotten fairly repetitive and there is little salvation in sight at this point as we head into another seemingly bare season between raids. The game engine is really showing its age with every update and expansion as more bugs are being released than features.

This game has absolutely fantastic PvE that is really brought down by the lack of any sort of quality of life fixes and an inventory management system that was sufficiently outdated in 2016. The loot drop system has not really matured in any appreciable manner which has left us with effectively the same grind wearing different disguises for years now. This game becomes utterly boring and more of a chore if you are even semi-involved at the launch of a new season. Drip-feeding us content is getting old and Im afraid they aren't going to budge on this just yet. Sunsetting has left us with entire arch-types of weapons being abandoned which is entirely inexcusable and yet another massive oversight by the dev team. Things need to change and they need to change now.

As an avid PvP player I feel as if they've all but totally abandoned us. There has been no new PvP content in ~6 seasons (adept weapons and armor re-skins don't count) and 11 maps were taken out of the game with the sunsetting yet none of them have been replaced. Hacking is still a major issue in competitive play, sword peeking destroys any semblance of risk when it comes to fighting, and stasis has completely shattered (ha puns) what little skill curve there was involving gun and ability play. Don't get me wrong, this game's gunplay feels AMAZING and the gun balancing is finally in a reasonable place, however all of this has been for naught with the complete and utter disregard for all the other issues plaguing PvP. I don't know how much longer the PvP community can take with so many players deciding to move on to other games.

The issues above are not even 5% of everything that is wrong, just some of my highlights. Hopefully the current direction is changed and I can one day change this review in good faith, but until then steer clear and check out literally anything else.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been a follower of the destiny franchise since the dawn of the first game, so I thought of making this review to help new players entering the world of destiny.

First of all, the good. This game has grown with me and made me fall in love with it long ago. The stories, the characters, the combat and the mechanics are absolutely fantastic. But in my opinion, there is something that stands out the most, and really gives the essence of a Bungie game. The Lore. The Destiny universe has some amazing backstories. This is what made me create a strong bond with the characters and their goals. The combat is amazing. With unique weapons, 3 classes and 4 subclasses to choose from, the ways to destroy your enemies become almost limitless. The huge world with interplanetary travel, is something that the game does very well and makes you get very immersed while playing. From the landscapes of ruined Earth, to the cold ice plains of Europa, Destiny does it almost flawlessly.

When the game released, Activision was the publisher. That was well.... Terrible. Activision really only cares about the money that the game will generate, so they kept bestowing microtransactions that would futher damage the already dying player base at the time. Fortunatly, Bungie managed to cut ties with Activision, furthermore allowing them to shape the game to the communities will. That event effectively saved the game.

Now for the flaws. First of all, the game needs attention. In order to reach higher light levels, obtain exotic and legendary gear, you will have to put effort in the game. In other words, you will need to do some grinding. Also, the game might be free, but Bungie keeps releasing new expansions. Don't get me wrong, most of them are fantastic, but they usually cost an arm and a leg to buy. For instance, the newest expansion "Beyond Light" is 40 Euros (Using my currency as reference). Also, for a HUUUGE part of the game (Strikes, Raids, Dungeons) you will rely on other players. There are matchmaking features in the game, but it can be a pain in the ass sometimes, so you will need friends to get the best experience possible (ESPECIALLY raids). Bugs are not very frequent, so you won't have to worry about them. Also, modders are extremely scarce. PVP is also a good experience, but I personally prefer PVE.

Overall, Destiny 2 is an amazing game, with lots of things to discover, kill and admire. I would totally recommend you to try it out, speaking from my 330 hour experience in game.\t\t\t\t\t
