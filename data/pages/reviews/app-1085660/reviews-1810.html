\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough my previous review was pretty positive and I used to want to recommend this game to everyone, I can't recommend it in its current state.

This game has a LOT of microtransactions now. Want to play a decent campaign? Microtransaction. Want decent weapons? Microtransaction. Almost everything worth playing is now microtransactions. Before, we had Red War, Curse of Osirus and Warmind for free. Three good campaigns which were free. All of them are now deleted and replaced with one campaign which can barely be considered a campaign it is that bad. Those three campaigns also used to be paid, and as if making these things paid then free wasn't bad enough, now they are unplayable even for the people that paid for them.

Bungie seemed to think it was a good idea to remove old content to introduce new content. Now I know that may have been the only way to introduce new content, but the stuff you removed was PAID content. Content that people put money into, now just gone so you can introduce lower quality content. With the new DLC coming out, Forsaken will be removed. Forsaken was probably the best campaign Destiny 2 will ever get, I invested 100 bucks into that campaign. Now it is unplayable. Now I can get no more bang for my buck. This fan favourite campaign is going to be deleted along with all the other campaigns. The thing that really pisses me off about this though, the new campaigns that mean the old ones need to be removed was originally going to be a Destiny 3. I would much rather have to buy a whole new game than lose the content I paid for and loved from the older games.

Finally, the whole game turned into too much of a grind. This game quite literally needs to become your life if you want to have any real high power level items. Your just not gonna get to a good enough power level just on a couple hours a week. Even if you do get to a good enough power level to compete in pvp and all that, it is just a toxic mess that you will wish you never took part in.

In conclusion, this game used to be really good. Although it was never as good as D1 in my opinion, D2 was worth playing and extremely fun especially if you had friends. Now, just no. It is not worth your time and most of the things a lot of players loved about this game are being removed. I personally think all the fun to be had from Destiny 2 has already been taken out of it, you can try the game if you want, though do not go in with high hopes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA grindy game without depth. The game bills itself as a "lifestyle" game where you can spend endless hours investing into upgrading your character, but a deeply flawed gear system means you'll have near endgame level gear just from leveling and the only gear to chase is the broken, bugged gear of the week before it's patched. This game is the definition of unrewarding, and all you'll hear from the player base is complaints about how game X is doing it better.

This has been one of the biggest disappointments in gaming, showing the greatest potential from a concept point of view, but one of the worst design executions you'll ever see on such a promise. The only thing going for this game is the lack of competition in the space, and the absolute disasters of competitors such as Anthem that attempted to enter this genre. All in all every single player that has stayed with or eventually left the game comes away with the same impression: There were some great moments where you got to see the potential such as classic raids and a few broken PVP weekends such as laser tag, but other than that you will mostly find yourself frustrated and feeling like this game just isn't worth the time investment.

And lastly, the lack of content and useless gear chasing has only been exasperated by the F2P era decision to sunset gear and activities under the guise that "it's too much for the dev team to handle." This is a development team widely criticized by the industry and press for being the slowest developer to address bugs and add/update content. And it's not just nitpicking - there's been game breaking bugs that are in the game for 6+months at a time.

Couple that with one of the consistently worst PvP experiences in AAA shooters, with incredibly buggy and laggy peer-to-peer hosted multiplayer, perpetually imbalanced weapons made easily available to bug chasers but not the general population, and such a rampantly bad combination of fundamental balance (team shotting) and hacking problems it led to the cancellation of the star PvP mode, there's not even the "log on and play a few rounds" PvP aspect to redeem this game.

Commentary on the game's development course from a player since the original launch of Destiny 1:

Overall this is exactly what you'd expect from a studio that used to be on top, but has had an internal meltdown to where it no longer can function properly. They have the resources and backing to try great things, but they don't have the right people to execute, and their own internal issues have shown that they can't sustain the path they are on. The attitude of the developer of always arguing with the community, telling them they're wrong or why they should like something they don't, just shows the hubris and disconnection from the player base can't be reconciled.

This game will always be there - between the financial backing from extremely large investment institutions and their previous relationship with Activision - the game is in no danger of dying. And that's exactly the problem. There will never be an impetus for change, and Bungie will never have to face reality that their decisions aren't working for the player base. And they won't have to. The funding will continue, F2P means they'll be able to show an active player base, and they will continue the course.

So the decision comes down to you, if you're reading a review. If you made it this far you either want genuine player thoughts, or you just are looking at player feedback with some other goal in mind, in which this isn't for you.

You will always see this game popping up, news stories about it, and well polished CG trailers trying to suck you in. It's a vapid, empty game, and it's not worth your time. It never will be either.\t\t\t\t\t
