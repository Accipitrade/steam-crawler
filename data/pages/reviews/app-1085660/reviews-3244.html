\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough it started rough with vanilla Destiny 2, the game is actually in a pretty good spot now, and well worth checking out, especially since the first chunk of the game is free.
So, straight up, the free stuff. What are you getting for free? You get what paying players got for the first year of Destiny 2 - three story campaigns, all of the strikes (dungeons), PVP and other activities loaded on top. You can visit every open area of the game - even the newest ones - but the most up-to-date content, such as campaigns, side missions, the newest raids, and newest end-game activities, are still locked away behind the expansion pack paywalls. This is some 60 hours of pretty good content that used to be sold as a AAA game. It won't allow you to get the newest gear, or conquer the newest raids, but it will let you see if Destiny 2 is something you enjoy and want to spend money on. I think it's a good compromise.

With that out of the way, what kind of game is Destiny 2? This isn't Borderlands, and it's not Diablo III. Destiny 2 is very much an MMO with guns. That means lots of grinding - lots of repeating the same activities, hoping for item drops, over and over, in an online only environment - but on a much longer time scale. Luckily, this is from the creators of Halo, so the gunplay is absolutely rock solid, and always a joy to play. With a clan, or tight group to play with, Destiny 2 can easily become your hobby, because of the depth of what it offers. It's just a really, really good shooter, with MMO-style gear.
It's also worth noting that the story isn't great, and never approaches Halo's campaigns. The writing - especially for the first three campaigns - is actually pretty bad, and the lore around the game can be hard to get into. Destiny 2 is gameplay-centric, everything else is secondary, and it shows in its story.
Lastly, the music, sound effects, and graphics are absolutely top tier stuff. There really aren't any free games that could hope to compare to what's on offer here. You'll often find yourself looking around the game world to drink in the best sky boxes in the business, or appreciate the fine details on offer. The music sells it all, and it can really feel like quite a special experience when it all comes together to produce some of the best audio-visual presentation around.
An easy recommendation. Jump in, see if it's your bag.\t\t\t\t\t
