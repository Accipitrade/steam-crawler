Update 2: This game still sucks
Update 3 || Witch Queen Initial Review: Best expansion this franchise has ever had. Waiting to see how the raid and post raid content stack up
Update 4: This game sucks

TLDR:
Game has obvious flaws, but the flaws are heavily outweighed by the rest of the game. Fantastic game, and I recommend it heavily. Score ratings for game and DLC below review. Also, there will be obvious grammar and spelling errors inside of this review. I am not smart, I just want to give my opinions on this game. :)

PROLOGUE:

First off, I have been a Destiny player since launch of Destiny 1. I have played the game through its highs, and talked ♥♥♥♥ about the game during its lows. Destiny 2 has been no exception, with the majority of my opinions on this game being negative. However, Beyond Light has rekindled the flame in my soul for this franchise. After the rough year we had with Shadowkeep, BL revitalized the format, using the best of the past and eliminating most of the negatives. There are still some very present negatives, such as no split sandbox, hackers, seasonal activities being vaulted, and so on. These negatives are outweighed by the positives.

STORY:
The lore has always been a massive problem for me. Destiny always seemed to open new doors and never close them. Who is the darkness? Why did the girl say she had no time to explain? What is an ahamkara? These were all questions that I had, and they were all eventually answered, but it took years. You could go and read the grimoire on destiny 1 to find out, but these aspects were part of the story, and not everyone wanted to analyze lore tabs like they were an English major. I am really enjoying how they are handling the lore now though. It is in the game, we see all our questions being answered in real time, and it is fantastic.

GAMEPLAY:
Destiny has always kept me around because of the gunplay. There is no equal in terms of how the game feels to play. It is just magical. Content wise, the game struggles. There is a seasonal system set in stone where content gets cycled in and out, with some of the new content just being reskins. I usually have no problem with this, but the reskinned content is expected to last months, when it only really lasts a week. For hardcore grinders, this becomes a problem and can lead to burnout. They try to keep it fresh through the use of timegated content being unlocked, which I actually prefer.

PVP:
This used to be my main attraction to the game series. I used to run carries back on the PS4 in 2015-2017. This changed when D2 came out, as the gameplay was completely redefined, and then redefined again and again. The sandbox was constantly shifting and the mechanics of the game were constantly shifting. I struggled to find my stance in this gamemode, and still do. Overall the PVP can be a very fun experience, but this is one of the most competitive shooters that I have seen. You don't hear about it really at all, but the amount of work people put into grinding this game to become the best they can possibly be is very fun to see, and many people put in this effort. However, when you constantly match these people and get dunked on, it can be annoying. Plus the introduction of the new STASIS subclass has really stretched my willingness to play. Overall a plus.

PVE:
♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ FANTASTIC. There isn't much to do, but this is a prime example of quality over quantity. The raids are masterpieces for the most part. These 6 man activities force groups to work together, improvise, and overcome some of the most challenging content in the game. The feeling of throwing yourself at a bossfight for 5 hours and finally beating it is a feeling that is magical. The only problem I have is the lack of difficulty in normal gameplay. You either get one shot, or you mow down bosses, with no inbetween. Most bosses are bulletsponges, which are usually melted anyways, and interesting mechanics are somewhat void.

GRAPHICS:
Theres not a lot to say here. It has it's moments to shine, and when it does, it really blows you away.

MUSIC:
This game has one of my favorite OSTs of all time. The music is constantly getting better too!

OVERALL VERDICT:
Base game (no dlc): 7/10
Forsaken: 9/10
Shadowkeep: 6/10
Beyond Light: 8/10
Full game with dlc: 8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs Beyond Light good?
The answer is yes. Most of the negative reviews seem to be based on week one experience, and it's obvious that week one experience is horrible. There was barely any content in the game back then, but now? I don't know about those butthurt people, but I'm having more fun than ever. I don't like the stasis subclasses since they don't provide any form of support for the team, but for a selfish playstyle it's better than good. After the release of Deep Stone Crypt, there's been a lot of stuff to do, but if you're playing 12 hours every day, then you'll eventually run out of things to do. For those who like to do raids, DSC guns are sick. They look cool, they're fun to shoot, and they're OP with the new perks.

P.S. who needs a long campaign!? If you're playing Destiny for its campaign, you're in the wrong game. If the campaign were any longer, I wouldn't play on my other characters at all because doing the campaign three times would burn me out. Grinding the stasis class perks is already a pain as it is.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
-PvE content-rich, not just some slap-dash story to justify PvP arenas/setting
-Content-rich story line with deep lore, enriched by (but not requiring) Destiny 1 experience
-Highly-customizable weapons/armor/class variations allow for truly personalized builds to suit any playstyle
-Tremendously satisfying character progression system encourages players to try to get *just* a little better, without completely decimating casual/occasional play
-Gun play is consistently satisfying, with each of the hundreds of guns (and their various sub-rolls) delivering a satisfying and unique feel (as is to be expected from Bungie)
-Multiple levels of challenge in all types of game play (PvP, PvE, PvEvP) provide something for everyone
-Hundreds upon thousands of collectibles, achievements, and rewards to feed that completionist side
-F2P path a fantastic introductory path with PLENTY of content available, seemingly all premium content either cosmetic, or expansion-specific (eg, no pay-to-win options)
-premium cosmetics also available through F2P currency (will require a lot of grinding, however the rates are achievable, the store is filled with options to the point of nearly discouraging FOMO, and save for limited time holiday events i've yet to feel like shelling out was necessary-- and that was just because i love me some halloween items)
-*Quite possibly the most welcoming, supportive, and toxicity-averse communities to date*

Cons:
-New player experience has been REMARKABLY improved, but some casual players may find themselves overwhelmed if jumping into PvP content too early; a living game like this may leave players feeling like they "missed out" by not being here day 1 (in my experience, catching up takes effort but is still very much possible; i no longer feel like there's anything i want that i havent completed within 1-2 seasons worth of play).
-Premium Currency strategically structured to always somehow be *just shy* of what you hoped to get
-Some content requires 3-6 teammates while lacking matchmaking, especially end-game content; game definitely loses its luster for the adamantly solo casual player.
-F2P+PvP=cheaters/hackers; Bungie has been working on various solutions, but as with all games with this combo, there is no perfect solution, and highest-competitive game modes (eg, trials) will likely continue to suffer for some time.

I came into Destiny 2with a lot of trepidation; while i had picked the game up at launch on other platforms, i hadn't touched it at all until about a year ago, and worried i would either be too far behind veteran players, too overwhelmed by the years of catch-up, or worse still, too put out by what i expected would be the usual toxicity seemingly inherent to the FPS MMO genre.

Instead, not only was I able to find community and support from new and veteran players alike, i found myself coming back over and over again to play with folks who weren't only "permissive" of my newb status, but *excited* to help bring me up to speed, to teach me about secrets, mechanics, hidden lore items, etc. As an average-or-worse FPS player, i thought i'd be sweating to keep up, but found that nothing was too out of reach to not at least try out, and when i failed, teammates (incl. randomly match-made folks) would patiently and understandinly explain what i needed to do. To be as clear as possible: this is a game where local chat is more often SAFE than not! :O

Fans of sci-fi games will depply enjoy the narratives and storylines involved, all of which center around themes of morality, mortality, and the value of prioritizing human life in a world where humans are no longer the apex predator. Time travel paradoxes, multiple universes, and the challenges of maintaining a "good" morality in a world painted with many, many shades of grey promise to have even the most jaded player asking "what happens next?"

But for those who couldn't care less about lore and just wanna shoot things, there's plenty of opportunity for that as well. PvE content includes story campaigns, open-world exploration areas, and end-game level 6-player raid challenges; PvEvP pits players against each other in a bid to defeat their Gambit bosses before the other team defeats theirs (with some cross-team fire upping the stakes, of course); PvP ranges the gamut from 1v1^12 rumble, to 3v3 elimination, 6v6 capture the flag, and much, much more, all with a diverse set of players providing competition for the most "how do i reload" players, to the "360 noscope on dk bongos" sweatlords, and everyone in-between.

Even if you are dead-set on not paying a penny for anything in the game, there's still PLENTY to do, both solo and with friends. Explaining the magic of this game is something many folks have tried and failed to do before, but if youtube videos and steam reviews aren't convicing, i sincerely recommend giving it a shot on the F2P track; you'll miss out on some of the best story content, but you'll get a great taste of what's down the road thanks to the newly-updated New Player Experience. If it doesn't work out, there's no loss beyond whatever time you give it; if you find yourself wanting more, you can rest assured knowing every cent is well-spent.

Hope to see you in game soon!\t\t\t\t\t
