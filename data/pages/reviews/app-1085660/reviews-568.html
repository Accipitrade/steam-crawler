\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow that I have read the comments for this game, I think it is a time to give the perspective of a person who's mom works for Bungie (Me). My background is im 15, I've been playing this series since beta of Destiny 1, and i have over 3k hours collectively between Destiny 1 and Destiny 2. I think with this I can give the game a fair review with these attributes alone so lets get into it. First lets start with the story, you know 1/3 of the game and its content.
The story after reviewing and refreshing my memory between the base game, Curse of Osiris, Warmind, Forsaken, Shadowkeep, and Beyond Light, is sub par at best. I think we can all agree the most fun things inside of the story part of the game is the start and the end. That's when the stakes are the highest, the climax is kicking in and honestly if you don't know about video game cliches it can be genuinely exciting. But theirs no middle, everything inside of the campaigns is all about the beginning and the end and I think that's the saddest part. The middle in most story's can be the most exciting part of the game, the journey, the adventure, the road to victory, that is what destiny needs. There is no growing of characters no learning new things, no actually experiencing a alive and breathing world. Nothing happens around you, you make things happen that seem to have no effect on the game world at all. This is not Bungie's fault however, because this is a MMO. I would just like something that people can grasp on too and say "Hey i did that i had a change on the world around me without having everyone else's character do the same thing in a cut-scene". Next is the mission design ill make this brief. Its BORING in bold, most of the story missions in Destiny 2 can be boiled down to running somewhere or riding on your hover bike to some disclosed location and scanning a thing, fending off hoards of enemies, or in rare occasion standing on a plate or placing in something somewhere. And just to spice it up sometimes they put two or three of these things together at the same time, which just makes it tedious. I know they can't make a new mission structure for every mission that they have but they could add som variety to the 5 game play loops that they have recycling since Destiny 1 seven years later.
Next is the Raids, Nightfall's, and the Dungeons, ie the end game content (I know trials counts as end game content but ill put it in the PVP section). I swear this is where all of the work goes into game play side of the game. I cannot explain to you how much fun some of these things are when you do it for the first time all of the puzzles, Ai querks, and overall the atmosphere is outstanding. This is where i think the game really shines in my opinion. But there is only one problem, after you do it once their is no challenge anymore besides that 1 kid that says he can run relic with 0 clears on his record. But jokes aside, playing raids after you experience them once or become higher power than the recommended light becomes a cake walk. Nothing changes and it becomes predictable (Not Including Challenge Modes), then the only reason why you are playing the raid in the end is to get the loot drop or the rare exotic your friend gets first try and he did nothing the entire raid. Next is Nightfall's, one of the best sources of loot in the game by far and the best part is, you can farm it forever. I have nothing really bad to say about these because this is the epitome of destiny shooting aliens in a moderate fashion while repeating a strike over and over to get loot. Lastly in the PvE section is Dungeons, I feel like these are very fun and they hold up pretty well considering raids are a thing. They are like longer strikes with a bit of different mechanics dipped into the mix just like a raid. In my opinion i think this is what strikes should have been and I think a lot of people can agree with me on this opinion.
Last but not least is PvP, where do I even begin. First Im a PvP main, I was top 500 in rumble last season, so i would think i have a good place to stand to talk about this certain subject. I'm just going to flat out say that the crucible is unbalanced and unplayable at its worst and absolutely amazing at its best, much like Destiny 2 at a whole. The problem is the unbalanced part of PvP is at a ratio of 80:20 comparative to the balanced part, which is not good. Ive been saying it for years and other people have been saying it for years "Bungie dont you think you are trying to cater to too many markets at the same time" and you know what Bungie did? Spit in our face sit us down with 8 season passes, A 60 dollar game with 5 expansions all equating too 175$ in end price and said "Your going to play our 235$ game and your gonna like it". And with this, and all of the content you get a game where Bungie swears is competitive but all you see in PvP is shatter diving hunters and Multimachs. All and all in this section, this game shouldent have PvP in it, at least in its current state with all of the guns and Ability's that have no balancing for any other part of the game besides PvE.
Now is the part where I offer solutions to the problems that I explained and talk about the game as a whole and conclude my argument with a few final words i have to say. First the story missions i would just take more time to find more engaging game play loops to engage the player into the missions without thinking its a chore. With the Raids I would just say the system is flawed with hype and release dates so i don't think this can get fixed with the current status of the game. PvP is also flawed with Bungie trying to cater to too many markets making it impossible to make the guns viable in PvE while making them unplayable in PvP or vice versa. Lastly is price tags and free to play. I don't think that this game is meant to cost this much in any respect, however i do understand that people at Bungie need to get paid and that's why eververse has the best menu in the game in retrospect to any other menu in the game. Free to play players also get no content at all getting the most bare bones start to any video game ever, getting bombarded with hundreds of menus when they get into orbit for the first time reminding them what their missing out on if they don't pay at least 100$ to get on the actual fun of the game. And that's not even the worst of it, they cant even compete in end game activity's with the loadouts their given and even PvP not even shedding a light on that having to be 1320 power level to even stand a chance in Trials of Osiris. Their are almost no options for these player's but to wait for Bungie to make these expansions free to play or pay for them.
Overall this game is a perfect example of a over complicated cash grab designed to squeeze every penny out of you while you wait for overhyped DLC's to drop with 3 missions and a Raid to follow every year. And yet i still play it everyday after school, on the weekends, during breaks, any time i get i play this game. And you want to know why its hope, hope that the game will change. And with that sliver of hope still inside me ill leave all of you with this as long as you enjoy yourself is it really a waste of time because I dont think so. Thank you to those who read and those who even saw the post I really appreciate it :).

Pros
- Great time with your friends
- Awesome time killer
- Very nice gun play
- Great place to meet new people
- Art
- Free to Play (In some respects)

Cons
- Pricetag
- FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)
- Requiring Good Hardware
- Lackluster Story
- Unbalanced PvP
- No Matter How Long You Grind it Feels Pointless in the end

6/10\t\t\t\t\t
