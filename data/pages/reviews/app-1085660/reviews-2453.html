\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe closest I can come to describing this game succinctly is "fake depth". The lore is long winded in order to seem important. The gameplay is repetitive. The enemies all fight similarly, and in many campaigns you can fight the same enemies in the same maps, giving the races none of the distinct attributes the races in other similar games (like halo) have had. I played 2.5 campaigns in the game and probably spent about 5 hours outside of the game reading up on the universe and lore and eventually came to the realization the game creators cared more about how the game looks rather than what you were doing or why you were doing it. The difficulty isn't real difficulty, all bosses in the game instead have "immortality phases" they go through where you can't damage them and have to fight waves of lesser enemies. The end boss for the Osiris campaign is basically three button press prompts after fighting passive enemies you can snipe from afar. The AI in this game would often stand around and do nothing until I directly attacked them, and the quest system is needlessly complex. I would grind for hours to get a weapon that then needs hours of grinding to upgrade only to find that its not even an interesting weapon to use. I eventually resorted to running through strikes in this game without damaging anything until the boss, just because its funny simply running past all enemy encounters and having no consequences. Most enemies drop no loot so theres very little reason to fight anything other than the end bosses. I could go on, but honestly I've played games in beta that have more actual depth than this skin deep boring shooter. Stay away.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game. It's getting me through the COVID-19 crisis. But if you've never played this game or the original, be warned. It's fun. It's got six-player hours-long raids, a high-stakes weekly PvP event, and deep fascinating lore. But here's what you need to know:
  • It's extremely grindy. It takes a lot of time sunken into it to be rewarding. The reason it's my most played game on Steam isn't that I enjoy playing it more than other games, but that it demands that I play it more than other games. Be patient, and don't expect to be too powerful at all for the first one hundred hours.
  • This content is quite limited without expansions and premium season passes. New content is delivered through one expansion and four seasons a year. Expansions and seasons are very hit-or-miss.
  • It's a co-op game, and not all activities have matchmaking, so you'll need to either have friends, join an LFG Discord server (I recommend "Destiny 2 PC LFG"), or join an in-game clan.
It's not a game for everybody, but I love it and you might too.\t\t\t\t\t
