The game really impressed me from the beginning.

Pros:
- Very smooth controls
- Fast-paced action - you barely have time to breath when the action gets hot
- Very nice graphics, especially for an MMORPG
- Awesome setting on planets and moons of our solar system
- Really varied places and enemies and even the quests I played so far are not *that* monotonic

Cons:
- Long loading times, especially when starting the game
- I often feel lost, because I don't understand the waypoints or don't know where to go next, which quest to choose next, etc.
- Bungie prevents game-overlays like Discord or FPS-counter\t\t\t\t\t

At first Destiny 2 failed to grip me, with its new light update changing the beginning of the game quite a bit from the very short time I played on battle.net. Many things confused me, such as where to go, how to manage your inventory and the overall economy of the game. However, after hopping on some raids with from friends, I started to see why the game had so much appeal.

This didn't immediately win me over though, as I still didn't know how to really play the game by myself. That was until I started playing crucible, doing strikes, and just sinking hours in to figure things out. And after that, I was hooked, and still am.

For new players, I would suggest you talk to the lady in the hanger (forgot her name) but right off the bat she has 3 campaigns that, in doing them, will help you figure out a lot of things about the game naturally just by playing. Those campaigns also will typically get you from level 750 to about 900. Now I don't know if this is actually how the game works, but I've noticed that the higher your light level, the more often better gear has a chance to drop. I am not gonna stand by that statement, because perhaps I simply just don't notice all of the gear I dismantle anymore.

Now is this the most engaging game that I've ever played? I mean, it varies. Somethings you can do in the game are more complex than others, while some things can be kinda boring, and involve a lot of roaming around. Honestly, with as unbalanced at times as it is, I enjoy gambit and crucible the most. They provide a pretty unique arena shooter experience, and the time to kill is satisfying. There's also quite a lot of strategy that can go into these modes, especially gambit. That being said, other than raids and survival, this is one of those games that you can kind of chill out to while playing, it's not going to expect all of your attention at once at all times.

I'm sure that someone who would write a review of the game would touch on a lot of the imbalance in the game, and other issues that may impact your experience. The reason I chose not to go into too much detail about these things is because they show up rather late into the experience, and primarily become a problem after you notice them. So I'll just quickly list off somethings that are there that do take away from the experience at times.

Class imbalance: The classes all have a bit of imbalance between them with hunters right now feeling a bit underwhelming, Titans feeling like they are in a good spot, and warlocks having good utility but not at all times. There has been talks of the classes getting changes to make each role more defined, so that's good to hear. But overall, they don't really feel like this defined "Knight, Mage, Assassin" RPG class design.

Weapon imbalance: Guns in this game are hard to balance, what with all the crazy abilities they can have and different ways they interact with PvE and PvP. This causes the meta to be a bit cheesy at times, and for some lobbies to devolve into a repeat of similar guns every time. This doesn't stop at guns though as exotic armor can have similar abilities and synergies can be made which can create a dangerous kit.

Boring missions: Not too much to say but some missions and other things you can do, can be seriously boring. There's just some missions that devolve into one area, not moving around much, with enemies pouring in, none of them being too dangerous, and it becoming just an absolute shooting gallery. There aren't a ton of these but you'll run into them.

In conclusion, this is a fun, fine, and meaty game. There's a lot to do, so much so where I feel like I am still finding out new stuff to do that engages me in spending more time in it, and I have a feeling this game will scratch a lot of itches of mine for a long while. While it isn't the absolute greatest thing I have ever played, it's something you can sink your teeth into and enjoy for a long while. Honestly I'm left pretty satisfied, and if any of this sounds like something you'd enjoy as well, I implore you to give it a try.\t\t\t\t\t
