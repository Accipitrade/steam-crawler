\r\n\t\t\t\t\tdestiny 2 is a one of a kind shooter which has a unique feel and game play, as well as very systematic flaws. The first major flaw with Destiny 2 is the new player experience. Many new players find themselves lost and without directions upon completing the introductory quests, which can be a put off for many new players. And the grind to catch up to the average player is also tedious and a lengthy time commitment. Another major flaw is the topic of balancing weapons and abilities. In recent years Bungie has made many exotics(unique an powerful weapons/armor which you can only equip one of (armor and weapon)) negligible by introducing perks on legendary weapons(second to exotics more common and accessible) that make unique perks exclusive to these items no longer unique. This poses several issues the primary issue being the relevancy of said exotic weapons. If a player can get an almost identical more accessible legendary weapon that allows them to use another exotic, why would the player bother with that exotic. Bungie also has a bad track record of severely "nerfing" certain weapons or abilities only to make them unusable and then not balancing these weapons or abilities for them be useful until a later date or in some cases not at all. Another problem is concerning the "free to play" experience. The term "free to play" is used very loosely in this context as you are severely limited to what content you can play, as well as there being restrictions in place on your free to play account such as dealing less and taking more damage, as well taking longer to level to the higher power levels. The topic of the free to play experience brings me into my next topic of discussion of the cost of destiny. The total cost of all the DLCs in Destiny 2 to be able to access all of the content in the game currently totals to roughly $210(USD). This price is excluding the seasonal passes which come to around $15(USD) with 4 seasonal passes a year. This is a large sum of money to commit to a game. You can pick and choose which content you purchase but you will not be able to acquire the weapons, armor, and exotics from the excluded DLCs. The game does have it pros, most of them being the mutiplayer experience. Destiny 2 is a very community based game which is very welcoming in most cases. It is also a fun game to play with friends, but that could be said about anything. Overall I would not recommend this game to any potential buyers.
