\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 very much lives in the shadow of it's predecessor.

If you played D1 and you're looking to play D2 as an improvement to the original, you will be disappointed. Vanilla D2 feels very much like DLC for D1, and only made improvements on this fact after 2 expansions (warmind and curse of osiris) with Forsaken. The majority of D2 players agree that Forsaken should have been the game state released as vanilla. This expansion introduced numerous new systems and added a lot of non-repetitive content. As far as repetitive content goes in D2, it wouldn't be an MMORPG without repetition, so expect to see repeated quests and activities as you approach and enter the late game. Most of what you do at this point is search for perfectly rolled gear in weekly activities.

Where a lot of players find fault in D2 is in the attitude of the developers. They seem to be burnt out on content. The introduction of a "seasons" system brought some of the most dumbed down, repetitive, and boring gameplay we've seen in years. The battle pass didn't help either, but rather made the playerbase lose respect for Bungie.

The bottom line is this: Destiny 2 will afford you a good amount of solid content, but only if you have the expansions - namely Forsaken. Forsaken is D2's Taken King (the expansion that "saved" D1). It is very easy to get burnt out on the gameplay after you reach light level soft caps and finish the expansion stories.

If you played D1 you should probably play D2, but don't expect polish even close to what we saw in the Taken King.

I give this game a 5/10\t\t\t\t\t
