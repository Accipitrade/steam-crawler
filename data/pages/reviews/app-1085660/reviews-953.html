\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've put a lot of time into this game and have had a great time overall. The abilities, weapons, story/lore, and dungeons/raids/Witch Queen campaign are phenomenal. i play endgame PVE and PVP at this point, and help new people learn raids and dungeons when I can. Destiny 2 is, at heart, a great game.

The great gameplay is at times overshadowed by poor game design choices, but usually these mistakes are fixed. There are definitely bugs, but most are inconvenient and not game breaking in nature. The game can be enjoyed solo or with friends, but much of the endgame requires groups of 3-6 players. in all honesty, the game is best enjoyed with friends; I firmly believe that the friendgame is the endgame in all MMOs. NOTE - the *real* game is not free to play. The F2P experience is essentially a demo, with access to the free multiplayer and a small subset of PVE activities. The monetization of the game is poor; there are cosmetic microtransactions, which is fine; however the inclusion of smaller content packs that unlock access to overpowered gear not accessible without paying is lame.

Despite this, I still wholeheartedly recommend the game; IF you're going to buy it, start with Witch Queen. It has a real campaign, a good story, great gameplay and good loot. I recommend MyNameIsByf on Youtube to get up to speed on the story. I'e had more late nights crushing raid bosses and beating dungeons in D2 than any other game at this point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti like this game, and enjoy it, so this is kinda a weird review. Something about the mechanics of the combat of the game really draws me in. I love the variety of weapons, both stylistically and also the different 'feel' of the guns. In that regard, this is an absolutely top notch looter-shooter. However, the game is not just held back, it's outright stalled by a few really fatal elements:

The story is, to put it mildly, weak as ♥♥♥♥. You're the good guys, just because, and everyone else is Bad. You have this mystical 'light' on your side, the enemies are in service to an equally mystical 'dark', both of them rooted in technology, which would be a kinda cool premise if it were delved into, like, at all. As it is, light comes from a giant mechanical ball, and dark comes from a horde of mechanical triangles. That's all we, the playerbase, need to be told, i guess.

The gear treadmill system is possibly the worst implemented i can imagine. Maybe i'm just being unimaginative, but it feels like the devs actively set out to sabotage their aforementioned * good* combat system. Or else this is the worst case of "the right hand not knowing what the left is doing" that i've ever come across. You have a system of guns which drop with random properties, with each different model of gun having a different 'feel' to it as well as somewhat-unique stats and some random properties which can combine to make a weapon incredible or junk. Fairly standard stuff. But the game drops these weapons at-or-slightly-above your 'gear level', and the uniqueness of each weapon means that when you finally have a gun you like, oh, um, another weapon dropped that's a few points higher, and if you keep using your favourite gun, you don't progress any further up the gear treadmill!
On top of that, higher 'treadmill' level doesn't translate into better performance, it's purely a stat-check for the highest level content (whatever that is at this precise moment). If you fall short of that check, you do less damage and take more damage from enemies that are arbitrarily flagged as being higher level, but - importantly - *you don't deal more damage to lower level enemies* if you exceed their level, no matter how significantly. This creates a system where actually progressing through the game feels horrible and unrewarding, forcing you to not only play suboptimally but not even being compensated for doing so with increasing character power.
There are ways to level up lower level weapons, but the 'resource' to do this isn't given out in incredible quantities, and recently the devs even put in a system which arbitrarily caps how high a weapon can be improved this way. Additionally, when a season ends, it seems like all your equipment gets bumped up to the new minimum power level - which was the old maximum you were striving oh-so-hard towards - so the most efficient way to advance in the game is literally not to play it.\t\t\t\t\t
