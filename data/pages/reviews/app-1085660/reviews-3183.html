I've had a love/hate relationship with Destiny
The hate primarily stems from the pricing of their expansions.. The game kinda entices you to purchase their (Bi-?)annual expansions which typically cost around $40. Not owning the expansions will also lock you out of earning some great META defining weapons/gear. (Although their business model might change now that Activision is out of the picture.) In a perfect worlds the gameplay content would be free with a focus on monetizing cosmetics instead. Right now many players can't enjoy what the game has to offer due to being locked behind a paywall. If paying around $70-$80 a year doesn't sound ideal then this game may not be for you.

With that main CON out of the way I'll now briefly list some PROs that come to mind. I can honestly say that Destiny is one of my all time favourite franchise due to the:
Super smooth gunplay (Bungie DuH). PvE, PvP, AND now PvEvP game modes. Hundreds of quests to work towards. Seemingly endless amount of loot. Vast locations and lore to explore. Beautiful and colourful settings. Has great optimization and one of the friendliest communities.

I put in hundreds of hours into this game on PS4 (Played since the original D1 Beta) and got reintroduced to Destiny with the implementation of CROSSSAVE which let me transfer my original progress over to PC. Feels like a whole new experience! Uncapped framerates. FOV. M&K. Eye candy graphics! <3 Great to know I can seamlessly play with the homies over on PS4, XBONE, or PC without feeling like my grind/progress isn't being rewarded.

Now they just NEED to add CROSSPLAY and the game will be closer to perfect. Player base/matchmaking will boom! Would be amazing to go through RAIDS and Story content together with all my friends (and for PvP maybe go the route of Fortnite/CoD which matches based on input device?). Lots of games are adding crossplay and I really hope Bungie follows suit with the trend..

The game is F2P with dozens of hours worth of free content to play around with. Give it a try, there's nothing to lose! I suggest playing through the free Campaigns at level 1 with up to two other friends to get a feel for the story and progression.
WARNING:
The game currently boosts your level to 750 which may make you a bit overpowered and you may get lost with where to go and what to do. The 3 main legacy campaigns are VERY easy to overlook. You can jump into endgame or go through the campaign with a couple friends. The choice is yours on where you'd like to begin.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing Destiny since The Dark Below and have to take a pause before writing this review. This franchise had a very poor launch, arguably one of the worst in AAA history. Despite the drawbacks I decided to give Destiny a shot. I loved the Halo franchise and though that Bungie would turn around. I quickly realized that the old Bungie died when Destiny was born.

With each expansion that came through more of the underbelly issues started erupting. Once TTK came out the real issues of the franchise, and Bungie, came to light. I left the franchise and told myself I wouldn't come back until the game was free. When D2 became free on PC when Forsaken I jumped back in the franchise. Playing through Forsaken felt like a momento to the old Bungie, a message that screamed "We made a mistake, want to put the past behind us, and to move forward."

I believe that Forsaken was an apology to D1 vets who got burned by the Taken King launch fiasco. I decided to hunker down and see if Bungie would turn a leaf and start making better decisions. When the split between Bungie and Activision happened it did help free up Bungie from deadlines. I know all of the issues with the franchise is not on Activision, especially when it came to game design. Bungie has slowly been making a change for the better, and for now I will wait and see what the future of Destiny holds.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayer since Beta, I have to say that this game has come a very long way. It is the most beautiful, visually wise, games I have ever played. It has the most satisfying gunplay of any game I've ever played. From earning your weapon drop and searching for the perfect roll, to setting personal milestones in PvP to test your weapons and skill against other players to earn very powerful rewards and some of the best weapons in the game. Armor 2.0 is new to Shadowkeep and it looks like they have improved upon that system with an entirely different endgame.
The PvE content is great and challenging now, the PvP is rewarding and challenging, as always. The game has its issues, but it is in a constant state of balance and Bungie has shown that they are committed to making the game that the players want to play. 9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
