\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThing is, Destiny 2 has the motion to be a great game with it's many activities: Gambit is a real solid gametype. Crucible provides (generally) an even challenge for everyone (barring Iron Banner, which is Crucible Control with it's own powerplay mechanic and power differences). PvE is straight up somewhat fun to go ahead and deal with.

So, you might ask? Why am I not recommending this game?

Having sunk over 700 hours in this game, Beyond Light was the nail in the coffin for me, particularly because parts of the game that was paid-for content? It is being vaulted, namely Mars and Mercury (Warmind and Osiris DLC respectively). This controversial move is said by the devs to be for saving up the game's disk space. It's no secret that most games we play now are total data resource hogs, for example Activsion's relaunched Modern Warfare game or Middle-Earth: Shadow of War (both being about 100+ GB in size). Furthermore, their cure for this instead of implementing something like 343i did with Halo MCC in the manner of having the choice to download the expansions (but still, probably having to make it so you at least have the weapons and armor downloaded for other players to properly show gear) - is to have you reinstall the entire game when this new expansion drops. Personally, I believe this vaulting is more or less a means to try and get people to buy their expansions, because when this vaulting happens, over half of the destinations that F2P players get are removed.

This means that most of the guns that I had earned in my travels will likewise be vaulted and therefore, hours of work I had put in to obtaining armor or that perfect weapon roll will now be gone. This implies that the developers really don't have a care to that godroll you had achieved in Year 2. But it is as I say it is - only an implication.

But enough about my personal bias:
Tl;dr - and other bits if you want me to get to the meat of the matter.

The Good
The game still remains a fun thing to play with friends. I have fond memories of tearing up Gambit Prime with my friends. I was the team's Collector, and I employed vicious use of Malfeasance and Python, both Gambit weapons that remain my favorites to use.
Numerous guns with numerous roles. While not so much mindbogglingly different in numeration as Borderlands, the perks on a rolled weapon can make it assume a role. Just expect to trash a lot of rolls.
Many ways to play this game offer many ways to cater to different players and playstyles. PvPvE for Gambit, PvE for most of the game, PvP for Crucible.
An expansive story with a lot of lore. A fully built world from the ground up.

The Bad
First and foremost, as I mentioned before, the progress you have may or may not matter depending on if the armor you are wearing and the weapons you are using are vaulted content. Some of this content was content that people have previously paid for.
Post Beyond Light, the only area a Free-to-play can access will be Earth. The Tangled Shore and the Moon are DLC areas (Forsaken and Shadowkeep respectively), and I don't have to explain Europa. This is because Mars, Mercury, Titan, Nessus, and Io are all being vaulted.
Because of this vault, this means a player may not be able to not only play through strikes from said areas, but also this means that they cannot play through the first three storylines of the original Destiny 2, as well as the Warmind and Osiris DLC. For a game priding itself on lore - removing it for saving up on 'data space' when data storage drives (Internal and External) are easy to get your mitts on.
The PvP modes (Gambit, Iron Banner, Crucible) usually have a frustrating meta, some of which being for directive rewards that are a major timesink to get. Not only the weapons are occasionally busted (such as Revoker, which can refund a bullet you've missed.) The "meta" is a common thing. And once everyone knows that meta, you're nearly guaranteed to bump into a player using it once a match. Before it got nerfed to ground, Handheld Supernova was a vicious one-shot grenade that players can just 180 and dust you when you think you have the drop on them.
Speaking of timesinks - Exotics as well as said directive weapons are a massive grindfest. Exotics themselves will sometimes have 'Catalysts' which you need to find. Then insert, only to grind some more - typically either by doing the mechanic the Exotic has (Looking at you, Sunshot), or by killing five hundred enemies with the gun.
If you want some directive weapons or exotics. Be prepared to have to play PvP eventually, or PvE. Whether you like it or not. Some of these Exotics can be headscratchingly frustrating, as during one of the steps for a certain Exotic named Lumina: is to immediately "deny" an Invader. What the game doesn't tell you is you need to get a Gambit medal where you kill the Invader within ten seconds of them entering WITHOUT them killing any of your teammates
Some interesting game modes, such as Menagerie and Black Armory are being axed because of this vaulting process. Both are PvE modes but with a twist.
Furthermore, some raids will also be going away because of this.
One subtree within each and every element tree of a class will be paywalled (the middle subtree), unless you shell enough money to get Forsaken or Shadowkeep (and likely Beyond Light). With the release of Beyond Light, a whole new element may be locked behind the expansion.

It's to this end I feel the game has lost it's meaning. I personally would say you're better off investing your time in other games that will reward you for your time. As much as I have enjoyed this game, the fact that some of my progress is being wiped as well as the game removing paid content, as well as removing free-to-play content is something that rubs me the wrong way.. It sort of reminds me of Wizard101

So all in all, it's a game that does initially reward you for taking the time to play it. Just don't expect all your achievements to be one of permanence - safe for your Triumph score - and select weapons that were likely a grindfest.\t\t\t\t\t
