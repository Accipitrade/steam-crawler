\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first bought this game when it was still on the Battlenet launcher around Season of Opulence and at first, I was pretty wishy-washy about it. I did part of the Red War campaign, played a bit of Crucible, and played a few strikes with my friends before I dipped from it for a few months to play apex legends.

I came back to it around the beginning November and I haven't played anything else since. I finished all the campaigns excluding Shadowkeep, I ran dozens of runs through Gambit, Crucible and Iron Banner, and most importantly I made my warlock look hot as hell. Destiny 2 gives you so much to do that it honestly gets overwhelming but when you have a plan, it eases up. You really can't get bored with this game, just a little brain fried by playing it so much. On top of that, the graphics are gorgeous! It's not fair that this game is so pretty. I probably have an hour or two logged in of just me sitting at the Tower, watching the Traveler spin and the Last City glow.

The characters in the game are really fun too! They all have their own charms and quirks that makes me remember them, and that includes the ones I'm not a big fan of. The one that stands out the most in my mind is Suraya Hawthorne. She talks to the Guardian with awe and disbelief every time, even though she know that we're able to do the things we do with our Ghosts and Light.

The only real problems I have with Destiny is that sliding in this game isn't fun, the quest + bounty sorting can be better and Suraya needs more things to say to me in the Tower.

All in all, Destiny 2 is a wonderful game that really sucks you in it's universe in almost every aspect whether you do more storyline things or pvp things and it's now one of my favorite games of all time. Do yourself a favor and give it a play, especially since it's free on Steam now.

Merry Christmas to whoever reads this <3\t\t\t\t\t
