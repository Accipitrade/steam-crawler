\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis hurts, a lot. I remember playing the beta for the original Destiny, playing the full game when it came out day one. Running through almost all the content the game had, and being excited for the next installment of my would be new franchise. I remember playing the beta for Destiny 2, playing the full game day one again and loving the story, even though some of the early expansions were a bit underwhelming (looking at you Curse of Osiris). But over time this game that I have loved for years and years has slowly turned into a lifeless shell of what it once was. Consistently, Bungie has decided that instead of new equipment to kit out your guardians with, that they should either re-release the same equipment, or slap a new texture on it, give it some small stat variations, and call it brand new. And when this wasn't enough, they then decided to start porting over gear and content from Destiny 1, which while I did love the gear from that game, it feels lazy and uninspired after all the previous re-releases. This doesn't even begin to mention the fact that the entire main campaign, plus the first, second, and third expansions have all been removed from the game. All this, plus the removal of fun activities such as Crimson Days and Faction Wars, makes this paid game gone free to play (that also removed 3 paid expansions) feel like Destiny 2 has lost it's light.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like Destiny. I think the lore is great, the cinematic cutscenes are top tier, the graphics are gorgeous, the music slaps, and the gameplay is fun! For any veteran of the game with hundreds of hours, the current state of the game is probably fine. But that's not me.

The game is just plain difficult to get into now. I have no idea what is going on in the story, I'm overwhelmed by all of the quests and mechanics dropped on me, and I am utterly disappointed that I can no longer go back to older content or even finish the content that I was working on when I stopped playing last, in Forsaken. Hundreds of hours of paid content, "vaulted" with bad excuses.

If Bungie were to shove every last bit of removed content back into the game and include an evolving world state that matches where you're at in the storyline, I'd be putting a lot more time into the game for sure. Not to mention how much better it would be for brand new players to start the game.\t\t\t\t\t
