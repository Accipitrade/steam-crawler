No amount of excuse making about "saving space" or "slandering bungie's good name" or my personal favorite "they're a small indie studio you have to give them a pass!" can justify this.

It's an abusive cycle I've seen play out too many times, and in too many games, even if in different forms.

EDIT: Since I've been free of Destiny 2 for almost a year now I have to say I don't regret my decision, and while it may look like on steam I had very little hours (around 84 it claimed) I preordered the game back on battle net, a fact that a person once called me a liar for saying, going "uh no it's a steam game checkmate idiot"

Since that review I did the classic steam review video decision to sink another 900 or so hours into the game, generally forced by friends who still clung onto the game. The game is honestly a confusing mess that often misses the mark while being defended by people who quite literally do not play any other games.

Expecting people to play these kinds of games as a second job is completely unreasonable, hence why also balancing games towards the pros tends to be more damaging for the game itself, something I've seen play out for the past decade to almost 15 years.

If you like the game great, have fun, if you defend removal of content (even if they are supposedly no longer going to remove content, it doesn't excuse the past 5 years) you're most likely a whale or someone who has grown up only really experiencing games being made this way, and it's honestly a shame.\t\t\t\t\t
