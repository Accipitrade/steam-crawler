It's a shame, because Destiny is at its best when it sets you against a horde of enemies and lets you rush head-first into encounters, but if the endgame content was any indication of how Bungie wants you to play their game, it's far less about being a 'power fantasy' than it is about strictly sticking to meta builds and slowly chipping away at enemies from either a safe distance or while peeking from behind cover, and given that their general design ethos over the game's lifetime has focused less on mechanical depth, but mechanical breadth, it seems unlikely that Bungie's roots as the creator of a much beloved shooter franchise is going to factor into the decisions that guide the shape which Destiny takes going into the future.

While I might lament the fact that it's on a road where its shooter-pedigree is being chipped away with every move towards becoming a fully-fledged MMO, there's still a large amount of content and activities to potentially make Destiny worthwhile for any kind of shooter fan, assuming you don't mind paying out the nose for it.

Overall: 6/10
