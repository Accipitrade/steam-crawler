[MOST OF THIS CONTENT WAS VAULTED TO RETURN AT A LATER DATE, BUT I AM STILL IN LOVE WITH THIS GAME]

Destiny 2 is the sequel to the Bungie hit-game Destiny, in which a benevolent entity, known only by reference as The Traveler, arrives to save the Earth, as well as ultimately (hopefully) the rest of the known universe. But it is upon this saving of the Earth that the Traveler essentially "dies," and releases a multitude of sentient A.I.-esque entities called "Ghosts," that have one objective and one objective only; find a host worthy of the Light and resurrect them in the name of the Light, dubbing them a Guardian, in which they will go on to protect humanity and those they ally themselves with, at any and all costs.

Virtually unkillable, except for extreme circumstances, these Guardians are granted immense and god-like powers, ranging from taking the elements and turning them against their foes, such as Arc (essentially a hyper-form of electricity) and Solar (the sun in the palm of your hands), as well as Void (bend reality to your whim), shattering the atoms of their enemies, forcing them to submit to their will, or creating shields and barriers to protect those who stand besides their fellow guardians and friends.

Destiny 2 is a gorgeous game, with a vast amount of landscapes and worlds you can explore and just take in. Every area has an unbelievable amount of detail and ambiance to it, and every view is breath-taking in its own right. Whether you're on the vast forests and lost cities of the Earth, or the mechanical-life world of Nessus, there are a multitude of sights that are pleasing for those who absolutely love a game with a good landscape. Tired of bashing your enemies in with your god-like powers? Take a rest and just stare up into the stars on Io, or explore the RASPUTIN Warmind compound on Mars and see what secret secrets you can find.

The gunplay is solid, and there is a massive amount of legendary weapons the likes of which you can obtain, whether by exploring and opening chests, earning them through hard fought victories in the crucible, strikes and raids, or from quests you find along the way that lead you to even more powerful weapons, known as exotics, that carry incredibly unique stats and perks that can be used to create different playstyles. Various and unique weapons await in Destiny 2.

Though the game had a rough start with it's initial release and main questline, the Red War, Destiny 2 continued through hardship and tough criticism, taking in every word they could. The first two expansions (which are free now!) Curse of Osiris and the Warmind questlines add an incredible amount of detail and lore-rich entries that you can find by exploring and completing missions and quests alike. Forsaken, the Year 2 DLC, was extremely well received, with massive amounts of players returning to experience it first hand and to avenge their fallen comrade. Forsaken also introduced the later seasons of the game, including the forges of the Season of the Black Armory, in which you aid an exo forge-master named Ada-1 to rekindle the flames of her lost and broken forges, to craft and create new and more powerful weapons, to the Season of the Drifter, which expands on a mysterious and potentially dangerous figure known only as "The Drifter," introducing more quests and lore most intriguing indeed. Finally, the end of Year 2 saw the Season of Opulence, in which we would return to a figure we had aided in the earlier year of Destiny 2, Emperor Calus, as his ship had now become infested with the disturbing and horrific Hive. Now, Shadowkeep, the Year 3 installation of Destiny 2 has dropped, and with it the Season of The Undying, re-introducing the Moon destination, bringing back old memories, both good and bad, as we have now finally encountered the entity known only as The Darkness, the penultimate embodiment of "bad guy."

It is with this, though, that I stop talking about this game and invite you, reader, to try it out. Test your power, grow fat from strength, and Become Legend.

Eyes up, Guardians.

[MY THOUGHTS ON BEYOND LIGHT]

My thoughts? I love it. It of course was going to be rough when it dropped. I expected it to. People always complained, that nothing could ever live up to Forsaken's campaign and the Last Wish raid. But now? With Deep Stone Crypt out? My god, I cannot believe how amazing it was to experience Deep Stone Crypt on day one. The soundtrack. The fights. The sheer experience of it all. My only regret? I'll never get to experience it for the first time ever again.

I look forward to Witch Queen.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Game is not Free to Play, it's Free to try, Game suffers from Bad Balancing with few hot-fixes, Sunsetting sucks, new players don't know whats going on, and also Warlocks currently suck (this was made before strand is introduced to the game).

As someone who started this game from Shadowkeep, I got to say that Destiny 2 suffers from very poor balancing. Sometimes a thing will release and is way too overpowered, and Bungie will take their sweet time to nerf it into an acceptable state (ex: shatter-dive taking 1 year and 2 months to no longer easily one-shot). They also tend to nerf things in a way that screams "we are innovative" that can either make said thing absolutely bad, change nothing, buff it to high hell, or (very rarely) fix the problem.

They also sometimes forget promises that they've made to the community, such as saying that they want to make destiny 2 less ability focused, then they release the 3.0 subclasses or new exotics that enable ability spam / overpowered abilities. Or when they said that they have a "renewed focus on pvp" which amounts to introducing one of the worst maps in the game (Disjunction), re-introducing (from Destiny 1) one of the worst gamemodes into the game (rift). Then they make said gamemode one of the random match types you would be forced to do in the competitive pool, going against the flow that other game-modes in the competitive gamemode pool. They also introduced air-born effectiveness into the game, and for about 2 seasons (6 months) they basically forced everyone to stay grounded before practically reversing the changes they initially made about air-born effectiveness so that every primary weapon can jump and shoot, just like before air-born effectiveness was introduced.

One of the worst things that happened to this game would be sunsetting and the D2 content vault. I'm not going to weigh the pros and cons of these two things, I'm just going to talk about the results of these events. Sunsetting was designed to do one thing specifially, and that was to remove all old content so file space could be freed up. Unfortunately, this includes the first 3 DLC's they made free for everyone to enjoy, so no more Red War, Curse of Osiris, or Warmind content for the new players to enjoy. On top of that, they removed a large amount of PvP and Gambit maps, as well as removed a crap ton of locations, strikes, raids, and other activities. You know the cool secret missions The Whisper and Zero Hour? They're no longer available, and will (probably) never ever have other missions that even come close to being as mysterious or challenging. Most weapons they put into the game aren't even new, they just re-issue old ones they "sunset" or brought back from D1, sometimes slapping on new modern perks to make it more relevant. I took a quick look at every single weapon I've ever grinded for ever since I've bought the Deluxe Witch-Queen DLC, and all of them were re-issues of old ones that could now be "crafted" for the 5/5 God Roll. Beloved, Austringer, Ikelos SMG are the main ones, with the rest usually having super niche rolls such as the Season of the Haunted scout rifle that can have both triple tap and fourth times the charm, or bump in the night with chill-clip and auto-loading holster.

All of this sunsetting stuff I mentioned wouldn't even be that bad if they at least made some new stuff for us to look at. But instead they keep re-issuing content we've already got. The Bungie 30th anniversary pack is literally just a "Hey look! We brought back these destiny 1 classics as well as sword skating, give us 30 dollars". Hell, the damn seasonal activity we have during season of the seraph has us going back to the previously sunset Mars location to do a gimped version of escalation protocol before running around to the boss area to do a DPS phase followed by depositing balls before continuing DPS phase. It's insulting, to people like me who hated sunsetting, we have these old locations dangled in front of us, as if bungie is saying "Lmaoo we didn't actually remove these maps, we just wanted to remove all the old crap so you have to run around grinding the weapons before you can no longer focus on farming them".

The magic that made me initially buy Forsaken and Shadowkeep is now gone, and I can't share the same experience with my friends like before. When I first started the game, I had my friend and I work tirelessly on The Whisper mission before we finally succeeded in acquiring the Whisper of the Worm, and now you just buy it in a damn exotic store. As if that wasn't bad enough, new players are only given a short description of the events that the Major DLC's covered in a D2 history page, so unless they plan on watching the cutscenes of the campaigns (which I doubt), they'll just quit because there is literally nothing to do unless you shill out a lot of money.

Now here's the special on class balancing in pvp. There are three classes you select, and they all have unique abilities. But as we all know, not every ability can be treated equal, which is why some classes are better, or worse than others. Warlocks used to be manipulators of the energy and reality around them, gaining understanding of the light / darkness to better use them. Now all they do is summon turrets or other sentient things to attack for us, which sounds cool unless you are literally a normal person who realizes that D2 tracking is aids, and would rather just have abilities that enhance yourself to do better, but nope! That goes to hunters and titans, and now all you are is the classic RPG healer or buffer or summoner that does -all whenever you need to do something remotely useful in PvP. I understand that some people may believe that I'm just not good enough at PvP, but whenever I play warlock I have to think in five different dimensions so I can at least understand how each move I do will affect the enemy and the way they think, but whenever I play as a hunter all I need is good aim & reflexes, and as I titan I literally don't even need to think too hard before I decide to throw a grenade, put down a barrier, or punch someone. Solar 3.0 for Warlocks was so bad that I would rather have the 2.0 system back for solar so I can do something that's more interesting than in-air gliding. Oh yeah, I almost forgot about void 3.0 and Chaos Accelerant, You only get 1 fragment slot, and no grenade damage boost. Nothing Manacles was butchered when released into D2 because the tracking is literally Chaos Accelerant tracking, so you can't synergize the two to do something cool. Titans are literally better warlocks in a large variety of ways. Utilizing Phoenix Cradle, they can give team mates a damage boost as well as healing on solar, meaning unless a warlock is using the Super Well of Radience 24/7, titans just support better in their neutral game. On void, Titans can give team-mates void overshields while also making enemies volatile or even suppressed with their shoulder bash, and Arc Titans are really stupid. Bungie decided that the subclass that was literally designed for melee combat would recieved boosted grenades that absolutely dominate the PvP scene, while giving arc Warlocks what I consider a stupidly cheesy special melee that I would condemn if it weren't for the fact that Warlocks literally have nothing else to use on that subclass outside of Arc buddies, which again, I absolutely despise. If you can't tell, I used to be a warlock main because I thought that was the way destiny 2 was meant to be played, sticking to one class while partially using other classes for some things. Now I just play Titan and Hunter because Warlocks have no Identity. Subclasses 3.0 was the distribution of Warlock powers, while giving them nothing cool in return. Don't get me wrong, Warlock is still strong in PvE, but literally no one cares about PvE warlock unless they are running well of radiance with star-fire protocol or lunafaction boots to support the team\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a nearly billion dollar game that only now has just fix SOME of it's glaring flaws. The dlc feel like glorified hikes with meta weapons locked behind a paywall. The matchmaking is terrible, the balance is awful, the pc features barely work so you'll be spending hours sifting through poorly designed menus trying to form impromptu parties for most things. The best part of the game is the raids, but to even be able to join it you need to have decent gear and spend time looking for out of game raid groups because there is no internal mechanism for searching for games.

Enemy design is attrocious, Taken enemies will often teleport into you as you take aim, then lob a giant projectile at your face that is far too big to dodge that also blinds you and prevents regen. There's no mechanics to master, there's no counterplay, the only strategy is don't be anywhere near them or burst them down. Cabal have shields but to beat them you have to SHOOT THE SHIELDS as if that makes any sense, and is counter intuitive to most games that have been produced. Scorn make these horrible little totems that can spawn behind you and suck you in until you destroy them, there doesn't seem to be a reasonable limit to where they can spawn these, they'll also frequently become mostly invisible and invulnerable. The hive are mostly benign but have things called shriekers and cyclops that do far too much damage and the only counter to them is to simply not be near their massive range and burst them down fast. The fallen, and vex are the most tame of these enemy types.

Character classes are wildly unpolished and don't make sense in the context of the game. For example, Titan shields are practically useless for cover since aoe attacks will snake behind it, and if the geometry of the floor is slightly off you can be shot through it, while the hunter class has an instant reload that's virtually useless outside of pvp. Grenades vary from incredibly useful, to the most useless and inconsistent attacks in the game, if they're not giant aoe dot ticks don't bother. Jumps are overall mostly terrible in an fps that inexplicably has a ton of platforming.

There's pvp, and it's the most poorly implemented, poorly designed, poorly balanced joke in any online game I've seen. To top it all off the netcode is absolutely trash since the competent people at bungie left after halo ODST, and crouch macros, teleporting infinite super players, and aimbots are RAMPANT.

The gear is almost all offensively mediocre, and cosmetic stuff is completely locked behind paywalls with tons of armor pieces effectively being worthless as are most weapon rolls. Occasionally some of it is available for in game currency, but it's expensive and it doesn't show up often in the crap tastic "eververse".

The gameplay loop is mainly to grind for hours to get mostly mediocre stuff, with some alright weapons locked behind tedious grinds, above all else this game doesn't respect your time. five thousand kills for this, 200 multi kills for that, do this mission 10 times on hard mode to get it's upgrade complete. Almost all quests amount to that. It's all frustrating and this is AFTER they nerfed the grinds!

This game is only good if you plan to not spend any money on it, and don't plan on playing most of the content, or if you just want something mindless to grind. If you want a decent f2p game, play Path of Exile.\t\t\t\t\t

I don't know if there are a bunch of bots or a sadomasochists giving this game positive reviews because this game does not deserve them. This game was originally 60 dollars, which myself and many others payed, now it is free. Slightly infuriating but hey if it builds up the player base and results in more and better content I'm down. Bungie used to be better than this, they used to make games with actual stories, compelling characters, and a gameplay loop that didn't feel like an uninspired knockoff of Borderlands. Oh yeah that's right, I havn't even talked about the gameplay because the rest of the game is so god awful that it wouldn't matter if it was good, well guess what, it's average at best. Maybe if there was more variety to the game but the only way to play this game and not become bored to tears from doing the same thing over and over again is to give Bungie more of your money for the next DLC, because after playing the same mission several times (which some you can't even play anymore) the game loses all fun.

you Bungie and your greedy, anti-consumer . You left Activision and somehow became bigger money 's than they could ever dream to be.
