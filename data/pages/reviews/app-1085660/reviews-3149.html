\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: I cannot recommend this game to people, no matter the state of it. Destiny 2 is an endless time-sink, for better and for worse. This game straddles the line between Looter Shooter and MMO: combining the immediate engaging gameplay of FPS games with some of the more complicated mechanics and the build-crafting & grind from MMOs. The result is one of the most addicting games I've ever played. The grind means you never run out of things to do, and the FPS gameplay keeps the game fun even as you pour hundreds of hours in. Recommending this game to people is like getting a heroin buddy.

Reviewing this game is tough because if I ever get specific, then the review becomes outdated as the game gets updated over time. In general , the game has been improving over time.

I'll start with explaining why despite everything this game does well, I'll never fully recommend it to anyone. This game is a massive time sink. If you are the type of person who likes to grind in RPGs, go for achievements, or collect items; you'll get absolutely hooked to this game. Despite how fun the general gun-play is, there's still a lot of grinding you have to do to raise your level for end-game content. Once you reach a certain point, there's only a limited subset of activities you can do to raise your rank, and it takes forever. Now admittedly, you do not need to reach max rank if you're just playing the game casually. The problem is this is a tough game to play casually. Every bit of this game is begging you to sink more time in and grind for that weapon you'll never use. I know I have already spent an unhealthy amount of time on this game. While I do wish I spent that time elsewhere playing games that have better stories or more engaging gameplay, but I also want to keep playing this game.

There's not very much in the way of story unless you pay for the DLC campaigns and the seasons. There's a tutorial quest and that's about it. Honestly, you don't really need to play the older DLCs to get 'caught up' with the story because most of them are self-contained. Most of the overall story is told through the seasons. If you weren't there for Beyond Light's seasons with Crow, then you're not going to be able to know much about him without looking up lore. For new players, you should just buy the latest campaign. If the story interests you enough, look up lore youtubers who can give you a better lay of the land.

Edit: Recently, Bungie actually made the new player experience better. Now they have a whole guided sequence that better teaches new players what to do. They will no longer need to be guided by veterans (as much as before).

Let’s talk about some of the positives. The moment to moment gun-play is incredibly entertaining. I don’t know how to describe it, perhaps “satisfying” is the word. It just feels really smooth and fun (from a PvE standpoint). But the game's main redemption is the more challenging content: Legend difficulty campaigns and Raids in particular. Raids introduce complex mechanics requiring coordination and communication. This makes them a fun challenge, once you get a fire team to do them (you can't match-make for Raids unfortunately). It's also very likely that you'll get stuck in one area because 1: you don't get what the ♥♥♥♥ is going on or 2: your teammates don't get what the ♥♥♥♥ is going on. High level nightfalls are challenging, but more in the sense that you'll die in seconds if you ever break cover. Also you need to grind a long time to reach a high enough level to play nightfalls (every season).

One of the game’s multiplayer modes is Gambit which can be labeled as "Competitive PvE". This is a mode where having some players who don't know what they're doing can absolutely ruin the experience. The mode has its fans, but I'm not one of them.

Crucible is the PvP mode. It's full of things that can one-shot you: from special weapons to supers and abilities. It's an absolute hell-hole, and I hate it. There's so many combinations of things in the game that Crucible will never be as balanced as dedicated pVp games.

Overall opinion: It's so weird to not recommend a game that I've spent so much time in (and will continue to). Destiny 2 is an endless time-sink, for better and for worse. I would not recommend it unless you're looking for a game to take up the next several months of your life (at minimum).\t\t\t\t\t
