\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe F2P area of the game is enough content for you to get a feel of the game to know if you want to commit to buying an expansion. The new light experience is not great as it makes you feel as if you need friends to help you figure out what you're meant to be doing which can feel a bit overwhelming at first but once you get the hang of it, the experience changes drastically. Forsaken and its annual pass is definitely worth it as it has a vast amount of content which you could play for 100s if not 1000s of hours to grind out good gear, access to raids which reward the most unique exotic weapons in the game as well as some good looking armour sets. Shadowkeep is a bit of a different story as it is a good expansion in itself, but it does not live up to Forsaken as it comes with only 1 raid instead of 3 but a lot of the current content and pinnacle power grinding (if you care about that) is done through the Shadowkeep expansion. This is largely due to the lack of content seen from seasons 8 through 10 and the fact that the seasonal events from these seasons are not here to stay once the seasons end which makes the Forsaken seasons much more worth the money one would pay for them despite the cost difference of the seasons from Forsaken to the seasons from Shadowkeep. Overall this game is for those with time to dedicate to it, and for those who do not, there may be a lot of content you may be less able to do as there is a certain amount of time dedication that one must put into the game to get worthwhile rewards. Beyond this there is enough content to play this game and nothing but (including both expansions) for months on end and if you enjoy the gameplay experience from the F2P part of the game then i would heavily recommend getting forsaken and maybe Shadowkeep if you've gone through the forsaken content and you really want more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros
- Good story with alot of fun and sometimes confusing elements.
- ALOOOT of content. Sometimes to the extent of being to much.
- Addicting if there is something you really want.
- The shooting aspect of the game is really solid when it comes to both audio and visuals
- Everything you can buy with in game currency is cosmetic only. So even though its a F2P, they dont rely on P2W
- There are also alot of different game modes such as Gambit and Vanguard.
-

Cons
- You can complete the game solo but you will not be able to enjoy all the content. There are certain guns you wont be able to get and so on.
- BUGS, do I have to say much more
- They throw alot of content on you in the beginning and it can be really confusing. Sometimes you dont know where to start.
-

Things I dont know where to put (because they are pretty personal)
- The game is really grindy. If you want a certain item you might have to do things over and over again, and sometimes that includes at specific times. For me thats not really a problem but I can see people not liking that aspect of the game.
- When you have created a character you cant change the look. Just something to be aware of (cus I had no idea, and now I look stupid).
- To be able to play all the content that they offer you will have to buy the Forsaken and Sadowkeep expansions (I played the free version for well over 100h and i had still plenty to do so you wont run short).
-

Final thoughts
Destiny 2 is a really good game. Lots of content and things to explore and just like every game it has its flaws, but I would deffinatly recommend it if you like a grindy game with a good community. The only thing i would recommend tho is to get into a clan or play with friends to be able to enjoy the entire game. Have fun :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not the best game, but it is pretty good!

I was influenced by the hate train when I heard about Destiny 2 for a while. Loot system being tedious and it wasn't really rewarding. I went in with some very unfair expectations, and yet it hooked me in. Next thing I know, I bought forsaken, I bought shadow keep, and was leading some raids. Clocking in at least 200 hours as I write this.

So some good things about the game:
-At it's core it's an MMORPG, and unlike most MMORPGs it's a lot easier to pick up, you don't need to fully understand everything to have an enjoyable experience.
-The highest tier rarity weapons, the Exotics, are quite unique in their own way. Bullets that bounce off walls, and guns that cause enemies to explode. But be advised, these aren't close to Borderlands Legendaries.
-Power levels, when power caps increase, you have a bigger reason to keep playing, to keep up and maintain that power score to the ceiling to roof so you could get a lot of great rewards and challenge yourself to harder quests.
-Raids, are very fun with the right group of friends, all are very well made. I'd recommend having only one guy that's run through the raids before in the party, it will be chaotic and satisfying to solve.
-Gunplay, it's just too good, haven't played a game with a more satisfying head shot.

So some bad things about the game:
-Story is meh, I love all the lore, game story is just not the best. It's got a lot of great moments but sometimes falls short. Especially some of the characters, looking at Zavala at that one, vanilla boy scout.
-Loot system. Not very rewarding, if you compare the amount of loot you get from Borderlands and Destiny, Borderlands will get you 10X more loot. That would be a very unfair comparison, Destiny is a bit more.... intricate about the loot, and the stats that go with it. It's more on stat building and grinding for the right roll that suits you. This is very much an MMORPG mechanic, so you'd need dedication.
-Strikes, some are fun, some are bad, all can be sped run, and all will be repetitive for better gear. It only gets exciting when you do nightfalls but if you aren't good enough, it will be hell. It'll be worth it if you need to do one for an Exotic....I hope.

There is a lot more I've missed out on different game modes, PVP, and other stuff, so TLDR, it's a nice MMORPGFPS that requires some dedication.
