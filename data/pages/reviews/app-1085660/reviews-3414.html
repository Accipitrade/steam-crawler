\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy 40 Hr Review. So far so good, I cannot stop playing. I am not a returning destiny 2 player but I played through most of destiny 1 with around 350hrs total playtime.

Pros: The game doesn't feel like a F2P game.
There's lots to do, even if you are playing for free.
Also, there's lots to earn, a seemingly endless amount. Please don't mess this part up Bungie.
There's a lot to like here, I've been waiting to play destiny for a very long time but could never afford it.

Cons: 750 light level to start is wayyy too high It took all of the fun out of "the grind" for me because I was looking forward to leveling up myself. In less than a week I already am nearing the hard cap.

Not an issue for me because I never played D2 on blizzard but, Most ex D2 players that I have talked to feel a little upset about the move to steam because it cost them lot's to get the past dlcs and now most of them are free. They don't feel like they are being compensated for enough.

The information in deciding to get the game was very confusing I thought I was paying 60$ for every campaign but instead I got every one but Forsaken. I would rather have gotten Forsaken with my 60$ than 4 season passes because I know that my friends that I play with are not going to want to buy Forsaken and I most likely wont be buying it because I have no friends to play it with.

Eriana's Vow is pre-order loot as well as being on the season pass which makes getting the weapon useless to me as I am already nearing it on the season pass after a little more than a week. Feels like a rip off and I had no way of knowing that'd happen beforehand.

Lastly, MULTIPLAYER IS BROKEN and needs balancing/overhaul. Probably the biggest let down of D2 for me so far is the multiplayer. I was expecting it to be just as fun as the D1 days but instead I was greeted with extremely limited modes, terrible matchmaking services, and a broken launching system that requires us to make a new lobby after every game.

I was expecting the king of the hill mode D1 had as that was my favorite by far.

The map rotation is very limited and I wish yall would just add the rest of the D1 maps. COD has recycled nuketown a handful of times now, you guys can reuse your maps too.

I personally hat having to get frags in order to get additional special ammo I like the D1 special ordinance system much better

TITANS NEED NERFING. This class, if i understand correctly, is supposed to be a tank(y) class. IMO, titans should have more tank-like movement/abilities. Instead, they have a super that is literally impossible to counter(even with another super(at least dawnblade does zero to it)), they can shoulder check round after round, for an instant easy kill which no other class can do, and they move fast as hell for tanks.

Gun balancing is not really an issue for me there are some annoying guns but for the most part if you fight fire with fire, you'll be fine, unless you suck.

Multiplayer modes need to be expanded upon. I as well as many others play this game as much for the pvp as we do the pve. I would like to see more options for fireteams of varying sizes I dont like that 6 man fireteams are limited to control and tdm. The amount of different gametypes is lacking.

You guys made Halo. If anyone should know how to make a good multiplayer system it's YOU.

Final thoughts: I know I wrote more cons than pros. I don't dislike this game at all. I believe that this game has a lot of potential to be perfect. The cons I did put are seriously ruining the experience for me and if things aren't done I will eventually give up on Destiny.

A side note: I find it weird that yall did away with common items because now if its not legendary its trash to me. In D1 I kept most rares and was stoked if I got a legendary. Now legendary items are like a norm and exotics are the new legendary.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDESTINY 2 is a direct sequel to DESTINY (2014), a sci-fi massively multiplayer first person looter shooter.

While I enjoyed DESTINY 1 back on the PlayStation 3 and 4, it was undeniable it was the best shooter gameplay in years. It felt familiar to Bungie's last hoorah with Halo Reach, yet different enough in all of the right ways to give this franchise it's own identity.

While DESTINY 1's gameplay was stellar, there was definitely issues with it's overarching story. The story felt like an afterthought, thrown together in a couple months so they could ship it with a "campaign", if you would even call it that. This was a huge point of criticism for the game, and I can tell you with certainty that Bungie listened.

The story? DESTINY 2 launched with the Red War campaign, which kicks off with a surprise attack launched on the Tower, which causes the retreat of the Guardians out of their home and placing them on the run. While simple on paper, it is truly all about execution and Bungie nailed the Red War. It takes me back to the days where Bungie's HALO had a great story to fit it's fast-paced gameplay. Red War nails that same feeling, providing a good story, though it can certainty be cliche at times. I would say if you like first person shooters with classic space opera stories, give this a shot. It's worth it.

The gameplay? Stellar. There is no argument here. Every single frame of combat is a thrill. If you loved HALO, this is that but refined in a way only Bungie could pull off all these years later.

The music? My GOD, the music. This is easily the best soundtrack in a video game of recent memory. The music compliments the story, encounters and atmosphere of every location you explore. The music is not just music, it is an extension of the game itself. As crazy as it sounds, this is one of the few games I play with music turned on. This game's music helps add to the ambiance of sorrow, peace, anger, or anxiety that these locations and stories provide.

With the launch of DESTINY 2: NEW LIGHT, the base game and all of it's year one (Curse of Osiris & Warmind) content is now available for free. There is no free to play paywalls or anything of the sort, all of the content is yours right off the bat. You do not level any slower, you do not start at a disadvantage, you are placed on an even playing ground with the option to purchase FORSAKEN and SHADOWKEEP if you choose to do so.

The update of NEW LIGHT also makes it so you start at Light Level 750 (instead of the original game's Light Level 10), which makes you eligible to participate in any of the content you wish right off the bat. If you truly wanted, you can make a toon, play the tutorial and immediately hop into the latest expansion SHADOWKEEP if you just purchased it and not have many if not any issues doing so.

PVP is balanced, disabling the Power Level advantages and making it a fair playing ground. It is very similar to the Halo games, just do not expect vehicles in Crucible matches.

I played through the Red War, Curse of Osiris & Warmind completely free of charge going from mission-to-mission. I did not have to do a single side quest or grind to "stay leveled". You can play this like a first person shooter campaign and you will not have a single problem to do so. And with the first two expansions of CoO & Warmind being free? You have easily, at least thirty hours of solely single-player narrative content there for the entrance fee of absolutely NOTHING.

(Side Note: The Red War, Curse of Osiris & Warmind campaign quest chains are available to access for free from the NPC Amanda Holliday in the Tower's hangar bay. Once you pick it up from her, you can hop into the story. NEW LIGHT is structured a bit weird in this regard.)

If you enjoy the campaigns, definitely purchase FORSAKEN at the very least. It is $24.99 and my favorite campaign in DESTINY 2 so far. It adds a rather lengthy campaign that is definitely longer than the previous expansions. It could be a game on it's own, it took me about fifteen hours to play through just the core missions. Definitely worth the purchase.

If this review got you hooked? Download it now.

If you're curious but not sure? Download it, give it a try.
