\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBackground info:
Destiny 1 release player, played almost all content on both games pretty seriously.

Preface:
To enjoy the game to its fullest, you really have to purchase all of the content that is currently playable (check the icon in the top right of the map if you have the game to see what you dont have). You can read other negative reviews to see how I feel about having to buy content then it getting removed to fit other new content.

Destiny 2 as it stands right now is a really enjoyable game if you like the PvE (player vs enemy for the laymens). The PvP side of the game is quite rough around the edges and probably still needs drastic changes to be "balanced".
PvE: A lot of replayability even if you have to play a lot of the same missions/strikes/raids/dungeons etc. Weekly/daily resets give you new things to do and chase to get your gear level up so you can enjoy some of the "harder" content in the game. Many guns are similar, but the option to chase down weapon "rolls" for different stats really adds replayability/grind to the game.
The game has changed a lot since the first year, and since destiny 1. We've gotten a lot more weapon/armor customizability and the character abilities have a lot of potential for variations of playstyle and something for everyone.
Again you can read some of the negative reviews to see my feelings toward certain ability choices/balancing that is in the game, but you learn to deal with/get over it.
The raids in this game are quite phenomenal and truly something all destiny players should experience.
The PvE in this game is something you can casually enjoy playing over weeks/months, or grinding every day as much as you can and everything in between. Its really what you make of it.

Gripes with PvE: You do end up doing the same strikes/missions over and over if you are looking for something specific and a lot of them are just mundane.
Most of the weapons and armor are just reskins that fit into specific weapon "archetypes", with predetermined rate of fire, weapon damage, reload speed, etc. However, this is what the weapon rolls help to counteract, and thus the meta of shifting certain weapons into other archetypes while maintaining the bonuses of their current archetype to improve their performance is made.
This game could be REALLY amazing if the developers would stop trying to make sure everyone can have the same experience on the game. Essentially, if they let loose and turned it into a full MMO style game with the armor and weapons, there would be so much extra life to the game. It would be truly one of a kind, instead of just another looter shooter with MMO aspects. (Every MMO style change they make to the game, it gets a lot better).

PvP: Oh boy. It needs work. A lot. You will want to smash your face into every keyboard you own until you dont have any money to spend, or until the world has ran out of materials to make new keyboards. Even if you're a good player. The developers walk a fine line when they add in new content (abilities, weapons, armors, perks etc.) because if they add something with PvE in mind, it can be extremely busted in PvP. There isn't a divide in the PvP/PvE balance of the game besides the physical damage values that weapons/abilities do. A lot of the abilities 1shot you, while the actual time to kill with a primary weapon is quite long depending on what you're using. To stay on an overview level, I wont go into specifics but a lot of the balance issues and problems that players face is due to the dev's wanting to keep the experience the same for players. Similar damage models for weapons, similar health levels, similar abilities etc. all thrown into a cod style map/gamemode. The PvE side of things bring in MMO style weapons and abilities that naturally players are going to use and abuse in PvP and it makes the outcomes of matches a lot less skillbased and more determined by what guns/abilities you decide to use or are able to get.
I know plenty of people that would disagree and say they actually enjoy using abilities and loadouts that are more cheesy imo, and thats fair as well.
Overall, if you can get past the annoying ability spam and splash of very broken weapons, you can have a good time and get some cool loot.

You can play both PvP and PvE relatively exclusive from eachother if you'd like, with content being pushed out for both relatively frequently.

Conclusion: I truly recommend this game if you havent played it or something like it before. There WILL be annoyances and things that you absolutely hate about the game, but the other parts of the game really allow for some true enjoyment as a player. If you are seriously considering it, play it. its free. if you like playing it from the taste you have, buy some of the dlc's (probably the current one first) and go from there until you have all that you want. There is an absolutely crazy amount of support and guides for the game for new players and experienced alike, so if you have no idea what you're doing have no fear. Watch some streams, watch some youtube videos and read guides online for how to get and do specific things.

If you made it this far thanks :)\t\t\t\t\t
