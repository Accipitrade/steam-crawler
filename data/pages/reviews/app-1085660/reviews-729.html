\r\n\t\t\t\t\t2000+ hours between Destiny 1 and 2 on PS4 and PC
I have a love/hate relationship with Destiny 2. Destiny is so time demanding that if i give it the time it needs then I miss out on plenty of other great games, but if i take a break from destiny to play through the other games then I fall extremely behind and miss out on loads of content.
Destiny 1 was a magical experience where everything felt rewarding. There was plenty to do for solo players and fireteams. Destiny 2 on the other hand started off alright and only go better all the way to shadowkeep and a while after. Then the game just feels like a list of chores you need to do every week or else you'll fall behind in power and story and the game will become a slog to catch up. All the content and weapons you want is locked behind annoying difficult challenges where bullet sponge champions are everywhere and enemies with splash damage 2 shot you behind cover and endgame content that requires a full fireteam to complete (hard to find non toxic players in the game now).
Even when LFGing for a fireteam all posts require 20+ completions of said activity within the first week of the activities release.
The world/story and gunplay are the best things about destiny.
Sadly the Gunplay only really applies to PvE as PvP has always had problems. PvP Doesn't get the good feeling of gunplay since 90% of the crucible is one shot abilities, Supers, and one shot special and heavy weapons. Primary weapons are for the majority of the time reserved for the first couple minutes of the match until supers are charged and players have started picking up special ammo.
PvE is 8/10 Great game when doing the fun solo content and great endgame content when you have a Group to play with that isn't toxic. Would be better if the pinnacle endgame content didnt make every enemy a bullet sponge and do insane damage.
PvP is 5/10 Can be fun at times, but often gets annoying dying instantly to a titan that happen to turn a corner where you were and panic meleeing for an instant kill, or spawning 5 feet in front of a roaming super after having just died to them, or playing solo against stacks that stack roaming supers back to back the entire match.\t\t\t\t\t
