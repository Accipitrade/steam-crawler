\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played Destiny 2 since its PC launch.

To me, Destiny is like a pug. While it may be ugly at times, the good certainly outweighs the bad. I've yet to play another shooter that has such a smooth FPS feel, plus the game is beautiful on PC. Check the specs and make sure your PC can handle the game, she's gigantic.

Make sure you join the Destiny Discord for all your LFG needs (I highly recommend using it to experience the raids and the end game multiplayer content as these are the highest points of this game).

CONCLUSION: Invest in Destiny if you want to play a quality shooter on PC. If you're looking for something that is story and lore heavy, and driven solely by solo play (and/or you are opposed to using LFG - see above) D2 may not be for you.

Try New Light, get addicted, call out of work for a week straight and I look forward to catching you at The Tower.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start off by saying I have over 200 hours from before New Light which is nothing compared to many people I have played with. The game has it's issues but Bungie is actively working to correct many of those issues. I've seen several complaints concerning the new player experience in New Light, the first mission is meant to provide a tutorial for controls with the rest of the world(s) left for you to explore at your own pace. Many of the complaints about the several worlds being level locked is false, the grind may be present but there options for unlocking those worlds, if you wish to unlock them faster for example you can play the story. To find the story go to the Tower and find Amanda Holiday, she will have "legacy" missions for you, these missions are the main story from vanilla D2 and the story for the first two DLC. Going through these missions will unlock those planets for you without having to grind out the proper amount of experience. The other main issue I have seen people complain about is the server instability, most people are forgetting that the servers were originally set up to run from a different launcher and moving from one to another alongside having ~220k concurrent players is going to be a burden for a game that before this had much less.

There are also some curators pushing forth false information, mostly that of "invisible" locks on the grind. AFAIK the issue was that towards the endgame content it was harder to level up due to certain xp locks. These were put in the game on Activisons request and part of a contract with the publishers. To give a negative review simply for something greedy publishers forced into the game 2 years ago is childish and shows that many giving bad reviews simply have not done their homework.

TLDR: Negative reviews are either misinformed or are trolls who simply hate the game from many of the issues that came from Activision.\t\t\t\t\t
