\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my opinion. I've only played this game for about 100hrs. However I cannot bring myself to continue. As a new player, this game feels unfocused and all over the place. The story content has been vaulted, and the remaining campaigns offer little to no rewards and make little sense. Unless of course you are willing to watch Youtube videos.

This leaves the gameplay. Classes are fun and gunplay feels great. I also found loot drops generous and I had a selection of powerful exotics in no time. However, the gameplay loop is tedious. It consists of weekly checklist activities that you grind out. You log in, run through your weeklies and repeat this ad nauseam. These activities are the same activities you will do every week. It is incredibly repetitive.

I am not new to grind or even opposed to it. I have spent countless hours in games such as Borderlands, monster hunter, outriders and even warframe (although i have choice words about warframe too). For me, Destiny gives new players very little reason to care. It's difficult to be engaged in the games world when it feels like an unfinished jigsaw puzzle and as a result, grinding feels pointless. The game is well received so clearly there is fun to be had here. Personally though, I did not enjoy my time with Destiny 2

Note: For reference, I purchased all expansions (including anniversary content)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t7 years of playing and I'm still hooked. It hasn't been a perfect 7 years, but it has created many memorable moments for myself and friends. Speaking of friends, the Destiny franchise is responsible for giving me the ability to befriend quite a few people, which has been amazing as I have grown to be a more skillful player from the help of friends. It is a tricky game to get into (even with the new player quests) but having a couple of friends with a lot or little knowledge will be the best way to get involved in the game.

Destiny has evolved like a roller-coaster, highs and lows in every which way you may look, one 'high' example would be the story. Bungie have made leaps and bounds with their story visibility, it is very nostalgic to think how crap the story was in vanilla Destiny 1 compared to the seasonal story players receive now which removes the necessity for new players to read up on all the lore. A 'low' example can be illustrated through the dynamic customisation of subclasses in Destiny 1, which got swapped for a more static customisation in Destiny 2. A more static customisation does assist in not having to think how abilities synergise together but it impacts how the player wants to play. The player might want to be able to throw a barrage of knives for a super ability but also have the melee ability of setting knives on surfaces as small traps which may provide a weapon damage buff (this is a very hypothetical example). However, subclasses are getting a rework and are slowly changing back to dynamic customisation in the Witch Queen DLC launch (22/2/2022), so that should be exciting (I am!).

The game isn't perfect nor garbage, it continually seeks to improve itself which of course falls flat occasionally. The community is quite solid and the experience Destiny has provided is invaluable from my playtime, I highly recommend grabbing a friend or two to mess around with the game and see if this your cup of tea.\t\t\t\t\t
