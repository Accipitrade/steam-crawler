UPDATE - 03/01/2021
---

This is a short second update, but with the announcement of cancelling Power Limit, I feel I can safely recommend the game again. I've been finding that content vaulting has been a helpful way of reducing queue times and streamlining the weekly grind, and there's potential in the future to reintroduce sunsetted equipment in interesting fashions down the road.

Bungie still has its work cut out to make Destiny 2 the best it can be. Rebalancing Stasis, implementing transmog and crossplay, and so much more. But with the crippling anchor of Power Limit taken out of the equation, I've got confidence again they can get the ball rolling towards greener pastures.

---
ORIGINAL REVIEW - 11/19/2019
---

Back in 2014, the following day after my birthday, I played a little game called Destiny. From the creators of Halo, it was a rudimentary looter shooter with an interesting sci-fi setting and a huge amount of potential for storytelling and lore-crafting. However, after just a month of playing the game, I found that PvP content was an overly unbalanced, infuriatingly unfunny joke, and post-campaign PvE content was quicker to dry out than the Sahara. And thus, I sold my copy, and never really looked back.

That is, until October 1st. When I downloaded Destiny 2 at the behest of a friend with the New Light update making it free-to-play, I figured I could spare two or so hours to see whether or not Bungie had learned the lessons needed to make the game as good as it deserved to be. And lo and behold: they... almost got it right. For the most part.

Destiny 2's current active strength is its approach to nonlinearity. Compared to contemporary MMOs like World of Warcraft, Star Wars: The Old Republic, and even pre-New Light Destiny 1/2, the game doesn't ask you to commit to a mind-numbing amount of catch-up grinding to get to the actual good stuff. You're mostly asked just to fill out the old level 20 progression grind from vanilla Destiny 2, which mostly consists of just doing Adventures and Patrols on each new planet you're allowed to access across the progression grind. Once you've done that run to level 20, you're effectively unleashed and permitted to pretty much do whatever you want.

Want to grind Crucible and become a PvP ace? You can do that. Want to do Strikes and Raids? You can do that. Just explore the open worlds and have a good time? You can do that! You're never really forced into a box consisting of hours of empty grind that leaves you drained by the time you're finally allowed to have fun on your own, and that's a HUGE improvement.

I've heard that The Taken King and Forsaken really helped fix intrinsic problems with Destiny 1 and Destiny 2, respectively, but I have done a little reading on my own. And honestly, I probably wouldn't have stomached pre-New Light Destiny 2 at all. With most regular activities in pre-Shadowkeep content being centralized at an adaptive scaling difficulty depending on player Light levels, there's no "zones" dictated for what exactly you do. And getting to the effective Light cap on your own terms is very much possible.

Now, the story definitely needs improvement. It tends to be incredibly basic storytelling loaded with tired tropes and bloating exposition, which can get exhausting really quickly. But while it misses the narrative potential still, as I just said, you're never forced into subjecting yourself to it. And that is, really, a great thing.

If you're looking for an MMO shooter that's good at killing some time, you'll find it easily in Destiny 2. It doesn't ask for much, and has a good habit of keeping you rewarded for your time. The story absolutely needs improvements going forward with future expansions to REALLY get players engaged, and solo play can sometimes get a bit stale since Destiny 2 is at its peak level of enjoyment when playing with friends in a Discord call. But regardless, I'd still recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

I decided to jump back in when I heard that Bungie finally split off from their publishing overlords. So far, I would like to say that I can definitely feel a difference in the game. While the story has definitely picked up more in the second game in general, I also feel like Bungie is really getting to put all their own decisions into the game. This will mean a truly epic experience now and as the game progresses into the future.

Pros:
- Awesome FPS gameplay/mechanics
- Story has really taken off
- Better overall balancing in PvP
- Pretty good ambience/soundtrack

Cons:
- Eververse is still there and extra as hell (few more serious options though)
- New layout of the game may make new players feel lost initially\t\t\t\t\t

I've been attracted to RNG loot type games (i.e. Diablo) and FPS games (i.e. Halo) for decades and this one really hits that spot in my brain that wants to chase after loot AND shoot huge bosses in their ugly alien faces. I play on both Xbox and PC and both communities are fantastic. When this game first released, I found the gameplay to be AWESOME. There still isn't another shooter out there that feels as smooth as Destiny. But sadly, the gunplay alone wasn't enough to keep my interest and it lost me within just a few weeks due to lack of content. However, WOW have they turned that around. I came back to Destiny about 6 months later to try again after a few content releases and it's been a weekly hobby since. The gameplay constantly feel fresh due to frequent updates, the big baddies of the game are always fun and exciting to fight against, and the Destiny community is fun to be a part of. It almost feels like there isn't enough time in a week for a working dad to play all of the content available before each week's reset, but I think that's a great thing. I love that I can play some activities one week and come back the next week to what feels like a whole new set of activities. I don't see myself quitting anytime soon and I look forward to what each new season will bring. If you like FPS games, aliens, space, an expansive story, multiplayer, and the never-ending chase for loot, I'd be shocked if you didn't like Destiny.\t\t\t\t\t
