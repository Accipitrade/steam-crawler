\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this game heavily for those who like deep lore and story, as well as those who enjoy grind games (there's quite a bit of grind, to get the best rolls on weapons for both PvE and PvP, high-stat armor for builds, junk gear to build up legendary shard reserves, etc). I started playing Destiny back when Destiny 1 first launched, and fell victim to having no one to play with. So I'd only do it in spurts here and there until I got bored/burned out.

When I heard Destiny 2 was launching on PC, I was ecstatic. Pre-ordered and everything. Played it until a month after the first raid was launched, then stopped due to burn out. Came back for each expansion release, and fell victim to burn out again. This was also back when the story telling was pretty poor. Fast forward to Forsaken, and I got hooked again, only taking breaks after a long period of solo play sessions. Once I had a dedicated person or two to run with, I became hooked once again.

The story telling has gotten substantially better with each Season and new expansion release, and the lore has gotten quite deep. Deeper than it's previously gone, exploring aspects of character backgrounds that no one knew about prior to the current point in time. Many people will claim this game is "dead" and "losing players", but that's far from the truth. There's bouts of time when the player count is low, due to needing breaks, or playing a new release that people were looking forward to.. but Destiny 2's playerbase is still going strong, and is still passionate about the game.

The base game itself is free, and limits access to the expansion content.. but it's still a good starting point to give the game a try. Especially if you like grindy looter-shooters. Just try to play with a friend so you don't burn yourself out, or give up from not understanding mechanics or getting killed numerous times. Push passed that, improve, and find your place in the fold, Guardian.\t\t\t\t\t
