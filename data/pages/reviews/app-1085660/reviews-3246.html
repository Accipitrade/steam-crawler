\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is great, just don't go in to it looking for a good PvP experience.

Think of it as a co-op PvE shooter that has PvP has an added bonus to blow off some steam. The story content is fun but you will probably only ever do it once or twice. The collections are insanely huge, it would take hundreds and hundreds of hours to complete even one single weapon collection. I have roughly 120 hours as of the time of this writing and I haven't even gotten half of the current expansion collections completed.

If you go in to this thinking of it as a PvP game you will be sorely disappointed at the number of aimbotters and lag abusers. (The game uses Peer-to-Peer networking and as such has no discernable anti-cheat, and people who run torrents to force themselves to lag get an advantage because lag compensation still favors people with bad connections over good ones in most every game.)

This is not an in depth review, just a quick post to share that I do recommend this to anyone looking for a MMORPG FPS game. (And the base game is free now, so why not check it out?)

Edit 7/20/2022:
- Just wanted to check in and update players who might be interested to see how old timer's opinions like mine change over time. 600 hours in on Steam and probably another ~500-ish on Battle.net (Too bad they didn't track play time on that platform). I think I can safely give an opinion.

Destiny 2 has a cycle that I think many live services do. An expansion will launch, the game is super hype. Everyone is having a blast. Then you get to the end-game and start to see the flaws. The lack of new content, the sunsetting (removal) of old content, the recycling of previously sunset (removed) content, bad PvP netcode (At least when playing against Chinese players), etc. But here I am again, playing the game solo and still loving the grind and exploration of new areas!

I think if you look, you will find this happens in every MMORPG in existence. As you start to run out of content I think it's natural to start looking at the flaws. But when I was having fun, I didn't care about them, so why should I care today? Bungie has a great track record of making up for the downsides, even if sometimes it's a bit of a blunder. But no one is perfect and this game is no exception. So today I enjoy the game and recommend it. Tomorrow I might rage quit and tell everyone to stay far away, who knows!

If 1,000+ cumulative hours (600 on steam) of playing Destiny 2 and the fact that I have "quit" and come back for every single expansion without fail, and enjoyed every time I came back, doesn't convince you to buy the game, then simply try out New Light, the free ediditon of the game, and see for yourself if this game scratches that looter shooter itch that you might have. Maybe you'll find a reason to rage quit the game and tell others about it too!

PS. The little bit of PvP I have played recently has felt fine. I don't know if it's because my PC hardware is better or if Bungie fixed some of the bad peer-to-peer netcode or maybe even put some ping limits in so you don't get stuck with teleporting wizards from another dimension. Either way it's worth noting that my biggest gripe doesn't seem to be that big of an issue today.\t\t\t\t\t
