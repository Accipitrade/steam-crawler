\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"I, ate a rotunda, of cheese, in a debacle, with Luke, Smith"

This quote is where i would like to start my review because it evokes 'bag-o-milk vibes.' This is fundamentally key in understanding what its like to play Destiny 2: its like drinking out of a Luke-warm (as in Luke smith) bag of milk. On one hand, you are drinking milk: a wholesome drink which willingly donates it calcium stores to us upon digestion. On the other hand, your drinking milk out of a bag.

Additionally, i would like to point out this game's tendency to hate on one of the most influential game developers in the platformer genera: Luwuke Smith, Senpai. This is a man who spent much of his life wasting away in WoW for our benefit, or in other words, so we didn't have to (because who the F* would play that game). This one accomplishment alone proves that he is the most qualified person to run a multi million dollar studio and propel a game to success.

Furthermore, this game is a goldmine of lore. For instance, I am a big fan of the Shaxx - Mara Sov wish-wall story, and the character, The Stranger. The story itself has so many unique twists and turns. It reminds us all about the importance of safe sex (not like you be having any sex after you download this game), and to always "keep the helmet on" (wink wink). And The Stranger: what a personality. Having a one-on-one conversation with this character is simply some of the best 30 second to 10 minute experiences you'll ever have, trust me.

On a serious note, the player base is one of the kindest you'll ever encounter in any game. If you decide to pick up this game, here's a 'noobies list of insults' to use on your team or the enemy after you lose a game:

- waffle hoarding pickle juice
- bag of milk
- block of over-ripened cheese
- washed up hummus smear
- tire tread
- beta
- banana holder
- snollygoster
- pillock
- smellfungus
- ninnyhammer
- hobbledhoy
- pettifogger

Because everyone's so nice, you may have to respond to some insightful criticism of your game-play skills. Do not fret, simply reply, "i am doing ur mum as we speak." This tends to silence the critics :).
