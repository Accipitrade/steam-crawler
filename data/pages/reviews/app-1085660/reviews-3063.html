\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm one of many who played Destiny 1 at launch and was thoroughly underwhelmed. I got deeply involved in development testimony from former or current Bungie members and as things looked more and more bleak for Destiny, I slowly started to regret my anticipation for it. Then it finally happened... The Taken King expansion costing a whopping $40 at launch for nothing but a reskinned enemy type, buffed class abilities and a new raid that players without Gjallarhorn couldn't take part in anyway, and leaving the old players high and dry with emphasis on building the community with new players and the introduction of microtransactions finally put the nail in Destiny's coffin for me, and I didn't touch the franchise since...

Watching from afar, I saw Destiny 2's release and constant inclusion in news articles about how it was consistently deceiving players with more grinding and putting more and more emphasis on the in-game store. One PC release later and a promotion including a free copy of the game on Blizzard's launcher and I figured, "It's free, why not?" And even though I didn't play it, and had only claimed it, I got a weird feeling... A feeling of hope for this game. A feeling that maybe... just maybe... it can turn around.

And then it happened; Bungie dumped Activision. In a baller move, the Legendary developer walked right out of Activision's clutches WITH the Destiny property license. An execution no one thought was even possible in these days of corporate license slavery and art acquisition. And it was this that caught my attention... Maybe Bungie was being held back, like so many others were/are, by a money-hungry mega corp whose only focus is pleasing other money-hungry investors.

So picked the game back up. Transferred my Destiny 1 characters who I hadn't seen in literal years, and began to play... And ladies... gentlemen. It's not perfect. But it is still solid. Bungie knows how to make a GD game. Mechanically, graphically, artistically this game is such a friggin' powerhouse of a title. Everything looks good and plays better. The Crucible multiplayer is some of the most fun I've had in a multiplayer game since Halo Reach. The game is also very rewarding, which may actually turn some players off, but I cannot hold all of these Legendary Engrams. I've even gotten multiple (yes, multiple) Exotic Engram drops. Something I couldn't get the first game to do in hundreds of hours of play. NOT. ONCE. But here I am, less than a couple hundred hours in, and I've gotten more than 5 Exotic Engrams to drop from random enemy encounters.

The Eververse Market is still in the game, but is limited to cosmetics. I'm not forgiving it for that (microtransactions are still a sin in every facet), but there are other ways and currencies the game offers to get these cosmetics that you can earn through play and the game doesn't bug the hell out of you about it like other titles do.

The story, at least the first one, is still kind of lack-luster. The lore is still hidden behind a bunch of reading and research, but it is there if you care. And the later stories are much richer with actual stakes and returning characters and references to the first game that even got me feeling a little nostalgic.

I... recommend this game. I still feel like Bungie cares about it's dedicated fanbase, and through this new free-to-play model, I think they're getting around to proving it. With numerous ways to earn rewards, beautiful assets and rock-solid gameplay, Destiny 2 has won ME back at least. As to whether you think it's turned around... I leave that to you. After all, why not? It's free.\t\t\t\t\t

The music is epic, the story is engaging, the characters well acted and often witty, and the level design is breathtaking.

Many times i have wanted to stop and admire the scenery rather than play through the interesting story missions.

Paying for a season pass or the DLC is a good move in my opinion as they both seem to provide a lot without making the game pay to win. a season pass is a good shortcut for a few bits of grind but tbh it isn't needed.

I give this game 9/10, mostly because i feel 10/10 should be something truly unparalleled. if they fixed a few of the things i dont like i would probably up that.

1.) a certain favourite character should have a comeback in some form, i have a few ideas how but dont want to say too much *spoilers*

2.) make the campaigns replayable after completion. i enjoyed the story progression when i played through it and would like to do it again without making a whole new character.

3.) bigger glimmer cap, i fill it up easily enough but when i need it it drains so fast, having a bit more of a buffer would be nice.

4.) no time or money locked items.
This is a big one for me and generally Destiny 2 does a good job it seems. but what i would like to see is if an item is first released as real money purchase only, after 3-6 months make it something that CAN drop in some way or be earned through a long grind. IF an item is intended to be limited time only, then if its an annual event make it available every year, if its a one time only release then after the time has passed make it so you can buy it with real money for people that arrived late to the game.\t\t\t\t\t
