\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI’ve never played Destiny 1 and wanted to see what the popularity was about. At times I wondered if this was a solo game or multiplayer game and it seems like end game content revolves around closed groups.

Pros
This game seems to have been designed by quantum physics nerds.
Very cool character concepts such as Lord Shaxxx, Hawthorne and Louis, Saint-14, the Cayde unit, and Zavala.
If you like an equipment you can keep it and upgrade it until you become bored of it.
Lots of explorations to do, the world is sandbox, but at the same time seems vast depending on which celestial body you choose explore.
Great soundtrack, and the environment is beautiful.

Cons
There are bugs in this game, and as with most programs there will be errors, but sometimes these bugs can be frustrating. For example, The Lost Forest map, right after completing the main objectives you’re sent back into Sundial Spire, the Sundial Spire has this spinning roof thingy that will automatically 1hko anyone who jumps into it simply because of games physics or someone thought it would be cool to have players die that way, well, in my case right after defeating the last boss of this map, instead of being teleported back on the ground level of the Sundial Spire to claim my extra rewards I was teleported back on the roof level and right into spinning thingy which instantly killed my character. When I tried to respawn, I was respawned in the boss area where access to the Sundial Spire was closed off.

Equipment, aside from adventuring and enjoying the scenery this game has to offer, seems like you have a life to waste, grinding for equipment and their mods will be the main objective, and that’s very tedious as the developers decided to throw an exponential algorithm in there for extremely hardcore players so you will meet certain unlockable weekly and monthly time walls which you will not be able to overcome unless you’re patient. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is the most fun I've had playing video games. I highly recommend picking it up, especially when the base game is free, but I understand that the new player experience is highly intimidating. D2 has an immense amount of content and almost no in-game tutorials or guidance, and this presents a barrier to entry for many new players. To counter this, I cannot over-emphasize the importance of looking through some guides or playing with people that already understand how the game works, as this makes it infinitely more enjoyable. The D2 community is very supportive, and shouldn't hesitate to help new players get acclimated to the complexity that are the playlists, patrol zones, and raids. Even after 1000+ hours in the 5 months since D2 came to Steam, I'm still learning how many parts of the game work, so don't be embarrassed to ask questions. If you put in the effort to understand the game, hopefully with like-minded friends and clanmates, then I can guarantee D2 will be your forever game.\t\t\t\t\t
