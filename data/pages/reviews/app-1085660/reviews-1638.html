\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy review is of course, heavily biased, as I have been in the Destiny universe since the early Destiny 1 days. I have a stupid amount of hours on this game on the PS4 and upgraded to the world of PC gaming this year.

Destiny is a game that has some of the cleanest gunplay you will find in a FPS today. Make no mistake, Bungie still knows how to make a shooter that not just feels great, but is also satisfying. The absurd attention to detail, the animated skyboxes, and the scope of the art make for a very visually appearance. There is an enormous depth in the story of this game through the lore you can collect through a variety of activities, both inside and outside of the game, with community content creators happily producing easily digestible videos for you to learn more.

There is a lot to do in this game, especially if you are joining as a new light player. There is in fact, so much to do, that it is incredibly confusing to navigate. This is where the game could really use improvement. My suggestion for you if you are new to the game, follow a guide online for a suggested play order. This will save you a lot of confusion down the line. Bungie could have implemented this better for sure.

The end game is where things become... rather repetitive. This is where you'll either find out you are in it for the long haul, or the game doesn't have enough for you. If you are enjoying the game by this point, I would recommend finding a clan or groups to play with. This really opens up the doors for alternative activities than just the standard grind to climb to max power and beyond. The dungeons and raids are amongst the best PvE multiplayer content you will find outside of traditional MMOs. There's something special about this universe and the way these games play. Few, if any games since have been able to match the feeling you get from completing your first raid or dungeon - especially if you are doing so without the assistance of videos and guides (blind runs, as we call it).

I can write forever about this game - there's a lot of good in this game, and there's a lot that could use improvement. The game can certainly be a grind, but the gameplay itself and the rewards you often chase make it feel all the worthwhile. There is a rich lore to be discovered, though it won't all be fed to you via epic cut scenes and in game dialogue. I will always recommend this game, especially to those that are looking for something to scratch that RPG, and FPS itch.\t\t\t\t\t
