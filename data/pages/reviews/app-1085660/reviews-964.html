\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game Destiny 2 has come a long way since Bungie's separation from Activision (Good riddence Activision). I took a break from Destiny 2 shortly after the release of the Leviathan Raid having completed it and felt the game itself had still just lacked the soul that I knew it could have. I've come back to the game within the past year and a half and am so excited to see a more lush story with attention to detail and intricate connections actually in the games story (not just lore) where you can tell the developers and writers really care about what they are creating but also NOW have the ability to focus on the games potential story wise, and not just exploited for profit (in my opinion...). I have really enjoyed what has been released thus far since my return to the game and am actually excited to see what the future holds for this universe and its story. Sci-fi does something for us. It gives us not only the inspiration to look up at the sky in wonder of what may be out there, but the drive to want more and become better. Star Trek did that. Star Wars did that. And now I believe Destiny has the potential to leave its mark as well, not only in great story telling but by testing our idea of what may lie beyond and hopefully give us a glimpse of what we dream to be possible and hope to see become reality (minus the killer aliens of course xD).

Now in terms of the game play itself don't get me wrong every game will have its issues, which Destiny 2 does have problems yes, and not every player will or can be pleased either. But for me personally, I feel a sense of ambition and devotion from the developers of this game that really impress me and give me hope for the games future, and makes dealing with bugs or unwanted changes less of a turn off. Bungie listens to it's Guardians, it's fans, it's community! The reversal of "sunsetting" is a perfect recent example of such. The game play feels great both in terms of destroying enemies with your super, as well as the gun play and reward systems. The story in my opinion continues to grow in depth and is getting better and better every season. I've logged in now over 1000 hours into the game! Many many great changes have been made and I know many more are to come to make this game better and better. There will always be hiccups and bumps in the road along the way in development. But that's what should be expected if Bungie is wanting to take risks in implementing something new for the hopes of making the game better. Don't play it safe, no need to reach for the sky when there's footprints on the moon, shoot for stars! \r\n\t\t\t\t\t(This review is written after the February 25th Bungie state of the game post, where they sunset sunsetting, thank god.)

TLDR: PVE is fun and will be even better down the line (with more raids and other endgame content). PVP is a broken mess in its current state but will also (hopefully) improve later on. The game is free to play so I recommend it to anyone who likes an FPS with fun gunplay.

Intro:
Destiny 2 is a roller coaster of good and bad. The lore of the universe and gunplay are consistently phenomenal while the content that people pay for is sometimes underwhelming or outright bad (looking at you season of the worthy). I have been playing Destiny as a franchise since the end of its first year and in my experience, I will say that the PVE content is overall excellent and the PVP content is often mediocre.

PVE:
The best aspects of the PVE experience are the endgame content and rewards associated with said content. Raids and dungeons offer challenging content with mechanics that make you think and rewards that will make you feel more powerful in all PVE activities. The harder nightfall tiers offer rewards that are actually worth the time investment, especially with the introduction of nightfall specific weapons (and hopefully armor at some point?). Exotic weapons and armor are also most easily found in these activities, which serve as accessories that when implemented correctly, can fundamentally change the way you play the game.

Both of these avenues of play are fun in their own ways and are highly replayable, but they are difficult to access for anyone who plays the game solo. The in-game looking for group (lfg) feature, guided games, is poorly implemented and unrefined. My one experience using it was a 2 and a half hour wait for a group of people who spoke fluent German but hardly a word of English (they were very nice for the record, but we did not get far into the raid due to the language barrier). I may be an outlier, but there is a reason that the majority of players use third party services to find others to play with. So, if you have friends I could not recommend trying this game out more (it is free to play after all), and if you do not have friends, nor mind the challenges of endgame solo play, then maybe consider investing in an annual expansion. That being said, there is still plenty of PVE content that the solo player can easily access and enjoy, though I would still say that (currently) the endgame PVE content is the best the game has to offer.

PVP:
Oh boy... the Crucible. Like I said in the beginning, the gunplay in Destiny has always been great. I can not describe how satisfying it can be to take a god roll weapon that you earned and kick some other guardian's ass with it. But man the PVP in this game is a mess. Across virtually all PVP modes currently, there is an emphasis on ability usage over gunplay, to the point that gunplay is often an afterthought. The infamous Stasis subclass (which is a fantastic addition to PVE) dominates the crucible and makes anyone who chooses to focus on basic first person shooter concepts, such as aim and positioning, look like a masochist. Who cares about using a primary weapon when I can use a grenade that pulls you out of cover (which you think would be a good position), slows you to the point that you can hardly move or use your weapons, and eventually freeze you for an effortless kill? Did I mention that this is all done with a GRENADE? Bungie has come out and said that they will be continuing to monitor and adjust Stasis going forward, but the current PVP experience feels awful in its blatantly overpowered state. Also, special weapons with quickdraw are a bad idea for PVP, but that's a much less major issue.

Of course, there's the big thing: cheaters. Again, Bungie is trying its best to address this problem but it is a major, long term issue that will continue to plague high-end PVP content for months (hopefully not years) to come. I personally, do not play Trials of Osiris (the "pinnacle" of PVP content) often, but it has suffered the most from the cheating problem. In its current state, I would not recommend this gamemode to the average player and even exceptional players will struggle to deal with people who literally cannot miss. (Once more, specific changes to this playlist are coming in the future but it kinda just sucks in its current state).

Conclusion:
Destiny 2 is a good game that is sometimes fantastic and sometimes awful. It is not for everyone but it offers a fun and engaging PVE experience with a PVP experience that often still feels like it is in beta. I am optimistic for the future of this franchise, so give it a shot if you can get around some of its weaknesses. After all, you can play it for free.
