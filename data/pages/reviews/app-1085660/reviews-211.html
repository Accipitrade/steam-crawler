\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayers who are seeking a free to play game will be sadly disappointed, the free experience is more akin to a demo. The majority of the content and features are locked behind season passes, expansion packs and deluxe edition exclusives. I don't think this type of monetisation should be encouraged, so I hesitate to support games that adopt it.

Newcomers to the franchise, like myself, will find themselves overwhelmed by the amount of options and game systems present. Many of them are unnecessary, and complicate the game without providing any meaningful depth. There are so many types of currencies and upgrade materials, some with similar names but different functions. For example, 'ascendant alloys' and 'ascendant shards' have completely different uses despite the similar name and icon. This unclear approach to nomenclature extends to your character stats, where "discipline" affects your grenade cooldown for some weird reason; and "intellect" affects how quickly you charge your ultimate. I have over 100 hours in the game, and I still don't have a strong grasp on some of the game's systems.

That said, the art design is absolutely awe inspiring. The environments in this game are beautiful in a surreal and otherworldly way. The same goes for the weapon and armour designs, and the multitude of customisation options. This is in stark contrast to the story, which -in spite of a few good voice acting performances- is dull and devoid of emotional stakes. Considering how much care and detail was given to the visual presentation, I wish the same amount of attention was given to the narrative, which is too often an afterthought.

The gunplay is unrivalled in how satisfying it feels. Everything from gun sounds, to enemy death animations are top notch. I never tired of seeing enemies disintegrate into glowing particles after an energy blast, or get ragdolled by an explosion. The game shines in its higher difficulty PvE content, nightfalls and raids. It requires thought to be put into your build composition and a greater degree of cooperation. On the other hand, the PvP and gambit are plagued with balancing issues, it's mostly build and weapon reliant, rather than on skill and teamwork.

This isn't exactly a completely negative review, as there is a lot to love in the game. I just felt the need to put this out there as a comprehensive heads-up to newcomers on what to expect.\t\t\t\t\t
