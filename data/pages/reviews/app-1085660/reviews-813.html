\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a great looter shooter. It feels good, it plays well, and you have good control over your character. If you want a game to turn your brain off for a few hours and grind something out, Destiny 2 is perfect for you.

I can't recommend it, though. It's become an absolute money sink in every sense of the word. Destiny 2 is still the only game I can think of that has DLC/expansion packs that expire. If you don't fork out an extra $60+ dollars every couple months (expansions, season passes, what are essentially subscription fees to play new temporary content that is required to actually play the game), then you just won't have fun. If you ever enter Crucible (Destiny 2's PvP mode), you will absolutely be stonewalled by people who have paid this month's requisite $60, simply because they're given unbelievably broken stuff.

Then three months later, all of that stuff you decided you would buy because you like the game -- everything you spent dozens upon dozens of hours grinding to hit that perfect roll or rare drop -- is worthless. Replaced with some new, equally as boring grind. Asking that you pay another $60 to get the initial "be better than everyone else" kit, followed by the same mission over and over and over and over again to get to the "I'm luckier better than the other people who paid for this season" feeling. Only for it to all happen again in another 8-12 weeks.

If that's the kind of thing you like, Destiny 2 will be a game you can sink 100s of hours in to. I've sunk so much time into it from back before they went this route, and it was incredible. But every time I think about playing it again, I just think about how in order to even be considered for anyone's party to do a raid or a nightfall (essentially challenging versions of missions that give better rewards), I would have to sink another 200 hours into the game to catch back up.

It's a chore that you pay a AAA price to do every couple months.\t\t\t\t\t
