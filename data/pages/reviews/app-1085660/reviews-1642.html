\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had a lot of fun playing this game and spent a good sum on myself and others. But, sadly - as will probably be the case with many others - have lost more and more interest with the news that content, locations, campaigns, etc. that I enjoy going back to would be dropping off the map.

When you buy games with a campaign, it doesn't disappear. you can go back and play it whenever you like. I paid for the original game when it came out, played the beta, and purchased the first two expansions, and then purchased Forsaken. And now those stories, locations, and everything that was, is going to just disappear to make room for new stuff.

That's called making a new game. I didn't sign on to trade the Destiny 2 that I purchased in for Destiny 3.

What Bungie is saying here to me is, "Buy the new expansions or don't play. And the longer you wait, the less you'll recognize this game when and if you do come back."

I don't think I can get on board with that.

As an example, I'll mention Cayde 6. He was a major part of the core game, though mainly comic relief, and the first two expansions, then died in Forsaken. Before long, -going by Bungie's plans,- he won't exist at all. They took this character that so many came to love and snatched him from us, but hey. That's how life goes, right? You lose people. But we lose one more expansion to new content, because Bungie doesn't want to pay the upkeep of keeping it all online, and he won't have existed at all. You can't take a beloved character away, then erase him/her as though they never existed. But bungie has proven time and again that they don't listen to fans. They don't care what we think. Unless, of course, we agree with them. Who actually liked that game collecting motes of darkness in multiplayer battle against monsters? I can't even remember what it was called at the moment. If that tells you anything. But I heard a lot of people complaining about it, and saw a lot of people saying they only did it as a grind to get gear and weapons. Yet, when you heard Bungie Devs talking about it, you'd have thought it was the next best thing to Free stacks of real gold on your doorstep every morning for life.

And apparently all the weapons and gear we had are going to be useless soon, if they aren't already. No, Bungie is what amounts to a bunch of Frat boys, playing their own game, impressed by their own reflections, and if you don't agree with what the devs personally, you aren't important. Everything you had will be useless, all the stuff you liked is going to disappear. It sure sounds as though Bungie doesn't care at all about us or what we may have worked for in this game. How much we may have played already? Pfft. Who cares? They don't.

How COULD they fix all of this, -if they actually cared to?
1. Allow the old content to be available in single player offline mode. You'd have to download it to access it, but you could still play the old campaigns. And it wouldn't have to be maintained server-side. Maybe only for the ones who bought it originally, and the ones who were signed up and playing when it was originally available, since they made it free.
2. Allow players to connect to each other via matchmaking systems for the old multiplayer content, which would be available to download also. Possibly as a secondary option.

But you don't just take away something that people paid for. People who supported your company and helped you get where you are, who you then crapped on by making the game free to play. Gamers support your product from day one, and then eventually lose all that they paid for? All the stuff they enjoyed? And what they did pay for becomes valueless and then disappears anyway.

Taking the content we paid for back is tantamount to theft. I don't recall there being any info presented at the time saying that at any moment Bungie might just snatch all of the content back, give us a totally different set of content... Sorry. Thanks for playing. Hope you like this new stuff, otherwise Eff Off.

There's something to be said for making a live world that evolves and changes and having that reflected in the game's content. But this is obviously not something they thought of in the beginning, and we were not made aware of it then. had we been, I may not have spent the money.\t\t\t\t\t
