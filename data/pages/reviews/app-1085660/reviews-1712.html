\r\n\t\t\t\t\t1500 hours on the Steam version alone. Been playing since the beta. I've bought the base game, the DLC, and even the premium currency. I don't regret a single minute or dollar spent on this game. It has its ups and downs, half the time it seems like the devs are intentionally trying to piss off the community. The Traveler only knows what they're trying to do with Crucible (PvP), but if I had to say whether it was net positive or net negative from launch I'd say they're going in a positive direction and at the time I'm writing this, the game as a whole seems like it's only going to get a lot better.

And this is just the game. Bungie itself is full of wonderful people all the way up to the current CEO (check the news). How much money have they raised for charity? How much support have they given to their streamers, not just the big ones. Don't even get me started on the community. I've never seen a community of such needlessly helpful people. I met a guy once who sherpa'd (guided) an entire family of widely varying ages through one of the raids... FOR NEARLY 24 HOURS. Once you make yourself a part of the community, you'll really feel like a part of the community.

Don't let negative news and seasonal slumps *shudders at the thought of Season of the Worthy* dissuade you. Bungie is very good at fixing only one problem and almost never on the first try but all in all I'd have to say that I'm excited to see what they'll do next. I am very much committed to their universe and the community they've fostered. I can't imagine putting 1500 hours into any other game. Next up is 3k.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really great game once you get used to the MMO mechanics! For people who are skeptical you must give it a try. it is really fun and the combat is superb which is what you can expect from the makers of COD. The game can be really grindy but that's how MMO's are so you need to get used to it and just take quest and stuff a step at a time. There is a big learning curve if you never played an MMO before. This game is great with friends and even solo even though the game is meant to be played with others and you'll miss out on stuff without a crew. The graphics are phenominal in terms of sci-fi space adventure like and I have had fun so far and will update when I have more playtime.

-1 point for being too grindy sometimes
-1 point for guns being tied down to different seasons, about to be binned guns, and expensive DLC
-0.5 points for not turning guided games into a great matchmaking system for all people to play with randoms

Overall I rate this game a 7.5 and recommend this to everyone who has the time and money\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic Game. It finally feels like Destiny again.

Pros:
-The pvp is very well balanced and there are only a couple of weapons that are too powerful but are manageable regardless. The -game is gorgeous and runs incredibly well.
-content updates are satisfying.
-DLC pricing is reasonable
-Story fantastic and the lore is deep an expanding. In game text is also interesting and full of information
-PVE is satisfying and loaded with content.

Cons:
-Peer to Peer PVP is showing it's age, very dated, laggy, and mysterious
-Cheaters are rampant on the PC release and the game desperately needs some form of anti-cheat.
-dedicated pvp servers are needed and bungie has not announced plans since the game was built on P2P architecture a long time ago.
-season pass ends at rank 100 and minimally rewards you as you continue (planned changes in xpac)
-small tweaks to scavanger perk needed in pvp to stop someone from getting massive amounts of sniper or grenade launcher ammo
-more diversity/surprises needed in public events

I want to expand on this last con as the public events are overall very satisfying although the old ones can be defeated so quickly that they barely matter anymore. Aside from scaling up old events, new events need more variety. Chaning the boss weekly isn't enough to keep the events fresh. They are fun and satisfying but would be exponentially better if there were chances for other things to happen like:
-sudden spawning of another faction fighting the existing faction (hive assault the fight we are having with the fallen)
-surprise mini bosses
-mechanics to grant additional boons for allies if a bonus enemy is defeated or something like this
-Certain weapons make the harder enemies too easy. Add a couple more champions to increase the need to kill adds on boss fight.

I say this because the events are predictable and are only tough when you are not at recommended power level. When you are on power level the content becomes too easy and becomes more grindy. This event can be run repeatedly for 1-2 hours before you're too fatigued to keep doing it. This appears to be a trend among seasonal public events, hopefully this improves with the new xpac.\t\t\t\t\t
