\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's fun. Plain and simple, the game is fun. It's fun doing things solo, it's fun doing things with friends. It's an MMO with a PvE world, and PvP sections reserved to special place for the people who like to bang there head against wall as a 867 light level guardian teabags your corpse after taking the easy route and using a SMG that requires 0 skill and just holding down the left mouse button!

... fun times.

The quests often times go through the same "Do thing X amount of times, deliver an indigo popsicle to a hidden area three miles away from the starting location, and do the hokie-pokie" syndrome that plague a lot of MMO's, but that doesn't happen all the time. Many times I find myself quite surprised, running through unique areas, beautiful landscapes, and fantastic new challenges seemingly out of nowhere.

Nothing in the game, I have found at least, is so bad that you never want to play it. Even when it's frustrating, it's still engaging enough that I want to keep trying over and over and over again until I win. And when it's dull, you can put it on the back burner, do something fun for 4 hours, and turn around and it's completed while you weren't even looking.

PvP is varied enough that any mode is worth my time to learn, master, and destroy (though I'm still struggling with the first, second, and third part of that statement). Gambit is a ton of fun, Crucible is a ton of fun, it's just fun.

Destiny 2 is just fun.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(Thisw review was written by someone who left D2 after the first expansion and has returned to Shadowkeep. I have purchased that expansion, but not Forsaken)

Destiny 2 is hard to review. Some of this games individual parts are... amazing. The shooting feels great, the weapon variety (during most versions of the game... this wasnt the case on D2 release) is terrific and there is incentive to grind for that perfect weapon with the top tier rolls, the world looks great and the ambience is on point. BUT.... Bungie have been incapable of crafting a decent story in this amazing world. The narrative is discombobulated and barely follows itself. This has not changed since launch and it is legitimately frustrating to see the team fail to create an interesting narrative. And if you are a deep progression minded MMO player.... you're gonna find this game extremely repetitive. The end-game pretty much consists of repeating the exact same group of 5 activities for a weekly cap and thats it. There arent many strikes (dungeons). Every area is re-used assets (this really hits home of youve played D1).

Quickly while Im at it: Dont get the expansions. They arent worth it,. not nearly. Its basically 50$ for 3 hours of campaign, 2 new strikes and one more raid, none of which are challenging.

I suggest getting f2p, enjoy that for what its worth and come back to it once they add content or make the current expansions legacy. Untill Bungie releases a true, full fledged expansion pack with new locations and months of new gameplay, Destiny is not worth money.\t\t\t\t\t
