\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gunplay is solid, the endgame PvE loop is solid *if* you either have a clan of reliable fireteam members OR are comfortable using the out-of-game LFG tools and discord. I'm not great at PvP, but that's not my main reason for playing, but PvP players seem to enjoy it.

Joined with Shadowkeep's New Light experience. They've definitely improved the Beyond Light New Light experience in terms of introducing you to all the vendors and activities to get new F2P players on board. Anything beyond the core playlists basically requires the current annual expansion and season to enjoy (i.e. *All* the current raids, until Vault of Glass re-releases.) This was not the case before they vaulted all the year one content.

As of writing, there's still no real onboarding to mid-tier play, when you start caring about armor and weapon mods and good stat/perk rolls (which differ for PvE vs PvP), which are critical to keeping up in more advanced activities. I don't expect handholding at the top end; learn raids, nightfalls, trials, etc on your own. However, growing from New Light to a decent mid-tier player either needs a lot of Sherpa guidance or a lot of time on Reddit/Youtube/Guides, as those systems are opaque. I suspect there is player dropoff here because there's just no real in-game explanation about how the guy you matchmake with can completely tank and 2-shot the strike boss and drop orbs of light all the time versus the fresh New Light graduate who's dumping entire clips into regular mobs and constantly getting revived. Same with Prime/Powerful/Pinnacle gear drops not being well-explained to newbies.\t\t\t\t\t
