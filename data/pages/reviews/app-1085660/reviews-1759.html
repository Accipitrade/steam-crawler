\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would definitely recommend Destiny 2 to any prospective MMO players or any gamer for that matter. The game has so many different elements and modes that appeal any facet of player whether you enjoy mindlessly shooting at enemies in a free-play environment, completing challenging activities with a group of your friends, or competing in PvP matches to test your skills against other players. Whatever you want, Destiny as a franchise probably has it in some way shape or form. Of course, it doesn't come without it's share of issues as any game does. Some of these issues are going to be turn offs for casual players and some are turn offs for competitive players, but for me the strength of Destiny lies in it's ability to connect people in any sort of activity and work towards a common goal.

Lets first start of with what I think are Destiny's biggest strengths
Multiple facets of play; As I said before, there are lots of different ways to progress your character through different PvE and PvP activities which reward you accordingly for the time you put in. This allows players to choose how they get to the endgame and are not limited to playing one sort of way to level up.
Detailed Armor + Weapon Systems; There are plenty of ways to build your character and ways to accrue stats to improve ability timing, super timing, mobility, resilience etc. You could make a viable endgame build that revolves around you punching enemies to gain a shield, and it will work in virtually any situation.
Enjoyable Endgame Content; Now the endgame content in Destiny could also be looked at in a different light, but I think that for people who are "completionists" or are veteran MMO players, they will see the enjoyment in playing Destiny's endgame. For the casual player, the raid content may be out of the question in some scenarios, but getting a group of friends together to tackle puzzles and different boss encounters is something that Destiny does very well in my opinion and the end results leave your team feeling accomplished. There is also endgame PvP where you can head into a highly competitive 3v3 elimination game mode and see how many wins you can rack up in a row to get rewarded more and more.
Satisfying PvP; For people looking for fun PvP, Destiny offers unique gameplay that works fairly well in the grand scheme of an FPS. There are tons of different loadouts that you can take into PvP and the point at which you are in the games story/ DLC content doesn't matter. Playing in any PvP gamemode besides the endgame Trials of Osiris or the rotating event Iron Banner modes are not power-gated and do not have any sort of requirement to participate or to do well. This is not without saying that leveling up your character and obtaining new weapons won't improve your performance though.
Community; In my experience with the 2,000 hours I have on console and the 1,000 I have on PC, I'd venture to guess that most of the Destiny community is mostly very positive and engaged with the game. I have been in raid and PvP groups with people of all ages and races which has been quite a great experience in a video game that I haven't found anywhere else. People on the Destiny 2 PC LFG Discord generally specify if they are looking for hardcore players or are just looking to have a good time which allows for people to choose their experience accordingly. For the most part, you are able to join a ground with no experience and have someone "sherpa" you through a raid and teach you the ropes, which I have done myself a multitude of times and it has always been a fun experience to take someone through a large activity like a Destiny raid.
Rewarding Progression; The amount of time you play is directly proportional to how much more powerful you get, no gimmicks, no ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. There is variation to what TYPE of loot you get and the armor stat rolls and weapon perk rolls. The amount of loot sources is immense enough for you to be able to get hours of playtime in more than one area of play and be rewarded accordingly for the difficulty of the activity performed.
Now lets talk about the bad (Yikes there's a lot)
Graphics; Now my friend told me about this and I hadn't thought about it honestly but this game literally has been on the same engine since Xbox 360.... which is pretty sad. There isn't a good dynamic lighting system and I had been pretty blind to how terrible the game looks compared to some of the new titles coming out recently. Regardless of if you play the game on ultra or not, it is absolutely not comparable to something like
Bless Online which uses Unreal Engine 3 where character details and shading are top notch.
Super grindy; Destiny is an MMO, which means that there is going to be a lot of playtime required to have top level characters. The way that Destiny requires players to level up is super monotonous and can become very, and I can't stress this enough, VERY boring. You end up doing the same few strikes every time you log on every weekly reset to finish your sources of higher tier gear. For casual players this isn't too much of an issue, but for anyone who plays the game a decent amount, you end up doing the same strikes, the same bounties, the same flashpoint missions, and the same heroic story missions over and over and over until your brain literally explodes from boredom. The way to relieve this is by doing the endgame content like the raids and the higher tier activities, but it's hard to do them with your friends because they aren't a high enough power level yet and you're left to LFG for some more players (which isn't inherently bad). The power grind is super slow for new players and even returning/ veteran ones, and it becomes difficult to stay attentive to the game when every day you get on and do the same things.
No Real Story; The only available story in Destiny is through the very short (but albeit pretty good) missions and the grimoire/ lore. The lore is actually very interesting and very fleshed out, and there is so much to the Destiny universe that would be amazing to play through.... but it isn't in the game. The lore content is available to read in game in the lore tabs, but the casual player is probably never going to read them, and they would much rather experience awesome story driven content in game. The writers spent so much time creating such an amazing universe, but Bungie seems to be more concerned with stretching out and recycling the same content from Destiny 1 instead of working to create new fresh worlds for us to play in.
RECYCLED CONTENT; We have had the same 4 enemy types since the initial release of Destiny 1 in the Hive, Cabal, Vex and Fallen with the Taken being added as a new enemy variant towards the end of the Destiny 1 cycle. I'm sure I'm not alone in saying that we are so bored of shooting the same 5 types of enemies for 7 years straight. There are so many different races in the Destiny universe that Bungie could use like the Ahamkara, the Arkborn, the Psions, or they could bring back some of the races destroyed by the hive etc. But it seems that Bungie enjoys throwing out more seemingly effortless DLC and re-skins of current enemy types in order to maximize profit without putting in extensive effort. It's so starkly different from something like Warframe which is also free and is constantly being updated with new ways to play and new places to explore. The main point of contention is that Warframe developers at least put effort into creating NEW content and in it's own right is a commendable way to franchise and promote their game.

Overall, I think that Destiny 2 is an amazing experience that casual and competitive/ hardcore gamers should definitely give a try. All of the problems with the game will hopefully be fixed by the developers eventually, and maybe it will be able to reach the potential that it has.\t\t\t\t\t
