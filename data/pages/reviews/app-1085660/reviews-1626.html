\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've always been a big fan of the "space shooter by Bungie" swath of games, Destiny 2 being the latest in a long lineage of very good games. For all it's flaws, Destiny 2 is, at it's core, a really enjoyable game. There is a special kind of magic that Bungie has put into this game to make you truly feel like an immortal space warrior, and so much care put into the special details like the way each and every gun feels and sounds unique. Destiny 2 can be actively hostile to new players given you're dropped into a deep, mechanic and flavor-rich world with nary an explanation--but for as much frustration and confusion as this can create in new players it provides a pretty lengthy, rich world to continue to explore over time. Destiny 2 may have a player versus player experience, but it doesn't lean as fiercely competitive as some other popular PvP-centric games, and the PvP is fun (despite some of the complaints about aim-assist and cheating); Destiny 2, however, shines in its PvE experience. There are so many interesting, unique places to explore and truly unique components across the various dungeons, worlds, and raids.

There are definitely some specific types of players I wouldn't recommend Destiny 2, but if you're a semi-casual person who like PvE content a bit more than PvP content but generally prefers a good mix of all of that with great base mechanics/feel and a lore-rich world Destiny should be a good fit for you.\t\t\t\t\t
