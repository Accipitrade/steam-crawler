\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is an MMO before it's a traditional Looter-Shooter. It has frequent updates at reasonable pricing, but it relies primarily on repeatable content in the form of Strikes (Dungeons), Crucible (PvP), and Raids. This means once you've played for a while, you will progress by doing the same missions repeatedly. If that's a dealbreaker, well then there you go, it's a dealbreaker.

The Gunplay is excellent, and there's a huge variety of interesting loot to chase. The build-crafting is very absorbing and allows for a vast array of different playstyles.

The World and Lore are utterly unique and very absorbing, especially if you're a Halo fan. The Art Design, Graphics, Music, and Sound design are all phenomenal, although that's significantly more subjective. The characters and writing range from Somewhat Interesting to Aggressively Bland.

I would not consider Destiny 2 a properly free to play game, there's simply too much content locked behind DLC for that to be the case. It's essentially an extremely generous free trial with no time limit a-la FFXIV. They are adding more F2P content over time however. It also doesn't have a subscription fee (no the season pass does not count).

Like many MMOs, Destiny has an insane amount of info for new players to absorb and it's not very good at introducing it to them, at least beyond the very basics. If you have a friend who plays the game I highly recommend having them show you the ropes.\t\t\t\t\t
