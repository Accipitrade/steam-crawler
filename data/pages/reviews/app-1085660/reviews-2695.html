\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent game.

A brief acknowledgement for the fact Bungie decided to take a game the corporate morons tried to ruin, like everything else they touch, ponied up their own cash, bought a semi-broken game back and continue to work hard to make it great.

The game mechanics are great. I love jumping around with constant action almost everywhere you go. Weapons are great, everyone will have their own preferences, but I find myself enjoying weapons I didn't think I liked every time I get a quest to grind random %'s out of who knows what for some odd quest.
Story line is excellent for what this is. I was totally engaged for every bit, no skipping. It's not over the top, it doesn't waste your time. We're not going on any feelings quests, there is just enough to keep you entertained between gunning down alien threats or opposing guardians. Cayde! There is a load of things to do wherever you go. If you're having a tough time getting into the game, just some random place, walk around, and quests of some sort will fall in your lap, (if you click em). The world design and visuals are superb. Voice acting, fantastic. Companion app is a nice touch, although I haven't had any luck with fire team match making yet.

It's not all good. Please understand, although some of the following annoys the hell out of me, I still can't stop playing.
The menus/UI are not intuitive. Quests and bounties share space, you max out at 63 (or close to it), between both. As a new player, and a thorough one that likes to collect and conquer every quest, not being able to fill up is rough. Many of the quest lines take a LONG time to complete, so if you load up early you won't be able to queue that many bounties each week. There is no hand holding with quests. It seems some will give you an idea of where to go next, most are puzzles to be solved by people with way more time than I have. I frequently find myself playing with a guide up on one screen.
Explanations for types of weapons, ammo, armor, element crap, stats, are all kind of lacking. I still end up looking things up outside the game to figure things out.
PvP: No Crucible yet, but Gambit is a blast. Yes, the 80hr/week crowd is here too, but not as bad as most games out there. The community here seems pleasant, which is a nice change of pace.
End game content is unreachable without friends. No one chats, or I'm missing it. Clan finder? Using the companion app to find a fireteam for end game content is... weird. I've been able to assemble a few teams, but nothing ever ends up happening. I'm still trying to figure out end game activities. This part of gaming was a lot easier when I had little to no responsibilities in life. Finding matching schedules and coordinating all that crap... been there, done that, more like work than a game. I wish there was a queuing system that actually worked for solo players looking to raid. Nerf my rewards, I don't care, I just want to see all the content.

I didn't play before Bungie got their company back. So I don't have any of the memberberry angst. As a new player in Destiny 2, despite a challenging learning curve, I'm having a blast playing this.\t\t\t\t\t
