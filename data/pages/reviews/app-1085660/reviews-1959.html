To compare this game with Destiny 1 would be unfair. Destiny 1 was a game well before its time. Destiny 1 you could spend countless hours on and never get bored. This game is the same, but the repetitive nature of the game can be a bit draining once you come to your senses. The raids do not compare to Destiny 1, although they are still fun to complete. If you are not a fan of animals then this game is not for you. The amount of error messages you will get consecutively will make you begin wondering if it is something wrong on your end. It is not. The servers are really that bad. The flurry of swear words following seeing "error code: beaver" will turn any man into a sailor. I wish I could recommend this game, but if you are not prepared to deal with the trash servers and the constant grind then you might as well not play. Do not get sucked into this rabbit hole. The leveling up process is fun at first but once you hit the level cap (which does not take long at all) the game gets very boring and repetitive. Oh and did I mention that the one PvP mode in the game that is worth playing is littered with hackers? Otherwise, the community is not too toxic. The grind is fun (at first) but the replay-ability is not there like in Destiny 1 and for these reasons I cannot recommend this game. Spend your time doing something better... please.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got to admit, this game doesn't care too much about new players and you'll have a very rough time getting in the game. I actually deleted the game 12 hours in because I've spent most of the time figuring stuff out. You really need either to find very good guides online or have someone teach you everything. Other than that, the game is pretty good. The story is nice and stuff (if you are into Star Wars / Matrix / Futuristic theme). Also, make sure you are patient because the mini-map guidance system is a joke and you'll find many errors. You'll be told to go a certain way, which is not actually correct.

Public Events, quests and so on are quite interesting and there is no pay to win aspect to the game. Yes, there is a "pay to skip" aspect, but only if you've already completed things on your first character. It boosts your power to level 950, but you'll still need to do the main campaign to unlock weekly challenges. It pretty much takes away 20 hours of gameplay that you've done already.

PvP is quite nice but can get annoying since this game si not PvP based and the matchmaking system might team you against players that can one shot you, while you deal barely any damage, but there is a mechanic that prevents such issues from affecting you by granting you Rank Experience even if you lose and you can promote to a Division, while losing, but at a slower rate. Also, if you are lucky, your teammates can carry you.

You'll have to train around with the jump mechanic, since it takes forever. Also, it is an important aspect to the game as there are many jump puzzles that you have to complete.

Make sure you have a Wiki, a Guide or simply Google handy when you get into some tougher quests. Also, there are many quests that are available to you, but you can't complete for various reasons (it got me to quit, really quick).

There are plenty of weekly and daily activities for you to complete. You should be able to complete all of them if you have around 1-2 hours per day to play.

Because I'm not that much into futuristic themes (add +1 point) + the failed attempt at a proper minimap + the horrible FTUE (First Time User Experience), I give it a 7/10.\t\t\t\t\t
