\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a casino with gunplay. A thinly veiled grind fest of reused content and player sabotage designed to suck time and money out of you so you can be rewarded with a higher number. Blatantly pay to win. Bungie drops new "content" with hilariously overpowered gear at the same time nerfing what was most OP last season so people are compelled to buy the new stuff to stay competitive. And the cycle continues all while the destiny community seems to be completely unaware. This is a game that prioritizes RNG. Skill and thinking outside the box do not matter in comparison to perfect rolls and fully ground out gear. Builds in this game are very simple minded compared to more traditional RPGs, but they are incredibly difficult to assemble because you make builds through mods and weapons NOT through your character's abilities. The necessary pieces to make builds are largely RNG based and time/pay locked. So in other words, this game takes "the rich get richer" to the maximum extreme. new players must spend hundreds of hours acquiring necessary materials, grinding for roles, acquiring mods, and leveling up before you can even think about playing any kind of endgame content PvE. Don't even attempt to enjoy PvP if you like competitive gaming. You will get steamrolled by the low skill players with OP gear. Not to mention the game insists on "balancing" PvP and PvE in the same sandbox, meaning PvE builds are nerfed to make them less OP in PvP, so nothing can ever have crazy or exciting effects. This makes build crafting fairly boring as you are effectively just increasing damage and defense rather than changing how you play the game. It is sad because this game's core is extremely polished (not surprising since it's basically Halo) but every game system around it is predatory.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a great game overall. It has some of the smoothest gunplay mechanics in the business to date, the dynamic skill tree and the ability to earn gear and take it with you wherever you go (PvE, PvP, Gambit, Raids) is a huge bonus in my opinion. I like the lack of restriction as to what quests you may pursue in which order and what kind of endgame freedoms you have, but it can be somewhat frustrating as a new player to come into this world at this point. (I've had more than a few friends lose interest due to lack of explanation and direction for new players.) That said, the players who stick with the game through the campaign missions and make it to endgame content will find what feels like a whole new game waiting for them on the other side. I can't say whether that's a good or bad thing for the game, but its accurate. Now that the game is free to play I think it's for sure worth a try for anyone who hasn't played yet, and I'd be willing to bet a large portion of those people would justify purchasing DLC by the end of their base game playthrough.\t\t\t\t\t
