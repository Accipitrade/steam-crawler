\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been playing Destiny 2 since it first came out and I have to say, it's been one hell of a ride.
This game has certainly had it's ups and downs with each update: Some seasons it can be the most rewarding experience of slaying hordes of enemies, and blowing up raid bosses for epic loot that one could partake in, however others it feels like a waste of electricity to even have the game running. Yet even in the worst of times for this game, one thing still stands out: The world-building.

The Bungie developer team might not be the greatest of developers, but they certainly have done an excellent job creating an immersive and truly epic tale for the Destiny universe. Even if you have never played the game, you can read through the lore books (They sell physical books) and be completely immersed in the story.
Now I am not saying the story is perfect in it's entirety, as there are some pieces that just don't compare to the rest, for instance the current seasonal story-line (Season of the Splicer) is quite boring and overall disappointing, but can you really expect a seasonal filler content to even compare to the legacy that expansions like Forsaken and Shadowkeep have left behind? And that is not even considering the immense magnificence of the story-lines from the original Destiny 1 such as 'The Taken King' and 'Rise of Iron'.

Now this may seem like an amazing game considering how I have described it, this is not the case.
Destiny 2 has many apparent flaws that are hard to overlook, for instance I will talk a bit about 'The Crucible', I don't want to go into PvE content as a majority of the the issues with that is that some content can be unrewarding, and typically has nothing to do with the actual content.
So:
The Crucible is the main PvP activity of Destiny 2 (And is a major component in Destiny as whole), where players can compete against each-other in a variety of game-modes separated between Competitive and Non-Competitive playlists. The Crucible has many issues which can make it quite un-enjoyable at times, I will name a few of them:

- Cheaters
This really explains itself. It is nearly impossible to not come across at least one individual using third-party hacks in any given day of playing Competitive game-modes. This typically wouldn't be such an excruciating issue if it weren't for that when you lose a Competitive game you lose Competitive rank (Don't even get me started on Trials of Osiris).

- Bad Tick rate
The Tick rate (The speed at which a server processes updates, think of it as server side ping) is phenomenally awful within The Crucible creating kill-trades and 'Ghost shots', which can force you to lose a game simply because the server decided your bullet was made out of rubber.

- Unbalanced equipment
Balancing weapons and abilities within The Crucible has always been an issue ever since the beginning of time, that is just the intrinsic trait of having a PvP activity with such an immense variety of weapons to choose from. However, sometimes certain abilities and weapons are just too good (I am looking at you DMT and 120HCs), making it so that you have to use them otherwise you are very simply at a disadvantage, which can completely take the wonder out of having such a broad selection of weapons to choose from. The cool new laser rifle you got from a long and grueling quest is never going to compete with the all-powerful meta.

And of course there are a lot more issues outside of The Crucible, mainly with seasonal content that I wont be mentioning, but despite all this, I will still defend Destiny 2, and even go as far as recommending it. I do not recommend Destiny because I believe it is a perfectly curated game that has no problems, but because I know that it isn't, and with that I can look beyond the flaws to see the beautiful potential that this game withholds. I truly hope that someday The Crucible will be perfectly balanced, and that Bungie will finally create an expansion that completely wipes away all the others before with how wonderful it is, but that will take time. If you are willing to see this game through to the end, whether it dies out or achieves greatness, that is your choice, but I know I will certainly be enjoying the ride.

TL:DR
Destiny as of now is not a perfectly curated game, nor is it a perfect PvP shooter.
However despite that the story might be captivating enough make you blind to these glaring issues.
Also I like to eats ants (just not the vicious ants).
