There are now hundreds of dollars worth of campaign missions and other content I can no longer play. When it's the story missions that help new players understand what the hell is going on.

Bungie are moving in a direction that reminds me why I have only played Seasons 10-17 and 21. I don't even play PvP and I am pissed off for that community. The excuses are insufferable about why more PvP maps aren't added. When it is clear as day that team at Bungie was almost completely moved to work on their new game Marathon.

Seasonal Events are a joke. Solstice is such a waste of the EAZ area and its vertical map height. Festival of the Lost is a shell of what it used to be. The real disrespect comes in offering the Event Cards for $10 USD.

When players pay for not only the overpriced expansions and season passes, all cosmetic content should be available to them through some version of an in-game unlock system. I think Warframe handles this well.

Bringing Cayde back is a cowardly way to try and revamp the player base. Which it seems they are going to do anyways.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo me the gunplay and mechanics of Destiny 2 are first class and I personally enjoy the ongoing story, characters, and even deeper lore of the universe bungie has set out to make. The time to kill on PvE enemies varies, but outside of one shot adds and health gated bosses it is short enough to be snappy without feeling like mowing through a crowd, and long enough to be challenging / engaging without feeling like fighting a bullet sponge. I have not played for too long in relation to the games life but from what I've observed PvP is in a constant state of flux with different weapons and abilities being made meta with every major or seasonal update. From the little bit of PvP I have played I enjoy the speed and time to kill of opponents; noticeably faster than something like Halo but slower than Call of Duty. When it comes to the looter shooter aspect of the game I could take or leave grinding to get the perfect random roll of a weapon or armor piece, but the loadout / build crafting that happens afterwards is something I really enjoy. The customization afforded to you in modern day Destiny 2 with armor mod builds and weapon loadouts gives me a great amount of enjoyment when I finally put together my perfect setup and try it out... not that everyone gets to do that. Destiny 2 is not for new players. If you are not actively playing every expansion, every season, every holiday event, you are missing out. Not "you are missing out on a good thing, come on and play already.", you are missing out and have already missed out and will be worse off in game because of it. The previously stated customization tools are not provided all at once. Until recently, of 70+ armor mods, there was a selection of 2 on a daily rotation (now 4), so rise and grind gamer. Multiple, fully fleshed out locations, raids, weapons, armor sets, and campaigns, full stories have been removed from this live service game with no other way to acquire them now or in the foreseeable future.
