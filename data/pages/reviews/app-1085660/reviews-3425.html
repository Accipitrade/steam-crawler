\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish there was an "It Depends" recommendation. So I'm giving it a questionable yes recommendation. The fact is, if you are looking for an online action-RPG looter-shooter, I don't know of a better game. You have the Borderlands series but that is sort of a joke on purpose and it is a "play to the end" game. Destiny 2 is much more realistic and continuous. But the perpetual nature of Destiny 2, and the struggle to define itself as more of an Action game or RPG, incurs some nasty side-effects. See below for details.

Pros:
+ Destiny 2 continues to be a solid action shooter with enough RPG elements to scratch your RPG itch. It feels great, it shoots great, it runs great on PC. It has some jumping, 3D platforming type content. There are lots of easter eggs and hidden things to explore. I love to play to unwind and just shoot things.
+ I never played the original Destiny. So I think the moon is a great zone with an interesting story line.
+ The promise of interesting new types of content, the Vex invasion, the annual pass changes. But this is mostly a promise, not yet a reality.
+ Some player friendly adjustments. Raised the glimmer (gold) cap. Re-usable weapon mods.

Cons:
- Bungie can't come up with a better way to keep you feeling like you are getting more powerful as you play without breaking the game (i.e. making the difficulty trivial), so be prepared for a significant amount of your advancement being occasionally reset for this sake. I started this expansion at the same "power level" as brand new players... Granted I have an advantage that I have a vault full of gears with perks, but many of those perk's effectiveness were reduced.

- The "infusion economy." The bottom line here is you will not have enough resources to upgrade the power level of your preferred equipment while you rise in power. That forces you to play with equipment you'd prefer NOT to use. This has been a historical problem that was previously fixed, but with this new expansion decided to break it again. And that's just plain stupid. Part of me regrets my purchase for this issue alone. All the other questionable design changes are forgivable, this one is not.

- The RPG/Action and PvE/PvP identity crises. Bottom line up front, it does both domains well but neither great. There is no player trading system. Certain gear's stats and perks gives an unfair PvP advantage, and if you lack those items your skill may not always make up for the difference. There are PvP rewards that are best in class for PvE content, and vice versa. Moreover, Bungie will adjust stats and perks of gear to balance one domain, but accidentally disrupt balance in the opposing domain. I like the diversity of activities both domains provide, but the cost at times feels significant.

- Armor 2-point-whatever. I really don't see the point yet. Just more grinding to get potentially back to the same level as my "god roll" 1.0 drops. This feels more like a "power reset" and "slow down advancement" ploy than a new shiny carrot to chase.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI pre-ordered Destiny 1. It was the last game I ever pre-ordered. They tricked people with their beta. The released game was completely bland and pretty empty. Over the years, I heard they added more and more and fixed it, making it a great game. I never played it again.

Destiny 2 gets announced and my friends went pretty crazy over it. I got it, but my friend gave me a copy, because I didn't trust Bungie anymore. Low and behold, turned out to be a pretty empty game again with still pretty pvp.

After it came here, one friend who was still playing it after all these years, convinced us it was a good game now. We went ahead and gave it a whirl. Sure enough, they added a large amount of stuff and it still has that satisfying gun play it always has.

Now, if you come in without someone to help you or you are completely new, there's so much that is so poorly organized, it could easily cause a meltdown. The quests are awkward and it's not clear what needs to be done first or second or at all honestly. Mod system will have you asking a lot of questions. You'll have to research into the light level thing they have. You'll rake your brain trying to figure out what is good, and what stats do what. The game still needs to be touched up here and there, but without papa Activision standing over them while they work I think Bungie will figure out wtf they need to do to make everything smooth and fun ( as long as it wasn't actually them making all the poor decisions at launch).

It's free so the only reason not to give it a whirl, is because of how much stuff you have to download.\t\t\t\t\t
