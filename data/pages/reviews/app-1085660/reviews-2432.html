\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has the most awful new player experience I have ever seen. It literally throws you in and expects you to find out everything on your own and not only that, it also relies so much on playing with other people that nearly 99% of endgame content is unplayable solo. There is in-game matchmaking for most activities and that's great, but for the really high power level missions, there is none so you are forced to use discord lfg servers and other third party websites you find people. And for the raids.....the raids is a whole different beast. There is so many issues with raids for new players I don't even know where to begin.... I'll start off by saying that there is in-game matchmaking... But guess what? no one uses it! good luck having to use discord or some third-party website again and pray that the people will be willing to teach you the raid. And even if you find a group, you're still screwed because most people in this community does not have the time or patience to teach you everything. They will most likely give you the easiest roles to do in the raid and call it a day. There are sherpas and clan sherpas(which is basically teachers) but they are rare to come across and you will more than likely have to schedule an appointment with them. If you are wondering, "why not just watch guide videos on the raid and just play the raid?" Raids in Destiny isn't something that you can just pickup and play after watching guides. There are so many mechanics and things to remember that most people will need some hands-on experience to really understand how everything works.

So yeah, overall there are so many negatives in this game but if you can perservere and keep not having fun looking up everything on google and youtube for explanations, and deal with all the crap I mentioned above and finally get to the point where you are having fun, this game is amazing. Unfortunately, until they fix the new player experience in this game, I can't reconmend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
