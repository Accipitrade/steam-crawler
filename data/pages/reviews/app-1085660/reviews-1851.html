\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny began under Bungie and Activision, and it was clear they screwed with Bungie's original vision. But unfortunately, even after the split from Activision, it doesn't feel like Destiny has changed at all to me. And don't be fooled by the 3.9 hours on PC. I've played this game extensively on PS4 with friends. And this isn't coming from a noob or someone who hasn't seen everything the games have to offer at this point in time.

To start, the worlds in Destiny are still empty and devoid of purpose beyond being "the maps" to play on. The enemies still spawn in the same spots in the same numbers and use the same crappy AI patterns. The NPC's still have nothing to offer aside from cookie cutter dialogue. And the Strikes, Quests, Raids and other mission types themselves are disgustingly repetitive with the "go here and kill enemies, move here and kill more enemies while the Ghost hacks this door or accesses this data log, move here and kill MORE enemies, move here and kill EVEN MORE enemies while the Ghost AGAIN hacks a door or data log, and then move here and kill a boss" formula that governs literally every mission. There is no variability to speak of. And because of that, every mission can be learned and predicted and done on brain dead autopilot mode after going through them a couple times. And that makes them boring and overly repetitive in a way that doesn't feel earned or in any way respectable.

You'd think that the company which began the Halo franchise could come up with more substantial things to do in their worlds than just killing enemies en mass along a linear path through worlds that look like they SHOULD be ripe for exploration and meaningful storytelling, but are ultimately lacking in any substance whatsoever and are just there to show you different worldly aesthetics.

I can tell Bungie did their best to make the world look like things are happening in the story as you go through the missions and tried to make you feel important to what's going on. But sadly, they fell short of the mark. Whereas every mission in Halo felt like it directly led to the next, the fact you can take breaks and do whatever you want between one mission and the next in the actual story takes away ALL sense of immersion for me. Each mission is separate from the next. And the only boss to this day that I've seen have any personality was the one we were introduced to in Destiny 2, Dominus Ghaul.

But he's literally THE ONLY ONE. Every other boss in every Strike, Quest, Raid, etc, have all been "bigger and badder" versions of generic enemies you've faced before with THE SAME AI BEHAVIOR as their counterparts, and have no story, personality, or other kind of immersive behavior or presence to offer. And even worse: These bosses serve no other purpose than fodder for you and your Strike team. Half of them don't even add anything to the story being told. They're just generic obstacles because "every mission needs a boss at the end." They're empty shells of what COULD be actual characters with stories of their own to tell if Bungie bothered to actually flesh them out and make them feel like they weren't just more of the same.

But there's a reason these bosses feel like fodder: Whatever stories exist about them in "the lore" are following "Tell, Don't Show," rather than "Show, Don't Tell." They tell us the lore and expect us to go "OOOOHHH!! That's COOL!!" But there's a reason "Show, Don't Tell" is the golden rule of story telling and not "Tell, Don't Show," Bungie. Because "Show, Don't Tell" actually works, whereas "Tell, Don't Show" does not.

The reason Ghaul of Destiny 2 felt like an actual boss was because he actually had presence and personality visible in his cutscenes between missions. And his actions spoke just as loud, if not louder, than the words that came out of his mouth. He's the ONLY actual boss "Character" that I've seen in this entire game. And he's the only Boss I actually felt good about killing because I felt I accomplished something and rid the universe of a truly dangerous and misguided monster. Every other boss left me feeling empty because they had nothing to offer than being fodder.

I know Bungie wanted to do something big with Destiny. And the basic building blocks are there. But they just haven't been able to make good on the promises that were made when they set out. Part of it was Activision. I get that. But now that they're not together anymore there's no excuse.

If Destiny wants to be the game it promised to be, it needs to overhaul the entire experience from the ground up. They need to make every enemy you face feel like more than just spawn-kill-loot fodder, and give them more variable AI patterns to mix up every encounter. They need to give the bosses actual presence and purpose in the story, as well as personality shown in cutscenes with them during the Strikes, Raids, etc so they feel alive and responsive to what you're doing and don't just show up like, "Oh, there's the boss! Aaaaaand he's DED!!" And finally, the story needs to have more urgency to it. It should feel like you NEED to play through the entire story without taking random loot-grabbing breaks by farming Strikes. When you can farm and loot grind literally forever between story missions, the story is ultimately inconsequential to the experience. And that's not good game design.

Thank you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is definitely some bad points to this game:
PvP, horribly in-balanced and a grind to become good at (many shotgun, grenade launcher and sniper users <gross!>)
The What the F do I do? factor: honestly can't explain but when you play, there is so much content you willl be lost as hell. Most of it wont be explained either unless you search on the internet. They did not do a good job of making this game easy to jump into. They have improved it, but ask people about what to do if your unsure. Don't let it put you off.

The good points:
Guns: Sounds great, look great.... their just great.
Levels/ design: just next level, everything looks just amazing, really adds scale and if you take time to look at and enjoy the scenery, its something spectacular.
Campaigns: All fun, some better than others but generally all good. Following story is hard, just like understanding the content but you can grasp the general direction and follow it.
Multiplayer: All of it: Gambit, reckoning, crucible, raids, menagerie, planet exploration etc just great, all have their ball aches, all have their triumphs. Its worth working at this game, the overall rewards and feel of the game, makes this one of the best MMO FPS games I have played. The online community can be dumb and toxic sometimes or plain ignorant but again, with all online games that is true. So take it with a pinch of salt, there is always good players out there.

Important Notes:
- Always better with friends if possible
- Internet search content: can sometimes feel limited but there is soooo much depth
- Focus on one thing at a time: there is so much to do but if you want to enjoy, pick something and get involved in it, you don't have to understand all of it right away to enjoy it.
- If PvP give you hate, get over it, all games are the same, spend some time in it, learning levels and getting better. Eventually it plays like any other game online.

Things I may have missed,
- You can be a bloody space wizard! (warlock)
- You can be a trashy OP douchebag (hunter)
- you can be a true man and punch all your enemies and problems (titan)

All content is free (in essence), some stuff can be a grind yes, but you can get all content eventually, but there is so much of it, you wont really notice or care. plus the base game is free so you thats pretty cool.

If your wanting to play this for the first time, you will be lost and confused, but go with the flow and you will have some great fun.

Finally:

So much could be said, its not perfect, but no MMO is, constant bugs and changes means it will always has its ups and downs. overall, having 2 other people to play with is ideal or more, not to say the solo stuff is not worth your time, it is and joining people isn't hard. Raids are the best if you can get 6 people and this game has the guy from firefly in it, sometimes.
This was made by bungie, bungie did Halo, its got some elements that are similar but with much better mechanics all around. Take it easy and enjoy it! its a good game and you can shoot many things.\t\t\t\t\t
