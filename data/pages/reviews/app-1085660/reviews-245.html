\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly this game has so much potential, but its absolutely wasted.
This game is pointless without buying the DLC, so I'll give my thoughts on the DLCs.
Forsaken: Worthless now, don't bother unless you REALLY want to do the Last Wish raid
Shadowkeep: Pretty meh story and the content is no longer relevant, the raid is fun aside from the final encounter, only buy it if it's on discount imo
Beyond Light: I liked the story and environments, but the content is largely irrelevant as it isn't the new thing, buy this on discount as well.
Witch Queen: Massive disappointment, lots of cave nonsense and same usual lame storytelling. Raid isn't fantastic and this DLC is required to do the relevant content.

For the game loop, it runs on a weekly cycle, where you complete activities called "rituals" to bring a "power" level up, this "power" level is just a number that determines if you can play hard content or not. You get loot from all activities, this loot is mainly meaningless and scrap fodder, making the activities feel unrewarding. The activities you complete are mainly boring grinds, with 3 main categories: Strikes, which a short pve levels with nothing particularly interesting; Crucible, pvp which has a whole host of problems that I'm not qualified to explain; Gambit, a pvevp mode that hasn't been properly updated for 2+ years.

The game runs on a battle pass model AS WELL as DLC, these battle passes unlock missions which are relevant to the main story of Destiny and have the usual bonus rewards. You gain levels on this through activities and special challenges and it effectively caps out at lvl100. When a new DLC is released, the content from the previous battle passes and related is unobtainable.

FOMO, this game is BUILT on FOMO. The battle passes being effectively removed when a new DLC releases forces players to keep coming otherwise they will miss out on the important story content (even if half the battle pass stories are filler), which is dripfeeded over weeks, with a final conclusion being released on the last week of the battle pass's season. Every battle pass release also arbitrarily increases the "power" cap by 10, forcing players to grind the boring content again to be able to access the harder content with better rewards.

Monetisation, this game isn't the WORST (I mean c'mon), but it's not fantastic either. There are yearly(ish) DLCs which demand a high price, which comes with the ever present special editions for a "better" offer at a higher price. The battle passes require a purchase to access their relevant content, a smaller transaction, but still required. Then there's the eververse store, a tumour stuck on this game that refuses to die. The battle pass (free or paid) gives you lootboxes every 5 levels or so that require you to open them in the store to get the cosmetic loot inside. Luckily, these lootboxes can't be purchased directly, but the battle pass can be ranked with money. The game does not use direct MTX, instead you buy silver so you don't know how much actual money you are spending, the only purchases that don't use silver are the large scale DLCs. This game is pretty big about cosmetics, and the eververse is mostly cosmetic only, but the items in the eververse store cannot be earned in normal gameplay. There is a "free" route for eververse purchases, using the grindy currency bright dust, that is pretty limited and cycles weekly adding to that feeling of FOMO. The store also has BS offers and has a load of items hidden that MIGHT appear on the daily offer, again FOMO. The non-cosmetic purchases are pretty worthless boosters so that's OK. For actually equipping cosmetics, its pretty good, except the transmog system, this system had to be nerfed by Bungie for how transparently moneygrubbing it was, multiple pointless currencies, long grind and a limit per season on "free" transmog tokens, it's still very grindy with the seasonal limit and the paid options is right on the front of the store to entice players for the "convenience". The DLCs are announced with preorders immediately, and in case you missed it, the GAME ITSELF tells you with a message on login and an annoying icon on the main map. Seriously Bungie, stop asking for my money.

This game is in desperate need of a massive overhaul, but Bungie probably won't. Don't waste your time or money.

No real comment on the story since it lost me a while ago and the *LORE* is friggin boring.\t\t\t\t\t
