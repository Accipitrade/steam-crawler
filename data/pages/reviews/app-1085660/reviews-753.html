\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEither you know what you're getting into and already know/are fine with what current Destiny 2 is, or like me you're coming back after several years away to find that everything you liked about the game is vaulted or gone completely.

Story Time:

I was feeling nostalgic and hoped to start a new game and replay through the original campaign, which I remembered as really good. Little did I know the original campaign wasn't included in the game anymore and the starting point in the game had reset to after one of the DLCs. Ok then, no problem. I can transfer my save file from PS4 with a couple hundred hours in the game and just keep playing the new campaign with my high level gear instead of the newbie gear in a new save file. So I did that, only to find that all my old legendary and exotic gear was completely worthless in the new Destiny 2. All it was good for was dismantling it for legendary shards or fusing it into new armor drops to make them more powerful. It was like starting the grind all over again, but worse because I had to watch as armory of favorited weapons and armor slowly dwindled away to make way for new items that were less useful and didn't look as cool. That feeling of being a futuristic intersellar badass warrior was tainted. Same old enemies, same old gameplay, same old grind, no feeling of satisfaction.

"If you want to make a new story and move on from all the old gear, why not just make Destiny 3" I naively thought to myself. But this was Bungie, post-Activision split. Why spend the money to make a completely new thing when you can overhaul everything about the old thing and piss off the fanbase in one fell swoop?

Anyways, the gunplay is REALLY good. Think COD meets Halo and it actually works. Every gun is satisfying to use. Which is all well and good seeing as how there's not a lot of content to play through. Or, to be specific, there is quite a bit of content to play through, but if you want to get the most out of the game you're going to be doing it ad nauseam. Emphasis on the nausea. Repeating the same daily/weekly/etc. missions just to get a random engram that may or may not contain gear that's high enough level to help you raise your light level gets boring pretty quick. Especially when all you're trying to do is increase your light level to make playing through an event you've done a hundred times a little bit easier.

I enjoy playing this game in bursts... but I can't recommend it to anyone. It's too much of a time sink and the devs don't seem to care about the player base, otherwise they wouldn't vault gear, areas, and DLC content THAT PEOPLE HAVE PAID FOR AND CAN NO LONGER ACCESS!!! Go look at the reviews for the recent DLCs if you want an accurate look at how the playerbase feels about the game lately.\t\t\t\t\t
