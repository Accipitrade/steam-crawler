\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review will probably get lost, but I came from blizzard.net, and strictly speaking quality-of-life, it has improved considerably. There are always a ton of things to do, and the game seems to have become more rewarding for people who don't like to do crucible (PvP) or don't have the initiative to get people for a raid or nightfall. Even solo, you can still have fun with this game, which is a rare thing these days. Even with the base content (which includes everything up to Shadowkeep), you'll still feel like you're in the loop as far as content goes. The addition of armor ornaments separates armor variance from gameplay as you can now customize your armor with various different armor sets. Now, coming from the previous client and as someone who already had progression when the game switched over, I had access to more of them, but you can get a new custom ornament each week (for weapons or armor) with bright dust, which is awarded just for doing weekly challenges and as a rare drop from various activities.

The new armor perk system is nice, it's intuitive, and the resource conversion that is used makes it easy to obtain high end resources as long as you make the small grind to obtain them.

Overall, it's a much better game on steam, and they made a good move by severing ties with Activision.\t\t\t\t\t
