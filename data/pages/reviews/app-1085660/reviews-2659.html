\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter all these years, Bungie still knows how to produce incredibly tight gunplay. The bread-and-butter combat from the HALO series is enhanced by the addition of class perks and supers, adding mobility and power.

SINGLE PLAYER: 5/10 (Average)
Setting: Incredible. Rich and diverse lore, great style and designs for world and aliens. Some of the best looking armor you'll find in a sci-fi setting, period.
Characters: Awful. The Vanguard are unbearable, and each DLC has made them even more unlikable.
Story: Starts off well, but slowly becomes unhinged. Feels inconsistent in tone and stoicism, especially when attempting to deal with comic relief (Ghost, Sagira/Osiris, etc). It's weird how the game tries to hype up the player as the super special savior of all mankind. It leads to the rest of the cast sitting around doing nothing.
Pacing: Super grindy, subsequent DLC are laughably short and full of open-world filler.

MULTIPLAYER: 8.5/10 (Very good)
Combat flows fast and fiercely. Both PVP and raid content is great. Connectivity is occasionally an issue, and Bungie is very capricious when it comes to deciding fireteam sizes for certain activities, but this is generally not a big issue unless you have a ton of friends playing (you won't). Regarding PVP, I can't help feel that there will never be a serious competitive scene as long as the game is hopelessly grindwalled behind misguided MMO systems of hunting randomly rolled guns or lengthy quest-locked pinnacles. Comp PVP should have been loadout based, with players having full access to the same PVP gear (eg: archetypal 110, 140, 150, and 180 rpm hand cannons with pre-locked but customizable perks) that can be balanced easily and consistently instead of having to make massive weapon nerfs that cripple the game every season.

Overall: 7/10 (Good)
For deathmatch fans, this is an above-average game with amazing style held back by many bad decisions. Gameplay loop has serious issues, especially if you don't enjoy PVP or raid content, but still want to get the best guns in the game. It doesn't help that Bungie keeps introducing convoluted systems each season that come and go, leaving no sense of stability to a game that is already pushing three years old.\t\t\t\t\t

From it I realize that these types of games exist to give developers something to do, and give publishers something to sell, and as much of those two things as possible. I've given it more time than I wanted to at times because of the way it's designed, only for the game to do what's right for itself and the developers at other times. I won't bother with another MMO.

Original review at 40 hrs: I hate MMOs. I hate MMOs because they're like videogames with no pants. The systemization is in your face and unapologetic. The game becomes reduced to a series of rote activities for the purpose of expressly filling a space for rote busywork to serve as a function of the monetization and player-interaction systems. This, then, becomes the body of the videogame. Then you combine that aspect with the trend that these games are some of the most-heavily monetized of all videogames and the busywork becomes caustic. They also attract less savoury player types.

There is no art, there is no soul. This high background-level of pants-downiness means that when something crafted or artistic does show up in the game, it sticks out like a sore thumb and is usually somewhat badly incorporated.

One positive thing I'll say for this game is that the moment-to-moment gameplay feels the least like an MMO of any I have played, which, full disclosure, isn't that many. The play style of trundling around an average or above-average-looking open world picking fights with respawning mobs that stand around waiting for you to do that, is transcended here. It all does feel a little more organic. It still feels completely like an MMO.

People praise Bungie's gunplay, but I still think it lacks oomph and feels far too much in the vein of xbox360 CoD style for my liking. It makes sense though because if they didn't invent that style of gunplay they pretty much codified it with Halo.

It's good, it's still an MMO.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThings I like:

-Stunning visuals
-Amazing variety of locations, activities, quests, weapons, armors
-Soundtrack
-Complex, intricate world and storytelling
-Fast paced battles

Things I don't like:

No matchmaking for raids or quests. While I can understand that doing raids with random people could be impossible - there's really no good reason why the game doesn't put you in a group for campaigns or local quests. Playing solo is fine, but playing together is fun. They added "guided" (being able to join clans or guides) system to raids but only for normal difficulties and in a peculiar fashion - removed it from the first raid which they want you try for introduction.

Overcomplicated, almost bizarre resource and items system. There's just way too many different resources, tokens and currencies. Obviously, you will figure it out eventually, but I don't see any reason for complicating both cosmetic and gear systems so much. Crystals that turn into other crystals that turn into boxes that turn into items, 4 different currencies, every zone having different pve tokens and on top of that there's a smorgasbord of pvp tokens as well. Hated token system in Warcraft and hate it here.

PvP being forced onto player. While it is avoidable - a lot of "introductory" quests require you to participate in PvP. On top of that, rewards for those quests are better than in PvE alternatives. In fact - after playing more, I can say that PvP is being SHOVED into player's face on every ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ corner. 8 out of 10 best weapons are tied to extensive grind in PvP, ALL of the top tier "exotic" weapons are tied to PvP. Even worse - a lot of unremovable quests and introduction bounties literally FORCE you to play PvP just to get them out of journal. That was upsetting, to say the least.

Unbalanced PvP. While gear means nothing in PvP - I fail to see how pitting several new players against several veteran players supposed to entice me. I've just started the game, you gave me an intro quest and want me to try PvP? Ok. But at least put me versus new players as well and not people who are wearing high level items and clearly have been playing for month's. In fact - there is no matchmaking whatsoever. The game just lumps any available players together. Sometimes you get lucky and sometimes your opponents are from the same clan, team and voice chat, so your match ends about 5 times faster than usual.

Things I hate:

Dead chat and lack of players. Because of the default settings - people aren't joining local chats, which produces a strange feeling of emptiness and loneliness despite seeing players around. Discord is fine, but seeing people actually talk in the game would've been better. Add to that low amount of new players, and you might as well be playing solo until 950ilvl - quite often the game can't even find people for strikes and a lot of zones appear empty as well. There is no crossplay either. So all those hundreds of thousands of players are separated on 4 different platforms.

No LFG implemented in game. My god. That one really was a complete surprise. You have to use browser, phone apps or discord to find people to play with, and official apps/browser doesn't even allow searching parties by their title. I really don't understand how something so basic isn't implemented in a game which has raids and a lot of co-op content.

Despite all negative stuff mentioned - this is still an enjoyable game, playing 12 hours straight non-stop. Rarely do I get hooked up from the first moments, and I don't think there was any other outcome... I mean, I've got to play magic immortal robot man while orchestral music with chorus was playing in the background as I shot some giant tank that looked like a bug.

I've never played original Destiny or any other looter shooters. Though I did try Anthem recently, and I can safely say it never stood a chance - everything it does, Destiny 2 does better. It's incredible how similar they look, sound and feel, and yet one game gets boring and stale after 2 hours of gameplay and the other one just doesn't.

-----------------------------------

Few tips to new players:

If you are just starting - game will skip all the previous campaigns. The original campaign (Red War) starts in the Tower. Unfortunately, they removed all the rewards from those campaigns, but you still get some drops from enemies and satisfaction of knowing the story.

You'll have to use YouTube and wikis to find a lot of information which isn't explained in the game.

Upgrade modules bought from Banshee 44, earned through weekly bounties or rewarded in quests and season pass are perhaps the most important resource - they allow you to level up your items to higher level.

Weekly challenges (available after you reach item level 900) are the only reliable way to get higher level items after 900. But doing PvP and PvE also occasionally rewards you with better items. Unfortunately, PvP seems to reward you more often than PvE.

There are no "correct builds" or "best weapons" but only in a theory. Eventually you will be required to use mods (I recommend at least anti-barrier rounds for weapons) and specific weapons... which as I mentioned are often tied up to PvP. Don't obsess with items until you are closer to 950 though.

There are four currencies in the game:

Glimmer (capped at 250k it's a useless filler used to accept bounties, buy other resources, mods, etc) you get it from anything you do in the game;

Legendary shards (used to craft valuable mods, materials and upgrade modules) you get it from some activities bust mostly from salvaging legendary items;

Silver (tied to real money, used to buy cosmetics);

Bright dust (another useless filler, used to buy crappier cosmetics that no one wants) you get some for free because the developer is too cheap to give you silver.

---------------------------------------------------

2 years later, there is just no way to defend the game anymore. Quality clearly has spiraled down, grinding has taken over, PvP is being stuffed in your face at every corner. On top of that, the game is still as unpolished beyond believe - small bugs and issues that persisted for years have not been addressed. Convoluted mechanics, quests and general lack of direction require you to use Google non-stop. Hell - I bought this game for a friend, and it just has been a week of constantly explaining dumb stuff to him, then defending issues cropping up every day, several times a day.

How are motes still dropping below ground in Gambit? Why is the campaign requiring you to do Gambit in the first place? Why is stasis (new subclass) requires you to grind for every single ability... while using stasis essentially without any abilities? Particularly requiring you to use "slow"... while not giving you "slow" abilities yet! How dumb, how uncaring of details and players you have to be to do this? Tolerable in PvE, absolutely ****king pants on the head insane in Gambit.

Why did the game FORCE me to do ENTIRE EDZ campaign again, as soon as I zoned in there? I did it TWICE already, second time because of yet another bug which forced me to complete it again. Can't even remove the quest from the journal.

How hard is it to fix Drifters icon blinking? 4 years, many threads on reddit and forums later. IS IT REALLY SO IMPOSSIBLY COMPLICATED?

I could go on, but the main point is - every aspect of the game is flawed in some way, and dev's clearly aren't paying enough attention to what people are asking of them.\t\t\t\t\t
