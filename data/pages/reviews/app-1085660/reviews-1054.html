\r\n\t\t\t\t\t‘Destiny 2’ And The Ethics Of Deleting Two Years Of Paid Content

Now that we have the full list of everything Destiny 2 has “vaulted” ie. deleting from the game with Beyond Light, I find myself pondering a question that has plagued the game since the content vault was announced.

What are the ethical or potentially legal implications of deleting previously paid-for content from a video game?

The fact of the matter is that Bungie is removing access to more or less two years of content. All of the Red War, Curse of Osiris and Warmind campaigns. Everything in Black Armory, Season of the Drifter and Season of Opulence. The full zones of Io, Titan, Mercury, Mars and everything in Leviathan, from four raids to Menagerie. Really, the only things left from Years 1-2 of Destiny are the EDZ, Nessus and the Tower, the loot we got from those first two years if we kept it (even if it’s being “sunset,” it’s not literally disappearing).

But this is kind of a wild situation because depending on when you got the game, you paid £50 for the base game, and £25 for each expansion. That’s £100 for content just a few years ago that simply no longer exists.

The only argument to remove the content is that "the game is getting too big". Even if the game was 100GB in size it would not be a issue. Hard drive space is very cheap now, a 1TB hard drive is like £35. So at that price 100GB of space would be £3.50. I think we can all afford that if we can buy the game!

The most toxic part about the content removal is it is simply to create a content void so players feel forced to pay for the latest release as they remove older ones. Subverting the original game into a subscription service.

Conclusion: boycott Bungie as they delete content you pay for without your permission. It is not ethical and it insults both the paying player base and all of the artists and coders who worked so hard to make that content.\t\t\t\t\t
