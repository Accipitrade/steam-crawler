\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the best shooters on the market today. Just look at the reviews, even the negative ones almost unanimously praise the mechanics.

Unfortunately most of the negative reviews have the same complaint, and as a huge fan of Destiny 2, sadly I have to agree, the game is just not new player friendly. Can it be played solo? 100% yes. Can it be fun solo? 110% yes. Is there so much going on at any given time you'll have no idea where to start or what to do? Unfortunately yes. In Destiny's case an abundance of content actually works against itself. If you're not new you will more than likely love the game as it is now. If you are new, you'll be so lost and confused as to what to do you'll feel neglected and won't even bother trying to figure it out. Understandable but, unfortunate.

I grew up being extremely competitive in shooters. Starting with Goldeneye 64, I've always been a top level FPS fanatic. Until many years later I finally I realized they innovated themselves so quickly that now they're almost all a copy of each other with slight variations. I got bored of them, and hardly play shooter's anymore. The ones I've found that were unique and tons of fun were unfortunately not AAA titles so died out way too soon.

However Destiny 2 IS different. It is unique in so much of what it does. The builds can be so meticulously done, I've seen people on here complain about how limited they are in class selection, when it's really quite the opposite, but only after you do a bit of digging. As much as the constant comparison irritates me, I do think Warframe is a good game to compare this to, at least external gameplay wise. Both great looter shooters, kinda similar in some mechanics, but one is first person the other third.

Like any game it's not perfect. It has a lot of little quirks that can feel limiting at times. I dislike how it gives you dozens of quests but you can only track 3 at a time, and even tracking those doesn't always show you where to go or what to do. It's also very challenging. I think people see Destiny and think Halo on PC, when it's more like Halo meets Borderland's highest difficulty (which if you've played you know what I mean). The enemies can be tough, ruthless, and overpowered. And surprisingly smart at times too. So while world grinding and missions are pretty simple, other stuff can be very difficult and is not for the faint of heart. So if you're looking for a casual shooter, you'll find it here, but not across the board. But the F2P basically includes all of the casual stuff so hey.

PVP is awesome. Don't listen to the haters. People can jump into any PVE event and do well, so they instantly prefer it because, like any game, PVP takes time to learn. You will lose initially and it will hurt your ego. Like in any game. BUT I think this is one of the easiest PVP games for new players to pick up since it's relatively straight forward and the map design is 'brilliant' in that they are so natural and easy to learn, you don't feel like you don't stand a chance compared to the person who's played the level 100 times. It's really quite impressive. They say the secret to being good at an FPS is knowing the maps, which I agree with 200%. Destiny 2 is the first game this rule doesn't really apply to imo.

I mean, it's F2P. What do you have to lose by trying it? Though you don't get everything, what you do get for $0 is an incredible deal imo. If you like sci-fi and first person shooters, I really don't see how you wouldn't like Destiny. It is incredibly overwhelming for new players, so I guess it also depends on if you view "too much" content up front as a good thing or a bad thing, and how willing you are to sort through it. If you are someone with little patience, this might not be the game for you. If you do power through you'll catch on eventually, and you'll be exposed to a game that immediately feels unique, has a large mostly friendly community, and all the loots you could ask for.

8/10 Would spam graviton lance again and again and again.....ooooh fireworks\t\t\t\t\t
