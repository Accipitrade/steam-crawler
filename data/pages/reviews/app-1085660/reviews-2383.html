\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny is a quite unique experience that no other franchise can match. At first It is hard to get a hold on what the game can offer with it’s overwhelming landscape and little to no hand holding, but once you understand the sheer amount of things to do in the game, there is no stopping.

Destiny 2 is a looter-shooter type of class-based FPS with a unique MMO approach. Neither Borderlands nor Anthem can compare to the unique mix what this game offers. Its strength lies in its excellent gunplay and the variety of weapons and armour. Even though the game supposed to be class based, no class is limited in the use of weapons and the sheer amount of campaigns, missions, bounties, seasonal quests, patrols, raids activities and many more, will keep you engaged for a very long time. Campaigns have lively story with awesome cutscenes that you can complete in solo if you are not fond of the multiplayer aspect.

For a player entering this massive world with so many things to do without hand holding or a clear progression, will be frightening and confusing. Bungie have not executed well the initial player experience nor give directions where to progress. The quest tab will be filled with hundreds of quests and things to do in the same time, and players feel being lost. However, once you have figured out the progression and what activity worth doing by either a friend or a helpful guide on the internet, you will quickly fell in love on what Destiny does best, and that is shooting and looting.

The engine is quite stable with relative of a few bugs and a very active developer, providing quick support, seasonal content and DLC campaigns. Destiny looks and feels great with beautiful landscapes, shivering dungeons and detailed models for weapons.

The best experience however it’s MMO aspect where you can play numerous content with your friends such as dungeons, strikes and raids and many more together as a fireteam.

The Good

  • Hundreds of hours of content
  • Great visuals and sound
  • An engaging living world
  • Satisfying gunplay and ample amount of loot
  • Seamless and non-intrusive Multiplayer/MMO experience
  • it is literally Free (Free to Play – only cosmetics can be bought, DLCs add new Campaigns)

The Bad

  • Classes other than the class abilities don’t offer anything unique. No real incentive to play another class like in a traditional RPG.
  • Clan system should be better explored to provide more in-depth content (clan strongholds, rewards, Clan wars or unique activities)
  • New-Player experience should be streamlined to provide some initial hand holding and guidance
  • Some bugs and a few technical or connectivity issues still present

Destiny 2 is a great unique experience which set the standard for open word looter-shooters quite high. As a currently free game which offers hundreds of hours of fun, I full heartedly recommend trying it out, if you are into this genera. I can’t wait to see what Bungie cooks up as the next installment of this franchise. Stay vigilant Guardians!
