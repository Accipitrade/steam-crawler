\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's start off with the fact I've been playing since Destiny 1 and year 1 of Destiny 2. Tldr: This game is a great way to kill time if you got nothing else to play, it caters to every type of player, pvp and pve AND PvEvP.

Now I got that out of the way.
Destiny 2 is simply put, a game where you can get lost in, especially if you're a new player, you will get overwhelmed with the amount of content the new Free to Play overhaul has done. It will guide you to introduction quests that we veterans didn't have access too. This is not the type of game where you can get everything done in few days, it will take up allot of your time because this is your traditional Loot and Shoot with grinding quests. For example, to get this season's Pinnacle weapon (Weapon that has a questline tied to it and can only be completed in that related activity, in this case I will be talking about Randy (Savage's) Throwing Knife, a Pinnacle Scout rifle that is amazing, you will need to get 450 Scout rifle kills on other players in the Crucible, reach a certain rank in competitive Crucible and get a mix of 15,000 medals, kills. This grind took me, a dedicated PvP player a total of 4 days dedicated PvP. The reward was so worth it, this is honestly one of the best scout rifles I've ever used.

PvE players can certainly pick this game up and enjoy it too, there's cooperative arenas known as the Reckoning, there is a cooperative activity known as Strikes, where you go do a sort of dungeon if you want to call it with a boss at the end. There is Raids that are heavily mechanic-wise that you will need to learn in order to complete and as a new player you get access to all Year 1 Raids which is about 6 if I'm not mistaken. Last but not least actual Dungeons, a fairly new PvE activity that Bungie has been experimenting with. There's a total of 2. The Shattered Throne and Pit Of Heresy, both have NO matchmaking like strikes, and both have mechanics you need to implement, this is what differs from strikes where you shoot and bumrush the boss.

Now let me address the elephant in the room before you make a decision to play, our community sucks, big time. Most of the players are Elitist, impatient, condescending. If you wan't a taste of it, go to their @BungieHelp twitter and just look at the comments on update days, the most popular expression of rage is "I took time off from work and you're bringing the game down for an hour for maintenance! Unacceptable!" That being said, there is allot of welcoming players. My recommendation would be to go on the Destiny 2 LFG Discord and look for a New Player friendly Clan.

Thanks for reading all the way if you did, this is one of few games that I could genuinely take the time and write a long review about.\t\t\t\t\t
