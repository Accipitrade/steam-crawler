\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat with friends and great for players new to MMOs, especially FPS players.

Full review below. TL;DR: Fun game to play with friends who enjoy the grind and challenges provided by the combination of PVE activities like raids and standard quests, PVP activities, and hybrid PVPVE actvities. It has problems, but many are going to be fixed in the upcoming expansion and associated updates.

As of the time of writing, the seasonal pass system is still rife with problems stemming from "fear of missing out" and the added content provided by the seasons are repetitive and get old fast. That said, the raids are still epic experiences and challenges for players who like the massive story-based fights and puzzles, the PVP "Crucible" has a number of game modes available that cycle out each week to keep things spicy and interesting, the hybrid PVP+PVE game mode "Gambit" features very competitive battles for more hardcore FPS players, and the selection of labyrinthine "Dungeons" provide challenges for the more cerebral players.

My biggest issue is with the unsustainable content. When a new players joins, they are initially bombarded with literally dozens upon dozens of tasks and notifications and things to do and see and complete to get their feet wet, but as the new player becomes better at the game and more experienced, the still-towering pile of tasks given out at the beginning begins to feel more like a chore to grind through before you get to the "real" stuff. Admittedly, much of that content can be skipped- I still haven't completed about a third of it- but it does provide good equipment for use in-game as well as achievements for the completionists. But the problem is still there. Combining that with the limit of 63 quests and bounties imposed on the player, and many players will really feel the need to get through that content just to free up space.

Thankfully, my big problem with the game is largely amended by the multiplayer experience. Up to 3 players can party up to grind standard quests out of the way quickly, and up to 6 players to party up for the massive raids and PVP activities. And these raids are HUGE. I haven't played many other MMOs so it's difficult to actively compare Destiny 2 to other games, but the raids are all-day experiences. The shortest raid I've completed so far took 6 hours of continuous gameplay with a guide open to get us past the hardest parts. Some raids even take multiple such sessions to complete.

Overall, I'd give Destiny 2 a 7.5/10 for the experience, with the content structure being the main thing holding it back. Recent official announcements have revealed the development a new armor transmog system that allows players to change the appearance of their armor for a far greater degree of customization, the development of a "Destiny Content Vault" that will begin cycling out old content for new or more polished stuff (later to be cycled back in on an as-of-yet undisclosed basis- possibly monthly?), and the return of favorite content from the original Destiny game, now polished to modern standards. With those improvements and others on the near horizon, as well as the apparent shift to more story-dense content, I see a bright future for Destiny 2 ahead and recommend it for anyone looking to sink some time into an MMO.\t\t\t\t\t
