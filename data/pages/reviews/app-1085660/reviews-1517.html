\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA monetarily predatory cash-cow game with way overpriced DLC gated content and capped in-game mechanisms to excessively draw out your game play, riddled with problems, bugs and confusion.

Take a short break? No worries, Bungie got your back for some confusion. For example; Logging in and being hit by info prompt after prompt, getting to your map/inventory and then seemingly for no reason, being dumped into a story holding your gun in your hands and wondering what the fishcakes is going on.

Ignoring all that, it's a very glitchy game, very clunky, not very intuitive with a scathingly horrible UI and multi-level burial site of options and menus. After 20 mins of staring at a loading screen you might think 'well, maybe it's broken' and then 60 seconds later it actually loads the map, then again, sometimes it's actually broken... And sometimes you'll load in under 2 mins - You have no accurate indication of loading, a massive time waster. This obviously isn't an assets loading issue on this system, but a server <-> client communication one.
Mobs ignoring you makes the game rather boring because they only pay attention to you when you reach a certain part of the map. Mobs firing through walls. Bosses disappearing mid-fight, requiring a restart of the entire level... Charged melee not working... Etc etc.
Quite a few long running bugs that still have not been addressed...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile my friends and I have played D1 since launch, and still do, none of us find the same spark in D2. I cant quite place my finger on it, but D2 just doesnt feel fun to me. Even the seasonal events are grind-focused. If youre looking for a fun looter-shooter, I recommend checking out borderlands or something along those lines. Destiny 2 restricts all the fun content behind 6-man required teams and forced PVP sections. This game feels more like a laundry list or set of chores you do before your mother gets home. Its mostly there to fill in the otherwise blank space. Unless its really cheap, I wouldnt buy this game.

Edit: Beyond Light Release
The game has drastically improved its post-story content. Im 62 hours into this game after my previous review, and im STILL finding new things to do in the DLC. I dont like that they more or less removed most older weapons from being viable, but it is what it is. Still wouldnt recommend without friends who also play, but the DLC is very fun and lengthy (but not annoyingly), even after the story-content.\t\t\t\t\t
