\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't recommend this in the state that Destiny 2 is in. I like the game; I bought the Foresaken and Shadowkeep DLCs and had a lot of fun before the great vaulting that came with Beyond Light, but I don't feel right recommending what is no longer a full and enjoyable game for both free-to-play and DLC-buying players.
Short version: the game is no longer a free game, its a free demo of several $40+ DLC with PvP that is slightly unbalanced towards the DLC weapons and abilities.

Long version: I've played since Forsaken came out and had a grand 'ol time messing about and playing legacy content while grinding out exotic weapon quests and convincing my friends to start playing so we could do the raids. Since Beyond Light was released and half of the content got vaulted, I've been lost for what to do. My friends who were free-to-play can't join me for any of the Forsaken or Shadowkeep quests, and stuff we can do together is limited to the rotating strike playlist, occasional open world events, and PvP. The strikes and open world events get very grindy once you've gone through all 3 or so of the ones you can access, and the PvP is unbalanced towards the DLC stasis abilities in low- to mid-level play. Bungie took out a bunch of the content to replaced it with DLC, and everything that's left is repetitive or frustrating after not too long.

Beyond Light itself is not even worth the price tag came with. To put it into perspective: Beyond Light adds a new location, new campaign, and new raid, a subclass for each class, a new game mechanic (stasis), and a couple new exotic items for $50. Destiny 2 on launch cost $60. The Forsaken DLC that added 2 new locations, a raid, new abilities for each subclass, a couple exotics, a dungeon (3-person mini raid), a weapon type (bows), a new enemy race (Skorn), and a new game type (Gambit) cost $40 on release.

If you want to play this game for more than a week or two without it becoming repetitive and/or frustrating, wait for DLC to go on sale and then buy it. The Beyond Light is not worth $50, and the way that Bungie took out most of the free content from their free game and replaced it with DLC feels a little scummy, for lack of a better word. It feels like less of a free game with DLC and more of a demo for the DLC with some PvP on the side.\t\t\t\t\t
