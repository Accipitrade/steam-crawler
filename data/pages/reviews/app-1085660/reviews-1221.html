\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter supporting this franchise wholeheartedly since the Destiny beta in 2014 and all the ups and downs since, I genuinely cannot recommend this game to anyone, let alone new players.

Bungie has crafted a huge and magnificent ship but have lost their way, and no longer have the means to man it. The lore is meticulous and interesting, the environments are unique and breathtaking, and the potential for the entire franchise is unmatched. Unfortunately, the developers are utterly helpless to utilize this potential. Instead, they have opted to use the game as an easy cash machine; I would surmise that the primary issue this game faces right now is that the cost to content ratio in every single expansion since release is dishearteningly imbalanced (as is the PvP sandbox). Beyond Light has become the prime example, and for many has put the nail in the coffin of dedication.

Evidence of this is currently flooding the many subreddits and forums focused on Destiny 2, so I won't divulge here. I would encourage you to give the threads in question a good read. To clarify, I don't think this game is just a disappointing chain of blunders; there have been leaps and bounds in the right direction, but for some reason Bungie finds it imperative to try and fix that which is not broken. I sincerely hope things take a turn for the better despite the incredibly unlikely odds. I love this franchise to death and I would hate to see it go out like this. Until that miracle happens, I would absolutely steer clear.\t\t\t\t\t

"Developing new maps for PvP takes away resources from teams working on Exotic Quests and things like the Leviathan patrol," they say, all while diverting the majority of their resources away from this game and towards Marathon and cramming the Eververse store like its their only source of revenue. I've enjoyed this game for a long time, but hearing the director of the game himself flat out admit that PvP will never receive the support that the PvP community has been begging for for years, and then proceeding to take Gambit, one of the game's three Free to Play core playlists, off of life support due to "lack of engagement" after neglecting the mode for years, is the real irony of this story. People always joked that the only game that could kill Destiny is Destiny, but I never really wanted to see that come to pass. Joke's on me I guess, because I should have seen the writing on the wall after the disaster that was Lightfall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a first-person shooter developed by Bungie and published by Activision. The game is set in a science fiction world where players take on the role of a Guardian, fighting against various alien races and other dangers as they try to protect humanity.

One of the standout features of Destiny 2 is its beautiful environments and character designs. The game is set in a variety of different locations, including lush forests, desolate wastelands, and futuristic cities, and the graphics are top-notch. The character designs are also well-done, with a wide variety of different races and classes to choose from.

The gameplay itself is a mix of exploration, combat, and character progression. Players can explore the open world and discover hidden secrets, or take on various quests and challenges. The combat is fast-paced and strategic, with a variety of different weapons and abilities to choose from. Players can also customize their character by leveling up and allocating points to various stats and skills.

However, there are some drawbacks to Destiny 2. One issue is that the game can be expensive to play, as it features microtransactions and a "Season Pass" system that requires players to purchase additional content in order to access certain features. Additionally, some players have criticized the game for being repetitive, with a limited number of enemies and locations to visit.

Overall, Destiny 2 is a solid first-person shooter with beautiful environments and character designs. While it has its drawbacks, including the potential cost and repetitive gameplay, it is still worth checking out for fans of the genre.\t\t\t\t\t

Beyond Light - Previous Review:
The newest patch for the expansion Beyond Light has ruined Destiny 2.

I've played a lot of Destiny 2 as a fairly casual player. I'd pop in for a bit here and there between patches and expansions and enjoy the new content, pick up some of the new gear, and try some new builds. Up until the new expansion, Beyond Light, the game has (mostly) been good. I can pick up where I left off farming for gear, unlocking new items, and testing out builds. It always felt like even a casual player could make progress and unlock most of the gear in the game. Some of the raid gear, pinnacle weapons, and harder group unlockable exotics and legendaries eluded me, but for the most part I had managed to farm a pretty sizable arsenal of gear and weapons.

With the newest patch that has all been stripped away from me.

Some of the weapons and gear I relied on the most have been "sunset" which is a fancy way of saying "removed from being usable in the game." Auto rifles like the Breakneck, pulse rifles like the Blast Furnace, sniper rifles like Tranquility, and other items I've spent lots of time grinding and unlocking are now unusable. This goes against a core tenant of Destiny 2 where you've been able to infuse your weapons and armor to bring them up to the highest light level each patch and keep your favorite weapons relevant. I saw the writing on the wall when they decided to make armor only last 3 patches. The game has gone from a fairly sandboxy mmo where you chase the content you want and target what you want to unlock (for the most part) to a game where they constantly invalidate your gear and force you to grind for more within short time spans before they "retire" the gear you worked hard for.

There are so many MMO's where you're forced to play them as a second job - where grinding is so central to the game play loop that if you don't treat them that way you can't keep up or enjoy the content. Up until now Destiny 2 had escaped that. However the greediness of the devs is starting to show. Instead of making meaningful, unique content they are moving to rely on forcing you to fear missing out on the newest hottest weapon and force you to quickly grind for it so you can have top tier gear for a bit before they retire it. It's the same consumer exploiting model that World of Warcraft and other MMOs have used for years - QUICK, SPEND EVERY WAKING SECOND PLAYING OUR GAME OR YOU'LL LOSE OUT ON BEING THE BEST. It's a horrible practice and one I wish would die in the gaming industry.

So for me Destiny 2 has no longer become a game I can enjoy. I don't wish to have a second online job. I want to play games to relax on my schedule. Destiny 2 is no longer that kind of game.\t\t\t\t\t
