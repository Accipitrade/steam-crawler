Things I like
The progression system even though its a bit difficult to understand with little guidance in game it does offer that feeling of progressive power up. However the fact that its poorly described in game and I had to use outside help to figure it out is does not make for a good player experience the main reason i tolerated it was that I am a huge fan of Dark Souls and Blood Borne. Which is a set of games that thrive off of the community and using external resources to learn about in game mechanics and story lines. I and the rest of the Dark Souls community are probably in a minority in this regard which is why I cant faithfully recommend a game like this to a casual gamer.
Raids are fun although can be a bit daunting. However if you can overcome a few elitist and some anxiety about meeting new people I think you will love this feature of destiny 2.

PVP can be hit or miss due to needing armor perks and a decent weapon. However they do have some casual modes like mayhem which is a blast but unfortunately rotates every week. Not sure I could recommend the game on PVP alone.

Overall the appeal to destiny for me at least is the varied activities and power fantasy. I can build up my guardian and take what I have found in other activities to another activity for more loot. Like i mentioned though there is a heavy reliance on outside resources like light.gg the destiny app and youtube for a better understanding of the game.\t\t\t\t\t
