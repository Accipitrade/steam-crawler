\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs of July 2023: I have finally fallen out of love with the game. Gun-play will always be among the best in the business, and the space power fantasy will always stay with me. However, the incessant need to keep players on the treadmill has finally driven me away, likely for good. I could deal with repetitive and arduous seasonal events for a time, but the overall fatigue with the game combined with connection issues (in PvE and especially in PvP) have made the experience nigh-unbearable. The decline in storytelling as well as the narrative seemingly going in a direction I'm not fond of has also been a source of detriment. While my interest in the story used to motivate me to at least keep up with the game, it will do so no longer.

I've been playing Destiny for nearly half of my time on this earth. The game has been with me all the way back to when I first saw my dad play the alpha on PS4. I've created so many formative memories and bonded with friends over this game. So, it's a bit difficult to finally step away.

For those looking to buy the game, I'll echo a common sentiment; Destiny 2 is free to **try**, not free to play. While I won't labor over points like the sun-setting of content, the fact of the matter is simple: you're getting very little to play with unless you shell out hundreds of dollars to experience all of the available content. Furthermore, a lot of content that currently is available is likely to disappear once the next DLC drops, so I'd advise not purchasing any previous seasonal content close to Final Shape's release date. The nature of the game makes balance and content a constant tightrope Bungie must navigate, and unfortunately they've done so much better than they currently are.

Overall, was a fun game, for some it probably still is. Just sucks to see both the state of the game and my interest in it decline.\t\t\t\t\t
