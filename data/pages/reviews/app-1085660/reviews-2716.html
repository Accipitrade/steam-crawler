\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been dedicated to the destiny franchise since it first came out. I played the destiny 1 beta, all of destiny, destiny 2 beta and all of destiny 2 so far so you can be assured that I know what I'm talking about.


Destiny 2 is a well designed game with gunplay that is one of the best i've played so far, though thats not suprising since it comes from the guys who made halo. There are 3 classes, hunter, warlock and titan. You can think of them as sci-fi versions of the rogue,mage,warrior respectively. Their abilities are well balanced in my opinion and the pvp balance issues come from equipment mostly. Said pvp is intense and thrilling 70% of the time while the other 30% is either you getting smashed or you bored smashing the other team. PvE is engaging but can easily become boring if you don't care about the story or setting, speaking of which. The story is a mixed bag in general. Not that it is boring or badly-written(most of the time) but it is conveyed in-game poorly. This problem was abysmal in the first destiny for reasons you can look up yourself if you're interested since "I don't even have time to explain why I don't have time to explain". Destiny 2 remedied that by having lore achievments you can unlock which give you in-game lore to read, which gives casual players who don't want to go and look up the lore ways to have it in-game thus enriching the playerbase as a whole. The biggest problem that has been looming over destiny 2 is the eververse, bungie's in-game monetization service. It was way, way worse than it is now but the wounds havent healed in the destiny community. I could go into more detail but i'll just say the name Activision and you can figure out the rest. Now bungie broke off from activision and the eververse isn't bad at all imo. You can earn in-game currency that you can use to buy certain things in it without spending money by doing the weekly bounties and i've bought like 5 things so far that are awesome without spending any extra money.

tldr- 8.5/10 would reccomend for amazing gunplay, interesting story and nice gameplay loop\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a game where the fun is gone right after you complete the campaign missions and instead is turned into this huge grind fest for weapons and gear. The best gear is locked behind raids and dungeons "THAT" if you're "NOT" in a clan or don't have a lot of friends online that plays destiny 2 becomes one of those "stuck in a rut" games.

Destiny 2 Gameplay is a "rinse and repeat" daily routine for the most parts, from picking up the same old bounties and grinding them out to gain power in your artifact to get the same gear drops over and over and over again. If you want the endgame gear you'll need to join the destiny 2 "LFG" discord and hope that the players running a dungeon or raid will allow you to join as the requirements and power levels are out there not to mention the pre-reqs are that you've completed said raids a bunch of times.

Local chat is empty as there is no one talking in them, inventory space is great but a complete mess. All your weapons, armor, ships, ghost shells etc are all crammed into the available spaces, its as if Bungie couldn't be bother to create "tabs" which separate the armor for each class but instead just slightly "grey" them out leaving you to pan over your inventory to check for duplicates so you don't run out of space.

PVP is unique and fun at times but for the most part a hack fest, there are matches in comp 3v3 where you actually have a fair game and can actually win a match or 2. There are several unique and powerful weapons that are available through doing PVP, however the grind is real and Bungie has a habit of nerfing the heck out of weapons making the time you sink into getting said weapon feel "lack luster" at best. There is no anti-cheat that i can see for Destiny 2 and the hacks are not only in pvp but in strikes.

If you haven't picked up destiny 2 yet wait for a sale, as I've purchased each individual "DLC" before it came to steam and at $40 for 3.5 hrs of content for a DLC is beyond pricey and not worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny's aesthetic is really the reason I play the game. It's the most interesting and balanced combination of sci-fi and fantasy I've ever seen, and I'd play it regardless just for that. The actual shooter mechanics combined with the different abilities and classes also makes for a fantastic experience, and the minute-by-minute gameplay is fast and fun.

Because of that, though, I do put up with a lot of BS this game throws at you, which for me comes in two forms:

Let's just get this out of the way now, but ever since Bungie went independent, they massively ramped up their micro-transaction cosmetic store, which for a paywalled game with paid DLC is just never acceptable. I bought a fantasy-sci-fi MMO and I want to grind content to get cool-looking stuff. That's how it's always been done, that's how it always should be done, and anything less is fraud. Direct your opposing and objectively wrong opinions elsewhere.

Secondly, the best content in the game is hard locked behind raids, which itself wouldn't be a bad thing, except there's basically no matchmaking for raids. I've dabbled in WoW, and the best thing about that game is that you truly can experience everything the game has to offer by playing by yourself. You can queue for a dungeon, raid, or PvP battleground and see the queue slowly fill up as people join, and on a good day, you can get into a full 25-man raid in less than 5 minutes. Destiny 2's raids take a max of 6 people and their pathetic excuse for a matchmaking system will take you an hour to find just 5 other people, and at that point it's more worth your time to just hop on to the D2 Discord server to find people to raid with. Well that doesn't sound too bad, right? But what if I don't want to talk to a bunch of randos, and what if I don't have 5 other friends who play Destiny? Tough luck, that's what. I could understand if it was only the best gear stats or a special ship or sparrow (hoverbike thingy that's basically a mount) that you get for doing the full raid, but no, they lock exotic weapons and ridiculously cool looking gear and weapons behind it too. WoW was so welcoming that back in Legion, there was a huge quest-line that you could do completely solo, and it culminated in beating the boss of the new raid at the time, and it seamlessly gives you the quest and tells you to queue up for raid-finder. You jump in, beat it within half an hour even with complete noobs and randos, you watch the cutscene, get your at-least-better-than-what-you-have-now-gear, and complete the quest. From start to finish, you can play the game that you bought the way you want to. This isn't even a problem for older raids, because even if no one else is queuing up, the power scaling in the game is such that content from two expansions ago becomes hilariously easy to solo, and you can get incredibly cool looking gear to transmog onto your current gear (i.e. your current gear has good stats, but you like the look of another piece of armor/weapon, so you just click a button and change it's appearance).

How about Destiny's raids, though? Almost all of them have mechanics that make it physically impossible to complete solo. In one raid, you can't even initiate the first encounter by yourself, because to do so requires you to kill a miniboss that only reveals his weakspot (which you need to hit to remove his invincibility shield) once you get right in front of him, only he has two weakspots, one on his front and one on his back, and when he reveals them, he makes a slowing field that also disables your abilities, which means you cannot physically shoot both his front and back spot by yourself. They regenerate if you don't destroy them both within one 10-second cycle of him opening up and slowing you (he also shoots you at the end of said cycle for 90% of your HP). This raid is from content a year ago, and it suggests you have a power level of 750, which is the base level everyone starts at now. I had my power level at 963 and it didn't matter. Still did 90% of my hp in one hit, the smaller enemies did just as much damage as regular enemies and took just as long to kill, and I couldn't physically start the raid because of that mechanic.

Wanna know the best part? There's an extremely unique gun that you can only get from completing that raid (with only a 10% drop chance, of course). It doesn't necessarily have the best stats in the game, no, it just has a completely unique mechanic, because that's how the exotic system works. They're guns and armor with effects that change how you play the game. There's a gun that fires a bunch of proxy mines that stick to surfaces and explode when enemies walk by. There's a bow that poisons people. There's a bow that lets you see through walls. There's a sniper that lets you consume a whole magazine for one super powered bullet. There's a gun that, when you kill an enemy, spawns a little ring that you have to pick up and makes your next hip-fired round an ally-seeking healing bullet that gives them and you a damage buff for a few seconds. AND, there' s a grenade launcher that shoots sticky grenades, and each grenade chains a lightning bolt between each other as they stick to surfaces. Guess where you get that one? Yup. The raid that's impossible to do by yourself. Most of those other guns though? You can get them by yourself. You gotta work for them, but they're almost all completely designed to be done solo or in a group. The lightning grenade launcher isn't statistically better than any other gun in the game (though its base mechanics do make it quite strong right now). The first time you get it, its power level will be quite high compared to your current average, yes. But this gun came out over a year ago. If you got it then and still have it now, it will do almost no damage to end-game enemies.

What's my point here? Imagine if you were playing a Legend of Zelda game, but it could be played multiplayer or solo. You really want to get the hookshot which allows you to traverse new areas in the game, but you can't actually get the hookshot unless you find 5 other real people and then grind one of the dungeons in the game for a chance of getting the hookshot. That's what this feels like. It's not just a piece of loot that will let you one-shot enemies until the next patch comes out with higher level enemies. It changes the way you can play the game, and that's one of the most fun things about Destiny. Chasing exotics is fun because each one almost adds an entirely new mechanic to the game. They're not just slightly stronger gear, and some of them even suck compared to regular gear. The point is that they offer a new gameplay experience. Locking that behind a 10% drop chance on mandatory 6-man content is honestly just cruel.

All of that is to say, Destiny 2 is a worth-while experience up until you decide you really want to get everything out of the game that you can. Play it for a few dozen hours every few months and don't look up what potential stuff you can get from doing specific content and you'll be fine. The moment you realize what you're missing and what's required to get it, if you're like me and want to be able to just play by yourself whenever you want, you're going to realize you've just wasted your time playing a game that forces you to play it a different way than you're comfortable before it will give you the content that you already paid for. That's truly an unforgiveable sin in my eyes, but I also understand that most people will just see this as whining, because most people are fine with finding some randos on a discord server and running a few raids every once in a while.

Get into this game at your own risk. It will try to suck your wallet dry while asking full price and demanding you play it a certain way if you want your money's worth. It's super fun, but it's basically like being in an abusive relationship.\t\t\t\t\t
