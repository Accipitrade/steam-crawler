\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I were reviewing this game as it was at launch, I'd give it a solid 8/10. It built upon the tremendous FPS mechanics of its predecessor, with an enjoyable - if rather generic - campaign, and an effective progression system. It wasn't a perfect game, but it felt like Bungie had listened to its fans during development, and they gave us pretty much everything we wanted.

Now, 4 years later, Destiny 2 is a husk of both what it used to be, and what we hoped it could become. A capitalistic mess, Bungie has all but eliminated the ability for anyone to continue playing without buying not only the DLC, but the season passes that come every few months as well.

Which, to be fair, we should have seen coming after they came out with their in-game currency and store back in 2018. But us humble gamers, ever gullible, believed Bungie when they said, "Destiny 2 will NEVER be pay-to-win!" And technically, since there really isn't any "winning" Destiny, they kept their word.

And what do you, the player, get for your financial investment? Re-skinned fights against the same enemies you've been shooting at since D1, with weapons and armor sporting the diverse color scheme of black and blacker, all accompanied by voice-overs that you'll ignore since you can daydream better storylines than the studio's writers can concoct through their creative process of ingesting too much Starbucks and narcotics, and struggling with crippling depression.

And all that's if you can navigate the endless server issues, since the majority of Bungie's budget goes to strippers, adult diapers, and paying off lawyers from the time Dave learned that it's important to get proof when girls say they're 18. This is all your fault, Dave!

In conclusion, don't buy this game. You won't like it. And even if you do, you won't be happy about liking it. You'll just get caught in an endless loop of checklists and requirements, broken up by occasional moments of slight gratification, while the world trudges on and forgets all about you.

The raids are still pretty good, though.\t\t\t\t\t
