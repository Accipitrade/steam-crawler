\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite enjoying many hours with Destiny 2 in the past, I find myself unable to recommend the game in its current state and most likely all future states. While the game has its strengths, including visually stunning environments, a vast and intriguing lore, and solid shooter mechanics, the issues have begun to outweigh these positives.

One of my main concerns is the game's business model, which has become increasingly monetized. The introduction of more microtransactions and the 'pay-to-win' elements can often feel overwhelming and detracts from the overall gaming experience.

The game's approach to content availability is another point of frustration. With each new expansion, old content that is said to be often 'vaulted' or removed, makes its way back into the game as a reskin. This seems counterintuitive for a game that should evolve and expand, not shrink/reuse. Balancing issues are also a concern, particularly in the PvP aspect of the game. Certain classes, weapons, or abilities frequently feel overpowered, leading to an unbalanced meta that can be frustrating to play against. Weekly balancing patches aren't anything to harp about. Pvp is no longer supported since MARATHON's announcement. That much is clear.

While I recognize the efforts of Bungie to improve and expand upon itself as a company, these issues have led me to reconsider my recommendation of the game. Bungie is no longer the Bungie of the early 2000s-2010s where they cared about their fanbase. They have now drifted into caring about their live-pay model that is predatory.\t\t\t\t\t
