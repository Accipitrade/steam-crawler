\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh Destiny... If you're new to the series, you're undoubtedly confused about how so many people can play thousands of hours on a game - and yet still gripe as much as we all do about it. And that's probably the biggest thing to call out about Destiny: it's seasonal model inherently means that sometimes Bungie will take a swing and completely miss the mark... but usually get around and making the fixes / adjustments that the community wants eventually. This means, in my opinion, the overall product GENERALLY trends positive - although you will probably have points every now and then where you'll want to step away and play some other games while Bungie tries to fix / balance things.


Other than that, the game's visually stunning and its gun-play and platforming feels very tight (especially compared to similar games like Warframe, Anthem, Outriders, etc. - all of which I adore in their own right). Classes each feel different and powerful - and most subclasses are nuanced and raid-viable - while there are a few support ones that ensure that you'll always be a welcomed addition to a squad even if you're unable to bring the DPS yourself. Bungie has gotten better at diversifying weapon aesthetics and perks so loot is fresh and rewarding (especially during your first couple hundreds of hours). They've recently added transmog - which is a wonderful addition for people that spend too much time playing dress-up with their characters (guilty, btw) and Bungie seems to have finally started to really open up their story with Beyond Light... I think it has a lot of people hopeful to see what comes down the road from a story perspective. The story's maybe a bit daunting and difficult to connect with at the very beginning - but this weirdly works out because as you grind out levels and missions, you'll continue to pick up more and more little details or plot points.

TL;DR: While not perfect, Destiny 2 is a great game and the playtime value on it is fantastic. So great, in fact, that a lot of us fans "see" how close it is to video game perfection and we sometimes get unfairly critical when Bungie misses the mark.\t\t\t\t\t
