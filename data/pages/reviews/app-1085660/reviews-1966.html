\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I bought the game, I came in with an open mind, but now, it's just not really worth the time.

Problem 1: The equipment looks ridiculous. If you're someone who enjoys the idea of taking military weapons and bedazzling them like some 3rd world militiaman, then this game is for you. I found almost all of my favorite loadout within the first week of owning this game and haven't found much of anything new or cool looking since. The exotic equipment, minus maybe 1 or 2 look terrible, which sucks, cause that's the carrot on the stick.

Problem 2: The activities are like sitting at the DMV for two hours to renew your drivers license just to find out they just take cash, then after getting cash, coming back to find out that they are closed. It's plain not fun to work on a quest for 3 hours in one mission, to find out through google that you needed to do X Y Z other things before you started the mission so that the boss you fight is the right boss. Oh, and then what do you get when you finally do complete it? Glimmer, the most common in game item; and some pants that look like they came out of medieval france.

Problem 3: Because of problem 2 and 3, the game is completely meaningless. What's the story? Who knows (or who cares), there's a bunch of wars and this thing called the Traveler. When I first got the game I looked into the lore and it's just as convoluted as it is cliche and simple. There's nothing in the lore that gives you purpose as you grind for hours to get nothing. There's no feeling of impending genocide or oppression.

Problem 4: Who/What am I? Oh, okay cool, I'm a guardian, just like everyone else. My character is composed of the least ridiculous looking equipment I could find. So he's not even really that cool, he just isn't ridiculous. But that doesn't really give my character identity.

Problem 5: There has been nothing in the game to give me any kind of emotional reactions to any enemies. What's the scorn? I dunno, when I shoot them, white numbers come out, when I shoot their heads, yellow numbers come out. Just like the taken, fallen, cabal, and so on. Hell, the cabal look like red guinea pigs dressed like something from warhammer 40k. If I die, big deal, I'll just respawn in a few seconds, so I might as well just throw myself into a group of them and maybe I can have fun artificially creating my own challenge.

On a separate note, the pvp side of things is pretty fun. Gambit is generally a blast and Crucible just feels like halo but with stupid armor, abilities and weapons. So there's that.\t\t\t\t\t
