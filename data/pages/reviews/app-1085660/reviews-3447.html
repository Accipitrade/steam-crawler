\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's like someone very deliberately took the worst aspects of Halo and Warframe and then combined them in such a way as to trick people in to thinking that at some point, soon, it would stop being a miserable and uncomfortable experience. But it doesn't stop being miserable. it's miserable the whole way through, and it just gets more miserable when you get to the "Real" content after the miserable tutorial campaign. This is the worst gaming experience I've had in years, and I wish I could have the 15 hours of my life back.

Destiny is Warframe if Warframe were slow, painful, and badly dressed. Just get Warframe. Warframe is a vastly superior game.

Update: My friends are still playing it, so I'm still playing it, and I want to reiterate; This game is trash. Bungie made a game with excellent FPS mechanics then rudely shoved it in to an MMORPG shaped hole, resulting in a game that is constantly fighting to invalidate it's own best qualities. It's just perplexingly bad.

The loot grind is pointless, because everything is pretty balanced and aside from personal preference there's no real reason to choose one weapon of a given class over any other. You can go out and dominate the PvP matches using the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ "Grey" tier gear that doesn't even drop in game if you want to.

The PvE has two difficulties; "Effortless", as though the game was permanently set to "I want to enjoy the story" difficulty, and "Punishingly difficult, but only because there are stupid gimmick mechanics that instagib you if you don't jump through the right flaming hoop at the right time". Both are incredibly frustrating; All the campaigns and most of hte free roam content can be completed without even really paying attention to the game. You can take your time in menus, ♥♥♥♥ around, and unless you walk directly in to mobs of enemies and let them pound you then you'll never face any real challenge. A lot of mobs can't even do enough DPS to take your shields down, even if you stand next to them.

And the high end "Raid" content just boosts everything's HP by a bit, then tacks on a lot of mechanics that basically boil down to "push this button/stand on this plate/pass this ball around before the timer runs out or you'll die".

So... if playing Simon Says with six other people while boring enemies with massive hit-point pools walk blithely in to your line of fire is your thing, go for it! I hated it and I don't understand why anyone would do it without being forced to.

I played Destiny for 150 hours, I want a refund and an apology from Bungie, and I would recommend you get Warframe.

One more thing: Most content only allows groups of 3 players. Some PvP allows 4 player groups, and some "raid" content allows 6 player groups. But for the most part if you have more than 3 players you're ♥♥♥♥♥♥. So keep that in mind before you go in.

1000 hours in. Trash game. The core FPS game is good, everything built around it is padding and skinner box ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ to trick your brain into producing dopamine because number go up. There's maybe forty hours of content in the whole game padded out forever by an asinine leveling system and RNG mechanics that don't contribute anything except RNG. I'm only playing it because my friends do. Would not reccomend spending money on Witch Queen unless you already play the game and enjoy it. The running joke that Destiny is like being in an abusive relationship is still true.

The people who say this game is like an abusive relationship aren't joking. They just released a new update, with a new level cap. Does that mean I can level up, make important decisions about my character, and enjoy new powers and abilities? Actually no. They did re-work some of the classes, but those are just unlocked by default. What leveling up means is that previously all my guns and armor were at max level, which means my damage output and damage taken multiplier was at 1x. But now I'm at the minimum level, so my damage and damage taken multiplier is at like .25x. So now I have to grind for hours, not to get more powerful or get new abilities or do anything interesting, but just to get my multiplier back to 1x so I can go back to doing the activities I was doing before they raised the level cap. Destiny's leveling system isn't really a leveling system, it's just a mechanic to pad out the grind artificially and suck up your free time. It's gross, it feels gross, it sucks, and you shouldn't play this game.\t\t\t\t\t

I thoroughly enjoyed playing the game, it really made my time go by. But I hate to say it, but when I initially played Destiny 2 I just felt so bored of it. My enthusiasm was all gone at the time and I hardly even touched the game. But then I heard this magical news: Destiny 2 was going to become F2P and coming to Steam. Last month I actually preordered Shadowkeep, and awaited its arrival.

I can't believe how much I was missing out on. I feel so reinvigorated with the game on PC, and I don't know why. Possibly because I've been slowly leaving my console in the dust and am finding it increasingly easier to play games from where I am all the time, my room.

Overall, the game I've played so far is quite enjoyable. While other F2P shooter games may be available, I believe this game is the better choice over all. It's just so satisfying to kill all the factions in the game. Whether it's Cabal, Vex, Fallen, Taken, whatever it may be, the effort and effects they've put into it just make you want to keep coming back to do it again and again.

By the way, welcome back Bungie. Hopefully your run will be better now without Activision holding you back.\t\t\t\t\t
