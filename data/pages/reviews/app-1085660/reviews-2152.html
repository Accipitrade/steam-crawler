\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would say it's more of a mixed review. The game is lots of fun, but it could do with a few quality of life changes:
-Needing an app to search for fireteams and clans feels lazy.
-You can't delete stacks of certain items that are no longer used, instead forcing you to do one at a time.
-Restricting respawns (30 seconds instead of 5) in random areas (i.e. not a boss fight or big battle) in strikes and quests feels frustrating if you fall off an edge.
-If your internet is less than perfectly stable, expect to have a bad time. This can result in one or more of the following: you come back where you left off (minus whatever happened while you were offline), you respawn near the start of the activity/zone, you get kicked back into orbit (and out of any activity you were in), you get booted to the title screen, and/or the game crashes. If the timeout period were longer, maybe it wouldn't be as frustrating.

Despite all this, I still think the game is worth playing. All of my issues boil down to internet problems I had and some QoL features I grew accustomed to in other games. Whether or not any of these will be addressed, I plan to keep playing from time to time--when my ISP isn't being a jerk.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you enjoy looter shooter type of games - Destiny is definitely a game worth trying out. Great visual style, highly entertaining lore and good variety of game play styles. If that doesn't get you hyped, well I have no idea what to add.
Beware of the issues though:
1) Servers are sometimes very laggy. You can get booted from activities or your opponents/teammates might just teleport everywhere and ruin the immersion as well as you experience
2) Cheaters in PvP. That is a big one. If you think they only use hacks in competitive game modes - prepare to get surprised. In every single game mode you will run into them. Usually it is the classic pack of wall hacks and aim lock. However, teleporting, infinite ammo shooting, self resurrecting wild creatures roam the lands of quick match search.
3) In Destiny 1 there was a lot of grind, but you wanted to do it, because you were excited to get your hard earned reward. Sometimes getting a new Exotic took months, or even years. Destiny 2 feels more like a chore. You dont have to do all the activities, but you kinda have to. But the loot does not feel as rewarding, since everyone gets the new stuff pretty quick. And then you get a PvP match where literally everyone is using the fresh out of the box new Exotic, ruining the variety this game offers
4) A good amount of "lazy" content. If you hate the classic "We already have this mission, but what if we make all the ads have extra shields and toss in some hard to kill champions in there" approach to new content. Well, prepare to be disappointed. Instead of adding new PvP maps, strikes, events, Bungie opts out to recycle the content. I cant be mad about all the good stuff being brought back from D1. But Legendary and Master lost sectors, higher difficulty nightfalls are the same exact content you already have, but with fatter ads. Very lazy game content that might be fun if you hate yourself.

Overall, can certainly recommend trying it out. At first the game will feel amazing, for sure. Only when you play it a lot, it starts getting too repetitive and predictable.

UPD: Less cheaters, but still content is very repetitive\t\t\t\t\t
