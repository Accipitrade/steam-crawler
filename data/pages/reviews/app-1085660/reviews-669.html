\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, id like to preface everything im about to say by telling you that i am a destiny 1 player, by destiny 1s end, it was by far the best game i have ever played, and that isnt something id say lightly.
I took a course in videogame design as well so i have a relatively decent insight into game development.
Ive been playing games since i was about 4 years old, i am currently 19.

EDIT: as of the time of this edit, i am 20.
My review was written from a hopeful point of view, after reading it through it was very unlike me and did not reflect my genuine views of the game, or its development team.

First i need to talk about the shift in the gaming world that took place in 2019. to put it as shortly as i can, the dedicated playerbase of the majority of games was the primary audience before 2019. in 2019 gaming went mainstream and with that the majority of games shifted to have a larger casual audience than a competitive or dedicated one. The result of this is once respectable titles being dragged through the mud but more importantly, the games players grew to love diminished in quality.

With a casual audience you can afford to be lazy, to be sloppy, but with destiny this had further implications of changes at the detriment of the competitive and dedicated players.
Changes to PvP made it so that you could not hit a shot whilst airborne, this was done because new players, the casual players, were not looking upwards in a game where you have a radar that shows you if a player is above or below you, with ample sound ques etc. new players were playing destiny like a boots on ground COD game and the result was the dedicated and competitive audience getting shafted.

Bungie consistently implements old armour sets from destiny 1 because just like almost every aspect of destiny 2, the designs just got worse. less inspired. there are very few armour sets that are original to destiny 2 that stand out, the majority that do are unobtainable and may never be again.

i could rant all day about how poor bungies choices as a dev team are, but i will simply describe them in a word and that is predatory.
Bungie is reminiscent of twitter before elon musk, bungie employees had every single weekend off despite having a live service game. that is unheard of in game development, you are effectively putting your food on a barbecue and leaving it to burn. this encouragement of laziness speaks volumes about how poor bungie is as a development team but, heres some further insight.

In early alphas of destiny 1, it was planned to have two primary weapons, not the primary special and heavy ammo systems it launched with. The systems changed because there was heavy backlash against a double primary system.

keep that in mind whilst reading on.

Bungie launched destiny 2 with double primaries. That is to say, they went against ample data they already had screaming at them that no one in their community wanted this system and yet silently implemented it into destiny 2 in what i can only describe as the most egotistical thing i have ever seen in game development.

I bring this up however because there was recent drama regarding an old movement exotic that belonged to the titan class. that exotic is now a part of the warlocks ability set despite being dubbed too strong for destiny's sandbox, much like titan skating which was also deemed to strong for destiny's sandbox despite still living on years after these statements were issued... on the warlock class.
The titan class does not make you feel like a titan. It makes you feel like youre a civilian that walked into the middle of space warcrimes. There is clear favour towards the hunter class because it is the majority of players but i digress.

This recent drama lead to many death threats being sent to developers despite it not even being a developer of bungie who leaked this information, that is to say they withheld it knowing there would be backlash.
What is interesting is that the developers came out and blamed the community for the lack of communication on the developers part.

Whilst i do not condone sending death threats, i think its a big wake up call if youre ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ up so badly people are sending death threats, but that isnt the point.

Bungie lacked communication from before destiny 2 even officially launched. they silently implemented double primaries knowing the community didnt want them. They have been selective at best with their communication since destiny 2 launched and dare to blame the community.

I have not found the sense of community i did in destiny 1 in destiny 2, it is painful to see what were once good childhood memories being stained by this sequel.

This is without even touching on the content of the game itself, they have "sunset" (code for removing without regard) half the game. so much so that nothing makes sense if you are a new player or in some cases even a returning one. The game is so convoluted with ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ that it is extremely overwhelming even when youre caught up on it all. It is a game you would have to dedicate a lot of your time to and to be completely honest it is not remotely worth your time sadly.

I have also been attacked by this community for bringing up legitimate concerns about the game, so not only are the developers making interesting choices, but they seem to have fostered a community of ignorant bigots which is never fun to interact with outside of the game.

I urge you to spend your time and your money on literally any other game. warframe is very grindy, but its community is warm and welcoming, if you are interested in a space themed looter shooter id recommend checking warframe out, it is free to play and wont remotely suck money out of you to the level destiny 2 would.

I hope i have talked you out of buying this game, it is an incredible waste of time and money\t\t\t\t\t
