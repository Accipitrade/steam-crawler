\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's set the record straight, even though I won't recommend this game to others, it's a solid game that I somehow still have fun in.

That being said, it is not friendly to new players, and with recent choices regarding payment models along with the stability of the game and how it's being developed, I think this needs to be said right now:

First, in regards to new players. There is almost nothing teaching new players what to go for in this game. Most of the tutorial doesn't come from the game itself, it's from other players. That's if you have other players to teach you. The popups don't do anything either, and only serve to overwhelm the player. This isn't new, as other reviews will point this out.

In regards to the payment model. For new players, it's daunting to figure out where to start or what to buy, and it feels like a big scam, as if Bungie wants new players to buy all the DLC for a large sum of money. Hell, even as an experienced player, it's feels like a scam:
  • As someone who bought Witch Queen and then wanted to upgrade to the Deluxe Edition, the Deluxe Edition upgrade isn't easily viewable on the store page. It's hidden behind another DLC page, or through a node in the game itself. The only thing viewable on the store page is the Deluxe Edition, a whole $40 more than the upgrade. And when Witch Queen went on sale a while back, both the Witch Queen and Deluxe Edition went on sale, but not the upgrade.
  • One last thing, if you happened to go the ala carte option by buying seasons separately with silver, but you happened to miss a season and wanted to buy it afterward, there is no tracker built into the game that tracks what seasons you've purchased, so you have to buy the Deluxe Edition upgrade, which would still be $40. So not only is the Deluxe Edition feeling like a scam, but so is the Deluxe Edition upgrade itself.
  • Of course, none of it is explained by Bungie themselves directly, it's either hidden in a Bungie Help page, or it's been discovered by players themselves.
  • Speaking of the ala carte option, it's not even the best pricing option. The Deluxe Edition, along with the seasons, comes with 2 dungeons. So if you were to use the ala carte option for the first season or so, then wanted the dungeons (using the paid dungeon key/pass) and the other seasons, you would need to pay more than simply buying the Deluxe Edition upfront. So your best options is either buying the yearly expansion without the seasons, or buying the Deluxe Edition. This is a common pattern you see in AAA games nowadays, but still, it feels scummy, as it punishes players for trying the content and wanting more.

Finally, this game needs to have time off of the seasonal model to redevelop core systems, both gameplay wise and game engine/server wise. All 3 of the core gamemodes (Strikes, Crucible, and Gambit) is either: Suffering with a lack of content, a chaotic mess, or completely ignored. In terms of the seasonal model, outside of going for Triumphs or Powerful/Pinnacle gear, I'm not going to bother with the seasonal content. Something with the seasonal model needs to change.

Regarding the game's engine/servers, with the growing amount of loading times, error codes, and other random bugs that will pop up, I honestly feel that the seasonal model takes away at Bungie's ability to tackle these issues. I think the game needs to take a break from one or more seasons to settle all or most of the issues mentioned above (including the new player experience and the pricing).
