\r\n\t\t\t\t\t1) Start Destiny. Make a character (obviously a Titan because you just want to be the Master Chief in a Bungie game again).

2) Shoot, blow up, punch (mostly punch), and maim intergalactic species in the name of fun and loot. The Hive are just space Locust and have no feelings. Also, Wizards are the females of the species?

3) Use loot to find new ways to repeat step two, ad infinitum. Swords are weirdly more useful than most guns? There's an SMG that makes you chain lightning between enemies because those stupid bastards shot you with electric weapons, so you're conditioned to be aroused every time something shoots blue projectiles at you.

4) Try Crucible once because you find out you can get endgame gear from certain activities, and then realize that peer-to-peer matchmaking for a competitive mode in 2020 is 2020-levels of ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.

5) Go do raids and repeat step two with puzzles, mostly jumping, sometimes sequence shooting. Rejoice that you receive loot that your compatriots have been grinding for weekly, or languish as some first-timer gets the very specific piece of gear you've been chasing for months. A happy medium does not exist.

6) Enjoy the music, particularly because Paul McCartney contributed one song to the original Destiny soundtrack and that is the closest to an auditory war crime humanity has ever been; he is, thankfully, nowhere near Destiny 2's soundtrack and thus you are safe.

7) Repeat weekly with seasonal content every 100 or so days. Sometimes this content is good. Sometimes it is bad. But it introduces new story, armor, and weapons into the game at a steady pace between major annual content drops.

Destiny 2 is repetitive at times, and those that find comfort in mundane tasks punctuated with sometimes engaging endgame activities and occasionally interesting story will find an enjoyable game. It's kind of a power fantasy in which you build your character how you want to play. There's a lot of freedom in building and customizing the weapons and armor of your character, and there are daily incentives to visit a myriad of locations.

It isn't going to blow anyone away on start-up. It's a slow burn that turns into a cozy fire by the time you reach the endgame activities. The true fun of the game in building a character you can effectively play, joining your friends for a variety of strikes and raids and sometimes ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ PVP, and finding new and interesting ways for the physics engine to kill you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2.

This game is a wonderful game, in fact, id go as far as to say it is one of the best games ever. But unfortunately it is not, due to its downfall in PVP. PVP in this game has one of the best/smoothest gun play ever, so many unique weapons, many fun builds, but the negative is that the PVP has seen a INCREDIBLE boost in cheaters, and server side issues, if your able to put up with a cheater every couple of matches, and have friends to ♥♥♥♥ around in PVP with, especially in the Survival playlist. I can easily recommend this game to you. About 85% of the hours i have put in have been in PVP. The PVE in this game is wonderful, amazing graphics, super cool secret missions and puzzles, and a endless amount of quests you can keep grinding for if you're down for that. But the best part is the raids. The 6 man activities, although now there isin't much incentive to doing them, you can still have a crazy amount of fun just even doing it for fun. Either way, the mechanics in every raid is unique and most raids have a somewhat harder difficulty and is a challenge to all, whether its 3 manning a 6 man activity, or 6 manning with handicaps, as long as you don't run out of ideas, the game can be a lot of fun. Downside to the PVE part is there is *too* much grinding involved in some quests. Especially now the bounty farming has decreased the player base by a lot, only because it's become a boring and repetitive grind. As all things have an ending, if your looking for a serious commitment, this game is for you. But be warned, you cant back out. Something worth mentioning, the learning curve in this game is quite massive, do not expect the moment you jump in you become some god gifted player, it takes a lot of time and effort to understand every game mechanic, and learn a way around some of them. As said, big learning curve so don't give up instantly. Destiny is best played with friends, so if you have groups of 5-12 people, you can have a load of fun in this game. Especially the private matches.

I have close to 2500-3000 hours in the Destiny franchise.\t\t\t\t\t
