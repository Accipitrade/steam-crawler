\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would love to recommend this game to new players. I cannot. Bungie started to "content vault" old content making it impossible to play the entirety of the story as it existed. So as a new player you either have to use youtube, or just not care about the story. What sucks about that is that destiny has a wonderful story IF you were here for it. Fear of missing out should not predicate the ability to tell a complete and concise narrative.

Until content vaulting is removed from the game I cannot recommend it to anyone who comes in fresh. Its not going to make sense. I am sorry if your friends play it and you want in, Bungie's expectation is that you just "deal with it" rather than fix a problem they created.


Another problem is gradually manipulative monetization. Personally I enjoy this game by buying seasons and PLAYING the content. However, the precedent that is set through monetization in Destiny creates a vibe where the game could be played in a pay to win context. That perspective depends on what you personally do and don't care about in a game with its monetization. The general idea is that 1. they release a battle pass, in that battle pass as you level you can get passive buffs that affect things like xp gain, currency acquisition rates, etc. The PROBLEM is that you can buy ranks in the season with real money. So...you can buy your way to getting faster levels, those better levels get you harder activities which drop better loot, whilst also buying your way into getting currency faster, which lets you acquire more loot, more quickly.

I also hate valve's review metric, I give this game a 6/10, 6 because its a wonderful fun game to play with friends, however, the story issues, aggressive monetization, and lack of routine quality of life checks make this game hard to digest, especially if you are new.

I do wish you were here enjoying this game, but you probably wont enjoy this game because its developers don't care about new players or monetization optics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're into the grind, then that's what this game is all about. This game gives off an almost Borderlands-esque with the shoot-em and loot-em type game-play. While these two games are completely different, there are still some things that Borderlands and Destiny 2 have that resemble each other. In both games, the grind is always well worth it. Perfect rolls in Destiny 2, and perfect parts in Borderlands. The way the game feels, especially with the loot system, really makes me enjoy this game and if it wasn't for that, this game would be missing something huge in it.

Otherwise, there are lots of cons as well. Bungie is an amazing developer, don't get me wrong, but their speed on noticing what needs to be changed is somewhat disappointing. Not to name classes or anything *COUGH TITAN*, but there are some serious balance issues on the PvP side. With me maining Warlock and most of the time nothing else, I feel I am always at a disadvantage. Now in season 1, the Warlock class was completely broken. Then, later down the line, Hunter, and now worst of all Titan. I absolutely CANNOT get into a comp game without the enemy team having 2-3 Titans. There is just no chance that I will not play against a team of Meta abusing players that rock One-Eyed-Mask and Seismic strike. Whats even crazier is that the whole enemy team is using it and it makes me unmotivated to continue the multiplayer grind, and while multiplayer is awesome and all (especially comp) these classes need some serious balancing.

P.S. - I don't use Dawnblade because tracking swords are insanely broken along with most of the Titan Ultimates.\t\t\t\t\t
