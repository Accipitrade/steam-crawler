\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game will entirely suck you in. As you start, it is very overwhelming, because the current "New Light," which is what they call the introduction quest line, spawns you into this new world, and you have no clue what you are doing, then get ~10 quests to your name and not a lot of ways to do them.
The introduction is made a lot easier if you watch a guide to start in Destiny 2 while the game is downloading (trust me, you will have time to) and once you really start to get into it, the game will suck you and you will have 100 hours in two weeks and multiple texts from your friends of where you have been for the past few weeks.
The community around the game is great, and as salty the subreddits can be, there is great people in there. If you can't find a group of friends that can do a raid, but you are too shy? There is an entire subreddit dedicated to helping people do that kinda stuff that are shy (r/D2sanctuary.)
If you are unsure whether you like the game at this point, I'm going to say one last point: the weapons feel and sound great. Every weapon has a unique feel that allows for a lot more freedom in your loadout, as it is more about what feels best to you, and is easiest to use for you, more than this item has better stats thus I must use it. Bungie excelled best there.
All in all, it is a great game that will suck you into it and won't let you out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnly been playing since the release of Forsaken when it was still with ActivisionBlizzard, but this has easily become one of my favorite games to play. With so many things to do, see, collect, fight, there's enough activities to participate in for grinding to still feel fun and enjoyable after a long session of playing. The story missions were great, too.
Crucible, however, is just a mess. I only do it for certain gun quests if I have to, but I'd never play it with the competitive drive others have for it. There's constantly hackers in Trials of Osiris, making anything that needs ToO seemingly impossible to get done unless luck is on your side with matchmaking.
240+ hours of mine have been spent on this game. That's not counting hours from the Blizzard platform. I haven't even done a third of what the game has to offer. That's how much content there is. It's amazing.
When it comes to raids and exotic quests, people are overall very nice and understanding about new people not understanding mechanics, people are willing to help you, I've only done the Leviathan raid as of writing this, but even when I'd mess up or someone else would, we just redid the area and nobody really got upset about it, just "that's ok, we'll try again!". I don't know if I got lucky or if people genuinely just want to be helpful and find fun guiding others. I'd like think this attitude carries through all the raids and dungeons and quests.
Highly recommended to anyone who wants to just have a chill game to play if you're not in the mood to actually do things, or to those who want a constant task list of activities. This game can do both.\t\t\t\t\t
