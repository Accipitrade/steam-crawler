\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAre you a shooter fan who enjoys the idea of an MMO but you don't enjoy the big MMOs available? Destiny 2 is the game for you! An FPSMMORPG that takes all the good things about MMOs and removes all the big annoyances. Yes, the game does have grind. But the big factor in grind is the moment to moment gameplay. Personally I can grind until the cows come home in Destiny 2 since the actual combat is so much fun, WoW, not so much. The PvE content is sublime, offering varied and unique content with the raid being some of the best content I have experienced in an online game period.

The PvP is something in this game I could never get into. These type of games NEVER leaned themselves into PvP but somehow this game has been able to harbour a rather sizeable PvP community. This is a respectable feat, but the PvP mains need to remember the type of game it is and as long as Bungie balance the way they do they will always play second fiddle to the PvE content. I personally wished they balanced the gametypes separately so everyone would be happy but what do I know.

I see a lot of disgruntled people talking about the "removal of content" now this is actually the main reason I decided to write this review. Yes, they have "removed" content from the game, with that, the masses jumped on the bandwagon of "Oh my god, Bungie are removing my purchased content! Worst company 2020". The actual plan of the removed content is to revamp it and reintroduce it at a later date giving the player a better incentive to come to these locations. Honestly, if the other big MMOs such as WoW and FFXIV had the chance to do the exact same thing to content that less than 5% of people actually did, would they? My bet is on yes. The sunsetting issue is another matter that I personally disagree with and could think of a better way of handling it (removing of purely pinnacle weapons) so there's the biggest negative I have with the game right now.

Overall, if you are a group of friends who need a new game to waste most of your free time on I cannot think of a better game than Destiny 2. The story if different whoever when it comes to solo play. The game is night and day when you play solo versus with a group of people you know and get along with. Even then, it is free to play to start with. So give it a shot! The worst thing that happens is you uninstall! The best thing that happens is your group of friends find that time sink game you all play WAAAYYY too much. Or you find a clan (through the various methods of clan recruitment found on reddit or other websites) and make a bunch of new friends. Or you dislike the game and have to go through the painstaking process of pressing uninstall. Pretty good gamble don't you think?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is coming from a person who has spent hundreds of hours in all faucets of the game. I haven't completed all of the titles nor have I 100%'ed some things in this game, but I am fairly knowledgeable of everything this game has gone through, from year one to now. This game has taken up several hours of my life, and I have wholeheartedly enjoyed this game. From the people I have met playing to the actual game itself. However these last few weeks of playing has convinced me that I can no longer support/put more money than I already have into this game. The progression...seems pointless and doesn't look like it will change any time soon. The constant chase for power and cosmetics has started to wear thin, its a cycle that never goes forward, just keeping you in a mindless loop of waiting for a number to increase. The world building of this game has worn thin...Bungie has failed to release anything Forsaken caliber in terms of what this universe has to offer since...Forsaken. After watching the story of Beyond Light and really picking everything apart...it seemed so cliche. As a person who wants to experience what the game has to offer, not read it in lore books, I can care less about the Fallen. The fallen in this expansion just seem to be used like a tool to introduce new abilities to the player, for paying..of course. These new powers of "Stasis" that have broken this game. I have almost never in my climb in crucible, either solo queuing or grouping, needed to time people out intentionally as a serious strategy. Playing against stasis with the base sub-classes is on the same caliber as fighting against a One Eyed Mask titan pre-patch. Stasis has literally done what it's name represents. Stall. The PvP balance and sandbox are in shambles in comparison to how they were pre-Beyond light or even during Forsaken. This is once, and only once, have I believed that Bungie has truly made something "Pay-to-win". Even in PvE the power of stasis is on par or even better than ALL of the base sub-classes. Shadowkeep was my last chance at this game. I spent all of last year judging the content, playing everything that each season had to offer, and it failed to live up to the expectations that I had. Forsaken was either the worst thing, or the best thing that happened to Destiny. Nothing has lived up to it, and Beyond Light is just trying to replicate some of it's formula with out being too interesting or deep. However Forasken did show me what Destiny can be at it's best. I hope Bungie can turn around and truly make something as great as Forsaken once again, because I want to come back and enjoy and be enthralled in this universe, but my last couple weeks and days of playing have shown me how stale the game is without forking up some money to the corporate over lords at Bungie.\t\t\t\t\t
