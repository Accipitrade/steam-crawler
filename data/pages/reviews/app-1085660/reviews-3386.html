\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's clear Bungie has no idea what they are doing. With sunsetting, all of the weapons and armor that I have worked for will quickly become obsolete. I've only been playing since November, and Bungie is already re-releasing weapons I have, just so I can grind for them again. Remind me, what's the point of all this again? This game has become so unbearably grindy, PVP is a joke, and the coop activities are bare-bones.

It's clear the developers are so hard up for cash that the only R&D they can afford is to develop more MTX for the game so they can have some cash flow and pay their rent next month. There's simply no reason to keep playing this game. The story is hot garbage. I cannot recommend this game as something to pay for because it is a terrible value. I cannot recommend this free to play because as a F2P player there's literally nothing to work towards now.

I find it genuinely hilarious people complained about FOMO in Destiny 2. This is an MMO. Welcome to the club! That's kind of the point with these kinds of games? Bungie has set out to solve a problem only a very vocal minority of their community voiced.

Side note, Eris Morn is absolutely terrible character. She sounds like a fortune-teller who is having a nervous breakdown. Her dialog is so jarring, out of place, and poorly written I wonder if it was written by interns.\t\t\t\t\t

Many people are correct to say, that it sucks to start this game for the first time, but you have to understand, that this could be played for a long time, so the systems, currencies and hidden things are there for a reason - exploration.

I dont really like the mentality of the last few years, where everybody tries to rush through every game as fast as possible, watches everything already solved and then is upset that there is no endgame left.

Destiny 2 is beautiful, the soundtrack is something you can have running without even noticing that its fitting perfectly, the gunplay is great, you can fly around - just a very good game.

Go in, play it for free and have hours after hours of fun, either by exploring everything yourself or just looking it up and collection everything

9/10 - just -1 for the rough start :)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is fun especially for returning veterans. Everyone starts at 750 and it's very easy to climb light level if you know what you are doing. They added matchmaking for somethings that didn't have before like nightfalls and solo que competitive. I haven't seen much exotic drops but then again I'm not actively looking for them.

For new players it may seem difficult and confusing though because after the tutorial it just drops you into orbit with no guidance of where to go or what to do. No notification of where any of the original year 1 story is (Red War, Osiris and Warmind). I'm surprised they took out year 1 story rewards entirely. So, yes Destiny 2 new light is not new player friendly, if you ever played warframe you know what I'm talking about. If you look up stuff before hand (google is your friend) you should be fine though.

If you want access to some of the exotic questlines you have to buy Forsaken to access them. Shadowkeep does not come with Forsaken only the new powers that came with Forsaken.

~Word of advice: Please look up how to make public events heroic when you are with other randoms especially in flashpoint areas. It helps progress flashpoint challenge a lot faster and not make the veterans annoyed.\t\t\t\t\t
