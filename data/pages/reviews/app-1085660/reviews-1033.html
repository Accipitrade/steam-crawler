\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can recommend this game to almost anyone. Bungie have for a long time been trying their best to get this game to where they want it, but be warned that the years and the struggle can make it frustrating. to play sometimes if you're heavily invested, or play PvP.

The singleplayer story is a bumpy road. Early Destiny 2 (most of which is now gone to be fair), was plagued by Bungie's former publisher (Activision) and their ... business model. But anything new tends to be very solid, and with deeeeeeeeep and rich lore to it. The most recent Bungie standalone expansions especially have been top tier.

The multiplayer PvE aspect is always very enjoyable, smashing through enemies together with friends has never been more fun, and the combination of Bungie/Destiny's AMAZING gun play and the abilites at play, you'll have a good time.
The End Game for some will be unattainable, requiring you to have 5 other to team up with and complete a 20-60min long raid activity, but when you can do it, its the best part of the game with nothing else like it.

The PvP is ... frustrating. Bungie has long struggled to find a balanced sandbox for its Crucible mode, and I doubt that'll change much. There are few people I know who dont get annoyed when playing pvp, but I know many who play Destiny 2 pvp exclusively despite this. Maybe its the gunplay, maybe its the skill ceiling, who knows.

All in all, Destiny 2 and the Destiny franchise in general has always been very promising, and it can be so much fun. Ive sunk thousands of hours and thousands of pounds across multiple platforms playing this game because I love and enjoy it. The game, the community, all of it. Its a game that isnt afraid to do certain things and push the boundaries, but in other areas it festers and are neglected. It mirrors the community who on one side can be the most joyful, friendly, outspoken and supportive community out there (see the annual donations made by the community as well as the refusal to let the game die) and on the other hand, can be so vile and toxic, with people who are either horrible people or abused cheats and other software to make other life hell.

Its a rollercoaster of an experience and honestly I wouldnt know where I would be without it.
All thats left to say is... eyes up Guardians, I'll see you star side.

P.S. Everybody in Destiny is Queer. There isnt a single person who is confirmed cis/het. This is good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2, Destiny 2, Destiny 2...

I'll start with this. If you're not ok with games that pretty much require you to spend money to have fun, this is not the game for you. Same goes for if you don't have a lot of time (or patience) to grind for gear.

If you do have the time and are ok with spending some money (generally comes out to $10 a season pass which lasts 70-80 days and $70 a DLC which has a pattern of coming out each September) then by all means go for it, just try the free to play stuff first.

The game's main focus is PvE, pitting you in strikes, nightfalls (harder strikes, better loot) and Raids (big ol' 6 people versus big bad) to get your loot and space hero fix. PvE get's updated the most so if you like that, you're in for a treat.

PvP is secondary in this game but still very fun, so long as you have some decent guns and know how FPS works. Sadly, PvP isn't updated or really taken care of at all by Bungie so things that go wrong in there tend to stay a while. Currently Stasis is the mainstay and kinda busted, but not unbeatable. It needs a few tweaks but as Destiny is a mainly PvE game, it'll probably be a bit. Try PvP second and make sure you do it with friends.

At the time of writing this I've sunken a good 845 hours into it. Unhealthy? Yes. Enjoyable? Very Yes.

It's a good game with some flaws. You hear the community whine a lot but that's because they love the game and want it to be better.

In short, it's free to play so you've got nothing to loose trying it. Hopefully it suits you. Be brave, Guardians!\t\t\t\t\t
