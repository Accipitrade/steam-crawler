\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've really enjoyed playing the game on PC. The graphics look great and the game play mechanics have been easy to use.

While I am member of a guild and I don't do anything with the guild and mostly play with match up strikes rather than a guild fireteam.

I have not tried the PvP as I am dreadfully slow and am pretty sure that I would be spending most of my time dead and waiting to revive rather than contributing to the team. Mostly I play a Hunter though I've tried the other two classes a bit.

I find the various game play videos you can find on YouTube helpful for learning how to find things in game. Seeing how good some of the people playing when doing a fireteam running a strike I realize there are a lot of edge mechanics and gear and weapons that I don't know.

To wrap up I've enjoyed this game. I like the various weapons and gear and the various planets you get to visit. I like the look of the world and the sounds which can be very helpful. The badges and achievements are interesting and I've been working on those as well as on my collection of weapons and gear.

I got the original base Destiny 2 game for free from Blizzard and have purchased the expansions since because I've enjoyed playing it so much.

The world is not completely open in that the terrain and structures are designed to provide boundaries for where you can go and where you can't but within the areas where you can go there are sometimes interesting things to see and there is an entire lore that is part of the achievements.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 1 Day-1 Vet here. Destiny has evolved quite a bit over the years since Destiny 1. People look back and say that D1 was the best, but that's only because of the shenanigans that went on; like the loot cave, pre-nerf Suros, pre-nerf Gjallarhorn (had the pleasure of abusing all three lol). Destiny 1 was a good game, but many think not good enough (and I tend to agree). Eventually Taken King and Rise of Iron are released, and made the game one of the best games I've ever played. The RoI Artifacts were particularly interesting. Felwinter all the way!

Destiny 2 comes out and its like the mayhem of Destiny 1 all over again. Not enough content. Except this time, the game play loop wasn't as good because they decided to neuter all of the guns so that nothing could effectively clear strikes. Things were harder, yes, but less fun to run around and complete. Then they patched the damage values, and things were spot-on again. Like Destiny 1, not enough content but the game play loop was there.

THEN..... Then Bungie split from (then) Activision-Blizzard. What a miraculous moment that was. Since that moment, the game has improved by leaps and bounds. Today, there is literally SO. MUCH. TO. DO. I can't complete even half of it on the weekly basis. There are nearly 3 dozen quests that lead to weapons, both exotic and pinnacle-legendary (exotic without the exotic slot requirement essentially) that are really fun to use and sort of remind me of the D1 Artifact effects. Some of them outright blow away the competition in one aspect or another.

_________________________________________________

VERDICT

PROS:
- Game runs amazing, vertical sync is the lightest I have ever used. Very little input latency with it on. I have a high end card though (2080).

- Micro Transactions stay EXCLUSIVELY to cosmetic items. They've had hiccups here and there but have since rectified it (post-company-split).

- They are VERY careful with the whole nerfing and buffing of their arsenal. Some may disagree, but I have rarely found a weapon that doesnt feel fun to use and doesnt shine in one way or another. In fact, I find it hilarious they allow some of the perk combos that they do, which is even better.

- They are now making star-characters like Osiris and Rasputin relevant!!! They used to shy away from that for some reason.

- Lore. No, seriously. LORE.

CONS:

- The game devs are afraid to explore more involved weapon/gear effects.

- They are doing battlepass-like seasons now. I hate that model so much, but at least Destiny 2 is liberal about the progression rate. Another game I love (Crossout) cannot have the same said about its battlepass system.

- SERVER LAG. Good lord, some days are worse than others but WOW! I have a completely solid-state computer I build myself. OC'd 8700K@5GHz - RTX 2080 - NVMe SSDs - 3000+ MHz DDR4 RAM - The list goes on. I literally transfer entire game directories between SSDs in less than a minute. I have a Netgear Nighthawk-class router with a CAT-6e shielded ethernet cable and a SOLID 100 megabit connection that gives me about 12.5 to 14.5 megabytes per second of download speeds and 5 to 8 megabytes of upload. TL;DR, update your servers Bungie for the love of God.

- They make patch notes with hidden changes almost 100% of the time, and the changes aren't small sometimes either. It's unfortuante, but they aren't the only ones to do this. Transparency about this kind of thing is just a no-brainer.

The game is absolutely worth the cost of the DLCs, absolutely worth the time investment and absolutely fun to play solo or with friends. Just a solid game that gets the game play loop it strives for and then some. It has a few hiccups but nothing at all to worry about if you're feeling hesitant about this game. The best part now is that its F2P.
