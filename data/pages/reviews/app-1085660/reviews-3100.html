\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter spending over 800+ hours playing Destiny 1 and its expansions, Destiny 2 just came out at a bad time for me. I was getting married, moving to a new place, and I just didn't have time to play much. I played a little bit of Year 1, but missed Warmind & Forsaken. Now that I've got the time for more gaming, I've jumped in head-first and picked up right where I left off years ago. While the quest structure and rewards systems can be confusing to new players, the loot-grind routine is actually pretty easy to follow once you get the hang of it.
Where Destiny really shines is the moment-to-moment combat. Weapons are satisfying, the movement is crisp, and the abilities really make you feel like a badass space magic commando. Shadowkeep is a great addition, and there is a ton of content for new and lapsed players alike. I've put in over 100 hours in a few weeks as I'm recovering from knee surgery, and I've loved every minute of it. I'm just now getting the courage to try a raid again, which was by far the most challenging & rewarding experience of Destiny 1. It can feel daunting at times, but if you have a headset & a few friends (or like-minded strangers that you can find through the companion phone app), you really owe it to yourself to give this game a shot.\t\t\t\t\t
