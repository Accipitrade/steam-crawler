\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have quite a bit of playtime in this game, so why a negative review?

Well, the good parts that kept me interesting are the flow and gameplay + visuals:
You can be a friggin space ninja, and the design is (mostly) beautiful.

What grates on me though for as long as I've played this:

Bungies greed has always been a constant nagging presence, but it's just gotten worse and worse over time. More piecemealed content spread thin across ridiculously overpriced DLC, with paywalls and nag screens galore. Years later, and still the same maps, doing the same grind, except even some of that has been cut out of the game now.

If you fork out for the DLC, the fairly limited amount of content you get, doesn't feel like it's worth anywhere near the asking price.
The story is lazy unimaginative drivel, the voice acting overly dramatic and monotonous, and you soon realize how shallow the game as you scratch beyond the shiny surface.
And the few missions etc. you get, aren't too bad, but there's just so little content, and then it's stuck on repeat.

"A single evolving world" is in reality just a paper thin excuse to keep reusing the same content over and over, and to charge you as much as possible, for as little as possible.

The basic grind of upgrading your gear (which gets reset every DLC, so you sort of start over) is fun - for a bit, until it gets super repetitive. And there's a daily cap for how much you can progress, just to make sure you come back the next day. This game is full of little things like that. It's like it was designed in a laboratory.
