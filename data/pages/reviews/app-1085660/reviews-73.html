\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have far more hours in this game than my Steam hours indicate because I played it extensively while it was still on the Blizzard launcher. I bought This game back when Warmind came out in 2018 and had a great time with it for a long time. It was a great game and I had tons of fun with it, but decisions in recent years that they've made have been utterly unacceptable.

Bungie lured people in by offering the base game and DLC content for free. I wasn't upset that they made the game itself, along with Curse of Osiris and Warmind, were made free to play. I was actually excited and happy that the game had become more accessible and was glad that the player count increased, but things went downhill. The game was incredible for a free to play product, brimming with content that was enjoyable to experience alone or with friends, until they announced that they'd be removing basically all free to play content from the game. I paid money for this game, and to suddenly find out that I can never play The Red War campaign ever again, or Osiris, or Warmind, or even eventually Forsaken which I paid $40 for on release. Suddenly the game had basically zero content for people who had been playing for free, and even most of the content I owned and paid for was gone. Not just the campaigns mind you, but even the raids and other associated content that I had hoped to someday get to. I'll never be able to play that content that I bought with my own money. Bungie claims it was because the game's file size had become too big and needed to be cut down, but that's ridiculous. Plenty of games have compartmentalized downloadable portions, and they could have created a separate launcher for content that had been removed so that you could still play through it. This was clearly a decision fueled by greed in an attempt to get people to pay money for their increasingly awful DLC that get worse and smaller with every release, since people were having too much fun simply enjoying the content they already owned and not purchasing the newer mediocre expansions. Unless by some miracle they bring back all vaulted content, I cannot recommend a game where the developers might suddenly, with hardly any warning, rip away the content that you purchased in an attempt to force you to buy their new garbage.\t\t\t\t\t
