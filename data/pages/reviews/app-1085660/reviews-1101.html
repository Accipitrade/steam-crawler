\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been playing Destiny 2 since the Shadowkeep expansion came out and I just recently started beyond light.
I was a bit overwhelmed with the content of D2 before Beyond light. There was just to much to do and it became tedious and not fun.
Now with Beyond light, half the content got removed, but you can still get every exotic in the tower at a special terminal which is a decent compromise.

D2 Beyond light feels much less bloated and feels much more streamlined. Instead of 6-8 tabs of Quests in the past, its now mostly 1-2 Tabs in my case. Its much less overwhelming and it feels much more fun to plan out what you wanna do today or plan on what do to tomorrow.
The story of Beyond light is pretty fun too, even though unlocking all the stasis abilities is incredibly tedious and frustrating :/

But Bungie made a good choice with vaulting the old expansions and content. Maybe they release the story related stuff in a seperate game/mode, who knows. Would be a decent compromise for the people who didnt play the story stuff yet. Or to make "summary" videos on youtube with all the cutscenes and important lore stuff to know about the old expansions. kinda like movies

But yeah. D2 is a great game and I really enjoy it. You should try it too!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have spent nearly 4000 hours playing both Destiny 1 & 2 on Xbox and PC. I'd first like to say that I've enjoyed my time playing and still do. But I wouldn't recommend it right now, hence my negative review.

(Content of the Game)
Probably the best thing players enjoy about D2 is what they first experience, which is it's Gun Play and movement. It's quite natural and easy to get used to, and personally the Halo type feel of the game is what drew me in. Even the unique abilities from the different sub-classes made for a great well-rounded fps experience.
The story of D2 still flows the same way as it did in D1, meaning that it's based off of the Traveler, The Darkness, and other alien species that now inhabit the solar system trying to kill humanity(The Last City). Looking back on D2 I will say that they did much better than Destiny 1. But unfortunately that isn't to Bungie's credit, that's mainly because Destiny 1's story wasn't good(Probably because there was barely any story at all). D1 was a great game at the end, but I digress.
If you follow the game's campaign you'll be introduced to likable characters(Some of which have side quests) and many missions that lead to beating interesting bosses that have intriguing backstories. You'll be spending most of your time playing either Strikes, Crucible, Gambit, Patrols and Raids.

Strikes are not quite what they used to be back in D1, mainly because they had specific kinds of loot you could grind for by opening special chests via skeleton keys. This was nice because it made the strike itself feel less like something on a chore list. The Strikes themselves aren't bad, but like any PvE activity it can get very repetitive.

Crucible however is very different. It's never been repetitive, although I myself don't personally like PvP related activities I do still think it's a very exciting and unique experience.

Gambit is the best of both worlds. You fight with three other teammates against waves of enemies until you summon and kill your 'Primeval' before the other team does. Lacking in motes? Enemy team ahead in killing their Primeval? No problem, just send one of your teammates over so they can take away their lead by preventing them from banking their motes or they can heal the enemies Primeval by killing them. But be aware that they can do the same to you. Gambit gives something for everyone to enjoy.

Raids are probably the best thing about Destiny. Raids are separated by Encounters. In each encounter you'll either face an intricate puzzle, a complicated boss, a difficult environment to overcome or some combination of all three. It's the height of the Destiny experience, and if you decide to buy the game I highly recommend you try it at least once with your friends.

Here's my first contention: Don't force people to play the way you want them to.
With the release of Bungie's latest DLC (Beyond Light) they've recently decided to add a new system called "Sun-setting" what this means is that certain weapons(Like 85% of the guns in the game) cannot be used in activities where Light Level is relevant, they did this in the hopes diversifying the Player's arsenal. And now henceforth any weapon you get will eventually be sunset depending on when the gun was added to the game. What is their reasoning behind this you might ask? Apparently It's because the weapons they created became very popular and very powerful. So popular in fact that they decided to make said weapons nearly useless instead of coming up with a more creative alternative that doesn't make people regret a weeks worth of work that went into grinding for a good roll on a weapon they really like. They did this mostly because they didn't like people using Mountain Top and Recluse all the time and also because couldn't figure out how to deal with Power-creep.
Now people often say in Destiny's defense that the game was always meant to be a FPS RPG and therefore Sun-setting is completely normal, and that's kinda true. But people don't get attached to their gun the same way they would to some wizard staff on WoW. Weapons have their own feel, that's why you could throw away the exact same gun that's got an entirely different roll on it than the one you're currently using. It's how you like to play the game. And to be honest, it's hard for me to enjoy the game knowing that in a few months the guns I've worked for will eventually be useless to me. That's why I personally don't feel this is a wise decision, I wish they found a better solution. But this weapon thing is just my opinion, many people don't mind Sun-Setting weapons and armor because they enjoy the hunt for something new. Which is fine.

Here's my second contention: Don't take away a good thing.
At the end of the day, there have been to many things I've enjoyed(not including Sun-setting) that Bungie has taken away for me to recommend the game to anyone. But despite all that, I still enjoy playing because of the community I've been apart of. I still play with some of the guys I opened the Vault Of Glass with 6 years ago. But if I didn't have my friends, I probably wouldn't play anymore.\t\t\t\t\t
