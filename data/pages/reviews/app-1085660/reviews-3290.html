\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly one of the best looter shooters out there. Seriously seriously recommended, however having the season pass and being caught up on DLC is practically a must.
In depth review below

PROS:
-Excellent gun-play, Absolutely perfect, should be a role-model for all devs moving forward for how to do guns right.
-Sound work and profiling is absolutely fantastic. Literally one of the best soundtracks in gaming.
-Top notch movement system with each class having extremely varied and satisfying jump mechanics
-Content for 'all skill levels' out the back end, whether you're ready to invest 7 hours a week, or 70
-Great story and lore if you take the time to find it

CONS:
-Money-money-money Season pass and DLC/expansions are simply put, a must. They lock you out of very important content
-Grinding. Its a looter shooter, you know its gonna be a grind. Some people like it, some people dont.
Now for the big ones. These are long as i tried to explain them well enough
-Gated & limited raid Matchmaking. This is a step that makes sense from a couple points of view, but the hassle of having to leave the game and search forums and third-party communities to try and find a group willing to take you and/or a few other people or friends is just way, way too much. Raids reward some of the most powerful gear and items in the game, and only a paltry 11% of the PC community has ever completed a raid before. Thats frankly an issue that bungie doesnt seem to want to address. MM in raids might lead to some new players making raids difficult, but it also opens a pathway for solo players to find raid parties without having to leave game, which frankly is the largest part of why people simply dont run raids. Traditional MMOs dont use matchmaking for raids because there are hubs located in game that offer people a chance to find groups to run them with. Destiny has no such thing, the tower is either filled with people minding their own business, people with chat disabled, or people from foreign countries who dont speak the same language as you. We NEED a dedicated way in-game to find and team up with other people who want to run raids, that is an absolute must.
-PvP... The pvp in this game is well, well its just not good. You have a substantial amount of the player base using "aides" in game to buff their "skill", and the radar system is so broken for people who get good at it that its been removed from competitive all together, yet they wont remove it from other game modes where it is heavily abused. Not to mention the insane meta that some builds have become with particular pulse and smg builds capable of melting down guardians in a super in a second or so tops from a considerable distance. The PVP in this game is honestly its greatest downfall imo. any amount of strategy or depth is entirely made void by people with radars who can see you coming from 360 degrees and half a nautical mile away, and builds capable of TTK in the .23s range, and top that off with a server delay long enough that you'll find yourself rounding corners and a full half second later dying to that random spray of bullets that missed you. If you're used to playing CoD you'll fit right in here, oh well but, even CoD has longer TTK. Its honestly hard to imagine the same studio which balanced halo PvP, balanced the PvP in this game.

Despite those very... long winded cons in my opinion the game is still great. I still recommend it. Yes those things are annoying and they can completely ruin a night or a session, but you'll still find yourself having fun. The satisfaction finally getting that perfect piece of gear with the stat rolls you need after hours of farming. The overwhelming feeling of superiority after utterly dominating a team that taunted you in the gambit pre-game. It just, it is honestly a great game, an 8/10, it just has some painful scars left over from their first game that they need to shake off or resculpt.\t\t\t\t\t
