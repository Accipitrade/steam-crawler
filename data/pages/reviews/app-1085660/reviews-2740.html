\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel like this is the falling into the stereotype of Destiny players saying "I hate this game, it's my favorite game ever," but it's hard to recommend this game right now to a new player, especially after the most recent DLC drop. It's always been hard to start Destiny 2, doubly so if you don't have friends that already play. With the lackluster campaign and raid of the most recent DLC (Lightfall), it's even harder to recommend to a new player to spend the 70$ for the game, and you're gonna have to spend money if you actually want to do anything in the game.

If you are willing to drop 70$ (and potentially more for all the older DLCs as well), the game is both incredibly frustrating and incredibly satisfying at the same time. I think the gunplay and ability design is unmatched, and the newest subclass strand is incredibly fluid and fun. That being said, years of tech debt are starting to pile up and the stability of the game is at it's absolute worst it's been ever. Raids are some of the most fun PvE coop activities around, but you'll have to deal with the lottery that is LFG, and beyond raids and dungeons a lot of the things to do are just mindless grinding, as expected of an RPG. PvP is in neither the worst nor best sandbox, but both PvP and the PvPvE mode Gambit have been very neglected in recent years, and the map pool in both modes (especially Gambit) gets very boring after a while.\t\t\t\t\t
