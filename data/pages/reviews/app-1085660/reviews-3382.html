\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 ended up being a pleasant, albeit extremely grindy, surprise. I enjoyed the first Destiny, which I played years ago on the PlayStation 3. But Destiny 2 ended up being one of the more rewarding, fun, and beautiful worlds I've immersed myself in. At least among MMO "looter shooters", it can claim that title. I'm not sure how it stacks up against games like Borderlands which feature much better mission and enemy variety.

I admit, I've jumped onto the Destiny 2 party train a bit late. I never played it! I waited until New Light which shifted the game to free to play, and this did a lot of good things to the game... but that comes with some problems too.

The worlds are quite frankly beautiful, cool, and awe inspiring. They are surprisingly quite varied, and the more I look at it, the more details I notice. From detailed sci-fi structures like the Futurescape on Mars, to the amount of color in places such as Nessus. You can't go wrong with the amount of lore, exploration, and beauty you'll find on these strange worlds. Only problem is that most of the lore is revealed in giant novels of text. If you like reading, then go ahead and immerse yourself in these stories. Not enough lore is revealed in the missions, but you can find objects to scan which in fact reveal more about the world via your robot companion, the Ghost. The places you go too just beg to be explored, so you'll stumble upon many things to scan or look at. So I never felt myself being too confused by the lore and this universe, I get the gist of the overall story.

The actual progression of your power is tied to, well, Power. Which is based on the gear you have equipped, including your stats. You no longer level up normally with XP, you level up a season pass instead that I find to be a lot better than Fortnite's because AT LEAST you get good and viable stuff in Destiny 2's, including useful resources ( in the free tier ). Other forms of progression include the various PvP and PvE modes, which all seem to have ranks if you use the matchmaking system. Rank up to get more goodies. You will often find yourself grinding a lot in Destiny 2, so if you don't like it then stay away from this game. I myself don't mind grinding if the rewards are good and the gameplay is good. In most cases, these two things are good in this game.

All abilties are unlocked from the start, so you can tweak your build however you want. This immediately gives a certain sense of freedom to Destiny 2: New Light. Having to work hours upon hours leveling up normally is fun and all, but having all of this stuff available for me is overwhelming. And not in a bad way! I can go to any world I want at any time, I can do whatever I want at any time, and there's hardly any level- I mean POWER restrictions. Much of the base/beginning content has a reccomended level of 750, which is the Power you start at anyway. Even the Shadowkeep world, The Moon, has a reccomendation of 760. This level of content freedom is something I haven't felt in awhile, I enjoy planning out my activities with this much wiggle room.

I didn't even get started with how rewarding this game is! I mean, it's very grindy. This game is extremely grindy. But I was fed a veritable waterfall of viable, cool, and fun loot at every turn. Almost everything was an upgrade of some sort. It became too much, because I had no idea what particular armor set I wanted to wear, or what weapons to use. This is one of the few games where grinding is solid fun, because you're doing it while being a badass and armored sci-fi hero... with magical powers. The gameplay is stellar, the gunplay feels amazing, and the enemies are all cool in their own ways. The lore is rich and makes Destiny 2 have this almost "mystical" vibe that I haven't seen in a sci-fi game since... well, the first Destiny. If you go into Destiny 2 knowing its primary focus is loot, you will have a great time, but all these other aspects help it along.

The biggest problem with this game is how grindy, disappointing, and repetitive it can get. The monetization seems somewhat manipulative on top of it, even if I do enjoy the season pass I'm not going to let that go so easily. A lot of the quests aren't that interesting, although certainly a few are. I do enjoy them still, but there was this distinct feeling of emptiness and this almost "half baked" feeling. No cutscenes, no epic scenarios alongside NPC allies, and just you running around doing baffling things to complete your objective. Too much focus on killing stuff to get the thing instead of cool mechanics... although it's nice that there's at least some platforming. But you often press buttons, pull levers, pick up power orb thing to carry around, and kill waves of things to defend things. That's just for solo questing though, as a lot of the public event content including PvE and PvP dungeons/raids, is really quite good. But the solo content, even if still a lot of fun, isn't as fun as playing with others. The grind can be overwhelming too, there's so many currencies and reputations to grind. Much of this holds back Destiny 2... but the gunplay, gameplay, beauty, lore, loot, huge worlds, and MMO content keeps me coming back for more. I want to keep exploring, finding those chests and lost sectors. I want to keep playing the amazing Gambit PvP mode. I want to keep getting cooler and cooler looking stuff. I want to keep blasting the faces off of badass looking aliens. I want to keep swinging around my giant flaming hammer. Destiny 2, despite all of the quite literally grinding flaws, is a solid and damn fun piece of multiplayer gaming. At least among MMO looter shooters.

There's this fear, deep inside, that Destiny 2 might be outclassed by a better game in the near future. Let's just hope Destiny 2 can get above just being a solid and damn fun experience, or it will die not learning from past mistakes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRe-review of this game after Beyond Light and Season of the Hunt.

For once I can actually say that this game is enjoyable. In the past I would not have recommended this game due to lack of player accessibility on most things. Now this game is way more accessible to beginners and returning players.

Gotta rip this band-aid off first. 2/3 of all content is missing. From IO to the leviathan raid only a few areas remain and old quests on such planets are now gone. They also added this New Light quest that allowed you to re experience the beginning of Destiny 1. While that sounds awesome to returning players. To me it felt nostalgic until it wasn't, then it felt very boring.

Ok now for the actual review...

Story: 6/10 Reason being that it is short and extremely forgettable and not very fun doing again on other characters.
Gameplay 10/10 Adding Darkness in the form of a freezing "Stasis" is very fun and it leaves room for more darkness subclasses in the future. Other than that it is destiny and it will always be fun to shoot cool bosses in the face.
Balancing: 8/10 Gun balancing in crucible has never felt better imo. A lot of different easy to get guns work well in crucible now. (Although guns from previous seasons are now stuck at a really low power cap so guns like recluse are no longer viable in crucible games with power advantages on) Thorn got a range nerf along with most other hand cannons, and pulse rifles got a slight buff.

Gambit is still annoying but sbmm seems to be a little better this time around.

Graphics 9/10 They look great for being a little old. And game runs great with the few occasional stutters when loading.

Exotics, EXOTICS!! Its is now way easier to acquire exotics. Since most exotics locked by quests that were on planets that now no longer exist are gone you can now get them from an exotic kiosk for an exotic cipher, glimmer, planet materials and ascendant shards. There are multiple ways to collect these items so you can farm exotics. Also HAWKMOON IS BACK!!

Now for the total rating of this game I give it 8/10. The game is fun especially for new players. Only downside is needing Forsaken and Shadowkeep for most things.

Edit: Vault of Glass is back and is better than ever. and more beautiful. Vex Mythoclast is back and with a new intrinsic perk.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've deleted my old positive review, because it's no longer relevant. This is not a game, it's a chore. After finally putting my fanboy-love for Bungie aside and accepting that the team that made Halo is gone for good, I was able to start seeing this for what it truly is, and I can no longer say I enjoy it or recommend it. Don't waste your life. Find a nice casual gaming experience instead. I recommend Avorion, it's like Minecraft in space, and it's quite good. The Outer Worlds will be on Steam soon, as well. And of course, Halo, because it's still awesome. But Destiny?

For starters, the whole 'live service' model of gaming needs to die in a fire, especially with story games. Something like an MMO like EVE Online, it works fine, because it's a proper MMO, and more of a player driven world. But for a game like this with a full narrative?

If you like the grind, you'll love it. But if you're looking for story? Look, the story is there, but it's also not, because you'll have to grind to get to that, too. And now they're doing this 'content vault' thing which basically removes a bunch of content from the game. Never, in my thirty years of gaming, have I heard of a developer removing content from a game in this way. And it's all justified because it's a 'live service' game, and they want to 'reduce the install'.

Bungie has a new IP on the horizon, apparently. I'm legitimately looking forward to seeing if it's good, and will check it out. I came to Destiny because it was free on Steam, and managed to get away with never spending a cent on it. Despite that, I did have fun, and it's a good game to play with friends, but if you plan on playing it casual, don't expect to get into much of the power-tier content, or most of the story, which you'll miss anyway because a lot of it went by in previous 'seasons', which is another way of describing 'temporary content' because 'live service'. Utter garbage.

No, this game is not for me any longer. I will be uninstalling it, more than likely. I intend to see if the next 'season' is an improvement but I suspect that sitting out the rest of the season will help ease my 'Destiny addiction' and I'll be able to just say, "you know what? I don't need this. I have a thousand other games to play instead, and all of them are actually more interesting".\t\t\t\t\t
