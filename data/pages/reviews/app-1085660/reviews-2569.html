\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I like the game enough to have put almost 70 hours into it but it gets stale fairly quickly if you are not into the ''MMO'' trope of collecting 20 different currencies, 500 different nonsense items and chasing that ever present magic dragon.
Most of your game time will be about making your ''light'' number go up and filling bars in 15 different daily/weekly quests just like in almost every other MMO on the market currently. I can see the appeal of it, but I just feel like it could have been done better.
For those who don't know, your ''light'' is an arbitrary number that increases your characters damage/damage resistance and nothing else. it is there to gatekeep the content that you are able to play until you reach a certain number. People who have played World of Warcraft since atleast the Cataclysm expansion will probably feel a bit of déjà vu from this description.

With that said, the gameplay itself is fairly entertaining, the gunplay feels good, the gear/weapons you can collect are kinda bland (with a few notable exceptions) until you reach a point where you start getting exotics on a weekly basis and though the enemies are sort of braindead (they quite frankly pale in comparison to Bungies old work in Halo), they do come in satisfying numbers/have some unique mechanics that makes up for it somewhat. The PvP can be fun but can also be quite a bit infuriating at times when the other team is using ''Flavor of the month'' gear/weapons/setups that can break the game in many not so fun ways, otherwise it plays very much like Halo without vehicles, so if you like that then you will probably spend a lot of time in the PvP mode.

All in all I had some fun but I can barely recommend it since you will probably only have fun in Destiny if you can stomach the arbitrary grinding and basic PvE aspects of the game. Only buy/spend time on this game if you intend to be playing it for a long time.\t\t\t\t\t
