\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh Destiny it is a good game, I would not have 900+ hours on it if I didn't like it. That being said I pre-ordered the game and have been playing it on and off since, getting to enjoy the content as it comes out.
Now If I was asked if I would recommend the game to someone who has never played Destiny 2 before, that is a different story. By now there is multiple expansions, which grant access to different activities, the leveling and weapons systems in-game have become much more complex, and players who have been playing the game for longer already have much better weapons and equipment; all this can make the game for a new player quite a handful.

So lets answer the question, would I recommend the game to someone who is considering the game now in 2022 and who has never played it before?
Short answer is yes, although I would recommend that person to try the base (free) game before hand keeping in mind that all that will give you is a taste of what Destiny 2 is today, and that if you do like the game after that, getting the rest of the content will not be cheap. The base game will introduce you to the mechanics and some basic game modes so you can get an idea of what the game is like.
Once you determine weather you like the mechanics and the basic game modes, now is time to decide how much money you are willing to pay to get the the real game, because the game being "free" is a total lie, most of what is worth doing in Destiny 2 is locked behind those expansions and season passes.
This in my opinion is the biggest challenge for new players, those who have been playing the game for a while have had time to prepare and buy this expansions as they came out. A new player would have to buy them all together or at least the "better" ones.

So basically would I recommend the game?
Yes, but only if you are willing to pay money for the expansions and season passes if you happen to like the game.
If you are looking for a free to play game, look somewhere else.\t\t\t\t\t
