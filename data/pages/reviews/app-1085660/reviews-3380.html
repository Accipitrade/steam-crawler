\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a review for the PVE content only.

Good :

- beautiful game and one of the best art direction I've ever seen
- great music score
- very good gunplay
- good voice acting

Bad :

- a generic story. Bad vs Evil thing. Very manichean and nothing interesting or unexpected
- in a pve perspective Destiny 2 is too repetitive even for a farming game.
- the itemisation system is bad. Too much useless gears and weapons.
- only one "exotic" weapon and one "exotic" gear allowed while there are tons of them available. Balance ? Well, they could have allowed players to equip more than one "exotic" gear and weapon and balance the game accordingly, like almost all other classical looter games do.
- quest log is a mess and a real nightmare for returning players who had a lot of pending quests before leaving.
- economic model is very confusing and I think it's made on purpose. I already paid for a lot of content and honestly I, after a while, don't even know what is the content I have access and what is "vaulted" (made unavailable by Bungie). But to be fair, there is a lot of things to do as a F2P player, that is sure. The season system is also terribly confusing.

Conclusion :

Destiny 2 is a good game with very solid gunplay and an exceptional art direction but it suffers from the repetitive quests and the fact that those tasks offer uninteresting rewards. It's basically a run to raise your item score in order to do more higher level of the SAME content in order to do Raid contents... Destiny 2 is based on that mmorpg mechanic but it doesn't fit well with this kind of game. Compared to Warframe in where you farm base materials to build the gear you have chosen, this is a very poor choice of game design.

Thumb up because Destiny 2 is now a F2P game with a lot of free content and you have nothing to lose by testing it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a new player who just started from the launch of the Free-to-Play model, I can honestly say I'm hooked. There game is FULL of different activities to do for all sorts of players, most of it being free. Because it can be a little overwhelming what you should do once you start, I would like to highlight what activities this game has to offer.

Open-World Exploring : There are 8 PVE planets you can travel to do all sorts of activities. They are unlocked one by one with the new free-to-play model, but are obtainable very quickly (This is to not overwhelm you from the very start). You could do some quests/story missions, bounties (side objectives that reward materials), Hunt down high valued targets for loot, search for hidden, Lost Sectors (mini dungeons), look for caches, and even join publics events in which all players on the map can join. There are players running around the same area and you can choose to play with them or alone. These planets are very fun to explore and you can earn planet exclusive materials to return to the different NPCs at each planet for rewards. All of these will give you gear and XP, allowing you to lvl up your Power Level.

Strikes : Strikes are similar to dungeons in other games. You go in as a team of 3 (or less) and go through the dungeon and see how the story unfolds. There are different levels of difficulty and each week there are new modifiers that change how you should approach the strikes (Such as taking heavy damage while in the air). With a higher power level you can increase the difficulty and have better chances for Exotic rewards. The gameplay is fast paced, and the bosses can be quite fun and challenging.

The Crucible : This is the PVP in the game and my favourite mode to play. There are a variety of modes to choose from, the most popular being Control (6v6 Domination). There are some modes that are always available (FFA, Control, 3v3 elimination) and there are some modes that change with every week which keeps the PVP feeling fresh. All the gear you get from the game can be used in The Crucible and the power level of your gear is balanced with higher or lower level players (Except for an event called the Iron Banner). You will be rewarded every game with Tokens to bring to the PVP NPC as well as new guns. If you really wanted, you could play this game EXCLUSIVELY pvp. Jump in with some friends and kick some ass.

Raids : I do not have any experience with Raids... but they are similar to Strikes in which you bring a group 6 players and fight challenging enemies, obtaining high level gear. You need to find the group yourself I believe as there is no Match-making for this mode. I wish I could say more but I have yet to try this. Hoping to do some raids in the near future.

Gambit : Gambit is a mix of PVP and PVE. You have two teams of 4 go up against each other in a race to kill the boss, the Prime Evil. You must run around and collect motes (up to a max of 15) to place into your teams bank. You need 100 motes to summon the Prime Evil. Depending how many motes you are depositing, you can also send an enemy to block the other times bank, forcing them to kill it first before being able to deposit. As the game progresses, You will be able to go through a teleporter that takes you to the enemies side (an identical map of the one your team is playing). You can kill the other team to make them lose the motes they are holding, and even heal their Prime Evil to slow down their progress. It's a very creative mode and once you understand it, it can be a lot of fun messing with the other team and racing against the clock.

Campaigns : You can also choose to play through the games 5 campaigns which are very story heavy. You can get them from Holliday in the shipyard (furthest east) on The Tower, the game's hub. As a free player, you are able to play through the games first 3 campaigns! Pretty awesome if you ask me. Each DLC comes with it's own story and campaign to play through. You can earn rewards playing this mode as well as exp.


What's fantastic about this game is there really isn't just one way to play. You are free to play the modes that YOU WANT to play. They all reward you with gear and EXP and even some of the most powerful weapons and armour in the game if your lucky, Exotics. I personally think they are all worth doing and a lot of fun. You will never run out of things to do, and each week you will receive weekly quests for each activity, which can reward you with high level gear (After you hit power level 900, which only took me a few days to do).

So do yourself a favour, dive into this awesome FREE game and try out each of the different modes, find which modes you like to do and have fun!\t\t\t\t\t
