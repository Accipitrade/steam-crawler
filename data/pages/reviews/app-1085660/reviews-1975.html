\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE: Editing it again because devs love to screw things up.

I've been a long-time player from the early days and here's my insight in this DLC.

Pro:
- Solid campaign, ends with cliffhanger note, some interesting revelations about the forseen "enemy" of the Traveler.
- Re-introduction of Eris Morn (missing since D1Y3) and the Moon.
- Unique Season Pass (aka Battle Pass tier) system which offers a variety of cosmetics for paid players and F2P.
- New raid with new mechanics, new "Overshield" system (to halt the Power Creep moveset) and new raid Exotic.
- New Exotics, as well as returning ones
- New Dungeon
- New "Ritual" weapons (successors to Pinnacle weapons)
- Beyond Light: New planet (Europa), new lore and new subclass (Stasis).

Con:
- Campaign is relatively short and very grinding (4 missions long, a lot of bounties to grind in order to progress).
- Story very lineal; ending with cliffhanger seems to be on purpose but clearly lacked oversight at a long run.
- Season Pass has been removed and you're forced to buy separately any season regardless.
- Season Pass tier gives little reward to an already-deprecated system.
- Past seasonal mods are deprecated once new season comes, ultimately deeming invaluable armor pieces with said seasonal mod slots (i.e - Season of Uyding mods with SoU armor sets, Season of Dawn mods with SoD Armor sets).
- Broken subclasses, and exotic weapons, that were not tested prior to release.
- Mandatory SSBM-ish system added to PVP mode, severely restricting yourself in a weird and arbitrary k/d system (either you get people destroying you or you get people that play cheating).
- Lack of communication from developers in important matters, while deflecting playerbase opinions (i.e - fixing PVP, adding more balancing, retuning deprecated exotics, adding more fun, etc).
- No Vendor refresh since Year Two.
- Raid armor sets are identical base mesh from Curse of Osiris' Eververse armor set.
- 2 New strikes that offer no nightfall loot, less an emblem or cosmetic item.
- Ritual weapons underperforming due to previous iteration's power creep (people still play with Y2 Pinnacle Weapons).
- Seasonal gear removed once new season is launched.
- No Eververse bounties, Bright dust pricing inflated and BD sources were cut low to 2 - 3 bounties from weekly activities "for balancing reasons".
- Eververse related: Adding boons that are too close to the P2W line.

Post Beyond Light add-on:

- Removed 75% of world loot (weapons and gear) from 3 years leaving basically nothing to attain or pursue. Pinnacles are now sold at kiosk for a joke.
- Due to sunset and DCV: NO Red War campaign nor raids, NO F2P for newcomers (newcomers got shafted hard). Only three raids exist.
- Bright Dust got shortened, again, reducing the seasonal amount you could grind from 45k to 23k (almost 50% out of the window).
- No new core activities: Beyond Light added ONE (technically two, but it's the revisited version of the Omnigul strike) strike and ZERO PvP // Gambit maps.
- Recycling: Beyond Light armor for three main City factions (Vanguard, Gambit, Crucible) is essentially the SAME armor (same design, same shapes, same elements) EXCEPT colors, and the "pinnacle" is basically a Beloved sniper with laces.
- Dull seasonal activities: Wraithborn hunts are the worst aspects of both Unworthy and Undying, crammed in a nice package of "go here and get this, go there and get that" for a gun with a perk locking system that doesn't work (this one CONFIMED by multiple players).
- Beyond Light plot: Back with Shadowkeep trend, it is again 5 missions and bounties for power level scaling. Very niche.

Overall, don't recommend this game. It has a lot of faults and most of the "F2P" aspects are just there to milk money from naive players and white whales that have complete disregard for thinking.\t\t\t\t\t
