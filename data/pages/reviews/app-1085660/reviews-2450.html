\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played D2 for a while on Battle.net when it was first released, and while the core gameplay was a lot of fun (Bungie really knows how to make a shooter), the end-game loop was pretty shallow and unsatisfying. There was a whole lot of "end-game content," like Strikes, that were nearly or literally pointless from a rewards perspective. Some content, like "Nightfall" Strikes, were both unrewarding and insanely difficult, making them just not worth the effort of doing. The raid was the only source of upgraded gear, and beyond being weird, convoluted and really contrived, it rewarded duplicate items more often than not after the first handful of completions.

I came back after the move to Steam and hearing that things had improved, and boy have they improved. To be honest, I never thought Destiny 2 was a bad game, but (having not played the first Destiny), I didn't know what I was missing out on. First of all, Forsaken is by far the best single-player campaign of the game so far, and the first one that had me actually caring at all about the story or lore at all. But more importantly, the end-game loot grind and character building strategy are way, way deeper than they were before. (They're also more complicated, so prepare yourself for lots of Googling.) Maybe the single biggest difference is that weapons you acquire now have randomly rolled perks, so receiving duplicate items is no longer an eye-rolling disappointment, but an exciting chance to acquire a better version of your favorite weapon. Beyond that, there are more armor stats and stat variability, a more meaningful gear modding system, a customizable "Artifact" that changes every season and has a huge impact on how your build works, and a whole lot more that I haven't discovered or managed to parse yet. All in all, this means way more opportunities to mix and match different class abilities, armor pieces, weapons, mods, and artifact upgrades to find unique and interesting synergies and really fine tune your character build to suit your play style. I'm 30 hours into the new and improved Destiny 2 and I really feel like I've barely scratched the surface of even understanding what's possible. I can already sense this game becoming my new long-term obsession, for better or worse.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a free game, you can't get any better than Destiny 2. You do not need to buy anything to enjoy the experience, microtransactions are all for looks from what I can tell - no actual new equipment (although I haven't looked through them in depth). You don't need a fancy gun/armor skin to excel.

As many before have said, there is a ton of content and things to do, I too feel I have barely begun to figure out everything there is to earn and build out. I finally purchased a season pass but its possible to play for a long time without getting bored or buying anything. Gun play is excellent and pretty much every weapon can be used successfully. Its possible to be satisfied without ever going into pvp. But I love the pvp components; there's a bit of a learning curve, but that's always the case. If you aren't a huge fan of all-out pvp, there's always pvp lite: Gambit.

It can feel grindy when going after certain items/quests, but it can sometimes be done with a variety of activities rather than the same thing over and over (not all though). Part of the season pass is making some of these grindy parts pass quicker, but again, its not necessary. There is also not much of a tutorial or background into whats going on in the story, it took me a while to figure this out and plenty of forum reading. I'm still learning how to properly complete certain activities that provide more unique equipment.

Its free and the game play is excellent, you have nothing to lose in trying it out except a few hours of your day. Even if you only play for 15 hours - we've all paid for games that short.\t\t\t\t\t
