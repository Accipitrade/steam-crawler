\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been so invested in this game both time-wise and financially, and have played on and off since Destiny first came out. I am tired of being fed content that mechanically and artistically is amazing, yet still feel I'm losing when I play.

1) I have to pay the exorbitant amounts each DLC and season that comes out. Stasis and Strand subclasses are locked behind DLC. Most of the dungeons in the game are locked behind DLC, and ALSO need to be purchased separately from said DLC. Many locations are locked behind DLC, including raids in said locations. Many exotics are locked behind DLC. Each season needs to be purchased in order to participate in a meaningful way, on top of generally needing to purchase the associated DLC and dungeons.

2) How many times do I need to replay the same content? I'm not talking about strikes, or even playing the same campaign or missions on different characters, but about how Destiny 2 was initially a worse Destiny 1, then how they reinvented the tutorial, then how they retconned parts of their existing content, then reinvented the tutorial again, among other things. And now, it's almost the opposite but still similar problem: if you care about the lore, or even just want to know what's going on, having not played previous seasons means you missed out on a lot. For example, why was Titan gone for so long? Why is Mars' open world gone?? Why is the Leviathan gone gone??? The entire map set to Titan disappeared since I stopped playing after the Season of Undying, back during Shadowkeep. I never would have known if not for the current Season of the Deep, and seasonal missions do an awful job of reviewing what happened. How am I supposed to know/realize why content I've already paid for disappeared? Speaking of which...

3) Content I already paid for has either been retired in a literal or useful sense. I am a primarily PvE player, so I was surprised when the topic of sunsetting was announced while I was on hiatus. Sunsetting isn't just nerfing, it's removing items from the game either literally or usefully (reducing their light level to the minimum value). Goodbye progress toward Recluse, Mountaintop, Luna's Howl, 21% Delirium, and many other weapons I wasn't using in PvP but were still great in PvE content. Goodbye Oxygen-SR2 and exotic and faction quest progress I had toward several different equipments. And as mentioned before, entire maps like the previously-mentioned Titan, as well as maps like Mars and the Leviathan, are gone now. I still don't know what happened with Mars, only have guesses. And as far as "replayable quests" they have, seasonal "campaign" quests are excluded from that list, so I guess I can only find out if I ask around or search YT...? So much for Leviathan raids and events, or Mars lost sectors and open world: I'll never be able to play them again, and with that have the opportunity to complete achievements or obtain gear from said locations.

4) Microtransactions suck. It's pretty universally agreed that microtransactions are fine if they're limited to cosmetics, and it's nice when currency to purchase said microtransactions isn't limited to paid currency: in Destiny's case, these are called Motes. It does suck, though, when microtransactions cause the game to become pay-to-win. Season passes are microtransactions, as are dungeons. Paying for the season pass doesn't only unlock more cosmetics and cosmetic currency, but also rewards a substantial amount of farmable material, and xp boosts to progress season pass and seasonal artifact material at a faster rate than those without the season pass. Especially this season, Deepsight Harmonizers are the worst offenders of pay-to-win seen yet. They allow weapons that are normally unfarmable or difficult to farm the ability to be crafted with perk choices, in a game where substats for weapons are farmed. Oh, did I mention that not only are Mote rewards cut, but mote costs skyrocketed? Now why would Bungie do that?

5) This one might be more subjective than the other points I made, but there is a LOT of time-gating in Destiny 2. I'm not talking so much about seasonal activities or daily/weekly activities as I am as previous DLC that still time-gates itself. Why is it that it still takes several weeks to unlock Stasis for each character? Aspects are still locked behind a limited pool of weekly objectives.

6) Why is there still a glimmer cap? Why was the game was "updated" to have to purchase aspects of subclasses that were previously unlocked for me, and each upgrade (of which there are around 15-20 per subclass) 10% or more of the glimmer cap?

The gameplay is fun for me while doing strikes, the campaign, and seasonal story missions. For me, the PvP is not very fun, but I know it's a huge draw to a lot of players so I'll leave out my opinion on it. PvP is another rabbit-hole of complaints and negativity I like to avoid. The raids are easily the best part of the game: they're pvp and with a larger group, and the payoff to the cooperation and time spent in raid is always tremendous to me: excellent armor, weapons, and cosmetics that look really cool. But through it all I'm really tired of feeling like I'm being sidelined by Bungie, what with the host of bugs and maintenances that were previously unheard of even two years ago. The retconning of content, especially paid content, and making me pay again for something previously given to me. And finally, having to pay for something so I can pay for something so I can pay for something so I can play the game to pay for something else to farm for something so I can play better, only to have that thing removed from the game. I'm tired of being squeezed for my time and money.\t\t\t\t\t
