\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI keep buying this game, bought Destiny one and all the expansions on PS4. Bought 2 an all its expansions on Bnet and shadow keep on steam. I keep falling for it hoping for this game to be good, and it keeps failing. The 10 hours on steam is not including the at least 200 I have put in on Bnet and the other 200 - 300 on PS4. In all that time I barely understand what I am supposed to be doing to advance. I feel lost like a new player and the game has nothing substantial in place to guide me. You are reliant on finding a clan who is willing to guide you. Personally I just wanna hop on kill come Vex and move on with my day.

They show the basics of this game well enough, but get comfortable with google because you will get a quest quite often that gives you an objective that means absolutely nothing to you and no other information to go on "go fetch the whatchamacallit from random person you've never seen before". They don't even tell you the zone to find this objective in most times its just sloppy quest writing. I am used to reading my quests in an MMO at the very least put the information I need to complete the task in the game.

You definitely don't play this game for the story, at least 2 has started to add some of the lore into the actual game not forcing you to read it on their website.

Everything about this game leaves you going "wtf does that mean" I enjoy open world but there is literally no sense of cohesion to any of your tasks. You will be on the moon one minute and then sent to the EDZ the next. It's cool they want to reuse old content but it should not be as part of a main content quest for the next expansion.

The best way I can describe this game is Chaos, it has no rhyme or reason to it. You just "do stuff" Though I will say the quest window is FAR easier to read now. I am super confused on why I am getting bounties or goals that recommend a power level of 970, that's not even possible at the moment. Why is that content even something I can access? I really need to stop giving Bungie money, like a fool ill keep coming back hoping its better and ill inevitably be disappointed every time.

It just seems like all of the issues from the 1st game have followed along repeatedly. Sloppy writing, lazy objectives, sub-par content extremely overpriced content (though the price is certainly better than it used to be) Shadow keep is NOT worth $40, the main objective takes less than 10 hours.

The only thing this game 100% nails is game play. The game play is FUN, but it just doesn't out weigh the absolute lack of organization present in every single system of the game. Its like everything was designed by a different person and no one was really worried how those systems would work together. So you end up with this monster of bolted on sub systems that someone has to make work and it ends up lacking any cohesion.

The game is in a really good place after launching on steam. People are super happy about it, I will lay money that player retention will drop through the floor. The population wasn't great before it left Bnet. Nothing they have done with Shadowkeep is going to change that, if anything players are going to leave even faster.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLikes:
Good customization of armor and weapons
Wide variety of weapons
Good variety of maps
Lots of missions (if you figure out where and how to play them) ie; main story lines, patrols, adventure, PVP, PVE, open world, specific missions for specific areas.
Graphics are awesome with gameplay fast and intense.
Game is responsive and does not lag out.
Cons:
Unless you played the original Destiny you will be overwhelmed and confused as to how to start and where. You will find yourself looking up help videos in order to learn most of how the game works and how to play.
Missions are rarely explained and left to you wandering around to find them and then figuring out how to complete them
Some missions require a team - making it very difficult for solo players.
Some missions are extremely complex and with no information on how to successfully complete them.
Summary:
A good game despite its complexity, will require a high tolerance of frustration to learn the basics.
Have an iron skin, you will need it as you initially blunder around not knowing what to do - you will be called on it.
Best played with a team, not the for solo players - community tends to be clannish and not friendly to outsiders.\t\t\t\t\t
