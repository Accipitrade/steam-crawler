\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: I love the game, but experiencing it comes at a price.

While I enjoy playing Destiny 2, I'm stuck in this perpetual phase of catch-up. I stopped playing for a few years, after they started removing paid content, but I picked it back up lately as I really had no other game to fill the void it left behind. Now that I'm back, my opinion of it is still pretty much the same as before. Fun gameplay, but the story is still struggling. And the story is only struggling, that I can tell so far, because of a lack of streamlining literally any of it. Like I'd said, I'm stuck in a perpetual phase of catch-up after having been gone since after Black Armory. While I played Shadowkeep and Witch Queen after they came out, I never really stuck around afterwards. Just left each time feeling more confused than I was before I had done them. The game has this issue where you have to know what missions go in what order, chronologically speaking. And while that's not a super big issue, the fact that it's been like that for years is kind of a joke. That on top of the content removal, paid or not, makes trying to understand the story in any capacity an absolute chore. Sure, I can find YouTube videos or whatever that can help with that, but people shouldn't have to go outside of a game to experience said game's story, unless they want to for some reason. Destiny 1 had that issue, but in the form of Grimoire cards, but at least it didn't suffer from content removal such as Destiny 2 has been. As someone who loves the story, gameplay, characters, lore and overall universe of Destiny, it pains me to see any of it being made to where players cannot ever experience it ever again. So as it stands, I have to say "no" to Steam's "Do you recommend this?" But my real answer would be "Yes, but just know what kind of game you're getting yourself into, if you do."

Pros:
- Amazing gameplay.
- Lovable characters.
- Unique and worthwhile universe.
- Fascinating lore.

Cons:
- Periodic story content removal, be there or be square.
- Missions are not streamlined, hard not to get lost or confused on what to do and when.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played this game for years and it used to be great. And back in 2019 I would have recommended it no question.
But over the years Bungie has screwed over the players and the game time and time again.

Back in 2019 there were only 2 DLCs: "Forsaken" and "Shadowkeep" They were massive expansions with a whole host of new weapons with a long and unique Campaign, and for a very modest price. The game had a total of 5 campaigns to play, 3 of which being totally free to the player.

Fast-forward to today and it's quite grim. The 3 campaigns are gone. And the Forsaken DLC has been stripped of it's campaign too, leaving nothing but the dungeon and it's exotics. All the while still charging full-price for it. New players will never get to experience these stories, and that is unforgivable.
It seems like Shadowkeep is untouched but aside from that all legacy content has been stripped from the game.

Replaced by over priced dlc to the point where you basically can't play the game unless you own the $60 plus expansion. (note Forsaken and Shadowkeep were $20 and $25 respectively)

Maybe the game has gotten better, I haven't seriously played since 2020. But as it stands now, I can't recommend anyone play Destiny 2 anymore. And it truly is sad.

"Look at how they massacred my boy"
A quote that can really sum up the state of this, and many other games.\t\t\t\t\t
