\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNew players may get "overwhelmed" by the amount of quests and activities. Most of all by the story quests since the game itself does not tell you which comes first and which not. And since many side quests and stuff (most of them available from the start) seem to assume you have gotten to specific stages of the campaign or at least reference certain happenings that happened in specific stages of the campaign, the ordering may seem chaotic- e.g. going on strikes that are pretty much final campaign missions with cut-scenes that you have no context about.

BUT, other than the "throw you in" chaos, if you can get over that by catching up and doing some research yourself, the world really does seem to open up. There are tons of content and it is really much a quest-driven game. I've personally called it "the Pokemon Go of gun-collecting" and it really is. The collector element is strong if not the driving force of the game.

PvP seems decent. I still don't quite currently know the meta but it's fun regardless. Though I don't know what it is that allows some players to 1 hit KO me with a melee when I have to 2 hit at least, but that seems kinda unfair.

Only other con is its long-ass load times. Want to get to the "choose your character" screen after opening it? Gotta wait a while. Want to travel anywhere? Gotta wait a while. Want to PvP? Gotta wait awhile in between every game. It's definitely something to consider especially if your computer isn't that great and you're playing on LOW or something. All that time from load-screens can really add up fast. Personally I'm usually alt-tabbing to do something else during every load-screen.\t\t\t\t\t
