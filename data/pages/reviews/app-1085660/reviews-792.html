\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh boi Destiny 2, mixed feelings really..... Don't let my hours fool you, I used to play this a ton when it was on Battle net. Bungie did old Thanos move and snapped their fingers, essentially deleting half of the game.
Imagine release of Destiny 2, you are a fan of series and decide to buy the best edition of it, to receive DLCs when they get released. The DLCs were really nice, cool storylines with bunch of weapons and armors to grind for. Then Forsaken gets released, solid expansion, ton of content, so much to do in the game. Nothing to complain. Later they released Shadowkeep which felt kinda unpolished, story content cut to minimum and only few notable strikes. It wasn't that great, but hey, not every expansion can be a Taken King if you know what I mean.

Then comes the day new expansion comes, Beyond Light. They decided to "Vault" ton of previous content to make the game more stable/optimized/balanced. Vaulting is essentially deleting ton of content from the game...a ton of good content, that us loyal fans PAID for. We supported the game from the early days, paid for the content, that basically got removed from us. Sure they said they will bring some back in the future maybe maybe not. If I go buy a car, I expect that they wont come few years later to my house and "vault" my tires and engine for "balance". No, we paid for it and we should have access to it as long as the game exists.

Now hearing from people that some of the Forsaken content is gonna get vaulted....Imo the best content in the whole game, taken away from us...that's honestly a really low move. There are other companies with their own MMOs, making year after year new content, and never removing this much stuff. Sure some things might be removed if there is good reason. But come on, if your game is too heavy, too taxing for servers and or PCs....optimize it, dont remove content, that's not optimizing. Removing content doesn't fix the problem, it creates a bigger problem, the problem that fans lose trust to the company, and to the game. Other companies can handle their huge MMOs just fine after years of content, without removing the content people paid for.

Sure there are people who either disagree or agree with this, this is only my opinion of it, but as someone who used to play hundreds of hours of this game back then when this was on Battle Net, I'm just sad to see what is becoming of this game.. and as long as content gets vaulted, I can't recommend this game and tbh I don't wanna buy new expansions either, let's face the facts, it probably gets vaulted eventually too...\t\t\t\t\t
