I think my first opinion is that the game is not a story game (as it should be). The game offers a-lot of lure but the story never really seems to align and if you try to learn it you’d have to read articles of story. When you first play, you launch into a starter area where you touch the light but Bungie Studios is quick to show the copy and paste missions. When I first played the guns were very balanced with the exception of a few rocket launchers and grenade launchers, it seems that every time they add a new gun in it becomes imbalanced and either dies or is the best. The game offers a lot, lots of graphical advancements, great mechanics, movement but it is all overshadowed because of broken abilities and sub-classes. I think the addition of subclasses in hindsight is a great addition because you want the players to feel broken using abilities. But that doesn’t really work for PVP because everyone would be using different sub-classes. Broken abilities should be stuck in PVE to progress stories faster, I think thats where Bungi studios messed up. With the release of beyond light, the game had a huge shift content vaulting, sunsetting and cheaters, thats how I wouldve describe the original Destiny 2. An updated D2 would now has an anti cheat, it seems like they are stepping in the right direction with both story and conquests, however that doesn’t really defend how many players stopped playing and the content from Destiny 1 to be re-used in D2 and called brand new, unfortunately parts of the community is super in denial and believes that re-used content is the same as new. However I can say this game its in its own caliber, not sure if it beats something like Warframe, but still a fun playthrough for someone looking to play an FPS SCI-FI expirence. 3/5\t\t\t\t\t
