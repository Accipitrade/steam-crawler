I'm sure you'll find something here.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny is a strange game, I know there are some problems with in-game purchases and PvP and various balancing problems/bugs.
But the gameplay loop is something I adore.The return of the bounty system was a welcome one for me and although Bungie leans into them a bit too much sometimes.
This is a grinding game in PvE and PvP, you choose your activity, fill up on any bounties you can complete at the same time and go for your life. If you want to get sweaty; farming of bonus bounties and completion of ALL activities on the map and playing more than 1 character is most effective.

Even though it can seem like alot, the real pull for this game is the incredibly satisfying gunplay. Exotics all feel awesome, getting a good roll of random stats on a gun feels great. Playing with your build and making something you truly enjoy is well worth the investment.

I DO recommend this game, I strongly suggest bringing friends along with you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the concept of Destiny 2.

I love it's story, the mechanics, the gameplay. Just about everything in it, except it's lack of story.

Now, the game has story. In fact a lot of it. My problem with it is the DLC that keeps coming out. Forsaken had a few cinematics and several story missions. The same could be said with all of the DLC. A few cinematics, story missions, good. But the season pass is utter garbage. With each season that comes out another 'event.' What good are these events if the story is in lore cards and going to an npc and listening to them talk a while? Where's the harm in making an actual cinematic, even if short, to explain what happens after? Season of the Undying didn't have a cinematic about what happened to the undying mind. If anything, it was just a minor nuisance that was eradicated by guardians many times. And now, the new season just has a bunch of filler. I want to get to the main story and find out what is going on with the pyramid, but instead it seems that in itself will either be released quite a while later as a raid or part of the season pass (with no cinematic) or an entirely 'new DLC.'

It's horrendous to think I enjoyed Destiny 1 so much more because of the actual story elements present in that game rather than the rinse and repeat of Destiny 2. Yes, the game play can be enjoyable. Addicting even if you have friends to play with and accomplish certain feats with them. But where is the story factor in all of it? The first part of D2 was ripped away from new light players by Bungie to become optional. My friends, who were new to the game, were given a bunch of quests that basically said 'go here and do that' and other little 'introduction' quests. But after that, they were on the light grind like end game players. Yes, you can go find the story optionally, but it's use is gone. Everyone's light is preset already to 750 which annihilates the need to go to any planet unless it's a flash point, daily challenges, or bounties. The story is ripped from them and they cannot enjoy it as they progress and become stronger with their character.

My friends have given the game up as it's a game they don't understand or have little interest in because of the lack of story readily given to them. Instead, I have to tell that what's happened and what is currently happening. But, even then they're still not interested.

TL;DR : Great story, great game play, horrible delivery. Hid away content for new players.\t\t\t\t\t
