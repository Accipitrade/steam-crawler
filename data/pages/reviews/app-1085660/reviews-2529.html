\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a piece of art, Destiny 2 is great. The worlds and landscapes are stunningly gorgeous and sublime. It almost seems as they let the art direction be paramount, then decided on how to write the surreal landscapes in as science fiction. The voice acting is top notch. And the guns feel good. And the characters stand out. My personal favorite is a bipolar AI on Nessus-- I always enjoy questing on that planet because of her.

As a game, it is just OK. My biggest gripe is there are dozens of dozens of quests and bounties each day to do. But they don't fit into my quest log, which apparently can only hold 63, and is stuffed full of storyline and easter-egg hunt quests I have yet to do. To accept these quests, you have to load in to a zone to pick them up. If I didn't recently buy a top of the line gaming computer, I would have already gone insane from the load times.

For a free to play game, it is hard to do better. This game is more stingy on player power creep than Warframe. And that is a good and bad thing. It is a good thing because it feels more of a traditional shooter, while Warframe gets pretty crazy with OP crap that makes shooting a standard gun with bullets seem like an oudated mode of warfare. On the other hand, Warframe feels more customizable, and the investment and gun shining and polishing you do in that game is much more rewarding than the Destiny system, where you grind your butt off for a marginal increase in power to your weapon.

At the end of the day, I'm attached to the world and lore that Destiny presents and look forward to the next chapter.\t\t\t\t\t
