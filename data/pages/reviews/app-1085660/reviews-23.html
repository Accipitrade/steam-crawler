\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMost of my time played was in year 1 and 2, so most of my time played was back then on Battle.net, before I left due to the seasonal model and sunsetting.

If you have time and money to keep up with the seasonal model, this game is unparalleled in terms of visuals and feel. The controls are snappy, shooting is incredibly satisfying, and there are plenty of builds for different play styles.

There are very challenging game modes, and simple game modes, if you're looking for a challenge, or something more chill. I thoroughly enjoy the Raid experience, and I believe whoever is on the Raid team at Bungie almost always does top notch work.

The cons are the game is incredibly expensive, and honestly. they should probably just move to a subscription model like other MMO's. Bungie also has a proven history of making poor decisions and having to walk them back and/or tweak them after the fact, like they did with sunsetting. Since leaving Activision, the experimental nature of decisions has felt much more pronounced to me. PvP is hit or miss, either you love it or hate it.

Overall I enjoy the game, and think it is a genuinely unique and special experience, but it has serious flaws specifically with monetization that need to be considered before playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been playing this game for its entire lifespan, only recently starting a fresh Steam account for personal reasons (roughly 5-6k hours total), and I played a little bit of D1 back in ye olden days.

Jesus H. Christ on a stick, what has happened.

Bungie broke off from Activision a while back now, and that was generally met with positive feedback. "The microtransaction focus'll be less heavy now!" "They can focus more on the actual content now!". That didn't age well.

We get more Eververse items than new weapons each season, seasons got more expensive, and they're charging 50 F-CKING DOLLARS for the worst DLC the franchise has gotten since Curse of Osiris. Bungie gets bought out by Sony claiming that they're working "individually" except that they have funding from Sony. And of course, less than a year later, BAM! Crappy cosmetic bundles that are stupidly overpriced themed around Sony IPs, following up two previous cash-grab promo sets for Fortnite and Assassin's Creed.

PvP is practically dead in the water at the moment. Years on end with unbalanced metas coming and going, scarcely any new content despite having more players daily than the PvE end. And now, with Marathon around the corner, it's all but confirmed that PvP is going to get barely any focus in the foreseeable future. The real slap in the face is them announcing that they have 3 whole massive maps indev for Marathon, while the Crucible (and godforbid, Gambit) get table scraps.

I've known Bungie has been sort of cheap for forever now, since people who bought Forsaken got Curse and Warmind free, and not the other way around. I shouldn't be able to pay for basically the entirety of Destiny 1 with the same amount of money it'd take for me to get a new Destiny DLC.

A fun discovery I made was that despite the Summer Sale, the Dungeon keys are still 20 bucks. Cheap move on Bungie's end since no sane person'd drop 20 dollars for 2 dungeons alone, and those sane people'd rather wait for the Keys to go on sale. Oh wait! You get the Keys and Seasons through the sh-tty ingame microtransaction currency! So technically they can loophole Steam, Xbox, Epic, and Playstation sales and still charge 20 dollars effectively! Get f-cked!

Come back when Bungie realizes how stupid they're being. It was an amazing game, up until Shadowkeep.\t\t\t\t\t
