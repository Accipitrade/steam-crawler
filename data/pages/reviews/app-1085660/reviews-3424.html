\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am changing my review after the devs have announced one of the dumbest decisions I have ever seen a studio make. Later this year most of the existing content and stories are being removed from the game to "make way" for new paid content. Everything people have already paid for and might enjoy, gone. Entire DLCs are being removed, campaigns, planets, important story characters, not just made obsolete or hidden but entirely removed from the game. I can not put into words how I feel about this, it is a mix of frustration and anger that I have never felt toward a game before. I really loved the story and locations in this game, that was the highlight as you will see in my original review before, I played through all of the content 3 times and enjoyed it and still went back to those locations on my characters even after they were no longer rewarding. Destiny 2 was an amazing experience, now it seems like they want to constantly gut content to funnel people into buying the new stuff, since there will be nothing else in the game to do.



My original positive review follows. RIP.

---------------------------

I haven't played this game a ton, but I had about 100 hours played on battlenet before the switch to Steam to give a better idea where I'm coming from with this review.

As far as looter-shooters go, Destiny 2 IMO is pretty meh, but it's gotten a lot better since the original release. Gear stats and power ratings don't mean much for most of the game, especially with the "new light" change where everyone starts at 750. Power rating on gear only really matters if you are fighting stuff at or above your power rating, which is very little content in the game currently, in all other content there is scaling happening that brings your power rating in-line with the enemies you are fighting so it remains partially challenging, but it means a power 900 weapon has no real combat advantage over a power 800 weapon in most content, so it feels like you are only equipping higher power rating gear to get even higher power rating gear to drop. Once you max your power rating out though, that's when you can start playing with mods and hunting for gear with better stat rolls to try and tweak a few percent of extra efficiency out, as gear mods and stats do not scale down.

For me the strongest point of the game is the story and level design. The story may not be the most well written, or cohesive, but it's put together very well with solid voice acting, character development, and cinematics. There is a drive to see it to the end and push you through the amazingly well designed levels, and it does make you feel for and understand some of the characters. I can't stress this enough, I have played a ton of games over the decades, the level design in Destiny 2 blew me away, it has some of the best environments I have ever seen in a game and I spent several hours just staring at and exploring some of them in awe. That's going to be very subjective of course, I am a big fan of sci-fi and I think most sci-fi games out there fail to really make you feel like you are exploring environments like this, they fall short on scale and detail, but Destiny 2 nails it. The arcology on Titan is so good it gives me chills.

The free to play update, some questionable decisions were made, but I can understand both sides of the situation. On one hand playing through 4 campaigns and raising power level from 1-750 probably takes well over 60-80 hours, and I can see why Bungie would want to entice new players into a game where they can jump right into the new stuff. I know there are players that are going to be turned off by that jump in power though, and feeling like they are missing out on an experience no longer offered. The campaigns are still there though, and playing through them even at power rating 750 will get you gear upgrades and feel about as challenging as it originally was (which was not very challenging at all).

So my suggestion if you want to give this game a try, find the NPC named Amanda Holliday on your map at the Tower, she's in the hangar, pick up the Red War campaign and play it through, that should give you an idea about what if anything you like in the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first began my journey in the world of Destiny back when D1 launched. I remember the underwhelming DLCs that launched for stupid prices, how Taken King made the game amazing, spending HOURS running through the Prison of Elders, and playing strikes with friends. When I heard Destiny 2 was going to be a thing, I was psyched. I bought the launch day edition on Xbox One. Got the Cold Heart exotic, and played through all 4 hours of the main story in one sitting. After that...? It was hallow. Back to square one where D1 was at launch. Underwhelming DLCs, unimaginative weapons, Armor that looked all the same, Supers that were almost the same to D1s. For years I waited around, periodically playing while listening to the Devs struggle to keep people around. There was a time where I nearly believed that Destiny was going to die off, and Bungie with it.

Thankfully, they cut ties with the cancerous wart that is Activision. The launch of the Forsaken DLC gave me hope and made me want to play again. Them giving away Destiny 2 for free on PC for a while, made me change platform. The visuals were better, gameplay more fluid, and the community was bigger and stronger than Xbox One. Then they announced D2 was going F2P. I knew from that very moment that this game was going to absolutely explode in popularity. To know that people who haven't played this game because of the $60 price, now having everything plus the first few DLCs for free, is an amazing feeling. More people need to experience what this game has to offer. Even if it's a solo gameplay experience. I fully recommend this game to anyone who wants to try it out for themselves.

Now, about downsides. One thing I'm not a huge fan of is how Destiny 2 is 105GB to install. For most users, 105GB is HUGE. Sometimes 1/10th of their HDD or SSD/NVME. Loading screens for slower hard drives is still really painful to wait through unless you have an SSD. The in game menus for changing weapons/gear/other stuff is still slow and sluggish. Much less so on PC but its still hard to change guns or armor in the middle of a gun fight without going AFK for a minute or so. The fact that they removed the feature of being able to use consumables to reload your guns is a huge bummer to me. It makes tasks like "Kill 50 enemies with rocket launchers" a huge pain because you get maybe 5 rockets to hold and sometimes you can only kill 1 or 2 enemies at once unless you play EP. Not being able to just use your glimmer to buy more Heavy ammo is just inconvenient at best. Explanations for things like world items need more details of where you can use them or what they are typically used for. The random flower things you find in the Forsaken DLC was just an item you find until I Googled it and found out that its used for enchantments, mods, or upgrades. Being able to know more about the world you're playing in by reading about it IN GAME has always been a major point of issue for the players. They seem to be working on it but it still needs more work.

Besides that, the game is amazing and its now free. The only thing you'll be losing out on is some time and disk space for trying this game out. If you enjoyed the shooting mechanics in Halo/Halo2/Halo3, you'll feel right at home playing this game. Worst that can happen, is you don't like it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fantastic game! The lore is so profound and rich, and the combat is amazing. I could on and on about how much I love this game, and my playstyle only improved with the smoother controls of a PC.

But it is because I love this game so much that I am very disappointed right now. Make no mistake, I ordered Shadowkeep and it has already surpassed all my expectations. However, after a point, a certain bug in the system made it unplayable. Like many other players, I have been plagued by the error message that 'the integrity of the game files must be verified'. First, it would crash the game and I had to wait several hours before I could start again. Never before have I ever had to do it with a game more than once, if ever. And it's gotten to the point where the game won't even launch. No matter what I do or what options I employ from online support.

I'm sure this is just an issue that comes with settling Destiny into a new program like Steam. Not everything is a seamless transition. Even so, I implore Steam and Bungie to make a patch and resolve whatever issue is affecting the game so severely. ASAP, if you please. I haven't even finished half of Shadowkeep storyline, and I've been waiting so long for it.\t\t\t\t\t
