\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, this will be a long one. Let me first explain my pedigree, I played World of Warcraft is 2004 (I was 17) then moved to DC Universe Online, Final Fantasy 14 (when it sucked before and then now when its awesome)and then a slew of smaller MMOs throughout 18 years. I hardly played FPS games until Destiny 2 came to PC.

I love this game but, like many game it's flawed. The core of Destiny which is in my opinion PVE activities, dungeons, and raids is absolutely phenomenal. The build crafting great within the parameters of the current season and for the Witch Queen specifically I've had a great time (though I do miss particle deconstruction). However, there are some major issues involving a lot of other elements of MMO-ish/genre splicing games that would give me pause.

The monetization shop isn't the greatest. I've paid 15 USD a month to support a game all its content. I've paid for wow expansions that cost at the retail cost which was 60 USD at minimum and still had to pay the recurring fee and was never bothered I got everything I wanted. Content, story beats, skins you name it and if I so chose I would visit a cash shop. However is destiny 2 they hold back on the cool stuff so you can spend money on it. This will be an unpopular opinion, but I would rather pay a nominal monthly fee and just stockpile bright dust and hopefully they'll stop advertising exotic skins in the Eververse shop when I unlock a new weapon or piece of armor. Also, so I wouldn't have to wait until a new season to unlock transmog, materials, that was a jerky thing to do Bungie.

The new player (New Light) experience is terrible. Bungie has a community of amazing content creators that have solved that problem mostly, but if it wasn't for the others who've dedicated almost a decade of their life to this franchise very few would know how to play this game. There needs to be better tutorials and ease of use for our budding baby guardians.

PVP has been stale for way longer than it should be. I understand development is difficult, almost impossible at times. Devs have to appease people without breaking the game. Balancing is nightmarish and has to be in a constant state of flux. I get it, this a profound task that shouldn't be taken lightly. However, as a consumer I trust you with my money you need to deliver. Everyone that buys a car, groceries, anything else expects to get what they paid for, the same can be said for video games. So give those sweaty, keyboard or controller playing movement gods their due they deserve it and please be swift about it.

Lastly the thing that give a game its soul is of course the story. Bungie has been finally struck an great chord with its story balancing but, it cost so much in regards to content vaulting. They should have just made another game instead of taken huge portions of the game away (I bought it on sale, so this is more for the day one guardians). You will never understand the feeling of watch the most beloved character shot dead by some jerk-faced emo kid. That one scene sold me on Destiny 2 and now its just a YouTube video.

So yes I recommend this game if you can look past it and have some Destiny 2 Sherpas to guide you along the way, but if you're like me and have few friends, questionable social skills, and a pension to play at odd hours. Think twice my friends\t\t\t\t\t
