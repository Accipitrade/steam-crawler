Definitely give this a try if FPS games interest you in the slightest. It may be easy to be overwhelmed by the amount of content readily and freely available to new players, but there are multiple guides online on how to relive the game as it first came out, tackling content as you go.

Destiny 2 is a great RPG-like FPS. Being a free game, it is a perfect pickup for anyone wanting to play a new FPS either alone or with friends, PvP or PvE. Since its initial release in 2017, this game has improved on its lack of content by including all year 1 content free, meaning you get 3 separate campaigns without having to pay for the game. Have you played through the campaigns and still want more? There are multiple activities to participate in, from Mini Games to Easter Egg Hunting, Public Events to Crucible, 3-person Strikes to 6-person Raids. This doesn't include the in-game achievement system known as Triumphs that give you even more to work for. Have you tried all these and still want more? Forsaken and Shadowkeep have been on sale 3 times since Destiny 2 has come to Steam. You can be sure these sales will continue to pop up as the holidays go by and new content is introduced for one of Steam's most anticipated games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI started this as a New Light F2P and liked what I saw enough to buy both expansions (Including the deluxe version of Shadowkeep) and $25 worth of in-game currency. In the past month, I've sunk over 400 hours into playing it, and I'm still coming back for more. (Compared to Team Fortress 2 and League of Legends, it's lightyears better)
The story is good, the campaign is easy enough for rookies to play it before anything else but has better replayability than some games I've played (looking at you, Nuka World). The PvE plays like an evolution of Halo, but some of the changes make balancing for PvP hard. (Curses on all OEM Recluse Mountaintop Juggernauts.)
Personally, my favorite game mode is Gambit, which plays somewhat similarly to the Competitive Firefight game mode in Halo Reach, as it's roughly a 75/25 split between PvE and PvP.
My one gripe is they got rid of the Drifter's fixed-roll Bygones, but *shrug* c'est la vie.\t\t\t\t\t
