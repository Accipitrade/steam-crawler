\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 has come a long way since its launch over 2 years ago. Between Bungie claiming its independence from Activision and changing some of the core philosophies about the game, it's safe to say that this is not the same title that we started playing in 2017.
The new seasonal model that Destiny has adopted is working well to keep players regularly engaged with plenty to do and chase. While some may suggest (myself included) that the ratio of microtransaction cosmetics is skewed a bit too far towards purchases, this can simply be chalked up as an unfortunate side-effect of a VERY large now independent studio keeping the lights on without a big publisher's wallet to back them.
Destiny is an incredibly enjoyable experience, and I recommend it to anyone looking for a fantastically addicting looter-shooter inside a universe full of rich lore and imaginative storytelling.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSootSummary: Best Value PVE MMOFPS on the market, Q1 2020 (yes, I know Warframe exists)

Background/Setup: Longtime PC gamer/raider (e.g. Conquest, EQ/DAOC/WOW). Played alpha/beta year 1 pretty hardcore, e.g. Trials of the Nine endgame PVP and Leviathan Prestige PVE. Came back to a ton of new content with some buddies, super fun co-op game. Setup is Ryzen 9 and 2080ti on 4k HDR.

Pros: Graphical optimization is top notch, best in class sci-fi Bungie feel on gun handling, huge loot pool, unique modifiers to base skills and mechanics (e.g. hold reload for unique weapon fire type), deep systems (if that's your thing), now has tons of Lore, F2P base-game (worth it), fantastic campaign missions post Red War (even Red War is awesome first time around). Fun with friends.

Cons: Patience required. Learning curve is LONG for new players at this point, lost some potential 'fireteam' buddies because of this. Confusing and contrived myopic currencies and mechanics sometimes. PC PVP easy cheating (like any PC FPS), matchmaking is limited, micro-transaction-centric revenue, time-gated content due to 'limited resources' (lol, yeah, sure, $1B spent total on Destiny 2 launch); PC UI is better, but overall still could use platform-centric optimizations. Some quests are just asinine and polarizing (e.g Whisper quest, I enjoyed it, others did not). Kind of boring solo.

Developer Feedback: Roadmap and utilize trending MMO concepts; PartySync in WOW is a great example that could be very useful. Statistical overlays like Diablo 3. UI/UX customization on PC would be a huge boon and provide Steam Workshop community development. More campaign content, stop trying to force PVP on PC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort Version: Things that plagued Destiny 2 in Year 1 still affect Year 3. When items are implemented, they are done poorly. Inconsistent treatment on completing challenges/poorly described or not defined conditions of challenges. A complete lack of respect on Bungie's part for players time. And a bunch more.

I played extensively during year 1, left for year 2, and came back for year 3. From the get go on PC, they should have had clan chat. That didn't get implemented until year 2 and when it was, it was a cluster because clan chat was mixed with all other chats. Have a big clan that talks a lot? Have fun seeing team chat. There are a number of features that should have been in the game from day 1 and either are just getting implemented or are still not there.

Nowhere in the challenge to Solo the dungeon Pit of Heresy does it say, "you cannot leave the instance" or "in one sitting". No, this is listed on their website. What the hell is the point of the checkpoint system if you cannot use it? Especially solo? Can't sit down for several hours to do it? You're SOL. Could you use the checkpoint system on the other dungeon? Yes. Why the change? Both dungeons are treated differently still.

Last major point is that Bungie doesn't give a crap about how much time something takes during holiday events, let alone some of the ritual weapons (which happen to be some of the best weapons in the game). Can only play on the weekends? Awesome, good luck getting any of the rewards that require you to grind your ass off. Example: The Dawning event. You get 1-17 dawning essence per event. Only strikes get you the highest amounts. Most events give you 1-5. How much Dawning essence do you need to finish off the event? Over 3000.

In the end, this is a major time sink of a game and there are better ways to spend your time. Please, take it from someone who has spent way too much time on this game: There are better ways to spend your time and money.\t\t\t\t\t
