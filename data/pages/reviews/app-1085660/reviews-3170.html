\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThree Years post-realease, Destiny 2 is finally the experience that was promised before the launch of Destiny. You can really feel the Bungie spirit permeating every aspect of this game. The memorable characters, compelling narrative all sprinkled with the distinctive writing style this studio is known for. That's not to mention the tight gameplay that successfully adds Powers to the tried and true Golden Triangle of shooting, melee and grenades, making it more of a platinum square.

There is always something for you to do in Destiny 2, no matter your party size. Solo queue in the Crucible for some PvP multiplayer action. Bring along a couple of friends and explore the myriad story missions or more difficult Strikes. For those who crave a more challenging experience, get a team of up to six players together to take on the game's most tightly assembled activities - Raids.

All of this, for absolutely free. Yes, you still have to pay for the Forsaken and Shadowkeep expansions, but there is still a full Triple-A product available, completely free. There is no paywalled progression, no in-your-face advertising to get you to cough up some hard earned cash for pay-to-win equipment or loot. Everything that you can buy from EverVerse (the respectfully quiet in-game microtransaction store) is 100% cosmetic. This is, of course, excluding the aforementioned expansions, but that's picking fly droppings out of pepper.

I will admit that I am a huge fan of Bungie. From Marathon all the way through Halo: Reach, I was smitten with the worlds this Washington State - based company were able to create. However, they lost me with the original Destiny. I didn't feel as though the persistent world, the one that was "always there for you" was delivered in the original experience. The relatively compact and repetitive loop on launch that continued even with further expansions left a bad taste in my mouth, and I left the game. I didn't even want to touch Destiny 2 at launch. That is, until the New Light release, when I thought to myself "Free is the exact right price for that game," and I picked it up. It wasn't but a few hours exploring the vast, complex and seemingly evolving sectors of the game's first available area that I decided that maybe I was wrong about Destiny 2. Maybe I was wrong to believe that Bungie would not eventually deliver on what they had promised. They had done so in the past, absorbing feedback and eventually making adjustments to address concerns.

So, I am giving Destiny another chance, and I think that anyone reading this that may have been put off by their first attempt should do the same.\t\t\t\t\t
