I'll start this off by saying Destiny 2's PVE experience is arguably the best it has ever been. That being said, as of right now, with the introduction of Void 3.0 and the fact that the other subclasses will be reworked in coming updates we are seeing the beginning of Destiny 2's first ever foray in to legitimate Power Fantasy. However, as far as PVP goes, it's another year of BUNGiE looking at the matchmaking and, instead of fixing it or even just reintroducing SBMM as a stopgap, their approach was to, lemme check my notes here.

To... hide the matchmaking list until you're loaded in to the match and will be penalized for not wanting to play vs a 3/6 stack of tryhards? Don't worry, guys. If they can't see it, it isn't real, right?

Credit where credit is due. BUNGiE is half-assedly attempting to experiment with ways to make the experience better for the casual player in that, instead of incentivizing stacks to play ranked or in a separate Playlist they have made occasional instances of "Freelance" Playlist that force everyone to go in solo and pray that they dont end up with sandbags on their team. This accomplishes nothing and addresses even less of the issue, but at least you have the option, now.
