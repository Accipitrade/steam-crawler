  • Fun gun play and movement
  • Content road map for the next 3 years

Cons:

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPROS:
• Good and fluid gameplay;
• Top tier quality soundtrack;
• Very good characters, that have seen more development recently;
• Very good environmental design, making each area feel unique;
• Puzzles are challenging and fun;
• High-end activities require more coordination and team work, making them feel like a group effort, rather than being carried by one high level player;
• Using a non-meta loadout/build feels very liberating.

CONS:
• Vaulting 3 years worth of content and presenting very little new content to make up for it;
• Power creep on some builds (specially on Titans) give very little incentive to play outside of what’s the meta for the season;
• Performance issues that make the game borderline unplayable for many people;
• Sunseting nearly 1/3 of the games weapons, and bringing them back with new seasons, after realizing that weapons sunseting wasn’t a good idea;
• Seasonal and playlist activities are repetitive and can get very stale;
• Removing quests from the game after a new expansion launch, making missions like Presage inaccessible for newer players;
• Almost no content for the Free-to-Play players outside a few Shaw Han quests in the Cosmodrome, giving very little reason for new players to stick with the game;
• Monetization in the Eververse Store can feel quite egregious and abusive with it’s pricing structure, encouraging you to buy more currency to buy cosmetics by pricing them slightly above the minimum quantity that you can buy (i.e. items costing 600 silver when the minimum to buy is 500);
• Bungie doesn’t have a very good communication with it’s community, often leading to PR blunders and avoidable Twitter backlash;
• The Destiny community can be quite toxic, viewing any and all criticisms of the game as a personal attack and responding with vitriol;
• Almost no stress testing for expansion or raid launches, leading to a myriad of issues that make the completion of such activities impossible with the frequent server disconnections;
• Some exotics that are very used, and very old, still have very little in the way of ornaments (like Crown of Husk having a single ornament, and Sixth Coyote having no ornaments, despite both being very popular options due to their effects);
• The use of FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) to strong-arm players to continue playing to unlock seasonal rewards that are going away and never coming back;
• The reliance on storytelling through items and entry logs that aren’t visited as often by the majority of the player base, and depending on a YouTube video to explain the lore;
• The storytelling from the point above is not, if barely, referenced on the main dialogues and cutscenes of the game, making such detail feel more like a fanfiction rather than canon;
• No new gambit maps for the 12 people world-wide that still play it consistently;
• Despite calling itself a MMO, Destiny 2 barely dips into the social aspect of one, making it’s peers in the ganre, like Final Fantasy XIV and Guild Wars 2, feel much more active;
• It’s been 5 years and the old Tower is still inaccessible.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Destiny Franchise. From its strikes in Destiny 1, to its secret exotic missions in Destiny 2, and the numerous raids that clans got to come together to play. All this content Bungie has made is legendary, including the massive volume of lore there are in the Destiny Universe. I never played Destiny 1, and only played a little bit of Destiny 2 until it came to steam, when I really started playing it. When I started again, I was a little confused about how I was going to come back to the game (since I stopped and refrained from playing it on battle.net because it was a game that I thought was impossible to come back to), but the game has changed, adding features that are more "newcomer-friendly" (this feature is called new light). I was able to discover a lot as I played, encountering the 6-man raids that were so fun to play when I came back. I felt like Destiny 2 had a team of devs that actually did work on all the bugs and paid attention to its community (unlike a lot of games I played), which allowed me to actively play the game. Now, I have a clan, I teach raids, and do activities and raids with my clan weekly. This game gives me so much to do each day, as well as allows me to discover more lore and features in the game each day. I really recommend this game to everyone since it is free, is a game that is friendly to newcomers, and one that has active devs and a great community. Thank you Bungie, for this legendary game, and cheers for the next 3 years in Destiny 2!\t\t\t\t\t
