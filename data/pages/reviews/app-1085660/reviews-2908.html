\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hate that I love this game.

Because Bungie has managed to create a beautiful universe and a gameplay loop that is one of THE best that I've ever experienced in over 20 years of gaming. But sadly it is also completely ridden with problems that it'll prevent any one with a shred of sanity from enjoying it.

Like, the bugs. Oh dear sweet lord in heaven, if any of those exist, there are so many ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ bugs. Sadly it's not just the silly ones you screenshot and send to friends to laugh about, but most of them are game-breaking, soul-crushing bugs that debilitate your entire experience of the game. Just google "Izanagi's burden quest bugged" and you'll find that one weapon that decides if you can do raids or not? Has been unavailable for months, and only recently fixed. The bugged elevators and load zones that can kill you in a dungeon that you're supposed to do solo, setting you back with literal hours of work. I could go on about examples here for days, but I'll spare us all that agony.

But that wasn't enough, there's the server "stability" issues. Stability put in quotes because I'm not sure if I want to defile that word from ever being sincere and usable again. Only today, on a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ friday when people just want to sit down agd game, we've been treated to a nice 10 hours of servers being unstable or just plain not working at all. And while the offical Twitter reports are indeed timely and factual, they're so frustratingly vague. "Investigating" and "Issues" is all we're really told, the rest is lost in the murky waters of obscurity until the servers are back up and everyone's happy again for a few days.

I truly hate writing a negative review about this game, but when I keep getting yanked out from exploring these incredible worlds and experiencing incredible gameplay, by immmersion-breaking ♥♥♥♥ like this? There's a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ limit to all men, and I've hit mine with this. Bungie needs to learn how to manage their game, or it will die. Screaming in unending agony and horror.

Seriously, don't spend a penny on this game (cosmetics or expansions) until we've heard back from the people in charge of this game, that things are going to change for the better. Unless you're fine with buying into a game that seems to have set it's path towards a slow, painful death.\t\t\t\t\t

Even though I really liked it, there were things that just ruined the game for me: the base campaign is pretty generic (even though it has some great moments) and what was available back then wasn't enough to keep me playing, after a while.
I came back a few months ago and, I have to be honest: this game has changed completely! I ended up buying both Forsaken and Shadowkeep because I was just having a blast!
If you're on the fence, just try the free to play version first (it actually has tons of stuff to do) and you'll definitely know if this game is for you.\t\t\t\t\t
