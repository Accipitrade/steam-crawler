\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's make one thing clear when it comes to Destiny 2. This game has some MAJOR issues that can completely turn people off from even wanting to install the game in the first place. There have been issues that have just been fundamental flaws from the launch of Destiny 1 that have still persisted to this day and there are issues that have developed over the past year with the launch of Shadowkeep and the excuse of a seasonal model. I know it may say i only have a few hours on the steam chart, but this is coming from a launch D1 player who is making his way from the ps4. Between myself and my group of friends we have easily over 15k hours clocked into the game since D1's release.

The good: This game is absolutely gorgeous on my pc and with a good rig it will run buttery smooth for the most part.(raids can be a little wonky at times) the sound design and music as a whole is pretty damn top notch and there are times that just sitting there and staring off into space while the music plays in the background can be pretty peaceful. As many many people have talked about before me the gun play, there are no games out there that have a feel like Destiny does. The raids may not be perfect and they're not World of Warcraft levels of insane, but some of my best memories come from completing them. While some prefer the raids found in Destiny 1 over Destiny 2 (like myself) the quality of them can easily be seen. They're not perfect, but it is clear that Bungie did put a lot of effort into crafting them.

The Bad: Now that I at least game the game a bit of praise, time to bring it down a peg or two. The grinding and the power level system since the launch of forsaken has been a painstaking tedious grind that has slowly evolved into the same rotation of different activities that have been in the game since launch and has not changed in 2 YEARS. This has cause a lot of the core gearing aspects of the game to feel incredibly stale and will get on anyone's nerves after playing for long enough. The Seasonal content that has come out this year is DREADFUL. There are tiny glimmers of hope that have dropped with them, but the time between each bit of interesting content and the little that is actually given is beyond infuriating. You will burn yourself out on playing the game at this point due to it devolving into a bounty simulator and rerunning the same few strikes over and over and over. I know this was an issue in the previous title and will not excuse it there either, but the drip fed content has gotten incredibly old at this point. Shadowkeep itself released 2 new strikes into the game and one is essentially another story mission. The dlc itself ended up being craft this armor set so you can go onto a spooky ship then fight bosses that you've killed in the previous two years of the game and an old raid boss from destiny 1. Only to end with a cut scene that hasn't been that important to the general game itself until THIS SEASON. There are many many many more examples I can give of Bungie dropping huge moments of lore or story events in the game and leaving them alone for years. (Uldren being made into a guardian by Pulled Pork, The Exo Stranger, ect). They are thankfully coming back to a lot of these plot points, but it's been far far too long to have left so many major events left alone in the story after hyping them up and making such a huge deal out of them. On top of having major things happen in the game or the lore that just seem to come out of left field like with the upcoming expansion. (Erimis suddenly working with the darkness and attempting to become what seems like the kell of kells.) The part that personally bothers me is the fact that unless you deeply look into the lore you would have absolutely no idea what the hell is going on unless you went onto YouTube and kept up with videos from guys like My Name is Byf or Myelin Games to flat out tell you what is going on in the story. I could go on and on about these issues, but anyone who has played the game as much as I have is painfully well aware of them.

Destiny from a new player's perspective: While as a veteran this game isn't in the best of places, for a newcomer Bungie has made it easier and easier to hop into the game and experience the story and the different activities to your heart's content (for the most part). I envy the first time raid experiences that people will have today compared to back then where everything was still being figured out and the meta was still constantly evolving. From what I've been told by a few friends who picked up the game at Shadowkeep's release it was quite a lot to understand at first, but once you got a grasp on how to play you could basically do anything you wanted outside of raiding with very little being explained to you or barrier to entry. There is also quite a lot of content to explore that sadly will not be in the game forever, but we'll be getting more into that later on. There are many different guns to hunt, raids to enjoy, classes and sub classes to tinker with and find out what you like the most. The game for a new player has never been better in my opinion and almost worth it as a free to player, though to be honest there's almost no reason to play this game completely free to play.

Sun Setting: This section is gonna have a lot of opinions, not everyone feels the same on what I'm gonna talk about and there will be debate on these two systems for as long as they exist. Please respect other people's opinions and let's just hope that regardless that these systems work out for the best since it looks like no matter how much people complain they're coming in a few months anyway. Sun setting: The very mention of this word is enough to spark extremely heated arguments in some parts of the community for to be fair very good reasons. The amount of grinding and hard work that went into getting pinnacle weapons and god rolls for certain weapons has been pretty damn insane. Anyone who has farmed for a god roll Spare Rations or Twilight Oath can attest or even worse the original mountain top or luna's howl\\not forgotten grinds. They were some of the most sought after pieces of gear in the game and people really really don't like having their toys taken away. That became very very clear to us and Bungie back with destiny 2's original release. So many people are upset over the fact that their 10's to 100's of hours of grinding for that perfect drop has now been completely gone to waste only for them to bring the guns back for you to regrind in the future. This has left a bad taste in many people's mouths due to the fact that Bungie is yet again removing content already in the game to "sell" it back to you with a future content drop. While this is understandably a serious issue there are a few good things about sun setting coming into the game. For one, it yet again makes it easier for new or returning players to hop back into the game at an expansion's launch and not feel like they have to go back and grind old content for the best weapons and gear to even compete in the current content like we have been doing for the past two years. There is also the fact it will help shake up the meta a bit and give players a legitimate reason to go and hunt new pieces of loot that you most likely wouldn't have looked at because the recluse existed. (something I'm very guilty of.) One thing that is coming with sun setting that just needs to honestly piss off is armor sun setting. This honestly has no defensible reason to be put into the game other then for an excuse of regrinding out high stat roll pieces for players to extend the grind and bloat playtime. Seriously Bungie, there is no good reason for this to be included in the game and honestly needs to be removed.

If you're new and need something to play, this game is worth it to try. If you're a vet then you know whether or not to play this game. Enjoy Guardians and thanks for reading this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove most / Hate a lot

Overall rating = 3/5 stars (rating drop on 7/30 due to bad changes in the game in prep for their expansion, Beyond Light; making all activities extremely difficult and constantly changing the drop rate and power of your gear).

I have a love/hate relationship with Destiny 2 (D2).

D2 is a beautifully designed game with large, detailed, and expansive regions to explore. You do feel like you are walking on different planets with varied terrain, geometry, and shaders that give everything a unique feel. The character generation is limited; albeit quick and simple with just a few variations to your character model (face, coloring, and decorations). Your body is fixed in size and proportions.

From an MMO perspective, they've done a decent job of creating content that can cater to solo, co-op, and competitive play. You can run about 70% of the content without ever having to speak to a fellow player; however, once you reach the upper-middle tier, content becomes very scarce. You either have to devote a ton of time trying to become an elite player; or find an elite guild who can carry you through near impossible dungeons, raids, and strikes. A lot of the upper-tier content is limited to 6-player teams with no matchmaking. So you have to be in a guild ("clan" in bungie-speak) in order to participate.

Worse is that Bungie continually forces extremely painful PvP onto casual players if you want any chance of advancing. The missions change ever week and a lot of the force you into PvP if you want halfway decent gear...and Destiny's PvP is severely broken. It's a haven of cheaters, aimbotters, and exploiters that Bungie does very little to prohibit or restrict.

All in all, the game has a story that makes it definitely worth playing, alone or with others, especially if you played D1; but expect a huge time sink if you really want the nice rewards.

Mostly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
