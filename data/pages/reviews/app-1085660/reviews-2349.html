\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been around with Destiny since the beginning, so yeah I've witnessed it's ups and downs. Also I have about 3k+ hours on D2 alone due to playing it on console(PS4) and got platinum on both Forsaken and Vanilla. Believe me when I say the hours I've put into this on PC are no where near accurate to how long I've played D2, if we want to include D1 all together we're looking at about 5.5k hours

Do I recommend this game? Yes and no. Yes I recommend this game cause when it comes to shooters and RPGs, I've had the most fun here compared to anywhere else, the gameplay ranging from gunplay and movement for example, is top notch and I have yet to have another shooter be as saucy as Destiny's. Destiny's pretty unique when it comes to gameplay. No because if you don't have/know anyone who plays, it can be an issue to get into it and/or see the appeal on why it's a critically acclaimed franchise. However, should you do get into this game, it does require a commitment if you wish to keep playing. I'll be primarily speaking on the PvE side of things, if anyone wants to dabble on the PvP side in the comments be my guest.

Story - Destiny's story has always been pretty weak, vanilla D2 improved on that a bit, Forsaken really kicked things up a notch and showed how serious and dark the universe can be, while Shadowkeep was more on making you uncomfortable giving it the horror vibe(Though the spooky scary theme loses it's traction on your second and third play through) and is just a prelude on what's to come from the armada of the angry Doritos. Don't expect anything amazing on story telling. To sum it up in a nutshell, you're a Guardian, and you in particular are the chosen one and you basically write you're own legend through the actions you do

Lore - Probably the best part of Destiny, the lore is REALLY where the awesome stuff, If you're looking for that awesome story telling, it will come from the lore tabs which range from being on weapons and armor to things you do in game. The world building being told through the lore is pretty dope, my favorite being how the Awoken(My favorite race) came to be, we've known pretty much almost nothing about them until Forsaken, personally in my opinon the Awoken's lore and backstory is pretty dark and deep. One thing to note about the Lore might I add there was nothing in game that you can read anything about lore or the game dropping tidbits here and there about it, you'd have to go on bungie.net or listen/watch to My Name is Byf(He'll be your go to guy if you wanna start diving into lore). They have improved on this since Taken King back in Destiny 1, and when you see how this is being told, and you hear stuff about how us D1 vets say how it was back in the day? Yeah it's really come a long way

Gameplay - As I mentioned earlier, the gunplay and movements is pretty top notch. Strikes are your meat and potatoes content, these are things that you'll be doing for a fair portion of your time solo, matches you with 2 others, and do whatever the strike tells you to do. Crucible is where you'll find PvP, they range from 3v3 - 6v6. You'll find Competitive(Glory) and Casual(Quickplay/Valor) playlists, once a month there's a mode where what you have and use, and level will matter called the Iron Banner, sometimes there'll be silly modes called Scorched or Mayhem, Scorched has you use Scorch Cannons and is rather stupid(In a fun silly way) and Mayhem is to sum it up in a nutshell, "Guns? I don't know what you're talking about.". Basically Mayhem is where your abilities regen 10x(I think) faster, made even sillier depending on what your build is. Gambit is a PvEvP mode, the PvE aspect is killing lots and I mean A LOT of enemies and picking up motes, depositing them into the bank to summon a Primeval which is a boss you have to kill, the PvP aspect is racing the other team on who can gather and deposit faster, all while at the same time an invader shows up every 25 motes to try and screw up your progress. It's an acquired taste but I've always been fond of Gambit, though sometimes I am not a fan of how long the matches can last(I've had one last about 45 mins to an hour, but that's a very rare occurence)

But aside from that the endgame will have you running challenging content like raids which are 6 player activities, and how they play is rather pretty diverse, ranging from long range engagements from Scourge of the Past, to hectic and engaging encounters like Garden of Salvation or Last Wish. Whenever raids release they've always been pretty solid. And if you don't have a full team or if you can only get together 2 guys with you then you have the Nightfalls, Nightmare Hunts, and the Pit of Heresy dungeon. Or if you're feeling ballsy dare I say tackle the dungeons solo, they're not easy to do solo but it is doable.

Characters - You play as a Guardian, create your own character, you have three playable races, Human, Exo, and Awoken.
-Human: I think this is self explanatory
-Exo: You're a humanoid war machine
-Awoken: You're a descendant of the human race, basically a space elf. Or if as I like to call them, blue people, or space smurfs

As for Guardians, you have a choice of three classes, Titan, Hunter, and Warlock, each come with 3 subclasses representing all 3 elements, Solar, Arc, and Void.

-Warlock: Mage isn't an accurate term, they're pretty much battle mages. They're the most fun imo and have some pretty strong grenades and ability regen. The subclasses this class has Dawnblade(Floaty boi/gril with flaming sword), Stormcaller(Angry Sith lord), and Voidwalker(Who knew Grapes can kill). Movement on this class does take a bit of getting used to but it pays off.. a lot. If being a mage using the elements is your shtick, Warlock is for you

-Titan: If you like the good old soldier kind of playstyle, the Titan is for you, They're fun, and punch things a lot. The subclasses they have is Striker(Hulk), Sunbreaker(Thor but if he used Fire instead), and Sentinel(Captain America).

-Hunter: If moving fast and emphasis on gunplay is you're thing, Hunter is where it's at, they're the least fun imho, I do like playing one don't get me wrong, they just don't appeal to me like Titan and Warlock. The subclasses Hunters have are Gunslinger(Deadshot), Arcstrider(Pole dancer) and Nightstalker(Hawkeye)

Pick one that you enjoy, and have fun.\t\t\t\t\t
