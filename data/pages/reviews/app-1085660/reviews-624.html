\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 has become my personal favorite game of all time. After taking a break from semi-hardcore gaming in WoW, and then breaking entirely after several seasons of Diable 3, I stumbled back into the Destiny universe one Sunday morning last October. It brought tons of Halo nostalgia back, but with the luscious looter flavor I'd found so enticing elsewhere.

Here I am, a year later, with more than 2200 hours played. It's still fun to play everyday.

If you are new to MMOs, or tend to be a solo-player, I must tell you that it is worth making friends in the community, whether or not that involves a clan. Much of the end-game content, and exotic-item rewarding quests will require you to have teammates (or be a god), and will not have public matchmaking available. The Destiny mobile app is great for creating or finding LFGs, though, and definitely worth using.

I also highly recommend the use of Destiny Item Manager (DIM) or similar third-party platforms for gear and vault management... so much more information at the tip of your fingers, and DIM has a great loadout optimizer that will help you get the mos out of your builds. Yes, Destiny is a FPS, but builds are definitely a thing, and often the difference between having fun in top level content or needing to stay in low level content.

So what are you waiting for? Come join the fun, and feel free to say "hi" if you see me around.\t\t\t\t\t
