\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 was rough on release. It was not a good time, but I came back for the PC release and stuck with it until Forsaken came out and had some of the most fun I've ever had with this series. Ever since then, it's been downhill, but I've stuck it out. Through Shadowkeep, through half the game being removed, most of my weapons becoming useless through "sunsetting", through zero PvP maps being introduced, gambit being altogether abandoned, being charged for things that used to be and should still be included in the purchase of a full ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ priced expansion, etc etc. Then comes Lightfall. An absolutely atrocious experience that feels as though it was whipped together in a hand full of months. Server instability running rampant, half baked environments, reused asset after reused asset, less than enjoyable game mechanics such as having to farm for craftable weapons in a lifeless world with depressing drop rates as a means of dragging out the "content", and most egregious of all to me, a story nine years in the making absolutely gutted by lazy, retcon riddled, intern-tier writing.

It's obvious that this expansion was used as a means of filling the gap that pushing the final expansion back a year would have created, which would have been understandable if they hadn't charged full price for this unmitigated dumpster fire. And they didn't stop there. They've doubled down on monetization, increasing prices for season passes, charging for specific dungeons, and removing the ability to purchase certain new cosmetics without having to spend real money. And where is all of this ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ money going you ask? Not back into the game that catches fire on a weekly basis. No, it's going into their new (old) IP, Marathon, so Bungo can milk dry whatever extraction shooter player base may or may not exist after EFT, The Cycle, DMZ all have done sooooo incredibly well. Part of me used to want Marathon to fail out of sheer spite for the way things have gone with Destiny 2, but I've long since moved onto the apathy stage. There's nothing quite as insulting as being charged more and more for a game as less and less work is put into it, only to have the devs hype up their next hypothetical cash cow and ignore any and all criticism on the ♥♥♥♥ show taking place in the mean time. At least now we know why there's been zero effort put into new PvP maps.

I want to cap things off with a quote from NuBungie about their mindset on a topic that hasn't applied to them for years:

"So much of quality and delight in our craft comes from overdelivery, right? From giving folks stuff above and beyond what they expected! We still want that - we still want to surprise and delight and sometimes overwhelm our players. But we need to be mindful of the patterns, and the implied future commitments we've set for ourselves each time.

It is HARD to tell a team, that has extra cycles and energy and want to do something amazing - that totally would be amazing and awesome for the game - to tell them "We should not ship this, because it is an overdelivery that will set us up for failure in the future."\t\t\t\t\t
