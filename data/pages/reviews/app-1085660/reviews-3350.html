\r\n\t\t\t\t\t100 hours of gameplay, I tried it all, PvP/PvE casual and competitive. God no... please, no more of this.


This is a console port unlike any other which as per usual, completely ruins the game for PC players. Everything is focused around the player having this controller in his hand, and it reeks of it everywhere in the game.

Cons first, since I do not recommend the game.


- Load screen insanity. Two loadscreens when you launch the game, then you need to press ENTER to confirm that you actually want to play the game, not like you have the option to exit but whatever. Then another loadscreen, followed by another loadscreen which looks like you have spilled coffee on your monitor. Chose your character aaaand loadscreen. Then chose where you wanna go or do, loadscreen with your ship flying.... another loadscreen with your ship approaching the planet/activity you chose and finally you can play. WHAT THE ACTUAL HECK?!?!?!

- Abysmal tutorial. Be prepared spending more time watching tutorials on youtube on how the game actually works than actually playing the game for the first 5 hours. Game tells you nothing, not how gear works, not how abilities work, how to do quests/bounties/activities etc. Nothing at all. Oh, and you start with all content available from the start, so good luck trying to figure out what to do first.

- Quests/Bounties. Since everything is available right away, you quickly end up in the main hub with your quests/bounties filled up and guess what, there is a cap. That's right, you can only hold 63 at a time, so if you weren't able to grab your story quest you are just doing random stuff in the solar system.

- The UI and map is awful, probably the worst representation of our solar system ever. Everything you click or look at is designed for that ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ controller, unintuitive, confined and compressed. So frikken annoying that everything has to be plastered with all these different symbols that you have no frikken idea of what they mean... and often they don't mean a thing.

- RNGesus is strong in this game. First, pray to RNGesus that the gun drops. Then kiss the feet of RNGesus to get the first trait that you want. Then pull his pants down and s*... you get where I am going, there are many layers of RNG. Guns have traits, few are good, most are garbage. Start praying if your GOD ROLL.

- Time to kill in PvE/PvP balanced for controller kids. PvE content is way too easy, even on higher difficulties and in PvP everyone just uses one shot weapons and handcanons. Like literally every player, because they kill so fast and its so easy to get kills with them. It's meant to compensate for the crappy aiming on a controller stick, so the challenge just isn't there... and in PvP? Get a shotgun+handcanon and that's all you need. It's a 1-shot competition.

- Guns in the game suck. Yes, there is the 0.1% of guns that are viable but when 99,9% of the guns in the entire game suck, the correct way to describe them is that they SUCK. Some of the 0.1% guns are fairly easy to get, others are locked behind RNG or endless grind. It's such a shame, because there are whole categories of weapons that just suck and should be ignored. Very simple to find out which are viable, just google it. Straight away, you see the META and guess what... CQC 1 shot weapons win.

- You need 5 friends to fully experience the game. If you are a solo player, you will only experience 1/3 of the game. If you have 2 friends that goes up to 2/3 of the game, and if you have 5 friends you get the full experience. There is only a group finder for casual PvE content. If you want to get competitive in PvE/PvP you need at least those 2 buddies, because that's also the ONLY way to get all the best gear.

- Gear is time gated. Yeah, have all the spare time in the world? Are you a gaming addict living off government wealth-fare due to extreme autism? Sorry brah, you are locked behind a weekly number of gear upgrades, which you can achieve in half a day. So for the next 6½ days... well, "personal narrative" comes into play. Even a casual player like me finds this annoying because there really isn't that much to do. Yeah, I could do some of the 63 quests that I have, but what's the point. I already have the best guns and I can't get more gear upgrades, so why bother. Progression in this game just doesn't exist outside the weekly quests.

- The story. Where the hell is it? Outside of the starting tutorial/zone... where is it? Where are all the characters from the cinematics, the cutscenes all that good stuff? New players want to get familiar with the universe and characters, but guess what they did lol... they dumped it all on a NPC hidden away in a hanger, and there is no directive to have to player go there and experience the content. Like what the actual FFFF xD For real Bungie? I mean, really? Google it, it's true. Want to play to the campaign? Off to a external site, to see how to even get started... for crying out loud...

Onto the good, yes there is some, but it is overshadowed by the bad:

- Because this is a console port, and as we know, current generation consoles are outdated hamster wheel antiques, the game is pretty well optimized. Probably the best optimized game on steam, you can run this game on outdated PC's and still get decent 60+ fps on high settings. My respect to the developers here, this is something you just don't see in PC games anymore. Well done!

- Visually the game is stunning. Absolutely gorgeous. The guns look amazing, the gear theme is incredible... I love the whole Knights with Guns in space theme Destiny has going for it. Weapons look so unique (unfortunately they don't feel or sound unique), the world looks so interesting and you want to delve in and explore lost sectors (once you watch a video on what they are and how to find them). The graphics, you nailed. THANK you for giving us the option to cap FPS at our desired setting too! And thank you for letting us turn off motion blur and film grain (Not sure why the F you put that in the game, but whatever).

- Leaving Activision. I am very happy for your studio, getting rid of that cancer and never again having to listen to anything that comes out of Bobby Koticks fat greedy mouth is a life-time achievement. In a way, you are more lucky than I am, I play Blizzard games and ... yeah :/ Destiny 2 really shows how creative you were able to get, but... when I go through the UI in the game... It's that same feeling like if you play Battlefront 2 in it's current state. Everything was designed with greedy micro-transactions in mind. I wish you the very best in the future!

Conclusion:

Destiny 2 doesn't feel ready for PC. It reeks of console mechanics and Activision leftovers. It lacks a massive overhaul in tutorials, UI and FPS game balance. You need to know this, what works on console, will probably not work on PC.

- New player experience needs to be a understanding of game mechanics, gearing and progression. Make them play ALL the story content before hopping into the rest.
- Weapons need different stats for PC. Have your seen youtube competitive players? It's all 1-shot, 1-shot, 1-shot. You can't push that onto e-sports. It's laughable.
- Make all 3 player and 6 player content available to everyone or at least implement a in-game LFG system. This can work in Destiny 2, it's a looter shooter after all, not a MMORPG like World of Warcraft Classic.
- Revamp loot rarity. There is no excitement in getting legendary weapons/gear, because you are flooded with them. Basically playing Recycle Simulator 2019. 99% of legendaries are trash, heck 99% of exotics are trash.
- You don't make matchmaking based on connection, ffs... K/D/A ratio, win ratio etc... those are fine, not connection speed, wtf. Every competitive game has that 1 dude, with 0.2 K/D doing nothing productive other than getting farmed.\t\t\t\t\t

If you like both, you can do this special gamemode called Gambit and it is PvE with PvP.
Destiny 2 is a game all about the grind, it is very rewarding and very fun along the way. Doing the 6 man raids is very challenging and fun. Then setting up a 3 man team to do competitive is also fun. There is a grand variety with what you can do in this game. All the loot that you get from the hard missions are rewarding when you can use them against other players of mobs.
The micro-transactions are not greedy. I have never spent money on this game.
Here are the only problems, the game is not optimized all that to well. My GPU usage is usually at 30% and CPU usage peaks at 40%.
Besides that 10/10 one of my favorite games out there, i'm glad it's finally on Steam.
Note: Don't buy Forsaken unless you want to play more story mode or don't want to be locked out of some weapons. I personally just bough Shadowkeep.\t\t\t\t\t
