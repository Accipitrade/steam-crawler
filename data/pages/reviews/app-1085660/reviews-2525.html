\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile Destiny is constantly growing and changing, there are a number of things I enjoy and would love to see fixed within various aspects of the game. I have put thousands of hours into both the current and previous installations, and have a unique perspective as a game developer.

First the likes:
-Gunplay. Every weapon feels and sounds unique, and provides many different ways to interact with the mission structure. The sound design and effects impress far after you have begun you legendary collections, while the exotic armors and weapons play off of each other to great effect.

-Customization. While Bungie has struggled in the past to provide ways to customize your Guardian, the addition of universal ornaments has been put to solid use over the last few months. Variety and ability has been handled well, and they are constantly adding new abilities and functions.

-Level/Enemy Design. Each area is amazingly crafted to use both the vertical and arena style spaces to full effect, without becoming a burden to go through. Each enemy faction and type is easy to pick out of a crowd, and varied enough to not feel like you are fighting the same 3 groups of gangsters with different bandanas and catch phrases. All enemy types have a purpose and function they fit well.

-Lore. This game is full of it. After getting through Destiny 1(D1) where the lore was separate from the game, and the people from those stories had little impact on the played game, D2 has taken that criticism to heart and built some amazing stories through both normal and seasonal content. Having the lore ready and available is also a nice way to spinfoil about where the many plot threads are heading, and where the game will take us next. The world feels like a place we have interacted with, and has been changed because of our action.

Now for the Cons:
- Lore (again). On the down side, where do you begin to explain to a newcomer where this currently are? How do you explain why Old Russia is the stepping off point, but you never return? How do you share the things that you have done over the last few years that has led to the current state of affairs in the tower and beyond? Sure, there are plenty of youtubers out there who explain things very well, but looking at the hours of content to pick up on can be daunting.

-The weapons you enjoy might not be what is needed to play the game effectively. Imagine this: you just got your first sword and love it to death. It takes out rank and file enemies without a problem, and can tear through some gold bars as well. But when it comes time for strike or raid bosses? "Those strats don't work well" is something you'll hear a lot. As the games and weapons are constantly balanced you will feel the pain of putting you favorite weapon from last season into the vault, possibly never to be seen again. This allows you to get decent with many of the weapon types, but seeing what you love gather dust is never fun.

-The Content Drops. Again, Bungie have made HUGE strides over the years, but they are still seeking that balance of content release and capability. Right now we get about 4 content drops a year, with timed content and stories that unfold over the months, but it has taken some time to get here. You can see parts of this if you play through the older content, where all of the content was made available at once and didn't particularly lead to many exciting climaxes. The last 2 seasons have been incredible though, so I have high hopes.

-Character Balance. Each character class in Destiny is extremely unique, having their own abilities and armors that complement various play styles. However, you will by no doubt here many stories of the glorious Warlock and Hunter master races. The kit available these classes is extremely versatile for both group and individual play. Sadly, and this is my opinion, the Titan class has pulled the short end of the straw, with their abilities needing more steps or armor to accomplish some of what other class super abilities can deal out. For example, Titans have an ability that grants an overshield for melee killing an opponent. The warlock, however, just needs to make contact with an enemy to receive said shield. While I'm sure many will make the argument in the other direction, each class tends to rise and fall as a balance is developed, so be prepared.

In conclusion, Destiny 2 is an amazing game to try for free and hopefully beyond for you. The weekly events and story pieces are involving and interesting enough to get you hooked. Most of the complaints I have can also be completely passed by playing with a clan or consistent fireteam. Learn you strengths, cover their weaknesses, and don't let anything stand before you. If there's anything you would like some help with, just give me a holler and I would be glad to assist.\t\t\t\t\t
