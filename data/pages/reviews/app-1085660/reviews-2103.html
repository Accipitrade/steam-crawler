\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe problem I have with Destiny 2, is the problem I have had with Destiny as an IP since picking up Destiny 1 about two weeks after it's launch on PS4.

Shoot, loot, scoot.

You see where I'm going with this.

At it's core it is a very good game for what it is. But I've found that as I get older I want WAY more complex and engaging experiences from games. Destiny's gameplay loop since launch of D1 hasn't changed at all. How you engage in that loop has changed, but not the loop itself. If I were to compare this to a game like Ark: Survival Evolved, which has what I would call a gameplay tree where the player can branch off from a loop at any point in time, Destiny as an IP begins to pale in it's implementation.

The variety of game mechanics in Ark is insane, and while I realize that Destiny is not Ark, the game could benefit from a serious shake up in it's gameplay loop.

To give you an example.

Since Destiny 1 day 1, players have had star ships they can purchase or find that have served no purpose other than to change the aesthetic of the loading screens. At no point in the 6 years of Destiny 1 and 2 has there been an attempt to put the player in direct control of these ships in any scenario. No star ship assault on a cabal command carrier stranded in space. Not even an attempt to mimic the earliest Warframe missions where players bounce between flying around in space and landing on an enemy capital ship to wreak some havoc. Which is odd to me because everything about the player ships and how they operate scream that this would be an amazing addition.

I think what really gets me is that Bungie sells Destiny as if they are building out an entire universe for people to enjoy but all you really get is hallways to run and gun down.

If you like the repetitive nature of looter shooters, Destiny is your game. If you feel like you need something more complex and engaging, I would recommend some the survival genre titles that have been continuously developed over the past 4+ years.\t\t\t\t\t
