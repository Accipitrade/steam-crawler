\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you happen to enjoy loot shooters, but cannot stand Borderlands 3 or any of the other options on the market (Including Warframe, which is the closest comparison to make but has its own sets of strengths and weaknesses) then Destiny 2 is probably the 'MMO' for you.

I quote 'MMO', as most of its actual meat is in instanced affairs. While the over world locales can be pretty sprawling and feel somewhat organic at least as far as player interactions are concerned, a specter of over familiarity and routine will eventually start to settle down around you when you have sunk enough hours into the game. The big ticket items are the campaigns and raids, and once you have gone through them a number of times their novelty wears off pretty quickly. (Especially when you are farming things like Nightfalls too for Exotics or the Black Armory Forges).

That is my main gripe with the game, (and some PvP balancing I guess,) but everything else I have to say is positive. The game world has a lived in feel, but it is a somewhat lighter touch as ARGs are sprinkled into the game's Seasons and events that change the course of the overall history of the lore based on character actions. Familiar locales are updated periodically with these choices in mind, so even if you hear Devron ask to meet you for the fifteenth time in a row farming Heroic Events everything else in a given area can feel pretty foreign as of a new update.

The three classes have enough variety to them that they do ultimately feel unique or fit a niche you might enjoy. A majority of the game IS some form of gunplay, so if you aren't a fan of FPS games you may not enjoy D2 in particular outside of Sword weapons+Titan self YEET. For those that don't mind or enjoy it however, there is a lot going on. They're pretty good at refining the core shooting experience, so all of your expected staples are there as far as weapons feeling different and uniquely powerful in different situations. Weapon sway, reloading speed, ADS acquisition, different kinds of sights and scopes on rifles, different velocities for projectiles, hit scan properties, elemental damage, piercing, and then a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ of completely exclusive effects on the most elusive weapons and armor in the games, 'Exotics' often entirely change how you approach basic play from moment to moment when going through different tiers of character strength.

Exotics themselves are neat, too. You can equip one weapon Exotic, and one Armor Piece Exotic, and they usually are powerful enough to warrant you changing your play style to accommodate triggering their effects as often as possible. My personal favorite currently lets my grenade cooking (which turns my grenade into a short range blast of scattered energy after a few moments) reduces the damage I take during that process on demand immensely, and also reduces the cool down of the grenade by a random amount per target hit by it. In essence, infinite grenades or damn near close to it in hairy swarms of enemies.

I could go on and on honestly, but I'd say give it a good day. Maybe go do the Red War story quest or engage in the current season activity to see how you like it. If you have what it takes to be a Guardian, you'll know. The Traveler will be waiting!\t\t\t\t\t
