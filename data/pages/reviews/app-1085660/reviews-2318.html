\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny is the meta for looter shooters. While Destiny 2 had a rough start featuring more story and technical beauty but missing end game activities, it has improved dramatically. The gun play is about as good as it gets, and they are finding new ways to add skill moves to combat. It has a lot of fun content. The downsides would include some lackluster new content between major releases, a lack of aesthetic loot to hunt as they look to sell those (understandable as it is now free to play), and so many guns the differences are less important. Multiplayer is fun, but PC hacking and ddos'ing are problems at times; it also lacks the variety Halo would give with big team battle and asymmetric battles featuring vehicles. It is mostly a small area, 3ish main corridors twitch shooter -- a bit disappointing considering the power of machines Destiny 2 can run on. Raids are very rich and complex, but most require around 6 people and a lot of time investment to enjoy which can be problematic for casual players; but there are some very nice group activities and dungeons that are a bit less complex for such players. I still don't find the strikes as fun as Destiny 1 strikes with their amazing burns, but they have been gradually improving. The trend started late in Destiny 1 life-span of giving enemies exploits such as everything in the zone seeing you and shooting sideways if you are on a sparrow as well as the trend to frustrate player tactics by trapping them in small rooms sadly continue, but there are some abilities and weapons that make this a ... bit ... more tolerable. A good game to pick up free to play, but you are suffering missing out on seasonal items and power levels so they are strongly encouraging you to pay a decent chunk for past and current content.\t\t\t\t\t
