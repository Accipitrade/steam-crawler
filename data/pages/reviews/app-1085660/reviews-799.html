\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a game of hurdles.

Typically, games will introduce hurdles to the player as they journey through the world. These hurdles exist to challenge the player, more commonly in the form of testing your skills, critical thinking and awareness... usually in the pursuit of providing the player with an award for their participation in overcoming the presented hurdle. This award is dolled out in the forms of power-ups, new tools and story developments. Awards are not only in-game, however, as players typically undergo their personal growth as overcoming difficult challenges provides them the opportunity to learn more about the world, their place in it and even about themselves.

Games have long inspired growth through adversity.

This is not a review of Destiny 2 as a video game. It's a review of the mental hurdles you must be able, and willing, to overcome in order to begin your journey through the world of Destiny.

In order to play and enjoy Destiny 2, these are the hurdles you must overcome.

Before beginning your journey through Destiny, you must be able to accept that there exists very few individuals and industries that truly care for or respect their audience. You cannot trust the word or intention of any individual, developer or cooperation with a repeated history of ignoring player feedback, complaints and even simple bugs. Bungie is no exception and despite the fact that talented and well meaning people do work for them, this is a company. Even if Bungie does want to create an incredible game, they want your money more than they want your feedback. To play Destiny, you will be expected, sometimes forced, to pay for content along every step of your journey. This has become commonplace in gaming and has unfortunately become acceptable to a large number of people, but for those who see this for what it is, simple greed, allow this first hurdle to be where your journey ends.

Next, you must be able to overcome the sadness, anger and outrage that will inevitably come when your progress has been reset or invalidated. If you know anything about Destiny, you have probably heard of the removal of a large portion of content within the game. Despite the backlash this deservedly received and promise by the developer that something of this scale would never happen again, the removal of content persists, silently, on a seasonal basis. Weapons are reintroduced with stronger perks than the previous iterations, invalidating the progress made to earn the previous version and new Mods are created with improved benefits for the new content released alongside it, all the while secretly removing, or depreciating, old Mods earned throughout your journey. With each new update, comes new tools for the player to earn which are typically and almost always, stronger than the tools they had before. There will never come a point where you stop chasing the new thing, due to fear of missing out and falling behind in progress.

Also, you must be able to accept that you will be confused, lost and without guidance as a new player to Destiny 2. Important tutorials, story beats and content, the vital who, what, where and why of Destiny 2's world has been removed and you have not been given an adequate consolation prize. Even if your journey begins today, new story beats and content emerges on a seasonal basis. However, not even this season content, which often is home to detrimental and important aspects of the world of Destiny and the direction of Destiny 2's future, is not immune to the chopping block. Once the season is over, you cannot return to the content of the past. In order to understand the world of Destiny, you must undergo extensive research on your own time, either watching or reading articles, forums and video essays outlining the game play, mechanics and story of Destiny. If you cannot commit to the tens, maybe even hundreds, of hours of required homework, you will be lost when you begin your journey through Destiny 2 and more often than not, you will not be able to fill in the blanks by yourself.

Finally, even if you can accept the greed, invalidated progress, ever moving goal posts and missing story, you must accept that no matter how much progress you make along your journey, Destiny 2 will not respect your time. There are no guarantees, safeguards or redundancies in place that will ensure you reach whatever goal it is you are chasing. You can fail to be awarded the tools, weapons, armor or cosmetics you desire no matter how long you chase them. You can spend the entire season grinding away at the games hurdles in hopes of reaching your prize, only for the ever present time limit on Destiny 2's content to come to an end.

Destiny 2 is a game of hurdles.

And I wish I tripped at the start.\t\t\t\t\t
