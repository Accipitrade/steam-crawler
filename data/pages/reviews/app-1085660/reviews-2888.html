\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI joined Destiny 2 as a new light player. I enjoy archery and sniper game-play in the games that I play, as well as a fleshed out world for me to feel invested in. Destiny 2 absolutely delivered on both of these desires. As well, the bounty system in the game rewards you for playing a variety of different weapons so I've also been willing to branch out more than I have in other games.

There is a large variety of play styles available, and almost all of the guns feel good to use (down to preference, the ones that don't agree with me work well for others) and the lore of destiny is quite expansive (and interesting, there is a 4h long YouTube video covering it, which I recommend). The story missions feel significant and the cut scenes are cinema worthy.

Beyond this there is an expansive end game with replayable 3 player missions (called strikes) that switch out weekly to give players a chance to farm specific equipment, as well as different levels of challenge to be approach as a solo player or with a coordinated group of friends. There are 6 player raids as well as open world planets (literally Mars, Mercury, Earth, etc.) to roam and join public events, start adventure missions, complete bounties, etc.

The PvP scene is also relevant, with some of the top tier guns only unlocked through achievements and expositions of your mastery. There are varied strategies and room for learning the maps and outplaying your opponent. This has been one of the first games that I have been willing to venture into the PvP scene and I have actually enjoyed myself. The NPC in charge of the PvP arena (the Crucible, NPC named Shaxx) will scream motivational and celebratory remarks in relevance to your match.

All in all, this game will move at your pace. With the changes in new light, most content is available to players from the beginning (with some that you would need to gain power to be ready to face). You can have a focused session of PvP, or a relaxed stroll around the open world with your friends. This game allows for you to focus on content that moves at the pace you want to move. At the beginning it will feel like there is a lot to take in, but when you learn what things are and what place every gun or activity has, you will find yourself quite at home. I highly recommend this game. I have had an excellent time.\t\t\t\t\t
