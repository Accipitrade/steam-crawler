\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, So the last time I edited this review was August 29th of 2020. That was before Beyond Light released. It is now April 18th, 2022, and there has been a LOT of changes and new content since then. And let me tell you, this game is at its peak right now. It has never been in a better state. There's a LOT of content (kind of, I'll get to that), there's Anti-Cheat, there's returning old content, which makes the Destiny 1 Veteran in me so happy, and the story is very engaging.

A word of warning though, the storyline goes on every passing minute, so if you joined for the first time now, you'll be insanely confused. Honestly, this doesn't bother me a ton, because the lore for Destiny is very interesting.

HOWEVER;

The biggest flaw of this game is that it is labelled as Free-To-Play. It's not. It's Free-To-Try. Without all the DLC, content is lackluster. You can do Vanguard Strikes, Gambit, Crucible, Free-Roam, and the Vault of Glass Raid. That's it. It will satisfy you for about a week, maybe more (kind of depends on when you play, and for how long) before you'll realize "wait, this is all I can do?" The only way you can get the full Destiny 2 experience is by getting most (if not all) of the DLC, Which as of now, costs about...$130 USD overall? When its not on sale? It's expensive.

I have no regrets about owning the DLC for this game. Destiny will go down as one of my favorite game franchises ever. But I would be a liar if I said this game was worth $130. There is no game worth that much. If you can get the DLC for this game for cheap, absolutely do it.

So yeah, that's my somewhat coherent review of this game.

TL;DR: Try this game out, get the DLC when they're on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
