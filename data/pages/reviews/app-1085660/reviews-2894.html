Another issue is how difficult is to do activities solo, like Bungie wants to force everyone to form a fireteam (a party) which can be a pain to set up and even more to find decent team mates, not about skill, but about human decency (team mates that aren't toxic MF), in some old raids is hard to find people because you have to use an external website such as LFG or bungie.net to search for fireteam members as there is no matchmaking built in. Edit 2 (november 2019): I really like Destiny 2. The shooting feels great, each class is unique and there's great variety of weapons and armor to create builds. There are build focused on damage but there are also builds (Titan and Warlock) that can act as support for team mates, TIP: The light level cap at the current season (season of dawn) is 960. DON'T upgrade low level items, save enchancement cores, upgrade modules for when you are at a higher light level.

There's many activities:
Exploration: Roam around the planet you are in checking lost sectors (mini dungeon), looking for small chests, participating in public events (special time limited events that reoccur every now and then. In order to unlock planets you must adquire an certain ammount of experience;

Strikes: Missions with a little story in it with an specific objective (PvE). TIP: Check for bounties at vendors at the Tower. (these are mini objectives like "kill x amount of enemies with precision damage (headshots)") The bounties can give a variety of items.

Nightfall: A strike but with difficulty levels (750, 920, 950, 980, each number is light level). The higher the difficulty the more score you get. TIP: Look for Lake of Shadows Nightfall, it can drop a rare grenade launcher. Also: Check your season artifact, that unlocks VERY important mods like overload and anti-barrier rounds to help kill champions in Nightfalls;

Adventure: Has a sword symbol on the map, can be acessed on the planet. Acts like a Strike but has specific small amount of story related to the planet you are in. There's heroic version that appear every week, is a more challenging version;

Crucible: PvP modes, I recommend not start here. Get legendary weapons first, get better at the game and learn the basics.

Gambit: A mix of PvE and PvP. I recommend watching youtube videos about it first because the mode may seem simple but it is not, it requires strategy. Can be fun even for people who don't enjoy PvP much
