\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI’ve been committed to the Destiny franchise since it was released in 2014. While sometimes this game can be bland story wise, there’s a lot actually going on in the games universe. Much akin to Dark Souls, a lot of Destiny’s story requires you to find it out on your own whether it be in a collectible, listening to dialogue, or reading books or wiki pages. If you’re not much of a story nut, Destiny does try to capture other audiences. There are PVP and PVE gamemodes and there’s also a hybrid type between the two called Gambit. The game also trys to attempt what seems like build crafting and min-maxing where you can chase down weapons and armor with the perks and stats you want. It’s a little more flexible now that where it started from, now your armor can have any perk you want it with (bearing it has the right affinity as the mod/perk, but that something you can work around with changing the armor’s affinity). On a final note this game has its ups and downs. At the time of me writing this Destiny 2 might be in what might be it’s biggest down yet. However every game suffers this flaw, “you can please all of the people some of the time, some of the people all of the time, but you can’t please all of the people all of the time”. As time goes on people will come and go as they discover new things and might eventually migrate to new game. That being said those willing to be committed to this franchise tend to be rewarded with a treat like a new item or secret event by the developers. Overall Destiny 2 has one of the most positive communities out there, but the game struggles with appealing to a broad audience and by not rewarding the casual players as much as the more hardcore. It’s a good game, but it has its quirks that sometimes sets it apart from others in a good way, but some of the time those quirks get in the way and prevent the game from reaching what it was supposed to be. If you like any or all of what Destiny 2 has to offer, then give it a try. The game is free anyways so you’re not hurting yourself financially. If you have any questions about the game feel to ask me and I can try to answer them.


It has now been about six months since this review has been made. Several patches have been made and a DLC has dropped three weeks ago. Even due to the COVID-19 pandemic, Bungie tries to stay ahead of the game in fixing issues as soon as possible. While there are some emerging issues here and there, they have been acknowledged and are working on a fix.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not that much into FPS games usually but this one is a pretty enjoyable PvE experience that doesn't rely on pure reflexes like quite a few games that go for PvE in this genre.

All enemies have at least one weak point (the head is one for most but there is one robot faction of enemies that is their "belly" and it is glowing yellow like the secondary weak point for the space-marine-like faction), rewarding precision shots with a fairly significant bonus of damage but without making it a hard requirement.

Most weapon types have their pros and cons but are pretty strong if used properly, the close range shotgun and sword have a bit of an issue against bosses since they all have either a move to push you away if you are too close or a melee attack with large reach that does a lot of damage but against other enemies they are very potent.
The ammo is somewhat limited since you need drops from enemies but it does not penalize the "spray" type weapons since the amount of ammo you get depends on the weapon (and type since there are 3 of varying rarity and each weapon only use one type).

Things can be a bit repetitive if you stay in the "open world" for public events since those events are always the same for the location (and each planet only has a few types), but there are a good amount of missions, and the environment is fun enough to explore with chests and special areas semi-hidden everywhere.

If you are just a solo player there are a few things that will be a bit hard to do, like raids and PvP but even then there is more than enough content to play for a few months at several hours a day, and if farming to min-max your equipment to the limit is your thing then there is quite a lot to do about that too.

My play time is not completely representative of the time I spent on it since I played about 20-30 hours when it was on blizzard's launcher, and without the end game content I am only just starting to hit the limits of the base level progression (weapons drop with a "level" of sort and your character's "level" is the average of all of your equipped gear) but I have not really hunted for any of the powerful gear yet or even bothered to upgrade anything so there is still quite a way before hitting a real limit.

The DLCs are absolutely not necessary at least until the late game, they unlock a few more things to do but that's about it (they are extra content but you can play quite a while without really needing access to that content).\t\t\t\t\t
