\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl:dr: Not worth all the money for DLC. The Sci-Fi and world-building are very unoriginal compared to other universes (Warframe/StarWars/W4K etc). The endgame is a lootgrind/gear micromanagement loop which disrespects player time. RNG infects everything. There is no real build diversity beyond a few meta builds per class (too few).

I've spent a lot of time in Destiny and Warframe is its closest "competition". Warframe is more "fun" and the SCI-FI lore is a lot better there.
Where Warframe has me in "Oh Wow!"mode quite a lot, Destiny just makes me shrug a lot. It sometimes seems it was written by a 14 year old: It's very.... I want to say *derivative* - without much originality in its story or character creation or world building. It all "looks cool" but - really I wish they had had a better writer at the start or just tried to go a bit crazier.

Destiny takes itself much more seriously thatn Warframe... Way too much. I can't describe Destiny as a "fun" game like I could with Division/Warframe/Vermintide: There is very little humour. Everything is "end of the world"/end of the universe" without any real letup. like I'm locked in the bedroom of an emo. Gameplay-wise there are too many limitations regarding how you want to play and classes are very restricted. especially in highend content. 99% of everything you pick up is useless. RNG infects crafting/loot pickups and especially whether you actually get something valuable after many hours of grind.

On a pure game-play level most content is fun to do the first time or two- but I hate repeating it over and over and over. It's stressful and can be confusing. I kind of zip through the story content (which is OK - kind of B-level Sci-fi fantasy with too much technobabble where every NPC has to be super cool and there's no agency or consequence to anything.) and then burn out very quickly the moment I hit what the end game really is: The Grind. You can tell you're in it when suddenly you spend more time micromanaging your stats and levels and repeatedly cycling through your quest log for those quests that are LEAST boring as you try to inch your way up the power level ladder and improve your gear incrementally (which requires hours and hours of grinding repetitive content to find the rare crafting mats). But the grind level becomes nearly unendurable: Raising levels minutely up and it feels like whatever you do it is going to just be a time-waste.

In the end spending time in destiny feels like a pretty time-waste. I don't think the Devs value my time as much as I do, and they expect me to commit to doing entirely uninteresting activities just for the sake of it. The flip side is that I spent a lot of money to stay current with the seasons and DLC so I'm really in the "Well I spent this much money so I'm going to play it" camp... but yeah I have a lot of regrets about how much time and money I put in this game.\t\t\t\t\t
