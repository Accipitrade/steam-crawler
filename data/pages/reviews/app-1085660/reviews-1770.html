Being a parent + playing games = possible

If you ever wondered if becoming a parent would mean you had to quit playing games for good, let me reassure you that, NO, that isn’t necessarily true. For one thing, you can be a complete tool and ignore your little one and partner and just play as much as you want… now, you SHOULDN’T do that, of course. What you CAN DO, is enjoy your favorite pass-time when your child is asleep (provided they ever go to sleep, lol ) or when your take turns with your partner in taking care of your little one. Well, this has worked for me, at least and I hope it will work for you should you decide to try having kids of your own one day.

Being a parent + playing an MMO/Live Service game = very difficult

While playing standard single player games and non-committal multiplayer games works well when your little one is away in dreamland, playing an MMO on the other hand is… well…. It’s possible sure, but it’s also difficult to the point of becoming almost impossible.
Unlike most gaming genera out there, MMOs or Live service games (as they like to be called nowadays) are very much different. The thing that makes them so different and difficult to recommend isn’t necessarily the grind, the boring gameplay loop, the community or the lack of things to do; it’s actually the commitment.

If you like it then you should put a ring on it

Playing an MMO is very much like being in a relationship with a person. While nobody is forcing you to exclusively date anyone, if you should decide to have a steady relationship with someone, certain things are to be expected of both you and your partner. One of the most important things is the commitment or rather, not seeing other people and exclusively focusing on your partner. In return, you get a happy feeling knowing that you always have someone waiting for you, someone to listen to your problems and someone who will make you happy as long as you do the same to them and, naturally, spend an appropriate amount of time with them.

Playing Destiny 2 is a commitment

Destiny 2 is an MMO. Many people like to say that it isn’t but it really is, there is no denying it. It’s arguably light on content when compared to other MMOs but it’s very much a massive multiplayer online game.

Once you finish the initial story mission(s) and a few mandatory quests, you will be caught in the Destiny gameplay loop. I know I make this sound like a bad thing, but it really isn’t. MMOs rely on this gameplay loop to keep its players engaged and Destiny 2 does the same thing, quite well, I might add. Sure, during launch they had problems with the lack of content but now that the game is free to play (mostly) there doesn’t seem to be a shortage of things to do.
If you should choose to give this game a try get ready to do, at least, a little bit of everything every (other) day. Do some PvP, finish a couple of bounties, play a strike, organize a raid or “How ‘bout a game of Gambit?”.

Guardian… guardian. Eyes up guardian!

In Destiny 2 you play as one of many guardians. Destiny 2 has a rich lore about its world that sounds overwhelming but is actually pretty easy to follow. All you really need to know is that, as a guardian, it is your job to protect the galaxy from evil forces that may threaten it by using your special guardian powers and lots of guns. There is a story with reoccurring characters but it’s hardly what Destiny 2 is really about.

Destiny 2 is a first person shooter and one of the better ones out there. Seeing how it was created by Bungie, Destiny 2 (and 1) received the same amount of polish that the Halo series https://store.steampowered.com/app/976730/Halo_The_Master_Chief_Collection/ received. The gunplay is excellent as is the movement and other bits and bobs. It’s just a lot of fun to shoot things in this game. You have so many weapon types that can suit almost anyone’s playstyle and the game always encourages you to learn more and get better at it.
No matter what you may choose to focus on in Destiny 2, one of the main driving forces in the game will be to always be on the lookout for better gear. Destiny 2 has a loot system where each piece of your armor and weapons has a level and your overall level (light level) is the summary of all the armor and weapon levels combined. At first this is as simple as killing a random enemy and getting a loot drop but, as you progress, it becomes exceedingly harder to gain even a single light level. That doesn’t mean it becomes impossible, but it becomes harder and you are required to do harder activities in order to progress.

If you are committed, you will always have something to do in this game…

While it may seem like a daunting task, progressing in Destiny 2 isn’t necessarily hard, it just takes a bit of time. Each day you will have things to do in order to improve your light level, season level or multiplayer rank. Each week, you can also do weekly bounties, challenges and a larger events such as raids or Nightfall dungeons (the harder ones). Even when you DO run out of things to do, there are always triumphs and weapon collecting. You will rarely have nothing to do in this game (it used to be different during year 1 but now it has changed quite a bit, for the better).

… and that’s why I’m very sad that I won’t be able to play Destiny 2 anymore.

I simply don’t have enough time to play this game. Destiny 2 isn’t just a game you can play, save and come back later after a few days. Even though there isn’t a specific rule written about how long you should play this game, you would need at least 3-4 hours each day to feel like you are making progress… and I simply don’t have that much time. Naturally, nobody is forcing anyone to play for that long, but you feel that urge to keep up with the rest of the players and it almost hurts when you really want to play more but have to quit because you have work in 6 hours after which it’s full on “dad mode”.

My fatherly duties, as well as, wanting to play other games is simply making it impossible for me to continue playing this wonderful game. Don’t get me wrong, Destiny HAS IT’S PROBLEMS, but overall is a fantastic FPS experience… it just requires time, lots of time, that some of us just don’t have.

The way I am now, I can either focus solely on playing Destiny 2 every night for about 2 hours, which would help me progress some, but not a lot, all while ignoring my wife and ALL OTHER GAMES I may want to play OR I can quit Destiny 2 for good and admit that I am never going to be a proper guardian despite my wishes.

I wish I didn’t have to go…

I wish days would have more than 24 hours. I wish I didn’t have to sleep every day. I wish I could freeze time itself for everyone except me and the entire destiny community so I can enjoy my favorite MMO since World of Warcraft, but sadly I can’t. Heck, I like Destiny 2 so much that I even bought this https://store.steampowered.com/app/824270/KovaaK_20/ so I could suck a little less at PvP. However, I am an adult and as an adult, I have to admit that some things were just never meant to happen. Not just Destiny 2, but MMOs in general will be out of the question at least until my child grows up a little bit.

If you have the time, love FPS games and want to become part of something great, get this game

It has problems, it has balancing issues, it can have cheaters in PvP but it’s overall a really great experience and if you are willing to commit to Destiny 2 and Destiny 2 alone, you will be in for one Hell of a ride.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Bad: It's basically a game of grinding Halo-like missions so you can get fractionally better gear for using in PvP.

If you like FPS action, that's all there is here, it's either shooting the same mix of enemies in missions or shooting at players. If you're not really good at FPS PvP you're going to have a hard time in Crucible, the PvP arenas. Since crucible is mostly farmed by kids who can headshot you while flying through the air and insta-kill you without effort, it drives newer players out so it's *mostly* now elite PvP fighters, and the time it takes to start getting close to being able to hold your own. There are PvE activities everywhere where you can join with teams matched to you, but most of the high-end content requires you to know people or get people together to complete so if you want the higher-end gear you will have to socialize and join clans.

The equipment system is ridiculous, with maybe dozens of different progression systems for getting fractionally better gear, and there's no real guide or consensus how to approach this, so you're going to have to study not just how the armor and weapons work with your character, but how different mods, elemental attributes and stats on your gear work, and how each new system of armor, slots and upgrades interact with each other. And there's a rarity to most gear that means you will grind and grind the same missions over and over and over to get what you finally decide you want if you can even figure out what you're doing.

The story is really good but it takes some catching up or it will make zero sense. It's space magic nonsense most of the time and when the same lines repeat over and over when you're grinding strikes you want to turn the audio off.

The game also is very pushy about getting you to pay for season passes and new content even though it's not necessary. You can get halfway through a long mission and then see that the rest of it requires premium content to finish, or requires a large clan activity to complete. Both of these can turn off newer players.

The Good: Despite all this, the gameplay itself is highly addictive and feels rewarding. They make the grinding satisfying and the incremental rewards feel satisfying to obtain. There's always *something* to do, so as long as you're enjoying the gameplay you won't run out of activities.

While the story is bananas, it's also so well acted with top-notch voices and a beautiful scope and quality directing that it will still captivate you as you get used to particular characters and their personalities and care about them.

There are secrets, there are places to unlock that open up new whole areas of the game, and there are constant updates that are adding events and content, and even though most of it is a variation of "shoot your way through this area" it's still addicting as you continue to refine your skill and equipment.

The premium purchases aren't overpriced and definitely feel like you're getting something out of it, with accelerated exp gains and rewards of equipment and parts as you level.

If you loved Halo, you will love this, but if you overplay it you will hate it, then if you overplay it some more you will start to love it again.

I could complain endlessly about this game but that's because I keep going back to it. That should say something.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter months of connection errors and kicks getting worse and worse I decided that maybe it really was me that was the problem. After all, Destiny keeps saying it is my fault essentially. I got a really nice $200 router and upgraded my service to a gigabit speed option from the 100mbps I had and made sure my new router settings were exactly what their troubleshooting guide suggested. After a couple months in this new configuration, I have to say, given enough time I thought I had made things actually worse. I don't know what thought went into their server system, I heard its peer to peer, maybe the hub areas are dedicated, doesn't matter but most of the time I cannot stay consistantly logged in and loaded to the tower and on occasion other areas too.

I bought season of worth season pass, real money put down in support of the game, and what do we get? 2 weeks, 2 update periods and a few hotfixes, and I could not continue the main story line because of a wide spread bug. Didn't seem to be any rush to fix it either after I logged in the second week and was still unable to progress. It was eventually fixed shortly after that but my god the priorities have been made clear. Tired of spending money and time grinding away only to be denied access, kicked, jipped because of faulty connection systems, bugs, poorly designed activities, and horrific RNG. I even started getting duplicates from Xur again from his weekly exotic engrams. I could go on and on but plenty of reviews here covers the continued issues, reddit and youtube are good sources too if you were on the fence.

I truly thought Activision was interfering with Bungie and making the game take directions that was not good for fans, consumers, and its artists for the sake of profits as these big investors do and was happy when Bungie dropped them. But actions speak louder than words and Bungies actions outside the thumb of Activision scream that Activision had nothing to do with the decline in quality and consideration for the franchises future, it was all Bungie clearly. I highly do not recommend this heap of rubble but since it is technically f2p I suppose you can do what you will. I am tired of fighting through the bad and making excuses from the glimmer of good that is there.\t\t\t\t\t
