\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI guess after 1041 hours I deserve to say something.

Gunplay: S-tier
Story: A-tier
Grind (perfect loot rolls): S-tier
Repeatable Content: B to A-tier?

The game does absolutely fantastic at making you feel and experience the power fantasy, and the gunplay is sincerely on a whole other level for those that enjoy picking up a different weapon, and it truly FEELING like a different weapon. 30+ Auto Rifles, and they all sound/handle/feel/fire differently - that goes without saying to all other weapon types as well. The "roll" on a weapon can truly spell the difference between it being a crap gun or complete powerhouse - so it heavily encourages farming for those right perks that fit your playstyle, or in some cases, the meta (for simply broken damage output). Exotic armor can change your abilities and supers entirely in some cases, and bring even more power fantasy to a game already heavy with it.

The only downsides I can really say are that basically all of endgame content for the game is 100% co-op. And there is absolutely no means of LFG built into the game. You'd think after years of Destiny 1, and now years of Destiny 2...they'd have something figured out by now. Instead, the community relies entirely on a Discord server, Destiny2LFG. However, brace yourself for impatient people who claim to always be the best, with tryhard personas, that will usually ragequit on something the moment you call out they are the ones lacking on something. My heavy suggestion is to use D2LFG to find a few decent folks, reach out to them on Steam and build your own friendships or join a solid clan of like-minded individuals. When you have at least 2 other folks you can consistently rely upon to play with you, you'll really see where this game shines. It's just a pity it's hidden behind simply missing an in-game system to connect with others, when the co-op content and aspect is where the games true genius is experienced.

I have never once regretted my purchase of the game, overall. Even in times when I'm burnt out, it never lasts long. I take a month or so, or even a season off - and I only come back ready to grind out the new weapons and rolls. They have changed up their methods for content, and have been releasing pieces at a time each week, which helps curb the burnout as well. I truly hope they keep this up. Thanks for giving me 1,000 hours of entertainment for my money, Bungie. I don't know many other companies who've had me hooked so hard.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think the world that bungie has built here is amazing. The lore and a lot of the story building in this game is incredible. On top of that, the gunplay is smooth, the classes are all good in their own ways, and the pve side of this game can be amazing. However, destiny is not a pvp game. Do not go into this game thinking it is going to be a good pvp game. Pvp is full of cheaters and people who haven't seen the sun in years because they were too busy using felwinter's lie on guardians in the crucible. The pve side of things, is much better though. Destiny most definitely has had its ups and downs, and I have taken many breaks from this game throughout the years. Honestly though, this game has a special place in my steam library. Unlike other games, I still remember the first exotic I ever got, I still remember the first time I ever cleared a raid, and I still remember playing gambit for countless hours to get a bow that I only used twice, but who could forget that right? In all seriousness, play this game. Read the lore, learn the characters, master the gunplay, but most of all, don't take the game to seriously or you will end up hating it with a passion.\t\t\t\t\t
