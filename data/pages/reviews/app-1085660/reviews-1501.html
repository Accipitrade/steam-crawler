\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a very casual solo player so am rating this as a solo experience and as someone who hates MMOs.

The good.

First off, the gameplay is solid and fun, even after doing the same strike mission several days in a row it was still an enjoyable experience. the art and averments are beautiful and stunning, and the design team deserves a raise for it as does the music team. Sound design is solid, and nothing ever stood out as off or unfitting.
The story and characters, unlike the original Destiny, are engaging and memorable. Every important NPC has a personality that I could not help but grow fond of and the story actually exists! (major step up from Destiny 1). There is a wide array of content that solo/casual players can enjoy with matchmaking for SOME of the more rewarding activities. There are also several game types in terms of activities.
Strikes, Heroics and Public events for PvE lovers (which I prefer.)
The Crucible, Iron Banner, and Trials of Osiris for PvP (which I have never been a fan of in any game but is surprisingly fun here).
And Gambit for PvEvP[Player versus Environment versus Player] which was a surprising joy and one of my favorite game modes ever.
\tRewards are constant and mostly exciting, even if you hate the reward you can recycle them into useful materials, and there are various challenges and bounties that relate to gameplay aspects for extra rewards and fun.
\tThere are, IMO, overly priced cosmetic micro transactions, which is its own thing, but they do have a rotating list of these items that you can buy with a rare in game currency. You also have to buy the Seasons pass per season, which are sort of like temporary content expansions, but theyre only a single $10 payment and, from what I have seen, lasts for months so I dont really mind. It is a subscription free MMO so they have to make money somehow and, as much as I love this game, I would not play it if it was subscription based, which is the main reason I dont play MMOs.

The bad/annoying

There is a lot of endgame content that is locked behind a mandatory multiplayer wall. While yes this is technically an MMO it is still styled as a single player game. Now this own its own isnt to much of a bad thing except that these mandatory multiplayer activities (Raids, higher difficulty Strikes and some dungeons) dont have matchmaking and require players to use OUT OF GAME sources to form fireteams or form/join a Clan. I understand that these activities rely on teamwork and cooperation but there are better ways of handling it, like peer reviewed matchmaking or just letting players work it out amongst themselves. There is a Beta Guided Raid mode where players can join a clan or group of Guides that are supposed to help and guide them through the Raid but I have yet to try this and what I have heard of the system is.not great.
Also, sometimes the enemy placement in missions can be a bit frustrating and rage inducing but that is more of a small, personal gripe.

But, in the end, I did end up loving this game and buying the expansions and pre-ordering Beyond Light. I love playing this game and cannot see myself not playing it in the near future. I got more then I thought I would from a free game and decided it was worth my money.
My advice is to do what I did, player the vanilla/ free game and, if you like it, buy the expansions, if not then it cost you nothing but time.

with that in mind. Or just don't

up with the ideal rate of release.

sharding blues)

All in all destiny is and probably will be my favourite game despite some flaws that are undeniable. The hobby of the game is coming back over time and future content looks to be going towards the MMO style more hardcore players have wanted for years.
