Old review:
It's a one in a lifetime experience. You either love it, hate it, or don't care about it, but there's nothing, absolutely nothing else that will feel like Destiny. Despite all its flaws and seemingly endless obvious possible improvements, this game's foundation is so solid (mechanic, gameplay, story-wise) that it will never fail, no matter what. It's the ol' reliable you get out of the box every time some other game fails you. It's rough around the edges, and fresh content isn't always there to greet you when you log in, so it's by definition the exact opposite of most other successful games that release flawlessly, and peacefully wither away. Despite whatever you, the reader, might've been told, this game is alive and kicking, and will remain in this state as long as the core gameplay loop remains.
Edited review:
I no longer can in good conscience recommend this game to anyone anymore. Some things stayed the same, and many more changed. I personally don't like the direction Bungie is taking this game, as well as their approach to the game as a service. Their core design philosophies, it would seem, do not align with delivering meaningful and memorable experiences. Maybe it's nostalgia glasses and I've just gotten too old. Either way, stay away. You will not retain good memories of this game, and you won't remember it fondly if you ever let it go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot recommend this game for anyone jumping into it right now as a F2P-player as there is still content that remains vaulted(temporarily removed) and that this game doesn't have a design I enjoy playing around anymore. Its great to kill time with but its a long-term commitment. I'm not F2P since I do own the DLC's but I can't agree with existing issues I have with the current game design.

- The story was fine for me but it might not be fine with some people so maybe read a synopsis on it somewhere or in another review. If you can enjoy the story, it'll definitely have its high points and a community to enjoy alongside it. Anyway, I'm just getting down to the gameplay related stuff for this review.
- For F2P players, Content is lacking without DLC/Battle-pass due to a large portion of F2P content being vaulted. This is nearly irrelevant if you're fine just jumping into PVP without access to a larger arsenal of potential builds. When it comes to PVE, it depends on playstyle and so on but there is content that requires specific variations of physical/elemental damage ontop of the type of weaponry that can be annoying to obtain without access to DLC or Battle Pass. Every Season they introduce a new Seasonal Artifact that has a different set of anti-champion mods which are necessary to participate and play in some content. You will be using weapons/builds that have to be built around the anti-champion mods and it'll remain that way for a season which is bad if you hate the current weapons for seasonal mods.
- Battle Pass is a bit of an annoying grind for anyone who takes long periods of break or plays other games. You'll need the unlocked stuff inside the Battle Pass to assist with your current seasonal experience. Battle Passes are designed to keep players playing but I don't agree with the gameplay loop that Destiny 2 insists on having me play with ontop of sometimes tying the Battle Pass to some ingame content that do have relevant gameplay value. I've only managed to finish a single battle pass and collect all the rewards but that was when I first played Destiny 2 with lots of interest before they vaulted some content. The subsequent seasons have left me burnt out from trying to finish the battle passes and I've failed to do it even with efficient methods because of the tediousness.
- The game is lucky that there are skilled players to disprove the content from DLC's as being "P2W" but for the casual F2P player experience, expect to get kicked around by people who have things that you don't have. This goes for any other game but just take it into consideration if you just wanna jump in as a F2P player. Its newer content so it should be an improvement in some aspects but the Devs need to consider their place carefully in this game.
- Its fine playing a single class at first until you want to play the other classes because now you must make a new character and replay through the tutorial and check off a bunch of other checkmark quests you've already done on your other character. Its a huge annoying setback for replayability if you don't want to go through dialogue, cutscenes, etc.
- You will need to use excessive amounts of online resources to get anything done in this game without stressing yourself over it. The game has quests designed like easter egg hunts for the entire community to work together and solve which is great for that side of the community. This design is an issue if you just want to get things done in a straightfoward manner instead of pulling up google/youtube. If you love taking the tedious challenge of going in blind for some really insane easter egg hunts then this game is right up your alley ya dang masochist. The rewards/experience in this game are fine enough to ignore this kind of weird design but just keep this info in consideration. Googling info for this game is somewhat problematic if you don't know how to filter search for results properly. There is a large number of outdated info or just things clogging up your searches such as numerous gaming site articles
- Inventory management is bad in this game. I'm using an online website/app that can directly sort through my items and also move them around between characters and my storage but this should not be necessary to help enjoy this game. Your Bank Storage is located in the social hub which means some excessive loading screens to get things in/out of your inventory space.
- So one of my biggest pet peeves with this game is that it greatly insists on forcing me to get out of my comfort zone and diversify my builds out of necessity and also explore almost every game mode to get certain quest objectives completed. Most PvP players will inevitably need to particpate in PvE because PvP barely has any diverse pool of loot to diversify builds. Every PvE player just playing campaign content will inevitably be forced to dip their toes in PvP because of arbitrary quest requirements.\t\t\t\t\t
