\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot of content to be enjoyed for free. Get the expansions at a discount and I'd say you'd get your money's worth. The world that Bungie has built is beautiful and wonderfully detailed.

Gunplay is excellent, with lots of gun archetypes to find something you like.

If you like lore, there's tons to discover in the Destiny world. However, lore is not delivered well. They're hidden and scattered in bits and pieces. You can even play every activity in the whole game and not get a good picture of what's happening.

Don't expect:
1) Fair microtransaction pricing. For a fully paid game that charges for each DLC, each season pass, the microtransactions are NOT micro at all. $10 for a single armor cosmetic is insane.

2) Quick updates/Good balancing. Bungie has a bad track record in how fast they balance things and how well those tweaks turn out. Nerfs are overdone, while some items can be meta for ages without any shakeups.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, let's be honest. People love to hate this game for its awful launch state and initial expansions. Yeah, it's true, but people need to look past that and look at the game as it is now. It's far different from what it was like initially.

I have 2500+ hours on Destiny 1 across all three years, and another 1200+ hours on Destiny 2, primarily played on PS4. This game is absolutely beautiful and fun to play. The big thing that keeps me coming back to playing? It feels fun. Using all of the weapons, utilizing abilities, and movement. All of it is great and it flows together so well that it keeps me playing.

This isn't a game for everyone, but it's still F2P. The best way to know if you think it's worth playing is by trying it out. Explore the main areas, do the New Light stuff, and see how it feels for you. If you enjoy it, then you can consider the DLC. The most recent one is The Witch Queen. Take it a step further and get the Season Pass, which covers the four seasons for the next year of Destiny 2 if you really like the game.

The grind can take a long time and a bit of commitment, but the grind isn't ridiculous if you just want to level up your character. But if you're planning on going for the other collectibles, be prepared to put in a lot of work.

Oh, and one more thing. When you make your character, make sure they look the way you want them to since you can't change them once you start.\t\t\t\t\t
