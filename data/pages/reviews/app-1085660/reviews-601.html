\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne more year has passed, Four more seasons have gone by and another major expansion has been dropped! I think that means its time for my Yearly re-review of Destiny? Well, whether I like it or not, I am going to write one, just like how Im not going to stop playing Destiny no matter how much I dont like it. Im in too deep and I need help escaping and the only person who can help me is todays sponsor, Bungie!

Yep, nevermind, they only want to sponsor a positive review, let us begin!

(Quick Hayley note, I dont want to give away story or mechanics for anything in the game, I believe that half the fun is discovering mechanics and that telling them to people would spoil the fun)

Lightfall

I like Lightfalls Campaign. There, I said it. Ive played it on each character and I definitely enjoyed it more on my solo playthrough. Its an interesting challenge again, however, the mandatory use of strand bogs it down, and after such a smooth campaign last year, it did seem a little rough. One thing that was especially good about it though, is Osiris, I know, another unusual opinion. With such quotes as
#Discussions_QuoteBlock_Author
Cant be that Ahhhh
and
#Discussions_QuoteBlock_Author
WE NEED TO DO BETTER
he has earned my respect, and hey, at least hes doing things, unlike 90% of the rest of the cast.

Weve only had two seasons as of writing, and were only 50% of the way through Season of the Deep. The seasonal model hasnt been hitting the mark this year, with more battlegrounds that will eventually saturate the vanguard playlist more after Final Shape is released, Weapons that dont really have a defined purpose (They so far have reissued old weapons, changing their frames, elements and firerates), and story points that really, REALLY dont make sense, such as a characters death that came out of nowhere, that hadnt been properly utilised since Red War, and that everyone definitely cared about, but thats another point for another day, come on back guardian.

Ritual Activities

Ok, on to rituals, and if youre a free player, you only need to focus on this portion as this is the only content available.

Vanguard Strikes used to be my favourite part of the game, tightly designed story missions with somewhat humorous dialogue that could then be made harder into GMs, then they decided to add the old battlegrounds to this list of content, which was ok when it was only six more missions, but now the playlist is so saturated with missions that I really do not want to partake in (Im looking at you Battleground Hailstone) so I no longer jump into the game and relax in the Vanguard Ops playlist. Grandmaster Strikes are the only reason I continue to play them, as the most difficult challenges in the game, but theyre only worth running when the Adept weapon I want is on (come on Bungie, I want my Osprey) and for that I have to deal with the aforementioned battlegrounds but really hard, and I dont want to do that.

Gambit is by far the best activity here, playing with a team to collect motes and put them in the bank and summon a big bad known as a Primeval. Sounds easy right? The challenge comes from another team trying to do the exact same thing but faster, and to mix it up a bit, both teams can send one person to invade the enemy team to kill players to drop their motes on the floor, or if the Primeval is summoned, heal it. Most of the time, Gambit games are fun having a really competitive back and forth, however, if an enemy guardian is running a weapon called Xenophage, kiss your motes, and win, goodbye unless youre a very good player of a very coordinated team. Please just remember to bank motes! Its that or disappointing Todd Haberkorn, and we dont disappoint His Highness, Todd Freaking Haberkorn.

Crucible is the playlist where my favourite game to play is the one where I see if I can go a season without Bungie making me play in the crucible. So far I havent managed to not, but this season is looking pretty promising!

Old Expansions

So, there are plenty of old expansions, but what content do they contain and are they worth it? Lets cover this quickly

Forsaken Pack: Contains the Last Wish Raid and the Shattered Throne Dungeon, thats it, dont grab it unless you have friends or are planning to try and public raid
Shadowkeep: This has a small campaign, some weapons that are mostly irrelevant, the Garden of Salvation Raid, the Pit of Heresy Dungeon and Nightmare hunts. These are quite fun to do a couple of times, especially if youre trying to do the master time trials! Id say go for it if you need some content to fill a seasonal lul, and also are raiding/dungeoning and want a new challenge.
Beyond Light: Contains a Campaign that unlocks a new subclass which is pretty good! Does take a while to unlock but its worth it, the Deep Stone Crypt Raid and Empire Hunts, which again are pretty cool and the weapons from them look cool too, but theyre also fairly irrelevant. Would recommend picking this up and if you arent raiding yet, start to.
Witch Queen: This has the best campaign, with weapons that I personally still use to this day, the Vow of the Disciple Raid and the Duality and Spire of the Watcher Dungeons, Wellspring, and Altars of Reflection, while adding the single best patrol zone into the game. Would definitely pick this one up if you were only looking to buy one expansion.
Lightfall: It has a good campaign and a new subclass, the Root of Nightmares Raid and thats pretty much it for the base version. The deluxe has four seasons that will be removed by the start of the next expansion and two dungeons, the first of which is Ghosts of the Deep. I would personally give this a miss unless its discounted to a reasonable price (about 25 for the deluxe)

Raids

Raids are the most fun content or the most frustrating depending on each instance. They consist of a six player fireteam working together to overcome team based challenges but know this, always make sure you speak up if youve never done a raid before. You may get removed from some groups but in the long run, it is way better to find a group that is willing to teach you as youll have an infinitely better experience. As steam has a word limit, I'll limit myself to a number rating only

  • Last Wish: 9/10
  • Garden of Salvation: 8/10
  • Deep Stone Crypt: 10/10
  • Vault of Glass: 5/10
  • Vow of the Disciple: 7/10
  • Kings Fall: 3/10

Dungeons

I prefer Dungeons over Raids, something about a three player team makes it more manageable, and the better news is that all dungeons are soloable, but id only recommend trying some of them that way. again, numbers for Steam's Limit

  • Shattered Throne: 5/10
  • Pit of Heresy: 8/10
  • Prophecy: 6/10
  • Grasp of Avarice: 4/10
  • Duality: 7/10
  • Spire of the Watcher: 10/10
  • Ghosts of the Deep: 1/10

Final Thoughts

I know thats a lot of info to take in, and I have way more to say, maybe ill make a video an hour long one day. I do not recommend this game to anyone. It will take your life and youll be stuck in it until Bungie stops updating it (Which looks like it may only be 2 years away!) however, go listen to the OST, some of the tracks are amazing, and I would list them but this review is long enough.

Anyway, I hope you can make an informed decision as to your potential purchase and thank you for letting me waste your time on a review for a game that will take way more hours than it took to write this.

Hayley Out!\t\t\t\t\t
