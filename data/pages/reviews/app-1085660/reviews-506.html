\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is *THE* best MMO you can play, with truly exceptional gun play, story, and polish. Any player, be it hardcore or casual, can find something in this stand out, free to play, FPS game. NameBrand.

Destiny 2 really is the pinnacle of "live service" games. And MMO's for that matter. It even stands on its own in the FPS category. There's truly nothing like it. Destiny offers so much, and asks for little in return. You have this, evolving narrative and world. new guns, several game mode types, cross-save, cross-play, and it's all free to jump into. I really do feel like they offer something for every kind of player.

Destiny is interesting in that it makes you the protagonist of the story that is happening right now. As Bungie adds in updates while you play, you experience the bits and pieces of an ever evolving narrative, and you feel like you're not just a part of it, but at the crux of it. You feel like you make your mark on the universe every time you boot up the game and complete some activities. You can play the old campaigns, but they almost feel as though they were someone else's memory... it's interesting. It makes the live service model make sense and FEEL alive. Like the universe is REAL somewhere out there in vastness of space, and time passes there just as it does in real life. The world building, missions, and game being designed around activities that constantly influence the overarching narrative help to sell this.

I don't have all the space or time in the world to write out how I've had some of THE most fun gaming experiences of my life with my friends playing Destiny 2. The team building co-operative experiences in this game rival nothing else. And I'll take those memories with me forever, probably. If you can bring your group of friends, and get into a pattern of weekly play, you will have a great time. If you can't, there's always people in various LFG's looking to play. Or you can forge your own story as a solo. But, know that the community is amazing. You'll find someone out there should you need them.

The only down side with Destiny 2 is that the burnout is real. If you want to play some of the games best offerings you'll have to grind. It may start as an enjoyable grind, but after I got to the top of my game and played the "day one raid" I was grinding for, with all my powerful gear... after that day, I felt less and less inclined to log in. I saw the story of my Guardian slowly fade away as I played other games. Committed to other stories, other consoles, etc. I watched as the universe of Destiny moved on without me. And it will move on with or without you. New story threads unfold, characters develop, and mysteries are created, and resolved. But before I knew it, it was tedious logging on every day, every other day, every week to get the boosts I needed to stay at the top of my game, and on top of the story. Eventually, it fizzles out to the point where I'm not even reading synopsis of the new story beats. Some of my friends join me and play other games amidst their burn out, and others wonder why I've abandoned the Light. It's probably the saddest burn out experience of any game I've ever played.

But you can always choose to boot Destiny 2 back up again. I don't think the burnout lasts forever, at least not with me. I certainly haven't played as fervently as before. But I have returned and done some things, and maybe you will too. Maybe you just want to do some PvP. Maybe you want to turn your brain off and complete bounties, or quests for a weapon you've been looking for. Maybe you're lacking games during a dry-spell in your life and you commit to a season with D2. It feels like Destiny 2 is always there for me as a backup game whenever I want to game, and I commend it for being that.

So, in conclusion, during your time, during your story, while you're playing Destiny 2; I can assure that you will have the opportunity to have some of the greatest gaming experiences of your lifetime. Should Destiny 2 resonate with you, the time you spend and the memories you make will be beyond rich, and unlike anything you've experienced before. Destiny 2 has had an incredible influence on my life, even beyond gaming, and I absolutely recommend it to everyone, because I believe anyone can have the same experiences, and feel very fulfilled with what Destiny 2 has to offer, and that is even if you don't end up spending a dime.\t\t\t\t\t
