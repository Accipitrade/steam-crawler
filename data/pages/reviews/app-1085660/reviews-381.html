\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a game that gets better the more you play it. Treat it like an MMO, and it will treat you; that is to say, if you make this game your hobby it's a brilliant hobby. I just hope you like inventory management (bless the community for apps like DIM, it makes this so much more palatable).

The DLC $$ overhead is MASSIVE, so only start investing if you enjoy the crisp and sexy gameplay (and go to third party DLC-buying sites, save yourself like... $100 or so). PvE, PvP, mixed, mechanical challenge, relaxed point-and-clicking, this game has a bit of everything to sate your palette.

New to Destiny 2? I will warn you up front, the first ~20 hours or so are the worst part! Destiny 2 thrives on its arsenal you can farm to hone your tools to a razor's edge. As a result, when you are lacking a vault that has been optimized from hours upon hours of loot drops, the game isn't that fun. Likewise, your armor can make or break how the game feels to you thanks to the 6-stat system. Bungie has more recently added the ability to mitigate this issue with armor farming accessibility, but for the first hours having no stats makes your base abilities feel awful.

Pro tips for an excellent newbie experience:

1) Invest in Recovery on Armor (especially with mods), the ability to heal yourself TWICE AS FAST on the high end is worth everything else. Maxing your Recovery as a new player makes the early experience so, so much better.

2) Seek out the community, this game has one of if not the best and most diligent community out there. YouTubers, app developers, servers, we have it all and we love New Lights/players. They will heartily share advice and pointers.

3) Download DIM (Destiny Item Manager) on your PC AND smartphone (phone is a bit awkward to use but still helpful). Learn it, and it will be a godsend for inventory management and build crafting/saving/equipping. This will make your life a million times easier; it's so good, people mistake it for an official app.

4) Avoid Crucible (PvP) and Gambit until you can get a loadout you are comfortable with. Seek community help for what weapons to use and avoid, what mods to equip, and so on. YouTube is a big help for that. These game modes are AWFUL to play if you lack the tools to play them. The more you play Crucible, the better it gets, I promise!

5) Look up guides for raids, learn one, grab the suggested arsenal and Power level, and use the Bungie app or Discord servers to LFG for raids. LFG- Looking For Group. It's the process of manually matchmaking to play game modes you are not automatically matchmade for. The raids are the highlight of the Destiny experience, and I have made many friends through it! The loot is also excellent. They are socially challenging, but that is arguably part of the fun!

6) Buy up and do Banshee's bounties. His reputation rewards are some of the best, and he gives you something called "Enhancement Cores." These Cores are essential to a large majority of loot progression, and if you keep 1 weapon of each type, you can earn 4 Cores for free (per character per day). Doing these will massively speed you along from your early game state!

7) Masterwork a Ghost shell you like first. They have powerful, loot-reshaping mods you can equip that will speed up and streamline your experience. Don't stop at one either; keep a shell for different game modes and loot pursuits too, to save time and resources!
