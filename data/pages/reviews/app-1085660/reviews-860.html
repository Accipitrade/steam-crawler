\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a good game if you've been playing since launch or are willing to buy every expansion up front. However, the new player experience is ass. There is no way to track progress through the game, nor any indicator of what to do next after complete the intro quest, which then again doesn't really teach you much about the game as a whole, so if you don't have friends to guide you through, it's gonna be rough. Raids and Dungeons are mostly fun, with their replay-ability varying, mainly depending on if you enjoy the content, which to be fair most Raids and Dungeons are pretty good, and how good the loot is. Other then that, PvE has Strikes, which are in dire need of an update, Seasonal Activities, which are ok to meh usually, Patrol, which other then the environments, is incredibly barren, and varies types of story missions. Gambit is a game mode that does exist, though their failure to add anything or iterate on the game mode is laughable as well as incredibly disheartening for those of us who see the potential in it. PvP has been neglected to some degree for a while, but is seemingly getting better. We FINALLY got a new map for the first time in I believe almost 3 years, and we've gotten another since then a couple months later, as well 2 new game modes (I know Rift was in D1, but it's new to D2). Balancing remains to be... well, most of the weapons are pretty balanced in relation to each other, though there are HUGE outliers that stand out miles above the rest and make the game play unbearable at times. The abilities are fun to use, and the updates from Trees to the Aspect/Fragment system has been overall very good, though this has created a sense of power creep. I think this is fine for some of the earlier content in the game, and maybe into some of the later parts too, just to help accommodate for new players, but it should be the difficulty for the entirety of the game. The content in the game does not seemed balanced to our strengths, and makes a lot of the game play feel boring, though there are exceptions, and when the game is challenging, it is fun. Speaking of challenging, the "Endgame", being Master/Grandmaster Content, is just you being locked behind the artificial barrier that is power level that can be bypassed by, get this, AFK farming, and faced with enemies that force you to change your load out to actually be able to play the game, usually making it so you can't use what you want to use. This feels incredibly backwards, especially with how hard they are pushing build crafting, and then again, if you want to build craft, you have to wait for 1 vendor to sell the mods you need, which could legitimately take months, and potentially over a year,

Looking at my hours, you can probably tell that I do love this game. I want this game to succeed, and I want it to be the best game it possibly can, but there are so many core issues with the game that just continue to get more and more apparent as Bungie continues to pump out new content as opposed to fixing the old. I would like to say that the story in this game has gotten SO much better in the past year its unbelievable, and that' genuinely is so exciting to see. I won't even get into content vaulting/sunsetting, as there is no point to argue about that since its happened is now gone, but it should be brought up as its taken some of the best content the game had to offer. Bungie does show they care about this game and it's community, but I hope they listen to us and fix some of the core game play loops, playlists, and just some of the in game systems to make it an overall more enjoyable experience for new players and veterans alike.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 right now is a weird game to recommend. On one hand, you have a beautiful game with a great soundtrack, satisfying gunplay, amazing raid and mission-style activities (like the Last Wish raid and the currently-in-rotation Presage mission, though I put Last Wish as the best Destiny 2 can offer), and a respectable amount of guns, abilities, and mods to play around with. On the other hand, you have a game that was forced to remove a crapton of content, a lot of it good, for the sake of hard drive space and development time; you also have a game that, once you become more enthralled with it, is kinda shallow for the more hardcore audience. The game now works in $10 USD, 3 month-long seasons with bigger paid DLCs towards the end of the year, and for ten bucks you get a decent amount of stuff. Thing is, that content wears its welcome quite fast and you will most likely find yourself without a notion of what to do come the 3rd or 4th week into a season; this is more a result of a dripfeed system than lack of enjoyment. The power-leveling grind, which is the main grind in D2, gets old, really, really fast. It is a "number go up" type system, where the only reason to get to the highest level is to do Grandmaster difficulty strikes, which are not rewarding at all time investment-wise save for a player title. For anyone looking to do PvP or solely PvP, just don't play this game right now. A new player subclass, called Stasis, has sucked any and all fun that was left in PvP, which was not much, because it is a subclass that freezes you in place with no real counter or ability to fight back. It is the opposite of fun; the crappy endgame PvP experience (cheaters, system that only rewards absolute perfection, STASIS F**K, etc.) doesn't help either. All of this sounds negative, but the game still has a lot to offer you as a player, but don't expect perfection in the FPS MMO looter shooter genre, especially in this game.

TL;DR, the game has its fair share of issues when it comes to hardcore, endgame-focused players, and has little to offer for the PvP crowd, but besides this and some iffy monetization practices (just look up Eververse and transmog for this game and you'll see), I feel that this game is on the whole very addictive and fun to play with a group of friends, or even solo.

FYI, this is NOT a F2P game. The F2P is moreso a trial with free events every other season or so, whereas all the actual content is paid. It's more like a World of Warcraft subscription system but less expensive, sorta.\t\t\t\t\t
