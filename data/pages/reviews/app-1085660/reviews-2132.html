\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've always enjoyed Destiny 2 but since Bungie's breakup with Activision you can tell they're really working to get this season thing down and make the storyline a lot more detailed and mysterious. The moon storyline itself was interesting and rather terrifying, in parts (but thralls creep me out, to begin with).

The downsides of the game still remain, though. While more populated thanks to cross-platform gameplay you can find yourself out on a planet with only one or two other people to be found sometimes, even during early evening hours when the game should be populated. You can solve this sometimes but going back to orbit and re-entering a planet but that shouldn't have to be the case. Allowing larger fire teams or allowing for more people on a planet/server would be ideal to help things feel more alive and would also help with seasons where you absolutely need a group of people around to complete tasks, like the seraph towers. On the other hand, having these things like the seraph towers a queued option could work too but seeing a populated planet would be better, IMO.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
Good gun play
beautiful settings
really smooth running while looking really good
great sense of progression
A pretty good character customization with endless sets of armor and weapons at your disposal
really cool attention to detail with environments and weapon design such as the trigger being visually pulled and the hammer cocking on the revolver type weapons
Cons:
pretty spotty story with a good one coming from an expansion every now and then
seasons (who likes these?)
most of the lore has to be found either outside of the game or in very well hidden text logs that are dotted throughout every map including the PVP maps where exploration and looking for collectibles is not exactly the primary focus
PVP can be a little unbalanced at times with certain weapon classes being heavily favored
Always online, since the game is an MMO

My conclusion:
A very fun shooter experience that is reminiscent in some ways to halo (this is Bungie, after all) with a sub-par PVP experience, in my opinion, but a good co-op and enjoyable single player experience\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a fan of the original Destiny, playing it all the way back to the first week of release, D2 just seems like a shadow of its former self. I played this when it first came out and my initial opinion was that it was a worthy successor to D1 that improved many gameplay and story aspects. Over the years, however, I've played on and off and my opinion only goes down. Really everything went downhill after it became a free-to-play game, and when the latest Fallen DLC came out, I just lost all my will to play.

The Big Problem
Too much content. You take a two week break and you miss a 10GB update (I'm exaggerating). Bungie already took steps to shrink the game because it got to an unmanageable size, and I was glad that they did this because I would have never completed every activity the game had to offer. Still though, there is way too much stuff to do and keep track of. It does not feel like a casual game anymore, it just feels like a neverending grind. And the worst part is that if you don't have a bunch of friends you can play with, you'll never really get anywhere. Destiny has NEVER been a reliable singleplayer game, but ever since the end of D1 days, all of my Destiny friends are gone, especially here on PC. Usually Bungie is able to dish out good singleplayer experiences, but not in the current state of D2.

This is all not mentioning the absolute trainwreck that was the latest DLC, which was a rehash of the same old crap I've seen before in House of Wolves and literally every single Fallen-centric storyline ever. The new stasis abilities were underwhelming as well. I couldn't even finish it, I got so bored of it in the first two levels.\t\t\t\t\t
