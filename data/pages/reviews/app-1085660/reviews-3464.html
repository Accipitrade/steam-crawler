\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile the product at launch was a shallow mess, the team at Bungie have made every stride as of Forsaken to make this franchise the best it could be. Having bought the rights to Destiny and separated from Activision they now have complete control of the series, and it costs nothing to come in and take a look, except one thiqq place on your hard drive.

The gunplay is snappy and satisfying to pop heads with, the worlds are gorgeous (except for Io which is just diet Mars), the raids and later stories are compelling, the goals to be made are rewarding taking you across exotic quests, dungeons, raids, adventures, missions, strikes, gambit, the crucible, nightfalls, the prior mentioned raids, secrets throughout the different planetary locations and more. I've got roughly 600 hours in Destiny 1 and somewhere over 900 in Destiny 2, and I love this game.

You'll find the community to be very inviting, and the old players who stuck around to be very welcoming and usually happy to help out. I'm seeing negative reviews pop up because people rightly despise what this game was for an entire ridiculous year, but what this game is now? Better than ever before.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like looter shooters, you will like Destiny.
If you like combat very similar to the Halo games, you will like Destiny.
If you absolutely need an engrossing and interesting story to keep you hooked on a game, you will NOT like destiny.

Destiny has fantastic lore. But basically none of that is conveyed through the actual campaigns, the strikes, or any other kind of mission. THIS INCLUDES THE DLC, THEIR STORIES ARE CRAP. I know most long time fans will say Forsaken's story was good, but I really don't think it was. They just killed off a guy that everyone liked and pretended that doing that would provide an emotional incentive to get invested. Didn't do it for me. And Shadowkeep was short and answered no questions that it should have.

BUT, the game itself is a lot of fun. Grinding strikes and getting new guns is a lot of fun. There is a definite sense of progression for at least the first 100 hours. After you hit the power cap, it gets a bit worse, but there is always something to do. This is a fun time waster game that doesn't require a lot of skill, and just lets you live out a power fantasy. I like it.

So if you can get passed the lackluster story, then you will probably have a good time. Plus the game is free, so you can just try it out and see if you like it. I would definitely not buy any of the DLC's until then.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love Bungie, I truly do. But I can't in good conscience recommend Destiny to anyone right now.
While it truly does at some wonderful experiences to the game, it ultimately failed in the long run.

Lightfall, the game literally did fall.

Pros:

The new subclass, Strand is absolutely killer fun to play. ---and wait, that's about it.

Cons:

This is a huge let down from Witch Queen the previous expansion.
There's dozens of new microtransactions. Shader (color) customization is locked behind paywalls.

Weapon ornanets? They're not really ornaments, they're just shaders.

The story is the most confusing it's been since Destiny 1 Vanilla. There's absolutely no explanation for any of the events in the Lightfall campaign, no why, nor when, no how, or what?
The first seasonal story makes absolutely no sense, and someone dies for no reason, no cause, no purpose, nothing.
The second season three months after launch is when we finally get to have some answers about the story expansion. So, if you didn't buy the season pass, you don't get to understand what/why was happening in the Lightfall campaign. Poor writing and money tactic if you ask me.

We always get new weapons for expansions. Nope, not this time we got reskinned weapons from Shadowkeep. They even mocked us in the weapon descriptions for being upset about it.

Promises were not kept for new armors for Gambit, Crucible, or Vanguard. (we learned that in the recently released half year mark state of the game that it's too hard)
^^ To tie into this, they continue to add more and more microtransaction armor to the store than they have before.

Major lack of exotic armor, and exotic weapons.

Some of the weapons that were advertised weren't available at launch. We have about 2 of them now, 4-5 are still not available 5 months into the expansions life.

No new PVP maps, no new Gambit maps, only one new strike. They recently stated that they do not have enough resources to properly take care of their CORE GAME MODES!

Bungie has always talked about not wanting to over deliver.
Let me tell you, they did not even deliver with this expansion.

This was supposed to be the biggest best build up to the final expansion The Final Shape the end of the light and dark saga. And Bungie, failed to deliver.
