The art style looks like it could have been by inspired by HR Giger but it's a lot creepier. What stands out as well is that it's a twin stick shooter. It may look a bit unusual with all weapons coming from one point in front of the ship but it works well. It allows for the game to bombard you with enemies coming from all directions even while you're manouvering through tight tunnels and obstacles.

Levels
There are 17 levels which are called missions including two exploration missions with maps. The regular missions all have different themes, enemies and bosses. Some also have turrets and a huge spaceship you can use.

Scoring
There's no scoring. This did make me think twice before buying the game because when you like a certain shmup it's fun to battle for a place in the top of the leaderboard. However not having to worry about how well you play a level is also relaxing. You can enjoy the game for however long it takes you to finish it in the difficulty that suits you and move on. I think it would be better if the sequel would get a scoring system but I'd buy it regardless.

Difficulty
There are four difficulties with an achievement for each level. Normally I would have skipped Easy but the only way to unlock all achievements is by playing the levels in every difficulty. Fortunately when starting out the levels are challenging even in Easy. The game actually feels a bit unfair at first with traps near weapon upgrades and hearts, but because the levels are scripted after a few runs you'll get familiar with the layout of the levels, know which pick ups are protected, when and from what directions enemies can be expected and you'll do a lot better. There are also plenty of hearts for you to pick up when you're low on health. During my first go through the game I alternated between Easy and Normal, so once I completed a level in Easy I did it in Normal while it was still fresh in my memory. Afterwards I completed the game in Hard and finally in Very Hard.

Weapons
You have three weapons slots and a bomb slot. You start with a gatling gun, a split laser and missiles with unlimited ammo. For each slot there are weapon upgrades with limited ammo you can pick up and overwrite your stock weapons. The split laser I find very effective but for tough moments some of the upgrades can be very useful to quickly clear a lot enemies. You do need to pay attention to which upgrades you pick up because some are better than others in certain situations. It also helps to save upgrades and bombs for a boss fight at the end of a level.

Controls
You can play the game with a controller or with keyboard and mouse. With keyboard and mouse you can shoot accurately but maneuvering the ship is a bit harder especially through obstacles. With a controller you can maneuver the ship very precisely but not aim as accurately, you can switch your aim to another direction quickly though and with most enemies being quite big I find using a controller more convenient. I remapped the bomb button to my left trigger using Steam's controller configuration so I could activate a bomb while shooting.

Conclusion
It took me 20 hours to complete the game in all four difficulties and I really enjoyed it. I think the game is suitable for shmup fans of a wide range of skill level. Like in most similar horizontal shmups it does take multiple runs to complete a level.

There are a few things gameplay wise that I think could be improved but since they would make the game harder I think I'll leave them as suggestions for a sequel like not being able to fly through walls while invincible after start or respawning, no new hearts on screen when you respawn after death and no hearts at all in Very Hard difficulty.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemote Life is a surprisingly good hybrid between a shoot'em up and a twin stick shooter. Graphics and level-design are excellent, and there's a huge variety in enemies, bosses, weapons and mission types.

An interesting design choice is that enemies and enemy bullets can go through every wall and object, while your own bullets and ship can't. It took me a while to get used to this, but in the later (more complex) levels, the devs use this to create some clever challenges. Shaped as narrow mazes with lots of moving parts, these levels are sublime and remind me of games like R-type from my youth.

Another part of the game that I enjoyed are the escort missions, where you have to protect a huge vehicle against enemy attacks. And in certain levels you can enter huge attack structures yourself, to blast everything you encounter in a thousand pieces.

My compliments to the devs for creating such a wonderful game. I really hope it gets the success it deserves.\t\t\t\t\t
