\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou could almost say this is the perfect old school arcade shoot em up game.

But that comes with a more advanced design, of course that is not a bad thing (graphics matters too). But sometimes things get too hectic on the screen and it will be hard to focus where bullets, enemies, and even moving walls are coming from. Resulting in dying from one hit, out of five lives you get at the start of rather long missions with a boss to kill at the end.

It might be just because of my age (my reflexes and eyes are not that good like 30 years ago). But the gameplay can get very chaotic even in the early missions.

Other then that there is no way I cannot recommend this game to anyone wanting a challenging game, with a great look and very good gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like shoot em ups you'll love this!
Remote Life harks back to the glory days of arcade games such as R-Type and Darius, with beautiful graphics, awesome sounds and an unusual but very effective weapons system involving a rotating gun!
The levels are well thought out, starting off easy but soon getting more challenging, and always fair. (If I lost a life it was down to my dodgy skills and 50 yr old reflexes, not down to bad game design!) The player ship handles great, and the weapons system, although unusual and strange at first, soon becomes second nature and is really effective, clever and original.
It has that feeling of "just one more go" to see if you get a little further and keeps you coming back.
At it's current price of around 3.60ish it's an absolute steal!
Grab yourself a copy while it's on offer, I'm glad I did. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have no idea why the sidescrolling sc-fi shoot 'em up REMOTE LIFE at this point of writing has a 90% positve rating besides the fact that the game game looks gorgeous because gameplay wise this is at max average and that would propably also be too generous in my view. The gameplay is slow and stale and the levels are far too condensed to really make it an enjoyable shoot 'em up experience. The player will have to fly through slim coridors infested with alien organismns while shooting blocks and enemies comming your way while waiting for doors to open so you can pass through and this mechanic simply slows the game down. Ive played a lot of shoot 'em ups and bullethells since the mid to late 1980ies and im not impressed by the game itself. I bought this game at a 75% discount at 3.99 euros but allready after a few minutes i was starting to contemplate demanding a rare refund of mine but as i do recognize that this indie dev actually has put a lot of effort into it and made an extremely beautifull but nevertheless not very interesting game i'll restrain from that as i'll gladly support indie devs that atleast shows some flair or originality. In the end though can i really recommend this game, not really unless on a 90% discount or something.\t\t\t\t\t
