\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like shmups a lot, especially horizontal ones like this. I see level design in horizontal shmups like Gradius and R-Type that tends to get lost in more bullet-dense games like many vertical ones. If you agree with me, then Remote Life is in a corner of the genre for you, with its own creative takes.

Part of the puzzle in other games is getting in front of things to take down enemies, and this game gives you 360 degree aim and weapon cycling. The game's levels and firefighting is built around the full range around you and I like it a lot. The levels themselves are nice looking and have good readability even though it's mostly metallic colours with high detail, and that's important.

Weapon cycling and the dozens of weapons that are actually in this game is a highlight that really helps the game. The weapon pickups have ammo which combined with the cycling gives you plenty of options on how you tackle levels. One other important thing is how bullets clash with one another in this game. This tactic combined with your ship being sort of big and unwieldy means that this is often going to be a better idea than trying to weave through fire. I can see why it's like this but the game really could have done with a smaller and more maneuverable ship, especially dealing with corners. You may like or dislike that aspect. Going back to the 'clanking' of bullets, they pretty much all clank with one another and the designs of the projectiles don't really make this clear. It's just not what I would expect but it's the game. Many extra effects like energy trails and smoke are deceiving and are best turned to minimum.

This game has got an ongoing story and side missions that are really fun to play, including freeroam segments and support vehicles you can shred enemy fleets with. A tutorial space to get familiar with the ship was also an idea I like. All kinds of extra fun things are in the game like this, and if the wacky text-to-speech voice acting is anything to go by, the dev team put a lot of love into the game with whatever they had. Having a fleshed out story at all is more than a lot of shmups can say.

I'm going to do my darndest to finish the game on Easy at least, but I've gotta get harsh with it now, because as optimistic as I am, there is some obstacles for me here. On my laptop (which for reference can run more intensive games than this), this game takes minutes to boot up, lags in bullet-dense scenarios,and has been crashing more and more frequently. I really hope this is just me or my machine, but it needs to be said that I expected it to run more smoothly. The crashes and problems have been frustrating but I will insist that this game is a rich shmup that gives me a lot of what I like about other ones. There's a sequel on the way and I'll be sure to support that one too, if anything I have plenty of hope for this game's flaws to be polished.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had high expectations for this game when I saw the screenshots, but after playing for a little while, i can say it let me down quite a bit... while it has good graphics and so on, what sold me on buying this game was the idea of upgrading the ship or getting a new ship - there are 5 ships (2 of which have to be unlocked by progressing through the missions), 4 colors... weapons are limited to 3 non-upgradeable systems plus the usual non-upgradeable super bomb, which happens to be a drop-down cluster bomb.

are there upgrades? sure there are, they're called 'extra weapons' and limited to around 100 shots (and they get toasted within 20 seconds of continuous firing). drones also exist - they just happen to have around 1/2 the firepower of the basic weapon your ship has, and they last about 5 seconds before disappearing

firepower ratings:

basic weapons A, B, C - 1.0
drones (floats above/below ship) - 0.5 (lasts 5 seconds)
upgraded weapons A, B, C - 3.0 (100 shots)
super bomb - 5.0 (single direction low yield, short ranged)
super bomb upgrade - 7.0 (3 shots)

all this while the enemies get more hp etc as missions progress... in short, there is absolutely no reason to even have these special weapon upgrades because they simply have no functional use beyond 3-5 seconds where stages last at least 60 seconds, nor do they actually make any difference because generally they're offered at locations that make them nearly redundant (in other words, if you see a 'crate' for a weapon, just ignore it, you'll do far better with the basic weapons the ship already has! in several instances the crates are merely traps to waste lives!!)

in conclusion, those who just want to play a shoot'em-up with peashooters, this is your type of game - for others, remote life 2 that the author is promoting, might be something to avoid if you've accidentally bought this one already\t\t\t\t\t
