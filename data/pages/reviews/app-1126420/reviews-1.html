\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to give this a thumbs down because for all the promise it shows, there is an overall lack of quality in every area except visual design. It feels like a game where the studio built out the game in full, but just released it before putting in the polish. There's no real transition between levels, sound effects are missing, level design feels half baked, and even though the music is good for what it wants to be, it's sloppily incorporated.

Level design: Levels have a lot of FU moments that just feel inappropriate. The second actual mission, for example, begins with a power-up to collect and some things to shoot to your left. Fun. Except if you spend to shooting them, you might not notice that on the right of the screen, a door was closing and you die now, but have to wait for the auto-scroll to push you into it. Not fun. Waiting for doors to open while you have nothing to shoot is also a big problem in the level design. Overall, there is just a lack of understanding in how to structure levels in order to emphasize the mechanics and fun of the game, partially due to an over-use of cramped environments.

For one last example, you might often get drones which fire shots alongside you fro 10 seconds and this kind of temporary power-up would feel really great... if only the levels then provided enemies to kill after getting them. But the corridors are often so cramped that your shots hit their targets while your drone fires at the wall.

I could go on about other areas I think were lacking, or parts that I think were really good, but the level design really saps most of the enjoyment I could otherwise get out of this and I had to ask for a refund.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI know this game was like a single person passion project, but geez... it's not the worst shmup I've played but it's far from good unless you're looking for a very, VERY specific feel.

Graphically it's pretty good. Probably better than a lot of shmups rendered into 2d from 3d. Kinda reminds me of the weird industrial rendered 2d stuff of the 90s. Movement is pretty smooth, light effects decent.

But the game is marred by a LOT of weird oversights and UI issues.

First, visually, no explosion or pop when you blast meteors to clear your path, and this happens enough early on to be noticeable. They just disappear. This is with graphic effects on High, so ... no idea what this is about.

Several sound effects clearly not leveled correctly. Some kind of siren enemy sound goes off in stage 2 that's just way louder than anything else going on. Music is generic electro rave weirdness, and doesn't fit the vibe, also looping poorly. Terrible voice acting and writing overall.

But hey, shmups are like that sometimes. Touhou looks like crap but plays great.

This game, though... it doesn't play very well. It's painfully slow. You get weapons by the dozen from icons floating around, each one having limited ammo. Of the three levels I've tolerated you're kind of cruising forward and dodging through lazily placed gates, floating spiky things, and shooting whatever stupid thing is sitting on the wall for a couple minutes at a time. No sense of power, only difficult thing is you start with the slow ship and have to unlock faster ships that have no other ability besides being faster.

Weapons are varied but in the "more is less" kind of way. You have these things thrown at you every five seconds and rarely have a good reason to use any of them. Your basic weapons are good enough to deal with most things and when they aren't, a type C weapon "Cavatious Gun" or something just blows up the entire screen in a couple seconds.

And, just to stab you in the neck, there's escort missions.

As for the UI issues... change your ship after a mission and you're locked in that space. Only way to get out is press Escape or start the mission. It's a whole other screen and feels weird. Tell the game from the main menu to quit the game and you have to go through a whole confirmation screen to get out, but press escape during the game and you're dumped back to the main menu without warning. Can't just press escape to get out of the game, but have to physically move the cursor over the Quit to get to the quit screen.

Throw money at this if you want. Like I said, passion project and all that... and if there was more work going into this game trying to refine it, I might be more kind, but it appears they're working on a sequel to this one with a lot of these pretty glaring issues front and center. I'm sure this will be up someone's alley, but in my opinion, it's like all those low budget twin stick shooters that came out on the XBLA days, no sense of pacing, just all flash and no soul.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is a hidden gem, very atmospheric, it has a dark, alien / gigerish style with some deep sea creature-like lifeforms all over the place. The enemy creature design is just fantastic, creepy and very unique.
Game is not a "standard" shmup in any way, it has a "one shot can kill you" mechanic, so its definitely not a bullet hell game. Its slow paced, you have to understand the layout of every map very well to succeed.
Sound design is excellent, i love the music, and the sound effects, narration is terrible text-to-speech lifeless diealogue tho.
Every level is replayable anytime, with 5 lifes. Yeah, you could say, what a casual, easy solution...
Well.. AHA.
This is one of the hardest space shooters you will ever get in your hands. Its just plain brutal, every single impact, bullet, little, invisible alien something can kill you, and you re done. And iam talking about the easy mode.
You can learn the layouts, get used to the maps, but it stays difficult, becouse you have to be very precise all the time with your ship fitting through narrow moving places, avoiding constantly changing obstacles while hordes of enemies are attacking you from every direction.

Remote Life was intentionally designed to be hard... but still, easy mode should be called easy for a reason.
Only 3% of the players got the level 10 achievement.... 1.6% got to level 14... on easy.

So, if u planning to buy this game, be aware: this is a dark souls of shmups. But its really a good one!

Pro:
- Great Art, Graphic Design
- Insanely strong otherworldy atmosphere
- Cool looking bosses
- good sound effects & music
- The satisfaction... if you can survive ANY level on ANY mode.

CONS:
- Game is so hard even on easy, its a joke
- No pause
- No checkpoints (and the game demands it, trust me)
- Text-to-speach narration
- Insanely difficult level starts, where u can lose 2-3 life instantly\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a shmup that takes inspiration from R-Type et al and gives it a fresh lick of paint. Graphics are superb, and utilise the Giger-esque bio-mechanical style of so many old-school shmups. There are options for old-school graphics filters, but the game looks best without them.
Aiming with a mouse (or stick) seems odd at first, but you soon get used to it and it works brilliantly. There is a nice selection of weapon power-ups with which to annihilate your varied foes. Bosses are suitably huge and intimidating.
The game is difficult, but not unfairly so. It's not a bullet hell shooter, and losing a life never feels cheap - you have to own up to your mistakes!
Can't recommend this enough for any fan of classic shmups!\t\t\t\t\t
