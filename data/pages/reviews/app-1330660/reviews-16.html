For the price, it's going good for what it has, but i'm bored out of my mind. I pushed to get to 10 hours. I wanted to give it a fair try though. I love Train Simulator and I love ATS and ETS2. If you have the money burning a hole in your pocket and you want to support this game, then by all means give it a go. Otherwise, I would wait to see how the game progresses and then decide later. The reason I did not refund the game was because by the time I finished the tutorials and did one run, I was beyond the measly 2 hour time limit Steam gives us to decide. To the developers, please keep up the progress though as you could very well turn this title into a major contender.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat is one of the bigger issues with train sims? Complexity, luckily that aint the case for this game.

W,S for power controll, E,D for brake controll, Q for switch controll. Thats it, dosent matter what train you drive, that is all the key combos you need to know to drive any train. No need to study RL operation manuals, no need to memorise a complex activation pattern like other train sims has, just set the GPS, follow the yellow line, mind the switches, speedlimit and signals, and enjoy. (and yes, x-box controller compatible)

You transport goods or passengers/mail, you can hire other ai drivers to work in your company, you gain account xp and cash from contracts that in turn unlocks trains+upgrades, a perk system for the driver, deliver goods to citys to level them up providing bonuses to you in return, maintain your train in the servicehall, manage your drivers, or simply be a one man/woman show.

You want it super relaxed or maybe you want some challenges?, automatic cargo delivery or manual cargo delivery?, there is an option and setting suitable for everyone, something that other railroad sims do not have.
This is a realistic category train game, and there is very few of them out there.

ofc, this is a work in progress. There is big issues and problems at current time, overhauls and redesigns are planned and there will be issues and problems for however long time it takes before the game is moved out of EA category, you are after all buying an unfinished product, that is what early access means.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm new to steam so this may be a rather noob question. If this game is early access and I have bought it does that mean that later on down the road I'll have to buy it again once it's not in beta?

I watched some people on youtube playing it and it seemed fun so I bought it. My first impressions have been mixed but more positive than negative.

It's a really fun map and I love having to call the dispatcher to get access to stations. The concept of the game is really nice and I like where it is envisioned to be heading with an open world massive map of Europe.

The issue last night while doing my first contract was that it kept routing me onto the wrong main for no real reason and a few times I had to back up and go through an interlocking again. My other main concern is that the signal system isn't explained very clearly and it honestly isn't making any sense to me.

In my books red is stop/yellow is prepare to stop/flashing yellow is advanced prepare to stop/flashing green is conditional proceed/ and green is clear.

However that isn't the case in this game and a simple rule book with the explanations may be helpful for people wanting to learn the proper aspects.

Another potential awesome addition in the full game would be level crossings with possible stalled cars!

Overall I'm really enjoying this game and can't wait to really get some hours into it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA cool idea/implementation. It does need some fine tuning but all in all, not a bad game. Very enjoyable.

update:
Some things that need attention and some things I like.

Pathfinding- it finds paths between two points but it could use some fine tuning. It ignores things like track direction. While not implicit, track direction is usually a thing, either trains drive on the left or the right. I believe Germany, it's the right side where there is double track. The GPS has me flip-flopping what seems like (it isn't), every other cross over. I don't know if they think I'm bored and this makes it "interactive" or what but my guess is just the pathfinding algorithm needs some TLC.

Station track mapping is confusing. Some stations, it's well indicated where to go to drop off/load your consist, others especially passenger terminals it's not marked at all. Berlin HBF is the worst. The pathfinding stuff is also an issue. I've radioed to let me board passengers and the path finding takes me to the wrong platform. As in it actively moves the orange tinted V to another platform.

Hamburg HBF needs more crazy German's Yelling "WACKEN" every few minutes...

The obese deer on the track seems to indicate there is a problem with deer eating too much. I find them hilarious.

Fog - this occurs way too frequently. I do like the effect, but I think 4 out of 7 trips to Hamburg I couldn't see anything. I've been to Hamburg (see 'Wacken' comment), and I don't recall that much fog.... but it could be seasonal and I've only passed through in August and February...

I'd love to see rain and snow some day.. but then Northern German in February the one time I was there, it was easily 50F/10C, so I'm lacking more exposure.\t\t\t\t\t
