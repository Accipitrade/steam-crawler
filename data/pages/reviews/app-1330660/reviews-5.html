\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery laid back and chill train simulator. Easy to get into, and for the more casual "simmers" who likes train games, this game should appeal to them.
In a way I would call it the "Euro Truck Simulator" of train sims, as it's focused in Europe, sees you hauling various types of freight, or passengers, across several cities.
There are options to buy, or upgrade, various trains, and you can also hire employees to work for you.

Overall it is a fairly well rounded game, and if you are into laid back simulators, I recommend giving this one a try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game. It's positioned well in a space between a full-on, realistic train simulator and a resource management game. I have played this for many hours, and I'll likely play more. So why don't I recommend it?

I don't think the devs know much about trains. I understand this is not meant to be realistic train simulator. But there is a minimum standard that needs to be met for a game where you spend most of your time in the driver's seat. The signalling system is unique, if not bizarre. The light signals are a weird mix of block signalling and speed signalling. I wouldn't complain about it here, except the placement of the block signals are wrong. You will find yourself head-to-head with another train in a stale-mate even though you've not passed any signal at danger. There's so much noise from signs indicating things like your approaching a bridge and that you've just left the bridge; but being on a bridge makes no difference to how you operate a train in this game. Yet necessary signals like routing indicators are missing, and you cannot read the rails because the switches don't "physically" change.

The most significant factor is my not recommending this game is that I can't recommend the devs themselves. This game has just left a very long early access period. Yet the game is still full of bugs. As of 1.03, the 3 hotfixes have added more bugs than they've resolved, and basic fundamental bugs are left outstanding. It also seems like the devs don't play the game themselves. If they did, they'd have discovered for themselves the numerous bugs in the track layout, for example. They've also introduced radical changes to the game play in the hotfixes, sometimes seemingly without announcement. I think these are symptoms of an immature/inexperienced crew without proper issue tracking, version management and quality assurance.

There's also signs this game is being positioned as a "DLC cash cow", in the way of games like Train Sim World.\t\t\t\t\t
