\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've got almost a couple hundred hours on TSW2, thus I feel I can spend a few words on here.
Once I started playing TSW2, this was the game I hoped someone would create: a ETS2 on rails.
Even though I love the game's concept, I must address some aspects I think are quite critical to the game's quality of life.

-Graphics-

The game does not look that bad, to be honest. It's a bit far from TSW2 but it holds, even though it seems a bit 'cartoonish' for now. I hope this aspect will be improved in the future, even though it's not the main issue as of now.

-In-game World-

I think it's very lacking at this moment.

Visually the world feels empty and souless, even though it's still more detailed than their TSW2 counterpart. Plus there are almost no AI trains, nor people in train stations.

-Trains-

Trains feel like moving boxes (as someone said in another review): you can't 'feel' the train's weight, there's almost no engine and tracks sounds and cabin details are kind of bland. While on curves it doesn't seem you're doing one at all.

Also PLEASE remove the floating head from in-cabin view and adjust in-cabin view to a lower level where we can actually see the controls and not only the road. It's impractical, at the moment, to try controlling the train with the mouse as you have to look down from tracks, witness a flying head, and try to manage your train.

-Physics-

I know the devs are not aiming at a 100% realistic sim, but physics seem a lot off to me: trains are actually loosing speed (when they should at least maintain their current or gain more) while going downhill and gaining easy speed while climbing, even with lots of cargo behind.
Emergency brake is sad, as it stops the train almost immediately, no matter your cargo load. There's maybe just a few seconds of difference but it still feels instant.

-Tutorial-

Imo, the tutorials need a just a bit more work to be properly functional. I personally got a bit confused as dialog windows did not specify correctly where to go or click at times.

-Sound-

Sound is lacking in every department to me (train, tracks, cabin), except for the horn that seems ok, but not that good.

-Personal considerations-

I'd personally like a bit more detail in train cabin controls and signalling systems. Considering a train game takes place on tracks, there isn't much else to pay attention to. The "adrenaline" of train games comes from pulling and pushing levers and buttons while paying attention to signals all while managing your speed according to the situation (hills, train stations, train weight etc.). Sure, you also have other trains and people to manage but I reckon it turns kinda pointless if you don't feel to be in a train at all.

-Conclusion-

I'm sorry but I personally think describing this game as a "Full release" is a bold statement right now.

I hope the devs will continue to develop and implement needed features to the game. I reckon it has a lot of potential to be good but it will take a lot of work and polish to get there.

As of now, I cannot recommend the game (even though I would like to). I'd tell you to buy it if you want to support its development as, to me, it's still an early access.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's great. You level up your character earning XP from jobs and your workers level up too. Features a skill point system in which you pick your perks on level ups like euro truck. Max level is thirty. I'm looking forward to the grind. You bring a certain amount of cargo over several trips to places in a city to unlock that cities bonuses. After you purchase all the trains, train upgrades and hire all the workers you can spend money on vanity items for collecting fun.

My first delivery I took cows from Hamburg to Gliwice It took around two hours. I passed through Berlin and stopped around Dresden for fuel. I encountered many deer sadly, hitting one in a minor accident. You have greater leeway in speeding before you get a ticket which is a pleasant change from other games. I encountered 4 oncoming AI trains all with no problems, I watched the lights and switched into an open parallel track. One of the trains was a broken down train stopped in the track. I never had to wait in a congested mess and I felt I could always detour around traffic without impedance.

After the trip I noticed one of my train axles was protruding out the side. I thought maybe it was a visual damage indicator from taking a turn a little too fast resulting in me slamming the brakes hard. So, I tried repairing the damage in the shed and it did not fix the axle. So, I think it is a visual bug.

The price is excellent for this game and it does a lot right for blending the casual train sim and euro truck mechanics. It really is a PB&J I would gladly eat all day.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving now played over 8 hours of this game, I can confirm I like it, a lot.

I have long fancied the idea of relaxing and driving a train to chill out (whilst listening to some music), but none of the other train games (yes, that certainly includes Train Sim World 1 & 2, I own both) were what I was searching for. I was looking for something I could pick up quickly, learn to do easily, and just relax and enjoy. TSW was not so easy to learn, and I found the tutorials left so much out that they didn't in reality teach you anywhere near enough to know how to actually drive the trains, or navigate the tracks. Is Train Life anywhere near as realistic as these games? No, of course not.

The graphics are beautiful, and it's realistic enough in that respect, but the driving of the trains is extremely simplified, and yet not overly easy. It doesn't get boring.

So if you are looking for an ultra realistic train driving sim this isn't for you, perhaps TSW 1 or 2 would be. However, if you are looking for a fun, relaxing experience, and just want to chill out driving a train for a couple of hours - with (optional, really) built-in company management (along the same lines as Euro Truck or American Truck, where you can either just drive, or you can build a business in a rather simple basic way) then this game is DEFINITELY what you are looking for. It's most definitely what I was looking for.

PRO's: Easy to learn. Tutorials quickly and simply cover everything you need to know to get going. Fun stress-free experience. Beautiful graphics, good sound.

CON's: Not ultra-realistic sim, if that's what you're looking for. A few bugs that the dev's are already aware of (big floaty head in the cabin can be mostly ignored, but does freak you out when you do see it) but they aren't game breaking and will be addressed in time, I believe.

All in all, a great game and WELL worth the price.\t\t\t\t\t
