Unfortunately they probably aren't, because signals often feel haphazardly placed...

It's surely a unique game, and I love the idea behind it. I like the scenery, it's well detailed for a railway game, you see random workers doing animations in areas you visit, and each type of industrial station has its own unique vibe to it, and the graphics also help in that area, as they are quite decent.

I can say that I will continue playing this game, and I enjoy it, but I will also continue being annoyed by it. The biggest reason for my annoyance is the fact that these guys have some very nice ideas in place, but the impression that they have left on me as a studio, is they're not too serious about this game. Most of the issues I described should be things that you deal with early on in your development process. I mean, buggy track angles that cause you to derail when doing 85 on a 120 km/h track in a railway game? If a single tester had driven a train just once over that track, it would have been noticed. I would have definitely left a positive review, if this game was "Early Access", and would have been happy to support it.
