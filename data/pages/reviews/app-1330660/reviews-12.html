\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is like ETS2 with trains but without the following:
Preview of vehicles before purchase.
Any kind of optimization.
The ability to change keybinds.
Proper map layout (this regarding signals being behind switch points, see below for more info)
Intelligent fine application (also see below for more info)

And with the following:
Bad map layout with signals that tell you far too late if a track ahead is occupied.
Idiot signal logic that block 4 tracks when 1 is occupied.
Stupid fines that require you to request access to a station before entering it, but when you load in to a game it places your train at the nearest station and you don't have access to it.
Tons of blocked tracks due to 'broken down train ahead' that randomly pop up, and a fine when you can't tell what track it's on until far to late. Sending you into reverse to go back to the last crossover.
And overall a lack of info given to you.
It does not have 'Freeform' cargo cars, but it does have 'freeform' passenger and mail cars. ('Freeform cars' meaning: Cars that you can pickup without requiring a contract to deliver to a specific location)

This game needs a hell of a lot of work yet to become anything relatively good. And for that I can not recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is in beta early access and it shows. Definitely lack of polish. However I marked the games as a do buy anyway since it is very playable and I enjoyed it. Most of the below will be negative because I want to be clear what you would be getting into. None the less, I enjoyed playing it despite the early access lack of polish.

Thoughts:

Arcade vs Simulator: This game is in the middle about a 4 on a scale from 1 Arcade to 10 simulator. You will not be starting or stopping locos. You will not be raising or lowering pantographs. No one is training with this game. On the other hand the throttle isn't a dial a speed - DCC style. You can't just drive into stations without contacting their dispatch first.

Role playing: You have a character. S/He gains experience and monies by completing contracts. You can use this to buy new locos and upgrade existing locos. Supposedly you can hire employees and have them complete contracts for you as well. I never got around to employees yet however.

Issues, the tutorial will get you started, but there is so much not covered that you have to figure out yourself. For example, the signal lights are not explained and it isn't as straight forward as you would think. Expect the first mission or two to be frustrating if you take them seriously. Treat them as learning missions.

The information provided for certain things is too limited. For example, if you encounter a train or broken down loco, (of which there are a lot, holy crap, more common then running trains.) it is hard to tell what to do. You are on the clock so you don't want to waste time figuring it out.

The map doesn't have enough information about the tracks. Which tracks are delivery in a station is difficult to figure out until you actually arrive. At which time you have to look for the orange 'V''s. I have had to back up and select a different track because I was on the wrong one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking for an arcade rail driver then this might scratch your itch. For anything a bit more train related (like a working signal system) look somewhere else.

Given that the developers have nothing sensible to say about their plans for bringing a proper signal system I have little faith it will ever come to fruition. For those who think it is fine as it stands, I have never seen a system with aspects on speed signs.
Passing another train gives you (still) fines. Main signals stand at the start of a block as opposed to only at the end (think of OpenTTD prior to path signals). Similarly signals show red because no proper blocks have been defined (and thus you are not allowed to pass another train).

A real bummer, because the potential is clearly visible. On a plus note the map and surroundings are nicely modeled.\t\t\t\t\t
