\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, I would like to say that I very much enjoy this game. It's sort of an ETS2 game but for trains. Now since I like both trains and ETS2 I had to try this.

Now when I first bought it the game performed terribly on my PC, even though it's a pretty modern one. So yesterday I reinstalled to check if things were improved and they were. The game runs quite smooth now, so thanks to the devs for doing whatever they did to improve performance.

Now for the game itself. The concept is great, you have a locomotive and you drive to various places to pick up wagons or passengers and bring them to another place. This is something that other train games don't really do, at least not in the way this game does it. Since it's not scenario based like TSW2 or TS. This also makes it feel more "open worldy".

Now I do think there still some things that are annoying.

For example: you could be driving around and suddenly there is a message that says there is a train approaching. Now this is handy if it is indeed driving on your track, because you could try to quickly change to a different track. But if the train isn't on your track, why even give a message? This (for me atleast) leads to a lot of confusion, because I don't know if I should switch tracks now or not.

Why is there even the possibility of a train driving on my track in the first place? It's not like that is something that happens in real life everyday. So yeah, this really is something that I'd like to see be either removed or improved.

Other "random" events are good though, such as an animal appearing on the tracks or having to use the horn while being near a road crossing.

Now finally, the overall gameplay is good and I get that it's still a beta. I think this game has a lot of potential to become the next big train game and I think it has a bright future ahead of itself if the developers keep updating and supporting it.

Current rating: 7/10*

* = Could change in the future :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t***Please take note***

This is a review of a (very) early access version of this game- Currently only on the second update.

I hate to use the term 'potential' but this game oozes potential. This honestly could be the best train 'sim' (I use the term sim lightly) that I've ever played... Even in its current state the world seems massive and the game seems to be 'complete' (*i.e. game loops are all there and working) and is mostly quite fun and engaging.

I LOVE train games and have played most of them and I find it very enjoyable to tool around on my virtual train while listening to podcasts or audiobooks- The only complaint I really have is that most of these are 'sims' without much 'game' to play- For instance no real persistence (outside of statistics being kept) and no real form of advancement.- Not counting just purchasing new trains and routes for extra (and real) money.

I've always wished that these 'sims' allowed you to build up cash, buy new engines and cars, upgrade and customize... Essentially being able to advance in the 'game' over time. My other wish is that these train 'sims' had a large open world rather than unconnected 'routes'.

This game fixes those issues and even in its current state there is ALOT to do and see here. Tons of cities to visit and a plethora of resources to haul around in a (seemingly) large and op[en world. You can earn money hauling goods and passengers, upgrade and customize your engine and eventually hire people to work for you and drive your trains (I havnt gotten to this point yet and dont really care about this feature but its cool to have so much to work towards)

The graphics are nice and it makes me happy to pick and choose my routes and how I will make my money and progress... In other words, I'm loving this.

I HATE trying to review early access games. Too many of them have great potential and end up abandoned... So In THIS review I am looking at this like a finished game- If what I have right now is all there will ever be- Is it worth the $14 (or whatever the price was). The answer is a resounding YES! If there never is another update I would still find this game to be well worth the price of admission just by what I've already seen.

That being said, there are issues. The navigation, the gps system, the AI trains coming right at you from out of nowhere, the signalling, etc all need work. The assets are reused far too much and there doesnt seem to be a whole lot of variation between area (again, I've only seen a small portion of the map and could be mistaken but I doubt it)- But the 'game' and the 'game loops' are solid. As it stands this is a good buy- If this is continually worked on and added to this could be the best train 'sim' out there in terms of fun factor.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am so sorry, I really want to like this game but at the moment the signalling is not working properly and is completely useless. In a railoroad game (early access or not) this should be the FIRST thing to do well.

Instead, you will have track sections with full green light and 120 km/h speed limit when suddendly you will find a red light at a crossing loop just because a train is coming in the other direction. As a result, anytime a train is coming you have to slow down/pull the emergency brake (but you will pass the red light anyway).

I even found a train coming on THE SAME track in the opposite direction and both of us had to come to stop facing each other! I could virtually wave at the other engineer! There was no indication of this happening, no yellow lights, no track protection, nothing! And then I had to slowly back to the crossing loop and switch direction. THIS IS NOT ACCEPTABLE, sorry.

I urge dev's to study (again) real world signalling and rethink the whole system.

I will keep the game installed and play it again when updated. If the issue is resolved I'll change my review.

Other minor issues that I can't understand as design choice and I hope get changed include:
* Reversing trains at Services Centers (ugh, awful idea... so arcade!)
* Manually managing switches during trips (that's a switchmen job; I could understand in a shunting mission, but not once you have the trip plan; at that point you should be under switch centers control and/or radio working with them).

I really look forward to seeing these issues resolved, as this game has a very high potential.

EDIT:
Nacon [Developer] responded (see below). I really appreciate the response and I really hope to see this change happen and to contribute to this game. I really have high expectations from their work. I know it's an hard work, but they can do it. I have high hopes on this.
Keep it up!\t\t\t\t\t
