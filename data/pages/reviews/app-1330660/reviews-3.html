\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is neither a simulator, nor has anything to do with actual trains. With the title "a railway simulator", this is basically a scam. If you are looking for something even remotely realistic, don't pay your hard earned money for this game, at least not yet.

First off, technical issues. The game lacks any way to set the resolution. The graphics settings are the worst that I've seen in any recent game. To top it all off, even with a system better than the recommended I barely get above 30 fps on low settings. I never had any crashes though, despite what some other reviews claim.

Now, to the actual gameplay part:

The train signaling is completely made up and no sane railway would use signaling like this. Basically, all signals are block signals. What's worse is that you have to change the direction of every switch by yourself as you get close to the switch. This whole signaling and switching thing is a reason to avoid this game. With "simulator" in the title, one would expect something that resembles real life, even if barely.

Physics is non-existent. Your wheels slip independently from your speed, slope or load. Any time you increase the throttle more than 10% at before a certain time (which is the minimum step for some reason) your wheels slip, even if you are going 140 downhill. It's basically a timer that lacks any physics. Train load doesn't seem to affect the breaking or acceleration performance either.

There is no other train traffic, except some random spawns of "incoming train" and "train broken down on track". The latter happens way too often, and the former is just a random encounter that you simply avoid by pressing Q before the correct switch that adds absolutely no value to the gameplay. There is no planning, no timetables, nothing that barely resembles a real railway operation. To add insult to the injury, the signalling makes this a lot worse, blocking you from passing even though your path is clear, just because the signaling system is nothing more than a basic block signal.\t\t\t\t\t

With that being said, the game is a lot of fun and found myself addicted. The problem is just how broken everything is. The tutorial is about 1 hour long and I had to skip multiple segments because I had no dam clue what they wanted me to do next. It gave me the basics and I learned how to do stuff ok, but it really was not well defined.

Once I got into the game the bugs became very evident. The most glaring of them all: the game NEVER tells you which lane is pickup or dropoff in a yard or a station. The GPS will always take you to one of them, but its almost never the right one. Almost every time I had to stop and painfully reverse out to get to the correct one. The game also failed to start due to fatal errors a few times, so not great. There are also many, many, MANY others. I also had a big issue when I first entered the main world as I tried to do passengers and the whole system just kicked me down. I could not pickup at a station until after I set a route on my minimap (which is just a weird design) and even still then I had to drive to the next station to get anyone at all since the first station said I was "off route". Wow. The worst bit was when I found out I only got 1500 in pay, but used 8000 in fuel... cool. So then I switched to freight. So I guess I need an electric train for that. So just a warning.

The gameplay is pretty straightforward and doesn't really engage as much as a Euro truck would, but that's not surprising since you are driving on rails. Literally.

The game is fun and I hope they will continue to work on it. Just don't go into it expecting the world.\t\t\t\t\t

It has the entire management and economy part of popular sims like euro truck, bus games, and even flight sims (albeit with addons).

The tutorial worked great for me and set my expectations. The simulator part definitely feels a bit off, but having so many areas in Europe to explore and actually use long sets of rail unlike TSW I was willing to move on.

And while there is so much to be excited about to make it fun to play, I am just incapable of playing. As soon as the tutorial ended I quit and played again the next day. My employee and progress that I thought I could keep from the tutorial was gone. Fair enough, a bit annoying but not the end of the world. I took a job in the city next to me since nothing was being produced in mine. Trying to get there was a disaster.

Whether I turn my graphics down or not, after a few kilometres the world stops loading in properly. It starts slowly with some objects not being loaded in and devolves into the ground missing and textures glitching onto the track. A short while after my distance to target stops updating, and finally. THE TRACKS DISAPPEAR. This instantly stops the train and now you have to go back to the main menu and force the game to load everything back in.

This happens so often that I can't be bothered anymore. Wonky physics can be forgiven for the management part I'm really excited about. But it seems that missing railway tracks is my limit (shocking I know).

I really want it to be good and I'll do some more adjusting on my end. But for now I've sadly used my frustration here. So really, if you skip the tutorial and have a go at moving between cities and your tracks wont disappear. You will have a great time I bet. If you run into the same as me, hit the refund before bothering any further.\t\t\t\t\t
