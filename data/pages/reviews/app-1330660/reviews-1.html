\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really don't have to say much since everyone else has pretty much covered everything. Any additions I'd make would simply be grinding that now decimated dead horse into the dirt.

But I will say this: I'm very new to the whole concept of driving trains. Unlike other sims such as CMANO, or WARNO, or the Wargame series where I do have an extensive understanding and knowledge of the ground - with trains, it's uncharted territory. I don't even know basic terminology - like one of the other players kept talking about a "consist." What the hell is that?!? Ahh!

So - having said that - I literally just got done playing the tutorials. And I mistook A LOT of bugs and bad decisions on my own lack of knowledge.

For instance, I was getting a little frustrated over the fact that it seemed like the track switching logic didn't work well. I knew where I had to go, but it seemed like the tracks kept taking me somewhere else. I'd pull forward, the track would take me the wrong direction. I'd have to stop. Reverse. Switch the tracks. And then I ended up somewhere altogether different. I was like ... WTF? Why can't I figure out how to switch the tracks so they take my train in the right direction?

Until I read reviews and saw others had the same problem. The game doesn't really give you a "preview" of where you need to go - and you can't switch the tracks until you're actually there. And that's often times too late. You have to eyeball it from your cab. Even knowing which track is the pick up track and the delivery track is impossible until you're there, and you have a 50/50 chance of the switch being in the right place when you arrive. If it isn't, it means reversing and switching. What's worse is that sometimes there are two or even three switches very close together - and since you can't set the tracks ahead of time, sometimes you have to backtrack miles just to get off the switches.

Anyway, like I said, I won't belabor the point. Unfortunately, there was just enough polish in this game to believe it was actually going somewhere. Like it or not, this was the game that became the final straw with me. No more wasting money on abandonware. Until a game is officially released with a version 1.0, I won't buy it. I'm tired of throwing away good money after bad - and that's just stupid. Fool me once, shame on you. Fool me twice, shame on me.

I'm still downvoting it because it's abandonware. Technically, the game is playable in its state - unless you're a hardcore train simulations buff. I'm not. It's still new to me. I was hoping this game could be a segue into the more realistic yet far more complex train sim games - but I guess not. I don't like being thrown into the river as a way to learn how to swim. Guess there's not much choice now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe intention with this game was to be like the truck sim games but with trains and the concept is there and there's some good to it. You take contract jobs or straight cargo from production to transformation or to consumers. It's like Truck Sim with the added progression of city levels where similar to Transport Fever delivering enough of the correct stuff ranks up cities. You get to make money and spend it, you can hire drivers to automate jobs. Passengers is also a type of cargo which you can also run with the correct trains. All of this is done on a appropriately large map that doesn't try to be real scale but does the job at having biomes and landmarks that sort of differentiate cities from each other.

Unfortunately to the chagrin of everyone who bought into it or was really hoping for such experience to be finally delivered this is not it. I cannot tell why it ended up this way but it was seemingly abandoned in a very buggy state which is a shame as if those bugs were ironed out the product albeit flawed in many ways would still be a functional and decent passtime.

In my opinion issues with the core design of the game and possibly bad management led to the game being led into an unfixable state. Both getting new content and some added QoL and features while further building upon a foundation of bad design leading to more problems, increased frustration and at this point no sales.

As far as the missions and progression go it's fine. Pick up and play kind of game like Truck sim without much hassle and that's good it's what the game is for. Where the game fails besides its bugs is the driving and related things and in parts map design.

Driving is obviously the most important part of the core gameplay and the first question that needs to be addressed is whether this wants to be a sim or not. In the niche of train or flight sims there isn't so much space for pure arcade and TrainLife sells itself as a lite-sim. Cabs are simplified and readable with not so much interaction although there was always space for improvement but the driving itself is failing to be either sim or arcady in a fun way. To put it simply weight feels like it has little impact on traction and speeds. Regardless of the machine and your load it always seems to break traction and slip if your push your throttle beyond the threshold. Notch 1 is good between maybe 10-20kph, 2 from 20-30 and so on. It feels very linear and when steep inclines and descents combined with weight come into play you realize you start slipping in descent and inclines don't cause you to break traction easier. It's very game-ified which would be fine if it had more depth to it. This lack of depth makes it very stale and a little weird too often than it can get away with.

The other part of driving trains is dealing with signals and pathing and this is also game-ified for simplicity but just not in a good way. Signals by themselves kind of work but they don't get much use at all. There are no dispatchers clearing paths only station radios that you ask for entry or to open the mission / load manager. Traffic exists in the game but not as an ambient thing there is no timetable. It is instead an "event" that you deal with. In any real case you'd drive the correct side of the track and you would cross traffic fine on the other track or you'd be slowed down by traffic in front but instead it only consists of a popup event that systematically implies someone is occupying your track, in front of you, very close and its up to you to dodge it by switching tracks which is also entirely done by you.

This causes problems as the event spawn can be in the wrong place, it's repetitive and unrealistic causing frustration. Track switching being done by the player isn't bad in itself but there is no accurate track map to look at to predict what you need to do to go where you need to. Instead you can only alter the next switch left or right and in complicated areas of the map it's not easy to figure out until you get used to the different types of track interchanges and stations.

A better way to do this would have been to give a accurate track map to the player, allowing him to pre plan switches from end to end. While driving, upon reaching the switches, they would be automatically set correctly so you don't need to deal with them. As far as AI goes, a simple ambient spawning and culling of AIs around the player would work fine similar to traffic in Truck Sim. They could have preset paths and ignore player switches to avoid bad routing. The signal blocks would work according to this and it would create the ambiance without causing much issues with "events" being brief traffic jams near cities and whatever.

As far as map design goes it's a lesser issue to me but worth mentioning. While the aesthetic is good enough imo with the landmarks and biomes, where the design fails is that it tends to have too many and too steep variations of grades and nowhere on the map do you really ever get to go very fast. I think it was mentioned that the engine didn't allow for fast traveling because of map loading limitations which seems weird considering TS and TSW do it with Unreal too (albeit with bad performance but i will blame it on dovetail rather than the engine). This means the map has that janky feel of being made to look real but the people designing didn't quitegive it correct proportions. A bit like comparing vanilla Euro Truck Sim 2 and its DLCs the new maps have much larger and appropriately sized roads and assets and more variety compared to the original map which had a lot of bad elevations and overly twisty sections that don't really exist in the real world.

I think if the game had been designed this way in the first place it would have had far less problems and would closer meet the expectations of the audience unfortunately this would have required a drastic revision of the core of the game at this point and it won't happen.

While it is different in scope, Derail Valley did all of this correct and is a much better game especially with the incoming overhaul for 2023. Get that instead as this is unfortunately going nowhere until there's some unexpected news from them.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had a chat with one of the developers. He was a very nice guy, we had a great long chat, so it pains me to have to really really not recommend this game.

First of all: the game is full of bugs. There is a reason that they have not been fixed. You can agree or disagree with why this is the case, but you should think of this game as abandoned and those bugs will never be fixed.

And the bugs are bad: trains popping up in front of you, making you crash and pay big to recover, signals not working as expected, getting fined because you turn the light switch in the wrong direction by accident while standing still in a station during fog, ...

But those things are not the worst. They simply took away everything that might be fun in driving a train.
- The controls are so limited that you can t do anything fun. Driving is super predictable, with or without hundreds of tonnes of cargo.
- Of course you need to manage the switches yourself, why would there be dispatching if you re the only one on the road anyway
- Of course there is NO traffic at all on the tracks, except for damaged locomotives popping up in front of you
- Of course you can go around all of those damaged locomotives by switching to the track next to you, which is always conveniently right in front of the damaged loco
- Of course you derail if you touch the other locomotive at 1km/h
- Of course you know in advance that a deer is on the track in 2km
- Of course you need to sound the signal to scare away the deer to not get derailed
- Of course you cannot turn around your locomotive or have it turned around at a station, but stations can place 20000 tonnes weighing wagons behind you in an instant
- Of course every farm, refinery, power plant, ... has a train station the size of your house where you can fix and refuel your train, turn your train (with 20 wagons attached) around, and of course... buy new trains!

Really... the list goes on... every bit of possible fun has been removed and solved with total nonsense.
And it is not about realism. I never expected the game to be realistic like SimRail or something like that.

I rage-quitted when I got fined while standing still on a track in a station, trying to turn on my fog-lights but accidentally turning my lights off instead. The game has simply been made to punish you in all possible ways and to not at least try to give you a world with any fun at all.

To say at least something good: the graphics are not too bad.

I certainly do not recommend this game, not even at its current sale price of 9 euros.\t\t\t\t\t
