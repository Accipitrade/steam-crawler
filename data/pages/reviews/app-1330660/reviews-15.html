\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo another early release but I really like the potential this game has. Having played other train simulators I found this very easy to get to grips with. The keys for operating the train are pretty straight forward so even a beginner will be able to get to grips with the game and the tutorial adequately explains the functions in game.

Although I know it's still an early release the only things that stood out to me were the lack of AI traffic be that other trains, pedestrians or road traffic (although I've only completed the tutorial at present to get a feel for the game). I also noticed that on crossovers there doesn't appear to be any checkrail.

I think this will be a very much played game and I thoroughly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the premise and idea behind this EA.

Sadly, this is pretty much all I liked in my short 1.5 hours of playtime.

In summary, the game feels very bland and lifeless. Landscapes are horrible. When comparing it to other simulators, the game feels more like an arcade version instead of a "realistic" simulator.

Albeit my short playtime, there was no motivation for me to continue to play. It's undoubtedly a very relaxed setting, but fun or excitement were lacking. The hook has to be there in the first couple of hours, otherwise I treat the game like a book. No use reading 200 pages until it gets interesting. That is just tedious! :)

Apart from all the ailments that come with any EA, there is a lot, really a lot, that still has to be done. The framework is there.

I refunded, even at this relatively good price, because I currently feel that, even as an EA, it has not delivered in my opinion, but will still recommend it though, for the good concept and that since its release as an EA, a very short amount of time has passed.

Dear Devs, keep up the hard work. Polish this game to a diamond and you might have a real game changer in the current train sim segment! Looking forward to following the progress and hopefully TL will return to my library in future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a train arcade game situated in a real world location but didn't feel real at all.
In contrast, Derail Valley is a train simulation game situated in an imaginary world but feel extremely real.

Where should I start ...

* Radioing with stations and depots, and decide where you want to go, and throwing switches on the mainline yourself. Nowhere in the world are there an railroad system work this way. (but for an arcade setting this is OK.) Drivers on the lines are almost always passively following signal and switches set by central traffic control with predetermined route and schedule, occasionally with diversion on stations/sidings to be passed by other trains etc.

* Crossover switch sections ... again, nowhere in the world put these kind of switch sections in the middle of mainline. A passing loop or siding would more realistic.

* Landscape, along with railroad curvature and gradient didn't feel realistic at all. Railroad's limited ability of curvature and gradient inevitably cause many cuttings and bridges along the path, "fighting" with the landscape, but it isn't felt in the game world.

* Locomotive and train physics simulation. How the power applies, how the wheel slips, how the breaking effort manifests, doesn't feel real at all. (Too smooth acceleration and declaration, no twitch on stopping if not reducing brake upon stopping, and the wheel slips even when applying minimal power on high speed - your engine is THAT strong?) And almost no sound feedback. Feels more like driving an remote-controlled model train.

You devs should really play a little Derail Valley. Just a little is enough, seriously. Take the DE2 available at beginning a short drive, couple it to a car, accelerate, decelerate. Listen how the engine raving. Listen when the wheel starts slipping and regaining grip on the rail. Listen how the brake, wheel and the rail scraping. Feel how the cars pushing and pulling each other. Then compare it with SG6 in Train Life, and you know where to start improving ...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, Let me start this off with saying that anyone that is comparing this to TSW 1 or 2 or Train Simulator, as all 3 of them are essentially the same game from the same developer. Dovetail/Microsoft. This is I no way shape of form anything like them.

Now, the better game comparison is Derail Valley. The graphics feel on par with where DV started, yes its kinda bare at the moment, but extra vegetation is easy to add in later. The also both play a feel very similar, DV you have to use the controls directly and go into the office on foot to get contracts. Train life make that simpler by you using a radio to call into stations to get contracts, as you cannot leave your loco. TL simple controls for switching signals etc. make this feel a lot like EuroTruck or ATS. This make for a lot less of a grind feeling in the game play. The fact the cities you service in TL grow and expand is a great concept. With the 4 hours I have into it so far as well, I haven't ran into any bugs. Not saying the don't exist. It is early access and has lots of room to grow.\t\t\t\t\t
