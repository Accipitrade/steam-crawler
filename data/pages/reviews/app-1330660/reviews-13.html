\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTentative thumbs up. Great idea, looks good, etc. But there is so much roughness and so many issues at this point it's a bit painful to play. Definitely worth the ~$15 USD I paid for it, though.

It is frustrating, not being able to do manual coupling (broken currently), not being able to see and avoid other trains (AI trains broken at the moment), and having GPS routing be such that some simple maneuvers are made very complex and unneeded track switchings are occurring all the time.

Also needed is better UI info, such as:
1. Distance to the next speed limit. They don't tell you this, so you are often forced to drive slowly for a very long distance. However, yes, we magically know the distance to the nearest deer trying to cross the track. Not only that, but they like to sit down right on the track. ...um...why?
2. More info on hills. If you are lucky, the game might flash some info about an incline (up or down) sometimes, but other times, you are in for a long slog with a weak engine and a heavy train, where your speed might drop to 10 kph if you don't give it a big burst of power before the hill. Showing a side view, like we see in other games, can indicate hill inclines. Also speed limits. We need something like this.
3. More detail in track routing. I click to build a route, and a thick yellow line clearly shows me turning to the left at a switch. But when actually driving the route, you need to be in the right-hand tracks because they proceed onto a bridge overpass that turns left. OK...you got me. Then, the GPS routing fails, not telling me in advance which way I should turn, because all it would say is "right=red (bad), left=red (bad)." With the geometry of the turn obscured by the GPS and the track choice not highlighted, again by the GPS, I totally took the wrong turn. Wow.

Am eagerly awaiting fixes to routing, better information available to users, fewer "silly" things like deer squatting on tracks. I'm hoping this game can become a little more train-driver friendly ASAP.\t\t\t\t\t
