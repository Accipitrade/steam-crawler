\r\n\t\t\t\t\tsome call it the marriage between TSW and ETS 2,for me it's more a mix of Derail Valley,ETS2 and a hint of transport fever,which is a very very good thing.

Room for improvement? Yes,I can see why the more casual approach but a little dash of realism train physics/conducting wise would really help.

So join us on this journey if their concept sounds interesting to you,cause it did to me and I'm really looking forward to where this will go in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI found out about this game by a coincidence about a week ago, got very excited at first but then becoming rather skeptical about it. Announced 3 weeks before the EA release by the devs.. right, who are these devs? The more I thought about it the more I expected a bugged, pre alpha game with barely any content and a billion bugs.

Got ripped off so many times by DTG with false promises.. might as well risk one again but atleast for cheaper (15 bucks compared to DTGs 30)

Thank jesus this game is actually fun as hell !! Game looks very good except the antialising which makes the game look blurry, trains sound good too and the gameplay is just great really. Always disliked this stupid boring train driving from a-b with all green signals. In this one you actually gotta be attentive. Signals can be red, there can be animals on the tracks so u gotta honk or stop the train, have to radio and ask for permission to drive in nearby areas like warehouses or stations etc. Very great concept.

Best thing though about this game are the long routes. Love it to just sit back and enjoy a long drive. Also great how theres a working minimap and its possible to drive without the HUD as the minimap and other infos are in the train itself which is great for immersion imo.

Like its really a great game but it kinda feels weird to think about its future. Like I dont know DTG left a mark in my head when it comes to train sims so im extremly paranoid I guess. Theres just so much potential to the game and im scared to be disappointed again. Love it though already in its current state. Thanks for making a wish of mine (ets with trains) come true <3

Stuff I hope to be added to the game:

-modding tools (very important)
-walking mode and sit as a passenger
-rebind keybinds
-more cameras
-better antialising
-ability to remove the hud

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs is advertised, it feels like the lovechild of Train Sim World and Eurotruck.

15EUR seems like a fair price for the entire package, given that is the sale price of a single route on TSW or TS. Having said that, it still feels very "early access" but what's there is playable and enjoyable.

This is exactly what I'm looking for from a train game. The simulation aspect of it is minimal compared to Train Sim or TSW - you just get in the cab and control the speed and break. There is no separate steps for powering on the train etc. This is made up for by the management aspect, where you can grow your company by upgrading each of the 14 available German cities, giving you a more ETS2 style progression path.

There are graphics options, and the game runs fine on my older PC.

Unfortunately no option to invert the mouse, but other controls can be remapped.

Some thoughts:
- Graphics are nice, but a step down from TSW - currently a lot of reused assets.
- Trains look fine - good from the inside and out, but don't have such granular controls as in a true train simulator
- The map is only Germany, and has only major German cities. The different sites at each city feel only semi-realistic. There are some recognizable sites, like the Berlin Haubtbahnhof and the TV tower, but then also farms and oil fields which feel out of place
- The track network itself has some pacing issues - at one point you'll have an 80km/hr section, but not even enough time to reach that speed before it changes down to 40km/hr

Some Hopes:
- I hope Germany is worked on before other countries are added. More hand-crafted assets would really help.
- a larger overall map, with more distance between stops would help the flow of the game
- I hope they adopt a similar DLC practice as Eurotruck where you purchase entire countries, rather than single routes.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit:

After letting it in the oven a bit i decided to take my hands at Train Life again, and oh boy, im impressed so far.

I mentioned in my original review that it was in my eyes an Alpha game, im glad to say that now its way more worthy of being an early access from a technical standpoint.

-----
Visually: finally it runs smoothly, running it on a 3070 im glad that it gets at least a constant 70 fps in Ultra/Epic and seems to be smoother overall, dont know if there is still the motion blur filter on top but it doesnt matter much when the result is as polished.
Roadmap indicates more optimization, i'll be glad to see more of it
-----
Sound wise: its a bit more rich, its still not filled by natural noise and stuff that you barely notice but its still much better, a new Mix has been added and some sound punch more than other and its a pretty good update on that.
-----
Gameplay: still "pretty basic" but with noticeable upgrades, you dont have the curve of an actual ICE at start and buttons still feel instantaneous for example, and so still not too much my thing so far, i hope it will have more of Button > Action > Consequence rather than Button > Consequence immediatly.
But again its an improvement
The Ui elements of the menus have been slightly improved and the overall feel polished a bit but it still feels Early Access on that.

As stated in the previous review:
Please devs: dont drop your game, it has a lot of potential


----------

Here's the previous review:

Its a decent Early access at the moment, however still in deep Alpha state, and its important to note that because its hard to justify 15 bucks on a game that is not entirely done, that is the price of a medium sized game after all.
Train Life doesnt seem to aim at being a small or even medium sized game, here we have a mix between Euro Truck Simulator and Train Sim world.


In its current stage its not good at both but it is really decent overall, the basic stuff are there, now for the paint coat.

Visually: Its okay, almost good, overall not out of this world but pretty, it suffers a bit of stutters here and there especially in more crowded areas which when running on a 3070 should not be the case.
Note: Has a horrendous motion blur filter on top of all.
An option would be that the game features a single action / single mission mode in order to explore more visually impressive areas before the career part.
Overall the visual aspect is the most polished one at the moment.

Sound wise: Best i can describe is "weak" it lacks sound design by a lot, environment is here but its gone on the Train aspect of it.
It suffers from the same critic i had for Train Sim by Dovetail or Train Sim World: the game is wayyy too quiet, take example on the original Microsoft Train Simulator where each action had a distinctive and memorable sound.
Overall it needs a significant work there.

Gameplay: Its basic but the core of it is right here, its inspired from Train Sim World but lacks the more UI approach and realistic approach of it, calling it a sim at this stage is a bit of an overstatement, the train goes forward and reverses, but has no dynamic to it.
Its also inspired by Euro Truck and i hope the World will be covered on the long run.
I would love a more hands on approach on the tutorial, a bit like Euro Truck, instead of "well its not really the campaign".
The Ui elements of the menus are kind of... off, its not as terrible as Train Sim World but would need some polish on it.


In conclusion:
For a game dropped today as of review i sure hope it will become huge.
If the devs manage to work on all the points above it will earn not only its price but can also become a reference of Train Simulation.\t\t\t\t\t
