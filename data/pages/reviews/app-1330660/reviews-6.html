\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt this price, I definitely recommend it for people who like these kinds of games. As others have mentioned it plays like a child between Euro Truck Simulator 2 and Train Sim Word. The core gameplay is fun in that aspect, and the liberty to make your own route in all the European map is really fun.

There are some rough edges that will most likely get addressed, like the fact that trains barely produce any rolling sound, but all in all I haven't encountered any major bugs that affects gameplay in a significant way.

If you are coming from TSW2 and wonder, this game has a LOT of routes compared to default TSW2, although the train mechanics are not nearly as complex (on purpose). If you like the sightseeing aspect of driving trains, you might really enjoy it. In some cases, the countryside landscape can look just as good (and sometimes better) than TSW2. However, the big cities like Brussels and Cologne could definitely use another coat of paint, as the assets are repeated too much and lack details. This could probably be developed further with updates, the way ETS 2 does it.

All in all, for this price, you definitely get a lot of content! I recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf this was still in Early Access then I would review it more positively. However, this is supposed to be a full release so here goes...

The Bad...

1. Trains don't seem to have any weight to them. Pulling a long line of cargo feels pretty much the same as running empty. Going up hills is easy with hardly any slow down and going down hills is similar without any real acceleration.

2. Various graphical things that should have been modelled. E.g. points don't visually move nor do the wheels on any of the cargo trucks. Wheels do rotate on the main train though!

3. No Field of View adjustment!! I mean, c'mon this has been a basic requirement of any game since widescreens came in. It's not hard to implement (I've done it myself many times) - it is just lazy!

4. Ultrawide screens do work but without the Field of View adjustment from the point above it doesn't make comfortable viewing.

5. 3rd person view also feels extremely constrained - let me zoom out more!

6. Graphics look good but suffer from a weird slow down on some corners and points. It's not an FPS slow down, it is a simulation slow down.

7. Game seems to want to try a launch into VR mode as it fires up my VIVE control panel even though it is not a VR game - which is weird!

8. Encountered a crash in the tutorial when switching cabs.

9. Some of the tutorial descriptions do not describe what you actually have to do in enough detail often missing out a critical step or two!

The Good...

1. It's Euro Truck Simulator for trains!

2. Far more content than you'd get with Train Sim World etc

3. Easier to get into than Derail Valley and has a much more detailed, better performing and larger world.

4. Little events that happen while en-route. For example animals on the track or a broken down train.

Overall I think the game has got a bright future if they actually sit down and fix the basics. However, this should never have left Early Access in its current state because it certainly isn't finished.

However, it is pretty cheap. I wish Steam had a "maybe" button for the recommendations because I'm totally on the wall.

My advice is if you're into trains then try it but keep an eye on your 2hr refund window.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo be clear at the start. This isn't a bad idea or a bad game even. It runs well and the idea is really cool and I have waited so long for this but unfortunately it isn't really in a state I wanted it to be in. The thing that throws me of is the realism. It isn't really there tbh. Well yeah. You drive a train on a rail and you navigate through countries to deliver goods, people or mail. But there is a lot to be desired here.

First of, the signals. It just isn't right at all really. It shows red if you can't pass it and green if you can't. That is all, No amber light that warns you that there is a red coming up. So ultimately you don't know if you are fine to go full speed through the next signal or if you have to stop. Luckily the locomotive have some kind of superbreaks cause it stops in a heartbeat with emergency brakes. The game solves this though by alerting you through the UI that a train is coming your way. It is a lazy solution IMO. Just get the signals working and you don't need that.

Then we have the switches. I guess it is kinda fun to switch it yourself but I would like there to be an option to chose between that and where you contact some sort of switch center who keeps track on where you are going so that you don't have to do anything except looking for the signals and manage the train.

Another thing is the service center. You go there to fuel up, service and upgrade your train. You can also buy new trains here. This is another fluke. In real life these are often kind of drive thrus where you just stop, refuel/service, and then be on your way. Here you need to reverse back pass the switches and then go forward again. It isn't to much of a deal but it gets annoying pretty fast. Just do the same as the load/unload. There you have a straight track where you can just stop on, load/unload and then continue straight right away.

A couple of small ones that isn't vital but still adds on the pile:

- To little traffic. It's supposed to be in the middle of a train-heavy Europe but it's really just you and like 10 other trains on the whole map.

- Bad physics engine. There is none of the train management in this game that would be desired. In uphills the train barely gets harder to accelerate no matter how much cargo you are carrying. The brakes is just too good to be true.

To sum it up. This isn't a bad idea or even a bad game. But there is still alot to do for me to enjoy it. This feels like a merge between ETS2 and TSW but it's the lite-version. I would totally understand it if this was a Early-access game but since it is in full release I just don't think it's enough to call itself a railway simulator. I'm looking forward to some improvements on this though since it has potential.

TL;DR:
The game is fine but is as much of a simulator as Stardew Valley is a farming simulator.\t\t\t\t\t
