\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great addition to the train sim genre. I have always been more interested in the logistics side rather than just driving trains. Train Life strikes a great balance between operating the railroad with a purpose and taking on the engineer role. I found that using an X-Box controller makes it easy to control the train speed, apply brakes, change switches and navigate to the other commands. The logistics part is interesting and it appears that your choices to take deliveries or passengers changes how the industries and cities behave. The driving part is interesting and dynamic. After deciding what to transport, freight or passengers, you plot your route using a crude GPS. From there you pay attention to the position of switches, red is bad, green is good, and monitor the current and upcoming track speeds. The developers have been active squashing the early access bugs and have temporarily removed AI traffic while they sort out some of the more complex signaling logic. I am liking what I see so far and look forward to seeing this become a staple in the railroading sim / game market.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit after Update #3 (their 'major update')
Still lots of problems. The GPS/System interactions are not clear, and random at times. Simple confirmation dialogs are missing, so you can loose a lot of progress if you don't know what the game is going to do next.
Stations are still a mess, and its hard to get to the correct platform (GPS is still useless for this).
Overall the game still has a lot of promise, but the execution until this point is very lackluster. I really wanted to like this game, but I think I've lost my appetite, and hope, for this one.

END EDIT

Very positively surprised by the potential of this game...it brings everything I was wanting in tsw2 and more and has a much higher replay potential than derail valley. The immersion is more than appropriate and the controls are complex enough that you get enough of a feel for actually controlling the surroundings and your train.

It's super EA at this point, and a lot of things didn't work at the beginning, that make me worried about the maturity of the dev team, features that were clearly broken were released, much more than just little bugs. I hope the release will serve as a lesson for future development, but time will tell.

I will cautiously recommend this game for the potential, but give it a thumbs down for the release. I'll come back in a few patches and maybe revisit this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game. It has captured me in the first four hours and I'm very excited for future possibilities. However, the signalling doesn't work. Several times I've suffered collisions that were not my fault, which has brought some frustration. There is an incredibly addictive gameplay loop here, and an utterly adorable and atmospheric world. The radio permissions system which I very much enjoy resembles a real railway in a simplified manner.

I find it difficult to see this game breaching the diverse market of train simmers, as signalling is incredibly confusing. In every aspect other than two regions this game shines with originality and charm:

1) Signalling - This system is near enough the worst signalling system I have experienced in a railroading game. Trains are illogically blocked from double track sections as though they are single track. I find this hard to understand as the implemented system is incredibly complicated. In the game as in real life, if signals work in this manner, accidents occur, which leads in game to an immense penalty. An Up/Down line system would be simple to implement, and without it I fear train simmers will be immensely put off. (I am an avid fan of Train Simulator, Train Sim Word etc, and drive some 50hrs per week currently.)

2) The world design is gorgeous, but there are regions especially around Munich which are unreal engine wasteland, this I completely understand from an EA perspective, however I hope to see some improvements made in this regard. ( in some locations overhead wire pylons are stuck in track. )

I see you guys are excellent developers with an ambition. I simply cannot recommend this wholeheartedly in its current state unless I hear that signalling will be totally overhauled and fixed, for now I will continue playing the game and keep myself posted.

I love this game, please continue to improve it, I have never been this excited for an early access.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is honestly an amazing game, full of future plans which all sound amazing, and a great team of developers which have made this game how it is.

The gameplay:
Very fun, and relaxing to be travelling through germany (there will be more countries added across europe, you can look at the roadmap here: Roadmap (via twitter) . But one thing isn't amazing, but I, personally, can easily deal with this and it is the signalling, and AI trains. A lot of people have been getting angry about these issues, but don't worry, at the time of making this the lead game designer just posted that they will be beginning to start patching the AI (will be disabled in this process, basically fixes signalling along with doing this), and hopefully everything will be great again! I personally like the radio system, it just adds to what is already a great game.

8.5/10 at the time of making this, with the AI, probably 10 if the patching of the AI goes well

Trains: So far, a decent collection of trains, keeping in mind that more will be added (see roadmap), they all look nice, with Polish themed trains (they are all fictional I believe), French, and Austrian. They all have the same sound effects in each group, which may, or may not change, I do not know.

8/10

Map: A very large map, but it is going to get bigger with the addition of more countries in the future, you can travel anywhere you want to without any restrictions and nicely detailed stations, though they do all look the same, if not similar.

9.5/10

Graphics: A nice looking game, with the settings ranging from low-epic, you can get you quite beautiful pictures in this game.

8.5/10

Performance: For me, I get 20-30 fps, but that is most likely due to me lacking in RAM (5.9gb total physical memory I think), if you meet the minimum specs, you'll probably get it to run at manageable frames.

You need a decent PC for this.

Overall I think this is completely worth buying if you ever think about it, worth the price.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA nice idea, but a few flaws mean I can't currently recommend. Chiefly, signal aspects are not clear, there are two types of signals, and it's not ever clear in the actual campaign which ones are accurate, I've seen a green aspect signal just to see a train just ahead on the next segment, then get fined on another green aspect signal with a clear track ahead. The GPS guidance seems to like switching tracks on crossing points for no particular reason at times, despite there being no obvious obstruction to route around. Hazards seem to be spawned in semi-randomly, rather than other trains actually being timetabled.

EDIT:
After revisiting the game, I still can't currently recommend it. I didn't encounter issues with random obstructions, but then I also saw no other trains on the line. I don't know why "London" is a playable region as it seems to have one passenger station and some random freight terminals, having London apparently stand in for the entirety of South East England. I get it's not going for realistic scale, but come on. Also, I'd expect to see third rail layouts, and the signalling in the UK is completely different to this portrayal. I honestly wouldn't have included London as a region if it were my choice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI see a lot of potential in this game. I like the idea of a scaled down map and company management like in ETS.

However:

Unfortunately, I couldn't even get to the management aspect as completing simple delivery missions is already a pain to do. The signals tend to all switch to red (yellow?), which you don't seem to get a penalty for, but the small signals at track switches, which are barely visible in the way too frequent dense fog, often turn red randomly. You do get a warning that a train is approaching, however it would be nice if it didn't spawn on the same track that you were on heading in your direction. When you leave the game while on a long delivery mission, you respawn at the start point without your cargo or contract after starting the game again.

The map is at times nicely done but then has some glaring flaws at other spots, for example with rails bending in a sharp angle up or down when an incline starts or ends. The train sounds are a bit strange. The horns and in-cabin sound effects are decent, though I don't understand why a diesel engine's RPM would be directly tied to the speed of the engine. On the outside camera though, the sounds are for some reason much quieter than on the inside, which makes no sense.

I'm honestly a little concerned that this might just be a cash grab, because I don't see all the issues being fixed and the game being polished enough to be seen as complete in 6-10 months, as stated by the developers. I think that short amount of time is unrealistic, especially considering they plan to add more map content as well. For now, I've refunded the game, because it still has some major issues, like the signals, or the lack of a save option during a contract, which makes it not very fun to play. I hope to see improvements made to the game, in which case I will consider buying a copy again.\t\t\t\t\t
