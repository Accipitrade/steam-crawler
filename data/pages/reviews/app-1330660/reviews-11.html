\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you buy this, understand that you have to like the simplicity of trains. If you just see it as "going backwards and forwards" it's not for you. If you like the chill nature of train sims already, you might like this.

Yes, it's currently got issues that need to be worked out, but it's still in Beta. The core concept of this game is great because it takes the train simulator idea we're familiar with, then adds a bit more interactive gameplay, plus the freeroaming, business management nature of ETS2.

I don't have much time in it yet, but it's what I wish other train sims had been *cough cough, looking at you Train Simulator 20xx-whatever you've changed the number to now*.

You have an expansive map currently covering a large portion of western Europe and can travel between stations, picking up contracts and coasting along. Graphics are average but realistically not any worse than other sim titles. Can't speak to the "physics" much - my trains go and stop, get slower uphill and faster downhill *shrug*. Some random events in place already, such as broken down trains or wild animals.

Crucially, it hands over control of points/junctions to the driver and also needs you to radio to stations to let them know you're coming. Those little additions to gameplay make a big difference.

The roadmap looks good too, with map expansions, more trains and fixes to signals (they're a little unpredictable at the moment).

For the price, this is an absolute bargain... If you like trains.\t\t\t\t\t

None of the signs are correct.
None of the operational requirements are correct. And frankly i dont mind having to set switches myself (something that will NEVER happen on main and secondary lines in Europe).

I don't know what they are attempting to do here, but MSTS with ProTrain might be 15 years old but does a WAY better job at depicting rail operations in germany and looks about the same.

I hope the developers can fix all the above issues at some stage but at this point in time:

1/10 - avoid if you are looking for anything remotely realistic with trains.
The 1 point is given because it didnt crash for me.\t\t\t\t\t

I'd definitely recommend it at this stage as well.

I've played this game quite a bit now and still not tired from this. The devs have done a good job in updating and optimising the game. This game oozes of potential and lets hope it does not gets abandoned by the developers once they make enough money (like many game studios nowadays do).\t\t\t\t\t

It informs you that a train has broken down on the track ahead. The goal is to switch tracks and continue on. However sometimes you miss the correct switch and immediately get fines 1000 euros. Thing is I have switched to a different track and it still fines me. It is hard to tell which track the broken train will be on. I am 700m away from the train which lies around a curve and I have no way of knowing if we are both on the same track and thus I am fined. This makes it less entertaining to me. I would rather not have those obstacles and just go about my day riding the rails. If this can be made into an option that would be wonderful. Maybe add in speed restrictions or other items to be fined for.\t\t\t\t\t
