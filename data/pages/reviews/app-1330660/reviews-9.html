\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have almost 3000 hrs in Train Simulator 20xx, as well as some time in TSW and TSW 2. The economy aspect of this game is EVERYTHING the other 3 are not. It's the only reason I purchased this game. It is fun, Fairly easy to get a handle on after the tutorials, and has purpose! So far it seems to be, imho, a mix of Truck Simulator and Railway Empire. The world is massive, and 4 hours of play I still hadn't completed my 1st contract. (due to a couple of restarts)

CONS:
So far it is limited to European map and locos, which for me is a buzzkill...no offence Europe, but your locos suck! I much prefer our NA Huge Diesel Locos...but I guess it's whatever you grow up loving, right?
The game I believe is still early access, so there are some hiccups. If you are a North American Rail enthusiast, then the signals make absolutely no sense. I also don't like the GPS system as it really doesn't do anything, other than show your destination on the map. It doesn't clear you for dispatch, and you are required to throw switches in front and back. If your at full tilt, the switches come pretty fast as they are bunched extraordinarily close to one another and if you're not careful you could get thrown into a dead end track,(happened to me) or thrown into an industry with no clearance at 120kmh.

There is also fines for being on occupied track, but with no stop lights prior to a switch or crossover, how are you supposed to know. Sometimes you do get a red light, which I did stop at, but still got fined...so that seems to be a glitch.

All in all, if you love trains, and love economy industrial type simulator then you will probably like this. I will play some more and possibly update my post after I get to finish load.

I do hope that the DEVS consider adding some North American stuff, or even develop a North American product, kind of like ETS2 and ATS...I would shell out some more money for that in a heart beat!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is neither realistic nor authentic. It may simulate life through the frustration of things not being what they are advertised as.

Its not realistic in the behavior of the train and not authentic in for example signals or signs. The GPS (why does a train need that?) is confused about which side of track it needs to direct you. There are signals like every 50m, sometimes contradicting each other.

You have to switch switches yourself, but when arrving at stations, you don't know on what track you need to go. Soo... make a guess, drive forward, see its the wrong track, drive back again, switch, drive forward, nah wrong switch, back again...

There is so much more things that bug me, like getting fined for having the wrong light for the weather at a start of a scenario, when the light was already on from the start. Why not have the correct setting? Or on controller the standard back-out-of-menu button in this game is B, but not for radio-coms. If you accidentally press B to close the com-menu, it actually activates the emergency brake.

This is really frustrating. Not recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI highly recommend the review by [15th] lolpox. I will largely be repeating him here, but just focusing on what are (for me) the biggest pain points:

1) As well as being unrealistic, with no signal-chains, the way the driver controls the signals in this game is ultimately a twitch game. When approaching a station with more than two platforms or loading areas, get ready to spam the Q key over and over in rapid succession. The consequences of failure are a fine and a heavy, annoying reversal. I don't mind *that* much that it's unrealistic; it's just not fun.

2) The coupling/decoupling/consist management is pathetically done. Again, it's not the lack of realism that gets me; it's that it's completely unintuitive, failure is catastrophic, and the game gives you no meaningful feedback on how to avoid failure.

3) The minimap is worse than useless; it's actively misleading. The direction of your switch isn't actually shown. Not to mention the station design itself; 'service centers' (basically developerese for 'marshalling yards, maintenance depots, and shops all bundled into one place) are placed badly.

4) Having the rights to ICE in Germany and nowhere else is forgivable; having ICE speed capped at a ludicrously low level because the renderer can't draw the not-exactly-oil-painting landscape fast enough is not.

5) Post-tutorial, the early game makes no sense. You have a loco, a company name, and $40,000; the initial contracts suggested are transcontinental odysseys with 10 cars (out of a possible 12.)

I get that it's early access but the basic errors here are many, and points 1, 2, and 4 in particular feel like they'd require an entire rebuild to fix. Point 3 can be fixed more easily but would have made sense as an early feature (instead of some aspects of the skill tree.) Point 5 suggests a dev team that have played their own game so much they've completely forgotten that not everyone has done so, and some kind of post-tutorial, early-game experience is standard fare in this genre.

I'm getting a refund. If the majority of the points above are addressed I'd love to give it another shot, but right now it utterly pales in comparison to the likes of ATS.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately this is nowhere near a simulator. If you're looking for a real driving experience, this isn't it.
Let me walk you through the problems I noticed; not necessarily coming from the nature of the early access.

- Signalling
This one was mentioned in other reviews as well. This is completely off from the real world, this is hot mess. I "somewhat" understand, what the developers tried to achieve here, and it would be "somewhat" acceptable, if there would be at least a signal-chain. But in this game all the signals are living their independent and useless life: you can get a "danger" (red) right after you passed a "proceed with full speed" (solid green). There is no exact speed indication with the signals only increase/decrease, but hey, at least you'll get two signals in an switch, one for each direction! (see the trailer, 18s in the video) Signals aren't indicated on the map (not the state, the signal!), also there is no such thing as reduced signal distance indication... Of course you'll hit that "danger" signal with all of your full speed...

- Physics (lack of)
Do you want to coast with your solo engine? Do you want to use the gradient to speed up/slow down? Barely any effect.
Do you throttled up more than one notch? Wheel slip. Did you lowered your throttle at 80 km/h, but you'd like to throttle back to the original notch? Wheel slip. Are you applying more brake than one notch "per time"? Wheel slip.
I have a suspicion, that wheel slip doesn't even depend on the physics, but simply on a timer: like "when was the last time you pressed this button". Not like the WHOLE circus around the wheel slip would matter, as again, not really existent physics...
You have 10 wagons behind you? Don't worry, you'll get almost the same braking performance, with the same wheel slip characteristic, of course! Dynamic braking? Handbrake for wagons? Hehe...
The whole acceleration/braking performance/slipping topic seems to be covered under "yeah, just but an upgrade" section.

- Coupling/decoupling
When you accept a contract and couple the wagons, those wagons are spawned from nowhere. While this is acceptable, in a simulator I'd expect way more, like "pick up the wagons from Siding #6". But the real criminal thing is the decoupling. Once you coupled the wagons, you can only decouple those in SOME of the station's track-sections (neither in any siding you picked, nor in open track). Why is this a big thing? Let's see in the next sections!

- Turning the engine / going around the consist
"Officially" you can only turn your train around in the workshops. You can't really go around your own consist, as you can't decouple anywhere (see above), AAAAND if you still try to do it in a station, (after going around) a buffer-to-buffer contact with your own decoupled consist will yield a "yeah mate, you collided with a foreing train" message and a fine. GG.

- Switching (wagons)
You wanna have some help during your switching? You'd like to see, if you already passed the turnout? You'd like to see, which turnout you will set with the "switch turnout" button? You'd like to see not only the next turnout's state? Nah mate. This section needs also a lot of developer attention, as it is completely missing.
Oh I almost forgot, the turnouts are not locked (again, simulator?), but your engine will not force to move the points!! You can go through a turnout, and it will still keep the original direction...

- Saving the game
Looks like the game isn't saving your actual state, it is saving your "expected" state. I've once lost 5 decoupled wagons (completely disappeared) as I was switching with the rest of the train. Loading the save/autosave put me on the station entry point (needing also a new access permit), with all my wagons gone. I still see the contract I took, but I'm unable to complete as there are no wagons any more.

- Upgrades/Skills
OK, so either this is a simulator, or there is no place for upgradeable skills like "Fuel Usage" or "Passenger Wagon Capacity". Also, upgrading your engine, like extending the fuel tank is like "umm, sure thing"... The rest of the somewhat-dubious upgrades I could live with.

- Career / purchases
Your company will start with an engine and a bunch of money, right after you "graduate". I mean, sure; but how about working for an already established company, or maybe as a freelancer for a competitor company initially? How about earning money for your first engine? Quick contracts, "jump in here, deliver there"? Nah...
Despite you have a "private company", you'll get all your wagons for free. Passenger, cargo, doesn't matter, just couple those shared wagons. Purchasing your own rolling stock? Premium passenger wagons? Setting a fare for your passenger train? Having different train classes? Liveries? Managing the cargo wagons? Nah. :/ Oh, I almost forgot this one: you can "involuntarily" cancel your taken cargo contract(s) by switching your engine into passenger mode...
As for the engines, well, there's no real market, I miss this aspect from many-many games... Like c'mon, this one isn't too hard. I literally created mods for other games just for covering this topic... No used locomotives, no competing engine brands, no lease, no rent, nothing. No possibility to buy the upgradeable parts like cheap motive power from scrapped engines or anything like that. I know, I'm dreaming, but hey, there's a simulation label next to the game...

Since this game is labelled as a simulator, I could also wonder about stuff like "wild animals warning sign", or "stations will not care, what do you wanna do there, which turnouts you'd like to set; you just have to request access", or my favorite: "stations only have control over their respective tracks, everything outside the entry/exit markers are LITERALLY wild west" (eg a rouge stuck engine can spawn in your way) - but I'll skip mentioning these for now.

Not sure if I recalled everything I wanted to rant about, but I'll not refund the game (although I wasn't far from that). I hope I'll be able to enjoy this, but the current state is definitely not for me. Anyone who'd like to have any kind of a realism or simulation, well... This game is not for you - yet.

I just have this "weird, but somewhat familiar" feeling this game will never go really towards real simulation during the early access phase.\t\t\t\t\t
