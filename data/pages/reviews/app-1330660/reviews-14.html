\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo first things first, this game has no business being only 22 dollars Canadian, there is easily over 40 dollars worth of content here.

I have only just finished the tutorial missions but I can already see the content that is available at launch. There is enough content here to keep me going for weeks between locomotives you can purchase and upgrades for said locomotives. There is also a whole company aspect to the game that is fleshed out enough to spend hours recruiting a building up conductors.

Third the game uses Unreal 4 so the graphics are really nice when you compare it to other games like Derail Valley. Which by the way is more expensive and doesn't have near the content.

Now for the bad:
1) You don't manually couple and decouple cars. You do have to select the radio and ask for permission to enter the yard, but once a little tool tip shows up you just stop the train and select couple of decouple

2) The radio isn't voiced and is a wall of text. This can cause you to miss signals and speed changes while you read said wall of text. I am sure there is some sort of voice acting planned so this is more of a gripe than a reason not to play.

3) There are bugs. The first two I can list of the bat are in the tutorials. The first one is when you have to drive you train to the first service center. I stopped the locomotive, pressed A on the controller and the tutorial refused to proceed. I ended up having to restart twice to get it to finish. The second was during the passenger tutorial. When I tried of offload it kept telling me I could not close the doors until the unloading of passengers was complete. This was also fixed by a restart.

All and all I would 100% recommend this game. Besides what I have listed above the game has full controller support for the Xbox One controller which was just the icing on the cake. Seriously this game is cheaper than taking my wife out for dinner and far more enjoyable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot recommended for now, too much things are missing:
- no traffic (no other trains)
- the signals and speed limits along the rail don't match what's displayed on HUD,
- the path planner (when using GPS route) keeps asking to change lane randomly between stations
- I can't load passengers if I have passenger wagons AND post wagons
- Cursor disappear in cabin view
- it's more a tycoon than a simulator: controls are too simple, accelerate/brake and go left/right. Doesn't feel like a simulation.
- no timetables for passenger trains. Stations accept and produce goods and passengers, you can go anywhere at your own pace. there are missions/contracts though that ask to deliver to a specific -far- location within a time frame.
- balancing is needed (too easy to buy a second loco and hire a driver)

But overall a good potential and I like what it can become. I'll check and edit this review on future updates.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTrain games are not exactly a highly competitive market so most are downright garbage. Train Life is not one of my favorites for good reason. A couple of the games I prefer are as follows with the first being a bit complicated to learn as it's Korean and you need to make notes about what certain things are and how to do things. The second is designed for multiplayer, but, I and many others play solo. The first is Hmmsim Metro. The second is RAILROADS Online! Check those for better games.

A large update came out. I have made a couple deliveries in Germany so far and glitches have been much better. Animals don't have faces glues to tracks. I'll keep editing this as I play more and if it deserves it will change to voting up.

Platforms you see the same folks standing around no matter time of day or night. Nobody actually boards or gets off. There are many issues. They are working hard at resolving things, but, I'm surprised they even released into EA with many of the issues that were apparently poor design and zero developer testing. I would have been embarrassed to put it out in EA.

More countries just came out. I have not been in them yet. They won't be having us restart at the end of EA, so, for now my character is top level and all my trains are top level as well as a few cities. So, I'll finish upgrading all the German cities to top level (all are either one level to go or 0 to go). Then I'll mostly take a break and come back to check now and then after changes while checking out other games. Once you have the max employees as I do and millions in the bank as I do and all the max equipment as I do then I'm not sure the reason for staying. Other than new countries to explore and level once you have everything at max level there isn't any built in reason for staying. No idea myself how to remedy that and provide ongoing goals. It would be nice if they could do it though. As is, you get bored to tears when all leveled and nothing to spend money on.

I wish I could do a neutral rating as this has potential if the team is up to it (which I'm not so sure they are capable of). You can get your money's worth, but, I strongly suspect there are much better made new train games available with far more to do like designing and laying your own tracks while building bridges etc.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tcan i recommend it yes/no but be aware it is not as advanced a traim sim as other sims.
example you only have 1 brake in the train and no ability to example put your train in reverse and help it to slow down your train when going downhill.
Also you wont see the doors on passengerstrains open/close ( maybe someday we will )
In general the game is much like euro truck simulator but with trains.
You can when you deliver your train select to automatic deliver it = you can stop anywhere in a station and decouple anywhere and get your money OR you can manualy drive to the correct location and decouple.
Next when you decouple things just despawn so that you can go pickup other wagons.
You can also drive to the depot at all locations and select to have the trains direction reversed and that alsoo include your wagons.. That is actual kind of nice.
The stations are kind of small. yes there are sidetracks but nothing to use them for except delivery or taking an alternative rute. There are no "life" in the game except a deer near the track now and then and static passengers in the station. But no moving cars, trucks, walking people or other trains or even parked wagons.

There is a gps system in the game wich do work pretty good but its not perfect. But it also help you switch tracks by lighting up the train switch with either red og green so you know if you have to press Q to change track ahead.. that is kind of nice so you dont have to look at a map to find your way.. But sometimes the gps also take you though a station vs just driving arround it via side tracks.

Els you can upgrade and buy new trains as you earn money and level up and as you go you also get better skill do do more jobs ect.

All in all yes it look like a nice game and there is allot of track to drive on and guess +25 citys and industrys
For the low price of 15 euro you do get quit allot of train sim but dont expect it to be as advanced as other train sims.

I can imagine that the game will evolve like euro truck simulator with country DLC's in the future.
hopefully more features and realism will be added in the game as development goes by so it feel less like an arcadegame and more like a sim. Example we cannot click most buttons in the cabin of the trains.
Oh i forgot to enter any facotry or train station you need to radio ahead and tell them what your intentions are els you get fined. The radio system is used to tell the game what you want to do pickup/unload ect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOK OK... First of all let me say I rated this title as "recommend" only because I think it has huge potential and, let's face it... if $15 bucks is a deal breaker you're in the wrong hobby. I usually don't pay for something I haven't gotten (my lesson was paying a landscaper for a whole job he was to do on a Friday and by Monday morning I discovered that he closed his doors and skipped town with my money) but I'm gonna make an exception in this case. For two reasons... first - It's worth the $15 as is! Second - it allows the devs to buy groceries on their way home tonight and not starve before they finish the game.

So.. what's right here? Just about everything!

1 - The maps are great and challenging.
2 - Graphically the game is very good and I expect it will be improved as time goes by.
3 - While controls aren't identical to other train sims, they are quickly adaptable.
4 - Conceptually, this is a total winner. ETS on rails! Double thumbs up!

Since I really don't know of any real deal breakers here this is my list of things I wish were improved and how to improve them (I'm sure most of it is on the dev's list as well)

1 - There is no option to customize the hot keys... what you buy is what you get. I keep hitting the emergency break when I want to blow the horn.
2 - Instead of raising deer with a negative IQ, give them some smarts. You have to come right up on top of them and hit the horn and hope they bounce in the right direction or you're gonna pay for damage and a lawyer to get the tree huggers off your back. While it's true that deer do get nailed by trains, those are the ones singled out by Darwin for the good of the world. Not all deer are stoopit!
3 - The signals do need a bit of work but they aren't the train wreck (excuse the expression) that the YouTubers think they are. If the Bloody English YouTubers drove on the CORRECT side of the road to begin with like the rest of the world, the signal problem they're experiencing would be half solved. But the signage/signals do need some work and, hey Devs... a short .pdf of what you programmed the signals to mean would be nice. Train signals differ almost from country to country. Let us know what you want us to do. This is one area that I'm absolutely sure the devs have on their lists. (by the way... having lived in England for several years, I firmly believe they are correct... driving on the left is a better system. Go figure...)
4 - The GPS... Does this mean Get Pissed Suddenly? Again, driving on the right side of the tracks does make the GPS work a little more logically but, come on guys! I just spent about 30 minutes trying to figure out which track I was supposed to be on outside of Nuremburg. Lots of backing up... Once the AI returns this problem may in fact become a deal breaker. As it is it's extremely difficult to use the GPS. Suggest that there be a GPS screen zoom function like there is in ETS. Maybe this is in the works. Whether it is or it isn't, this is the only deal breaker I've encountered. That or toss the Tom-Tom and use your cell phone...
5 - Unless you guys make it a whole lot clearer where people are supposed to go in terminals and cargo areas (the invisible red arrow and incredibly transparent red tracks are not contributing to my fun-O-meter in the least) we really need a free camera so that we can fly around and find the right pier. Maybe there is a free camera somewhere but I've worn the markings on my keyboard off looking for it. The three camera views you get using the C key just ain't enough.
6 - Lastly, and I know this is in the works, bring back the AI. But, when you do, please give us a short manual we can look at so we know how we are supposed to interact with the signals. Hey!! We're gamers... not retired railroad engineers.

Overall... I can honestly say I've already gotten my money's worth out of this title and am expecting to log hundreds, if not thousands, of hours in it like I did in ETS. I'm not sure I'm going to go back into the game at this time to try to figure out how the hell to get out of Nuremburg or give it a month or so to see what the devs come up with. But I gotta say... this one has the most potential for a load of fun I've seen in several years. I can't wait to see what's on the close horizon. If you have $15 and can avoid buying the Big Crap for lunch, this is a great place to spend that cash.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far I have the following complaints:
1. poorly simulated train performance. The control respond and train performance has a simplistic logic, there is no feel of weight, engine power, or dynamics at all.
2. poorly modeled cockpit system. All trains has somewhat different control panel layout but share exact the same system, functionality, and buttons.
3. Prefabricated track patterns all over the world. Lack of diversity in track design and station layout. Station layout are also simplistic, and design of stations makes no sense practically.
4. Simplistic driving tasks. No coupling uncoupling, stop your train anywhere. Pick up your passengers at the end of platform and it will work. No accuracy in operation is required whatsoever.
5. Signal system is messed up. Speed limit are indicated by lights and separate from block signal. You get green light when there is a train occupying the track in front.
6. Unexpected events are expected. Moose on track every five minutes, and you can always honk it off.
7. Unless you are operating an EMU, You have to visit a depot to turn around. However, there are many terminal passenger stations. That means, you have to back up from the station blind.
8. Empty world, no AI trains or stationary carriages. Stations are empty.
9. GPS is guiding you to the wrong side of track.
10. Quiet world, quiet train. Sound is static.
11. If you want to buy train parts or locomotives. Nobody will tell you what are they and how they are different except their names and maxim speeds. Train parts sounds useless as the game doesn't tell what's different between these parts.

I might update my review if things get improved. But for now, it's not a simulator, nor is it fun to play as a game.\t\t\t\t\t
