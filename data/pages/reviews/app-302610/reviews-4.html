\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoson X is a simple, but enjoyable, platforming experience. You control your character by deciding when to jump, or when to move to the "side" in a circular path, avoiding obstacles or avoiding falling off the stage. As you go through each level, you have to try to hit "boost pads" effectively to increase your speed and get a higher score. With each stage, the overall speed and complexity of the levels increases, and while the basic formula is very simple, it is very addicting.

I played the original free version online, and this version has more levels and is more enjoyable than that one was.

I highly recommend it to anybody who wants a good timing-based platformer without overly complex mechanics.

It took me only 5.6 hours apparently to fully complete, and I probably only took 3 or 4 hours to do so if you include me replaying a few levels just for fun, so it isn't a particularly long game. If you are not very good at platforming games, it probably will take more like 10 hours to get through with a bit more practice.

Still, for $2.99, it is definitely worth the cost.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is definitely an endless runner done well - the controls are simple (arrow keys and WASD to determine your jumps) and so is the premise and gameplay - You being a scientist using your own body to discover particles in a large particle accelerator which may or may not destroy the world.

Graphics are lovely and minimalist, though that doesn't stop the game from being trippy if it wants to be. The music, although simple, can sometimes be a bop, especially when you're supercharged in a level (i.e. over 100%).

Be warned though, this game is hard, especially on the Anti and Dark Levels (I never really got the hate for Anti X Boson, but Dark X Boson was tricky for me.), but it can lead to two things, the difficulty makes you determined to discover the particle, or it makes you spend money since you broke your monitor in a fit of rage.

Overall, it's a decent game - especially for those who love endless runners combined with total trips. 9/10, would violently collide with a planck over and over again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of my favorite mobile games before it came to Steam. This is an endless runner that requires you to focus really hard on making the right choices on the paths you take, because if it isn't the right one you'll have to start all over again on a level. Even when you're on the right path small error as in not holding the directional or jump button long enough can be instant death. The goal is simple; fill your energy bar by running on the pulsating blue platforms. The whole game is about trail and error as you learn the path layouts as they've all got a pattern to them. They are random, but they give you a visual cue on what the right path is about to spawn, but it's up to you to pick up on it.\t\t\t\t\t
