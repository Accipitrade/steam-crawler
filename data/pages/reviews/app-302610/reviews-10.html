\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPositives:
- interesting concept for progression. unlike other twitch games like super hexagon (in which you’re just waiting out a timer for 60 seconds until you win), this game forces you to be proactive instead of reactive, requiring energy from blue platforms to progress. the level could feasibly go on forever if you let it, only ending if you die regardless of whether you got 100% energy or not. (100% energy is required to unlock other levels)
- difficulty progression is great in one isolated level. you start off at a predetermined speed, and as you gain energy from aforementioned blue platforms, you start to pick up speed. this reduces the amount of time you get to make choices on where to jump, and makes the level much more exhilarating and challenging.
- graphics are simplistic and polygonal: i personally quite like how the graphics look.

Negatives:
- as a consequence of the way of progressing through levels, this game can just as, if not more frustrating than others of its genre. failing at 59.99 seconds in super hexagon can be super infuriating, but you do know that you’re just 0.01 seconds away from completing the level, and often this is enough motivation to continue. in contrast, boson x can trap you at 90-95% energy level, and decide to give you heaps of difficult challenges with no blue platforms to allow you to complete the level. this is likely much more annoying.
- levels are very distinct from each other in terms of difficulty, as the difficulty of the “y boson” levels (levels just before the final levels in each world) are often much more difficult than the actual hardest levels.
- music loops very quickly and can drone on and on. most “songs” are only 20-30 seconds.

Overall: i would recommend this if you enjoyed super hexagon/games of a similar style, and want to try something similar, but different. however, i can see this not being very fun if you don’t particularly enjoy this style of gameplay and how aggravating it can possibly be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR:
Major compliments: Hypnotic music, generally challenging, and very easy to lose yourself in for a while.
Major complaints: The Dark Graviton level hurts to look at due to the flashing. Several sections in later levels feel unfair. The time between a fail state and the restart is painfully long in certain cases.

In 2013, the Higgs Boson was discovered, confirming a prediction made half a century earlier and acting as an effective death knell for many attempts to contest the Standard Model. It cost 13.5 billion dollars total to find it, and involved shooting tiny objects with other tiny objects at face-melting speed. About a year later, Ian MacLarty and Jon Kerney released Boson X, a game about tiny scientists hitting tiny objects at similarly face-melting speed.

Boson X is a restart-em-up, which is a genre everyone knows about but nobody seems to have made a name for. It shares the title with games like Hotline Miami and Super Hexagon. To be a restart-em-up, a game has to meet a few conditions.

  • The player has very good control of their actions.
  • The process of losing a game, the "Fail state", is achieved almost instantly after a mistake is made with very little chance to recover.
  • The process of restarting the game is nearly instant, with very little loading time in between attempts.
  • While not a requirement, many of these games feature hypnotic visuals or soundtracks.

The problems with this game are immediately visible when you judge it based on these criteria. The jumping in this game, the only method you have of moving, has a strange quark I haven't seen elsewhere: You drop like a solid rock when you let go of the key. This is something the game builds some obstacles around. While this does give you more control over your character, I've choked and lost runs many times because I held a key down for too long.

And when you choke, you feel it. The restart is instant, but there will be many runs where you realize a femtosecond too late that you've made a mistake, and you then have about two seconds where you can't do anything while you watch yourself tumble off the side of a platform. This isn't a problem I've seen with other fail states such as smacking into a wall, but since falling is much more frequent than hitting a wall it's much more obvious.

Though the walls are where this game feels almost unnecessarily challenging. Several later levels, especially once you start getting into the "Dark particles" section of the game, have obstacles that are claustrophobic; small tunnels you have to quickly hop through. Hitting the sides kills you, as does hitting the walls in front of you. The regions between these two spaces is so small that the timing to get through is almost tau precise. This goes double when you're at a very high charge level, and the margin of error is nonexistant.

The most extreme problem, and the reason I stopped playing long enough to write this, is something that I'm not sure you can get around. Simply put, the Dark Graviton level HURTS to look at. The main quirk of the graviton levels is that there are platforms that race toward you, and these platforms flash a little. To make the dark graviton level a bit harder, every platform seems to flash slightly, though not as much as the actual fast ones. A clever way of making it harder, but not in the way I think the developers predicted. My eyes stung after trying to clear it, which can partially be attributed to this game rewarding you keeping your eyes gluon the center of the screen. Simply put, if you're photosensitive, playing that level is fission for trouble.

The thing is, even with these issues, I do wholly enjoy playing this, if only in short bursts. Except the Dark Graviton level. Call me electroweak, but by that point I hadron most of my conclusions about this game's strong forces and weak forces. All in all, the game has it's ups and downs, but I'm thoroughly charmed.\t\t\t\t\t
