\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay: This game is a series of Dark Souls bosses. I seriously found myself having the same exact approach to each level as I would a boss. I’d start up a level and fail usually straight away. Often the level would seem impossible or unfair at first. I’d feel like giving up and often I did. But upon returning and attempting the levels more, I found myself slowly inching that energy bar up towards 100%. Essentially I was learning the levels move-set and its new tricks. Each level completion was such a release of tension and a sense of victory and accomplishment, that I failed immediately afterward, unable to rack up any bonus points. This game is a platformer, where you run through a hexagonal world of ever changing platforms. The game has 18 levels in which I spent over 10 hours. The levels obviously ramp up in difficulty, adding in new mechanics here and there to keep the player on their toes. The goal is to get your energy level to 100% and is achieved by running on the scarce blue platforms.

Design: The level design is randomly generated from a base set of platforming segments that all flow together nicely (when you do figure it out). This is important as each level segment has to flow into every other segment. You wouldn’t want a game to have these randomly generated combinations to run you into an impossible scenario, making the game unfair sometimes. The visuals are blocky and basic, but serve their function. The sound design is overall decent. The music can be a bit annoying at times and I found it’s not really any help (or hindrance) to listen or not. One level in particular, I finally beat when I muted the audio. But this could be due to the increasing pitch that is triggered as you ramp the energy up. That sound mechanic really adds tension to those final percentage points.

Overall: I’d recommend this game to anyone wanting a platforming/runner challenge.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the few games I know set inside a Particle Accelerator - Boson X is like a 3D Super Hexagon as many people have probably already noticed. Its also just as addicting to play. The music is amazing, the levels are pretty and well designed, the challenge increases exponentially (entropy joke) and is genuinely enjoyable as it is frustrating. It even graphs your progress (or lack of, sometimes) for you.

Also how many other games include a "Choose your Physicist!" for a character-select menu? Apart from something Star Trek related but thats besides the point...

A 4th group of particles on top of the Standard, Dark and Anti-Particle research facilities would be awesome, but its genuine fun already.

Only complaint aside from the frustration factor is sometimes the levels can be a bit too trippy, take frequent breaks it causes eye strain very quickly for me.

8/10\t\t\t\t\t
