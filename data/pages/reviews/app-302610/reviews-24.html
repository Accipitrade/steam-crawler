Overview
Boson X is a rotational infinite runner with a nice premise, addictive gameplay and challenging difficulty. You play as one of three characters of your choosing and run along platforms inside a particle accelerator, in the hopes of discovering a new one! This is one of those games that is simple to grasp, but hard to play! If you thought Super Hexagon was fun and difficult, well, now you have this Decagon to try and master!

The Pros:
  • Fitting Visuals - The game's focus on fast moving objects and rapid player movements make choosing this minimalist art style very fitting. There are no textures, just solid colors, and it just works! You won't get distracted by textures, instead all your attention is to be averted to your movements. Also, it kind of makes sense, as you are travelling between particles. Things to small to have textures (or geometrical shapes, for that matter, but we'll let this one slide).

  • Level Generation - The levels are put together using procedural generation, but not by randomness, There are modules put together in a random order. So, once you'll play enough of a level, you will start to get used to the more difficult steps. This is great, because you can learn without having to go through the same level over and over if you fail.
    What's more, each level has it's own theme - different modules, different speeds, different color schemes and even unique pieces!

  • Addictive in Nature - Even though later levels will make you want to scream because of how hard they are, dying is sometimes enjoyable, and just makes you want to play again, because you are confident you can pull it off! Also, a great game to play when bored.

  • Scoring and Leaderboards - Instead of scoring you on how long you stay alive, or how far you've gone, the game scores you in percentages, and that increases each time you run on blue platforms. Once you reach 100%, you pass the level, and go into a sort of hyperdrive with increased speed, a change of color scheme and significantly increased difficulty. Leaderboards are also wonderfully done, with scores broke down by level, in total, and friend comparison.

  • Reasonable Achievements - The achievements are all based on finishing the levels. There are 18 in total, but don't think this will be an easy feat. You may be surprised how challenging this game can get.

Cons:
  • Surprisingly.. None - It's rare I never find a con in a game. This one is the exception that enforces the rule. The game does what it sets to: Gives an addictive, infinite runner game, with no garbage on the side - just plain, good, challenging fun. For people that would consider the difficulty a con, you are missing the point. For this genre, a more merciful experience will do nothing but dampening the fun.

Conclusion:
If you like infinite runners, are up for a challenge and you want a great game for cheap (also featured on sales for next to nothing), this is an easy purchase. Buy with confidence.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, you shouldn't play this game if you're looking to relax. Like seriously, you're only going to get yourself more stressed than you already were on first place. Although I had my share of fun, now that I've finished it, I never want to see it again. So please devs, don't you dare to make a sequel such as 'Boson X² - The revenge' because I'll surely buy it and probably write another similar review (if I actually manage to survive until the end).
Ok, now for an overall scope: The graphics are ok, the soundtrack not that much but that's ok, because I've played while listening to my own playlist. The level design is simply insane and somehow reminded of this DOS game called 'Skyroads' (which I always loved). Besides that, it's really cool the fact that you're able to select different characters to play with, because it'd be unfair for a single soul to receive such a large amount of punishment during the research. Last but not least, if you're looking for a good challenge for a fair price, this game is definitely for you.

Now just as a bonus, here's a transcript of my experience with this game:


New Level

- These textures are seriously broken! Oh wait, it's meant to be like that.
- Look at THAT. I'll never get through this thing.


Deaths

- So why am I still alive? Ah, of course, I'm not.
- What does this blinking red platform do? Oh, that...
- Oh no, not that crazy pattern once more...
- What the...?! Three of these in a row! Three in a bloody row!!!
- Dang! I won't make that mistake again!
- ... I've just made the same mistake again.
- Come on, I definitely kept the button pressed on that jump.
- Almost there! I'm gonna make it! I'm gonna make it! No, I'm not...
- 99,18%!!! Oh, the humanity!!!
- Well, I suppose that it can't get harder than that...
- ... It actually can.


Level Cleared

- 100%!!! Yeah, now let's try to... Aaand I'm dead.
- Yay, I can't wait to see my ranking position! Oh, never mind...

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis really is an overlooked simple but well made little game. I noticed and picked up Boson X when it appeared in the weekly offers at -90% on the Steam store, less than a year after release and that was clearly a steal. It's a small rotational platform runner title that clearly went unnoticed and despite being fairly short and lacking much content, it's definitely worth picking up when on sale, if not at full price.

Highlights

Most of the low priced little indie titles on Steam aren't well realized, or come with too many problems to be worth the hassle, but Boson X is short and comes together really nicely. It's polished more than you'd expect and the sound and especially the music in particular is worth noting for being fast paced, at times thumping, and fun, while also tying into the levels really well.

The entire game is packaged under a scientific theme of physics, and from the chemistry lab looking title screen which reminded me of Half-Life 2 and Portal, to the physicist and particle references, it's themed subtly but well and compliments the gameplay.

The random placement of the platforming elements falling into place along with the randomized directions, speeds and distances of certain things all make for a changing difficulty and unpredictable level. Some of the earlier and later levels in particular can be both easy and very challenging in the same playthrough, and it added a small level of that "want to play again" and "just one more try" feeling.

Mistakes & Annoyances

There are some annoyances with this title, but at the price and length they're mostly forgiveable, but more so they don't actively break the game and don't take away very much overall.

As mentioned, there's a nice level of unpredictability built in by the random selection of speeds, times and platform locations which probably adds more than it takes away, but at times it's definitely more annoying than if the section had been given fixed timings. Dying at the same sections because there's no way to predict the placement of a block up ahead, or crossing lightning paths which may make it impossible to chose a safe direction are frustrating more than they need to be and should have had more attention spent on at least making them more fluid. In short, the game is often unfair, and that's the last thing you want in a game involving precision, timing and player planning.

The other complaint is the lack of content or length of gameplay which clocks in at an average of around 5-6 hours, which is great value for money, but because the mechanics are fairly flat, even unexciting at a push, it means there isn't anything to make you go wow or keep you engaged for very long. The developer has mentioned all the open license libraries and given links to the engine and more in the file directory, presumably to encourage modding/development, but Steam Workshop is a common request and honestly would've added a lot to this title, it's a shame it hasn't been added.

Recommendations

Fully priced, Boson X is available on Steam at just £1.99/2.99€/$2.99 and with an average gameplay length of 5-6 hours, it's great value for money. It's also regularly on sale for as much as 90% off, which makes it nearly free. It's probably not going to rock your socks off and keep you playing, and if you have a long backlog it might be worth missing out on, but at the price it's a fine pick up for anyone taking an interest from the store page.

* You can actively edit and/or replace the music and sound via the /data directory. You can even edit the character models and animations.
* Run, a flash game with similar, albeit flatter mechanics is available on Coolmath, Kongregate and other sites to play for free.
