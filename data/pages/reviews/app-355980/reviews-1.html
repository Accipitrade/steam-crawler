\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was pretty good. It was challenging and had a lot of strategy involved. The towers work well together and the goal is to find efficient combinations that work given the unique challenge of the level you're on. There's often more than one way to beat a level which makes things interesting.

While there's enough strategy playing through the levels the first time, there are runes you can apply that increase your XP gain while also making the levels harder in certain ways. It adds a new element of strategy.

Really good for ~30-40 hours of play time IMHO.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly this game will frustrate you. Strategies are limited to a very fine level of possibilities. Creativity alone or things that make sense to you won't work. The game is satisfying in that the vast majority of levels are fun up until weird things happen like your super expensive barriers exploding exposing your dungeon heart in the late parts of levels where you're broke? or enemy dudes being flung over your meat walls and barriers directly into your dungeon hearts. Some of your traps will betray you causing the above mentioned problems.

I've played 31 hours and beat about 75% of this game then ran into a wall that was plainly not fully beatable. The enemy is unrelenting and will keep coming but your traps will suffer down times or be too slow to stop anything even fully upgraded. Fun for the challenges and for some time wasting but not much more than that. Price is fabulous when on sale!\t\t\t\t\t

Is Dungeon Warfare Worth It?

To be honest, yes. Very much so. Not only does it have a low pricepoint but it's a solid game with varying mechanics, from levelling up to traps and spells.

There are tons of positives about this wonderful game, however:

- I feel it takes a lot from Gemcraft. Some people won't agree, but the use of runes to challenge yourself, the wavestones, the layout of certain traps feels very much like GemCraft. Not complaining, just observing. Afterall, Gemcraft is one of my favourite games.

- Music. Although this is a very personal take, the music could've varied. It's one track for every dungeon. I will often play muted and put my own music on instead, so no biggie.

- A faster way through waves would be nice (similar to a call-all early). Instead, we can only do one wave at a time, and even then it's towards the very end of that wave. Possibly even a 3-4x speed instead of just 2x. This could be something we unlock as we progress.

That being said, I'd still give this game a 8.5/10. It's a very good game but a few QoL changes could make it insanely good.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've played a lot of tower defense games, you'll especially appreciate this. There's just an extra level of time and effort put into it, even if the graphics seem early 90s.

The low quality means it can do some things outside of normal tower games like 1,000 enemies and multiple effects. It's definitely cousin to one of those physics game like Powder Toy where you bang stuff together and see what happens.


FOR TOWER DEFENSE NERDS

* Doesn't play this stupid "spend as little as possible" game for bonus points. Those rules mean you end up playing against yourself.
* Option for towers that act on floor, wall, and location (e.g. set up a monster to stand over a spike trap with an ice spray hitting intruders). So you can have 3 towers acting on a single space.
* Tower setups can collaborate or they can interfere with each other, which is fun to experiment with.
* Despite the simple graphics can become a LOT more complex than other TD games.

CONS

* No one is going to be surprised by this but it's not a beautiful game.
* Towers can get a bit samey. I mean the spike trap + monster overlap tower combo is just way too effective.
* Enemies could use some additional variation. I basically seem to only ever have problem with the thief and horse types.
* A LOT of multiple destination maps, making bottlenecks impossible. Like the challenge is multitasking rather than optimizing

WISHLIST

* Would love to be able to play entirely via keyboard (right now a mouse is required)
* Ability to zoom out more.
* More speed options, e.g. 3x speed or maybe 2.5.
* View of how many kills an individual tower has acheived.
* Option to disable screen shake.
* Different sound set, as I got tired of the squishy noises and ended up muting audio.
* Ability to upgrade by hovering and pressing a key rather than constantly having to right-click and choose upgrade.

PROS

* Tons of maps, lots of configurations, lots of boosting and lowering of difficulty without feeling like it's nerfed. Good map quality and variation.
* Not just gently pushing down enemy health but actively throwing them off the map. A lot.
* Barriers working 90% of the time except for when a unit that attacks them appears. I really like that mechanic.
* None of that "try to spend as little as possible" nonsense so many other tower defense games lean on.
* There are essentially three games here for every map: the standard run with available towers, the unlimited run, and then coming back when you have more towers unlocked.

UNCLEAR BITS

* I beat the game (I think) and still haven't unlocked all my towers. Not sure what I missed.
* Supposedly allows for custom maps. Haven't tested that.
* Enemy pathing is sometimes unclear. Sometimes you'll put up a tower that you fully expect will mean turning left is faster than turning right but it doesn't quite work that way. Not sure why.
