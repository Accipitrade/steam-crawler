\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo not understand the hype behind this game, me and my lady had a horrible time trying to play this game. It's buggy, frequently crashes. And despite having a really good setup the graphics even on high look like I'm trying to play at 1080p Ultra settings on a gt 210 or something, it's pretty bad. On top of that it has load screen freeze issues, the equipment is all free unity assests and not even good ones. Maps are bare and boring, ghosts are predictable as well. No challenge what so ever. The Menu with the car to find lobbies and etc is pretty confusing as well. Honestly they have a lot and I mean a lot of bugs to be worked out and really the entire game needs work, it's not finished. I cannot recommend anyone buy this game, especially for the price tag of $12. It should be $2.99 or ftp. I guess if you read other reviews you can see what other people liked about the game, but unfortunately I did not find any of them to be formidable in the absolute least bit. I give it a score of 1/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to start out this review by saying that in terms of pure content and passion, this game stands above the rest. It's got so many spirits to deal with. 18 in total to be exact. Each spirit interacts with the environment differently which can actually help with identifying them quicker. Don't forget to look for notes though. Don't want to find out you need the spirits name later and not be prepared. Not only are their interactions and evidences unique to themselves, but how you deal with them when you want to exorcise them is pretty unique to. There are a pretty good number of options such as shotguns with silver slugs, knives soaked in sheep's blood, simple spoken exorcisms, and a good old fashioned gravesite bbq. The selection for maps is far from lacking as well, ranging from tiny apartments, to large churches with scary catacomb-like basements. Visually this game is pretty simple as it falls into the standard unity flip graphics, however there is enough variety in the maps and lighting to keep it looking pretty good. We also get some pretty good customization options which is always appreciated. You have 6 different models with 3 purchasable clothing sets a piece. Your gear can also get alt skins by putting the time in and exorcising spirits enough to expert them. The expert system also teaches you more about what the ghost does that others don't. My biggest gripe for this game is in how buggy it is. Thankfully the dev shows a good willingness to work with the community and address bugs as fast as they can. If you're looking for a good ghost hunting game, I'd say this one is well worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tpretty janky and not polished very well, i run into a few issues at the start but then it was better.
its very confusing going from phasmo to this and learning that creatures actions like opening doors and light switches dont give fingerprints and emf and its more like it just randomly happens if that creature can.

didnt think i would play it 12.5 hours in one day because i didnt sleep.

likes:
lots of completely different and interesting creatures to face that are also visually different.
all the different ways to kill them and how satisfying it is to do so.
fun to become an expert on creature and learn all there quirks.
i am looking forward to getting to level 50 and getting a look at how the class system works.
i really like most of the tools/weapons used especially the video camera and its cool style.
your not asthmatic and can run longer than 3 seconds.

dislikes:
as stated its very janky and even more Indie looking than Phasmophobia was originally.
very basic animations for weapons etc and pretty bad lighting specifically outside and the car.
volume is a bit all over the place on some levels with elevation, if a door opens above it can be deafening.
sometimes antagonising a hunt to learn what its afraid of causes it to hunt inside me and instantly kill me.
baba yaga seems impossible to defeat on Winchester difficulty, you have to get her to teleport you to be able to kill her after but every time i have tried to survive in the forest she instantly kills me and even using holy water which is meant to do something she ignores it and kills me and i dont even get out the forest, by far the hardest to kill.

overall it is a 7/10 and is good but it has so much potential in terms of just fixing the jankyness and getting better textures/models/lighting and animations with some polish could be easily better than Phasmophobia and should lean more into the "supernatural" (tv show) game play of hunting them not just identifying them. and unlike Phasmophobia actually add creatures completely unique from others that i would look forward to face and learn to become an expert against.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am enjoying this game quite a bit.
I love the fact there is a feeling of progression - you have goals to fulfill (identifying and killing a certain number of each creature) and various things to unlock. And it is not like most ghost hunting games where you only need a few hours to unlock everything, in Metaphysical you will gradually be working your way up for tens of hours!
They already have many maps, from smallest to giant, and although they are not extremely detailed, I do not think it is really needed for the concept of this game. There are also many different types of creatures and they each have some uniqueness to them (some are really cool). You also gradually unlock further information on them which can help you more quickly identify them if you pay enough attention. It really feels like a good investigation that stimulates your brain.
What I really appreciate is that you only lose items you are holding when you die. It allows me to be way braver if I don't have to worry about losing heaps of money. That you are able to hold way more items than in most similar games and have infinite sprint are perks that I extremely appreciate. It makes the game different, more relaxed and focused on the investigation and getting rid of the creature than on fighting to stay alive.
Currently the only part I am not that happy with are the controls. There are still some things that need improvement to make the experience of controling the character and using some of the tools smoother. Also I believe there is a need to improve the performance to make the whole game run better. I was forced to really go down on the quality to avoid lagging, more than I usually have to for similar games.
The best thing, though, is that the developers seems to be very enthusiastic about their game and are constantly working on improvements. So I have high hopes for the future of this already pretty good game.\t\t\t\t\t
