\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was surprised to find out the game's been out a year with the amount of bugs. I like the idea, I love the tools, I enjoy the amount of ghosts, but the bugs just outweigh everything currently. I had games where I had only 4 notes and it was split between 2 different people. I've had games where the name of the ghost wasn't even in any of the notes in the house whatsoever. I feel like there's something wrong with the random rolls of the game and may be a conflict there. Items are gone if you don't bring them back to the car and the walking/sprinting speeds are horrendous. Also, there are no scares in the game whatsoever. The ghost does very little events. It's a horror game without the horror, but what exists of the detective aspect of the game is nice. I would say for now it would be a pass for me until there's a heavy focus on bug fixes and more scares (horror games should be scary imo). This game suffers from quantity over quality. The quantity of ghost types and tools are there, but it falls drastically short in quality of scares, polish, and mechanics. It's a pass for me, but a part of me really hopes there is improvement in the bugs and scares mostly cause then I would come back, stream it frequently, and change my rating for sure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have only played single-player so my review is based entirely on solo mode.

This game has a lot of potential and is a lot of fun to play. The maps and graphics are great and the gameplay is decently fleshed out. I love that each ghost type has different models with different methods of elimination. I also really like the progression system. I think there is enough unique gameplay to set this game apart from many other ghost-hunting games.

There were a number of sound issues when I first started playing but most have now been fixed (Thanks Devs!). They also recently fixed sprinting which was my largest complaint at the start. There are still some UI issues that need correcting. Also the door opening/closing physics are wonky. At this time the swinging doors physics are not relevant to the gameplay so making a simpler single-click to fully open or close mechanic would be best.

I definitely think the game is worth playing in it's current state but some UI fixes, and more audio adjustments, will go a long way to improve the experience.\t\t\t\t\t
