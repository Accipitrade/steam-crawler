\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think it's pretty fantastic but for the price I'd love to see some additional features. Granted considering the niche nature of this simulator and the difficulty of the modeling involved, it's still extremely impressive as is and I can personally justify the price as a quant geek and analyst. I'm having a lot of fun with what is already here but it still feels borderline early access and I hope development continues for at least the next 6 months-1 year. Keep in mind that as others have stated, this is much more of a tool or sim than it is a game. You can upload coordinates, wikipedia URLs, CSV/Excel databases, etc to place objects even. But if that is what it's going to be, it would be awesome if they went all out with it even if it means paid DLC or a raised price if their budget demands that.

For starters, please allow for more advanced fallout modeling and just give us the option to enable it if processing power is a concern. Also, a simple distance tool similar to in Command: Modern Operations would allow the scale of devastation to sink in a bit better when viewing the physical effects of warheads, both kilometres and freedom units/nautical miles would be greatly appreciated. A system to simulate fires spreading with the wind would also be extremely cool, perhaps added when the nuclear winter over time feature is added that currently has a placeholder. Aegis sea-based missile defense systems would also be awesome to see, as well as naval fleets and land divisions/brigades as additional available targets. Bonus points if those land and sea assets can destroy installations or infrastructure with conventional means (if they make it there in time), or at least letting them march through the rubble as some Cold War doctrine called for. I envision what is currently here mixed with a very light version of Command: Modern Operations. I am going to play around with the editor since it may already be possible, but dummy MIRVs to fool air defense also seems like a fun addition.

Other wishlist features: a more simple diplomacy/scripting system so countries can respond as they likely would in real life such as unifying NATO or more specific combinations of countries. Warheads from as many nuclear countries as possible, including other WMDs such as chemical, biological, and EMP effects as you already have many of the necessary simulations in place. As much real world weather data added as possible would be very cool as well, though there seems to be a decent amount as is. Parameters so knocking out a command center or headquarters actually delays or disables a retaliatory strike from certain silos/batteries would make for even more interesting scenarios, since I believe I've seen some silos fire without anyone commanding them from within a radioactive hellscape. Perhaps they can be fired on a local level in some cases which is why that happens? If so, a simple delay if their HQ is taken out or a % chance they are delayed as local commanders make up their minds would add a nice touch of realism without needing a ton of additional modeling or dev time.

Mod ideas: as intensive as it would be kudos to the first person to create a combined scenario with every single nuclear site, air base, relevant satellite, sea base/submarine, industrial building, air defense installation, etc in order to create a full worldwide simulation, even more kudos if it covers both modern day (China, India, Pakistan, etc) and Cold War. I'd wager the kind of people buying this often have half decent PC setups to run that. If it is possible within mod files, gamifying this so you slowly research new missiles, warheads, defense systems, radars, etc over time and scramble to deploy them to overwhelm the enemy before stalemate is achieved (think the games ICBM or DEFCON) would be incredible but maybe a little far fetched. Even more so if there is fog of war enabled and you have to use satellites, radars, land force recon, etc to plan strikes, but man would that be cool. I can't wait to see what pops up in mods (hopefully Workshop too), and I am very grateful that Bit Mirror and Slitherine are taking the time to build out such a specific simulation in the first place.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic and truly terrifying "tool" for learning the real effects of nuclear war.

"everybody dies" right? no not at all. If you live inside a target zone you are in deep trouble. But if you don't know what to do when the bombs fall... for most people you might as well be at ground zero. The good news of nuclear war is its very survivable.

This tool might seem crude at first glance but its the most advanced tool to come out for the public since "nukemap" and its far far more advanced. After looking at some nuclear exchanges i quickly put my personal plan of survival into action looking at multiple fallout patterns based on the games built in "countervalue+counterforce" plans (Aka full on nuclear war with most deployed weapons). I picked a small city/town that i would shelter in that is slightly more radiation safe than my current location and set the AI "person" to get into a vehicle and drive full speed to said location during the exchange. He survived the trip with very little radiation exposure and sheltered inside a large brick basement. (however im not sure this game simulates flash blindness he was blindsided by a distant blast)

Anyway. Most people think its the end of the world but it really is anything but that. After about 72 hours sheltering from fallout (assuming you were not running around in it and got dirty) you will almost certainly survive and be able to leave said shelter. In hotter zones you might have to wait a bit longer , but the best plan of action is , after 72 hours of sheltering in place , leave for a cleaner area.

Keep in mind there is no way to REALLY know where the wind is going to blow. However you can use all information available to you to make an educated guess. This game gives you 4 different wind patterns based off the time of the year. So if the place you think is safest is "clear" in all 4 scenarios , it just might be safer going there if you live in a hot zone.

This is a must have for nuclear war nerds , survivalists , and disaster response people.

Fantastic software I hope the devs continue to make it more and more advanced.

(if you want a "game" this is not it , this is more of a tool to observe the effects and actions going on in nuclear conflict. For more of a game experience , check out "defcon" and "icbm" on steam)

EDIT (response to comments)
Seems people in the comments were offended by my "nuclear war is survivable" take. Well too bad. It is. The "global nuclear winter" theory was proven wrong decades ago. TLDR nuclear weapons cant eject soot or frankly anything else high enough in the sky for it to stay there "blocking the sun". Nuclear fallout from nuclear weapons is only directly dangerous for around 72 hours and its danger falls off a cliff after that. After around 2 weeks its barely there anymore and you could start full scale outdoor labor. Commenting on the "food supply" is hilarious. 90 million urban eaters dying would take quite the load off the existing post war food supply in the country. The united states is a gigantic farm with cities in between. Would there be food shortages? sure. Would there be some mystical famine that kills the rest of the population? no. Would there be hundreds of countries that would quickly be able to send aid in such a situation. yes.

Nuclear war is not the end of the world. 60 million people died in ww2 over the course of like 6 years. nuclear war would be the worst thing ever to happen to humanity. But that would only beat out ww2 for the top spot.

The biggest danger of ww3 isnt the death caused in the nuclear exchange. its the next 200 years of other countries fighting over the rest of the planet.\t\t\t\t\t
