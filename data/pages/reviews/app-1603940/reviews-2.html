\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like this simulator. It's not a game, mind you - at least not yet - so don't go in looking for DEFCON-like experience. You can play it as a super in-depth version of DEFCON, though, but without any conventional battles, so it's straight to DEFCON 1 with you. You can imagine any hypothetical scenario in your head and see it play out. Israel launches nukes against Iran, but then a chain reaction causes several dozen countries to burst in nuclear flames in a matter of hours? Sure. What if the Canadians had this 40 Megaton ICBM and used it to destroy Ohio? How would it go if I use my Pershing II mobile launchers to destroy those silos and prevent a retaliatory strike? What if a Global Thermonuclear War broke out and I hid in a basement of my brick house - would I survive? It's all up to you. Yeah, UI is rough and there are some features that are presen but have no effect or aren't accessible yet. Some are quite 'raw', like sattelites. I wish there was an option to move your forces around, more conventional aspect to wars in general (again, just like in DEFCON). I wish there was more humanitarian and long term global impact to show the horrific consequences of even a 'limited nuclear war'.But even at this early stage of development this simulation has a lot to offer. And I can't wait for what comes next.Everybody dies.\t\t\t\t\t

Things are poorly optimized and information is not well presented. Text descriptions are not properly formatted and have typos. A lot of the elements seemed cheaply built like many other low quality shovelware titles put together in Unity.

As others have mentioned, the price is much too high for what it is. I even believe the price is too high even if it did not have any of these issues.

If others are interested in this sort of thing, I highly recommend sitting out for at minimum a year from this review and reassessing the progress that the developer has made at a later date.\t\t\t\t\t
