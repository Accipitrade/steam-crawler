\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery pleasant game !
From the Title menu to the maps, the visuals and the musics / sounds, will put you in a new universe.

As a non-try harder player, I was quite worried about not enjoying this game as the difficulty is everywhere (and that's not a bad thing, I enjoyed being challenged by the tutorial).
But thanks to the campaign (I took my time to read and to think about my actions), I started learning the game and enjoying it straight from the campaign.
I also like the concept of mixing the building/management ressources game with a rogue-like.

The normal games being a bit to difficult for me (still having trouble managing my food and not destroying the map), I'm currently playing custom games with my own parameters (mainly without food :P).
It's really great to be able to do that, even if the game is planned to be difficult, and to discover a chill and contemplative way of playing.

I will, for sure, keep playing for many hours.

Congratulations to the team, I really enjoy playing your game !\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn its current state, this is an early access title. I really wish it would have been released to early access, and the price not quite so high, as I think I would have happily followed it to full release and been happier with the experience.

It's very rough around the edges. Within the first 10 minutes of gameplay I was met with typos (the word onward is spelled wrong in one of the first 10 instruction messages) and events that would not trigger (during chapter 2, I was unable to get either pupil to gather resources, I must have taken "too long" to explore, as when I retried I was met with a message event that allowed me to do so, definitely didn't happen the first try and I was hard locked out of progressing). I think that the game does have some fantastic concepts and that the overall theme is well portrayed, but the UI is bland to the point of often being ugly with large SNES looking text boxes for small amounts of left-aligned text, the right click function to access the movement action has no transitional effect it just kind of pops up, characters and other objects overlap each other, and more like this. There are just a multitude of little things that really detract from the experience. And its a big deal with the price, I think if this game was 10 dollars, I wouldn't be so critical, at 5 dollars, I'd probably have stuck with it, but at 22.49 on SALE, I refunded. I may come back to it later but at this point my expectations are set pretty high, and I'd really like some polish to this experience if I'm going to give it a second shot.

Side note, only bothering to give such criticism because I think it's worth it, I don't bother to review otherwise. Never fun to not say anything nice so I will say one: the music is excellent, this was nailed and really embodies the experience and two: like I said before with the themes, the aesthetic and immersive qualities attributed to this almost spiritual pilgrimage of the characters is done very, very well. The majority of the graphics are great, and suited nicely to the overall experience. But this is why little things like awful looking text boxes are so jarring. Not, in my opinion, good corners to cut, but lots of potential, so I guess we'll see!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't review often, but I've been really enjoying this game, and it seems like it's getting quite a few negative reviews with impressions that don't match what I've been experiencing, so I wanted to offer a different perspective.

At its core, this game strongly rewards careful planning and familiarity with its systems. The tutorial Campaign doesn't cover everything it should - of the criticisms I've seen, this one is valid. You should probably expect to lose your first game, or at least have a rough time, because you're figuring out the stuff it didn't tell you.

However, the other criticisms I've seen a lot of, especially about how easily you can lose to RNG, are a lot less valid, at least once you know what you're doing. I can imagine getting a start with particularly debilitating traits on multiple starting colonists and being unlikely to succeed, but I haven't gotten that bad of a roll there yet, and even if it happens sometimes, it'll probably be obvious immediately and you can restart the run if you want.

As for event RNG, it's pretty easy to deal with - you'll always see minor problems coming at least 6 turns in advance, sooner with research, and most of them can be mitigated by making choices: don't have people working the turn an Overwork event pops, or make sure everyone's working though Boredom, and they won't get sick; if important buildings get damaged, either repair them early enough that the next event won't destroy them or make a plan to move on from the loss if it does. In the mid-to-late game, you can use Knowledge Points at the Council building to negate upcoming events - if you don't do it too often, this will let you keep from folding to the particularly bad ones. This doesn't work on the Major event at the end of each level, but you can usually be gone before then if you want to be.

Finally, there are complaints about not having enough resources on the map to pay the cost to the next stage - yeah, it happens, but again there's counterplay. You can always see the costs for the next 2 legs, so if you see a good stage ahead, but after it there's only 1 path and it's expensive, consider going somewhere else. It's risk vs reward. If a map is low on a needed resource, the Market can let you trade for it (though the rates are horrid, especially when not upgraded).

In my opinion, this game is difficult but ultimately gives the player the tools to deal with the difficulty. If you like building/resource management games where planning well is important, I'd recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as me hurts me to do this, I have to give it a negative review for now.

The game has great asthetics and the mechanics are interesting. I especially love the ability to specialize each pupil for certain jobs. But that's as much praise I can give it for now.

This game has a lot of problems that cumulate to an annoying experience that afterwards made you feel like you wasted your time. A lot of reviews here mention the absurd amount of high impact randomness and they are not wrong, there is a lot of that in this game. However, my biggest issue doesn't lie in the negative traits or bad random events. You can play around these to some extend. Not always, there are some combination of random events are pretty much a game loss but that hasn't happened to me that much. The worst part about all of this is the map generation. Sometimes it presents you with only resource heavy paths of wich barely any of the resource spawnd in the map itself. Maybe there is more to the market but as it stands I was unable to ever compensate that issue with trading let alone you are able to build a market in the first place. The exchange rates are ridiculously high and upgrading it probably costs the resource you need and doesn't improve the exchange rates to be worth it. I've lost several runs I was doing well in because the path forward required me to have 1200 of a resource which wasnt present on the map. You can spend knowledge to see what the future paths cost but you cannot know exactly what is on the next map.

Another thing I want to mention is that the balance is insanely harsh. Your production feels very inefficient compared to what is required from you. It's also the reason why a random event hits so hard. You sometimes cannot afford to play around it and in both reagrds it may lead to a loss easily. The upfront cost of aquiring any resource on top of the consumption makes having anything but the bare minimum (sustaining yourself and the cost for the current path forward) almost impossible in later stages (if possible at all due to map generation). The game feels like it requires you to have so many more resources than it let's you produce especially since it only hands out rewards to you if you have this randomly selected obscure resource.

And while I like the pupils gaining skill in their job, I also don't like how certain actions are locked behind them. This is mainly a problem because it takes so insanely long for you to unlock these actions. For the first 3 maps you have so many dead tiles on top of already empty tiles because you cannot interact with them. And dealing with multiple auras feels almost impossible without a druid but since he is a higher tier profession you generally don't have access to him until late and he is such a burden to your tribe.

All in all, to me, map generation is the most frustrating part of this game. It just feels awful to have a good run and then sit there unable to do anything because only 200 rock spawned but you need 500 to progress. "Welp, guess I die".

I want to keep playing this game but everytime I do and it ends like that, it feels like a punch to the face.\t\t\t\t\t
