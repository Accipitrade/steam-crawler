It needs rebalancing, manual save options, and general bug fixes. The negative RNG effects are far too strong in the standard play mode and there's often no way to recover if your run doesn't go well consistently. The challenge is nice, but it seems like it would not have been too much to ask for difficulty scaling options. As is, it feels like it's forcing you into one play style so that you're forced to rush to victory. I like to take my time and level up my characters so I created a custom game with negative RNG turned off. After a few hours in the playthru, it started to crash no matter what action I took. There is no way to periodically back up the run so I can go back and try to prevent the situation that was causing crashes; you simply need to start a brand new play-thru and abandon your hours of play. There's some replay-ability, but sooner or later I'm going to get bored of having to choose between racing the RNG timebomb and playing on easy mode. I get that the message is that you need to respect mother nature and take only what you need or else you'll fail, but limiting play styles limits the appeal of the game as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAFATE is great. It feels like a love child between FTL and Oxygen Not Included, with an absolutely unique aesthetic. The controls are simple, the resources basic, but the ways to play are numerous. It is up to you to play, and fail, and play again, before you start to grasp what you need to focus on from the start.

The lore really pushes towards a philosophy of balance, but I as surprised at how the game mechanics naturally push you towards balance as you play. Every decision is a compromise between opposites. Some examples:
* some resources now, or more efficient resources later
* cheaper buildings vs versatile expensive buildings
* Fewer villagers (fewer mouths to feed) or more villagers (can get more done)

Every time I play I get absolutely engrossed in the progress of my villagers (called Pupils), trying to determine the most effective tasks to assign them for the long term well being of the caravan. I also get lost in the beautiful art and music, which help to suck me into this enchanting world.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wasn't expecting this game to be balanced. I wasn't even expecting it to be close to balanced. I was expecting to do some light analysis, break the game, feel clever about it, and move on.

But when I start a game with the "hardest" tribe, and it hands me a scenario that is easier than the game I played with the "easiest" tribe, it's hard to even feel clever about breaking the game. (There was some luck involved in getting that result, but still.)
