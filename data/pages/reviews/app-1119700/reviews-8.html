\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot for the faint of heart.

The Tutorials aren't the best and it is possible to fail them easily (took me 4 attempts at Tutorial 4 but I managed 1-3 and 5 on my first go).

I've seen comments on the 2nd tutorial that you run out of stone before you gather the 100 needed to progress. Don't panic! The character that mines the last of the stone will hold it in their hands for a turn or 2 while walking back to the quarry. As long as you leave the character alone, it will take the resources back to base. Tip for the whole game, not just the tutorial: if a character is "Returning Resources" DO NOT give them any instructions. This is said in tutorial 1 but is easily missed. This is especially true for rare resources (like the Rhinofallows).

A FATE is tricky to learn, difficult to master and impossible to predict. For example, more than once I've said to myself, "it will take me exactly 15 turns to get enough fish to progress," only for a vagary (minor negative event) to come along and stop my fisher(s) from working for 10 turns, thus taking 25 turns when I only had 24 available.

9/10, my failures are my own, normally I've gotten too greedy, too impaitent or took my eye off the ball and didn't leave enough time to plan. 3/10 I've failed due to chance. And yes, those numbers are correct.\t\t\t\t\t

There aren't many computer games I feel the urge to tell people about. But this game... this ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ game. It is precision aimed to target me, specifically, as its market. Since picking it up 3/4 days ago, Steam tells me I have sunk almost 24 hours into it already. The first night, I was up until 7.30am the next day. It has sunk its claws into my brain and it will not. let. go.

It is a resource management/action economy game about cute little spirits going on a migration across their little spirit world to evade a flood. Depending on tribe, you'll have slightly different start and play conditions, but you have to feed your little people, they level up in skill efficiency the more they do of a particular role (of which there are like 7 main roles and a number of specialisations off that), and you always need more of everything.

With the 24 hour playtime, I've managed to win just 3 times - on easy maps. Starvation or flooding are constant pressures, with random events sometimes popping up in the last few turns of a go completely skewing your chances - but even then, they don't feel particularly unfair, 'cos there is so much replay with procedurally generated maps, randomised starting traits, and different objectives at each map on how to proceed.

Even the inventory mini-game is enjoyable! You have a finite amount of space to carry resources into the next map, and each of the resources take a different shape for fitting together. You can spend some of your resources to upgrade the amount you can carry, at the opportunity cost of not being able to make your caravan more efficient in other ways, or to save those resources in case of needing them for objectives, or to spend on expensive tribe-wide upgrades... Some of the buildings you need to harvest resources can be carried as well, and they're all irritating, irregular shapes that need to be worked around. But there is an INCREDIBLE sense of satisfaction in getting the inventory screen 100% efficiently packed.

Yeah, so, if all of that sounds like the exact thing that will consume you completely, all I can say is: BE WARNED. It turns out that people need sleep.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very addictive and entertaining rogue-like resource management strategy. It certainly is NOT overwhelmingly difficult like many reviews claim, no more than most other rogue-like games.

You play as a group of cute shape-shifting "pupils" who are travelling towards "the Eye" to escape the seasonal flooding of their world. The branching path takes several stopovers and at each stop you get a small map to explore and exploit, where you are required to gather a certain amount of specific resources required to move on to the next stopover. The problem is, at each stop you only get a finite number of turns before the area floods.

A major challenge is providing sufficient food for your pupils during the stopover, while also being able to gather other resources. There are several sources of food, including hunting, gathering, fishing and farming, but all of them except gathering require a special building, and all food except gathered fruit called "pepkins" require preparation in one of the two production buildings.

Random complications called "vagaries" occur periodically and their impact can be anywhere from completely trivial to utterly devastating, depending on your level of prep. A special building can be built in the game to help you predict vagaries long before they occur, as well as cancel them completely limited number of times.

After you have collected and paid the necessary resources to move on, you have to decide what to take with you, because the space on your caravan is limited. You can take some portable buildings as well as resources, and you have to make sure you carry enough food and basic building materials to be able to comfortably set up in the next stopover.

The game is clever, but unfortunately the limited tutorial "campaign" doesn't teach you all mechanics, or provides confusing information. For example, the tutorial provides you a trained druid and explains how to soothe auras and create potions, but it doesn't explain how to actually train a druid on your own. It also makes it sound like only druids can soothe auras, while in fact any worker can do it.

Tutorial also implies that vagaries are somehow related to your workers exhausting resource hexes, and that you get punished for exhausting. I've seen a few people comment on how frustrating it is to manually try to prevent pupils from exhausting resources (workers pick which hex to harvest automatically based on the closest available), but as far as I can tell from my own game, it is unnecessary because vagaries are completely random and the frequency depends simply on how far into the path you are.

Overall, I'm definitely enjoying the hell out of this game. I've finished West and South tribes on short path so far, moving on to East. If you think a rogue-like game based on resources rather than combat is your cup of tea, I highly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
