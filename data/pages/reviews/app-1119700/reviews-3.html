\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe controls ui and game design for this game is what kills it in my opinion
For example the fact that units are selected after some commands but not others makes giving the wrong command to the wrong unit a common occurrence. The amount of times you need to restart because the game changes who you have selected about 5 seconds after a turn has already started...

There are countless notifications for everything you are already aware of, but nothing for the things you are not. Like bakeries running out of food. There is also no warning for the flood. so if you click one time too many, you lose and have to start all over again, several hours wasted by one click.

There is little to no reward for completing the game or bringing resources to the eye, despite what was hinted at in the campaign. There are no real unlocks, just tribes with different skins, and a few new random traits that don't add much; there is no real progression.

There is no real point levelling people up, or collecting copious amounts or resources, exploring ect.
The games actually quite easy when you realise you can pretty much just ignore all the mechanics and just make food to win. But this takes out what little fun was actually in the game anyway.

Its not fun to play for progression
or achievements
nor is it relaxing
or quick

I feel like this could be a really great game. But the fact the devs seem to have made these intentional features, and they punish players for no good reason.

2/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOriginally, I left a negative review for this game, because the "campaign" made it seem like there is no save option (not for one halt/level, not even for the entire run), nor a "reset turn" function, which is also mentioned in some negative reviews. Upon completing the "campaign", however, I ventured into the "play" section of the game. What I found was that, first, this part of the game does indeed have both a save function, and a reset turn function. Second, the "play" section is, in fact, the campaign of this game. What is designated as "campaign" is nothing more than a dressed-down version of the full game, or rather a tutorial. This is a pretty serious oversight, as most people consider the campaign section of a game to be most representative of the full game. That is to say, most people know that the tutorial is not representative of the full game, and will thus push through the tutorial, into the campaign section, before leaving a review of the game. The fact that this game does not properly distinguish between tutorial and campaign may actually be the cause of a significant portion of negative reviews. It pains me to think that the solution to this would be as easy as changing the text of two of the buttons in the main menu. Because this game is actually pretty great. It feels a lot like Frostpunk on iron man mode.
There were a few other complaints in the negative reviews that I find to be fair, but, in the end, most of those you can control for, once you're aware of them.\t\t\t\t\t
