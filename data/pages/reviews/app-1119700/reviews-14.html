\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm mixed on the review for this game. It definitely has potential to be great. Basically like FTL and Civ combined but with a false relaxing exterior. Going into this, I was expecting the game to be relaxing. One you could jump in to to accomplish the task of getting to the end, while taking time and effort, would still be an enjoyable experiece. But the game is brutal. Some of the brutality is just how the game is set up and a small part of it are some minor details I'll get into detail below. Basically you can feel pretty good about how a game is going only for it all to go down the drain in a turn or two. Definitely a game that makes you wanna rage quit. It's a very aesthetically pleasing game; but I'm not sure it's the type of game that will make me wanna play it again. I enjoy games like FTL that are tough to beat, but it doesnt work for this kind of gameplay, imo.

The small details I mentioned above that make it a bit more difficult is just how I find my mind deals with some aspects of the game.. Each run starts out easy enough. It's basically just resource gathering. You send a 'pupil' out to gather resources. You'll need various variety of resources for various reasons. Because of this, a lot of the game is just waiting for your pupils to collect the resources so you just click to go to the next turn and it starts being automatic for you. (Space is the hotkey for it so it's super easy to skip to the next turn.) So at the start, when it's easy, it's basically just waiting for your pupils to collect enough wood, food, etc so I just tap Space a bunch. However I find myself taking this habit into later gameplay when bad events are more prominent. I end up missing a bunch of notifications for events the turn they happen because 1) I'm in the middle of tapping, and 2) the alert noises/visuals aren't prominent enough to catch my attention. Another annoying part of the game is that it's easy to mix up villagers and accidently select the wrong one to do a task. And sometimes I find a villager not doing any work after I skipped to the next turn a bunch. It'd be nice if the game worked like Civ where if a unit isn't doing anything, you need to give it a command (Even if it's just wait/defend) before going to the next turn. Another issue I found is that there's no warning when your food starts to run low. I usually only notice when I'm out of food (after there's a change in rate of food gathering/consumption) when all my villagers start taking damager per turn. And even if you set all your villagers to switch to gathering food, that can still take a few turns to collect and store so there's a good chance they die.

Basically, the aesthetic/ general gameplay of this game doesn't seem to match how difficult it is. It feels like if Tom Nook killed you if you didn't pay off his loan in enough days.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game expecting it would make me feel the same excitement I felt playing XCOM or Frostpunk - feeling tense because I only had limited time and limited resources to get my animal pals to safety. Or maybe the feeling I got from finally cresting the top of Slay the Spire.

But actually playing the game after finishing the tutorial, I just felt...kind of bored. Not tense, or relaxed, or excited for anything. There were nothing about the buildings or upgrades that made me feel "oh, I've definitely got to get that." The skill wheel mostly works itself, offering modest upgrades but not really changing gameplay overmuch. And the random vagaries, random shrines, random auras, and random villager quirks all felt like they were just in the way - obstacles between me and doing something exciting and meaningful. And this is outside of that one time I spawned in to a small canyon halt into a space completely surrounded by mountains that my caravan could not cross.

I don't want to trash the game - there are some legitimately neat things here to experience. Like the choice between cheap disposable buildings or expensive ones that travel with you, at the cost of storage. Or the idea of trying not just to produce lots of goods, but just the right amount. But there's just none of the fundamental joy here from a city-building game, or a resource-management game, or a rogue-like, as far as I could tell. It looks and sounds very pretty, but that isn't enough to make me want to actually play it again. I do hope the developer(s) keep at it with this and other projects. I feel like they're just on the cusp of something awesome, but not quite there yet.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- Art is beautiful
- Concept/lore is special - this + the solid artwork tells me someone put a lot of love into this.
- mechanics are creative, I enjoy the way your workers (Pupils) can switch jobs get better. Not the most novel thing in the world, but I've always enjoyed that kind of mechanic!

Cons, and the reason I can't recommend this:
- way too hard. The tutorial ("Campaign") does a nice job of teaching you the mechanics. What it does NOT prepare you for is the way everything will spontaneously fall apart. I don't know whether the "Vagaries" (negative events) come too often, or what, but at this point I'm uninstalling and writing this review after the 4th or 5th aborted attempted to complete even a single run. In all cases I never made it farther than halfway, and in all cases, things went from "going great" to "complete collapse" in an eyeblink.
I can't imagine it's intended to be this hard, given the variety of starting Tribes, but after 5 attempts not even getting halfway, and thus I STILL don't know what you unlock and get to build upon with each completion... I'm just done. They need to reduce the difficulty or offer different difficulty levels or something, because as it stands, the sudden failures occurring during what otherwise feels like a good run is a huge bummer and super demotivating.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm torn on recommending this game, but for a lack of neutral recommends, I'll give it a negative one in relation to it's average positive score.

Essentially, it's a cross-over between Civilization, a roguelike and a pawn placement game. Combined with a very simple (but fitting!) art style and a likewise very artistic/spiritual theme to it. Sound does it's job and is nothing to write home about, in either direction.
Controls are about as fine as they can be for a simple mouse-.controlled game, though I did occasionally run into issues in sending the wrong workers to the wrong task, because the game switched focus from one worker to another in between turns. If you spot that mistake before the next turn, it's fine as the game comes with a 'reset to begin of turn' option.

Regarding the gameplay... I'm both intrigued by the detailed mechanics working into each other, and the astounding precision that has gone into balancing the game to be a legitimately difficult logistics puzzle in most of it's levels/halts. I'm just coming off one run where I was exactly one turn from having my entire tribe spiral into doom due to not bringing enough food to set up shop. I specifically had to withdraw two workers from non-critical jobs to basic food gathering, just to squeeze out those 2 extra turns in between starvation until the cooking house was set up. Beautiful!

HOWEVER, those moments are regrettably rare, as the game, once you put some thought into it, comes down to a rather simple formula of picking your trades and then executing a "1 guy gathers raw food, 1 guy processes the food, everyone else gathers/builds" kind of blueprint. It took me a couple hours and A LOT of deaths to get to that point, but now that I'm there, the game appears... too easy? There's nothing left to do once you got the optimized formula, as the random events don't tilt it enough to matter, and with a minimum of foreplanning the randomness of the levels and availability of resources isn't an issue either.

Essentially, the challenge of the game boils down to learning it's detailled, but otherwise simple system, and then not falling for one of the MANY stupidity pitfalls (such as not repairing, not having medicine stored ahead of time, not investing into mobile buildings, spending that barely-sufficient ressource you need to move, overproducing one resource or not spending knowledge to bypass the actual critical events when you come across them). And that's it.

At this point, I'm not sure whether I'll replay the game much more. Sure, there's a couple more traits to unlock, a couple more different (even customizeable) starting setups.. but it's all same-y, and the lack of any real overarching story (beyond the introductionary world building provided by the tutorial) kinda makes me a bit moot about it.

Overall, it's not a bad game. But it's also not a great one, and I don't see how I would put many more hours into it... it does have a low 20€ price tag though, so that is kind of a saving grace, I suppose?

I'll give it a 6/10. A neutral review, if that option would be there, and a negative one to adequately compensate for the (as of this moment) 70% score.\t\t\t\t\t

The gameplay starts easy with requirements, at the mid point, things get challenging and this is where you get the fun out of it. At the near end, all the random disasters become annoying and probably turns the winning condition impossible to win. And it feels more of a chore than fun as it goes on.

Once you completed the game, the unlocks are of more disadvantages than advantages, which is ok to give the rogue-like feel. But how are you going to get creative in winning the game when you are struggling only to survive until the end game? This is where the fun part dies off. Well the only way to play that is custom game but you probably lost motivation after doing 1 or 2 runs which is already very long since the gathering of resources already feels too grindy.

To be fair, the dev did a good job on this game. Love the artwork and sound effects and concepts, but the core of game was not well done. The game has a lot, and I mean A LOT of potential but some on it are wasted. You know it is there but you cannot see it. I am afraid to say I cannot recommend this game, because what hits me bad is that I don't feel the worth of replayability in this game.
