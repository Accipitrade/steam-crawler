\r\n\t\t\t\t\t****STILL NEEDS BALANCING:

Picked up this game after seeing it on the store page and watching a few Twitch streams. Reviews are not wrong - this game is super unique, beautiful and gives me a very Northgard/Frost Punk/Civ sort of management feel.

However, this game event on the first "tribe" is very difficult and is mostly due to random events. I feel the game needs further balancing; perhaps increasing the time before the tide comes on each halt, maybe less overall events, or mixing them with some positive events for the minor ones or having them occur progressively more towards the end? I have learned that rushing the Council mobile building allows you to spend Knowledge points to predict/avoid the minor events but these start right from the first halt.

Another note is the "campaign" which appears to largely be an adorable tutorial is unfunctional - the goal on the map required me to get stone (or iron, cannot recall which one) to move the next Halt, however the tutorial led me to get the opposite and then I was unable to proceed.

REALLY looking forward to this game once some tweeks have been made!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs Far As The Eye is a gorgeous turn-based resource management game where you navigate your caravan and "pupils" (workers) through a series of randomly generated maps, finally arriving at The Eye to complete your journey. Sound simple? I have yet to make it past the second "Halt" (map) due to the brutal difficulty of this game. In order to make it out alive, you have to balance resource gathering and building structures with a never-ending onslaught of random negative events that poison your meeples and destroy your buildings. If you are a fan of steep intellectual challenges of the worker variety, this game is for you! I like it enough to keep playing, but honestly find myself wondering if the developers actually play-tested their own creation before unleashing pain upon the world... My only complaints, aside from getting smacked-down by nature every time I play, are some minor glitches with the tutorial system, odd English typos and grammar problems in the descriptive text and a skill tree system that could use a bit of UX polish. Overall, As Far As They Eye is an enticing, pretty little hex-based indie that will keep you screaming at your monitor for weeks on end.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI too believe that this is a nice game if you enjoy roguelike games.

When I first saw the list of buildable buildings I was worried the game wouldnt have much depth, as there are exactly 15 buildings which can be built.
So .. I was worried I will just figure out how things work and lose interest in the game after a few days, because I found some safe winning strategy.
Luckily it was different!
The overall difficulty is quite high. After the tutorial campaign you get to play 4 tribes with increasing difficulty (you have to start with the lowest difficulty tribe). However, even that tribe took me quite some effort to win with.
There might not be much buildings, but it is quite a tough choice when to build and operate which of them and which ressources to take with you after the mission.
It is highly RNG based, so you gotta like to work with randomness and make the best out of things, as a lot is determined by random traits of your characters, the random map layout, random bad events (Vagaries) and random goals you have to achieve to get to a new waypoint.
But for people who like the challenge of figuring out a way through a random situation (like me), this game proves to be quite an enjoyable experience.
I still think Ill probably lose interest once I played through the fourth tribe.
But considering Im only at the second tribe, I figure it will be rather weeks than days that Ill play this game.

A lot of reviews mention the Vagary system to be broken, but I believe it is perfectly fine. The Vagaries are negative events which randomly happen to you and add some pressure. To be honest: If it weren't for the Vagaries the game would have little challenge and would be fairly bland once you figured out some working strategy. At least they keep you on your toes and force you to adapt, and the more you learn about the game, the more you will also consider possible Vagaries in your choices.
Yes, they can hit hard if you are unprepared.. but thats part of a roguelike. You lose, you start over, you learned something new and make it better next time.
And really, there are numerous ways to counter them. There are game mechanics which allow you to predict them earlier or to manipulate them (slow them down or cancel them out entirely). And in a pinch? Remember that some of your buildings can just be taken out of dangers reach...

Anyways, don't get me wrong, Im not giving a "clear thumbs up" here. Its more of a neutral "yeah, its ok and I like it, go for it for a solid experience."-thing, where I would give a neutral review with a slightly tilted thumb upwards if I could.
6/10, I guess.
Not the game of the year for me ... but by FAR not as bad as some reviews make it to be.
Best decide for yourself if it makes you curious. But bring some patience and tolerance to losing if you do.

Edit:
I have to add to my above review, that shortly after the launch a patch was published as reaction to the reviews which criticized the balance, vagaries and difficulty.
To summarize: They made a lot easier, rebalanced some things to be less punishing and took out some content alltogether. As standart game mode (no option here), so you can't really play the game anymore like it was initialy.
I was thinking about downvoting my rating to 5/10 (and by that putting it to not recommended), but considering I didn't feel motivated to try the game with the new patch yet, I believe that wouldn't be fair.
So just take my above review with a grain of salt, and keep in mind that it was written before that patch. I don't know how much of a challenge it currently is.\t\t\t\t\t

-Refreshing gameplay, a good ressource management roguelike

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tINITIAL RELEASE:
The game has a great concept, but it feels like it's in early access.
It has serious issues in the rng of events and also traits.

The tutorials are good, but it misses the large and important portion on more advanced buildings, upgrades of buildings and the importance of traits (both good and bad).
I tried to complete my first game after the tutorials for the next 12 hours.

It took me the first two hours to understand the importance of the positive and negative traits (I thought it was just a cute extra fluff). The brutality of those negative traits waaaaaaaay out play the positive ones.
First game was a start with 2 pups that refuse to use the caravan to forage, while the 3rd ended up dying because it was clumsy (loses health every turn). Neat...

Next game I was about to go into the next halt and an event with rats stole all my resources to get to the next zone.... Okay...

Every lightning event take out my population camps, not once randomly. EVERY. SINGLE. TIME.
I had all the wood on the map removed at the start of that map in a bad random event. This was the last halt to the Eye, no way to recover, since i need wood to build EVERYTHING. The previous halt had two events to kill off mobile buildings in a row... Great...

I tried restarting until I had ok positive and negative traits, but the events that popped up are never good and having a continuous roll on of horrific events didn't help. I had no way of knowing how to prevent or at least stall them. It was brutal.

I ended up playing a custom version of this game, no negative traits, no random events and won the game really easily. It showed me how important it was to have advanced professions (there is also a trait that stops those) and how to use the other caravan addons (since lightning took away my house buffalo every other game). I didn't know that the knowledge your pups accumulate was useful. As events constantly took it away most of the time or it was for getting to other halts.

TL;DR: It needs to be rebalanced.

SINCE 1st UPDATE:
A lot of the issues I've had have been addressed or being fixed since the initial review.

A lot more of the balance issues with the vulgarities are being fixed, so I'm happy to say my house buffalo are not being imploded with lightning.

The shorter games are good additions.

Still hate the clumsy negative trait.

Happy the devs are going to be adding additional tutorials on upgrades and advanced buildings.

Game isn't quite there yet, but its getting close.

Not recommend as a complete game.
Recommend as early access as there are still a few tweaks needed.\t\t\t\t\t
