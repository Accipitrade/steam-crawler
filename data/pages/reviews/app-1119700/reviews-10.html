\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest target audience of this game is someone who is patient, likes cutesie art and gets attached to characters he himself can rename and shape throughout a journey. Turn-based resource management and task-based progression of your tribe in games that range from half an hour to about an hour and a half are on the menu. You have the option to save your game and resume later but if you're like me you'll forget your setup by then. The tiny animal characters are very cute indeed and the music is relaxingly inoffensive. The game appears to sell a min/max experience, but going for such tactics will likely leave you frustrated. You're best off making most of your cast a diverse jack-of-all-trades, which is what this game probably went for. If that's the type of experience you want you will probably like this game.

Biggest critique of the game is its unpredictability that is easy to miss. The game has mechanics such as sudden events that are supposed to tweak the gameplay but oftentimes feel like a nuisance or straight up unfair "you lose lol". It doesn't happen too seldom that you skip a few turns in a row because all your workers are busy doing something, but in doing so you might stumble on without noticing events and ask yourself "why do I suddenly need wheat to progress?". If you are impatient the game punishes you with moments of frustration, and it does not do a good job of explaining this in the beginning.

Second critique would be the abundance of resource types coupled with unpredictable needs clashing with a game-to-game diverse experience. Resource harvest is done by specialized workers that progressively get more efficient, and oftentimes you will suddenly be asked for a resource you don't have a specialized worker for later on within a margin that seems quite unfair. This circumstance would want you to collect almost everything, but the game actively discourages that by not telling you what you need later and limiting the resources you can take with you from point to point. At the same time the later perks of a dedicated worker are shimmering in front of you. Sometimes the game hints at subtle differences between specializations, but if you play as if you potentially need everything you might very well miss out on these gameplay changes as the lower progression is limited to "more efficiency". It feels discouraging to try out a new "build" as the environment in general rarely seems to allow for you to mix up your game, and although the different resources hint at a distinction from one another these only become apparent later on.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game and I feel like I should put in a positive review for it because I see a lot of negative reviews talking about things that were pretty contrary to my experience with it. I think the game may have been updated or changed because most of the negative reviews focus on how punishing the RNG is and how there's no way to counteract it and your runs will be ruined purely by a negative event. Some of the negative events are pretty harsh but you almost always have advance notice that they're coming, and you can usually plan around them. You can also build a special building that lets you predict them further out or even cancel them a limited number of times. Now, this building is never mentioned in the tutorials so I'm not surprised people don't really notice it, but it's there.

Overall I really enjoy this game, it's a fun balance between relaxing worker management and tense moments of crisis management. Beautiful aesthetic and sounds really add to the experience. I'd say the major shortfall of this game is that some of the mechanics are poorly explained, like the aforementioned council building or how harmony works (look it up on google and you'll find a fairly detailed wiki page). It is definitely a roguelike so be prepared for the procedural generation to give you a pretty nasty situation, and try to plan ahead as much as possible. You can see the next path so you can start saving up resourced for your next path before you reach it! The council can even let you see further down the path.

If you like resource/worker management games and you don't mind losing a few times then I recommend checking out this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t1) There's a lot to like about this game! It's a cute world with nice, but simple backstory. The traveling caravan mechanic works very well. The game has a pretty unique feel and flavor. In the world of so many ripoffs and copycats, this stands out!

2) This really feels to me like a spiritual successor to FTL (Faster Than Light). This means it's HARD! And you're often at the mercy of RNG. It doesn't mean the game is random, it just means if you don't know how to adapt (or how to prepare), you're going to have a bad time. Again and again. Until you get better. (I'm nowhere near as good as I'd like to be yet.)

3) Even though this game is not Early Access, I'd say by its quality that it should be so. If you come in with that expectation, you won't be surprised. (E.g. Buggy UI on Mac OS X basically forces you to play in a windowed mode. The tutorial sometimes gets into weird states.)

But yeah, this game is pretty hard. There was one training mission I have failed a few times. But it's satisfying to go back to it and optimize your build orders + resource priority until you get it right (and with time to spare!).
On the first "real" mission, I failed because I ended up in a canyon with low forest (that quickly got destroyed by fires) and 2 out of 3 available fish sources were "golden" which meant I couldn't use them. (Devs, if you're reading it, I'd make the golden resources still usable as normal.) So I couldn't collect the required amount of fish (and I couldn't try to go to another halt, since that would require constructing things with wood). Anyway, now I know to be careful about these things in the canyon.

I hope as I get better at the game, I'll get more fun out of adapting to various situations + exploring different strategies. (Just like with FTL.)

My top asks from devs right now:
1. Fix UI problems with Mac OS
2. When you travel to the next place and arrange your resources, save that placement even if you decide not to travel. And also reuse item placement when you travel from the next halt onwards.
3. Sometimes a halt simply doesn't have enough resources to do one of the paths. This should be fixed.
4. It's hard to remember all the different halt types (e.g. that canyon doesn't have many forests). It would be nice if when you hover over a halt it showed the amount of resources that will be there (even if it's approximate or a range).
5. The map doesn't always reflect the game state. E.g. during Earthquake, the birds are still around making you think you can hunt them.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very tentative yes.

Yes, dying due to a single mistake is part of a roguelike. But fighting the UI and the controls isn't, and neither are presenting false choices.

The terrain types aren't balanced; there's not really much point in the branching map when there's almost always a clear path to the finish that is dictated purely by food supply. Even with an assload of upgrades there is only so much food you can take with you.

The late game is nonexistent; it's either "did I pick the route that lets me skip halts" or "am I stuck somewhere without food and just lose?"

It's a very unique game but for your own sanity don't play it on the higher difficulties.\t\t\t\t\t

First of all, I do not even love this game. I like it, but thats it. I'm writing this because I am a guy who cruises through the negative reviews on every game I'm interested in before I buy it. So many people were saying this game is impossible and that they've never completed a run after [so many hours]. I really don't know what to say about that... This game is hard as hell, you need to think out everything you're doing, and its unforgiving (these are all qualities that I like). ----but It is not impossible.

I read like 20 reviews by people who could not complete a single run, but I just beat the game on my first try. I beat it on my first try, and honest to God, I'm a complete idiot. I like to get -faced drunk (I'm hammered now, forgive any grammar issues), and play video games. I beat this on like 16 beers. It is not impossible.

If you're interested in worker-management games, and you want to play against a difficult rule-set, this game is pretty solid. If you can't afford to drop $20-30 bucks or whatever, don't go for it. If you're like me, and you like difficult games, and make more than minimum wage, buy it. Its pretty solid; you'll have some fun with it (I only have like 5 -6 hours prolly at the time of writing this, so I don't know about replayability).

Biggest cons? For you 'negative review' guys out there? The art (though I do like it) makes it difficult to understand whats going on without paying close attention.
