\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to love this game so much. The art style is great, the animals are cute, and the use of color is really fun. I mean, look at that skill wheel for abilities, it's great.

The game trips over itself with the UI. There's a lot that's left unexplained, I was frequently mousing over things expecting some sort pop-up to expand on the details because I didn't have nearly enough information to make informed decisions. And if you make uninformed decisions your adorable little critters will starve to death over several agonizing turns where there's little you can do.

Starvation got me in both games I played. The game tells you how much food each worker consumes as a base, but each one can have individual modifiers, and if there's a way to tell what your overall food consumption per turn without manually calculating it or watching the change between turns (and *then* manually calculating it) then I never found out how, and while a low food warning exists, by the time it pops up it's likely too late.

There are technologies that look like they do one thing when you mouse over them (increase volumes per stack or reduce stack sizes in your storage), but only when you click on them do you realize that you must then specify what type of item you want to apply them to. Which is fine, but simply revising the tooltip to "Increases the volume per stack *of a resource of your choosing*" would make it way more transparent.

There's a lot of potential here, but if the game is gonna brutalize me with starvation that's inevitable enough that I can't change it but slow enough that I have to click through several turns to actually die then I expect to meet me halfway and not make me run manual calculations and do google searches to understand what's going on.

I sincerely hope the devs will make a pass through and just flesh out the UI a bit, because I really do believe there's something good trapped behind it.\t\t\t\t\t
