\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game does few things, but it does them so very well.
What drew me in was my love for pulp and Indiana Jones-type stories.
What keeps me coming back is the passive competitive streak this game awakens in me.

I run a few levels with a ghost, get to know it, judges its playstyle and know that it failed.
It's a bitter sweet, one sided camaraderie.
I walk past its eventual corpse and I toast it, for it showed me treasures I might have missed as much as it withstood my chide remarks as I found a treasure unopened.

Up to a point at least.

You want to know how it plays? Sure, I'll tell you. It's a twitch-style FPS with decent precision in the controls. Your aim gets good and your whip knows where to go.

You want to know what will get you? Stress. Keep your head cool, and don't rush ahead.

What this game does good, and so very very good, is the subtle mind game of stress.

Only one player can "Win" any given temple. Implied is the fact that if you don't finish the temple before the probable player who does, you'll be thrown out and miss all your loot.
The guardians will keep you moving forward. But they will make it seem as if you have to run.

It's not a perfect game. It's a good, solid 3/5. You get good runs, you get bad runs. Same as any rogue-like-procedural-RNG-Whatever.
Sometimes you get good blessings, sometimes you don't. Sometimes you get good blessings and get cocky, sometimes you get bad blessings and get careful.

All in all, it's kind of like Ziggurat but without enemies and with a whole lot more challenge. Mostly because you will want to rush it, be fast, be agile.

And that's what'll get you. Every time.
