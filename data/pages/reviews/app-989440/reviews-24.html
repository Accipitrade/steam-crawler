\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou're an explorer. No, not a famous one. Not yet. You run through procedurally generated temples trying not to get impaled, burned, poisoned, and lots of other fun ways to die. You get a cool whip that allows you to Spiderman everywhere. You can get Blessings to allow your whip to go longer distances so you can make up for things you're lacking in real life. My whip is this long (in game):

--|-----------(cool decorations)-------------------(cool decorations)----------------

Or, you can even get Blessings that allow you to hover. Can Indiana Jones do that? Yes? Ok, sure, which movie? It was a deleted scene from the alien one? Oh, I didn't know that. Yeah, that makes sense. Aliens can probably make people float.

RECOMMENDATION

In its current state, I can definitely recommend the game - especially for $24.99. First of all, it's an entirely unique concept to the roguelike scene, and they execute it extremely well. There are a few issues with this game so far that I will write down below so you can decide if it's worth it to you or not. I see a lot of potential in what they can add to this game.

GAMEPLAY
• Select a whip from a variety of whips that have several benefits (more health, more coins collected, etc.). These whips have several tiers and do super cool stuff.
• Run through procedurally generated temples to secure an idol. Secure treasure and keys along the way to gain Blessings and access deeper areas of the temple later on.
• Experience real heart pain from dodging many different types of traps: spikes, axes, other pointy things, angry Gods, the crushing reality that you'll never be good enough to reach the end, holes in the floor, darts, and even lava.
• Use Blessings purchased with treasure to modify your character and whip. This unlocks a wide array of movement abilities for your character, and can make your whip longer and faster.
• Help free other players' phantoms from temples by successfully completing a temple. This gets them their whips back! Really, it does feel nice when I complete a temple because it frees all those poor souls.
• Explore three different areas: green temple, wet cavern with dart shooting statues temple, and super hot fire temple.
• Treasure is placed in really unique areas so it encourages exploration. At the same time, you're being rushed because a Guardian is actively trying to kill you. As Robert Frost would say, the Road Not Taken might be a good choice here. You could get some more $$$.
• Try dying to experience the endless purgatory that is being a phantom. Or, buy your whip back with keys.

ISSUES

Developers have been keen to address issues and have already released multiple patches. To me, this is a good sign.

LAG
• Players, including myself, have experienced significant FPS drops at random times in temples. This could be caused by a number of things, including the number of phantoms, optimization issues, graphical settings. At the moment I haven't seen anything that addressed it.

DISCONNECTS
• I have not experienced any in my time played, but lots of players report this. I believe it's been addressed by devs in several recent patches
• Cool warning sign in game of disconnects.

MOVEMENT ISSUES
• Occasionally I've run into issues in tight spaces where ledge detection is too sensitive. Usually small square holes in the Caverns.
• I've also heard of double-jump failing rarely. It might result in a really annoying death that's totally not your fault.

TEMPLE GENERATION
• Very minor issues where some dart traps will generate next to pillars so darts won't move.
• Another small issue I've seen is where breakable pots generate inside of walls.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like parkour? Do you like tough but fair games that require practice and commitment? That's what this game is. This is a very simple, very well executed idea. This game represents a series of hand crafted "chunks" are thrown together to create unique temple runs. Play the game long enough, you will eventually learn and memorize each of the unique chunks. The game is at the right difficulty level where this doesn't get boring quick, and as you get better at it, you will appreciate the chunks that you do recognize.

I write this review very early into playing it, and the game currently suffers optimization issues and possible memory leaks. The developers of this game have a winning idea and concept. It's on them to realize this game as a success or failure based on feedback and community interaction now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t24/10-2022
Came back to check out the adventure mode. Stage 1 littered with Phantoms in the way, opening chests before you get there, obscuring your vision while trying to get through traps. I don't think this game has ever had a good use for them. Ever. They're annoying and in the way. Get rid of them.
Still a big thumbs down. Game's first iteration is still its best version. They've done nothing to improve that great feeling from back then, only remove from it.

Review update - 29th March 2022.
Complete overhaul of most of the game with the latest update. It came after 3 months of 100% silence. It's not a fun runner anymore. Powers are limited to 1-2 uses per floor now.
Review still stands, but I'm even less inclined to recommend it now. It's not even a fun runner anymore when you have to think if you want to use a double jump or a double whip in the air "power" on a floor. Used to be fun. Not anymore.
Soon a single digit player base. I expect this game to be abandoned within the year. I can't see how the developers can keep this up with zero sales/increase in player base for much longer.
Shame they decided to change everything, make earlier stages far harder(it's not hard for experienced players), make silly rooms(dark rooms which give zero hints as to where to go), and limit your power ups instead of coming up with other ways to limit the player from becoming a parkour master. Like 2 movement blessings maximum but release 4-5 new ones of equal strength to double jump and wings. Like wall running, wall scaling, etc.
And still no multiplayer. This would be 10 times more fun with friends but no. Run with ghosts of randos.

Review update - 11th December 2021.
Not much to add, other than the first part of my review kind of doesn't hold true anymore. The game has now been ramped up so much in difficulty that if you're just looking for a good time running through temples in your spare time, nah, forget that. Even the first temple is going to put you through your paces. Infinite spawning homing minion bombs is now a thing as well, because dealing with them once wasn't enough of a challenge. They spawn again if you ran away from their spawn point. That includes, if they spawn in a hall and you have to run backwards to avoid them. They spawn right back. Deal with that on top of the guardian chasing you ;)
The devs seem to listen to the discord community more than think what the overall community would need. The guys there play the game A LOT and demand challenge. Maybe add more temples and make those harder instead of making even the earliest temple hard? Review still stands. Can't recommend it, not even to casual players anymore.

First time review -

Would you like to run through temples, collecting gold, getting blessings from statues to improve your run, gather whips and relics to fill your collection hall? If yes, I recommend.
If you on the other hand like tight controls, feel like you're in control of your deaths, a game with mechanics that are consistent, performs well under pressure, has a wide variety of whips and relics, then no.

So here's my biggest issue with the game. Its controls.
After +49 hours of this game I still close it down for the most part in anger as I do not feel like my death was my own fault. Cleared the inferno temples for all the red whips already, so it's not just a lack of knowledge around rooms/traps. The anger can come in a couple of different ways.
The whip doesn't latch on to what you were aiming at. When you hold down right click and press left click you're in "latch mode" and will latch onto things. You can't open chests or activate traps. So why is it that when I do that in very critical situations, usually mid-air, I just get a normal whip crack instead of the "grab on to the ledge" whip? I don't know. I don't think the devs know. But it's a run ender in many cases.
Second thing, at this point in time, the game is a proof of concept. It could easily be a mobile game and just be as is but as a PC game, it's very bare bones. You've got a total of 18 whips to collect. That might sound like a lot but the effects on each whip is very dull. Some just +1 health, +2 health, +3 health, very creative. And then there are clearly terrible whips, like "heal by dropping coin in water, but if you get hit at any point in the run, you lose ALL coin". Zero point in running that whip.

I did play this game a lot and that would contribute to the fact that I already find most rooms incredibly easy or boring already. There's little variety even after just 10 runs.

But the game IS still fun and I would highly recommend if it wasn't for the fact that the most important thing you should be able to rely on just decides to not work here and there. And the fact that it has very little content overall.
Only one game mode. This game would be perfect for a speedrun mode but once a temple is cleared it's erased and noone will ever run it again. Why? That destroys any kind of "share the temple code with your friend and see if they can beat it!" competition between friends.
Coop/PvP would also be amazing. Help your buddy along the way, through traps that only 2 players can go through, then backstab him and steal all the loot in the end.

They've got the groundwork laid out. I'll return to the review if they fix the few things I find missing. Mainly whip control, more of everything, and game modes. This could easily turn into a new Fall Guys. Forgotten because the devs decide to focus on cosmetics and barely any new actual fun content. But we'll see :)

EDIT: Adding even more to trash controls of this game. So as you fall from a high place, you get an icon telling you to safety roll. That icon pops up WAY before it's needed to be pressed, tricking you into thinking you need to press it.
If you do press it at the right time, not really hard(but don't pop up with a "you can press this now" warning if you can't), then you roll forward. Unless you hold back, then you don't roll forward. Unless you really need the roll to NOT be in a direction, then you will most definitely roll in that direction.
Ran through the first floor. The temple was massive. Ran with a 1 heart whip. Get to the final room before the blessings. 1200 coin gathered, crazy big floor 1. Jump down from the final chest room, safety roll, holding left, as there's a trap to the right at the bottom of the fall. Where do I end up? To the right. In the middle of the trap. Dead. Thanks game for having such wonderful controls.

EDIT: Just want to put even more emphasis on the fact that the whip controls are souring the otherwise nice temple run experience. When a whip mechanic is as important as it is in this game then it can't be faulty 1/4 of the time. If I could give a thumbs down +1 I would. Why do you keep playing? Because when it does work it's a good fun game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know WHAT the marketing team is doing. This game is absolutely awesome, I'm shocked it hasn't taken over twitch already. Dodge traps in a procedurally generated dungeon, If someone completes the dungeon, that exact dungeon will forever be closed, never to be ran again by another player nor found in that exact format again. If you die, you lose the whip you took with you, and the loot you've gathered. But when someone else completes that dungeon, you'll get it back. Of course you can buy the whip back early with the keys found in chests. You can even share a code with a friend so they can attempt the same dungeon, and try to get the relic that evaded your grasp. (and get you your whip back)

There is no pay to win, there's not even pay to anything except buying the game.
This game is an instant classic.

_______________________________
Note to Developers: Something that would make this game even more popular would be leaderboards, such as fastest completion times, number of relics obtained (a leaderboard for each tier of relic), and amount of souls released. Especially that last one, as that shows you how many players you personally played better against, and coincidentally helped out, giving you a sense of satisfaction.
_______________________________

Two things many of us would like to see as well, would be:

1. To see how many souls were released after obtaining a relic and closing the dungeon, since multiple temples merge into multiple caverns, and multiple caverns merge into a single inferno room, It would be great to see just how many lost souls were freed. Even If It only frees the souls that you met during YOUR run, I'd still enjoy seeing the total number in the end. Having a leaderboard for that would be half of the job done.

2. Having the option to "return the favor" when someone retrieves your whip, giving you a list of their lost loot and a chance to help out someone who helped you.\t\t\t\t\t
