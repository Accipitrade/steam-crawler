\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you were a fan of Mirror's Edge back in the day, and have been looking for something to scratch that first-person platformer itch, Phantom Abyss will do well for you. It's responsive, quick, and above all else, addicting. Not hard on the eyes, either. Basic premise: you are a Phantom, and you are trying to escape what I'm assuming is the Abyss. Each run comprises a series of floors pieced together randomly from a selection of pre-built rooms. A Guardian chases you through every floor past the first and grows progressively more dangerous as you progress. After a few floors, you have a choice between grabbing the relic for the current layer, or going into the next, though you'll need keys to do the latter. It's simple fun and extremely satisfying to feel and see yourself whipping - both literally and figuratively - through floors as you become more familiar with their layouts. There are different whips to unlock as well that each have a pro and a con, which adds some wrinkles to later runs, but be warned: failing a run will cause you to lose access to any but the default whip until a certain amount of time elapses (?), or until one of your Steam friends clears the run you failed (which there is a fairly simple system to facilitate). Good fun all around, especially when seeing other players' Phantoms running alongside yours.\t\t\t\t\t
