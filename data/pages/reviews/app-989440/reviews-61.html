\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty rad concept though the stakes aren't that high. I'm sure there are plans in the future to run tourneys or whatever, but as it currently stands, Phantom Abyss is a fun time as you run through tombs with all kinds of traps and test your dexterity against the ghosts of players that have failed before you. For each run, you get only one shot and if you fail, you leave behind your own ghost so others can see how stupid you were.

The controls are simple, responsive, and solid. You have pretty much your standard WASD controls and a whip that you can use to grapple pretty much any surface by aiming with right-click and whipping away with left-click. You can use this in combination with jumping and sliding around to navigate each trap-filled tomb and try to come out alive by grabbing the relic at the end. Eventually, you'll be able to ignore the lesser relic and dive deeper for an even stronger reward, but I haven't yet mustered up the testicular fortitude to try it out. However, if you do manage to get all the way to the end and grab the legendary relic, the temple is closed forever. You have three hearts available so this isn't a one-hit-and-you're-done affair, so those who are worried about that have nothing to fear.

The tombs themselves are randomly generated and while you should be able to share seed codes if you fail and let your friends have a go at it, at the time of this writing the feature is bugged out and currently unavailable. That said, the generation seems to consist of simply plunking rooms together so sooner or later you'll start recognizing places you've been before. However, this isn't necessarily a bad thing as you can quickly get through the familiar to find the unfamiliar. If you're the more venturous type, there are several nooks and crannies to discover containing chests with money and keys/medallions that you can keep if you survive.

While there is a sense of urgency to each run, I'm not certain it matters. The game claims that only one player can complete a particular tomb and after the relic is obtained, it closes off for all other players. However, once you're in the map, it doesn't seem like you're racing against anyone who may be attempting it at the same time. If you have enough patience, you can get through the tomb and my first relic was nabbed by seeming to be the only patient player. Perhaps I'll try standing still to see if I get booted when someone else completes the map just to be sure, though my understanding is someone has to make it all the way to the end to lock it off entirely rather than just pick up the first relic they come across so I may be waiting a while.

In any case, part of the attraction is seeing the people who have come before you navigate the tomb as you do. They do so in the form of ghosts like in most racing games and while they can provide hints at what to do, I didn't find anything to be too confusing and only died dumb deaths due to trying to rush things. The other part is discovering chests off the beaten path filled with keys and money for buying buffs from statues at the beginning/end of each segment.

That's right, segments. Tombs are broken into multiple parts rather than being a single continuous run, which is fine. I'm sure there's a reason behind it and it doesn't hurt the game. However, the trick is that as you complete each segment, the tomb's resident god (?) wakes up and starts trying to hinder your progress either by directly chipping away at your health or messing up your mobility/vision somehow. The deeper you dive, the angrier and more aggressive the god gets so while it helps to be patient, it rarely helps to stand still.

If you like games like SEUM, Cloudbuilt, and other games of parkour-esque dexterity, you might enjoy Phantom Abyss, although the goal here is simply to make it out alive rather than set record times. It's really fun to navigate the obstacle course and the player ghosts can give you enough drive to keep at it if you have a competitive edge to your personality. Proving you can do what others can't is exhilarating and actually reaching the end of a tomb is satisfying.

The only real downside, as I mentioned above, is that the stakes aren't too high. Sure, you only get one go at a particular tomb, but if you fail, you just generate a new one. Whether you're the first or the twentieth soul to go through it, it doesn't matter so long as you're the singular one that completes it. Sooner or later you'll see rooms that you may have failed before and you'll learn how to get through them so nothing is truly lost in terms of experience. If you manage to pick up any keys (medals, basically) and bring them back, you can spend them on new whips that alter your basic abilities or stats. If you drop them, however, you can (should be able to) provide your tomb code to your friends and they can help you recover your lost stuff including any fancy whip you paid for and brought along. Either way, I'm sure new features are on the way that will raise the stakes and make the game truly thrilling, but in its current state, it's still a fun obstacle course challenge that is challenging to navigate and a blast to control. Unless the devs make some truly awful decisions, the only way to go is up!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(Post- 7/2/21 update)

My computer does seem to be fanning a little less with the recent changes, but, now the whips have stopped working. I brought a cable whip and could no longer pull myself anywhere meaning I had to stop early on in a temple because I couldn't get up onto a platform to get any further >;l

I... would really like this game, but, as it stands now, I can see 2/3 of the guardians becoming impossible to deal with by the second.... "layer". See, you can grab the relics in the green layer with a standard-to-hard difficulty based on the guardian, but I suspect that green relics won't be enough later on, and, after you get your first green relic, the game ushers you- though it doesn't *force* you -to go the the deeper layer for a "more powerful relic".

The deeper layers are insane. Even just the one after the green layer (the blue layer) forces you into making split second reactions and decisions while running through numerous traps and the guardian effect, as well as positioning yourself, or running in a certain direction, while you get sniped at by tracking totems.

There are two other layers past the blue one, and the blue one has the intensity of the above.

To fully explain why this might become impossible:
The three aforementioned guardians are: The Masked Defiler, The Devouring Rage, and an eyeball who I've only gotten twice. Every time you go down a floor, their abilities intensify. This is a problem with the eyeball and Rage, because the eyeball has a tracking laser on you that you need to outrun, and Rage is a persuing face who will go through all walls at you. The eye's fire-rate increases per floor, and Rage's persue speed increases... Except Rage gets hard in the green zone, and probably impossible later.

Defiler doesn't count- he may be a cucklord, but he just shoots out 1 more bomb per floor, all of which frequently miss (though that can be bad if he shoots where you need to go, and where you could go back to, hence cucklord).

The only buffs you get are via whips (which have different tradeoffs) and blessings. Blessings require Gold that you get from chests throughout the runs. It's not preserved between runs, though... and most of them show up when the guardian's active, meaning you're a god if you manage to get over 3 blessings in a single run, and whips... seem to show up at either certain conditions or if you die with one of the non-standard ones equipped. That's right, you can lose those if you die... in a game where you die a lot- but! You can reclaim the whips if you have enough keys, which are hidden in their respective zones.

As it stands, maybe just farm green relics until the game might stop you (it also might not), because anything past that's going to be a nightmare to do, at least as of right now.\t\t\t\t\t
