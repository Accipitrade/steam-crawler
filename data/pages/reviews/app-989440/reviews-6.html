The hook of this game is other player ghosts are seen. There isn't as many people playing these days which is a shame because seeing a mob of like 50 of them is a sight to behold. Their deaths would make reaching the final areas easier to reach (each one gives a potential max health upgrade). It basically encourages people to go deeper in the temple and balances the difficulty in a natural way.

Even as an early access title it's still a lot of fun. And I can't wait to see the release version to come out with the final area.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I'm partly making this review in the hopes the devs will see it since they look at feedback and sometimes address. It will be a proper review though.

This game is a very interesting concept, based mostly on platforming and navigating ancient temples for relics, which are naturally full of traps. Very much in the style of Indiana Jones, down to the character models, signature whip, and of course, your hat.

Overall, exploring the temples are incredibly fun to explore, and when you get into the groove of it, it can be an incredibly engaging experience, looting chests, dodging traps, and trying to speedrun against the other phantoms in the tomb.

That being said, there are a few issues I've encountered in my playtime, some more major than others:
First of all, the controls can sometimes be incredibly frustrating. One of your most crucial tools is the whip grapple, which lets you clear much longer distances. For some reason this requires you to hold the right mouse button then click, or use the toggle to go to grapple mode. The issue is that this means that if you panic and misclick, you'll do a normal whip strike and fall to your death. There's no real reason you can't just have grapple on the right mouse button.

Another issues that comes up now and then is consistency. This mostly happens with spike traps, since there are various different types, and they damage you at different times. Some can be run through without taking damage, others will still damage you until they're about halfway back down. Also, the guardians are incredibly mismatched. While adventuring through the ruins, a guardian will spawn and start to try and kill you. One shoots lasers, one drops gas bombs, and the last one chases you down. Of these, the chasing one is far more frustrating to deal with than the other two. While they usually only spawn in large areas, the devouring rage will always spawn, and since a lot of the maps have side chests, or make you double back, it forces you to just run, and sometimes get stuck since it just floats through the walls.

My last biggest gripe is that you can't see Phantoms in the temple until after you enter. The phantoms are previous players, who may have died there. The issue with not knowing how many there are is that many of the whips will have an impact based on how many people attempted it previously.
The whip that gives you twice as much essence per phantom is useless if there are none, whereas the DIY whip is terrible in a dungeon with 40 of them. Even if the whip doesn't have a direct impact, you may only want a short run to get a T1 relic to bless a whip, find one you haven't gotten yet, or vice versa and you might want to go for the end of the temple. These can both easily be ruined if you enter a temple that's been almost stripped clean, or one where you can't gather heart containers.

Overall however, I definitely recommend this to anyone who likes 3d platforming, especially since it's this fun even in early access.\t\t\t\t\t
