\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has some issues but the core game play is so damn fun it doesn't really matter. The platforming feels fast and tight; just moving around in this game is very enjoyable. If you're a fan of 3d platformers and/or roguelites definitely consider picking this one up.

Nitpicking:
At the time of this review, the game is still early access and it does show signs of this. There's no ending to the story. After about 20-30 hours, temples start feel repetitive. Most of the whips don't feel well thought out and going through the effort to prestige a whip is kind of pointless as it doesn't actually improve (although I did prestige all the whips because it was fun).

I'm very much looking forward to the 1.0 update.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImma be straight up, the amount of reviews on this game + when they were made really doesn't do this game justice in any way, both in a positive and negative light. I've checked a few reviews to sorta get a general sentiment from other people about what they think and here's what I've noticed:

1: People pretty consistently agree that this game has had a scatterbrained development process. Each review tends to only reflect a single update or perhaps changes made from first update they played to the last, so it's not universal among reviews, but the consistent changes with/to whips, relics, modes, and ESPECIALLY mechanics seems to have driven some people off from this game. I wouldn't agree with the sentiment that this game has "failed" early access, but the testing phase really *needs* to result in a very polished gameplay loop that optimizes whatever systems are in place in the game and right now I don't believe they can promise that, which makes sense for early access . . . except for all the flip flopping of mechanics that make me believe the devs don't have a cohesive view of the future of this game. May some higher-ups are being jerks about it or maybe the scatterbrained feel is because of the multiple different stages of early access this game has gone through with early access being way more of a test run than a close version to the finished product, but either way, reviewers (myself included) sorta think the inconsistency is off-putting.

2: Reviews that came off the rip (at the early access release) seem to be way more positive, and also an order of magnitude in size more common, which is why this game has the rating it does. I believe the many problems this game has can almost certainly be salvaged (with a consistent end goal that is) but I understand a ton of the more recent negative reviews being well . . . negative about it. I say that to bring up that the positive *and* negative reviews tend to (at least recently) agree that the game needs to be polished and more consistent between updates but how much that affects the review changes widely from person to person.

3: Now, the good stuff. I've neglected to talk about the actual gameplay feel, but despite inconsistencies across updates that leave a weird taste in everybody's mouth, if the roadmap is to be believed, 1.0 may just answer a lot of those problems that currently exist. Many reviews mention the old whip/curse systems, old modes which were changed or scrapped, or just updates seemingly . . . getting worse (on top of being all over the place like I mentioned above). But, the upside is that the gameplay, despite never seeming "perfect" at any point, still carries a TON of potential here. Playing the game really shows that the game has the fluidity to make a solid gameplay loop. I don't see many people complaining about the controls or even the traps all that often, but when people are . . . it's literally a skill issue. I don't use that phrase to dunk on other people necessarily but this game IS rather hard, so anybody who wanted "FPS TEMPLE RUN 2023" will be sorely disappointed. The runs are punishing and occasionally a bit unfair, but that's just luck. There may be some really hard to win layouts, and you could easily screw yourself over without any way of knowing it, only to be forced to double back and get hit with an angry floating douchebag directly in your way, but these cases are almost always survivable. The upside to the game as it is partially comes down to the fact that it does still feel good whenever you're not in a claustrophobic situation, and even then you just have to figure it out and have patience. Patience is not for everybody, so don't play this if you aren't patient, but the difficulty lends itself to a type of gameplay that needs some adjusting to regardless of if you're patient. So, in general, the gameplay is by consensus good. There are places that could see improvement, but I'm going to prop this game up as an example of sorta bad vision from the devs being saved by the gameplay, because that's how it feels to me. Whipping, sliding, air control, responsiveness are all good, and I think even the negative reviews tent to agree there.

OK, so if that is what the reviews have told me, where exactly do I stand?

Well I'm going to be a bit optimistic and suggest that 1.0 should fix some problems. I can't make promises but that is by far the best chance for the devs to earn more positive reviews. This next update has been a long time coming, so it may end up being make or break for a ton of the people on the fence, myself included. I really, really want to like this game, and for the most part I do, but the inconsistency needs to change. The last handful of updates totally scrambled which aspects of the temple's mechanics felt good/rewarding, and I think if those are set right then this game could end up as an 8/10 or 9/10. I'm not gonna even bother rating it right now, because the thing I care about is seeing what potential the game has and how the next update does or doesn't reach that potential. For now though, I can say I've enjoyed what I've played, banking on the fact that the full game will just have better temple mechanics/game design to go with the fluid movement and really good concept, so a slight thumbs-up from me for now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe actual gameplay of this game is great, and addictively so. The platforming, the traps, the whipping, it's all very fun and challenging. But my biggest question going into this game was: is the ghost feature a useless gimmick? And it kinda is yeah. But that's mostly because the game makes you start in adventure mode. In Adventure mode, there is a few dozen ghosts running around, giving the whole thing a chaotic Fall Guys find of feeling. But they barely matter because you're not racing them or anything. They can activate some stuff but that doesn't really matter. They're mostly noise (and making the ghosts steal coins curse impossible to play with).

The ghost experience is however much better in Classic mode. There are way fewer of them, you can collect extra hp from them when they die and since the temple can only be completed a limited amount of time, it actually feels like you are one of the first few to get the relic instead of one among hundreds.

But on the other hand, collecting high tier relics is much easier in Adventure mode since you don't have to go through all the "acts" like in Classic mode. Adventure mode also has a ranking which is.... just a glorified progression system tbh. The amount of points you lose by dying is so small, you can offset it easily if you don't die right at the beginning of the temple.

So, uhhh, I feel like it would be very easy to just have the good part of both into a single game mode? Pretty much Adventure mode but with the Classic ghost system. But maybe that's just me idk.

The only two features i'd really like to see added to the game is more twists on the guardians because they are boringly repetitive at the moment and actual character customisation so if friends follow your ghost they don't see just a random skin.

Overall 9/10, great gameplay, weird use of the gimmick at times.

EDIT: Ok, going through this again, the thing I missed about Classic Mode is that the more players fail a temple, the easier it gets because of the extra hp you can pick up. It's a super fun dynamic and really makes the ghost gimmick fun and worth being in the game. I like the shorter levels of Adventure Mode but it's overall just less fun.

ALSO, the color highlight on traps is just the guardian's color, it doesn't mean ANYTHING, don't over-think it.\t\t\t\t\t
