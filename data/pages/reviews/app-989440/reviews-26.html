\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame appears to be played on a server, not locally. Do not bother playing/buying if your upload rate is lower than 20 mb/s.

While generally this game has a lot going for it: quick action, solid platforming, high stakes traps, etc. There are several issues. First, is the massive 2-3 second lag between cracking a grapple whip command vs your character actually executing the path. While for most actions this is workable, however, for some such things as ways to manage long decent, what ends up happening is you fall, crack your whip, smack the bottom for damage, then your body goes flying back up to where you cracked it getting a jump 2 tiles above your target point, and then you take fall damage again because it didn't execute when it was sent while you were looking 2 tiles below you 3 seconds ago! The whip lag seems to get shortest when the area has no opponents and has very few player activated obstacles, but gets worse the more other phantoms are in the general map.

Additionally, there are certain commands that require reticle focus which should be proximity based or at least have a much wider reticle activation range, mostly this applies to chests.
Finally, rolling at the bottom of a jump requires hitting the button within about 1 second of actually hitting, this is a problem when there is occasional inputs that don't get recorded. Holding the button so "I want to roll when I hit" isn't a thing in this game which is extremely frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far with my experience of this game, id like to say that overall i would give it a positive review, The movement style is very fluid and reminds me of the parkour from games like Dying Light and Mirrors Edge. The addition of Blessings and the variety of whip blessings and curses change how you would typically play this sort of game. As far as movement goes, this game is fantastic.

The scenery of the game, Architecture, Player models, and more importantly the Guardian models are all fantastically made, the use of colour is well thought out and really helps sell the Aztec theme that you have tried so hard to accomplish. Personally id like to see a variety in level design such as Ancient Egypt themed pyramids with the Egyptian Gods as guardians or a Greek theme with a similar design and Guardians. Maybe in a future update ? who knows as this is still early access.

The rewards for completing dungeons (the Green, Blue, Red and Yellow tokens) can be used to unlock a variety of different whips which make progression easier. My one drawback with this however, is the ability to remove the negative effect on the whips by simply completing a dungeon (of any level) while using it. I feel this makes some whips (like an early game whip, which grants 2 extra hearts at the cost of 30% loss in money) more desirable, as once you have gotten rid of the curse, it just becomes an upgrade. Other then that, the rewards for completing a dungeon are minimal (at least from what i can tell after 5 hours with a low level dungeon taking around 15 minutes to complete, 30 for a medium and 40-45 for a large depending on player speed)

After those 5 hours however i have noticed 3 things that i wish could be changed, one of which is purely cosmetic and doesn't effect the game play what so ever, and the other 2 are massive parts of it.
Firstly Character models. More specifically the inability to chose who you are. The game has a system so that every new level of the dungeon you enter you will become a different character model and so will the phantoms of other players around you.

This creates a barrier between you as the character and you as the player because there is no real connection, you don't feel like you've lost someone important when you die because your character (an anonymous group of individuals with no name) changes so often.

If it was my choice, i would say have a selection of characters similar to that of the whips, for example, a normal Indiana Jones based character who you can use and die with an infinite amount of times. Then introduce characters with different trades, such as they have +1 hearts at the start if the game but they move slower and have a larger hit box or an opposing smaller character who is faster, has a smaller hitbox but has -1 hearts at the start of the game. This is so a death actually feels like a death and not a rinse and repeat sort of deal while also changing how people will approach different challenges.

The second issue is Invincibility frames. In this game upon taking damage from a source that doesn't black screen you (so spikes, hammers, darts, fall damage or anything similar) you get around 2 seconds of invulnerability and then continue as normal. However when you take damage from a source that does Black screen you (pitfalls, boulders, moving roof/walls or anything similar) you are taken to a black screen teleported back to a "safe" zone and are then ready to continue, However you do not gain the same invincibility frames meaning if you are crushed by a boulder, you are black screened and moved, but while you are black screened you can still be damaged by darts, the guardian and various other damage sources despite not being able to see or move which is a totally unfair mechanic as it is unavoidable damage that you will have to carry with you until you succeed or die (especially this is more likely to happen in the red levels of dungeons as there are more trap variety in close proximity)

and finally teleporting Guardians. currently 2 of the 3 Guardians rely on teleportation as a form of movement and sadly this can cause issues where the Guardian will "camp" a thin corridor or a unavoidable jump making progression impossible without waiting for the Guardian to simply leave. I was stuck in a corridor blocked by the Eye of chaos because it wouldn't move and continued to shoot lasers at me for what felt like 5 minutes before i gave in and accepted death. I personally think that by whipping the Guardian with the whip that you are given, should force it to cancel its attack and teleport away, making situations like this more fair to the player.

At the end of the day i understand this is an early access rogue like Temple run of sorts where luck is a big factor, hence the randomly generated rooms. However i think the small mechanics mentioned above go beyond needing luck or skill as they create impossible situations with no chance of being overcome.

overall the game has been a positive experience since purchase and i would recommend my friends to get this game despite it being a "single" player experience. Maybe one day we will see a VS mode where you and a friend are thrown into a dungeon together to see who can live the longest or complete the dungeon first. but who knows.

solid 8/10 game, i cant wait to see where the updates take it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a tremendously fun romp, but there are certainly bugs to be ironed out.

The main issue that I've experienced (Outside of all the additional features that are coming later) is that the AI Pathing on the Guardians will create situations where the player is incapable of winning. The Guardian will frequently spawn or appear in the next door or hallway. Sometimes they pop up in the passageway to chests, in the ceiling, directly above the player shooting down, etc. These issues and others make this a reasonably frustrating adventure. I've experienced a problematic magnetism to certain edges of hitboxes by the whip's targeting. This issue frequently leads to the whip not latching to the edge or surface you're attempting to grapple, causing you most frequently to fall to your death. There are chests that will spawn in walls, areas that have no doors.

As far as the rest of the game goes, the upgrade system is well developed and makes getting your whip's blessed (Thereby eliminating negative attributes) appropriately challenging. I like that they've essentially required a speed run with modifiers using each whip in order to rid yourself of the negative effect.

The puzzles and traps are reasonably diverse and the different areas provide an environment that allows for quick recognition and fluid gameplay. The movement mechanics are well utilized and fun. The timing on the fall damage negation needs adjusting and the optimization needs serious work, but I'd recommend it for what it is. It'll get really fun when we're allowed to bring other players in with us.

I can't stop playing it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTry and bring out your Treasure hunting Instinct, for a first person roguelike this game has all of the core parts down it just needs more expanse to play around in.
Playing through the game you tend to get one of two experiences, either you get no phantoms and you get to set the record and scour more, or you get to run with the phantoms and deal with their interactions.
The first time you beat the game you'll likely do it with the bamboo whip as it lets you heal while near phantoms

This leads to the situation of you getting to run with another persons ghost like they're your best friend in tougher stages-
That is until you find your BFF's body on the 2nd to last floor, crushed, you'll finish the temple for Him you decide. And that I did!

This game is a unique experience! for an early access game I just want to see what the devs make of it, the main things that need added are more level layouts, additional guardians or unique events in the temple to spice up runs. Overall 8/10
The only issues I've found are occasional crashes, and the need for a little overhaul of the later levels.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: I enjoyed playing the game hopefully will again in the future. But right now I would not recommend purchasing the game.
It's just too repetitive, too random, there are too few phantoms, and so on.
Wait and see where the devs take this game before you buy.

You will barely ever see any phantoms at all. Those screenshots could be considered false advertising. At MOST I had 6 people die in a dungeon before I arrived, but even then, all those players went through the dungeon at vastly different speeds than I did. It's a rare sight to ever see one aside from the start of each level.
Honestly, at that point, why have them at all? They should instead record them for each room individually and have them loop as you go through that same room.

Right now, I'd say the game is fun for the first 4-5 hours. Once you reach the blue levels, all the fun gets drained quickly from the game. At least give me the option to skip those green levels, instead of making me go through them over and over again, just so I can even attempt to manage those blue levels.

What about those blessings you can buy? Most of them are useless. A lot of them are movement based, e.g. give you the tiniest of double jumps. You already have the whip though, which is your main source of mobility. I don't need a pitiful double jump when I can just use my whip way more effectively. The only blessings worth getting are those extra hearts or heals, so you can survive the next unfair guardian spawn.\t\t\t\t\t
