You step in to learn more about this mysterious place.

A new adventurer, eh? Have you also heard of the grand treasure that lies beneath these temple grounds? No? Let me explain:
In the olden days, there was a king, ruling prosperous and well. He was promised immortality for a deed to the gods: Sacrificing his own folk. For that reason, the temples, filled with traps, were built.

Once enough souls were sacrificed—captured, a greed among the gods came to light: They were to betray the other gods for their own sole good. The others realized before it was to late, and halted the betrayal, by imprisoning the greed in the temples themselves. There, in the chained statue the greed lies.

The couragous explorers that had protected the other gods became the guardians of the temples. Among your trial you will surely encounter one. To them, we are invaders, so be on guard! Not only guardians will you find, but also phantoms of the deceased. Phantoms of other explorers that tried to claim the relic of immortality, in the deepest depths of the temple, solely equipped with a whip. But every trial comes with a risk: Your life.

A great price to pay, for a chance to retrieve the relic. Though what if I told you: We are already dead. We give our lives without having one; we die yet again though already dead. The repeated torment may feast on your mind, yet there is no escape. There is no place to return to except the temples. We are already captured, and so are you.

You set foot into this temple, you will not leave again. You are now one of us. You will return to this place, from time to time again, as all of us do. The temple may consume you whole, yet we still have more to give. We all want to escape these temples, but there is only one way: To claim the relic of immortality, in the depths far beyond our reach.

Therein also lies my question, to you: Will you walk alongside us, to claim the relic? Will you be strong enough, to outlast us all, yet free us from death? Tell me:
Will It Be You?\t\t\t\t\t
