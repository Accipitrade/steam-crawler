\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhantom Abyss is a frenetic action platformer roguelike with a satisfying gameplay loop and a respectable skill ceiling. It stumbles a bit due to occasional dodgy controls, clipping issues, and trap configurations that interact poorly with each other and heavily tank performance, but these can typically be worked around, and the game entertains well for the current cost of admission.

Other reviews have already touched on the high and low points of the current game (including the occasional unreliability of the controls, which they will likely fix). To add: in addition to being fun to play, it's been highly entertaining to watch amongst my friends. We've been sharing codes with each other to see who can delve furthest into the highest difficulty temples, and success or failure, it's been a joy for couch play and Discord play sessions. The journey and experience are the focus of this game, which plays well for watch parties, but poorly for folks more interested in loot or progression to their experience.

In fact, the current unlockables can be completed fairly quickly, and for very high skill players, the game may currently not be challenging enough (though rest assured, the challenge level is quite high). To address this, it appears an even higher difficulty is already planned. Player cosmetics are another likely feature that does not currently exist; it seems natural that they will do this eventually to supplant the current system, which picks randomly from a set of prefab adventurers on every run. So the promise is there, but what's already here is quite good despite its occasional lack of polish.

One other thing to add: for folks reading about how difficult and nausea-inducing the dodge roll mechanic is in this game, the visual effect can be turned off by disabling Camera Roll in settings, and it's possible to bypass having to time the button press by whipping downwards to reset momentum.

Happy temple spelunking!\t\t\t\t\t

Phantom Abyss is one of the most exciting new roguelikes I have played recently. It pretty much is an Indiana Jones movie in an action packed video games and also adds a ton of super unique mechanics like the asynchronous multiplayer that I will touch on later.

So overall in the game you are trying to make your way through a dangerous temple in order to find sacred relics hidden within.

When you start the game you are placed at the start of the temple and the game tells you how many other players died in the temple you are about to make your way through. And you will immediately notice there are these blue ghosts that are trying to make their way through the temple just like you.

And these ghosts are the previous attempts of actual real world players. Because the super awesome gimmick in this game is that when you die in a temple you can never attempt it again. The game instead lets other players attempt the same exact temple you tried.

And when one player actually completed the temple that temple is sealed and no one else can attempt to beat it since you already gained all the treasure from it.

And so when you are playing the game and you see these blue ghosts you know they were real players that attempted the temple. You can kind of use them to your advantage by allowing them to stay ahead of you and potentially show you where traps are, where chests full of coins and loot are, and more.

And overall its just really awesome to kind of not be alone in the temple. Because obviously you are actually alone in the temple but being able to see all the other players previous attempts in the temple makes the game feel very social.

But lets move on to talking about how the gameplay actually works. Overall the game is heavily platform and parkour based. Most of the challenge in the game comes from dodging traps like spikes, pitfalls, darts, and more. And you start with 3 health which allows you to takes a couple hits before dying. Most traps including falling into a bottomless pit will only take away one health, and there is also fall damage which will deal half a heart most of the time but if you do a roll when you land with perfect timing you won't take any damage.

But the main thing at your disposal to help you deal with all these traps and challenges is your trusty whip. You have a whip that allows you to pull yourself to pretty much anything in the world from ledges to platforms as long as you are in range of it. And this actually adds a lot to the game.

Firstly it allows you to save yourself if you start falling in a pit or are about to get hit by a trap as long as you have a fast enough reaction time. And secondly as you start to get good at the game and get used to the temples you can use it to really speed run through the game.

It really makes you feel like spiderman! Haha just kidding, thats a stupid meme but for real it does make you feel like an indiana jones type explorer going through temples looking for loot and it is sooooo freaking satisfying when you get some awesome plays off by using it.

And when your going through the temples you will find chests that contain gold and sometimes keys for later on. But you want to go out of your way trying to find as much of this as you can. And the game does a good job at hiding some of this loot wether its up in the ceiling hidden away or tucked away in a corner that you have to deal with a ton of extra traps to get to.

But getting as much of the gold as you can is super important because at the end of each floor you will be able to spend your gold to get an offering of one of two different randomly chosen perks. These can be things like giving your whip more range, or giving you a double jump, or allowing you to heal some damage you took. And these are super important to increase your chances of making it further in the temple.

But as soon as you make it past the first floor the guardian of the temple will be after you. And there are multiple different ones but they all will spawn in certain parts of the temple and will try to kill you. One of them slowly just heads straight towards you and tries to kill you by touching you, one will shoot sun beams at you, one will shoot poison orbs at you and more.

And these cause you to have to start making your way through the temple as fast as possible. When these are on your tail you dont have infinite time to explore for coins and chests, though the game will definitely tempt you with them still.

So later in the temple the game starts getting extremely intense because you want to try to go out of your way to get coins to help you get more perks but you also want to just go as fast as possible to not die from the guardians.

And if you make it past several floors of the temple you can claim the relic and win the temple and as mentioned before no one else can play that temple because you won it.

Now there are a ton of extra paths that allow you to go even further, and there's a ton of stuff in this game that I have not even discovered yet but thats definitely a good thing. There definitely seems to be a lot of replayability to do a bunch of stuff I havent even discovered yet.

And another big aspect with the game is the fact that you can buy more different types of whips with some of the keys you get in the temples. They all have pros and cons so one might give you an extra heart container but cause you to take damage if you touch water for too long. And some of them actually interact with the ghosts like there is this one whip that gives you double coins from chests but if a ghost opens up a chest before you then you dont get any of the coins.
And I absolutely love this as I love the gimmick the game has going for it and all the interactions between this stuff is awesome. And if you die in a temple using one of these whips you bought you actually cant use that same whip again until someone beats the temple you lost in it which I love because it just solidifies the gimmick and makes you feel like you really did die in that temple and your ghost is there waiting to be freed by someone else.

And the temples visually look super amazing, the soundtrack is super intense and adds to the game really well.

The game is in early access so they have a ton of plans of things they are going to be adding to the game but overall if it looks like something that is fun you should definitely pick it up right now. It is in a very solid place and I think you can get a ton of content out of the game especially if you are into parkour, platforming type of games.
