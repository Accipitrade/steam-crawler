\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is no roadmap till this date (End August 2021), there are still crucial bugs in the game whether its controls sided, abilities/gameplay related or technical bugs or issues that are present since release.

There is also a lot of missed opportunity here. The basis stands. You have randomized temples in the way that you have rooms that are aligned in random order. The whole system allows for basically infinite combinations, which means they are able to create dungeons for every player forever.
In that sense, only one player can finish a temple, once the temple is finished, its closed and erased.

The missed opportunity is in one case the lack of different traps per level. That means it all plays basically the same, even the patterns become similar.
Since a lot of traps are also unique to specific sections and cant appear on others (like Boulder Traps can only appear in the Inferno Area but not in the first 10 levels (2 Areas).
Another missed opportunity is ofcourse the lack of direct interaction with players.
No coop or multiplayer racing with a friend, no player taking contro lof a Temple God to protect the Relic trying to kill other players.
Its solely seeing ghosts of already dead players.
Ofcourse it takes direct inspiration of Demons Souls here.
But even Demons Souls went further by having, for example, the Monk Boss calling another player from his world to fight the player in the current one as the boss, or allowing communication through notes.

Im a huge sucker for asynchronous multiplayer and in my opinion so many games could benefit of that as it doesnt require stable netcode and connection to guarantee a lagfree experience, is easy to maintain and much easier to implement into games than live multiplayer, while still providing a lot of interactivity.

But here its just not enough, the Phantoms of other player are more an annoyance than providing anything.
The "you can see where they die and learn from it" doesnt apply here anyway.
Anyone stating this either lies or hasnt played the game.
Theres barely any use to that let alone a real benefit to follow another phantom.
That is because they can use different whips, get different perks than you, have different amounts of money available etc.
That means a phantom you see might go a way thats simply not possible for you to take.
Learning where they died also doesnt really help. Traps are quite well visible to you and even if not, the game doesnt show well where and how a player died.
In Demons/Dark Souls for example the phantom of a player repeatedly dies so you can see how it happened, here they just disappear.
Beyond that, they trigger traps. Which is both a problem and possibly a help to you.
Yet i found it to be most enjoyable to star ta temple alone and have no one played it before.
Especially since some Whips have the disadvantage that Phantoms can steal your stuff.

In that sense the game design is super inconsequent and outright hypocritical.
Phantoms can smehow trigger traps, open your chests and even steal your coins and other resources, but they cant open shortcuts for you.


Another missed opportunity is the lack of an editor to allow users to create their own dungeon experiences and mixing stuff together, to then upload them as downloadable levels.

All of that set aside, the main problem next to some really bad design decisions and controls being clunky as hell, is the lack of communication.
The developers somehow decided to go completely silent about development which is an outright stupid idea.
I can understand to not enter every discussion on every feature, but at least be transparent about the process of development, what is planned, what will come soon, what will come later, what the general scope seems to be or whether they have taken note on bugs, issues or whatnot.

People of the community reporting a bug on the forum should at least get a resonse making clear that the bug has been noted.
However you wont even find a roadmap or other info about development anywhere.

Because of this and other issues i cant recommend it right now.
If you die hard playing some "Indiana Jones"-alike game, and i agree theres a certain lack of temple treasure hunting in the gaming industry, then go for it.
Bu ti f you can, just wait until end of the year to see where it has come as right now it only received one major update since release.\t\t\t\t\t

The second you realize the game wants you to spend more time searching for stupid ass chests than you do running for your life is the second this game will die to you.

I really feel misled by the marketing for this game. I was advertised a fast-paced platformer with lots of asynchronous multiplayer. What I got was a very slow-paced platformer with basically no asynchronous multiaplayer at all.

The average number of ghosts I get in my temples is literally 1, with the occassional run having either zero or two ghosts. Right out of the gate, the whole super meat boy style death run is a false advertisement.

The big draw was supposed to be "only one person can beat any given temple, will you be the first"? Which makes it sound like players would be racing against each other to clear this temple.

Wrong. Each temple is only occupied by ONE PLAYER AT A TIME. There's NO PRESSURE to complete it before anyone else, it's impossible NOT to complete it first, unless you die. Which means, you're better off taking your time and analyzing the temples rather than running through them.

Couple this with the fact that there are chests hidden all over which drop coins that buy temporary upgrades, and the result is that all the ghosts you'll be in the temple with are spending all their time checking corners for chests rather than running through the temple.

With players taking their time farming for upgrades and not being pressured to hurry through the course, I wonder if this partly responsible for the completion rates on the temples being way too high, and the fact that people are completing temples frequently is what's making there be so few ghosts in any level. I had a level with zero ghosts, and I completed it. That entire run literally never impacted another player's game. And I wonder if that's the case more than not?

All this turns the game into a deeply empty experience. The pacing slows to crawl, which in turn ruins the gameplay and atmosphere, which was already bad given the literal complete lack of any music whatsoever. I'm literally running through these things thinking the game is muted until I use my whip.

If a developer reads this, this whole hidden chest and upgrade system destroys this game. It should be much harder to complete levels, there needs to be a incentive for going fast, not checking every ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ corner for secrets. Players should feel like they're trying to escape from their death, not hunting for petty upgrades with all the time in the world to do whatever they want. There should be more ghosts per level, faster sessions, and less upgrade bloat that honestly just makes the game worse.

I didn't notice any performance issues while playing. I was playing on epic preset at native 4k, exclusive fullscreen. My issues are not technical in any way, all of my complaints are with the design of the game itself\t\t\t\t\t
