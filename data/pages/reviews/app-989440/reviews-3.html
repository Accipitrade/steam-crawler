\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I picked this up months back, it was a challenging but fun sort of parkour simulator. The whips all had some tangible benefit alongside a near-crippling detriment that you could remove by completing a temple and securing the relic at the end. One such whip is one that gives you more money from chests -- which you need to buy buff and heals mid-run -- but if any of the phantoms (other players that have tried the temple) get the chests before you, you get nothing. With a variety of whips, it was rewarding to clear the "curses" off and get a variety of whip effects.

As of today, clearing a temple only gets you a reskin of your whip. You have the ability to use whips with only a curse, a boon and a curse, or nothing; there is no net benefit to using any whip over the other, in which case...why not just unlock skins that you can apply to your singular whip?

The updates over time seem to make things less and less rewarding. If you like playing games and making progress beyond minor visual differences in a part of your screen that you won't be paying much attention to, then save your money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t For the price and what you get from the game can actually be kind of nice, but as it is and with the history of the developement, the game is just punishing. Not really making you feel like you are getting somewhere.

It has some interesting temple rooms, and some interesting ways to deal with treasure and accomplishments. But in the long run, they keep removing ways to be good enough to get through the temples.

They start you in a mode that basically hand holds you until you repeat so many times that it finally gives you a tough temple. From then on, you might get better, but more likely, you will stall out and just keep rubberbanding around that ranking.

Or you will have unlocked a secondary mode which gives more access but is generally going to be ramped up to max difficulty by the end, and you still won't have the necessary power or skills to get through the levels.

Simply put, it sort of starts out with some challenge and then gets really dull with how little you can feel like you are progressing. Rerunning the same thing regularly hoping to get one different item just to "finish" the game gets old when it's such a hassle to play through.

Again, it isn't the worst, and gives you some good times for the money you pay, but the game is going to drain the fun out of running temples pretty quickly, and leave you just sort of faking it until you finish the game.

That or they will patch it again and guarantee that you no longer have fun with the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was originally a fun parkour adventure game in the theme of Legends of the Hidden Temple, make it to the end of the ruined temple(s) and collect the artifact. Why? Because they're shiny! Along the way you'd get different whips with different powers and debuffs, and if you beat a temple difficult enough you'd remove the debuffs from the whip and get a blessed version.
Now though, blessed whips are quote "never returning" end-quote ("Ask The Abyss", October 27 2022) and the devs seem to be going out of their way to remove player agency and make the game as frustrating as possible. Your 'reward' for clearing a temple is cosmetics (YouGetNothing.gif), and you're honestly better off playing with a whip that has no powers but no debuffs either. For those reasons I have to recommend against this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer: This game is still in early access and as such many features aren't final and things are subject to change!

I have played this game on and off since it first became available and have always thoroughly enjoyed it. While still only being in early access, it *had quite a lot of niche tasks to make runs unique and to improve the whips you use on runs. It's safe to say that the game was going in a really good direction. Although recently with the new adventure update, it seems as though some decisions were made on content to cut and changes to be made that overall seem to have added a "story line" to the game or some lore to follow, although it's left legacy players in the dust.

Having completed the game (collected every idol including infernal), I now find that I practically have to restart the game as the new story features don't recognise my previous progress and force me to redo tutorials, play through base temples and tediously do everything I've already done. While it allows me to explore some of the new temples and traps in the game, it's quite redundant for me to play despite wanting to continue the story as all the idols I collect, I already have! Along with this, the very tedious task of blessing whips prior to the adventure update amounted to nothing now that they've been removed. With me having spent time to bless every whip and for that to now be removed is quite disheartening and there is no indication as to why they were removed. While I do understand things can be removed during early access, it doesn't make sense when the feature was optional and could be toggled. So now all the cursed whips are basically just cosmetic as i'm unsure why anyone would re-attempt infernal temples with whips that don't have any other use than a niche buff.

Overall I think this game has some really great potential and I have enjoyed it a lot in the past, it's mostly the direct that it seems to be going which i'm unsure is one that considers the players. I would wait to buy this game when it's heavily on sale or until it's fully released and you've thoroughly considered it's game play at the time. I don't see any real point in playing this anymore if you played it before the adventure update. Hopefully that changes in the future.
