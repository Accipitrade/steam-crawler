** Originally reviewed at release, updated review after the Adventure Update**

The game is still very fun and I am going to leave this review positive because the mechanics are great. That said, the developers removed whip blessings and I am so angry about it, I have uninstalled the game. I will only revisit this game if they put the blessings back in. I will explain what those are in the "Negative" section below.

Positive-
- Pretty simple parkour mechanics, easy to learn.
- Graphics are cool and the art style is pretty.
- The phantom system is pretty neat. I like watching how other people played the temple.
- If you die in a temple, other phantoms can collect your "spirit" for more health or points, so there is a "benefit" from your death, which is cool.
- Not a complex game. Straight forward and will offer a fun challenge. A good little time sink.
- The devs have updated the game with a decent amount of content since release and continue to do so!
- Adventure mode is like a ranked system and it is really cool.
- Steam achievement added since I last reviewed! Sweet!

Negative-
- They removed the whip blessings and this is the worst change ever. Whips have both a positive effect to help you through missions and negative effects as well. Before the Adventure update you could complete a temple with a whip and it would "bless" the whip and remove the negative effects from it. After the update, all blessings were removed and now the whips have negative effects regardless. This is really not cool because some people worked hard to get these blessings and it feels like it was a step backwards in progression as well. A lame change that I hope they revert. It is one thing to nerf blessings... Like maybe make it take three temples to bless a whip instead of one, but to outright remove it from the game seems wrong because players worked hard for those blessings.
- Steam cards would be nice.
- When you fall sometimes it feels like the timing on the safety roll does not work as intended.
- Some RNG of the temple creation can create impossible areas to complete without taking damage.

Suggestion: Why not add a "hard" toggle where whips will not have blessings on them and if players complete temples they get a little extra rewards or something. Then the regular mode, players can still have their blessings.

I think the game is a good game and if you pick it up you will have a good time. I personally just hope they will give us back the ability to bless whips as it was a fun progression mechanic.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Good game, needs work.

Honestly I was a little torn between a positive and a negative review, I ended up going with a positive because ultimately my grievances with the game can be fixed.

I believe this game has a lot of promise. The idea is great, it feels good to play, for the most part its a good time through and through. Having the different whips to provide new challenges and using them as a motivator to continue to go for lower floors in order to upgrade them was a nice way of adding hours to the average playtime of this game, and for the most part the game runs good (though there are some problems).

Now for some bad stuff :/. Id say the first 5 hours of this game are like magic, it feels so good to run through a temple dodging traps, and guardians. But after that or maybe even sooner the magic quickly fades. By the time you reach the red floors each run is going to last a MINIMUM of 30 minutes. That's if you're running past every chest and blasting past traps and not getting hit. Realistically your average red floor run is going to last 45 min - 1 hour. This means you can spend potentially upwards of an hour on a run just to die by some cheap trap placement or unfortunate guardian spawns (I'm Looking at you Eye Of Agony, And Masked Defiler), and come out the other side with nothing to show for it, and an hour of your life lost to the void. This (at least for me) makes it really difficult to play for very long, not only because of the MASSIVE time investment for just 1 run, but also the amount of frustration that emerges from within me after an hour of work and nothing to show for it is enough to discourage me from continuing. Like I mentioned before when you die you temporarily lose the whip you were using and any keys you may have collected during the run. You can only get the keys back if someone else beats the temple, and you can buy the whip back with keys if you have some to spare, otherwise like the keys the whip will be returned to you upon completion of the temple by another player.

To top it all off YOU CAN GET DUPLICATE RELICS. which i'm okay with especially when you take the whip upgrade system into account, but the problem is you can get duplicates right out the gate...........Like......Your third run. When your talking green dungeon relics its not that bad as an average green dungeon run will last 10-15 minutes. But by the time your at the red dungeon or god forbid the assumed upcoming yellow dungeon, you can spend upwards of an hour just to get a duplicate. In my opinion duplicates should only start happening after you've collected all the relics on that tier, though i suspect there will be many people on the other side of the fence defending it just as much as i'm criticizing it.

Performance is another issue (at times). For the VAST majority of my time with this game it has run butter smooth, though sometimes for no apparent reason my fps will drop from 144 to ~25. I don't think i have to explain why 25 fps in a fast paced parkour game is less than ideal.

I'm not a game dev so i don't know the best way to fix the problems I've mentioned. I could give suggestions but its probably best left to the professionals. It's early access so things can be fixed, and that's probably why despite the giant wall of text explaining the problems of this game i'm still giving it a positive review. I'm certain that with and vocal community and a receptive dev team, this game can become something special.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a game! WOW, a game you can enjoy and rage at the same time.

Recent Overturn update adds a whole game changer! Added to info below and amazing!

Graphics: Some decent graphics with decent quality and it runs super smooth on any system.

Audio: It's a good sound effects with simple whipping, monster chasing dark throwing sound effects.

Controls: Super easy with barely any keys needed and something you can pickup and play easily BUT a while to master.

Game-play and everything else after: The game is a maze runner, but with a unique twist.

It is single player as such you can run around by yourself and the ghosts you can see are the asynchronisation from that run who died, so you can use that ghosts to see where they died and use that as a skill to help you.

Each section will get harder and faster which makes you go and get some skill.

When you progress through, you have an option at specific points to get a relic but then it's closed to you forever, other players can go and get the better relic but you cannot.
If you die, that's it, the maze is closed to you and your phantom will remain to run the maze until it's completed.

Once a maze has been mastered with the best relic, it's closed forever and that one player will be the only one who will ever have it, so it's 1 winner etc per maze and if you die, you cannot ever go back and it moves on!

Each section as well, the coins you find you can use to purchase perks to increase you called blessings which will help you go through the game more until you eventually get to your death or you succeed but 100% worth using them wisely.

Recent update on 23/08/2021 called Overturned added rotating corridors to bigger open deathly drops to smaller jump area's and a lot more things to kill you. This is a good update!

Would you recommend? Yes, 100% it's such a unique take and Hot Lava was awesome but this has me hooked even more!

After a while you get the feeling that lots of the parts currently are the same... Yes, they said it's procedurally generated but I am getting a lot of the samey samey assets, walls, caves, caverns, area's meaning I know each angle to take, look and go which is sad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've seen finished games less polished than this.

Its got its issues; duplicate relics, mega frame rate drops when there are phantoms or in the larger levels, no advanced graphics settings, occasionally unfair traps and balance issues.

However, there is plenty of solid gameplay here, loads to do, looks great when its not chugging and is just generally one of the most entertaining concepts I've seen for a while. Tonnes of fun. Just needs a bit of polish and then it'll be fantastic, but for now its just great.

Ideas for the Dev team:
- 20-Man Race for the Relic game mode. 20 people, works the same as normal but no chests and have the first 10, then 5, then 3 to the finish line and then have 3 racing for the relic on round 4.
- Take away duplicates until all of that tier has been unlocked, for people who want to beat the game its just kind of annoying.
- Have more things to spend money/keys on. The gameplay is fun at its core I just think it needs more elements to avoid it tiring quickly.
- Have whips lose blessings if you lose the whips? Means people will save better ones for deeper runs and so on. Increases stakes too.
- More graphics options for lower end machines, I don't think the game has the graphics to warrant the required stats and my mid tier machine chugs in bigger rooms or with loads of phantoms, hopefully further optimisation will help this anyway.
- Please take away additional movement from rolling, the animation is borderline sickening and landing a big jump just to roll off is irritating as hell.
- Add a speed incentive, not sure what this would entail but jogging through all of the traps is a lot of fun but there's not a lot of reason to do so. Time based treasure rooms at the end, bonus coins for speed, free blessing, whatever, just a reason for pulling crazy stunts.
- Lastly, just a guide would be helpful. A lot of the elements go unexplained and it would just be useful to have something to go to with questions in game. Why do certain whips unlock at certain times? Why are there duplicates and what triggers them? What do the keys do? What are the whip blessings? Being a beginner at this can be confusing so just having a document in the pause menu would help a lot.

That's everything I got, generally, I love this, can't wait to see where it goes!\t\t\t\t\t

I really wanted to like this game.

BUT it becomes repetitive in terms of level variety randomness SO FAST
It only takes about 2-3 runs to see how non-random the room/level layouts are.

The pacing is much slower than I expected;
And there is no reward for completing the run as fast as you can
(unless I'm missing something)
There is also no reward for "almost" finishing a level, which means you can waste a bloody hour for nothing.

Such a "High-Risk High Reward" game-play loop could work if levels were MAX 10-20m long and if guardian spawns were fully balanced and fair, and not spawn in front of your player.
But A FULL HOUR for trash? 0 Progress for almost finishing a level? not even some currency to spend? that kills the game.

See - Checking for hidden chests and slowing down the pace could be rewarding, if that meant you could KEEP SOME kind of reward in case you lose, then, at least, we'd have a reason to risk health points in order to open "dangerous to reach chests", instead of just playing safe...
Why? because most of the in-level currency goes on hearts & things to avoid damage anyways.

Another note - The mantling animation and rolling animation could be much improved, it feels laggy and non satisfying.

ALSO:
PLS, give us an option to disable Anti-Aliasing and all other forms of Post Processing effects that aren't currently exposed for edit in the UI.
While running the game on MAX settings, the input delay is VERY noticeable, and perhaps unacceptable, even with a High-end rig, I cannot stress this enough for a Parkour-Reaction-based game.

FIX:
If anybody stumbles on this review and wants to manually disable the Anti-Aliasing, go to:
C:\\Users\\*YourUser*\\AppData\\Local\\PhantomAbyss\\Saved\\Config\\WindowsNoEditor

Add the text below to "GameUserSettings.ini" -
[SystemSettings] \nr.DefaultFeature.AntiAliasing=0

And the Text below to "Engine.ini" -
[SystemSettings]\nr.DefaultFeature.AntiAliasing=0

And the text below to "Scalability.ini" -
[AntiAliasingQuality@0]\nr.PostProcessAAQuality=0

One of these will do the trick, and your game will run much smoother with less input delay.

I'm leaving the review on Recommend for now, because I have faith in the developers.
I'd love to see this game becoming better with time.

It has the potential and charm, and I believe that with a few changes that revolve reward, pace, and variety, the issue might be solved.\t\t\t\t\t
