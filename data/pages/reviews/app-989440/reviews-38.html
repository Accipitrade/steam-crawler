\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhantom Abyss is a game that's go, go, and go again. I keep wanting to throw it on just for another few runs, the gameplay is so smooth and well-designed that Phantom Abyss is enjoyable whether you succeed or not. The feeling of running through traps and narrowly avoiding otherwise certain death is something that only this game has offered me in the way that it does. It's incredibly enjoyable minute by minute.

Within the Temples, rooms do often repeat themselves, though traps are almost always different in layout and types. This makes subsequent runs faster (as you get used to a certain room layout), though you still have to have a little caution for the traps. Some of the most fun experiences I've had in Phantom Abyss so far have been from runs that ended in death. Though some runs where I have actually claimed a Relic within the temple have also been incredibly thrilling.

So, if you like the look of Phantom Abyss, I'd definitely recommend it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really love what this game is trying to do. It is a fun rogue-lite game for people who like speedrunning and parkour in games. I've seen other people say that the game doesn't really force you to go fast, but I have to disagree. You can choose to end the temple early after floor 3 and floor 6, but if you don't choose this, the game will continue to the final floor at floor 9. All this time, the temple's guardian is getting more powerful. This is what, in my opinion, keeps you moving fast. They begin firing more frequently, chasing you with more speed, or cutting off more of your routes. The only issue with guardians is that the developers need to fix their sometimes unfair placement and add more of them to the game. The guardians can basically spawn right in front of you and end your run due to no fault of your own and just RNG and that feels really bad.

The progression system is pretty simple. Every time you go back to the hub, there is a new whip for purchase. The whip will have a positive effect and a negative effect. If you complete a temple of equal level of rarity of the whip, it becomes blessed and the negative effect is removed. This helps you complete more temples to get more keys to unlock more whips. Repeat! The only problem is that, in my opinion, this isn't explained well in the game and a little more explanation could be helpful. I know I'm not the only one that thought that extra whips were obtained by completing temples.

In the temples, your progression is in the form of blessings. By going out of your way to open chests, you'll get coins during each run that you can spend at the end of each floor to unlock abilities that will help you on your way. As other comments have pointed out, there are a few blessings that are just ridiculous to not choose. Double jump and anything that makes your whip more easy to use are just a must. Elevation beats 90% of the traps in the game. More blessings would really help, but I think that the blessings combined with the unlockable whips leads to some fun combinations. For example, I had a whip that made me immune to fall damage and got a blessing that gave fall damage a chance to heal me. The effect: I could jump off of cliffs and never take damage and actually heal myself. It gave me a somewhat ridiculous way to keep my hearts up! I just wish more blessings were available to get these kinds of combinations going.

For the phantoms... honestly, there is almost no impact. Some of the whips will give you effects based on other players phantoms such as a whip that heals you when you are near them or another whip that takes away coins when you are near them. But for the most part... I felt that this mechanic was basically just a gimmick with no real impact. If anything, having other phantoms open up chests before me was frustrating as I'd have to wait for the coins to finish coming out of a chest rather than opening it myself and continuing to run.

I also think that the impact of completing a temple needs some help. As it stands, the whole "Only one person in the world will complete this temple!" is really cool, but... nobody knows you completed it except the 10 people whose loot you free and they are more likely thinking "oh neat, I get my loot bacK" and then closing the popup. I hope that a sort of temple map gets added later on that shows completed temples and how many people died trying to complete it. Some sort of way to commemorate your completion of a temple other than the relics in your hub (which give no indication of the temple you completed) would be neat and capitalize on the feeling of claiming a temple as your own.

Finally, the temple generation. Personally, I both love and hate the "random" generation. I like that you can enter a room and have an idea of the layout, especially when the devouring rage is chasing you at 5 m/s lol. But, it also demystifies the game after about 5 hours of play time. You pretty much will see every room layout in that time. I think this would be okay if there were more traps in the game. I think the later levels handle this better where even if you know the room layouts, almost every square inch is trapped and you really have to watch your steps. I'd love if the whole temple was more like this!

Overall, I'd say the game is worth it if you love games that focus on fluid movement and give you some adrenaline rushes when you are just about to complete a room and you hear the guardian right behind you. It's an amazingly fun concept but definitely needs some more time to fully develop. As it stands, I'm happy with my purchase and will play it all the time, but I think the average buyer could be easily disappointed with what's in the game as it is. If the trailers look like something you'd like and the reviews all saying that the temples are "samey" don't scare you away, you are going to have a really fun time.\t\t\t\t\t
