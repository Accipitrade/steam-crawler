\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've spent a lot of time on this game and it is an awful lot of fun to play. You might die a lot and especially later in the temple on a long run, but I personally do not mind dying later in the game as I have a lot of fun playing it.

Running through various rooms and traps while being chased or attacked by a Guardian. No temple may be exactly the same and only one person in the world can complete each temple, but you do see a lot of the same rooms and traps which can get a bit repetitive. Do bear in mind that this is in Early Access and I am sure that over the next year they will add in a lot of variations to rooms and traps plus bringing in more features and zone, but it is still currently an awful lot of fun.

Performance issues with the game can be a bit much for the game at times such as occasional low FPS drops and putting on more resources onto the CPU, particularly with more phantoms and later in the level. But again remember this is Early Access and I am sure that over time development and optimisation will be worked on to smooth out performance for all computers.

In short, I think this is an excellent game and well worth it if you like this type of game. I am greatly looking forward to what the future of this game has for us and I will be there every step of the way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA roguelite 3d platformer: what more is there to love! I've collected all the whips and I'm currently going through getting every whip blessed. T1's where easy enough, T2's are kinda annoying and T3's are quite the challenge. Playing the game is a lot of fun, but there are quite many annoying things about the game.

One of the most annoying things is the randomness of relics, and collecting every relic is extremely annoying since it doesn't seem that you can do anything that would help with the chances of getting the relics that you are missing.

Another annoyance is the small amount of different rooms that can generate, which would be a smaller problem if some of the rooms weren't so massive that it just feels like going through the same thing over and over when going for the latter tiers of relics. That's why you really can't play this game for longer sessions, as you can get quite impatient on the upper floors and you begin to take unnecessary risks, making it almost impossible to get through the last floors without dying.

The blessings require quite a bit of work as well. The luck based blessings are something that should be either removed entirely or buffed. There is no reason ever to take blessing that has a chance to heal you instead of taking fall damage, as you never want to take fall damage, and the universal chance to dodge a hit has so low chances that you'll be lucky to have the effect come up once before dying.

All in all I'd say that I've had a lot of fun playing this game. However, if the genre doesn't make you want to instantly go and play it, you might want to wait for a while for the devs to make some adjustments to the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNormally, I'm not into Early Access, but Phantom Abyss checked too many of my boxes for me to put off, and I'm glad I got it. It's a little barebones and I've already completed all three levels included (though I'll be back for more) after four hours of play, but what's here is really great stuff. If you love first-person exploration, fast-paced platforming/puzzle solving, and racing in semi-randomized environments, you'll get a kick out of this.

You play as a phantom who can run, jump, and use a whip to move yourself around the semi-randomly generated environments. While it doesn't offer parkour platforming on the level of Mirror's Edge or Dying Light, your whip lets you be creative in how you move around. The levels are template-based so they aren't totally random, but considering how deadly the traps can be, this is a good thing. There are a wide variety of traps, and all of them are pretty easy to read while moving.

The big twist the game throws at you is one of three Temple Guardians who chase after you and attack you in some way. The challenge amps up as you get through each level. This is what makes this game so interesting and challenging. Now, you not only have to navigate a new and unique environment, you have to do it quickly and decisively. It's sort of an action-platformer roguelike with some puzzle and horror elements thrown in. It's a really unique and intense mix that is a ton of fun.\t\t\t\t\t
