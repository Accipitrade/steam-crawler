\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnything that Devolver Digital publishes I have a high opinion of. That doesn't change with this game but it doesn't exactly pass that with flying colours. It's not brilliant, but it's not bad either and I do think it has the potential to get to be brilliant with subsequent updates.

The game has a hell of a lot of promise but it does fall short and is inconsistent in a number of areas throughout. This ranges from imbalance of the guardian spirits to various whip mechanics.

First and foremost (And the thing I'm most willing to forgive for now) is disconnects/crashing back to the main menu. This has happened a few times and it's more than a bit annoying, especially when you lose whatever whip you were doing the run with when you haven't died or done anything to warrant it. I can't imagine that it'd be a hard problem to fix to prevent whip loss when it is the games fault rather than the players fault. It also begs the very important question of what happens to this game if it doesn't sell well? Or what happens on the inevitable day the developers eventually pull the plug on the server? Will this still be playable in some format?

I've found that damage can be SUPER inconsistent in this game also. There can be times when spikes will pop up, I wait for them to be all the way up, and then I can walk into them without issue. Other times where it'll hit me once (Sometimes even more than that).
The difficulty difference between the spirits that guard the temples is fairly massive as well. I think that the Masked Guardian and the Eye are fine as is but the Devouring Rage is SIGNIFICANTLY more difficult to deal with. Especially how you do the final relic room on whatever floor, it doesn't stop between lighting the burners, like with the others. There is no way to slow it down or force it back either, which is a fairly big problem when most of the rooms are tight narrow corridors (This gets amplified in later stages).
There's also a lot of things the game doesn't mention. If it wasn't for watching other players ghosts doing things, I wouldn't have known about a lot of the in game tricks and tips that I do know. It'd also be nice if the money you collect during a run could have at least a little effect once the run is done. Like at present, it doesn't matter if you finish a game with 0 currency, or 500.
Finally, I think that the whip cooldown rate should be significantly lowered by default. In a game that actively encourages you to go fast via. the guardians, it's annoying to have to waste precious time standing around because of an arbitrary whip cooldown, and even those it's small, it does breakup the flow of fast movement severely.

Over-all the game is great as a whole. I have enjoyed my time with it, I think I got my moneys worth but I think it has the potential to be SO much greater!

Edit: The servers are currently down for maintenance so I full on cant play my game anymore at all. There should be a way of playing this even if offline. DRM Doesn't belong in the indie scene like this. Amending my review to Not Recommended until this is fixed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm kinda iffy on rogue likes, especially action ones.

But this, this is something special.

PREFACE

I'm not exactly sure how to put it. It's not childhood nostalgia (Legends of the Hidden Temple), it's not Temple Run x Mirrors Edge (both great games that have consumed a ton of my time). This is unique. It's punishing, really freaking punishing. The fact that there is no time limit (unless you get the Devouring god), makes this game a masterpiece.

PROS
This game manages to make you feel like you have to run. It's mechanics are all in favor of doing it faster. But there is no time limit. So what is all of this speed and parkour movement for? For making things difficult for others. THAT is a truly bizarre and unique concept that only Dark Souls has managed to achieve. Ghosts interact with the enviroment. Someone running super fast will trigger traps that you haven't even seen yet. Why is this a pro? Because it gives secondary purpose, alternate reasons for your actions. Do I make this easy? Do I make it harder? Split second decisions invested in your survival, but others failures (if you're a pessimist). This game, being a runner, gives you more engagement than any other runner I can think of. Your actions have consequences, not just for you, but others.

The game play is super smooth. Very well done. Everything reacts as it should. I would say the whip natural cooldown should be shorter, but I'm certain that's just a whip I haven't unlocked yet (and who knows at what price).

The design is solid. Simple solutions to simple problems. If you find an area too hard, your whip will save you tenfold. I cannot stress how many times I've died when I could've whipped a surface and lived. It's about 99% of my deaths, and they are all preventable. This game is very well balanced.

The ghosts. On the other side of the spectrum, the ghosts are AMAZING. For one, if you follow one, you know they are gonna die, and that is satisfying in it's own right? These people couldn't cut it? It's time for me to SHINE! Such an amazing motivator and adds truly unique replayability.

MEHS

Music. Visual style. Whip perks and faults. None of these things really convey what they are supposed to do. They should probably be in cons, but they are not intrusive, so they will remain as "Meh".

The music ramps up at very strange times, like when you're waiting for traps to disarm or just staring at a wall. It's really discombobulating and makes you think the god has spawned behind you, when in actuality you just stood next to a trap. It's also extremely dull. After playing it for two hours straight, I can't remember a single melody.

Visual style is forgivable. It's just meh. Honestly, it would improve even from a different color pallet. But for now it all looks the same. Every last bit.

Whips perks and faults are extremely contradicting for play styles. Plus one hard but you take damage from water.... So, it balances. You basically work for whips that don't change anything. There should be more reward, with less faults. Because at the moment, my keys are useless because nothing beats the balance of a normal whip, regardless of play style.

CONS

Aside from the occasional bug. Nothing. My Meh's were a bit more brutal, and could be considered as cons, but this is and EA title, and the devs at least seem like they are paying attention. So I can't really list anything here.

I take that back. I'm not sure if it's a difficulty thing but christ almighty give me more gods than the poison ball bastard, he's 90% of the game, the other 10% being red ghost from pacman. Just seriously, add more, or rotate more, or do anything to not give me these same MFs every ttime.

CONCLUSION

This overall is a masterpiece in the making. They've taken action rougelikes, and combined it with a runner, and it actually works. A runner game that has appeal to the not-runner crowd. I hate runners, with a passion. But this is just down right fun. Bliss from beginning to the (I'll never ever see) end. But I like that, because despite the repetitive temples, it's just a blast to run around and find all the secrets. Saving yourself last minute by whipping at the perfect moment. And when you die the same way the ghost right in front of you did. Nothing discourages me from hitting the start button. Because despite how bad I am at the game, I know I'll have a good time.

HUGE CONS!!! - You can hook a ledge and save yourself from fall damage, but the game will not recognize it as saved. You will get teleported to the start of the level with damage and coins missing\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely a fun game, however even with a fairly mid-end GPU I experienced quite a bit of stutter in the Inferno (And the main hub)

A lot of googling and people say the files fall in %appdata% so it took me a bit to find it for Linux.
If you're using Proton and Steam Play, the location for editing GameUserSettings.ini and the various others mentioned in other reviews can be found in something akin to:

/steamapps/compatdata/989440/pfx/drive_c/users/steamuser/Local Settings/Application Data/PhantomAbyss/Saved/Config/WindowsNoEditor/

For those who want my quick edit that fixed my issues for the most part, in GameUserSettings.ini near the bottom, I used the following. I'm not someone who needs "Epic" level graphics, and for those who just want to play and enjoy with a decent boost from in-game UI changes:

[ScalabilityGroups]\nsg.ResolutionQuality=100.000000\nsg.ViewDistanceQuality=1\nsg.AntiAliasingQuality=0\nsg.ShadowQuality=0\nsg.PostProcessQuality=0\nsg.TextureQuality=1\nsg.EffectsQuality=0\nsg.FoliageQuality=0\nsg.ShadingQuality=0

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWould i recommend the game?


Yes but be cautious. I'm having lot's of fun already and if you like dungeoncrawlers, roguelikes or (ik ik, i can't believe i'm making this comparison myself) the unforgiving nature of any.. let's say... soulslike, you definitely have to atleast try it. But get ready to tilt due to some connection bugs.


That being said i'm now going to list some negative and positive things about Phantom Abyss.



Starting with the negative:


- The FPS can become quite choppy at times. First i thought that my PC is having troubles but after further digging through the internet i found out that a lot of, if not all have this problem.

- There is a bug where you can get randomly disconnected (even tho you never actually lost internet connection) from the game which ends your run and is equal to a death. That means you lose everything you worked for which get's quite scary if you decide to go for a longer run. However, the dev's are aware of that issue and are currently trying to solve it.
(Right after writing this Review i was disconnected from the game right when the "Relic claimed" animation. It still counted as a death and i didnt even get the Relic.)


Now to some positive things:


- There are a lot of moments already where i do feel like Indiana Jones on crack, speed, meth, cocaine and mountain dew amp game fuel. And that, to be quite honest, is an incredible feeling. At the same time i never have the feeling as if a run is going to be way too easy.

- Aside from the already mentioned bugs and FPS issues i havent run into any other problems. Because of that and the fairness of the traps and bosses (for the most part) every death feels justified and earned (unless it's due to a disconnect).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly feels very good so far. Not 100% polished but where there's room for improvement, there's also a ton of room for growth. This could easily be the next big streamer title.

My only real problem so far is that the grappling indicator is not entirely consistent. For those who haven't played or watched it played yet, to grapple (on M+K) you hold right-click, then aim at a surface within range of the whip. If in range, a small green symbol pops up to let you know. But sometimes pressing right-click does nothing. Yes there is a very short cooldown after grappling before you can do it again, and you can't do it again until you land anyway, but sometimes the "grappling mode" just doesn't activate. You have to let go and keep pressing over and over again until it comes up, and sometimes (especially when being chased) you can't even spare an extra second waiting for it to function properly.

It's a big problem, and yet it's relatively manageable. Needs fixed ASAP, but it doesn't hinder the game enough to make it unplayable.\t\t\t\t\t
