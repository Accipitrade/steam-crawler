\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a really novel concept, it's a roguelike parkour infinite dungeon simulator. It's kind of like Cyber Hook meets Dark Souls phantoms. It's cool how you can share the seed of a dungeon you ran to see if perhaps your friends have better luck and I'm sure that down the road the game will only get better.

As it exists now though, the procedural generation needs work, even after only about an hour in the game I was already seeing repeating patterns pretty consistently. Also, speeding through a run is near pointless because you're not actively competing against others so the trailer showing all these wild slides and sick parkour moves is cool and all, but you can be as slow and calculated as you like to make it through. I'm sure time trials / speedrun elements will be added in later too.

As it stands right now though, this is not a $25 indie title. I will definitely check back on this one but for now it's refund time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHighly recommended for anyone who's a fan of first person platformers. The lack of a timer/speedrunning component is unorthodox but definitely a welcome change for people who like this sort of challenge without the pressure of speedrunning.

While the procedural generation can start to feel somewhat repetitive after a few hours of play, I don't really think it detracts from the fun. The more you know, the more you can flow, ya know?

Generally I see this as a great start into Early Access for the current price. I think its a killer concept that is well executed thus far. I hope to see a TON of content added in the form of more room variations, more trap types, more "subtle" variations (for example the opening room with a broken bridge and two chests, one on left and right, why not procedurally give it traps now and again or randomize the gap size) as well as more polishing up the guardians as they feel a bit rough at the moment. More boons, more whips, etc etc.

Also a lot of the UI is pretty rough looking or just plain confusing, but I chalk that up to EA.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall a competent parkour spelunky, but missing some content. The game needs a timer to force you to move through the dungeons, or to fill in the dungeons with more exploration-focused gameplay. Right now you can wander through largely empty spaces and collect a few extra coins, but the perks aren't always useful, so that time ends up being wasted. The procedural generation needs some more variety as well, given how often you're seeing the first biome and how much time you spend there. The gimmick of seeing other players' ghost also just does not add anything to the game. It wants to be Dark Souls, but Dark Souls is about slow, methodical exploration and surprise traps. This game is about speed, and so you get a clear warning for all the traps. It's a bit of a mismatch, and you feel that disconnect all throughout. I'm reviewing this in Early Access, though, so there's plenty of time to add and refine, but right now the game just isn't going to stick very well.\t\t\t\t\t
