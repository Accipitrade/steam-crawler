\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR Summary at the bottom.
So I want to preface this by saying I do quite enjoy this game. The fast-paced platforming action can be fun and rewarding when the random generation works well.

My first gripe with this game is the random generation. I just find that far too many times the random generation is building areas that are nearly impossible to avoid damage in, especially when combined with the guardians. I often find that the devouring rage (a guardian that slowly follows you through walls, requiring you to keep moving forward) often doesn't mesh well with the random generation. So many times I have been stood in a corridor of spike traps that require me to keep stopping and starting.
A specific example of when the traps are almost impossible to avoid is spike traps at the top of some stairs. You can't see them and when you're running through the temple you *will* take damage from these traps. I think that the random generation needs some tweaking and some traps and guardians balanced a bit more.
While on the topic of guardians, two of the guardians randomly teleport throughout a room while they attack you and I feel like this needs changing a little so that guardians can't block the path forward and also so that guardians can't spawn right in front of you while you're moving. I once died within the first few minutes of a run because after jumping off of a ledge, the masked defiler spawned right below me mid-fall and it killed me (I had a whip that took away 2 heart containers).

My second problem with this game is the bugs. I understand this game is still in early access but I feel like they've released the game too early and aren't focusing on some major bugs that are affecting the foundation of the game. The game is a platformer and as such, the character controls and the interactions with the environment are imperative to perfect. The controls for the most part actually do feel very tight and responsive which I love, however the whip feels sluggish (which might just be me) and its grappling length feels very inconsistent.
On the topic of interactions with the environment (and the environment itself), I feel like the rules here change a lot which is likely down to bugs again. One example is again the spike traps. I feel like sometimes I am able to walk through spike traps after they have shot up and I won't take damage however sometimes I find I do and I honestly can't say which is intended (however if I may, I recommend that walking through the traps after they have come up as that can help with balancing them with the devouring range).

My last issue is less with the game and more with the developers. For an early access game, I would expect far more transparency from them than I am seeing. While this itself doesn't necessarily mean the game is going to be bad, it isn't a good sign. How can we trust the developers to ensure the quality and future of this game if we barely hear from them? I would like to note that this might change in the near future. In their most recent update information, they talked about being hectic with the disconnect issues and, now that they are fixed, are looking forward to talking with the community more about the game.

Overall I think that this game could do with some more time in the oven before I would call it fully playable. The random generation feels unfair at times and the bugs make this game hard to enjoy sometimes. The developers not being very talkative about the game and its future is concerning but this could change. In the end, if you're looking at buying this game and are interested, I would say maybe wait a bit beforehand to see if some of these major issues are fixed and if the developers start to become more transparent.\t\t\t\t\t

(specs at the end of the review). I know from working with unreal for years that it can be a pain to keep performance in-check but this is unacceptable for the type of game it is.

How the whip is implemented is questionable. a button has to be held down to aim and maybe(if the whip wants to) when you press another button the whip will hook. this is one of the games main ways to move around. why can't the hook/grapple behaviour not be set to one button press? and the special attack(i assume because left click does an attack and i have not gotten a new whip yet) can be set on another. A suggestion would be to have the hook/grapple on left click and the special attack on the right and removing the unnecessary right click hold aiming. it you don't want to have the position indication be shown all the time you can change the colour of the aiming point that is already present in the game to indicate if you can reach.
these changes will align the users experience with the fast movement of the whip.

when using the whip to travel across the floor the position you end up is very unpredictable. this gets worse when trying to grapple to a moving platform. the indicator suggests you should end up where you aim but you often don't.

the rotating chambers/ traps are incredibly clunky. if a chamber continually rotates then this is not reflected in the physics of the character and the character will suddenly rotate very fast after what feels like sticking to the ground.

Lack of graphics settings does not allow you to control scaling. Low setting disables everything and uses 50% scaling. medium has everything turned on and uses 71% scaling. the problem is that scaling looks bad. give the player the option to control the graphics settings. i am pretty sure the game runs and looks better if all the graphics options are turned off but scaling is at 100% then running the game in the default medium settings. I am glad i know where to find unreals default file locations but no player should be required to change files to enable or disable basic graphics settings.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun game made worse by bugs, performance issues and a dwindling player base.

The game is a very fun rouge-lite platformer centered around the fact that a level can only be completed once by one person, then that map is gone forever, along with that you can see the ghosts of the players who have died previously. Your goal is to get that relic to prove how superior you are.

The game struggles with performance for me, and reading through other comments this seems to be a common issue. It would appear (at least based on my fps) that the entire map is rendered simultaneously, which leads to major drops in frames on bigger levels, which means the further you get the harder the level gets and the worse your frames get, not a great combination.

One major selling point is the whole phantom aspect of being able to see other players failed runs, akin to the dark souls series, but the player base doesn't seem large enough for it to really mean something, the most phantoms I've seen in a level is three and I've gotten more empty ruins than I have shared ruins.

Lastly but most importantly the game is buggy. On multiple occasions while playing the screen will just go black and you will be teleported or just have your character launched in the air for seemingly no reason, this has killed multiple runs of mine since dodging traps is hard when you're blind.
The whip, your main mode of transportation, seems to have a mind of its own and will sometimes drag you along like it is supposed to but other times just smack the wall or ledge and send you falling to your death, the whip indicator does not help and will often not show up and when it does it sometimes smacks the wall instead of grappling.

TL;DR
A very fun game that will frustrate you because of performance drops and bugs that ruin the game, the player base is small and as such you won't encounter a lot of phantom, something I personally wanted.

I can't know if the bugs are because of the recent update but if it is it didn't spend enough time in QA\t\t\t\t\t
