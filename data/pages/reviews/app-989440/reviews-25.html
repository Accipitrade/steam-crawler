\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhantom abyss is a satisfying, easy to learn 3D platformer. Taking a very simple concept, it polishes it and adds simple extraneous elements to create a - while not wholy addicting loop - a certainly satisfying one.

As it is early access, there are issues. For instance, random generation can very rarely create impossible temples with critical paths blocked by seemingly impossible traps or pillars. The game is also missing at least 1 floor, though I suspect there may be two for a total of 5.

The whip system is an interesting twist on progression, but the only one that persists within the game. This means that much of your success comes from experience. There are some subsystems that have interesting dynamics, like the ghosts of the past revealing secrets or dying to traps to inform you, or manipulating the blessings shrine to gain a heal when you need it most.

The guardians apply pressure to the player in different ways to force them to advance through the level and create stress. The eye of agony and the masked defiler are fair and balanced; the eye constantly harassing you with lasers while the mask creates puddles of poison that slow you and keep you away from areas. The devouring rage, however, is largely considered to be unfair, as especially at later levels the devouring rage is too fast to dodge amongst the other traps you are looking to avoid. As a result, many players do not bother trying the temple beyond the first floor if devouring rage is the guardian. I believe it needs a rework; it is an interesting idea but does not suit the kind of platforming the game is looking for.

I also believe there are not enough guardians or room variations in the game right now to allow the randomness to shine. However, the groundwork is set, and the potential for a fun and challenging platformer exists.

By far the greatest feature is the whip. It is the kind of mechanic a game developer would seek to emulate for the fun of it. I thoroughly enjoy the satisfaction of the movement through it and the ways it can be used. It is a genius method of movement and carries this game.

Overall satisfied with my purchase of early access. If the developers stick with the game and continue to improve and add variations of traps, I will stick with it. I may not play it often, but I will recommend it.

I would happily suggest ideas for traps or guardians, but I believe the developers are gifted enough.\t\t\t\t\t
