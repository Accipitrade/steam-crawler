\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely fantastic game so far. You run through progressively harder dungeon levels. Each level consists of 4 stages (floors) after which you can choose the reward or keep playing harder level with more sophisticated traps to get better rewards.

Controls are simple but you can do a lot with them. You have jumping, sprinting, crouching/sliding and your trusty whip you can use to access hard to reach spots. You collect treasure through your run and at the end of each floor you can spend it on upgrades such as double jump, longer or faster whip etc. Making exploration interesting and rewording.

From 2nd floor you are chased by one of the guardians that adds extra pressure to run though dungeon rather than just casually walk through it. Running and jumping feels great, you get nice momentum and flow of movement.

The feeling I get from this game is the same kind of feeling I had playing portals for a first time. You get surprised all the time with how much you can do with tools you have.

Finally fantoms. It's such a cool feeling to start running along 20 ghosts of other players and slowly as you decent down the levels herd gets smaller and smaller and finally you are the only one going down the path knowing no one has ever got that far in the dungeon yet.

Dungeon layout is randomly generated but rooms become familiar fast, notice familiar not repetitive. You learn certain layouts quickly but they are always having some twist keeping you on your toes

As not huge fun of fast paced 1st person games this one still got me hooked.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t====================
The Short:
If you enjoy fast paced platforming and feeling like being as close as you can to one of our favorite childhood lost-ark raider, than you're in the right place. I've beaten the highest tier on this game about three times and I have every intention to complete that collection of idols to accompany my hard-earned cardboard box.
====================
The Long:
-If I recall correctly, you're playing a prisoner trapped in a temple alongside a forgotten god of the Abyss and, for better or worse, both of your escape is tied to your ability to gather "relics" and restore the forgotten god's power. That's about as much as you get for the story; which is refreshingly concise.
-After completing the tutorial and acquiring baby's first whip, you'll soon find yourself delving into the dungeon, stumbling about as you get sliced, impaled, crushed, bludgeoned, cratered, incinerated, suffocated, and even eaten. Given time, it all starts coming together as you then begin recognize the patterns various traps have and the proper timing to navigate your respective hellscape. Sometimes that happens deftly, other times it's by the skin of your teeth.
-Currently there are three tiers/stages of which you can rush through, each with five levels a piece, and it seems there is the framework for even more content later on.

The Good:
-I've found the game to be surprisingly fluid. Takes a few runs to get a feel for how much reach your whip actually has and what maneuvers you can get away with, but it doesn't take long to get the hang of it. You can take fall damage, but there is a mechanic that let's you negate that damage and it's fairly forgiving. Before you know it, you'll be trapeze-ing about like a circus acrobat.
-Even if you fall down into a pit, you're respawned back at the beginning of the room you were in. You lose some of your health in the process, but at least it's not a run-ending penalty.
-The traps all have tells to them, making itself known and giving you but a brief moment to react before you suffer the consequences. Being able to dodge out of the way, or avoid activating the trap entirely, is going to depend almost entirely on you as the player. Alternatively, a wayward soul can also activate some traps or deactivate others briefly by using their trusty whip on said traps.
-The addition of guardians, the closest thing to an enemy you'll have in this game, are unkillable and constantly tailing you and ensure that you cannot take the entire game at a slow and steady pace. It gives the game a sense of urgency that otherwise would be lacking and I believe that Phantom Abyss is all the better for it.

The "Bad":
-The rooms you run through have a "prefabricated" feel to them, and the randomness comes into play when the game decides what order its going to lay out those rooms for your level. If any of you have ever played "Gunfire Reborn" you'll probably know exactly the feeling I'm describing. You learn quickly that the rooms themselves don't change so much as the way/order the game lays out these rooms from run to run. Treasure chests seem to be in the same place every time, every ledge remains in its proper place, and even the same holes in the floors are all still crying out for maintenance. When you encounter a room enough times, you can quickly pick up how to navigate these all-too-familiar setups.
-Elevation beats almost all the traps you invariably encounter. Don't get me wrong, the existing traps are all fantastic! However, as the player you can pick up two very specific powers that not only make the character incredibly mobile, which has its merits, but can trivialize entire networks of traps in the later stages of the game because there's little to no risk in taking to the skies. I would like to think of myself as a decent game, but I can largely attribute the success I've achieved in the game thus far to the combination of those two powers specifically.
-Trap variety is somewhat low, but the game compensates well for this currently by making up for their variety with quantity and we can only expect so much given the game (at the time of me writing this review) only just came out in Early Access. Still, I hope one day I can see flamethrowers, caltrop throwers, sticky floors, slick floors, quicksand, bat swarms, spider webs, Deep Rock Galactic style ceiling predators, exploding chests, mimics, mines, mimes, and the inevitable death room that seals you inside and floods with water until you drown.
====================
The Conclusion:
Phantom Abyss, at the time of me writing this, has only just been released as an Early Access game. Seldom to I concern myself with Early Access games, but this one brought a sense of primeval enjoyment I rarely experience. It doesn't burden its players with endless resource grinding, complicated crafting systems, flashy combos, or an arcane narrative. All you have to do is grab the whip that best suits your play style, step into the Abyss, and pray to the forgotten temple god that you have enough time for just one more run before you have to leave and be a responsible adult again.\t\t\t\t\t
