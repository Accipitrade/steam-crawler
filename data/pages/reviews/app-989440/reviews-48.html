\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhantom Abyss is great. It's got a little Early Access funk, naturally, but the majority of it is superb. Movement feels fantastic. You're extremely fast and have so much air control over your character. The whip is extremely lenient thanks to its ability to snap to ledges, which is much less frustrating than it sounds.

The gameplay loop isn't terribly deep right now, but the gameplay itself is very fun and the perfect amount of challenging, for the most part. The guardians can complicate things, though the devs are thankfully open to adjusting aspects of them that feel less fair. There're multiple options for whips that can change how you play, though some of them are objectively better than other once you remove the curse from them. A particularly bad example is two whips in the same tier that provide one extra heart or two.

The performance is a little lacking. I'm not sure what the problem is, but as of typing this many people have had severe performance issues with decent rigs. I've had some frame drops, though thankfully it's still playable. The devs have stated that this issue is high priority for them, so hopefully that gets fixed soon.

All around, the game is really fulfilling to play. You just feel cool jumping and grappling around, just narrowly avoiding a guardian and squeaking into a safe room.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game definitely has some jankiness to iron out, and I'd really like to see more lines of progression implemented into the game.

Two design decisions stand out to me as being poor:
First, when ledge-grabbing, your camera snaps to horizontal instead of maintaining the direction you were looking like you'd expect, I find this very disorienting.
Second, while it doesn't affect me much, the tuck-and-roll camera effect is excessive and seems to really bother a lot of people. I'd like to see these effects get a toggle in settings or for them to be removed entirely.

There is also very little in terms of progression at the moment and it doesn't take long to start seeing repeating patterns in dungeon layout, especially on the earlier floors which you will be spending a lot of time in.

Other than these two major complaints, the game is pretty interesting and has a lot of potential. Devolver has a fantastic track-record, so for now I'd recommend following this game and/or adding it to your wishlist. Hold off on a purchase especially if you like more variety in gameplay or have a sensitive inner-ear.\t\t\t\t\t
