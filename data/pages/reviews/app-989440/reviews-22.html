\r\n\t\t\t\t\treally great game. little gem

I love this game and keep coming back to it but wanted to give a more extensive review.

In order for this game to keep what it has going there's a few quality of life changes i would strongly recommend.

1. that sticky wall grapple thing. Allow it to be toggle on/off in the options - Most PC gamers do not like it when something takes control of our mouse cursor. We would rather have it off.

2. The grapple sometimes does not grapple the surfaces! - this has caused me to die many times from safe jumps when the grapple just didnt connect to the wall and i fell to my death. I wish this was a one off but i would say at least once an hour it failed.

3. Hoping for more randomisation with more set pieces being added in. Earlier dungeons need more randomisation for sure. Add more room archetypes

4. Puzzles - It would be great if you could add pushable blocks to create puzzles or actual puzzle elements to get past some areas. Expanding on the single ball to the door

5. Add dangerous/risky loot reward areas. Adding a seriously difficult/tricky route to loots or idols would be great.

6. Swapping whips in dungeons. - Rooms where you can swap your whip for randomly found whips would be awesome.

7. More Biome types and guardian types - Look to add things like Ice biomes with slippy floors, desert biomes with quicksand traps

8. Dungeons - It would be cool if you could randomise some dungeons in with skeletons or undead or something we can whip/trap. Imagine entering into a room where the doors lock until you defeat a few waves of baddies, or survive a few waves of traps... like a room where you have to avoid falling rocks, or spinning moving blades you got to dodge for a few minutes, or skeletons chasing you. How about a skeleton with a key that you have to find to get out the room or something.

Theres so much you can do to this game to make it even better than what it is now. but for now it is awesome and theres so much you can do to expand this and make it better.

What about co-op dungeons?

Dungeons where you have to have 2 people to complete them? like where you have to push a block from upper to lower area so the other player can get out? - you could try a double whip move for coop where people can whip onto someone elses whip to pull them up or something. or where people have to stand on 2 pressure plates to open doors/puzzle areas etc...

Overall i highly recommend this game, its got a good 20 hours in and maybe 40. I'm hoping they realise the gem they have here and invest into making this better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell... after 26.4 hrs I can say the game is fun. It lacks content however. You'll go for a bit unlocking all whips and upgrading them, getting all relics could be a goal but in the current stage I just didn't want to do it. I like the game, and what I'll write will be fairly critical, just a heads up. Also the game currently is in Early Access.

Levels:

The first levels are hard at first, then it becomes very easy once you know them. The same applies to every floor after. (currently third tier is the highest). The rooms are procedurally generated, however you will encounter the same layouts with different traps inside, so once you encountered a room you know exactly where the chests will be where the exits could be where to look for stuff basically, which is not bad in itself, but currently there aren't as many rooms in the game to be ideal for the experience. You'll encounter the same room at least 3 or 4 times, but sometimes even more frequently in a single run. Traps are in different places though, which adds a little challenge sometimes, but it mostly does not. Trap variety is also fairly limited.

Whips:

The whips are a fun touch to the game, giving different bonuses and negative effects, which can be removed by completing a temple with the corresponding difficulty. Many of the whips however touch on the same stuff and are generally very boring. (eg. you earn more gold, the shrines cost less - those 2 are basically the same effect, more hp - multiple whips with that) Tier 3 whips however have some fun stuff on them, spicing up the gameplay somewhat, which makes them a ton of fun.

Shrines:

The shrine system gives you upgrades for the run, costing gold which you find in chests. It is pretty good, however getting hover and double jump is just way above anything else you can get and there are passives which are generally useless. Needs a little tweaking. Costs are fairly ok, however some whips can really break the system, making you an overpowered demigod really fast (for the first two tiers the third is fairly challenging).

Ghosts:

You can see other player's attempts in the temple as their ghost appears and does what they did before they died. It is a really good concept, it is fun to see other players fail, but the game doesn't do much with it. Some whips have some limited interactions with the ghosts and you can't whip the chests they opened (that does give you the gold instantly, you can still go up to the chest to get the money if a ghost opened it), but apart from that the game does nothing with it.

Final thoughts:

I had fun with it, but after 26 hrs I have seen most if not all of it. I like the game and hope that it will have much more content in the future. If you are into platformers, rougelikes, or Indiana Jones you will love the game. If you get easily frustrated you will most likely not. Hopefully this review helps somewhat deciding if you'd like to buy it or not.\t\t\t\t\t

TLDR: Intense game requiring quick thinking and reflexes. Unique feeling to each temple. About ~50 hours of content to 100% the game (depending on skill)


When I first reviewed this game several months back I gave it a negative review due to its lack of content, poor performance, and overall quality. Now that the game has gone through a few major updates since then - I changed my review to positive. 

What happened since then? The major updates they added fixed the majority of the concerns I had with this game. With every major patch that gets added it creates a whole new feeling to the game. The temples, being procedurally generated, create a unique feeling to each one. Since I'm a fan of pros and cons here is my personal list.


Pros:
  • Tense moments requiring quick thinking on your feet
  • Music that will make your palms sweaty
  • Unique feeling to each temple
  • A sense of progression with whips/blessings/relics
  • Great movement system
  • Challenging at first that gets easier over time with more skill/knowledge 
  • Developers that listen to the community and have active QnA sessions


Cons:
  • Minor annoying bugs (guardians spawning in inconvenient spots)
  • About ~50 hours of gameplay to 100% the game (depending on skill)
  • Game is too easy at this moment for me (I clear a temple almost every time)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate 23.10.2021

As people have asked I have played this again. And no surprise it still has lag spikes, especially in the last areas. Gameplay has not changed so my opinion in that regard stays the same.

The new rotating rooms introduced new issues for me though as you get clipped through walls sometimes. Hit detection on the interjecting edges (between moving and not moving room tiles) seems to be busted as well.

At least there's finally some communication regarding this game but overall it still suffers the same pitfalls (haha funny joke) as before the recent updates. There also still is no new content as far as I could tell.

Should there be another major update I will revisit it again but some of the new traps have made playing the game more frustrating for me.

Old review

Current state of this game is a bit worrying.

There are performance issues even on higher end hardware which cause stuttering leading to situations where you can't avoid damage anymore. I am running a 2080ti, with a i9 CPU, 32 GB of RAM and still get these issues.

Then you have the procedural generation of levels which is good for replayability but it can actually lead to a guaranteed loss of your run as well because certain tiles can block your way forward.
You also have no immediate control over what relic you will receive at the end of a run unless you check for level codes which get reset. This makes the progression of the game very odd.

But the worst thing right now is that the dev is way too quiet. There was no update or post for over 20 days now, I even checked the Discord server. Nothing. The concept itself could probably hold some more content but there is just no info on what's going to happen next.

Kinda reminds me of Fall Guys which burned out it's fuel very quickly too.

To end this review on a more positive note, I did have fun in the 30-ish hours and completed nearly everything the current content has to offer. I hope that there will be better news in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
