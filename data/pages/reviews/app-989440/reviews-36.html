\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot bad but not what I Expected.

I saw about 2 ads for this game and then immediately bought it because I thought it was basically the game "Hot Lava" but with a "semi-competitive death race to the finish line" type deal mixed in.
I wish that was the case but what I got still is not too bad.
I hope in a future patch they add in a sorta Death-Race gamemode where you actually race other players to the relics.
As it is right now you are essentially playing a single-player, randomized, Rogue-Lite, Deathrun Thing with the ghosts of other players guiding you to where they died.
I have not read or watched much about this game but from what i have played I am pretty sure that the way this game functions is it Randomly generates a dungeon and then gives that dungeon to a player for them to try. Since they are the first player to try the dungeon it will say "No one else has been here" along with the type of guardian in the dungeon.
Once the game gives a player a dungeon no one else can attempt that dungeon until the player who has it dies. when the player who is trying to complete the dungeon dies the games saves their replay as a ghost and sends it to the next player over and over until someone beats the dungeon upon which that dungeon is deleted.
You can learn a few things from watching the replay ghosts as you run through where they do but when it boils down to it you are still playing single-player and cannot interact with the ghosts aside from having them activate traps and treasure chests ahead of you.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t-Despite the devs best effort, there's no incentive to move quickly as the guardians are easily manageable. The best course of action is to take it slow.
-Money is either abundant or scarce beyond belief, often resulting in 0 or 2 perks upon reaching the first statue.
-Dodge roll is jarring and nauseating, as it actually flips your camera, unlike for example, mcree's roll in overwatch.
-There is currently no brightness slider, so players with a harder time seeing in the dark are left at an disadvantage with the scenic temple lighting.
-Inability to toggle off player trails leads to secrets being shown for you, especially when multiple people have failed a temple.
-Special whips purchasable after getting your first non-tutorial relic are left in a temple if you fail, only being returned upon payment of special keys or another player completing the dungeon. This game is single-player, with asynchronous multiplayer technically existing, so such a bizarre setup for returning single player items is annoying and frustrating.
-Player character is seemingly randomized each run, with their being both male and female grunts being heard. I hope a player customization feature is on the roadmap, obviously after gameplay improvements and additions are made first.

HOWEVER, all of these are nitpicks, personal biases, and mindful judgements solely belonging to myself, and the devs still have at least a year left of early access to address everything, so despite all the seemingly negative comments, I recommend this game, at least while its on sale for 20 USD. The devs are listening, and I look forward to seeing what becomes of this game.

Written 06/28/2021 after a positive time playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game. What you see in the trailer is what you get. I have yet to have a disconnection or lag, but I also have nice rig to play on.

Breakdown on the game as it was released as:
You take a whip and either go into a random dungeon or use a code to do one a friend has failed. There is a third mode that is disabled due to bugs.

You then try to do 3 levels of dungeon, which gets harder the farther you go down with a boss that gets stronger as well. at the end of the third level you have a choice, either go for a tier 1 relic on a final level or go deeper where the traps and stuff get MUCH harder. Another 3 levels and you get the choice a final time if you what to get a tier relic (so you do either 4, 7, or 10 levels total to get a relic or die trying).

at the end of each level there is a blessing shrine. You may buy blessing for 150 (+ 50 gold each time you have done it). You get a choice between two of things like slow fall or longer whip range.

They have changed the whips from what you see on the streamers. You start with only a basic whip and you have to get a tier 1 relic to be allowed to get the tier 1 whips. They can come with a good effect and bad effect (that can be removed). If you die with a special whip equipped, it gets locked until someone beats that temple (or a deeper one it funnels to)(If a bit you could use resources to unlock but I think that has been removed).

However if you have a special whip and you grab a relic of the same tier with it, the bad effect will be removed.

The higher tier whips are better. For example, their is a tier 1 whip to start with 1 extra heart. In tier 2, there is both a 3 heart bonus whip and a 1 heart bonus + turns off a certain danger whip.

I have been having a blast with it.

Tips:
Your whip can open treasure chests from a distance.
Their are a lot of chest spots (and there can be double hidden chests in the hidden areas).
You can grab whip the wall to jump over traps.

Things for Developers:
Please balance the blessings. Double jump is just a far worse slow fall, edge slide is near worthless, and the heal 1 heart (while useful) is so much worse than a extra heart also heals you it pains me to see it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start off this review by spouting some random nonsense. I myself am a speedrunner. I speedrun games like New Super Mario Bros Wii, Mario Odyssey, Super Mario 3D World, and Spongebob Battle for Bikini Bottom Rehydrated. How does this info relate to this game/review though? This game always forces you to speedun the select temple you are given no matter what color it is (green, blue, red) and thats how that info relates to this game, I heavily enjoy the feeling of speedrunning the temples in this game (even if I die in them) and the constant feeling of being on the clock even IF, you are the only person who has ever played that temple. This game forces you to be on the move constantly and forces you to think ahead and plan routes you may or may not wanna take everytime you enter a new room. The traps in this game are alright and are REALLY easy to memorize and get the hang of after you play for a while; but whats interesting about them is the combination of all the traps in a room. Theres no specific room for a specific type of trap (unless its a room where the giant pillars are coming down from the ceiling), every room always has a different variety of traps that will combine/work together to attempt to screw you over, slow you down, kill you, etc etc. The guardians are also a nice feature to this game to help you be constantly on the move or try to screw you over/kill you.

Overall I believe this game is amazing and I completely love it, Truth be told, I kinda talked bad about this game when it was presented at E3. But, that was because I thought the concept of the game wouldn't work out at all and I thought it would become a total flop because of that. But even with me talking bad a bit about this game, I still held high hopes for it and those high hopes were fulfilled and I fell in love with this game immediately after my first temple (despite me failing miserably in it). One feature that I would love to see in this game would be a race feature (maybe this is already added into the game and its the removed "gamemode" in it due to bugs-) where you can invite your friends to race in a temple. If one person dies in said temple, they spectate the others until someone wins. How would someone win you might ask? Well, either the temple would be infinite and get harder and harder overtime and someone would just win because everyone else died in it or there would be a relic at the end of it and someone would have to grab it in order to win.

Now here comes some random/useful tips(?). Try your best not to die in a temple- If you die in a temple, you lose your whip until someone completes the temple you died in or you have to buy it back using keys you find in the temples (Im unsure if you can buy better whips with rarer keys in the game since I've only ever gotten whips that cost me green keys). If you're tired of holding down right click in order to hook yourself to an area, press middle mouse button and it'll turn on an autolock feature where you won't have to hold right click to select an area to swing yourself to (please keep it mind it can be a bit wacky at times). Coins seem useful to have, so try to grab as many as you can before you enter the temple and also be sure to buy blessings (upgrades) as well everytime you're about to enter a new part of the temple/cave. And if you ever wanna listen to some tunes while playing this game, here's some songs you can listen to (this is all personal preference):
"Twin Dragons" - Scott Pilgrim VS. The World The Game
"The Gauntlet" - PayDay 2
"Death Wish" - A Hat in Time: Seal the Deal
"Purple Coin Run" - Super Mario Galaxy
"Gusty Garden galaxy" - Super Mario Galaxy
"Ebb and Flow" - Splatoon 2
"Gym leader Battle" - Pokemon Sword and Shield
"Marine's Theme" - Pokemon Sword and Shield
"Discussion -SCRUM- " - Danganronpa V3: Killing Harmony
"il vento d'oro" - JoJo's Bizarre Adventure Part 5: Golden Wind
Did I mean for this review to be this long? Nope. And I apologize for getting carried away. Thank you for reading all the way through this if you did :)\t\t\t\t\t
