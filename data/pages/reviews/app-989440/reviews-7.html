\r\n\t\t\t\t\tphantom abyss is a game that gets harder the more floors and areas you complete, with you being able to end the run at certain points or can continue if you have a certain resource. the catch is that players before you may have died in the are you are exploring and can use them for your benefit. after a not so thorough game play session starting around 3 am, i wanted to make a not so serious biased review.

gameplay: in a normal run you would see dead people and while i did, there was far less few. i can see over 50 people in a place making it very lively in the past, but now its only none to 10 at best. skill does come into play, i can reach pretty far with a normal whip and no blessings with the health making it to where i can't get hit with many things, with some deaths being my fault, and some being unknown. there are newly added dark areas, making you legally blind, and needing to carry a crystal to see a bit. its only a bit however and you might need to take it slowly before getting hit.

movement: the movement is pretty decent, you can control it very well and dodge many traps with it. however sometimes moving objects can be very jank, most of it being rotating and jumping. sometimes it can be very helpful, and sometimes get thrown into speeds of mach 10 into rotating blades.

new health: the health was usually three, getting increased by blessings or whips overall. the new health system makes phantoms a near necessity for most, because you now only have a basic health of one, getting increased by the amount phantom deaths you collect. this is challenging because this can screw with new players because phantoms sometimes the place is new or very few visited.

energy system: i barely the blessings because reasons, but from not so good sources, they have a energy system for some perks, like double jump. it regenerates for a certain amount after a floor, however accidental use may be a pain for some.

randomness: the game i feel like is overall pretty random, with movement speed to how long the floor will be, you can finish a floor within a minute to seven. this comes to most likely resources, traps, areas, and overall how many phantoms you might meet.

overall: the most reviews i seen was lack of players being able to fill the areas, leading to a very big difficulty spike, and making a majority leave because of imbalances. i say that the gameplay is pretty balanced, but im also the person that is able to play with nothing but the beginning whip, so take that with the salt mines. i gotten my 10 floors beaten with a red mask following, it was fun but then i somewhat pondered then what, upgrade the whips, while the beginning might be fun, i wondered what next, i can try yellow coins but what will they do?

i realized i only played this for 8 hours, meh its 6 am already.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly, I actually do love the idea of this game - but recent changes are...questionable. Mind, I'm not whinging here - this is the dev's game and they can do what they want - I just don't like what they've done.

Ghosts of previous players can trigger traps now, and in ways you don't expect - combo'd with the low starting health, that's a tad harsh. You've now got less starting health, but can gain it through other player's deaths - but you can (often) start a run with no-one else having been there before...

I know runs in rogue-lites can be severely affected by luck, but these changes are just going to frustrate people when there's no 'free health ghosts' about - and when there are, hang back a bit to wait for them all to die/not affect your run. To me, the focus should be on the player's skill, and their failure should be on them alone.\t\t\t\t\t
