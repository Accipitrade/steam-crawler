\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI saw this game a month ago or so, and looked at it and thought meh, liked the idea, but thought it looked a bit dated.

Found that it was updated and this time took the time to read what it actually is and does and OMG the concept is simple but BRILLIANT!

It reminds me of an up-to-date 3D version of an old, old, old Spectrum 48k game called Tutankhamun (micromania 1983 - ouch I played this when it first released).

This game is basically what you'd imagine Indiana Jones and Lara Croft are doing while they are waiting for their next films/games to be made.

It is such a simple premise, but the execution is PERFECT. Not sure if it is my rig, but I can run it in 4K 120hz capped at 120fps and it is buttery smooth (maybe G-Sync is helping this out too) and I have no performance issues.

The control of your character is so tight and spot on. It really is a dream to play, I have not missed a single jump and thought "wow, that is a bit harsh". In other words, I know when I miss a jump it is because I have messed up and not because the game has glitched, or has been unfair and this is imperative for a game of this nature.

I am not a massive multiplayer gamer, so I love the asynchronous nature of things. If anything it helps build tension as all the phantoms run off and leave me pondering where to go next. To get to Floor 4 of a dungeon, have everyone else disappear while you make your way into the maze, only for someone to cross your path about 10 minutes later really gets your heart leaping. To have an element of MP in a SP game is superb.

The fact there are no battles is brilliant, yes there are enemies to dodge, but your biggest enemy by far, is....yourself as you start to doubt yourself as to whether you can make that jump, or get through the rotating room, or time that platform movement.

I think a cool idea for a future update would be for players to leave messages (e.g clues or red herrings) for those that come later.

Another option I would love would be to re-visit a tomb after you have died in to allow you to see the end of it, but without sealing it for good.

Even without these minor points this game is a must-buy in my opinion and I cannot wait to see where the devs take this next!

TLDR
Graphics: Superb, they just fit the gameplay
Control: Spot on, could not be improved IMHO
Concept: Love it, love it, love it!
Overall: Stop reading this and go buy it! ;-)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the most part, I really enjoy this game. It's one of the very few, very rare three dimensional precision platformers out there, and It even has a risk/reward system! There are special whips you can buy and unlock with "keys", each with unique benefits and curses. You can remove those curses by obtaining a relic of similar rarity. At any point if you lose a run, that whip and all your keys will be "captured." You will need to wait for another player to beat that temple to get the whip back, or spend keys to get it back instantaneously. It's very pseudo-cooperative gameplay.

It's great for a hardcore gamer.

I will say however, that I do have some small gripes with the game that people should be aware of. For example, some whips will have a curse where the other phantoms can steal your coins. This is fine on paper, however early game you will notice some phantoms seemingly running way faster than you or any other phantom. Apparently there is a whip in the later game that has a method of increasing your speed one way or another. This is very counter-intuitive for new players, as there's literally nothing you can do about it. You just kinda gotta pray one of those phantoms don't show up when using a whip where phantoms can steal coins. This is the strangest design flaw I have seen in recent years.

Another gripe I have is the performance. Most temple rooms run fine for me. However, there is some strange phenomenon where certain parts of a temple will tank your fps for no good reason. I'm sure in time this will be fixed, but I wanted people to be aware.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGioco di nicchia, deve piacervi il platforming in prima persona (come a me) oppure avere un forte interesse per l'ambientazione stile avventuriero in tempio maledetto strapieno di trappole, oltre a sopportare l'idea che un paio di errori e si rincomincia da capo (roguelike).

Non trovo troppo valore aggiunto nella meccanica dei fantasmi dei giocatori precedenti, è utile all'inizio per imparare dal loro esempio (finchè non diventano cadaveri, ovviamente) ma una volta imparato il gioco danno fastidio sia perchè possono attivare certe trappole sia perchè il gioco risente a livello di prestazione.

Tuttavia, liberare i fantasmi di amici è divertente, dal momento che potete scambiarvi informazioni su quanto quel particolare tempio sia un bastardo e suggerimenti su come superare certe stanze particolarmente difficili, oltre ad avere la soddisfazione di aver aiutato un vostro compagno di avventura a recuperare il loro bottino perduto.

A 33 ore ho fatto tutto quello che c'era da fare nel gioco, almeno 5 di queste sono da afk quindi 28, ma le ultime 8 sono di grind per ottenere tutte le reliquie (potete trovare dei doppioni, cosa che reputo estremamente stupida). Probabilmente, se vi impegnate, intorno 5 ore completerete quello che per ora è l'ultimo tempio, e se non siete interessati a sbloccare e benedire (togliere il malus che controbilancia il bonus di ogni frusta) la maggior parte delle fruste, probabilmente sarete soddisfatti e smetterete di giocare.

Parlando delle fruste, la maggior parte è abbastanza noiosa ma facile da sbloccare e benedire, tuttavia quelle di grado più alto sono sia difficili da benedire (dovrete finire l'ultimo dungeon con grossi malus come morte istantanea da danno da caduta, per esempio) ma danno dei bonus molto interessanti/forti, quindi anche dopo aver "vinto" c'è della sfida in più.

Per concludere, è un gioco per persone a cui piace un gameplay veloce e che si sviluppa momento per momento, d'azione.\t\t\t\t\t
