\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhantom Abyss is a perfect example of why I love indie games. Compared to AAA studios, indie developers have to make their game stand out from the rest. One of the ways is with a really creative concept, and that's exactly what Phantom Abyss has. In short, Phantom Abyss is about raiding temples for their relics like Indiana Jones, but the main difference is that you don't have plot armor and when you die, you lose the loot you got. The game gives the player a choice to pull out of a temple with your loot or go a level deeper, with better rewards but increasing difficulty.

The game does a lot of things well, one being movement. The movement is really fun and you feel very agile dodging pits and spikes. Combining the movement with your whip makes traversal a blast, and a must. Different temples are guarded by different guardians, three types as of right now. All of these are meant to keep the player moving and not standing still waiting for the right moment to go between or over traps. One of them straight up chases you, and these also become more deadly as you go deeper. The one that chases you increases in speed the further you go down, forcing you to make quick decisions so it doesn't catch up.

When I went into this game, I was scared that it wasn't going to be that hard. I was completely wrong, and during my current playtime I have been at most able to get a relic from the second floor ONCE. The game gets hard, but what helps is a sort of roguelike progression. As you play, you unlock different whips that do things like give you extra health or make you heal if you stand close to other player phantoms. These are really diverse and actually change how you play, but when you buy them they have a negative effect as well. To get rid of it, you need to obtain a relic of the same rarity whilst using that whip. The difficult thing is that if you die with a whip, you won't get it back until someone either completes the temple you died in or you pay extra to get it back.

One bad thing with the game is the lag during later stages. It can get very bad and sometimes completely freeze. Although I think as they have some early access feedback it will get better with time.

Even with the lag, this feels like a much more complete early access game unlike a lot of others. I have only gotten a relic in the second area, so I have a lot more to do, and the gameplay loop is very satisfying and doesn't often feel very same-y. Overall I definetly recommend if you think the concept seems interesting.

TL:DR Phantom Abyss is a game with a very unique concept. The movement combined with the whip and the gameplay changes that come with different whips make it very diverse and fun. The game is a balance of wanting better loot but the challenge getting greater, and it gets HARD. As of now, later stages often lag the game a lot, but I think it will get better as time goes on. For an early access game it feels like it has a good amount of content and I recommend if you think it looks interesting.\t\t\t\t\t
