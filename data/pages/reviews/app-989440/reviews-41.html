\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis might become a game worth purchasing, but I don't recommend it at it's release state.

Performance is somewhat bad. The more "ghosts" there are, the worse the game performs. High end rigs were even having problems with CPU utilization, so they patched in an FPS cap to help with this. I've never really heard of a game running too well. But okay. For a game that is fairly unattractive and occurs in fairly closed in spaces, why is the performance so bad?

Whip balance is pretty atrocious. It gets even worse when you "bless" a whip, which removes the negative penalty. Some of these whips just don't seem worth it compared to others. I think they're going to have to completely rework the balance here.

Likewise, guardians are not very balanced. The red guardian that follows you is the hardest one. It puts a hard cap on how long you can take to beat a level. The other ones are a nuisance, but they don't force you to be as fast a possible. It's completely RNG which one you get, and you have to choose your whip before knowing which one you're facing, so there's no strategy there.

But at the end of the day, what really kills this game for me is it's just not that fun to play. For being a parkour style game, it lacks the smoothness and fluidity I would expect. Using a whip to "hookshot," for example, requires you to hold right click to aim then left click to whip. Rolling to avoid fall damage is both visually jarring and wonky to perform...and made completely pointless because you can just whip the ground before landing to prevent fall damage. There's also little rocks and waterways scattered around on the ground that will stop you dead in your tracks.

And the game is aesthetically dull. There is very little music, so the game feels completely dead. Where's the frantic temple run pace? It's certainly not conveyed in the music. Environments are repetitive and uninteresting. I REALLY hope they introduce more biomes, becuase after a mere hour in, I was already bored of seeing the same structures and the same room layouts.

And this is without mentioning any bugs I've found, like weirdly behaving spikes, pitfalls that don't kill you so you're just stuck in a hole, or inconsistent fall damage.

Wait until they make this game into something interesting, because it's not there yet.\t\t\t\t\t

To escape you must collect relics to help a giant statue get enough power to free you. This is done by exploring the ruins, each one you can only visit once and can only be beaten by one person. You can see ghosts of other players who previously tried to beat that level. The feel of Phantom Abyss is 3d Temple run, or first person Indiana Jones. This is a pretty safe recommend IMO. a few bullet points

-I felt like I should edit this in. There is a disconnect bug that kicks you from the game and causes you to lose your whip. It's very annoying and can lock your game for a bit. The developers are hard at work on the fix but if you get the bug you could be SOL for a while

- I felt weary about getting Phantom Abyss because it's a rogue-lite, which I played many of and got burnt out on many times. It's unique enough to not be subject to that burnout. at least for me.

- I felt weary about getting Phantom Abyss because it's in early access. The content on EA launch is actually expansive and feels a bit more like a full game than say Gunfire Reborn was on EA launch. Still not fully complete but enough to be fun for a while.

- I was a bit worried about how the gameplay loop would follow something like rogue legacy where leveling up direcly made the game unrecognisably easier. Thankfully I haven't seen that yet.
