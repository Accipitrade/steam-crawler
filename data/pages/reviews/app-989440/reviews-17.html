\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE: The devs made a bunch of strange decisions.
There are changes and additions which could have improved the game, but NONE of the following have been explored:
- Changes to blessing RNG
- More interesting choice of upgrades and blessings
- More upgrade/blessing/curse synergies and anti-synergies (to boost whip buffs or cancel out curses)
- New guardians
- Guardian modifiers (e.g. modifier on Eye of Agony which gives it a triple-shot but slower shot speed)
- New biomes (e.g. blood temple, sun/holy temple, pestilent/rotting temple, cosmic/astral temple)
- Consumable items (e.g. single-use grappling hooks/parachutes/dynamite)
- Character cosmetics (e.g. cool hat for my phantom)

Instead, we got the following changes:
- Removing blessed whips: Hurts the sense of progression and made difficult temples harder.
- Adventuring Rank: Unnecessary. The old meta-progression of unlocking whips was challenging but less of a grind.
- Player essence and Vine Boom sounds whenever a phantom dies: Meh.
- Somehow, the game runs noticeably worse now. Frame skips are common on my RTX 3090, which IMO is particularly bad for this type of game. No idea why, but you might have better luck than me.
- Phantom death disables trap: This was actually a good change.
- New enemies: Good in theory, but these hazards are less interesting and intuitive than the standard traps and environmental hazards.

This game had so much potential, but the devs haven't done anything creative with it since it first hit the market.
I would instead recommend other games such as Deep Rock Galactic if you want a game with good random map generation, or Neon White if you want a game focused on speed-running movement mechanics.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot enough content to warrant a $25 price tag, in the 30 minutes I played I already witnessed the same chambers recycled at least 3 times, and that's not an exaggeration. Where's the cool music? Where's the tense chases?!

At first I thought the tutorial was going well, I didn't feel like quitting from it, so that's a good thing. But then it fell apart when I did my first run. Not a single trap was shown in the tutorial, so I was immediately overwhelmed. There's nothing tense about the game, I slowly ran through my third run and ended up beating everyone else, I don't quite understand the concept of the game because of this. There's really no music (which, imo, really makes or breaks a game like this) so 90% of the time you're awkwardly running through a temple trying to figure out what to do.

The chambers most of the time are okay, but some of them are open ended? Not sure why you'd make that a thing considering it's supposed to be a race. It just feels like empty, unused space just for the sake of adding more "content" to the game. There's random traps in obscure areas no-one will ever go, most spike traps you can literally walk right around, or when you can't, you just have to kinda sit there and wait for the spikes to retract, pretty lame. No way around them. The deaths are also super underwhelming.

Yeah, I understand this is early access, but this is like a closed alpha if anything.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBare in mind at this point game is in Early Access and may vary significantly

Game concept is pretty great, simple yet unique. You are running for your life in a procedural generated maze filled with deadly traps and a guardian that increases aggression progressively. In addition to that, you run with phantoms of previous adventurers that failed to make the mark so you can see their progression in your run... both to find secrets / chests, and also possible impending death (they also interact with your world, and traps)

Game-play is fluid and surprisingly responsive, i haven't felt cheated at all in any of my runs, and ultimately my failures were due me rushing things or simply making mistakes. It honestly feels really smooth
It's important to note, that while the game runs on a very simple base concept and extremely simple controls... the complexity of the game is far from simple. So it will take some time to master, it's easy to get frustrated when things don't go our way.... but really, it's about learning to pace our run instead of just complaining that the game is unfair.... because so far as i've seen.... it really isn't, but it ain't easy either.

You can collect whips along your way in order to boost your play, but they come with a detrimental curse that powers down something (but you can remove it retrieving a relic matching the whip rarity). And the thing to keep in mind is, that you lose your loot (keys) and your whip if you die, and thus joining the phantom squad. You cannot retry a given temple (generated) so you rely on others to complete it full to retrieve your stuff for free (or you can pay to retrieve your lost whip). You can see your trapped phantoms at start

Music is nothing noteworthy... but really, i don't think that in itself is a bad call. All of my attention is pulled into survival, i don't think a strong music element would benefit the need of awareness and reaction required to survive.

So far i am really enjoying the concept and look forward to see this game grow
If you can deal with dying and getting back up.... it's a blast :)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Despite its flaws, this game is really fun, and once the issues have been fixed I think it'll be one of my "always" recommendations.

I picked this game up after watching Nerdcubed's video on it, since it seemed like my perfect game. Thankfully, it's lived up to my expectations. It's a fun, satisfying time, and I can definitely see myself getting dozens of hours in this.
The game does have some flaws though - most notably the one that many other reviews have mentioned, which is the framerate. In some temples it's fine, but others are barely playable because every few seconds there'll be a massive lag spike. It's definitely still playable 99% of the time, but it's still worth mentioning. As well as this, there's some more minor bugs, such as guardians sometimes not triggering for entire floors (sometimes two floors in a row), which kinda takes away a lot of the tension. There's also a few very minor visual glitches such as there not being any floor underneath some gaps (the player can't reach the gaps in question, so it's only visual) and sometimes being able to glitch the camera above the ceiling for a split second.
As much as there are flaws, don't let that put you off this game. I'm having an absolute blast, and once these issues are fixed I think it'll be a game that I recommend to everyone.\t\t\t\t\t
