\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played Phantom Abyss off and on since I first heard about it, which was almost immediately after release. I saw it for what it was; a challenging, yet interesting game where you dive into temples and try to reach the end. And it always felt great reaching the end of a temple, because you had to learn as you went. I've never liked the whip loss system, but it had enough of a community where if you died, you typically didn't have to wait long for someone to reclaim your whip for you. In fact when this game was in that stage, it was fun to play, easy to pickup, but still challenging to complete.

Then about a year ago (2021-ish), the devs started adding new rooms, new traps, etc. Good, right? Well it would be, if they hadn't spammed every temple with the new traps, making the fluid movement system obsolete and pointless.

Fast-forward to now (July 2022): I got on to see the state of the game, hoping for some good updates, only to be blindsighted by the fact that they've started players with only one hit. ONE. HIT.
It was 6 before (3 hearts), now it's one hit.

In other words, new players are going to find this game extremely frustrating because their focus is going to be entirely on not getting hit a single time, rather than solving the puzzles and navigating the rooms.
I honestly don't know what the devs are thinking with this, but it limits their potential player base considerably. If that's their goal, that's on them.

If you had asked me a year or so ago, I'd have recommended the game as it was, because I found it enjoyable to play.
Today, however, I cannot recommend this game to anyone because it has become the type of game I don't play often; unforgiving, and one-hit-kill. The challenge was fine as it was, but they made it worse.

It wouldn't have been so bad if they started with that, at least I could've gotten my money back, but this started as a game I loved to play, and morphed into one that I couldn't get back into after a break.

I'm not going to tell you to buy or not buy, I don't care. I just wanted the devs to understand why they've lost me as a player, and a customer. I don't have any intention to come back to this game, given how insane they've ramped up the difficulty. If they had options for players to play legacy features, or toggle certain aspects of the temples (one-hit kill goes back to normal 3 heart health like it was, for example, or remove X number of traps so it was like it used to be), it would help legacy players come back to it. But they don't have that, and probably never will.

It's a shame too, I used to enjoy this game a lot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game's game-play is relatively fun, but the devs have made some terrible choices about some mechanics that completely ruin the game. In the last update, they changed it so you ALWAYS (no metta upgrades for health) start with one HP. This is supposed to be balance out by having the ability to gain health from dead phantoms that have failed that specific level prior to your attempt. However, this concept falls apart immediately when you take into account that NOBODY PLAYS THIS ANYMORE. This makes it so almost every single one of your runs will be on a level never attempted before, meaning you are basically stuck one one HP the entire run as the blessings are incredibly unreliable. This makes the game way too punishing, without even taking into consideration the game takes your whip away from you when you lose. These terrible game decisions made by the devs in very important core game-play mechanics lead to an awful gaming experience. I could not recommend this game any less.\t\t\t\t\t
