I was afraid that it would be stressful / high mastery level for me to play (I'm a noob when it comes to these sort of games). But happy to say that the game is enjoyable for noobs like me :D And I've even gotten my first mini relic! (not talking about the end relic for a temple).

Overall a really solid EA game in my opinion :) You don't need a beast of a pc to play this.\t\t\t\t\t

Follow my Curator Page if you're into Strategy Games!

Phantom Abyss is a roguelite action-adventure.

Usually, I write fully fleshed-out reviews and explain the game but since most people will know what this is about, I will keep it short. The review will be updated if I feel the need to after playing more hours.

Basics
You need to defeat five levels of a temple to get its relict. Only one guy or girl can beat a temple, then it's closed forever. When you try a temple, you will see up to 10 other people trying and dying. You can get chests with gold, which will let you buy special abilities or more health on statues.

Aside from jumping and ducking, you have a whip. You can stop constructions with it, or pull yourself up. In the temple are dozens of traps. Some more than obvious, others will surprise you for sure. That's all I'm saying. I don't want to spoil anyone.

Controls
The controls feel very fluid and satisfying overall. Playing with a controller is good enough. You will easily learn all the controls, and a short tutorial will explain the basics anyways. It just feels great jumping from a cliff and rolling off when landing.

Playing the game
just feels great. You never know what comes next. It's exciting to go into the temple, see other people try it while knowing, they didn't beat it. Maybe you'll be the one beating the temple, maybe not. It's very hard I can say for sure but the feeling of getting into the next level is like I never felt before. The gameplay loop is just addictive.

Bugs & Crashes
The only negative I can think about is the crashes. The game crashed often and it sucks. Especially if you're on level three and four and almost won...
Using a controller is also a bit bugged. Sometimes the controller just bugs out (Usually in menus) and you need to move your mouse, then it works well again).

Longevity
I can't say much about that yet. I guess it depends on how good you are and what the devs do during early access. The excitement will die down sooner or later for sure when you're beating temples but as this is difficult enough for the general audience and hopefully, new traps and elements of a temple will be added, you'll probably have fun in the long run.

I definitely recommend the game to anyone into jump&runs or platformer. I'm usually not into that, but Phantom Abyss got me hooked already. I'm sure I'll play dozens of hours 'til it gets boring.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fun! The first hour or so was learning the mechanics and getting a feel for the movement, it was a bit frustrating but I'm glad I stuck it out. The game has performance issues for sure, but that's to be expected for early access and hopefully the devs will get that sorted out quickly.

The price is a little steep at $20 I think, but if you like first person movement puzzle games you will enjoy this.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is really fantastic. It's in Early Access right now but it doesn't really feel like it. I have found very few glitches and the areas and mechanics seem very fleshed out. The visual style is unique and fun, it really feels like you are exploring temples full of traps and loot.

Most importantly though, the game is very fun to play. Its fast-paced, frantic platforming. The Temple guardians are a fun addition that force you to play fast and well. Some of the guardians also promote getting certain abilities. Such as the enveloping rage (or whatever it is called) might convince you to buy a longer whip so that you can scale through areas faster so that he doesnt catch up. While the Defiled mask (or whatever it is called) will keep you moving at all times so that you dont get stuck in his poison gasses.

Really fun game, great music, great audio design, great graphics, and its just plain fun. I highly recommend this, even in Early Access.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs an huge Indiana Jones / Uncharted / Tomb Raider fan I have been anticipating this game for a while now. And now it is finally here in all its Early Access glory. And boy it is mighty fun. On my third attempt, my muscle memory came into play and I was finally able to get hold of my first relic. And even in a temple where no one had been before me. This is the perfect game that is easy to pick up but hard to master. You wanna be fast but you also want to approach cautious. So slowly a graceful dance develops around the traps and to the end of the temple. Every now and then, the whip didn't grab, which led to frustration when the race was going well until then. But it wasn't that often. Something that need to be fixed though is that some guardians can spawn right in front of you in a narrow hallway so you have no chance to dodge especially when it can shoot lasers or poisonous gas. The asynchronous is fun to look at. I rarely paid much attention to what the previous adventurers were doing though, but when another tomb raider made it to the last chamber with me, it felt a little bit like the game Journey.\t\t\t\t\t
