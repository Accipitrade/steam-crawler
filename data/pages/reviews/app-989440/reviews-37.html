\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played a ton (about 5-6 hours total now), but I'm still really enjoying it. I like it in small bursts, but never for an extended period of time. It's fun. It's frustrating. It's challenging. I only have so much patience for dying repeatedly. But it's still a really neat game with an interesting design. I find myself popping in for 2-3 temple runs then stopping for a day or two.

When I last played it, there was just one mode and it was sort of a press your luck mechanic as you progressed through the temple. You went through a few zones of easier traps/challenges, and then had a decision to make on whether to continue to the next, harder section, or to go for an easier relic. Eventually you get to another decision point where you choose to go through the hardest section for a better relic or stop on intermediate. I never made it that far (and still haven't).

Since then, they've added an adventure mode with a leveling system to determine the temple difficulty (while still keeping the classic mode). They've also added a daily challenge mode. Both modes are a welcome addition to me that provide similar experiences to the classic mode while providing slightly different challenge levels. Adventure mode starts out relatively easy and is great for getting started. Daily challenge is randomized and can expose you to medium or hard zones right off the bat rather than a progressive challenge. It's an interesting way to explore harder areas before you're really ready for it.

Additionally, new traps and challenge types have been added (such as rotating rooms). It adds a lot of diversity to the runs that was lacking a year ago. It's fun exploring these new traps, and equally frustrating dying to them. All are well designed and add layers of complexity and freshness to the game.

Overall, it's a great game to kill time with and push yourself to complete the perfect run. In a lot of ways it reminds me of the 2D N+ game, but 3D and with an Indiana Jones vibe to it (though speed is definitely the focus here).

For $25 I think it's worth the price.

The game plays as a first person platformer, as a child between Indiana Jones and Mirror's Edge. You're an explorer trapped in a massive temple-prison, with a local divinity for company. The way out of here? Get into the temples and get as much relics as you can.
You've seen the gimmick; each temple can only be attempted once. Once you're done, either in victory or death, that's it. You're not allowed back into that particular seed ever again.
Temples are, as of this writing, separated in 3 sections of 3 floors, each more difficult than the last. At the end of each section, you can decide either to attempt to clear the final antechamber, get the section's relic (plus some keys), and end your run here and now... Or keep going for a better, more valuable relic, if you have a key to open up the door.
A guardian also shows up as soon as the second floor, and will harass you regularly. They will get more powerful as you go deeper into the temples.
The phantoms of the other players who died in the temple you're in are also with you, showing you the way and tracing back their steps before their demise. They can help you or hinder you: on one hand, they will trigger traps and potentially show you where coffers full of money are located. On the other hand... Well, they will trigger traps. And them getting first to a coffer is an hindrance (if you're the first to open it, all loot is auto-collected. Otherwise, you have to waste time just gathering everything).

The actual gameplay mechanics are very solid. Movement feels super good, and the whip allows for a great deal of mobility once you get the hang of it. There IS fall damage, BUT crouching right before impact will make you do a landing roll, completely negating it. You can slide, jump, grapple with the whip, pick up coffers from afar... All good stuff.
Dodging traps also feels good. There's an audible "Click" once you trigger one, so you can react before getting hit.
Collecting money for coffers also allows to get roguelike-style blessings at the end of a floor, including more mobility options like double jump, slowfall, and even sliding off of a cliff to Looney Tunes your way across for a couple of seconds, which is especially hilarious when combined with the other options.

Whips can spice up your gameplay experience. You can buy alternate ones with keys you unlock during your run, which will have 1 to 3 boons and a penalty, depending on its tier. So you can have +2 HPs, BUT blessings will cost you more money. You can heal by standing next to phantoms, BUT buying a blessing will damage you. You can have +1 HP and see coffers through walls, BUT phantoms will steal coins if they get to the coffers first so you have to race them. And so on.
However, if you die with one of those whips, the temple will capture it, and you won't be able to use it again until another player clears that particular temple - or you can just pay a fee in keys to get it back.
You also lose all the keys you looted on death, but again, a player clearing a temple and freeing your phantom will bring them back to you. Keys you managed to bring back to the lobby are saved for good.

Claiming a relic equal or above the tier of your whip will also bless it and remove the penalty entirely. Which, IMO, is both a good and a bad thing. On one hand, it motivates you to give a shot to whips that can be too detrimental for your style and make them more usable for deeper runs, as well as giving you a reason to gun for lower-tier relics every once in a while. On the other hand... The penalty motivates a certain playstyle, and you do lose on that when it's purged of it. And once penalties are off, not all whips are created equal.

When it comes to other problems, the room's patterns sees a lot of repetition, even early on. Granted, it allows you to speedrun through temples once you have some runs under your belt, but still, it's sorely lacking in that department for now.
Performance is sometimes spotty, especially in deeper levels. Struggling not to get killed by a guardian and the millions of traps with 1 HP to claim that very first tier 3 relic is one thing, but doing it at 15 FPS is downright cruel.
Some blessings are also luck-dependant, and I'm not a fan.
The grappling from the whip is sometimes a bit clunky, the range is not always clear. It's fine once you're used to it, but it can trip you up.
And you do lack some feedback about what happens exactly when you clear a temple, especially considering the whole gimmick. Some numbers about the number of phantoms saved and seeds closed by your efforts could go a long way.

All in all, it's still in Early Access and it shows, BUT it's one of those GOOD Early Access titles. Either get it now, or put it on your radar for later, both are fine. But regardless, I really, really enjoy my time with this game.\t\t\t\t\t
