\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe temples do get a little repetitive despite the proc gen tech behind it - but the competitive aspect of being 'the first to clear the temple' is a good drive and motive. The gameplay is smooth and using the whip to get that life saving hook onto a platforms edge can be really satisfying and heart pounding. Although only three at the moment, the bosses add an awesome dynamic 'ticking time bomb' mechanic in certain areas, really pressuring the player to either progress forward or avoid fatal attacks. The music that comes in at certain points or when bosses arrive is fantastic in my opinion and really adds a layer of heat as it proceeds to get more intense the closer the boss gets to you.

Over all, really great game to boot up here and there to try and beat a temple! Looking forward to seeing what this game becomes during it's early access development.

Edit: Great to see the devs releasing the 'Overturned' update, adds a bunch of new challenging and spinning rooms (literally). Fantastic to see new content coming in. Even after almost 10 hours of playing this game, I still always find it a thrill to jump back in and beat the next temple.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game looks fun on paper, having an old-school deathrun appeal to seeing how other players died, and I had the most fun early on where 12+ people died and we ran as one big group for a time but eventually several issues occurred:

1: Lack of players, not since my first day of playing did I ever encounter a temple where there were any ghosts, every one saying "No one has been here before". It makes the game feel very empty and lonely.

2: Guardians cheating, they are there to pressure you to not take every room inch by inch but sometimes they force you into losing health, go down a one-way tunnel to get a chest and SURPRISE, the Masked Defiler appears behind you and shoots 8 gas bombs into the room, since even touching a Guardian is deadly you have no choice but to absorb the damage.

3: Repetitive Temples, the game is proceduraly generated meaning after a dozen runs I started to recognise room layouts and trap placements.

I can't really complain about shallow content or Aldec running out of things to say after 2 speeches because Early Access, but I can make a recommendation: Don't let Guardians appear in treasure rooms / alcoves (Devouring Rage is the exception), there are no traps or other deadly hazards and should be a reward for exploration, not a corner to back yourself into.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is not optimised, at all. Camera feels really sluggish to control, thanks to the framerate dips and negative mouse acceleration, as well as the imprecise mouse sensitivity slider and motion blur. Movement feels floaty, like they loaded up the UE4 FPS preset and used the default movement. The whip is clunky and unreliable - the green indicator is ironically not a very good indication of where you can or cannot grapple to, often you can use it from further away, and sometimes it lies to you and you end up falling to your death because you whipped a wall instead of grappled it. Having to hold right click to "ready" grapple then left click is annoying, I wish there was just one button to grapple.

That aside, I like the concept. If they can iron out the bugs and drastically improve the performance and general user experience, I may revisit it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game could have been enjoyable, as a fast paced rogue-lite platformer. Instead it does everything in it's power to make you craaaaaaaaaawwwwwlllll through the levels, most traps spring and catch you because you were going to fast. Then there are the later levels where there is just no way through without taking damage, which wouldn't be a big problem except you only have three hearts and each trap will hit you for a whole heart. (But for some reason fall damage is only half a heart??!?!??) Sure you could have 5 hearts or more (if the game cooperates), but even if you do some traps hit you and send you back to the beginning of the room. Only for you to have to climb this hellscape of guaranteed damage again.

This is bad design plain and simple. A shame that this is so utterly soulwrenchingly disappointingly mediocre. I'm not even talking like 5/10 this game has to be a 3/10 game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe idea for this game is absolutely just a ton of fun.

It's a 3D platformer in which you're fulfilling your fantasy of being a famous Temple Explorer/Runner. You dodge traps, run from Temple Guardians and even dodge gigantic Boulders, all while inside a procedurally generated temple that only 1 person will ever complete. YOU MUST CLAIM THE RELIC! All of this experience is made complete with a whip that allows you to grab high ledges which can help pull you to Victory.

If you die, your character during that run becomes a 'Phantom', locking any loot you had on you inside that temple, which can be eventually free'd by another person who completes it.

The core game loop is great. The concept is fresh and unique and the game is a fantastic, ton of fun.

Nice thing is, there's no specific 'session time' you have to worry about. You can dive in for 1 hour and make progress, or end up like myself and play it for multiple without getting bored.

Snag it! You won't regret it. Excited to see where the future of the game goes.\t\t\t\t\t
