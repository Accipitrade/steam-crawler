\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell it's not bad though I think I should probably have waited for a sale.
The route is reasonably well constructed with a mixture of custom assets and a few old soldiers from the Kuju RS days.
The Class 320 as others have remarked is a bit bland and not sure why a 158 was included rather than a 156.
My biggest gripe is the lack of scenarios. No standard scenarios and a grand total of six (6!) career scenarios of which two are using the 158. This is a route with multiple branch lines and a complex service pattern and no attempt has been made to represent that. I know one of the drawbacks of TS vs TSW(2) is that all scenarios have to be individually created and tested - there's no all day timetable - but for Â£24.99 I would have expected far more effort to have been put in. At least one train in each direction for each service group and at different times of the day/seasons, too.

So a cautious thumbs up - 65% score from me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot right with this. I wasn't holding my breath given the standard of the 380 and GARL but the difference is like night and day. Everything from the stations to the saltire livery on the 320s is fairly accurate and, having used the line much more in recent years myself, doesn't feel too far from the real thing. Bit annoying that, after going to the trouble of including DOO equipment at every station, once the doors have closed the driver receives two bells - 320s do not operate with conductors.
Also, it's a shame and bit misleading to have a 158 scenario from Corkerhill Depot to Helensburgh Upper that starts outside Bellgrove station missing out the City Union line - if its not included then why mention it?
However really good that the lines to Milngavie and Balloch have been included, it makes the whole add on feel more complete - hopefully we'll see an extension out to Airdrie and more types of traction in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
