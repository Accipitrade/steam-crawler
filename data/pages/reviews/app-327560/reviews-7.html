\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed this game. It had just the right amount of crafting, problem solving, and resource management. You felt much like the character throughout the game, hopeful that you can pull it off with your current supplies.

The small map is managed with a compass... this is intentional & brilliantly added to the tension of being outside.

The story isn't there to throw you for a loop. In fact, it guides you through your goals as if it really is someone on the other side trying to get you through this. It felt a bit like Apollo 13 in that regard, "Houston, we have a problem".

I recommend this game. This is 100% worth the price of a movie rental or two.\t\t\t\t\t

I would recommend playing at the normal difficulty until you arrive at the power station. Death at the higher difficulties is frustrating because you may have to load an old save to recover desperately needed consumables. I quit playing this game for a while because I was too impatient to think through my path. I died in the first minute of the game by accidentally turning in the wrong direction. A lot of my deaths were just getting lost. Make sure to craft and use lots of items to help yourself. It's not the prettiest game, but exploring it and making progress is exciting. The ending is picturesque and worth the effort.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNear Death is a survival-lite game, which means I was able to play and finish it. You can craft a dozen or so items total and you spend a lot of your time wandering around in almost pitch-black hoping to find a sealed off room that you can warm yourself in so you don't die of hypothermia. The rest of the game is a well paced fetch quest that doesn't overstay its welcome.

---------------

PROS

+ casual. I am not good at survival games. But it is a genre that I would like to get into. Near Death kind of bridges the gap between a walking sim and The Long Dark (which I couldn't even pass the tutorial of).

+ banter. I liked the back and forths between our main character and the other person at the end of the line we are telegraphing.

+ pacing. The game ramps in a good way and I felt like I had learned how to properly survive before it put me in the Antarctic blender and made a slushie out of me.

-----------------

CONS

- "please dear, just stop and ask for directions"
"I'M NOT LOST!" I hate games that have a map but keep it real and make me use my brain to actually navigate it. A game that puts a big red X on my current location is my friend. Yes I know this is part of the game's difficulty mechanic but still.....not only do you have to guesstimate where you are on the map but the wind is battering you around and turning you off course and of course you just ran out of batteries for your flashlight.....

- that *structure* that collapses in the game. You know who you are. You know what you did. Not cool.

-----------------

Near Death is pretty good. It's a bit rough round the edges but it was a bug-free 5 hours of play for me. It allowed me to play a genre I generally suck at and feel like I was crushing it. The game does offer difficulty levels so maybe it can be more challenging if that's what you need.

Not me though. I was quite happy to have my inventory fully stacked almost all the time and have plenty of kerosene for my heating lamp.

Seriously.....I don't know how those penguins do it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort yet sweet, a fun survival game with an emphasis on crafting to survive the harsh environment. Primarily, you'll be venturing into the dark seeking out a building to find a way to escape the climate, which includes repairing generators, patching windows, etc., to make safe zones to weather out the storm.

In my opinion it manages to balance out the scarcity of items with the brevity of the game to a reasonable degree, although I must say I did have times when I had half a dozen items I couldn't use but consistently could never find one item to complete a recipe. This is a fairly minor complaint as it didn't impede my progress but it was a little annoying, could probably be easily remedied by allowing players to loot more contextual objects to make the buildings more dangerous. If I can patch up windows and repair electrical boxes, I think I should also be able to salvage lightbulbs or plastic ties from repairs made before, just to liven up the gameplay loop and make the buildings seem more lived in before the evacuation.

I strode through this game in about 4 hours but it's a fun experience for the time it takes and for me at least I found the environment to be fun, almost reminds of Penumbra. Absolutely worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t
