\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was very good. An interesting story using some solid concepts with good execution in a bite sized format. Pretty much the epitome of 'I'll buy this, and probably gift it to friends now I've seen how good it is.'

It's not particularly long (except maybe if you 100% it which I might do). I finished it in an evening, but the entire map is open at the beginning only limited by your resources and 'knowing where stuff is.' Using cold instead of say oxygen like in Subnautica is interesting and the item progression as you go along makes sense.

They did one thing near the end that I thought was exceedingly smart and clever. I can't really talk about it but you get a good look at your surroundings and realize, this map ain't that big. The weather just makes it seem so. Bravo on that design choice, really set a good tone to go out on.

My one complaint would be that (unless I missed it) the blowtorch wasn't really introduced. I figured it out after dieing at a place it's required, was just kind of annoyed that I died :-p.

edit: I forgot one thing, no HUD markers. I love that you force the player to actually use a map. This is incredibly rare in 2018 and a welcome departure.\t\t\t\t\t
