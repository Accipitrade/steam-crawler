Urgency and Purpose

Those are the two words that I would use to describe my experience playing the game “Near Death”. To survive you must have a purpose for task at hand and do so with urgency. Every second spent out in the open away from a source of heat brings you another moment closer to death.

There is no need for large animals or scary monsters hunting you when the wind and the dropping temperature of the air you breath can kill you just as fast! As the game progresses the intensity of the wind literally pushes your player character around the environment as you struggle from one location to the next.

“Near Death” has great atmosphere and soundtrack. There is basic crafting and inventory management. It also forces you to read a map and the terrain. As with real life, before GPS on every phone anyway, there is no blinking dot on the map to tell you where you are. It can be easy to get lost in the near white out conditions and perish.

The only downside to this game is the relatively short time it takes to complete. Although, I say it is a short game I don’t mean it to sound like I feel cheated in any way. There is a story to follow and a mission to complete. Extending the story any longer would have detracted from the Urgency that you feel while playing.

Was it worth the cost of the game? For myself, who purchased the game during a Steam Winter sale the answer is absolutely. Is it worth the full $15? That would be harder to say. Would I still have enjoyed it as much? Without a doubt! If you aren’t convinced pick it up during the next sale.
