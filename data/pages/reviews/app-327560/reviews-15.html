\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNear Death is a very short, but very atmospheric and intense survival game. There are no monsters or enemies and the only adversary is the elements. You have to play smart, listening and watching for clues and indications of overexposure, all while finding a way out of an abandoned Antarctic facility. There isn't much in the way of dialogue, a few lines in the beginning and very end of the game, and the breathing of the protagonist (which is one of the indicators of how cold she is). The survival elements are well implemented, as in you can't warm up in a room with an open door, broken window or wall, and only buildings with power restored will provide any warmth. Heavy winds will impede your movement, so using rope polls and crouching will prevent you from staggering.

The only drawback of Near Death is it's length. You can beat the game on normal difficulty in about an hour and a half to two hours. Although I don't knock it much for it's length because it's more about the experience than anything else. For anyone who loves atmospheric or survival based games, give this one a shot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNear Death leaves you stranded at an abandoned Antarctic research station, trying to survive the brutal weather conditions as you gather the resources you need to survive and make it to safety. Atmospherically, the games works very well, your sense of isolation is made very real - you have only a teletype with which to communicate with the outside world, the base is falling apart and you can only go outside for a very short time before you need to find a warm place. So you have to plan your moves carefully and make sure you don't arrive at the next building without the necessary kit you may need to heat it, quickly.

Equipment is scarce and you have to make tough decisions about what to make with the equipment you find, crafting is a crucial part of the game. It is the only game I have played that has found me scouring buildings for a single cable tie.

The weather is well implemented: the wind will blow you around so you may have to crouch to make forward progress; visibility in the permanent night is very poor so you need tools to help you find your way around the sprawling base; and everytime you need to close an external door you have to fight to do so, failing is often the difference between thawing out or turning into a popsicle.

Overall it is an excellent, reasonably challenging game that doesn't out stay its welcome. I made it last nearly ten hours but I am slow, so your mileage may vary. I think it is worth full price not for the playing time (obvs!) but because it is a very well implemented take on the survival genre that doesn't rely on gimmicks just a reasonably authentic feeling implementation of trying to survive on a windy night at -100 degrees.\t\t\t\t\t
