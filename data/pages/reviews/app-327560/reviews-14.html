\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend this game while on sale. 15 bucks is a bit much, but that might just be my poor speaking.

Near Death turned out to actually be quite the experience. While some portions of the game will leave you a bit peeved over the experience, such as searching high and low for an entrance to any of the buildings within the arctic base, it still does manage to deliver a experience that can be quite 'chilling'. The only enemies you'll find in this game are time, the wind and the deadly chill of arctic temperatures.

Resources are relatively scarce as you go along, which is both to its benefit as well as a detraction. The random containers surrounding you can contain anything, including a fat lot of nothing whatsoever. The little kerosene lamp you start off with can be a bit infuriating in the early portion of the game, not because it isn't funcitoning, but because its fuel resource is so scarce.

The game has positively no means of guiding you. Again, both a benefit and a detraction. What they wanted to get across wasthe ease by which you will die in the arctic, however at the same time I do have to question how far you have to go to deliver this without making a game that simply isn't fun. As I started picking up on how to navigate, this became easier to deal with, but a number of times you will die just from getting lost. Panic inducing the first few times and then simply frustrated as you try to figure out what it is you're doing wrong.

I give it a thumbs up. Again, 15 bucks might have been a bit much, I'd have preferred it at 10. But I won't say it wasn't worth my money. If you're interested in a game that's just trying to give you a unique, lonely experience, then this is something I can definitely recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic game! Alone in a deserted antarctic base, its a race against plummeting temperatures and nature's undifferent fury to find a way out before you freeze to death. Despite having no supernatural monsters or crazed murderers chasing you down( a la The Thing), the game is tense and dangerous. One wrong move can have you wandering into the blizzard to die far from warmth. Its human wits and spirit against one of the most inhospitable places on the planet.

I really felt like it was a race against time to gather the needed parts in the face of exhaustion and worsening weather. They really did manage to craft a great survival narrative with only an abandoned base, a blizzard, and a teletype machine. At times I was reminded of such stories as Apollo 13 or the Martian.

Not super long at about 3 hours per run, but just the right length for the narrative, and I'm definitely going to play through it again on the higher difficulties.

Recommended!\t\t\t\t\t
