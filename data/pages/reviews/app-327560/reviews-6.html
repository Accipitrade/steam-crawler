\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA pleasant surprise when I first found this game about three years ago. It's a very simple game in objective and gameplay, but it's very focused and delivers a very tense experience.

Near Death is about trying to find your way off an abandoned Antarctic Outpost and not freezing to death. The game is short, but the experience is satisfying as you plan out your next moves before heading outside. The temperature mechanics the game relies on is very refreshing to see and handled very well. There is even a great detail put in the game where snow and condensation melts in a heated room. It also is the main danger of the game, being too cold will begin freezing you until you have a few last breaths left to find your way back inside, or die as you become lost in the antarctic darkness.

Narratively, the plot is simple, but effective. The characters have easily relatable interactions and dialogue, which is conveyed mostly through teletype machines found throughout the game. You can see characters making pauses as they type and think about what to say next, and even make a reference to a famous movie which is fittingly chosen for the situation.

One last point is that the game has a great soundtrack that conveys isolation and the coldness of Antarctica perfectly. It's one that I've been listening to quite often when doing homework or getting into a mood for solitary ambience.

A short, but very good and focused game that gives tense survival gameplay and a great, isolationist atmosphere through presentation and soundtrack. Give it a go!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNear Death is a short survival adventure game where the player is stranded after a plane crash on a abandoned research station in Antarctica during winter time. With no chance of being rescued it is left to the player to find a way to reach the nearest lived-in station.

The game is reminiscent of The Long Dark, but without any wild animals attacks or the need to drink and eat.
The only enemies in the game are the cold and the wind. The cold will kill you if you stay outside for too long. And the wind will hinder your walk making it difficult to reach a shelter or a given destination.
Another difficulty in the game is orienting yourself. You only have a general map of the research station as well as a compass, and you have to figure out by yourself where you are and how to go where you want in the dark. This aspect reminded me of Miasmata.

Crafting in the game will allow you to build tools or equipments to help you resist the cold and easier find your way outside. You will also have to fix various damaged systems. But items used for crafting are limited, something I realized too late. You will have to adequately manage when and where to use some crafted items, otherwise the last part of the game might prove quite difficult.

Graphically, the game looks really nice, and the soundtrack and sound effects convey perfectly the anguish of the player situation.

In all, it was a short but fun game. I recommend it to anyone who enjoy games with winter-like settings.\t\t\t\t\t
