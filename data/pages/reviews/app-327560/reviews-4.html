\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNear Death is by a San Francisco company Orthogonal Games http://www.orthogonalgames.com/press/index.php or more precise Kent Hudson who works for Ubisoft Toronto and has up to a decade of Game Design and Game Director experience up his sleeve. Orthogonal is his personal side company and with it, has so far produced 2 games ‘The Novelist’ and this one. Unfortunately, a while ago he reported to a fan that he has no personal plans of creating a third indie game simply due to the amount of time consumption it takes to create something which he would be proud of. In all honesty, I hope this anti-stance eventually caves. A good creator must create, must inspire, must bring into existence and Kent Hudson just has the magic vision to be able to create an exceptional experience.

In near Death you play the part of a pilot who has been forced to emergency crash land, due to severe weather, nearby an abandoned Antarctic Station called Sutro. Rescue options become void as the deadly blizzard intends to whip into the worst category. Only option remaining is for you to reclaim the station and get it back up and running, bring it back to life.

It’s the clever mechanics of the game that makes this a sheer joy. To gain warmth you need a powered and sealed room. To get power to rooms you need to fix circuit breakers hidden throughout the station. If you are standing in an unpowered sealed room then your forced to warm yourself up using a portable heater. However, you want to use this option sparingly as you only have a limited number of refillable canisters for the heater, so your best option is to find those circuit breakers and fix. However, much of the station is in bad need of repair with blown out windows and even unfix-able holes in the walls. If you come across these rooms you need to either fix the windows taping canvas across them or simply leave as there is no way your portable heater will work under these situations.

What makes this game mechanic a sheer joy and inspiringly clever is that on the easy setting you will have a more generous spawn rate of searchable materials to use to fix the station, meaning that you will be able to practically defrost the entire station and as you power it, you will also get to witness how the various station rooms transitions to life with personality. In fact, the end of the game allows you time to simply wander if you wish, fix all you can and feel good about the differences you have made and accomplished to what was once a bleak and dire setting. Yes, you get to bask at a finished and largely fixed station under the glory of a heavenly starry night and become awed at the tranquil setting of the lights from the station that seem to glitter in harmony.

On the hardest 'condition one' setting though, if you are brave enough, you will not have such a generous spawn rate of materials and will be forced to make really important life survival decisions as to which rooms are really important and a must to repair and how you can become resourceful and clever using the safe rooms you have already made safe.

Another awesome game mechanic which even trumpets your participation into being meaningful is that you will be forced to learn that not only do you need to make preparations across an arctic death trap to gain crucial material, but that you will also need to be clever and resourceful enough to prepare ahead of time for even your return journey or alas become a frozen tomb. This is done by planting out guide ropes, using a blow torch to make frozen doors work again, laying out light beacons so you can see in the fiercest of zero visibility and be prepared to abandon what you are working on and comeback especially when weight and wind are against you.

Its very seldom I ever speak lavish praise about an indie title, but I really don’t want this one to be lost, obscured by the blinding number of cold, don’t give a toss titles, that indie developers shovel out there. My only disappointment was that I wish there was an additional story about Sutro about its use and initial abandonment as each room defrosted or at least a secret room which became available with such information for those people gallant enough to work out how to survive with zero deaths in the inhospitable ‘condition one’ difficulty. A developer oversight which I am completely assured would only make a gem of a game sparkle in harmony with the heavenly night sky and the glitter of the powered lights at Sutro Station.
