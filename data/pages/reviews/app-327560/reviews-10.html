\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA rather brutal but meditative survival game where the only tracked mechanic is warmth. The HUD is minimal, and you'll only be aware of the cold's harsh effects through disorienting filters and labored movement. The one interesting hiccup is a last boost of hypothermic stamina providing a last ditch attempt at reaching safety.

With warmth as the primary threat, the broad crafting system is focused primarily on how to keep the cold out, navigate quickly through the cold, or boost warmth-producing tools and clothing. This streamlined system supports a small but labyrinthine environment, the danger of which escalates with one's lack of preparedness.

It's a brisk and simple game without a ton of complicated systems to keep track of, but this deceptively simple layout actually contains a very thoughtful resource management system. How many windows do you repair in anticipation of warming a room, do you instead insulate your pants for minimal but longer-lasting gains, do you boost the beam of your flashlight or wire power to a building to turn the heat on? And always the question, do you spend a little bit more time wandering the freezing dark to pick up a few extra supplies?

The game is pretty great for the few hours I played it. Concise, clever, and very engaging.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of those hidden gems that you find by accident, and end up having a great deal of fun and immersion - Just not damn long enough!

Extreme isolation deep in the antarctic, and hard survival that is simply executed but turns out to be nicely challenging, and in many places, rewarding.

Almost feels a bit "The Thing" ish - No monsters here though! Not even another soul within hundreds of miles. Just you, a small gas heater and an abandoned research station in the antarctic and no apparent way out - and that's really all that is needed to keep the atmosphere eerie, lonely and generally desolate.

The sense of isolation you start to get in Near Death is palpable. So much so that the intermittent teletype sessions you have with some helpful fellow hundreds of miles away in McMurdo, become a bit of a life line and tension breaker as you move from task to task.

The achievement that shoring up a compromised building to keep the cold out is also awesome, and I found myself thinking that they could actually base a whole other game around fixing up this broken down old station and turning it into a bit of a home while you wait out the long, dark winter, something to survive in and also take a bit of pride in as a base builder.

Who knows? maybe the developers will see this review one day and my wish will be realised in the form of a DLC where your main objective isn't to bug out, but to bug IN with the help of some additional content and tasks/scenarios.

Even though short in comparison to other titles out there, the game is fun and I've done a few run throughs without it feeling old, so IMO, well worth the modest price.

8/10 would get blown off a radio tower in the blinding snow again\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGrabbed this while it was on sale for super cheap and I have no regrets. It's a cute little survival game with a bit of an unsettling atmosphere - no ghosts, monsters or whacked out killers - just absolute desolation with the risk of succumbing to the hostile weather. The story line is simplistic, there is no grand backstory or character arc here, but it works well with the mechanics of the game: accumulate enough resources to explore/survive a decommissioned Antarctic research base while plotting your escape.

The puzzles are simple but resources are sparse, hence you'll have to be prudent in your choosing of which items to craft. Obviously keeping yourself warm is the first and foremost priority as even with upgraded equipment the blizzard will drop your body temperature really quickly, especially on higher difficulty. (I kinda cheesed it by cranking up the game's brightness so my flashlight wouldn't eat through batteries, leaving me to wander around lost in the dark while freezing to death.) Also, if you're an achievement junkie like myself, reloading saves to snap up the rope and light pole cheevos works like a charm.

Think of it as a watered down version of The Long Dark.

*Edited for transparency sake to state that my hours played are bloated because I idled it for card drops before actually playing it. Seems like the average playtime is approximately 3-4 hours. Also, it's now less than half of its initial release price but I still advise to get it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
