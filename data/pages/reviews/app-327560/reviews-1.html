FOREWORD:

Ok, I feel this is the best way to play this game:\u2028
>Wait until winter.\u2028>Put on some warm clothes.\u2028>Make some hot chocolate or your hot drink of choice.
>Play the game.\u2028
OVERALL: \u2028
It’s a nice short run, I’d say worth about $6.

This is a survival game that has you battle against, not an alien or zombie, but a true enemy of mankind’s history: lack of warmth.

Now, if you have never battled against extremely low outdoor temperatures, this game may not have the same effect on you as it did me. And that’s the best part about the game, in my opinion. I’d be VERY surprised if the developers themselves have not been to Antartica… or North Dakota. That is what the game feels like to me: a developer experienced very low temperatures once and was very inspired to invite others to feel what he/she did through a short simulation. And it was pretty decent!

GAMEPLAY:

Good ol’ survival game kleptomaniac item-seeking with crafting system. When you step outside any area that’s exposed to the cold, you have limited time until you can find refuge - enclosed spaces are your friends.

I personally hate the survival game mechanic of “event A happened, go do task A. Event B happens, go do task B” type of chore-running, and what I would like to say is that Alien Isolation did that with a multimillion dollar budget, while THIS game DID NOT do that with a, what, $2000 budget likely spent on outsourced codes and some assets, if that?

You do run from point A to B and C to take care of tasks, but it feels perfectly logical. You’re in the middle of nowhere, no one can come in time to help you. To quote the game, you’re burning many calories “just to talk”. Whatever you need to do to survive does not feel unnecessary or tedious at all. It feels like what you would really need to do in that situation, and you feel just as vulnerable.

Little replay value.

STORY:

It's pretty straightforward, no surprises. You're a woman trying to survive in Antartica.

MUSIC AND ATMOSPHERE:

While not the most memorable, the music and atmosphere do exactly what they’re supposed to do. The world feels hostile, stormy and violent, yet silent and calm. Every time you step outside, you feel it. Every time you step into a cozy space, you feel it. That’s all I would ask from this type of game.


.... dude, isn't the cold amazing? It's just a lack of molecular movement, little energy - yet it kills you! That's badass.\t\t\t\t\t
