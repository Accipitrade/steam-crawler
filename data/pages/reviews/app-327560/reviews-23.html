\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished it. If you're into well-crafted survival fantasy, this is a great solo experience.

Near Death is small - it was made by a small team. But it's very good, inside of that box. Despite the way it feels when you start, it's more than a traditional "level design" based game. Puzzles are fodder for ciritcal thinking and there are a number of solutions to the main conflicts in the game - cold, directionality, time. It feels less like a game you "beat" and more like a game that's been crafted for you to make your own story within.

What could be better? The world is a little dead - which mostly works in the setting, but when you come upon something flapping in the wind it becomes clear how much more movelent it could have. And the objectives are almost all linear. I'd love to see an update that lets you take on more objectives in your prefferred order, so you can optimize. That'd enable speed runs & might get some pretty tense let's plays up.\t\t\t\t\t

DISCLAIMER: This is a full review, and NOT a first impressions review

Near Death is the next game from the developers of The Novelist, developed and published by Orthogonal Games

Buy this game on sale if you can, but only buy it for the full 15 EUR if you want to support the developers. This is a good game. It was a very immersive, very tense, and harrowing journey into the Antarctic wasteland

Near Death takes homage from John Carpenter's The Thing, however, there aren't any enemies in this game, aside from the frigid weather of Antarctica in the middle of a raging blizzard

The survival aspect in this game is really something. When you're outside for too long, and you begin to succumb to the -100C kind of cold, you genuinely feel relieved when you make it to a spot to warm up

You have to use your supplies wisely and always make sure you keep warm

This game does not hold your hand. There are no quest markers. All you get is a map, and it doesn't even show you where you are on the map

You have to use common sense to navigate your way through the blizzard to the many different buildings of the abandoned Sutro Station, which has fallen into disrepair. You need to place light posts in the ground to mark paths to different buildings

You need to make a rope trail between buildings so you don't get staggered by the strong, freezing winds. You need to make use of the crafting system by collecting supplies wherever you can in order to survive

Stay warm. Collect supplies. Jury rig said supplies into things you can actually make use of. Just keep going, and try to survive

If you want a deep story, look somewhere else. If you want a ton of content, look somewhere else. If you're looking to slay monsters and collect epic loot, look somewhere else. But if you're looking for a fun, challenging, and actually immersive survival experience, then this might be the game for you

I can honestly see myself playing it again on harder difficulties so I can get the rest of the achievements from the game and also because it's fun!

RECOMMENDED :)

Really nice atmosphere, really nice gameplay and ideas.

For me it was a $10 games, that play through lasted a day session and an hour after..
good price for the length,

worth playing. actually one of a few games i remember fondly.\t\t\t\t\t
