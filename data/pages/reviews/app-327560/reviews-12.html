The graphics may not be fantastic but the combination of the audio, pacing and lighting really draws you in. Also, the story, whilst being uncomplicated does the job quite well, keeping the player going through the tasks. Another point of note is how well the voice acting is performed, which is surprising for such a small title.

It can be argued that it can be difficult to understand where to go. It seems to me this is intentional, as it is really amazingly to get lost in the snow and blizzard. It all adds to the tension\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNear death isn't so much of a survival game, as a walking simulator where you freeze to death if you don't get to a closed off room with heat.

The game starts with you crash landing near a deserted base in Antarctica, where you are then tasked with walking between the buildings, restoring power, and collecting items to help you escape.
Collecting Kerosene canisters is of upmost importance as it is largely the source of heat through most of the game. Using it wisely is the difference between running out and dying and making it to the next area/restoring power.
Getting warm can only be accomplished by being in a closed off room with the kerosene heater on next to you or the power to the building turned on. If those conditions are not met you will slowly freeze to death. This is signaled by audible shivers, exclamations of how cold it is, and eventually the screen turning black and white with the ability to sprint removed. You are generally fully warm when the character says "ah, that's better."
There is a crafting system, but mostly it's just covers for windows, repairs for turning on power to the various buildings, and a couple upgrades.
Once you have restored power, and checked the food and water stores at the base, the rest of the game is going to the rest of the buildings and bringing back several items to the garage.

Most of the game is dark interiors and a raging blizzard outside. The visuals are understandably dark and dreary, so it's easy to forgive them not being exceptional.

The sound is largely just howling wind, so not much to say about it.

Near Death is all about staying warm and collecting supplies from around the Antarctic base. It's short enough that instead of getting bored of the fetch quests you actually wish there was more.

6/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great realistic experience! I started out playing on normal difficulty but ended up going to easy. My only complaint there was that you seem to freeze to death in just a couple of minutes and it was really hard to navigate that fast. You have a map but there is no indicator on it telling you where you are, so its very easy to get lost in the snow storm. You do have a compass though which saves your life. I like that aspect, more realism. This takes place back in the early 80s if I remember right, so no gps available to the general public then.

Took me about 4 1/2 hrs to complete the game. Once completed you could go back and do it again under more difficulty, there are harder settings. The controls are pretty good in this and it runs solid, no crashes or lagging.

The graphics are very good in this and you really feel like you are out stuck in a big snow storm. You have to salvage parts to fix things. One example. you patch up windows so that the room can stay warm when you heat it or once you have established power in that building. There are big heavy things you have to carry a long way in the storm, and those parts are quite tense!

If you like great atmosphere and that feeling like you are all alone stranded in the middle of nowhere then this is for you! It is a definite challenge. I had originally set my gamma really low but ended turning that up some because i couldnt see a thing! Turned up some the game became more realistic. The world they created here is beautiful yet deadly.
