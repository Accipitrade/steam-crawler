Life is Strange 2: Wastelands is a third episode of the interactive adventure game, where we get the chance to see what happens to Diaz brothers next.


Pros:

+ Amazing cozy atmosphere
+ An emotional story that is interesting to follow
+ Beautiful soundtrack
+ Colorful graphics
+ Serious topics

Cons:

- The whole episode can feel a bit dependent on actual problems of nowadays life and society
- Pretty predictable scenes and outcome of the episode

The second episode showed us the real potential of this game. The developers focused more on the Diaz brothers' relationships instead of pushing the political aspect and harmoniously entered Captain Spirit into the story. By the end of that episode, it was clear enough that the whole story will dramatically lead us to the not-so-happy ending.

Episode three with a very symbolical name Wastelands shows us the continuation of the story and brothers' attempt to save some money and finally leave the country for good. This time, the developers show us a very dark side of the refugees’ life and young criminals’ way to earn some cash.

I could clearly feel how Dontnod tries to follow modern trends and they are not afraid to talk about LGBT, equality and other social nowadays problems.

Story

Diaz brothers have to face the cruel reality, where they have no other choice rather than facing drug dealers and helping them trimming the weed to get money for the new life. During this time, Sean continues to teach Daniel how to control his power and how it’s important to keep it low profile. However, it’s not easy to explain such things to nine years old kid.

Gameplay

In this episode, Diaz brothers are focused on saving the money for the Mexico trip and to do so they joined the dubious group of people. These people are basically some refugees, hippies, young men with no future who work on the weed plantation, trimming weed plants.

From the very first moments of the episode, the player can see that Sean has a bad time with Daniel, who acts as a true nine years old kid and refuses to listen to his older brother. He is the main reason, why Sean is getting lectured by the weed plantation's owner - a serious black guy with a shotgun, someone you don’t want to mess around with.

In general, the whole episode feels pretty lazy, there are only two locations and not so much stuff to explore/interact with. On top of that, the player faces weird conflicts between the characters in the camp and most of these fights are completely far-fetched. Most part of the time, characters show us how bored they are deliberately yawning during campfires or just hanging around the camp killing time and asking useless questions.

The episode tries to reveal us relationships between two brothers even more based on the typical conflict between relatives, but it looks a bit weird and not natural at all. Their conflict goes across the whole episode while characters continue to do chores around the camp, trimming weed on the plantation, talking about their plans and sharing personal dreams and experiences. And of course, let’s remember that we are dealing with Life is Strange game, where the player has to make choices, that unfortunately never really affect the story in a major way in this episode. It means that the ending of the episode will be pretty much the same, the only difference will be with the characters that will remain in the story or not. The sad thing is that these characters never feel like they are the part of the story, they look just like some extras that nobody cares about.

At the same time, the third episode follows pretty much interesting formula called Chekhov's gun and in this case, I mean it textually. There is one scene at the beginning of the episode that lets us understand, or better say predict what is going to happen in the end. Can’t say either it’s a good thing or not, but what I really didn’t like about it is that developers never gave me a chance to avoid it or change the situation.

Despite everything that has been said before, I truly enjoyed the episode. It is still the same Life is Strange game with its vibes, unique atmosphere, remarkable music on the background and that special mood set in the game that really makes your day. The way Dontnod makes you empathize Diaz brothers and feel like you are eager to see what happens to them next in this stalemate is simply brilliant. This three hours of the episode felt like three minutes to me and now I will desperately wait for the next episode.

Graphics

Graphics remain the same in the third episode and it still has that cozy warm atmosphere, where you wish to stay for as long as possible because it’s completely charming. The way how they portrayed the forest with its beautiful woods and night lake is absolutely breathtaking.

Sound

Life is Strange was always the game that could steal your heart with their soundtrack and perfectly picked songs for the right moments in the game. The episode starts with one of my favorite songs Gorillaz - On Melancholy Hill, but then they added Justice - D.A.N.C.E song and it was very weird and unexpected to hear such song in this game, I was incredibly surprised!

Conclusion

Life Is Strange 2: Wastelands continues the story about Diaz brothers in a bit unusual way, trying to talk about serious nowadays problems and trends that are really important for the majority of people in 2019. It ends with a powerful cliffhanger making you want to know what happens to our beloved brothers next and this is probably the hardest thing for the fans of the game.

Enjoy!

Regardless of your choices, Daniel:

Kills a Cop
Steals a puppy
Trashes a motel room
Tricks a kid into thinking he can fly
Gets you fired
Tries to rob a drug dealer
Blinds you in one eye
Joins a cult
Throws you into furniture after waking up from a coma
Watches a guy beat you half to death with a gun
Expects you to take the blame for all of this
Sure, he's 10 years old. But at some point, you have to wonder why you didn't talk to the FBI.
Developers try to use "narration through the environment" and they think that just throwing dozens of useless items on the levels with commentaries is enough to dive into the story. Guess what, it's not! The funny thing is that in the first game that thing worked extremely well because it was skillfully combined with...you know...THE PLOT! And it meant something there, we could find some extra information about people around us, especially in the second episode with Kate. Here, it's just useless garbage that doesn't bring anything to my understanding of the universe, characters or events. Nothing changes if you miss all this fluff.
Daniel tells us that Shawn spends too much time with his new friends, but the game DOESN'T SHOW IT TO ME! In the second episode, Daniel says how much fun they had with Mushroom and DOESN'T SHOW IT TO ME. It's all somewhere there behind the curtain.

That weed cutting...Seriously, who thought that it would be a great idea? You just sit and press two freaking buttons one after another for 5-10 minutes straight! AND EVEN IF YOU MISS you don't get any limits or penalty, IT JUST STARTS ALL OVER. Who decided to add this "extremely engaging and breathtaking gameplay" here? This scene could be easily replaced with a cutscene, but, apparently, developers noticed that gameplay in this 3-hour-long episode lasts like 30 minutes and desperately tried to add anything there. Anything at all. What a waste of resources and time, really.

Daniel and Shawn are NOT characters I can sympathize with. You know why? Because they are DUMB! And we could clearly see that in the first two episodes. Partly including the third one.

Okay, I understand that I'm not looking at English tea club members, but there is TOO much swearing here. Literally in a sentence that consists of 10 words 3 of them can be swearing. Are you ******* serious?

Even cool music and great camera angles that were present in previous games are gone! I mean, yeah, music is a subjective topic, I get it.

I could go on and on, really (I mean it). And maybe someday I'll make a direct comparison of LiS and LiS2, just to be shocked how an interesting and mysterious adventure turned into a story about idiotic characters who are digging their own grave over and over and after that moan about this unfair world. I'm really disappointed I can't return my money back. I wish only good luck to developers and hope that they understand that there is still a chance to make something better. All in all...what a WASTEland...







Os desenvolvedores tentam usar "narração através do ambiente" e acham que apenas jogar dezenas de itens inúteis nos níveis com comentários é suficiente para mergulhar na história. Adivinhe, não é! O engraçado é que no primeiro jogo essa coisa funcionou muito bem porque foi habilmente combinada com ... você sabe ... O LOTE! E isso significou algo lá, nós poderíamos achar alguma informação extra sobre as pessoas ao redor de nós, especialmente no segundo episódio com Kate. Aqui, é apenas lixo inútil que não traz nada ao meu entendimento do universo, personagens ou eventos. Nada muda se você perder toda essa penugem.
Daniel nos diz que Shawn gasta muito tempo com seus novos amigos, mas o jogo não mostra isso para mim! No segundo episódio, Daniel diz o quanto eles se divertiram com o Mushroom e NÃO ME MOSTRAMM. Está tudo em algum lugar atrás da cortina.

Que corte de maconha ... Sério, quem achou que seria uma ótima ideia? Você apenas senta e aperta dois malditos botões um após o outro por 5-10 minutos seguidos! E mesmo se você perder você não tem nenhum limite ou penalidade, tudo começa completamente. Quem decidiu adicionar esta "jogabilidade extremamente envolvente e de tirar o fôlego" aqui? Essa cena poderia ser facilmente substituída por uma cena, mas, aparentemente, os desenvolvedores perceberam que a jogabilidade nesse episódio de 3 horas dura 30 minutos e tenta desesperadamente adicionar alguma coisa lá. Nada mesmo.

Daniel e Shawn não são personagens que eu possa simpatizar. Você sabe porque? Porque eles são DUMB! E podemos ver claramente isso nos dois primeiros episódios. Em parte, incluindo o terceiro.

Ok, eu entendo que eu não estou olhando para membros do clube de chá Inglês, mas há muito palavrões aqui. Literalmente, em uma frase que consiste em 10 palavras, 3 delas podem ser palavrões. Você está falando sério?

Mesmo música legal e ótimos ângulos de câmera que estavam presentes nos jogos anteriores se foram! Quero dizer, sim, a música é um assunto subjetivo, eu entendo.

Eu poderia continuar, realmente (quero dizer isso). E talvez algum dia eu faça uma comparação direta de LiS e LiS2, apenas para ficar chocado como uma aventura interessante e misteriosa se transformou em uma história sobre personagens idiotas que estão cavando seu próprio túmulo várias vezes e depois que lamentam sobre esse mundo injusto. Estou muito desapontado por não poder devolver meu dinheiro. Desejo apenas boa sorte aos desenvolvedores e espero que eles entendam que ainda há uma chance de fazer algo melhor. Tudo em tudo ... o que é um WASTEland ... Collapse\t\t\t\t\t
