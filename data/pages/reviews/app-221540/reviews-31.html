\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt took me a while to figure out why I didn't like this game anywhere near as much as I did the first one. It finally dawned on me the other day that it was the script.

Not the plot. The plot is standard B+ sci-fi fare, and is fine. The script however has a major issue.

In the first game it was you and your AI partner fighting to save the world and every interaction deeply involved you and made you feel like you were a pivotal part of what was happening.

By contrast the dialogue in this game is mostly just you overhearing a group of slightly crazy computers talking to each other about what they're going to do if they ever figure out what's going on. It's rare that you're involved or even referenced in any meaningful way and compared to the 'forced-partnership-evolving-into-strong-friendship' story-line of the first game it leaves the experience feeling rather hollow. You no longer feel integral to the process the way that you should.

It also doesn't help that the graphics somehow feel like a step backwards from the first game. The towers now look rickety and gone are the truly alien-looking insectoid cyborgs from the first game. This time we're fighting slightly blobby...animals? The design is uninspired and I'd rather just have the aliens from the first game back again, thank you.

There is fun to be had here and it is still, for the most part, a well put together tower defense game. It just lacks the charm and atmosphere of its predecessor. If you're going to buy one tower defense game make sure it's the first Defense Grid. If you're going to by two, then this one is...fine? It expands on the story from the first one although it feels less compelling and actually slightly ruins a bit of character development from the original but it's interesting enough.

If you like tower defense games in general this is a decent game. If not I would avoid Defense Grid 2.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty good game, but I highly recommend the first Defense Grid game (with DLC) over this one. DG2 feels like an expansion more than a sequel and really does not advance the gameplay in any meaningful way. It has a few more options, and the graphics have been slightly upgraded. But the campaign is entirely forgettable, and the chatter of the voice actors gets distracting after a while.

Do yourself a favor and get the first Defense Grid game. It's just seems more immerse and better put together. DG2 is not a bad game by any means; just not an improvement over the original, which is really a classic.\t\t\t\t\t
