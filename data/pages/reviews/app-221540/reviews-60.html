It's obvious that this game was designed for single player and added in multiplayer as an after thought. Not only after a story multiplayer mission does it not continue the story, your only two options are to exit and recreate a lobby to select the Next mission or go back to select the Next Mission Anyway rather than the typical "Continue" type mechanic. What the hell type of thinking is this?

There's no reason to play Multiplayer. None. Nothing.
You share resources in multiplayer. They are Completely shared. It turns into two different people creating something together to one person directing where things go and the 2nd player sitting along for the ride. It is Ridiculous.

For example the tooltips still pop up for the Fast Forward feature which is a Single Player only option.


This is lazy game designing and should be marketed as a single player game. It *might* be decent in the single player aspect but I Still find the gameplay itself boring and horrendous. There was a CLEAR lack of effort in this god awful game and I strongly urge ANYONE looking to purchase this to rethink their decision. With quality like this you're Much better off investing in a different game or saving your money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, so first of all: I am a big fan of DG 1 and I like DG 2. There. This is a review to dispel some of the wrong and inaccurate information in many negative reviews, that almost made me NOT buy the game!

I am currently at Level 6 out of 20, so I have completed 1/4 of the game. This gives me enough information to write a first review. Here are some of the main complaints and what I think about them:

1. People complaining that the game is too easy and everyone can beat it on gold and that they can't show off their virtual penises and don't know how to distinguish themselves and increase their score.

A: No, the game is NOT too easy. There are 4 difficulty modes. I play on normal. Normal is pretty relaxed so far. There are 2 difficulties above that. I imagine they can be pretty hard. You also have additional challenge modes. You should not get bored unless you're maybe extremely good, but the average john will be challenged with normal alone.

Your score is calculated whenever you kill an enemy alien. The score depends on the alien, how much money you have when you kill the alien and how many cores you still possess while killing said alien, while floating cores (the ones not at your core station) count only partially. Only cores that have been taken out of the map do not count at all. Apparently, the scoring system works very similarly to DG 1, but in real time. So this point of criticism is not valid in my opinion.

2. People complaining about the resource system and that they have removed interest.

A: This is technically not correct. You now receive a fixed income rate per "tick", namely 10, that is true. At the end of a round or every second round (I have to admit I haven't exactly figured that out) you get bonus money based on how much money you have NOT yet spent, meaning the more money you have available the bigger the bonus, i.e. interest. This is explained in the 3rd mission I think. People need to read and pay attention and not provide misinformation (almost every negative review that I read had this issue). So, not only does it pay off to play conservatively in terms of score, but also in terms of money and thus longevity/sustainability. Edit: I forgot to mention that you do no longer gain credits by killing aliens, that's true and it didn't even occur to me...that's bs. That's also why the command tower is gone I think.

3. People complaining about how horrible the UI is.

A: I have no idea what they are complaining about. People just complain. The UI is perfectly fine. You can get all the required information, it is simple to use etc. You can activate paths, you can show tower impact ranges, building and upgrading towers is as easy or easier than before AND you can play the game with a game pad just as well as with mouse and keyboard, which is brilliant. I personally am a mouse player primarily but I like to use the controller for this, because I can and its easy to use. (Update: Just checked and you can also use it in DG 1 but it is slower. Works fine too, however.)

4. People complaining about the graphics and style.

A: I have to agree here that DG 1 simply had clearer visuals. They tried too much with DG 2. It basically still looks the same, but with a lot more clutter and effects. Aliens and towers are harder to distinguish, although Id say towers can are still easy to distinguish. Aliens on the other hand feel a bit bland, but mostly I can still see what Im dealing with. The UI also tells you in the wave information. Personally I wish they had not changed the engine. It didnt make a lot of sense and probably cost a lot of money. Its not ugly, however.

5. People complaining about the performance.

A: Personally I couldnt see any real lag, even with larger alien crowds. Maybe this happens later on. I did notice, however, that the game seems a bit sluggish in general.

6. People complaining about the sound.

A: This is unfortunately absolutely correct. The gun towers simply drown out all other sounds. You could barely hear them in DG 1, but in DG 2 one or 2 gun towers feel like a Manowar concert. You can still hear most other towers if you are in the vicinity, but especially the concussion and the cannon tower are basically silent towers, which is very sad, at least in the cannon tower case, because that oomph rocked! I always did hate the concussion tower sound.

7. People complaining about the unimaginative level design.

A: Cant really comment on that. So far the first 5 missions were pretty basic in design. You have to keep in mind, though, that this is not an expansion, but a new game, thus early levels must be simple to have a good learning curve for new players. Will see how it will be later on.

8. People complaining about the story and the AI chatter.

A: The story iswell, its a TD. Who cares, really? I do partially. Fletcher is still great. The others are ok. Before each level you get glimpses into the aliens mind setthis seems interesting. So far the chatter has been ok. I think you can also just disable the sound for that? Not sure. Just waiting for the raspberries!

9. People complaining about the workshop integration.

A: This is actually a pretty strong negative point for the game. Apparently you can vote for maps in the workshop, which then become available on the official DG store. I checked and one map seems to cost TWO dollars!!! TWO. Not only is it ridiculous to sell them, but the price is so over the top it is not even funny anymore. If you value community created content, this might be the point for you to NOT purchase the game.

Now some general info:

-\tAliens: There are a few new ones. Some that regen health. Nothing too interesting. Im not sure if they took some out. Didnt look like it, but could be. All in all, pretty boring stuff if you played DG 1.
-\tTowers: The tower encyclopedia in game does not list ANY new tower at all. In fact, the command tower is now missingI think thats the only 1 though.
-\tBoost blocks: You can build these cheap blocks to create mazes and put your towers on to give them additional effects like increased damage or cloak detectetc. The concept is ok, the execution ismeh. You basically want to build them for every tower, because they only cost 50 credits and without them a tower can simply do 20%!!!!! Less damage. That is huge. Ofc, the upgrade costs money, but still20% more damage for 250 credits? Still a good deal. Plus you get extended range and fire arcs. I dont particularly like it. It is a bit clunky.
-\tTower bonuses: You unlock bonus abilities for your towers, which you can choose before a mission. For example, my laser tower currently does 15% splash. This is a nice addition, but they could have added more interesting effects. Maybe there will be better ones later, but I somehow doubt it.
-\tDifferent commander bonuses: You can now choose a special power at the start of a game. No longer just orbital laser.

All in all I would say that DG 2 is a good TD game. IF you have played DG 1 you might find that better in some aspects. It also has more maps to play on. IF you have NOT played DG 1get DG 1 lol. No, seriously. DG 2 is not a bad game, but DG 1 is better imho. It is not as bad as people make it out to be and I will recommend it, but ONLY at a severely reduced price. Right now, it is 66% offso only 8 euros or so, which is ok to get a few hours of new tower defense stuff.
Overall, however, the changes and additions, wherever they are, do not warrant a full priceand the community map selling is just plain evil. The creators probably get some small amount for each purchased map, but if you are a map creator and doing this for the money, youre doing it wrong. I loved creating Star Craft Maps back in the dayit would have never occurred to me to charge anything for thatpffft.\t\t\t\t\t
