\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is decent but feels incomplete. For someone that never got to play the original the story doesn't really make sense. There is no recap or refresher as to what happened in the first game but if all you care about is the killing then...well you don't care. Furthering the story issues the dialog pacing is terrible. Voice acting is decent and the writing fair, it's just lines aren't triggered fluently and often times the story dialog takes place at terrible times leaving you missing things or unless you adjust the volumes the sounds of hundreds dying drowns things out.

Various other things also don't feel fully fleshed out like tower info and upgrade details. Rules about vertical planes and how it affects a given towers range and ability to fight are not explained. Line of sight issues, some times the specials you get from commanders do not work other than to show the visuals, play the sounds and incur the cool down, but the actual effect is no where to be found.

My primary complaint is the aforementioned feeling this is only half a game. It's incredibly short and while fun it feels less compelling than some online / free flash tower defenses. If this game was $12 I'd say grab it but I don't quite feel it's worth the $25. This isn't to spark some large debate over development / production efforts against asking price but rather I think the game could have been presented in a much simpler way that would have required less development cost and time, less system resources for potential buyers, and an over all better experience. Next time maybe they could consider leaving out gimmicks like the zoom. There is nothing great looking about anything and zooming in doesn't help with anything. In fact it makes things very difficult. If you want amazing looking you play games like Crysis. The 3D modeling and effort put into this game just seems like a waste when they could have made something just as fun as a 2D top down game and spent their time and effort on $25 worth of game rather than bland 3D modeling.

In addition to the very short nature of this game I thought the level builder and community map making would give it more value and replay-ability but sadly with far too many games these days there is a catch. Basically this game is another micro transaction ♥♥♥♥♥. Community maps must be purchased meaning the replay value that might have given its price some worth is more like a lure to soak you for more cash. Frankly this makes me really mad. I thought I was buying a game, not a platform for them to sell me more ♥♥♥♥. It's like paying to have some ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ salesman come into your home and try to tempt you to buy more ♥♥♥♥ all day. I get it, companies need to recoup costs, pay bills and their employees need to eat, but this has become a massive (and dishonest) practice in gaming these days. We used to buy triple A titles for say $60 but now you pay $60 and then they nickle and dime you until that $60 game is $300. Sure Defense Grid is $25ish, but to get more than a few hours play you have to start shelling out $2 a level with no real limit to how much you could end up spending. They also know psychologically it's easy to get someone to dump $2 300 times over a few months than to say upfront the game costs $250 or something else equally insane.

Frankly my initial impression overlooked the story problems and I had fun until I realized nothing really made sense, there are still lots of problems, the game is incredibly short and the real dirty trick is they made the game off the backs of the fans, then they monetized the efforts of the fans creations for themselves too!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA major downgrade from DG1 in terms of depth.

  1. They cut the resource interest system and put in it's place a purely by wave reward so you're encouraged to spam towers.
  2. There's very little alien variety, all of which look exactly the same, it's seriously hard to discern them outside of the next wave panel
  3. There's very little change in strategy in different maps, just place a bunch of AoE towers in a funnel with a temporal and you can complete most levels like this.
  4. Complete removal of flying waves/aliens.

The developers are also trying to sell you any community-made content, all of the workshop content is locked until approval by the devs to be sold in the game.

If you want a great tower defense game, wait for a sale and pick up DG1 and its DLC, it is superior in every way to this game except for the graphics. (Although it has better visual design where everything is much easier to discern)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this new version. Big, big fan of the first DG, and now a fan of DG2 as well. It's not radically different from DG1, and thats a plus. Yet there are significant improvements like the rotating perspective, new towers and attackers, more gameplay variations for each map, ammo variations, larger maps, etc... All with nice, fluid gameplay and no lagging. I am using a mediocre AMD APU grapic processor with only 512mb dedicated ram and I have it set to 'Very high' graphics..

Intially I had noted that I didn't want all the A.I. chatter, but I have since found that I could turn them off, turn down the frequency or just lower the A.I. volume in the settings. Nice. People at HP were thinking proactively.

This promises to be a well spent 25 bucks. If you liked DG1 or TD games in general, buy it. For those not already familiar with DG, think of traditional Tower Defense games in general, but with everything bumped up several generations using a gaming engine (Gamebryo) used by some of the larger and more expensive game titles found on Steam.

As time goes by, I am enjoying this more and more. Becoming a DG addict all over again.\t\t\t\t\t
