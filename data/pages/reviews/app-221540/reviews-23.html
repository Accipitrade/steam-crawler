\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSetting the bar (bit full of ourselves, aren't we?) as the definitive tower (about 10 variants) defense game, Defense Grid 2 introduces new worlds (5 or them, like it matters. Only visual variations) and threats (fast/slow, shielded/armored and a few creative ones) to test your tower placement (and on some maps, path creation) strategies. With a bold new look (compared to DG1, I guess - which I haven't played) and the addition of new game modes (definitely more modes than you count - I stuck to the basics), player-versus-player (nope, don't care) and multiplayer co-op (how does that even make sense), every play brings (the ever same) fun new opportunities (what?) and challenges (find the spot for the right tower).

There is some multilayered story in the background going on which I guess would be easier to follow if I had played the first. I haven't - I was never really sure what the scenario was. Not that is matters. Build tower, kill endless stream of stupid aliens. Just don't build a wall where it would block their path. That would be very unsportsmanlike\t\t\t\t\t
