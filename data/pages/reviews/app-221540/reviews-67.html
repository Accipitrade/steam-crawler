\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: It's just as addictive as the original Defense Grid.

I've played 83 hours of the original Defense Grid. No, it did not have 83 hours of content, but with the original campaign and several DLC packs we were given room to play with. And tweak, fiddle and generally mess around with what was possible. With all the different game modes, you could play each map dozens of times. DG2 gives us all that and more.

What's different? - Shiny new graphics! More voiceover artists! New aliens!

The only "new" tower is the Boost Tower, which goes under other towers. This makes whatever you build on top taller, and you can spend resources to activate a Boost Tower special ability: 25% damage bonus, cloak disruptor, score enhancement, etc. This makes command towers obsolete.

The other tower change is the missile tower, which now also shoots ground targets.

There's been some tweaking in the damage outputs, so that guns aren't always the most efficient option.

Is it worth the price? - I was a little unsure about $25, but I've been totally sucked in by DG2. Music, graphics, gameplay are all great. There's even a plot this time. So yes, it's worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just loaded up the original game to make sure I wasn't going mad... The original is fantastic, I got a lot of joy out of it.

DG2 on the other hand is absymal:

* Graphics - Surely the plan was for them to be better than the original? Subjective maybe, but they arn't in my opinion
* Lagging/frame rate - This game hasn't been optimized... I wouldn't be surprised if this is improved upon in the future but not at time of full release
* Story - I didn't mind General Fletcher so much in the first game, but now he's got his friends with him they get on your nerves pretty quickly.
* Interface - unnecessarily confusing and cluttered.

If you've never played DG:Awakening - buy it! It's a great game. If you have and you liked it, and feel this will be as good - it's not, at least not in it's current state.

Seriously, I can't see anything that has been imprvoed upon - the towers look rubbish compared to the originals, the boosts are a boring addition... I wish they took the original engine, added some new towers and aliens, some new maps - I'd have happily paid for it. Why reinvent the wheel - and make it a bit more square?

4/10\t\t\t\t\t

Now to DG2:
- menus and GUI are plain horrible (try it and see it)
- resources raise at 10/tick, regardless if you have 0 or 5000 - what's up with interest and its tactical value? (here I have to say, maybe i haven't played long enough to get to that point and it is in game later on, but not at beginning (it was in DG1))
- graphics: its really ugly. Screenshots were beautiful, game itself is not. It makes you believe screenshots are art-concepts and not from within actual game, cuz those few colorful pixels moving around on your screen are far from what screenshots advertise. Further, the game itself feels "wrong". I don't know how to explain better, but movement of towers building, of towers upgrading is just "cheap". DG1 had it done superbly, each upgrade of tower took exactly the same amount of time and you knew exactly when it will collapse and come back up as stronger version. I don't doubt that timing-wise here it's the same, but the way it is animated? It feels cheap and wrong.

Having said that I played 3 missions, scored 3 golden medals and what I am still wondering about (after I closed the game as I had enough of dissapointment) is - how I could NOT get them, since i killed all aliens and kept all cores. Where is the difference between my score and score of other players if all aliens are killed, game lasted same duration (all waves) and we got same amount of "resource ticks". Where does the difference appear?

And even positive posters wrote about stupid secondary tower abilities upgrade system and getting mk2 type abilities over mk1 in total random fashion.

All in all what I grasped in 36 minutes of playing is that this game appears to be colorful cluster♥♥♥♥ of pixels made for 10 years olds who get sick of repetitive stuff and challenges in 10 minutes, having lots of extra abilities compared to dg1 (secondary tower abilities, colorful-yet-ugly tower animations and circles and generals to help with) and it has absolutely none of old DG1 charm.

I'm going back to DG1...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor me DG 1 was pretty much perfection. The GFX was clear and minimalistic. The aliens and towers very distinctive. It was a game with enourmes replay value due to clever mechanics and good expansions.

The good news is that it`s still Defense Grid - which means a quality game with good mechanics, and loads of different game types based on the campaign maps.

The bad news is that they made some changes to the flow and the mechanics of the game. They removed or cut "juggling enemies" from the game which is a massive mistake imho. It`s one of the core mechanics that made DG 1 stand out against other tower defense games. (I assume it will be reintroduced in some future DLC)

They`ve made some changes to the towers as well. Most of the towers are pretty much identical to DF 1 when it comes to actual mechanics despite the new paint job. Command towers are gone though, but their functionailty is now tied into a new "tower unit" - the "boost tower" which gives increased heigth and various custombuild options, so you can have a gun tower with increased damage or anti stealth - that sort of thing. Missile towers can now target all units, so it`s no longer an exclusive anti aircraft tower. (I haven`t seen too many aircraft so far)

There are also more commanders this time and each commander have different "command powers". This just means than in addition to the orbital laser (smart bomb) you get alternatives - like increased tower damage and that sort of thing. It`s a bit like the command powers from Advance Wars.

I haven`t finished the game yet, and hopefully it`ll last me months and months like DG 1 did, but I`ve played enough to have an opinion on the game. To me it feels like a slightly more clunky game with slightly more sterile GFX and aliens. It feels like there`s a bigger emphasis on visual deception. You spend a lot more time on figuring out what`s going on. What parts of the map are background and what parts are actually important. It`s not always clear what range your tower has or if it`s even on the right altitude. To me that`s the wrong sort of complexity. I prefer the visuals and the design to be clear, so you can concentrate on what`s important - placing the towers, juggling the aliens and so on.

In the end it`s the difference between a perfect 10 (DG 1) and a score of somewhere around 8-9 which only prolonged play will reveal. I consider 5 to be average, so still a good game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you liked DG1, then you'll like this. If you like tower defence, then you'll probably like this.
+Boost towers are great and allow for in-depth defence (although most levels don't use it) and nice buffs
+Missiles can now attack ground units
+Still the best example of Tower Defence done right
+New alien types spice the gameplay a fair bit, and make you rethink your strategy
+Ability to expand build spaces in levels was nice
+One level where the playing space expanded halfway through was awesome, but it wasn't done again
+Fletcher is great as always
=Removed flying enemies. Flying enemies didn't add too much anyway, so this isn't a big loss
-Boost Tower's Disruptor ability renders Tesla Towers pretty much useless. You don't need them as anti-shields when Boosts completely remove shields in range
-Missiles render Cannons useless. They deal more damage faster, and have far better range. The only downside is they're more expensive, but that rarely matters anyway
-The UI isn't great, somewhat buggy, and doesn't look very good compared to DG1
-Graphics settings are just a single drop down box, instead of splitting them up
-Too many characters which don't add much to the story.\t\t\t\t\t
