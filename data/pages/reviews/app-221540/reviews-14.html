\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMore fantastic tower defence, plus some multiplayer modes. What’s not to like?

Menu UI is pretty horrid. Multiplayer isn’t compelling - the best mode is the mode where each player gets a random allocation of buildable positions and forcing them to work together to craft something sensible - also a good place to share ideas on how to maximise points.
Different waves rarely require reconsidering strategy. There’s not a whole lot of variety here.
As with the first game, missions can sometimes drag on - thank goodness for fast forward.

Otherwise an easy recommendation, although a light one - there is still plenty of good stuff going on here, and especially if you like tower defence.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe original Defense Grid: The Awakening released in 2008 and was one of my favorite Tower Defense games. So why, when the sequel was released in 2014, did it take me until 2020 before I really played it, and 2021 to write a review?

I'm not sure I know the answer to that question. I mean, I had launched it and played the first couple of missions before 2020, but it wasn't until late last year (about 3 months ago) that I finally went through and completed the full campaign.

Okay then, so what did I think of it?

It was okay. It was perfectly competent. It was good. But somehow it just didn't appeal to me like the first one did. And what does it say about the game, or my enthusiasm about it, that I couldn't actually remember whether or not I had completed the campaign when I started to write this? I had to launch the game again today and check.

I think that, like many aging games, Defense Grid 2 was a product of its time. It was probably a very enjoyable game when it was new and Tower Defense games were still fairly relevant and closer to being wildly popular. But I think the appeal has worn off for me and Defense Grid 2 doesn't seem to bring anything new enough to the formula to make it stand out in my mind.

While this review may come across as negative, my feelings on the game are more of a neutral-positive. I spent almost 20 hours in the game, and I generally enjoyed my time with it. But just a few months have passed since I completed it and I had already forgotten. Give it a try if you think you'd like it, but don't go out of your way to get it and play it if you don't think it looks very appealing.\t\t\t\t\t
