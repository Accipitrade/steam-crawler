\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI almost gave an "I don't recommend this game" review, but ultimately I realized I've played it for almost 40 hours, so they must have done something right.

That said...

The "mouse is always locked in the middle of the screen" interface is terrible. I have gotten used to it, but I still don't like it.

I also think it's unethical for them to actually sell game maps that users created... especially at such ridiculous prices. I'd expect to be able to buy 3 or 6 levels (that the game creators make) for $1 or $2, not one single level for a dollar! The user-created content should be free, period. Maybe if it were there would be a lot more to download and DG2 would sell more copies.

Anyone who hasn't played DG #1 should seriously consider buying it, even at full price. This company does deserve your support, and we can only hope they will listen to users and make a DG3 that makes up for the DG #2 letdown. DG1 is perhaps the best tower defense games ever conceived, and I say that having played and conquered at least 9 or 10 other tower defense games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort version: Very bad interface and mechanics.

Long version: DG2's user interface is in several ways inferior to a lot of the free TD games you'll find on places like Kongregate.
Let's start with the cursor lock. DG2's normal view 1) locks your cursor to the center of the screen, moving the play area around it, instead of moving the cursor, and 2) locks that movement to the "field", not letting you mouseover anything but the current board for tooltips or information.
Also there aren't any tooltips.

When you click a tower, then, your cursor is locked to the "Select upgrade" area of the tower. A tower is listed as having Abilities, but these abilities are displayed only as nameless icons outside the upgrade area, so you can't mouseover to see what they do.

There is very little concrete information offered to the player. The Next Wave indicator has only vague outlines hinting at 'small aliens' and 'big aliens'. You can't mouse over the waves for info or tooltips, and you can't mouse over the aliens on the board either.

Tower stats aren't given with any sort of numbers, but with sliding bars of vagueness:
Damage --O----
Rate of fire ---O---
Range -----O-

There's no indication of whether late-wave small aliens have more hit points than early-wave small aliens, or if they're just more numerous.

There's no "Send next wave now" button, either, to take advantage of the various AOE effects one has available.

Go play something like Gemcraft or Monkey Bloons TD instead if you want a good tower defense.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is so darn addictive! I started playing one level...and four hours later, here I am. The levels are fun. The different game modes add variety and keep the game challenging. There is something sinful about plotting to kill so many waves of bad guys. Planning your tower placements so they have to run almost in circles. Creating killing zones where waves of bad guys just get demolished. The variety of tower types also keeps the game fun. Do you lay down a bunch of inferno towers and BBQ the bad guys? Do you setup laser towers and just melt those fools? Put meteor towers in the rear and watch while they just hurl destruction down upon the bad guys. Once you get everything setup just right, you can sit back and watch the bodies of bad guys pile up.

This game is just pure fun.

I found myself replaying maps just to try different tower placement strategies. One map I played seven or eight times in a row.

What was really neat is seeing how your score compares to your Steam friends who play the game....in real-time. As you're laying waste to mobs, the games shows you what your Steam friends did on the same map and at the same time. It's hard to describe but it's pretty neat to see. When the map is over, you get a fancy graph that compares your game stats to your Steam friends. It shows how many towers you deployed, how many were upgraded, how many cores you lost, etc.

The more I play this game, the more I like it.\t\t\t\t\t
