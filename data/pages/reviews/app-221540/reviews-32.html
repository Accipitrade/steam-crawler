\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefense Grid 2
Rating: 7/10
Genre: Tower Defense

The Good:
+ Multiple modes and difficulty per level. Some modes cap your resources, can not upgrade towers, more resources, etc. These modes offer a lot of replay value (*See Bad section for further details)
+ Multiple tower types for different situations.
+ Multiple enemy types that are more creative than your usual tower defense games. Some enemies when killed disable all nearby towers, one enemy warpâ€™s near your cores and brings in other enemies etc. There are lots of enemies that will require multiple tower types.
+ For a tower defense game, it has a pretty elaborate story. You can easily skip the story if it doesnâ€™t interest you.
+ A new feature over the previous game is that you can outfit each tower type with different items. These items can significantly improve each tower type in a variety of different ways. Items are randomly given to you after completing a level.
+ Your score is tracked in real time and compared to others on your friends list.

The Average:
+/- Another new feature is the ability to spend resources to alter the map. Most of the time, you just get more space to build more towers however there are a few maps where you can add entire new sections of the map to reroute enemies. This is a good feature however I felt it was under utilized as most of the time I never bothered with it, at least on the lower difficulties.

The Bad:
- The game features steam workshop, however, the developers must approve the levels before you can purchase them. It seems the developers are very slow if not completely abandoned approving any new user created levels.

-This game suffers the EXACT same problems as the first one. Although there are plenty of modes, many of them feel very repetitive. Once you figure out what works in a level, you can play different modes with the exact same layout. However, these modes are optional.

- AGAIN , Why can't I map every control function to the mouse?

- Multiplayer is dead making it impossible to get every achievement.

Conclusion:
In some respects, this game takes one step forward and two steps back from the first game. It seems the developers did little to change the underlying formula and the new gameplay elements added donâ€™t really add much. As it stands you have a decent tower defense game that does little over its predecessor. I guess I was hoping for something more.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rate the game Defense Grid 2 3.5/5. Pretty good but this sequel to the quintessential tower defense game is not as good as the original.

Get this mainly to conclude the game's story (if you care about that), particularly if you got all of the original game's DLC packs. The level design is far less complex and less interesting than in the original and this game isn't as long as the original either (only 25 levels) if you include the original's DLC packs. Besides a nice graphical overhaul, most other differences between the original and this game are negligible. The user interface is actually a little more confusing while still doing most of the same things as the original. The music is still extremely boring but again I was able to pause the game and use the windows key to successfully minimize the game and play other music in a media player on the desktop without causing Defense Grid 2 any problems when I returned to it. The potential greatest benefits of Defense Grid 2 are probably its multi-player aspects (none of which I tried out).

Defense Grid 2 stays close to the core gameplay of the original (perhaps too close) and, like the original, can easily become boring and repetitive despite its numerous difficulty levels and gameplay modes. As in the original, the story plays itself out in the form of spoken dialogue as you progress. The voice acting is at least adequate. There are no plot choices which can be made and there are no alternate endings. You either win or you lose.

I also had a strong feeling that so much more could have been done with the gameplay mechanics. There's a lot unrealized potential here in the form of different features, turret upgrades, turret types and enemy units that could potentially exist, and so in my opinion, this game's full potential remains woefully untapped. As it is, Defense Grid 2 is pretty good but often feels disappointing as it so clearly could have done so much more if its designers had been a bit more creative, a little more ambitious and perhaps taken a few more risks. Get this game for cheap.\t\t\t\t\t
