\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile it's certainly not as wholesome as the original, this sequel is a very intelligent successor to the classic, featuring enhanced mechanics, notably cleaner and more colorful visuals, and more nuanced and interesting enemy types (like one that can disrupt your towers, some that heal those around it, and a drop-ship that spawns enemies right at your cores). It also features what is easily some of the best game balancing I've ever experienced -- as early as Mission 3 I spent half the level biting my nails, but the game was always generous enough to give me enough resources to where, unless I missed something, I rarely came close to defeat. This makes the game simultaneously Hard and Easy at the same time, and it's pretty brilliant. If I had one serious complaint, it's that there are several levels with multiple entry and exit points for the enemy, and you don't know the initial path the enemy will take, resulting in using resources on towers that won't even be needed until a later wave, all because you assumed they'd be coming from the other direction first. But this is hardly a serious problem, and very easy to overlook considering how much fun the game is overall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike many here I will say DG1 is not only better but the best tower defense game ever. DG2 however is a step down in quality of gameplay and can be extremely annoying.

The story of this one is awful not the premise of the story itself but you end up with so many voices talking and most of it is nonsense that contributes nothing to this game. It also becomes painfully obvious what is going on but of course the characters are too clueless to figure it out.

The actual game-play is less satisfying and the map quality is not on par with DG1. None of the new towers are anything exciting. For some reason flying aliens are non-existant anymore with no in game explanation given. Did we hunt them to extinction in the previous title? Did they abandoned the alien cause? Who knows. Instead they are replaced by a dropship that lands right on your cores platform. Meaning your cores will be touched no matter what on all missions that have these. You cannot destroy the dropship either only damage it which reduces the health of the aliens that come out and when they all exit then it dies on its own. The scoring and earned currency system is also changed for the worse in this title.

Even the 'dlc' quality if we can even call it dlc is worse. You can buy community maps that haven't been added to in forever. (You can get other maps elsewhere for free anyways) while DG1's DLC was actually good like the Portal crossover which had GLaDOS.

Don't get me wrong it's not a bad game it's worth playing if you love tower defenses. But it will forever live in the shadow of the first title.\t\t\t\t\t
