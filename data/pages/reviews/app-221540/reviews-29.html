\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun sequel to one of the best tower defence games out there. While this is very much "more of the same", it doesn't quite feel as compelling as the original. Most of the levels have a couple of very obviously superior strategies.

And while the boost tower mechanic that allows you to raise the height of towers and apply a bonus is nice, it's cheap enough that it's a no brainer and once you've unlocked it there's almost no reason NOT to use it.

Overall it feels that while some new mechanics were added, actual diversity of solutions and viable gameplay choices have narrowed.

Despite that, thoroughly enjoyed playing the game, but can't help but feel that it's a downward step from game one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the 9th review out of my 107 games in my library currently...this means that I don't review games a lot, only the ones that deserve them...or not!
I have to say that Defense Grid 1 was a fantastic game for me and one of the only 4 games I have completed 100% with achievements and all (268 hours of game play).

Defense Grid 2 somehow...feels like something is not right. It is very difficult to explain and even do a negative review to such a good first game's sequel. I'm a programmer myself and understand that creating such thing as a game is extremely difficult. I do not bash at the developer's work, I understand them...but there is something in the synergy of the result that doesn't feel right...from view distance I guess (everything seems too small, even GUI) to the way new features are introduced (specially the new laser cannon types), I don't know, It doesn't seem to appeal me. You just have to try both games and experience it for yourself unfortunately.

I installed the game, played it, got bored, forgot about it, played something else, came back, forgot about it again, played something else, came back again, played one more time before dropping it entirely. I barely made 6.5 hours of game play in more than 2 months while I made 80+ hours into 2 new games in 2 weeks.

To conclude, buy the game only if you are a new player to the franchise or an old player trying to confirm my experience (which I warn you it could be that good).

UPDATE: I played 30 extra hours just to get my "Master Strategist" OCD achievement (Earn 100 Gold Medals on any missions). It was painful, I just wanted it to end. And yet, I still don't know what is the criteria for earning points in the game!
My recommendation remains the same. Uninstalling game now.\t\t\t\t\t
