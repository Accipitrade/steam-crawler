\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat's there to love?

+ The funny yet decent AI voicing is back with more to go with the story!
+ The towers are well-drawn and all works well with different enemies/maze idea though we could do with better explanations/descriptions.
+ Various game modes with challenges to try for, and even without flyers in this game, the higher difficulty can be a nightmare to manage.
+ Co-op mode is love/hate game (I enjoyed it though I doubt you'd find anyone for this anymore).

What's there to hate?

- Game story = voices in the background (I meant narrations; not the scary voice that tells you sacrifice a goat) and not much to do than to just listen. It is good but at the same time sad, cause most of the time, you will be busy planning how to maze the towers/what's coming next/which tower need upgrading etc. I was on a higher difficulty and I barely have time to listen to the AIs talking. It is like your mom/wife nagging you to go and sleep when you are trying to finish that one more turn; did you really heard what they said?
- The menu and shortcuts could be much better; while it works, I expected a smoother hotkey control with lesser mouse clicking, especially in the game menus. The navigations are so restricted/controlled for some reason...
- Limited maps that come along with the game. The gameplay is fun, the storyline decent, and worthwhile for the discounted pricing but that's pretty much it. You will need to look for the community maps but generally once you cleared the main maps, only the challenges are left with some other game modes for the same maps. I find it boring once I know how to maze each specific map and it grew stale from there on. P/S: Extra maps from game makers = chargeable (boo).
- High scores tracking is a fun idea but pointless when you realised that most of your friends cheated!!! *Yes, they gotta be cheating cause their Godlike scores are impossibruuuuuuu*

Summary:

Get it when it is discounted if you want a good tower defense game.
Don't get it if it is not discounted unless you really have nothing else to play and you want a TD game.
Don't get it if you are looking for something with a lot more hours/meat to it ('less you like repetitive games with little to no changes except for high scores).\t\t\t\t\t

I much prefered this one over its predecessor as in addition to the previous game's scoring method, you have damn near another dizen ways you can decide to take on a map, leaving you with lots of replayability.

My only real complaint with the game is that scoring is rather counterintuitive for a tower defense game. I can still support this design decision as it does add another layer of strategy for when you want to try to top a personal (or global) score best as things become less about making the safest maze possible, but rather the most profitable, which adds to the game's replayability if that's your thing. Additionally, there are downloadable maps (have I mentioned the replay value?)
