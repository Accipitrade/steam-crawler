\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEasily the best Viking type game to this very day! A brutal, fun and unique hack n slash game with great enemy and level variety with a awesome soundtrack that just sets the mood so well. The levels are done in a old school shooter way, which means they are big and there's a lot of them! You have several types of weapons and multiple different tiers within those types. You also have powers by collecting Runes which depending on the weapon do certain magical abilities. The voice acting works, sort of a cheesy action movie style, the art direction and armor designs are a sort of gothic meets viking style which is pretty awesome and memorable. There's even a awesome mod that allows you to play the campaign in coop or even download some of the custom campaigns to play together. This game truly is a hidden gem within the genre, something if you're a fan of action and adventure games or Hack n Slash you shouldn't miss it. There's also a fun multiplayer mode and the game runs great on modern systems.

All in all, a lot of fun, exciting and easily replayable. It's still crazy you never hear about it especially since the people who made this did the Heretic/Hexen games. So check this title out and here's to hoping the new Rune game they have coming out is just as good.

Til' Valhalla!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first I didn't recommend the game, because a lot has been removed, but then it was pointed out to me that they could be replaced. Which led me to this mod:

http://forums.steampowered.com/forums/showthread.php?t=2753315

It's very easy to install and brings back all the missing levels.

Although some of the levels can drag out the game a bit, it's not worth loosing some story elements and some of the best parts in the game.

Rune: Viking Warlord as it was originally called, is a great game (although not mythologically accurate in the slightest). It's got great mechanics for a game this old (in some ways much better than the average modern game).

With all levels intact I will give this 10/10

A very fun thing to do in the game is playing multiplayer in the arena with moon gravity and have a toss-fight! ^,..,^\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving a lot of fun replaying this classic. Takes a bit of fidgeting with the .ini to get 1080p working in 16:9 but it's nothing crazy.

This game has very good level design! A real sense of adventure comes from the unique layout of most areas and the fact that many of the puzzles and routes are not obvious. There are many instances where when you figure something out, it feels like you yourself came up with the solution rather than simply recognizing the path and taking it. The production team had some very clever people on it, indeed. Plus when you bite off a lizard's head it makes an apple chomp sound. The whole eating lizards thing is probably worth the price of admission alone.

I've been streaming this recently and the most common comment I get is "This looks cool and I wish I'd have played it back in the day." But it's not too late!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvery time I thought the game was about to end, it carried on. That is not a complaint! It's the action RPG that keeps on giving.

The setting is unnerving, with dark crevices and spooky noises in every environment. Indeed, throughout the entire Hel sequence, the distant agonised screams of lost Norsemen was quite unsettling... which is an amazing thing to accomplish in an age of detailed and intricate horror and gore.

It's not just the eerie setting that makes this a good game, it's the wide array of creatures and enemies you encounter, and innovative boss battles that can rely on wit and cunning over hacking and slashing.

It's a fantastic game that holds up after all these years. The only negatives I must mention are an awkward Sound-related bug towards the end of the game, and switching to a higher resolution didn't seem to work - my screen wouldn't be centred.\t\t\t\t\t
