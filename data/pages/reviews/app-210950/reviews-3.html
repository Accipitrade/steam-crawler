The multiplayer aspect was visionary for its time and could easily be considered a precursor to todays battle-royale games. The controls are exceptional; there are a multitude of weapons and enemies.

In what other game can you behead someone, then pick up their head, throw it like a projectile and behead the next enemy?

I bought it for nostalgic reasons. It hasn't aged as well as some of its peers but is well worth the full price if you plan to put the time in.\t\t\t\t\t
