\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe enemies in this game are annoying, tedious and not fun to fight. Theres no stunlocking when you hit enemies and enemy attacks are never telegraphed, so you're just instantly damaged. Because of this, the combat consists of you spamming move forward and move backwards really fast a certain distance away infront of an enemy to bait them into swinging their weapon thus exposing themself for a quick moment and then you move in, hit them once then immediately back off, rinse and repeat until they're dead. They should have made it so that small enemies move fast and attack very quickly but do little damage and get stunlocked by your attacks, while big enemies don't get stunlocked and their attacks hit hard but their attacks are slow and nicely telegraphed. Due to these flaws in the combat and the stupid enemy AI I would find myself cheesing the game, for example there was a rectangular table that was in a corner of a room with both ends touching the walls, I jumped over the table and then would slash the enemies with the very tip of my sword as they were stuck running into the table on the other side, there would also be situations where I would be crouching ontop of a handrail that was tall enough that enemies weren't able to attack me, meanwhile I would be slashing the top of their heads as they were stuck running into the handrail.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWithout being overly nostalgic, Rune is placed on my masterpieces list. I had the pleasure of discovering it way back in the begging of the 2000's, when it came bundled with my first PC capable of 3D graphics, so this review might seem a bit biased.

The combination of Viking aesthetics, along with awesome level design, engaging third person hack n slash which keeps you on your feet on higher difficulties, a handful amount of puzzles and some platforming elements make this title a must try for any fan of the above genres.

Rune aged decently, for a game built on Unreal 1. The combat still feels pretty smooth, with its arsenal of swords, maces and axes, each having their own unique magical effect. While fun, some of them can be underwhelming, and you might end up not using them at all throughout the campaign.

Compared to today’s standards, it’s a quite lengthy title for a game of its genre, and I guarantee you’ll enjoy it.

Human Head Studios recently updated Rune, an 18 year old game, so this tells a lot about their commitment and passion.

Bonus points if you listen to Viking Metal while playing this gem!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRune is a terrific viking simulator, where you play as Ragnar, the keeper of last rune of Odin. Ragnar's father and fellow tribesmen are killed by Conrack, who wished to destroy last rune, in order to free Loki and unleash fumbulwinter, Ragnar is saved from death by Odin, and now must save the world from coming Ragnarök.

And that is basically all what you need to know about story. Rune is one of rare example of videogames, that succed to tell the interesting story without much characters developing, interesting dialogues etc. There, a clash of sword against axe excel any dialogue, a chopped head or severed hand is best resolve of conflict, and, the most important, traveling through locations, evolves in an actually very interesting journey.

But, because nothing is ideal, there is some of negative side:
1) in a rare occurence, ai can glitch to a point, where enemy just stands and doing nothing
2) without software manipulation, the brighntess is broken on windows 10, and game looks very dark (but honestly, it is very suits it)
3) at least in single player, some rune powerups are almost useless.
4) and a biggest downside, is that Rune Gold is a lot superior to Classic. Unfortunately, it is not available on Steam.

In Rune, there is no prolonged exposition, no digression or excursus - only you, and a virile journey into grim, frostbitten and hellish land. Absolutely worth it, Classic or Gold edition.\t\t\t\t\t
