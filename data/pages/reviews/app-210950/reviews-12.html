\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRune is one of the best hack and slash games I've ever played. The gameplay remains solid even compared to newer games of the same genre. There are 15 weapons to use. There's puzzles to solve. Then there's food to eat and ale to guzzle when you're wonded from the slaughtering. The multiplayer still works, though there are a few tweaks and quirks to fiddle with depending on whether or not you use a router or have custom firewall settings in order to play with others on Steam version servers. There are a number of older version servers running and active that Steam users can join and play on.
I recommend this game to anyone that likes full 360 degree splatterhouse games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start off by summarizing whether or not I think you should buy Rune Classic:
Absolutely. Rune is an excellent game and a good time for anyone who enjoys vikings, chopping things up, and fighting vikings with other chopped up vikings. Definitely something that deserves people's support.

However, I cannot stress this enough: I highly recommend getting the original over this version.

The added enemy types, while a welcome thing to see, are superfluous and poorly thought out. More often than not, creatures that ostensibly should be on the same side fought themselves before even giving me so much as a glance. In trying to hit me, they hit eachother, which would anger the other enemy and make it attack.

It all depends on whether or not this has been patched since my playthrough.

Additionally, Rune Classic cuts and modifies many maps from the singleplayer campaign, even to the point that a major plot point is completely omitted. Granted, in the grand scheme of the game this is inconsequential because you're probably just here for viking-themed hack-and-slash fun, but it annoys me to no end. At least the original files are still there, which is more than you can say for most remakes that give a game that kind of treatment, but it still seems odd to me that so much was cut out when most of the changes seem to be centered around online multiplayer.

On that note, I am also still not a big fan of the moving around of the rune skills, something I feel was done solely with Multiplayer in mind. The special abilities of each weapon were different in the original. The handaxe is too strong, the roman sword is no longer the perfect zombie-slaying weapon you can find in the underworld... But I honestly can't tell if this gripe is pure nostalgia for the way it was, or if it is a genuine detriment to the game's design.

There were also a slew of new bugs and glitches that were not present when I later tried out the original release, on the same machine no less! Objects spawned incorrectly, the man-o-wars that are supposed to come down from the waterfalls would get stuck, plants in particular appear upside-down until you brush into them and jiggle them into their upright position. I also have strange issues with stereo sound where the volume levels seem to be completely backwards.
Very glaringly obvious issues, yet not everyone seems to have them... So I can only assume that, in modifying the game and (probably) making it run better on modern PC's, they also made it much less stable on specific setups.

All in all, this remake seems to have had the most care put into the online component, which arguably was Rune's strongest point in the first place. If that's what you're here for, Rune Classic is the game for you. But if you like a strong singleplayer campaign, stick with the original.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEnter the world of the Vikings in Rune Classic.
You play as Ragnar who must stop the evil Loki and his minions, both human and mythological monsters, from unleashing Ragnarok, the End of Days in the Viking myths, on Midgard, the name given to Earth by the Viking Gods.
There are plenty of weapons to be used in your quest to save Midgard, you can even take an enemy's own head and use it as a weapon.
And many of the weapons have special abilities that are powered by Runes, that can be found in the various areas you will be going through, and you can also go into a bloodlust state, which was extremely notable for Vikings called Berserkers that would enter an nearly uncontrollable trance-like state of fury where they just ignored any damage done to them and just kept on attacking their foes.
A must buy for those who enjoy Viking myths as well as third person hack and slash games in general.\t\t\t\t\t
