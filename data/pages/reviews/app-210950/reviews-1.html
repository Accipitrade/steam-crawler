\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRune is a fantastic game still.

Rune classic is slightly shorter than original rune but in some regards that's a good thing, as the original chapter 1 and 2 had a tendency to outstay their welcome i.m.o.

The additional new enemy types apply mostly to chapter 2, adding much needed variety to enemies encountered during that chapter.

For those unaware of what Rune is, it's one of the tightest hack and slash games of its time, with swordplay on par with what would only come in later games like the souls series. This is not to say Rune is a souls like. It's a hack and slash, not an rpg and dodge-rolling and immunity frames are not a thing.

Overall an oldie goldie game and a well made tune up. I reccommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOver the last several years there's been a trend of rereleasing old titles, often from the 90's and early 2000's, with a lot of improvements to make it run on modern machines. Sometimes these go so far as to improve the graphics, fix old outstanding bugs, or otherwise markedly improve the experience compared to that of grabbing your old discs off the shelf and simply reinstalling and replaying.

Rune Classic is not one of these.

A lot of the fault with Rune Classic (henceforth simply called Rune in this review) is inherent to the old product. The combat isn't interesting and has no nuance or detail to it. It's just poorly waggling back and forth while mashing the attack button and soaking up damage while killing the enemy. Blocking sometimes doesn't do anything and other times is automatic, saving you from damage without any real indication as to how or why.

The levels are long and ponderous and towards the last half to third of the game, annoyingly labyrinthine. They drag on and on and with the unfortunate dullness of the combat there is nothing engaging to see or encounter, just pressing on for the sake of progress only. The platforming starts fine but towards the last third of the game it's devolved into jumps you can barely make that are extremely punishing without the feeling of accomplishment for making it through the segment.

The game is visually outright ugly, not due to the old graphics (as plenty of older titles from around this era hold up due to quality art assets) but due entirely to style. The models, the textures, everything is hideous and drab. It is a wholly unpleasant product to look at.

But beyond all of these issues are the technical ones. It honestly feels as though almost no effort was put in to updating the product at all. The game crashes if trying to select a modern resolution higher than 1080, requiring the use of a third party Unreal Engine renderer. Audio is absolutely and entirely broken, oftentimes spatialized in bizarre ways opposite of where it should be (right to the left or vice versa) or just being a 100% mix to one side or the other. This also happens in the game's handful of cutscenes, it is absolutely inexplicable. Trees and bushes either get stuck or "unfold" before your eyes, models sometimes fail to render or don't correctly locate correctly, visually there are a wealth of small bugs that don't actually affect the gameplay itself but definitely help undermine the experience.

The vast majority of positive reviews Steam threw at me on the product page while I was writing this one up are all referring to the original release of Rune. Back in 2000 it was a new game with some new things. It was one of the first games to feature a viking setting, it had melee combat and smooth platforming. It had dismemberment. But all of those reviews I read are with rose-tinted glasses and the tinting is so heavy they've become blinders. Rune as a product has not held up well, was not actually fantastic upon release, and this particular release is a barely functional product dumped onto the market with frustratingly little attempt made.

This release isn't worth the full price $10 they're asking for and quite honestly unless you are absolutely desperate to play this exact game, it's not worth owning at all.\t\t\t\t\t
