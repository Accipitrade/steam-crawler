\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRune Classic is tpp action game based on Norse mythology, released in 2000 and powered by first Unreal Engine.

The story is showing the conflict between the gods Odin and Loki and the preparations to Ragnarok, the end of the world. Depending on player actions at the end of the game it offers two possible endings.

Graphical it's dated game but some locations still have nice design. Audio is done ok with average voice acting and some good backround music that is played in key moments of the game.

Combat is probably the best part of the game with quite demanding skirmishes, often with multiple enemies. Even on normal mode you can easily and literally lost your head.
Ragnar the main hero of the game can use four types of weapons: swords,axes,hammers and maces.He can also use shields and rune power that each weapon has. But if you are bored of fighting with normal gear you can always grab a limb or head that your dead opponent have lost in the duel and use it against other enemies!

There's also multiplayer mode where you can prove your skills in the combat against other players. It's challenging but at the same time quite fun to play.

If you are a fan of tpp action games and like Norse mythogy then it's in my opinion a must have in your Steam library.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't believe I missed this game back in 2000. It may have taken me 15yrs but I have finally played it. So, what did I think?

I freaking LOVE this game! It reminds me of Severance: Blade of Darkness/Enclave, which I enjoyed in my youth. Graphically it holds up all right and you can still make out what is what. The story is good and works to motivate you to continue, even if it is rather simple. The Norse setting and theme is good even if certain structures and characters don't always portray that. When facing other Vikings you get taunted with things like, 'I'm going to drink mead from your skull' and 'Look, a troll has wandered in from the cold', which adds to the immersion and causes me to smile.
There is much plat-forming in this game, with ledge climbing/jumping and rope/chain climbing. Some can be tricky but enjoyable.

Combat is, well ok. Not brilliant but it gets the job done; satisfying when you hack/slice limbs and heads off. Enemy AI is quite shocking and sometimes they just stand still and let you kill them. This just makes me laugh, it was made in 2000 after all.

Overall a great plat-forming, hack 'n' slash action-adventure game. It has its flaws yet I cannot get enough of it. Make sure to grab the mod that adds back the missing levels that were cut from the Gold version. Makes sense to play the full game.

8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played many classic games in my life, I play old school games since I'm young, but I have never known about this game until recently, I know some games that are even older, I know probably a lot of games, more than there are on Steam but I did not know this game.
I must admit, this game is really great for a 2000 game.
It feels like a RPG but it is not.
The gameplay is really good for a game of that time, even better than a lot of nowadays games.
At first, I thought this would be like Gothic but it is not.
It's more of a viking game than anything else...
But I love the viking lore so I'm totally happy about it.
They've added new enemies and achievements for a game that dates back to 2000.
I truly respect this developer.

The game is awesome, GOTY? Probably but I could not choose since there is also Deus Ex, Diablo II, Red Alert 2, Hitman Codename 47 and so many other great games from that time.

9.5/10 I'd suggest it to any viking, old school and action fans.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRune Classic includes the original Rune and the Halls of Valhalla expansion. Gameplay is very similar to the original; however, there are a few noticeable changes.
- In the single player campaign, many of the levels have been shortened and some have been removed completely. This does help keep the game moving along (especially in the first cavern areas); though, fans of the original game will likely notice a few memorable areas missing.
- A few new enemies have been added: skeletons, hellhounds, and frost giants.
- Some of the weapon rune powers have been swapped. For example, the viking short sword now has the vampire rune power, and the viking broad sword now has the befriend rune power.

Overall, I still like the game. The only complaint I have is that a couple bugs have not yet been fixed (as of version 1.10).
- In the cave area just after Ragnar gets off the large beetle, there is a cliff area that will kill Ragnar if any items (weapons, shields, body parts, etc.) fall into it.
- Near the end of the game, there is a cinema scene where the sarks are breaking through a wall with hammers. This scene causes the game to crash unless you turn the volume down to zero in the game control panel.\t\t\t\t\t
