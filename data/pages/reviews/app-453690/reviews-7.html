\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game at the beginning, absolute RNG garbage from mid to end.

There is no story, no "lore" in game just some random village and some random castle you have to go through to kill some E V I L sorcerer. Period.

Graphics are nice, but animations are almost non-existant.

Music is annoying and repetitive.

Gameplay... Almost every enemy has "defence" stat which translates as "chance to hit". No matter what the game shows the chances are terrible - fighters will miss 3 out of 4 attacks to enemies with 40% defence. Killing even 1 enemy with 70% takes ♥♥♥♥ ton of time and I literally saw my fighters miss 2 attacks in a row against enemy with 10% defence. Traps are OP, they do guaranteed damage and AI is so stupid you literally can put a trap right before his nose and he gladly step on it. There is no "back attack" or flanking, your gnome can stand with four enemies around him and he'll block all their attacks (or won't because RNG in this game sucks), same goes for enemies - you can spend several turns attacking one enemy from several directions and he'll block all atacks because his ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ defence is 70%. There are active and passive skills, but half of them are useless, like taunting barrel for gnome, others are a must have, like the ability to remove traps for the same gnome.

Game quickly becomes boring and annoying because of all RNG elements. And, during on of the missions my archer shot the enemy and game just froze - I could open menu or map, but couldn't continue the fight, the arrow was just hovering in air...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGorgeous looking and great sounding dungeon crawler which unfortunately gets very frustrating and tedious after the first few missions where i found my party of two or three characters facing off against ten or more enemies packed into one room only to die or barely survive where my one single surviving character gets butchered in the next room facing the same overwhelming odds.

I can only choose between two or three characters depending on the mission even though i have unlocked all of them which if memory serves me correctly are six in total. So here i am on missions where having at least four characters would have helped greatly but am unable to do so. I have heard it is even less in multiplayer mode.

The above is made worse due to character skills and magic unable to recharge so you can only use them once per mission. Potions which allow more then one use of skills or restoring mana for magic quickly get used up due to the overwhelming number of enemies.

There is no way to pan around the map so many times i am unable to see behind enemies which is a major pain when my character is surrounded by a bunch of giant shield carrying orcs as i try to have said character do something. In one case my barbarian died and my healer was going to cast a resurrection spell but could not because my slain character was not visible. All the while the same healer can cast bless and party heal spells from anywhere on the map without needing line of sight.

Combat is also a mess especially in later levels where am constantly missing yet enemies keep escaping death over and over as they walk through my mages fire wall as the escaped death icon appears over them with diabolical laughter filling the air as they get close enough to do damage. Combat feels very unbalanced with way too much RNG going on.

Here is hoping with many a crossed finger that Brain Seal Ltd much anticipated upcoming pc board game version works out all these kinks and turns out to be as much fun as the first DARK QUEST game is.

DARK QUEST 2, so much potential yet so unbalanced.\t\t\t\t\t

My play time was about 11 hours through the campaign. The difficulty level keeps you on your toes and you can definitely get your party killed if you are not careful. RPG elements are light, if you are looking for story-driven D&D a la Balder's Gate, this game is not it, it's pure dungeon crawling, baby!
