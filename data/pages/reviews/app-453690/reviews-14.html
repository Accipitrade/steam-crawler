\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only played through this game once.

I noticed that it was easy to play through the second level several times to get 4 skill potions per run if you just surrender the dungeon instead of completing it. Suggestion: Make it so that you could only replay levels for the first, second... time after completing the entire game once, twice, etc.

I found that the logical choice was to use the Dark Monk and the Dwarf up until the chaos levels, at which point it was necessary to swap out the Dwarf for the Knight. I chose to use the Archer as my third character, but I feel the difficulty of the game would be about the same using either Barbarian or Wizard in place of the Archer.

The inability to walk to squares that you could not see because of the camera angle was annoying.

There should be a keyboard shortcut to toggle "confirm moves", it would be nice to be able to change it without having to go into the menu.

There were some weird glitches, but that did not detract from my overall enjoyment of this game. I'll admit that I got pretty into this game for a couple of weeks, but there is not that much to it.

In my opinion, the highlight of this game is playing with the Dark Monk. Its abilities are all pretty fun, and its even more fun with the Staff of Teleportation. I enjoyed using Chaos Wave to do tons of damage, and using Friends from the Past to buddy up with your enemies.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm loving this game.

I'm only about half-way through this game (and played some Custom Maps), and I am already hoping for new content (like another DLC campaign).

I got this to play a co-op, turn-based, fantasy RPG with my friend, and it has been super addictive. I think I would compare it to Warhammer Quest as far as similarity in game because I've never played Hero Quest.

After the tutorial, you start out in the Village. Generally, you choose a group of your characters for a certain quest, swap out any items you want assigned to each character, make sure the characters have the potion types they may want, and select the quest (you usually have a choice of quests, and they get harder each time you repeat them, so it's good to advance the campaign).

Once in the quest, each character takes a turn by moving, using a skill, using a potion, and attacking. Then the monsters get to do the same to you. There are variables and twists that can alter the core turn, but that is the basic sequence. It's pretty easy to learn, and it gets levels of complexities added to keep it challenging.

There are some great guides out there for the game and the map editor (I'm excited about creating my own maps).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHighly recommend if you enjoy turn-based dungeon crawls. Can't recommend highly enough, actually.

A great game that has me wishing for more. The design is simple and fun. I've never actually played Hero Quest, but I've played IOS versions of the Warhammer equivalent, and this game definietly scratches that itch -- far better in many ways.

The dungeons are not proceduraly generated, they're each hand-crafted and it benefits from this greatly. The fights, encounters and challenges continue to feel fresh and different.

The playable characters are fun and each have their own strengths. There are a few options to pick from (six) and you usually crawl with three at a time.

The game is not deep but playing it, I wish it were. I want more, which I guess is the hallmark of a good game with a solid design.

I plan to pick up Dark Quest 1, the original, to just measure the changes made (just looking, the visuals are a big improvement) and to support these devs, they deserve it. Hopefully they're working on Dark Quest 3, with it hopefully having the same enjoyability and more depth.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you have ever played Hero Quest the boardgame, this game will feel very familiar..

The good:
The art is really nice.
The music and sound is good.
Feels like an oldschool dungeoncrawler.
6 character classes with few, but pretty interesting skills and items.

The bad:
Some people complain about the UI, but I really have no problems with it..

Another thing is when you need to backtrack to a room you missed,
and have to walk each individual character through all the rooms area transitions.. it takes a bit of time.
It kind of feels the same as a DM in a game like Hero Quest, who do not want your party to jump to the end,
but want you to roll dice for every move to get to the staircase, even if there are no enemies on the board.
I have no major problem with that either..

I have played a few hours, unlocked all characters, but not finished the game yet.
It does not seem that long, but it might be a game that is enjoyable to try to beat on hardcore,
and something you can just jump into to take a couple of dungeons when you have some spare time.

It is not the most complicated game, but it has enough there to be enjoyable
and the price is on point, so yeah I recommend it!
