\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame actually is a nice concept, but technical side requires a lot of polish.
I am already tired after encountering 2 gamebreaking bugs.
1) You character falls under playable area, you cannot move or do anything, all you can do is to retreat and then restart. It's fine when you just started, but I was near the end of the dungeon.
2) You cannot move at all. Same as #1: if it's early thats fine, but if it's late all you can do is to abandon and restart.

Other issues:
1) Moving is bad. I mean really bad. You want to move this hero in that specific spot during a fight, that happens to be between 2 enemies? Good luck trying to click exact field without either clicking enemy, selecting you other hero.
2) You are placed in a very small room and exit of that room is "hidden" behind your other heroes? Sometimes you need to click like 15 times to finally not select your other heroes, just move to that spot.
3) RNG or bad mechanics? It says in character screen, you have 60% accuracy, you go to fight, and sometimes during a fight (lets say 10 attacks), you are not hitting a single hit. Both: your and enemy stats asks for a lot.
4) Music is so loud. I have it on 3%, yet it's still louder than other things that I am running in the background at 100%.

This is riddicoulous.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are definitely bugs in functionality. I have experienced all of them, but it would take too long to explain. At the end of the day, it doesn't really matter. You will get over it. I beat hardcore mode co-op with a friend where we were only allowed to use 2 heroes on each map. I am not going to spoil which characters and setups are broken. It is for you to experience. This game is phenomenal. That's really all I can say.

Here are my complaints:
The final boss was a little lackluster considering all the potions, items, and abilities you have acquired at that point.
There is no achievement for completing all the levels. You can skip levels and have *finished the game*.
The achievement to get 100,000 gold is pointless and a waste of time.
Achievements could have also been a little more creative.
2 of the 6 classes are pretty useless comparatively - balance the classes a little better.
You shouldn't be allowed to abandon quest mid fight, even in hardcore mode. It makes for low consequences because if you are going to die, you can just leave and retry.

Things I love:
Being able to give control to your friends to decide who moves first for more strategy
Being able to use abilities and still be allowed to pass control and then continues your turn later
Different story paths with different settings and enemies
Fate and magic resist are great mechanics. Would love to see more mechanics like these in future games.

Suggestions for Dark Quest 3:
Experiment with enemies summoning enemies. This could help make the final boss more difficult or serve as a tool for common rooms as well.
Add more levels
Make it more difficult
Improve achievement list
Maybe add a few more types of attacking other than melee, magic, arrows. Examples - a musician attacking with sound, a whip user for longer ranged melee, aoe attacks that have like a range of squares they can hit, etc.
Fix bugs, at least the ones that have to do with functionality\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou start off playing the barbarian character and explore various dungeons by speaker to the elder in the village.

As you accumulate gold you can buy potions/equipment or hire other heroes to join your cause. There are around 15-20 missions in total however I frequently replayed my favourite ones again and again to gain extra gold and magic pots which are essentially traded for upgrading skills. This game is essentially turn based and the below are a few tips:

For your turn each character you can use the following actions: a skill/moving and performing a normal attack for one such character. Note that I was using all my actions for one character and then moving to the next character.

I realise now that I had the option of say moving one character then using another character to perform a normal attack and then using another character to perform a special attack.

Use the mini map: Sometimes it can be hard to keep track of where you are and you might end up missing out on loot; places where you have not yet explored are visble but are faded out.

When you find the treasure rooms use the dwarf for this mission if you have upskilled him in the art of getting extra gold.

A decent game and well worth the money spent.
