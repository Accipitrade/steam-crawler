\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only played this in single player. I was not expecting much so was actually pretty pleasantly surprised how much fun I had even if it felt pretty old school. Recommended for a short romp through dungeons in a D&D sort of setting. You can unlock all six characters in one playthrough and after the early-game, most levels will allow you to use 3 at a time. There is no leveling up - instead you enhance your heroes through skillpoints (by finding magic potions in the dungeons) and better equipment as purchased through gold. Thus, it is quite easy to catch up an unused hero to your party.

Also mixing the cast can be beneficial as some excel at certain things than others. Some might easily die in areas with lots of ambushes for example, so speccing for survivability may be better. In other cases lots of AOE may help; and in some scenarios like against a lone boss, crowd control is useless.

It's a short game and can be completed in one weekend. I topped out just shy of 7 hours. After playing through once there isn't much point in playing again. There is a hardcore mode for challenging yourself or co-oping with others as reasons to keep playing if either is your things.

Anyways, fun short game, would like to see a sequel of sorts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot to enjoy about this game. Tragically, there are a handful of things which absolutely ruin the experience.

Pros:
-Diverse and interesting classes
-Diverse and interesting items and potions which can accent the classes
-Moderately challenging strategy
-Levels replayable at harder difficulty
-Downloadable maps via the steam workshop for potentially limitless gameplay

Cons:
-Multiplayer is broken. A decent chunk of the game is only playable by two characters. The majority of levels are playable by three characters, and at the end there are a handful of four character levels. Four players are allowed in a party, but the game is bugged and will not allow four players to play together at all. When you drag a character into the fourth portrait slot to begin the game, that character merely replaces one of the three characters there. Furthermore, each player is only allowed to control one character. So if you and a friend wanted to tag team a four player mission, you would be forced to do so with only two characters.

You would assume that a player could slot two characters in and control them in a multiplayer game. You would assume that since the game will adjust difficulty based on the number of times a level has been played, it would do the same to allow three players into a two player mission. You would assume that since the game allows four players to be in a party together that four players would be able to play together (or that it had even been tested, which it clearly wasn't). This is sadly not the case, whether it be a campaign mission or a downloadable map. On top of that, we encountered numerous bugs where we would have to back out of a level because somehow our games had fallen out of sync, or restart the client altogether because the game invites were broken.

This game would have gotten my recommendation with all the praise in the world for its charm and challenge. But since my group of four bought this game explicitly so that we could all play together, I can't give it a thumbs up. It's very disappointing to see the devs working on their next game while key components of this entry remain completely and utterly broken. All in all, this is least disappointing as a single-player experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's cute but very simplistic and the strategic layer is not very deep. I would not bother with this as there are much better titles in its kind.

PROS

- Cute art
- Simple and playable single handed, nice for when having launch or eating (should be a mobile game really)
- The fact that it is short to complete alevitates the fact that it is so simple, it doesn't overstay its welcome and ends just about when you're bored of it

CONS

- Lack of attention to details, it is often hard to click something because your hero or a monster is sitting on it or in front of it and blocking your click
- It surprises you with level ending, while you haven't done all the rooms... at first it's a very specific door, but other missions end when you kill a monster or click on an item, for this reason it's hard to know what is gonna make it end and you do miss loot because of that
- What characters and abilities are best is very obvious and there is not much variety in what heroes you pick because you do need your tank, your healer, and your magic damage, so the choices are too obvious
- Same thing with skills, the good ones are very far and above the others so you don't really strategize as much as just pick the obvious efficient skills\t\t\t\t\t
