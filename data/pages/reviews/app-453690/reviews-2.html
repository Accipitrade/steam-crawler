\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite really liking the first game I feel the sequel is a superior game so far. Mechanics play about the same as the first game with new graphics, features, and a little more detail to the fantasy world you’ve been cast into. Still a neat little turned based adventure with some improvements and flaws.

Pros
(+) Nicer animation that the first game – like a cross between Rune Scape and Original Warcraft.
(+) More hero types and party combinations. I find the archer to be slightly overpowered, but I’m glad she’s on our side.
(+) Potions no longer have a chance to do nothing unlike the first game, and their cost has been reduced.
(+) Wizard has more spells, but they can only be used once unless you use a potion.
(+) Better music. First game just had the Main Title and Town music.

Meh
(/) Still a loot-and-buy heavy game. Most of my money goes toward spent potions so I haven’t gotten a chance to try blacksmith items.
(/) I wish each hero had a chance to spot traps. Makes it necessary to either have the dwarf or archer in the party, but even they can only see a trap if its within 2 squares unless you upgrade the ability.
(/) Hardcore mode with achievement to be unlocked to punish players with Completionism Compulsive Disorder.

Cons
(-) Not a big fan of Wizard spells only being able to be used once. Wish there was a cooldown instead of needing a potion for it.
(-) Barbarian skill tree seems lackluster compared to the dwarf and archer. Lots of chance abilities but nothing immediate.
(-) Kinda lame having and 80% change of hitting or triggering an ability only to have it succeed a fourth of a time.
(-) Needs better chances of perception and sanity tests.
(-) You start with far less health in hardcore mode, making your health bottles going toward trap recovery.
(-) Multiplayer is pretty dumb - only get one hero per player so on missions where you need two or three can be pretty taxing. Also, sometimes items like potions and gear don't show up even if you selected them pregame.

Still a good game despite some flaws. Hope I get to finish this one unlike the first game.\t\t\t\t\t

1. Great old-school feel to this game.

2. Party-based, with 6 characters to choose from. You can control up to 4 characters per quest, but usually only 2 or 3.

3. While you're in the village, you can pay to resurrect dead characters.

4. You don't have to mess with leveling up, or learning where to best use skill & ability points. None of that stuff exists here.

5. Some good challenges exist, especially when you're outnumbered something like 8 to 3.

6. There are 2 quests which are nothing more than gold runs, and spending that gold right afterwards greatly helps your characters to prepare for what's coming later.

7. Although the game is simple in itself, the biggest "thinking part" of the game is deciding which characters to choose for a particular quest. There are some quests where 1 party will fail greatly while another party succeeds easily. You sometimes need to find balance between melee, ranged, and magic.


CON
1. Your party has to move and attack one-by-one, which can be a little tedious.

2. Despite what the description on the Store Page says, there is no crafting of equipment or potions. You just buy everything as you can afford to.

3. The selection of equipment items and potions is very limited.

4. Equipment items can only be swapped between characters while you're in the village.

5. Each character can only carry 1 or 2 potions at a time.

6. Even though the minimum specs are such that a wet potato should be able to run this game, I can't play for very long (about an hour at a time) because the game causes my computer to run hot.

7. When I tried multiplayer with a friend, we only got to do 2 quests. Then the game glitched, and we were invisible to each other. Could not find a fix, and so we could not continue to play together.\t\t\t\t\t
