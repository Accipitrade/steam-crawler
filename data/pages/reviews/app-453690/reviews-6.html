\r\n\t\t\t\t\tClickety-click-click........

Decent little Indie game but unfortunately suffers from a number of issues which make it tedious to play:

- The controls. For some brilliant reason, the developers decided this game had to be played entirely with the mouse. All those nice quality of life features you're accustomed to like pressing 'M' to bring up the map, ESC to close menu screens etc, are sadly lacking. Now you have to move the mouse and go clickety-click-click all over the place to do anything.
- The interface. You can eventually have a party of 4 characters but you have to move each character individually in a dungeon. Click once on the character to select them, click again on the tile you want them to move to. Click once again to move them to the selected tile. Rinse and repeat. For every single character in the group. You also can't rotate the camera so selecting a character that is partially obscured by another character or enemy on the screen can be a bit tricky.
- Fiddly mechanics. It's wonderful fun when my barbarian character misses 3 or 4 attacks consecutively against a goblin with a 20% block chance, yet that same goblin needs only 1 or 2 hits to hit my barbarian who has a 40% block chance against the goblin's attacks. And this sort of RNG bs seems to happen regularly. Makes me feel like I'm playing XCOM all over again.
- Poor tutorial for beginners and no in-game help function. You will have to look up any help for this game online.
- Repetitive music. Rather just turn it off completely.

I also came across an issue where my gold all disappears each time I enter a new dungeon. Not sure if this is a bug or how the game is supposed to work. What's the point of accumulating money if it just keeps disappearing each time I enter a new dungeon???\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes, it's a Hero Quest clone. (For those born in the 90s and later, Hero Quest was very popular fantasy board game.)

You play as fantasy characters (Barbarian, Dwarf Warrior, Paladin like Knight, Mage etc.) who have to walk through dungeons, slay monsters and search for treasure. Sounds good, doesn't it?
It's a turn-based game, so you have time for tactics. You can level up your heroes and buy new gear. The dungeons are only slightly repetitive, but there are a lot of little differences to keep it interesting. There are many monsters with different skill sets, nasty traps and hidden passages.

The style is a bit comic; it has certainly some tong-in-cheek humour and looks very nice. The sound is also fine.

If you like these kinds of games, I can recommend this one to you.

The only real negative: It's a bit short.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLook, I love HeroQuest, the board game that Dark Quest 2 is based on, so I had to try this out, especially for the price.

Unfortunately it just does not live up to HeroQuest's awesomeness. The lack of personalisation with the Character sheets is disappointing. They don't even have names and you cant name them. HeroQuest also had good gameplay balance and immersive rooms with doors and furniture for a board game and Dark Quest seems to be lacking that? Just the same old cliffs, bare rooms and corridors all over the place. Hard to believe a board game actually did that better.

The balance seems completely off. In HeroQuest you could play all 4 Characters right from the get go. Here you are forced to start with one character and earn the rest. And the Monster to Player ratio gets insanely unfair way too early in the game and the Monsters seem to have just as high chance of blocking hits as players, which most definitely was not the case in HeroQuest. The monsters only had one block shield on each dice, so it was like rolling a 6 to block each hit. The players had a much higher chance of blocking hits against the monster fodder, because they were Hero Adventurers of course! Not in Dark Quest 2, the overwhelming amount of monsters block many of their hits and the players fail to block a lot of theirs, leading to their death very quickly. Also in HeroQuest any player could search for traps. In dark quest you need the dwarf. Only the dwarf can search!

But then again, you can go back and redo the old easier quests, you have already done before moving on with new quests. That can allow you to gain more loot more easily. Which makes me feel like this has been done on purpose to create a repetitive grind in order to get your party well equipped enough to keep progressing through the campaign. It really cuts down that element of feeling like you're achieving something and making progress. It puts you in a boring ground hog day experience and I don't like that.

Dark Quest 2 is sort of a feel good bit of nostalgia for me being a HeroQuest board game fan, but at the same time its very disappointing and fails to deliver what truly made HeroQuest a great game. Plus you can't beat the social aspect of sitting around a table with a few friends and beers playing it. Sorry Dark Quest 2, I still love HeroQuest and you make me wish even more that I currently had some cool nerdy mates around to play it.\t\t\t\t\t
