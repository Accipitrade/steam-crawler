\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeing a big fan of HeroQuest, I was very excited to find DarkQuest II. It satisfies my cravings for dungeon crawling and it is fun to play. I wish there weren't so many limitations on potions and weapons, but I can deal with that. It makes you have to pick and choose, so it adds to the strategy. The only negative that I find is that all the adventures I have been on so far limit your party to three. That is what made HeroQuest so engaging is that you had a complete party--A Barbarian (fighter) A Dwarf (second fighter trap disabler and lockpick) An Elf (Archer and Magician) and A Wizard (spell caster). If you could run four party crews it would be a stronger game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would rate this with a neutral review, but only barely, if it was possible here on steam. But since there's no such option, then I can't in good conscience give this a positive rating and by extension I can't recommend this game.

Calling it a "poor man's Darkest Dungeon" would be giving this way mroe praise than it deserves. About the only good thing this game has going for it is the art which is indeed very well draw and has a good "dark fantasy" aesthetic.

But the gameplay was only "average" at its rare best moments, because otherwise it's very mediocre because the game is rather simplistic and even then it is poorly balanced. Some classes are way stronger (dark monk, dwarf, paladin knight) and more useful than the others, and the upgrade system is not that rewarding.

Also the UI and menu navigation is very poorly designed and reeks of mobile design.

If it's very cheap and you literally have nothing better to play then maybe get this for curiosity's sake, but I don't recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
