\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is a good old fashioned dungeon crawl, clearly inspired by Hero Quest but with references to other games as well (the blue potions look like Golden Axe, and there are Diablo references here and there as well).

Each level you have to select a small number of fighters, 3 for the most part of the game, which forces you to plan ahead carefully. If, for instance, you pick Barbarian (Melee DPS) and Knight (Tank/Healer) you have to decide whether you want a ranged attacker (Archer), a trap spotter (Dwarf) or a magic user (Wizard). You can also carry a limited number of potions. Different teams may lead to very different approach to the level.

The interface is not the best, but unless you are using a controller we are not talking of something that makes the game unplayable, in fact you get used to it quite quickly.

I finished the game at normal difficulty in about a day of play (taking pauses) so it's not particularly long. While levels can be re-played, this probably makes more sense in co-op. Hopefully new levels, and new classes, will add some life to a game that is overall very fun and pleasant.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: Potentially fun casual turn-based game, ruined by offensively bad UI and controls.

WARNING: Quite the wall of text incoming.

The good:

First of all, if you are looking for a tactical challenge rivaling X-COM or Battle Brothers, look somewhere else.
What we have here is a rather simple and casual turn-based game with some light RPG attached to it.
Not every game has to be super hard and complex, so this isn't a downside to me.

You are in control of a bunch of heroes, of which you can take a number with you on each dungeon.
Dungeons are relatively short (I don't think you'll need more than 15 or 20 minutes for each) and filled with traps to step into, monsters to fight and lots of loot to find.

Graphics and sound are adequate, if a bit too cartoonish for my taste. It does fit the cheesy tone, though.

While casual, it isn't fully mindless, you actually have to make sure not to do really dumb moves that would get your characters killed and have to make good use of your abilities - as well as try to send a well-rounded team.
That said, the game is on the easy side of things and I very much recommend playing on Hardcore, or at the very least Normal. Easy will most likely put you into sleep.

As you progress through the game, more heroes will be unlocked, items can be bought and found magic potions are used to increase abilities and skills of your heroes.

I'd say it would be a fun little casual turn-based RPG to be played in small sessions or on the side.


Unfortunately, the game has such significant downsides that make enjoying it almost impossible:

The UI and controls must have been designed with maximum waste of time in mind.
I have no other explanation for the things I will list now:

There are no hotkeys. I kid you not. 2018 and someone releases a turn-based tactical RPG without any hotkeys at all. ESC does not open the menu, nor does it close open menus. Tab opens no inventory or map, neither does M or I. Space or Enter does not end your turn. Everything requires moving the mouse around. It is a mind-boggling waste of time.

No minimap, either. And the map that is there opens not focused on your current location so you have to scroll around each time to find your room. What a waste of time, again.

Leaving a section of the dungeon requirew you to move every single one of your heroes to the exit manually. Every time, each section. When having four heroes with you, that means clicking eight times (!!!!) to leave an area. I would somewhat understand this, if the game allowed you to split your group, but that's not even possible. The developers obviously didn't care to have any kind of different logic attached for singleplayer vs co-op. A baffling waste of a player's time.

Menus in the town are their own version of "how to waste the maximum amount of time of my players".
Is your idea of a good level-up interface to drag-and-drop a UI element onto a skill in order to improve it, instead of just clicking the ability?
Do you like having to drag-and-drop hero portraits around to rest, instead of clicking on them or - even better - having them auto-selected as there is no thinking involved in selecting them anyway?
Just how much do you love having to repeat "click sex worker, select menu, drag-and-drop a SINGLE hero, confirm" for every hero you want to receive a temporary bonus, despite the fact that other menus at least offer multiple slots to drag multiple heroes into?
And don't you totally dig having to drag-and-drop heroes into the slots for a mission instead of auto-selecting or just clicking them?
If the answer to any of these is "no", then... well, bad luck.
I don't know if the logic behind this nonsense was "immersion", but a drag-and-drop action is not immersive, it as an utter waste of my time.

The character that has the ability to uncover traps in an area around him will still gladly walk into them, if the move command was given before the trap was discovered. Brillant. If you like moving in mini-steps to waste your time a little.

Good luck clicking on anything to top-left to top of one of your characters. The sprites block the view, so clicking anything there is quite the hassle. This is solved by moving the character around so you are able to click, e.g. on the exit.

The cherry on top? No linux support despite using Unity. Obviously, a dual-boot or virtual machine to test-run the binaries would have been asking too much. At least it does run fine under Wine.

All of this gives off the impression that the last 30 years of game development never happened, the developer never played a PC game or what we have here is a weird mobile port. But the game isn't even available for mobile!
I have rarely been this dumbfounded by shortcomings in a game as much as here. All of these could have been avoided by anyone ever having played another PC game taking a look at it and saying "Uhm... you might want to change this".

Conclusion:
Should the horrible UI and controls ever be fixed (or rather, something decent be implemented in the first place), I'd actually recommend this. Until then, if you do not have a Gandhi-like patience for a game wasting your time without any need, avoid this.

And finally, dear developer, repeat after me:
I shall not waste a player's time. I shall not waste a player's time. I shall not waste a player's time. I shall not waste a player's time. I shall not waste a player's time. I shall not waste a player's time. I shall not waste a player's time. I shall not waste a player's time...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think that this game could have a lot of potential if more content / variety was added. For example, crafting is limited to stock items - no means for customization and once the available items have been crafted, that is it - no more. I think the game description of 'crafting' is therefore a little misleading. It would also be more fun if monsters dropped some loot, or you could find the odd useful hidden item, rather than just gold / items to sell.

Monster types are limited to variations on Orcs, Goblins or the odd Chaos Warrior. Traps and Secret doors are not really challenging - i.e, no 'search for' option. The variety of room type is also limited, and quickly become tedious / boring. What about some outdoor environmments? Also carrying a max of two potions? Seriously? Even the simpler, but more accurate homage to Heroquest 'Mighty Dungeons' (I have the tablet version) allows you to carry multiple potions.

I know it pays homage to the original Heroquest, but what about bringing in some of the concepts from the 'Advanced Heroquest' game? - this was more of a half-way house toward full AD&D and could be more fun, though I guess this would probably require a redesign in coding, though I am sure a few elements could still be added to enrich the game.

A plus point, however, is the availability of the workshop and user maps - some of these have been fun and more challenging than the main quest lines.

Note that I cannot comment on any networking issues, as I don't play multiplayer.

All in all 5/10 for me, though I do like it, it is just that it needs more. I would recommend this, once finally released and out of EA, but only on sale, not at full price, at least not until further content / variety was added. Obviously my comments are based on the EA version, but - it is the late EA and so should therefore be very close to the final product, baring the very last map.\t\t\t\t\t
