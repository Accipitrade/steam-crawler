\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not often do you find a computer game that can function well as both a video game and a table top dungeon crawler. It's turn base mechanics are pretty simple, you get moves and abilities based off of Golden Axe Potions you find. (Ok, well they look like the magic potions from Golden Axe) and you got yourself a grand ol time hacking and slashing green skinned freaks.

Each class has something to offer beyond their more obvious roles... Warrior is tanky and loves to do high damage, where as the Dwarf is tanky but loves to disable traps, Archer is high damage but good on utility like the dwarf...the Knight is tanky but can heal... the Monk kills everything and the Wizard... Wizard's stuff... and sometimes makes obscene balloon animals (not really).

Though...the campaign itself seems short with a tight curve on difficulty in some respects - the game shines really bright on 2 aspects. Yes you can replay levels but each successful time ups the difficulty...and you can play custom maps that add to your "Phat Lewt" as you cool kids would say. (No? Kids don't say that anymore!?)

Though while some custom maps are great... others are just straight awful - but that's the blessing and curse of user generated content.

Overall I'd say it's a fun lil romp though personally I'm curious about actually playing this with a couple of friends and seeing how well this translates to multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t- Yes it's simple

- Yes there is a version for phones

But that doesn't mean it's not fun. On the phone version there is no online co-op, so that's a major difference.

And the simplicity was a huge part of the appeal of the original Dark Quest. It's just here it's gone isometric and has character models, where before it was top down tiles.

It's more elaborate, but it's still inspired by a tabletop for ages 9 to adult. So that means it's not like these huge RPG's with endless stuff to learn.

If you're young, you can just get on and enjoy. And if not, this is something for when you're not wanting to go to the effort of quick real-time reactions, or anything too stressful. This is one that can just be played for a quick quest or two at a time, not endless massive sessions to take over your whole life.

It's a little resource management, finding then remembering where the exit was, grabbing loot, managing combat, levelling up... rinse and repeat.

One can tut at spelling errors or the occasional clunk like needing to move a character out of the way to do something with another, or reveal the tile you want to action. But none of that is really that important in a casual experience like this, where the emphasis is on simple enjoyment;

Bottom line: Unpretentious fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI beat the game in normal mode.
7/10

It's a dungeon crawler kind of game with simple rooms to navigate until you reach the end or complete a simple objective.

The game is a bit basic and there isn't much depth to the skills, items, or story.
It feels like it could have been developed for mobile phones.
However, I did enjoy the time I spent beating the game (over 13 hours), and that alone is worth the sale price.

The graphics are pretty good but not 'amazing'.
The sound effects are basic but efficient.
The music is actually quite good.

I would recommend this game on sale but not full price.\t\t\t\t\t
