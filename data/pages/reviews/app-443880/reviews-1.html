\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun rogue-like. It's turn based but animated very well so that it doesn't look it at first glance. Great character and enemy designs. It's very hard. Took me 23 hours to get a win. Your stats are determined by placing cards in a grid, and the cards affect each other so you have to arrange them right. You can also gain powers that recharge when you explore and powers that run on tokens you get from permanately discarding cards. Combat is mostly deterministic and based on a day/night cycle that shifts every 5 turns or so. Enemies will be on defense or offense based on the time of day, and many enemies have unique properties you have to keep in mind too. Really fun, really hard, and really interesting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter Nothernlion’s recommendation about this game, saying it’s a hidden gem buried under all the Mass Effect Andromeda drama, and he definitely wasn’t wrong. At the time I’m writing this review I’ve played for about 2 hours, only making it to the third world once. Your success in this game relies the game’s perfect use of perfect a balance of skill and RNG. Gameplay wise the game uses an interesting combat system with the use of 2 stats, offence and defense, and a day/night cycle that makes all monsters easier or harder to fight. It explains these mechanics very well in the tutorial. Not to mention the game’s amazing art style and soundtrack. Another little quirk I thought was nice is the game’s “not-so-serious” atmosphere it built itself. Overall I’d recommend this game at full price.\t\t\t\t\t
