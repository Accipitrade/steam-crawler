\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuick Score: 3/5

Notes: The game started off pretty good. Then it started to get a bit scuffed. Then it got really bad. Then it got so bad it started to be kind of good again for comedic reasons.

The normal playthrough is an absolute joke and you could easily clear the whole game without ever dying. The hard playthrough is quite substantionally harder and the very final stage specifically is an absolute nightmare. I 100% the game in 2.3 hours, and probably almost 1 full hour of that was on the very final section of the last stage on hard mode.

The biggest issues with the game, and they are BIG, would be the following.
-There are multiple enemies and even full blown boss fights that you can just kill around the corner without them ever even being able to shoot you
-The camera can be incredibly ass at times, and it just so happens it is at its absolute worst on the very final boss fight
-The game has some weird ♥♥♥♥ happen sometimes with your cursor where in the middle of some fights, the fps will dip, and your cursor sensitivity will spike erratically making it very difficult to control. This could maybe just be a mouse issue, never tried the game on controller.
-Some bullet patterns are incredibly difficult to understand due to the camera perspective. Hard to get a grasp on exactly where the bullets are in 3d space and how to navigate through them
-The options menu and layout is hideous, make sure you set your resolution settings, even if you are on full screen, otherwise the game defaults to a comically low resolution that looks terrible.

Overall though, despite all the glaring problems, I would say it was a relatively fun, and a pretty unique gameplay experience at least from anything I have ever played. Would suggest giving it a try if you can ever catch it on decent sale, just go in expecting a ♥♥♥♥ show.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview after completing hard mode.

I'm a renegade sector shill and usually like everything they put out, but I have a hard time recommending them to people. They tend to be interesting and fun design wise but lack the content and polish to justify their usually high prices. This is the first game from the dev that I think is worth the price asked even if you aren't already a fan of the dev.

Very interesting take on cabal style shooters. Definitely leans more into the bullet hell aspect of games like Thunder Kid which is good IMO since the bosses of those games far outshined the platforming. For Escape from Terror City, the stage design is excellent in addition to the bosses due to the more focused bullet hell gameplay.

Hard mode was a real treat as its truly brutal stuff. All enemies are buffed and there are dozens of new enemies added to the game. Re-learning each of the stages was a very engaging experience and very rewarding to finally complete.

Ultimately the game suffers from the same issues as most renegade sector games. The game is very short, 1-2 hours for just normal mode. Options are severely lacking which limits accessibility. Assuming you do hard mode however the gameplay and design is more than enough to justify the asking price IMO.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHot Dam is this game fun!

It reminds me of contra or metal slug, but in a 3d world environment. Sure it may not have as much polish as a shoe from the 18th century, but there wasnt any glaring flaws. I had the camera clip in to a wall while playing when i got to close the the back wall of a certian part of a map, only ran in to it once, and when i did, i just moved fowards a little bit and i was back to seeing the whole game.

The level design is amazing, and what i mean by this is, every level has a choice for its path, theres a fork where you can choose where to go, you will get some unique mini boss's then it will branch back with the other path before the final boss of the level.

I wish the game had some gun power up's. Its just the one gun through out the 4 levels which dose get boring, but the amount if enemies makes up for it. All their move sets even change once you go on hard difficulty.

Overall took 1h to beat the game on normal with 6 deaths, working on hard mode right now but i dont think it will be such a easy feat as normal was. Id highly recommend this to anyone who knows not to go in expecting perfection from a small studio, you will greatly enjoy this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far I've played through Escape from Terror City to completion once, and I definitely plan to go back and bang my head against the hard mode.
It's an over-the shoulder, semi on-rails shooter in the style of Cabal and Wild Guns with multiple paths through each level and secret shortcuts. Normal difficulty is moderate, but not too bad, especially if you've played a lot of bullet hell shooters.
The art style is right up my alley, with pixel art textures on low-poly models (think early-mid 90s Sega) and the dialogue evokes poor translations of the past but happens to be in perfect, enjoyable English.
There was one bug I encountered a few times in which barricades went up before you fully entered the next area, blocking you from progression, but checkpoints are very forgiving, so it was never too much trouble.
But otherwise, if you're looking at this game and have some inkling that you might like it, you probably will. Big recommend from me.\t\t\t\t\t

The game has a weird, clunky aesthetic that I'm into. There's good enemy variety, weapons, different levels, etc. to hold one's interest for at least a little while.

It does feel a bit unfinished. The camera skips around and the aiming is clunky in ways that feels less like a feature of the aesthetic and more of an issue with the game itself. If you look at the game pictures, you'll understand the aesthetic and gameplay pretty clearly, I think. If that looks like a fun distraction to you, I recommend checking it out. So far, I'm enjoying it, and I will be finishing the game as soon as I can take another break from work.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a nightmare made playable. The music is disorienting and offputting in a way I find absolutely captivating. The graphics... well they're clearly cursed, but it's very much an aesthetic. The gameplay itself is fine. The platforming is very slippery but they don't ask you to do too much of it except in one key choke point that I found mildly enraging in the second level. The bullet hell is wildly overstated since the hitboxes of the bullets seems to be much smaller than their size would lead you to expect. The shooting is inoffensive, and that's about the best and worst I can say for it. You're much better off using a controller since the lag on an analog stick won't feel anywhere near as alien and frustrating as the the input speed reduction the game forces on players using a mouse.

I like that Escape From Terror City offers branching pathways even when they're not necessarily made clear to the player, for example the train level where you can fall off the top of the train and continue through the cars. I assumed I was just dead but was delighted to find it was a completely alternate route. Upon completing the game it unlocked a hard mode I've yet to try but honestly I don't feel particularly drawn to it. For me this is a game you play as an experience, I came for the feeling of stepping into someone else's nightmare, not to experience one of my own.

I don't know that this game really offers up enough of that to justify it's $10 asking price, but it's really hard to put a price on how you experience a piece of art and what it means to you. For me there just wasn't enough on offer here beyond the initial rush of diving into something supremely strange and alien. I'm still recommending it though, because I think it's an experience worth having, especially if you catch it on a sale.\t\t\t\t\t
