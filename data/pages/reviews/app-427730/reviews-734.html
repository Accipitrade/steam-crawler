\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is, without a doubt, fun. It is very fun, can provide quite a few hearty laughs, and even can prove to be a bit of a challange, as the game is, even admitted by the developer, asymetric favoring the baby. The gameplay quickly turns father players into either bumbling panicked messes trying to baby proof anything and everything as fast as possible, or a cool and collected father, going from drawer to shelf, covering power outlets and locking cabinets. Meanwhile, baby players turn into death machines, trying to find every way possible to get an edge up on their dads, drinking bleach, using their blocks to reach high places, etc. etc.

While I do rather reccomend Who's Your Daddy, this reccomendation comes with a bit of a disclaimer. The fun and laughs this games offers wear off rather fast, at least in its current state. Some of the most deadly items to the baby spawn in the same location every game, while all the items useful to the father are completely randomized, and the time it takes trying to search through all the drawers and cabinets to find them, is just time that the baby is given to find one of its, rather easy, deaths. Objects like knives and forks are a dime a dozen, and it makes it very easy for the baby to overwhelm the father and get an easy, yet entertaining, death by electric shock.

Overall, for its (current) $5 price tag, Who's Your Daddy is a fun little package filled with good laughs and somewhat addicting gameplay. The game is still in Early Access, and still has room and time to improve on its formula, but until then, be aware that the $5 you're spending are going to yield abour 2-5 hours of fun gameplay, before it starts becoming a bit stale and unfun. I hope to see the game improve, and I can see potential, but at it's current stage, it's only good for a short laugh, before the game becomes a bit boring. Buy at your own risk.\t\t\t\t\t
