\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat started out as a joke on Funnyjunk.com from a despised, sad cat man, turned into the family friendly game of the year. "Who's Your Daddy" is the sleeper hit of 2015, the brain child of Thehumanidiot, turning decades of volunteer dad work into a full hands on simulated experience.

The goal of 'Who's your daddy' is to make sure that your spawnling doesn't remove himself from the gene pool too quickly (High School is the most appropriate time for this.) You do this by making sure that the baby can't eat dozens of batteries, stick forks into electrical sockets, and drinking blue juice under the sink. Alternatively, if you are playing as the baby character, it's your job to be as much of a little ♥♥♥♥ as humanly possible. This is more choice than a Telltale video game.

The graphics are nothing to write home about. The dad is a heteronormative cis white male, with the baby able to change its appearance based on what actions it has undertaken. It's possible to turn the baby into Shrek if you eat too much blue juice, for example.

The soundtrack consists of one song that constantly loops. It is actually the newest hit single by young money rapper Drake, where he dances like a complete bozo on film. The song plays in the main menu, and from the in game radio, a statement of how every single song on the radio is exactly the same. Not only are we treated to a parent trainer, we also have a strong message about the media today.

All in all, 'Who's your Daddy' is an ideal game for anyone that wants to have a nice friendly game. The price is cheap, the graphics inducing nostalgia, and the gameplay is simple yet effective. I fully anticipate when we're treated with 'Who's your Daddy' at EVO 2016.\t\t\t\t\t
