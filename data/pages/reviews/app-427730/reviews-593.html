\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWho's your daddy? No, seriously, who is it? Because the lack of parenting seen in this game is horrifying!

------------------------------------------------------------

Gameplay
In a game that closely resembles cat and mouse antics, you are tasked with the objective either committing or preventing a suicide. Sounds dark, right? It is. Who's Your Daddy is a unique game that throws you into a 1v1 match against a son and his father. As the baby, you will attempt to kill yourself through any means possible. See that fork over there? Yeah, jab it in the outlet. That bleach looks quite tasty, too. Yum!

Are you a good enough father to prevent said death? Baby proof the whole place and keep your child alive. Test yourself in this humourously dark game.

Graphics
This game is not in Early Access anymore (updated, thanks Beruka) but, in my opinion, feels like it is. There are so many graphical glitches, sometimes it just gets downright annoying. You will fall through the Earth randomly or see abnormal human features (which brings new meaning to the phrase "knees to chest! Knees to chest!"). This is just naming a few things though...

Audio
Cute scuffling sounds, squeaky toys, and an annoying soundtrack. This pretty much sums up the noise that is Who's Your Daddy. As the game progresses you will eventually forget about the repetitive music, but if you're the type of gamer who notices everything... just turn it off. It's quite annoying.

---------------------------------------------------------

Pros
+ Straight-forward mission: no gimmicks or confusion
+ Great multiplayer (some of the most fun that I've had with my friends)
+ Various other features that allow you to explore the game more (Daddy's Little Mistakes, etc.)
+ Cute sound effects that really compliment the game; soundtrack was annoying over time though
+ There are people to play with (online), but I'd recommend playing with friends to avoid aggressive trolls

Neutral
± It would be nice if we could spawn in random locations instead of just in the same spots. This way it throws off the repetitive nature of the game and it's more challenging. As it stands, my friend can just stand outside the door at the ready for me

Cons
- Like stated previously, this game has some graphical issues. You will fall through the Earth sometimes and it just gets irritating. Nothing that a "quit" button can't fix, but hopefully this gets polished soon
- Easy to troll whomever is the baby; my friend and I had a go at one another where we just kept holding each other hostage. Perhaps a "pick up" timer could be implemented? If there is one, then I never noticed it because I legit held my friend until the game ended

---------------------------------------------------------

This game is a great game to play with friends. There really is no denying it. Just help "Baby Beanie" get into the Afterlife - he really wants to go there.

Overall
7/10
