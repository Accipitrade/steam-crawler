\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to be honest, I do not like this game. No not because of me being a cynical fudgsicle who hates my own grandma, I actually really respect her, she's great. No not because it was made with Unity, although the majority of Unity games are like a horse stable ground, just a load of crap. But because the gameplay, the bugs, the glitches etc ruin the experience for me.
So because people do seem to like this game, I will try my best to show both sides of the arguement. So witn that being said lets get into the pros and cons, shall we..?
Pros
-The stupidness. Now this may come as a surprise to you but when I first played this game with my little sister and Steam friends, I really enjoyed this game. I laughed so hard I cried several times. The character models are goofy, the ways to die are goofy and the ways to protect the kid are ... actually quite serious. It was fun to play.
-The couch co-op. Whilst the game is definitely easier to play local with a supported controller, to the point where otherwise you have to share a keyboard which is never good, I will look over that fact becuase couch co-op is surprisingly good. But nothing outstanding about the co-op is shown so I'm gonna move on.
-The achievements. With pretty much all of the achievements being hidden, the game only gives you the objective in game and doesn't tell you how to go about doing so. It really adds to the challenge such as microwaving pills or cleaning up the toys... well actually they are both explanatory, but the reward for doing them are fabulous. Not only do you unlock a steam achivement and a physical in-game trophy, you also unlock special powers for doing these chores. Excluding cleaning up the toys that is just being clean. These range from becoming SUPER-DADDY THE GREATEST SUPERDAD ON THE PLANET! or... well... I can't think of anymore but you get the point.
Cons
-The controls. It is not easy to control this game. It is considerably easier using a controller than keyboard (again bringing myself back around to my first point) as controls are conflicting often resulting in standing frozen in place, not being able to leave menus, not being able to use objects or even not being able to move the camera around. It is super frustrating and is sometimes impossible to play.
-The gameplay. Oh god it's boring after like what the second time?! I don't care how many different ways there are to die when either drinking bleach or burning myslef are the easiest options. I don't care how many ways there are to save the baby, I'm fencing him and and always having pills on me. You see what I mean? It's too easy to win (especially as baby) or lose (especially as daddy) and the matches always end the same, baby wins or daddy almost stops baby from dying in time but fails.
-The graphics. The Witcher 3, beautiful environments and stunning visuals, 2015. Dying Light, great open but tightly cramped cities with great visuals, 2015. Ori and The Blind Forest, stunning artstyle, stunning environments and (again) stunning visuals, 2015. Who's your Daddy, oh god put it in the bin and set that bin on fire whilst performing an excorcism on your Steam Account to rid yourself of these ugly demons, 2015. I can usually play a game with terrible graphics. I mean I like Unreal Gold, DOOM 1993 and Max Payne (although I haven't played Max Payne yet.) But, Who's Your Daddy is so bad sometimes that I get so distracted by the terrible textures, that make you wonder if the game is still being worked on, because it looks like a missing grey developer texture. But no, this game is fully released and Evil Tortilla have made another game Zooicide. I'm done. For a game that came out in 2015, it should not look like a farm field, a minefield of brown stinky turds. And it isn't even the limitations of the Unity engine, Cuphead was made on Unity and (subjectively) the game looks outstanding!
Overall, I just don't like this game. It was fun at first, but now it's aged to pieces, the game looks like a Unity asset rip and the gameplay is terrible, 2/10. I wish I could refund.\t\t\t\t\t
