\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt a glance, Who's Your Daddy might look like just another one of those garbage games that somehow passed through greenlight - but unlike most of those games, Who's Your Daddy is actually surprisingly fun. The game's concept is fairly simple, in which one player plays as a baby and tries to kill themself using a variety of household objects, while the other player controls a dad trying to protect the baby, providing a shockingly realistic portrayal of actual parenthood. There are a variety of ways for the baby to kill themself, like eating batteries or dropping a toaster in the sink, giving the game replayability by challenging yourself to kill yourself in creative ways. The dad gets various objects to try to protect the baby, like a tool to lock drawers and a baby grabbing handle to temporarily pick up the baby, and can also do chores around the house to get powerups like Dad Bod, which allows you to carry heavier things. The game is only in alpha right now, but the developer seems to be fairly diligent in adding regular updates that add new features and ways to die, and at five dollars per copy I think it's a pretty good game to get together with a friend and have a few hours of laughter.\t\t\t\t\t
