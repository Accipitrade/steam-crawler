\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've only ever given one Chinese visual novel a chance before this, and while I can't say I had a bad experience, I couldn't bring myself to finish it all. It was too long and the gameplay loop too tedious to make it worthwhile. Here, after only eighty minutes of playtime (as of this review), is an experience that truly surprised me.

LoveChoice is an anthology of short stories revolving around what it means to love. At the time of this review, there are only three stories available, with the promise of more to come. I will review them individually, recounting my experiences with each of them.

GAME:
This is the most straightforward of the three stories, presenting itself as a typical visual novel with some cutesy art up front. I followed my gut instinct during my first reading; like many people who reviewed this game, I ended up with a bad ending. I couldn't unlock the second story without getting one more ending, so I tried choices I hadn't done. For some reason, it didn't work. It might've been by accident or something, but I realized that the game was telling me something. I was going through the motions, blindly picking choices as if I were brute-forcing myself to a good ending. It works for most VNs, but not this one. I won't spoil what it is you have to do to get the good ending, but I was frankly surprised at how I had to think outside the box.

DISTANCE:
I can see why they had to lock DISTANCE behind GAME, because DISTANCE expects you to have gotten the good ending in GAME. It will test your knowledge of the new mechanics and bend your mind and heart trying to get this couple together. You don't really have to do anything special beyond that, but you'll feel as involved in the relationship of these new characters as the ones in GAME. Things get a bit deep here; the characters feel quite human in the decisions they make.

DETECTIVE:
I wasn't sure what this story would do to shake up the gameplay, or even what story angle it would explore. These stories are all quite simple when you look at them, but there's beauty in their simplicity. This story follows a married woman who suspects her husband is cheating on her with another woman. The game pretty much turns into a point-and-click adventure as you scour the house for clues pointing toward the husband's infidelity. The outcome (again, without spoiling) was quite unexpected, and it fit quite well with the general tone of these stories.

From these three stories alone, I have high hopes for the remainder that are being written. On top of that, the presentation of each story is fantastic, from the art to the music to (in some respects) the dialogue. I admit that the translation does have its problems (I ended up catching a line that went completely untranslated in GAME), but I understood enough to follow along with the story. Also, minor complaint, but I wish this game had a 'skip read text' button. :P

Bottom line: it's cute, and a breath of fresh air. I'd even go so far as to say that this is what Doki Doki Literature Club should have been. Looking forward to the rest of the stories! :D\t\t\t\t\t
