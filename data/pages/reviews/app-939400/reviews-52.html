\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLoveChoice is a collection of, at the time of writing, three short stories that resolve around the topics love and relationships. The stories vary in their length with a reading time of 10 to 30 minutes for each story. This surely isn't much length-wise but it's surely enough to leave an striking impression behind.

When I played the first story for the first time I just thought it's just a run-of-the-mill Visual Novel with dialog-based choices that determine which ending you will get. After a few minutes I reached my first ending and thought "Well, it's probably a normal ending" and I was quite shocked when I read the letter with all sentences being red and getting the message that the couple split up.
In the second run I decided to take the other options and still got a bad ending. Just why?!? There aren't any other options, or are they? In my third run I noticed that there's more than just the dialog choices as there's other ways to interact with the couple and the environment in nearly all scenes which can change the outcome. And this is probably the part where this collection of stories really begins to shine.
Instead of presenting you just one dialog box after another with a few choices in between, you also get some basic gameplay of point-and-click-adventures. After getting the hang of it most of those interactions are easy to assume, but there are still some which aren't that intuitive. One example is a scene where you have to scroll the mouse wheel to see more dialog-options. There wasn't any kind of visual hint for this possibility and I guess most will either stumble over this by accident or by looking up a guide (for me it was the latter option). Nonetheless this is only a small flaw and you will get the hang of the possible interactions in a follow-up run.

About the stories themselves:
I don't want to spoil much about them as it's best to experience them with as few knowledge as possible. All I will say is that it's a nice change of pace from all "standard VNs" as the couples are presented in a grounded way and you will most likely feel familiar with some of their traits. Personally I felt reminded of myself more than one time. And they surely show you that it's not only the words that matter but also the actions, but even if you give your best you might not get the best outcome and have to accept fate.

Presentation-wise LoveChoice is quite minimalistic but still nice to look at with a mix of crayola- and pastel-like CG-scenes. Personally I enjoyed the main theme but otherwise I have to say that I barely noticed the music, but I have to say that is barely a thing I notice while reading a VN.

Overall I would recommend this to anyone that's slightly interested in stories about love and relationships and wants something with a grounded story. It might help you to see things from a new perspective.\t\t\t\t\t
