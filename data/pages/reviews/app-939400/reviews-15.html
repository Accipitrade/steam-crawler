\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDuring the Lunar New Year sale, this game was under $1 and had overwhelmingly positive reviews, so I just mindlessly picked it up while buying a couple other games. After playing it over the course of 3 streams, this short game has proven itself as an innovative, insightful, and profound look into love an relationships. I'm going to put up front that if you like romance, are in a relationship, or are looking to be in a relationship, you will probably like this game, and should just buy / play it now to avoid any possible minor spoilers.

Gameplay

LoveChoice is split into 3 subgames,"Love.Game", "Love.Distance", and "Love.Detective" each with their own self contained story. While, at first, LoveChoice seems to play like a typical visual novel, you come to find that having a successful relationship requires much more than trying to find the right reply from a list of canned responses. Each subgame is also unique in how it uses the gameplay to tell it's story. "Love.Game" plays more like a typical visual novel, "Love.Distance" plays like a hidden object / spot the difference game, and "Love.Detective" plays like an escape room. But the thing that makes this game special is that if you coast along playing the game as you expect, you're not going to get the best endings. It's when you go above and beyond, breaking through the established norms of the genre, do you become a better lover, and get the good endings.

Art

The art is cute and nicely stylized, and the cgs make for wonderful desktop backgrounds which can be downloaded through the game itself. The music is nice but is never very remarkable outside of the song played during the credits

Story

Each of the 3 stories feel extremely real, to the point where I legitimately thought some of them were retellings / expansions on events that happened in real life. Akaba Studio also manages to cover a wide range of emotions through it's storytelling, showing you the exciting beginnings of a relationship, the problems that arise, the complacency that can form. "Love.Distance" made me tear up by showcasing the bitter reality of sometimes needing to let go of the person you love the most. And "Love.Detective" made me tear up by showing one of the most romantic anniversary gifts I've seen.

Overall

In it's 2 hr runtime LoveChoice made me smile, tear up, and reflect upon my own relationship. I found it's subversion of visual novel mechanics to be refreshing and also meaningful to the themes the game presents. I believe LoveChoice presents love in a way only that can only be experinced through the medium of video games.

//////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

LoveChoice is a beautiful look into relationships and can be enjoyed by couples, fans of romance, and just about anyone.\t\t\t\t\t
