\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was really cute and touching. The music is good, the art design is really simple but cute. Each story, and each ending is well done and worth reading. I don't want to talk too much about it as it's better left experienced.

There are some translations/grammatical errors for English in a few places, but it's easily overlooked as you get the gist of it anyway. There are three different stories, each with their own gameplay gimmicks. The first has this minigame which can be tedious if you're going for everything as if you don't get 11/11 of the things you have to re-try. The problem with that is you have to wait for the entire minigame to end before being able to retry it which gets tedious. Most of my time was waiting for that minigame to end so I could retry

Otherwise it's fun, it's just clicking on a thing that shows up an not hitting the bad ones. Eventually it hides one under a hat and shuffles it around to find, those are always fun to me. And the last one has you needing to follow two hats with pop ups to distract you. The third has a short match three minigame in it which is cute.

The only other criticism I have of the game is it doesn't convey what you are allowed to do as a player so without a guide or just trying everything out of the sake of it, you might miss how to do some things. I tried to wait at one point, but nothing seemed to happen so I just assumed I couldn't wait. But you can. Some games you have to do certain things so when a game doesn't require that it's nice to know there's more freedom so you don't find yourself confused.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has truly been a pleasant surprise for me. It is simple in it narrative structures yet I couldn't call it shallow. It approaches relationships realistically without being dark, depressing and toxic. I really love how it manages to capture, both, the sweetness and difficulty of ordinary relationships -- making the game endearing, engaging and relatable (to me, at least). I'll admit that I was skeptical of the praise after my first, surface, playthrough. But once I realized that there were so many little things to find that completely change the narrative, I was hooked!

Performance-wise I haven't encountered any bugs or crashes (for those concerned about it being an early access game.) The English for the Love Detective story could use a bit of smoothing out, but it's nothing that makes the scenario unintelligible.

I sincerely hope that the devs are not finished with this game and that we will see more like this from them in the future. This game is very clearly a labor of love and it's been beautifully executed.\t\t\t\t\t
