\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLoveChoice is a game with three different storylines, each with their own sets of choices and endings, with a price of roughly $1.99 USD as of the time of writing this, not including additional content.

And to be honest? I wouldn't argue if they bumped that up to $4.99 or so. It's worth it.

Not quite enough content to be worth $10, but definitely more than $2 or so.

LoveChoice is a sweet and charming game created and produced by a small Chinese developer with a limited team and limited resources. Nonetheless, it's clever, adorable, and touched my heart. The choices don't always make sense, and some are rather difficult to figure out in order to get the endings you wants, but if you don't, please don't get discouraged! And while the English translation may be a bit below-grade, it doesn't detract from the story or make it difficult to understand. All-in-all, it's a very cute little game, and well worth the money, especially for a cheaper game like this. I absolutely adored it, and if you like cute romance indie games for a low, low price, then you probably will too!

Also, the music was great. :3\t\t\t\t\t

Revision:

Alright so I gave it a second chance after seeing the developer's response to this review.

My opinion hasn't changed.
First of all, the first big mistake here is that this is a love game and I shouldn't be looking for hidden ways to progress. It kills the flow of the gameplay and it's sometimes unnecessarily difficult to find the alternative answer.

The big elephant in the room, however, is how AWKWARD these dialogue options are. They don't get any better once you find the "correct" answer. There's barely any character development so most of the times I don't care about either two and I feel that I don't have to work at all for them to "fall in love". Therefore, I'm just presented with a relationship that starts and ends in a matter of a couple of screens. If that's appealing to you, well, maybe this game will please you.\t\t\t\t\t
