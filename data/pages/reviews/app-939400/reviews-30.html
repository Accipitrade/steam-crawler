\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's something really charming about small passion projects. They don't tale a lot of your time, but they get their point across and you feel a strong connection to the developer and the message they are trying to get across. LoveChoice is an example of one of these games.

LoveChoice is a small Visual Novel game that breaks you into 3 different stories. The first story is how a man and woman meet at a Game Jam and they develop a relationship. Depending on your actions they will stay together or break up. The second story is about a boy named a Stan and a girl named Grace. They start as childhood friends and eventually become more, but their dreams and aspirations separate them into a long distance relationship. The final story is about a wife who is nervous that her husband is hiding something from her and decides to search the house to see what he is hiding.

One of my favorite things about LoveChoice is how it knows what players expect from a Visual Novel and turns it on its head. The first story, for example, will always lead to a bad ending if you play the game like a standard visual novel. However, at the end, it gives you hints on what you need to do to get the good ending. A lot of times it's not about making a choice, but looking for the third option *hint hint*. Because of that, it made the stories and the lessons so impactful and I really loved it.

The music was also very beautiful. Another thing I loved was the mini games and little effects they added as well. There would be scenes where your mouse cursor is pulled or repelled away from a certain thing on the screen and I really loved that effect as it really hit home how tough it was to make said choice or decision.
I have no gripes with this game honestly. It is short and to the point, and once I figured out what it was trying to teach me, it made me appreciate it greatly!

Final verdict? This is a wonderful game and will only take 1-2 hours of your time at most. The lessons and stories were really beautiful and I cherish the them greatly. Definitely check this one out if you're looking for a unique Visual Novel game with a nice premise. Thanks for reading!\t\t\t\t\t
