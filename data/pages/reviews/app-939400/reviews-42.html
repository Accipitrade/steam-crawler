My opinion on the game:

A style of game in which you follow three romance stories with scenarios that will be different each time depending on your choice and actions. I am pleasantly surprised by the number of possibilities in each story, which generates some difficulty to finish the game completely! The OST, the drawings and the landscapes are very beautiful and simplistic, which brings a certain originality. Nevertheless, I'm a bit disappointed because of the length of the game which is not long at all, even very short, indeed I took barely 2h30 to finish it despite the difficulty. Anyway, I had a great time, this game is not expensive at all, so why deprive yourself!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGiving this game a thumbs up because I want to support the developers. But don't get me wrong; there are quite a few things that feel a bit wrong here. It seems to be a trend in these interactive "shorts."

The first-time experience is designed for failure. I remember reading a review here that went something along the lines of "realise where you're going wrong and you'll understand why you were single for so long."

Sounded like a challenge – so I took it as one. I played the game twice, and "lost" both times. Then I read more reviews, of which one pointed out there's an "interactive" aspect to it that I may have overlooked.

I played the game the third time, and voila, that "helpful" review I read was indeed right. If I had done something like this, I would've "won" the game.

Now, here's the problem: How was I supposed to know that's how the interaction works? There's zero indication or hint to show it'd work that way. Hence, it's a design problem, not a player problem. The "think outside the box" strategy seems to overstretch quite a bit with visual novels and at this rate, it's frankly super tiring.

The game indeed does carry a heartwarming message. But the binary design direction feels like a cheap sucker punch each time.\t\t\t\t\t
