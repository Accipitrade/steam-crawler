💌 𝐿𝑜𝓋𝑒𝒞𝒽𝑜𝒾𝒸𝑒 拣爱 💌

What's more sought after than a sequel to Half-Life 2, but rarer than a 21ᵗʰ century bug-free Bethesda release? It's, of course, a gamer girlfriend — the one thing that male gamers desperately want but can't seem to get. All jokes asides, LoveChoice 拣爱 is a short but sweet title starring a boy and a girl both who have a deep passion for making video games, as well as for each other. In this interactive story-telling game, you'll be playing as the boy in his determined endeavor to make the right love choices in his life. LoveChoice — the most unusual way to hunt for restaurants and darn onion cutting ninjas.

The story starts off at a game jam where the player, along with the rest of the other indie developers, is attentively working on game concept ideas. During the jam, he happens upon a girl nearby his group's table. He approaches her and they begin a casual chat, and thus, they set off on their new relationship.

Along the way, you'll be met with a few challenges such as figuring out what movie she wants to watch or playing a curious minigame to find some decent places to eat. These tasks, as Ye from Akaba Studio put it, are supposed to show how,

" you should put more effort in and do more preparation for your dating."

It's a great message to those who are in a relationship to always understand that "loving your partner" isn't always a cakewalk. It's quite important to put some day to day effort in making your lover feel genuinely special to you.

LoveChoice's art style has a definite charm to it with an innocent and almost childlike presentation. Both the girl and boy have a distinct, warm delight to their character complexion to complement their adorable appearance. It's quite a contrast from most visual novels in today's market where the characters have jumbo eyes and generous body proportions and features. The minimalistic style of LoveChoice gives me a desire to see more of this kind of artwork in storytelling. Additionally, the music is superbly outstanding — using soft and gentle soundtracks to effectively support each of the story's scenes.

Spoiler Alert!
This next part contains light spoilers, so I encourage those who've yet to play the game to skip on past.

After playing the game through a couple times I always ended up confused and bewildered as to why I didn't get an ending that satisfied me. Each playthrough had left me more puzzled than the last. Which choice was I getting wrong? Did I miss some sort of secret choice within the dialogue options? That's when it hit me like a sledgehammer on a bag of Jet-puffed marshmallows. What if the girl didn't want me to choose the dialogue options? What if there was a deeper, more hidden love choice? This is where things took a rather emotional turn for me. It's difficult to explain what a surprising eye-opener it was to finally realize what LoveChoice was truly trying to tell me. All along, the girl wanted me not to just "say," but to "care and do." She wanted me to consider her true heart's desire, even though she didn't explicitly mention it to me. From then onward, I began to diligently search of ways that I could make true love choices for the girl I cared for — and it was absolutely beautiful. Finally reaching the ending I so desired was certainly a joyful experience. According to LoveChoice's main designer Ye,

"I think the "hidden choice" like some answers just don't come up in your mind default in real life. They are usually of our comfort zone and need our thinking. Human tends to forget this kind of options, especially when it was to ones close to us. I am not saying the default options are bad or wrong, I just want to say the one we truly love always deserve a better answer."

LoveChoice obviously isn't an AAA budget title, nor is a fully fledged indie game. But I can say without a doubt, it gave me a more heartfelt experience than any game I've played in a long time. It compelled me to deeply think and consider my own current relationship along with the "love choices" I should look to make. Although I may not be able to guarantee the next person who plays LoveChoice, a touching and moving experience — it is definitely a precious story that deserves to be heard by all. Sometimes, we need don't a whole 20+ hours of playtime to get a decent gaming experience. Sometimes, all it takes is a short message and a developer's effort to create a game from the heart. Thank you, Akaba Studio, for making such the game I didn't know I needed in my life. I hope that the next stories released in LoveChoice will be just as a heartwarming experience.\t\t\t\t\t
