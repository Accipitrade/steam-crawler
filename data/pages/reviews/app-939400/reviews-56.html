\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe art style is very cute but the writing makes little sense. Even ignoring the awkward translation.


Less then five minutes in, the main characters are already dating. So their relationship has all the depth of a puddle. And then within twenty minutes of that, you are already at the final confrontation of whether they stay together or not. Everything in between is just stock relationship subjects like talking and arguing; which are glanced over without the player learning anything about either character. The player makes vague dialogue choices which determine what happens to the relationship, but the dialogue choices have no clear behavioural alignment so the result you get is pretty much random. Especially when the first choice has you outright ask the girl you have never met to be your friend, or outright ask if she wants to hear you rant about your teams game concept; in the middle of a competition about said game concept when the girl is your opponent. Neither of which is socially appropriate, but apparently the game still labels one as positive and one as negative.

It really does not feel like whoever wrote this had any idea as to what they were doing. There is no pacing or depth to this "relationship" simulation at all. The player learns almost nothing about either character through the entire course of the plot, the characters are just relationship dolls. They do not even have names.

It displays highly surface level understanding of what a relationship is like, and lists exclusively stock "relationship" struggles and interactions as the entirety of the plot.


This does however serve as a marvelous example of how superficial average human relationships are. Built on visual and chemical attraction completely void of any level of personal understanding or friendship. Glass partnerships entirely dependent on the two parties serving their designated gender roles and adhering to societal normalcy. The connection held together by the baseless yet intense feeling of "love" which keeps both sides fiercely attached to each other without any level of personal intimacy. If at any moment either side stops playing by the chemical encoded rules, the relationship immediately shatters and then they move on to remate with a new partner a few months or a year later and try it all over again. A system entirely designed to serve one ethical purpose, reproduction, and nothing else. So, this plot.

Yes it did make me sad, but mostly because it reminded me of how shallow humanity is. This is the opposite of what I am looking to experience in fiction, I'm trying to escape from reality not be reminded of it.



The developer thinks I feel bad because I got the bad ending, when all the problems I cite with this plot are about the plot itself and not the ending. Way to try to dismiss my entire point by point dynamic criticism, with a single delusive excuse. Just like how the developer thinks that "paying more attention" will get you the better ending, in a game where all the ways you influence the ending are unmarked neutral dialogue options which have zero positive or negative qualities to them so it is physically impossible to tell what the game is interpreting as good or bad because the dialogue options are either both bad or both neutral.

At least they did not throw a tantrum, like I have seen other developers pull, but they are still just as in denial as literally every developer that bothers to respond to my negative reviews.\t\t\t\t\t
