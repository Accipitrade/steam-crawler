\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a dating sim. For a dating sim to work, you need to have likable characters. That being said, the protagonist is pretty unlikable, with all of his responses to his love interest being either dismissive or completely unrelated to the conversation being had. The love interest doesn't really have a personality or even a name (neither does the main character), which makes it difficult to get attached to her. The only ending I could get was the two breaking up and it doesn't make you feel anything because you don't know or care about the characters. I'm honestly not sure if there are any other endings, because no choices seem to have any impact on the story. The game takes place over the course of three years, but can takes at most 15 minutes. Making choices gives no indication on whether or not the story changes, as they all result in the same dialogue. The dialogue in general doesn't make much sense a lot of the time. You'll have one character say one thing and then the other (or sometimes even the same character) say something completely unrelated, which makes everything feel very unnatural. Overall, I'd say there're definitely better options out there, but the game is only $2, so if you wanna try it, go for it. Plus, the art's cute.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLoveChoice simply put is a game that is able to grab the heart of its audience and really feel every word that is told on screen. At first, I was a bit skeptical of LoveChoice with it's short run time and "Overwhemingly Positive" review status. I still bought it because as they say, you can refund it if you are not satisfied.

I did not refund it. I was beyond satisfied.

The art and soundtrack of the game is what holds this game together. The cute and simple art style manages to reinforce that feeling of wholesomeness that is present throughout all the stories. I personally think that this art style was the best way of portraying the story as I was more invested by the cutesy vibe of it all. The soundtrack accompanies the entire game with nice mellow piano and memorable melodies for certain moments.

The gameplay....well it is a visual novel, so this isn't something that I should heavily criticize on. My only criticism here is that I personally think the mini-games are unnecessary. It feels like they are there to pad the time and are there for the sake of this. I'm talking about the first story with its mini-game as I was really annoyed by it every time after the first.

I'm not sure if this counts as gameplay but this is where the game truly shows its excellence. This was when I decided to make a review. The game is very clever and deceiving with its choices. You are forced to think outside the box to get the endings you want. The moment you realizes that there is more to this game than meets the eye, you will captured by it and will never let go. The way they implement this simple trick matches so well with the theme of love. When it comes to love, sometimes we think we only have a certain number of paths we can go to but in reality, sometimes we should make our own path despite what path is being shown. It is amazing for this reason alone.

Please play this game. You won't be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
