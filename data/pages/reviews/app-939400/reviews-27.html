\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is someone's relationship advice as a game. Cute artstyle, but not worth your while if you already understand the concept of "treat them like a person" "let them explore who they are and who they want to be" and also the third lesson is straight-up "trust that your spouse isn't cheating on you" which isn't exactly helpful.

By the way, in that third lesson, the story gives you the option to ask your husband about the suspicious phone call that you saw (which is what makes you think they're cheating in the first place) but even if you choose to confront him, literally nothing about the story changes. I thought the whole point of the first story was to communicate well and try to understand how your partner feels! Not to just brush things off and simply *assume* that everything's fine between you two- especially so, when we later see in multiple examples around the house (such as not cleaning the litterbox and not helping with laundry) that your husband hasn't exactly been a great comfort, and appears to leave many chores for *you* to do instead. What happened to balance?

Also, while the gameplay *is* charmingly simple, it's actually quite difficult to *see* what you need to do. Light puzzle spoilers dead ahead: needless to say, the way objects are highlighted by the cursor hardly makes them stand out at ALL. I'm almost wondering if the developers decided to do this to extend the playtime. After all is said and done, it feels like half of my time with this game was spent randomly clicking the screen because barely anything in the backgrounds stands out enough to look like it should be clickable. And don't get me STARTED on how they made you use the scroll wheel that one, SINGULAR time. If it were a regular game mechanic, I could forgive it, but wow, you REALLY do just have to *guess* to solve that one.

And one more thing- one of the dialogue choices in the first story- that being: "can i hold your hand"- is a perfectly fine thing to ask, and I don't know why the game treats it as thought it "ruins the mood". Have you considered how important consent is? Even for seemingly simple things like that? You don't know what any given person has gone through in their lives. Physical touch isn't automatically welcome from an acquaintance just because you've had a nice night with them so far.

But hey, it was two bucks, right? Ya get what ya pay for.\t\t\t\t\t

I was dumbfounded, I thought we were doing well, but apparently not. Needless to say, my first time playing it didn't go over well, and so I decided to give it another go. I eventually found that there lie something beneath the surface of every encounter, sometimes no words were needed for a perfect moment, and other times you realize just bending to another's will isn't right, and there must be an understanding among the two people to maintain a stable relationship. Although I failed spectacularly my first time playing LoveChoice, I realized that only made me appreciate the game on my second run even more. I looked closer at the text and no longer was it just cold text, it had meaning and emotion behind it. My failure helped me learn from my shortcomings, and I worked to better myself and unknowingly bettered myself too, if only by a little. Surprisingly, this candy bar priced game made me take a look at my self, and gave me more enjoyment in the first 5 minutes than some A list games give in the first 2 hours. I never played a dating game before, but I recommend people who also haven't play this one. In conclusion, you should definitely play this game, I mean you spent a couple minutes reading this, so I'm sure you have a couple more, just ruffle though the couch cushion to find a bit of change and enjoy the game.\t\t\t\t\t
