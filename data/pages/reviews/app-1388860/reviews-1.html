\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not flawless (see below), but it's an easy recommendation, especially for fans of intricate puzzles, interactive stories, escape room-flavoured games and so on. I was sucked into the game and didn't put it down until I was finished with it (well after 2 AM, which, admittedly, might've contributed to my feeling a bit confused at times).

The story is a bit too convoluted, as are some of the puzzles. Good news is that both can be rectified somewhat by reading slowly and carefully (no cursory scanning for us folks who tend to read too fast!) and re-reading the chapters after finishing the game.

The game is also a little bit buggy - sometimes scrolling gets stuttery (clicking & dragging rather than using the mouse scroll helps), sometimes you have to click on something more than once for the click to register (a bit unnerving when it happens during the timed sequence), and the part where you have to close your eyes is poorly implemented (on mobile, you would pinch your screen; here, you have to double-click somewhere in the lower right corner). Nothing game-breaking, but it would be nice to see this fixed.

Hint for when you complete the game: the email address is there for a reason; if you complete some additional puzzles, you can send them and get some nice back-and-forth with the mysterious person behind that email address. To be honest, I didn't know how to piece some of the extra info that I got, so I didn't experience it myself but looked it up - thankfully, you can find the email exchange in one of the walkthroughs. I love ARG-adjacent bonuses like this!

I will definitely check out the next game from this team!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe concept and execution of this game/story is well done... I recognised the potential immediately in the previews, and very much wanted to both enjoy the game myself, as well as see it succeed for its well executed, unique design.

Unfortunately, the story's genre just isn't one I tend to like, and I wasn't able to get past that. I rarely like mysteries unless it is combined with something else I enjoy, such as science fiction, fantasy, history etc... I hoped that the other elements of Unmemory (the puzzles, blending of game and story, etc) would provide that 'extra something' that makes a mystery story worth it to me, but in this case it wasn't. Perhaps if a new game was made in this style, but with another genre as part of the core storyline, I would find it worth keeping and finishing. Perhaps it also would appeal to a wider audience, garnering greater success.

I imagine that for mystery fans, the game is probably very immersive and interesting. Although I did see some disappointment in other reviews that the ending was a bit predictable (and upon deciding I was not going to finish the game, I spoiled myself to see if I'd agree, which I do), but to what extent a predictable ending makes or breaks the interactive 'journey' of the story/game for mystery fans, isn't something I would know.

This is one of those games I wish Steam had a 'Maybe' or neutral option for rating a game, rather than a simple 'Yes'/"No'.... Ultimately, 'Yes' I recommend it because it is a well executed, unique creation... but 'No', I don't recommend it, because the core mystery story did not engage me and the ending was too predictable.

Up until Chapter 7, at least. There's a section where you have to hide in a wardrobe and then close your eyes. On mobile, you can do this by pinching the top and bottom of your screen and sliding inwards to close. But I only have a single mouse cursor to work with on PC, and so I obviously can't do this. I tried everything that I thought would work (dragging both the top and bottom with the mouse inwards, scrolling with the mouse wheel, etc). But nothing worked, and now I'm essentially softlocked from progressing further.

I'll change my review if someone gets back to me soonish regarding how to progress from this part. But until then, I can't recommend it because this part simply does not translate well at all on PC. Still a good game, but maybe you should get it on mobile instead.

EDIT: The devs got back to me after I emailed them; you have to double click at that section to progress. Not immediately intuitive, but at least I got past. I ended up enjoying the whole experience, and I'm grateful to the devs for immediately getting back to me regarding this issue.\t\t\t\t\t
