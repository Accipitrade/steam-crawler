\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinal Fantasy 4 is the biggest leap forward the series ever took. Adding the active time battle system and switching a Vancian casting system for a spell point one. These two facts alone make this a must play for any JRPG enthusiast.
Having played most of five, six, and seven I've never been a fan of the ATB in the past. It is one of those systems that to me always felt like it favored the computers way more than the players. Finally finishing four did not change my opinion on that in the slightest. The battles can often feel like you're barely able to keep your head above water trying to keep track of what all is going on. This is somewhat due to the wonky framerate issues the steam port has that make the battles run faster than they should and although there is a fix it is a little silly that you need to patch it yourself.
The spell point system I feel completely different, it is such a welcome change to be able to use all of your spells freely rather than being locked to particular levels. All of the classes they give you end up being quite fun and interesting, and although I didn't really use the augment system until near the end it adds a layer of depth I wish I had looked into much earlier.
Narrative speaking this game wasn't breaking any new boundary. All of the final fantasy games have been super metaphysical and weird and this one was no different. The summary of the story would be rather short but make you sound completely incoherent to anyone who isn't familiar with the game already. That's not necessarily bad, but it is worth noting. The character writing is what really shines. I feel like this is the first time that you were given actual characters to play as and even though they have some pretty cheesy scenes I found myself really getting into their roles. The addition of voice acting and cut scenes was also a nice touch as well.
My biggest problem was the general shotty nature of the port, I had quite a few bugs, as well as known issues that Square isn't patching. As far as cons are concerned: the music sounds a bit different (which might annoy some folks), the voice acting isn't the greatest, and the battle menu feels a bit clunky. This was the first RPG I ever really played religiously, so nostalgia is a huge factor for me. I own just about every version of FFIV; I know the game, the characters, and the details by heart. It will always be meaningful to me. Conquering your personal darkness? Being a hero? Love? These are still fairytales I can get behind.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis classic SNES title has been brought back and revisited and revamped but all the nostalgia is here. If you're an old man like me and played this back in the day, day one ont he SNES, then I have to say, you're in for a trip down nostalgia lane. If you've never played this, well then, get ready for a great story, and an all around super fun time.

From the beginning to the end the story doesn't relent, and you have to make your way through a world that just seems setup to stop you at every end, but you're the hero of this story, and they play that perfectly into the story arc.

When growing up, this was the game that had me. It wasn't the mighty graphics, or the amazing cut-scenes (as those really didn't exist... just sayin') it was the music, the conversations, the mini-stories, how it all pieced together. From one battle to the next, There truly was something about this. And revisiting it, amazing. Give it a go if you haven't, and if you have, play it again dammit, its a great game! One of the best in the entire series.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you want a finely tuned classic JRPG with a great story and memorable cast of characters you can’t do much better than FF4. This DS port edition adds some modern conveniences like being able to continue where you left off when you die, quick saves at any time and rewards for exploring an entire floor to make grinding feel like..well..less of a grind. I enjoyed the 3D pixilated graphics, still looks retro while adding clarity. The new battle menu is cumbersome but workable, the voice work is alright and adds a nice touch to the classic musical score. Attacks, magic, abilities, and challenging foes make battle engrossing. Install the 20FPS battle patch for a smooth experience.

35 hours of gameplay, controller support and moderate but not excessive grinding along with the ever changing cast of characters make FF4 a very accessible classic JRPG. Pick up a copy, put on your Blue Mage hat, hop on your Chocobo and party like its 1994 all over again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I was a kid I was hooked on this game. I didn't have it in my collection and had to rent it from the (before blockbuster) video rental shop a dozen blocks away. I would use my allowance, and cash I made doing odds and ends for the neighbors, and bike to the rental store, rain or shine, and rent it nearly every weekend until I beat it.

It was one of the first games that really entangled me with its story, the battle system was easy to understand, the difficulty could be challenging at times, and the music was just perfect.

But has it aged well?

Definitely. This is a good port (originally on DS) and it makes some decent improvements to the game.

I just wish that the other SNES era FF's got the same service for their ports to PC, especially FF6.
