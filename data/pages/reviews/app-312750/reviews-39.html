\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe soundtrack was the only thing I needed to hear to incentivize me to play this game. I'm not exaggerating when I say this is one of the best soundtracks the Final Fantasy series has to offer. I am head over heels for it!

Can I just say how nice it is to play a JRPG that isn't shoving padding down your throat? Seriously, how often do you find a JRPG that has great pacing and doesn't make you waste time on frivolous, tedious nonsense or arbitrary fetch quests during the main campaign? This is just a smooth glide from one scenario to the next. The pacing is solid, the music is great, the story, well, the story has issues, but I do enjoy most of the characters and I like how useful they are! This is really good!

The added difficulty of the game with remixed boss strategies is wonderful, too! It's really fun to play this game with added features and cool little tests of skill to throw off players of the original release of the game. This is the best way to handle a remake of a game, at least in my opinion. I don't know if I'll ever tackle the super bosses - they require a little too much grinding and precision for me to feel like it's worth it, but if the worst thing I can say about a game is that its side challenges aren't my cup of tea, I think things turned out pretty darn well. As far as classic Final Fantasy games are concerned, this is a darn good time. I highly, highly recommend this one.\t\t\t\t\t

While other games eventually surpassed it, it showed me that a video game could pull you in on a narrative level while still having interesting/challenging gameplay. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the Nintendo DS reimagining from 2007 of the beloved SNES classic, which introduced 3D graphics, voiced cinematics, a remastered soundtrack and expanded on the base game by offering additional story bits and gameplay elements, like a new ability system and new game+.

Since it is based on a 2007 game, this version is beginning to show its age, with somewhat dated – yet still charming – 3D graphics. There are sadly very few built-in graphical options to improve the visuals. It is, of course, always possible to apply some post processing via NVIDIA settings or Reshade, but it is disappointing to not have more options from the get-go.

Strangely enough, the game’s FPS seem to be locked at 15FPS, what makes the combat feel sluggish and clunky. I couldn’t recommend enough to download the fan-made patches that increase the framerate to at least 20 FPS, for a much better experience.

Last but not least, the game tends to freeze and even crashes regularly if you have the Steam overlay and/or Steam cloud enabled. I suspect this would not happen on all rigs but, after some research, this issue seems to happen on a wide enough scale to warrant at least a disclaimer in the launcher from Square-Enix.

All, in all, even though this is probably the best version of FF4 out there (at least until the next one), it is hard to recommend the steam edition given its current state. It only does the bare minimum in terms of ergonomy and configuration options for a PC port, and the obvious lack of Q&A and optimization are unacceptable from a multi-million company like Square-Enix.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll of the superior gameplay of the DS release, with the same shoddy port job as FFIII.

As long as the FPS lock isn't a deal breaker for you (which it oughtn't be in a turn based game like this), then I wholly recommend this game. If it is, then give it a pass. Having said that, they didn't touch up the models, textures, renderer, or the quality of the sound files to take advantage of the better hardware of PCs, so it's a little jank.

7/10 - Maybe one day Squeenix will give a damn about their ports.

PS. You can raise the FPS of combat via the .INI file, but I wouldn't recommend bumping it past 20 since the combat animation speed is tied directly to the FPS (something that probably made sense on the original DS release, but they were too lazy to fix when they ported it to iOS or later to PC).\t\t\t\t\t
