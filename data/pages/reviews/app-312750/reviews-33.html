\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinal Fantasy IV is the mobile port of the DS remake.

Compared to other versions:
Graphically, whether you prefer the 3D models or 2D Sprites (whichever version that may be, SNES/GBA/PSP are all different) is highly subjective, I do like the 3D models though the textures are very obviously made for the DS's tiny resolution and can be pretty ugly at times.

Gameplay-wise this version of IV is slower, and I'm not just talking about 15fps in combat. I'm talking actual combat speed, fights take far longer than they really should in no small part to due to the longer and fancier animations, and it can feel real sluggish at times especially late game. The UI doesn't help with this given that it was designed for mobile and doesn't account for PC play. Unlike the PSP version, there is no party-switching, this is how the original Super Famicom version played. This version (and the mobile version) also lack the Whyt Eidolon from the DS version and it's respective functionality and features.

Thoughts on the game itself:
IV suffers from a lot of classic FF-isms, reused enemies (recoloured), invisible/hidden paths where you must navigate pitch-black (and not just for optional loot, there are required-to-progress paths), in-general just a navigational nightmare (but HEY at least you have a map that closes every time you talk to someone, enter anywhere or reach a new floor, not to mention it doesn't have any labels on the overworld).

Story-wise I was never really into IV's plot or characters, while the cast is fun enough they were never all too interesting or complex, and given the plot bleeds into another complaint of mine I lean more towards disliking it.

You see, compared to III, strategy is restricted by your plot defined party and characters come and go frequently. This is bothersome for a few reasons, for a start if you're playing blind you'll not know when to unequip characters and may end up losing gear other party members could be using in their stead. You're also stuck with the toolset defined by the party you are given, so for the majority of the game they give you a very basic broad party disallowing any specialisation that would come with the classic job system found in other Final Fantasy games. Obviously there are benefits to this, bosses can be tuned in a way that best fits the current party. The drawback is a significant lack in player choice, on how they want to approach each situation, especially notable with bosses where there tends to be only one or two effective strategies for beating them.

There is something I do like about this version of IV, the superbosses Geryon and Proto-Babil. The original version of IV didn't have any, whilst the GBA and PSP versions did, Zeromus EG (lame) and Brachioraidos (good but Edward exists). I prefer this version's set of superbosses, Proto-Babil especially was a tough fight. Though it's a bit perplexing that they are the only superbosses in the entire franchise that can only be fought in new game+, at least to my knowledge and I'm not counting FFXIII-3's final boss.

I don't know how to end reviews, if you're interested in playing this instalment I'd recommend looking into the various versions of the game to compare and weight the pros and cons yourself before deciding on a version to play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Steam port of the 3D version of FFIV. There's three kinds of people I would recommend this game for.

1: Those who have nostalgia for the old FFIV, and would like to experience the game in a different way instead of playing the same old iteration for the thousandth time.

2: Those who like FF games that are more on the challenging side. Boot this one up in hard mode and enjoy your time. I'll say this as a warning: watch out who you give what augments to. Those carry over to your next playthrough, you can only face the two optional superbosses on your second or third playthroughs, and one of them is hard enough that you'll want to be careful with multi-playthrough decisions like that.

3: Rare item hunters and completionists. The infamous 0.4% rare item drop rates are back, but now there's more such rare items to get. You can boost this to 0.8% though. Well, if you want to fight the strongest superboss, you're going to be farming these. A lot. As in, for many, many hours. And to get all achievements, you're going to need to get 10 of those rare drops at the bare minimum, and probably more to ensure your team is properly equipped. It's a big chore, and you best be ready for it if you want to "complete" this game. Alarms/sirens can be used to trigger all the battles you need though, except for one of the rare summon drops IIRC.

If you're one of the above people, you should have an enjoyable time. If you're not, then maybe think twice about this one. If you're new to the FF franchise, I do not recommend this as a starting point. 2D FFIV is one of better games in the franchise for beginners, but this version here is designed more with veterans in mind and may leave you disappointed if it's one of your first FF experiences.\t\t\t\t\t
