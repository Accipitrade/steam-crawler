\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would highly recommend this for those who loved the original and newcomers to the Final Fantasy series alike. I am trying to be objective from my being a big fan of the Final Fantasy series and still give a rounded opinion. So while It may look like I am being picky it's to be fair to newcomers and fans alike.
The graphic transition from 2-D to 3-D in seeing what could have been possible given FFX/X-2 even pre HD remaster I believe could have been done way better. The character skins were badly pixel ridden, polygonal, and grainy. Yet, in spite of those graphic flaws I still love the look of the game overall. The world was highly immersive in this rendition. The story was spectacular to begin with, with the addition of voice acting and new never before seen story just boosted my opinion over the top. If I had any complaint it was Cid, I expected him to shout "Cid Beat Airship!" like Animal from The Muppets said "Animal Beat Drum!" Still the rest of the cast giving it their all as they did more than compensated for that. They really did give the old story depth and dimension that I never thought possible. The additional bosses and content give it even more replay value than the original had. I truly recommend you buy this game and just experience it for yourselves. You won't be sorry. P.S. Square-Enix if you read this review please, if you are going to make old 2-D titles and make them 3-D please do so with the most modern tools and care. Please keep up with finding voice talent on par with this title. I am very impressed.\t\t\t\t\t

Final Fantasy IV
(This review refers to the PC version.)

I am a big fan of the Final Fantasy series. I started with FF VII on Playstation in the 90ies, played part VI - XII and liked all of them more or less (mostly VII and IX).
This one is a catastrophe.

Some of the characters are quite nice, but the plot developement is a mess. Your party has to find crystals (yes, crystals), and as if this wasn't boring enough, you don't even know why until you played through half of the game. The dialogues are mostly dumb, and even the dynamics between the characters are not even close to feeling well done.
All in all, the story lacks depth and is made of cliches. I liked about two or three moments.
In addition, playing this for the first time, you can't even be sure which characters will survive the next dungeon, because frequently someone dies. Your party never has more than five members, see? This is really annoying, because you trained them for nothing..

The battle difficulty (I only played on hard mode) is mostly lame, but the bosses are too strong, for there are always at least two characters in your party, who have not even a third of the other's HP. Oh, and all those fake battles ...
Not to speak of the augment system - it's just no fun for collectors or completionists, neither are there any hints in the game how to use it.

Most of the characters have a nice unique style, but towns and dungeons look boring and lack of details. Don't expect to be enchanted by beautiful places as you are used to from other parts of the series. The castles e.g. look the same even on the other side of the world. I really missed this special FF atmosphere.

Last but not least, the menu is inconvenient and the controls react slowly, as well during combat. Speaking of combat - there are no graphics showing you how much HP/MP your characters have left, just numbers.
I'll stop now ...

All in all, I couldn't enjoy most of my playing hours. I didn't even complete the game. And this feels good. ;)\t\t\t\t\t
