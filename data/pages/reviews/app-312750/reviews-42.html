\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough I had initially given this game a bad review, I changed my mind as I played it. It is certainly an improvement over Final Fantasy III.
The beginning of the game is its weak point: battles are slow and the game demands a lot of grinding. There's also a useless character: Edward, the bard.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinal Fantasy IV is and always will be my favorite game. It was the first RPG I ever played when I was a kid and it is easily the game that I have replayed the most times from start to completion.

With that being said, I can say with 100% certainty that this is not the best representation of FFIV to ever exist. I will admit that I greatly enjoyed the DS version of FFIV, which this port stems. Unfortunately it appears to be hobbled by strange technical problems. Some of the problems that I have noticed are low frame rates in battle, a letterboxed view even in full screen resolutions and sluggish response times for controls when using a controller. The game also seems to flicker intermittently in battles when you are playing it via Steam Link.

I can't say that I am encouraged to continue playing FFIV in this state. Considering the age of this port, I doubt Square is going to bother addressing any of the shortcomings. As you can probably guess, I don't recommend getting this unless you haven't played the DS version and cannot bring yourself to get it on Android as well.

Edit: Well I stand corrected, Square actually went and patched this game recently. And so far, I can tell that the resolution problems I noted up above are gone. Also the controller support is a lot better, so that is no longer anything I would complain about. I may have to change my decision on this one...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinal Fantasy 4 is a fun little JRPG. I never played the original so this port was my first experience. I had a fun time with this game and it ran smoothly for me. I did not have an issue losing saves or crashing. It has the common problem as all other Steam games of this nature with Quicksave and local saves in your User folder. If you click continue, you're continuing from the last screen transition. If you want to load a save from where you last manually saved, you have to use the load option and not the continue option. Your saves are in your user folder under App Data. If your user information for Windows changes or is altered, you could lose your save. This isn't a issue with the game so much as an issue with your Windows User management.

**If you like my reviews or plan on buying this game and want to chat, feel free to send me a friend request.**

Cons
Similar to other early Final Fantasy games, the directions on where to go next get lost as you progress in the game. You're expected to backtrack and find the one NPC that pushes the story forward. It doesn't happen often, but it does happen a couple times.

For story reasons, the game changes up your party often. It's very hard to get a good feel and rhythm with your party until the end of the game.

The Augment system is not explained well. I do not agree with other posters that have said you could ruin your game with the wrong Augment path, but I do think the wrong augment path can cause frustration if you're not using a guide and you do plan to 100% the achievements like I did. I only understand the augment system because of supplemental research. It's not provided in game very well and if you want to 100% the achievements you will have to play through the game twice. The game limits you to only two New Game+ without completely starting over. I could see myself having been very frustrated if I had to play through a third time.

The game relies on really low RNG for some items. It took me a long time to get all the rare equipment even with the help of one of the augments.

The menu should have been reworked with the update. The biggest thing is going to target all. The option is to the left, but you still have to cycle up and down to get to it. There isn't a manual sort option in the items that I found.

The characters don't get much development. They kinda take a backseat to the story. They are just there to support getting to the end and beating the final baddie.

PC Port of the 3DS version isn't perfect. The developers room for example is a lot easier and you can't do the Whyt stuff in the PC version.

Pros
The story is amazing for a JRPG. I don't want to spoil it, but you do go around the world trying to stop a baddie and ultimately fight a big baddie at the end. But, they add a lot of flavor and nuance to it. Every plot point in the story feels satisfying.

The game isn't grindy EXCEPT for the rare item drops for achievements, which are optional. I never had to stop before a boss and grind or felt underpowered in a dungeon. I never had to grind equipment. Occasionally there would be new equipment in the shop, but it was more of a trap? Your old equipment did better in the next area because of the monster types. I never ran from fights and always seemed to be pretty strong. I was even playing hard mode.

Every boss felt different. You know those bosses in JRPGs where you pretty much just attack and heal? All the bosses here had a gimmick. For example, they'd counterattack physical hits, but not magic attacks or they'd have an animation and you'd have to do a certain ability to cancel it or you'd have to defend when they do it. That kind of thing. You will die if you're not using a guide and are on hard mode. But, you learned something when you died. It's the experience. Sometimes you'll go to a boss that does something and you win by figuring out the gimmick without dying. That's a good feeling in a JRPG.

Graphics were alright, I think they did a really good job bringing FF4 to the future. I never played the original to compare, but I really thought the graphics here nailed everything I needed. Nothing amazing, but they aren't 1990s bad. Golbez looks like a beast.

I recommend this game if you're a JRPG fan, you want a good story without a grind, or you want a JRPG with gimmicks in boss fights that are fun, but not too challenging.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're a 90s or earlier gamer like me and this looks familiar, it's because this was first released in the US as Final Fantasy 2 on the Super NES in 1991 (the "actual/original" FF 2 was an NES title only released in Japan until the late 2000s). It's the same game but with improved graphics (PlayStation-quality outside of the current-gen quality opening movie) and voices added to some of the cutscenes, plus remixed music. It's also the same as the iPhone/iPod/iPad version released a few years ago except for having 2 difficulty levels to choose from. Although the graphics are much better than on the SNES I would've liked them more if they were less pixelated and were closer to more recent FF games, or at least PS2-quality or better. There is controller support too but the in-game dialogue doesn't reflect this so you'll have to figure it out yourself (not too hard though). Other than that, it's another great classic that's worth buying if you're a Final Fantasy fan. If the price seems a little high, get it on a sale or buy the bundle that includes the sequel, The After Years.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very enjoyable JRPG. Very linear for the first many hours, but eventually opens up and has a lot of interesting places to explore. Some very memorable creature designs. A few of the bosses require some interesting strategic thinking. The random battles make it very slow, like many JRPGs, but this is part of the genre.

The low-polygon 3D graphics are cute, and it runs well even with a low powered computer. I enjoy playing this on my laptop, and steam cloud saves transfer to my other PC easily.

This is a pretty faithful adaptation of the original game. Only slightly different from the DS version. I've replayed this game in many forms over the years and I think this is a good version of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played this game at least 50 times through in my childhood (early 90s, original NA SNES port), I have some seriously mixed emotions about this rendition of my childhood memories. From the very beginning, my main problem is the camera, why is it not an option to zoom out, for the love of all that is good. The music is just as fantastic as the original, maybe even a bit improved, but that's a stretch. The difficulty, at times, is absurd. I'm not sure if I chose the hardest difficulty available but, there's been several instances where I was just flat out wiped with zero chance to come back. The "Continue" option just kind of let's you do the fight again if you failed, borderline stupid but, I found myself using it. A handful of the enemies counter with some kind of super powerful attack, didn't really exist here but, trust me, don't just "attack" Valvalis or Golbez (in the Dwarf Castle) mindlessly, you will lose. Maybe if you are severly overleveled, this won't be an issue. But my god, when (spoilers) someone rejoins you after talking to Giott, that Golbez fight was just stupid, never have I had a problem with that fight in my life until now, it took forever. All in all, it's fun, the augments are interesting, the text is close to exactly what the original offered, and my list of complaints aren't long. Visually, I think it sucks, the original was just far more appealing, but I don't really care all that much about that. I guess it's worth a shot, or just play the original. You'll probably like it better.\t\t\t\t\t
