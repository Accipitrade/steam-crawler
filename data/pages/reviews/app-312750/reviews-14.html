\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know why I haven't left a review yet, but here we go. I owned the original on SNES when I was younger, and unfortunately with an incredible lack of foresight, sold it along with my SNES yeeeaaars ago. Anyways, needless to say that I clocked in a wild amount of hours playing this game back in the day, but haven't touched it for a over a decade, I'm sure.

This remake is incredible. The chibi-ish art style is great and fits, though the story is a serious one, because it's done so well. Especially because the devs made the call of staying closer to Amano's artwork (from what I can tell) than the OG sprites (albeit the OG devs for SNES were limited in what they could do, sure). Fantastic low poly models. I love low poly shiz. The voice acting is also well done, besides the usual cheese here and there. The re-translation of the dialogue is superb and elevates the storytelling far above the original.

All in all, I highly recommend the remake. We'll always have the good ol' classic, but I LOVE seeing a classic reimagined and expanded upon (if it's done well, of course! Which this one is!)\t\t\t\t\t

Port of a Port of a Remake
I'm aware this game gets a bit of flack for not being more enhanced and even the voice acting (not sure why i feel the select cast of VA's works fine) but overall this is a good port and it has bonus content included (not as much as complete collection on PSP but still) including the exclusive bonus boss exclusive to the DS version.

Texture and sound
The sound is crystal clear and not crunched by the DS speakers so Edward doesn't sound like he's whining on every word he says it's more dramatic sounding if you ask me (not saying its good either but still) which I feel a lot of games could adapt DS counterparts to modern systems and people would by it knowing the audio isn't Bad Audio Basement Show levels of quality. Audio for the opening keeps stuttering for me however SE is nice enough to include the MKV in the root folder which runs no problem in VLC... Textures could of received more of an update or at least feature an HD texture pack as free DLC for steam users.

Gameplay & Controls
Gameplay is fine as far as it goes although the use of the map feature is a little meh as they could of had a mini-map in the corner of the screen rather than having it overlay the entire screen even if the full map view helps with 100% mapping in dungeons, only problem with gameplay is the menu as it doesn't display any stats forcing you to go into a sub menu just to check status, HP, MP, etc. which is weird considering TAY fixed this and adding a mini-map. Controls I feel are a bit poor and using a controller with it is no problem but, there's no way to casually walk so you're forced to take 2 steps before going into a full bolt.

Final Verdict
Definitely pick this up if you want to play Final Fantasy IV with additional features. Pixel Remastered I'd recommend if you want a pure vanilla experience with HD textures, however if you can I'd strongly recommend picking up the PSP version as not only are you getting FFIV with all bonus content of the GBA version but you're also getting all of TAY as well as a interlude episode branching the end of IV with the start of TAY.

Pros
+ This is the definitive version of FFIV you can get
+ Autosaves before encounters, events, and transitions
+ Audio is clear to hear

Cons
- Still has IOS like controls even with controller support
- Textures don't look well at higher resolutions
- have to use sub-menus just to check HP, MP, and status\t\t\t\t\t

It's pretty obvious the story for it will pull at least somewhat from FFIV - so I wanted to get the experience in before the expansion dropped! I wasn't disappointed.

First of all the addition of voice acting, 3D cutscenes, and QoL adjustments give this game new life. However, people should remember under the surface it's still a remake of a 30 year old game; so the narrative and other aspects of the game are pretty basic - not bad by any means - just basic.

Not necessarily a bad thing, but this game is notorious for locking the best gear behind atrocious item drop rates... seriously, it's bad. Cheers to the folks who stuck this one out!

I look forward to reviewing the Pixel Remaster version of IV. :)\t\t\t\t\t
