This is mostly because of the well-crafted storyline that is remembered fondly by everyone who played the game in the 90s. The combat system is robust traditional japanese RPG goodness. Then I played FF III, enjoyed the $#!t out of it, took my time to perfect it. It was more of an endurance test then a test of skill.

Now I'm playing this and I love it. I enjoy farming for items, and battles are crazy hard in this one. You gotta really strategize on some boss fights.

If you expect good music, you sure got it. It's from the best jalapenese guy ever. You'll find yourself singing the Chocobo song when you're cooking some jalapeno beans, humming the Overwold music when you yourself actually get out to overworld. Your neighbours will probably call the police after hearing you yelling the epic Baron Castle song in your shower which is straining your lungs, so police is really needed if you've got smoker's lungs. In another scenario, if your lungs are ok and the neighbour is into FF too, he or she might as well join you

If you want to love the game, you want to download a patch for 30-fps butter-like battle performance because there's a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ 15-fps cap what the hell. That's really not good for an active time battle, here's a link:

https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=436082829

So yeah, I grew (even further) out to love the 3D ports, BUT 2D shiz are better still.

I recommend playing the 2D PSP version first (and for FF III the NES version, that's right)
and then coming back on this after a pause, preferably when you forget most of it and you want to remind yourself of all that fun you absorbed.

If you're gonna do just that then play on hard for maximum enjoyance and strive on getting the achievements, they're an insanely fun distraction from the story :)

TL;DR Read the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ review ye lazy bastard.

Oh yeah, and augment system will probably suck if you don't know what you're doing.
If you want a fun set of skills for your characters, you want to check out some guides.

This game has a New Game+ and another New Game++ which transfers your augments, learned or not (also Summons, Adamant and Onion equipment equipped or not, Dark Matter, and Tails) which also features two superbosses: Geryon and Proto-Babil.

For further details investigate somewhere else ;)
I want you to have a wonderful experience with this game, Square Enix did a marvelous job joining with DotEmu and Matrix for remaking the absolutely unexchangeable masterpiece into a more profound and life absorbed one. This piece of Japan is truly worth every dime you're willing to give and I promise you, the best of your time is being spent with this one. Enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like Final Fantasy, but I don't understand why everyone likes this game.

PLOT-Everyone loves the plot, and I would agree if not for the last 3/4 of the game. For the first fourth, up until you obtain the airship, the plot is amazing. A well-written tale of questioning authority and redemption which makes great use of the world map and towns. But then, the redemption plot is resolved, only about 5 hours into a 20-25 hour game. What replaces it? Not much. It feels like you're being dragged around by a story where your characters do nothing but fail over and over again, until random aliens show up with pitiful foreshadowing and now its the penultimate dungeon and an alien you never heard of before is behind everything.

GAMEPLAY-The gameplay is simple JRPG fare but effective, until you realize how necessary the augment system is. This system is brand new to the 3D versions of the game and is awful because it lies to you. Near the start of the game you are told that you obtain augments by talking to people. Seems great, right? After tons of towns of never getting augments I reached the end of the game and discovered my characters were too weak to take on any bosses. Confused, as I seemed to be a fair level, I looked up augments and learned that you do not get them by talking to people casually. Most of the augments that power up your characters the best are received from characters who leave the party if they are given enough augments before they do so. Since I know the plot, I did not give the characters who become unplayable any augments, which means I permanently cannot receive the useful ones and the bosses are powered up to still be tough despite the new system. This is a horrible flaw and caused me to give up on the game at the final dungeon.
To be fair, this problem is not present in the 2D versions, but those suffer from overly easy bosses.
I also take issue with the world map. While it is great at first, once you obtain the airship you realize how empty it is. There are maybe two town you have not visited. The game boasts three world maps, and yet the second one has only one town and two dungeons and the third map is empty except for one dungeon and one optional mini-dungeon.

OVERALL-Final Fantasy IV is a hit because of how important it was to the JRPG genre, and that's fine. But a weak plot and miserable use of world maps cannot be saved by an augment system which either makes certain encounters near-impossible to win or makes them pathetically easy.\t\t\t\t\t
