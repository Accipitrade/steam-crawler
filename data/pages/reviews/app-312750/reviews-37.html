\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI could only perhaps recommend this version to people that played the original versions of the game to death, and want to play a much more difficult version. As an example, if Square made a FF7 or FF10 version that was this difficult, I'd probably enjoy it for a playthrough, just because I love those games and played them to death.

I honestly can't recommend this game to anyone else. I want to, because it's Final Fantasy, and even though I've never beat 4, I've played enough of it in this version and know enough about it from other sources to know I'd enjoy it. If FF4 was more similar to FF3, a bit less story focused, then this would be okay. FF3 is designed to be a grindy, hardcore, retro-rpg. The original FF4 is not, it's very story focused in comparison and plays more like modern FF games.

The problem is that the 3d remakes of the early games require an INSANE amount of grind and have a massively increased difficulty. The only exception to this is FF3, that game is difficult as is, though the 3D version is supposedly a bit harder.

That said, I don't play Final Fantasy to grind (extra content, sure. Not for the main story). I do play it for difficult bosses that require a bit of strategy to get through, maybe you die once or twice. THIS though.... if you go to a boss even slightly under leveled you just get stomped. Maybe even on-level, think I'm level appropriate right now. If so, the AI is so brutal it isn't even remotely fun for a first playthrough. Most FF games you can find a workaround with enough planning in these situations, and if you DO have to grind, it doesn't take hours of grinding in most cases.

I primarily play for the story. And the amount of grinding required in this to continue the story is just asinine. I love Square, but holy crap they really messed up.

And just to be clear, I really enjoy FFX, which arguably has the hardest bosses in any of the games, and I didn't know any of the cheesy tactics (and avoided aeon overdrive cheese) when I first played it. I could beat those bosses in enough attempts to continue the story and have it flow correctly. This game doesn't do that. This game makes me stop and grind hard.

I honestly wish I could recommend this game because FF4 is one of the best in the franchise based on what I've heard, read, seen, and played from other FF spinoffs....

Not this version.
I might play through it eventually, but as an adult with other things to do, other, more recent games, other hobbies, etc, I can't.

Edit: btw, I haven't played for 40 hours, probably 10 or less. Had I, I probably would've beaten it by now. Left the game up a couple times while falling asleep, big picture mode. Time to beat this version's story is like 25 hours I think, so I'll put it this way: Based on that, it has me wondering just how much of that is pure, raw, difficult for the wrong reasons at times, grind.

Edit 2: Apparently the answer is about 12 hour difference. Looked up a playthrough of the SNES version of FF4 and it was around 12 hours. Time to beat this version's story is about 24.

SO. That means that they changed the difficulty and gameplay so much in this that it DOUBLES the required time to beat it. FF isn't about difficulty and grind. The only real exception is optional content like the Weapons, golden chocobo, etc, in 7, and FF3.

The ONLY reason I can think of for them to do this would be that other JRPGs released around the same time had at least 25 or so hours of story, if not 40ish. So how do we remake a classic, short RPG and increase its playtime to modern standards? Ruin it with obscene difficulty that ruins the game's flow, apparently. I love Square to death, but they REALLY botched this version. Like... just wtf.

To be clear, I don't necessarily mind grinding or difficulty in games, as long as it doesn't bring the story to a grinding halt if the game is story heavy. As a FF fan for 23 years, this version does NOT feel like a Final Fantasy game, only in story and setting. In literally just about every other way, it fails.

You're better off getting FFIV the Complete Collection on PSP/Vita, buying a SNES and the original cart, or emulating if you have the means.\t\t\t\t\t
