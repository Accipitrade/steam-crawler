\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game should be the Gold Standard when comes to “remakes” for it was properly done. The faithfulness that the developers at Matrix Software that took the pains and applied the wisdom to properly enhance the locations from the original that was unable to execute due to the limited capacities on the Super Nintendo (Famicom) now have been given the proper authentic vision the original developers envisioned.

For example: Damcyan (the desert-kingom) has taken the “middle-eastern” flavor and Fabul with its “Chinese” reflection; and of course, Eblan with its “ninja” embracement.

It’s pleasing to know that the original key developers returned to give support to this project that resulted in a Masterpiece.
-\tTakashi Tokita served as the Executive Producer. His credits also include Chrono Trigger
-\tProducer: Tomoya Asano
-\tFinal Fantasy Director VI, IX and XII - Hiroyuki Ito served as the Battle Designer
-\tand others.

FFIV originally was release during the golden age of the 16-bit RPGs on the Super Nintendo (SNES) under the
U.S. Title: Final Fantasy II.
It was re-release on the Playstation 1 (PS1) in the Final Fantasy Chronicle bundle with Chrono Trigger. This “remake” is masterful, with some of the original developers that have returned; to bring forward a well done – Masterful product is so rare…that I question why Squre Enix (originally Squaresoft) hasn’t continued to do so with other titles like…FFVI?

Story:

The story of FFIV is quite engaging. You’re dealing with themes of genocide, betrayal, atonement and sacrifice, along with plot-twists…that’ll keep you on the edge of -

“what’s going to happen next!?"

Script and Voice Acting:

Superb. I’m still in awe how well the Voice Acting and Script Dialogue for this game is.
Example: Rubicante – his screentime is very little and short but it’s so damn memorable. Not to mention also the faithfulness of all the characters design!
Within the game, you have the option to rewatch all the cutscenes (which is an excellent feature) that I’m glad they added. Golbez VA is powerful like Rubicante is. Where you not-only see his strength as a character but, the powerful delivery of voice work of the script carries an impressive performance that really shows the passion, the developers went through in recreating this masterful game.

Mechanics:

The battle mechanics is the same “classic turn-based random battle” that has been a staple of the series. Rather than the free roam "Kingdom Heart style battle." The authenticity of this game remains faithful to the original.
The character class in FFIV each has a pre-defined class that can’t be modified. However, characters that come and go from your party can impart augments-items that can bestow that particular character’s special ability to another character. This does offer some customization. This is a new feature that originally wasn’t introduced in the original SNES version. I’m actually very happy for it, because one of my favorite augments is the “safe-travel” no encounter feature. If you’re like me, likes to obtain 100% Steam Achievements, this augment is necessary.

Music:

The Legendary Nobuo Uematsu. Enough Said.

Visuals:

Masterful when comes to this remake. For those who played the original and now get to see everything faithfully done in 3-D. From the “Land of the Summons” to the Tower of Babel and the memorable characters: CID, Golbez, Cecil, Rosa, Rydia, Rubicante King Giott and the “Lali-Ho” dwarfs and the list goes on and on…the locations of each “kingdom” you visit. All great and memorable. From the Underworld of the dwarves to the Moon and the Lunarians. All faithfully done.

Conclusion: Buy the Game
Any true FF fan who knows the classic FF games, needs to do themselves a favor. And pick them up FFIV Remake.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Final Fantasy IV" is often considered one of the landmark titles of the franchise, and rightly so. In this remake, which brings back the cut content from the SNES original, this readily-available venture has more to offer. Compared to the three previous NES titles, the game took major strides forward in developing larger, more-fleshed-out stories and game mechanics that would become more prominent in the later games.

Those strides include the ATB system. Rather than inputting all of your commands at the start of a round and then having the faster characters go first, you have to make your decisions on the fly. This adds a new degree of tension to battles, as you not only have to choose what to do, but also have to select it as quickly as possible. The enemies also get the same treatment, so you have to respond in real-time to what your party is contending with. Need to launch a physical attack? Well, if the enemy casts a powerful ability, you might have to instantly change your plan. The game does allow you to place certain commands on the battle screen options right away, so you can always have your Curaga or Shell spells available as soon as possible. I DO recommend a mod to make the battles just a little smoother.

While the job system from "Final Fantasy III" is no longer present, the classes of characters you have will always allow for some balance. Though this may take away from some of the expansiveness of what your party is capable of doing, the game partially rectifies this with the Augment system. Augments allow your characters to use particular skills that other party members were capable of doing. This will allow you to, perhaps, have your dragoon use Bardsong in order to heal the party, or let your thief character Cry to lower the defenses of your enemies. Each character is only allowed a certain number of Augments, so deciding who to give them to and how many you should equip allows for some variety. Plus, the game does not "force" you into using certain Augments in order to win, unlike "Final Fantasy III" where some bosses were virtually impossible unless you had certain jobs in battle.

The story itself is the product of growing pains. As the first major leap forward in terms of deeper storytelling, the story has not necessarily aged very well. The number of times that "Final Fantasy IV" pulls the fake-out trope is staggering, to the point where you can see them coming from a mile away. Character development also tends to...just kind of stop at certain points, as revelations are less about the characters growing and more about things happening TO them. The story also necessitates some rather forced party reductions. There are some characters who you'll play with for awhile, then they go away and you'll never see them again except in dialogue. The later games would do a slightly better job of giving you party malleability for the final pushes to the end. To clarify, the story is NOT bad; it's just basic.

As far as aesthetics, the sound is okay. Some of the tracks are just as brilliant as you remember, but the remake has a bit of an audio-balance issue. The treble especially is a bit too blaring, making certain dungeons and locations (Baron, for instance) more of an assault on the senses rather than atmospheric. The SNES version's sound was simply given more care. Much like "Final Fantasy III," I don't mind the graphics or sprites--and the voice acting is surprisingly good!--but the dungeon designs are a little more cumbersome. Like a lot of early RPGs, there simply isn't enough differentiation between floors, rooms, and locations. This doesn't necessarily mean that you'll get lost, but rather that they feel a bit stale to travel through sometimes.

Any other complaints I have are too minor to make any substantive difference, but I do have to say that I wish the enemies were just a bit more generous with giving gil. Unlike other games, you'll find yourself wanting to sell your armor or weapons more frequently than in other installments. Just be sure that you don't accidentally sell a weapon or armor that may come in handy for a future dungeon...though granted, by the time you realize that, it might be a bit too late.

If you've ever played the original SNES version, then you know that "Final Fantasy IV" is not a difficult game. This version, however, cranks up the difficulty, even including a Hard mode and a New Game+ option. I love these changes, as it makes the journey more of a perilous adventure than a stroll.

Though it may still have some rather unfortunate flaws, "Final Fantasy IV" is a huge step in the right direction for the franchise and RPGs as a whole. It still has a wonderful charm that can keep the RPG fan or RPG newcomer satisfied, and heralds that the future of the franchise would indeed have a Golden Age on the horizon. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tFFIV is the first instalment without class sharing, which every character has his or her own unique abilities and specialties. In addition, it is also the first instalment with deeper storyline with different characters in different plots of the story. However, it may have alot of characters but lack of character development. The pace of the story is very good, which you dont really need to grind to level up.

The battle system is the traditional rpg turn based system. What unique about this game is that every character has its own unique abilities and specialties. What it makes even more fun is that it requires you to plan out your strategies and tactics ahead before facing against more difficult foes and bosses as the game progress. If you plan out well, most of your battles can be set to auto battle mode. Otherwise, it would take alot more time to complete each battle. Therefore, you need to understand each foes and bosses strength and weakness along with their battle patterns in order to set up the strategies and tactics for each dungeons and boss fights.

I highly recommend this game. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

My issues stem from a completionist standpoint, wherein you need to hunt down 5 items and 4 summons with a base 0.4% droprate, only able to be raised to 0.8%. With the improved droprate, several of the items took me well over 500 encounters.
Most of these encounters being rare you must either farm gil for Sirens to force the spawns, or gamble based on the monster's appearance rate. Either way, expect over 100 hours of farming mindless autobattles to platinum this one.\t\t\t\t\t
