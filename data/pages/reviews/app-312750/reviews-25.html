\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFFIV in general is a great game. Whether you are playing on the original SNES, the PS1, the DS and even here on Steam. So many good experiences. One thing I do like to mention is that the game really puts in interesting predicaments... like giving you magic users but no ways to heal their mp because Ethers drop so little in the beginning of the game. I have played this game before but have never played this version of it and I wanna say that I have grown a new appreciation for Tellah. Such a wonderfully designed character. The textures are smooth in comparison to the DS version and the voice acting is still a nice added bonus. Though I do wish the voice acting would have been for every line in the game like more modern Final Fantasy games. Anyway, overall this version is one for the books and glad I picked it up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRidiculously slow paced battles, even when set on max speed, locked at an extremely low unchangeable frame rate, (there used to be a framerate mod but then they updated the game randomly and the mod got disabled). Towards the end there are extremely long, repetitive dungeons that all look the same, with far too many enemy encounters that have far too much health, way too spongey, and go on for far too long.

NEVER GO TO THE MOON. IT'S A MISTAKE.

Could've been good, but dragged out PAINFULLY long in my opinion. My least favorite Final Fantasy of the the games from I to IV. The music in this one is the least inspired and least memorable thus far out of all the games in my opinion. Not horrible, but just nothing special. The story is okay. I like the design of some of the bosses and the occasional voice acting is a nice addition.

I wish I could recommend this one. The original version of the game instead of this remake may be far more enjoyable, and maybe more to my taste, but I haven't tried it. This one had my interest for a while early on in the game and the pacing was good for the first dozen hours or so. But towards the end things seemed to be dragged out unnecessarily long and there's no way I think anybody would ever want to replay it. I could say I didn't enjoy this because it was too dated, but for some reason I enjoyed the first 2 final fantasies way more than this, so I'm not sure that's it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow a pretty solid entry for the Final Fantasy franchise. This version of was my first experience with FFIV and after it's completion I am not disappointed. Plus voices and visuals are pleasant considering this was a remake for the Nintendo DS and the game-play, very fun.

Final Fantasy IV does a good job managing difficulty; this is most notable for its boss fights. Almost all bosses are unique and have their own strategy for defeat. ATB systems keep the player on edge, quick on the mind, so you better find the weakness while you've got time. It's doable. Not once did I feel overpowered, nor under-powered. Gaining new augments / abilities kept the game-play fresh and satisfying upon obtaining and contributed to more adaptable strats. With a balance as proper as this one will mostly likely not be compelled to rage quit, rather be motivated to complete presented obstacles.

For a game that originally came out in 1991, I must say I enjoyed it's story thoroughly, especially the characters, who felt like they had actual personally for the first time. Even if it feels dated, overall it was cute, and clear to see it influenced the more complex narratives in future entries that fans love today. I can't help but to feel respect and hope for others to recognize this pleasant experience.\t\t\t\t\t
