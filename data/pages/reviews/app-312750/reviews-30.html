\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA timeless classic, and one of the best ways to experience Final Fantasy IV. While the textures intended for the Nintendo DS can be offputting to some, I find them charming, and the voice acting still holds up to this day. This was one of the first FF games to feature a deep plot and memorable characters, and in my opinion is one of the best places to start if you're interested in the franchise. Mainly because it was also my first when I played it on the DS.

I will say though, do look up a guide for augmenting, It's not immediately obvious which augments are useful and which are meant to be discarded on non-permanent party members. And you can mess up your entire playthrough by giving Cecil something stupid like auto-potion, or missing out on Omnicasting from Fusoya which, when put on Rosa makes her into one of the most valuable party members for the endgame. That was a mishap I ran into when I played on the DS as a child. I do plan on going back and 100%ing this game. No matter how long that may take.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe classic SNES epic adventure comes to Steam users in this 3D remake. As there are two categories of potential players of this game - newcomers to the adventure and players of the original looking for nostalgia, this review will be split for both audiences.

Review for newcomers:

For those who did not play FFIV on the SNES (or in one of its re-releases), the critical acclaim and fan base to this RPG is vast and legendary. Coming to this twenty-nine-year-old game, even with updated 3D graphics, is a bold choice, likely motivated by good experiences with later Final Fantasy titles. How a new player with feel about FFIV will depend heavily on what the player's interpretation of a good JRPG is. FFIV is a game from which the modern JRPG has evolved, but it may not be fully representative of what JRPG's are today.

Following the style of FFII, FFIV features a not-quite-ensemble cast of characters in that the party changes and varies throughout the game, but there is never a time when the player will customize the party from a larger ensemble (like in many later Final Fantasy games). One of the charms of many Final Fantasy games is the unique and broad array of characters that can join in the adventure. FFIV is the first Final Fantasy game to really give the player some very unique characters like an elderly sage and five-year-old twins.

If newcomers are experienced with later Final Fantasy titles like FFVI, VII, VIII, or IX, then the game play will feel similar as FFIV was the first in the series to implement the pseudo-active, turn-based ATB system. For those unfamiliar with earlier JRPG battle systems, the battle system may give a fun challenge as the earlier turn-based Final Fantasy games required a bit more strategy than the most recent titles in the series. The overall flow of the game follows the now-traditional mode of epic story-telling, challenging boss fights, and long periods of grinding.

The fundamental soul to FFIV is its story. Consider the game's release occurred in 1991, the epic story is vast with elements of budding romance, death and loss, betrayal, redemption, perseverance, and sacrifice. Unlike modern RPG's, the tale is not accompanied by high-resolution cinematics, even in this 3D remake. (Although the addition of voice acting is a nice addition.) Players that need realistic cinematics to be immersed in a game's tale may be disappointed. Those that can become immersed through text-based story-telling will find FFIV's story deep and rich enough to easily compare with modern JRPG titles - and in many cases, easily surpasses them. The story focuses on Cecil, a dark knight struggling with internal conflicts that are exacerbated by his king's cruel use of military force. Faced with the vast repercussions of his dark deeds, Cecil embarks on a quest to right his wrongdoings and find the answers to the king's cruelty and his own identity. Joined by a wide cast of characters with varying strengths and abilities, Cecil's journey evolves into a quest to foil the plans of a mysterious sorcerer, Golbez.

FFIV is, nevertheless, a game that is nearly three decades old. JRPG's have changed in significant ways since 1991, and newcomers to FFIV may find some aspects unsavory. As with most JRPG's of the 1990's, FFIV requires a great deal of grinding to make story progression. Players should expect hundreds of random battles throughout their journey, which many find too tedious to be enjoyable. Graphically, the game does not stand up to other titles of the time of the remake - mostly due to the fact that the remake was for the 3DS and not a more robust system.

Review for experienced FFIV players:

Besides the new 3D world of Earth, little has changed in our beloved FFIV epic. Minor tweaks are made, such as Namingway's name-changing ability removed and the addition of a few small cut scenes. One notable addition is the inclusion of voice acting. Although more care could have been put into this new audio device, its inclusion adds a nice new effect. (And apparently, "Cecil" is pronounced with more Japanese-sounding phonetics as opposed to the English "See-cil." Who knew?) The 3D remake does not add much to story-telling, although a few cutscenes are notably more enhanced by these updated visuals.

The most notable addition to the game is the augment system. This system allows players to find certain items (augments) that will provide some benefit to the player. These benefits can be fairly benign or extremely powerful. Augments are assigned by the player to certain characters and only take affect if those characters are in the party. They are optional. So, those looking for an experience as identical to the original as possible can effectively ignore the augments. Of course, the included minimap option is a nice addition to the game as well.

This 3D remake still feels like the beloved 2D tale from the early 1990's. If the player is looking for a way to experience the nostalgic saga without resorting to emulators, then they will find this 3D remake suitable to fulfill that gaming itch. While it may look different from the original, all the important bits, including the wonderful story, are still there.\t\t\t\t\t
