\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinal Fantasy IV is a classic and this port of the DS remake is well done. The story is much improved over FF3 and you can tell that the series starts to pick up steam here. The story, as always, is unrelated to the other numbered games in the series, however, there are themes, character classes, moves, enemies and summons that reoccur throughout the series.

FF4 features the ATB, Active Turn Based, battle system, where a meter fills and then a character can attack. Overall I like this system, but found myself slowing down the speed in the options for some tougher boss battles. The characters have predetermined classes in this game and learn new skills and spells at predetermined levels. Characters will come and go from your party at times, but this doesn't feel bad and actually makes battles more interesting early on as it forces you to adjust tactics a bit.

The story and the characters in the game were well developed and kept me interested throughout. There was voice acting for cut scenes that was surprisingly better than I expected. The music in the game is very well done and the atmosphere as a whole made me nostalgic for the Final Fantasy games I played when I was younger - although I never played FF4 before.

The biggest issue I had with this game is it suffered from needing large amounts of grinding and unless you know the game well, you really need a guide to ensure you don't miss some key items, side quests and equipment. I opted to skip some of the side quests later on in the game and found myself under leveled in the last dungeon. It was at this point that the game became a struggle to hold my interest. i don't enjoy tougher battles that net you small amounts of xp that you need to grind for hours, so I just did my best to press through the last bit of the game under leveled and it hurt the overall experience for me.

Overall, I do think this is a good game as long as you enjoy an amount of grinding and you follow a guide to ensure you do all the side quests. I think if you did those that would reduce the end game struggle and make the experience much more enjoyable. While this isn't the first Final Fantasy game I would recommend you play, it is a worthwhile choice for those of you looking for a traditional Final Fantasy experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer: I have played and beaten every FF since the first (except 13). I cherish the stories, and the way they are told.

I have beaten the game, and completed every side quest.

Pro:

Great gameplay
Good story (more on this later)
Lots of side content (quests, hunts, etc)

Con:
I won't go for a bullet list. The problem with this game is the lack of continuity in the story.

Too much content was reserved for DLC. There is historical precedent for sections of a FF game to be played with select characters. This game doesn't do that.

I recommend playing in the following order to get the most of the story and character development:

Play Episode: Gladio when he leaves the party during the main game
Play Episode: Ignis during the section in Altissia
Play Episode: Prompto when he leaves the party late in the game
Play Comrades: before entering the final chapter

I make these recommendations because they develop characters in major ways that are referenced in the main game, yet the lack of said development really mutes the impact of revelations in the main game.

If you don't use these recommendations, you will be exposed to revelations that will have absolutely 0 emotional impact to them.

That is my main complaint with the game. It builds up major character events, yet leaves all development of them to side content. The story development is completely lacking in the main game, so much so that major events lack the emotional impact that the developers were obviously going for.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe graphical style was a little jarring at first, though I find I adapted quickly to it and now prefer it over the original 2D graphics, most if not all the 3D models share a heavy similarity to their 2D counterparts, making them all work in 3D while keeping the original ideas. Due to it being a port of a DS game the screen is very zoomed in, which makes navigating without the map painful so if you don't mind having the transparent map on the screen constantly you shouldn't have too much problem getting lost, giving you items as a completion reward for filling the maps makes having to use the map less of an issue if you like to go for 100%.

While I recommend the game for the casual playthrough, I do have issues with getting 100% due to how the "Tails" drop rate works, you could find yourself grinding enemies for hours to get these rare items that shouldn't have such a low drop rate, same goes for the rare summons. As for the post-game content, there is only really the two superbosses in this port. The Gameboy Advance port of the 2D game had a bonus dungeon with its own different superboss. So either way you are going to miss out on some post-game bonuses but since they weren't in the original game I wouldn't worry about which game version to pick due to them. Instead pick whether you want the original improved 2D game or this 3D re-make with some voiceacting, both have their own charm.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDecent port of an amazing game.

The game plays just like all the final fantasies of the time: ATB battles, exploring the overworld etc.. If you've played any FF before X, you know the drill.

FFIV is lifted above many of these by great characters placed into a fanastic story. The story of Cecil and his friends sucks you in without being quite as complex as the PS1 titles.

In terms of gameplay, not much is changed from other early titles. The only thing that may bother some is that customisation is limited to special "augments" with most abilities simply coming with levels. Personally that didn't bother me but if you're looking for a job or materia system, it's not that kind of FF.

The port is the DS remake, which was then ported to mobile and steam. I had no real issues with it, other than small artefacts around the text that strangely hovered above letters. I played it at 2K and it was locked at 30fps.

Overall, a must be JRPG fans and whether you buy this version or the PSP/SNES sprite version is up to you.\t\t\t\t\t
