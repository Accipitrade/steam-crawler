\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCertainly worth the purchase.

Everything about this remake feels streamlined in a way that keeps the player moving from plot point to plot point with as few interruptions as possible. All of the aged hangups you'd find with other JRPGs of its era, such as slow, monotonous combat, dragged-out dialogue, and aimless plot progression, are mostly absent--or at the very least, dulled--from the overall experience. It's a welcome change to such a weathered formula, and while I played this release on the DS years ago, it still feels fresh and passionately-made.

If you're a fan of JRPGs or the Final Fantasy series, this is a no-brainer must-play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of my favorite games of all time and is the first Final Fantasy game I played when I was about 9 years old. It was called Final Fantasy 2 then on the SNES (since it was only the second Final Fantasy game to have been released in NA at the time). It was different in vastly different ways, and not just in terms of the interface (there were some weird censorship shenanigans going on back then), but at it's core the story is the same.

While somewhat simple by today's standards, at the time it was pretty groundbreaking to have a story this mature released on a game system that was considered a toy for kids. It's themes of love, duty, cruelty, authoritarianism and redemption will always make it a timeless classic in my opinion. If you've never played it before, it is well worth your time and if you have played it already, this version is fantastic and adds a lot to the original game without taking away any of it's charm. It's a very good port and works very well on modern systems. Highly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
