\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaying FF4 was an experience. The game wears its heart on its sleeve, telling a story that may be a little simple but is told with full conviction. Some moments might feel cheap, but it can still get to you. During the ending cutscene I was a full mess. On a gameplay level, the game's a lot harder than you might expect by reputation. The SNES FF4 was infamously easy, but this is a port of (a port of the mobile version of) the DS remake, which ironed out some bugs and rebalanced the entire experience toward hardcore fans. Choose the easier difficulty option if you want a smoother ride; I took the harder option (which preserves the DS difficulty) and there were certain points where I nearly locked myself into an unwinnable situation. I still had fun, though, but this experience might not be for everyone. The game is a lot more linear than I though it'd be, but that helps keep its story cohesive. There are sidequests and hidden loot to find if you want to go off the beaten path for a spell. I think the most divisive aspect of the game will probably be its low-res 3D art style; I certainly enjoyed it (it reminded me of tabletop figurines, which added a charming level of personal involvement) but if you want 2D pixel art, you won't find it here. All in all, a great time, though the game did crash after I finally (finally) beat the final boss, meaning I had to see the ending on YouTube. Ya win some ya lose some\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI highly recommend it. It's an endearing game from its pixel-style textures to its colourful characters.
The story, as you'd expect, is very classic Final Fantasy. Crystals are being stolen from their kingdoms and you've got to stop the person behind it. That's the tl;dr. It gets a lot more complicated than that obviously, haha. It's a good story!

If you're a fan of Final Fantasy XIV, I definitely recommend this. You'll recognise quite a quite few things, especially so with Endwalker being heavily inspired by FFIV.
It's certainly interesting to see where FFXIV gets some of its stuff from! Lali-ho-ing dwarves and moon bunnies await!

Honestly I think this has aged better than some of the other FF ports, particularly from the PS1 era, imho. It was obviously made for smaller screens, and yeah, you're gonna see the pixel textures on the characters very clearly. It'll be a bit jarring at first, but after a while, you get used to it and it's actually quite charming.

The biggest thing you'll notice is that the battles are locked at 15 fps and feel like they're running in slow motion. That can be fixed with a simple patch! Makes a world of a difference, trust me.
