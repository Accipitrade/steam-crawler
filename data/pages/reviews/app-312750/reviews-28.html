\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFF4 is another great oldschool RPG, however this version differs from the one I was bit familiar with (the oldest one) as it's a 3D remake.

First of all, the bad things about this version:
Menus have weird inherent lag to them which is very annoying when quickly navigating around, I have found no fix for this.
Another really annoying thing is battle framerate, while the overworld seems somewhat fluid, the battles seem to be capped to something like 15 FPS if even that which makes them incredibly janky and slow.
There is a "fix" by the community that can increase it up to 25 which makes it playable but it also speed up the battles which can cause issues even with the Wait mode.
While they might not break the game, both issues are incredibly annoying and might ruin the game to some people.

Aside from the aforementioned problems the game works and plays like a charm. This version has all kinds of good stuff from additional cutscenes to voice acting, music is weirdly low quality though despite the tracks themselves being good.

There's also a "new" addition of augmentations that seem to be geared towards min-maxers and multiple playthroughs, I mostly grabbed and used them as they came but some seem rather gamebreaking. For example I had the main character equipped with both Counter and Draw Attacks and he almost singlehandedly butchered some fights, I'm not sure if they have done any rebalancing otherwise. I later read that since I hadn't pooled augments to specific characters I missed some like Dualcasting which would have made the game probably ridiculously easy on the main healer/support. Overall I'm not really a fan of the system even though it adds some depth, since it feels like you need to use a guide.

Combat feels bit less "all-in" than in earlier titles as you don't get slapped to low health from single hit nearly as often and having support capabilities on multiple characters makes it easier to recover. Managing multiple characters is also pretty fun but with the fps "fix" you need to be fairly fast even on slower battle speeds. Aside from some surprisingly tough enemies that cast party wide big damage abilities, most of the combat was fairly easy until endgame. I'd imagine taking bit of extra time and leveling up would make a well prepared party able to just auto combat through non-boss encounters.

One of the things I really liked though was how the spells work. Instead of common buying or finding them as in previous games, you gain them as you level up. Also having early access to lots of spells lets you play a lot more strategically, even though some of them seem to make fights perhaps too easy.

Another thing the game does really well is bosses, they often have their own identity and the fights tend to feel like they have custom rules. For example one boss pops out some gas that reverses effects of damage and healing occasionally and another has multiple bodies and uses ability combos with each other.

Compared to previous games the characters have bit more meat to them, but they still kind of feel 1-dimensional, the constantly changing inner dialogue you can check on menu depending on your progress is nice addition though.

While the game flows fairly nicely and has few smaller forks for optional things to do, it's still pretty straightforward. I had pretty good time until the last dungeon that has similar issue as FF3 had where it seemed to last forever. At least this time there were save points.

Overall this is another cautious recommendation, mostly due the issues I mentioned at the beginning. If you can get over them, the game is pretty good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would only recommend it if it's on sale and you understand what you're getting into here. If you can get it deeply discounted it can be a fun little nostalgia trip with an asterisk*

The pacing is pretty decent. I hardly did any grinding and when I did it was to acquire specific items. The extra fights you end up getting in trying to complete the map achievement and wandering around to do the side quests are basically all the experience you need to get through the game, provided you put some forethought and research into your desired character builds.

*I played it on hard mode. With experience as a former FF4 rando racer with a pretty decent record- so I'm taking that into account with this review.
-If that was the hard mode then the normal mode is probably fairly dull.
-If you're already intimately familiar with the 2d versions then this provides some fun new challenges and interesting new fight mechanics that still pay homage to the original* with another asterisk. The combat and character building is basically where all the fun is.
*The balancing is all over the place. Most of the boss encounters are alright. But some bosses are a complete nightmare (complete with RNG fight scripts) others are complete pushovers with no clearly defined difficulty curve throughout the game and many of the nightmare bosses are completely cheesable, which sucks because throwing a hand full of cheese at a big scary boss really kills the atmosphere in some spots.

The music is meh. It's really a matter of preference but I think the new versions of the music in a lot of cases really failed to capture the original character of the game. And overall the sound design feels a little weak.

The voice acting is surprisingly decent for what it is, but there's absolutely no rhyme or reason as to which cut scenes were voice acted and which ones weren't. And the ones that weren't voice acted aren't skippable.

If you go into this thinking "Yeah. I'm going to go all completionist on this game!" Think again. Without providing any spoilers completing this one resigns yourself to the mercy of pseudorandom number generation. Some people will complete it without even trying. While others have reportedly spent absurd amounts of time trying to get a specific rare drop needed to complete their augments without any success.

Also the game crashes a lot. The only thing that makes it playable is the fact that the game automatically quick saves every time you exit an encounter or go through a door. So you can just load the game back up and hit continue. Then suddenly once I reached the final dungeon the game mysteriously stopped crashing. The quick save also makes wiping on any given encounter completely inconsequential because you just end up back at the last encounter or last door/stairway. You could go by the honor system and just reload your last save but if the game gives me cheese I'm going to eat it.

TLDR:

If you've only ever played the north american SNES release then it could be fun and challenging.

If you've never played any version of FF4 before I would definitely say play literally any other version of this game. This would not be a good 'first FF4 experience' for anybody and for the uninitiated in the FF4 universe I would definitely recommend starting with one of the 2D versions.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game a few years ago since I'm a great fan of the Final Fantasy saga. I had already played this game three times in a row on the DS during a summer break and at the time I played it, I absolutely loved it.

I think this game has one of the strongest stories along with FFVI, for example. I absolutely love the way everything happens, it's pretty well connected. The characters are great, and even though there's romance between Cecil and Rosa, it's not exactly the main plot, so in my opinion this is great. There's drama, mystery, plot twists, treason, love, friendship, redemption... Also when you think the plot is almost over because there's nothing else on the map to visit... Surprise, a new whole map to explore! It's great. The only think I don't really like is how characters keep dying... NOT, because they come back again and again, like, can you stop lmao the only one that ACTUALLY dies is Tellah...

The gameplay is exactly like the DS port. You have some sidequests, hidden eidolons, augments... And there are even superbosses! You can't complete everything on one playthrough, at least you need 2 to finish the bestiary and the augment thing. But I like it so much that I don't actually care, I'd do it gladly. The graphics are optimised for PC. They are not the greatest but they're cool, knowing the game.

So if you're willing to try an old Final Fantasy game, this one is great. I definitely recommend playing this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been replaying all final fantasy games and this is the first bad one I've encountered. I loved it as a kid but damn it didn't age well. In final fantasy 1 2 and 3 you had a lot more customization, sure you had to grind the party felt like yours, it was in your control and it's successes and failures were a direct consequence of your decisions earlier in the game.In final fantasy 4 things are a lot more..ubisoft-y. It feels more like a movie with no fail state, everything is on rails and you don't have as much room for customization. Here are some examples:- Vendor and drop items basically don't matter, most important gear comes from story progression- You don't buy skills, you either gain them as you level or as the story progresses- Your party isn't fixed, it changes depending on where you are on the plot, so it doesn't feel good to equip good items on a character, nothing guarantees they will continue on your party for long.- Active battle system doesn't make any sense in a turn based RPG, it only means you have to play in a slower speed than you would on FF1~3, or that you don't have time to organize a proper strategy or even read what the skills do during the battle.On the bright side the story is a lot better compared to 1~3 and the bosses have way better mechanics, you actually feel like fighting a boss for the first time in the series (for me) instead of spamming your stats against the boss to see who wins.All in all, final fantasy IV is not very much worth of your time, FFIII does a better job of scratching the nostalgia itch.\t\t\t\t\t
