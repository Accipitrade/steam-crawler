\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinal Fantasy IV (3D Remake) is plagued with the same problem all the 3D Remakes have and that is bad controls and rough graphics. If you played these games on 3DS or mobile you are probably more tolerant and able to see where the 3D Remakes shine in the form of extra content. Unlike the Pixel Remasters (which is my preferred way of playing the older Final Fantasy games), these 3D Remakes often have more classes, bosses, dungeons, and items compared to the Pixel Remasters but there is something about the 2D art style of how the game was originally made that just feels right.

Similar to my other reviews for the older versions/3D Remake... the game is fine and isn't my favorite version but it has extra content and if you are a Final Fantasy completionist like me, you are going to play it anyway. I will ultimately give this game a thumbs up but if I could choose a middle-ground with "meh" that would more properly reflect my feelings on the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of my favorite Final Fantasy games from my adolescence. I actually like the graphics in this remake which seem to be a point of contention. The musical score is just as great as I remember it too. The story is good. Maybe not the greatest ever, but I still enjoy it. The voice acting is actually pretty good too. You can actually rush through this game pretty quickly, which kind of surprised me. I heard this was the 'hard' version of the game, but I only found it difficult in one fight really at about the midpoint of the game. Other than that, everything is pretty much as I remember it with a new coat of paint. Not a big fan of some of the achievements requiring insane drop rates though. Kain's animation can sometimes mess with the interface when he jumps off screen too. Other than those nitpicks, I think this is a great game and well worth playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is with reluctance that I cannot recommend this game. The voice acting is surprisingly good for a DS game, the actual animations are fine such as they are, and I do like the augment system. In addition, at least at the beginning of the game, it feels pretty balanced in comparison to the GBA version. When I tried that version, I almost literally just spammed the attack button for the first hour and I never took a single hit in damage, that's how weak the enemies were.

Unfortunately, this game starts falling apart the further you get into it. It's clear that the game was made harder in the form of harder mobs, bosses, and a slower start for your characters in battle, but not actually adjusted so this was balanced. Thus, there is one area where you are flat out likely to softlock if you haven't been spreading out your saves, and random encounters can be stupidly unfair (on more than a couple occasions my entire team was killed before I got a single action, and I wasn't even very under-leveled). The dungeons often have completely invisible passages in them so I hope you love mashing your face against a wall or looking up a guide to find them.

At the end game things ease up a bit in that if you have been getting the best augments you can kind of cheese the unfairness a bit. And there's also a source complaint. Basically all the best augments in the game are possible to miss if you don't know how to get them. Basically, if a character will leave your party later in the story (no it doesn't give any indication as to which party members will do this), if you give them 1-2 (or 3 in one specific case) augments before then you will eventually get access to certain augments for your own use. Probably a consolation prize for using valuable expendable items on characters who will just leave but it essentially means the best abilities in the game are locked out unless you have planned ahead.

Lastly the story is just bad and the characters act as the railroaded plot demands.

Overall I can't recommend this game. It's a 5/10 at best in my opinion. Go play FFVI instead if you haven't already.\t\t\t\t\t
