\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA turn based RPG with a linear story progression and random encounters. Originally released in the early 90s. You control a party of up to five characters with different kind of magical or melee skills. The party composition changes in certain points of the story. Your final party will consist of a tank, a healer, a summoner and two melee attackers. You also need to use different strategies against certain bosses or mobs, like debuffing, buffing, item usage or attack order.

It has an open world and offers some optional content like grinding rare pieces of gear or acquiring rare summons. A 2nd playthrough offers two additional bosses.

The game ran well on my PC without any major bugs. It is originally a Nintendo 3DS remake and thus uses low poly 3D models with cartoon like colors.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinal Fantasy IV is lovely in the way it tells a simple classic fantasy struggle between good and evil, anchored by a diverse, memorable set of party members, and effective in the way the story gradually escalates over time. I've only played this 3D remake, so I don't have a point of comparison for how faithful the remake is to the original story (I've heard that it hems pretty close, at least), so taken on its own merits, I think it's a great game and absolutely worth playing.

The ATB system originates from the SNES version of FFIV, and it feels fully formed here, making the combat engaging throughout and encouraging you to quickly figure out your usual rotations to make sure you maximize your action efficiency - the last thing you want is for everyone's ATB bars to just pile up while you fat finger your way through menus. As someone who's generally terrible at JRPGs, job systems, and party management, the fact that FFIV literally took all of that out of my hands by aggressively rotating the party and giving everyone a static, clearly defined job was a godsend. There's still quite a bit of strategy to consider even without the conventional job system, and determining the exact right niche for the party members I had at any given time was fun and kept me on my toes. My main critique of the party rotation is that some members felt more like guest characters than true party members, even if they had big, important moments during the times they WERE in the party - Edward, Palom and Porom are the ones that stick out in my mind the most in this regard, though Fusoya's another notorious example.

Story-wise, it's nice and straightforward, following Cecil as he realizes he's the baddie and breaks free of his chains to become a hero, with plenty of twists and turns throughout, though fortunately nothing too ridiculous. Until the endgame, that is - the last segment of the story kind of felt like it came out of nowhere, zooming out the scope of the game like crazy and kind of losing me a bit by the end. Given that it spirals a little out of control, I still felt that it mostly stuck the landing, leaning more into a grounded family connection than the high fantasy silliness otherwise in play in the endgame. Everyone gets their happy ending (except Kain and Tellah), and it feels like the world is fundamentally different from how it was at the beginning, so it's a success overall.

I know this port used to be pretty bad before Square Enix properly optimized the controls for PC, but now that it is optimized, it felt great to play. The DS underpinnings hold it back a little - the framerates are atrocious and hardcoded that way, and the graphics suffer from not being filtered through the muddy, low-res DS screens - but the voiced cutscenes are nice to have, and it honestly just felt good to play. It's a great, appropriate art style for a game like this, and it conveys the world well.

I can't tell you if you should get this version over the Pixel Remaster, or playing the Advance version or even just the SNES original. What I can tell you is that if you choose this version, it's worth it, and you won't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk I am going to keep this short as possible. I love Final Fantasy IV, and it is one of my top 3 favorite games of all time. But this game does something that is so unforgivable I can't recommend anyone getting it.

This didn't just happen once...but it became noticeable in a specific part. I was choosing a spell to cast, and scrolling through the monsters to target all. At the same time another character attacked and killed a monster. Whenever this happens the monster selection list disappears....and the game then selects whomever it is on at that moment, even though you do not hit a button to confirm.

Thus my spell that I was still scrolling through the listed enemies to hit all of them...only targeted one monster without me hitting a button to select anyone. This is unacceptable. It opens the door to many problems with wasted attacks to even possibly healing an enemy by hitting then with their affinity when you are trying to target someone else. Add that this game has active battle with adjustable battle speed....inexcusable.

There are so many versions of this game. I recommend getting another version unless you are that adamant about seeing it in a different viewpoint. Sadly I am and will probably continue dealing with this massive screw up. (This is the 4th version of the game I have played, or 5th if you count After Years....and easily the worst.)

EDIT: I just beat the game, and the issue only popped up a couple more times. I got used to the timing of selecting the enemy.

So after now playing through the game and learning to avoid that issue...would I recommend it?

Nope!

Play another version instead.\t\t\t\t\t
