\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall Score: 8/10

I finally get to play this classic RPG.
I wish After Years is available as well.

A few thing I didn't like were the Map System.
I wish they show dots once you went inside those hidden paths so you can at least remember where they are.
I got lost on Slyph Cave and Final Dungeon trying to look for the hidden paths.

The other thing I was getting annoyed was how quickly the encounter rate is.
Good Lord, just a few steps boom battle again.
I know this is normal for JRPG, but trying to find that hidden path and dealing with this was really testing me.

The last one was the sub-quest about the Rainbow Pudding.
I spend hours in that antlion dungeon trying to get that rare drop, along with the moon surface, but no luck.

Otherwise the game is good.
I already know some of the plots thanks youtube reviews from Pro-Jared to Rabtoons, and many more.
Even if I knew I still find myself engaged.
The only one I missed was Yang's sub-quest, since end up going to the moon first because the music of the Lunar Whale was really good, and I got so hype. :D\t\t\t\t\t

The good

> A very solid translation. They did a good job with the dialogue. It sounds very authentic
> This is a very balanced version of the game. On the harder difficulty, it can be quite challenging. in the early game, they do a good job of nerfing Cecil a little to help make the others more useful. It adds some strategy to the game
> They have a wide variety of side quests and extras which add a lot of value to the game. They feel very generically woven into the game.
>They have voice acting which is decent

The Bad
> I don't like the 3d graphics too much. I feel that if they were going to go 3d, they should have went ahead and did it on the ps3 or 360. Both systems were new and fresh at the time. On the ds, 2d would have looked nicer.
> The extra bosses and getting enough of the powerful auguments are locked behind a second play through.
>The onion gear can be very grindy to get. It can compete with the likes of a Disgaea game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly this is a fairly good game, but you should probably just get the 2d pixel remaster. FF4 in itself is definitely a classic, can't go wrong. The story and characters are fairly memorable and I think the voiced cutscenes are neat. I would recommend hard mode which is apparently balanced around the difficulty of the original Japanese version of the game. Surprisingly, some of the most challenging things I've experienced in the franchise are in this game. In my opinion there is a higher level of genuine strategy needed than is typically required in Final Fantasy games. More than a few times the game had me sweatin' and it was fun. However, there is some extremely annoying stuff about this 3D version of the game in particular that I will outline with a pro and con list.

Pros:
Music is good, sometimes not as good as the original tho IMO

Hard mode is genuinely challenging and not just for extra bosses

Voiced cutscenes are cute

auto-battle

Augments are fun to use

You can play with a controller, just a keyboard, just a mouse, or mouse and keyboard

Cons:
The art direction in itself is good IMO, but the models and textures do not look great upscaled like this, and kind of got annoying to look at after a while

The game feels weirdly slow, it's hard to describe, but it just feels kinda sluggish. FPS might be lowering in battle but I'm not sure

The UI is just boring and not aesthetically pleasing by any stretch of the imagination

There is a system for map completion where if you search 100% of any given map, you get a free item or couple of items. There are 2 problems with this:
1. There is more than a handful of times where you have 99% of a map and there's literally like a pixel unmapped, so you have to run against the walls and do all this stuff try to map that tiny invisible last 1%.
2. Probably one of the worst things about this game; there is no menu or anything to look at maps you've completed. This is an issue as, there is a VERY useful augment you get for completing 100% of every map. But if you map out 100% of the final dungeon and don't get it, that means you're running back through every single room of every dungeon to see what you missed. There are also several maps that are missable to 100%, so you might end up running through the entire game again just to realize it was one of those. Being able to check overall map completion would fix this entirely, but otherwise it's just a completely awful system.

Augments are extremely annoying. While yes in practice they do compliment the gameplay quite nicely, acquiring them and using them is just a pain. There are many that are missable that can only be acquired by giving augments to characters who eventually leave the party, some you can also only get in ng+. Now maybe I just wasn't paying attention, but I went through my entire first playthrough not knowing this and it ended up being a massive inconvenience. Honestly the main thing that ruins them for me is them not being interchangeable between party members, they are consumable and most of them you only get 1 of until NG+. I had many situations where I wanted to try strategies that would require having different augment setups but was just stuck with them on the wrong characters. It was very frustrating.

One of the super bosses is very annoying to fight and just kind of strange. This isn't entirely a con, as I ended up finding a strategy to beat it that I found amusing since it was so cheesy. Otherwise though IMO it doesn't feel like a meaningful challenge, it's just kind of confusing and cheap until you beat it and are like "dang that was stupid." But that's just me.


Note about getting 100% achievements:
You have to be kind of hardcore to 100% this game. I'm not sure that I regret it, but I don't think it's worth doing. For me a 2nd playthrough on ng+ just really wasn't fun at all. There's also dealing with the map and augment issues as I stated earlier. it's also really not advisable if you're adverse to farming for rare drops. There are I think 9 or 10 drops that are 0.4% drop rate you must get. This can be increased to 0.8% with an augment, but hoo man this can be a grind if you're unlucky. Because of the sluggishness I mentioned earlier, this game does not feel the best to farm random encounters in.

Note for Japanese learners:
May as well include this as I played this game in Japanese which is my 2nd language. I'd say it's pretty beginner friendly if you know the basics, not a ton of kanji, probably can pick up a lot of words in this game to familiarize yourself with both FF and general JRPG lingo. I'd suggest putting the battles on wait and setting the speed to slow though if you're learning so you have time to read things.

Overall it's a good game, just almost certainly inferior to the pixel remaster version. Has some pretty annoying issues that may or may not be less prevalent in a casual playthrough. IMO not worth the 100%, but do it if you're a bad enough dude and/or hate yourself.\t\t\t\t\t
