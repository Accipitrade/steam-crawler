\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a fairly decent story and a huge cast (12 playable characters). While I am more of a fan of the original 8-bit version, the remake is still quite enjoyable. It doesn't suffer the serious slow-down of the previous Final Fantasy III re-make, this is a welcome face-lift from it's original version.

Difficulty is average for a FF title.

Gameplay is pretty boring, honestly. It's easy to get lost for a minute if you're clicking through the dialog, but asking around or visiting a guide can set you straight quickly if you're lost.

The main reason for playing this is the story, as Characters are set in stone and can only be altered by the additional abilities you give them. There's not a lot of flexibility or customization. But don't let that deceive you, it's still rather important which abilities you equip.

This is probably one of the easiest FF titles out there that plays more like a movie than a game. Yeah, there are fights, and somewhat long dungeons along the way, but you can save often, and there are only a hand-full of places where you can't save, but there is the option to save and exit but you lose the save spot when you resume the game. Nice if you are in the middle of one of the few longer dungeons and want to take a break but not leave your PC going or whatever.

Overall due to the noteable characters, history and storyline, it's a quality FF title. Worth a play for anyone that enjoys RPG's, especially of the FF variety.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is one of the first Final Fantasy games to have the ATB system people loves, but is also one of the hardest games to be played, right on the beggining of the game you'll notice that, even on normal difficulty, the game enjoys making you pass through situations of peril from time to time. But in case you get defeated on the party, at least compared to Final Fantasy IX port, the Continue button works here, so you can retry the boss fight you got stuck all over again from the same point before starting it, turning it a bit less frustrating.

The characters of the game, there isn't much choice about who you bring with you, since the plot determines who goes with you and when, but on other hand, is the ONLY final fantasy game ever made where you can have 5 party members at the same time, and that is a lot.

The plot is not really that great, Square tried to make a plot that involves all characters and situations of friendship, love, betrayal and stuff, but they ended up making a plot way more tolerable than Final Fantasy XIII, just kidding, this game plot is far superior to FFXIII plot.

The port also brings new stuff, like a quest line involving Namingway, who you may end up finding on your adventure, aswell as the augment system, which allows you to bind skills of other characters and certain bosses on your characters, but I only spent them on Cecil since I know he's the only party member that will be present until the end of the game, and I have no idea if you lose an augment if It's placed on a character that has left the party.

Now, the game is 100% good? Well, not really, the port is quite ok, first of all, must keep in mind that this game port was actually made for 3DS and Mobile, but boy, they could've give a special treatment to the textures of the characters, on the intro you can already see the squares of the pixels on the character sprites, being that secondary characters, those pixels are far more visible, also counting that a few things from the 3DS game could come to PC, like being able to see the characters on the lower part of the screen when at the shop screen, It's not a very important thing, but at least makes the shop less boring.

Now the biggest problem about this port, is the hud itself, when you open the inventory, the first thing you want to see is your character status, but in this port, instead, they priorize the character thought more than the character status, so to see if your characters are injured or with bad status, you have to select magic, ability or any other menu command that involves managing your characters to be able to see the status of your party.

That doesn't end here, the hud may end up hindering you on battle, since sometimes the command menu may close from nothing, making you lose a few ATB seconds, aswell as sometimes the arrow may get confused when picking either to select all monsters or a monster on the back row, making you lose turn by not casting an AOE attack, but casting a single target attack, the navigation on the battle commands is bad aswell, there is too much information in it, and you can lose a good time trying to move to the command you want to choose, and even worse, sometimes the hud resets and send your cursor back to the first item, making you lose more time, or even use an action by accident. Compared to this hud, the hud from the Final Fantasy VI port is 100% better, because it brings only the necessary to the view.

But on other hand, you can change the ability list your character can make use of, you may even add a quick slot for some skills you use most for your character, and even select which ability the character will use when on auto-battle, but damn, couldn't they make the quickslot appear on the side of the normal battle commands? So instead of scrolling down/up to then reach the quick slot, I could select right and pick the skill I want to use, there are several ways they could've done the hud right.

So, In my opnion, If you deal with the hud in the game, which actually ends up being an extra difficulty to the game (nearly got my team wiped by Barbaricia because were trying to deal with it), the game is quite fine, there are even a few cutscenes, beside, since they begun adding cutscenes in the game, I wonder why they didn't do cutscenes to a lot of other dialogues in the game, since there are a few dialogues, even some after a cutscenes has JUST ENDED, which returns to the old and plain dialogue without voice acting, at least now I know on what game Onmyoji has based their story system on, but in Onmyoji's case, they did it wrong.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFF4 is one of my favorite games in the series and I TRIED to like this but this was just a very painful 10 minutes of play. The graphics are downright garbage, it looks and plays like a poor mobile phone port, the models are lower res than ps1 and super enlarged with pixelated textures, the effect is that everything looks bendy and weird (the armor and person is a single model with no seperation so the armor flexes as they walk and such, it doesn't look like armor it looks like everyone is wearing weird FF themed skin tight pajamas). Then bizarrely on top of this the text and menus are HUGE, they take up nearly half the screen on 1080p for a single line of text.

I would have ALMOST been able to forgive this and keep playing but it doesnt end there.

geForce 1060 6 gigs video mem, 32 gb ram, I7 3.6ghz and i got...around 20 fps at the peak. But it's not a real 20 fps, no, the game is RUNNING at 60fps, but it's only rendering at 20! So now on top of everything everything jerks around and jitters when it moves, it's really weird seing a 3d model that looks like it only has 6 frames of animation (which is honestly what i think they were going for, animation frames on a 3d model, but they gave the 3d model LESS "frames" than the original sprites!)

but wait, there's more!

the controls are AWEFUL. This game was clearly designed for touchscreen play only and the mouse and keyboard barely works. You can remap them with the launcher but how everything works is downright bizarre, you have to really play it to understand what i mean.

do yourself a favor and play the GBA or SNES versions, they're far superior to this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're here, you probably already know FF4. So how's it compare to the original? Well, mixed, but ultimately I think this is the best version I've played.

Cons:
- The graphics. Your opinion may vary, but I prefer the old 2D sprites, even if they are dated. The 3D is uglier.
- Voice acting in a video game, yikes. Turn it off immediately.
- No consequences for death? Maybe the most baffling decision & biggest detriment to this version of the game. If you die, it just kicks you back to right before the combat started. So this means there is really no consequence for dying, and sometimes it even makes more sense to just commit suicide than to run from combat or waste items. Bad.

Pros:
+ The difficulty. The addition of Hard difficulty has breathed whole new life into the game. In the original game, you can probably beat it just by spamming basic attacks and the occasional heal. Hard mode offers an actual challenge, and you'll have to utilize all your spells and get some of that tactical goodness going.
+ Auto battle option. All JRPGs need it

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis a rather shoddy port for an otherwise great game. This 3D version was a remake originally for the Nintendo DS then ported to Mobile then ported the Mobile to PC and it shows. What really angers me about this port is the controls, the menus and the lack of cursor memory in the Options. Also you cannot use your weapon from the items menu during battle, I can only manage this through setting it up as a default option for Auto-Battle, but you can't aim specific targets for the weapon items effects.
In the DS version it was VITAL to budget your MP, so you use a Healing Staff as an item to restore a small amount of HP for the whole party and it scales up with Spirit, same for elemental rods that scale with Intellect, but now here's the problem: You can't use the rods in battle, again you set up Auto-Battle to use it, but you can't select an enemy to use it on.
In the later game you can manage using Auto-Battle options to get by, such as when you get the "ultimate rod" because it hits all enemies. Early game is not relieable, so your Black Mages will be burning their MP to end the fight.

This version has the same bumped-up difficulty as the DS version on Normal and there's Hard mode as well for those that hate their life. The DS version had New Game+ to collect every Augment and customize your characters, but I won't be completing this version to find out.

I can't recommend this. Get yourself a PSP and buy FFIV:The Complete Collection, it's by far the definitive version of FFIV available with extra content that was originally on the GameBoy Advance, and a bonus "Interlude" chapter that leads up to 'The After Years'.\t\t\t\t\t
