\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter finally forcing myself to play through and beat this game 4 years after I bought it and promptly dropped it, I'm not entirely convinced that there's anything to be gained from playing FFIV in 2018. Graphics are whatever (this version does indeed have truly awful ones), the music is still good (voices should be turned off as soon as you can open the menu), but the gameplay is simply tedious and unfulfilling. Last but not least, the story which I once considered a standout in the medium as a child in the early 90s now fails to impress me whatsoever as an adult.

If there still is any value to be found from playing FFIV though, THIS is definitely not the version to look for it in.

Honestly, it's a real shame that they decided to port the mobile port of the dicey DS remake instead of the more faithful (opinions on "the after years" are truly mixed) PSP remaster "Final Fantasy IV Complete Collection." Should they ever release that on PC (don't hold your breath, odds are it would be bugged to hell even if they did) it would probably become the definitive version, but until then I'd say go with the digital version of that from PSN and play it on a PSTV while hoping that it also runs on PS5 when that inevitably comes out. Hell, even the actual DS or mobile versions are probably better ways to experience the remake than this Steam port.\t\t\t\t\t

It will forever hold that place in my heart of teaching me how fun RPGs can truly be. However, I haven't played this game since the original SNES version. There were a few cool changes like map completion bonuses, augments, and some voice overs. The graphics are sooooooo ugly though. Pretty much the ugliest rendition they could have managed. For those who are wondering THIS IS NOT HOW THE ORIGINAL GAME LOOKED. I remember the original FFIV having these amazing art renditions of the characters in the character screen. Why would they get rid of those?!

Anyway, I'm feeling nostalgic and wanted to play a classic. Still enjoyed the game even if it has this weird ugly wrapper.\t\t\t\t\t

watch as he finally confronts golbez in a scripted boss fight, then is revived by his friends and fights him at full- *glances at 2 hp of all characters and rydia with a defense stat of "paper"*

So if you haven't figured it out quite yet, final fantasy iv...physically hurts to play. It's not bad...it just hates your guts and will throw you into unfair situations faster than you can say "tellah won't die, he's the best character!"
from a magnetic cave, to a literal lava dungeon, to boss fights where the scripted sequences exist to inconvenience you and make the already insufferably hard fights harder, to a final boss that just spams the same ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ AOE over and over again, final fantasy iv hates your living guts and will remind you of how much it wishes you would go die in a fire every time you die because you didn't kill a summoner fast enough, which, i assure you, will be quite often.

FFIV is a game you have to play intelligently, you have stat boosters, you have party members who do specfic things for specific situations, you're required and expected to actually think and strategize per fight. eventually you'll get used to killing stone lizards and blowing your mp load on every dragon you see. do NOT play this game expecting an easy ride.
it will take your manhood and twist it off then ram it down your throat before lighting you on fire.
