It must've been a faulty cartridge (I bought it on ebay). Fast forward nearly a decade later and I see that it's available here on Steam. I bought the game, and only just now have been able to get around to beating it. This is only the second Final Fantasy game I've beaten in my life. Overall this was a pleasant experience, and I look forward to playing the After Years.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is one of the best old-school Final Fantasy in my opinion, I do not know about the original release but this remake is quite fun. I played FF III (3D Remake) before, It has great customisation but a rather forgettable story and this game is sort of the opposite on those points FF IV (3D Remake) has a good story but almost no customisation which was a bit of a bummer but not enough to deter me from enjoying the game. The story has its power of friendship/love moments but most of them were fine and it is not like the game world is very in-depth in the first place. I did get attached to most of the characters maybe it is because there is voice acting during the cutscenes (nothing pre-rendered only in-game camerawork). The gameplay is ATB (Active turn-based) and I feel like 5 characters on that system is a bit much, 4 max is what I personally prefer otherwise you need to slow it down or give an option to pause the game when you have to pick the actions of a character ready to act. There is an augment system allowing a 1 time key item to be used on a character to teach them either a passive or an active ability that have to be allocated into a really limited menu sharing the space with all the other actions during combat like Attack, Items, Magic so at most 2-3 free slots and in most cases 1-2 so it's not that great but most of the augments are not that good honestly so I do not know it is just a strange thing to me. Aside from equipment the augment system is the only customisation offered by the game and there are never enough party members and most characters join/leave the party during the story at times so it is a static composition that you just use and have not much inputs on. Personally I am fine with the gameplay but I remember FF III which offers plenty of customisation and it feels a bit like wasted potential. I did not have technical issues with this port, I cannot say I ever cared about the music it was not bad but nothing memorable to me and I would say the game is far less grindy then many other FF games even while only doing the main story exclusively.

8/10 Good story, good combat, low customisation, good port, decent music and a good game to pick up if you are looking for an old-school JRPG or a Final Fantasy.\t\t\t\t\t
