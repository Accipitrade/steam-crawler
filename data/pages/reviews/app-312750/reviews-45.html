\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinal Fantasy IV (the remake to be precise) is a great successor to the third game. This remake was developed by Matrix Software the same company that developed the remake of the third game. Even though this game did received a domestic release as "Final Fantasy II" for Super Nintendo. This is the only version avalible on Steam as of this writing. So if you happen to own the original version then you know what the game is like, but as its debatable if Steam will do any negotiations of licensing the first four original games, this version will suffice. From what I've gathered this was a Nintendo DS released game originally like the third game's remake with enhanced graphics and sound. The plot is the same as the original's plot with some small side stories added in. The game is about a dark knight named Cecil who is a dark knight who serves the king of Baron. Cecil leads the kingdom's army known as the Red Wings to attack the city of Mysidia to steal the water crystal(one of the four crystals of the blue planet), despite being loyal to the king Cecil has lately been questioning the king's motives and goals.
When Cecil showed a sign of disobedience to the king, his rank was strip down and ends up going on a mission with Kain (Cecil's friend) to deliver a ring to the Village of Mist. The ring in question turn out to hold a magic seal locking away a horde of monsters. When the seal broke, the monsters turn the Village of Mist into ruins. Cecil and Kain ended up getting seperated after a young girl named Rydia summons a monsters to rid of the terror brought upon her own village. This leads to Cecil embarking on a journey to not only learn of his true purpose in life, but to learn the king's true plan and what value do the crystals have to a man named Golbez.
Final Fantasy IV had a great cast of strong characters, a never ending list of monsters, and a dramatic yet satisfying story. The plot borrowed elements similar to Final Fantasy II(Original), where certain characters either leave the group or die. I won't say who, but just don't expect every character to remain in the group. The previous games were limited to only four characters during battle. This time it was increased to five characters maximum. There is no job listing option for the characters, who are all default to their own skill and title. This remake offered visuals that the third game's remake lacked such as more cinematic FMVs and voice acting during certain FMVs. Which I do liked how Matrix Software took notes on their last game and looked over what they could do better for the sequel. Even if you played the original Final Fantasy IV before, this is highly worth the time to play. Final Fantasy IV is a game I would list as a top favorite.\t\t\t\t\t

It does offer some new mechanics in the augment system. If used properly, the augment system allows you to have OP characters. It isn't explained in the game very well but it is worth looking up game guides about it. The augments also carry over to multiple play-throughs.

If you see this game on sale and feel nostalgic about playing the SNES version then there is probably enough new features to justify a discounted purchase. If you haven't played many 90s era JRPGs then you may not like this as that is essentially what it is. This is not a great port but it also should run on even low-end PCs. I didn't experience any bugs in my playthrough. It plays fine with an xbox360 controller.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is for those who don't know FF4 and are giving it a try here for the first time, as I did. I have loved some early 90's JRPGs in my day, and I figured that this was classic enough that it had to work for me. I get the joy of nostalgia for other games, but what's here without that prior experience? 25 hours in, and (I imagine) a stone's throw from the end, and I dislike it more and more. I'm going to list some irks, and if you find yourself coming to similar titles' defense, then please enjoy this one.

- Mid/Late 90's 3d. This is a personal preference, but as soon as the game started in full size, I was mortified. I liked a lot about FF7, but Cloud's hair wasn't one of them. There's a 2d version of this game which has the prequel/sequel AND nicely enhanced sprite graphics, which sounds preferable.
- For a supposedly story-driven game, this one has shallow characters and just about every cliche to ever appear in a JRPG. You play the world's most accomplished black knight who falls for every stupid villain trick imaginable. It's not as zany as it sounds.
- The combat. The repetitive combat. Once I learned that almost none of the combats outside of the boss fights require anything but pushing the attack button, I used auto-combat and just read while the game played itself. Again, I don't mind classic JRPGs as a genre, and this must have been compelling in its 2d version 27 years ago, but it has very little to offer someone in 2018 in my opinion. The boss battles are fun little puzzles and aren't too much of a marathon once you solve them. But even these don't matter - enemies you've just trounced will walk around just fine and meet their goals to spite you.
- The combat again: every 5-10 seconds while exploring, you get punished with another minute of battle. Why is my modern gaming PC waiting for this IOS game to come to life for a full 3 seconds between an attack and a counter attack? Not cool.
- It's unkind to people who haven't played other versions. I'm not talking about the difficulty, because outside of a few bosses, there isn't much difficulty to it. I'm talking about unkindness in the way that the devs built into this game an assumed prior knowledge of the story. For example, you'll be rewarded for giving augments to characters who leave, but how l am I supposed to know the future?
- The music. Just kidding! It's catchy, diverse, and inspiring, and I want to put most of it in a game that I like.

In sum: if you're not longingly nostalgic for a prior version, I don't think this IOS port has much to offer gamers on the PC platform. it's a light RPG that overstays its welcome with repetitive drudgery, unmemorable dialogue, and imagery you've seen 100 times.\t\t\t\t\t

8/10

Golly, sometimes I forget how long JRPGs take. But, oh, was it worth it.

My first Final Fantasy was Final Fantasy X, which I played not long after it came out, so that’s the benchmark for JRPGs for me. When I go back and play the older titles, I can’t help but compare them - see how much simpler they were, how lighter on story, how shallow the characters, and I just can’t connect as well.

Well, not for this game. I think I ended up liking this game more than Final Fantasy IX, which puts this game at my second favorite Final Fantasy of all time. (For reference, X and XII are tied for first)

Cecil is by far the best Final Fantasy leading man - starting off conflicted and unliked and troubled, but then reforming to a paladin with THE BEST outfit and color scheme, where he’s kind and reserved? Sign me tf up. I usually play FF games despite their protagonists, who I find only okay at best, but Cecil was a good draw for me. I really enjoyed the beginning, which was leaden with character building, interesting conflict, and a flip of the usual script. Going from Final Fantasy III, which had a much simpler story, to this game must have been an incredible leap at the time, and it still holds up today.

I really liked the 3D port - I’m a sucker for pixel textures and simple models like this, and they all look great! I was doubly surprised to see some scenes were even voice acted! That was a nice touch, and it helped update the game a lot.

Everything else is standard beloved JRPG fare - either you love it or you hate it. If you’re a fan of later games and want to begin exploring the earlier games, Final Fantasy IV is a perfect place to start.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAchievement Difficulty 10/10....

WHY?

well, RNG. With a drop rate "speculated" to be 0.04% for items needed to obtain some achievements such as "Adamante Armor" and "Onion Armor", your chances of getting these items are about as slim as you or anyone getting all of the weapons in a COD supply drop.

however the game requires your time and not your cash, you may get lucky or you may spend hours upon hours upon hours, fighting the same enemies over and over, only to find worthless treasures. But when you see those words written out spelling your item you have been seeking you will breathe a sigh of releif before realizing OMG now i have to do this again and again and yes you need i believe 6 different items.

Game Difficulty 6/10

I played on Hard from the beginning and it wasnt too bad, random encounters seem almost too abundant but it helps and if you want to 100% the game, the time it takes you to get the items i mentioned above, your characters should be in the low 80's in not maxed at lvl 99 by the time you finish the game.



Overall

this game requires two playthroughs, luckily the second carries over all your items and abilities you've earned. I recommend trying to obtain all you can in your first playthrough so on the second you have far less to worry about.

all in all i do recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy favorite RPG of all time but I'm referring to the SNES version not so much this remake. There are a LOT of things I don't like about this remake...for example, the difficulty is amped up quite a bit and not necessarily in a fair way. The bosses (the fiends of the elements) are insanely difficult compared to the SNES version. It's really based on LUCK if you win or not and that's stupid.

Some of the characters look dumb in this version too...Kain, for example, look ridiculous and Rydia looks better in the SNES version (at least her adult form does). Anyway, besides me nitpicking, this game is still great and I'd recommend it if you love old school RPG's or even just RPG's in general. The cinematics are a nice bonus and I like the voice overs. The soundtrack, of course, is beautiful.
