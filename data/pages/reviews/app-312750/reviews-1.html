\r\n\t\t\t\t\tObviously, Final Fantasy IV is a very good game. Some grindy parts haven't aged all that well (there are certain parts of the game that have huge difficulty spikes, requiring you to spend a couple hours just grinding for no other reason than to gain levels. Those parts are boring), but the rest still holds up fantastically. While earlier FF and Dragon Quest games were important, Final Fantasy IV was really the blueprint for the modern JRPG, and because of that it hasn't been all that dated more than 30 years later.

The question is - do you buy the 3D remake or the pixel remaster? There's no right answer. The remake has some great things: more cinematic cutscenes (the big ones even voice acted), and I would say a world that feels a bit more diverse. However, you can tell that the game was made for a small screen (it's a port of the mobile version, which itself was a port of the DS version). There are certain things like the camera feels REALLY zoomed in a lot of the time, making it hard to see where you're going without the map overlay, which takes up the whole screen. If they simply implemented a minimap for this version of the game, that would have been great.

Also the music is better in the pixel remaster.

All in all, recommended buy. Whether you buy this one or the pixel remaster depends on what kind of art style you want and how much you value more cinematic cutscenes. Either way, it's a great game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to be upfront and say what I think a lot of other people have said: this is not the best way to play Final Fantasy IV nor is it a great way to play if you've never played Final Fantasy or even a traditional JRPG before. The Complete Collection version for the PSP or the Pixel Remaster are better versions for a more authentic experience to the original with options for making the game smoother if you don't have much time to drop on an RPG like this. This is a port of the DS version which was definitely made for people that had already played the original cause it remixes the game in a lot of different ways.

Final Fantasy IV 3D does two things quite exceptionally. The presentation is truly excellent, using the best 3D that the DS could manage, and it's a huge step up from Final Fantasy III 3D. The character and enemy models are all highly detailed and more realistically proportioned than you'd expect from this series. I've seen comments about the framerate being low during battle, but this is related to the ATB system and the battle speed and will only feel sluggish until you have three party members. The world map is done in a somewhat cel-shaded look which is easy on the eyes and dungeons look pretty good with far less repetitive layouts and textures compared to FFIII 3D.

The real star when it comes to presentation are FFIV 3D's fully animated and voice acted cutscenes which are shockingly really good. The cinematography makes use of dramatic camera angles and closeups in a way you'd expected from Square's big budget new releases, and the voice cast do a fantastic job of portraying each character, no matter how small, even in English. I dedicate an entire paragraph to this topic because there are a lot of cutscenes and they change up the feel of the game considerably. More dramatic weight is given to events large and small for better and for worse. While the main story excels with the heightened presentation, the lack of characterization is unfortunately spotlighted and you'll notice far more that most of your party have little to do in the back end of the narrative. In the original this was because you could switch out members of your party in the second half and they never really designed endings to their storylines as a result. The remake removes this feature to facilitate the story, however, so it becomes even more noticeable. Whether or not this bothers you is up to personal preference and/or nostalgia, but I think the main story itself is strong enough to hold up the flaws just as it was in the original version of the game.

The gameplay has been switched up considerably. The name of the game in this rendition is extreme rebalancing. This version is considerably harder than any of the other versions, especially early on before you have a lot of options. However, I never once felt like the game was unfair outside of a couple of bossfights because of the ways the gameplay has been enhanced. In most Final Fantasy games, stuff like instant death and status effect spells are completely useless because their accuracy is terrible and most enemies and all bosses are typically immune. FFIV 3D actually makes all of these abilities useful against most enemies and bosses, and because the enemies are so much more powerful you'll be using them often to gain an advantage in battle. In many early Final Fantasies, the solution to overwhelming difficulty was grinding, and you can still do that here if your really want to, however I never had to grind once over my entire playthrough. I was always powerful enough to proceed and I always had enough money just by playing the game and trying to do all of the sidequests. The game is extremely well-balanced so long as you actually make good use of your entire arsenal.

One area I do think the game fumbles the ball is the Augment System. This is a feature they added for the remake, and it's a good system that gives you a lot of tactical options for how you build your party throughout the game. However, there are a few things about it that really bring it down. The first is that there are several party members that you come across and will permanently leave later on, and you can give these party members augments. In fact, in order to get certain augments, you have to give every single one of them at least two augments which is impossible in a single playthrough. Furthermore, the game does a terrible job indicating where to find these things. Some of them make sense like returning to areas where a major event took place, but some of them are just randomly in dungeons that you've already visited even if you have no reason to return. Between that and having to give them to temporary party members, I would recommend having a guide open the entire time so you can snap them all up even if it's a first playthrough. It sucks because you're pretty much spoiling the entire plot this way, but there are so many useful abilities you can get out of them that will help ease the difficulty of the endgame without mitigating it that it's not worth passing them up. Not only that but this system was foolishly designed with Newgame+ in mind when FFIV has pretty much no replay value because it has one set story, and I'll say right now that the augments don't provide a significant incentive to play it again.

All in all, I think this is a great game and a great rendition of a hallowed classic. While it's not the best version of the game, it definitely is my preferred version because of the updates and improvements to the gameplay of the series and the heavy emphasis placed on story. While I wouldn't recommend this to newcomers of the series or first-time players, I do think it is worth a playthrough to those who have played Final Fantasy IV before and are looking for a different kind of experience from it.\t\t\t\t\t
