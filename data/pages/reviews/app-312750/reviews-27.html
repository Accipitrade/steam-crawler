Attention! Make sure you really enjoy the active time battle system in this game.
Watch the gameplay on YouTube. Because the way the combat system is implemented here may not please everyone. If you don't like it, then don't waste your time and money.
For the first 15 hours it seemed to me that I would get used to the new combat system. Since in FF3 the turn-based combat system was very well implemented. And in FF4 I was haunted all the time by the feeling that I needed to think and act faster. And in FF4 can be up to 5 characters, so sometimes you don't have time to do the actions for all of them in time. At the end of the game, I just tried to run away from every fight with enemies. By the way, if you do not run away from all minor enemies during the last 5 hours, then these 5 hours will turn into 10 hours. Because for some reason they shoved a bunch of enemies with a lot of lives into the end, which they have to beat for a long time. And it's not exciting at all.
About the plot. It is really much more complicated and interesting than in FF3. But, the game loves to take characters from you during the plot, and then sometimes returns them. It pisses you off a little. I liked most of the boss fights. But some bosses were impossible to complete without guides. :)
And don't forget! In this game you cannot change professions, they are already pre-selected by everyone. This is due to the plot, but nonetheless.
Have a good day.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinal Fantasy IV was one of my favorite games from the SNES. My brother and I would stay up all night playing this to try and beat it before we had to return it to Blockbuster Video. I have such fond memories that I could have easily just given this game an automatic thumbs up.

I did buy this game for my DS when it first came out. It was like seeing a childhood friend after a long absence. While the story itself isn't different, they updated the script (thank god) and made it a little less cheesy. The graphics are a sort of hybrid anime-chibi and 3D versions of the original art. The voice acting is very good, and the fully rendered cinematics are amazing. It made me fall in love with Final Fantasy IV all over again.

A lot of people aren't fans of the original few Final Fantasies because they can be tough or confusing. If that is how you feel, then give this game a try. A lot of the tropes from future games started here, and the story is just as epic as it was back in 1992. Cecil's adventure is one of the best "Hero's Journey" stories you'll ever come across, and there are many awesome twists and turns that don't feel forced or obvious (well, sometimes... I'm looking at you, Kain...)\t\t\t\t\t
