\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've beaten this game on SNES, GBA, 3DS, and PSP so I feel qualified to write this review despite not having completely finished the single player mode on PC. While this game isn't perfect (it's not even the best port of FFIV), it's a very competent port and a great way to experience the game if you're not interested in emulation or buying a PSP.

The game's biggest flaw is front and center as soon as you start it up: It's ugly. I was never a fan of the paper doll art style even back when it launched on 3ds and it looks even worse blown up on modern PC displays. These graphical shortcomings are all the more glaring when you consider how bad it looks compared to its PSP counterpart.

The gameplay feels almost identical to the original SNES version of the game, though it does seem a bit more difficult at times. This is especially apparent on boss fights, which often took me multiple attempts to clear when playing on Hard. It also adds an "augment" system to the game where you can find and teach various abilities to your party member.

While the augment system is a nice addition that allows for a little customization of characters (something that was completely lacking in the SNES version), the implementation leaves a lot to be desired. The game gives you no hints as to where or when they appear and you run the risk of missing them if you don't know. It's the sort of design choice that almost forces the player to use a guide.

Is this game the best version of Final Fantasy IV? No. That distinction still goes to the PSP version. This game is, however, a very competent port of the 3ds version of FFIV and absolutely worth playing for those who don't want to bother with emulation or those who want the challenge of the added Hard mode.\t\t\t\t\t
