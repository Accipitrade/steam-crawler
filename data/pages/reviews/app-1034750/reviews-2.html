\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a nice little game you can play trough in under 4 hours. I really love the take on it. its very different from other detective games. you have at some points different choice options that will shape the story to an extend.

PROS:

- it has simple graphics and i think it is better because of it.
- you kinda see the stories from other people from a third person view and i really like that. you are not directly involved in them but rather see them unfold before your eyes.
- there is something really appealing about spying on other people in general. learning about them and their releationships with each other.
- the voiceacting is really good.
- the music is good.


CONS:

- sometimes the characters feel stiff but this is nothing really annoying. if you consider the overall graphics then its something you can easy forgive. maybe it ads even more to the charms overall.

- there is not really much to say else. i wished that it was a bit longer with a little bigger plaza and more people but it is just fine as it is now. i really like it overall and i like the idea of spying on people from the balcony with a partner that does the footwork. i hope there would be more games that explore this take in the future. like some other city and different crimes. it could be a really new and interesting take on the detective genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a pretty neat detective game in which a wheelchair-bound retired cop assists an anarchist journalist in solving the murder of her informant. Gameplay consists of spying on people from a balcony through a pair of binoculars or a camera, taking photos, and then posting your drawings and photographs on a board to aid in your deductions. Graphics are rather simple, with characters being all sorts of anthropomorphic animals for some reason. Especially praiseworthy is the quality of voice acting and incidental music. What didn't impress me, however, were the shoehorned LGBTQ elements that bring absolutely nothing to the story and they are so shallow as to be completely unnecessary. And for this reason alone this is a thumbs down from me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTFC has an interesting plot, but the gameplay and actual meat of the story was a bit lackluster to me. as previous reviews mentioned, there is a lot of potential that could've been dived into for this game, and I do agree that it felt disappointing to have the main character say he didn't remember what he did, etc etc. but all the same, I did feel like the way the topic was introduced was pretty good. my other disappointment with this game is that it's less actually solving the mystery and more following along. yes, there is information presented that isn't truly relevant to what happened, but there's no real consequence to getting your theories wrong. overall I enjoyed it, it was an interesting story to follow along to for a few hours, just think the plot could have been enhanced more with more details & connections.\t\t\t\t\t

Considering it only took me 3 hours to finish and it still felt like a bit of a drag, it seems half bakes at best. Shame, was excited for it too. At least it's somewhat pretty when the characters aren't turning by sliding or moving through walls.

The narrative was ok. But not good enough to carry the games weight (or so to say the rest of its flaws) on its own. I wish they would've focused on their strengths or developed the further on to iron out the very blatant issues with it. Currently the games issues are truly to its detriment. Talking about police brutality as the police characters play a stock stomping animation and the other character playing a stock prone animation just throws out any and all weight we might feel to a funny occurrence instead. Considering you are bound to a single position with a camera or binoculars, you could've just put both those characters behind a wall we can't see through and play a fall animation on the victim to leave the rest to our imagination instead of doing something so clumsy.

The game does take it self super seriously and I would say with these flaws this would make it a great comedic experience but rest of it is so slow and tedious the main mood stays as boredom instead. I say don't bother.\t\t\t\t\t

Cons:

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat I love about Mi'pu'mi Games is that the time and place their games take place is very important to the story. It's not just set dressing. This one takes places as Spain is making the transition to democracy, which not everyone is happy about. As a retired police officer, you team up with a journalist to solve a murder that took place on your street. It's like a combination of Rear Window, in that you're observing and photographing, as opposed to doing the investigating yourself. Your partner goes and searches for clues and talks to people while you direct and look out for her. Overall, I thought it was quite good. Nice writing and voice acting, great pacing. At around 3 hours it was the perfect length. The story was very engaging, and though there wasn't a ton to the gameplay it was satisfying in a voyeuristic sort of way.\t\t\t\t\t
