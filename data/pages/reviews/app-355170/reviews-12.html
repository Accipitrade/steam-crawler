\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSam & Max is one of the strangest, zaniest, and funniest LucasArts games out there, and I think it's fantastic because of it, never being able to really expect what happens next and always keeping me guessing as to what will happen next. While it isn't one of the most complex LucasArts adventure game stories out there, it is definitely one of the more memorable, with the interactions between Sam and Max in particular being particularly funny and memorable standouts.

That said, there can be some confusing puzzles involved that may take some time to get the solution, and while the new interaction UI certainly helps give more space to the game's visuals, it does take some time getting used to, especially when one is more familiar with the older LucasArts games. Additionally, while the story itself is indeed funny and strange, it definitely isn't the sort of game for anyone wanting to look for anything deep or thought-provoking.

These nitpicks are minuscule compared to everything Sam & Max has to offer, however. It's one of the funniest, unpredictable, absurdist, and memorable LucasArts games out there, and a game for anyone just looking to have a fun time.\t\t\t\t\t
