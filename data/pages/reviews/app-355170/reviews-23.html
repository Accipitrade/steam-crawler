\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSam & Max hit the road.
What hasn't been said about this game already?
It is, in my opinion, one of the best games of its genre.
I played it as a young'un, and now I'm playing it as a slighty older'un, and it's still an absolute joy to play.

The visuals, while very low resolution compared to nowadays, still hold charm, and the low resolution doesn't make stuff unrecognizable.
On the contrary, the very solid artstyle still makes every character as expressive now as they were back in the day.
The music, and this might be down to personal taste, I still enjoy greatly as well. The midi tunes are wonky, wacky, and fitting all throughout.
What has aged the least, at least in terms of how funny it is, is the dialogue. Sam & Max remains one of the rare games that actually produces laugh-out-loud moments, and the voice acting is just hilarious.

On the gameplay side, it's an old lucasarts adventure. Talk to people in wacky dialogue trees, find and combine items in bonkers ways to solve puzzles, and laugh your ass off in the process.
Interacting with the environment is pretty simple for an older point n click, so there's not much of an entry barrier.
So yeah, in closing:
If you like point 'n click adventure games, get it, it's one of the greats.
If you played the newer Sam & Max episodes, do yourself a favor and check out this old one, it hasn't lost its charm over time.
If you never actually played any point 'n click adventure games and wanna start with an early but already comfortable game: this one's for you.

Now if you'll excuse me, I have to go ride the Cone of Tragedy.
