\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSam & Max Hit the Road is a classic point-and-click adventure game from Lucas Arts. This adventure game uses traditional mechanics set in the world of Steve Purcell's Sam & Max world.

Sam & Max Hit the Road came about during the golden years of Lucas Arts' video games with titles like Secret of Monkey Island and Day of the Tentacle. Sam & Max fits into these great games with its own quality experience filled with some challenging puzzles and quirky humor.

Mechanically, Sam & Max operates like most adventure game - the player rubs various interpretations of keys on various interpretations of doors. Sam & Max stands as a unique entry due to its heavy reliance on humor and absurdity. As with the Sam & Max comics, much of the comedy centers on the violent banter of the two main characters, Max's heavy sarcasm and sociopath-like approach to interactions, and utterly absurd story. Setting the characters in a video game adds fourth wall breaks.

Those familiar with Sam & Max from the comics or other video games will find Sam & Max Hit the Road a very worthwhile play. Those new to Sam & Max who enjoy adventure games with a tone of absurdity will get a kick out of it. It's fun, challenging, and worth quite a few laughs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t- Excellent and incredibly sophisticated writing coupled with a very simple yet engaging story

- Voice acting is not too bad for the time, but I warmed up to the Telltale Games voices that came later down the line

- Clever. elaborate, mind boggling puzzles, that require you to think very outside the box. You need to put yourself in the mindset that you're in a wacky world and need to embrace it as such. Once you do, the puzzles become much clearer and easier to grasp.

- Fluid and wonderful sprite animations that truly are something else for the time, LucasArts did a crazy good job in that aspect.

- It's not the longest game out there, it's more of a short and sweet, compacted story. However the game is not expensive in the slightest.

The debut of The Freelance Police's video games, and not the worst one to start with. It's a short one and done story with no bearing on the future Telltale Game's plots. However you could go straight ahead and hop on the Telltale ones, which are quite close to this one in design and charm.

But don't misunderstand, this is still a grand experience that will make you think, laugh, and question your poor logic deduction skills. But man is it worth it to play through.\t\t\t\t\t
