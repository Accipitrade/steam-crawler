\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSam & Max Hit the Road is a classic from the LucasArts point-and-click days. The art direction is great and fits really well for the wacky world of Sam and Max, and still looks good even today. Everything is really well animated, nothing looks too stiff and it utilises slapstick excellently to get some laughs out of the player. Visually the game is brilliant and timeless, which is always nice from old games. The OST is really catchy, I don't think it's anything outstanding but the tunes sprinkled throughout the game are good enough for you to remember but at the same time not distracting from the gameplay.

I think what sets this game apart from many other point-and-clicks is the dialogue, this game's dialogue is absolutely great. Conversations flow so naturally and there's so many jokes in between, it's actually incredible. There's even dialogue for when you keep trying to pick up an object that can't be picked up, where Sam gets gradually more annoyed until he gives up on the player, starts whining like a dog (well, I mean he IS a dog so...), and Max scolds you for it, telling you that if he didn't find Sam's sobbing funny, he'd "come out there and rip your limbs off". Stuff like that is present throughout the whole game, and gives the game loads of charm and memorability. IMO this is the main selling point of the game and makes it totally worth the purchase.

The gameplay is about what you'd expect from a point-and-click from LucasArts, some pretty wacky solutions to the puzzles. You're given a lot of items and a lot of locations to travel to the more you progress. Although this sounds like a good thing in theory, leaving the player experimenting with a bunch of different tools to creatively solve puzzles, in practice what tends to happen is you spam a bunch of different objects together only for Sam to keep saying "I can't use these things together" every single time, forcing you to resort to the game's hint book only to find out you had to do some other thing in a completely different location that you would've never thought of. I do appreciate the puzzles, and while a lot of them do make sense, I guess, there's just so many different things to pick up and so many different locations throughout the game that it just becomes a complete ball-ache after a while. Your inventory just gets filled with useless objects that do nothing until the later sections, and you're just overloaded with locations where you just keep back-and-forthing until you either miraculously figure out what to do or resort to the hint book. Seriously why the hell are there three Snuckey's? You only need to go to ONE in order to progress through the game! The gameplay is very of-its-time and in my opinion was greatly improved in the duo's later Telltale episodic adventures.

This is a great point-and-click and I'd recommend giving it a go if you like those games, since it's only Â£4.79 which is a reasonable price for the content.\t\t\t\t\t
