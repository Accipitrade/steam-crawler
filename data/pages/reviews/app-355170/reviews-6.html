\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou’ve gotta love these charming ol’ LucasArts point-n-click adventure games, especially when they feature two lovable characters and a whole lotta weirdness.

That said, it's kind of hard to casually recommend this one since a decent amount of the puzzles take a sizable leap in logic and, boy, does walking around take its sweet ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ time. Having access to so many locations on the map at once might’ve not been the right fit for the game. Keeping track of what corners might not have been fully inspected is pretty disorienting. But hey, I’d expect nothing less from a point-n-click game that was released in 1993.

I’d reckon that if you’ve already made it through a season or two of Telltale’s rendition of Sam & Max, Hit the Road gains a lot more appeal, which makes it easier to put up with its shenanigans. From all the little contextual animations to the dead-catchy music plus the cheeky (and slightly aged) dialogue, you can tell that so much heart and humor has been stuffed into this one. Don’t overlook it, but understand that this nearly three-decade-old adventure game tastes better after you’ve had a good taste of the ‘modern’ main dish.\t\t\t\t\t
