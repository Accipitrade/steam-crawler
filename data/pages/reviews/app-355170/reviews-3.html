\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're considering playing Sam and Max Hit the Road, there are a few things you should know before diving in. Firstly, the user interface has not aged well. The game relies on a lot of pixel hunting and obscure area transitions, which can be frustrating at times. Additionally, some UI interactions can be unexpected, like controlling the binoculars. Skipping phrases can sometimes skip too much, missing out on important hints. The lack of running on double-click can also be frustrating, as it often feels like you're wasting time waiting for walking animations to complete.

That being said, the game does have some redeeming qualities. The story and atmosphere were decent, although the style of humor may not be for everyone. Personally, I found it to be a bit too try-hard and only laughed once during the entire game.

As an enjoyer of point-and-click adventure games, I somehow missed Sam and Max Hit the Road. While I wouldn't necessarily recommend it in 2023, I do think that it could benefit from a modern do-over with item highlighting, running, and more hints, resulting in a much better game than the origin. Overall, I felt like I wasted my time playing the game, and only did so to be able to say that I had played it.\t\t\t\t\t
