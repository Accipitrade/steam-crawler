\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Sam & Max Hit the Road" is another fantastic point and click adventure game from LucasArts - Double Fine published right in the golden era of the genre. It is easily one of the most hilarious games ever made with an iconic protagonist duo, witty dialogues, gorgeous surreal world, crazy npc characters and nice music. Its puzzles are clever and well built too. Problematic control mechanics and a lot of pixel hunting will annoy you and will come close to ruin all the joy. Nevertheless, it is a must play first game of a legendary series.


Manometer: 8/10


The good
- Fantastic, cartoonish, pixelated graphics.
- Gorgeous, big, surreal world full of paradoxes. There are a lot of places to visit.
- Incredible, weird sense of humor full or irony. Well written dialogues and witty jokes. One of the most hilarious games ever, although it is not for everyone's taste.
- A good variety of clever, well built puzzles. It can be chaotic sometimes but if you are careful it gives you enough clues to solve every puzzles.
- Crazy, unique characters. Sam and Max are an iconic duo, that you will both love and hate.
- Cool, detailed animations, although I would like some more fps.
- Great sound design with a lot of funny sounds and excellent, convincing voice acting performances for all the characters.
- Nice music. Each place has its own unique music track.
- They have added some mini game activities (carbomb game, dress-up book etc.).
- It is cheap for a classic.

The bad
- Control mechanics are problematic. Serial mouse commands will force you to cycle through all of them until you find the desired one. Sam and Max walk boringly slow and there is no double click for run or instantly jump to the next screen. You cannot skip dialogues even if you have seen them before.
- A lot of pixel hunting. Hovering over interactive objects will not accurately change the shape of the cursor. You will miss some objects or some available paths.
- Max is used too many times in various situations and often in a weird way. This is too convenient and sometimes not creative.
- Sam keeps giving the same generic answers when something doesn't work. "I can't pick that up" or "I can't use these things together".\t\t\t\t\t
