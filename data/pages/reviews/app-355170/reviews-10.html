\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSamuel and Maxwell earned a special place in my heart from the very moment I remembered the time my cousin had to stop playing one of the Telltale games because Max said "♥♥♥♥♥♥♥" in front of their religious family. In remembering that, I finally decided to actually give one of the games a whirl, and settled on the first of the series; I originally played it on an emulator, but decided to buy the actual game on Steam to support the developers once I had a device that could handle it.

"Sam & Max: Hit the Road" is definitely a unique title, in that behind the facade of colorful and family-friendly, it has a genuinely morbid sense of humor geared more towards older audiences, which makes me infinitely glad that I found the series later on in life. Its actual gameplay has a penchant for being incredibly confusing, as 90's point and click adventure games are wont to have, so I'd suggest using a guide... after you've already tried everything, because for every attempt at something you make, there's ample opportunity for a hilarious one-liner that you otherwise would have missed.

Gameplay-wise, as I stated, it can be a bit much, especially if you're not usually a point and click kind of person. The model of the emulator is also a bit wonky and isn't explained much, but either reading a guide or just pressing buttons until you figure out what works definitely helps. But completely disregarding that, the humor and wit put into the writing alone will serve as more than enough reason to invest in such a genuinely timeless game. Happy Bigfoot hunting!!\t\t\t\t\t
