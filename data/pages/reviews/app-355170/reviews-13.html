\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is with a heavy heart that I conclude that I didn't like the game enough to recommend it. I love adventure games, and thought I would surely love it, and while it's not terrible, it has several flaws that make it less than ideal. This is the first time I play a point and click adventure and didn't love it at least at its best parts.
1. the mouse pointer is not exact enough. I tried clicking on things just for the game to register me clicking on the thing next to it more times than I can count. To make matters worse, Max actually is clickable, and frequently will place himself in the way of other clickable objects. in the restaurant atop the world's largest ball of Twine , this meant that I couldn't solve the puzzle, because the game thought I clicked on Max instead of The telescope .
2. too many of the puzzles require moon logic or are timing based. Nothing sucks quite as much as having to redo a section because you were a little bit slow or miss-clicked somewhat. not to mention concepts used for a single puzzle, never to be seen again.
3. It is often difficult to tell the difference between clickable objects, and the background. Compare this to the first monkey island games, where everything you could interact with clearly stood out. Now to be fair, the action symbol changes when there is something you can interact with, but at times, due to point one, with Max being in the way.

What are the positives?
A pretty good soundtrack to listen to.
Many game play options, including picking between subtitles, audio, or both, black and white mode, and higher speeds in case you want to speed through parts.
The writing is pretty good, with lots of nods to earlier games, and popular culture. I even laughed out loud a couple of times.
It's a pretty decent game, but due to the issues I wrote about, it feels like a chore at times. I rarely felt clever after figuring a puzzle out, but rather annoyed.
If you really love adventure games, sure, give it a try, maybe with these points in mind you'll have a better time than I did;
But I'd suggest watching a play through instead. See the fun parts, but skip the frustration.\t\t\t\t\t
