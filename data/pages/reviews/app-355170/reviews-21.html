\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was a surprise find for me.

I was looking for a different sort of game, one that hadn't been over-recommended by Steam's algorithm-- and by almost impossible chance I stumbled on this. Not only did I get the sense that this was a very old game, but by reading its initial release was back in 1993-- and by staring at its graphics-- I immediately understood.

This game is a classic. (Unquestionably).

And Sam & Max are a familiar pair. I've heard of them but have never formally introduced myself to their video game counterparts.

My utter surprise at not only the humor, but the once again somewhat ridiculous puzzle-solving (because it's Lucasfilm, as well as the 90s) brought back an old nostalgia that I'm engaging with even as I write this review.

Do I recommend it? Yes.
Do I recommend it full-price? Eh, maybe if a buck or two was knocked off, that would have sated me a bit better.
Good animations? Yes. Classic.
Good humor? A wonderfully specific type of humor. A little gritty and very self-aware. Ladies and gentlemen-- this was the original Deadpool and Cable, but for kids (Sam & Max comics came out in 1987, Deadpool made his first appearance in 1991). So if spoken interactive lines like, "Vertical silverware storage" and conversations such as, "I really respect Flint's business acumen"/"Please, Sam, don't use the word 'acumen' again" don't tickle your fancy-- you're out of luck.

I loved this game's style more than I loved the somewhat frustrating gameplay. I even did my best not to consult a guide and I actually made some decent progress once I got back into the mindset of the 1990s and their zany, but beautiful meticulosity.

It's something that was more refreshing than I hoped.

P.S.

I'm really glad I have such bumbling fingers whenever I type in the search bar-- so game on into the vast depths of childhood nostalgia.

I double-dog-and-rabbit dare you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA classic that still holds up. It worked flawlessly, which is more than I can say for the first time I played it over 20 years ago when I ha a crappy soundcard that, for some reason, could only play the voices in the game or the music, but not both. If you can get beyond the pixelated graphics, this is a funny game to play though I do admit that I had a few areas that were frustrating for me. Either because I couldn't see certain items that I was supposed to pick up or I couldn't see "doorways" to get me into other areas. One sequence (the VR securiy system) was particularly frustrating because I didn't know exactly where to click even though I knew what I was supposed to do. I had to do that squence several times before I found a walkthrough that was specific enouigh to tell me where I had to click. Some of the puzzles have pretty obtuse solutions, even if the graphics were more clear, but still, if you love the characters of Sam and Max and their surreal humor then you should play this classic. Also, there are plenty of walkthroughs out there that can help you get by the frustrating parts.\t\t\t\t\t

That being said for those who don't have much experience with old point and clicks, I'd highly recomend finding a hintbook, getting a friend or two that maybe know the game to help you along or even just finding a straight up walkthrough because the puzzles in this game much like many of the puzzles of old adventure games are unituitive and often more complex than need be. This game is a flawed masterpice and if anyone is looking for a fun point and click, I think this is the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t
