\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame isn't as terrible as some of the reviews are making it out to be. Yes, it does have some issues and yes they can be fixed with an update. However, for $35 its not terrible and has a good amount of content to play. At its current state (forgiving the bugs) I'd give it a 7/10 if I had to rate it.

PROS:
Good number of tracks to play
Good graphics (despite a few texture bugs I noticed)
Soundtrack is okay
Tutorial feels good and informative for new players
The "hub" you spawn at after each race is a nice touch that makes the career cool
Bikes aren't terrible in my experience but can be problematic for some

CONS:
UTVs and ATVs feel like you're riding on solid ice, very hard to control sometimes
Some of the physics are a little buggy across all three vehicle types
Bikes feel a little heavy and hard to control
Bikes can be hard to control if not using pro physics setting
Paywall for the licensed manufactures

BUGS I NOTICED (as of release date):
Music suddenly gets louder when racing
Textures are low quality and take a bit to load on certain clothing items
Certain rider animations are clunky (specifically in the starting gates)
Randomly would hit a weird spot in a rut and would cause my bike or ATV to freak out

If you really wanted to try it I'd say go for it but waiting for a few patches wouldn't hurt anything either.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played damn near every MX game, and my honest opinion is that this game feels like it wasn't finished yet. The tracks are fun and the game plays decent enough. However, you could thumbs up this review if you agree. The bikes feel like bouncy balls. there are times when you land perfectly and the game feels good, but more times than not, I feel like I land my bike and it bounces up in the air like a golf ball on the pavement. On top of that, turning is pure chaos, you take the inside berm and can fly over the top of it and there is no penalty, you will still carry enough speed to stay ahead of your opponents. I appreciate the arcade-like gameplay as opposed to the Monster energy game I've been playing recently, but come on. a little realism on the tracks is still wanted in an arcade-style racing game. If you are looking for a good ♥♥♥♥ around game to play with the boys, I think you'll enjoy it enough because of the large tracks with giant jumps to hit. But if you are buying this in hopes of a solid dirtbike racing experience, you won't get that with this game. If simulation style racing is what you are after, I recommend Monster Energy Supercross or MXGP ( but even they arent perfect). Overall, I think the game needs work and I'm not sure if we will ever get updates to correct that. I paid $35 for the game ( 4th of July sale ) so I will be keeping it, but if you are reading this and the price is $59.99 I would reconsider your purchase.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter reading the rest of the reviews I've come to the conclusion that all of these people are just trash gamers who want an easy experience.

First let me get my only gripe out of the way. The sound, and particularly the mixing with the music, needs a patch. Engine sounds are synthetic but nothing different from beloved titles like Forza Horizon 4. Motoheads are sound ♥♥♥♥♥♥ and there's no getting around it. Luckily given All Out received a sound fixing patch back in the day, I expect the same to occur here.

As far as the physics goes, I think this expands excellently on the physics of All Out, and fixes a bunch of issues by slowing things down. The heaviness of the bike feels real, and the sliding allows easy scrubs off jumps.
What I gather from the other reviewers is they haven't bothered tuning their bikes at all, because whips can be sped up significantly by tuning your chassis, and sliding can be significantly reduced by tuning tire traction. It's as simple as that!
People don't actually play the game before giving a review eh? Coming from a person who's held many top5/top10 times on the All Out leaderboards, I can tell these other reviewers just don't want to take the time to learn how to enjoy the game and be quick. You cannot just huck your sticks left and right and expect to be successful. It takes time to learn how to both control your slide and whip aesthetically. The point of a free whip is that it's not supposed to be easy. I personally wish the hardest difficulty was a little harder as a good tune can easily outpace the best AI. All Out received an AI upgrade midway through it's life cycle and I expect the same to happen for this game.

The rest of this games strengths are as follows:
- Vast open world scale with excellent detail
- Excellent outdoor track design
- Simple yet effect progression through story mode missions
- Fantastic free whip physics unlike any other moto game I've played
- Great sense of speed in every vehicle
- Killer soundtrack (Finally a game soundtrack with a little bit of testosterone)
- Engaging supercross stages that are scaled very well.
- Decent customization with real brands
- Actually good voiceovers for story characters
- New and fresh gamemodes (Trials engages the same part of my brain that Dirt Rally 2.0 does and I love that)
- A preload mechanic that takes skill to master + a not-so-OP clutch boost mechanic
- Terrain deformation that won't ruin a track in just 2 laps.
- Graphics that hold up to modern day titles (cannot speak for the console editions or your brother's shoddy PC)
- Bangin' price (especially on sale)

At the end of it all, every mx vs atv gets review bombed because there's a cult that just wants rainbow to remaster mx vs atv reflex. Reflex was a great game and I enjoyed it, for the record. But they're not going to do that. Ever. I'll happily play Reflex modded for the rest of my life.

For the rest of you that want a new challenge to conquer, while scaling the massive open world stages, this is the game for you! For the rest who want a casual experience I would recommend Monster Energy Supercross. They make one every year, it's basically a subscription! LOL
