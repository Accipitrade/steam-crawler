\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite the reviews I have read and watched, I found this game to actually be quite good. The physics feel great, the tracks are the best in the series, the free-ride areas are massive and fun to explore, the first-person camera is PERFECT (I cannot go back to third person after trying it), and the tuning system is much less of a mess than it was in the past. Additionally, the career is quite substantial and enjoyable to play through.

The only really, really glaring flaw this game has is its absolutely abysmal AI racers. I am not amazing at MX games, just somewhat decent, but on the hardest difficulty, "G.O.A.T", I finished every race in the career (yes, even the 450 races) on a 125 bike just to handicap myself and still placed 1st on my first try on almost every race (there were only 2-3 exceptions throughout the entire MX career). On your home track, the AI literally all ride up to the same point and just stop moving completely by the second lap, almost every time. They don't even try to get unstuck - they just sit there, parked.

Overall, it's a fun game in its current state. I've played every game in the series apart from Supercross/Encore, and I believe this to be the second best (with the first being Reflex by a large margin). I did not play the game before v1.19, so I was not around to experience the shoddy launch physics and crashes, but as of now the game is quite worthwhile (especially on a $20 sale).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGraphics are good but I do not like the game play. There is not many settings that allow you to tweak the game play to your liking. The tutorials at the beginning can be quite annoying, they force you to go through all of them, simple or not. It is also unfortunate that when you first start out, you are stuck with one and only one option for a vehicle, and it is the 125cc dirt bike. I would like to have the chance to at least free roam and try the ATV or UTV and be able to choose a career based on which I prefer.

The handling characteristics take some getting used to, but the overall physics seem okay.

As others have mentioned, there are WAY TOO MANY ADD ONS and not enough included in the game. It reminds me of an EA game and their micro transactions.

The trails in free roam at the farm are not trails, they are generally flat and wide paths.

The sounds need a lot of work. If you don't believe me, look up a video and hear for yourself.

I did not try the multiplayer so I can not speak on that.

The AI also needs a lot of work. Sometimes they will stay ahead of you the entire time, or at least the person in first, no matter the difficulty or how hard you try. Then, towards the end of the last lap, their engine must blow because it's like someone locked up their brakes and you go flying by. It seems as this is an attempt to make it a challenge until the very end but it is very noticeable and immersion breaking, nothing about their behavior is realistic and actually worse than many arcade racing games I have played.\t\t\t\t\t
