1. The audio/music experience is annoying. I felt like I couldn't get the music loud enough without the engine sounds overpowering it during racing, even with the music at 100% and the engine volume at 10%. Then, randomly during racing, the music will get so loud it's unbearable. Furthermore, the inability to choose what songs actually play is a feature I feel like should have been a no-brainer. I can think of MX games from the PS2 era that had that feature and it was great. I don't like some of the music in this game and I have zero control over it. Kinda sucks.

2. When I started some events, I forcefully got put on the starting bike, even though I had a tuned-up and selected Kawasaki. Why would I purchase a separate bike and upgrade it just to get stuck with the starting one? I tried to figure out why I kept getting put on the starting bike even when I clearly had the Kawasaki selected when I started the event, but could not seem to find any logical indicators as to why. I thought maybe some events were locked to a certain CC, but I couldn't find the CC listed anywhere, so it didn't seem like that. It's frustrating when you are being passed by AI riders in straights because their top speed is faster than yours and you can't do anything about it, especially when you have another bike that is better but the game won't let you use it. Maybe there is some logical reason behind it, but even if there is, it should be stated somewhere.

3. I was feeling experimental and switched the transmission setting from automatic to manual, just to see what it was like and maybe what the game demanded in terms of gear shifting. I couldn't really find any significant difference between the two. The rider still shifted up and down gears automatically in both modes. I didn't seem to have any extra controls (I tried to check the controls/keybindings menu to see if there were any controls listed, and then realized this menu doesn't exist). I didn't see any kind of tutorial or mention of the manual transmission mode either. So, to my knowledge, there was no difference between automatic and manual, other than I could hear the engine revving on the upshifts a little more, but I didn't have to really do anything. Pressing the clutch didn't seem to have any effect other than throttling myself, so there was no point.

4. Lastly, the riding mechanics are pretty smooth feeling compared to some older games, but it also feels impossible to really hit anything correctly. I started out with the dual-stick handling and got decent at it, but it's hard to not get sideways hitting jumps and I end up losing all my speed in a lot of turns because I end up over-leaning and it spins the rider around. I couldn't preload, seatbounce, or scrub consistently either. Could I probably get better at them over more playtime? probably, but I feel like it's a mish-mash of too many inconsistent suspension mechanics and it's ridiculously hard to have the speed and power at any given moment to actually jump and land anything properly. Sorta like others have said, the bike is too heavy considering how much speed you can realistically achieve to be able to jump and keep your speed most of the time. I switched to the single-stick handling mode to see if it was any better, and it felt about the same, I was just doing less.

I could have probably tolerated the riding experience and just tried to upgrade my bike far enough to out-stat the other riders, but the music and bike issues were the nail in the coffin for me.\t\t\t\t\t
