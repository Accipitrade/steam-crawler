\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to make an early review, unbiased and based on my 8 hrs of game play. First thing I'll say is yes, it has it's faults just like many other releases. All will be fixed in the weeks to come imo quite effortlessly. Generally speaking the game is still fun to play.

They've taken the steps to incorporate more freeride ability which to me was always one of the main reasons to play and KEEP playing the game, ie: MX vs ATV Unleashed, ATV Off Road Fury 2, the racing was there but the roaming was always more appealing.

In terms of the racing, so far I've managed to get 1st in all MX events I've done, some harder than others but it's playable unlike what I've seen from most people. It does have issues and again I feel as though these things are inevitably going to be handled.

My advice to anyone would be to find out for yourself, and give it a chance to iron out the kinks, instead of reading the vast majority of these reviews, especially the people that have invested less than 1 hour of time into the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMX vs ATV Legends is a massive step in the right direction. Do not listen to all of the All Out fanboys, that game was horrible and one of the worst mx games made with extreme arcady gameplay. MX vs ATV Legends is a great game after playing for some time, still not as good as MX vs ATV Reflex or Alive sadly. The bikes are somewhere in between of these two games when comparing the heaviness of the bikes, it is very easy throw a huge whip but thank god it is not at all like All Out. MX vs ATV Legends got the absolute best mx tracks of every mx game i have ever played. They are very well thought out and have a good flow to them. Sadly there is a massive lack of customization which i hope will be fixed with some addiotion of gear and bike parts.

PROS:

+ Great Gameplay
+ Good Graphics
+ Very good deformation of tracks with ruts
+ The best tracks of any mx game in my opinion
+ Massive amount of tracks to be played, and different modes (Something other mx games nowadays do not have)


CONS:

- Bike sound, i have been told that this should get better with patches
- Bike upgrading does not make sense, tuning makes the bike faster while parts are only cosmetic
- When throwing the bike back from a whip it handles bad and does not look good
- Sliding is way to much at times
- Next to none customization and there is no graphics kits for the bikes?!
- OEM bikes should NOT be dlc, do better
- AI is too slow
- Next to none customization

Overall as i have said, the game is good and worth picking up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPROS:
+ Graphics are really solid. While not staggeringly photorealistic, the colors pop really well and it's fun to look at. It might be my favorite visual style in the series so far.
+ Gameplay is fun. It feels like All-Out, mixed with Unleashed. It's very arcade-y, very videogame-y, but fun.
+ Track design is AMAZING this time. Really feels like the good ol' days of MX games. Lots of crazy jumps.
+ Lots of tracks and a lot of variety.
+ The career mode is actually pretty fun, it doesn't feel like such a grind to unlock tracks.
+ All the game modes, including the new trails mode are fun.
+ Base game is $35 at launch.

CONS:
- Customization is sorely lacking, especially with the rider gear. There's only a few brands to choose from, each brand only has 1 or 2 color variants, and you cannot change the appearance any further than that. Easily the most bare-bones customization in the series.
- While the physics are fun for the most part, the cornering needs more work; The bike tends to drift to the outside a lot and it can be difficult to pick lines and follow-through. Adjusting the Tuning does help a bit, but I still think it could use a bit more work.
- The FPS camera constantly drifts to the right on SX tracks (It's mostly fine elsewhere). I really like the FPS camera (a first for me) and the customizable settings are great, but it feels unusable in SX.
- The audio is mixed like a commercial; the music can sometimes be stupid loud even on the lowest setting, while the bike can barely be heard at times.

FINAL THOUGHTS:

This game is fun, it does not feel like a cashgrab. If it was $60 I'd have a lot more gripes with it, but for what you get, I think it is priced fairly. However, it will not blow you away. It's not Reflex 2, but it is a welcome entry to the series, and I do think this game will have a much longer life than any of the yearly Milestone releases. the base track designs alone made it more fun than All-Out for me. All of the issues I've listed can be patched out or improved upon, and I really hope Rainbow does so. I'm looking forward to seeing how it is improved upon.\t\t\t\t\t

The stock bike is horrible but once you get enough cash to mostly tune/upgrade 1 bike (6-8 races) you start flying and also start building rhythm. They also added a new seat bounce mechanic that lets you pull off nice step up/step downs off tables especially.

There are tons of tracks to play on and most of what ive played so far has been pretty good, looking at the track list ive played less that 1/4 of them.

My main complaints about the game is there just isnt that much to customise your bike and rider with compared to older games but they also had less tracks.

There are also some bugs but nothing that cant be fixed in a patch or two and the AI even on G.O.A.T difficulty, i am lapping in supercross now.

If you liked mx vs atv Alive/Supercross Encore or All Out you will like this.\t\t\t\t\t

I feel this is a typical console port to PC (with maybe some mobile aspects thrown in there) and in it's current shape it really needs polishing. Worth $36 on sale, sure, but I feel like if it was really polished like a game with a release date that's been pushed out until summer of 2022 should have been then I would rather have waited even longer and paid another $30 for better quality.

The audio is a mess, the first 125cc you get sounds amazing idling and pretty good while riding (especially while in first person view) but revving with the clutch pulled in sounds like a toy weed whacker! The music I eventually shut off because even on the lowest setting it would randomly start blasting out of nowhere. In the tutorial it's even worse.

The menu does not look great on a 34" curved ultrawide, it's like it's stretched to fit terribly. Gameplay is fine though otherwise. Graphics are beautiful and the terrain looks really good! Deformation looks good in first person.

Speaking of first person I feel the physics and the way the bike handles with pro physics and dual stick control on is fantastic, I switched back to third person for a bit and instantly wanted to go back to first, it's so good. The game makes it easy to ride around and finish races but to actually win on a normal difficulty you really have to ride the bike correctly with body and bike control and hitting jumps perfectly. It is a bit of a learning curve but ohhh man does it feel amazing when you hit a line perfectly!

The tracks in this game are a ton of fun! If you're trying to absolutely send it off everything in a stock 125, you're bound to crash, so you definitely have to learn the tracks and hit lines with a bit of thought put into them if you don't want to eat it and actually place on the podium.

I wish there were name brand vehicles from the start or unlockable through play without having to buy them as DLC packs. I hope some of the little things will be polished in the near future. As of right now it's worth it on sale if you can stand to make it through the tutorial and if you like to ride in first person view!\t\t\t\t\t
