\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnpolished, but something good hides underneath.

Pros:
- The dual stick mechanic feels great. Sliding into corners, boosting out of the corner with clutch, and use preload + seat bumping to launch off a triple feels really great.
- Ground physics generally feels good.
- Nationals and Supercross tracks are well designed and are a blast to ride.
- Tuning system is OKAY. Wish you could tune during a time trial mode / free mode and not just in the garage. Tuning is not well explained in game (what is handling?)
- Game runs well and looks really nice.

Cons:
- 250 2-stroke sound is headache-inducing bad. I have to turn the engine sounds all the way down. The 4 strokes are tolerable but not great.
- A.I. unfortunately sucks. I easily beat the hardest A.I. only after a few hours of gameplay. Not having a challenging A.I. is going to seriously dampen the longevity of this game for everyone, unless the online community thrives.
- Trails (the checkpoint) tracks are mind-numbingly boring, especially the desert themed ones. Twice I have caught myself thinking about work problems while slogging through these (again on hardest difficulty, winning by 50+ seconds. I promise I suck at this game, the A.I. is just that bad.)
- Tracks and bikes are locked behind completing the career mode. I wouldn't mind that if I could just focus on Supercross and Nationals only. The trails tracks are just a huge buzz-kill and are unfun, and there are way too many of them in succession.
- Air physics need some work, but are tolerable at the moment. Scrubbing and whips feels pretty clunky. I do like that you can bring the nose down a bit in air, that is a fun mechanic.
- Can't rebind keys :(
- Some bugs that I'm sure will be addressed soon, like manual transmission not working (stays in automatic no matter what setting I choose).
- A seemingly pointless Parts and Sponsorship system. I have zero incentive to interact with these systems at all. Why not make them interesting by tying them into the gameplay loop somehow?

Fans of MX games will probably be able to overlook the problems and will enjoy the National and Supercross tracks. For everyone else, wait for a sale or for the problems to be addressed by the devs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Post Update*
Bikes now feel a lot more grippy and not like you're sliding on ice. UTV racing still feels like a joke, not in the way they handle, but being rear-ended constantly. My first UTV race, i had to check if i was playing Wreckfest.
ATVs are okay, but are lacking a lot until upgraded.
Licensed bikes should have been included, but are not, just like the last few MX vs ATV games.
The open worlds are fun for a little while, but i haven't seen anything spectacular as of writing this review.
Ultrawide looks pretty good except when i am customizing my rider. He looks stretched horizontally and smushed vertically. Have to put the game into Windowed mode for it to look right, which is no big deal.
Track selection is pretty good. You will learn not to full send on a jump you are unfamiliar with pretty quick due to a sharp turn just ahead.
The trail racing is by far the best mode of this game.

Should you buy this game?
I'd say if you're a long time MX vs ATV fan, go for it. If you're new to the series, it will take a little getting used to, but the learning curve isn't too steep.
