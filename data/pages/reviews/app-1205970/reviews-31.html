\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhy is physics the end-all for these games? If you want real-tier physics, go play MX Bikes or Sim.

MX vs. ATV Legends is rough around the edges (I agree with complaints about audio and customization) but it's still FUN. The tracks and environments are huge, detailed and full of character. The racing is hilarious (watching the AI whiskey throttle off track and go flying/ragdoll into space is too good) and with the right camera setup feels fast. The new custom first person camera is one of the best I've seen in any arcade MX game.

I didn't know that the upgrades were in the "Tuning" section of the garage, so if you're struggling with races definitely prioritize upgrading your bike. Before upgrading, I thought I was playing the game wrong (casing every jump, missing corners, etc.) but after upgrading everything on track seems doable now.

Seriously, go set up a high FOV first person camera, upgrade your bike, and go explore one of the insane new free roam maps. Rainbow isn't pumping out the same bs every iteration like Milestone, they are actually trying new things and it's worthy of supporting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDecent arcade MX game that we'd all expect from this series. Amazing tracks. It does have some day one flaws but I can look past them for 35 schmeckles. The flaws are the audio. Fades in and out, blares music, bikes sound weak like a lawn mower. The game punishes you for trying to bark corners, without throttle control you blow ruts and slide the rear out and drift. The bike mods seem to do little to nothing to the actual power, traction or handling of the bike. If you get the IRL bike packs they're way bigger than the stock bikes so your rider will glitch through the seat and look like a child riding their dads 450. This series really shines with the modding community of bikes, skins and tracks though. If they can work out the traction some and in air physics to free the bike up some this game will be solid. Right now I'd give it 2/5 stars. It's still better than a milestone game like MXGP or MESX and I'm comfident the modding community will give this game real replayability with friends and solos alike.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off it has to be said that this game looks and plays like a BETA, however if you play these games for the same reason I do you'll still have fun just casually playing and enjoying what is there. There is a lack of customization and the physics are a little weird sometimes, but it's still enjoyable. As much as I could go on and compare this to the glory that is Unleashed like some other people it's not really a fare comparison. Unleashed is from a different generation where the games were more arcadey and DLC and game updates weren't a thing yet. I feel like this game will get improvements over the next few months and things will be fleshed out. To sum it up if you still play this series for nostalgia from growing up with the originals and just want some casual fun, then you'll have a good time with this and the $40 rather than $60 price tag was a good choice on the devs part since it's not a full on super long triple A title that'll take far too long to beat. At the end of the day we play this series not to be competitive, but to have fun, so why should we take the game so seriously that just a few small graphical or cosmetic things deter us from a game? If you do decide on getting this game then I look forward to tearing up some dirt with you if we ever cross paths, and my the best rider take the gold.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHad a crash to desktop after an hour and was about to refund. The first hour isn't fun at all. The tutorial is annoying and it takes a while to get into the controls. But after giving it a second chance, the game turned out to be fun. Trails mode, the open maps, is the part where the game shines. The tracks are long and fun to ride on. I have no issues with physics and twin stick controls. I would say it's more arcade like, but fun to bash the tracks.

Not everything is great. There are a few issues, of course. NPCs are pulled out of the worst job simulator games. They just stand there, solid as a rock, with no personality. I would have liked a garage or barn, where all of my team is. A place to walk around. But this is far to amitious for this game.

The graphics are nice, but not on a top level. The game fights with pop ins sometimes. First Person is a blast and the camera setting I recommend. Customization could be a bit deeper, with more items.

The most annoying section is the audio to me. No matter who was responsible for the mix, I hope you guys do overhours. At one point the music began blasting out my stereos, that I was worried about waking up the entire neighbourhood. Then it cuts off the music entirely in the middle of a song, just to continue very qiet. Music off is the only solution. Bike engine sounds cut off on full speed. You have delayed the game. This is not acceptable!

So after all, I can recommend it for what it is. A fun motocross racer with great tracks, OK graphics, and OK price. But it needs fixes. If the audio and some minor bugs are fixed, I'd have no problem with it.

I know this review is a leap of faith. But despite the audio I have fun playing the game.\t\t\t\t\t
