\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSure glad I didn't listen to all of the terrible reviews! Get past a few issues that will be patched ASAP and a few janky animations and this game is incredibly fun (and rather relaxing in free-roam).

Let's get the bad out of the way immediately: the audio - turn off music right away (it'll get patched), manual shifting - leave it to automatic for now (it'll get patched). Other than that, turn on pro physics and dual-stick steering and you're good to go. First-person view seems to be a must (for me at least), playing in 3rd-person made the handling feel completely wonky. I had some weird flickering ground shadows that I simply needed to update my graphics drivers for and that was fixed.

Some people are saying there isn't a free-roam, there sure is: Exhibition > Quick Play > Freeride. Many reviews seem to mention the physics or the handling: you can re-run all the career races for as much cash as you need and then simply begin TUNING your bike. Add as much grip or slip as you'd like. The progression in the game comes from the idea that your first ride is pretty weak and you get to improve it (and others) as you earn money by racing in the career mode.

Tutorial was a breeze and took maybe 15 min max. This is an absolutely solid racer that still may need some fine tuning (starting animations with the elbows is hilarious) but for it's current price of $35 it's a steal if you're into the idea of free-roaming and racing some simply amazing tracks. There are two huge free-roam areas currently in-game, (you can free-roam the race tracks as well). I'm hoping they expand on the racing tracks but also on the free-roam areas - apparently we are going to get 22 new tracks this year.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you look at the playtime on the negative reviews, most of them are a half hour or less, don't believe anything they have to say.

The game gets significantly better once you get better bikes with upgrades, unfortunately this is usually just passed the 2 hour return window depending how much time you spent free riding the crappy 125 around the compound.

I do recommend this game however I feel like it released in an incomplete state. I would have been happier with my purchase if it was labeled as early access to be honest. Pretty severe lack of polish all round.

People's complaints about the physics are more or less cancelled out once you're on a bigger, upgraded bike. Yes, it's still a bit too slidey, but, if you tune your suspension all the way soft and your tires for the most grip, most of that issue goes away. Also learning good throttle and brake management is key. You don't want to slam on the brakes into corners and go full throttle out of them. You're better off feathering or easing on each. I got much faster when I learned this and the game immediately became way more fun.

I do think they specifically need to adjust the traction on berms/ruts. I am fine with sliding the back out into a berm, but once that back tire hits the inside of the berm, it should hook up and go, not slide right over the berm. Ruts also give the opposite of the desired effect. For some reason, they slow you right down like you're stuck in the mud when really you should feel the bike get down into them and dig in for traction. So far, you seem to get punished for getting your bike perfectly into the ruts instead of rewarded and this should be changed.

Audio is also mostly horrible across the board and it almost sounds like all the sounds were just placeholders and they didn't have time to complete the audio before release. 2 strokes are literally missing a sound file or something for max revs so the bikes go completely silent when at max revs which is really weird. Bike sounds need a rework all around and the whole game needs the audio mastered because the volumes are all over the place.

I do have high hopes that the devs will continue to support this game, listen to the community and make the most requested changes. MX vs ATV All Out is SUBSTANTIALLY better than it was on release and that's because the devs listened and implemented requested changes. Still not my favorite, I prefer Legends physics to All Out's, but still far better than release. I feel like the same will go for this game, the devs will improve it over time without a doubt.

Overall, the game is VERY fun once you get used to it. High fun factor, high skill ceiling. Momentum and flow are everything.

Story mode is cool and a nice change of pace from just doing random events for no real reason.

Tracks and environments are exceptional.

Sound track would be good if it was properly mastered.

Overall, I think a lot of the game's criticisms are warranted but are also highly exaggerated. If you're a fan of motocross and arcade racers, you will find something you like here if you just give it the chance.\t\t\t\t\t
