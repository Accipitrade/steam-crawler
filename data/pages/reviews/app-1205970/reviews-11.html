\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter putting in almost 40 hours and finishing just the Motocross career with doing maybe half of the ATV and UTV careers, I think I'm ready to give my verdict on this game. This game has a great foundation that you can have plenty of fun in and it has hours of gameplay. It's the things that it tries to do on top of that foundation that don't really take away from the experience but don't really add much depth to them either. The actual *racing* is fun. The physics are just right and give you a great risk vs reward. Balancing your speed on a knife's edge of going as fast as you can without going too fast that you can't take a corner or wipe out on a particular rhythm section. This was enough for me. The game is fun. If you want to know more about the different aspects of the game, read on.
This game has been something I sit down and play when I have some spare time just to listen to music and throw down some laps. It's the actual riding where the game shines. Rainbow Studios knows how to make the game feel good to play between the physics and track design.
There's plenty of content here with a number of different tracks and races during the main career. Going from small engine bikes to the bigger/faster bikes was a nice progression and the track design is fantastic. How you race a track can change based on how much power you have on your bike. There are certain jumps you can't make on a 125CC bike that you can on a 450CC bike, as an example. So your speed and how you ride the track change. As mentioned above, the actual physics are great once I got used to them. The "game feel" is great, now I'll touch on the stuff that isn't quite as good.
The AI feels a little bit like set dressing because even on the hardest difficulty, I found them rather easy. Once you get ahead of the pack, you can typically run away with the lead. Because racing itself was fun, this didn't bother me too much. I often just made it a game to see how much of a lead I could have by the end of the race but it is something to note. There are a few bugs still in the game that can be frustrating when they come up. There were a very small number of times AI racers teleported way ahead of me in a track such that it was impossible to catch up and I'd end up getting 3rd or 2nd instead of the 1st place I was clearly running away with. As I mentioned though, that was rare. There were also a few instances where I didn't see myself collide with the top of a jump or a hill or something as I was going over them by my rider would crash regardless. Again, this was fairly rare. The game has a system in place that lets you upgrade your bike, but I was able to purchase the "best" parts fairly early and just kept it the entire time, so I didn't really get anything out of this system. The cosmetics are fine. Nothing too special, there's a decent bit of customization but this game doesn't let you play dress up with all of your favorite gear brands, if you were looking for that. This didn't bother me, as the customization present was more than enough for me. The story here is nothing to write home about, but I wasn't expecting hard hitting story here either. What's there is just to tie races together and give you a visual representation of how you are progressing through the career. Again, it's fine. I actually like that the farm you are on gets upgraded as you go along to add more jumps and tracks and trails.

So where does that leave me?
In summary: I'm not sure the current mixed rating is justified. This game has a solid foundation and racing itself is fun. They've ironed out most of the bugs (minus a couple here and there). The feeling of speed this game has and the "flow state" that you can enter as you rip around the track is addictive. I prefer this physics system over the slower feeling games of the MXGP/Monster Energy Supercross series. The track design is fun and adds some strategic depth to the game as you try to pick your lines. It's the extra features that they have added onto this one that don't really add much but don't take away from the game much either. I think Rainbow Studios should be proud of this game. They remain one of the best studios in this genre and still provide a fun motorsports game. I'd love to see them iterate on this game, as I think they could take this foundation and build something truly great on top of it.\t\t\t\t\t
