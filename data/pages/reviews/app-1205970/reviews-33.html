Physics are not improved at all, bike is very slidey and does not feel planted at all. In air control is also worse than All Out.
I hate how in the Career mode you have to go through what the game wants you to do instead of choosing an Outdoor set of events or a Stadium/Supercross set of events. You have to get through 8 or so outdoor events to get 3 supercross events and then go back to more outdoor events.
All in all, don't waste your time unless they make a drastic bit of changes, and I don't have the faith in Nordic to do so. I would love to be proven wrong, but I'm not going to hold my breath.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore you read, I have only played about 4 hours. Now that has been stated, let me get into the review. First and foremost, I believe this game is just fun. I play MX vs ATV games for the arcadey fun, for sim racing I will play MX Bikes. In my opinion this game has a great balance between fun and challenge. The physics do take time to get used to, but once you do they feel fairly nice, although slippery. I find that it is very important to tune your bike before judging the game, as without it the bike is fairly slippery, and not great to your preferences. Personally, I believe one of the best parts of the game is the track design. Each track is developed very well and is extremely fun. The nationals are my personal favorite, but in this game they somehow made me enjoy supercross and even the trails mode was super fun. Now that is out of the way let me get into some of the flaws; The first major one being how unironed this game is. The music goes from super quiet to super loud randomly, which is a big problem if you like game soundtracks like I do. Another flaw is how slippery it could be. It isn't too bad but it takes some getting used to. The customization is not very good in my opinion, due to there not being many options or even graphics kits. Personally I do not care about this but it is preference. I feel the biggest flaw is that at the time of this review you cannot create private multiplayer race lobbies with your friend, or choose what track it is. All of these flaws can be fixed, and I am sure they will be fixed, as All Out had a pretty sloppy start too. Now I will say some features I hope that they do release: Tag. Tag was a super fun game mode for me and my friends. The 350 MX Models; These were always my favorite bikes in all out. This is just my personal review so i hope i helped people out.

TLDR: At the time of this review I give the game 7.5/10. I have a crap load of fun playing it but it needs to be ironed out.\t\t\t\t\t
