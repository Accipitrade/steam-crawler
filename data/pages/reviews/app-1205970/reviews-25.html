\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTracks are good, but thats about it though.
The physics are horrible, bike bounces alot and slides quite strangely.
in air physics are bizarre, there no real sense of gyroscopic effect so you You can basically spin the bike and then change the axis of the spin mid flight. Whips for example are not really whipping in the sense that Gyroscopic forces act but rather just tilting and spinning the bike all over the place and not really adhering to reality.

Hard to explain this but I can turn the bike in the air and it will just stay where I leave it if I let go of the right stick where as in the real world the gyroscopic forces would cause the bike to slightly want to return to forward/upright facing direction, thus creating the whip sensation. MX vs ATV reflex really captured the sensation well where you could flick the bike off the lip of a jump and the momentum would almost carry the whip through and then only light throttle would help guide the bike back to moving direction. - This was a 2009 game and the gyro forces were translated well.


Track designs are good, and there is plenty of tracks however track textures are pretty bad and some objects around the track looks like they were 3d modelled in windows paint 3d
Some tracks have terrain erosion and others don't have any at all. I think the Utah free ride map is one in particular that makes no sense as it contains alot of sand although it did not deform or create tracks...? as appose to harder packed super cross tracks where for instance had substantial amounts of erosion after consecutive laps.

Sounds are not very good. the bike engine sounds like they are poorly chopped sound clips from youtube videos and cut together to make the bike sounds. The tones and revving sounds like its all patched together from different bikes or chinese tik tok videos or something.

Gear and bike customisation is there but there is far less items and variety than past games, another step backwards in my opinion. like one color or design from each brand. wtf?

I would of refunded it but I felt like I had to play it for a for more hours to really make sense of things to throw a review together. But yea not very good really and i'm stuck with now trying to get some moneys worth of entertainment from it sliding and bouncing all over the joint spinning my little bike all over the place\t\t\t\t\t

As a long time fan and relatively hardcore player of MvA and other dirt bike games. I can say this game took a few hours to get used to as it plays slightly different to past MvA titles.

But once I got the hang of it I absolutely fell I love with this game.

Track and level design is absolutely fantastic. A great mix of real life and arcade fun.

I won’t say the game is perfect because it isn’t. But the devs seem really dedicated to listening to community feedback and are willing to improve the game overtime.

I highly recommend this game to everyone who is a fan of first bikes or the MvA series\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIm 41, i've been riding dirt bikes all my life and have been playing most MX game since ATV Off Road Fury, Motocross Madness, Freekstyle, Reflex etc. I left this series after Supercross Encore when Monster Supercross came out and haven't came back until now and i'm so disappointed. There is so much wrong already.
-Bike Sounds are so SO bad.
-If you crash or reset, not only does the bike audio fail but there is NO dumping the clutch and taking off... its like the bike takes a few seconds to get into mode, feels like were starting in 3rd gear. (I'm beginning to feel like after you crash and reset, its still stuck on the crash mode and takes a second or 2 to let go of that coding and resume control. There is always a 1-3 second lag on not only bike sound, but throttle response after a crash/reset)
-There is a terrible physics where bumping into things, landing weird, causes the bike and rider to bounce into the air like a trampoline. Terrible.
-Upgrading your bike is a fail. As soon as i got enough credits, i did what anyone would do first with upgrading parts and i upgraded my suspension and exhaust only to see NO stats for the parts. Come to find out... upgrading your parts is JUST COSMETIC! WTF.
-I found out "TUNING" is where you improve the bikes stats. WHY? Thats not AT ALL how it works... upgrading to LVL 1,2,3 of each part of your bike.... wow.... this is going so far away from actual motocross and supercross riding.
-The characters dont move at all, when they talk... nothing happens with the NPC... you just hear them talking. WTF.
-The parts you buy and install... yeah it doesnt tell you what you have installed, they just all say "owned". Sweet.
I would love to go on but im already tired of re-living all these problems while writing this, this game sucks and im tired of Developers putting out unfinished games and really just reaching for our pockets instead of giving us BETTER games than the last and FULLY tested and bug free... something needs to change and developers need to be held accountable for the products they create and offer. When i grew up, and you bought a game from the store, that game was fully tested, mostly bug free and ready to go on launch day. Now we buy 3/4 made games and rely on the developers to continue to develop and fix the issues in which they hardly or rarely do and most of the time put out updates that don't even fix the issues, but add more ♥♥♥♥ instead.

*******
Update
I felt it was only fair. A lot of issues have been addressed, some not completely fixed, some improved a lot. The clutch works better but not great, bike dies too much on harder landings and takes too long to get moving, it still feels like the bike is stuck in a high gear in that moment, 2stroke 250 power going up steep hills and blipping the clutch to get up is still a struggle and shouldn't be as much, bikes crash on landings i dont feel they should be crashing, these bikes have amazing suspension and landing and a downward decline should also absorb impact but i crash on down hill senders everytime, WAY too many immediate resets for barely going off track, very unrealistic... cant reset in real life... you go off track... you enter back in the track safely without gaining a position, ground physics seem better, bike control and a great reduction in bouncing (it only happens when you send it off big into a rock bluff or something). I've played an additional 6hrs after this last update and have to say im starting to enjoy it. Although there are still bugs and things to improve, their response in the update changelog was a breath of fresh air. Its safe to assume they wont stop working on it with how this last update went. 🙏🏽\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t3.5/5 right now, but I do recommend it- let me tell you why. First, the good- physics, especially after the update, are smooth and satisfying. Playing on PC with XBOX Elite controller. Bike weight feels great, especially in the air. Handling manners on the ground feel great, making it easy to thread narrow trails at high speed. The absolute shining gem of the game though is the level design. The outdoor trail and invitational races you'll get to in career mode are hands down the most authentic-feeling dirtbike riding I've ever done in a video game. Exploring the massive amounts of trail in the more open-world levels is great too. First person camera is all I've used. Camera is adjustable, and easy to fine tune. Wide FOV makes the first person mode completely usable, especially in SX. To me (riding dirtbikes since I could walk) this game plays really well, and is extremely entertaining. The bad? Bikes are not very good until they're modded. No pre-race screen to change bikes/settings once you load into the track. Bike sounds are terrible. Sound of the bike is not connected to the throttle at most times. Music replays the same few mostly bad songs in repetition. Small glitches, etc. The point is, I think the small stuff can be patched and made better, but the core of the game is great. Maybe should've been baked a little bit longer, but hey I'm here for the ugly stuff.\t\t\t\t\t
