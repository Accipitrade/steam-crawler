\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd wait 6 months or so for them to fix most of the issues/missing content.

The tracks and free ride are by far the best part of the game, really well designed and filled with booters and great elevation.

Physics are good and bad, they get better once you tune up the bikes a bit, at release they slid around corners a lot.
Since then they have already patched the game to give the bikes more grip in corners.

Scrubs feel great, whips not as great. For some reason the bike doesn't come back in the air by itself when running with pro physics, but turning off pro physics makes it easier to bring it back. Go figure.

The game has an actual career, with a linear set of events you can go through for each individual type of vehicle. Only really been playing through the MX career but it has a good variety of events. The game sorta has a story of you helping an old motocross rider save his farm.

Customisation is just flat out unfinished. Barely any licensed gear, no graphics kits, and you can only change colours of the unlicensed gear. Oh and the licensed bikes are locked off via DLC.

So yea, it's not reflex 2, but I still recommend it if you enjoyed their last game, and you really wanna play a new mx vs atv game right now. Otherwise wait 6 months or so for them to iron out the kinks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdating this previously-negative review:

After running a check for corrupted game files and fixing an error, I can now play the nationals track that used to crash my game.

I've finished a four-hour session since then, and I now cannot rate this game highly enough.

After a long hiatus, the series finally has a worthy successor for Reflex and Unleashed.

However, there are a couple of things you need to do:
1. Suffer through the first few series of 125 and 250f races. The tracks are mostly built for open class bikes so you will case a lot of jumps and you will struggle to keep up with the bots even on normal difficulty. It's only an hour or so, and this time on a slower bike will give you a good opportunity to learn the new physics system. This is important because jumping straight onto a 450 would have you yeeting yourself into orbit at every corner, which would actually be less fun and probably make you more likely to give up. Get onto the 250 two-stroke and then the 450f and the experience will completely change for you.

2. As soon as you can afford them, buy both the far left extreme, and the far right extreme, of every tuning mod. Go to the compound, throw all of the mods for the far left extreme on your bike, go behind the mechanic's shed to the nationals track and rip a few laps. Take note of how quickly the bike turns, how easy it is to lose traction, and how quickly the bike whips in the air. After that, throw all of the mods to the far right and see how those factors are affected on the next few laps. Sort out your tuning and the next race you will immediately notice an improvement in your standings. I've ended up running everything to the far right, regardless of the type of track. This makes things sensitive, but this seems like the most capable setup which allows you to adjust riding style to suit the terrain.

3. Keep in mind the differences between two strokes and four strokes - the two strokes seem to prefer to maintain momentum and rail outside berms, as they wheel spin a lot when clutch boosting from a standstill. 450fs have much more grip and are easier to control.

4. As soon as possible, go to settings in the pause menu, go to audio settings, and drop the engine noise down one bar from default. this lets the sound track drown out the worst elements of the engine noise while letting the better parts shine through.

Everything I say below assumes that you've followed the instructions above:

This game has taken All Out as a base, and completely overhauled it to fix all of the main things that I personally think All Out got wrong.

Ground physics are VASTLY improved. In fact I would consider these are the most satisfying, logical ground physics of any MX vs ATV yet. Reflex included. These games all have moments where you land a bit sideways and just bounce 30 metres in the air for no reason. Legends has this feature also. However, of all of the previous titles, including the milestone series, this game does the least random bouncing and silly physics stuff. When you crash in this game, it is almost always your own fault, which is something that other games have shown is actually really hard to achieve, apparently. If you blow out a rut or go over a berm, you were going too fast. If your rear wheel slides out, its because your trail braking was too hard. If you get wheelspin on the way out of the turn and you spin around and stop, it was because you were too heavy handed with the throttle while still leaned over, or you didn't have the right stick in just the right spot. This game relies on, and rewards, smooth, controlled inputs of the brakes, throttle, and both of the sticks. It also rewards proper body positioning, such as counter balancing by leaning away from the direction of the turn, leaning forward to weight the front for grip in the turn, or leaning back to put power down with the rear. All Out allowed you to control whether the rider sits or stands by shifting the left stick backwards and forwards, but these inputs have far more effect in Legends, such as when standing to soak up bumps, or sitting to seat bounce and jump higher. The sticks can control both how high, and how far you jump, independently. Scrubs are unreal when you can pull them off properly.

Terrain deformation is spot on now, as is the bike's reaction to the newly formed ruts. The formed ruts feel the same to hit as the pre-formed ones that are there at the start of the race. There is a skill to it, but if you get the speed right, you can rail any rut you want without bouncing out of it, or pushing through it, or just gliding over the top like it isn't there. Ruts work now, and this is partly due to the fact that...

NPCs now follow multiple different lines on the tracks!!! No more do bots clump together a mindlessly rail a perfect single file formation on the exact same line lap after lap. No longer does this formation rip a metre-deep trench through the track in less than three laps.

Another note on NPCs - they have definitely gotten smarter. Rather than blindly smashing through you on their pre-defined single track around the course, they seem to know you're there and react. I've had them actively dodge to get out of my way and save themselves. I've also had them swoop in intelligently to try and block pass me. they're still slow as ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• even on GOAT difficulty but as least with the new AI they are more than just a mindless obstacle to be avoided at all costs. This leads me to my next point.

Rider collisions are also significantly improved. no more slight grazing of an elbow causing a random triple backflip and a race loss. They're still not perfect, but the key thing is that crashes as a result of rider contact so of make sense now. So they don't feel cheap like they used to. Also tough blocks and trackside objects are now a bit more forgiving to hit. This may not be realistic, but removes one of the most frustrating, flow-disrupting things that happens in all mx games. If i wanted realism, I'd play sim.. or actually ride motocross. I want to have fun and hit big lines. Another segue..

Remember Ensenada? remember how ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• huge those jump lines were and how satisfying and exhilarating it was to nail those big ridiculous jumps and berms? yeah those types of tracks are back. ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• yuuuge jumps. So nationals are no longer flat wide tracks like in all out.

If you were good at whips in All Out, you'll pick these up in no time. All Out's whips were insane, like the bike weighed nothing. Legend's whips, once you bike is tuned, are not as fast, but i certainly wouldn't call them stiff either.

Can't wait to see the replica MX tracks released throughout the year.

Overall, as I said, significantly improvement over previous titles. I would truly say this is the best MX vs ATV yet.

Track pass is probabnly gonna be worth it. But don't pay extra for the OEM bikes. Unless you reaaally need a yellow, blue or green bike.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow was this game released to the public? For money even at that. So i will complain a little bit then give you all the pros and cons so far with 25 hours put in to the game. So I 90%'ed the MX career, and its sad to say the least. Same tracks over and over again, I guess it's ok since you have 125, 250 and 450 classes. But its pretty repetitive to say the least, then when you go to the ATV career its literally the same tracks in order. Tracks were ATV's have no need to be on, pretty lazy honestly. The game is all types of buggy, like it will say you are off track when you are smack dab in the middle if it, for some reason in the dessert in the cave the music gets louder (like turn down your volume loud). The MX control was a pain but you kinda tune around it and get used to it. But the ATV's its pretty sad, on the super easy mode you cant get past 3rd place? then the UTV's? lolololololol its like driving a 240 in the snow.

Pro's:
-It's fun for what it is on the MX side
-It took me 25 hours to 90% the MX career (I just need to go play the tracks on the "switchable paths")
-that's all i got.......

Con's:
-The game was slapped together like a 240 the day of a drift event
-All you do is slide around like nothing
-You can set up PERFECTLY for a triple and still end up short and crash but yet the AI will do it or over shoot it
-Hardly any super cross its all open track
-You have to BUY yes pay REAL money for your favorite brand, pretty petty of them to do so, I would understand a single pack of really good sets. But no and you get punished for it, hardly any customization for said paid packs of REAL BRANDS
-There is hardly any real gear, maybe 3 FOX sets but no helmets
-Graphics are trash, you have to play in first person to get any feel and control
-The dual stick control is dumb thank god you can turn it off
-Tuning does absolutely nothing
-Tire brand/tread mean nothing
-Burms in turns are useless like literally, you hit it to bank a turn and you slide up it
-In the 450 super cross good luck, the ruts are insane
-Sponsor's? Yea don't even bother with them, they do absolutely nothing but give you like $100 every so many races or maybe a part that you never will use.
-Also with the sponsor's the "sponsor challenges" in the race are pointless, they give you a few bucks but that's really it
-No real sponsor's, just there junk fake ones, and there's only 4 so you just keep bouncing between them and you can only have one at a time.

I understand this is MX vs ATV and not like monster super cross but they had a shot to compete with these other games and they blew it. If they had the customization they do in monster energy 3 this game would be pretty legit. But nope, might as well bring back the trophy trucks and helicopters.... They just did there first bug fix today (07/02/2022) so we will see what that brings. But if you made it this far, I would say give it 2 months before buying this. After playing it I see why they launched it and put it on sale the first day.\t\t\t\t\t
