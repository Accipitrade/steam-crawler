\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game needs a little work. Let me explain.

I've loved most of the Rainbow Studios games since I started playing ATV Offroad Fury, through the MX series intro with Unleashed. This game stands out as a different style on how they wanted the player to feel. It comes across as though they wanted to create a more "realistic" feel with the vehicles in this game, and honestly, I love it.

The 3 vehicles you get to play in the game (as of right now), are the Motorcycles, which range from 150cc 2 strokes, 250cc 2 and 4 strokes, and 450cc 4 strokes. The second vehicle you can select is a 450cc ATV, and the last one is the UTV (kinda a sporty side-by-side like a Polaris RZR if you know what that is).

The game itself feels like Forza Horizons had a butt-baby with an MX game. It definitely feels a bit more sluggish, and less responsive on turns than most of the franchise in the past. But once you start to work around the little nuances of dual stick steering, or how bad you'll get thrown off in ruts or digs, or even corners, the game is one of the best.

Speaking of dual stick steering, it seems a few people playing do not understand so let me clarify a little (this is in reference to MX only, it doesn't matter as deep on the other 2 vehicles). The left stick is for your main turn, basically left or right on the handlebars. Up and down on the left stick is seatbounce, to gain a little more height on your jumps, at the sacrifice of distance. The right stick is lean, to tilt your character left or right, and help you dig deeper in the tighter corners, or lean back for a wheelie over some whoops. Like any MX game that I know of, gas and brake in the air is to lean forward or back to do a flip, so make sure you aren't holding down those unless you need to rotate. The last thing that's really necessary, is the clutch. A slight tap of the clutch when 80% through a tight corner, or at the tail end, will give you a little bit more traction and really help assist you to get that bit of speed you might need in something tighter like supercross.

Once you break down the game into individual vehicles, you get a little more complex on controls, but nothing that's too hard to learn. MX has one of the most responsive feels, but as the engine size gets bigger, you definitely feel that weight in corners. ATV feels very fun, it's the most straight forward of the 3, and probably the easiest. And the UTV is arguably the most fun, but also the hardest to learn and get comfy with, it's a freakin boat.

It should also be noted that the 3rd person view feels really slow and slightly clunky, but I'm sure there's some people who enjoy that more. But I play exclusively in 1st person, as it feels MUCH better, and almost fits the "realism" part of the game I enjoy so dearly. It should also be noted that you should almost immediately be upgrading your vehicle, as it gets harder to hit jumps and actually land them properly, without adequate power.

All of this good aside though, there's a few very blatant problems. Luckily they aren't game breaking, just annoying.

Firstly, in 1st person, going over any jumps or bumps, your character tends to throw their head up and to the left, probably coded in to help you see where you are landing and what's ahead of you, but for the slightly bigger whoops, or if you case a lot of jumps, it gets kinda disorienting to get thrashed that much without control of your camera or any settings to dampen the effect.

Second and definitely the one you will notice most, the audio needs an entire rework. Getting your MX going above 2nd gear, you'll hear the bike re-start its throttle sound, so it almost simulates revving your bike really snappy and fast, it doesn't sound natural at all. And it only gets worse, the faster you go.
The 250cc 2 stroke, the 3rd bike you get. God I want to love 2 strokes so badly, but this specific bike ruined the entire experience, until I shut all the sound off on my system. The exhaust sound will leave your ears bleeding compared to other vehicles, and sounds terrible. The only way I can describe it is gargling spoiled milk. Putting frozen beans in a blender. Putting spaghetti in an upside down lawnmower. You get the point, it's terrible. I think it wouldn't sound so terrible if it wasn't clearly the loudest vehicle in the game, by a longshot. You hop on a 250cc 4 stroke and it's really tame. You can even hop on the 150cc 2 stroke and it sounds fantastic. But that god forsaken 250cc 2 stroke is like microwaving bologna in olive oil for 40 minutes, at deafening levels.
Speaking of things being really loud, the freakin music, of all things. It's really quite good when you are free riding and exploring, as the sound is almost attached to your player or just background like it should be. But for some reason, it's like the Dev team wanted to make dynamic stadium speakers, like you would have at a real MX event. You go into the Campaign mode or really any race for that matter, and you get music blasting at levels that will make you flinch with every percussion or beat. You could throw your headset across your house and you'd hear the music fine, unless it's an endurance style race, where the map is huge. In that case, the music is usually either glued to the start and finish, or to the crowds. But good luck with that tinnitus on the smaller maps. I'm sure your friend's jet engine fan blowing into their mic, is more quiet than this music.

Oh and there's a third thing that I cannot stand. The maps "Vallis Wind Farm" which has an A, B, C, and D variant. Normally, the map is fine. On MX you might struggle a bit as you need to understand how the rhythm of landing jumps works, which I got stuck on because I didn't know you could upgrade the bike to help land more jumps, or to get more power coming off of a botched landing. But once you get into UTV's you'll for SURE hate the map. They decided it would be a bright idea to stuff 8 freakin vehicles, each being about 2x the size of a golf cart, on a single lane map, where even ATV's struggle to not hit each other. It's immediate congestion right off the start, and god forbid you get the holeshot through the start. You have to wrestle this machine, as it fights you to stay straight on the landings, and as you go over countless jumps of all sizes, hoping and praying that you won't just barely budge the stick to the side a little too far and have every other driver pass you while you respawn. It seems like changing difficulty does nothing to help you as, to assist my pain, I set steering to 1 stick, difficulty to very easy, and made the rider physics not pro. But it feels identical to any difficulty, or any setting. I even race it in 3rd person to help see what the heck is happening, but even then it's a tense struggle. BUT on top of all of this, there's literally 7 other guys with their foot through the floor, bricking the gas like they left the oven on at home. It's a nightmare to try to land straight or to control your vehicle and try to get a 1st place win. BUT IT DOESN'T STOP THERE...
You have to complete map variants A-D MORE THAN ONCE. The game is measured by seasons, so when you get to the next year, you have to run it again. Off the top of my head, you have to race the set of 4 (A-D), 4 times through the career, PER VEHICLE. Granted, the ATV and MX are much easier to grasp, but still, it gives me the heebie jeebies even thinking about trying to 100% get first place.

Fourth, and minor at that. The snow maps, there's specific sections of it like the starting/ finish line, or the huge left 180 corner, where I get horrible lag. I have a 1080ti playing on 1440p monitors, and other than this one specific map, I get zero other stutters or hitches in the gameplay.

TLDR. The game looks beautiful, it plays great (once you get the controls down), and it's definitely something I would recommend, as long as you have a controller and are alright with first person racing. But it has its issues.\t\t\t\t\t

The ATV are good, but they're not my favorite anyway. But the the UTV, the turning is touchy and tricky to get the hang of, but the NPC's like I wasn't sure if they were from Wreckfest because it was just like playing bumper cars the entire time. I can not beat them like this on any other race type. From what I noticed is that when there are "bottleneck" points (where the track gets very skinny) the NPC's slow way down like some congested traffic going through it.
