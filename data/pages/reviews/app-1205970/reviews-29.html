Big problems with MX vs ATV Legends
1. The sound of the 2 stroke, so far.
I've only ridden the 125cc and 250cc 2-stroke in game, so far. So this may be better with the 4-strokes, who knows.
The problem: The sound of the bike is not in sync at all, with what is happening visually or with the controller.
Ex #1 - After crashing, the sound of the engine is silent for the first couple seconds once you get going again. Even when twisting throttle and the bike starts moving...no engine sound at all
Ex #2 - The silly wind-out sound when you hit the bike's top speed. The 125cc bike has a top speed of 70mph. When you get to 70, the bike gets quiet with a sort of constant rpm sound. I understand they are trying to convey that you've hit the rev limiter, but the sound of the engine should absolutely not go nearly silent.
Ex #3 - This one really makes it difficult to ride becasue riders/players use the sound as a sensor as to what to do next. If you are riding a fast outdoor track with the throttle wide open, never letting off over jumps and rollers, you'll get the "on the ground accelerating and switching gears" sound even when in the air. In 1st person view, this is horrible because you do not know when you're in the air or touching the ground.

2. The throttle response
If sitting still on an uphill, you pull the throttle and many times the bike does not respond at all. It should at least rev. Pulling the clutch would cause a rev in reality. This game has very poor ties with how an actual bike responds.

3. Manual shifting
The default buttons for shifting up and down are the left and right sticks, pressed down. This is next to impossible to use because you'll nearly always be moving the riders weight or steering the bike in directions you do not want to go, when trying to switch gears. Terrible terrible button assignments. Note there is no controller button assignment feature, so you're stuck with this horrible design.

Remaining issues after 7-1-22 update
1. Sound
Partial throttle gives lower volume version of full throttle. This is not what a bike engine does when fed less fuel. It's cheesy, please pay more attention to the sounds of the bikes, as it plays a major factor in first person play.
Dead silence while holding full throttle and jumping. This happens at times, but not all the time.
Still have the 2 seconds of silence after a crash, but holding throttle (bike moving).

2. Button mapping needs to be added, badly. Nobody wants to manually shift with the two ticks/down. It disrupts steering and balance

After completing a career, here are more complaints.
1. The bike sounds in this game are an all-around joke. The 250 2-stroke sounds like a snowmobile. No gearing sounds, half throttle is just the same sound as full throttle, but quieter. Extremely weak in the sound department.
2. Sand feels like frozen dirt. There's no sandy feel to it at all
3. Where is championship or series mode? I have to load the pro motocross tracks one at a time, weak.
4. The AI is so bad. I can win a career while single jumping every jump on every track.
5. Popping the clutch does nearly nothing

Addition 3 to my list of complaints about this game.
1. Floatyness and bouncing

OH MY GOD HIRE A SOUND DESIGNER!

The sound needs to match the changing of the gears, particularly in manual shift mode.
