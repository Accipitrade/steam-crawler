\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSkábma is a really special game. As a person of indigenous heritage myself, the utility of a game as a cultural repository is a really groundbreaking approach. I can't speak for the Sámi people, but throughout the gameplay I found myself yearning for a version of the game that pertains to my own culture.

The gameplay is really fun in and of itself, but more so it's a really creative format to (virtually) explore Sápmi territory, introduce yourself to Sámi mythology and spirituality, and get a feel for the material culture. You get to soar through and transform gorgeous landscapes looking for magical clues, what's not to like? Additionally, I think it's really valuable that the project was led by a person of Sámi descent.

Yes, it can be a little easy to get lost in the map as other reviews have pointed out--but this isn't out of line with the paradigm of many indigenous cultures. It's important to develop your own relationship with the land, and you can have a rough time if you don't respect it.

Perhaps it felt a little slow to get started, but once you gain the first Familiar (special ability) the gameplay and environments bloom into something truly unforgettable. Skábma is a unique instance of fantasy that carefully balances cultured sensibilities with a vivid imagination. Definitely going to play it through again, and I can't wait for Skábma: Polar Night :)\t\t\t\t\t
