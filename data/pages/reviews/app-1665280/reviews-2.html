\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are a person who enjoys nature, mythology and values preserving cultures, this is a game for you
A fascinating jump into the Sámi culture that I'm sure many people have been wishing for. Skábma has quite a lot of information about the traditions, mythology and every-day life of Sámi people without info dumping you with a ton of lore text. The atmosphere of the game is very dreamlike and magical with a little twist of creepiness involved. I personally have an old potato of a gaming laptop so I couldn't yank up the settings without having major lag but even on low graphics the game was very pretty and I could enjoy looking at the scenery.

The soundtrack is very fitting and pleasant to listen to - big thumbs up for having traditional Sámi singing throughout the game. The story and lore is engaging and interesting - not to mention the characters, bless Nana forever - although towards the end it felt a little bit choppy/rushed.

As far as negatives go I have to agree with everyone else on the mechanics, there were many times I got quite frustrated with the wind attunement as it didn't properly activate when needed. Another thing is the camera, which was very difficult to navigate with in caves and during a chase scene I ended having a butt on my screen. Final boss as a character was cool but damn the battle mechanics were quite unfair with the boss spawning randomly in front of you and yeeting you constantly in the air - the wind attunement issue also made this more frustrating. My fps did plummet at certain places but I'm going to put the blame mostly on my old laptop, I'm sure this would run better on a proper machine.

You're a Noaidi Àilu
there's no Hagrid in this game but you can pet reindeer

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was ready to support this game for its cultural relevance and I think you should too. Sámi are definitely not represented in media enough, let alone in video games. But I really did not expect Skabma to also be this good on top of that.

I think the game especially shines on the story, atmosphere and music. The characters are fantastically written and you should not miss any dialogue in the game. Everyone can be talked to multiple times at all points of the story and I have both learned a lot about Sámi but also never once felt like I'm playing an education game. I especially LOVE the spirits of trees and they are well worth trying to find. Their stories are a joy to read through,


The gameplay mechanics took a sec to get used to. There are definitely some technical difficulties and the game does not give a tutorial on everything. But the experience and story more than make up for ANY issues in the game. I did check on some videos on Youtube at a couple of points for help and that made me understand the mechanics much better. And when they clicked, all the movement skills kinda flow into each other in ways that made simply going around the environments extremely fun.

I think some things are very underutilized like the shield seems to just be a "get out of grabs" thing. And I wish there we more puzzles in the game. But that really just makes me more excited for a sequel. If this game's systems are fine tuned a bit, I can see there being a full-length sequel that I would be happy to pay full price for to support these developers. Fingers crossed.

I definitely did not find all the collectibles but will most likely go through another round to try to get them all. All of them were a worthwhile reward and I just want to know more about the grandfather and Jean through them. I feel like I did not get the full picture yet :D

I have laughed, cried and feel utterly mesmerized during this game. I strongly recommend giving it a chance.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are looking for a semi-open world adventure game with main/side tasks to complete, great scenery/graphics, and great music then this is your game. With frequent save points, even inexperienced gamers like me can man the controls enough to beat the game. It keeps you interested by slowing introducing new abilities that can unlock more places to explore and hidden objects. Meditation spots in-game are for great for appreciating views and reflecting. There are moments when you get attacked, such as in the swamps and the final boss scene, but the majority of the time it's a relaxing adventure game. The controls for walking through caves can get janky; you can't always use the mouse to point in the direction you want-it's like you can only walk in 2D using the ASWD keys. It would be nice if after the final boss you were able to explore for the missed items/locations before returning the antler. I loved playing this game and recommend it for adventure seekers who don't want a shooting game! And with that cliffhanger I can hardly wait for the sequel!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaven't seen an adventure game about Sámi people before! It feels more of an adventure/puzzle game in the beginning, though it evolved into more of being a platformer towards the end. The cultural angle makes it worthwhile to try out on its own already, but it's a fun game also without the context.

A few pros and cons:
+ Pretty graphically, especially the environments.
+ Actual Sámi voice acting? That's a first at least for me.
+ Movement options towards the late game make the traversal fun.
- It's easy to get lost at a few spots during the game.
- Chaining some actions together too quickly lead to dropped inputs during platforming, which occasionally make you fall to your death.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSkabma - Snowfall is a beautiful game. Lovely artwork, a cultural lesson on an indigenous Nordic people (the Sami), and a heroic quest with small boy as an unlikely hero. The audio track is recorded in the native language, with translated subtitles. Although there are not a lot of NPCs, there is quite a bit of dialog to step through, requiring multiple visits to each character (and/or their spiritual counterpart) and conversing until the dialog begins to repeat.

I have spent many hours and am only about half-way through. As a completionist, I decided I was going to find all of the collectibles. This is a daunting task, as the landscape is like a maze. Forests, caves, swamps, twisting and turning paths, cliffs, valleys, etc. You have a magic drum that provides some clues but there is still a lot of traversing/searching required. Many collectibles are Sami artifacts and campfire stories so they are worth tracking down, even if you are not achievement-driven. The quest involves finding and activating friendly Spirits in order to combat the forces of darkness that are overtaking the land. Each friendly Spirit provides an ability that is required to move further along the path.

My issue with this game is that it requires a lot of precisely timed platforming. It is not just jumping from point A to point B. Instead, there is jumping, dashing, landing and turning on mid-air wind funnels, activating abilities mid-jump, etc. This requires the coordination to push multiple buttons/keys in perfect sequence and my skills are lacking in this area. The camera angles are often disconcerting, as the mouse does not always scan as one would expect and, sometimes, objects in the environment impede your view. To quantify my struggles, there is an 'achievement' for dying 50 times which I earned very early in the game. I've ended up using a controller for most of the gameplay, as moving with the WASD keys did not work well for me (especially in tight places and tunnels).

The game has auto-saves and multiple manual save slots. Your progress (in terms of collectibles and major tasks) is always saved but you may not be returned to the same physical location when you reload. You may need to climb/jump back to the top of an area to retry the jump that you missed.

Overall, this is an excellent adventure with a sweet story that I would recommend to those who enjoy platforming. If you are a not a fan of leaping into the fray, you might consider an alternate title. Had I realized how much precision is required, I might have been better served by auditioning for Cirque du Soleil than embarking on this journey. As it stands, I will keep jumping to my death because I really want to see how the story ends. Plus, where else would I get to pet a reindeer???

1st Added Note - The developer has posted a video in the Steam discussions with tips for air jumping. I have been making this harder than it needed to be and am delighted to have received some expert tutoring!

2nd Added Note - After countless, vain attempts, I have never been able to master a timed jump sequence out of a black horde that is required to access several collectibles. I have not been willing to abandon the related achievements in order to continue the story so my adventure still continues.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking for a game with very interesting world-building, lore, and a strong sense of exploration, this might be your cup of tea!

The story and gameplay encourage you to explore. I'm the kind of player that loves to look for collectibles, especially if they offer more insight into the lore, and this game rewards that interest. The little stories attached to the collectibles have made me chuckle or say 'aww' more than once, and also taught me more about an interesting real-world Sami culture I had very little previous knowledge of.

Though the game isn't very heavy on fighting, I feel like it is a true adventure game. There is always a sense on urgency and forward momentum, which encouraged me to continue even after failing a hard platforming section. Every time the game rewarded me with a cutscene of the fun monster-hunter Askovis I felt like the journey had been worth it, haha!

The latest patches have pretty much eliminated the technical issues that were present launch, like controller issues and adding more visual guides to the expansive open world. Two thumbs up for the devs for their quick response!
On another technical note: my computer build is absolutely outdated, but the game still runs pretty nicely on it. What surprises me the most is that the graphics look absolutely STUNNING on low!\t\t\t\t\t
