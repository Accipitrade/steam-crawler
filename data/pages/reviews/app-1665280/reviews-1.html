Visuals
There is a variety of types of environments, including some interesting weird dreamy landscapes, as even the forest (the place you'll be in the most) evolves through the game's main events. Overall, they look great. It is what you would expect from a very small team tackling relatively realistic exterior visuals, complete with the very strong volumetric lighting effects. The quality of the animations tend to vary, from very outdated humanoid animations to some surprisingly beautiful special effects.

Important note: although the game doesn't seem to state it, for any change in the graphics options to take place, you will need to reload your save file!

Sounds
Get used to hear drumming a lot as this is the single most important mechanic of the game (though you can lower its volume in the game options). Somehow, I felt like it went well with the non-invasive, calming musical score. The music did tend to loop a tad quickly for my taste, but overall, great musical design. The audio ambiances are also well polished. Also note that the dialogues are spoken in Sámi language. In this case, it makes absolute sense and is the right move without a doubt.

Controls
The movements felt a bit weird at first (especially how you go much faster forward when jumping), not as perfectly crisp as you'd expect from most platforming games, but I got used to it fairly quickly. You get a few moves that significantly complexify the gameplay, but in the end, I didn't have much problem problem at all, at least using a controller. Now, I don't understand the shield mechanic much and it seemed very poorly explained to me, but it's also the least important mandatory mechanic, so whatever.

Gameplay/Difficulty
The game is on the easier spectrum when it comes to platforming games. There were a few spots where performing properly meant also dealing with a camera that would not exactly help you by itself, but those are rare. It definitely takes a while to get there, but the last few levels bring a little bit of challenge, though I wouldn't expect experienced players to fail very often.

That being said, my biggest disappointment is found within the gameplay, as the second half of the game is a slog to go through. There are some exceptions (blowing up carts was neat), but otherwise the game expects you to repeat a whole lot the same thing (jump, slide, jump, slide, evade one enemy, jump slide...) through levels that frankly overstay their welcome. This is notwithstanding how most of these levels have very low visibility: I get that we're trying to build a dark and heavy atmosphere, but in this case, it amplifies how the whole thing's a repetitive chore.

There are collectibles to be found, and they are the source of very light tidbits of information regarding the Sámi people, which is nice, but the map lacking any clear "you are here" symbol makes it just enough annoying to explore that I don't feel like finding the last few secrets. Also, going off-road to find secrets once you actually have all the necessary powers to reach them all potentially means having a very tough time finding your way back on track since the indications you get from drumming only appear in specific areas at specific times.

Lastly, the "bosses" are a bit of a mess. They manage to give a bit of a sense of urgency, but they're also surprisingly not very harmful (very easy to fool and evade), which make the underlying challenges (running places mostly) a tad tedious. Nice idea though.

Story
The game nice and unique in its representation of a group at risk of losing their culture. The story itself is accessible enough while providing some neat references to the Sámi people's beliefs. It's hard not to be disappointed by the ending though. Without spoiling too much, I don't mind open endings, but in this case, it was explicitly chosen so as to prepare for a next game, one which as of now, may or may not ever exist from the very little information I found.

Overall
The regular price point makes sense. There are a few important elements that almost made me not recommend the game (the tedious length of the later half of the game most of all), but it's unique enough and sends a globally positive message.
6/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to love this game in it's entirety. There's a lot to really like about it. For starters, it's beautiful - the dev team have done an incredible job of bringing Northern Scandinavia to life. The story if very engaging, and I loved learning about the history and beliefs of the Sámi people. The game play concept was also quite novel - using different familiar spirits to control parts of the world. Overall, it's it pretty solid.

A few things were a little disappointing. The character models really annoyed me - the overly large eyes just felt...off to me for some reason, especially when everything else is so gorgeous. There were pretty awkward loading screens between different sections, which broke the gaming immersion - I get that the game has to load, but it was quite off-putting. And the handling of the characters was extremely janky in places...I almost stopped playing in a couple of places simply because I couldn't get the environment to respond in the way I needed. Coordinating the drum to affect things, and just jump/ glide was really awkward for me.

It's probably worth picking this up in a sale for the visuals and story, and hopefully future games can fix some of the rougher edges on the gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a small indie studio's first game it's pretty impressive. It has it's quirks, which the studio has actively sought to improve on based on player feedback & it seems several gameplay enhancing changes have already been implemented. Clearly some passionate people worked on this & hopefully they've managed to learn a thing or two here to bring an even better gaming experience in whatever game they work on next.

So, onto the review.

Firstly, it's a pretty atmoshpheric game. The story is told through movie like cinematics which are entertaining to watch & I especially have to give the studio props for including actual Sámi singing & voice acting in a game about their culture. If you care about accurate cultural representation & want to learn about the Sámi people through a short but fun adventure, by someone with a Sámi background, then consider giving this game a go. I'm not going to spoil anything regarding the story or characters here, so you'll just have to play it to find out what's great about them.

I will have to say though: Do have some patience when picking this game up. It has a couple tight platforming sections, so depending on your general skill level the playthrough might not end up being a complete cakewalk. However, don't let that scare you off from the experience; There's more substance here than a couple jumps. For anyone interested in the world & story I'd still say it's worth a try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs others have said, the idea of an indigenous story-based game is great. This game has real potential. Unfortunately, it's entirely let down by its lack of optimization and nightmarish controls.

Optimization
I have a good PC. I've yet to play a game I couldn't run on its highest graphic settings. Yet, the second I started playing this game, the opening cut scene was stuttering like I was trying to run it on a potato. The game's lighting is pretty intricate, but the graphics and level of detail aren't meant to be lifelike. I'd rate it around Valheim's level of complexity in environment, and I can run Valheim just fine. The camera controls are SUPER sensitive, so much so it was almost nauseating when I swung the camera around. I had to adjust it a lot. The camera will just plain not stay behind your character model in tight spaces, either, which is obviously a problem. When you're in tight caves, all of a sudden you can't steer your character model using the mouse and can only turn using WASD, thereby 1) exaggerating the camera sea sickness and 2) making it impossible to see where your character is going when she turns a corner.

Controls
I love the drumming mechanic. It had so much promise. Both of the issues I mentioned above would be annoying but bearable if it wasn't for the platforming controls. To put it bluntly, they're garbage. I'm not a huge platforming person, but I've done enough of it to recognize how BAD it is here. The dash ability is poorly described and poorly timed, and there's seemingly no way to drum while dashing, which becomes arbitrarily necessary to pass one level. Friends, I tried this one jump for HALF AN HOUR. I played this game a total of 82 minutes. I tried it every which way, using every key combination and timing I could, and just fell repeatedly to my death. Not wishing to repeat that experience goodness knows how many times for the rest of the game, I gave up. It's really a shame, because I really wanted to play and enjoy this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVisually stunning. Soundtrack was a perfect fit. Story keeps you wanting to push forward to find out what happens next. Very interesting to get a glimpse into the Sámi people of the 1600s.

Definitely some major performance issues causing FPS drops. Running a Ryzen 7 2700X, 16gb ram, and an RTX 2070, I was surprised I couldn't keep a consistent 60fps at 1080p. Mostly areas with heavier particle effects and lighting would dip to mid 30s sometimes. I did end up dropping all settings to their lowest, and that smoothed out almost the entire game. The difference in the quality of visuals from High to Low was barely noticeable.

The game mechanics were quite enjoyable up until you start getting more of your powers. Just feels less and less fluid the more powers you have to combine, especially mid flight or crowded.

The level design is gorgeous but difficult to find your way as you progress and especially if you try and find all the collectables. I wouldn't have minded it as much if accessing them remained open at any time after receiving all your skills. Unfortunately some have to be timed perfectly with the right chapters and if you miss the opportunity, there is no going back unless you restart an earlier save or restart the game entirely. After getting stressed with the maze the Forest was, I gave up on the collectables entirely unless they were right along my path.

Once I let myself just get back into the story and character conversations, I enjoyed it right up to the end. Need more games out there like this with a non violent approach to mechanics and story progression. Despite the few things that frustrated me, overall its a must play. Looking forward to more from the devs.\t\t\t\t\t
