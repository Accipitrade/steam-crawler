\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo preface, I am an avid adventure game veteran and therefore my expectations may have been a little steep for this type of low-end priced game; however, I do believe I gave Related a very fair chance, played until the end and at that end I just can not recommend it to anyone other than perhaps a beginner that really wants a casually weird gaming experience. (I would give it neutral but that is not given as an option by STEAM).

'Related' seemed to be trying too hard to be something yet it didn't really know what it was trying to be, or perhaps it was trying to be a metaphorically tragic psychological horror game yet really didn't offer any substance. As a matter of fact, this game really didn't feel much like a "horror" game at all ; to me it was more childishly gross in places perhaps, but certainly not "horror". As for the psychological part, again, I didn't get much sense of that either except perhaps a generic hint at childhood disassociation but there weren't enough details nor immersion factors to really get anything but a slight hint of what was being experienced and even that on a shallow level. The game basically felt very disjointed with puzzles stuck in haphazardly and I never felt empathy nor attachment for the characters at any level.

I did appreciate the general feel of it's graphic art, it was nicely drawn (albeit unappealing feature-wise for the characters) and the character had an almost zombie-like
gait (or the effect of someone with a load in their pants?) so it felt awkward and a bit annoying in that respect as well.

So, with consideration of all of it's features as a whole, Related was simply a rather ugly game overall.... and bottom line, it just ended up being a sad combination of dull, repetitive and eventually just plain boring since toward the end I found it was more of a chore to get through than a game to look forward to play and remember and recommend.

With all of that, I actually do hope the developers give it another shot with more character development, deeper story and more of an immersion quality and perhaps polish out the character to be a bit more appealing and add some quality music. As is, I would pass.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is basically a beta... it has room for improvement. I'll recommend it as a very short 30-40 minute chapter that doesn't feel finished. The mechanics could use some tweaks/improvements, and the game feels like it could use some basic polish as well. I paid like $1.99 for this game on sale. It was worth that.

First, I love this TYPE of game and have since the 90's. For those comparing the game to Fran Bow, I'd say that's generous at this point. It could be if the story wasn't subpar. The horror elements are extremely light and not particularly creepy to me. The general atmosphere is good though.

I'll second what others have said about the art style and sound design. It's lovely. The game is pretty easy all around. So the fun, for me, should come from the story combined with creepy art and sound.

Sadly, the story and dialogue aren't great. First, I know more about Thomas than Jessica/Julie. That's sad. This is what I know about them:

1. Jessica maybe used to have a sister named Julie (unclear but an obvious guess)... who is now a split personality or something of that nature.

2. She/they have been in the orphanage since infant/toddler age.

3. Whatever happened, it was long enough ago that she isn't "supposed" to remember.

That's literally all I've got.

I'm guessing this is an ESL author? Missing words, wrong words, and "not quite what that means" word choices are all present. There are also actual typos (e.g. "As I far as I know"). I wish as much care was taken with the words as the art.

Most of the time, the meaning was still clear. But there were plenty of times when the meaning was murky because of the wording. That made the "story" fall flat for me. While the graphics and sound are subtle and haunting, the dialogue is clunky and lacks that subtlety. I'd try to find a proof reader/writer to give it some polish. If it was as refined as the rest, I'd have fallen in love with this little game.



Misc other notes for the devs:

There were some long load times between scenes. After the board game scene, there was a fatal error and crash just as the screen with the giant stuffed animal loaded.

I'm a big fan of the 'Right-Click Close All' dialogue mechanic. If you accidentally trigger a dialogue tree you've already read, Right-Click kills the whole thread so you don't have to click through them all again. It makes it faster and smoother when you're exploring a scene where several areas all trigger the same conversation (like, say, each table of kids in the cafeteria triggering the same dialogue).

Clicking the dialogue box should move the dialogue forward. Currently, you have to click anywhere outside the box OR click the actual forward arrow inside the box. I swear... I clicked the box at least half the time. I could get used to it, but it seems easy to implement.

The drag-drop feature is a little jerky with items getting stuck on nothing.

I guess in summary: If the story was even half as good as the art and sound design, this would be a winner. It wasn't enough to guarantee that I'll come back for another chapter. I'm somewhat intrigued... but more by the story that I've created in my head than what you gave me.

For the time being, I'll recommend it. If no changes or future installments happen for years, I'll change it.\t\t\t\t\t
