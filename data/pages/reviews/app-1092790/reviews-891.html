\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is an absolutely outstanding delight to play through. It mashes up several genres in a way that works so well, in a way that I would have never thought possible. You’re given the main basis of it being an dark card game where you build a deck to your own preferences, while simultaneously being trapped in an escape room; learning the game rules, the ARG world that shrouds everything, and uncovering further mysteries with dashes of horror mixed throughout.

While your main fixation may be to just “win” the card game initially, uncovering the secrets hidden within these ambient escape rooms and settings are equally as satisfying and necessary if you want to truly experience and finish the game. It’s not all too difficult to piece together a lot of the puzzles, which makes it all the more gratifying when you put two and two together.

I adored the charm and mystique that most of the characters radiated, and having some occasional humor bantered amidst the bleak circumstances you’re in really puts the icing on the cake.

Personally, I spoiled Act 1 for myself on YouTube, but I decided to get the game and finish it blind because I was intrigued. I could have never predicted how far the game would continue to go, it’s really just that good. The rest of my playthrough was mostly done in conjunction with a friend in a voice call, and let me tell you, solving puzzles together and encountering some hilarious battles (i.e. Fighting them and others straight off of my friends list, submitting forbidden files to the Archivist) was so endearing and made my playthrough leagues more enjoyable than if I were to go it alone.

The climax of Act 3 and the subsequent finale was executed in such a bittersweet and beautiful way that it chilled my spine and made me reminisce on all of the characters I’ve met along my journey during my playthrough, while also leaving me curious about what things I could have missed or what further lore there might be to uncover. There’s a lot of story to go around for anyone who wants to find it, all wrapped up in this little world of its own.

A magnificent experience all around. I haven’t played anything as captivating and enjoyable like this in a long time, and I would definitely recommend that you give it a shot.\t\t\t\t\t
