\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's good, I think? No spoilers in this review: it's good, then ok, then weird.

First part is *excellent*. Incredibly good storytelling, very interesting gameplay, spooky but not scary.

Second part is ok. Good if you really like deckbuilding, kind of a slog if you don't.

Third part is kind of weird. It feels like the first part, but with weirder mechanics and a weirder story. While the first part had interesting new unknowns you were constantly trying to figure out, the third part basically only had 1 unanswered question that you kinda know the general answer to. Fun bosses, though.

The finale is great and also not-so-great. The gameplay leading up to the ending is quite interesting, and I really liked it. However, the actual ending is just strange. Supposedly, there's a secret ending that involves multiple ciphers, translating Binary into Polish and back into English, and some other stuff. However, the ending as it stands is a bit unsatisfactory because it's so strange.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit 2: Decided to put this at the top since there was an important update to the game. With Kaycees mod there is a lot of replayablity which was my main issue with the game. It's in beta right now and there's no new content outside of new cards and abilities as well as some lore and some other minor stuff. The issues with the story still stands and I doubt they will ever fix it. A few people seem to think the story is amazing for its ARG elements but I don't see it (this is coming from someone who thinks that stories are a very important part of a game, not someone who doesn't care about stories).

With some additional modding this game has a lot of potential and I look forward to them focusing on the rogue-like card game. If you pick this game up, I wholeheartedly recommend you play through the first section of the game, stop when the game makes a major change, then then switch to Kaycee's mod, which can be accessed by first opting into the beta through and then through the menu. There are more instructions on the update letter.

Do not forget about this game, if Kaycees mod sees play, there may be other expansions in the future which would greatly increase this game's lifespan.

---

I won't spoil anything outright, but if you read this you might get a sense what's ahead. If you have 20 bucks and don't care if you find it an okay game, then just buy it. But if you're wondering if this game is really worth that 20 bucks and don't care if you get some stuff spoiled, then keep on.

First and foremost this is a card game. All other elements outside of that fact are nice, but ultimately what you are getting is a single player card game that combines mechanics from both MTG and Hearthstone with extra elements (and in some cases restrictions) thrown into the mix. Rules change often, and it can be jarring at times, but it keeps things fresh. The mechanics aren't hard to understand, though it does feel like order of operations is more of a guideline. 15 hours in and sometimes I still get a shock when the game decides to trigger things in seemingly random order. This can get especially frustrating later into the game when mechanics become more complicated and more effects are thrown into the mix.

There is a story here, but I just can't recommend this game on it's narrative. It's cringy at times, the game forces you to slow down if you are getting content too quickly, and really it's just not that interesting of a story. Honestly I think the game would be better without some elements of the story. You will know what I'm talking about if you play the game. Don't get me wrong, first third of the game was really well made and honestly if they had kept it like that while making the game more difficult it would be a for sure recommendation. Though after the first 1/3rd of the game it's like seeing someone you know just slowly deteriorate as a person.

Balance was sort of neglected. It's by no means a broken game, but there are certainly areas where this game is just not as fine tuned as it could be. Part of the issue is the scale of the game itself, they seemed to have really bitten off more than they could chew and it shows in the latter 2/3rds of the game. I haven't finished the game, but right now I get the sense that they started with grand plans and then scrapped them to push the game out the door. Who knows, maybe there's more to it, but that's how it feels now.

So far as I know this is a one time play experience. I could not imagine myself replaying this, even the first 1/3rd of the game. What's worse is that there is no editor. This has a lot of potential to be a hub for community campaigns you genuinely could make some interesting things with this engine, but sadly the developers don't seem to be interested in that. Some people are modding the game, but due to the lack of support from workshop, I don't think it will go the way that BoI or other rogue-likes into eternal play.

Edit: I played the rest. The ending is bad. Game is fun, story is trash. Just to explain, much of the emotional pull is not deserved and the fact that there is a very long interactive, unskippable cutscene at the end really just grinds my gears.\t\t\t\t\t
