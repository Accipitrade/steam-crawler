\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe core game is fun and unique, the wider story is interesting while fairly held back, and the characters are complex enough that you will be able to articulate every facet of why you may or may not want them to die.

The most important thing to know about the game is that it's a horrible choice if you only want to play a card game. Inscryption will throw new mechanics at you, disrupt your deck, and TAKE MY PRECIOUS ANTS AWAY!!!!!! Q~Q! WHY GAME!?!?!? WHYYYYYYY!?!?!??!?!?!??!?!?, all of which will be frustrating if you JUST WANT TO PLAY WITH YOUR SWEET PRECIOUS ANTS IN PEACE!!! (deep breath) WHYYYYyYyyYYYyyy GAAAAAAAAME!!!!!, or want a consistent experience.
This is a puzzle game, not a card game. It has cut the ragged skin off of a card game and hidden itself inside it's hollow husk, and will hop out of that skin to stab you thirteen times with new mechanics, new strategies, and, most dreadfully,... Cabin Puzzles... Which require... (audible disgust) GETTING UP!!! (thunder cracks threateningly in the distance).

Maybe I'm just bitter because I liked the card game by itself... (If anyone knows another game where I can stab squirrels for profit, please let me know. (Something LEGAL, Jerry))

TLDR: Inscryption is a great puzzle game for people with card game experience who want to be told an excellent story.
Good fish.\t\t\t\t\t
