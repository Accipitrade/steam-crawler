The game is a deck builder roguelike with a lot of similarities to Slay the spire in which you pick, upgrade, morph and even sacrifice your own cards. If it stopped there it would be an amazing game of its own, but it doesn't. The game throws surprise after surprise beginning with the scape room stuff and later on a developing story around a few characters you will meet.
Without going on to spoiler territory the game changes a lot from section to section but maintaining the same good old mechanics you are introduced at the beginning.
I am still trying to find secret ending or more information on the whole backstory of the game so maybe when I find out more stuff, I can update the review.
10/10 one of the best impulse buys of the year

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBUYER BEWARE

This game is not a roguelike game. On Devolver Digital's page and in professional reviews, in the demo and in the marketing campaign, it is described as one. After three to seven hours (depending on RNG, the speed at which you complete puzzles and if you are good at roguelike deckbuilders) the game ceases to be a roguelike. The remainder of the game is the same card game with none of the roguelike elements from before.

Now, you're likely thinking "oh I see, in that case, I will keep playing Act 1 forever". You can't. If you pay attention to Act 1, you will notice that the card draws are scripted. You have some variety in how you built your deck but the game will use algorithms to give you the best chance at winning around the three hour mark. This is because the game wants you to win Act 1 to push you forwards in the storyline. The storyline is the most important part of this game to the degree where it doesn't have to be a card game at all. It could have been a visual novel with anime styled characters and been the same. It could have been a movie and been almost the same.

Unfortunately, the changes in gameplay occur after the two hour mark. Unless you get a customer service representative from Steam in a good mode, you will not be able to refund it if you bought the game by mistake. The worst thing you can possibly do in this situation is to keep playing and see if it "gets better". If you are not having fun already, the game's other segments (an FMV segment and another non-roguelike cardgame) will still not be fun. And the more you play, the worse your chance at a refund gets.

Right now, the only people capable of issuing a refund is the developer, who has yet to comment on the marketing mistake. I would recommend not buying this game if you are a roguelike fan, not even to try, and to purchase another game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I did love this game, it is not a card game roguelike like the trailers for the game gave off, its a game trying to tell a story with card game elements.

If you are looking for a really in depth and expanding card game, I would look elsewhere seeing as this game pretty much ends after 10-12 hours and the story comes to a conclusion and doesn't have a ton of replay value outside of finding secrets or you really enjoyed the gameplay loop.

Similar to this publishers other titles its a game you can really only experience once, but this one does give a gameplay loop that has some semblance of replay value if not a ton with the card game.

I enjoyed the game but just be mindful if you are looking for a card game to sink 200+ hours into. This isnt the game

EDIT: With the New Official Mod for the game, there actually seems to be a good reason to come back and keep playing. My review remains the same for the base game, but now there is good reason to pick it up if you want to try the new developer supported mod.
