\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a multi-deck game with a somewhat convoluted story. The story is told via blogs and by the characters of the story. It's a bit of a back and forth and can be disorienting to a degree. You play the role of the blogger. Finding a copy of the game by happenstance. making this not your story but his own development. This framework allows for the inclusion of puzzles within the game that unlock other cards.
The game can be repetitive as you learn how to beat bosses or need an explicit item to achieve final victory. In fact the initial entry does not allow you to progress until you've gained an understanding of bones, if you progress further than intended the opponent cheats and fills the board with bears, As the game progresses the deck mechanics change giving rise to multiple resources that the deck can use. Often times you tailor the deck incrementally, finding modifications or extra cards along the way. In other areas you are able to completely tailor a deck much like most card games but the decks have altered so you're dealing with new decks. In other times you are allowed to completely design a card which can be near game breaking. Some of which can destroy cards that could not be targeted through normal means such as starvation cards.
In short how well you like the game depends on how well you adapt to new mechanics.
This is the calvinball of deck games, ever changing, sometimes frustrating but never boring.

Additional note, Kaycee's mod is a challenge mode of the first act without the story beat interruptions.
The rules are somewhat altered and there are handicap conditions to pick from. While not as extensive as the main story, it does unlock additional cards found in later acts that didn't appear in act 1 during story - or at least I never encountered some of them during story. This gives a slight variation and additional replay value for quick play.\t\t\t\t\t
