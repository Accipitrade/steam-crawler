\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption was a very entertaining game both in the respect of its narrative and gameplay.


On a more technical aspect, I have not found any particularly flawed game design decision and the graphics and atmosphere did really work well all throughout the game. The music especially is really well put together, giving a feeling of adventure or dread depending on which part of the game the player is currently at.


With everything positive being said, I have the following pet peeve : The bear spam brigade in the first few playthroughs really did not need to be there. It would have been okay that very first time to teach the player about the chandelier (Or whatever it is supposedly called), but past that point, it took away from the gameplay. I understand from Leshy what he meant by "Too fast, too soon" since there are things that have not yet been unlocked to get "The True Ending", but just let the player beat the game once normally, the outcome is identical to when you lose against the guy anyway. I know I was told by the Stoat that he cheats and I'm comprehensive about that too, but still it is annoying and could even be frustrating to some people.


Should also mention that the game is a roguelite in the first act with Leshy, but loses that element later down the line, if you are the type to enjoy games with permadeath / upgrade after death mechanics, this game will sure as well disappoint you.


Over all, I recommend the game. It is a pretty solid game all things considered and you would likely find enjoyment while playing it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA card-battler game plus a fourth wall breaking "haunted glitchpunk" aestethic. The first act is the strongest, if you are just in it for the card-battling you can just finish that act. You (the player) are in a wooden cabin forced to play a card-game against a menacing opponent who hides in darkness. you can get up from the game board and solve light adventure game puzzles in the cabin to gain advantages in the card game. The card-game is similar to Magic: The Gathering plus Slay the Spire, but with disturbing overtones revolving around blood and sacrifices. You can only play cards not by having Land, but by sacrificing others creatures (and the seemingly sentient sacrifices tremble as the knife hovers over them). The random encounters revolve around meeting desperate and starving characters from frontier America.

If you like plot-driven games, it is worth continuing through later Acts. Pretty impressive amount of content for a indie title, and the advanced mechanics hinted at are very intriguing. But it was probably good to end the game when it did, or it would have dragged and overstayed its welcome.\t\t\t\t\t
