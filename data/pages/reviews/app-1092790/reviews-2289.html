\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely the most subversive game I've ever played. Games that try to tow the line between what's expected and what's completely out of the box tend to either be lackluster or too lost in their own head. I am incredibly pleased to see Inscryption falls into none of these categories, and is one of the strongest narrative experiences I've experienced in a very long time.

If you even have a middling interest in roguelike card games, or even if you hate them but love a game that can utterly surprise you then do not wait another second and purchase this game. It won't be what you expect. If you want another dime-a-dozen roguelite there's plenty of games for cheaper you can pick up. This is for someone who wants to be thoroughly tricked by a game and taken for a ride, so it won't be pleasant if you're looking for something in specific. (Despite this, though, the card mechanics present throughout the game are not an afterthought, its genuinely an incredibly fun card game.)

There's not much else I can say, but trust me: It is incredibly rare that a game like this comes out. Play it before it gets ruined for you. A 10/10\t\t\t\t\t
