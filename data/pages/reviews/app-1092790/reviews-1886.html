\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is easily one of the best games I've played this year, and possibly my new favorite card game. Not only does Inscryption boast fantastic card mechanics and a really fun gameplay loop, but the game also contains endless twists and turns from the beginning of the game to the end.

I've seen it described as a horror game, and while the game definitely has horror elements (see content warnings below), I wouldn't necessarily describe it as a horror game. It's definitely more just a game with a very strong aesthetic, that aesthetic being "trapped in a creepy cabin with a mysterious being who forces you to play card games". You've probably heard people talk about the game as being "surprising", but the surprises implied there are "oh, I didn't think the game would do this!" surprises, and not jump scares or anything like that.

If you like card games, deckbuilder games, or anything with strong aesthetics and good sound design, please check this game out. And do yourself a favor and jump into the game with no prior knowledge - don't look at screenshots, lets plays, or anything like that, because this is a game that is meant to surprise you.

Content warnings: Body Horror, Teeth stuff, light claustrophobia, implied murder\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh, there's only so much I feel I can say about this game in a review for someone who hasn't played it.

The core card game mechanics will feel very familiar to anyone who's played TCG/CCG's before. But the systems are scaled down to simple resources and a limited board size, making it engaging and easy to get familiar with for anyone who hasn't. Before too long, you may even start looking at your tug-of-war health as a potential resource to use, intentionally taking damage to squeak by at 1 hp, just so you can last another round with a better setup.

Cards will most of the time state clearly what they will do, and there is always an order that turns will play out in. So the fair encounters can sometimes become more akin to puzzle solving challenges, where you need to work out the best play to mitigate or counter accordingly with the deck you've built, through adding, modifying, and mixing cards as you make progress along the maps. All in all, it's a very solid card based rogue-like on the surface of this gem.

The style and themes of the game, I feel more than accomplish what it sets out for with flying colours, albeit with some darker tones. With colours and sound effects that are ominous and dramatic, yet fit nicely when and where they pop out, making a solid small scale horror-scape.

The story and narratives of the game are carried along with, and explain the card game inside it, using the space you (the character) are playing in with the card game itself and vice versa. With good use of storytelling devices, and a bit of playing meta with the fourth wall that the developer is notorious for, there's definitely a concrete and completable story line with a definitive ending to be following and involved in, at least more so than one would imagine from the usual rogue-like.

But I think if I say more than what's in the trailers or what I already have, I fear I may ruin some of it's surprises. So please do yourself a favour, and check out this game out! (There's even a short demo you can play to get a feel for it yourself)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is extremely eerie and interesting, although the last third of it is kind of weak. It takes a lot from games of the same developer, like Pony Island; however, it feels as if the story is way too shrouded in mistery to be comprehensible or even engaging at a deeper level like Pony Island was. It also pulls some of the same tricks, but with a worse execution in my opinion.
At the end you just go through the motions in order to watch the story progress. It appears to be a roguelike at first, but I finished the first act in my fourth try and after that the game only gets easier, wich feels like a bad design choice. I OTK'd a lot of bosses on the first try, and Im not even good at card games, this one is just too easy and the RNG almost always favours you. The mechanics arent that complex to stay interesting for the whole length of the game, although that feels a bit more like a conscious choice from the developer in order to keep the story in the forefront.
The characters are charismatic and interesting, though annoying at times. Sadly, you dont get to spend much time with most of them anyways.
There is a ''look for all the secrets'' side to the game that I honestly wont explore after my first playthrough, wich will be my last unless I feel like playing it with my GF just for the shock value of the first act.

An overall interesting narrative with superficial card game mechanics that almost get repetitive before the end. I would recommend it, though. I had a lot of fun.\t\t\t\t\t
