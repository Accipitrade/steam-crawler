\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven after reading the justified negative reviews, I was not prepared.
Unlike what others claim, the game doesn't "becomes something different". It's still a card game. The presentation of Act 1 is MILES better than Act 2, and I don't think the idea of it being a "love letter to CCGs" makes that any better.

Act 2 is so horrendously bad and broken it ruins the whole experience.
Act 2 is just straight up unfair with the game mechanics, because it changes the mechanics of the game you have been used to, and introduces new mechanics without properly tutorialising them.

Here's how it gets it wrong (gameplay spoilers only):
In Act 2, your resource cards (eg: squirrels) are now part of the main deck. You cannot choose draw a resource card. See, in MtG, your mana is in your deck, but you don't lose mana when you spend it; allowing you to use progressively more expensive cards as you gain more mana. In Act 1 of Inscryption, spent resources is permanent because the card is destroyed, but that's okay because you can choose to draw a squirrel to guarantee you can play a card for at least 1 sacrifice. In Act 2, you can go three rounds in a row with an empty board taking hit after hit because you are not drawing any squirrels and have a bunch of 1-cost cards in your hand. And, of course, the opponent still doesn't have to spend anything to play their cards, so they can keep playing cards every round to exponentially stack the pressure on you. It is BAFFLING how broken this is.

Act 2 removes totems, items (bottles, pliers, etc.), deathcards, and all of the other interesting side mechanics and replaces them with new resource cards that don't work because of what I've previously discussed. Act 2 is such an amazing shift in quality it gives me whiplash. I really cannot overstate how much I hate Act 2, when Act 1 was SO good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've fully beaten the game (and found most of the secrets).
It's rough around the Edges, there were a number times I clicked something and the game locked me into a choice I didn't actually intend to do.

My biggest criticism was that Act 1 is WAY BETTER than Act 2 & 3.
I was constantly thinking "Gee, I wish I was still in the cabin" for the last 66% of the game and that lowered my enjoyment

Act 3 would be 99% better if you could add and remove cards from your deck (like you could in Act 2), as it stands right now you're better off just keeping your starting cards and upgrading them rather then building ANY other type of deck. 3-4 great cards is a turn 1 win, +40 un-upgraded cards feels like a guaranteed loss.

After struggling for a while in Act 2 my strategy became more or less just "stall until I get Orobus" (something I continued doing in Act 3) and that made both Acts especially boring and trivialized most of the bosses/game play.

The Scale looks cool BUT It's also kind of hard to read (at a glance) exactly how many life points are left, which is really important to know at ALL times, both for actually winning and getting the bonus gold.
Its always frustrating when you accidentally end an encounter too early or you die without realizing you were exactly XX points from death. Which is something that happens surprisingly often with the current UI.

GREAT Story tho! That twist ending is what really got me to write a positive review.
Just wish there was more Act 1 game play.\t\t\t\t\t
