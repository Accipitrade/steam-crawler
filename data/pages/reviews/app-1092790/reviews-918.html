\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like card games? Do you like horror? Do you like ARGs (alternate reality games)? Do you like mystery? Do you like stimulation (not that kind, you perv!)? If you answered yes to any of those questions, then I highly implore you to give the game a shot. You may or may not have seen the popular card game Inscryption around as of late, but know that this easy to learn yet hard to master game of sacrificing animals to summon stronger creatures is the framework for the entire game, but NOT by any means all it has to offer. What else, you ask? Well, without spoiling anything: escape room style puzzles, a very large selection of cards, and all in all, you have your endurance tested by four ancient scrybes. I won't say anything more because I recommend going into this game blind if possible. The story, albeit with an admittedly somewhat abrupt ending, took me about 14 hours and intrigued me unlike nearly any game I've played since Daniel Mullins' first and second games on Steam, Pony Island & The Hex. I'm still finding secrets and new stuff left and right in my second playthrough. Daniel has a consistent way of really hooking me as a player with intrigue, mystery, and solid game mechanics, and thus I recommend this game highly.\t\t\t\t\t
