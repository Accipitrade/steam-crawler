\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is incredible for multiple reasons, all of which I'm sure people have said over and over again, but I'm going to list them anyway.

The storytelling is phenomenal, and the game does a great job at building up tension and giving you hints as to what is going on. The entire time I never felt as though the story was difficult to understand, or even piece together. Now this isn't a bad thing, I actually think that having a story that is both incredibly well done, but also easily understandable is a great thing; it just means that more people can really get into the game's story and appreciate it more.

Every single minute just has gushing atmosphere, there was never a single moment that I didn't feel like something was super off kilter or just odd, and for a game with gameplay mostly centered around a strategy game, that is stellar.

The one issue that I think I really had with this game was the segment where it becomes a Gameboy-esque setting. Now this isn't because of the setting, no I think that the setting and style is done incredibly well. I actually have more of an issue with how you play the actual card game during this section, in fact I remember getting incredibly frustrated with this one segment quite a bit due to how I felt as though it was just incredibly half-baked. I don't enjoy the idea that how good my starting hand is mostly determined by pure luck, I don't enjoy the fact that if I need a squirrel to put an attacking creature on the board that I have to wait three turns just to do that, and the entire second part of this game was a lot of that.

Overall, I would recommend this game to just about anyone. Not just people who are into that kind of turn based strategy, or strategy card games, I mean quite literally anyone. Minus the oddball bumps here and there, I think this game has something for everyone to enjoy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game began with a powerful beginning. You seemingly wake up in a wood cabin. The darkness presses in around you and all you can see are a pair of swirling eyes staring at you. No explanation at all for your predicament, you are merely given a tutorial and off you go!

The first act is easily the strongest act of the game; the villain's verbosity combined with the eerie mystery surrounding the setting, the talking cards desperately trying to find a way to escape, the myriad objects, totems, pliers, knives, hooks and squirrels that save you time and time again, this is the player's first impression. The desperation to win, to free the other cards, to save yourself from this nightmare, creates a truly powerful feeling to power on. The satisfaction of killing each boss, of confronting the mysterious figure in the dark and laying him to waste, it all crescendos to a shocking ending to the first act.

Then the game loses its s***.

The second act removes the setting of the cabin, destroys the glorious art style that permeated the game and made it so beautiful in its evil, the rustic feeling the well-worn table and cards provided and replaces it with an NES-era version of the game.

Don't get me wrong, I still enjoyed the game. The new mechanics, the creepy secrets that I found were fun and intriguing, but in the journey to tell a thrilling story, the second act sacrifices what the first act did so well and it hurts my satisfaction with the game. I felt like I was just slogging through the game so that I could get back to the cabin and enjoy that feeling of pulling my way to victory literally with my own teeth. Without the art-style of the first act, the second act doesn't feel like Inscryption, it feels like a fan-made 8-bit version of the game. After you defeat the final Scrybe, you go to the island in the middle, and declare which Scrybe you will replace.

Then the rug is pulled out from under you, and the third act begins.

No longer are you playing the first act's card game, where blood and bone is required to battle. Now energy and magic are utilized on floppy disk cards inside the starkly lit walls of a factory. The third act, once again, changes the setting of the game, and once again, I feel discombobulated. The setting is unfamiliar, there are new rules, and it all is not to my liking. The bosses are enjoyable, and there are some new mechanics that I did like, and the feeling of desperation that the first act possessed returns, much to my enjoyment. But to me the change in atmosphere and setting is sticky and uncomfortable, like shaking a sweaty hand. This was where I lost interest in the game, and had to force myself to finish it: the change bothered me too much.

The story is both intense and strange at the same time. I loved finding all the easter eggs I could see, piecing together the story and finding out what the game is really about. My only qualm is that sometimes the way the story is told interrupts the gameplay, and it doesn't flow well with the rest of the game. The game's ending is a thriller, and the last five minutes are intense, mournful, and beautiful.

The game has an excellent story, stupendous gameplay, and chilling characters with a Game-of-Thrones style plot. The major downsides are the constant change in setting and atmosphere, the strange forceful inserts of plot details into the main tale, and sometimes frustrating new mechanics that can be punishing if you don't grasp them quickly, All-in-all, a fun game, definitely worth 20 dollars, but only 20 dollars.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest story game I have ever played, hands down.
While there are not many negative reviews, many that I have seen seem completely BS. People are entitled to their opinions, but when people miss the point so badly, its hard not to say anything. I guess they disliked the game for all of the wrong reasons. I am also not trying to say all the negative reviews of this game are bad. Some are pretty good, and give good reasons to why they didn't enjoy the game. If someone didn't like the game because they found the card game dreadful, that makes sense. But many of the negative reviews seemed to enjoy parts of the card game, but still gave it a negative review for reasons I will discuss later in the review. I am going to address some of the common criticisms I've seen, and try to persuade people into playing (and hopefully finishing) this game. Minor spoilers I guess, but it shouldn't really spoil anything for you at all.

First, the steam store page does not spoil the game, and many of the negative reviews I have seen have called this 'misleading.' Well, the game does change a lot between the acts, but it is still (mostly) the same game, just with added mechanics. However, I guess the game changed enough for people to get butthurt about it, and I honestly do not understand exactly why. To me, it is part of the appeal and uniqueness of the game- and if the game was only act 1, I wouldn't have been as amazed as I was with it. The game changing between acts also kept it fresh and helped create the masterful pacing of the game. I'm assuming many of these negative reviews are a bit old as well, because there has been a mod released that allows you to play act 1 endlessly. I haven't played it, but if you really only liked act 1, you have that option. I still think you should play the rest of the game, just for its story. I think saying the steam page is misleading because it changes from act 1 seems childish. Not many games have this aspect, and to dislike it for one of its most unique qualities... its just something else. Especially considering the slop the video game market is filled with and the new (but essentially the same) COD games people are fed every year. When you read reviews like that, you understand why many video games have been reduced to uninspired time wasters. It is a bit sad to see, especially if you view games as an art form. I hope not to come off as pretentious, but when the game mostly changes setting and atmosphere between acts and to say you were mislead because of the changes... it just boggles my mind a little bit. I guess I don't understand how someone could love act 1, but then hate the rest of the game.

I have also seen some disliking some of the card mechanics in the game. And again, I think some people kinda missed the point. This game is a card game, but to me, that wasn't its focus. The story is its focus. The card game as more of a vehicle to push the story forward, and you know, make it a game. And yes, the card game is somewhat broken. Some cards are OP and others are complete trash. But it is fun, which is what a game SHOULD be. In addition, the game being unbalanced allows lower skilled players to still beat it, and experience the story. However, I suggest you try the demo and if you truly despise the card mechanics, then do not buy this game. If its not your thing, its not your thing. But if you have just a little bit of fun, then this game is truly an adventure. And again, I just don't understand how someone could like the first part and then completely dislike the other acts.

I think people disliked act 2 the most, and this probably explains why there was such a negative reaction to the game changing. I thought act 2 was fine, and while many mechanics were introduced, you could ignore them or learn them. They even gave you a deck auto-complete if you wanted to ignore the deck-building side. I will say- act 1 was very good and the acts afterwards were considerably weaker and (a lot) easier. But no game is perfect, and act 2 and 3 were still fun. And again, this is completely fine if you come into the game for its story and not its card mechanics. There are many older, better, and more balanced card games out there- but that's all they are: card games. This game is far more than that. If you liked act 1, you should still have some fun with the other acts.

In summary- game has decent card game mechanics, but the main focus is the story. If you are coming in expecting the best card game in the world, you will be a bit disappointed. But if you come in expecting a great story, you'll be happy with your purchase.\t\t\t\t\t

The overall basic card game is actually really fun and intuitive. Basic mechanics that build off each other that I feel could work as a real-life card game, and if it was, I would definitely buy it to support these creators. EDIT: after playing through the game a second time, Act 1 is a lot more fun. When you know what to expect with the gameplay loop, it becomes far more enjoyable. please play this game

I loved the "escape room" vibes I got from the first Act, but the rogue-like really hurts that part of the game for me. Everything after Act 1 is incredible.

If you get this game, I highly recommend you grind out ouroboros in the second act, and then pick it back up along with the secret boss card in the third act, it will help tremendously. I won't spoil anything else, because the rest you should really discover for yourself\t\t\t\t\t
