It was heavily RNG based in the beginning, then became really easy after i died a few times. Then, everything changed, all my cards, ability to choose squirrels, and everything else that made this game possible was stripped away. i was forced to die, die, and die again, all while struggling to understand new mechanics, with bosses that were impossible to make headway on.

I really enjoyed the game up to this point. I've been trying to avoid spoilers in this review, because it IS a really clever, creepy, and intriguing game. but the huge spike in difficulty has prevented any forward progress.

Honestly if there was a difficulty slider so i could turn it down when it was too hard, or up when it was too easy, my review would be positive. but as it stands now, i can't even finish the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption stars you in an insane man's cabin, forced to play a card rogue-like game for your own freedom... or so it seems. Note that I will try to keep this as spoiler free as possible, but there is one game concept later in the game that I would have to point out.

PROS:

~The game nails a creepy aesthetic throughout the entire game, which makes sense when you progress in the story.

~The art on all the cards are very good, and sometimes they have alternate skins, one of which made me chuckle

~The music is great, often a bit ominous and strange, and sometimes mystical

~The characters you come across are generally memorable, a few of the minor characters are a bit on the boring side, or characters that aren't memorable, but all of the main characters are fun characters, even if they are a bit on the evil side

~There are a TON of cool concepts and mechanics in the game, such as the ability to get up and explore the room you're in, looking for hidden secrets, puzzle clues, and new cards.

~The story is much deeper than what it seems like. Despite it looking like a simple concept, it took me nearly 12 hours to complete, and I haven't experienced most of the game

~The game may have a horror theme, but I wouldn't call it a horror game, or at least one that relies on jumpscares, gore and blood, or any psychological horror, so if you don't like that, it has none of that

CONS:

~THIS ONE CONTAINS SPOILERS, IF YOU ARE NOT PLANNING TO RECORD THIS GAME TO COMPLETION, SKIP THIS ONE
During the P03 section, there is a boss battle that uses your own personal files as a weapon. this ACTUALLY uses your files on your computer and displays them. If you're a streamer, I might just turn off the screen for viewing for a moment while you pick.

~There are a few exploits to the game to just break the game that I don't believe is intended. One example is the Ouroboros, which if you have the right setup, you can make them deal 100 damage, and have 100 health, only for two bloods. I think the developer might have overlooked this, but it could have been potential kept in just to make it an OP move

OVERALL RATING: 10/10

This is honestly a great game, and my game of the year for 2021. There's very little in terms of complaints against it, and there's much more than the 12 or so hours I have in this game. For $20, this is definitely worth it, and if you like card games or like Pony Island and Hex, then there's no reason not to get this game, especially if it's on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
