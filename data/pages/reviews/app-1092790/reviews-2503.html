\r\n\t\t\t\t\t For those that have already played one of Daniel's previous games, Inscryption is what you would
expect from his new game; Original, well made, with both quality and entertainment, while also being his most ambitious project yet.

Most people divide the game into 3 main parts, of those, both the 1st and 3rd are amazing, although not on the same level of creativity or novelty present on The Hex or Pony Island.
The problem tho, is in the 2nd part, it feels to much like a gimmick that should've ended a lot sooner, instead, you have a considerable amount of playtime into a narrative-wise good chapter, but gameplay-wise poorly version of the rest of the game. I wouldn't complain if said narrative was used to construct an even more elaborate 3rd chapter, but unfortunately, that's not the case, its mostly self contained, and the things it carries on to improve later on, are very scarce, and not really that exciting.

Another point i see a lot of talking is the card based mechanics, and without getting to much into spoilers, it doesn't seems to be balanced, because it isn't , one of its charms is that the way to win is to use whatever you have, and to reduce the number of retries, the game tries it's best to point you into some pretty op stuff.

Overall, it is a good game, especially for someone with no prior experience with Daniel's games; But when i look back to his previous works, i can't stop seeing how much more there could've been in this game, maybe it would get repetitive having the same surprises every game, but still, it felt like there was room for something more elaborate, more risky even.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are 3 chapters to the game and just be aware that what you see on the Steam page is Act 1.

The first Act is objectively the funnest and most interesting part of the game. However, it's also the shortest.
Act 2 is a little less fun. You can deckbuild with 4 different mechanics/themes, but the dev put story before creating a fun and balanced card game. Like I said, the first act is the best part because it seems to be the only card game that had any thought put into it.

Act 3 is even worse. For some god awful reason, the dev thought that making the least interesting and most boring card mechanic from Act 2 it's own section was a stroke of genius, when it was really a stroke of stupid. Act 2 was at least somewhat enjoyable because you can easily trim and mold your deck, but in Act 3 the game bloats your deck with whatever cards you chance upon. You'll find that removing cards is more uncommon than you'd like and you end up having to rely on busted upgrades for your basic 1-cost cards since you'll be drawing trash anyways from your main deck.

Act 2 was a little longer than Act 1, but Act 3 was abysmal. That one took the longest and I slogged through it like a chore. Most people think that Act 2 sucks more than 3 because it switches to 2D instead of 3D, but that part didn't bother me. The shift in game mechanics between 2 and 3 were the more annoying parts. People enjoyed Act 3 better because it returns to Act 1's escape room puzzles, but they are much, much more optional.

The biggest pitfall of the general card-game is that you can only draw one card per turn. However, this rule is not so bad when following the mechanics of certain themes. It's only under the Act 3's robot theme that it becomes a problem.
For the main theme on the Steam page, the resource mechanic is that you sacrifice other cards to play more cards. Some cards have additional resources/types, such as bones from dead cards or extra blood.
The graveyard theme orbits solely the bones from the main theme and it has additional cards that help supplement the play style.
The magic theme has you play a specific type of resource gem on the board; So long that resource is on the board, you can play any card that matches the color of the gem.
And the dreaded robot theme gives you a rechargeable resource that you only start with 1 of. At the beginning of each turn, the resource recharges and the max amount you have goes up by 1, up to 6.

The biggest slap in my face was that at the end the game decides to let you play a little bit in the 3D world of the other 2 missing mechanics/themes. And both of them were infinitely more fun than the basic-♥♥♥♥♥ delay-your-turn-over-and-over-to-be-allowed-to-play-an-expensive-card mechanic of Act 2. I wish that the developer made Act 3's theme a much smaller portion of the game than the other 2 themes. The other 2 themes held my attention much longer and also provided interesting ways to manipulate and control your side of the board, rather than having to wait each turn for more resources/waste your turn hoping you get the sigil that ups the max by 1 (up to 6 still).

I understand that in each Act the game is telling a story by fundamentally changing the gameplay and that you should be expecting this kind of thing from this dev. However, the gameplay suffers for it and it's only at the literal last mins of the game that the game lets you return to fun. The dev should have picked a more fun gameplay mechanic for the longest Act (3) instead of teasing me with the ideas and possibilities of the other exciting mechanics at the very, very end.\t\t\t\t\t
