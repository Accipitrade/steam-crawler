\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game you should expect to lose. Often. But don't worry, that's part of how the gameplay develops. Once you get the hang of things, you should mostly only die to a poor shuffle. And by then, well, to avoid spoilers let's just say losing won't sting so much anymore. Some choices you make along the way effect some dialog, and there are lots of little secrets to be found. Some you need to move the game forward, others are part of a meta-game that extends into the real world, and some are just for fun.
Personally, I really want to see a future version of Inscyption in which we see what the game could have been if ... well, if what happened hadn't have happened.\t\t\t\t\t
