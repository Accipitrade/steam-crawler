\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of my favorite games in a while, lot of flavor and fantastic characters. The music is usually really good too and every act in it is unique enough to feel worth playing individually. The game took me just under 10 hours to beat but the content you are getting is very high quality. I'd say its fairly replayable to check out some puzzles/secrets you missed and trying new deck builds in the different acts, although I do wish there was a longer/more established act that you could return to so that you can play around with the mechanics more.

For me personally I think my biggest problem with it was the ARG stuff and the whole Lucky Carder thing interrupting gameplay at times. Most of it is out of the way and completely ignorable so if you aren't interested in it you can mostly skip it and enjoy the ambiance of the game, but there are times where Luke will say ♥♥♥♥ like "that cards OP!!!!" while you are playing and it completely rips you out of the immersion. The context of you playing as a guy who is playing the game is actually really important and helps you understand/appreciate some characters more but them slowing down gameplay and interrupting your experience to say pointless stuff is really annoying to me. Personally I would have liked to immerse myself in the world more without constant reminders that we are playing through someones experience of playing a game. I think the world and story was engaging as it was and having a bunch of the secrets and plot centered around a scuffed card game youtuber was kind of a missed opportunity.

ARG annoyances aside, the game is still amazing. The characters all feel unique and despite not knowing them for very long, you really do appreciate and feel like you understand them by the end. The music and art style all do a fantastic job of setting up the ambiance of the game. The gameplay and card mechanics were both easy to understand and fun to learn more about. The puzzles were difficult at times but never overbearingly difficult. There was clearly a lot of passion put into this game and I would say its definitely worth a buy.\t\t\t\t\t

Without giving too much away, this game really surprised me a number of times. Making a deck on the fly is always fun, but I wasn't expecting to be able to make decks a bunch of different ways and explore so many card mechanics I'd never thought about working together with each other. It also didn't slow down the story at all either. It was amazing how much I wanted to keep playing this game to see it to the end.
8.5/10 Very well done. Played Pony Island in the past and this was definitely right up the same alley, so I loved every part of it. Would love to see more card stuff or just more cryptic analog horror stuff like this in the future!\t\t\t\t\t
