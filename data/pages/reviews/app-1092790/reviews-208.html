\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMixed feelings about the game.

Beginning Part:

The game starts out as a mid, less balanced, less interesting version of Slay the Spire.

Then you start to realize there might be more to the game. You explore the cabin, finish some Room-like puzzles. Find some cards that can talk who are mostly good. You have the sassy one. The brooding cool one. And the other one.

The game leaves a lot of mystery. What is outside the cabin? Who is this man? Why are you stuck in a time loop?

You finally finish the roguelike, solve all the puzzles, whatever.

Then you escape the cabin.


So you escape the cabin to find that your actually playing the game "Inscription" and all the characters are Ai and the dude who you were fighting before trapped them all in cards. You are some guy playing the game. Basically the story is actually a metanarrative very similar to DDLC, Undertale, and Superhot. Unfortunately, this game came out in 2021, where this kind of story has already been done 3000 times in the indie game space.

After this point the roguelike element is removed, instead it is an RPG. the game is made significantly easier because of this. They make the game a whole lot more complex but there really aren't that many synergies cross decks so it isn't that interesting. Just spam op â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥, win.

The third act is just more of the same. The Room elements are back though. Not particularly challenging. The sassy one wants to start the apocalypse or whatever. You beat him.

Then you do a victory lap playing against all the AIs. Main character sees some old files. Then you see that the main character is murdered because of this. None of this is explained because they want MatPat to try to explain it in his videos. They throw a bunch of lore in there that doesn't really answer stuff. I think they just intend to keep stringing him along as long as possible to get Game Theory clout.

Would have rather just been a story about a guy forcing me to play cards in a cabin ngl.
