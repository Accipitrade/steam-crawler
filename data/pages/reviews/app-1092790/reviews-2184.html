\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I played through it and got to the end. This feels like a lovecraft novel in that it makes the mistake of assuming something being unknown is enough to make it disturbing. Inscryption starts out well enough. It sets up several mysteries to be solved and does a great job indicating that the game you're playing is not the real game. However for a game that suggests such vast lore it feels oddly truncated, ending at what I assumed would be the halfway point. Additionally the card game which serves as the game's central mechanic is just not that fun. It has it's 'aha!' moments but it's middling at best. The end result was just not worth grinding through all that.

**spoiler-ish**

The main reason I walked away from Inscryption without liking it was that the answers it finally provides are just not that interesting. We see flashes of some hidden truth and we see that truth's effect on the protagonist, but we never see that truth ourselves as the player. This leads me to believe that the developer set up far more than he could deliver in that regard, and chose to leave an ominous blank instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has 3 acts. The first act is a 10/10. Just perfect. I played the last two acts just to finish the game. Would I have been given the second and third act separately as a gift, I would not have played them. They are not bad but extremely mediocre. They are as fun and complex as brushing your teeth. This makes the marketing brutally deceiving. Paying 18 bucks for the 4-hour first act was barely worth it. I strongly advise to only buy it for the first act for a very steep discount. Don't bother with act two and three, you might suspect some great gameplay to come, still being high on act one like it's cocaine, but there is just disappointment waiting ahead. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the worst games I have played this year simply due to what I would actually consider false advertising. All of the market lead me to believe that this game would be a card based rouge-like similar to slay the spire, instead it's only that for 3 hours before the game shifts into a completely different, lesser genre. I'm using lesser because it just isn't fun, and it isn't what I paid for. The game's tone and gameplay nosedive after 3 hours. I cannot recommend this game at all, if you're familiar with this creator's other games I'm sure you will enjoy it, however the narrative adds nothing for me, I understood the story and what they were going for, however it comes at the cost of a game I really enjoyed and would have considered a 10/10. I really feel like this game is a 3/10 in my mind.\t\t\t\t\t
