\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe only reason I'm recommending this game is because of act 1.

The first act is incredible. It's suspenseful, the animation and pacing is next-level, the art style, and the absolute creeps you get from staring into his eyes in a dark room. It's unlike any deck builder I've ever played and the actual card game itself is so simple yet complex (aside from high-cost cards being near worthless since the matches are so short and can be finished in literally a single turn if you have two 3/1 flyers).

But I only wish the entire game stayed in the first act.

I have to give credit to the devs for making such an original and creative game, but act 2 and 3 simply can't compare to how good 1 is. Not because it's not good, but because you only get to play a few hours of the best part and are forced into pixel art after something that's incredibly immersive. It's not that pixel art is inherently bad (stardew is one of my all-time favs), it's just that the game subverts your expectation, but not in a good way - in my opinion, at least.

I ended up finishing the game, but never felt the same sense of immersion as I did in the first act. I wish you could at least replay act 1 against leshy with all the new decks you've learned/acquired after beating the game. I'm sad to not recommend this because it truly is one of the most original games I've ever played, but this is like watching the lord of the rings and then having to watch the hobbit just to get to the end.\t\t\t\t\t
