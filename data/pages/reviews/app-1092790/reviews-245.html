I usually dont care for long games because I dont think the longevity of a game determines if the game is deemed "good" but rather by what the game has to offer and how well crafted the game was made and holy hell is this game well made. This game is made for both seasonal Card players and people that don't play card games at all! it is so well crafted that a person like myself, someone that is not into card games, wanted more and more the longer I played. The story telling of this game is incredible and its aided by the characters in the game! The way how the game introduces new mechanics and then includes them right after you learned it, is so magnificent! Its so amazing how in the 2D section all play styles are combined while in the 3D is more catered to the scrybe and they add their own rules. Even if you forget what something does, It included a QOL that can just remind you what it is by a simple click, so you wont need to worry about looking it up or spending time reading rules or anything! The way all these different genres of games blend together into one package is honestly so impressive and never in my life would I thought a game can blend this well and be so fun and addicting to play. The game has great atmosphere as well with the music and environment, which aids a lot to the story of the game I know a lot of people Enjoyed the first Act of the game more than the Third because Leshy is a great storyteller and role player at that! but I thought it was so genius on how P03 was a complete contrast! That was the whole point! He is a lifeless robot that is focused on min maxing the Game play of the card game and thinks storytelling is not important, the best way to see it is like if P03 was the big corporations making triple A games for money and Leshy was the Indie dev that actually cared about the presentation of their game. Even the music for that section matched the atmosphere of where you play (a lifeless factory) and the character you're up against (a lifeless robot) By the time I was at the end and done with the game I said out loud "Thats it?" because I wanted so much more! It got me craving for more content and I Really hope Daniel Mullens makes DLC for this game, I dont even care if I have to pay for it, this game deserves every penny. Seriously! when I beat P03 I thought I was going to be able to go back to the islands and pick a new Scrybe! I was trying to get through it quick for that reason, I wanted to see the other 2 in the 3D setting! I thought it would play out something like the island expand a bit and you have to fight a few more new people in each region, then get a new scrybe into power and I was so dissapointed that it didnt happen, instead we got a teaser of what Grimora would of looked like as well as Magnificus, and I would of absoltuely enjoyed a Yu-Gi-Oh arena style with him. Not only that but the 2D section could of had so much more as well, there's a whole seperate way to play on that Act that I kept thinking of it as a stand alone game.

BUY THIS GAME. GO IN IT BLIND. DONT WATCH THE TRAILER. PLAY THIS NOW!\t\t\t\t\t

Alright! Aside from that? Forget my nitpickiness and do NOT hesitate one second trying that game, it is AMAZING. First of all, it is a Daniel Mullins game, one of the best indie dev of all times. This man is a remarkable artist, some sort of David Lynch of the video game medium and I will promote each of his games, every time. While not being an absolute masterpiece, Inscryption is Daniel Mullins current masterpiece for sure. The amount of thinking and work that has been put into this game is TREMENDOUS, and as a huge board game fan myself, you can feel Mullins love for card and board games too here. I reckon Inscryption could come out as a LCG codesigned by R Garfield any day, AND I WOULD BUY IT! If you ever cared for games like MTG, NetRunner, Smash Up, Heartstone, Slay the Spire and so on, you should try this game. And THEN, if you care for amazing narration, psychological mysteries, creepy thrillers and the like, ALSO try this game. The music is amazing, the characters, the intricate way Mullins creeps you out while wiggling a fascinating story thread in front of you to grab it with shaky hands... the mechanism of this card game is SO unique and so well oiled and smart...
...alright, screw this, that is a 95%, I give this game a S, it might be imperfect but this game is a one unique game in a 20 years or something, that is a little gem of a masterpiece, FINE. But I am onto you, Daniel! If your next game is even better, Inscryption goes back down to rank A!!!!
