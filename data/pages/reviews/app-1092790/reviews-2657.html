\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first time I feel safe to say this:

It's the best game I've ever played.

I never made a complex or deep review, but I'll try my best this time:

Music: 4.5/5
-The soundtrack of this game mirrors so well whats happening on the screen. I've got chills more than i can remember only by hearing the themes when i started something different. Only thing that keeps me from giving a 5, is that sometimes when you are planning a lot, the music repeats, and it can get a bit overwhelming. But, i mean, it's an horror game.

Storytelling: 5/5
-I finished the game like 4 times? i guess? and i still think i dont get somethings. It never failed to get my curiosity flowing, and I'm a GREAT investigation fan, so when i started seeing stuff hidden... I almost c*me in me pants. Every character has already its own background, and everytime i play, i discover something new. Absolutely amazing.

Playability: 4/5
-Sometimes, the game isn't... well, fair. And it can be very frustrating for both people that like to play it slow, or people like me, who hate to lose. I'm not that good at card games, but i play a ton of strategy board games. And it feels like the game is just joking around with you sometimes. However, i found it quite amazing how easy it is to understand the mechanics of the game. My father doesn't play any videogames that are strategy-based, and he dived into Inscryption and has been loving it so far.

Bugs (?):
-I was a game tester and a glitch hunter. I still do glitch hunting, but as a hobbie. Now... i broke this game in so many ways that i dont even know if they were intended. I found some soft-locks, or some ways to get to places I'm certainly not supposed to be. However... I don't complain about those, since ir kinda adds to the uneasy atmosphere of the game.

Graphics: 5/5
-Wonderful. I cant quite put in words how much i love it. I play on a very bad laptop, so i used the low quality settings.... and i still love it. Everything, from the Tube TV simulation, to the old gameboy style. Just gorgeous.

Price:
-In my opinion, worth it. Bought for myself. Bought for my father, bought for 2 friends. Would buy it again.

Overall:
-Now, if you like slay the spire and expect something like this, you're underestimating this game. I played hours of StS on my friends pc, and ive done A20 to the point not even mods were giving me that much joy anymore. Not saying it's a bad game, its far from that, StS opened a lot of opportunities for other card games.
But with inscryption, you would expect progress. A story. To get involved with characters so much you forget it's only a game. That's good game design, that's Immersion. And you totally got me.

Deepbeneath.\t\t\t\t\t
