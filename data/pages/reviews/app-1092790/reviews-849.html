\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA masterful exploration of the card game genre.

The three acts experiment with different aspects about card games. The first act puts heavy emphasis on the player's presence in a tabletop game. Manipulating your hand and the board has a distinct tactile feel with the sounds and animations, miniatures and props are used by the antagonist to simulate a distinct vibe, descriptions for sigils are written in a rulebook. The second act strips away a lot of the details and changes to a simple pixelated aesthetic. What I found interesting was that although the flair of the tabletop environment was removed, the sigils and gameplay remained intuitive from what was learned in the first act. The third act goes even further into the video game aspects, mechanically and aesthetically. All acts offered a unique look into what can be done within a game with distinct rules.

Cards in Inscryption have 6 elements: name, image, cost, attack, health, and effects. What Inscryption does uniquely as a video game is manipulate these values. There are many traditional card games that have been transcribed into digital media, but are confined by the limitations of their past state as physical games. What Inscryption does in the first act is set a very grounded atmosphere. The cards start off simply, with most cards having one, maybe two, relatively simple effects. What it does from there is something only video games can do easily: Cards can be spliced together to add together attack and health, cards can be sacrificed to transfer their effect onto another card, teeth can be plucked to tip the scale in your favor. The beauty of the gameplay, like most rougelike deckbuilding games, is designing and creating a set of abilities that will bring victory. The cards in Inscryption don't just play off each other, they blend and merge, both mechanically and physically. Most cards only attack the space directly in front of them. Mantis god costs 1 blood, has 1 attack, 1 health, but hits the 3 spots in front of it like a fork. It is a simple card with a simple, strong effect. Buffing its attack or health is nice, but you can also do things like sacrifice a cockroach card to give mantis god the ability to go back into your hand if it dies on the board, you can give it deathtouch so it instantly kills any creatures it damages, you can splice two of them together to improve its stats, you can duplicate the card. The game gives you simple tools and it is easy to learn how they work, but it is up to the player to learn how those tools work together.

I revisited the game after Kaycee's Mod updated was released and once again ended up captivated by the gameplay. Inscryption is a unique game that is worthwhile to experience.\t\t\t\t\t
