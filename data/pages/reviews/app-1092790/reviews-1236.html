\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could give a, "maybe".
So much good this game has, but also that small bit that makes me doubt whether or not I'd actually recommend it to a general audience that just sees the front page and nothing else.

If you're on the fence about getting this game, I'll name two things for you to look out for (and these include minor, hopefully the right amount of cryptic, spoilers; you have been warned):
a. Looking at the store page, disregarding the reviews, taking in the main tags and screenshots, do you like the look of this game?
b. Know what developer you're dealing with; what makes his games stick out from all the others?

Now I recommend you to combine the two and think about whether or not you like the sound of it.
If not, reconsider getting this game. Maybe watch someone else play it instead.
If you do, great! In that case I can highly recommend it to you. Because it'll excel at its task.

Could you name it a plain case of "target audience"? It is hard to say. This is not, "just" the game on the steam store page.

Inscryption is not bad at all. It is a fantastic game: it just can get really, really disappointing.\t\t\t\t\t
