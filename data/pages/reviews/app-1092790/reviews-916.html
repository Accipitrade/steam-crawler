\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fantastic game, especially if you love card games. I will preface this review with saying that I loved most of this game. There's 3 acts, and all but the last act was fantastic, although thematically it seems like the last act is in contrast to the rest of the game, and the second act is the in-between compromise.

The story is pretty good but it is very meta, depends on some ARG(Alternate Reality Game) mechanics(there was a final secret of the game that was discovered via a secret message written on paper in one of the videos?), and a lot is not explained in the end. I saw some theories that this game is directly connected to Pony Island in the whole demon-controls-game-and-wants-to-spread-evil concept, but I'm not sure on that. If you don't care too much about story or don't have the energy to to play detective sleuth *and* focus on gameplay at the same time like some kind of ADHD reddit internet historian, then you can still enjoy the game. Just don't expect to understand most of it. Especially with the videos, there's just so much going on and information that is redacted / edited in a way that you can't hear or see what's going on. Personally I hated the videos in the later half because they just seem to be edited for spooky atmosphere than story and uh... the game isn't very scary at all, in my opinion.

Gameplay is, y'know, cards. All three acts have their own style. The first is the style that everyone was advertised / became familiar with. The other 2 acts aren't the worst, but they're significantly less balanced and less immersive than the first act, and I think this is the biggest issue that a lot of the negative reviews discuss, and I agree entirely. The second act was okay, I felt it was a little bit too difficult considering most of the cards are useless and I missed the mushroom secret because I just straight up didn't draw the cards required for it, so that was a bummer. It also ends the same way no matter what. The third act for me was THE WORST. I can't stress that enough. Either I didn't fully understand the intended tactics to be used or I just got bad draws but I genuinely thought I soft locked at some point. You *can* technically grind in Act 3 but god it takes forever and it is incredibly easy to lose all of the battles. I actually lost the first battle in the act due to a bad starting hand and the game has no comments on it, so presumably that's not intended. ♥♥♥♥ me I guess? It is a much more straightforward and mechanics-focused act though, and I think this is intentional.

The difficulty of the game is all over the place.

On the topic of the cabin in act 1, I've seen a few references to players suggesting that there's "more going on than you think" in the cabin. This is either a misunderstanding or a lie. The cabin, at most, has 2 or 3 optional *not secret* functions that aren't story-related, and they all serve to give you more cards, which are all redundant. The rest is technically also optional as my understanding is that you can go through the first act without unlocking the stinkbug or the wolf, but they are story-related. I like the puzzles but when I looked up the secrets, I was aghast at how incredibly obscure they were. Here's a freebie : You can get an upgraded smoke card by constantly putting out the candles next to the safe. How would you know this? You wouldn't unless you for some reason had an inclination to constantly put out candles... for some reason.

Maybe this is a problem of this part of the game just not being for me, and it's also not required to continue the game, but it sucks when you learn you missed stuff that, really, you would never have found without looking it up. It's not immersive, it's not very interesting, and really it just sucks because act 2 and 3 are devoid of secrets for the most part and they both get boring without hidden stuff to go poking around for.

If the game had been entirely the first act, I think a lot of the displeased players would have been much more impressed. I'm not saying the game is bad, it's just not what everyone thought it was going to be, and this seems to be intentional with how the trailer of the game only shows the first act, and I think a lot of people felt cheated.

The music and art is fantastic, and I will say that I actually thought the last act looked the best. The first act has a very cartoonish look to it, which isn't bad but I just felt it detracted from the "horror" factor. The third act is dark and dreary and you get a fantastic scene where you're being looked down upon by three of the big honchos of this world.

To expand on the difficulty, I'll speak on Kaycee's mod, which is an unoffical(I mean, it's not a DLC or anything and it's really hidden as to how to access it) expansion by the developer. First off, I think it's actually pretty good considering how much is added and it is a lot more difficult and rogue-like-y than the first act of the base game. However, this mod really reveals the issues the game has with getting bad draws, Leshy sort-of cheating by somehow having 5 cards on the board in 2-3 turns, and to make it worst you don't get death cards in this mod, meaning you're entirely left up to the RNG of what cards you get, what starting draws you get in battles, etc. It's a mess, and it's clearly not an experience meant for most players, so it's a major bummer coming out of the base game where we STILL don't have an endless mode for Act 1.

The gist of it is that you play Act 1's card game from start to finish. No continues, no death cards, very few puzzles and secrets to solve, and honestly it's just too damn difficult. To unlock stuff after a run, you have to have the required challenge points too(yeah, challenges are a thing too and required to unlock new starting decks, challenges, etc.), so really you're encouraged to make the game harder for yourself as you go on and I don't see any reason to do so other than personal torture. As far as I can tell, it's the first act of the game and you just play it over and over. I've unlocked 3 out of 10 dev logs, so either you have to play through it 10 times(and it's difficult enough playing through it once without losing) to unlock the next act, or it's just the first act over and over again with a difficulty that is WAY too difficult.

Really, I would recommend this you enjoy playing card games but it is NOT easy and in some areas it is NOT fun. It's not fun to get a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ hand and have to restart from the beginning in Act 1, it's not fun to get ♥♥♥♥♥♥ card draws from card packs in act 2 and feel like you're soft-locked, and it's definitely not fun to have the same issue in Act 3 only to find out that the bosses are piss easy. The game goes from great, to okay, to bad, in order of acts. If they ever make a game expanding on Act 1's concept then I'm game, but until then I don't think I'll come back to play this again and I won't torture myself with Kaycee's mod either. It's unfortunate really but this game doesn't have much replayability at all. I suggest this game if you really want to play a unique card game and don't want to spend hundreds in actual money(be it physical cards or sleazy ctg games online).

EDIT: What really broke me and made me make a review was Kaycee's mod. ♥♥♥♥ is wack, yo.\t\t\t\t\t
