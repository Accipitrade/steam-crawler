The game has fantastic gameplay (for the most part) and I think one of its biggest shortcomings is the fact that it focuses so heavily on its "story" which can actually get in the way of the actual fun. Either way, I think it is a good game and well made, but I will dive more into the details later on in this review.

Note: There will be spoilers up ahead. I will mark which areas contain spoilers, but just forewarning you.

The Good:
-The core gameplay is a lot of fun and Act 1 and Act 3 have fantastic gameplay. I never really got into card games and most card games I find rather boring, but this game managed to create a game with very minimal and addicting card game mechanics that can get really deep while still being easy to understand.

-Act 1 of the game is fantastic and that is 99% of the reason why most people bought this game. I think pretty much everyone who saw this game online was hooked by the Act 1 premise. It's intriguing, mysterious, addicting, a lot of fun, and teaches you new gameplay elements in a clever and entertaining manner. Act 1 is pretty much perfect.

-Act 3 is also a lot of fun. Without getting into spoilers, I think there were a lot of elements of Act 3 that were quite enjoyable and the ending to the game was pretty good for what the story had been building up (though the last 30 seconds of the game are not something I'm a fan of).

-The animations, design, music, and atmosphere are all really well done in Act 1 and Act 3 (and even a portion of Act 2). I liked the fact that each new map had its own atmosphere to it and there is plenty of personality in the world that is set up in this game.


The Bad: Here is where we get into spoilers. Everything in this section is spoilers.

-The story of this game is a bit disappointing and while it does manage to save itself towards the end of Act 3 and the finale does give the game an emotional send-off, I still think that the game shot itself in the foot by enticing the audience with a really interesting premise and then just giving us another "meta-gaming experience" which is what literally all indie games do nowadays. For an indie game to NOT have a meta storyline with "2D sections", "code breaks" or "fake computer crashes" would actually be surprising as this is a very well worn path and many of the "shocking" elements of the game are cliches at this point.

-The beginning of Act 2 is broken in terms of gameplay (especially if you choose Magnificus). Act 2 is quite literally one of the WORST transitions I have experienced from any game and is downright busted in the first half of it. The issue with Act 2 is that it adds about a dozen new gameplay concepts all at once and it doesn't teach you how ANY of them work. To make matters worse, the ONE gameplay mechanic that is crucial to the entire game functioning is the one that you don't have, that being squirrels. I know on paper that sounds like an interesting way to force players to try something new, but the problem is that the vast majority of cards you get are non-functional unless you have something to sacrifice and if you chose Magnificus then you don't have anything to sacrifice. I tried the "auto-deck builder" and it also doesn't work and would give me a deck of ALL MOX GEMS WITH NO MOX UNITS! I kid you not when I say that I played the first hour or two of Act 2 without getting a single victory because there would be instances where there would be no available plays for an entire battle and all I would do is hit the bell on repeat.

-Continued..: Once I beat my first battle and purchased a squirrel card I was able to steamroll my way through all of Act 2 without any real issues. Seriously. That was it. Just ONE SQUIRREL card made all of the difference to be able to play Act 2 and I never really needed to change my deck after that point. What I had made in the beginning of Act 2 was incredibly good at beating the entire Act 2 campaign but one squirrel was all of the difference between being able to beat every battle with ease vs not being able to beat a single battle.

-I should note that I think the mox, energy, and bone mechanics are all a lot of fun, but the way they are introduced and executed is horrendous. Act 3 actually teaches you ALL of these mechanics again in a proper and engaging way, which confuses me as to why Act 2 even exists. Why make a broken and unfriendly second act without any real hints of explanations to the audience and then properly teach all of those mechanics later on in the game as if it were for the first time? The only guess I have is that Act 2 was a last minute idea and that's why the game teaches you all of the elements out or order. Either way, it's not fun.

-Act 2 is broken in terms of story: Act 2 is where the game does a huge reversal and where everything Act 1 set-up is essentially flipped on its heads and the mystery is taken away. You thought you had the souls of humans in your cards, right? You thought Leshy was a bad guy right? You thought the pile of dead corpses was creepy, right? Wrong. This is all a video game within a video and all of the talking cards you had are gym-leaders in a retro 2D pokemon inspired game (again, this is a cliche at this point) who have witty dialogue and banter with each other. To make the story even worse, you are supposedly starting a "New game" which would imply that Act 2 is actually a PREQUEL to Act 1 as Act 1 is already in the middle of a game and Act 2 is supposed to be the origin of the game in Act 1... but it isn't. In the "New Game" all of the characters remember what happened in Act 1 and it is stated as a direct continuation of the "previous saved game". Not only is Act 2's gameplay broken and its story a jarring flip, but it also doesn't make sense narratively speaking.

-The last 30 seconds of the game are dumb. Seriously, Luke just gets shot by some girl where sunglasses walking around with a gun outside. I wasn't sure if this was supposed to be intentionally funny or not, but the moment came across as comedic to me due to how anti-climatic it was and how little it added to the narrative. If anything, it actually confuses the narrative as it takes away the mystery from the game itself and leads into some big "corporate conspiracy" that lessens the impact of the idea of a game with "evil data" that has the soul of a girl stuck in it. It felt tacked on and kind of destroyed the atmosphere that was built up beforehand.



Long story short: I think this game is incredibly well-made in Act 1 and Act 3 and while I think its narrative is a bit disappointing at first, I do think that the game manages to mostly save the narrative at the end and convince the audience that "this is what the story should be". I can imagine a lot of people not buying into the game's narrative and ending the game after Act 2 being a mess, but I can say that it is definitely worth getting to the end of the game.

Even so, I can still imagine there are a lot of people who will be disappointed upon discovering that this game isn't really a rogue-lite horror card game, but it is actually a story driven game pretending to be a card-game pretending to be a horror game. Because of this, I really wish the game had a roguelite mode or something like that as the core mechanics of this game are REALLY good and a lot of fun, but it's a shame that they can only be played in context to the story and not on their own (as many had hoped).

Who knows, maybe there will be DLC for a rogue-lite mode or possibly more "maps" to go on, like Magnificus or Grimora's map.

Good game, if uneven and frustrating at times.
