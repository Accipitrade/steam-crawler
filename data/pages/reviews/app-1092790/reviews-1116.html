\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic game, very innovative and very fun. As I'm sure you've already seen, this game is best played blind. If you're curious why, I'll do my best to explain in the rest of the review without giving too much away.

This is a story game. The gameplay is fun, yes, but everything you do in it is a vessel for the story. The game will try and push you towards story progression, it'll hold your hand if it thinks you need it, and I think it's better for having done it.

If that's not your type of game, don't worry! There's a free, official (despite the name) expansion, Kaycee's Mod, in beta at the time of writing and planned to go live in February. It adds a lot of replayability and some new content and balance changes. If a roguelike deck builder is what brought you here, and you end up not liking the main game, this is for you. New starter decks and challenge modifiers are introduced as you play to keep things fresh, but nothing is carried over between runs. (unless you quit one early, heh)

This game is a lot longer than you might think. If you've watched anyone else play the game, and thought you might have seen too much to justify a purchase, I assure you, it's still worth buying. I had already seen the entire thing, knew all the secrets and plot twists, was trying to go through it as fast as I could, and it still took me a good 7-8 hours. (I bought it on Epic first and rebought it on Steam so I could play the Kaycee's Mod beta, so add 7 hours to my total here)

I absolutely love this game, its lore, its execution, everything about it. It's an amazing experience and I highly recommend you try it out. It reminds me of being in fandoms as a kid, theories and lore videos I would watch for hours, and the game itself is just plain fun. I watch my friends play through it and see their reactions, we talk about things that happen in the game, we play through Kaycee's Mod and share the runs we got, it's great. Good game.\t\t\t\t\t
