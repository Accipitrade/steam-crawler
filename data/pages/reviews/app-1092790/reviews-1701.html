\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was enamoured with this game while watching some Let's Players online and had to stop halfway through an episode so i wouldn't spoil things because i knew i wanted to play this one myself.

All i can say is the game is separated into different sections and I really really felt let down by the last. I loved how novel, engaging, the seemingly impossible BUT still doable challenges of the card game mechanics. I loved the thematic elements, the creativity, the weird irl videos, you name it. Everything was enigmatic and engaging. The second section continued this for me with a different gameplay style and energy. And then the last one just... felt disappointing. It became tedious, repetetive, stole the charm of encountering this type again. i felt like i was forcing myself through it and eventually dropped it.

I think folks who don't mind repetition in their game would greatly enjoy it. And those who are fanatics surrounding irl card games like magic, yugioh, etc would get a real kick.

However, if you want to play this game i'd just encourage keeping in mind the end parts are not as novel in my opinion as the others. It definitely becomes more tedious and repetitive. And if you aren't familiar with basic card mechanisms this game will be grueling.

I love everything that went into this, just wish the ending sections and honestly the story ending were different. I felt a bit let down. If the first 2 sections were either the game ALONE or just those two parts together i would have felt much much better. it felt great up till then.

A grand old time until a bitter ending.\t\t\t\t\t
