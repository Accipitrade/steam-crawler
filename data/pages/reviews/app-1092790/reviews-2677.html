\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBrooo this game is a lot more than meets the eye.

I thought that the demo was already damn impressive with its art style and mysterious lore from watching ManlyBadassHero's playthrough of it. I'll try not to spoil the game because I haven't gotten far myself and I just found out this ♥♥♥♥.

Anyway, the gameplay is very simple and the best way I can describe it is like Face-Hunter from Hearthstone albeit slower and focusing more on positioning and roguelite elements. Each minion has attack and health values that can be improved throughout your runs. Unfortunately to summon most minions, you need to sacrifice a certain number of minions to summon the good ones. Usually to meet this condition, the game allows you to choose to draw either minions or squirrels (mana) which emphasizes a more tactical approach rather than topdecking. The goal of the card game is to attack your opponent by adding "attack weights"
to the enemy side of the scales in which you win by making theirs fall completely down.

The gameplay doesn't end there as it incorporates a sort of puzzle in the game room where you play against some psycho eyes who may or may not enjoy taking pictures. Your ultimate goal is to figure out a way to break the cycle of being forced to play the game then being killed.

Sorry if the review is shoddy, but I'm writing this review to give Inscryption credit because I am honestly impressed by the high quality and a sort of revival of decent card games over the years. (Screw Seedlock)

I highly recommend you play this game before possibly spoiling yourself, it gets surreal real quick.

Have a nice one my dudes.

Update: I just finished the game.

I have to say that this game is way more META than my initial impressions led me to believe. Just like the developer's previous game, Pony Island, that has a sinister plot underneath its coding. The "found footage" acting is how you'll be mostly exposed to the overall lore of Inscryption. It just so happens to be complemented by the cool secrets that you can find as you play through each act. My favorite secrets are in Act 1 with Leshy because most of them have an impact on gameplay that makes the roguelite easier.

The one issue I have with the game is its last act because it drastically changes the gameplay in a way that sorta screws you early (especially if you didn't play with such card mechanics earlier in the second act). It takes a bit to get better card upgrades which can turn the third act from being a bit annoying to a joke.

10/10 Well crafted than most AAA games now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are too many perspectives to approach this game, and that is the problem.

From a simple perspective::

If you take a linear aproach to the game, it's overall SHORT, there's too much content for ACTS, which ends too quickly.
There are a wide variety of cards and trinkets that you can use for different purposes throughout the ACTS, but in the end, the "card game" is pretty easy to beat.

From the complex's point of view:

Half the game feels like it was made for Easter Egg and Achievement hunters.
I'll be damned. All I could think throughout the entire experience was:
- DEV MUST BE G@Da@!m "CICADA" FAN
I bet I only found 20% or less of the hidden stuff. There are hidden secrets, very hidden secrets, and apparently ones that I can only find as an ultra mega takes community 6 months secrets. (^$##@@e.g. the floppy CTRL -C trick %#@$%*wink, wink*)

Overview
- It's a game for people who enjoy multiple extensive replays.
- The best acts are too short to use all that you gather.
- Replay value feels like it was only made for achivement/puzzle seekers. Gameplay Falls flat out boring and repetitive compared to other games like Slay the Spire, since this game was made to be replayed.
- The story is good. Very unique. With many creepypasta vibes.
- Soundtrack is OK to mehh. Very far from top notch soundtracks for low budget games like Undertale.
- Gameplay = Tantalising for the first playthrough.

Final Opinion:
I feel like the developers did too much for too little. I'd rather wait 2 to 3 weeks to read a forum thread or watch a 15 to 30 minute youtube video that outakes all the important hidden messages and secrets, than play a repetitive card game for 1 to 3 months just to re-visit to TRY AND FIND HIDDEN STUFF.
I bet the hidden content and endings will be very cool and fun to watch, minus to hard to seek factor.

The gameplay has key elements from a mix of very cool games such as Slay the Spire, Legends of Grimrock, The Witness, random creepypasta games, etc. It's unlike anything I have played before. But falls short for "Casual Replayability" at it's current market price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is good, really really good, ESPECIALLY the beginning. The gameplay is tight and very fun.
I'm going to focus on the story, which I have a lot of mixed feelings about. This game is split up into three very distinct acts, with completely different aesthetics, while the gameplay remains the same. Going in blind, I assumed that the game was only act 1- the first act of the game is all that is shown on the store page. Upon reaching the end of act 1, I felt satisfied. The fact that there was MORE beyond that felt MIND BOGGLING. it was an exciting "Oh ♥♥♥♥!!" moment.

Unfortunately, the game beyond what you see on the store page is really disappointing. Part of it has to do with the (extremely cheesy & poorly written) story, and part of it has to do with the map layouts past Act 1. Act 1 works so well because of the nature of the maps- The way you choose which nodes to move to can influence your deck composition greatly, and theres a lot of strategy in regards to picking where you want to go. Its very rogue-like, and I think on its own, Act 1 of this game could've been fleshed out to be its own independent, rogue-like experience. The picking-a-path aspect of gameplay is removed entirely after Act 1. Navigating overworld maps becomes a chore instead of an interesting choice. Acts 2 and 3 are completely railroaded, with rewards given to you at predetermined points. They fix this somewhat by providing frequent shops that allow you to make SOME choice to customize your deck, but they aren't a replacement for the lackluster overworlds. (There ARE a few moments in Act 3 that are really good, but they're buried underneath a slog of boring map navigation.)
The story past Act 1 is really disappointing too. You'll understand what I mean when you watch the youtuber videos for the first time. The acting is terrible-- also: who films card pack openings with their full body in view of the camera?! people who make card pack opening videos on youtube usually only have their hands in frame, with the cards clearly in view for the viewers. They don't care about the youtuber, they care about the CARDS. aararrgg it just felt like the developers have never even watched pack openings before.
That tangent aside, where Act 1 left me feeling content and satisfied, Act 3 left me feeling wanting more. Its weird, they had a perfect point to end the game at, and they kept going, but then they stopped WAY too early after they kept going. I had an expectation that I would be able to play full campaigns against each of the various narrator-like characters, but I was disappointed to discover the game ENDS after you complete only One of them.
Additionally, the "deep dark secret" that the game hides from you is never revealed. After all the hype, I was expecting some sort of tangible revelation. Not divulging what the "Old Data" was a real let down. Its Implied to be some sort of demon or spirit but the lack of details about it is extremely disappointing. You can't have a mystery and just leave it unsolved-- thats a cop-out ending. it sucks.

Do I recommend this game? Yes. The first act is wonderful, the atmosphere is amazing, and the story is interesting! However, they never bother to answer the interesting questions that the plot raises, and it just left me wondering "why did this game have to keep going if they were going to just blue ball me in the end like that?". If you want to experience a really ♥♥♥♥♥♥ haunted video game cartridge creepypasta story that feels like it was written by a 13 year old, keep playing past Act 1.\t\t\t\t\t
