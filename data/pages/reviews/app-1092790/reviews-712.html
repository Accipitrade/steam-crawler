\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe base game is fantastic. Honestly only complaint for the story is that it wasn't longer. Just a ton of fun filled with surprises and great gameplay mechanics.

However, I have to say, Kaycee's Mod needs a lot of work.

Before anyone starts saying "Roguelikes are supposed to be based on RNG" and all that hemming and hawing to defend Kaycee's Mod, the game mode is just completely unbalanced. Sure, RNG plays a part in many rogue likes. "Slay The Spire" can rely heavily on RNG. However, there is also a fair amount of skill involved. And even in losses in STS, I always felt like I learned something, or got closer to winning the game.

Kaycee's Mod does not feel that way at all. There are certain rounds where you are just going to lose. From the get go that match will be a loss because of the cards you draw and what Leshy is going to play. Relying so heavily on card draw luck is not fun. And also the fact that to unlock more content, you have to keep making the game harder on yourself is infuriating.

I have played Kaycee's Mod for nearly ten hours now, and I have only won once. And so many of my losses feel like utter BS because there was no way I was going to win because of RNG. Skill takes almost no part in the mod. It is just purely RNG luck. That isn't fun to play.

It is just sad that the fun of the story mode is sucked away from Kaycee's Mod. When really, Kaycee's Mod should be about making that fun last as long as possible.

Kaycee's Mod needs heavy balancing work. Until then, I don't think I will be playing it anymore. Which is unfortunate because of how much I loved the story. And the story is worth the price of the game alone. Just don't expect Kaycee's Mod to give you that same fun. Instead expect more frustration.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSomewhat spoilers ahead:

I absolutely loved the first act of this game and binged it right away. Hooked me immediately and I couldn't stop playing...just one more duel, one more map...
It was fun to encounter new tactics with the wooden figurines and play combos with the different effects of the cards. The little escape-room-like riddles and tasks on the side were a nice addition and I also enjoyed them.
Then after some progression act 2 starts and for me that part basically equalized the strong start of the first part. The love I have for the first part, the hate I have for the second part. Although I'm generally fine with pixel art games and also enjoyed Pony Island in the past, I found this transition very unfitting. To make things worse I found the gameplay of the second part nothing but annoying and tedious.

Disclaimer: I took the Undead-Deck, so I don't know how the experience would be with other decks. The bare basics like sacrificing animals and/or collecting bones was kept, but tons and tons of new mechanics were implemented, without really introducing or explaining them. Just deal with it. Mox? Circuits?

With my Undead Deck I didn't even use half of the available Undead cards (which really aren't that many), simply because they were just too expensive and I'd be beaten thrice before I'd get a 7 Bones card out. Even getting out a 1/1 Zombie for 2 Bones could be challenging from time to time. So instead of playing thoughtful and following a tactic like in the first part of the game, the second part was to me like doing the same fight for ~15 mins again and again, until I simply got lucky and drew cheap, playable cards in the beginning, so I could just rush the enemy with 0 cost skeletons, which generate bones after the turn. That wasn't a challenge, enjoyable or thoughtful, but instead just waiting for the right rng.

The second part basically drained all the excitement and fun I gathered during the first act and when the third act started I was like...meh. It's definitely better than the second part. It's worse than the first part. My motivation is lost anyway. I doubt ,that I will finish this game.\t\t\t\t\t
