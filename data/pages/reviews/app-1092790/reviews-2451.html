\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is an interesting and unique game, but upon finishing it I felt like the concept was stronger than the execution. It's hard to say much without getting into spoilers, but I'll do my best to give an overview without ruining some of the twists.

At its core this is a very solid card game/puzzler that goes through various transformations, breaking itself down into three "acts" that are all distinctly different in the way they play. While the gameplay is consistently fun, the visuals wonderful, and the atmosphere dreadfully spooky, it suffers a lot from the fact that the first act is by far the most engrossing and well-developed part of the game. The tight, challenging gameplay and intriguingly straightforward premise of the first act give way to less robust card games and a plot that focuses more on asking spooky questions than giving the player compelling answers.

While the concept of Inscryption is very cool, by the time the credits rolled I was left feeling somewhat disappointed with the story. It never really evolves beyond the premise of its initial twist, which in itself I found less intriguing than the starting scenario of playing cards with an old man in a mysterious cabin.

Marrying card games with adventure game puzzles is an awesome idea, and I have to praise Inscryption for its novel concept and beautiful presentation if nothing else. Unfortunately I feel like the game's execution doesn't live up to its ambition. It's full of clever tricks and surprises, but there's not much depth to them at the end of the day. As much as I hate to say it, I think Inscryption has more style than it does substance. I felt like the game desperately *wanted* to blow my mind, but it never managed to deliver any of the real gut-punch moments that similar titles have managed.

All in all I do recommend the game, because novel experiences like this are few and far between. I adore games that are unique, creative, and unafraid to take risks. If you're looking for something that'll surprise, engross, and unsettle you, then I thoroughly recommend this one. Just be aware that the start of the game is by far the strongest part, and the payoff may leave you somewhat disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
