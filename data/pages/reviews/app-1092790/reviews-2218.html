How to describe Inscryption?

I was sold (by other players, not the dev) that it would be a Slay the Spire style horror game. I didn't get horror. What I did get was a cast of characters that, despite having limited means to communicate, were fully fleshed out. To be fair, some-- or rather, most-- were ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, but you know what they wanted and how they felt about getting there. At no point in time could I have guessed where the plot was going, but every twist made hints that were previously given make sense. Nothing seemed to come out of left field as it was happening or in reflection afterward. In addition, the meta plot to the game gave just enough information to create a tantalizing series of questions. So much so, that, after the end of the game, my partner and I spent hours digging through the Discord and subreddit to understand it.

This was a cheap game, but I could have paid $100 and it still would have been worth it.

10/10, absolutely recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

BUT
