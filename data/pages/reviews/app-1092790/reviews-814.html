\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is one of those games people refuse to actually talk about, for fear of spoiling the experience. I kept hearing and reading vague descriptions about how great the game was and how it was best experienced not knowing anything at all about it. The thing is, an 11 hour hour playthrough later, I'm still not entirely sure what's going on.

The gameplay loop is mostly a really fun, easy-to-learn hard-to-master card game that's fairly quick to pick up and can get in-depth fairly quickly. Throughout the game; mechanics are regularly switched up and the gameplay never has a chance to get stale.

Then on top of the card game, you can get up from the table you're sat and look around the small room you're in. Then, just as you think you have an understanding on what's going on... Well, you'll have to play the game and find out for yourself.

The core game is fun and interesting enough to have kept me engaged for the time I was playing. I did find the purposely low-fidelity graphic style and constant camera movements made it a little difficult to concentrate on the game for too long, which had kept me from trying the new "endless" mode they added recently.

I'm not a fan of horror in general, I will say I appreciate the lack of jump scares. The creepy atmosphere is very well done and there's a constant sense of not know where the game might go next that keeps you on edge.

I'm definitely interested in hearing other's theories about the story, there was also a number of things I know I missed which may explain things further. I wouldn't be surprised if the game was hiding further secrets too!

Overall, I would definitely recommend Inscryption, especially for fans of card games and deck builders. And also for fans of unique games. It's refreshing to be able to write a review that doesn't compare a game to similar titles, because it's true: I've not played anything quite like Inscryption.\t\t\t\t\t

First off, the card battling mechanics are well designed and fair. The story loop goes wild with different styles, so there is not much of a chance to really double down on a single strategy for the whole game. However, there is a post-game mod which provides an endless challenge mode. Even in this latter mode you unlock new cards, abilities, and items that were not in the story but you have more control in terms of structuring your builds.

The story, graphics, music, and holistic design of the game feels really solid. It is hard to go into any detail without spoilers, so I will just say do not go in expecting your first hour or two of gameplay to be the general gameplay loop. Strap in for some wild twists and turns.\t\t\t\t\t
