\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLoved the atmosphere and was curious about the storytelling events that the game was using.

Gameplay was good until it introduced a mechanic where you get to create a card using components of other cards. You'd choose the lowest cost card, the highest stat'ed card, and the most useful special ability of the selection the game offered you ending up with a low cost high power useful card.

I ended up with a card that could win every match the instant I drew it. While luck is certainly involved in getting a card that bonkers it is still pretty easy to create cards that are simply much more powerful than the base cards. I was only an hour into playing and the game was introducing cards and scenarios that I hadn't seen yet. I didn't get to see any more of them either because I drew my massively overpowered card and won the match instantly without my opponent getting to take a turn and use those new creatures.

I am a huge fan of Slay the Spire, Rouge legacy, FTL, Griftlands, I like rogue-likes, I like deck builders, I get that part of the fun of randomness in a playthrough is that occasionally you get really lucky and that run you're just gonna be stronger maybe to the point of being so strong that a normally challenging game is going to be an easy run this time. But this wasn't a combination of rare happenstances or an unlikely payout for a big gamble. It was just a card in my deck at the start of my second run that won the match the instant it was drawn. It was a zero cost 7/7 and a match is over when you deal 5 damage to your opponent. Every match starts with you playing a card that you can afford the cost of and attacking with it if it can so I want to stress that the card really was winning matches single handedly. While it might have be rare to make a card that staggeringly overpowered it doesn't need to be close to that insane to neuter the games challenge. A 1 cost 3/4 or a zero cost 3/2 or any number of good special abilities and decent stats would make the difference between a challenging run and a predetermined win.

Because I had played for so short a time I asked around to make sure I actually was understanding the mechanic, that the game wasn't going to throw me a curveball and change things up. The responses I got indicated that yes, occasionally you just get a card that is an order of magnitude more powerful and the run is over barring some clicking and reading.

It has a cool theme. The games aesthetic sure seems like it wants to put you in a place of danger against an unknowable force demanding suffering and offering harrowing challenges. Your damage tokens are teeth and if you want a bonus token a figure shrouded in permanent darkness hands you a rusty pair of pliers and tells you to go find one. That is not the environment to give me insurmountable power at zero cost. It's like James Sunderland finding the BFG9000 in Silent Hills first trash can. Both silent hill and the BFG are incredible but putting them together turns it into a joke. One I'm a little bitter to learn that I payed $15 to not laugh at.\t\t\t\t\t
