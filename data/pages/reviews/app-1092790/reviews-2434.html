On my second attempt, I made it to the second to last boss. I was pretty certain of my first assumption.

However, upon beating the "last" boss that was in the beta, I realized that was only the surface of this game. Not really a card game guy, but with how genuinely fun it is at the start, with the slow and steady encroachment of new mechanics, I got completely sucked in. Overall, definitely my new favorite card game, and my personal pick for GOTY. Can't recommend this title enough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDaniel Mullin's previous release, The Hex, was a whirlwind of a game that introduced, blended and discarded genres with abandon. So it was with justified suspicion that I wondered whether Inscryption was REALLY a card game. Perhaps that premise would be shed in the early hours as the game introduced new, weirder ideas.

I was wrong.  Inscryption has plenty of weird ideas. It dabbles in other genres and has dozens of tricks up its sleeve. But it is, at its core, all about the cards.

This could have been disappointing had the cardplay felt rote or derivative. Instead it is compelling and clever. It's the engine that powers the game's wild, twisting narrative.

Like The Hex, Inscryption is a fourth-wall-breaking game of constant escalation that expands far beyond the apparent boundaries of its premise. Unlike the Hex, Inscryption's escalation is in service of a deep and playful exploration of a single genre. Its mechanical flourishes are just as impressive as its narrative ones.

It doesn't have hundreds of hours of depth like Slay the Spire, Monster Train or other card games, but that's not what it's trying to do.  It's trying to show you as many cool things as it can as quickly as possible, a wild ride that lasts 10-15 hours before bowing out. I had a great time.\t\t\t\t\t
