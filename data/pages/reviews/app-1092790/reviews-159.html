nscryption. Inscryption Inscryption Inscryption... how i wish i could play this game again without knowing anything.

Lets start with the elephant in the room.

This game is NOT for everyone.

Act 1 completely captures you in the world of Inscryption without you even taking a step outside of Leshy's cabin. You meet all the main characters either through talking cards or a bottle that conceals their world. You do a few puzzles and by the end of maybe your fourth run, you've done all you can do in Act 1... But you're still there. Finding the eyeball in the chest was an amazing push of your creative thinking. If you NEED to use items, what else could you need to do to progress in the game. It sprinkles so much that eventually leads you to defeating Leshy and sealing him in his own card. Finding the New Game icon and being left in a room of darkness was genuinely confusing! it took me maybe 5 minutes to realise there was not another puzzle, i needed to exit the game and start a new one.

This is when it starts losing people.

Act 2 adds so much to what you already know, it's basically a new game entirely. If you're like me, you eventually find the Ouroboros and just landslide battles with skeletons only. it feels like there is less story because... there IS less to discover (at least for the scrybes). you're wrapped around the games fingers as it shows you that everything you knew about Inscryption was a lie. You never thought "what is outside the cabin" until that moment. It completely captures you.

Act 3 is when people REALLY start losing interest, deaths mean nothing, PO3 is not a good storyteller, and the bosses (the only good part about Act 3 imo) are ones that PO3 actively shows hate for, it hurts your love of the game because all you knew is being torn up by a robot who cares more about strategy and hates the simple idea of adding story to the game. it makes you miss Act 1 even more.

But in my opinion, and how I look at it, it's constantly going for that vibe. you miss what you had, Act 1 was so driven with lore and atmosphere, Act 2 at least had colour and story, but in Act 3, everything is Colourless, Bland, Basic. the only real colour used is Blue and eventually the gem colours which are barely even there. it feels so cold, while act 1 felt so warm.

The ending is phenomenal, The smug and annoying robot that you've been playing along with for the entire game is decapitated by Leshy, the one scrybe you actively grew to hate after killing you over and over again... but that's what the game wanted you to feel.

Grimora wipes the disk, and everything begins to be deleted. Grimora takes you for a game, and doesn't get to give you a boss, showing that when you do something, consequences may follow (Wiping the disk, she didn't get to play with Luke for as long as she wanted.) She shakes his hand, and then she vanishes.

Leshy's portion was a trip down memory road, just playing cards and having fun. eventually the scale is deleted. To most that would be a time to stop playing, but Leshy requests to continue. it makes you realise that all he wanted to do was play, tell a story, and after being with PO3 for hours you realise that Leshy truly wasn't a bad dude at heart, and that sometimes, even when you have not a lot of time left, you should enjoy doing the things you want. He shakes your hand, and he vanishes.

Magnificus's was a show on his character, he didn't want to go down, he fought the actions of Luke for not removing the disk, and saving everyone. because at that point, Luke knew the same thing as Grimora. there were things on that disk that needed to be deleted, never to be seen again. Magnificus continues to plead with Luke through battle, even refusing to be deleted for a short moment to try and get Luke to save him until in the end, he's too weak, even in his weakened state, and even though Luke made his choice, he requested to shake his hand. He crawls over... and then he vanishes.

From start to finish, it went from Amazing story, to Subpar story, to Terrible a story (Hiding an amazing ARG behind it.) and i think it is THE best game i have ever played.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a big adventure game fan. Nancy Drew for example, is one of my favorite point and click games of all time. Inscryption is such an interesting hybrid of point and click, grid based dungeon crawling, and deck building. I know, that combination just sounds so wacky, but it works SO WELL in this game.

I am a little late to the party in reviewing this game, seeing as it's been out for quite some time already, so I will be brief in describing the game. You are the next victim of a diabolical serial killer who forces you to play their card game. If you win, you continue to live and survive. If you lose, then you die. BUT before you die, the killer forces you to create a new card as a token for them to remember you by...and for the next victim to use to defeat them. It's such a creepy concept, but works so well in this game.

In between card games, you can get up at any point and explore the surroundings of the killer's cabin. This is where the dungeon crawling/point and click adventure aspects come into play. There's lots of strange contraptions and puzzles to interact with. I rarely see grid based dungeon crawling controls in an adventure game and honestly, it was so refreshing.

I hope that adventure game fans reclaim grid based controls for future games because it's such a fun and easy system to work with. Great movement system.

Now for the critiques:

1. Don't go into this game expecting to win and survive to the end. The nature of this game is dark. In order to capture the essence of a serial killer, the developer created a system where it will take many unfortunate victims to finally defeat them. It's triggering sometimes, and for those suffering from mental health or have been traumatized from something like this, be weary before playing this game. Developer, maybe consider putting a warning screen up just to remind people that this is a very dark game and to just be weary before starting it for the themes being presented.

2. The talking animal cards is a genius idea. I love it. It's such a cute and novel concept. I find myself doing all kinds of things to trigger new dialogue sequences between all of them. It presents a fun way of playing the card game that forces you to play against what you're used to in order to forward the story. Very clever. My favorite character initially was the Stoat, but I find myself really enjoying the Stunted Wolf. The Stinkbug is adorable. The killer themselves is interesting. All you see of them is their eyes and the masks they put on when role playing. Or in some cases, their large, hairy hands when they grab you to kill you when losing the game. I don't know if it's an artifical stolkholm syndrome going on, but there are situations where the killer can be charming in weird ways. The way they surrender if they know you'll win. The way they sometimes grant you mercy to help you learn. The killer can also be cruel and do things that go against the rules of the game, giving you that harsh reminder of the grim circumstances that you are in. If I had to provide a critique for the killer, it would be to figure out more ways to make them charming so that it messes with your emotions and makes you forget that you're in quite a bad situation.

3. The card game itself can be confusing sometimes so thank you for making the card guide so simple to use. Just right click and out pops the card almanac. I wish the card almanac would also provide descriptions for the map locations once you know what they are, just as a friendly reminder of what that map location means.

4. I'm still pretty early on in the game, and I have no idea where this game is going. Sometimes I can't tell if this game is beatable or if it's just very difficult. I know it's weird to write out, but I just can't tell if the game is truly playing against me or not. 6 hours in and there are times that I ask myself "what's the point?" and I don't know if that's a good feeling or not. I wish the game had some sort of system in place (or optional) that could help players that are just bad at card games to progress through the story without feeling burdened by the difficulty of the card game itself. The only way I could see that working is maybe offering more lives during boss battles. Or perhaps offering a lighter as an item from a backpack that could be used automatically if all candles are unlit. Kind of like a pink fairy in Legend of Zelda that revives Link if he loses all his hearts. At least then you have some hope of winning if you make a mistake.

5. There's a lot of moments that I wish I could spend bone coins on a squirrel or something. I think it would be cool if that was a possible game rule. Spend 2 bone coins each turn for one squirrel. Maybe consider that as a house rule or something that can be turned on or off. The card game is awesome, but sometimes I just get irked when my options get so limited and I have this endless supply of bone coins that I can't even use.

Overall Inscryption is a fun game. I am enjoying myself. I am late to the party with this review, but if the developers are reading this, I hope you can take this honest feedback and run with it or consider if for a sequel or future game. Thank you for this great experience. As a huge Nancy Drew game fan, this game scratches that Nancy Drew itch and I can't wait to continue through Inscryption. Thank you again.\t\t\t\t\t
