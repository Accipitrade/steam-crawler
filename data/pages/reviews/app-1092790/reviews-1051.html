\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDaniel Mullins has learned a lot since Pony Island. I enjoyed that game when I first played it, but in hindsight its messy story and over-reliance on being meta drags it down a bit. However, Inscryption does this much better. This is definitely because of its stronger sense of immersion within the world of the game. Pony Island has this constant border around it to remind you that you're playing a game within a game. Instead of this, Inscryption simply establishes that the game you're playing is being recorded and that is it; it allows you to be immersed without breaking that immersion (for the most part) and it does it brilliantly. I'd rather not go into spoilers so I'll keep this short. If you're looking for a good spiritual successor to Pony Island with great gameplay and a fantastic soundtrack, this is the game for you. 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t
