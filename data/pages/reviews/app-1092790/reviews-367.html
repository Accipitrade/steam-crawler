\r\n\t\t\t\t\t6 hours to unlock the game, 14-15 to beat. The earliest part of the game is purposefully unfair, so tough it out until you unlock a few things and it'll get easier to manage. When I say "unlock the game", I mean there's a second act that unlocks a lot more mechanics, which was refreshing since the first section does drag on a bit too long. I don't really like card games, but the variety of cards and abilities kept it interesting. As your play through the board game aspect, you can choose different paths to alter your deck by combining, boosting, removing, and gaining cards. All of this combined makes for a fun game, along with the storyline that helps keep you engaged.

And of course this dev has a particular style which is always fun to experience, if you're unaware then just commit to buying this game or one of his other games, they are all highly recommended. About those other games, Pony Island was light on mechanics with limited depth but a story that really drew you in. The Hex had more mechanics but each held little depth, but a story that drew you in. Inscryption has 1 mechanic with a lot of depth, and a story that doesn't pull quite as well but is still good and helps break up the monotony that card games can turn into.

The rest that I have to say below is about the free expansion (Kaycee's mod), basically I don't recommend playing it unless you really really like the early game. It ends up revealing the limitations of the core mechanics, also it doesn't include the expanded mechanics of the later parts of the game which feels a bit wasted.
The mod is so brutal that it will make you realize how unfair the base game is, don't play it unless you are really into card games or know how to build decks. I lack these skills, I don't like card games much at all, so maybe you'll have a different experience. I just didn't realize that the advantages granted to you in the regular mode have such an impact, and really cover up the lacking core mechanics. Granted I might just be extraordinarily unlucky, for example I've had runs where 4 out of 10 cards in my deck were OP and could single-handedly win me a fight, but somehow in 4 to 5 battles in a row I won't draw a single OP card. This has happened in at least 3 runs in a row, is it on purpose? You can't cheese your way to victory in this mod, even though that's how you are able to beat the regular game? Honestly it's managed to reduce my glowing opinion of the game overall.\t\t\t\t\t

my playtime isnt accurate btw, for some reason it didnt track playtime when i was using a mod manager so i have like twice that i think since after i beat the game the first time (and second time, since i wanted to go through again to find all the secrets) i played with mods until the kaycee's mod update released

also here's a few tips if you are just starting out:
-value is great, but a smaller deck is often better than a large one full of good cards as that increases the consistency of your draws
-getting a game over in the first part of the game isn't a bad thing, and in fact, you might consider throwing the game to intentionally die a few times once you make some progress, to make future runs easier
-geck is legitimately one of the best cards in the game, don't underestimate it
-same with ouroboros (in case the hint the game gives you about it isnt clear, every time it dies it gets stronger forever, not just for that game, like if you pick it in a future run it will retain the stats it had, if you slap fecundity (the field mice sigil) on it you can sacrifice it over and over and get a 2-cost 100/100)
-in the second part of the game, there is no maximum deck size, the 30/30 it says in the deck menu is just the minimum number of cards for a valid deck, stall tactics are viable if you can get enough cards)\t\t\t\t\t
