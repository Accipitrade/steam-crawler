Surprising!

First: There's no way to explain why this game is good without spoiling it. I'm glad other reviewers made that clear to me, so I'm doing the same to you.

I'm just going to say Inscryption is not just a card game.

Also, the only other game I have ever played which did something similar to Inscryption was Cliff Empire -- and I'm only saying this because, if you check that one out, you'll have absolutely no idea what I'm talking about. If anything, it's just going to make you very confused. But those developers deserve praise for the same initiative, too.


Second: Inscryption is a good game, yes. "Surprising," as I entitled it. But 50k+, overwhelmingly positive reviews might be a bit of a stretch. Not that the game's developer does not deserve it for pulling something like this off on his own, but imagine it actually has 3k, 92% positive reviews and you'll get a fairer, less prone-to-disappointment idea of what you are getting into.

The stuff I cannot spoil for you is exactly what is responsible for the discrepancy between the game's current rating and the one it would, otherwise, probably have gotten. So, do go into the game without knowing anything about it.


Third: Some of Inscryption's story is revealed in a bit of a cheesy way. You can't really blame the developer for it -- if he had hundreds of millions of dollars to spend, he wouldn't have to bother going through the massive amount of work it takes to make a game like this.

You'll know what I'm talking about when you get to it. And what I actually wanted to say is: see everything because, if you don't, you'll be curious later and there's no way to do so again after you've moved on.


Fourth: You may be disappointed at first, after playing for 2-4 hours. If you find youself asking, "Uh... that was it?" rest assured: no, it wasn't. Keep playing. As you can see, I played Inscryption for 15 hours, and that is how long it took me to actually finish it, once.


Finally: The game is overpriced.

I can appreciate how much work goes into making a game. I can. And yes, the guy who made Inscryption deserves your money. But he got a little greedy.

I paid $50 for this game (read US$50 if you are American, same thing). In the US, I'm guessing it costs about US$15 or so.

Pay US$10 for it, if you can. That should give you an appropriate bang-for-buck.\t\t\t\t\t
