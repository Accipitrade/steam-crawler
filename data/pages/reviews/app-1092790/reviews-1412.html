\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort Review:

This game may look fodder for youtubers, but it's an awesome play.
Inscryption is a really solid game that is very enjoyable to play through, even though its second half is somewhat lackluster. It was absolutely worth the price. The only really bad thing about the game is how in love with itself the story becomes later on, pushing a sort of boring "creepy-pasta" vibe that just never connects in a satisfying way to the excellent gameplay and characters. Be prepared to binge it if you buy it - my girlfriend forced me to start playing it and I spent my whole weekend on it.

------------------------------------------------

Long Review:

This game changes dramatically as you play through it, progressing through 3 major gameplay types. The first (and longest, spanning a good portion of the game) was incredibly compelling, with fantastic, satisfying gameplay and an tense atmosphere that made me want to continue playing nonstop. At this stage, the game is simple, fast, and brutal - winning is exhilarating, and losing is frightening.
The second phase of gameplay is less exciting - there's fewer consequences, and a much more simple environment. It does play with the gameplay in really neat ways that were pretty neat to work through.
The third and final part is quite fun, but the story really starts to go off the rails here. The characters carry it home right at the end, though, and completing this portion is interesting just to see how everyone ends up.

I didn't expect much out of this game, but I got quite alot. It seems to have a "Markiplier/Jacksepticeye" vibe to it at first glance that turned me off from it (no offense if you enjoy their stuff), but as soon as I started playing, I was blown away with how addictive and intrigued I was. Not many games can claim to have such fantastic gameplay and atmosphere at the same time, and the way the game initially trickle-feeds you story tidbits without ever showing the man behind the curtain was, legitimately, amazing. Unfortunately, once you have an idea of what's going on, the game becomes much less interesting from a story perspective. The characters are great, and the game is well written, but there's a few major goofy choices (such as live action videos by a previous player) that just drag the game down. If the game hadn't tried so hard to force this weird meta-plot onto the player, this would be a legitimate A+ game for me. The games not bad by any stretch, but I was definitely rolling my eyes every now and then after the first half.

The ending is something I'm still not sure not sure about- the characters who were less fleshed out suddenly get alot of spotlight, and not getting to know them until right at the end left me with a melancholic feeling. I think, honestly, that it was a good way to end it - I just haven't gotten past missing out on them yet, haha.

All in all, great game. I'd totally recommend picking it up. At least do yourself a favor and check out the demo. Just don't buy the game on a worknight unless you want to go in real, real sleepy.\t\t\t\t\t
