\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot going on in this game and all of it is amazing. If you like card games, there's a lot to dig into here. The mechanics are incredible and very in-depth. For the first part of the game, it's like Slay the Spire meets Yugioh with the general aesthetics of a dark fantasy D&D campaign. Later on, the mechanics adapt and evolve, taking influence from Magic the Gathering among other things.

HOWEVER, if you're here for a roguelite, you might be slightly disappointed after a while - the gameplay drastically changes after a little while (you'll know when it happens) and moves away from the roguelite concept (though once you complete the rest of the game you can go back and continue the roguelite section all you want). Here's hoping that the devs add an infinite mode, or even (🙏🙏🙏) a DLC that adds additional bosses.

The game is absolutely incredible overall, but I am far from the only person who would easily pay for a whole other game based around just the initial roguelite section - that's just how good it is and how much potential it has. (DLC WITH ADDITIONAL BOSSES PLEASE 🙏🙏🙏🙏🙏🙏🙏🙏🙏🙏🙏🙏) I had just under 30 hours at the end of my first playthrough, and after going back to get all achievements and see most secrets, I've just kept going through the roguelite mode because it's fantastic.

And for those who are looking less for the card game than everything else about it - the story, the characters, the world-building, the ARG elements, and so on - all of that is similarly fantastic and tightly-written with a lot of emphasis on unfolding mystery. The characters in particular deserve special mention - the main characters are incredibly complex, and the entire cast is very fun.

TL;DR: Great game with excellent story for normies, incredible game with incredible replay value for roguelite deckbuilder enjoyers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is amazing.

The Story is definitely the main selling point, it's well told and fun to discover, especially the secret dialogues
Meanwhile Escape Room extras awesome and enjoyable.

The only critique I have is the Deckbuilding. It is the only 9/10 in this game, but it is also a 11/10.
Because it is not balanced (as you will see later in the story). Which means you shouldn't play this game expecting a "Slay the Spire" like deck building. Instead you can go bonkers with the cards and forge your OP Deck.

Slight Spoiler:

Your Deathcard is the key to utter OPness. I managed to create "Ultimatum" , a deathcard with 1 Blood as cost, 6 Damage and 14 health and the fly defender ability. Later I managed to sacrafice a Mantis God so that "Ultimatum" became a one shot monster.

Feel free to tell me your most ridicolous deck or card, I am quite curious.
