\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot really a fan of reviewing products or games, but this has to be the exception. Everything from the graphics to the interesting game mechanics made this entire game come together. I've personally gone through to the ending, and this game had me glued to my chair for a whole day. So after beating the game, I just had to make a review for the game

But let's start with the card battler game play. I've played a lot of card games both digitally and physically, and this game seems of a mix between Hearthstone and Slay the Spire. I reminded me of hearthstone due to the card gameplay itself having a variety of mechanics, while with Slay the Spire, you could build your card deck in Inscryption as you traversed further into the game. With the aspects of juggling your deck of cards and managing different mechanics, the card battler was a great point of enjoyment in the game for me.

In terms of puzzles, most puzzles weren't really too challenging. The game was able to provide insight and hints to help you figure them out. In the end, most of the puzzles were made to either progress the story along, or help you with more cards or items to make your runs easier.
