i wish act 1 was a lot longer, or at least, i wish the rest of the game followed the same style and kept those horror elements. after act 1, it lost a lot of the horror aspect for me, which was disappointing, as the atmosphere of the first act was amazing. i felt a lot of tension, and as i did not look much into the game before buying it, i thought act 1 was going to be what the whole game was like (or at least most of it) - and i thought the story was going to relate to leshy and the cabin.

as much as the latter half of the game shakes things up stylistically multiple times, the gameplay slowly became a bit more disinteresting to me. i will say the finale was amazing.

from my perspective, i guess i wish this was a different game than what the developers wanted, but i also think that the game given from what they wanted was executed very well. and i still enjoyed it a lot.\t\t\t\t\t

Amazing game, but not for the faint of heart (or temper)
I adore this game. The concept is stellar, the graphics are lovely, the systems for deck-building are fun, and I love going back for more bell-ringing. The escape room aspect of the game is utilized incredibly creatively, and you're greatly rewarded for solving the puzzles the room contains, not to mention the underlying plot of the stoat card. If you like puzzles, duels, D&D, deck-building, and fighting extremely tough odds and don't mind setbacks, this game would likely be a great fit for you!

Disclaimer: If you're easily frustrated or are at a low-to-moderate skill level for deck-building games (no judgement!), you probably won't have a great time with this game. It's designed to be difficult and very punishing for mistakes; you are not supposed to win easily. The game will even purposefully throw extremely strong creatures at you if you progress too quickly without dying enough (you unlock more mechanics whenever you die).

Additional disclaimer (very minor spoilers): Some items in game meant to help tip the scales (literally) are... bloody. One is a pair of pliers that rips out the player's tooth, and one is a knife that takes out the player's eye. The visuals aren't viscerally graphic (a momentary red screen and a reddened eye, as well as a partial loss in vision), but if you need to, brace accordingly.\t\t\t\t\t
