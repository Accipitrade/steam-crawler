\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm only a few hours in, but I'm really enjoying this game so far!

Edit:

I've finally beaten the game, so can give a better review of it. I had finished playing through Pony Island and shortly afterwards saw the announce trailer for Inscryption. I haven't played many deck-builder games, and they typically aren't games that I seek out to play. I don't specifically dislike them, but there are just other genres I'd typically rather play. Despite this, I thought Inscryption looked interesting.

I was happy to see that there was a demo available for the game, which I played as far as it would let me and really enjoyed.

I finally bought the game during the Steam Autumn sale and beat over a period of two days. i had assumed that things would get weird in the game from the trailer, and it would swap away from being a deckbuilder. This is sort of what I was hoping for - minimal deckbuilding game and "weirdness" as was the case with Pony Island. The game DOES remain a deckbuilder throughout, but I was totally okay with that. I'm watching my friend stream it on Discord, and it's making me want to play through again just for the card mechanics.

As I mentioned previously, I enjoyed what I played of the demo, but the full game exceeded even those expectations. Definitely in my top 5 games of the year!\t\t\t\t\t
