\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[Edit 12/14/2021] The Developer is adding in a free DLC for an endless roguelike mode, I can now in good conscience recommend this game wholeheartedly. I have no issues with this game, it's fantastic and you should buy it now. [End Edit]

Inscryption is a very difficult game to review. While I do think it's absolutely worth the price, and thus a recommend, I find myself with mixed feelings about it. The beginning of the game is an absolute masterpiece of ambiance, roguelike addictive gameplay and intriguing premise. There really is nothing to say about Act 1 that isn't a glowing recommendation. It's fantastic. The premise is very simple, the card game is based around sacrificing creatures to call more powerful creatures, but there's more too it than initially promised. You start to question your actions when you realize some cards have very literal souls and speak with you. It challenges your own personal morality, dealing with the urge to survive at the expense of others. And then you'll die, a lot. But because it's a roguelike, that's exactly what's supposed to happen. On death, you get to make a new card using pieces of other cards you collected, as well as having a few more tricks up your sleeve to do better the next time.

It's unfortunate, then, that Act 2 and 3 take a very different approach. I will not say Acts 2 and 3 are bad, far from it, they are very creative in their own right. I will say, however, that there are some people who will feel a bit cheated when they realize how different the game is every act. I have to tread a very thin line here, because it's difficult to avoid spoilers. The story and lore of the game is certainly interesting, however I feel like Act 2 and 3 essentially remove replayability that could have been in this game. I had the most fun just deleting my save data just to see if I could avoid any deaths. While all three acts continue the deck building element to the game, they do not keep the roguelike element to it, and I feel that's a real shame.

With that being said, the game is far from bad. It's quite delightful, in fact. But I do think there was just a little bit lost from the later segments. I would, without hesitation, buy a DLC for this game which added more roguelike features to Acts 2 and 3 in some sort of endless mode or something. So, if you're buying it for a card game roguelike, maybe think twice about buying it if you'll take offense at losing it all when the game changes on itself. Otherwise, the game is absolutely good, and I would recommend giving it a try.
