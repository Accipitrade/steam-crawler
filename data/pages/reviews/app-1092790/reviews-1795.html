Gameplay: 8/10
Atmosphere: 10/10
Story: 10/10
Music: 9/10

Final score: 9.5/10

Positives:
If you liked the demo, you will LOVE the game. It's full of strategy, puzzles, and a LOT of unexpected surprises. There are several twists that will keep you not knowing what's going to happen next, but the mystery will be so tantalizing that you will have no choice but to press on. It's incredibly creative and can be downright hilarious at times. Definitely the type of game for almost everyone. This game really does keep throwing new content at you right until the very end. The only major warning I would like to provide is that this game is essentially a time machine. The first day I picked it up I immediately started playing when I got home from work and only realized it was time to stop when I looked at the clock and noticed that 8 hours had passed...and then I kept playing for another hour. Amazing stuff.

Negatives:
All that being said, the game is not quite perfect. It comes very frustratingly close to a 10/10 game for me, but it falls just short in terms of balance. The game does unfortunately have a balance problem in that sometimes you will be completely unable to win with the cards you've drawn. Most of the time when you lose it's because you overlooked something massive, but that will never erase the frustrating of playing flawlessly and simply having terrible cards. The game also seems to try to compensate for this by knowingly giving you some unnecessarily busted options that can eventually be summarized as "♥♥♥♥ off, I win". The game seems to prioritize story higher than gameplay concepts which is a bit of a tradeoff and may be the root of this problem. This is as far as I'll be able to go into detail on it without spoiling it though so I would advise you to buy this game while keeping this in mind.

Conclusion:
I accidentally left the game open while I was asleep so my hours aren't entirely accurate, but the fact that I've played 30 hours of this game will tell you that I had a great time. This game is well worth the money and if you've watched the trailer and are looking for a sign to buy it, this is it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: After finally finishing the game, I'm coming back to edit my review based off the WHOLE experience. (I'll leave the original below for posterity).

So, the first act of the game is phenomenal. Sound design, ambiance, gameplay, all of it. It's a super fun section, and I genuinely enjoyed playing it. My only regret was spoiling it for myself and accidentally finishing the act before I wanted to as a result. Act two? Still not my favorite act. It's considerably easier towards the end of the act with the help of overpowered cards, but the first two bosses are like pulling teeth and it's incredibly frustrating to get past the starter deck (terrible cards!!). Not to mention, some of the decks are considerably more useless and weak than the others, and the sudden introduction of so many mechanics all at once is SUPER overwhelming. The segue could have been smoother. A lot smoother, actually. The segue is nonexistent.

Act 3 was really good! I've been seeing others say it's not as good as act one, but admittedly I think the third act is a strong contender for the first. The aesthetics are kind of an eye strain, but the new gameplay mechanics in it are actually really fun to work with. No more instant wins in one turn, and (usually) no more instant losses from a particularly bad pull. The bosses in act three have better mechanics than in the first act though, no question. Its a fun and interesting challenge without it really feeling unfair like some of the bosses in the first act (act 2 is the WORST about unfair boss mechanics though).

The story is admittedly very confusing and open ended, but I think the open ending is intentional. And, despite some pretty frustrating moments in this game (looking at you, act two), the ending actually managed to make me feel bad for these characters in this silly little card game. I wasn't expecting something quite as deep, but it really caught me off guard. It's a really good ending, frankly, bittersweet and still (of course) maintaining the Horror Aspect.

I think, after experiencing the full thing, it IS worth the money... so long as you're willing to suffer some frustration to get back to the good parts. There IS a light at the end of the tunnel. A smug, smug light. But it's there.
