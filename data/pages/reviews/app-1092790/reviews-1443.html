\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow. Where to start on this one. It's gonna be a short review.

Worth every dollar.

That's the TL:DR. So, the actual review. This game is best played thinking it's a card battler. Because it is. It isn't as in depth as many CCG's and deck building games I've played (most physically to be fair, not on Steam). Well, not as in depth as how many cards you get. It's moderate, but not extreme with the card selection. However, the rule set is simple to grasp, and then adds in so many new rules as it goes that it could start to overwhelm. Due to EXCELLENT game design, the overwhelming sensation doesn't last, as each new mechanic is introduced with an easy battle to learn it, then scaling up from there. The game doesn't introduce a new game mechanic, then beat you down with it. This game is paced so well and keeps you playing it until the end.

So the Story. It has a story. It has a great story. It's debatable how much of that story your allowed to understand, and how much is left cryptic, and that was my only downside to this game. It does NOT explain itself well toward the back third of the game. That isn't to say I didn't like it. I loved the story (that you should definitely not look up before you play this. Go in blind) but right when I thought I was getting answers, I realized I was getting NOTHING close to answers. To be fair, they wrap up your story, but the deeper sense of rabbit hole is just way too much left wanting answered. It's hard to write this without spoiling, and again your better off just playing it to see it for yourself. The story is great, the game is great.

I got 20 hours out of this game (remembering I play slow and love savoring the game). I expect someone could probably do a first run in 10 if they full throttle through it. Still, for the price, what you're getting is a great story with some truly impressive twists and turns in both story plot and game play. This game stays fresh right until the end, and if it weren't for the big reveals in the third act leaving me with more questions than answers, i'd say there wasn't a bad part of this fantastic game.\t\t\t\t\t
