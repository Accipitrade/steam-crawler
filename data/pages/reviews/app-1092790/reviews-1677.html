Avoid any media or review coverage of this game until you played the game yourself. Its great and worth your time. I tried to write this review as spoiler free as possible but its still really hard to do.

Where do I even start? This might be the hardest review I have ever had to write. Even purely story driven games would often allow me to just talk about the overall game and presentation, but here even something as simple as that is problematic. The simplest and most complex thing I can say about Inscryption is that its an incredible art piece that uses the gaming medium to its full potential. It really does feel like there is no other way that this piece of art could have been conveyed, and as a result it turns into something truly unique. Something that is absolutely worth your time.

Well lets just start with the most basic part of any game I guess. Gameplay, from what I can say about the gameplay, its a tabletop card game at its absolute most basic. But the game gives you more freedom then you would expect going into it, right off the bat you can walk away from the game and just walk around the table. Even without all the small story and secret things to see around your table, its still so incredible how the developers managed to create such a heavy feeling of just walking around, it feels incredibly creepy and unsettling, like you are in control but at the same time you are not in control. The actual card game gameplay is fun and addicting but can get a bit repetitive at times, as far as card games go its overall really simple.

So what about the story and presentation? I sadly can't go into much detail but I will say this, its an absolute blast and once you get started the game sucks you in and really makes you constantly want to get back into it. I dreaded going to sleep because I wanted to play that badly. The presentation of it all is haunting and leaves a deep impression. And the story is devastating, I will say that for anyone who is squeamish this might not be the best game for you. The whole game is incredibly creepy and without going too deep into spoilers the game also has some incredibly heavy moments.

So did I enjoy this game? Of course I did, I loved it. But I still feel like a lot of potential got wasted, the game clearly has a ton of work put into many different aspects. Its such a beautiful, atmospheric and fun piece of art. And yet I just feel lost, it feels like there is supposed to be much more then there is right now. I only have a couple of achievements left but none of them seem to be story related, its going to be sad seeing the 100 % achievements on this game and having to put it away. And that honestly could be the biggest compliment I can give to a game, if it gives me so much sadness for finishing it just because I want to play more then you know this is a damn good game. Absolute recommend to almost anyone, even if you aren't the biggest fan of card games. The only people I think should avoid this are people who really can't deal with creepy or scary things, the atmosphere in this game can be absolutely terrifying, like someone is always watching you. But if you are willing to give this game a shot I guarantee you will be blown away by the incredibly quality and love put into it.\t\t\t\t\t
