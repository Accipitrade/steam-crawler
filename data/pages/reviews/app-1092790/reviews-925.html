\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo. I just finished the game - about 20 hours for an immersed full run with room for mistake -- and I have to say, this game is phenomenal: simply a masterpiece.

I came for the core gameplay - juicy, tit-for-tat card exchanges and rewarding deck building -- but this game is SO MUCH MORE. I stayed for the wonderful storytelling, the dark mysteries, the multimodal presentation, the creepypasta atmosphere, and the nuanced characters. Suspend your disbelief, don your finest headphones, and be prepared to be checking the dark corners of your home with hairs on end, as you lose yourself in a chaotic and mystifying world.

I was genuinely elated and speechless numerous times throughout the game, whether from the engaging plot, or the challenging gameplay. Avoid watching let's-plays or reading articles etc. if you want an unforgettable first time experience. This game is one of the best I have ever played. It has earnt an immediate and secure place in my heart. It invigorated for me that sense of near tearful euphoria that Ocarina of Time (for instance) ignited in my childhood; that lives with me today.

If you like trading card games, creepy vibes, compelling story, immersive soundtrack, and a consistent but varied journey, then I can guarantee you will not regret purchasing this game: This is one of those rare purchases I hold absolutely no remorse for. For me, this was perfect.

PS. I see people in threads saying they are unsure as to how to progress through the game? My advice is to just play - win or lose the game will guide you along with story cues -- don't be afraid of dying. This game is a masterclass in game design IMO. Just let it show you.
It can be difficult in parts - be it the puzzles or card matches -- everyone's mileage will vary. However, I suspect many here welcome the challenge, and if not, perseverance is key. If you get lost just talk to NPCs, retrace your steps, etc.\t\t\t\t\t
