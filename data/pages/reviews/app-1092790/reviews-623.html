\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like creepy cabin / lost-in-the-woods horror and roguelike deckbuilders, then you'll probably like this game. If that's not your favorite style or your favorite genre, then it's hard to see what the appeal is here. The card game at the core of "Act I" is competently designed, but ultimately somewhat simple; each card has an attack rating, a health rating, and perhaps a special ability or two. The only truly novel mechanic is that you typically must sacrifice your weaker creatures in order to cast your stronger creatures; unlike Magic or Hearthstone or Slay the Spire, there are sharp limits on how much currency (blood, in this case) you can save up before you have to start funneling the currency into innocent woodland creatures which you will then brutally murder in order to summon wolves and spiders.

Some of the rules of the card game are (intentionally?) unclear as a result of the gothic/horror presentation. For example, instead of a comprehensive rulebook, you get short notes written on parchment covered in blotches of ink. This is cute until you lose a game because you misinterpreted a rule and have to start over from the beginning. There is a short tutorial, but there is nowhere you can go in the game to get a clear, detailed set of instructions about how the cards' special abilities work. As with all roguelikes, you can also lose through no fault of your own, simply because you got a bad draw. Again, you must then start all over from the beginning.

I've been told that the card game is only the first of at least three gameplay modes included with this title, but I beat the final boss of the card game twice, and I was not allowed to advance any further because I had neglected to find some Macguffin or other -- instead of getting to see Act II, I was dumped yet again back at the title screen with no significant increase in my abilities. The game drops hints about how you can correct these problems, but at this point I'm out of patience. Whatever exciting things are supposed to happen in a smoky log cabin in the woods will have to happen without me.\t\t\t\t\t
