\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are purchasing this game solely for the card game elements in it, don't. Every game this developer makes has the goal of undermining your expectations and if your expectation is a single player card game, that too will be undermined.

That being said.

This game is incredible. I found myself getting attached to the characters and became excited when I started to realize what was going on and found clues shining some light on the background of the characters you meet throughout the game, both the main characters and less important ones. The card mechanics are ever evolving and keep you on your toes, and while it can become frustrating needing to re-tweak your strategies throughout it, it feels really good once you've figured it out and start cleaving through the battles.

I highly recommend this game. And if you have played Pony Island or the Hex before and enjoyed it, you absolutely will love this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very interesting and has some of the most unique storytelling through gameplay I have seen, but I can't really recommend it. I bought the game after watching maybe an hour of gameplay of the first section and thinking it would be a fun roguelike with a creepy atmosphere with some lite puzzle room element. This was true for a fairly short amount of time but it rapidly changes into a completely different style of game that differs heavily from what I was expecting.

While the plot has some interesting components to it I did not buy this game to play through a narrative I bought it for the aesthetic and advertised gameplay, both of which change rapidly if you have a decent amount of experience with rogue like deck building games. The new aesthetic and gameplay I flat out do not enjoy and I would have refunded the game on the spot if I had gotten to it sooner as it is painfully irritating to playthrough.

Finally while the story is told in a bit of an interesting way a lot of the deeper lore is done through an ARG, which I loathe, which also detracts from the experience. Additionally the story is not spectacularly interesting so much as the manner it is presented in makes it interesting, so having to dig through an ARG to get the last drops of lore and explanation makes the whole experience that much worse.

I would not pick this game up if you are looking for an interesting rogue like deck builder because that is not what the game provides.\t\t\t\t\t
