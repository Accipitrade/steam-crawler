\r\n\t\t\t\t\tby the few negative reviews, I'm starting to think y'all never played a daniel mullins game before

if you know and like his work, you know what you're in for (in that you never REALLY know what you're in for). I will say that the first act is the strongest (and 2nd's the weakest imo), but I enjoyed all of it, especially once you figure out how to bust the game wide open. Doing 21 damage on one card with 21 hp so it functionally can't die? Feels good man. Nearly one-shot the moon. But it was definitely a steep learning curve to get to that point. I appreciate the post-post game that lets you replay certain segments, lets me get that good serotonin flowing.

as for the narrative, I personally found it a little... meh? Pretty straightforward, even when you seek out the secrets. Imo, the big reveal at the end of the cyphers wasn't like.. anything I wasn't already expecting. Was not particularly invested in Luke's story. The real draw is the boss gimmicks, imo.

overall, if you're a hardcore card gamer who'll get frustrated with a card game that isn't really made to be a proper card game, you'll probably find this game frustrating. If you're casual normie trash like me--but also aren't discouraged by fail-try-fail games like darkest dungeon--you'll very likely enjoy yourself. If you're looking for a Deep Narrative ARG, hold out for a sale. I recommend this game, but only to a very specific audience.\t\t\t\t\t
