\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start this off saying I haven't fully beaten Inscryption, to some that might mean my opinion is meaningless but to my credit I have gotten to the very final act of the game and have plenty of thoughts on the game as a whole. This review will have some slight spoilers for a story that doesn't matter and game moments that actually matter.

Inscryption is an odd game, it's the first rouge-like card game I've ever seen with a fantastic style to boot. It's gameplay is fun and requires some actual skill mixed in with your overall luck of the draw. Honestly it's quite good and I'd highly recommend playing it. This is all to say, if the game was only what was advertised. The game's advertising has shown mostly the first act. To my knowledge none of the second act has been shown and while there are pictures of the third act, they don't fully represent the act in its entirety. Act 1 of Inscryption is the indie rouge like card game with an old graphical style and horror aesthetic I paid for, while acts 2 and 3 are disappointments I can't seem to get over after how wonderful the first act was. Normally I wouldn't fully mind this, even though I think when only one part of a game is good the game is deeply flawed, yet I simply can't ignore how the game drops off in quality after the first act. Not to mention the length of the first act, beatable in only a few hours. (I believe it took me 2 or 3 hours) No most of this game is not what's shown in advertising and instead a hodge podge of 3 acts all different in their own ways but with the second and third not living up to the quality of the first. The second act while interesting in its deck creation completely drops the rouge like elements and graphical style I paid $20 for, to instead become another 8-16 bit pixel styled indie game. A look so generic I can't help but find it a bore personally. The gameplay while remaining similar is just not as fun or engaging as the first act had felt. It isn't exactly bad but it's 100% not what I paid for and I was disappointed to see that the game dropped off in a way I hadn't expected. The third act slightly fixes this but comes with its own problems. The new board and the way of going about each area you explore in this RPG like card game is again not like the first act. To my knowledge, it's still not rouge like and instead intent on being an RPG maker type card game. While the gameplay is similar it's just not as good. The energy cell mechanic is just not as engaging as the Yu-Gi-Oh style sacrifices the first act shows off. It's less engaging and requires way less critical thinking than the first act had. I can't say the game ever really gets to the level of its first act ever again. I am aware you can continue replaying the first act but it is just too short to do that over and over without getting bored and beginning to long for what the game could have been. I suppose I could get into the story but honestly the acting was such a joke I couldn't even try to immerse myself in this ARG type of story. To some they might enjoy it but personally I couldn't care less. To summarize I would recommend knowing what you're getting into before purchasing as the advertising liked to hide its lesser parts away like covering a boil in makeup. I honestly can say this game while not horrible is a very big disappointment and I can only hope the developer is willing to attempt to remedy this with a sequel focusing entirely on the first act. I can't in good faith recommend this game, which saddens me quite a lot.\t\t\t\t\t
