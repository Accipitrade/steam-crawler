A love letter to TCG fans

Having the main protagonist as a trading card enthusiast... hell, even the menu options are CARDS.

The funny thing is that, at the end of the day, this is not so much of a card game. It's an adventure, puzzle game, escape room game... you name it. I fell in love with the stinkbug and the stoat from the beginning.

Despite the comparatively short play time compared to others, the game has left a very lasting impression on me. Reminds me of the motto of Game Kitchen, another small studio: "We make meaningful games". And I believe that this is what Daniel Mullins is after. Shining example of art in the videogame medium, I will call this "Game of whatever year this was released".

Clearly a lot of work was put into this. One gets a sense of seemingly endless creativity, just one surprise after another.

Do not be intimidated: this is not a very difficult game, most people plow through and beat it, even if you don't know what you are doing (like I really didn't).

I think the Steam achievements tell the story. I can see an incredibly high % of players pushing through and reaching the finale, higher than the % of players that will beat the first boss in many others. Also, the number of positive reviews should give you a pretty good hint.

As the award it won says, this game is "excellence in game design". Never too frustrating, it chokes you, but not to death.

I have played the Linux native version, full playthrough + some Kaycee's mod, no bugs no issues, all the FPS you need and more.

The downsides of Inscryption, (and this is just me trying to be picky): no multiplayer, not very long story (even though there's a lot of content condensed into it), and forget about it if you don't like puzzles.
