\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is easily the most engaging, interesting, and deceptive game of cards I have ever played.

Before reading further, I urge you to play this game raw without any knowledge from these reviews. A lot of the experience of this game is dependent on the player not knowing anything. Trust me, it's a good game.
However, if you still want a heads-up on what exactly this game is, feel free to continue. I'll keep it brief.

Inscryption likes to masquerade itself as a deck-building, rogue-like card game where your captor host plays a set of cards which you need to play your own cards to counter. over the course of a game, you may encounter new cards to add to your deck, NPCs that offer the player various services, and events which alter the flow of the game. The player only gets two strikes per area before losing the game and starting from square one again, this rule excludes Bosses where the player is forced to play perfectly if they wish to proceed. Scary as that may sound, the game rewards players that manage to maintain good play. Yes, you lose your extra life during bosses, but you also gain a special card as compensation. The game is full of moments like this, meaning every action the player makes never goes to waste.

There's SO much more to this game than I have stated here, but saying any more would spoil the experience.

Inscryption is an absolutely enthralling game, and with only 8 hours of play as of this review, I can tell I've barely scratched the surface.

Play it for yourself and see, there's a free demo waiting to be played!

Good luck, Challenger.\t\t\t\t\t
