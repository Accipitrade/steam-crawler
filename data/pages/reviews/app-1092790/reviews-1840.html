\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an incredible card game - and I dont like card games. The mechanics, and art style are amazing - and I was completely enthralled and loved it. The gritty feeling of having to play a game with a threat across the table - a dark card game mixed with escape room and good storytelling carried so hard in the BEST way. I fell in love with the game and was invested in it from start to about 5 hours in - until Act 2.

I dont know who thought it was a good idea - or who thought it was quirky, or... I dunno? Interesting? The art style goes from a grungy, horror, tense situation... to some sort of undertale looking knockoff with pokemon mechanics, having to fight several area 'masters' in card games rather than continue the horror-esque story it was telling.

The artstyle and music (that cover the advertisement sections of your steampage, with no more than a 3 second reference to act 2) completely cease, and now some awful 16 bit dreamcast styled music and graphics are now applied. This change, this utter turnaround, was enough to make me stop playing.

I was so flabbergasted by how this game 180'd that I couldnt sit through it, I had to ♥♥♥♥ off and play something else.

Whoever came up with the first act... you are a genius. Art, ambiance, style...kept me engaged and wanting more. Whoever did act 2... I dont know what to say. Please dont. Dont ever again.

**I still recommend the game** - some people probably like that kind of thing, and the first act was totally worth my time and attention. The rest of it, however... personal option, ruined the experience.

((EDIT))

I decided to take the time to read the negative reviews to see if perhaps I was the only one - This game has some of the most well written and well spoken complaints about the very same thing. People who agree the game is great - but is nothing like it was advertised, and are disappointed that what they were shown was actually the smallest aspect of the game. And honestly? Its an utter shame. Because this game was truly amazing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tgonna wander into MAJOR SPOILER TERRITORY for this one, so if you want to preserve your lack of knowledge of this game, don't read past the following paragraph. Also I’m bad at spelling so have mercy on my punctuation.

Inscription is by far one of the most controversial games I have played in a long time, and also one of the hardest games I have challenged myself to review due to its drastic changes in gameplay after the first 'act' which is absolutely nothing like the demo advertised and the countless secrets hidden behind perplexing riddles that are near impossible to understand for the target audience to understand: card game players. I honestly think one of the issues about this game, people had an expectation of what this game is supposed to be before purchasing and playing through the game. Because of this, I will be reviewing not from the perspective of the media, but as the perspective of myself, who only watched the trailer for the game and brief footage of the demo. My non-spoiler review for this game is that Inscription is a great soul-driven game, but has so many problems surrounding it that hinder me from actually cherishing this game. If that didn't make sense: I liked playing the game, but after I did a whole playthrough of the game, I don't ever see myself revisiting this silly card game.

spoiler time

I'm going to review this act by act, so major spoilers after act 1

I, whose most time spent on a card game was collecting pokemon cards for their flashy appearance as a kid and who played about 3 MTG games with a friend in my dorm building, I had no experience deck building or understanding card meta whatsoever. However, Inscription made learning not only easy, but impossibly quick, and on top of the encouraged failure at the beginning of the game to show that 'runs' in this game don't exactly equate to a hard reset. This game also has the unique feature of being able to stand up from the table you play at to uncover secrets in the cabin you play in. I loved this part of the game because the puzzles were too hard to understand and solve, but made you feel like a damn genius when you solved them.

act 2

Pretty much the same as the first act but I feel like the drastic change in meta and also having to learn 4 completely new mechanics (2 of which I don't entirely enjoy) made the difficulty extremely sharp and borderline frustrating. The secrets, however, became even more obscure and rewarding, and I managed to discover all but 1 secret which I believe involved setting up cards in your deck like another card game. This is also where you have to start to learn how to cipher and break certain codes to keep up with the lore and as someone who failed my AP compsci course, I missed a lot of important lore due to just not having that knowledge.

act 3

Act 3 returns to the same formula as act 1 but is kind of worse due to the gameplay being slightly less fun due to Po3. Also, there is one puzzle that you only can attempt twice and that really pissed me off because I didn't screenshot the solution and couldn't get up to get it due to being locked into a cutscene. Also if you made a deal with the devil in act 2 the devil will show you a secret but the footage cuts out so only Luke sees it, and honestly that ♥♥♥♥-tease of a reward for me setting up a puzzle that went through an entire act upset me. If we are playing as Luke or through Luke in this game, yet can’t see everything that he sees and does, then I feel like I’m getting half of a story, or even worse, half of a game. I did really enjoy the boss mechanics though, which really shows the unending fountain of creativity in this game.

finale

The finale is kind of quick and pitiful as you see everyone you played against in Inscription die before you but it's kind of hard to sympathize with these characters due to them being horrible to you in the past? like one character literally tortures his students by transforming them into agonizing shapes and he wants me to shake his hand after playing a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ card game with him? No thanks. Then we uncover OLD_data and Luke (not us, AGAIN) gets revealed a ton of ♥♥♥♥♥♥ things GameFuna did and then gets shot by Amanda, who presumably steals back Inscription too late due to Po3 uploading the game to the internet, or does Amanda do it because Po3 was too late??? I don't even care at this point. It's a cool idea by virtue of us playing Inscription, we released it into the public domain that the steam store is, like a modern day Pandora's Box, but I just don't feel like it was executed well due to the sloppy ending and illusion of choice in the end. Even if I passed my AP compsci course and unraveled everything in this ARG (this is my second ARG, my first was The Binding of Isaac: AfterBirth +, which made the community pay for half the game and clues to unlock the full game, which is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ ridiculous in my opinion) AND got every achievement, Luke would still be dead, and id just left with 4 more achievements and a community behind my back trying to solve the same ♥♥♥♥ I am.

conclusion

Inscription reminds me a lot of a video on youtube called Kaizo World, which I love, but I believe a game should stand on its own, and that it shouldn't need scavenger hunts to fill in major cracks in a story. Maybe as we continue this ARG will recover some corrupted footage on Luke's recorder? But I'm not that confident. Inscription from a marketing perspective did its job and made me hopeful for more, as I'm playing through Pony Island at the moment (which apparently takes place in the same universe and I should have played it first? What?), but until we get a complete story, I'll continue to have a sour taste in my mouth for this game.

So like, go play the game, it's fun until the end.\t\t\t\t\t
