\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game fills me with a special & humble kind of excitement that I once used to hold so highly for Hearthstone. Truth be told I never would've expected this would be the game to scratch that same itch that Hearthstone has left me with in recent years.

For all intents & purposes, Inscryption is made by card game fans for card game fans. The game oozes with this eerily atmospheric tone that just keeps you on the edge of your seat, a delightfully devilish combination of intrigue, touched with a dash of of suspense to top it all off; A mysterious mask-wielder narrating your journey through this DnD-like campaign, with sentient cards breathing that much more life into your roster of cards. Despite having a pretty clear Roguelite gameplay loop, every run just feels that much more different from the last.

Combat is pretty intuitive, & highlights The Game Master playing a Dungeon Master kind of role super well - you can always see what minions he is about to play a turn early (so you have an opportunity to strategize accordingly), & for that extra bit of wildlife atmosphere, sometimes there will be rocks & trees already on the board to take a few hits for you & your opponent. Health is a pretty unique concept too, opting out the idea of players having separate health pools in favor of a single health total shared between both parties, with the score being kept track of tug-of-war style.

Strategy runs pretty deep, & Inscryption encourages you to make use of its utilities to the fullest. So deep that I don't think I can reasonably get into detail about it in this review while still making it comprehensible. A TL;DR of it would be something like this: I've outright thrown a few runs simply because I wanted to try & pull off a gimmick. Did I care that I ended up losing most of those games? Hell no! I'm losing plenty of runs over here & I'm still having a blast.

Without delving into specifics (partially because of spoilers, & partially because I still don't know what to make of it), the story is a very nice touch too. Really sold me on Inscryption being, as I mentioned before, a card game made by card game fans. It uhhh, definitely goes place I never would've expected it to go.

I'd say this is both a good & bad thing. It's a good thing, in that it's a cool story that I wanted to constantly learn more about. It's a bad thing because it explores ideas & concepts I REALLYYYYYY wish were elaborated on further in the core game experience. I will talk about it in further detail because it's relevant (though contains endgame spoilers), but it is SUPER interesting that we have entire combat sections fully functioning for the Dead & Mages (even a functional game board for the Dead!!), but it's not used at all outside of their fleeting moments in the finale.

That said however, I have hope that this may not be entirely in vein, as there is a DLC mod currently in its beta stages titled Kaycee's Mod, which brings the original DnD Beast board to a more traditional roguelite, fit with challenges & the ability to unlock more starting decks/cards/etc (I still haven't been able to beat a single run of this as of writing.....). If this DLC is anything to go off of, I'd be THRILLED to see this for all 4 major boards themes. I never did feel like we got enough time with the Gems & Circuits mechanics, truth be told, & I'd love an excuse to fight the P03 bosses again!

I'm genuinely quite impressed with Inscryption as a whole. I can see me racking up some serious hours in this game, far beyond what I expected going into it.\t\t\t\t\t
