\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt took me a long time to really get this game and i'm so glad i stuck with it. I wasn't sure what it was that kept drawing me back. Partly the aesthetic, partly i think the random element is like a void that calls to me. I've always liked the idea of a card game but when i try to get into them they're too complicated and hard to actually strategize within. This one streamlines and simplifies things to a point that makes the cards easier to conceptualize, and then the game practically lets you break it in small controlled ways to make it not only fair, but unfair in your advantage...until it's not. I also like how there seem to be cards that i have that the other guy doesn't, and we play by slightly different rules (you can see his cards one turn before he spawns them in, but he doesn't have to sacrifice squirrels)
There were some specific small problems i had in the first hour or two. For starters, spoilers, i thought the 7 was a / so it took me forever to get the stink bug and i was getting pissed at the sloat for constantly badgering me about the thing when there was only one bit of the combination available to me even as i was paging through the book...and without giving too much away, after i found the other stuff in the room i felt pretty stupid but in a good way because now things are really moving.
I can still only play in bursts because of my vision. in terms of accessibility for me specifically this game gets a 3/5. +you can change the dialogue scroll speed and manually advance other dialogue, +easily recognizable sigils, +characters have different voices, -bad font, -hard-to-decipher map icons, -the woodcarving animal heads can be hard to tell apart.
overall good game really glad i got it and decided not to refund it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tso huh... I bought this game today, at 14h (2pm) when I closed it it was 20h30 (8pm30) I played this game for 6h straights...
been a while since a game "caught" me like that. I ate my dinner and pretty much went to back to finish it immediatly. the huh, plot is great and all but that have been covered in pretty much ever other reviews.

what I really want to talk about is the core gameplay, the card game. It's f'cking great. a bit on the easier side for a rogue lite deck builder but it's not unwelcome (and it will still require a good ammount of skill to overcome, you won't win by just clicking the cards, trust me I tried)
it have the perfect ammount of mechanics imho, not so much that you are overwhelm but not so little that you can't have unique strategies.
The HP is a scale and it's one of the best thing I ever saw in a card game: when a player take damage weights are added to their side of the scale, and when it tipped too much on one side (easy to visualize with a great design on the scale itself) that player loose, this mean that offense and defense are intrinsically linked and the game make perfect use of this feature.
honestly, the card game is amazing, even if you remove the plot, the atmosphere , etc... I would still be playing this game just for how good the gameplay is.
not to knock down the game ambiance, it is as great as the card game is, and I think I made my case that I love the card game.

I guess I should add some downside ? huh well the game doesn't have a setting to switch your key to AZERTY keyboard, despite offering french language option. since it's a rogue like you will die and it take maybe a bit too much time to return to the game and huh, idk there was a part where the instructions weren't really clear and I had to check online to understand (but that issue might be located between the keyboard and the seat rather than in the game...)

honestly this game is just great, my only regret is to not have bought it sooner\t\t\t\t\t
