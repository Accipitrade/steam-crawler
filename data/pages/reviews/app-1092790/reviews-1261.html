\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I had to rank this game on a scale, I'd give it about 3/5 stars. As someone who is not and never was a battle card game player this was definitely outside of my comfort zone. I tend to enjoy more puzzle adventure style games. This game however, I thoroughly enjoyed. There was just enough of a story to keep it interesting and the game mechanics never felt impossible to understand. There was good game play variety so I never felt like I was repeating the same battles over and over. There were a lot of clever puzzles as well. The reason I would not give this game 5 stars is the overall story. It was waaaay too convoluted for me. I mean, I like a story that gets you thinking. But this was just ????? Without spoiling anything, for those who like to play a game and feel fulfilled or like you accomplished something by the end, this game is not for you. For those of you who think you are good at finding game secrets and will surely figure it all out in 1, 2, or even 3 play throughs, this game is still probably not for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is really incredible. It's better to go into it knowing as little as possible, but I will say this:

As a long-time player of trading card games, Inscryption feels like a love letter to the genre while also being a fresh videogame- and horror-inspired take on them. Additionally, as a lover of cryptozoology, escape rooms, and as someone who grew up reading videogame creepypastas in the 00's, this game hit at a perfect cross-section of interests and nostalgia. I finished the game a while ago but still cannot stop playing the mod expansion, and I wish that it was a real printed TCG because I would spend so so so much money on it.

Seriously, Inscryption will hold a special place in my heart, and I cannot recommend it highly enough to anyone else who loves deckbuilders.\t\t\t\t\t
