\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't really care if this is a spoiler or not, but spoiler if you were paying for the card game based rogue-like with deck builder elements as progression, then that's not what you get. The trailers and screenshots of this game make up about ... 3 hours of this game. The initial half of this game is amazing, and exactly what I paid for. It was frustrating at times, but you very quickly start to unlock things that are intentionally game breaking in your favor, like a totem that makes your resource cards immortal, infinite, murder anything in 1 hit, etc. After a while you can create and sacrafice cards to make an insanely powerful deck.

Then the half way point happens to you and the game stops being fun.

This was a pretty decent and charming roguelike game for the first half. Though once they do their midgame switch it's just completely unbalanced, and worse yet you have zero means to overcome challenges. The first half of the game offers tools, items, and puzzles that all give you a gradually growing powerbase so that eventually you can overcome the entire run with a huge sense of power. Part 2 removes literally all of that and constantly puts you in battles you simply cant win because they refused to give you a playable set of cards. Doing a single battle over 40 times because you have no other options and have explored everything only to realize it's not playable is an amazing waste of time.

I'm only 5 hours into this game and I already have had so much frustration with how terrible the design and balancing of their suprise twist that I never want to touch this again, or any other game they make.\t\t\t\t\t

I saw someone play a tiny bit and instantly knew I was going to buy this game. It was very entertaining going through various styles of fights and deck-building. As expected, since it is a card game there is a bit of RNG that will leave you frustrated, but it is very easy to return to where you started and/or have an even stronger deck than before. There were some fun moments where I really enjoyed the mechanics, and it made the game much better.

For a few cons: In regards to the bug I have found, I often click a bit too fast that the game doesn't register what I was doing. Sometimes the controls were a bit wonky where I could not change my viewing direction that I wanted to look. Sometimes the player's hand covered some crucial information regarding the stats of the card. While these were not game-breaking and annoying to the point of not being able to play, I still found these small nuances to be noticeable.

I do wish there was a more "free-play" mode so I can just have fun with a deck and go face some challenges, but I so far do not think there is any of the sort other than replaying the entire game from the start.\t\t\t\t\t
