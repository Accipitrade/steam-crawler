\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very interesting game in all regards.

The visual style is unique and greatly lends itself to the horror atmosphere. The gameplay feels incredibly varied due to the escape room aspects (though the deckbuilding aspects seem deep enough to stand alone, particularly in part 2 of the game) and changes in style, but feels cohesive enough that you don't lose immersion because of it. However, this "chaotic-cohesiveness" is primarily due to the nature of the story, which I feel would have been better as game executed differently.

Just to be clear: the writing for this game is very good. However--

Story spoilers:
Rather than the player being the protagonist, you're really following the actions of a card-game blogger named Luke; these recordings are used as "intermissions" to explain the meta aspects of the story. While interesting as a narrative tool, these breaks made it difficult to immerse myself in the story. I feel that it would have been more effective if these sections were told from a 1st-person perspective, similar to the "escape room-esque" segments of the game.

Personally, I feel like the meta-story influenced too much of the gameplay, especially at the ending, which over-emphasized the "this is a game world"-aspect to the point of absurdity, in both the literal and philosophical sense; rather than a pensive, thoughtful ending, where the bosses talk about their mortality in a way that will stick with the player (the first two partially achieve this), it felt overly rushed and much closer to an escape sequence (which, while not inaccurate, does not create as strong of a story as there could have been).

Rather than focusing on the meta-narrative, I would have preferred more depth regarding the in-game world and the characters: more than simply just challenging the bosses, I would have liked to have had actual dialog with them. Most of the interactions felt impersonal (which I feel was due to the focus being on "it's just a game"), and so I couldn't get very attached to them--and, as a result, the ending didn't feel very impactful. Similarly, the horror behind the "OLD_DATA" is somewhat dulled due to the fact that we don't actually get to hear the whole story (which is probably intentional, in the "you-don't-want-to-know-this" sort of way; it's still unsatisfying).

I would recommend this game for the gameplay alone, though the story itself isn't bad.\t\t\t\t\t
