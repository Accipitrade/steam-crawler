Half Amazing, Half Slog, All Interesting

Light spoilers, as I talk about the overall structure of the game, but not the story.

I have been into card strategy games as of late, and I am a big Devolver fan. I'd heard about Daniel Mullins's other games and how they all get, at the very least, the "you've never see anything like it" recommendation. When this game came out in October, it seemed like it was perfectly atmospheric for the king of all month-long holidays, Spooky Season.

I began the game thinking that it was little more than what the advertisements suggested: a spooky-themed evolving card game that was going to maintain its creepy orange, brown, and red palette from the beginning to the end. Without spoiling too much, I would say this is only about 40% of the game. It then begins going into different kind of gameplay with a healthy dose of The Ring (2000). I was excited by the new phase of the game, but then it changed again . . . and again. . . and again. . . and by that time, it was beginning to feel frustrating, convoluted, and, most bizarrely, too long.

The design choices here are not being made particularly wisely. After the game's first mode, which is the only mode represented in the game trailer, the new modes dilute the power and atmosphere of the game, bending it into a shape that doesn't seem to fit its own mold. The first mode is the most developed, best designed, most successful, and spookiest. The second phase feels like a detour from the game I showed up wanting to play, but it was still interesting. By the time I got to the game's third phase, an absolutely atrocious and boring tech-y blue color scheme (particularly egregious after the warm cabin-in-the-woods Halloween rich palette), the game started to feel too long. By the time I got to the fourth phase of the game, it felt way too long, in a way that gave me a frustration-exhaustion headache, a combo lethal to superlative enjoyment.

If you've ever seen a great movie and found it was the director's first, and you thought, "I can't wait to see what they do next," and then the "next thing" is not great and makes the first great thing feel like a fluke? That is how I feel about the modes of this game. Ultimately, it's a game that doesn't seem to know its strengths or what would make it worthy to play. I don't think the designer's impulse was incorrect, necessarily - more is often better when it comes to indie games from practiced game designers- but it does feel like the last 20%-30% of the game is a slew of mechanics the designer wanted to devote time to but it was taking too long to develop them.

In retrospect, I think the portions of the game outside of what we see in the trailer feel like DLC and would have been better as DLC. The vision has a lot of integrity, however, and I really do appreciate what it's doing, playing with structure and expectations in a way that make a memorable, and at times confounding, experience.

I will add: if you read this review and think, "Does that mean it's not worth the money?" The answer is, oh, it's worth the money, but it's like getting the perfect sandwich and then being served half-baked fries, stale chips, and unseasoned beans as sides that you didn't ask for. That's how good the first mode is. The rest is a bit of a sideshow, but the main event is truly enjoyable and worth it even at full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow. What an outstanding experience with such an inconspicuous indie game. This game is so unbelievably fresh, engaging, and entertaining. If you are someone who loves new rouguelike indie games, and loves a thought provoking game with several mechanics that are revealed and later understood, then this is the game for you. The story is outstanding, and as a total bonus to the story, the actual gameplay and its mechanics are MASTERFULLY crafted. I was never into "card games" or any type of dueling card game like pokemon or yugioh, however Inscryption goes above and beyond with an easy to learn hard to master system that has you carefully planning your moves and synergizing various cards and items to achieve the desired affect. As many other players have stated, it would be an incredible gift if the developers released a stand alone version of this games card system that could be fully fleshed out and endless, which I would buy in a heartbeat as the card mechanics are simply the best I have ever seen.
TLDR: If you like mysterious indie games with a puzzle undertone, ABSOLUTELY buy this game and play it now. Don't wait for a sale, for 20$ this is one of the most interesting and captivating indie games I have ever played, you will not be disappointed.
