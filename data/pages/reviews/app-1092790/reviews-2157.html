\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow that I've finished it I can give a review.
First off, not much that can be said about this game because it is so much more than the trailers and I don't want to spoil anything.

What I can say is that this game does an "ok" job at executing what it is going for. You will love this game when you start playing it, almost certainly. But I can't speak for how you will feel as it progresses. The mechanics are solid, and the story is interesting and engaging.

Finally, I will give credit where credit is due: this game is a perfect example of games being art. This game does exactly what you should do if your aim to tell is to tell a story or convey a message but you also want to make a game. I'm sick and tired of people pretending games like Gone Home or other indie game-minimal walking simulators are "games" simply because you have minor control over your character. No. This is how you do it - you make a good game, one where the gameplay itself tells the story. I get it, most indie game writers have 0 writing chops and would never get a published novel written, but you can tell a good story AND make a good game at the same time, that is how it should be, and Inscryption does it well.

For that reason alone I will recommend this game. The gameplay is solid, the story is ok, and I can't vouch for how you will feel after it is all over, but this game is an experience and I recommend you experience it.\t\t\t\t\t
