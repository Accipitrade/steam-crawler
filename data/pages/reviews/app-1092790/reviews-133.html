\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCan't tell if this is a game I will need to dump another 20 hours into, or another 2000 with how much it is breaking my bones with every mistake I make, and every cheat it pulls. And I don't know if I should gamble that much variable time away trying to figure it out. I'll be back for at least the twenty hour mark, and this game is certainly creative and fun, but punishing in ways that just make me want to uninstall it and say 'I've never played that, and it doesn't look like a game for me...'

(Edit 1)
Without a guide, or having watched your favorite you-tuber beat it first, the game is, without a doubt, the most miserable time you will have. Without any kind of puzzle solving know-how, the game expects you to invent ways to figure out what it wants you to do while only giving you the barest hint. While you are struggling and pulling out your hair, the game will repeatedly kick you in the teeth, mostly because of misplays. And when it isn't down to misplays, it's down to you being put up against an unwinnable situation. Then when you lose enough of those, the game hard-locks some of the story away because 'oops, you didn't do the thing that we dropped super-vague hints about'. Oh? Restart? You can't. Not until the game lets you.

I am assured that there are a huge amount of people who love and adore this game, many of them I follow on large platforms. And the entire concept of the game is something that I really enjoy, a rouge-like card based puzzle game that does it's own thing art and story-wise. But when those things meet in actual game-play... it falls apart hard for me. Having multiple buttons being locked away so you can't use them is a nice tease if the player is ever given any kind of hint as to how to unlock them. It feels like bullying when you just can't stop the terrible run you are on and restart when you'd like to, or rebuild your deck when you've been given a terrible choice.

I really wish that I didn't have to give this a negative review, because I don't feel negative about the entire experience. I wish there was a 'middling-to-meh' button that I could press to give this review instead of the blatant 'No'. Because, I know of several of my friends that would love this game, and love poking and prodding around it's brains to see how it works. But I'm just not one of them.

May this game find you, and myself eventually, a fan.\t\t\t\t\t
