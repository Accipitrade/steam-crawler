In all honesty, this game is overhyped.

I must admit that my playtime of just 2 hours may not be extensive, but within that time, I encountered an unbearably time-consuming nature of the game that led to immense frustration. While I understand that there is a large demographic who adore these types of games, as a Disclaimer, I want to offer insights for those who, like myself, may have similar preferences.

Let's start with the Pros. The game's atmosphere is undeniably cool, and the main cabin where you play and explore holds a certain level of intrigue. The intense nature of the game and its interesting storytelling were aspects I appreciated. I'm certain that if one persists, the game will likely become engrossing—I have no doubt about that. Additionally, the card play and sound design are satisfying and polished. The overall gameplay feels sharp and has a somewhat linear progression.

However, there are Cons to consider. This game proved to be exceptionally challenging, and while I understand that appeals to many players, it presented a grueling difficulty level for casual players like myself who enjoy strategy but weren't prepared for such a steep learning curve (and yes, I'll admit that I wasn't particularly skilled). The game throws you in without much guidance, and I genuinely expected a tutorial or some leeway to ease into the experience. Consequently, I found myself not fully engaged from the start. Furthermore, if you happen to die, you're forced to create a unique death card, which, while cool and distinct, is a lengthy process that feels like a significant time sink, often leading to frustration and the desire to quit by pressing Alt+F4.

The puzzles within the game started to become irritating as I aimlessly wandered around the dark cabin, manipulating objects without clear context. Gradually, I grew tired of sitting in my chair, and despite exploring the cabin, there was a lack of refreshing elements. The game soon became repetitive, and I lost interest rapidly. Each aspect and mechanic began to annoy me, creating a domino effect of frustration.

As I invested more time, my deck filled up with cards that never seemed to appear, and concepts like sigils, damage, health, bones, and blood became confusing. My deck lost its power and purpose due to the overwhelming 20+ cards within it. While I understand that strategic choices in limiting cards exist, as a learner, I found no breathing room to grasp these concepts at all. I couldn't find a way to put the strategy into practice without being immediately overwhelmed or devoid of any chance to figure things out.

Ultimately, I have chosen to refund the game. I would still encourage others to give it a try, as personal preferences vary. However, in my own experience, I did not enjoy my time with the game. I may revisit it someday, intrigued by its unique and interesting aspects. If you relish being thrust into the deep end with no help and appreciate a hardcore challenge, I am confident you'll find this game to your liking.

That's all for now. Until next time.\t\t\t\t\t
