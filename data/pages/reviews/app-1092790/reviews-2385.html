\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is by far the most conflicted I've felt on how to deal with steam's "review" system. I left the game feeling sort of unsatisfied, but I feel like a lot of people who aren't me would like it. The game has an insane amount of content, but at times felt short and like it was rushing you to get to the next setpiece. I knew what I was getting into knowing the developer's past projects, but I still was surprised how frustrated I got with the game being interrupted with an ARG creepypasta plotline. So do I recommend the game or not? I guess my strategy this time will be to not click either the "yes" or "no" buttons and let my gut instinct kick in after writing the entire review. I will say that if this game already has sold you it's best to go in blind, since it's pretty much impossible to not spoil things, but I will keep spoilers to an absolute minimum and tag what I think is applicable.

Inscryption is "sort of" a card game. On reflection, it's "sort of" a lot of things. It's "sort of" an ARG (it's definitely got a real ARG behind it, you can look that up if you want to, but I'm not participating in it myself), it's "sort of" a creepypasta, it's "sort of" an adventure game, and it's "sort of" a roguelite. Without spoiling anything, this is one of those games that jumps genres a bunch and the main draw is to "see where it goes next". Or so you would think. My problem is that despite fully knowing this going in, the card game itself was interesting and strategic enough that at a certain point I kind of just wished I could play the card game by itself and ignore all the side bits going on. I was fully ready to experience a multi-genre weird game, but I wasn't too enthralled by the main character Luke, and I basically semi-fast forwarded through all his cutscenes (there's too much "hmm"ing and "hah"ing and occasionally "this is weird guys" for me to get interested). I eventually regressed to only really caring about the weird core gameplay and the various evolutions it took, and to that degree Inscryption impressed me. Out of the 8 hours of playtime, I would say almost all of it was playing the card game, and most of it I enjoyed.

Eight hours is a pretty good run time for Inscryption, and I honestly was impressed by how much content was in the game. The problem comes from the fact that the story kind of "infects" the game and makes me question the quality of the gameplay and how much I actually enjoyed each section. In the first part of the game, there were several times where I had no idea if I was supposed to lose, the game was badly designed and put me in an unwinnable situation, or if I just made a bad play. I'm sure there are concrete answers to these questions that fans of the game could tell me, but while I was playing the game blind I certainly had this question a lot. It doesn't feel fun to deck build and make strategic plays only to learn you're supposed to lose because you have to watch a certain cutscene to unlock some sort of card or lore tidbit to progress. Getting up from the table is cool, but I found the cards constantly backtalking to me really annoying especially since I didn't have attachment to many of them as characters.

This got better over time, however. In the further parts of the game, I felt like the strategy and scope of the game really grew and increased to a point that was really fun. Even when the scope narrowed a bit, I still felt like the core gameplay was at a point I was really enjoying. The gimmicks at this point of the game were par for the course for a "creepypasta meta horror" game, but it was still well executed and I was having fun. But then the ending hit, and despite how previously impressed I was with how long the game was, I still felt rushed to a conclusion. The problem isn't really the lack of content, but more the pacing- I felt like I didn't get enough time with (most of) each card game to really have fun with the deck building and strategy. The ones that do go longer, you only have one "save file" so you can't experiment with different builds without deleting everything. It kind of felt like if you were on a rollercoaster, and halfway through the big drop the ride just ground to a halt and started another chain lift.

I fully understand that this is pretty much the point of Inscryption. It's not supposed to be the next slay the spire, or hearthstone, or what have you. And I was ready to accept that going in. But after playing it, I felt like the developers made the really weird accident of making the card game too good and the other elements too annoying (sometimes) and that made me wishing I could just play more of the actual game in a randomised setting without any of the attachments. Luckily, there exists a sort of "chapter select" at the end of the game, so you can play more that way, but am I not just replaying the same story beats over again? I can go about doing different builds, but the enemy card layout is pre-programmed and will (almost) always act the same way.

I don't know. I feel like I totally get what this game is trying to do, but I just didn't vibe with it, despite thinking I would. It kind of feels like two games had a head on collision and half of each were implemented into eachother. For most people, I think that would be fine and the game will be 200% worth it for them, but for people who really like card games, you might feel similar to how I did and I would be wary on recommending it.\t\t\t\t\t
