\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven while out shopping I was thinking to myself "what a f!@#ing game". I have never played anything so unique. It kept me on my toes throughout the whole playtime. Expect the unexpected.

THE GOOD:
- The mechanics in the game (which change quite often) are all great and make you think things through. Just expect the unexpected.
- The visuals make the whole experience on a whole different level. I do not think it would work the same with visuals from a 2021 AAA game. And again, Expect the unexpected even here.
- The atmosphere. Dark, mysterious and you are just not ready for what is next. It is just one of a kind experience. You know what? Expect the unexpected.

THE BAD:
- I believe the game is not so balanced if it comes to difficulty. Some parts of the game are super easy even further in the game while some can be a bit on the harder side just a couple of minutes later. But I would not call it a major problem since this can feel different to another player.
- Few parts of the game were a bit boring. But it is probably because the game throws at the player so many great ideas that I kind of wanted it to be like that all the time. But to be clear, the boring parts are just a few and again, this can feel different to another player.

As you can see I am even defending the cons I have written. This just shows what a pleasant surprise this game was to me. I believe this is one of those 'one in a lifetime' games. Unique in every aspect. As you can see I have written "Expect the unexpected" a couple of times in this review. That is because I believe that this sentence is what fits this game the best. You have no idea what are you getting into.

Just buy it and get lost in this madness. It's awesome and worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe static boss fights combined with procedural map generation with a roguelike story to tie it all together is masterful and lends to deep strategy and replayability in order to complete the main story. The visuals are fantastic - and to my surprise the game runs fantastically on my Surface Pro 8 (no graphics card). 10/10 so far.

Things I'd like to see in future releases/sequel is:
- More to explore/discover/unlock in the cabin
- More interactions with talking cards (they got a bit repetitive eventually)
- More personality/variation in the new resurrections.
- My starting deck started great with the stoat + 3 random cards (often the wolf), but got worse as the stink bug and stunted wolf joined.
- I'm sure everyone is looking for a physical edition and a 1v1 vs mode. Of course this would change the game dramatically as it would either be 1 row of cards each, or 2 rows of cards each and the addition of "queuing" cards ahead of time. IMO this would make strategy much less reactive and more "believing in the heart of the cards"\t\t\t\t\t
