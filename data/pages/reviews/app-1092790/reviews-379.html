After that it surprises you with a second act that just does away with the style and charm of the first. I know the third act is somewhat of a return to that style but grafts on more needless features that I think detract from what this game does well. The first 3-5 hours spent on the first act could've been the whole game, plus an endless mode or something, and I probably would've found my money's worth.

This isn't a bad game, I can see why some would enjoy the subsequent acts, I just don't think they match the much-toted first act and kind of blind-side you with how jarring they are.\t\t\t\t\t
