\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption does for deckbuilders what Hades did for roguelikes -- it shows just how much narrative potential lies latent in the genre's gameplay and its contextual surrounds. The game blends satisfying, quickly paced card battling with spooky escape-room-esque puzzling, ultimately wrapping the player in one of the best narratives I have ever experienced in a game. While initially challenging, the game does an excellent job getting the player up to speed with the gameplay before adding new mechanics and throwing them off the deep end -- far farther than you could imagine. A richly immersive experience with the satisfying narrative intrigue and atmosphere-building of an art film, let alone an art game. Go in blind!\t\t\t\t\t

SPOILER FREE!

I had a lot of fun playing this game and finding fun card combinations to beat all of the bosses while simultaneously solving the different puzzles to progress to the next part of the game. All of the cards are relatively unique (other than the frog, which I got pretty sick of) and there are plenty to unlock. Being able to transfer sigils (which act as abilities for cards, for example, move right every turn or something like that) is also a fun gimmick that can be used to create very powerful cards. After beating the entire story, you unlock Kaycee's mod where you can do an actual game (not story/puzzle-based) version of Inscryption. There are different challenges that reward you more points than others and also plenty of changes to the vanilla cards to make them better balanced. Alongside this, there is a pretty prevalent modding community for the game and there is lots of fun things there that I found myself messing around with.\t\t\t\t\t
