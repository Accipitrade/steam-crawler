\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game does not deliver on its promises. Others may tell you to go into the game blind, but honestly there's nothing of note to spoil about the story. There are some big surprises, sure, but nothing more meaningful to the story than the revelation that Yoda talks weird in Star Wars.

The game keeps hinting at an interesting story to be revealed, if you just keep playing, but then never delivers. (In normal gameplay, anyway. I wouldn't be surprised if more of the story is kept hostage at the price of more community engagement.) It ends with a slog of a third act, and the biggest anti-climax I've ever seen. None of the questions posed in the game are answered, none of the 'big dark secrets' are revealed. The story is a writing prompt at best, and not even a particularly interesting one.

This is not a new phenomenon in games, but this time it additionally egregious because the game could have been so good. The gameplay and atmosphere are absolutely phenomenal. It honestly feels like the 'story' was forced onto a team that wanted to just make a good roguelike card game, or was used as a quickfix to tie together the different types of game they wanted to make.

Ultimately it's not a bad game, but it infuriates me to no end that we have been denied the experience of what this game could have been. You can see this reflected in the community, too. Overwhelmingly people are creating real-life versions of cards, modding in new sigils, and making videos in the style of Leshy's cabin. A lot of the game is sacrificed for the grand story, and it is not worth it by far.

If you do choose to play the game, I recommend you don't touch any film rolls you may find.\t\t\t\t\t
