\r\n\t\t\t\t\tActually one of the coolest games I've ever played; it's hard to talk about without spoilers (and probably the best way to enjoy it is without even the vaguest spoilers) but the way it continually peels back new layers to both its narrative and gameplay, introducing new mechanics and subverting other ones, is absolutely genius. I guess one thing I would say is that while the game seems at first like the story is telling is more of a spooky mystery- and the gameplay reinforces this with its focus on exploration, and finding clues in one area that end up helping you in other areas of the game- it winds up being more of an eldritch horror... which basically just means don't expect all the loose story threads to come together by the end. Maybe there's more secrets I haven't seen yet, I do know people online are trying to figure out some of the clues hidden in the game rn but I would hazard a guess that lots of stuff will stay unexplained just because of how this kind of horror relies on the unknown to be spooky.

Anyway 10/10 definitely a must-play\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is one of the most fun, memorable and interesting games I've ever played. That is saying something, considering I have a strong dislike for roguelike games and card games.

From the very start, you will be pulled in by the game's unique atmosphere, playing a strange card game against a mysterious figure hidden in the darkness. This setting means you'll both be playing the card game/roguelike game and solving mysteries around the cabin.

The game has a memorable storyline and will not stop surprising you and surpassing your expectations, up to the moment the credits roll. If you're familiar with pony island you may know what to expect. Don't look up any spoilers whatsoever, because that will worsen your experience. Just trust me: you're in for a wild ride!

The gameplay itself manages quite well to stay fun, fresh and challenging. The game constantly introduces new mechanics to try and strategize around, so you'll never find yourself trying the same thing over and over. You'll be thinking about the game even when you're not playing it, because it's simply that interesting.

Length-wise, you'll finish the game in about 10-12 hours, I think. I didn't find every secret there is and didn't solve every puzzle, but it took me around 11 hours to finish. In my opinion, that's the perfect length for a game like this, because it allows Inscryption to pace itself well, keeping the game from getting stale at any point. Like many, though, I wish there was an endless mode. I just can't get enough.

Overall, a great game. If you played the demo and enjoyed yourself, I'd definitely recommend ;)
