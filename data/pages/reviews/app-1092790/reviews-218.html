\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a story rich psychological horror atmosphere game where you, the main character is locked in a wooden cabin by a creature and you are to battle him in a card game while he plays the role of Dungeon Master and you go along playing the game against him.

This game is all about the story: what is this card game, how was it made? Who am I? Who is the creature playing as the DM? How did I get here? Where am I?? You find out as you play.

The game is divided in several parts which isn't shown in the screenshots. SPOILER: the game has 3 different card games in 1! That concept is really cool. The first part really hooks you in. The atmosphere, the narration, the environment, the creature playing as DM is just perfect. The cards are boring at first, but you can make fun decks once you start unlocking cards and objects. You have some freedom of movement inside the cabin so that you can unlock more cards and objects to help you in your quest to get to the end.

After that the game turns into something else, something much less interesting sadly. Although I said the concept to completely change the graphical style but keep the overall essence of the initial card game the same is a cool idea. And to be honest it's well executed. However the first part is much better than the other 2 parts. The next 2 parts are not as fun and there were a few confusing parts to figure out. The card game becomes less fun due to the game queiting down. At first you really have a terrible deck that just loses automatically to some enemies: that is never fun.

Being set up to immediately lose isn't a fun experience. It never is. If you're good, you should be able to overcome some challenges. But to never have a chance at beating the first enemy thrown at you is just something else.

The story is still interesting enough to keep moving. However once you get to the ending it just gets uninteresting and complicated and the desire to continue on drops immensely. You stop caring because everything takes way too long while weird things are happening. You're basically just wishing you could go back and replay the first part of the game.

Which you can! After you finally finish the game you can play a game mod where you can replay the first part of the game and also activate extra challenges to spice things up.

I was overjoyed and ready to play this game a lot more. But after playing a couple of games I realized to my (psychological) horror and dissapointment that many of the interesting cards (hint: cards that are good to experiment or make combinations with) are removed and you lose many extra features. So not only do you already face more challenges, you are also handicapped at the start with a weak deck, bad/ standard cards and less deck customization options.

Why on earth would you show many fun and interesting cards and mechanics and then remove them in the replay mode is beyond me.

So in essence:

- interesting start
- weak middle and end
- removal of many mechanics and cards in replay mode.

Makes me give this game a thumbs down. I'd almost say buy it just to experience the first part of the game. But that would be silly.\t\t\t\t\t
