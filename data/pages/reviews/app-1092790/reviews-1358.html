I usualy can't stand horror games, but Inscryption doesn't really do horror, it does dark ambiance far more than that.
It also is a roguelike where dying is frequent but interesting every time, with a great balance of difficulty and progression.
And a deckbuilding game with surprisingly forever more depth the longer you play.

The scenario is nothing revolutionary in retrospect, but managed to surprise me at every turn, and the way the player discovers the world of this game is amazing.

Great game. Go in as blind as possible. The game will give you the eyes you'll need itself :)\t\t\t\t\t
