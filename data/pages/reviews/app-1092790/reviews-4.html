\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is a massive bait and switch. Without too many spoilers, what you are seeing in the steam trailers is not at all what you are getting long-term. If you read any of the other negative reviews, any and all of them will tell you all about this.

What other reviews don't generally mention is what the community will generally say as a counter to this; "keep playing and eventually you'll get to 'kaycee's mod', it's made for people like you who just wanted what was on the steam page, trust me it's just more of act 1 it's great!".

Kaycee's mod is in itself kind of another bait and switch. It's a very hardcore roguelike that ditches any story or atmosphere. The puzzles are essentially completely removed, there is almost no reason to ever stand up from the table again, and there is no new content to discover (in terms of new boss battles, new maps, new interactions with any NPCs, etc. You do unlock a small handful of new cards). Instead the difficulty is bumped way the ♥♥♥♥ up as you are served the same old reheated stale leftovers from yesterday over and over again, but doused in a bucket load of sriracha sauce. The joy of discovery is gone, the tension is gone, the atmosphere is gone, all that's left is pure punishment.

Kaycee's mod is designed and aimed at people who are into the hardcore card game deckbuilding experience, who've come at this from a background of already having mastered slay the spire or hearthstone or etc. People who get their rocks off on refining their deck and micromanaging odds and exploiting loopholes mercilessly, because the game will now punish you if you are not exploiting every loophole as it expects that you are stacking your deck as hard as possible. And that's fine, I can understand why that might be appealing for some people. But the community will hype it up as being a replacement to act 1 and it really is not, it's a completely different type of game meant for a different type of gamer. It's nothing like the the intense, moody, horror/escape room/puzzle solving/deckbuilding hybrid that you think you've signed up for based on the store trailers and anything you've seen about this game and the first few hours. That stuff is gone, long gone. You think you're buying one thing, but it will hard swerve into something else pretty quickly and in the end you will only ever end up getting maybe 3 hours of what you thought you'd bought.

(Also, please note that if the above sounds really appealing to you, to get at kaycee's mod you're going to have to slog through about 10 hours of yet another different kind of game targeted at a different kind of player first. At least part of the 'real' game sandwiched in between act 1 and kaycee's mod seems to be targeted at meta/ARG fans who want to spend all day watching theorycrafting deep lore videos on youtube. The game just cannot stick to a target audience.)\t\t\t\t\t
