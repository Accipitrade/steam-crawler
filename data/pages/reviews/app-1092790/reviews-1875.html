\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finally beat it. Twenty-two hours (ish) later, I've finally beat this game. After countless unlucky draws and playing while I was half asleep and using the wrong strat, I've finally beat Inscryption. And I loved every second of it.

Inscryption is from the dev that brought us Pony Island, so there's some hidden elements that we still haven't figured out just yet. The ARG is mostly solved but still on-going and if you want to pop in, go right ahead. I'm not even going to try.

This is not a normal roguelike which I noticed a lot of people saw cards and came flocking for. Not it, bub! This is not a normal horror game. It's a weird hybrid of multiple genres that wants to tell you a story using cards and card games. So maybe it's a horror cardbattler? Honestly I'm not sure what to call it, genre-wise. Inscryption is a game that will, at times, remind you of Hand of Fate 2 (important that you think of the sequel) and the original Pokemon TCG game for the Gameboy Color at the same time. That might not seem like the combination you might want but it really made it for me.

So let's address the elephant in the room. Yes, this game has some uneven pacing. As you defeat what you believe is the bulk of the game, you're surprised that there's a whole different section...and then another after that! And it would be spoiling the entire plot if I elaborated other than your "crazed hermit in the cabin" is just a piece of the puzzle. In fact? You, the player? You're an active part of all of this as well. You'll find "Easter eggs" in the game which are part of something bigger and more sinister.

We said this was Pony Island dev.

As I kept playing, I fell in love with the antagonists. Which... surprise, they're antagonists! They're not very nice people and to be honest, they never promised you that they were nice after you helped them. They were compelling despite what little we had to go off them and I wanted more.

I think that's the problem with the game and why people say Act 2 is weak. I feel like Act 2 should have so much more exposition (not too much though) so I can better understand these characters and what Inscryption should have been as a card game and virtual game. What was this universe supposed to be? Honestly, now I'm interested. And what makes it all the more depressing is we do get some amazing fights that make me wish we had even more levels with these amazing characters. How dare you make me cry over a zombie lady and a talking bush? I had feelings for a jar of slime and his acceptance from his teacher.

I'd say I would want a sequel but it almost feels like it would kill the mystique of the game and considering how it ends, well... You really can't do a sequel on that.

The game teaches you by killing you often. But you're rewarded with each death. Each death makes you stronger and wiser. Act 2 is the kindest with deaths and won't give you a single set back. Act 1&3 are a little more punishing but consider Act 1 to be the meanest tutorial ever... and even then? You need to find the way you like to play. If you can't figure out your groove in a few fights, you're going to die. Act 3 just builds on this and gives you the stakes of reclaiming things you've dropped.

Puzzles in this game can be often infuriating. I'm someone with a severe issue with math due to my heavy medication and assorted health issues. Luckily, most of the math problems are addition and use the logic of the cards. You can either brute force it or slowly work it out on paper. Thank you so much devs, I really could not handle this otherwise. I am so grateful you had mercy on me. No sarcasm. I could cry because it was made easy... and yet I couldn't solve it without help. But you know what? I'm still so grateful there was no sudoku with airhorns or someone screaming at me to do multiplication drills.

Yes devs, I had fun with math about 90% of the time in your game. Thanks. I appreciated it.

Other puzzles are still numbers based but you might have to solve a clock puzzle or remember patterns. Get your camera and remember stuff. You'll be rewarded handsomely and sometimes you need these things to proceed. And then sometimes you just get completely overpowered cards. It's a living.

This game has some flashing lights and effects that might cause some distress and health issues! And there's a handy dandy option to turn it off. Devs.... Chef's kiss. I can still have scary without someone flickering the lights and go "OOOOOOOHH SPOOKY!" But the scary comes in the storytelling. What's wrong with the data? Why did people have to die? Why are these characters aware of who you are and the people outside? Now... Again, the ARG gives answers. Some answers. But no jump scares. It's just dread that slowly builds from the idea that something is deeply wrong with this game we're playing and it's very forbidden that we have it.

So long as you adjust the settings, I don't see why anyone can't play it. It's also relatively safe for older children to play as well if you're looking for something to keep an older sibling/nieces and nephews occupied if they like looking over your shoulder. They just have to like a lot of reading...

I'm a huge fan. I never got to play Pony Island and this is pretty great. I certainly got what I was looking for and I'd love for updates for more fun things!\t\t\t\t\t

This is one of "those" games where you should know as LITTLE as possible before going in, so I'll be very vague.

There's elements of thriller (not quite horror imo), strategy card gameplay, roguelike mechanics, mystery, puzzles, and a HEFTY story/lore setup. It knows exactly when to stop holding your hand to let you figure things out for yourself.

This game has some of the most fun strategy mechanics and unique card gameplay I've ever seen. It's simple to learn, and fun to break. I haven't been able to stop imagining making my own cards in real life to play it at a table since I played.

The art and music are easily my favorite out of any game I've played in who-knows-how-many years. It's BEAUTIFUL and multiple times throughout playing I legitimately stopped and sat still for a full minute just to enjoy the music, and it was well worth the experience. (The first 3 boss tracks got added to my D&D playlist for big fights INSTANTLY.)

The mystery is very amazingly set up, but a few of the "meta" parts of the story rub me the wrong way. Enough to knock it to an 8/10 for me, when it normally would've been like, a 12. I will recommend you not look too deep into the meta/ARG stuff of the game unless you're SUPER into it, because it definitely hurt the experience of the mystery for me, personally. But maybe I'm in the minority there, who knows?


All in all, easily the game of the year for me despite some things that I would very much like to make fun of.\t\t\t\t\t
