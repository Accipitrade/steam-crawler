\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll keep this brief and vague so as not to spoil this game because to fully describe what this game is would itself be somewhat of a spoiler.

This game is, at its heart, an adventure and puzzle game wrapped around a very smart but simple card battler, which evolves in complexity as the game itself does. It's very good, never gets stale, has a remarkable amount of tactical flexibility as a strategy card game, but the real selling point is actually the "story" of the game, which is much stronger and more poignant than I expected. This isn't a game that you'll grind or likely replay hundreds of times (probably only once or twice), but it's a well crafted experience that you'll enjoy and likely never forget.\t\t\t\t\t
