Every time I think the game is getting more and more ambitious, it takes it one step further. It feels like the natural expanse to the same devs that made Pony Island. I don't know how they kept this feeling so dang good while expanding this much.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is nothing short of excellent, avoiding the usual problem of rougelikes in that it introduces a lot of new elements over the course of multiple runs, gives context and narrative purpose to your actions and enough going on besides the card battler to stop things from being uninteresting or tedious.
The demo gives a good taste of the full game, although you will have a lot more options in terms of mechanics as you progress past the first boss.
Beyond the "main" gameplay, that being a card battler, this provides a meta-story about a videogame with well-written characters, good puzzles and atmosphere as well as an entirely optional secret hunt. In that aspect it is very similar to Pony Island and The Hex, the two previos games released by Daniel Mullins.
It really is very good as a game and artpiece and worth supporting financially.\t\t\t\t\t
