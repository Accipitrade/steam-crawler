\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to give this a positive rating, but I just can’t. It’s filled with too many frustrations at every turn. It’s like the game is going out of its way to circumvent the player from having fun. The card game itself is good. Just tell the player how to play, throw in a few ways to build up a deck, and just let it be fun.

Instead you get an overdose of an absurdly ambitious story, which is clearly a couple of steps too complicated for the developers to actually incorporate into the game. In lieu of clear directions, you get vague hints and tips, that orient you towards unexplained mechanics. I was constantly having to look up how to progress, which is terrible for any game. The puzzles weren’t hard, but I wanted to play the card game. The card game is *good*, just let me actually *play the game*.

Right when you start figuring out the mechanics of the card game, and actually having fun, it switches you to a completely different area where 60% of what you learned goes out the window.

The meta-story is about some idiot Luke who starts freaking out and going crazy because he finds some long “lost” collectible card game, and also because he’s too stupid not to stick randomly found disks in his PC. Even the tiny amount that I was interested in the plot was flushed down the toilet because the entirety of the story itself isn’t contained within the game. It extends out to some ARG – which is the opposite of what I consider fun.

I don’t necessarily regret buying this game (it was on sale), but I wish I had paid less.

GRAPHICS: Decent, pixelated style. Overall irrelevant to the gameplay.

SOUND: Below average to terrible. But again, irrelevant to the gameplay.

CONTROL: very simple, which is fine for this type of game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was pretty torn as to whether I should recommend this game or not. The card game mechanics are decent, but RNG can be pretty brutal at times. In fact, I had a lot of difficulty getting out of the first act till I got very lucky with a random encounter that gave me unlimited resources in the card game. It honestly might have taken me a few more hours or pulling up a guide for puzzles had I not gotten lucky with that random buff. I don't think that points towards a well made card system.

I found the storyline in the second, third, and final act to all be pretty mediocre. I think my biggest issue is that the game you end up playing is not what is really sold to you. Every screenshot here in the store is only from the first act. I don't want to spoil anything, but things change pretty dramatically after that. Its like you went to a BBQ restaurant and got a sampler platter but only a fourth of it was BBQ. Even if the rest of what you get is tasty, you wanted BBQ and not sushi. I think I would have enjoyed the game more if I knew what I was getting into, because it was not what I expected or wanted when I bought it.

7.0/10.0\t\t\t\t\t
