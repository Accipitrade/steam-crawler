\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome game, exactly what is expected from the developer of Pony Island in a world where developers can learn.

Multiple genres, but they are better blended together this time. Rushed ending, but it isn't so abrupt and manages to hold the action the whole way through. Tries to convince you of a world within the game, but doesn't smack you with the concept repeatedly. Easter eggs and secrets for trying things outside of the puzzling. Unique concepts either never before seen or hardly seen at all. All the hallmarks of Daniel Mullins it seems.

The core gameplay is great, being a deck-building rogue-lite at its core and a bit of point-and-click puzzling on the side that isn't shoe-horned in like it may have felt in Pony Island. Plenty of main-line mechanics and options to build a fun or OP deck in the first act of the game, hugely expanded and complicated deck-building in the second act, and slightly more restrictive yet still varied in styles in the third act. It might a be a little too easy, or maybe I'm just decent at this game.
The puzzles range from easy (all the ones you can see) to hard (the ones you probably won't know even were in the game after you've beaten it. Twice.) Most reward you with extras that should help you, some reveal a hint or another piece to a grander puzzle. Solid implementation.

The visuals are spectacular, as the game has moody atmosphere at all times. Using a combo of texture (sharpness), lighting, and filter effects, the game is stylized to be distinct from much of other games out there. The sounds are distinct (to the point where I recognized the candle-snuffing sound down the elevator before you go back up it), the music is good and fits, and the setting is well-thought-out. There's really nothing to dislike on the Eye side of things.

So yeah, ultimately a great contender for Indie Game of the Year. What a shame it's gonna lose to MS Flight Sim because TGA award categories are GARBAGE (like, who thinks Strategy and Sim are practically the same thing? Idiots.) Fit for everyone, really. I feel like just about everyone should play this game since it's all-around good.

The complaints I have are mostly minor personal nips. The game felt better to play in the first act (card/deck progression) the third act's card/deck progression didn't feel too good (the power-ups felt less useful on these 0-1/1-1/1-2 cards) forced "recycle" stations, no cards felt like they were particularly strong except the one I made [and I made it for the one boss too, you are welcome whoever gets "Big Smart You"] but the extra lane was nice, I personally hate tabbing out a game to translate binary (especially when the game minimizes and tabbing back in moves the binary out of view somehow [i.e. the videos auto-playing]), some of the puzzle rewards feel like they hurt you more than help (i.e. a card you didn't want/won't use or a dedicated and permanent item slot hog), the "hint" on how to beat the first act was not enough (I ended up making 6 nice Winking victory pictures on the door before I did it EARLY enough), lastly (and mostly) the Checkov's Gun check with the, uhmm... plot device in the third act after the 2nd-to-last achievement is earned (spoiler-free, nailed it) was SUPER SUDDEN. Too sudden. It wasn't out of character, but it didn't seem planned either. Literally "oh look, plot device."

So yeah, buy it. You will enjoy it.\t\t\t\t\t
