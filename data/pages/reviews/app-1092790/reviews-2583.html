\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWent into this game blind after watching the trailer. A horror card game was not something I had seen before so I was interested to try this out.

Took me around 11 hours to finish the game and I can gladly say that this game is phenomenal. I can already see it being a cult classic for card game fans. For $20 (currently $17.99 at the time of this review), this game has everything you'd ever want from a narrative based horror card game.

Everything from the art style to the puzzles and game mechanics were fresh and unique. Each portion of the game has something new and interesting to add to the core mechanics while also maintaining the eerie atmosphere that keeps you intrigued about every new detail you figure out about the game master and the player.

The horror theme persists through item and card interactions such as the scared shaking of the cards when they are about to be sacrificed . Some cards also interact with the player and give hints on how to solve puzzles while others mock the player's moves.

The creators have put a twist on the usual roguelite progression where instead of getting points for an upgrade tree, you solve puzzles in the environment around you to gain extra cards/items that are permanently added to your starting deck/item slots.

Clues for some of the escape-room type puzzles are found through the game's progression and I found the search for clues to be quite satisfying. The other puzzles are your normal card game puzzles where you have a board state with some sliders to change which units are on each side. They were interesting and most of them didn't take too long to solve.

Each of the bosses in the game are unique some are more on the fun/goofy side and a few of them have mechanics that you would never expect from a card game. For spoiler reasons, I can't tell you about my favorite boss fight where they made me go through my hard drive for files to put on the scale .

I would love to go on a rant about the story and how there is more to this game than just a roguelite deckbuilder but that would ruin the experience for you.

I'd 100% recommend this to anyone that has tried a card game or is interested in trying out a card game. The game is relatively beginner friendly and is a joy to play. Thanks so much to the creators for making such an incredible game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe card game in the cabin is not the core game, but Act 1 of a narrative. There's a lot of spilled salt in the discussions and reviews over that fact, and it could have been more obvious in the marketing.

I still recommend this game. Although the card game is not as fleshed out as a real property like MTG or Yugioh, it is still plenty fun. Even though the creepy cabin only lasts for part of the game, the card game persists, with different twists in each new setting. And the story told by those new settings and mechanics is a good one.

There could have been more segments, or branches leading to alternatives. And the story leaves us with a lot of unanswered questions. But even though the story and the game-play leave you wanting more, they are still complete on their own and well done if you appreciate what they are going for.

I would have liked more replay-ability, or a longer, deeper one-time experience. But I'm happy with this game and now that you know what you're in for, you should be too\t\t\t\t\t
