\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's okay. The first act is a phenomenal masterpiece and really draws you into the story and atmosphere of the... well of just the first act really. Everything that comes after that feels like is was made to be worse than the first act for narrative reasons. The card game drops the rogue-like encounters and choosing of paths FTL-like gameplay for a scripted, linear experience encounters. The card game essentially just becomes puzzles to solve with a bit of RNG to give the illusion of strategy. Act 3 returns to form, but with an intentionally (see spoilers below) less well crafted world and experience. The rogue-like elements remain absent and the card game is further simplified to only include on of the decks from act 2. Either you play the right cards in the right order or you don't.

In Act 1, the DM of your experience is Leshy. Narratively, Leshy is one of four masters of the card game Inscryption who vie for control of the world. Last time around, Leshy gains control and imprisons the other three masters.
Leshy really enjoys narrative and atmosphere so the experience he crafts is a very visceral and immersive atmosphere: "YEEEEEEHAAAAWWW THARS GOLD IN THEM CARDS!". You beat him, unlock new game and "reset" the world to begin the power struggle between the four masters anew. Act 2 sees you once again tip the scales and your actions lead to the robot P03 taking control. P03 is a min/maxer who only wants to play efficient games. The world P03 creates is a lot more spartan and the atmosphere really only exists as a weak cover to tie together card battles. P03 often says things like "Oh this is uh... the east... umm... yeah it like sucks here I guess? Sorry". As a result of this narrative choice, Act 3 comes off a lot weaker and since Act 1 precedes it, I spent the whole rest of the game wishing I could go back to the cabin.

If you've played a card game before you'll probably take like 2-3 hours to beat the first act, so if you can pick this up for under $5 I definitely recommend you grab it! If you enjoy "Hark! Look how uncaring and haphazard the design is" bits, then the rest of the game may tickle that itch for you, but I'd just stop after Act 1 if I could do it again.\t\t\t\t\t
