\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: Dev added a challenge mode that takes place exclusively in the cabin. This game is now everything I hoped it would be.

Great game, amazing atmosphere, especially in Act 1. Like playing a card game in The Room. Bosses were all memorable. It took 10'ish hours to beat and that's without exploring every secret.

As it stands, this game is an "experience", and will subvert your expectations. Without trying to spoil anything, the change in presentation and dropping the "rougelike" mechanic in Acts 2/3 ruffled some feathers. Acts 2/3, while unexpected, are interesting if you give them a chance, though admittedly they are underwhelming compared to Act 1. Would be great if Act 1 was extended and/or replayable.

Pros:
* Amazing atmosphere
* Memorable bosses
* OP card combos

Meh:
* Puzzles were alright. Some better than others.
* Over arching narrative between all 3 Acts was meh

Cons:
* Act 2 felt sluggish and diverged too much from Act 1
* Act 3 wasn't bad but not as good as Act 1
* Didn't like the Archivist Boss fight. On the face of it, the filesystem access is a cute gimmick but for me it broke my immersion. Call me old fashioned but enumerating my disk is not what I look for or expect in a game. Games like Doki Doki and One Shot handled the file system gimmick better since they limited the scope of what they were doing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is phenomenal. I very seldom make reviews for games, but I firmly believe this game deserves it. At first, you may think this game is similar to games like Slay the Spire and other deck building roguelikes, and it is, but it becomes soooooo much more... and it gets... different.
Unfortunately, if you're the type that's here to strictly play a deckbuilder roguelike with hundreds of hours of replayability, you're going to end up a little disappointed. It's only got about 10-12 hours, but that's great for a game of this price. Inscryption is the type of game where you may come for the gameplay (which is still incredibly good), but stay for the story. The story's got some very interesting twists and it may even make you a little emotional toward the end.

Overall, buy game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEverything about the first act is amazing, from the atmosphere to finding subtle ways to break the game. This is the most roguelike part of the game, and it's fun incrementally exploring the cabin (although very short-lived) .

The ladder half of the game is disappointing only in comparison to the first half, not in content. I enjoyed my stay, highly recommend.

General spoilers for the rest of the review:

The switch to second act was at first kind of refreshing, the 2D world was charming, it was neat to open card packs and experiment with different decks. If you're not a fan of the sometimes tedious process of deck tinkering, then I can understand frustration with this part. Also, the novelty of the 2D-esque atmosphere, particularly for duels, soon wore off, and I felt myself yearning for the crispy atmosphere of Leshy's cozy abode. The 2nd act over-stayed its welcome slightly; but overall I liked the twist and the nod to deck building was enjoyable for a first play through experience. This section is not really that re-playable in a roguelike sense.

Anyone who has completed the game probaly knows what's coming up next. The monotonous 3rd act, easily the least enjoyable part of the game. Still very enjoyable, but compared to especially the 1st act, felt a tad bit tedious as it dragged on for quite while. I like the idea of alternate versions of Leshy's cabin, with the game play and environment stylized to the particular game master, but PO3's place felt pretty empty and card play in the 3rd act was, although having some compelling elements, very easy and repetitive. All the bosses were too easy, especially since this is the final phase of the game although the music and flavor of each boss was pretty great. I liked the bounty hunters, but even they were kind of underwhelming.

I actually liked the ending a lot. It felt a little abrupt and maybe an alternate ending would have been nice but that's okay.

The wizard man's Yu-Gi-Oh duel disks was an awesome one off but that's it. Grimora's chess board and skeleton mechanics definitely has potential. I think everyone was a expecting for Grimora and wizard man to be the next game masters but instead I like that the game gave a nod to each without having them overstay their welcome.

By enjoyment: Act 1 > Act 2 > Act 3

By time in game: Act 3 > Act 2 > Act 1

I love roguelikes and hope we can see some DLC or a spin-off of Inscryption dedicated to the replayability and content expansion of the first act with increased difficulty, more bosses, more pathway options, squirrel head part of the woodcarver's inventory rather then automatically owned, cameo of Grimora's chessboard style, custom cards like in the 3rd act, etc. The custom cards are cool but should not carry over to the next run IMO for the true roguelike experience. \t\t\t\t\t
