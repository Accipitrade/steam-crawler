\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is amazing if you want to get slapped in the face about every 10 minutes of playtime. You will play this game thinking you know what this game is about and then realize you're wong and then you'll again think you now know what the game is about, and you're wrong again. Inscryption takes a lot of unforeseen turns and while the game is not tagged as horror on steam, you should play this game knowing it's a horror game. Literally don't look up anything about Inscryption before you play it, because it's really one of those things where you want to know as little as possible when starting the game.
The sound design is amazing, especially one of the early boss fights has such a great background song, that I wasn't even mad about having to fight him multiple times. The game is so full of content it is *literally* like buying multiple games for the price of one. There are so many small things to uncover and connections to draw and fun riddles to solve that you can hardly get bored of the game in it's ~15h runtime.

However, I would still recommend reading some of the negative reviews of the game, because a lot of people seemed disappointed that (mild spoilers for the rest of the paragraph:) what is advertised in the trailer is only act 1 of multiple acts, which didn't bother me at all, but I have to admit I was a bit confused when the game basically asked me to play an entirely diffrent game for like at least 2hrs, but that doesn't mean I didnt find that part fun. But keep in mind, that what you are seeing in the trailer is only about 1/3rd of the game.

tl;dr: Inscryption is a great, absolutely uncanny expierience that feels adjacent to reading the story of ben drowned as a kid in 2010 on some weird forum at 1 am in the morning, filled with great mechanics and strategic fights and and many many many "what the ♥♥♥♥"-moments.\t\t\t\t\t
