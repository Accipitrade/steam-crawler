\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Inscryption is a fun card game with simple mechanics resembling Yu-Gi-Oh! The game is easy to learn and becomes more enjoyable as you progress. The 2D and 3D graphics look good, but the transitions can be jarring. The music suits the game well. The story is engaging and has unexpected twists. I enjoyed the game and found it worth the time spent playing.

Rating: 7.9/10

-

Basically, Inscryption is literally a card game. It's you and your opponent playing it's own over-simplified Yu-Gi-Oh! Every time you hit the opponent directly, they gain points by the amount of damage you did. The points go onto a scales, and if you or the opponent's points weigh more on their side, they lose. It's a lot of back and forth till you manipulate the game in your favor and start playing with overpowered cards and mechanics, but it's really fun.

-

9/10 Fun - I had a lot of fun with Inscryption. I didn't think it was going to be as fun as I thought at first, but I'm glad that I was wrong. I was thinking that the game was going to be boring or really hard, since I over think things. However, the game is not bad, it's not boring, and I found myself wanting to play it when I "overplayed" it while on a 10 minute break from it. Inscryption does card gaming really well.

8/10 Gameplay - I'm not big on the whole "card game" genre in gaming, although I do play Pokémon TCG in real life. So, when I decided that I needed a break from Pokémon, and I saw that Devolver Digital published another game, I knew that I would have to try out Inscryption. Inscryption is easy to learn, and it makes it easier to play as you progress, and if you learn throughout your play-through how to work some of the special effects together, you'll breeze right through the game no problem. I won't lie, it did take me a little while to understand how the game even worked, and how the sigils, card synergies, bones, and sacrifices worked. (Personally, bones don't work for me, I hate them, lol.) At first it felt like it would take me a long time to beat the game, but once I picked it up, I breezed right on through.

8/10 Length - For the amount of time that I played beating the story, I'm definitely not complaining. There's more after you beat the game, too, but I have other games to play, so I won't continue. I got more than what I paid for, though. I'll be coming back for sure.

7/10 Characters - There are definitely a lot of secrets in Inscryption. I felt that I met the characters too late to even care much for them since I was having trouble in the beginning to figure out how the game worked, though.

9/10 Character Design - The characters look really good when it goes into 2D and 3D forms. I really didn't have any problems with how a character looked, as the "ugliness" of a character just worked with the atmosphere or environment.

6/10 Voice Acting - Okay, there is voice acting, but like, not really. The main game, there is no voice acting, you'll be doing a lot of reading. The extras, there is voice acting, but I was able to fast-forward them, and I didn't care for them at all. More so, it kind of disrupted the flow of the game to me.

9/10 Animations - The attacks, the card playing, the turn-ends, pretty much the game is simple, but they do look good.

8/10 Music - The music definitely goes with Inscryption, and you notice it very well when you're sitting there thinking about what moves to make when the opponent literally has there whole board filled with creatures ready to one-shot you.

7/10 Graphics - The game looks great, but I wasn't a fan of going from 3D to 2D to 3D, and then I realized too late that you could take away the film grain effect, but even afterwards in the 3D part still had this weird look to it that wasn't as appealing as I would have liked it to be. The cards look fine, and when you meet the 3D versions of some characters, they look really good.

8/10 Story - Inscryption has a great story. It's much deeper than you initially think it is, and you definitely can't see it coming from a mile away. The story is fresh, it's not new, but it's not overused, at least in my experience. I thought it was a great story. I'm not going to spoil anything, but I thought it was going to be something like this and that, but it ended up being this and that instead! I don't like stories that I can predict are going to happen, because, you know, been there, done that, but I didn't see anything coming from a mile away. The story did take my by surprise, therefore leading me to continue wanting to play see what would happen.

Overall: 79/100

You don't want to spoil yourself on this one, the game is awesome! For that reason, I'm not going to go in-depth on this review even if there's a lot of awesome details about the game, because it's best if you find them yourself!

When I got this game, I decided to get it in the Inscryption: Beginner's Deck bundle, and played through/100%'d the games in the order they were chronologically released in. If you like games with a lot of story and secrets, I'd honestly recommend doing the same! It's not necessary to play the other two games to enjoy Inscryption or anything, but they're still very interesting games, and the games sometimes make small references to previous games.

Out of the three games, I've found this one to be my favorite. It has a lot of the best elements from the other two games, and a lot of awesome stuff on its own. The gameplay is awesome, the story is great, the characters are really cool, the atmosphere is amazing, this game is a wonderful experience. If you have any interest at all in the game after seeing the trailer/screenshots, I'd highly recommend just getting the game! Now stop reading my review, and start playing some Inscryption!
