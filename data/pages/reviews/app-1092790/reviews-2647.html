\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this game, but don't be expecting the traditional card roguelite experience. This is a 10 hour atmospheric narrative driven card game with interesting gameplay mechanics and great soundtrack, but at the center of the game is a weird story that is best experienced going in blind. The game doesn't really have replayability as, while the gameplay is fun, doesn't have diversity when it comes to the enemy encounters between playthroughs.

So, enjoy this if you're looking for a narrative game with surprises. I'd avoid if you're looking for a highly replayable card roguelite like slay the spire. Also, there is a very jarring tonal shift in this game that will throw many off (which also lower the replayability for me but I don't think replayability is what the game was developed for). I wasn't a huge fan of this shift but it did fit the story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is so much I would like to say.
I am not easily impressed, I am a writer myself, and half of my days are spent imagining crazy scenarios to write about: and what we have here is, one of the very few art pieces that has inspired me deeply, probably forever.

It was all so random. I would check steam store in the card games category, once a few months: and I have stumbled into this. And luckily I have made one of the best decisions after playing the demo: I bought the beginner deck bundle. (the bundle including all the games from the same developer)

Pony island is something special.
But when it comes to hex, it is mind blowing.
I mean, at instances like these products of utmost love, creativity, and effort: I start to question the reality:
"What if I even write some masterpiece one day, it will worth everything, yet will only meet ordinary response."
Sure pony island has exploded a bit at its time.
But what about hex? I have never heard of it before, but it deserves a place in history of games as a milestone.
Sure, some people could fairly say: "these games are not special as you think. They are just "meta" that's it."
But surely, there are many kinds of meta art, and mostly they are just low effort.
I mean hex was on par with adultswim productions, I am talking about titles like "too many cooks", "final deployement 4"
And even then, Hex surpass those videos projects on many levels, mainly in being a game and having a longer duration to experience it.
Well that was supposed to be review for Inscryption, and here I am talking about Hex, but I was never a good reviewer, coming back to the topic at hand:

Inscryption, is just one of those, once in a lifetime games like ftl, witcher, bioshock, and many other titles that is too obscure right now to give as an example. And that is because this game is an improvement on already a pinnacle game of Hex.
This game nails the meta aspect,
nails the character development,
nails the emotional circumstances,
nails the logic and authenticity,
and lastly nails, of course the game mechanics.

Not to give any spoilers, I will not dive deep at all.
But, before finishing, I would like to point out an ironic reality:
Screw card games.
I love card games, I don't know why exactly, but they pull me.
But card games betrayed me here.
Don't get me wrong: this game is more than enough as it is,
But I can't stop myself comparing it to the Hex, and then I feel like: the variety in general, the diversity of gameplay could be better: and I blame the card mechanics for it.
I mean, designing cards and mechanics for them is really a badass job, very crushing, impossibly tiring, and I feel like, if there was no deep strategical elements to think of, inscryption could surpass hex on the morphing of the game to different variations:
Because hex followed more of a storytelling atmosphere, and that kind of atmosphere gives such a freedom of space to go crazy; I would really like that here in inscryption, if there were more levels, and maybe even more shallow in each level for that: but building around a card game does not allow that, I understand, and I blame the existence of card games for that. My favorite genre, stole from me a bit better version of inscryption.
And the worst part of this, the part where it is ironic is that, if this was not a card game, like I told you at the beginning, I would propably never play hex, and never find the chance to submerge in this awe inspiring universe.

I will be waiting for the next game.

With love, and deep appreciation,
Berkay Midilli.\t\t\t\t\t

Huh.

... I think the combination of squirrels + "spawn worker ant in your hand when being played" as a totem is too cheap.

Anyway, while I'll go try to hunt the true ending now, do I recommend the game?

♥♥♥♥ yes. The card game is simple to understand but just challenging enough to entertain, never frustrating (my 2nd run just almost got ended by an elk cuz I didn't quite understand it's mechanic yet, there, the games "you can mess up and loose once and still continue" mechanic felt fair cuz it did let me continue) the music is beautiful, the graphics set the atmosphere well. I like the bossfights and the items are helpful but never too cheap.

I quite enjoyed my time with it and can't wait to dive back into it tomorrow, see about the what I assume to be the true ending and other combinations that I can make work!\t\t\t\t\t
