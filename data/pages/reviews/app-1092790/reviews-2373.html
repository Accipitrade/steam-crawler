\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a good card game wrapped in a truly unique story. The details of the card game mechanics vary along with the overall progression of the game. If you feel like you get into the groove, the game will alter its style in order to surprise you. I liked the visuals and the dark atmosphere of the game since they underlined the story. A downside in my opinion is that the difficulty of the game cannot be altered. I would have loved to make the card game matches more competitive; especially in the end phase of the game.

If you are looking for a decent card game with reliable game mechanics and competitive matches, continue searching. But if you are willing to accept some downsides w. r. t. the card game mechanics, you will get a great game that knows exactly how to combine it with a great story.\t\t\t\t\t

Like others, I'd say go in blind and learn as little as possible before playing. I'd also say tough it out if you feel stuck and really use your noodle because if you can solve the puzzles on your own it's that much more satisfying.

Also, I would like to see a rogue lite game made from the first or third acts--incredible mechanics that can easily translate into a crazy good, addictive gameplay loop. I could play the first act for at least another 20-30 hours *at least*. Can't wait to see what these devs do in the future.
