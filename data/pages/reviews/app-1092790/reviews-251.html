\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt takes the developer and script writer to be extremely brilliant to make a fair meta game, otherwise the plot would simply be predictable, then lead to cringe, awkward and dumb performance.
Inscryption, on the other hand, is a perfect positive example of dealing with multiple layers of environment set and its interaction with characters' normal routine acts and incident reaction & decision making. Its unique performance and narrative rhythm empower the vivid characteristics of the characters with very limit length of plot.
The art style is impeccable, major art resourcing were not reused between different acts, which makes the first time clearance experience (of about 5-7 hours btw) always motivated by the new visual style. (I was confused when I entered act II as I supposed the pixel UI wouldn't last for long...)
If evaluate Inscryption as a deck builder game , the game's mechanics are very solid as it's well pre-proven, not much innovation on this side but still is way enough to support the roguelite alternative play.
My only complaint is that the play style of the two remaining scrybes in act IV was not expanded enough, resulting in their prominent art style being as fleeting as it was. I believe the mechanics perspective, there is still design space for their stylized features.
So, well done Daniel Mullins Games, you deserve all the compliments, looking forward to your new releases.\t\t\t\t\t
