\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun deck-builder roguelike with some puzzle twists. I like the basic mechanics and the way they are developed over time. There are some very fun "rule-breaking" moments as well. The progression of learning the cards is good, and there are some clever guardrails that encourage you to learn the new mechanics and use them.

I don't want to include spoilers, but I will say the changes that happen later in the game were fun for me but also felt a bit... Scattered? The changes were definitely interesting and developed the story in a cool way. But I didn't think they were super necessary, to be honest.

I also went into the game thinking it would be more like Slay the Spire, where I would have to really bash my head against the game to make progress. Instead, I found it was pretty easy. I did run into a few sticking points in the later game but overall the difficulty was not high. It's more of a game you play for the narrative and joy of discovery than the mechanics.

So, overall, a fun deck-building game with some neat ideas about out-of-run progression for roguelikes and a fun story. I would pitch it as an inventive twist on some roguelike tropes, with interesting use of horror and puzzle mechanics on top of deckbuilding. But I would warn players that it does not have the same depth and challenge as games like Slay the Spire. I also found the late game and conclusion of the story dissatisfying. Overall a good game.\t\t\t\t\t
