\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can attest to the flair and ingenuity of Act I - the sound effects are chillingly good, the cards display some level of original thought and the game does a good job of balancing the delicate task of inducting new players and letting them figure out how to beat the game. The developers are exceptionally judicious with their use of silence to evoke a sense of creeping dread.

The game takes a fairly significant dive in the second act, largely due to a radical change in its art style and presentation, before returning to what makes the first act good in the last and final third act. After completing the game - or so I thought, I later learnt that there is much more to the bits of stuff you encounter in game than meets the eye. The game can be really complex if you are up for a challenge.

Buy the game at least for the first act - it really is quite an experience, melding deck building and escape room puzzles together and integrating them into a cohesive and ultimately, horrifying, narrative. They have even managed to cram some "live footage" segments reminiscent of "Her Story" and "Telling Lies" into the game to make the narrative even more convincing. Speaking strictly for the deck-building genre, I enjoyed this game as much as I did for Slay The Spire, followed by Griftlands and last of all, Monster Train.\t\t\t\t\t
