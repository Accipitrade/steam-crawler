\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvery so often one of Daniel's games gets the public's attention. The only problem people have is finding ways to talk about it without ruining the fundamental experience.

It goes without saying that the novelty of 4th wall breaking quickly becomes stale; and out of the box ways to interact with a game are also finite. Once you've played one game by Daniel M, you've caught wind of how exactly those play out. Prior to Inscription, he's fallen into a trap so to say of trying to make Pony Island over and over again.

Inscription is a breath of fresh air that retains a lot of the creator's style. Daniel's flare of creating unconventional mechanics is applied to create a card game, with tools and story taking much inspiration from his previous experiences, leading to a well polished and thought out experience.

Most approachable out of all games by this developer, although a requirement to beat it is getting good at the game and perhaps a little too much rng. Overall a solid experience but I'd expect some people be stuck on certain parts.\t\t\t\t\t
