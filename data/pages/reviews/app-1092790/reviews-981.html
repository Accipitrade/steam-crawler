\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like Inscryption, and it definitely has it's upsides, but two things killed the Act 1 experience for me. First, the non-boss card battles are over too quickly, win or lose, which makes luck the main determinant of success. If the game is going well, your first hand of cards will allow you to win the game in 1-2 turns, with minimal interaction between your cards and those of your opponent. Or sometimes you'll draw a hand that you cannot play anything on the first turn, in which case the match is effectively over. You will never be able to catch up with the damage dealt by your opponent. As for the boss battles, they are even more luck-based, even if you know the boss's gimmick.

Second, the game's animations do not respect the player's time. That's an unforgivable sin in a game where you are expected to play and replay the same fights, and experience and re-experience the same events dozens of times. Animations that are interesting the first time around quickly lose their luster when you're seeing them for the eighth or ninth time in a row. These animations are unskippable and do not play back any faster. Text speed cannot be hurried either, and no matter how quickly you click, there is some minimum delay for which it is required to be on-screen. That's really bad game design. I shouldn't need to click at all for the initial text to play, and all text should be instantly skippable upon clicking. The worst thing is that a few events do get shorter upon second or subsequent playthroughs, so it's clear the designers know this is a problem. They just don't care enough to fix it more broadly.

TLDR: Inscryption is a game whose luck-based nature requires many repetitive playthroughs, but which is not designed to minimize wasted time during those repetitive playthroughs. That ultimately killed it for me.

I had some fun with Inscryption, but also a lot of frustration. I think overall Fights in Tight Spaces or Slay the Spire represents a better bang for your buck if you are looking for a roguelike deck builder that respects your time.\t\t\t\t\t
