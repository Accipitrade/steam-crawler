\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery much is Inscryption.

Story/Puzzle game first, card game second.

Would very much recommend it - even to those pretty on the fence about card games. I find most card games too much to get my head around or, at the very least, lack the effort to get seriously invested into a card game.
Inscryption is an exception to this rule; the card game is simple whilst still being complex enough to remain interesting. This is merit to my prior statement. Inscryption is not *just* a card game. This aspect allows it to cut corners in card game complexity. Not just being a card game allows the game to cater to a range of players - which I would argue is an absolute positive. Those similar to myself: who do not generally enjoy card games, can take more satisfaction from the storytelling. Those who appreciate puzzle games will enjoy that aspect. Of course, those who take pleasure from card games can absolutely take joy from this game; I do not feel the simplicity of the card game takes anything away from the gameplay.
Simplicity instead takes away instead from replayability - in my opinion. This, however, is not necessarily a negative. Incryption is not a competitive card game. It is a story/puzzle game. Story and puzzle games are best enjoyed once, leaving a lasting impression on the player. Inscryption sure has left an impression on me.

Many games aim to be jack of all trades. Many end terribly with underwhelming gameplay. Incryption has managed to be a jack of all trade type game, whilst retaining the enjoyability of all gameplay. I strongly suggest you stop sifting through reviews, and just glance at the 'Overwhelmingly Positive' review label.

At the very least, give it a shot. Try out the demo, and if you're still not sure, you always have two hours to test out the actual game.

This is a game worth both your money, and time.
