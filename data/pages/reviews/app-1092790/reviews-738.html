\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery well made game and somehow reminded me of There's No Game: Wrong Dimension with its changing style.

Act 1 of the game is spectacular and definitely earns the review score; however the rest of the game (artistically) and the playstyle changes dramatically and I cannot say I liked them as much. Act 2 definitely questioned my choice of playing the game but if you persevere, I should say that Act 3 and particularly the ending is wonderful. Just give me a game like Act 1 and make it 10 Acts and I'd play that in a heartbeat. If I wanted to make a tier list, I'd easily rank them as Act 1>Ending>Act 3>Act 2.

If you're not a fan of card games I'd probably not recommend the game since after certain points it is heavily leaning on deck building and automatic building is useless. Though I have to say that neither the game or the puzzles are nerve-wracking.

Considering the wonderful regional pricing, I'd argue that it's a unique game that you should experience one way or another unless you genuinely dislike the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
