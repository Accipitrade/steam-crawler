\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fun, neat idea for a card game. There's some sp00ky mystery element to what's going on behind the game, but the actual gameplay is unique and constantly changing to keep you interested.
One major complaint:
I know some people probably enjoyed Act 2 (top down 2D world) but I don't really care for the look here even if it's 'retro' inspired. My other complaint with Act 2 is that they don't ease you into the magic elements very well, I was still trying to make sure I completely understood everything from Act 1 so I'd prefer if they eased you into new gameplay elements better like they did in Act 3 (robot world). Having the energy and gem/vessel system slowly introduced to your cards is much better than just saying "lol there's magic cards now, figure that out". I'm aware that there's a book and it's not impossible to figure out, but it takes some of the fun out of the game when I feel like I have to teach myself the rules of the game instead of the game teaching me through gameplay.

That being said, Inscryption is a very neat game, the live-action acting was not as impressive as my last experience (Dr. Darling in 'Control') but it was still pretty decent and I think they did a good job implementing the found-footage story into the game. I'll definitely be playing this one again in the future as it's a fun card game and reminds me a bit of poker in the sense that there's both luck and skill involved. The ability to upgrade and craft your own OP cards is a lot of fun and there's something to be said for both scraping together a win from dire circumstances, as well as rolling the computer with an insane deck build.\t\t\t\t\t
