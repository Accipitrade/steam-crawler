\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a Daniel Mullins fan. I played Pony Island before it was 'cool' end even bought the game for my friends to promote it. I picked up The Hex, not even knowing it was one of Daniel's games, fell in love with it, then realized it was the Pony Island guy. When I heard he had a new game, I was very excited.

Well I've put nearly 5 hours into it and... I don't like it. There will be some spoilers about mechanics and stuff, but nothing that should really hurt your experience. I'm allowing comments on this review so people can throw hate at me. Maybe that'll teach me to like this title. So why don't I like it?

I'm not a fan of card games. In fact the only card game I've ever truly loved was 1997's Magic the Gathering on DOS (shows my age). I don't hate them; I've played other card games, but I just never really loved them, and Inscription is a rogue-like card game.

Pony Island and The Hex had you progress in the game. Inscription only allows you to progress in the story. I don't like how my cards are always my adversary. No fight is far. I also don't understand the death cards. It's infuriating to me to have to go through such a long process to make something that's not even used in your next playthrough. I've never seen a death card in my deck. After the 10 time I was just completely over it.

I frequently have not been able to get to the 2nd boss because of crappy draws. Not anything about my skill or how I played my hand, especially considering that you usually only have 1 opening move. But when the game is dropping flying creatures, or creatures that hide and continue to do damage, and you have no way to combat that, I just don't like it. I died so many times to unfair fights I eventually decided I didn't like it. And I cannot find any metaphor for the crappy card game that makes me excited, or even motivated, to proceed any further.

In 5 hours I have yet to beat the 2nd boss, who is a worst cheater than the 1st boss. If that's the case, then the 3rd boss must be even more of a cheat. Why would that motivate me? I'm saying this as someone who's beaten Dark Souls multiple times. This game doesn't balance at all and a lot of players say that's what they like about the game. It's what I very much dislike.

And that's it! Playing an hour of a crappy card game, where I'm constantly being cheated on, so I can unlock 1 new story element isn't fun to me. Sorry\t\t\t\t\t
