\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption, right off the bat, is a gorgeous game.

The game mechanics are thoughtful and fun, the levels presented are challenging in a way that makes you want to come back for more after each loss. Every time I lost each game, I was excited to come back to my farthest point, to build upon my strategy. The addition of mechanics to each part of the game made it a constant learning experience, with constant deck rebuilding.
The 3D visuals are gritty and moody, creating a lovely, chilling environment as you get to the escape room aspect of the game. The lonesome cabin you find yourself in, combined with the haunting OST, make both the card game and exploration aspects extremely atmospheric.
But the main hook for me, personally, was the story and characters Inscryption presents. I implore anyone who hasn't played the game to consider purchasing it and going in blind, which really brings out the most of the experience. But, long story short, the tale this game weaves is charming, heart-wrenching, and straight-up chilling. I often was so caught up in the tactical aspects of the game the horror elements came at me when I was least expecting it, leading to a wonderfully horrifying experience.
