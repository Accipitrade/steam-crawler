\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn enjoyable little game, with a few nits about it. Without going into spoilers too much, the game is broken into 4 acts, though only 3 are main acts.

Act 1 is what you see in the demo, and is a real winner in atmosphere and gameplay.
Act 2 have a very different art style and gameplay, i will admit that the game felt a big bogged down during this act.
Act 3 is similar in art style and atmosphere to Act 1, and the game play is similar, though slightly altered.

The good:

* The act of collecting cards and empowering them (or creating your own) is incredibly fun
* The are some good tactics in the game, but it's not as overbearing as actual card games.
* There are puzzles throughout the game, that aren't super hard but are fun to solve.
* Art style in Acts 1 & 3 are really good, especially the boss fights in Act 1.

The Bad:
* I really, really wish I got to play more of the game styles that were present in Act 4, especially the "board game" one.
* Act 2 felt slow, I felt there should have been more story bits in act 2. I would have liked to seen the cards "talk" to me as they do in other acts. That method of story telling was really good, but not utilized enough as in Act 1.
* Not that the story was bad, but it does seem to take a turn once you finish Act 1. You can kinda pick up on it during act 1.

Overall, I highly recommend this game - the amount of game play, the type of game play, and the story are decent for the price.\t\t\t\t\t
