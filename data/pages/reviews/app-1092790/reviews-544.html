\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA gust of fresh air in an Indie market that, despite hiding many small unrefined diamonds, is slowly becoming more and more repetitive in its ideas. Great work from Mullins, who is showing a constant growth in the quality of its games, and is at the same time creating a narrative across the whole "Mullins-verse".

The only weakness I can find in this game is the narration, which is a bit messy especially towards the end, and with a few major mistakes that might turn up one's nose, but do not compromise the otherwise brilliant writing. Gameplay-wise, on the other hand, it's the most addictive card game I've ever played.

A well-deserved 8/10 for this exquisite game.
