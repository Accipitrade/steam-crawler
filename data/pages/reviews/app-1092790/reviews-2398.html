\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWARNING: This is NOT a deckbuilder.

Do not buy if you want a deckbuilder. Go back and beat Slay the Spire on Ascension 20 and we'll talk about a spectacularly well-designed, well-balanced game.

Inscryption is an old-school story-driven adventure disguised as a deckbuilder. The actual gameplay elements are too easy and almost none of your deckbuilding or playing decisions matter (which is too bad, because the core mechanics have promise, but battles are nerfed to the point of being irrelevant.) The author even admits to spoonfeeding the player so that they can win and don't miss out on story. Which is fine. Just know what you're buying.

Recommending because it's a well-executed story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti have never played a game with half as good an understanding of when a concept will overstay its welcome. the game is pretty cleverly designed to move you along to something new pretty much the moment you've got the last idea figured out. most games are terrible at pacing - mass effect 2 is considered a great game mostly because it had "pretty good" pacing - so playing a game with such a complete mastery over it is a real treat.

the gameplay is simple but very clever. i particularly appreciate the asymmetrical nature of how you play vs how the ai plays - the basic game mechanics are stacked very heavily in the player's favor, so when the computer throws some absurd ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ down on the board it always feels like a fair curveball rather than the game just being mean or punishing.

in general the real game is learning the game - beating the game is very easy if you play optimally, the game is all about figuring out what that optimal play is. once you've figured it out, the game does some manner of twist or new mechanic in pretty short order so that you're always learning how things work and adjusting your play to what the new optimal is.

at a glance you should already know the atmosphere and presentation are fantastic, i'm not going to go over that too much but the release shortly before halloween is pretty good timing.

tl;dr at 9-15 hours inscryption is a brisk, engaging play all the way through. probably my GOTY\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun and interesting card game with a touch of Undertale's approach to story writing. Exact card game mechanics change depending on Act. Act 1, arguably the best arc, takes a dark, horror, roguelike approach to the gameplay. Act 2, arguably the weakest arc, plays like the old, Gameboy Pokemon TCG; while still dark, it is a sharp contrast to Act 1, but it grows on you eventually. Act 3 is a return to Act 1's style, but drops the roguelike for a more traditional progression. There's also an ARG going on, for whoever is interested.

Overall a fun, interesting, and dare I say emotional game. The ending moved me even if I hadn't been with all the characters for too long. Personally, I think it's worth the full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBuyer beware.

Really great Act 1, I bought this on the strength of the Demo. Very atmospheric and fun. This lasted a couple of hours where the games changes and strips away all the elements that made act 1 great. Instead it becomes a very dull and basic version of the first act, complete with stripped down graphics and atmosphere. Lost are the cool rogue-like elements from the map.

I've heard Act 3 goes back to something like Act 1. Unfortunately Act 2 is not very engaging and I'm finding it hard to to stay motivated to play through it. I've played too long in Act 1 to get a refund so maybe I'll try and find a speed run guide or something for Act 2 so I can skip it and see if the 3rd Act makes up for the disappointment.

The store page is misleading, both in screen shots and trailer which shows mostly footage from Act 1, which is not representative of at least Act 2. If you want an example there is a blink and you miss it shot of the what the game actually becomes at 31 seconds in the 1st trailer.

Feels like a complete bait and switch, would not have bought had I known.

A real shame, the Demo (Act 1) was absolutely brilliant.

Buyer beware.\t\t\t\t\t
