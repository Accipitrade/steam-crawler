\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere do I start with this game? How can I describe near perfection in a manner that brings nothing but praise and highlight to its artistic and wonderful design? I have played this game for almost 80 hours, I tell you this so you may know that I genuinely have taken time to consider this game. So if you'll have me, I'd love to tell you more about the elegant masterpiece of game design that is Inscryption.

There is a tone to this game. One that bears dark, yet has the attraction to pull you deeper in each time you play. You will become accustomed to failure as you play. This game plays so simple, but grows in complexity each turn. It's complexity changes how you play, what you play, and why you play. I started playing for the tone and excellent story that Inscryption has, but I soon found myself charmed by the witted game play mechanics of a simple card game I could not describe as anything but short of a masterpiece. It is so simple, so charming, so artistic, and so.... well, powerful.

Each victory becomes increasingly satisfying. In the base game along there is plenty of content, but I found myself drawn fully into the additional content Kaycee's Mod. An endless rouge like card game that ceases to challenge my intellect and problem solving capabilities. You begin to understand the game and its patterns. I spent days trying to achieve one of the hardest achievements. Keep in mind I am no completionist when it comes to games, but this was different. I was drawn to it simply for the joy and endless challenge the game provided.

The story is just as satisfying as the game. Both are magnificent works of art and purposeful design that draw you in. I found this game hard to put down and I think most ages including the elderly can enjoy this game. This game was designed to be taken at your own place, a feeling of control in the game. A feeling that grows the more you play. It is that need to control and best the game that makes me want to play it.

This game is valued in my opinion above its asking price. I believe it should also be cited in an art museum for its beautiful craft, story telling, and significance on what a game can be.

More than what is expected of it.\t\t\t\t\t
