\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, so I love Slay the Spire (200+ hours of playtime) and Monster Train(100+).

I was EXTREMELY on the fence about buying this. I actually got it after I returned a game that wasn't what I thought it was for Steam credit.

Buy it full price. Sale is great, but the "story" makes it a full price purchase for me.

Hopefully more in the future for the game as far as expanding replay value which it does not have compared to the two above deck builders i.e. no daily challenge.

STORY

And because of the "story" it was a VERY original experience for me. I am sure it is inspired, but as a 33 year-old man I was surprised and it felt fresh and new. There is a point where I did feel like I got stuck a little or I would have likely beat it in one marathon while I was off sick last week.

Avoid spoilers. Go in dark for those dark web Inception vibes.

GAMEPLAY

It's a solid deck builder. It has great mechanics and I guess you could say four different card deck mechanics. It's a lot more expansive than that, but not so much as MT or StS. And that's not a negative at all. The story isn't making up for anything here. It really is in my opinion a perfect balance of story and gameplay.

REPLAY VALUE

There is no new game+, no daily challenge, there is multiple endings I guess?I only finished it once. And certain achievements you can unlock that you can miss. You may not see every card, but realistically not much there to me. Still the game is 10000% worth it even if you only play it once.
