\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is often very hard to make a cursed and found footage game but as far as it goes this is probably one of the best examples on how to do it right. Everything from the initial mystery, story buildup and climatic ending was done very well which keeps you guessing and theorizing as to what the ♥♥♥♥ actually happened in the end long after you have finished playing the game. The game play at the start was pretty hard I must admit due to your lack of cards but once you get the hang out of it and build a good deck it becomes really fun and still challenging (but fairer? ). The bosses were one of the things that stole the show for me, every one of them is unique and has an interesting and often unfair advantage against you which makes you in some parts think out of the box, I won't spoil the boss mechanics since it's one of the things that you need to play against in order to appreciate it. The aesthetics of the game were haunting spooky and pretty unnerving at the beginning but after that it became a bit cartoonish and not that great. The soundtrack for me was fine but nothing really that memorable or special. I would suggest buying this at full price since its definitely worth it, hopefully they will release a physical deck some of these days so that I can start collecting and building a real life deck to own my Yu-Gi-Oh loving friend nerds.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a fun adventure but there are some complaints.

I could see how act 2 was sort of not well designed gameplay wise, the game could've been better if you learned the card mechanics from each "card maker" in sequence THEN you move onto the final act which would be the amalgamation of all the different mechanics you've learned.
rather than: you learn the sacrifice set mixed with a few bone cards mixed in, then jump to all the different mechanics at once, then throw all that away and go back to using robo cards of only a single set.

maybe in the sequel they'll perfect the gameplay progression. However this game seems to be more story focused considering the cards are meant to be broken.

The shift to 8-bit wasn't very appealing either, it just screams "we couldn't afford to make this part of the game to the same standard". Even if there's a story reason for it there could've been a story reason for that not to be the case.

so:
Act1 is 8/10 (act 1 would be a 10/10 if it was challenging enough to be engaging, the moon boss is a joke)
Act2 is 5.5/10
Act3 is 9/10 (like act 1 but there's a bounty system so the game gets progressively harder the longer you aren't killed, but orobot exists soooooo)\t\t\t\t\t
