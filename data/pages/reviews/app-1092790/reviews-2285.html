\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are a fan of deck building roguelikes, then there is zero reason why you should not get this game. There appears to be great care put in to the mechanics of the core game loop, and I was continually surprised by how thoughtful the devs were with how people would be using their game. An example: I went to a location where you get items, not realizing I already had the max, as I clicked I yelled 'no' thinking I'd just wasted a move, but instead I got a card that gives items when played.

Everything in the game feels like it has that level of detail baked in to it, allowing you to come up with cheesy tactics that the game welcomes. In one location, you can combine 2 duplicates to make a better single card, finding that in addition to creature cards, I could combine a special currency card which later unlocked special enhanced cards at a trader.

Put another way, this game allows for a striking amount of depth in theorycrafting, while also giving you a lot of opportunities to actually make decisions -- I've played other deckbuilder roguelikes where I'll have a strategy I want to employ, but RNG prevents me from ever getting started. This one feels like you have influence and opportunity.

On top of that, the core loop is fairly simple (once you understand the mechanics, which are a bit more difficult than something like Slay the Spire) and fun while making you think truly tactically about where and what cards to place.

tldr: this is the best deckbuilder I have played in a very long time, maybe ever.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ta thematic masterpiece. I'm hesitant to say too much of anything because the very last thing I would want to do is spoil any of the surprises the game has in store. suffice to say this is far more than just a card game, as I'm sure you'll well expect if you've ever played any of daniel mullins' other games.

the gameplay is really neat and is expanded upon over the course of the game, and thematically speaking this game made me feel things in ways that I've never seen another game do (intentionally, at least)

it's an experience to be sure, and one that I'm honestly really sad is over. I suppose that's one potential criticism depending on what kinda game you're looking for: this is a game that has an end, so if you're just looking for a neat roguelike to play forever you might want to look elsewhere. as a related point, because this is a more akin to a game about card games rather than a card game itself, the game is not actually particularly difficult. it starts off very hard, but the game keeps giving you tools to make it easier so that you can progress. if you're experienced with card games, you might actually find it difficult not to win after a while. this isn't necessarily a problem, it didn't get boring for me at least, but I thought I'd at least mention it.

one final point, and we're veering into nonspecific spoiler territory here, is that the story of the game isn't really revealed fully at any point. it's the kinda game where all you get is bits and pieces, and the finale, magnificent though it was in many other ways (I don't think I've ever felt so many emotions in quick succession before), only served to draw attention to this fact.

all in all, fantastic game.

me talking to myself about the end since none of my friends have played this game [MAJOR SPOILERS (duh)]:
I miss Leshy already :( he was already my favourite character despite what he did in chapter 1, but playing that one last game with him in the finale as his time drew close actually brought tears to my eyes.

I'm also very curious what the games of the other two scribes would've been like, since we only got the slightest glimpses, but I know that it would've been a mistake to put any more of them in this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat I loved: The beginning. The cabin was the best phase of the game. I was so happy to play this survival horror deck building game where my enemy was unseen and was this terrible mask switching persona. The cards were fun and interesting, and it was a really good time traveling the cabin's maps and figuring out the puzzles inside and unlocking little bits of story and extra cards and effects. All in all the Cabin phase was a 10/10 experience.

What I liked: The plot line fore the first phase of the game was vague enough that I was this meta "player playing a player" of a game, and that was fun. The Death cards were a welcome consolation prize to dying. The cards were unique and interesting, and the effects that you could add into the cards made them that much more fun to play, as you could really empower low tier cards to be amazing.
The music was ambient, and the graphics were fantastic. No complaints.

What I didn't like: I ended up going for a ride that I didn't necessarily want to go on. I didn't appreciate the game changing tone and graphics from phase to phase. It just wasn't what I was expecting , and to be perfectly honest, I sort of rushed through the other content in the game hoping to go back to the Cabin.

What I hated: Robotopia. I wont spoil anything , but I found that I did whatever I could to just get this section of the game over with. I sincerely didn't like it.

All in all, if you're going to get this game, be prepared to have a bit of a game shift from phases of the game.\t\t\t\t\t
