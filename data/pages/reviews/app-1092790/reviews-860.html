\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game in three parts. All parts feature a card game, and play by (more-or-less) the same rules, but beyond that each is a very different playing experience.

I'm not going to go into the second and third parts, as there's really no way to talk about them without giving spoiler's to the game's excellent plot.

What it comes down to is that, although the game - averaged out through the entirety of a play through - is very good; mostly because the first part of the game is so excellent that the decline in the second and third parts don't manage to pull it down sufficiently to ruin the game (additionally, the coda of the final act was also really well done, so the game does redeem itself right at the end).

What makes the first part of the game so special? Atmosphere! Everything about the card game that you're playing, to the room you're playing it in, is dark, twisted, and tainted. Your cards are summoned out of the blood and bones of your own creatures (all your creatures look visibly nervous when you pull out a new card to summon). Power-ups involve sacrificing one creature to another, and everyone and everything you meet seems just a little bit off. Victory points are teeth on a scale, and there's an item that you can use to help tip the scales in your favor - don't worry, the pain is only temporary.

Sadly, the second and third parts just don't manage to capture that same creepy energy (excepting, perhaps, the third part's themed boss fights).

Still, I had a blast playing - definite recommend on sale, and worth considering even at full price (there's a demo for the curious).\t\t\t\t\t
