I love this game. But like all things I love, I vivisect it...
...to see how it can improve. Keep in mind I think this game is somewhere between 8/10 and 10/10. Part two is in the comments/replies hopefully.


1. Rework the movement sigils.

The 3 movement sigils I believe need fixing are the Sprinter, Hefty, and Rampager sigil. They all move right after your turn. The only difference is when they begin to move left.

This wouldn't be a problem if it wasn't for how I believe sigil probabilities are calculated. Just to keep the math easy, we will remove all sigils except those three and the airborne sigil.

The way I believe the game is set up now, there would be a 25% chance each that you’d get the sprinter, hefty, rampager, and airborne. That means that there is a 75% chance that you would end up with a movement card.

You’re disproportionately going to run into the same boring movement sigils. There are probably plenty of players that would agree it gets frustrating when you’re trying to trade a wolf pelt for a card, only to have decent cards marked disproportionately with movement sigils that are more trouble than they’re worth.

(Which reminds me: these movement sigils that are more trouble than they’re worth. Mostly it’s Leshy who benefits from having them. Us gamers have small ape brains. It's hard to keep track of all the moving cards. Leshy can capitalize off of that. That’s about the extent of these sigils’ use. Pretty lame.)

The solution is twofold:

A. Group all movement sigils together probabilistically.

Using the above example, if all three movement sigils were grouped together probabilistically, there would be a 50% chance you got any movement sigil, and a 50% chance you got the airborne sigil. In other words, there'd be a 50% chance you'd get airborne, and 16 1/2% chance each you got any movement sigil. If something like this is already the case imma feel pretty stupid.

B. Give every movement sigil something a little extra to make them fun.

Maybe with sprinter, you can choose which direction it goes. Maybe with hefty, you have the option to make it not move at all. Maybe with rampager, you have the option to Insta kill one of your own cards.

As a side note, making the hefty sigil easier to play would make the high cost starter deck more viable imo.

2. Make sigil rules more consistent.

Case in point: The beehive card and the ant queen card.

The beehive card makes a bee every time it gets hit. If you give the beehive a sigil, then that sigil spreads to the bee.

The ant queen makes an ant the second you put it on the board. However, if you give the queen a sigil, the ant it makes doesn't get that sigil. The only reason I can see for doing it this way is that ants are broken. However:

I. They're not.
II. Even if it was broken, the game was designed so that you could make unstoppable unbeatable decks. Stopping at the sigil’d ant queen/ant combo seems to betray the point of the game.

3. Kinship should be more prevalent, and there should be more tribes.

Many cards have Kin. The bee and the ant are part of the insect tribe for example, and are considered kin. This has important implications for gameplay.

However, there are 28 cards that don't have a tribe. (To save on word count, imma just count kaycee’s mod.)

There are 8 cards in the airborne tribe. You could add the bat. You could also add the zombie parrot. Wouldn’t be useful for players, but it would make the totem boss battle challenge against the pirate boss in Kacey’s mod more challenging.

There are 7 cards in the canine tribe. If it was instead the beast tribe, you could make the Urayuli, grizzly bear, and possibly some others candidates eligible to join.

There are 9 cards in the hooved tribe. You could add the daus. This card is widely seen as not viable for gameplay. This could possibly change that.

There are 14 cards in the insect tribe and 9 cards in the scaly tribe. Of the remaining kinless cards, there's none really that I would add to these tribes.

If they added a rodent tribe, they could put the pack rat, field mice, rat king, and beaver card in there.

If they did make a rodent tribe, they would almost certainly have to make more rodent cards. The rat king, pack rat, field mice, and beaver cards (most of the rodents in the game) all cost two blood. Their cost is mostly due to their sigils, not base stats. And the beaver doesn't even have a good sigil.

(Note: The dam building sigil of the beaver wouldn't be so useless if it weren't for the fact that the dam cards are automatically played. Maybe a beaver should put two dam cards in your hand, and you can play them whenever you want. Maybe whenever beavers attack trees, you get a free damn in your hand.)

The porcupine is already in the game, only costs one blood, and has a decent sigil. It's a prime candidate for joining the rodent tribe. You can make a black rat card that could be used to kill the settlers at the campfire event.

There's not very much three blood potential that I can find in the rodent family. Potentially you could use The Colo Colo from Mapuche mythology. Depictions vary, but it's either a large rat, Feathered rat, or a snake with a rat head. It is said to drink people's saliva while they're sleeping, eventually killing them. Symptoms are typically that of exhaustion. Maybe it's a three blooded or bone card, and it has a sigil that lowers the attack stat of an enemy it hits? This card could be a member of the rodent, reptile, and bird tribe simultaneously due to its mythology.

I don’t think that’s enough for the rodent tribe to be viable. But that might get things started.

If they added a waterborne tribe, they could put the shark, otter, and all three of the tentacle cards in there. Maybe the Kingfisher is a member of the airborne and waterborne tribes. They could also add some unique aquatic cards as well. See point 7 in the comments for more aquatic card ideas.

The amoeba could be a member of every tribe like the Amalgam. There's also potential to create fun blob tribe and add a couple more cards.

This plan wouldn’t give every card a home. But it gives a lot of them one. Instead of having 27 cards without a tribe, now there's only 8.

The remaining 8 are the Mole Man, The mole, the cat, the skunk, the stoat , the Warren, the possum, and the raccoon. If they were to somehow make a “generic small creature” tribe, then every card would have a tribe.

Just trust that if the genre seems like a fit for you and you are interested in card games with some original ideas and dash of dark comedy amidst the dread, this game has a lot of great moments waiting for you. More formally: A great deck builder that's flexible enough to let you approach problems from the angle you find interesting. It keeps the stakes feeling high the whole time, but flips the script often enough that you get to constantly think on your feet. The vibe is creepy and glitch/curse which feels appropriate for the medium.\t\t\t\t\t
