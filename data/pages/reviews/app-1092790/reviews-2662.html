\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[Review for people going blind into their first Daniel Mullins Game]
"A". A fun story driven card battler seasoned lightly with suspenseful horror atmosphere. Don't go into it expecting a hardcore strategy card game the likes of Slay the Spire. Inscryption is a card game with a buffet of mechanics that invites you to try and break the system while it simultaneously throws you curveballs. The battles aren't super hard but the accompanying story is a leagues more than you get from a standard card game and the lite horror atmosphere keeps it spooky without cheap or heavy scares.

[Alternate Review for people who have played Pony Island/Hex]
"A+" The best Daniel Mullins game so far by a landslide. The surface game in Inscryption is much more fleshed out than previous games with the same angle you've come to expect. I'm amazed at the amount of work put into this third game especially at this price point. The amount of surface gameplay, puzzles, lore, secrets, and deeper secrets packed into Inscryption will require more hours and replays than I have put into the previous games combined and I'm excited for it.\t\t\t\t\t
