\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a beautiful game. There's quite a lot to talk about here.

When you first start in, it seems like just another roguelike card-strategy game... but, without spoiling anything, it quickly transcends its category in wonderful and surprising ways. The puzzles are just hard enough to be fun, failure is punished only a little - and the fourth wall keeps retreating in novel ways.

The sensory experience is very well-considered... the music supports the unhinged narrative and inscrutable characters. It doesn't stick to one artistic theme for too long, while managing to keep the content of the game itself somewhat consistent - this really let me enjoy some of the visual detail that the game has to offer.

It's not an extraordinarily difficult game - it's more about paying attention to details and finding clues that help you make great leaps than it is about optimizing and improving gradually. That said, I got stuck on some puzzles, grinding away meaninglessly for an hour or two before finally taking the time to think about how to solve them.

It's hard to say much more without spoiling anything - there's a lot of commentary on gaming as a hobby and on the artistic space that games occupy. I think this game will have an even greater reputation retrospectively.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is SO MUCH FUN!!

I think a lot of people must be reluctant because they find the graphic style unappealing or the fact that it is a card game but they really don't know what they are missing out! Every time I played, the game keep surprising me with new elements of the story or game mechanics.

There is a whole meta story over the story so I will try to keep it to the basic story. You're a prisoner of a crazy dungeon master and he forces you to play his game. Every time you fail, you die, and it's actually someone else who plays the game. But the thing is: you can use what you learned from the previous game to try to defeat him at the next one.

The rules of the card game itself start very simple and they are introduced piece by piece (at very death). This is important because every game against this DM feels different.

As someone who is really not into card game and not into dungeon crawlers, I must say I really really loved this game! Dear reader, please don't miss out on this one, it's a truly fantastic game!\t\t\t\t\t
