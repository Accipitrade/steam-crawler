\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is great! The cards are fun to use and the various mechanics are all easy to understand for the most part. The puzzles are engaging, and the music is phenomenal.

The main issue I have with Inscryption is the direction for the lore/story, and how it was presented to the player. I did not feel very interested in the 'outer lore', regarding the narrator and the creation of the video game Inscryption. The narrator's interjections felt a bit annoying and like they took me out of the moment that the rest of the game had been building up, and were often one-liners or short quips. I felt much more interested in the actual game itself, and felt that this was its strongest point.

I feel in many respects, Inscryption gets analog horror right and is a very good game. I still recommend it for this reason, but the hardest part of any analog horror story like Inscryption feel invested in the greater lore of the game, and one of the most important parts of that goal is having a likable main character. I just had a hard time liking the narrator, and the way he interacted with the audience took away from my experience overall.

I may change my mind about this as the story progresses and I get to experience more of what Inscryption has to offer, but as it stands, the narrator's use as comedic relief has been a negative overall for me.\t\t\t\t\t
