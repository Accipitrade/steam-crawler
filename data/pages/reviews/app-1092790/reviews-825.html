\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't really put into words how great this game is... Just kidding I can

Inscryption is one of the most unique game I've ever played. It's a seamless blend of a card-deck-building-escape-the-room-horror game that focuses solely on the most important thing in this damn industry... Entertainment. From the moment you start you're thrown into this mysterious atmosphere that raises several questions, where am I? Who am I? And what/who is this shady being in the corner of the room beckoning me to play a game of cards with him? Immediately you're thrown into this card game you have no idea how to play, with a few pointers, you navigate through the first few levels and then you're on your own. You see, Inscryption is a game about dying and learning from your mistakes and getting better with each run that you do. With each death you learn a new trick, a new strategy that will help you get further coupled with the mechanic of creating your own "Death card" combining a few of the stats of the cards you've collected thus far.

There is so much to learn in this game man, the different points on the map along your journey, what those different icons are going to bring, maybe an upgrade or something that may hinder your progress... The thing about this piece of media is that everything is untold, sinister and uncertain. You don't know what you can trust and what you can't. A campfire allows you to upgrade a stat of your card and this is concurrent until a certain point where they just fudging kill off your card if you try your luck and this out of the blue just to keep you on your toes... This is just only one example of the encounters you'll have in this game without me spoiling anything vital.

And the bosses... Oooooo, they so fancy with their own mechanics making you hesitate while you think of your next move, uncertain of what they're gonna whip out next. You've got a lot to dig into with the bosses alone with the way they work, the differences they bring to the table while remaining fair. Also, don't get me started on the soundtrack, every track is perfectly thought out and encourages the themes of each boss or area you find yourself in.

Every once in a while between playing this fantastic card game you're given tasks to get an item from the cabin to continue the story. This allows you to take in your surroundings and explore this cabin you need to escape from - and this is exactly what you need to do in order to progress. In fact you can beat Leshy (the figure in the dark... Not really a spoiler giving his name since it holds no substance in the greater scheme of things) as many times as you want, but in order to actually progress you need to escape his cabin of horror. The narrative to this game is wonderful - Told solely through trapped spirits in the playing cards you wield, you are given hints on how to progress and I will note that the puzzles in the game can be solved by the most casual of players granted their perception is up to standards. The puzzles don't contain any working out of things, any extreme brain power to understand - all the answers are there, it's just your duty to put the pieces into place and when you do and finally escape the confines of this space you realize...

That this was literally the first act of the game, right onto part 2... No seriously, I wasn't expecting this but Inscryption actually contains about 4 different games in it. This is a long ass game, I mean I got about 10 hours in and realized I was about halfway through. The game changes about 3 different times and this is excluding Kaycees Mod which is another act of the game offering more of a challenge. Not going to go into much detail here about the other acts since it's something you need to experience for yourself, but just know that it includes one of the most intricate achievements I've ever unlocked as well as the most interesting boss fights I've ever played against in a game before.

With everything said about this game that I have already I will need to inform you that I've only scratched the surface - There's an overall plot that is brilliantly explored in an interesting way, loads of unique mechanics, game design and much more all waiting in this underrated gem. One of the most unique and engaging gaming experiences I've ever had, I give Inscryption a 10/10. Top of it's class in what it seeks to accomplish.

Now before I go I can never leave a game review without some cons so here it goes:
In certain parts of the game there are some cards that are way too overpowered and made me feel like I was cheesing my way through the second and third act. The first act thus felt like the most polished part of the game, but it was mainly just due to this fact.
Speaking of which, the third act of the game felt the easiest due to the overabundance of buffs you get for your cards. In fact, I only lost to one fight in the third act and this was only due to the fact that RNG didn't give me any cards from my deck that could deal damage in the first round so I couldn't set up a strategy.
Actually the second and first act felt like a breeze compared to the first one due to a farming strategy in act 2 (which mind you is required to get all the achievements so it's kinda forced in a way) and like I mentioned the overabundance of power ups in act 3.
In a way I eventually found the game to be way too easy and noticed this coming to the close of act 1 once I started to get the hang of the mechanics and strategies you could perform.

Regardless, this is one for the books. I'm intrigued to see what this studio produces next and look forward to it. ♥♥♥♥@t that's the longest review I've ever done...
