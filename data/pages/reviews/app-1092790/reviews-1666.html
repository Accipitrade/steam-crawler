\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is honestly going to be hard to review a game like Inscryption, and other reviews may do this better. But I just can not keep my fingers off the keyboard before I at least attempt to do this work of art justice.

As you may have noticed, the Steam reviews are plastered with people telling you to keep away from spoilers, and I completely agree. The game's story is made so that it would be best for you to hop in blind.
Spoilers would ruin so much of the story, and the blows of awe that you will experience in this game would become fully lost and bland. For the love of all that is holy. Stay, Away, From. Spoilers. Inscryption will blow you away with its amazing story and engrossing plot. If you let it.

The game is loaded with interesting and engaging mechanics. Right from the start of the game, all the way to the end. I was throughout the whole game blown away by the ever-expanding universe of mechanics that could be featured in but one game.

The music, sound effects, and such.
Inscryption has a great soundtrack that immerses you fully into the game. Much of the starting music has a dark very dark theme, that sells the first parts of the games. Not only that, but the music matches the characters that you meet in the game perfectly.

I can not recommend this game more. It is great in so many ways and makes you want to keep on playing till the end of time. Unfortunately, that is not possible. The game may not be one of the longer ones, but it is 100% worth your time, and price.\t\t\t\t\t
