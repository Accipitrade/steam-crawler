\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't really know what this game was before I bought it. I just saw a cool aesthetic, cards, and high effort from the devs.

Now that I know what this game is, I highly recommend it. It's not just a card game, and it's not exactly a Roguelike. Ultimately: This is NOT a Slay the Spire. If you're expecting that, you're going to be disappointed.

This is more like a story game that is told through a constantly evolving set of gameplay mechanics, meta-narrative elements, visual & musical styles, and quite a bit of ARG fun on top. The rules are ALWAYS changing. I never knew when it was going to end until it ended, and even then I learned that the mystery goes deeper, and new secrets are being found.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll briefly echo the concerns of many other negative reviews; This game feels unfinished.

The first Act is by far the best part of the whole experience. Unfortunately, the game shown in the advertisements is only 1/3rd of the game, and is quickly abandoned for a worse version of itself. After defeating the mysterious owner of the cabin you've been trapped in, you're plunged into a series of poorly acted found-footage style videos revolving around a trading card youtuber, marking the beginning of this game's downward spiral into meta-commentary hell.

The cabin section, or first Act, is one of the most thoroughly enjoyable games I've played in quite some time. It brilliantly unfolds into a complex rougelite deck battler married with clever escape-room style puzzles. This section is dripping with atmosphere, featuring ritualistic sacrifice, self inflicted bodily dismemberment as gameplay mechanics, and a stupendously composed ambient soundtrack, all packaged in a grim retro horror inspired escape room. Its great stuff.

The real shame is that this style of gameplay could have been continued through 3 more sections, each with their own unique atmosphere and mechanics, if only the developers had opted to. But instead, we are left with one of the most dissatisfying bait and switches I have ever had the displeasure of experiencing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust beat the game and I wanted to leave my spoiler-free thoughts.

First and foremost, the game is genuinely really enjoyable, breaking up the various acts with pauses that allow you to watch videos that make the whole thing double as kind of a mini ARG that left me and a friend invested in checking the binary and looking for secrets. While I will agree with people who are questioning the validity of the rogue-like aspect beyond act one (because the game doesn't function in the same way we're used to rogue-likes behaving), the game never really loses the flair of being a semi-horror card game.

By far, my favorite act was the first because it felt the most like what the game was advertising itself to be. It also personally felt the most mysterious due to the fact that the cabin had plenty of secrets and puzzles to solve without making you feel overly confused or like you were going far out of your way to try to solve them for just the benefit of an achievement. They also foreshadowed events down the line, which was a fun twist.

My least favorite act was act three, in which it takes a return back to the style of act one while, in my opinion, having a more grating soundtrack (though it fit with the style it was going for) and more annoying battles. I wasn't a huge fan of the sound of the bell and played the game with my volume at a much lower setting than I did in the last two acts. The puzzles in this act also suffered from mainly carrying over from the two acts before it, though the bosses were more entertaining than in act two. By far, in my opinion, the bosses were the saving grace of the act.

I definitely would love a DLC relating to the end of the game. Without going into spoilers, I can't say much more than that.\t\t\t\t\t
