\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot going on in Inscryption, and it's a lot more than the store page shows (and that's for story reasons, which is the main drive of the game.) Overall, it's still a good game, but it's best to come in with open expectations.

Eventually you leave the cabin and like most people, I wanted to spend more time there. Some people have issues with what follows, not expecting it or otherwise longing for the first act (that's Leshy's cabin, folks, what you see in the trailers and screenshots,) but I never really had issues with it dragging.

I feel like the biggest flaw of the game is that the great big surprise of the story isn't actually all that surprising or rewarding. The story is told in a well enough way, although there's some information that seems lacking even if you dig into the secret depths of the game, which I guess is okay if you just want a quick version of a deeper plot. There's a lot of speculation on breadcrumbs, but most of them seem to be dead ends or otherwise bland. Personally, I found it dissatisfying and further research never uncovered anything that helped to make it engaging for me. I struggled with feeling like I'd missed something, that I'd have to do some file digging or replaying, but that doesn't seem to be the case. On the other hand, things like the plot device of the videos felt fantastic.

That said, I can't say this game was a bad experience, but if you want to dig your nails into a deep and intense narrative, this might not quite scratch that itch.

I still believe it's worth playing.\t\t\t\t\t
