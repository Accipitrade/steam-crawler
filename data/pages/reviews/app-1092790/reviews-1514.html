\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I am probably the last person to even pick up a game that has horror in it's category, I have to say that Inscryption was the perfect level of spooky that didn't end up feeling like I was waiting on bated breath for jumpscares. The graphics quality has this nostalgic value that reminds me of this game called the 11th Hour that I remember my dad played as a kid. In that regard, I took the lo-res quality of the game to help with it's setting.

If you like drafting mtg or other card games, Inscryption is immediately an addicting experience, turning the mechanic of deck-building into a story-rich adventure, with each card gaining it's own emotional value and character. While the learning curve is steep, and it feels imminently difficult sometimes, every loss is actually just another opportunity to impact the story of your game. This game was impossible to put down, and the 20 hours or so I spent beating it flew by, and by the end I found myself watching the credits enraptured by the beautiful and complex experience that Devolver Digital produced here. Such an awesome game! It will go down in my list as one of my favorite games I've ever played, and I honestly had no idea what I was getting into.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven if you read this review having not played the game and for some reason choose to click on the spoiler tags, you'll have no idea what I'm talking about and not really be very spoiled, but if you've played it, you'll know EXACTLY what I mean.

I loved the beginning, the middle was rough until your deck gets built out because getting pieces on the board can be difficult depending on which route you took, and the end is repetitive and frustrating with clearly less testing and polish than the rest of the game. The middle was a lot more reasonable with the four leaf clover item letting you re-roll your starting hand once you find it, and I honestly wanted it to go on much longer than it did once it hit it's stride. But the last part of the game left such a sour taste in my mouth just engaging with the stupid battery mechanic your first several turns that I really don't know if I want to bother finishing the game, and with how invested I felt after the beginning and how much fun I was having with the built out deck and meta weirdness that was a talllllll task. I really just want an update to this game that makes the end of the game more like the beginning or middle, or that fixes the ending to be able to stand on it's own better.

The fact I'm recommending this game anyway speaks to how good the rest of the game really is. But honestly, I NEED this dev to release an update that fixes the end. That may sound like getting hung up on something small if you haven't played this, and honestly it's hard for me not to come across that way unless I spoil a lot about this game, so just think of it as your favorite book, series, game, movie, etc. getting towards the climax, and just before it can it ends. It's that kind of incomplete feeling. I wish I could give you more than "it feels kinda like when this happens" but trust me you'll get it once you play it (and you should play it).

And you know what? If they did fix it, I would have no problem paying the same price as the full game for a DLC that adds more of either the beginning section or the middle section as a 15+ hour extended experience. Please, Daniel Mullins, finish this masterpiece.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing part 2: I honestly hate it more than I hated playing through part 1. Part 1 has this super ominous, yet cool style and presentation. I wasn't a fan of Part 1's gameplay, but the presentation was SUPURB. Part 2 is LITERALLY that watered down and in 2d, and also somehow EVEN LESS FORGIVING. Despite having a permanent card inventory and the ability to modify my deck between battles as I wish with a wide verity of new cards, I can't really use most of them. It turns out, that the rules you played by in part 1, are only half of the card game! It sure is a damn shame that I barely have enough of the new cards to actually use them in a deck. Because these other cards of different requirements to play them. Requirements that are cards I don't yet have. But what's EXTRA frustrating is that I can't just fall back on old tactics cuz I don't have enough of THOSE cards either. Part 2, in my experience, is spent floundering trying to make a decent deck, because you can only get card packs by winning battles (which there aren't very many of) or buying them with foils... which you can only get from won battles that overkill your opponent. Basically, in order to get the cards you need to make deck that can win, is to win, or win HARD... which you can't really do without cards. Also, the presentation REALLY goes down hill hard. I can not stress that enough. The game becomes infinitely less interesting to look at and interact with the second you start part 2. No more unique puzzles that require multiple runs to solve. Instead you're given a combination lock with no penalty for failure, meaning the solution is to just try combinations sequentially until you brute force the correct one. Like, seriously: don't bother, unless you're confident you can get lucky enough beat part 1 quick enough to get a refund.

And I do mean lucky enough, because section 1 of this game is PURE rng. Some of it is how you play, don't get me wrong, but you CAN'T BEAT section 1 unless rng ALLOWS you to.

It doesn't matter how good you play.

How many times you turn it off right before death to try again.

If RNG says "you die here" there is NOTHING you can do, but sit make yet another ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ death card.

At first, the death card was cool and ominous, but it quickly became more like the game was rubbing it in that I didn't roll 15+ 20 times in a row. Because the death cards quickly become the best cards in your deck by a mile. Near the end, I contemplated deliberately throwing at a certain point just so I could flood the loot pool with OP death cards. Spoiler Free tip: Don't do that. You're only screwing yourself.

For example on how RNG dependent this first section is: I have only benefited from 1 totem combination. There is so much RNG involved that I have found exactly 1 combination of totems that did ANYTHING. It's the squirrel head. ALWAYS grab the squirrel head. It doesn't matter what sigil totem you get, preferably one that buffs either attack or what you get from sacrificing them. But yeah, I haven't gotten any use from any of the other totem heads. I always got totem parts that were either bad sigils or for cards I had none of. Usually both. I can not tell you how infuriatingly common it was to only get totem heads for types of cards I had chosen not to spec into, only to never get that opportunity again, or alternatively: I get a totem head for a card type I'd WANT to spec into early on, only to never get that option in my run. I actually can't tell if it's deliberate, it easily could be given the nature of this section of the game.

If I wanted to put my fate in chance, I'd go to Vegas. At least there my potential reward would be money.\t\t\t\t\t
