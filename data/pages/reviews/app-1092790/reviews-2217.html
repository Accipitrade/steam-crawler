\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game. Definitely one that's difficult to review without spoiling it. The game as a whole is a great journey with fun and unique mechanics and a fresh approach to deckbuilding. I've never played the dev's games before this one, and I was delightfully surprised. Totally worth a pickup.

I guess the only real downfall is you think you're buying one thing and get something a little different. Not all aspects of the journey are as rewarding as other parts or as fleshed out, but it's still good. After beating it, you're left with a feeling of what could've been if the same level of focus that was put on the advertised portion of the game was done with the secret portions that come after.

My only word of caution for people on the fence is you're not getting a singular deckbuiding experience. The cabin woodsy beastie-vibe is only maybe a third of the game? But like I said, it's totally worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just finished the game last night, and I've been pretty solidly hooked on it the last several days after trying out the demo.

It's super creative, the atmosphere is great, the card mechanics are great, the art style is great, and there's a lot more content than I initially expected tbh. It's "weird" in a kind of a Stanley Parable way (without actually being anything like that game). I've found that I kind of love "psychological horror" games (at least ones where the tag isn't added in Steam as a joke).

As a lot of other reviews have pointed out, don't go digging too much into the game before playing, because it really is best to start off knowing nothing and just go with the flow through the game. Play the demo if you're not sure. (There's a lot more game here than I expected from playing the demo though.)

I've got ~20hrs in game at the moment, and while I'm not sure I'll do a whole nother playthrough, I'll probably at least try to go back and look for stuff I might've missed, or just to enjoy the card mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t
