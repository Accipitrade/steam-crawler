\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBased on some of the negative reviews, I thought that this game might not actually be a card game throughout its entirety, but it is. The art styles change, and new mechanics are introduced, but progressing through the game is 100% tied to card battles.

There is a beginning and an end to this game. The ending is fantastic, and the gameplay along the way is really interesting! I will say that once you get the hang of the mechanics, the game becomes extremely easy, but I think that is purposeful so that the pacing doesn't fall off a cliff.

If you only want to play the first part of the game, there is an option to replay it after game completion. And if you do not wish to complete the game, do not pick up the caged wolf and you have yourself a nice rouge-like experience that will go uninterrupted.

Please developer, I beg you, make a standalone expansion to this game that adds new cards and events with the atmosphere and aesthetic of the first act. Highly recommended if you have even an ounce of interest in card games. The first act is worth the price alone, but everything after that is gravy.\t\t\t\t\t
