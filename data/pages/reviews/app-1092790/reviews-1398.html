\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only dislike very specific parts of this game and only because they weren't what I had come to expect from this game. If you truly are in love with card games and every single thing they have in store, I believe you can love this game. That said, I'm writing this to give you all a heads up about what to expect.

SPOILERS FOR MAJOR ACTS OF THIS GAME ARE AHEAD. YOU'VE BEEN WARNED.

The first act of this game and material found in a lot of the promotional material for this game is utterly awesome and fantastic. There is tons of nuance to fighting Leshy and interacting not only with the cards and their secrets but also the secrets of the cabin. It feels truly unique and of the same caliber of this developer's other games. (Pony Island, The Hex, etc.) I loved everything about that Act 1 and it had me hooked to play the rest.

Everything falls off as soon as you enter the game's second act and Leshy is no longer the only over arching opponent. The second act is in a retro inspired if not 16-bit world that has you fighting Leshy as well as three of his companions. My gripes immediately start with the setting change due to me feeling like it's a massive departure from the alluring style that Act 1 is immersed in. That minor gripe aside, the game play feels noticeably different with the introduction of three other play styles based around Leshy's cohorts specific attributes of magic, death, and energy. This wouldn't be so much of an issue if I felt like you could mix and match but the battles you face make that an uphill battle at best. All of this wrapped up to make Act 2 a slog and zapped my fun for the game.

Act 3 built on this by turning one of Leshy's cohorts into the main opponent, a la Leshy in the first act. This is a marked improvement over the second act but the differences are still weighed down by the fact that the opponent replacing Leshy is just a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. He's not likable and unfortunately that makes playing against him less fun than the playful rivalry you can work up against Leshy. It doesn't help that the twist and the "Act 4" of this game don't help to lift up the game from it's middle acts. This is entirely my subjective opinion but it feels like a poor attempt to replicate the twists of Undertale and it fails the execution.

There is a saving grace to this game though and that is the mod provided by the developers. It's called the Kaycee mod and is a rogue lite of the first Act. If you end up buying this and disliking the later acts, know that this game isn't one for the trash with this functionality.

TL;DR This game has an amazing opening first act. I highly recommend that section and the rest of the game is up to you to decide if you like it. Watch a play through of the first act and a bit or buy this and refund it if it's not your jam. All things said though, show this developer some love by keeping this on their radar as their stuff is nothing else if not incredibly imaginative with true gold gems in them.\t\t\t\t\t
