\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is intense, difficult, and wonderful, and if you love a good story this is for you. If you want a rouge-like similar to the likes of slay the spire; however, this game is not that simply because it is not technically an endless game. Despite this, this game still managed to be one of my favorite games this year, if not for the past few years. Definitely GOTY for me, up there with similar games I love from this year, Deathloop for example.

If you like stories and card games, horror and escape rooms, or just the feel of a rouge-like, please play Inscryption.



This game starts off as a seemingly normal rouge-like/rouge-lite, with a seemingly obvious end goal, escape the cabin. Over the course of the first act, you learn how the card game works, beat bosses, and play on changing maps similar in form to the maps in Slay the Spire. Upon finishing the escape room aspects of the game, you defeat the 'final boss' of the game, but that is just the beginning.

After this, you move on to life action videos pertaining to the lore of the game, to a pixelated rpg, where you keep playing Inscryption as you learned it. After that, you move to yet another 3d beautiful part people call Act 3, and then the game ends. That's right, it ends. That is why this is not a true "rouge-like"/"rouge-lite". The game is not endless, and it does end. While you can replay the game from the start, it is a story and it doesn't feel the same as booting a new run in Isaac or Slay the Spire.

tl;dr - The game is great, but if you are expecting an endlessly replayable Rouge-like game like Slay the Spire, you won't exactly get that. Hoping that the Dev's add DLC that adds this as a bonus, but as of writing this review, the game has an end. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is so well executed! Everything feeds into the spooky atmosphere of this game, the mysterious narrator, the candle-lit room you are in, even the game menu. Inscryption borrows a lot of aesthetics from games like Hand of Fate and Slay the Spire, but adds its own mysterious flavour to it.

The mechanics of the main card game that you play at the table are simple, but nice. Rounds can be really short if you are lucky.

All the scripted events in this game make it feel more like an escape room that you play through once, than a "true" roguelike game that you can play over and over, so I'm wondering about the length of a full playthrough and the game's replay value. But up until this point, it's a unique and entertaining ride, for sure!

Edit:

Yep, this game is amazing! People left negative reviews because they expected this to be just a roguelike deckbuilding game, so the developer made free DLC that does exactly that. This developer deserves your money.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought this game because of the trailer. Haven't played the devs previous games, but seeing it was published by Devolver gave me confidence that this was going to be a another banger.

This game starts out well. There are different parts/acts to this game and the first one is excellent. The creepy and mysterious atmosphere and surprisingly gripping card gameplay was exactly what i was looking for.
This lasted for 4 hours (Might be misremembering), after that the game changes quite a bit. Everything that made the game enjoyable and fun, lessens for pretty much for the rest of the game. The game does go back to play similarly to the first part, but i was completely taken out of the game at that point.
More focus is put on the narrative, which i did like, but the changes to the gameplay and increased repetitiveness made the game not fun to play. At points it was very boring.

I did finish the game though and i think i liked it. It's definitely a unique game, storytelling wise. And like i said, the first part was excellent so there's that. I don't recommend it though, because what feels like majority of the game, was not fun to play.
