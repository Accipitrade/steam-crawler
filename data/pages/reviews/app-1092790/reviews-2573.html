\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a legitimately fun game, but I feel it's the most disappointing of Daniel Mullin's works thus far.

The good:
The plot, for what it is, is rather interesting. The story throws in some fun little "gotcha" moments and homages to certain genres of horror.
The card games are fun, and the card-manipulation mechanics are some of the most enjoyably-OP things I've seen in a game like this.

The bad:
Some of the puzzles can be a little esoteric. The final puzzle of the first arc, involving the camera, has little pointing towards it beyond the tool's presence.

My main problem, however, was with the plot. With The Hex and Pony Island, you felt like characters were being developed on top of the hyper-meta narrative. Here...eh? Your antagonist gets a brief skosh of development at the very end, but the "protagonist," as it were, is comparatively bland, and his travails surrounding the game Inscryption somehow managed to feel bland.

SPOILERS BELOW:

I enjoyed the brief Marble Hornets-styled scenes with the camera glitching being explained by a low battery in the beginning, and Luke Carder was a likable, albeit pretty bland, character. The game manages to ramp up tension adequately with GameFuna, but the payoff is so generic and noncommittal as to feel like a let-down. I get that providing too many specifics surrounding the horror that reduced Luke to staring at a pair of scales and laughing would dampen the effect, but "People died over this, the game's development was mysterious, and a lot of this is redacted," feels like store-brand SCP stuff. I definitely missed some plot points because of the Trader sidequest, but contrasting the creepy and oppressive atmosphere of the card game with the utterly generic "corporate goons are out to get me" storyline in Luke's 'real world' just left me feeling underwhelmed. I'll probably go back through to figure out the other plot elements later, but as it is I just don't have it in me.

Still a good game, still would recommend, but this developer has done better.\t\t\t\t\t
