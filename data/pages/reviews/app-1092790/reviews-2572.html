\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great atmospheric roguelike deckbuilder with some really clever ideas. There is a solid variety of mechanics that interact to create some really cool cards. The pace at which the game unlocked new mechanics was neither overwhelming nor too much of a dribble. The atmospheric elements enhance the experience. I don't want to say too much about them lest it ruin some of the fun surprises.

I've played the game for 9 hours. That was a single sitting, which happened to start late in the evening and go til the early morning. So, maybe be aware of that risk. My partner was mildly annoyed that I'd played through the rest of the game without them, as they had been enjoying my running commentary before they'd gone to sleep.

A previous game by this developer, Pony Island, might give you an idea of what to expect.

If the name Glittermitten Grove sounds familiar, you might be interested in this. I think that's about all I can safely say.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm very mixed on my experience with inscryption. I went into the game expecting what you see on the store page and what I played in the demo. While you do get some of that, the bulk of the game is not what you see on the store page. Now I get that a lot of people seem to really like the story but I honestly just didn't care. after the 1st part of the game I was so let down by the quality of the gameplay I just didn't have any interest in the story the game was trying to tell. Some non story spoilers ahead


Without going to far into spoiler territory the game is split into 3 sections. The 1 you see on the store page being the 1st and best part of the game. This 1st part of inscryption is a 9/10, seriously I don't think that 1 single aspect of he game was unenjoyable for me. The deck building, the combat, the horror, the escape room elements and puzzles. It all landed and I was having a blast.

Enter section 2 everything that was amazing about the 1st part is either completely absent in the 2nd part or done much worse. deck building? still there but more like a tcg than slay the spire(or you know, the 1st part of this game). escape room elements? while you could argue they are still there, it was mostly a handful of very simple puzzles that required next to no thought or effort to figure out and solve. Psychological horror? pretty much all horror elements are absent outside of the last couple of minutes of this section. I think the closest thing to a compliment I can offer this part of the game is that the bosses where mostly ok. If part 1 was a 9/10 the 2nd part was a 4 maybe a 5 at best for me.

And then we have the 3rd section which is much more similar to the 1st. Except everything is just done worse. The game board/world just isn't as thematically interesting as the 1st. The combat isn't as good or hard as the 1st section. Only the last boss of this section ended up being memorable, I literally don't recall a single aspect of the other bosses in this section since they where so easy and boring. The escape room elements just where not as fun. And unlike the 1st part of the game where it felt like you where progressing with each new thing you solved, I don't feel like a made any progress towards anything by solving the puzzles in this section. Also I would argue that the puzzles just weren't as good. Some of which being simple tasks you could argue weren't even puzzles. If the 1st section was a 9 and the second was a 4 the 3rd and final part of the game was maybe a 6 or 7 for me. It just felt like the 1st part done worse in pretty much every way. I would say that this section is probably a 6 or 7 for me. Not bad but it just doesn't live up to how good the 1st part of the game is.

Overall the game is probably a 7/10 for me but with how meh I felt about the game after the 1st part I just can't see myself recommending this to anyone.\t\t\t\t\t
