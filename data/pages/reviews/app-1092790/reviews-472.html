\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a turn-based, horror, card game combined with various puzzles. Daniel Mullins developed an interesting and challenging gameplay around basic elements from cardboard games, like acquiring new cards and losing old ones, increasing attributes and etc. He also created his own elements to spice up gameplay and increase replay ability. But gameplay is not the only amazing thing in Inscryption, soundtrack and written story are pretty good as well.
The world and lore of Inscryption is both intriguing and well-written. There’s a lot of questions before playing and after, so there's quite a lot to talk about. But there's so little to tell because of the spoilers. I’m not going to spoil it for you, so you’ll have to discover it for yourselves. But there is room for one hint - the story of this one is much heavier than it may seem at first glance. You wake up sitting in a dark, wooden cabin against…something…or someone, fully covered in darkness, who simply wants to play card games with you. You don’t know how you got there. And you don’t know who are you playing with as well. But one thing is certain, the only thing you can do is continue playing against him, and hope for the best.
Gameplay in Inscryption is truly fascinating. It really makes your brain work up a sweat, because of constant cycle of thinking through your turns. You have to carefully plan your next moves and be ready to act accordingly, since it’s pretty easy to lose over a small mistake.
Game presents you a simple road map, resembling a pathway through the forest, filled with events. Your goal is to pass through this map and defeat bosses. You can’t go back and replay the same events over and over, and you can’t see the whole map. Each road map is randomly generated, so on every playthrough you will have different layout for events and battles. Each event will impact your card deck in one way or another, they can add powers to your existing cards, give you impactful items or an option to trade. There are a lot of events to go through and every single one of them is narrated for maximum ambience. Those events are important for buffing your card deck and you want that to win battles. It comes without saying that losing a battle comes at a cost. Remember that you have room only for one such mistake. If you manage to lose twice, you will start from the very beginning, so don’t treat it lightly.
At the start of each battle, your deck is always shuffled and the cards you’ve been dealt are always random, so you can’t depend solely on one card and win every battle with it. Though nobody stops you from buffing just one card and developing some kind of a strategy around it. In fact, it can be quite viable, if you’re capable of correctly managing your deck, and if the gods of RNG are merciful.
Deck management is another element that makes gears in your brain turn. As mentioned above, your deck is always shuffled on each encounter. You always have to take that into account, if you don’t want to lose in one turn. That’s why planning your actions on the map is very important, because only through events on the map you can modify your deck. Unnecessary cards can become an end to your playthrough, if there are too many of them. And you may want to keep some of them to increase the synergy across your cards. So, you’ll be constantly put in a choice between buffing your existent cards and removing the unwanted ones.
During your trip through the woods, you will be accompanied by magnificent soundtrack. The vast majority of music in Inscryption is melancholic and it does perfect job in supporting the ambience and describing surroundings. Each “theme” does a great job in expressing corresponding emotions. Can totally recommend listening to this masterpiece post-playthrough.
Inscryption is great for both, new players and veterans. There’s an intriguing story, interesting gameplay and mesmerizing soundtrack for new players to enjoy. While veterans will be more interested in building strong card decks, achievement hunting and Kaycee’s Mod. It can be quite long-playing. Though the game is much…larger…than it may seem on screenshots.

Inscryption is the true GOTY 2021.\t\t\t\t\t
