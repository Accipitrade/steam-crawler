\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI usually dislike horror games. I find many of them to be a bit generic, overdone, or dull.

3 months after making this review, I finally beat Act 1. It was a frustrating experience, but somehow still enjoyable. The gameplay felt unfair, but the atmosphere and plot was intriguing.

And oh boy, what a ride. Act II had the best gameplay because it felt like a "normal" card game, complete with multiple deck types, and then Act III showed the other theme of this game, the difference between gameplay centric and lore centric players and how the Act III DM (preferring gameplay) despised the approach of the Act 1 DM (lore).

This is a really fascinating game that does well in spite of Act 1's shaky gameplay. Act III is supposedly better, and there's cooler card variety, but frankly I liked Act II the most.

Act 1's atmosphere is what Devolver was advertising and it's amazing. People who enjoy narrative games more should just play Act 1 for that alone. But the whole experience is fascinating too. I don't want to post future spoilers, so all I can say is that this game has really frustrating mechanics only to reward you with quite a fascinating tale and better/improved gameplay in later Acts\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the best deckbuilders I've played. I would even say I like it more than Slay The Spire, which was previously my gold standard for deckbuilders. Inscryption's main mechanic is sacrificing cards to play stronger cards on the board, and this gets you to consider how long a card should spend their time on the board. Maintain your ground and try to have one card on a tile at all times, or sacrifice a bunch of them to play a strong creature like a bear. I also thought the story was good. It uses a lot of fourth wall breaking and reliance on metanarrative, but in a good way. A way where something hideous is lying underneath, and you want to figure out what it is exactly as characters in and outside the game world are stricken. Overall, this game is great. The second act is where the quality goes up and down, but this is a game I'll remember.\t\t\t\t\t
