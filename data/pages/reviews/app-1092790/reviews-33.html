\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played through this to completion on ps5 so disregard the hour count. This is probably my favorite indie game of the past 5 years, its between this and tunic. Dan mullins is an expert at utilizing the medium of gaming to tell his stories and this is no exception. i was gripped from start to finish, and without spoiling it takes a really interesting turn about halfway through. i know the segments after the main one in the cabin are a little divisive but the quality never dropped for me, i legitimately find it to be a masterpiecce. It also helps that on top of being an engrossing story its a genuinely fun game. I love dan mullins' other works, but i was admittedly more into pony island and the hex for their stories and the unique ways they were told rather than their gameplay, with a few exceptions in the hex. Inscryption however has an incredibly compelling and addicting gameplay loop, even in acts 2 and 3 contrary to what ive heard from others. Just bear in mind that the second and third act of the game aren't the same as the first. The 1st act is a card based roguelike, the second act is a traditional deckbuilder where you open packs and actually build a deck, and the third act is more similar to the first act with a few mechanical differences. I haven't seen any deckbuilders similar to this aesthetically or mechanically, although granted I haven't played many deckbuilders. I found the difficulty to be well designed with only a couple instances of rng ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ me over, I found the story to be incredibly interesting and unique especially after you clear that first act, the soundtrack is atmospheric and immersive, I found all 3 acts to be incredibly enjoyable and as I said this might be my favorite indie game of the last 5 years. I almost want to say 10 years. goty 2021, go and play this immediately\t\t\t\t\t

I also really enjoyed the soundtrack, it really added to the atmosphere and I often find myself listening to it when reading or studying for exams.
