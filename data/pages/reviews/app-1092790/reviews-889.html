TLDR: If it's on sale for 5 bucks, it's worth it. Download the mod that is being developed for it, than you might actually enjoy it.

This game is not what sells itself to be. If you deciding to buy the based on the demo, or the trailer, you are setting yourself for major disappointment, because that's just one third of the game. Very well made, expertly crafted part of the game, but depending on your skill and luck, it can be over in less than 4 hours.

I have to repeat this. The first hour of the game is NOT representative of the rest of the game.

After that, second act begins and it's a completely different game (and it also falls apart). No longer are you in mysterious cabin with a stranger, collecting cards and engaging in a rougelike deck building game. No longer are you thinking about the strange new cards you will be able to create on your next run. No longer are you thinking about what king of cards or runes you will uncover. All that mystery, is thrown out of the window, for frankly shi*ty Pokémon Red ripoff from original Gameboy.

3 more unique Decks are unlocked for collection and cards are now gathered in packs. However, everything is thrown at you at once. While the introduction of the first act guided you trough the basics of your deck, how sacrificing works, how the game works in general. Now you are thrown into a whole new game, with 3 entirely new decks to collect, without any introduction whatsoever.

The most problematic part is that in order to learn, you are going to need to read, but because this is a Pokémon ripoff, everything will be in scrollable textboxes. Have fancy ultrawide monitor? 4k? FullHD? Game doesn't care. If you want to know what a card does while in battle, prepare to scroll mindlessly because the text is only 2 rows high, for 10 rows of text. Add to it the fact that the fame uses barely readable font, so even the text that is there is a chore to read.

And this is in the part of the game, that introduces 3 decks, each of which has unique mechanics, interactions and meta. Add to it a bland artstyle compared to the first part of the game, no unique cards like the ones in the first act and meh characters and it becomes clear why people really dont like this part of the game.

The third act, if you get through the slog that is act 2, is similar to the act 1, however it is a downgrade in every single way. It tries to be soulslike, but just like act 2, it's sh*t.

The game master is no longer the lore telling, mysterious stranger. Rather, it is uninterestig, taunting, downright hostile robot.

The art, compared to the first act, it's uninspired. Yes, it makes sense in regards of what kind of story is the game trying to tell, but don't expect anything on the level of the first act

The gameplay. You are no longer on semi set path. You are now exploring an open-ish world, however you are still limited to 4 directions. Death is now extremly punishing, since on death, you respawn on a checkpoint and everything you cleared ahead respawns - wasting your time. You also lose money on death (dropping it on the ground). If you die trying to get to it to pick it up, it's lost forever (ala darksouls). There are shortcuts you can unlock and you can fast travel between checkpoints (bonfires).
Upgrade stations still exist. You can enhance your cards and you can probably combine duplicates.. but at this point, damage is already done.

I haven't finished act 3, because i just couldn't bring myself to do it. By this point in the game, you would expect to have a good deck, having a knowledge of how to play it, how to approach the bosses, thinking of what insane combination of cards and sigils will you play on your next run. Instead, after 20 hours, you muddle around with 1 attack and 1 defense cards, because you have to rebuild your deck from the scratch. Death isn't an oprtunity to create something unique, it rather exercise in frustration, because you wasted your time.

Not to mention, act 3 is mechanical deck and act 1 is a beast deck. So, if you didn't pick mechanical deck for act 2, you are not only building it up again, but also learning it as you go (and this time around, without any tutorial and with antagonistic person sitting opposite of you).

Overall, only third of this game isn't complete trash. The way it's marketed, trailerd.. it's complete lies. The demo is representative, but it doesn't represent the entire game.

The dev is working on an update, on a beta branch of the game. It's goal is to extend the ACT 1 into it's own rougelike game. When it's done, than the game might be worth it's asking price. Until then, steer clear.

Inscrypted is an exercise is poor game design and I wouldn't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
