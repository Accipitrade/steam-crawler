\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't need to say it but Inscryption is probably one of the best indie games that came out this year and is the best game I've bought in 2021 overall. Of course like any game there are sections that I don't enjoy as much as others but everything else about this game absolutely outweighs those few sections and makes the $20 it costs an amazing value for what you get.

(I'll do my best to avoid spoilers but to properly describe the game there may be some that I have to mention)

The first Act of Inscryption is absolutely amazing, it takes a couple of runs before everything is unlocked for you but after that you get to play what I would consider the best part of the game gameplay-wise. There are tons of strategy options and lots of secret or hidden things to discover both in the Cabin and with your cards. Every run that isn't successful tends to be another run where you learn a new aspect of the game, what another map marker does, the best ways to use a certain card, and another answer to something in the cabin. Even as I died time after time I couldn't resist starting another run and I did so for the entire first day I played the game which ultimately ended in a successful run as I started to get better and formulated strategies on how to use my cards. If this was the entirety of Inscryption the only thing I would ask for is for there to be more maps and bosses because of just how encapsulating the RPG/Cardgame is.

(Spoilers begin)


After you leave the cabin and enter the 2D version of Inscryption I started to enjoy the gameplay less, it changes some of the core components that you've spent the entire beginning of the game learning and on top of it a large amount of new mechanics are introduced. The game does at least try to section out these new mechanics and focus on them one by one but I didn't feel that I had anywhere near enough time to really become good with any of them. Aside from my problems with Act 2's gameplay I really enjoyed the story aspects it has. Stuff from Act 1 is contextualized all while expanding on everything you've come to learn so far and eventually progressing further.

Act 3 was kind of up and down for me as it mixes parts from the two previous acts into a new format that works surprisingly well. You lose some of the rouge-like aspects that were present in Act 1 which means that success is a bit more guaranteed (which for being this far in isn't the absolute worst) and for most battles it focuses on an improved version of one of the combat styles from Act 2. By giving allowing for more time to learn the new mechanics in a similar environment to Act 1 I was able to gain a pretty good understanding of how to play with the cards I barely used in Act 2. This act doesn't develop the story much as you play it but towards the end it really ramps up the lore and gives yet more context to the story.\t\t\t\t\t
