\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI originally reviewed this game positively but after experiencing much more of it I'm changing my review.

Bear in mind: I got into deck builders by playing slay the spire, so naturally that game shaped me in terms of what I'm after when playing a deck builder.

With that being said, I can't help but feel like this game has an identity crisis. Its more of a story game with deck building elements than it is a deck builder. For that reason and others I have to give it a thumbs down.

First of all, the combat is not rewarding. All deck builders have RNG, but this game is soooo heavily RNG its to the point of just not fun. The only way you'll win is if you HAPPEN to draw that ONE good card in your deck early on. This seriously sums up 99% of the fights you get into.

For example, there was a particular battle I got stuck on in Act 3. (The theme was the P03 stage) I kept losing to a certain battle and every time I came back the battle was configured exactly the same. Same units, same quantity of units. It took me 5 tries and the only way I won was because I happened to get the perfect starting hand. Otherwise it was gg.

It gets to the point where winning takes such a huge roll of the dice that when you lose and have to start all over its rage inducing. I do not feel inclined to go out of my way and play this game for fun because it's like pulling teeth.

Secondly, the deck building aspect. Its... bad. Oh hey here's 3 cards. YOU MUST PICK ONE! Oh, they're all trash? Add it to your pile of 80 cards anyway. Have fun! I mean, there's 0 synergy. Again, the deck building also comes down to "Welp, I have 2 GG cards. Everything else is trash." If you don't find those GG cards early, game over because every enemy just cheats. Yup- enemies do not have to abide by the same rules you do yet they combat you in the same way.

What I mean by that: enemies fight you with the exact same cards you fight with, except without all the restrictions. Surprise! The enemy can place 5 ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ cards in 1 turn and you're stuck with 1.

TL;DR - If your idea of a deck builder is anything like a "traditional" deck builder (Monster Train, STS, etc) you'll probabblllyyy be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd like to start by saying that if you're buying this game strictly based off what you see in the demo and on the store page, you may be disappointed. The second and third act of the game do not play the same way shown in the aforementioned mediums. That said, fans of Pony Island will be very familiar with the style being presented here: this game is a weird and wonderful trip that has some very high highs and plodding lows.

Much like Pony Island, not everything is what it seems. This game is essentially several games within games centered around the card game aesthetic with a psychological thriller motif. There's a lot of lore buried away in the game that may take several playthroughs to find. The game is built on an interesting premise, although at times the execution can be lacking. Act 2 of the game slows down the action significantly and adds new rules that sometimes feel more burdensome than fun (the gem system can feel tedious). There's also a false choice that leads into Act 3 that made me wonder what the other options could have yielded. This game, once again similar to Pony Island, feels more like playing a disturbing tale written by a horror fan.

Act 3 was the most fun segment during my first and second playthrough of the game. Act 2 was slightly more bearable the second time, but overall, it feels incredibly slow compared to the other 2 acts. The final moments of the game are all phoned in for the story, but they do introduce interesting playstyles that I would have enjoyed experiencing like I did Act 1.

Overall, as a fan of Pony Island and weird wonderful horror movies, I enjoyed playing the game and exploring what it had to offer. As I mentioned before, if you're not familiar with Pony Island and only enjoyed what's in the demo, the abrupt change of pace in Act 2 may kill your enthusiasm. Personally, that section of the game reminded me of hunting duelists in the card game present in EVOLand 2. The second section felt like a grind and the slow pacing weighed down the narrative. This game is incredibly interesting with many twists and turns. I recommend it to anyone looking for something that's not quite what it seems on the surface.\t\t\t\t\t
