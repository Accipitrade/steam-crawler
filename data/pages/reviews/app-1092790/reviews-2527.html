\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to start by saying that this is a 10/10 game and I recommend it for everyone, maybe even people who don't like card games.

That being said, it is not the perfect tower-climbing deckbuilder. It has all of the pieces there but it never puts those pieces in the same place.

Once you beat the initial tower-climb (and you've done some puzzles right), you move onto more things.

The disappointment of this game, to me, is that all of these extra things after the tower-climb, are so good, and I'm such a big fan of tower-climbing games, that I really, really wish there'd just be one definitive game, where I get to use cards designed for all parts of the game at the same time. Or maybe even multiple definitive campaigns, eg, a campaign made by the necromancer, where you move like on a chess board, or a campaign made by the wizard, etc..

At the end of the day, I will still probably pop into this game often to do a run or two, and for that I'd say it's still a very good roguelike.\t\t\t\t\t

Inscryption: The Most Inventive Digital Tabletop Game of the Decade

Edit (~7 Hours in) This game is worth every single second spent in it. I've just had my mind absolutely melted by this games direction and I'm completely flawed. Truly unique.

Woah: First things first, it needs to be said that this game incorporates so many different aspects of game design from the many obvious inspirations of games equally great (Myst/Rivern, D&D, Slay The Spire) while being so creatively fresh with the perspective the games world reveals itself to you as, it is genuinely hard to pigeon hole this game in any genre.

Inscryption is a nebulous, encapsulating, genre defining experience, and is very much worthy of it's own pocket in the realm of what makes a game a game. Not Rogue-like. Rogue-compare.

The card mechanics are sound, very straight forward, easy to grasp, familiar yet creative and they handle well. It's a good deckbuilder, can't say much more given what's already been said.

The story in it of itself, told through your companion card, Stoat and the ominously anonymous "Game Master" more like reasonably benevolent, homicidal psychopathic maniac genuinely seethes an aspect of dread into the atmosphere continually. Despite the CRT effects you immediately forget the initial soundbite the game starts up with, implying you're just a dude who fired up what could be an old game cart.

Inscryption is a very good game. A must experience. Genuine Art.\t\t\t\t\t
