\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti slogged through the whole game because of the hype, and it was not worth it at all. i kept waiting for the curtain-pull that made the game not a drag, or would at least make the story interesting, but it never came. people kept going on and on about how many unexpected twists the game takes, but it only takes, like, two. it's certainly unique and all, but it's not this revolutionary story that people made it seem like it was. the payoff was not worth the grind of having to finish the game.

TLDR: game is waaaaaaaaaaay up in its own bum, and doesn't earn it by delivering a good story OR a good/fun game. the twists and turns and expectation-subversion you may have heard about is extraordinarily exaggerated and not really that interesting at all, especially when not complemented by a fun game. which it is not. the game is not good, the story is not good, and yet the game acts like it's good enough to get to have the flat-out antagonistic relationship with the player that it does, which i will elaborate upon in the rest of the review.

spoilers ahead.





the first "act" of the game is truly awful, for several reasons, but two big ones.
1- the game has walls that halt your progress very suddenly for no reason. if you do too well on your first two runs, the game force-kills you for no reason other than making you restart so you can see its up-its-own-ass dripfeed nonsense. the only reason i didn't get out and get my refund after the first force-kill was because of how many people are fellating this game, and i wanted to make it to the payoff. the payoff doesn't exist, by the way.

2- it's very easy. exceedingly so. the game has almost no challenge whatsoever, which also makes it nearly guaranteed that anyone that's played a video game before will end up running into both of the force-kills i mentioned in #1, which is just awful. and since you are forced to do at least three runs of this extremely easy game(probably four if you don't google what to do with the knife, and carry it to the last boss, like one would assume you would have to do when the game tells you that you need to use it to defeat leshy), it gets very, very boring.

the second act is whatever. it's as boring as the rest of the game has been, after you get over the fact that it's a different game now, which takes like, twenty seconds.

the third act is the best part of the game, but that's not saying much. i still felt like i was just slogging through to get to the "payoff", which never came, since this ended up being the last act of the game. the best part of the act, and the entire game as a whole, were the somewhat humorous and unique boss fights in this act. which is kind of sad. they weren't really even "humorous". more like "novel".

now i will list some complaints i have about the entire game in general.
1- the player character (the card game youtuber guy) is insanely unlikeable. it made investigating the lore in the recordings nigh unbearable since every moment spent listening to the guy talk is miserable. not to mention, he isn't even confined to the recording-watching sections, and also yells out at random times throughout acts 1-3, and every time he did, i hoped it would be the last time.
2- the sound assaults. there are certain parts of the game that just blast your eardrums for no reason. i had my volume down even lower than i usually do for games, so that i could watch videos while i slogged through, trying to get to the nonexistent payoff, and the loud parts still made my ears ring before i could react and pull off my headphones.

i learned to always temper my criticism with compliments, but i can't really think of a single positive thing i can say about the game, honestly, other than that it tried to be unique. it failed, but it tried. the gameplay is really bad, the story is really bad, and the game feels like it's pushing you around like an elementary school bully instead of trying to provide you with entertainment.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me make one thing clear: I loved this game.
No, seriously. The horror elements, the little puzzles (I didn't mind being told what to do, I tend to need that due to the fact that I'm not always good at puzzles), the card games, the fact that the game got more and more expansive every time you died. This game was shaping up to be one of my, if not my favorite game ever made (Yes, it was on the level of Undertale and Our Life for me. That's very hard to hit).
And then we get to Act 2. Spoilers from here on out, but TL;DR: If you like this game for what it is advertised as, DO NOT BEAT IT. Die at the final boss and repeat that cycle. Trust me, you'll be better off.
And so of course, the marketing deceived us. I will admit, I got curious and looked up a few things. I thought people were exaggerating. I thought "Hey, maybe the genre is gonna shift to more walking around or be more based on escape rooms and puzzles instead of the maps. I can cope with that." BUT NO. INSTEAD, IT GOES 2D! Not even lying to you, the battle system almost looked unrecognizable due to how suddenly they changed things. Instead of the only thing that was in the demos besides the footage (which was totally cool and I enjoyed seeing it) WE GET 2D! And I'm guessing that the "Escape Room" part is literally just Act 1 (take that with a mountain of salt though, I don't have confirmation). Welp.
Look, Daniel, you're really good at what you do. But here's the thing: No one would be mad if you advertised the game as something with a genre shift. Did Doki Doki have a genre shift? Yes, but it did not change the core gameplay, and therefore there didn't need to be a warning (besides the obvious trigger warning). For something like this, where it changes the entire gameplay, style, and completely alienates you from Act 1, THERE NEEDS TO BE SOMETHING TO INDICATE THAT THE GENRE SHIFTS. HARD.
I wish I could love this game. I really do.
I hope the team takes this project as a learning experience and makes the best parts even better. Just don't neglect giving people what's advertised because you want an ARG.\t\t\t\t\t
