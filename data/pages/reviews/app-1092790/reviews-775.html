The first act of this game is VERY STRONG, in fact I probably would have enjoyed the game more if it were just this and nothing else. However, the tone and style shift of act 2 was so jarring that it made it difficult for me to progress with cards I was dealt (pun intended) with the same investment that I had in the early game. Act 3 was... something. Did I enjoy act 3? I'm not sure.

Ultimately, playing Inscryption felt like playing several card game demos who arrived in a trenchcoat claiming to be a singular game - while there's a lot of interesting and cool ideas, and many things I enjoyed, their presentation resulted in none of them being presented to their fullest capacity. The gameplay loop(s) are fun, but the overarching "story" wasn't particularly compelling to me; while there's some deep lore in here for folks who REALLY like to dig for it, the payoff isn't worth the amount of work it takes to find it.\t\t\t\t\t
