\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOK so this game was fun. I finished it. The characters are quirky and interesting and the story is pretty engaging. Some of the fourth-wall breaking is neat. But I was pretty disappointed considering the number of "best game I've ever played" reviews out there, and I wouldn't personally recommend it over the other high quality deckbuilders out there.

Mechanics - the game will strike those who are used to card battlers as simplistic and unchallenging. In a couple of the acts there are easily discoverable OP combos that ruin the game. Those times that you lose aren't really instructive, it's more just based off luck most of the time since the deckbuilding mechanics are limited. Arguably this is because in this game the cards are a bit of a foil for the rest of the story so the easy progression is there to get you through the game. The second act is arguably the one with the most strategic variety, but because of the order you have to do things, most builds aren't practically attainable so again you're quite constrained. Similar comments apply to the "escape room"-style sequences. If you're used to escape rooms, you'll be wondering what you've missed because the puzzles are too simple. That would be fair enough, but...

Atmosphere and story - This game has a serious identity crisis. There are three completely separate aesthetics used for the major parts of the story, combined with other again different elements thrown in. 3D art, low poly, pixel art, video cuts, it's got it all! Characters that made sense in one part are still there in another where they are now bizarrely out of place. The story itself is meant to be unsettling and a little hard to follow on purpose I think, but honestly I don't believe it hangs together and ultimately the ending was an unsatisfying conclusion to me.\t\t\t\t\t
