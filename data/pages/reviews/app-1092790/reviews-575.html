\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is one of those meta games that does something pretty interesting with its narrative and is very likely to just make you go "woah" out of nowhere. It immediately brings to mind games like Undertale and Oneshot, in that sense. That being said, more than those games, Inscryption keeps playing with and reinventing itself, both narratively as well as mechanically. And mechanically the game is pretty interesting from the get go, with a surprisingly engaging card game that changes its rules just enough as you play to remain familiar, while still remaining fresh throughout it's 8 to 12 hours playtime.

Inscryption really is as good as people say it is. Maybe not as emotional or thought provoking as Undertale. Maybe not as deep mechanically as Slay the Spire or competitive TCGs. The final act is possibly a bit too long for its own good. But the whole package is enough of a mix of different concepts to be very enjoyable throughout.\t\t\t\t\t
