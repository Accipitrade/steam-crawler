rewriting on finishing the game. i spent a long time humming and musing to myself on this one, on one hand i can see why people are annoyed, and on the other there not reviewing it for what it is.
but here is the kicker, you cant talk about it with out ruining the best parts , so that will follow on latter, as its better un spoiled
IS it worth the full price? i honestly cant say, get it on a sale if you must, but pick it up ether way: i would like to see more by these devs. maybe they could go bigger and better next time.
it is worth being played because there are so many moments i found myself going " oh Sh*T yeah!" when the mood swings and the music kicks in and they play there parts.
arc 1 "one hell of a boss!!"

but those are taken from you to do what ? ending is horse sh*t. i think that is fare to say.

on the whole this is not so much a game at all, but a story being told. and that was a twist wasn't it. so its how it tells this story and how the game play supports the story being told: is what we must review:
we spend arc1 building a deck in what we feel like is just an tutorial with one hell of a boss, wow, such a moment!! if we are ever to replay this game again at all it was for that moment.
i was looking forward to the odd feeling of training wheels going away, with the way the deck / power ups was being into'ed, built as a slow "this is how it all works." to come off and to be let out into this rich world setting to build and follow this along. but its only there to slow us down.
it all means nothing.
you get to a new area / arc and just get a new deck.
the sprite world is supposed to be how the game was pre curse(?), but it tells us nothing, some things happen, but they dont pay off, the story is not told, your just left to go ?? ? and if your picking this up at a later point , a year or more? you not even going to have the forums posts or web walkthroughs that actually explain what is happening in the story because the game dose not do it.
i refused to look them up because if they cant tell there own story in the game, why should it been known.
i did like how the binary code didnt mean anything, wasted time typing decoding zeros and ones, and also liked the locations lat & long given were nowhere. but according to the internet there are ARGs in this game, and that's just ♥♥♥♥ ant it. i didnt find them .
im just, not a fan of them. and in something that's superposed to tell a story? no0
also, if you pick to replace someone else in arc 2, nothing changes but 2 lines of text, so you don't need to go back to this hell hole part of the game.

arc 3 is such an meh flat "at least im out of sprite world" but still so much less then the 1st.
its more game themed and for it, less fun. i dont know, its hard to talk about this one the most, he is "pulling off his plan" but then we are slowly building up a deck again that's only so it takes longer to play the game and this time , we are not dying.
and so there is that... the same way always being able to re-spawn leaves you shooting everything in GTA "it means nothing". creeps in.
the bounty hunters are the most interesting fun and going for an high star rating is recommended.
you find some things, there is some "hidden data." to be found / traded but ??? why dose he scream at the end of this game? the only thing i did look up (and how i found out about^ ARGs), no one knows. no one understands .
, but it felt like a punishment to want to find out the story ? i was 1 pelt short and . i just didn't want to put up with it any more.

then that ending ? wow, they look interesting. whole new decks again, only to be taken away so fast, maybe worth seeing again how it plays out if you play your hand perfect. ???.
but as someone who tells stories, this is not how you do it. its all, just out of your hands.
and then it closes . after seeing what we did not get? i don't think many people could reopen it after that. that was some huge bad story telling. \t\t\t\t\t
