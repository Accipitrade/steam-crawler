I will also recommend the game with increasing amounts of spoilers and details. If you are on the fence -- I suggest reading my minor spoilers which may help push you one way or the other.

VERY MILD SPOILERS

This game is a meta game, with significant amounts of detail being poured in by the developer. The primary draw is in *learning* and *experiencing* the game rather than playing it. Overall the deckbuilding mechanics are poor compared to other games, but this is an amazing game because of the meta aspects. If you have any interest in meta games, this is a very solid recommendation.

HEAVY SPOILERS

The game plays out in three acts, with each act functioning very differently from the last. Each of them plays very differently, with only the final act "overstaying" its welcome. The final ending is exceptional. There is also an ARG that plays out if that's your sort of thing. It has been solved by the community.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a tough one for me. If there was a neutral option, I would most definitely use that instead of negative. However the absurd amount of positives this game has gotten will negate this one negative.

So basically the advertising/images and overall selling point of the game is a rogue-like/lite card deck builder game. However do a little research and you'll find the game goes down a rabbit hole and quickly becomes something different than advertised so definitely be wary when making this purchase. While it's not intrinsically a bad design choice, I signed up for a deck builder based on the current steam pictures/videos/advertising so it ended up being off-putting when the game completely switched bases.

There are arguably *four* sections of this game but the fourth isn't really a section of the game that you play through, and is more or less a series of scripted events to somewhat tie together all of the overall messy and spaghetti of a story the game tries to convey. The first and third portions of the game are the closest to deck building rogue-like that you will get with the second portion of the game being a blend of deck-building and pokemon style gameplay.

Woven throughout this game is somewhat of a story that is neither vary engaging or very interesting. A lot of the story is told through "found-footage" video logs that were just tedious and boring to watch (so after a while I just didn't) about a guy at his computer wondering what's going on. The protagonist isn't very interesting and I found myself not really caring at all about the messy and vague story being conveyed (again being conveyed primarily through forcing you to watch cringe video acting instead of telling the story through in game mechanics).

I wanted to like this game, I can appreciate what the developer tried to do with this game but in the end I found about only 50% of the game enjoyable and the rest of it became just a slog to grind through. It's not a bad game, I just really wouldn't recommend it to anyone seriously interested in deck builders or CCG style games. This game is more of a silly love-letter to the genre with a lot of satire sprinkled throughout.\t\t\t\t\t
