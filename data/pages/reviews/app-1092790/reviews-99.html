\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou are entrapped within a small cabin somewhere you don't know, with a mysterious being whose haunting presence looms over you at every moment. Your one way out is to obey its rules and beat it at its own vicious game.
But beware: you are most certainly not going to make it.

Rarely if ever have I played a game that grips you by its atmosphere and tone just as Inscryption does: From the tight enclosure of the environment, ominous and throbbing sound effects, a ragged and stained artstyle to the frantic shivering animations... playing this game turns your room into an abandoned shack and holds you hostage until your life has run out.

Be it my most beloved aspect of this game, it far exceeds my expectations in almost every other regard.
The gameplay mechanics are well thought-out and intertwined, maniacal texts bring characters to life and the beautiful soothing soundtrack always steps into the forefront.
Analysing the board, taking your time to strategise two steps ahead and building a potent card deck is key to win but can be so easily neglected by the wake of your arrogance.

The only criticism I had with it was a rather low second stage but I can't express myself further without spoiling the plot. Just know that it is worth pushing through and finishing all of its chapters, additionally unlocking a very fun endgame.

If you can bare to fail a few times until you have learned this game's mechanics and systems or are not too easily discouraged by minor dependencies on luck for your playthrough, then you will find absolute joy attempting to master and abuse its systems for your sake and win the game.

In my eyes, this is a must-play for everyone and an icebreaker for who have thought not being interested in card-playing games.\t\t\t\t\t
