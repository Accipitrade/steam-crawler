\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't really like card games. I used to as a kid, but I find games being targeted at children and the sheer number of cards on the market allowing for old, perfectly balanced decks prohibitive. Inscryption has none of that, and has an interesting cat-and-mouse story and puzzle game aspects that draw you in. Also, this game has some unexpected twists that can't reasonably be predicted (this is a good thing).

If physical cards come out maybe I'll buy them and get to be one of those early masters of a card game for once. There's a lot of potential here, for not only player-vs-player but also asymmetrical RPG style gameplay like this game, and maybe even sequels to this game if the developer is interested.\t\t\t\t\t
