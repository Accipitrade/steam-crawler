\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI spent the weekend playing Inscryption from beginning to end, so here's my take. I'll do my best to mark any spoilers.

TLDR: Game good. Bought it on sale for $10, and it was a good use of that money.
The game comes in three acts, and the first act is phenomenal. You probably saw your favorite youtuber playing it. It's the one where you're in a cabin playing against an old man. The atmosphere is top-notch, and the card play is excellent. Spoilers begin below.

The second one I was less fond of. On the one hand, it introduces a few neat mechanics, such as gems and electricity. That's kind of neat, but there's a big difficulty spike here. Additionally, the style of the game switches to an 8-bit aesthetic. It looks nice, but it isn't quite the primo spooky vibe of the previous act. This, I think, is where a lot of people quit playing Inscryption.

Next is Act III, this is the last real level of the game, but there's quite a lot to it. You're playing against P03, a robot guy. He's a great opponent, but like the old man in the cabin, he's also willing to explain new mechanics. By the end of his act, he's almost sort of endearing. The vibe isn't quite as spooky, but we're back in 3D, which is nice. It's closer.

Then there's the finale. I won't bother spoiling the story. The finale has a bunch of different mini acts for you to play as the story wraps up. I feel like a lot of them were ideas that didn't make the final cut, but that just makes me wish they had. Imagine if, rather than the 8-bit section, each Scrybe got a full act like the old man or the robot did. Perhaps we'll get a sequel with more of that.

All in all, the game is good and I recommend it, especially if you like deck building.\t\t\t\t\t
