\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFair warning:

**Do Not Buy This Game If You Are Looking For A Roguelike Deck Builder. This Is A Story Game First And Foremost And You Will Find Out The Hard Way.**

**Spoilers ahead**

No game has ever made me feel so betrayed. The gameplay you have seen on the trailers and demo are only 1/3 of the game's playable content. After beating the "Final boss" you are immediately locked into the newgame+ route. This is where the mask slips and you're treated to a wildly different game. The cardgame aspects are still there (infact it is still the main gameplay loop) but all the mechanics you've learned up until this point become irrelevant. No more room wandering for secrets, no more companions, no more items, no more gritty horror artstyle. Instead the game literally resets into a pixelated card game RPG and the lore is forcibly shoved down your throat.

This was a mistake. The beginning was flawless and the mechanics and horror elements were immaculate. Which is sad the game takes this route as you had a perfectly good game already. Instead, im left mortified by this completely different game. Everything i loved about the beginning is gone and im left with something else. That's the real horror. The balance also seems to suffer from oversaturation. With the removal of items/upgrades and the addition of 3 new deck mechanics, im often left with far too many unplayable cards. It almost seems worthless to have any of the expensive cards as often times you wont have the resources to play them as they are not given to you like the squirrels were (at least at the beginning).

I am reassured by gameplay videos that at least some of the games original aspects may return like the 3D tabletop, but not enough to say i will enjoy them as much as the beginning. You can forget about the sacrifice mechanic entirely and enjoy a turn based mana system which has been done to death.

As much as I complain, this is still a good game with a lot of polish, i just wish i knew that the game that i would fall in love with was going to be so short lived. If you don't mind any of what i said above, i would whole heartedly recommend this game, but i personally wouldn't get it knowing what i know now, and I'm sure many others would agree with me. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t-- Presentation --
Inscryption is a horror game that's more subtly unnerving than in-your-face scary, which is unique. The creepy atmosphere is very well done, thanks to the art, music and sound design. The art is dark and grainy, but clear to read at the same time. It looks cool, supports the mood and doesn't get in the way.
The game is very immersive. Using the pliers just feels painful, and when I used the dagger you get later on, I physically winced and looked away from the screen in real life, it's super good.

-- Replayability --
You should know that the roguelike element is actually quite downplayed. It doesn't have that much replay value after you complete the game (I beat it with 16.5 hours of playtime). If you're looking for something you can sink 100's of hours in, Inscryption is not that kind of game.

UPDATE: a new endless gameplay mode has been added!

-- Gameplay --
The main gameplay is fun and requires you to make tough decisions. Some of the boss encounters have such wild mechanics that you really shouldn't spoil for yourself, these were the highlights of the game for me.
I did find it a little easy to make OP cards though, which, although being satisfiying, trivialised some of the encounters. There are plenty of optional puzzles that make the game easier when completed, but the lack of difficulty made it kind of unnecessary to actually engage with those puzzles.

Overall, Inscryption is a very unique experience. It's one of those games that's more fun the less you know about it, so just try it if it sounds interesting because it's definitely worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t
