Act I feels like a "Slay the Spire" kind of run where strategy and luck both play a part. Of course, there are cards like Ouroboros which are completely OP, but you need some time to discover these untold secrets. With just a few runs, you will learn the basic of game mechanics. Just be creative with the ways you exploit them. It feels like an endless cycle until you defeat the boss and enter Act II.

Act II is a bit brief and with a sudden information overload where you are introduced yet more game mechanics. Due to the Ouroboros I obtained in Act I, it became relatively easy to beat the bosses here, so I didn't explore the map fully but manged to go into Act III. That being said, Act II is more like a transition so in terms of depth and liberty, it is less than either I or III.

Act III returns to an Act I like card building game. Just keep your deck small and compact so you can draw the cards you want. Avoid taking in new cards unless you have a way to use them elsewhere (recylce/ trade for new cards). It's fun to see your friends' avatar as cards in your boss fight, interesting mechanics.

The ending is dark and dramatic which helps you stitch together the short clips that you saw in Act I and II. The game destroyed itself, together with the OLD DATA and then it resets itself.\t\t\t\t\t
