THIS IS NOT A PURE DECKBUILDER OR CARD GAME (NOR A ROGUELIKE).
This is a trippy horror-adventure game that uses cards as a base to move the plot forward.

That is for informational purposes only. Still,¬† it is a very interesting, original, and unique game in many aspects, but there are just too many positive reviews, so I will focus on what is bad:
1. act 3
2. Card gameplay
3. confusing, never explained, and purposely too enigmatic plot

ad 1.¬†
The best part of the game is Act 1‚Äîbest graphically, most engaging, and most interesting. Even the story could have been fully told there.
Act 2 is different in both good and bad ways.
Act 3 is a chore with too many filler fights with non-existing deck building (you have only one run and one try for your deck, so where is Roguelike there?) just to get to a linear ending.

ad 2.
I heard that gameplay was purposely made "easy," so almost anybody can beat the game. The problem is (as you can see from reviews from other people) that this is not true. The game is not easy if you are not good at card games; it is very random (especially act 1) and hard initially (all acts), where you have a weak start.
The game gets easy when You get powerful cards that can, in 1-2 turns, just win you a game (like a mantis god with a +1 or more power bonus) or some flyers with a lot of attack. It gets even absurdly easy then: draw a card, play it, and win‚Äîevery time. Works on all bosses in Acts 2 and 3. To be honest, it is even hard to call them bosses‚Äîjust another random easy fight.
There are a very limited number of cards, and most of them are useless or just very weak. It feels like playing Magic the Gathering with only common creatures from one specific set.
You get reset on your deck in every act, which could be refreshing but is just boring, and you can't really build a good deck here (like in Slay of the Spire)‚Äîyou only need 2 good cards. Synergy doesn't matter if you don't have the time, possibility, or need to try or make it.

In conclusion, an average or even below-average deckbuilding game

ad 3.
I do not know this game maker. Some people call him genius, but I know one thing: if you can't understand the plot unless you play his other games, then F... this.
SPOILER ALERT:
I really like mysteries and Dark Souls' storytelling with minimal information, but this is different. This seems so dark, mysterious, and convoluted just for its purpose. Why is there "old data" in the first place? Why couldn't it be just a magic-evil game as simple as that? Questions above: even after watching YouTube videos explaining the story, there is still no information at all, and everything is a guess and not in good sense (good sense would be a protagonist walking toward the sunset).
On the plus side, this left me with some uneasy feelings and questions. Made me feel something, which is always good in the game.


TLDR:
This game is medium-core in terms of mechanics. Do not play this if you want a roguelike or deckbuilding game.
This is more of a quiz/adventure/horror game, a strange experience with vibes similar to Doki Doki literature club but with card game filler.\t\t\t\t\t
