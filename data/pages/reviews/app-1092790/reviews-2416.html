\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start off this review by saying I absolutely adore this game, which one might have guessed by the positive recommendation. There is an immense amount of depth to it that is already amazingly fun. It's difficult to master, but satisfying once it is. That having been said, I did find the ending somewhat disappointing. Without spoiling anything for readers, I'll say this isn't because of its creepypasta nature. I actually really enjoyed that aspect. Rather, we're teased with the prospect of much more content to the game, but it's simply a set piece for an abrupt ending. The game rushes through what I assume were intended to be the foundation of two more chapters with intriguing characters and new mechanics, but unless I missed some important choices or puzzles, these are, as mentioned before, only a tease. Not wanting to spoil anything for myself, I looked up to see if there were multiple endings to the game but not how to get them, and if I should replay through to see if I can unlock these things. It seems it is the developers MO to leave cryptic hidden messages within the game, which is commendable, but still not quite what I was hoping for. All the same, sudden ending and feeling of missing content aside, the game is still quite fun. Just don't expect what you might feel should be the "full" experience. At least as of this posting. I'm still hoping for an update but I don't believe the developer is planning to do this, either out of creative vision or lack of resources to develop it further, both of which are understandable.\t\t\t\t\t

1. I wish we could have spent more time in the lairs of Grimora and Magnificus just like with Lushy and P03. I'm interested in knowing what kinds of worlds they would have set up for the player, and it's a shame that they're delegated to 2 fights that are way too easy and unimportant.

HOWEVER, if you read the games description and played that demo and think you are getting a solid rougelike deckbuilder, DON'T BUY THIS. It isn't slay the spire or monster train, if you looking for a rougelike look else where because this isn't it. But I still stress that if you like card games and are willing to do some puzzles and deal with some shenanigans Inscyption is a easy 9/10 though its a shame its rather short.

Overall I only game this game a thumbs down because I, like many people was looking forward to a new rougelike deckbuilder and judging the game by thats its a 3/10 at best.
BUT if you can look past that and instead go into it looking for a linear story based deckbuilder, its a 9/10.
So again you want a rougelike? look elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t
