\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I finished the game.

The game had me hooked right from the start. Mystery was building, the villain was compelling, the atmosphere was dripping with suspense and I was almost entranced while also being constantly kept me on my toes.

But then it changed.

This might be considered SPOILERS; so here's your chance to look away.

The game takes an abrupt left turn in order to tell a story, which I personally just wasn't interested in or felt was very compelling.
Throughout the rest of the game, I was still secret hunting and hoping for some more twists and turns to come up, but unfortunately I basically predicted the entire plot from then on. (the hidden backstory plot didn't help soften the whiplash you get from the change to act 2)

Mullins, I know you're the ''Meta guy''. But the meta aspect is so brute-forced into the game that I just didn't find it compelling or enjoyable either. And honestly, I think that if you had stuck with the act 1 portion of the game and fleshed out the story, the cabin and gameplay the game could have been absolutely amazing. But as it is, it feels like a simplified predictable mess.

The gameplay itself also takes a nose-dive after the first act. The additional gameplay elements are mostly gimmicks and the one prevalent strategy is just playing aggro. The fact that you only draw one card per turn and have such a tiny hand size and no worthwhile available draw option makes most of the attempted complexity pointless, as they lose to the aggro strategies before you can even attempt to use half of your collected cards.

Here's hoping that you make a standalone version of Leshy's game. It might be a pipe dream, but it would be a great thing.


The game is a borderline bait & switch as it stands.\t\t\t\t\t
