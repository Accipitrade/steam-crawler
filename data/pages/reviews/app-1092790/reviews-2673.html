\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI find myself wishing for a mixed opinion option. I find myself angered and frustrated by what I was sold versus what I eventually got. The advertisement, demo, and what drew me in are the first part of the 3 parts to the game. The biggest issue is the game and trailer seem to hint that there's MORE to the cabin but in all actuality, if you watched anyone go over the information about the secrets in the cabin, then you've basically seen them all except for 2/3 extra things that are needed to progress the plot and then leave the cabin.

My biggest issue is that the cabin becomes short if you know what you're doing. Even ignoring the game forcing you through a few extra loops in order to have enough time pass to actually unlock certain things, (That's fun to learn there are hard limits. Although you can surpass a soft-limit if you get lucky and are good enough at understanding the mechanics) the cabin is relatively short compared to the other 2 parts of the game. And that's kind of problematic. The first part of the game is my favorite, and I argue, the best part of the game. The mechanics have the right feel, relative balance (there are a lot of unbalanced cards purposely put in there to make sure you are 100% guaranteed to progress at some point), and a very good aesthetic. The mechanic of the side deck with the squirrels is one of my favorite parts. I generally dislike card games but this deck builder scratched my itch.

But, if you've read any of the negative reviews, you then run into the big issue. I won't go into detail for spoiler reasons but the game changes twice more. The second part felt like the longest but the 3rd part was probably actually longer. The second is essentially a traditional collectable card game. You have to build your deck for the duels and it introduces a bunch of new mechanics. But the main issue for me is that lack of side deck. I have had fights be over quickly because of insanely bad draws. Some of the 4 factions just kind of suck. It's also not helped that you are literally getting 4 different mana types that all function differently compared to the cabin where you have 2, the sacrifices and the bones. The second part is a slog for me. It's not fun and has no interest to me. If the game had just been the second part, I would not have bought it or played it.

The 3rd part is a variation on the first. Although the 3rd part eventually uses a lot of the mechanics of the different factions from the 2nd part. The worst feeling though is that the cards you get just didn't interest me. The system involved what was essentially a mana system like Hearthstone, only you can increase it faster with some card effects. The 3rd part at least has the side deck and some interesting mechanics but it doesn't have some of the better deck building aspects of the first. There is no resetting your deck. It just keeps growing larger with a few minimal ways of removing cards that are not repeatable. I consider this a double edged sword. I also found the cards a lot less interesting in the 3rd part. I don't know why but the abilities just didn't draw me in. The lack of variety in your side items also didn't help me. The side items are essentially set in stone with a refresh every checkpoint. There's not the assortment you get in the 1st part.

Funnily enough there are also 4 bosses in all 3 parts and the 1st part has the best bosses. I had to actually think about how to approach each boss and got caught on their gimmicks the first time. The second part I can't really evaluate because I just hate the system, ignoring the bosses. The 3rd part, I can comment on 3 of the bosses. One of them is laughably easy because of it's save-state type gimmick IN YOUR FAVOR. Another boss is kind of easy with how things are designed if you know your computer and where its oldest and largest files are. The final of the 4 bosses is insanely dumb. It is so RNG based that it took way too long in order to finally win it. And I only won because of someone else because of how the gimmick works. It wasn't a hard won victory. It was baffled relief that I didn't have to attempt that fight again.

Now for the story. I'm going to keep it big picture and just say that the straight story kind of made sense. At least, the plot of the characters within the game. The larger plot didn't coalesce for me. I probably missed a bunch of secret information or something. The reason I say this is because I got to the end, saw it, and then went, "Is that really how they ended this? Why?" It's not the worst ending but I saw someone ask if there were alternate endings because of just how baffling the choice they did for the "RL" plot was. I expect there will be some analysis videos by youtubers that will be required for me to even understand half of what's being hinted as happening.

End Summary: I do not want to say this is a bad game. It isn't. The problem is that it's deceptive in how it advertises itself and the best part IS the beginning. And that's my biggest issue. The beginning set up expectations and I was let down by the rest of the game.

Edit: After a second time through in order to gain the achievements I didn't get because achievements weren't fully working on launch, my opinion has stayed the same. The 2nd Act is a type of game I don't enjoy and the 3rd still has its same issues and actively sabotages you by offering you way too many cards when a small consistent deck is much more important in Act 3. Especially since there are no easy ways to remove cards if you get too many.\t\t\t\t\t
