Disclaimer: this is a review from someone that doesn't like card games.

-------

TL;DR:
If you know the author, you know he likes those meta stories of "a game within a game" and stuff; this is no different. If you played "The Hex" and "Pony Island", you know what to expect. Playtime is around 10h, 20h at most.

- If you like puzzles and Easter Egg hunts, this is a great game.
- If you like card games, pick it up on a sale.
- If you don't like card games but like weird games, it's a maybe/worth a shot.
- If you came because of the "Rogue-like card-game" fever, you'll be disappointed very pleased in the endless mode "Kaycees's mod" (despite the name, it's a free included DLC).

- Finally, if you don't like card games but are curious about this one, watch some gameplay, play the demo and then decide if (and how much) you like the mechanics (keep playing the demo) or the mystery (buy the game).

Be warned that if the game tells it's gonna delete a file, you better believe it and make a back up of it!

-------

Inscryption is... an experience. Why? Because it is a game but the focus is not exactly on the game or the story, but on experimentation. It plays around with the player. Are you playing because of the mistery, of the enviroment, the card mechanics, all of them...? Well, none is fixed. Everything is ever changing. You liked this? Too bad, its gone! You played this way? Oops, not anymore!

In many ways it tries, in a friendly way, to introduce players on card games, with the good and the bad including the grind of opening packs of cards! The game's mystery is pretty much a creepy-pasta one, with all its seriousness, parodies and depth (or lack of); a mix of the tone both his previous game had.

That being said, is it a good game? Yeah... it is. But it really feels like those good old flash games. More precisely, 3 games in 1. Then, why the negative review? Because... it felt just as shallow as those old flash games. But maybe that's probably because the game parodies itself all the time (with a hilarious reference/joke at the end, might i add), never taking itself too seriously.

Another thing that bothered me is that it breaks its own immersion. The game pretends to hack your computer at times, but then it shows its hacking the protagonists computer, but your files and friends are shown. This dissonance in narrative really ruins the mood that the own game creates. You can watch Markiplier's playthrough and see how annoyed he is when the protagonist butt's in the story.

Still, i enjoyed the gameplay and part of the story. Like i said, if you like puzzles and weird, creepy-pasta games, this is a good one. If you like card games or is curious about the fuzz, wait for a sale. If you don't like card games or came because you like rogue-like deck builders, stick to the demo only.

Oh yeah, the game also messes a little with your files and friends-list. That might bother some people but it's nothing too serious. Just Alt+TAB out of the game and backup the files before selecting them.

If there's a DLC expanding the story and enviroments, i might change to a positive review.

-----

Edit: Well... the rabbit hole was a lot deeper! The true game background story was a huge Easter Egg hunt, fully documented in a .doc file called "DaBigKahuna's Big Ol' Catch-Me-Up Inscryption Guide(Secret Ending Hunt)". Also the free DLC includes more lore and an endless mode for the rougue-like fans.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot just any card game!

I saw a youtube video about this game, the video was about 3 hours long but I watched only 20 minutes as I did not want to spoil myself, but got me hooked!
I love card games but mostly because of the strategies it involves, it makes you think. I got bored of games such as Hearthstone and the likes but I said to myself to give this one a try since it's on sale. I made an amazing choice!

I will try to make it as spoiler free review as possible:
It is a card game with slight horror vibe to it as you are stuck in a cabin with some kind of supernatural psychopath killer and you need to beat him at cards to escape, some cards can speak to you. At some points you can take a break and look around the room and there are some PUZZLES to solve in order to help you out in defeating the cabin guy. A combination of card game + puzzles + once you beat the cabin ..... holy sh** you need to play it to find out!\t\t\t\t\t
