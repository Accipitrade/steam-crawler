-In-depth deckbuilding game
-Superb storytelling
-Creepy af plotline
-So many layers to this game

Cons:
-3rd act can be tedious
-We aren't smart enough for Daniel Mullins

Overall:
This game is fantastic, the story is spine-tingling from start to finish, and every new layer of plot reveals just makes it more and more unsettling. This is a masterpiece. If you like deckbuilding games, at all, you will love this game. Remember that winning and losing are, for the most part, irrelevant. It is more important that you play the game, explore, and experience everything there is to experience.

As a spoiler-lite warning for those of you buying based off of the ambiance in the trailers - those trailers are all of act 1. There are 3 acts in the game, and while Act 1 is a cabin in the woods, Act 2 goes full 8-bit overworld and feels extremely different than Act 1, and Act 3... I will say nothing, because to describe it is to spoil some of the plot. Just understand that the ambiance changes, while the core gameplay stays mostly the same (with added depth as more events are experienced, to the benefit of the game itself). I give this warning, because while I was able to push through that initial whiplash going form the superb graphics of Act 1 to the (intentionally designed) 8-bit garbage-tier graphics of Act 2, not everyone will necessarily handle the shift as easily.

Don't look at spoilers, genius. You're just another one of those player-types, aren't you?

Bottom Line: One of the best, most intelligent, most cleverly made horror games I've ever played. Buy it at full price. Give Daniel Mullins more of your money. Buy it for friends. Get Inscryption out to as many people as possible. Let them share experience. The whole internet deserves to experience this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMaybe I'll change my mind if I can bear to pick this game up again and actually beat it (EDIT: I did), but right now I wish I could refund it after 10 hours in. Inscryption centers on a card game where your opponent always has better cards and doesn't have to play by the same rules. Consequently, you die and start over a lot. Initially, this is accounted for in the game and is not much of an issue, but by the third act the game becomes prohibitively difficult and extremely punishing, and dying is no longer an opportunity to do anything except get reset back to an earlier checkpoint, after which you must slog through everything you just did again. At least 1 of my 10 hours played has been spent re-fighting battles where one wrong move kills you - and, of course, they're semi-randomized, so there's always new chances to slip up and lose your progress even if you've won this battle before. The mechanics of the card game do not really evolve at the same rate as the difficulty ramps up, and you end up wasting a few hours by the time the novelty wears off and you realize you haven't had fun in a while. Game isn't worth it unless you're some kind of card game aficionado or you think some quirky ARG addon will make up for problems with the fundamental game, but judging by the overwhelmingly positive reviews, maybe that's everyone else who bought this.

EDIT: I ended up finishing it in the next few hours. I looked some things up and remembered to use some mechanics that had slipped me by, but I maintain the third act is excessively difficult. A lot of sites and people mention this is actually a fairly complex and well-put-together card game. I don't play many card games so I can't speak as to how it compares, but I did find the card game very engaging and addicting in the first two acts or so. I had a very fun time learning how it works and coming up with strategies where as I wasn't able to hold my attention in other card games like Hearthstone or Magic: The Gathering. However, I do still think the gameplay gets harder to the point of no longer being fun as it goes on, even with all the items and powerups and strategies at your disposal. The third act is probably not enough to nullfiy how fun the beginning of the game is, so I would say it is still a fun overall experience, but I don't know if it's exactly a must-buy. The plot is really interesting while you're in the game and is revealed in a very creative way, but once the smoke clears it doesn't really stick with you. It does stand on its own as a moderately creepy campfire story-type tale.\t\t\t\t\t
