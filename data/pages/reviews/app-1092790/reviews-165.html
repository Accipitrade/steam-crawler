\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough the creepypasta, early 3D style works for the game at first blush, but starts to feel a little "cost-saving art style" than "well-realized pastiche" after a while. The card game half of the game is frustrating, as you constantly have to move your point of view to see the AI's current board, future moves, and your own hand. Every other digital card game I know of, and Slay the Spire specifically, let you see the full gamestate that without breaking a sweat, as a missing feature it's a little embarrassing.

A lot of the presentation works, and finding interesting combos in your deck is fun. The ability to fuse cards in particular is a strong selling point. I like the various characters, and the game itself is fun, although my deaths rarely feel earned. There are events, resembling puzzles, but more likely, you stumble upon them by blind luck, that are an interesting mutation of the game, but not in and of themselves satisfying to solve.

There are a lot of good ideas here. If usability (like only using pictures for creature abilities, so you have to click on everything all the time to see the rules text, aformentioned annoyances with having to move your view around constantly, and poor support for when your hand of cards is more than about 5, and better indication, other than the symbol, that a creature has flying) was better prioritized, I'd happily flip my review.

I plan to finish this game. It's unique enough to be worth a slot in my library, and has a certain charm to it. I regret paying $20 for it, and I think that I probably would have been happier with another game on my wishlist instead, but I played it enough that I'm past the refund period, so I guess I'm keeping it. I plan to finish at least one run, but I'm not hugely invested in the underlying story/mystery of the game.

Excited to see what the devs do next.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first third or "act" of the game was really fun. The game took a nosedive into the same terrible "multi-genre" approach the studio took with Hex. I'm not sure why this got so many positive reviews. It doesn't really hint at what the rest of the game will be like, and the last two thirds of it are such a drag. The card playing is fun, but the story is one of the worst. If you like the idea of playing card games and getting stronger, go play Hearthstone or Slay the Spire for some of the map elements. If you're looking for a good story to accompany those elements, look elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t
