\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI usually don't ever write gushy reviews for stuff I like, and I tend to stay my distance from the review scene, but I have to say this:
This is the best $20 I have EVER spent. It's a great price for what you THINK you get, and an even better price for what you actually get. It has quite literally everything I look for specifically in a game.

It's story rich, it has a genuinely fun gameplay loop, its aesthetic warms my heart every time I boot up the game, and more that I refuse to talk about, to not spoil a single thing.

It's not perfect, but there are so incredibly few things I can nitpick about this game. It seems short on a first playthrough, but that's countered by the crazy rabbit hole that is -REDACTED-. Sometimes the game can put you in a quite literally unwinnable situation if you draw a bad topdeck, which isn't terribly common, but frustrating if it does ever happen. And lastly, (less of a nitpick) the DEEP sadness of not being able to play this game for the first time ever again.

If roguelites and/or deckbuilders inherently disinterest you, but if you've got $20 and a burnout of your favorite games; give Inscryption a chance. It might just be your next favorite game. I know it's mine.\t\t\t\t\t
