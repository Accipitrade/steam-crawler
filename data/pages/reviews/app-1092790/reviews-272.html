\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm writing this review from the point of view of a Christian. Many of you may not understand why it's such a specifically directed review, but its because many like me may be on the fence about purchasing this game based on some of the choices in the theme (goats, sacrifices, etc).
I'm gonna be honest, I was having a really hard time purchasing this game regardless of my feelings of having wanted it for a long time. I'm a big Lucas Pope, Papers Please Return of the Obra Dinn fan, and I knew if I wanted to play something with a similar feel ever again, I'd need to do it with a Daniel Mullins game (seriously Lucas and Daniel must be long lost siblings or something).
I wasn't sure what to do, until I read an interview that asked him if this was actually directed with a sinister purpose, and he gave an honest response of not being a believer in God, and using biblical things as a sort of mythology for some of his creations, without any basis in real use of Christiological Abrahamic elements in his games.
To be honest, plenty of games are made by all sorts of people that may or may not believe and may or not be guided by an ulterior motive. If I stopped playing every game with something unbiblical, I think I'd have to just uninstall steam altogether.

The game itself is a masterpiece and Daniel is a master of his craft. It has all kinds of uses for sacrifices and blood goats and so on without ever really becoming a serious nod to anything. Little bit of a spoiler but if you cant stand a playing card ripped or being "sacrificed"... again, a card, not a person or anything, just cards with cute animals inscribed on them.

The sound design is AMMMMMMAAAZING! I sometimes wasnt sure if sounds were coming from within my room or via my headphones, really well done, but again, nothing sinister in the sounds, nothing referring to anything biblical, no real life demons, just a bunch of made up stuff.
The card game is so good, the story is pretty great, and something you REALLY need to experience going in blind. I felt like I was part of the thing that was happening (can't tell you what the thing is, you gotta see it for yourself), but it was so good. Can't recommend this game enough to any Christian with strong understanding and foundation in what is what regarding their faith.
If you are easily shifted and prone to being lead into turmoil by anything, then I would suggest to stay away until you can say, fantasy doesn't bother me.\t\t\t\t\t
