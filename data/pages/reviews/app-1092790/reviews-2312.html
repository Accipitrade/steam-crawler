\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have just "started a new game". If you have played the previous Daniel Mullins Games, then you might know that this might not mean what it means in other games. Needless to say, I have probably only just started "the real game" or the second chapter or whatever it might be called, however I felt the need to post an early review because of the quality of the "first chapter". I love good card games and deckbuilding games, if there is a sense of progression and it is not a grind fest -- and Inscryption has managed it exquisitely to make me play "just one more round" even if I fail. The meta game is strong and rewarding, but the motivational kicker so far for me was the ability to stumble upon or create strategies and card combinations that are really powerful and seemingly unbeatable, which in turn allow progression in the meta game to the point where the aforementioned "second chapter" or "new game" is so phenomenally different that I would not be surprised to find myself on Pony Island or in The Hex at some point.

The game has a wonderful sense of mystery and strangeness that encourages me to continue to explore all of its little nooks and crannies while also being a really fun and atmospheric card game battler. Either one would have been an excellent game, the combination is just gold.\t\t\t\t\t
