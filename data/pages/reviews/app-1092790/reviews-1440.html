\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played video games for most of my life, it's hard to find new kicks. I think my only unexplored frontiers at this point are: MMOs, grand strategy, and RTS. So going into Inscrypition, this game was my:

First ever Rogue-like, and
First Card Game/Deck Builder
And ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ do I LOVE these kinds of games.

Inscryption is full blown addicting. Not having any experience in the card genre made failure almost inevitable but is failure ever fun in this game. There is this creepy atmosphere where you aren't even sure what is going on our why you are forced to play this card game with a mad Game Master and every death gives you a bit more insight if you are perceptive.

--

A few people already posted they avoided Inscryption for X or Y. So despite what discourse is out here, let me tell you a bit about it. It's a card game through and through. A lot of the discussion around "go into Inscryption not knowing anything," really pertains to the story which, in my opinion, isn't all that fantastic. It's more like the journey and atmosphere that greatly outshines the story. The story is secondary imo.

You play in this cabin and there are light adventure game elements where you can explore the very small cabin to unlock new ways to gain edges in your card battles against a deranged GM. That's the gist. The card gameplay is immensely simple, easy to understand, quick to pick up, and addictive. I sound like a typical Magic the Gathering fan at this point, but boys does the artwork on these cards look good.

If you love animals, all of the cards are these kinda creepy sort of Folk Horror depictions of natural animals. They are drawn with a feroscity to them, like animals you would fear. After a few runs, you'll find yourself saying, "YES! I got a Praying Mantis" and I just love that about this game. It isn't some weird rune mage, it's literally a grizzly bear, a giant toad, or other woodland creatures to get you excited. Some specific cards are actual characters in the game and can speak to you. They also speak to you, the player, in context of playing the card game. It's strange but both cute, scary, and immersive.

The Game Master is really the star. The pace of this game is fast and you progress through the game board while the GM role plays as creepy villagers, evil bosses, and so on. The music complements the atmosphere so well. Sometimes, you forget you are playing a board game and really get spooked by his presentation.

Overall, Inscryption surprises me in many ways, but it mostly shines a light on a fantastic genre that I wasn't aware of. It's raw gameplay would be fine on it's own, but all of the added mystery and atmosphere makes the vibe of this game top notch. To be completely serious, I found the atmosphere in this game to be more satisfying on a mysterious horror level than Resident Evil Village. And that isn't an apples to apples comparison because Inscryption isn't outright scary like RE is. But this game kept me on my toes and engaged more than any other game in 2021 or recent years for that matter.

It is very engaging to say the least.\t\t\t\t\t
