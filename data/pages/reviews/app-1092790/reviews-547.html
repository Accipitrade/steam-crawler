\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthere has never in my life been a time when i was willing to buy a game twice, much less a deckbuilding game, a genre i didn't even like... before i found Inscryption.

that's probably the best review i could possibly leave wrapped up in that statement alone, lol. i'll give more details of course, though.

discovered this game through a youtuber, instantly fell in love with it despite my prior distaste for deckbuilding. my partner got a copy and i played it through the shared library, then quickly became dissatisfied that we couldn't both play at the same time. so i bought myself a copy. i've NEVER done that before, and there have been many similar experiences throughout my life. this was the only one i loved enough to go through with it.

this game is just so good. the gameplay is fun, the aesthetics are wonderful, the music is chef's-kiss superb. the worldbuilding and characters (won't say too much to avoid spoilers) are really engaging and interesting to learn about. and, for me, it's one of the few games i can endlessly watch someone else playing - i just enjoy the game that much.

now i'll talk some spoiler stuff. PLEASE don't read this if you haven't played it; do yourself a favor and go in as blind as you can. the big game-changer (literally) was such a complete blindside. the trippy meta stuff was incredibly interesting and fun, and added so much to the overall experience. act 1 is probably most folks' favorite, for good reason - personally though i think i genuinely loved every single stage of this game in equal measure. the cast of characters is so colorful and interesting - i absolutely adored Lonely Wizard and Goobert (who doesnt), and all of Leshy's friends really grew on me over time, especially the Angler. i'd have to say P03 is my favorite Scrybe, though Leshy and Grimora give it a run for its money. As for Magnificus... well, it was fun to have someone to dislike, and i will say his Yu-Gi-Oh style duel at the end was an incredibly funny and nostalgic surprise. there's also the ARG element for people who like digging into mysteries and such (although it's concluded already, but i know i still like to dive down rabbit holes even if they've been figured out).

kaycee's mod just provided a perfect topper to this whole experience, which by itself was exactly what everyone had been asking for - but it also provides the perfect modding opportunity, allowing for a ton of personalized gameplay options.

i don't know what else i can say, man. i genuinely love this game so much more than probably 95% of other games i've ever played.

if you are even sort-of considering getting this, DO IT. if you aren't sure if you're going to like a deckbuilding game, try this. if you want something that's gonna be a lot more than your average deckbuilding game, definitely try this. and if you just want a fun game you can keep coming back to (especially if you're not opposed to modding it later on for additional replayability), please try this.

seriously. can't recommend it enough.\t\t\t\t\t
