\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is insanely good.

Gameplay never stops evolving, even in its final moments. The entire game consists of learning new mechanics and stumbling across your own unique strategies. One of the reasons I love games like Deus Ex, Prey, Dishonored, etc. is due to the openness of solving problems. In other deck-building games, they tend to push you more towards a theme of deck (e.g. Poison deck in Slay the Spire, or Clan choices in Monster Train).


Inscryption offers that if you'd like, but you can overcome with completely mish-mashed solutions. Giving a wolf the trifurcated strike from a mantis god, and then drawing him specifically with the magpie? Or how about making a deck that runs off of every different summon requirement so you can multi-summon and pull off wacky combos? Creativity is encouraged, and it kind of reminds me of Cruelty Squad without being overly obtuse in its presentation.


There are some rough tonal shifts in the game which seem to be more of why there are negative reviews here. Walking into each shift, I felt about the same honestly. Within some time after each shift, I was in love with the current section. Think of these like Acts, and each one is very different than the previous.


If I have any one complaint about the game, it's that some puzzles (Not just room puzzles) stretch to being overly obtuse and requiring the use of Google to piece them together. There are some really big benefits for players that could be missed out on if not clued in, and important pieces of the story may be forever lost. I like to requirement to adventure outside of the lines to learn the game, but some of these feel too out of the way.

Great game, highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
