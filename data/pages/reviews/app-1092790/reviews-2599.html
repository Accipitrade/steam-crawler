\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are looking to buy an "artsy narrative game with cards", like I was, you're going to be very happy. I wouldn't say this game is a god-send or anything, especially as somebody who's been around the meta-horror block a few times, but it's still an interesting story, and, unlike a lot its kin, it has serious strategic gameplay to boot. The last 20 minutes in particular were truly unique and memorable. Excellent use of $20 and 13 hours for me. Your mileage on length may vary; I get the sense I was pretty "average".

If you are looking to buy a "card game with an artsy narrative", I cannot guarantee your happiness.

Mechanical spoilers follow, but if the last line made you nervous, it's probably worth it before spending money: What you see on the store page is merely "Act 1". Act 2 transitions from a rogue-like deckbuilder, to a 16-bit collectible card RPG that brought me back to my GBA Yu-Gi-Oh days. That may not sound like a big change, but it really is. There's a huge difference between changing a deck as you go, and having to actually plan one out. Act 3 is much more like Act 1, so you'll have that to look forward to.

There are a few, shall we say... "vocal critics" in the forums who didn't take kindly to what I described. In all fairness I also prefer act 1, and would like to see an endless version or something. It's okay to not like the direction this game took, and even to want a refund, but please understand the difference between "what you were promised" and "what you expected" before you call "false advertising".

Now then, I have to go get in touch with the Alfred Hitchcock estate for a refund on Psycho. I thought Janet Leigh was gonna be in that whole movie!\t\t\t\t\t
