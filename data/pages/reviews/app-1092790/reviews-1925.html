\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could give a maybe.

The game started out really strong. Super interesting and a unique blend of different game types. There's puzzles in the cabin, the deck building and roguelike part is interesting, the story is unique. It's great. Then the game opens up even more in later acts.

However, 9.5 hours and I've beaten the game. No, I didn't do all the ARG puzzle stuff. I signed up for a unique card playing game, and that's what I enjoyed.

The third act is when the game really opens up. There's four different decks to select your cards from, so there's a ton of experimenting you can do. However, most of the fights you do aren't even that difficult. I spent a minute or two here and there selecting cards I thought would go right, and I'm either an absolute deck building master, or got completed lucky in what I picked, because I only died a handful of times. Most of the fights were relatively easy. Then, the game is over.

The reason I say maybe is because the game is a bit short and I feel a bit let down by the end of the third act. I wanted to experiment more, be forced to change my deck up, slowly discover more cards and be tested a little more when it came to the battles. Instead, I felt like I got all the cards relatively quickly, picked the ones that seemed to work well the best (primarily low energy cards + 1-2 low bone count cards + a few low blood count cards (ignore the mage deck)), and the rest are generally a breeze. Didn't really feel like I got to experience the game enough before it was over.\t\t\t\t\t
