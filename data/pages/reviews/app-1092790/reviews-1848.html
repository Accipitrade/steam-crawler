Throughout the entire game you're given new information, and just when you thought you were getting good at the game's mechanics (I mean, it's just a card game, right?) you find out something else.

An amazing game, truly something the developers should be extremely proud of creating. Now get off my review, scroll up, add to cart, and purchase the game already, no one else is telling you otherwise.\t\t\t\t\t

I am pretty livid right now. I spent a few hours absolutely loving the game. The reviews were overwhelmingly positive and so I thought I was making a good purchase. It was slay the spire but with horror elements and escape room and that's what I was expecting I had bought. I truly did have a ton of fun with it and was on the edge of my seat the whole time.

The problem: Out of nowhere, the game suddenly does a HUGE tonal and aesthetic shift about 3-4 hours in (which is conveniently time for after the refund window that Steam provides. Coincidence?). You're not in a cabin anymore - you're playing an RPG game with cutesy pixel-art graphics.

I wanted creepy cabin psychological horror card game!!! There was no warning that the game was doing to do this and all of the advertisement and storefront information suggested to me that I was buying a very different game.

I understand that developers have a right to artistic liberty, and honestly I don't know that there is anything that could have been done to advertise this game in a way that prevents players from being blindsided.

I guess what I'm trying to get at is this: if you're buying this game specifically for the creepy, psychological horror StS sorta thing - you're only going to be getting that for 3-4 hours. \t\t\t\t\t
