\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a deckbuilding roguelike with some escape room puzzles blended thereto. It comprises three unique acts, which look quite distinct from each other but play similarly. Without getting to any spoilers, it also features some of the best FMV in video games.

The first act, by far, is the best one. You immediately find yourself in this sinister environment. A befitting music and creepy characters accompany it leaves a very strong first impression. I don’t think the game keeps the momentum throughout all acts, but it does offer well written dialogues and a very interesting story which keep you going.

I have found the FMVs the strongest aspect of the game. They just fit so perfectly to the mood the game is trying to create. And the weakest aspect? Unfortunately, it’s the card game (the core mechanic) itself. It is serviceable, but I can easily think of plenty of deckbuilding games with much better designed card game mechanics.

But hey, you should know that you won’t be playing Inscryption to play a good deckbuilding game. You are here for the experience and I can assure you it will be a memorable ride that will stay with you for a long time.

8/10
