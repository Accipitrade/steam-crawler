\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSIGNIFICANT SPOILERS AHEAD

I really, REALLY wanted to give this game a positive review. The first 4 hours were some of the most fun I have EVER had with a deck building game. The mechanics were interesting, the presentation was phenomenal, the atmosphere and story were gripping. Honestly, if the entire game was just the first act but a bit longer, I'd give it a perfect score. And that's what the game falsely advertises itself as.

But that's only act 1. Inscryption is actually 3 separate games, and in my honest personal opinion, only 1 of them is any good.
As soon as you finally beat the 'final boss', your reward is that you are now forced into a completely different game that removes everything that made the first act interesting and mechanically engaging, and replaces it with a generic faux-retro top-down affair. The roguelite game structure is thrown out the window entirely, and the game introduces THREE entirely new gameplay styles, all of which are more frustrating and mandatory to succeed, and none of which are nearly as engaging as the one from act 1.

I was sold horror-flavored roguelike slay the spire, and right when it gets good, they pull the rug out and serve you 8-bit narrative-driven hearthstone. Oh, you didn't want that? That isn't what was advertised or what you paid for? Well ♥♥♥♥ you, this is Inscryption.

And if you're willing to trudge through the entirety of act 2, the game has the audacity to reward you with a tease at a return to form, reverting the presentation to the higher quality of the first act before replacing the game's mechanics yet again with one of the new options presented in act 2. It was at this point that I gave up entirely.

I'm genuinely disappointed that a game with such a strong start fell so hard, I loved my time with the first third of the game, but the absolute slog the rest of it becomes left an awful taste in my mouth.

As it stands, Inscryption is a 4-6 hour long 10/10 experience that I only wish lasted longer and went untainted by the 12 hours of pure frustration that follows. Get it on sale so that you still get your money's worth when it stops being good and you stop playing.

Update:
They've added a supposed 'endless mode' that lets you play the game that you were actually told you were buying, but it's locked behind wasting your time and energy completing everything I described above. Pass.\t\t\t\t\t
