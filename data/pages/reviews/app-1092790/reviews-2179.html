\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis here is a beautiful gem of a game that mixes card, little element of roguelike, and puzzle, don't look up guide because the experience is unique and more fun if you figure it out yourself.

+PRO
-Unique game , a lot of suprising moment.
-Gameplay and story is engaging, never a dull moment
-Some very cool boss with interesting mechanic, my fav is Golly
-OP cards when you are lucky can be very fun
+CONS
-The card game itself is not very deep, and once you know the rule, it is pretty easy to craft a winning deck (abusing the high attack, low cost card to win fast)
-Can be a bit repetitive when you get to act 3, though it is alleviate by some new mechanic
-Grimoira and Magnificus doesn't get enough love : ( . I want to play with them too, and give magni a nice firm handshake he deserves
-Sadly have little replay value although technically a roguelike game, but is story heavy\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game a lot. The card playing mechanics are not perfectly balanced for the scaling that happens. But I don't think it's intended to be. There are roguelike mechanics where you're meant to die to progress the story and increase your power in the form of making new cards. There's much more to the game than that. But I can't say much. Think Doki-Doki or Undertale.

Spoilers ahead as that's all I can cover without them. I'm going to be vague as possible but you've been warned.

As you progress through the acts the game changes entirely. This is less so one game than it is 3 games spliced into one with an overarching puzzle solving aspect with lots of bonuses and easter eggs.
Act 1 is absolutely incredible and had me hooked, and begging for more.
Act 2 fell flat for me and I didn't really care for it much. The balancing was way off and a lot of it felt tedious. But there was still some shiny moments that made it worth it.
Act 3 started off slow but as it ramped up I LOVED it and it had some of the coolest boss mechanics of any game I've played.
Act 4 was a bit of a rushed mess. But it's sort of intended to be? It introduced some REALLY cool animations and mechanics that I'd have loved to explore. But sadly you don't really get to use them except in the loosest sense of the term. I think this is a missed opportunity but it could also be argued that it's an artistic decision in the game's design.

In conclusion:
I took my time and hard a hard time with a few bosses so my playthrough took about 13-14 hours. And I had a lot of moments that made me go "Whoa!!!" so I'd say it's well worth $20 and anyone who is a fan of card games or puzzle games should play it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; Inscryption is very much in line with other creations from Daniel Mullin Games.

At the heart - it is a simple, but well crafted and engaging deckbuilding game. Though, it is the story behind the scenes which will keep you intrigued. (...as always with games from this great developer)

While this is the first game from Daniel Mullin Games where I've ever had to take a few breaks out of frustration - (only due to the RNG nature of deck building games) - I still had a blast playing Inscryption and wholeheartedly recommend it for anyone who enjoyed "Pony Island" and "The Hex" (and is not allergic to deck building games).

@Dev: Thank you, Mr Mullins - for many hours worth of great entertainment. Already looking forward to what you might come up with next.

Edit(20211217): Now with an "Endless" mode coming up (available in Beta at this time) the game just took a great leap in value.\t\t\t\t\t
