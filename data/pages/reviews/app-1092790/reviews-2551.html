\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo spoiler section:
Truly an amazing game. Definitely worth every penny, really amazing quality for a game of this price range. Really outshine games in the price range and some above. I thought I was buying a game like "Slay the spire" in which I have 300+h but it really aint it. Its less rouge like with way more story telling involved. Would 100% reccommend if you like Card and/or story games but PLS GO IN BLIND; the experience is much more enjoyable blind bc you dont know what the hell will go on (not gameplay wise, the gamelplay is easy to understand).

Semi Spoiler: Wished there was an gameplay part for the other 2 main characters and not just brief sections. Would 100% buy a DLC to be able to have the gameplay for this types. Doesnt need to have even a part outside of just the card game still would buy it.

Spoiler section:
Tl,Dr: hoped for roguelike, didnt get it but storytelling compensates. Act 2 felt weak but it is still pretty good. After you beat the game you can go to each Act at will so I can now enjoy as I hoped.

As I said I was expecting an Slay the spire so keep that in mind. The game is divided in 3 acts, each is a card game but varies in style by a mile frome each other. I initially would have liked that the game was just act 1 because again I was blindly expecting a rogue like type game, however the lore/storytelling quickly overcame this mid/late Act 2 to the end of Act 3.
Act 1: this is what you see on trailers, beta and probably most videos. When you get the "true ending" and beat the guy with the camera you go to act 2.

Act 2: is a pixel type game like the first pokemons (visually), turns out the "original" inscryption is this game, there are 4 card types and 4 leaders. The old man is a leader and the cards you have been using are from his type. there are beast (old man), dead (necromancer), tech/robot (robot lol) and magic (wizard dude) cards. The gameplay here opens up a lot in the card aspect but gets kinda slower since you are more exposed to the storytelling and there is more dialogue than the gameplay/card game here. here you progresively get cards from all types and you can actually mix card types.

Act 3: you return to the non pixel gameplay like in Act 1 but instead of the old man you are with the robot, hence here you only get to use robot cards, all is tech themed and get to move around the house but now its the factory from Act 2. Also in act 1 you move up random paths in the board, here you have a non-changing map and losing gets you back to a checkpoint and respawns enemies (there are enemy rooms) but doesnt send you to square 1.

"Act 4" is basically the ending and there is not much to do, you experience a 3d (non pixel) version of the other types of card games that look amazing but just for a brief moment since the ending is (Spoiler) basically the game is deleting to save people from the game bc reasons (will not try to explain what the hell happened). Now you gain free access to all Acts (even ending) and basically can repeat the parts of the game you enjoyed the most and go for those achievments you missed. (no need to replay all Act 1 and 2 for an Act 3 achievment)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game, or at least the start of it. (I'm gonna keep it brief so I don't spoil it. IF it isn't clear you should get this game)

The pros:
It has a great start and the rules that are taught to you are natural since it has the cover of a being a board game

The dark gritty art style is amazing, it adds so much character and emotions to the game

The gameplay is incredible, completely unique and each damage you deal or take feels impactful.

The music fits the theme nicely and adds suspense against bosses or just fits the mood when you are playing with the puzzles

SPOILERS:

Okay I really just want to move on with my problems with the game. I REALLY liked playing with Leshy. and when that was taken from me, all I could think about was getting back to playing with him again; I just powered through act 2 and 3. Act 3 just didn't feel as fluid as the first (was much more drawn out and easier). There were no stakes after Leshy: If you die, whatever -- just come back and fight again (opposed to starting over and remaking your deck). The Robot should've been the starting Host. Leshy was just too fun that it ruined what came after him.

As for Act 2, erm, I don't know how I feel about it. Just very weird, almost tedious at parts. It kind of bogged down the game and felt like filler.

There's another gripe I have with Inscryption, why the 4th wall breaks? It felt unnatural as though it shouldn't even be apart of the game. It didn't add anything to the game and instead hurt it. It made for a bad story and when I finally realized that it was an integral part of the story, it just made me sad. I think they should've went for a more simplistic route instead of some convoluted plot. Did not like them dumping several uninteresting videos on my lap to watch, made for bad story telling.

The ending just left a sour taste in my mouth. Made me realize what the game could've been if it hadn't focused on all these computer shenanigans. We could've had more intricate gameplay and other unique board games to play. And after the end, I didn't feel like playing again. You have to restart all the puzzles in Leshy.

I'm just a little sad about what the game could've been. There could've been dynamics between the Scribes and better story, or just a better overall experience. I am still satisfied with the game I got, it just left me hoping for more.
