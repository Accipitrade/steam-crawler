\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA simple card game that's easy to understand and play, that is what the core experience of Inscryption is.

The first part of the game is an engaging, partitioned version of the core card game that expands on one of four mechanics, deck types if you will. It also functions as what is essentially an extended tutorial on how the core game functions before any complexities are introduced. Its very in depth and you can play just this portion on repeat ad neusiam as its so in depth. If you complete the game in fact you're rewarded with just that.

The second part of the game is the crystalized version of the core game, you can build a deck around one of four mechanics and travel around a small map testing your deck against other decks which usually focus on a couple of cards, mechanics or a specific deck type. This second chapter really unlocks the game and shows you what it can do and what ideas the creator had in mind.

the third chapter is much like the first chapter, the game focuses on one particular deck type but peppers in mechanics from the other deck types.

the game ends at this point, and the worst part? It gives you a taste, a proof of concept, just a fragment of what could have been the fourth and the fifth chapters exploring the other two deck types you hadn't yet experienced, complete with different perspectives on traversing the world and finding other decks to battle.

Finally the game tries to weave in an ARG meta narrative as apart of the main narrative and it really doesn't work at all, its boring and takes you completely out of the game, it threads loosely into the main narrative and ignoring it completely lets you enjoy the main narrative focus which is much more interesting.

In conclusion, the game is fun, its definitely worth the money, there's no obvious bugs or glitches.
But go in knowing that you're purchasing a game that has a very clear point in the narrative where the games creator has gone "I don't to work on this any more" and quickly tied up the plot to ship it.

You are purchasing a complete game with an incomplete narrative. Be warned.\t\t\t\t\t
