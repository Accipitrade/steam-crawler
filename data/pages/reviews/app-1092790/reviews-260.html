I cannot believe I am saying this about a deck building rouge-lite game (my favorite video game genre, bar none), yet alone one developed by Daniel Mullins, an excellent game developer but this... THIS... was almost certainly the most disappointing video game experience I have ever had.


Mind the wording, DISAPPOINTING, not bad, or awful, or terrible. I can absolutely see how someone might get a kick out of this game. As much as I hate to be a broken record, as this has been said time and time again about this game, and I am most certainly very late to the party, but it is all about Act II and Act III, which are not, and fairly so, advertised at all for this game.


Everything after Act I is an utterly unenjoyable slog. I cannot believe the ennui I experienced after Act I. For the record, I could not force myself to finish the game after halfway through Act III. It sat installed on my computer for a month before I finally uninstalled it knowing I would never go back.


I completely understand why Act II and Act III are completely hidden away and not advertised. Its for the story and """twist""" of the game. Mullins is known for this, it does not surprise me. I do not blame Mullins at all and it does not upset me in the least. However, Act II and Act III combined are a much larger portion of the game than Act I and I purchased Act I. I would never have purchased the game that is composed of Act II and Act III.


And no, I do not find Act III to be "exactly the same" as Act I. However, I admit my issues with Act III stem more from a certain robot than the gameplay but only barely. I've been lucky and probably pretty permissive when it has come to video game experiences over the years so I can say with confidence that I have never experienced a greater and sharper letdown than this game. Shows me for having such high expectations, but Act II genuinely took my breath away with how far and fast my enjoyment fell off a sheer cliff. Act III was more of the same.


The genuinely incredible atmosphere of Act I is completely gone by Act II as well as the thoroughly enjoyable antagonist. Replaced by far less interesting adversaries. The least interesting of which is made the focus of Act III.

The art style of Act I is some of the best I have seen, while Act II's is a letdown to say the least, but I suppose its fine if you enjoy pixel art, but I feel Pony Island did a much better job with the medium. Act III's art is just off putting but not horrible or anything.

What Act II adds to the gameplay is shallow and frustrating at best while Act III seems to (purposefully for story reasons) mock and spit on the excellent gameplay of the first Act. Not to mention (especially in Act II), the game throws so many new mechanics at you so fast and it does not feel like you are given any time to get used to any of them before another is thrown at you or the Act ends.


I've had many a personal friend of mine give me grief over how critical I am of Act III (and the fact I've had no one personally challenge me on my disappointment in Act II compared to Act I has not escaped my notice). I admit I don't have a complete and coherent answer as to why Act III bothers me so much. I guess it was just the gutpunch of the sheer cliff fall from Act I to Act II, then taking the worst story bits and characters from Act II and making them the focus of Act III while spitting on the mechanics and theming that made Act I so great (again, on purpose, for story reasons). I was genuinely tired by the time I got to Act III. Genuine ennui. Like the joy got sucked out of me. I'm not trying to say that Act III is the "WuRst g4Me 3VeRRR!" But it did happen to be a part of my most disappointing game experience ever.


It is frustrating because it feels like someone took all the parts to what should be one of the best game experiences I could have and threw them in a woodchipper and Elmer's glued the pieces back together in an effort to make some sort of "modern art" piece that doesn't look like anything and is just a pile of trash. This story does not justify what Mullins did with the quality of this three act structured game.


I do, however, want to clarify Act II and Act III are nowhere near the worst games I have ever played. Act II and III are below average gameplay quality at worst. My feelings about this game have just as much to do with the quality the later two acts compared to the first and how the game flows and is composed. It is disappointment and ennui slightly more than it is "2/3 of game bad."


Kaycee's Mod doesn't make up for it, either. It's just not worth the money. Act I done better with the endless mode and new content is still not worth the price of admission.


I was expecting more, and, more importantly, much different. No developer is "obligated" to meet a customer's expectations, I suppose. Certainly not a game like this. However, if you are pulled in by what you've seen in video thumbnails and official advertisements, know you are in for a completely different game after 1/3 in, and I happen to not enjoy that 2nd and 3rd third whatsoever.


Also it is overwhelmingly unlikely for someone to come across Act II and the severe whiplash of a game changer that it is before the two hour mark. I don't think that was by design or anything, it is just rather frustrating so buyer beware.\t\t\t\t\t
