\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo understand my views on Inscryption and why I think it's special, we need to go back to 2021. While 2020 had been a banner year for releases, the pandemic was really starting to affect the release schedule in 2021 and beyond. Game after game had been delayed or outright cancelled, including some projects I was really looking forward to. By the time October rolled around there were only a few major releases left, and many of them were almost guaranteed flops. But I held out hope that this awful year could have at least one last hurrah in terms of video game releases.

And then in November a friend clued me onto Inscryption.
And a single game managed to outdo every other release in the past year.

Inscryption is a roguelike deckbuilder in genre only. I generally cannot stand deckbuilders, but the mechanics and gameplay that Inscryption brings to the table pulled me in and refused to let go. The sacrifice system is easy to understand, yet allows for highly strategic gameplay and difficult choices as you choose which cards to play and which ones are better to help bring in a new card. The scales are a much more interesting score method than a straight up "this many points to win" counter, as they make you consider incoming damage along with the damage you deal out. And that's not even mentioning the new mechanics introduced in acts 2 and 3.

As for the story, well that's a mixed bag (minor spoilers from here on out by the way). I found myself gripped by act 1, but act 2 was very hard to get through as the story simply grinds to a halt while you fight with annoying new mechanics and a much simpler artstyle.. Act 3 was much better, but still didn't reach the peaks of act 1. The finale saved it for me though, pulling me back in with an ending that left me speechless.

Overall, Inscryption is a 10/10 in my book. Very few games can come out in the last few months of a year and still qualify for a personal GOTY, but for Inscryption that honor is well deserved.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI picked this up because I'm a fan of the developer's work - I really liked Pony Island, so decided to take a chance of Inscryption; while I'm not normally a fan of card-battle deck-building games, this one's approachable enough that I was able to get through without too much a struggle.

It's got an interesting story going on, and some neat mechanics that were fun to play around with. I also like that it's relatively short; it doesn't overstay its welcome and you can burn through it in a week or so. If you DO want a longer experience, Kaycee's Mod adds a ton of replay time. (NOTE: Kaycee's Mod is not an external mod you have to download and install from a third party).

All in all, happy that I bought it and played through it. Looking forward to what's next from Daniel Mullins.

TIPS:
- If you're struggling, explore the cabin more. There's a lot of little puzzles and tricks there that can power you up for all future runs. Hint: extinguish the candles next to the globe.
- The 'death cards' can be absurdly powerful if you plan ahead. Making a low-cost, high-stats card with a useful symbol can carry you.
- Don't be afraid to burn cards out of your deck. This makes the rest of your deck more predictable and increases your odds of drawing something good.\t\t\t\t\t
