\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo spoilers but I must say this game comes in multiple parts, and that the change from the first to the second is like playing a near completely unrelated game, aside from some of the base mechanics. If you enjoy the well crafted atmosphere, graphics, gameplay and tension of the first part, then the second part gives such massive whiplash that if you're like me it will make you put it down for good. The gameplay becomes much more convoluted, adding many systems that don't work together and the presentation takes a nosedive through the earth's crust.

It's not that the second part is an affront against game design or anything, but it's like if you're halfway through playing Mario: Oddyssey and it suddenly turns into a 2D flash-game remake you played as a kid in the school computer class. Apparently it gets good again later but I'm not sifting through that. That said, if 17 is your entry price to 4-6 hours of a novel gaming experience and/or you have a high tolerance for getting through a game's 'slog' I think I'd recommend it, at least on sale. I stayed up til past midnight playing after buying it in the evening because I was hooked. After playing part two for 15 minutes I don't think I'll play it again.\t\t\t\t\t
