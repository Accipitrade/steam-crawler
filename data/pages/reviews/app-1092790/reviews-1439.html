\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a wonderful exercise in blending narrative and mechanical genre that is ultimately a very uneven experience beyond the first 8 hours. I would love to recommend this game in its entirety, however I can only recommend what people are calling "Act 1," and then I strongly recommend that you allow yourself the freedom to quit playing having completed that section. The game at that point has shown its hand, and never reaches the highs, either in narrative or mechanics, achieved in Act 1. If you do continue, unless you truly love the new card game mechanics, I advise reading the online guides around empowering a specific card in Act 2 that returns in Act 3, and then acquiring that card as early as possible in Act 3.

As an aside, I found the found footage component of the game particularly lacking in execution. The goofy, amateurish execution cut the legs out from under any serious emotional investment I may have had in the spookiness of the story. If the point was to be silly, then it contrasts with the authentic chills driven by the Leshy/escape-room horror components of the first act. All-in-all, I was left confused and disappointed by the inconsistent tone. I'll be excited to see what comes next, the bones of this game mechanically were quite brilliant.\t\t\t\t\t
