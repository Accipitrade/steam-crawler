\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is immensely creative in its mechanics and atmosphere. I was quickly sucked into the mystery of the game, and have a hard time pulling myself away! Both people new to the genre, and those familiar with deck building/card battler games, who are fans of horror will find something to love here!

Edit: Having played more, I am still loving the game, though I feel it's worth updating my review to note some things. With out any major spoilers, just know that the aesthetic (and some gameplay elements) change drastically in the 2nd and 3rd acts of the game—completely different from anything that is shown on the store page screen shots and trailer. Act 1 is still my favorite of the 3 (the initial style and gameplay loop as shown on the store page) and I look forward to playing Kaycee's Mod (currently in beta) since that is meant to be an "infinite mode" of that Act.

Just wanted to add that the tone and style shift is a little jarring, and while not unwelcome, comes off as somewhat disingenuous when compared to how the game is represented on the store page (the first trailer only briefly shows/implies the ARG elements and 4th wall breaks that come up periodically). Fans of Daniel Mullins's previous works may be familiar with these elements being present in his games, and may expect them, but it would be nice if it was shown a little more to be advertised thoroughly in case that might be a turn off for some.

BUT, still, all that being said, I absolutely love this game! I am more than happy with my purchase of both the game and the soundtrack (will definitely be using some tracks as ambience for D&D!), and I am not far off from 100% achievements! Like I mentioned before, I hope the game continues to see updates for Kaycee's Mod as it progresses through beta, because I could (and do!) play this game for hours!\t\t\t\t\t
