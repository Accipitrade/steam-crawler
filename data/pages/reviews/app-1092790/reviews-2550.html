\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I don't regret playing the game, and it's a very good game for what it's trying to be, I feel like I need to write this as a warning to others like me.

If you're okay with card games, but not -really- into them, you might want to pass on this one. Yes, the section the demo is from is fantastic, it shows off the best part of the game... but that's the problem. It -is- the best part of the game. Much like Mullins' other work, there's more to the game than meets the eye, and unfortunately that more is not actually better.

There are points in the game where the card game portion becomes a lot more involved and unforgiving than the starting section, and if, like me, you played the demo and went "oh, I can handle this, lay it on me!", well, you might be wrong. I blew through that first part, loved every single bit of it, then spent the next 13 hours slogging through the rest, having fun maybe 30 minutes of it all.

I came for the gameplay in the demo and the story. The latter delivered. The former ended up being a fraction of the full experience. If I could have paid 5 bucks less for just that first section and the story it would have been my GOTY, as is, I don't think I'll ever replay it.\t\t\t\t\t
