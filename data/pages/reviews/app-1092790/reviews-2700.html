Though I have not completed this game I feel my time with it so far has been more than enough to give a resounding recommendation. I cannot stress enough the level of artistry, intrigue, and genuine passion that permeates throughout this entire game. It's rare these days that I find games that genuinely fill me with a desire and need to continue playing. I want to give some pros and cons of this game on day one to maybe express to those who may not know about much about this game some of what to expect.

Pros
- Deeply thought out and designed world. There is without a doubt a true artist or team of artists at the helm of this game
- There is an ocean both deep and wide for the player to explore and unveil
- Gameplay is easy to understand while allowing for players to get creative with their solutions
- There is more game here at 18 dollars than what most single player experiences offer for Triple A price

Cons
- It can be jarring and confusing at times
- If you get frustrated with trial and error or roguelite style games it will be difficult

I implore everyone and anyone to play this game. More so I ask you to try and beat it. There is a story here that needs to be told and you need to experience it.\t\t\t\t\t

Feel free to check out the gameplay here:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=C1Na6PQeAFY

Advantages:
1. If you like card games like GWENT or Black Book, you'll LOVE this game. The mechanics are NOT the same but it's just as interesting to learn and master. If you (like me) enjoy finding ways to create op decks and strats, you'll love this!!
2. I love the secrets everywhere and the thematic of sacrifices. So you'll have to think not only about card matches but also about other puzzles that sometimes require out of the box thinking. But if it happens that you get stuck, the game has very well thought and immersive hints that will help you get back on track! Amazing game design!
3. Well, the ambiance is soo good! This is just pure joy to play and to be a part of that universe. You even get to create your own death cards when you die. It's just so good!
4. It doesn't get boring. It has different stages with their own style and puzzles so don't worry that it's gonna get repetitive. It's not gonna.

Disadvantages:
1. Some people encounter game breaking bugs but it seems to be rare and I personally haven't faced this problem.
2. Some people may find the sections of the game past the first less enjoyable. It's not my personal case but I wanted this review to be objective and honest so that's the feedback from some other players.

Overall:
I'm having a blast! I'd rate this game 9.3 sacrifices / 10. If my opinion changes, I'll update the review. It take about 8 - 10 hours to beat the game so imo it's worth the money and it's so much fun! If this review was useful, please drop a like so it could be useful to other people as well)

Thanks for reading till the end and have fun playing the game (if you decide to buy it) <3\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing the demo, I couldn't wait for the full game today. This may be the first time I feel like I'm really playing against a person when I'm not. Inscryption combines the fun sense of discovery and collecting on a path to duels using what you have accumulated. Very reminiscent of Hand of Fate, what's funny is I enjoy this more, where Hand of Fate added actual graphic 3D character play components. I haven't quite untangled some things yet, like uncertainty of my progression, when I beat a boss, and die, then I'm back to an earlier boss, am I starting over? It's confusing because it takes you back to creating a special card of your own, which I think should be a one time or progressive thing (as in ANOTHER card of your own). It would be great if they also allowed you to port in your own image for your own special card (which you name). There's a sense of accomplishment when you outwit your opponent. Recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
