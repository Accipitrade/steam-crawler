\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat conceptually, but it's pretty evident the devs have never actually designed a card game before. Pretty intriguing for their first attempts, especially during the 2nd portion of the game, but the balance is nonexistent and the game will often just randomly decide that you lose (or pull out card arrangements that are downright unfair or impossible to beat unless you have VERY specific cards/decks). I understand why there's no option to refuse taking a card when presented with the options, even when refusing a card would actually HELP your deck, but it's frustrating nonetheless even when you do find a decent or powerful card/combination, because you're never going to draw it after clogging your deck with trash.

This remains increasingly true in 'Kaycee's Mod.' It's nothing but a dice roll, with minimal options to actually influence the gameplay beyond crossing your fingers and hoping you get extraordinarily lucky. The "balance" towards losing extremely frequently during the 1st portion of the game is getting (supposedly) stronger cards for it (though this was rarely, if ever, the case), while losing during the 2nd and 3rd portions of the game just allows you to challenge the fights again, while being able to collect stronger cards. Kaycee's Mod is just a 'get lucky or lose no matter what' flip of the coin.\t\t\t\t\t
