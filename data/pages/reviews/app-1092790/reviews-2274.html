\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is an interesting game having gone into it blind. All I knew was it was a card game with rougelike elements. I had fun my first 2 runs, and killed the final boss on my third. Only to find out because I didnt explore the room enough, I had to do a fourth run to actually unlock the full reward. The reward? Act 2 and extreme whiplash as the feel and mechanics of the game completely shifted. I won't lie, I nearly quit about 30 minutes into act 2, as it was completely against the groove I had been feeling in act 1. When I eventually did finish act 2 which took a very, *very* long time compared to act 1, act 3 was a return to feelings from act 1, but in worse ways. All the time I was playing I kept thinking to myself the good times were coming, that the fun mechanics and visuals from part 1 would come back, but they never did. It felt like I got rug pulled by the first act, to be perfectly honest. It could be my fault for putting expectations on the game that it never promised, but it just felt like a really fun card game that was under-developed because it was just a vehicle for the story they told, and they didn't want to spend any more on the vehicle than they had to.

If you're someone into story games, the story of this game is decent. But if you fall in love with the first act's gameplay, be warned. Act 2 feels like an absolute slog if you enjoyed yourself in act 1. Even act 3 still felt more unpleasant and grindy than act 1 did. If the whole game played as act 1, I would have highly recommended the game, but I can't recommend it as a game based on the other acts.\t\t\t\t\t
