\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been mulling over my feelings for this game for a while after beating it. I think that the reason that people seem to generally enjoy this game is because they are both invested in the story and the card game itself. Like other negative reviews have said, the game really falls off after the first act, but the reasons for that are kinda complex for me so I'd like to delve into that. Spoilers ahead, obviously.

In the first act, the feeling of wanting to best Leshy provided me as the player motivation to keep going. After killing me over and over again, it really made me want to beat him and escape his cabin. That same conflict does not exist in Act 2, simple as that. Although it offers a more complex card system, I actually preferred the simplified combat of the first act. Without conflict, and without a fun card system, the only thing left to push the player forward is the story, and while I generally enjoyed Luke's videos, they were spread so far between the gameplay that it started to wear me down.

Act 3 doesn't really improve this experience either, and though I would consider it better than the second act, the lack of creative map design and atmosphere by PO3 really dragged this act down as well. It doesn't help that the map that he ends up using is basically just the same map that Act 2 used, which is a far cry away from the rogue-like randomly generated map design that was used in the first act. This would all be well and good if PO3 wasn't so dry as a character. I wanted to beat Leshy because he offered me conflict. Every time I lost to him I ended up dead and being turned into a card, but every time I lose to PO3, I just get sent back to a checkpoint and get to challenge the same fight over and over again. I don't know if the difficulty got lowered over time, or if my skills just got better and better, but being sent back to a checkpoint is a clear example of lowering the difficulty. Beating PO3 doesn't feel rewarding or satisfying, and I wish that the time spent with PO3 was split up with the other two scribes, because I did enjoy interacting with them at the end of the game. and I especially enjoyed that last interaction with Leshy. It proved to me that the game was still fun in its more simplified form not because of my own biases towards the first act, but because what made the game fun was interacting with the charismatic characters who could actually stimulate my adventurous spirit.

As for the end of the story, this might surprise some of you but I didn't really enjoy that either. I think that the game itself doesn't give enough context for a regular player, and when your game is putting all of its eggs into the story that's not something you wanna hear. I understand that the whole point is that half the story is provided during gameplay and the other half during and ARG, but even looking into it further I feel disconnected because none of the things described happened to me personally. Maybe it would've been cool if I had discovered all of this stuff myself, but that's not something I'm really interested in. As for what we were given in the game itself, it felt very unfulfilling. I think the biggest cop-out was redacting all of the information the old_data gave Luke at the end. I don't want to hear Luke freak out about it, I wanna freak out about it myself! And then having him die without knowing the whole picture just feels anti-climactic to me.

All in all. I think a lot more could have been explored with the story, and I feel like a lot more could've been done with the fantastic groundwork that Act 1 built. I don't even think that this is a bad game, I just think that it has so much wasted potential. If you had a genuinely good experience with this game I'm glad that you did, but amongst all of the overwhelmingly positive reviews on steam, I just wanted to say my piece as well.\t\t\t\t\t

Easily one of my most enjoyed games of last year.

I'm personally not a fan of the genre but I feel like Inscryption would have instantly found itself to be one of the greatest's of its like, it's just that fun.
