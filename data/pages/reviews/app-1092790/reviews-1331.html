\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt feels like it was written in stone that this game was going to be good given that this was made by the same people who made pony island and like pony island, the game feels like an epic pastische of the same game. Whereas pony island seemed thematically about the metaphorical stages of developing a game and how this vision adapts and changes, inscyption feels thematically about the way different people approach the same game. TLDR its very good and worth the money. Below i'll put more thoughts on it for those that care.

Compared to Pony island (since the games seem to be related in the same way that supergiant's Bastion and transistor are), the game sags a bit past the first stage (the first stage being basically everything you see in the trailer). I think this is because the mechanics introduced in later stages don't sufficiently build upon the game for the time you put in. It incorporates some of the elements from the 1rst stage into the 2nd and third, but the parts that the game has to leave behind are a bit of a problem. The reason why i bring this up is because this is somthing pony island actually did pretty well. Everything established in the first bit of the game comes back to make the finale really epic.

in terms of the gameplay, there are aspects that i find really amazing but also somewhat broken in other areas. The way life works in the game is genius allowing you to use the damage you take as a resource to bide time. At the same time, i also got a totem combination that made my base unit ridiculously overpowered allowing me to steamroll the game in one spot.

Its a little sad that the devs kind of dropped the ball past the first bit of the game which prevents the experience from being rounded off but what is here is still worth the money and still worth supporting the devs over.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoing to be one of the relatively few dissenting opinions here, but I figure someone needs to balance the scales at least. (Also long)
Most of my review I'm going to have in spoiler tags, and even then I'll avoid going too deep into it.
However, I do have to give one tiny spoiler because it's the most important part that I think players will either love or hate.
This game is entirely dependent on a meta narrative
If you really like meta narratives, this game could easily be for you. If, like me, you don't really like them or find them on the silly side, I would recommend immediately dropping this game to find another.

If you're not a huge fan of them, but are still thinking of the game, here's my full review:
This game is split into three 'acts' which other reviews might have keyed you off on. The first act is actually a pretty incredible experience. The art style looks amazing, the music in many places is also great, and the gameplay is pretty strong. They manage to have a very good blend of card game, horror and escape room, with incredibly creepy items, hints of something bigger afoot, and some nice mysteries. It does however suffer from some rather unbalanced gameplay and game roadblocks, mechanics that attempt to force you to die so you have to restart. It also lacks variety, since all the bosses are the same each time and use the same mechanics.

However, this all falls apart at the end of act 1, which is when the meta narrative really takes off, and where my interest dropped sharply. You get saddled with some annoying comments from a person sometimes coming over your gameplay for one, which is just immersion breaking and irritating. More importantly, act 2 is almost a completely different game, and it feels way less developed and thought out than act 1. A whole slew of new rules are thrown at you with no explanation as the card game aspect gets pretty split into four sets. the first two are from act 1 and work well together. One of the new ones only fits in a bit, and the other one completely conflicts with the rest.
You also lose all of the most interesting mechanics from the first act, so the gameplay feels far more sterile and bland for the second act. To make matters worse, the bosses are just... bad. And just to add to the list of issues, the entire horror aesthetic and atmosphere of act 1 is completely gone. It'll never come back. There's not even a penalty for losing. You're free to try again until you win by RNG. This is easily my least favorite part of the game.

Act three brings you back more to the style of act one, but it's worse in every way so it really doesn't bring the game back up. The game is once more a type of escape room card game like act 1, but with a lot of worse and more... sterile elements. Once again there's no real horror, and all the interesting flavor from act 1 was completely removed. The antagonist of this act is bland and mildly annoying, not coming off as a threat at all. The card enhancements have all been similarly sterilized since most are now just... plugging a card into a machine and choosing an effect.
This act also has the moments that the meta narrative fans all seem to love, but as someone who isn't a fan of them, they're really cliche and easy to see coming a mile away. Without too many spoilers, this game pulls the 'oh look, we have access to your computers internet and file system' twists(?) that somehow are supposed to make it more impressive.

Overall, I probably wouldn't have been as annoyed with this game if it hadn't been for the way it was marketed, almost feeling like a con. I haven't played other games from this dev, the gameplay footage and description don't mention that it's based on a meta narrative. Most of the reviews tell you to just play it without spoilers, so they aren't really a help either.
The final nail in the coffin in this aspect comes from the roadblocks I mentioned way up at the top. Without these roadblocks, act 1 would be far shorter overall, and because of that, I was already past the refund time when I learned it was about a meta narrative.

If this game had been entirely focused on Act 1, with more bosses, more variety over all, and some better balancing, I'd probably have recommended it as one of my favorite card games of all time. Sadly, instead it's a miss for me. \t\t\t\t\t
