\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy initial review was negative and is featured here:

As others have stated, the atmosphere and mechanics of the first Act of the game really gripped me. I enjoyed the rogue-like aspects and being given semi-random cards to build my deck with.

Act 2 completely changes the art style as well as how the game feels. Sure, it is a deck-builder and many of the mechanics remain the same but it is no longer a rogue-like. It becomes a constructed deck-builder and it felt like I had to change my deck for each and every fight which was tedious. Many battles came down to what I drew early on, and so many of the unfair advantages bosses have just make the game a grind.

+++++++


Edit to my former review. After playing the game through another time I see the purpose of the game a bit more clearly. It feels like a love letter to other deck-builders, and explores various styles of play in a linear fashion.

It is by no means a perfect game but I enjoy it now for what it was rather than what I had initially hoped for it to be.\t\t\t\t\t
