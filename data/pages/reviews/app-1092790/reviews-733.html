\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow ironic that a game about wasted potential had so much wasted potential

There is so, so much about this game that works; I'd say the first act is a masterpiece that balances style, gameplay, and intrigue perfectly. For those of you who've played the game, you might point out that I might just be agreeing with Leshy's game design principles; that aesthetics matter more than mechanics; which in my case is absolutely true. It was painful for me as someone who loves rich atmosphere in my games to go from drinking in the drip and pizzazz of act 1 to pushing myself through the drip and pizzazzless act 3 (and act 2 to a lesser extent). My hopes were further dashed as the final act teased me with Grimora's game, which seemed to be a return to the spirit of the first act, only to cut it short of even the first boss battle. I feel a bit cheated, I was so in love with the demo that I bought it without hesitation the second I finished it, and I really wish the rest of the game had the same feel. I think P03's game would work if it weren't 1 of only 2 board game segments, and the final act does read as if the developers ran out of time/money to finish the other 2 Scrybes which is totally forgivable coming from a small dev. Despite all that, I adore the way this game brings its conversation into the gameplay itself, and allows to player to come to their own conclusions simply based on how much they enjoy each segment. And I could forgive every monotonous part of this game if not for Luke ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Carder.

This is the point where the artistic decisions become unforgivable. I struggle to think of a single positive aspect Luke ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Carder (as he will hereby be known) adds to this game. He is intrusive at every step of the way, constantly chiming in like the irritating third wheel he is to remind me that this is his story, not mine. I don't give two ♥♥♥♥♥ how Luke ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Carder reacts to a tight situation or a good card, I'm already reacting. This is like if they made Petscop a real game but still kept Paul's commentary in; completely unnecessary and a total mood killer. The metastory, in my opinion, adds absolutely nothing to the meat of the game, it actually cheapens it quite a bit (especially that goofy ah ending holy christ) and not to attack individual artists here, but the acting leaves a lot to be desired. If you want to put an ARG in your game, have some decency to make it an optional website or something. Hoping to enjoy Kaycee's mod better, but that doesn't ease the sting of wasted potential in the main game.

EDIT: after playing Kaycee's mod I've decided to change my rating. I'm still not over the moon about it but it's consistent and entertaining\t\t\t\t\t
