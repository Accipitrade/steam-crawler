\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can safely say that Inscryption is/was quite unlike most other games I've ever played. And even if you've played stuff like it before, I'd still thoroughly recommend you give it a shot. Whether on sale or at full-price, Inscryption is worth your money.

Another shining example of "indie" games beating triple A titles out of the water, this fourth-wall-breaking psychological-"horror"-esque deckbuilder/cardbattler combines multiple types of videogame media into a cohesive and incredibly memorable experience. While somewhat light on characterisation, the few characters presented in this charming card dueller are well-crafted and tell an excellent story (or indeed stories) that will keep you glued to the screen for a good 10 hours at least. But, beyond just the main story, there's also a glut of additional content for achievement hunters and lore enthusiasts alike.

To tell much of the story of Inscryption is to accidentally reveal spoilers, but the important thing for you to know is that you will be playing a novel card game against (and with) a colourful cast, in many forms, and in ways that are almost guaranteed to come as a surprise (unless you've read a plot synopsis ahead of time!) But the best way to play is to jump right in yourself. And if the "Overwhelmingly Positive" rating is not enough to convince you to buy it, I don't really know what else to say.

For me, I've capped off my playthrough(s) and exhausted content at around 100 hours. Some of that was spent playing through the post-story content while watching other content, and some was no doubt from leaving the game on to do other things, but the majority was spent thoroughly enjoying the story and achievement hunting. While it may not necessarily give you 100 hours of content, it will still give you plenty of enjoyable moments, even if only you complete the main story.

So go on and get it! And don't be spooked by the aesthetic: there's no jumpscares ;)\t\t\t\t\t
