\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is an excellent game - it has very fun and varied gameplay, an intriguing story that I couldn't put down, and an interesting art style that complements both. I was constantly excited to try out a new strategy, solve the next puzzle, beat the next boss, master the next mechanic. All in all, it Inscryption a work of art, well worth both your time and money.

The game has two flaws. First, there is (surprisingly) a lack of replayability. Figuring out the mechanics and winning is much more story-driven than designed to stand alone, even though it could. Hopefully this will be corrected in a DLC. The second flaw, at least to me, is its rather depressing ending. Not to say that it was poorly done - indeed, like the rest of the game, it was done very well. The problem is that on the face of things Inscryption promises spooky/scary card game fun, not sorrow. When I want to have time off and have some fun playing games, surprise sorrow is not always appreciated (especially if done well). Hopefully this too, may be improved in a future DLC.\t\t\t\t\t
