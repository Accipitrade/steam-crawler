\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*SPOILER FREE REVIEW*

This game made the experience of uncovering mystery under the guise of just playing cards an exhilarating experience I can't recommend enough.

The deckbuilding and game mechanics in Inscryption are superb. I never felt overwhelmed by the amount of left turns the basic gameplay gave and I was continuously surprised by how much I warmed up to whatever was thrown at me. And it's deep, very deep.

And for the rest of the game? Excellent. If you have the 20+ hours you may need to 'finish' it's well worth it. If you only want a casual deck-building game, then the price is worth it merely for the atmosphere and satisfying game animation.

*this is a post-launch review before any major updates so it may become even better for casual and legacy players in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: The game changes dramatically and kinda sucks after 2 hours so just get it on sale if you really need to play it.

Listen, it's not as if the game is bad or anything. I kinda liked it. But I'm being asked if I would recommend the game, and the answer is that I would not. And that's because of a huge problem with the game's entire design.

So, the whole thing is supposed to be this creepy sacrificed based card game in a dark cabin. That's what the game's original prototype "Sacrifices Must Be Made" was like, when it was originally a ludem dare game jam game. That's what the store page and demo shows you. But it's actually only like the first 2 or 3 hours of the game, the rest of it is not that at all.

You know how people go "I wanted to like Brutal Legend, but I didn't like how it suddenly turns into a RTS game"? This game does something similar, where it totally changes how the game plays. It's at least still the same genre, card games all the way through, but it changes how it works constantly and the theme changes a lot. You go from this great atmospheric cabin to a GBA pixel style game. And then from there to something that's mostly holograms.

It's annoying because the first chapter of the game is a fun card based rougelite, think something like Slay the Spire. I just want to play that over and over. But it's the shortest chapter in the game because it's just the tutorial. Everything you see about the game is a glorified tutorial, and it sucks that it changes so hard.

Honestly the changes just makes the game drag on. The third act is the longest and the least fun. You're hard restricted to one deck type and you'll just be using the same cards over and over against the same random mooks over and over. At least the rougelike elements of the first part constantly reshuffles your cards when you die, so you find new synergies and combos over and over. And you're constantly getting cards from 4 different deck types in the GBA part, so you can easily refine your deck and change it up whenever you want. Part 3 is just so tedious in comparison.

I'm complaining a lot but it's not as if the game's like, bad or anything. It's just that everyone wanted the game to be part 1 the whole way through. Unfortunately, Inscryption is actually a sequel to The Hex in disguise and most of this game is just expanding on all the mysteries and lore about The Hex. And that makes Inscryption's story kind of boring. Because hey spoiler alert the The Hex is ALSO just a sequel to Pony Island. Satan makes evil soul sucking video games, parts 1 and 2 and 3. And it's kinda boring when the game's all like "what could the mystery be???" when it's clearly just Satan from Pony Island. The game's haunted cause Satan. Game's corrupted by evil because Satan. Satan because Satan.\t\t\t\t\t
