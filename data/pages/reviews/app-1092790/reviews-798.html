\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has multiple acts.
The first act is what you see in the store.
It is very enjoyable. The atmosphere is amazing and the card game is great and the escape room type progression integrates well into the game. Losing two (one vs bosses) battles end a run.

The player is given multiple ways to deal with bad rng such as:
- 2 draw piles, 1 for monsters, that mostly cost blood or bones to play, and one for squirrels, which have no cost play but are weak on their own. Stronger monsters can be played by sacrificing other monsters or squirrels. That way you don't end up unable to play any cards by bad draws.
-multiple paths so you can chose events that can potentially beneft you more / harm you less
- consumable objects

There is no complex deck building aspect as the deck resets at the sart of each try.


Then for act 2 the games design changes drastically.
Suddenly the great dark atmosphere is replaced by an 8-Bit aesthetic and even the card games rules are changed. There is deckbuilding and there now is only one draw pile. So you can end up being shafted by rng
badly, but now you can redo battles.
I have stoppled playing here as I do not enjoy these aspects of act 2 at all.

Act 1 was great but cetainly not worth the 20€
