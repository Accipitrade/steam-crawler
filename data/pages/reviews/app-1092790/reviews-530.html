\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA reluctant recommendation, because while I enjoyed the game and story, it still left me feeling a little dissatisfied. I've avoided mentioning below anything to do with the actual happenings of the plot or characters.

Other than nearly giving up in act 1 due to how Sisyphean it felt - I enjoyed the strategy (esp in act 3). The characters were interesting and diverse, and the best part of inscryption.

My biggest peeve was though that the game isn't complete in itself - if you want to get that sense of completion after you reach the end, you are required to search online for answers. I understand this must have been extraordinarily fun for those who played this from release, but now... I found the trek to find the rest of the information irritating. Learning how other people cracked the codes is not particularly enthralling to me, yet it felt like a necessary step to actually understanding what was going on.

Not saying anything should actually be changed about the game. I just wish I had seen someone mention how the plot relied on information outside of the game and wasn't contained entirely within gameplay. The best way to experience this game was to play it immediately after it came out, and have followed along with the community as they uncovered it. It was made to be uncovered, but now that it is...

Like I said, I enjoyed it, but I still feel dissatisfied.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is amazing. The story made is broken into 3 acts, all of which have amazing atmosphere, and story telling. The card play throughout is tense and well designed, despite being in the players favor for the sake of progression. To describe act 1 without going into spoilers, this part of the game is broken into two main parts: a small cabin, using escape room mechanics to both unlock new cards and items, as well as uncovering lore and progressing the story; and the table, where you play the card game itself against an (at that point) unnamed game master, using a "Slay the Spire" inspired map, and incredible boss fights.

Don't let the "Psychological Horror" tag fool you. Aside from some creepy imagery, and unnerving flavor text, the first act of the game is actually rather calm, and soothing; and any horror elements are completely gone by act 2 and 3.

After you beat the game, a full rogue-like mode is made available called "Kaycee's Mod" (despite the name it is part of the base game). It's an infinitely re-playable version of act 1, with new and re-balanced content. By completing optional challenge levels, you can unlock new starting decks, cards, events, items and lore, as well as a whole list of new achievements.

In conclusion, i recommend the game, even if you don't like card games, or rogue-likes; the setting and story alone makes this an amazing game worth your time and money.\t\t\t\t\t
