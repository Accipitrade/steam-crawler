\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you haven't played this game yet, I highly recommend you buy it and do so before you learn too much about it. It is the best surreal game I've played since Undertale, and it is best experienced without any spoilers.



=== SPOILERS BELOW ===



Overall, I was impressed with Inscryption's level of detail, amount of content, and pacing. Acts I and II were timed out well so that I never got bored with them. Act III started to drag on near the end, but it was never bad enough to make me actively disinterested in the game.

The game was not particularly difficult for me, especially after discovering a combo in Act I that got my Ouroboros to 100/100, which ruined a lot of the difficulty of the next two acts. This is not a major complaint though, since I was more focused on the story, and the post-game content offers extra challenges.

The video camera sequences were interesting. I'm normally not a fan of pure exposition in video form, but these sections were short enough and provided enough backstory that they were tolerable. There were some sections of dialogue that felt forced, and slightly ruined my immersion, but overall the acting was decent.

The finale was very well done. I actually choked up at some parts, and it really left me longing for what a full act would've been like with the other two Scrybes.

My biggest complaint about the game is the credits. After going through an emotional finale, followed by a climactic (if somewhat predictable) ending video, seeing the credits roll completely ruined my immersion. This is a game that does an impressive job of pretending it's not just a game, and being pulled back to reality by a credits sequence felt like a major miss. Obviously all the artists involved deserve to be acknowledged, but I wish it had been presented differently.

Other than that one complaint, I thoroughly enjoyed playing through Inscryption. I'll continue to play through the post-game content because I enjoy the mechanics of the game, but this will also go on my short list of games that I wish I could play through again for the first time.\t\t\t\t\t
