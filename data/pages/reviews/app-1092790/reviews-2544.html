\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a game well worth the price tag. It's high-quality, in gameplay, style, presentation, and variety. They've managed to cram quite a bit of card game mechanics from a variety of different games into the cards seemingly seemlessly, and even managed to craft entirely unique gameplay styles that feel seemless to switch between.
Make no mistake, Inscryption is a wonderful game, one I'll remember for ages, and even has some real, genuine replay value, which was not what I was expecting from this game.

That being said...
Without getting into spoilers, the ending is... Disapointing. Not in the way of "it Ruins the game", not even remotely, but the ending just feels bad. To the point where my playtime went from a thematically satasfying 13 hours to 14 hours because I was looking over every aspect of the game, trying to find out how to get the "Good" ending, whatever that entailed, because CLEARLY I had found the bad ending first, like what I figured was intended.
I even went deep into every single folder of the game's files, thinking maybe the game had gone with a ONE-SHOT type approach and required you to open the game's files to beat it, with the file you clearly had to edit or delete being hidden in there.
But... No. That was the game's ending. And it genuinely feels bad, both in clearly intended ways, and clearly unintended ways, too.
And that... Sucks. I'm not gonna sugar coat it; it's the lowest point of the game, and right in what should be its highest, too.

But, again, I still wholey reccomend the game. Though the ending is dissapointing to say the least, as I said, it doesn't really negatively effect the game that came before it, which is still an amazing experiance that you're not going to get from anywhere else, even the original gamejam game and I'd argue the demo itself.
Unless you hate card games with such a heated passion that seeing the code of one touch your computer would be enough for you to smash your memory drive and restart fresh with another, it'll be a genuinely fun, interesting time with what is easilly the most interesting card game I've ever played, with some of the most interesting characters I've ever experianced.\t\t\t\t\t

I think this is the game that finally sticks the landing on all counts. For once, the central gameplay was given enough attention to be genuinely engaging on its own without needing the surrounding plot intrigue to keep the player going. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh boy. Where do I begin?

TLDR; Inscryption is a 10/10 story experience and if you have $30 AUD and a weekend to spare, get the game and thank me later.

I haven't written reviews in the past because, well frankly, I don't tend to finish games. I'll try not to put any spoilers in this, but I'm just excited to tell you how much I love this game!

I'm not much of a singleplayer game sorta person, but when I saw Inscryption I was very curious. I did enjoy card battlers in the past, as the gameplay involves a sense of strategy that require a methodical thinking out of plans to execute... and when that plans plays out well, it is immensely satisfying.

I've had many moments like that in Inscryption. A powerful combo of cards played here and there, a clutch 1 extra heart of damage that was calculated just well enough to save me from a loss. However, that part is only a fraction of what this games bring to the table (pun not intended).

I didn't know much about the story and since it was very new, neither did the people I asked around. When you play this game, you're taken into another world, one that is delightfully colourful even in its dark and dreary atmosphere, as the game immerses you in a story of life and death. I've had moments IRL when I'm not playing the game that I get reminded of it. It's addicting- a short and bittersweet story which I will definitely remember for a long time.

The pace of the game is just right- I didn't feel bored at any moment of the game, and the storyline keeps you on your toes- just like a well written netflix series. I think most people can tell when they're playing a game, whether the developers put their heart and effort into making a game vs when it isn't present. In Inscryption, you definitely can feel the former. You get to experience the masterpiece of a game at a pace which matches your skills. And each chapter has breaks in between, so you don't feel like you're overwhelmed with constant gameplay.

The soundtrack and SFX also deserve a special mention- both were simple, but effective. The different themes throughout the game were beautifully contrasted, with each character giving off such different vibes by using the same sounds (I loved all the characters in it, even a few side bosses throughout). I loved how each act of the game had a completely different feel and unique flavour. It complemented the art styles perfectly in my opinion, and I would not want to see it changed any other way.

I don't think Inscryption would be that challenging for most people, but it for sure isn't a cakewalk. At least for me, I had failed a few times to get through the story- but the game is not punishing of those who make mistakes.

All in all, I think this game is well worth it's full price, so if you can get it for less than what it is, you're pretty much being PAID to play the game.

Give it a shot, and if you get stuck, well... what ever you do, don't email the game company ;P\t\t\t\t\t
