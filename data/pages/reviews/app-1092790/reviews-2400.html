\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good game, much longer than I expected it to be and I can say I fully enjoyed my time with it. That said, there are some negative points that I feel absolutely must be addressed.

"Sacrifices Must Be Made" was an amazing demo that hinted at a sinister story, hunger, and a child. Inscryption makes it very clear it's going in a different direction early on, and I can understand why. However, in terms of story, Inscryption and "Sacrifices Must Be Made" are totally different games. Inscryption is a new story in the paint and soundtrack of "Sacrifices Must Be Made". It's a cool story, no doubt, but I would have preferred to see how "Sacrifices Must Be Made" played out.

Moreover, there is clear padding. It's almost meta in how obvious it is. Luckily, the card game mechanic is fun and so long as you don't get a bad first draw and get completely steamrolled, you can usually reason your way to a victory. However, it was a bit eye rolling.

That said, it's hard to get into real criticisim and praise without getting into spoilers, and this is the type of game that absolutely must not be spoiled. The game itself is very fun, and the story is interesting. I would recommend it without hesitation to my friends.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am really disappointed with this game. I really don't like saying this because I have enjoyed Daniel Mullins's previous works a lot, but this game is deceptively bad, which I'm going to try and explain without spoilers and focus on the mechanics.

Quite shortly into the game, around the 2nd or 3rd hour of play, the fundamental rules of the card game change in very minor ways, but ways that completely ruin its balancing as it becomes a lot more at the whim of RNG. Instead of being able to choose between 2 decks, your monster cards and squirrel cards (for those unfamiliar, the former cards usually require sacrifices to play, but squirrels don't and act as a free sacrifice to play on the board), from now on you get a completely random card. This means it is now common to get turns where you cannot play a card, and it has even often happened where I lose a match just by being unable to play any round until I lose.

From what I understand, with this change in the rules the best way to play is the most boring way to play, i.e. play your strongest monsters as much as you can, rather than monsters with interesting additional mechanics, just boof your opponent hard and fast.

Basically, after a fantastic first couple hours, this game careens downhill in fun, in how interesting it is, how tactically diverse you can play the game. It really feels like I was sold a game most of which I find unenjoyable by the advertising and demo only showing parts from the first couple of hours.\t\t\t\t\t
