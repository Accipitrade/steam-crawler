The Best of its Genre

This will be a largely spoiler free review to the best of my ability, it is a very difficult game to review at any length without spoilers but I won't put any in here.

In truth, Inscryption is in my opinion one of the best games ever made and is easily the number one in its genre. How the developers managed to fuse a deck-building game with psychological horror, two totally polar opposite genres into one game is nothing short of something that's worth being universally lauded.

The game starts in a cabin with a mysterious figure, you can't leave the cabin and this figure, who acts as a sort of Dungeon Master, leads the game and you play along. Most of the game is deck-building and playing with its set of unique rules, it's honestly not that hard to learn and you'll likely end up finding it the most enjoyable part. The gameplay for the card-playing aspects is genuinely fun.

Around the cabin you've got little things to do, you can leave the table at more or less any time, a few puzzles to solve here and there that are worth doing and will aid you in your quest to beat the mysterious cabin figure.

That being said, I'll mostly end my review here, what I will say is though, the game only gets better the more you play it and you'll be pleasantly surprised at what you find. The writing is phenomenal and philosophical as well as downright sadistic. The Soundtrack is also an absolute banger.

I'll recommend this game to anyone and everyone, it's genuinely some of the most fun I've had in the last year.

10/10, don't even wait for a sale, it's worth the full price, Go Fish!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAre you interested in the world of trading card games? Do you wanna keep trusting the heart of the cards? You won't get an Exodia The Forbidden One, but your cards can be shred to pieces.. More pieces than the ones you would have to gather before trying to summon the aforementioned creature. So keep in mind there’s no turning back or salvation for your poor little companions. Not even a referee will be able to say: since that invocation is banned in competitions you can keep the parts again! Let me tell you that here, this is not going to happen… You will have to fight and strengthen your deck, yes, but for your own survival. So… Like a famous card duelist once quoted: It 's time to du-du-duel!

Okay time to get serious here * BRAIN PLEASE NO MORE YU-GI-OH REFERENCES!*

The story is one plot twist after another, and let me tell you that never in my life have I enjoyed a card game so much. The mechanics are the most polished I ever found in a game of this kind, the originality of the matches, the story itself and even the design is, simply put, mind blowing. Even the non existent tutorial and the way they guide you to learn all the game mechanics that keep piling up one after another is brilliant.
Sometimes you will not know what to expect next and that will keep your survival instinct at the highest level possible, so you can just stare at everyone with suspicious eyes not knowing if you can even trust your own cards anymore.
The power of friendship will not help your deck to improve and keep getting deadlier, your choices will. So be careful cause sometimes the devil likes to wear prada and other times he likes to bust your deck out of existence.
Are you not photogenic? Don't worry! Your fear of cameras may even be useful to you…

My personal advice to survive this journey is: DON’T TRUST ANYONE, NOT YOUR FRIENDS FROM STEAM, NOT YOUR OWN CARDS, AND NOT EVEN YOURSELF!

Personally, I believe Inscryption is one of those games that you have to experience firsthand, and give it a chance, but better do it without seeing anything previously.. So the events that are going to happen end up catching you by surprise.. There are some moments that even reached my heart.
I cried, not gonna lie when you are almost at the end of the game and the opponents thoughts at the last moments of their life were only wanting to give you a handshake because they considered you a worthy rival….
I bought and completed this game on PS4 and then started to play it on steam, even though I’m not so fond of card-based games tbh.

The developer even released a free update which unlocks the infinite mode for the card du-du-duels!

10/10
