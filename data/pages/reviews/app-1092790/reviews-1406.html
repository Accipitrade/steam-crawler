\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDid I enjoy this game? yes. Was playing fun? Yes. However overall it leaves me shrugging and thinking it could have done so much better. Story wise, nothing really matters in the end, I will say that without spoiling things. It was a mystery that had me interested, and in the end, I honestly felt insulted. The actual card game is quick, but with quick games, comes a reduction in potential. Often times you just get a trash start. I do like how over time you can modify the deck and upgrade cards. It is a really interesting way to do things in a way that isn't really possible with a real physical card game as easily. Fun, but also your deck keeps getting trashed right as you get the hang of things. After finishing the game, I thought I might go at it again, but felt like there was no reason to. I never felt like I was able to fully explore and enjoy the game as you get kicked back to square one before you can really enjoy things. That being said, it is the journey that should be enjoyed, but there isn't much in the way of going back and redoing things. Still, I commend the developers for trying something unique. As far as asymetrical card games go, I found this to be pretty decent. Is this worth it? Yes, I think so, but don't expect it to really be satisfying or to have much in the way of the game itself. I think it was meant to be more about the story, but again, I found the story to actually not be very interesting. Again, no real way to go into detail without ruining the plot, but don't expect it to be clever because it really isn't. If you want to kill some time, this is a solid experience to play if it is on sale. I didn't feel like the price I paid was equal to the experience, so try for a decent sale first.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIMHO this is game of the year for 2021.

Take Hand of Fate (another game you should play), replace the Arkham-style brawling with strategic card games, add more puzzle elements, a sprinkle of ARG, throw in some truly brilliant boss designs and Inscryption is what you get.

The card game design is inspired. It's tricky to get a hold of at first, but it's fascinating how much they do with it, especially when the other races get involved. It's really, really excellent and the card upgrade systems make it all the sweeter. Plus, the boss fights go the extra mile to keep you on your toes; no two are alike. Even with a strong deck, there was always enough new content that I looked forward to the next fight. It wasn't like Hand of Fate where I just wanted to skip ahead because the encounters were overwhelming. That said, Inscryption is broken into 3 parts and the 1st part is the hardest, by far.

One major gripe is content gating in the 1st area (which is partially why it's so hard). If you win too quickly, the eyes across from you turn red and summon a nearly unwinnable monster combo. He does this 3 times and 2 of those times require you to start over from the beginning (which is *extremely* bad because you lose all the special cards you got from the escape room and there is nothing you can do to get those cards back). The same applies to the first three-stage boss fight. If you haven't solved all the escape room puzzles by the time you defeat this boss.... you have to start over and play the entire game again. It really came as a surprise to me and left me *this close* to uninstalling Inscryption altogether.

To put it another way: Inscryption wants to be a rogue-lite, but it's narrative-focus and card upgrade system encourage you to become so powerful that you never need the rogue-lite elements. The ONLY means it has of addressing this is to cheat so you're forced to restart twice. The problem is that it never does that again. After the initial cheating, I never died again in any of the 3 acts.

That small issue aside, once I hit part 2, I was hooked and kept playing until I finished. There's so much more I could say, but here's the important part: This is a serious contender for GotY 2021.\t\t\t\t\t
