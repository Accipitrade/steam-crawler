\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game achieves what many fail to achieve. In just 3 hours of play I felt like I had played so much longer, and it was never boring or frustrating. The gameplay loop of attempting to progress through the game board, dying, discovering secrets to progress the story, washing, then repeating is so fun.

I don't want to spoil anything so I'll try to be vague. The characters you'll meet early in the game are super intriguing. The game master, cloaked in shadow, provides an inviting albeit devious ambience to the game. The satisfaction and epiphany you feel when you discover the solution to a puzzle is very rewarding, and the early game is littered with this.

Once you FINALLY beat the game master after many failed runs, its not over...suddenly the game changes entirely...I won't spoil it as I am just starting this part, but it will certainly shock you if you don't spoil it for yourself.

TLDR: Game is awesome. If you like Slay the Spire, or other card games you will enjoy this. Everything in this game is wrapped up in a spooky little package and it is incredibly fun. I'm excited to continue. Oh, and for the price the developer is asking you will get WAY more than you expect. You will not regret buying this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tObviously, a big point of contention amongst reviews is that the gameplay shifts significantly after certain points.
Personally, I was delightfully surprised by this, and I'm glad that a game is willing to be this subversive.
But, unfortunately, there were some massive issues with the implementation:
1: After the shift, there's no way to go back to the original gameplay, until you've beaten the rest of the game, which can be upwards of 8 hours. And then, it resets literally everything, including the long auto-lose tutorial.
2: The portion immediately after the shift is... so bad. Just... absolutely awful. Combat goes from feeling smooth and atmospheric, to clunky and unsatisfying. Just two underpowered decks bapping into each other. It gets way better after it shifts again, but regardless...
3: This game is not balanced, at all. The first two runs are brutal and unwinnable. Then it briefly hits a satisfying sweet-spot of hard-but-fair. But finding just one overpowered combination on the third or fourth run trivializes everything, including the "final" boss, and triggers the shift. And then it becomes even less challenging.

As the game currently is, I can't honestly recommend it. Its amazing subversiveness doesn't justify how unsatisfying most of the actual gameplay feels, due only to relatively minor issues.
But if they release a properly balanced and fully-fledged roguelite mode, then I will happily change my review.\t\t\t\t\t
