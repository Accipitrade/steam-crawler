I've never seen anything like this game before, and I'm so glad I gave it a try. It's just fantastic, a huge recommendation from me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is difficult to recommend....

Hear me out. I know this game is getting acclaim across the board, but I need to judge the game as a whole. The game can be summed up in 3 acts. Chapter 1, 2, and 3.

Chapter 1 is utterly brilliant. Moody, mysterious, dark, and all around fun to play. It is the strength of this game. If Inscryption was nothing but this first chapter extended, I would give it top marks and make it my game of the year.
Chapter 2 is just.. ok. Nothing really special, and a bit annoying at times. After you get over the initial surprise, it doesn't have much in it besides a pure RNG fight. Far more random change than the first chapter.
Chapter 3 is just tedious. It is a long slog through something I really just couldn't care less about. It also restricts gameplay to a single type of combat whereas the previous chapters have multiple.

Which leads me to my biggest issue with the game. Caring about the characters.
In the first chapter it is easy. There is just you and... the creature. You spend time figuring out how they act, how they feel, and maybe a hint of their reasons. Past this point in the later chapters we get barely any character development for anyone. It is a huge miss on the games part. still.. there was so much work put in this game, and it is certainly unique. So I'm going to recommend it, at least for the first chapter.\t\t\t\t\t
