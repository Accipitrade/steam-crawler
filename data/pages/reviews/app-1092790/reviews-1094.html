\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are reading this review, then you should stop. Go play the game yourself. Going through as blind as possible is the best possible option that you can make for this game. Go play it.

Still reading? Well, I won't spoil anything, other than basic and minor game mechanics (but not all of it).

Inscryption is a card game where you have to play along with a disturbing game master. However, it's also an escape room game. So you have to solve puzzles around the cabin. This, however, is a small part of the game. Inscryption is a deceptively simple game (I say simple, but there's a lot of strategy you need to learn). There is always a twist around the corner, and you will NEVER know what will happen next (going in blind).

There has been so much love and dedication poured into this game, making it one of the most surprising and interesting games I have played in a while (but I haven't played Undertale yet, so what do I know). Go play the game. The music is amazing, the gameplay is amazing (gets a little getting used to) and the story is on another level with so much hidden secrets and lore. Go play the game. Full on blind.
