I didn't have this feeling of my hair standing on end since I've finished... Bioshock Infinite, I guess? Yeah, that's what completing Inscryption feels like, Bioshock Infinite and the Blair Witch combined.

By the time credits ended I was genuinely concerned this game deleted itself and maybe something else from my hard disk.

Ummm, details... details, right? So... Yeah, gameplay mechanics are easy to grasp and do not feel repetative and boring 17 hours of playing later. I mean, core gameplay is fun.

Atmosphere is fantastic. This game still gives me creeps. And the way it tears down the 4th wall... no, I mean, it washes it down, it breaks it down, the game enters-your-own-home-and-threatens-you-with-a-gun down, that's what I mean.

It's worth 10 times its cost in gameplay fun and emotional charge. I can only think of a couple of AAA titles that keep up with it.

If you have doubts - don't.
