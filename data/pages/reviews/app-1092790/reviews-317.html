\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is overrated.

It starts off with gameplay in line with what's shown on the store page. Decent concept and gameplay loop despite the typical RNG flaws you would find in any game in this genre. But, just as you start to get into a good groove it goes off the rails by trying too hard to lay on the "meta" narrative to be edgy and progressive. And essentially throwing a lot of new stuff at you for the sake of just stuff being thrown at you.

The game is broken into 3 acts which could honestly be 3 different games in the same genre. Your cards/deck/strategy really don't matter from act to act as the slate gets wiped clean and you have to in essence start over each time - both in getting cards, building your deck and in picking up entirely new core mechanics that again, could be their own game. And all these things are thrown at you with no explanation and fly in the face of how you play the game for the first several hours.

Bottom line, I feel this game tries to be too much. There's some good ideas and mechanics in each act here but it's a shame the game never settles on any as it prefers to reinvent the wheel each time. I really think the game would have been better to just focus on the concept in Act 1 which is how the game is depicted on the sales page. Instead, the result is just a bunch of stuff that could be good if you were actually able to invest the time to get used to it. But you're always being shuffled off to the next iteration.

By act 3 when the game changed completely for the 3rd time I'd had enough and walked away.\t\t\t\t\t
