\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic idea for a game completely ruined after the first act. It loses it's style, it's tension, and reveals it's mystery to be banal and cribbed from much more popular franchises. Not to mention it is completely void of any real challenge unless you get painfully unlucky. Scattershot throughout the game you will get live action footage featuring some of the worst acting I have ever seen like someone watched the Blair Witch Project and thought, I can do this worse.

Much of the escape room is optional, it simply rewards you with a few extra cards which you really will not need. Some of the puzzles are engaging though, and I would have liked to see more emphasis on this part of the game rather than the simplistic but engaging card mechanic.

Edit: While I still have problems with the structure of this game on the whole and the cheese live action component, they have now added a mode that allows you to play the first section as a rouge like card builder. After spending time in this mode I have come to appreciate the style and indie feel of the gameplay and atmosphere. Worth a pick up now especially if it is on sale. It's nice to see people trying things with their games even if every part of the design does not land.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlmost certainly my favorite game of all time, I finished it over a span of 15 hours in two days then went back to get all achievements.
Be very careful of spoilers for this game if you intend to play it, it is an experience just as much as it is a game, and while I only watched a few minutes of gameplay I still mourn losing that experience if just a little.
There are a lot of cool little secrets hidden away, and most of then actually give the player gameplay rewards which I always like.
I've personally loved this style of gameplay for a while, and the rest of the games that have, what I can only think to call meta-gameplay, have mediocre or just bad gameplay (in my opinion). Even if you don't like card games or horror, so long as you can stand these I still would recommend (rather basic cardplay (once again, in my opinion), and not a single jumpscare (personally I wouldn't call this a horror game but there are certainly many creepy things in it)).
Although I will admit (minor spoilers), the ending caught me very much off guard.
Gave me a lot of STIMULATION.\t\t\t\t\t
