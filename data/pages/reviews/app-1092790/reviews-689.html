\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption begins with you playing a pseudo rogue-like card game against a man in his cabin. Throughout a run the player can acquire different items and cards to help build up their deck and help them venture further along in the game. Yet, Inscryption is so much more then this. Within the cabin there are puzzles to solve and secrets to find, and after all this the game continues to evolve itself. Just as you get comfortable with the game new aspects are introduced completely diverting expectations.

Then once you have reached the end, you get access to a full on rogue-like version of the game with added modifiers and extra unlockables.

Inscryption is an experience that shouldn't be missed, and without spoiling the game I cannot express what makes the game so special. Just go and play the game, and don't look up any answers to anything or you'll ruin a well-crafted experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is not your usual card battle game.
It is mixed with some rouge lite elements and small puzzles.
the game has a unique style and some fantasy story elements with a good number of mechanics that help it stay fun.
While there is a good chunk of things in it, the game teaches you the mechanics and the world around you quite well.
I see it as one of the best games I have played, and it is going to leave a good mark in the genre.
I highly recommend it.
While there are some random elements ( that cards you pull from your deck) you can really learn to make good strategies after playing it for a while and understanding the core mechanics.

A true unique and wonderful experience, clearly made with a great passion. has that unique flavour that is missing from AAA games.

Even if you are not a fan of deck building games I believe you will enjoy this game.\t\t\t\t\t
