\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR

A very unique card game/rogue-like combination. The story is definitely the biggest selling point for this game, but if you enjoy card-based games then there is quite a lot to like.

Gameplay:

Like most other rogue-like card games (of which there is a surprising amount of) your entire objective is to build a deck and items that you can use to beat a host of different battles/bosses. Depending on where you are in the game, you are given a specific starter deck and you must build upon what you are given (using different tile spaces that offer a host of different options to increase the power of your deck) or by obtaining card packs. It is by no means the most deep card based battler on the market (thanks to its 1 lane combat), but there is a surprising amount of depth considering how you can use the cards at your disposal.

Story: Definitely the games strongest selling point. Without spoiling too much, just go into the game with an open mind and don't look up any spoilers (because the twists are what really separate this game from a lot of its counterparts). Really just gonna leave it at that and hopefully you can experience everything as intended to see why so many people hyped up this game.

Achievements: A good mix of interesting challenges that have to be accomplished in a run to beating Kaycee's Mod (the re-playable section of the game that is unlocked once you beat the game itself) on its highest difficulties. The Kaycee's Mod challenges are definitely going to take you the longest to complete (especially doing an all skulls run), but nothing that is unattainable for those that are willing to put in the time to learn the intricacies of the game.

Final Verdict: As someone who has played numerous rogue-like card games I think this game honestly sits somewhere in the middle. It's story is definitely something that puts in head and shoulders above most games of the ilk, but I feel like there isn't all that much depth to the battles themselves as you pretty much always just try to make a deck that will kill the opponent on the first turn (granted, this isn't the case for every deck and archetype, but is without a doubt the best way to play thanks to you and your opponent only starting out with a measly 5 life per battle). I think if you could maybe tweak player/opponent health and potentially add more cards (which I highly doubt will be the case, because, as of this post it has been 1 year since the last update) then the game would be in a much better place.

Still an enjoyable game that I think most people who enjoy these types of games will find at least some enjoyment from.

7.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
