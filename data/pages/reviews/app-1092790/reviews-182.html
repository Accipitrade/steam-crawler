\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game on a whim during the Steam Spring Sale. I'm happy I did because this turned out to be an incredible game. Inscryption is broken into three acts; and while the latter two acts aren't as captivating as they could have been, the first act more than makes up for it. The art style and atmosphere of the first few hours make the game worth getting. Gameplay-wise, coming from someone who's not a huge fan of deck-building games, I found that the game straddles a pleasant line between being easy enough that it isn't frustrating and being hard enough it's not boring. It's probably not deep enough to milk 100s of hours out of, but you're not going to get that out of every game-- and again, the game's absolutely worth getting for the atmosphere alone. I didn't come into this game expecting much, but it's been along time since a game captivated me the way Inscryption did. I'm honestly surprised this game isn't talked about more, as it is an absolutely fantastic game.\t\t\t\t\t
