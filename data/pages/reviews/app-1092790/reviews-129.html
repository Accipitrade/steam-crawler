\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic mesh of genres into a truly unique game. I had so much fun playing, but I do wish there were difficulty settings - I found it a touch too easy at times.

I was, however, really sincerely disappointed by the ending. It felt very sudden to me - the way things were going, I expected to have a turn with each of the Scrybes. I did not feel sad to see them go because I barely got any actual time with them (aside from Leshy, who the game also tries to make you feel sad for, which also doesn't work as he's spent half the time murdering you). Grimora's and Magnificus' 3D boards both looked like a lot of fun to play, and you get less than two minutes with them in the whole game. It entices you with interesting mechanics and more gameplay and then takes that away from you. You can replay the Inscryption you see in act I through Kaycee's mod, which is great, but I desperately wish that included access to the other Scrybes' boards. It just feels like the game was cut short halfway through, which I suppose is technically the point, but I'm still left disappointed because of it.

I was ready to tout Inscryption as one of the best games of all time before the ending happened, and now I'm left simply feeling that it's a good experience. All in all Inscryption is absolutely worth picking up for a bizarre whirlwind of a game that you'll love playing, but if you get soured on a game by a disappointing ending, it might be a bit risky.\t\t\t\t\t

In another boss fight, the boss will access your Steam friends list. Your Steam friends will be then used as cards to attack you.
