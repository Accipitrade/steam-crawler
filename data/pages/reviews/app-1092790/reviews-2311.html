\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood game. Very spook.

Oblique version: Basically an 8 hour 4th dimensional novel about the failures of programmers to predict a certain late-stage institution's ability to warp global currents in its own favor(and the increasingly perverse pressures demanded to right this disequalized system's inherently monopolistic paradigm) plus the frightening implications of recursively recombinant systems as they perplex. What's great is you the player get to experience the inside of these systems as an actor truly immersed in the frame story from both angles, entrant-aspirant and tyrant-emperor. Inscryption spends ample time in each modality. It gets into especially interesting territory regarding the underlying mechanics of such sandboxes wherein a consumer is led down a path of achievement belying an ulterior agenda. Thus the overall control of the system can be seen to ever be in the hands of its founder and not its leader(s). Each level of the narrative is a reflection of the other levels, including realspace. Inscryption isolates contemporary proliferation of unparsable data-oceans and the obfuscation therein provided even as it is created in that same, seemingly ineffable reality. Furthermore, the game's meta-cohesion is polished to a "mirror" shine. The felt experience of you the player seamlessly overlaps your avatar. This fact alone makes the mystery more tantalizing -itself an intended effect referenced in the ludonarrative. Incredible work.\t\t\t\t\t
