\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpoiler Warnings, so be aware.

Most of the promotional material (teasers, demo, etc.) only cover Act 1, and make it out to be a full on horror deckbuilder, which is only half true. As many people have said before, Acts 2 and 3 are completely different, and are not the traditional "horror" genre that most people would think of. It does, however, remain a deckbuilder, but now with a lot more mechanics. Act 2 introduces the two new deckbuilding mechanics, energy and gems (Techno and Mage), but does not really explain what they mean yet. In addition, it becomes a 2D old-school-feeling rpg, where you explore a map and do the same style battles (with the new mechanics potentially), all in 8-bit graphics. Act 3 is where the two new deck styles are explained in more detail on how they work, and it returns to the 3D style of Act 1, however, the ambiance is completely different, as it is now the Techno boss running the show.

Throughout Act 1, there really is not many hints to the actual story of the game, though occasionally, there are small snippets and cut-ins. After the completion of Act 1, you finally get to see the first part of the story through a series of cut scenes that are found on a camera's storage. This continues to set up throughout the rest of Acts 2 and 3, with many different characters referencing the "OLD_DATA," and its corrupting influence on the game itself. By the end of the game, we see that our character has gotten himself caught up in a government cover-up, and the game was written on the disk to overwrite the OLD_DATA and hide it.

Overall, it is a phenomenal game, with several surprises, interesting mechanics, and some really cool ways to break the game (intentionally coded it, there are even some achievements for doing so).\t\t\t\t\t
