\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is simply amazing as a sort of psychological horror game, or just unpredictable game in general. If you are planning on buying this game for the card game alone, I would not entirely recommend it. I personally enjoyed the card playing aspects of the entire game, but others have not been satisfied. If you are looking to play this game for no story and just the cards, I definitely don't recommend. This game is designed to be an overall experience for the player, not a standalone card game.

For completionists, this game's 30 achievements are not hard to get, you get about 15 to 20 of them automatically on just one blind playthrough, and the rest I got in just a bit more time after beating the game. It takes about 15 to 20ish hours to get them all if you are efficient.

The rest of this review will involve mild spoilers, so only read on if you are ok with that, otherwise skip to the very end of the review for more of a sort of final verdict.

The hardest part of the game is the first act. The first act of the game is also what many people consider to be the best card-game wise. I disagree, finding all three acts to be great in terms of card gameplay, but the third act is noticeably easier, and the second act is clearly based off of the Pokemon TCG game for Game Boy. This made it very enjoyable to me as a retro enthusiast, but the second act also does go from the card game and puzzle room aspect of the first act to an RPG retro card game vibe, which does not sit well with many who played it as it is such a sudden change.

Again with the third act, it is pretty easy in comparison to other acts, but it still has its moments and I found it extremely enjoyable. It's more meant as a way to start wrapping up the story and put a sort of end to things.

The game is overall an amazing experience if you decide to play it for story and as an experience, rather than just a card game.

It is currently one of my favorite games of all time, and I am now, after getting all achievements, playing the beta version of Kaycee's mod, which makes the first part of the game effectively infinitely replayable, adding a lot more content and even some lore, and making the card game aspect more balanced and fun. It's a must for anyone that wants to continue playing Inscryption but has gotten about all they can from the base game.

To end things, buy this game unless you want a standalone card game. If that is your intention, there are other card games out there, if you get this game for just that you will likely be disappointed and miss out on the rest of the potential for this game.

10/10 as an overall experience, this is a VERY unique game, of which I doubt there is much else like it, but don't buy it expecting a standalone card game experience aside from the (currently in beta) official mini-expansion Kaycee's Mod you may choose to play.\t\t\t\t\t
