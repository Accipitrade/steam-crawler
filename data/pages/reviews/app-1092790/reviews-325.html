\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe strong concept, the story, the atmosphere of this game are things that someone doesn't really expect to make such an impress while starting a small indie card game. The way it evolves and transforms into a weird storytelling experience is magnificent. Playing card battles is immersive and all these small puzzles make the gameplay even more interesting. The dark, peculiar world of "Inscryption" is something unique that you have to experience by yourself.


Manometer: 9/10


The good
- Fantastic concept. It uses its narrative means by such an imaginative ways that they transform a simplistic card game into a really unique experience. It's a lot more than what you're initially expecting.
- Amazing, weird story with tons of mystery and twists. Also, it has great pace and the way it evolves will surprise you.
- Simple but very immersive gameplay during the card battles. You will always want to play one more match. By time it evolves and some new mechanics enter the game so it never becomes boring or repetitive.
- Great atmosphere and awesome direction. It gets you immersed right from the beginning inside its mysticistic, dark world and it makes you wonder what's going on there, where are you and who is this guy at the other side of the table.
- Well written dialogues. Talking cards is a nice addition and actually they are more of npcs. To get you sympathize or attach emotionally with your cards is really clever.
- Some interesting boss fights that force you try different tactics and tricks in order to beat them.
- Wonderful music that enhances the atmosphere and gives the tone during battles.
- Aside of the card part the game has a lot of hidden secrets and small, clever puzzles that give it extra depth and put you in a total different process of exploring and discovering the world around you.
- Some insanely crazy hidden secrets connected through Mullins' games' universe that you'll never actually find by yourself but it's really fun to search for them on the internet.

The bad
- The gameplay of the card part is very immersive, but you'll master it pretty quickly and although it evolves, it never gets really deep. Soon, it feel less challenging and after the ending it has little replayability value.
- Although it has fantastic atmosphere and you don't really need better graphics for this kind of a game, some 3d images look low res and have a lot of noise.\t\t\t\t\t
