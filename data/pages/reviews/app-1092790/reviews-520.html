\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSPOILERS FOR ACTS 2 AND 3

This game is awesome. A phenomenally designed card game using your own creatures as a primary resource (the secondary ones introduced in act 2 are cool in concept but aren't very good in execution. blood and bones are really the most fleshed out and well thought through resources), act 1 in the cabin is atmospheric, eerie and downright blood curdlingly tense at times.

Act 2 is probably my favorite, even if act 1 is infinitely more replayable. I love the subversive conceptc of the game turning into a 16bit rpg and for as flawed as they are, the mox are an awesome resource and a fun deck building challenge to tackle, even if the mox deck is probably the weakest one in the act.

Act 3 is... rough to say the least. PO3 is an intimidating villain and seeing his take on what he thinks is "Leshy's imperfect Game" was novel at first, but the fact that it comes with built in perma-death and that your decisions are impossible to change make it a miserable slog to get through once the novelty wears off. Energy is a poorly thought out mechanic and your extra deck being unable to interact with it like act 1s squirrels makes it all the more clunky. Botopia is an interesting setting and the "cabin" PO3 has you in is much larger than Leshy's leaving more puzzles and more to discover. But overall, Act 3 is easily the weakest.

Ultimately, if you like card games, roguelites, horror games, strange stories, and Leshy the best character Leshy, then play this game. The strong parts more than cover the weaknesses and Kaycees Mod provides a truly infinitely repayable version of Act 1 for once you've beaten the game.\t\t\t\t\t
