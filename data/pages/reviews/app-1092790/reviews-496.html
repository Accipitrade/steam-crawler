\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*DISCLAIMER!* If your looking for DOWNSIDES to this game, scroll to the BOTTOM of this post, for I spent a while complimenting this game towards the top of this post.

Inscryption is a great card game. It's got plenty of funny, interesting, and OP cards for you to use an fight against. The boss fights are actually challenging, there are gambles and buffs you can use to permanently alter your cards, and this game actually managed to make me invested in its story as well, which very few games have managed to do over the years. The best part of Inscryption, in my opinion, is altering your cards. To give an example, you will sometimes be given an option to buff a card's HP, power, or tack an entirely new option on the card (such as flight or death's touch). On one of the first runs where I beat the final boss, I had a yeti card, which is very strong to say the least, and I granted it flight. This meant that I could sometimes one shot the other card master because they have 5HP, my yeti has 7 attack power, and flight allows you to hit the card masters lifepoints directly! Sadly I couldn't one shot bosses with it, because they have multi forms. (it just means I had to use it twice or thrice instead). ANYWAYS, if you enjoy turn based strategy games with fun looking cards and game mechanics, you'd likely enjoy this game.

My favorite things about this game are
1. YOU CAN MAKE AND MODIFY YOUR OWN CARDS! (this just means more replay ability in the long run, as
well as different ways to mop the floor with those pesky bosses)
2. When you fail, you get to make a card of yourself for the next life before you die, (this is cool because you can create your own card, with its own custom stats and name, for your next life to use.)
3. The player isn't the only one to mold the game to their liking, the actual bosses have plenty of rule bending BS for you to learn from and deal with. (which is not only a fun challenge that spices up the game, but its also a tip of the hat to YU GI OH, because in the TV show, they oftentimes didn't follow the game's rules, which I find to be funny.
4. The story is actually engaging and cool, unlike most other games where the story is: "GO BEAT UP THE BAD MAN BECAUSE REASON". In this game the story has actual progression, involvement, and is about your character surviving and discovering the truth.
5. This game is (sorta) multiple games in one
6. I really enjoy the music
7. The game doesn't cost much $$$

NOW! On the flipside, if you hate turn based and strategy based gameplay, puzzles, (sometimes) unwinnable scenarios, RNG, or a game that constantly switches up game mechanics depending on the battle, then I wouldn't recommend this game to you. This game is also a bit on the short side as well, as in if you just play the basic mode only, I'd say it takes around 20 ish hours to complete. Some bossfights can be straight up unfair, an intentional design choice, mind you, and other fights simply boil down to RNG. However, I'd say you can win the vast majority of battles using your brain, but not all of them, so do expect to die here and there.
