\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most charming games I've played or even seen in a good while. And I'm not just talking about the unique and strange game play, I'm also talking about the art style and graphics of the game as well. How in Act 1 it's 3D but still pixelized, but in Act 2 it turns a classic top down 2D/Pixel style. And the decision to make characters have their own sounds when they talk adds a lot to the feel of the game. The game play itself is really fun and cool, I genuinely really liked how despite all 3 acts being card related, they switch things up entirely and you have to relearn how to do most things. And the way they executed *how* these card games and rules work is honestly amazing. Not to mention, combining a card game with the horror genre? and making it an ARG? I really hope to see more games *this* unique from these developers because it was a great experience. Oh, and the boss fights in act 3, some of the coolest mechanics I've ever seen in a game, one of them connects you to the internet and create your own card to send to another real player, and if they win with your card then you win, but you can also receive cards from others too; another boss makes you dig through your own computer files to find the biggest sized files you can and depending on how large it is, you do extra damage; and I could talk about the other two Uberbot bosses but I think I've made my point.

-Great soundtrack to listen to as you play, it really captures the mood of the game, especially the boss fights. (IThe "Scrybe of Magick" song is soooo good).
-The style and graphics of this game, as well as the character designs themselves is super charming to look at. (Despite the dude being a bit annoying, I really liked Po3. Also Lonely Wizbot best character 100/10 best boi).
-The game play itself has some of the coolest mechanics I've ever seen in a game, really shows how much thought and care the developers put into making this.

The story itself is a bit confusing. I get that it's suppose to be an ARG and you're suppose to figure it out and all - and that it's probably my fault for not understanding it completely - but I did get lost like halfway through, especially towards the end when somehow the game disc is sentient or something? There's nothing wrong with the story itself, maybe just the way it's presented. EDIT: I just found out this was an actual ARG where people had to find the Inscryption floppy disk irl, I just did a bunch of research on the puzzles and what people had to figure out to get the full story, I completely take what I said before this back because now i understand that it's suppose to be hard to understand on purpose. The developers really went all out with hiding secrets, they even hid stuff in past games that you have to play to decipher codes in this game.

All in all this is one of the cooler and more fun games I've played in years, can't really think of another that was - for the millionth time - this unique. I mean there's really no better words to describe this game besides unique. Thank you developers for this experience.\t\t\t\t\t
