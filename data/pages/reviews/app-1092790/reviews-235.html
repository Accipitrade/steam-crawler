\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like card games and rougelikes, you will 100% fall in love with Inscryption. At first it seems quite simple, but it quickly grows into a complex and difficult yet satisfying game. It doesn't end at the end, either, as the game always has something new to offer. Even after completing the main game, there is a massive post-game that adds new cards and ups the difficulty significantly. The lore is interesting, engaging, and compliments the gameplay well. Same can be said for the graphics which are coherent, memorable, and pleasing to the eye, and the audio, which is subtle and atmospheric. You really do feel like you're in a log cabin playing a card game of survival.

If you want to test out the gameplay before buying the game, I highly recommend going to Daniel Mullins' site and trying out Sacrifices Must Be Made. It was his Ludum Dare 43 entry that later became the foundation of Inscyption. The core mechanics are the exact same (card health/damage, sigils, sacrificing, etc.), so it will give you a good idea of whether or not you would enjoy Inscryption (which I'm sure you will ;)).\t\t\t\t\t
