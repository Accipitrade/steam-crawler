\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf 20 bucks for 20 hours of gameplay sounds good to you then take this game !!!

Spoiler free review:

Okay, so there are a couple things you need to know though... first, the game is not entirely what it seems, there is a story and it's hard to talk about it without spoiling it. Just know that the game is a card battler, you do quite a lot of deckbuilding and it is very possible you start to think "what is happening right now?". The game has beginning and has an ending.

Also, it's not entirely a rogue-lite so don't buy the game thinking this is what you will get with hundreds of hours of replayability, there is a reason the genre is not among the tags of the steam page... but it blends into it. Again, hard to explain without any spoil.

You will buy the game because you like card games and the story and the atmosphere is most likely what will make you come back.

The game is quite simple, you will, however, encounter some harder battles which can be won by working a bit more on your deck and defining a strategy. You will also encounter a few puzzles and a bunch of secrets which will extend the story.

The gameplay itself consist of exploring a board game and battling with cards which have simple mechanics.
What is really interesting is how the game will succeed to introduce new features every so often to keep you entertained.

The card battle in itself consist of a scale going from 0 to 5, each time you do damage or the opponent do damage the scame will add those numbers, the heaviest side (at the 5 mark) lose so the key is to know when to go full defense or when to go full offense to tip the scale in your favor.

Your decks consist of two parts, one is the meat of your game, the big boys which will establish your strategy and the other is a pile of cards you will use as ressources or as a last ressort to block an attack.
Each cards have a set cost (there a few different type of cost which I won't talk about to prevent spoilers... but the very first one you'll learn about in the tutorial is blood, sacrifice a creature to get one blood) and a set health and power.
Power will do damage, health won't replenish at the start of a turn so damage will add up until the card reach 0...
The card can also possess up to 4 symbols which will give them various powers and greatly modify the strategy.

The cards will always (except special symbol) attack in front of them, the board consist of lanes and creatures will attack/defend what is in front of them. (nothing in front of them = hit to the player which will add to the scale)
If the opponent plays a power 1, health 3 and your hand only consist of 3 power 1, health 1, do you want to spend your time destroying the 1/3 during the next few turns or do you want to set them on different lanes to attack the opponent's health? This is the kind of question you will keep asking yourself from start to finish.

In the end, I wasn't expecting at all what I got from the game but let me tell you, it was totally worth it.
There isn't a single regret to have, I loved it and I don't think you should look twice to buy it especially if you like the weird / slightly horror genre (not that the game is scary but it can be a bit gloomy maybe, understand, there is no jump scare or anything of the sort to give you a heart attack)\t\t\t\t\t
