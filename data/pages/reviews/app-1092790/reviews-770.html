\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscription, at first glance, looks like a traditional tabletop card game. Waking up in a wooden cabin, you are greeted with the card master, who offers freedom only after beating him. You start the game with 4 cards, gathering more cards the more you play. There are upgrades, bosses, trading, and lots of other elements from the tabletop genre.

The twist is that you are also playing an escape room, were you are searching for clues on how to escape, gathering secrets from talking cards, and solving hidden puzzles. Once you have finally gather all the clues and beaten the card master, you realize that you are in an ARG. You are playing through the perspective of a card enthusiast who found the game buried in a forest.

If the game is started from scratch, you start playing through a top down RPG, similar to undertake but with card game elements. You choose between 4 classes and is ordered to beat all the scrybs, who are 4 being who rule the game.

Once beaten, you start playing through a Sci-fi version of the game, but this time in a factory fighting a robot. Your coal is to help the robot reach unlimited power through playing.


My opinion of this game is that it combines different genres together, making the much more enjoyable than traditional card games. It also adds hidden lore, challenging modes, making the game replayable.

I recommend this game for any one who wants a card game that has more than just cards.\t\t\t\t\t
