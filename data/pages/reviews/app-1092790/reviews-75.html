\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is one of those games which is practically a must have for anybody. It is neither too hard to be played by a casual gamer, nor too easy not to be enjoyed by the hobbyist. In one phrase: it is pure, video game joy.

I am not one typically accustomed to card games. I have played Magic the Gathering, as well as Hearthstone, before, but have never quite clicked with them. Inscryption is a game which introduces its mechanics at a pace brisk enough that one does not get bored, yet fast enough that they game never feels truly stale. Some features of the game, such as totems or death cards in Act I, bring the game a freshness that only deepens upon every failure and subsequent retry.

I won't say anything as to the story of the game out of fear of spoiling any of it but it is a mystery worth working through in the game. It messes with the mind and toys with your perspective throughout.

I rarely think a game worthy of it, but Inscryption is truly a 10/10 masterpiece. With an incredible art style, affordable price, and content-packed gameplay, this is one of those game which will stand the test of time as "one of the greats."\t\t\t\t\t
