\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, so when I found out there was gonna be another game from the Pony Island guy I was stoked. I played the demo and was MORE stoked. I just finished the game. I don't think stoked is the right reaction?
IMPORTANT CONTEXT
Without spoiling anything, this game *will* change on you. The style in the demo and the store page is only a part of the game. If you don't want to play a game that deviates away into (arguably worse) different territory, I don't think I recommend. IF however you get invested in the story, or have played other games by this creator, you should definitely stick around to see how meta things get and how the story develops

PROS
-The style is awesome, even when things don't stay the same they're always self-consistent, so it's almost like playing different games entirely
-The mechanics in this game are great, a lot of deck and card games just use energy or points or something, but Inscryption doesn't follow that exactly and provides more interesting deck combat
-SOUND DESIGN IS TOP TIER LIKE DAMN IT'S SO GOOD
-Love love love the writing, characters (except one in particular I will not name) are very interesting and the story all unravels in an interesting way (it could have been better I will get to that)

CONS
-Pacing. This game feels very hurried around the end, a lot is thrown in at once, rather than taking the time to flesh out the story and the more minor characters. I finished this in 8 hours but honestly it could have been much faster. It's a shame, I want more of this world and its characters, especially with what you learn in the story later in the game
-The changing style (in my opinion) is not for the better. The stuff presented in "Act 1" so to speak is seriously one of the coolest gaming experiences I have had in my life. After that, the rest seems kind of tired. The story was the only thing keeping my going by the halfway mark.
-While there is no lack of variety in this game, I feel the variety weighed down the experience as a whole. Without time to develop more of the content, it seems kind of shallow once you realize the limitations of your actions.

OVERALL I enjoy this game. I think it's worth playing. I also think it's worth playing The Hex because the prior knowledge from that game enriches this one (I also think The Hex suffers similar problems as this game, but whatever lol). My biggest problem with the game is that I need more. I want more lore, more character development, more of the mystery of this strange game being revealed. There is SO MUCH that could have been done here, and I wish that more time was taken to flesh it out and develop it. I absolutely would have waited for more. If you are interested in light horror, ARGs, or just want some really neat stylistic gameplay, pick up Inscryption.\t\t\t\t\t
