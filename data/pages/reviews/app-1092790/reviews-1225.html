\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got all the achievements for this game and put almost an entire day's worth into it so i'm qualified to review it. Overall this card game is not what you would expect from your average card game like "Legends" or "Hearthstone" or even other card games. Where its only a card game back to back to back. This card game is more so a story/puzzle/card game. Which I will say has amazing puzzles which will make Sherlock Holmes jealous and has so much strategy and tactics that when you finish the game, even you can be qualified to be a military strategist. Aside from that note, the story is very interesting and does make you want to search every nook and cranny just to be closer to the truth. With only 3 Acts, every "Act" has its own "take/perspective/spin" on the game and it does offer a decent chance at replayability. However it does have 2 slight issues. Issue 1. Its glory. Being that the game does begin to lose its luster or shine as you go on and the majority of what players might find "fun" and/or "challenging" can be mostly found in Act 1 of the game. Which to be clear, is not to say the rest of the acts are boring (which they are not as they too have lots of fun and easter eggs), but the majority of the "drive" or "detail" and i would go as far as to say "effort" is found within the first part of the game. Now Issue 2. The story. the story does however not lose its luster, but keep in mind I spent 1 day beating it and 100% this entire game. Now if I were to soley focus on the story aspect of the game. You could in theory beat it in less than 4 hours....which if you ask anyone is short and sweet which I didnt mind as the game was good, but the game at the end. Especially the story, does leave you wanting for more. Now i wont say spoilers, but you really do wish that you could "fight more." Then again for a game that was worth 20$ I will say it does play like a 40$ game and is worth the bank for your buck.

Good

- Addicting
- Amazing Art Style
- Challenging Puzzles
- Amazing Critical Thinking
- Flexible Gameplay and Freedom
- Good Story
- Amazing Secrets
- Epic and Very cool boss fights
- Decent Replayability

Bad

- Can beat in less than 2 days
- Leaves you wanting more (not in a good way)
- The Game is much more fun and challenging in the beginning

Rating For Gameplay 9/10
Rating For Game Overall 7/10\t\t\t\t\t

Part 1 ; Although sometimes you get shafted early on, you will get something OP every other run. Seeing as this is the part of the game that Kaycee's Mod lengthens, I do not understand how people can sit and play for hours after beating the main game. This was my favorite part of the game and I was excited to see what more lie in store, but I was sorely disappointed

Part 2 ; Not much to say about this. I think I only changed 4 cards out of the starting deck, but you could probably beat it without changing the starting deck at all quite easily.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDear, Challenger.

I separate my Steam library into 3 categories: Multiplayer, Replayable, and Experience. This game, my dear friend, is an experience. Experiences are events that leave an imprint on you after finishing and this is one of those times where you sit back and enjoy the ride for what it is.

When I figured that the game was nearing to its end, I cried. At that point, I realized that we weren't playing to win. We were playing to enjoy each other's company. I suppose you could say that the real Inscryption were the friends we made along the way.

Take your time, Challenger. Breath it in. Experiences don't last forever and when the memory fades as life goes on, you will smile, grateful that such an event has come to pass. Cherish your friends and family. Get out of your room and offer to play a simple card game with them.
