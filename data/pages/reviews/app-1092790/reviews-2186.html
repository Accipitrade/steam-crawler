\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(Potential spoilers regarding not so much the story as the game's overall nature)
Kinda meh. Gameplay is a chore and practically a formality (you can go through the game with default decks with minimal adjustments and quite effortlessly, too) while the story, in the true mystery box fashion, builds up a lot of mystery and anticipation but fails to deliver much in the end. In my highly esteemed game journalist opinion, Inscryption as a game felt as if it emerged from a union of Undertale and DDLC, only that the child conceived came out a tad malformed, having inherited too much from its first parent (dreary redundant formality of gameplay which gets stale a lot faster than in Undertale) and too little from the second (game's duration could have been twice as short while still delivering the story about as effectively).
In the end, even though I struggle to recommend the game, I would still urge you to buy Inscryption if only to experience the story's *first act* which is, undeniably, incredibly immersive and captivating (even if gameplay starts to drag even that early on). Then, if the remainder of the game also makes you yawn all the time, just watch whatever's left on youtube or anywhere else where you'd have the luxury of fast-forwarding past all the tedious and largely meaningless moments. All in all, an alright game which felt like a massive chore to play for about 70% of the time it took to finish it but wasn't bad overall, just not good enough to recommend it decisively\t\t\t\t\t

From beginning to end, this game keeps on giving. The core mechanics of the card game are very solid, so much so that if a physical version existed, I would hop on that ♥♥♥♥ immediately. To top it all off, the story is the most enthralling one that I've experienced in a long time. My only complaint would be the semi-boring grindy bits that appear during the middle of the game, but pushing past those is far from a challenge, and after all that the game picks back up again. Overall, Inscryption is a game that just cannot be missed.
