\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first review I've ever written and I hate that it has to be negative.

This is not the game I paid for or was expecting from the demo and the trailers.
I'm not going to spoil the plot, I've worked hard to put up my complaints without going into plot specific detail.
Honestly, I feel that they only showed you what they did in the demo and trailers to make it really hard for people to dunk on this game without giving spoiler reviews. Thus keeping their Steam reviews mostly positive, because the first part of the game is excellent, but once it goes past the trailer expectations, it goes downhill rapidly.
This way, reviewers feel they can only comment on the First Act without spoiling the game for the community and so can only keep to the positive parts that were advertised.
I think that's pretty underhanded, if I'm honest.

If, like me, you don't play card games that much, but liked the mechanics and got the feel from the demo that it was going to be an ARG where you had to go down the route of 4th wall breaking sacrifices for your deck of disturbing animals, you are going to be massively disappointed.
If you do play card games and were looking forward to a deep dive into deck building and 4th wall breaking "how far are you willing to go for the perfect deck" ARG plot, you are going to be massively disappointed.

Don't get me wrong the First Act is amazing. Exactly what was promised and a lot of fun.
The ARG elements are hinted at and look intriguing.
Then the First Act ends, those ARG pieces are thrown out for another set that aren't half as interesting and the game takes a nose dive.

EDIT: I played through this part again and deliberately ignored all the things that would lead to Act Two, because I was curious. It just sends you back to the beginning, there is no alternate ending. I get the feeling that there is only one ending to this game and you are railroaded to do what the dev wants.
All feeling of choice is a lie.
In fact it got so exhausting hearing Stoat trying to get me to do the plot that I kept sacrificing him for extra bones. They really hit you over the head, even giving you visions of the safe if you die enough times without doing what they want.
It also struck me how much harder this Act is compared to the other two. In fact, one might play the First Act for long enough that you can't get a Steam refund once you hit Act Two and realise the game isn't what you were expecting and stop playing out of disappointment.
Pretty scummy if you ask me, even if the dev didn't do it on purpose.
Especially since the next Acts are not in the Demo and Act Two is literally shown in a single second flash at the end of the trailer.

The Second Act had me mostly going "what the heck?" and not in a good way. There were three new card decks, one good, two mediocre. But you could choose which one you wanted to play with, including the original deck, or you could mix and match. So I played through it, not enjoying it as much, but waiting for the pay-off.

EDIT: I realise that this isn't in the comments enough.
The Second Act is not atmospheric like the First Act. You are thrown straight into a 8-bit game that looks like it made in modern RPG maker. I think it's supposed to be "oh look, this is the original game, before the spooky stuff happened" but if this is the case, then it is not of that era and was definitely made with a modern engine. It doesn't have that cute retro feel that a lot of 8-bit devs strive for. In fact, going back into it, knowing what to expect, it is very hab-dash and over simplistic for what it is trying to achieve.

The Third Act pay-off was being forced to basically play through the Second Act again with a new layout. However, you could only play with one deck, the most boring and uninspired of the three new ones, so the Third Act was pretty tedious. This was compounded by the fact that the NPC you play against was just as boring and annoying. The other characters had so much potential, - why was I forced to play against the moaning one that had no personality at all? I didn't care about them and I resented that I had kinda cared about them in the First Act, I really felt tricked. The bosses introduced in this Act were just gimmicky and did stuff which completely messed with the flow of the game, jarring me out of the experience constantly until I was just muttering "will you get on with it" or "no, not again. Can I just play the card game, please?".
I think they were supposed to unnerve you..? But they just fell flat and used some of the gimmicks from Pony Island, but not as well, and for far longer than was enjoyable.

EDIT: Again, because it isn't mentioned enough.
Two of the boss gimmicks pretend to take personal data from your computer.
One asks you to sign a contract granting access to your computer. Then it gives you a yes/no tick box, where you cannot choose no - the yes icon swaps around. Then during the fight it pretends to make you take files directly from your computer by reading your harddrives. It even tells you it will delete one that you choose after making it into a card. I can't stress enough that this is a gimmick and the game CAN'T do what it threatens. But if you aren't used to this sort of gaming trick, it might seem alarming.
The second boss takes your steam friends and makes cards out of them to attack you. It uses the real profile pictures and names of your friends. I've watched at least one YouTuber who played this and was mortified when their friends list was broadcast to their entire subscriber base. There was no warning beforehand.
Again not going into plot specifics, but people might like the heads up.

The finale was frustrating as it introduced the other two card games as they should have been played, but you only get to enjoy them for a few minutes before the plot removes all stakes for playing them.
Oh, and the ARG is pretty uninspired stuff and the Big Bad Reason you're playing this sentient card game is a simple by the numbers plot. I was waiting and waiting for the twist, but it never came.

TL;DR: What looked like a fun ARG horror game is let down by the writer wanting to be edgy and unexpected in the worst ways possible. The plot takes a nose dive after the First Act and just gets more tedious as the game progresses.
The finale is frustrating as it shows you what this game could have been, but never got the chance.

EDIT: Final thoughts.
I honestly feel that I was tricked out of my money.
Whether the dev meant for this game to be a bait and switch, that is what it became.
Anyone who sees this on the front page of Steam is not going to know who this dev is or their game history. The people who did know pre-ordered, like I did.
Hell, I played and loved Pony Island. It was silly and the plot was a bit uninspired, but it never lied to me as to what kind of game it was.
Inscryption lied to me, plain and simple.
I won't be buying from this dev again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can only give this game the biggest recommendation possible.

I have not felt this way about a game in a long time - the kind of enjoyment and intrigue I get is the kind that only happens when you’re playing something made by mad geniuses.

Many lesser games would have only been the card game element... but this thing is so much more beyond. The shifts that occur can be downright shocking.

In short: don’t look for spoilers. I didn’t and I’m so thankful.

It’s a “collectible card game battler” wrapped in an escape game wrapped in a fever dream. The card game is difficult at first but quickly becomes unbalanced as you learn to manipulate it.

And just when you think you’ve got it... you realize you’re the one who’s been manipulated this whole time.

It’s fantastic stuff. Well worth the price even though I doubt it will have much replay ability once you’ve completed it. The experience is worth it alone.\t\t\t\t\t
