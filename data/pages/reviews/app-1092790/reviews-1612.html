\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is something truly special. At its core it is a card game with mechanics that change as the story progresses. Even if you're not a fan of card games I'd still ask that you check out the demo. I have no experience with games like Magic: The Gathering and my enjoyment of them is usually mitigated to light games of poker, but Inscryption is something I cannot put down.

If the horror-elements of the game put you off I will say it is mostly atmospheric with the exception of occasional self mutilation. Check for triggers but overall it's not too heavy in that aspect.

If you intend on ever playing or watching a let's play, look up as little as possible. I am usually neutral on spoilers but this is a game that is best to go in as blind as possible. Have fun getting strangled for being bad at cards~!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know what you think when you see this steam page. A quirky card game thats kind of spooky and seems kind of vague about what it is. Some weird video clips? A talking jar of goo? What is this??

This game is hands down one of my favorite games I've ever played. I thought it was a card game with a small story to unlock. It's so much more. This game is going to keep you guessing around every corner about what is actually is and what's coming next.

It's slightly (VERY lightly) a deck building game, slightly a rogue-like where you keep trying to win and start over.

It's got amazing stylistic grahpics, a true original idea for a game that's fun and interesting, and it made me so excited to start the entire game over the second I finished it. I cannot reccomend this more if you're on the fence about it.

DONT LOOK UP ANYTHING, just give it a try.

Oh, and good luck, Luke. You've been a good friend to me.\t\t\t\t\t
