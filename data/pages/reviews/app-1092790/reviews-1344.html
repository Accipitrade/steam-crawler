\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen people look for games to play, they generally are looking for unique style, stimulating gameplay and a story that enraptures you as you continue to play it, making you continuously crave more.

Inscryption is one of those games that achieves all of those to a stunning degree. Truly a game best played through completely blind, below are my thoughts on the aspects of the game with no actual spoilers.

The Gameplay: Engaging and addictive. There are no better words to describe the actual gameplay itself. Inscryption truly has a way of roping you in with a seamless "practice round" at the start, allowing you to clearly understand how the game is played. As you continue to explore, you come across different checkpoints, each one providing unique effects to enhance your experience as you build your deck to become stronger. The game is built on the theme of sacrifice, and when first beginning to play the game, the struggle of knowing what to sacrifice and the risk of doing is beautifully crafted. I cannot stress enough how enjoyable this game was to play for me, and I have difficulty believing others will feel differently. The way the card game itself is crafted is very well balanced, with certain exploits new players can discover through their own exploration that are incredibly fun to find. While the card game aspect does change slightly as you progress, the overall theme of sacrifice is never lost, and learning to use it to your advantage is definitely one of the most satisfying parts of the game.

The Story: Mysterious, dark, and deep. Inscryption has one of the most incredible stories I've ever seen. Without giving away too much detail, the story of the in-game characters revolves around a balance and unending desire for power and control. This is not just a simple card game, there is so much more beneath it. As the player, you are in the dark for the majority of the game, only really receiving clues about the lore of the game through subtle hints via exploration and progression through the game, with a finale that truly shakes you to the core. Inscryption also comes with an ARG (Alternate Reality Game) that is even deeper and darker than you could imagine.

The Graphics: Unique and incredible. The overall tone and mood is truly visually appealing for the game you are playing, from the lighting to the usage of specific colours used to help create a realistic experience for the player that makes them feel like they truly are a part of the world they find themselves in.

The Soundtrack: Phenomenal. A wide range of gentle background music that you almost don't notice, but upon hearing it elsewhere, you are thrown back into memories of playing the game. The main track from the Deathcard Cabin (named Deathcard Cabin) is one of the most simple but elegant tracks for a strategy game that I've probably ever heard, able to be subtle enough to be almost unnoticeable; calming in a sense, with occasional strong strums to bring you back into focus. Eerie, dark, and uncanny, but not enough to be frightening for the most part. The soundtrack is superb, and definitely worth giving a thoughtful ear to.

Overall, Inscryption is a true masterpiece in all aspects. I very much look forward to seeing how Daniel Mullins (Developer) continues to build upon the game, whether with actual physical cards themselves or expansions of the gameplay. I cannot recommend this game enough to anybody, as it has all the aspects of what an amazing game is. Serious kudos to the entirety of the development team.\t\t\t\t\t
