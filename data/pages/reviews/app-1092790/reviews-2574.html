"Inscryption" is a game where a simple description or a simple demo cannot do it justice. This is a game made by the mind behind "Pony Island" so that should tell you something. If you've never heard of "Pony Island" then let me lay out for you that there's a ton more to this game than a simple card game with dark and broody graphics. The screenshots and demo for this game give you a feel for the "ACT 1" of the game. Act 2, the gameplay and presentation will diverge greatly from the way act 1 plays and act 3 will return to a game style more in line with act 1. But this game will be a acid trip of wtf for all players. This game will mess with your head and then some. Gameplay changes greatly as you progress through the game. Mechanically AND in graphic styling.

So word of warning: If you buy this game, fall in love with the card game in the beginning, beat it and hit act 2 and are dissappointed, you've no one to blame but yourself. Either stop playing, or wipe the saves to start over, or man up and play through act 2 to get to act 3 and return to the playstyle of act 1. Don't cry about false advertising. False advertising is stuff like saying the game is multiplayer when it is not or saying that special bonuses will be given out when they are not. Don't whine about how you were misled. If you've played or watched a playthrough of "Pony Island", you should have some idea of what you're getting into.

A large number of negative reviews are people that impulse purchased the game rather than investigated it before dropping money on it.\t\t\t\t\t

THE BEST INDIE GAME OF 2021
I've immediately come to write this review after finishing the game.

In an age of microtransaction, outrageous cosmetics, and the interference of real-world problems and agendas in triple-A games, INSCRYPTION shows once again that true gaming quality can be achieved and proven even more from indie studios.

Review
The game at its core is a card game so for those looking for a card game experience, I assure you that INSCRYPTION offers a very sublime experience of that and more. This also includes brain-teasing puzzles which will push your logic and sense of curiosity through the roof.

The story is engaging and the villain interesting but remember that everything is not always what it seems and surely there's more than meets the eye.

Overall, I thoroughly recommend INSCRYPTION and I truly believe that the game can be a heavy contender for the Indie Game of the year (2021).
