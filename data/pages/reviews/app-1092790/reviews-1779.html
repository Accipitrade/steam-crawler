\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption was an amazing game, from start to finish. How to put it without spoiling it...? Well, I'll try my best. This was the only deckbuilding game I was really good at, having played a little Magic and even less Pokemon, but I feel like I might be able to build a decent deck for those games now, should I ever want to. This would be a great introduction to the people who do want to try deckbuilding out, even if sometimes it comes down to RNG, for pulling the right cards. It's not really scary but it is light on horror, just enough to keep one interested. The puzzles are a little difficult at times. I feel like the puzzles are very Metroid-y, with little explanation; the only way you can find them is...to find them. You have to explore nigh infinite possibilities to get maybe one hint for what to do next, which could just lead to another hint. I was first introduced to this game by watching Jacksepticeye and Markiplier play it, and I was actually intrigued when I saw it. This game was well worth the money spent, and I saw that the dev may or may not be making real life Inscryption cards. If you see this, know that I will definitely buy those cards, because I really like the concept of this game. Thanks to the dev, and the entire team of Inscryption for making such an amazing game.\t\t\t\t\t
