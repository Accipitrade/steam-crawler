\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn absolutely brilliant game, Inscryption is a horror deckbuilding roguelike puzzle room metagame that hits all the right buttons, even when you don't. It's difficult to review a game that is more rewarding the more blind you are to it, so without spoiling too much I definitely recommend this game if you like the core mechanics roguelike deckbuilding, as that is the main constant gameplay--but the other aspects of the game are not new modes by any means, but follow a story that changes the game drastically and culminates to a creepy and satisfying finale.

The "horror" of the game is never a jump-out-and-scare-you tactic--just at least mysteriously and at times very creepy. Puzzle room parts of the game are in the minority, but very well done. What makes the game stand out hard is a very fun card metagame--meaning elements outside of the game are often used and referenced in creative ways.

If Inscryption was just a roguelike deckbuilder, it would still be great--but the steps it takes further makes it one of the best games of 2021 easily.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPart of this game is a fun deck builder with a thick, creepy atmosphere, a great art style, and a fun gameplay loop. Unfortunately, the rest of the game is a 2d RPG, and then an exploration filled Soulslike with the deckbuilding mechanics becoming gradually less important and less fun. Overall, the game is a creepypasta. Ever read a lost episode/game creepypasta and went 'Oh man, I wanna play that!'? This is that. I enjoyed my time with this game, but by the end, the bait and switch and the feeling that the rest of the game was rushed really left me cold on it, and I can't recommend it in the long run.\t\t\t\t\t
