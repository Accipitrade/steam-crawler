\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is more than just a card game it's a game full of puzzles and mysteries, with an entertaining storyline. When you first start a new game, you expect some sort of casual deck-building game but, as you progress you realize it's not just that. Starting out you are introduced to a card game that is quite easy to learn, but the more you progress the more you realize it's not just a simple card game. You are able to get up from the desk and explore the small room you are in that is filled with puzzles and mysteries, just like an escape room. The room you are in is act 1 of the game. In act 2 the game becomes a pixilated game that is more focused on deck building with short puzzles. This might be off-putting to those who were enjoying act 1 of the game and wanted more from it, but don't worry if you like act 2 or not when you finish it you proceed into act 3 which is the final act of the game and it reverts back to being like act 1, but with a different set of mechanics, cards and opponent. There are two stories to the games, one inside of the game and one outside where you watch a guy named Luckycarder. I enjoyed both these stories, none of the story elements felt misplaced and worked really well. Inscryption is not just a simple card game, but a game full of mystery and puzzles that are fun, definitely would recommend this game.

One of the most interesting and impactful experiences I've ever had in a video game. The game tells a story not only with character interactions and narrative, but also through the gameplay itself. If you're somebody who wants a new Slay the Spire or is only interested in the gameplay, there is still fun to be found here but you will probably find the experience lacking. However, if you are able to connect to the characters and understand how deeply every aspect of the game is tied together, you will likely agree that it's something special. It has a cohesiveness and attention to detail that I believe puts it among some of the best and most artistic games that have come out in the past decade. Some aspects of the narrative are definitely lacking, but there's too much good to ignore. Plus, you can break the hell out of the card game, which is great fun. Overall, an incredible game that leaves me anxiously awaiting whatever Mullins does next.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would warn future buyers that the game changes drastically after the first act and becomes something completely different.

EDIT: As of yesterday, the dev announced a free DLC mod mode that will much better match what people have wanted. I do hope the devs add the other characters in as well and expand on it, but time will tell. In the meantime, huge step forward and very nice of the dev to listen to everyone!

Some like that change and some do not. I heavily disliked the changes the game took in tone and in gameplay, but the first act made up for it in my eyes. I'll replay that portion of the game once I slog through the rest purely for completion's sake.

The first act was fantastic, but the rest was...less so. I bought it solely for the first portion of the game as I was very, very on the fence about everything that followed after. I always check games prior to purchasing them because of games like this that advertise one VERY small portion of the game then change radically later on. I almost didn't buy it because of these changes, but the first act is so fun that I really wanted to try it out.

It would have been much better if it kept the dark tone from the past demo (the whole "let's eat" one, not the latest one) and expanded on the portion of the game that is advertised. The setting, the gameplay, the story...I enjoyed it up until that first part changed into something completely different. Act 3 is similar but not nearly as charming (yeah yeah because x character is y thing but I don't care, it was incredibly boring). The taste of what could have been near the ending is even worse...had this game kept the style of the start and used the other characters in that manner, it would have been a perfect game in my eyes.

The story degraded heavily...there are parts I enjoyed but most of it felt forced. The meta stuff was...not great. It wasn't appreciated when we had all of Leshy's portion to look forward to instead. The introduction of the other characters and the situation they were in was alright, it could have worked, but the setting and tone of Leshy's part was just too perfect. I felt terribly disappointed when it went suddenly to pixel rpg mode and cut most of the gameplay stuff I enjoyed (customizing your creatures was incredibly fun, the sigils were cool, etc.) and completely changed the tone of the game. I love pixel games, I play tons of them and grew up on Pokemon, but it was disappointing after the lovely tone the game had set for us prior.

Act 3 is a chore and I despise it. I don't care if it's a robot and supposed to be "dull", everything felt a huge step down from Leshy's portion.

The ending was great, I genuinely cared for Leshy and to an extent the others as well, but seeing what could have been left a bittersweet taste in my mouth. Seeing that we could have played the game in a similar style to act 1 with all the other scribes...yeah. I wish we could have had that game. I see reviews mention this each and every time so I'm glad it wasn't just me. Maybe a DLC or something will introduce it, but it really should have been the core game.

Overall I have enjoyed the game if only for the first portion. Some said you can download the demo and just play that to enjoy it all and I would agree to some extent. You would miss some bosses I think, however, and those two are very fun as well. I bought the game solely for act 1 and have enjoyed it for act 1. I'll replay that portion when I finally get through the rest...but it's been a chore.

A lot of people enjoyed the twists and turns the game took, I unfortunately just wasn't one of them. Even so, I can't leave a neutral review and I would say this game IS worth getting if only for the first part of it. If they ever add DLC to expand on act 1, I would definitely go for that. I loved it so much, I never write reviews this long, it just hurt so much to see something so beautiful end up not staying beautiful.\t\t\t\t\t
