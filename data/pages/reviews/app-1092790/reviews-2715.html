Sacrifices Must Be Made.


What a gem.

I can recommend the game based on the atmosphere alone: picture Blair Witch Project meets <insert trading card game here>. Also the Witch is a nerdy Dungeon Master.

Pros:
- Easy to learn, hard to master asymetric cardgame. Very entertaining gameplay loop.
- Quite innovative deckbuilding. You build your deck from limited random cards. Cards might use different currencies, might benefit from certain card types, etc. You can empower your cards to create easily overpowered ones, or downright sacrifice one card to buff another. Quite a lot of combinations to choose from.
- Cards with personalities! Will you get attached to a card that you might sacrifice later? Welp, at least don't get too attached to your teeth...
- Leave the table and explore the claustrophobic room you play in. Interact with trinkets under the eye of your gnarly companion to find secrets.
- Bathe in the gloomy atmosphere! The sound of tooths hitting the scale, measuring remaining life; cards being drawn, pawn moving across the board - everything is just right and sets the tone for the game. The graphics are also reponsible for this: even something simplistic as cards portraits have something uncanny about them (i.e. the grizzly bear). Not to mention all the trinkets and baubles just barely visible in the gloomy room.
- I haven't stumbled upon any bugs as of yet. It is nice to be able to play a bugless game for a change.
- Some encounters seem to punish you from rushing the opponent. Some might find it simply unfair, though I think it has been done in a correct way, both thematically and mechanically.

Cons:
- Can't think of any at the moment, honestly.



Sacrifices Must Be Made.
I will sacrifice countless hours on this title for sure.
