\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend the game as far as the card game aspects go. I was interested in the story until Act II, then I completely lost interest when it started acting like a creepypasta. I LOVE narrative-heavy games, but I HATE fiction that only works on the basis you believe it's really happening. I can't suspend disbelief if I'm also supposed to believe real people were involved. I was never really given a reason to care about Luke, so I didn't care when he died - why would I? The game makes it clear that someone other than you is playing the game pretty early on, but the story ends up so vague that the narrative artistry clearly took precedence. You really have to work for answers, and unfortunately I'm not invested enough to do that, so it means having an unfulfilling experience. I think the ambiance in Act 1 was beautifully done, and I came to resent Leshy, but at the end of the game the tone felt sympathetic to him... Two points in time with no line connecting them.

The short of it: I think the game has a strong first half and falls off. I am personally disappointed by this game, as I feel it fails to deliver narratively.\t\t\t\t\t
