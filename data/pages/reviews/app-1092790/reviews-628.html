\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm really not sure how this game has such stellar reviews. The AI literally, blatantly cheats. It does not have to follow any of the rules of the game that you learn. It's just tossing out powerful cards left and right, playing multiples of them in a round with zero resource cost while you're skipping turns trying to save resources to play a single card. That, or sacrificing half of what you have on the board to get a card out that will probably be annihilated the second the AI decides to just line the table with enough damage to completely end the round in a single turn.

There are scripted events where that happens on purpose, which are annoying enough. I already think that's lazy game design. But the AI does it just in normal play. You'll get handed the absolute worst cards in your deck, never see any of the good cards that you have, and just get screwed by RNG over and over again, having to restart all the way from the beginning each time you get crushed by the AI that has zero restrictions on it because every game on steam just HAS to be a rogue-like/lite.

And the room puzzles are the most basic point and click stuff imaginable. This ain't Myst. It's a glorified flash game with a laughably unbalanced card game stapled onto it. I thought some of them were harder at first, but after basically giving up on the cards, I skimmed through a guide and all the puzzles I thought I couldn't figure out were just things I wasn't allowed to open until a certain point in the progression. That I'll never reach. Because you have to beat an AI that cheats in order to get that far.

I usually run the table on card games like this. From Gwent all the way back to Tetra Master, I've had zero issues. The decks and odds are literally stacked against you, and I don't get how that's supposed to be fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is by far the best card game that I have ever played. It is very fun, challenging and the game looks very good. If you are looking for a good strategy game, buy inscryption. The soundrack of the game also amazing.

I also LOVE Kaycee's mod, it's so fun and replayable, the challenges are a great way to make the game harder and I also like the new cards and starter decks that the game introduces to you when you complete a run.

The ARG is also really cool and we can once again realize how good Daniel Mullins is at telling a story.

On a scale of one to ten, this game gets a 10. No, 10 isn't enough. A game like this deserves more than just a 10. This game is more than perfection, I'll probably never play a card game like this ever again. Because of all of this, I'm giving this game a 15 out of ten.\t\t\t\t\t
