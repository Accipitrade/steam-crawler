\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good:
- Immersive storyline with unpredictable twists, very fun deck building rogue-like experience as well as tons of weird and unsettling stuff because this is a Daniel Mullins game
- Extra mode that further builds on the best gameplay experience from the storyline
- Great characters, especially the lonely mage
- Tons of puzzles, secrets and <REDACTED>

The bad:
- Difficulty spike in middle section that trips some people up, but not really a big deal in my opinion
- There is no sequel yet. I need STIMULATION, Daniel!


In conclusion, this is a really great game and definitely worth your money and time. I recommend buying all of Daniel Mullins games in a bundle and going down one of the weirdest rabbit holes in gaming. The only critique that I really have, is that I wish that the player had more influence over the story in form of branching dialog and multiple endings, but that isn't a fault per se.
All in all, Inscryption is a wonderful game, a true passion project and a must play if you're a TCG nerd such as me.\t\t\t\t\t
