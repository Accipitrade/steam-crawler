Overview
Inscription is a well-made Horror game genre with many well-made Card Game mechanics in it. The horror genre is not anything such as Jump-Scare type. Far from it. It is an Eerie game experience with many horror elements and psychological horror elements in the game. It is up to you on how far you are willing to dive deep into this ocean of horror. It is a story game with a very pleasant Card Game experience. Highly recommended to try the demo first. However, be warned that the game in of itself lacks replayability.

There isn't much of a story. Most of the story elements to this is figuring out "who" is playing the game. There are lore in the games that you will have to figure out in order to understand the story. However, that requires more deep dive rather than a story being laid out to tell you what it is about. Therefore, lacks the involvement for most people, unless you, the player yourself, decides to jump into it.

Gameplay
☐ Journalist
☐ Below-Average
☐ Gamer
☒ Above-Average
☐ Dark Souls

The game is divided by 3 "Acts" and in each Act, you will face different challenges in the Card Game aspect. You will have to steel yourself and accept the challenge. You have no choice. In Act 1, you are presented in a different style Card Game rather than in Act 2. Act 1 is more of a 'Dungeons & Dragon' vibe Card Game where you have to choose your journey to reach the destination while in Act 2, you play the "Original" game of what it is supposed to be. Find the secrets if you wish. They are all in there. In Act 3, it is similar to Act 1 but you will have to find out for yourself, should you ever play.

There are no unique sounds but how they used the normal sounds that you'd hear in everyday lives, is what makes up for it. The music is not much but provide enough of the eerie atmosphere that you need. All of it is to enhance the horror aspect in most cases. Music are entirely divided in acts, thus it will disrupt your flow but nonetheless provide the eerie experience. If that is what the game is trying to achieve, then it succeeded in my opinion. However, in terms of gameplay music, it isn't anything amazing yet pleasant.

☐ Above-Average

Each Act provides a different type of Replayability. It is hard to explain without diving into spoilers, so I will not do that. Instead, the replayability is best at Act 1 rather than the rest. I would say that it is a 1 time experience, due to the lack of it. However, it does not stop you from replaying the same Act over and over again, should you ever enjoy them. I hope you do.

☒ Not Recommended

As much as I enjoy the game, I do think the price of the game does not justify the replayability value to the game. It is a one time experience and it is up to you should you enjoy the game. The Card Game mechanic is well made and it is made for you to succeed. Therefore lacks any challenge at times for you to face. However, the game does not shy itself from "challenges" if certain conditions are met during your playthrough, as the game are very well made in order to stop you, should you made a move that the game does not want you to.\t\t\t\t\t
