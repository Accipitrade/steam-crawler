Over the years my focus went more and more towards multiplayer games since anything else seemed to be repetetive to an unbearable extent.

Well then along came this game.

It confronts you as the player with your own expecations to video games. Expectations that have been created by putting in countless hours into the monotonous content that so called AAA-studios feed to the communtiy.
This experience alone was enlightening and refreshing alike, whilest being allowed to whitness true creativity.

When I finally watched those end-credits it reminded me of beating Super Mario World for the first time back in 1992.
I just sat there, feeling weirdly touched by what I just went through waiting for another curveball.
Of course my expectations were too standard, again.

Inscryption is the most unusual and fun game I have experienced in many many years. It's a game that oozes with so much energy and dedication, something that I didn't think games together with the reasons behind their developement had still in it.

Thanks and kudos go to its creators for putting a game out there that feels like true rebellion.

Oh wait, there is someone at my door.
