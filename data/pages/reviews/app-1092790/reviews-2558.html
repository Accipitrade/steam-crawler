\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start by saying that I absolutely despise card type games; I would be the last person that would ever be found dwindling in the likes of Magick the Gathering, Yu-gi-oh, Pokemon, but something about this game caught my attention.


I saw a glimpse of this game on social media and I decided to look it up, the grimdark-esque theme of the clip that I had seen was enough to hook me, along with the design of the cards, the sound design, and the way the game played.

Installed the demo on Friday 10/15/2021, I played the demo all weekend and decided that I needed to pre-order this game to play it on its Tuesday 10/19/2021 release date, and sure enough, I played it the Tuesday night that it came out til just about 1AM despite having a full time day-job just because I was so pulled into this game.

I originally thought that playing against Leshy was the entirety of the game, but....boy was I entirely wrong.

The direction that this card game takes, and the changes that it makes throughout the story was absolutely mind-blowing for me, and I can't express this enough. Every single minute I put into finally beating this game was worth every penny that I spent because I enjoyed every bit of the story and every bit of the gameplay and never once did I get bored or feel as if the game was dragging on.

By far, this single game by itself, was one of the best games I have played in a very long time.

This game alone, makes me wish that there was a physical version of it, so that I could nerd out and force me friends to learn and play it against me in a real scenario.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gameplay is extremely entertaining. For me, it felt like a simpler version of hearthstone, but with enough depth to make you still think pretty hard about how to build your deck and sequence your actions. The difficultly level was just right. As for the story, I did not follow it close enough to be able to do any deep theorization, but what was there on surface level was very enjoyable and compelled me to keep playing. The first person aspect of the gameplay and the fact that the narration is directed towards you made it really feel like you were there sitting in that cabin playing cards with a shadow man. If I were to compare this aspect to another game, Welcome to the Game would be the best contender. In addition, the atmosphere and art were wonderfully done and gave a creepy vibe to the game.

While this game rightfully deserves my praise and other's, I do have 2 complaints.
1: it is very short. I finished the game right below the 8 hour mark. To be honest, the longest part would probably be the beginning where you have to restart your run every time you die. Everything else after that goes pretty quickly. But even this longer part only took me like 4-5 runs before I was able to progress to the next segment.
2: As a card battler rogue-like esque game, there is practically no replayability or incentive to start over or keep playing after you beat the game. Pretty much all you're able to do is go back to earlier segments of the story and replay them. There's no harder difficulty or challenge run or anything like that. With a combat system so fun to play with, it's just kind of a shame that there's no continuation of the gameplay. I can only hope that maybe there will eventually be a patch or something that will make the game more replayable.

Overall, I love this game. It's a quick little thing that you could dump from 2 days to a week into to finish, it's not too expensive, and most importantly, it's very captivating with both the gameplay and the atmosphere/story. For any enjoyers of card battlers and new players, I would recommend giving Inscryption a chance.\t\t\t\t\t
