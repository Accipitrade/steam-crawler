\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is not what you think it is.

Do not buy this expecting a roguelike horror-themed card game. It is a lot more story-based than the trailers make it seem. A *lot* more story-based. What you are paying for is an ARG, not a rogue-like. Not even a particularly good ARG.

I beat the game. It's still an overall good game (7/10 or so), but I cannot give a good review. It's simply not what I paid for and not what was advertised (vague hints do not count, they could have been interpreted as literally anything else).

Maybe if they make an Endless Mode my opinion will change. Until then I cannot recommend this.

(Also of note: certain fans of the dev will defend every decision made in the promotion of this game. Calling negative reviews whiners. Clown awards, etc. Really mature stuff. To those fans I say this: I played Pony Island, I liked Pony Island. This game is not Pony Island. This game is not nearly as good as Pony Island.)

(1/30/2022) - With the introduction of Kaycee's Mod, this game now has an "Endless Mode". The main game is still kind of not great and the ARG is more funny than it is compelling (I assume this was unintended). Kaycee's Mod is still lacking, and I feel like that game mode needs another update to really be a good roguelike.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a special game.

I don't just mean a really good game, I mean a special game. The kind that only shows up every couple of years. The kind that you enjoy with your entire heart. The kind that when it's over, you desperately want more. You want the gameplay to go on for miles in every direction, for hundreds of hours of playtime until you've played it so much that you're sick of it. It's ridiculously immersive, and while the gameplay itself never gets ridiculously complex, the strategies you can form and the constant changeups the game throws at you ensures that it never feels stale.

As for the first 'twist', I agree with others that it would've been fine if it had just been that cabin for the entire game, but that being said what did happen is still fantastic. The game is like five different versions of itself stacked on top of each other, all the while keeping you guessing as to what comes next. I REALLY hope we get a DLC for the other two, but even if we don't what we have is still enough.\t\t\t\t\t
