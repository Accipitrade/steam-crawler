\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMisleading marketing is its biggest let down. Wish I could have refunded it honestly. Act 1 is enjoyable and full of fun surprises. Act 2 is filler and Act 3 was so boring and frustratingly tedious that I was glad when it was all over.

I'm aware of the developer and their "twists" but for a game that claims to be story rich, I found the actual story pretty bland. Act 1 built up such an intrigue with its play style, atmosphere and music. That puzzles are incorporated into each death is such a great way to encourage replayability and soothes any frustration of having to start over from the beginning. Sadly, all of that is completely lost from Act 2 onwards and by that point, you've already committed the hours into it to prevent a refund.

The finale showcases an impressive demo of possible card game designs which mostly left me feeling frustrated that I got stuck with *this* game and not *those* ones. Can't imagine wanting to wade through Act 2/3 again but I would have easily enjoyed an endless Act 1 mode.\t\t\t\t\t
