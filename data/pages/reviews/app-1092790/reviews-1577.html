\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm so torn on whether to recommend this game or not. It does some things exceptionally well, but some things were done incredibly bad. As a contrast to the public opinion, I will hit "no" on whether I recommend it or not.

I was excited for this game, looking forward to it ever since I heard it was coming as a fully fleshed out version of "Sacrifices Must Be Made". I pre-ordered it in a sense - I don't ever do that, for the record. It goes without saying that I loved "Sacrifices Must Be Made", despite the incredibly short experience it ended up being.

Even without completing the first act though, this game commits a cardinal sin in game design. I don't mind just saying it flatly because I feel like someone who is looking to buy this game should know: this game extends play time artificially by putting you against a near impossible to beat deck if you haven't done what it wants in the order it wants. I can say fully that I do not understand what whoever was in charge was thinking when they greenlit this. You do not have a boss in a competitive game of any kind kill the player because they haven't done the proper things to advance the story. This is especially egregious considering this is a strategy game. The game will do this to most players multiple times. That is unacceptable.

Moving on, the surprises the game has in store for you are usually fun, and the game itself is fun and well structured. All you can ask for a card game, really. When I enjoyed the game, it was marveling at the unique ways it presents things it has already taught me in a new way, or messing with me in fun ways that I won't spoil. The story is disjointed, and poorly implemented, though. Especially at the ending, where character motivations do not make sense in comparison to what is going on in the game. I don't even like the way it's told. Spoiler warning: The jump from first person perspective to following Luke Carder ruins all immersion in the game, and simultaneously ruins any empathy or care I'd have for Luke.

That being said, I do like the game. I enjoyed the time I had with it. I love its aesthetics and can see myself enjoying collecting the cards and playing in person with friends. Spoiler warning: I find myself really wanting to actually get to play against Magnificus, with the Yu-Gi-Oh duelist aesthetic. It is just unfortunate that I'm one of those kinds that the story of the game is just as important as the game itself. This is what I will sum this review up with: Are you looking for a fun, stable, and unique card game? Then pick this up. Are you someone who doesn't like card games but have interest in this game for its story elements? Then I'd recommend you leave this one alone.\t\t\t\t\t
