\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is hard to describe. If you enjoy deck-building games with simple mechanics but lots of variation and lots of curve balls being throw at you, you will enjoy this game. The atmosphere is dark, with lots of messed up stuff going on around you, and there is a fun story too, so there is lots to keep you playing. The less you know the better, so if that description appeals to you, then get it.

If you are thinking of not getting it because that description is not up your alley, then let me give you some MILD SPOILERS to describe why this game isn't just good, its great.

The game is will constantly surprising you. No really. As soon as you think you have figured it out, it pulls the rug out from under you. I cannot adequately describe how meta the game is and how much the game changes as you as play. Not into card battlers? While it is big part of the game, the real draw is the story, mystery angle you find several hours in. The actual game mechanics are easy to master, its just a matter of thinking and learning as things are given to you, so even if this description is not normally the sort of you game you play, power through it because its not the real core of the game and it is actually pretty easy to get laughably good at this game.

Bottom line, don't research this game, don't spoil anymore than what I have written here, just get it and let it take you for a ride.
