This is one of those times that I have to diverge from the crowd. The first act is quite fun. The second and third acts, however, are a downgrade in design and aesthetics. The game actually got irritating to me after the second act to the point that I just wanted it to end.

If the game had been built around the style and aesthetic of the first act, I would have enjoyed it thoroughly.

The second act was built more around a deckbuilding concept, but the game was so easy that there simply wasn't any point in using almost any of the cards the game kept giving you.

The third act was a boring slog. I found it mechanically and aesthetically less interesting than the first two acts.

I did not appreciate the game designer schlock that they built the story around. I ignored most of the videos. Yes, I know they're crucial to the narrative yadda yadda, but I'm just not a fan of the meta "what's going on with this crazy game world" trope.

In fact, the whole game ultimately felt like schlock to me. I could have done without the experience.

This game strikes me as another half-baked one-man job that has some great ideas with gaping holes in them.\t\t\t\t\t
