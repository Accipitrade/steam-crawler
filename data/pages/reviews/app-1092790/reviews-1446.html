\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to play one more game ;-;

While it is true you can't say much without spoiling. There really isn't many games out there that really take advantage of their medium to tell a great story, I promise you that this game is one of those few. It might not be a true roguelike (Kaycee's mod is supposed to amend Act 1 to be this, but I still wouldn't really count it as one), as it actually has a (somewhat) linear story to follow, I strongly believe it is something very much worth experiencing.

The game can be a little frustrating at times (I'm quite bad at puzzles and had to look a lot of things up, and even then I couldn't find a lot of the secrets in the game), but I believe as long as you enjoy the deckbuilding/cardplaying gameplay I don't think you'll hate it.

I hope with all my heart that there will be another expansion or DLC, or really *anything* more for this game. My final verdict is: if you're looking for a unique, immersive experience and enjoy card games (MTG in particular), I say please get this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can not even begin to describe the intricacy and attention to detail this game's developer has. The core concept is amazing, balancing a horror / rogue like style card game with a copious amount of humor and tense moments. The game play is amazing with it's beginning mechanics ever evolving and the infinite amount of possibilities with the sigils. Combining, sacrificing, removing cards to your will to conform your deck into your own personal wishes, completely unseen to me in card games before. The twisted yet calm atmosphere throughout the entire game leads to a sense of uncanny uncomfortably leading to game play where losing battles isn't your only worry. The lore. Sometimes put in your face, yet sometimes hidden deeper, and sometimes even having a hidden meaning. The realism aspect also adds to the eeriness of Inscryption. Creating real life characters you begin to grow a liking or interest for as you play through the creepy card game. The story also leaving you wishing it wasn't over at the end. Wishing you could have just finished that one last card game. The game should 100% be played blind, and if not played blind it isn't the same experience. 10/10 Would recommend. Most likely my favorite game of 2021.\t\t\t\t\t
