I was told to go in blind and that is a bit of a double-edged sword, as without knowing what you're actually playing, it's easy to get frustrated at the pre-determined nature of the game. But knowing too much would have spoiled the fun I eventually had, once I figured out what I WAS playing.

I eventually figured out that this was not the game advertised on the store page. Not really, anyway. It's actually much better in a lot of ways. It's also not the game you start off thinking it is. I still have a few gripes, but by the time I finally beat the game I walked away wanting nothing more than to play it again. So I did! The real problem with this game is that I don't even know how to review or explain it without spoilers. The card mechanics are fun as h3ll and I love every part of them. As a forever DM in my real life D&D campaign, it was nice to feel like a player again for a bit. I ended up really loving these characters and the story, but that last part is my main gripe.

Given the nature of the game, I understand why that is, but I still dislike that. I didn't look up the solution to the puzzles and am actively working to that ending by myself as best I can, though I am aware that apparently the end result is basically a URL to a YouTube video.

Still, that's not the point of the game honestly. I feel like the final card game against [REDACTED] when the scales get [REDACTED] actually sums up the game perfectly. In the end, I found myself playing just to play.

This game is an experience, one that I initially misunderstood. For that, I apologize. I am leaving my original review intact here because hopefully this entire thing as a whole will give someone some idea of how good this game is. It's frustrating at first, but I'm glad I didn't give up.

Also, this game officially gave me THE BEST scare that ANY game has ever given me. My real name is Casey, spelled that way, but people I am close to have sometimes "cute-ified" it (for lack of a better term) as "Kaycee". Now, spoilers kinda, there's a character in the game named Kaycee. Once I was introduced to the Death Card mechanic, Kaycee's card was the first one I came across.
