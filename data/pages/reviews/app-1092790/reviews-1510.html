\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt last, STIMULATION!!

Non spoiler review: It's good, it's everything I could ever want from a game. go in blind and experience it as how it should be experienced. Ik it is usually advertised under the guise of "roguelike deck building game" which is true, but that's not what it's about. This isn't just a card game, this is a Dan Mullins game, and that means you can expect whatever the mechanic of the game is, whether it be a deck building or a 2D scroller, is only the medium to carry out the story.

Not saying that gameplay isn't important, (it really is and it was fun for me) but I'm saying that you shouldn't come in a game expecting something super specific, I've read a lot of feedbacks (not that significant proportion) that they're disappointed bc it was not what they thought it was, so uh... that's a possibility if you do set your expectations up.


ANYHOW
Spoilers ahead but not too much!!
Oh god where do I even begin, I guess I should review Act 1 2 and 3 separately but I am not that organized, so here's a whole chunk of my thoughts.
I really did enjoy all of it and I love how the game has no "right" way to play it, it's fun seeing how different ppl approach the game challenges in their own way. Even within Act 1, each boss fight gave me smth new to work with and it never got boring. When you beat Leshy at his game it doesn't just end there and I'm glad, as you try to figure out how to escape the cabin, the restart cycles (aka RNG) gives you the opportunity to explore different ways to play around with your deck. If you're here for the deck, the story gives you an excuse to keep playing, if you're here for the story, the cards will keep you entertained while you work things out. The game got smth for everyone.

Once you figure out how to leave the cabin, Act 2 came and it's a major game changer, literally. The world expanded and so did your deck (decks?). It was a rough start for me in Act 2 bc I wasn't used to all the new cards and I kept dying until I got some more skeletons in my arsenal, but it was a blast trying to figure ♥♥♥♥ out while also exploring the surroundings of other scrybes and what their deal was. Again, everybody got smth from the game, new lore, new ways to play, new bosses. STIMULATION! Really charming art style too, I most enjoyed the soundtracks. Have I mentioned how good the sound work in Inscryption is? I wish more people pay attention to that. By the time I reached PO3's factory it gave me a very OFF vibe, not off, OFF, the game, reminiscence of zone 1, i love it.

ACT 3 MY BELOVED
Okay, I know, the atmosphere is life less, cold, dull, the map is "boring", PO3 isn't being a good GM/DM, but man, that's what I love so much about it. I'm guessing this is because I don't play card games, or other games that'd require a DM so I don't have any expectation or preconceived notion on how a deck game or how it should be like. I love the dystopian atmosphere, the industrialized vibe of the game play, the bleakness and coldness of it all. It's what I'm into so this is a no brainer why Act 3 is my biased fave.

It is less immersive comparing to Leshy's version of the game and while you can say PO3's play style is different than Leshy, I'd say that he's just as passionate about his craft, it just happens to be different than what the player/Leshy would want. Leshy is all about flavor, even when the cards get sacrificed it feels significant. PO3 is all about optimizing, the cards don't get "sacrificed" anymore. Ofc you can use the hammer to discard unwanted cards but that's not the same as using the bones and blood of your fallen brethren to summon new ones. Every cards, everything is just a mean to an end (even us). Even act 2 has stark contrast to act 3. Act 2, super colorful, gives you SO MANY CARDS to mess with, act 3 says "enough" it's all ab "productivity", streamlining, etc.

Personally I love act 3's play style because it rlly suits me, I hate having a too much cards, I love a limited deck and having the option to permanently remove a card from your deck is lovely. I know act 3 feels like you have less options to customize your cards (other than those endless sigils upgrades lol) but imo gameplay wise it makes sense. You have act 1 and 2 to experiment with everything you want, now it's time to make your choice.

Character wise, I do love PO3 a lot, he's such an ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ but hey, without the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ where would all the ♥♥♥♥ go. He's so perpetually snarky and annoyed I really wish there's more options to banter with him, I was really reluctant in finishing the game because I was enjoying this final act so much. I wish we can spend more time with Grimora and Leshy, they're both so charming, especially Grimora. The wizard is okayyy I have mixed feelings ab him, not really into what he did with his students.

All in all, I really love how the game's got something for ppl of different crowds to enjoy, I'm sure you'd find something you love, and the story is not one to disappoint.\t\t\t\t\t
