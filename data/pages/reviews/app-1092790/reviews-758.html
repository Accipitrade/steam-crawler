\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe atmosphere that the game pulled off in the cabin is some of the best I've seen. That alone was intriguing enough for me to play through the first part of the game, mechanics aside.

Cardplay-wise, the game in the first part is really fun and replayable. It may seem simple at first, but once you find out you can extract sigils, combine cards, and build totems, the possibilities are beyond endless.

Tbh the middle portion of the game drags a bit, but the finale, which is quite similar to the first part, is great fun too. Also, the story is quite deep and interesting, albeit a bit confusing.

All in all, I definitely recommend this game, though it might not be for everyone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA lot of reviewers seem to only focus on the aspect that this game perfectly connects a rogue-like card game with horror elements. But tbh it is so much more.

In my opinion, this game is a great example of how many possibilities video games have to represent/express art - many more than any other medium. On the other hand, it shows how difficult it must be to create a game that combines a lot of these possibilities so that it is artistically appealing and valuable and that it is also (very) fun to play.

Also, I'm fascinated by how after 1/3 of the game time you can think the game is over when some puzzles aren't solved or cared about solving. The game gives hints that you should solve somed riddles or should think about different items but it does not rub it into your face like many other games.

This game is a masterpiece and deserves to be played by anyone who is excited for a new and fun gaming experience and wants to enjoy the opportunities this medium gives people to express themselves artistically.\t\t\t\t\t
