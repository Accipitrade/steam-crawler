\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a deck-building card game with wonderful atmosphere and a unique and interesting narrative. Most importantly, though, the card game at the center of it is fun enough to play that it successfully carries the game. I'd describe it as fairly difficult, and somewhat reliant on the player thinking outside the box to find viable strategies for the harder fights. So make sure you're prepared to mentally invest in getting good at it.

One piece of advice I'd give to new players: while many of the secrets in the game reward you with extra plot or gameplay advantages, there's a significant amount of codes and plot threads that are mostly part of some elaborate and obtuse ARG. Don't expect to be able to decode some kind of path to an alternate ending, because there isn't one. I was a little disappointed to see that many of the the things I'd written down didn't end up getting used or explained as some final, ultimate secret. Aside from that minor issue, I think the game pulls off its mysteries very well.\t\t\t\t\t
