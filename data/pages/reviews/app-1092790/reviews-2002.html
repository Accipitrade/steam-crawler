\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is one of the best indie games released this year. This game feels like the capstone of Daniel Mullins Games. Amazing visual styles, great story, awesome gameplay, and a genuinely creepy meta-story (as always).

This game is a deckbuilder. A game where the main appeal is to build your deck of cards as if it were a trading card game without the trading. The actual card game itself is very fun, but also incredibly broken and unbalanced in a way that favors the player by a huge margin. And that is perfectly fine, in fact I love it. Also, I have been asking for YEARS for games similar to Magic: The Gathering Shandalar, or Pokemon TCG for the Gameboy. Now this game is more so multiple games in one, much in the style of The Hex, however I will not go into detail.

Graphics: 9/10, the art style(s) are perfect and set up for the feeling that the game wants to get to you. The dark of the cabin, with a creepy art style sets the mood for Inscryption. And just watch the trailer.

Sound: 6/10, I couldn't point out any specific thing about the soundtrack, but it wasn't offensive at any point and it did add to the experience.

Gameplay: 8.5/10, The gameplay is good, but I feel like it is too simplistic. The fact that you win the game by putting weights on your opponents side of the scale until that side reaches the bottom is a cool way to make it so that you have to have dealt at least 5 more damage than your opponent has. However, the low amount of 5 makes the game very quick and defensive strategies impossible.

Difficulty: 4/10, the game isn't difficult, especially if you have played other deckbuilders before, but there is a level of artificial difficulty you will need to account for. Also it is easy to be TOO good for the game and in this scenario the game forces you to lose for the sake of the story.

All in all: a solid 8.5/10 in my opinion, however I understand that there are some people you will LOVE this game and others who won't care for it.\t\t\t\t\t
