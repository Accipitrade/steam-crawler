\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption made for a great change of pace from the usual games I play. It's a wonderfully eerie card game mixed with escape room elements and is full of super weird moments. I managed to go in pretty much blind and on a few occasions I was audibly asking myself wtf is going on!?

The card game itself I found really interesting and the way mechanics morphed or adapted for reasons kept things fresh. Once I got my head round the game I did find it a little too easy at times but on reflection this makes sense, I think the journey through Inscryption is meant to feel pretty organic and getting your arse kicked repeatedly because you don't know how to build a viable deck wouldn't be very fun.

There are also times when you aren't playing cards, and I found these moments really engrossing. They offered a different kind of tension, a definite sense of unknowing and a rewarding feeling of puzzle solving that kind of remind me of 'The Room' games.

I'm no good with scary games but I feel like scary would be a stretch here. There's a definite sense of unease that persists throughout and the atmosphere is just weird but these are only heighten the experience.

I was surprised to finish the game after 10 or so hours but it really was a wild ride. There's also a separate game mode where you can just play the TCG in a rogue-like fashion that looks fun and worth a try.

In conclusion, Inscryption is unique and wonderful. It was hyped up loads last year for good reason, I really recommend it!\t\t\t\t\t
