\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe biggest flaws of this game are that the second act will completely throw a lot of players off -- it is very very different from the first act in presentation and it kind of drags a bit -- and it feels like there's a sort of lack of content. I don't want to be harsh about it because the game is still very substantial, and the creators managed to put together a cohesive narrative that kept me interested despite feeling like I wanted/needed more. You could also say that that is just a result of the nature of this kind of game -- it's trying to emulate that whole "found footage spooky vid series" thing that has been very popular on the internet since, well, forever.





I really enjoyed my time with this game, and I hope that there will be more of it in the future, whether that comes in the form of post-release patches, mods, a sequel or even just other games being inspired by this one. If I could have one wish for a future game, I'd like something revolving entirely around Leshy or a character like him -- I found him much much much more interesting than the other Scrybes and his whole aesthetic is really unique and cool.

Give it a shot if you like card games, weird spooky stuff, or both.\t\t\t\t\t

Definitely an Indie title that was worth the purchase.

This was acquired during the 2021 Christmas sale and for the money you get a lot of fun gameplay and interesting brain teasers.


What is this game?:
This is an RPG / Table Top Cardgame / Rogue-like. The game provides you with a very basic understanding of this mechanics and trust that you will learn and adapt as you continue to play through its "campaign"

What you should expect:
If you have had experience with other table top card games you should find yourself right at home. There are elements of deck-building strategy in addition to the common 1v1 card game tropes. The types of decks you will be playing with all have unique summons, conditions, and rule sets that greatly set them apart. You will have a lot to learn and get your hands on as you play.

You should also expect to be a little frustrated at times. This is the rogue-like component that makes card encounters challenging and rewarding to get through. However, some encounters can feel a bit "cheap" and "unfair" as you go from early to mid game. The power creep towards the end of the game does remedy the grind, but doesn't stop from being challenging and fun.

Don't expect to finish this game in one life, and you should probably expect multiple re-tries after some *seemingly* impossible encounters/scenarios.

Cool Mechanics:
RPG Elements, Branching choice path, Deck Building, Escape Room Style puzzles.

Criticisms and Flaws:
While the game places a lot of emphasis on player learning and agency it can be a bit sparse in teaching you the nuances of the card game. There is some elements of card building strategy, however it is well hidden behind a lot of trial and error. Perhaps that might be an area where some more seasoned players might enjoy, though some might like a bit more information.

Overall:
Really enjoyed my time playing Inscryption. Puzzles are sufficiently challenging, and hints are cryptic as the name implies. You will feel rewarded for figuring out the next progression stage and beating the "bosses" of encounters.
Wins feel very well deserved as you build your strategy and make you decks more powerful. Very balanced vanilla experience with acceptable amounts of player frustration (you wouldn't get that sense of satisfaction if it was just a walk-in-the-park).
The encounters can be quite challenging which adds to the tension going further and further into the rogue-like parts of the game. It never becomes too overwhelming, and the game rewards you for smart play and persistence.
The game also has some very cool art and designs (you get a great mix of modern graphics and retro gaming). This is probably the one thing that draws most players in initially as the art and the style is hard to ignore. Inscryption made me judge it by its cover; and happily find that the contents matched the presentation.

Rating (if you care for that sort of thing):
9/10
