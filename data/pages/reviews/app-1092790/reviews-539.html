\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not one for card-based games, nor am I a fan of roguelikes; I don't care for difficulty in lieu of a strong narrative, nor does it hold my attention when the world building isn't there. Inscryption is much more than it appears to be; not only is the atmosphere unsettlingly original, but the gameplay itself is tied intrinsically to the game's bleak truths that become apparent very quickly, and it feels like much more is at stake should you not be willing to sacrifice. The economy of its design is elegant, and I found myself incredibly addicted once the rules were made clear. This is more than just a game in more ways than one; don't pass up the opportunity to pick this one up, even at full price.\t\t\t\t\t
