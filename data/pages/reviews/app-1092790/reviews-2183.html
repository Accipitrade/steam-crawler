This game does sooooo many things right !
First: it takes a seemingly simple card game and the moment you get comfortable the game redefines itself, never getting boring.
Second: If you've played other games from this dev (Pony Island, The Hex) you know he has great game design ideas. And if you have not.... now you know.
And lastly: it is one of these games where you're left with this happy empty feeling. Sadness and happiness for the journey that happened and the cards you nutured or the characters you met and had to let go. I'll never see my 5/18 River Snapper again T_T

It took me 15 hours to beat the game taking it slow and it is worth every penny even if you buy it full price, in my opinion at least :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonal score: 9/10. I love this game, although a criticism could be that it's strongest act is the first one (the other two are good but are very different than Act 1). In the past, I fell in love with Mullin's first game Pony Island (don't let the name fool you) but thought the second game, The Hex, was just okay. But this game felt like both a return to the subversiveness of Pony Island and something new and unexpected. The meta-game is most important here, and is great, but I even found myself really liking the card game aspects, and usually I hate video game card games. Mullins also continues to grow in his/their ability to tell a complete story with a satisfying end. Highly recommended and I will definitely purchase again if given the chance on other platforms.\t\t\t\t\t
