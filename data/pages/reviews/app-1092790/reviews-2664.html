\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, note this game is about a 10 hour experience for someone going through the game at a moderate pace. There's still some extra content beyond finishing the game, but a heads-up for the duration of the game, which is absolutely worth its price point. This game definitely takes after games like Undertale, OneShot, and many other games that benefit from going in as blind as possible. Discovering all of the twists and quirks was genuinely amazing, and something I absolutely enjoyed going through in one sitting.

The story, gameplay, and ambiance are all in near-perfect balance. Not only is the core game loop something immensely fun and addictive, but the events going on around it kept me going on and on, never wanting to truly stop and just come back to it later. Be sure to clear your calendar before you start, you'll be hooked perhaps literally for a while!

The only potential con I'd mention is that the game is very tightly knit and contained. There was so much I wanted to stick around longer as a gamer, but as it comes to the story and intents of the game, it still makes perfect sense. More of an emotional "oh no" than a strict critical point.

Overall, I highly recommend Inscryption. It's like something I've rarely glimpsed in the past, and the experience was uniquely amazing.\t\t\t\t\t
