\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery rarely does a game have me this engaged, excited to see what comes next. I haven't played any card games since Club Penguin, so I can confidently say that you don't have to be a fan of the genre to enjoy this. The game's horror also doesn't come from overwhelming jumpscares, but a consistent feeling of unease brought on by it's various mystery elements.

A lot of reviews will say something to the effect of "go in blind" but there's good reason for it: the game's greatest strength lies in it's surprises. The core deckbuilding gameplay is solid, but straightforward enough that those surprises are what keep you on your toes, never sure what to expect. Sometimes a game can be enjoyed even when you know whats coming, but here I think experiencing those twists and turns yourself is essential.

It's not quite a 10/10 though: the game crashed a couple of times out of nowhere, and there are few questions brought on by the logic of the game's storytelling.

All the same, even with those issues, it's an easy 9/10. The music, sound, gameplay, and narrative all come together in such a way that makes for an utterly unique experience, and one I think most people would enjoy if they give it a chance.\t\t\t\t\t
