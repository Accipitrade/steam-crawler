\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNOTE; This game has 3 parts, part 1 is easy and surprisingly short and is essentially the only thing show in all trailers and media. Part 2 is an indefensible slog. Part 3 is even worse. UNDERSTAND THIS IS NOT A CARD GAME; It's a bizarre 4th wall breaking meta ARG real-life "go outside and dig up a floppy disk in Canada" type experience. IF YOU ARE LOOKING FOR A CARD GAME, KEEP MOVING.

In general, I found this game distressingly easy. My first run I got literally blocked by the game, the second boss telling me I had gone "too far too fast" or something to that effect, and spawning in 8 flying bears making victory literally impossible and forcing me to die. I felt like I was trapped in Harrison Bergeron, the game forcing me to lose because I had played too well. I personally don't like it when games just force the player to lose only because they were winning, and this game has that mechanic, it's extremely frustrating to lose only because you played too well. Three lives later I had beaten what I presume is the final boss, without having the opportunity to accept any of the boons he offered due to bad RNG. This game is not hard, don't believe these reviews saying the game is difficult, it's just a card game, use your items, play smart, be efficient, and you'll cruise to easy victory.

Inscryption falls into this absurd 4th wall breaking meta-narrative this dev's other games have done in the last act, I guess they can't help themselves as all their games seem to do this. If you're expecting this game to stay a card game you may be disappointed, this dev seems truly incapable of making a fun game that doesn't have some wild meta-narrative about video games that completely changes what the game is.

If you're really into deck builders, be warned you cannot at any point, as far as I can tell, actually build or edit your deck. Every new game is a brand new deck the AI picks for you and you can't change it outside of events. You get stuck with dead-draw terrible cards until you manage to find something in-game to sacrifice them, and the character you sacrifice the cards to whines at you because they're unhappy with your choices. The game will sometimes even let you create a card, and then never let you actually draw or use it because you don't get it on the only run it could appear, ie the run after you make it. That's right, you get to make a card, but it's only good for one run, and then it's gone into the aether, even if you never actually RNG'd an opportunity to pit it in your deck.

What can I say, this game could have been really good, if there was more focus on balancing the card game itself, and less focus on the big 4th wall mid-game shift. If I wanted to play "Her Story," I'd fire up "Her Story," I wanted a card game, and got a short and mediocre one.\t\t\t\t\t
