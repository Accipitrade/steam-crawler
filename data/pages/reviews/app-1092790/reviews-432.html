\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is divided into 3 acts. If you're too good, you will never find act 2, and that's probably for the best. Later acts get steadily worse than the previous one, with act 3 taking any charm the game had and shooting it in the face for a lackluster, predictable story that is entirely spoiled if you know what a single word means.

Act 1 is a fun, if a bit needlessly gruesome, rogue-like deckbuilder. Act 2 turns it into a GBA pokemon card game with the same basic rules as act 1, but removing the rogue-like elements and letting you freely edit your deck with the massive amount of cards you get, making the game significantly more easy and boring, along with introducing 2 other types of cards. One of them (Wizards) is complete garbage until act 3 changes how it works, and even then, it's an effect in addition to the cards' default states that just buff other cards' stats slightly, rather than allowing you to play the cards that actually help you win, like they did in act 2.

It seems like it's drawing inspiration from all over but failing to understand why any of it worked, and frankly: takes concepts that were unique, charming, and fun in their original contexts from popular games, and echoes them here to leech off those games' popularity. All to tell a story that goes from a somewhat okay mystery, to a solved mystery... and then continues for next to no reason to tell a meta-narrative that is especially boring, and calls back to a DIFFERENT few games, where they bait the internet into making content and advertising for them by having mysterious, unexplained secrets for youtubers to point at with a bid red arrow and a shocked face at 3am. It, like most other ideas it borrows from wildly better games, is unoriginal and makes me sick of seeing these tropes borrowed because they ran out of ideas and want to be the next Undertale/FNAF/DDLC/Slay The Spire/etc all at once.

You had a decent identity trying to be a rogue-like deckbuilder in act 1, and it seems like you want the other seven identities to get free exposure online. It's sad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite frankly, Inscryption is an absolute masterpiece. One of the best games that came out last year, and just one of the best games ever. I constantly find myself thinking about it and it is a must play with so much hidden depth waiting to be discovered.

Pros:

+ Oozing atmosphere - This game is creepy. From the figure lurking in the shadows that narrates your adventure, to a world that just seems constantly ever so slightly off. Inscryption is outstanding for just keeping you engaged, locked in place as you creep ever forwards through the story. There are so many fun twists, so many unexpected turns and just an overall experience that will wrap you around its shadowy finger.
+ Deck building is great - The card game is incredibly solid. The puzzles of each scenario are so interesting and fun to try and solve as you build an odd deck of creatures that somehow maybe might work its way to victory, or maybe just crumble to a pile of bones, but at least you still managed to have a weird experience doing it. Strange effects play off one another in fun ways and each run will throw something different at you as you creep closer to the end.
+ The soundtrack is honestly exceptional - For a game so loaded with wonderful story and gameplay, it cannot be stated enough just how cracking the soundtrack is. Every moment of tension, every grain of atmosphere and every sliver of game mechanics are all made all the greater from the game's music. It's a perfect mixture of rumbling creepiness and overt horror. Delicious.

There's nothing really bad to say about Inscryption. There's a constant sense of the new that pulls you forward as you discover more bits and pieces and try and solve more puzzles. While you can always get up from your table and go elsewhere, you won't want to with this one.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption has intrigued me for hours of playtime. At the same time it's frustrated me so much that I definitely wouldn't recommend it for the simple fact that the frustration has greatly outweighed the fun I had.

I like the story, the secrets, the buildup, the suspense, the music, ... But aside from those this game has some serious issues.
You cannot save or undo a mistake. And on the one hand I get it: the game intends you to make some mistakes, since dying is part of the storyline. But the UI of the game is such a monstrosity that you're constantly making these errors. I've misclicked soo many times on the wrong encounter on my path (just because the icons are all looking the same without any explanation), or chose a card that I already had because I thought I was just advancing the speech, or upgraded the wrong creature on the campfire because I didn't see how to replace it with another one.

This all leads to runs that go great at first and then you make a mistake that completey ruins it. And in the next run you don't get a hold of that Ouroboros card anymore, and your deathcard is just a waste and... well, after the 7th run of the same ♥♥♥♥ all over again, screw it... This game stinks.\t\t\t\t\t
