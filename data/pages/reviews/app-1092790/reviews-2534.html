\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCan't really recommend this at full price, but maybe pick it up if it's ever on sale for like 50% off or so.

The meta experience of the game uncovering a larger story is a cool concept and this would be a great implementation of that concept if it weren't for a few major flaws:

The card game:
The roguelite deckbuilder at the core of the entire experience is.. almost okay. There are lots of nearly interesting and fun mechanics, but at the end of the day, it ends up being quite easy and feels a lot more shallow than the number of mechanics might initially imply. Many of the encounters play out pretty similarly. It feels like a lot of the synergy building that makes good deckbuilders tick is missing here in lieu of letting the player create game-winning, OP-for-cost cards that you just wait to draw to move the story forward. It's a shame, because it feels like there is a lot of potential in the core game if it were to be fleshed out, but obviously I understand that wasn't the goal with this game.

The story:
As some others have stated, it does feel very creepypasta-ish. Unfortunately, this facet of the game also suffers in a similar way to the gameplay. Each subsequent act is progressively shorter than the first. There really wasn't enough story development for me to care about any of the characters or the "dramatic finish".

Summary:
This game felt like an exercise in connecting story threads and individual, but related games into one overarching story, which it accomplished, but only just so. Core gameplay ended up feeling quite shallow and repetitive with few interesting player decisions or optimizations to make. Story didn't move me, but perhaps it will hit you differently. There are a handful of moments where you think "oh, that's a cool choice" but after you've moved past the cool meta aspects, 10 hrs of relatively shallow gameplay and story with no replayability is a bit of a bummer. I still appreciate what Daniel Mullins was going for in trying to make something larger.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a wild ride and I could not recommend it more.
Lets run through why.
Gameplay. If you're not huge on strategic deck building games then maybe you'll be a little underwhelmed by the gameplay but this game isn't trying to be more than what it shows. This game is a roguelike deck builder on the surface and if that's what you're looking for, inscryption's simple but interesting mechanics are sure to provide some good fun.
Story. I went into this game not knowing much of a story existed at all. All I can say is that experiencing this game blind was by far one of the best decisions I have ever made. Nothing made me more excited to jump back in after a loss than working out the mysteries of this weird card game with talking stoats and a weird swirly eye man dealing the cards. By far one of the game's greatest strengths.
Music. At first, the music was very underwhelming to me. Nothing to write home about. However, upon progression and hearing these same dreary hums as I trudged my way through the maps of the game, I really began to feel immersed in the world and story and as the game went on and allowed even more diversity for music, I was nothing but pleased as devolver's track record of amazing sound tracks did not falter.

Overall, this game exceeded my expectations and I could not have been happier. If you're looking for a decent couple hours in a dark moody cabin playing pokemon with a weird old man in the dark, this game will not disappoint.\t\t\t\t\t
