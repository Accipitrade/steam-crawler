\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore i start, Do i recommend this game? Yes and No, I recommend *Some* of this game, After which point, The game is nowhere near as good, And only much later becomes a shadow of what it once was. So, i choose "Yes" Very tentatively, Because i want this kind of game to be around more, But still want them to learn from this game's mistakes.

Act 1 is the best, By far.
Act 2 is the worst, By far.
Act 3 is somewhat of a middle ground, although it leans towards Act 1, With its own issues.

Now, Here is why. Act 1 is what you get sold on, It's a card game about discovery, survival and mystery, It's difficult, but not in a bad way, And every step further you go, the easier the path behind you becomes when you inevitably fail and must try again, It's not just about winning the card games, but about trying things and finding out on your own how to escape. It's fair, and it's just a good example of a card game, with well blended mystery and an intriguing concept.

Act 2 is very different, Ignoring the differences in gameplay style and graphics, although disappointing as the style of Act 1 is so striking and charming, Act 2 is the worst act for one simple reason. RNG. While in act 1, and to a lesser degree, act 3, Are based around stretegy, do you draw a squirrel, that can be used for sacrifice, or to block an enemy attack at a crucial moment? Or do you draw a card that requires a cost, but is notably stronger? Act 2 is not this. Act 2 is throw together a deck from the many, many cards the game spams you with, Making not a one of them matter to you, And rather than employing the dual decks mechanic from the other acts, It is simply about RNG, Will you draw squirrels? Will they survive to be sacrificed? In my experience this goes for death deck too. Energy deck is rather boring in theme, It's the old and well used style of gaining +1 max energy point every turn and using that to summon cards, See Heathstone, for example. I haven't tried the Wizard deck as of yet, so i will refrain from commenting on that. On top of being entirely RNG based, Act 2 also drops all pretense of punishment or cost for losing a battle, the only cost is your wasted time, as you attempt the same fight over and over and over until finally you draw cards enough to put up a fight, I hated this and I forced myself through it so as to experience act 3, I believe firmly that Act 2 should be completely re-balanced if not anything more drastic, I can see many players less interested in the overall story getting bored and giving up at Act 2, and never touching the game again.

Act 3 is a closer experience to act 1, thankfully. But don't be fooled, It's not exactly good either. While it has the board, and its own way of exploring and decent battle concepts, As well as the ability to step out of the board and interact with objects in the room around you, like act 1, to try and make your escape, It has two very glaring issues, First is that while the idea of risk is involved again, after act 2's complete failure to punish you for losing a battle, It is done in such a way that it barely matters and only serves to waste yet more of your time, as instead of dying, restarting and learning from the previous run(s) You just respawn at a checkpoint and have to fight a couple battles you have already won again. The second main issue with Act 3 is the Cards themselves, While you will get new Sigils and the return of the "Squirrel deck" (it is no longer squirrels) It falls flat on one rather important issue, and i've seen others complain about this... The cards are useless, Most of the cards you will find are 1/1 or less, and will compare to much stronger cards on the enemy side, To the point where i had to defend from cards around 8 / 8 with good sigils in a certain fight with at absolute most a single 4/4 that only activated every two turns, and the Ouroboros i had spent the last two acts building into a 15/15 god, Which i never got to draw because of the amount of junk cards i kept being forced to pick up while desperately searching for something usable after being tired of having no useful cards.

I haven't finished the game just yet, But i'm far enough in Act 3 to where i feel like i can reliably review it.

TLDR;
Act 1 very good card game, very good mechanics, very good story.
Act 2 just remove from the game, pretty much nothing here is any good.
Act 3 re-balance cards and add more risk to losing.

Overall Story: 7/10 [] (Yes i gave act 2 a Zero, it has no story until the last three seconds, upon which it is suddenly act 3.)

Scale from trash to best card game? Honestly, It's hard to give this a solid answer, because i love act 1 and hate act 2 so much that i just want to separate them in my mind into each act being a completely separate game that was mashed together near the end of production, after years of nobody really knowing what they're doing other than "spoopy card game" and honestly it feels like it.

So, Act 1 9/10, Act 2 0/10, Act 3 6/10.

I'll just end it here, You get what i mean.

Overall i'll borrow a page from a certain youtuber [Josh Strife] and say that i rate this game...
My friends violently beat me to death during a boss fight, and i couldn't do anything about it, out of Ten.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, the game has great novelty and continues to make itself interesting even after making it all the way through a run.

However, I cannot get over the amount of times the other side of the board just gets RAMMED with stuff you can't deal with. I have beat the whole thing once through already. And to say it requires luck is an understatement. There are some truly broken combinations you can make, and you have to rely on those to win or you get totally steamrolled with an opponent who can just put as many cards as they want, of whatever power they want, at any time.

Prime example, I'm running through this run at breakneck speed. I have a card that will literally allow me to win before the opponent can even play, and I've drawn it on my first hand like 4 times in a row. Not even fun. Then I finally don't draw it, and what do I see across the board from me prior to even the 2nd boss?

An alpha with a raven egg on either side. Followed by an alpha with an elk fawn on either side. Like yeah, I guess I'll just to crawl in a hole and die. Too bad I don't have my mantis god crazy hybrid card to help me out.

And this is not a case of leaning to heavily on one card and then not drawing it. I had a pretty good deck which I thought was going to take me all the way to my 2nd win. I had multiple mantis gods, a mantis I had spliced together, 2 wolves, my stink bug, and a totem making my squirrels stronger. Like I was stacked. But then the game just decides, "Nope, this is where you lose," by putting a wall of strong cards against you which you have NO HOPE of outpacing.

This has happened time and time again and is the cause of probably 75% of the losses I've had. The other 25% being misplays, bad draws, etc. It is usually just because the other side of the board was filled right out the gate. As an experience, I recommend. As something "fair" or like an actual card game roguelite, I do not.\t\t\t\t\t
