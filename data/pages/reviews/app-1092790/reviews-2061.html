\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpoilers for the story and gameplay:




So Inscryption is an almost perfect game. The story is enticing, the gameplay mechanics are fun, unique, and intuitive. Alongside all of that the game even has story moments built-in to encounters/situations.

Now for the spoilers:

Act 1 was perfect. Story pacing was awesome and the gameplay only really got stale after a good 10 hours of JUST playing this part of the game.

Act 2 was weaker than Act 1 in terms of story but in terms of gameplay act 2 is MUCH better than act 1, because it introduces like 5 new mechanics which all change how the game can be played. Other than gameplay, however, act 2 suffers from a lack of content. Where Act 1 took roughly 5 hours to beat on my first playthrough, Act 2 took two hours. Even when coming back to Act 2 to check out all the secrets(and there are quite a few) that only added like 30 minutes of content.

Act 3 is better than Act 2 in story but much weaker in gameplay. Of all 3 acts I think it's clear why most people dislike act 3 the most. It's repetitive, droning, and sometimes just downright annoying. Act 3 suffers from being a (mostly) open-world.

So encounters aren't pre-planned like they were in Act 1 or Act 2. In Act 3 every encounter is made to be purposely generic enough to not be affected by the player's choice of progression which would be fine if Act 3 introduced all it's new mechanics quicker, but to even unlock the first new mechanic of act 3 a player has to spend 1-2 hours getting to, and beating, a boss.
There's 4 bosses in act 3 and despite them having super unique and cool mechanics, the journey to each boss doesn't feel like progression at all.

Overall I recommend the game on it's merits as an awesome horror card game. When the game deviates from the horror is when it kinda falls flat on it's face. On the bright side though there's room for a potential sequel!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me begin by saying that I've been getting bored of the gaming industry lately, and this game is the first time I've had fun in a very long while. Probably my favorite game of 2021.

If you're someone who's skeptical because you sorted by negative and saw a lot of reviews claiming false advertisement, that statement is only semi true. without spoilers, the game is broken into three acts. You may have seen people mention that the things shown on the store page and in the advertising are only in act one, then the game drastically changes after that. That's the part that's true. However, the caveat to that statement is while the atmosphere of the game certainly goes in a different direction at this point, as well as the game play, the quality remains the same. The reason this isn't shown in advertisement is because this game is actually incredibly story based, and a pretty wild story at that. Anything spoiled would really hurt this games impact. The devs clearly put lots of care into the game and for the price tag its a steal. The 15 hours of game play I have at the time of writing this review was the length of my whole play through, and every second of it had me engaged.

I'll admit that that the change in direction is a little jarring and almost caused me to stop playing, But stick with it past you're initial disappointment and the game starts right back up. (I'm talking to you everyone who dropped it after the switch. It's worth it. trust me.)

My last word of advice if you're considering buying, just do it. Stop looking at reviews and video's cause a no spoiler play through of this game makes it 10x better.\t\t\t\t\t
