\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave you ever started to watch a tv-show everyone recommends, but you just can't seem to get into it? Yet you force yourself to dredge through it in hopes of finding something of merit, only to find out every episode is somehow worse than the last? That was Incrysption for me. The game tries to set up a spooky mystery in order to get you to endure its god awful gameplay. It is not worth it. It feels like pulling teeth, and I think the game secretly knows it since it lets you do it. You'd have more fun hitting a hoop with a stick.

Let's say you DO swallow back your vomit and try to beat it. Guess what? When you get to a boss the game will suddenly flip the table, pull out a hammer, beat your toe to a pulp, and go: "Oops sorry, we don't want you to get further than that in this run, try again", and then hide the toe hammer somewhere else.

Let's say despite all of this, you sick masochistic ♥♥♥♥ decides to withstand the torture in order to reach its conclusion. You assemble the perfect deck. You remember to bring your steel-toe boots. You got all the pieces of Exodia, a royal flush, a 30/30 C'thun, and also collected all the Captain Planet Rings outside of the game board. You finally slay the dragon, and what's your reward? Some ♥♥♥♥♥♥, badly acted found footage made in order to goat some doofus on Youtube to gather all the ""secret lore"" of the game. Explain it over a monetized 42:11 video, and declare it the most genius piece of media since Moonlight Sonata. Oh yeah,that and another act of the game that everyone UNEQUIVOCALLY agrees is worse than the first act. Turns out, we didn't even hit rock bottom yet!! What??? Maaaan, go play anything else.

Thanks for reading my 1st grade essay. Now, if you'll excuse me, I will be outside playing some hoop stick.
