\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWoah. Alright. Woah.

A game that you lose and lose at, and when you finally win you lose all over again. Triumph and defeat.

If you like a card games, this is probably up your alley. If you like mysteries, this is also probably up your alley. Don't look up the answer, it's worth finding out on your own time.

The ending was impactful, at least in my opinion. The painting disappearing in particular was especially poetic. But if you you're the type of person who wants every possible loose end tied up (like me) the end will probably feel incomplete to you. However, once you finish the game, I recommend looking into the Inscryption ARG. It's already completed, and you can see the final result. The final part of the story.

It took me about 17 hours to beat, I would think? It shows less in Steam because some ended up being offline. -_- Anway.

I'll probably continue to play specifically for Kaycee's Mod (which you unlock after beating the game) and a few other achievements I missed. Still though, even if I never play again, this was absolutely a worthwhile experience, and I firmly recommend it if it sounds interesting to you.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoing in you might have some ideas about the kind of experience you're about to enjoy. They are all wrong.

Say what you want about Inscryption, say that it has a bit underwhelming story with couple of cheesy moments mixed in. Say that its sudden shift from the initial superbly established style when you enter the second part of the story can be jarring and possibly off-putting to some players. Say all that, sure, but through all the unpredictability this game loves inflicting you'll always come to the conclusion "God, it's all so cool".

Very few games can hook you immediately like this one can. By intentional design the beginning act of the story is most immersive as the main antagonist takes story-telling very seriously. That's as far as I can go without spilling the beans. Game's simple yet immensely rewarding mechanics are awesome to abuse. I mean hell, if you don't abuse them it becomes borderline unfair anyways, it basically wants you to cheese the system as much as you can. As you progress through it still keeps its charm and the core part of it throughout. Should note that soundtrack is surprisingly fantastic too. I say surprisingly because had this game not existed I couldn't tell you what sort of music would fit for a world like this, yet experiencing it as I play, it was rich and perfectly fitting for this game's atmosphere. I am also glad to say that for the people who only wanted the first act alone can enjoy it endlessly with many added challenges and new features after they complete the story. Fact that more than half of my playtime was spent in completely optional and bonus mode can attest to its fun.

As for the story: I'm not really sure but I think the main lesson was we should all leave the floppy disks to 20th century and never touch them.

Goodbye.
Good game.\t\t\t\t\t
