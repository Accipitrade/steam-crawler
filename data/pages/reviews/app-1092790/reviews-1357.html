\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame starts of with an extremely interesting concept. At first the game seems too simple, yet after certain events happen, the game switches pace considerably. The game has many hidden quirks that will help you pass the game even quicker, though the game itself isn't necessarily difficult. The build up to what seems to be the primary boss fight is well done, though the actual fight itself is extremely underwhelming. The amazing art style, the beautiful game design, and the fun 3D mechanics of the game are all thrown out the window. The game seems as though it isn't really sure what it wants to be, and the developer had too many ideas and jammed them all into one game. Later on it turns into a borderline pokemon knock-off. It also attempts some weird 'ARG' style lore, while it is completely devoid of any heart and soul to it. The card game is amazing. The game isn't quite as amazing. The story is even worse. At times the game seems to try and baby you through it. It gives you ample opportunity to manipulate things into your advantage while not having any of the bosses seem to adapt. You can quite literally make a card with no cost and 10 power and 10 health. This is game breaking and results in a piss-poor experience later on when there is absolutely no challenge at all. I have decided to take a break from the game after about an hour of the 8-bit section. The game becomes exhausting, and the beautiful visual style is replaced with a black and white mess at times. It's really sad, I want to erase my save files just so I can play the beginning again because that was the best part of the game by far.\t\t\t\t\t
