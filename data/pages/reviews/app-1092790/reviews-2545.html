\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am actually very bad at deckbuilders, but knowing Daniel Mullen, I trust that I will be in for a good time. And was a good time did I get indeed. The story, twists, and gimmicks this game offer was amazing!

But I don't want to talk about the story too much, but more about how genius this game was paced in order to make me actually enjoy a deckbuilder.

The first part of the game is a pretty straight forward deckbuilder rougelike, basically like slay the spire. But unlike slay the spire, making absolutely broken cards is way too easy, but the game allows it, game breaking cards are game breaking cards, even if I'm using it against a crook or the final boss, no edge cases exist. The first part was basically a power trip and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ did I enjoyed it.

The second part expands the game, revealing that I've only been playing with 25% of the cards the game actually has, the amount of cards to play around with essentially quadruples. Because it is quite overwhelming to use all of it, the game decide to get rid of the pressure of a rougelike and let you lose as many times as needed. I personally enjoy the game toning the pressure down, because even though I can die as many times as I want, I think the second part is harder than the first.

The third and final part of the game actually didn't go in the direction I expected. I expect, it to be exactly the same as the first part, but this time you play with all of the cards. But instead, you are again stuck with only 25% of them, and while you lose progress from losing, it is not nearly as extreme as in the first part. What truly changed instead, is the cards you play are now a lot more balanced, so no more power trips, but just a lot of head scratching for actually good plays. I think this part of the game is kinda weak, but it really paid itself of with all of the bosses in this part of the game. Their gimmicks are some of the most unique things I've seen and are definitely very memorable moments for me.

For story reasons in the game, it definitely feels like the game was cut short. Either I have to wait for the ARG to be fully dug up or a DLC to be announced, I'd say it doesn't matter too much, because what is offered in the game right now is already one hell of an experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, this game should win GOTY! It has everything a gamer could ask for even though it "just a card game"! There is so much to this game that it is hard to know where to start.

The game design is a mixture of Hands of Fate (an outstanding deck building game) and Slay the Spire. Instead of being your character up, your build your deck and upgrade the cards to help progress through the card. Without spoiling the game, the type of cards you get to add to your deck and the ways some are created are the most unique designs I have seen that are enjoyable and actually add to the immersion of the game. The map is the same as in Slay the Spire: a road with branches that lead to different events. The card battle is much like Hands of Fate with the items, currency, and buff mechanics. The difference being the cards do the battling instead of a third-person slash and dash mini game. Difficulty is hard to determine due to the rogue-like genre. I enjoyed the learning curve and was punished for some mistakes but some players may find the difficulty plateaus at some point.

Sound Design is unmatched. There are a few moments where you will pull your headphones off your ears to stop the bleeding due to sudden screeches unfortunately. Aside from those few moments, the music and SFX create a very immersive atmosphere with some bangers hidden in there!

Aesthetically, everything from the cards to the map to the rooms to the bosses are fit perfectly together to create whatever immersive theme presented by the level. Although, you spend most of you time looking at a map or a boss character who controls the map, the visuals are never stale! The bosses change themselves into characters you meet along your journey as though they are performing a One Person Show. ( One boss plays a character who flips his mask and ends up being a shopkeeper you met in the woods! It blew my mind when he did it! ). The backgrounds change like different sets in a play. If the design was meant to be like a Broadway production, they nailed it!

There more to the game than just collecting cards and beating bosses. It's hard to go into these aspects without spoiling the game but there are tons of hidden puzzles with great rewards, clues to puzzles cleverly hidden during gameplay, and cut-scenes to tie everything together into one pretty good story. It's a Daniel Mullins game so it can get a little dark (not just in brightness!) but the pull no punches approach makes the game that much more enjoyable and satisfying to complete. It is also a bit META for those that don't like that type of game interaction.

I think the only problem would be replayability. Then again, it's only $20 so I think you get more than enough game play for that amount of money. The game does seem to have multiple endings so I am excited to see what happens on a second playthrough. I actually haven't beaten the game yet myself but I watched a streamer beat the game and I REFUSE to believe that is the only ending. Especially in this game!

Overall, I am very impressed by this game! It has been a while since I have purchased a game and thoroughly enjoyed playing it. This game has so much character it makes you want to keep playing it even after your 10th death!\t\t\t\t\t
