Beat the game in 12.2 hours (Finished it all in one day which is kinda unhealthy, but hey good game + School
holidays).
As much as I hate on the second two chapters they weren't bad or even mediocre, they were still very enjoyable and challenging.
There's a bunch of lore and secrets that I feel I haven't even grazed yet and will certainly call for more playthroughs!
9/10 - However, as much as I enjoy this and its style, no game will be everyone's cup of tea so take this with a grain of salt if you haven't played games like this.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing - recommended even if you are not really into card games. The atmosphere and story make it all worthwhile on their own already. If you feel like playing something uniquely different while also warmly familiar this is it. If on top of that you at least barely like strategic roguelite card games and/or escape room games you will be in for a very special treat. It is well worth its price with about 20 hrs for a normal playthrough without searching for all the (probably) insanely well hidden secrets. It seems to have very deep lore which basically screams for one or two game theory episodes (get on it MatPat!) and pretty much all the content creators are already milking this like crazy. But please just play it yourself because the experience is outstanding and fun all the way through which is definitely lessened by just watching.

Only real downside I could find is that the game is a bit too easy. Especially in the late game I found myself just cruising through the game with my op deck that got even more insane through normal progression, spanking enemies and even bosses left, right, and center. That was certainly a LOT of fun and it is clearly intended for the player to feel op. However, similar card games like Monster Train and Slay the Spire find a better and more rewarding balance between difficulty and deck creation freedom... at least in my opinion.
