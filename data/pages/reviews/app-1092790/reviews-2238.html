\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love it, it's a solid 8-9/10 game. I probably would have given it a 10, but I had hoped it was focused on the animal's aesthetic and gameplay more. After playing Sacrifices Must be Made, I was excited to see expansions to it. The animal part can honestly be considered nothing less than a masterpiece at a 10/10. It was by far the most gritty and fun to play, and was also what I was anticipating playing more of - a game about sacrifices with a feral wildlife theme. There is almost no mention of anything else; the description markets it as woodland animal cards. It was fleshed out, mechanically sound, and most of all, extremely fun.

The card mechanics got a little convoluted after a while, especially the magic and energy, and after learning and loving the first card methods, felt sort of annoying, chore-like, and unnecessary. The bones were fun; the others, not so much. They felt like they were there more for "explaining lore" than being actually enjoyable. The game with Leshy was also far better to me than any of the other acts.

The ending was very abrupt and pretty underwhelming for me. After the first part, I found myself wondering when I could go back to play more with Leshy. I suppose I found a lot of the 'story' or ARG stuff really unnecessary too, and not at all scary. I didn't really care for any of the characters except the four Scrybes, who felt pretty unneeded themselves (aside from Leshy). It almost felt like it was trying too hard to be meta.

I feel like the story could have been conveyed without all of the sudden, less fun game twists. The tone COMPLETELY changes, and it's not a fun change - it's like when a rollercoaster makes you feel ill instead of getting an adrenaline rush. Even then, I don't think the idea to make it "digital horror themed" was a good idea. The real footage was generally pretty boring aside from him finding the coordinates and the floppy disk, and his dialogue took me out of the experience while playing. During the first part, where you presume your character, YOU, dies and is turned into a deathcard, is horrifying! ...until you realize that it's, ironically, just a videogame, and you hear Luke complaining. So absolutely nothing is at stake. I believe it worked far better as what SMBM portrayed it - a prisoner of a shadowy figure, or a lost traveller, forced to play games to eat. The sentient cards in this made me so excited to see what they were about, given the camera and all! Do failed attempts on deathcards turn into animals? They started morphing visually, and I was thinking that maybe the animals were previous players who had failed, and I was infatuated - were they turning into humans?! When Leshy said that "the cards may just be cards, but the pain they feel is real", I got shivers! Having that in a game? Or being playing an abandoned game like this, with the threat of becoming a card in reality, being forced into the game? That would have worked great as a roguelite AND had the meta element. But, as soon as I saw what Leshy looked like, the fear was pretty much gone. The Scrybe arc completely annihilated any fear I had left. Simplicity suited this game perfectly well, and it feels like it was overcomplicated in both mechanic and story for very little reason. It's not roguelite, either - it should have been. Chapter one and similar things like it was what should have been done. Not a linear, boring "story mode". The original gameplay loop was perfect. If it ain't broke, don't fix it.

Don't take me wrong - if you enjoyed the game or the story, that's great! I loved it too. No shame there. It's a good game. Not bad by any means at all, it's definitely fun for the most part - I'd be insane if I said it wasn't well-made and generally able to be considered good. This is just my two cents based on my preferences, which won't suit everyone.

TL;DR, Leshy part best part, wish there was more animals and Leshy coz Leshy good. Also game got overcomplicated and unfun because game tried meta story at expense of enjoyable gameplay and scare-factor coz adding more stuff and unneeded complexity always = good. Gonna go back play more Leshy now. He fun.

Also, to anyone who agrees and loves the first act with the beasts - if you haven't played Sacrifices Must be Made, that's the gamejam masterpiece that preceded this game. It's basically the card game from chapter one and might satisfy your cravings.\t\t\t\t\t
