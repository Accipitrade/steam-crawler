\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I can respect what this game does, I think it executes it poorly. This review is going to mention a lot of things that many might consider spoilers, so if you want to go in truly blind obviously don't click the spoilers. Personally, I think this is one of those games you can enjoy almost as much simply watching someone else play it, than playing it yourself.

Mild spoilers about the nature of the game :

The game initially presents as a sort of deckbuilding roguelike - this is probably my favourite part of the game as many others have noted. It's dripping with style and generally this part is quite fun. However, the rest of the game diverges from this in terms of gameplay, making it more of a linear story game with a lot of meta elements, such as the ARG that accompanies the game. Your goal shifts from beating the game you're presented with to essentially escaping from the game


More major spoilers and my issues with the game :

The story of the game suffers because of this ARG element and the decision to frame the game from the perspective of a third party. There's a lot of vague loose ends and the game ends VERY abruptly, without giving any real closure on the story that is the core of the game. It has big pony island/undertale vibes in terms of the ways it 'breaks' the rules of how games are supposed to play, but having played these sorts of games Inscryption feels like it falls VERY short of others like it. This might just be my personal taste in storytelling, but especially if you don't engage with the ARG portion of the game, almost nothing about it makes much sense beyond there being some vague evil code embedded in it. Especially if you've warmed to or become attached to any of the characters in the game, well you because you're not getting any real closure for their stories or natures beyond a brief pre-credits victory lap. The ARG portion is also essentially dead unless you're driven to be a part of those kinds of communities and got here in a very brief window after release - and it feels like a significant amount of work went into it, making the experience feel incomplete if you merely play the game and read up on the ARG elements. Which sucks, because the whiff of a story the game presents to you, actually within the game is mostly compelling (I honestly dislike the recorded footage segments and would rather just have the whole game take place from within itself or with an unknown third party rather than a known one.
