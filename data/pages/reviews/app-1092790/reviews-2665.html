\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been wanting more of this card game ever since I played the gamejam version, I got 8+ hours on the demo in anticipation and ~20 hours as soon as I could after release. I am obessively thinking about what a multiplayer version of this game could be- I am simply always craving more.

If you like playing TCGs and don't want to break the bank rougelike deckbuilders hit the spot. This game has compelling evolving mechanics and if you plan correctly you can earn a satisfying gamebreaking easy win against the bosses. Though, even good plans fail, and I've found my self thinking hard even when I had built a fantastic line up. Every time I think to myself "I think I'm going to win this game, but I wish it was longer" ... it gets longer. I haven't finished yet. But I've made good progress. the ENTIRE game changes multiple times, I am on the third iteration of the game I also adore how GMs are represented in the game

I LOVE INSCRYPTION- I WANT MORE\t\t\t\t\t

I went into this thinking it was going to try and be the next slay the spire, with the twist of having escape room puzzles to spice up the gameplay, and while it does have that, it has so much more, for better or for worse.

I also was lead to believe that this game had spooky/horror undertones but it would primarily focus on gameplay, I was wrong about that as well as I would call this game a full on horror game to an extent, if you get scared easily, there are a few parts in this game which gave me chills, so be aware.

If you go into this game completely blind like I did I would compare this game to Undertale personally, not in terms of gameplay or mechanics, but in terms of my expectations being set kinda low and the game opening and expanding to encapsulate so much more than what I ever thought it would.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIgnore the fact I've played this for a short while, I was mostly playing with my friend via a very scuffed stream co-op.
This game is not for people that only want the raw game play, don't get me wrong, the gameplay is phenomenal throughout the entire game but I cannot lie, the cabin is easily the best part, followed by Bot-topia and finally the game world, but they're all great . The game is not made solely for the gameplay alone, the creator did a fantastic job making an atmosphere where one feels like you understood the whole premise and feel of the game before being dunked into a vat full of meta horror, betrayals, twists and a whole load of story.
Roundabout review aside, I cannot recommend this game to people who are only in it for the gameplay, the game excels in its storytelling and meta horror aspects, as well as leaving hidden secrets all around waiting to be found and for a theory to form around the story.
Tl;dr Short version(tm): Gameplay falls off a bit, but the meta-horror and storytelling only go up and up, worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t
