\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I was planning on doing a whole pros and cons list once I finished this game, however I honestly don't think there's much to be said other than: GO PLAY THIS GAME. It starts off as a spooky card battler. So I had initially assumed it'd be like a normal card game, maybe some light story and a few jump scares... But this game is so much more than that, I don't want to get too detailed otherwise I might spoil something. All I can say is what I expected to be a simple card game, took more than 13 hours to complete and at the end I was completely blown away by how good it was. Easily my best game purchase as in probably at least 2 years, I highly recommend this game, it's well worth the price and I plan on checking out some of the developers other games.

the only negative thing I have to say will be listed below as it is a light spoiler, so consider this your warning.












*LIGHT SPOILER*
The only negative aspect about this game for me personally, was the second act, because it switched into an 8 bit game style, and I'm not a huge fan of 8 bit games, and I personally found the slightly different card mechanics a bit annoying. This is of course just minor personal preference things, and I wholly recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot much one can say about the story without taking away something from first time players, but I will say it was a phenomenal ride. The way this story was told couldn’t exist in any other medium and have the same impact.

The graphics, while not pushing the limit, are VERY aesthetically pleasing. It’s a particular art style that’s bound to stand the test of time.

There’s a modern 8 bit feel to the sound effects and music. Not much more to say about the music, except it’s purchase worthy. No voice acting in the game, but the atmosphere more than makes up for it.

I’ve never been particularly interested in rogue-likes or deck-building card games, but Inscryption has now made me a fan of both. It has an addictive loop that made me set alarms for myself, so I didn’t forget to eat and sleep. With the addition of Kaycee’ Mod (as of this review still in beta), a portion of the game (arguably the best) is given infinite replayability.

Inscryption is a one-of-a-kind experience that I highly recommend, even at full price.\t\t\t\t\t
