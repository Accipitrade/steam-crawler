\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoy rogue-lites and deck builders and after watching a bit of Markiplier play this game, I pulled the trigger and binged the whole game. 8/10
Pros:
Dark and moody atmosphere and story
Intriguing world with lots of hidden stuff
Interesting game mechanics

Cons:
-See post-review for cons-
-------------------
***Spoilers after a short review***

I really enjoy the base game and mechanics on offering in Inscryption.
You play as a person stuck in the wilderness in a cabin with a crazy shadowy figure who just wants to enjoy a little bit of card battling with you.
The art style is dark, moody, and atmospheric, and you can get up between battles and explore your environment for clues, puzzles, and other neat little 'escape room'esque mechanics, but nothing TOO breaking from the core gameplay.
The game world map is a bit like FTL's map, with branching paths, multiple encounter-types, and unique characters along the way.

In a moment I will LIGHTLY discuss spoilers, as its a big part of my ending disappointment with the title.

Still here?
OK then...
SPOILER ALERT
.
.
.
The game is actually kinda two games in one with an ARG element.
I really enjoyed the art style and game play of the opening few hours. I was hoping that once you 'escaped' that more of the world would open up, perhaps with additional puzzles and more content. While that was true, the game completely shifts from the atmospheric and imho beautiful art style into a Mario 3-style overworld.
Yes there are more of the things that I liked as explained above, however everything changes into a cutesy style which took me out of the enjoyment I was having within the cabin.
There are also new game+ play mechanics and deck-types (with differing card costs than what you have dealt with up until then) that open up as well at this point.
Narratively, I know why they did things this way but it was quite disappointing to me as I had enjoyed what had come before.
I have since solved many of the puzzles on my own, and when stuck I checked online for some hints.
I kind of wish that the game continued within the first 'world', or at least gave us a branching option.
I had zero clue about the first 'ending' until I came upon it myself and was genuinely excited for what came next.
However, the 'what came next' was like a spiritual sequel in a lot of ways, which took me out of the world the game had set up until this point.
Even with my gripes, this was a great game for anyone who likes these mechanics!\t\t\t\t\t
