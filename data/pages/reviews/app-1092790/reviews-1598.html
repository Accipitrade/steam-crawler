\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a huge fan of the deckbuilding genre. I put far too many hours into Slay the Spire to count, I've been totally digging Fights in Tight Spaces even though it's disgustingly hard with the starter decks, and I thought Banners of Ruin was spectacular. At first I thought Inscryption was going to fall into the pile of "truly epic", but then I started looking deeper.

On the surface this game is 100% deckbuilder. If you don't like deckbuilding games, there's a reasonable chance you won't like this game too. The issue is that with the exception of the absolutely brilliant art direction/story that is Act 1, it's not a very good deckbuilder - and I think this was deliberate. While trying not to spoil anything other than "there are multiple acts", I personally felt that after Act 1 this game fell off a cliff that it couldn't survive. The main reason is that the game is storytelling first, and deckbattling second. There's an entire escape room/narrative that the game (rightfully) wants to tell, but it did so by often making the deckbuilding take a back seat Even your battle in the game is cut short for story reasons.

Act 1 was great though, honestly one of the most unique and fun experiences I've had in a while. But even then the "winning" hands I played through in Act 1 won because of deck breaking mechanics, not because of clever or artful card play. It's as if the author deliberately threw in a way to push the narrative forward for people who might struggle with difficult deckbuilders. Then Act 2 happened - which was such a shift in both tone and difficulty (in terms of I don't think I lost a card battle all of Act 2), I suddenly found myself seeing the game for what it is - a story, a reasonable one, but honestly not one that grabbed me. Even the escape room mechanics were tossed out the window in Act 2, and while Act 3 brought them back, it never returned to the brilliance of Act 1.

For fans of the storytelling genres, it's worth trying to see if you like the mechanics enough to play it. For fans of deckbuilding genres, except to be reasonably entertained for awhile. You may or may not feel like I do (and that's fine, we all have our own preferences), but don't expect a Slay the Spire. The closest game I can put this with is Griftlands, which I think beats this game in spades in terms of replayability and depth of story.\t\t\t\t\t
