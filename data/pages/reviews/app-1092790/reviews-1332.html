\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Recommended, even if card games are not really your style.

I rarely post reviews, but I'm honestly impressed with this game. I had watched maybe a few hours of someone else play it and once I felt I had understood how it worked, a bunch of new mechanics opened up. After I saw that, I knew I had to play it for myself.

The game has a meta story and everything the game does is to fit into the narrative. The visuals, sounds, and overall performance is akin to something you might expect from "A strange game you found at random coordinates you receive." Therefore, I don't feel like I have to defend the stiff movements, grainy filter (which you can disable in the settings), hard edges, and the like.

Regarding the gameplay itself is something a little more difficult to express without spoiling some of the game. The simplest way I think I can talk about it is that there are puzzles just about everywhere. Obviously the main focus of the game, the cards, are very well thought out and if the card pulls are skewed for or against you, I couldn't tell. However, there are more puzzles than just what's on the table. The cabin has quite a few things to interact with and the game very smoothly lets you discover little trinkets hidden around you.

I, just about, liked everything about this game. I haven't played anything from this developer previously, but if their other games are anything like this, regardless of scale or genre, I believe I will check them out as well. If there were anything that I would recommend as constructive criticism, it would simply be to add more[1]. Everything was truly delightful and I'm honestly hungry to learn more about this world that was crafted. I feel like playing this game is like watching a movie, but seeing it through a keyhole on a locked door. It's like you were able to fully enjoy the movie, but so much was basically hidden from you. Another game/games that take place in this world would be the perfect key to unlock this door and give you a full view of an intriguing world that beckons to be played in.

As a personal sentiment, If you've ever wished to be able to completely forget a movie, book, game, show, or whatever so that you can experience it all over again, that's how I feel about this game.

Now if you'll excuse me, I'm off to see if there's anything that I've missed.

[1]: I felt I should clarify this by saying that I know there's a lot here and I'm mainly referring to some gameplay that happens near the very end of the game. I would like more of that, games with that specific kind of gameplay in mind or perhaps an addon/s for it. Who knows? There could already be what I'm looking for locked behind some code. I certainly wouldn't put it past the developer.\t\t\t\t\t
