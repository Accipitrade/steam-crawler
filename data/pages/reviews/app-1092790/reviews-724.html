\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI honestly feel like this game sucker-punched me a little bit because it vastly changes the game experience after the first section to one that I genuinely disliked and found poorly designed on many fronts.

Spoilers inbound: This game is incredible for the first part, but once you beat Leshy you're transferred to a totally different artstyle world that is painful to look at. I'm a fan of pixel art when it's applied well, but in this section, it's genuinely borderline illegible and gave me a headache while playing. On top of that, you entirely lose the reward system of a roguelike that was enjoyable in the first part and are instead railroaded onto a super imbalanced, linear world of opponents with zero cost cards that are exponentially stronger than your own, and no ability to upgrade or fuse cards like the first section of the game had. The game basically 180s from being a random-gen map each playthrough that allowed for diverse playstyles to a headache-inducing overworld with no measures of balancing your deck to your opponent's. Honestly a shame.\t\t\t\t\t
