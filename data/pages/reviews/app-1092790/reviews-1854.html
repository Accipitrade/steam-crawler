\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat creative and fun game with many twists and turns that keep it interesting. Without spoiling too much, the game is a meditation on a cell-based card game where positioning and deckbuilding are key. This formula is expanded upon and changed throughout the duration of the game, and most of the time it's very fun and interesting. There are several unique and interesting ways to change and modify cards in your, so there's a lot of room for creativity and it's exciting to find busted combinations of attributes.

I would say the game starts off the strongest, has a little bit of a slog in the middle (but still interesting in its own right), and finishes pretty strong once again. As far as replayability goes, I've gone back and played through sections a couple times and I'd say it holds up. The first playthrough is definitely the strongest though, since as you start to learn what works and doesn't work I'd say the game becomes rather easy. There are certain mechanics in the game that try to scale the difficulty to the player's power or punish the player to some extent for having decks based entirely around 1 or 2 OP cards, but I wouldn't consider these mechanisms to be too effective most of the time. Still, the variability that comes with card-crafting/modification keeps it exciting enough to want to come back to every once in a while.

Overall a very creative, unique, surprising game, definitely one of my fave indie releases of the year.\t\t\t\t\t
