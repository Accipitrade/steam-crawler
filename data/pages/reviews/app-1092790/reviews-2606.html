\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit - After updates I am going to change my review to positive, I think I can probably conclude the game was worth the money, even if I didn't enjoy the majority of the game or the ARG part, I think the kc mode made the game worth it. Original negative review remains below:

-

might update this in another week once I've fully gathered my thoughts on it, I have fully finished the game though

I can't really recommend this game; if what you see on the store page is what you want to play, over half of the game is not as advertised. Personally for me the game is just not interesting enough that it really justifies it.

In terms of what I was expecting, I was kind of just expecting the game to be what was in the demo/the original game 'sacrifices must be made' which I found super compelling.

This is exactly what I got in the first 6 hours of gameplay, which were absolutely worth the money. I will say the game is actually quite easy, and spoiler alert, this might stop you progressing through the game. I beat the final boss of the first act a few times before I'd actually figured out what was going on. I never felt a reason to use the tool, and so I was stuck for ages. The final boss is very beatable just on basic cards, so if you actually get a real build going it's very simple. Sometimes the boss will just kill itself after 1 round.

Sound wise, graphic wise, gameplay wise, I probably could not have asked for more, the game is absolutely drenched in the atmosphere you've seen for yourself in the demo, and if you haven't played it I absolutely recommend you try it.

Spoilers ahead:

-


-


-


Unfortunately, once you've reached act 2, the game takes an absolute nosedive. Gone is the entire basis of the game, the theme steeped in atmosphere pretty much just disappears in favour of some poorly done meta-gameplay.

I'm sorry, but if your game pulls the ever-overdone 'it's a game within a game' schtick, it better bring something interesting to the table, and I don't think this game did at all. I didn't find a single bit of the game profound or even remotely interesting. How many games have you seen where the characters realise they're within a game? That is exactly equal to the amount of games you've played that already covered this games ground. I can't really think of any specific moments where I thought 'this sucks' but I also can't remember any specific moments where I was actually impressed or interested. I dunno. The combination of game within a game and 'found footage' vhs theming, it's just so overdone that I was looking for way more, especially to justify leaving such a great theme.

I've played through the game once, and I've gone to look up to see if I missed some kind of important interesting plot points, and I genuinely haven't, this is the same tired plot that every game-within-a-game uses, with an ARG attached, which is absolutely fair I suppose, if you're the kind of person who loves to go on forums to read about games, then this game might be right up your alley. Personally I love a good puzzle; however, once you get to the point where you're actually asking me to go and download old games of yours and I'm supposed to make this connection to understand some deeper plot, you've lost me. The game doesn't do enough to keep the average player interested, and yet I'm expected to go and fish a certain depth in a certain older game to even comprehend what's going on behind the scenes. I really can't count this as content that's accessible to your average player.

In terms of gameplay, absolute catastrophic failure past act 1. I've already said the game is very easy, but once you take out the atmosphere that you get act 1, and that element of sometimes being obliterated by RNG, there really is no point to the gameplay at all. In act 2, you walk around beating every boss with autofilled decks, and the choice you make at the end doesn't matter whatsoever, then in act 3, I died one time, and I wasn't even sober at the time.
I think there's a very fine line you have to toe when you're making a game on turn based combat. Too hard and you have an inaccessible game, but too easy and you have a game like this, where even a drunk moron like me can just click through the game with absolutely no challenge. Being totally honest, the random fights make for zero challenge at all, and then the boss fights you can just retry with no downside, which is a stark comparison to where you had to go through 3 maps just to reach the final boss in act 1. The stakes actually get lower and lower.
The issue is that the game at its core is designed to be very easy, which is how it stops you from just dying repeatedly in the prospectors area, but once you're out of the roguelike part of the game, it just comes apart at the seams. It would take an absolute genius to create something where the difficulty of mechanics is workable for both a roguelike and a game where you can retry fights as many times as you'd like, and the developer has not managed to succeed in this impossible task.

I'm not even just saying this for some type of cringe gamer brag, I died plenty of times in act 1. It's just genuinely that once you're out of the starter part of the game it really is just done being at all challenging.

In terms of act2, the game takes on a top down form where you essentially go into pokemon style 'gyms,' you know the drill, please defeat minion 1, 2 and 3, before you can fight the big boss gym leader, all of whom appear to run identical cards, and the gameplay is really nothing more than what you might see on a yugioh gameboy colour game. By the time you've actually unlocked all the cards to start seeing how the game actually works, it's already over.
In terms of act 3, it plays exactly the same as act 1, except the penalty for dying is gone and the game is far far easier. Throughout both of these, I noticed that not 1 of the bosses actually 'got me' on the first try, no real interesting mechanics. I mean it tries it a few times with some 'whoa the game has access to YOUR steam friends and YOUR hard drive!' moments, but frankly, again, it's been done before and it just doesn't bang like it would have done half a decade ago. Also, remember all those fun cards you were introduced to in act 2? Yeah, those are gone. Even the gem mechanic is completely pointless.

I really cannot understate how much worse the game actually gets past the originally advertised gameplay, all you really need to know is that the game obviously plays second fiddle to the story. This isn't necessarily a bad thing, but for a game like this, with the story being this basic, and the deep lore being so inaccessible that you have just as much of a shot figuring it out having not even played the game, it really does make you wonder why you even bothered.

It really does feel like act1 is the proper game, then act 2 and 3 are just filler garbage.

In the end, I don't really hate the game, but I would have paid 40-60 for an expanded 15+hr version of the main game, whereas the version that was released made me feel like a moron for even buying it, and certainly made me wish that I could get a refund. I can only advise that you simply watch this game instead of buying it, because I promise you that you have missed absolutely nothing by watching a letsplay.\t\t\t\t\t
