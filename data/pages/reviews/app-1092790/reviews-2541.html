\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed the first 1/3 of this game.

The section at the beginning of the game is the only part that is advertised. The player is trapped in a cabin with a madman, and you have to play a card battler rogue-lite game against him where each loss is a death where you have to start over, usually with some small gains to assist you in getting farther next time. The art style works very well, the music and atmosphere are great, and the gameplay of the card game is surprisingly fun. One of the biggest issues I believe card battlers have is getting screwed by RNG, and this section fixes it by letting you choose between drawing from your deck at the start of your turn, or by drawing a squirrel (which essentially works as a mana source). This way, you never feel like you are screwed out of any further options, and always have a route to progress in battle. Also, you have the ability to stand up from the table where you are playing the card game, to explore the cabin you are trapped in, filled with little puzzles and mysteries. If the entire game was dedicated to escaping this cabin, with some added depth to improve the longevity of the cabin puzzles/card game, this would be an incredible game.

Unfortunately, as I said before, this is only 1/3 of the game. After you beat the cabin section, you are given some exposition (in the form of one of the lamest IMO forms of storytelling, which is found footage), and thrust into an entirely different game. Everything that is good about the first section of the game is removed and replaced with an inferior version. The game becomes bland pixel art, the music becomes grating, and the gameplay becomes both more complex (in the introduction of 4 card themes, 4 different mana sources, 10-15 new card effects) and more simple/dumb (they remove the mechanic where you can choose to draw a mana source, thus making you fully reliant on RNG). Every single battle after this ENTIRE game change devolves into starting the battle, seeing that you got a bad draw, conceding, and trying again. The changing paths of the first section are replaced with a few static NPCs that you have to battle over and over until you win, with no variation even of the cards they are going to play. I was not able to make it through more than an hour of this new game (during which I beat 2/4 of the bosses and experienced 3 of the 4 zones), because it is terrible. According to other reviews I've read, this second game is much longer than the first section and is then followed by a third completely different game, which some people say is even worse than the second part. Basically, this game is not worth it. It is ~2-3 hours of an amazing game followed by (from what I read about the total play time) ~7-9 hours of what seems like a mediocre student project game. This is not worth $20. If you are looking at the images and marketing and think this game looks cool, I agree! But that isn't what 2/3 or even 3/4 of this game is. Maybe watch an LP of the first 2 hours then find something better to spend your money on.

TL;DR: This game is a bait and switch. After the first 1/3 of the game the literal entire game changes to something that is a massive downgrade. Save your money, watch the first section on youtube or something.\t\t\t\t\t

No attack manipulation outside battle, no sigil change (except that accursed sadistic magician), no deathcard, no pelt trader just ordinary coil to open pack, no item to help battle (except hammer to kill our own card). NEW card rule NOX (until now i cant understand nox rule) based on magician boss and energy based on robot boss. Thank god blood sacrifice leshy and bones based on necromancer boss is the same as act 1. Overall, Act 2 VERY DISSAPOINTING for me.

Act 3 is mixed for me, is like chopped off act 1.
Fixed route, card generation still random though starter card always the same, no deathcard when losing just drop money that can be picked later and go to provided checkpoint. Sigil can be manipulated, add to card for a price that keep increasing on shop. Card battle energy based on act 2, no more blood and bones. Nox available as buff near the end.

Well after 3rd playthrough, i use OUROBOROS card or something method. When that card die it's attack and health increased permanently. LvL it up a bit on act 1, grind seriously on act 2 (achievement 666++ damage), on act 3 usually my ouroboros card attack already 200++ to generate overkill money for buying sigil. I successfully create 2 OP card.... BUT that card creation on act 3 is near the end, just 1 or 2 fight before the boss.
OH MY GOD, i grind so hard on act 2 and act 3 to create OP card just for 2 fight and boss battle... so underwhelming.
Why not put card creation on middle act 3, why put it near the end......

For reference i think this is the only game that i have 100% achievement, and i just like act 1
I see Demo is act 1 propestor, imagine when i'm buying it and act 2 become different...
The ending is good tough, i almost cry because the ending very touching for me. Dunno about supposed "secret" ending.

Not recommended on full price, wait for sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the time of review, I have completed the game in its entirety. No secret left undiscovered and no achievement left to earn.
While I myself enjoyed the game greatly and recommend it as a good experience, I feel like a couple points should be addressed before that.
  1. : The game itself is divided in three major Acts. Those are marked by a wild change to both gameplay mechanics and graphics. I'll try to address those without spoilers.
    • Act 1: It's what is making you consider buying the game. It's what is shown in 98% of the trailer's time and is certainly the best part of the game.
    • Act 2: It's completely different graphically. It's the 8bit parts shown in the other 2% of the trailer's time, if you rewatch it, you can get random glimpses of what both navigation and combat are like in those. Now, this is the part which is probably causing most of the negative reviews on the game. While the card-game mechanics themselves are vastly expanded and add actual deck-building as well as implementing different gameplay styles for different deck types and the story going wild places and explaining some things, the game itself loses the charm that made most buy it by watching the videos. Many say that this is the longest part of the game, but I disagree. If you count the number of fights and outside-battle things you can do, this part is probably the shortest of the three but certainly feels like the longest due to that loss of atmosphere and suspense that Act 1 had.
    • Act 3: Story-wise, it's the finale. While the finale is heavily debated itself, I say they did a good job since it's being debated at all. Gameplay-wise, it somewhat goes back to the style of the first act, but not quite. Further elaboration would require spoilers. This part cuts off deck-building entirely once more, giving you certain cards to start with and allowing you to develop the deck and cards along the Act, like on Act 1. Side items make a come-back, but are static and recharge on checkpoints. The graphics go back to being 3D with you facing the opponent on the table, but the theme of the place and opponent are different (again, can't elaborate due to spoilers) and not as interesting as Act 1. There is absolutely no footage or teaser of this Act on the steam page.
  2. : There is no option to access the boards in a "skirmish" or "infinite" mode. This game would certainly make me go back and play it a lot more if it did, as well as make me recommend this game more often.
  3. : Some of the boards, cards and game mechanics are only accessible in a very limited part of the game and some exist for only one fight. (If you completed the game, I'm talking about the Scrybes of Magic and Death) Adding those to the mentioned "skirmish" or "infinite" mode would be simply amazing.

TL;DR: First act is the best and is what people are coming for. It's the most enjoyable of the three, even if it has less mechanics that the second. If you decide to buy it, be warned that graphics and gameplay change wildly between acts.\t\t\t\t\t

I came back after a month away and finished this game in one big sitting. However, my return to the game was at the beginning of Act 3 which returns to the same gameplay style as Act 1, so by this time I had all but forgotten about Act 2.

Act 3 was a return to form and I thoroughly enjoyed it. The story is a bit whacky and ARG-esque, so if you're into that stuff you'll like it.

Towards the end of the game, you share touching moments with some of the antagonists and I even started to get all teary eyed.

It's a great game, let down by a strange mid game style choice. Is it worth the full price? By the end of it all, yes it really is. Just be prepared for a bit of a kick in the balls at some point. I guess, all good experiences has its share of being kicked in the balls.

I won't change the thumbs down, simply because of how misleading it is.\t\t\t\t\t
