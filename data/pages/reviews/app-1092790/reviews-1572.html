\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA work of genius.
This game does for computer card games what Undertale did for JRPGs, twisting the genre into new territory while giving it enormous depth and breadth, not to mention length. Just like Undertale, we have a layered story that bends the fourth wall but still stands on its own as an interesting world to explore. Also like Undertale, it adds new mechanics to the game's core; in Undertale, combat was turned into bullet hell segments, while in Inscryption, progression in the story happens outside of your success in the card game, depending on a series of escape-the-room style puzzles. Its a very successful marriage, giving the whole game a spooky feel that is reinforced in the story.
The gameplay is the main event, though, and it is so, so good. Explored fully through multiple styles of play, the card game of Inscryption is crunchy and fun, with dozens of little secrets and hidden techniques that make mastering its mechanics a joy. The long term strategy of deck construction and the nail-biting tactics of turn-by-turn plays are extremely well balanced, and across the whole course of the game we see many variations on what aspects of the game take center stage. It never gets stale, there's always new things to learn and new challenges. The puzzle sections help to add pacing and variety, and the story itself is a well told spooky mystery, one that plays on themes of urban legends and internet horror stories.
All in all, a masterpiece.\t\t\t\t\t
