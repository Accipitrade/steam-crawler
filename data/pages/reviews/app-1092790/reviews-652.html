Inscryption is an adorable floppy disk backflip.

[light spoilers ahead]

The first detail that I noticed in Inscryption is that you can't start a "New Game". That you could only "Continue". Clicking on the "New Game" results in a minor screen glitch.

"Oh! Haha xD! There's going to be artistic value in this game!" I thought to myself. "I was in for another Devolver Digital wild ride!"

The Wild Ride
It all began when I asked my friend "Oh! How long did it take you to beat the game?" He didn't reply. So I checked his recent playtime and incorrectly guessed that he beat it in a mere 4 hours. At the time I was hovering around 3 hours.

Inscryption's beginning might seem punishing and "intrinsically deep", but that's just the zoomer power fantasy that you're supposed to like.

And sure enough, it was a true grand spectacle! All you need are a few hand-crafted cards to simply bulldoze through literally anything. With the right combination of stats, ability, and cost, your new "meme" could be uniquely OP. That's pretty cool!

But it does devalue the gameplay a little, since all you need is one run with your meme card to just meme the game. Everyone talks about how fanatical their cards were in the beginning of the game. Because well...

Gameplay 2
Inscryption does this weird thing where it wakes you up from the power fantasy and instead shows you how malleable its gameplay is. Instead of a concentrated power fantasy, it's a colorful dream of originality.

At the highest levels of each deck variant, you can glimpse the true power of each deck. You can mix and match cards from different styles to truly differentiate your INSCRYPTION GAMER STYLE.

Act 3 and Finale
After gameplay 2, you'll be in Act 3. It goes back to that spoopy power fantasy of a wild ride. The only difference is the flavour and the gameplay sleight of hand. The mix-up that's enough to keep the game fresh until the finale.

While the gameplay format was wonderful for its setpieces, the gameplay might have felt weaker than the wild ride. Resource build-up is slower than the zany-random-chaos-steroid-Adam-Smasher-Smashed-Inscryption-type power fantasy.

The finale was when you could glimpse the true vision of Inscryption. The artistic value in a 3D pixelated floppy disk card game! It's easy to dream about what this game could have been. If the creative power fantasies collided with the rest of the worlds, Inscryption would have showcased true mastery with originality.

Criticisms
But unfortunately, Inscryption decided to tug at your heartstrings for no reason. The writing, while amazing, felt off for things that wasn't supposed to be sentimental in the first place.

Perhaps if the power fantasy were to be saved for the final sashimi of the horror card game omakase, it would have been a delectable note to end on. Perhaps if it didn't rely on sentimentiality, it wouldn't have stifled its true potential.

Conclusion
At the end of the day, Inscryption is still a very impressive card trick. Yet another Devolver Digital classic that can invoke some happy vibes with its name alone. But story wise, they took the Hotline Miami route and tried to make it a full-bodied story. But to me, the sentimentality is a grim reminder that we'll never have truly nice things in life.

The biggest problem with Inscryption is that the beginning zoomer power fantasy was exceptionally well-crafted. It had everything - from the artistic vision down to the gameplay! Sprinkle in a little zoomer RNG and you're guaranteed to have your buttons pressed for the first few hours of the game.

If you never make it past the beginning, you won't see the extent of the artistic vision. But if artistic vision isn't your forte, that's fine! Inscryption still has one of the heaviest beginnings of all indie games! The beginning alone is enough for me to recommend this Inscryption on your credit card.

9/10.
Killer backflip, floppy disk!
