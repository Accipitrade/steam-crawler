\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will attempt to do this without any spoilers. This game probably isn't what you think it is. Get it anyway. Stick with it. There were a couple of moments where I was losing interest, but it was because I was trying to play it like slay the spire which it very much isn't. If you're on the fence and reading reviews, negative ones mostly talk about gameplay details, like various things changing and ruining the experience. Ignore that. The moment to moment gameplay isn't really the thing that is so great about Inscryption.

That said, in a vacuum, the card game is one of the best deckbuilding games out there IMO. It's simple yet strategic. My only complaint is that there is some weird pacing with how new mechanics are introduced, but I think that might be intentional. The mechanics aren't all as well thought-out as the stuff in the beginning, but it doesn't really matter that much. The card game overall is very easy to cheese. You'll find a lot of infinite combos that will feel like it takes away the challenge. The bosses introduce dumb rules that force you to play in dumb cheesy ways that make your previous strategies feel pointless. Again, doesn't matter. That's okay. It isn't trying to be slay the spire. Find the way to cheese it and do it. The card game is a puzzle you need to solve in order to progress.

Just know what you're getting into with this. It's a narrative game with a very fun deckbuilder as the thread that holds the story together. If you aren't a narrative person, if you hate point-and-click games, this might not be right for you. If you want just the card game, there's an expansion they released that lets you play it as a rougelike. It unlocks when you beat the game, but you can unlock it with a key command from the beginning. Still, I can't imagine anyone getting this and not playing through the story. Do yourself a favor. It's worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
