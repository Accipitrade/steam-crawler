\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is an absolute gem of games, in an age of digital card games being lazy cash ins, Inscryption is a fun and thrilling ride with a good ass story behind it's basic but never stale mechanics. It is brutal to failure and that raises the steaks perfectly without it feeling unfair ever (Unless it's meant to be unfair for story purposes that slides in my book.). As a normally rootin' tootin' shootin' game type player I was oddly fascinated by seeing the mechanics in a video (Markiplier) and knew I needed to play it for myself this time; the only other time this has happened is with the also instant classic of Undertale.
I was going to wrap this up but then I remembered the fact that I never mentioned that Inscryption? Yeah it's a horror game, which I found myself doing throughout playing as well in a very good way. Right when I would get comfortable in an environment the game would slap me across the face and remind me I was not safe where I was, to always stay on my toes, and to think things through. Inscryption does what I like to call rubber band horror; it builds and build and builds only to SNAP when you least expect it to, and the build is the perfect uneasy environmental details with the snap sending a shiver down my spine rather than a FNaF style scream.
Overall? I would say it has little to no bugs and the ones that do exist are so specific I don't really expect them to matter to most players. The horror is well executed to not be cheap, and the gameplay can't be praised enough. The design has it's own style that is perfect to set the "you're in danger" mood when it wants to and to make all the dialogue punch juuuuuuust right.

With ALLLLLLLLLLL this in mind I would rate Inscryption; 9/10, a wonderful product that I can't praise enough, definitely my vote for Game of The Year, and if it ever exists I can't wait to get my hands on a physical deck!

Youre going to love it. Thats it, thats the spoiler. You will dig this game if youve ever liked another card game/puzzle game/psychological/ARG before.
If youve never tried a game like that before, I still recommend it, as its both easy to get into, and easy to get lost in. I mean that in the best way, its not a simple timewaster, but it also isnt serious business. A game with substance and value, but doesnt require 40+ hours to get the hang of.

I only have a few hours of playtime so far, and I have to say, the way the game evolves over time, all while telling you a story, without actually *telling* you anything, is fantastic. Im really impressed with the amount of creativity and genre blending going on here, solid recommend from me.

And seriously, dont look up any guides or anything on your first playthough, as the game is best experienced raw the first time. Spoilers and sneaking peeks will seriously reduce the enjoyment this game is offering.

EDIT: I just beat the game, total play time is 10.5 hours, with probably around 15 losses or so, and no extreme secret hunting or anything. Probably a bit on the short side for most hardcore gamers, but its a sick 10.5 hours you wont regret. Shame the game was so good, made me play it too much, beat it too fast lol.\t\t\t\t\t
