\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf Steam allowed for a neutral review, I would do it, but this way it is just one of those times when I am giving a not recommended review of a relatively good game.

So to explain the non-positive review (without story spoilers):

The game itself is divided into three acts.

- The 1st act is just amazing.Everything in it is just right: the card mechanics and deck-building, the visuals, the hints of a story and the atmosphere. The actual card game is well-balanced to offer some challenge and not be too easy or boring. It is truly captivating.
- The 2nd act is just a letdown. Instead of a visually stunning and mystery-gripping atmosphere, you suddenly play a pixel card collection game. Even the quality of the card play falls down here, mostly due to unbalanced mechanics - roughly half of the cards/combos are just not worth playing due to how weak it is compared to the rest. The rough-like aspect is completely thrown out of the window in this section.
- The 3rd act somewhat returns to the captivating atmosphere and art style of the first one, but after a couple of encounters, the game here just becomes repetitive. Some encounters however are quite nice and together with the puzzles, the overall feel is OK.

- The 'meta story' is probably the worst aspect of the game. Firstly, the way most of it is told is distractive if anything else; the 'real-life short videos' just completely ruin the atmosphere. Secondly, the story itself is unimaginative and boring. The overall impression it leaves behind is that it was just randomly and forcefully paired with the game for no reason at all.

I wish that instead of attempting to mash together ten different genres the focus of the game stayed on the great card game and mystery-horror setting showcased mostly in Act 1, instead, it gradually turned into a clown fiesta by the end, which was highlighted by the pointless ending.


Overall: 5/10

- Skip this game if you want it to be like the things showcased in the trailers all the way through; it's not
- Buy on sale if you want to experience the great 1st Act
- Buy for full price if you are a fan of decent-mediocre card & card collection games, occasionally paired with an over-the-top and slightly badly presented mystery plot
