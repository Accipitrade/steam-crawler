I'm uninstalling out of annoyance, but I'd still recommend this game. It's a wholly uneven experience because it's first act is incredible but a degraded experience follows. TBH I probably would have been more satisfied if Act 1 was the full game and this was significantly less of everything else. However the discovery of third act which basically feels like restarting the game on hardmode is enough to deflate what was left of my interest. Still a great game at its core, but the second act sucked the fun out of it, and the third act just isn't for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother deckbuilder game that could have been more and seemed to be more than what it was first appeared as, although I like deckbuilder games and have had played multiples and enjoyed them, I was expecting a genuinely unique experience from the start of it but then was heavily disappointed as the game went through.

To start, the game paints itself as a sort of deckbuilding game that appears to be an escape room! I was thinking that this would be great, as you went along you got clues on how to escape the room or something, and it does so! Eventually I thought the game would have different escape rooms with different themes based on which deck you chose after making it through act one; however, was sorely disappointed at the sheer linearity of the second act. It basically made you just play card battle after card battle and had little to no puzzles in it that were at least considerably unique.

The third act was more of a disappointment than I thought it'd be, don't misconstrue that it wasn't put together well; however, it was ended early in a way I wish it didn't. You literally couldn't fight one of the people painted as main boss enemies in more than a friendly sparring match due to the story. I would have been ecstatic to fight this boss in his own world with his own puzzles and trials, but they cut it short.

Then there's the mod, an addition to the game after clearing it. It allows you to go through the standard, beginning of the game, maps with additional modifiers. I wouldn't have been so disappointed in it if it weren't for the fact that I beat the all modifier challenge on my second try with the basic deck.

Overall, the game is definitely a noob friendly deck builder with somewhat difficult elements, but certainly left more to be desired. If you find this game on a heavy sale I'd recommend buying it as it would be worth the purchase then; however, buying this full price does not seem worth it with what they could have done with this game.\t\t\t\t\t
