\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscription is my favorite game. It's truly a hidden gem.

Inscription is a turn-based card game that also masks as an escape room! The puzzles are fantastic, the art style is beautiful, and just when you think you've got the game figured out, it pulls a hidden card from up it's sleeve! Literally!

I've played over 30 hours of this game and I just can't get enough. Even after that, there's still more to play. The game is carefully crafted so there is always something new to try and so you can be the boss of your own deck. I love the idiosyncratic reactions to each tiny detail in that they help form a larger story.

On my first play through, I thought the characters were boring and lack-luster, but after a couple runs, the game finally reveals itself for what it really is. You form bonds with the characters and begin to see the true motives of each one. They feel like real people after a while.

There is a point where I feel the game becomes a little repetitive, and there were some parts that felt the style was lost. But I can understand that some things just aren't for everyone and I am able to look past it for the story. :)

The game devs certainly did a fantastic job with the making of Inscription and I am so honored to have gotten to play this. It truly is a lovely game.

8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMinor spoilers ahead. Hard to talk about the game without spoiling it.

It's certainly one of the more unique and creative games I've played. First time I played it, I ended up dropping it, because it's kinda slow at the start, and also, it requires you to start over several times in order to progress, which can be counter-intuitive. Me, I instinctively dislike this, because I want to overcome the difficulty even if it's very hard, even (especially) when the game basically forces you to lose. Technically, you can still overcome it even when the game is basically shoving a ton of scary stuff at you to force you to lose (but then you lose out on hardcoded progression you're supposed to get between attempts). But it's more of a moonshot. So yeah, not a huge fan of that kind of game design, even though it's kinda integral to this game's design that you lose and start over. It made me drop the game the first time I tried it, and I'm pretty sure it made a lot of others drop the game, or its demo, without really getting to see the best parts of the game. At least, when I talked to a good friend who tried the game, they had that same experience. So that's a shame.

Not a knock on the game, but one frustration is that it's very spoilable. Can't really tell people about the game without spoiling things. That's frustrated me when trying to tell my friends about it. Hard to get people interested in it when all you can say is "it's very unique". Even saying something like "it's a rollercoaster" or "it has several twists and turns" are kinda spoiling it...

Tl;dr - worth it, play it.\t\t\t\t\t
