Disclaimer
What you are about to read is my best attempt at a Spoiler-free review, but it's difficult to not hint at anything, you have been warned!

First, a list of bullet points (no spoilers)

PRO:
+ Fantastic Narrative
+ Masterful Athmosphere/Tension
+ Great Concept
+ Creative use of the Video Game Medium
+ Unexpected Twists and Turns
+ Daniel Mullins

CON:
- Sometimes a little RNG heavy
- Achievements aren't really all that fun, and also just kinda weird in a game like this

Review:

Inscryption is a game by Daniel Mullins [Games], it's a Horror Rogue-lite Deckbuilder, and a really competent one at that, though sometimes the game will screw you over really bad due to RNG.
Your game starts with only the continue option available, no new game may be started. Immediately you are thrown into a tutorial, the "game master" opposite to you and shrouded in a thick shadow. "Perhaps you've forgotten how to play this game, so let me remind you." He remarks (or something to that effect).
On the surface, the game is simple, you have two decks, one with squirrel cards which cost nothing to play and one with normal cards, which usually have a blood cost (represented via blood droplet icons), you'll have to sacrifice your other cards to play these (as many cards as there are blood icons).
And now, you're on your way - bosses, loot (usually in the form of new cards), random events (Oregon Trail-style) - the whole deal, which you should be well acquainted with if you've ever encountered a roguelite or even a Choose Your Own Adventure game.

But tread carefully, for not all is as it seems.

As always, massive thanks to Daniel Mullins, for continuing to tenaciously push the envelope of game design and storytelling, and for gracing us all with his art!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNon-spoiler review: It's a roguelike card game that most reminds me of Slay the Spire, but as if you were playing it within a horror movie. It has some minor flaws, but if you like card games, roguelikes, or psychological horror games, it is probably worth the price. If you like all three of those things, it is absolutely worth the price.

Spoilers below.

The game can essentially be split up into its three acts: Act 1, where you must beat the character Leshy in his cabin; Act 2, where the game becomes a top down (well, isometric) RPG; and Act 3, similar to Act 1, but you are in P03's factory and must defeat him instead. There's also the intermission videos- a faux card game youtuber, slowly unraveling the mystery of the video game.

I think most people who play this game would agree that Act 1 is absolutely masterful. The card game it introduces is fun, but more to the point it is absolutely dripping with atmosphere. The first time you lose, the stakes become clear: each player is playing for his or her life. If you lose, Leshy, the eyes in the dark you play against, takes your picture and you become one of the cards in the deck, which is a wonderful (though totally abusable) mechanic. Each boss is a character played by Leshy- he dons a creepy mask and puts on a show. It's like you're locked in a basement, playing Dungeons and Dragons with a serial killer DM who will kill you if you lose. It's fantastic.

Act 2 and Act 3, however... they are by no means bad, but I just would say they don't stand up to how good Act 1 is. They present the same card game (or, a very similar version), but I'd say the fantastic atmosphere is gone. There are some arguments to be made that this is an artistic choice- that P03 does not care for the atmosphere, and therefore, he didn't bother creating it- but I still had some moments playing these acts thinking "I just want to get to the end" rather than enjoying the ride. I think most people replaying the game will spend most of their time in Act 1.

I also personally didn't like the "youtuber" intermission videos. I think the "found footage" style was the right choice on what I'm assuming was a shoestring budget, but I'm just not sure I found it believable. I didn't feel the agent at the door was threatening, I didn't really get scared for the main character. This is no fault of the actors, who I think did a great job. I'm just not sure the videos really added anything except adding one more layer to the meta story.

These, however, are really minor nitpicks on what is a really well done game. I can't wait for the endless version which is coming- I will gladly spend more time in Act 1.\t\t\t\t\t
