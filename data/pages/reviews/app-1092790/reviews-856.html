\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter completing Inscryption I guess I can give it a recommendation, with many reservations.

The first time I bought the game, I refunded it almost immediately because of the interface. It is a strange mixture of mouse and keyboard control that doesn't work well.

When the game went on sale at a good discount I decided to give it another try. Am I glad I did? I'm not sure. When the game is good, it can be really good, but at times it is so frustrating to play I just wanted to be done with it.

The basic card game mechanics are good. There are three acts, and each has a slightly different card mechanic in it. Wrapped around this is a puzzle layer that for me was not much fun to play. The puzzles are either easy (and therefore boring) or obscure.

The cards games could definitely use some balancing. Many boss battles are easier than the non-boss battles. Some card mechanics are a bit obscure.

There is a weird story that is told in bits throughout the game. More weird than interesting to me though.

Still, if you like card battlers the game is worth it for that. Ideally, I would like the card battling elements to be improved and everything else to be removed, then the game would be great.\t\t\t\t\t
