\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile Inscryption is a roguelike deckbuilder, it plays more like a CCG. Rather than gaining a series of abilities for a lone hero, you instead collect a menagerie of units to attack and defend on your behalf. Still, you have choices in how you go about collecting and upgrading your cards much like standard roguelike deckbuilder games.

Despite its initial appearances, Inscryption is ultimately a very narrative-driven game. As such, the game includes a number of easily abused systems to ensure you can reach the end of that narrative if you stick with it. The story itself is not one I found to be especially exciting or emotionally gripping, but it was decently clever and has a good amount of hidden details for those who go looking for them.

While the game is fun, it has significantly less replay value than others in the genre. Now that I have seen the story and know the gimmicks that will ensure I win, I feel there is little reason to go again. Still, getting through that initial playthrough was worth the effort.\t\t\t\t\t
