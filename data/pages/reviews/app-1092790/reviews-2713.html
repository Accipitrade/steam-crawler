\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fantastic, the original idea was great. The pony island dev pickup was great. Everything about it is great. I have absolutely zero complaints about it so far. It's just an all around great time, spoopy for the month of october and alot of fun synergy and such like Slay The Spire. Making for a great little horror card game with replay-ability.

Shoutout ManlyBadassHero for showing this game and allowing me to enjoy such an experience.

Review part 2:

After going through the game and seeing the flip side of it, I have mixed feelings about the direction. While I still will hold true to the top part of my review and think it is an absolutely fantastic game. I wish it built more upon "Sacrifices must be made" rather than the Pony Island direction it took. While it is a very neat concept and the whole meta style going on, Sacrifices must be Made's version of the game with that heavy Slay The Spire inspiration was phenomenal. I really would like to see some future updates to that aspect of the game, if not just general mods that add boat loads of content to it because its incredibly fun.

Where as the second half of the game "the real" game is just kind of lackluster. Go around collecting packs, go around like a pokemon gym, beat the 3 trainers, fight the boss... I just wasn't feeling it. There was a lot of insane synergy you could pull off in the original part of the game, a lot of really strong deck building and making your own cards. Even though with the baseline of cards it was already so powerful. The second half of the game is just lackluster in the sense that its like every other card game, you draw, RNG kicks in, no mana this time bucko, or no creature this time bucko, whatever it may be and it causes you to be in a predicament of figuring out how to build a deck based off singular draws that may or may not screw you lol. All in all the second half of the game just gets very generic from that aspect where the Sacrifices Must be Made part of the game gives you that option to make your next move which is super beneficial to the player to not be slumped over in a single turn. With the base of 5 hitpoints per player, you either rush people down, instantly beating them, or you play alot of defense and throwing cards away hoping you get your stuff out to then go for the overkill bonuses.

TL;DR
First half of the game - Amazing, undeniably fun, alot of synergy, alot of strong cards, innovative, an all around great time, heavily influenced by slay the spire. Easy 9/10

Second half of the game - Hearthstone, Top Deck & Pray. The mechanics were cool, the gameboard stuff was cool, but just short lived gimmicks. More stressful and heavily reliant on RNG. Maybe a 5/10.

(Also Squirrel totem with Undying or Magpie eye (Card search) is so ♥♥♥♥♥ fun because you juts feel like a god, I would love to see how crazy an end game could get with decks like those lol)

Review Part 3: Kacyee's mod.

Kaycee's mod was a great move by the dev's. Good job we're very happy now, Would like to see it built on even more really. Was what interested alot of us in the game. "There will be sacrifices" was a solid idea, was like a mix of yu-gi-oh and horror. Just a solid concept with some really appealing gameplay and mechanics, though I'd definitely remove the "Misplays" from stats, i've had literal perfect runs where I feel that I made every best move possible granted to me and I would end with like 15-20 misplays. Misplays by what factor? What caliber? What can I do better? Theres no answers for that lol. It's just an AI saying it woulda did something different in some situations and considering it a misplay. Just think it's not really a good stat overall since it doesn't tell you anything, if you could review the "better plays" it would be more interesting, maybe see a perspective you didnt have. It's only a minor thing, I know but I just found it kinda silly as a stat to have a mark of every misplay it thinks you made, even on flawless runs of the challenges lol. Theres also the intentional 'misplays' you'll make trying to rack up extra kill points so you can get more pelts and use a trader during some runs and what not too. But thats a whole extra layer to it too lol.

Also spoiler here:



Theres still stuff to this game I've yet to learn. If you feed a ringworm to the fire guys, it actually removes them, for example. Theres weird mechanics like that hiding inside of this masterful side of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuch a disappointing purchase after how interesting the demo was made to be.

The game that is advertised in both demo and trailers, screenshots, and everything else is about 4 hours long. What you play in the demo is a good portion of that which you are playing again. Which even that could have been okay if the gameplay loop wasn't incredibly repetitive in itself. Usually rogue-likes/lites are designed in a way that even after you lose you get to go into the next run with something exciting that makes you feel progressed. The next-run-benefit of Inscryption is Death Cards and one of the bosses giving a board mechanic to use If you don't get the death card you wanted (or its awful from a bad run) you really just go into the next run without much of anything.

The cabin puzzles were also a great idea but I had everything solved within the first 4-5 resets. Again, most of what you see in the demo is what you're getting in that regard with a few extras sprinkled from progressing farther in.

At the end of it you are given New Game+ which is something else entirely. I lasted about 30 minutes of the 8-bit graphics and music before I felt like I got bait and switched.

TLDR - Top value of this game is about 5$. This is a real sad thing to say considering how many good titles Devolver Digital puts out at the 20$ price point with way more interesting content.\t\t\t\t\t
