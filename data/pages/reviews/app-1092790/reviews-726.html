\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter about 10 hours there is a moment where the game tells you it is going to bring up your hard drive on the screen. There isn't a "no" option. You can either Alt + f4 and uninstall, or you can give it permission to bring up your actual hard drive and display it on screen. I was streaming at the time. Nothing bad happened, but I think the game should come with some kind warning. This wasn't cool at all. It was a stupid attempt at immersion and contributed exactly nothing positive to the game experience. I am pissed enough that I uninstalled.

The game itself is not scary but rather a charming (at first) horror themed card game. The card game is pretty basic. It only saves itself from being redundant by adding a strange meta experience.

Maybe the game gets better or worse, but after accessing my hard drive and displaying it without my permission I was done. I don't want to find out what other wonderful immersion elements they added.

Maybe something like: "Click here to delete all photos from your PC" oohhhhh spooky. Or just a bad move.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first started playing Inscryption, I tried to guess what game the mysterious man holding the deck of cards was going to play with me. So I guessed, "Go fish" and he just stared. Next I said, "Fold" but he again didn't do anything again. It wasn't until is asked him to "Yahtzee" did I realize that I was in for a wild ride. And it wasn't until I unlocked the cabinet containing the ant did I realize that this was probably tied to some stupid ARG on the internet somewhere. I know this because the Weasel card keeps talking to me in morse code. I'm afraid the other cards are going to start doing it too and I won't be able to hear what the mysterious man is trying to say to me over the erratic beeps and pauses. Steam describes this game as "story rich." All I gathered is that a man trapped me in his house to play cards and take selfies with him, and then a robot did the same thing. Maybe I should play the ARG to find out the rest of the story. However, I'm warning you. The second I hear anything about the abandoned disk of an old video game created by a forgotten developer who died by drowning in a lake, I'M GIVING YOUR GAME A 25 ON METACRITIC!

7/10\t\t\t\t\t
