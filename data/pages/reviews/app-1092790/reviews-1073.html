\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: This game is really good, and you should definitively play it, blind if you can. However expect to put in a bit of work.

As stated above, this game is really good. The card games you play allow for some really fun and creative combinations of cards and abilities. I went into this game almost completely blind, knowing very little about it. This makes the stuff the game throws at you, all the more satisfying; and it throws a lot at you.

However, I would personally have liked to have been warned about the following before I set my expectations and started the game.
It requires a decent amount of work. You may be thinking: "Obviously. It's a card game with a big ruleset"
To avoid spoiling anything; all I'll say is that there are more rules than you think.

A non spoiler-ish tips, regarding my warning
When given the option, I recommend choosing the deck you're familiar with. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably one of the most interesting indie game I've seen in a while. I found it through a Markiplier play-through and was stunned by the graphics, so as an aspiring game artist I had to check it out for myself. I wasn't sure I'd like it, since I have no experience with card games like this, but it was suprisingly easy to get the hang of it (in the most basic sense of the phrase; I still miss a lot of stuff, haha). Plus, after playing it, I found so much more to love! The soundtrack is amazing, the environment is stunningly stylized, and the game-play evolves as you go to keep things interesting. And of course, the story is amazing and takes a lot of unexpected turns. I'm on my second play-through now in hopes of uncovering all its hidden secrets - and, if nothing else, to just re-experience the game. I honestly wish there was a bigger fanbase for this game so I have people to geek out with, so please check this game out if you like puzzle-solving, dark storytelling, stimulating game-play and unhinged old people.\t\t\t\t\t
