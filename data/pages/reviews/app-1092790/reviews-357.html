\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Thanks goes to Youtube gamer, 8-Bit Ryan, for introducing the game on his channel!

Each time when I thought I beat the game, there's still more to the mystery to solve, the world opens up more. And now having finished the game once for sure, I'm replaying the whole thing again, because I want to know if anything has changed within the game world and its characters (and if I can't beat it again with handicaps this time).

This is odd for me, because I usually am not inclined to replay games, or card-based games (I blame Witcher 3 for making Gwent part of the gameplay and opening my eyes).

This game has a very, very well done concept, horror/mystery story, and game mechanic that lends itself to very interesting types of decks, while allowing you to make a rich amount of customization to your cards and tools to change up your strategy in the face of changing enemies. While I died way very often, it becomes extremely empowering once I figure out how to overcome challengers even with a weaker deck. I literally beat the first half of Act 1 with an unending amount of squirrels!!

You can also exit the game anytime outside of a card battle, so take a break is not an issue. While I'm not too sure about the main gameplay of Act 3 being as good, it might because I really got my butt kicked A LOT in Act 1, so I might have gotten way better by Act 3 to handle it.

Do consider picking up the game and work towards at least beat Act 1. I can't get into too much deal without spoiling the surprise and unexpected changes. Just remember to experiment a lot, learn what your tools can do and think outside the box!\t\t\t\t\t

Hard Recommend.
I've barely played this game, but can already tell it's genuinely amazing and worth the hype. I've also never really played any games like it, nor did I think I would enjoy them - I was wrong.

The level of detail and care for its design, exemplary in its own right and wildly refreshing coming from a lot of AAA and AA titles, it feels revolutionary honestly, even though smaller devs have been doing this for years. Some could say it's graphically questionable, and usually I'm deterred by retroism, pixel art etc., but I admit this game is beautiful. The sound design is not what I'd expect, and that makes it somehow so much better. The annoying thing is, you'll see people be careful to describe the game in any way, for a reason. It's more of an experience than a game to me, I was so caught up in it all that I didn't start to genuinely try (think very carefully about my actions) until 2 hours in when I realised I would have to, to even continue.

I encourage you to buy this game and try it for two hours - even without discount, even if it's not your type of thing (that was me). I absolutely adored my first two hours more than any recent game I can remember, and I can only hope it has the same level of amazement in store for me in the hours to come.


SPOILERS: THINGS I ENJOYED IN MY FIRST 220 MINUTES
ONLY FOR PEOPLE WHO HAVE PLAYED THE GAME TO DEATH

  • Upon entering the game, I realised the menu was...different...and I knew this would be interesting
  • The animations immediately caught my eye, honestly beautiful how everything comes to rest on the table, or inversely, leave it.
  • Fighting the Prospector and the Angler for the first time, I felt an actual sense of dread from the music, I wondered if when the music suddenly jumped, it represented a bad play by myself - I still don't know.
  • I chose the glowing eye to replace it the first time, and found my vision modified in an interesting way, an avid indie game player might expect this, but I was wowed by the fact this simple thing happened, it's what caused me to stop and write my review.
