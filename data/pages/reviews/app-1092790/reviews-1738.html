\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn my many years of playing video games, there is a particular "mechanic", an aspect of games that never fails to make me moist... synergies. Powers working alongside other powers which create whole new powers or a game winning strategy. Abilities that work fine as they are, but which are suddenly transformed by the introduction of another ability. That's this game.

Inscryption is a a kind of roguelike card game. You (the player playing as a player playing a floppy disc version of the game?) are trapped within a darkened cabin, facing off with a pair of manic swirling eyes. With no clue as to what is going on, you follow along with the instructions the entity opposite is giving. These are very basic:
~ Attack or defend using your cards
~ Predator cards require sacrifices to play
~ Attacking an open space does damage points against your opponent
~ Enough damage points means a win

Once the tutorial is complete, your first playthrough is just for getting used to these initial simple rules. A game piece is moved along a procedurlally generated map with every victory, taking you along a path that leads to new cards, creatures. and characters. The game only truly starts opening up ...after you lose. Which you will. There is no choice. Your lives are represented by two burning candles. If both are snuffed out, you lose. New rules are added after you lose, exploration becomes more fruitful, there are more things for your captor to discuss with you. Eventually, you learn what's going on, and perhaps even how to end the cycle of games, death, and rebirth.

The things that really makes this game worth playing though are the sigils (abilities some cards have that make them behave differently) and totems (which give all cards of a specific creature type a sigil). You can have sigils that mean another card is created in your hand. Sigils that mean your card will protect itself from harm after your turn. Sigils that give cards buffs and debuffs that affect other cards on the board. And then there's the alter on the map... which allows you to sacrifice a card... and place that card's sigil onto another card! This is where the game really becomes something I want to sink hours into. I have managed to create a totem that meant squirrels (your typical prey card for blood sacrifices) allow me to pick any card from my deck when played. I've had the Stink sigil on incredibly powerful defensive cards, which means any opposing attacking card has it's attack power reduced by 1. You can apply the flight sigil to a card with ridiculously high attack power, meaning the card "flies above" any defending card and attacks your opponent directly for a sneaky win! There's a sigil that allows any card to attack in three different directions... If you had a card with 7 attack power attacking three spaces, thats 21 damage. OH, and, by the way, you can apply more than one sigil to a card... I chase the perfect card. The card that costs no blood or bone sacrifice, that never dies, that returns to my hand, and that attacks in all directions with a strength that increases.

I am Cpt. Ishmael, and the perfect card is my white whale.

P.S. - As an aside, overkill is a thing that you'll want to pay attention to. Looking at what cards your opponent has in-play, and what cards they will play next is crucial to working out how to maximise overkill. Doing this will allow you to purchase more valuable pelts from The Trapper, and trade those exotic pelts with The Trader for more powerful cards.\t\t\t\t\t
