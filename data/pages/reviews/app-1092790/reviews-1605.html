\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is exactly what it says in all of the tags. Its got a bit of everything in it. That said, the game itself is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ great. It retains all of the escape room elements throughout each act and doesn't skimp out on the gameplay at all.

The Kaycee mod is exactly what everybody wanted. An endlessly (not endless - there is a difference between the two that hardly anybody cares to acknowledge for some God awful reason.) replayable rogue-like mode in Act 1's rogue-lite experience.

With each successful clear, you unlocks more lore behind Kaycee's development process of the mod, talking about all the weird stuff she's experiencing, you get new starting decks, new challenges and more. There IS a very RNG reliant element that essentially dictates whether you win or lose depending on the encounters and the cards you are given to choose from. It can be very frustrating knowing you did everything within your power to fight back but it wasn't good enough because RNG says so. You play flawlessly and still lose and have to start a new run all over again.

Magpie and kingfisher show up A LOT so keep an eye out for them, get yourself a goat and put magpie's sigils on it and pick a grizzly and buff up the two like crazy. Visit the doctor as often as you can to get dupes of the wolf and grizzly and your odds of winning are essentially 100%. You can get a lot of quick and easy wins turn 1 if you have a small enough deck or enough magpie sacrifices. It can easily break the game and save your runs.

You can also quit mid fight before the map shows up to retry a fight if you have a good deck but don't want to throw away a good run due to bad RNG. This is recommended if you're getting frustrated by RNG walling your progress. Just try not to abuse it. That defeats the purpose of it being a challenging rogue-like.
It still has a long way to go to be the ideal rogue-like experience the game mode needs to be. I've never been one to defend rogue-likes being player friendly though, that's for sure. It appeals more to a certain audience than to the whole spectrum. But it is possible to make it appealing towards more than just a select audience. But that requires a major difficulty dip and a lot of balancing in the player's favor. Which is usually not the most fun. I want to earn my crushing victories. Getting defeated and not being able to do anything about it generally is a sh!tty feeling and can be a very frustrating experience. Again, do nothing wrong but still lose. Then again, that's TCGs in a nutshell. Top decking can be rough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA legitimate masterpiece. I have not played a game this good in a long time. I came into the game expecting a standard deck building rogue-like but it much more than that. It is a beautiful story, and the game really feels like an adventure. It took me under 12 hours to complete the game but I still have achievements to get and there is good replay ability with the game. There is a mini expansion with an endless mode in beta right now that will hopefully fully release soon.
I will for sure replay the game again a few more times. Even if your not into rogue-likes or card games, this is still a must buy in my opinion, its a great story driven adventure.
The game is kind of hard to explain, but in short, there is amazing visuals, attention to detail and great characters and narrative, replay value and more.\t\t\t\t\t
