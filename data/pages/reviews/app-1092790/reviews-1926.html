\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a card game, but it's more than a card game, but it's also just a card game.

I don't even like card games. Yet this one? This one I loved.

It's relatively short, it's concise, it's easy to pick up. It rewards experimentation, the atmosphere and writing is perfect, and it not only reaches for big and bold things, it lands them all perfectly when it'd be so easy to stumble and fail on any one.

The only thing I can ever say is to go in blind- let all the surprises happen. Play the demo and let it hook you.

I wish I'd played it sooner, I'd seen friends talk but saw it was a card game and didn't think much of it. I came back when I saw the reviews were all good, that it was by Daniel Mullins Games (Pony Island!), and there was a demo that I could at least try it out in case this card game was different.

I'm glad I did, because it was.

There are a scant few games I think about constantly, keep coming back to, and recommend breathlessly and annoyingly to friends. Inscryption is one of them.

I'm sorry the review is a little vague and feelings-heavy, I'm sure plenty of other reviews will go more into detail. But it's a special game that knows exactly what it wants to get across, keeps you interested, shakes things up, hints at deeper mysteries, and then ends in complete satisfaction.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has been a wild ride from start to finish.

The gameplay, as it's core, is a card dueling game, with roguelike elements, You not only add new cards to your deck, but also alter them in interesting ways, such as sacrificing one card to grant it's special effect to another, or combining two of the same card into one, stronger version. This is the core gameplay loop, however, aside from that, there is so much more to the game. You explore environments, in a very classic dungeon crawling type of control mode, and solve little puzzles, almost like an escape room, which will open new lore and mechanics and advance the story.

The visual and audio design. They are amazing and easily among the most unique and interesting I have experienced. Every looks, feels and sounds very satisfying. The animations, the card arts, the board, the effects...

The tone of the game. The game has a very dark setting to start off with, and also has elements of psychological horror, in various forms. I would like to keep this spoilerfree, so I can unfortunately not elaborate on it fully, but within the first minutes of gameplay this will become apparent. You can't start a new game, only continue, and when you do, you find yourself in a dark cabin, with a shadowy figure across from you at a table. That said, it also has some hilarious writing. It isn't all doom and gloom.

------SPOILERS BELOW-------

Now, my main gripe with this game.... the live action video bits. I could have done without those. They are not badly done, but at best I feel like they don't add a whole lot, and at worst, they personally take me out of the game a lot. There is a story to them, and while I found the story of the game itself very interesting, the tie-in live action recordings kinda boiled down to... quite literally, the haunted-game-from-a-garage-sale creepypasta trope. I could take it in stride until it was made clear that you, who has so far just been you, in a first person perspective(even getting asked your name to put on a card) was actually this dude from the live action videos all along. It kinda ruined the ending for me a lot.

-------SPOILERS OVER-------

Nevertheless, this was an amazing experience that I kinda stumbled upon. A real hidden gem this was and I finished it in about 2 sittings, which is rare for me!\t\t\t\t\t
