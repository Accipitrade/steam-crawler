\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh, I know I'm just another voice in the pool of people that recommend this game, but I appreciate the genre bending, the meta aspects of it, and the fact that for a horror game, it isn't really horrifying... just intriguing. The core mechanic is card battling and deck building, and the core mechanic is generally fun (occasionally frustrating when you don't get the cards you want, but that's deck building for you), but the rest of the game is cleverly done. I feel it loses steam a little bit near the end and begins to feel a bit more linear than the beginning is, but I also think I may have rushed some of the end and probably didn't find all the secrets that were available. So maybe I just need to replay it... which I'm happy to do!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWent in totally blind to play this the weekend of halloween. This was one of the best games I've played over the past 3ish years. Mechanics are very fun, there is a fun mix of learning and experimenting, the story was entertaining, a lot of the actions are very satisfying (sound design, the way the cards move, some of the horror-esq character conversations). Just awesome, one of those games I will wish I could play and experience for the first time again.\t\t\t\t\t

The game forces you to die over and over while "coming back stronger" which is the most annoying thing I've seen in this game.
You get punished very harshly for a single mistake and all fights have to be precisely calculated, while the draw of cards is purely random.
Having bigger decks means you are ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up already and the more cards you have, the more ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up you are.
Dying makes you have to play all the hidden mini-games again to have this "slightly early game boost" so you won't cry much.
The game menu is very confusing, although you will get used to it. It is a blend of 80/90's classic 8 bit menu but then in game it is completely different, something terror/horror with tons of after effects like film grain and chroma aberration. Which is the first game that I kinda like and this games makes it work nicely...
But the way the game is designed is very bad.
Most people will say good things about the game, but be aware that patience is the only key thing here, because no matter how good you are, RNG is always against you, and you will die over and over again until you have cards so strong that the enemy can't count you anymore. That alone is the biggest flaw, because it doesn't matter if you have skill or not.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought Pony Island and The Hex without knowing anything about the developer or the connection between these games. The bundle was on sale, and I saw the reviews were good, so I bought them. Pony Island was short, so I finished it quickly, and thought: "OK, the setting was probably novel when it first came out years ago, but nowadays, it's just cliché. Nevertheless, it's a short and interesting time filler." Then I played The Hex, interested in knowing what the developer came up next. While I found the mini games mostly boring and dragged out, towards the end, the story picked up steam, which made the whole experience worthwhile despite the flaws. Finally, I played Inscryption. It is like the combination of the two previous games, but crank everything up to 100 while still being fresh and novel, with the perfecting of gameplay and art style. It is indeed once in a lifetime experience, no matter how many games you've played. I have nothing but admiration for the developer.\t\t\t\t\t
