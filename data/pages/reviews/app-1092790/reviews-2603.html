\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving just wrapped up my full playthrough, the short answer is I was extremely impressed with this game, but will caution it's not a Slay the Spire / Monster Train 20 tier deckbuilder. (Reviewer background, I'm Ascension 20/19/20/15 on StS and Covenant 20+ on Monster Train).

Having played both of Daniel Mullen's other games (Pony Island & The Hex), I was braced for a weird, meta-type game that would combine a variety of both serious and trolling game styles mixed into a strange mystery/story-- Inscryption pulls this off beautifully.

About half of my playtime was learning to survive Act 1 (the heavily featured/advertised StS style roguelight card brawler, mixed with Escape-Room esque developments)-- I had a great run and finally blitzed through the 3rd and 4th stages for the first time, only to stumble into a very different Act 2-- and I was happy for the change-up, personally.

I will acknowledge that the difficulty curve on the following act(s) felt much lower by comparison (Act 2 allows for retries on battles though I rarely lost, and I honestly don't know what happens if you die in Act 3.. it rarely was a close match), but they remained interesting and carried my interest until the end.

TLDR-- don't buy this game because you want a 60+ hr ascent through rising difficulties-- play it because there are few games able to pack so much interesting meta-content in 10 hrs while also having well-tuned mechanics to sustain the ride.\t\t\t\t\t
