\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Potential Spoilers*

Inscryption was probably my favorite game of 2021. The first section takes cues from roguelikes, deckbuilding, mysteries, and (strangely) escape rooms. The loop is fun and creative, and the permanent feeling of unease addicts you to the feedback loop. If we were looking at the first section of the game in a vacuum, it would be one of my favorite games in years.

But we're not looking at just the first part. Taking into account that parts 2 and 3 exist, the game feels a bit disjointed. I don't think I'd have enjoyed the game if all three main sections were skinned to play like part two. That doesn't tank the game, but it does beg the question of why chapter exists if it forces the player to consider whether or not said chapter ruins the whole of the experience. It doesn't ruin the experience, but I definitely caught myself wondering if it was worth finishing the game. (It is worth it, though.)

To make an analogy, it's like if you ordered neapolitan ice cream and the flavors were chocolate, vanilla, and a copy of Bjork's greatest hits. Surely it appeals to SOMEONE, but you ask yourself why someone put a CD in your ice cream in the first place.\t\t\t\t\t
