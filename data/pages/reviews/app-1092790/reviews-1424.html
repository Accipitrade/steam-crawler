\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI must be missing something...

I downloaded this game after hearing about it as a game of the year contender on the podcast Besties. I absolutely love Slay the Spire and wanted to check out more deck building style games. I knew going into the game that there were multiple acts and the game play would change in Act 2. But the reviews led me to believe this would be a mind-blowing game....it was not.

Act 1 has some great features and I real enjoyed this portion! The mechanics were fun and I enjoyed the puzzle aspects of the room escape as well. The story even had me hooked enough to keep me going. I wouldn't say it was incredible, but overall, I would recommend this portion of the game.

From there, the game become boring and laborious. It felt disjointed with no real goal in mind. The graphics and cards were hard to follow and hard to see. I felt like I was just going through the motions without understanding. The story is what kept me going at this point; I really wanted to find out what happened! But that was a huge let down as well. I am still left wondering what actually happened.

If you have disposable income and are curious, I think the first part of the game is worth it. But once you get to the next part of the game, do not feel obligated to keep going just to get to a resolution...you won't find one.\t\t\t\t\t
