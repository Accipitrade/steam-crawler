\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty neat game overall if you're into battle card games like Hearthstone or MTG. As a warning, the card game campaign is generally a tool to propel you through a mystery suspense narrative, but there's still a fun game throughout it all. The game unlocks more features and introduces changes over time, but the game steadily gets more and more complex, introducing new abilities, archetypes, resources, etc. Where I personally think the game shines is in its boss battles, where your opponents use powers that can affect the board and game as a whole. I don't want to spoil anything, but they are pretty fun and definitely take advantage of it being a video game instead of a physical card game, some are really unique.

The game definitely requires thinking, but you unlock stuff over time, so if you suck, you'll eventually get snowballed with powers that are in your favor. There's a lot of customization too, such as power ups that let you add new abilities to your card or generally control your deck setup, so it's a pretty fluid and fun experience. I did beat it in around 10-ish hours though, so keep that in mind if you're worried about price/gameplay ratio. There's definitely some optional stuff that I missed and could go back to do, but I definitely saw most of it. Still really enjoyed my time with it, keep an eye on this game!\t\t\t\t\t
