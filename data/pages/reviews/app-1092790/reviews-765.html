\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpoiler free review here: this game has two parts. First part is more of an escape room experience mixed with a card game. The game knows how to build atmosphere, the sound track is inspired and thematic and game play mechanics also work to immerse you into the game that much more. I imagine at times it can be punishing for people new to video games but if you understand the basics of card building games, you'll fall in love with the system and even find out how to exploit the game soon enough. The second part is taking the mechanics of the first and turning it into a challenging rogue-like that genuinely tests your skills. It's still a little broken but because of the player vs computer/GM aspect of things, it almost feels like you found a secret the developer didn't think you'd get to. I guarantee if you want a game like FTL, Into the Breach that steals hours and hours from your life because of how addictive it is but can also be in the background of you doing other things, buy this game. Sooner or later you will fall in love with it's mechanics, music and atmosphere. One of the best purchases I've made without a shadow of a doubt.\t\t\t\t\t

Like most other people, I played this game as a standard game and enjoyed it a lot, even if I was left a little perplexed at the end.
After seeing what I felt was clues to nothing within the game (ciphers, binary...etc), I decided to google and it turns out this was indeed a whole ARG real world puzzle in a similar style to many Defcon badge puzzles you may have heard of from the yearly hacker conference in Vegas.

Sadly by the time I had finished the game, this had been solved by what I can only imagine is a group of very smart people over in (the official?) discord server.
I hope another game comes along at some stage and I can be there from the start!

The game was great, but the ARG element was super impressive, even just reading DaBigKahuna's writeup on google docs.

Recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
