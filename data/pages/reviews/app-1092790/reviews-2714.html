READ BEFORE BUYING

Following Daniel Mullins' legacy of games that subvert expectations, Inscryption is NOT a typical rogue-like game.
It may appear that way at first, but (without spoiling anything) the game's focus shifts into meta-storytelling the more you play, losing some of the core mechanics that make it a rogue-like.

The card battles and deck-building remain throughout the experience, and they are amazing, but if you're mainly here for a rogue-like, be warned that this might not be the game for you.

I personally didn't mind the deviation, because I was interested in the story that the game had to tell, but that doesn't apply to everyone. I've seen many people say that they felt 'cheated' that the game wasn't what they thought it was from the trailer and the demo, and I can certainly see why.

In my opinion, there is not that much replay value after you beat the game besides looking for extra secrets. There simply isn't enough variety in each run to warrant pouring hundreds of hours into like The Binding of Isaac or Slay the Spire.

Still a great game, but if you're looking to buy it might not be the best idea to go in blind if you're exclusively wanting the gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
