Normally, you would imagine that this would be a negative, but in this game's case it is a huge positive. It helps builds the mystery and atmosphere quite nicely and kept me immersed for... a good while

Sound Effects: [9/10] I can not express enough how amazing the sound design for this game is. All of the sound effects are impact and leave you truly immersed. Has this game is on the horror side, the sound effects came in full force laying a creepy atmosphere through out. It is hands down the best part of the game and the developers did a wonderful job.

Visuals: [6.5/10] SPOILER WARNING The art style for this game is what drew me in to begin with and what I was surprised to find out is that what was shown in all of the promotional art, wasn't all that there was to offer. The art style for this game changes partially throughout the game where I loved some aspects but absolutely hated others. Roughly 2/3 of the game features stellar art, but there is 1/3 of the game where I just found as an eye sour. But in the end, the art that I liked stood all on its own and took the weight off of the small bits that I didn't enjoy.

Negatives:

Gameplay: [4/10] The game might seem difficult at first, it reality it is quite simple. Once you understand the base level mechanics of the game, everything else quickly falls into place that it doesn't pose a challenge before you realize you got to the boss fights. I enjoy card games and I thought that the sacrifice mechanics in this game would make it challenging and exciting. But there are so many things to exploit that what at first seemed like a hindrance ends up being irrelevant. In almost all of my runs, I quickly ended up having unlimited energy sources.

I'm sure people who don't care about stuff being difficult won't mind, but I enjoy difficult games, and this game was not hard.

Narrative: [1/10] MAJOR SPOILERS

Ah, the narrative. This game is story centric for a large part of it, which is sad because I never found myself enjoying it. There are a lot of meta video games where the characters are "alive" and responding to your actions with meta commentary so you have to be real original in the modern era if you want your story to feel unique. This is where Inscryption fails. This game follows the "meta" concept of characters in the game know they are fictional, mess with game files, make it scary like it will effect you irl. Done. This has been done countless times in the past that it just doesn't do it for me anymore.

The game tries to be original by hinting at a big secret hidden beneath it all, but that secret, is extremely lacking in content.

In the end I played this game to the end, but I didn't enjoy it by the end, to me, it felt like a chore.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is an incredible game, an expertly crafted game where narrative and mechanics are woven together. I can probably say it's the best game I've played this year (and we're in December).

Inscryption is a deck-building card game with an overarching creepy theme. I will say as little as possible about the story, but I can definitely say this is a plot-driven game where a key factor is the allure of mystery and the wanting to get to the bottom of things.
The card mechanics are a love letter to popular card games, where their mechanics were mixed and matched to create a very fun (if a bit imbalanced) card game which is super easy to learn but gradually becomes more and more complex.
This is definitely a subversive game, where players are encouraged and are constantly rewarded for exploring and looking around. I struggle to think of games more rewarding for exploration.

I did have a few gripes with this game, but I will not list them here as they fall under the spoiler category. All I'll say is that they're minuscule in comparison to how fun, interesting and thought-provoking this game is.

This is a must-play for fans of deck-building games or mystery fans. Only thing I can think of in this game that will scare some away is if they hate spooky games.\t\t\t\t\t
