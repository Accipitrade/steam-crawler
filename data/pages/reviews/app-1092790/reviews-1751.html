\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rarely become as emotionally invested in a game and story as I did with Inscryption.

Go in blind. Don't read comments, don't look at the community content. Don't watch a playthrough, or read a wiki. just...play. It is an experience of a rare kind.

That being said, Inscryption is a game about a horror filled journey of card matches where you build your deck and try to survive, and as is to be expected from the creator of "Pony Island," not everything is as it seems. Between rounds of card battles, you get a chance to explore your environment and unlock the story piece by piece. Some things are easy, some parts of the story are not obvious. As the story progresses, so does the game mechanics, and the game world is never quite just what you'd think.

I had tears in my eyes as the game concluded, a rare feat for any game to achieve. Unlike may games with a rich story and cast, the game felt complete, and I am quite satisfied with the whole experience.
