\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is good, but overall, it feels just a little bit less than the sum of its parts. The card game is amazing, the 3d and pixel art visuals are stunning and unique, and the characters both in the fictional game and out are compelling. Some of these things just didn't gel. For starters, if you are looking for a game to scare you, this isn't it. Yes, fear is subjective, but the bits that were supposed to be overtly scary, like your captors boss battles, just didn't do much. This might partially be because after the first time you die, you know that your punishment for death is gonna be weird hands grabbing you and a scene where you get to create a card. I could recommend this game on the card mechanics alone, but I was hoping for a little more depth on the story side of things, and while the video sections were cool, they more gestured at a greater story rather than telling it. I hope this developer does something with the card game alone on this one, but until then, it's worth the $18-20.\t\t\t\t\t
