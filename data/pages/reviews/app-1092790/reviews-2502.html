\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a good card battler rogue-lite in the same vein as Slay the Spire, however approached in more of a single-player complete game, with a spooky-ish story.

You'll battle many enemies and bosses, each with unique mechanics and abilities, and all while searching for clues on where the story will go next. If you die, you're back to the start or a checkpoint to go at it again, usually with more knowledge or boons to continue on.

Without revealing too much, the only complaint to be had is that the first portion of the game isn't a standalone or longer version. The music, effects, sense of horror, and fear of the unknown is truly amazing, and I wish that it could have been made even longer.

The game changes pace a few times throughout the story, and builds on or replaces certain themes throughout. This culminates in an ending that is indeed worth the multiple hours that can be invested in the game simply completing it once.

The story is very entertaining, and the ending again reminds you of the first portion of the game that really hooks the player from the beginning. Characters that you meet or see throughout the journey frequently pop up, making the world feel alive and coherent.

Because the game had me hooked until the end, and because the ending sequence is jaw-dropping and leaves you wanting more, I give the game a solid 8/10.
It has replayability per se, but if this game's success shows anything, it's that this playstyle is fun, addictive, and worthwhile of a sequel or further updates.

Definitely worth $20.\t\t\t\t\t
