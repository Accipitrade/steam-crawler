\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game after early recommendations from a friend and the fact that the reviews appeared really positive. From what I gathered, this was a rogue like deck-building game with a horror them. As I am one to enjoy the spooks in October, I figured it would be a great choice.

**Spoilers ahead**

The game starts out great; you have this creepy pair of eyes telling your story while you build a deck, add and remove cards, power them up, etc. Basically, all the usual stuff you see in games like Slay the Spire or Monster Train. Heck, there's even some really cool Easter eggs in the cabin where you can use clues to unlock other cards to use. It took me a few tries to get the hang of everything, and I was eventually lucky enough to create this OP infinite squirrel deck to summon giant monsters. At that point I steamrolled everything but it was super satisfying.

After that, you're shown this screen with a bunch of video clips of a guy opening packs of cards, and then digging up a floppy disk with the Inscryption game on it. I guess this is where the developer wanted to add some more story elements. However, the game, in my opinion, completely falls apart at this point.

What happens is this: instead of the spooky rogue like setting from before, you are now in this 8-bit, top-down RPG setting. You have to fight 4 bosses while finding packs of cards to build decks, while completing little puzzles throughout. At first I didn't think much of it. I assumed it was a short section to try mixing things up a bit, but what you get instead is one of the most brutal, dragging experiences I've ever had in a game.

I need to preface this by saying that I am no deck building expert, but this section was one of the most infuriating experiences I have had with a video game. You get to pick one of four themed decks and acquire cards by beating enemies or finding them in chests, but the cards are random. It was probably due to really bad luck, but I had a really difficult time developing any good synergy with the cards I had. I was able to beat the regular guys in a few tries, but ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ are the bosses frustrating. I probably attempted one of them at least 50 times before I actually got the RNG to favor me, and most of the cards seemed borderline useless, especially once you get Mantis Gods in your deck and can just triple hit the board over and over once you've figured it out. But even after "solving" the bosses, I felt that it dragged on way too long. I uninstalled the game before even finishing the final guy because I was frankly tired of it at that point, and the story wasn't interesting enough for me to continue.

I think the worst part is that I feel cheated. I understand this developer is known for making unconventional games like this, but literally almost all of the advertising I had seen for this game framed it as a rogue-like deck builder with a ton of replayability, but from what my friend told me, there's really not much of a point to replay the game. You can replay certain acts, but you can't just simply replay the rogue-like part at the beginning, which is the part I liked the best and envisioned spending a ton of time on.

If this game was solely the first act in the cabin, this would be a top gaming experience for me, but I personally cannot recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, this is my first game review I think. I played the demo and absolutely loved the game I purchased basically halfway through it and believed a lot of the great reviews here.

Spoiler free TLDR; game just changes after act 1 into something completely different from the demo and what the store page is advertising. I'm not saying new mechanics are added, or that gameplay is switched up, the rug is literally pulled from under your feet and you are left struggling through a tedious and boring game hoping you will eventually get back to the magic of Act 1 but you don't. also zero replayability so if you were looking for a deck building roguelike to sink more than 10 hours in this is not the game for you.

*****Spoilers ahead so please do not read if you don't want to see:******

So on to the substantive criticism. The game does what it advertised SUPER well in the first act. as i said i purchased like 30 minutes into the demo because the atmosphere, gameplay, and roguelike elements keep you hooked and doing run after run trying to beat Leshy.

However, after you beat him and you're presented with the New game option a part of you is excited as you are hoping that now you get a Leshy card in your deck and you replay with what you have in possibly a much more difficult version of the first Act. especially if you did a couple runs and found some game breaking card combos.

But no, you are thrust into a boring 8 bit world where you start with a crappy deck and fighting through it is miserable and is 2-3x as long as the first act. The cards you get suck and all of the roguelike elements from the first act are completely gone as you go and battle 4 bosses and their minions. this last an INCREDIBLE amount of time and most of your battles are just won because of RNG or you were able to buy 6 mantis gods from a trader and just breeze through most battles.

Then comes Act two and you are back in a 3D world and you're like FINALLY we are back to the main game. Things arguably do get better after this point. But that is only by comparison to the slog that is Act 2. you face off in a new monotonous board in yet another completely linear progression to fight another 4 bosses. you get a single deck and the new boring mechanics from Act 2 are carried over. you get some power ups and shops but the whole thing just feels meh. You can get up and solve some of the puzzles around the area but why would you. It's this drab blue and gray environment, no longer creepy and the NPC is incredibly boring. There are some cool mechanics like the NPC reading your hard drive and files and adding your steam friends as cards. but those are just two second blips of wonder in a boring and incredibly long 3rd act.

You finally finish it, and make it through the awful found footage aspect and the game ends. You play a fraction of the boards from the other two NPCs which are awesome for the 30 seconds they last. Then the game ends and you have a choice to just replay any act from there. But again, you already know how to cheese the first act so starting over is just a waste of time.

I sat with this for a day, and i thought about my feelings and if i just played the demo i would love it. But 2/3s of a game being awful and pining for the beginning just doesn't seem right. I get the developer was going for some rug pull, but it lands so flat, makes the store page seem borderline scammy as you do not at all get the experience you pay for, and there is zero replayability with the core mechanics that make Act 1 so amazing.

Seriously, just play the demo and go on with whatever other games are out there. I would consider changing my review if they added a sandbox or endless mode to this. Where you can fight the 4 bosses on their boards with the rogue like elements of Act 1, or even just an endless mode of act 1 where you can keep re-rolling and playing against harder and harder bosses/game difficulty. but it is clear that was not the vision for the game so i don't see that coming true.

Sad to say i really cannot recommend this game at all.


UPDATE: While i cannot change my mind on the above, it just really wasn't the game for me and I did not enjoy 2/3s of that playthrough. Maybe ARGs just aren't my thing and it wasn't clear going in that this was the case.

BUT, the dev has released a free expansion that turns the cabin into an endless roguelike. I've been sinking hours into this and it was the one big thing I was upset with! So i have to change my review to recommend, for this price point i would pay that amount just for Leshy's cabin so I am satisfied!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a very good game - if you know the dev, you know that things aren't always what they seem at face value (a card game roguelite). The card game itself though is surprisingly enjoyable as a means of moving the narrative along. New mechanics are introduced at the perfect pace to keep things fresh and interesting the whole way through.

My one complaint is that the tone/mood/setting of the game changes abruptly a few hours in. I didn't dislike where the game went - it was enjoyable the whole way through. However I think if Inscryption had instead doubled down on the "cabin in the woods" horror setting that it opens with and uses in marketing... I would've really liked to see how that game would've been. I don't know. The game I played was very good, but I'm mourning the game that it wasn't.

Liked it a lot better than Pony Island which I found a bit shallow. There's a lot more meat on the bones here.\t\t\t\t\t
