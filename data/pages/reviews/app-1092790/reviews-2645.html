\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery much like a previous title from the same developer, Pony Island.

If you buy this, you're getting an interesting experience much more so than a deck building game.
It took me 12 hours to finish and there's probably another five or so in replayability to finish off secrets and the like, but I think it'll be a slog.

For the length, I think the game is somewhat overpriced. At some point you understand what the game is about and go 'I get it' and it gets annoying when you don't really get to focus on 'your deck' as much as being forced to go through numerous ones to play the game the game wants you to play.

3 pieces of advice:
1. Overkill on a fight means currency. This stays consistent and is important.
2. Losing is not punishment, rather it's quicker progression.
3. When you have a choice between four decks, go with the one you know (The first one - Beasts). The second deck can be a little irritating to play with.

It's not a whole hearted recommendation. It has fun ideas, but it takes itself too seriously in my opinion and the mechanics aren't as good as the developer might think they are.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCreepy, eerie, but most of all... INTRIGUING comes a horror rogue-like card game. As a fan of rogue-like card games and this studio's previous work, I knew I had to try this game out!

I'm still going through it, but overall I'm very happy with the complexity of the card game. Despite the horror genre (which often leads to simplified gameplay), there's enough in the card games where there's always a new mechanic to learn. Be warned; although it is a horror game, there's a lot of card-related gameplay. You better be ready to throwdown a lot! I also believe your skill in card games can dramatically affect the length of the game.

The storytelling and environmental effects are incredibly immersive; the overall theme has me returning for more. As for the horror aspect, I don't play too many horror games since I am very bad with jumpscares. This game focuses more on environment and themes in order to invoke uneasiness; which is right up my alley!

Conclusion:
- Moderate complexity in card gameplay
- Great storytelling and visuals
- No jumpscares (yet!), but still very horror-esque
- Super worth it for a horror-loving, card-slinging nerd like me\t\t\t\t\t
