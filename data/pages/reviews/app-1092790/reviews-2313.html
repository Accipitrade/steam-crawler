\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredibly surprising game. I'm not usually the type for Card games of sorts, but this one's very impressive with its atmosphere and delivery. Even though its separated into 3 acts with varying gameplay, each is fun and offers different challenges (Part 1 is definitely the best for sure).

For Part 1, feels like I could play it for hours. The card gameplay is fantastic. The horror and uncomfortable atmosphere was amazing and really felt unnerving. Part 2 was nice, but didn't feel like much of a challenge as all stakes are removed entirely. Part 3 was a good blend of the first two parts, but felt lacking in terms of challenge (especially the two final bosses). It felt like the game should've had a sort-of scaling difficulty built in more as all parts have certain aspects that allow you to give yourself massively overpowered literal trump cards that'll let you completely breeze through.

The lore is definitely a bit awkward as it slightly ties to other somewhat-unrelated titles by the same dev, but the actual game characters themselves are really well-made, especially the first one you meet. The ending of the game itself (but not the actual full end) was bittersweet and definitely left me wanting a whole lot more.

I'd absolutely like to see this game get an extended version of the game more focused on the characters you face rather than the lore surrounding the creation of the game and our unfortunate friend who learns about it.

Vote for this in The Game Awards for sure, it deserves the recognition.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI do recommend this game since I found the game ITSELF to be enjoyable. I love the music, the aesthetic, and the game play.

BUT (spoilers again...)



The fact that the game leads to an ARG that requires you to know things about the game's creator, previous games and even kickstarter games he's made... was a real disappointment for me. I am sure Daniel Mullins' fans loved this since it seems it's part of the overall universe of his games, but as someone who had never heard of him before and was recommended this game, it was a let down that I couldn't get the "whole game" just from the game itself. honestly? I thought I was buying a video game and not an ARG. I know that you could play it without the ARG and it's fine - that's why I still recommend it. And this is coming from someone who loves ARGs! But there is no way I would have solved it on my own since it required prior knowledge of his other games! And honestly... I didn't find the ARG parts of it... that's interesting. Maybe because I wasn't actively involved in the puzzle solving, but that's also the problem. ARGs are time sensative. If you aren't in on it from day 1, then who gives a ♥♥♥♥, you get to see someone else reveal all the puzzles for you. I loved the game, I loved the atmosphere. I wish there was more IN the game itself!

So if you like card games... definitely play it, but don't be too hopeful for a good ending that answers all your questions unless you want to read the documents on Daniel Mullins' discord server.\t\t\t\t\t
