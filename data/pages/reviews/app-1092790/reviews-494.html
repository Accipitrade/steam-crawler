\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played Inscryption for the first time in December of 2021, streaming it to a friend who had already completed the game. He kept hyping up a final boss but the world seemed to steadily decrease in stability as the "story" went on. It's not as big as a "I have to install this now!" game as it was around that time period, but I'll still try to avoid spoilers in this review.

Long story short, Inscryption is genuinely THE best game I've ever played. Not because of gameplay alone, or characters alone, or anything that might be described to say the best game of some certain genre.

Gameplay wise, this game is tough. You will lose. The game makes you think long and hard about each play you make, and employs the use of different distractions (such as dialogue) to make you question whether you made the right play. Dying and respawning is part of the game, a full mechanic, not just simply a respawn as it is in practically every other game.

Characters wise, they are absolutely stunning. Each one is compelling and beautiful. While it may seem bleak in characters from the beginning, as the game goes on you will meet so many more faces each with their own tastefully developed personalities and backstories.

Story wise. This is a big one for me, as I aspire to be so many things that somehow always circle back to making a good story that people can enjoy. Inscryption's story is breathtaking. I genuinely had to stop, get up, and walk outside after finishing this game. Not in the usual sense of "touch grass", of course. I had to go and appreciate the world around me. The plants, the animals, the sky, humanity. I had to breathe it all in and remember that life is omnipresent, it's always going to be something we're dealing with. Life, death, eternity. All from this little card game.

Seriously though, you need to play this if you haven't. It's even Devolver Digital.\t\t\t\t\t
