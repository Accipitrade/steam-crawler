\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played on the Mac beta. Depending on when you read this could still be running, could not, could be actually out. Definitely gotta play with your laptop plugged in cause most section will eat your battery alive.

This game is incredibly fun with the main story of it and I cannot recommend highly enough never watching anything beyond the first act of it if you intend to play it. I am surprised there is not a fan base similar to undertale with this game but it is a lot more mature audience targeted so I guess that is why. I don't want to spoil anything because the story is a huge draw of this.

Gameplay wise it is simple and you can get by just fine whether you are familiar with card games or not. I play a lot of card games but at times it still was a decent challenge, but not hard by any stretch.

The Kaycee's mod that comes built in is amazing and what really makes the glowing praise for me. If it was just the main story I would still recommend but I have the feeling the story mode would lose a lot of appeal on a second go around. My limited play time in the Kaycee's Mod is where I will be spending my time. Think the skulls modifier thing from Halo challenge runs.

TLDR: If the game was $60 I would be much more hesitant to recommend for everyone but at the $20 price its amazing and perfect for its cost. Took me around 11 hours to beat the story. I can see myself spending a lot more time in the Kaycee's Mod expansion thingy that is built in. I am mad at my friend who reccommened the game they didn't force me to play it earlier. 10/10 from me and a must play for people who love a good story and a deceptively deep card game. Definitely not a 100's of hours thing at least from me. But I can see myself spending at least 10 more hours with it in the near future. And probably something I would go back to in the far future.

Also to the people who made the game if you read this. Be proud of yourself. This is beautiful and artistic. It is a work of art and doesn't fall into the pitfall many "art games" fall into where its not a great game once you get passed the artistic qualities. All around amazing. Thank you for this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the first time a phrase "too good for it's own good" comes to mind.

I'm not gonna write plot spoilers, but describe some aspects of the pacing so be warned.

For the first half of the game I was blown away by how it constantly unveiled new layers. And did it in such a way that made them play off each other.
But then, closer to the end it became a break neck pace that made it impossible for me to enjoy each new quirk and mechanic to a satisfying degree.

So as weird as it sounds this game simply has too much content compared to it's lenght.

There is an "arcade" mode after you beat the game, but it's not nearly as satisfying as the main story (whch again is a first for me). It's more difficult.

The graphics and atmosphere are 10/10 but sadly all I can give the game overall is a meager 9/10\t\t\t\t\t
