\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is not what it appears to be from the store page. Don't get me wrong, what you see is what you get - except that's not all you're getting, there are parts of the game that are, for the purposes of the game flow and design vision, not advertised. That includes a sudden gameplay and graphic change at some point in the game, that lasts for a while. If you're not into that kind of surprises, think twice - but let it be said that once you finish the game (or press Shift+K+M) you unlock a purely deckbuilding game mode in a roguelike, replayable manner. I'm not going to explain more, because spoilers - you can look it up elsewhere.

That said, however, I am having a good time with the game. One thing I feel is a little exaggerated is the "psychological horror" and "deeply disturbing odyssey" part. It's neither, it's pretty par-for-the-course; it would have been considered disturbing and eerie maybe five, six years ago, but since then we've been fed so many "oh so deep and darkly disturbing" plots that this one really isn't anything special anymore. That doesn't mean the plot is bad; it's actually quite interesting to piece it together. It just isn't as special as advertised.\t\t\t\t\t
