\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is it's own little controversy of existence. The first act of the game is PHENOMENAL! Possibly one of the most unique deck builder, escape room, or even simple horror games I've played in years. The graphics are dreary yet pleasing, the format of the story telling with the map and interchanging masks leading up to a stand off with Leshy himself is a thrill ride from start to finish. Unfortunately, once this part of the game ends (might I add, with quite the dramatic finish), it start's to creatively divert in a way that almost doubles back on everything charming about the game. Granted, experience with games like FNaF and other surreal world breakers using the pixelated spin-off idea is not a topic I'm stranger too, however the game's Act 2 was equally too little to get ahold of and too much to handle. It nearly rids of the unique sacrificial playstyle given in the first act, and becomes way too much like any old deck builder that's existed. The puzzles and fights are fine enough, and it definitely settles for a good halfway point. The third act throws this game entirely off for me and almost wants to spit in my face about it. Don't be fooled, I get that's kind of the idea with P03 non-chalantly subjecting the player to the system they mocked, but it foes a little far. You go from carefully planning your item and card use and building up fluid momentum, to just painstakingly placing cards that match the energy bar until something eventually works. I got what I assume is roughly halfway through the third act as of writing this, and I question whether or not I'll sit down to finish it. I recommend this game entirely for the first phase of the game, and honestly since the progression of this game is built to be somewhat optional, I'd call this game worth it just for repeating the first act. Beyond that? Not impressed,\t\t\t\t\t
