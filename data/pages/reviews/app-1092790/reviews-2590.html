\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis will be...no, this is my GOTY, no doubt. 10/10

This is a puzzle game covered with rogue-lite card battler.

The developer successfully combines multiple different game elements like rogue-lite, card battler, puzzle, etc. into one game. The card battler is the key theme of the game, however, there are many interesting puzzle wait for you to solve.

Card battle mechanism itself is very simple, which reminds me of many card-games I played before. It’s simple, it’s fast, it’s very strategic. It also leaves some IMBA combo to help player push through the story-line relatively easy after several attempts. I think that’s why it is tagged as rogue-lite on steam.

Yes, story-line. One issue about card-battler game is game plot. Generally, when you play a game like this, you are looking for innovated game mechanism and fun of RNG. This one, however, has a very intriguing plot. Find out yourself.

Love it, enjoying it, and eager to see what is waiting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd give this game a really good/ really good. It has been a long time since I played a game to the point of being dutiful, and an even longer time since I was actively waiting for the release of a game! This game was easily better than I anticipated, both story wise and game-play wise. The only concession I can think of is that the game doesn't let you have multiple save-files, which is a terrible pain for secret hunters (like myself) who just want to go back to previous chapters without losing all their progress. And not only that, but when you beat the game, the stats of the Ouroboros (an in-game creature) is reset, so I can't even go back through the game with a sort of new-game-plus buddy to speedily get me through what I've already spent hours on. I wish the game had a save system like Deltarune, where you can leave saves in previous chapters before finalizing the save at the chapter's end, and when you go to a new chapter you manually choose what save to base your progress on. I understand why the devs didn't put in saves, for story reasons, but since they went to the effort of a chapter select after beating the game, I wish they would've also included back-up saves for the after game, letting me not use hours of time, just to repeat myself. Unless they include saves, or something of that nature, I don't think I'll be replaying this game anytime soon. But the first play-through is easily one of the best games I've ever played, and I managed to find tonnes of secrets without the use of any guides at all! The game is well worth it's price, though as a word of warning, be sure to do everything you can think of before finishing off a chapter, as once you finish the game you will (and should) probably take a long hiatus from the game. I myself am taking glances at pony island.
Edit: Coming back after a little break and dang, that Kaycee mod was a good call. Because it's really bloody good, and it offers a fun spin to the game's first section, while giving that post-game content/ challenge I looked for. Also I hope my original review didn't come off as too negative, because I really do love this game and it's story, it's just that one gripe really hit me hard.\t\t\t\t\t
