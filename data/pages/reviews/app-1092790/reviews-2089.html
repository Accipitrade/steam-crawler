\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the game that keeps on giving.
Just when you think its over, there's more.

Overall, a very enjoyable experience. Having seen (but not played) more games by this developer, I knew I was in for a lot of plot twists and challenges of the idea of a "game", and I was not disappointed.

This game maintains a air of mystery and intentionally starts with absolutely no information given to the player. It slowly attempts to introduce characters and establish a timeline. The conflict between the story setting and the gameplay, the interruptions of one by the other and the polar-opposite worlds of gameplay and "cutscenes", are incredibly well execute and make for an interesting narrative option. You don't know if you are the protagonist, if you are controlling the main hero of the story, or if it's all done and you're just watching it play out as a movie. This is a particularly interesting feeling.

Gameplay wise, the game dabbles with several different executions of the same deck-building idea. As a card game, it offers great customization of the deck, sprinkled with a hint of chance to keep it interesting. Personally, I enjoyed the roguelike part (1st) of the game the most. The rest were interesting but I could not say I'd like to play the second one again. The last few glimpses were great ideas and would have enjoyed to have given them a try.

And of course, art and sounds. As said, this game dives in many different styles and executes all of them incredibly well. The seamless change between tabletop and FPS is simply perfect. Animations are fluid, sound is on-point and I can see myself adding to certain soundtracks to my collection.
Fair warning though, there are flashing lights and ear-piercing sounds to be found... but that's ok.

I have absolutely no trouble recommending this to anyone who enjoys this genre. I also think it would be great as a lets' play with friends.

8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
