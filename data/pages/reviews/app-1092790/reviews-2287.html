Wow, I was hooked from the start.

This game is a love letter to Rogue-like CCG's while also delivering amazing narrative dialogue that provides you with enough mystique and curiosity that you just want to keep pulling on this thread that unravels into quite the spectacle.

It's twisty and turny and like other reviews I do recommend going in as blind as possible, the game has dark themes and enough humour to slice through the "Horror" themes without feeling too cheesy. If you're familiar with card games, moving through this will be like riding a bicycle with more than enough mechanics and card interactions to keep you having fun.

This has been the best game I've played this year and I binged my way through it, with each waking moment being away from my computer having me think about what is going on with Inscryption, and where this story is taking me. It was a wonderful ride that I'm glad to have experienced.

I would recommend this game to anyone who enjoys Card-battlers and great narrative dialogue.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a Slay the Spire style deckbuilder with a layer of Stories Untold spookiness, livened up with some Magic: The Gathering influences. Played in a rougelike format, you'll unlock more cards for your deck each time you play, getting potentially stronger with each run. In a cool twist, you will also unlock new mechanics outside of the in-universe "game," as you explore the cabin your captor has you in.

The deckbuilding elements are strong, and any imbalance tends to favor the player, keeping frustration from drawing poor cards to a minimum. The real highlight here is the atmosphere and storytelling. It's not really a scary game, but it has some pleasant spookiness to it like a campfire story, if your storyteller had you kidnapped and locked in his cabin in the woods.

And as you've probably gathered from the other reviews, it's best to go in cold. Even the trailer shows some of the cool surprises you'll run across. I personally didn't think every bit landed as hard as it could have, but all of them got me grinning.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhenomenal game with an extremely strong opening act, and a decent mid and weaker late game. Without spoiling much, the game could do with an expanded end game and/or an endless mode for the first act.

The first acts game board based progression and roguelite style of play was surprisingly fun, finding or earning upgrades to future runs as you go. It sadly falls to the wayside later, when death becomes a mere inconvenience. I could play that first act for hours though. I probably will.\t\t\t\t\t
