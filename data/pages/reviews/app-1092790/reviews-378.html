\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is absolutely wild, and I love it so much.

First off, I want to talk a bit about the gameplay, as you spend most of the game in card battles (which isn't a bad thing by any means). The card game that the devs have created is very well established and quite fun to play. It blends well with the roguelike mechanics of Act 1 (and maybe future acts but I haven't finished it yet). For people who are fans of card games and deck-building, you'll find a lot to love out of the gameplay alone. In my opinion, it's got just the right amount of complexity so that it's not overwhelming and confusing to players, but has a deep enough system to allow strategic players to shine. It's a fun card game that I'd definitely play outside of the game with my friends and family.

However, the most impressive part of the game (in my opinion) is the story, themes, and writing, and the creative ways that they're integrated into the gameplay. This game is a master of tone (which is impressive considering how many times the tone changes), and this makes the game incredibly immersive. I think the game's setting and tone peaks in Act I (but this may change as I haven't finished the game yet). I'm in love with the creepy, disturbing, and ominous world of Leshy's cabin, and I think its small size actually helps it in this scenario. Every item and design are carefully crafted to fit perfectly with the game's theme for example the damage in Act 1 is counted by teeth.

The story is also incredible, it's really difficult to put it all into words, my only recommendation is to go into this game blind and enjoy the ride. It takes so many turns that I never would have expected, the very nature of this story is a testament to the vision and creativity of the devs, and I am just in love with everything about it. It greatly benefits from being told through a game, the gameplay and the story are interwoven so cleverly and it's just so fun to experience.


Play this game!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a fan of deck-building AND puzzle games, every second of Inscryption was an absolute treat for me. The card game itself is so fun. Finding out what new cards can do through experimentation, discovering little easter eggs from different card combos, and building a deck by creatively using what events you choose to take between battles make every run fresh and engaging in new ways. Even as the card game evolves over the course of the acts, you're still testing new limits, seeing how old mechanics can mesh and harmonize with new ones. There are also escape room elements which I loved slowly uncovering and picking apart, a process heightened by the spooky setting and the stakes that come with every death.

I know that some of my friends didn't like the split of escape room puzzle, card game, and meta narrative. They felt like the puzzle were distracting them from enjoying the card game or vice-versa, which I'd suggest you keep in mind when considering whether or not to try it. I love every one of those layers individually, so getting to hop between them at different points in the game was awesome for me. This is very much a Daniel Mullins game, so if you enjoyed Pony Island or The Hex, you'll like this game too. Inscryption takes the stuff from there to the next level.

Also Kaycee's Mod (which is an official, free add-on, not a community mod) is incredible for players who most enjoyed the deck-building. Kaycee's Modfully focuses on that and throws in new events, cards, and challenges. It's practically a whole second game with how much time I'm still getting out of it.\t\t\t\t\t
