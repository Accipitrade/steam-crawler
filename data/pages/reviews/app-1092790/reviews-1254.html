\r\n\t\t\t\t\t'Inscryption' is an absolutely fantastic time through and through.

If you love card games, adore the many differing mechanics among those that exist, even remotely like some of the ones that exist, or anything in between, I highly recommend you stop reading reviews and give this a play.

To accurately review this title, I found the card game itself to be among some of the most fun to play. The mechanics involved work tremendously well together, and as the game opens up over time, the more that are added just keep making it interesting. At times, I did like some aspects or 'genres' of the card game over others, but it never disappointed.
The actual story was a delight, and I'm amazed at just how deep it ended up being. If people haven't already discovered all the secrets, I can see this being talked about for years in the future, especially with what additions are intended for this universe and the card game.

My rating for 'Inscryption' is a classic childhood hunt for plastic Easter eggs. So much fun already comes from the idea and action of playing the game, but as you progress, secrets are consistently revealed and every step unleashes more of the same tasty treats you've come to know and love, with a plethora of similar flavors that might introduce you to other new and potentially favorite concepts.\t\t\t\t\t
