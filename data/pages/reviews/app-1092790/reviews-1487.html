\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe screenshots and advertising made me think this is a spooky deck building roguelike game with a lot of replayability.

It is not!

Most of the game is nothing like what you see in the screenshots.

People keep recommending to "go in blind", but having done that I think it's a horrible idea. There is a big bait and switch.



SPOILER alert:
After about 3 hours the game changes. Literally you start playing an entirely different game than what you purchased.

You are now playing a low quality pixel game:
* with mini puzzles that are either way too easy, or way too obscure (e.g. you find the required clues in the settings)
* the deck building aspect is suddenly more like a trading card game
* the fights are very poorly balanced
* there are 20 new mechanics in play at once, nobody explains them, but it also doesnt matter because of the lack of balance.
* none of the cool mechanics from the first few hours exist anymore.
* only good thing is, that after a couple of hours the game changes again

I regret my purchase but what I regret the most is that I kept playing after the game stopped being fun.
The start screen teases a "new game" mechanic, and the change of the game made me hope that the game would change back again, would become fun again.
Out of 13 hours played, I hated 10 of them.

Oh and did I mention: Apparently you can find hints and clues in the video footage if you want to go on a puzzle quest. After some research on the internet those even include GPS coordinates, so if you don't mind hopping into an airplane and traveling to another country you can keep the puzzle solving going!\t\t\t\t\t
