\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rarely write reviews before I finish a game, but I've heard enough positive buzz about this game, but also a lot of people bouncing off of it before the game gets good, and that is just a shame. I know I will finish Inscryption, and maybe I will edit my review. The game deserves to be on GOTY lists - it is a good enough game that every podcaster and game reviewer should know it.

It's Slay the Spire, but you are trapped in a cabin with a maniac, and there are a lot fewer rules or cards. You move a character on a map filled with Slay the Spire-esq encounters, trying to assemble a deck strong enough for the final boss. There are some Rogue-like elements where you unlock new cards, items and rules, You leave the game, explore the cabin you are trapped in. But then there's a change. I'm going to tag the next paragraph as a spoiler, but it is not really a spoiler. In fact, I wish I knew the next paragraph before I played.

After you beat the game once, you learn some things, and you play again. (It is a Rogue-like after all). So you beat the game a second time. Now you learn a lot of things, including that you were only using about 1/4 of the cards, there is a deck building mechanic, and a lot of new card abilities. It is a bit overwhelming. But pushing past that overwhelming point is very easy, and quitting at that point means you quit before the game gets great.

If you like deck building.... I bet you would have fun. I hate deck building, and was upset when it lost that Slay the Spire feeling. But there is an "autocomplete deck" button, and it works well. I beat everything just by deleting my deck, and pushing autocomplete every now and again, and it built me a good enough deck with my newly unlocked cards each time. One or two bosses might have been easier if I had optimized my deck for them, but I did not need to. I never built a deck.

So don't drop when the game changes, it gets better.\t\t\t\t\t
