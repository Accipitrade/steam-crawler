\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is one of those games that’s hard to review because, like a magic trick, it loses its charm upon close examination. How do you review a game that’s best experienced completely blind? Fortunately, you don't really need a review of Inscryption. This is because Daniel Mullins was gracious enough to provide a demo you can try out for free (something I really wish more developers would do). If you’re truly concerned about the quality of this title, give the demo a shot. But if you’d like to hear what this particular random internet guy thought about Inscryption read on at your own risk.

I’ll keep it brief. I thought it was a pretty good, but uneven game. My biggest problem is that once Inscryption throws its skirt at the end of act one, it never quite hits its stride again. In particular, the second act is weak as hell. The intriguing art and sound design of the first act are discarded and replaced by an utterly bland 2D pixel art aesthetic. I could forgive the downgrade in atmosphere if the gameplay didn’t also take a serious hit. Act two introduces a bunch of superfluous side-mechanics that muddy the clear and focused design of act one. Because of this, I found myself completely disengaged from the newly introduced card collecting aspect of the game. This is worsened by the fact that most opponents during this act provided very little challenge, so I didn’t feel incentivised to create new decks to counter their strategies. I basically rolled through this chapter with my starter deck. Thankfully the third act gets back on script, though it never quite reaches the highs of act one (even if it did have some funny bosses).

Gripes aside, I still had a lot of fun with Inscryption and I can easily recommend it. If you enjoy card games, you’ll probably enjoy this unique deconstruction of the genre.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAct 2 has me going through the kubler-ross 5 stages of grief...

TL;DR, if you are buying this game for its horror, rogue-like elements and macabre deck building, you will be disappointed when all of that gets thrown out after only a few short hours into the game... buyer beware the steam page is misleading.

Minor Spoilers Ahead

I bought this game for a few reaons. Bit of background, I did not play card games as a kid, and generally never got on the pokemon/earthbound/undertale hypetrains, so keep that in mind moving forward.

What drew me to inscryption (act 1, at least), was the gritty horror narrative, the rogue-like elements, the graphical and auditory aesthetic, and the macabre undertones of a card game based on sacrifice, collecting bones, and playing against some sort of demonic entity.

What made me stay was the memorable characters (with their own motivations to unravel), the cabin puzzle, escape narrative, and the fact that the gameplay of choosing to either sacrificing a squirrel or drawing a playing card created some extremely tense, high stakes situations. Mechanically, the card game was amazing, and had a lot of potential too grow in the true roguelike fashion.

So imagine my horror, having gone in virtually blind aside from some youtube videos of act 1 and the steam pages marketing, to have the game transform into ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ pokemon in act 2.

It sacrificed the horror narrative of being trapped in some dank cabin with a demonic entity, playing a card game for for your very survival for smashing the bell over and over until you either die or get the rng needed to beat the boss.

It ruined the amazing UI and aesthetic of sitting at the table, looking at a range of horrifying and unexplanable phenomenon for some creepypasta tier, b movie/found footage vibes fused with a boring, grey, drab GBA backdrop.

It killed the core, advertised mechnics of suriving an ever evolving rogue like while building a frankenstien deck out of animals and players trapped in the demon's game. It even killed the core mechanic of choosing to either draw a squirrel, or risk playing for a better card - something that in my opinion defined the gameplay loop of the actual card game.

The worst part is i dont understand why the dev decided to shoe-horn in his found footage horror narrative and change core mechanics when what was on the table was compelling enough. You could literally expand act 1 after killing lesh by turning stoat (po3) or stunted wolf (mox) or whatever into the new gamemaster, and just have it take place in a new cabin room with new puzzles, card mechanics and rogue-events instead of destroying a formula more compelling then any i have seen in gameplay in a long time.

There was so much room to expand the formula in act 1 instead of going all m night shamlayian on a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ like they did in act 2.

And yes, im aware of kaycees mod. Thats not the point. The point is the dev had fire in a bottle, and decided to throw it a wall instead of stoking the fire more.

God, im just so disapointed.\t\t\t\t\t
