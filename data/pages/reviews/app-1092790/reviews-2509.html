\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not so much into card games but I am into Daniel's games. His games end up questioning the structure of games, often in really interesting ways. So this game was an insta-buy for me.

Unfortunately I got to a puzzle in Act 2 - about 25 hours in for me - and suddenly realized I. Did. Not. Care. I had exhausted all interest in playing this game, or more interesting the game had exhausted my interest. I realized Id did not care about this puzzle, or any of the puzzles in this game. I did not care about the characters. I did not care for the gameplay loop. I didn't care about the story. I didn't care about the meta-story. And after nearly that 25 hours of playing I quit and won't look back.

Part of this game is a really good, solid game with a compelling narrative to keep things moving when the card game falters. But the subsequent acts (or segments, or whatever) simply lack the same cohesion. They feel half-ass. Now given the meta-narrative, maybe that's the point, but it's still *boring*. If I'm to care for the meta-narrative, I should have reasons to care for it. A boring book about how boring books can be might well be itself a boring book. I'm sure if I'd pushed through I would have "gotten" it or whatever, but....why? I checked some reviews and the subreddit and it doesn't seem like there's much on the other side of where I was. I wish there had been a complete walkthrough because that would have let me fast foward to...the end..or whatever.

Now that said, the initial card game in the first act - the one you're most likely to see in most trailers and promo material (though more of the meta narrative is also presented in some material) - is really fun. I had a great time discovering new combos and slowly leveling up, and figuring out the small escape room pieces of the game.

That strong first act followed by a very boring, laborious and tedious second act, is what makes this a tough game to recommend.

I'd recommend this if there's a complete walkthrough to help navigate some of the tedious parts. For now though, it's a no.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a narrative adventure first, card game second. It never stops being a card battler at its core, but do consider that the story takes main focus here all throughout until the very end. If you've played some of Daniel Mullins' previous games you already know what to expect.

The card battling systems gets increasingly more complex the further you get into the game, and not only does it get harder but also it gets easier to exploit (pretty fun if you're into that stuff). Don't be afraid to experiment with your deckbuilding, especially past the first part of the game.

The story is good but I wouldn't say great, there's some good moments especially near the end but it does feel a bit too creepypasta-esque at times. Not my cup of tea but I didn't dislike it necessarily.

Overall it's a pretty solid game. I'll gladly keep supporting Daniel Mullins in his efforts to break the boundaries of videogames and deliver solid releases that feel fresh and fun to play.\t\t\t\t\t
