\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a huge fan of both puzzle games and CCG's I really wanted to like Inscryption. It seemed like the perfect game from the store page. A roguelite CCG that was also a puzzle room? A dark mysterious story and tone? What's not to love? After playing through the entirety of the story mode as well as the getting to level 10 on kaycees mod, however, there is unfortunately a lot not to love about inscryption, with my personal problems mostly having to do with the difficulty and the narrative.

My difficulty problem is that the game is too easy, both in the puzzle sections and the card game sections in the story portion of the game. The first section of the game, the only truly roguelike portion, is easily beatable in your first 4 or 5 runs and 2-3 hours. The other sections of the game are even easier, and each took me an hour or two at most. The CCG sections are very easy to break, with turn one kills being very easy to achieve in most stages of the game. As for the puzzles, answers either directly given to you, or solutions were so simple I was able to accidentally brute force them while trying to figure out the rules to the puzzle in question.

Kaycees mod is a supposedly better balanced rogulike mode available after completing the story, sort of like NG+. It has slightly better difficulty, with adjustable difficulty settings and fewer puzzles. In this mode however, the game is hurt by aesthetic choices made for the story mode, with the first person, point and click controls that were made to be immersive and creepy in the story mode becoming constrictive and awkward once you have to do many runs in quick succession, with many of the puzzle elements they were designed to support removed. Kaycees mod is also, unfortunately, still very unbalanced. There are a few plays so clearly optimal (using insect totem for example) that cause the game to feel restrictive once you figure them out, with the starter decks you unlock as you play only serving to restrict your game plan farther rather than giving you access to exciting new play styles.

If the game was just easy, I wouldn't have a problem with it. I've played walking sims before with little to no challenge and have enjoyed them, but without going into spoilers, a significant part of inscriptions narrative and tone hinges around you being an underdog, trapped in some dire circumstance at the whims of an all powerful being. Unfortunately the game is so easy that you never feel that way, you're almost always bulldozing through encounters, only facing challenge when the game actively cheats, or changes the rules of the game fundamentally in way you haven't encountered yet. You feel more like you're kicking a puppy than engaging in a battle of wits against a dark mastermind. The game also gets very meta, and without spoilers it gets meta in a way that I personally didn't like, I found it very intrusive and uncomfortable in a way that was probably intentional, but wasn't fun in any way shape or form.

To this review I will add a caveat. I've been avoiding spoilers in this review because I played Inscryption partially spoiled, and I think this may have soured my game-play experience somewhat. Inscryption's story is very meta, and relies heavily on creepy-pasta aesthetics and shocking twists for it's impact. Even knowing a rough outline of the game before jumping in was enough to suck all of the interest out of the genuinely engaging story decisions this game made, and ideas it explored. Unable to engage with the story, my focus was drawn much more to the game-play, which I disliked for the decidedly subjective, difficulty reasons mentioned earlier. The game-play didn't really hit it's stride for me until Kaycees mod, 10+ hours into a game I found myself struggling to enjoy, and even then I found it broken and unbalanced after 20 or so attempts.

Given the plethora of other positive reviews for this game it is very likely you will like Inscryption. It does some genuinely clever things with narrative and genre and despite my complaints has solid core card gameplay. If you haven't been spoiled on the story and the game sounds interesting you should probably give it as shot. My negative recommendation is very personal. I'm not that in to creepy pasta as a genre, I don't particularly like meta fiction, and I am a puzzle game and CCG fiend. I found this game easy after spending thousands of hours on MTG. Gwent, Hearthstone and LOR, and played it immediately after playing The Witness, a very hard puzzle game which I probably subconsciously compared it to. If this sounds like you, maybe give Inscryption a pass, and look elsewhere for your CCG fix.\t\t\t\t\t
