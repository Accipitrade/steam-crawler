\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMost of the negative reviews are because people came in expecting something this game is not, so here's your mild spoiler to avoid that: This is NOT a 200+ hour roguelike deck builder. It is a finite (20-30 hour) story-based game that has roguelike elements, which relies on scripted events as much as it does RNG.

This game goes so many places, and every one of them made me gasp or laugh or just hold my breath in suspense. The atmosphere is spot on, and no matter how many new threads were added to the plot, they all tied together to be really satisfying. (Plus, I'm a sucker for found footage stories, I'd completely forgotten about that part of the trailer and it made it that much better for me)

The mechanics are very "friendly", even the puzzles. Something's always happening to move you forward, even when you lose or get stuck. Great story pacing, there was a point where I worried it would turn into a grind for better cards but that turned out to not be necessary or possible. Some people are saying that they liked the first part of the game so much better than the others, or at least that it was their favorite, and, without spoiling anything, honestly I thought that was kind of the point...?\t\t\t\t\t
