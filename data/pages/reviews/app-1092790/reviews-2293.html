\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe depth of Inscryption is staggering. The lore and in game mechanics work in tandem to potentially make this the most unique strategy game I've ever played, despite having several thousand hours in other card games. There are parallels to the decision making of Slay the Spire, occasional concepts recognizable from other card games (such as Poison/Death-rattle abilities similar to Hearthstone) although believe me this game has it's own share of refreshingly intricate and new content/game mechanics.

I was already sold on the game when I saw the flow of the board, and the whole idea of sacrificing minions is awesome and a cool spin on "Mana". What I didn't expect was the lore. The 5.8 hours I have reviewing this game are non-stop. It's currently 5:18am and the sun is about to flash-bang me. To say this game subverts expectations is a colossal understatement.

I wish I discovered this game sooner. Goes in my must play list no questions asked, and I think it should go in yours.\t\t\t\t\t
