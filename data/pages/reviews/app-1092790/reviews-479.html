\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStory mode was pretty fun, although certain story elements were pretty jarring and ruined the immersion. Overall I would recommend this game even to people who don't normally like card games, as it's very approachable and has fun mechanics that keep the gameplay fresh.


SPOILERS
----------
Challenge mode/Kaycee's Mod is significantly less fun for me, since unwinnable situations are pretty frequent. If there was a game mode that combined the free-form deckbuilding of Act II with the gameplay and aesthetic of Act I (minus random card pickups), I would rate Kaycee's Mod significantly higher. I could also accept just being able to refuse cards (pelts, random pickups, etc.), or being able to thin the deck on demand. I understand that this style of gameplay would completely defeat the point of several Inscryption mechanics, but I have come to realize over the past several hours of gameplay that Inscryption's level of RNG is more frustrating than fun for me. \t\t\t\t\t

Spoiler Free Review:
Inscription is a roguelike deck builder similar to Slay the Spire, but with some quirky mechanics thrown in such as standing up from the game board to walk around and trigger effects. The core card mechanic is a bit like Yu-Gi-Oh in that there are limited board slots for creatures, and you need to play small creatures for free to sacrifice them and play larger ones. This can be a bit frustrating at first since the AI never has to sacrifice anything, yet the game is presented as a duel between player vs game master. The overall game presentation gets pretty edgy at times, with the focus on killing/sacrificing creatures and the "if you die in the card game, you die in real life" story hook. However, none of these complaints take away from the experience, and I think the game is overall a very solid purchase.

There are a few negative reviews of the game that used to be on point with their complaints, but those reviews are completely obsolete with the launch of an official free add-on called Kaycee's Mod. It's styled as a mod for in-game presentation purposes, but it's an official expansion. Either way, it removes any hesitation I may have had about recommending this game.

MASSIVE SPOILERS:

The main issues with the game come from the 2nd/3rd act plot twists. After your first win, it's revealed that you're playing a "real-world" guy who is playing this game he found an Inscryption floppy disk buried in the woods. After you complete a few objectives and beat the boss a couple of times, the game pivots into a semi-open world Pok√©mon style dueling game where you make your whole deck beforehand and get random cards for winning duels. The card game uses some of the same mechanics, but the gameplay is significantly less fun than act 1. On my first playthrough, I soft locked in this section because the random cards I won were trash. After act 2, there is another pivot into a sci-fi adventure card game using similar yet different mechanics, and a healthy dose of edgy presentation that frankly doesn't make a lot of sense in context. For example, you are asked to root around in your computer files to create cards, and the files will be "deleted" if you lose the card. But if we are supposed to be playing some other guy, shouldn't it be his files we are searching through?

The issue with all of this is that the gameplay is just straight up worse than what you started out with, and the story takes a nose dive into edge-town with characters that talk about "the OLD_DATA" and multiple fourth wall breaks, and a mystery about where this game even came from. This all culminates in a terrible final act where you play the game while it's being deleted which basically amounts to "click the button to advance the story" and then your "real-world" protagonist gets murdered. Its somewhat interesting, but I found myself just wanting to go back and play the original game the whole time. I would struggle to recommend this version of the game at full price, since it definitely isn't what I paid for.

Luckily, the devs added Kaycee's Mod, which removes everything else and fleshes out the act 1 roguelike deckbuilder. They even keep in some of the more interesting bits about the backstory in the form of "dev logs" that you unlock along the way.

In summary, while I may have reservations about the main game, Kaycee's mod makes sure that everyone gets what they paid for.\t\t\t\t\t
