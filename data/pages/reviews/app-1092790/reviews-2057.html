\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery good. I'm not a fan of most card game video games, but this was really fun!

I finished the game in about 16 hours and feel like I've probably seen most of the content, taking my time to explore everything I could find without too much trouble.

Also, interesting choices with how the game progressed, I'm not sure I would have done it that way, but it was certainly an interesting avenue to explore!

More in-depth review:

OK, so, there are basically 4 parts of this game.

The first is the part in Leshy's cabin. I thought this part was really very good and satisfying. I was excited to see how it would develop. For instance, longer and more complex maps, stronger enemies, maybe a wider board, lore developing about the guy who's playing this game with you...

(side note: after completing the cabin segment and replaying the game, I am struck by how railroady the first segment is. Even if you know everything ahead of time, you have to lose at least once! Poor sportsmanship on the dev's part. Let me sequence break!)

However, it then takes a hard left turn into crazy town and becomes a pixelly card rpg. I didn't think this was BAD per se, and I thought it was interesting to see the mechanics & cards in a new light (as it's now a more conventional deck game), and I thought it was well designed to be brief, and I really like OFF rpg (which this section seems like a less stylish version of), but boy... my enthusiasm for this game sagged HARD in this section, like it was a chore to play this version of Inscryption. Eventually I cobbled together a deck that won more than it lost and I felt alright about this section.

Thirdly, the game becomes robot world. Again, an interesting twist on things, and better than the pixel rpg but still not quite as good as the first part.

The fourth aspect of the game is the ARG/FMV sections, which are scattered throughout and at the end of the game. This plot was interesting, and I definitely see what it was going for (creepypasta/arg style, like ben drowned, five nights at freddy's, or petscop), and it achieves that, but... it doesn't quite do it for me. It had its moments, but it suffers from all the problems of creepypastas and ARGs, and some extra missteps. WRT creepypasta, the usual mistake is that there's somehow a magic computer that wants to hurt you, or something, which somehow always manages to feel like a dumb concept. And this plot overcomplicates it by adding several characters who seem to be self-aware, yet also exist as their fictional selves, and somehow the world transforms from 2d to 3d sometimes and everyone just rolls with it? WRT ARG... listen, I liked the video bits, even though the acting on the woman was strangely (lovably) bad, but on the other hand if you think my idea of fun is typing a bunch of long random phrases into a fake terminal with no tab completion, you've got another think coming.
As with so many games, the true ending of this game is reading a wiki.\t\t\t\t\t
