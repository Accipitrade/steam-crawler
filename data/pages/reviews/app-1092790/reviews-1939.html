\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR : Misleading Steam Page, will disappoint alot of buyers after 5 hours (changing entirely the mechanics and becoming 2 others games only story driven), which conveniently change after it's too late to ask for a refund (since game can be refunded on Steam if you play less than 2 hours). Scummy behavior, loved the first game (what you can see about the game on the Steam store) hated 2/3 of the game that was not mentioned.

----------------------------------------------------------------------

If the game was only the first part, expanding even more that concept, I would have recommended it, because it's awesome.

However, the game switch to a 2D RPG-like card game, with a gameplay more random and way less funnier than the first act where you could travel on the map, merge cards, etc. Afterwards, it switches to a 3rd part, which is again different, and I'm really disappointed.


The first part was amazing, and I would love to see a game taking that particular concept, instead of a game turning into a second Pony Island.

I feel a bit scammed, because it wasn't mentioned on the Steam page that the game will eventually entirely change its concept. I thought it would have been 10~15 hours of the same card game, but it become another game (which is unfun and entirely different, I simply had to pray for RNG-luck and spam retry to eventually win), and become again another game which still don't have the same concept than the first one. People say you should not read anything about the game before going in, and I tried to play it without getting spoilers, however, by doing so, I didn't know the game I bought was actually only 1/3 of the entire game, and that the 10 hours left of this 15 hours game is... well... completely differents games, and not the same than what I liked.


I get it that the two others parts with entirely differents gameplay were "surprises", but I still find it a bit scummy, because none of these two parts were even shown on the videos and pictures on its Steam page. I don't feel like people would like me as a gamedev if I were to entirely change everything that people liked about my game (and why they bought it in the first place) after the first 5 hours, without even mentioning it anywhere.

Anyway, I would suggest to read about the two others parts at least a bit, and to go in only for the story, because the cool concept the gameplay had doesn't remain.\t\t\t\t\t
