\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a run-based card battler that’s set in this eerie, escape room-like cabin setting. The card game is simple: various animal-based cards can be drawn from a deck, and higher tiered cards can only be played by sacrificing other animals already in play. Your strategy will balance around which type of cards you have access to because squirrels, who don't need a sacrifice to play, have to be drawn from a separate deck. Runs can change depending on which spaces your character lands on—cards can be sacrificed to make others better, totems can change behaviours of different animal types, and items can be utilized to give you a slight edge.

The card battling is engrossing on its own, but the game goes deeper than its surface level presentation. I desperately want to just say screw it and write out every little detail and secret that makes Inscryption such a cool game, but doing so would spoil its most fascinating parts. Yes, it's a run-based card battler at its core, but its hidden secrets can potentially reveal a deeper game. Not every moment hits as well as others—the middle section hinders the game's momentum, and its best parts could easily be missed—but Inscryption is absolutely worth seeing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game to me is highly revolutionary. The amount of detail and the level of complexity as the story advances is truly remarkable, I would definitely recommend it to any fan of the puzzle/escape-room genre as it takes a standard roguelite deck building game and pushes it to its limits (even beyond the 4th wall). That being said, I would also suggest that you play the developer's previous titles before this one because it kind of grabs some elements from them. However, having played other Deckbuilding card games, I did find the difficulty to be not challenging enough to my taste though it gets a bit tricky at some point.

In conclusion, all in all a great game that is totally worth your time and money if you're into the Cardgame and escape-room genres.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpoiler Free Review

I love this game. It's fun in its own right, with an excellent card mechanic that carries through several weird changes as the setting changes. I loved the horror-movie vibe throughout the game... several different horror movie vibes actually, as the story progresses. I thought the last third of the game before the finale was the weakest (I can't really say why, without spoilers), but the finale itself had me tearing up.

This is a worthy successor to Pony Island; a linear story in roguelite trappings. Unlike Pony Island (which was excellent, but which I will never replay), the gameplay in Inscryption is more interesting and solid, able to stand on its own ignoring the story aspects. Plus there were a few choices I made during the course of the game where I'd be interested to see how the story progresses if I'd picked differently.

This is one of the best games I've played all year... good mechanics, excellent story, fascinating game design.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStop reading reviews and buy this game aldready - it's the best in the genre in a long time! The price, even at full price, is an absolute bargain! Stop reading... buy and play.

This game has a strong narrative so make sure to look up as little as possible to get the most out of the experience - most reviews here spoil too much already. I put together the basic (non-spoiler) premise of the game below if you were lucky to find this review before getting too much spoiled.

So what are you in for mechanics wise? The core gameplay is that of card battlers and roguelike elements/draft. The complexity is satisfying and is constantly evolving throughout the entire experience and never becomes dull. However, this is not a game for you if you are looking for a hardcore game to grind and solve - it has difficulty but is self balancing to ensure your progress. As an experienced card game/roguelike player that enjoys challenge I found the difficulty just perfect for the experience it was designed for (and oh is it good!).

The game is truly haunting. Everything from the theme, gameplay mechanics, sound, music, and story is brilliantly put together in the best package a card game has ever had. Visually it is distinct, and imaginative. Familar features are presented in refreshing ways, nothing is plastered on - theme prevails. And the sound... the sound! A paramount part in creating a horror setting and they nailed it.

And finally, if you think you have figured out the game about 2-3 hours in - trust me, you haven't. Enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t
