\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSPOILER-FREE REVIEW:

Right from the get-go Inscryption places heavy emphasis on tone and atmosphere.

You find yourself a dark cabin, facing off against a sinister figure who appears as nothing more than a pair of menacing eyes in a roguelike card game where the stakes are high: a fact that is made clear almost immediately.

This is the perfect introduction, and reaffirms the player's expectations for what the game will be: a blend of horror and mystery. You find yourself with the urge to uncover your next clue; to figure out exactly what is going on in this terrible game, while shocking revelation after revelation only serves to deepen your fear of what might come next.

I find the game's beginning segment to be its strongest. It guides the player along and presents a tough yet surmountable challenge that might not be beaten on your first few attempts. But through each run new mechanics, items and cards are unlocked, paving the way for you to overcome the hurdles in your way. The mechanics complement each other well and the Slay the Spire-inspired nodes are rewarding, both in how the little story snippets add to the game's storytelling, and their gameplay effects that improve your chances at victory.

While a number of twists midway through the game introduce some drastic changes to its overarching loop, the card-based gameplay remains fundamentally the same. There were many moments where I found myself wondering "just what was Daniel Mullins thinking when he decided to pull this off". I did however find the combat in later parts to be a lot less tight than it was initially. The card pool felt diluted with new effects and mechanics that didn't mesh well together, leading to a much less satisfactory feeling when playing cards (especially in comparison to the polished game flow in its first segment).

Conclusion: When you play a Daniel Mullins game, you're expecting a wild ride. Inscryption truly lives up to (if not utterly surpassing) that expectation. I wouldn't recommend going into it any other way than completely blind - if not, you'll be ruining the game's many surprises for yourself.\t\t\t\t\t
