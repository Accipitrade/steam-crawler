\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a wonderful game that utilises metafiction to create an absorbing story with genuinely enjoyable gameplay. The characters are multifaceted and the game does not shy away from challenging the players views of them through changes in perspective.

People often rave about the way Daniel Mullins, the creator of Inscryption, tells his fourth-wall-breaking stories, but as a person who is not necessarily the best at playing games (which means I have not finished many of them) Inscryption's gameplay was enjoyable yet challenging enough, that I have gotten all of the achievements on 2 devices.

The turn based deck builder mechanic gives the player great way to learn to strategize the gameplay based on their own tastes, while the RNG lenghtens the playing time in a way that did not annoy me. I also found very much joy from finding ways to break the mechanics, since the aspects of metafioction made me feel like I was playing the game "correctly", and not that the creators of the game had done a shoddy job.\t\t\t\t\t
