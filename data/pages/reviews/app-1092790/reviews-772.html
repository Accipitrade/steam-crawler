\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs there such a thing as a "perfect game"? Very rarely does a game come along and really challenge all of your expectations and do something new and fresh in such a way as to have you sit back at the end and really soak it all in.

Going in blind to Inscryption is a good idea, but the only fault of the game is that sometimes you can get stuck without knowing what next to do. Once you clear that (in hindsight, relatively low) hurdle, you're really given a gift of an experience. I don't think it's out of line to google something when you're stuck, but I wouldn't suggest that as the first course of action.

People avoid spoilers like the plague for good reason, but it's probably important to know there are three major acts to the game. Most screenshots and such that you see online are from act 1. I spent way too much time and focus trying to beat act 1 in order to get to the later stages of the game, but I don't think that's the right approach in retrospect. The real goal is to figure out the symbols and rules of the game in act 1 that carry over to the other acts. Everything you learn is built upon and there's no wasted effort. I was under the impression the game 'reset' in act 2, but that's not entirely the full story.

If the entire game was just act 1, it would still be brilliant. The way the story, gameplay mechanics, and art direction evolve over the course of the game is just a perfect mix.It's really worth your time to see the adventure all the way to the end. I was honestly frustrated at some difficulty aspects of act 1, but by the end of the game, every aspect encountered so far was like a warm blanket of nostalgia coming back for a warm mug of cocoa.

If you're into deck builders, meta commentary, pokemon, robot economy balancing, escape rooms, and light horror elements, this is your game right here. You might be asking how all of these themes tie into each other, but the only way to find out is to experience it for yourself.\t\t\t\t\t
