Wow, where to even begin. My words won't do this game justice.

This is one of the best games I've ever played.

This is by far the most fascinating "card game" I've ever played.

At its core it is a deck builder with rogue-lite elements. Sure, but this game is so much more than that.
To that, I don't want to say anything else about the game, because as many others are suggesting, going into this with as little information known is the best way to play the game.
If you are interested, just buy it. Stop reading this right now and just get the game. It will be worth it.

Now, the rest of this will be tagged as a spoiler, so its only really for those who are simply interested to hear about the game instead of buying it or whatever.

The game immediately grabbed my attention upon launching it. As many games, this had a "New Game" and a "Continue" option at the start screen, but I couldn't select for a new game, I could only hit continue. This lead me into the game already questioning the nature of it.
The game begins and it has an interesting art style, one that is dark and gritty with an old school feel to it. There's a set of large white eyes staring at me from across a table cloaked in darkness. Throughout the game I learn about the mechanics of the card game, along with getting familiar with the strange environment I'm in. Early on I am given the option to stand up from the table and walk around the room in a grid-like movement style. This added an entirely new element to what was otherwise known only as a card game has now become an escape room of some sort. If "escape room" isn't a suitable example, a room that consists of various puzzles, riddles, and other related tricks. This, along with some particularly interesting cards that spoke to me, and the ominous figure that challenged me game after game, I was learning a story here. The game progressed in a way no other card game has. When I thought I reached the end and beat this opponent of mine, a new beginning happened instead. An unexpected twist. A series of video clips become available to view and they introduce a character who apparently makes videos about card games. He was opening packs of cards. Inscryption cards. He opens one pack that included coordinates drawn inside. He goes to those coordinates and digs in a specified location uncovering a floppy disk with the label, "INSCRYPTION" on it. He's all spooked and such, as am I. He goes home and launches it.
To speed the rest of it along a bit more..
This guy starting the floppy disk game results in an entirely new game starting. It's a totally different graphic style and game style, but it was still a card game. Just.. different. I wandered around a world in a top down RPG style kind of way, battling foes I encountered, upgrading cards in all kinds of interesting ways, and just when I thought I beat it. I'm surprised again, I'm returned to the original art style of the game, but with a new opponent, in a new environment, and with new puzzles. Oh, and the card game, that was different too. The core mechanics were the same, but the gameplay shifted. Think of it like a mod or an expansion that altered or overhauled the game in some way. At this point into the game, it felt like it was a 3-games-in-1 sorta thing almost.

The game was consistently blowing my mind. I was constantly wondering what was going to happen next. Take the items for example. In Act 1, I'll call it, one item is a pair of pliers. What do they do, you ask? You use them to pull out one of your teeth which adds 1 damage to your opponent. This is because damage in the game works with a weight scale. Each damage you deal to the opponent a tiny object, such as a tooth, gets added to the scale. If it tips all the way over to their side, you win. Then there's the dagger. An item you initially acquire through some of those aforementioned puzzle challenges. Using the daggers results in stabbing yourself in the eye, digging it out and then placing it on the weight, counting as an extra heavy weight, therefore adding a lot of damage. But, holy ♥♥♥♥ you just ripped your eye out in a card game and now you can't see half the screen and everything is pulsing red.

This game is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ crazy.

Luckily, at the end of battle, you are provided a selection of new eyes to choose from. Some look strange. One in particular looks, almost magical.. Selecting that one allows you to see some invisible markings around the room you're in, adding yet another layer to the game. And to think it was only attainable by digging out your own eye.

I've been rambling, and I could honestly go on and on about this game.
I loved it, it's top 10 maybe top 5 for me.
I gotta think about my top games of all time some time, make a list.\t\t\t\t\t
