\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't have much experience playing deckbuilder games but I really enjoyed this one. I realized it's not about the genre, but the quality of the game itself. Inscryption is one of the most unconventional games I've ever played, it's meta, it breaks the fourth wall, and then it changes into an entirely different game. It completely defies expectations. And despite being rogue-like, death actually rewards the player and brings in more lore and storytelling. Death is actually exciting in this game.

The game is actually separated into three acts, the trailer only covers the first act. There's a good reason why some people only enjoyed Act One and wanted the game to stay like that forever, but I'm one of those people who also enjoyed Act Two and Act Three, although I agree Act One alone makes one of the greatest games of all time. The dark, sinister style of Act One really sets the mood. That being said, Act Two and Act Three expanded on different ways of playing the card game, bringing in some classic RPG elements, environmental strategizing and different decks with their own unique mechanics. Each act brings something new for you to experiences, while taking away something you have grown to love in the previous act, so whether it is good or bad depends on personal preferences. Act Two reminded me of Starcraft where Zerg, Terran and Protoss each have their own ways of resource generation, strengths and limitations. You have the greatest freedom to mix the decks together to build your team however you want, unlike Act One where it's half RNG. Act Three is kind of like Act One but with more environmental variations, defensive play and timing strats. The stage becomes more like a realistic battlefield if you use your imagination. I liked all three acts tbh. I also appreciate the fact that there was a mod released just so people can play Act One nonstop.

Overall this game is one of the most creative games out there, it is a must try!\t\t\t\t\t
