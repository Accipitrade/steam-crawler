\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is massively overrated. Here's why:

This game is doing some sort of weird narrative story thing where it melds together different games in this trippy experience. The problem is that the core games that compose Inscription are just weak.

For example, you start in this escape room cabin, but the actual escape room is weak. There are tons of escape room games on Steam. Just go play one of those instead.

The core card game SUCKS MEGABALLS. There is minimal strategy. Every run has literally boiled down to "buff up 1 card as much as possible and win as soon as you draw it." I'm not even kidding. I have lots of matches where I literally won on turn 1 before the opponent even had a chance to do anything. If I don't draw that card on turn 1, the strategy is to just stall until I draw my one-turn-kill card.

Also, the visual clarity is among the worst I've ever seen in a video game. The game will literally just not tell you what some keywords do. The game also heavily relies on a tribal mechanic, but the game doesn't actually tell you which card belongs to which tribe. You have to just sort of reason it out.

I AM NOT MAKING THIS PART UP: YOU LITERALLY HAVE TO LISTEN TO THE PLAYER WHINE ABOUT HOW BORING THE GAME IS DURING THE FIRST CARD GAME.

Inscription is 5 mediocre games masquerading as a good game, kind of like 5 kids in a trench coat pretending to be an adult.

If you want to play a good narrative game, go buy a good narrative game.
If you want to play a good puzzle game, go buy a good puzzle game.
If you want to play a good card game, go buy a good card game.
Because Inscryption is like a solid C- in all of these categories.

I couldn't even finish it because card game #2 was a hideous, unclear mess. I wish I could refund it.\t\t\t\t\t
