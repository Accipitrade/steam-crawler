\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a game!

I heard that this game had a great reception upon release but didn’t really look further into it and pushed it aside due to being busy with other games. Bit late to the party but I’m glad I finally went in blind because it was an immense amount of fun.

When reviewing a game, I’ll usually explore and explain various aspects about the game but going into detail in this circumstance will probably give away too much – and saying this probably already gives too much away.

However, I will talk about the gameplay since this is obviously a major component. It’s pretty much flawless. Inscryption provides a unique take on the deck building genre with the incorporation of different mechanics and rulesets that change throughout the playthrough, ensuring that gameplay doesn’t get too repetitive and keeps the player thinking. Card abilities and traits, items, and events throughout the journey allow the player to create decks that fit their playstyle. Some players may find the gameplay frustrating due to the RNG elements (e.g., bad draws). But, since it is a roguelite, there is always progression and a sense of assistance to reach the end.

Those that enjoy learning about the limits through trial and error, finding out what works and what doesn’t, and figuring out beneficial combinations until ultimately mastering the mechanics to develop a completely overpowered deck, will definitely find enjoyment out of this.

If you have the slightest interest in the gameplay, pick this up and go in "blind" as you’ll be in for a treat.
