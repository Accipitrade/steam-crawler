\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is possibly one of the few Video-Game Card Games that I saw and immediately wanted to jump into, and I wasn't disappointed. Personally an overall great game, a neat story for the most part, lots of secrets and different mechanics and tons of fun characters. I'm also above and beyond pleased with how the Developers have gone back and added a FREE Challenge mode that if fully completed can add an Optional Cut Boss from the main game.

-Positives;

~Some very nice musical tracks to help accompany the game and set the mood.
~A solid Card Game that evolves in the overall story, but also comes back to basics with a twist.
~A Free Challenge Mode added by the Developers after they released the game.
~Lots of fun characters to interact with.
~Multiple different decks to be made and played with.
~Good Atmosphere for all locations in the game.
~Some references to very old timey Card Game Video-Games that I liked a lot.
~Unique bosses with their own Mechanics, some that even play at the Fourth wall.

-Negatives

~I really cannot state enough how disappointed with the ending I was.
~Spoiler Reason Ahead: The Live Action and 4th wall breaks when done about the Main Story regarding the Old_Data both didn't make a lot of sense and kinda just felt forced in, if you want to see a game put in recordings of humans that make sense I recommend 'Control' or if you like 4th-wall breaks maybe Undertale

~Some of the Decks are obviously WAY more powerful than the others and that just makes it a bit harder for you to choose them.
~After the First of 4 Acts the game feels like it runs by very quickly, whether this is just because of skill level after the first Act or because of the game actually being easier I can't say.


Overall I personally would say that WITHOUT Kaycee's mod this would be a 7/10 but with it I have to give it an 8/10 just because the Developers actually cared enough to add something for the people who wanted more and enjoyed the game a lot. I can only hope that they do the same for other Acts throughout the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt’s always such a treat to pick up a game developed and published by names unfamiliar to you and end up falling in love with not only your experience yet the talent and people that went behind it. Inscryption is one of those games that oozes personality and makes a solid connection with their player that’s been falling out of the spotlight with the rise of modern game titles. So kinda like video game coffee beans. Snort ‘em for a nice palate cleanser (semi-responsibly).

I definitely think my childhood obsession with Yu-Gi-Oh! contributed to my enjoyment and it fortunately kept me from watching the initial playthrough I was following since if you also never felt the touch of a woman and follow stuff like Hearthstone streams, they’re far more frustrating than entertaining as they always seeming just avoid playing efficiently as you. Honestly it’s much better to close whatever video you're watching and pick this up with the vaguest idea as possible! Playing through this blind definitely sells everything about Inscryption. And, depending on how you see it, that can be a bit of a double-edged sword. Card games are always addictive and repetitive, the game mechanics of are no different by incorporating somewhat similar rules from what we may be more aware of (like the aforementioned Yu-Gi-Oh!) however with its distinctive style and giving the player full reign over whatever deck they want to build and however they want to play, it always ends up feeling fresh with the bonus of once you get tired of one mechanic you have another to try!

That being said, the story itself is not as replayable. It’s something you experience the more you play and people might call that a bad thing given the modern market/releases benefitting largely from replayability and longer playtimes. Completionists would definitely have a field day as always but to try to explain this as best I can... If you are aware of the 1999 movie Sixth Sense many people say you can only watch that movie once and any other time will never be as good as the first since you already experienced that big plot twist moment. I’m excited for the endless Kaycee’s Mod and upcoming changes, I’m also more hopeful for a new game title from Daniel Mullins. Some developers (companies especially for that matter) stick to release new content for their most successful titles either in the form of digital or physical merchandise rather than turning over a new page and letting characters/chapters have their endings instead of overstaying their welcome.

There’s more than a fair share of secrets to be found and mysteries the community has been solving, the story on GameFuna doesn't feel over yet! I’d like to see how the production team keeps painting this all into one big picture or perhaps we’ll be left to interpret the blanks. Who knows? At the end of the day, I picked up Inscryption hardly knowing anything about it and even less on the ones that made it. When the credits rolled, I expected to have finished a charming time waster rather than write a review and follow a few Twitter accounts. That might be the scariest part of Inscryption of all: how it’ll have you saying “alright, just one more match” as you keep playing right into the hands of its macabre world.

Whatever expectations you start tacking on it’ll surpass with how challenges keep changing yet at the same time remaining familiar enough so you won’t get lost as you step out of the cabin and into the carefully orchestrated love letter to retro roguelike rpgs.Once it's played you right into the palm of its hand, you'll have trouble letting go when that time comes sooner than you hoped. Whatever fuzzy emotions you may feel after completing, the game might leave you thinking the same thought throughout:
"What the f---?!" - Luke Carder
