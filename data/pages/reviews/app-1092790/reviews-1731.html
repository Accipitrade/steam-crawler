\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: They made an endless mode for the 1st act and I LOVE IT! But the review below still stands since it's the 'Main' game.

They could make a whole game based on the 1st act. The 1st act's atmosphere, card mechanics, and game events were so fun unlike the last two parts. The game's main page and demo only shows the 1st act which is a bit misleading. Getting to 2nd act and onwards was very disappointing.

The 2nd was so DIFFERENT from the start of the game with different art-style and atmosphere. They took away the rouge-like mechanics after the 1st act. There were no longer perma-deaths and you could basically grind the game out to create the best deck. Nothing felt at stake anymore which made the gameplay boring.

With the last part, they thankfully returned to the same art-style but it still didn't feel the same. It took on a more futuristic atmosphere (which would be great if you like it). There was also no perma-death in the last part for some reason. You get to keep your cards after losing which was so frustrating. You couldn't remove cards easily so you would be stuck with your trash deck until you beat the whole game.

And one small thing, the person you were 'playing' as, just took away from the immersion. If they removed that guy and have you play as 'yourself' and tell the same story a different way, it would be much better for the immersion. It's so jarring whenever that guy talks mid-game.

The reason why the 1st part was so fun was because of the perma-death mechanic. It forced you to play with different cards each run. You would accidentally discover combos! Making/Combining your own cards was brilliant! I don't recommend this game because 1st act, the only good part, is just 1/3 of the whole game.\t\t\t\t\t
