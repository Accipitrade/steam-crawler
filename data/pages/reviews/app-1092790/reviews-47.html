\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have no idea how to review this game. I almost want to say I recommend it for the first act alone.

Other reviewers insist that you go in blind so that acts 2 and 3 catch you off guard, but my personal experience with the game's progression wasn't overwhelmingly positive. See, I love rural horror, and everything about the cabin/gameplay shown in the trailer was compelling to me. I enthusiastically participated in this part of the game. And then there are essentially two different "styles" of the card game once you master this first segment.

I don't think I liked act three. I tried to keep an open mind, but the gameplay loop and aesthetics were less enchanting by the time I got halfway through it. I'm not an Easter Egg kind of guy--I like hidden bits of lore and implications, but I dislike having to do a ton of legwork in order to sort out seemingly vital information. I completed the game and had to look up the lore/rundown on the Wiki. Maybe a couple things just didn't click for me and the story was actually quite obvious, but idk.

This game is very meta and the web it weaves is experimental and different. I actually got Marble Hornets vibes sometimes. The layers often work in its favor, but I just kept wanting to go back to playing the game that was presented in the beginning.

The mechanics and rules of Inscryption are super solid. The loop is fun even if you lose to a boss a frustrating amount of times. The card game itself is just very fun. The graphics and character designs are great. The music is eerie and atmospheric. There's a natural incline of challenges as the story adds a few new mechanics to the base card game that you've come to master. I buy what the story is trying to sell me for a hot minute. I'd purchase and play a separate game that /just/ consists of the cards presented in act one.

This journey isn't for everyone. I get not wanting to give spoilers, but I feel like every newcomer should be warned that what you see is only a third of what you get. Your investment in it will vary. Get it on sale if you're not a huge fan of big stylistic changes.\t\t\t\t\t
