\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe strongest aspect of this game is not knowing what's coming next. If that sounds like an experience you're into, you should stop reading reviews now and play it.

Just remember not to get too attached to anything.

Information is very cleverly revealed over time. Mechanics are tweaked, reimagined, and combined. Ideas are deconstructed, you might say mercilessly. As soon as I felt comfortable with something, the game pulled the rug out (or just mocked my attempts by parodying its own features). I found it delightful.

Of course, so much deconstruction has a toll on the mechanics. In its most straightforward form (mostly the beginning), the game has good progression and balance. Later on, things get so ridiculous that it's mostly out the window. I found the difficulty dropped dramatically and I was mostly just enjoying the process of 'breaking all the rules.' I can see how that's not satisfying for people who prefer to master a more stable system. But I think it's clear that's not what this game is about.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr If you put Slay the Spire, Doki Doki Literature Club, and Yu-Gi-Oh (MTG comes a close second) in a blender and sprinkled some MTG in while you're at it, Inscryption is what would come out.

My first glance at the store page and it looked more or less like any of those other deck-building roguelikes. Like Slay the Spire, or Monster Train. It had lane-based combat, and a spooky horror-esque setup, and more than a few ways to shift the game in your favour. Certainly worth a wishlist, but not further attention. Then I read that it was made by the creator of Pony Island, it went from light interest to a must-buy.

My first exposure to the game was with a friend. He'd bought it, and streamed it to me while playing. Needless to say, I was hooked, and by the time he got to the second boss (and I had to leave) I was shouting instructions to him on how to play to win. Before I left, I had already bought the game and set it to download.

Armed with some foreknowledge about the game, I got to the second boss in the very first run, beat him on the second, beat the final boss (of that part of the game) on the third, and properly completed the escape puzzle by the fourth. I'd have been satisfied with the game if it ended there and just offered extra modes and bonuses for challenges and certain kinds of completion runs. Needless to say, it went off the rails entirely.

To prevent spoilers, I will not describe exactly how mundane the next act was, but suffice it to say that I want an entire open-world RPG based on act 2. Preferably with a choice of side deck this time.

Regarding act 3, suffice it to say it was the scariest gameplay I've ever experienced, and none of it in any 'jumpscare' sense. True horror. And also another gamemode.

And the finale, well. It makes me wish the game was twice as long as it really is, just so I could experience the other two gamemodes it teases. Alas. Maybe a DLC later.

Basically, 10/10 game, if you don't have it and are reading this, get it. Don't stick around to wait for a sale or anything. For the amount of time I played and the great experience it was, I would have happily paid double for it.\t\t\t\t\t
