\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played all three of Daniel Mullins full releases and it frustrates me that always end up disappointed by the end of his games.

Why buy into it then? Well, every time, the premise sounds interesting. It's not fun to put down a developer that is definitely found a niche of types of games he likes to create, but this game is so self-absorbed with its lore that it makes it even more damning when there is very little interesting under the hood.

So first and foremost, the presentation is great. This game is about subversion like his other games and I won't talk about the narrative not to spoil the experience. But the start of the game and the whole premise is intriguing. I will say though that I wish the game had fleshed out what it had instead of going for "twists" that really aren't that surprising, nor interesting, just like his last two games. More than that, it really feels like the game has nothing to say, since its story is incredibly predictable and cookie-cutter. It sucks because I feel like this game could be so much more with a proper writer and a more focused narrative. All the clues that link back to the ARG that followed the release of the game kind of show where the priority really was. Maybe the game just wasn't made for people like me, who was looking for a sharp and compelling indie game, instead of a lore-fest slog with a flimsy gamplay loop.

The gameplay is pretty easy to break, which isn't a bad thing. The game being a single player experience allows it to give crazy tools that the player has to learn and understand to create comboes that just wipe any enemy in front of you. But unfortunately, the gameplay gets pretty stale pretty quick when you realise that the presentation is what makes the game great: it really feels like some early parts of the game are so much more fleshed out in their presentation and gameplay that when things are switched up, you are left wishing to go back to that type of gameplay. Again, a shame, especially because the puzzle/exploration parts suffer from the exact same issue.

There is a beta right now that gives a rogue-lite mode for the early parts of the game, and it's pretty fun. It still is in development, so when it comes out, I might change this recommendation, but for what this game is for its price, I can't justify it.

I'll probably give the next Daniel Mullins game a try to support this type of projects, and hopefully I won't be disappointed yet again.\t\t\t\t\t
