\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: With the release of Kaycee's mod, an official fully fledged roguelike mod for the game, my original review (previously negative) is now outdated. While at the time of writing Kaycee's mod is still in beta, it is already a very worthwhile addition, providing a fantastic roguelike experience that exceeded my expectations, and even providing new lore! Play the original game and have a good time with a highly polished, enjoyable, and cinematic card game based narrative experience, then dive into Kaycee's mod if you want more. The original review is preserved bellow for old times sake. I still agree with some of its criticisms, but my overall impression is now positive. It was already a good game and Kaycee's mod make it one of my favourite games of 2021, and easily my favourite card game of the year.

Vague Spoilers ahead.

In short, this game is not the deckbuilding roguelike the storepage and the early hours of the game advertise it as. You certainly get that experience, and because it so effectively sells you on that premise, at first you don't feel bad about abusing the myriad broken combos that trivialise the experience and which are very easy to find, because you assume that you'll have much more time with the game and that there will be more content and much harder challenges in later runs, and it makes you excited to try and abuse these broken combos under much tougher conditions, knowing that you'll probably try and chase them and die in the process. That doesn't happen. You find a broken combo once and you're done. Since I had played the demo, I was much more familiar with the game's mechanics and I was able to beat the first part of the game with relative ease. This feels good at first, but I quickly realised that I had cheated myself out of a more enjoyable experience, one in which I would have taken more time to explore and find secrets, and overall just have fun with what's on offer.

The later portions of the game are still fun and contain extremely clever twists, but in terms of gameplay they are far weaker. They are weaker, I feel, entirely because only the first part of the game is an actual roguelike, while the later portions are more like card-based RPGs that have no consequences for death, making the game feel much easier and less tense. The lack of consequences for death allows you to grind your way up to the perfect deck with ease, (not that you need anything close to a perfect deck) and the game's mechanics really feel best suited to a roguelike structure. The escape-room style puzzles also start to feel like they're there mostly as a formality later on, whereas in the early hours you're led to believe that escape is necessary, since the Game master gives you the option to do things such as cut off a finger in order to deal damage in a tight spot. None of this realistic body horror is used in the later parts of the game, which is upsetting. On the other hand the final four bosses are actually incredibly well done and unique, and there's still a spooky atmosphere in the final hours of the game of a slightly different flavour, but none of it holds up to the beginning.

The very end of the game also hints at much more gameplay and narrative that seemed like they'd be really enjoyable, and it wasn't lost on me that the narrative was showing these things to you and then not delivering in order to hit home an emotional point, and the game was successful in its goals in that regard- the ending is truly incredible in terms of narrative, themes, and spectacle. However, it just leaves me wanting more from these alternate gameplay styles and from the early hours. In addition, the narrative as it rests also feels incomplete. The ARG aspect of the game seems intriguing at first but none of it feels like it pays off in any meaningful way, and since we're not given a lot of specificity it also feels more unoriginal than I think it should. I actually quite like the actor for the live-action segments. Yes, he's incredibly awkward, but I definitely think that's the point.

There's also a feeling of needless self-awareness and self negativity at the very end of the game that somewhat irks me. There's the occasional line referencing the 'hackneyed card mechanics' of the game, as well as a few pot shots at the way the game presents itself, such as a false boss fight. In the moment, these jokes are pretty funny and they lend well to the emotional impact of the narrative, but in hindsight it makes me wonder if the reason the gameplay wasn't more fleshed out was because the devs didn't respect the core design and aesthetic of their own project. Considering how much work was put into this project and how good it is, I can't make that claim in good faith, but the game still leaves me with that lingering doubt, that disappointment that festers and makes me doubt if I should even be wanting more. It's a feeling I imagine a lot of people who play this game will be left with, not everybody, perhaps not even a majority, but still a significant amount I would wager.

It would be easier for me to accept the game for the rollercoaster of meta breaking stuff the game actually is if the early hours didn't sell me on a very different, much more enjoyable deckbuilder. In an ideal world, I would happily take both the roguelike (a real one that actually scales in challenge as you win more, by using something similar to the ascension system in slay the spire for example) and the spooky meta horror game it actually is, but if I had to make the choice between the two, I would pick the roguelike. I'm leaving the negative review for that reason alone.

I should also quickly mention that the game seems to have an ARG surrounding it (beyond what's already readily apparent in the game) and lots of secrets and codes and such to decipher, which could potentially lead to more narrative, new endings, or even more content once the community figures it out. Since the game is still fresh at the time of writing, however, anything that may be discovered has yet to be found. In addition, not everybody pays attention to social media and ARGs, so even if new content is discovered, the average joe may think that what's currently here is all there is, never discovering what else may be out there. As it is, there's already a couple of secrets the community already knows about which I missed on my blind playthrough. Overall I would much prefer it if the game part of my purchased videogame was actually readily accessible within the actual game, not god knows where else, if it even exists.

I would advocate for the developers or even the modding community to make updates to the game in order to really allow the gameplay to experience to shine. Would happily change my review to positive in such an event.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLong and short of it, this game has three acts and while Act 1 is amazing, the other two acts are a let down. The game is still good and worth playing, but be prepared. The hidden story lore is there if you want to deep dive, but it really doesn't add much to the game in my opinion. It did keep my interest for at least a bit, but by the end I was like, well, that was disappointing.

Slight spoiler warning here. If you want gameplay details, here they are for all three acts. Make a decision on if you want to play after that.

Act 1 is an excellent horror rogue-lite card game / escape room puzzler with naturalistic lore and overtones.
Act 2 is a GBA style card collecting/battling RPG
Act 3 is another escape room puzzler, though the card game is no longer rogue-lite and instead plays like a more traditional card game RPG, go to a room, battle, etc. with a techno aesthetic. It also breaks the 4th wall...a lot.

It is still worth it for Act 1 and some of the story bits did surprise me. There is also always a palpable feeling of tension, unease, and wrongness, even in the other acts. But you either need to be really open to different aesthetics and styles, or really open to deep diving into exploration and secrets and lore of a game within a game to get the most out of this.\t\t\t\t\t
