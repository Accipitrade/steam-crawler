\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game. A lot, actually. But I'm sure if you've read one review of Inscryption, you'll probably have already heard someone saying they just want Act 1 on repeat... and that's unfortunately pretty true. Everything that made the game hyper appealing to begin with, such as the dark aesthetic, the sacrifice mechanics, the escape room vibes, etc. Are over once you "beat" the game one time. The story is nice, but has flaws.

I don't feel "betrayed" like some people do. I'm just a bit irritated. Acts 2 and 3 (and 4 if you consider the end game an Act) were... honestly kind of slogs to get through. Act 2 felt entirely too easy with no punishment for losing a duel and Act 3 had a punishment that was very easily mitigated. Act 1 had real tension: losing twice (or in some cases once) sent you back to the very beginning, making you rebuild your deck from scratch.

Act 1 is Binding of Isaac or Slay the Spire; you die, it's back to square one with the only help being knowledge and mechanic learning. Acts 2 and 3 is Pokemon, where losing either has no punishment or a monetary loss. The difference in tension and feeling is immense. Not that Pokemon (or Act 2 or 3) are "bad", but they are just so vastly different, you almost get whiplash.

I think this game should be played for Act 1 alone. If they were to expand on that, I wouldn't even hesitate to recommend. As it is, it has a strong start and seems to stumble towards a finish.\t\t\t\t\t
