\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat an AMAZING game. I was captivated by the atmosphere in ACT 1, and while I sucked at it, the gameplay was really fun! This was my first ever deckbuilding game, and I enjoyed strategizing and seeing how I could pull myself out of tight situations. The story is also very interesting, I never know what's coming next! After I pieced together what the story was after the ending, I was blown away.

Although I enjoyed the overall game, I was a bit thrown off when act 2 happened and changed the whole aesthetic and gameplay of the game. Originally, I didn't really like the change, but I gave it a go and proceeded with the game. In hindsight, I think Act 2 was pretty crucial to telling the story of what Inscryption truly was, and in the end, I enjoyed exploring the world and characters and chewing on the little tidbits of lore I could catch.

I was thrown another curveball when Act 3 happened, but I was more intrigued than annoyed. I think I enjoyed the gameplay in Act 3 as much as I did in Act 1. I love the techno aesthetic and I enjoyed P03 hosting the game.

Then, the climax of the game as Inscryption breaks down in front of our very eyes. Each of the Scrybes (expect P03) bid the player farewell, with an extra gut hitting moment with Leshy that totally didn't make me tear up.

While there is the surface level story, there's also a more hidden story, hidden in creepypasta-esque events in-game, character dialogues, as well as with the ARG. I am a gigantic nerd who has nothing better to do with her time, so I really enjoyed chewing on the little tidbits of lore I could find and discussing it online.

I loved Kaycee's mod as well! The mod added extra challenge to Leshy's game, and I enjoyed getting more unlocks and strategizing.\t\t\t\t\t

☑️ Recommended - buy it on sale
🔳 Avoid
Time to finish – 13 hours
Any technical problems? None
Worth revisiting? Yes, for KayCee's mod
✔️The first Act is simply brilliant. It has a spooky atmosphere, a cool antagonist and really smart card mechanics which make building an interesting deck a lot of fun. However, after that...
❌The second Act is shockingly bad and the third Act, while decent, never hits the heights of the first Act
Overall – Inscryption feels like a missed opportunity. There are four main antagonists, two of them get their own Act, Leshy (Act 1) and P03 (Act 3). The game would feel so much more complete if the devs had decided to give the two remaining bosses each their own Acts, instead of the weird, almost lazy, Act 2 the game has instead. The obvious template for the game was right there, why they didn't just do it, I have no idea. Still worth playing for the excellent first Act though but Inscryption could have, and perhaps should have been so much more.\t\t\t\t\t
