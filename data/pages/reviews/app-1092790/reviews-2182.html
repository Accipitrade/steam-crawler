\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll say it straight out - I kinda hate how the game turned out in the end. I probably won't be picking it up again or a sequel, because it's very clear that the developer has a very specific vision here. They want to tell a story that goes into the meta genre, with ARGs and content outside of the game. I can fully respect that and I wish them the best.

But to do that, they kinda threw this gleaming jewel of a game concept out of the window for the purposes of story.

If your jam is deck building and/or escape the room and/or high ambience like Darkest Dungeons and/or rogue-like and this is what you are in for Inscryption, I gotta say it to you - this will not be the focus throughout the game. The starting act has all of these genres mixed together in a very compelling and awesome way, with your opponent narrating everything, a somber and grim ambience... it's good. WAY too good. So good that after I was done with it, and things *change*, my mood started to diminish with time. Something similar came later but at that point, the "story" was becoming more prominent than the gameplay mechanics established first.

I still went after all the story, including the ARG elements, and I came out angry. It's a game with inside knowledge from the creator's previous games, an ARG that is done and undoable and it kinda turns into a sort of creepypasta. If that's your thing, go for it. If you want the deckbuilding, roguelike, escape-the-room puzzle elements with the ambience that's close to Darkest Dungeon in some ways... Well, it's still there, but don't get attached to it.

I'll still recommend because it *is* a good game. Just not the game I really liked at the start.\t\t\t\t\t
