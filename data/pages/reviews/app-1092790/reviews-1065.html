\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis, and all Daniel Mullins' games so far, had me hooked from the beginning. I was a bit hesitant to play this game at first, as card games are not my favorite genre, and I don't care for roguelikes. Thankfully, only a portion of this game is roguelike, and it's very forgiving. It did not take me too long to progress beyond this portion of the game, and I did enjoy it!

The game is primarily a card game, with an interesting and weird meta-story, as Daniel Mullins's games are wont to do. But there are some puzzle solving aspects which are fun, and even challenging at times. I played through act 1 without using a guide, but then realized this was most likely going to be a one-and-done game for me, so I decided to use a guide to make sure I didn't miss any secrets. Thankfully, by moderately using a guide, I was able to accomplish pretty much everything, though I did miss some minor things that I wont go back for.

The graphics in this game are by no means visually impressive, they are exactly what they needed to be for this game. The music is awesome and accentuates the mood of each situation perfectly. The sound effects are top-notch.

This game is completely worth the asking price, but if it's on sale, you'd be crazy not to pick it up. If you aren't crazy about card games, or roguelikes, don't worry, you'll love it. A friend of mine told me this was his favorite Daniel Mullins' game so far, and it was hard to believe it could be better than the other two, but I agree, it is. Definitely get it!\t\t\t\t\t
