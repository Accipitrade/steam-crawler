\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn short, this game friggen' rocks. I can't say much about the story and progression because I don't want to spoil too much, but I will say it has been a real ride so far. Those of you who are familiar with Pony Island, the other famous game by this dev, will probably guess that there's more to this game than meets the eye.
The single greatest thing about this game to me so far is that it is split into several acts (trust me, you'll know when you get to a new one) and each of these could stand on their own as a game if they had more content, even if they all build off the central card game theme. The timing of the act changes is absurdly well measured, you'll never be in one act for too short or too long. Just when you think you've mastered the game and you finally have a thorough understanding of the new mechanics, KABOOM new act!
The first act in particular is phenomenal, and it's what got me hooked on the rest of the game.
My only complaint so far is that the second act is a bit rough. I can't say much with spoilers, but I will say I wish I got a short tutorial for all of the [REDACTED] rather than just the one I picked. Definitely a noticeable lack of explanation there. I didn't figure out one of the two new mechanics until way late into the chapter.
Overall, if you like big brain moments, deck building, intrigue, and escape rooms, then this is the game for you.
Almost wish there was some sort of multiplayer so I could pit decks against other players, but it would be incredibly hard to do so it's fair for player two. You'll learn a lot about the power of the first turn in this game.\t\t\t\t\t
