\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've played either of the previous two games by this developer (Pony Island and The Hex) you sort of know what to expect out of the story - cursed games, sentient games coming to life, meta themes, secret clues hidden in the options menu, and that sort of thing where you're best served not looking up too much about what is going on in the game before you experience it yourself. This game is no different.

Gameplaywise it's also fairly well constructed. It's based for the most part around a simplistic battling card game that feels like those old GBA versions of Yugioh or the Pokemon card game and where you're trapped in a sort of death game that seems very reminiscent of Hand of Fate (intentionally so). The deckbuilding and card game is pretty well done too and the core gameplay is pretty fun, even if the game starts cheating you. Then you also have some escape room type elements where you solve puzzles outside the "game" and doing so makes you feel smart, like you're cheating when the game isn't looking and getting your revenge.

It's an altogether enjoyable package in total.\t\t\t\t\t
