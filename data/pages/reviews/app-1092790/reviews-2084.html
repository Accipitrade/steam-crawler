A great card game with actual plot. I'm going to try be spoiler free here (which is important to your experience). It's a Slay the Spire like game, but with no action cards. You use an energy based sacrifice system to play cards, and a fun shared-HP system (using a scale) in matches. Cards can have traits, traits can be transferred, and different traits can stack. You can carry up to 3 consumable items, which persist throughout runs. Each boss brings a twist, a new rule (for that fight) that changes gameplay, which adds to the fun. It's important to note that beating the final boss does not finish the game; you must complete some puzzles to progress the game (and plot) - this confused me. I also recommend collecting all achievements before defeating the final boss, as you may have to restart the run.

I'll start with the game board, or map. You have multiple paths to pick, which can branch our 2-3 times and then merge. Nodes are balanced, so there's always an available node regardless of which path you take. Nodes are same type horizontally, so you will an event node or combat node in the same row. At the end of every region is a single boss node, where all paths will merge together. You can "step away" from the board and explore the room you're in, solving puzzles and pressing random things to gain bonuses.

Cards have multiple properties marked on them, such as the name and picture, which serves no functional purpose. There is a sacrifice cost (with the blood icon) in the upper right corner, an attack and HP value, a "family" (e.g. wolf, insect) and optional traits. You can have up to 3 (maybe 4) unique traits, which can affect the way the card interacts with other cards. Traits can be transferred to another card (assuming there's space on the target card) by "sacrificing" it at an altar, which is one of the random event nodes you can encounter. Sacrificing it in this way will permanently lose the card. However, this is useful for various reasons, including thinning our your deck - it's very hard to find card removal, as you cannot "build" your deck. You can combo multiple traits together to get nice combos like counting as 3 sacrifice, undying (returns to hand when it dies), and gain a copy of this card when played. The best and most important card in the game is the Ouroboros card. Each time it dies, it permanently gains +1/1 to its stats, which carries through runs! Once you're familiar enough with the game, you can easily farm and raise the card's stats. Once you're happy and farmed enough, you can 1-shot battles with the card and gain insane amounts of currency.

Each turn in a match consists of 3 stages: the draw phase, summon phase, and battle phase (in that order). There are no limits to how many cards you can draw, hold, or play. It's possible to draw, play, hold, and summon your whole deck in one turn (with the combo). The board has 4 slots to play cards, with potential obstacles (0/x cards) scattering when the match starts. Additionally, there is a 2nd row, on your opponent's side that shows you the cards they'll play on their next turn.

At the start of your turn you are required to draw a card. You have 2 decks to draw from: one is your actual deck, and the other is a squirrel deck. Squirrels are 0 cost 0/1 fodder cards, which can be used as a wall to block 1 card's attack for one turn, or to count as a sacrifice to summon another card. During the summon phase, you select the required sacrifices, which will disappear from the board, and then you can choose which slot to place your new card into. It's during this phase, and the next, that showcases how important positioning is. In the final phase, the battle phase, the turn player's cards will attack the card opposite it. 0 attack cards won't attack, and once a card reaches 0 HP it dies and gets removed from the board. If there is no card opposite, that card will deal damage to the character, which will be indicated on the HP scale. When your opponent's card dies, overkill damage is applied to any cards. Overkill damage to your opponent's HP will great Golden Teeth. Some cards will have a flying trait, which will ignore the opposing side, and directly deal damage to the character. There is also a water (submerge) trait, which hides the card during the opponent's turn, and lets them deal direct damage. Against opponents with those decks, you will want to deal high damage fast, or you may lose.

Most cards require sacrifices, which is an energy system. Sacrificing (and losing) a card will grant 1 sacrifice (1 blood) towards playing a new card. A card could require 4 sacrifices, which means sacrificing up to 4 cards (there is a maximum of 4 cards on the field). Some special cards (like the goat) coat as 3 sacrifices, so you only need to sacrifice that one card. You can only sacrifice 1 card to summon 1 card, so a goat cannot be used to summon 3 cards.

Each card battle, you a presented with a scale on the left hand side of the screen, which indicates the combined HP of your player and your opponent for this match. The scale has 11 values, with the middle value being full lives for both players. I will refer to these values as -5 to 5 to simplify things. Each damage you take from an opponent's card will deduct one life from the scale, and once you reach -5, you lose the match. As this is a shared HP system, the value of the scale can move all the way from -4 to 5 (9 damage), so it's possible for you to be on the brink of death and clutch a victory from a topdeck. Conversely, this can drag out a battle if you're at a standstill, and it can also mean you lose in one turn too (although this is very unlikely).

The full review is in the forums, due to reaching the max character limit: https://steamcommunity.com/app/1092790/discussions/0/3202618375234744040/
