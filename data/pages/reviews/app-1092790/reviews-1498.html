\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think the game is worth it's sub $20 tag, but this comes with a couple of caveats. This is a spoiler-free review to let players decide whether this is worth your money and gaming time.

Type of game: This is a card battler game with different modes. With the Kacey Mod (an official free DLC), the game can now be properly advertised as a Card Battler Rogue-lite game with a story mode.

Art/Style: The absolute best part of the game is its art, and the lo-fi 32 bits Sega Saturn / Dreamcast style dungeon-crawling graphics.

Story: Typical well-written indie game. Good characters, good twists, mysterious enough to keep you engaged. Decent writing for a game.

Gameplay: You should not buy this expecting a solid Rogue-lite deck building game. The first few hours (and promotional materials) mislead players into believing this is a stylish, well designed "Slay the Spire" deckbuilding game. It is not, and that is very unfortunate. The core gameplay is unbalanced, easy to break and sub-par. Opposing enemies literally have 6HP and you can easily use zero mana or one mana cost cards to deal 3 plus damage. The most OP passive skills you can always fuse to your zero cost cards, which breaks the whole experience. Battles are literally decided in the first two turns, which trivializes 90% of the energy/ramping/synergy mechanics that the game has.

Once the story unravels, the players realize the gameplay mechanics are a backdrop for the storytelling, and not the other way around (how it should be with games). Even now with the Kacey mod, it's evident that the gameplay was just a backdrop and not intended to be a balanced experience. Further, the story gives an unneeded sense of urgency that makes players spend half the game rushing through it instead of enjoying the deckbuilding mechanics, as unbalanced as they are. In fact, the last 5 minutes of the game includes two different game modes that the players get to barely enjoy.

Conclusion: If you are expecting a solid deckbuilding game with great graphics, please do not buy this game. Any other deckbuilding game I can think off is better than this. If you want a decent indie storyline… Get Celeste or Undertales for a fraction of the price; or go play Hades, Transistor, etc.

The first few hours are fantastic, but after you understand the initial mechanics, then you start seeing the issues mentioned above. All the deckbuilding battling mechanics are there, complete with complex deck mechanics. With mods and community patches this can become a solid card game in the future, but I highly doubt it will ever be on par with the better deckbuilding games. And evenif that were to happen, there won't be story-telling to carry it anymore, just basically an endless deckbuilding mode devoid of substance.

This started as a Recommended review but after laying out my thoughts, I would honestly advise to spend your time and money elsewhere. This is not a bad game; but it's more art than game; and even then, the art part is not good enough to make players accept the proposal that there's not a proper deckbuilding game here to enjoy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst 1/3rd of the game is masterfully done - great game mechanics and atmosphere. I don't typically care for roguelikes nor deck-building games, but this (read: first 1/3rd of) game does it in a way that keeps things interesting and doesn't involve a lot of downtime reading cards and managing your deck.

Unfortunately, the game takes a nosedive after that, and devolves into generic indie game dev tropes, and completely subverts all the game mechanics that I really liked about the first part. The roguelike mechanics are removed, and you're left to manually manage your deck - grinding the game to a halt whenever you get stuck and need to rearrange your cards to get past a fight.

I usually like when games do a mix-up midway through to change things up and put a pause on any repetition that might make the player lose interest, but this game did it in a way that just makes me feel like I was scammed.\t\t\t\t\t
