Ouroboros is OP as hell and once you have a good deck set up around it the only reason you don't kill every boss before ending your first turn is because the game cheats against you as much as you cheese the heck out of it. It's just amazing.

Because of how unpredictable this game is, I went into what I thought was the "final boss" with what felt like an impossibly, unfairly, OP deck and was still worried I was gonna loose (which I did, the first time).

The story telling and atmosphere of this game are super unique and honestly makes the game worth it just for that.
