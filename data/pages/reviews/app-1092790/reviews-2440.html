\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a pretty incredible game. One that you should definitely play yourself instead of watching, if you think it looks fun.

As with Daniel Mullins' other games it's a very subversive experience, and it manages to incorporate elements from an interesting set of different genres without sacrificing in any one area. Also, I'll mention, from here on there will be very minor spoilers for things introduced extremely early in the game.

The major gameplay consists of card play, and deck building, with a variety of interesting mechanics to keep you engaged. For example, some cards will require a sacrifice of blood to be played, with each card on the board being worth 1 blood, but some specific cards being worth more. As you go you'll also discover more and more sigils, symbols present on the bottom of some cards that imbue them with special abilities, such as flight, which allows a card to attack over others. Items will also be unlocked as you go, allowing you one time use abilities that are replenished at set points. These mechanics and many others come together over the course of the game to keep things fresh, and create a good level of complexity and strategic gameplay that will make you treasure the moments where get to just play cards. Also worth noting is that if you're someone that likes to be rewarded heavily for good strategy, you'll have fun with this, because the game doesn't hesitate to let you build your deck to be totally broken.

I mentioned you might "treasure the moments you get to play cards" because this is a horror game as well, and much more so than I was originally expecting. It has a sort of escape room feel to it, and it does a great job of building an uneasy atmosphere at almost all times, so while you won't be getting jump scared or chased down as you would in something like Outlast, if you don't have a thick skin when it comes to this stuff, you won't be comfortable often, and being deep in thought with a table of cards in front of you starts to become a safe space.

As for the story, it is, perhaps unexpectedly, linear. The deck building card game presentation may lead you to believe it's a roguelite akin to something like Slay the Spire, but this very much so has a start, and an end. It is similar to roguelites however in the sense that the story doesn't ever intrude on the gameplay too much. There are a few moment's that are basically cutscenes, but they're not common, nor very long compared to the stretches of gameplay between, and you even still have some control in them. In terms of it's contents, it's very difficult to say anything without spoiling too much. What I will say is that if you enjoy a story that creates a sense of mystery, likes to play with your head a little bit, and doesn't hesitate to derail itself, or, in fewer words, is very subversive, then you'll enjoy Inscryption's story a lot. It had a good amount of moments that surprised me, made me stop and think, or left me taken aback for a bit, the kind of thing that I personally find to be very memorable and enjoyable about games like this.

On top of all that, it comes at a very fair price point for it's playtime. I took 19 hours to beat it, though I tend to take a lot of time to think, and it includes some moments where I'd left the game open to go eat or something. Perhaps a more accurate measurement of playtime is about 8 and a half hours, as that's what you'll see for the video length on youtube when you search up a full playthrough. It'll also of course depend on your experience with card games and your critical thinking skills, but point is that for 20 dollars anywhere from 8-19 hours is a good amount of playtime.

So overall it's a great game. The card mechanics are pretty deep and engaging, the horror is very well done, and the plot is extremely alluring and kept me interested throughout, but didn't ever take up too much space compared to the gameplay. I bought it on a whim after watching some on youtube, and don't at all regret the decision. It's something you definitely should experience for yourself first, if interested.\t\t\t\t\t
