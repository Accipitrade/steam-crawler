Inscryption is not a card building game you're supposed to play repeatedly for a long time (unless you mod it). It is a limited and very well put together narrative experience riding on some card building mechanics that are either (1) tight and well designed or (2) kind of wacky. It's an amazing ride, not without its flaws.

If you want something like Slay the Spire, you're not getting that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I look for a game to play, I try to find something I think I'll like. But at the same time, I want something new: whether it's an intriguing new game mechanic, an inventive style, or a unique experience that will stay with me for a while. Inscryption checks all those boxes and more, having far exceeded any and every expectation I had for this game. I try not to proclaim games as a must-play, since everyone has their interests, but man you've gotta give this one a try! Truly a unique experience.

Summary

Inscryption is a card-based game--which, if you're like me, that isn't your cup of tea. But here's the thing: unlike other card-based games, this one's actually interesting. You've got a bit of a mystery to solve, a story to unravel, and a madman or two to outwit. There's a roguelike element to the game, but in the same caliber as Hades and Risk of Rain 2: even if you fail, you get a little bit stronger.

In terms of visual style, the graphics look pretty cool. It's... pixelated? If you took a picture of someone and converted it into a lower resolution, then made it more pixel-y. Kinda has that 8-bit/old-school vibe around the edges, but it comes together nicely and looks fantastic.

One of the main things that makes Inscryption feel authentic is the atmosphere. The music, while present throughout the game, is more of a background accompaniment. But when the music does kick it up a notch, it's GOOD. From background noises like wind and trees rustling, to the... slightly less subtle atmos that accompanies boss battles, it adds to the game to make the experience more real. You'll want to bust out your good headphones for this one.

The other part of the game--and in my opinion, the best part--is its story. The story, even just a hint of Inscryption's story, is what caught my attention in the first place. Sure, it's a card game, but... what if it's something more?

Eh, it's probably nothing.

Is it a scary game? Looks kinda spooky...

Short answer: Kind of. But nothing that'll make you screech like a banshee.

Look, I don't like scary games, they're not for me. I think a lot of horror games nowadays hinge on well-timed (or cheap) jumpscares. But if there's a halfway decent story... I might be compelled to look into it. Maybe.

But thankfully, you don't have to worry about that for Inscryption. It's more of a spooky game than a scary game, and I think that's great. I don't have to worry about having a heart attack while I'm trying to focus on the story. I can play this game at night, at home by myself, with headphones, with the lights off, and still sleep peacefully afterwards.

... Well, maybe with the lights on.

Admittedly, there's a few spooky moments. But these don't seem to be intentionally scary--in some parts, it's the game saying, "pay attention," or, "hey, something's new." The rest just makes it more authentic. The game doesn't make you focus on the spooky bits, so it's not really scary.

You don't have to worry about Inscryption haunting your dreams. At least, not in a bad way.

But seriously, as a non-horror gamer, I recommend this for non-horror gamers.

Okay, but what's the selling point?

If the fact that it's a card-based, deckbuilding game makes it good enough for you, yay! Buy it.

If that same fact makes you go "ugh," then-- wait, come back, the review's not finished! I'm on your side!

The story and experience as a whole is going to make or break it for you. Now, I can talk all night about how fantastic and interesting Inscryption is, but the base mechanic is a card game. It's not like you can skip it, you're gonna have to use at least a little bit of strategy in building your deck. But, hey--this is the part where you remember Steam's refund policy: try it for an hour or so, and if you still hate it, refund it. But at least give it a try, even if you don't like card-based games.

Inscryption is a game, but it's also an experience--and it's not one I'll forget soon. Easily one of my favorite games of all time. DEFINITELY my favorite deckbuilding game.

One-sentence summary: I've never wanted to punch a robot in the face so badly, 16/10, would die again.\t\t\t\t\t
