The card-game is the biggest facet, of course, but bears mentioning because of how enjoyable it is to learn and, relative to a game like Slay the Spire or even Monster Train, how easy it is to master. This feeling of mastery gives the rest of the game an odd sort of quality, like there's something you're just not understanding that is absolutely crucial. I don't want to say more than that.

As far as cons go, there's a shift that happens in the gameplay at a certain point that made my brain hurt to the point where I had to take a break for a few days. I can't tell yet if I find that admirable to the point of brilliance, or a misstep in design.
There's also a puzzle that's sort of a facsimile of the card game that I didn't love. Small potatoes, though.

In summation: the game is excellent. Buy it.

EDIT: Just finished the game. There's even more to it than I realized when I wrote the above review, though my opinion has only been bolstered: this is a masterpiece.\t\t\t\t\t
