\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere comes the dissenting voice,

What you're being sold on isn't actually what you get. There are roughly three to five hours of rogue light game play up front with a really a cool and well designed system that's super enjoyable. But it's also shallow and you're going to master it very, very quickly because it's not the game. I can't stress this enough, the part that you are buying and that is actually fun isn't a full game.

Once you've beaten what would normally be your first "run" the game changes into a really bull crap meta narrative. It still has different variety of card play (there are two other major sections, one is tcg-ish and the final one is. . . card rpg?)

And enjoyment shifts majorly. Loved the first section. Hated the middle section actively. And the final section is pretty middle of the road. I was okay with but it's not something I would ever seek out. So most of my experience wasn't positive, and the part that was positive wasn't even the majority.

And because the part I loved isn't fully fleshed and shallow, I don't know if I'll even come back and play that section specifically.

Otherwise, it's presented nicely but the meta elements are infuriating and I often actively hated whoever wrote this. At the time, i assumed it was the actor. Yes, a real human actor, but I think the production is bigger than that, so whatever.

It think's it's being clever. It isn't. Everything past the first 10 minutes of section two is pretty creatively bankrupt. This game was made because people had a "mind blowing idea" for a game, and wow is it ever not.

And that's where I stand. If you can't tell an engaging story or otherwise have an engaging game from start to finish, just make a game that is fun to play. They clearly knew how on some level. It's a shame they abandoned that. It's a 5/10 and I wouldn't recommend it to the advertised audience. If you loved Pony Island and you want to play a more produced version of that play this game. Alternatively, if you are okay dropping 20 dollars on the shortest rogue like every made, go ahead.

And I'd like to address the massively positive reviews here, but the numbers speak for themselves. The number of people who have the steam achievement for clearing the "final boss" is astoundingly high. But the drop of for achievements that you get just by playing the rest of the game is steep. It's hard to read these reviews as knee jerk reactions to people that haven't put in the 3-5 hours to "beat the game" before realizing that's all there is that's fun.\t\t\t\t\t
