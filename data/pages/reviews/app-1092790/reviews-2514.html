\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like card games? You'll like Inscryption. Do you like story games with a mystery? You'll like Inscryption. Do you like Daniel Mullin's crazy meta-trips? You'll like Inscryption. Do you like all three? Then this is your dream game.

Unlike the author's previous work, this game is focused on a central gameplay loop, in the form of a fully fleshed-out card game. You will be playing this card game throughout the entire length of the highly atmospheric story, owing greatly to the presentation provided by the ominous gamemaster sitting opposite you at a table. (Think Slay the Spire meets Hand of Fate.)

And because this is a Daniel Mullins game, neither the gamemaster nor the table are just metaphors. You will be routinely getting up from the table and exploring your surroundings to solve the mindbender of a mystery that is hiding behind the game you're playing. As you do that, new cards and items will be added to the card game, and when you do specific things in the card game, more things outside of it will unravel. This unique mix makes for a highly captivating, addicting experience. One that will easily have you playing for hours at a time. And each time you'll think you have it all figured out, it will throw a new curveball at you to keep things fresh.

The only shortcomings that I see in this work is that learning to be decent at the card game is in fact mandatory, which might turn off people that are in it solely for the story. And the ending feels very abrupt, unless you engage in the wild ARG side-game Inscryption has going. Nevertheless, the rest of the ride is one hell of a ride to go on.\t\t\t\t\t
