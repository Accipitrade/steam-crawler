\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnexpectedly emotional yet unfulfilling ending. This is, of course, due to the real ending being hidden behind a ARG that's long over.

I encourage you to play this game, solve the puzzles without any outside help, and then, and only then, look up the jabbering nonsense that makes up the ARG. I have nothing but respect for the people who solved that ARG, I got as far as the command line prompts and when I realized I was dealing with oblique cyphers, promptly lost interest.

That said, the "real" ending isn't honestly anymore complete in my opinion, it's more of an after-credits scene that does you no help; like Ferris Bueller coming out of the shower and telling you to "go home".

Back to the game itself:
Claustrophobics need not apply as darkness and locked doors are used to great effect and the animation only ever reinforces the notion that you're in the sway of a murderer. But there's a weird air of routine about all the arcane murder and complicated puzzles that creates a sense of foreboding.
I hit a few bugs and even though there's kind of a "rogue-y" element you may still feel the dreaded "that was utter ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥" moment once or twice, but I urge you to push through it. The card game is not balanced, but this can be incredibly fun, because once you learn the rules, you can make it unbalanced in ways you can control or ride like a crashing wave of middle fingers to obliterate every event in the game. Once I got the hang of it, I ended up with one or two cards I could play for free that were very nearly "I win" the card.

Once the game opens up, though, you'll have to adjust and set your ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ cannons on new sights.\t\t\t\t\t
