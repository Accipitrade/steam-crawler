I think it will be revered as one of gaming history's best examples of how to expertly unfold gameplay mechanics and story as one fused element. It's a bit like The Witness where you slowly peel the onion layers back, but it's more direct about conveying the connections to previously seen stuff (not to say that it isn't swathed in intrigue). I had so many satisfying "Ohh that was that huh !" moments.

All in all, Mr Mullins has sold me on card games and any of the future worlds he dreams up! :)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fine, just fine. It's separated out into three major acts, with each act utilizing a different genre to show off the base card game. The first Act is the roguelike that all the promotional material shows off, but the other Acts don't exactly fit that mold (avoiding spoilers). To put it simply, I think if you're enticed by the first Act you should purchase the game and check it out, but I personally didn't realize the game would change, and I'm not loving the last portion of the game at all compared to its beginning.

Personally I was getting trounced throughout the game, but I can expect that with roguelikes and was looking forward to it. I had a ton of fun with the first Act, and enjoyed both the card game mechanics and rules and the escape room shenanigans that the game utilized to proceed further. Unfortunately, I didn't realize that the game itself shifted from this roguelike to a different genre, so that really hampered my enjoyment, even though the second Act was also fun. I again enjoyed proceeding further into the narrative and playing through the story of the game.

The third act is what's killing me right now, though. For one reason or another, I'm having a hard time proceeding forward, and I'm not too sure why. I tend to just lose the first battle that I get into, then spend a couple minutes respawning, walking back to where I lost, picking up my sack of coins and...ramming my head at the battle, until I win, proceed forward, and end up losing the next one. I'm not getting anymore enjoyment out of the experience, and though I understand I could approach the game in a different way (the first one that comes to mind is try to save up coins to buy new cards at the shop/upgrade cards, then return to the fight) the options available neither feel powerful enough to alleviate my woes and let me continue forward, nor worth the effort in doing so. At this point, I think I'm done with the game. Bummer, but that's just me.\t\t\t\t\t
