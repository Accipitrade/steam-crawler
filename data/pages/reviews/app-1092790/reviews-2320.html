\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Very good game worth the price tag, 9/10. Best played fresh with no knowledge of the game.

So first off, the game describes itself as a roguelike but I don't really see where its coming from? The game has replayability but I don't believe it's to the extent of a roguelike. The game better describes as, a card game adventure I'd say. You go through, battle cards, uncover story and proceed to the next act until done. There are 3 acts in the game, and although I've seen other reviews say the one act or the other is bad, I don't see it(?). I truly believe all the 3 acts are good and enjoyable, my least favourite being Act 3 as I didn't enjoy the mechanics as much as the other but still very good.
I'd say I enjoyed ~90% of the game. Thought the graphics even though not being high fidelity, reeks of style and aesthetic. I very much enjoyed the look and feel of the world. I can't say much about the audio side however as I played it with pretty low sound as I usually do in my games but I didn't hear anything that was negative. The 10% I didn't enjoy was (spoilers) the real life stuff with Luke and the non game story. I thought the idea of the 4 scrybes was cool but the real life plot line was pretty uninteresting for me. .
Even though I wouldnt call this a roguelike really, I will be replaying Act 1 cause it is very enjoyable, and I do hope that through an update, DLC, sequel, or whatever means that it is made into a true roguelike with more replayability and growth as it was a great time and great card game mechanics that I liked even though I typically don't enjoy card games.\t\t\t\t\t
