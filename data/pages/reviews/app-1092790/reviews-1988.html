\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe only way to review this game is to spoil it for other players; So I'll try not to spoil the best details.

- You get more than you bargain for from it.

- There's a small learning curve that isn't difficult, but careful cause it expands.

- The story is intriguing.

- The concept is somewhat played out but still interesting nonetheless.
To expand a bit on this, the premise has been done several times.

- Lots of secrets that are shallow enough to see as you play, and others that are... more in depth and not even fully discovered.
Expanding on that, the current juicy super secrets aren't even fully figured out yet.

- Lacks re-playability outside of achievement hunting
Have heard that they will be patching in an 'endless' mode which will give it some mid-term playtime

All in all it's an expertly done game that has a ton of polish in the first couple acts, the later acts kinda fall short of the ambiance that the game had been projecting and the drive to finish the game stemmed from the story that the early acts had and nothing really from the later acts. Gameplay felt a bit more rushed at the end though it is possible this was intended due to the nature of the story. Whatever it may be, the beginning of the game is a solid 10/10 and unique, by mid way it's still a solid 9/10 but getting Omori vibes, but the final acts feel more like 6.5/10 that's completely different from expectations but not in a good way.

Overall it gets a solid 8/10 from me, worth the price and money, may fall short of expectations made in the beginning.\t\t\t\t\t
