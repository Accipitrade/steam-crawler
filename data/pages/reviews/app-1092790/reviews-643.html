\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are thinking about buying this because it was marketed to you as a rogue-like/lite, don't buy it. All screenshots and gameplay you've seen is probably just from the first part of them game in the cabin, which is the first 2 hours of gameplay. After the first act I guess you would call it, it stylistically an mechanically changes drastically, where they feel like two different games based off of one. The second act is only about 4 hours but feels so much longer and loses any interest I had in the story (not even gonna talk about the youtube style videos that contain half the story and everytime it happens brings the game to a screaming halt). When you hit the second act you can instantly redo fights (You will lose on the second turn or win the "fight") and with this you lose the last pretence this game had to say it was a roguelike, because every fight they will do the same moves, only difference is the cards you draw. Even though the third act returns to more of the first art style and gameplay many things are quite different. Losing still doesn't mean anything, besides having to walk back to the fight from a checkpoint two screens away with no danger in between. Your deck in act 3 will just keep increasing, you can't really get rid of cards. Feel like I got scammed on a by the marketing, I would recommend slay the spire instead if you want a rogue-like card builder.

TL DR: You get two hours of a roguelike then becomes a linear story card based game. Only randomness in the game is your rewards\t\t\t\t\t
