\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAct 1: Act one is absolutely splendid. The actual gameplay of the turn based card game has been done countless times before, but Inscryption breaths fresh new life, being in my mind, one of, if not the best game I have played in this scene.
If you're thinking of playing Inscryption and are reading this beforehand stop here. I can vouch that the first few hours will be a blast, but my praise ends there.
Note: (It's not nearly as luck based as many people like to comment about.)

Warning: spoilers from here on

I really did want this to be a positive review but the first third just can't make up for the rest of the game.

Act 2: I got to act two probably around three or four hours into playing and just like everyone else says it's true this is the falloff point for this game. The main reasons for this act's flop are, A they flip the style dramatically, and B they try to cram SO MANY things into solely act two. In act two alone you are introduced to about 60 or so new cards, a battery meter, magic gems, electric currents, and more with little to no ease in. Compare this to the slow and steady introduction of lives, bones, and the different types of items and spaces of act one. In fact the ease in was so non-existent that I didn't even bother to learn the new systems until act three and in act two I just used the cards in the beast deck plus skeletons to wipe the floor with every enemy that came my way.

Note: I think it's worth mentioning how you only ever see act one in promotional material they don't even have any mention of them in the game description. You could argue this is to prevent spoilers but I'm going to take a leap and say they just didn't want to show act two as the style is far less, "pretty" and would turn many people off from the game. (I do think that they refuse to show act three because of spoilers though.)

Act 3: After the very unpleasant hour I spent in act two, can act three make-up for this failure by ending the game with a clean finish? Kind of. I was expecting the game to keep going downhill after act two, and while it's not nearly as good as act one, act three is definitely better than act two. Act three is what act two should have been. Act three is basically act one but with robot cards and worse in practically every way (still better than act two though lol.) The first problem with act three comes in the form of Po3. Po3 is not NEARLY as interesting as Leshy, essentially, wherever Leshy succeeds Po3 fails. (I would go deeper into detail about this but I think this is generally agreed upon so I feel no need.) The second problem in act three is the area itself. The cabin is so interesting because of the mystery, where the factory has no sense of this. For one you have already been to the factory in act two you know the basic layout so to cope for this they would have more secrets and discoverable things here right? wrong! In the factory we have one good puzzle, it's the lock combination finder to get to the goo world. The two other puzzles are more of the same things we did in act one, and then we have a lore dump. I won't make this any longer with why the actual board game sucks but to summarize it, it's empty and repetitive.

Note: One other thing I want to cover is the file deletion sequence that everyone seems to freak out over, I admit it is pretty wild to have your files actively dug through by a game before your eyes, but it doesn't actually delete your files it leaves a note saying you should delete it because that's what you agreed on in the game. Just test things before you make crazy accusations. I can confidently say that no game with hundreds of thousands of downloads and 45,000 steam reviews would get away with blatantly deleting files right in front of you.

This game feels like a great game that wasn't long enough so the developers haphazardly stapled on some more hours of sluggish content. But of course that is not the case as 99% of the lore comes from acts 2&3.

Summary: You will love the first few hours of Inscryption it is an innovative turn based card game with a horror aesthetic and puzzles to boot. You won't like the other 2/3s of the game though. (Unless you REALLY like lore)

(It would still be really badass to get a real life board game of act ones cards and rules.)
