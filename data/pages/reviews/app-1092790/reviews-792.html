I will skip the basic summary of the game as anyone who is looking into this likely has seen the store page. This is a game that I think does a very good job at teaching the player and introducing interesting mechanics to shake things up as it goes along. Really?) The first thing you'll hear opening the game is plot-related from a guy you may or may not appreciate being part of the game. Without spoiling anything, just know that what you're seeing is only the first act of the game. And don't worry, if you want to play more of what you're seeing on the store page, you can. The updated mode is called Kaycee's Mod and unlocks as you beat the game. Which brings me to the gameplay.

The game was designed much as a story experience. Some things in it are made to be broken. It's still a game so you can't go completely braindead, but if you're worried about not being to complete an Act, don't because you're eventually going to find the right combo that lets you end encounters in one turn or lets you spam infinite cannon fodder. Then there's Kaycee's Mod. Kaycee's Mod is meant to just be the card game you're seeing up above. Don't worry, it's rebalanced for difficulty (Not a good selling point unless you've already played the Act 1 portion really.)

KCM is a good deck-builder to casually play whenever. Play KCM. Use the challenge level to unlock new cards. Push the limit. Get salty that your single life run got ended because another challenger gave you a totem encounter where you had to fight double strike murder.

Fun game. Would recommend. Don't staple squirrels.\t\t\t\t\t
