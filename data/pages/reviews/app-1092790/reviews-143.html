\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to defy the crowd and say "no" to this, especially since I'm aware the current (first) chapter is considered the most fun, and have little hope that slogging through any more will improve things. First of all, the visual and sound design, meant to come across as unsettling, are really just grating and repetitive; even from a basic experiential standpoint the game wore out its welcome after the first hour. Second and more importantly, the game tries to be a quasi-roguelike in which you are repeatedly allowed to die and start over so that you feel the "horror" of failure but then go into the next attempt better equipped than before. But the lack of player agency is so total -- down to the outright inability to start a new game until you've completed the current one -- that you're really just dependent on ever-thinning luck to rebuild your deck even as the complexity of the opposition continues to grow, meaning I expect to be stuck indefinitely in card hell until some incredible stroke of luck manages to break me back out of it. (Adding to which is that all the several environmental pickups I accumulated during the first run are permanently gone.) Again, I think it's trying to hit us with the "horror" of helplessness and setbacks, but that works better in a fast-paced action or platforming title than in a grating and slow-paced deck builder. So far the overall experience is just so insulting to the player that I honestly see little reason to waste much time on it.\t\t\t\t\t
