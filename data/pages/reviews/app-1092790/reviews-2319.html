\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst and foremost, I want to make it clear that I personally do not usually enjoy nor play card games. This review is solely from the perspective of someone who was captivated by the atmosphere, writing, and general aesthetic and thus this review is aimed towards those who somewhat feel the same. This review also contains minor story spoilers.

I remember seeing this game first shown during the Devolver Digital direct and immediately blew it off as something I was not interested in. It just seemed like yet another generic card game to ride on the popularity wave that has been somewhat up and coming the past few years. However, upon watching a few streamers play it, I was immediately interested by it's simplicity and atmosphere. The writing and mood were FANTASTIC and it was easily one of the most unique games I've played this year thus far and I looked forward to advancing and figuring out the puzzles in the cabin. While I am not usually someone who plays or enjoys card games, the mechanics were easy to pick up and the difficulty curve, while sometimes frustrating with a roguelike aspect to it, was very satisfying to over come. Leshy's animations were foreboding and creepy: long, bony fingers occasionally peaked from the shadows to impatiently tap the table, a spider skittering across, thinking you can peak at the faint silhouette of his face only for him to lean back into the shadows. It was all so captivating. Each failure opened up new mysteries to be solved, and skills learned. The final boss fight felt momentous to me. I had done it, i had beaten the game master!

Then the final act was done and I was thrusts into a new style of game, this time an SNES style card collector game with a whole new slew of mechanics to learn. I must now build and balance my own deck, learn of 4 different classes of cards, and everything learned and gained from chapter 1 is completely thrown out of the window, including the horror and roguelike aspects. The section you see during all promotional material, and everything shown on the steam store, lasts a total of maybe 2 hours depending on how you play. It truly is like a completely different game and chapter 2 is a complete SLOG to play through, especially if you do not have the patience to forget and relearn a new set of game mechanics and the burden of managing a balance of cards and decks with new balances. The story turned bad, with terrible irl recorded cut scenes that were just boring to sit through. It was like being sucker punched.

Chapter 3 somewhat tries returns to what Chapter 1 had done, but with slightly more complex mechanics, yet to me it still fell short and reverts to the generic story line of "computer bad, evil awakened AI is the true boss" that has plagued indie games for nearly half a decade now. It was quaint in 2017, it is annoying now.

The atmosphere of chapter 1 never returns, and frankly I would even go as far to say that it would even be false advertising. Everything you have seen streamed, every screenshot you see, the entire demo, every promotional material on the store page is ONLY FROM THE FIRST CHAPTER which is only maybe 2-4 hours long. This is NOT a horror deck building roguelike card game and it has ZERO replayability. The whole experience will last you maybe 10 hours from start to finish. If it were not for the fact that I have already spent 4 hours playing this game, I would have requested a refund from Steam.

Perhaps I am being too harsh on this game, and I am clearly not the audience for it. The effort and polish and thought put in this game is a very rare thing nowadays, and I applaud the developer on what he has created. We need more unique games like this to keep the market fresh. But if you are a casual player, or do not have the patience for card games, then this may not be for you and I would not recommend it. Just play the amazing demo and you would have experienced the best that this game has to offer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played card games since the inception of Magic: The Gathering. The first act of this game leaned heavily into that style of play, and I enjoyed that, along with the atmosphere, and the intrigue on offer in the cabin. Having finished the game, I kinda wish I had stopped after act one.

Once you make it through the first act, the game turns into some kind weird jrpg or grid-explorer, that carries the card elements into it and multiplies the variety 4x, but has none of the atmosphere, tension, or intrigue. In fact, other than the occasional RNG difficulties, the rest of the game was pretty easy by comparison, and the new cards relatively pointless. Sprinkle that in with some meta-narrative and game-genre bending, and I would say this game tried to be too much, all over the place, and came away not being great at any of it. By the end, I was just clicking as fast as I could, hoping it would end in the hope something special would come of the overarching narrative (It didn't).

Don't get me wrong, the deck-building part has a kernel of something special, and if they had just made a card game with the styles on offer, without all the extra meta nonsense, this would have been solid. Overall, though, I would say give this one a miss unless you are 100% crazy DEEP into both card games, roguelikes, and maybe jrpgs or old Grimrock-style adventures.

Finally, I have to agree that the screenshots and description are somewhat misleading for this title.\t\t\t\t\t
