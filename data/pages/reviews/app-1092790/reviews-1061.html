\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an Indie masterpiece, any TCG enthusiast will love the playstyle that Inscryption provides. Now that being said, new players will feel confused at the inception meta narrative of the game which can't be helped. The game thrusts you into the perspective of a man named Luke playing a videogame on a flopy drive which he finds in the woods. *note you won't know this until you finish the first act of the game* Upon playing you are thrust into a tabletop style cardgame with interesting characters and mechanics.

I give the story a 9.5 out of 10
The gameplay 10/10
If you played Yu gi oh, dnd or magic the gathering/hearthstone, You will love the crap out of this game.
lasting remarks, The developer is highly educated in religious culture.

Now in terms of Christian parenting. Should you let your child play this?

Yes as long as they are above the age of 10, or have been involved with card game motifs.

For Christians wanting to protect their child or have religious concerns based on imagery or iconography. Here's the education of what you should know beforehand. (Ie relating towards conceived satanic imagery, and perceived paganistic symbology)

Inscryption is a game that Heavily focus's or plays fun of folklore commentary. It has allot or Jewish symbology towards the demonic realm that many Christians believe is hell, known in hebrew texts as "the wilderness." It shows the symbology of sacrificing a goat to the wilderness or adversaries of the Jewish God. (See ie Goat and bonelord) It harkens back to old customs of a priest praying over a goat, and transfering the sins of man or a nation to said goat for sacrificing as a means of cleansment. The game also has references to the ancient custom of Idolatry regarding totems, where a spirit or in this case a sigil inhabits an image of a card race archetype.(Ie wolf, bird, insect, and reptile) In ancient times pagans would make a clay Idol of a foreign god they wish to worship and conduct a ritual to open its mouth so that the spirit of said god would inhabit it. People then would sacrifice to the idol in exchange for power. In Inscryption this is an optional game gimmik meant to aid in card battles that doesn't bare the same depth of representation.

Okay now in regards to the pentagram, the symbol has been co-opted throughout history. In terms of the ancient origination for Judaic Christian beliefs, it represented the adversaries of Israel when Babylon was using the symbol to represent Ishtar during their times of war and conflict. In the case for this game, the symbol is used to highlight the use of magic and foreshadowing events in the background meta story, which is a supernatural spy noir mystery. You can rest assured that your Child that is over the age of 10 will not be exposed to anything Harmful to your views or values, nore will they involve themselves with the ARG aspect of the game post completion. Most of what would be considered concerning to Christian's would only be in the first Act of the game, which can be reassuringly demystified with the proper explanation and education.
For sited education resources, See Dr. Michael Heiser

Kudos to Daniel Mullins for making a great intellectually and culturally inspired game. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t
