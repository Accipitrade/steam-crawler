\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed Inscryption a lot, but...I just wish it was something other than what it is.

Spoilers likely follow.

It's not clear from the start what kind of game Inscryption is supposed to be, but you very quickly get a sense that you must expect the unexpected - that there's a whole lot more to the game than the extra-atmospheric "Slay the Spire" sort of spinoff that it first seems. But unfortunately, that sense never quite leaves, even after you've completed the whole thing. You're never far from thinking, "Okay, I'm enjoying this...but what's the ACTUAL game? And when do I reach it?"

I want more from Inscryption. And while that's often a good thing - it's always better for a book, movie, or game to make you wish there was more of it, rather than that there was less - it also can be very unsatisfying. I enjoyed the trek through the different "eras" of the card game, and the different rules and presentations each offered. It reminded me of Portal 2, where you get to experience "what brought us here" - that is, why the game/setting is in its current state, and how it used to be. But it was all tied together with this "the game is an evil entity that's trying to control you and the only way out is to delete it" nonsense that just wasn't necessary at all.

There's a really mechanically sound deckbuilder here. It does open itself up to being too easy (once you merge that 0-cost 4/3 death card with a Mantis God, for example, there's no longer any challenge), but on the whole it's fun, with plenty of mechanics that you can take or leave depending on your desired level of complexity. But I would have appreciated Inscryption so much more if it had just been presented as a journey through a card game's history, rather than some goofy story of some YouTube guy who finds a possessed game and decides he needs to beat it.

I'm still recommending Inscryption, without a doubt. There's a reason it has 97% positive reviews, and that's because the gameplay is quite good. But the story they chose just felt like it was one big missed opportunity.\t\t\t\t\t
