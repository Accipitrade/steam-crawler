\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA lot of the other reviews for the game suggest playing the game blind without looking at any videos, screenshots, or spoilers, of which I agree to an extent. I also wouldn't really look at the trailer for the game on the store page as that gives a lot of information about the story away that's more to fun to see first hand. But there are still things you should know to decide whether or not you'll want to buy the game.

When I read that this game was a horror deck building card game that's best played blind, I was ready to buy it immediately. I like all those things, and I've been screwed a few times by researching games that are best played blind because I never expected to buy and enjoy them only to change my mind too late. Inscryption was also on a small sale for about 10% off.

This game is, in my opinion, a mystery game first and a card game second. I bought this game expecting something like Slay the Spire, except with horror elements and a cool story (this is what a lot of the other reviews claim). If you're looking for something to come back to again and again, this isn't it. The fictional card game you play, 'Inscryption', is pretty fun and engaging, but takes a back seat to the story. A lot of the bosses are very fun and cool, just easy. I never felt challenged, except for the beginning. After each loss you get closer to winning as the game gives you stronger and stronger advantages over time. The game wants you to win so it can show you what comes next.

Trying to be spoiler free, there certainly is cool stuff that comes next. I just think that the important thing buyers should know is that this game doesn't have strong replayability. The strongest parts of the game are the presentation and the twists. Once that's over, you don't have much. It's a bit of a shame because the card game has some serious polish and love put into it. You never get to see 'Inscryption' at its best. There's always a mechanic or story influenced decision that changes how 'Inscryption' is played. Whether you think that's for good or for worse is up to you. For me, I just feel upset that 'Inscryption' had to be sacrificed to tell a story that captures your attention, but nothing else once it's over.

To comment on the story, I feel like it and the expanded ARG are something you would find on creepypasta.com from 2008. It just doesn't really make any sense, but it certainly held my interest until the end. It's an interesting story that isn't very good once you have all the pieces. Small spoiler that doesn't really matter: Hitler is involved for some reason.

This review sounds negative, but I still enjoyed the game and would recommend it to people who know what they're getting into. I didn't know what I was getting into, but still ended up begrudgingly happy. I just wish it was different than what I got.

EDIT:

They released a gamemode where you can play the first iteration of 'Inscryption' as you would a normal rogue-like deck building game, which alleviates a lot of my complaints of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

There is no shape or form to describe how amazed and completely blown out I'm after playing and completing the game at 100%. Making this easily one of my favorite games of all time. This is not your conventional horror game and it is a testament on how storytelling, ambiance, music and characters can make such a unique and superb experience.

There are only 3 complaints I have with this game:
The first one would be that the game ends, the second would be that it left me craving for more and the third and final complaint would be that the game becomes less of a horror game on Acts 2 and Act 3 (still there are some horror elements present)... however, the way the story is told, the twists it takes and the subversion of expectations make this point almost irrelevant for me.

You really need to play this game for yourself, you are making a disservice to yourself not doing it.

Final score
10/10\t\t\t\t\t
