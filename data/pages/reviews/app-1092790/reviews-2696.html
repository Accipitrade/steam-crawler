\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm just gonna skip the 2nd "half" of the game where it is very different than expected and stick to the part that I came to play, the section in the cabin.
If you liked the demo, then stop there, a large portion of the game won't be that. Honestly I'd want my money back, but hey they got me, so according to Steam they get to keep my cash.

It's a wonderful idea and really well executed. Love the mood but I'm just not a fan of how it all came together. I liked the card battler and the escape room feel of the cabin but the plot and the heavily scripted nature of the cabin battles wasn't my cup of tea.

It's well done but in the end I feel like I'm spending every battle just looking for the way to cheese through it. The card battles are more like puzzles, so typically when I reach a new puzzle you lose and die until you've seen all the scripted 'gotcha' moments of each battle. The game is built around this loop of the bosses springing a new mechanic on you that you'll have to cheese your way around once you know to expect it. The game does this all very well but it wasn't the experience I expected.

The gimmick with the talking cards also didn't go as far or remain as interesting as I expected from the demo and trailers. As far as I can tell the demo has about 70% of all the stuff you'll do in the cabin, and I was able to find all the clues and unlocks in the first hour or 2 after re-completing what you can play in the demo.

If you enjoyed the demo, I say stop there and just keep playing the demo.

Edit: After completing the game I stand by my original feelings. The game becomes & remains something different than pitched and that I don't think is fairly shown in the marketing or demo. Mainly I don't think the game was improved by this switch in style, and the gameplay is changed but not improved.

Edit Again: The Kaycee's Mod update does give a great reason to pick up this game if you enjoyed the demo, it does a better job of giving the feeling of a rougelike card battling game and adds some interesting new challenges and difficulty. I haven't wanted to go back and delve into the new update but it deserves to be mentioned as a step in the direction of what I hoped the game was going to be when I first tried it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo start off: I have more than the time it shows. I spent the entirety of my playtime prior to the review on offline mode, so the actual hours spent aren't being shown, but i digress. (add about 6 hours to whatever it shows that I have)

This game is really cool. It's extremely well-polished; to the point where I'm thoroughly impressed. They thought about everything, and didn't leave anything out. There were times where i was wondering "Oh, I wonder what would happen if I were to try this?" then I did it, and it just works, and fits in with all the other systems in the game. They really thought about what players may and may not try to do, and decided to implement things into the game for all of those options.

Aside from the well-polished nature of the game, it's really fun. As far as deck-building-rougelites go, this one is actually fairly unique in what it does. Sure, there's inspiration/elements from slay the spire, but i don't think you can really step away from that in this genre. Inscryption is the most unique one I know of so far, at the very least. It has a cool blend of puzzle-solving elements in it, some I just guessed through, and slammed my head against tbh, but as far as the card-battles go, they play out far more similar to a game of MTG or Hearthstone as opposed to the usual deck-building-rougelites which focus more on dealing damage to specific characters, rather than the cards themselves, and thats a nice change. A normal card-battling roguelite is something I feel like I havent seen much of. That said, its not JUST a normal card-battling roguelite. There's certainly more to it, but that's for you to discover.

I have to say, the story is quite interesting. I guess I'm fairly invested. I'm still curious about what's going on, and its keeping me hooked. I won't say much more than that to avoid spoiling anything, but the story is good.

The sound design and atmosphere are phenomenal as well. The graphics are fairly simple, yet enough to set the mood, but combined with the sound, it gives you that feeling of playing an actual tabletop game, just with a really creepy looking dude looking back at you the whole time. It is a little dark, but that's again, to set the mood for the game's creepy/horror feeling that they want you to experience. That said, the game isn't actually really scary, so if you're on the fence because you were or weren't expecting to get the **** scared out of you, that probably won't happen.

This game is definitely something you should experience for yourself. I watched a bit of gameplay and reviews before buying it, but theres a LOOOOOOT more to this game than what you can see in those videos. It's worth checking out for sure. I hope you give it a try!\t\t\t\t\t
