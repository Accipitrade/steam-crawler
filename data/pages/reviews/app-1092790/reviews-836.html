There are three acts, and act 1 is the longest by a little bit and by far the best. Act 2 and 3 are mediocre. The story, despite what the rest of these reviews might lead you to believe, is horribly mediocre. If you've played an indie game from the last seven years you won't find it surprising, shocking, or subversive which is clearly what they're going for. It does push the boundaries with what they've included superficially, but the plot is garbage.

However, I've had a lot of fun with the endless mode, which is essentially a repeatable and more difficult Act 1. The game mechanics encourage you to break the game wide open, and the sheer difficulty stops your victories from feeling cheap. If you can get through over half of the main game, you'll be left with a very fun roguelike.\t\t\t\t\t
