\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary: Worth playing. Some missed potential, otherwise it'd have been a masterpiece.

Description: Slay-the-Spire-like roguelite deckbuilder wrapped up in a couple of layers of other game type.

Narrative: Really good from a character standpoint, particularly during the first part of the game. After that . . . well, it's strange to say it like this, but the weirder things got, the less weird they got. I don't really know how to elaborate on this without spoilers, but by the end of the game I was simultaneously pining for the first part of the game again and ready to be done with it. There's a fine line between good creepypasta and self-insistence, and this started out walking the wire like a world class trapeze artist and ended hanging from the line and scrabbling to pull its way back up. But it's still a good time!

Gameplay: It's a good time. Apart from (and glommed onto) the deckbuilder system itself, he game includes some nifty mechanics that current-gen players may not see coming.

DIfficulty: A bit easy. You might finish parts of the game without actually seeing all the interesting narrative stuff. (Imagine playing Hades for the first time as a top-tier twitch gamer--same thing, but you don't have to be particularly skilled to be good enough to miss things.) The post-game is harder but removes some of the really fun mechanics; I can't really recommend it.

Audio: Excellent.

Graphics: Appropriate.

Mods: Don't be fooled by lack of Workshop support and Nexus presence. There's a decent-sized mod library at https://inscryption.thunderstore.io/ that might give some more life to the game once you've finished it. Definitely don't do any of that until you've finished it, though!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn incredible game worthy of the praise it receives, not only is the gameplay loop in the early and late game entertaining with various strategies and combo possibility but the story itself is engaging, thought provoking and slightly emotional towards the end. The characters are incredibly fleshed out despite seemingly not having very much in the way of screen time (If you played you know which ones) and i had more than a few laughs while reading the dialogue.

What made the game even better was the greater mystery that the game presents not even a few moments after beginning (But really kicks off shortly after your first loss). The presentation and "Piece it Together" style of story telling really made it a blast to put things in order while constantly developing new strategies and all the while trying to figure out the next puzzle!

Ultimately the game is an absolute solid masterpiece of a game with brilliant yet simple game design, a striking visual style that constantly keeps you engaged (and sometimes creeped out) and a mystery that will absolutely bring a "Eureka!" when you finally put it all together!

10/10 and a highly recommended pick for those that like battle cards and puzzles\t\t\t\t\t
