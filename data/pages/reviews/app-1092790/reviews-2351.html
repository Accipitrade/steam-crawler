\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo start this review off, I have not finished "part 2" of this game. I don't know what comes after. But I LOVED this game up until this point. Last night I sat there for 2 hours grinding like a Runescape bot so that I could just get this over with. I realized then that I'm probably just going to go play other video games now, and maybe you'll feel that way too. I'm surprised more people in the reviews don't, honestly.

I almost feel like I've been baited. The first part was astounding, but I honestly loathe the next part. This is a game that really needs you to not spoil the game for yourself, and I wont spoil anything other than vaguely explaining gameplay mechanics really in this review, but the promotional content for the game hardly even acknowledges the existence of anything past the first part. I get that you wanna surprise the audience but it feels like it was more important to subvert my expectations than it was to deliver enjoyable gameplay. You could have at least delivered enough information to help me know if this game was for me or not. YOU SOLD ME ON PART 1, but then you deliver part 2 and I feel betrayed, almost angry.

Going into part 2, the story, characters, sound, etc remains excellent, though subjectively I MUCH prefer the graphics and the richer, non chiptune-like soundtrack of part 1. However the quality of the gameplay after part 1 took a sharp nose-dive for me. In part 1, you acquire cards and powerful buffs for your cards over your "journey". (I use these quotes to explain the game in the sense of pure mechanics, without spoiling how it technically works in terms of story, visuals, etc.) You do this by moving across the paper map (look in the trailer video) and encountering a wide array of cool "events" that allow you to make a diverse array of choices for how you want to make your deck stronger. This is how your deck is built. I found this really fun. In part 2, there are no powerful buffs or events or anything at all really. You only get card packs instead, 1 of 4 themes, each containing 1 rare and 2 common of the theme you chose plus 2 random cards. I do not find this fun anymore. This is now "every other card game".

You lose your squirrel stack, squirrels being cards you sacrifice in order to play useful cards, and the squirrels are put into your deck instead. This makes drawing cards much less reliable, since you can no longer decide whether you want to draw a squirrel or a card from your deck, and since each match of the card game usually last around 5 turns or even less, it's basically made it so a lot of my matches are decided just based off of what I topdeck, or draw upon the opening round of the match. What happens if you only draw cards that cost sacrifices, but you don't draw something TO SACRIFICE? Or vice versa? What if you only draw cards that cost 2 or more, and you only draw 1 thing to sacrifice? If you don't get something on the board you will most likely lose in 2 turns, which means 4 cards you draw on opening, and 1 card after turn 1. So 80% of what will save you from immediate failure is what you topdeck. It's really "fun" topdecking a bad hand 4 times in a row, and just knowing there's no point in trying in those games. Just forfeit and try again. Oh and by the way, your opponent doesn't have to sacrifice cards. Your opponent just plays them for free.

This frustrating RNG is compounded further by the fact that in part 1, if you lose you lose 1 of your 2 lives and then you "die and restart". In part 2, it's more like pokemon where if you lose you can just talk to the guy again, there's no penalty. This made it so the difficulty of the game needed to be ramped up since you can just keep trying over and over and it's not important if you lose once or twice.

So here's how that played out for me: I get to the "first boss" of part 2, I proceed to lose 20 times in a row because I drew bad cards, and then I topdeck a good hand and win without any effort at all. My brain has now turned off. This no longer feels like a game where I need to try and optimize my decisions. You probably could. Part 2 lets you alter your deck at will from a reservoir of cards you accrue, so you could definitely create some synergies. But when my matches go so WILDLY different based off of what I topdeck, I don't want to bother because it really doesn't FEEL like it matters.

Here's what really frustrates me though: Part 1 is like a rogue-like. What happens if you get bad cards? You're probably gonna "die and restart". You can't grind infinitely until you're ready to proceed, you just proceed. What happens if you get bad cards in part 2? Well you can't "die and restart," so in order to avoid soft-locks you gotta be able to grind infinitely. And you can. And it is SO INSANELY BORING. It's a target dummy that LITERALLY does not fight back. If you go look at the steam guides for this game, half of them are how to grind out part 2 as fast as possible, which really says something about how fun it must be.

Here's something funny: Remember how there are 4 card pack themes? Well they're themed after the "zones" in part 2, and you only get card packs of the appropriate theme in each "zone". When you first enter part 2, you get to choose your first card pack to start your deck, 1 of 4 themes. After you do this, you promptly go find out that 2 of the "zones" are locked away until you beat 1 "zone". So what happens if you chose one of the card packs of a "zone" that's locked away, and let's say you get cards that RELY on other cards in the same theme that you DON'T HAVE and you CAN'T GET until you BEAT AN ENTIRE ZONE, and what if THE ONLY WAY TO GRIND FOR MORE CARDS IS LOCKED UNTIL YOU BEAT AN ENTIRE ZONE. If this happened to you, would you find it funny? A friend of mine is currently finding this very NOT FUNNY. I personally think it's HILARIOUS.

Honestly, I think objectively the quality of part 2 is a fair bit lower than part 1. It feels like all the love went into part 1, and part 2 was an afterthought. Either that or they always wanted to have this wacky meta part 2 thing, but they didn't really plan it out until they had already settled on how part 1 would work, and then they were like "wait ♥♥♥♥ I don't know how we're going to get this to work well but still be different from part 1" so they just slapped it together best they could.


TL;DR:

They brilliantly executed an idea and instead of making the entire game around this 1st part, they proceed to launch you into a quite middling 2nd part in order to hit you with the "wow this game is meta and crazy" factor. Once the surprise of "wow haha it's a different game now this is so META" wore off, I missed the old graphics, the rich music, the visual clarity of the cards, the unique control and feel of the game, and without spoiling it, the perspective you're immersed in. I hate the new new graphics, the plain chiptune music, the frustrating over-pixelization of the cards and board, the standard, non-unique control and feel of the game, and the new perspective you find yourself in. The few gameplay changes instantly turned the game into a mindless, rng, grindfest for me. And yes, I AM BEING MORE HARSH THAN I NEED TO. I only do this because over the entire course of part 1, my passion and love for the game grew and grew until it reached the climax. And then they sweep the rug out from under you. Part 1, the stuff that you see in all of the promotional content (minus like 1 second OR LESS in the middle of the trailer), the stuff you play in the demo, and apparently the only content that was in the beta of the game, is only part 1 and not just the entire damn game. I was so confident that this was my game of the year, and now I feel like this game just broke up with me. Sad times.\t\t\t\t\t
