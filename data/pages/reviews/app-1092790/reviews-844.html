\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike my flying Mantis Gods, I'll get right to the point. Inscryption is a genuine indie gem. One of the few indie games that's actually as clever as it thinks it is. The visuals are distinct and clear and the UI relating to card abilities is amazing (It's not like Yugioh where a card's abilities rival the length of The Decline And Fall Of The Roman Empire). The soundtrack is absolutely phenomenal and match every single fight brilliantly. The difficulty of the game (provided you DON'T use the Ouroboros) is decently challenging to both card game veterans and newbies alike.

But the core of the of the game is its characters. And I can't say much about them without spoiling stuff, but if you thought the gamemaster in the start of the game was interesting...hoo boy are you in for a treat!

Honestly, when I first saw the concept, I was scared that it would force itself to stay a horror title with no greater premise than the same "mundane thing or genre that isn't horror BECOMING horror" that has PLAGUED Steam since the FNAF days. But clearly I wasn't giving the game enough credit. It's a masterful game that manages to be funny, touching and ominous without every feeling schizophrenic in tone while being backed up by an immensely satisfying core gameplay loop.

Play the hell out of this game. $29 may seem a bit high, but it's worth it. I'd bet you my best Geck on that promise.\t\t\t\t\t
