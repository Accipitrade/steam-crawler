\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I could describe this game in one word it would be Ritualistic. The art style is like straight out of a cultists wet dream and the gameplay is ritualistic in a derogatory sense of the word. It is kind of annoying you have to wait for all the small story interactions, and the defeat sequence is just as annoying but necessary for the progression of the game, so skipping it is impossible. This is a rougelite type game so its easy to muddle your way through till you win it. I wouldn't say this is a non-recommendation but I am saying there are more than enough annoying things to not give it a glowing recommendation. However; It is definitely interesting enough to buy for 20 bucks and play a few hands to experience the atmosphere. I appreciate the experience and I'll probably have a few nightmares from it. Congratulations to the dev team.

Addition:
I finished through the game and I would give a negative review if I felt petty enough but since I am committed with that masterful ritualistic comment i had in the beginning of the review, I'll have to deal with it. Game mechanics got thrown out the window during the last third of the game. I was banging my head on a wall trying to get through some of those battles with the ♥♥♥♥ deck and mechanics that you have to deal with. If its RNG then you need more chances for dynamic play styles. If its atmosphere you are going for, then you need more guardrails on the track for the first 2 bosses. I had no legitimate strategy for my 3rd deck by game design. I didn't have that problem with the first 2 thirds of the game. It ruined the experience of the horror because I just wanted it to be over with. I didn't have a problem beating the last 2 bosses, in fact I one shot them. I have nothing more to say really. Duck you.\t\t\t\t\t

The following review contains a few spoilers. I put them into a spoiler tag but still: If you want to go into the game without knowing ANYTHING about it, don't read my review. Even without reading the spoiler tag, you will spoil a bit of the game because… it's impossible to describe what I don't like about it, without giving pieces of information about it.

Inscryption SEEMS to be a horror roguelike deckbuilder.

Battle
Card battles are very fun and addictive. You start with a small deck of four different cards. Most cards need sacrifices to be played. For that, you can draw squirrels. They can't attack and just have 1 live but can be sacrificed to summon stronger cards.
Each turn, all of your cards attack either the enemy or the opposite card. If cards reach zero HP they are destroyed for the rest of the battle.
A scale decides who wins. It will balance out depending on who does how much damage. This means, there are no Lifepoints or health points which you need to reduce to zero.

You can also use several different items during a battle, like playing extra cards, putting something on the scales, etc.
Cards have dozens of different seals attached to them (for example "flying", which means the card can do damage to the enemy directly even if a card is in the opposite field, and many more).

Maps
The game consists of three maps, each with procedurally generated nodes but the bosses are always the same. After the third map, you need to play against the game master. During your run, you will meet a merch, a trapper, and several other different events to merge cards, strengthen cards, and more.

Escape Room
The game takes place in something like an escape room. You can stand up whenever you like and look out for secrets, etc. You will find new items, solve riddles, find new cards, etc. All in all, most riddles are pretty easy to solve and the game will almost tell you what to do.
This isn't too bad considering how good the overall atmosphere is. How the gamemaster tells the story and events, the lighting, music, and visuals are stunning.

A masterpiece?
All of the above-combined results in a masterpiece. Well, maybe except for the fact, that there are too few cards, events, enemies, and maps. The game described above would be a masterpiece if you add more of everything. There are so many little details making it a fun and addictive experience, I can't even describe all of them. Another reason for me not to describe everything like I usually do is: The game turns out something completely different. It's just misleading.

The End? (Contains Spoiler)
After beating the gamemaster (shouldn't take much longer than 3 hours) the game ends. Or you thought so but in fact, you need to restart the game. This was just 1/3 of the whole game as I read. What makes this worse is the following: It gets a completely different game. I was so disappointed, I couldn't play it anymore. The game isn't completely bad after that but it's just not as good anymore. The visuals are gone, the music gets worse, the story is just.. Well. I get that this is on purpose and the store page, pictures and everything else is misleading to not spoil any of that. But for 20€, I can't stand the fact, that everything good about the game is just gone after 1/3 of the game and (almost) all of what follows is something completely different. This is not what I expected nor wanted.

Conclusion
Inscryption wants to be so much more than a roguelike deckbuilder. Why do they try so hard to be edgy? If it would be what the store page describes plus more cards, enemies, events, and maps, I would call this a masterpiece. But because of what I described in the spoiler tag, I can't.
The game has MANY positive reviews and I can see where they are coming from.
I'm really disappointed in Incryption. I just want them to release a fully fleshed-out game of what I was promised in the demo.

Because of all that, I can't recommend the game to fans of roguelike-deckbuilder. If you've played Monster Train, Slay The Spire, and the likes, you won't get what you're craving for. This deckbuilder is a bit too thin in that department.

But I certainly do recommend this game to anyone looking for an experience. This is, what the game really is.
