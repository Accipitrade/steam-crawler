The game is very good, very nice card progression, and a death mechanic that rewards your future runs VERY generously depending on how far you progressed. Best part is--you don't necessarily need them either, winning the game can be easily done with just good upgrade pathing. This set it up to be the most explorative and fun rogue-lite I've played in a while--but after a few clears it just stops????? Like it just stops being that game --literally. Don't get me wrong there's totally nothing wrong with changing the graphics, the setting, the cards, the tone of the story, hell even the POV is changed. The game just switches into an incredibly boring RPG maker game --that while yes, has the some of the existing mechanics. The game becomes akin to yugioh where it's just hours of "just read the card 4head" and no intro whatsoever to the cards you just got. Oh you drew a bad hand? That's tough, because theres no squirrel mechanic anymore either. Because ♥♥♥♥ you, never summon anything ever again, I hope you explode into a bunch of tiny little shards

The story goes on hilariously too long with a meta bait-and-switch theme. But its not the good type of twist you would imagine. After your first clear the game proceeds to literally insert REAL LIFE videos of actually the corniest white man imaginable, which follows him into the woods where he discovers Inscyrption (the game) on a floppy disk. After which, he returns home and puts the disk into his computer. From there you proceed to play as normal but with him randomly throwing in one liners. and he ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ before. every. single. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. boss. It's such a hilariously bad tone shift, it seriously sucks. The game actually goes so hard, but peaks in the first 5 hours. If you got this game, I recommend playing it up until you obtain the Enduring Victory (Defeat Leshy) achievement, then uninstall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a fun deckbuilder with a bunch (and I do mean a bunch) of cool mechanics. Some of which are part of the card game, and some of which are part of the meta-game which (minor spoiler) takes the form of multiple other overworld types. Both part of the game are good. My only complaint is the lack of controller support.

The meta-game is weird, disorienting, but fun. The weird stuff it does is quite inventive. Maybe too inventive. I accidentally reset my save file because past interaction with the meta-game led me to believe that the triple confirmation was hoax and it wouldn't actually work. Yeah.... the game might have been too inventive with some of its experiences.

That said, I really enjoyed playing Inscryption, and I'm probably going to replay it because it really is a fun game. Maybe this time I'll notice more of the foreshadowing that the writers put in.\t\t\t\t\t
