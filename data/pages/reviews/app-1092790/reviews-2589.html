\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm marking my review as a spoiler since my problem is with the gameplay. As much as I like how much effort was put into this game's visuals I'd have to go with a thumbs down. I got a fair 12 hours play time but probably enjoyed 2-3 hours out of them.

I am a bit disappointed of the game. From the screenshots I thought it's a deckbuilding roguelike puzzle game set in the cabin world. But the cabin world was about 1/6 of my play time. This was by far the best part of the game. Everything else seemed unnecessary. Act 2 was meh, the 8-bit art style there didn't have anywhere as much impact over the visuals as the cabin did. The 3rd Act was maybe the worst. The PO3 world was just plain and uninteresting. The deck synergies there were quite weak. The blue hologram was actually hurting to watch and had to stop playing for a day since it puts a strain on the eyes. After that the taste of the other 2 worlds was kinds OK but really short lived. The Scrybe of Death would have been cool to play but the Mage was Yo-Gi-Oh ripoff.

I would change my opinion if the game progresses in some way that the cabin world is the main thing. Otherwise there is no replayability value. I know you can select a chapter once you beat the game but the cabin world is just too short with almost no progression once you have played through it for the first time. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a breath of fresh air with how creative it is.
I don't normally play card deck building games, but the atmosphere got me hooked and the plot twists kept me playing.

I did a single play-through, struggled with a few of the puzzles for a bit, and got through it in 13 hrs and 23/30 achievements (if that gives you some sort of idea how long the game is). It's a perfect length for what this game sets out to be. Any longer and I think it would have been too much. The fights can be difficult but the game is well paced.

There were some really big annoyances mid-game with loud glitch noises and glitchy video's (those who have played will know what I mean) it was really annoying, especially while playing with head-phones...but the game runs flawlessly and I didn't have a single bug my entire play-through.

Get this game. Even if you don't play deck building games or rogue-like games... get this game.
The overwhelmingly positive reviews it has is well earned.\t\t\t\t\t
