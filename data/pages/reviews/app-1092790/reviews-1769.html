\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished the game. I'll add more than what a lot of the other reviews stated without spoiling anything.

First, the plot. If you're older than your early twenties, this isn't going to be anything special. There's no amazing mystery or unexpected payoff. BUT, it is a fun journey, and that's what matters. What's important here is the game play, and its great!

The game play is three styles of card game. The first is a slay the spire like gameplay around one type of themed deck. The second is a magic the gathering build your own deck type of game play based around four themes. The third is more of an rpg where you collect and power up your cards, but don't start over from the beginning if you lose.

The card games themselves are great. The regular card rules have a nice rythm to it, and is fun itself. Games are not typically long or drawn out affairs, yet still require decent thought and strategy. The real gem is the variety of encounters and game play mixups the game throws at you. It did not feel annoying, but fresh.

I'm an intermediate card game player, and without guides, it took me around 9 hours to beat the entire game. But that was 9 QUALITY hours, with little down time, no grind, and encountering new things at a steady pace. For the price, this is more than worth it, and if you have even a passing fondness for a good card game, you can't go wrong here.\t\t\t\t\t
