\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is no game that I'm aware of that feels as personable as Inscryption. From every aspect, there's this unique love put into the game that is hard to match. The game itself is much more then just a deckbuilding game (a genre I'm not super interested in) but as a genuinely perfect atmosphere. The visceral behavior, the sound and visual effects, and the art design as a whole is a holistic and incredible experience.

The game excels in environment in a way most games dream of. To whether it's a good deckbuilding game, I have no idea. But the design in the world adding together makes for a lovely time, at least for me. Ripping your teeth out has never been so enjoyable. That and the sheer freedom the game provides in absolutely destroying it? It's like nothing else I've played. An incredible game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really do recommend this game for the most part, but only if you understand a few things.

Everything you see on the steam page is only a third of what this game actually is and it's the only part I can recommend fully. I think it is really misleading to not have pictures any of the other acts included in the steam page since act 1 is the only part most people are really buying this game for. I can honestly say if this game was only the second act it would not be nearly as popular and I certainly wouldn't be playing it. I think the developer really should've took the first acts mechanics and mystery cabin scenario and expanded on it with the other card types and scribes instead of the direction he took it in.

As it stands it was an incredible first 5 or so hours in the first act, then my interest dropped off a cliff from there. Act one had an incredible mix of mystery and card play, but once I left the cabin the story seemed more like youtube theorist/streamer bait that completely lost my interest. Some other negative review explained the same gripes I had with the game mechanics of the rest of the acts very well so I would recommend reading them I you really want more detail.

Look for gameplay of the later acts online before you buy, because if you like what you see then I would say it is definitely worth your 20 dollars. At the very least I recommend you to buy this on sale and install Kaycee's mod after you have finished the game (or after act 1 if you really can't stand the later acts). Kaycee's mod expands the first act to be infinite and is by far more engaging than the rest of the game to me, hopefully the developer expands on it in the future.

Is it literally a masterpiece like some other steam reviews say? Not to me, but that doesn't mean it's a bad game, just one that could've been better in my opinion. I am still glad I bought it, but just be aware that what you see isn't all you get.\t\t\t\t\t
