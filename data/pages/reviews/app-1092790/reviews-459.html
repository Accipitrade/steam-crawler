\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCCGs are a dime a dozen. This one switches up the formula. You're essentially in an escape room puzzle with an imposing figure who you play cards against to move forward. I don't want to spoil anything but the mechanics are extremely clever. It's easy to learn but difficult to master and you'll kick yourself for making one dumb wrong move when one good move would've won you a match. It's all incredibly slick and clever. I love the PS2-esque graphics as they frame the narrative well. My only complaint is you will die a lot and the whole resetting/taking a death picture for a new card takes WAY too long. If you want a game that gives you a sense of eerie and wonder look no further. It has vibes. This is a simplified review but take it that it's highly recommended. You can get just as much playing it for 10 minutes as several hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are a few things I can say negatively about the game.
1st: The Marketing: The marketing material featured in the promotional material puts a major focus on the first of three acts. While everything advertised is in the game, the art style changes in the second act and third act come as an unwelcome surprise. While people (myself included) loved the dark twisted card game aesthetic unseen since season zero yugioh, not everyone wanted the style to change in the second act to a Gameboy pokemon art choice. Since a majority of a playthrough takes place in the second and third acts, the exclusive focus on the first act can come across as misleading. I was annoyed at first, but I treated the game as if it was done by three artists/developers and each wanted space to take artistic liberties to make their own game.
2nd: Forced Deaths: In the first act if a player has not died before encountering the Angler or the Trapper and the player is winning the boss fight, a horde of bears will swarm the field resulting in near certain death. While the player will be introduced to the bones mechanic upon revival, it can make a person quit the game out of fear the bear swarm will happen again. The scripted deaths certainly seem a creative way to introduce a new feature for the game, though it does seem to punish a player for playing the game proficiently enough to not die prior to the 2nd and 3rd boss fights.

Now the good stuff: Art style for the first act was by far the best. Each act had their own allure but the first easily was the most polished. Kaycee's mod is highly recommended for players who want to replay the first act and test cards that were not put in the original game in addition to increased difficulties and achievements. I never got into the arg element of the game, but it was fun to see the youtube video explaining how it worked.

I would easily recommend the game to anyone who played yugioh prior to 2017 or who likes a darker themed video game. This is the kind of game your mother feared you would play during the satanic panic of the 90's and early 2000's. Only now, you can have her grandchildren show her it is real. Halloween is next month as of writing this...\t\t\t\t\t
