When I first saw gameplay of this on Markilpliers channel, I was IMMEDIATELY gripped by the ever-threatening darkness that embued my screen, so expertly crafted with an enemy that in the first act, for all we know, was all powerful; we never get to see more than a pair of hands, eyes, and a few rotating masks, and thankfully not having seen any trailers or done extensive research into the game, I was not AT ALL prepared for what was to come.

The "found footage" aspect of a game is something I've only ever seen a few times before, and this EASILY takes the #1 spot out of anything remotely close to that category. While the second act does by itself feel a bit off from the wholeness of the game, it still serves as a good segway into the complexity of the world that I didn't even know needed to be explained. Each of the three talking cards were hinting, literally from the first 5 minutes of playing the game, at what was going to happen in the second act. They were similarly powerful, just with amnesia due to being made into a card. And the twist villain being the first friend you meet in the game? Absolutely did not see it coming! The friendly stoat who had been on your side ended up being the true villain and we fell for it for just about 2/3 of the game.

The third act brings everything full circle; quite literally having much more similar, though admittedly not as immersive, mechanics and design as the first act. It brings that same feeling of a predator playing with prey that every psychological horror game wants you to feel, but just isn't quite as standalone story telling as the first act was. If there was an endless version of the first act, or a card game of it, I would immediately spend however much money it costs to buy it.

The Golly fight was absolutely my favorite of the second two acts. Not only did we get to use a card that someone else made AND fight our friends as cards, the specific part where the card that we make, who it goes to, and how well they can use it ACTUALLY DETERMINES WHETHER WE WIN is one of the most intuitive and unique quest steps I have ever seen in any game, let alone a horror or card game.

The fourth wall breaks are IMPECCABLE; Deadpool levels of logical conversing in a way, both directly and indirectly, as the Challenger and Luke. The whole time we are playing Luke, a YouTuber who is recording the game and rarely makes commentary when something happens, it startles me back into the reality that this is still supposed to be a "found game" being played by some person. And finally, the ending. both of them. The ending in the game makes sense. With the Old Code being supposedly what gives the characters sentience within the game, it makes sense that to prevent it spreading they would want to delete themselves to ensure it happens. But my god was it hard to let go of these characters to up until a short while prior, were still the villains of the story. All while the game is slowly being deleted in the background. Playing one last game with each of them shows how well developed these characters became after interacting with "Luke." Speaking of Luke, the actual shock ending? WHAT THE ♥♥♥♥. The second I heard the knock I knew it was going to be bad, just not immediately the worst it could be.
Obviously there is so much more to be discussed in this game and it's clear that an immense amount of time and love went into this game. So i'm going to leave this review here and give some scores on the big aspects of the game.


tl;dr:
Act 1 Overall: 11/10
Act 2 Overall: 8/10
Act 3 Overall: 9.5/10
Found Footage: 10/10
Background Lore / World Building: 10/10
Easter Eggs: 10/10
Immersion: 9.5/10
Mechanics Act 1: 10/10 - Easiest to understand / not too many parts / explains what each thing does.
Mechanics Act 2: 7/10 - Somewhat messy / very different structure compared to the rest of the game.
Mechanics Act 3: 10/10 - Once you get the hang of Act 3, it's really fun to play through.
