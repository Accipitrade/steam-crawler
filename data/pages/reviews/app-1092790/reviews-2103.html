For those who have never jumped into the deck building card game genre I feel qualified to say this would probably be a good entry point having never gotten into Slay the Spire or any of it's ilk myself. The game starts simple enough and does a good enough job of teaching it's mechanics for the player to grasp the basics without much issue. Despite the game's simple start, the process of building better and better decks, experimenting to find powerful new synergies, and uncovering new items and secrets quickly form an extremely rewarding gameplay loop.

What really elevates this already great little card game to GOTY status is the game's second layer, the aforementioned escape room elements. In this game, much like playing cards at an actual table, you have the ability to just stand up and step away from the card game. Surrounding the desk is an enclosed space, loaded with objects to inspect and interact with. Clocks, paintings of the cards on the walls, vaults that can be opened to name a few examples; each a puzzle waiting to be solved and the clues are often hidden back inside the card game itself. Completing these puzzles can add new cards to the loot pool, give powerful permanent upgrades, or progress the game's narrative.

These two layers complement each other perfectly. Go into the card game, find a clue to a puzzle, solve the puzzle in the game world, and unlock some new item or piece of knowledge to bring back into the card game. This structure makes it always feel like progress is being made, no matter how slowly or how poorly the last attempt to get through the card game's map went.

Only a brief word on the narrative as it's hard to describe what made it great in too much detail without getting too close to revealing some of the twists and turns. It's an absolutely thrilling journey, offering unique horror beats, moments of grand spectacle, some real emotional hits, and the occasional injection of odd and silly fun. An eccentric mix of elements for sure, but much like the gameplay, the narrative makes it work brilliantly.

In case it somehow still need to be reiterated at the end here, Inscryption is fantastic. A brilliantly conceived project built up of superb individual pieces that somehow layer atop each other perfectly, creating a product even greater than the sum of it's already excellent parts.

Also if you play this, try and complete the "Kill an enemy with Squirrels" achievement. It's a fun little puzzle to solve with a few different correct answers. It's also incredibly funny to watch it actually work as you basically perform this game's ultimate disrespect upon your defeated opponent.\t\t\t\t\t
