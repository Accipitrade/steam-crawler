Mechanically it is rather complex but it is very very easy to approach and understand. The mechanics prod at your imagination and encourage you to do your best to break it in anyway you can and I always appreciate games that operate with this mindset. All of that fun with the atmosphere and aesthetics on top? Perfect 10/10 masterpiece if a game... that is , until you beat it. Snap the picture of the elusive Game Master and suddenly you are transported into a gallery of silly videos and Finally given the option to start a new game. and when i say a new game i Mean a new game. The game changes from being an intimate and frightening purgatory with a sadistic game master to being a 16 bit deck builder with a nonsensical plot. It goes from being the most fun ive had in ages to being absolutely no fun at all in such a short amount of time. I have yet to play Kaycee's mod but I'm told that it not only makes the first portion of the game infinite but it also adds cards to the game to mix up the gameplay. Of course , none of that will save the fact that the mystery of the game master and his world is spoiled for me and I will no longer be able to appreciate it for what it was before. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot recommending this because it's bad, but because it's really just OK. I saw all of the glowing reviews and avoided all spoilers and dove right in. Only to find something that was interesting and different, but not mind-blowing in any way.

The core element of the game is pretty fun. You battle with cards and you mod cards and the more you learn how the modding works the easier it gets. It doesn't feel all that balanced. After a bit I was able to make a few cards that just destroyed each opponent. As if the real challenge was in learning how to manipulate the game itself. Which is interesting if not that satisfying.

What made the first part a slog is just the general slowness of the game. Everything takes time. Dying takes way too much time. Trying to get a clear view of how many life units you have left takes time.

There are some extra puzzles to solve but they're never all that tricky, just push the story along a bit. There was one style of puzzle I never really understood, but it didn't matter. Brute forcing it only took a couple minutes.

But then, spoiler alert, all this changes for act 2. At this point the game takes a huge dive in quality. You get tons of cards, but you never need to worry about them. After a couple stabs at building a deck I was able to roll over the rest of the opponents here.

The third act gets better again, but still has the same general UI and pacing issues as the first.

Overall, what sets this game apart seems to be the meta nature of the game itself. If that sounds new and novel you'll probably have a much better time than me. I just feel like I've been here many times before in other games, books, and movies, and it had nothing new to say in that area.\t\t\t\t\t
