\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Gameplay
This game has some addicting gameplay, especially in the first act. It acts very much like a rogue-like card game in the first act, and in subsequent acts it feels more like an adventure game where you build a deck through random card pulls. The card mechanics are very interesting and fun, and just placing a card on the board is usually an interesting mechanic that requires some thought. The unique and rare cards are all actually unique and can be used in very interesting ways. There are many points where you can fuse cards or sacrifice a card to put it's sigil on another card of yours, which makes the game have lots of depth where you can build your entire deck around one or more cards that you made very powerful. It didn't take me long to understand how the game works and how to play it well, which I liked because when I lost it wasn't because I didn't know something but rather a misplay that I can learn from and do better in the future. Without going into spoiler territory there are plenty of actually interesting puzzles and secrets to find in each of the acts, I normally don't like puzzles in games too much, but (especially in the first act) they are very interesting and some almost felt like you were solving an escape room. The boss fights are all also unique from each other and have fun mechanics that change up the regular gameplay. The only downside from the gameplay side of things is like I have mentioned, the first act of this game is very good, however the other acts don't really keep the same feeling going into them, the third act is close but it isn't as satisfying. Also the difficulty goes way down after the first act, the second and third I rarely even lost a match, and loosing didn't mean as much. In the first loosing twice would send you all the way back to the beginning, and in Boss Fights you only had one chance to win. Spoiler: After you beat the game you can mod it and it lets you play any of the acts you want so this really makes the game re-playable.


Story
I really appreciated the story, it may not be a masterpiece but it still made a game with really solid gameplay have something else going on as well. There are two sides to the story, what is in the game and "outside" of the game. Without getting too into spoilers, you are basically playing a guy playing this card game, and in the game there are entities taking control of the game from one another. An interesting part in the game comes up where it will actually access your computer's files and go through your steam profile and reference it at some points, which I thought was really cool and immersive.

Graphics
The graphics really elevate the game, it has some serious style. It is dark and gritty, in some acts, the card art is excellent. It changes style throughout the acts as well which I appreciated because I don't like staring at the same thing over and over again. Also the character design and UI are both very pleasing to look at.

Sound
The sound design was excellent. With these kinds of games I often listen to something in the background, but at least for the first playthrough of this game I would normally turn off podcasts or music when playing this game because it had very satisfying sounds. From sacrificing cards, to the slight wood creak in the cabin, to moving your character on the map it was all great. The score is great too and in tense moments where I felt I was going to loose a run the music really amps up that feeling.

Final Thoughts
The game is great, I loved every aspect of it. If you are considering buying this I think you should, even if it isn't on sale. Especially if you like card games, strategy games, rogue-likes, or puzzles, this game has something for you. I went in not expecting anything or really knowing anything about the game and was happily surprised at everything this game was. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t
