\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGames like Inscryption are the reason I prefer indie games to AAA games.

The developer of this game had a clear vision of a weird, creepy, complex genre-mashing and has executed that vision to near perfection.

As a deckbuilder, it's great - the cards you get have a fascinating amount of variety (sometimes a bit too much, making it a tad difficult to create good synergies with some of them). Most of them are reasonably useful and there aren't too many total duds. Lots of strategies come into play regarding which cards you want to pick to play and which you just plan to use as a sacrifice to strengthen your other cards. It takes some time to think this way (my brain started out in slay the spire mode where I didn't want to sacrifice any good cards) but the game is clearly designed around quick matches that are a desperate struggle to survive by winning quickly.

As a roguelite, it's great too, but its greatness doesn't seem apparent at first. Runs start to feel samey in the first few hours, and the bosses tend to feel a little unfair. That's until you get to the escape room bits - where you walk around the cabin and solve puzzles to get more cards and various perks. That's when the game starts to really open up and certain strategies go from mildly interesting to enormously effective and even overpowered. Then you realize that for every death, you get to create your own card, which can often lead to totally overpowered decks that you'd think would plow through each run... but you still have to be incredibly careful not to mess up.

Overall, it's just an incredibly well thought-out experience and I haven't even gotten to how... strange and surprising it can be as the hours go on. That part is more what you need to find out on your own and not have spoiled by reviews. Amazing game.\t\t\t\t\t
