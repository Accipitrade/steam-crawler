\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the card gamers, strategists, or theorists among those who view this game, I would like you to know, for that it is amazing. Start to finish, this game was a trip in which I did not want to get off.

The mechanics: THEY'RE INCREDIBLE! The generalized mechanics allow for clever wording, sneaky tactics, and some absolutely insane plays to come out of no where. The mechanics are one of those easy to pick up, difficult to learn. The RNG of the game and how it works can come into play with how lucky your pathing gets, but it's a card game, it's to be expected.

The Plot: I WANT MORE ANSWERS DAMMIT! The plot itself cannot be even barely described because it'd spoil so much of it and genuinely messes with your head from the moment you begin, to the moment you end.

The Visuals: I LOVE THEM! The general pixelated feeling really made this game feel like there really was more to the surface then when you started playing, and you learn that super freaking quickly.

The Puzzles: Honestly, I like them but not to a huge degree. The puzzles are an integral part of Inscryption and sometimes, some were really fun, and others... I didn't even get to do them to be completely truthful. I write this as someone who has fully played through the story, and I can safely say that some puzzles don't even need to be completed to beat the game. I feel like the puzzles may be the weakest link, but that link is still really strong.

IN CONCLUSION: Would I recommend this game to any TCG, mystery, or just plain ol' strategist fan? Absolutely. This game is amazing, especially for it's price point, and genuinely made me smile, and horrified at different moments.\t\t\t\t\t
