This was truly one of my favorite experiences I've had in a game. I can't go into much detail about the game itself, mainly because it's so unique and original that I wouldn't be able to describe it without spoiling any of it. Anyway, I truly recommend you try this Masterpiece of a game, especially if you like Heavy Story Driven Games with interesting and unique Gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, so good. So well made. The atmosphere, the setting, the music, the many twists and turns the story takes - this is it. The best indie card game I've played. The less you know going into it, the better.

Two minor gripes: (potentially Linux problems) the game has no FPS controls, and will jump to hundreds of frames. This turns on the fan and gets the laptop running hot when just 60 fps would be fine for me. I would REALLY appreciate the game not be so resource hungry or at least add an fps toggle in the settings. The other thing is the option to scroll with the mouse while at Leshy's table is missing in the Linux version but works in the Windows one. (Mouse is a Logitech 403 Hero if that matters) I'm on the native Linux version (Pop!_OS laptop) and workarounds are a pain in the butt to setup for stuff like this, but I appreciate the work that went into making a native Linux version in the first place.

Thank you Daniel and everyone on the team, this is such a good game.\t\t\t\t\t
