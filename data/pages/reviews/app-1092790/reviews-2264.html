\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a game that is difficult to talk about due to its nature, but I can easily say the following.

THE BOTTOM LINE: This game is absolutely amazing and you should play it.

Thoughts:
+The game continually evolves its mechanics and adds a lot of both depth and just plain cool things.
+The game's aesthetics are really solid and sell the intended atmosphere.
+There's a lot of play variety in the mechanics, so you have a lot of options to play and win how you want. The things that got me through the game and broken combinations I used may not be your thing and the game will happily let you use all the options I never touched as long as you put thought into your strategy.
+The exploration mechanics, while minor compared to the playtime at the card table, add just enough variety and interesting puzzles to keep the game from ever feeling stale.
+Most of the story is really cool and I loved how it played out.
*The difficulty is a little weird, and that it gets easier as you progress kinda feels backwards, though it doesn't detract from the experience.
-The bits where the story stops you after major moments like beating a boss just so you can sit and watch the pieces of the other storyline take you out of the game flow too much and aren't cool enough to be allowed to distract from the radical bits as much as they do, though thankfully it's not often.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is truly a masterpiece. I hesitate to say "mostly a deckbuilder" because the game goes so much deeper, but on the surface and for a lot of the gameplay, deckbuilder is the correct tag for it. But it is so stylized and the overall feel of the game is a delightful mix of creepy/foreboding and something homey in the depths of the dark mysteries within. The sound design alone deserves an award and the storytelling is amazing.
I will definitely be replaying this one a lot. I give it the highest recommendation and wish everyone involved with the game the very best.
