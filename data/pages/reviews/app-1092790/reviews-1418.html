\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game enough to critique it. It's one of those games where the less you know the better the story experience will be. I'll talk about the 3 main acts of the game in order without spoiling too much though fair warning right here. I'll go into a more spoiler oriented section at the end for now here's my game play review.

Act 1: This is the best part of the game, period. If you know nothing of the game, the story is off to an amazing start. The creepy and eerie aesthetic of the game is awesome of some of the dialogue suggests a deeper plot to keep you invested. The gameplay in Act 1 is the most fun part of the game. You have a deck of cards, and a dedicated deck of cards to sacrifice in order to play most of your cards. You have a choice to draw a sacrifice, or draw a card from you deck. It starts off as a roguelike where you chose a path that leads to different scenarios and you build your deck as you go. You also get access to 3 items that can swing the match in your favor if you play them right. It's great, it works well and makes you think. But by Act 2 all of this magical gameplay is gone.

Act 2: In Act 2, the game introduces 3 more different types of cards each with their own gimmicks and the point is to manually build a deck with synergies across all 4 card types. Sounds neat, except it over complicates the game and in the end none of it matters because a lot of your success is left up to rng. In Act 2, you draw completely random cards within your minimum 20 card deck and from what I found you either draw nothing but cards that don't do any damage or cards you can't play yet because you lack the necessary resources. There's no dedicated sacrifice deck, instead squirrel cards can be placed into your deck and drawn at random. What's worse is that your opponents don't have any form of rng. Their encounters play out exactly the same; same cards, same spots, same order. Once I realized this was the case for the entirety of Act 2, I didn't even bother commencing battle until I had a decent top deck I could work with. I can't express enough how many times I conceded from a fight on turn 1 because what I drew was just not what I needed at the time. Don't believe me? Here's Markiplier playing out the exact thing I'm talking about, no matter how much time you put into building your deck it does not matter thanks to rng, I know it's not just me who experiences this flawed game design.

https://youtu.be/FFbPN7kBOHQ?t=1186

This shift in gameplay ruins the magic of Act 1 and the big twist that starts off Act 2. It had such good momentum and for me personally it was ruined by some pretty strange and honestly poor design decisions. Also, ♥♥♥♥ magic cards, they are the single most useless thing in Act 2.

Act 3: The plot thickens and here I am thinking we're going back to the beauty of Act 1's gameplay. Which we kinda do here. It's still plagued by these annoying preset encounters from Act 2 while you draw random cards but here you have a dedicated vessel deck that helps out. Act 3 does have some neat rng encounters in the form of bounty hunters who will spawn if you have a high enough winning streak. The best part of Act 3 for me were the boss fights. Without giving anything away, these boss fights are pretty creative in design, they are all unique from each other and introduce these one time gimmicks that were pretty fun to experience if not a little sketch with what the game requests from you.

Story: With the gameplay stuff out of the way, I'm gonna talk about some of the story so here's another spoiler warning for you. The story has a good start from the beginning of Act 1 to the beginning of Act 2. Then after that you're kinda just waiting to be done with the game. Here's the thing, you have this youtuber who's trying to uncover the mystery behind the digital version of Inscryption and he's contacted by the makers of the game who respond in a really hostile way. This gives off some pretty great horror vibes when you review some of the footage and see some of the corrupted files and the main character gradually go crazy over this game. There's so much talk about something called the OLD_DATA which supposedly has some horrific data that is better left buried and forgotten which is how our protagonist first discovered the game. As you try to uncover the mystery of the OLD_DATA you catch glimpses of what it's all about throughout Act 2 and 3 but by the end of Act 3 you are stopped dead in your tracks by one of the scrybes.
Here's my issue, in the beginning of Act 2 you are told to pick an ambition to replace one of the four scrybes, when you first get to this part, you have no idea what this means or why it matters but you pick one to get a starter deck from the scrybe you chose. You then go on your way to defeat all 4 scrybes to complete Act 2 and fight the scrybe you wish to replace one more time. In my first playthrough, I chose P03 since he's the first of the other scrybes you meet in Act 1 in the form of the stoat card. When I beat him I began Act 3 with P03's version of Inscryption which lead me to believe that each of the 4 scrybes had their own version of the game you can play if you chose to replace them in Act 2. This little theory of mine was further supported by the end of the game when you meet all 4 scrybes again and play a match of their own versions of the game complete with their own aesthetic and atmosphere. So after I beat the game I went back to replay Act 2 all over again but this time with the ambition to replace Grimora since she's the one who backstabs the crew and "deletes" the entire game in an effort to bury the horrors behind this game again. This gave me the impression that I could stop her by playing through her version of the game. When I finally reach the end of Act 2 again I go to replace Grimora, the game says naw and makes me fight P03 again and replay his part of the game again. Here's my theory as to why that happened. This time playing through Act 2 I made an effort to uncover the mystery behind the bone lord that requires an offering. Once I got the offering it wanted, it lead me to a secret room, gave me a holographic key, and warned me about the OLD_DATA. I'm guessing because I had this holographic key I had to play through P03's game again in order to use it. I didn't mind this because I thought I was finally going to uncover the big secret behind the OLD_DATA so I was pretty excited at this point so I didn't mind playing through Act 3 again especially now that I got a good strategy down that let me coast through most of the battles.

HERE'S MY BIGGEST COMPLAINT! Once I got to the place where I needed to use the key I go in and meet the bone lord again and he says, "Okay I'm gonna tell you everything about the OLD_DATA but you can't record it." For context, you're playing as the main character who is recording himself playing the game and this is shown when the camera battery dies periodically throughout the game which blackouts your screen. That meant that the main character turns off his camera WHICH BLACKOUTS MY SCREEN SO EVERYONE GOT TO HEAR THE JUICY BITS OF THE OLD_DATA BUT ME! WHAT THE ♥♥♥♥ GAME! REALLY?! It really made me play through the second half of the game just to blue ball me at the end? What the hell was even the point? Why even include any of that if you're not gonna actually tell me anything? There's no follow up to that as far as I'm aware and you just finish the game normally again with Grimora deleting the game again.

This is where I called it quits on uncovering the mystery myself and just watched Game Theory do all the background research for me. Pretty bummed about that whole experience to even go back and test my theory and try to replace Grimora again without offering anything to bone lord. I just didn't care anymore.\t\t\t\t\t
