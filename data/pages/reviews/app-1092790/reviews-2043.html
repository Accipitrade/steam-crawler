\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNew plan. This game isn't getting a review on behalf of its gameplay or setting anymore. In fact, I'm just going to go ahead and spoil where I'm at in the game right now because if this game has such a reckless disregard for the privacy of its streamers, then it doesn't deserve secrets of its own.

There is a fight after the first big twist of the game (as it turns out, you don't spend the entire game in the room that's featured so prominently in the promos) where you are asked for permission for the Archivist (the necromancer who is actually the stinkbug) as portrayed by P03 (The technomancer robot who is actually the stoat) to access your computer's files.

At this point, you do not have the option not to give permission-- it may seem like you do, but if you scroll over the 'no' button, it becomes 'yes'. And this is a really big problem if you happen to be a streamer, because now you can do one of two things: allow your files, their sizes, and their birthdates to be displayed on your screen or stop playing the game right then and there. There's no streamer mode to prevent this from happening. You just have to consent, stop or playing, or stop streaming.

I hate to let my review be overshadowed by this glaring ethical issue, but I feel like I would have changed my mind about buying this if this scene were disclosed up front. I CERTAINLY would have changed my mind about streaming it.

Not cool, devs. Not cool.\t\t\t\t\t
