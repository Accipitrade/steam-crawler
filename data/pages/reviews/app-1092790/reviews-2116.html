\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI went and got all of the achievements prior to writing a review because I wanted to see the game in it's entirety.

Considering I also recently randomly went back and got all the achievements in Pony Island, Inscryption without a doubt has much more substance. Continue reading for a more in depth look at this game:

*Potential spoilers ahead*

*You can turn back now!*

*Okay, I am going to talk about it now...*

*Are you sure?*

*Alright!*

With that out of the way...

This game is fairly on rails with the narrative, but it does offer a lot in terms of mechanics. Once you get the hang of Act 1 with Leshy, you are tossed a different art style and two more mechanics to think about, so it keeps you on your toes for your first run.

At a certain point, you are given a choice: pick a big boss to fight!

This falls short though because the game forces you into the intended path, which makes multiple runs slightly... predictable and not worth doing again right after going through the game.

What really gets you, is that each "boss" has their own style and slightly different mechanics, which you get to taste in the final huzzah at the end of the game before the credits roll which makes you really want to see more of them.

Final say after all this?

It's totally worth the price tag.

Aside from the limiting path, Inscryption is a fun take on deck building games, and I would love to see more added to this game in the future. Well done!\t\t\t\t\t
