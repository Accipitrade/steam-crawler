After that it stops being a rouge-like, you collect cards and can rearrange your deck whenever you like. It also becomes way too easy and its just boring at that point. After that the rules change again, and it somehow becomes even more of a joyless slog.
Regarding the plot, the game justifies the bait and switch with a trite haunted-game creepypasta story.

I feel totally let down, I watched the trailers and read user reviews, and nobody warned me about this. Blink and you'll miss it: the trailer shows 2 seconds no context pixel art representing the 2nd act. And to all those reviews saying "don't look up spoilers", those are either chatbots or actual 10-year-olds who think this is mind-blowing. It's not.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have mixed thoughts on this game. I went into it mostly blind (watched a youtuber play through half the first act until I noticed this game was made by the same person who made Pony island, I LOVED that game).

The first act was amazing, I had a bit of trouble until I really understood the power of combing strong cards with better abilities . I loved the vague story about the cabin and the player. I loved the music, the graphics, the way there was two decks to pull cards from (your cards, and the squirrel cards). I loved that you could right click on the ability symbols and a book would appear explaining what it meant. I loved the system where you only get to make 1 mistake in each area, while with bosses you can't make a single mistake otherwise the run ends .

Then I was thrown into act 2. It's now a pixel top down adventure puzzle game. You roam around the overworld, then go into areas where you have to collect cards, solve puzzles, battle various npcs with your deck of cards. Now, you can collect as many cards as you want, but you must build a deck with 20 cards exactly. Squirrels count in this number too. Gone is the two decks to pull from, gone is the helpful book explaining the symbols on cards, gone is the charm, vibe and music of act 1. Gone is the anxiety of mistakes causing your run to end (you can lose fights as much as you want, there's no penalty, unless bosses are different?). And there's mana for some cards as well? In the first act, new mechanics were introduced a bit at a time, but here there's so many news things thrown at you at once.

I'm really glad they added Kaycee's mod, which makes act 1 repeatable with challenges. There might be a way to unlock it without having to complete the entire game first which I'm hoping for. For me act 2 isn't enjoyable to play, I might try to unlock Kaycee's mod while I watch someone on youtube play through the rest of the game :(

tldr: The story is great, act 1 is AMAZING, but the card battles in act 2 feel off, they aren't enjoyable like act 1 was. I haven't played the rest of the acts yet so I can't comment on those. Kaycee's mod makes the first act of the game repeatable with challenges, so if you like act 1 I'd recommend that.\t\t\t\t\t
