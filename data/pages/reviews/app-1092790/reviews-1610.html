\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot emphasise this enough:

IF THE SCREENSHOTS INTEREST YOU, DO NOT BUY THIS GAME.

The screenshots depict only a very small part of the experience, and they are not representative of the full experience.


Imagine if you went to a Five Star restaurant, and everything about it was fantastic. Not just the food, the drinks, the decor, the service; all of it fantastic. You enjoy a delicious appetiser, and patiently await your main course. The server comes over.

Instead of giving you a delicious main course, the server drops the food all over you, whacks you over the head with the plate, shoves you to the ground, and forces you to eat the main course off the floor like a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ animal. And the food you're now eating off the floor isn't even good.

Now I know there are probably some people out there who will pay good money for that experience. However, I'm sure most other people would probably not enjoy that, especially since they were expecting Fine Dining.

Look this metaphor got out of hand, but basically, that is Inscryption.

Inscryption has 3 chapters from my understanding, but the second chapter of the game is genuinely terrible. Not only is it a massive step down from the first chapter in every way, (gameplay is fundamentally the same but worse, music, visuals, atmosphere and tone), but it's also terrible in its own right.

I understand there is more story in a third chapter of this game, but I would rather gouge my own eyes out with a rusty knife than continue playing. Anything it could offer, be it juicy story or a return to the original gameplay, would not be worth the pain of playing any more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is easily one of the greatest games released this year. Even in an absolutely stellar year from Devolver Digital it manages to rise above the competition.

Inscryption is a psychological horror game that combines Table Top RPG formatting with Deck Building and rogue-like mechanics. The game-play is incredibly addictive with a high level of depth. To your your ability to explore the room you're held in, there's a good feeling of progression as you solve the many mysteries that the cabin holds. I'm only three hours in but can see myself sinking an incredible amount of time into it.

Highly recommend, my Indie of the Year.\t\t\t\t\t
