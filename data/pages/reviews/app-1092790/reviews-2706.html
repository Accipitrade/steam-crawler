\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this on an instant whim because of its style. I have not been happier with an impulse game buy in a long time. Like anything else Devolver publishes this manages to be unique while staying extremely accessible.

The action is immediate and engaging. The player is ramped up naturally in difficulty as new mechanics are introduced. The play has a simple structure that is well fleshed (literally) out with many fun and interesting permutations.

On visual style alone it's hard to *not* recommend. Mood wise, this game puts me into the vibe of a childhood halloween night watching goosebumps and playing the spookiest flash games I could find. Except, the expectation one has seeking out that experience is delivered on. The game respects the player enough to let them fail.

If you like Slay the Spire, MTG, and rogue likes, you should give this a shot.\t\t\t\t\t

The above does not mean that I consider the game a copy, in fact it feels quite unique , the rules of the card game feel simple and complex at the same time, and you can create some truly broken and fun combos.

Each run is not that long, but in order to get to solve all the mysteries (which I'm certain I haven't so far) you'll definitely have to beat the boss multiple times (which is not easy if you don't get a decent deck). You also have to solve some puzzles , which I'm pretty sure I solved by accident.

All in all a great game, certainly worth the asking price\t\t\t\t\t
