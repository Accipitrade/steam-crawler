\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a strange and original story. Totally captivating.

When I was a kid I would watch my stepdad play 7th Guest on our home computer. I would stare, on edge, unable to name what exactly it was about the game environments that got under my skin. From the moment I was sat at the wooden table, unnerved by the claustrophobic chiaroscuro, I was transported back. As soon as those odd, blue eyes creaked open it was like the little skeleton hand - the cursor for 7th Guest - was wagging its bony finger in my face all over again and I was watching my stepdad click his way down a pitch black corridor.

This game made me get up and flip on the lights a few times, but I am also a candy-ass.

The play itself is fun and presented a healthy challenge for me. Then again, I'm no expert in card games so a veteran might not find it very juicy. I think anyone who likes card games could have fun building interesting strategies with the mechanics available to the player. If anything, play it for how peculiar it is. Cool game.\t\t\t\t\t
