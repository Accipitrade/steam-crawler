\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are wondering whether to get this game, don't hesitate and just buy it. Even for the full price you will get your money's worth given it's length and how well put together it is. Inscryption is altogether a truly unique experience with fun puzzles, interesting lore, and addicting gameplay. The one big downside to this game would be that the 1st act is by far the most fun making the other two feel more like slogs to get through. Don't get me wrong, the other two acts are not bad. They introduce some new game-changing mechanics; however, it's much harder to create extremely OP decks that utterly destroy every level which is imo the most fun part of this game. I guess the only other minor downside would be that the live-action cut-scenes seem kind of amateurish with rather awkward acting. Almost feels like the game-makers hired their theater friend to do the videos for cheap.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs this game creative and innovative? Yes
But is it fun and immersive? Not for me

Spoiler free review:

Overall
This is a 3 in 1 game. Act 1, Act 2, Act 3 are totally different in terms of graphic and gameplay, but overall the concept is quite similar. Each act last around 2-4 hours, depends on your playstyle.

Pros
- Creative and innovative. Game tried really really hard to be different
- Requires certain level of thinking, planning and strategy to win

Cons
- The 3 Acts concept is just, a turn off to me. The moment you pick up the learning curve and manage to beat the final boss in the first act, BOOM! 2nd act happens, and the player is forced to relearn and adopt to a entirely different graphic and playstyle. It feels like the game is trying too hard to be at everything, end up it's good at nothing
- Certain cutscenes and areas gives me motion sickness and almost trigger seizure and epilepsy for me. this is a WARNING for players that is photosensitive
- This isn't a card game where players can freely customize their deck build to suit their own playstyle. This is more like a puzzle game, where you gotta solve each and every puzzle in a specific way in order to win. And oh, those various arch types? It is so imbalanced that at least one of them is completely useless, in the sense that you can beat the game without even using it.
- Horrible graphic. The game tried like real hard to allow the player to experience the classic/retro/nostalgic feel. However 90% of the time the graphic felt so garbage, I need to take a break every 30 mins in order to maintain my sanity
- Misleading demo video by streamers. All they do is to stream the first act (which is the best act in the entire game).

Conclusion
- Despite the overwhelmingly positive review, I simply do not vibe with this game.
- The 3rd Act is so badly made, it's basically a discounted version of 1st Act. Worse graphic, dumbed down gameplay and way more repetitive which I assume is to artificially lengthen the game.\t\t\t\t\t
