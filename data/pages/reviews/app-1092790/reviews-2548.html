* I enjoyed the hell out of it. A certain segment of the game felt weaker than the rest (despite technically being the most content-rich one), but it still delivers.
* Inscryption is a deck builder at its core, and yet, while there's plenty to like, appreciate, and figure out about the gameplay, it's not quite meant to be the highlight of the experience.
* It is, for better or for worse, not really meant to be replayable.
* It certainly leans hard into the horror territory, but it doesn't rely on cheap tricks in the spirit of jumpscares.
* Compared to the dev's previous works, I'd put it this way: The Hex was a cut above Pony Island, and Inscryption is many cuts above The Hex.\t\t\t\t\t
