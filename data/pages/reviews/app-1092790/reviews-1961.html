\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGo into this game as blind as possible, hold off watching gameplay and try your best to not look anything up, its so much better that way.

(spoiler free review)
I'm not normally a fan of card games due to them usually being confusing and feeling dull because of repetition, plus my attention span just sucks. This game isn't like that at all, just when you start to get the hang of things it pulls the rug out from under you and keeps bringing something new to the table. The pace is great, even if you struggle with card games you should be able to move along the story at a good pace. With that, experienced card players probably won't be into the gameplay as much as others since it is easier, but that's not the entirety of the game. The plot, the characters, the puzzles and hidden secrets help make the game entertaining on top of the gameplay, so even if you're experienced it should still be enjoyable.

Really my only complaint about this game is the ending. Though satisfying, it does also leave you longing for more. I hope we get a sequel of some sort, I haven't been into a game this much in a long time.\t\t\t\t\t

Update: I will add that if you are in search of pure deck-building, a game that puts mechanical depth and balance before everything else, this is not that game. Inscryption sacrifices balance in favor of flavor, using a (still rich and satisfying) card game to tell a horror story. Your host does not play fair, and you'll have to find ways of fighting back anyway.\t\t\t\t\t
