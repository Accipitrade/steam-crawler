I will preface this by saying I completed this game in one sitting, so some points may be slightly biased.
Daniel Mullins has mastered the 4th wall breaking horror esque game. The pacing feels just right (albeit until the 2nd act) that keeps you engaged without making the gameplay so tedious you want nothing more than to make every run as quick as possible. I think it is important however that the game be broken down into parts to be explored separately.

Visuals and Sound Track

Mullins has always done well on the visual front, but I think this game is light years ahead of Pony Island in style and quality. Act 1 perfectly portrays the eerie feeling that the gameplay provides with a retro but still modern art direction that feels unique to other deck builders. Little touches like the slightly low poly models and a haze that is reminiscent of vcr artifacting that sells the aged look. Characters hiding in the shadows make themselves feel straight out of a lovecraft story with features that can hardly be made, but that makes it all the more terrifying. Where the game hits it most on the head however is sound design. While the tracks are not beautiful or something to listen to outside of the game, they are exactly what they need to be. Visceral reminders of the plot and your surroundings. The clanking of the pickaxe and deepening bass during the prospector fight absolutely make for an incredibly intense section. Every track builds on itself and make encounters drastically more interesting.

Gameplay

The core mechanics aren't drastically different from any other deck builder style game, and that is okay because it still makes for an interesting enough playthrough. The creators understood there is no need to reinvent the wheel... unless you do. At some point it was clear the basic principles of gameplay would get boring and as a result more mechanics are introduced over time that keep it refreshing without becoming too overly complex you need a masters in game theory to get anywhere. I feel like the game did suffer a bit during act 2 as there is little introduction to new concepts and from this point on the game felt a tad more monotonous. Speaking of act 2, this was easily my least favorite part of the game. While I personally dislike the visuals (to each their own) I felt the change in UI was jarring and made for a much less enjoyable experience. Some things were difficult to read and it just felt unpolished but not in the way the rest of the game is. Past these issues it strikes a perfect balance between difficulty and fun puzzling without becoming ragebait.

Conclusion

I wouldn't call this game a masterpiece. Sorry. But it is a great example of how a unique visual style and refreshed gameplay can make for an intriguing playthrough. Act 2 was definitely the least enjoyable by far, and that may be due to the fact I played the majority of this game in one sitting, but Act 1 certainly made up for it.

I would say this is an 8.5/10
Phenomenal sound design and visuals, engaging gameplay, but the pacing was just off making some points stretch on for hours uneccessarily.\t\t\t\t\t
