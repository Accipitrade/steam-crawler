\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game probably might be underrated, since its just an indie game. But I am not lying when I say this is one of my favorite indie games of all time. If you've played Yu-Gi-Oh, or Magic The Gathering, or something similar than this is the game for you, of if you're just looking for something new, this is still something to be checked out. I love this game and for a number of reasons;

Reason 1 - The Card Game
Because I am a huge fan of Yu-Gi-Oh I fell in love with this game extremely quickly, the mechanics of the cards start out super simple, and as you progress through the game you need to take into account different builds, play-styles, and different ways to counter the many changes bosses will throw at you. If you manage to enjoy this game in its entirety then you would love actual card games such as Yu-Gi-Oh or Magic

Reason 2 - The Story
I love when a story starts off super vague, and you just have to fill in the pieces yourselves. You load in the main menu, but instead of clicking new game you click continue as if you already played this before. You load in and the first "NPC" you meet starts off dialogue as if you've been there before, but plays it off as "Oh you forgot how to play, well let me tell you" and so on. As you progress further in the game the story becomes more clear, and you begin to understand the world around you. Eventually the fourth wall breaks with its own little side story. I found this particularly cool because most games that break the fourth wall come out as cringey, or it just breaks the flow of the game, Inscryption does it seamlessly as if this is the cutscene of the game.

Reason 3 - The game as a whole
Inscryption does many things right as far as mechanics to the story. There is a part of the game where if you didn't find a specific piece for the first act and you beat that final boos, you just restart as if nothing happened, with a few hints of your previous playthrough, until you find that specific piece, so technically this game can be unending. But I digress, this game has mechanics I haven't seen in any other video game, or have at least attempted but failed at. You, the player, will enjoy this game the more you play, the further you progress you become more attached to the game by the cards you find, the puzzles you complete and the characters you meet, then once you finish....BOOM, this explosion in your head happens, you just want the story to continue, you want more cards and more characters to love. This is the only game I've ever played from Devolver Digital and I really hope that they come out with a sequel to this, or at least an expansion because this game deserves a ton of love, because of the love that has already been put into making it.

1.: This is a story-based game with card battler mechanics NOT a rougelite card battler game with a creepy background.
