Normally when a put a review on Steam I do a rather clinical list of pros and cons. I don't think Inscryption can be boiled down to just numbers. Instead I'm just going to relate my thoughts about the game.

First up, don't let people trip you up with saying "well it's a card game" "well it's an escape room" "It's an ARG". It has elements of all those certainly, but to describe it as "just" any of that is a disservice. I worry that people might hear those things and not want to touch it. You don't have to be a big fan of any of that to enjoy the game.

It's card aspect is the largest mechanic of course, but the card mechanics are much simplier than all the big boy card games you probably think of. You shouldn't feel overwhelmed with this one. The first act is actually the hardest, and part of the first act is in the free demo - if the demo doesn't feel too tough for you, you're probably good for the full game. In the later acts there's nearly no punishment for losing, unlike the first which sends you back to the start roguelite style.

Likewise, a disinterest in escape rooms shouldn't scare you from this game either. You can certainly just play the cards and progress. The game starts dropping hints more and more heavy-handedly the longer you go without solving puzzles. Or if you're into puzzles, you can rush them down. The second act doesn't even have escape room elements.

Finally if the idea of an ARG bothers you, most of what's happening is explained rather directly. You can dig around for more #DeepLore if you want, but the onscreen story is rather straightforward.

The game only takes about 10 hours to complete and I was giving some extra effort to trying to find secrets. This keeps the game quite tight and concise. It never overstays its welcome, and constantly gives itself new coats of paint and little gameplay changes that keep you engauged without being so big as to confuse you. And an Endless Act 1 mode is being worked on for those of us who DO want it to overstay its welcome.

I think the best thing though is how invested this game makes you in the characters. You can go from being utterly creeped out by a character, to wanting to do anything to play just one more hand of cards with them by the end. I hate to invoke the word Undertale, as it whips people into a frenzy. But the impressive character personality work here is similiar.

Probably the only thing I find slightly disappointing, and I'm trying to explain this without spoilers, which will be difficult. The game is from a perspective of another player playing the game. I think the end of the game would have more weight to it if it was meant to be YOUR actions. Because instead you're watching what a made up person did, for me it took out some of the impact. But this is overall a mild complaint.

10/10
