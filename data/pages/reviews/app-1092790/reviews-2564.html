\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a Daniel Mullins mind♥♥♥♥ that keeps on giving. But it has the advantage of being the man's third game and since The Hex and Pony Island, he has nailed down his strengths as a game developer.

Unlike both The Hex (which could be considered a precursor to this game) and Pony Island, Inscryption works as a fully fleshed out narrative rather than something that comes off as experimental. A whole heap of thought went into writing the background lore of the game and Mr. Mullins has gone even further to provide us with a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ insane ARG hidden within the game to unlock the game's "true" ending. Inscryption runs deep, and just when you've thought you're reached the bottom, the floor falls out from under you and you are dropped to an even deeper level.

In terms of the game's gameplay and pacing, it certainly is the best deckbuilder that's come out since Slay the Spire but under no circumstances will you get the same mileage out of it. To avoid spoilers I will leave you with only this: Keep your mind open and you will walk out of this experience satisfied.

I would dare say that Inscryption is a true game of the year contender. A story with more twists and turns than a crazy mouse rollercoaster and an aesthetic that keeps on giving, with a soundtrack that perfectly amplifies the moodiness and bleakness of the setting. This is a game that everyone should play. Even if you're not into deckbuilders, its narrative is too damn good to pass up. An indie masterpiece.\t\t\t\t\t
