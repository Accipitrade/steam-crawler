\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought Inscryption after playing Sacrifices must be made and watching YouTubers play the demo of the game.

Overall I would say I enjoyed the game.

I felt the first section of the game was the most fun. This part reminded me of other rogue-lite games I enjoy playing.

I read others felt the future sections were so different they could not enjoy the game anymore. I can't say I hate the direction the game went in.

I felt the story was pretty meh. I did not think it was too interesting. At the end of the game, I just wanted to play more instead of learning more about the story.

The twists in this game were not so interesting I think as well.

Would I recommend this game? I suppose I would.

Just keep in mind what you see in the store and in the demo is not the whole game. There is more to the game than what is shown.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will try to spoil as little as possible in this review.

First, I should say that this is both a deck building/card battler and an escape the room/puzzle game. If you dislike either of these genres, you will likely dislike the game (regardless of how much you like the other genre). I'll say that I played this game for its deck building side. I don't mind puzzle games, but that wasn't what I was playing the game for.

I'll start with the puzzle side of the game. Early in the game, the game is filled with small bite size puzzles to solve, many of these being similar to Escape Room puzzles. Some, however, are tied into the card game that is being played, where you must make certain plays in the card game to solve the puzzle. While initially a unique mechanic, I got stuck on the puzzle part of one of the puzzle+card challenges, leading me to spend multiple hours trying playing through the card game that was leading no where (which was tedious, but more on that later). I eventually had to consult a walk through to see what I need to do, and I didn't go "that makes sense". What needed to be done seemed somewhat random (I can see many players accidentally doing what needed to be done, but there was little to tell a player that they had to do it).
The puzzles taper off in the later part of the game, making the card battles the main focus. There is a post game puzzle related thing, which I have not looked into, so I can't comment on that.

As far as deck building, for the majority of the game, it is very difficult to build a deck that has any cohesive strategy. For the most part I was just trying to balance what I could pay for with each card I added to the deck. The only real "cool combo" portion of the game is where you can upgrade cards, and combine abilities together on one card that is strong. However, as these combos are limited to individual cards, being able to execute the combo is purely based off of whether you draw the card or not. Ultimately the game is a really bad deck builder, so if that all you are looking for try a different game.

Now the card battler side (what you do with deck you build). The card battling system is very typical and simple, nothing I haven't seen else-where in the genre. Overall, this part of the game is quite easy. The only times where I felt I faced a challenge is a when the game threw a surprise I did not know about at me. However, after the first time you see each surprise, it is predictable, and rarely posed significant challenge past the first attempt.
As mentioned in the deck building part, the game allows you to power up individual cards, but deck synergy is rare. This can make card battles some what swingy, where you play back and forth, then draw your power card and immediately win. Overall, the battle system works, and there are some interesting challenges interwoven with the puzzles, but with how many battles you have to play to complete the game and them posing little challenge, they become very tedious (especially when I played so many due to be stuck on a puzzle).

The game has some fun twists and surprises, but for me these did not make up for the terrible deck building and tedious card battles. Its possible that fans of Escape the Room/Puzzle games would have a better time with this game, though again, a significant portion of the game is based solely on the card battles.\t\t\t\t\t
