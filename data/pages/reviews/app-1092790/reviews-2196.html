A WELL CONCEIVED MIX BETWEEN A CARD AND ADVENTURE GAME
Inscryption was for me a good surprise. I have never heard of it, and I got notified of its release, just because I follow the Devolver page on Steam. The captivating retro graphics shown in videos and trailers immediately got my attention and I purchased the title knowing just that it was some kind of card game setted in a fantasy world. I was pleasantly surprised to discover that there is much, much more. The main plot is simple and tense until the very end and breaks constantly the fourth wall with the player, the ambience is dark and creepy, characters are charismatic. The game starts off like a board game where you will encounter different events that will help you to build and empower your personal deck. Every card has abilities that can be very useful to beat the enemy, but in some cases may also work against you. The player needs to develop his own strategy, in order to win the match. Incryption is entertaining and full of surprises, there is even a 32 bit-like RPG adventure section that reminded a lot of Pokemon, a good trip in the memory lane.
Moreover, the replayability value is insane. You can try new strategies, cards or different paths every time. The AI is really good, it seems to play against a real human player, winning sometimes can be really tough. There are no difficulty options, therefore the game will gradually become harder, your deck will become stronger, so the one of your opponent.

FINAL VERDICT
Devolver Digital gives me hope, as it helps indie developers to get their own projects on the market, while satisfying the requests of an audience that wants to spend time with niche titles. I hope that in the future they will add a PvP mode, where you can host matches on your network and have fun with other players, or just a PvE option, to have a little bit of fun, without being forced to play the main campaign every time. I spent 20 euros for it and I think that is worth every single cent. If you like horror games, mysterious atmospheres and cards, you absolutely need to add this in your Steam library.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthis is an incredibly immersive, beautiful, atmospheric art piece that I just didn't enjoy. The dialogue, character design, sound design, and environmental storytelling are amazing! The card battle mechanics, though, aren't really that engaging. I have played a TON of deckbuilders from monster train to slay the spire to monster slayers to trials of fire etc, but the actual gameplay mechanics here didn't grab me. If you are really good at deckbuilders (I love them but I'm no savant), and you can play through this game with no deaths and enjoy it as a story, you are going to LOVE this game. If you are like me and you die a lot in every game, this game will get stale the fifth time you face the prospector, and all the art design in the world can't keep that from being tiresome.

There's a demo you can play to give you some idea if you'll like it; I played the demo and loved it so much I bought the game! But still, two hours later, I realized I just wasn't having fun any more. I get the impression they were going for a Hades-esque "the story doesn't stop when you die" sort of thing, but in Hades at least there was some metaprogression so you felt your deaths contributed to the story and next time you'll probably get farther: here, your death just means you're going to fight the prospector and fisherman again and hope you get better cards.

Again: this is a GREAT GAME that just wasn't for me.\t\t\t\t\t
