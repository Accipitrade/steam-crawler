\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEasily one of the best gaming experiences I've had in recent years, possibly ever. It is the perfect synthesis of simple mechanics and a meta storytelling flair that kept me more engaged than I've been by a singleplayer game in a long time.

I don't want to talk the game up too much, but as someone who has been captivated by the roguelike deckbuilder since Slay the Spire, Inscryption rather blows my mind in terms of what can be achieved within that paradigm, and keeps me very optimistic about the kind of games that might follow in its wake! It feels like a consummation of several threads that have been woven in disparate areas of gaming over the past few years, and tops it off with a visual flourish that cements its place for me in the pantheon of 'games as art'.

As others have noted, I wouldn't want to say too much more, other than to note that Inscryption is absolutely worth your time and money, whether on sale or not.\t\t\t\t\t
