\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI very rarely leave reviews on Steam games. I should, but I don't, nearly ever. I also do really not like card games, virtual or otherwise, as a rule of thumb; as a child, I was deeply traumatized by this one time some old man bought all 300 of my Pokémon cards at a garage sale for a quarter when I was 8. (This really happened. I had a first edition holographic Charizard in there. A Venusaur too, I think. Even at the time, I was upset.) Also, I am bad at card games. I tend to prefer RPGs, MMOs, shooters, and top-down or resource management strategy games. But not card games. Please keep all of that in mind when I say this:

Inscryption is without a doubt one of the most engaging and fun games of any genre at all that I have ever played in my life. It is simply incredible. Inscryption has one of the best soundtracks - and some of the absolute best sounds in general - that I have ever had the pleasure of experiencing. The writing is phenomenal beyond what I have the vocabulary to express, and I write fiction for a living. I was introduced to the characters as they came to me--largely as enemies--and left them feeling as though we were friends despite their very best efforts to impress upon me otherwise. I felt such deep, immense regret that the game was ending while it was ending. I think that despite everyone's best efforts to stop me, by the end of the game, I *did* become another Scrybe: I was horrified to find that I, too, just wanted one more round. One more fun character. One more interesting conversation. I didn't want the game to end, either.

There is something in this game for people who prefer any given genre you can think to name, except for maybe first person shooters, which would find it to be maybe only a loose fit (and only in certain parts). There are so many secrets and little puzzles to explore and find that you could be busy for months and even in real life, if you're into that kind of thing. Inscryption is, in short, worth far more than I paid for it. This is one of the most magical, compelling, engaging games that I have had the privilege of playing in years. The secrets that lie within it are still not entirely within my grasp, but you can bet your ass that I'll be going out of my way to find them.

If you only buy one game this month, or even this year, buy this one. Play it as completely blind going into it as you possibly can, with your headphones on and cranked up and with an open mind that is ready for anything. (If our new mutual friends are to be believed, a lot more anythings are coming whether you like it or not. You might die. We might all die. But at least you won't be disappointed. I wasn't.)\t\t\t\t\t
