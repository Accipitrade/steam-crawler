\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSteam Deck Rating: OKAY
Cross-save compatibility: **NO**

I'm not going to go into the actual game, since we all already know the game is amazing. It's a deckbuilding card-based game and it's made by the Pony Island guy. That alone will sell most people on it.

I'm here for the Steam Deck information. And it's... okay? Not great, but it works?

Performance is perfectly fine, although it did devour the battery, consuming about 80% charge in 2 hours with no limitations placed on the game's usage of energy or framerate limiting. You could probably run the game locked to 30fps without issues.

Inscryption is very much a mouse-focused game, and as a result, you have to emulate a mouse with the pad or stick (using A or R2 to left click), which becomes cumbersome in later phases of the game. It hardly makes the game unplayable, just a little annoying.

The biggest sin to me, and why an otherwise phenomenal game gets a mere "okay" rating, is the lack of cross-save compatibility. See, Windows and Linux saves are not automatically compatible... different file systems or something, I don't remember the fine details. But unless the developer goes in and addresses that, games are not guaranteed to have cross-save compatibility. It was a huge black mark in Blasphemous' book, and it's a black mark in this game's book, too. Valve *really* needs to make whether or not the game is cross-save compatible one of the bullet points on the Steam Deck rating for games.

Inscryption is a fantastic game, and it's absolutely playable on the Deck. Just be aware there are some warts to be overlooked, when playing it not on its native PC design.\t\t\t\t\t
