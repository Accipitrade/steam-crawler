\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption, story aside, is a fantastic Roguelike deckbuilder with plenty of deep mechanics that keep me coming back to it. To be spoiler free, I'll be a little vague here, but the game is roughly cut into three acts, and personally, I really only prefer the 1st and 3rd acts of the game. That being said, what is there is so strong that I find myself launching this game again and again.

Would I recommend this game to people who like Rougelike deckbuilders (akin to slay the spire)? Yes
Would I recommend this game to people looking for a good horror game? Eh... Kinda. The horror themes are strong enough don't get me wrong, but there were no moments in the game that particularly frightened me as an observer. The atmosphere invoked by the theme of the game however cannot be understated, and is at it's best masterfully done.

My only comment to the developer of this game is this; Please please please add an endless mode of this game, or DLC to expand on the... 'other' major characters in a similar way to our Friendly neighborhood wood spirit Leshy. I would have loved to see the other two worlds of the other two Scrybes (all I can say without giving too much away) expanded upon as much as the first, and the card game really is at it's strongest in the first act, and towards the end of the third with the techno themed area.

tl;dr All in all, love the game, would recommend, and I really hope to see some DLC or expansion on what is here especially in regards to the card game, because I love it, and simply want more. The story aspects are strong, but not what I love most about this game.\t\t\t\t\t
