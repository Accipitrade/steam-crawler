\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's rare that a game goes beyond what you're expecting from it, and Inscryption is a game that managed to surprise me. There were many points where I would have understood if the game quickly wrapped up, but it was a game that kept going in the best possible way. It was also one of the first games in a LONG time that I finished and immediately restarted - I ended up completing it 3 times and going for all achievements.

Best elements: The gameplay is amazing, simple to learn but with depth to master, and is a love letter to numerous TCGs. It is also rich with little references to games such as Magic the Gathering, and a very blatant (but appreciated), nod to YuGiOh for those who go far.

Less Great Elements (Not bad, just eh): Some gameplay elements that are introduced feel tacked on and unnecessary, and much less thoughtfully designed than certain core mechanics. Luckily these mechanics feel very optional (though there are attempts to encourage the use of them) but it does leave a weird taste in the mouth - a strong sense of "what if".
