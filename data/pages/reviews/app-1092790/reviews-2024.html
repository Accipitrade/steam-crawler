\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Hearthstone Kobolds and Catacombs Simulator!"
Okay let me explain:
I saw Markiplier play this and thought "damn, the aesthetic is lovely!".
I got into the basic rules, and then stumpled upon a few problems:
First: Due to the nature of the game, you're punished for making good moves. Case in point my first fight against the prospector: "You dealt 5 damage on the first turn! great, all your cards are now gold that can't be used!"

Okay, Roguelike, sure. Let's make a card and move on.
And so here's where the 'hearthstone simulator' really comes into play:
In "Hearthstone Kobolds and Catacombs" you can select a *pre-made* deck from one of the classes.
The problem: It's a premade deck, and you can't get rid of the nonsense/wasteful cards.
If you could literally craft your own deck from the ground up. That would be Great!, But you can't. You're not allowed to.

So like Kobolds and Catacombs this game falls prey to the, how I would say "bad starting hand".
Sure I can sacrifice as many creatures on an altar as possible... that doesn't help if I'm stuck with an annoying useless wolf and that damn stoat that keeps talking to me. If we could just select the cards we start out with. I don't think my first 'defeat card' even exists anymore thanks to the games "whelp games over choose you worked to build to slect a blood cost!" oh it only gave me the option of the one card I'd like to circle back into the pool.

Eugh.
As I said it's quite charming and a welcome break from MTG but honestly if you enjoy REAL customisation. It's infuriating. I'd put it at 50/50 love n hate. I just want to select the starting deck.\t\t\t\t\t

Update 18/03/2022, we got the infinite dungeon crawler!
Kaycee's mod has been released I can recommend this game now!

Once you beat the entire game (all acts), you unlock Kaycee's mod, it allows you to play the game in the style first act and has some welcome rebalances, so progression is a lot more focused on you being able to build around limitations. I can agree with the nerfs, for example there is a sigil which generates a duplicate of a card once played, the issue being you could infinitely duplicate the same card, thus you could in theory never lose with certain cards. Kaycee's mod fixes this, by not allowing the duplicated card to create yet another copy. You have challenges, which can add another layer of difficulty, but they are optional, so if you want you can just play somewhat vanilla forever.

It makes up for what was expected and offers potentially hundreds of hours of content. 10/10 from me! Best part of all? They added the pirate battle!

Where is the infinite dungeon crawler?

First off this game is fun, controls and interactions are a bit clunky, so it takes some getting used to. What disappointed me and others. was that this game isn't solely about cards, but it was a very enjoyable experience and the ending was quite sad.

You might be thinking why isn't this game solely about cards? Here's why:
This one of those games where there is something going on in the background and you will notice it as you progress. That could have been left out and the game would have been just fine, but this developer apparently has a track record of ARG-style stuff, so I guess they got appeal to their niche... The found footage is pretty boring and I couldn't care less about yet another horror game with stupidly complicated lore (looking at you FNaF). It's also very immersion breaking and contradictory towards the end to be addressed as "Luke" (? Or whatever his name is, that's how little I cared), but at the same time it interacts with your PC and hardware. Very poor continuity. In a lot of ways not keeping the lore focused on one thing actually hurts the experience.

People excusing the almost misleading advertising saying "Oh, you should look at the developers other games" or "It would spoil the major plot twists". I fully respect that, that's why there is spoiler formatting in my review, but I shouldn't need to know a developer's track record to want to buy a game, I should be concerned whether the product in front of me lived up to my expectations.

As harsh as I was I don't care if it's a DLC, but a procedurally generated infinite dungeon is urgently needed... One for each of the scrybes.

Overall:
7/10 it's a lot of fun, but outside of the niche crowd it will leave you feeling a little dissatisfied at how story was handled.

Will rate it 10/10 when we get a DLC, but most importantly the pirate battle, because that looked like the most fun thing ever...\t\t\t\t\t
