\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a Gem. It's a deck builder, it's long term deck strategy, it's roguelike, it's artsy, it's mysterious, it's dark, it's weird, it's magic, it's tech, it's undead, it's deep, it's cheesy, it's fun. Lots and lots of fun.

It takes a bit of learning curve to really understand the mechanics of the cards and the events available to you as you work your way through Leshy's game; and sometimes it seems slow... But this is only the beginning of a much bigger journey, and this period is really necessary for you to hone your game... because it changes on you, often. There are many layers to the game that really make you think, and that's something you don't get very often anymore.

This is a tactical game, no doubt - you cant win the war if you don't win battles... but there's strategy here too that makes you start thinking at the beginning what you will do next go around. And yes, there will be MANY go around's - but that is part of the fun, and the training! Accept it.... enjoy it. Have fun with it.

Before you know it, as you feel you're becoming great, you'll be taking a new spin on an old familiar game and learning NEW things once again, building on your experience. It's quite satisfying.

If you like deck builders, you like mystery, you like a deep story and a fun game mechanic, pick this up. You wont regret it. It far exceeds the acceptable fun:dollar ratio.\t\t\t\t\t
