\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot as trippy as I was expecting, especially coming off the heels of The Hex, Inscryption is still an excellent experience, one that sets its best foot forward first. The gameplay loop consists of a a deck-building game set in an escape room, with another meta layer on top of it. The loop cycles a few times introducing new visual styles and rules based on the new DM hosting the game, however none are a far cry from the first cycle. That said, you get to learn about the characters of the world and get to experience remixed themes/visuals each time. I particularly enjoyed the escape room element of the game, I kept finding myself poking and trying to interact with objects in the playspace to see if something would trigger.

I enjoyed the creepy atmosphere, the soundtrack, the changing styles to match the DM of each cycle. I didn't care much for meta layer, though I'm sure there will be folks who parse it for clues for some other discoveries. Having said that, I look forward to checking out the mod that extends the first cycle.

bugs: only suffered 1 actual crash, which I thought was an intended moment of the game messing with me (probably the third battle in World 3 (Botopia). Occured randomly during one of the enemy turns.\t\t\t\t\t
