\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe story ranges from 'meh' to bad, and while the actual gameplay is quite fun and very enjoyable I feel like winning is too luck dependent for my tastes. Losing a run right at the end because you simply didn't draw the right cards is probably the most unsatisfying thing ever. This is less of a problem in the 2nd and 3rd sections of the game as they're a little less luck dependent and losing is a lot less punishing. The post game, however, is just a continuation of the first section, which means you will be playing it a lot more, only now it's even MORE punishing. Besides the luck element, the first section was probably my favorite, which makes sense why it would be chosen for the post-game stuff, but the sheer feeling of disappointment and blue-balls when you get a really good run only to draw your worst 4 cards and lose immediately is incomparable.
I do also want to touch on the story a little bit so this isn't all just me whining about losing a couple games, I thought the story was kinda stupid. You can just ignore it for the most part but every now and then it will rip you out of your immersion to have you sit through 10+ minutes of cutscenes. I know you can skip these, but my point is that you shouldn't have to, they should either be woven into the actual story/gameplay better so that it doesn't murder your immersion when it happens, or it just shouldn't be there.

Also, I know this review is almost all negative but that's also because I feel a lot stronger about the things I didn't like. This is a fun game, don't get me wrong, and I can see why a lot of people would like it, but as a wise man once said "a perfect game is no match for good RNG".
Story: 2/10
Gameplay: 6.5/10
Overall 4.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is an incredibly brave and commendable game in that it hides what the game actually is so well, that it has gone massively under-appreciate and has not performed nearly as well commercially as I think it ought to.

To try and explain as succinctly as possible what the game actually is for the benefit of anyone deciding whether to play it or not;

Inscryption is to card games what Doki Doki Literature Club is to dating sims/visual novels. It is both a card game and a work of metafiction about card games.

Inscryption labels itself as a horror game but I think that label is misleading. There are some dark themes, unnerving elements of presentation and a handful of startling scenes but the game isn't a traumatic experience in and of itself. Most of Inscryption's runtime is spent in a relaxing flow-spot native to single-player card games not unlike that of something like Slay the Spire. It shifts frequently and effectively between a campy, light-hearted tone and moments of tension, anticipation and mystery.

The core mechanics of the card game Inscryption bases its gameplay on are very simple, but the game makes consistently iterates on it and finds ways to keep it fresh throughout the runtime. Some of the mechanics that they introduced right at the end of the game were incredibly creative and made me beam in appreciation. The game has the confidence to fully allow the player to break the game and create incredibly overpowered combinations of cards and mechanics. The game isn't concerned with being carefully balanced or even particularly challenging, it instead uses the card game as a vehicle for the experience as a whole and I think it is incredibly successful in this, taking full advantage of the single-player, digital format in which it exists.

The narrative behind Inscryption is delivered with an impressive balance between subtle hints and clues that prompt the player to make connections or recognise dark omens, and having enough transparent substance delivered to keep the player's attention (as opposed to pretentious Nolan-esque movies, for example). It was an incredibly rewarding experience to be drip-fed this story through various forms and self-references, consistently making tangible progress to discover the full and dark truth of the game while never QUITE being shown the full picture or being spoon-fed any of it.

I loved this game. I don't think it has a great deal of replayability and I won't be playing tons of the endless mod afterwards but as a short-focused 12ish hour experience I found it to be a rewarding, inventive and thoroughly engaging/interesting game that I would strongly recommend it to any fans of card games, however casual.

It's not revolutionary or a must-play or anything, but I think it's a game that should be applauded far more widely than it currently is. The more games like this that exist, the better the gaming landscape becomes as a whole.\t\t\t\t\t
