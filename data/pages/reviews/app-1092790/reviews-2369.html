\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start off by saying that I do not like card games, but I do like this card game. The aesthetic just gets me, I love the adventure puzzle elements, if some of the more meta elements being a bit cheesy. The whole journey of the first play through was incredibly enthralling. I do not want to write spoilers into the review, but I do think its an important point to talk about how the game changes over time. Some of the changes were a little hit and miss with me, as I found the beginning to be the most interesting in terms of aesthetic and game-play. Not to say the game gets worse as you progress, it just gets different. Some people may like the latter part of the game's atmosphere and game-play more than the start of the game.

Overall, this is a really immersive and intriguing game, enough so to make a card player novice like me enjoy almost every minute of it. There are only a few gripes I have with the game that I can speak of. This be a bit spoiler-ish, so if you wish to avoid that, just consider this review over. I wanted something a bit more from the game after I hit credits.

As I said, the beginning of the game's aesthetic (how many times will I say aesthetic in this review?), was my favorite, but another section towards the end really hit my dopamine bone. Specifically, Grimora's board right at the end of the game. So, here comes my biggest gripe, there is no free play for each of the character's boards and decks. I would love to have seen more than just Leshy's and PO3's boards play out.

If I could hope for anything to come to this game, which would make me come back to it more often, it would be full implementation of each of the character's boards in something like a free play mode/gauntlet/ rogue-lite, contained adventure instead of just offering checkpoints of each of the main story's acts. Again, this is only a problem for replay value, and the first play-through is still fantastic.

I recommend this game for anyone who likes puzzles accompanied by story and role-play.\t\t\t\t\t
