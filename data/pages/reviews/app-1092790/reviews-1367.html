\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll leave two reviews: Spoiler-Free and Minor Spoilers.

Spoiler-Free:
This game lives up to the hype. You're best off knowing as little as possible. Go in blind and enjoy the ride.

Highly recommended.

~ ~ ~ ~ ~

Minor Spoilers:
If you need a little more convincing... this game starts as a rogue-like mixed with escape room and evolves significantly from there. If you are at all interested in exploring what is possible in games as an art form and inventive storytelling through meta-narratives you owe it to yourself to play this.

My only complaint would be that the pacing can drag a little between the games' three main acts. If you're feeling impatient after a major story event (you'll know when) take a quick break and jump back in later. They're all fantastic but I definitely felt some narrative fatigue slipping in when I blasted through the whole game in two sessions.

Lastly, this is a game with an ending. There is certainly some replay value here but I often see the game compared to Slay the Spire and I think this is a bit misleading. The game is absolutely worth your time but this is not a rogue-like with hundreds of hours of replay value. Putting this point in simply so you know what you are in for.\t\t\t\t\t
