\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInitially I really enjoyed the game. I liked the spooky atmosphere and being trapped in a room with a crazed hillbilly dungeon master with little puzzles that must be solved in order to escape. The card game is somewhat RNG based but fairly straightforward, you draw either a squirrel (mana card basically) from one deck or a power card that costs a squirrel or another card on the board to play from a different deck. You can alter the cards at altars to make them more powerful, you can get supplies that can change the tide of the game, a knife that's essentially an insta win, a pair of scissors to instantly destroy your opponent's card etc. When you lose you had to restart from the beginning, which can be annoying but that's the nature of most card games like this. You will occasionally be tossed into fights you have to lose to further the story, which can be annoying, but overall it never crossed the line into frustrating. Even though I had a few complaints about how the card game mechanics worked I overall enjoyed it enough to keep playing. It took me about three hours to beat that part of the game and to move on to the next portion.

The second portion of the game is just boring and not fun. The art style goes from spooky dark cabin to that of a Pokemon game for Gameboy color. The mechanics of the card game also change and become less interesting- some combination of frustrating and boring. The mechanics go from what was described in the first paragraph to: you have a deck with 20 cards, you start with a few and then randomly get handed one per turn after that. If you draw 4 squirrels in a row you just lose. If you draw 4 power cards in a row with no way to get them on the board you just lose. Losing just means you have to replay the fight over and over and over and over until RNG allows you to get the right cards in the right combination to win. I've put in an hour and a half to this portion and I'm bored with basically no motivation to keep going based on how uninterested I am. I'd like to see how the game ends but I'm bored enough with the game play that I don't know if I'll bother to finish.

Considering all of the promo material was from the first part of the game (spooky cabin) I think the developers knew that was the best part of the game and that showing the second part (bland 8 bit) would be underwhelming.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile the game is quite enjoyable, I found two problems with it. First, the first 1/3 of the game has a significantly better theme/story than the remaining 2/3 of the game. It's regrettable that they didn't just expand upon the first chunk of the game, flesh it out more, create some replay-ability. However, this isn't the case- the game exists strictly on a linear path. It could have been incredible if each playthrough caused a different end to the game.

still... 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
