\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend this game with a big caveat.

The initial part of the game is great. It was a bit easy for me as I have played card games my entire life, so I have to be forgiving on it not being very difficult. However, past the first "chapter" of the game, I felt the actual game you play is overshadowed by the story and becomes more of a plot device than an actual game. Starting with "chapter 2," the game you actually play becomes trivial and the only thing left to drive you forward is how much you care about the story, which is fine for those who mostly care about story and are enjoying it. Personally, I felt the story was a bit uninspired and only unique a few different aspects. It's hard to go into detail here without spoilers, but lets just say the only reason I finished was I enjoyed the first chapter's game mechanics so much, I hoped the mechanics got better later on and they never did. After having played through the whole game, I would say the game is a 6/10. I find myself sometimes logging in to play the first deck building game from time to time as I think they really had something nice there mechanically. It's not perfect, but it's fun. I wish they improved on the first chapters game mechanics and made the game more intricate and involved as the story progressed as well. Unfortunately, it only got easy and less important. The story is not very good, but you can't help but be curious as to where they are taking it as it's one of those weird "campfire ghost stories" you just want to hear how it ends.

TLDR: While not the greatest game ever made, or even close, it was worth its money and enjoyable for the one play through.\t\t\t\t\t
