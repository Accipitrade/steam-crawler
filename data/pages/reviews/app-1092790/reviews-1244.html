\r\n\t\t\t\t\t1) no controller support at all. For a game that has 6 inputs (back, forward, left, right, accept, cancel). Yes, steam's controller mapping system can give rudimentary support but moving a cursor with a stick is always lame.

2) Only One save state, autosaves only, no undoing mistakes. No resetting your game. Don't like a choice? too bad, keep playing. No way to let someone else try the game without breaking your own state.

3) barely explains anything about what's going on. Who are you? What is your motivation? Why should you care about any of this? What's the point of the game?

4) interesting metagame nuances seem to be what people love about this game. Storytelling through escape-room-like puzzles with obtuse interfaces. but that doesn't compel me.

5) unwinnable matches based on choices you've made that you could barely understand or guesstimate. Made the wrong choices or don't understand what's going on? Too bad you lose, here's your permanent penalty.

6) cardgame interrupted by cards talking to you out of nowhere and maybe that's supposed to be charming or interesting but it's hard to focus on the cardgame when you're thinking about what the cards have to say. You have to ignore their motivations to satisfy your own, OR, you can try to follow their advice and fail and get mad.

7) New rules added with each card match. Some matches have extra rules out of nowhere. Most of these extra rules are explained once and only once, and you're expected to remember what the symbols on the map mean.

8) Card match not going the way you want? Too bad. You can't go back and go a different way. You have to finish making the mistake you've made, based on a choice you made on your own fallible short-term memory, or, look up a guide and miss the entire point of the game.

9) using a guide completely ruins the game. Any info you look up will make you have an inherently worse experience with the game. So you must make all your choices uninformed. And suffer for each of those decisions. It's just grossly unfair and upsetting.

10) Do you like being upset at a game because the game is inherently unfair? Then you'll love this game. Do you like feeling zero attachment to your cards when they are literally talking to you and demonstrating personalities but you MUST kill one of them? Total sociopaths would love this game. But well-adjusted and empathetic people will struggle. Often.

11) Arcane UI. No real explanation of what anything is or is meant to represent. Violation of all conventions for user interface and user experience. Which makes it feel lazy, or cheap. I wouldn't call a decision like that 'artistic' but I'm sure someone would say I'm not being fair.

12) Games are supposed to be fun. This game is particularly fun if you're autosadomasochistic, obsessive, or looking to feel abused by a piece of software. For everyone else, play something else.

After the 3rd time I died in an unwinnable position, I felt sick to death of playing this game. It's just so needlessly obtuse and shrouded in pointless mystery.
