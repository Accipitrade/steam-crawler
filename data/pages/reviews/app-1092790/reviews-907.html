\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is not often where i do game reviews. Only for things i genuinely enjoy. I know i'm just adding to the hype of this game, but it deserves every single great review its gotten.

Inscryption, is an atmospheric, rogue-lite, card game, set in a cabin that you desperately want out of, going up against two floating eyes hidden in darkness. The games atmosphere is impeccable. Its creepy and eerie, sitting in a log cabin, with overgrown spots, and freaky artifacts, sitting alongside an obscured figure who seems both amicable and sinister.
The whole plot of this game revolves around cards. You need to learn the mechanics, which are wonderfully done, and executed perfectly. Daniel Mullins, like his many times before, has crafted a game with a very interesting style of game-play that keeps you engaged. The game prioritizes you to think about your moves, because, while its a card game, its a puzzle game at heart. Each encounter is created specifically, and deliberately. Some are tough, others can be a cakewalk, but you have to think at each one, and you have to plan a deck out that can work in every instance. The game gets even more complicated, as it allows you to merge cards to get a card with extra abilities, combine cards that are the same to make them even stronger, and so much more.
Story is something i won't go into here. Its an amazing story, filled with twists and turns and is something i never could've expected when i started playing the game.
The game even has a modification in the works as a free (for the moment possibly) DLC that allows you to play constantly as an actual rogue-lite with no story, and just continue boosting difficulty and challenges until you can't anymore. Its getting updates quite consistently.

Overall, inscryption is well worth your money, and time. Its best to stream it to friends because once everyone gets hooked on, everyone will be helping and will probably wanna see it through to the end. That's what happened with me at least.\t\t\t\t\t
