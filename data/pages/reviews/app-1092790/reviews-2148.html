\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the strangest games I've ever played. It completely changes style mid-game and is interspersed with live-action plot segments. My big complaint is that none of that stuff works. The game begins with an eerie sense of mystery and wonder that then descends into- for lack of better description- total nonsense. The final seconds of the story are college student film cliche. The first act of the game has great atmosphere and had me totally entranced; by the end, I had been ready for the game to be over for several hours.

Having said all of that, I still recommend Inscryption on the strength of the first act and the card game mechanics. The card game is genuinely fun, although it gets a little overwhelming in act 2, when you go from 1 deck build style to 4, and you gain the ability to build your own deck instead of gaining cards in a Roguelite fashion as in act 1. The game is fun and challenging and offers lots of twists and turns; your enemies disrespect the rules of the game in all sorts of fun and surprising ways. Still, this is basically a recommendation of the first 4-6 hours of the game, because the story goes flying off the rails in an uninteresting fashion that left me feeling that all the mysteries of the first act were thrown away.\t\t\t\t\t
