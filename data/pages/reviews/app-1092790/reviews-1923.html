\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWarning: Some spoilers are included. If you wish to have the best experience just know I do recommend this game and leave. Continue reading at your own discretion.






It is not often I want to take a game review seriously. Most of the time they are either games that people know are good, so I do not have to say much, or it is just dumb attempts to be funny. Inscryption will not be one of those reviews.

As someone who gets way too invested in fictional characters and interesting lore, this game was right up my alley, especially after watching Markiplier play it and seeing it was made by none other than the man behind Pony Island, and I knew I had to at least try the game.

As of writing, I just finished the game after 12.6 hours of game time, and I already aching to boot the game back up and restart from the beginning like I had just bought it.

The story is beautifully crafted like it was woven by the world's most articulate spider, the characters are written with such a perfect balance of complexity and actual personality that you are constantly wanting to pay attention to every piece of dialogue, and the gameplay is engaging. The game was constantly keeping me on my toes, even for a card game, Inscryption's mechanics constantly had me on edge as one wrong move and I would be rebuilding my deck from the start once again.

However, I began to fall in love with this game the moment act two rolled in. Daniel Mullen's creativity knows no bounds. Most of the issues I had with some of the characters were rectified shortly as I started, and even made me feel sympathy towards the Scrybes.

Act three was by far the most heart-breaking act and is what made me want to rewrite my initial review. We spend the entire time playing an interesting sci-fi remix of the initial gameplay, only forced to do your captor bidding before watching their plan fall apart... Just to watch the character's we had gotten to learn about and watch as their personalities developed slowly and forcefully taken away... Upon the reawakening in Leshy's cabin I genuinely nearly cried as I watched this familiar set slowly crumble in dust, all the while this monster that had killed me over and over, insulting me for my shortcomings and my mistakes, constantly trying his damned to ruin my perfect run... was being nothing but honourable and respectful... just wanting one last game, no matter the steaks and no matter the state of the game...

All the live-action elements of this game are phenomenal. Hands down. Great acting, interesting plot development, ARG elements, and puzzles within the videos themselves, while it all being neatly wrapped in a bow of mystery and intrigue.

Each new mechanic that was introduced was either a Segway into a much more complex yet interesting mechanic or was the set-up for the next piece of the puzzle, everything from blowing out a candle or collecting a knife had a bigger underline purpose as well as being useful in actual gameplay.

The sound design and music are downright amazing. Jonah Senzel did a fantastic job and I hope he is involved in more Daniel Mullen work. Boss themes are intense yet fit both the character and the setting perfectly, sound effects are brilliantly utilized, resulting in a super immersive and engaging experience.

At most, the only problems I had with this game were questionable choices for dialogue and character personalities, along with some pretty boring/unfun boss fights. However, apart from those, I can say, full-heartedly this is not only my most favourite game in recent memory, but it is my personal game of the year.

If you have the money, please, do yourself a favour and pick up this modern work of art.\t\t\t\t\t

I liked the twists and turns, seeing the game heavily takes inspiration from Ben Drowned and similar stories but to be honest, the second act felt a bit weaker. And the one after mechanically as well.

Overall an enjoyable game, something new again.
