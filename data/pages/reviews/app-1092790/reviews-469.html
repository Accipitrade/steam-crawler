\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscrpytion is a great game. The overall experience it provides is unique and mostly enjoyable. Story and setting are intriguing, and the gameplay is pretty solid too despite some flaws, which I'm gonna to share my thoughts on. Hope I'm not spoiling too much.

I need to at least tell you that this game has 3 acts since I really want to comment on each of them. In Act 1 (what's being showcased on this store page) the music and atmosphere are brilliant, while the gameplay is decent as well. Some cards are way too strong - it's been explained that the game is designed this way so that players can actually win and play through the whole game, which is nice of the devs, and they nerfed some of the cards in another game mode so if you like a challenge you can just finish the game and unlock kaycee mod. Card draw is a bit rng but manageable - you have 5 cards in your starting hand, 4 cards randomly chosen from your own deck and 1 squirrel card (most of the time you play your cards in the cost of squirrels as you need to sacrifice at least an animal to play another animal card), and you don't get to draw a card in your first turn, so an unlucky first turn could immediately lose you the fight. BUT at least in Act 1 after 1st turn you get to chose to draw from your deck or the squirrel pile, so you can usually avoid unlucky situations by building your deck properly and calculating the risk of draw from which card pile.

Act 2 feels refreshing but surely is a downgrade from act 1, both atmosphere-wise and gameplay-wise. New mechanics and 3 new decks (undead, magickal, technology, on top of the beast that we are already familiar with from Act1) are introduced in this act. While some of the new boss mechanics are interesting, most of them don't really add much to the game as they don't require specific counterplays, can easily be ignored, or simply don't affect the difficulty. I feel like they are just there to be gimmicky, which is fine I guess. And then we get to the cards - it is just bad. You get to build your deck with cards from 4 different types, great, but the fact that your sacrifice cards are mixed into your deck makes card draw extremely inconsistent. Sometimes you wouldn't even be able to play a single card for 2 to 3 turns because your low/no cost cards are at the bottom of your deck. Of course I wasn't flooding my deck with 50 cards that I wasn't able to get the cards I needed, I had around 25 cards but this still happened quite a few times and it felt frustrating. Act 2 is the weakest part of this game imo.

Act 3 is a bit similar to Act 1. The cost of playing cards however is now energy, which you gradually get more and more as turns pass. You get 1 energy in 1st turn, 2 energy in 2nd turn, you get the idea. You are expected to play more defensively since most attack cards cost 3 or more energy, which makes early game feel kind of slow. It does get better mid game though, when you start getting different upgrades that allow you to advance energy progression (battery saver) and lower card cost (gemified card), and the upgrades are going like crazy near the end of the act. Very OP but also very fun.

Even though I am criticizing what I don't like in this game, Inscrpytion is still a great game. Play the whole game and experience it yourself. It will be worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
