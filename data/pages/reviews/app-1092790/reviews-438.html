\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike others have said, the advertisement is misleading. I saw a few LPs of people giving the game a shot, the visuals looked great and the deck building seemed interesting, so I decided to try it out. It's the spooky month so what better way to spend it than a spooky game?

Slog, exhausting, irritating, unfair. The game wants a story to happen as you progress so it does require you to lose at certain points in order to restart. Fine. Even after losing so many times that new dialogue stopped appearing I still found myself getting hands that felt impossible to win with. Even looking up guides or helpful comments barely did anything.

"Try the cockroach strat!" I would if the game let me get a cockroach card.
"Try the undying squirrel strat!" I would if the game would let me get the right sigil (cockroachless).
"Focus on single sacrifice cards!" I did, a 1-3 stoat loses to a 2-4 elk every time. Trust me, I checked.
"Make sure to power up your cards with sigils!" I do and then they go to the bottom of my deck.
"Solve the painting puzzle, it gives good upgrades!" Great, all I need is a goat (goatless).

The idea of failing and starting over, this time with more power, is totally fine. The issue is that you never feel like you get more powerful. Your cards remain weak, and the cards you power up remain trapped at the bottom of the deck. Even when you do get those moments of success and build up a solid deck the game will suddenly decide to play a 4-7 moose alongside a few 2-4 elks enhanced with flying on the first turn while you're stuck being dealt all the cards you didn't upgrade because the cards you did upgrade have a phobia of actually being played.

Eventually one of my successful playthroughs gave me the ability to draw any card I wanted from my deck. Lucky for me I had a death card with zero costs, seven damage, seven health, tri-attack sigil, that I lovingly named '♥♥♥♥' who I was now able to summon at will and steamroll all the flying moose and elks I wanted.

My reward was an 8-bit whiplash of a second act, tagging along like an unwanted little sibling during your one night out with friends. It was a complete waste of a weekend, the most joy I got out of this game was hitting the refund button.
