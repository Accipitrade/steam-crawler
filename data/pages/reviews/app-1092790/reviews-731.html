\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpoiler Containment Protocol (SCP) is in effect. This review will have three sections; Safe, Euclid and Keter. Safe has no spoilers at all, Euclid has very minor ones, and Keter can potentially ruin at least one of the twists. Euclid and Keter are spoilertagged.

For maximum enjoyment, skip reading, do not watch gameplay, just go right in.

Safe (no spoilers)
Inscryption is an interesting card game roguelike deckbuilder. It's a little Slay the Spire, a little Monster Train and a little Magic the Gathering. Your Game Master leads you on through an adventure, where you attain new cards, strengthen existing ones and add some cool modifications which can completely overturn the game - and use them to fight against him in encounters of different difficulty.

The amount of mechanics is high, but mercifully they're introduced at a reasonable pace, and there's always a convenient handbook ready to explain everything. Figuring out the synergies and eventually learning to break the game is a big part of what makes this fun, so I won't write too much about that topic.

"Sacrifice" is a big mechanical theme in the game - you can sacrifice your cards to pass their unique "sigils" onto other cards, you can risk your cards for a chance for them to get much stronger, and finally you can simply get rid of them for a permanent buff. There's more, but this is probably enough for you to get the idea.

Perhaps most interesting mechanic: you have an ability to design and use your own cards. This game is an absolute treat for anyone who loves card games - and even if you don't, this is a great time, because this is just a damn fine game.

Euclid (very minor spoilers)
If you took a cursory look at the trailer and the screenshots, you've probably noticed that you can actually get up from the table, and walk around the room. The Game Master even encourages you to do so. When you do, you'll probably notice that the game has a lot of doodads to play around and interact with - puzzles to solve.

You'll also realise that the door to the cabin is locked. And this is when it strikes you - this is also an escape the room kind of game, to which the card game is seemingly only a backdrop.

To make things weirder, you'll realise that one of your cards is actively talking to you - and not just about the game, also about the situation. The card also wants to get away, and gives you hints as to where to get started. That gets the plot rolling. Because, yes, there is a plot, a meta one of sorts.

Solving those puzzles serve two purposes: one - some things you can do give you permanent advantages in the card game, and that's a welcome thing since you have to do good at it to progress. And second... move the plot along. Both of those things are exhilarating to do, give you a rush and is generally an amazing thing. This is a huge part of why this game is so highly rated. So I suggest you stop reading now and go get the game - experience the rest yourself.

Keter (gives away a certain twist)
Are you sure you want to read this? Just get the game.

As you progress, you'll eventually reach a certain change. Because this is in fact an unfolding game; and the trailers and the screenshots are carefully curated to hide that fact.

The "playing cards against a guy in a cabin" is merely the first part. Admittedly, it's the best part from the gameplay standpoint - but the other parts are very heavy on the story element, and you WILL want to get to the bottom of this, perhaps to give some closure to the characters in the game that you've met.

After you're done with the first part, you'll also be given a bunch of strange videos, seemingly made by some less known youtuber. Those videos add another meta level to the story, and are just as good as the rest. They keep you on your toes and guessing. They're well made, in the sense that they really have this low budget youtuber feel - and the pacing is good. A story within a story. And yes, they will make a return.

The amount of artistry is insane. The ending leaves you speechless. And while the final parts of the game aren't as interesting from a gameplay standpoint, the story they're telling will keep you awake and wreck your nerves. I found it impossible to get up from my desk, even though it was late; I had to continue. This, my friend, is the testament to the gripping storytelling this game throws at you. And don't worry. I spoiled you quite a lot, but in the grand scheme of things, you know nothing. So go and play it already.

As for criticism - my biggest one is that the story is, in fact, not self-contained. Some questions will remain unanswered. And I really don't care for such design. But the game is great, so I'm forgiving it.

Enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t
