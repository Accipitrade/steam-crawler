\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome deck building card game with freedom to build your own strat. Perfect dark mood and it left you wanting for more challenge.

Update:
After pouring 50 hours in this game, the card game is still highly enjoyable and stimulating. Really love the different sets of new cards and sigils from ACT 2 and Kaycee mod. The card game never feels dull, and the developers encourage you to explore and adopt different deck to embrace various challenges. The rng in this game was never too unfair as well.. and can be at times (?) most of the time, a successful run relies on how you synergize your cards in your deck or a good rng itself. If you enjoy a card game or a strategy game, Inscryption is totally worth a try. But, even if you have no previous experience with a card game, go grab it. I don't play virtual card game myself, except solitaire and yugioh, and those were years ago. I personally came to this game for the lore, but stayed for the lore and card game.

For the gameplay now, there are a lot of emphasis of uncovering the lore. The gameplay is divided into 3 ACTs. ACT 1 probably remains the strongest part of Inscryption. It nails the mystique element that was seized in a card game, supported by mysterious and horror atmosphere. Then, ACT 2.. the gameplay itself is the weakest part. The atmosphere from ACT 1 suddenly disappears. The game suddenly squeezed into an 8 bit graphic, which kind of toned down the ambiance from ACT 1. As the result, it makes the gameplay experience rather underwhelming. I didn't find the card game as engaging as in ACT 1, but.. still very interesting as it introduces you to a different range of decks and playstyle, as well as an insight into its world building. Finally, ACT 3 offers you the most interesting part of the game. It introduces ARG element in the card game that surprises you every time.\t\t\t\t\t
