\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is absolutely phenomenal! I'm not usually one for card-based gameplay, but I downloaded the demo to check this out because I thought the vibe from the videos/screenshots seemed up my alley. I ended up sinking 3 hours into the demo because I enjoyed it so much before ultimately purchasing the game.

If you are a fan of metanarrative games like PONY ISLAND or SUPERHOT, then you will not be disappointed with INSCRYPTION. There's a couple layers of metagame/metanarrative that you participate in while following the story that complicate things in really positive ways. Similarly, different gimmicks are introduced later on to keep the core card-based gameplay feeling fresh, and you can leverage whichever gimmicks feel most comfortable to you to play through the rest of the game in a way that suits you best.

The biggest caution I would give people about this title is that it drastically changes its art style at one point, which can be a bit jarring when it happens. It eventually goes back to one similar to the demo/beginning of the game though, so if you find yourself not liking the change (as I did at first), then you can rest assured it will revert back again.

All in all, this was a 10/10 gaming experience, and I would highly recommend it to anyone who thinks any of the above sounds cool!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo start, the game is not a fully a roguelike. Once you beat the first part of the game, it becomes more linear. I have no issue with this but this may be a turn-off to some who are pursuing it. The game is also what I consider horror light. The theme is definitely appropriate but those who are sensitive to it should have no issue. I alos consider this a good thing.

The deck building of this game is great fun, but for someone who's played only a few of these, I quickly found some devastating, combos, and anyone willing to experiment will likely find a strong card or 2 to look out for.

With regards to length, it's a bit tricky to estimate. I may have been lucky regarding the first section as it only took me 3-4 runs to finish it, but I also thoroughly finished up the next two bits, so I'd suggest 8 hours is a decent estimate depending on player skill.

I had a great time, for the most part, in terms of gameplay, some parts definitely feel better than others. the lore itself met my expectations throughout and I'd be willing to take another look at the game if any more work went into it in the future, this is an easy recommendation for people who like deckbuilding.\t\t\t\t\t
