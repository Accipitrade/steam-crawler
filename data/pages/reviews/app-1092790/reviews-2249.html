The game uses the advantages of its genre to create a unique storytelling format. It tells you the story bit by bit, always keeping you on the fence wanting to learn more and more about the world of inscryption. The story is so captivating and bizarre at the same time. It's full of twists that I didn't expect.

Inscryption is definitely one of the most fun games that I have played in some.

This was without a doubt my 2021 game of the year.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a contender for one of my favorite video games I've ever played and I am not someone who has ever played a deck-building style game. The core fundamental card battling gameplay is easy to learn and yet still allows a vast amount of unique playstyles to be applied. Without giving too much away the card type gameplay is constantly evolving. As soon as you think you have mastered it and built the perfect deck, Inscryption turns itself upside down. These changes that are presented throughout are very pleasant and they will drive the player to find out what is the next trick it has up its sleeve. Where this creativity really peeks is in its boss fights. These battles were some of the most memorable and well thought out boss fights I've ever experienced. Inscryption also has very fun puzzles as a secondary type gameplay. They are not very difficult but they help break up the game and do a good job of marking your progress. What really makes you interested in solving these puzzles is the atmosphere. It is incredibly unnerving and this is compounded by the near perfect sound design and score. There is an overlying story which also keeps you going to the next battle, or helps lift up your spirits after a tough loss. The game is truly a masterpiece which takes so many fresh ideas and seamlessly translates them into an experience like no other.\t\t\t\t\t
