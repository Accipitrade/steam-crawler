\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption hasn't really clicked with me the way Slay the Spire, Monster Train, or even Ratropolis did - and that's ok. It's really well put together and someone (likely many people, given the 97%/97% Overwhelmingly Positive reviews) will enjoy it.

The story and themes were fantastic. Right at that sweet spot of disturbing and intriguing. I actually think the Steam store-page video does the dark, well-executed themes a disservice.

Mechanics-wise, it's deep and seems balanced. Some boss utterly slaughtered me, but you can usually see how you can do better. And you can do better by both improving at deck-building and being better informed on how the boss behaves in battle, especially its story actions at certain thresholds.

I couldn't get too excited about any of the combos I was figuring out, and deck building opportunities felt a little too limited for my taste. So eventually I hit a boss wall and just wasn't motivated enough to keep figuring it out. But I enjoyed the time I put in, and plan on picking it up again sometime.

Otherwise, great freaking game, love the feels, and I envy the folks who really click with the card library.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust play this damn game. If you're reading the reviews and haven't played it, just do it. Even if you've seen some stuff I'm sure there is still much more to discover. Hell, I watched 2/3 of the game before buying and I still think this was one of my best purchases (this is coming from someone who second guesses a lot before buying something).

Everytime you think you've seen it all, there's more around the corner. Everything is beautifully polished: the gameplay mechanics, the sound design, the music, the visuals, the writing and, last but not least, the plot. It's been long since I've seen a game that rewards this well the player for experimenting and exploring, and, to be honest, the ending made me genuinely sad. If it wasn't for the ability to replay it I think I'd be grieving.

Finishing the game by itself also made me feel... something. Part of that may come from how the game and its characters treat the player, with references to many loved card games (sometimes even borrowing mechanics) and a very high level of self awareness. It's very clear that the developers always have the enjoyment of the player as their first priority. I guess you could say it's a love letter to card games sent through a "horror game" envelope.

There is not a single aspect of Inscryption that is lacking. Amazing idea, amazing execution, amazing game. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t
