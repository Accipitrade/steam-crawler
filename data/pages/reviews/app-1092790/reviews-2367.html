\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn the surface, Inscryption seems like a simple game about pitting woodland creatures (among other strange entities) against each other at the behest of a strange shadowed madman. But underneath lies a rogue-like that involves some pretty deep strategy and multiple styles of play. Along your journey through the 3 bleak and threatening environments, 4 if you count the final battle, you'll meet an interesting cast of characters, from a simple wood carver lady, to an nutty prospector. Each bares their own personality that makes for some entertaining dialogue while the services they provide create game changing opportunities, such as invoking an idol that gives your creatures extra functions on the board.

If you feel the need to stand from the card table, you'll find a relatively small room that's filled with strange objects. Once you look around, you find that the game switches from playing cards to an escape room challenge. It's a welcome addition that brings new light to the rouge-like formula by making you think about things beyond the deck you're building during your current run.

Should your run come to a sudden stop, death puts a new twist on the permanent unlocks we see in almost every rouge-like. When you die, you're asked to create a card which shows your current, about to be dead, character's visage. You pull stats from random cards you collected and in turn get to use said card(s) in your following runs. It's cool, if not a little annoying that you have to go through the process every time you fail a run. These cards, provided you collected good commons, can be incredibly powerful and make for some pretty overpowered playthroughs.

In summary, this is a very well thought out game with some great ideas and fantastic execution. It's challenging, thought provoking, and above all, VERY fun to play over and over. I have not finished the game in its entirety, despite having completed a run, so I have yet to see how deep Inscryption's rabbit hole goes. But with how much I've been enjoying it, I'm sure I'll be very reluctant to reach the bottom.

BUY THIS GAME!! It's fantastic.\t\t\t\t\t
