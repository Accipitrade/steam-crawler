If you came after a card game, look for another game, the card game at the beginning of the game is really amazing, it was really well worked, the balancing leaves something to be desired but it still works however as you progress through the story and start the act 2, the game is horrible, it looks more like a pokemon where you have to go after the cards than the card game at the beginning, honestly, horrible, if you have courage and willpower to continue act 3 it's better but still leaves a lot to be desired, the game comes back with some elements of the first act but very simplified, the mechanics present in the first act are simply forgotten and ignored, still better than act 2 but continues horrible.

Story--
Act 1 really manages to trap you in the story, the way to tell the story is very interesting progressing along with the gameplay, you feel involved with what the characters are telling you and often listen to them, but everything goes downhill after the second act, the game goes through a big ludonarrative dissonance, the story just can't hold up, (at that moment I just wanted to close the game and never open it again) act 3 is no different from its predecessor, it goes back to the standards of first act but in a very simplified form and linear, the story that started a masterpiece simply got lost in the middle and got boring and forced

conclusion--
If you're more open minded it's a good game that gets really bad later on but still playable, the narrative form constantly changes making it uninteresting. the card game of the first act is great, but a pity that the great game is only in the first act.
Card Game: 5/10 (only good in Act 1)
Story: 4/10 (only good in Act 1)\t\t\t\t\t
