\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEndless "♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥" moments from beginning to end. Several consistent and interweaving storylines concurrently happening at once, with gameplay that drastically and substantially grows in complexity and depth over the entire playtime. Certain mechanics that show up for single battles or acts could easily have been spun off into their own game, and it only makes each battle or act that uses those mechanics feel even more special as a result.

The most novel thing I can say is that this game made me enjoy a deck building game, a genre which I usually don't care about. I genuinely want a deck of the game in real life, and I can see myself replaying it (or enjoying the optional Kaycee's Mod,) for a long time both for the card game and to find and understand the secrets I'd missed in my initial playthrough. I cannot recommend this enough. Even at full price this is well worth it in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t
