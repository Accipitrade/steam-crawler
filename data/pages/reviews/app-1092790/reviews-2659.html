\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI beat it, now I'm scared and sad... This game is amazing. The aesthetic, the graphics, the gameplay and the story are all just mwah. There's so much to the game but its about 10 hours of content maybe more. All I can say without spoiling much is that this game is a very natural feeling puzzle game that plays like a roguelite. The puzzles are fun, satisfying, and you really don't need to stretch that much to solve them. Furthermore its not rank in file like Slay the Spire and all the card roguelites that came after it (not saying that STS and its followers are bad but its fresh and new feeling) This game feels fresh, feels horrifying, and it just feels good. Of the games I played in 2021 this definietely shoots itself to the top and regardless of beating the story (Which by the way, I am very sad now) I am going to play the heck out of the first area over and over because its just such a solid game in that way. My main criticism of this game is the fact that the different areas of the game aren't as fun as the first in my personal opinion.
P.S This game has Roguelite elements but if you want something thats as replayable as games like Into the Breach, Slay the Spire, Ring of Pain, Reigns, or Fights in Tight Spaces this ain't it. Well you could certainly try to make it last but its hard and best experienced by going through the story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is NOT a roguelike deckbuilder, and as Daniel Mullins games usually do, there is a bait and switch to the kind of game it is about a third into the game. If you are looking for an experience like Slay the Spire, best look somewhere else. I find this game is an enormous love letter to campaign based CCGs like the Yugioh titles or the MtG adventure game from the 90s, if you specifically like games like the Hearthstone adventures, Yugioh or Magic I cannot recommend this game enough.

While I was initially disappointed by the bait and switch at the end of what I thought was gonna be the whole game, turns out I ended up liking that game more, and it kept building on the foundations that the game set you up with in the first couple of hours and turned out to be some of the most fun 6-7 hours ive played in a game, and I absolutely missed a lot of secrets.

An improvement on the Daniel Mullins formula is that once you beat the game you can jump back in when you want to at a specific 'act' in the game, allowing you to try and find some secrets you missed, or try going through a different route.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t3 Blood Sacrifice Squirrel Totem Deck building is fun.
1 Shotting first boss on first round to only summon infinite 4/6 bears was a fun and stupid loss. lol
Cabin section was intriguing. Wish they polished and expanded this more to let the player delve deeper into the lore.
Soundtrack was great for each boss fight.

Old School 2D Battle Graphics section was ok. Beat that entire section with a mage (cause I like casters) deck even though it probably would've been easier with an undead and/or mix with beast deck.

Robot Section was ok.
Split Sentry gun carried me until I could make my own custom OP card lol.
Had a very weird mix of cards so it made it more difficult. This section was the only part where I died the most. Losing steam at this point, but pushed forward through it.

Last section dueled with the Pharaoh.

The whole hand shaking thing was a bit weird. Might be a real life card dueling etiquette reference thing that I'm not aware of since I haven't played yugioh, magic, or pokemon since I was a kid. There was also a push to try and make it seem emotional with the 3 fellows getting deleted, but it didn't hit at all because we never got to really form a connection with them or get to really know them. Kind felt rushed/forced to attempt to create some sort of emotional reaction.

I don't want to give this game a negative review and wish I could give it a mixed review as it doesn't deserve this nor does it deserve a positive one. I went into this almost completely blind. Didn't watch trailer or read description. Only saw a youtuber play this for a little bit and then decided to give it a try.

I think this game could've cut out all the video/acting part as I completely skipped all of that after watching one of the short clips due to how cheesy it was. It's difficult to execute found footage well. I think if the developer focused on replayablity, progression, and various deck types after beating the main story, this could've been a very nice game to keep playing. As is, it's sadly only a short one time experience that I can't recommend easily because it lacks a strong story and gameplay loop. The foundation is there and I do hope they expand on this as it has greater potential than what I've been presented with.

P.S. Leshy, I hate the sound you make when you talk and your stupid bell. It makes a very disappointing noise when I want to end my turn to utterly crush you or lament in my impending doom.

*UPDATE*

They decided to do a modded expansion to part 1 of the game. Wonderful and worthy of a recommendation instead of the previous "No". I hope they really expand further into this by bringing in the other deck types from the other game modes and balancing it, giving a good progression/replayability system, and adding new content.\t\t\t\t\t
