\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLIGHT SPOILERS: I'll be honest, I found the gameplay annoying in Inscryption and if I didn't already know that there was a meta-story happening before I started, I may not have gotten through the first act. So if you are struggling with an undercooked card game and didn't know... yeah there is something deeper going on. Keep banging your head against it, it goes different places than where it begins.

The game consistently sacrifices its gameplay in order to serve this narrative, and I found that I was not invested enough in the drip-fed story to ignore those sacrifices. I felt annoyed relatively often with this issue. Ultimately, however, it's a beautiful game and a fun ride that doesn't quite get as deep as I think the game wants itself to be. Worth the pricetag if you want a vaguely spooky experience with some card game on the side.
