Inscryption is a well crafted game that is well worth the price. The game eases you in to it's world and gameplay smoothly in the first act, slowly introducing new cards, new "sigils", and new rules as you progress. The looping mechanic in Act 1 is an interesting one and the escape-room-esque room exploration between battles is quite engaging. The bosses in Act 1 all have interesting mechanics too that challenge the player to strategise well. By the end of Act 1, I felt like I knew the game and its mechanics incredibly well.

Without giving spoilers, Act 2 takes a bold change of direction. The card based combat is still mostly the same, with some new rules and many new cards being introduced. This sudden introduction of new content felt quite jarring initially, but looking back I'd say this was a fairly well done step in the difficulty curve. The change in artstyle and between-fight sections was a bit too much of a departure from the previous act in my opinion, but Act 2 doesn't outstay its welcome and the art is quickly back to how it was when Act 3 starts.

Act 3 builds on Acts 1 and 2, taking the style of Act 1 whilst retaining some of the mechanics from 2. Not much to say in this section; Act 3 takes the best part of the previous 2 and does a good job of wrapping up the story.

All in all, the game is fun, the mechanics are interesting, and the story (both in and out of the game) is engaging.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend listening to everyone else, and not reading this, but if you must then i have written some little details below.

The first time i saw this game, i ignored it because it looked a little strange, but dang did it surpass my expectations. I never played a game like it, and i want more, i would pay for more.

The best part of the game for me was the first act (even though you have to play through it multiple times when your playing the fun way, without looking up the secrets), but the other acts had their own fun parts, and the way that the playstyle changed with each act kept things fresh, and fun. Plus, the second act was really fun (act 2 secret: i used leshy's deck, and the undead deck together, and killed everything with ease, muwahahaha).

My critiques would be:

i wish that there was a way to play the acts in an endless mode after you win the game (no matter how difficult that wish might be). being able to play a longer game in the first, and second acts was really all i wanted to do when i beat the game.

Golly, one of the last bosses, was pretty annoying in the way you had to wait for her as she did her searches, and all that.

I needed to google the puzzles in Botopia since they were a little challenging, and i couldnt find all the holopelts for the life of me.

Altogether, that all i could think to complain about, this game was wonderful, and you should play it immediately, without using the ouroboros card, and possibly without finding the squirel head, and extra candle; that much should keep the game challenging for those of you who want to raise the difficulty on the first act.

So now for the trade: i'd offer a hundred golden pelts for a DLC, or sequal... or i'll be takin them teeth (haha, my attempt at a joke, you might give the jab a reluctant nod if you play the game, but i doubt its worth a all out LOL).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very cool, very stylish game! I'm giving it 4 out of 5 stars. There are actually multiple styles, I had originally thought the entire game was within the confines of the cabin and that, in and of itself, would make this a great indie game. But there are actually several other areas and I've put 16 hours into it and I don't really see any light at the end of the tunnel. So, what I'm saying is, there's a lot of content here. As for the story, there are multiple stories going on at once, and while it's easy to follow it would be hard to explain. What I can say is that the main game play is a card game and secondarily there is an escape room around the board. The card game is easy to learn, for the most part, three of the four resource/card types are pretty easy to understand. As for the last one, I can tell it's supposed to be trickier due to it coming very last and not being explained super well. It is difficult for me to want to wrap my head around it since there really isn't any vs., free-play or even practice mode (from what I can tell). So I'm just basically trying to beat the game but if there were some extra incentive to master it, that would be nice.
As for what I don't really like is that if you lose it REALLY punishes you in some sections. In a few sections losing too much will cause you to have to start back over with a starter deck, and some parts it's just a slog, you may have to have 3 consecutive wins to get to a check point. There are also bosses which have their own rules that you have to learn on the fly; but if you don't the punishment is usually pretty harsh. The computer doesn't really need to worry about resources, most times it plays fair, but if it wants to have a full board on turn two you just need to deal with it. Even a very consistent deck will brick (be dealt a hand you can't play) every once it a while so losing after three ten minute long games is very frustrating. There are also a few puzzles I just can't understand. They are basically like those "checkmate in 3 moves" puzzles that used to be in news papers. You have to basically win the game just knowing the values and sigils but it's hard for me to visualize; and I don't think it's just me because most lets players I've watched also just kind of do a trial and error approach instead of trying to visualize the game playing out in our heads.
Again, it is fun but there really isn't any good reason to be a total master that the game demands in some parts. If there were an online versus mode or even a free-play mode I'd be more apt to try to master the more complicated elements of the game. But as it stands I'm just trying to be good enough to play the game. I realize it's made by a smaller studio so they probably aren't able to incorporate every idea they want but it would be cool to see where it can go if it becomes successful. Maybe an online card game, or even a card or board game you could play with friends if it's just a huge success. I feel like game breaking cards like oroboros wouldn't be as over powered in a 2 out of 3 duel and you could also have a death card mechanic where you draw 3 cards and pick the cost, draw 3 pick health/strength, draw 3 pick a sigil just like in the game where it could be amazing or weak based on luck. It could also be like a handicap for the loser in a two out of three duel; if you lose you create a death card... I don't know food for thought. Ultimately a great game, just a tad difficult at places and again not a lot of reason to totally master it currently. It very much has a hit game potential!\t\t\t\t\t
