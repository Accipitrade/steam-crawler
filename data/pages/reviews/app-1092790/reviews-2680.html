\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9 out of 10
Incredible game.
~~~~~~~~
Pros:
- It has simple, but well thought out mechanics.
- The story is thorough. Little to no plot holes.
- imaginative, the game constantly feels as if it's throwing you for a
loop by switching between the card game, escape the room,
and horror elements. All while introducing new mechanics.
///////////
Cons:
- Some of the battles are unwinnable at times, especially if you find yourself adapting & winning quickly.
(This may be due to pacing issues the story requires.)
- The ending felt a bit rushed. The story ended more abruptly than hoped.

Other:
- One and done game. There is little to no replay-ability. Yes, you can go back to the very
start after, but there's no recursive or endless mode at the end for those wanting more.
That being said, it did make me want more afterward.
- Price tag is a bit high for the viable play time IMO. Takes an estimated average of about 11 hours for the average person to finish it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have barely started my journey with this game, but let me tell you...
This game is by definition, a true masterpiece.

The detail, interaction with the world is on a really high level. The gameplay is really fun, enjoyable, rewarding. There is a ton of combinations, upgrades, card mechanics and at the same time, it's not something you will study for longer than an hour or two by just playing the game and reading the tooltips of these mechanics.
Story-wise, this game pulled me in and made me immersed like barely any game these days.
The atmosphere is literally next level.

Sound design.. That is a different shoutout from me. This game has some really fenomenal soundtracks. Especially during bossfights - these are totally amazing. They will hold a special place for me on my playlist.

And one thing I want to mention - you can try the demo to see the basics of the gameplay. It's a great thing - just don't compare this game to any other deck builder. It's a totally different, unique journey that you have to experience.

Flawless. Maybe only if you hate RNG - then frankly any card-drawing game won't suit you.
10 / 10, trust me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaven't had the chance to play it too much yet. From what I've seen, it's an absolute 10/10. I'll make this more lengthy once I've completed it.

Edit:
I'd give it an 7.5/10. I can't tell you why it's not a 10 without spoilers, so if you don't care about spoilers read ahead. If you do, all I can tell you is that it's worth buying and playing through. Its a fairly simple card game, but it does a lot right. The whole experience is what makes the game truly stand out.

Spoilers ahead. I wouldn't read this if you were considering buying the game, it's just worth experiencing for yourself.

The game is split up into 3 main acts, with a bonus 4th. I would honestly be happy if we got just act 1, but with a bit more balance to deathcards, and maybe some alternate endings or alt paths, new biomes etc (binding of isaac style)

Act 1 is everything you see in the store page. A rougelike card game with fairly simple to understand mechanics. What makes it truly stand out is the deathcard mechanic. Upon the death of a run, you can create a card based on cards from your deck. You choose the cost, stats and special attributes. Now, this makes the game really heavily unbalanced, as you can get a free cost 9:9 with the ability to draw itself when played. Finding a deathcard in a run is rare, but if you find a good one and draw it in a fight you're pretty much guaranteed to win. Personally, I think act 1 is an absolute 10/10.

Act 2 is a pixelated 'pokemon' like deckbuilder. It has the same 4 lanes as act 1, but introduces 2 new card types. It also develops a story, and introduces some ARG elements that persist for the rest of the game. This section also removes the permadeath of a character. Losing a fight in act 2 results in restarting the fight, or restructuring your deck and trying again. Act 2 is even more unbalanced than the first act, but it is a lot shorter and there is far less to do. Act 2 also has the least fun bosses, at least in my opinion. I think act 2 is probably a 6 or maybe 7/10, but that's because I am not too into my pixelated RPG's

Act 3 is very similar to act 1, except there is 5 lanes and it uses the other 2 card types introduced in act 2. Act 3 also removes the permadeath, but replaces it with a waypoint system. If you die in act 3, you drop all your money and go back to the previous waypoint. This to me is just more frustrating than permadeath, but still had the same satisfaction when you win a fight. Act 3 is by far the hardest part of the game, at least in my opinion, because it feels less forgiving of mistakes. Act 3 also has the most interesting boss mechanics, that I honestly refuse to spoil because they're just cool. I'd give act 3 a 7, but it's probably an 8 because of the cool boss fights.

Act 4 is where a lot of the negative reviews regarding the game feeling 'unfinished' come from. In acts 1 and 3, you play the game against 2 of the 4 'Scribes'. In act 4, while the game corrupts around you, you play against the other 2. However, you don't play against them for very long, so a lot of the negative reviews I've seen are just people complaining that these two Scribes weren't full acts. Personally, that doesn't matter to me, a 12 hour game is completely fine from an indie studio. I'd give act 4 and the ending to the game a 7. It's fairly concise and definitive, and there's nothing really to complain about. It also does nothing really all that special.\t\t\t\t\t
