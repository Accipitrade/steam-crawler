\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption gave me a bigger sense of wonder than any open world game I have played in years. This Daniel Mullins joint sees you locked in a cabin against your will with a strange figure whose visage is obscured just out of sight while you are forced to play their sinister card game wherein when you lose you are killed and "become" a card that can be acquired in your later runs. The somewhat grainy graphics style mixed with gravely audio and music that is both rustic and foreboding at the same time sets a scene that piqued my curiosity unlike anything I have seen before. As if the scene wasn't strange enough already, you are also able to get up from the table and walk around grid-style in the room you are trapped in and solve puzzles and mysteries around the room escape room style. This is only the premise...there is more weirdness to come both in terms of gameplay and plot. The card game itself is very competent and feels almost more like a traditional card game than a TCG or deckbuilder in terms of gameplay. You sacrifice squirrel tokens to play other creatures in one of 4 lanes and your creatures and your mysterious opponent's creatures attack each other/you directly in order to win. If you don't care for deckbuilders at all then this game may not be for you, but if it is something you have even a passing interest in, then this game could not be more worth your time. It is one of the most atmospheric games I have played in ages. The visuals are interesting, and the music and ambient sound (especially bosses) is incredible. Saying anything more would be spoiling, which would be absolutely criminal to do to this game because it will take you places you would never expect. If you haven't been spoiled on this one already, don't miss out on one of the most creative and interesting indies ever made.\t\t\t\t\t
