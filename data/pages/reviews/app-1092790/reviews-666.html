\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those games where the less you know, the better it is, and this game makes you rethink everything quite a few times. Without spoiling the game I can't go into what happens throughout its main story, but even just what's on the surface is very fun on its own and has a great atmosphere.

Another bit of the game that I really liked, which took up most of my playtime, is Kaycee's Mod. After beating the main story of the game you're also given the option to play a free built-in part of the game called Kaycee's Mod that lets you play through runs of the first act without the story elements and a lot of small changes, and lets you add challenges to the run to increase challenge level, which after beating a run with the challenge level requirement met unlocks more cards, challenges, and starting decks, which in turn let you add harder twists to the game to raise the challenge level and progress further.

That's about it, good story, great gameplay, and a lot of replayability.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a wonderful deckbuilding roguelike.

The game is divided in three major acts where the gameplay slightly shifts with new mechanics being introduced in each act.

Story is pretty solid I really like the horror and escape room kind of vibe this game gives and I think it's just enough to keep everything fresh. It rewards you if you decide to get up and solve a clock puzzle through the hints.

Each act also features it's new rules and mechanics and you are encouraged to try out new things.

The bosses in this game are pretty fun and creative as well especially the ones that you encounter in Act 3 are pretty creative. Every boss kind of adds to the horror element and to the overall story.

If you're done with the story mode and you just want to have fun there's also an endless mode where in each run you challange yourself with modifiers that keep each run fresh and the game rewards you with new starter decks and new cards for doing them.

Overall buy on sale.
10/10
