\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I could thumbs up and down simultaneously I would. There's a lot to like here, particularly in Act 1. The biggest issue I have is the deceptive marketing; it's all for Act 1. The subsequent Acts are a completely different game, and not what is advertised.

That being said, I really enjoyed Act 1. Loved the mystery and intrigue, the antagonist was hilarious and scary, and the card game was solid. Act 2 was fine, getting to build your own deck was good, built a fairly solid wizards deck. But Act 3... holy boring batman. You have your custom deck stripped away, replaced with a terrible robot transformers deck. Add an ugly UI, a blank slate antagonist, and a tedious slog through the maze that is the Act 3 game map.

Act 3 was a really bad design choice, I probably won't bother trying to finish it. If I could bring my custom deck from Act 2 into Act 3, I'd give that a go, but as it is it's just not worth the slog playing with bad cards and bad mechanics.

This game has left a bad taste in my mouth, despite starting out so sweet. I can't recommend it because of the deceptive marketing alone, but the disappointing Act 3 nailed the coffin on this one for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter hours of getting put in my place by the Charmin Bears, I finally beat Kaycee's Mod with all challenges enabled! I have to say this game has been hours upon hours of a good time. From its dark and eerie atmosphere to its comedic side characters, I enjoyed the game from start to finish. All in all, if you enjoy card games with interesting mechanics as well as a decent story line (the third act wasn't as necessary, but I understand the idea behind switching from thematic to balanced gameplay), I strongly recommend it.

On a side note, Kaycee's Mod was a great idea for creating a balance between preventing infinite or game-breaking combos, while maintaining the thematic atmosphere that the game does best. My only suggestion would be to allow the player to create a side stock of custom death cards that can be use between mod runs. That being said, these death cards still shouldn't be able to break the mod as that would be untrue to the spirit of the mod itself. It's just nice to be able to add your own touch to things.\t\t\t\t\t
