The mystery in itself is marvelous and was a treat to follow!

I am going to be looking forward to a hopefully second title in the future to play!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a waste of talent, it really makes me sad.

I have never seen such a great, polished game with this quality of gameplay-loop and atmosphere throw it all away because of plot/narrative shenanigans. I don't know whether I should commend or spite the dev, and I can't bring myself to care enough about it to the point of deciding so I'll just leave it be.

-----------------------------------------------
Now, what is Inscryption?

Inscryption is yet another one of those meta-indie games harboring interesting, unusual mechanics that (unfortunately) chooses to rely on its "oh so silly, quirky and fourth-dimensional" story instead of perfecting its clear, objective core (gameplay & atmosphere) strengths to a bigger degree. Because of that, it falls severely short at trying to deliver a proper, complete experience to the player (i.e. the tone and gameplay of the game are all over the place).

Now, of course you 'wouldn't know' that just by looking at the store page!
Since it's purposefully 'hidden' (for the sake of the plot's efficiency, of course!).

It really is the classic videogame tale of making the gameplay serve the narrative as a whole, and while that can indeed (work be a huge recipe for success in the right hands, this instance in particular it just feels like the developers accidentally made a phenomenal core game all by mistake and... went along with it, believing their oh-so-different 'narrative' would be the star of the package. Well, it isn't, and it only hurts the game. Besides, it's not even done well (and I think this is what truly hurts the most).

I wish I could readjust the entirety of the game post-Act 1 for something else. I don't know, maybe change the plot and make the player 'choose' between the other 3D card games. Be more subtle about the overly-focused meta-plot and make it more of a side thing. There are tons of options, but just DO something, anything, to make the game more concise gameplay-wise, tone-wise and able to take itself more seriously after what you've done with Act 1.
-----------------------------------------------

Result: A great game ruined by ARG.
Worst part about it? Plays great.

Make your own judgement on whether to buy it or not, but you deserve to know what you're getting into, before you (genuinely) get into it.\t\t\t\t\t
