\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNormally, this is a game wherein it appeals to people who like deck building games such as Slay the Spire, Rogue Adventures (mobile) and Dominion, the board game. HOWEVER, this game also combines elements of "escape the room" games and even loads of puzzle solving amidst the classic deck building elements you see.

Getting into the first hour of gameplay reveals that there's a narrative inside the game that's beyond my own expectations.

Pretty much a solid game based on my first impressions.

UPDATE 1:

I thought it was just a simple game of slay the spire interface and mechanics with "escape the room" elements but turns out that there's more to the game than what one can simply read off the reviews from. It got me thinking hard at its puzzles and even got me surprised at the bits and pieces of its narrative.

Not gonna lie but the future of storytelling seems to be promising when you have this, Hades and even 13 Sentinels: Aegis Rim made in the past 2 years.

Definitely, a strong contender for the The Game Awards 2021 "Best Simulation/Strategy" and probably my GOTY (among the games released this year that I've played).\t\t\t\t\t
