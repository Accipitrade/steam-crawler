\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Inscryption is a surprisingly thoughtful and complex card game made up from complete scratch. I've never been one for card games; The most of any card game I've ever played is the occasional game of war or blackjack. That and Gwent. Gotta love Gwent.
So playing this game was quite out of my comfort zone, but I was really brought in by its atmosphere. It's a pixelated, retro-style game with a big emphasis on mystery and loopholes pushed through more loopholes. The story to this game is super complex and interesting and there are a lot of interesting things to glean from it. The game begins with you stuck in a cabin with a serial killer/psychopath dungeon master named Leshy and the main goal is to beat him at his own game without dying. But... well, just play and see what happens.
This game is so fun and the addition of Kaycee's Mod, an endless roguelike version of Leshy's cabin, there's no end to the builds you can make or the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up combos you can craft. For most of the game (most), you spend your time moving through run after run, gathering different cards on each try, with the goal of making a deck good enough to win.
