\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGames like this are what make me wish Steam had a "middle ground" option rather than just "recommend" or "don't recommend". To put it simply, this game is fun for the first "act" (all of the content that is within the trailers and screenshots here on steam) and I was ready to join everyone else in saying this is one of the best games to be made, period.

Then act 2 happened. (Spoilers under here.)

.
.
.
.
.
.
.

I came here for a creepy, well made card builder rougelite. I got that in the first act, but after the home video segments with Carder- which honestly, if you aren't here for meta ARG stuff, I would just skip it- and the introduction of the three other systems of Inscryption, I felt... disappointed. Rather than the different systems feeling as if they were all part of the same game, they felt like spin-offs of the original game you learn in Act 1, but without any of the interesting resource management it had to offer to begin with. Using the Tech packs as an example- it's likely the only other type of deck players would have experienced, since it is used in Act 3 similar to the use of the beasts in Act 1- the energy packs don't feel like a strong enough resource mechanic. You are no longer sacrificing beasts, deciding if you want to draw a squirrel card or chance for a damage card, instead the squirrels get replaced with energy bots that do little else but act as blockers to damage. They are also the only card you can play for your first turn. After that, your battery charges up cell by cell as the battle progresses, but using a hefty card that needs five cells doesn't require careful planning, just waiting. Often times in Act 1 I would sacrifice weaker beasts to play a Grizzly or Shark, but that mechanic is simply gone in Act 3. Not to mention that Leshy is replaced with Po3 who doesn't have HALF the personality of Leshy, making the "dungeon crawling" portion of the game feel lifeless and tedious. When navigating the map in Act 1, you would get dialogue from Leshy that would immerse you in the world, with him taking on different masks for characters. Act 3 tries a similar thing with Po3, but his -all attitude just doesn't make the interactions work.

TL;DR, Act 1 is solid and a great game- which is especially telling since the DLC for this game expands ONLY that portion of the game- and Acts 2 and 3 are disappointing. If the first Act ever gets a real card game out of this game's success, play that instead of the full game. If you really want to see what the hype is here, buy this on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
