\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPossible spoiler alert!

The first act has great gameplay and atmosphere. You are trapped in a cabin in the woods with an unpredictable maniac, where you are forced to play a board game with gruesome mechanics (like sacrificing body parts to survive). The board game functions like a rogue-like deckbuilder (a la Slay the Spire) and uses unique and interesting mechanics. The board game also runs in parrallel to an escape room mechanic where you can get up off the table and walk around and have to use clues hidden in the board game to discover solutions to the cabin's various puzzles in order to escape. These two gameplay elements blend together well and this is by far the best part of the game, and unfortunately, it doesn't last very long, it is just the first third of the game. This could have been a full game in itself, but it was never fully fleshed-out, instead it gets aborted by a goofy fourth-wall breaking genre-shift. At this point, we are introduced to a badly-acted meta-plot involving a youtuber who gets embroiled in some nonsensical government conspiracy, which simply ruins all immersion. The meta-plot takes itself completely seriously but is so ridiculous that it spoils any chance of respecting the game's story from this point on.

The second act completely changes the atmosphere and gameplay, and plays a lot like the Pokemon Card Game for the Gameboy (a spinoff of Pokemon). Not very good. It kind of brings back nostalgia, but this is also not fully fleshed out. The third act is yet another shift in gameplay and atmosphere, more similar to the first act but nowhere near as good in either aspect. The card mechanics for this act are basically hearthstone but as an RPG. The finale is what ultimately prompted me to give this a downvote. It is extremely anticlimactic, leaving you feeling frustrated and unfulfilled. You get teased with two more game modes, which you don't get to play more than a brief demo of, and then the story finishes with loose ends in an unsatisfying way (with a "too deep for you" ending that is left intentionally vague).

Overall, this game is a case of "too many chefs spoil the broth". Instead of being a single, great game, and sticking with what works, it is one great minigame and two lousy minigames duct taped together by a bad story.\t\t\t\t\t

Honestly CAN'T recommend this game enough. With beautiful visuals and captivating story-telling, this game had me hooked from the beginning all the way to the end and on the edge of my seat throughout. People really aren't exaggerating when they say how lore-heavy the game is, and even though I still don't understand all of it, I really have to applaud the developers for how much clear love and hard-work they put into this game. Things were thrown at me left and right that I won't get into as to avoid spoilers, and I found myself getting attached to characters that I didn't expect to get attached to. What starts out as simple and sweet unravels into something gorgeously complex, and keeps you morbidly curious throughout. If you decide to get this game, my enjoyment mainly came from not knowing what would happen next, so I suggest going into it knowing as little as possible. AMAZING game.\t\t\t\t\t
