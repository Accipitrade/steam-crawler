The only complaint I would've had is that I didn't like the first change from Act 1 (Leshy's cabin); and it's game-style, art-style, and play-style) to Act 2 where it introduced an entirely new game-style, art-style, and a lot of new play styles. But by the end of the game I came to appreciate all the new mechanics that kept being implemented. It changed just enough to keep you interested (so it doesn't get repetitive) but keeps enough to hold the magic from previous mechanics.

The pacing on the story line was 10/10.

The characterization was 10/10 hated Leshy during Act 1&2 and felt bad for P03 since he was the stoat, ended up hating P03 and cried a LOT for Leshy by the end of the game (i miss him i'm so so sorry) .

The art style was a solid 10/10 no matter how many times it changed.

The game mechanics were a 10/10, personally, it was all very easy to understand and I was able to get the hang of things very quickly no matter how many new elements were introduced.






TLDR: 10/10 game. Made me cry. Was not at all what I was expecting but was pleasantly surprised.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is super cool. For starters, the mystery it sets up by only having a "continue" option from the start is really fun and interesting. Then there is the actual gameplay, which is a lot of fun too. The fact that there is a rulebook to help forgetful players is also nice and useful. I like the inspiration it takes from slay the spire with the way you progress and bosses being at the end of each area, which provides a nice flow and more strategy to the game. On top of all of that I like the puzzle-solving aspect in the cabin which is really fun, and the helpful hints that the talking cards give. But I still haven't mentioned my favorite mechanic yet, deathcards. Whenever you die in Leshy's cabin, you get to make a special card which is the culmination of your run, this card can then appear in future runs. For 3 different aspects you are allowed to choose one from 3 different cards. You can choose the cost of it (sometimes you can even get them for free), the stats (health and damage), and the most OP part, the special abilities, I'm not entirely sure if they have a name or not, but you can essentially put special things on them, like the ability to go back to your hand when the card dies, or the ability to be sacrificed infinitely. This usually results in a very powerful card that makes the game very fun. If you have the money for this game and enjoy strategy and turn-based combat, then you should 100% at least give this game a try.
(please note I don't know everything so if I got anything wrong please correct me in the comments!)\t\t\t\t\t
