\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is an interesting case for me, and I would put it up there as perhaps the most creative game I've played this year. I'm not usually that into deck builders, though it would be selling the game short to assign it to one genre. Without spoiling anything, it's got quite an ambitious scope and culminates into a fairly grand adventure.

One area of criticism I have for it, and perhaps other players feel differently, is that the pacing somehow felt a bit off. There were a couple points where I put it down for a few days and then came back to it. It didn't necessarily keep me engaged as much as it seemed to for other folks putting up as game of the year. With that said, it's one of those games that actually takes a friggin' risk instead of just being another redundant cookie cutter experience in an overcrowded genre. For that alone, I'm happily departed with my cashola. This is the kind of game that will remind you that these are works of art that can be as subversive or effectual as any other medium. If you like supporting creativity as much as I do, I can't think of a better title this year spend your money on (except maybe Wildermyth).\t\t\t\t\t
