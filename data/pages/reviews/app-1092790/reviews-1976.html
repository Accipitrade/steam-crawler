\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has a great card battle system and gripping story that i wished i went in unspoiled.
if you like card battles i highly recommend it, and warn you to not read any further.

There many secrets and puzzles hidden around which makes each run more interesting and exciting, there were some annoyances here and there, eventually your deck gets filled up with so many cards that its difficult getting the cards you need to get started, and sometimes there battles you just can't win, either because of unlucky starter hand, or just not enough resources to get enough damage on the board to win the tug of war, yes the health system rewards neglecting defense as you just need to get 5 more damage than your opponent.


okay ready to be spoiled?

Act 1 came in the strongest, rogue like card battler with a great material system, I don't have examples as this is my first card battler, but I have seen people compare it to slay the spyre.
Act 1 was as in my opinion the best, its gripping and the resources system is much easier to deal with than the last act.
there many cards with different effects and costs so diversity is a double edged sword, having many cards means you dont always get what you need, but might also get the other summoning cost item.
While the entire rogue/card battler part is going on you also have puzzles/challenges around you to complete.
If you complete a certain puzzle you will get the item needed to get to Act II

Act II, was in my opinion the weakest, the game took the sharpest turn away from what was advertised, its closer to pokemon than a rogue like, the art style changes from 3d/ink to pixel but you get 3 extra deck types, each with their own gimmick, I personally played a necromancer deck, while it was a rough start as it resources in Act II aren't a separate pool like it is in Act I it did work good when I got my cards set, which also is different to Act I, your deck is no longer random, you can choose which cards to add to your pool with a minimum of 20.

Act III, goes back to how Act I was, but with a different resource system, unlike Act I resources fully regenerate per turn, but takes 5 turns to get to maximum level (unless you use specific buffs on cards) so you are limited to weaker cards in the beginning which can be very difficult to manage from time to time, as all of my 1 cost cards had no attack, and much later got thorns so at least taking a hit cause a hit of damage back, but I have had plenty of battles where my pulls were terrible leaving me with not enough energy to do damage, but too many attacks to survive.

Ending, I felt like the ending was a sneak peak of what could be coming in inscryption 2, or what was cut for some reason, with the remaining 2 scrybes battles each having a unique (battle)board.
