\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an excellent game that I recommend to a sizable number of people. It is a stylistically interesting game, more so perhaps than the promo material lets on- however, (spoiler), that is broken up by jaunts into the life of a probably intentionally unlikable and annoying real-world streamer. It's not unbearable, but you are forced to watch these to get a more balanced view of the plot, which is core to full enjoyment of the game.
The soundtrack is a really pleasant surprise- it's excellent and fits precisely where it is. It's a fairly lightweight game as well and the settings are flexible enough to run it on most pseudo-modern computer. You get a lot of bang for your buck, visually. It's well-composed, lit, and rigged.

This is high up there on my list of best-liked indie games, and is frankly even better with the release of (possible spoiler) Kaycee's Mod, which adds another layer of replay value and extra lore, which is perfect after one's first playthrough. That goes double if you, like me, enjoy the very beginning of the game. I do wish we got the early-game experience with (significant spoilers) the other Scrybes- P03's was fun, and the contrast between Leshy's endgame (death and re-incarnation) and P03's (reboot) was funny. Grimora is a great character, and I loved what I got to see of her and her cameo at the end of Kaycee's. Magnificus... well, every race has to have a loser, I guess.)

If you like deckbuilding games (I'd compare the AI difficulty to Wingspan automata), or you like fearsome critters/Americana lore, this is a perfect game for you. If you like the difficulty of games like Darkest Dungeons, this is a good fit. Really, even if you haven't in the past, I'd encourage you to give this game a shot. If you're on the fence, Manlybadasshero on Youtube offers an enjoyable playthrough.\t\t\t\t\t
