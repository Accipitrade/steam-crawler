\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my first review on a game, so I might miss some stuff when writing this.
I just finished Inscryption and I gotta say, it really shows when a game has a lot of heart behind it's development, what an experience this game was! Card games never kept my attention for too long and I usually suck at them, but Inscryption has insteresting takes on the mechanics that are intuitive for someone that isn't particularly into card games. Some people said that it relied a little too much on luck, but honestly, the game was lenient enough on some aspects so that I wouldn't feel too frustrated when I lost. What also helped the game to not get stale or frustrating was the ambiance brought with the the incredible artstyle, an immersive soundtrack, interesting characters and the evolving plot.
Also I'd say the game is better experienced without prior knowledge aside from "spooky card game", I've thoroughly enjoyed the game, though it might not be what some people are looking for, since it doesn't rely on strategy and deck building as much as your usual card game, but I'd still recommend you to play the demo, the setting might just get you hooked.

TL;DR - If you want an immersive experience I recommend it 100%, easily one of the best games I've played this year.\t\t\t\t\t
