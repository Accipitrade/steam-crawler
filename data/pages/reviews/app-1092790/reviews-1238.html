Inscryption is a treasure trove of squandered potential.
This is the first time I've been really torn about how to review a game. Inscryption is a surprising game, and, for the most part, fun, but it fails severely in some ways that I have a hard time forgiving it for. [TL;DR: the store page is like when a movie puts all the interesting parts in the trailer. Act I is exceptional, but the other two acts are very different and not nearly as fun. The story is engaging and surprising, but it leads you on. It ends up almost completely senseless and has very little emotional impact because 1) the central conflict goes unexplained and 2) almost every major character is simply unlikable, and they all lack depth.]

Firstly, it seems that everyone agrees that Act I of Inscryption is the best part. It was practically a masterpiece: it was gorgeous, the card game is nearly flawless, the ambiance is perfectly creepy, and the puzzles are cool. It was the most fun I’ve had in a game in a while. If I were just reviewing Act I, it would get at least 9/10. In fact, Inscryption might even be worth the price just for that! But the rest of the game falls flat. It changes so significantly in playstyle, art style, and music style after Act I that I felt a little bit duped. It’s okay for games to have surprises, but it changed a lot. So, if you want Inscryption, know that what you see on the store page is only about half of the experience.

So what went wrong?
The issues multiply from Act II on. Let’s talk gameplay first. In Act I, the card game—the gameplay’s core—is glorious. It's addicting and unique. In Act II, it suddenly becomes convoluted and gimmicky (the complicatedness at LEAST doubles). In Act III it is less convoluted, but less engaging than in Act I. (I had to start virtually every battle with the exact same play—boring.)

Now let’s talk plot. (Beware spoilers from here on. I’ll tag serious ones.) The plot has enough twists and turns (with some good foreshadowing) that it will keep most people interested the whole time. The problem is that the plot ends up very surface-level, like you read a wiki summary of it. When characters happen to have motivation, it is superficial. The reasons why and how they turned up where they are is not often explained. Furthermore, and more gravely, the main conflict of the game—the OLD_DATA—is never elaborated. We just have to believe that it is bad, without actually seeing it do anything bad. The only time you almost find out why it is bad is during an encounter with the Bone Lord. This encounter is a secret, so many players won’t see it. But even if you do get the scene, the game cuts out the camera, meaning the main impetus for multiple antagonists’ actions remains completely unexplained (and the player feels unrewarded for having found the secret and following it through to Act III). I still don’t understand what the main antagonist P03 gains from their plot, nor why GameFuna would go to such lengths to supposedly protect the world. These are serious, glaring issues that affect the story to its core. I’m honestly baffled that so many are lauding this as a supposed GOTY with an incredible story.

Finally, let’s consider the characters. The attempted tragedy of the game completely fails because not a single character leaves an emotional impact. They are all flat, even the protagonist, who has no hint of a personality besides his interest in card games. By the way, the acting is OK—I assume it’s meant to be endearingly cheesy and nerdy. Is anything at all at stake for him in this story besides himself? We don’t know. All of the antagonists are single-minded and similarly undeveloped. The antagonists try to make you pity them at the end, but I couldn’t scrounge up an ounce, because they are all horrible people. Most of them literally torture and kill other people. Some characters are hinted to be important or powerful but end up disappearing without a trace, having no ultimate effect whatsoever on the overall story. These are the Bone Lord and the mycologist. As for the protagonist Luke’s real-world enemy GameFuna, their goal is vague and their motivation and origins are completely unexplained. Their bizarre actions only leave the player shocked and confused by the end of the game, because, again, the true evil of the game is never expounded upon. This is why the fourth-wall breaking doesn't end up working or scaring the player—why should we be worried if we don't even know why the OLD_DATA is bad, or if it can affect anything outside of the game?

Verdict
The card game is fun, and many people will enjoy the plethora of gimmicky variations on it once they wrap their head around its labyrinthine rulebook. But I'm also a story gamer, and unfortunately Inscryption's characters are so shockingly one-dimensional that none of them leave a lasting mark. Where is the humanity? The relatability? The pathos? With all these glaring story issues, I’m honestly confounded why so many are touting it as the game of the year, telling people to stop reading and buy it already. My advice is different: Buyer Beware! If you purchase, you’ll get half of the game you thought, and a half-baked story rife with squandered potential.


...And then there’s the ARG. Many players aren’t aware, but Inscryption includes coded messages that the community cracked to learn extra lore. I have read the findings, and they don’t explain any of the fundamental issues I’ve described. The final piece of the ARG even shows a polar opposite ending to the story. Why would that be hidden behind codes that took large groups of people weeks to solve?

Honestly, Inscryption’s saving grace is something not included in the base game—Kaycee’s Mod. It’s a beta that exists because of fan demand and it makes Act I a nearly endless challenge. If only Inscryption could have maintained that level of quality throughout its story...


I have tried to be thorough while leaving the review informative enough without spoilers for potential buyers. If you’ve already beat the game or don’t care a bit about spoilers, here is a list of questions I feel did not get answered in the story.
Why and how did the Stoat and Stinkbug change appearances in Act I?
Why was Leshy unaffected by being turned into a card at the end of Act I? Isn’t that how he imprisoned the other Scrybes?
What is the pov of Act I?
How did the 3 Scrybes escape P03’s plot and sabotage him in Act III?
Was there a reason that P03 chained up the player in Act III? Luke could get up from his computer in real life and stop playing at any moment.
How did Leshy overpower the other Scrybes? Was it because he was in possession of the OLD_DATA? In that case, how did it end up in the Dredger’s hands?
Why are the Scrybes constantly locked in a power struggle? What will they use their power for?
Where did the OLD_DATA come from? Does it affect anything outside of the game?
In what way is the OLD_DATA evil? How could it affect “the fate of the world”?
Can the Scrybes control the OLD_DATA?
Who is the Bone Lord and why does it know about the OLD_DATA? Does it know more than the Scrybes?
Who is the Mycologist and why can they take over control of P03?
Why does P03 know that the internet and Steam exist?
Is there a reason the Scrybes are sentient and aware of the real world?
Why are some of the videos on Luke’s camera corrupted?
If GameFuna was a front to protect the world from the OLD_DATA, who created them? Are they good or evil? Are they hoarding the OLD_DATA’s power for themselves, as Hitler presumably was, or are they protecting the world? How are they so powerful?
Why didn’t or couldn’t GameFuna simply physically destroy the cartridge, killing the OLD_DATA?\t\t\t\t\t
