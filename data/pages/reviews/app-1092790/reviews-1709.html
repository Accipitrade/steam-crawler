\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like it. It has fantastic atmosphere, and the mechanics seem like they could be fun. But it's just way too luck-based. There's strategy, yes, and that does get you a bit further, but there's only so much you can do when you keep getting cards you can't use until you're so far behind you can't really catch up. And that's before even talking about how buggy it is. I've rarely been able to play for more than 15 minutes or so without it either locking up or just outright crashing. I'm currently at a point where I don't even know if I can continue, because it freezes when loading my save. I've managed to get past that once in a dozen attempts to load, only for it to lock up immediately as soon as I entered a card match.

This game could really benefit from a puzzle mode, similar to the puzzle boxes, where everything is in a fixed sequence, so figuring out the right order and the right moves would be how to approach it, as opposed to continuing to die (and create worthless death cards that further clog up what you get) and restart in the hopes of maybe getting something better.

Deck builders are a tricky thing to get right, and Inscryption falls more on the "frustrating" side than the "fun" side.\t\t\t\t\t
