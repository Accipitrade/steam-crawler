\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is NOT just a regular card game. In its core, Inscryption is a psychological horror adventure game that has you absolutely astonished, everytime it completely reinvents itself. The game is absolutely meta. It breaks the 4th wall hard, creating an immensely spooky atmosphere and so you continue playing until you find all the information to puzzle this all together. However, don't kid yourself: you will not be able to understand EVERYTHING. Not only did the developers build an incredibly deep lore, they also hid an ARG, an RL scavenger hunt of sorts, in the game files, that eventually leads to an alternate ending. And talking about endings: This was the first game in quite some time that had me in that "Wow, I am absolutely gobsmacked"-state when the credits rolled. By far the best indie game in recent years! Do not miss out, if you like something meta along the lines of "There is no Game", or "Hack'n'Slash".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr If you are looking for a card game like Slay the Spire or Monster Train, you have it for 2-4 hours (depending on how good and lucky you are). After that, it's an entire different game!

The Long version

I would give this game a thumbs up, if the game finished in the first act... As you can see by the trailers and the screenshots in the store page, they all belong to the first act of the game, which is so freaking fun!

After the first act, you are presented with a second and third act... But these, are not like the first, it's an entire different game. All the fun mechanics, gameplay and graphics from the first act, are thrown out of the window!

I went in blind, without reading reviews and when I passed the first act, I was super exited! Saw the second act starting and was a bit surprised, but ok? Unfortunately, I should have stopped there... When the second act started, it was so boring, nothing like the first, completely different game, almost like it was made by a different developer.

Honestly, was fun for about 3 hours.\t\t\t\t\t
