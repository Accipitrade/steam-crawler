Act 1 is the delight that I was hoping it was going to be. I played the demo and was absolutely thrilled to see that it was everything promised and more. The difficulty is quite nice, but with recent updates, can be utterly shattered. Additionally, the requirement of a death after victory to beat the cabin puzzles is a bit cruel to someone who is replaying the game with the knowledge and skill that comes with it. All-in-all, great first time but poor replay ability unless you're intentionally bad at the game. The only critique I had on my first run through was that I had to replay the demo, but a small critique given the overall quality.
Act 2:
Absolutely my style, and I loved evrey minute of it. I loved the secrets, the style, the feel, and even the absolute terror you can cause with the legendary Oroborus! Sadly.... I didn't find any of these on my first run through. I didn't get the game breaking Oroborus, nor the obelisk, nor the Mycologist (because it just didn't exist at the time), nor the bone lords secrets, etc... Definitely better now, but lacking when I first played.
Act 3:
Okay, now for me to on the game. Act 3 was atrocious and horrible. When replaying the game, I had to abuse the Orobor-bot in order to speed run act 3 because I was that sick of it. The only good part of act 3 is the bosses, and the ending fair well. Literally no other aspect managed to intrigue me, even with the latest update. I didn't care for the Mycologist, or the Bone Lord, nor the fun/unique cards, nor the energy style cost system, nor the items, nor the room design, etc... This just wasn't it chief... If you know what is good for you, use Act 2 to build the Oroborus up, then use 4:00 on the clock puzzle to get it back in Act 3.
Kaycee's Mod:
A return to for for me,and a well-deserved one. I did a video on my YouTube channel called "Why I continue to love a game I cannot bet" which was all about the joys I was having with Kaycee's Mod. Yes, there is brutal and cruel RNG, but that is what you get in an alpha state. I have managed to beat it, and I still come back to experience more and more, because it is well worth it. I look forward to the changes here impacting the main game. One wish I had was that, since the player can handy-cap themselves for challenge points, perhaps a reverse system? Handy-cap Leshy, but at the cost of losing challenge points yourself. Might do well to balance the game.\t\t\t\t\t
