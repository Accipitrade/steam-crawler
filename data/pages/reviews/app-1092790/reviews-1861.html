Inscryption was one of the most delightful games I have played in 2021

It combines card-building roguelike with the unease of psychological horror and some tinges of puzzle-solving in an escape room like environment.

There are a lot of different approaches to this game as you clear it, and without spoiling, I will give my take on each.

3D, in the cabin : It is the beginning and for me, the best part of this game. There is no denying it: The culmination of when you first see those hypnotic swirly eyes talk to you, the gameplay, the presetation of the DnD-esque board and your surroundings and how it blends this warm, antique feeling with the paranoia, confusion and fear of it all.

It flows naturally for the most part, the puzzles you complete have a sense and a purpose to the story and the "characters" brought to life, quite literally, are rich enough.

Camera viewing : For me, a natural way of bleeding exposition. There are better ways to convey a story, but this wasn't overstaying its welcome. It kept me intrigued, and the person portrayed felt real, which is a thing half of the games can't quite nail on. The story itself had an "unsatisfying", yet very appropriate conclusion to it. My gripe is that we cannot play with Grimora and Magnificus the same way we did with Leshy and P03.

2D world It is a good section. A bit annoying if you choose a CERTAIN starter deck like I did, but other than that, it is a series of puzzles and card battles until you defeat 4 big baddies. Falls short on the aesthetic, and with such a strong start, it is hard to keep impressing. While being a bit worse in comparison, it is still GOOD.

3D 2: Electric Boogaloo It is the same concept as the beginning 3D section, however, the atmosphere and thematic are COMPLETELY different. Not better in my opinion, but different. You deckbuild in the same way as the first part, but with many more catches to it. More challenging, but ultimately less flavourful. Very enjoyable nonetheless with some sass added to it.

FINALE Where it mostly explains the grievances and thematic differences between the 4 scribes, while accompanying the ending to its cutthroat ending. The scribes are themselves parts of what a developer can be: Lore focussed, balance foccused, Presentation focussed, or even just an excited person overall. As it comes to a conclusion, the pieces fall together, and the *why* what happens, happens, becomes fairly obvious.

Overall, the game is pretty damn good. Oozing with atmosphere, great gameplay for the most part (if you absolutely love roguelike deckbuilding), good complementary puzzles and a good story.
