\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike I've seen many other users say in other reviews, I now also wish that Steam had a "Mixed" or "Neutral" review score. There is more to this game than the store page and promotional material shows, or perhaps less depending on how you look at it.

While the game throughout my 9-10 hours of playtime kept the playing card mechanic central to its design, it changed everything around it up quite drastically. I don't want to spoil the later 2/3 of the game but don't get too attached to the style of gameplay that you start with, I know I did.

Everything that this game does is done pretty well (art, animation, sound, story, originality, puzzles) the fact that it dropped that original roguelike deckbuilding Slay the Spire-esque gameplay really stings. It is a trippy, dark and pretty cool ride with unique challenges and some fun puzzles, but I honestly wish the full game would have just stuck to the original gameplay formula as it did that really well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is just amazing. The start is a bit slow, but once you get past that. it really bumps up in speed. A lot of people really love the beginning though, so this slow bit is likely not to bother you too much. Then, there's something that will happen that will be jarring, but if you make it past that, it'll be worth it. It's the thing people in the negative comments are talking about, and I don't think they're in the wrong. I almost quit too, but I'm glad I didn't. The jarring bit itself also got more interesting the further I made it into it, so yeah. I think it's jarring because you expect a horror game, and for a little while, it seems like it suddenly isn't a horror game. But it is! Don't worry, the scary will return.

This game keeps throwing new things at you, it keeps trying to do new things and it keeps being fresh throughout the entire 11 hours. I've never seen so many mechanics being thrown at me in a way that isn't tedious. It almost felt like I was being spoiled somehow; that any other game developer could've stretched this game well up into the 20h+ category without having the game feel slow or grindy. So yeah, the game mechanics are awesome. Slightly frustrating at times, but overall, totally worth said frustration.

The narrative is also pretty great. The ending didn't make a lot of sense to me, but somehow, I enjoyed it all the same. I was sort of high off the ride at that point, to be honest. A lot of the narrative relies on drawing certain cards, doing certain things, etc. You have to puzzle your way through the game's narrative, and that's awesome.

I don't know what else to say. It was just awesome!\t\t\t\t\t
