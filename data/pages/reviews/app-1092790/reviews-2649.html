BUYER BEWARE
So I feel like I need to write a review for potential future buyers as the marketing isn't really "honest" about this game.
If you are coming into the game expecting it to remain the same throughout your playthrough be prepared for changes.
The game is divided up in different acts with a overarching meta story that plays throughout.
The Demo and from the videos advertised is only showing you 1/4th of the total game. So many first impressions are coming only from this part of the game alone and nothing else, luring buyers into a false understanding.
Also there is a few certain bosses that will ask permission to access your harddrive which raises a flag of concern. This opens a in browser directory of your computer listing the name/size/and date of your files and even later will display the name and icons of your steam friends on enemy cards. Some might find this a invasion of privacy especially for those that would want to stream or publish their game play online.
The game itself is rather enjoyable with rather simple card game play with the occasional puzzles in between. There are lots of secrets to be found as well. The characters are all rather enjoyable and the atmosphere of the game is always shifting. Its certainly an experience that's worth going through.\t\t\t\t\t

The "story", if you can call the jumbled mess a "story", is complete high art meta nonsense made confusing and pointless as an end rather than a means. Youll walk away not with a message, but wondering why the developer even bothered with so much effort for literally no pay off, and wishing that the first section had been the entire game, in my case. Is it worth 20 bucks? Thats a maybe. It doesnt seem like any choices made actually impact anything, so replayablility is about zero. Frankly, I enjoyed it, I probably would have had more enjoyment saving my money for something I could have played with more than once. You live and learn. Buy at your own risk, but know what you spend the first 2 hours on IS NOT the entire game, and after youve overstayed the steam refund period, maybe 2-3 hours, you get to the actual meat of the game which isnt half as enjoyable as that first taste.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will preface this by saying that I found Inscryption extraordinary overall, but the marketing is very dishonest indeed. This is NOT a moody card collecting roguelike as the store page and demo suggest. I can best describe the experience by saying that, after a few hours of the wonderful section highlighted in the store page, the game was like BORED NOW, LET'S SPOOF 8-bit POKEMON FOR A BIT . The roguelike is built to be experienced, enjoyed, broken like a Kit-Kat, and then you move on to the rest of the game.

Regardless, I very much liked the game. It completely got in my head and I could barely pull myself away. I enjoyed Mullins' previous games in spite of their awful gameplay. In Inscryption, this was not a problem - the game plays very well even as it leaps through different styles and repeatedly upends its gameplay loop.

Recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
