\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(Spoilers)
It's a good game and experience overall.

The biggest problem of the game is the 2D part in the middle. It is completely boring and the mechanics built up in the first part of the game are completely forgotten for a deckbuilder. The worst thing is that while new cool mechanics are introduced, you can play through the part without even reading through them, just hoping you topdeck the correct cards with the correct deck in order to win. You don't even have the choice of drawing squirrels or not, making the second part a bad RNG heavy experience.
The third part gets good again, but it's also a bit too gimicky at some points. The bosses were pretty cool and the gameplay was fun, but the athmosphere of the first third of the game is not matched here. There are some secrets you can find while walking around, I guess.
The part which I dislike most is the story though. As this is supposed to be a story game, it's pretty lacking. The real life plot with the protagonist Luke didn't interest me at all, and it ended up being kind of generic. "Guy gets mysterious game noone else has a copy of and gets threatened by the game company". The clash between the four scrybes in the game world was much more interesting, but much less developed. The ending sequence was actually pretty cool, playing against each Scrybe once more and shaking their hands.
I still think this game is well worth the 20 Euros and the visuals and card mechanics are mostly made with a lot of thought put into them. Still, the marketing seems a bit misleading.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like card games, then look no further. If you hate card games, I have a feeling there's still a lot for you to enjoy with this game. The art is so varying and unique, the story (which I didn't even expect in a card game) was rich and full of twists and turns, and the soundtrack is a blend of retro game themes and one from a horror movie. I came into the game having seen 30 minutes of gameplay on youtube, however I was blown away at how the rest of the game developed. There are puzzle elements, some rogue like elements, and plenty of hidden details and easter eggs. Bottom line, for $16.99 (sale price at the time) this game is an absolute steal. Took me about 10 hours to beat, not sure if that's fast, slow, or average, but there's tons of replayability, whether its experimenting with fun deck builds in the first part, or revisiting the others for anything you may have missed. All in all, buy this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery interesting game, with a very compelling story that keeps you wondering "just what the heck is going on here?". Unfortunately, it never really pays out on this wonder. It ends what feels like abruptly, and with a bit of a weird direction than you would think it would go. There are talks of a secret ending being in development and there are secret ciphers and whatnot hidden in the game that lead to a bit of an apparent real world rabbit hole of some sort, so there may be something more there. But to the average person who doesn't have the time or know-how for that sort of thing, it may be a bit of a miss until others figure it out and post about it online. Still though, pretty fun game up until that point. Really hope there is more to be had in future updates.\t\t\t\t\t
