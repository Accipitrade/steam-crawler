An absolute blast of a card battler. Inventive, stylish, and equal parts escape room, roguelite, deckbuilder, and... Blair Witch?

The mechanics are fun, but it's the polish and execution that really make the experience.

The developer took the central conceit of deck-building roguelites and built a meta-narrative experience on top of it. It's like Hades and Slay the Spire had a baby, but then a paternity test revealed Pony Island was actually the father. (which makes more sense after I found out Pony Island is from the same dev)

My only gripe is the game perhaps tries to be too much. I think the Blair-Witch-esque found-footage aspect was a bit over the top, and honestly distracted from what made the rest of the game so enjoyable. Another minor complaint is that I found the "first section" of the game to be far more interesting and polished than any section afterward, so after beating the old dude, the rest of the game was a bit of a come-down, even if it was still a good time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat potential but missed my expectation.

Be aware that you buy "bundle" of mixed card-game-genres and those are there just for telling story purpose. It is story-based game mainly. In my opinion this doesn't fit well with potential audience which is either casual player who (after playing Pony Island/Hex) want story, not challenge or either hardcore über gamer which want challenging card game like in demo.

And below long version what I like and what I don't.

Three acts, three feelings. Excitement, sadness then frustration.

I know other games from author, seen demo and gameplays and I expected some rouge like card with *elements* of escape room and pinch of psychodelic/glitchy vibes. Unfortunately this game consist of three acts and *only first is heavily advertised* wrongly suggesting that whole game focuses on act 1.

(And no, showing two-seconds-length-glitchy-frame on other parts of game in trailer doesn't tell me anything, I could get impression those were minigames as well.)

First act is about rouge-like we see in demo, it took me ~2-3h to beat it and I rate it to 8/10. I won't rate it 10/10 only because it wasn't balanced so well. Buffs we get after deaths are insane and final boss is weakling with easy to exploit soft spot (not the case for B-wall).

But in the end it was super fun, so well designed mechanics (so well that I couldn't believe that was only bait, more on that at the end) and wouldn't mind standalone game based on this chunk of game.

Second act is about deck builder with retro pixel style which reminds me Pokemon TCG games on GBC, it took me ~1-2h and I rate it to 7/10.

What I don't like is that I couldn't willingly decide when to jump to this world. I wanted to go back to first act solve mysteries which I left here but no, there was no warning that taking ... causes ... and poof... You have to enjoy this second act. I was sad. But after grief passed away I quite enjoyed this part of game too.

I reduced my rate for this act from 8 to 7 because this act didn't keep dark-mysterious-escape-from-room vibes from act 1, it disappeared at all at expense of explaining (to be fair quite good) plot. At this moment I knew that there is third act and I knew when I'm going jump into it, y'know, there was that mystic-area-that-is-locked-at-beggining-of-journey-and-light-up-like-x-mas-tree-once-you-achieve-something. Okay, let's move on.

Third act is dungeon crawler with deck building kept in blue techno-cyber-punk style, I rate it to 1/10 because it made me almost rage quit on few moments, I didn't like it's "aesthetics" and music was dull ambient (compare with act 1 bosses). But okay, even if I was never into cyber-punk style games, it isn't a valid reason to rate game (or part of it) so low.

What made me really angry and then frustrated (and I was already because I wanted go back to solve secrets from Leshy cabin since act 2, remember?)... so frustrating thing here is fact that in this act game is punishing you for yours mistakes greatly and there is no reset-lets-try-again button.

Once you add a bad card to your deck you have to deal with it. Add to it that you can't do basically anything meaningful in your first turn. Yes, in this act fights are dull-slow due to core mechanic and you are at mercy of rng gods that you will be offered something that you could play before your 5-turn (if you survive such long).

Later I managed to forge something playable from what I got on beginning, got some buffs and managed to go through most of the game being just bored instead of frustrated. Second thing which wasn't so good is lack of minimap of "dungeons". I often found myself going in loops (later found a minimap, but available only at checkpoint).

I could gramble more about shop costs and missing descriptions of goods offered but I will pass.

I can say that act 3 presented some interesting break-fourth-wall mechanics but I was tired already because of whole act annoyingness. And I really hope that-scary-thing-that-was-suggested-by-game wasn't real thing (and wasn't buggy).

Epilog had good moments (games with scribs) but it doesn't lift my personal rating that much.

Final words. I feel like I bought a bundle consisting of three games but there is a catch in there that I'm forced to play in order (and timeline) forced by developer. Also sacrificing well designed core game that you see in demo for a plot you didn't mention at first place is not fair hence I can't give positive review despite neutral (8+7+1)/3 =~ 5.3/10 rating.

P.S. Playing with game-genres was very well done in Hex and advertisement was fair too so I'm not asking for anything that is impossible.\t\t\t\t\t
