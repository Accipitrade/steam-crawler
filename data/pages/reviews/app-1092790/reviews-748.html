\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow I know how the ladies feel when I'm staring holes at them from across the room.

4/10
A lot of potential, with weak difficulty and lacklustre payoff.

What started out as a creepy card combat game with an interesting backstory ended up ultimately being a disappointment.

The gameplay
--------------------
Think of a card game meeting a locked room

The card game is a cake walk once you figure out the mechanics, and that's BEFORE the game decided to make it even easier on you by giving you the ability to use constantly-refreshed power-ups and unlocking more and more permanent boosts to get you through. The locked room is where you earn those power-ups. The game is designed to gradually keep pumping you up until you make it through - which is a shame. I felt like I didn't earn anything and that it was just handed to me. Acts II and III were dead simple - 100 chimps on 100 computers would have passed it, no infinite amount of time required.

Some of the boss fights are cool, but outside of the first act (and until you know the techniques), they are all way too easy to beat.

The aesthetics
--------------------
The opening act is pretty creepy and intense, especially the music. The second act looks and plays terrible. I actually had a game breaking bug that forced me to uninstall and play from the beginning again (so take my hours played with a grain of salt). The third act mirrors the first to some degree, but wasn't as pleasing to the eye and not nearly as creepy.

The story
--------------
The mystery is what kept me playing to the end (after the first act). There are some subtly dropped clues that apparently play into unlocking "the true ending." As you progress, you unlock a bunch of video records which basically act as a secondary story with very little overlap. Interesting and unique enough, but the payoff made 0 sense, even after googling WTF was going on. To be honest, trying to connect the two stories made no logical sense.

I may have missed some secrets in my play-through, but... What happened to the person who broke into Luke's house in the middle of the night? Why is Luke calling a lawyer and not an exorcist after what he just went through? If the company wanted their disk back before something bad happened, why not just gank Luke in the first place? What does the death of Lukes sister have to do with anything? Why was I not fully explained what the Karnoffel code was?

For the record, I was in absolute love with this game until I hit Act II, then it just became too weak in pretty much all aspects. And since the mystery wasn't really fully solved, I'm left wanting.

A waste of time? Probably not... it was an interesting enough journey, but not one I would recommend as a MUST SEE.\t\t\t\t\t

I have rarely felt so compelled as to come write a review immediately upon finishing the game, and this is one of those rare few times. So, let me be brief lest I word-vomit praise all over your eyes, dear potential player.

This game is a masterpiece. It's so incredibly difficult to pause in your play through once you've begun, so be warned on that and enjoy the deep dive.

This game appeals to one's competitive side with its elements of difficulty, it instills an itch in your brain that forces you to keep playing to 'scratch' that itch until you've completed, and then you feel the ghost of that itch as you sit digesting all you've just experienced and wondering what more there is to this... and yes, even after you've beaten it there is more. From what it seems like, much more, though I've not touched it just yet because I'm writing this review.
