Daniel Mullins is to Toby Fox what Alfred Hitchcock is to Walt Disney; in other words, this is Undertale only all grown up and ready to blow your mind. I've been a fan of Mullins since a certain niche critic turned me on to Pony Island. A surprisingly inventive affair for all it's simplicity and spunk. Mullins has truly come into his craft here. Creating a game that's more than puzzles and secrets, but a truly terrific game you'll have trouble putting down. I started at 11:30. It's 3:30 AM now. I'm supposed to be at work in three hours and I haven't slept.

It was worth it.

The cardplay is simple, versatile and fun!! And I need to be clear about something here: I HATE CARDS! Well, that's not exactly true. I like traditional 52-french stuff, but Magic? Pokemon? Hate the stuff. But I LOVE playing Inscryption! I have no experience with deck building or any of that junk, but I was able to pick up and finish this in just four hours! ..... Or so I thought. Turns out, the absolutely intense hostage situation that forms the core of the story is just the beginning. But maybe I've said too much.

Atmospheric. Inspired. Absolutely Incredible! Even if you think there's nothing in the trailer that you'll like, you're just wrong. You're wrong! And I feel very sorry for you. Best game of 2021, and yes, I know what else came out this year. PLEASE TRY THE DEMO!!

UPDATE
Once you finish the cabin chapter the game opens up in a way that is more than a bit prosaic. I wouldn't blame anyone for checking out, but [REDACTED] The final battle is worth seeing, even if you aren't exactly playing. I want to replay and do the unsolved mysteries. There's definitely replay value if you're resistant to looking up the solutions online.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't normally do this, but I feel compelled to write a review before I've even completed the game.

Just to create some perspective, I've played the other two games by Daniel Mullins and was very underwhelmed. I completely bounced off of Pony Island (boring/cliche) and felt that the Hex, while containing some interesting ideas, sort of failed to deliver an engaging gameplay experience and suffered from low production values (kind of feels like a newgrounds flash game). So please don't think I'm some fanboy when I say that Inscryption may be the greatest video game experience of all time.

First of all, the production values on this game are EXTREMELY HIGH. I'm completely blown away by the effective use of mixed-media as it fills out the gameplay experience and contributes to the world-building. The mechanics of the game so far are also extremely tight and allow for some very interesting and compelling combos and strategies. But above all else, the tone of this game is unparalleled. It goes from creepy, to funny, to heartfelt, back to creepy, to mysterious, to almost too serious, back to creepy and so forth, without ever losing its footing, all to create an unbelievably gripping and compelling narrative. It uses all of its tools to draw the player in and keep them guessing, pilling on new elements to keep them engaged and going deeper and deeper into the mystery of the narrative. It does something I never imagined a rouge-like could accomplish: pull off an effective, genre-bending narrative that keeps the player interested at every step and wanting more and more, and then delivering on that more.

I'm only 7 hours in, and its possible that the game may not stick the landing, but at this point I feel like I'm enjoying the experience more than almost every other game I've ever played. Incryption is weird, unconventional, and a little disturbing, but all of the elements mix together perfectly and the execution is flawless. Bravo!\t\t\t\t\t
