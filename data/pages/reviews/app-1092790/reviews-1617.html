\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you remember in the Gold and Silver Pokemon games when you beat the main story in Johto and get to go to Kanto to play out the original story? It feels like your about done with this game then it unfolds into another version of itself, and it is just amazing. The concepts you see in game remind me of YuGiOh, the Pokemon TCG, and Magic the Gathering all condensed into an easily playable but hard to master package. The story FMVs that play between sections of the game reveal a dark story of a Youtuber that covers different card games and stumbles upon the game Inscryption in real life. The mysteries get even deeper when traversing the different sections of the game, leaving bread crumbs of what happened to the original maker of the Inscyption card game. If you are a fan of Slay the Spire like games give it a shot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can see why this is game of the year for many people.
Everyone is right, the less you know the better going into this.

But to highlight a few things which I loved:
Every mechanic they introduce in this game is incredibly well designed and engaging, you could probably make a game out of each individual one.

Atmosphere is so perfectly balanced; spooky is the obvious vibe, but the way they balance this with often funny or goofy lines of dialogue is perfectly done. Sometimes if something leans too much into 'serious psychological horror' it can become stale, they definitely inject enough comic relief and personality to keep the balance.

The music is incredible, it's often quite lowkey, but any time i pay attention to the music I found myself admiring how good it was, the time that I wasn't paying attention to it, it was perfectly complimenting the rest of the game I was immersed in.

This is a one of a kind experience, that I would recommend to anyone, especially if you like card games!\t\t\t\t\t
