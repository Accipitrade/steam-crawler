\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbout the first third of this game is stellar. Great ambiance, story and gameplay. There's a bit of repetition if you die, but the overall difficulty is just right, so it doesn't get frustrating. Without spoiling too much, after the opening section, the game takes a left turn and changes style wildly. The overall polish of the game plummets. The graphics are either poorly drawn pixel art or a cold, emotionless twist on the opening. Worse, however, is that there is zero difficulty. The card combat becomes trivial, with the earlier cleverness, especially in boss fights, gone. The puzzles, to the extent that there are any, are too trivial to be interesting. This gameplay is intermixed with a cheesy set of videos, which are very dull to watch, that try to connect the events of the game to a real world character whose plot arc seems ripped from a bad J-horror film.
If you quit the game after the first pseudo ending, you'll love it, but if you keep playing, whatever joy the opening had will be drained.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd like to put an asterisk on my recommendation. The first half of this game (Leshy's cabin) is excellent. I loved the rogue-like elements and the card game was super fun. But, and spoilers, after you beat Leshy and unlock the adventure mode thing, it takes an extreme nose dive. The way you play the card game is completely changed and the rogue-like elements are completely removed. Several times while fighting any of the enemy characters, I would either get all squirrels and nothing to attack with or the opposite, all attackers but with no sacrifices to play them. A game that I was willing to say was one of the best I played this year, became something I no longer enjoyed playing. I guess if you like more traditional deck building card games, you may enjoy the second half. But, it was not for me.\t\t\t\t\t
