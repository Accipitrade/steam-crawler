\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuper fun rogue-like card game with a really somber, unsettling atmosphere and a mysterious story. The Soundtrack is superb and is very evocative of the feeling of sitting around a campfire on a cold winter's night listening to stories as you try to warm yourself from the chilling wind.

I really love the pixelated art style of the game as it is very nostalgic, while putting a lot of detail into every part of the cabin you're in; The card art is also very beautiful. the idea of a card game with the focus being on animals and insects is a really original take on the genre.

The deck building is really fun, allowing for some truly broken builds that are nigh impossible to lose with. Fights are fair and if you're smart enough you should be able to make it through basically every fight no matter what your deck is. All cards are fun to play with and no card feels particularly weak or unusable. The fact that you have two pools of resources with which to summon cards onto the field means that you rarely feel helpless or out of options


There are a lot of secrets to discover around the cabin that you play in; the deeper mystery behind the game is engaging. Losing never makes you want to quit and I regularly found myself excited for the next run, wondering what cards I would be offered and what build I would end up with this time.

I highly, highly recommend this game for anyone that even remotely enjoys card games or rogue-likes. Hell, even if you don't I think you should definitely give it a shot anyways if only for the atmosphere and soundtrack.\t\t\t\t\t

Edit: First part of the game is bombastic stylized oppressive strategic game design brilliance. The combination of the card building, constant developing game mechanics, beautiful sound design, exploration, narrative and puzzle solving elements makes this a perfect title.

Once you beat this you are forced to play an 8 bit version of this title. Sounds familiar I'm sure (Freddy's and similar psychologically cerebral games). It worked for those games. This feels like low quality padding. Everything that was 10/10 about the first part of the game is now 2/10. No graphical design (ugly 8 bit sprites of every old and new card), no color, the sound and music is now a form of torture, doubled the gameplay mechanics/card variety in a format that makes you not want to explore what is new, no more narrative flourishes (just reading text bubbles now with terrible sound effects).

Honestly ♥♥♥♥ this dev. They should've ended it with the first part at 8 hours easily worth 2-3 dollars an hour of gameplay for the quality design and replay-ability. If they couldn't make the last part of the game in the same world they either A) decided dubiously it was better design for "THE STORY" or B) got lazy. I honestly want a refund for this art school drop out chin stroke bs. How do you go about creating perfect design, on every metric, and go and somehow revert literally EVERYTHING to mediocrity.

What a squandered experience all because of laziness under the guise of artistry. Avoid like cancer\t\t\t\t\t
