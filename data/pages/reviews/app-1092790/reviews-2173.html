\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame of the Year 2021 for me.

This is a puzzle box/escape room-ish game with a fun and weird story where one of the bigger puzzles is a deck builder that gets fed into by the other parts of the game.

The deck building and card game itself is incredibly interesting and has a lot of systems going for it, so people who like deck builders can get a great kick out of this. It certainly did for me, since I really dug into Slay the Spire back when it was in early access and after it came out.

For people who are not really that much into deck builders, the game is meant to be beaten. You will get hints, help and other things from "outside" the deck building aspect.

The story has a nice mystery aspect and feels nicely eerie.

Also, if you start the game, let the full intro run through and just don't click through it. :)\t\t\t\t\t

Inscryption is an absolute masterpiece and easily one of my favorite games of this year if not of all time.

Inscryption is a game by Daniel Mullins the creator of other awesome games like Pony Island and The Hex and if you have played either of those games you kind of know sort of what to expect. There are tons of 4th wall breaks, lots of unsettling and creepy puzzles, and bone chilling physiological horror moments.

So you start the game trapped in an escape room with a creep murderer that is forcing you to play this roguelike deckbuilder sort of game that has an insane amount of depth in it. You go down different paths adding cards to your deck, sacrificing cards to power up other ones in your deck, getting items that can help you in combat, and more.

And just the core gameplay itself really holds up. Even if there weren't all the creepy and unsettling moments, the back and forth gameplay of trying to tip the scale in your favor by attacking your opponent is really satisfying. And there are a ton of unique keywords that the cards can have that all make them play differently.

And as you play the game and get further and further trying to complete a run you will find clues and hints of things you can interact with in the escape room you are trapped in that will further unlock the secrets of the game.

And it is just so freaking cool to be able to get up from the table at any time in between fights and it really builds this extremely creepy atmosphere.

And once you get far enough in the game, the game throws some crazy freaking plot twists and huge changes to the game that I really don’t want to spoil because it’s part of the experience and all while you are doing that you are slowly being shown video recordings that tell an extremely dark story and as you keep playing the game and getting deeper and deeper in the rabbit hole of the game it just gets more and more intense and amazing.

And it’s not only the story and horror atmosphere that keep getting more intense and unique, the gameplay itself starts to change from what you are used to and get comfortable with so you never feel like you are being forced to play the same type of thing for too long.

Each mechanic and type of gameplay the game shows off never overstays its welcome. By the time you finally start to master the game it does a complete 180 and takes you out of your comfort zone which is amazing.

And like I mentioned earlier, the game's presentation is absolutely incredible. You will be completely immersed in the world and won't want to stop playing.

It took me about 11 hours to complete the game but I could do another playthrough in the future to try and find all the secrets I missed but I overall feel very satisfied with my time in the game.

There are rumors that the dev is working on an endless, more traditional roguelike mode to add to the game which would be awesome to increase replayability but even if that doesn’t happen this is still an experience that is super unique and was 100% worth it.

If you still haven’t played this game I would highly highly recommend it because it is easily one of the best games I have played this entire year.
