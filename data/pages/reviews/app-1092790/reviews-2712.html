\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to address the concerns, rumors and defamation in the community regarding that this game is an allegory of Daniel Mullins past where he played MTG in a Furry Convention.
I REPEAT, THIS IS NOT TRUE
Now onto the review:
SACRIFICES MUST BE MADE

Inscryption is the succesor game from the prototype called 'Sacrifices must be made' thats available on itch.io
If you never played the prototype, nor the demo the game can be summed as a card strategy game (like your typical MTG/yugioh/heartstone) witha twist of escape room puzzles.
thats on the surface... as it is with Daniel Mullins games. Below it there usually is a deep and intriguing 4th wall breaking and expectation shattering mind melting fever dream of an experience. I saw it in Pony Island and in The Hex. Is it in Inscryption aswell? I cant truly tell yet after 10hours.

The game is split into 4 parts of which after 10hours of the BETA (yes it was full game) im mid part2 and nothing crazy the likes of The Hex/Pony Island i have not encountered. It COULD be later on but cant tell for sure. The 1st part is a EXTREMELY SOLID card game and mind me i dont even like card games. It combines roguelite elements with unlocks that carry through playthroughs and is amusing as HELL. The ARG additions are also amazing and extremely interesting as from what i have seen already.

i will update this review after i finish the game in its entirety to give a better view on the product\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpoiler free.

If you like what you see in the trailer, buy it.

The sounds, the atmosphere, the gameplay feel really on point. But the masterpiece is how the story is presented. After 4,7 hours playing straight, I feel like I have been played, I dont really know how Im supposed to feel, what I am really playing or how the developers came with this idea. All I have to say is that I am impressed and I can't stop playing because im engaged with the game I want to know how it ends (?).

Long time without playing a game that made me feel that weird.

Recommended 10/10.

PD: I ve played a lot of deckbuilders/roguelikes so I didn't struggle with the game mechanics and difficulty. Some people will need more time to reach the point where I am at the moment (not close to the end, for sure).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWas looking for a new game to play and then came across this, thought it looked really interesting so decided to pre-order it.
And wow it really is an interesting game, at its core its a rogue-lite card game with hints of DnD meaning you start the run with not a whole lot but gain more cards and increase their strength throughout the run to try and beat bosses.
It is very well done and is a great concept on its own but then there are also puzzles and easter eggs around the room outside of the card game, upon completing these puzzles you will unlock cards and abilities which will boost your runs from the beginning.
Very fun and I am looking forward to finding more cards, bosses and secrets!!

Edit: After completing the game I have found that the replayability that I was expecting from a roguelike card game just wasn't there. It's difficult to explain without giving spoilers, but it kinda feels less rewarding when you complete a run. I hope that an update happens which fixes this and improves the replay value.\t\t\t\t\t
