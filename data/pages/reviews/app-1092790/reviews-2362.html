Pros:
    *adds new mechanics semi-regularly to keep things fresh.*each mechanic is individually simple to understand, complexity arises from interacts between systems.*good art/atmosphere work, and great music
Cons:
    *gameplay shake-ups might not be to everyone's liking.*no major alternate routs for different gameplay/endings*some bits feel a bit to RNG heavy, even for a card-game based

SPOILER REVIEW:

The three different games played across the three 'acts' of the story are all fine, Act 1 is easily the best, though act 3's isn't far behind, act 2 feels like it needs work, or maybe it's weaknesses are just more exposed the the nature of "encounters" in that part of the game, fodder cards(things like squirrels, skeletons, and basic mox/gems) being mixed into the main deck there was the "to RNG heavy" bit I was talking about.

I'm not sure how I feel about the weird meta-story going on, I think it worked way better then it should, given the "you are playing as this guy who is playing the game you're playing, but it's actually cursed/possessed/self-aware AI(?) for him" premise of it, and while the creepypasta style of story works best with unexplained/unanswered mysteries, the ending of that story "about" the game and they guy playing it still feels unsatisfying(though the end of the game within the game itself worked just fine, I'd say an other all net positive)

I really hope an update/expansion/DLC adding acts for the other two Scrybes gets made, the brief glimpses we see of their separate gameplay modes would have worked was really cool(though I could understand if they made it so first time playthoughs were railroaded into the current sequence of events, the bit during the ending where Magnificus's game was revealed to a a YuGiOh reference complete with Duel Disks was great and works extremely well as is), and if they got added that could open up room for alternate/extended endings.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed this game, the unique take on a card game style and the deck building in it was very fun to experience. Even getting a game over, it still feels like you're still taking a step forward in progress. The story was fascinating and slowly piecing things together up until the conclusion was fun. I enjoyed every bit that this game had to offer.

I strongly recommend this game to anyone looking for a unique Singleplayer story with a slice of card game like gameplay. There's plenty of tid bits and secrets to get a hold of and learn through. Don't feel too bad if a simple stroke of bad luck ends your run, just get back in there and challenge it with a different approach.\t\t\t\t\t
