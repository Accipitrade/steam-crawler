\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game like no other you have played.

Inscryption is a mix of storytelling, roguelike-deckbuilders and escape room genres presented in multiple styles that I wish I could get more of. After finishing the game, you'll want to go back and figure things out even more. Maybe try different approaches to reveal some of the hidden story.

If you're looking for a challenge, this isn't it. You will be able to figure out the roguelike gameplay fairly quickly and it shouldn't give you much trouble. However, this is one of those games where it's more about the experience than the challenge. The creativity employed throughout the game is stellar, with a lot of meta elements coming into play.

You should get this game, if only for the experience - but it's tonnes of fun too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, the game is great.

It also is disappointing.

I really feel conflicted how to rate it.

But the truth is: To truly recommend buying this game, I would have needed to leave the game with a good feeling.

I didn’t.
Why?

The first part of the game is too good. I actually enjoyed the card-game there much more than in slay the spire. The atmosphere was great and the escape romish tasks also fit well.

Then the game goes through two more “big parts” that are just worse.

Even though I love pokemon; I love pixel-RPGs; I enjoy card-games, the pixel-RPG-part felt like a slog. Like work I had to do. It did not look good, there was no real atmosphere to immerse yourself in, and the card-game felt bland and uninteresting. Also, it was too easy. Played through the entire part almost only with the starter wizard-deck. Just swapped three or four cards. Never ever before managed a card-game to make me not like the card-pack opening part...

I worked through the pixel-area hoping the game would go back to its former glory.

It did not.

The third part, while “good” and better than the second, still lacked the great atmosphere and sense of threat of the first, and the gameplay also did not reach the same level. It felt like an okayish fanfiction of the first part. Good enough to keep playing, but not good enough to really enjoy it.

The final scenes, while fun, didn’t really got me.

The life-action videos also were a big miss for me. Felt unoriginal and boring.

Also, the “game accesses your computer”-parts felt too rare and kind of out of place. Tacked on. To summarize: The game seems to lack an overall design, and the different “games” of this game vary greatly in quality. I liked the game but did not enjoy it. Maybe, only buy on sale or just play the demo. A version in which you only play the first part (probably with more content and replayability) would be great!

To put it in points:

Part 1: 10/10

Part 2: 2/10

Part 3: 5/10

Final: 4/10

Videos: 2/10

PS: I knew about the developer, but Pony Island felt like a game where all parts fit together. It felt satisfying and original. Inscyption sadly does not.\t\t\t\t\t
