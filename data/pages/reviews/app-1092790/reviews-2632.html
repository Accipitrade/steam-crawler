\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDamn it.

The story, the game, the theme, the atmosphere is incredible. The twist, the surprise, the gotcha is well done. I like both aspects of the game (I know there's more) so far but I knew it was coming before playing the game for very long.

The cabin, the cards, the map, the board. The worlds, the deck building games, the changes to rules and mechanics. I don't mind them at all - I think it was part of the experience I've had so far, and look forward to the others that await ahead.

However - I must leave a negative review. The game is not what is solely advertised on the store page here at all. If you expect what is shown, as the only game you will play, as (possibly) the majority of the game you will play, you will be surprised. Pleasantly surprised perhaps. Unpleasantly surprised more likely. The surprise will likely occur after you have played the game long enough that you will not be able to refund it.

I knew this at the 30 minute mark having been suspicious of what I saw in the trailer (just about 2 seconds are dedicated to the surprise), and decided that I could appreciate the design decision made by the developer after I searched the reviews a bit to see if I was right in my guess.

However I do not like the precedent it sets. I feel at least two pictures should show the surprise so that buyers are aware of what is waiting for them. Other games may likely follow this... shady marketing. I realize the developer was probably not intending this - it was a twist on the player. I'm fine with that - but I can't.. I can't help but feel that it's a road not to be traveled down.

Damn it. If not for how it is presented on the store page I would gladly give it a thumbs up... but it's borderline false advertising. Again - I LIKE the game AND the surprise but I feel like it's... dirty. Well done. But still dirty.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI did enjoy the game a lot. The first playthrough is awesome, creepy, enticing, the story and characters draw you in and the cards have interesting enough mechanics to let you feel like you're improving your gameplay.

I was a little baffled about the narrative "Luke" character, I felt like that broke immersion. I thought the game would have had similar impact without any of the live action cut scenes.

Where the game really falls apart is at the end and then beginning a new game, the TLDR, like others have said, is that there is no replayability (careful the rest is full of spoilers): I chose to replace P03, so I was quite excited when I finished the game to try the other two Scrybes, I thought the ending was giving you a taste of what a round against them would be like, you start off against Leshy, then choose the next 3, maybe things will even be different in the cabins on subsequent playthroughs? I restart, everything is the same... So I get all the way back to choosing a Scrybe, only to be forced to go against P03 again, I was pretty baffled. They'd gone to the trouble of making these alternate 3D worlds, only to give you one round against them that didn't even matter. I was hoping to play against Gremoria and Magnificus, but that's not possible. Also, they could have made some things leftover on subsequent playthroughs that were like remnants, but everything is completely wiped after you see the credits and it's like you just downloaded the game. It drove me a little insane.

Overall, I liked the game, it's better than a lot of other games, it has a good rhythm, it's a fun story. It's easy for a gamer to sit here and say they could have done more, but it feels like they laid a groundwork that they didn't take advantage of entirely, and really they created an expectation for it with how clever and intriguing the first playthrough is.\t\t\t\t\t
