\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy honest opinion on what went wrong with Incryption. I think this can be summed up best in a single statement:

I found out too late that the developer is a writer first, and a game maker second.

This is where every issue that surrounds the game stems from and why the gameplay hits such lows later to make way for story.

I think everyone can agree that the initial marketing for the game was flawed. There has been tons of talk on that and I don't think I could add much to that conversation. Basically, this isn't one game, it's three, and you're locked out from going back if you liked one prior until the game is over. The marketing failed to make it clear, which is a failure on the developer and the publisher. This game tries to sell itself on the mystery which dissuades spoiling yourself, so most people don't look further than what the trailers show because they don't want to ruin the mystery. You probably wouldn't buy & play an Ace Attorney game after watching a full playthrough, because watching it spoils the point of playing the game. A lot of people probably had this mentality going in.

Even with story being the main focus of the game, it honestly failed on that front too. The game is a vehicle for an ARG, and it's pretty clear to tell the base game suffered because of this. The scribes were a cool concept, but the delivery just didn't stick. Everything involving The Lucky Carder just felt like someone acting out an old video game creepypasta. More effort went into the ARG than the main story and it is obvious once you get past Act 1.

Act 1 was great, no question. But nothing else in the game ever hit the same highs act 1 did, and the fact we needed to invest nearly 6-8 more hours just to go back was very annoying.

I think another user I saw in the discussion board put it best, so allow me to paraphrase:

This game is like going out to eat and they mess up your order. You gave the restaurant money to purchase the burger they had on their menu because you wanted a burger.
Then when the waiter comes to serve you, they hand you a steak. Look, steak is good, I like steak, but I ordered a burger. The menu said this was a burger. The picture of the food showed a burger.
But they still gave you a steak.

When you go to complain to the waiter saying "I didn't order a steak, I wanted a burger," another patron leans over an says, "Oh, you idiot! You shouldn't have ordered from the waiter! You should have ordered from the cashier if you wanted a burger!"

This is the issue. I didn't order steak. I don't want steak. I want the burger you put on the menu, and I shouldn't have to take an extra step to order it that I couldn't have known before hand!
I would have gladly ordered the steak another time when I felt like having a steak! The steak is well made, but I wanted the burger I ordered.\t\t\t\t\t
