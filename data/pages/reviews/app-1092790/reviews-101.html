\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is ok but the reviews saying it's the best game ever are way over the top. It currently has a 97% positive rating which puts it in the league of games like Portal, which I feel isn't right.

What this game does well is mostly the atmosphere. It starts off giving you a cosy and eerie vibe, and makes you want to find out more about what all this weirdness is about. But for me, the gimmicks wore off quickly, and the core gameplay and controls were more dominant.

The main downside to the game for me is the user interface. It's clunky, slow, and makes the game a slog to play through. I want to finish the acts so I can comment on the full story, but I can't be bothered. This is partly due to the interface and controls.

The card gameplay itself is pretty basic, and there's not much strategy. I don't think this is the main point of the game, but I still feel it could have been better. I've been spoiled by the creme de la creme of card games such as Slay The Spire, so I don't want to be too harsh here. The gameplay is fine. Just nothing mindblowing. Definitely not "best game ever" level though.
__________

Summary:

+ Good atmosphere that draws you in and makes you want to explore the weirdness.
- Clunky interface and controls make the game a slog to play.
- Gameplay isn't particularly interesting for me. Some people will find great enjoyment from the escape room style puzzles and gimmicks, but it didn't do much for me.

I would give the game a 5/10. It's fine, but not game of the decade like some reviews would have you think.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is unique enough from other Deckbuilders/TCG’s/Roguelikes to recommend, but it sadly feels unpolished in some ways.

The good:

- Awesome rogue mechanics. Anytime you lose a match, the way to win that match scenario next time is via one different decision. This effectively motivates you to try again, and do better next time.
- Game keeps your interest long enough to overcome its tough hurdles.
- Simple to learn, lifetime to master gameplay.
- Progressively increasing difficulty, especially in the postgame (aka Kacey’s mod) means that you, the player, get better at the game and keep learning. It was awesome after clearing some Kaycee mod challenges to redo act 1 & realize how far I’ve come skillswise.
- Fun horror and escape room mechanics in addition to Deckbuilding adds variety to the game & makes it stand out from other Deckbuilder games.
- The game mechanics change slightly on you in act 2, then changes again for act 3. This keeps the experience feeling fresh. Reaching each next act feels like an amazing accomplishment.
- The ability to play up to 4 different card styles, especially in act 2 (sacrifice, bones, batteries, gems), mixing and matching different cards & styles in a single deck.
- Steam achievements plus Kaycee’s mod keeps you interested in the game well past the 20 hours needed to clear the main game in one playthru.

The bad:
- Main game (Acts 1, 2, 3) felt “too short” at 20 hours. Yes there’s a few Steam achievements plus Kaycee’s mod to keep those dedicated playing up to 100 hours (like me), but it would’ve been nice to have more main game content.
- Act 1 is disproportionately longer and tougher than acts 2 and 3, which can potentially discourage a lot of folks from playing the game long enough to reach the fun stuff in act 2.
- A lot of the game is very luck-dependent, and I’m not even talking drawing the wrong set of cards in an individual battle, as good deck preparation/building can work around much of this. I’m talking about opportunities to gain cards/items you want or enhance cards in the way you want, especially for act 1 & Kaycee’s mod, and don’t even get me started about Skull Storm here.
- The money/teeth in act 1 & Kaycee’s mod isn’t usually good for much besides pelt and knives. Creator might consider taking a lesson from “Slay the Spire” here.
- Acts 2 and 3 feel more story-driven with less polished gameplay than act 1 and Kacey’s mod. It almost implies that Inscryption was meant to be two different games (act 1 & Kaycee’s mod being one game, and acts 2&3 being a different game).
- The first time you’re asked which god you wish to replace (start of act 2) it means something (affects which cards you start with for act 2), however the second time you’re asked (end of act 2) does not mean anything in the game, and it SHOULD.
- In act 2, you need to change up your deck a lot depending on the card battle, but you are only allowed to save one deck (the one you’re using right now). Most deckbuilder/tcg’s let you save different decks here, and not having access to this in Inscryption means losing a lot of time writing down on paper & recalling card names for your different preconstructed decks.
- Playstyle times are uneven. Act 2 mentions 4 gods and gives you access to all 4 play styles (sacrifices, bones, batteries, and gems) & their corresponding gods with ability to focus on 1 or more styles as you see fit, but… you get a full dedicated act in 3d with sacrifices/Leshy (Act 1) and a full dedicated act in 3d with batteries/P03 (Act 3) with tiny amounts of bones & gems in acts 1 & 3, and you only get a small amount of dedicated time in 3d with bones/Grimorra & gems/Magnificus during credits, making me really wish to get more time with them.
- Some of the Steam Achievements are too obscure, difficult, or require a ton of retries due to “game randomness” and should be rethought out, especially Devil’s Play for Act 2, Agonizing Remorse for Act 3, and Skull Storm for Kaycee’s Mod.

Suggestions for improvements:
- Multiple difficulty modifiers for main game to accommodate players at all skill levels (applies to acts 1-3): Casual, Regular, Hard, and Nightmare. Casual is for folks that never played a deckbuilder/tcg & means hard-saves (ability to try again when KO-ed) for each card-battle in acts 1-3 (effectively act 2 difficulty for all acts). Regular is for folks that played a deckbuilder/tcg but aren’t good at them (this includes myself & likely most people playing this game) & means hard-saves only at start of acts & on winning card-battles against bosses or reaching checkpoints (effectively act 3 difficulty for all acts). Hard is for folks good w deckbuilders/tcg and need a challenge (I know some folks in this category) & means hard-saves only at start of acts & losing a card battle means redoing the entire act (effectively act 1 difficulty for all acts). Nightmare means no hard-saves & losing a battle means redoing all acts (meant for when you get good at this game). Maybe also reward players in-game for trying a difficulty one step higher than the one they just finished, to encourage them to progressively get better at the game.
- Ability to “correct your deck” or items at a price. Ie places on the map to purchase desired cards or items or modify/chuck cards in a specific manner, making the in-game money mean something (especially for acts 1 & Kaycee’s mod), and reducing some of the bad luck of getting stuck w trashy cards in a playthru. Thinking “Slay the Spire” here.
- The second time you’re asked which god you want to replace (end of act 2) affects which god you go into 3d against for act 3 AND changes the ending of the game. This means the ability to get a full act with bones/Grimora or gems/Magnificus if you want, and improves main game replayability. Selecting sacrifice/Leshy for act 3 can give you something tougher than act 1 & akin to level 1 (10 challenge points) of Kaycee’s mod, which arguably is fair for most players and different enough from act 1 to mean something.
- Steam achievement for beating each god in act 2 using a different deck - one that does NOT contain their playstyle, forcing players to learn all the playstyles.
- Steam achievement for beating each god in act 2 using a deck focused on that same god’s playstyle. (You can call it “Taste of your own Medicine”)
- Similar to how sacrifice/Leshy in 3d has some bones, or how batteries/P03 in 3d has some gems, add in secondary playstyles to 3d for the other two gods. Maybe do batteries as the secondary for bones/Grimora in 3d and sacrifice for gems/Magnificus in 3d. This implies access to all playstyles as secondaries in addition to primaries.
- Similar to how Kaycee’s mod is a tougher variant of sacrifice/Leshy in 3d, maybe make postgame mods for the other gods in 3d with varying challenges/modifiers and levels too? Could be awesome to have a “P04 mod” similar to P03 act in 3d, but with tougher maps/bosses/bounty-hunters, for example.
