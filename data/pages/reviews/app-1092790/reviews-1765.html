\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best games I've played in the past few years. If you love card games, puzzles, unique, cryptic narratives, 4th wall breaks, and being constantly surprised, this is a must have. Although yes, at its core it is a card game, so if you react to names like magic the gathering, hearthstone or slay the spire like a conservative to a trans teen in a netflix series, maybe it's better to experience this game in some other way (though it's still worth to do it). However, maybe this one will speak to you, or at least all the other stuff that this game has to offer will compensate for the card play. I feel like people should be cautious of that. On the other hand I've read some opinions from people that stated they hate card games, and loved Inscryption, so take it with a grain of salt I guess. You'd have to believe in the creator (and yourself), and just try it out, it's not too expensive so even if you won't become a fan or simply dislike this game in general, it'd still be worth the experience (or maybe not, I don't know, I'm not your dad, it's your money :)).

To me, as a card games fan, it wasn't a problem at all, even though Inscryption is not a GREAT card game imo. It is worth noting though, that because of the nature of this game (it's very self-aware ;)), and things that are happening plot-wise (which I don't intend to even mention), all the things that are 'problematic' with a core (card) gameplay, were simply irrelevant to me and I just enjoyed the ride. However, to avoid sounding too dramatically, the same 'problematic' things I mentioned, do not have to be seen as such, and with proper attitude, you may indulge in all of them, and find your own way to make it work for you (you are given a lot of tools in the game to do so).

On a technical note, everything seems great, no crashes, good performance overall (even on my old PC without external gpu, had to play in lower res though :((.

Oh, and the audio-visual presentation is also amazing. Can't reveal too much, but if you like spooky ambient and unnerving soundscapes, low-poly/low-res 3D textures (also very sp00ky), and... some other things :) you'll be delighted.




---SPOILERS AHEAD---

If I can find one thing that I feel is somewhat negative, is that the act 3 slightly overstayed its welcome to me and felt the least fresh and innovative (and kinda like a chore since it had too many fights between bosses) cause it simply reiterated on the 2nd act (though yes, still with its very own set of quirks and what not, but I just can't shake off this feeling). It surely compensated with what it brought to the table story-wise, and the final section was worth probably every second of it.\t\t\t\t\t
