\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a very hit or miss game. On the one hand it is an incredibly fun card game with fun and engaging mechanics and a well written rival, but on the other, it is a poorly written and executed meta-narrative that completely destroys any immersion and just comes off like an early 2000's creepypasta.

The actual 3D portions of the game, Act 1 and to a lesser extent Act 3, are incredibly fun to play and incredibly immersive and strategic with wonderful characters to top off a wonderful portion of the game.

Where this all falls apart however, is the Meta-Narrative sub-plot. Luke is incredibly annoying and the game would've been a lot better if he wasn't there in the first place. Him butting in to say "Aw ♥♥♥♥." or "That card is OP!" did nothing but shatter my immersion into a million little pieces and annoy me. The only time this meta execution was during Act 3's bosses which managed to turn the fact that you were playing a game into interesting mechanics while also not shattering your immersion.

In conclusion, Inscryption is an incredibly fun card game that is dragged down by an annoying, poorly written, meta-narrative. 7.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
