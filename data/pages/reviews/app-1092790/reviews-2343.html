\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI started off loving it, but grew to dislike it more and more as I progressed from one act to another. My second playthrough had me constantly being handed runs where the opponent had insane card and stat leads that a guaranteed loss was stacked in two turns consistently, to the point that now I find no portion of the game enjoyable. Sure, card games do have some randomness to them, but having half a dozen runs in a row ended by unbelievably ridiculous starting stat-leads on turn two, and sometimes even turn one is just rubbish. There were multiple battles I got that were flat-out unwinnable by anything except the insanely unlikely chance of having zero-cost death-cards that had insane stats from previous runs ending up in my deck, and also in my hand from turn one. If it was an occasional thing I wouldn't mind, but almost always once per run is a special kind of garbage game balance. You can also end up with runs where the map opportunities you get are completely worthless, where totems you get don't work with any of the cards you have, or where you're stuck with forced chance-games that leave you wasting a tile to walk away with nothing because they were the only option that showed up on the map that got generated for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a very good game, one of the more memorable indies I've recently played. I infused Ouroboros the world eater with the power of bees. The mechanics and animations are snappy and the gameplay is smooth. The first time you play is a scripted tutorial, it's the same every time, including the cards you pick up - so expect to lose that one. Not going to say much else considering the nature of the game, go in blind, if you like 'card games' in general you will absolutely love this.\t\t\t\t\t
