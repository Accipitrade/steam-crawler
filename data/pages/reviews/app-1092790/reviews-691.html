\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is clearly made by someone who is intricately familiar with deck building games and who, like all do eventually, has grown to despise the community that propagates these games. It is an unflinchingly sarcastic take on what it is like to be drawn into this universe.

You, the player, are unwittingly trapped and forced into playing a card game by a shadowy loner who has clearly gone insane over their obsession with cards. When you start, you are thrust a pile of cards with nonsense writing and symbols and given little to no direction on how to play this obviously rigged game for the sadistic pleasure of your captor. As the game develops, it is made increasingly obvious that you are here to die, over and over again unless you are willing to go through excruciating pain; and are willing to sacrifice your sanity and torture yourself and every living thing around you until you eventually beat this ego-maniacal pub stomper at his own game. Thus becoming no better than your captor and potentially starting a horribly abusive cycle.

Despite the obvious hate for it's real world cousins, its a pretty sound card game with solid resource management and great progression. With some highly rewarding battles that keep you engaged through the inevitable death repetition while offering some unique death perks as an admittedly meager reward for your pitifully hopeless player character.

The one thing this really drives home, particularly in the endgame, is that all card games at their core are nothing but RNG slogs. You WILL be mercilessly bludgeoned over the head and beaten to your knees by this recurring theme, and you will thank him for it. Despite all the blustering about "synergy" "proper deck building" "strategies" "deck thinning" "card fetches" "deck piloting" and most importantly "price per card," it doesn't matter how high you rise in your respective card gathering community, you are nothing compared to the all powerful RNG-sus. With noone on your side other than the omnipresent king of financial ruin himself Loot-cifer waiting, so he can turn your hard earned money that you got with that time of your life will never get back so you can turn that pittance you call a pay-check into some form of serotonin or sense of pride so you can, at some point, get out of bed and face your day to day life.

Because lets, be honest where would we really be in life without our respective doses of card-board crack?\t\t\t\t\t
