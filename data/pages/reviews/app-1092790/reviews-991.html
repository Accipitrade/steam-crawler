\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will write a spoilerfree part and a spoilerfull part.
If you consider buying it I advise you to not read the spoiler part as it will cost you an experience that I for one wish I could experience again.

For the spoilerfree part.
The majority of the game is a roguelike-card game. You can give your cards sigils that have certain effects, like direct damage, blocking, double attack etc.
card come with a cost to be played so you have to follow a certain strategy.
If you like cardgames you will probably enjoy this one.
If you're like me and you do enjoy cardgames but usually don't buy cardgames since it's mainly fun with friends, don't let that stop you if you like puzzles. (I for one, love puzzles and this game has a few)
If you liked games like Undertale, Pony Island, Doki Doki Literature Club or Omori I can safely recommend this and not elaborate further.

A last adivse: DO NOT look this up. I would even go as far as say: don't watch the trailer but I am sure most of you already did.

Now for the not spoilerfree part:
This is the last chance to look away.

This game hit a nerve. It combined elements that I love such as hidden lore, secrets, puzzles and a compelling story that slowly unravels as you uncover more details.
Inscryption did exactly that.
I will split this review in all Acts and then in a general part.

Act 1:
In my opinion the core of the game and by far my favorite part. The atmosphere was amazing and after finishing the game it feels sad and nostalgic to look back to this. I remember being frustrated by all the cheats the entity (Leshy) used, but as my knowledge grew the rounds became much shorter until the only real obstacle for me was the Trapper.
The soundtrack here was amazing and more than enjoyable. Jonah Senzel captured the whole atmosphere to a t.
The cabin puzzles were fun however I wouldn't have been mad about a few more.
A negative point here is that I disliked the Lucky Carder comments. It kinda broke the feeling for me. Brought a certain distance between me and the game. The 4th wall breaks then felt less meaningful and more scripted, which of course they are. But I like the feeling of terror when a game plays with me.
(Psycho Mantis reading your memorycard style.)
Having the Lucky Carder as the player will continue to be my Number One negative point throughout this review however.

Act 2:
I saw bits in the trailer and I somewhat hoped I didn't. That way I wasn't too surprised to be in an 8-bit styled world.
Again I wished for a bit more puzzles here but that's just me loving the puzzles they had in the game so far. I would have also loved some more dialogue between the NPCs maybe but thats just nitpicking. Still this part was my least favorite. But I still loved it.

Act 3:
Same as Act 1 however I liked Act 1 a lot more. Again the Lucky Carder Webcam reveal made me a bit disappointed.
With all the datasecurity and all it would probably be a lot more diffcult to do things like writing a mail to the steam e-mail and acting out as gamefuna or something but I would have loved to be more involved instead of an unknown person.
Also the talking cars demanded to be sacrificed instead of spared but it made no difference.
The ending made me incredibly sad so that was very well done.

Now to the general part.
I would have loved to see ever Scrybes world like we did with P03 and Leshy. As in, you replace each Scrybe and get to their world and in the end P03 tries to do his masterplan because he needed that time to prepare or something.
I also love the cardgame and I would definetly buy a spin-off with just the cardgame and potential item unlocking through puzzles. I really really enjoyed that.

OVERALL:
It got my heart. And it is now one of my favorite games.
Thank you for that!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWords cannot describe how much I ADORE Inscryption.

I'm not much for card games, so I didn't pay as much attention to it initially when it came out.
But then - I encountered a video in my youtube feed of someone playing it. I literally had to force myself from not continuing the video when I realised that I NEED TO BUY THIS ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ GAME.

Inscryption is the perfect balance of failure, repeat & continue onward without making it feel cheap when you lose everything you gained thus far. Similarly to Hades, it progresses the story further and also strengthens you, making you feel as if dying is meant to be a part of the gameplay loop rather than an annoying hinder.

Admittedly I was initially so bad at the game that I never even got the "too fast, too soon" encounter that I know others have gotten, lol.

As for the acts, I would say act 1 is a clear winner in terms of how good it is. The mystery, the horror themes, the strategies behind each card you obtain and upgrade, and the choices you make along your path toward the cabin. The lore is also perfectly balanced to where it's not overbearingly in-your-face and unskippable, but also stays subtle and leaves you wondering what certain things actually mean.
Also the music. The ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ music. I'm in love.

Act 2 is the weakest out of the bunch for me - I treated it more as a trainng ground for my Ouroboros using my amazing undefeatable super-deck (feel free to copy):
1 ouroboros
3 black cats
7 squirrels
9 burrows

As for act 3, it really picks it up again. The eeriness returns, and leaves you with a feeling of that something bad is creeping closer constantly.

Inscryption is, to me, a 10/10 game.
One of the best games I have ever played - in any category.\t\t\t\t\t

The card system is great, and has some unique mechanics you might be familiar with in games like MTG, but others that are unique to it alone.

My only honest complaint about this game is the length. You'll get about 13-16 hours on it depending on how much of a of a 100% clear you want, and how long it takes you to do puzzles. I really enjoyed the game, and I wish there was some kind of play mode after you finish it, or that there was more to the story than the existing acts. Still it's hardly a complaint to want more of a good thing. Certainly worth picking up if you get the chance.\t\t\t\t\t
