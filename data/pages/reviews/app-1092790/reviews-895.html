\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter getting all of the achievements, I feel like I can now officially say, Inscryption is one of the greatest games of all time.
The gameplay is incredibly deep, but still does a great job of teaching new players and is easy to understand (That doesn't mean it's an easy game though!)
The art is phenomenal, and every card is easily distinguishable, none of them looking too similar.
And the story, OH the story! Surprisingly enough, this little card game has one of the most interesting stories I have ever experienced. So interesting, in fact, that it had an ARG! Solved now of course, but you can still solve it yourself if you don't want spoilers.

So, would I recommend Inscryption? Definitely! I honestly can't say there's a single part I disliked. The closest I came was struggling with rng to get a couple achievements, but that didn't last very long at all.

Anyway, I hope this review helped, I'm off to start my Kaycee's Mod journey!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInteresting and takes a lot of risks. Most of these risks pay off to create an engaging and addictive experience.

However, if you are looking for a worthy successor to Slay The Spire and other great deck building games, this is not it. If I was being harsh I would say this game puts style over substance, which would disappoint me more if the style weren't so cool. I just wish each section of the game had a little bit more depth, and was more willing to explore the mechanics that it introduces.

Interesting piece of story telling, however I feel it sort of wastes it's potential as a great card game. Maybe Kaycee's mod will fulfill that itch.
