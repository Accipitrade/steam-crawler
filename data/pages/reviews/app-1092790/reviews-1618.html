\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start by saying this, after having beaten the game. I played it blind, and you should too. Don't read this review, just buy the game now.

That said, the game is very fun. I beat it after 12 hours with some cheese in all 3 of it's parts, which I would say is easily it's strongest suit-- it's cheese. I got through the second part, most memorably, with a very powerful 7/7 creature that decimated people in one hit. I got lucky with my wolf and got two grizzlies in the second half, as well as getting two boons at the last portion. I did amazingly because of the snipers and the orobouros I got in the third segment. But I didn't just get these and win, I felt like I'd EARNED them. I felt like I had actually gone through the effort to get the tools I needed to succeed, and I had gotten lucky enough to be able to do it.

My favorite part is easily the second segment, and it's the only one where I wasn't able to do all the puzzles. I like how it has a mix of not making you have too many cards but also giving you a sort of randomized feel. I don't know why, but the third part didn't feel very random at all because of all of the customization I could do in it. I'd say it's probably the weakest part of the game (not to say I didn't like it) as the middle and first parts just shadow it in fun. And that's the main reason I picked up this game, to be honest.

Now, why I kept playing was the story. It enthralled me that this strange man kept killing me over and over, making cards from my dead body. I loved it, I was absolutely stunned every time I died. I love stories in games and this did not dissapoint-- it was fun, it was interesting and it kept me wondering what would happen next. It feels a bit like a creepypasta in execution, but I still loved it all the same-- it was a blast to get through. I especially loved all the acting, I was incredibly immersed. My only qualm is I feel like it ended a bit quickly.

I will say that I think that it could be a bit better in some areas. I don't like how they ACTUALLY delete your files without warning, but I didn't mind when they did warn you. I liked how new and inventive it felt, but some things went unexplained and when I tried to click on the symbols for information I found myself accidentally taking the card or deleting the card. It was somewhat frustrating and could be solved with a hover system. I love learning things on my own, but I think in a roguelike you want a good grasp of what you're getting.

All in all, I totally reccomend this game despite some of it's issues. It was very fun and I enjoyed it the whole way through. I hope this game gets really popular, because it deserves it.\t\t\t\t\t
