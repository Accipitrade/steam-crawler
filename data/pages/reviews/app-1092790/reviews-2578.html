\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this game is incredible, and I would recommend it with a caveat. I'll start out with the caveat then get to the positives-

This game is story driven, experiential, and experimental at its core, using a deck building/card battler game as the conduit. This is from the developer of Pony Island, so if you go in expecting the "whole game" to be what is in the screenshots on steam and that's what you want, you will probably be disappointed (even though the parts in the screenshots are fantastic and fun to play). Without spoiling anything, the game changes in significant ways throughout the course of playing the game. Probably the most important parts of the trailer to tell you about the game are the scenes that don't have anything to do with the "scary cabin card game" aesthetic. Some spoilers for after the first 2-3 hours to provide more context: after about 3ish hours, you will probably "beat" the first part of the game. After this point, the game changes drastically when you start a "New Game" and it no longer uses the "scary cabin card game" setting .

That being said, if all you're looking for is a horror themed card battler, this is probably not the game for you.

Now onto the positives-

- The game opens to an old interface by making you "Continue" rather than "New Game" and instantly makes your mind run wild with questions. This is a small thing but it really got my mind running and I think it's cool.
- The game is continually evolving throughout the game. You will play and get comfortable with something only to have the game pull the rug out from under you and force you to rethink your strategies.
- The story is cryptic, weird, interesting, and presented in an incredible manner.
- The game is rife with secrets, puzzles, and has (as of right now) an entire ARG being pursued by the community of fans.
- The game really does some fun and unique things with gameplay and interactions that I've really never seen before in a game. Things like (light boss mechanic spoilers): getting your steam friends and their profile pictures to use as enemy cards, having you select a file on your computer to use as a card and threatening to delete it (it doesn't actually do this) if the card is defeated, etc.
- The length of the game to beat it is probably 10-12 hours, with a ton of secrets (many of which feed into the ARG) to go pack and discover.

Overall, this game is up there as one of the better games I've played this year and has certainly made an impact.\t\t\t\t\t
