Think a bit before you buy

I'm so torn with Inscryption, but I feel like I want to write a review as a warning amongst the overwhelmingly positive status on steam, unfortunately that means spoilers which sucks!

Minor Spoilers will be provided from here-on out to make my points-

What you see on the steam page isn't the entire game, it's not all this dark deckbuilder card game that the marketing makes it out to be, even if the developer has made stranger titles in the past I'm very conflicted on this.

You can play for hours in the first part, but once you get through it you'll be met with something different, perhaps not as good or polished but you can't refund it since you've played too much, even though its purposely trying to be something different and messes with you I find it misleading. If the rest of the game was as good as the first part I wouldnt say this but it is unfortunately not.

Bigger Spoilers from Here

The Cabin section is by far the best part in my eyes, the most polished, it has the strongest aesthetic and the suspense and mystery it builds is amazing. Most importantly even though it can be very RNG heavy and unfair it's fun! The core game is fun to play, fun to look at, fun to learn the mystery and fun to beat.

The interlude with Mr Carder was interesting! I love card games and I watch some TCG youtube so it was a niche thing but it's my niche. Going into Act 2 it was fun, I liked the aesthetic and the changes, even though they weren't as strong I appreciated them and they were cute. The gameplay here takes a hit by changing and opening up, I was never really pushed to change my strategy however so I feel like it could have been handled better. The twist here at the end wasn't that great but the Carder storyline kept me wanting to progress.

Act 3 is where it falls apart, initially interesting but then you realise ... it's the same game again, but worse, less freedom, the aesthetic is the same but toned down and worse, theres no tension, the escape room is bland. The "Dark Souls" money pick up mechanic wastes your time compared to how Act 1 seemingly gave you hints as you failed. Even the puzzles are annoying and set you back to the first part of a 3 part puzzle, even though you've completed it, it wastes your time.

That's the biggest thing, is it wastes your time by being the same game but worse. Undoubtedly it's trying to say something by doing this, The Stoat or P03, whoever, is having his try but it's the same old thing but worse, maybe it's making fun of the people who say "I could do that better". I appreciate trying something like this, but it's just not fun to me, it's frustrating and disappointing, I feel like a lot of people will criticise others for saying they wanted more Act 1 ... but honestly I really dont blame them because it just gets a lot worse, and i'm sure it feels even worse since we've seen it done better within this same game.

On a final note I managed to decode the binary earlier in the game and found the Karnofel Code, leading me to discovering the ARG stuff, which seems really fun, but ultimately something I feel i've missed and tainted by playing more of the game.

Overall I had fun for a while but I've stopped not wanting to finish, which is such an odd experience, but this game just frustrates me when playing now. If you're deadset on experiencing this then go ahead and try it! You might absolutely love it! If you like card games you'll get something out of it, but if your looking for the next Slay the Spire, Dicey Dungeons or deckbuilder I would avoid, because it's not that.\t\t\t\t\t
