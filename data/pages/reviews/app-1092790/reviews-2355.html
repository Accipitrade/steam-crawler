\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will try to avoid spoilers as much as possible, though some issues can't be discussed without some. Do not continue reading if you care.

Inscryption is a very fun and interesting game that brings a lot to the table (mainly cards). Act 1 was wholly enjoyable as a mix of rogue-lite card battler and escape room. Unfortunately the game decides to flip the table for Act 2 and then again for Act 3. I didn't care for the overarching story at all since I felt it was poorly implemented/tied in.

If you expect to improve your deck in a Slay the Spire manner, go elsewhere. The amount of control you have over your deck is nowhere close and you never know when the game decides to wipe your progress to change into a completely different game.

If they had simply extended Act 1 with 2-3 more bosses and given more control over deck design, I could recommend this game but as it stands it is not worth the price (17€ at time of purchase) I completed everything in under 10 hours, including secret boss and the few extra objectives. There is no reason to replay.\t\t\t\t\t

TLDR: I would absolutely recommend this game to anyone who enjoys card games or “haunted disk” style games.

Passing mention of what lies beyond act 1 ahead, proceed at your own risk

It is rare for a game to just be a game. Especially when that game is made by a person and not a company. Inscryption is certainly about something. Although I am not entirely sure I can say with any certainty what that something is. As is the case with any “haunted disk” style games.

From a game play perspective each segment of Inscryption plays out like a well thought out collectible card game with puzzle elements. And while personally I would love a “non-haunted disk” variety game in each of the styles of the scribes it isn’t necessary. Fun and compelling card mechanics, interesting but not overly complex puzzles and a spooky atmosphere all make for an experience that I will not soon be forgetting .

It is rare that I find a game that is so completely itself and so nearly perfect and true to that self.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm breaking this review up into the 3 Acts the game has (that I currently know of). So if you don't want minor spoilers tread carefully.

TLDR - Act 1 good, rest is iffy.

Act 1 is incredible, sadly once it's over the game kind of, drops off in quality. If Act 1 was the entire game (made longer of course) I would love this game a lot more. 10/10

Act 2 is... not great. It mainly serves as a way of introducing you to new mechanics that will be in Act 3, I'm also pretty sure it exists to pad out the game. To each their own... but I absolutely hated it. 2/10

Act 3 is passable, I especially liked the continual improvement of the cards, but no where near as good as Act 1, Act 3's length and deck building focus should have been given to Act 1. The other 2 character's styles were interesting, personally I'd have liked to play a bit more of the Third Character's game, not the Fourth that one sucked and absolutely was just put in to pad out the timer. 6/10

All in all I'd have bought this game if it was just Act 1 as it is now... thats how good Act 1 is.
When can I buy the physical card game? Please tell me I can have the physical card game! I will build the physical card game if I must, I REQUIRE IT!

-SPOILERS BELOW-
I really don't like the whole, "self aware video game" ♥♥♥♥. Doki Doki did it well but I feel like it was entirely unnecessary in this game, I really didn't give a ♥♥♥♥ about the overarching story if it didn't happen inside the Cabin. Every time the screen turned blue I audibly groaned in annoyance. Not only was the acting... not great, but I just found the plot really boring. Every time the guy spoke up and broke the 4th wall the only thought in my mind was, "shut up". Leshy is the game, take him out and you remove the charm, he's the DM, he's the heart and soul. His presence adds tension and mystery. P03 is a smug prick, I was counting down the seconds till I could be free of him. Grimora is barely memorable. And the old man doesn't have much of a character at all, so much so that I don't even remember his ♥♥♥♥♥♥ name. That part in the ending with Leshy I will admit was quite nice, just slapping away at each other for the fun of it, probably should've saved him to be the last to get deleted cause once he was gone... I checked out.\t\t\t\t\t
