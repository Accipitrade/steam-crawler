\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start by saying this,
first I won't spoil any of the game tricks (aside that it has acts)
Second I wasn't really that much exposed to other games from Daniel Mullins.
This was my first full game from him, and first card game that I legitimately completed.
But this game absolutely rocks.

Its Playable as hell where people absolutely adore, act one where most people show the game off
And call it the best part of game.
Its so, so far from truth.
Each act adds up to a absolutely unique card game experience where you don't need to focus so seriously on what is best way to win because you will eventually get there slowly step by step.
And each act is unique in its narrative.

But I digress,
The games gameplay is simple,
The atmosphere is tense and dark, the game does not pull any stops,
It gives you the feeling of genuine fight for your own life.
The dark figure DM it for you, because I can't call it differently does it so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ well you will feel engaged into actually caring for what happens, and what you will choose to do.

For me there isn't any but one complains, (which is related to the story so won't talk about it too much).
To be frank, There are few things missing I wish I could do in inscryption but due to story reasons it isn't really possible.

But whole experience is perfectly crafted,
Other games of Mullins are also made astoundingly, although they sadly didn't get as much explosion as Inscryption, so go and check them out. Pony Island and The Hex.

And if this review gets you to play it, all I can say.
Have a good draw and remember
Sacrifices. Must. Be. Made

[Also Mullins, if you see this
For the love of god please consider making DLC for this
Just for the gameplay.]\t\t\t\t\t
