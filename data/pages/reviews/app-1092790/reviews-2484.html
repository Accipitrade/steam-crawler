\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI expected no less from the Pony Island devs, honestly this exceeded my expectations by a lot. The story is incredible, gameplay is great, can sometimes get a little repetitive in Act 3 when you get stuck in specific spots, best thing to do there is go back and buy a card pack, helps more than you think.
I couldn't recommend this more honestly, if you think you'd like this game, I highly, very highly recommended you get it. Make sure to look around, explore, try things.

Big Spoilers and story talk start here

Act 1 was amazing honestly, great intro to the game, a way of teaching the game without seeming like pandering, it fits the story perfectly, its not just some gimmick stuff so it works. The ability to get up, walk around and move and incredible too, interacting with things, finding secrets that help you along your way, and of course, Stoat and the other talking cards. The talking cards are a nice little touch that help continue the story and keep pushing you to beat the game, helps give some freshness and keep the game play from feeling stale, it creates a sense of wonder, encouragement to the player to beat the final boss to finish this.

Act 2. I'm unsure how I feel about Act 2 if I'm honest. I don't hate act 2, but it felt a bit lack luster compared to Act 1. I mainly didn't like how you had to switch to magic gems for the tower boss, but thats a me thing so, ignoring that. Act 2 was alright, I liked all the gimmicks the bosses had, energy was a fun introduction. It was a pretty good transition from Act 1 to Act 2. Act 1 taught you the basics, Act 2 expanded on that and lore mainly, you learn so much story from Act 1, it really makes you want more, to find out more.

Act3, Act3 my beloved. I absolutely loved Act3, The music, Gameplay, Style, its all I could want and more honestly. This Act, like the others gives more reason to play, more questions, and as you keep going you get closer to finding out these answers. I also really liked seeing a place in 2d first then seeing you in it in 3d. It was really cool to explore around. I do wish Act 3 played out more like Act 1, but I understand Act 3 was also a big story part, I'm not complaining because I love it either way. The fun new little gimmicks too are nice, bounty system, overclocking, etc. New cards, new features, pretty cool honestly.

Act..4? The final act I guess you could say, where everything is being deleted. I loved seeing they went through all the effort to make different fights with each boss, it makes me wish we could, like a skin for Act 1 (I'll get into that after this). It was heartwarming and saddening, I loved it so dearly. An amazing ending for an amazing game. I also can't forget to mention the "Old_Data". Personally Args are out of my kind of league, I dropped em a while back but its still great to see them being done, I know people still love to do them and its great.

Wishes and wants
(more spoilers ofc)
I'd love for there to be a sort of, balanced Act 1 infinite mode, you can replay as much as you want, maybe add a few new encounters and such, no op extra help cards, or death cards so you can't win on your third playthrough lol. I just think It'd be a nice addition ♥♥♥♥ Act 1 alone has so much potential alone. I'd also love to see Act 3 have something like Act 1, as well as infinite, though bringing the mechanics from Act 3 over to Act 1 would probably bring some problems with balancing etc. Another idea would be to maybe only just keep Act 1, infinite not death cards etc like I mentioned, but you can change who the person your fighting is. The battle mechanics and styles were already made, it'd be a shame to let them goto waste if I'm honest.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't finished the game, but I'm slogging through the end.

I enjoyed it for a while. It's easy to make splashy combos, and each rung on the metaprogression ladder is a big boost. But once I grasped its core mechanics and boss patterns, it became solvable. This is odd for a deckbuilding game; their usual mark of quality is a high skill-ceiling discovered over dozens of runs.

But it's not only a card game; it's a Daniel Mullins game! So, it's got some creepy ARG game-within-a-game stuff. As the game continues and reveals more of its story, after the novelty of card battles and room puzzles wear thin, the game cleverly pivots to radically different gameplay with a purer narrative focus... Oh, just kidding! You more and more frequently tackle card battles and reused puzzles. Oof!

So, what about the narrative? Well, as the game shifts focus from its haunting atmosphere to story specifics, it goes from delightful mood piece to conspicuously manufactured creepypasta. All the characters and larger concepts fit into archetypes you'll be familiar with from involuntary brush ups with internet horror. There are few central mysteries to unravel. It's not deeply thematic, it's not a watertight thriller, and it leaves little to the imagination.

Between my unsuspended disbelief and the card combat grind, I sense a disinterest in Inscryption's cards beyond the trendy narrative hook. And as a narrative, it feels interchangeable with any number of post-Pony Island streamerbait games (and yes, I know it's from the same guy). You'll enjoy Inscryption the less familiar you are with deckbuilders, collectible card games, and contemporary horror. From me, that's a condemnation.

If you're hungry for card games with narrative aims, you could drop fat stacks on Arkham Horror: The Card Game or try Griftlands, a campaign-centered deckbuilder here on Steam or Switch.

The following complaints contain spoilers you could glean from trailers, but to play it safe: Within the fiction of the game, there is a two-player competitive card game named Inscryption released in the 90s trading card game boom. The game you're playing is heavily based on it. But this game wouldn't function in tabletop; computer opponents telegraph their plays via Slay the Spire-style action queues the player doesn't use. You are infrequently prompted to view found footage of a card game YouTuber, and it just... feels inauthentic. I can't think of a single time I saw someone crack packs without a hand-cam or with a green screen. It's conspicuously more in-line with found footage media than practical for a card game YouTuber.\t\t\t\t\t
