\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter completing my first play through of the game, I cannot recommend this game enough. The style and feel you get from the different encounters, the different mechanics introduced with each boss, everything just feels natural and works well. This is easily one of the best games I have played in a while and I genuinely believe that Inscryption will be at the top of my list for my favourite games for a long time. It is highly recommended to play through the game blind, but even if you don't its still a lot of fun.

SPOILERS BELOW
The only thing that could make this experience better would be an arcade mode to just freely do encounters, I would spend countless hours doing that. As well as an option to choose your dealer, or a mode to go through all the dealers in the 3D world. The art style of Grimora's Bone Board was probably my favorite, but all 4 of the scrybes had wonderful enviroments. Nothing could have prepared me for the magical yu-gi-oh battle at the end, but man I would pay just to play regular encounters with that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really liked this game. I liked the narrative and the gameplay. However, since the narrative can be experienced by watching someone else play it, I'd have to REALLY like the gameplay, right? And I kinda do, BUT, the gameplay is chained to the narrative and to be honest, the narrative gets in the way.

Let me explain. You get to play the card game a few times throughout the narrative. But in the different parts, you start from square one each time. Then you play the card game until you win, and then you're just... done... with that narrative section. So you lose everything you built up towards. There is only one section of the game with actual deck building, which is what I really love (Pokemon Trading Card Game for GameBoy is 10/10 for me).

All of that process (start with a crappy deck, build a good one, win, start over) makes sense in the narrative and is done well within the narrative. But it absolutely kills replayability. I just want to have like a free play mode or boss rush mode or challenge mode with deckbuilding, so I can continue to enjoy the gameplay aspect of the game without being chained up to the narrative I've already seen again. I get that that's not what the game is supposed to be. At times it even critiques this exact attitude. So in that sense, this game is very much successful at what it is supposed to do. But me personally, I don't recommend games you can't replay at least a couple times. And this game was designed to be experienced once.

- - -

Edit a year later
Switching to recommended with the addition of the Kaycee's Mod update, which allows for more replayability and roguelike deckbuilding.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game opens up with a deranged old man forcing you to play a strange, dark board games seemingly of his own invention and he will not let you leave his home until you do so under threats of violence. A delightful reminder of what my Game Night's used to be like before the pandemic.

Inscryption is a well thought out, engaging, multi-level game with a lot of complex ideas and secrets that are clearly hinted at but not easily solved. The core gameplay is slowly built upon as progress is made, slowly evolving into a deceptively complex beast with lots of interesting options for players to engage with. The story is a little cheesy at times, but it's compelling enough that you want to uncover more of it as you go. If anything it feels like every aspect of it could have been expanded out even further so that you could enjoy each of its offerings a little longer. The difficulty spikes a little from time to time, but either some luck, changing strategy, or pursing other objectives to build strength are usually sufficient to overcome them without too much trouble.

Highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
