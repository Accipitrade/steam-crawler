\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are familiar with Mullins' work I would definitely pick this game up; he has again mashed together several genres of video games in an intriguing way just like his previous works. The story again completely demolishes the 4th wall not only by reaching out to you, the player, but by creating an entire world outside of the game itself. The game currently has an ARG going on and I think the conclusion of the ARG might add more content, but we'll have to find out.

Now, if you're not familiar with Mullins' style or previous works: know that the focus of his works usually seem to be focused on the blending of genres rather than any specific genre itself. The deck-building, roguelike and escape room genres are there, yes, but none of those areas are perfect or revolutionary in any way. I will say that I did really like a lot of the card mechanics that were implemented and enjoyed making different decks focused on the different mechanics. If you're in it for the just for the cards, you might actually find some unique and interesting additions to the genre. Just know that the main focus in the game isn't just the deck-building so you might end up disappointed with the progression later on.

TL;DR - Mullins' makes genre blending games with 4th wall narrative breaks. You might be disappointed with the progression of the game because it doesn't entirely focus on the cards.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a deckbuilding card game with some impressive atmosphere. You get to play cards for your life, but you can also just get up from the table whenever and try to solve some puzzles (sort of like an escape room). It is a game that I feel benefits from knowing as little as possible going into it, and I don't plan on spoiling anything significant here.

There are some rogue-lite elements, but I would not compare this to something like Slay the Spire. It has a story with mysteries to solve and an ending. Think you can escape the cabin and learn what's going on?

First off, I would recommend going in blind if you're into a game that will surprise you. If you need the essential review, it's an entertaining enough card game with a lot of good scares and twists in there. The gameplay isn't immaculate but if you're down to put in a little brainpower to deck-build and some outside-the-box thinking into the escape room part, you'll have a good time.

But I have to give credit where due, this is one of the games that followed me for a while even when I wasn't playing it. After beating it for the first time, it feels as if there's tons of secrets in here, so much so that while the game was very fun, I don't feel like I got a complete story. I'm interested in seeing what the community digs up in terms of hidden content, easter eggs, and whatever else the devs kept well enough hidden from us that they knew we weren't gonna catch it the first time... if it's in there. If it's not... it's a somewhat hollow, if spooky, story.

Not a perfect game, but a solid 4/5 after my first run, which took about 11 hours. Still an easy indie GOTY contender. I'm not too much into card games myself but I still had a lot of fun.\t\t\t\t\t
