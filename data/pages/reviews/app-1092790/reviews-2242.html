\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood video game is good. It is also a video game, unlike those board games from your past. You can interact with it via a mouse and keyboard, but not other household items such as your tooth brush or car. I like video games. In fact, there was a time in my life where I wanted a Playstation 2 video game console so bad, that I sold my bike at a garage sale to get one. This ultimately ended up being a mistake, due to the fact that I was still 12 years old, and could not yet drive a car. And when I did eventually end up getting a car, it didn't run very well at all... In fact, my bike was actually far more reliable. I regretted that decision... I do not regret my decision to buy this game. I've already spent 16 hours with it, and I only bought it 3 days ago. I have a wife and a full time job, so usually I wouldn't spend so much time with a game unless I really enjoyed it. Especially around the Christmas season. Work is busy. Whenever I'm not at work, I'm thinking about it. It's a retail management position and it requires a lot of energy and planning on my part. I started taking melatonin to help me sleep when I'm thinking too much about work, but it's losing it's effectiveness. It mostly just makes me wake up feeling more drowsy than I would if I had actually stayed up all night playing Inscryption. I tried to balance it out with coffee, but coffee is starting to trigger panic attacks for me, so I can't drink it as much as I used to. The anxiety it makes me feel could be comparable to the anxiety the card battles in this game make me feel, only the game's anxiety is good. This game also doesn't let me use a gamecube controller instead of a keyboard. So even though I do recommend that you play this game, it's with a rather huge grain of salt. The Gamecube also had a keyboard attachment for the controller, despite having very few online games. Infact, Mario Kart Double Dash is the only one that I can readily recall off the top of my head. Inscryption is a better game than Mario Kart Double Dash. Mario Kart Double Dash is a bad game. It felt slower and clunkier than Mario Kart 64, and it didn't feel like it controlled as well either. I also don't feel like having two characters on the same kart really added anything to the overall experience. Perhaps Nintendo felt the same way, and that's why they never revisted the idea in future instalments. Mario Kart Double Dash has a Metacritic score of 8.7, Inscryption has a Metacritic score of 8.4. I don't know why that is. Don't these reviewers realize how much better Inscryption is than Mario Kart Double Dash? I'm losing focus... My dog won't stop barking... It just gets louder and LOUDER. I keep telling him to stop, and he looks at me as if he understands my request, but then he just starts barking again. Either he doesn't actually understand me, or he just doesn't care. I don't care that Mario Kart Double Dash received more favourable reviews than this, but I do want to understand why. Much like my dog, the question gets louder and louder. I shouldn't start so many sentences with insubordinate clauses. Please forgive me. I'd give this game a nine out of ten, but I'd give Mario Kart Double Dash a five.\t\t\t\t\t
