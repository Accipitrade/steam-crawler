The first time I wish there was a 'middle' option for recommendations. Inscryption has stellar gameplay and oozes atmosphere, but is held back by a rather sudden and abrupt ending when things feel like they should be kicking off - leaving one with nothing but disappointment.

Inscryption is a deck building roguelite puzzler which oozes atmosphere. While Steam has the tag of psychological horror attached to this, the game more-so just has a spooky theme than anything remotely resembling psychological horror going on. A lot of time went into the aesthetics and world of this title; which makes it such a shame that it ends so suddenly. Something I found out after the fact is that there is apparently an ARG going on - and completed by the community already - but there is no indication in-game that such a thing is taking place.

Mechanically, the game will be very familiar to those with experience in other deck builders. A resource must be paid in order to play other cards; an example in this case would be playing a 'free' Squirrel card and in order to play a card that costs one 'blood' resource, that Squirrel must be sacrificed. The higher the blood cost, the more creatures on the board you must sacrifice in order to play that card. There is a scale which counts points of direct damage to the player/opponent and your goal is to overpower your opponent on the scales. Support items exist that can alter the game, remove cards and adjust the scales.

At periods of time you will be permitted to leave the table and explore the room you are in, solving light puzzles that can be easily brute forced. These puzzles will give you more cards for your deck, support items and help move the narrative along - often time these can lead to lore and world building elements as well.

What things Inscryption does well should certainly be lauded, yet its flaws ultimately drag down the entire experience.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter completing my achievement-hunt for Inscryption, it's time for me to leave a review for this rather one-of-a-kind card-based adventure.

Guess I'm back to achievement-hunting with the new Kaycee's Mod update. This review will probably be updated after I've played a good amount of the game mode. With that said, the review below is based on the main game mode.



Review Summary (TL;DR):

9/10 - I was hooked from the very start, wish there was an endless mode so that we can explore the levels and lore even more with Grimora and Magnificus. Having gone into the game with zero expectations and prior knowledge of what the gameplay is like, Inscryption feels like a short and sweet love letter to trading card games, with the icing of deckbuilding, roguelike, horror and meta elements on top of it. For anyone who is interested in getting this game, I'd suggest to stay open-minded for what it will throw at you.


Pros:
  • Unique Storytelling
    I sadly have not played any of Daniel's previous games, but Inscryption stands out in the sea of video games (in general) with its incredible and unique plot. Each Act surprised me at every turn and you can't really expect where it's headed, which is a good thing, considering most recent games are rather predictable in their campaigns. I'll definitely remember this game for the years to come, and I just wish there was more.

  • Diverse Styles/Environments
    Inscryption offers its Acts in different styles, moods and environments. While those changes may seem too rapid and inconsistent for some people's liking, I quite enjoyed the variety Daniel has laid out for this experience. I can't really say too much beyond that without spoiling the core beauty of Inscryption.


Cons:
  • A Stray from the Pack
    If you're looking for a classic card battle game, this ain't it chief. With how much of a novelty Inscryption is, it does not retain the gameplay elements and modes you would expect of your usual card/deckbuilding game. As mentioned before, this game really does stand out, both in a good way (Unique and Memorable) and bad way (Inconsistent and Novelty Design).

  • Replayability
    After finishing your first playthrough, you would probably want to have another go at the game again. While Inscryption does allow you to replay its Acts, it is not as enjoyable as the first run, nor does it offer a new and good repeatable experience as the game's emphasis is on its narrative, not its replayability.




All in all, I hope you do have a great time experiencing this game like I have.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to give this game a Thumbs Up but i couldnt...
The Demo really made me buy this game and then i happily redid the first part.
You can summarize the Game, without Spoilers, into kinda 4 Acts.
Act1 is more of the Demo. Act2 though is something totally different and i hated it.
If i would have know how much the Gaming Experience will change from Act1 and what
i experienced in the Demo i wouldnt have bought it. Refund didnt work ofc bc more then 2
Hours have already passed until Act2. Thats the reason why i give this Game a Negative Review.

What i got promised in the Demo and Act1 is not there anymore in Act2 and i hated all of it even the Battles.
Graphics, Puzzles(God! I hate the Puzzles! They are so bad and illogical and not Fun!!!) and always this Dread of
not getting explanations for In game things happening.

There is really cool Game Mechanics and a Fusion of different ideas from different Card and Boardgames but as a "real"
Campaign and with more Focus and Emphasize on the Mechanics i would have enjoyed this more. The Story about the "Main Guy" is ok, but nothing different from Blairwitch Project. Everything seems foreseeable and it doesnt have any real impact on the storyline. Alot of Gameplay Elements seem like Easter Eggs and alot of Game Mechanics disappear almost instantly and i always felt like "Oh! This is cool! Oh... its gone already? What a shame..."

The whole game feels like a tease and then becomes a cockblock. I really liked Act1 and Act3 but Act2 was Horror for me and Act4 felt so convoluted and rushed and left me with disappointment.

tl;dr : Really liked the Game Mechanics but the Meta Storyline and Game Elements(especially the obnoxious Puzzles) make this a Negative Gaming Experience. For 10 Euro i wouldnt have complained but for 20 Euro and 12 Hours of Gaming (mostly stretched from Travelling back and forth, loosing my mind from frustrating Puzzles and retrying certain Battles because of RNG or unfair Setups) i felt cheated in the End bc the Main Card Game Mechanics are reduced by the story, Puzzles and "Travelling".\t\t\t\t\t
