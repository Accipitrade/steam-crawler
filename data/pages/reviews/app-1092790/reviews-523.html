\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game, where all of the positive stuff you've heard about it is completely true. While Daniel Mullins's prior games have had pretty good stories, they've always just felt a little lacking in the gameplay department. This is an issue that Inscryption (for the most part) doesn't have. The card gameplay is super solid and highly addicting, to the point where Mullins even released an expansion to flesh it out more. Beyond the stellar gameplay, the visuals and audio are super well done. The art is very appealing to the eye, and the sound design is incredibly atmospheric and satisfying (on top of all of the songs being bops). While the rest of the elements of the game are really good, the story steals the cake in terms of best aspect. Plenty of great twists and jaw-dropping sequences that really get you invested in what's going on, and the way that it simultaneously ties in with Pony Island and The Hex while not needing you to have knowledge of them to understand what's going on is super well done. There are no details that don't tie back in with something else, and you'll always catch something you didn't before, making the game pretty replayable. This game is another great addition to Daniel Mullins's very solid roster of games, and is totally worth getting and playing.\t\t\t\t\t
