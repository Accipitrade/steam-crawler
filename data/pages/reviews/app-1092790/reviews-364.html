\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is easily one of my favorite games. I bought it last year on the strength of an intriguing premise and interesting art style, but I wasn't expecting it to be as deep and impactful as it proved to be. The game is a roguelike deck-builder with a meta-narrative. Like other Dan Mullins games, it interrogates narrative tropes of video games by deconstructing itself and closing the gap between game and player.

The acting (Kevin Saxby, aka the Lucky Carder, who plays regularly via Steam broadcast), art direction, and music are all excellent and really add to the creepy atmosphere. I don't want to get into the story, as it's best enjoyed when you go in completely blind. Don't read any guides. Just lose yourself in the madness of the OLD_DATA.

10/10\t\t\t\t\t
