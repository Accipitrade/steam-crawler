\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a story driven game first and foremost, with gameplay elements of rougelite and card game implemented. Either way, I enjoyed this game thoroughly. The characters, art, and music are all my favorite parts of this game. I won't give too much away, but the art does change drastically when you get to the mid-game. Don't let it drive you away though. I still found the game charming.

Edit 1: I will say, a valid complaint some people had was that it wasn't advertised as well as it could've been. Fortunately, I had the knowledge it was a Daniel Mullins game and looked at a few reviews to get a better idea of what it is. For people who want a purely deck-builder, with high replay value and not the story fluff, it's definitely not for you.

One major complaint I will add: I WISH they had more content for certain sections of the game, mainly towards the end. You know what I mean, if you've played this already.

Inscryption is definitely not a perfect game. It's a good game with those few glaring flaws. If those flaws were polished, this would've easily been a game of the year for me. I'm still giving it a thumbs up because the positives outweigh the negatives.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was promised a spooky roguelike card game, but the game was something different for 2/3 parts of the playthrough. This lead to me being superbly disappointed.

That said I will still recommend the game because it's exceptionally well made, especially the first part. The first part oozes style and creativity, and it managed to hook me in ways I could have never dreamed of.

The latter part of the game changes drastically in the roguelike and atmosphere part. In the later parts first I felt overwhelmed, then it became tedious. It no longer was the concise experience I was promised so playing it felt a chore. Also the last part wasn't very challenging. The presentation was top notch though.

To me the ending was disappointing, and falls into the category "I'm 14 and this is deep". The acting left something more to be desired as well.

All in all I think this game is worth playing just for the first half alone. It's a masterclass in game design and gives you a thrilling roguelike card battler with some unique tricks up its sleeve.\t\t\t\t\t
