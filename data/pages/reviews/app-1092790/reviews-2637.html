\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe biggest highlight I think are the beautiful and evocative visuals. Everything about them is just incredible. Soundtrack and sound design were also pretty great.

The card play is pretty intricate and mixes well with fun in-game narrative. What it introduces throughout works surprisingly well. I found myself drawn to the game again and again thanks to the card and progression system. However, it could get a bit frustrating and/or swingy at times. Perhaps not enough choice to make it deeply satisfying, not only in terms of playing but also deckbuilding. There were times were I couldn't do anything against my opponent, and others were it didn't matter what I played.

The puzzle solving was fine. Definitely think some puzzles lacked clarity, as there was a lot of guessing on my part due to not understanding its core mechanics. Some are good, some are too point-click-adventure-y.

I also think the overarching narrative wasn't amazing. My main gripe was its side-plot and ending. Felt too divorced and underdeveloped. However, I did very much enjoy everything else. Most of its characters and settings are pretty fun.

Overall, I recommend this game. Its audio-visual experience is pretty magical and its card play is very engaging. The narrative beats that work, work well. 7/10\t\t\t\t\t
