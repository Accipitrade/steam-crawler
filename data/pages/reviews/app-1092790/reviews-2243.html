The best part is that its Horror Elements aren't easily replicated elsewhere, except for Resident Evil 7's 21 Banned Footage. The game is puzzling at times, but don't let this daunt you, it'll give you all the chances you need to solve it, showing you snippets and other things. The only real bar is intelligence in this regard.
The mechanics of the game are rock solid and extremely varied, as a Card Game it is top notch in terms of balance and game-play. Various things aren't explained in detail, but this is okay because its' all part of the puzzle.
The end of each act is very satisfying and very much worthy of inclusion as a "Masterpiece". Though the game only took me 12 hours to beat, I hope to play more as time goes on, really, the first section has the most replayability by far.
I hope to see a DLC with this game, for each of the Scrybes.
Truly, the experience is wonderful and simplistic, and it doesn't take long to beat the game, I did so in 12 hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game's mystery is just as rewarding as its gameplay. The game begins with a really solid blend of deckbuilding rogue-like and escape-the-room elements, but there's so much more to it than that as the game progresses.

If you like subversion, this game keeps on delivering the whole way through. It's the kind of game that you think about when you're not playing it even. When I finished the game I kept going back thru it to try and find ways to make overpowered decks as well which was incredibly fun.

To talk about how much I like this game any further would risk spoiling it. But I will say if you enjoyed Eternal Darkness I'd say this game is a must-play. There's a demo too if you wanna see if you like the card-based gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
