\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn easy to learn game that can be very challenging when the player ... plays badly. As the player continues on, the game gradually becomes more dynamic and more fun+interesting. Once the gameplay is understood, the game does not seem more challenging at all even though mistakes can be costly. In fact, the game seems to get easier, provided you properly balance cards to sacrifice with cards to attack with.

The plot is very intriguing! The game has 3 parts to it which, aesthetically, feels very different -- even though the card game remains throughout. This may detract some players after getting invested in one aesthetic only to switch to a very different one. It is very tightly bound to the overall plot which is delivered as a mystery for the player to piece together. ... By the end of the game, I believe it is pretty obvious but some of the big lore-dumps are still delivered in the form of a kind-of puzzle which does not make it immediately obvious.

Watching a playthough does spoil the plot but does not affect how fun the game is to play. I don't regret, not one-bit, watching one & a half playthroughs of this game. It's still interesting and fun to play! The characters are still fun to interact with.

If I could find one problem, it would be that the game does not teach "proper balancing" between cards that are easy to place and sacrifice for more powerful cards that can attack. It does teach the mechanic, but doesn't teach how to balance it. However, this is VERY MINOR because the death-mechanic allows you to easily start again and add more powerful cards -- giving the player to catch-on to a proper balance between cards to sacrifice and attack with. And it's a fun concept to learn! To the developer's credit, literally "teaching" this would likely result in a period of very dull gameplay that would lessen the games replay value.\t\t\t\t\t
