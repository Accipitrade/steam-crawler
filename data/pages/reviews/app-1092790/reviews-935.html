\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanging my recommendation with an added note: Inscryption just doesn't mesh well with people who don't know Mullins' previous works or just don't like meta-sudden-game-changey stuff. Even with the addition of the 'Endless Mode' with Kaycee's Mod, it ends up feeling more like a slap in the face to those who bought the game for what it was billed as, as the mod just hides more Lore behind increasingly difficult-RNG battles. There is no way to justify to people without that caveat that the game is worth it. Its an okay card game at best and a lackluster 'but the lore!' game at worst. If you want to be trapped playing with a smarmy Card Dealer and actually BUILD a deck.. check out Hand of Fate and skip this one.

Original Review:

I hate to say I'm very mixed on this one compared to Daniel Mullins' other titles. It does feel a little off in the way the game is presented. The horror-style of Act 1 that is promoted drips with atmosphere and unease. Its excellently done. What throws off people, especially those not use to Mullins' previous titles, is the sudden whiplash going into Act 2 and eventually Act 3 of the game, but Act 3 being less out-of-left-field. Is it bad? No, Act 2 is charming and old school, its just not what its hyped up to be. Act 3 returns back to unease with its cold and robotic theme.

The deckbuilding is well.. personally, kind of non-existent. You'll catch on to strategies with the cards, but a lot of the time the game is purposefully being too easy or railroading you into a forced game over in favor of finding more lore. While its not horrible, it feels a lot less organic in execution compared to discovering secrets in Mullins' previous titles. In the second Act I tried to build my own deck and mostly failed.. which resulted in me just using the Auto-Complete option to get through it. This doesn't mean its not possible to choose your own deck, just that I had a rough go at it for some reason. So if you find yourself in the same situation, the game is polite enough to help us types out so we can continue with the story.

The characters and story wasn't intriguing for me. If you haven't at least played The Hex, there is so much callback to that game that a lot of the lore of Inscryption will just feel like "...ok? cool, i guess." and that is a shame. So if you happen to read this review and are interested in meta-make-you-think games- this game will hit you far better if you have previously played at least The Hex. It isn't required, but it does help with the lore-bits to get-it about small things. Pony Island also is the original source of 'Evil Satan Code', its just The Hex has a better story with it.

Though to be fair, Mullins' heard the pleas (read:refundoutcry) and requests for an extended-cut of Act 1's horror-esque card game and as of writing this there is a beta in progress that can be played called Kaycee's Mod that's being added into the game. Though it is meant to be much more challenging and a lot of the ease-of-play factors for story-mode will not be in the extended-mod variant. So this is a good thing for those wanting more of a challenge with the uniqueness of the Beast Deck of Act 1. However, it is still incredibly RNG driven to the point where suddenly railroaded into gameover is common.

It was still a play thru, but the mix of found-footage felt a little boring and could use some polish in the future if this is going to be a thing. The card games were decently designed, but the RNG can feel artificially limiting the same way 'Time Gated' content makes me feel, so I didn't really enjoy myself with them as I have with other card-strategy based games. In the end the game isn't just about the cards, but the evil inside the code. The narrative is more important than the deckbuilding, but the narrative is left too obscure and open for interpretation. Its much too hollow to stand on its own and didn't continue much of anything from previous lore-established games. There needs to be more meat on them bones to keep more than just diehards around.. and most people don't care to google ARGs related to the game as thats not really a good way to 'build' a story.. ya know.. outside the game.. where actual players of the game don't see it.\t\t\t\t\t
