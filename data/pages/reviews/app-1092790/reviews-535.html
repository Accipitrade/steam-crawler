\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's hard to say something about Inscryption that hasn't already been said - it's an amazing, lore- and strategy-rich deck building game with a dark and enthralling atmosphere. The reason a lot of people are intentionally vague when reviewing the 'full' game is because of how much it slaps you in the face with its insane twists. If you think you know what this game is going to do next, you don't.

I first got my hands on Inscryption after watching a couple let's plays on it. After only seeing it for a couple minutes I already wanted to try my hand at it, so grabbed a copy and began to play. The rest is history, and it's now one of my favourite games.

I loved it so much in fact, that I began a YouTube series where I'm building a real-life version of the game as accurately as possible, and it's been a blast so far. It's also only been made possible with the awe-inspiringly kind attitude of the developer, Daniel Mullins, in releasing the game's assets and encouraging homebrew versions of the game to be played in real life with friends.

If that doesn't sell you on how much of an impressively fun game it must be to play, the 'Overwhelmingly Positive' reviews should. This game, while it now seems to be dying off slightly in popularity amongst gaming channels, was an absolutely insane experience to have for the first full play-through, and sitting together with friends strategising the best hand to play, and the best synergies between cards was a great experience and one I made many fond memories during.

In short; if you think this dark-looking deckbuilder only has what's on the Steam page within, you have no idea what you're in for.\t\t\t\t\t
