\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm sorry to say this, but the main game or story mode may be one of the most disappointing things I've ever played. Now let me preface this by saying that I don't hate twists or that the trailer should've shown all of its secrets. I am saying, however, that the marketing for this game feels highly deceptive.

Now, this is where I will be getting into spoiler territory, so if you don't wish to be spoiled on the game's big "twist", stop reading, please.

Now that people who don't wish to be spoiled have left I will discuss my main problem with the game. Firstly, the ad campaign repetitively marketed itself as a roguelike deck builder with a dark atmosphere, some escape room elements, and having a fantastic art style. However, this is not the case as the game has what people are referring to as "acts". Now, the "acts" in this game change the entire gameplay experience in such a way that it no longer even resembles the game that I had purchased, to begin with.

Firstly, this argument "Well you saw Act two within the trailer, didn't you? Well then, you shouldn't be surprised that it changed." Now, this one is also false for another reason than it only appearing in a "blink and you'll miss it" moment. Games are known to have minigames within them correct? Well, that is what I thought that was since "Act one" was roughly 98.19% of the trailer footage.

"Act two" is drastically different from one in every way that it could be. Firstly, the art style that I came for and had adored is now replaced by what I can only describe as having a similar art style to the GameBoy Pokémon games. Now, I love Pokémon and grew up on the Gameboy games, so I do love that art style, however, this art style was not what I got attracted to the game for and it was only shown within the Steam Page for a total of I believe three seconds.

Lastly, a common argument against my argument is this "If you had played or seen the Author's other works you would know that this game is not all it seems." So by this counter-arguments logic if I examine another one of his games I should've known that this game would change styles, then let us compare the marketing to Pony Island another game that is made to not be all that it seems at first. First, on the Steam Page for Pony Island, it says word for word "Pony Island is a suspense puzzle game in disguise. You are in limbo, trapped in a malevolent and malfunctioning arcade machine devised by the devil himself. It is not a game about ponies." This right here indicates instantly what you are getting into. It says very clearly that this game is not what it seems and is fairly open on what it is. Now let's compare that to the description for Inscryption. Inscryption's summary reads as such "Inscryption is an inky black card-based odyssey that blends the deckbuilding roguelike, escape-room style puzzles, and psychological horror into a blood-laced smoothie. Darker still are the secrets inscrybed upon the cards." This gives the buyer next to nothing in way of informing them that the game they purchased will completely change.

In summary, I am disappointed and feel cheated out of my money as the game that I purchased was not what it was marketed as and had little to no hints that my game would change on a fundamental and in such a drastically different way.
