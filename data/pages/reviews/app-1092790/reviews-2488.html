\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome game overall.

I've been waiting for such a long time for this to come out.

The early stages of the game were everything I had hoped for, although I wasn't a big fan of the later portions of the game. Despite this, the finale was surprisingly dramatic, incredible, and well worth the endurance.

Warning - Mild Spoilers -
I wish that the dev would've doubled-down on the whole "starving-man-trapped-in-a-dark-room-with-some-sort-of-forest-monster" scenario for the entire game. This is really what shines the brightest.

In my opinion, the 8-bit portion was actually a drag to play through, and the modified robot roguelike wasn't nearly as good as the first portion.


This being said, to me, the game still averages out to be an excellent experience overall, and a finale this good is a rarity among games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a must-play Halloween/any season Psychological Thriller/Horror Card game. I highly recommend you go in knowing very little about the game as to make it so much more enjoyable. So I won't spoil, but the Plot was very engaging. I was expecting the game to end sooner than it did and neither did it overstay its welcome. Amazing game play mechanics in and outside of the card-game sections. Incredible Visual design from 3D models, textures, and environments. The pixel 3D style felt just great to be in. Even to add to the experience was the outstanding music and sound design which made everything enjoyable even while doing nothing. Can't say it was the best story pacing but it fit the narrative well. The game balance wasn't the most fun even being a single-player experience. Some battles I got stuck on for long periods of time and would require anywhere from moderate to incomprehensible amounts of luck. However overall this game is Absolutely worth its price and I heavily recommend you play Inscryption and have some fun like I did myself.\t\t\t\t\t
