\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat really makes me love Inscryption is how simple, yet very enjoyable, the card game is. Unlike many other rogue like card games the cards don't have long texts that take a long time to read and understand enough to really be able to think about how to make your deck. Instead inscryption focuses on all cards having an attack number, remaining health, specific cost (such as blood which means you must sacrifice a card or bone which you get every time a card on your side of the field dies from any means), and sigils which have very straight forward effects like flying which makes all attacks direct attacks, or many lives which lets them be sacrificed for blood without dying. Cards can also be upgraded like other rogue likes so you can get some interesting combos of these sigils. More than anything I love how some rare cards even have effects which aren't listed.\t\t\t\t\t
