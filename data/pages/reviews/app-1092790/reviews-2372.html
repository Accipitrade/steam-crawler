\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start out by giving this masterpiece of a game a generic rating on a scale of 0 to 10. Taking my feelings and emotions about the game at the moment of writing this, having just completed the game.. I want to say that Inscryption is a 12 out of 10. But, thinking about it more, and really debating why I think this, I see the flaws that this game has.

The pros:
Inscryption allows for a very freeform playstyle. There is no meta, but there is minmaxing. You can make one, singular creature so strong that it cannot die, or you can spread the power, allowing for a consistently good hand.
This, coupled with the large variety of strategies, the emotional elements of some select cards talking, giving them souls and emotions, along with the fact that each time you die, something changes, grants a very fluid experience. No two runs will be the same without you deliberately seeking out a specific playstyle.

The music. It's amazing. The tracks are clearly made specifically for one use, and they perfect it. Every game is improved with a good soundtrack, and Inscryption? It's no exception.

The cons:
The game has a very, very steep learning curve. The early game may feel frustrating, even unfair! You have to learn everything the hard way, and then, finally, when you think you're about to succeed.. you miss that one incoming damage that kills you. It sucks. But, it's part of the experience. And without it.. would there be any satisfaction in besting the hardships?


While, yes, this is a very loose and perhaps even uninformative review, I do hope that you learn something from it. And, perhaps, you'll even buy the game due to this review.
The game is amazing. The story is.. mindblowing. And the ending.. Oh, the ending. It made me tear up, over a character I did not think I liked.

This game, Inscryption, is my choice for the game of the year.
Daniel Mullins, if you by some miracle read this, I want to say thank you. Thank you for the amazing game, and for an experience that I want to return to, even after completing it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Great game, but not nearly as rogue-like as you might expect. More linear, but still intriguing and fun. Card mechanics and characters are captivating.

I grabbed this game on sale because it seemed like an interesting title with beautiful art design, and if some of the footage were to be believed, a rogue-like. Having spent virtually the entire day playing it after purchase, thoroughly entertained and captivated by the visuals and card gameplay, I can say it was well worth the purchase.

With that being said, any rogue-like elements within Inscryption are woefully minimal compared to the other portions of the game. What is set up as an interesting hostage situation in a derelict and wonderfully unsettling cabin quickly turns into a far more linear "Haunted Game Cartridge/Disc" type story. With the player slowly uncovering more information surrounding the game from both within and outside via found footage type interludes between acts.

Knowing the developer, this isn't entirely out of left field, but it was still a blow to my overall enjoyment as what I expected to find was quickly overshadowed by a more set and direct pathway to a final resolution. The gameplay I first fell in love with quickly changed into iterative alterations on the overall card game, but without the initial rogue-like elements I adored. Even worse, the game doesn't come with a free play or alternative challenge mode of any kind, so if you wanted to experience a specific iteration again you'd need to reload to that segment of the overall narrative.

Ultimately that's my main critique for this title, It feels like extraordinary gameplay and mechanics held back by a lackluster-at-worst, mediocre-at-best ARG horror narrative that forces the player along while taking away what made the beginning acts so enthralling. I have hope that future updates or DLC may add more replayability to this game, but until then I'm satisfied leaving it on the shelf after my single playthrough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like the concept behind this game and it was fun to play through and mess around with some broken cards interactions. However there's a few CONS to consider before buying this game:

1. It's very short. I think you can legitimately run through the entire thing in less than 8 hours on your very first playthrough.
2. If you've played a lot of card games in the past you will find this game way too easy.
3. The way the story is told is very interesting but I didn't like how meta it became. It's difficult to criticize this without spoiling the story so I'll just summarize my point by saying that it's not for everyone.

If you're not a veteran card player and just want a short interesting card adventure game coupled with puzzles I would still recommend this game. It's good.\t\t\t\t\t
