However, it's not going to provide you with nearly unlimited hours of gameplay like some of the more popular deckbuilders out there. Inscryption has a compelling and puzzling narrative that will pull you in outside of its card-based mechanics, but the limited scale of the game presents an issue for those looking to get more out of the game. Inscryption is pretty much good enough for the initial run through, and even though the devs had added an endless mode since release, it's not enough for me to justify the full price of this game. If you want longer, more rewarding gameplay options, look at the other great deck builders out there on Steam.

Play Inscryption--you'll probably like it for its narrative! But don't pay for it at full price and expect it to provide the same value as similar games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSeveral months ago I played Pony Island and loved it, I didn't wait for a better discount to get Inscryption.

I have never played any card games, but Inscryption is intriguing. After a few runs I realized it is more than a card game - likewise, Pony Island is more than an arcade. They both have something deeper.

Inscryption is more darker, intense, horrifying and confusing. In a degree it reminds me of the birthday party puzzle in RE:7. I beat the game in 10h, yet the story is not finished, I have so much doubts. It is good to have deeper meanings, but Inscryption hid too deep and vague. I will give a positive review, but far less to be my favorite.\t\t\t\t\t
