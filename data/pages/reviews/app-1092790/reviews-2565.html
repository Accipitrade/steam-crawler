\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't be deceived: this is not a card game. It is a puzzle-solving visual novel.
For what the card game is worth, it is a pretty neat concept and works really well for the first 2/5 of the game, and (d)evolved into an enjoyable, even if brief, experience. Expanded on, this could become a really good card game with many interesting quirks.
For what the game is trying to achieve, it unfortunately fell a little short. While the story it tells goes through well, it had a great potential but wasn't ambitious enough. "I wanted to spend more time with Magnificus". "I want to spend more time with Grimora". Two of the main characters are left to the side just to come back in the end to join forces with the player. By this point you understand Leshy's resolve and he's given redemption, which is amazing. But then the two remaining characters could as well not exist and everything would be the same, except for Magnificus' emotional scene. Both him and Grimora lacked screen time, and the player is supposed to feel for them while barely knowing them.
This, however, might the this game's only flaw (other than the demo being half of the game).
Sadly no replayability, because the ambience of the game is extremely good. Will be missed.\t\t\t\t\t
