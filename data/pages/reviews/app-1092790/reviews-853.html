\r\n\t\t\t\t\tParts of this review may contain spoilers.

To start off, this isn't a horrible game but I don't think it's a great one either.

The RNG is beyond infuriating how random it is and how many times you get a huge disadvantage compared to the AI just because you had an unlucky roll.
Every time you die you start from the beginning with basically the same starter deck you initially started out with.
After you die you can craft a "death card", they are special cards you can make as a combination of the cards you collected through your run but it's also based on their ridiculous random luck generator so you can almost guarantee it's going to be a garbage card.

I do really like the concept, music, sounds and style of the game but the gameplay drawbacks are too much for me to endure playing for more than a few hours in a row.

If this game had some way to improve your luck or overall strength and quality of cards over multiple runs I would probably enjoy it a lot more but I feel that the way this game is supposed to be played is incredibly boring and repetitive. The maps are sort of randomised each run but still comes down to the same bosses, same cards, same unlucky rng, overpowered AI etc.

After getting the hang of the game a bit I even tried bringing a good deck on the first boss after fully wiping my save and restarting the game and when you are actually about to defeat the first boss he goes "berserk" and spawns in 8x super OP cards so you have no chance of winning because you HAVE to die and you HAVE to lose. I hate games that force death on you to continue the story or to make you "learn" the game. That's fine for people that enjoy that kind of game but it's absolutely not something I enjoy.

I will give it a few more tries but it's not a game I would recommend if you're just into regular card battle games.


Update after playing 7 more hours:
It just gets worse, all the screenshots you see in the Steam Store are just the 1st part of the game and even though it's frustrating, that was the most fun part of the game. The 2nd act turns into some kind of Super Mario map-style RPG where you have to defeat bosses with a way to complex card system, you get several additional types of cards that make it very complicated to figure out how to build a good deck. The bosses are all very tedious, every single one just has the "defeat my 3 minions before you can face me" style and then the combat itself was sort of underwhelming. I expected something like the final battle in the 1st act.

The "voice overs" from the guy who is supposedly playing Inscryption are very unnecessary, boring and annoying and, to me, add nothing of value. The videos of him talking about the game are just dumb and could've been left out of the game completely and probably make the game more enjoyable as a whole. You can easily skip/avoid these so that's a good thing at least.

The worst part comes after the already bad 2nd Act and this is where I stopped playing after like 15-20 minutes, Act 3. The 2D-ish game you were playing now turns into a 3D world with boring gameplay, boring puzzles, boring maps, boring interactions and so on. Very disappointed at the game because the 1st Act art style is one I really enjoyed, the 2nd I didn't really care for and the 3rd is just horrible. It's not what was promised, none of the screenshots in the store show the 2nd or 3rd Act art styles. If I would've known about the 2nd and 3rd Act I would've held off buying the game completely. I feel cheated because it's a completely different game you're playing and it's not something I would've bought if I knew beforehand.

The game would've been like a 6/10 before the 2nd/3rd Act but because of above mentioned issues, this drops to a 4/10.\t\t\t\t\t
