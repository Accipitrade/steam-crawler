\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn absolute work of ART.
A large portion of this game is not on display on the store page or any of the official advertisements for it, and this is for good reason. Inscryption is not just a game, but an experience that you should witness first hand through and through.

However, do be warned that Inscryption requires a certain level of perseverance by the player, as the gameplay can be rather unforgiving and overwhelming at times, but becomes much less taxing once you realise that sometimes it's best not to overthink things too much, especially the closer you get to the end.
The game opens up with a very ominous, psychological horror tone, but even if this is not very appealing to you, I urge you to push on and see the secrets it hold further on down the line. Things become much less ominous once you realise that the gameplay is very much comparable to a session of Dungeons and Dragons or some other tabletop roleplay format, and that your opponents are just a bunch of roleplayers trying to seem cool.

If you have any experience with one of the many collectable card games out there, the references and homages that Inscryption makes to most of them will have you cracking up and feeling overwhelmed with nostalgia.

But, even if you don't relate to this, the subtle (and sometimes not so subtle) humour this game throws at you here and there landed for me pretty much every time and had me laughing like an idiot on the regular.
This is until you get hit straight in the feels by a pain train out of nowhere and can't help but be amazed at how brilliant of a rollercoaster of emotions it all became in the end.

So in conclusion, PLAY IT! You won't regret it! :D\t\t\t\t\t
