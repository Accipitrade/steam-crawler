\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere to begin.

Inscryption is, vaguely speaking, a roguelite deckbuilding TCG. Sort of. The gameplay is hard to describe as anything other than "Imagine a TCG, but with a world map". The gameplay alone is worth the $19.99 asking price. It just clicks. It's not dissimilar from games like MtG or Yu-Gi-Oh, but it has enough of its own identity that comparing it to The Big Three just seems wrong. You just gotta play it to get a feel. There's a demo, go give that a whirl.

The story is confusing, and definitely jumps the shark a bit, but I've always liked my stories to be a little corny. It's not really too too important if you don't care about it, but there is lore for all you lore nerds out there. Not my cuppa tea, so I can't really speak on how good the story is, other than "it was fine, I guess".

Kaycee's Mod is the definitive Inscryption experience, being essentially what I thought the game was when I first bought it. And, like I said before, this is worth the 20 bones by itself, if I'm being honest. The game mechanics are given room to be fleshed out and to flourish in ways they couldn't in the original game, and the meta feels much more akin to what you'd find in a real TCG.

Although there are some balance issues -- the fecundity nerf was BADLY needed, and there's never a reason to play anything but bug decks what with ant queen and mantis god -- the game is mostly balanced, although sometimes a bit tipped against the player, especially in Kaycee's Mod. (KM Ouroboros is fine, but main game Ouroboros is... a sight to behold. Did its sigil HAVE to stack across acts??? I spent most of Act 3 fishing for my Win Condition card, which was less fun than it sounded like in my head :c)

But, that's it. Some balancing stuff that can be patched is, quite literally, my only gripe with this game. Everything here is nothing short of perfection, through and through. Buy this game immediately, and tell all of your friends and family to buy it too. You won't regret it, and neither will they.\t\t\t\t\t
