\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption starts off as a Atmospheric Roguelike Deckbuilder with Escape Room Tones and a underlying mystery. Unfortunately, this is, as many have said, the best part of the game. Starting off with their best foot forward unfortunately ruined the rest of the game for most, due to them expecting every other act to have the same level of detail and atmosphere. I am not one of these people. I went in expecting it to be like the rest of Daniel Mullins' games, where the Story, Puzzles, and Meta-ARG takes precident over the gameplay itself. And in that regard, it delivered to decent success. I would not hold it to the same level as the Hex, but the effort they put into this game shows.

Echoing what many have said before me, I would love a update to expand further on the first act, and perhaps provide more insight to the other two Scrybes' sections. I am not expecting such a thing to come to fruition outside of modding though.

All in all, play the game if you like Mystery, Secret-Hunting, and Story elements. If you liked the Deckbuilding and Roguelike Elements of Part 1, then I suggest purchasing a game such as Slay the Spire instead.\t\t\t\t\t
