Act 1: Fun gameplay, and pretty interesting puzzles. I like the variety of cards and even when I lost my battles I still kept playing.

Act 2: Not super fun, didn't use a good portion of the elements... It's also heavily RNG based due to both squirrels and monsters being in the same deck. However the change in gameplay style is unique.

Act 3: Very fun. Didn't use the circuit mechanic, though. My favorite part had to be the bosses. The difference between act 1 and act 3 bosses is that act 3 is significantly more interactive. Using your friends list as cards, using your files size to weigh the scale and even having you delete a file! (Note this doesn't delete a file, instead it pulls up a readme on said folder politely asking you to delete it yourself.) Overall really fun. I feel it lacks the style and options act 1 had, though. but it makes up for it with the amazing bosses!

Lore & Ending: Can't really talk much about the lore since I didn't read into it much. However, the ending really caught me off guard. The part with Leshy actually made me quite emotional. It was a sad ending in an emotional way, and afterwards the second part of it shocked me for a good minute or two. Overall amazing ending!

8/10: Great game, Would be close to 9 or ten out of ten in act 2 was more fun.

Tl;dr: Act 1 and 3 are super fun, act 2 is too luck based, ending wasn't expected on my end. \t\t\t\t\t

It doesn't have as many bells and whistles as most modern games today, but it uses that simplicity to bring you into the world it builds. I feel as though many players will overlook it, is simply because of how much the game saves for the very end. It's a gradual buildup that leads to an inevitable conclusion, and a lot of players aren't able to take a slow burn game without losing focus of the point of it all.

Very minor spoilers from here to below

The story is intriguing and personal. You might find yourself getting attached to meaningless cards or characters, because the time and strategy you put into the game makes you feel that much closer to it. The 4th wall breaks, the hidden story elements, everything creates an awesome world I implore anyone to give some time. It really is a game that evolves all the way up until the end, always keeping you waiting for the next iteration, next run, and whatnot.

On top of all that, the game has very touching themes which make the impact of the game itself just that much more powerful. You get this feeling that an inevitable conclusion is fast approaching. As the game goes on, the pressure, the more tricks, and the more stuff the game has to show you all the way until the very end.

There's no good way to go about explaining it without major spoilers, so if you're a fan of meta-breaking horror, strategy card games, games that try new concepts, and games with replay-ability, just trust me on this one. Great game.
