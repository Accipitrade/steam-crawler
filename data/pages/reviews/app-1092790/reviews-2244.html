The train is named 'Inscryption.'

You are served a steak dinner. The steak is a bit bigger than you imagined.

Why are the other diners are wearing late 19th century attire?

You tentatively take a bite of the mashed potatoes. They're OK, you guess.

A server pours you a glass of wine. He describes it as 'vintage' with 'mature, complex flavor.'

The victim suddenly dies in your mashed potatoes.

The rotund man across the way has an expression of surprise, but also amusement.

You accidentally bought a ticket to a Murder Mystery Train, didn't you?

You mourn your mashed potatoes briefly. Second later, you decided it's not a big loss. They weren't that good anyway.

You take a sip of the wine. It's not complex or mature at all. It's sweet and probably very cheap.

"No one drink the wine!" another actor shouts from the other side of the cabin.

The Sherlock Holmes lookalike meticulously tests every glass of wine, one-by-one.

You heave a heavy sigh. Your ticking steak is getting cold.

Why is your steak ticking?

"There's a bomb in the steak!"

A man in a purple vest throws your steak out the cabin window. Everyone claps.

You sigh. Your aching stomach grumbles angrily.

Seconds later, an explosion is heard in the distance.

In the moment, you sustain yourself on mashed potatoes spooned carefully from the periphery of the victim's faceprint.

You feel weirdly sleepy. You hazily see a woman rush up to you.

"This man has been poisoned!" she shrieks.

A concerned crowd forms around you as your eyelids slowly-but-surely lose their fight with gravity.

You just wanted to eat a nice dinner on a fancy train.

Over the shoulder of the woman, the rotund man's eyes glow menacingly as he silently stares, watching you die.

You struggle with your enfeebled body to point at the server.

The rotund man mechanically shakes his head back and forth. "Wrong. Better luck next time."

You use the last of your energy to roll your eyes.

But once you reach a certain point...

***UPCOMING SPOILER! ***

The game does a massive 180 completely out of nowhere. It was such a wild change to the game that I was almost convinced that I messed up somewhere. You solve puzzles and follow clues to try and reach where you thought you would continue the storyline further down the same rabbit hole. Instead it goes from this amazing, dark cardgame, to like...Pokemon Red? Gameplay changes, it's not as fun anymore, and somehwat disappointing.

Definitely was not a fan with the change. I was enjoying it for the original content, not the drastic shift in content. Still a great and fun game, but get ready for a complete change in gameplay. Leshy is king and the best card master! Shoutout to my girl, Grimora though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is an amazing deckbuilding card game roguelite, with great story and an amazingly dark atmosphere that completely changes after you complete the inital third of the game. The game is split into 3 different gameplay sections each with unique changes to the gameplay system that adds a variability to the card gaming system that never let me get bored as I tried to master each system and optimize for the additional mechanics the game added. After completing the game I felt satisfied with my choices and loved the complicated story and it's implications. Overall I really enjoyed my time with the game and am even considering going back in to complete a 100% achievement run when I can.

I took a look at the negative reviews and found that most of the complaints are regarding the fact that there isn't more of the first section of the game. I can see the reasoning for the reviews, being that there is no gameplay in the trailer from the other two-thirds of the game, but from my perspective I just thought that they likely have not seen any other Daniel Mullins Games works as these always include a twist. Based on playing both Pony Island and The Hex, I was eagerly awaiting to find out where the twist would be in this game.

Opposite to the negative reviews, I'm not really into deckbuilding games like Slay the Spire and others many mentioned, but as this game kept throwing new choices and mechanics at me it kept the gameplay interesting for me and kept me engaged. I believe that if the first part were longer and that if no new mechanics were added, I could see myself losing interest in the game opposite to the people who lost interest after the first part. Overall I think that either way there was going to be one crowd that was displeased as most Daniels Mullins Games fans are likely here for the story and the gameplay is a neat second.

*Edit just wanted to point out that I'm not a review guru, but I just wanted to get my thoughts of the game out since I loved it so much.\t\t\t\t\t
