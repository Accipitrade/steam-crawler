\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHoly ♥♥♥♥♥, this game is good.

Just to be clear, this game is another meta indie title about a game that knows it's a game. It has 4ish "acts" with very different gameplay in all of them. The images on the store and the demo are only Act 1. This caught me off guard, since after I played the demo I thought the game was going to be a straight-forward Rogue-Light/Deck Builder/Puzzle Room game, but it is not that. It has those elements but only in the beginning.

The actual game is extremely fun. I loved the cards and all the crazy combos you can make with them. I am not going to delve deep into gameplay because you can just play the demo, but I do have some criticisms for the game. Act 1 is the best part of the whole, which is kind of a shame since it's so short. Act 2 is really boring. I'm sorry, but the retro 8-bit visuals simply pale in comparison to the awesome look of the 3D assets in Act 1, 3, and 4. The controls in this act are also really ♥♥♥♥♥♥. You move way too slowly and the deck builder mechanic is actually a detriment to how the game works. Being able to have a ♥♥♥♥ ton of cards and having to place them in the deck while keeping track of sinergies between 4 incompatible energy systems is not fun. I just used the auto deck builder and said f it.

I also really dislike how act 2 does a poor job at explaining the new mechanics with the cost of cards. Act 1 properly explains how sacrificing works and you have to die at least once before the game explains to you how bones work. Both bones and blood work surprisingly well together, but when you get to act 2, they just throw in your face 2 extra new systems with no explanation whatsoever.

Act 2 also loses the fun mechanics of Act 1, being able to select a card to become stronger with the possibility of losing it, or sacrificing sigils from one card to another, or fusing cards (it still exists in Act 2 but it's worst and then later is completely gone).

While the "wanted" system is interesting, other things aren't. Moving around the map is really not fun. Not having an actual map to see where you are is also annoying. The energy system in Act 3 is literally just Hearthstone, and it's not fun. Your enemies don't have mana, which means they always start with a big advantage. Having to slowly build mana and not being able to do ♥♥♥♥ in the early turns just to spam broken cards in the later turns is not fun. It's simply not as interactive as Blood and Bones. Another problem with Act 3 is the map. Since it isn't randomly generated like in Act 1, it means you can memorize how the battles work, and it also means a lack of veraety for the gameplay.

I LOVE this game, but a part of me feels like maybe if the game was just the same as Act 1 for the whole game, building upon the gameplay, it would have been better, maybe? I don't know, I also wish we could have had more gameplay with the other scrybes. Their gameplay systems seemed really interesting (except the wolf, the 3 gems system is just not fun).

Just buy this game.\t\t\t\t\t
