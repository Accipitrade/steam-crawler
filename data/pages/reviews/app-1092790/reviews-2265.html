\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't want to spoil too much with this review, and echoing some of the other negative reviews I'd give this game a mixed score if I could. With that out of the way:

This game spends its entire running time writing checks that its ending can't cash. The opening act is by far the most polished and entertaining, and the gameplay quality absolutely falls off a cliff afterwards. The second act is terribly balanced and mostly an exercise in frustration, and the third act is dull, repetitive, and drags on for far too long with the exception of a few very clever boss encounters. What keeps you going after the first act ends is a drip-fed narrative hinting at some greater mystery, but the game ends abruptly with no sense of closure.

It's possible that there are small bits of exposition that I missed, but I just don't care to play the game again to find out because in retrospect the card game upon which the entire narrative is built just isn't very good. The roguelike elements in the first act do a very good job of hiding it, but once those are gone the gameplay loses its lustre and only keeps the player hooked with the promise of narrative closure that never arrives. What a missed opportunity.\t\t\t\t\t
