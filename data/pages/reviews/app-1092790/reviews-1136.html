\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a trip. A lot of people said "play this without looking up too much about it" and it's true. Play it. It's very clever and it's entertaining.

That said a few things.

  • Realize this game is longer than you think. There's 3 "acts" and each act has things change as you go on.
  • The game starts off... iffy. Like you're not sure if it's fun or not. You get it and it seems simplistic and kinda fun but not that great. Keep on playing. You're supposed to lose and play through it a few times. The more you play, the more powerful your deck becomes (and thus the easier it becomes). You're not supposed to really understand what a lot of the map icons means - you can look them up or you can just learn them as you click on them to see. You'll find each act starts off a bit slow and then becomes very addicting as you go.
  • Act 2 introduces 3 new deck types, though the undead deck type uses the same idea as the bones part of the beast deck. You'll always use the Beast (act 1) and Tech (act 3) though so you might want to go Undead or Magic for act 2 if you want the most variety.
  • There's more to the game than just the card game. If you're only concentrating on the card game you're missing things.
  • There are a few secrets that you won't figure out unless you play a lot and even then you probably will miss some. I'll list some of them below. These aren't spoilers really and yes, it's revealing a secret or mechanic that you're supposed to learn yourself, but they're not huge secrets.

Secrets / mechanics you probably won't know

  • Campfire. It allows you to buff your creature up. Later in act 1 you can use the same one multiple times but every time you use it there's a chance the people will eat your creature and you lose it.
  • Ringworm. The card looks like utter crap. There's only one real use for it and that's to use for the campfire and only when you get to the stage where you are allowed to use it multiple times. If you put it on the campfire, they'll eat it and you'll lose the ringworm. Next campfires on the play through will have no people around it so you can use it to max without risking your creatures life.
  • Mycologist (mushroom). Will take 2 of the same card and double their power/hp. Very useful but you need doubles.
  • Lightning bolt icon in act 3 (overclocking). Honestly I don't like it. If you click on it, you pick a card and it'll get +1 power and if it dies, the card is eliminated from your deck. Your cards die often so... personally I think I'd avoid it.
  • Sliding puzzles - I'll admit this took me way too long to figure out. The sliding puzzle is in the shape of the game board. Your goal is to do the right amount of damage. When you hit the "bell" it'll act like you executed your turn and determine the amount of damage you would do if that was the board.
