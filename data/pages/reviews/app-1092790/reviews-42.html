\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis really starts off strong but goes on to stumble and fall flat on it's face. I have not played the Acts after I (I didn't even know there was enything else), but from the reviews, it only gets worse.

The Beginning is very good. If you're interested in the atmosphere from the trailers or like card games in general, I can reccomend you give it a try at least, because it's executed very well.

However, the first issues cropped up about 4 hours in, when RNG would screw me repeatedly, making entire runs doomed from the start. This is a roguelike game, so apart from the first few scripted deaths you may die over and over and have to repeat the first few stages over and over, because the game gave you garbage loot. While the maps and encounters change each time, eventually you will get bored. Or the game shoves amazing cards down your throat the first time and you can oneshot the entire game.
Unlike most roguelikes, there's almost nothing you gain from restarting. You may have some more knowledge of mechanics, but the main death-boon has an extremely low chance of actually appearing in your next run. Or ever.

After the first Act Boss, the game reveales the biggest let-down imaginable
This game has a boring meta-narative shoved down it's throat. Yes, I didn't watch the trailers, so it's partailly on me. But it gets even worse: nothing you did mattered. You are playing a completely different game now. All the narative arcs set up before are now gone. Even the cool artstyle and themes are screpped. After all, coming up with strange and unsetteling things is easy, but explaining them is hard. \t

I'm glad I've tried this game, but I'm also glad I've stopped.\t\t\t\t\t
