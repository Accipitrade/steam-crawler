+ Chilling tale of obsession and nerdlife.

+ Wonderful atmosphere and use of a claustrophobic environment.

- Some dead end moments where logic does not seem to apply. Not in a fun or challenging way. Have your search engine on standby.

- It's a MULLINS game - unpredictability is predictable. And shortness.

Twisted, a tribute and unpredictable...

...which is pretty remarkable for such a linear game.

But after other Daniel MUILLINS games, perhaps the unpredictableness is predictable.

It also is very hard to review without trashing the events which unfold.

It is a found footage game crossed between simulations of a card-collectable game.

It has some very creepy moments and for a game which is not all about creeping thorough open environments with whatever enemies jumping out, it is quite tense.

If you like the idea of a horror mystery filtered through obsessive gaming, this is for you.

Don't look too much online - apart from the bits when you have very little choice. You'll know when it happens.\t\t\t\t\t
