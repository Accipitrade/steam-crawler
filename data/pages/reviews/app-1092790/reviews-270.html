\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a card battle game similar in vein to what you expect from something as Slay the Spire. But, like Slay the Spire, it's a game that can really make you feel awfully dumb in what you try to do. Like it can be insultingly challenging and I admit that the first half a dozen tries into this game, I was really running in blindly so I waive any knocks in this game about difficulty since I was just feeling out it's mechanics and gameplay.

Though comparatively speaking, Inscryption yet hasn't reached the stress levels that Slay the Spire has done long ago since I last played it.

Regardless, this game does have a few unsettling aspects about it's atmosphere, it's intriguing with the use of animals and their capabilities to be used within battle. The setting of you just going along traveling through the woods is progressing enough of a story for this game to latch onto. From there, it is what you make of it and how far you really want to go.

The one key feature I found that stood out was the ability to have cards in you deck from when you died previously. You can build these 'deathcards' that are absorbed into your deck, combining the traits of some of the selected cards from your deck to be used later. I've not seen a card game function like this to memory so it's a nice addition, as it could also be used as a means to keep trying over and over again until you've made it all the way through.

I recommend this~\t\t\t\t\t
