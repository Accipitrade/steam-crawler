\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption or also known as Animal the Gathering is a breath of fresh air compared to other card games on Steam. The gameplay is enjoyable and easy to figure out during the first act. The atmosphere is creepy and perfect during Halloween as there seems to be a large presence of warm colors such as orange and red. You WILL lose a lot during the game, however. Each "death" you'll be given a "Deathcard" which will bear your name on (or whatever name you want to put on there). You can complete puzzles outside the game area within the cabin. Collecting more rare cards and, no spoilers, VERY important cards, you will be on your way to defeating the final boss

of Act 1

There are 4 acts within the game. While the first act tackles the 'animal' cards, Act 2 goes through a classic approach appearing as a retro game like Pokemon (in this case). Act 2 introduces new card types such as robot characters who use energy, dead type characters who can only accept bone coins, and magic characters who can only accept certain colored moxes. Acts 3 and 4 also have this so it's not thrown away after the 2nd act.
There is a deeper story within the game that does take some effort getting into due to it being like an ARG.

Overall, Inscryption is a fantastic card game that's incredibly addictive

\r\n\t\t\t\t\twell uh

I don't really know how to go about these.If I'm being honest most games don't intrigue or inspire me enough to write these. As a result, most of the reviews I've ever written are either underwhelming, repeat the same things others have said, or just aren't interesting. Even then, the few I've made that *are* decent usually aren't insightful enough to warrant any kind of engagement at all from those reading. I'm worried this will just devolve into a wall of text that's more sentimental than informative, but I don't think I'm worried enough to keep from doing it. That being said.

This game is, on its face, a very fun roguelite with some interesting atmospheric quirks. For a lot of people, that's all it is. And that's fine. That's the reason I bought the game myself, after all. Without spoiling anything, This game is so much more than that once you look deeper. Maybe I'm being a bit over-the-top or dramatic, but I've felt so many things playing this game that I highly doubt I'll be able to forget, not the least of which was being thoroughly satisfied and engaged with the game at nearly every moment. If you have a passing interest in this game, anything at all, please, buy it. It's more than worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
