\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Truly interesting and story ridden game, though only if you're looking close. While most seems speculative at best, many of the characters you meet (and beat) on your way through the separate stages of act 1 are as real as you are though may only still exist through the games you play. Here is where my playtime went sideways, I was under the impression that it was ONLY a card game with a linear or as near you may get such as slay the spire or other rogue-lites

I was wrong.

Something you may find yourself saying as you travel through the world of Inscryption. Each twist and turn drives the point home that as much as you are pulling yourself towards victory - you may not be the main player in your own game. Each other aspect of your game seems alive and working with or against you. Good choices or perhaps even different ones can impact your play in unprecedented manners. That being said, some aspects can become tedious, learning to play for act 1 is not nearly the same as learning to play for act two and so on. Rules are different and even some objectives can change - thankfully keeping most of the core loop the same. Overall, I would recommend the game for those with a morbid curiosity, though its somewhat detracted by the fact it didn't follow much past the first act.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpfront I'll say it was a highly enjoyable game on multiple levels, visually, aesthetically, narratively, and mostly mechanically. It wasn't overtly long, but that's honestly a boon these days with too many games taking longer than necessary.

Some mild spoilers ahead.

I honestly enjoyed Act 1 a great deal more than the remainder of the game, both visually and mechanically. Act 2 was the worst of it. While the change was interesting at first, the battles weren't as enjoyable, it lacked the creepy aesthetic appeal of the other acts, and four different mechanics all at once quickly became disjointed and irritating. I also just personally never liked the idea of Moc in Act 2 and never really used any of the Wizard cards. I will admit it worked a bit better in Act 3 when joined with the Empty Vessels, if only a little bit. Act 3 was enjoyable, but not as much as Act 1. Energy & Mox simply wasn't as fun as Blood & Bones to me, plus it lacked the enjoyment of the randomized "choose your own adventure" of Act 1. Simply put I could have played a whole lot more of Act 1, though I do admit the swap up between the Acts kept things from growing too stale.

Over all a good game well worth my time and money. I'd certainly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
