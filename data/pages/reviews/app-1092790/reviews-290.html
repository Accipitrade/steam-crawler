It's split into different segments, and as the game went on it lost what made me interested originally.

While I thought the game was FINE after the first part, I remember back to when I first started playing the game and was absolutely amazed about what was going to happen next.

Unfortunately the REALLY COOL part of the game is dropped for a differently focused story, which some might enjoy and others might feel is completely terrible. Personally I thought that expanding on the first part in a way that kept it's creepy aesthetics would've been far more interesting, even though I was fine with the story provided apart from some twists near the end.

Overall it's fine, and if you want a game theory style lore story then go for it, but I do wish it went in a very different direction to what I was hoping for when I started :(

Additions after playing 21 extra hours for full completion:
I have EXTREMELY enjoyed Kaycee's Mod.
Although my previous complaints remain about the post-First Act gameplay and story, Kaycee's Mod is an EXCELLENT Rougelite deckbuilder.
Focusing on what made the game fun to play while expanding on it with new item and card unlocks, and an AMAZING challenge system to make it harder for yourself.

I'm writing this review after finishing a "Skull Storm" run, where you turn every challenge that you've unlocked on at the same time for an excellent final challenge.
So while I still dislike where the main game went, the fact that this even exists alongside the main game (which other people still might like overall!) pleases me greatly with very fun card strategy, even giving some lore here and there (which, to be honest, might've made me like the main story more by the end.)

Excellent mode. I think I'm happy overall with the game now, my gripes with the story remain, but I've had a very fun time.\t\t\t\t\t
