Inscryption is a game that should be played with little to no knowledge about it.

After playing the demo I was intrigued by the fun mechanics and art style of the game, this alone was enough to convince me to give the game a fair shake, and for that price I figured, "why not".

Nothing could have prepared me for what I was about to experience. I want to say as little as possible, AS I STRONGLY ENCOURAGE ANYONE READING THIS TO ENTER THE GAME BLINDLY IT WILL NOT DISAPPOINT YOU.

If you need more convincing I'll get more into detail about the game in the next paragraph. LAST WARNING

The game consist of III ACTS, each one better than the last. If what caught your attention is the "horror" aspect of the game I assure you it will surprise you. By the end of the third act I was just completely in shock and in awe of what I just experienced. The mechanics are fun, constantly improving throughout the game giving you more options and upgrades to use on your deck. Your strategy will be unique to you, like your cards your cards. I truly think no two people have or will ever play the same deck as you did. Everything about this game is incredible: The way it narrates such an interesting story, the art style, the music, the plot twists, the dialogue, the puzzles, the horror, the variety, the length, the gameplay. It's all so unique and creative that makes you wonder how they did it while putting such a generous price tag on top of it.

This is a game that makes you wish for more and breaks your heart when there´s not.
(10/10) GOTY

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had high expectations when going into the game as the trailer looked simply amazing. And going away from the game after finishing it I was even more amazed. Without spoiling much all I can say is it's a 9/10. It was way more than what I thought it actually would be from watching the trailer. But no one cares about the story or the atmosphere that the game perfectly delivers right? You are here about the gameplay. And all I can say is it's just about right. There were a few issues that the game heavily suffered from. Like the whole second part of the game felt way too easy I was destroying the opponent's left and right without even opening one of my brain cells. And collecting cards felt truly pointless as most of them I never used. But that was only like 1 stage of the game that felt like that. The rest were completely on point with the gameplay and I just might return back to it to try out more ways to outsmart the ai.
And also the puzzles were good and easy to understand and solve.
In conclusion is inscryption worth your money? Absouletely YES.
If you like card games with some nice ass story with a bloodly good atmosphere and some mixed in puzzles then it is for you.
Atmosphere - 10/10
Sound/music - 9/10
Story- 10/10
Gameplay 8.5/10
Puzzles 10/10
Which roughly puts the game at 9/10
*buy it*\t\t\t\t\t
