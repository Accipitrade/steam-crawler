If you like games you can play with just your mouse, you'll love this game. If you like games with a ton of replayability, you'll love this game. If you like games like Hades you'll love this game.

This game should be way more than 20 dollars in my opinion. There's SO much to it, without giving away spoilers, that you don't realize when you first start playing it. Steam only says I have 9 hours in this game but they're very wrong, I've played this for close to 50 hours, and I'm still having such a great time with it.

It's similar enough to MtG or Gwent, etc, to be familiar and easy to pick up, but it's unique enough to easily stand on it's own. The mechanics are fun and always changing, the different paths you can take add so much replayability, and the characters/lore keep you wanting more information about the world/story.

Easy 10/10 from me. If they ever release an actual card game for this, I would absolutely buy and play it. I've played Magic and Pokemon for over 10 years and this feels so fun and new. Buy it, play it, love it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is worth playing if you love card games. In a lot of ways it celebrates many aspects of both paper TCGs and deckbuilding roguelites. The initial atmosphere and strategy gameplay is worth the price tag, even if the game drastically falls off after first act. This game is essentially the best version of Artifact that Valve could never figure out.

The unfortunate truth is there is a hidden masterpiece buried in this game that could have been. With overall aesthetic and fun strategic gameplay, it is clear there is a lot of love packaged in here. Unfortunately, after the first act the game proceeds to tell the same story that's been told numerous times at the expense of fun gameplay. While it's probably the most interesting meta-textual game I've played recently, anyone else getting bored with this overdone concept? I think the only moment I'll remember from the final act is just a callback to the first act of the game.

Maybe down the line Inscryption will be expanded upon to give a game mode where the card game can really shine, until then it's just a 20ish hour game that sacrificed replayability and greatness for a forgettable and barely fleshed out story. Perhaps this disappointment was the goal of the developer, but I think most people agree the cabin is where the good part of the game is at.

Wonder if there's any way we could get Leshy to develop a standalone game.\t\t\t\t\t
