\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJUST BUY THIS GAME; You won't regret it!

Inscryption is, without a doubt, one of the most entertaining, creative, engaging, and mechanically exciting games I've played in years. On top of that, it has an incredible amount of gameplay content! I mean it, I was satisfied enough to pay the full price just after enjoying it for a few hours, and then I discovered (twice now) that there's still PLENTY more game to go past that point! Like finding the reverse castle in Symphony of the Night, times x2 (or maybe more, haven't finished the game yet!).

Also, do yourself a favor and try to go into this game as blind as you can. Discovering and puzzling out what's going on with the game's story and lore without any previous knowledge has been an amazing experience on top of the already addictive gameplay.

And if you can help it, don't wait to buy it at a discount.

I know, it sounds weird saying something like that, but games made with this much passion, love, and talent (and you can tell just by playing there was no shortage of those in this one) are so few and far between these days that I think us gamers should try to support and reward those rare creators that make them possible.

By this point, you might think I'm overselling it, and I don't blame you. But I promise you; I'm not (at least not if you like card games, great quirky stories, and genuinely unique artistic design). If you still have any doubts, though, just download the FREE DEMO and see if your cup of tea. Just know that the demo only shows but a tiny fraction of all this game has going on, and it takes a little bit to get going (as with most games with rogue-like elements!).

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR I don't really care for the story but this game is amazing if you can get through the story.

NEW: After playing Kaycee's Mod for hours I can say with confidence that it is broken. Short of getting really good RNG, it's not entirely skill-based, it's just too many nerfs to everything and unclear direction.

I think that, in this case, going for more meta results in a less satisfying game. I know I'm in the minority here, but I would have loved to see the other arms of this game fleshed out in a rogue-like, or at least complete, way as well. Maybe once the players finish, the option to revisit fully realized parts of this game becomes available. Oh, well.

Edit:
I was going to try out the Kaycee's Mod addition on my laptop, but for some reason my files didn't carry over so I ended up playing the whole game through again. I liked it better this time through, but I stand by what I said, the story, while fun, distracts from what is an amazingly competent card game. (Really several styles of card game.) I wish the story was the optional thing you unlock at the end instead of Kaycee's--which is more what I really want out of the game--being the endgame unlock.\t\t\t\t\t
