\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game provided me with the most profound experiences I've ever had in gaming. I wasn't sure if I'd like the game at first because it's a card game and doesn't show a lot of what it has to offer up front and now that I've played it I completely understand why. You have to go into this game in a complete vacuum to get the best out of it. This is both a blessing and a curse in that the difficulty (mostly referring to RNG/luck) can put a lot of people off at first who aren't already invested in having a good time with the game. Referring or recommending this game can be difficult to someone who's expecting a fast-paced action heavy title. This is a slow-paced grueling experience in which little details may matter in everything you do and death and failure are a core mechanic of the game.

When I did my playthrough I learned as much as I could about the game and despite having a good time while failing and dying repeatedly I decided that I don't have enough time to finish this game legitimately. So I powered up a cheating application and made it significantly more difficult for me to fail. Interestingly enough, it is still possible to lose in this game while having unlimited health and your cards costing nothing. I used this as a vehicle to explore the story and it made my time with the game much more enjoyable, though it's not what I'd recommend for everyone. What I experienced when I finally met that shadowy figure face to face changed my perspective on what a game could be.

This is an artistic masterpiece with an unfortunate facade of RNG based difficulty. While the game does reward your failures and make it somewhat easier to make it through as you learn the mechanics, the fact that it is still a card game inevitably means that some cards will be better than other cards and if you don't draw the good ones when you need them, you might fail your run. It would be nice if the difficulty could ease up a bit more as you play more but the reason why there is no such system is a core part of the story.

There's a reason why this game has overwhelmingly positive reviews and if you're interested in it and invested in having a good time despite failing repeatedly you should get it. If you're looking to power through this game, ignoring all the story, you'll be doing yourself a disservice but no one can tell you how to have fun so go for it anyway.
