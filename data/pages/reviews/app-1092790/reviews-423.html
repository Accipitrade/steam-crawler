\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHIS REVIEW WILL CONTAIN SOME GAMEPLAY SPOILERS

Inscryption is a fantastic blend of roguelite card battle and horror genres with some minor escape room elements. And while gameplay is not as deep or complex as something like Slay the Spire, it is quite well executed with plenty of varied events inbetween battles. But where Inscryption outclasses all other similar games and, frankly speaking most games in general, is atmosphere. Old school visuals, unsettling score and haunted cabin aesthetics work together amazingly. Thats why act 1 is one of my favorite moments I ever experienced in gaming.
GAMEPLAY SPOILERS INCOMING NOW
Yes, act 1. Game is divided into 3 distinct acts and if you are not familiar with Mullins'previous work the change from act 1 to act 2 will probably be quite a shock for you. Not saying acts 2 and 3 are bad. On the contrary, they are quite good but following that fantastic opening act is not easy. Gameplay changes are not as drastic as in The Hex (Mullins' previous game) as they are still centered around card battles but the style and atmosphere changes quite a bit. Act 3 is more similar to act 1 with some gameplay differences.
So if you are coming into this expecting horror version of Slay the Spire (I dont blame you, it was more or less marketed this way) dont.

KACEEY'S MOD
That being said, Mullins has heard the cries of act 1 roguelite fans and gave us a free expansion which is unlocked after beating the game. Kaceey's mod is basically what people unfamiliar with his work expected Inscryption to be. Now both fantastic main story mode and roguelite mode are both in the same game making Inscryption a truly fantastic package.

VERDICT
I may raise the score to perfect in the future, depending on how addicting Kaceey's mod will turn out to be but for now Inscryption is a solid..
9/10\t\t\t\t\t
