\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's fine - but it's also repetitive, tediously so, and uninspired conceptually. The core card game (which you will play, over and over again, in painfully similar but tediously different forms) is not bad - I especially enjoyed Act 1, with its tremendous visual/aesthetic style, fun writing, and intriguing mystery. The problem is that you play it again in Act 2 - and again in Act 3. Over and over again. With minor, ultimately quite pointless, differences.

And after you unravel the mystery of Act I, the story becomes vastly less compelling. It enters the dev's trademark Meta Level of Games as Storytelling Art Form - which is, frankly, boring. Oh wow you mean the game I'm playing actually harbors a dark secret? Oh no, there's a corrupted data file called OLD_DATA portending doom? Gee whiz - the game is evolving as I play it?

Yawn.

I went in to this game completely blind, and I was, at first, engrossed - and then richly disappointed. It's not a bad game - just hardly the mindblowing wowie popowie zOMG experience it's cracked up to be in countless, breathless reviews and commentary.

Which is the confusion and frustration at the heart of my experience here. The story unravels, and I expected something amazing, but I got - just a card game, pointlessly extended across three ultimately uninteresting story acts.

Kaycee's Mod, an unlockable gameplay mod where you JUST play a more challenging, rebalanced version of the first act is probably all this game, ultimately, needed to be.

Are there better card games? Sure. Is this one good? Yes. Does it deserve its reputation as a masterpiece in video game storytelling and a "love letter" to video games as such? Absolutely not.

Video games tell stories by having stories - not by being, in a complicated, redundant, and tedious way, a story about themselves being a story while having a story.

Imagine reading a book that's about a guy reading a book about reading books. Good use of a college degree to perform the literary equivalent of parlor tricks, but nothing worth writing home about.
