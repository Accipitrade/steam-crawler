\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI LOVE INSCRYPTION. I came into it thinking "Well itll be this cute little card game and its got overwhelmingly positive reviews, so it must be worth a shot" (if you still think this PLEASE go in blind it is definitely the best way to play, SPOILERS AHEAD). And it turned out to be worth it in every way.

I love the deteriorating ARG elements, I live for this stuff and it had me whipping my phone out to convert binary and text and trying to decode hidden messages, which I absolutely love. Its the same feeling I had when playing doki doki, and I think this game deserves to stand up there with it. Even without those elements, I found myself playing act 1 for 4 hours straight and looked up to notice it was 2 in the morning (not something that has happened in a while). No music or TV in the background either, just straight concentration on beating the ♥♥♥♥ out of the Prospector and Angler. Act 1 (while not my favorite in terms of story progression) was definitely my favorite in terms of the base card game, I couldn't get enough of it. That being said I did find it a little easy, sure I died a few times but overall I wish there was a higher difficulty setting unlocked after beating, or something along those lines. This is coupled with a few....loop holes such as my accidental discovery of the immortal squirrels totem, which took my through act 1 at lightning speed, I literally couldn't be beaten. Moving on to act 2 and 3, I love the ARG progression in them, I think the little tid bits of recordings were just the right amount of creepy and info for first time players to cling to their seats wanting to know what happens next and what already happened. However, I was not a big fan of the retro sections card games. It felt as if I was thrown into an entirely new system and left on my own to swim or sink, and even after figuring it out, I still wasnt a big fan. I suppose on the bright side it gave me more appreciation for how good Act 1s is. P03s card game was better, but still not as good as Act 1s. I enjoyed P03s card system, although it did feel like that section dragged on a little long, as did the retro section before it. Though, as much as im pointing out flaws I feel the need to remind that I throughly enjoyed this game and plan to complete more runs, especially of Act 1. To add one last thing in, I really craved a challenging boss, not to say the others werent in their own way, but maybe having to battle Luke at the end, with a completely chaotic mashup of all 4 decks, bones, squirrels, the whole shabang (that would be really really awesome, as DLC maybe hmmm?). To summarize, a few flaws but a really good game I really really enjoyed. Buy it\t\t\t\t\t
