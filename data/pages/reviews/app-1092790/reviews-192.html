Inscryption was possibly one of the most emotional and best ending to a video game I have ever played

I feel like the sigils is what makes Inscryption very unique from other card games they are really interesting in the fact that you can grab a card to sacrifice its sigils onto another card later is really cool and there are so many varieties and the fact that they are simple to understand just elevates the gameplay even more since you have to think about the sigils on your enemy's side and what they do the game makes this easy you just have to right click on a sigil to go to where it is in the rulebook

Inscryption is probably one of the greatest games I've ever played the cards, the atmosphere, sigils the music they are all great it took me a few days to actually fully collect my thoughts after beating the game there were so many twists that I was not expecting this game mixes

the card-based roguelike gameplay into something very unique the First Act is so unbalanced in both ways using the Death Cards you received you can make a really powerful deck you can't say the same about

act 3 act three is all about balance and following the rules which just made it really unfun and when you die you just go back to the previous checkpoint to fight the same guys again with the same moves so that part of the game is really repetitive and boring

but the part after that was so good

now let's talk about the ending
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
the ending

this is possibly one of the best endings to a video game that I have ever played Leshy ripped off p03's head in a unexpected twist while he was monologuing but then in another twist
Grimora starts deleting the game and this has you going through every scribe in a battle but all are cut short due to them being deleted all of them are very sad but Leshy is a exception

despite me dying multiple times despite me being extremely mad about beating him and having to restart because I did not grab the eyeball to get the film roll and I had to play through the entire game again I saw him as nothing more than a thing that is blocking me from progressing through the story I did not like them as a character at that time

but when I return to the cabin after beating Grimora I could do nothing but tear up a bit as I heard the familiar music and when I was given my old deck that's when I broke the moment the scale disappeared is the moment I figured out that Leshy was not the Beast I thought he was

he was just a dude living a hobbit lifestyle that just wanted to play his game he wasn't actually killing people he was aware that you were a person on the other side of the screen the prospector, the angler, the Trapper/Trader they were just merely characters that Leshy played and he let you play his game for his entertainment and for your own but when I realize this it was already too late

we continued to play a couple more rounds but when the bell disappeared I knew it was time we shook one another's hand like we were old friends the best dungeon master is about to be deleted right in front of me,

Inscryption somehow made me really sad to see Leshy go, a character that I didn't even care about at the beginning a character that I just saw as a wall to get through the story a character that made me so pissed off because he killed me despite me beating him

but yet I cared about him somehow and that's how you make a character's ending

but that's not all there is an extra game mode called Kaycee's Mod

I can't describe it very well you can go here

anyways those are my thoughts
