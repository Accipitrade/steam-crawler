TL;DR
-------------

A roguelike deckbuilder that is NOT like Slay the Spire. Actual unique gameplay.
Amazing execution, allows for much experimentation in deckbuilding.

-----------------------
Horror aspect
----------------
If you are wondering if the game is too scary for you: probably not. No jumpscares or stuff that haunts you at night (I assume). However great atmosphere, does have blood & gore scenes.


-------------------------
Long review:
----------------

One of the best games I played in a long time.

The only strategy horror game I know.

Recommended for: People who like roguelike deckbuilders (Slay the spire, ...) and games with a dark/horror theme (no jumpscares or anything, just general atmosphere, has blood and gore scenes)

Game length: Can't estimate yet how long the story is.

I really like that the amazing core gameplay is surrounded with an overarching story and mystery.
The thing I enjoyed the most is that it allows for suprising and unique secret gameplay interactions (can't say to much for spoiler purposes, but you can ask in the comments if anyone is curious about some interactions I found).

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is to deckbuilders what Portal was to puzzle platformers.

I don't say that lightly; I have a reverence for Portal that elevates it far above most games in my eyes. But I'll explain:

Portal took a great idea for a puzzle platformer and built an incredibly immersive, entertaining, and enthralling world around it utilizing a slow-burn style to continuously pique the player's interest and attention, ultimately delivering a unique, unforgettable, and one-of-a-kind experience that stands in its own class of quality.

Was Portal perfectly balanced? No. Was Portal a long game? No. Was it the absolute pinnacle of brain-busting puzzle gaming? No. But my first run of Portal was one of the most fun gaming experiences I've ever had, and I would argue that it's nearly a perfect game.

This game is absolutely incredible. But the less you know about it, the better. I highly recommend it to anybody who is a fan of deckbuilding games, especially if you also enjoy immersive single-player experiences.\t\t\t\t\t
