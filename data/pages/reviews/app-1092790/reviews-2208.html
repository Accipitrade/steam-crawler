\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this game is worth trying yourself, but I can't bring myself to finish it.

I love every game this guy's ever made. It's my ♥♥♥♥. Pony Island and The Hex (The Hex especially) are waaay up there on my favorite games list. I loved this game, too, until Act 3. I honestly don't know wtf the dev was thinking with Act 3. It's just a worse version of the two Acts that came before, with less personal input and customization, less interesting lore (which I understand fits the antagonist's whole deal - but the lack of lore still makes it less engaging), and less motivation to even see where the story's going. I feel like I'm banging my head against a brick wall for all the good time spent in Act 3 is doing me. The horrible Dark Souls-y bonfire system just draws out the pain, whereas with a rogue-like at least I can start a fresh deck upon death.

Honestly, I just don't have any motivation to finish the damn game, because the antagonist is now so much less interesting than they were in Act 1. Computer-faced, logical, "strategy is everything" is such a boring bad guy that it's not even remotely gripping me anymore. The strength of the game got immediately dropped after Act 1 - the potentials for deck building in Act 2 kept me around, and at least I was still getting some lore. Then Act 3 just murders any potential for fun and sticks you back in the thick of the literal worst version of the Inscryption card game I could conceive of, with the most boring aesthetic imaginable - tech/computers. It's just not interesting compared to the atmosphere of the game's opening, and I really *really* wanted to like it.\t\t\t\t\t
