Just imagine you play the hex or pony island but randomly after half an hour, you lose and have to replay that section. That is what adding a card game did to the formula.

I am a guy that has 750 hours in Slay the spire so I don't mind playing a card game over and over... but this is a bad card game and that's where issues arise. The main mechanic is that you need to deal 5 damage to win, if the opponent dealt 4 damage then you have to deal 9 damage. That mechanic made sense in the game-jam version where it was all about sacrificing your body to win the game but that is no longer a core mechanic and instead pushed aside to random item drops which you don't even have most of the time. I am going to leave it at that because more would be a spoiler.

But as I said in the beginning: at the start I felt like quitting. Later on you get the ability to deck build and that makes rng not really an issue anymore since you can easily build for each encounter. So after that it became a much more enjoyable experience.\t\t\t\t\t

This game is great. Its honestly my favourite game of this year. Get it and you won't regret it.


This is a game that scratches many itches for me. I mean a deckbuilding rougelite card battler with great music and graphics as well as excellent story and world building? Sign me up.

Possible Spoilers Ahead


The game is split into 3 acts which all have unique gameplay and graphics. My favourite gameplay act was definitely act 1 as I felt that it was extremely fun playing around with the different mechanics to try and whip up the craziest combo for a run. I personally feel that even without it being a part of the story it can act as a standalone game.

Act 2 stands as my favourite looking act and reminded me alot of an old pokemon tcg game on the gameboy colour or duel master games. I especially enjoyed this act as was alot of fun crafting different decks and playing with different synergies. This is also the act where most of the characters of the game are introduced.

Act 3 was focused on combining the deckbuilding aspect of act 1 and the gamplay mechanics of act 2 and was relatively fun but I had more fun in the other acts. Towards the end we are able to interact with all the different scribes towards the end of their lives and it really allowed me to appreciate how much thought has gone into them.

All in all this was honestly a very well done, perfected game for me and I recommend this game to anyone who is interested.\t\t\t\t\t

While the full price may be steep for a seemingly small game, this game offers a lot in regards to content and replayability.

I don't even know where to start with this game. It's unique in every way possible, drawing no inspiration from anything even remotely similar to it. I sadly didn't start this game blind, I watched an hours worth of a playthrough before I dropped what I was doing and bought this game. Even still, this game has wildly exceeded my expectations. Go into it blind.

As other reviews have mentioned, Inscryption easily tops any game I've played this year, and will consistently be on my recommendations to anyone looking forward to unique, jawdropping games.\t\t\t\t\t
