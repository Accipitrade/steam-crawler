\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey had it nailed in the first half. Having a clearly deranged villain force you to play a card game that may or may not be the history of how you got into this thoroughly creepy situation is a great way to create a story that incorporates the card based game mechanic. The little puzzles you are encouraged to do outside of the card game are a really nice break and compliment to overall creepy vibe. The card game itself is unique and engaging as well. My only criticism is that there is a second and third part to the game that are, ok, but break with the suspenseful atmosphere of the first third. It's worth playing all the way through, I just found that the first half was an absolute delight while the back half was mediocre.\t\t\t\t\t
