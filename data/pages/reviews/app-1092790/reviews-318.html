But even still, the game is highly captivating and charming to play through. It's challenging as hell, I'm not gonna lie. Especially once you touch the Kaycee's Mod side of things (that skull storm achievement was hell and I think I hate bears now lol). But it's equally rewarding, and suuuuper fun to make really busted card combinations.

Inscyption is very much a "if at first you don't succeed: die, die again" kind of game. Every failed run is a learning experience and it - as well as exploration - will ensure you're a bit better prepared and equipped for your next run. So don't be discouraged if you fail a few runs; it's all part of the progression. :] In saying that, I would suggest holding off on touching Kaycee's Mod until you've completed the main story; both for lore reasons, and so you have a better, broader, understanding of how your cards, sigils, items, and totems work. It'll make things a bit easier and a smidge less aggravating. Because you will die a *lot* more in that than in the main game, thanks to the challenge modifiers. I adore the final boss that replaces Leshy, [Royal.] Real vibrant personality; made the fight a legitimately fun experience to go through.\t\t\t\t\t
