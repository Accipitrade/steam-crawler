\r\n\t\t\t\t\t* no spoilers until marked * Inscryption is a fresh inspired perspective on the rouge-like genre and should be played by any who remotely enjoy deck-builders. A set of eyes and facades become your opponent the same way any good game master sets you out on a journey to become the master of your own game. The cards feel personal after some time and if treated properly, will change and grow with you. For any looking for a good mystery there’s much more to the story than who’s behind the masks. The escape room aspect gives a pleasant interlude to the dread of what the mysterious figure will throw at you next. Depending on your tolerance for swearing this really can be a game for anyone in the market for another out-of-the-park Devolver backed indie title.
* spoilers ahead * I preface this section with a reiteration of my recommendation, as apparent by my hours this sort of criticism is not a deal breaker. While the base concepts for the second and third act are attractive in a vacuum, from the more open independent deck building of act two, to the “hoh hoh you let me into your computer” spook of the third act boss battles, the first act has set the standard for the player, a standard not met by the subversive meta-play changes. To elaborate: the first act’s escape room design along with the gradual introduction of new sigils and items through death and thus, a clean slate, is traded out in the second act for a tidal wave of new mechanics almost alienated from one another if not for a handful of middle-men cards. Simple grinding strategies take center stage as the player gathers up every card to make a beautiful deck only to draw a poor first hand à la Gwent. The third act requires meticulous deck planning optimized by NOT taking every opportunity to gain cards but grinding once again to earn a card that will put your money in exorbitant numbers. Looking at the gameplay stats tells it all, while (at time of writing) 79% of players beat the final boss of the first act, but only a little over half of these players beat the final boss of the third act. These concepts are great but they would almost serve better as DLC, as sacrilegious as it sounds.
This is however a set of complaints utterly turned on their heads when factoring in the beauty and brilliance of Kaycee’s Mod. The mod recently became a part of the base game, not requiring the usual 3rd party installation and the addition makes the title. Every positive aspect of act one is elaborated on and improved, new sigils, cards, items, lore, challenges and even different starting hands makes the mod a love letter to all those unfortunately disenchanted by the later acts. If you enjoyed this game’s introduction for its almost puzzle-solving type card play I deeply encourage you to try Kaycee’s mod.
Mods and acts aside this is an excellent game and I cannot recommend it enough in what feels like a somewhat dry season for games.\t\t\t\t\t
