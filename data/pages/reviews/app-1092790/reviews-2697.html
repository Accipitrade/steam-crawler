\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe biggest criticism I've read in other reviews is because people felt misled by the trailers. To a degree, this is true, it's not a puzzle game. There are some small puzzles in it, but hardly any. This is a card game done in the style of Pony Island. It's not like Pony Island, but you can tell it's the same developer seconds into the game.

This game feels like it's trying to be a cross between Hades and Pony Island in the form of a card game. As weird as that combination sounds. The antagonist is like the ultimate DND dungeon master. He has prepared a mind blowing amount of props and can cycle through them in a way no human could ever compare to.

The game is very fair if you know what you are doing. Unfortunately I didn't, and learned from watching various streamers how badly I did at the start.
My biggest criticism is with Act 2 onward. All those amazing graphics you see in the video and the creative DND like setup is all in Act 1. Act2 and forward go back to the low definition Pony Island stuff. Those are good too, but they aren't anywhere near the level of quality I was coming to enjoy in the first act.

I am somewhat disappointed with the cabin puzzles. I was expecting a more integrated experience there. The parts I've played feels like a cheap add-on section. I was hoping that you'd activate some effect in the game and the host would make you play a puzzle in the cabin as a result. But that never happened. Instead it's just hiding various unlockables that'll make future runs easier.\t\t\t\t\t

SPOILERS IN THE REVIEW FOR THE SAKE OF LETTING PEOPLE KNOW THE GAME IS NOT JUST WHAT THEY SEE IN THE TRAILERS

The Good:
- Amazing meta atmosphere like all Daniel's games;
- You can pour hours on it;
- Lots of secrets and cool stuff;
- 3 sections! First one being the best one!
- On the first section you can actually work your way with most skill and less on luck;
- Cool concept in general;

The Cons:
- Aspect Ratio issues that weren't there in the Demo, and are being ignored despite a lot of people complaining about it;
- Unless you're a deckbuilding experienced master, stop at the end of Part 1 (after you beat the Cabin section). Because when it goes to part 1, unless you used the cheap Ouroboros tactic, ♥♥♥♥ gets intense based more on LUCK than anything. Wasted hours of my day to small to almost none progress;
- No difficulty settings. You can't fully enjoy what you paid for if you can't beat the difficulty set by the dev;
- Again, part 1 is fun, the rest is not. Not sure about part 3, though;
- Hype bummer. I've been waiting this game since the very first prototype and always have been a fan of Daniel's games, they were all amazing. This one, not so much.

That's all I can say, and too late for me to refund now. Good luck!\t\t\t\t\t
