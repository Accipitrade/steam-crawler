\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved the first few hours of inscryption. The creepy aesthetic, the mystery of the dealer, the cabin and the talking cards, the puzzles, everything. I was so impressed with all of it.

But then, it was all taken away. Everything that made me love the game was abandoned for the sole purpose of fueling a meta plot about how the game characters are self aware. The game reveals its "original" form, which is a top down pixelated rpg maker style adventure. At this point all of the charm is gone as well as my desire to play. It didn't help that the cards changed to fit the style; they were visually a mess and impossible to identify with a glance. After you slog through the pixelated part of the game, it goes back to the first-person sitting-at-a-table kind of game, which is a step in the right direction, but the creepy aesthetic is still gone.

It's such a shame that an incredibly well executed concept was sacrificed to give life to something far inferior- ANOTHER "genre defying" meta plot from Dan Mullins.

In the words of the Stoat:

"Total misplay."\t\t\t\t\t
