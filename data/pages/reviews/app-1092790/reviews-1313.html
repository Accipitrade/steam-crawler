\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst-off, I want to say that I'm a fan of deckbuilders (I started with Slay the Spire and then Monster Train). I wanted something new so I searched steam and happened upon this game, knowing nothing at all about what to expect. Now that I've played it, I'm going to split this review into 3 sections: the blind section, the relatively non-spoiler section and then a slightly more spoiler-heavy section.

Blind section:

This game is a slay-the-spire-like with unique mechanics. Gameplay consists of a small board in a retro 3d world where you and the opponent can play just a few cards at a time. Each card has some amount of health and some amount of attack power. Cards on either side can do damage to the card in front of them, or the opponent if no card is in front of them. Doing damage to the opponent tips a scale in your favor, and taking damage (without the defense of another card) tips it in the opponent's favor. There are various sigils that each card can have (or be buffed with) that make interesting modifications on these mechanics. Tipping the scale +5 in your favor results in a win, tipping the scale +5 in the opponent's favor results in a loss. There's also a metagame - you can step back from the card table a walk around the retro oldschool MACOS-like room solving various puzzles the progress between games and affect he gameplay. I won't say anymore here - the best way to play this game is blind so if that's enough to interest you than I encourage you to stop reading and figure out the rest for yourself.

Relatively non-spoiler section:

This game begins by focusing on playing a particular slay-the-spire-like card game against an NPC named Leshy. I spent about 20 hours playing against Leshy over and over, and I thought this was a pretty good deckbuilder but wasn't quite sure where the "overwhelmingly positive" reviews were coming from. After spending some time solving the metagame puzzles, I discovered that there is MUCH more to this game than meets the eye. I won't spoil anything further in this section of the review - other than to say that it's very much worth figuring it our for yourself.

If none of that convinced you, let me get into the more critical review. The next section will utterly spoil the game, and in my opinion it's an experience best had blind. But if you were planning on skipping this one anyway, I might as well tell you about exactly what to expect.

The spoiler-heavy section:

I nearly stopped playing this game after 20 hours of what I initially thought this game was. I figured there might be a few interesting story-elements to unlock, but I'd otherwise seen it all. Boy was I wrong. After solving all of the metagame puzzles and *truly* defeating Leshy, I couldn't have been more surprised by what happened. I was thrown into a 2d pixel-art world where my avatar had to go around challenging different contenders to card battles. These battles had the same basic mechanics as the earlier games, but with uifferent cards/sigils that totally changed the strategy of the game. Part of the lore revealed in this section was that there were 4 different "Scrybes" who created the cards: Leshy, C03, Grimora, and Magnificus. All of them have radically different decks and playstyles. After defeating all of them in this 2d world, P03 takes over and you're thrust into another, total different, 3d world. At this point I got excited - This game has an incredible amount of content, am I really going to play a game as in-depth as Leshy's original challange 4 times? (The answer ended up being no, but it's a small disappointment in an otherwise amazing experience). C03 did in fact have an entirely in-depth game with it's own metagame and all of the above. But it took me a bit less time to complete than the original Leshy campaign. After that, a few storyline events make it clear that the game ending soon. You do get to enter Grimora's 3d world briefly, and Magnificus' 3d world even more briefly, but their experience's were not nearly as in-depth as the other 2. While that was slightly disappointing, at that point I had already far-exceeded the amount of content I expected this game to have. So my final thought is this:

While this game has not transcended physical form and reached ultimate enlightenment, it is still an amazing experience. I highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
