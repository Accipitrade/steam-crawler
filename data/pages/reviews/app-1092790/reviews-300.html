But if you find it is your thing, like I did, despite not really being into card games, you'll be immensely grateful for diving in blind. I certainly am now, having finished it after an unforgettable holiday streak. So, go ahead and play it. The rest of my review is just an attempt to sum up, spoiler-free, why I loved it.

Inscryption is a phenomenal example of creative storytelling through engaging gameplay, it achieves what few titles do: it opens up and grows under your fingertips, as if organically wrapping itself around your skill level, giving you just enough learning opportunities for you to grow into it, keeping you engaged even more. The game rewards you as you progress through it, while hinting at a lot more that's going on beyond the apparent. It transforms and morphs into a much larger, richer and complex organism as you become a more skilled player, not through blind repetition, but through learning and absorbing the nuances of its unique ecosystem. It throbs with its own magnificent life, plays with your expectations and swearves your attempts at second-guessing where it might lead next, offering surprising directions with seemingly unending artistic creativity. It is an immensely satisfying gaming experience through and through and an achievement of game design that deserves to be savoured, praised, and supported.

You can only get so many great gaming experiences and this is definitely one of the rarest and most unique ones.\t\t\t\t\t
