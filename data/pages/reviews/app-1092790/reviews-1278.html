This is one trippy adventure so far and I wish bigger developers made games like this instead of the standard "safe" way of doing things. This is why Indie gaming is so important because people are willing to take chances. Back when SNES/NES was around you would get a ton of experimentation which brought us a lot of variety.

If you don't like Rogue Lights I would still recommend to play this game. I am not the biggest fan of them because you repeat a lot of the same stuff to get there but this game helps you along I think. This is more an experience than anything else. I would recommend this game to anyone and don't look up spoilers or how to get through anything. Just sit back when you have some time to kill and revel in the strangeness of it all.
