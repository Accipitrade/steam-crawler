This game has flaws.

The art, style, atmosphere, and originality make it special. It's very inspired, and that's what saves the game.

Otherwise it's a roguelike deck building borrowing from Slay The Spire, except much simpler and limited, and having the same weakness : you can just forego 70% of the cards and game mechanics to only play with epic overpowered cards. But since this game is much simpler, it's much easier to do this than in Slay The Spire, and you can one up all bosses.
Once you've cracked the game mechanics though, which happens early, there is still a lot of playing to do. And it becomes boring.

Which leads to the other major flaw : it seems that the author wanted to tell a story first, and then create a game. Since the game is simple, he ran out of play content but he still had a lot of story to cover. So the game becomes repetitive, and I lost interest before finishingt it.

I still recommend it because the game is good while it lasts.\t\t\t\t\t
