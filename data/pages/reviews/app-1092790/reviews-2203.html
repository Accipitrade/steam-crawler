Much the same way earlier iterations of the internet had exotic oddities and random things to stumble across, Inscryption allows players to discover the mystery and menace of the darkest corners of the web.

What is unique is the complex narrative that presents layers to characters, odd switch ups that subvert the player's expectations, and a complete narrative that feels satisfying when the credits roll. I did have a few times when the literal luck of the draw frustrated me, but such is the nature of card battlers, and Inscryption's puzzle-like battles feel akin to cracking a safe; each run at a difficult battle becomes a rewarding experience as the sacrifices chain together and your progress drops into place like weighty tumblers falling in a lock.

Sacrifice your expectations, offer your time on the altar of Inscryption.

It's only a game.
