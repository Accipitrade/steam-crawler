\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInscryption is a story focused card game with heavy puzzle elements. If the three of those together appeal to you, you'll probably enjoy it immensely.

Players should anticipate some moderate horror elements and violent themes, but largely stylized so as to avoid anything excessively graphic. The general aesthetic is wonderfully well designed, effectively building atmosphere while looking nice enough to enjoy artistically.

Overall, the TCG aspect of the game is fun and generally well designed, being both easy to learn and satisfying to play, although ultimately being highly exploitable and a bit shallow. This comes across as intentional in the context of the narrative, but can be frustrating for players looking specifically for the card battler aspect.

If you've had any experience in deck building games like Slay the Spire or Loop Hero, you'll be able to finish the main story in around 15 to 20 hours if you take your time and look to complete optional objectives/puzzles. Well worth the price tag, particularly if you can get the game via a bundle or sale.

Try to avoid story spoilers if you can, as it works best as a blind experience in my opinion. I will say that while I very much enjoyed the ride, the story left me with the same feeling as the card game: highly enjoyable, while failing to delve as deeply as it made me hope.

All in all, an absolutely excellent indie game, that's willing to take several risks not often seen in contemporary game development. All this together earns it a strong recommendation.\t\t\t\t\t
