\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start by saying that I'm fairly critical of card games on the computer. But DAMN this game is pretty dang good. The atmosphere is on point, the aesthetic and hits/ spoilers are clever and enjoyable, the puzzles are easy but intriguing enough to make you want to figure them out (You'll get around to it ;3).

Though some combinations are a bit over-powered (such as Squirrel + 3 blood) + the big guy who's a 7/7. Or upgraded sharks with 5 damage, there are plenty of runs to be had that force you to think about your moves. The bosses (as far into the game as I am) are unique and have a fun challenge to them, but can be easily cheesed with the above strategy with little effort. Work around? Just don't do it?

I'm not finished with the game yet, and truthfully I don't know how much left of the game there is. But if you are thinking of getting the game, or are just starting out? Make sure to stretch your legs and walk around between fights-- You'll understand once you start playing!

8.5/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMINOR SPOILERS AHEAD - The game is fun, engaging, and interesting and while it falls flat for me in some places it is still worth the experience. Maybe wait for a sale if it doesn't appeal to you from the demo.

The first few hours of this game are engaging, intense, and full of super interesting mechanics. The card mechanics are simple enough to make understanding the rules easy, but complex enough to make it difficult to fully master. The visuals and music are great and the cabin is a great set piece, creating a bleak and creepy atmosphere. The progressing story happening both in your face and in the background kept me engaged and wanting to know more.

While the rest of the game is enjoyable, for me it never managed to engage me as much as the first few hours did. In the effort of not wanting to spoil anything just know this game is much more than what is advertised. It's a multi-media multi-layered story that changes around so much seeing what the game would turn into next became the driving force that kept me playing. The story, unfortunately, loses me a bit as it continues, and the ending is a bit lackluster without spoiling too much.

Overall, Inscryption is a very fun and engaging experience, especially in the first few hours. While my enjoyment of the game started to dip after act 1, it is still a worthwhile experience. Playing through it one time took me nearly 8 hours and it should be known that there isn't much replay value (unless I'm missing something). $20 may be a bit expensive, if any of the cons of this game seem like a big deal, I'd wait for a sale.

7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis gets the slimmest recommendation. Gameplay is pretty good (people used to digital collectible card games will fit right in, especially if you're the kind that do puzzle challenges in them), but I can see why (despite calls for it) this would not be workable as a multiplayer game, or have much longevity single player beyond the ~20 hours of playing to finish the story. It's more of a "okay, you can do this" and "cool, that's how that works" kind of game than one where you spend hours exploring deck ideas and creating your own personal inventive combos and themes.

The UI has snags and roadblocks that isn't quite justified by "it's retro!" or "it's indie!", e.g. at times you cannot expect that you can save and quit the game without losing out on parts of the story, a pretty big bummer.

The story itself is... passable. It will appeal to the kind of people that like to sit late at night and tell each other ghost stories and such. Certainly made a lot of sense to play it around Halloween. But it's not deep, and it doesn't go anywhere unexpected. The story very much *wants* to hint at something deeper, but there just isn't enough (hardly any) worldbuilding to carry it to the finish line (and the ending doesn't really have the impact they're clearly going for), either in the surface or the meta layer. The atmospherics are pretty good at least.

I can recommend it if what you're looking for is something akin to single player puzzle packs in well-established digital-only card games, or if you /really/ love ghost stories and/or the whole aesthetic of meta games.\t\t\t\t\t
