\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you play a lot, you need this.

I will update this as I use more items over time, but so far this is what Iv'e noticed:

The Volkatronixx, which fires electric bullets, has a 100% chance to stagger biters and virals on hit. Not only that, but the stagger effect is Area of Effect, so one single bullet has the capability of stopping a group of 2-6 zombies for about a two seconds. This also takes any rifle, so you can take a stinky ol police semi auto rifle, and turn it into a group-stunning full auto death machine. The scope also does add magnification, so it's much better than iron sights. One very important thing to note is you can kill your teammates with this gun, so be very careful and try not to be a bad person.

Update: So, I've gotten around to using the revolver, the Vindicator 2000. The revolver combat wise would be the same as a six-shooter. Same accuracy, and damage, for your respective Legend rank and firearm skill. Where this weapon shines over all other revolvers, however, is that this revolver carries 15 rounds. Yup, you read that right, it doesn't even make sense, but there it is and it's awesome. You can easily clear up viral groups in one cycle with this. (Just found out, the magazine size is dependent on the weapon you make it from. Since, you can make this revolver from pistols, I suggest always using a pistol with high magazine capacity so you can surpass 6-8 shots per cycle.)

Now for the Neon Boomerang Blade: The Blademaxx. The Blademaxx unlike many other re-skins can be crafted from a variety of blades, but also can be further crafted into blueprints, much unlike the L4D weapon skins. For example, you can turn your one handed sword into a Blademaxx and then attach Boltor Poison or something like that and King/Clicker mods, so the skin won't hinder your weapons max damage output like the L4D and similar ones do.

The character skin/buggy skin is just personal opinion. I personally wouldn't wear the character skin over other glowing skins in the game, because from the 1st person perspective, you're not often seeing the effects as much as something like the Trick-or-Treater. Otherwise it is a very nice Miami 80's themed skin.

As for the buggy skin, I think it's one of the better glowing skins available. The tires have a pink glow around them and the car has plenty of very bright stripes of white neon that you can see very well from any angle in your buggy. Very visually pleasing.

Overall I highly recommend this DLC. All of the weapons feel great to use and are extremely viable for almost anything you throw at them. I'd go as far as to say the two firearms outclass all of the other weapons in their respective classes. For anyone who plays Dying Light a lot or really enjoys the game, this is a must have. No doubt!\t\t\t\t\t
