\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is far from perfect, and one can't help but see where bugs or little annoyances that really shouldn't be there. These include relatively small things the mouse pointer not disappearing, limited (and sometimes confusing) physical and clothing customisation in created wrestlers. There are also bigger issues like the limited online player base, lack of crossplay, and lack of a lobby system, which together makes it hard to find online matches.

That said, there is also a lot to love about this game if you are a fan of AEW. Lots of love went into the wrestlers and the career mode, and it really celebrates the rise of AEW. There is a pretty deep roster, and the wrestlers feel and look as they should. I can have a lot of fun even playing against CPUs. Although the physical customisation is lacking for created wrestlers, the movesets, traits, and skills go pretty deep, and you can end up with a very authentic feeling wrestler.

The game could be a little or even a lot better with the right patches and some potential add-ons, but even as it is, I think it is pretty good, and I keep going back to it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt its core, Fight Forever is a great game. There's a good selection of characters, an easy to pick up (yet hard to completely master immediately) grappling system and the little references, taunts, and weapons that AEW fans will love. Older fans will love how familiar AEW:FF feels to N64 wrestling titles, like VPW2, WCW: Revenge and No Mercy. The only major drawback is the lack of unique match types - more options, rule customization and gimmicks are desperately needed.

I have not tested online as no players were available when i attempted to match. I preferred playing through the ROAD TO ELITE mode with AEW characters. Having said that, the create-a-wrestler mode is anemic and should be completely re-done/revamped/reimagined as it's impossible to create unique characters with such a limited selection of assets. Body parts, body morphing, clothing types, etc are all pathetic here. The mode should've been held until it was ready for prime time.

Visually, Fight Forever is adequate. The animations, however, are simply impressive, and the game is running smooth as Orange Cassidy on my PC. i7-9750 6 core CPU, 32GB RAM, Nvidea RTX 3060Ti 12GB.


Wish list:
-Ability to slow matches down or change damage sliders to create more epic, drawn out matches. Since the core mechanics of a great wrestling game are here, I believe this is possible. AEW on TV is known for wrestlers sometimes kicking out of multiple finishing moves/after big spots, so the game should have this too.

If I am 100% honest, the gameplay and minigame are carrying this game the most. It is super fun to play the game. But that is mostly it. This is not too bad of a wrestling game but not a game I would go in all the time. Fun to play with others, not so much solo. It is missing some features like "Universe Mode", but the Custom Character Road To Elite story is okay, I was not really into the story, the only thing that got me "Oh? This is interesting" is when you got to the end of Challenge Yourself.
JukeBox or the music list is good, and all the songs hit hard, at WWE 2K games there aren't many songs I like maybe hear the same 10 songs whereas in this I have maybe 30 or so.

-[Custom Characters)-

The customization is not that good really, not much you can customize and it is not that deep either. The big negative about this is you have to complete Road To Elite with each custom character you create, which could take up to 6-10 hours, depending on you. You can't have separate stats between Road To Elite, Online, and Exhibition. Now I think Road To Elite and Online stats should be the same overall. Because I imagine if you have max stats on Exhibition it won't be really fun to play against Online.
There are unshockable though it is mostly taunts or poses. Which I think is fine really.
You can't add custom decals or details, there isn't much you can add to make a character you want and overall it is just okay.

-[Custom Arenas]-

This is a bit more customization-able but it isn't that deep. No custom decals are making this quite bland if you wish to make a more wild arena you wish to make. It is just okay.


-[Voice Acting]-

To be honest, everyone sounded bland and didn't sound that good. They talked more than anything, hopefully, if they plan to make another they can make everyone sound more interested.

-[Story Patch I got]-

EDIT: I played on hard difficulty.

For playing blind, not going into how to play, I did decent with a 12-11 score at the end. I did not get all the endings for all of them but most of the scenes were just okay. I started with "Unlikely Friendship" at All Out, then got "Inner Circle Breaks Out" at Full Gear, then I got "Relationship Problems" at Revolution, and ended it with "Challenge Yourself" at Double Or Nothing.
Most of them I got backstabbed, which I always found funny, and eventually won World Championship. To be honest, the most interesting part of this whole story I got was that my character was going through self-doubt and fought against it then manages to win. The only part where I was interested.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview: The fact they did not even give us all the roster parts makes no sense. What is worse is that half of what they give us is colour locked or you cannot add layers to them. Why can I not put horns on most of the full-face masks (why are body art/tattoos so limited)? The more I think about it, the less I can defend it.

No way they sat there and thought this was okay... right?

I did not buy the game thinking it would be on the level of 2K. But this is silly levels of lacking in major areas. The fact you cannot respec after finishing RTE also seems silly. It is not like we're gaining more points from it, and it is annoying to go through a second time with the same guy (now reset) if you want to change their stats. There is also the chance they won't get as many points as the first time around.

Add on the freeze bugs online, the broken moves and skill combos (lack of match types, even if some are in RTE but not the main game). It makes the game a chore more than anything, and when it is hard to find any online games due to the PC player base not being as big as the others, it becomes far more annoying than fun (if you even get a game that is not a maxed out super CAW or the like).

I think it is fair to call this game massively overpriced. Look at the DLC. It's £10 for two wrestlers and some mini games and £4 for one wrestler (two versions of the same guy. A guy who should have been in the base game. Matt Hardy).

While I like the game as an idea, this is stupid levels of pulling the leg here. The fact they have not even patched the game to make us at least have the full roster parts unlocked in Creations is questionable, and the fact CAW layering is a mess still is dumb. Add on the bugs and brokenness (online and offline), like getting stuck inside the ring or frozen in an endless grapple, or annoying parts of the game.

It makes you wonder if removing stuff like mini games from the creation process would have done the game a world of good.

4.5/10

If it is on sale for £25 at some point (plus DLC) buy it then.\t\t\t\t\t
