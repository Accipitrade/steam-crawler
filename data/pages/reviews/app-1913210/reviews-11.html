\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to like this game. The gameplay is pretty good to be completely fair, it's nice playing a wrestling game that's just fun and arcade-y. The graphics? It's not that great to look at but they server their purpose, so good enough imo.

But I have chosen to refund the game? Yes.

There is an insulting lack of content. A small roster of wrestlers to choose from, few match types to play (there isn't even a steel cage, which I think is pretty fundamental to wrestling), and worst of all there is so few options to choose from in create-a-wrestler that they honestly might as well have not even included it in the game. I'm not exaggerating there. It's one of the worst character creators I think I've ever seen. (I guess they thought pointless minigames were more important than the character customization that is the whole reason some people even buy these games in the first place.) Oh, but I'm sure they'll add more content in DLC's...

Which brings me to my final straw. If this had been a budget title, say $30 or maybe $40? I could happily recommend it and would be leaving a glowing review. But it's not. It's $60 + day one DLC + season passes. For that price tag this lack of content feels like a slap in the face.

Don't bother with this one. Even if you're a huge wrestling fan who is desperate from an old school arcade style wrestling game. Your time would be better spent on installing an emulator and playing an old WWF title. And I say that as an AEW fan.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first wrestling game since Here Comes The Pain in 2003 that is worth actually playing rather than using as a creative platform. It's very enjoyable to play with a handful of things to be aware of before you buy the game.

-It needs a little extra polish in the gameplay department, nothing that can't be fixed post release
-If you only want to make your own characters forget about it, CAW mode sucks and needs a lot of work. Not only extra parts but more functionality. Thankfully the game itself is actually good so you can just play a video game instead, which is unusual for a wrestling game, hence me mentioning it here.
-No idea about how well online works, I don't care either. I did try it once though and when it worked it worked, when it didn't it was wonky. Since it's P2P keep in mind that maybe something like parsec would work better.
-Tazz is not in it. Dealbreaker. But that's just me. However, there are some other strange omissions. Like Evil Uno, the guy who promoted the game like no other isn't even in it. And what about the Acclaimed and Daddy Ass? Those will likely be fixed eventually, however, only through paid DLC.
-Mods are not officially supported by the game but they are more then just tolerated. The official AEW games Discord has modding channels. So if you're into that, go nuts.

Rating: Fun/10\t\t\t\t\t

Fun game with flaws

There are a few of flaws with the game, but I have been having fun with it. This is the first wrestling game (not counting Wrestling Revolution) I have played since WWE 2k16. The gameplay is amazing, the minigames can be fun when played during Road to Elite (Never really played them outside of that game mode), it runs great on my PC and I only had 1 crash. The controls are straightforward and easy to grasp. The create a wrestler could use some love, it lacks a lot of features.

My biggest issue is Road to Elite. It is fun to play and reminds me a bit of Here Comes the Pain when I was a kid. It goes on for 1 year and has storylines leading up to each PPV. My issue is that it can get very repetitive and for some reason some of the snapshots don't appear when I do the correct event before a match. From what I have heard, there is a glitch with Road to Elite where it resets your custom wrestler's stats when you start a new playthrough of it.

I haven't done online yet so I am not sure how it plays, but from what I have read it is peer 2 peer connection which isn't the best. The game was made using Unreal Engine 4, which means it uses the Epic Games Online service which asks you to connect an Epic Games account every time you log in if you don't. I haven't tried yet but I read you have to link an Epic Games account in order to play online but I'm not sure. I should mention this as I have seen people complain about the Epic Games account issue, you DO NOT need to install the Epic Games launcher to play this game. You just need to link your Epic account if you have one.

Overall I would recommend this game for AEW fans and fans of wrestling games in general. In the current state that it is in I would probably wait until a sale to buy it.\t\t\t\t\t
