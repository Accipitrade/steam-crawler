A deeply flawed but ultimately fun game.

AEW: Fight Forever is a divisive game, just like AEW itself is. It's not going to win everyone over and (as you can probably tell from the comment section) pissed a lot of people off. Ultimately, I'm going to go over why I decided to give the game a recommendation.

Personal background:
I've been playing wrestling games all of my life. My first was WCW/NWO Revenge and from there branched out to pretty much everything forward and backward. From WWF Wrestlemania on the SNES, all the way to Smackdown Vs Raw 2006. I attempted to play a few of the games after (such as the 2K games) but quite frankly I didn't feel they were fun to play, I really hated how wrestling games went more to simulation than arcade style. Also, the few times I played online were dreadful. Some people really enjoy this, I've always hated having people who used the exact same spammy moves (ala a Spike headbutt or Triple H knee lift), so my experience has always been local co-op or single player.

Cons:
  • The Price for what you're getting.
  • Duplicate moves and that take up space and don't look good.
  • Game is currently unplayable for some.

First and foremost, I'm not sure what is causing it, but there are people who literally cannot play the game based on something in their setup conflicting with the game. I've had a seamless experience so far, but someone who bought it with me to play on Steam as well, literally can't play three minutes into any match with the game either crashing to desktop, or crashing then bluescreening their PC. I have an Nvidia card and they have an AMD card, maybe that's why? Who knows, but that needs fixed ASAP.

The only really major thing I consider a detraction from my experience is, the game feels unfinished in many regards (but I'll get to why I don't think that's a detraction for me, yet) but the one part of that that's kind of egregious are the moves when making a custom wrestler. In classic Yuke's style, the game has multiple ways of doing moves. The thing is, some of these moves just don't look good or are almost identical. Most of the character specific versions look good, but they are definitely pulling from older animations for others and they didn't look good then, and including them in a game that already has an extra level of jank to it just makes it feel very weak, like why not just put the best looking ones in the game?

And really the price. Again, I'm having fun with the game, but for 60 USD (70USD if you bought the Elite Edition) you're asking a lot of people with the state the game is in currently to shell out that much, and for two of the DLC packs in the Season Pass it wasn't even clear whether they were ready now or would be coming out later.

Middle Ground
  • Wildly inconsistent quality.
  • Lack of (current) content.
  • Game feels unfinished and rushed in certain aspects.
  • As wide as an ocean but as deep as a puddle content-wise.

I wanted to include things that to me might be a con to some but aren't really a con to me in the greater scheme of things or that don't detract from my experience.

When this game shines, it really shines, which is why I put the overall quality in the middle ground. Many moves look awesome, I like some of the customization options, things I would never have thought of to play around with such as the custom effects on entrances and controlling those entrance effects. Same thing with a lot of characters, there are some characters that actually look really well done. Most of the women in the game actually look pretty good. But then you have some, like the Young Bucks, and you think sheesh what happened? Some things have a ton of options, but then others are non-existent. Some minigames are awesome like Shida baseball, the fan catching game or the bomb throwing game) but then others like guess the card that flew by or the trivia game are dreadful because they just go way too fast.

The game just feels like it needed another two years and either AEW, THQNordic or both said "It has to come out now". And what we got is what is here. There's one or two spelling errors in the moves as well as extraneous spaces that show me this was a decision made very quickly. I'm reserving final judgment because like Cyberpunk or No Man's Sky, or any game that gets post-launch support, maybe THQ and AEW will really put in the work to make this game great, they've at least hinted that they were more interested in that then doing a yearly release.

Pros:
  • The game is actually fun.
  • It's a mix of many nostalgic wrestling games.
  • Road to Elite has been fun.
  • Easy to pick up and play.

The thing that you've probably seen mentioned if you've looked at other reviews is, this game takes great inspiration from wrestling games of the past. WWF No Mercy, WWE Day of Reckoning and Here Comes the Pain, etc. It wears these inspirations on its sleeve, and if you're from that time, if these games were your favorites growing up? You're more than likely going to have a much better time with it. Road to Elite especially reminds me of a combination of No Mercy's championship mode and Smackdown: Shut Your Mouth's career mode. It has very tongue-in-cheek writing that doesn't take itself too seriously and works with the game.

Most importantly, it's just fun. That may sound like a broken record to some looking to quantify in these reviews why "fun" supercedes all of these possible negatives but it just does. I bought this game at full price even with the mixed reviews coming out and with all of the jank and things I wished were currently in it. It's obvious the people who worked on this game had a passion for it, the foundation for a truly great game is here, it just needs both more time and content to get there.

Final notes:
I didn't bring up jank or glitches as positives or negatives because ultimately, every wrestling game has had them. There are a few that bug me, but nothing worth rage quitting over. Also, to any possible comments of "Well they had X amount of money and Y amount of time" or "But these games they're taking inspiration from are better". Listen, in the same way that AEW has only existed for four years as of this review and WWE for 50, Fight Forever is the very first console game AEW has ever made. It took 3 years to get from Smackdown 1 to Here Comes the Pain. Fight Forever has the potential to fix, change, and add a lot of things in that time to address concerns. Will they? That remains to be seen, but I'm personally willing to give them that chance.

To the devs:
If you are looking at reviews, here are some personal suggestions:

  1. You have a great foundation of a game, please make some kind of roadmap for the fans of the game to see what changes, fixes, and DLC are going to be happening in the future.
  2. Don't focus on "fixing" current character models, taunts, moves or anything, the priority should just be more content. Add in Cage matches, Trios matches, backstage areas, more content for Road to Elite, add more created wrestler options, etc. I'd suggest even making big content patches / DLC releases around this to address the lack of content. IE "This patch is going to be about expanding Road to Elite. This patch will be about expanding Create-A-Wrestler," and so on.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game should show why the No Mercy style of wrestling games just doesn't work in modern day. The controls& the reversal mechanics are just bad. All this game tells you are the very basic buttons that need to be hit, but then everything else you just have to figure out for yourself. Running is a joke as you are stuck & committed to run in one direction once you press the button & cannot change your direction mid run. Some of the controls are also incorrectly given to you & i'm shocked nobody caught that b4 release (For example: Manual Targeting tells you to move the L stick + face buttons to select your target when that is clearly false, as it ACTUALLY is just pushing in the L stick w/o even using the face buttons at all). Normal Tag matches are the worst aspect about this game b/c it truly shows how awful the mechanics work, especially if you aren't the legal person. It gives you a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ in ring timer that once it runs out, takes away all control you have & forces you out of the ring & into your corner. There should be no reason that feature to exist in a modern day game. It doesn't help that playing Road to the Elite constantly puts me in Tag Matches & Tag story lines that I wan't nothing to do with. What's even worse is the AI obviously knows how to abuse this to it's advantage, so you'll wind up losing most of your matches unless you just take full control & never tag out. Speaking of the AI, sometimes it is super dumb or it has physic powers & reverses almost every other move you throw at it & lands the most ridiculous moves on you b/c of how weird the hit boxes are sometimes. The momentum meter is outdated af b/c it does not seem to matter at times, you can be in the green & still lose to a simple move b/c the game only flashes for a few seconds which body part is damaged, yet I've wrestled almost a full Casino Battle Royal Match w/ an injury and almost won (Which I definitely would have won if this game didn't make eliminations so easy for all shapes & sizes, regardless of how great your health is). But once you're momentum is in Danger, YOU'RE FINISHED, doesn't even matter if you barely have any body damage as you will never kick out. So it can really be 50/50 that you'll get a squash match or at least a half decent match out of the game. Create a Wrestler is very limited, although I'm sure there will be more added overtime, but it's kind of a joke what is available at launch. Road to the Elite feels kind of cut & dry for a "story mode" & they were hyping this up alot upon release, but you just repeat the same things over & over again. I'm rarely ever given "choices" to make in this game, as it constantly throws me into Tag feuds that I want nothing to do with. I was looking forward to a great alternative from 2k, but sadly Wrestling Empire is somehow slightly better & more fun than this game & it's only $20 compared to the $60 they're charging for this unfinished product. This game obviously needed WAY more time to be polished & patched up before slapping that price tag on it.\t\t\t\t\t
