\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAEW Fight Forever is a really good wrestling game that I'm having a lot of fun with. I do think it's price is pretty steep given some of it's faults.

Game-play is easy to pick up and learn. Move animations look good, although they're not perfect and there are times when you can notice that. The roster isn't as deep as I'd like it to be. Most of my favorite AEW wrestlers are there, but there are some who are noticeably absent.

Game matches and types are fun, but I do wish there were more match types and stipulations. -For example; Ladder Match, Falls Count Anywhere Match, Lights Out Match & Exploding Barbed Wire Death Matches are exclusively 1v1.
-There does not seem to be any special match types for 2v2, 3-way, & 4-way matches outside of normal exhibition matches.

There is also a Casino Battle Royal match which is fun but there are definitely bugs.
-I had duplicate wrestlers come out during the match.
-I had a bug where wrestlers stopped coming out unless there was only 1 person left in the ring. There were many wrestlers who could have come out and filled the ring, but they trickled in allowing only 1v1 at a time.

Road to Elite mode was more fun than I was expecting. I am still on my first playthrough, and I am enjoying the experience so far. I am definitely going to play this game mode a few times.
The minigames are a fun addition, the few that are included in the base game are OK, but I understand the season pass will be adding more as time goes on.

The FTR DLC is weird because you getting a tag-team DLC, but they don't come into the game as a tag-team that you can select.
-I had to go to the Creation part of the game to create a "custom" tag team, but really I was just pairing Dax and Cash together for the game to recognize them officially as FTR.
-I had to make sure I selected the correct entrance animation, video, finishing tag move, etc. It would be nice if future DLC like this did this process for you for tag teams.

I think what will "make or break" this game is how they handle this coming season pass of content, and what their long term plans are. I would be a little annoyed if a newer, better version of this game came out next year (like WWE's 2k Series), but if they support this game for 2 years or so with updates, roster additions, new matches & minigames. If you enjoy wrestling games, this will be a good casual gaming experience that you can enjoy by yourself or with friends, this game just feels like it should be ~$50.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot a bit of gameplay under my belt on this now and have some comments.

TL;DR - The game is fun, and it is an excellent baseline for what could be a fantastic sequel. This game is not much more than arcade fun though. If you're looking for a decent Create mode, you're going to be disappointed. If you're looking for heavy storylines, the career mode is woefully short. It's fun but far from perfect.

Long one:

The game is fun. When you are playing it, it is easy to pick up, feels familiar if you played any of the N64 games in the Yukes pedigree, and has a good sized (though limited) roster. Unlike many AAA games released these days, this one released without crippling performance issues. It is easily playable right out the gate. Pop in a controller and play a game that feels like a direct descendant of many great games of the past.

It has excellent bones, and is an excellent first product in that regard. I have high hopes for it as a series or at least with solid expansion pack like DLCs that aren't just roster expansions. As is, I had a great time hooking up 4 controllers and playing it with my kids. Which just made me feel young again.

The bad though... The creation suite is awful. It has less options than things like No Mercy, let alone modern offerings from other companies. I hope this changes.

The graphics are fine. Nothing amazing, but it works and in game it's not really noticable.

Some control interactions are funky. I dislike the autotargeting system. Just a couple quirks to get used to.

The limitation of only having 4 characters in the ring feels odd for a modern wrestling game, Especially for a company that regularly has 6 man tag matches. We especially are going to have an issue if they try to implement the Blood and Guts match.

The game modes are good, but I wish there were more. Like a Cage match, or a coffin match. Anything more than what's here.

The roster is solid, but is missing a lot of the depth of the actual roster and is very out of date. There are a couple of neat includes too.

But, that said, it is fun. Worth picking up on sale. Full price...? Probably not unless you're a massive fan of the company and don't want to wait.\t\t\t\t\t
