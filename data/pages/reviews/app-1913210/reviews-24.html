\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall solid game.
Pros and Cons
Core gameplay is the best of any wrestling game on Steam.
The art style is fantastic, and at a very light 18GB install size, this game is very well optimized.

however there are some issues that make the value proposition worse.

The game is far too light on gamemodes, and matchtypes at launch, and while it could definitely be improved with updates, atm it feels a light in that regard.
The choice to use Epic Online services is really strange to me. The game requires an EGS account despite not even being available on the EGS. It's not terribly invasive, just moreso confusing.
The use of Easy Anti-Cheat is lame af but to be expected at this point.

The game is fun as-is, and promising, but as you can see, there's a number of (fixable) issues that stop it from being exceptional.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't describe my disappointment after spending these first few hours with Fight Forever.

The core gameplay is fine, if not lacking polish. But the real letdown is how underdeveloped the Create a Wrestler mode is (and, by extension, the lack of a community workshop). A fully-featured CAW mode is a foundational piece of wrestling games and to see it so poorly fleshed out here is truly puzzling.

- There are only 8 (EIGHT!) faces to choose from.
- You can't change the size, shape or position of any facial/body features.
- The eye colour choices are horrendous - you have your choice of black or various shades of neon or pure white.
- There are only 5 eyebrow types to choose from.
- There are only 17 hair styles to choose from. There are 47 non-DLC wrestlers at launch. Where are the other 30+ hair styles that are already in the game but missing from CAW??
- Facial hair: 8 styles, and 3 of them look exactly the same.
- Body types: Jacked, Skinny Jacked, Fat Jacked, or The Incredible Hulk
- Body Settings: changing the Fat or Muscle sliders only affect the body and thighs. You can try to look lean, but your head, hands and feet will still be enormous. The Weight slider doesn't affect anything visually.
- Headwear: NONE of the choices are actually viable, except for maybe the ballcaps and toques. The rest are a joke, especially the flag themed hats which are purely there to be filler.
- Why are there SO MANY options for facial/body scars? There are 21 face scar choices - more that double the number of FACES.
- Why are there SO MANY options for arm/knee sleeves and wrist bands
- You can't wear shirts/tops in ring - only as part of your Entrance Attire, for some inexplicable reason.
- Bottoms are mostly awful camo-themed pants/shorts or flag shorts. There a 2 Omega pants, a Nakamura ripoff and that's it.
- There are 100 (ONE HUNDRED!) tattoo options. This would be fine if they gave us more than 8 FACES and ZERO SHIRTS to work with.

These choices and omissions would be tolerable if this was Yuke's first time making a wrestling game but all they had to do was cherry pick the best parts of their past games and slap an AEW sticker on it. How hard could it be?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt took a while for it to finally come out, but we now have AEW's inaugural effort in the realm of wrestling games. I would recommend it with a few caveats:
One, the window dressing around the core gameplay is pretty lackluster right now. The actual matches are fun, but the CAW feature you'd normally expect from modern wrestling games is woefully underdeveloped, and matches are limited to 4 players simultaneously.
Two, the roster is especially out of date by wrestling game standards, probably about the same as 2K22's roster ended up being when it finally came out.
Three, if you're buying it at launch, you're doing so with the understanding that it's launched in a stable state, though not necessarily a complete one. If I recall correctly, in interviews around mid/late 2022, Kenny Omega - who served as a creative advisor for the game - explained that various bits of content were shelved so they didn't get lost in the weeds forever, with CAW features being chief among them.

All of these things may end up being fixed as time goes on, hopefully through a mix of free updates and DLC.

As far as gameplay goes, it's reminiscent of the AKI titles on N64, as well as Yukes' own Day of Reckoning games on Gamecube, with a quick match flow that keeps the person taking punishment from being stuck doing nothing for extended periods of time. It's all quick and responsive, though there are some eccentricities like fairly weird-feeling running. Move animations look a bit faster and impactful than the mocap animations used in 2K these days. I don't think it's quite to the level of elaborate recreation as the AKI game animations were but everything's got a little oomph to it that improves the game feel.

Graphics are... well, they are what they are. They're not going to compete with 2K's models at all, but everyone is recognizable, if not up to date. The character models are by and large better than Firepro's sprites and the MDickie games, though that's not saying much.

All in all, as it is on day one, I'd give it a slight thumbs up like Orange Cassidy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs soon as I started watching AEW in early 2020, I immediately wanted a game. I soon found out that they were making Fight Forever, and I was extremely excited. Although that was 3 years ago, the game finally came out and I honestly think it delivered. However, there are cons.

Starting off with the pros, I like the graphics. I personally don't give a ♥♥♥♥ about graphics, but these really aren't as bad as everybody's saying it is. They look like realistic arcade graphics. AEW also specifically said they are going for an alternative to the WWE games, which is an arcade game. They did not plan on making FF have 2K like graphics. The mechanics are also very fun to play around with. 95% of the moves actually look great, and I haven't had an issue with any of them. Entrances being short is both nice but also a little annoying, but I'll get into that later. Having the ability to control the camera angle and the pyro is really cool and a fun little idea. Road to Elite is fun so far, but I haven't fully completed it yet. The weapons are great, thumbtacks being in a wrestling game is a great addition, and the blood to go along with it is great as well. Not having commentary isn't really a big deal either. I would've loved the AEW commentary team being in the game, but the commentary being repetitive is something that did bug me in the 2K games. The roster is alright, but I will elaborate more. I haven't played any of the minigames, I don't really care for them but I like the idea of there being more to do.

Onto the cons. To start off, the roster is way too small for AEW. There's less than 30 (pretty sure there is less than 30), and they aren't up-to-date. It took 3 years for the game to be developed, it would've been nice to have an up-to-date roster. There are certain wrestlers on the AEW roster that should have been included too, like the Dark Order faction, the full Blackpool Combat Club, Samoa Joe, Toni Storm, Jamie Hayter, Saraya even, and a bunch of other AEW originals. The fact that the Women's Champion (Toni Storm) isn't in the game is a huge problem. AEW desperately needs to add more roster members, as the DLC's haven't fully come out yet either. Most of the roster has their early AEW look too. Jericho is still dressed like he was in 2019, Sammy still has his old stupid haircut, Trent? has hair, the Young Bucks have their old 2019 attire, but you can just buy the newer one with ingame cash. There is barely any women on the roster as well. I don't usually use the Women's Roster, but there's less than like 10 women on the roster. The referee mechanics are also just very painful. They count slower than my grandmother walking down the stairs, and once they get near the wrestlers, they start to slowly walk, then they get down on their knees, get into position, and begin the count. Thats their only job too. They can't get hit, they don't seem to get involved, and they're slow. It makes it painful to have a long match, or just a fun one. The running also just looks hilarious. Entrances are fine short, but I love some of the themes in AEW and would've loved to hear them longer than in the menu. The creation suite was honestly a complete let down. There is ♥♥♥♥ all else for creation. There's like 10 hairstyles, barely any attires, and you can't do much with that. There's no community creations either. Community creations would be great to have. There should be many more match types than given, like Blood and Guts, Steel Cage Match, Tag Match modes, Stadium Stampede, 6-Man Tag, the World Trios Championships should be in the game, the International Championship should be in there as well. GM Mode was also promised (at least rumored) and there is nothing of the sort. Collision should be added, Forbidden Door should've been here on release, and the new Dynamite/Rampage stage design should have also been here on release. Dynamite Grand Slam, Fyter Fest, Road Rager, Fight for the Fallen, Christmas themed Dynamite. Thanksgiving themed Dynamite, New Year's Smash, Beach break, St. Patrick's Day Slam, and Winter is Coming should all be there as well, as these have been in AEW for a few years now.

Although these are stressful issues, I would say this game is a major hit for me and it was worth the wait.

Solid 8.5/10
