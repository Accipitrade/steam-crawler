Product refunded
\r\n\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just couldn't help but feel disappointed by this game... It was exciting to get a new wrestling game other than WWE and it was exciting to have THQ & Yukes back as they have made most all the great wrestling games of my childhood. It is just unfortunate that this failed to capture any of the magic of those games. I may be being a little harsh given this is AEW's first game but for a game charging the price it does, with the devs it had, I would've at least expected a product that felt somewhat complete.

The game advertised to be a throwback to the old days of wrestling video games (specifically n64 era games) and not chase the simulation based style 2k has been offering for years. Overall, I think that is a cool concept, but I feel the concept may have been taken too literal. There is one particular example of this that stood out... The controls felt dated.... It truly captured the old style wrestling game controls with little things such as a single tap sprint button where you could only sprint in a straight line. The controls seem to go after a easy to learn simple basics, hard to master the fun stuff concept. It took me a while to learn how to a finishing move. Now, I will admit, I only have played two and a half hours of this game, and I am sure it would've felt better over time, but I see no reason not to at least take some inspiration from the WWE franchise whose controls have rarely been a week point for 15+ years. I would hope that future games in the series decide to smooth these out and start to modernize it. It would also be cool if the controls where explained better in the pause menu. Now, instead of being Mr. Negative, for at least one point I would like to praise the arcade like nature of this game. It is nice to have some fun over the top stuff in wrestling games that are completely optional for those who want it. I remember having my mind blown playing WWF Smack-Down: Just Bring It on PS2 as a kid and loving things like brawling to the backstage, the streets, downtown, the parking lot, the locker room and just having a grand ole time. I would always go back stage and hop in a jeep and run it at my brother (Who i would play with a lot back then.) It was cool seeing little things in this game like riding Darby Alins skateboard around the arena and doing tricks off wrestlers heads. I would love to see more fun concepts like this and concepts of past wrestling games. It looks like this will continue has the trailer of the stadium stampede mode showed wrestlers driving golf-carts and riding horses and shooting fireworks at one another. It is just a shame that that mode will still have the outdated controls the main game has.

Early on when the game was announced and shown off, it showed an over the top, cartoonish, art style. This on its own would be fine, however, the game that shipped showed a clear art direction change half way through development. On one hand you have wrestlers such as Jericho & Omega exhibiting this original style but on the other hand, you have wrestlers that aim to be more on the realistic side of things. The models are proportioned realistically and have good resemblance to their real life counter parts. With the mixture of character models exhibiting different art directions and it shows the devs never bothering to go back and update older models to create a sense of consistency. Its baffling Kenny Omega has the worst looking model in the game considering he apparently had a hand in the direction of the game. Again, I do not care if the game has some poor looking models that have exaggerated body shapes. I just thought the inconsistency of the wrestler designs really clashed with one another.

The DLC is so far proving to be a huge disappointment. the $30 price tag to get 6 wrestlers and a few mini games is a bit steep. However, It is not one of my main complaints. I was hyped to get the FTR DLC as FTR/The Revival are my all time favorite Tag team ever. This being said, It sucked to see that the game does not automatically make them a tag team in game. You have to go through the extra work to create the team. It is a somewhat minor complaint but it seems like something THQNordic could have done easily. The same could be said about the hardy boys with the Matt Hardy DLC. This leads me to my next point. Matt Hardy being locked as a Pre Order bonus while Jeff Hardy is on the main game is wild to me considering Matt has been with the company for years while Jeff is still relatively new to the company. This felt like pulling a character from the game just to entice people to buy the game early.

This also leads me to my next point... The steam version of the game never was up for pre-order. You could only buy the game post launch meaning if you wanted the Matt Hardy pack, you had to fork over even more money. (Also to note I didn't spend too much time trying to figure this out but I could not figure out how to access the other Matt Hardy attire... That was weird to me.)

Entrances not showing the full entrance is not a huge issue. Especially for a first in franchise game. However, The game goes as far as to add options to change camera angles and set off various pyro during each entrance DURING the entrance, which is a cool feature if the entrances where longer than a few seconds. This leaves the feature to feel kinda pointless.

The creation suite seemed really bare bones and not even worth using much. Only having a handful of options for attires and call names was disappointing but again, something I hope gets better with future iterations of the game.

These are my main gripes and I know I only have a few hours of play on this. I didn't even touch road to elite. I just knew very quickly that I wanted to refund this game and wait to catch it on sale in a year or so. I felt really disappointed. I love AEW, THQN, and Yukes, so I just hope the next game is better.

TLDR, this game is a promising start but not worth its current price tag.\t\t\t\t\t

It needs more. More of the roster. More CAW options. More moves. More customization. MORE MORE MORE.

Summary
Fun pick up and play AEW pro wrestling game that mechanically lands somewhere between older Smackdown games, Day of Reckoning, and some light homages to the AKI games. Very little meat on the bone, but what's here has been fun and stable, with one deal breaking bug exception that prevents Owen Hart from unlocking.

Who's it for?

Anyone who doesn't care much about CAW. Any passionate AEW fan. Anyone looking for a wrestling game that doesn't take itself too seriously.

Who's it not for?

Anyone buying it for Owen Hart. (Unlock is unfortunately bugged.) Anyone demanding deep CAW. Anyone who demands a serious tone in their wrestling. Anyone who demands endless modes like Universe, or things like GM mode. Anyone who wants crossplay, rollback, etc. Anyone looking for true AKI style gameplay and mechanics. To be clear, this game does not play like No Mercy or the other AKI games. It plays like Yuke's games but with some AKI elements sprinkled in.

PROS:

  • Easy to pick up and play. Familiar for anyone who's played the Yuke's or AKI games. Matches have a fun flow to them.
  • Takes some adjustment if you're coming from 2K, but once I got the reversal timing down it was extremely satisfying and fun. The ability to hold strike block when rising to crouch and then attack is a nice nod to the old AKI games.
  • The best blood I've ever seen in a wrestling game.
  • Some of the weapons are fantastically fun, if a bit silly.
  • Intergender matches.
  • Exploding Barbed Wire Deathmatch.
  • Story Mode (Road to Elite, henceforth referred to in this review as RTE) is a fun, breezy experience that doesn't take itself too seriously and has replay value via a handful of different storylines that can be unlocked randomly or by fulfilling specific requirements. Allows you to rank up your Create-a-Wrestlers (CAWs,) and can be replayed quickly due to its brevity. (Completion can take as little as a couple of hours.) The corny puns may not be to everyone's tastes, but I liked it. The unpredictability of who will show up during certain segments was also fun. Real footage represents AEW well.
  • Includes Owen Hart, thanks to AEW's involvement in his foundation. This is the first time we've seen him in a game officially in decades. BUT... (See cons...)
  • Not a single crash, major performance issue, save corruption, or any of the other hairpulling issues I had in the 2K games showed up in this game for me.
  • Proper variable refresh rate and higher resolution support, which is another thing I cannot say for the 2K games in recent years.
  • Substantial move variety in CAW.
  • Lots of fun character quirks that show up during matches.
  • Four unlockable characters.
  • Some of the mini-games are fun.
  • Decent selection of arenas.
  • Decent roster.

https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3002222861

CONS:
  • After a grind of 100 exhibition wins (138 just to be sure) and despite getting the achievement, Owen Hart did not unlock. Known bug. Will change my review to positive when/if this is fixed.
  • CAW is the most limited I've seen in any licensed wrestling game in decades. A dozen or so faces and hairs to choose from. No sliders. Extremely restricted clothing. Clunky to use (must go into entrance attire to put a top on, for example.)
  • Due to restrictions, CAW entrances suffer from an inability to wear a hat to the ring without also wearing it during the match.
  • Once you've seen the very small number of stories in RTE mode, there's little reason to replay it other than levelling up CAWs.
  • Not many branches or stories in RTE, and not enough RTE mode awareness of choices/championships, etc. Winning titles in one "block" of RTE seems to be forgotten in the next.
  • Title holders don't come out wearing their belts in RTE mode unless it's explicitly a title match.
  • Entrances last about 7 seconds. While this is a nice throwback to games like No Mercy, here it feels more like it's due to lack of funds and development time.
  • No crossplay (despite Kenny Omega saying there would be a year ago, and this was not announced until the day before release, which is unfortunate community management and marketing.)
  • No dedicated servers. Peer-to-peer only, which often results in lag.
  • Grappling opponents when they are getting up will always result in them reversing, no matter how or when you do it.
  • Weapons are thrown away after two strikes.
  • Some moves damage incorrect body parts. (Cody Cutter from the top damages the arm, crossface chickenwing damages the body, etc.)
  • AI will almost never use signature moves or specials (finishers) if an opponent is downed. They will stand there and do standard moves to them, or pin them before they're ready to be pinned, until their signature or special status is lost.
  • Cannot control tag partner in RTE mode once they're tagged in. AI takes over, so you are at its mercy for tags.
  • Far more tag team and three way matches in RTE than I'd like. Even in singles title branches. This is something all wrestling games seem to love doing. I've always disliked it, and this game is no exception.
  • Not enough alternate attires. Most consist of city-themed T-shirts we can unlock in RTE mode.
  • No GM, Universe, or endless style modes.
  • Some mini-games are too fast and irksome in my opinion.
  • The vast majority of unlockables are just taunts or entrance elements, which given the limited CAW makes unlocking everything feel like a time sink without much to show for it by the end. (Hopefully this will be expanded upon, but... see the final con below...)
  • While not included at the time of this review, a planned free update will include a 30 player online battle royale mode, with the shrinking safe area typical of those games. Some datamined evidence suggests the possibility that this may be the mode where additional CAW parts and other content will be unlocked. If this proves to be true, and we were sold a game meant to be a spiritual successor to classic wrestling games, only to then be locked out of content unless we play an online battle royale mode, especially if via Battlepass as rumored, it would be a huge bait and switch, and is not what I signed up for. Especially given how painfully limited CAW is at present. So you may wish to hold off on purchasing until we see what form this takes, if this is something that matters to you. I will update this review to reflect those changes in the future, for better or worse.

Overall

I've been having a lot of fun with it. Matches flow well, weapon spots are incredibly satisfying, the quick pace of the story mode is refreshing, and the skeleton Yuke's and AEW have crafted here is solid. It's definitely nowhere near as mind numbingly frustrating in terms of stability and design decisions as 2K22 was (see my review of that game if you're curious as to what I'm talking about, and many of the same issues persisting in 2K23 is why I'm not buying it,) other than limited CAW. Unfortunately there is a known bug preventing Owen Hart from unlocking, which is a deal breaking time waste for me.

The game needs more content and quality of life improvements. More CAW parts, changes to entrance attire, improved AI in some areas, and an expansion of RTE's branching elements to provide more awareness of players' choices. If they can actually pull all of those things off, this could be one of the most enjoyable wrestling games I've played in a long time.

For now, it's a fun but flawed alternative to the 2K games, and a good representation of AEW's product, generally speaking. I just don't want to see it turn into a battle royale online battlepass style game with that update they've announced. I will update this review when that content drops. For now, the game is fun enough for me to recommend it with caveats, but not until the Owen Hart bug is patched.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will start by saying Fight Forever has its problems, that much can't be denied. At launch state, it's got its glitches, random match types (Gauntlets and multi-man ladder matches) are locked behind Road to Elite for seemingly no reason, there's an arbitrary limit of 4 people in a match at one time, and its creation suites also have some unfortunate limitations, like not being able to add custom logos or even use all the same attire parts that the base cast have; if you're expecting it to look anything like 2K creation suites, you're gonna be disappointed.

On the other hand, what it does right, it does damn right. The core gameplay is really good - I've been having a blast through every match I've played (even the ones where I got my ass kicked) - and while many groaned at first when they heard you had to do Road to Elite to build custom characters' stats, it definitely has more than enough ways to keep you entertained for a while, with each runthrough getting different storylines and such at random instead of a predetermined path, keeping it feeling fresh every time you go through it.

Overall, if you know you're gonna be disappointed by the limitations it has right now, I'd say not to buy as-is right now; wait a little while for new content patches to come out, hold out for it to start getting discounts and then consider it. But, I've been having a blast with it personally even despite its problems, so when a discount does roll up, I'd recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
