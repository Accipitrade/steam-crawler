I say we, as fans, should support this effort so it can get bigger! If devs continue to listen, I'm sure it'll get there.\t\t\t\t\t

Like a lot of people in the reviews here, I stopped playing wrestling games heavy when the WWE 2K series was introduced. I've picked up a couple of yearly iterations of the game, not once feeling excited to play it. They're not bad, just not my thing. I missed the dumb fun in older games like SvR, No Mercy, Here Comes the Pain. If you were a fan of those older titles I HIGHLY recommend giving this game a go. Worst case, you really won't enjoy it, play less than 2 hours and ask Steam for a refund.

The gameplay is solid, it's a lot of fun. I mean A LOT of fun. I have the game on PC and PS5 and have played a total of about 15-20 hours so far across both platforms. This game is incredibly simple to pick up and get the hang of, HOWEVER, every time I play I am finding new things that we can do as players that the game didn't announce to us but were left for us to find out. It provides some real life "HOLY SH%T" moments.

The story mode experience, "Road to the Elite" is a fun experience. Each situation, decision, and outcome is said to alter the path of the story, thus providing you with a different experience each time you play. It's quick enough, to not feel like you're sinking too much time trying to figure out how to unlock some missing characters, but long enough that it doesn't feel pointless.

The creative suite is by far the weakest part of this game. As a player you're provided with very minimal options up front. They have a good variety of moves, music, and entrances, however, the actual CAW customization features are disappointing. Luckily, THQ has seemed to rescind their original stance on modding, allowing for ProWrestlingMods to create their own offline launcher, release modding tutorials, and even will be adding official channels to the discord for them. The devs have already shown us that they are willing to listen to the players, we can only hope that they keep this up. Who's to say they won't do an overhaul on the creative suite in the future?

With all of this said, Fight Forever is fantastic. I have heard that this game was released with the planned to be built onto and added to in the future, rather than going the yearly release route. I am excited to see what the devs end up doing with this game, but if it was left the way it is, I wouldn't feel that my money or time was wasted.\t\t\t\t\t
