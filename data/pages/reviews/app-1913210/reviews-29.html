\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPeople anticipating this game: I'm so excited, we're getting a return to No Mercy!!!

People when this game came out: wtf this is exactly what I asked for, I rather spend 200 bucks on Bugfest 2K23 with the invisible man showcase, now THAT is somehow worth money, the lovely unfinished mess that it is every year.

Okay, I did the jokes that hopefully drove away the empty headed dumb ♥♥♥♥♥, so let's talk. First off, I did get a review code for this on PS5, but truth be told, I don't think I was even able to fully discuss the game due to a few issues I'm not sure I can discuss publicly, but now that I've played the game more now that it's officially out(and love it enough that I bought it on PC), I feel I can be more accurate about this game.

To get this aside, I tried the game on Steam Deck. Despite them saying it runs fine on there, you're gonna have to set up Proton 8.0-2 to get it to run, and even then it's gonna take a bit to boot it up. Other than that, it runs great, but MAN is it a battery drainer. In addition, no, your Epic Games account login info is not malware like that one guy said, it's literally just asking for your account so you can play with people who got the game on EGS. "Oh no, I have crossplay with Xbox? But they're GREEN!!!"

Now onto the game. The gameplay is an almost full return to No Mercy and encompasses what made it great. The action is a fresh take on wrestling games that DESPERATELY has been needed with WWE 2K having been on top for so long that people like me play 3 modes and then abandon it till the next entry. The modded CAWs of MyRise characters upped the replay value, but 2K hates replay value for some reason and put a lock on them. Money is such a burden, really.

The Create a Superstar mode is largely what it was in No Mercy, but that doesn't exactly mean it's good, cause I'm sorry to say it's not. I'm a minimalist with this mode. I choose whatever face is the first one, put on facepaint, and then choose the attire I want. Every other CAW is downloaded online. However, for the people that aren't like me, this sucks. I personally hope they're able to patch in more options, but I also wouldn't be surprised if they pull a Bungie and just make a sequel with most of the problems fixed and then continue to support that entry.

Create a Moveset is better, but I personally think it's lacking in a few move categories, and it would definitely benefit from a better search mechanic. Create an Entrance is short and sweet, like the entrances. I love how you can just use pyro in every entrance(except for Malakai who just uses groundlights), I admittedly have fun just mashing the buttons to make the pyro go crazy. The match type selection is admittedly lacking, but....no, it just is. The Exploding Barbed Wire Deathmatch is neat, and the Lights Out match is fun even if they're sanctioned despite the entire point of that match existing is to not sanction it for the record books as AEW would not want any part of the bloodshed that match is known for.

The Ladder Match is okay, but sadly, you can't do a diving finisher off a ladder through a table. Hell, you can't do a diving finisher off a ladder, period. AND you can only set the ladder up in the middle of the ring. Some have set every weapon you get from under the ring is a ladder, but in my experience, most of the time I wasn't able to get a ladder. I then found there was a prone one on the opposite side of the ring. Still dumber than Ryback.

Road to Elite is an interesting story mode that you can use to bulk up your CAW, but big warning. You are NOT going to be able to upgrade everything and get every single skill, as the mode only lasts 1 in-game year, which is technically only 16 in-game weeks because of the 4 PPVs. No Fyter Fest, no Grand Slam, and no Fight for the Fallen(bring that one back please, btw). Plus, if you use the CAW again, you don't get to keep what you unlocked as they don't exactly overlap, but you can have your extra signatures and finishers. You just can't upgrade their strength without officially unlocking the slot for them. I have no idea if anything unlocked from the first playthrough sticks around after the 2nd playthrough, and I don't wanna test it so as to risk losing any skills. It's also how you unlock the late great Mr. Brodie Lee(how you get the match against him is admittedly funny, if not a bit messed up), and Paul Wight.

Paul Wight hates you, the storyline you unlock him in hates you, and no matter how much you try to show it love, it is going to freaking suck. Long story short, you're stuck having to beat him in less than 3 minutes, and he's insanely unfair. It also doesn't help that because he's a giant, he's mostly resistant to strikes at first. I heard the best way to win is via count out, but every time I tried that, I had to close the game and restart it(you can't just restart the match, and quitting the match itself counts as a loss), so in one of my failed attempts, I felt I roughed him up enough outside that I could affect him with my strikes, spammed signatures for a bit, and hit him with 2 finishers straight. I might have needed a 3rd, but I was too miffed to register that moment.

I have not tried the online yet, and I admittedly have no interest in it. On top of that, intergender wrestling is back in AAA wrestling games!!! If you face a woman as a man to vent frustrations or just for the sake of having power over one, please seek help or never interact with society, ya sick bastard. There's also an in-game shop you spend in-game money on, with ZERO microtransactions btw(thank Christ), and the money is earned at such big amounts and so quickly that not once does it feel like a grind. Oh, that reminds me. You don't actually unlock Brodie and Paul, you "unlock" them in that you unlock the right to buy them in the in-game store. Veerrrrrrrry stupid, if you ask me. Oh, and you get the right to unlock Owen Hart by doing 100 Exhibition matches. even with this game not feeling like a grind when you play it, that DOES feel like one. Now, in all fairness, this is how games used to be before publishers had greedy ideas to fatten their wallets just a bit further, in that you had to work to unlock the content, so....yeah. *So retro* or whatever.

Of course, like with every WWE release since the HD gaming era started, there are bugs that will need to be patched out, and it includes a weird one where some wrestlers don't have their full signature list visible in the pause menu section, affecting but not limited to Lance Archer, Nyla Rose, and even CM "I'm gonna cause AEW to die because I don't know how to own up to my actions, aren't I?" Punk. Weird alias, if you ask me. I ain't gonna pretend others' experience are bs, but all I can say is I haven't experienced anything major or gamebreaking. Mostly just stuff you tend to see in a WWE release.

In conclusion, this game's worth your money, as the gameplay has me coming back with such an amazing fresh take on it, to the point that on PS5 I've beaten Road to Elite 4 times, and plan to play it more. It feels fresh to do it with different wrestlers cause of the different things they'd say as well as the different interactions you'll have in it for some of the storylines. I honestly don't understand why everyone is crying about "no content" like a parrot when they probably buy the same game every single year with no changes outside of a roster update. And yes, I do mean Madden.

I look forward to what this game will offer in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
