\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I'm a wrestling and AEW fan, I'm going to rate this game 7/10

The game is so much fun, love the arcade type. The controls are easy to learn, the jukebox is the best addition of the game, and I'm having a blast to play my favorite wrestlers in the ring.

The only problem is the lack of customization options, the limited roster and match modes, and it's a shame the entrances only last a few seconds. But I've been following the game development since it was announced and I remember they said they had to cut some stuff because there was some problems with the development, but they promised they're gonna add new content later on.

Then again, they delayed the game launching a few times and it was too much of a risk to delay again and again. But I'll give them credit for at least launching the game without too many bugs, at least in my experience. Despite the problems, I'll keep playing this game while I look forward for the new content and updates!

If the devs are reading this, I wanna suggest to include more announcing name options for wrestlers. And please, please consider of adding the mod support! It's the best part of a wrestling game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very slight positive here, the core of it, especially the combat system is great, feels good, etc.

Content is severely lacking. 4 man matches total, 3 way ladder match? nop just 1v1. Repeat these kind of restrictions for most match types, limitations that shouldn't exist.

Creator mode is lacking, it's enough to let you have a little fun but it's 50% of what the PS2 era wrestling games had. Story mode is a semi-repeatable almost rogue-like 3 ish hour experience. It's fine and fun to take custom characters through.

All in all it's a very very good core with not enough supplemental content or systems in anything else. DLC can fix a good chunk of it, but particularly the creation suite and potentially match types will need a sequel.

Worth maybe $20 the way it is now. If you want a casual fighting game and the 2K sim-style isn't your thing, itt'l do for now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a long time fan of Yukes's work for the earlier WWE games, I knew I had to get my hands on AEW Fight Forever. Unfortunately it didn't take me long to see their craftsmanship of making entertaining and content-packed arcade wrestling games has gotten rusty.

Pros:
- A lot of weapon variety.
- Small details like having a wrestler carry 1-5 belts simultaneously during their entrance.
- Moves sound and look impactful
- Focused more on arcade wrestling which is refreshing from playing WWE 2K.
- Daily and weekly challenges to complete for extra cash.
- Simplistic creation lessens the time it takes to create wrestlers, arenas etc. and get into the action sooner
- Mini-games
- Mixed gender matches

Cons:
- Road to Elite story mode is short and can be finished in under 15 matches. Official wrestlers cannot have their stats be altered so earning upgrade points is completely useless, and custom wrestlers have their stats reset every time when they start a new story mode.
- Created wrestlers are broken online.
- Signature move spam fest. Build up your momentum bar once and you can use Signature moves infinitely until you get hit or decide to do a Finisher.
- Extremely limited creation suite and match types. No custom matches.
- No way to reset win/lose records for the roster.
- Menus easy to get lost in and complicated to navigate around.
- Created wrestlers stay weak until you run them through the story mode.
- Some tweaks are desperately needed such as button mashing either not working or not building enough escape progress to escape a pinfall. (It took just 1 non-finisher move on me at full health by an opponent and all the buttons I can mash, to lose). Referees need fixing too because when you go for a pinfall attempt, instead of running towards the pin like normal, they need to hop 2-3 times towards the pin which only prolongs the pinfall and this is annoying if you are in a multi-man match.

It's best waiting for a deep sale until Yukes fixes the problems and adds more content. I don't want to see the first wrestling game Yukes had made in a long time get mistreated, binned and forgotten. I really want to see this thrive so we can hopefully get back to enjoying wrestling games that doesn't take themselves too seriously and offers us good laughs and good times.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know what to think of this game. I want to enjoy it, but there's so much lacking and so much wrong.
*Character creation in 10 year old mobile games have more options. This is perhaps the best part of any wrestling game and they've really dropped the ball here. I loaded up No Mercy just to compare and even still No Mercy has more/better options and the process is far more streamlined on NM.

*Computer controlled wrestlers have the worst AI. I did Road to the Elite and started the Casino Royale. John Silver entered and did the same school boy roll up 16 times in a row (I counted). Would have been more but I eliminated him. I thought this may be a one character bug, but Miro, Scorpio Sky and Moxley did the same thing and just repeated the same move. Doesn't seem like intelligent programming.

*Lack of match types makes no sense. You get like what, 6? 6 match types and most are just 1 on 1, 2 on 2, 3 on 3, 4 on 4. That vanilla mess is what makes up the majority of the match types.

*Only 6 tag teams. There are 7 belts (5 singles and 2 tag team belts) in the game. That means we have more belts than tag teams and one of the tag teams is Anna Jay & John Silver. I mean, what? They have 4 real tag teams: Best Friends, Bucks, Lucha Bros and Death Triangle and 2/3 of Death Triangle IS Lucha Bros. So, 3 real tag teams. Then the Anna / Johnny and Jericho / Guevara

*Health, health bars, signatures and specials are completely baffling and make no sense. I enter the ring, do one arm drag, one strong kick and one taunt and I already have a signature. AND, after I do 2 or so signatures then have to taunt to get my special, I can just repeat the signature/special loop over and over as long as I do 1, maybe 2 moves. And the health. I get hit maybe twice and I'm down on the mat writing in agony and cannot get up for 30, 45, even 60 seconds. This game is utterly random.

* Entrances are a big deal in wrestling. Wrestling games have worked hard to make you feel like you're right there with incredible, life-like entrances, music and other aspects. Fight Forever, on the other hand, might be the worst modern wrestling game with entrances. Yea, eventually you'll skip them of course. But these are already half skipped to begin with.

* No Acclaimed. Massive fail.

*The reverse and strike guard is so wonky and feels horribly random.

* Sending an opponent into the ropes has the worst controls. You can direct them to go, lets say, left and they just randomly go whichever way they decide. Sometimes it goes where you direct them, sometimes random other directions. It's so frustrating to attempt to fire them into the ropes and they take two or three steps and just stop.

*Instead of working on improving the game play, adding more match types and story modes or enhancing the character creation, they decide the best use of their development time and money was to add mini-games. Stupid, pointless, mini games. Is this Paper Mario or a wrestling game?

There's a lot more, but I think you guys get the idea. I am utterly unimpressed and disappointed, and I'm having buyers remorse with this terrible game. I hope there's improvements down the line, but right now there's no possible way I can suggest this unless you can find it for $9.99 or less. It's hard to believe they spent years in development and this is the product they released. I got a few hours in and I can't go any longer. After my lackluster character creation and that abomination that is Road to Elite or whatever and the utter mess that is the games AI, there is no way I can stomach any more of this mess. While I have No Mercy loaded up, I'm going to play a real arcade style game and at nearly 24 years old it's still vastly superior to this garbage.\t\t\t\t\t
