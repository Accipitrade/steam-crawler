\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy first wrestling game was WCW/NWO Revenge and I was still playing it up until 6/29/2023. The only times I stopped in the past was for Here Comes the Pain and for a brief time with Fire Pro. The simple pick up and play aspect of this game is spot on with what I loved about those games. Matches are fun. That should always be the primary focus. So for that alone I recommend this game. Every match type in the game is fun because the base game is fun. My favorite mode is probably lights out with the Ladder match a close second. I do wish a cage match was in the game.

If you are into creating robust CAW then you will be disappointed by the creation suite. I was able to make a decent Bret Hart though so it isn't un-workable. Just not up to the level we have been accustomed to since SvR 2011.

Road to Elite (the story mod) is fun. I have been in some pretty entertaining stories. I played first as Kenny Omega and became the first AEW Champion and re-wrote history. Now I am playing as Bret Hart and just joined the inner circle. Some of the activities can get repetitive but I do think it is more fun when playing with a CAW as you have more to do with building up your character.

For me this is a game that I will keep coming back to over and over again just to play a fun wrestling game for 5-20 minutes a match. Its not a simulation and it wasn't trying to be. Although I did discover in my last match before this review that if you kick out at 2.99 it will flash that up in a cool way similar to how Fire Pro does.

8/10 from me with hopes that the creation suite and match modes can be expanded upon in free updates down the line.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is literally not finished.

Extremely weird AI bugs, an absolutely bare bones wrestler edit, very beta-like tutorial and feel for the most part. The game is supposed to be an homage to the AKI wrestling games and while the groundwork is there for a good game and the engine is very well done, this thing needs to be put back in the oven for quite a bit.

The game does feel like the classic AKI engine (WCW vs. the World, WCW/NWO Revenge, WWF No Mercy, etc.) and I have to give them credit for that, but that's about the only thing they got right.

Every other thing about this game feels like they built a good engine, and were just starting the framework around it. It's that bad. And honestly --- I am not an anti-AEW troll, I am a fan since day 1. Do yourself a favor and do NOT spend over 50 dollars on this game until it's worth it. In all honestly, in a few months it will either be massively improved, or on sale for half price.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe more I play it the more fun it is! It's just a fun wrestling game that reminds me of the older great ones. It is a bit bare bones atm, but there is some depth there that's under the surface. Yeah graphics could be better, customization could be better and so forth, but this is easy to pick up and play and has surprising depth in the ring if you care to learn what each character can do. Orange Cassidy can wrestle a whole match with his hands in his pockets...the attention to detail in the character move sets are awesome. I've only played 4 matches online so far but all have worked just fine. Just like any wrestling game it's better with friends. I have no problem supporting this kind of game and can't wait to see what they add in the future!\t\t\t\t\t
