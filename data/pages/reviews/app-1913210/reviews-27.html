\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a high quality wrestling game, very reminiscent of the games of the N64 and PS2 era. It has a few bugs as of the time of me writing this, but they do not make the game unplayable. I clipped into the crowd on occasion when brawling on the outside and and a few goofy hit detection moments, but it's a far sight better than 2K20.

It is VERY easy to pick up and play. My wife is a somewhat casual and cozy gamer who is fairly new to the genre and, once she realized that in training mode she could just beat up on Christian until she learned the game controls she was quite pleased and learned quickly.

The minigames are fun diversions and good ways to play the game in party mode when you get tired of exploding barbed wire death matches, and the story mode is very similar to a classic Street Fighter or Mortal Kombat experience with a little bit of management thrown in to allow you to choose different ways to organically advance the story down different pathways. From what I've seen after two play throughs, losing doesn't mean the story is over, you just go on to a different branching path of the storyline.

AEW: Fight Forever isn't the absurdly polished next gen photorealistic wrestling game WWE puts out, but does that really matter if they weren't aiming for that as the goal? They prioritized accessibility and arcade feel over realism, and it turns out creating something that's supposed to be fun rather than realistic makes for a pretty fun video game. The addition of the in-game currency is a bit odd as you'll likely buy out the store after 20 hours of play by my estimation, but it leaves me hopeful for additional free content down the road.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHidden master piece that requires time. The fighting system is outstanding once you figure it out. Blocking and timing are the hidden most important part of this game. It's confusing for wrestling you would think offense would be the key.

Like:
Hidden Depth - there is a constant record system of all matches and history of highest ranked matches (it will be neat after a year or so to see how insane some Win/ Lose records will be)
Fighting system - blocking is all timing based and it's skill based, so a skilled player will beat a button masher but the button masher can also be competitive
Fun - it had a video game vibe, it takes it self serious where it should and has fun with everything else (you pitch a wrestler as a baseball c'mon that's awesome)

Dislike:

AI - The AI has been either dumb or god mode. I've only played normal and easy so far. The AI needs some tweaking.
No GM Mode - WTF Tony Khan this is literally the Tony Khan mode. Some GM/Franchise type mode should be playable. It's a 1st game so simple is fine but a let down.
Glitches - it's day 1 so it's expected nothing bad but theres some that shouldn't have made it this far

Overall - Could be a CHAMPion with some work but bare bone day 1 it's really good and fun. Really fun with a group of 3 to 4 people.
IT'S INCREDIBLE IF YOU ARE A AEW FAN, N64 WRESTLING FAN, or a WRESTLING FAN. BUT there is a learning curve and adjustment time needed to get a feel for the fighting system.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLets do something different and get the negatives out of the way first.

- For some reason you need to have an Epic account to play this game,
- The create a wrestler section is seriously lacking which means no CAWs of outside wrestlers or wrestlers who weren't added in the game... At least none that look good.
- Serious lack of Arenas, All out, Full Gear, Dark, Rampage and Dynamite( which the last 3 are basically the same)
- Music for some wrestlers like Orange Cassidy is outdated
- The controls are wonky. For example LB/RP are used to block attacks and grapples. They are also used to get in/out of the ring. RB is also used to grab weapons from under the ring, but not just anywhere, you have to be at a specific spot at the side of the ring to grab a weapon... its somewhere near the middle.

Now the positives

+ The roster is relatively deep.
+ There are limited match types but the match types are fun to play.
+ Wins / Losses count throughout the game, from singles to career mode, they all count and are shown during entrances.
+ When your wrestler bleeds, they bleed all over the ring mat, etc.
+ The career mode, despite feeling cheap with JPEG backgrounds, does have some unique ideas. It gives you a sense of what its like to be a wrestler, going from town to town, etc.

Overall thoughts:

Its a good starting point. Though I can see why this is priced at last gen console game prices. Is it worth $60 or even the $50 that I got it on sale for the day it released? (thanks summer sale) probably not. However, if you have a group of friends who are into wrestling games, I can see this being loads of fun like the old WCW / NWO and WWF games on N64. Most of the things I complained about could be patched or expanded upon via DLC (though I doubt they will). If you can pick the game up for around $30, I'd say its worth a play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWas fairly hyped, thought I set myself some realistic expectations for the game, where I wasn't quite jumping into the "OMG NEW GAME!!!" train, while at the same time not being overly negative about the small things I was noticing in the trailers and gameplay. Somehow, I still overhyped myself.

I do havesome positives that I'll try to start with here. The game has potential to improve at least some of the gripes I have, so it can become an OK game that I'd recommend on sale. I think the blood is fairly decent, there's some nice stuff in the way moves and the combat feels, it's just that it's a bit bare bones (I'll explain below,) there's a nice little charm to a lot of the game that you can tell there was some love in parts of the game.

The gameplay...it's somewhere between a 4 and a 6 out of 10.

The moves feel like what you're normally used to, however because of how the controls are mapped and how the mechanics of certain things like grappling work, I feel like it's really limited and almost clunky. I don't know how to express this properly, but there's just no polish or depth to the combat. You'll hear that a lot in reviews I would say. Not just in the combat.
The movement is horrendous, your character feels like it's old, tired and works with children, it's sooooo slow. To multiply this as a problem, the run/sprint button is a one press - one direction run. You cannot run in multiple directions.

The graphics are mostly alright to me, however there are just certain parts and certain characters that just look like they're not even in pre alpha. I think it has a charm to it overall that I would've been onboard with - had the gameplay been a 7 or above.

Match types...I don't want to say I'm disgusted, but literally every major wrestling game for the last...I don't know how long, has had a good variety of match types. This game just feels like there's about 3 or 4. I haven't counted them, and I dare not look into it, or I'll just get depressed. Again. The match types that it does have, are almost as depressing as the lack of quantity of them. The Casino Battle Royal match cannot hold more than 4 people at one time. It only spawns the next person when someone gets eliminated. The way to eliminate people or get eliminated is atrocious too. So heavily disappointed with this match in particular. I think this one sums up the game, there's so little polish and depth put into the game modes, that one of AEW's flagship matches gets put into the game, but it's like it's explained by some lad down the pub who barely speaks the same language as the person putting it together.

The career mode feels like it's not even in a pre-alpha stage. This one was going to be the saving grace for me. Then I played it and I can see why Cody jumped back to WWE. It's like they played Wrestling Empire for a day, then tried to put it together on the last day of the college deadline.

D-von - Do NOT touch that table. If you touch a table in this game, even by brushing off of it by the slightest of touches, it will fall over, and that lovely table spot you were hoping to do, will be ruined. This deserved it's own little paragraph.

Referees are atrocious (lol AEW refs bad.) Worse than their real life counterparts. Don't get me wrong, I don't like to hate on the refs in AEW, I quite like some of them, but they do some awful indie stuff in real life. In this game, the refs are so slow, they make the snail that's chasing me from Earth 118-49-C look fast.

I don't have a clue - with all of the delays this game had - how this game released in the state it launched in. I wanted it to be good, and I have some hope that the devs can turn it around into an alright game. Don't think they'll fix the core issues, however, it's Yukes, they're normally great, so they do have some credit in the bank so to speak.

One last detail as well that sums up the game. The commentary in the game is only in certain parts in cutscenes etc. It's for some reason not in all cutscenes, even ones that are almost the exact same as one you'd have watched a minute before, but honestly, I wish it was disabled altogether. They get JR and Excalibur to read out their lines like "It's Saturday Night, you know what that means" as if they're at the commentary table. Well that's how it's supposed to sound. The way they actually do it however... They basically get JR to read out something as if it's him reading a prayer at a funeral. I don't know how it got past the recording booth, let alone how it was allowed into the release or past QA.
