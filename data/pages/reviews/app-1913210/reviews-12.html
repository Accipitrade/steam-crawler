\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, to be honest I'm divided on my decision but have to give this a thumbs down...for now. AEW: Fight Forever clearly has effort put into its content from what I witnessed and played, but frankly not worth the full price as it stands at this time. Let me start with the positives:

The controls are great, not what I'm typically use to with other wrestling games but when you get past that learning curve its rewarding with fair but challenging fights. Create-a-Wrestler is always welcome in a game like this, and the match types available are pretty fun too. When it comes down to fighting the matches can be a blast and reminiscent of earlier generation wrestling games.

The big negative: the drawback is the anemic amount of content. Everything I saw in AEW was well polished in presentation and in execution...but compared to even WWE games from five years ago it feels like you have an unfinished but well crafted game on your hands. The entrances only go so far as to the start of someone's entrance with no flashy stuff walking on down to the ring or getting in to hype up the crowd...the arena customization options are about as limited, and match types underwhelming in number. Even character customization feels like a 4th of the options the average WWE game has. There is a career mode, however it doesn't particularly stand out to me...and the lack of a mode equivalent to WWE's Universe mode with managing entire shows worth of matches really demonstrates how bare bones the content is at the moment.

I really want to like this game more, but the limited content for a full AAA price tag just doesn't seem worth it. Maybe this review will change over time, pending further updates and additional content, but for now this game comes off to me as a discount buy.\t\t\t\t\t
