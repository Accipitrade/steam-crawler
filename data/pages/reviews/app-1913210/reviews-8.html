\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very detailed, niche, and fun. It's not perfect, but I'm excited for what else they add to it.

As a longtime wrestling fan, and wrestling fame fan, i love this game.


Playing online is addicting, since matches rarely go past 5 minutes, so i find myself not worn out after a couple of runs. Playing ranked is very satisfying when you get a win, there are some balance/cheesing things.(Bail Out, Guradbreak, In Unyielding Poise.).

It is a very difficult game to master, you definitely can not play this game well with just button mashing, there is a flow to things. Like a fighting game though, when you play someone else who knows what they're doing, matches can get pretty exciting.

Road to the Elite is fun, reminds me of Yakuza the way the cutscenes play out.


If you are looking for more of a simulation, this is not for you. If you like Fire Pro Wrestling, Virtual Pro, King Of Colliseum, or the old AKI games, you'll dig it.

It appears very bare bones, but with the recently announced Stadium Stampede mode, and just the general idea that it's going to be updated a lot. I am optimistic for the future.


8/10 from me.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA lot of fun but not perfect by any means.

The game play is sort of a blend of the old PS1 Smackdown! and N64 No Mercy / Wresltemania 2000 games, which for me is exactly what I wanted in a wrestling game. This is the most important thing to me, the core of the game is excellent.

On the downside -
The AI is a bit of a pain during Tag, Ladder, 3 way & 4 way matches. 1 on 1 and the Battle Royal matches seem to work out the best. There's not as many match types as I'd like, should be a steel cage one for example. The CAW is limited and the main roster is out of date and seems to be missing a lot of wrestlers that should be included. The pinning / kick out system is not good, it needs to be much easier to kick out of pins both for the player and the CPU. You can get pinned without ever taking a signature move or finisher, the button mashing system for kicking out favours the person doing the pin far too much and need re-balancing to get longer matches and avoid cheap early wins/loses.

All the downsides are fixable IMO, it's already much more fun than the WWE 2k games and with some tweaks would wipe the floor with it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGotta get this out straight away.
This is legit the most fun that I have had with a wrestling game since...WWE 2K15???
It brings back that love that I have had for wrestling back when I was no-lifing SvR 2011 and copying all of the CAWs from online forums and trying to get platinum (which I never could after the servers shut down). The gameplay is fun and chaotic, the models look WAY WAY WAY better than the goofy portraits that they chose for the game menus, and I really don't mind the visuals at all. They really make the game stand out from 2K's clearly visual focused titles. (I'm sorry, I don't enjoy the "simulation" gameplay that every sports games are leaning towards.)

THAT BEING SAID.

This game is absolutely overpriced for what we have at the moment.
I wouldn't say it's UNFINISHED but MAN do I wish there was more...stuff. This creative suite is actually quite laughable, the match types have a lot left to be desired and the main campaign is silly but goodness could they have gotten a better performance from JR or just use Excalibur? AND MORE ACTUAL VOICE ACTING? I mean, they have all of the wrestlers VOICED IN COMBAT.

Would I recommend this game RIGHT NOW?
If you are an AEW fan or a fan of the old N64 games, go for it! Other than that, wait for a sale and more content. They definitely seem like they have a road-map and I think I trust them to add more to this title. All in all, I am thoroughly enjoying it and can't wait to see what else AEW and Yuke's has in store.\t\t\t\t\t
