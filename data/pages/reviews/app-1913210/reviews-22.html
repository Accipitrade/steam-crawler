\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy wife and I love this game! As a huge fan of the older AKI games, such as: No Mercy, Virtual Pro Wrestling, WCW vs NWO, etc. this game pays homage well and plays like a modern version of those games. It was developed by Yukes after all!

The Road to Elite mode is fun, hilarious, and surprising at times, although it's short around 4-5 hours overall, the replay-ability factor is super high with the branching stories and situations that can take place. I'm on my second play-through of it currently!

The CAW suite has a great foundation and I'm optimistic they'll add a ton more items and content in the future. There are tons of items and entrances already in the game that point to wrestlers from NJPW, Impact, ROH, and WWE both being either create-able or in the game officially later on. Buy it! Play it with your friends!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately the concerns I had watching the trailers/teasers before release were not taken away after actually playing the game.
Wrestling feels clunky. Create-a-wrestler is about as basic as it gets. Create-a-moveset doesn't even have the ability to filter by move type, and from what I've read in another review has issues saving... So, maybe after they actually finish the game I'll see about potentially buying again.

Couple disclaimers. Firstly, I love WWF No Mercy. This game is nowhere near that, and frankly it felt a bit overconfident of them to compare themselves to a legendary game like that. Secondly, no I will not give them a break for it being their first game. As the devs that worked on this game are not new to this genre...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you liked No Mercy on the N64, it does play like it as promised. Matches are fast paced and counter-heavy in more difficult fights.

The only Issues I found were that when knocked down you can be down for a long time even when damage is light, and there's a bit of button bloat like having the switch target on L3 (so constantly switching targets when trying to do certain moves), but these can likely be rectified later.

The CAW is very barebones appearance-wise, but the movesets are wide so you can make a fun to play character. Though hopefully the options for appearance will improve in the future.

The story mode is corny but fun to play. It has routes based on win/loss and lots of fights to make up for how short many fights can be. Took 2-3 hours to finish one journey through the World Champion Route

Have not tried Online. There are concerns about the Epic Account requirement, but as far as I can see it is optional.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't ever write reviews but I feel the need to leave a positive review for Fight Forever.
This game is very weird in the best kind of way.
Spiking Kenny Omega in the face with a giant bouncy ball. Taking a selfie with Jon Moxley in front of a JPG of the Liberty Bell. The bone crunch sound effect every time you do a move outside the ring. Being able to spam insane pyro during the very brief entrances. This game is wildly goofy.
This game is incredibly rough around the edges, too, and it does feel unfinished in places- character creation is limited, voiceover work is all over the place, etc. But the core gameplay is solid, the mechanics are pretty easy to pick up, and I can huck an exploding propane tank at the Young Bucks. It's such a wild departure from what I expect from a wrestling game in this era, and for that I have to give it a thumbs up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn the last couple of years we only had a choice of two major wrestling games: the glossy simulation of WWE2K... and the insanity of Wrestling Empire, a title that is more drugs than game.

AEW Fight Forever is somewhere in between, slickly animated, pure arcade fun but with a degree of "unrealism" that give it a quirky, even gonzo charm and personally there's nothing wrong with that at all. Another title, another voice, a distinct one no less, in the realm of wrestling games in the vein of an era when there was as much choice of wrestling game as any other genre.

While I know this will not be for everyone, if you are a wrestling fan you owe it to yourself to at least try this title, for the sake of wrestling games at large (and try not to be a internet wrestling community tribalistic **** about it).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPOSITIVE-WITH HEAVY CAUTION

I'm reviewing as positive, because there is a great foundation here, and I want to see the game grow. BUT, there is ALOT that needs to be done by the devs to get it there.

So, let's get the positives out of the way. The meat of the gameplay is great. Controls are responsive, and everything mostly makes sense. Fight Forever has struck a perfect balance between the pure spectacle of the 2K series, and the pure arcade feel of FirePro. There are however some glaring exceptions. It should be possible to put someone into a seated position from the ground (you can only do that with certain moves), and there was no reason why we can't have additional slots for rear grapples and corner grapples.

The graphics are....... 'not great, not terrible'. People have been unfairly throwing around N64 or PS2 graphics. And they're only that bad when you compare them to 2K23. Take that out of the equation, and it would be fair to say they're on par with earlier 2K games from early in the PS4/Xbone era. They're...... acceptable.

What's NOT acceptable however, is the creation suite. I heard rumours that the creation suite was going to be mid. Mid would be a compliment. What we have, is embarrassingly lacking. And I have examples. Oh boy do I have examples......

*We have the barest of bare minimum sliders. You get 4 preset body types, and you get to change height, bodyfat, and musculature. THAT'S IT! You have very few preset faces, which you can't edit in any way! Oh, and let's talk about hair. Female hair presets have 2 pieces repeated 3 times per piece. You could have saved 4 slots just by giving us a length slider.

*We have so many merch shirts. Basically, the same T-shirt repeated over and over again but with different logos on them. Why? Why can't we just put decals on the one shirt? Why are we being lazy and wasting clothing slots?

*On that note, why don't we have a socks layer? Or an accessory layer for lower bodies? (belts?) Any reason we can't throw a shirt or a jacket over that one piece leotard as entrance attire? Why can we only have a single basic tattoo? This is stuff Yuke's were doing for WWE cas during late stage THQ. Why is none of this here?

An arguably as equally egregious offender is the lack of moves available for your moveset. The way moves are categorised are poor at best, or non-existent at worst. And even how you can build your moveset. Your controls in a grapple are bound by high strikes-which can only be punches, low strikes-which can only be kicks, and throws-which can only be well, throws. You want to create a pure grappler? Or a boxer? Or a brawler? Maybe a fancy-pants martial artist? NOPE! So the barebones creation kit extends to your moveset as well. And the worst part: certain things like slots for signatures or finishers are locked off until you play path to elite. So to unlock those slots, or even simple attributes, you have to take 'some guy or gal' through a boring ass story mode which...... I just don't care for.

And then if that wasn't salt enough, Yuke's has sealed away any hope of modding-something that 2K seem to no longer give a ♥♥♥♥ about in comparison. OK, I get that they want to protect their DLC model. That's fine. But then if that's the case...... open up the workshop. That way, they can actively police and curate community content.

Buuut hey. You can do full contact intergender matches-something that you haven't been able to do in WWE games for over a decade. Also Cody's now in both companies current games, so that's nice. Y'know what? On that note, let's make him the guest character for Tekken 8. And Mortal Kombat '1'. And SF6. AMERICAN NIGHTMARE EVERYWHERE!

Despite this mostly scathing review, I'm marking this as positive. Because, I see potential for the series to grow. And it NEEDS to grow, because WWE need a fire lit under their asses on all fronts. Even on video games which they have lorded a monopoly over for FAR too long.\t\t\t\t\t
