\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAEW: Fight Forever, is it All In? Or All Out?

In terms of gameplay, it proves itself to be a bunch of wacky fun. Playing singleplayer against AI bugs weren't noticeable yet many of the clunky mechanics that have been in other pro-wrestling videogames for decades once more make their appearance.

*Opponent Targeting
*Stiff Controls
*Lack of Direct Gameplay Guidance (Not Tooltips)

For Gamemodes there's a mere handful, making it feel like a games conference tech demo instead of a fully-fledged finished product. 1v1 is probably the best option, with tag-team bouts proving capable too... But that's about it.

*Ladder Matches 1v1 Only
*Battle Royale Can't Be Customised (Females/Males in the same match)

Looking at the roster, it's certainly not broad... Which was expected, but when John Silver is included yet his tag team partner Alex Reynolds isn't, that makes you scratch your head.

*Huge Lack of AEW Talent (Evil Uno, Ethan Page, Claudio Castagnoli, Jake Hager, The Acclaimed, Billy Gunn, Brody King, Buddy Matthews, Shawn Spears, Diamant, Penelope Ford... The list goes on)

Customisation is also a feature and is sadly lacklustre, if you're a casual gamer not wanting for much you can create a decent looking character but probably keep it to one, since they'll all look similar.

*Severe Lack of Options and Variety

This is only after a short while with the game, and I certainly hope Yuke's manage to use this as a base to build upon. But, as the game itself is complete there's no improving the janky gameplay and that might mean that this is not revolution we hoped for but rather a downgraded knock-off version of the already quality devoid WWE2K games.\t\t\t\t\t
