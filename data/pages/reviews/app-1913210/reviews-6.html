This is a good first attempt at a wrestling game and I know they are planning to do a bunch of updates coming up. My biggest concern is it being abandoned after like a year like their mobile game was.

This is not the most polished game. I won the TNT championship in the story mode but I guess cause of the way it’s structured I lost it at sone point and it never appeared on my character. Multiple glitches are easy to find and the woman’s rooster is so much smaller then the man’s. Luckily the woman can get all the titles and the matches are intergender which makes up for it.

The gameplay is fast and fun and doesn't feel like a slog. However the button mashing to escape a pin is kinda bull especially if your like me and cannot get good at button mashing.

Customization is neat. I’m not a fan of lots of sliders and adjusters but with the paltry selection of options it kinda feels like they would have been welcome here. Plus the fact that there’s like no ring attire for the women and you have like 5 wrestling bras and that’s it kinda sucks.

Overall I see the potential in this game as long as they update it in the coming months I will continue to recommend it. I would say if your not a big AEW fan and prefer the 2K style of game, wait for a sale before you buy.\t\t\t\t\t

Pros

-Not verified yet, but works great on Steam Deck at max settings with no tweaking required.

-Strong nostalgia bait here. Feels like an old arcade style wrestling game a la mid 2000s WWE Smackdown v. Raw or Here Comes the Pain

-Gameplay is engaging, matches are fun and countering specifically is difficult but rewarding.

-Intergender is back for those who are into that.


Cons

-Road to Elite feels kinda low effort and empty

-Customization is barebones; VERY few options for custom wrestlers, very little to unlock, lots of recolors of the same attire, no in depth body or face morphing; basically just presets; Yuke's definitely need to do better here and add more options in future updates

-Hardly any match types

I've enjoyed playing the game a lot, but it falls in the same vein as a super fun early access game. At $60, it ain't worth it. Elite Edition is basically just crumbs so don't bother.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAEW Fight Forever lacks depth but is still very fun to play and is probably the most pure fun I've had with a new wrestling game since WWE All-Stars. It won't be for everyone (at least not at launch) but for those craving a new wrestling game that turns away from sim style gameplay for something a little more fast and fun then it's worth giving a shot.

+Gameplay is great! Easy to pick up and play but with enough depth for more experienced players to really enjoy.
+Each wrestler really feels unique and a great reflection of their real-life counterparts.
+The animations are fantastic and add to the impact of the gameplay.
+Road to Elite single player mode is short but has different paths to explore that encourage multiple playthroughs. Plus the RPG-very-lite between match stuff reminded me of the Yakuza games a little so that's cool.
+Lots of weapons, crazy matches and blood!
+Great to play with friends!

-The creation/customization suite absolutely sucks right now but THQ is planning on expanding it (and the game a whole) with updates so hopefully any big gripes will be fixed over time.
-Roster has some notable absences
-RTE might be too basic for some people and the cool Yakuza-like stuff can get repetitive after several playthroughs.
-Ranked online matches are fun but there are too many people cheesing them or quitting.
-Odd quirks like moves not being sorted, match types in RTE that aren't selectable in VS, "customizable" wrestler attire thats limited to t-shirts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI write this review with the full knowledge that making a video game starting from almost scratch is hard.

It is entirely too hard to time a counter. It is entirely too hard to kick out of a pin. Even with 'feedback' turned on it is not useful in showing you how and when to counter, or how effective your button mashing is to get out of a pin. I honestly think that the button mashing to get out of a pin is bugged as I have yet to kick out of any single pin I've ever been covered with. Training mode is very bare bones, does not offer near enough options, and does not offer basic training scenarios to help with any of this.

I understand that rosters have to be locked in at a certain point, but even at the certain point they were locked in, there were not nearly enough wrestlers to justify some of the 'stables' that were set up in game. They are obviously going to sell them later as DLC in bite size chunks at an inflated price.

Every wrestler you create yourself needs to go through Road to Elite to have any chance of competing with the pre-made roster. There's not a whole lot of customization options, and even the power fantasy of creating your own move list is severely limited because most of the options for your character are locked behind a Road to Elite playthrough.

All that said, and despite the game needing a LOT more work, the framework of what they have to work with is promising, and I have hope that further iterations, whether they happen with patches or a future release, will live up to the vibe they were attempting to recreate as an alternative choice to the options currently available.

Thumbs in the middle.\t\t\t\t\t
