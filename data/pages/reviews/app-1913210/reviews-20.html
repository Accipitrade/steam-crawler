\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoundation really polished.
Game has potential to be one of the best wrestling games.
Road To Elite seems shallow, but if it is a foundation of the mode. It is really strong one.
4 Acts, has 4 PPV, those have 4 Dynamites each. So in those 4 weeks leading to the finale of the act, you get to choose how you deal with your time (get 4 turns every week), you can do minigames (earn cash, exp), go fight on AEW Dark/Rampage (money/exp), eat (energy/random interaction with wrestler is possible which can lead to challenge, or just a specific text written for let's say Omega, that actually fits what Omega would say). You can go to Gym (exp, also can find wrestlers), you can go Sightseeing (mental health, also interaction with wrestler), and you can also do a press conference (mental health, energy, interaction). After you complete all 4 acts, the story mode ends. You have to start again, with another wrestler.
Point of this mode is to unlock hidden wrestlers. Not easy to unlock, as every act is random. There are 4 acts, every act has 3 stories each. And you can get act 1A, act 2A, act 3A, act 4A. Or you can get Act 1C-2A-3A-1B (it's rng, while certain wrestlers have higher chances, and win/loss also give you branching out on ACT you are in).
It seems the devs might update this mode with endless feature. As there are mechanics that would made more sense to be in endless run. I expect if the game sells well, that they will eventually add even more branching out acts. And that will open up possibility for continuation of seasons. Similar to Smackdown 2 on PS1 where you had Main story repeat every year while everything else was randomised.

Combat: At first I thought it was really clunky. But, after I learnt how to move I noticed that the combat has great depth. You actually have to move around, use tactics, learn defence and right offence timing. You also learn about different and unique wrestlers. The styles that combined with their stats makes each wrestler encourage a specific tactic. From agile luchador like Rey Fenix to immovable wall Wardlow, from aggressor Lance Archer to cheater MJF. You learn about each wrestlers strengths and weaknesses, you find opportunities and threats that you have to acknowledge.

Customisation: Lacking a lot of ways of creating your wrestler, you can still make something if you are creative (for example Roman Reigns), but as of now there are not many choices. But, yet again, the creation suite if it is a foundation upon which they add content. I can see it being really good. Right now, the create a wrestler seems like a template for a create a wrestler mode. I wouldn't buy this game for create a wrestler. 2K far superior in that case.

Lack of Features: Lack of modes, only 4 wrestlers can be in the ring. That is what I heard so far. I don't think 4 wrestler limit is a negative. I think it is a really big positive. Think about it, when you played 6 man, 8 man matches. How much control you had. For me, it was fun to play once or twice. After honeymoon phase passed, and I learned how to play, those modes looked broken and unbalanced to the point they were just annoying to play. That is why 1v1 and 2v2 always were more fun to play. 3 way and 4 way matches are already really chaotic, so why add even more chaos. In 4 Way even when it is chaotic, you can at least do something without everyone constantly getting in your way (or camera view going as high as the sealing).
The choice to keep this game a Balanced Arcade Fighter really shows. As the matches flow fast and when you know how to do what you want, there will not be ton of things you can't control getting in your way. Matches last around 3min. But if you are a good player, you will avoid getting hit with a finisher or a signature (make sense for a wrestling game).
I wish there would be more modes like 4 way Ladder, 3 way ladder, Steel Cage (pin/submission), Iron Man, Two out of Three falls. I can see them adding those matches later on in few years. Depends on how much devs and publisher listens to the feedback.
This is where the price tag comes, it's a lot of money. Still, we all know Pro Wrestling is a niche culture, and Pro Wrestling Games aren't most popular fighting games (Tekken,MK,Street Fighter). It's expensive to keep servers running, and you can't keep a product alive if it only costs you money without returning anything for your investment. It can't be just good faith, I can see the price tag validated for those who want to support the development of this game. It's not called early access, as it is finished and can be played and enjoyed. But, if they do actually intend to add more content to it, then DLC and the Price tag is the only thing that will give them a reason to keep it going. Smaller audience, bigger price.
I bought a game, that I a AEW total Mark really like. And I also wanted to support the product that I got a bit of faith in. I knew it will be bad as it's their first game, but now when I play it, it is actually a fun game. By the way, for those who want to play online, go join AEW Fight Forever Discord group (just get Discord, do that for all Online games, best place to find normal non toxic people to play any online games with). Devs encourage to join the Discord as well, because you can find Online matches, but it takes a lot of time to search via game. And there are ton of people in official Discord group that wants to play against anyone and just have fun.

I gave a long summary about this game and why I see ton of potential in it, while it is also a good game already if you know how to play. But, that summary (that took 2 hours to write), got deleted after I clicked Allow Comments.... Anyone want to destroy me and my Main Eddie Kingston are welcome to invite.

I recommend this game to those who like to learn the mechanics of a game. There is actual depth that is hard to see at first. Great foundation game-play wise\t\t\t\t\t

The controls are quick to pick up and you can pretty much drop anyone into the game and make the fighty people hit each other unlike the 2K games. If you have friends this is the wrestling game you all can sit on a couch and play. Key positives for this game is extremely ambitious music selection, multiplayer fun, and core fighting experience. Negatives include weird roster omissions of legacy/important AEW names such as Uno/Stu, Acclaimed, etc, limited create-a suite, and hit or miss aesthetic choices

Pick it up if you: Love AEW and Oldstyle wrestling games and have friends

Pass it if you: Hate AEW/Don't know anything about AEW, care about aesthetics over substance, and don't have friends.\t\t\t\t\t
