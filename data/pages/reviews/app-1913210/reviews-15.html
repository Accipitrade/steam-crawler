\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan. I knew I should've listened to my gut in not getting this game til 6 months later to at the very least give them time to work on some of the game mechanics more.

I've NEVER once refunded a game. I cannot recommend this game in the state is in right now. I've been playing wrestling games since they first appeared and though lots of them as of recent have been less than stellar, I used to love it when Yukes / THQ did them back in the day. Now I love my nostalgia like the next oldie and all, but we did NOT need the clunkyness to come along as well.

Here's a brief breakdown.

Graphics: Everyone asked for the nostalgia that was in the old WWE games from the PS2 era and you got them on here.
Not the best graphics compared to the recent WWE2k games, but they were ok. However there are A LOT of clipping issues.

The controls: Oh boy where to begin. If you're expecting a smooth counter transition on here like on the WWE games? Well let's just say if you have hair or any hair left on your head, then you're gonna be bald by the time you even remotely learn the counter mechanics on here. The hit boxes themselves are seriously off. So even though you know you've connected with certain attack combos, the game doesn't register them correctly. Or at the very least fast enough. If you're screwed up and are at the receiving end of getting pinned and getting counted, forget about mashing the 4 keys / buttons it places on the screen. I've tried everything from mashing all button at the same time, mashing them separately along with just moving you analog sticks (I play with controllers) around and you still lose bouts. Oh and also, if you're in a 3 / 4 way match? Forget about any hopes of winning when even you knowing the patters of each opponent and they still manage to pull a move on you. They hella team up on you HARD in this game instead of focusing on each other mostly like the other wrestling games. And if you're in a handicap match? put the controller down, you ain't doing jack-shish.

I played each mode on the game in order to take a break from the aneurysm that was the career mode and still most of the headaches you go through on the career mode in regards to the fighting mechanic is a lot worse on exhibition. The only thing I didn't touch was online. If it was bad offline, I could imagine the migraine that would ensue IF it would be online.

I REALLY can't believe I'm going to side with WWE on this one, but man, I was so looking forward to this game ever since it was announced and even bought the special edition too. I was met with disappointment sadly. I wanted to like the game, but with all those issues with controls, and lack of options on certain things, it's just not worth it. I'm at the process of refunding.

OH! And the CAS, the place I normally like to spend hours on in the OTHER game? It doesn't have a robust selection so you can forget about creating something very unique. SO much things they have / NEED to add in even if you have the DLCs from the special edition. More variety of hair, more outfits, more outfit options to place things on with not a lot of restrictions (and let me tells ya, there are lots of restrictions). Lets not even get me started on the entrances which are possibly 10 - 15 seconds long. The only thing different is that you can put effects and pyros and can time them on the ramp... but for what? The ring entrance is almost non existent.

I will actually say that the mini games stuff on the career mode is actually fun though. So this wasn't total shishshow. Still, it won't get me to continue to own this game. Now I know they will be adding things in the future and such, but man do they have to fix the fighting mechanics really hard. Like I said earlier, I wish I waited for another 6 months at the very least in case the game was a lot more ironed out. Who knows, I may get this game again IF (and that's a HUGE if) they fix the mechanics, add lots of other things for your CAS as well as add options for more clothes onto your person. Lastly, extend the blasted entrances please, that was one of the things I loved about wrestling in general, not just wrestling games.

So if they fix the game for the better and it's on sale, I'll probably pick it back up, but for now? I definitely WILL NOT recommend this game. Unless you're a masochist... to each their own.

So, I'm just gonna say this clearly since this has just become a hassle. DO.NOT. BUY. THIS. GAME. Especially for the many reasons I've listed before the update, but also if you wanna have a hard time trying to request a refund.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun game, I appreciate the return to form on an arcadey wrestling game, something that the WWE 2k series cannot provide. If that's all you want, and you have friends to enjoy it with, maybe this game is for you. But unfortunately, this game lacks single player content of worth. The career mode is fine, if a bit unremarkable, but there isn't much else from there. The creation suite is an absolute joke, wrestling games from 1999 had more options than this. And without a decent creation suite, I can't find the game having much longevity for myself. I do hold out hope though that this game will be supported long enough to start to shine, instead of just relying on yearly releases that just take babysteps every year.

If you want alternatives for fun arcadey wrestling games, maybe consider giving Action Arcade Wrestling (formerly the Chikara game) or MDickie's Wrestling Empire a shot instead, and wait for this one to go on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
