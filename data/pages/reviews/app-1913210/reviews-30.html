\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is terribly disappointing to cut a long story short.
While this game aims to emulate the style of WWF No Mercy, it fails to match up in quality and polish and makes very little attempt to innovate or improve upon the archaic design choices and limitations of an over 20 year old game, and falls short of it's inspiration in all aspects.

I went in to this game with some hope and expecting to be surprised by how fun it would be, but I felt deflated immediately after playing my first singles match.

The gameplay is okay but not up to scratch with No Mercy, or any of Yuke's previous WWE games in the past 20 years, there's less depth and polish here than other titles. Worth noting is the hit detection isn't great and you'll feel bewildered when your strikes and grapples miss for seemingly no reason, you have to be on-point with your positioning here. The AI can also get stuck unable to move around downed wrestlers, and they seem to spam taunts alot. The blind button mashing for pins and submissions, while paying homage to No Mercy and present in many older wrestling titles, feels outdated here. TNA Impact managed to implement a nice visual for the button mashing to indicate how close you are to kicking out and added some great tension to pinfalls, something that could be easily implemented and disabled for those who want a more classic experience.
My feeling while playing this was that, while this plays like No Mercy, why would I pay so much money for a game to do a poor job replicating it rather than innovating it? Outside of new moves and weapons there is nothing new to experience here. The short entrances I feel also exemplify the fact that this is taking too much in the way of inspiration rather than trying to improve on the game they've pulled from, the short entrances were seemingly a space limitation of the N64 game, as No Mercy's predecessor, Wrestlemania 2000, featured full entrances, but had much less in the way of visual quality and overall content to it's sequel. Here it seems like the entrances are like this to save time and money, under the guise of paying homage.

I'd say my biggest gripe with the gameplay is the match length, my singles matches ran around 3-4 minutes every time, and there are no settings or adjustments you can make to dictate whether your matches will be long or short. When you or your opponent are in the "danger" state, and a pin attempt is made, consider the match over. I think a big reason for this is the incredibly fast rate in which the momentum meter builds and falls, which feels like a poor and simplistic implementation compared to No Mercy.

There is so little content in this game, there's a Season Mode called "Road to The Elite", which from what I've seen doesn't interest me personally but you may get some enjoyment out of it. From what I've played of the mini-games available at the start, they are just a novelty, something you will play once or twice and laugh at, and never come back to. The creation tools are quite frankly embarrassingly limited as you just chose from a very small selection of preset faces and generic clothing, and there is no Universe or GM Mode which while not mandatory, are the most popular and requested modes in wrestling games. Then there's the shockingly low amount of match types, there are no Tables matches, no Iron Man matches, no Submission matches, and most surprising of all, no Multi-Man Ladder matches or 6-Man tags, both of which are staples of the company. There's so little to do here and it is so incredibly unacceptable for a game of this price that took so long to develop.

It's also important to mention the bizarre omission of wrestlers who should have made the cut, notably there's a huge lack of tag teams, with only 4 in the base game, and people who have been with the company for years who deserve a spot on the roster. And it's incredibly shameful to split Matt Hardy and FTR into DLC, and I don't see any good reason for it other than they wanted to make some extra cash.

So what is there left to do? I love to boot up random wrestling games and play exhibition matches, but when every match lasts only a few minutes, and there is so little polish, so little to do, and so little variety, it's not at all worth playing over the plethora of great wrestling games out there. There is the upside of the AEW branding and roster, but sadly I can have a much better time just downloading creations or mods of those things in the WWE 2K series, some of which can be much higher quality than the character models on offer here.

This is the result of 2-3 years of development from a company who has been making these kinds of games since 1995, yet this feels like a first outing in the genre. There's alot of hope going around for future updates and DLC, but how much of this game will change? What's the roadmap outside of the first batch of DLC? Not to mention that the DLC packs are shamefully small, will all future wrestlers be drip-fed in packs of 2 to maximise profit? We know nothing right now and while there is room for improvement and a possibility for a great game buried in here, this is a terrible package that does not in any way warrant charging full AAA price for so little. Personally I can't see this game getting a complete turn around, and I have a bad feeling that from here on out we're just going to be receiving small updates and character packs, but I'd love to be proven wrong and will gladly re-review this game if that ends up being the case.

I'd say for $30 or even less this would be a much more justified purchase, I probably would be much more forgiving if that were the case, but in it's current state and at it's current price point, it's not worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t
