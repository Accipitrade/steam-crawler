\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou NEED to give this a chance. Seriously! It's a different timing than 2k, but once you learn the reversals a bit, and how to grapple and do chain moves and go behinds, you will have so much fun online and be bodying noobs! Look, this isn't WWE2k23, and to me, that's a good thing. It brought back the key things I enjoy from the N64 classics, AND a few elements I enjoyed from "Day of Reckoning" for Game Cube. It's not meant to be a wrestling sim like 2k. It's meant to provide a FUN wrestling game. Not a game that you have to be a brain surgeon to master. Once you push past the small learning curve, online ranked is so fun. So, So fun.

They're already taking bug reports very seriously on their discord, and a rep from the dev team was there on launch day to take reports, answer questions, and give support. The odd bug that there is doesn't effect gameplay, and at worst are funny to watch. and clip for content. These are bugs that they are already working on the patches for. It's a highly dedicated team, behind this game. A quick visit to the discord showed me that much.

One thing I will say is that their story mode "becoming elite" or whatever (I forget the name) leaves a lot to be desired. It feels like a dating sim with all the times you have to take your wrestler to dinner. XD

Some expected an AEW version of the 2k series. It's not, and thank god for that. That's not to say it doesn't feel like a wrestling game. Getting into the more chain wrestling elements of the grapple system is so satisfying.

Entrances are short, which is good, because we usually skip them on the 2k series anyway. Tell me when i'm telling Lies! :P Not only that, but you get to make different pyro explode whenever you want, as often as you want, and toggle camera angles. It deepens the immersion for me, without going overboard.

All things considered, I can confidently give this 7.5 stars. (Eat your heart out, Meltzer). While I enjoy the depth of the 2k series, sometimes I just want a quicker, fun experience, This game scratches that itch. It's something your friends who don't like wrestling can enjoy. I mean, Electrified barbed wire ring? Being able to throw an exploding propane tank at your opponent? Slamming someone into tacks and seeing them wince as the tacks visibly stick in their back? Enough blood loss to warrant a plasma transfusion? (You can turn that off if you're squeamish homie, don't worry).

The gameplay won my heart, and assuming things will be patched and added to an already FUN game, I'm ALL IN, Bay Bay!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAEW: Fight Forever strikes a great balance between retro and modern wrestling games. It successfully harnesses stylized graphics and a punchy animation system akin to the Def Jam games. It borrows the momentum system best known from WWF No Mercy but the gameplay feels closest to something like Day of Reckoning.

Certain wrestlers have distinct traits such as scenario specific momentum buffs (i.e. receive a temporary buff to momentum when your opponent kicks out of a pin or after a springboard attack, etc) and the chosen roster is a great start with more wrestlers to come via DLC. The result is a fun game that encourages you to play it like a fighting game, as opposed to a spot simulator. This is driven home with things like Ranked online multiplayer and over-the-top weapons and match types. Experimentation is encouraged here and doing so is quite rewarding.

Whether or not you like the nuts and bolts of Fight Forever will largely be based on preference. The game feels simultaneously limiting while delivering various quality of life changes from 2K that will ultimately result in you having to decide what matters more to you. For example, Create-A-Wrestler is stripped back and lacks a reasonable amount of options/customizations but entrances can be controlled in the moment with self activated pyro, smoke, lighting and camera changes. Grapple moves can't be interrupted but you can self interrupt your own taunts so you're not caught in a long helpless state. Selecting moves when altering a moveset is troublesome due to the giant list of moves with no filtering, but a clickable scroll bar on the side makes navigation much easier than scrolling with the thumb stick. The UI is clean and communicates well, which results in less situations such as 2K's famous "Copy to" and "Copy from" options or accidentally backing out of a creation and losing everything.

As another example, the story mode is short and lacks much ability for the player to make decisions, but the progression system and outside-the-box RPG elements create a truly fun experience. Speaking of the story mode, RTE mode is closer to an on-rails season mode than it is a full fledged story mode. It's not afraid to show some personality and lean into what makes wrestling wrestling in the first place. It has a certain retro feel to it, while still being unique and engaging. It's short and lacking a ton of content, but pushing forward feels fun and after one playthrough I'd honestly give it another go around.

Sound design is excellent. Each wrestler's grunts and groans sound great, as does mat impact and weapon sounds. The jukebox is filled to the brim with theme songs and even 8 bit versions. The one exception here is the eating noises during Dining side activities. Seriously, Yukes, we don't need to hear all that chewing so vibrantly.

The groundwork is there for AEW to create some modern classics moving forward and I feel like this title is close. With the rumored features to come (Traditional BR mode with vehicles, Battle Pass, etc.), this game has the potential to be truly great and unique. But as it stands, it positions itself as a valid alternative with a unique, fun and nostalgic playstyle that feels great as a brawler or as a multiplayer party game. It's safe to say that your friends that don't care for wrestling would still have a laugh and some fun in an Exploding Barbed Wire Deathmatch. There are even casual control modes for the uninitiated.

All in all, Yukes has definitely proven that they still know how to make a good wrestling game. Fight Forever is at its best when it separates itself from the pack and the result is a clear path forward to a competitive market in professional wrestling video games.\t\t\t\t\t
