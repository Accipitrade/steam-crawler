\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood old AEW: Wait Forever... is it worth it ?

The short answer is yes, it is and it can and is very much so an enjoyable experience. It has some nods to the old school n64 titles while existing in a more present atmosphere.

Are the graphics pore visible realism ? no.. it is far from it.
Are they better than PS2 as often compared to ? of course! People grab for low hanging fruit usually and intent was never hyper realism by any stretch and that was communicated early, so as for visible presentation.. as expected and met my expectations.

I have 2k series if i am looking for a more realistic experience and on that same note a deeper creative experience. AEW lacks in the creative department, did not keep me coming back.

Online when you can get a match, smooth so far, the odd laggy match but that faults the peer to peer portion imo.

Content, it also lacks which is where in it's current state paying full price for it may be a sore spot, i did not mind as i was under the impression it was going to be built upon. I just hope that remains true going forward as there is a lot of faces missing that should be features in this game and would be a shocker if some never get included IE: Private Party, Acclaimed, Butcher and Blade, Evil Uno, Sonny Kiss, The Factory, etc.

The game play is really where i enjoy it a lot and it does keep me dipping in for some matches, albeit limited at the moment and in hopes of getting more variety, it still plays very nice. I definitely recommend if you enjoy wrestling and you like a more arcade approach that has nods to some old school favorites, just know it is more of an investment than a full toy box.\t\t\t\t\t
