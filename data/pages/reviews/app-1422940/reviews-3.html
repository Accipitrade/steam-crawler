\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far, I'm having fun. I own and have flown a paramotor. This simulator is fairly close to reality. While not quite as exhilarating as hanging under 20 or so square metres of fabric zipping along a few feet above a field, or enjoying the view from a thousand feet up, this is about as close as you can come to it sitting in a chair in your house. It's far less expensive, and a bad landing doesn't hurt at all!

At this point in time, this is an early release of the simulator, so there's not a lot to it yet. You get a few spots in Sun Valley, Idaho, USA to fly from. You can choose a paraglider or a paramotor. There is a PC demo version that has some ridge soaring and spot landing goals, but no VR. Give that one a try to see what is available without VR. More features to come in the VR version as the author works on them. If flying a paraglider/paramotor is something you'd like to try, this gives a fairly good approximation so far. This is not a high energy fast action game. It should be looked at and evaluated as a simulator. If you are looking for a fast action rocket powered flying game, check out Glider Island, another game by the same author.

Flying a paraglider is a more sedate activity, especially if you are up high away from the ground. You can lean back and just enjoy the view when at altitude. Ridge soaring is a little more action packed, but still not something where you have 23 monsters jumping at you every second kind of action. Trying to slalom through the trees in the paramotor is about as crazy as it gets.

So, if you think flying a paraglider or paramotor might be fun, give it a go. Finding the best lift along a ridge line, and being able to gain altitude in a non-powered aircraft is fun and challenging. You can have visualisations turned on that help you see the winds flowing along the ridge. Flying along into a thermal and suddenly being taken on an elevator ride up into the sky is always great. Trying to stay centred in the thermal takes some effort, but with persistence, you can find yourself up at cloud base, flying around some of the best simulated clouds I have encountered. The author does a great job capturing the ethereal nature of the clouds as you fly through them.

So, if you are prepared for an early release simulator, with not a lot more than just the early basics available, give it a go. I'm looking forward to being able to help the author develop the program by providing feedback and support.\t\t\t\t\t
