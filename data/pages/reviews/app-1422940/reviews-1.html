\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game, I've no experience flying paragliders IRL but it feels pretty good in VR. I do fly sailplanes though and it does feel like the lift mechanics could do with a little tweaking, especially ridge lift.

For what it is, the game performs poorly given the graphical fidelity but it still runs smooth enough for it to be enjoyable. The only major issue I've found is that the game stutters a lot when flying low to the ground, especially so in when using the speed wing.

Overall, if you've got an interest in flying and a VR headset, I'd highly recommend picking the game up. The recent updates with additional launch points have been a huge improvement, I hope the developer keeps working on it because this could end up as a real VR hidden gem if the performance issues are sorted out.\t\t\t\t\t

I am playing with a XBox controller and trying to center the thermals or trying to stick the landing is almost just like the real thing. The graphics are quite good and the maps are beautifully crafted and fun to explore. I am absolutely hooked and enjoy some chilling and relaxing flying from my armchair. you can even fly with your friends or other players and talk to them via the integrated radio.

if i could suggest some improvements that would make the simulation even better, that would be a wider choice of wings (EN-A, EN-B and so on) and a Vario with a Map to track my flight path. But even without these features this game is definitely worth trying for both pilots and aviation aficionados\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery happy with this sim.
I used to fly sailplanes back in 2005, around that time I enjoyed the Condor sim ( dedicated to Sailplanes )
It had similar graphics 18 years ago to this 'Glider sim' which I am writing about today (2023).

I then took a course in Paragliding but did not complete the course due to health reasons.
Even though the graphics are outdated in comparison to recent released games ( using Unreal Engine 5 ),
Glider Sim does deliver a great gliding experience, and I do recommend this as a sim but not as a game.

I do believe that adding a 'dog fighting' element to this sim and other gaming features with arcade and realistic levels of flying (especially now there is online multiplayer)) could attract a larger group of gamer's as well the few pilots that buy this sim for the moment, and may make financial sense.
Only suggesting :)

I recommend this sim - five stars from me.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite a good start but still needs work. glider handling good, graphics not bad, playability poor. Hang sim and flight unlimited are better and they were from 20 years ago. These games must be hard to get right though as there haven't been many that get it right. The hills don't do much, they need to have more lift added, even more for the smaller wings. The small wing is almost un-flyable as even though you are on top of a mountain, it barely has enough lift to get all the way down without hitting the floor. The fun with a small wing is doing tricks but there isn't enough height to do them. You don't really get the feeling of scale or speed. The wings should be visibly smaller from the third person view. Since the last update it takes ages to load, runs with glitches and sometimes crashes. low graphic settings don't make any difference. The multiplayer didn't work and the multiplayer maps wouldn't load, I've only been able to play one map, on my own. I so want to like this game but I find myself getting frustrated every time I play. Its supposed to be relaxing. Now I've played for over two hours so I can't get a refund, yet another game that has changed following an update. Please keep working at this, It could be the best glider sim out there with a bit more work. On the good side I see regular updates so there is hope.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've held off with reviewing Glider Sim as it has been under constant development for quite a few months, but just recently the sim has been given a physics update and in the last couple of days it has been given multiplayer functionality. This has really made a huge difference and made it into a must-have sim for the paraglider/paramotor pilot.
Highly recommended, especially in VR.

I now have over 30 hours in the sim and over the last few weeks it has been going from strength to strength. The physics engine has been overhauled and tweaked and it is now as close to the real thing as you can get (I have 4 years RL paragliding experience). There are still a couple of things that I would like to see adding (big-ears, full stall) and these may well be added in the future. Ridge and thermal lift have also been reworked and now are very life-like indeed.
The graphics engine has also been overhauled and a new map added which is pretty amazing. Performance on my Reverb G2 is also exceptional now that it is using OpenXR instead of SteamVR, which means I can also use the OpenXR Tools and Toolkit.

One of the best things about the sim is the multiplayer aspect, and with the fan-base growing there are generally a few other pilots around to share your experience. Th sim includes a working 2-way radio so be sure call out where the best spots for ridge and thermal lift are!\t\t\t\t\t
