\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile the product does what it says on the tin, the way the devs are treating its development is questionable.

Despite being a single player game, you are required to have an internet connection to play.
Presumably because they only want to maintain a single build across all platforms, most of the game looks, shall we say, not great.
There is zero code to handle landing. Once you are on the ground you flop around like a dead fish as the physics engine (which while quite basic but is serviceable enough) fights with the ground until you restart.
Zero options for rebinding controls
If you want to view your flight path after you finish your flight, or not have to constantly hold in the grip button you can, but you also have to pay 750$s a year for "harness edition".
The portion of the map that is hand crafted and therefor "interesting" is quite small. Outside that there is nothing but procedurally generated mountains.

Currently there are no competitors on the market so if you want to paraglide in VR this is kind of the only option that you have, but if you do not have a VR headset I would not waste the money. Plenty of free paragliding simulators which do a far better job.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGlider Sim is a very fun game I did in VR,
through this perspective, the experience is even more intense and the views are worth it again and again.

You can watch my VR gameplay here: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xlI5EucFeag

In Glider Sim you will be able to choose from a number of choices to fly,
a Paraglider, Paramotor Trike, Miniwing & Speedwing!!
You can also determine the altitude where you start, and there are also wind and heat options that provide the right weather statuses you are looking for!
The location in the game is a very vast detailed mountain area with a town and you can choose from a series of points in this map where you start your flight!
You will never get by with 1 flight, there are many routes you can take to get the most out of the game to both quietly enjoy the simulation or to provide the right action by dodging or doing somersaults or possible other adrenaline-boosting maneuvers.

What I also really liked to do was watch an episode of a series on netflix DURING a flight in glider sim,
with useful programs such as OVRdrop or OVR Toolkit you can bring up windows in your VR image with which you can then view websites or other programs, in my case it was a window with netflix, but with the volume of the game a little bit lower of course , you don't want the wind at a mile height to ruin the audio of the series ;) but the view while watching a series is not nothing, AND you also leave your smartphone alone during a series.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I love this game! I am an avid paraglider pilot and do speedflying as well, this game gives me that fill on those windy days when I cant fly, or when I just want to get used to the rhythm of turns and the sensitivity of controlling a wing. Should you go rip down a mountain after this game with no training? of course not. I can say though that input on the toggles and the leaning through turns, is about as close to any game has come to the real physics of a glider/wing in flight. This would be an awesome tool for flight schools(Ideally VR), many athletes in sports envision what they need to do before going out and doing it. I can say that my flying has improved a bit from this game, what it does teach you is how important assessing your altitude, the wind, obstacles, glide ratio and the pressure in the wing in a loop process as you move at 80-100 km/h down the terrain. If that doesn't thrill you, then throwing on a good spotify playlist and casually flying around the peaceful mountain resort level is a fun way to spend a night. I hope that the development of this game continues, this blows steep/gta parachuting/gliding and pretty much any other game that tries to confuse outsiders about our sport; out of the water. Keep up the great work~\t\t\t\t\t
