\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never played Total War before so I decided to start with the first game. I was immediately hooked and I have already put in around 50 hours and I plan to keep playing. I've heard a number of things about old Total War games crashing on newer computers but I haven't had a single issue. I once had the game freeze a couple of times at the same point in time but that was just a bug in the game. It only happened the one time and I've played through the campaign at least three times now. I think this game holds up despite its age. Maybe that's just because I haven't played the newer Total War games so I'm unaware of the features yet to be included this early on in the series. In any case, I'm still having a blast playing it and I'm not even a hardcore fan of the series or a hardcore RTS player so take that how you will. It offers a number of scenarios to keep you occupied with the campaign along with an entertaining Mongol Invasion expansion and historical battles/campaigns. It might not be a ton by today's standards but this was released in 2001 so what're you gonna do? Either way, I highly recommend checking this game out if you're a fan of later Total War games or if you've ever considered getting into them like me. My gripes with the game are really just small details that popped up here or there that I felt could've been improved (and I'm sure they were improved in later games.) I do wish the game had a better tutorial and that the in-game dialogue wouldn't cut out often but that's apparently a common problem with the Steam version of the game. You aren't really missing much though as the dialogue is simply for cosmetic purposes to immerse the player in the historical era. The dialogue in the cutscenes has worked perfectly for me so far. And just know that it is possible for newer computers to run this game without serious issues as I've been doing it for weeks now. It's a tad simplistic compared to more modern RTS games but I think there's a tremendous sense of fun and even nostalgia in its simplicity.

Edit: I did just want to add that after playing the game more, I did discover that a number of the campaign battles do end up crashing at random but I guess it is a common problem and I have found fixes for this online. I recommend looking up a thread by the Steam user Raizen called "FIXES- for Historical Campaigns" if you are also having trouble with these battles. The fixes worked for me and the battles are flawless now.\t\t\t\t\t
