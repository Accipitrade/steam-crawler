\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe father of the Total war series, and still among the best. Ambitious in it's scope back then and still even today. When this game came out when I was 13 there was nothing else like it. It's still very playable for a 22 year old RTS wargame, and a wargame it is. Mden Total war focuses on the flashy, visually stunning battles. This entry is a war game and you have to think before you act. Battles are a lot slower and realistic as a result.

Yes it's more retro than the modern games but still has many unique gameplay elements compared to the rest of the series. Firstly, like Medieval, the campaign map is unlike the rest of the series. It's more akin to Risk moving pieces around. Armies go to province to province rather than walking around the map. It's more simple sure, but that simplicity comes with the benefit of a viciously effective campaign AI that will punish you for any mistake or exposed flank.

The second unique thing is the Mongol Campaign. Total War nowadays tries to make unique campaigns but they've never quite topped the Mongol one. Here there's only two factions, the Mongols and Japanese. Already it's unlike any other TW campaign with multiple factions. It gets weirder. As the Mongols you cannot recruit ANY units nor build any buildings. That's right, you are not in control of of whether you can replace losses or anything. Whether you receive reenforcements is up to an off map AI representing Kublai Khan. Conquer more territory, the more he sends you. If you take too many losses and thus can't keep taking provinces, you loose.

To help you conquer, your armie is made up of mostly Korean conscripts. Poor quality and outshined by samurai, but your army also has a nucleous of Mongol light and heavy cavalry. Perhaps the most OP units in the entire franchise, they will melt just about any unit they come into contact with. They're incredible fun to use. You also get gunpowder units.

It's incredibly hard to lose a battle as the Mongols, your units are just too good. However as I mentioned you don't get to decide when or if you get new units. You will win battles sure, but you'll also take losses. Take too many and you won't have enough troops to garrison conquered territory and fight constant rebellions behind your lines, you won't be able to take new territory and thus no more units. You're doomed to fail.

It's a perfect war focused campaign where you are really OP, yet you can still easily fail if you aren't careful. The Japanese can recruit new units and stack after stack will appear and fight back.

10/10.\t\t\t\t\t
