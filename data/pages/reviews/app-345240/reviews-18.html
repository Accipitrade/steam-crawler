\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat can be truly said about SHOGUN that hasn't already been said? The very first of the seemingly all-powerful Total War series, Shogun laid down almost all the basics we now take for granted: the turn-based campaign map, authentic historical settings, in-depth real-time tactical battles (which, as always, can be resolved by the AI if you don't feel like fighting them), a tier-based upgrade system for settlements and buildings within them, family members, shifting alliances, and much more. As must be expected from the first game - especially given that it was released in 1999-2000 - some parts seem a bit rough and ready; the campaign map for example looks more like something you'd see in RISK than in a graphically powerful PC game, and the diplomacy options are sadly limited to offering alliances. However, the game more than makes up for these failings with its sheer atmosphere: from the ambient background soundtrack to the 'deep background' of the "Way of the Daimyo" manual, the game and its setting pull you in every time. It is not as stable on Windows 10 as later games, but this is true for almost everything released in that era and it's still stable enough to enjoy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShogun: Total War

Pros:

>Era the game takes place in is just epic! - samurai warlords fighting to the death for control and honor
>Gameplay is simple and fun but with lots of content - managing your castles, building your armies, making alliances, sending out assassins and spies to get the drop on your enemies and of course the epic battles! (Granted its the bare bones by newer total war games but its still so enjoyable to play.
>Music - again pretty simple but goes well with the theme of the game.
>Game modes - can choose to have a full campaign or just have battles if looking to jump straight into hordes of warriors fighting to the death.
>Intro and ending are epic with great voice acting.

cons:
>Graphics are dated by today's standards but i feel they hold up well for the time of it's release.
>Length of the campaign could be considered small by today's standards.
> Compatibility issues - i did not have any issues windows 8.1 nor windows xp but i am aware others have had them.

Overall - I loved the game and can see why the total war series took off like a rocket after this game was released.
8/10\t\t\t\t\t
