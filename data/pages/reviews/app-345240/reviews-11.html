\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile Shogun: Total War is an old game and the graphics are obviously dated, the gameplay makes up for it and the strategic aspect (although not very complex) is here. If you played other Total War games, it is easy to see that a lot of their features originate from Shogun 1.

I haven't played this game back when it released and only tried it a few years ago, yet I did have a lot of fun while playing the main campaign. The battles and the controls are a little bit clunky and will take time to get used to, but everything else is pretty good and enjoyable.

I would still recommend this game if you are into grand strategy games and would like to experience one of the classics of the genre. Many Total War games that came after Shogun 1 have improved certain gameplay aspects quite a bit, but that doesn't mean that this game is not worth playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t6/10 (mainly due to the age of the game rather than any actual problems)

So I finally got round to playing this classic after seeing my Uncle play it 20 years ago and I'm so glad I did!

Although the game UI has not aged amazingly, the battles that are at the core of any total war game are exactly what you would expect, if maybe a little clunky with troop movement because of the previously mentioned UI

The AI is competent, the province movement (rather than the later campaign movement range) adds an extra flavour as well, since your enemies can invade some provinces from almost anywhere, forcing you to keep troops on your border even if you are "allies" (alliances in Shogun 1 effectively being a non aggression pact rather than a true alliance)

This can also be seen in ports; ports allow a player or the AI to move their units across the map, causing a headache to any would be Mongol Conqueror in the expansion pact.

Overall this game is at it's "worst", exactly what you would expect, a 20 year old Total War game

At it's best it has some nice surprises and although lacking a large unit roster (same as Shogun 2 due to the time period) I had an absolute blast playing through this title, rediscovering my love of the Total War franchise as I did.

Definitely pick this up if you have a spare couple of hours and a desire to see where it all came from!\t\t\t\t\t
