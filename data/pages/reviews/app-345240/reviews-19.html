\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, what can i say about total war's origin ...
First off the graphics are that of pixels which some find meh but i personally like the art style.
The gameplay itself is good, it's not as smooth as say...shogun 2, but this game has a certain charm to me, the game doesn't hold your hand as much as modern total war games and is very much like a grand strategy risk (but with assassin and badass geisha)
The UI itself is quite clunky in all honesty, it's something which has definately been improved by later total war games and this i feel is one of the 2 aspects that let this game down a bit, the other happens to be the controls, they are'nt too bad but there's a certain annoyment that comes with them, for example if you are used to right click movement this game is left click (not a big deal but it does cause annoyment occasionally) and the battlefield feels very all or nothing in terms of speed (ie either go 50 percent and wait 40 mins for a fight or go 200 percent and miss it entirely) the cinematics are good for the time and while they may not have aged well by some people's standards i feel they are still at an acceptable standard.
the game is very enjoyable if you can get over the ui and movement and the music makes up for that on it's own, in short i think the majority of readers would enjoy this game and for ¬£6.99 it is a real bargain.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking for a quick to complete board game-style strategy game, this is what you want. The campaign game is fun once you get past the initial learning curve and the bland art of the board.
The battles however show their age badly, clunky controls and very rudimentary (and retreat-happy) AI do not make for a particularly entertaining combat system, best to autoresolve most battles unless your personal input will make a significant difference.
For all the combat map's problems, it must be said that the siege battles are roughly on par with those in the latest total war games and the map variety is far superior!
If you're an avid fan of Medieval Total War 1 then this should be a refreshing change while remaining familiar.

Mods: Beyond very basic player-made scenario edits, forget it.

I would recommend this game when it's on offer simply for the merit that it's the start of the series, and perhaps in satire towards the Warscape engine-based games. However beyond novelty you're better off with Shogun 2.
