\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe original large scale real time battle simulator. This game made the name of the Creative Assembly and started off a massively successful franchise. For any game that made a splash back in 1999 it is increasingly difficult to describe just how mind blowing titles like Shogun: Total War were in their time. Getting beyond the rose tint of nostalgia I hope to encourage you that while not without its significant flaws, and notwithstanding its relative crudeness regarding more modern titles, Shogun: Total War really deserves the accolades it got. It remains one of very few games I ever bought on release day after playing a demo for a month, back in a time when games came only on CDs. *emotional sighs

Overall: 7.5/10
In terms of the real time battle engine all the important parts are there, in my humble opinion it gets as much just right in so many ways that have been subsequently lost in later titles as it missed out or got wrong by being the first. Medieval II is probably the best entry for new players, but if you like the Sengoku period representation and/or your laptop won't even run Medieval II, then this game is well worth it.

+Atmosphere & overall presentation: 8/10
All elements balanced to perfection, like a good samurai. Music and SFX are excellent, the quality shows its age a little but the choice and direction is gritty, brutal, and tending to realism. Attention to detail shows a real historical respect and care for the subject.

+RTS gameplay: 9/10
AI has less complexity to deal with than modern games, and so do you. Battles are very fluid and a lot of fun. This is where the original Shogun really shines, and is the real spark of the entire franchise.

+Low requirements
runs comfortably with half decent Intel onboard laptop graphics, doeesn't need modern amounts of ram. It's an old game so to be expected, but you don't need a gaming laptop to run it.

+/-GFX: 7.5/10
Weren't all that special even for their time, but again the direction was understated, realistically subtle, and captured the Japanese seasons with all their weather extremely well. The sprites hold up well for an old git like me, better than the first 3D entries in the series IMHO your mileage may vary.

-Controls: 7/10
Pre-standard controls, selecting specific multiple units is very slow, no number groups or drag boxes. Awkward camera, elevation and pitching are very limited, this is a neccessary compromise due to the use of sprites. Difficult to get a good view on some terrain.

-Strategy layer & early / late game balance: 6/10
It provides context for the battles, but little besides. Diplomacy and espionage are as basic as they could be, the map and army tokens play out more like Chess than grand strategy which is what they were going for and I dig it but it's clear the focus was on the tactical battle layer, with elements like the throne room feeling empty and unfinished due to its lack of significant uses.
The major problem with the campaign is that due to the way the economy scales with territories, the most difficult part of the game by far is very early on, even on normal difficulty it's a real struggle. Right up until you reach an inevitable tipping point, beyond which even expert difficulty doesn't make up for it. If you don't reach that tipping point first, one of the AI opponents will. It's a problem with later titles as well but it's barely mitigated here at all beyond the significant random and vicious nature of the AI opponents who will as often gang up and 4 v 1 you, as fight each other to the death ignoring all else as their undefended lands splinter under rebellions/invasions.

-Lack of variety
What you don't like the purity of spears, bows and cavalry? Katana not sharp enough for you? Go back to bed you RTS peasant. If you aren't fascinated by the romantic idea and history of the Warlords and their samurai in the slightest, their martial prowess and brutality, then you aren't going to really 'get' this game. I understand. For me, Rome Total War just didn't and still doesn't register.

-Shogun 2 does everything better
Yes it actually does, from gameplay to history, it only loses slightly in the over saturated overly romantic atmosphere and environment, arrow trails etc. and other gamey aspects of the modern engines. There's something very visceral about hearing the painful screams echoing through the trees in the fog across the valley as your archer commanders shout 'uTE!' to the answer of 800 bows loosing another volley, that seems entirely absent in later titles. The decades of refinements and additions can't be argued against however. If you've got a comp that can run it, there's just a lot more game there for the same period representation and a similar if not quite so authentic atmosphere.\t\t\t\t\t
