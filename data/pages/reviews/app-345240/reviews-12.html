\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe graphics and gameplay are quite basic compared to later entries in the series, and there is very little variety between the factions/start points except where they start and what military. I enjoyed this game a great deal when I was younger, it is a very solid strategy game with a lower level of complexity than many of its rivals. It gave me a solid introduction to Sengoku Jidai as a historical period, which I scarcely would have been aware of otherwise.

The campaign map closely matches a Risk board, with a similar turn based system, followed by player controlled, real time battles where armies engage. Managing your economy is as simple as setting high/low/middle tax rate and then rolling the dice to see how much money you have to spend. There isn't an overwhelming number of units to learn the strengths and weaknesses of. In that sense this might be a good way to test the waters with Total War games or Strategy as a genre (rather than dive into something more complicated or AAA=$$$). The historical battles provide a decent challenge if you find the campaign too easy.\t\t\t\t\t
