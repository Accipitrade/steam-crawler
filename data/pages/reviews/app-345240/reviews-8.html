\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI very much enjoyed this game. While I started this series with Rome 1, therefore skipping Shogun 1 and Medieval 1, and this game looks very dated and archaic by even the standards of Rome 1, I actually found myself having a lot of fun with this game. It's definitely dated, and obviously I'm not going to recommend it over the likes of later titles simply because of how dated it is, but I can say for those that are thinking to pick this one up should do so.

The game is very basic in terms of what it offers, but what it offers is decent enough and the gameplay loop is enjoyable. It's got the age old Total War formula that any fan will instantly recognize and become familiar with: turn-based campaigns with real-time battles. Unit rosters are basic given it's set in feudal Japan amongst purely Japanese factions, but this also offers the chance for more balanced battles. I'd also be remiss to not mention the Mongol Invasion DLC, which takes place during the failed Mongol invasion attempt of Japan in 1279 and you get the Mongols as a new faction, complete with new units.

One feature that is present in Shogun 1 and Medieval 1 that Rome 1 and other games removed was historical campaigns. If you like historical battles, which this has, then you'll like the historical campaigns, which is basically a series of individual compilations of historical battles following a particular military commander, like Oda Nobunaga and his famous battles. If you're passionate about military history like me and love re-enacting famous battles, the historical campaigns is right up your alley. It's honestly a shame they only appeared in the first two Total War games.

Overall, I'd give this game a 7.5/10. I very much enjoyed it, and despite its age, I think it's actually a good start for new players being introduced to the series.\t\t\t\t\t
