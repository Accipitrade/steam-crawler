\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game achieves an ideal "one-more-turn" dynamic, even after all these years.

The art style is evocative, and the simple, elegant board draws me in. Art on the battle map is definitely very old, but it's functional.

The combat can be clunky. Sometimes the course of a battle or the behavior of units does not seem rational. If a single horseman in a unit of cavalry is engaged, the entire unit will stand and fight until it destroys or is destroyed - you can't tell it to break off. This makes cavalry much less effective than in Rome: TW. Also, archers can only run away from the unit they're targeting, so if two infantry units are chasing them, you have to micro them or they will be wiped out. Lots of infantry seems to be the key.

I wasn't expecting to get sucked into this game. But it's so hard to save and exit. I know if I just play one more turn, I'll always be able to accomplish something cool.\t\t\t\t\t
