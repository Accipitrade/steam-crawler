\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this game on the caveat that you have expectations for a 2000 game.

Shogun is still playable in modern times but is lacking many quality of life features. Unit movement on the campaign map, province management, battle controls, and a few other UI issue are tedious. The core game play is still fun though. There is still depth to combat. Factors such as unit stats, flanking, terran, weather, fatigue, moral, and formation still effect how combat plays out.

The campaign is much more simple than newer total wars but you still have unit management, provinces usually have some type of bonus, and you have buildings. Throw in agents and you do have a total war experience.
