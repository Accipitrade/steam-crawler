\r\n\t\t\t\t\t15 years after its release, I'm playing this game for the first time, and as my first introduction into the Total War franchise. I LOVED it! I can't say how it compares to later titles (which I assume grow in refinement and complexity -- which may not always be a good thing). Even non completionists could enjoy this!

This game had a lot of firsts, or near firsts, and certainly the best in its day. Specifically the incorporation of some civilisation/economy management but also tactical battlefield elements like weather, inclination, morale, formations, etc. I could never really determine or feel the impact of weather, but all the others mattered in a very tangible way.

Out of the battle, provincial management is quite simple. It was not at all overwhelming as I feared (I'm looking at you, Europa Universalis). Unit management could stand some improvements, though, as could scrolling across the map (so slow!).

During battles, enemy AI was decent, and clever use of the terrain and proper use of unit types could net some truly satisfying victories.
Sometimes units don't behave to movement commands in any way that made sense, which is EXTREMELY frustrating, but this was very rare.
Unfortunately, the camera controls suck, nothing more nothing less.

All that said, I spent many delightful hours trying to come up with the best army configurations for my fighting style, making and breaking alliances, leading armies to victory or defeat and hiding behind a wall of castles I'd devoted years to developing, and making my people the best fed and most satisfied in all the land.

There are also a surprisingly large number of ways to play the game and modes available. There's a lot of replay valuable. So even in 2019, I highly recommend!\t\t\t\t\t
