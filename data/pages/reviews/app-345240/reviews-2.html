\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe #1 best thing about this game compared to Medieval Total War is that this game won't crash all the time. A save file can fail to load in Medieval every time you try - they get corrupted or something and you're forced to use an older one. I haven't had that problem with Shogun yet.

This game is really incredible. It's only $2.50 on sale too - an absolute steal. Frankly, it's worth more than the $10 they're charging for it now so don't even wait for it. I'm kicking myself for not getting into this series sooner. Shogun is the perfect introduction to the series. There's plenty enough going on here without everything they added in the sequels. You don't have to worry about generals' loyalty in this one. You don't have to keep a hundred troops minimum in every province. You don't have Dread or Piety or Acumen to take into account. Your generals don't develop traits randomly every single turn that either boost or detract from their effectiveness in battle and they won't start a civil war against you. Shogun Total War isn't strictly better or strictly worse, it's just the perfect starting point to the series. You really should get used to these basics before advancing into the later entries. Playing Medieval before this is like learning multiplication before addition.

I found troop formation to be extremely intuitive in this one. It comes way more naturally than in Medieval. 100 hours into Medieval Total War and I never got them out of single line formation... ugh, the shame. The troop formation button doesn't stand out in Medieval. I think, maybe, at first in Medieval I was afraid that if I didn't stretch my troops across the whole map, the enemy would get horses behind me and force me to flee. And then maybe I just forgot about the troop formation button b/c it blends into the background. Now I understand better and when I go back to Medieval Total War I'm going to get my revenge on those Saxon and Mercian huscarls. The Irish are so hard to play in that game though, it may not go as planned.... In any case, I'll be playing this one for a long time before returning to Medieval.

Did I mention how much you'll learn about history playing these games? It's really interesting.

Oh and there's one thing that I believe is a lot better in this one: You get paid annually, just like in Medieval, except that 1 turn is only 1 season instead of 1 year. This means you get paid every 4 turns instead of every turn like in Medieval. This makes you think about how you'll be spending your money over the next 4 turns instead of just the current turn. I don't know why they would change this, it's my favorite aspect of Shogun and it's really surprising to find. It gives each turn in the 4 turn cycle a different feel. For example, the turn before you get paid you have extra incentive to conquer a province b/c if you take it you'll get paid more money on your next turn when you get paid whereas if you wait 1 turn, you won't get any utility from that province for a while. It's such a cool strategic aspect that's unique to this game, even if it doesn't really make sense that you'd only be paid at the end of Autumn (it's not just for harvest, mining and trading income also only get paid out in autumn, not to mention not all food would be sold at harvest anyway).

I still love both of these games so much. Possibly the best $2.49 I ever spent, I really could never ask for better value. I now own 10 total war games but I've still only played 3 of them (besides Shogun and Medieval, I got Warhammer 2 on another gaming platform for free - my first TW game). I can't wait to see the rest.\t\t\t\t\t
