\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCute Bite is a visual novel with stat raising elements developed and published by Hanako Games. You take on the role of a female butler (whose name can be customized) who serves a vampire master. While you're out doing errands one day, an accident occurs at the manor where the vampire master resides. Upon returning to the manor, you find out that your vampire master is no more. However, his vampire daughter survived, so you take it upon yourself to wake her up from her deep slumber. Although the daughter is technically an adult, due to her long sleep, she's in a very weakened state to the point that her body has reverted to that of a child. Also, she has no memory of who she is and such. Thus, it's up to you to guide her so that she can return to her adult form and then make the blood bond with you. If this doesn't happen within a year, then you'll slowly die. That's the basic gist of the story.

As for the gameplay, Cute Bite is mostly a stat simulator although there are occasionally choices which can affect stats and possible relationships. As you plan out a weekly schedule for your vampire mistress, you must choose among various classes, jobs, and/or resting periods for her. These activities will affect various stats, such as etiquette, fighting, etc. Of course, classes cost gold while jobs earn you gold. In addition, during the weekends, your vampire mistress can do other activities, such as shop for items that will help raise stats or improve her health or stress. There's even a RPG minigame where she can fight human victims, and depending on how good her stats are, she can successfully defeat them and take their blood, improving her health. Other activities can be unlocked as well, depending on certain actions taken, like classes or jobs. In addition, there are some possible yuri romantic interests to pursue although the actual romances themselves don't happen until after your vampire mistress returns to her adult form. Many endings can be obtained, depending on which stats are the highest and such. There are even bad endings, depending on certain actions taken. Also, there are lots of CGs as well as plenty of Steam/in-game achievements and Steam trading cards.

Now that I've gone over the story and gameplay, I'll move onto my critique. Cute Bite is a sort of spinoff of the Cute Knight series, one of Hanako Games' early game series. I've been a huge fan of Cute Knight, so when I found out that Cute Bite was in development, I was excited to play it. First, I liked how the story starts off. It doesn't waste time getting right to the action. I found it interesting how the butler, realizing that she was now free from the old master's control, chooses to awaken his daughter rather than take advantage of her freedom. However, I suppose it isn't that much of a surprise since the butler has been used to looking young and under servitude for many years. There is an option to look into the old master's death and the circumstances behind it, although I won't reveal the details for spoiler purposes. (In case anyone's curious, the butler is a possible romance option although the butler makes it a point not to discuss certain topics with her mistress due to her amnesia.) Although there are romance options to pursue, as I mentioned earlier, the actual romances don't happen until after the vampire mistress (whose name can be customized) returns to her adult form. Romance is more of a minor component, though, as the stat raising takes up the majority of the gameplay. Once your vampire mistress returns to her adult form, she can end up pursuing one of many career paths. Although I enjoyed getting to see these different endings, the stat raising can feel a bit tedious and tiring after playing through some time. I'd recommend that if you aren't used to stat simulators, it's best to take a break in between runs. I will say, though, that I enjoyed seeing the different romance endings, and there's quite a variety of them. ^^

As for the art, I liked the style used. It's pretty much in an anime style with appropriate colors used to depict the nighttime setting, as the game itself mostly takes place at night. (After all, it makes sense since vampires are strongest then. XD) The sprites and backgrounds are well done, and I adore the chibi art for the stat raising part. In regards to the soundtrack, I liked the tracks used. Most of them depict that nighttime, slightly scary theme (although the game itself isn't actually scary - it has a mix of drama, comedy, etc.).

Overall, would I recommend Cute Bite? If you like Hanako Games' other games and don't mind stat simulators, you should give this a try. For those who played Long Live the Queen, Cute Bite might feel a bit similar although I feel like the gameplay here is somewhat easier (once you know what you're doing). It's a fun game and especially fitting to play around Halloween and any time you're in the mood for a slightly scary (but mostly, fun) game. At full price, I think it's worth getting although you can always wait to get it on sale (and it's in a couple of Steam bundles, too). ^^

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA cute little 'princess raising' sim, with story and mystery and stat raising and saphic romance!

I was initially concerned that since the vampire character is a child for most of the game, this was going to be the old "Oh no it's perfectly fine to romance her, she only LOOKs like a child, she's really hundreds of years old!" trope. But I've played virtually all games the devs have released in the past so I trusted them to handle this well. And I was not disappointed. While you can get to know potential love interests, and set up the later romance, no romance actually happens while 'Buttercup' is in child form.

Succeeding at the game is pretty straightforward, but getting specific endings/romances takes planning. I do love games that you can just DO and figure out as you go along that don't punish you for not knowing the mechanics from the start.

The art work is cute. The dialogue and events are funny. The stats building feels satisfying. I love this game!\t\t\t\t\t
