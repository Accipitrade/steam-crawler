\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCurved Space is an absolute gem that effortlessly combines the charm of Mario Galaxy with the adrenaline-pumping intensity of a bullet hell shooter. From the very start, I was captivated not only by the game's enthralling gameplay but also by the fantastic people behind it, the devs who poured their passion into creating this masterpiece.

At first glance, the unique curved environment is striking, reminiscent of Mario Galaxy's innovative design. But don't be fooled by its friendly appearance; Curved Space rapidly escalates into a heart-pounding bullet hell experience. The waves of insect-like enemies swarm you, demanding quick reflexes and precision in every movement.

I was pleasantly surprised by how Curved Space manages to suck you into its universe, keeping you engaged throughout. The relentless and hectic combat is a true test of skill, pushing players to their limits while providing an exhilarating and satisfying challenge. Each victory brings a sense of accomplishment that few games can replicate.

What sets Curved Space apart is the intriguing storyline that ties each level together. It's more than just a barrage of enemies; it's a captivating journey through space, uncovering mysteries and exploring new dimensions. The attention to detail in crafting this narrative elevates the game to another level, making every level feel purposeful and interconnected.

In conclusion, Curved Space is a thrilling and addictive galactic adventure. The combination of Mario Galaxy's whimsical charm and bullet hell's high-octane action is an absolute delight. The passion and dedication of the devs shine through, making it an experience that will undoubtedly stay with you long after the final boss is defeated. If you're a fan of intense arcade shooters and a gripping storyline, Curved Space is a must-play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very cool twin-stick shooter. Its standout feature is of course the traditional 2d twin-stick gameplay mapped to 3d surfaces. But there is a lot of depth here beyond that one hook. There is a good selection of weapons that provide quite a lot of variety. Playing with the beam weapons as they wrap around the surfaces of the arenas is probably my favorite thing in the game. The lash mechanic is loads of fun as well.

There are score-attack game modes, and a number of score based gameplay mechanics, but I've mostly ignored those and played the campaign. The amount of content in the campaign is impressive, and there are some nice twists that surprised me.

I found the default camera to be too tight in and twitchy for my taste, but it's highly configurable.

A minor complaint and word of warning: when you finish a campaign boss battle and choose an upgrade, the upgrade UI has an option to return to the main menu. Don't click it! You have to continue playing and let the game exit the level. Otherwise the game won't save your progress, and you'll need to replay the level. The game does warn you, but I found the whole thing unintuitive enough that I lost progress twice before I understood what was happening.

There is so much cool stuff going on in this game. If you like twin-stick arena shooters, and particularly if you like the score-attack variety, then you need to check this out!\t\t\t\t\t
