Each of the three factions has very different and interesting mechanics for their units. They all have units split between the same 5 classes, but "warriors" from all three factions play *very* differently, with unique mechanics, upgrades, and pressures that make them feel very different. Its hard to have such engaging and diverse mechanics in a game with tight tactics, so I want to applaud the devs for that,

Translation is a little rough at time, but nothing I couldn't figure out. Everything IS translated, its just with chunky, slightly broken, or a little weird with phrasing. Definitely recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not finished with the game. I'm on the Sixth Chapter of the Easiest Tribe [Ninelie] (of three [Bronion and the Bird People]), and I got my butt handed to me. Working on that now.

Pros:

-Nice Graphics
-Plenty of Trait Variety
-Consistent Unlocks
-Encouragement to Use Different Characters (Especially With Higher Difficulties)
-Plenty of Challenge
-Multiple Modes and Difficulties
-Lots of enemy Variety and Boss Fights
-Pause Menu Encyclopedia for Unknown Nodes and Other Helpful Infos
-Generally Fun and Makes Me Use My Brain

Neutral

-Slight Grind(?): The buildings and home area upgrades help your gameplay. This may result in you needing to spend a couple sessions grinding for materials on a higher difficulty of a previously completed chapter (those give bonus upgrade materials upon Victory) when you run into a progress wall. I have no complaints with this because it's been fun for me so far.

Cons:

-English Translation is Passable But Expect some Occasionally Rough Translations:
---20% Chance you'll run into some English translations that sound like they could work one of two ways.
---Because of the English Quality, it may take a moment to get how the Pause Menu Encyclopedia is organized.

-Some Bosses May Feel Unbalanced
I say some because there are many Bosses where I got my butt handed to me purely because I did not read their skill previews, or that have environmental effects that I did not acknowledge. Pay attention, I beg you. It will make your life easier.

For the situation with the few "Unbalanced Bosses" --- There will be some points where the boss you get hard counters your team. This can be frustrating. Most of the bosses that have this will typically be the Duplicating Bosses (you'll know when you meet them); and the bosses that Stockpile-Power with their (Very) High Defense Build. These are my only complaints about the bosses (3 or 4 of them so far out of many). Every other boss so far has been pretty great.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately, the English translation is not as good as many Steam reviews would have you believe. Typo mistakes aside, its clear that what's here is at best edited machine translation, and many mechanics boil down to "try it in combat, find out which of the 2+ possible interpretations of a tooltip is correct"...which after a couple dozen or so times in a few hours got to be too tiring for me to want to keep playing. And this is excluding the mechanics of various map locations which aren't explained well either. I've played a fair amount of foreign/non-native English games in my time, so dealing with shoddy translations isn't new to me...but this isn't a genre where translation trial and error is very fun.

From what I do see out of one full run and a few abandoned ones where I'm testing mechanics...balance is also pretty bad in the sense that there are many ways to break the game alongside many underpowered tools. There's rarely an interesting decision point in the sense that one option tends to be so much better than its alternatives.

In isolation, neither of these complaints would be total deal breakers on their own, but in combination, I can't recommend a purchase until/unless these issues are resolved.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game's current state has so much potential, and even then its still a solid play. The demo right off the bat to me was a good sign as I easily sank 5 hours into it and after buying it for only 12 bucks was able to add another 30 hours on top of that. The Fire Emblem style of combat mixed with a roguelite style of progression through stages gives way to a fun gameplay loop with potential for a multitude of strategies and playstyles. The nodes in each of the maps are randomised giving the ability to evaluate risk and reward based on your situation whether you want to clear an elite battle for the ability to get the resources to instant level a unit or go straight to the boss and avoid the risk of losing vital pieces to your gameplan. So far I have played the beastfolk and cleared Chapter 7 with a warband of all the 5 classes each with a different set of challenges for me to overcome. Especially in the Guard run where my tankiness was instantly rendered redundant by both a boss who is on a ranged only stage and one that has an instant kill gimmick but both of which were solvable with the resources available to me. The main problem that you may run into is skill translation and interpretation, some skills and their interactions may be unclear but after some experimentation I have come to better understand their conditions and functions. But overall for the package and the pricing I honestly think its worth and if you are still unsure just play the demo (although it plays on an older version).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very fun game once you get a hang of the mechanics. Played in Chinese so not sure about the translation quality, but the Chinese version looks fine. There are soome QoL improvements I'd suggest tho:

1. Allow us to check on our members at anytime (i.e. by pressing esc and shows an extra option like Member List). This is for when we get a new relic/member and we want to see how to synergize/combo with the team better. Sometimes we don't remember all the details so a quick check like looking at our deck like in Slay the Spire would be awesome.
2. Some encounters shouldn't spawn that late. I got the "send injured member away for 3 battles and then they 'd return with full upgrade/level" event... great, right? The problem is that it's literally the LAST CHECKPOINT BEFORE the LAST BOSS. I have cleared all battles up until that point, so it's basically a very powerful event that I got no benefit from. Devs pls look at your event generation condition and make sure events like these don't spawn too late.
3. The tutorial needs to explain where to see character/member exp. Yes, I know where it is now but it took me a while to find where to look.
4. Similarly, please explain what does sending our members away for jobs mean, and where to find them once they are done! Yes, now I know they at the upper right corner, but naturally I was looking at the map itself and wonder why the areas are grayed-out but the members hadn't return yet. Better still, why not just make the return event automatic? There's no reason to not accept the sent-away members since they bring back upgrade materials!
5. This is very important: pls make it easier to see which tiles we can move to and which ones we can't! Sometimes there are elevations on the ground, or things you thought it'd a normal tile but it is a barrier - like the flower looking plant in stage 1, I really thought those flowers are just part of the vegetation , but turns out we can't move through them but bees can.
