\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: Game is out now, still has translation issues but it's nothing too awful. Game is fun.
I don't usually play games like these, but I'm really enjoying this one.
Here are my thoughts on the game so far:

Positive:
  • Unique artstyle/theme.
  • Clans have distinct and fun gameplay differences. (ex: a unit from one clan could capture wildlife mobs while another clan can fuse 2 units together for a 2 cost mecha-unit.)
  • They're all waifus from cute to badass. As a bonus a few units have their armor break when their health drops too.
  • It's easy to restart or leave a fight.
  • The game is cheap($9 w/ EA launch sale) and has a good amount of content for its cost.
  • So far the dev team is active and responsive, already rolled out a patch to alleviate some of the balancing concerns.

    Mixed:
  • Progression outside of a run feels lackluster. Upgrades won't make you feel much stronger. (doesn't really bother me)

    Negative:
  • Balance is all over the place. The traits characters gain on level up range from useless to insanely powerful.
  • Some challenges and bosses feel overtuned on the higher difficulties.
  • There are typos and translation errors. It's definitely playable, but inaccurate or incorrect tooltips can be annoying.
  • I've run into a few bugs where traits should've activated but didn't. Although it's hard to say whether or not they were due to misleading tooltips or not.

    I'll try to keep this review updated if there are any big changes, but so far so good. Many of the negative reviews seem to be focused on balancing problems and other things you'd expect to improve during EA. If you like the demo or what you see in the trailer give it a shot. You could do a lot worse for $10.

I very rarely rate or comment on games on Steam, but I really want to like this one.
Nothing better in gaming than tactics, roguelite, and waifus. So, I look better to future updates and changes; especially more roguelite/sense of progression.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is essentially a roguelike tactics game with 2 modes with 1 having a progression system and chapters and the other a more classical punishing roguelike mode. There are also 3 different factions with their own units, faction buff, faction unique mechanics (one group uses animals and can become mounted warriors, while the other can fuse units to make armored mecha units), and different variants of the same class. With the ability to add more passives onto your characters when they level up. By the end you should ideally have some broken synergies like any good roguelike.

The only few criticisms that I have so far is that it is hard to tell what direction enemies are facing (can mess up back stabs), the combat preview can be deceiving as it does not take any passives into account, and the translation can be rough at times. Though hopefully this will be resolved with time.

I can still see myself playing this game for some time still as I am currently only on the 3rd chapter with the first faction (currently 6 chapters/difficulties per faction). It's nice to see how much content their already is for an early access release. I can't wait to see what the game will be like at full release :D

Honestly a very fun game please consider trying the demo if you are interested in tactics games (tactical rpgs), turn based roguelikes, or games like fire emblem.\t\t\t\t\t
