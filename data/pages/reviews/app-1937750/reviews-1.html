Every time your characters level up they get to choose one new ability from a selection of three. I have been impressed at the variety of combos that are possible. For example: In one run my opening artifact caused enemies to take damage when my characters died - as reviving is limited, I built a strategy around sacrificing summons. In another run I had stealth for the first three turns - this gave me the option of launching an ambush or clustering together to buff the entire party.

The game also has good length. At 28 hours I have finished the campaign for the first tribe on medium difficulty. There are three tribes total (all different mechanics), an endless mode, and alternative boss fights. So it would be easy to spend 100 hours enjoying this game.\t\t\t\t\t

Amazing Rogouelike Turn-based RPG

First Thoughts - Yes translation is off and I think I might get the overall story but I'm not too sure. Ain't a deal breaker though. Once you get the hang of the game and understand it, It gets better and you start to enjoy it a lot. Music is solid and so are the sound fx for attacks and such. You can speed up the game in settings so it doesn't feel too slow either. Art style is great.

Gameplay
- You can start off as any of the 3 Clans Such as Ninelies, Evernight and Bronian. Each clan has their own Unique characters and traits tied to those characters. Traits are special passive skills that are unlocked when leveling up and each character has their own unique 1st trait. The rest are attainable based on that CLASS. Bloodthirst for an example is a warrior trait tied to Ninelies and can recover 30% of the damage dealt as HP.
- When you're out on the map you start off at the camp. The first two areas you can access are the Altar which gives unique effects (Treasure) like - 1 hp to enemies every start of battle. The next is a Campfire that always guarantees a Character you can recruit. Max 10 but battles have team limits based on battles and you start with 5 or 6 limit unit your clan levels up as you play. As you land on each event it opens the next area connected until you reach the boss unless it's a battle event. In order to reveal the areas connected you'd have to battle. At level 1 it takes 3 Battles to level up BUT that character HAS to be in the battle.
Meta progression
- Wood is gained from beating bosses even if you fail a run. You'll gain wood. There are also random events that'll give you the choice to send a party member to grab wood. Wood is used for Unlocking more Treasure to have appear in runs. Start with more Coins (Used to recruit, buy equips etc) You get the gist.
- Stone is the attained the same way as Wood. It is used for your start of runs to bring up to 3 "Props" which you also use Wood for to unlock them but has to be unlocked in a fixed order. Those Props are used on events such as an encounter Event. If you have a Star god Prop you can use it to skip an Encounter Event (battle) and give all your characters 3 experience points.

Overall Thoughts
The game is quite fun and a nice fresh of breath air in both Tactics turn-based and Roguelike. The "builds" you can do with one character by itself or with the team is amazing and must have taken a lot of thought. At one point I felt like I needed these particular traits for one character to be op alone. Next thing I wanted every character to have these traits so that they could all synergize well. And if my One Guard (Tank) didn't have a certain trait then my other characters would fall off in Damage or Survivability. I'd say check some Youtubers out but most of them either talk too much trying to explain things or aren't that quick to pick up the game. Either that there just isn't many so You'd have to try it yourself. It's $9.99 so that's more than worth. There's a lot more that I didn't touch up on but it's not all that important.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun turn based tactics game, the main draw is that there is an absolute ton of characters to pick from across 3 factions and you find/unlock them as you play through missions. Each faction's characters are quite different from each other with different mechanics they are themed around. As characters level up you get a random selection of skills from their class in addition to their specific unique skill. Many characters have synergy with others so it's great fun building teams around certain mechanics. There's also gear, artifacts and in game events that let you flesh out your team. Combat and team building really is the entire focus of the game, there is a story sort of but it's forgettable and the "ok" translations doesn't help. That said, the gameplay itself is quite fun and I had a lot of fun discovering new team combinations to play with on each playthrough.\t\t\t\t\t
