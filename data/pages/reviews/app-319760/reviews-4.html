Great Concept, Shoddy Execution

First, one thing I want to make clear. This game doesn't take 72 hours to beat, I simply left it on for long periods of time. Second, this is a negative review, so why did I even bother finishing the game? Because I have a compulsion to finish games once I start them unless they are literally unplayable. Don't be like me.

Now, onto the game. This is a hybrid where you build towers and send monsters out of your barracks to attack. The only control you have over the monsters is which direction/path they take when encountering intersections, I really like the idea for the game, but the developers did a poor job of implementing it.

The AI in this game is pretty wonky. You will experience moments where your units will destroy a tower or kill an enemy and then just turn around confused before eventually reorienting themselves and going the right way again. The units also stack on top of each other, so visually you often can't tell how many units are actually in one spot.

Managing towers quickly becomes unsatisfying as well. Many of your towers do not have a range greater than 2, with only one tower with a range of 4. This isn't terrible at first, but eventually you encounter enemies that prioritize targeting towers instead of units. These units are very strong and typically take down your towers in moments. Additionally, the places that you can and cannot place towers often make no sense and prevent you from using them to the fullest. You'll see positions where you want to place a tower, only to learn that this pile of bones or whatever the developer has chosen to add to the map blocks you, or that you can place towers in places where they can never actually hit enemies. There are traps that you can place on the ground, but they damage your units too so good luck using those effectively. Combined with the odd paths created, it just comes off as sloppy, like no one played through the game themselves to iron out these pain points.

Many of the units and towers are useless or at least inferior to others. I was curious if anyone else had this experience and saw that someone beat the game on Hard by using just one unit, the Minotaur. I was already using this unit primarily, but just to test it I did the same. So that's one unit that can likely carry you through the game. The towers are much the same, with the fire towers and gargoyle boulder throwers being the only useful ones you really need.

The story is already nothing to write home about, and it is made worse by the mediocre English translation. There was one part where the text wasn't even in English for me, but German (Screenshot here). The story itself is confusing and the ending was very unsatisfying. It's sloppy, and again makes it seem as though no one played through the game.

You may be looking at this game and thinking "This looks interesting, and it's so cheap!" Don't bother. There are better tower defense games and better ways to spend your time and money. Move on and don't waste either.\t\t\t\t\t

-I wish the game had a multiplayer feature.
- On the title screen there is a little editor tab that is grayed out with red text painted over that reads "coming soon!" which is disappointing considering I would have liked to play around with that feature.

The release date for this game was 2016 so I'm pretty sure the developer(s) have just abandoned this game and I will probably never see those features but all in all it's a pretty cool little game and I got it for like $0.50 USD on sale, I'd say it's worth a buy even at it's base price though.

This is a good game I recommend that you buy it and play it till your eyes become as red as the blood and gore in this game.\t\t\t\t\t
