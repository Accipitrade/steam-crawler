\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fun game, with simple graphics, a humorous story and surprisingly complex game play. (For example it has a simple but much approved of ability to select what targets the towers shoot at first.) Calling this a tower defense game, while true, doesn't really cover the entire story.
Heads up though, it's been out awhile, it seems like the promised level editor is probably not coming, and the English dialogue and explainations often make no sense or are incorrect. (I swear I would do a complete edit for free if I thought the developers were still around.)
The story is cute though, I find the characters interesting and the dialogue quite funny. The controls are (mostly) solid, and the music fun without getting annoying.
The only real dissapointment is that I had hoped for more functionailty at the keep, it would have been nice to spend 'blood' on improving tower and/or units, etc.
Not a must buy for tower defense fans like myself, but quite fun for as long as the game lasts...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI snagged this game on sale for 49 cents. At first I thought I’d found a little rough gem, but upon completing the game I can safely say to anyone do not purchase this game! The hugest disclaimer you should know is that you cannot 100 percent this game because one achievement is tied to creating a map in an editor that does not exist and the developers have abandoned work on it.

Initially the game was fun, graphically it’s charming, the characters witty and you go about forming your marry band of misfits. Then you’ll notice that the game relies heavily on the following to generate a huge sense of artificial difficulty.

-Limited Tower Range
-Spamming Enemies
-Traps that degrade rapidly (made worse by enemy spam)
-Tower destroyers that you will find difficult to counter because of their health and the limited range on your towers
-Limited tower placement area
-Enemies gain health the longer the match goes on, if you stalemate it’s a guaranteed loss. With the spam and other issues you’ll stalemate a few times.

The bad balancing is easily overlookable with the exception of three levels, but the final level is outright abysmal in regards to the shortcomings of the game becoming a major hassle. Now the worst part is the game’s ending. This is your only spoiler warning and I’ll keep it vague so you can experience it firsthand.

It turns out you are being manipulated by the muse, to kill your friends who she wronged, kill the other gods of war, and then commit suicide in one of thee worst endings I’ve ever had the displeasure of experiencing. You get not closure, no satisfaction; the real villain ends up getting away with everything. This is made all the more painful as the game has an arch where all your characters soften and become more peaceful while exploring what Raelius will do once there is no more war.

Gameplay

Decent TD core machanics +

In later levels, specially on Hard you need no tactics.
You have to rush otherwise the enemy will swarm you with troops,
which get more health and power, the longer you take.

Even the best paced TD will be overrun and if you take to long you have no chance of wining.

Pathfinding sometimes screws you as well. If your units attack a tower they
allways walk back a few tilles and make a surcle. And with enemy troops advanceing
you can get stuck in limbo.

Also the enemy troops atacking towers don´t follow the path and take shortcuts.

Same with your heroes. Some abilities are much more effective then others and specialy the
charm is as usless as it gets.

The story is ok. Nothing new nothing old and some fun moments.

The achivments are also not 100% possible because of the missing editor.

Overall a playable TF for fans.
