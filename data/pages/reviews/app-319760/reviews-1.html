\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh My Gore! is a mediocre gore/horror themed 2D retro pixel tower defense game with an amazing gimmick... you play the "bad guys". Yeah, it's been done to death. This is a routine TD where you get pretty free tower placement and a few spells/abilities to dump on mobs as well so you don't get too bored between building towers. There's dozens if not hundreds of TD's on Steam and this one doesn't stand out.

From a technical perspective, the game doesn't meet basic minimum requirements that most PC gamers expect as standard.

The artwork here is terrible, it's a blend of old retro pixel "art" used as a substitute for contemporary PC graphics, and some of the most amateurish MS-Paint style drawings I've ever hurt my eyes on. It's unclear if this is due to lack of budget to arrange someone who can do graphics properly, or lack of talent, regardless, the overall visual quality of the game is extremely low as a result of these decisions.

While there are options to change the resolution for the game, all this does is scale up the simplistic 2D art assets used to make the game, which makes little or no difference to the graphics quality. Without any other substantial graphics tweaks, it's not possible for gamers to improve the lacklustre 2D visuals.

The developers didn't design the game for modern gaming PCs, as such the display resolution caps out at 1080p, a very low resolution that became mainstream back in 2006 and became obsolete when 4K entered the mainstream in 2014. The game simply won't look right on modern gaming displays due to this failure on the part of the developers.

These technical defects push this game below acceptable standards for any modern PC game.

Oh My Gore! didn't appeal much to the people who own a copy of the game, either. It has achievements, and they show us a very clear picture that the game didn't really capture any interest from gamers. The most commonly and easily attained achievement is for killing 100 elves, you can get that within 10 minutes of playing (with your eyes closed), but only around 11 percent of players bothered to get that far before uninstalling the game. Hardly a success story, gamers just weren't all that interested in the game.

Reviewing SteamDB to check how popular this game was with players reveals a surprise... there's a very healthy spike in player counts for the game. But this isn't consistent with the achievement stats, that show only 11 percent of players bothered playing the game for any reasonable amount of time. How is it possible for this game to have so many concurrent players who weren't even playing the game? Trading cards. Players will use card idling software to collect the cards and sell them, but this won't trigger any achievements in-game. That tells us people only really bought this game for trading cards, and that's a damning indictment of the woeful quality. A closer look at the numbers shows the game just has a couple of players every week running up the game and idling it for cards, then deleting it. We must ask how it benefits gamers for there to be so many games like this, with no merit as a serious game, that only generate sales from people idling and selling the trading cards.

Oh My Gore! is relatively cheap at $2 USD, but it's not worth it. Given the defects and quality issues with the game, coupled with the unrealistic price, this is impossible to recommend.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay so... I don't hate the game. I don't. It's a fun mix of flash style strat game and tower defense. It really really reminds of me some of the best Armor Games flash games. The issue is, sometimes it reminds me too much of a flash game. Around level 6 there's a giant leap in difficulty. I quit playing after level 7, not because I kept dying, but because I was in a neverending stalemate with the opp. The enemy would continue getting stronger, and I could place more and more upgraded towers, but my troops could only be upgraded within the skilltree, and there's a food limit, so you can only send out a certain amount of troops.

So, I end up in a situation where the incoming enemy troops cut through my troops like butter, and then, in turn, my turrets turn them into swiss. I can't move my defensive line of towers up because every time I do, there's an enemy that melts anything it's too close to.

It's not a terrible throwback to old flash games, but it really, really plays like one.

If the dev seemed to be still supporting it, I'd give it a tentative thumbs up. Alas, the Editor mode has been Coming Soon for over 5 years, and it doesn't seem like the dev is very interested in this project at all anymore. Sucks, it could've been a cool little gem.\t\t\t\t\t
