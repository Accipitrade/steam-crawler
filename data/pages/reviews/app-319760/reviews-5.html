\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh My Gore! seemed like a game tailor-made for me. I love tower defense, copious gore, and I far prefer being a villain than a hero, especially whenever you can be a necromancer. Oh My Gore! features all of this, but unfortunately, I found it fell far short of what I wanted in terms of tower defense.

Oh My Gore! has beautiful old school graphics, dark humor, and interesting locations and music. However, its problems begin with its lack of direction. Because the store page advertises it as tower defense and I'm well-versed in the genre, I started looking out for pathways and strategic places to set particular towers. It soon became clear that Oh My Gore! is a very weird mix of tower defense and tug-of-war strategy games. The tower defense part of the game is only half (one could argue even less) of the gameplay, and the game doesn't do a particularly good job of telling you what the goal is for each level. For example, one early level tasks you with razing a town, but it doesn't tell you how to do that before it starts sending waves of goodly warriors at you. Do you defeat so many waves of warriors? No. You have to send your own warriors down the same path, get to the town, and destroy particular buildings. You have no control over your warriors or even your hero.

The tower defense part of the game isn't very good at what it does, either. Sure, there are some interesting towers, but nothing we haven't seen in other games of the genre, and the large pathways make it difficult to place your towers in a worthwhile place. The game also gives you traps to play with, but because you're sending waves of your own soldiers down the same path, you risk harm to your own units. Also, traps deplete in health over time (poison pools get smaller, etc.). The towers can be upgraded in a generic fashion (improvements to damage, range, etc., but no upgrade pathways). The game doesn't alert you of this, nor does it tell you that goodly units can utterly destroy your towers until one is already gone. This becomes more of a problem in levels where there are multiple pathways and multiple barracks. Basically, you must fight the flow of enemies on multiple paths, utilizing towers and waves of your own units (who take from the same small resource pool). Making this more frustrating to do is the map navigation, where you must scroll for a while to check on multiple paths and waste precious seconds.

Oh My Gore! isn't a terrible game. It's just not great at tower defense, which is the element I looked forward to most. If you're someone who loves tower defense games with a twist (like Bardbarian, which I also didn't care for), you might like this. But I find that changing the genre to such an extreme degree makes it lose sight of its identity and the main reasons why I play TD games. Oh My Gore! is beautiful, particularly when enemies collapse in giblets and blood overwhelms a path. But try as I might, I failed to have any fun.\t\t\t\t\t
