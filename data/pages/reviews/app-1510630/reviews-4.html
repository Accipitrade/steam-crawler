Pitch-Perfect

Bonte Avond, a group of four talented musicians, has made a game that makes me smile every time I think of it. Everybody Wham Wham is a very short experience, but it's incredibly cozy, and perfect for a winter morning.

We play as a silent protagonist with a pumpkin hat who arrives at the annual Wham Wham the Snowman Man Annual Snowman Building Festival, a 10-day contest where participants compete to create the best snowman. Meanwhile, festival organizer Wham Wham struggles with his inner demons, and seeks to hide a terrible secret from everyone else.

Each day, you are given the criteria for a snowman, and have about 2 minutes to make one with the correct size and decorated with items within the day's theme. Traditional snowmen have stovepipe hats, coal eyes, and carrots, but the game also includes fantasy items like wizard hats and dwarf beards, spooky items like witches' noses and vampire fangs, tropical items like sunglasses and leis, and a number of other fun decorations. Items are in short supply, though, so you may have to gain an edge by stealing items from and otherwise sabotaging your fellow contestants.

Every evening, you are rewarded with the opportunity to spend time in town, chatting with the contest judges, fellow festival-goers. This is the best part of the game, as the voice acting and general tone of the writing is super charming and there's all sorts of lovely musical numbers and interludes that are 100% worth the game's price.

Since release, Everybody Wham Wham has gotten two major content updates; a Halloween update which adds a bunch of spooky content, and a post-credits after-story which feels almost like a game of its own.

All in all, I really enjoy Everybody Wham Wham, and I am looking forward to Bonte Avond's next project, Once Upon a Jester.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEverybody Wham Wham is one of the best and short story games I have ever played.
I laughed harder playing this game than I have in months.
After playing the whole story in just 2 hours I encountered literally no bugs so bravo in that front!
While the story is simple I found myself transported to a delightfully absurd world straight out of a children's book that made me laugh out loud with its endearing humor.
Within the first 5 minutes of booting up the game I got the meet a snowman singing a Jazz number about competition requirements and the wonderful and silly energy didn't stop until the very end. The whole cast of crazy characters kept me enthralled the whole time and are most certainly a highlight of this game. The developers did a good job of creating a world in which everybody has the complexity to be a little bit happy and still carry a little bit of sadness as well.
The game play itself is also creative and fun and completely unlike anything else: you get to build snowmen and cheat in a way that is totally awesome and unique.
I have programmed with Game Maker before and I am very impressed with the masterpiece that these developers have created.
I could say more but I wouldn't want to spoil the game -- you should just try it for yourself.
My one criticism is that the game should have a multiplayer mode because it would make a FANTASTIC party game!
I do understand though that networking can be frustrating in Game Maker though.
If there were multiplayer I would totally buy this game for my friends to play at a party and if you ever make another game I will be sure to pre-order!
10/10 and good work!\t\t\t\t\t
