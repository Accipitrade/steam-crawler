\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very cute and relaxing experience (with some very funny moments). The visual art is PRIMABELLA and the soundtrack is PERFECT-EL RINO and gets stuck in your head even when you're not playing the game.

There are some things that I'd like to see more in the game though, for example, I'd like to be able to interact more with the characters (I don't mean talking to them, I like the way that the main character communicates with the others, but I'd like to have more of those moments when you get to know them and they tell you about their lives), also, I'd like to be able to interact more with the scenario, aaaaaand (even more important than the others) I'd like to see multiple endings, I wish we could be able to convince the characters to change their minds about some things (which could impact the ending).

Despite that, I think this game is a great experience and definitively recommend it. :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLots of games nowadays lean towards an epic length. Several hours of gameplay where it is pretty easy to see the developers padding for time. That's not necessarily a bad thing. However, when you don't have a ton of time in between life stuff, I find games like Everybody Wham Wham's length and impact absolutely perfect! Similar to how a short story condenses an experience within less pages, Everybody Wham Wham keeps the player interested with a fun plot and hilarious mechanics. The set up is perfect.

The gameplay is simple - build a snowman within the criteria. There's a time limit to consider and fun criteria like "fantasy" snowman. You find yourself rushing over to the accessory hut and trying to grab all the items that seem to fit "fantasy". But watch out for the other competitors who are also eyeing the stand for the perfect wizard hart or witch nose. When it comes down to it, you might even have to sabotage competitors in order to up your game. It definitely feels more intense than a regular dress up game. And it also has a lot of personality put into it with judges walking by each snowman to give scores and your competitors distraught expressions when you've successfully sabotaged their carefully built snowman. There definitely is some strategy and quick fingers needed to avoid being caught acting as an absolute scoundrel.

Bonte Avond is also uniquely a team made up of indie musicians. I absolutely loved the soundtrack of the game. It's definitely like playing through an indie music album with hilarious jokes and memorable tunes. So. Many. Great. Songs. Fantastic musical of a game. I found myself humming the main theme to Everybody Wham Wham throughout the day. Can't wait for the soundtrack to get released. It's a great game for those looking for a short sweet experience and a way to wind down.

Best way to enjoy the game is definitely with a cup of hot cocoa in the middle of a snowstorm. It's cosy and a great way to destress. Building snowmen, enjoying the quirky world Bonte Avond has built, and meeting some adorable characters. Really recommend this game to those looking for a short but memorable experience.

Everybody Wham Wham
The moment I saw the art style on this one I figured it would be a cute and wholesome game. I wasn't wrong. In Everybody Wham Wham you take control of a contestant wearing a pumpkin head who doesn't talk in a snowman building contest at the Wham Wham the Snowman-man Snowman building festival. There are a range of memorable characters to meet and talk to. Overall the main game is 10 days. Each day you will be able to peruse around the festival and talk to characters before getting to the next day in the contest.

You have to build a snowman that coincides with the criteria for that day. There is a theme and size given with each day. The other fun thing about this mechanic is some of the resources are limited. You might have a fantasy theme but have to beat the other two contestants to the items. Since the way the ai increases in difficulty each day, it makes the game fresh and interesting as you progress. Aside from the charming and wholesome dialogue I have to say the big shining point in this game is the music. You can hear the main song in the steam trailer but there are more great songs and interludes throughout (including a pretty great daily radio broadcast).

The Minor Gripes
I honestly can't say I have many gripes about this one. There were a few times I couldn't make the size as correct I would have liked to because the snowballs spawned a bit further away, but this may have also been because I always made a mad dash for items first. I wish there was more to the story because there are what I felt like are a few loose ends. Rarely there was dialogue repeated which I mostly noticed with the washed up reindeer Rudy.

Aside from that I think what's here is great. It would also be really great if there was an unlockable gallery of sorts to either replay scenes or listen to the radio broadcasts again without having to play the whole game again.

(っ◔◡◔)っ ♥ pros ♥
+Cute & wholesome\n+Great soundtrack and dialogue\n\n
(っ>▬<)っ x cons x
-Minor loose ends with characters\n

So we've got a short, fun, and wholesome snowman building game with lots of charm and a fantastic soundtrack. I hope the developer works on more projects because this was very enjoyable. I put about two hours into the game to beat it. There isn't currently any achievements to obtain. There is a quick play mode if you just like the snowman building aspect to the game, however the story is so charming it's worth going through.
