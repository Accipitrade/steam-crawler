\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is a 2D vector graphics based space shooter where the only objective is to conquer and destroy everything else. You start in a small ship in your own faction with only one station to your name. You gain resources by destroying enemy faction ships, collecting resources from nearby asteroids, and expanding your territory by activating unused stations. Once you have enough resources, you can design your very own ships to the fullest degree as they are no mandatory templates or spaceship hulls to work off of. You can build ships that can build other ships on the fly and command a fleet of your custom made ships. All the blocks that make up a ship must be researched first, and the only way to gain blocks to your "datastore" is to destroy enemy ships with said block.

*** Pros ***
Vector graphics and particle effects mean zero load times and small game size (~100 MB)
Custom build all ships from movement to weapon systems
Extensive tutorial to teach the games mechanics
High replayability as you can unlock other factions ships
Good soundtrack and sound effects

*** Cons ***
Large battles can get confusing because of particle effects\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game. Simple mechanic yet very addictive: you can create any kind of craft you want. Be it a big and slow heavily armed cruiser or a fast and nimble smaller fighter, the combination are seemingly endless.

+ A free roaming procedurally generated "galaxy" populated by different factions that fight each others for sector control.
+ Attractive but simple graphics with lots of different particle effects.
+ Greatly optimized resulting in perfectly fluid gameplay and rigid body physic despite the hundreds of objects interacting simultaneously.
+ There is a strange but appealing organic, underwater feeling to the game.
+ Soundtrack fits nicely with the game ambiance.
+ Incoming MODs support via Steamworks.

- Some factions could have been better balanced and lack variety in spacecraft design.

If you like old school 2D space shooters, give this one a try. The ship designing aspect adds a nice touch to the genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game I've been looking for. Reassembly is easy to understand, rough to master, with multiple different ship construction strategies that may or may not work, whether alone or together.
Your objective is to collect resources to upgrade and build your ship(s), eventually providing your ship with a factory block that can spawn a fleet for you. Enemies are generated off of other players' designs, copied through the use of wormholes, keeping you however from ever encountering real players, just AI versions of their fleets.

Pros:
-No frame drops, I've rarely been lowered in video quality throughout my time playing
-Expansive maps, plenty of sectors generated swiftly with new stations to capture and factions to fight
-Comfortable building interface, with useful tools like mirror and copy and paste to make everything much faster to build, putting you in the fight sooner, plus a lot of weapons and tools to make specialized ships like drone swarm spawners, rocket carronades, snipers, etc.
-Nice artstyle. Very minimalistic, but it is still nonetheless fun to watch your ships "reassemble" as the new parts are traced and come to life in the middle of a battle
-No loading screens. Literally none.
-Test feature, which pits your newly made ship against another of the same value to test its strength in a real battle, before you try and fly a space dumpster into a battle and waste time

Cons:
-Slightly repetitive soundtrack. Good music, but it seems to loop a lot. Maybe I'm just playing too much :P
-Instructions and controls must be looked up a couple times to learn how to do everything (ie Toggle hud, commision fleet ships, set child cycles for your fleet's ships, etc)
-My biggest problem: Ship control. Most games like this have the WASD keys stay in a locked position of North, West, South, East. This game, however, orients control around your crosshair, meaning you have to constantly check which way your shape is facing and reorient yourself to fly properly. Very hard to get used to.

All in all, I'm giving it a 8/10. Fantastic game, and I highly recommend it, I would just like to see some new features implemented and that movement control thing given a second option.

Things I want to See Implemented:
-Co-op multiplayer. Allow a friend to join you in your faction and grow alongside you. Perhaps a combining force of an intelligent gunship and bot spawning mother-ship, I thought this would make a fantastic duo.
-New music
-New weapons and hull parts/utillity pieces, some ideas are:
> Repair drone. Accelerates rebuild speed.
> Cloaking system
> Repulsion/Attraction blocks, for pulling in enemies into short range weapons or pushing them away from a long range ship
> Kamikazee drones. Name says it all
> Barrier construction. Some kind of attachment that uses resources to build walls for defense, or maybe even dispense some kind of acid or magma to ward off pursuers.
> Parasite drone. A drone that embeds into the core of a ship and changes its faction, rebuilding it and putting it in your fleet, maybe even copying the schematic into your inventory


So those are my ideas and reviews of the game, hope it helped and you agree!\t\t\t\t\t

This is a beautifull game that makes you rage when the other factions kill you brutally and joy when you destroy their cities to death.
Ok now to the true review.
CONTENT:
This game is full of content:from giga weapons to strange asteroids that explode on contact,every faction has its own weapons and parts that makes you feel you are playing a different game when you switch faction.
The map is HUGE and every time different with tons of biomes.
GRAPHICS:
The game graphics are extremely simple but beautifull at the same time,they are very clean and outlined with lit colors.
CHALLENGE:
You'll start with a small weak ship and you are going to build your way up to a powerfull mother ship with hundreds of drones...
at least if the other factions don't destroy you first:every faction is hostile,no one will help you except some ships and some stations that are from the same faction as you.
So the game is very hard at the start but in the end it's easy as eating a piece of cake.
ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE:
While in combat the ai is formidable,3 ships can kill you in no time dodging all of your hits,into the travel factor they often crash in to asteroids.
Multiplayer:WARNING MAY CONTAIN SPOILERS.















I'VE WARNED YOU.





















Ok now i can talk.
The multiplayer factor is divided into two parts:wormholes and tournament mode.
Wormholes:
While exploring your world you could find worholes ,with them you can do two things:send your fleet in the world of another player(it's random),the sent fleet will spawn as an "agent" that whill destroy the players stations,or you can travel physically in to another world that is different from the one that you were in.
Tournament:
In tournament mode you can challenge other players in a fight between fleets(you can select the ones that you have built in single player).
Final vote: 10/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t5/10. Game runs well and controls are feel a bit odd, but manageable. There are factions, in the general scheme of things, they are the same. For a game about modifying and tweaking your ships, it shoots itself in the foot with combat. Combat has the depth of a kiddy pool. Which links back to the factions being similar. There is no cloaking, emps, damage differences between shield and armor, and no afterburners. Just to highlight a few things. Balance is questionable. No real economy outside of combat, trade maybe? Docking for stations? A campaign? Those don't ruin the game just felt missing.

Bottom line for around $12 it should give you a good few hours of fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're a sucker for design-it-and-fail kind of games (like I am), then this game will be the crack to your addiction. The simplistic parts that you get to design your ship with is all you need to create the coolest looking space-ships this side of the galaxy. It's not as simple as "plug the device on and go", you need to build your ship CORRECTLY or it won't maneuver through space! Don't have enough retro-thrusters? You better hope you can back off the enemy fast enough by turning (and not dying from losing your back end). Didn't think you needed thrusters to turn? Guess you're going in one direction infinitely. It's a constant failure zone, and I just LOVE IT!

It's a very grindy game, so if you don't like grinding, I would certainly stay away. And the sandbox mode isn't very intuitive either. I tried to build something in the constructor (in the sandbox mode), and I didn't even get things to build with. Don't get fooled with sandbox mode, it isn't a free build-any-ship-for-free mode @.@ If you don't mind grinding as you play different factions (have to unlock them), then you'll find HOURS upon HOURS trying new designs and seeing if they stack up with the best.

24 hours of Gameplay later: This game is even better! Again the game is grindy, but the different factions (without mods) force you to build your ships in different patterns. Have a good design for faction 1 (Terrans)? You're not using it on Faction 4 because there are no long-triangle blocks! The only downside with ship-building, is when you test your ship against others' you need to fight these really cheap min-maxed ships that do nothing but fly backwards when your ship catches up with every default ship in the game; actually it's the CPU attacking the other ships, but it's still cheap tactics that win.

Also, the sandbox mode isn't as convoluted as I first thought. I can create any ship I want with the right commands, and even see the default ship palettes for each faction. Unfortunately this isn't explained very well and you need a tutorial outside of the game to understand which commands do what (and that Faction 1 is actually Faction 8 for example). You can still build any ship you want in your save game, but the sandbox is very misleading (not much of a sandbox :/ More of a programmer's blank sheet of paper). I'm still getting my money's worth out of the game; Time to conquer the galaxy!\t\t\t\t\t
