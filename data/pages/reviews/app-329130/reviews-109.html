\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first saw this game I was overjoyed. I bought it, even though $30 is quite a steep price for a game of this size, but for the most part it was worth it. Ever since I bought it the Dev has been active in updating the game and has made some major improvements on the ai and visuals as a whole. I think one of the latest updates that gave the ships and stations a glow based on their color added even more to the game's beauty. I love the idea of being able to design my own ships and take over the galaxy. Not to mention getting to see other people's creations (and getting to blow them to ****ing pieces).

But this game does start to get old after a certain point. At the moment there are 7 factions, and I've been able to get to the endgame of just about each one of them, but after you do that.... there's nothing really to do. You conquer one galaxy, move on to the next, and do the same exact thing, and the hardest ships sadly are always the same, (the blue and pinkish purple ones) which are located in the middle, which sadly never changes. Faction balancing is also a problem right now. The red faction atm is completly useless and almost impossible to get anywhere. The blocks it has are far weaker than any other faction (other than its weapons, which do pack a punch) and its ships get completely destroyed almost instantly when you're in the starting stage with the faction. Even when you start creating bigger ships with them they get crushed by other factions who have far better blocks. That brings me to my next point, the lack of options when it comes to blocks for some factions. For example, a faction like the green race (the 2nd option in the menu) has 47 different blocks, whereas some of the others will only have a little over 20 (and are very limited when it comes to weapon variance).

Another thing that should have been in this game but is not is multiplayer. Sure there's going to be tournaments where you can pit your ships against other ships, but only with the ai using your ship instead of you... If I created a ship to my liking, I want to use that ship to my liking in a pvp environment. Sadly the Dev said that this is probably not going to happen beacuse he'd have to take the game apart to implement a multiplayer function. So if you're going into this game thinking that that's going to be an option. It's most likely not going to happen.

Overall, this game is very fun and I would definatley reccomend buying it, but wait until after it comes out this coming thursday (the 19th) because it's going to be going down to $15 I believe. The creativity in this game is nearly endless, and many good times can be had, but unfortunetly you won't beable to do this with friends in the same galaxy and once you do conquer that galaxy, it's going to get repetetive.

I'd give it an 8/10. Pick it up and enjoy it if you want a creative experience in the sci-fi world.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a bit like SPaZ with total ship customization. Completing objectives like capturing points and killing enemies awards you with upgrade points that you can use to buy more weapons and systems for your ships. You can start from the ground up to customize your ship, which gives you the freedom to make a spectacularly crappy ship or the ship of awesome, all governed by how many upgrade points you put into ship total size. It's so fun, and I constantly go back and tweak ships when I unlock new techs.

The price is higher than an indie game, and that's a bit of a negative, but there is plenty of value in the ship customization. There are different ship races to unlock, so there is a bit of replay value. The ship races differ dramatically, so it feels different evey time you start fresh with a new race.

Creativity in ship building is the shining point of this game, and I'm glad I bought it.

This game is fun and functional in its early access state, so don't worry about buying something buggy and unfinished. It's good!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never posted a review on Steam before, but Reassembly compelled me to do so.

Summary: This game is still in beta. It also has some of the most immersive aesthetics and mechanics of any game I've ever played. I have learned to approach it with the caution of a recovering drug addict in a pharmacy-- if I click the icon I can expect to lose at least several hours of my time, if not portions of my health, dreams, and/or general human function.

Criticisms: The only reasons I would hesitate to recommend it to my friends in its current state are 1) the effective lack of directed multiplayer, 2) the comparative ease once you start building capital ships, and 3) the lack of polish/balance across some of the playable factions.

1) I found in my playtime that some of what I enjoyed most in the game was the ship design testing process, where at any point you can place your design in the hands of an AI and have it fight best-of-three battles against up to five different AI controlled ships of similar power levels. I would love to have the ability to put my designs up against the designs of my friends and other players, whether one-on-one, faction vs faction, or in larger skirmishes or king of the hill matches.

2) Speaking of the king, once I completed a 8000P juggernaut and spent some time retooling my fleet even the center factions posed little threat (provided I didn't park in a plant faction death cluster or otherwise act a fool). As well, there was no longer any real use for the upgrade currency one you've maxed out the P limit and purchased all the weapon tiles. I haven't tried to achieve complete dominion of the entire game map yet, but the endgame isn't presently as enjoyable as the 20-30 hours I spent reaching it with the first faction (which is a pretty happy problem, granted).

3) As for the factions, the first one has tiles complete with descriptions giving you an idea of each tile's use, as well as some flavor text. The other factions (more recently added to the playable roster) are a bit of a mixed bag, and often require playtesting in order to determine what certain tiles do, or why two similar appearing tiles may have very different power costs. I'm sure this will improve as the game nears release.


Conclusion: This is an excellent indie space sim that melds the fun parts of designing ships with elements of RTS and simple asteroids-style gameplay. It is worth the $30 as is, and I'm excited to see the rest of what the developers pack in before it hits release.\t\t\t\t\t

I have played Hundreds upon Hundreds of rpg's, sandbox's, adventure and action gerne etc.
This Masterpiece took the elements from many of the games I held dear, and assimilated them all into a free spirited sandbox rpg with not just replayability, but literally the best customization and diversity i've ever seen in an rpg/sandbox.
