\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is THE definitive lego game for lovers of space and starships.
It always surprises me when developers are able to boil the concept of 'fun' down to such a simple, gratifying level. And that is exactly what you get with this game; an intuitive fleet building experience where the fun doesn't stop when you hit the building menues.
I'd argue it starts there. Though admittedly half or so of my game-time was spent afk (as with most games I play), I have spent roughly 20 hours building ships, and maybe 5 actually cruising around. The 'tournament' style mode you can throw any ship you've been building into is incredibly rewarding, and oddly relaxing. Sitting back and watching the Dreadnaught you've spent an hour on go ripping through other people's lovingly crafted creations is a great system that both allows you to test out your ship against a variety of other players' designs, and serves as eye candy to the nth degree.

The game has a concept of 'agent' fleets, in which a fleet designed by other players invades your world. There is a peculiar feeling I get from this game that stems from this idea, and not even necessarily when I'm playing it. The fact that some poor player will at some point accidentaly have a run in with my fine tuned, massive killing machines gives me a sadistic pleasure that I would say is almost entirely unique to this game.

A gem of a game for people of all ages, and now that it has modding support added, who knows what can come out of this game.

Starting out as a single faction to pick, and a basic little ship, you start out a bit causious, trying to gather resources to build your ship up and try to take control of the map with a fleet of your own design along side you.

I have to say that one thing that really impressed me is how optimized this game really is. It might seem like the simple nature of just polygons and other shapes would run smooth, but the effects and lighting that can take place during a large battle between many ships can cause your system to lag. Surprisingly though, it takes several hundred ships in that battle before it really starts to take any effect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis games has essentially redefined the "build your own spaceship" kind of games. Sure, it's only 2D - but that gives the modding commuity much more room to push the boundaries! In terms of goals there are basically three:

Destroy all hostiles (thus stay alive)
Design awesome custom ships
Have a relaxing cruise through space to the games sweet soundtrack.

In a bit more detail though; some people dislike the deliberate inaccuracy with space physics, but I think it helps to simplify the playing and strategising. Having blocks with different features (such as ncreasing thrust/torque, beam weapons, cannons, missiles, resource generators) the creative potential is only really limited by the block limit and the RAM of your PC (I play this on a 8GB RAM laptop and it works OK, but best results come from higher RAM).

All in all, I cannot praise this game enough!\t\t\t\t\t
