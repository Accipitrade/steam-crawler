\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not a bad game, but it's not a great game either.

It seems like with all the factions the programmer had some larger plans for this game. Such as inter faction trading or some sort of diplomacy.

Sadly, there is only one thing to do in this game, build ships and blow up all the other guys ships. It gets old fast. Really fast.

Also, the ship designer really could have used some more work. All the parts don't fit together correctly. With some of the ship parts, the connection is just slightly off from the other parts. This means your dealing with small slight single pixel splits in your ships... arg...

If the developer for this game ever decides to improve this game, or bring out a sequel with high level functions like better trade and faction interaction that is more than just all out war all the time, I will be interested. Until then I have to put this game on the shelf.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is a decent sandbox shooter that has a relaxed atmosphere. The best part, obviously, is building your own ships and experimenting with designs. As you gather resources you can purchase better parts. Each of the factions have a unique theme and require different strategies to design. The ship building interface is very intuitive and easy to use, which is a big plus.

It's a nice, chill single player game but I think overall it lacks depth, which I will describe below.
There's really no need for specialization against any particular foe. With such flexibility in ship design I was hoping this game could push this concept more. Once you have a decent ship it works against all factions almost universally. You can fiddle with designs for a while, but in the end, more expensive parts will make you better. With many factions it's quickest to destroy enemy ships for resources, so that becomes your sole focus. Once you max out your ship, basically nothing stands in your way. Unless you tried to get all the Steam achievements, like I did, there's not much of an end game to keep you playing. I spent most of my time on this game grinding out achievements.

In conclusion, it was fun but lack of depth put me on the fence on this one. After getting all of the achievements, I feel like I tapped out all of my reasons to keep playing. I would have liked to see the ship building aspect developed further. I'm leaning on the fence to recommend this game, but it was still fun for the time being. If you feel like this game might be something you'll be interested in then you'll probably enjoy it.\t\t\t\t\t

- You bought Reassembly.
- You have not bought Reassembly.

This game is that sandbox you played in as a kid. While most people were playing GI Joes and army men, you were pretending that firetruck was a star destroyer. That Tonka truck was a Carrier. This game is that sandbox, and that Carrier is real.

Anything in your mind can be made in this game. Do you want to build unrealistic space gods that spin at 2000mph while sneezing death? Do it. Maybe you want to build a true to design Star Destroyer, or the BattleStar Galactica? I've seen CIS Control ships, the Serenity, a Borg fleet, and a literal pirate ship with tiny whale escorts. If the blocks fit, you can build it. And the blocks always fit(not).

As for actual mechanics, this game starts you out as a tiny little fighter. You destroy enemy ships, reactivate defunct friendly stations and gather resources. These resources allow you to upgrade your ship, until one day you too can build a battlecruiser the size of a small moon. You can also produce your own fleet, which can in turn produce its own fleet or follow you into combat or both. There are tons of ways to play the game, and little things you will find that feels like your own little trick.

TL;DR: This game is too much fun. You will get addicted. You will stop playing, and then come back and splurge another 50 hours on it after you think you forgot about it.
