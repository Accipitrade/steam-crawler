\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is the like the crust of a video game sandwich; you might hate it, or you might think it's the best part of the sandwich. tl;dr: Reassembly is a game that lets you design your own ships, fight pre-generated factions, and take over the galaxy™.

This is one where you find out if you want to be an engineer or not. The game is relatively simple in concept, but can get very in depth with designs, to the point where you spend a couple hours creating the perfect snub-fighter only to scrap half of it because it doesn't fit with the rest of your faction's theme.

You can play in a creative way, using out-of-the-box weaponry and ideas to destroy enemy ships (such as using tractor beams to ram into an enemy) or you can simply brute force through anyway (such as attaching infinite plasma cannons to your borg cube). It's like a Minecraft for space warfare, but without the same universal charm.

I liked it, but it really isn't all too unique in nature. I think it fills the role of a "cult classic" pretty well.\t\t\t\t\t
