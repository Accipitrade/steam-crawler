\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn Reassembly, you start out as a little ship which is just a minion of the station or carrier which spawned you. you collect resources and bring them back to the station in order for the station to make more allies to fight with you. each time you do this, you get credits which you can use to upgrade your ship and buy new part. Once you get big enough, the roles switch. You get to a point where you get a part which lets you spawn ships. your resources now go to making your own fleet which follows you and fights for you. Those ships will also bring you the resources they collect, allowing you to make more. If you put enough storage on your ship, you can even spawn the massive stations which intially spawned you in the begining.

I love the game and it never gets old. There is always a new ship or even fleet to fight. Just as you think you have built the most powerful ship, an agent comes in and kills you and your fleet in seconds. This makes it so that you have to tweek your own design in order to compete with the new foe.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a surprisingly addictive game.

Pros:
- Cutting enemy ships CLEAN IN HALF piece-by-piece with my massive laser array and watching the remaining pieces tumble through space gives me a warm fuzzy feeling.
- The ship designer is probably the best one I've seen in any game that lets you construct vehicles.
- There are several unlockable factions which have completely different ship components for lots of replayability.
- There are occasional bugs, but so far they're minor and don't detract from the fun.

Cons:
- The friendly AI ships currently don't do a very good job at following you or fighting.
- Some obscure parts of the interface aren't very well explained or are kind of 'hidden' (but aren't hard to figure out once you find them).
- Factions don't have names (just numbers - which are easy to mix up), which makes them cumbersome to discuss with other players.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was a weird experience. It looks like a RTS, but plays like a 2D action game. It gives you the feeling of an ant in an anthill, fighting against the minions of other swarms. But also you grow and evolve and start to get your own swarm, that follows you.
All in all, beside being a space shooter, it really has some organic feeling. It also could be on the bottom of the ocean. Or maybe small microorganisms.

Your ship is made of different shaped geometric objects. Hull parts, interior and weapons. Zoomed out, they give surprisingly good looking shapes. After you design your first ship, you are thrown in a crazy living 2D world, where everything tries to kill you, that has a different color than you. This isnt always user friendly. Flying too early too far out of your territory you face enemys, that are 10 times the size of you. While you explore, you unlock commando points, that give you new weapons or upgrading your ship to be able to use more parts to be bigger. Also your surroundings generate ressources. Everyone is gathering them. If you kill an enemy, he will drop his ressources, if you die, they will also loot you. You can turn in those ressources on allied bases to get more commando points, also that station will generate new allied ships. That way, every faction on the map is constantly growing at attacking the borders of the other faction.
As the game continues, you will find more and more ressources, unlock new weapons, until you are that big, that you stop selling your ressources but generating your own minions, that follow you. If they are big enough and have a factory, they also will be able to gather ressources and build ships.
If you are bored with your universe, you take your fleet and go to a new one (reset the game, like a new game+).

Every enemy faction has unique weapons, buildings, ships and tactics. Some of them can be really annoying, most of them are playable, after you once defeated them.

While working perfectly (I only had one massive crash), the game has a lot of flaws that bug me:
- there is no real war: The world only moves around you. If you arent present, the areas arent loaded. That way, you only have the illusion of a living world, but the only acting thing is you.
- enemy AI is korean like, allied AI is on drugs: Having some maxed out follower ships, then seeing them flying into an enemy fleet like idiots and dying like idiots makes you question the game. Seeing the enemy flying player designed ships better than the player is capable of, makes you rage a lot. It is no fun fighting the AI. They will dash forward, attack you and retreat like cowards, when you fire back. That way, a lot of small ships are capable of slowly poking you to death. If you manage to hit them, they will run away, like on crack, never giving you the chance of actually destroy and loot them. Some ships, even split in half and totally destroyed managed to escape me with 1 or 2 remaining thrusters and the commando bridge. A strong AI is cool and challenging. A perfect AI, that plays safe and never do mistakes is frustrating. A lot! Its not about them being OP, its about them always fleeing a fight.
- Charging my lazerz: directed beam weapons > everything. You can defeat 90% of the map by using massive amounts of the charge beam laser. Then put it on point defence. Then laugh like an idiot watching those skirmishing ships flying towards you to do their stupid hit and run, seeing them exploding because your point defence automatically targets and destroy them. Your other weapons will be massive amounts of plasma cannon to melt down everything that cant move away, or, if you like it with more precision, gaus rifles to shoot those poor bastards without them even knowing from where you hit them.
- After reaching max size, the game is over. There is nothing to do. Even the big ships, that chased you in the beginning, are small fishes now. I even managed to cut one of them in half with a single salve of gaus shots. Since you have the lasers, you dont have to care about the small ones ether. Simply ignore them. They will come in weapon range, then explode.
- If you are bad at designing and crafting games, just dont.

Reassembly is not a decent game, it is an awesome game! But has major problems with balancing, AI, mapping and simply has no endgame.\t\t\t\t\t

I started it up with the intention of playing for 5-10 minutes just to see what it was like and the next thing I know it was three hours later.

Graphically the game has style, though it is somewhat hard to tell from the screenshots that I have seen. I personally like the vector graphic look and reminds me of Asteroids which I played the heck out of in my younger years. The game is a bit hard to get into and I am sure I am going to have to watch a number of vidoes to understand all the concepts but it is easy enough to play with and pick up enough to enjoy.

This is one of those games that you just have to play for ourself to see how addictive it is.
