\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is one of the best games I have ever played. With enjoyable combat and clear goals, you don't get bored of playing. The music is stunning. It definitly has a learning curve, ecpecaly with the ship editor. You do need at least a meh pc for the late/end game. As your fleet grows larger, playing on a mac will drop below 5 frames per second, and the game will stutter to a halt. But, with a decent pc it is fine. I have an i5 with a 1060 3gb, and 8 gbs of ram, and it runs flawlessly. Gameplay wise, it is extreamly fun, for the first 50 to 70 hours, but then exploring and minor ship upgrades begin to feel repetitive. That's were the classes come in. By destorying an enemy ship over 1000 p, you can play as it. This allows for nigh on infinite gameplay, and is well done. Each class has somthing defined and special about it that makes it feel like a new and fresh experiance. The only thing is, you may find is that the grind will get very intence later on. But, this game does a very good job in that it dosn't feel like grind. Some weapons or parts might cost over 20000 of the games currency, but it is ok that it does, because it adds much more staying power for the game, because you will feel motivated to play it more. I have played over 8 full days of this game (Over around a month), and my only real complaint would be the lack of classes like borg being vanilla playable, or classes like 15, who you don't even see! Also, there is a screensaver mode for some reason, and its beutiful, but not implemented or usable for some reason.

Pros:
Many hours of entertainment
Well made
Cheap
Controller support
Beautiful
Gives you that happy feeling
Trigger happy

Cons:
Not quite done in a few small senses
Will waste a lot of your life\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all: I love this game because of its minimalistic (retro) style and because of its addictive game play.
The graphics could have been done on an Amiga 500 with its very good vector graphics capabilities and would fit there very good too (-> retro style). The 'what nice thing could I change next on ship (s)' works very well and keep you playing for hours.

The other side of the medal unfortunately is that the game runs good only on windows with the recommended hardware settings (but there I had no crashes and strange behaviour yet). It seems that the game was tested only on better graphics cards and perhaps nvidia only? My laptop AMD card does not work that good. On linux I get no visuals at all.

The controls work good but still need some fine tuning: menu flows, showing the state of used controls.

Perhaps it should have been marked as early access game so far even if there is hardware out there were it works flawlessly. It is still beta in my opinion.
With the very addictive game play experience already all energy of further development should go into bug fixing to run on more hardware and OSes I think.\t\t\t\t\t
