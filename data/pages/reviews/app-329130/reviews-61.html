\r\n\t\t\t\t\tToday, I'm going to write a review for Reassembly, and I hope it reaches everyone who is thinking about purchasing this game.

Where to start...
Reassembly throws you into a hostile universe, riddled with factions that care only about your destruction.
When starting, there is a very gradual, and intuitive learning curve, with just enough complexity to keep you coming back for more. Even after unlocking all factions, and playing with a few which are community created, I still find this game highly replayable. Reason? Space domination of course!

There still seem to be a few balancing issues with several of the factions, but this game is damn well polished.

- Very high replay value. With an endless combination of building possibilities, Reassembly rivals some of the more well known indie games. With multiple universes/galaxies to explore, and constantly discovering new player created enemies. I have to give this game a 9/10 for replayability alone

- Very nice points system (R, C, P). The system seems designed to make you feel very powerful, and like you are accomplishing a lot when playing. However it does not make you feel over powered (except some player created factions). Player factions aside this game makes you feel like you are getting your moneys worth. 10/10

- Excellent use of propulsion/collision, and super cool, almost retro feeling graphics make Reassembly feel very polished. 9/10

- Amazing ecosystems. Reassembly has one of the most organic ecosystems I have ever encountered. From enemies repopulating, to the way a single space plant can launch a seed, stick to another asteroid, and begin growing to produce more seeds and plants, alone, makes this game worth it's price point. ShUT UP AND TAkE MY MUNNY! 10/10

- Music. I don't even know how to explain this one - The soundtrack to Reassembly is one of the absolute best soundtracks you could ever hope for in a space game. It really makes you feel at home in the game, and is extremely well done. Whoever created the music for Reassembly deserves a raise of epic proportions! I would have to say that the soundtrack drew me in more than anything. Usually when playing a game, I like to mute the music volume, and play my own tunes, but with this, I just like to turn the music volume up! Bravo to the Maestro! 10/10

Overall I have to say that I have fully enjoyed the experience bestowed upon me by Reassembly. If you're thinking about getting this game, I absolutely recommend jumping the fence, and going for it! I hope to see updates to this game in the future, but as of right now, the game is amazing as it stands.

Overall score for this game:
9.7/10

Great game, and very cool creations by the community. You will enjoy this game, if your thing is ship building, and quasi space exploration.

Thanks for reading! I hope you enjoy it as much as I did!\t\t\t\t\t
