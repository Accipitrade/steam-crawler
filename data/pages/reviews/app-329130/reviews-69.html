\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust bought this game and played 4.5 hours straight. The ship building is really addicting. It's awesome how you have to balance your thrusters, weapons, modules and how it looks. It seems incredibly balanced. Except for the fact that the guns are ridicolously strong. But since this game supports workshop, and I see other players having around the 100-1000 hours played area, I'm sure you can probably fix most stuff you don't like with a mod. I would probably pay the full price it's asking for this game (I paid 50%).

20 Hours played: Well, the ship building is still as addicting as when I first started playing this game. It's so cool to kinda understand ship design stuff when designing your own ships. For instance, you realise your front is very vulnerable and you add more armor to the front of the ship. But since you want to keep the total mass down, and you realise you don't need all that armor in the back of the ship, you get an old school design where the back is your weakness. And all the games where you have to take something down and the game tells you to shoot it's back for more damage suddenly makes sense.
Another example is maneuverability vs armor vs damage. You want your ship to have massive amounts of armor, and you want a ton of guns on your ship. But you dont have any space for thrusters. So your ship can't move. You take away some guns and make room for thrusters, but you have too few and too small thrusters to move your big ship. You take away even more guns to an "acceptable" point, and you put in more thrusters, but your ship still moves super sluggish. You redesign the whole ship and make sure you worry about how it moves at all times. But the bigger your ship becomes, the harder it is to find enough spots to fit your thrusters. Makes you understand why big ships are always portrayed as super slow moving. Amazingly fun tinkering in any case!\t\t\t\t\t

Unsurpassed. THE BEST ship building game I've ever found, and I've tried a lot of them.
No grid. No arbitrary nonsense. Beautiful game world.

Modding support is such that there are thousands of modded factions, weapons, and more.

[]
Reassembly is a lovely game.

It's pleasing simplicity makes it easy to play with. It's not oppressively realistic like some other space titles, and is focused on giving you a fun spaceship architect fantasy.

Modding also allows you to craft and tailor the difficulty of the game to your liking and alter the game's default palettes to include entirely different blocks and systems.

The world, in and of itself is thoroughly beautiful, and immersive in just how simple and clean it is to look at.
The plants live, breathe, and spread on their own.
The ships and factions have their territory, and fight to defend themselves.
But are not psychopathically aggressive, and display a degree of awareness.
They are individual battleship captains on a quest to expand their empire.

And you.
Are the mad person with a little ship and big hopes, who is gonna stop them.
With seven total playable factions, ( and many more from mods )
Each with their own palette, and weapon systems.
Reassembly offers a serious value proposition in terms of time, replay ability, and sheer relaxation.

Mould your faction into what you want it to be.
Conquer the galaxy.

Then, do it all over again with a fresh set of ships.
Or, live semi-peacefully with the farmers. Building a vast ecosystem of your ships.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, I was surprised to find out that I hadn't written a review for this game yet, so here it goes:

Reassembly is probably one of the most unique games I've played. Essentially, it's a top-down RTS-ish shooter game, where you get to build your ship from the ground up. This means unlimited possibilities in your playstyle, but each individual part you build can be destroyed. This means that when building you have to take into consideration the armor and placement of features on your ship.

There is also a massive faction system in the game, and your whole goal is to make sure that yours prospers. This might seem tricky, but you can build and hire allies to help you out in securing more sectors of the universe. Eventually, your goal is to either take over the entire universe, or go through one of the wormholes at the center, travelling you to another universe. You can also upload your fleet that you assemble for other players to battle. The fleet you upload will appear as an "Agent" fleet in someone else's universe. Agents are player uploaded fleets, and there are 8 per universe, so it isn't as easy to take over the universe as it first appears.

One of the most interesting and subtle features is that you are evolving the faction that you choose. All of the ships/stations/etc. that you create can be rebuilt by all of your allies. This doesn't seem like a very big deal, but later in the game it is. After a while of playing in the same universe, I noticed that I, singlehandedly, evolved my allegience of farmers into the most powerful fighting force in the universe. This means that you can alter the playstyle of your faction into something completely different then how they started.

Something else to note is that this game is incredibly modder friendly. I had never actually modded a game before I bought Reassembly. All I knew how to do was install mods that I downloaded online. However, I found that Reassembly's files were incredibly easy to navigate, and I could tell exactly what I was looking for when I saw it. All I had to do was open some files in Notepad or Notepad++, and just look around for the things I wanted changed. Again, the files are really self-explanitory.

I highly recommend this game to everyone who likes top-down shooters and building games. I give it a 9/10
