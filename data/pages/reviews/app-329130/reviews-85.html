Update (5/4/2017):
Wow it's hard imagining how hyper I was a year ago. Stat update: 90 products, 1 review, 2,276+ hrs, still a good game. Come visit us at our community discord at https://discord.gg/DSvmNbY when you get the chance, random stranger, passerby reader, friend.

Edit (3/21/16):
It's been 4 months. Looking at my stats it says: 51 products, 1 review, 1400+ hours; I know what you're thinking... DO NOT CALL 911, I have assuredly not been taken hostage by this game. I just happened to leave the program running 24/7 every now and then. There is totally nothing to worry about (please don't judge me!)...

I have found that most of this game's replayability lies in (1) - creating and enjoying the diverse player-created mods and (2) - pitting ship designs against each other in competitions amongst friends as a frantic cry for help

Mods are incredibly easy to work with (using and creating) and can be downloaded or turned on/off with minimal hassle. With absolutely zero bias none whatsover, for a quick graphics spruce-up, you can visit my graphics-upgrade mod at http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=644263599

Actual Review (12/10/15):
I've always had a soft spot for the build-your-own-spaceship genre. I enjoyed Captain Forever, Battleships Forever, Gimbal, as well as Stardrive 2. I have no idea how I didn't find out about it until now.

I think Reassembly takes the great construction and space combat elements of all those games and puts it in one solid package. I was surprised to see it run smoothly during heavy action (unlike Stardrive), In its category, the decent amount of gameplay depth and accessibility help make this one a top tier 2D-space-builder-shooter. Sure there's some things that need fixing (such as the tutorial checklist showing up every time a new game is started) but even so I would wholeheartedly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
