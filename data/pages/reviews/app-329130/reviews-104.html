With a screen full of particle effects the game runs smoothly.

You can design any 2D shape you can think of given the limitation of the pieces per faction. Practically any design is possible with enough pieces.

Ship design is done by you. You shape it's toughness, layout, engine placement, maneuverability, weaponry, and other things related to each faction.

There isn't much direction, but the game is simple, shoot everyone else. There is a simple tutorial to get you to realize some things that might not be apparent.

Some achievements are weak, but others actually take time and effort.

Other player's ships show up in your universe to battle against.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet be break this down into a few categories. Graphics, Sound, Story, Controls, Gameplay. I will end this review with my honest opinion after just about 30 hours in game.


Graphics:

The Graphics for this game are simple. And that is what this game's charm is riding on. 2D. It is simple, but because of this, this game has a very good feel to it. Like games of yesteryear. The game, with it's simple graphics. also has much diversity in the way it presents said graphics. From lightly colored tints, to bright shades. With the geometric shapes, the games has as much variety as you give it. And if you allow yourself to become immersed, the game will provide the rest.

Sound:

The sound in this game was surprisingly not what I was expecting. I was expecting simple, "Pyew Pyew ZZST" sounds for the weapons, and the classic "Rocket Burn" sound for the engines. No, Certain weapons all have a different sound. And the further you are from the source of the sound, the softer the sound will be. Zoom out fully, and the sound is just a whisper. Zoom in fully and you get the full range of sound. Do not expect huge Orchestral themes with this game. It keeps it simple, and does a great job at it.

Story:

Story? Simple. You own part of a Galaxy, and you conquer other sections. How, and when you do this. That is for you to decide. Not much else to it. Simply put. Your story is your ship. It is what you make it to be.

Controls:

Control are simple. Mouse cursor to aim. Or, if you like, Hit the "R" button to change controls. Personally, I go for the Mouse to aim, and W, S forward and back, A and D to turn. Gives it a more of an old school vibe to it. Makes the game feel more fluid on my end.

Gameplay:

Gameplay. Oh so simple. Anything shoots you, shoot back. You died? Well then. Try out a new build. In my honest experience, going for pure SP from the very start, helps. Once you reach Max (8000) then you can have some fun. I went for a build similar to current day Naval ships. And let me tell you. If you build to win, using your own creative imagination. You will have some deadly ships. And When you get to build huge huge ships, you become a force even to other players ships. And, like many others have said, there is no multiplayer in this game. Instead, you get to have other players ships, ported to your game. And, depending on how good they are. You get to feel their wrath.

Now, for my Impression of this game. I have to put it in a few simple words. A breathe of fresh air. Why? I encourage you to see how many good, space sandbox games are out there, that have a flow to it. Yet do not take endless hours to fully experience what this game has to offer. Yet the deeper you dive into this game, the more layers of strategy it takes to overcome. Though that is not to say the game will endlessly become more challenging. It is, as I have said, as you imagine it to be. And that, folks, is where this game has it's core. Some, like me, will find this game charming. And others, will of course, be left feeling a bit empty inside. That is entirely okay. So in the end, You can give this a try, and find out for yourself.\t\t\t\t\t

Video review:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5dDObkYTzJE&list=UURPe3hzgc7y8NTczf8AffDg

What is it?

Reassembly is a ship building game. Your goal is to design the most effective warship and conquer the sector. Of course, you're not alone here. There are factions that control territory in the sector. You belong to one faction and most others are hostile, although there are a couple neutral factions.

Gamplay consists of designing your vessel using preset parts. In the beginning of the game, you'll most likely spend your time gathering rescources that are generated by the local fauna and bringing it back to your factions stations. You will exchange the rescources for credits that allow you to purchase new ship parts and also alow you to build a bigger ship. Later, you'll likely build a warship capable of using the resources you collect to build your own fleet and wreak havoc on the opposing factions.

Your faction will grow and expand out into the sector and become stronger and you design ships to be put into your fleet. Once your done with one sector, you can enter a wormhole and enter a whole new sector.

Pros:

-The ship building system is simple and easy to use. Putting together a ship is as simple as a drag and drop experience.

-The graphics look amazing. Despite being simple polygon graphics, they really are captivating. The light show that a large battle creates is quite a sight to see.

-You have choice about what you will do. You can choose to be anything form a simple harvester to a fearsome dreadnaught.

-Competition within your own faction. As you bring back rescources, your station builds ships. These ships then go out in search of rescources. This will force you farther and farther from the safety of your station. I find that interesting.

Cons:

-Despite what the devs may say, there kind of is one way to build a good ship. The armor plating pieces do not count towards the size of your ship. The best way to build a good battleship is to slap on as much armor as you can.

-There is no interaction with the factions. There are no quests, no diplomacy, and no alliances. The only path to victory is conquest.

-Grinding. In order to build a large ship, you will have to harvest a lot of rescources. If you don't build a big ship, you will always dies. Put two and two together.

Really, this game boils down to trying to build the most powerful ship. That's enjoyable, and the exploration (at least before you've seen everything there is to see) can be quite interesting. I doubt that I'll play this game far into the future, or that (in this current state) it will remain a big thing for long. However, it is still a good game and I've definitely enjoyed playing it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have loved this game since I got it.

The game is a simple and fun top-down space shooter with the ability to design and fly your own space ships, and even design an entire fleet with ships ranging from small shuttles and fighters to large destroyers and frigates.

There are several different races (some of which you can't play) that unlock while playing (and destroying stations from a race you haven't unlocked before), each with their own technological pro's and con's, but they are all (reasonably) evenly matched, and where one race falls short in say shield technology, it makes up for armor or speed.

The universe is generated in sectors each controlled by the majority of whatever ships are in that sector, so it can at times be hard to claim a sector completely when there is just 1 little core still floating about. The universe is limited in size, but thats alright since flying over one edge of the map puts you at the opposite side without a teleport or cheap backend trick. Watch out for pirate fleets though; These are, from what I gather, uploaded by other players and are randomly selected, which means that in one universe you may find ships you won't encounter in the next. This uploading is done through anomalies called wormholes that allow you to upload your fleet (designs) or fly to another universe. The latter will generate a new universe for you while you keep your resources, credits and ships.

The only downside I experienced so far is not being able to destroy core blocks from certain stations. Usually this happens after a few hours of playing and may be memory related, but other than that it plays like a dream. The thing about player created content worries me a bit though, but we'll see how well balanced the game is over time.

A definate recommendation and big thumbs up for this gem.\t\t\t\t\t

Reassembly is an extremely fun, addictive game.
It has a pretty unique concept that was executed really well.
If you like spaceships, expansion, customization, dogfights, big ass (faction) battles, factions, or just nice things in general, you should get this game.

Pro's
+ Addictive
+ Infinite customization
+ Expanding is fun
+ Massive battles
+ Several unlockable factions
+ Asynchrous multiplayer
+ Has more depth than you think
+ You can make your faction exactly how you want it to be


Cons
- Once your faction is unstoppable, there's pretty much nothing left to do
- Other players sometime make huge spamming death machine ships which they then release into the wild, meaning they can pop up in your galaxy too. Might take over your galaxy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeautiful and deep.

The designer did a good job making the exact configuration of your ship very mechanically relevant. You can spend hours thinking about where to place your thrusters, armor, turrets, ect.

The progression isnt bad either- there are 12 factions, each with many parts to unlock. Its hard to describe how different it feels going from a faction that lets you make things out of squares to a faction that forces you to use rhombuses instead.

Fighting player made fleets is also pretty awesome.

The minigame of "Can you make a ship for X points that beast everyone elses ship that they spent X points on" is actually really interesting and deep as well. Watching the AI pilot your ships is quite interesting (though its a shame there isnt a more formalized way of pitting your designs against specific friends' ships).

This game is basically a better, more fleshed out version of the spore cell stage.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't write many reviews, but when I do I like to write them for games I like. This is one of those special games that I will take the time out of my day to write a few words about.

First: off I love how there are three different control schemes for the game, you don't realize how important this is untill you try all three. It is exceedingly useful because not all ships will work with one control set up.

Second: it's an interesting idea to have the universe populated with other players ships. It makes the game fresh. There are some problems with this system, for instance if you make a really weak ship and it is put into someone else's game it's pointless, but for the most part it's good.

Third: the building system, now this system has some major problems, mainly because I have not used it enough to completely understand the logic behind it. I will try to explain the good things it does and the problems it has. It functions like the builder in banjo and kazooie nuts and bolts the difference is it's two dimensional and you have a keyboard and mouse so it isn't frustrating to manipulate It for the most part its balanced, I think it would help if the basic blocks had a cost, for instance I made a cube with the faction you start with and though it's slow it is almost unbeatable because it has so much armor. This could be fixed if the basic parts had a low cost, maybe raise the cap to 10k and make the prices on the other parts higher so it evens out. The biggest problem with the building system is the blocks have to be exactly aligned for them to snap together, basically imagine legos but the pegs are the sizes of needles and you are making giant ships. This ends up with blocks that are a few pixels off of being perfect, this bugs me to death, it's probably the biggest problem with this game.

Fourth: The plant faction is too op, it is too easy to make a swarm of ships and to overrun any opponent you may have.

Fifth: Another amazing thing this game does well is the different factions have different base blocks, for instance there is a faction that is focused on triangles, this forces you to build intelligently.

There are many more things in this game that are worth a mention but if you made it to this point I will sum up my opinion on this game with, a resounding recommendation. All though it has it's problems the game as a whole is fun and for 15$ there is almost no reason not to buy it, I have played almost thirty hours of it at the point of writing this reviews, thats fifty cents and hour I have bought triple a games that are less enjoyable than this( looking at you tomb raider, Shadow of mordor, farcry 4, and many others).
