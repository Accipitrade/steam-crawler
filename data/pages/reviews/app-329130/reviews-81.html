\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't often find a game that I can't put down - but this is one. Now officially my favorite chill-out game. It's a strategy game set in a rich living world. Design your ship from scratch using basic building blocks. Pilot it round a large area of space using Newtonian physics - i.e. you carry on moving in the same direction once you have built up speed, without further thrust, and regardless of the direction you are pointing. The game uses a wrap-around map. Harvest resources, trade them to improve your spacecraft. Fight enemies and try to take space sectors from other factions. Recruit allies and form a fleet. Put a factory on your ship and spawn new ships. Travel to other maps using "wormholes". Fight other players' uploaded fleets (not true multiplayer but a contest of designs). You can also use the wormholes to upload your own fleet designs. Unlock new factions to play.

You could make it a fast and furious battle or a leisurely affair - up to you. I'm 9 hours in and feel I'm only just beginning to scratch the surface. A real indie masterpiece and a credit to the developers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I love the idea of this game, and hope it gets multiplayer in the future, but I have a few problems with it:
1. Ships can be made insanely fast, too fast for most weapons to even have a chance of hitting.
2. All turrets have the ability to turn 360 degrees, making ship design as simple as slapping as many guns into an armored ball.
3. Engines can be inside your ship, meaning there is no way to damage them without first destroying the components around them.
I love how the building works, but I feel like there's still a lot of balancing that needs to be done, since the meta just dictates blobbing up as many guns as you can fit with either:
a. of armor (which is entirely free most of the time, so you could make a massive armored box at the very beginning)
b. of engines, making you pretty much impossible to hit with anything but lasers (although the speed is best put into use by the AI controlled ships, since they can instantly dodge out of the way of almost any attack)

I love this game, but I hope it gets the things I mentioned.

Edit: I just also need to add that the only part of your ship that the AI shoots is your core, so it is optimal to leave extruding parts of your ship unarmored...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a simple concept expanded upon and made extremely entertaining (read: addicting). All you do is build a ship out of 2D shapes, shoot other ships, and try not to get blown up, all while making your blocky ship bigger and better. And yet, the game manages to do for me what even big-production space titles like Elite: Dangerous couldn't: absorb me in an expansive, living universe that I got hours of fun out of. It's easy to jump into, there's unlimited creative potential in making your ships, the destruction effects are satisfying, and you never run out of things to shoot, because the only thing there really is to do is expand your territory by destroying enemy ships, of which there are in (sometimes frustrating) abundance.
The "passive mulitplayer" mechanic is enjoyable, because while you can't very well taunt over the wreckage of destroyed player ships hoping to give your enemy some quality killcam footage, you can still feel smug knowing you just trashed the MVP of someone else's universe without expecting them to come back for revenge.
The game world in its geometric minimalism is surprisingly atmospheric, almost organic, and this feeling is certainly helped by the Brian Eno-esque music. There's no real plot that ties the 6 factions together as far as I know, but I feel like any kind of story would weigh down the smooth simplicity of the gameplay.
All in all, Reassembly is probably the most fun you can have in space without doing cargo runs.

(For bonus funtimes: Be the Sentinel. Get the Armor Spikes. Make buzzsaws. Be unstoppable.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter some ridiculous number of hours assembling fleets of warships from fighter to super-death-machine 2X, I decided that this is probably the best top-down shooter I've ever played. Whether your death-machine is incredibly manueverable and flies circles while spraying white-hot death, or a slow-moving tank that absorbs hundreds of shots on its mile-thick armor plating before its quintuple banks of burst lasers burn through the enemy to the core, you'll have fun dismantling it, making it better, over and over again, unlocking new parts, more points to use on them, as your drone-launching, resource-harvesting, station/fleet-building monstrosity burns its way through the universe.

After I maxed out the first race, of course I did the same with every one, having fun the whole time, testing my ships against the normal ones as well as the user-designed ones until I had achieved the proper mix of lethality and style.

You build an incredibly customizable ship, an equally customizable fleet, in fact you can get a good list of ships and stations done and switch universes so that only your stuff is out there; or go back and order all the existing stations to change to your designs, telling stations to become warships and vice versa.

In summary; they could make it better. Could add more races? Maybe some more meaningful level of large-scale control? Yeah, this one's only good for a 9/10. 4.5/5. 97/100.\t\t\t\t\t
