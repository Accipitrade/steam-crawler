\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor someone the likes of me, the concept of building your own shenanigan and pitting it up against other contraptions has always been fascinating to me. Seeing who comes out on top, your ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ box, or the other guy's weaponized dildo with missile spam sprouting from the tip. To your surmise, the dildo won.

Reassembly conforms to a style of a top-down spaceship shooter. Controlling your own manufactured ship, your "goal" (though indirectly implied) is to dominate every sector on the map by removing the inhabitants that subside in said sectors. Attempting to dominate every sector can be met with an implication of meeting that inevitable doom ship that you don't have a chance against.

//-Factions
There are 17 total factions in the game, but only 7, by default, are playable. Each faction is unique in their own ways, particularly design, weapon versatility, and how well they fend for themselves. Most notable feature they carry is what they specialize in, a faction might hold the title of being the most balanced faction, while others hold the title of owning the most powerful weapon arsenal, but at the cost of lower defenses. It all comes down to the preference of the player.

//-Agents
Agents hold a synonymous definition of player-made ships. They occur when a player travels through a wormhole, in which they have the decision to stay or leave to a newly birthed location. Upon leaving their current world, their fleet (along with their ship designs) are uploaded and randomly placed through other player's worlds. In the form of a large fleet, Agents are forces to be reckoned with as their design holds true to a lethal mind of a human player: ship design ingenuity. Okay, the last part is an exaggeration, but they typically have better designed ships than NPC's

//-Progression
There's not really much to hold when on the topic of progression for this game. It's made simply through three easy mechanics.
1) Killing enemies to gain resources
2) Return resources to a ship/station with a factory installed, so you can trade resources for currency.
3) Purchase ship parts to your liking.
It's simple, but obliterating ships for resources is fun anyway.

//-Steam Workshop Support
Unfortunately the game doesn't hold a tremendous amount of following to really accommodate for the Steam Workshop. The game can be modded to a certain extent by bringing in new factions, a modified world (either bigger or more content placed), graphical enhancers, etc.
Though initially excited for the idea of bringing in new factions built by players, it can only go so far. If you're playing with a custom faction, attempting to enter a wormhole will prompt you with an error message exclaiming that you don't have a fleet, or that your fleet couldn't be uploaded to the server because your custom faction isn't officially in the game. It is where I realized that I've met the limitations of the Steam Workshop Support and with a frown expression, I leave it to that (from the time of this review, the game has been presumably left alone and will no longer receive developer updates [Last update given was November 10, 2016]).

But hey, what's not to love about this game. It's like revisiting your old childhood memories when you played with your Legos. It's fun putting your ideas into reality (in the form of a videogame). Stepping on a Lego is just like meeting that gigantic enemy ship, add in a swarm of missiles, a few lasers, and a deathray. It's all fun in the end, right?

Go build some ♥♥♥♥, or ship.\t\t\t\t\t

If you're reading this you most likely know it's about building spaceships. Unlocking new parts by exploring and harvesting the universe. Upgrading your old ship or building a new one from scratch. Recruiting allies to join your fleet. Fighting huge battles against alien races. Ships will regenerate as long as their command module is not destroyed. There are many different factions to play (you'll have to unlock them though). The game runs smoothly (only one crash so far), the building is simple but with enough depth to be interesting. Plus: it's modable! Again, lots of fun.

However, at this point I'm getting increasingly frustrated. Every single game I started ended after a couple of hours (at the most) because of what I believe to be a flaw in the game's design. At one point in every single game my ship bumped into a floating piece of spacestation debris without me noticing immediately that the (piece of) spacestation was in the proces of regenerating. And within seconds my ship was "overgrown" with spacestation, unable to manouvre or shake and wiggle itself loose. At that point I usually (rage)quit the game just to start again the next day because the gameplay is still fun. But add to that a bug (?) that is semi-gamebreaking: After the first time it seems I cannot use wormholes anymore (these take you to new maps). All I get is an error notification. This could have something to do with the mods I'm currently using (didn't test that yet) but anyhow it's getting increasingly annoying.

So yeah, great game if you like designing and building spaceships to fight huge battles with. But I would advise to take a look at yourself first: do you think your frustration-tolerance is up to the standard required? If so, by all means, buy this game, 'cause in the end it's lots of fun.\t\t\t\t\t
