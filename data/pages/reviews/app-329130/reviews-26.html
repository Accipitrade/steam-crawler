\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is a procedurally generated twin-stick "massively single-player" shooter with a lot of interesting features. Don't be fooled by the apparent visual simplicity of this game. Years after my purchase I still find myself coming back to this game occasionally, largely because of its excellent music design and smooth gameplay loop.

In this rogue-lite asteroid-like sandbox shooter, you start off with a pretty complete-looking ship that you can fly around and encounter different alien races that have their own divergent ship design philosophies. As you merrily blow them up, you'll accumulate upgrade points that let you tack on some extra thrusters and laser emitters. Very early on in the game, you'll realize you can no longer keep tacking on weapons blisters and need to delete your ship and build it back up from scratch. This is where the fun happens.

The ships you design through your upgrade points can be saved to the cloud and spawned as enemies in other peoples' games, which is how it gets its "massively single-player" status. It's an interesting alternative to PvP, uploading your best ship designs to appear as enemies for other players. Assembling new spaceships is really fun and engaging and I was able to play and build new ships easily with my standard gamepad.

The game includes a wide variety of unlockable alien races to play as which is a huge part of this game's replayability. They did a really good job at making asymmetrical playstyles - aliens that have totally different approaches to combat and resource farming that you can fight against and eventually play as.

There's this magical combination of textureless graphics with smooth atmospheric background music and arcade style gameplay layered over building/resource management and combat that makes Reassembly a unique gaming experience that is worth checking out. Don't be turned off by the dated visuals, the developers have really created a novel gaming experience here that is worth your attention.\t\t\t\t\t
