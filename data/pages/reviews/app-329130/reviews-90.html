\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun twin stick spaceship shooter with an organic feel to it similar to Pixeljunks Flow. You collect food called R(esource) and trade it for C(redits) to unlock new ship parts and raise your point allowance to stick more guns and thrusters to your ships body.

Damage taken is reflected on your ships, parts are removed and will regrow fairly quickly. Half your thrusters missing on one side of your ship? Have fun trying to escape without smacking right into the next obstacle. If you command block is destroyed you respawn at the nearest factory station or ship, while losing all R. No big deal, chances are you randomly crash you ship into the next asteroid at least a couple of times.

You expand territory for you faction by activating space stations and feeding them resources. Once you unlock the factory block you can start building your own fleet with your own designs, and even pop out your own stations. Massively fun for a bit but it's really a one trick pony.

The good
  • Controls are good, even though you can build clunky ships.
  • Ship Designs are fun and designing your own fleets is quite neat.
  • Lots of ship designs from user generated content, also workshop support now.
  • The Organic world with it feeding and growing cycle is sweet, even if only rudimentary.
  • Offer at least 6-7 hours of good fun. More if you enjoy the editor, optimizing fleets and trying out the different races. I doubt we see much more support from the dev that adds more replyability, but you never know.


    The bad
  • Controller support isn't complete, feels like 80% is there and the rest just never got done.
  • Tutorial is a joke, it just checks for input and a couple of conditions, I accidently skipped almost half of it in the first 3 seconds. At the very list it needs to disable other inputs and walk you though it step by step.
  • Simple block ship designs tend to do better than fancy. Wrong incentive.
  • Editor has a snap function for connecting parts, nice but it only works 50% of the time making it almost useless.
  • Some ship design are rather clunky and the AI does a good job of smashing into every solid body, Funny the first two times but it does get old really fast after that.
  • Low replayability, the simulation aspect of the world isn't really complex enough to allow for new situations to arise.


    Looking forward to a sequel, I would love to see the core concepts of the game flashed out and some support for drop in multiplayer.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's like good old "Asteroids", but with lego SPACESHIPS you've designed yourself. Except there are half a dozen different building sets, each with a completely different meta. Long vs short range guns, one fraction can grow it's own space plants for resources, another has no shields and hulls you must build from Penrose tiles!

Progress up from piloting nifty little fighters, through hardy brawlers, up to god-like monster ships with 100 strong halos of LASER drones, chewing up enemies to birth a fleet of self-(re)assembling ships out your rear end, all of your own design! Deep emergent game play that's immensely gratifying and dusted with sci-fi references to movies, TV and space opera novels. The most perfectly suited geekery I've ever experienced. :o)

The 'tournament' feature is basically Gratuitous Space Battles, with easier controls, where you can pit anyone's designs against each other in a space arena. The over-all experience of the main game is basically what Spore should have been; expanding out smoothly from the tidal pool stages. One of the few things that sometimes irked me is when friendly ships zip in and nab your loot from under your nose. I would have been tempted to make this game entirely RTS oriented, with truly conserved resources (shared within your faction). But the design is definitely spot-on as it is: putting you in the action and keeping the simulation scale (almost) within the capabilities of (my) PC hardware. It's interface is minimalisticly simple and pieces snap together in an impressively accurate anticipation of what you intend. It all lubricates that design, test, refine loop, preserving a perfect mental 'flow' state until all hours of the morning...\t\t\t\t\t
