\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA beautiful gem of a little game. I used to have ideas after playing flash games in my youth that are similar in design to this. What if this were just bigger and better? Like a dream this game answered that question. It would be awesome and it turned out according to my playtime this is something I needed. Its a simple, laid-back experience that manages to be extremely satisfying and addicting. The game is exactly as advertised. So, if you didn't like what you read then you might as well move on, but if you needed some convincing or reassurance I will help.

+ Simplicity. The premises are simple and brilliantly executed. You start as a small ship in your faction. You need resources known simply as R. So, you get them by either picking some up from farms or destroying anything that isn't on your team. Your ship is built out of blocks that are interconnected after returning your R to a station you can redesign your ship using cash. During combat pieces that are disconnected will fall away, but don't worry intact pieces can be tractored back in and reconnected. Destroyed pieces regenerate. Bring R to your fellow stations and buy upgrades. Rinse and repeat till you conquer the whole map and become stronger and stronger. You should note that the enemy is doing the same thing.
+ Reassembly. As I explained previously ships regenerate missing pieces and can tractor in unused intact pieces that are lying around in the depths of space. After redesigning your ship will disassemble and regrow or recall those pieces forming the new ship. Dying allies can save your life as their intact pieces rapidly repair you. There is something elegant and immersive about these mechanics that makes being in this universe that much more enjoyable.
+ Designer. All ships are based on the core. If the core takes enough damage, you die dropping what R you were holding. Thus, you build around the idea that everything can be reassembled as long as the core survives. The interconnectivity of the pieces actually presents a lot of variety and strategies in the techniques you will use in your designs. Placements need to be actually considered because even though the game is 2D the game simulates mass accurately and exploding pieces can trigger dangerous chain reactions. Its pretty detailed and leaves plenty of room to show off some style as well.
+ Combat/Controls. A swappable set of control schemes are available for mouse aim. The main one is mouse aim rotation and wasd moves you relative to your mouse. Second is mouse rotation, but wasd is based on absolute screen directions. Third is for heavy ships that you want to aim freely with because rotation is bound to qe and wasd is relative to the ship. Combat is generally pretty simple left mouse controls guns, right mouse controls missiles and drones (can be customized). Shoot to destroy the targets core. It is incredibly satisfying chewing through enemy ships and even fighting with stronger ones that test your designs capabilities or fleeing from those who are stronger. The whole combat loop is just perfectly smooth.
+ Factions. Factions offer unique strategies in both building and combat. Some are specialized to certain things making things a little more interesting for other play throughs. Though not all are equal. Some factions are definitively weaker, but there is challenge and excitement in playing them as well.
+ Fleets. Once you get into a game enough to have some cash. You can recruit or even spawn fleet members that will follow you everywhere. This is simply implemented, but many factions can get powerful synergies from combining different designs or you can just use them as fodder in your conquest.
+ Agents. Around your little galaxy there are wormholes. You can interact with them and send your current fleet into either a new galaxy or upload your fleet to other players galaxies. Yes, periodically enemy player designed fleets will come out of wormholes and attack everyone they come across. These ships/fleets can often be more difficult to deal with. A nice fun way of introducing other players stuff to a single player game.
+ Mods. Mods are directly integrated as an actual part of the game. Starting in the form of new custom factions to play. New ideas are also implemented. The all-important shader mod has to get a mention because it makes everything gorgeous ("Splinters Graphics Overhaul"). In the end we get a fantastic custom experience. Even enabling brutally difficult challenging factions if you want.
+ Performance. Will run on any device smoothly except when you come across the dreaded plant faction. Then all of our computers are futile. There is a hard engine limit that is reached at extreme amounts of projectiles/missiles/ships. Better computers will make it higher, but ultimately nothing can be done about it.

- Single player. The game doesn't need it, but it would have been great to have some kind of real multiplayer.

Its always nice to have a little game like to rely on when you want to just have simple fun. Being usable on all systems is nice bonus. For me it functions as a Zen game. Its engaging enough to keep your attention, but its never too much to cause any stress. I love it and maybe you will too.\t\t\t\t\t
