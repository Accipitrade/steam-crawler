\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly feels half-finished, and in the long run it's really disappointing. Yes, designing the ships is fun, and testing them is fun, and the big battles are fun. However, the fun runs out quickly and there are UI issues which pretty much kill any real possibility of sinking a significant amount of time into the game due to the repetition and frustration involved.

The User Interface has a very "Early Access" feel to it. At one point after purchasing I came back to the Steam store page expecting to see an Early Access tag that I had overlooked.

At first, UI clunkiness seemed like a small problem. I liked the game. I was having fun gathering resources, turning them in, and using the cash flow to buy new techs and to make my ships bigger. Eventually I unlocked the factory, and my ships were large enough to use it. At this phase of the game you think "Cool, now I can make fleets of ships and kick even more ass!"

And you can, but putting a fleet together and keeping it up and running through numerous battles is just such a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ chore. It feels like work.

It would be so much better if you could configure the way your factory makes ships. Here's how it works: you move up to 4 ship designs into boxes at the top of the fleet screen. These designs will be built by your ship's factory when you press 'F' in-game.

The issue is that pressing 'F' builds the first ship in the queue, then the second, and so on, repeating in order and there's no way to change this. For example, you can't set up four keybindings for the 4 designs.

Occasionally you can win a few big battles without changing your production queue, but more often than not you find yourself going back to the fleet screen over and over again to rearrange your build order. This is my primary complaint about the game. I might have reccommended this game and played it for months if I weren't forced to return to the awful fleet screen hundreds upon hundreds of times, simply because the developer only gave us one terrible way to build support ships.

This problem is only amplified if you decide you want to design and build some space stations. Building stations seems to be the only way to hold new areas you've moved into. If you move to an area of the map and clear out all the bad guys it will change to your faction's color, but the only way it will stay your color when you leave is if there is a station left behind which can spawn AI ships to patrol the area. So, basically, building stations is important if you want to conquer the map.

The problem is, there are good and bad places to create a station. The 4-ship repeating production queue gives you no easy way to be careful about your space station placement. If you want to be strategic, you have no choice but to go to the stinking, godforsaken fleet screen and drop a space station design into your production queue each time you find a good site where you want to lay down a new station. You'll have to make sure you drop it in the right place in the queue or it won't be next. After you've built the station and set up ITS production queue, you will have to return to your OWN production queue, delete the station, and replace it with another design.

In an area with a ton of drones? Fleet screen. Found enemy agents with large ships? Fleet screen. Is one particular type of your support ships taking heavy losses? Fleet screen. Hey, this is a great spot for a station. Fleet Screen.

So, the UI is bad. I should probably change gears: There's simply not much to do in this game once you've optimized all of your designs and you've assembled a nice fleet of ships. Once you can build full-sized ships, you will steamroll anything and everything with no surprises and no challenges to keep your interest. I was intrigued by all the talk of a huge, dynamic galaxy to conquer but the reality is that the endgame is just one-sided, imbalanced action and little else.

The map is terrible. It's hideous. It's a super-chunky grid with different colors to represent factions. The result is very ugly, and gets more and more atrocious the more you explore. You can't zoom or scroll the map, and you can't change or hide the overlays. The minimap in-game is just as bad. It's hard to see useful information, and you can make it either small or smaller.

There are other issues as well. The AI is not that great. This is really an issue when you spawn 10 support ships and pray to god that they don't cause a pileup and all get left behind in an asteroid field. This will happen even with small ships that are the most maneuverable craft in the game. Yes, your fleet that you saved up resources to build can easily get left behind on a high speed trip because they are truly terrible at avoiding stationary objects.

Also, ships get stuck together all the time while they are "rebuilding" themselves after taking damage, or right after they spawn from your factory on your main ship. It's laughable, as the two ships are merged and the AI ship is using all of its thrusters trying to go somewhere making it impossible to go where you are trying to go.

So all-in-all this feels like a half-finished Early Access title with potential. Unfortunately it's a finished game.

Summary: Game feels half-completed. Good ship designing, good action, VERY BAD interface, nothing rewarding to do after unlocking all the parts and build points.\t\t\t\t\t
