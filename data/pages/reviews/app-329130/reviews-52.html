\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a good game.

You build spaceships out of 2D geometric shapes. Balance energy generation, thruster power and placement, total hull strength and shape, and weaponry. You have a limited total point limit (P) to work with. Increase P with C. Earn C by selling R to friendly stations, or by exploring. Get R from harvesting plants or killing enemy ships and stations.

The game plays out much more like a petri dish of evolving life in an ecosystem, except you're the one doing most of the evolving. If I had to suggest two things, it would be that there be more permanent landmarks of sorts (planets, or notable points worth fighting over) and that factions would expand, grow, and shrink on their own, even when the player isn't present. A native option to disable Agents would be nice too, although there's a mod for that.

There's also a lot of neat custom faction mods, featuring bizarre block palettes and more powerful enemy fleets to face off against. It's at least worth the asking price, really.

7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good game, and though it looks pretty, it is not as deep as you'd expect.

Pros:
It looks pretty. There are many factions to play as. Each faction has their own modules for the well-built ship designer. There are thousands of new blocks, and hundreds of new factions, you can play as (and against) by installing workshop mods. You can develop a fleet, including ships that build other ships which then build other ships. There is a decent variety of weapons (beams, missiles, projectiles, etc). Early-game exploration is very easy to get into, and the tutorial while not perfect is very, very good. Keybind options also allow you to set any weapon to 'autofire' (fires automatically against all enemy ships) or 'point defense' (as autofire, but also targets missiles). So, the controls are a lot less fiddly than you'd expect, given the customization options. Multiple control schemes are supported, too, so whether you want to use the mouse or not, the game has you covered.

Cons:
There is not as much depth as you'd think there would be. Many of the ship modules are just resized versions of each other. Technically each faction has its own series of reactor modules, but aside from some different numbers, they're functionally the same. At a certain point you will have a ship that has enough shields and focused DPS to take on any other ship in the game, at which point... there is suddenly very little left to do. Workshop mods can add more content, yes, but they're not remotely balanced, and as a result while there may be more content there probably won't be a lot more challenge. The AI is prone to using your ship designs, which can lead to out-of-control ship self-multiciplication if you're creating a ship entirely devoted to factory production rather than combat. Speaking of which, it is trivial to bypass the entire game's progression system by simply adding more and more resource-producing modules to your ship (which create resources out of thin air) and sitting next to a station so they're all instantly traded for currency, which you then use to purchase permanent upgrades. Within 30 minutes of doing this, you can unlock everything available to your faction, without ever fighting any other ship. This is the sort of thing I mean when I say the game has some balance issues.

Overall:
This is a visually pretty, easy-to-pick-up, and surprisingly fun spaceship combat/design game. It isn't the deepest such game, and it certainly isn't the most strategic, but what you do get is accessible and fun. You won't play it for a very long time, but for the time you do play it, you'll probably enjoy it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally really good game for the price. Simple 2D space shooter with a few amazing twists.:
9/10 for the cost really. If you remember how dissapointing Spore was.. this makes up for it, hugely.

Pros:
- Like a better version of Spores first level.
- Like an endless version of S.P.A.Z.
- Craft your own ships and reassemble it to anything you can come up with.
- Make biological ships that look like cells (probably something what intelligent beings would build anyway.)
- I really like the customization involved, i'd like to see more weapon and ship colour choices though.
- Design deployable fleets. (Kinda like drones)
- Properly good weapons and 2D visuals.

Cons:
- Some of the player made 'Agent' fleets are insane, which require farmed resource to beat, I don't think you can gather them quick enough.
- Not much assistance from the player capturable bases. (It'd be nice to have a mini instant fleet, or some missions.)
- Biological / spherical ship designs with high shielding and one type of directed / aimed weapon are likely to win overall.
- More explanation about how the mass and thrust ratio aters ship handling would be nice.
- Ideally I want to know how to counteract mass heavy gun arrarys to still be fast in space.
- Obviously I could add more thrusters, but where, and why in that location?
- Colours are typically 2D(retro), but does your head in a bit, too neon.
- It's a christmas day game. Replayability fades fast.

=====

I've only played the standard game. The modding community could be brilliant on this. Yeah PvP can off.\t\t\t\t\t
