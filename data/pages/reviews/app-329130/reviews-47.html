\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- Very fun to learn the ship/resources and physics of acceleration.
- Fun rolling around with your fleet completing an entire map.(Except for the random dissapearing act they like to pull)
- Nice simple artwork design.
- Several playable races with different pros/cons and ship pieces.

Cons:
- It is definitely a indie title, very unpolished in some area.
- Controls are very wonky, unresponsive at certain times.
- Unbalanced/broken units uploaded by other players to ruin a map clear. (See summary)
- Your fleet will randomly dissapear when traveling emptier parts of the map.

Summary:
I really wanted to like this game, but the fact that it's a indie titles shines through. My main annoyance is that it wants you to explore but if you try to explore at anything but a snails pace you end up losing more than half your fleet, making you feel like a necromancer in diablo 2 jumping between teleports, randomly losing your minions. Also, people like to upload broken/unkillable agents that make it so that you can't kill them, and they teleport directly to your units. (I ran into a invincible saw blade agent that would teleport into the middle of my units when i left its area.) So it makes the game unenjoyable when trying to clear a map. It's a good weekend game if you can get it for 5$ and look past the above annoyances. I do look forward to seeing a sequel/future updates.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty good game with an excellent soundtrack that sets a mood. You can design your own ships but the shapes are mostly irrelevant. Its not like you need to develop lift or be concerned with drag. Your main problem is pushing through the many obstacles and tendrils that might catch on your ship. The result is that you'd ideally want a smooth exterior with a point on the front. The cooler looking ships often get caught on lots of things and aren't as practical as a box with a point.

Ships also get caught on things and won't rotate for some reason. That gets annoying. Thrusters don't heat up and they don't have exhaust so you can mount thrusters internally and point them at each other. There are lots of pretty, fascinating areas. They don't have any explanation. Why did this planet develop plants that shoot at passing enemy ships? I don't know. It doesn't make any sense but it sure looks cool.\t\t\t\t\t
