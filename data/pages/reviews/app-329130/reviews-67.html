\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game's selling point is you can build yhour own spaceships from an array of building blocks, such as a shield block, cannon block, command block, thrusters block, hull blocks and so on. It then uses a physics engine to move it about, so you need to balance the vessel well and provide enough sufficiently powered trusters in all directions to be able to move and turn.

Weapon firing can be set to autotarget, so in effect you just move slowly around while your cannons destroy anything nearby so you can pick up loot that is needed for upgrades and unlocking new building blocks. Enemies will fire back and destroy your ship block by block, but after a while the blocks auto-generate.

You can also build AI controlled ships, based on your own designs. Those ships can in turn build other ships, so you can dominate the galaxy quite soon. Then it is time to enter a black hole for a fresh universe, where you can start over from your current technology level.

A challenge with this game is that controlling your ship is cumbersome. Even if you design it well, it will still handle sluggish, and I found myself getting stuck in space debris a lot. So, if you approach the game as an action game, it will be very irritating. Approach it more like a strategy game, and it makes more sense though.

Another issue with the game, is it has quite limited scope. Once you unlock all upgrades, there is simply not much to do. You will have no use for the money you collect, and you just aimlessly float around to repeat what you did before over and over. Would be nice with some variation, and perhaps some quests. This makes the game a rather short experience.

Overall, it is a fun game with some fresh innovations.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of my first proper reviews for a game and i've chosen this because i've played it for a little while and noticed it hasn't had very many reviews. I'll briefly outline the pros and cons of this game:
Pros:
.virtually endless universes
.you can fight against other people's creations
.endless possibilities for ship creation: any shape, size, weaponry and thrusters can be put almost anywhere
.it has a great soundtrack but some people may find it repetitive
.multiple factions (groups of ships) with different weapons, ways of playing and fighting and different abilities
.it is extremely satisfying when you know that you've destroyed another player's fleet with your own creation that you built from scratch
.you can control fleets, wreak havoc on all the other ships and easily take over a whole universe if you put the time and effort in
.easy, addictive and enjoyable once you get the hang of the controls and the build section
.if you can't be bothered building a ship, you can import one and then add to it (on the build menu, go to import>Steam(wherever your steam folder is, go to that place first, mine is on my desktop so i would go import>desktop>steam)>steamapps>common>reassembly>extra_ships or ships depending of faction and then choose a ship)
Cons:
.it's $15
.difficult to get the hang of
.no real storyline but some people will like that
.it is time consuming to build a really good ship from scratch
.you will die quite a lot even if you are extremely careful

Overall i would say that it is a game worth getting if you are into exploring, sandbox, creating stuff, blowing other stuff up or destroying it, collecting, upgrading and controlling a fleet of 81 ships at once. Although it takes a bit to get used to (for me it was 30 mins), it is very enjoyable and worth getting when on sale. I was lucky enough to get it for $5 and it was worth every cent\t\t\t\t\t
