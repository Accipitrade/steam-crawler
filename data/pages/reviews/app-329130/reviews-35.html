\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9/10 - Excellent game that succeeds at what it advertises, but if you aren't really into a building-focused play style then you'll probably find yourself quickly bored.

Full Review Below

Reassembly is probably one of my favorite indie releases. The premise is simple: build your own ship, then squadron, then fleet. Then take the fight across the galaxy.

The meat of the game play is in building your own ships. If you're the kind of player who loves full creative freedom, and the simple loop of building something from scratch -> seeing how it does -> revising, then you will absolutely love this game. If you'd rather just figure out one thing and then go shoot things, you'll probably get bored relatively quickly.

That being said, the "shooting things" play style is very well supported in the early game, because you gather resources from destroyed enemies and can use them to unlock new bricks. This changes once you're out of things to unlock, as their is no longer the rewarding gameplay loop that comes with unlocking things.

On the presentation side of things, the graphics are gorgeous and the soundtrack is rich and moody, making it an easy game to get lost in while you experiment with different options and different builds. The first play through impression that this game leaves is magical. You'll be discovering new types of ships and civs pretty consistently for a while, many with their own tricks and challenges that you'll need to adapt to.

Eventually you'll discover everything the game has to offer, and you'll be left with optimizing your fleet, designing mother ships and stations, and attaining dominance. If you get bored of one civ, you can always play as one of the others. Each one has its own style and weapons, complete with different shipbuilding styles due to the differing tile sets.

When you get bored of what the base game has to offer, then there's an incredibly lively Workshop scene ready to add myriad new choices. I played for about 30 hours before even considering adding Workshop content.

To top it all off, the developer recently became active again and added a fascinating expansion that explores gravity wells, as well as tightening up the core game. Even if you don't like the new gravity "terrain" features, the expansion is worthwhile for the new soundtrack alone, imo.\t\t\t\t\t
