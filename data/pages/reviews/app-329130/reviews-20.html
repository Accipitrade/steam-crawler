\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Tinkering in a garage" is going to be a game collection I start soon.

Reassembly is a relatively simple game about taking basic shapes, putting guns on those shapes, and then playing spaceships. The control scheme will always be a point of contention as the game tries to mitigate your hilarious engine placement, consider a controller.

The game is generous with the amount of space you have for creativity, and often if you do a little finagling you can make most things fit. Anything from small fighters to gargantuan battle cruisers are craftable and the shape options mean you can make it look like almost anything you like. Check the community hub, your favorite spaceship is more likely to be there than not, and if it isn't, you can go ahead an fix that.

The game itself puts you in a space (the size of your choosing) and encourages you to build a fleet... or just be a huge lone battleship, your call... and go blow up other fleets, claiming space stations and purging the galaxy of all spaceships that are not your spaceships.

It's a little hard to hit some of the smaller faster ships, but that's why you have beam weapons and tracking missiles. There's a solution to your problem and there is no perfect ship.

Although some of the more Kite-y frigates are a little frustrating to deal with.

Not a perfect game, but it's real easy for me to be stuck in ship building mode for hours when I really should be asleep.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is a Simplistic 2D ship building and stellar expansion game, I mainly play for the creative freedom with a not too overly complicated building engine.

With your ship you'll need several things, Resources to make credits, to then buy Power, and parts to expand the sizes and versitility of your ships, To get these, you need a ship, that requires a core, and the core needs protected, so you armor it up, witch needs propulsion to move, and weapons to defend itself, that need power, but power cells are explosive, so you need to seperate it, and the other modules from your core, needing more propulsion, and Repeat, untill you've ended up building titanic battleships pushing the limits of the game engine, like i have many many times over.

This cycle of gameplay has had me obessed on making fun new designs, layouts, shapes, and functions of warships, from my classic and horrendiously slapped together Scalpels 1-5, and Lancers 1-5, to my current Heavy Battleship Delta X, further up even, Juggernauts, Sigma, and Voulge 1. This game has had me enthralled and has been more than worth the pricepoint, if you like these kind of building games, In my Opinion, This is a Must-Have.\t\t\t\t\t
