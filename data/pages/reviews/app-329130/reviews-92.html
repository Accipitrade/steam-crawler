\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an amazing game that I have spent (probably too many) hours on. It's based around the concept of being able to both design and pilot a ship to conquer the galaxy with (perhaps with the help of your homegrown fleet). There are tons of options/possibilities, and that's incredibly exciting.

TL;DR-Amazing game, would be the best game ever created if it weren't for the disgusting pile of bugs/glitches.

There are a lot of reasons I would recommend this game despite the issues I'll comment on farther down.
-When you get hit somewhere your health bar doesn't drop, you lose that part of your ship. Your thrusters got blown off and your only weapon faces forward? Sucks.
-Every faction truly plays differently. It's not a game of "What weapons am I gonna rambo with this time?" It's more like "These are my options, now what am I going to do with them?"
-The physics are great and really consider a lot of important factors such as center of mass and thruster location.
-The music is astounding. Gives a nice space-y feel and also compliments the actual gameplay quite well.
-Buildup from small to large ships doesn't feel grind-y and boring. It's just a different phase of the game, where instead of taking on giant death machines you go around activating stations and collecting resources from easier sources.
-Ships made by other players spawn in your world, so you get a lot of variety.
-The graphics are great. Frankly, I'm sick of repeated textures and copied/pasted environments. The basis on particles makes each game experience unique both visually and in regards to gameplay.


However, this game feels like it never left alpha/beta phase. There are insane numbers of holes in regards to instructions/development. A minor example (that still bugs me quite a bit) is part tool-tipping. Many have descriptions while even more have no real explanation whatsoever. The number of typos is also embarrassing.
The number of general options is also a joke. Why do I have to turn autozoom off every time I start the game? And why do I have to deal with that stupid tutorial every time I start a new file? There's no skip option! And don't even get me started on those quests that never go away (why should I have to minimize the HUD just to sweep the pathetic tutorial under the rug?)

The biggest issue has got to be the bugs, though. So. Many. Bugs.
-The game crashes frequently during ship construction or even just flying around. Or sometimes it will freeze for 2 or 3 minutes just 'cause.
-Sometimes I catch allied ships desperately trying to kill themselves on asteroids. I mean, just slamming into them and then keeping thrusters full speed ahead until they finally explode.
-Sometimes the above happens to me and gives me no control over it. Even if I'm holding "X" to stop the freaking ship.
-Lag lag lag. I know this is a particle-based game, but come on. Going through Spiky Plant territory means sending the game into slow motion (about 1/2 time, maybe slower).
-Weird glitches regarding exploring. Sometimes my territory goes from my ownership to "unexplored" or no-ownership unexpectedly. Even if I'm sitting in the middle of that territory with a few stations and a fleet of 50 ships. There are other similar glitches, such as arrows pointing to stations that don't exist.
-Lots of glitchiness surrounding drones/missiles. Particularly during ship construction. Sometimes the parts just do really weird things like spawn a missile inside of the ship, which leads to all kinds of glitchiness. I once had a ship I was working on explode into a few pieces IN THE EDITOR because of a stray missile.

There are a few non-bug-related issues with the game IMO, but those are actually pretty minor. Compared to the bug issues, I often forget they're even issues at all.
I could go on and on about all the glitches in this game. It's insane and needs to be addressed more immediately. I love this game for the ideas/concepts and gameplay. It's really fun and has a lot of awesome mechanics. The brilliance and creativity that went into making this work of art are just enough to overlook the pile of crap that is the coding/attention-to-detail.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI see the appeal of this game, but once a-freaking-gain, it's a title that shipped long before it was finished.

I LOVE these types of games. Build ship, gather resources, build bigger ship, smash enemies. That part of this game is fun. However, it's a broken PoS. Half the things don't work properly.

List of broken stuff:
-Drones fly off without reason into space and die.
-Missle targeting flys around in circles for no reason.
-Auto-target turrets don't auto-target 75% of the time. They sit there idling.
-Auto-fire doesn't auto-fire when enemies are nearby 75% of the time. They sit there idling (noticing a pattern here?).
-Ship re-building randomly doesn't work. Once, I was half-dead, went to allied territory and went to take a leak. Then I made a sandwich. Came back 5 minutes later and I still wasn't rebuilt. I had to exit and re-enter the game.
-Sheilds randomly malfunction . No, there was not an EMP, the sheilds simply didn't want to turn on so I had to go kill myself to get them to work again (even though dying in this game isn't much of a consequence, it's still annoying!).
-Thrusters on the side of the ship do jack-all. No matter what angle I put them at, strafing is a hundred times slower than going backward and forward. I put LITERALLY 10 times more thrusters on the sides of the ship than on the back of the ship, and no change. Barely any strafing at all. A thruster on the side of the ship does 1/100th the power of a thruster on the back/forward of the ship simply for the fact that it's on the side. I'm not sure if this is a glitch or if it's designed to be like that, either way, it's really damn annoying.
-Half the time, the ship I'm currently flying either wants to go forward, or turn. Never both at the same time, but one or the other, exclusively.
-Entering or exiting a menu resets the volume of the music to MAXIMUM. I play with my speakers pretty high up and turn most of the in-game volume down. This usually isn't a problem except in this game. Every time I go into the menu to change my ship, my fleet's ships, check the map, or pause the game, the volume for the music cranks all the way up to 100% for about a second, deafening me. This is more of a personal problem, but it's just another glitch that shows the game released too soon.
-Every third game, my base spawns in the middle of no where. No enemies within a hundred miles. Just dead, deep space between me and them. At the beginning of the game, you're especially weak, so you rely on your allies to help significantly, but it's impossible in these games, so the best advice I have is to just remake it. Another annoyance.
-Every few minutes more I play this game I find another glitch/bug/thing I don't like.

Positives:
-Music is awesome. It gets my blood pumping at times and at others it makes me sit back and relax to the joys of the graphics.
-Looks awesome. The graphics of this game are amazing. Beautifully done.
-Ship building is simple, yet deceptively complex. You can't simply stick a massive shield on one side of the ship and have it work properly. You need to counter-balance it with another shield, or something with similar mass. Center of gravity of the ship actually matters and you can easily build a ship by accident that can only fly in circles. I love that!
-Individual part destruction. When a part gets hit, you lose it, simple as that. If you lose your left aft engines, then guess what, you're flying in circles 'till it regenerates buddy.
-Individual part regeneration. After a short period (dependant on the part), it regenerates, free of cost, without having to go back to a station to repair and refuel.
-Unlock a part and you can use it as many times as you like.
-Free ship re-design. Re-design your ship over and over and over aaaaaand oooooooveeeeeeer again all you want, it's free. You're gonna need those remakes to create a good one. You don't even have to enter a spaceport and whatnot.

Once again, I'd love to love this game, I really would. There's a lot of great features and design aspects of the game I really love, but it's just not finished. It's just yet another game released before its time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat open-world shoot-em-up with a fantastic modular ship design system. I could (and probably already have) spend hours just designing and testing out different ships. I've played it for hours and there are a number of things I really like, but I'm going to focus on the areas that need improvement for this review:

My biggest complaint is the lack of end-game goals. Once you reach the upper limits of ship size and amass a big fleet, there's pretty much nothing that can stand in your way and the game becomes trivial. At that point, even if there wa s a win condition, there's no real reason to keep playing.

One of the other areas I'd like to see improvement in is the various factions. The first faction, Terran, has a lot of different technology to play with, but most other factions only have a few different weapon types, etc. This made playing the factions I'd unlocked less fun than I had hoped.

Finally, the ship AI leaves a bit to be desired, specifically their ability (or lack thereof) to navigate around obstacles. For a game set in space, the world is densely populated, and I've frequently had issues with AI ships getting stuck behind asteroids or accidentally getting latched onto other ships (the more irregularly shaped ones). Even trying to turn your own ship around in similar situations can be difficult with the default control scheme (mouse rotates ship).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR Reassembly is a game that won't let you go even after dozens of hours, keeping you busy building your own Death Star and then sending a fleet of those to ruin someone's day.

The Spacetacular Spaceventure.

Reassembly is all about space and freedom of it. There are seven factions available, each with their own unique playstyle. You can peacefully grow plants and extract energy from solar panels, or you can set up your own fleet of doom and plunder the galaxy. You can take things down with precise lasers, continuous beams, play a pew-pew-em-up with plasma cannons and if that doesn't help - spam them with missiles until they die. For those who desire exotic methods of space annihilation, make your own sawblade of death. Choices are limitless.

Right in from the childhood.

There are just three resources in this game: R(esource), C(urrency) and P(ower), each made to limit or unbind your possibilities. The cycle seems to be simple - R is traded for C, C is spent on P, and P is how many hardware you can put into your ship to earn yourself more R. But it's harder than it sounds. You don't just buy hulls, glue cannons on them and go gunning things down. You build your own ship piece by piece, and that's where LEGO nostalgia hits. Space, energy and Power efficiency is what makes it hard and beautiful. You can put tons of armor and guns on your ship, but thrusters just won't carry such mass. You can be tanky and quick, but with little to no offense available. You can put powerful guns and quickly execute hit-and-run tactics, but two shots will annihilate your little scrap piece of death.

Spiky plants.

You'll find out soon enough.

Dark spirit fleet has invaded.

So, you've made your bulky space cannon of extreme vengeful execution of space heretics and conquered the whole galaxy. And that's it? No, of course not. There are wormholes and agents.

Wormholes is what gives Reassembly infiniteness. When you approach a wormhole you're offered to either pass through to a newly generated galaxy, or to send your current fleet to other worlds in form of agents.

Agents are what slows you down. They are well-equipped fleets made by other players, and they are extremely hard to defeat. There are usually 6-8 fleets on the galaxy map, and they're moving towards you. Slowly. But when they reach you, they start plundering your stations and ships. Until they are defeated, they'll find you and destroy you. But the reward for killing them is worth the trouble.

Boooooring!

Well then, recently there had been added Steam Workshop support. Test out all the new player ships and factions, unleash their power on others or get wrecked by their overpoweredness. I'd say that the choice is yours, but you don't really have any choice. The galaxy's cruel.

Conclusion

The best singleplayer experience I had in a while. For how much this game already has, I give it 10/10. For me, this is the indie Game of the Year.\t\t\t\t\t
