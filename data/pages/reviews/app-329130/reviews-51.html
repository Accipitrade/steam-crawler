\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow can such a simple game be so damn fun. Do you like space ships? Do you enjoy customize your faction down from space stations to little cargo haulers (that collect your resource so you build a gargantuain fleet)? Do you like a game the says... kill anyone who isnt your color? Well?

Each faction at the purchase of this game is unique in how they function, some factions are easier then others because of this. But over comming the difficulty of combat using your on inqenuity to not only claim sectors of space but to duke it out with the big boy AI in this game feels good.

Its especially fun to import your own fleet... so you can fight your meta of that faction while playing the faction you orgnially designed your ships to fight. Fun ♥♥♥♥. (steam workshop is open for this game.. So i belive you can just dump some one uploaded fleet for some more interesting fights). It also super fun to fly thru former frontlines and see the floating debris. Trying to navigate a fragile and super fast fighter thru graveyards of dead ships and crashing into the hulls of dead capital ships you killed hours ago is really... cathartic.

There is no story. There are no quests. No diplomacy and politics. Kill Them all. And grow space plants on the hull of dead ships just to mock your foes. Would be amazing with Multiplayer.

I can highly recomend this game. Because its a fun game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA typical Reassembly game:

You start out weak and have to grow in power by fighting even weaker enemy ships;
You die, rush in to try to get your resources back, then die again until you maybe get that stuff back
You see all the cool parts you can put in your ship and dream of making the prettiest and most well though-out ship and fleet;
You die some more;
You make your ship more powerful, using that super cool strategy you made up;
You go out to test your super cool strategy against enemies and die;
You get more parts and continue upgrading your ship;
As you keep dieing and adapting your ships you realize there's no one strategy that can make sure you always win, so you make the ships to your heart's desire;
You attach a factory block to your ship and get crazy power hunger and try to create the biggest fleet ever;
It's harder than it looks and they all die;
You die too by the way;
OH GOD AN AGENT IS COMING FOR ME;
The agent (super strong player-made fleet) slowly but surely makes their way to you;
They wreck literally all the opposition in the way, leavng behind a path of doom;
You run from them while trying to get stronger, and eventually think you're strong enough;
You aren't;
You die to the agent many times as you insist you can beat them after 10 tries;
OH GOD THERE'S 3 MORE;
The agents eventually converge to a single point right next to one of your stations and fail to destroy each other;
You somehow beat one of them;
Die 413 times to the others;
Beat them as well;
Fragments of agents, your once glorious fleet and some killer plants are scattered all over the place;
A random ship comes out of nowhere and wrecks you;
There are no winners in war, only losers.

Also, there's a lot of grinding and fighting gigantic bee/ plant cities that swarm you with bullets, as your fleet fails to do anything remotely logic.
There's a lot of death and grind, so it can get kinda frustrating. However, designing and adapting your ships is pretty fun, and seeing your crazy strategies work out against the OP agents is quite rewarding. So, overall, the game is just cool. Not the most fun game, but simply cool.
Also ,the developers actually listen to the players and fix the bugs, so that's nice.
And there's a Steam Workshop filled with varied mods.
If you don't care too much about some grinding, this is a good game to have.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat performance, consistent updates, exceptionally made and satisfying mechanics, limitless creativity and one of the most active and brilliant modding communities I've seen for a game are all features that are present in Anisoptera's game Reassembly.

I thoroughly enjoyed every second I played of this game, both vanilla and modded, and consider it to be one of the better games I have played on Steam. The ship customization is robust and freeform, the combat and movement systems are intuitive and well made, and every faction felt entirely different to every other faction you could play, allowing for virtually infinite numbers of experiences.

However, the game did lose it's spark after a while for me; without a true reason to come back to the game, a compelling story or an evil you must overcome, it seemed to be built purely to have fun whenever you got bored and not call you back for more every time you finished playing.

On the whole, I would argue it is worth the purchase, but if you do not have the commitment to play a game that doesn't call you back for any reason other than "I want to play it," don't feel bad for giving this game a safe pass.\t\t\t\t\t
