\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game might have simple graphics and no real story to it but for what it is it is really close to perfect. Very easy to get into but plenty of depth the more you play. Everything is so polished and well done that you can really tell the developers put a lot of time and love into getting things just right. The only issue with the game at all I have seen, and it is unfortunately a kind of big one, is that when you form a fleet of ships to follow you they really don't follow you all that great sometimes. I have had over 100 ships with me then just by flying ahead straight to explore for a bit that number dropped down to 55. You can go back and get most of them added back to your fleet but that really shouldn't be a problem at all. I'm hoping they add the ability for your follower ships to simply warp jump back to you if they get too far away at some point soon to fix this, or something.

Upgrading your "P" so you can build bigger and better ships or add on to what you have is pretty fun and addictive, and producing your own fleet of ships you designed, including ones that can build other ships too to make a self replicating army, is great.
Once you are at the point where you are flying a giant mothership armed to the teeth and can dominate the galaxy, you can always go to a new galaxy or start over playing as one of the many other very different and fun creatively designed factions you've unlocked.

Other player designed ships invade your galaxy, which makes sure you always see something new and often challenging. I can see a ton of hours going into this game for just about anyone. You don't have to be really creative to make fun and effective ships either, but creativity does add to the fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never write reviews on Steam, but I believe Reassembly has earned one. This game is easily worth the pricetag. The gameplay is fun, with nice yet easy-to-run graphics and good control scheme (rebindable too). The factions each have their own strength and weaknesses. My favorite is the Tinkerell faction; their modular guns can plow through any obstacle. My best experience is with the games many mods. Many add new factions, adding more enjoyment. My favorite mod removes the P limit and allows you to build ships easily 30 times over the normal limit. Combine that with the Tinkerell and you get weapons akin to the Deathstar with engines. With a simple FWOOM it pierces through asteroids and obliterates a station sectors over.
Pros:
-Fun and modifiable controls
-Plays on almost any PC
-Many factions and weapons
-Ungodly mod support that brings your enemies to their knees
-Deathstar
Cons:
-The Command mode could be more fleshed out, allowing for bigger and better battles
-AI could use improvement, I've had my own fleet push me into many bad situations (asteroids)
-I think Anisoptera is stealing my soul\t\t\t\t\t

Note: I put cons and improvements because I think the cons are covered in mods and the improvements seem very possible to update in vanilla or put in DLC. These also don't greatly impact the gameplay. It's worth every penny, buy it!

Pros:
-Beautiful colors and light animations.
-A good amount of variety.
-The ability to map reset through wormholes allowing extended play.
-OR export your current fleet into somebody else's map through wormholes!
-The ability to export ship designs.
-Pit your ships against others in tournaments! (browse through the workshop for those fun times)

Cons:
-There aren't many weapon choices per faction, unless you're faction 4.
-The block limitation is annoying. Why can't I make a huge, intricate ship?
-More polygons for building, please!
-The AI of my fellow comrades is sometimes very disappointing, especially when they pilot a ship that I created.

Improvements:
-There needs to be a friendly way to implement mods through a modloader.
-Great ship editor. There could be a fill/brush tool and a more dynamic selection method to be near perfect.
When I say dynamic selection I mean selecting a group of blocks with a freeform selection, instead of the clunky box method.
-More anchor points on the polygons in the ship editor.
-You could easily add a story and missions. Annihilating other factions is fun and all, but I want a purpose. This game could easily have lore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is hands down my best purchase on steam. Reassembly is basically tangram spaceships with guns. The UI is absolutely flawless, and the building system is easy and intuitive. The animations are smooth, colorful, and beautiful. Reassembly offers a world filled with asteroids, derelict stations, plants, dangerous enemies, and sometimes even dangerous plants. You destroy enemy ships for resources, which you can then turn over to your faction for money to upgrade your ship size or unlock new equipment. If you find enemies that are too tough for you, you can still earn money by exploring and rebooting dead derelict stations or fighting weaker enemies. Wormholes allow you to reload the world and start fresh, and your fleet that you have designed gets uploaded to spawn in the worlds of other players. Whenever you start a world, fleets that other players have made will spawn as agents at the wormholes and start heading towards you slowly. This offers a challenge for late game players. Once you hit the upgrade ceiling, you can play another faction, and when you have played them all, you can mod more in. There is so much to do, and you can drop the game and pick up where you left off in an instant. The game can run on practically any PC, and I recommend it to everyone.\t\t\t\t\t
