\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr Spore meets SubSpace (aka Continuum), 8/10. Remember SubSpace? Need I say more? I do? Okay.

As others have said, this game is extremely addictive. In a good way. The ship editor in itself is hours of lego-style building fun, but add to that a really immersive single-player world with player-made ships and you have a game that is both simple and compelling at the same time.

Definitely worth the money, tons of replayability. Summarizing my experience as follows:

Pros:
- Great physics / destructable levels, and combat is super fun.
- Awesome soundtrack.
- World is huge but not infinite. That's a good thing for a space game.
- Building ships is surprisingly fun. I'm not usually the type to want to buy a game then spend hours in an editor customizing things. The editor is very featureful and forgiving (undo, redo, copy paste), unlike many other in-game editors out there.
- Sending your ship into a wormhole uploads your fleet into the ether so they can be run in other player games as the bad guys, Spore-style! In other words, tons of player-made content.
- If you get sick of your world, fly into a wormhole and explore a parallel dimension instead.

Cons:
- Controls are a little quirky, especially on gamepads. Don't use a gamepad. Occasionally you'll find yourself slamming into an asteroid and getting completely stuck because the "fly backwards" key gets completely rotated and you and up flying into the asteroid and flailing around until you're killed by a passing enemy.
- Ship editor is missing a MANUAL test flight option to get a sense of the maneuverability of the ship you spent an hour making.
- Ship editor is missing important info about total mass / total thrust / total energy created which could help to approximate the above stuff. Only total resource capacity is listed-- everything else you have to calculate yourself.
- NO MULTIPLAYER! The player-made content is fun in SP, but a game like this just begs for multiplayer.
- Once you've maxed out points, Actual gameplay is 1-dimensional. Mostly consists of flying around and shooting everything you see-- or if you're like me and setup auto-fire, it's just flying around and letting the ship shoot everything for you. You can explore to find new bases, but that doesn't really give you anything.

On the last two points, I wonder if I'm just misunderstanding some of the game dynamics, but it seems like unlocking bases is just there to occupy your time. I also don't really know if you can ever wipe out another faction. It would be great if:

- You could watch bases expand over time, including size and number of ships, so you actually have more allies flying around. Ditto for enemies that go unchecked.
- You could actually attack enemy bases and eventually wipe out factions. It would be awesome if each faction actually had a stronghold base you have to kill in order to wipe out an enemy.
- There were new factions added over time, including to existing regions. Maybe I haven't played for long enough, but it seems like the same factions just exist in perpetuity.

There it is. Lots of cons because the pros seem obvious to me. It's damn fun! It could be even better if the above things were addressed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is awesome. Ever played a game and wished you could tweak your ship? Well now you can.
Any type of ship imaginable is buildable: Carriers that launch leigons of drone fighters; Battleships with heavy cannons and a myriad of point defense lasers; Missile Cruisers with racks upon racks of guided missiles; Destroyers with cannons, lasers, beam weapons, and torpedo launchers; Fighters with rapid-fire plasma guns, lasers, and rockets. Weapons can be bound to control groups and can be set to fire in grouped bursts or in a continuous chain.

The game starts you out with a well-made tutorial. It provides guidance on learning the game and getting your fleet up and running (yes, you can build ships that can build more ships), without being intrusive or pushy. All of this building requires competing for and collecting the resources that grow on nearby asteroids. These raw resources can then be used to construct additional ships or exchanged at one of the friendly stations you've activated for credits to fund research and fleet upgrades.

The physics in this game are fantastic. Fire a heavy cannon, and your ship recoils. Hit something or blow something up, and chunks fly off and blow apart. The field of wreckage that remains after an epic battle is part of what makes this game so satisfying.

The graphics are beautiful. Colors and effects are vibrant. (Just go look at the screenshots!)

The music is suitable and soothing, but not particularly noteworthy, but the sound effects from firing weapons are right on point.

There are still a few bugs. The game never seems to remember my control settings, and the game sometimes has trouble starting (restarting the launcher seems to work for the latter).

TLDR: I don't usually write reviews, but this game is too awesome not to. It is a definite must buy and well worth the price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, Is all I can say really.
This is just an incredible game, I bought it and started playing at around 8PM, Next thing you know, I take a second glance at the clock and it's 5AM.

There are 7 factions, they all play and work differently, One faction is the high damage, high armour faction, but all but one of their weapons cannot do damage to shields. Another faction has incredibly low armour/health, but has the highest damaging weapons. You unlock the other factions by killing large valuable ships of that faction, once you kill a certain amount, or a certain value of ship, You unlock that faction to play as.

Ships are assigned value based on their size, So for instance, if your build cap is 500P, and you build a ship up to say, 460P, your ship is worth 460P. Most factions will cap out at 8,000P, and it will take some time to get there.

Inside each map, There are usually around 8-10 "Agents" Agents being player fleets spawning in your world, They can range from being total garbage, to an impossible force. Each time you go through a Wormhole, the way you change maps, It will spawn your current fleet setup randomly in other players worlds.

The gameplay is very nice, It plays like a twin stick shooter and it works. The game itself is very well optimized, even after spawning around 150 large ships it still ran smoothly and at a decent framerate. The graphics are nice and stylized.

I really have nothing negative to say about this game, It's just amazing.

With 22 hours in the game, I've managed to completely clear only 2 of the 7 Factions, By that I mean, unlocking all the parts, upgrading the build cap to max and building a very powerful ship. With it's recent price drop to 17$, It's DEFINITELY worth the price, You'll be getting a lot of game.

Solid 10/10, You NEED to buy this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is a game that is difficult to categorize. There are elements of building, space combat, and rts (in a somewhat abstract way). The game allows great creativity in shipbuilding style and combat philosphy.

I find this game to be great fun. You can start with a pre-built ship, or make your own right away. The game includes an AI driven tester that will pit your design against other designs so that you can tweak and perfect your ship before flying it. I prefer to fly around and earn some credits first for upgrades. The goal is simple - expand your territory, earn credits, use credits to upgrade the power level of ship you are permitted to fly, unlock upgrades and build better ships.

The game learns from how you play and from facing your ships. Eventually you will face ships made by other players. Also you will unlock other factions that you can play which have different ship components. The replay value is immense as no playthrough will ever be identical to another.

The graphics are clean and simple, not ultra-detailed, with nice effects, slightly retro and modern at the same time. The game includes excellent controller support for in-game play, but for ship creation you have to use mouse and keyboard. I think controller support for the building phase of the game would probably be awkward and difficult to implement, so I don't blame the devs...

10/10 If there is something wrong with this game, I don't know what it is!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am REALLY amazed about this game. This game absolutely nails the playability and usability. For me this game is up there with FTL and Minecraft, where you can just keep playing and playing for the fun of the experience itself, not the winning.

The building of new spacehips, trying out combinations, and just trying to make things look good is a lot of fun. Combined with the amazing slickness of the editor you can spend hours in a Zen-like mode of tweaking, playing, and tweaking some more.

What sets this game apart as well is that you fight the creations of other players, and that others will play against yours. That makes you care about your creations much more than you would have in a purely single player game.

All in all highly recommended if you like 2D space shooters and building games.\t\t\t\t\t
