\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like Battleships Forever or Captain Forever, this game is for you. (They're both freeware so go try them if you're interested in this.) It takes the concepts of both, and throws in a bit of Starsector (namely the fleet mechanics) for good measure to make a game I've poured hours into already. (As of writing, I've played it for 10.8 hours. I bought it yesterday.)

While some have also compared it to Gimbal, I'd say only at the very start. The comparison starts losing ground once you get the bigger ships.

Where this stands out is the.. well, game. BF and CF are both fun for a short play, but don't have many backing gameplay mechanics. Reassembly gives you economies, waring fations, fleet control and quite a bit of variety (Though I'd like to see mod support in the future for even more pieces/factions). Each faction also plays differently in various ways, for example, one can plant and grow their own resources, but seem to be lacking in firepower, another has literal nukes, but no shields, and so on, it forces a change in tactics when it comes to ship design that keeps things fresh. With all that in mind, it's still a sandbox, outside of the tutorial, what you do is purely up to you with zero guidance, I personally like that, some might not.

It should also be noted it seems to run superbly. I have a fairly powerful machine, but any game with 1000s of ships/particles will slow it down some. This doesn't, I've had fleets of hundreds of ships facing off against each other, and it has stayed at a stable 60fps.

All in all, one of the best games I've purchased in a very long time.

EDIT: Apparently mod support is already somewhat in the game. I got ahead of myself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is beautiful. A physics-based WASD space shooter nestled in the most enjoyable sandbox builder I've ever played.

Pros
  • Your ship is constantly rebuilding itself - leading to great gameplay moments like sniping enemy cores, narrow escapes, on-the-fly hull redesign, etc...
  • Instead of being a solo ship, you are part of a faction. You gain acess to new tech and bigger ships by advancing your faction's power which does a great job of making you feel integrated into the game world.
  • Each faction is very diverse, with its own unique attributes and building elements. These constraints help shipbuilding to stay fresh and encourage experimentation.
  • Fantastic ship editor makes it fun and easy to make your designs real.
  • Good tutorial to get you started with the core mechanics.
  • Community content - at any time you can approach a wormhole and upload your fleet. These fleets can then spawn in other players' games. What makes this awesome is that you don't just encounter single ships from these fleets, but whole communities with multiple ship styles from the same user.
  • A sense of progression. I typically lose interest in sandbox builders where you have to collect resources to use in crafting. Reassembly uses point-based ship construction, so you don't have to collect resources to build your ship - just to expand your point cap. This gives you concrete goals to work towards over time, while again providing constraints to motivate continual re-design of your fleet.

Cons
  • You haven't purchased this game yet! Go support these developers!
