This. Beautiful. Game.

For those who enjoy having the ability to build literally almost EVERYTHING for yourself, this is very, very, very welcoming to the creative minds out there. Every single aspect of the ship is COMPLETELY up to you, as you build your ship (and soon) fleet; not just build, but evolve.

At first, you might get lost real easily by how the game starts out, dropping you in the middle of the game, letting you get your hands in the thick of it with no real instruction. EXPERIMENT! This game is literally endless, with no real, true penalties to stop you from doing whatever you want. From a few simple polygons, you clump it together slowly, piece by piece, and learn what YOU enjoy, and find to work, and improve off it. Not only that, but this game can conform to the way you wish to play easily, so there is NEVER any one, right way to play.

Wish to go in hard and heavy, with a giant, bulk-head cruiser? Go ahead, and charge at enemies with a hailstorm of laser beams. Or stay it safe, and strafe your enemies and snipe from afar with powerful missles and energy shots to obliterate the enemy. It is entirely put into the hand of the creator: You.

I bought this when it was at $30; I would do it again and again. I found it good enough to actually post a review for the FIRST TIME. With a lovely $15 price tag, and with how well sewed this whole game is, based on such a simple concept and turned into something limitless, I could nearly argue it's the best game I've ever played in my life thus far.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game rocks. It sucked me in completely for a couple of weeks. I'd look up at the clock and think omg it's been 6 hours! I'd go to work the next day on 5 hours sleep. It stirred my gamer blood! The ship building freedom is top notch. You build any kind of ship you want particle by particle and there are lots of smart, handy features in the ship building editor to save time. You can hold down the mouse and drag it across an area to select multiple components or you can just double click one piece and everything attached to it will be selected.

A quick ship building tip is to just build one side of the ship, say a wing, select all the pieces then hit c to copy. Move the cursor to a blank part of the screen and hit v to paste, then hit m to mirror (to invert the selection). Now you can just attach that perfectly mirrored section onto the opposite side of the ship if you need symmetry like for wings.
Symmetry is also important in placing your thrusters. You are in space so your ship isn't going to stop moving unless it crashes into something...or you put thrusters pointed in the opposite direction to the direction you're traveling in so you can break!
Also each different playable faction has some unique pieces to build with. Sometimes the minor differences aren't obvious, like where pieces don't have as much mass as other factions or they have slightly higher armor, but when you stack all those pieces together into a ship then the net values really make a difference in speed, toughness, or resource carrying capacity. You'll want to look at the stats on the ship pieces closely if you're into ship building.
If you're not into ship building there are premade ships available with each faction that you can use and just modify to your taste.

The game is beautiful to look at and very organic. To me it looks more like the world under a microscope filled with living, evolving organisms than it does a space frontier. Much like organisms, the ships actually create children. You can set a creation queue for yourself once you have a factory as part of your design. But you can also go into command mode by hitting tab and click on an allied vessel that has a factory and drag child designs into their queue so you begin to shape the future of your factions expansion without actually managing the execution. Just hit tab again to go back into pilot mode and go on your way. The allied ship you just programmed will now create what you set for it via the AI.

Once you've got your perfect fleet together you can find then fly into a wormhole. You will be given an option to follow it or stay. If you stay a parallel version of your fleet will upload to infect another player's galaxy through one of their wormholes while you remain where you're at with your original fleet. I just really wish there was some way of knowing how successful these invasions were, like a scoring system players could access to get some sense of how good your designs were.

The tournament mode doesn't pit you against other players. It just lets you test your own ships against your own designs and a small selection of premade designs. I would love to see a true tournament mode where you download player agents and test your ship against theirs with scoring. The game lacks true player interaction in real time but atleast we can see other player's creations as they come into our own galaxy as enemy agents controlled by the AI.

Unfortunately I do think there is a balance issue that needs to be addressed with faction 4. You will eventually find certain enemy agents that are typically oblong, travel at high speed, and shoot enormously long particle rays at you at range even from off of the screen! That's faction 4. They almost made me rage quit because they really get too many advantages, but I perservered and went back to the design editor. Their weakness is maneuverability. Their big bad weapon of choice is not turreted so the whole ship has to turn to target you with the death ray. This is not a huge disadvantage because their ships are inordinately fast but if you build a ship that can quickly side step their line of fire and either swarm them or ram them so that they can't turn into you then you can take them down...if you can catch them. Trapping them against asteroids is a typical ploy. Really though, no other faction gives nearly so much trouble...

The only other criticism I have is lag. No it's not really just a hardware issue. I play many other games where it's not an issue. The devs themselves have stated that they've worked really hard on the optimization but the fact is there comes a point where there are so many collisions going on in the galaxy that it can be taxing. I have an i5-4670, 8G ram, nvidia GTX 780 so my hardware is good. But I like faction 2 whose strength is cultivating resources and building up a fleet really quickly. Once I push out a bunch of ships that then create their own AI controlled child ships, the game has alot to track. The devs said to go into options and lower the physics updates per second value so I'll try tweaking that more. But it kind of makes me want to put the game aside now and come back later, maybe playing a different faction and not use a swarming strategy. If you get lag you can also follow a wormhole to a fresh universe, but that too can start to get repetitive.

The fresh content is really about fighting other player designs as enemy agents enter your galaxy. I hope the devs continue to work on the lag issue (maybe instancing parts of the playing field to lower the amount of AI activity needed as the game progresses). I'd also like to see them expand on player interaction by scoring invasions, and maybe add some simple tiered goals with rewards for flipping sections of your galaxy to your faction. I think those things would add alot to the game's longevity for me. Regardless, this is a really creative game. I love looking at it and playing it. It was worth every cent.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe level of enjoyment you get out of this game will depend on two things, how much you like starships, and your level of creativity.

If you don't like sci-fi and disliked art class when you were young, there is a fair chance you won't get much traction with this game, but if you are creative, imaginative, and love sci-fi, this game is a splendid creativity tool.

It could use some more parts admittedly, some factions have close to 40 while others have only 17 or 16, and it has a few factions that aren't playable at this point, both things I hope will be rectified in the future, but despite a few flaws, this game is undeniably a jewel, a unique and interesting concept.

In the end a Review is about one thing, is the game fun, and for me, and likely for those others who enjoy science-fiction and creativity tools, this game is definitely fun, prepare to sink hours into tweaking the designs for your fighters, capital ships, and stations.\t\t\t\t\t
