\r\n\t\t\t\t\t This game is an amazing sandbox ship building game that has a voxel based ship building system. You get a palette of ship parts consisting of weapons, armor, propulsion, and other unique abilities and quirks specific to each faction. There are 7 playable factions in the game and each one has their own playstyle. However this is a sandbox game without much of a storyline behind it. Your only goal in the game is to conquer the galaxy however way you see fit. If you have a hard time finding reasons to play games or get bored easily this might not be the game for you. If you like to build or have a unique imagination -all the while blowing things up with your pew pew lazers- then you will enjoy many hours from this game.

Reassembly is truly one of the greater indie games on steam and i hope more people will enjoy it like i have. The DLC adds a couple of faction specific weapons and modules that change the game (including the cool gravity wells and slowmo zones), so i would recommend buying the Fields Expansion for a couple more dollars. 9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly. This game languished on my install list for a year before I finally launched it two nights ago. Since then I have played 15 hours. The entire weekend.

Big pluses:
    \t
  • Ship design UI is good. Very good. The controls and visual feedback for this part of the game are nearly flawless, and by "nearly" I mean, "rarely new parts will snap to a place I dislike and I have to dismantle a wing piece to get things to fit properly".
    \t
  • Ship flight control is very simple but manages to inform your ship design process very naturally: I went from merely clicking things together to having opinions about things like "proper placement of boosters and afterburners" very quickly just from flying around. Player weapon targeting controls and auto-targeting AI (should you use it for a particular weapon) are pretty good too.
    \t
  • AI unit flying and weapon targeting seem very good in dogfights, adaptive even.
    \t
  • Faction ship variety seems pretty decent; I have so far unlocked the "red" faction and was pleased to see a big difference in both visual design and general function of components.
    \t-I longed for more parts to unlock for the starting "Terran" faction, so look forward to seeing even more bizarre options when I unlock more factions
I really enjoy the gameplay and was pleased to see that dev is still alive for this title. Some additional features that seem very needed are:
    \t
  • Allow the tutorial to be disabled!!! At least after first activation.
    \t-The quests for developing a fleet could be separated, as some may enjoy the milestone tracking for each game.
    \t
  • Increase the lower end of hitbox size for station resource turn-ins!
    \t-Currently, some resource stations (basically, any ship with a spawn node) are difficult to interact due to small size, presumably due to scaling with the size of parent ship body.
    \t
  • Add an in-game database of self-made builds, viewable from the main menu and independent of any save file!
    \t-I want to iterate over the course of my gameplay in a fashion more graceful than saving-and-restoring lua files
    \t-It would also be handy to allow players to see all ship plans currently being used by their faction during a game.
I enjoyed this game so much that I found myself wanting even deeper gameplay. Some suggestions to that end:
    \t
  • Allow play as a faction that only spawns exactly what player wants , allowing:
    \t-Precise placement of new static ships/space stations
    \t-Complete control over creation of new spawners
    \t-Easy control of existing spawners and their spawn pools--I don't always WANT my new ship design to become the fleet mainstay
    \t-The above options would add a heavier real-time strategy element to the existing fun of designing and shooting. Note that things already turn this direction once you start building your own fleet, but allowing this from the start this would give more ownership of your faction!
    \t
  • Give options for more control over fleet resourcing if the player wants (in the vein of spawn control point above):
    \t-Add UI overview of all resources currently owned by fleet ships
    \t-Add some sort of resourcing AI that can be attached to ships designed for the express purpose of gathering (a slight variation on existing behavior of smaller AI ships)
