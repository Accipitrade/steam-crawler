\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is an infinite sandbox game of LEGO spaceships.
Ok, now I know I'm bad at making reviews but...

I was always obsessed with LEGOs during my childhood, always wanting to build more and build bigger, but physical LEGOs can be expensive. Not an issue here!

Reassembly's building mechanics are also notably different than that of other machine building games, unlocked blocks can be used as much as you want, there is no manufacturing process, as ships will simply regrow, regenerate or Reassemble, although you still need resources to spawn allied ships, and resources mechanics are simple, only one type of resource.
More importantly, Reassembly's blocks are not limited to a grid! I guess this is very difficult to implement properly, to a 2D game maybe not so much, but 3D? I guess not.

There are 7 playable factions, and each one of them feels unique, all have their own blocks, some have equivalent blocks, but most don't, base game player factions feel very balanced in terms of stats, but AI isn't, AI handles spinal (fixed) weapons pretty terribly, seen in faction 4 (Thinkrell) and even worse in 12 (Bee), there isn't much that can be done about it, but AI does feel very refined everywhere else.

My favorite aspect of Reassembly is the Ship Core, every ship needs one central command module that acts as the heart of the ship, and if destroyed, the ship will also be destroyed, so you have to protect it well, not like Robocrap where you need to destroy 75% of the enemy robot instead of a command module.

Only frustration I had so far is AI spinal weapons on faction 4 and 12, and Conquer the Galaxy achievement, where you pretty much have to kill every single enemy in the galaxy, though recently, it is possible to select galaxy scale and omit factions, so it's kind of cheesable now.

Oh, there's community mods, some will add way more content than the base game, do check out the workshop will ya?

Overall, being able to build a ship that is designed to fight feels very satisfying, not like Machinecraft's paper ships, being able to build a massive warship that can kill everything in sight is like a dream come true, a feature that real life LEGOs don't have.\t\t\t\t\t
