\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game, but it gets stale very quickly and the developer didn't deliver on the promises made during early access. The game is exactly the same as it was when it came out on Steam, with the only major addition being a "tournament" mode where you plop a couple ships into a ladder to see which one wins out.

The aesthetic is interesting, but ultimately the actual game is shallow. Most of the gameplay comes from designing your ship and seeing how it fares in combat, but the combat is doesn't have strategic depth other than ramming or kiting. Your design slowly moves away from what you'd call an actual spaceship and more towards a geometric shape with internal engines. A basic square filled with as many weapons and shields as possible is simply better than trying to create anything that's actually interesting. Ship combat, therefore, devolves into a blob of shields mounted on a square careening through space and slamming into other blobs of shields in an orgy of lasers and missiles. It looks nice, but carefully designing a ship isn't worth the effort.

The design system is simple and easy to understand, but in practice it just falls flat.

The "living world" is also not really what you'd expect. Capturing territory across the galaxy becomes an exercise in Exterminatus from Warhammer 40,000. You bring as many ships as possible and cleanse every single enemy ship from an area of the map, which then switches colour to your faction. That's it. You don't get any resources, your own ships don't start to spawn there, not even a message appears on screen. A part of the map changes colour, and you move on to the next area. Eventually this devolves into chasing one tiny little gnat of a ship that can't even hurt you across your territory because it keeps reclaiming tiles. It's tedious.

Don't get me wrong, I liked this game, but it is very shallow and the replayability just isn't there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimply put, this game is amazeballs! Seriously, buy this game. I would never have imagined someone making something like this or me going over it and yet here it is; At it's core just a handful of basic ideas, geometric building blocks, and one giant helping of awesome-sauce slathered on top to drown the whole thing in.

Here's what to expect:

1. A Procedurally generated space sandbox so the game is literally new every time you start a new game.
2. A palette of glowing geometric blocks with which to build your epic spaceship/station heart out.
3. Your own fleet lovingly mouse-crafted by you from one of 7 factions unlockable by playing the game and dominating your enemies.
4. 100s of stock ships from the 12 (or 13?) distinct in-game factions populating your space-box (that's "space sandbox" for those of you catching up).
5. One of my own ships showing up in your space-box bent on dominating your universe via the "Wormhole" system that allows AI controlled versions of other Reassembly player's ships to appear as "Agents" and lay down some good ol' fashioned obliteration on your ass.
6. Out of control radiant geometric space-plants growing on nearby asteroid fields that SEEM to not pose a serious threat compared to the other guys with plasma-cannons and burst-lasers but just wait....just wait...
7. Moddable factions (currently in beta) where you, yes YOU! can create your own block palette, ships, and other faction goodies to inject into your game or upload to the Steam Workshop and let others play with your wonderful (or evil...) creations.
8. An awesome in-game soundtrack by recording artist Peaks.
9. Hours to fly by as you have an amazing time playing one of the greatest games there ever was or is or will be in this dimension or the other infinity of them. Seriously, buy this game.

What not to Expect:

1. True synchronous multiplayer....sorry folks, this doesn't seem to be a thing in Reassembly but who knows about the future? *wink wink Arthur wink wink*
2. Boredom. In fact, kiss that goodbye because Reassembly will be filling his usual spot from now on.
3. Buyer's Remorse. "This game is good. This is game is very good." - Everyone\t\t\t\t\t
