\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game of the holidays 2016 for me with 100+ hr and counting...

I have the same amount of fun playing the game than building new ships, mainly because both have been done equally with care...


Once you get the hang of it (and unlocked the pieces you need), building a ship may take time but is a breeze. You can Copy/Paste (old Ctrl-X-C-V) an entire ship, a bunch of pieces or put pieces one at a time... For example, to make a circular ship (think Borg Sphere), I made 1/4 of the ship, then Copy/Paste and snap the 3 other parts together to make the sphere shell of the ship. Next step was to put weapons, shields Generators and such, then try your new creation in space...
Of course, how you can build and what you can put depend on the faction you selected. Some factions has no square hull or no shield module, for example...


Once in the battlefield, you'll notice that all ships are definitely not created equals and you need to adapt your 'piloting' accordingly:
- Like most game of this type, you have to keep a balance (or not) between Firepower, Armor, Speed and Mass of the ship...
- Your pilot square is not only the thing that keep your ship together and mean death if it is destroyed, but also affect the center of gravity of your ship... That means that for a ship of the same shape, you will drive it differently if you put the pilot capsule at the front, center or rear of the ship... Negligeable if you build a small fighter, but more obvious if you build a 'Star Destroyer' kind of ship... And putting it too close to the extremity means the enemy need to punch less shield/armor to hit it... Life if full of compromises...
- And perhaps what surprise me (in a good way) the most is the feeling that you're not alone. Your part of a faction against other factions. Of course, you have a major infuence on your faction progress (you unlock new parts with your hard earned Credits, your faction will spawn and use ship you created, etc...) but when you pass next to an space port, you may see a new ship spawning and going for enemies and/or ressources alone (piloted by the AI, of course...).
- You can fly solo or order others ships of your faction to follow you, giving you the feeling of commanding a small armada. Of course, AI is not the sharpest tool in the shed, but it is competent enough to follow you and attack your target, giving you an numerical advantage against powerful enemies... Just don't expect they'll use asteroids for cover or retreat in time when in trouble.


Add to that mods from Steam Workshop, and you have plenty to see and do...

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is a sandbox game about building your own ships in SPACE and fighting enemies and controlling there sectors. Now to my serious review.
I played over 31.2 hours and I have discovered lots of cool things about it, like uploading your fleet so it will become a agent and attack other Player's worlds when they either start a new world or go to another. Another cool thing I witnessed is that the Ship Costumization is so simple and you can costumize it 100%, Block Limits is the part I don't like about ship costumization, you cannot create extremely huge ships because of this. Another one is that your enemies will continue to grow, everytime you remove parts or add in new parts to your ship, your enemies will know this and evolve there ships to something stronger, even that one Red Faction you thought was extremely weak but later they evolved and can pack a punch to any other faction. The one thing I really hate about Reassembly is your allies. You're allies won't do anything. They just waste there time, sitting around in there bases, patrolling it and not attacking or exploring other sectors of the map, which I strongely hate. Not only that, the enemy AI also seems to suffer this as well, with this, you feel like you're the only one in the whole map exploring while others are just sitting and spending there rest of there time in there bases, flying aimlessly and then wait until getting recruited. Now that I finished gathering up all the things I know and loved and possbily strongely hate about, I made a list.
Pros
Ship Costumization: Extremely good, giving you 99% freedom creating your own ships, but where brick limits come in, stopping you from creating a massive ship or even a gigantic station.
The AI: The AI can be smart but extremely stupid at times. The only thing the AI actually knows about is attacking there enemies, gathering resources and heading back to there bases for ressupply in order to create more ships, and the new ships go on and on with gathering resources, bringing it back and create new ships, this is a endless cycle. I was hoping for them to attack your sectors or attack other sectors and spawn stations there, at most of the time in my opinion, I think there very dumb.
Graphics: The Graphics are nice, when you shoot, the laser particles, missiles and drone particles show very well, even the particales thrusters make, are extremely nice, though there are a few mods on the Workshop to make it better, but at vanilla-balanced they look a little nice, looks epic when a couple of ships fight each other but that's rarely the case because the AI.
The Map: The map is extremely huge, big enough for you to explore, may take up to five months to explore the whole map. The map has a bunch of living inhabitants, including your allies. The Map also haves asteriods, plants, spikey plants and other special factions that might find and destroy you. I'd say the map is epic and suitable for a exploration game.
Updates: This game rarely gets updated, and may never get updated again by the developers. Updates are extremely rare for this game, and you may or may not have seen "Reassembly updating" or anything like that. Don't buy this game if you don't like very inactive Developers or like this game in it's current state.
From what I know for the ending of this review, I'd say I recommend this. To those who like the game in it's current state with extremely inactive developers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe age of humanity has long passed.

Scattered across the universe, agents of destruction lay dormant for millenia.

Until the awakening.

Sentient war machines, relics of a universe in turmoil. Forged by their ancestor to complete one mission.

Annihilate everything.

The endless conflict continues to march into the vast darkness of space. Battles rage across the cold void, annihilating planets, moons and asteroids. Cosmic obliteration for a purpose long forgotten.

Technology has been captured, assimilated, refined and transformed into brutally efficient self-replicating mechanisms of war.



That's basically what this game is all about. You get a ship that you can customize and make it stronger and eventually you will get some kind of a factory block that allows you to construct other ships that may or may not have a factory block to continue the cycle and conquer the galaxy, then by entering a wormhole you can choose between doing it again or share your creations with other players in the form of agents that will relentlessly kill anyone they see.
The game is really light-weighted and have high modability as well.

10/10 Good game to play while idling in TF2\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSlowly rolling through the galaxy with your self-replicating, custom designed, fleet of death: 10/10

Sending that fleet through a wormhole for someone else to battle: 11/10

This game is equal parts Minecraft (sandbox and building blocks), Gratuitous Space Battles (bullets, lasers, and missiles everywhere), and vector space shooter (gravity and coasting). And it has that satisfying GTA San Andreas-style area control. There's something very satisfying turning that map your color one square at a time. You go back a while later and your designs are settling in and making it their own. Unfortunately, there is one downside, I see a crash every couple hours. Given all the madness on the screen that's understandable, but frustrating nonetheless. Fix that and/or making saving a little easier and this game would be even better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is nearly perfect. If there were a multi-player arena where players could go against other players in a duel or FFA, then it would be perfect.

First of all, it captures the the ship-building and physics of Astro Battle (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astro_Battle), but... better. More parts, more customization, and factions with different techs that support different playstyles. It's more forgiving to newbs too - each individual thruster doesn't need to be keyed for steering - the computer takes care of that. But Astro Battle was only PVP, and you couldn't play it when nobody else was online.

But it also captures the mayhem of a giant 2D dogfight the way Hellfighter did (http://www.dracova.com/hellfighter/). Both Astro Battle & Hellfighter were fun, but neither lived up to its potential. I think they're both abandoned now, but I still have the memories. This gives you an AI to fight with lots of ships, but lets you customize yours and potentially every one in your fleet. Best of both worlds.

And you can artistically design your ships, colors, space stations to shape the world. In that regard, it reminds me a lot of Spore from EA games, only this is is combat-oriented. Spore was incredibly fun in the early stages, but lost out when you got to the galaxy stage - the single fleet that capped out at half a dozen was the only thing making the economy go, and the galaxy quickly became too big for your little fleet. In Reassembly, every ship has an independent captain and the "economy" works for each individual. You don't need to babysit your empire, so it's not tedious - and there's no cap to the fleet you can have, so battles are FUN.

And there is a decent AI to play against. Not as good as PvP in some ways, but better in other ways. The AI lets you test your own designs against - your own designs, for example. And you can fight the AI-controlled blueprints that were designed from other players. It's almost as good as facing off with other players. There is a tournament mode that lets you upload different ships/fleets and then watch the magic.

And finally, there is a neat little environment where space vegetation and debris from other ships can be collected as resources to build other ships from factories, and you can get a fleet that can maintain itself and increase it's numbers based on strategic settlements and/or battle victories.

The different factions make for diverse opponents, and once you defeat a battleship from another faction, they become playable if you want to play as another race. And the biggest challenges are fleets from successful players - or the AI playing with custom ships designed by players, anyway.

Everything about the interface is user-friendly, but not cumbersome. The designers did it right - including lots of shortcuts in the ship designer to make building big things easier. You can tell they are perfectionists. But they also made it mod-friendly in case you have a different version of perfection. I can't say enough good things about it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBuy this game. This is easily one of the most immediately enjoyable titles in my Steam Library. Over the 10 hours I have played Reassembly so far, it has brought me more joy and excitment than Skyrim, Fallout 4, and the Saints Row series combined. For a game that is essentially a customizable version of Asteroids, this title births a spectacular amount of content at every turn. Depending on how you choose to play, it can be a bullet hell shooter, a top-down sandbox survival, or a space fairing cross between Terraria and an RTS. The mercifully laissez faire ship customization means that it only takes a matter of hours to get to a point where you can build your ideal spacecraft, shred a section of the star system, jump through a worm hole, and scrap your initial design to take on new, more powerful enemies. The opportunities for customization only multiply as you unlock new factions, each with their own unique powers and challenges.

All of this beng said, the game isn't without a few technical flaws, the main issues involving communication between local save data and the Steam Cloud. Though it is totally possible to save all progress on your personal computer, after a successful initial run, I have found it completely impossible to start the game through Steam or get it to synchronize with subscribed workshop content...not that the game needs any of that, frankly. However, the issue stands as a glaring flaw in an otherwise wonderful game.\t\t\t\t\t
