\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a very good idea, and the trailers present a very cool premise, build your own designs, take them into battle, and conquer the galaxy. The problems begin to arise almost immediatly, with a tutorial that tells you WHAT you should be doing, but not HOW you should be doing. Add in unresponsive flight controls where you are telling your ship to go up, and it's still drifting bottom left (For clarification: yes I do know how newtonian phyics work, that's not the issue). Plus a UI that is vastly limited and not very understandable, and you've got a nicely designed ship that doesn't work well, and you have absolutely no idea about what you should be doing or how. Then we get to the next problem: fleet building. Sure you can build your own designs, make stations that churn out ships, but they all automatically join your fleet. Why is this a problem you ask, it's a problem because the ships completely clog the nearby areas making it impossible to navigate, you end up spending half the game in an interstellar traffic jam, and that's when they are not ramming you for no reason, stealing all your resources, and stealing the credit for killing enemy ships. Seriously, when the ships that you built and command kill enemies, you don't get any reward for it. They are literally a financial liability. A great concept, poor delivery.\t\t\t\t\t
