\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was fun while I played. It has an interesting style of gameplay, and you can build your own ships. These ships "evolve" while you play, and you can unlock factions. There are a few quests, but mostly in the form of completing tutorial objectives. I enjoy seeing other players fleets (agents).

But then I completed one faction 100%, unlocking all factions along the way. Made an 8k P ship, a few stations, and a few additions to my fleet. I completed another faction 100% in a little under two hours. And now I wouldn't recommend this game to anyone. If you are efficient and play the game (the way it wants you to play), any eagle-eyed player would see there are game breaking ships that can be built in just about every faction.

Personally, I feel the game has little content and replay value. While, exciting at first, it just drops off. Stops. Turns stale and boring.

Pros:

-Good gameplay and shooter physics
-Great graphics made better by Splinter's Mod
-Seven Factions with different parts and playstyles
-Lots of variety, but only when you face off againt agent fleets

Cons:

-Once you figure out the game, you can max your faction fleet in about two hours (getting to 8K P and constructing your fleet).
-No real endgame beside tournaments/sandbox
-Too easy (at 8k P and your wits, you'll decimate everything)
-Too repetetive, even changing factions
-Not enough blocks
-Small Workshop selection of Mods (mostly factions)
-Game Breaking Ships (See forums for threads or the guides on this)
-Agent fleets can troll players making it not hard, but annoying (ships with 99% missiles or drones for example)
-Not enough real variety
-No real bugs, but there are some issues (getting stuck in quick regenerating stations, thrusters on ships not activating).

The game just left a bad taste in my mouth. It's a cool idea, but it doesn't really feel complete. There isn't enough to keep me coming back. There was so much potential, and it's wasted. Maybe there will be a #2.

If the game were a dollar, or maybe two, the value would match the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is a sure way to have a ton of fun. Its simple, and doesn't have much in it - but its just so beautifully executed that I have a hard time criticizing it. The first thing you'll notice is just how damn good it looks. The simple style mixed with the flashy bright particle effects make every ship look fantastic, even if your not a skilled builder. Basically, you start as a faction with a little plot of territory in the galaxy, and must build ships with increasing size and power, collect resources, conquer and annihlate opposing factions, and eventually control the whole galaxy.
You manage your faction in a simple screen that essentially says "what ships should we build?." Not every ship you construct will be your own, you'll find yourself making everything from Carriers to Gatherers to Melee brawlers, all supporting your faction on a galactic scale, not just following you around.
Pros
-Visuals: Just look at them. They're mesmorizing...
-Simplicity: This may not be for everyone, dont expect complexity and depth. Though this DOES NOT MEAN ITS NOT SKILL BASED, more on that later. The simple building system allows you to make pretty ships that are functional in minutes, and make enormous detailed bohemoths in about 20 minutes. Its just so easy to do, you dont even have to be creative, really. (That is not saying there isn't lots of room for creativty.)
-Skill: Outside of building, you've got to know how to pilot your ship. ou can die fairly easy and lose control of your entire fleet, so you've got to be careful. You can play just bumrushing everything, but you'll die a lot. I recommend lots of mobility, even in ships that wouldn't ordinarily need it. I cant count the amount of times I failed to remember to make good thruster systems and ended up with a floaty brick.
-Faction Growing: By far the games greatest strong point. Over time, as you see A.I. ships you created flying about and taking territory for you, it gives you a nice feeling of progression. You can even costomize what the stations you capture will look like. I found myself building unarmed civilian vessels and giant mining ships just so that our territory would seem lived in. I build a cheap but huge metropolis that I spammed all over my land so that it looked like there were cities.
This stuff is not entirely nonefunctional. I found that building mini mining ships that gathered all the R (resource) they could, letting all my stations spam them out, then just recruit them all so they'd give me more R then it costed C (credits) to hire them. I would then sell the R for C and make a fortune.
-The organic galaxy: Unlike most games, the galaxy is all in one map. Its not particularly big, you can fly across it in five to ten minutes with a fast ship. (Though you likely wont make it, the amount of enemies flying around and waring with eachother will kind of get in the way.) However, I think the map is better off small. You see, if it were big like actual space, it'd be empty. This map is anything empty. In fact, its a bit of a cluster****, and thats a good thing. The map is one giant asteroid field, inhabited by countless factions all fighting for territory. Whilst exploring, you will frequently find crashed and destroyed ships, stations, etc. And there will always be plenty of ships to fight. Once you get bigger, most of them wont be two much of a threat. But every now and then, a giant mothership will pop out of nowehere and blow you to pieces.
Ships dont just spawn when you get close, they all came from somewhere. And you can, if you blow enough of them up, completely genocide them. The factions will all be trying to do this to eachother (though they likely wont ever succeed due to the sheer amount of eachother) and you will constantly stumble across epic space battles and land grabs. Indeed, the whole galaxy is a warzone, and a chaotic one at that. There will sometimes be so much going on screen all you can see are particle effects( despite this and my fairly low end PC, the games runs like butter) Being able to zoom in and out greatly adds to the games scale as well. Oh yeah.. And also, SPACE PLANTS! SPAAACE PLAAANTS!
-Soundtrack: The fantastic ambient space music fits the game perfectly, with its Psychill electronic style making for a very relaxing genocide war.
-Tournements: I had a fun time with two friends, just playing the game locally during a get together, in which we all build ships of the same P limit and made them fight in the tounrement. Making your ships fight eachother is addictive enough without it being a competition between friends, so I recommend checking it out.
-Gameplay: It plays a lot like the old arcade space shooters, just far smoother and a bit more physicsy (eiiy? Eee?) This works really well with the open world aspect, making it a joy to play. Works really well as both a timewaster and a longer effort. There are a number of ways to play Reassembly, you can spend all your time making perfectly crafted fleets and sending them through wormholes to fight other players, or you can just make one ship and be a lone wolf, building your ship to perfection over time. Me? I prefer to play it on a large scale, assembling fleets and building military strength.
-Agent System: One of the better semi-online systems I've seen. Basically, you can upload your best fleets into wormholes, and they will appear in other players worlds to wreak havoc. Its kind of like Dark Souls's world invasion thing, but you dont control the fleets you send out. They are A.I. cotrolled. Agents appear as little red triangles, and vary in strength. (depending on the P (ship size) of the fleet the player sends out). If you destroy their flagship, the fleet breaks up and stops building new ships. They will spread out, raiding your stations, but will eventually all die.
This is a fantastic way for players to show off their creations. Although you will inevitably see a few giant blocks full of guns, most of the designs I've seen have been quite good looking.
Cons
-Lack of weapon variety: Weapons are a crucial part of the game. Sadly, most weapons are just generic projectile weapons, with some doing more damage then others. To be fair, there are lasers, drones, missiles, AoEs, but every faction seems to have most of those. The faction variation is more in their visual style and armour then their actual difference in combat. There are some more interesting weapons, such as the Proton Sword, the Borg Drones (Yes, Borg), and it seems as though every faction has at least one special gun, I would like to see more weapon types in the future. Stuff like wave guns, tracking lasers, force guns to blow enemies away from you, etc.
-Can get repetitive: Make sure to play this game in successive bursts. Playing for hours and days at a time will get boring fast, its better picked up and played for a few hours every now and then.
Procons?
-Balance issues: I dont really know about this one, its undeniable some factions are better then others (Red sucks, Crystalline are gud) but I wouldn't really call it a bad thing. Its more of a progression system, as the tougher factions are usually closer to the centre of the map. Make of this what you may.
Overall - worth a buy. One of the best games I've seen come off Steam, and an excellent example of what Indie Devs can do!
