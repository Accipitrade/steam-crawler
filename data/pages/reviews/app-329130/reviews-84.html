\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPeople who should buy this game:
You enjoy building custom ships and pitting them in tournaments against other custom ships in AI vs AI matchups, much like Gratuitous Space Battles, but with far more detailed shipbuilding (and less "gratutious price gouging"). To me, this is where the value of the game is at and where the vast majority of my invested time has gone.

So if you get the game, I suggest you play with it for an hour or two, "Test" your designs against other ships and then google for "Reassembly forums" and find the online community and therefore the tournaments. That will take you down an amusing road that you can indeed spend 100+ hours on, as I have. I really enjoy designing and testing ships.

People who should probably not buy this game:
Anyone looking for a long lasting, interesting single player experience that does not involve building ships for tournaments. While this game does have a roguelike(?) portion to it, and I suppose some people may get dozens of hours of enjoyment out of it, there's really not much to it. You fly around and "conquer the galaxy" but the mechanics of doing this basically involves flying around and shooting everything in your custom ship, which IS entertaining for a few hours, as you grow and tweak the design, but I felt that part got old fast. It's just not very interesting. Maybe if the galaxy had more actual strategy to it or an overarching metagame... I mostly just play as a faction enough to unlock all the parts and then go design ships for possible tournament play because the galactic battles seem so droll.

Still, I can't NOT recommend it considering I've put over 100 hours into a game that I think I pay $9 for. This game probably won't knock your socks off but it's neat and interesting and not a bad way to spend a few bucks if your interests are at all like mine.

Oh, search YouTube for the tournaments too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI... honestly have no idea what's even going on this game. Everything happens so fast and without explanation, tutorial objectives seemingly completed for a reason I can't really determine just because I happened to blink at the wrong time. If it's not that, I'm getting annihilated in 1-2 hits by any opposing ship, unable to defend myself because the default selection provided is so painfully underwhelming.

Scrapping and building from scratch isn't viable, either, the starting weapons absolutely worthless and block shapes that aren't particularly conducive or varied enough for sensible building in the long run. Could you theoretically grind for resources to eventually build the ship you want? Sure, but it seems counterintuitive when the core direction is undoubtedly combat.

That's not to say Reassembly is completely flawed, of course; the music and art direction are indeed very good and it is a neat idea overall, just not very well executed.\t\t\t\t\t
