\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had trouble connecting with Reassembly. The game sets itself as a sandbox game, but the interface is an action, top-down shooter. These two sides limit each other, as the designer tried to make an interface that would work for both. The resulting interface was not fun to use.

The game really wants you to be excited about building your own ship and interacting with the world of other AI ships, but it does not give you incentive to do that. There is a lot of bumping into things, trying to figure out the build mechanics, and not really having any place to go.

I played this game on Ubuntu 18.10 with a gamepad controller. The game was probably not intended to play with a controller, as the ship steering and menu options were very difficult.

I would only recommend this game for people who want to collect resources to build ships with lego-block type modules. People looking for a more typical space exploration game (such as Space Rangers or a StarconII) should look elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is great. Upgrading and building ships is straightforward. Conquering a galaxy is quite fun. There are mods that add alternative starting factions (or even completely change everything). Unlocking new factions requires getting good enough to take out an early boss.

Cons:
Zero story whatsoever. You're a ship with an allied faction and a galaxy full of enemies. That's the story.
If you build a fleet, your wingships don't generally work very well. They target what you target, and that's about it. You can release them and they will go about their business, but mostly idel. Useful for holding territory though.

Pros: Good gameplay. You can play at the level of anywhere from a single ship farming resources to slowly build up your faction all the way to a massive dreadnought leaving a trail of destruction wherever you go. You can build a fleet and spread them throughout the galaxy to claim turf. If you like customizing ships, it's very easy to snap together blocks and build a wide variety of ships. You can drop the schematics for these into allied stations (or stations that you build, even), and they will automatically build them as soon as they get enough resources to do so.

It's a great sandbox game with a small community building new factions for players to play.
Developer is still dropping patches, 3 years after release.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is an interesting 2d space battle game. Collect resources, defend bases, kill enemies. Explore the map to uncover more areas which contain deactivated bases for one to reactivate, as well as more enemies (quantity and type). There seem to be several enemy factions, which one can unlock for play as one progresses through the game. Ships are thoroughly modularised, and as one progresses through the game, one can earn currency which can then be used to purchase more or different modules - related to increasing efficiency in resource collection, defense, and attack.

It seems as if one of the draws is to eventually create the perfect space fighter, with modules up the wazoo. One has the choice of creating monstrosities of random pieces all over the place, or making something beautiful and pragmatic. I think there is a mechanic to uptake enemy faction modules but I have not progressed far enough to encounter this feature.

The graphics are very basic but it is very serviceable to the type of game. So to are the sound effects. No complaints from me.

I usually play much slower-paced games, so I currently find this game to be a bit more frenetic than what I am used to, but it definitely provides that action if that's what one is after. I drifted into a heavily patrolled enemy area and was immediately beset by 3 different warring factions and... well... died very quickly. It's definitely an exciting experience. I recommend this to people who like playing 2d space shooters with thruster mechanics plus those who like constructing their own special upgradeable space fighters out of an assortment of pieces\t\t\t\t\t
