\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI pirated this game a while back, promptly forgot about it, and then decided to buy it a few years later. It's still pretty good, but I don't think anything's changed since then. There's no overarching objective aside from "Reach the wormhole and repeat". It's good mindless fun though, and I'd recommend it if you got it on sale like I did.

Building ships and applying some real life armour principles (spaced armour, SLAT, etc.) is pretty fun, and you'll get to experiment a lot. I haven't gotten into the other factions much, but it's a very good game to go hogwild with creativity. You'll have a lot of fun building anything from practical ships to completely absurd designs just for the hell of it. Honestly, I think games like this carry themselves more on the creativity factor rather than the per-objective factor, and if you're a person who enjoys that, I'd recommend this a lot to you. If you're a person who would rather go min-max and then complete objectives; you'll find that this game would be quite lacking for you. Nothing against those playstyles, but this is definitely a game you'd get more mileage out of if you like to experiment more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game because I got sick of waiting for the guy I watch to upload.
I regret not having done this sooner.

Reassembly is a game which is pretty basic and does not require you to understand complex mechanics if you just want to play the game.
You are given shapes; you assemble yourself multiple ships and blow everything else up.

However Reassembly is one of those games in which vanilla isn’t everything, the normal playable factions are more like the tutorial level, the game becomes more challenging by the appearence of agents (Player-created factions).
You can even add your own agents or factions to the world generator, so you can go and try beating and succeeding your past self if you start a new save.

Each faction is different, in shapes, in playstyle, even in AI behavior resulting with the game becoming very replayable and the amount of possible play-throughs becomes pretty much endless as soon you take a look inside the workshop and spot the amount of ways to spice up the gameplay.
And even if you are still not satisfied, the love the developer put into this game becomes even more visible when you start reading through the documents he left for the community to make your own mod.

Concerning the music, while playing my brain was in shock, due to sudden realization.
I am normally the type of guy to turn the music off and runs his own playlist in the background, but the music of Reassembly (especially from the fields expansion) felt so in-place with the overall atmosphere of the game, even if I thought it was enough, I couldn't turn the music off.

However, the game is not perfect, there are small and annoying bugs you may encounter:

-Some ships forget what they look like whole or completely vanish.
-You sometimes encounter invulnerable ships which take no damage till they are rammed in an asteroid or take damage by driving into one
-Sometimes a part of the game becomes a black screen.
Quitting and reopening the game helps though.
-The game can crash sometimes, is normally no big deal unless you were in edit mode.
I recommend saving on a regular basis and making a copies of your ship (So you can still go back, since you would lose your undo-steps), depending on the complexity of the design of the ship you edit/build.
-Collision can mess up sometimes with ships suddenly clipping inside each other and sometimes can get the enemy or YOU inside an asteroid.

Overall the game is magnificent and I recommend to people who:

-like building games
-like puzzles, because the editors of some factions feel like one (especially the red faction if you try to build artistically-pleasing)
-wish to have themselves as teammates and enemy
-like blowing things up or explosions in general
-often have the fantasy of throwing things including your foes, your allies, your fleet, yourself, a rock or everything just stated into a sun
-like the small moments (They are almost guaranteed to exist.)

I also recommend to keep the rotation (r key) on keyboard settings, adding a spinal weapon or weapon with slow projectiles and no homing for your mouse-controls which turns this game into a factory of satisfaction, as long you go for greedy shots and hit.
