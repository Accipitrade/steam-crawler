\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly...a game where you can build you very own fleet piece by piece. A very interesting and fun game that I would reccomend to anyone and that I think anyone could get a kick out of. The only thing is how long you'll stick with it. Now, I've only played this game for about 9 hours, so this is more of a first impressions, so take everything Isay witha grain of salt if you wish.

This game is certainly an interesting idea that was executed rather well. They made the building system rather intuitive, but it is a bit difficult to master. I did enjoy when I had my fleet and I was just cutting a warpath through my first world until I got to the wormhole and sent my fleet to other worlds and logged off for the night. It's a game you think you'll for an hour or 2, only to find yourself walking out your door screaming "WHAT YEAR IS IT!?!?" However, my only real problem with it is that is all you can do. To me it feels like a game that will get repetitive instantly.

Again, only played for 9 hours myself, so take what I say with a grain of salt. I would reccomend this game to anyone to give it a go. It is certainly worth the 20 dollars, especially if you get real into it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've always been a sucker for customisation and Reassembly offers the one of the best ship-building experience that I have ever had. It's nice to see a game where ship design, thruster placement and mass distribution directly affects performance. Seven playable factions each with their own unique weapons and playstyles. Depending on your combat preference, some factions are easier to play than others. You kill things, research new components and increase the point limit for your ships.

I bought this game during a sale but after playing it I can say that, for me at least, I would have been happy to pay full price.

The only things that I would change in this game would be an option to disable tutorials when you start a new faction and perhaps a few more components for some of the factions that only have two different types of weapons.

Quick edit since I started playing the game again: The only feature I feel could use improvement is AI targeting. The bots (and missiles) do not lead their targets AT ALL which can be a big source of frustration as it causes them to them miss most of their shots, depending on the weapon. Charge-up weapons also don't seem to fully charge when they are on automatic or bot controlled.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is a space combat game, in which you build your own ship, and can also design all the ships your faction spawns.

The ultimate goal is to conquer the galaxy, eliminating all other factions (I've never managed).

It's got a great atmosphere, the galaxy really feels alive, with plants shooting their spores from one asteroid to another, all the different factions doing their business, battles raging, quiet stretches of space littered with the remains of destroyed ships, asteroid fields and wormholes.

It also has a sandbox mode (with command line and all), and a tournament mode, where you can organize battling championships.

All in all, it's a great game! As you can see (or not, depending on when you are reading the review), I sank over 92 hours into this game in two weeks. That's quite a lot - and for good reason, it's super addictive. It felt quick because I sank so many hours into it in a short time, I think, but it's got great replayability, I still play it occasionally.

Slightly dissapointed with some unlockable factions, and the UI isn't great (no way to save individual ship weapon bindings?), but I don't regret buying the game at all, it was (and still is, to be honest) a blast.

[EDIT (Feb 2016)] - Binding issue seems to be fixed! Devs are still fixing bugs and facilitating modding! :D

Most people should be comfortable with full price, it's not an expensive game at all.

Go ahead and buy it! :D\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI must say, I don't come back to games that often.
But this is one of those gems that I can't leave alone for longer than a week or so, it's a masterpiece.

The idea? Make a ship, blow up other ships, farm resources, make an army, use the aforementioned army to destroy other ships and make your faction inevitably powerful and build super destroyers, for the sake of entertainment.

But it's so much more than that, it has beautiful graphics (even on lower settings), doesn't destroy your framerate, allows free building of pretty much whatever you want, in terms of ships / stations, the only part of this game that I feel is lacking is in both the modding and sandbox areas.

They could use a little work, not for me, but for those inexperienced in the usage of command lines.
Aside from that, if you like action/exploration, then take up this game, i've had 90+ hours on it and I'm still not bored.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow.

This game is phenomenal. A true gem. It's like a rabbit hole that goes far deeper than you would assume at face value.

There is a lot of hidden depth in this game, from the simplistic yet very rewarding ship construction to the different ways varying races specialize, such as farmers who can cultivate plants into resources as opposed to annihilating all who oppose them with gigantic rail guns.

The game also boasts aesthetically beautiful fully destructable ships and environments, and a gorgeous soundtrack to boot.

In a lot of ways this game has me thinking this is what Spore should have been. Only with living spaceships.

Buy this game. Support awesome developers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI started playing and before I knew it, 5 hours had passed. This game is fun & addictive. It's a strange mix between bullet hell shooters, real-time strategy and Spore (the species evolution game).

Build & customize your ship, research new building blocks, add on a factory, start producing ships for your fleet, build space stations, conquer the map. This last bit... tough!

The ship builder/designer is a game unto itself. Construct a ship by taking your command module and attaching blocks to it. Armor blocks, engines, power generators, harvesters, automated turrets, manual fire weapons, missile launchers, drone launchers, etc. Then press the test button and watch the AI duke it out in an arena against 5 different ships with similar construction cost in 1 vs 1 battles. It will highlight strengths & weaknesses... and it'll give you more information on how to further tweak the perfect ship.

I think I spent over half of my time with this game in the ship builder and battle simulator. It's just so much fun!\t\t\t\t\t
