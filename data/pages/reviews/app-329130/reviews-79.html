\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is a game that takes place in a genre of space games that allows you to create your own ships and use them to fight other ships. The main difference is, Reassembly does this is a very unique and different way. You have full control of what you ship, or station, will look like, except for the manditory command module. You can use a variety of parts for all of the playable factions and many modded factions. You can build a fleet to your specifications and can use it to conquer the entire universe that was generated for your faction to reside in. Each faction has its own special parts with their own specifications aswell as their own types of weaponry.

(Note: These numbers I am using are not based on faction id's in the Sandbox.)
WARNING: SOME PEOPLE MAY CONSIDER THIS NEXT PART AS A HUGE SPOILER. DO NOT READ IF KNOWING THIS INFORMATION SPOILS THE GAME FOR YOU. Faction 1 "The Terrans: The starting faction, it is very well balanced and generally simple to use. Faction 2 "The Farmers": Largest variety in parts, this faction tends to behave neutrally, but can be very deadly later on. This faction is known for its seed planter. Faction 3 "The Reds": This faction has the weakest hull armor. They do however, have the most parts on their ships, due to their blocks not being able to scale. This faction also tends to have the most weapons on their ships, and very good point defense. Faction 4 "The Tinkrell": This faction has customizable weaponry, making them have the ability to obtain the most powerful weapons in the game. Faction 5 "The Crystalline ships": This faction has very strong hull armor and some of the most powerful plasma cannons in the game. Faction 6 "The bees": This faction has very powerful lasers, and the most powerful thrusters in the game. Faction 7 "The Sentinels": This faction is well know for its nukes and obliterator burst lasers, a certain type of part can even used for melee damage.

Pros:
-Massive level of creativity able to be put into your playthrough.
-Many ways to play.
-Variety of factions to play as.
-Many ships to fight.
-Very active workshop.
-Great community and fan base.

Cons:
-Their is a P limit based on the command module, amount of thrusters, and amount of weapons the ship has.
-Their is a block limit based on the amount of blocks your ship has.

Reassembly tends to the needs of players who like combat, building, or both. Their are many different ways to fight, just like their are many different ways to build.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a solid and fun game that combines space shooter elements with a sand box and ship design. The fact that you can both upload your designs to appear in other players' universes and encounter theirs as roaming and often deadly 'agents' along with the game's 7 inherent playable factions (there are some non-playable ones as well) plus workshop support make this game a lot of fun.

You start with a simple small ship but you can unlock both more systems to build with and larger ships via combat and resource gathering and can unlock the other factions via battle so you can try their unique playstyles in future games. The best part of the game's factions is that while they all have some similiarities, they are quite diverse and not simply reskins of each other. Playing the starting Terran faction is quite different from the Reds which are different from the Sentinels and so on.

You eventually get to build ships from your ship and can keep them with you or release them and they'll defend areas as your control expands. Your AI allies will even build copies of your ship and you can use the interface to affect their production queues.

You also have a lot of control options and can even set how your weapons are keyed so you can fire every weapon via the LMB or put even big cumbersome weapons on point defense duty if you like. There's a lot to love here.

Heartfelt recommendation from me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Amazing unscripted top-down space combat/expansion indie game with great community and plethora of quality mods to add content to game. If you liked SPAZ or Starsector, you’ll LOVE this game!

For a game made by one guy, heck, even compared to other indie games with teams of developers, Reassembly ranks up with there with my favorites. Reassembly can be compared to Space Pirates and Zombies and Starsector, 2 other top-down space exploration/combat games; however, Reassembly has them both beat in customizability, unscripted expansion/development of the player faction, and ambience. The gameplay is easy to get into and gets as complex as your creativity can make it, yet still retains simplicity which makes it easy and fun to sit there creating a large complex spaceship tailored to your desires. As you expand and grow your faction, your AI will begin building ships you design for them and in order to expand more you could employ many ways to take over sectors of the galaxy. From bringing a fleet of ships to having a carrier with masses of drones or even piloting a singular dreadnought to carve through enemy territory, or even coming up with your own tactics as you can create any kind of ship you want block by block (even copy pasting big chunks of ships to create modular designs) the designs are limitless!

As for quantity of content, the base factions are interesting but when they get bland the developer made Reassembly open source allowing anyone to easily create their own factions or even universe altering mods and upload them for anyone to download and play from the Steam Workshop. The community, while small, is one of the nicest and most dedicated I’ve seen, and you’ll definitely never run out of quality new factions to download and try out or play against. This includes mods that change the entire galaxy to a star wars themed galactic war to mods that create a 2 faction war between humans and zombies in space, to... well just check the Workshop and you’ll see all the amazing stuff going on!

P.S. Nominated this game for the "Just 5 more minutes" Steam award because I've definately played this instead of slept despite having class in the morning.\t\t\t\t\t
