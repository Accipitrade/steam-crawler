\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is an outstanding game, I highly recomend it. Collect resources, buy new parts, design your ship to it's full potential, take over new territory, unlock new factions, and the process repeats itself.

But each time you play there are new challenges and new ways to play, offered by the seven diverse factions included in the game, and the thousands of workshop mods and contributions. The graphics are incredible and really bring the best out of a 2D game, offering vibrant and extensively customisable colour schemes. Arguably the game's best feature, however, is the stunning ship design platform that far out-plays any other vehicle design I've ever seen in a game, allowing you to place, rotate, and resize each individual block of each individual ship that you build. The way you build your ships determine how well they will do in the diversity of space, having to tough out aggressive forests of spiky plants, angered factions, explosive rocks and swarms of teleporting drones.

If you are particularly happy with a fleet you have created, you can pitch them up against your steam friends' fleets in heated Tournament mode, or even export them and send them to the workshop for anyone to try out.

The soundtrack is also very nice, fitting nicely in the background of the game. Whether you are battling a fleet of crystalline ships or spending time tweaking your mothership's design, the music creates a simple yet entertaining space themed aesthetic to suit the game perfectly. (plus you can buy it separatley as DLC; if it's DLC worthy then it's gotta be good m79)

Despite the single-player gem of a game this is, many people including me have been asking for a multiplayer mode for a L O N G time, so if you are thinking that this is a space-themed MMO, you've come to the wrong place. There also haven't been many major updates of the game for a while, so I think the devs are happy with the game for now; don't expect any major new content being released.

If you want to see some gameplay, I recomend a youtuber called Deluks Gaming, he does very good Let's Plays that teach you the basics of the game (plus many tournament videos) and can give you a feel for it and whether it's a good game for you or not.

The game I think does take a while to get hold of, despite that you can never lose and there are infinite save files for you, and I think it attracts a particular audience, which is why it isn't a lot more popular than it currently is. Hopefully this has been helpful, I really can't stress how good the game is; take advantage of the sales and get it while it's cheaper ;)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game rocks! I think I bought it in a bundle not knowing what it was.

It's really cool because you start out with these little ships that you can keep upgrading and building up. As you fly around the galaxy blowing ♥♥♥♥ up you can use the raw materials you collect to upgrade your ship.

Eventually you can get a factory on your ship and you can use raw materials to build more ships until you have a massive armada that will rain terror (or in my case, missiles) down on all who oppose you!

Then there are wormholes so you can go to other galaxies and do the same thing.

Then, after playing it like 8 solid hours I discovered that there are other factions to unlock which have totally different modules and weapons for the ships!

Cool game, only downside I see is no multiplayer? Although in a way you can interact with other players' ships by uploading your fleet. I occaisionally ran into a fleet that another friend of mine on steam had made.

Ah, well. It's definitely worth a try for the price.\t\t\t\t\t
