\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA pretty fantastic game, that is just a bit to heavy on the sandbox side for me. I should clarify, that I just figured I would try this game out last night, since it's been sitting in my library for ahwile. It was almost everything that I hoped it would be:



The Pro's


Awesome space battles, intuitive, fun, chaotic and beatifully designed

Easy ship editor, that is only mildly frustrating to use at times

Interesting factions, and well thought out gameplay

Massive universe: really captures the space adventure feel

Cool faction system



Con's

No goals or mission objectives. For some this is not a con, and I can appreciate this style of pure sandbox gameplay where the goal is to get a bigger ship to destroy bigger enemies, so you can get a bigger ship. However, I usually like my games to have some sort of goal to work towards, otherwise after awhile I lose interest.

There are Bosses, but I haven't had a "fight for my life" or a "holy Sh** that ship is huge! retreat" moment

Ship builder could use some refinement, nothing major, but it can be frustrating

While the world is vast, the terrain isn't very varried.



I started playing around 8:00PM yesterday and binged straight through the night. I went to bed for a few hours around 6am, then woke up and went straight back to it. It felt really great to have that "addicted" feeling in a game, I haven't experienced that in ahwile. That being said, In that 9-13 hours, I feel like I have experienced most of what the game has to offer. There are other factions to explore and unlock (I think I unlocked 4 or 5 during time played so far) but after ahwile without a purpose and when you've built your big bad mothership, you start to wonder what to do next.

Some people were complaining about the grindy nature of the game, and actually I felt that was done quite well. I felt the grind relaxing, and I was always eager to find new enemies, get more resources, and upgrade my ship. I would love to see some DLC that add's proper bosses, Missions, new terrains, new factions, some sort of primitive economy, trading, selling, bounty hunting, different way's to mine, some sort of story line, or anything that adds more of a reason for me to actually *want* a bigger ship. As it stands it is fun to create a bigger fleet just for the fun of it, but the pressure of a more well defined enemy, or additional base mechanics could add a whole new level to gameplay. I really think this game had the potential to be the next terraria, in that the gameplay is a blast, and it's also a unique experience. It's old so I am not hoping for updates, but would gladly pay for some DLC or a sequel.


I think 15 dollars is a bit pricey, considering that after this binge I will likely not pick the game up again, but I just paid 13.75 to go to the movies the other day for 1.5 hours of so/sso entertainment. 15.00 for 9-13 hours of solid entertainment is a good value in my book.

If the dev's are out there, for the love of god make a sequel. Consider adding some sort of endgame, and you'd have a real winner.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf this game would have received a little bit more work from the dev it would be perfect.
What is there is pretty good but also gets dull rather rapidly. I particulary like the graphical style with the intense colors and the kind of 80s computer games like presentation. The designs of the enemy ships are also very cool. From space-bees to space-plants, the factions both playable and unplayable have unique and cool designs. The game shines when you stumble into big conflicts between multiple factions. Its all pretty blood-dragon-hotline-miami-esque if that makes sense :D Or if you are as ancient as me it might remind you of standing in an actual arcade. Only presentationwise though. The scale of the universe and amount of stuff going on on screen is pretty huge.

The ship building is very enjoyable and a lot deeper than I initialy thought. From gunships to carriers to "ramming-devices", there are a lot of feasable designs you can go with. Different factions will also have different equipment.

My two problems with the game are that, for one, while you can test your ship against other ships directly from the build menue, the battle is automated for some reason. This just seems odd. Also the game picks random ships that have a comparable cost to yours. Since I'm not into multiplayer I would preferre it if you could just fight one on one battles agains the AI with a ship of your choosing (Like fight your old ship with your new ship)
While the former is more a nice to have, the end game is where the real problem is. You "can" conquer the universe in this which at first glance seems to be the endgoal of the game. The idea being that you can create stations that in turn can create more ships. In practice this means that you just dump out at least one station in every sector and then move on. There is not really any strategy involved in it. Nor is it interesting. Personally I just discovered the universe and then got bored. It would have been cool if the "empire building" was a lot more fleshed out than it actually is. It seems to be just tagged on and is ultimately boring. Maybe if you're a 100% completion guy that still appeals to you, for me personally the game ended after I discovered everything once. It has been a while since I played but I believe the game is already complete. While it has mod support, I don't imagin core game mechanics are something that is exposed and changeable by mods. It is therefor likely that the basebuilding aspect of the game can be considered final. This is a real shame. In my opinion the game could be interessting for manytimes as long if that would have been done a little better.
At any rate for the price you can expect 20-30 Hours of cool exploration and unlocking higher and higher ship classes. Then it ends anticlimacticly when you discover that you could conquer the universe now but it would also be pretty dull.
Still if the game seems at all interessting to you from the trailers you should get it. Its uniquely pretty and pretty unique.\t\t\t\t\t
