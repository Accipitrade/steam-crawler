\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou build ships, fly around, blow things up, and suck up space plants for money. A very good time waster.

The ship construction is very intricate. You build ships brick by brick, and tock by tick, just like Legos.
Although I like it, usually I just try to find the base models of ships and modify those.

It's also very easy to make mods, and there are quite a few good ones in the Workshop. There's even a helpful program by a guy named TTFTCUTS, located here: http://atg.lychnobi.com/ttftcuts/rwdk/

The soundtrack is very relaxing, and I'd recommend getting the OST. Be warned, though, you don't get it in MP3 format, so you might want to (or have to) convert it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassmebly is a remarkably...unique...game. There isn't necessarily much to do, just explore, kill things, get money, get bigger. Same as a vast amount of other games. There is something about it though that's just SO ADDICTIVE!! With 172 hours clocked up overall I just keep coming back to it. Itll get boring after a while, of course, but then you get nostalgic for it and do the whole thing over again.

EVERY time you play is completely different. Whether it's something as seemingly insignificant as your shipshape (yeah I noticed what I did), or something as big as other players fleets invading your game and, 9 times out of 10, giving you hell, there's always new ways to play and experience the endless, yes ENDLESS (provided you go through wormholes), reaches of space.

Overall I HIGHLY recommend this game, even if just as a pastime. Might not necessarily seem fun to some of you but blink and hours have passed.



Only a few things I'd recommend for the devs to...tweak:

1) Don't make space so bland!! Where are all the nebula and black holes and planets and stars and all the beauty that makes space sooooooo interesting and [welcoming?]!! Even if just in the background it would be great to see something other than darkness.

2) Make drones mean something!! When only effective when ALL YOUR POINTS are thrown into drone launchers, there's no room for being able to combine them with other weapons. Except as a distraction they're practically useless.

3) Why not add some of the most popular mod factions into the game itself? (Disclosures included of course). The base factions are great...at first....but when you know exactly what their strengths and weaknesses are you start designing your ships the same way all the time, thus contradicting one of my previous pros.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is a great little game that is reinforced by mods and the community around it. It's a pretty simple game, where you build spaceships, blow up other spaceships for currency that you then use to build a bigger, better space ship. You start out as a tiny fighter, but by the end you will be controlling a 100-ship strong fleet to take over other galaxies.

Since every ship, station and asteroid is made out of different shaped parts, each individual part has it's own health and can be destroyed. Because of this, when building your own ship there is a ton of strategy involved as you can't just slap parts together and hope that it works. There are hull parts, which are just basic blocks to use as armour or to get the right look on your ship, Thrusters, which you need to have all around your ship so that you can turn, reverse, brake etc and weapon/utility parts which can be something from a homing missile launcher to a shield generator to protect your ship. Each part requires uses up a certain amount of power and you can increase your total power by upgrading it.

When you destroy an enemy, explore a new area of the map or re-claim a damaged station you get some of the games currency. You use said currency to do one of three things: Unlock new parts, which allows you to put an infinite number of said part on your ship as long as you don't go over your power limit, Hire ships to follow you around and join your fleet, or increase your power limit. You can also get 'resources' from a destroyed ship or a few other things which you can turn into currency by going to a station and trading with it, or spend to build a ship if you have a factory attached.

There are multiple factions that are all randomly placed in the 100 x 100 or so grid map, which loops around if you go of one side. The factions do vary, and if you destroy a ship worth 1,000 currency from a faction you will unlock them to play as in a new game, instead of the default Terran faction. Factions will expand by them self, destroying ships of your faction and others to build more ships and stations for them self. Most of them will attack you on sight, but there is one faction that is passive and just harvests resources, making them a good early-game target to grow stronger fast. When you design a ship, your own faction will also build said ship every now and again so you can end up with a huge fleet of just your ships if you want.

There isn't really any end to reassembly, but there is a sort-of end goal to try and achieve. Conquer the galaxy. If you can explore every grid of the map, and wipe out every other faction from it, you will get an achievement and the game is essentially over. There are wormholes, however, that bring you to a brand-new galaxy so you can start all over but with a huge ship and a fleet that you brought with you. If you do this, your ship and fleet will appear in another players world as an AI controlled boss type of thing.

All in all, reassembly is a great game that can last for a very, very long time, especially if your into modding or just installing mods, and is a ton of fun no matter how many hours you've played for.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best part about Reassembly is not the building, but the environment you are presented with. While admittedly, the AI needs some serious polish, it behaves in a way that you don't see in games of a similar genre. Rather than it's goal simply being "Kill the player", it actually appears to be "Reproduce and Survive". Almost everything is dynamic, including the flora (It actually reproduces). This game gives the impression of a living, and breathing organism, constantly in flux (It is worth mentioning that nothing is simulated outside of your "vision", which is for obvious optomization reasons). I love watching smaller ships collect resources from plants (Once they or their seeds die, they turn into 'Resource'), and larger ships acting as predators. Anywho, while many aspects are very, very, rough around the edges, I would seriously recommend this game for any fan of the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
