\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou can tell the developers put lots of effort into the game. It's simple to learn (although the controls are not intuitive at first, give it time), and it can be as mindless or as strategic as you want to make it. The building aspect is a simple point and click system with the ability to copy large portions, mirror them, and place on the opposite side of your ship. Space battles are just good oldfashioned blow-crap-up fun. Getting destroyed by a difficult faction or fleet just gives you motivation to improve your ship building skills and battle strategies. And the music... Absolutely immersive, and is a decent jam while blowing ships into smitherines. I highly recommend the DLC soundtrack. i cant imagine playing the game without it now that I look back. The only negative I can see to this game is the fact that it does tend to go into a lag fest when you enter a bee infested or thorn infested territory. In those situations, the lag is manageable (it feels a bit like slow mo, so get ready to fight those battles reaaally slow). Otherwise, avoid those territories and that problem never occurs. One of the reasons I bought this game is to get my spaceship building fix, while still being playable on a non-gaming laptop. Most games of that genre are highly demanding and don't run well unless you have capable hardware. If you are in a similar situation, look no farther than Reassembly. Runs nicely on crap - medium grade hardware.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game a veeeeeery long time ago (it was still in early access if I remember well), and it is still by far my favorite once-in-a-while game. When I'm not sure what to do, I launch Reassembly and play for a perfectly undetermined amount of time.

Reassembly is the perfect relaxation game. The music is both calm and upbeat, making the transitions between travelling, gathering and fighting almost seemless. Vector graphics also help to the surreal feel, and they allow the game to run very smoothly on most computers (as long as you keep away from the damn spiky plants, spamming projectiles until your computer fries). Fighting is also a spam-festival, but somehow keeps your game running good (and there is never FPS drop: only a physical simulation slowdown), and it is just exciting enough to be fun without making you hyper-active like playing Quake on accelerated speed after injection of pure Montain Dew essence. Death happens a lot, but isn't too punishing most of the time, and you usually tell yourself "oh, well" and keep going. Building is complex enough to make you activate a few brain cells without frying them, and permissive enough to allow for some beautifully shaped motherships,or if you are like me, spamming of pulse lasers to make an one-shot cube. Speaking of absurd powerful weapons, The tech system (a.k.a factions, unlocking weapons and upgrading your ship) is diverse and smooth enough to keep you going for quite some time.

My personnal recommandation is to spam the same mid or end-tier weapon to make your gunships, except for the tinkrel (purple guys) able to make their own weapons: here just make a fat-ass death-pouring valve, it's hilarious. And of course never forget the anti-missile cannons.

My personnal favourite faction is the Red, because the lack of shields (hardest faction, by the way) forces you in making a swarm of well-designed ships, usually quite cheap and fast to produce. Unless you try to attack a ridiculous player-made gunship, few fleets can compete with a 100 ships army.

The only complain I have is the lack of true multiplayer: you can see other player-made fleets in your world (agents), but that's it.

Otherwise, I highly recommend getting this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIntroduction
The moment your given control of your ship in this game a terse tutorial quickly shows you the controls and basic mechanics. Then it tells you to kill everyone who is colored differently from you, that's racism. Or is it spacism? Since it's in outer space... I think... for all I know it feels like your a bunch of bacteria fighting for control of someone's stomach. Either way, Reassembly delivers an enjoyably modular destruction sim where you are the only person in your entire race who has something very special, initiative.


TL;DR
+++ Mods
+++ Shooting pieces off of ships
++ Ship Building (Way better than any other I've seen out there IMO)
++ Pretty visuals
+ GENOCIDE!!! (pretending your Alexander the Great!)
- Genocide (knowing your actually like Hitler)
- AI isn't great (especially if you have followers)
- Vanilla game has a total point-cap of 8000 (there is mods that "fix" this)
-- Taking territory can become grindy
--- Lack of reward for taking territory


GAMEPLAY

The game plays like a twin-stick shooter in that the default controls have you aim and shoot with the mouse and control your thrusters with WASD. The controls have several different configurations and can be rebinded if desired. I've found the default controls to be sufficient.

The game starts you off by choosing one of the several factions (more if you have mods) and plops you in some friendly territory equipped with the outer-space (or inner-gastric, I don't know) equivalent of a dingy. You then must use your meager might to collect purple floaty-things called "resources" (creative huh?) to trade in at friendly factories for "credits" (again, the innovation!). The resources usually grow near plants attached to asteroids/food-globs found throughout the galaxy/stomach. You can also get resources by killing black/white/red/yellow/etc colored ships. You will then use the credits you get from trading (aka, going near any friendly factory) to purchase new parts and a higher point-cap (lets you add more pieces) for your ship.

About half of your time spent in Reassembly will be in the ship designer. It's quite easy to use once you get the hang of it. Everything you would expect to need for a ship is present here (as long as you've unlocked the right parts). You build around your core (the part that you should be protecting) with pieces ranging from armor, to point-defence, to generators, to drone-launchers. What is it with games nowadays and drones? Well, just like real life drones these ones break really easily. Unlike real life drones you can keep spamming them out and overwhelm your enemies.

One of the problems with the game is that the ship builder only lets you upgrade you point-cap to 8000. This prevents you from building those giant ships shaped like various things. However, don't fret, there are more than a couple mods that rectify this horrendous hindrance to holocaustic, wholesale, hellenistic, Hitlerism.

Another thing this game gets wrong is that it presents you with a daunting task of occupying every sector of the galaxy/gut with your color. This requires exterminating every single thing that isn't your color. Some would say this exceeds Mount and Blade: Warband levels of grindy conquering. Plus there are wormholes ({sarcastically} wormholes, riiiiggghhhht) scattered through the galaxy/gastric that urge you to go to a different world entirely (or upload the designs you've built).

Despite these flaws, the game excels in letting you shoot everything and watch it break. It is immensely satisfying to watch an enemy capitalship get it's primary weapons turned offline as you separate them from the main body with a giant cutting laser, or any other of the MANY weapons to be found both in the vanilla game and mods.


Story

Once upon a time...
life evolved/someone swallowed
into many races/some bacteria
that fought for control
of the galaxy/of the abdomen
I'm just kidding, there is no story past what you imagine.


Graphics

The graphics in this game are highly modifiable and can be absolutely beautifully (I'm talking like, epileptic levels of lights, shaders, blooms, etc.) or look like a flash game for those of you using potatoes. But hey, that's still fun. I myself played at potato level for several months after a hard drive failure {shudder}.


Conclusion

All in all, this game is definitely worth your time. I bought it at full price, but you could wait for a sale (not that I've ever seen one for this). Even if you just have 15 minutes, Reassembly can satisfy that modular ship shooting itch with it's varied factions, massive possibilities and amazing ship builder. So get this game go have fun taking over the galaxy/belly!\t\t\t\t\t

It's fun, but it has limitations, such as the whole universe and world not being constantly active, leading you to go to every place to clear it out yourself, rather than do it Sim Ant style, and build a fleet so powerful, that your allies jsut take everything over through sheer attrition.
Other limitations include:
- A VERY limited Tournament system, only allowing one fleet to engage another person's single fleet. Not one fleet from one save, or faction. One fleet PERIOD. Also you can't manually control ships either in PvP combat, so that's extremely lame.
- No Co-op, which this game could badly use on the "Conquer everything" achievement, which requires you to have a ship in every quadrant, which.... good luck, there's over 2000 of the things and hte map only updates in proximity of another player, let alone triggers and moves normally. Going fast enough can make it painfully obvious, which is possible to do without any mods btw.
- The 8000 Ship size limit is fine, but when you're grinding up to that point (which IS a grind with some factions, with others, it's just plain tedious in every way) the imagination tends to run wild with about a dozen or more ideas that aren't possible without using mods.
- Agents (user-submitted fleets controlled by AI only) sometimes don't spawn correctly and you gotta regen your world to get them to spawn correctly. This doesn't stop Agents from killing stations though, since, if Agents fought any of MY stations, none of them would survive, especially with my terran faction that abuses missiles (Hint: Lasers eventually do nothing)
- Some weapons are just straight-up overpowered if spammed, whilst other weapons are jsut pretty much useless. Case and Point: Photon Lances, Missiles and Drones are 3 overpowered-when-spammed weapons. Everything literally pales in comparison. Missiles can overpowerd and overwhelm literally everything.
- Railguns are useless for the most part, which is sad because Railguns are 50 times more powerful than any lasers, missiles (save for nukes) or fighter/bomber in any amounts irl. Hint: Because Railguns are literally matter shot at the speed of light, or atleast faster than the speed of sound, which is still deadly on that level.
- AI doesn't redesign ships to counter your powerful warships, as a result, you can eventually design ships that nothing has an answer for, at which point the game's no longer challenging, which wears out the fun pretty quickly after a point. Case and Point: Terran Ships literally made up of a couple of shield generators, and literally a hundred missile launchers.
- You can't stop the game from Auto-pausing without pausing, then unpausing as you immediately tab out. Reason why you'd want to do this: You may have a ship that revolves around puking out space stations for the purpose of just flooding the universe with stations that can also slowly puke out ships on it's own.

The game's more or less a 7/10. Good, but not great. Plenty of flaws.\t\t\t\t\t
