\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is a beautiful, enthralling top-down spaceship-builder/space combat game with sturdy mechanics and an intuitive building system that deserves the praise it has recieved.

The basic building blocks are two-dimensional shapes (guns, hull blocks, engines, and so on,) which are a joy to connect and shape into fighters, freighters, capital ships and stations. As the player becomes more experienced, new shapes unlock, leading to some truly impressive creations. What's more, the fleets that other players have made are imported onto the map as 'Agents', meaning that, in addition to the various types of standard enemies, the player has to go up against the creations of fellow players - controlled by the AI, but built by humans. Some of the are quite brilliant.

Unlocking the most expensive pieces can be a grind, and the game doesn't tutorialise nearly as well as it should (there is a fair bit of advice online), but after many hours I still find plenty to keep me busy.

And if you ever encounter one of my capital ships, here's a tip: keep astern. For the life of me, I can't seem to design a ship with a decent turning speed.\t\t\t\t\t
