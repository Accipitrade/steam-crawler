\r\n\t\t\t\t\t2 hours in and I knew it was a great game, 15 hours in I had to tell others about it! So here I am.

A 2D top down space shooter whilst accurate, is a poor description for this game as there is much more to it than that. The graphics although basic are perfect for the game. The sound is also well chosen.

You start in a pre-designed ship but as time goes on you can add to the design or just create a new one from scratch. There are a decent variety of weapons and other modules to choose from, like rockets, missiles, torpedoes, mines, lasers, projectile based weapons and crazier ones. Your ships can also house a factory to build other ships on the fly, which in turn can have a factory and can build. With a bit of time you can have a fleet of AI controlled ships following you around until you dismiss them to do their own thing, and they do. AI ships not under your control will continue to gather resources and contribute to your factions efforts. Time to colonise and control the galaxy!

The map is large, and the factions varied, although I am now starting to get slightly bored of the existing factions, but there are many more in the Steam Workshop.

My only criticisms of the game are as follows; Agents, which are player made ships that spawn into the game and severely ruin your day, are often too good and numerous when they first appear. You could ignore them until stronger, however once you give them a foothold in the galaxy they are really hard to remove. In addition, you reach the endgame of progression in terms of unlocking blocks and maximum ship size too soon, normally after a couple of hours.

All being told, an excellent buy.

EDIT: Nominated it for the Steam Award “I Thought This Game Was Cool Before It Won An Award” Award.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou know, I've been waiting for a game like this to appear. I love space, spaceships, and I love games where there's something that lets my creativity run wild. Glad I found this gem!

This is a game where you control a single ship of faction you choose at the beginning of the game. You can customize it however you want it with the pieces the faction provides. Each faction is unique in a way and none are the same. You fly around, you shoot enemies, gather resources, unlock new modules, grow in power, build up your ship to become an unstoppable destroyer, unlock other factions to play as, and so on.

Of course you don't need to fly alone, you can build up your fleet, or hire pilots from your faction to join you and fly with it. You'll soon be able to conquer the entire galaxy. Also, the galaxy has a fair amount of 'agents', which are fleets that were made by other players. Most of the ones I've seen so far were pretty formidable so if you see a red arrow, you should run until you really feel confident you'll break their ships with ease. Oh, and if you're done playing in that one galaxy you started in? Use a wormhole to fly to another one! This will also upload your current fleet, and chances are, someone, somewhere, on planet Earth, will be soon encountering your very own fleet on their computer. And he'll probably have trouble destroying you.

What's truly beatiful about this game is how simple it looks, and how complex it actually is. You could actually consider this game some sort of art. You start in a tiny sector, and you can see pretty early on how dynamic the world is. The AI harvests the minerals, grows in size, expands, it gets better with you, and so on. For example, I once built a single ship that could build stations. I manufactured it from my flagship, kicked it from the fleet so it could be independent, and guess what. Soon enough, when I came back, there were like, 15 stations there, and a whole lot of tiny little fighter ships scuttling around. It was an awesome sight. To be honest, the game kinda looks more organic than scientific. It's like actual organisms battling against each other. Sort of.

Well, either way, if you like space, spaceships, and space battles, if you like being creative, if you like shooting stuff up, if you like seeing your ship grow from a super fast tickling drone to a slow, massive hulking beast of absolute annihilation, if you like sending your enemies to oblivion, then chances are you're going to appreciate this game. It's got a fair amount of replayability, and even though it looks simple, it's really lots of fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is, in a nutshell, spaceship editing and combat done right. The editor is a snap to do just about whatever you want to do. For example, you can copy your ship and stick the new one on the front of your original ship in less than 5 seconds if you're even halfway decent at using the keybindings. Another good thing is that you can change reassembly from a miminalistic game to a Tron-esque visual style, allowing you to run Reassembly on an old or new computer and use whatever RAM you have. And if you're STILL not convinced, it has mods, adding new factions with surprisingly well-written lore and enormous assortments of weapondry. Some even add entirely new block shapes (sounds unimpressive, but just try to build a functional spaceship out of pentagons) . Then there's the challenge faction mods, with oddly shaped blocks, low energy generation, and an array of other problems to deal with. Overall, Reassembly gets a 19/20. If you're on the fence about it, buy it on sale for the price of three chocolate bars. However, it's worth noting that it hasn't been updated in two years, and is probably not going to be updated again. Also, player fleets are hilariously broken, easily wiping out anything if built decently (read: with enough thrusters to move and a gun that isn't short-ranged) since they're either cheap enough to build an army, or powerful enough that only 3 or so annilate everything. And so far there aren't any mods that buff enemies enough for them to stand up to fleets, though at that point fleets would be mandatory. Melee damage (only with blocks that boost it) is also quite powerful, but balanced somewhat in that having enough maneuverability to counter melee blocks' mass is difficult. There also isn't a good endgame, as by the time you've built up your ship to the default 8000P (ship power, used for adding functional blocks to your ship) you're usually strong enough to blast through almost every faction alone. And then only one more ship turns the middle sectors from challenging to simple, especially if you specialise it. Despite this, it's still a fantastic game, but about 40% of that is the excellent mods. Grab a friend, each design a ship and send them into tournament mode for a interesting competition.\t\t\t\t\t
