\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is almost perfect.

I just bought this game today but I've already had my share of experiences. The tutorial was a bit meh but after I got to the point where I could start exploring, the world opened up to me. While I wish that there was a little bit of written lore (an update would be nice!), the lore is almost self-explanatory simply based on what a faction's ships *do*. The mind wanders just you yourself wander, trying to infer whatever you can about this colorful, geometric universe.

The combat is good and the physics behind movement take a bit of getting used to. This game isn't as much about that, though. It's all about building your perfect modular ship. Currently I am rocking a missile dreadnaught, though it has a few weaknesses. There is no *best* kind of ship in the game.

I would highly reccomend this game, and would urge anyone to buy it even if it's not on sale.

Now, as for things that could directly be improved upon (either through modding, updates, or hell, I'm so pleased with the game I'd pay for DLC) I'd like to see even more modules (sometimes I feel like I want even bigger guns) more factions (I want to see more cool and outlandish ideas that either impressed upon me a sense of wonder or terror), and story-mode/objectives (Some kind of diplomacy or even flavor text would be a nice addition to the game). This would be the first game that I would have liked to have still been in early-access because of the idea of even more content, instead of groaning. And believe me, I know how bad it gets; I bought towns ;-;\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game. At first I was a little skeptical over the price, but I'd say I got plenty of enjoyment and my money's worth. I very, very reccomend this game.

This is one of those "Easy to learn, hard to master" games. At least for the first few hours. There is so much variety in just the starting faction's arsenal that you'll spend plenty of time exploring your strengths and weaknesses. Then, over time you'll create the perfect vessel, one that is a Jack of All Trades or a Master in a specific area. But it doesn't stop there. You can build a factory on your ship, and then spawn other ships you've made to help you fight. (Assuming you've got the resources) Then, those ships can have factories too.

Another great feature is how both friend and foe alike will utilize battlefield debries to improve their armor or weapons.

I like to play as a huge mother ship that is good at crowd control and sniping. So I make my ship's factory produce micro vessles I made, that pack a lot of fire power and speed at a cheap price. I even made a version that has it's own shields. They only cost about 300 RP, which means I can crank them out like nothing. I actually felt a sense of pride when I saw them descend upon the Bee faction and absolutely wrecked them. They would gang up, six to one, and blast them to pieces. Hell, I had so many of these drones that they'd just crash into their ships at insane speads, cleaving off parts. I was prouder still, when I saw my lil guys loot their victim's shield module and use it. So they had the yellow shields of their enemies and my blue shields. :') Papa is proud. Except for the one that picked up a massive thruster and then spiraled forever, completely combat ineffective.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know if I'm the only one who did this, so I ask: Did you like creating impressive, huge ships in Lego and make them battle (using your imagination, of course)? Well, now you can do it for real, on a game that actually creates the battles for you (Imagination is overrated). With no true plot (You are part of a faction, you battle every other faction. That's basically it.) and no central objective, Reassembly is just pure fun. You design your ship with parts that actually have a significant impact in how your ship moves, fires and defends, and you battle other ships, some even designed by other people.

I had a blast playing this game, and spent my whole day experimenting with different weapons and factions, balancing my fleet, spending credits to make my ship bigger and bigger, and dying because my ship was too fast and bumped against a wall, causing instant explotions. If that sounds fun, then by all means, play this game. You'll love it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReassembly is a fantastic game! It's a pretty looking, lego-like game, that lets you build your own ship from a measly fighter to an unstoppable jauggernaut capital ship. There are quite a few unique factions to unlock and build with that are all pretty fun to play as with their own special weapon types and building blocks.

Although I full heartedly reccommend this game I think it's important point out some gripes.

End Game Is kinda boring
-Reassembly is one of those games where you become so powerful that there simply isn't strong enough competition to personally test your work of art against. There are some fleets you can battle that can be difficult but often times they can be really easy.

Faction Economy and territyory system isn't fleshed out
-Gaining and losing territory seems very ambiguous and pointless beyond %100 conquering the map, which is tedious and time consuming. There doesn't seem to be any benefit of having territory. You're supposed to give resources to ship building factories that will then go out and gain territory, but this is kind of un-engaging past bringing the materials in.
-There aren't really any "capital" territories that faction forces are strongest at. Having these would bring in some depth to the world, adding difficulty and strategy to your campaign. Attack the capital of a faction and cripple it's economy so it can't reinforce other territories as easily. Some factions have cargo ships that don't really do anything but get killed by you for some free money. Giving them something to do that has benifits would be amazing for this game.

Beyond anything I've mentioned the game is a great time! If you're even slightly considering picking this up, do it! Especially if it's on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
