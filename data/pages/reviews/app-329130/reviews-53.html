For those who enjoy games which thrive off your own imagination, this game is amazing in its simplicity.

You build ships from a pallete of different blocks, thrusters and weapons.
Different factions have access to different block, weapon and thruster types.
The effectiveness of your ship will depend on how you've constructed it. Placing is everything.

Other player's fleets can arrive in your universe. BEWARE.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRecommending just for the concept. Being able to build your own ship completely from scratch using tons of different blocks is always fun. This works especially well this the game comes with mod support.

The cons...
As most people have complained controlling your ship is incredibly difficult. Especially once you start building your own ship or thruster start getting blown off.

The game has a steep learning curve. You are given a few base blueprints but almost immediately will be adding to these bases which you're absolutely going to mess up. I guess a lot of the fun is learning what works anyway?

The blocks don't always fit together. It's important in ship design to try to keep your ship compact and as connected as possible. Unfortunately, block shapes often make this impossible. You'll be fighting with angles and tiny gaps frequently unless you stick to square blocks which limits your functionality.

Agents are a pro and a con. They're better than you, and you'll find this out quickly. They're designed by people with the same max ship size as you, but with a far better understanding of ship design, alongside a quickly regenerating fleet. It's a challenge but often frustrating.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI found this game to be very good. It's fascinating with the simple graphics that gets combined into such weird things as the game progresses.

You harvest "space flowers" and trade up. Your ship is entirely of your own design limited only by the current max threshold, but that will rise when you get more experience. At one point you can make bad ass battleships that can produce other ships, of your choosing. Suddenly you swing along not-so-empty space with a whole armada of minions helping you.

After the ship designing, the best feature of the game is the regeneration. Say you dive into an enemy nest and after a while you realize that this will not work, then you escape and the ship will slowly regenerate as if nothing happened.

And the music is awesome. It really captures the feeling of being alone in the big black space, and it also captures the feeling of "things are heating up, I better watch out" and so on.

I learnt the game from this guy: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=swxfEZJtZAk

He also runs Reassembly tournaments where normal to super-huge custom designs of the different races fight each other in 1-on-1 sandboxes. Worth watching even if you don't play the game. https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL8vKf_mXH-u7A8xd-NghdMRaeNCkVEJBm\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the best 2D non-economic space wars game I've ever seen. Especially if its on sale you must get this game.

The amazing thing is it's simplicity. R is the only resource, P is the only limit on ship size, and C is your only upgrade/tech point. Collect R (either find it or kill enemies and steal it from them), carry R back to base to get C, then spend C to increase P and unlock modules. Then rebuild your ship as desired. Perfect. Oh, and all ships in the game heal themselves slowly over time which keeps it action-oriented (at least when you're not trying to improve your ship design).

There's actually a little more to it than that, such as the ability to command fleets of ships and use a factory module on your ship to build more ships, but even without that it's fun as hell. After 7 hours I haven't even gotten into that part yet and I'm already amazed.

The graphics are very minimalist vector style but still kind of flashy with all the particle effects. No complaints there. And the music is good too.

So don't buy this expecting a detailed economic simulation with space trading, a storyline, technological realism, or any of that stuff. It's quite abstract, but it's the best 2D space wars-like game I've ever seen and I haven't even touched on all of the game mechanics or multiplayer yet. The guys who wrote space war for the PDP-1 in 1962 would approve.\t\t\t\t\t
