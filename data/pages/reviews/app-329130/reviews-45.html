\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI usually never write reviews, but, Reassembly is one of the few games I give a hugely positive review on.


While sandbox-style of games may not fit and or belong to everyone, to people who do enjoy taking the time to build (And fly), this game is amazing.
The game by itself is good, as you can either play the slow expansion way as your faction's control grows, or you can play the agent-hunter and wormhole way, racking up credits at break-light speeds. All the factions themselves have their own uniqueness. As playing every faction the same way will never work.

The Terrans (The starting faction) are a basic race that introduce you to the game with basic missiles, drones, basic point defense, basic lasers, and basic projectile weapons. The Terrans also do have unique weapons, such as the plasma mortar (One of the few weapons that explodes into shards), charge lasers, and dumb-fire rockets and torpedoes. The Terran hulls are quite strong, as combined with their shields, can survive extremely well provided you design your ship decently.

The Farmers are a long ranged race, that can have specialized brawler ships. Their basic hull is...... well, not weak (Reds...), but nothing compared to Terran and Sentinel hull. Though their half-block armor block that can grow plants (The best armor-per-space block) can make any ship much more survivable. Their short ranged weapons are quite weak (Besides their lasers which are decent), and should be reserved for point defense, with the exception of their short ranged AOE weapons which can easily annihilate even the biggest of ships with ease. Though their long ranged weapons and missiles are, without a doubt, some of their greatest weapons. Along side their shields, they are one of the best ranged race in the game, well, long ranged brawler.

Ah, the Reds. While this race is absolutely brutal to beginners (Yes, I understood your pain), they can be one of the most powerful race in the game. A well-built Red ship can be extremely powerful against ships with no AOE or decent firepower. Their hull...... eh, can we even call it hull? It's more like connecting pieces to your guns and ship systems. Though having the hull be there to protect you is very important. Red ships are focused on short ranged DPS battles. As most Red ships, with their Tempests guns, will utterly burst down any armored-low damage ships. Their lasers are also good with their damage, though they lack range. Their drones are utterly terrible, due to their drones not having a turret compared to the Bee Laser Drone. Though their Nano Repair Drone is very useful to help the Red already quick regenerating hull regenerate even quicker (Along with their Tempest spam).

The Tinkrells. The most utterly broken faction in the game. If you wanted to snipe, and one shot a enemy agent or station from over 5000+ range away, Tinkrells are for you. Their modular spinal cannon can potentially be one of the most powerful weapons in the game. As you can increase the max range, the damage, fire rate, and even velocity with certain modules. Their point defense lasers are also absurdly good in mass, being able to stop utter swarms of missiles and drones (Though their small point defense laser sometimes gets out ranged by drones). Combining this with shields, they have decent survivability. Though their only weak point is the insane need for generators, and their weak hull. Though the Tinkrell are a swarming faction, as they, along with the Farmers, are the only race that can produce their own R.

The Crystalline. If Reds had missiles and usable hull. The Crystalline are either known for their insane missile spam, second only to the Farmers, or their brute strength, arguably better or on par with Sentinels. You should never underestimate their main weapons. Though they do have a weakness against fast missiles and drones as their point defense is a little on the lacking side. The Crystalline are probably one of the best brawlers in the game, and the second best missile spam race.

The Bees. The race of shields and deadly spinal lasers. The Bees have some of the best thrusters, able to zoom by areas in a blink of an eye. Their shields are also quite good, with good size and health. The Bees have two drones, a defense drone meant to immobilize smaller ships, and two large variants of a laser drone that do extremely good damage. The only problem is the cost of the drones. Though their point defense is quite good, the actual damage against ships is actually terrible. Their main damage dealing method is charging at enemies with their spinal lasers (Which deal a STUPID, yes a STUPID amount of damage). Though the range is the biggest downfall of the spinal lasers, forcing you to close in. Their natural buildings also produce R, but at a rate that isn't useful in normal gameplay.

The Sentinels. Without a doubt one of the most powerful races in the game. Ever wanted to play a race where you stayed in the middle of a entire warzone and came out fine? Well, the Sentinels armor will not let you down. 1000 health in a 1x1 square for 1 P is insanely good. Even if you don't want to spend P, their second level hull is still very good. Their armored spike also gives them the infamous melee spinner (Or as we call them, the Bade Blade of Death). Though their weapons are usable, both the turrets struggle with shields. Their spinal mounted laser though is meant to help burst down shields, and damage key systems before the shields come back up. Or you could just overpower their shields with enough guns...
The Sentinels also have two 'super weapons'. Both cost a arm and a leg to buy, and a limb to use on your ship, but they are both extremely powerful. The Obliterator is as if you took their spinal laser, put it on a turret, and gave it the power to punch through shields, and at times snipe command modules. The...... well, Nuke is a absolute pain to any unshielded ship (And frankly, all ships that don't have the biggest Farmer shield, the best Tinkrell shield, and the Terran Station Shield). One shoting Prometheus's from the Crystalline is always fun, though being sniped by agent's nuke isn't so much.


The natural spawning factions aren't much of a threat (Except maybe some Tinkrell ships, the Crystalline, and Sentinel), though the agent fleets may, at times, be extremely difficult. Sentinel spinner fleets and Tinkrell fleets especially. Though fighting them and beating them after a long fight feels extremely good, especially when fighting ships that look and preform well.

And being a sandbox, you can either go with a box with a massive arsenal of guns and shields, or be creative in fleet designing. Either way is fine with me......



Though, lastly, if you buy this game, enjoy being Reassembled!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is so simple. Throw some shapes on a grid to make a ship, add guns. Blow up orange guys who die with a single missile. You just run around and spam missiles at everything, which most of the time they don't track good enough to actually land a hit, except on the slow orange guys. The rest of the guns never have the range because all the enemies kite you. So in my opinion the missile is the only way to go. Maybe a tough laser in case something wants to randomly bump into you. That means there is really only 2 weapons in the game.

Eventually, you run around until you find a wormhole. You enter it, only to realize you just don't have the desire to clear another map.

10 hours of game time and that is giving it too much time. Half of those hours were just testing my new ship design in the battle simulator. I have no desire to load it again.

Don't be fooled by the high review percentage. The same crowd that loves Geometry Dash loves Reassembly. That would be ones who need flashy lights to entertain themselves. Anybody else will hate it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan, Reassembly is great. I love the music, I love the ship building, I love the atmosphere, and I love the different factions. But obviously this is a review and I can't just say things like that. So how about I expand on what I just said:

The music is great. It really captures the essence and atmosphere of the game, and it really fits the theme. It captures the emptiness of space with some of it's songs, but space isn't just empty. It's serene, silent, and it feels like everything slows down when you're experiencing it. Even when this is not the case as you're fighting cybernetic bee people, or very angry plants, or special agents that wreak a path of destruction through the whole galaxy, it'll make you feel at peace. It all sounds similar, using similar themes, instruments and motifs, but every track is different, and very recognizable once you play the game enough. Overall, the music in this game is pretty fresh, and a few songs will definitely get stuck in your head.

Next, the ship building. Building your ship is an integral part of the gameplay, maybe even the core of the game, as you obviously need a ship to traverse the vast and chaotic galaxy you're set in. Ship building is fun, and pretty easy to use. I say easy to use, and not easy, because you definitely need a good bit of skill to create a ship good enough to not explode in. Whether you have this skill naturally, like a lot of people do, or have to learn it and get better at it, like I did, it's definitely pretty difficult to build effectively. But that has nothing to do with the editor: It has a ton of hotkeys to make things easier, and it even has a tutorial to show you how to use them. The different blocks are readily available to you, (after unlocking them, of course; they can't all be free, can they?) And you can easily rough out the general shape of your ship in no time. Although I do have to say something: Since the blocks are in a grid, they don't always connect the way you want. Of course, the blocks aren't just squares, there's triangles and beams and trapezoids and hexagons and different kinds of triangles and beams, and this means that not all of the building blocks play nice with each other, visually. They may function fine, but they might not connect the way you want, and it's frustrated me many times. Symmetry is pretty difficult if you're dumb like me, especially since there's no mirror mode. There is a mirror button, and a copy and paste, so you could easily just build one half of your ship then paste it over, but usually I don't think about that and my ship ends up looking fine mostly, but with a few little odd measurements that I can never really fix unless I spend like 30 minutes tinkering. But if you're smart, unlike me, I'm sure you'll be able to build your ship smooth, with little to no problems.

The atmosphere in this game is pretty great. It's a two dimensional game, but it's still very immersive if you let it be. As previously stated, the music is a large part of this games general feel. It represents the emptiness of space, but still with danger and discovery. There are large portions of the map that are just empty. You can zoom through them at maximum speed and have nothing but darkness, the only light coming from your ship for moments. But all other parts of the map are filled with life; Not only the many various factions vying for control over territory, but with plants; nearly all of the asteroids are covered with plant life, shooting seeds and actively repopulating. One faction even actively exploits these plants; they produce the resource they need to build their own ships, and so they farm them; one of their weapons is simply a seed shooter, their ships have special blocks to grow plants on and transfer R directly to their storage. Large swathes of the galaxy are empty, as you'd expect them to be, but the rest of it is almost like a living being, there's always activity; nothing is ever still: ships are moving, plants are growing, life and death. Your own ship, part of one of the factions, is simply trying to stay alive.

There are many different factions in the game, all with their own niches. Different strengths, different weaknesses, some even entirely different playstyles. There isn't much lore in the game, so I like to make my own, decide what their culture and society would be like. All of the factions are so different, and they all have different themes. For example, the Reds. All of their ships are fast shooting, regrow the fastest out of all the factions, have MANY small ships, and take the most damage. To me, this reminds me of something insectoid; Maybe they're giant insects, with some kind of warrior caste society, where the military controls everything. Or the Bees. Their faction is heavily bee inspired, with black and yellow buildings, lots and lots of drones, and large hive like buildings on asteroids. Again, insectoid, right? But they also use a very large amount of lasers, so maybe cybernetic?
You can build your own narratives about all of these unique factions and what they might be like outside of the game.


Anyway, that's enough of me being dumb and embarrassing myself by saying things like that. Reassembly is a great game, and I definitely went very overboard with some of my descriptions, but I truly love this game and hopefully I always will. (Google the OST to listen to the music!)
