\r\n\t\t\t\t\tArcade-style graphics.

Design your own ships. The part shapes are just occasionally odd enough that getting things to line up becomes part of the fun.

Test designs by letting them autopilot versus random other designs. Center of gravity and healing rate are important. Some ships are practically a huge gun with a seat and thrusters strapped onto it!

I spent hours not even playing - just designing a ship and watching it play against other designs in the test phase.

Once you are happy with a design (or just want to shoot something yourself) go in and harvest resources where the bad guys are, which also allows building up a fleet to team up against opponents. Building a fleet is pretty cool - although they are something like a herd of cows... not too smart and they get stuck in the weeds sometimes. Playing the fleet dynamics is a whole game by itself!

The flight is very space-like where you have to oppose thrust to slow down, which makes it feel very swimmy (I think why they say welcome to the tide-pool). In that regard it feels a little like Spore's bacterial stage, only flying takes much more concentration.

Replay value is sky-high, as optimizing ship designs at all size allowances allows starting over and trying new ideas.

Also, the game feels very different than other games because you have complete control how your ship handles (although once it's damaged and your engines have fallen off it will handle very differently until it heals again over several seconds, relatively fast). Trying to control a damaged ship reminds me of Robocraft, though this game is much more twitchy.


TLDR:

If I had to pick a favorite game so far in 2015, this might be it. The gameplay is straightforward. There are always more things to want to try out, which takes time to get there and try them. And Space feels alive. This is more of a tadpole simulator than a space game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I've got about 100 legitimate hours of playing this game. That's split between playing the singleplayer, building ships for tournaments, watching my ships compete in tournaments, and sharing and battling ships with friends.

This is, in all honesty, one of the best space-ship games I've ever played. The game itself is built on very simple components: simple physics, simple graphics, and simple 2-D exploration. However, it is the way these parts fit together under the fantastic music and brilliant vector-based combat that just draw you in. Very immersive, very fun, very addicting, and by far the best ship builder in any space game I've ever played. The ship builder and sharing tools are really the highlights of the game.

What there is to do:
-Build ships from scratch
-Fly ships in open 2D space
-explore procedurally generated content (As with all games that rely on procedurally generated content, the wow factor goes away pretty fast. Thankfully, this isn't what the game relies on to stay fun)
-Unlock new ship pieces and build better ships out of the scrap of your defeated foes.
-Build a factory and begin constructing a fleet
- Battle fleets made by other players at hotly contested wormholes (There is no multiplayer, but the AI is VERY good and it pilots the fleets that players themselves upload. It priotizes the fleets made by steam friends)
-Enter the wormhole to upload your fleet
-share ship.lua files with friends and pit them in 1v1 battle in tournament mode to see who can make the best ship.
-7 playable factions

That's about it. One last note, it seems that the developer is pretty much done with the game. There haven't been any major patches since the launch and the forum activity has died down quite a bit, but I believe the last thing that's being set up for this game is compatibility with steam workshop so that all future content will come from the game's players rather than the devs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like construction and customization, building a fleet of ships, leading your fleet into huge battles with other players' fleets for the sake of expanding your galactic empire, buy this game.

Don't let the 2d vector graphics throw you off. Reassembly is a relaxing game with a great soundtrack, infinite creativity, and great battles.

Several different factions, all with a different selection of weapons and hull blocks, gives much more replayability and keeps the gameplay from getting repetetive. The real fun is the ship building, though. Test your ships in simulated battles against other players' designs, and then use them in the game.

Basically if you ever dreamed of building and commanding a fleet of spaceships, you will love this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemember those little fridge magnets you used to play with pretending they were space ships? That's this game, but better.

Of all the space, building, sandbox, and customizable games I've played, this one is way up in my top favorites. For those who love to be creative, this game is a must-get.

PROS:
+ Full customization of ships and stations, shape by shape. This is a huge plus for those who love to build things from the ground-up.
+ Ship building is very well structured and stable. Rarely are there frustrating moments where blocks don't do what I want.
+ Semi-open world (the world loops, but you can travel to new maps too.)
+ Nice graphics & well-built. Nothing too fancy, but still fitting. This game isn't too demanding in general, in terms of hardware.
+ Easy to get the hang of. It doesn't take too long to figure things out.
+ Forgiving gameplay. If you die, you lose all your resources that you were carrying, but if you're nearby enough, you might be able to rush back to pick it up.
CONS:
- Frustrating at times. There will be times when you have those "BS" moments, and indeed, it gets rather annoying sometimes.
- Trial-by-fire learning curve. I advise against going to the center of the map until you're at max build limit...
- Full customization. For those who aren't able to commit to building your entire ship, there are only a few pre-made ships available at the start. Again, for me, this is still a Pro, but it depends on what games you're into.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere to start-

Reassembly is a top down 2D action spaceship building/ combat game. Sadly thats all it is though. This does not mean the game doesnt have some fun in it! You can encounter other players ships and send your fleet out for others to find. You will fly through space collecting resources and fighting other ships. It reminds me alot of first stage of SPORE. Ships out and about everywhere, collecting to power up.... Then some behemoth comes along and kills everyone.

Pros-
- Easy to use builder. Doesnt take long to build ships.
- Decent combat. Sometimes the AI is wonky but all in all good.
- Huge areas to explore. Though after a couple times it feels the same.
- Neat factions each with their own strengths, except 3. Faction 3 is wet tissue paper hanging on toothpicks. Dont hit a rock too hard or bye bye ship.
- Good ship physics. How you build your ship completely affects the way it moves. Only complaint here is the seemingly never ending drift.
- General fun gathering. If you like the first stage of the game SPORE you will enjoy this.

Cons-
- Ship building is somewhat limited. You cant make really huge ships. This is limited by the max 8000P also once they reach a certain size the ether have no speed or no guns.
- Combat is decent but thats all there is to the game. Collect, size up, fight.
- Agents. I love the fact I find other peoples ships, I dont love that sometimes I run into massive fleet 10 mins in that destroys me so hard space rips a new black hole!!
- Some factions are unbalanced. Faction 3 is so soft on the hull that most my deaths came from hitting something too hard. I know it says hard mode but come on now tissue paper is not hull material. Faction 1 and 2 have the most building options. 4 can become really strong really fast. Like cut ships in half kinda strong. After playing all the factions I find 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 left me feeling let down. They didnt have as much to work with as 1 and 2 or were just 1 trick gimmick factions.
-Not all factions are playable. While you can mod to make them so, they are incomplete. I would love to see the borg like faction become playable or the plant type.
-Nothing but combat. I already mentioned this but it really is the biggest issue. Litterally all you do is collect, size up, fight, repeat. The game needs something.

All in all id say 4/5 stars. Sure it has alot of downs but that did not stop me from spending 40 hours surfing the stars. I recommend this to anyone who loved the first level of SPORE. If you liked that you will like this.\t\t\t\t\t

Reveiw:
This game is basically Space engineers on Steroids. Where you design a ship and die a lot of times ( if you're me) or succeed greatly and gain resources and currency to spend on your upgrades and your ship. The ships are completely buildable and you have a large selection of guns to choose from and use (or abuse) on your ship. The map is randomly generated and people are able to upload there fleet of destroyers to your world in the form of agents. These agents are few in number and provide a challenge when you have made your ship so overpowered to the point where you raze other small ships. You can also hire and build allies, and there isn't much of a penalty for death.

11/10 would die 1,000,000 times and kill everything again\t\t\t\t\t
