\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very interesting and clever game focusing on creating spaceships out of parts and battling other spaceships made of parts. There's no story or really a true goal other than perhaps destroying the player-made "agent" fleets that roam your galaxy, but if you enjoy the ship-building aspect, Reassembly has plenty going for it.

Contrary to what you'd expect, the ship-building itself is the real challenge here. Each faction has its own selection of weapons and other utilities, yes, but with that also comes a unique selection of geometry with which to build your ship. Creating a decently functional ship often comes down to whether your ship is structurally sound as well as having enough guns, and did you place enough thrusters? Did you protect the thrusters? Does that important area of your ship have a continuous hull connection for quick regeneration?

Also, if you ever get bored of the vanilla factions or want to change things up a bit, there's Steam Workshop mod support, where people have created a good deal of cool stuff.

If you like spaceships and the idea of an architectural challenge, Reassembly is here. (currently $4.49 for winter sale!)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot it on Steam sale for $4.49 and it's an absolute steal at that price. The ship designer is very easy to use, even with making ships with complex shapes. I do wish there was some sort of mirror mode but copy/paste/flip does the trick.

All in all tons of fun and would love to see a sequel.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, with this many hours under my belt at this point I feel at least somewhat qualified to write about Reassembly. If you want the short version of what I have to say, then I absolutely reccomend this game for anyone who likes either building things or modding games.

If you want the more in-depth version of what was just said, then continue reading.

What is Reassembly?

Reassembly is, in my opinion, best viewed as a modding and creative sandbox. You have a few vanilla/base game factions to pick from to introduce you to the mechanics of the game, and to the campaign mode where your objective is total conquest of the map. There is also the steam workshop which is home to many user-made mods of varying quality and age.

Base Game / Vanilla Gameplay

Vanilla campaign mode can, unfortunately, begin to grow stale after a while once you've obtained all of the factions. However, that isn't saying there is nothing to do beyond that, however. You can begin to refine your ship designs in the direction of either form or function, and if you join the Reassembly Discord Server, you'll find other professional ship builders to confer with, as well as the occasional tournament to participate in if you enjoy competitive building.

Modding, and Why You Should Learn to Mod Reassembly

I'll put it simply: Reassembly has one of the easiest-to-learn, hard-to-master modding APIs of any game I've come across.

If you indeed have interest in picking up reassembly modding, there are many guides on the subject and while most of them are a bit dated by this point, a lot of the basic rules still apply. Recently modding has gotten even better with things such as custom cannon barrel size/amount being added, as well as support for custom backgrounds. There are planty of mods to steal from take inspiration from on the workshop, some of excellent quality, some not so much. If you put the time in to truly master modding, you will be capable of some truly amazing things that put the vanilla factions to shame. This is probably the biggest reason I would reccomend Reassembly to someone for. Seriously, at least try it, and if you aren't impressed, it isn't the end of the world as there is also plenty of mods you can simply subscribe to on the workshop and mess around with.

Closing Thoughts

Overall, I think Reassembly is a hidden gem. It's $15 (without any discounts) for near-endless amounts of gameplay and creative opportunities. Even without the modding aspect of the game, there's at least a solid 50 hours of gameplay in store with the vanilla factions alone. If you are even remotely interested in top-down space games, ship-building sandboxes, or modding, then you should by all means pick up Reassembly.\t\t\t\t\t
