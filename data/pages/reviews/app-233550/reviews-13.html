One of my favorite parts was how, even though it's basically an action game, a story of friendship between two people who can't even speak each other's language emerges. This story is entirely shown through little things they do to help each other throughout the game rather than showing it through dialogue or narrative. it wasn't until Ico that I saw a game more beautiful than this (I guess that shows my bias, since the entire review could just as easily have been written about Ico).

Now, of course, it feels a little dated. The controls aren't quite as tight as I remember, and it seems much shorter (of course, I remember how to solve some of the really hard parts that stumped me before). But I think it still looks great and tells a great story.\t\t\t\t\t
