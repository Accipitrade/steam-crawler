\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother World (US title: Out of this World) is one of the greatest cinematic 2D platformer. I really loved to play it when I was young. Considering, that this game was only done by one single person (except sound and music), it was a quite an amazing job. Eric Chahi was the living proof in 1991 that even indie studios like Delphins Software can make great games.

Plus points:
* a nice cinematic story line (without narrative parts) and a good action-adventure
* enhanced high res support/filters and new detailed background pictures, which fit in the artstyle of the game very well
* enhanced sound and music filters
* you can switch off the enhanced graphics/sound/music anytime to enjoy the original retro feeling
* 3 difficulty settings, forcing you to completely overthink they way you engage enemies (details below)
* gamepad support and customizable keys, still keyboard felt to be more precisive for playing

Negative points:
* the game seems to run a litte bit faster than the original one, making it more difficutlt when it comes to timing
* the game is rather short, if you figured everything out you can master the game in 30 minutes (speedrunning in 8)
* get accustomed to dying a lot in this game as it is the key concept of the game (learn by doing the hard way)
* unclosed story - especially if you cut out the very bad sequel, which was even exspelled by the original author

I played the new version in hard difficulty. First it felt like I was rusted a lot, especially in fights. Dying to situation I shouldn't. Things that worked out easily back then didn't at all now. After some time I figured out that the shield your gun can drop is wearing off faster than usual and the enemies are more accurate hitting you. This rendered using the mega blast of your gun useless as your shield wears off before you can actually fire it. I had to rethink my tactics and found out that you can win most fights only by spamming shields and wait for small gaps in the enemies defense. So it was doable. I really liked that this small change in the game mechanism was a refreshing thing.

I enjoyed my small "time travel" into the good old days a lot - and I will hopefully find myself playing through this game from time to time again for a zillion times :)\t\t\t\t\t
