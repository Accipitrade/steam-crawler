\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty old, but pretty crazy! Gameplay is pretty obtuse in the puzzle solving department as many of the "solutions" are just doing events you already figured out in a randomly specific order, or just shooting something that looks like a background object. The platforming requires surprisingly tight timing occasionally and with the muddy controls that was a bit difficult.

Only needed a guide for like 2 parts and it took me 2 hours to get it 100%. Really engaging story and cinematic presentation that blurs the lines between cutscene and player input! Heard about this from some interviews with Fumito Ueda (Ico, Shadow of the Colossus, The Last Guardian), and I see a lot of inspiration from this game in those, especially Ico.

Worth spending an evening on and worth buying if on sale for <$3 USD.

I will say the "full controller support" is an absolute lie. Just use the main community config and have it automatically map to your controller of choice (dualsense worked great for me)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother World. What can I say that hasn't already been said in Another Review (tm) over the last 30 years? You already know you should play it, if nothing else than for the gaming history lesson. So, I'll review the coolest bit (in my humble opinion) in this, the coolest of games....

The Gun.

https://steamuserimages-a.akamaihd.net/ugc/836952370141334783/8140A3D7BACB515F788F48208212C919C50D521A/?imw=5000&imh=5000&ima=fit&impolicy=Letterbox&imcolor=%23000000&letterbox=false

Sleek, black, minimalist, with sharper edges than the protagonists smile, it is everything a tool of hot polygonal death should be, and more. Primary fire is a thin, red, classic laser bolt that doesn't just *ouch* a hit point of damage away from it's intended target...it instantaneously flash-fries and boils all of the flesh off the body of the victim, leaving a charred, super-heated skeleton that a moment later bursts into a cloud of ash. SWEET. Badass. This supercharged NES Light Gun painted black would be plenty enough to carry us through just about any alien death planet we find ourselves on, but wait, there's more....if you hold the trigger for an extra second, it creates a charge...release the trigger, it releases the charge, which is a particle shield that absorbs any instant death laser being shot at you! It doesn't last long, but you can charge it repeatedly to make an overlapping shield that lasts quite a while, which gives you plenty of time to test out another function of your incredibly awesome extraterrestrial toy, the super laser. Hold the trigger for an extra second past the shield charging stage, then it builds up to a barely containable level of power, releasing a thick, white hot column of Gods wrath that instantly obliterates any foe, particle shield, door, and/or anything else. Best. Gun. Ever.


~Post review remarks; 40 minutes playtime at time of review, that's beating the game, plus 10ish minutes messing with the options, 5ish minutes bathroom break...yeah, it's a short game. Took me YEARS to finally figure it out and beat it when I was a kid, but once you learn the trial and error, it's a quick one. Still one of the best.\t\t\t\t\t
