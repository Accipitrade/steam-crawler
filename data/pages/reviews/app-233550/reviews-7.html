\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother World is a classic 2D platformer from the Amiga age. It's more of a work of art than a video game. The graphic and sound design have stood the test of time, but the gameplay has not. Running at a lightning fast 15 frames per second the game has a considerable degree of input lag. Combine this with early 90s game design and you get a lot of frustrating deaths as you tediously claw for every inch of progress through trial-and-error. You are punished for not knowing, and punished again for the slightest error in execution. The game is really short but it certainly won't feel that way by the end of it.

All things considered I'd still recommend it for the experience. Another World is an iconic piece of gaming history that serves as a window into the past. It's also a prime example of what narrative games are supposed to be. It's a cinematic experience driven entirely by gameplay and not by cutscenes or dialogue that interrupt the flow of the game. If nothing else it is unique in its ability to tell an interesting story entirely without dialogue of any kind.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t ERIC CHAHI'S TOUR DE FORCE

Regardless of my personal take on it (given bellow), this is a game of the platformer genre and as such relies heavily on trial and error - a.k.a. dying a lot - to move forward, overcoming the obstacles and combat sequences.

Another World was designed in a time without the Internet as it exists today (walkthroughs, video guides and the like). It was forged on the principle of ‘death’ and ‘failure’ being a major part of the fun. And when you finally figured out a part where you were stuck for weeks, wow… No feeling quite like that one.

Granted, how each platformer deals with that axiom of trial and error is always up for debate. Let alone deciding whether it achieves a healthy balance between ‘fun’ and ‘frustration’ when failing is an intricate part of the playing process.

With all of that said, almost 30 years later, Another World remains contemporary in my opinion, didactic to say the least. It deserves to be called a classic, and as such it should earn a place in your gaming shelves, even if you find it to be excessively frustrating. This is such a game that deserves to be played, at least once.

Another World is history, a major part of the gaming world and its early days, but it can also be your future. I for one will always go back and revisit Eric Chahi’s masterpiece, as I have done countless times with those that came before and after me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing port of one of my favorite childhood games. I never completed it back in the 1990's, but it was so much fun to just discover this world once more. If you love being dropped into a scenario with very little back story and get immersed in a visual narrative, then this is a classic worth picking up.

It has it's flaws, yes, it does requite some precise timing in places, and sadly it does crash from time to time, and yes it is short, but games back in the 1990's weren't always super long epics. All of that is easily over looked by great visuals, solid game play, and a great save mechanic that auto saves as you hit major mile stones. You will get frustrated, but once you complete each puzzle you'll feel very much rewarded . Definitely look up a walk through for one or two puzzles as they're not obvious at first, but try them out before you do, there's way more enjoyment that way.

10/10 (even with it's flaws!)\t\t\t\t\t
