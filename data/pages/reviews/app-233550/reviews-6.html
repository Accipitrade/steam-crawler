\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother World is a beautiful game that aged horribly. It comes from a time when developers had to stretch runtime with punishing difficulty and a lack of hints or guidance, expecting players to happily die 20 times over and spend half an hour solving a puzzle without knowing its rules. Unfortunately, that's what most of AW's gameplay is about. Either linear platforming and basic shooting requiring absolute millisecond accuracy, or obscure puzzles which ask you to pick the dev's brain to find the one and only solution he considered, no matter how inconsistent or unprecedented. This makes for a very frustrating gaming experience, which drags down the wonderful rest of the game. I'd love to enjoy this bizzare adventure, explore the different world and interpret an alien culture. The art still looks great almost 30 years later, the atmosphere immerses you in the world and you never know what might happen on the next screen. When everything falls into place, it feels like playing an animated adventure movie. But the times when that actually happened where maybe 20% of my actual playtime, because all the other times were hindered by outdated unintuitive design. This game is best enjoyed as a walkthrough on Youtube.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat could I possibly say that hasn't been said about this groundbreaking classic? It is wonderful to revisit this years after playing during its original release. Leaving the fog of nostalgia aside: Another World (or the name by which I will always cherish it: "Out of This World") is a fast paced action packed game that plays more like a point-and-click adventure than a platform shooter. Just like other games of similar style (such as Cyberia) you cannot lose in Another World, but you will have to work hard to win and you WILL die often. The art is really unique: innovative for its time and still holding a charm all of its own today. On a more technical note: the port is not the greatest. Make sure you run the game with compatibility to Win 95 to avoid constant crashes.

I would recommend every one play this at least once. If I could I would give this two thumbs up!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother World is a classic iconic game from 30 years ago. It is possibly one of the first 2.5D platforming game, though absolutely primitive in how 3D it looks. The storyline follows a scientist who is sent into another world when his experiment goes wrong. In this alien world, he will need to survive and find his way to safety. During his adventure he finds an alien friend whom he saves from being captive.

As a whole, the game was absolutely ahead of its time when it came out. However, I will be honest it is mainly loved, including by me, from the nostalgia of playing such a legendary game. Otherwise, the controls are horrible sluggish. The animations are not as smooth as say something like the original Prince of Persia, and the graphics to be honest lack detail and color. It's truly the nostalgia that allows me to give this game a thumbs up.

This is the 20th Anniversary Edition. As you play you will notice that it now offers higher resolution graphics compared to the 320x200 original graphics. The sound and music have also been remastered as well. You can change back and forth with a single button between the classic and remastered versions which is cool to see the difference. Mainly the difference will be the blocky pixels, not the details of the scenery.

If you enjoyed this in your childhood, definitely check it out. It even includes achievements if you enjoy hunting those down, and they are fairly easy to unlock. If you did not play this 20 or more years ago, then you can always give it a shot to say you played a classic game in the history of gaming, but your satisfaction and your entertainment factor may not be there compared to more advanced and smooth platforming games that have come out in more contemporary times.\t\t\t\t\t
