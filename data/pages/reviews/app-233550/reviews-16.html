The difficulties also come of as daft and silly, the first obsticle is to jump over some very small worms, but the protagonist can only jump about 4cm up, so they are a lethal hazard to him. The problem is compounded by the far-between-save-points. It feels like the designers figured that this was a very short game and they had to complicate things to buff up playtime. This is not a challenging or difficult game. It is an unforgiving and sadistic game.

The only thing I really learned here is to never again try my all time favourite "Flashback". I cannot really recommend this to anyone, new players are guaranteed to be disappointed, and people seeking to relive their youth should consider letting the sleeping dogs lie..\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn 1993, my family got a Macintosh LC III that ran system 7 for its OS. What it also ran was "Another World", or "Out Of This World" as we knew it as here. And what a great time we had playing this fantastic old atmospheric platformer game until we finally passed it, a great deal of time later.

I don't even know how long it took us, maybe even over a year. Looking back that's funny because you can easily pass this game in under 40 minutes if you know what to do.

This version has been given a beautiful modern HD treatment here that is faithful to the original game. Apparently the original polygon engine is what allows for that. But of course this version allows you to toggle between the old and the new sound/graphics, if you want to be reminded of how much worse it originally looked.

The HD update makes it look as amazing to me as I believed it looked in 1993. However I don't know how much the average player would enjoy this game today, if they didn't already have their own personal nostalgia for it to begin with.\t\t\t\t\t
