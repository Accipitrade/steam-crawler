\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWant a cinematic platformer? That's the pitch! It's a rare genre! Then check out this Cheapness affordable gaming review of Another World!

It has very deliberate platforming and movement as this genre is is known for (Kind of what you want from this genre, kind of like how I love the earlier fixed viewpoints in Resident Evil games). Finicky but it is fun... you can die in funny ways! The combat is a tricky sometimes but simple. Put up a shield and shoot them before they get theirs up. There is at least one area where I needed a walkthrough but all in all an easy, short game... like movie length! Thus the value for money does take a hit (Kind of a decadent suggestion). At least it's easy to run even though it was remastered a bit to a lovely polygonal style (You can revert to the original graphics at the push of a button though) with a purple, beige and black colour palette and a unique alien world (Tech built into stone). Has no overt narrative, just visual cues depicting your alien prison break essentially.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort and sweet, Another World (or Out of this World as those of us who first played it on the SNES know it as) is a classic, and a great example of what a single developer can accomplish (well before the era of game development programs to guide).

I won't say much about the story, you really just have to experience it yourself. Great example of minimalism that doesn't feel the need to insult your intelligence by putting text and dialogue heavy scenes everywhere. You learn about the world of the game through playing. Keep in mind, this is a game from the early 90s so don't expect a 500 hour epic narrative.

Achievements: Using a guide you can get through everything in under 2 hours, but I highly recommend completing at least one blind playthrough without a guide just to experience the story as it is meant to be seen. It is a very short game overall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all I have to say that this review is talking about this version of the game, not the game itself. The original game is a classic and way ahead of it's time. It has those old school moments where most people will have to look at a guide, but that is Ok, most games from that time are hard and I appreciate that.
The thing that is very frustrating is the port of the game that is on sale on steam. The menus look like a cheap android game. There are almost no settings available, especially for audio and the game is quite loud. The controls are very buggy and sometimes unresponsive. The game uses the same button for shooting and sprinting, so a lot of the times you will start sprinting like a moron to the enemy instead of shooting them. I also encountered controls getting stuck, so the character would keep moving in a direction without me pressing anything. I understand that this was the original control scheme for the Amiga port, but you can still bring some quality of life improvements to a modern rerelease. Secondly the game runs at very low framerate, I'm talking single digits to 15FPS most of the time. I'm guessing the original games speed was tied to the cpu, and the developers couldn't be bothered to fix it for today. That's what makes some of the gunfights unresponsive.

SPOILER: A massive problem is that if you advance further than you were supposed to, and forgot to do something before, the game already has made checkpoints, you can't delete them and start fresh. At the middle part of the game, there is a level where you need to shoot a lantern and hit an enemy below to kill it. If you miss and continue forward, you eventually meet this enemy and can't progress. But because the game made checkpoints already, and if you load an earlier checkpoint the game still uses the already made ones, you can't progress. I had to disable steam cloud and delete my local save game, start the game all over again from the beginning get to that area again and made sure I killed the enemy with the lantern before progressing further.

Overall the game is amazing, very difficult and requires patience and trial and error, but this release of the game is terrible and I don't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
