A brutal blast from the past

This game is pretty old yet has aged quite well. Although the animations may look crude with all the things we can achieve now you need to keep in mind that when this game got first released this was very revolutionary.

Keep in mind that this game is brutal, there's hardly a margin for errors and if you do make an error then prepare to play the scene all over again. And trust me: mistakes are easily made. Overlooking only one small snail sized monster can prove fatal when stepped on it.

The story

You're a researcher who gets ready to conduct an experiment with a particle collider. Unfortunately a lightning blast hits the main circuit and an explosion happens, sending you to 'Another world'. A hostile world filled with strange alien beings which are all out to get you.

The controls are very simple; you move your character left and right and can perform specific actions. In the first stages of the game this is limited only to kicking with your foot, later on you'll also be able to fire weapons.

Summing up....

Considering the price of this game I'd say it's a no brainer. A platform game, sure, but one with some serious history behind it. It may get frustrating at some points, but once you proceed to finish a level it'll also be very rewarding.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is not for everyone, I mean that, as it tests patience, memory and simple skill execution.

I will note this game is not always kind to players and I'm not blind to this. It can be so harsh to get as far past a part you were stuck on only to die and be placed back right at that point in the game... It's pretty heart breaking and I've had to take a step back just to breathe and think it through again. I've been able to make it through quite a bit of the game on my prior memory of what to do but that doesn't mean it's easy. The amount of times I've had to replay a part or died knowing I was just a simple jump away is the most aggravating thing I've felt but I love the challenge.
There is no traditional form of saving here as it is based on progression, once you make it to a certain part you can back track to whatever section it allows but until then say hello to pushing through a lot of areas you've seen before.
The controls can be a little so-so depending on what you're use to but some adjustments can be made. I've honestly haven't looked far into how the controller support works as I use the Keyboard simply based on personal preference and I've adjusted it to my liking.

I'm practically in love with the story and style of how this game is built. It's not hard to follow based on visual cues and interactions. There's a lot going on it doesn't require any legible dialogue to actually follow along, the aliens speak but nothing you can really make out, which makes it really accessible to a lot of people. There's also a lot of subtle little details thrown in I didn't even find until after a few deaths. Nothing major just fun interactions between characters or details in the backgrounds.

Definitely a favorite of mine, but it might not be a favorite of yours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuick:

3/5. A quick review is not the way I would like to score 'Another World'. I cannot precisely grade this game in a brief manner. My opinions on this game are mixed, and to some degree contradictory. I like the game very much. At the same time, I do not recommend it. I will attempt to explain this in the 'More' section.

More:

My experience of playing this game was frustrating more than anything else. It is unforgiving. The controls lack precision, and the button mapping makes little sense. For example, the button to run is the same button to shoot. When you release the run button, sometimes the player will shoot. Sometimes the player slides, when you are attempting to stop running. At first, this seems like it's not a big deal; until you try jumping to clear pits or obstacles. The precision required to successfully navigate some of these hurdles is poorly matched with the unresponsive controls. I don't know if you need perfect frame-by-frame timing for some jumps or not, but I do remember several occasions where I could have sworn that I pressed the jump button and the character simply ran off of the edge of a cliff or into the jaws of an enemy.

Nothing is explained in this game. If you do not like using a strategy guide or referencing video walkthroughs, this game will irritate you. If, like me, you like to figure things out on your own... then this game is simply not for you. The frustration and repetition required to proceed without outside help is not worth it. Some of the puzzles have no way to understand them other than trial and error. It is virtually impossible for someone to finish this game without dying at least once during their first play-through.

I first noticed that something was strange with the design of this game when I played for several hours during my first run and I had already acquired nearly half of the achievements for the game. In my mind, I was on perhaps the second level out of twenty or more levels, and I thought that I had a long way to go. When I saw how many achievements I already had, I began to suspect that the extreme difficulty and lack of information on how to complete puzzles was being used as padding to artificially extend the time it takes to complete the game. Sadly, I think I was correct. This game is extremely short. If you don't die, and you play at a moderate pace and without too much backtracking, you can probably complete this game in about 35-45 minutes. It's that short. When you compare that much 'fresh' game-play to the 5 hours it took me to complete it, you can begin to see that no matter how high the high points are, the duration and depth of the lows do not make up for them.

Herein lies the conflicted nature of my thoughts on this game. I loved the artwork. If you are a fan of old-school sci-fi, then the themes will probably vibe with you. The story is very simple, yet captivating. When the game-play was not getting in the way, I was enjoying myself. However, when I reached the end of the game after about 5 hours of play-time, I felt very little joy. The amount of frustration and time that it takes to complete this game is not worth the little bits of wonder and awe that you experience throughout. It may have been when this game was released, but I am very suspicious about that being true. I am equally suspicious about the glowing reviews I see.

Overall, I like this game for what it is. I am glad that I played it. Yet I would not recommend it to anyone. In the case of 'Another World', 'the juice is not worth the squeeze'... as they say. If you are looking for a modernized vision of a puzzle platformer, and what 'Another World' could have been if it were made today... then I suggest you check out a game called 'The Way'. It is in many ways the spiritual successor to 'Another World'.\t\t\t\t\t
