\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game I did not really get to play during my childhood and to be honest if it was the 1990s I would probably be saying this game is awesome. The problem is short of nostalgia, it is not really a great game today.

If you look at some walkthroughs for this game it can be done in 20 minutes. The thing is this game you know exactly what to do but the game simply makes it as frustrating to do as possible then makes you play the previous 3 or 4 screens prior again and again. Often you need to be either perfectly timed or perfectly aligned or its try again time. The game pads itself out this way. Which for a time when engines were basic and so was disk size developers were smart to do it this way.

I understand that this is a remaster and that is fine. If you enjoyed the game in its original context then it is probably fantastic to have this. I am sure it holds a lot of nostaligic merit to it. But for a modern audience the gameplay is frustration for frustration's sake and as I hold no nostalgic attachment to this game and my first playthrough is 2018 and not the 1990s where I wanted a 20 minute game to end up lasting a long time; I can only really judge it based on the gameplay visible to me as a first time player of the game and to that end I cannot recommend this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn short, great first half, second half leaves a lot to be desired.
Up until around the palais area the game was great (I even enjoyed the chase scene where the water is quickly spilling over into the caves which seemed to be almost universally disliked by others!)
However once you pass around the midway point the game becomes a bit too much of trial and error and over-reliance on throwing waves of enemies at you causes much frustration.
It left a hollow feeling inside when I knew that I only managed to pass a certain tricky area because of dumb luck despite trying the same method 5-10 times previously.
The puzzles from the first half which were clever and logical dry up and the flaws and bugs in gameplay really start to show. I did not use any walkthroughs but completing puzzles in the first half gave me those eureka moments whereas the second half felt almost like a chore.
However, i would overall recommend this game to show where we've come from in terms of good game design and the atmosphere even 25 years later still holds up.
While frustrating and difficult this game is one that should definetly be played - just keep a spare keyboard handy!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHOW DID I MISS THIS GAME IN THE 90s????!!! I absolutely love it!! There's no question why it's achieved cult status. It's an excellent combo of side-scrolling action with clever puzzle-like situations, but in a very unconventional sense. For an adventure-gamer like myself, I feel like it dragged me into an unfamiliar gaming dimension that was otherwise unknown to me. The graphics and animations evoke so much mystery, intrigue, and other-worldliness, often with just a smattering of pixels. And the controls and "laws" of the game are seemingly so simple, yet the game never fails to catch you by surprise with unexpected functionality that's incredibly rewarding and refreshing. You can tell that there is a meticulous attention to detail, even when viewing it in its original 8-bit form. And I'm not sure if the original version had the same sound design, but the sound feels so thoughtfully constructed and well coupled with the actions, establishing a very fitting atmosphere. This game literally had me laughing out loud, not necessarily out of humor, but at how smart the game design was. Like a, "Ah ha! You clever bastard! You're surprising me at every turn and I love it!" But also the deaths are pretty great too. And the pacing and minimal cut-scenes are SO GOOD. It leaves you with so many unanswered questions and forces you to use your imagination to close some gaps, but that is truly what makes it so beautiful.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been hearing about this French classic from 1991 (but with the name "Out of This World", which I prefer) for a long time and only now I got to play it. It still holds up very well after almost 30 years. It's an ambitious and creative puzzle platformer with varied gameplay and beautiful rotoscope animations (the graphics were updated, but the soul of the game is intact; you can revert to the classic pixelated original if you want _ oh, and there's no HUD or any other information on the screen, it's very cinematic), set in an alien world that reminds me of "MÃ©tal Hurlant" in terms of imagery.

The greatest problem for most people will be its difficulty. There's a lot of trial and error involved, since you may be lost and not know what to do or where to go, but this is part of the game's fascination. There's also a lot of frustration and repetition, because you'll die over and over, even when you know what you have to do.

I must confess that I played until the middle and gave up; I was content to watch a playthrough and see what I missed. I'm glad I did it, because it really is a hard old school game and I took all the beating from it I could endure. If you're more masochistic than me and love platformers, by all means, play this game. It's worth it, even if it's short and with an ending that screams for a sequel (one was made, but it was a flop).\t\t\t\t\t
