\r\n\t\t\t\t\tClassic game that everyone should have played through at least once.

Pre-cursor to Flashback, with the same rotoscoped animation that puts it way ahead of most other games of it's era and great visuals.

There's an epic tale of survival and friendship as Lester finds himself zapped to strange alien world and chased by almost all of it's inhabitants. It's tricky in places and has enough different death animations to put Limbo to shame. There's a richer plot in this game than a large number of modern titles and *not a single line of dialog*.

That's storytelling, folks.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThose were the days.....

When i first played this game in 1992 on DOS I almost went crazy. It was like something i never seen. And back then it was the best looking game out there (well at least to me it was) If i remeber well a friend of mine did play it on his Amiga but it didnt look as good as on DOS, while most of the time with other games that was the other way around.

Now fast forward to 2017 two feelings come over me:

1. OMG I am getting old!
2. WOW this game is still good (yes its remasterd but still the gameplay and story are the same)

For its day this was a revolution.
Please keep in mind this game is form a time before Doom, Heretic, ROTT, Quake, heck even Wolfsenstein 3D. This was pure magic for the 10yo verson of me. Now the 35yo version still loves it and not JUST out of nostalgia -it does help tho- but also cause this is still very relevant today. And for all to enjoy even without an emulator!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes. Get this game. Learn what mind blowing graphics were in 1991, remember a time when a hacker, a vision, and way too much time in front of an assembler is all it took to put a game to market. Sparce in music, no hint system, just you, your Buddy, and your blaster that always seems to go out at just the wrong time vs an entire world of aliens.

This game, it's hard. Brutally hard. This was a time when progress was the reward for those who took the time to master the game, because storage constraints meant the game had to fit and ship on 2 or 3 floppy disks. You will die, you will die a lot, and you will die brutally. This is the game the wise elder geeks cut their teeth on, this is why they complain about today's modern games that spoonfeed you hints and story without subtext. Despite everything that would be a game's deathknel in today's modern game scene, it works. You piece the story together by interacting with the world. The deaths are unfair, and unapologeticly so; as this alien world is not fair, the odds are stacked against you to survive. If you died doing a thing, you learn not to do that thing, and try something else. Remember as you see the password scene though, that you will be back at the puzzle in seconds due to your fast gee wiz machine, back in '91 your mistake would earn you 30/40 seconds of loading time to ponder just where you went wrong. Press on, because every obstacle can be overcome. You can get through in about an afternoon of determined play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother World is a milestone in computer gaming evolution. For younger gamers it might be not clear why, because in comparison to modern titles AW is of course ascetic and visually poor, but at the time of it's release in early 90's it was a little revolution in platform type of games, further continued by Flashback and many others, mostly because of animation and movement of a character, but also due to diversity of adventure and puzzles - every level is something different, you're not doing boring stuff here.

As regards remastered version, the most important thing is that, unlike new version of Flashback (which is a bit dissapointment by the way), it doesn't change the game design and mechanics except adding optional higher resolution (you can switch it during play by pressing "p" key) and levels of difficulty.

Before i started to play, after more than 20 years, i had thought that it was going to be just a little nostalgic return to Amiga times, but this game "still got it" and it took me for a couple of hours again - i just had to finish it once more and i hope i'll be able to do this again at it's 50th anniversary ;)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most classic side-scrolling, shooting, adventure, exploration, puzzle-solving, platform game in existence.
Another World originally came out for the PC back in the 90s with many other game ports. This version available on Steam has been tweaked with numerous upgrades on visual graphics. It still retains the 2d aspect of the original, but utilizes the HD format with the sharpest look on screen, and a better improvement on quality sound and music.
Only drawback to the game is that it's short. I was able to complete this in just an hour. However, for newcomers, you will have a hard time trying to navigate your way through this 2d adventure as it is loaded with enemies, traps, and missteps that will give a game over in an instant. I won't provide any spoilers, so check it out online or read through wikipedia for more info. I highly recommend that you try solving the puzzles on your own as it brings more challenges.
If not, whichever way you play it, than this game won't disappoint. It all depends on the gamer's experience.
Good luck trying to escape from Another World.\t\t\t\t\t

I think that should be brought out immediately to avoid any dissapointment, which is why I'll start by giving the cons of this game. Of course, it's a short piece of work, so there isn't exactly a list for pros vs cons.

But first, to describe what this game is. Another World is a cinematic platformer along the lines of Abe's Oddysee, though not as long and with a much different structure. The game constantly switches between cutscenes and gameplay, and perhaps mixes the two frequently.

The number one problem with this game is, sadly, the gameplay. The controls are the main frustration. I kind of wish that they'd polished them for this edition. And admittedly, they kind of did. Before I found out this was on steam, I was going to try this on an emulator. The controls were so screwed over there that you couldn't jump as long as you were holding down the action key and the left. They fixed that here, but it can still get finnicky. Even for a DOS game, I find it unreasonable that there be one general action button for both running and attacking. This means that if you have to kill an enemy on the run, you have to flat out stop running (and be sure that you stop by letting go of the direction key, not the action key, or else you won't shoot immediately.

However, even with the control polished up, there's also the puzzle design. There's combat too, but I see it as a puzzle in of itself, since, typically, a puzzle will inolve the player dying over and over again. This is usually the result of guards practically being in every other room. Some will even shoot you the second you step inside. This is counter-acted with check points that are basically fifty yards apart. And here's a tip; if a check point seems to be sending you back a considerable distance, then that probably means you missed something that creates a path further down the very short line. It might seem at first that there are multiple ways you can reach your goal, but trust me, you'll have to visit every screen at least once. And you figure out why through trial and error. Not that trial and error is always bad, but it goes a little to far here. The player character does different actions based on what situation he's in. Pressing down while running won't make you combat roll anywhere besides that one patch of ground where your gun falls that one time. This made me feel that I was missing a specific action in one segment where I was stuck for over an hour before having to look at a walkthrough, which always makes me personally feel ashamed bad design or not. Which leads me to tip number two: don't overthink it.

But if you can handle frustration as if you have the ability to smash bricks with your head, then there are things to enjoy about this classic. Mainly the plot, which is completely told through imagery and gameplay, as frustrating as it is. I mentioned cutscenes earlier, but they're mostly so quick and abrupt that they are practically seemless. And the pacing is spot on. The alien place you end up in seems to go from barren wasteland to chaotic warzone over the entire course of your visit. And the graphics, especially the remastered scenery, are breathtaking.

The reason I'm recommending this game is the same reason I'd recommend a classic novel like, say, Don Quixote. It's a great story, but there is just so many dated phrases and lengthy tangents that it's hard to get through, the same as how Another World is an excellent interactive story littered with old fashioned DOS clunk. But if you're fine with that, then I say you should give this a go.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am one of those who love and played this game back in the 90's. What I remember is that this game was very ahead of its time in certain ways. It was totally unique, though the graphics were somewhat outdated, the type of cynamatic quality it had made it totally interesting. I highly recommend that if you have purchased this game, you check out the bonus video that goes into a bit of interview of how this game was created. I enjoyed the insight and the history behind the game which helped me appreciate it even more.

The game play is designed for a much older system at the time (Amiga if I remember correctly), though it seemed to port well to dos and many gaming systems at the time. The original I believe was designed around a joystick and two buttons which really limited the type of actions you could do. This game like many others has you depend on timing and pattern recognition. It can make the game difficult at times, but I would say the remake is actually much easier than the original. The original was more arcade style, where you were not able to save or automatically keep track of points in the game. Everytime you died that game would give you a code that would be a save marker to that point in the game. Also like many games in the 90's, you could expect to die a lot. It is mostly trial and error which can really frustrate people. Battle is all about timing your shots and shielding right, while also memorizing how your enemies attacked from the last time you died. Its tricky, but also provides a lot of personal satisfaction when done right.

I kept my game with the remake graphics and sounds just to see and experience the new touch. Everything seems to feel the same, especially when using a gamepad. Switching back to classic is fun too. But the remake does make things feel a bit new. I also have not experienced any crashing like some others have mentioned. My first session of the game I was able to accomplish 3/4th of the game in one sitting. I got tired or I would have finished it without a single crash. This also was a sign to me that things felt original and it didnt take me too long to get right back in to the rhythm of the game. I had to relearn many things but overall I could remember the basic points. The game is not all that long once you know what your doing.

I recommend this game if you are familiar with this type of retro gameplay style. It probably isnt for everyone, but this game is definitely significant to my life and childhood. Nice to have it in my steam library.\t\t\t\t\t
