\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat's the game about?
A lone engineer goes back to the lab he works at, at night, because he's forgotten his kid's diapers. He boots up his computer, drinks a beverage containing hallucinogens, and then trips out for about 2 hours, thinking he's in outer space with an alien friend that beats up people with the strangest of sounds.


General feeling after finishing?
Warm. Good. The improved graphics are a welcomed change to it. Are we in a movie?


Story?
One of the best bits of it. It's not complex, but it's dreamy, and unravels with a nice pace and some surprises. I really felt out there for a while, and it's only a pity that it's so short, and it doesn't even have a single word written or spoken.


Gameplay?
Some parts are difficult to grasp (more due to the controls than the game's design), like jumping at the right moment, for example, but in general it's simple and well adapted to what it wants to convey. There's basically only basic movement (up down left right jump) and a gun. The gun is one of the better aspects of the game, actually, because it fits in so well with the story. You advance by discovery, there are checkpoints so no fear of dying, but the game doesn't "try to much" to lead you in a given direction as much as others, and there's also a lot of trial and error.


Graphics?
Minimalist but impressive. Rotoscoping, here, like in Prince of Persia, makes for a very smooth atmosphere.


Sound & Music?
Again minimalist and at times a bit distracting, but the effects (especially the gun and the elevators) are really nice.


Replayability?
Once every couple of years should be fine, but yeah, definitely worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
