\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWonderful remaster of a classic 90s game. Nostalgic. Innovative for its time with its use of cinematic effects. In terms of narrative and gameplay there is little to no language or UI elements. The brutal difficulty is ameliorated by frequent checkpoints.

Ports and version differences:

Another World / Out of This World was initially released in November 1991 for the Commodore Amiga and the Atari ST 16-bit home computers. The game was ported to DOS and the Classic Mac OS not long after. These ports feature expanded new areas and higher difficulty. The DOS version ran at 320x200 resolution in 16-color mode. The Mac OS version ran at 640x200 in 16 colors.

The game was ported to Windows 3.1 in 1994 by 1C: Maddox Games. This release is essentially the same as the DOS port.

The game was ported to Windows XP in 2006 through joint efforts of Eric Chahi, DotEmu, Mana Games and Magic Productions. This version is referred to as Another World: Collector's Edition. It features enhanced higher resolution graphics in 256 colors and higher quality sound. It is possible to switch between the original and remastered modes. In addition, the difficulty has been scaled down, with twice as many checkpoints.

Another World: 15th Anniversary Edition, released in 2007, is essentially the same as the Collector's Edition. The only difference is the included bonus material: an audio CD with the game's soundtrack, two developer diaries, a "Making of" video and a postcard signed by Eric Chahi (in select countries).

Another World: 20th Anniversary Edition, the version of this review, has only cosmetic improvements over the previous two editions. The visuals and the audio have been slightly tweaked. The difficulty mode can now be adjusted on the fly by selecting one of three levels. It includes the same extras as the 15th Anniversary Edition. Amiga ROMs are included as a bonus.

I was glad to see a native Linux version, but unfortunately it didn't work on my system. YMMV. Information online suggested running the game with native system libraries. I opted to run the game with Proton instead. With Proton it ran perfectly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt seems clear to me that Another World is one of those games that isn't made for all audiences. This game is one of those iconic examples of classics that all of us who love the history of video games have heard and playing this is more than just the gameplay experience, it's the understanding of what that game means in its time.
With a look from the 20s of the 21st century, the game can seem simple, short, and difficult due to its high punishment of instant deaths that forces you to a large number of replays due to the various trials and errors until moving on to the next part of the game that will definitely kill you many more times (Thanks for the large number of checkpoints the game has, without it the difficulty would be even more difficult)
The game was originally released in 1991, and I don't believe that any of the 3 points mentioned above are necessarily negative, I believe that they even integrate well with the other qualities of the game. The visuals are highly charismatic, going very well with the soundtrack and the sound effects very well done. The innovative cutscenes help us to immerse in this sci-fi so unique that any frame is easy to identify as being from Another World.
Lester is a young scientist who ends up in a strange world after an unsuccessful experiment. Everything and everyone in that world (with the exception of 1 being) are harmful and want to assassinate Lester and their only objective is: to escape.
Technically flawless, innovative, historically important, captivating, charismatic, unique. Another World is a "must play" for those who like the history of video games, it is interesting for those who like different experiences. Definitely a unique game. Will it please everyone? Will not. This is bad? No way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI understand that the game was innovative at the time and that it was the passion project of a visionary. I'll even grant that the art and concept remain cool to this day, which is quite a feat. But is it fun to play? Not at all.

The game is a series of novel instant death obstacles and the only way to progress is to encounter them, get killed by them, and then try again. This wouldn't be so terrible if you were not always respawned several frames back, forcing you to redo not just the part that killed you, but all the insta-death traps that preceded it. This means that game play boils down to mashing keys in the right sequence at the right time - a kind of guitar hero where you die if you miss a note and then have to replay the last 3 songs to get back to the one you were just playing.

Some will argue that this game was made back when games actually tried to challenge the player. You know, that golden era where men were men, and women were men, and the term snowflake had not even been invented yet. I'd argue this game was never really fun, we just didn't have much to compare it to or much else to play at the time. Save your money and just watch a video of someone doing a speedrun. You get all the nice visuals without any of the grief.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother World is an iconic game that was ahead of its time when it was released 30 years ago.
It involves a lot of trial and error which can be frustrating, and is probably one of the reasons why I never made it far on the Sega Genesis version, "Out of This World" as a kid.

Since this is the 20th Anniversary Edition, there's some cool features like the HD graphics which can be toggled on or off, the remastered sound effects, three difficultly settings, and an option to change your resolution.
Unfortunately I wasn't able to run it at 4k. In fact setting the resolution to anything higher than 1080p would cause a black screen, and continued even after restarting the game. To fix this I had to go into my users\\documents folder and delete the AnotherWorldUserDef.xml file, then set the resolution back to 1080p.
I did also experience random crashes which was annoying.

It was still fun to play and do recommend it if you're into these older type games. 👍\t\t\t\t\t
