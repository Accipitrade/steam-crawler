\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, for those of you who have ever played a little game called "Kennedy Approach" back in the '80s, this game is a worthy successor to the title.

The premise of the game is easy; you are an air-traffic controller at an airport and need to manage incoming and outgoing planes.
The actual game quickly gets more difficult as you find planes queueing up to take off, planes trying to land at landing strips which are already in use, and more and more traffic incoming.
You need to keep your wits about you to make sure planes don't crash into one another, which would end your career (as well as a lot of lives).

Definitely a good game which would work well on desktop, laptop, tablet, and some bigger screen mobile phones.

The ONE issue I've found with the game? You can't change the keyboard keymap. But you can play it just fine with just the mouse if you can't figure out how to use the set quick keys.

I am definitely recommending this game and hope more airports will be released through DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a nice casual game, fully controllable with the mouse. Difficulty ranges from slow and easy to fast and hectic.

For years I've been waiting for the successor to MicroProse's "Kennedy Approach", released back in 1985. As far as gameplay goes, this comes close, but you don't have to direct outgoing planes to their destinations, weather is always perfect [edit: patchy clouds added 6/26/15] and you don't stack planes in holding patterns, but you get more control over them on the ground and have to manage who's crossing what runway and when as they taxi back to the terminal. Also, there are different types of planes with different speeds to manage.

As of 5/28/15 there are 8 different airports to play, and the developers claim that they'll be adding more as time goes on. [edit: A ninth airport was added 6/26/15]

This is NOT a simulator, and you'd probably be disappointed if you were looking for one. But if you're looking for a game where you have to manage chaos without a large learning curve, this is a good one for under ten US dollars.\t\t\t\t\t
