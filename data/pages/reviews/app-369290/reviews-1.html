\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is fun puzzle game. If the subject matter interests you, you'll probably like this to a point. For a casual play through, on slower settings, this game can give hours of fun.

More importantly, it is riddled with game-breaking bugs that can take the fun out of the more intense settings. Seeking high scores and the achievements (if you enjoy chasing those) can become quite tedious and while not impossible, certainly not an enjoyable pursuit.

Those achievements were why I came back almost 7 years after release...and I'm beginning to think I might have simply forgotten the reason I gave it up in 2016. Perusing through the forums shows that these bugs existed around release, or were added in with various updates, and were either not fixable or were simply unfixed.

The biggest issues:
1.) Planes will automatically reselect their own runway designations, after being told to land at a different runway. This doesn't happen all the time, bit it has happened continuously on Zurich, London, San Francisco, for sure. This makes planning your game impossible, and with the end of some runways so close to the edge of the maps, there's little time to see it and try to fix it. And on the harder modes, there's rarely time to fight with them. In a mode like "Building Traffic" where one crash ends your game, this is a full-stop game killer.

2.) The planes will crash on the taxiway, when they are out of your control. I'm not talking about if they are stuck and backed up on the runway or something. I mean when one is driving down the taxiway and it is hit by another crossing a runway for example as if they weren't waiting for each other like they do at push back. I'm not sure what sequence of commands allows this to happen, but its frequent enough to be a problem.

3.) Speaking of push backs. Planes will go crazy when pushing back from the gate. I've had multiple planes just drive in reverse across tarmacs, runways, and the dirt only to stop when they inevitably run into something. There's no control over the planes once a push back has been initiated so again its game over in the harder modes.

4.) Planes will land, take the entire length of the runway, and then take off again trying to return to their runway of choice (I think this is connected to number 1 somehow).

5.) Expedited off doesn't seem to work as expected (but it might be intended to work the way it is, I don't know). It makes sense that a Boeing 777 needs the whole runway. But those little prop planes, once slowed down and expedited off, should divert onto the closest taxiway to clear the runway--they don't do that even if you hit the option as soon as they land.

6.) The "speed up" and "slow down" shortcut keys (probably the ones you'd use the most) will affect all the planes in the air instead of just the one you selected. Thus making them entirely pointless to use and incapable of serving their time-saving purpose.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat casual game! Your job is to handle incoming and outgoing airplane traffic at different airports, and try not to crash them. There is no campaign in this game, but you have 4 different mods you can play. Endless traffic, Building up traffic, Move up to 100 airplanes, and I forgot what was the 4th one, but I played mostly these 3 mods.

Different airplanes fly at different speeds, so if they intend to land on same runway, you need to take care that slower airplanes expedite quicker off the runway to make way for a faster and bigger plane. Also runways that cross each others often carry extra risk, cause there is a possibility that airplanes will crash at those crossings while landing/taking off.

Sometimes, to spice up the game, some of the runways that you desperately need get closed down, because of repair works, or sometimes neighboring towns-people will object to noise, so airport closes temporarily some runways, and that brings only bigger chaos for you to handle.

You need to think fast, and react even faster. Some airports are harder to handle than the others, but this game will bring you joy if you give it a chance. I completed everything there was in game in cca 25 hours, but I bought it for like 2$ at summer sale, so it is a no-brainer.\t\t\t\t\t
