\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEasy to learn and difficult to master. Treat it like the basics of runway management then will find a lot of fun.

You need some practise and thinking to survive the 100 plane challenge and further master each of the runways. Some runways (e.g. London) are mostly or fully about clicking your mouse ASAP; some others (e.g. Schiphol) are much more strategic and holistic.

Then even deeper in the game, more planes on land and in the air. And for the sake of higher scores, the job will pragmatically involve more non-real-life bug-solving e.g. redirecting 3 planes from landing on the same stupid runway, or stop that Concords from bumping into slow propellers in the front, or order your Corcord to steal that 1 second gap in the intersection and take off, etc. These will keep you busy enough and suddenly your game ended because planes have crashed in somewhere you neglected, or too many planes simply clogged the choking points, regardless of your best efforts. It is sometimes nonsense and mentally punishing, but you always enjoy it like your weekly high-level Sudoku and calls it a day.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAirport Madness: World Edition is the sixth release and a worthy ATC (Air Traffic Controller) RTS contender by Big Fat Simulations Inc within the Airport Madness series of games and beyond.

Released in 2014, almost 3 years after the excellent Airport Madness 4, it raised the bar of the ATC simulation genre to a new high, mainly due to moving from the current fantasy style airports into real world examples, in addition to improving gameplay in almost every aspect. Here's why:

+ A mix of 12 real-world famous airports (5x USA, 5x Europe, 1x Alaska, 1x UAE)
+ Efficiency/performance meter to benchmark progression
+ Optional cloud cover for increased realism and difficulty level
+ The addition of window mode (at long last)
+ Progressive rankings and leaderboards (with increasing difficulty)
+ Steam achievements and trading cards

- Would have liked a broader mix of airports from other continents/countries
- Too many Concords thrown in the mix for my liking
- Not as challenging and with less diverse achievements than its predecessor (AM4)
- No custom scenarios/challenges/disasters to solve
- The window mode is not scalable (looks really small in a 4K VDU)
- No steam workshop integration or any kind of customisation

-----
TL;DR: Even though Airport Madness: World Edition is an overall improvement over Airport Madness 4, it's predecessor felt more complete and satisfying overall, perhaps due to all the extras it included. Still, AM:WE is as addictive and as stats show the most popular title of the series to-date, so a definitive thumbs up from me as well.

-----
PS: Perfect game achievement was accomplished in under 20 hrs, so more seasoned ATC players should be able to get it much faster than that.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is more exciting than you might think. It is also quite challenging. I had too many crashes.

Basically you try to land as many planes as possible without having a crash. One crash means you're out. You get a variety of airports and airplane types. They have so many Concordes in the game despite the fact these planes have been phased out. I just wanted to point that out. Haha!

Anyway, the different airports pose unique challenges. You have airports where the runways don't cross each other, and so are easier to manage. But there are also airports where runways cross each other so you have to be careful.

I am still trying to get all of the acheivements.

The one improvement that I would suggest is to make it possible to pause the game by just hitting the Esc key. In the game, we have to click on the pause button in the screen. Maybe it's just me but I usually pause the game by hitting the Esc key and accessing the Menu. So, I had to get used to clicking on that pause button in the middle of the game screen. But that's just a small thing.

Either way, I will recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one where I wish steam had a neutral option.

Its a casual air traffic management game (not complex enough to be called a simulator).

What you get is 12 different airfields (and they do feel and play different from one another). You manage landing, taxi, takeoffs, as well as speed and choosing which runways to use. The game uses 2D graphics, which frankly doesn't bother me one bit. The graphics do not have a negative impact on the gameplay and I find the retro feel to be cute.

The one big feature that is missing from the game is managing planes in the air circuit (aka traffic pattern for USA people), which was dissapointing.

Still, there's several modes you can play and 3 difficulty settings, which work well. Hard will prove challenging and its for those who really want to improve their multitasking skills. Easy offers a more relaxed and casual experience.

CONCLUSIONS:

I bought the game on sale for 5 euro and its not a bad price for it. I'd certainly reccomend it for this price.

However I feel cannot entirely reccomend getting it for the current full price of 10 euro. Don't get me wrong - the game is fun but doesn't have enough content to justify the normal price.\t\t\t\t\t
