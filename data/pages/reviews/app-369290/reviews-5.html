\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rate this game 3.5/5. Pretty good.

This is probably the preemminent version of airport madness. This is essentially an fairly unrealstic air traffic control puzzle game where you are supposed to try to keep the airplanes at the various runways from running into each other and having accidents as each airport becomes progressively more and more busy until wrecks become inevitable. There are several airports to choose from, the sound effects are increadibly realistic and the music isn't too bad though often repetitive enough that you may wish to turn it off after a while (which you can easily do). This is a fun little puzzle game which gives you a lot for its relatively cheap price although you should probably endeavor to buy this when it is on a special Steam Sale for even more bang for your buck.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWay back in the day -- back in 1986 and when the Commodore-64 was king, there was this game by Microprose called, "Kennedy Approach." It rocked. Thank God for "Fast Hack'em" is all I have to say.

One of the cool things about the game (and the Commodore-64 in general) was the speech. It was pretty cool to hear air traffic controllers communicating with airplanes.

Like everyone, sometimes I am somewhat nostalgia and I remember this game fondly and with a certain amount of yearning -- I didn't get to play "Kennedy Approach" for very long because soon I was off to college and was too busy drinking beer and eating pizza.

The speech on this game is really cool but it's only the beginning. I've been wondering for a long time what a modern version of "Kennedy Approach" would like and sound like and why one didn't exist. This game is incredibly fun, addictive, and is a worthy successor.

You are an air traffic controller and your job is to guide planes to land (on which runway) and take-off. Part traffic cop -- you have to direct planes to cross landing strips and to the gates.

The graphics are great -- top-down, static except for the planes flying around. Oh and when the planes fly into each other there's a neato explosion.

Variety? Sure, there are about a dozen airports to choose from. I like Anchorage -- I have no idea how well the game's layout mirrors the actual airport. But you can imagine that each airport has its own strengths and challenges that would make no two games the same.

Easy to learn, difficult to master, the easy mode is what you need to learn the ropes -- tell planes to slow down or speed up; direct them to different runways. Manage your time and space well and your efficienty rating goes up. Be flexible in your approach with a general plan of landing but understand when you either have too many planes landing or taking off that can't be accommodated by your general plan. Timing can be everything -- real estate is expensive and so runways overlap and criss-cross. One mistimed step and two planes are landing on top of each other, or are crossing the runways at inopportune times.

Truly, this is a great game and deserves some of your time and money. Well worth the $10, absolutely, and sure to loom large in peoples' minds as a great simulator!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I have seen a few reviews for this saying that you don't really get the full ATC experience from this game. Really? Do you really think so? If you had to actually sit down and write those words, then you aren't thinking very clearly. For the rest of you, no, this game will not give you an ATC simulation. I used to be an Air Traffic Controller and no, this game is nothing like the real thing. Then again, it's not supposed to be exactly like the real deal. This game was developed to be used on mobile phones, and it's pretty fun too. Basically, it's a timing game. Don't worry about breaking separation, the game doesn't recognize separation. Don't worry about radar ops, there is no radar. You control three things: Sending the plane to and from the hangar, landing the plane, and giving the take off command. You don't deal with final, arrival, departure, none of it. You can control the speed of the plane in small intervals, and yes, that will become pretty important if you're going for the achievements, but that's about it really.

I have sat down and beat this game in its entirety and I actually enjoyed it. It's not a stressful game, and it's not really a "super fun" game either, but it's a great time waster, and it's pretty easy to 100% if you sit down and go for it. It goes on sale from time to time, so if you don't want to pay $10 for it, just wait for it to go back on sale. I do recommend it. There are other games out there that give you an accurate ATC simulation, so if that's what you're looking for, avoid this game. For the rest of you, have fun!\t\t\t\t\t
