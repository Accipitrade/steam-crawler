\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not sure if me and all of the other reviewers even played the same game, because this was a snooze fest.

You assume the role of a would-be air traffic controller as you attempt to keep the traffic at a busy airport moving smoothly and without crashes. Generated planes appear every few seconds to land on a random runaway, meaning you must be quick in your reactions and timing. Gameplay is as shallow as clicking on planes and directing them to runaways, to take off, or to taxi, while ensuring that no other plane will cross its path and cause a crash.

That's the whole game. There is no depth, there is no fine-tuning, no cool mechanisms to play with, no story. There are a few different airport maps, which do offer some variety in strategy as runway intersections change things up a lot, but in the end, it isn't enough to liven up the otherwise drab gameplay. The graphics are as good as a flash game from 2002, if not worse. Backgrounds are static with the only varying element being random cloud cover, which often obscures the view of incoming planes, hampering your efforts. Additionally, there's no indication of the stats of each of the three (four?) different plane types, so unless you've already caused a few crashes, you won't realize that Concordes are much faster than any other plane.

All in all, this game is fun for all of 10 minutes. 2/10 would not recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is something of a follow up to the (free) online Airport Madness series, and like most games that began on a website sometime last decade, AM:WE is a simple but entertaining offering.

You play an air traffic controller at one of a number of real world airports; your job is to keep the airspace open and runways moving as fast as possible. Fail in this task, and you get to see physics in action as two planes attempt to occupy the same space simultaneously.

A simple but addictive task then, which is helped by the welcome inclusion of ATC background radio chatter to really get you in the mood. The maps are suitably varied, ranging from a single runway in China to the interlocking 4 way chaos of Amsterdam or Las Vegas.

However, it should be said that there is little on offer beyond this. 4 game modes and an achievement system add only variation around the same theme. Occasional random events (closed runways) add to the challenge, but absent are the emergency landings, space shuttles and weather/daylight systems of other entries. The UI is straightfoward, which makes the gameplay enjoyable but the menus feel sparse.

To conclude then, AM:WE doesn't offer the world, but what it does have is an easy to use, entertaining and enjoyable premise. Give it a go then, but perhaps wait for a sale first.\t\t\t\t\t
