\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTheresa's backstory could have been an interactive movie/cutscene and it wouldn't have taken anything significant away from the experience; it was mostly mindless running/sneaking with the occasional moment of character development.

Is it worth noting that I have not completed "The Madonna of Sasau" quest, so I can't give any insight on that.


The dog companion is a gimmick, and a poorly implemented one at that; he consistently barks and yawns even in stealth, he drinks from damn near every puddle he can find (maybe that's 'realistic'), he'll bay constantly if you go indoors and chose not to interact with him to give him the "wait" command (which takes about 3 lines of dialogue, instead of just being a keybind).

In combat, he'll walk in-front of enemies and either mess up the AI pathing or be hit or block your swings with his body (which will likely drop his loyalty to 0, causing him to run off).
When on horseback, he'll noticeably spawn directly in-front of or beside your horse when you get too far away.


The rest of the content is acceptable, but how could you go wrong with a new shield and some potions?
This should all have been in the game originally- or as a free DLC.

Get it in a bundle and/or on sale, if you really must have it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSweet. Sweet jesus, where do I begin with this one. Having not read the individual dlc packs, wanting to experience everything first hand. I walked my way into this quest, decked out in Warhorse armor as I'd just spend the last few hours searching for all the ancient maps, expecting to woo this lady off her feet after a few short remarks and something cheeky to end it. What I was greeted with, was a dog, of which I got to interact with twice, and then a "Conversation" about Skalitz which thrusts you neatly back to the start of the game.

Neatly placed in Theresa's shoes, I decided to remain optimistic about the fact I was just ripped away from my hard earned suit of armor and hard fought skills. I venture forward to the first quest, ready for something. Anything. And I get handed a fetch quest. And then another. And another. Then your friend wants you to steal a lucky dice from someone so she can magically appear with said dice and then somehow that convinces him to ask her to the dance. Yeah, nonsense thats surely important to the story. Sure. I go through with it, generally annoying task with no real reward.

I saved Daddy getting grilled by the guards for last. And boy oh boy was this the right decision. Quest starts off simple enough, dummy A wants to warn dummy B stealing is wrong and they got caught. Dummy B thinks everything is dandy and wants to go get the lost stolen goods, but cries about a map so as to not get lost in the mine. I naturally said ♥♥♥♥ that, being annoyed at this point as I'd spent the better part of 3 hours mucking about in the beginning of the story again, and chose the faster option. Sneak around in a pitch black mine crawling with people that beat you on sight with 0 direction of where to go. I've spent another 2 hours getting pissed off in a dark ass cave because its got a guard in every hallway you need to go down to find the silver.

In conclusion. I've reloaded a save prior to talking to Theresa, and will be talking to her about the dog and ONLY the dog, I suggest you all do the same, unless you're concerned with the backstory of a town that burnt down, and people that are dead or provide next to nothing to the main story. If I wanted to play in the main village, I would have spent an hour or two there as Henry, in the beginning of the game. Not for several hours progressing a character that doesn't matter whilst I'm also balls deep in the main story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPeople don't seem to understand what this DLC is exactly and that's why it's getting down voted for silly reasons. This is story building, It's about tying up loose ends when it comes to peripheral characters you remember from before the attack. It gives a little more backstory to the people who lived with Henry during a time when their lives we're very much normal and undisturbed by encroaching war. It also gives insight on how life might've been for a peasant woman living during this time. This DLC takes a seemingly bland character who existed only as a convenient plot device and gave her context within the intro. After playing this I really felt the impact of the attack that propelled the whole narrative and reason behind the the protagonists journey, it really gave me a sense of what it would be like to be scattered to the four winds and then left at the mercy of the medieval world around you. It also gives you a chance to begin mending that by giving you quests that center around helping your old neighbors. I liked this a lot.\t\t\t\t\t
