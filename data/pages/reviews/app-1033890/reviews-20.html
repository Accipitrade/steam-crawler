\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm gonna save you the trouble and say outright that you should avoid this DLC if you're expecting high level gear or are playing the game in order to complete it rather than enjoy it, or are just achievement hunting. Additionally, the issues with getting stuck on terrain and general collision errors are still present here and they can have a really negative impact on some of the stealth portions of this DLC, some of which are lengthier than others.

In short, skip this DLC if you don't really care much for Theresa or getting to know some of the side characters. If you're already in love with this world, I would still recommend coming into this with lowered expectations. Treat it as what it is: a short one shot that you can use to take a break from the main quest or larger side quests, not some grand 20 hour experience.

I would recommend playing this if you've picked up the Royal edition, since you've already gotten it. If not, wait for a sale if you really like KCD, or wait until the Royal edition goes on sale again. Otherwise, don't buy it full price.

Spoilers ahead.

This DLC isn't necessarily horrible, but it's not exactly good, either. From a barebones view, this DLC is a 2-4 hour excursion that sees you doing more fetch quests than anything else. However, what it does do really well is give you a glimpse into what Skalitz was like before the raid, as well as what Theresa and her family were like. It was nice to see how people lived and what side characters' relationships were like with each other before everything went to hell. This scenic part more or less makes up the first half of the DLC.

On the merit of storytelling, it's fairly average, though that's not entirely its fault. Overall the narrative and attempts at emotional connection were really hindered by the gameplay, especially where Tinker is killed off and Sammy dies (the gameplay moments being the awful "scavenge for supplies in the dark to save Sammy" bit and the overall jankiness and unreliability of Tinker to create openings in a fight). This is my first playthrough of the game and I have yet to finish the "Courtship" quest, so bear that in mind when I say that I definitely got to know Theresa "better," but I don't think that counts for much when I barely knew her to begin with.

Theresa, depending on how you play her, more or less becomes a superhero in this DLC. A miller's daughter ends up doing everything from sneaking about and lockpicking chests to fighting Cumans with her trusty dog. That isn't to say that these are bad things, but like I said before, with Sammy's death being hindered by that awful fetch quest, Theresa's determination to save any stragglers near the end of the DLC can come across more like busy work rather than her trying to honor her brother's memory. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNO SPOILERS - The DLC is two story arcs for Theresa and Johanka, two brave women from Skalitz, the village where Henri comes from.

The first quests are more light-hearted and relaxing, but later some are rather heavy and stressful, as it should be (it is fitting).

I feel it is an excellent addition to Theresa's character, who is close to Henri. The story for Johanka is darker, but a great addition fitting the severe religious order of the time.

Johanka's story delves into the religious culture of the times, is well written and has a very decent level of historical quality!

Difficulty warning - Johanka's story
For Johanka's quests, it is played with Henri in the present time of your game/playthrough, and it is rather hard on many aspects. I recommend you have the following before starting it:
a) be able to pass Speech checks of 15 ;
b) have good experience with combat in the game.

NOTE that her DLC quest will only be unlocked after you have finished other quests around the Monastery in Sasau (Pestilence and In God's Hands).

Theresa quest is her recounting of the past and is rather easy. I recommend you do it early as it deepens her character; especially if your role-play that Henri likes her.

TIme
I am puzzled by folks noting 6 hours to finish the entire DLC (6 for Theresa only makes sense). There are 9 quest arcs/chapters, each often have 3+ tasks (sometimes 7+ tasks!), each with many dialogues and cut-scenes. Some require searching and sneaking in mines, forests, riding back and forth, etc. The last chapter for Johanka, sprinting like mad, took me *well* over 2 hours of dialogues, running back and forth, and cut-scenes!

Not to mention some dialogues choices and actions are fatal! I did a few reloads.

Doing Theresa and Jahanka *entire* quests takes about 10-12 hours (more or less).

( I took 18 hours as I explore a lot, sneak a lot, etc. I did *not* try to get all achievements.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is quite honestly one of the best DLCs I've ever experienced for a game, if not the best. Playing as Theresa experiencing a normal, bland day sets up perfectly for the intensity of the Cuman invasion immediately following it, it's a real whiplash of emotion. It's one of those stories that solidifies just how "not special" the main characters are; these are just survivors trying to make the best of a hellish situation.

Just as I thought that couldn't be topped, we get a quest centered on Johanka a good 70-80 hours later, with some of the best writing and immersive quest design I've seen in an RPG. It's a questline that asks the player just how far they're willing to go with their role-playing--where they end and Henry begins--and how well you commit to it. It's rare when a game calls out the player sometimes, I usually admire it when they do. Then there's all the little things like having a bustling town of conversation all at once, rewarding going the extra mile in what you do, and bringing in characters from previous quests to really make you feel accomplished from your adventures.

The game itself is so far a 9/10, but this DLC is a 10.\t\t\t\t\t
