\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC was a pleasant surprise and awesome little detour from the main quest. I especially appreciated the story and the stealth elements. I feel like I got better with some specific skills and came away with a much stronger and fuller picture of what life in Skalitz was like...the awkward conversations between people who obviously dig each other, bartering with tradesmen, helping a friend pick flowers to brew a special beer, hunting for treasure, and appreciating all of the frustration, humor, comfort, and tragedy of being part of a dysfunctional family.

It's not perfect by any means...the fetch quests get old, the game's save system can have you spending more time on certain quests than you'd like, and it doesn't do a whole lot gameplay wise that affects the main story.

What it does, however, is tell a pretty great story. If that's your kind of thing, don't skip this DLC. I loved it. 8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoth questlines are great, I have no idea what people are complaining about.

The Theresa Questline adds a lot to your emotional depth towards your hometown, and your connection with Theresa as a character. But I do understand the people who complain that they suddenly got thrust in to a huge unskippable questline by accident, the game should probably warn people. It is really easy to accidentally start it at the start of the true open-world part of the game, when most people just want to explore and get a feel for the game. The game does try to warn you a bit, but I think the warning should be more explicit.

The Johanka Questline is also great and adds a lot of non-combat challenge to the game, and also adds a stronger emotional attachment to certain characters.

Both questlines are long and among the best in the entire game, add a lot of emotional weight and immersion to the World and its characters. I heartily recommend this DLC, though they are very story-driven and relatively non-combat oriented, so if that is not your cup of tea...\t\t\t\t\t
