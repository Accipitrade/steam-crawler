\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA more in-depth review of this DLC can be found in my video review covering all the game's contents: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Vxau31IPHgo

Overall this is the best DLC of the bunch, but it has some caveats since it contains three parts.

The dog companion comes with a Houndmaster skill and traits, providing some new gameplay features. He's fairly useful, especially when hunting, but sometimes his AI and pathfinding make him janky.

The second part, the one which most people complain about, is the Theresa quest line. I don't really blame them. While it provides a neat backstory for some of the characters, it locks you into the quest up until completion, making the dialogue option that triggers it a 3-4 hour commitment. Which wouldn't have been as bad as it is if the quest design wouldn't waste so much of your time. You're forced to make long trips on foot consistently, really not enjoyable at all.

But the third part is by far one of the best written content of the entire game. It's a questline in which you help another of your former neighbors, a woman named Johanka, make sense of her visions. What follows is a fairly lengthy quest, with many very interesting situations, and some of the best character development you'll see in Kingdom Come. I won't spoil it, all I can say is that it does an excellent job of portraying the role of the Church in the Medieval society. I recommend the DLC at its regular price solely for this questline. If you enjoyed Kingdom Come's dedication to historical accuracy, this is mandatory to get.\t\t\t\t\t
