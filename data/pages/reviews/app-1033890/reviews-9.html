\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed the two quest lines in this DLC, and on a practical note the dog is very useful even if you skip the quests. Benefits of the dog: finding items/special spots, alerting of nearby enemies (growling), attacking enemies distracting them making combat easier, chasing down enemies/animals.

As for the two quests:

Theresa's quest you play as her before and during the events in Skalitz. I liked being able to explore the country side while it was still populated and getting to know the npcs a little more. Yes it is a little annoying since you have no horse and have to run everywhere but the area isn't huge so more minor annoyance. This is primarily plot/town quest line rather then combat, with a some stealth towards the end, though you can also fight if you choose.

Johanka's questline is similar as it involves a lot of talking to npcs but you still play as Henry, generally it's problem solving but you can choose how to end each quest (whether to be more greedy or benevolent). And at the end you can do a cool courtroom/investigation which has different endings depending on how well you do or if you care at all.

If you're looking for combat like Bastards dlc you'll be disappointed but if you like "town" quests with plot and character development it's a good time. Honestly I would buy it on sale just for the dog even if I didn't care about the quests.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis two-part (two separate quests) DLC adds the ability to have a dog that you can use to hunt, seek interesting points, and help defend you in combat.

The Theresa story line is unpleasant and poorly implemented. It is a retelling of the prologue from another person's point of view and you are unable to return to the main game until you finish the quest. Because all events are supposed to occur within a 2 day time span you cannot sleep or pass time. YOU CANNOT SLEEP TO SAVE AND THERE ARE NO AUTOSAVES IN HARDCORE MODE. The only way to save is by using a limited resource potion that saves the game. YOU ARE TRAPPED IN THIS QUEST UNTIL YOU FINISH IT. This is an awful story that basically just amounts to a bunch of fetch/messenger quests. It's also guilty of the all too common game development fallacy that "if the next objective is too close that means the game isn't challenging" that you see in open world games. Making me run from one side of the map to the next is not a "fun challenge" the way fighting an enemy is supposed to be, it's just an annoying inconvenience that developers falsely equate with difficulty.

Now, the second quest in this DLC is actually interesting and enjoyable and I would willingly replay it but I will avoid spoiling it.

It terms of the dog mechanic, it's somewhat helpful. It's nice to be able to use the dog to hunt and help fight off enemies. The problem is that the dog isn't really restrained from doing these actions unless you tell him to "stay". It gets annoying when you're trying to interact with something on the ground and he walks in your path (which happens so often that it feels like it was intentionally coded that way). If you're trying to level up your bow by hunting the dog often starts sprinting after an animal causing it to run away which again gets annoying. The combat mechanic seems subpar because while the dog will randomly launch himself at animals he doesn't seem to do the same in battle. While you're getting surrounded and having your ass beat by Cumans and an enemy dog, your dog is off biting someone that wasn't even a threat.

He seems to ignore enemy dogs unless you specifically tell him to attack one and they are the most annoying enemy because they latch on to you and prevent you from moving and are able to chase you down if you're trying to run from combat. Also, them being such a small target, it's difficult to line up the cursor to tell your dog to attack them so it would be nice if you're dog attacked other dogs by default which seems more realistic anyway.

Overall, most of the negative reviews have to do with Theresa's quest which is a fair point since it makes up 50% of the DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Woman's Lot comprises two quests, and includes a dog companion for Henry.
In Theresa's quest you play as her, starting right before the Cuman invasion that destroys your hometown. There quest isn't really focused around combat, but rather starts as just another day in Theresa's village life, doing chores, and ends up as running away and hiding from the invaders.
In Johanka's quest you play as Henry, as usual, and revolves around some visions from the Holy Mary that she has. The story is pretty good and sets Henry on a path of reflecting on his misdeeds throughout his adventure, seeking redemption.
I really don't think this DLC deserves the hate it's getting from other reviews. I thought the stories are well put together, where Theresa's offers a more grounded inisght into her daily life, and Johanka's a look into medieval religion. Some people complain they started Theresa's quest not knowing what they were doing, and were forced to play as her for 3 hours, but the game clearly lets you know that picking a certain dialogue option with her will lead to the DLC quest.
