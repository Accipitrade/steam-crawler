The best DLC of the game - and, yes, it's because of the dog

By far the best DLC for "KCD" and the only one I find indispensable ("From the Ashes" is good for making tons of money and "Band of Bastards", for getting the best gear). Especially because it adds Mutt, a dog that will accompany Henry and make his life way easier: combat, hunting, finding treasure and pursuing fleeing foes are greatly improved because of him. And with Mutt Henry gets a new skill: Houndmanship.

It also adds the huge quest "The Madonna of Sasau", concerning Johanka and religion. It comes with several subquests and more than one ending. It's one of the best quests of the whole game.

And it's the only DLC that lets you play with another character. You can experience Theresa's point of view during her life in Skalitz, the attack and how she managed to help Henry at the end of the Prologue. You should play her quest right away, because it works as a 2nd tutorial, especially for stealth, bow and arrows (of course she won't be a melee fighter; she can't wear armour neither use any proper weapon besides a simple axe) and working with a dog. If you're replaying the game, you can skip it, because the experience you get as Theresa is pointless for Henry. But it's definitely important for story reasons to play this at least once, as it will give you way more information about several of the characters.

I've read some negative reviews and almost all of them are incredibly stupid; as I said above, this is way more than just Theresa's quest. Don't listen to dumb people.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFar and away THE BEST DLC for the game. It can be started at any point you want to take a break from the main game (Don't leave it for the end, its better as a mid-way break). I would also recommend getting to know Theresa pretty well beforehand to sympathize with her as a character.
On to the review: Its amazing. Its immersive (to the point where you have to complete chores and live the life of a mill maid). That may not be appeasing to everyone, but I encourage you to let yourself be immersed to fully enjoy it. It made me laugh and cry. Trust me, it is emotional. Without spoilers, the dlc consists of two stories: one where you play as Theresa in Skalitz, and one as Henry where you help out Johanka. They are both top-tier and give you tons of player choice and non-linearity to the point that I was shocked how my decisions affected outcomes.
In short, its truly amazing and I hope Warhorse continues with this style and quality in the next Kingdom Come, despite the negative reviews. I think these players don't appreciate the realism of this rpg and they want more combat, which I understand but there is a lot of combat in the main game and the Band of Bastards dlc and this is much more story focused and it benefits greatly from it. Definitely a buy!\t\t\t\t\t
