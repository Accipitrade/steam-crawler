\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can absolutely recommend this DLC. There were several hours of content, and it was varied and based heavily on character development, and getting to know tons of background characters, rather than quests that amount to "kill all baddies" or the like. While it's based on character development, that's not to say that it's a story on rails, because it's not. As always in Kingdom come, you have a myriad of possibilities, and sometimes I was astonished how well thought out the consequences were. True, the Theresa story line won't have that many consequences for Henry in the end, but the story itself is worth it. The Theresa story line is good, and the Johanka story line is fantastic!

I bought this DLC at full price, and for me it was well worth it!

Buy it if you want to further immerse yourself in the universe, and develop the characters of Theresa and others. Don't buy it for loot for henry, because there's not much and the dog's not all that OP.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSorry guys, I love KCD, and I really wanted to like "A woman's lot", but this DLC didn't live up. Prequels are tough, and I question the wisdom of making a prequel specifically about this. Anyways, here's my breakdown:

What went right?
-Giving me Savior Schnapps, boy was that the right call, there are points that I would have rage quit without them.
-I enjoyed the opportunity to see Skalitz from another ground level perspective, and meet some people I didn't get to meet as Henry.
-It was nice to meet Bianca from a different perspective. She was obviously very important to Henry, and I hardly got to meet her in the core game.
-The scene you already know about where Henry saves Theresa was well done. It was everything it needed to be.
-Without spoiling, the scene in the mines was excellent. It had a goal, stakes, tension, and I at least imagined the possibility of failure. It's conceivable that the story could continue on a failed path instead of loading last save. I don't know if it does, and I don't feel like replaying this DLC to find out.

What went wrong?
-Lack of any stakes or tension through most of it, due to it being a prequel. Efforts were made to introduce life and death tension, but I already know who's going to die, so they can't string me along with any hope, and it's not a shock when anyone dies. That might not have bothered me, if they hadn't been trying to string me along with false hope and shock me when people die.
-There aren't any important choices to make, likely because multiple possible outcomes would interfere with Henry's story. I just follow the bouncing ball through a first person cut scene. Sure, there are a couple people I can be nice to or not nice to, but nothing that really matters.
-There is often only one way to solve problems, and it's sometimes hard to notice the one thing I needed to press 'E' on before Cumans busted the door down and killed me...like 4 or 5 times, before I found a way to glitch them, kill them in slow time, then find that stupid one way out...when it looks like there should be options...that don't work.
-Invisible barriers that kept me on the plot rails. And the invisible barriers dumped me into the very enemies I was trying to go around...FFFFFFFFFFFFFFF!!!!!
-A couple times my hand was forced and I had to fight. The Cumans were surprisingly easy to kill one on one. Group melee is as unmanageable as it always is in KCD, but all up I think I killed 8 Cuman soldiers. Should Theresa be able to do that?
-Fetch quests...so many fetch quests...just filling time I think.

What could have been better?
-Theresa's home needed more escape routes. I only have seconds to decide what to do, if it visually looks like a way out, at least give me a fighting chance. KCD is supposed to be all about thinking outside the box, and only one bouncing ball to press 'e' on is very out of character for this game.
-There are already logical break points in the story, at each of those have it cut back to Theresa and Henry talking, with the option to hear the rest of the story later. I spent the latter half of the DLC wanting to get back to playing Henry.
-Minor point, but am I the only one who's bothered that nobody ever seeks shelter from the rain. Anyone who has played this DLC (*cough*, or developed it) knows what scene I'm talking about, there should have been a laboured effort to move to shelter. It's kind of like when movie fire doesn't make smoke...it bothers me.

Anyways, that's my feedback. I recently bought all the DLCs as a package deal and I'm playing through again. I'm sorry to say, this one isn't worth the time, let alone the purchase.

God be with you Henry!\t\t\t\t\t
