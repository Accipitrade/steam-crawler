\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt needs to warn you about what you are getting into. Like so many others I thought it was just another dialogue option. I was relatively far into the game when I finally decided to ask her about it, so I had a bunch of nice things like a horse, armor, and weapons: things you noticeably don't have in this DLC. It feels like the Devs wanted to pad out how long it takes to complete the DLC by making you run around all over the place. It was just point your analog stick in a direction and hold B for a few minutes, talk to someone, and repeat. I started off doing all the optional side quests but eventually got so irritated I just wanted to rush through it all as quickly as possible since that's the only way to get out without reverting a save. It doesn't help that you already know what happened to everyone. I normally enjoy watching the cutscenes and listening to the dialogue, but I was spamming B to get through everything. I already know everyone dies so I'm not going to get attached to them. Having to search all over the place for flowers and bandages for a brother that you knew was just going to die immediately afterwards was horrible. I straight up wanted to just kill him myself and move on. The dog that was nothing more than a hindrance the whole time died the way he lived, stupidly. I don't get why Timmy is so much more annoying that Mutt, might just be because I was already so annoyed at everything that he just seemed more annoying. Ignored all the other people you could potentially help at the very end and was super happy to be done with it.

The parts involving the mines were the worst part. I got Sammy to draw a map, didn't matter. Must have been bugged because the page was blank and had to google where to go. Having to get to the mine after the invasion was horrible. They're all over the place, sneaking is really difficult, you have to trial and error which mine to actually go to, and again, you're walking for like 5 minutes assuming nothing goes wrong. Died like 3 times to enemies spotting me and once to walking someplace I wasn't supposed to and the game just immediately killing me.

This DLC basically takes everything bad about the base game and condenses it into this. If you want to say that's to let you know what it's like to be a woman in the Medieval period, fine. It did its job because I was extremely miserable the whole time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC is terrible. The story could have been covered with a cut scene. The game play consist of tedious fetch quest in which obvious solutions to problems are ignored. For example you need to find cloth or bandages; there is a large cloth bag full of apples right near where you start but you can't use it, or if you have extra clothing you can't use it, instead you must find normal bandages or salvaged linen from the clothing of corpses. One can get stuck in a crash loop if you choose the option to help the man with the twisted ankle in the angel of mercy quest. I legitimately like the main game so trust me when I say it was upsetting to not be able to return to it until you complete this DLC. The dog constantly got in the way of stealth kills. I cannot understand why one cannot put on the enemy armor to try to deceive the enemy. The caption during the loading saying people could not cross dress back then is not a good enough justification in an emergency situation.

It has content but the content is not good. It is the bits of an RPG that you do to get a reward but the reward is not worth it. I had to play for around 5 hours due to bugs to complete. I would have paid for the DLC just for the dog companion even though it does get in the way sometimes. just like real dogs. I did get a kick out of your shadow, your head doesn't move and your necks shadow stretches, had a good laugh about it for a few minutes but when it got dark I lost that small bit of fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA modest thumbs up. It's a 'meh' add-on. It adds companion a dog which is very use, which isn't really needed. The dog is kind of cool, but enemies don't really react to the dog, so it breaks immersion. For example, you can be trying to stealthily sneak around and the dog is running around barking and making noise, however, the enemies don't react to it. I assume that was done to prevent the dog from being a total negative.

The Theresa story is somewhat interesting, I feel like the writers did a fairly good job with the woman's perspective. Where the gameplay fails a bit is that the developers put in some very artificial barriers that stop you from taking a path that you think is the safest way to complete a task. By barriers, I mean you are running along the side of a hill and suddenly run into an invisible wall. You may have spent several minutes traversing terrain between enemies to get yourself there so that you are carefully avoiding confrontations with well armed and armored enemies. But the devs didn't want you to do it that way, so try again. Load, try a path, die, rinse, repeat until you get it right. Sort of like a Hitman mission. It's not really much fun, I enjoyed the cut scenes more than the gameplay in this DLC.

The main reason to buy this DLC is for the story, it fills it out a little more. Other than that, there is nothing compelling, and it may end up a frustrating thing to play through.\t\t\t\t\t
