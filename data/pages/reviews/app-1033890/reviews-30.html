\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC has a lot of negative reviews, and while some of them definitely have good points, a lot of them is just based on their unreasonable expectations for it.

Firstly, it's important to understand what kind of game KCD is, it's a hyperrealistic game, and so just that alone makes it not fun for many people.

I knew that this DLC would be very different from the main game. And it is, it's kind of like a story and simulation, showing the life of a woman during a te when women weren't allowed to do much. And yeah, of course its a challenge, what did these people expect? That we were gonna get to play some unrealistic girl who somehow can manage a sword and wear armor? No, like the game already explained, this is just not the case for the time period and to be honest I found that to be fun.

From a realistic standpoint, also thinking of my own limitations as a woman, I nor Theresa would be able to just take on a bunch of heavily armored men.

I personally don't like stealth, and at one part of the game this is what you do for the most part, so I kind of skipped that with cheats. However you can easily finish it by going inside the mine and grabbing a torch on the ground.

Theresa's model can wield a sword, so if you get to a point where you can't stand the stealth play you can just grab a overpowered sword by spawning it. But it's important to understand that you aren't supposed to be able to kill every cuman you meet and so getting upset about that is quite stupid. You are after all playing a woman who has no sword training, no strength training and etc, you're playing an average person.

I had fun doing errands and exploring the map, if you check out the achievements guides you can see that there's a lot to do, I think it was quite short, I wish you got to play more as her, but had fun with it nonetheless.

The only thing I don't like about the dlc is just the dog, I'm not a dog person, while I enjoy the company the constant barking really drives me mad lol. There is a mod that aliviates it a bit, but I still think he barks way too much.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed this DLC for the most part and really enjoyed getting to play as Theresa and see her story, but there were parts of it that felt extremely tedious and frustrating. I suppose there should be a bit more of a warning that the DLC is going to start besides a dialogue question with Theresa (which throws you into the DLC whether you like it or not lol.) So research this DLC and know what you're getting yourself into before clicking willy-nilly on certain dialogue options with Theresa, which is what happened to me! I didn't mind and enjoyed the DLC, like I said. But I could see how frustrating this could be for some, as it would take you out of the main game for a couple hours at least and there is no way to close out of the DLC and get back to the main game except reloading a previous save or finishing Theresa's story.
I would say this is going to be a worthwhile DLC for some and a frustrating slog for others. Personally, I felt it added to the story and I enjoyed it, but I played this relatively early in the game (new player) so I'm not entirely sure how this DLC stacks up against the rest of the game as a whole.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a surprise to see a mostly negative rating on this. Undeserved and mostly player issue I can tell you.

The storytelling of A Woman's lot is one of a kind. Not only does this show the events leading up to the Skalitz raid from Theresa's eyes, this also is a great showcase of what was expected of women in their daily lives back in the day. Maybe not in all detail but you can read between the lines and understand a lot about the roles of women in that kind of society. That part of the story is told amazingly and creates the framework for the events.

Now to the actual playthrough: as the player you know pretty much exactly what is going to happen and you get all possibility to "unknowingly" prepare Theresa for the inevitable. If you rush through this and expect to just glide through the second part of this to just stand idly by and watch everything happen and you not having to put in the least bit of effort then you will have a very hard time. If you are the kind of person who expects to just win "everything" with both hands tied behind your back, don't buy this. If you actually enjoy at least a tiny bit of challenge then buy this. If you like to overcome the impossible then it's a MUST BUY!

I actually prepared Theresa for battle (for fun, I will spoil that later), and my Theresa killed 30-odd Cumans all on her very own, which of course is totally unrealistic and did so not without some cheesing. So don't blame the game for being bad. Any playstyle works, except the "let's run into battle like a headless chicken"-tactic.

Stealth tactic spoiler:
Once the Cumans attack don't waste too much time but crawl through the window above the wooden chest to get to the barn. Turn to the little stream and quickly follow it down to the walled up ore separation workshops. Go around them and up the mountain to the right of the complex get to mineshaft "C". You will not have to fight a single Cuman in this portion of the game if you follow the above advice. If you chose to help your brother in the next chapter then you might have to fight/stealth kill one or two lightly armed Cumans.

Combat/cheese tactic spoiler (from how I played the DLC):
Do just enough chores to unlock the map. Only do the main chores once you really want to progress in the story.
The important bits are to go to Henry (when asked by Bianca), have a sword fight with him and practice mainly your defence. You can win easily over Henry if you defend 90% of the time and stab him once or twice after he has made 2-3 slashes.
Gamble - a lot. Once you've emptied the gamblers pockets he will always come back after about 15 min so you can do it again. Two of the traders that are on the square have Saviour Schnaps, the lady at the store has some I think, Bianca has 4, the herb trader down at the farms has some, outside the collapsed mine shaft is one on a box next to the entrance. Get all of them with your gambling money. You can also make more money by joining bow competitions.
If you don't know how to shoot a bow: First, get a bow that's your strength. Secondly, learn to aim down the arrow. When aiming, take your time. You have the first half of your staminas time to aim. If you get into the second half of your stamina you can reset your bow by pressing the right mouse button, charge your stamina and start again. When aiming the bow will follow the pattern of a laying eight, perpetually. Learn to use that to your advantage when aiming and once you've managed to hit your mark, hold your mouse still, remember at what position of the laying eight you released the arrow. You will be able to group your arrows neatly when following this advice, I can tell you that with confidence because I totally sucked at the bow in the beginning but managed to regularly win my bow shooting contests and make money.

You need to pracice the bow not only for your own skill, but for Theresa's skill as well. Sadly the competitions don't do much to increase her skill. But that has a sollution. Buy all the arrows the bowman at the target range has, you can as well buy his bows (you will be able to use the stronger bow later). If you can, also get all the arrows and bows Biancas brother has. Take all your gear and find the spot where the boars roam (pretty much west from the bow competition, in the woods), go uphill behind it and wait for boars to spawn (or deer, if nothing else). Make sure they are between you and the mining buildings that are downhill. Chase them down to the buildings. There are a lot of hedges and buildings there where the boars and deer can get stuck so you can actually shot at them point blank without wasting any arrows, ignore the meat, just loot your arrows back once done. Because having a lot of spare arrows and a decent bow is vital for when you have to fight the Cumans it's important that you do this until you can use the stronger bow and sword without problem. One of the traders on the square has a regular sword for sale. You'll need that. (At the shooting range there are two barrels with arrows, I left those alone but they would be to your advantage if you somehow manage to get them without being arrested.)

Gambling is a safe way to make money. At some point you will be able to actualy get a lucky die, which does absolutely nothing. Buy every potion that gives you any health back. Sadly you can't make your own potions. I only had one health potion and that happened to be enough but I wished I had had at least one or two more. Once everything is said and done, and you trained Theresa you should have about 10-11 Saviour Schnapps, 2-3 health potions (forgot their name), about 60 arrows (not all the best quality sadly), one somewhat strong bow and the Trusty Companion sword. Also a tip for later, get all the vegetable food as that gives you 1-2 health back a piece and about 15 bandages. If not for yourself you'll need them later.

Once everything starts going down the drain you are in your house with the Cuman. Backstab him and crawl through the window, go to the stream to your left (two cumans are there, looting a guard who has a better sword that yours, you can get that later), follow it down to the ore washing complex, go through its back gate and behold the flume. Ignore all Cumans and go to the right of it, and jump onto the ledge that is between the water wheel and the house, attract some Cumans and pick them all off with your bow and stabs with the sword once they position themselves in front of the ledge next to the wheel. They might get you sometimes but this is your safe spot, except for when you attract archers and the odd brave Cuman who choses to come after you. You can kill most relatively easily from there and attract them from far away and run back there. When shooting at them, remember to not aim for the breast plate of the most well armed parts of the body like visors and helmets. Aim for open face, legs, chainmail and charge your bow fully. A weak shot will most often just bounce off. This you can pretty much do until you have to run too far to get new victims. With your skill in defence you should be able to kill the lighter armed cumans in duels, but never engage two people at once unless you're truely amazing. Your dog can help attract people to your position as well as delay them from catching you. The dog can also eventually kill them but it will take a lot of attempts. Your dog doesn't die at this stage, only runns off when it's too badly hurt, it always comes back later. If not anything else, then that alone would be a way to kill everybody on the map, if you have the time and don't get too bored by it. You can not backstab captains and I think people with shields are also resistant. If you try to sneak and backstab someone, park your dog somewhere because he will ALWAYS mess it up for you.

Have fun, I know I had, and even without this cheesy challenge I liked the storytelling a lot, it humbles you in regard to civilans suffering in war.\t\t\t\t\t
