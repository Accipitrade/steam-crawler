\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou have to be prepared for the different direction this DLC takes you in. I was initially disappointed the Theresa plot line takes you out of the main story for an extended session, but once I got used to it, I found it quite moving. It has classic show-don't-tell elements, where the designers can convey a lot simply through her facial expressions as she watches events from afar. You feel for her and her lot. And there are some interesting nuggets about the attack on Skalytz.

The Johanka storyline is structured more traditionally, in that you can play it as part of the regular story. But I honestly found it the most moving writing in a video game I've ever experienced. Again, you have to have the right mind set because it takes the adventure is a decidedly spiritual direction. I was able to become involved in the story, which involves the nature of truth, the danger of pride, the restoration of repentance, but if you can't immerse yourself or put aside modern sensibilities, it would be more difficult, possibly seeming like annoying fetch quests, including probably the longest walk I took in the whole game. But getting the happy ending was among the most stressful and most satisfying things I've accomplished in this game. My relief was palpable! So well done!\t\t\t\t\t
