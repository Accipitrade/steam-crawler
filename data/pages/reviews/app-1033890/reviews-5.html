\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I really liked this DLC.

The Johanka quest line is great. You need to do a lot of right choices to get the good ending, but the game tells you about basically all of them, so I don't think it's a problem.

Theresa's story is good, and provides more depth to the characters from Skalitz. There are two minor problems with it though:
- It should be clearer that you will be leaving the main story for a while. Yes, Theresa does tell you that "the story is a bit long", but it should be an in-game popup or something warning you about it. Especially considering that this quest unlocks early in the game, it's possible to accidentally switch away from the main game at the very start.
- The combat is a bit imbalanced. Judging by the reviews it looks like most people tried fighting with a sword and failed horribly at it. I used a bow, and it honestly felt a bit overpowered to be able to fight several armoured soldiers as an unarmoured woman with no combat skills just because the bow kills anyone in 1-2 shots. Thankfully it's not a big deal, as combat is not a big part of the DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

I honestly see no flaw in it. The Theresa part is fun, because it adds some depth to the character, and it can make you see the Skalitz characters differently, but the Johanka quest (that unlocks later in the game) is simply sublime.
I don't want to spoil anything here, but it is simply one of the best side-quests chains in the game.
And then of course, you have Mutt the dog. Extremely helpful to have a dog, for hunting, as it makes the annoying task of hunting with low bow skill a piece of cake (he is a saviour in that early game Hans Capon quest in the woods), treasure finding and of course fighting multiple enemies, as he can briefly tie up an enemy and give you some time to fight the others. The Houndmaster skill tree is easy to raise and of course very helpful in general as it grants a few beneficial buffs.
A Woman's Lot is an excellent DLC. Ignore the negative reviews and give it a go.
