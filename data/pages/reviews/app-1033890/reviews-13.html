\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIgnore all the negative reviews calling this DLC "boring". The content is excellent and fits seamlessly with the rest of the game. It's just that it's heavily dialogue-based, focusing on fleshing out the side characters and giving the overall story more emotional depth, as well as providing huge amounts of sociopolitical commentary.

The first part of the DLC is essentially an extended tutorial, which complements the base game neatly. It is far more forgiving, which allows the player to experiment more with the mechanics. Thus, it is best played as soon as it becomes available, which is immediately after the prologue of the base game.

The second part of the DLC is arguably one of the most compelling B-stories I've ever seen in a video game since the Bloody Baron's from The Witcher III.

From a mechanical standpoint, you also get a dog companion, who is very useful gameplay-wise, but unfortnuately has very little personality.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKeep in mind:
Half of the reviews have only played HALF of this DLC.

The first half, you play as Thersa before and after the events of Skaltiz. From a storytelling and worldbuilding side of things, this is pretty interesting, although since you play as a typical village girl the gameplay isn't anything special. If you really like the roleplaying part of this game, or the beginning intro with Skaltiz, you'll enjoy this part.

The second part, and the one less talked about, actually is a very involved questline with Johanka that takes plays around halfway through the main story. Each one is decent each which remembers the player's choices, and culimnate all to a final quest that takes into account each of the previous quests. I don't want to mention much of it on account of spoilers, but its a decent amount of content. I really enjoyed this part myself, and many of the reviews neglect its existence. If you are hesitant because of the first half, i highly recommend at least looking into this quest line before you write this DLC off.\t\t\t\t\t
