\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to say, I'm glad that Warhorse Studios finally found their way with how to make DLCs. After the first two of the four promised DLCs turned out to be, let's say less than stellar in my opinion and left much to be desired, I was pleasantly surprised with the third DLC (Band of Bastards), which set a completely different tone compared to the previous two DLCs and was genuinely enjoyable to play through from start to finish. This turn of events made me even more so interested in how the studio would tackle the last advertised DLC, especially one that revolves around women in the middle ages.

Well, I'm happy to report to anyone looking into getting this DLC that it turned out just brilliantly! Without wanting to spoil the experience to anyone that's yet to go through this DLC (I reckon it's best for you to just experience this in a natural way yourself), the DLC offers up a bunch of great new quests revolving around two prominent female characters that Henry meets along his storyline. Oh, and you also get your very own dog to accompany you on your adventures!

One of these character's perspective will help fill a plot hole present in the main storyline that isn't addressed in the base game. Seeing this plot hold being filled, and experiencing it from the perspective of the respective character itself (and not Henry) not only helps fill the holes left by the base game, but also opens up a new gameplay dynamic that's not really offered in the base game.

The second set of quests revolve around the other character that Henry chooses to help. Henry's decisions in these quests will have a deep impact on the affected character and may even make the difference between life and death, so choose wisely! Hint: Nobody expects the [bohemian] inquisition!

Overall, this DLC offers a much greater focus on storytelling and has far more cinematic cutscenes than any of the previous DLCs, which makes it really stand out from the rest. In fact, on some occasions, the cutscenes truly immersed me into what was happening on the screen. Those moments made you feel like you're more watching an interactive movie than playing a video game! Props to WH Studios for achieving that, great work!

So should you buy it then?

I reckon you absolutely should! There's really no reason not to if you already enjoyed the base game and/or other DLCs you previously purchased. This one's simply a cut above the rest in terms of quality and not getting it would mean that you'd simply miss out on some quality content that WH put out with this one.

If its price is off-putting to you, consider waiting for a sale or for the DLC's price to naturally drop over time. It seems like the previous DLCs also dropped in price substantially compared to their release prices so I wouldn't expect this one to be exempt from that process.
Alternatively, if you're looking into getting the game first and just wondering what DLCs are available, you may also want to consider getting the newly released Royal Edition of the game, which includes all the previously released DLCs plus some extras. It contains really all that the game has to offer and for a very good price too. So if you're just looking into getting the game, I recommend that version as it's worth it even if you dislike some of the DLC offered as part of it (you'll still get your money's worth out of it, that I can assure you of).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinally, after three consecutive atrociously overpriced DLCs, we see a return to form for Warhorse who have now proven that they haven't forgotten how to make and release quality content without simultaneously charging you through the bum for it too. It's a decent-sized DLC with interesting quests, great writing, and choices that actually have consequences. I have only a few small complaints: the advertised playtime (as quoted by a dev on a dev stream) is allegedly 10-15 hours, whereas I only managed to get 9 (and that's without skipping any dialogue scenes, so I don't know how you could reach 15 hours unless it's implied that you're supposed to play it through more than once); the DLC was actually a little disingenuous in its marketing - for over half of the DLC's new content, you're actually playing as Henry and not Theresa; and finally, some Steam achievements are broken, it took me multiple tries to get a few of them to pop properly.

But overall, it's decent (not exceptional) value for money and provides multiple thoroughly enjoyable experiences that will appeal to fans of the base game. 7.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving experienced the full game and all other DLCs for KCD, I bought A Woman's Lot the day it was released. The journey of Theresa during the main stories prologue was a amazing. The writing was spectacular as it wasn't just a new "Henry" character, but a completely different style of narrative with different difficulties, all while maintaining the same feel of the original game. And then there was Johanka's story.

For Johanka, you play as Henry still, but you are assisting Johanka through her "visions" and the repercussions of them in a time appropriate manner. I was honestly quite disappointed with the DLC until the very end. I won't spoil anything, but I was emotionally drained by all the work that went into her quests and how everything culminated. I even scum saved at one point to try and get things right.

Overall, Warhorse's development team has done another amazing job in what seems to be their final DLC for Kingdom Come Deliverance. The artists, voice actors, writers, and musicians should be proud of every minute of content they gave us. This was the perfect icing on the cake... and remember to tell your new dog that he is a good boy!\t\t\t\t\t

4.5/5
