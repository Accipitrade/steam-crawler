\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC was one of my favorites. I enjoyed it more than Band of Bastards but not quite as much as From the Ashes.

While Theresa's story is certainly interesting and it was fun to play as a different character than Henry, the true gem of this DLC is the Inquisition story line in Sasau. This was a very well done side quest and it was cool to see a realistic version of the famous Inquisition of the church.

Another perk of this DLC is the introduction of the Houndmaster skill. Although I played all of the DLC's after completing the main game, I was able to get my Houndmaster skill to level 10 by just playing the DLC's. It is a natural addition to the game and is certainly useful in a fight. I recommend this DLC at full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKCD has always been about immersion, and this dlc is no different. Allowing the player to experience what's it like to be a woman in medieval times, specifically focusing on Theresa's point of view is smart, since most players would have already been familiar with her, up close and personal.

This dlc ties some loose ends in the original story. Ever wondered why Theresa got along with Henry a little...too well? Ever wondered why Theresa's uncle is a miller? Ever wondered how Theresa survived the cumans, and later ended up saving Henry? Did it ever occurred to you whether Theresa taunting those bandits was mere recklessness or a plot twist? Also, how is it that you see Matthew, Fritz and Johanka all coincidentally ended up in Sasau? The dlc even goes far to explain the origins of Bianca's ring, you know, the damned ring you must have hauled with you for 90% of the entire game?

There's plenty of negative reviews, and its pretty understandable. Theresa can't equip armor, can barely sword fight, and worse still, can't ride a horse. Gameplay is focused mostly on objective seeking, i.e. fetch quests. You can skip most of the quests, but some has story significance and can alter how you complete later objectives. So, if Theresa can barely fend off a dog, and we already know that she was nearly raped but somehow managed to stay alive, how did she dealt with the cumans swarming the entire village? The answer is obvious: she can't. A third of the game is stealth focused, and anything more than a 1v1 fight will likely end with you cursing at your keyboard. Many of the negative reviews focuses on the boring gameplay, but its more than likely the stealth sections that really pisses people off, especially if they skipped the early quests as the later stealth sections can get enormously brutal without some key items.

This dlc should be recommended for those who loved the original KCD game since it has serious plot significance, unlike the other dlcs. If nothing else, this dlc also opens up the chance for Henry to command his own dog. If you ever played through the game without the dlc, and wondered how OP dogs are in the game, well, here's your chance to own an OP dog of your very own.

Oh, and to the devs: You could've dealt with Tinker's death a little better. No seriously. I already knew the dog won't survive this, but the way the he died felt real cheap and lazy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood:
First. I recommend solely off the additional things this DLC brings, like houndmaster. It's awesome, thank you. It's also very cool that an additional perspective to the prologue is available through Theresa if you choose to participate.
There is a well animated backdrop with the invading army running over the hill and burning Skalitz. It just looks cool. Good job. Theresa is a great character with a lot of depth.




Bad:
Why I'm giving it the thumbs down. The quest timing / presentation.

I didn't realize I was starting the quest. It's presented as soon as you get done with all the initial tutorial crap (prologue) in the way of speaking to Theresa. Henry wants to know how she escaped Skalitz. I legit thought it going to be dialogue. But no. It was the DLC quest itself. The DLC quests are tedious and the DLC feels like a second tutorial..... GOOD GOD.

The first tutorial is so long and brutal, but now you have another one for 45-90 minutes where you're probably at a point where you just want to do RPG stuff. Explore and run around free. The game JUST opened itself up to the player.

The mines are REALLY annoying. Theresa sucks at combat. Unless you're the perfect sneak master (or really patient), you will probably have to fight because the fetch quest items are next to enemies.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good dlc but it is important to know what you are walking into.
This covers the day before, the day of, and the day following the initial events of the base game but from Theresas point of view. She has no training with melee weapons but has some skill with a bow and her dog. Once you begin you must complete it to return to the normal game. The story itself is a nice change of pace from the rest of the game however it does get tedious. When the raid kicks off you don't have many options other than use stealth and a bow which can be annoying if you don't like that play style.
Once the side story is complete you will gain access to the to a new skill that deals with having a dog and the butchers dog will be able to follow you around and help you.
Overall a good dlc but i wouldn't call it a must have unless you really want the dog helping you. grab it if it's on sale\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWomen's Lot is the final and in my opinion best DLC for Kingdom Come: Deliverance. It is also a lot longer than the rest, expect 4 to 10 hours if you are prepared to go flower-sniffing.

However, and this is a very strong however, make sure you realise what you are getting because this is by no means something that will appeal to all!

Contrary to the other DLC, that focus on economy (From The Ashes), puzzles (Amorous Adventures) or combat (Band Of Bastards), Women's Lot focuses on role-playing.

The DLC follows the stories of 2 women, independently of each other: Theresa and Johanka. The purpose of both stories is to have you experience what it must have been like to be a woman in the middle-ages and - I can guarantee you - it will not be a pleasant experience. However brilliant this DLC may be, it is depressing, it is boring and it is brutal. And that is exactly what makes it brilliant. If this is not something you can live with, I strongly advise you not to buy this DLC because you will be disappointed. Even the name of the DLC is a dead giveaway ...

Theresa's story in particular has you play *as* Theresa before, during and right after the Skalitz raid. You get to see Henry's story from another perspective, but also given some background info on for example Johanka and what exactly happened to Theresa.

Like you'll read in many other reviews, you mostly get some menial tasks. Fetch-quests; boring, stupid and totally un-epic do-this-do-that stuff. And that is exactly the point. You are not a glorious knight, you aren't even the son of the town's blacksmith, you are a nobody and your personal value is not much higher than that of a farm animal. You can't fight, you're always the weakest, you can't do anything. You must do what others tell you, how annoying or horrid it may be.

You'll be suffering, you'll be miserable, you'll constantly feel that the game is unfair to you. And just when you think that you're done with the dreadful quest you've been given and expect that the worst is over, the game throws an even harder or horrible task at you. "You want me to do what??? You've got to be kidding right?"

Nevertheless there are some lights in the darkness: just talking to other villagers for example, about other menial things, or seeing Johanka smile when you did her a favour, ... It are those small things that make it all worth it. I literally spent over an hour talking to every NPC in and around Skalitz and was surprised by the effort that the developers must have put in what is, in the grand scope of things, a completely irrelevant part of the DLC.

That small-chat with other nobodies - before any Cumans are introduced even - for me alone was worth playing Theresa's story. Like I said this is a role-players DLC and you should only get it if you want to actually role-play.

The second story is a set of more traditional quests, where you - as Henry this time - help Johanka. But the theme remains the same and don't go expecting a happy little story about Snow White singing peaceful songs in the company of rabbits and deer and riding off in the sunset with a handsome prince ... Be prepared to have your face pressed down in the mud and to be trodden on once again. One of the possible outcomes of that second story is without any doubt the most brutal scene in the entire game.

Other than the awesome thematic stories and a sinking feeling in your stomach, you'll not complete this DLC with much to show for it. There is no great loot, you'll not get rich (or more rich than you already were), you'll not earn a great title, ...

However, the DLC gives you access to your own dog with a special skilltree.

The dog is nice to have during combat or hunting, or just to keep around you for company. Again, this DLC is role-players territory and having your own dog is part of that.

For me, this DLC is pure gold. For you? Perhaps.\t\t\t\t\t
