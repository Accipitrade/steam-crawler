\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe DLC isn't bad, it adds some interesting alternative gameplay, and I would recommend it (Actually I do) if it wasn't for a very big BUT which makes me give this a thumbs down.
And this BUT hasn't as much to do with the DLC content per se as with the consequences for normal gameplay: It adds dog companions. Not just for the player, but for random bandits and Cumans as well. And that's my gripe: It basically eliminates stealth play where you could sneak into a bandit camp at night and murder them if your combat skills are still too low to take on 3 or 5 of them at the same time: With the DLC there will always be a dog present who growls at you and attcks, alerting the sleepers. It doesn't matter if you have the animal friend perk from the stealth skill tree since this is apparently it's own mechanic, independend from the usual dogs sleeping in the frontyard who will no longer bark at you. The new mutts will always growl, always attack and always alert their masters. I don't know if this is as intended or if it is a bug, but I do not like it. At all. If you're cool with this since you always rush in where angels fear to tread anyways then this should be no biggie. But if you're into the stealth side of the game the DLC is not 100% beneficial to your favourite gameplay mode.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as KCD DLCs goes this one gives something for what it is worth, but it also brings its own stupidity with it.
For the price you pay, you better expect that 7h of fetch quests won't throw you off. They aren't bad, just you can plainly see what they are - a simple filler between story. In between them, there are some solid memories to be found. For example, that moment where you used to save Theresa from Cumans is very well done playing from her perspective. Of course there are others like it as well. So all in all, I would say that quests gave me around 10h of gameplay. Of which 7h where kind of meh and other 5h genuinely fun.

To be honest, I was looking forward to this DLC just to get a dog. And boy is he dumb... I mean full ♥♥♥♥♥♥ mode. He won't do ♥♥♥♥ for you at first. I took me like 5h until having him gave me some bonus (+charm), while he was complete nuisance in combat. He won't help you at all, just get in the way for the most time and get hit by you or the enemy. It is very annoying. You try to make combo against enemy on hardcore mode and in the middle of it you hear whining. Apparently, while you where concentrated on your enemy, your dog decided to go for a drink from puddle between you two. And this happens all the time. After a while, when you train him to help you in combat, he will be running around enemy waiting for animation to kick in, which sometimes never even start. So you can't rely on him at all. It's cool when he manages to help, but it is too wonky to acknowledge him as your companion. Even as background character, whose main purpose would be to keep you company, he fails miserably. Often time when doing simple tasks like picking herbs he will suddenly get in the way and instead of picking herb, useless dialog shows up. He will scare off animals when you hunt. It's cool when you can order him to fetch what you've hunted, but he will do it so slowly that you are better of just go pick animal yourself. He will block your way when you sneak and try to rush for knockdown until enemy gets up and spots you. Only after playing game for 10h or so with him you will start to feel that he becomes a bit useful.

So all in all, for your money you will get a lot of filler quests, some great and memorable quests, a useless dog for the most part, some new weapons.
Looking at the price, I would say it is worth it. But don't have your hopes too high as I did.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't like the concept of super long flashbacks where all the progress I make doesn't translate into anything useful for my main character. I'm also not a fan of closed of areas in RPGs where you are kinda stuck doing terribly designed tedious quests. That trap can be a temporal one (as it is here) or being constricted to a certain area.

This feels a bit like the part in Dragon Age where your party gets trapped in the mind of a demon and you have to spend a lot of time solving puzzles with new mechanics only to get back to what you were doing and progress the main story.

The Theresa story also ruined the love story and both characters. Thankfully I don't really care that much about them in the first place, but I'd surely be taken aback slightly when confronted with the fact that my main man Henry was training with wooden swords only to be beaten by a girl who never trained a minute in her life. Said girl then proceeds to kill a bunch of people in a mine (because it's easier than sneaking past them), kill a hand full of cumans and rescue Henry who now that the girl he really loved is out of the picture can fall in love with her.

Up until now I had imagined that the burning of Skalitz was kinda bad, but in fact it was all coming up Theresa, that day! For her the family was baggage. Lazy people and criminals that dragged her down and tried to marry her off for money. And then of course the guy who she really loves was happy with another woman. Seeing her family and that woman being killed by Cumans was the best thing that ever happened to this criminal murderer. Now she can trap the guy she loves by guilting him into liking her and she's all doing it with the help of criminal millers, which makes even more sense now.

At least for wasting all these hours on tedious quests that reveal the pure evil that is 'Theresa" you get a shield and a doggo.
