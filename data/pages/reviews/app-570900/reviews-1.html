\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSince the world was "shut down" due to the pandemic crisis, probably a lot of us have missed not only the social gatherings but also all the cultural legacies - from theatre play to such a thing as sculptures, paintings or other forms of art.

Virtual Reality technology was probably like a relief in all of that, through the possibilities of its use, not just for gaming, but also for more stimulating and intellectual things. "Art Plunge" is one of these VR creations, where instead of playing in some very demanding game, we are facing cultural and artistry legacy, without leaving our home environment.

Instead of a casual game, once you put on your VR set - you end up in the gallery featuring 5 paintings you may choose from; Girl Reading a Letter, or Starry Night are just examples, same goes for the very famous worldwide Mona Lisa, which is also appearing and is possible to be "seen differently".

You see: Once you choose the art in the gallery, you may view it from the inside, turn around the space created inside of the art, and expose yourself to all the details on a whole new level.

I'll be honest here: While I adore Da Vinci's creations and the legacy he passed on to new generations, the moment when Mona Lisa was following my movement with her eyes was quite astonishing.

As a result of the proportions of characters from paintings, the active environments, vibrant colours, and the sensation of space surrounding the player, we have ready even more enjoyable VR experience than we might imagine.

"Art Plunge" is something you might spend your time with for like 20-30 minutes. It is about stopping for a moment, exploring the art and enjoying yourself just like during the "real" visit to the art gallery or museum. The only minus here is the fact, that there are just 5 paintings to interact with, and the possibilities of interaction itself are very limited.

On the other hand with the price, size, quality and idea itself, it is worth buying on Steam and giving it a go. I don't regret any moment or a second spent in it - at all.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOMG! The 3D Mona Lisa is outstanding! I can say it adds more value to the masterpiece by Leonardo! You know that Gioconda's eyes seem to follow you when you move in front of the painting, right? Well, here you're in front of a living 3D Gioconda, a very realistic and accurate minimal 3D animation. She moves slightly her neck, her head and her eyes to follow you when you walk in every direction; even when you kneel down, she bows her head in a very realistic way! She looks alive! Her smile, her facial expression, here they gain more nuances and meanings. She is a bit enigmatic, a bit naive, a bit childish, a bit sly, a bit flirtatious, a bit seductive, a bit attractive. Sometimes it's like she's playing with you! Few accurate movements of the eyebrows are enough to give more depth and nuances to the enigmatic expression of the Gioconda! Developers dare to confront the Leonardo masterpiece and transpose it in a 3D context; the result is very smart, fresh but still respectful of the original work. Leonardo would appreciate!
I liked also the poetic atmosphere of the Girl Reading a Letter at an Open Window, with the view on the harbor beyond the window; but I would like the reflection on the window to be animated too! All the 3D paintings are worth of your time, very respectful of the original works. This is the best way to enjoy art in 2021. However you need a high definition crystal clear headset, at least like the Reverb G2, and a good GPU.
Just one complain: you cannot do a walk in the 3D paintings, you can just take a few steps around! A minor one: the black hole at your back is disturbing. That's ok for a less than 2 euro app!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am very on the fence about this title. I've seen people saying good things about it for a while so I finally picked it up. To be honest I'm a little nonplussed by the experience. I can see what they are trying to do with it, but I've had a VR headset for a few years now and, to quote Blade Runner, "I've seen things you people wouldn't believe". Stepping into a painting at this point doesn't have the wow factor that I think they are relying on. I am however going to keep this one and try it out on people who haven't experienced VR to see how it works with what I'm assuming is their target audience.

As far as the actual experience goes, it's a mixed bag. Starting with the best:
- Girl Reading a Letter at an Open Window is really well done and gives you a feeling of being in a location.
- Mona Lisa is nicely done in the foreground but I'd have loved to see some depth added to the background.
- The Birth of Venus is pretty good. The individual characters do have some nice detail and motion, but they way they float around each other does feel a little like you are seeing cardboard cutouts of each element being moved around on strings.
- The Creation of Adam is a bit weird. Their interpretation of the larger background is hard to fathom and it feels like this detracts from the original more than it adds.
- Starry Night is the one I was most disappointed by unfortunately. It feels like the painting has had some movement added to it, but then it's just been applied to a sky box. For such a dynamic painting the immersive version just lacks any depth for me. There is a little village in the scene but it almost becomes just a flat texture spread across the wall.

Again, I don't think I'm the intended audience, but for me it felt like it showed potential but then failed to live up to it, especially when you compare this to something like Dreams of Dali which is free (but has it's own issues).\t\t\t\t\t
