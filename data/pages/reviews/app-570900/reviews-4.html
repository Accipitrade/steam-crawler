\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis lets you look at a few (very few) famous art pics in VR.

It was cheap enough so I decided to give it a look.

I didn`t expect subtle animation. The Creation of Man picture was quite effective, though I was a bit disappointed that the word `God` was not with a captal `g`, seemed disrespectful somehow. The Birth of Venus was good too.

It was quite effective.

But it was the paintings that were painting from life which really got to me.

In the `Girl reading a letter` picture it looked like she was alive as she was blinking and moving slightly, then I looked out the window to see birds flying. I wanted to see more, but then the `holodec` squares pop up, bursting the immersion.

In the Mona Lisa, it was like she was looking at me, if I moved to the left and right a bit her head and eyes followed me. I noticed her chest is moving slightly to her breathing, which is one reason why she seemed alive. Nicely done.

For a moment it felt like I`d stepped back in time and was standing in a completely different and wondrous period to today`s world. It was a breath of fresh air.


Criticisms... Too few pictures for the price. I sorely wish they add more paintings from other artists such as Reubens, Van Dyck, Degas, Eduard Monet, delaCrox, the Pre-Raphaelites and more. I`m really interested seeing the down-to-earth depictions rather than impressionism which doesn`t work as well in my view.

Also the girl`s reflection in the the "Girl reads letter`, does not animate with her. Fix that and it`ll be perfect.

Maybe even go further and give us full 360 views in the art or area they were painted in!

Thumbs up, please do more.\t\t\t\t\t
