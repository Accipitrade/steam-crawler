\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a game with a religious theme, I'm impressed. It's not trivia based and the tactical decisions that have to be made provide an engaging challenge each game round. I enjoy the strategy of using different prophets with different abilities, and the rank up feature is unique. Once I realized you need to hard press to build altars as quickly as possible and only "manage" the number of sin clouds and idols on the map (as opposed to eliminating them) I did fairly well.
But this game is punishingly hard at times. The random aspect of the card draw can be absolutely devastating. Regularly I would think I have a game in the bag and then a series of card draws would make it impossible to win and seal my fate in defeat. Some individual random card draws are so powerful they can completely flip the game and negate several rounds worth of work and decisions. I have been 1 altar away from winning several times, only to see a card drawn that turns the majority of my altars into idols and the game is over.
So If you don't enjoy challenging games with the potential of uncontrollable random events that will destroy any strategic position you may have held, this game may not be for you.
I am giving the game a thumbs up primarily because I personally happen to enjoy the challenge of playing games that can be hard or impossible to win, and I enjoyed the clever and unique implementation of the game's biblical theme and setting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun Bible based game; I didn't even know these existed. It reminds me a little bit of Pandemic in some of the mechanics, for instance the spreading of sins.
Each turn is represented by the reign of a king of Isreal (hence the name). You have to move your prophets around to the different cities of Israel to fight the spreading of sins and idols. Random events can make the turn either easier or harder, depending on whether the current ruling king was a good or a bad king.
The game mechanics are easy to understand and the tutorial well made. within a few minutes you can start your first game. There even is a Study mode where every round you have a question about the Kingdom's era as told in the Bible.
The game looks very nice and clear, without any bright flashy colors. Very relaxing.

In conclusion, if you want a board game different from all these be cunning / make money / crush your friends types, you can try Kings Of Israel.\t\t\t\t\t
