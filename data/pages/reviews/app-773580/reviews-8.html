\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like tycoon/business sims and especially game dev ones, this one's right up your alley. Having said that, a few things to note:
-In terms of complexity and attention to detail, it ranks higher than both Game Dev Tycoon and Mad Games Tycoon, but lower than Software Inc. The only exception to that is the hardware platform feature (i.e., releasing your own console), which can be fleshed out much more. Right now it feels like an afterthought just to match their competitors.
-It's less sturdy than its counterparts. A lot of seemingly trivial things can crash the game (e.g., pressing Esc trying to move a misplaced door)

Having said that, it's as fun as all its counterparts. In particular, the game development cycle doesn't end up being about stuff-all-features-you've-researched-so-far in all games. (disclaimer: I played the other 3 before I played Game Dev Studio, and Software Inc isn't 1-to-1 comparable).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe concept of this game is great! I'm not, personally, a huge fan of pixel graphics, but the premise of the game is good enough that I got past that part. The biggest issue that I see with this game's execution? Is the sheer randomness of employees asking for raises and requesting vacations. Wouldn't be a problem in and of itself, except that if you don't grant them these things? They get angry and quit. Meanwhile, it takes you five years to make a game of any significant quality with the staff that you can afford to keep around. So, what ends up happening? Is that everyone goes on vacation mid development, which slows your development down, and then requests raises, increasing your overhead, while you're still waiting to collect on their efforts. Twenty years into my gaming studio's existence and I've only managed to produce six or seven titles? None of which ever got a rating above 7.5, btw, despite my attempts to put the best technologies available into the games.

And then there's the rapidfire rise and fall of platforms, which cost you an exorbitant amount of money to even get licensing for. So, you wind up spending 150k on a license that you never get to use because the platform was abandoned before you even got the first game completed due to the above mentioned situations. I purchased this game thinking that it was a business model simulator, but it turns out that it's really just a Roguelike mock up of one. I am sooo glad that it was only ten bucks. I would be infinitely more furious if it had been any more than that.

This game would be absolutely phenominal if the devs would just stop with the RNG-based crap, and maybe do some actual research into platform generation cycles and such. (In one playthrough attempt? The 'sony' equivalent washed out and went bankrupt after their PSX equivalent model was released. Like. Within a year. But hey! The Apple equivalent made a great console that playthrough!
