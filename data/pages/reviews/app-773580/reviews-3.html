Good Addition to a Burgeoning Genre
It's strange how a seemingly very niche genre seems to actually gets a decent number of games for it. Game Dev Studio seems to both have more depth than the other two games I've played (Game Dev Tycoon and Mad Games Tycoon) while also being less polished than either of those.
While there is far, far more going on in the management sim side than Game Dev Tycoon, these aspects don't feel quite ready, with object placement at times being tedious and employee characteristics not feeling quite as important as they seem to be. However, the ideas feel like a big step in the right direction and much more thought out than in other examples.
While it is able to bring a slightly different take on the game creation process, it still nonetheless has a detached feeling at the end of the day with more of a business lean than a creative one. The lack of Themes and Genres was definitely felt though there appears to be mods to help with that. Expansions and Engine Creation felt much more intuitive and acquiring the few themes and genres present did feel more realistic than it's competitors.
When it comes to physical production, Game Dev Studio seems to follow the same path as Game Dev Tycoon and ignore physical production for the most part, unlike Mad Games Tycoon. But where it exceeded both was it's interest in character development with more detailed personalities and specialties.
Unless I missed something, the game conventions and award shows did feel clunky and a bit obtuse, felt more tacked on them anything else. I did appreciate the wide variety of advertising options though.
The graphics are certainly appealing, feeling very Stardew Valley like. I wouldn't mind if it had been a tad more polished around the edges but it's comfy. While the music doesn't disrupt the mood, it's not very memorable.
Overall, I feel like Game Dev Studio and the other two games I've repeatedly mentioned are all progressing towards the same conception and adding their own takes, hopefully a sequel to one of them merges what works between them to get a fully fleshed out and realized example of the Game Development game.
I recommend Game Dev Studio and would really like to see it developed more or see a sequel. The price feels fair though I'm not sure if it would keep everyone going very long unless they were already a fan of the genre so may seem a tad high. At discount though, it's a gem.\t\t\t\t\t
