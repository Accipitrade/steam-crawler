I am writing a review to such a thorough extent due to my passion in tycoon and managment games. Any game developers exhibiting effort and innovation in this area deserve feedback for their product.

The developer is showing a lot of love to the game surprisingly and is keeping up with bugs and crashes reported.

This game has displayed a pioneering design and depth to game development games with its focus on designing a complete experience of the world of game developing as opposed to the raw game developing structure of all other game dev games. The game lacks in areas that are of minute importance (partly) and can easily be fixed with tweaks, adjustments and additions to content. I will go on to describe the pros followed by the cons of the game:

(Scroll all the way down for verdict)

Pros


Game depth

This is the most innovative part of the game. The developer/s has done a great job of including as many aspects of the gaming development world as he can with features ranging from group activities to an array of individual game development skills and traits exhibited uniquely by staff.

Liveliness

The game has managed to create an interactive atmosphere both in the immediate scope of the player (in the player's game developing studio) and the world in which the player interacts. Let me explain

Staff will chat around about different events going on in the game world and even the games they create and release. The fact that most of the information they talk about is accurate and in some cases enlightening (in that they might talk about the features of a new engine which came out recently and other stuff) creates life to the game. Every employee seems to be alive and be able to articulate their own thoughts. Something which has not been seen in other games.

The game has also done a good job involving the gaming world by creating rivalries between gaming studios and adding a plethora of events which take place around the world. From gaming expos to platform and technology releases; most possible events that take place are there.


Modes

Game modes is a feature new and unique to the game dev genre in this game. while new and uncertain, it does add a lot of potential and from the top of anyone's head one can imagine the possible scenarios that can be rendered and simulated. The reason why it's in the pros is because it has potential as opposed to the quality of the modes currently in place.


Options

The game does a decentjob of making the player feel like they can chose to do anything they please. Almost every component of the game has options associated to it that the player can change in some form and the game developing process is a grand example of this feature. You'll find that when a game is being developed there are about 10 decisions you can make associated to the game. In some areas however, the contrary is evident but those options are more related to objects and materials.



Cons

Overdiversification (The root of all good and evil)

While the developer/s were able to create this game and encompass a great scope of the game developing world. It feels like it has created issues in places that needed a larger focus and especially the most important aspect which is game development (which I will mention later).

Game development
Easily the most important aspect of the game has had both a positive influence and a negative. You'll find that they've innovated the game developing process by staff interests; Contributing staff can boost an aspect of the game by giving input from their interest (a staff member may have an interest in martial arts which will boost the combat aspect if you include it.).

While this may be a great addition to the game developing process, the dev/s have overpowered it; if you do not have a staff member boosting an aspect then the game will perform worse. Seems legit so far. However, if you do not include an aspect of the game which is not boosted by employees at all, then the game will also perform better. In fact, in most cases if you manage to only include boosted aspects in a game then it will likely recieve 100% for that section.

Leading to the following problem. A game can lack in aspects directly related to the theme-genre and still get 100%. You can create a game about World War 2 and if you don't include guns, it's okay as long as every aspect which can be boosted is boosted. You'll likely get 100% if obviously you're including all other features on the engine. This makes for an easily exploitable system and at heart, it chips away at my interest slowly, game after game.


Objects

The only good thing coming out of the placeable objects in the game really are tiles, it's nice to be able to change the floor for once in a game dev game. However.... inhale

There is a miniscule amount of objects in the game, lack of diversity and progression to unlock new items really makes this area of the game dull. Employees do not care about the environment in which they work in either and thus all you really need is a bit of food, water, a toilet and you're good to go. This however isn't a very important aspect of the game, it just kind of exists. It would be nice to see performance enhancing environments such as in other games or employees desiring a minimal standard for the environment.


Tutorial

I think the developers had the wrong idea of tutorial when they created story mode. Most of the revies I've seen say there is no tutorial. Well there is, but it's called story mode. I believe a tutorial should be created to guide the player through all the aspects of the game without caring about money. Give him 2 mil and explain literally everything without pressure. In story mode (tutorial mode), you have been hired by an investor to make games. He walks you through the basics and then says "ok now I want a game in 2 years or you're done"... and you really are done if you dont satisfy, if you take your time you're done. Not a very friendly new player environment.

In my opinion story mode is a good addition to the game. It would be nice to create some sort of personal achievement scheme where the next goal is to create a game which sells "x" amount with no time limit while increasing the challenge as the player moves to next levels etc. That would be very interesting.

Tutorials should have NO CHALLENGE to the player, they should be simple and complete going from square one to a complete knowledge of the game while guiding the player. I would love to see this implemented and trust me, so would the rest of the community.


Overall
The game has made undoubted innovation and pioneering into the game development theme and deserves credit for it. There was a lot of effort shown through it and as I mentioned earlier, most of the cons are minute issues which can be easily remedied. Some of these issues are however very important and need fixing asap. Remember this is a game developing game and that aspect should shine above all else. A great effort nonetheless and I'm hopeful the dev/s maintain this momentum and continue to care for this game.

The game is worth the $10 it asks for

The Verdict as of 14/3/18 for this game is
8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was one of the testers of this game before it was released and I enjoyed it back then, but now the game has come a long way and has added a lot of new stuff that will create, personally, one of the best Game Dev experiences.

Why GDS, over other 'Game Dev' games?

GDS goes more deeper than others. Some other games feel like choices don't matter, while in GDS you will only get better if you make the right choices.

Creating games isn't easy. Because a game has more features doesn't mean it will be better, especially if your knowledge in some aspects isn't high. Basically, you can't make guns realistic if your team doesn't have the knowledge of how guns work.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn amendment to an old amendment:

Since this is still one of the highest rated reviews for this game, I feel like adding a disclaimer about Mod Support. In the past, I've said to not get excited about its functionality in the game as next to nothing was being made.

Having now made three to four mods for Game Dev Studio, that does not come as a surprise. Mod support for Game Dev Studio is fundamentally broken and may never be fixed. Easily the most indicative sign of this is that the official mirror for the source code has been 404'd for years at this point, and the only real solution available is to use to an incomplete and unofficial reproduction of it created by someone who was trying to do everything themselves before the code itself vanished. But let's say you can ignore that and you want to make a mod. Two out of the four I've published immediately crash if you install them through the Workshop, but work perfectly fine if you add them through the game's internal mods folder. The reason for the crash is so absurd that it cannot be on anyone but the developer—a variable that is vital to the game itself, that would outright break the game if it was absent during specific tasks, somehow becomes null if you access your mods through the Workshop. You also can't update your mods, as that, too, has been broken for years.

I've enjoyed my time adding new content to this game, but it really isn't worth the effort at this point in time.



This is about the third time I've written this review, and for good reason: the developer, who is really awesome and dedicated to their project, keeps on adding stuff. While the original release of this game could have had a little more content, the frequent updates put out by him has not only fixed this, but has impressively improved upon some of the games faults at launch. While some people will buy games on instinct if they know just how dedicated the devs are to community feedback, I know not everybody will. So, why is Game Dev Studio worth 10 bucks?

Game Dev Studio is a game that could easily be compared to something like Mad Games Tycoon or Game Dev Tycoon. While it does lean on being somewhat similar to the former, Game Dev Studio has it's own identity. Game Dev Studio has to be my favorite development tycoon game I've played yet, and for good reason. This game embodies everything a good, somewhat realistic, tycoon game is without emerging it's players in hours of tutorials. It manages to be both complex and fun at the same time.

Other than arguably being more strategic than it's more casual peers, Game Dev Studio has a bit of a different portrayal of how games are made. Game Dev Tycoon makes it look easy, and isn't exactly what I would call realistic (having to build new engines for different methods of storytelling and dialog is absurd). Mad Games Tycoon is realistic, but focuses on being more involved with the business of development. Game Dev Studio is like that, but it nails down the independant side of game development more than it does the professional side. Making games in this isn't hard, but it takes time and, more importantly, money. Games,
good or bad, will flop without proper marketing and support. It's really easy to make the perfect game only to go bankrupt because people aren't buying what you've made. Game Dev Studio takes more patience to stick with than any other game, and that it can pull that off without being too frustrating is astounding to me.

So, yeah. If you want a more casual experience that you can easily play with your brain turned off, maybe wait for a sale because that isn't what this is. If you, on the other hand, want a fun, strategic experience that will have you constantly failing and succeeding the face of all odds... get it! This game isn't perfect, but it's great and with the amazing support from the developer, I believe it will only get better.

The TL/DR
Edit*
After 90 hours, I definately got my monies worth. I could not be happier, updates have been consistent and dev actually listens to community. Not bad for a 1 person team. Support this one get more great games from responsible dev's!

Buy it, its worth it.

My views:

I patiently waited for this game and had it on my wishlist followed for 3 months prior to its release. I agree that the game seems early access as other reviewers have mentioned. However; what other reviewers are not talking about is the insane speed patches come after a bug is reported in the forums. The developer is ontop of the game, more so than I have seen in other early access/released games. Multiple updates a day! I have over 600+ games in my library, few are indie titles, and some early access. Out of all of my games I see updates for this one most often and it just came out! That says something, I have had pre-orders on almost all of my games (yes im that guy who pre-orders everything) and have never seen the speed of response shown by this developer before!

About the game:

In Game Dev Studio every early decision is crucial if you make the wrong hire, place a desk in the wrong spot, or flop your first game expect to be punished. This level of difficulty is refreshing as this game DOES NOT OFFER A SECOND CHANCE. It is a brutally realistic concept, if your business fails your done, no loan will save you. I personally enjoy this level of difficulty in a tycoon style game.

Early game:
Early play is frustrating, you will fail alot and restart from scratch. One of the ways I overcame the early parts is to save before making a game incase of a Oh darn release. You can lose money on your games. I do not remember if it was possible to do so in Game Dev Tycoon.

Mid Game:
This was the most fun for me as the building mechanic and second office mechanic were refreshing to see in the tycoon genre. I honestly think this was the best implementation in Game dev stylized games I have ever seen; granted, it is buggy, but we will get to that aspect. It is fun to start playing around with multiple teams working on different projects/patches/xpansions. And very rewarding to watch your bank account increase beyond Art of Electronics wildest dreams and smash them into oblivion with microtransactions, Muahahaha!

End game:
Like so many other games, the end game is just not there. Tech to research, engine updates, new genre combinations are just not there. Also if you invest your games into only PC (I am PC 4 Life) you get no platform variation at the end. Competitors are also non existent at this point since you basically smash them mid game. The fun in the end game for me was designing and starting multiple offices and experimenting with team variations.

Value and Longevity of game:
*Note: this is before Mod support implementation and I am sure Mod's will only add to the value and longevity of this game*

I typically can defeat a tripple A title in a day to three days. Game Dev Studio took me 2 days to defeat. When you think about value per play hour that averaged $0.81/hour (8.99/11 hours) I would personally say that is an amazing value as typically I average 4+/hour. Therefore I can only recommend it as an absolute buy at a sub 10.00 price point.

I truly do believe this game would have benefited from an early access period/beta. However; the game, did not get them so the developer can only do what he can. Hindsight is 20/20 there is no reason to bash him for this as I can think of several * cough Elex, THQ * games where in hindsight developers would have benefited from doing things differently. I can also think about times in my own life where I would have done things differently.



The 0/10 Scores I would give:

Graphics 6/10 For indie
Story 5/10
Strategy 10/10
Depth 10/10
Audio 7/10
Tutorial No hand holding here 10/10
Patch Support 10/10

Overall:

58/70 = 82% favorable in my personal opinion\t\t\t\t\t
