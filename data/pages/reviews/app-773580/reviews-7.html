3/5
Rating this game would be a lot easier if Steam had a star-based rating system, rather than just "yes" or "no". Game feels very early access, with the potential being stuck behind the developers seeming kind of lazy (not to diminish their work, but more could be done with this game)

- Employees are too critical
to making a game worth anything. I've been up to 50 something employees, and making a game worth $40 is still not really possible, even with $5m in the bank. Salaries and running costs are just too high by the time the game is finished. You would have to have a company of at least 120 employees I'm assuming before you can even easily pump out $40 games within reasonable time spans. Having more than a few employees requires you to use the complete automation of employee raises and vacation system, or you will get pestered with messages every few seconds about how everyone wants a raise. This makes everything feel out of control very fast. Employees also have very few names, and most of the non-russian names (which there are a lot of russian names) are tacky.

- Employees never use some types of objects, like workout benches.

- Offices look very same-y,
and this is not helped by the fact the game has a very limited decoration set and only 5 maps to choose from - only one out of the 3 or 4 I've played on so far allows you to expand a building outwards, and not just up. Randomly generated maps, especially for a 2D game, should not be that hard - or even allowing us to start on an empty plot of land and building a building from scratch so I can plan the layout efficiently.

- The game has very little instructions
on what you should do in certain situations, so a lot of trial and error will happen. The tutorial basically only explains how to make a basic game and that's about it. The game gives you basic information for a lot of things, without really explaining what you should be doing to accomplish something. It teaches you practically nothing about how publishers work, what game dev cost coverage means, or what share percent means. They don't cover any game development costs whatsoever from what I've seen, so it really is confusing. What does increasing the development time of a console do? There is no explanation, beyond a minimum amount of time for it to function properly.

I also had no idea I could slander rivals or anything like that until like 8 games later.

- Scenarios are kind of mediocre
overall. There are details on newer maps than older maps, making the old ones show their age more. Some have very tight time-frames, and I personally don't like building a new floor every 10 minutes to hire another 10 employees to meet the deadline.

- Construction balance is poor.
A lot of items in the game are cheap and give massive bonuses to development time of the game, which shows imbalance. For some reason, air conditioners cost $4000 and a massive amount of money each month, when I could just build a bunch of bean bags and do the same thing for a lot less. Things that have entrance tiles require all entrance tiles to be accessible, when there is clearly enough space for someone to access the object. A potted plant 5 feet behind two desks facing away from eachother is not going to stop people from accessing the desk from the side they are going to be accessing it anyway.

- In-game news feed
does not bring enough attention to the negative things going on in your business, and one very successful business I had was ruined because everyone left due to not enough coolers.

- It's annoying to manage designers.
I have to either chase them down by looking at everyone individually, or go to the make-new-game screen to get them in a list.

- Character creation is limited and practically pointless, as you never see your own character
, and traits are basically a waste of time and an underdeveloped feature; there should be downsides to all traits, but Silver Tongue has none whatsoever.

-/+ Graphics are kind of lame
Tall grass, trees, doors, and some other things look like they were made terrifically early on in the games development, and look out of place compared to everything else; very gross (look sort of like a nuked meme). Other graphics in the game look very nice, like the road sprites and wooden floors and carpets.

!!!--- This is partly due to shadows.

-/+ UI could look a lot nicer.

+ Game depth is good
compared to Game Dev Tycoon. You are not restricted to making "sequels" after researching sequels like 15 years into the game, which is very good and honestly, a lot of research requirements before being able to do simple business ventures in GDT is what makes GDT bad at what it does. This game is overall far more logical in how you can do things, and having better stories in your game does not require you to make a whole new game engine, like in GDT.

+ Realistic development;
You researched a new feature? You can just update your custom game engine, like a real game company would! Instead of spending a year making a new engine to implement that feature, costing you time and money, you can just do what would be reasonably done. You can also refab your engines which were made poorly to give them better stats.

+ Employees getting a drink doesn't stop them from working on the game

+ Soundtrack is good

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9.7 / 10. "No need to wait for a sale, just buy it already."

Considering this is a small indie game by a 1 person developer (albeit with some help), this game is very good. In my opinion, it is better than Game Dev Tycoon due to the features, scenarios, and options available to the player out of the box without the need for mods (although mod support is also included!). Why the 9.7 and not a 10? Because out of the box, I wish it had more assets for my offices so I could be more creative in designing and building my offices. I'd also prefer to have the ability to name my character, but I don't seem to have the ability as far as I can tell. These are, however, very minor setbacks in the scheme of things and won't really impact your ability to enjoy the game to any capacity.

Highlights:
Sequels and Expansions without needing to "research" them to unlock the ability (ala. Game Dev Tycoon)
Excellent Mechanics for Game Engines, Platforms, Publishers, etc (better than Game Dev Tycoon in all ways)
Platforms have HP which your games can directly affect (0 HP = platform is out of business)
Game Conventions and Game of the Year Awards
Game Editions (seriously, really good addition but not necessarily super obvious)
Challenge Scenarios
Buying out competitors / Stealing employees from competitors / Going to court, etc
Supports multiple offices and teams\t\t\t\t\t
