\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played the 'game dev' boom since the beginning, back when Kairosoft polished up their formula and released a mobile sim based on--you guessed it--running a game dev studio. Most games since have simply polished Kairosoft's approach, with popular games like Game Dev Tycoon pretty much perfecting it.

Game Dev Studio does its own thing, and for that alone its worth the price.

This is more in-depth, less streamlined than Game Dev Tycoon but way more accessible and enjoyable than games like Mad Games Tycoon--which was bogged down by too many extra features like cumbersome furniture placement and an overall drab aesthetic.

Game Dev Studio has its share of cumbersome property management, but the solo dev has implemented plenty of optional tutorials and useful tool tips. The music is chill synth and the graphics, which some have complained about, were a-OK with me (I'm still scarred by Mad Games' tubercular potato design).

GDS has some features I haven't seen in other game tycoon games: some pretty unique starting scenarios, group activities for employees, a friendly modding scene, custom automated tasks, and actual long-term variety. Reviews don't ALWAYS sound the same. Employees talk to each other, immersively informing the player of updates like new available tech to research or recent engine releases. And other little touches. All the great innovations of the game tycoon genre are here: mix-matching sub-genres, in-depth game feature customization, employee specializations, developing your own engine, etc.

All in all, I'm super happy this didn't turn into another Mad Games Tycoon OR a simple Game Dev Tycoon clone and I don't regret my purchase. I look forward to seeing more from this developer, and would recommend if you need something relaxing or that scratches that daydreaming-about-being-a-game-developer itch.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - Flawed (but fun!) indie game about game development.

If you've played 'Mad Games Tycoon' or 'Game Dev Tycoon', then you've already played this game. The concept and gameplay have a lot of similarities with those two.

You start with a tiny game development studio and a small amount of capital. You'll use this to generate revenue, expand, and gain popularity.

Creating a game is as simple as selecting a few options, assigning a team, and releasing it when it's done. Game design choices amount to spending more time/money for a better product, or less for an inferior game. Other choices (such as 'genre' or 'theme') need to be in "correct" combinations to net a good review score.

Overall, it's good. It's fun, but I'll probably be done with it once I get all the achievements. And now, the flaws.

First, the game is way too easy. Seriously. Even on the highest difficulty, it's nearly impossible to fail. Even when you make bad choices with the game design. I've never gotten less than a 5/10 review. (Update: I've gotten lower reviews 3-4 times, but they are easily avoided.) All games that proved unprofitable were minor losses. Which ultimately did not impact my company.

Second, pop-ups suck. A lot. You'll be bombarded with these non-stop. (Once you get to mid-game.) Every tiny detail (for some reason) requires a pop-up. Most of these pop-ups are unimportant. I don't need you to tell me my game is popular, to see that my game is popular. That information is already clearly laid out on the screen. It wouldn't be so bad if I could just hit "enter" to clear the boxes. (Or turn them off in the options.) But I can't. Each and every one must be cleared by clicking on the "OK" button with the mouse.

Similarly, the game will interrupt you with employee nagging. This isn't altogether unwelcome. In fact, I think it's a great feature. (As it makes your employees seem like real people, and not mindless game sprites.) Unfortunately, it comes up way too often. Every few minutes someone will want to go on vacation, ask for a raise, or schedule some group event.

The game does allow you to set those to automatically approve. But then... what's the point of them asking? They never ask for too much. So there's no reason to deny them. There's no reason to sift through their dialog, at all. It's irrelevant.

There's one you can't turn off, though. And that's the nag concerning price. Not releasing a game at the recommended price? Your manager will pause the game to complain, and your 'release game' action will be cancelled. Forcing you to do it twice, each and every time you release an overpriced game. I'm charging just $5 more than average. It won't hurt sales. I know the game is going to be 9/10.

Third, is employee management. As long as you have one team and one office, it's easy. Once you have just two offices and two teams, it becomes difficult. Why? Let's say you want to move one employee from one office/team to another.
  • Make sure you have an empty desk.
  • Go to the property menu.
  • Select assign employee.
  • Find the employee you want to move, and click on them.
  • Find the empty desk, and click on that.
  • Exit from menu.
  • Select employees menu.
  • Select manage employees.
  • Go to the team tab.
  • Select the team moving from.
  • Select manage team.
  • Find the employee, and select them.
  • Select 'unassign' from team.
  • Back out to the team selection tab.
  • Select the other team.
  • Select 'manage team'.
  • Scroll all the way to the bottom.
  • Select the moving employee from the 'unassigned employees' list.
  • Select 'assign team'.
And you have to do this with each and every employee you move!

To be fair, you could unassign all employees, and then assign each team to their buildings. However, this also means that certain considerations might be invalidated. Such as the person that needs to be in a less crowded room. Or the ones that demand to have books. Or having the correct books on the shelves for the people in the rooms. Or the manager that wants a private room. No matter what, you end up having to do this the long way.

Why is any of this a concern? Why not just have them in different buildings? Because you'll get a 5% penalty to both teams for having one member not in the same team and building.

Fourth, is the buildings. They are fine, mostly. (There's a distinct lack of decorating options. But that's minor.) I like that all your buildings are in some office park. But I don't like scrolling across the map to keep track of multiple buildings. It would be nice if I could jump between them somehow.\t\t\t\t\t
