\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: Buy this, it's good.

Game Dev Studio is a very charming game. While playing it, I had the impression that Roman Glebenkov put a lot of love into this. It's mostly details that lead me to believe this. For example, you can adjust the weather intensity in the options menu. Why can you do this? What is the point? This is a game about designing video games, and a very enjoyable one on top of that. But that's just what I mean: You don't need to have a feature like weather intensity in your game-dev-simulator-game - but this game has it :) It just feels warm.
That's also true for the people in the game: They're more than just names that produce a certain amount of points in some category. They get sick, they go on vacation, they may want to join a rival company, they retire... You can hold a wide range of team activities which they will enjoy more or less, based on their personal traits. It may not seem like much but this does make the game more enjoyable.
But it's not just these side features. The core of this game, the game development aspect is nicely done. It's quite intuitive and the tutorial levels are very well done to really help you become the greatest game developer the world has ever seen. Also you can give your games safe, non-copyright infringing titles, like "Hall of Beauty" or "Mallout" or "Dame Stev Gudio", if that is something that you enjoy doing.
And if you're up for a real challenge - go play the the way the game was originally meant to be played. That's actually a difficulty setting and it was definitely the right way to go.
If you've read this far, you might as well just buy the game and play it for yourself. You've already invested so much time reading about it :)\t\t\t\t\t
