\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is pretty darn good atleast for the first 10 or 12 hours before you run into the main issue of this game: lack of end-game content. Obviously time until you reach the end of the content will vary per player but I had hoped there was a good end-game gameplay loop but there isn't, you just run out of features to add to your engine and game which makes it pointless to continue playing.

Also I don't like how there is no list of all companies. You only have a list of 3 direct comptetitors of your company and they don't really seem to do anything, I've seen companies with 40+ employees make 0 games for decades until they go bankrupt.

This game does have some excellent mod support although very few mods at the moment (and terrible documentation, and the documentation that exists cannot be searched but the full source code of the game is available which helps). I don't see much replayability without mods or unless the game gets some big feature updates in the future.

The review responses and conversations with reviewers and employees are very static and makes me want to completely skip and ignore them.

I see several areas that could use some improvement but for the price this is a really good game and I think it'd be hard to dislike this game if you're into these types of games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe management aspect of this game is fun - albeit a bit tedious once you get multiple studios or a single big one, but thankfully there are options to help with that.

Money spending is fair, just because you spend a bunch of money on a game doesn't mean you're getting it all back - you need to conserve your money for important things.

The game making part is pretty good as well - you can do a quick bugfix yourself at the end, or you could hire QA and even extend development time.

There have been constant updates to the development beta branch, showing that the game's creator wants to add more when he can. Two of my favourite additions are multiple floors and game editions, but there have been more things, such as occasional bugfixes.

There are rival studios, which adds a lot to the game, meaning you have to compete with them - whether that be through bribing their employees to work for you, buying them outright, making better games or even playing dirty, which is a fun experience if you want to go that route.

There are a few story scenarios, two notable ones being one where your publisher has kicked you to the curb and one where you must make a highly successful console.

That is also another thing - you can make consoles in this game. Of course, only when you get a ton of money, but it's a nice feature, since you can edit basic specs, change its looks, hire a specialist and choose if you want it to be easy to develop for up to being an outright a powerhouse.

Employees have a lot of depth when it comes to skills - they can be good at managing multiple employees, excel at making art, make some great tunes, act as a PR representative with reviewers and more - there's also how quick they work, traits, hobbies and a few more little things that change up a few details.

This game does support mods. The developer of this game has released a few sample mods so people can look through the code and see what they need to do, which could be really helpful. Right now though, there aren't that many but the inclusions of mods is a really good one, which could potentially lead to even bigger mods.

Overall, if you've played Game Dev Tycoon or Game Dev Story, I'd say this game is worth your time - it has more depth and functionality than both and has kept me going for quite a bit.\t\t\t\t\t

But we don't buy and play games just for the looks of 'em.

The UI needs much improvement. You always feel that one click too many to complete any of your actions. For example, booking for conventions is burried somewhere in the menu of the game that you develop (btw the convention event needs a much better animation).
Or for example, attributing new points to your staff, which could be a 2 clicks matter, instead you have to select your employeee, open the tab with its personal info and then award him the attribute point.
Also, the events/news tab is too cluttered and rolling too fast even in normal speed. Same story with the "upgrading hardware" event which should have been more intuitive (like leasing or buying equipment, or even trying to develop your own hardware)

The game could really use a much slower pace (similar to Epic speed in Civilisation games) and off course a tech tree.

It also needs more questions to be added on the interviews you give for promoting your games so they don't get overly repetitive.

A negotiations mechanism is needed badly. It would really offer much to the game to be able to negotiate the salaries of your employees, their raises, the price that you have to pay to publishers etc. Or even to purchase technology from another company, or the rghts to a movie or a book or even a game from a rival.

Nevertheless, the developer seems to listen to the community and really trying to improve the game on the suggestions from the users. The game is a very honest and very decent effort that may leaves much to be desired but does not leave a bad taste on your....mouse.

Also there are not many great tech tycoon games out there and for all those reasons, i recommend this game, hoping that it will improve more over time.\t\t\t\t\t
