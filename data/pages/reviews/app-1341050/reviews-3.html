\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game.
Contrary to popular belief, no, this is NOT a trash fire. It's certainly not perfect, there are many questionable decisions and there's definitely a fair bit of ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ you have to wade through and definitely don't pay more than $15 for it, but if you're willing to be more open-minded about game design, if you're willing to give the game a few hours and to really TRY to understand it, it's a really enjoyable throwback platformer. The main reasons people hate this game seem to be solely because it uses older 3D game design tropes but applied to a modern release.
"Breaking the game" is entirely intentional. Puzzles are SUPPOSED to have multiple solutions in this game. Sure, the main, designed solution to a puzzle could be to use a shooting costume and hit multiple switches with perfect timing- or, if you have a parkour costume, you can use it to figure out an alternate route. If you have one of the few costumes that stop time, you can use it to physically hit both switches while time is stopped, giving you the same result. Hell, the reward for raising Tims in the hub world and collecting enough Rainbow Drops lets you get the ultimate costume that allows you to cheese any puzzle or platforming bit that you deem to be ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, or if you want, you can keep trying with your other costumes. It's dumb and goofy and has many caveats, but just exploring the worlds, looking around and collecting statues, it puts you into this satisfying flow state, and since it's so open-ended and lax with it's rules, it makes for a fantastic casual collectathon to play if you just want something easy and pleasant.
There's really a ton to love here, but it's absolutely not for everyone. However, if you enjoy late 90s and early 2000s B-grade 3D platformers like Chameleon Twist or Gex or Ty the Tasmanian Tiger, I really think you'll enjoy this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, full price is asking *far* too much for this game. I bought this on sale for twelve bucks and it's barely worth that.

The opening visuals are absolutely stunning and had me really excited to explore this world, only for the actual gameplay and interface to be confusingly lacking in a way that feels unforgivable for a modern game.

Gameplay:
Explore semi-3D-platformer levels with your movement input and your jump/action input. That's it. Sure you get a few powers that you can cycle through (that take an annoyingly long time to transition), but if one of those power ups is say, a fireball, then congrats, you have to change again if you want to be able to jump at all. There's a few enemies and boss fights but they are exceedingly easy/simple and don't really feel like they even get to show off their mechanics with how easy it is for them to take their 1-2-3 standard boss hits.

There's something kind of like a Chao garden built right in with cute fluffy bird creatures called Tims and that's pretty cute and the clocktower Rube Goldberg machine is pretty neat even if I don't know what it's really there for right now.

Story:
There's not much in way of dialogue other than Balan himself and he is a *fascinating* character from the get go. There's also a character that seems to be the same kind of being as Balan who is the big cool villain, but I haven't gotten too far. The lack of dialogue wouldn't be an issue for me, I think, if the gameplay itself felt like there was more a part of the narrative.

So far, however, there doesn't feel like there's really any stakes or goals, which I'd be okay with if the resolved mini-stories didn't feel so out-of-nowhere. There's a celebratory dance scene at the end of the first 2-level chapter and it's silly and cute and meant, I think, to evoke that dreamland vibe, but it really just made me wonder if I was hallucinating because it felt so uncanny.

Interface:
So when I said you have a movement button and an action button, I meant it. The game only allows movement and select/action. So in your menus, you have to navigate to the actual "previous menu" selection to close all your menus rather than just having a "back/cancel" button and that's just simply unacceptable for a modern videogame by such a big developer. It absolute blows my mind that this wasn't addressed.

This game is trying to be cute, simple, and fantasmical wonderlandy and for the most part, it gets that right! But the problem lies in that it is so simple that it's lackadaisical in execution and comes off feeling as if this project was ripped out of a teams hands before they could actually get it play-ready, and that's just sad.

I'd like to play more some day for the story but considering I bought it so cheap on discount I might just cut my losses and watch a let's play so I don't have to waste time on it myself. I wonder if I can get a refund.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow. This was an experience. I accidentally bought this game, so why not? Let's review it.

This is a game entirely consisting of horrible game design and poor choices being shoved to the finish line by a bunch of corporate suits meddling with the developers and the esteemed Sonic creator Yuji Naka.

The game design isn't even aggressively bad to the point where it's fun to laugh at kind of like a badly animated movie, it's at the fine line where's all aggressive bland everywhere, in every single aspect. No fun whatsoever, except for one part. Actually, scratch that, that part wasn't fun at all, it was just freaking wierd. I'll get to that later.

Tons of feature creep, every single part of the game feels like putting a square peg through a round hole. Every single one of the powerups, aka costumes, are completely worthless. Most of them are created for exactly one obstacle, and on top of that, you lose the powerups instantly when you take a single point of damage. Completely useless.

Tons of stiff animation lock and bad transitions throughout animations. Movement feels very clunky and uneventful. A cardinal sin for a Super Mario 64 style platformer.

Tons of bad decisions, like who the hell approved the idea to create a level and constantly warps around you like you were standing on one giant trampoline? I was nauseous within seconds of playing through that level. Who the hell approved of "Balan Bouts"? They are literally the exact same QTE over and over again!!

There are two parts in the whole game that aren't completely awful.

1. The intro cutscene. Not because is introduces the game and the Wonderworld it wants to show off, but because the only fun part of the game was watching Balan dance around like crazy. What a fun character to have. Gotta love that character design of his too. I love Balan as a character, he has so much potential as a Willy Wonka type character, until Square Enix screwed up all that potential in the pursuit of greed. :/

2. Remember where I said "except one part?"

Well, here it is.

There are hilariously out of touch dance numbers through out the whole game after you save a character.

And they absolutely hilarious and stupid in the best way possible. They'll catch you off-guard because of just how random and unreal they are. I suggest watching the video "F it up Farmer Ted" to get what I'm talking about. It is glorious.

And the amount of effort put into the music is endearing, at least. The devs actually created another freaking language to use in the music, or in this case, the dance numbers. I believe it's a combination of Japanese and backwards English, at least that's what Balan speaks.

https://news.xbox.com/en-us/2021/03/17/creating-musical-happiness-for-balan-wonderworld/

So, it's actually very sad to realize that the devs put all that effort into the dance numbers only to be laughed at because they were rushed by suits. I mean, it just enhances the comedy of this tragedy of a game, right?

To sum things up:

Balan Wonderworld is a bad game. Easily one of the worst games ever made.

It is not even funny-bad like the game Ride to Hell Retribution or Fast & Furious Crossroads, it's just pathetically bad.

Do not buy this game.

Do not buy this game even ironically.

You should know not to do that even after today's news: Square Enix, publisher for Balan Wonderworld, just sold a lot of IPs to Embracer Group. They now have Tomb Raider, Deus Ex, and many others.

Why did Square Enix do that? It turns out they are converting their entire business centering around NFTs.

Expect Final Fantasy 16 to be full of NFTs, there's no hope for this company anymore.

One last thing:

Why am I giving Balan Wonderworld a thumbs up..?

Well, despite how awful this game is, I'd still rather take Balan Wonderworld over The Witcher 3.

My boi Balan > Geralt of Rivia

Sorry, fanboys. :3
