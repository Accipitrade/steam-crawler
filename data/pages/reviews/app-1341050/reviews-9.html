\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWelp. Guess it's time for my personal opinion about this game. I'm not usualy writing some reviews, cause it's not my thing... But why the hell not? So let's do this!

Balan Wonderworld is a pretty charming game about two kids and our handsome person in white. In every act you need to save hearts of people who... Well... Had a pretty bad day or even days. Depends on situation. And. Yeah. Pretty simple. Complete two chapters and fight the boss to help people in hard times.

Alright. About Design of levels and characters. For me it's fantastic. Colorfull and yet funny.

About gameplay... It's ok. Each costume ( 81 to be exact ) have jumps ( with special effects ) or no jumps ( usually just fire or bombs ). A little bit hard but you'll get used to it sooner or later.

Soundtrack - incredible. I just love it.

And last but not least "Balan's bout". For some people it can became annoying. But for me... It's not. First time simple but in later acts it will become challenging. The only problem I have with it is... If you didn't get a perfect result ( for statue ) you need to fight the boss again and then return to golden hat. And you'll repeat this until you'll get a perfect result.

Oh right! About bosses. Very simple and yet interesting.

So that's about it. Little happy and positive game. Not that great and not for everyone but I see some potential. I just hope to see that universe again in the future. Until then. Have a great day! And remember! Every second was an adventure. =3\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally charming, visually gorgeous, great designs and great art direction, memorable music. Balan is a really fun platformer, it's pretty basic and simple with it's share of flaws but it's not a bad game. A lot of people talk about the one button system and the lack of a jump in some power ups like it's a bad thing but...that's the entire point of the costume system. You need to decide to prioritize combat oriented suits or traversal. There's also 12 worlds with 2 levels each and a boss, with a 3rd level unlocking for each world later, a small hub area with a lighter take on chao garden mechanics and plenty of secrets to find and unlock. Honestly the hate for this game is incredibly unwarrented. The PC Port also ran at a solid 60fps pretty much my entire playthrough with no hiccups. Overall I had a lot of fun and will probably try to 100% the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is just...really sad. It looks so nice (mostly.) The music's so nice (mostly.) But the game itself is such a Nothing-Burger. It's like they wanted to make a game that's accessible to everyone, but just went too far.

It's set up like a Collectathon, but finding the collectibles is about as complicated and interesting as "I hope you have this power you brought from a different level." No thought, or puzzle solving, or interesting/difficult platforming involved. Every now and then there's a glimmer of "that's an interesting place to hide one of those," but the majority of the game is braindead.

It's piss easy, like a game for actual 3 year-olds. There's like 30 or 40 different powers/abilities to use and maybe two or three of them are worth a damn. Most are unsatisfying. Hell, just movement and jumping in general is unsatisfying. Everything, and I mean EVERYTHING, is done with one button, meaning half your powers and abilities activate literally whenever the hell they feel like. (It often uses the exact phrase "when it feels like.") They also have very niche uses, meaning so many are one and done and just shouldn't exist in the first place. Oh btw, the settings menus also use one button. Used to hitting the "b" button to back out of a menu? Jokes on you, you're accidentally changing some settings again.

The enemies are so ineffectual I wonder why they're even in the game, and bosses are the same way except the later ones which don't get harder, they just take longer, much like the levels themselves. The "main villain" is about as pointless. Very underutilized. He just shows up sometimes and snaps his fingers, summoning pointless enemies, most of which can literally just walk away from. They seriously have no purpose in most levels. It's not an exaggeration. They're just...there.

There's no investment in any of the mini stories that make up the game because every chapter's done on a different character. It's all very surface level and you spend your time wondering what any of this has to do with YOUR character who has problems of their own. As of the start of the final chapter, those have yet to be resolved.

I'll be honest, I haven't beaten it yet. I just unlocked enough not-jiggies to open up the final chapter. This is at about 9.6 hours, and I've been taking a leisurely pace through the game so it's maybe 10 hours in total, and I'm having one hell of a time making that final push to the end of the game. I wanted to like this. I love games with whimsical dream-like settings, which this has in spades, but there's no other way to put this:

This sucks. Definitely don't go in expecting Nights if you're one of those people. I didn't want or expect that, but it would have been miles better than this.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst things first let me get my personal complaints out of the way:
∙While I respect the devs choice of wanting to tell story without much dialogue the game could have benefited from a hint system of sorts since certain stuff like the checkpoints allowing you to change your costumes are not explained or told at all unless you kept up with the game's social media accounts or fans of the game.
∙Balan Bouts and the minigames only resetting after beating the worlds bosses is annoying and time consuming. Unlocking trophies by getting perfect scores on them is not a bad thing, but they both could have greatly benefited from either having the option to try again at the results screen or resetting when you complete or leave the act they're found in.
∙The costumes are fine and fun to figure out especially since ones found in later levels will help you find trophies, costumes, and drops from earlier worlds, but some are worthless.

Now that I got my complaints out of the way, I had a lot of fun with this game! It's a nice slow paced puzzle platformer that has the feel and aesthetic of an old-school Sonic Team that this reviewer grew up with and misses to this day. The designs of the worlds, theme and artistic wise, are very pretty and colorful and were a treat to look at. The music is very relaxing to listen while playing through each act and the music from the boss fights were also pretty cool to listen to. As mentioned above the costume gimmick was, bearing some duds, was fun to use and helped make exploring previous to find stuff the player couldn't get the first time around a lot of fun. The stories that each of the characters went through was very realistic and relatable and seeing them overcome their internal and emotional hardships was great to see. As cliche as it might be, I love seeing positive outcomes like that.

To end this off I totally understand the complaints people have towards this game and I do kind of agree with a few of them, but the good stuff I found managed to outweigh the bad for me and while I have yet to 100% this game I can say I do not regret giving support for this game and if by a miracle it gets a sequel I would love to see it's potential flourish and it's issues fixed or made less annoying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnce you step foot on the main hub world aka not Chao Garden, it shows that quality was focused on the cutscenes (minus Leo for the lack of animation for the kids behind him). Not just that, there is also another immediate red flag. All of the face buttons, are your jump buttons. That includes your costumes. Speaking of costumes, the costumes that attack (minus the teleporting genie costume and the ones that shift to first person) cannot jump. One word WHY?! I'm fine if the game is built around the lack of ability to jump like the first two Skylanders games, but there are sections that require you to do some jumping. For example, after you beat the Chapter 8 boss, you want to go to Chapter 9 right? At the beginning of Chapter 9, you need a costume that can jump. If you don't have a costume that can jump, then its back to the costume changing room for you. To further my point, the costumes in Chapter 8's boss room don't have the ability to jump. Are you kidding me?!

Instead of ranting on how Box Fox and Balan Bouts QTE sections sucks, there are things I enjoy in Balan Wonderworld. One, like every bleh 3D Sonic game, the music is amazing. I cannot get enough of Chapter 4, Chapter 5, Chapter 8, and Together just to name a few. Two, I enjoy the challenge of finding the Balan Statues. They taught me to look carefully in every level. Also, there are alternative solutions to get the statues like in Chapter 3-1, you can use the Bubble Blower from Chapter 2-1 to get that statue without the need of the lizard from Chapter 5-1. Three, the boss designs are cool especially Princess Marey, Hydrac, and Hooverton. Four, the Tims are cute. I'm a sucker for cuteness despite they're a water down Chao.

Overall, I did have some fun with the game, but glaring issues that make the game bad cannot be overlooked. Final score 3/10. I blame Arzest!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played through the main game, I can safely say this is a very fun and good platformer, especially with the day one patch fixes (particularly regarding upping the movement speed, and which does include removing the seizure inducing glitch you've probably heard about by now). As someone who felt like the one button control scheme felt weird when I first played the demo, I very much got used to it as I played that and especially the full game. Also, even though some costumes aren't as good or useful as others, I got some mileage out of all of them and I had a lot of fun experimenting with different ones during the levels and bosses, and speaking of the levels beyond the first world the level design really opens up and gives you lots of opportunities to explore these beautiful levels, and more importantly rewards you for doing so with drops, statues, etc. way more often than not. And of course the music, graphics and art direction, and story are also high points to me. My only real complaints are that the camera can get wonky sometimes, some costumes are better than others as I said before, and how some of the later bosses and levels can get frustrating if you're trying to go for everything (though imo they're still fun and well designed). Basically, if you can look past or at least get used to the one button controls and perhaps lack of dialogue in most of the game, and like games the reward creativity and exploration, I'd certainly recommend trying this game out. (P.S. I'd also recommend checking out the novel after you beat the main game if you want more ;) )\t\t\t\t\t
