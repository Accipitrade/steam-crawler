\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is unfortunately probably like a 5/10.

It's a one button game and suffers from it pretty badly - You cannot jump with certain abilities and cannot swap to being 'uncostumed' (where you could atleast jump). The movement in the game is extremely simple, no momentum or anything so the game is fairly linear. There aren't really any puzzles that you'll have to think to solve, so critical thinking is pretty much at a minimum - At best you'll think about when exactly you need to hit a boss.

The powerups aren't 'unlockable', they're 'storeable' so if you want to use them in other stages or have spare for use in your dressing room, you need to farm them. Powerups take ages to respawn, as well as keys, so farming would be tedium. Normally this isn't necessary, but if you ever take a hit you'll be waiting around to collect another powerup.

Many of the 'stars' in this game are collected via a quick-time event sequence that doesn't ever change, and there can be sometimes 3 in a stage, all pretty close together sometimes as well. If you don't perfect every QTE (as in, if you don't score a 'perfect' on your timing, not if you miss), you will not collect the star from the challenge, and it will disappear until the next time you visit the level - you'll also have to suffer through the rest of the now pointless QTE section.

The best part of the game is multiplayer because its fundamentally flawed. You can jump in this game at any point as long as you haven't jumped since you've last landed - meaning you can fall off of a platform and jump midair at any time. This means in cooperative mode, when you 'latch onto eachother' in the unified-control mode, you can now double jump as one body, which breaks a lot of the game. That combined with hovering or speed abilities means you can jump around many of the locked gates in the game or across massive platforms, or up columns meant to be too high. This was fairly enjoyable, but for the wrong reasons.

The strange farming simulator at the base of the hub is unecessary except for 100%. It's nothing compared to like a chao garden and is ultimately unengaging, though it is funny seeing the little dudes roll down a big rube goldberg machine.

The game is very, very easy. And when its not being very,very easy its because you got bonked or fell off of something and now have to have a practice in tedium and patience waiting for a powerup or climbing up a really simple section again.

Wouldn't recommend it, anyone complaining about the controls or the camera or anything like that i wouldn't really bother listening to - They weren't bad (fixed since the demo apparently) and ultimately this games' basic loop is just fundamentally flawed. Until they let you have infinite of every costume at the checkpoints (once you unlock them) and remove the key-and-powerup system for always-present powerup pickups game kinda sucky. Not worth 60.

The good parts of the game undeniably are the cutscenes, Squares' internal CGI team did them and they did a killer job. Yuji Naka's designs paired with the quality effort put into the cutscenes made for some really charming moments. The designs outside of the cutscenes are also charming - and the art for the levels and powerups are also unique. They have a few powerups that just shoot and a few that just float but ultimately every stage is dynamic and doesn't really reuse art or anything like that. The music is also more than serviceable.

Wouldn't buy it unless it went on a steep sale sadly. Yuji still has it with the art design for sure but the game design is super weak sadly.\t\t\t\t\t

TLDR: Very classic/old-school feeling platformer. But the main appeal is the amazing visual, catchy soundtrack and beautiful story. Price may be a bit high for what it is and there were some nitpicky stuff, but I'm having a very wonderful time.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What I liked:
-Music is very good. I don't get tired of listening to it. I'm still waiting for my soundtrack.
-The visuals. Movie sequences are gorgeous, character designs are unique and fun.
-The story. Each character is likeable and easy to sympathize with. Balan the maestro is funny, and even the enemies have enough personality to make them lovable.
-There is minimal words and dialogue so everything if up for you to interpret and feel.
-Gameplay & control is very basic. I suck at 3D platformers so it was the right level for me.
-Stages is linear, but also rewards you for exploring.
-There are a lot of stuff to collect. Requires backtracking, costumes from other stages, and much thinking.
-The game does get harder and trickier in later stages. It will also spawn stronger enemies if you keep winning.
-The only penalty for getting hit/falling is your active costume disappearing, and there are frequent checkpoints.
-The chicks (Tims) are useless in tougher battles but it's fun to see them all spin-dash weak enemies. Oh and they will bring stuff for you. You can also make them very, very fat.

Some nitpicking:
-Even with all the backtracking, 2 stages+boss per chapter feels a little short.
-There's a lot of walking, especially when you're replaying a level for trophies.
-Each costume has limited functions so sometimes you need to shuffle them around a lot.
-Can't tell if some of the collectibles/costume characters you see can be acquired now or will be accessible later.
-Enemies respawn every time you re-enter an area, so unless you're farming for drops it gets annoying.
-Some tougher enemies are extremely difficult to defeat. Good thing they are optional.
-The game doesn't explain much so I'm sometimes lost on what to do or unsure about controls.
-I am not good at QTEs so the Challenge mini game stress me out.
-The dancing sequence feels repetitive and a tad too long after the first few times.
-There was one stage where the stage warps around you and it made me feel dizzy.
-A little laggy in some parts, though that may be my PC's issue

Stuff I figured out mid-game:
-You can skip most cutscenes (anywhere you can't move) by holding A
-If you forgot what the costume does, the description is written in the pause menu.
-If you hold A on the checkpoint you can swap costumes, go back to the Tim hub, or enter other finished Acts.
-When you get 4 costumes, the one on the far right gets stored in the costume menu. So you should keep collecting useful costumes when you can.
-In the costume screen, you can press L/R to select slots you wanna swap. Until I realized this I thought I had to pick all 3 slots in order every time.
-You can breed Tims when they're very fat (has speech bubble with Tims) by throwing a smaller Tim at it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR - Shines in later levels but still has flaws to be priced at 50

+ Variety of costumes
+ Secret levels
+ Level themes
+ Music


- Models are lack luster
- Costume switching
- Animations could be better
- Balan Bouts


I will admit, I enjoyed the game the more I got into it, it shined at level 4 and onwards for me not only in level design but also costumes to choose from to fit your playstyle.
The first chapter did not do it justice at all and the textures on the first chapter are very poor compared to later levels.

I enjoyed the level themes, specially chapter 9 and 10 which looked amazing to me and was nice to sit back and just enjoy the designs around each corner.
This game is best played taking your time to see what secrets you can find, what costumes to plan for future visits and really to just enjoy your surroundings.

The music I pretty much loved for the entire game, excluding the last chapter. Each one fits the theme and stands out from one another to where you are bound to know which song is from which chapter and remember it.

80 costumes are nice but a few of them I found legit pointless and 2 of them to be legit troll costumes specially in later levels where they do nothing for you but if anything, make you mess up on purpose.
Some costumes I enjoy and have a select few I will always pick over others to go into a level with, specially secret levels which are a challenge and you need to plan for.

Also switching from costume to costume became a chore in later levels specially when you needed 3 not even in the level for one trophy. This was not fun specially having to go back to the levels to get the costume back specially if at the very end of the level and you had to rewatch each animation in the entire level.

Some costumes are kinda pointless but have their moments or a little fun moment but other costumes I just hated, they looked ugly or cringy and your characters animation and dead smile just makes it worst to see every costume intro. Some even have repeat animations to others.
I feel the designers just couldn't choose which designs to drop and leave behind so they threw all of them in there. Would of worked better as finding the alternative skins for the costumes and switch to instead of it being its separate slot.


The running speed was increased from the demo and some costumes even moved to other chapters.
I just don't like how some trophies were replaced with more Balan Bouts. The bouts are ok, for the first 4 chapters but then you realise each new shot you see is just re-used animation made to look like it fits together but it does not. I got bored of seeing the same animation and being forced to watch it just to press a button and hope I get excellent or great. They became tedious later on specially with the animations getting longer.

I do wish the background for Balan Bout was actually of the level you were in to make it seem like he was doing his own thing while you were exploring down below but no, most it gets is three different sky textures. Alot more could of been done here specially with Balan's model being downgraded. With the systems this game is on there was no need to make them that flat.


The ending surprised me, twice and the final boss was actually fun even if abit confusing at first. I don't like the design however, too generic specially for a game like this where a better and more interesting original design could of been done.
Same goes for Balan's train, would of been better for each part of the train to gain the level theme you just completed and look more outstanding than looking like a plain iron train :/ I feel certain parts of the design concepts were left till last minute to push in all the costumes. But now I am just nitpicking.


The game has potential, least to me. I am not one to want all new games to be action packed and fast paced all the time while having super long levels. If you take your time with it, you can get some hours out of it but it really is down to you.

I will say however the price is abit high for what you get. I only continued my pre-order to support the creators who I do feel sorry for in this out-come but it needed to be lower priced because you can tell some parts were very lazily done and so much more could of been done with it.
I hate the "it is a childrens game" excuse, there has been plenty of games for children which has not spoken down to them and has still had excellent animation and design to them.

There is no reason to replay levels unless going back with a costume from a later level for a trophy you missed or Balan's bout which is a pain to retry so often.
I do understand why many hate this game and feel it should flop but I am still willing to give it a chance for the area's it shines, it just needs abit more time in the back to be acceptable.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI... kinda like this game??

GRANTED, Im only two and a half worlds in and was relatively buzzed at the time of playing. While streaming, I did explicitly state that I went into this game quite biased as a) 3D platformers are my favorite, even if theyre not particularly good, and b) my expectations were set by playing he demo. I will wholeheartedly agree that the first world does not give the best impression. Its not broken so much as it is serviceable at best, and I still think it was a very strange decision to have had the story cutscenes play only after Act 2. It creates a disorienting experience without the context of who and what the worlds are themed after. Once you crush the first boss, you start to understand the rhythm of how the storytelling goes, but I think it could have been reconsidered.

Chapter 2 is such a vast improvement, it makes you wonder why the first world remained as is: Neat costumes, visuals, a cool sense of verticality, statues that start to require more exploration, and a cool boss that, while still easy, definitely demands more from the player and is a good sign of things to come.

If anything, the pace and structure heavily reminds me of the first Blinx if anyone recalls that one. The hshima influence definitely goes beyond that of the character designs (although there is an enemy, a chompy lil guy that will try to bite if you stand over his shadow, whos straight out of Blinx!). This includes faults as well. Its frustrating to see design flaws from twenty years ago read their heads in a 2021 release: in Blinx, you had to backtrack to recollect certain powers of you happened to run out of them, and its the same with Balan though much more forgiving. The costumes are a one hit and done affair, and while you can get multiples to store and swap at checkpoints, it can be more tedious than necessary if you make a mistake.

In sum, this is a kids game. A very, very young kids game. And thats okay. The marketing is quite clearly geared toward parents and their children as an early video game experience for when the Odysseys and the Spyros are just a bit much. While I am way outside of this demographic, I do appreciate a breezy, weird as hell, nice to look at refresher in between stuff that requires a bit more brain juice.

Cautiously recommended if youre curious now at full price, but give it a hearty go at a discount.\t\t\t\t\t

In short: Bad performance, clunky controls and gameplay, little platforming, easy puzzles

"Language" only affects subtitles, not audio
You can choose 30 or 60 FPS, you can also choose to V-Sync or not to V-Sync. Considering the FPS choices, there's no reason to disable V-Sync, as it just causes tearing with no impact on framerate.
In menus, all buttons (A B X Y) are Confirm, instead of the more common A Confirm, B to go back
The very first cut scene lagged and stuttered significantly
The first level took more than five minutes to load in, wouldn't allow opening Steam overlay during the load, and then crashed upon loading.

Reboot.

Load into hub fast, first level loads fast.
Significant stalls upon picking up or losing a costume.
NPCs fading in/out is weird.
The entire world curves. See Minecraft Acid Shader or Animal Crossing Shader.
The camera is fixed at a kinda close range, and the world curving up around you makes looking around a bit difficult.
The world curving effect also stutters some, seems to run at 30 FPS despite being set to 60.
It's not much of a platformer. Not all costumes can even jump. LB/RB to swap costumes, left stick moves, right stick rotates camera, Start pauses. Every single other button activates whatever the costume's ability is.

Costumes lost on taking damage, and can be brought out of storage at check points. You have a quantity of costumes, and more copies are obtained by grabbing diamonds that look like Mario Kart items.
To grab these diamonds, however, you need a key, which is a bit pointless when there's always a key nearby.
The costume system is really just clunky overall. You may accidentally pick up a duplicate, thus bumping off another you needed, and have to backtrack just to get that costume back, or take a hit and have to go back and either withdraw another costume or find another diamond.

Hubworld: Feed birds gems. Birds grow. Throw a small bird at a large bird to get an egg. Birds turn a wheel to raise a counter. The gems don't directly raise the counter; you have to wait for the birds to turn the wheel. That's also assuming that's how that functions, because I'm not entirely sure the gems are needed if you don't mind waiting.

Second level: Long load time. No distorted curving this time.

Cutscene: Same lag and hang ups as the first cutscene.

Boss: You hit it with the tornado once, at any point. Standing around, jumping on you, or the period after the jump that would be the "vulnerable" phase in other games. You then use the pig to slam onto a pillar. Any one pillar of the three. And then it kinda looks like it freezes, and gives you the death cutscene, followed by a dance party, followed by the farmer in a cornfield looking like a Boom Beach commercial.
The hardest part of the boss is making sure you pick up both costumes you need, and dealing with the camera that'll angle up, and then actually move upward such that you can't see your character or the floor.
Also, this boss dies in one hit, as opposed to three of the tornado dude miniboss?

World 2: The key is now a flight of stairs away from the first gem. Some of the costumes are slight changes from others; the dolphin can jump out of the water, but the jellyfish has the shock thing that's really not necessary. There's a bubble ability, which doesn't seem to have much more reach than the kangaroo from before.

There's Balan's Bout minigames that'll double your gems if you play them right. It's a series of "quick"-time challenges, just press any button when the images line up, or mash buttons when there's multiple images. Each of these sequences is an awkward 30 seconds apart, and there's a long period of about 3 seconds where you can press a button to get an Excellent on the phase.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAight so I just finished the game, clocking in at almost 11 hours, still missing about half the trophies and I haven't gotten all the costumes. But since I beat the final boss I figured i'd share my thoughts on the game.

So lets start with comparing how the game controls between the demo and the final release with the Day 1 Patch. So the patch made it so movement is a whole lot faster and that jumping isn't very floaty like it was in the demo (aside from certain costumes). From the looks of the patch they at least addressed an issue that players of the demo were most vocal about, that being movement.

Moving on from that lets discuss the content that wasn't in the demo, that being other costumes and stages. I really do feel like the stages that were in the demo were probably the weakest examples to show off the game as the stages that weren't in the demo (Chapters 2, 3, 5, etc) had a lot more going on in them in terms of puzzles and exploration. I honestly had a blast in those specific chapters listed above, I also found it nice how there were times for certain trophies my brain clicked on how the puzzle to get them needed to be solved with a costume from another chapter that i've already used.

Another thing that I liked even back in the demo is how open certain puzzles are, there is a clear way to solve it as intended, but you can also use other costumes to find another way to achieve your goal (I have used the sheep costume to sequence break so many times). Also now that we're on the topic of costumes, lets get into them a bit more. The game boasts having over 80 different powerups but if we're being honest, many of the costumes are just variants of others. (The Pig, the Duck and a robot costumes all do the same thing but having slight variants in behavior, this is best shown in how Air Cat and Frost Fairy are similar in function but behave differently with Air Cat keeping a constant horizontal level while Frost Fairy rises higher in the air with each step.) this is both a downside and an upside. The downside being it makes the costume roster feel bloated with duplicates, but its also helpful since it creates a buffer of a specific skill in case you lose the costume.

One last detail regarding costumes that is a downside is an issue we all know well enough: All buttons being tied to one ability. Not all costumes can jump which means you will be switching between them incredibly often, and with how long it takes to switch its a chore. I get that they wanted to make a simple controlling game like the old days, but there's a reason games aren't made with details like that these days: Its tedious and takes control away from the player which is always a problem. Even with certain costumes adding them, the game lacks general mobility options that are very important for a 3D Platformer (Examples: Mario Odyssey, Hat in Time). You get some extra mobility but it requires having specific costumes with you. If i'm being honest the game would be a lot better if it didn't make you pause so often, and while this drags the game down from what it could be, it doesn't make the game a disaster as many would want you to believe.

As I was typing this up I almost forgot to touch up on a detail, that being the Tims. I feel like their implementation is sorely lacking. You can barely interact with them on the isle of tims, their contribution in stages is pretty minimal, and they're not explained well. One last detail that I want to touch on is the bosses. When it comes down to it, they're pretty easy to beat but at the same time they do something that I love: they encourage you to experiment. Bosses have three Balan trophies tied to them, each one is gained by damaging the boss in a specific manner and that encourages you to experiment with the costumes gained in the chapter and makes you go "Hmm, how can I hit the boss with this?" You can go into the boss and just beat it with the abilities you find easiest to use, but for those who want to experience a little brain teaser, they will be rewarded.

After all is said and done, I decided to give the full game a chance after being very iffy on the demo, and simply put, I was right to give it that chance. It isn't a perfect experience, it isn't a great game, it has it's rough edges, but I still found joy playing it. The game has it's negatives along with it's positives, but I don't truly believe it to be this horrible game that many would claim it to be. When I played the demo I gave it a cautious 6.8/10, but after playing the final release, I can safely say I bump it up to a 7/10. However i'm not sure i'd recommend it as a full $60 purchase. If you're willing to put up the money to play the game after what i've said, go for it, you might find it to be as entertaining and engaging as I did. If not, then its fine to wait til it goes on sale for $40 or lower.

The exploration and open ended puzzles are what kept me hooked and had me see the game to the end and wait til I absorbed the majority of it's content before making this review unlike most that i've seen where players barely played an hour before wanting to demonize or praise it.\t\t\t\t\t
