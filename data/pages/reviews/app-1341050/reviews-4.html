\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember, before this game came out, looking up this game after seeing some cool fan art and then watching the reveal trailer and being SO HYPED; And I have to say I love it SO MUCH!!

I can see why some don't like it, the controls can be weird sometimes, and the camera sometimes is kinda finicky. But, to be honest, it never really slowed me down or discouraged me from playing, (though it did make me fall more than a few times lol).

I also really loved the Isle of Tims! It had some of the Chao Garden vibes, while also doing its own thing. And the fact that the Tims follow you into the stage and help you, (Collect hidden items, help you fight, Ect.) Is really great! It feels less like an unrelated feature, and more like a side-task that can help you. (No hate to the Chao Garden tho lol)

It's got some of those retro game vibes that I love, without feeling like its too stuck in the past. (In my opinion)

One thing I think you should not do is play this expecting it to be just like NiGHTS. I feel like if you go into this expecting NiGHTS 3, (or something similar,) it'll definitely disappoint you. Its got some similar vibes, but both games have their own unique aesthetics, game play and stories.

This game seriously makes me SO HAPPY! And I LEGIT was crying when I finished it lol. Most of the time I'm playing this game with a smile. Even when I fell or messed up, I never felt too mad or upset. Of course, people react differently and feel differently when different things happen. But this is just my experience.

TLDR; This game makes me really really happy and I truly do recommend it. It may not be for everyone, but I love it so much!\t\t\t\t\t

The worlds are so pretty and creative, the gears in my head were always turning about just what kind of costume I needed to either speed things up or get an out-of-reach statue, and the story, while simple, was still as heartfelt and meaningful as I'd come to expect from Yuji Naka. And the music! WHEW! Banger city, I tell ya! Hoo WEE!

I won't debate that the control scheme is weird, but it's not like the levels weren't designed around it. It reminds me in a lot of ways of Super Mario Bros. 3, avoiding hits so you can hold on to your special abilities, carrying them from level to level. The way that costumes from one level can help you find things in another, however, reminds me more of Mega Man X. Maybe for a future playthrough I could try and route out a path of least backtracking...is what I would say had speedrunners not definitely done that for me already, lol.

Gosh, though, Balan Bouts? Ech. I mean, the only real problem I have with them is that you need to score all Excellents to get the Balan Statue. The word "Great!" should not be that painful. I think just making it so you only need to avoid any "Oops"es to get the statue would be absolutely fine; doubling your drops is more than reward enough for doing it perfectly. As is, those things are WAY too stressful!

But back on a positive note, the boss fights are SICK, and the last one? SUPER sick! SUPER awesome ending to this, well, pretty okay game. If you ask me, it's a really good time. I bought it day 1, full price, and don't regret a thing. Maybe you'll feel different about it, but that's you, and I'm me, and I say, I had fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou gave up on the first date, I learned to commit and love again.

Anyways, given how I have more hours than one may expect, I think it is time for a review of sorts. No, you won't get a usual breakdown of the graphics, controls, and some number that's on a scale one to ten that you jump to anyways because we've really been able to reduce things to just numbers.

Sit back, you might need a tall glass of cold milk for this one. It's going to get very spicy.

See, I'm someone that is generally considered an outcast from society for a wide array of reasons beyond my control. In my early youth I had to deny and hide a lot of what made me, well, me in order to "fit in". I couldn't admit I had certain disabilities and mental illnesses, that was a punchline and insult back in my day and still kind of is. I was in denial of how I should express myself and my love because I was taught that straying from the norms was gross, annoying, and the worst thing ever. It was also around this time I'd follow whatever people said was good and what was bad. Videos full of casual tossing of slurs and talking about the classic games were better than the newer stuff, I ate those up like crazy. Sure as a kid I had played stuff like Billy Hatcher and the Giant Egg and Sonic Heroes but I was a big bad teenager now, I could watch videos with swears in them and didn't need "baby games".

Looking back, I wasn't truly happy, except for one time when a happy little accident occurred. See, I was a fan of Mega Man back in the day, and everyone, and I mean EVERYONE went on about how Classic and X were the best and everything else was bad or not worth it. This was around the time of Mega Man 9's release, and the concept of buying a game digitally was very new to me. I ended up walking out with a copy of Mega Man Starforce Leo that day since hey I figured it was still some kind of Mega Man, I could rage about it on a potential youtube channel if it ended up sucking.

I cannot imagine what my life would have been like without that mistake, as that game changed my life in a lot of ways. It was a game ultimately about coping with depression and trauma, and helping others, all wrapped in a cutesy little RPG. In a way, it changed my life, and taught me that I was missing out on a lot by following what everyone else was saying was good instead of me discovering on my own what was good.

Fast forward a number of years later. I'm an adult in the year 2020 on July 23rd. I had already figured out a few years prior that I was gay, trans, and loving it, but unfortunately I had hit rock bottom in terms of a lot of things in my personal life. To spare details, I had thought about taking my own life to end it all right then and there because it felt like nothing was going to be good ever again. I had reached out to a texting based hotline service at the wee hours of the morning, needing to talk to someone. The volunteer on the other end, with their kindness in helping me calm down, told me that even if things are bad now, there's always the possibility of tomorrow holding something better.

I had gone to bed that night, not expecting much. I had heard maybe mutterings about an Xbox presentation or something, the only thing that really potentially interested me was possible Yakuza Like a Dragon news but that was a localization of a game that was already out. So my expectations were very, very low for the 24th.

I woke up to get a message from a close friend of mine. They linked to an article and told me that Yuji Naka and Naoto Ohshima, were teaming up to bring the very game you're reading this here steam review for. Watching the trailer, I was filled with a truly indescribable amount of joy and hope in my life, a feeling I don't think I had ever felt in my life.

I had something to live for again.

Despite turbulence leading up to release and after, my joy for Balan never once faded. Yes I was going against the mainstream consensus, but I already didn't fit in with the mainstream mold, how I loved was different, so were my tastes. Sure to some I seemed like a goblin living in a swamp but what they thought didn't matter to me. Why make a few random strangers who acted like authority because they wrote steam reviews and had twitch and youtube channels satisfied at the cost of the happiness of myself and my friends?

Like MMSF, I can't imagine my life without Balan. I was not only given hope in life, but was able to feel absolutely true happiness again without restraint of the mainstream's standard. Look, I had played the contemporary and """better""" platformers you've all heard of, but I only liked those. They played great, but they didn't capture my heart and imagination in the same way Billy Hatcher, Mega Man Starforce, or Balan did.

An important lesson is to stop worrying about what others think is the best, and start finding your own definition on what's the best.

What someone may say is a janky piece of trash may be a game you find beauty in it's art and story, and even that the janky gameplay may compliment it. What someone may say is the greatest game ever made, Game of the Year every year may frustrate you. Everything is a case by case basis, your tastes aren't the same as mine. You are you, and you should cherish that.

Really, games and media in general cannot and should not be grouped as either "Citizen Kane" or "The Room" with no room for in between. There's much more of a scale that's gotten much more black and white in our numbered review-loving landscape of review videos, and it's even more limiting when we now expect things to be home runs out of the gate or else be banished to the shadow realm for all time. You never know what could influence bigger and greater games in the future, or if certain themes can speak to someone.

I mean, nowadays you'd probably laugh at some nerd on a forum making Rom hacks of some SNES RPG that sold poorly in the west because it was too unconventional and cartoony compared to Final Fantasy, naming songs he composed based on failed piano covers of songs from some weird PC-98 game after Japan only SNES games passed up as being a "lesser" version of the beloved Chrono Trigger. After all, FF and CT are the more "worthy" games to play and be inspired by, right?

But you suddenly don't laugh when that nerd ends up making one of the biggest indie games of the 2010's and a song so iconic by just it's first few notes.

You may parrot the phrase about making turds shine, but I proudly say that one man's trash may be another man's treasure, their muse even. You never know what may be important or influential to certain people, especially those who create things for you and me after all.

So find your own definition of love, and stand on stage proudly, let your passionate voice ring throughout the audience.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter streaming this game last year all the way to 100% Completion, I honestly cannot grasp how anyone can hate on this game. Sure, it has a few inconveniences and questionable design choices, but let's be honest, what game doesn't? You get PLENTY of content even by the full, original, price of admission standards - 12 worlds called Chapters, each with 4 levels called Acts (one of which for each chapter is post-game content, and the other being the boss) for a total of 48 stages. If you like to explore, these stages are PACKED with secrets (some are extremely challenging to find) and even rewards you for going to places many would overlook as being out of bounds.

Difficulty wise, the game is very easy and chill (unless you're a sucker for punishment and try to keep the game's real-time auto-adjusting difficulty in Hard Mode), but that's expected when the target audience for this game are children; but don't let that cause you pass this up because that makes it the perfect game to play when you just want to unwind and have a feel-good time.

The game got a lot of unfair hate for the controls and costume mechanics, and while these aren't ideal, they're honestly far from being unplayable or bad. Yes, the game uses only two buttons - one for cycling between costumes, and the other for either jumping or performing a costume specific action, but what the negative reviews won't tell you is that your ability to jump is rarely encumbered. The majority of costumes grant you passive abilities that don't require button inputs (such as letting you climb webs, run really fast, freeze time, shrink, automatically launch projectiles, etc.), or enhance your jumping ability in some way (i.e. gliding, hovering, a Sonic style homing attack, double jumps, flight, swimming, etc.). And yeah, you lose costumes upon taking damage, but again, what the complaints don't tell you are: a.) that the game is easy and almost all combat is completely avoidable, and even then you won't be taking damage often at all; and b.) you have an inventory! After you collect 3 costumes, any additional costumes will replace the right-most one you're carrying by sending it to storage. You can then access any of your stored / spare outfits at any of the level's checkpoints via the dressing room. There's also no limit to how many of each you can store.

Another factor people complained about was some costumes making others obsolete, which is true as later worlds contain better versions of costumes you once had but that's normal in some videogames, it's called progression. Is it perfect? No, but it's far "unplayable" or even "bad."

The Balan Bout QTE (Quick Time Event) mini-games are another point that got a lot of unjust anger for being just that, mini-games. People were probably expecting these to be fully playable on-rail sequences like NiGHTs, and while that would be awesome, this game ISN'T NiGHTs, and has no obligation to be! Let Balan be Balan and NiGHTs be NiGHTs. Anyways, they're short, unobtrusive, mini-games that award a Balan Statue if you get a Perfect; with the only annoying design choice being that you'll need to defeat / re-fight the world's boss each time you wish to retry it. Thankfully, the bosses are easy and can generally be dealt with in two minutes or less (the only real challenge in each boss fight is finding 3 unique ways to uses each boss' own attacks against it if you want the Balan Statues for those).

Hopefully this clears up some concerns about the game and convinces you to give Balan a chance. If not, you're missing out on a really fun casual 3d platformer with amazing music, cute graphics, a generous amount of memorable level designs that reward thorough exploration, and an overall feel-good time. Either way, I'm having a wonderful time in Wonderworld.\t\t\t\t\t

Balan Wonderworld is a constant contest for the worst power up.
You're probably already aware that the game is bad while you're browsing the store page. Unless you're streaming a playthrough of the game with your friends dedicated to mocking the game, don't buy it. Touted by some as the worst game of 2021, Balan Wonderworld is an incredibly boring experience. Balan is an experience I can't even recommend to game designers on what not to do, as the mistakes Balan makes are so obviously wrong decisions that I struggle to imagine how they made it in the final cut. If for some reason you want to play it to see how bad it is, pay as little as possible. The $60 launch price was straight up theft for the unlucky souls who bought this game day 1. Balan Wonderworld is a tedious 10 hour adventure that I never want to touch again.
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2711338897
As a 3D platformer, fun movement is essential for a fun time. Balan Wonderworld does not have that. There is no ground pound, no air dash, no homing attack, no double jump, no spin jump, etc.. All you can do in Balan is jump. If you play on a controller the four primary buttons and trigger buttons are ALL bound to jump. And if you're playing on keyboard, Balan Wonderworld does the pleasure of having the pause button be MIDDLE MOUSE. The game doesn't even tell you that so I thought you straight up couldn't pause the game until I read a Steam guide near the end of the game. Side rant about the controls aside, the main gimmick of Balan is that every single normal ability you'd have in any other platformer is locked behind a costume powerup you have to collect in every level. There's 90 total and oh boy some of them are so pitiful that you might as well call not call them power ups. A good majority of them are straight up copies of power ups you've already got. Pounding Pig and Pounding Robot are functionally identical yet you unlock them hours apart. Jellyjolt and Diving Dolphin both allow you to swim except one allows you to jump while swimming. Any time a power up offers you a projectile to shoot, you have to stand completely still to fire, and even worse, shooting the projectile takes away your ability to jump since everything is bound to jump. The only joy I got from these powerups was finding out how bad they were with my friends. Finding out that there's 2 power ups dedicated to singular purposes like riding a train track or creating a ladder was a genuine treat to my friend group. There's not a single puzzle that combines the use of 2 powerups and you will never want to use a powerup after its 2 second puzzle purpose is over. My personal favorite useless power ups have to be Gear Prince, which lets you open a door and Lovely Lantern, which makes the area slightly brighter in a whole 2 parts of the game. Mario Odyssey but made by someone who doesn't play video games is a good way to describe the power up system. Especially since you don't even become the enemy, you just wear a fursuit of them.

The level design is also horrendously bad. Every level feels the exact same with the only difference between any of them being a painted-on skin that only serves a cosmetic purpose. I'm fairly certain the only level with a unique gimmick was the clown world with its rotating platforms and hallways. The amount of backtracking needed to collect a bunch of the trophies to progress the story certainly doesn't help either. The only breaks from the blandest platforming in existence are quick time events that require pixel perfect reaction time to the prompts to be useful and these weird sports minigame that offer some of the game's useless coin/ring equivalent. The only possible saving grace in the gameplay would be the reimagined Chao Garden copy with the Tims, except it's incredibly neutered and watered down. There's barely any customization for the little buggers and it randomly appears and disappears on every single one of them for what does exist. There's not even any sort of minigame for raising them like the Chao Karate/Racing, you have to do nothing and wait. Waiting is the minigame you get for raising your Tims. Boring platforming, quick time events, and a waiting minigame is the entire game.

The best part of the game is how easy you can abuse exploits. The only good powerups, Air Cat and Frost Fairy, which let you fly through the air for a short time, allow you to completely break the game. The amount of air time and maneuverability provided by these two costumes allowed me to skip every "puzzle" needed to collect the various trophies that needed costumes from other levels. It also allowed me to straight up skip major parts of levels. Oh and swapping costumes gives you invincibility frames, so many of them that you can play the entire game without taking damage because of it. Bosses and enemy encounters went from dodging attacks to pressing a single button to just ignore damage. That exploit is so easy to pull off that everyone who's played the game will have figured it out at one point or another. I'd also like to point out that every single NPC disappears when you get too close to them. The only reason I could see for this decision is that the developers were too lazy to code in collision detection. It led to the game being one of the most isolated platformers I ever played and I know that was not the intent.
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2711341118
The only area of the game that had actual effort put into it is the animation, which was contracted by a separate studio. It's hard to praise talented animation when it's for sometimes confusing storytelling and done by a completely different team. Like there's genuine effort in the animations, but it's not from the developers AND I have no idea what's going on. The story involves you going from person to person to help them with their problems. Some of them are fairly easy to understand like oh this girl is being bullied to his farm was destroyed. But others are straight up bizarre with their plots involving an evil dolphin or a man snapping because he lost a single chess game. One of the morals taught from them was incredibly bizarre considering the solution was to time travel so a little girl could see her pet again. The last thing I can praise the game for points back to the art again, and that's the character design. The creatures you disguise as all look neat, especially Balan himself. His character design is genuinely outstanding. But does that really matter when you don't even play as him? Oh and while the actual cutscenes have talent behind them, I don't think I can say the same about the dancing scenes that appear at the end of every level. They're the same scene with like 3 total songs and it happens at the end of every level. It's so jarring to see the well made animations and then going to the moviemaker dances.
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2711341952
Overall, Balan Wonderworld sucks. Even the name is stupid, you're going to call it Balan Wonderland like 50 times whenever you're going to talk about it. The only fun you're getting out of this game is the mockery of it as a product. I am genuinely astounded as to what happened in the development process for the game to turn out like this. Balan Wonderworld is a 2/10 and it's only keeping those points for decent art direction and being a functional game. Not a single interesting idea is experimented with here and every idea that is executed is as boring as possible.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is absolutely beautiful! The art style, the character design, the soundtrack and cutscenes are all breathtaking. The game has enough character to rival Hat in Time which is no small praise as every single bit of creativity and artistic thought the creators had were put into this. Very wholesome and the dance scenes after each boss always made me smile or laugh. The positive energy the game has is through the roof and this is something that is very rare today.

Honestly the hate this game gets I think is just ridiculous. I know it is common today to complain about everything ever, but I do not believe this game to be bad. It is clear it could have been A LOT better regarding the actual playing of the game. The fact that there is only one button to do everything, so many costumes, quite annoying minigames with no real way to restart if you mess up, bugs and glitches is somewhat a huge turnoff for many. The final boss is very annoying in how time consuming and pointless it is on many parts. I have seen many people complain about the Isle of Tims part, but that makes no sense to me as it is completely optional. The cuteness and innocence this game provides us is very welcome and I think the bashing this game has received is not accurate at all.

As a huge fan of old platformers I find this game very fun and experiencing this really made me happy. I do recommend this game and I hope it will get a sequel someday that will correct the flaws of this one and make it the game this one wanted to be. Nonetheless, this game was fun.\t\t\t\t\t
