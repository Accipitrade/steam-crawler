\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNOTE: Obviously I am playing the PC version. I have heard other versions, particularly the Switch version, are absolutely ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ terrible, and I believe it. I also am using a somewhat powerful gaming laptop, so I have not experienced any performance issues whatsoever. Keep this in mind in regard to my enjoyment of the game. I can't speak for people with weaker systems or on different platforms.

All in all, the game isn't half as a bad as people are making it out to be. It's not a bad game, but it's not a great game either. Just relatively decent and somewhat typical for a platformer of this breed. I love the aesthetic, and the character designs, especially the Tims -- who do a lot more than I initially expected, and actually became useful in later levels, which was a nice surprise.

First four or so worlds are incredibly boring, making for an absolutely abysmal first impression -- but, they are somewhat shorter than later worlds, at least from my experience. Levels as a whole get a bit better, as well as a bit bigger, as you progress. World 6-2 is probably where you will finally find a sense of freedom in exploration, after obtaining the Air Cat costume. Worlds open up in groups, as do acts, which I wasn't initially aware of, so you can access World 6-2 right after the completion of World 4, I believe. None of this is any excuse for starting out so subpar, but hey, I had and am still having fun with the game. It does have parts of it that are poorly done, and there are design decisions that don't make the most sense for a modern platformer, but it has its merits, and is not the worst game ever made. By no means is it unplayable or nothing but frustrating. A lot of what people complain about, from the one-button mechanics to the costume system, are not actually all that frustrating save for in very specific situations, which didn't occur often enough for me to throw my controller or anything. You just kind of get used to it, and it becomes hardly noticeable for the most part. Quite a few spots were fun to explore as well, especially once I got the costumes that allowed for easier platforming and exploration. Many collectibles are well-hidden, and as it turns out, a good deal of costumes also serve as almost a secondary collectible, being hidden out of sight! All necessary costumes are very obvious and present, but some others are tucked away and it was interesting trying to find them. I even found some really good ones that only appeared in like one obscure spot, and on one hand, why there? But on the other hand, well, I do feel rewarded for exploring. So that's definitely something.

The meta lore of the whole thing really intrigued me as well... Very interesting stuff. If you play this game, be sure to pick up the novel or something so you actually know what the hell is going on. It really is a crime that so much of the story isn't even featured in the game, but to me there's something almost charming in weird, borderline incomprehensible games. That's just my taste I guess.

I will say this though: Do NOT buy this game for $60. In my opinion, this is more of a $30 or $35 game in regard to quality. I love Yuji Naka's work, and I can tell he tried with this game; however, the mismanagement by multiple different parties, (and presumably crunch), is obvious. Early levels feel extremely limited and very empty. There is a sense of something missing in many different areas. The game is not lacking in heart, but rather, lacking in life, especially in early levels. I feel it could have really benefited from a delay. I do understand how many people believe a delay could not have "saved" this game, but honestly, I feel that is disingenuous. Rather, a more accurate statement would be that a delay would not be able to make this game meet the high expectations of fans -- but, it very much COULD have made it better for what it was, and improved what was already there. Again, it's a little better later on, but still not a fantastic game. The best parts of the game are about a third to two thirds of the way in onward, and that's kind of sad considering how much hatred it has gotten in such a short amount of time. The game does have some good in it, but very few people can tolerate it long enough to see it. I would brand this game as more of a curiosity than a masterpiece in any capacity. Pick it up when it's on sale, if that ever happens, and behold it in all its nonsensical strangeness. Give it a chance beyond World 4, and see what you think. You may like it, you may hate it, you may even be totally indifferent to it, but it's definitely a conversation starter in and of itself regardless.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBalan Wonderworld, despite its obvious shortcomings and bad press, turned out to be a much more positive experience than I ever expected. I can't recall another game in recent memory that I bought as a joke and ended up genuinely wanting to 100%. This feeling goes for me playing more efficiently than I do in most games. Pros and Cons, short of spoilers, as far as I think, are these:

PROS:
- Great aesthetic design; Every character and costume has a good margin of care put into them, with a miss here or there, but for the most part are amazing. Definitely the high point of the game.
- Boss battles are interesting and fun, offering three different ways to damage them and all share the prior point of being very aesthetically pleasing. Same goes for the pre-boss Mini-Boss, which is simple, but usually fights in an interesting way.
- The cutscenes are very well animated at the end of each chapter (after you beat the boss, not the pre-roll before the boss fight).
- Despite the "every button is jump" argument, it controls much better than other people advertise. I went in expecting PS1 tank controls and I feel like it controls just as good as any other platformer.
- While the costumes can be bunched together by use, they're all still fun to use, and gives you options on how you want to tackle a puzzle the game proposes.
- The levels encourage you to see the game in-full and gather costumes from later parts to solve puzzles proposed in earlier chapters. Plus, it encourages you to take downtime in the hub world to develop the Tower of Tims.

CONS:
- Levels tend to get cramped, with some rooms being small enough to make simple mistakes like bumping into an enemy or falling in an avoidable hole in the floor.
- Some boss battles are confusing in how to get all three Balan Statues, which made me want to either clear it with the one attack method I found out or looking it up.
- Needing to clear the boss before being able to redo Balan's Bouts is unnecessary, would be better if you could just leave the act and come back.

Overall, I understand why Steam's overall review score for the game is "Mixed". It's not perfect, and I didn't experience the demo's flaws before the Day 1 Patch (since I didn't own the game yet), but it's a far cry from the disasterpiece that I was told it was before trying it myself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter achieving 100% completion, I'd like to start with a simple phrase:

Balan Wonderworld has got to be one of the most unfairly hated and criticized games of all time.

This game goes back to the 90s, when 3D platformers were just catching on and developers were finding their way around three-dimensional movement. This game doesn't offer any complex mechanics beyond free movement and a simple jump, however, the developer worked around these self-imposed limitations to make a fun and engaging experience for all ages, though I have to state that this game was primarily made for children.

Now, the game does offer a wide variety of costumes for players to obtain. Some of these costumes change the gameplay style and even prevent the character from jumping, which some might find annoying. It is important to mention, however, that levels are designed around the costumes' abilities; not being able to jump while wearing some costumes is also taken into account in every level's design, although if it proves annoying, you can always switch back to a different costume that allows jumping.

In typical 3D platformer fashion, there's also a ton of collectibles that extend the duration of the game, and there's other sub-objectives, such as taking care of Tims and restoring the Tower o' Tims, which will keep you playing a bit longer. To add to this, there's a hidden Hard Mode which is activated by defeating a lot of enemies without getting hit. Hard Mode is active when enemies become red and finishing a level grants you a red stamp instead of a blue or yellow one.

Balan Wonderworld also features beautiful CG cutscenes similar in style to those used for the Sonic the Hedgehog games. Speaking of, Yuji Naka, the lead programmer for the aforementioned series, worked as the director for Balan so those familiar with his works might find this game enjoyable. Also, as for the character designs, Naoto Ohshima, the designer of Sonic the Hedgehog and Nights, was also involved in the creation of this game. Every character was designed by him, and it shows as they're all very reminiscent of those two series.

I can tell from playing this game that it was made with a lot of love and passion. The premise is heartwarming, the music is memorable and very pleasant to listen to, and the gameplay, although not complex, is fun and enjoyable.

For $60 I'd say I did get my money's worth, but a lot of people might prefer to pay less. It took me 33 hours to achieve 100% completion which I think is a decent amount of time for the price tag.

If you were born in the late 80s to early 90s then there's a chance that this game will bring you back to that very early era of 3D platformers, back when games were simple and pure fun. If you're a fan of that, then this game is definitely for you!

And to all of those who have already bought the game and are just starting...

Welcome to Balan's Wonderworld! Hope you enjoy the show!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish the game had turned out better.

After having completed the game I can say without doubt that the game is not as bad as I heard some people say it is. it's not a good game by any stretch but it's not that bad.
I liked the art direction and the music, but the level design felt a little bland at times.
The decision to use every button for a single action (even for the menu controls) annoyed me to no end as this prohibited jumping in costumes that had other functions.
I admire the aspiration to put 80 costumes in the game but feel that more than a few of the costumes are redundant as they fulfill similar if not identical roles, that is not to say that some aren't fun, I personally liked the sun and moon costumes.

During my time playing the game there were no glitches or bugs so it's got that at least. I have seen footage of the game struggling but have personally had no noticeable drops in frame rate.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBalan Wonderworld has lovely style, amusing ideas, and a lot of heart, but it has trouble delivering satisfying gameplay.

I think the best way to describe Balan is that it would have been a fantastic game in the 2000's or early 2010's. Having played through the entire game, I don't think it really has any huge, glaring issues. Rather than trying to be everything and a half like modern big budget titles, Balan's goals are simple: You're going to explore fun worlds and discover fun costumes. It's all just meant to be good plain fun. Nothing is too intense. The graphics are fun and creative, the character designs are spectacular, the music is phenomenal.

The concepts are high, the difficulty is low, and if you're looking for a relaxing game to pick up and mess around with after work or something light and safe to give to a child, it certainly delivers. The biggest problem is that it doesn't quite fit the initial $60 price tag. I believe if Balan had shipped as a $39.99 game it wouldn't have received such harsh criticism - but more on that later.

I'm a huge fan of Yuji Naka and the NiGHTS series in particular, so I was really cheering for this game. I wanted it to be good and for people to like it. That's my bias, and it would be remiss of me to let it stop me from giving an honest critique of the game. There is a lot of missed potential here. Clearly the devs had big plans, but it seems that due to some internal issues at Square Enix and a rushed production cycle, things didn't really pan out. Balan Wonderworld is a simple game. There's not much challenge to be found - and when there is, its often a result of mediocre level design. Yes, it's a platformer, but the best part of this game is the puzzle box like way you can apply different costumes to all the levels - something that requires backtracking. This isn't terrible in and of itself, but its not what people tend to look for in a title selling itself as an action platformer. There's a bit of a mismatch between the game and it's presentation that hurts it. If you don't like replaying levels, you won't like this game.

There's also a strange divide between the simple surface level mechanics and the hidden background mechanics. The latter of which are fascinating and are one of the few things that make me feel like a kid again - in the online age of gaming, every title is picked apart and all the surprises are mapped out by Polygon and Kotaku hours after release. The obscure mechanics of Tims, Lance mode, and the 3rd acts came as a huge surprise, and frankly I was elated to be surprised and confused by a game in this manner again.

Then there's the matter of story... Oh Yuji Naka. You had such faith in what you wrote for Balan - why couldn't you have included it? Cutscenes are scant and with very little dialogue, making the plot of the game confusing and often putting it on the backburner. Now, this proves an issue when your gameplay is weak and the real thrust of the game is its style. Balan Wonderworld would be SO much better if I didn't have to read a companion novel to know what's going on. There's real heart here, but it doesn't come across to the average player! It's a tragedy!! The characters and cutscenes are expertly made - why aren't there more of them? Why is there so little in game focus on this story Naka was so proud of? Yet another missed opportunity. Throw it on the pile.

At the end of the day, I enjoyed my time with this game. It's inspired me to create fanart, I listen to the OST every day at work, and no doubt I'll be dropping some money in merch. But it could have been better. I wanted Balan Wonderworld to learn from the mistakes that kept Journey of Dreams from being a great game, but it ended up repeating them. I still think this game is worth playing, and I know I'm going to have more fun with it, but it's all quite bittersweet as I look over someone's beloved project and realize it could have been so much more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am going to preface this review by saying that no, this game is not perfect. It was hyped for by a lot of people, and I understand people being let down--but this is a game I truly want people to sit down and, at the very least, try for themselves.
The story is heartwarming, validates many kinds of mental and emotional traumas, teaches empathy, and leaves a lot open to player interpretation. If you have had an event one of the characters go through happen in your life, or maybe it happened to someone you care about, this game will use its story to say, "hey, it's okay to feel this way. These feelings are all valid. If you're going through this, you can and will get through this."
The gameplay is one that encourages exploration and experimentation using the many costumes. It's one that you have to take slowly and take risks with, and stocking up multiples of costumes is an absolute must. (Personally I recommend the two Air costumes and Double Jumper--those are big ones to stockpile, ASAP.)

Now, I've talked about what I like. What don't I like? Well, a number of things. The "one button does everything" complaint is one I share, but it allows for coming up with strategies and helps a younger audience--which, allow me to stress, is likely the kind of audience this game is targeting--get through the game without becoming overwhelmed by trying to remember what button does what. I'm also not a fan of how some costumes don't let you jump and how some act on their own, but as someone that has 100%'d the game, allow me to assure you that the majority of the costumes DO allow you to jump, and the costumes that act on their own are few and far between. You can easily switch to a costume that allows you to jump if you need one, I assure you.
There's also some levels that seem to have the camera act as if the level is on a sphere, namely chapter one,
which can be disorienting to some. However, most levels are on a flat plane like any other platformer. Where Balan Wonderworld has a fault, it has a reason for doing or makes up for it somehow.

If I could give Balan Wonderworld a proper rating, I'd give it a 8 or 8.5 out of 10. The story and music are where the game shines brightest, yes, but this is not a game to overlook by any means. Give this game a try--take it from someone who was excited for this game from day one, and will never let it leave her mind.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game's ill reputation is not unfounded, but I couldn't bring myself to click the thumbs-down button for what is, at its heart, a charming little story about people finding the courage to overcome the various roadblocks life throws their way. The novel is, of course, far better in this regard as it actually takes the time to flesh out the characters and their struggles, but I don't think the game does too bad on its own merits.

Presentation-wise, the game also has hands-down some of the best-looking cutscenes I've ever seen, bordering on Pixar-level CG. The music is certainly no slouch either, from the breathtaking intensity of the Hyrdrac boss theme to the jolly catchiness of the Clocktower stage theme.

Gameplay-wise, the game is severely held back by its one major flaw: its bizarre commitment to having only one action button. The fact that this renders several of the game's costumes incapable of jumping leads to strange situations where the player, being equipped only with non-jumping costumes, is wholly incapable of making any progress without sacrificing a costume, making some parts of the game a lot harder than it feels like it was probably intended to be.

I still insist, however, that there is fun to be had here for those willing to overlook this (admittedly quite major) design flaw. My recommendation is buy it on sale and give it a chance. And also PLEASE PLEASE PLEASE READ THE NOVEL IT IS VERY GOOD.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is like staring into a nightmare abyss of weird choices and straight up bad design with just enough polish and shiny graphics to trick you into thinking this is a good game.

It is not a good game.

I wanted to believe in Yuji Naka and Naoto Ohshima so, so badly. I practically grew up on their projects in the late 90s into the mid 00s. If you showed me the trailers for every game that came out this year, Balan Wonderworld would be a top pick. Naoto Ohshima went bananas with the overall art direction and it SHOWS. Visually, the game is one of my all time favorites. It feels like a Gamecube game made for 2021.

Everything else sucks. The platforming is mediocre at best, some of the music sounds actually plagiarized, the costumes only get worse as you approach the final battle, the story is told in a separate 280 page book that reads like an incomplete script, and the gem system for the Tim raising is worthless RNG for the most part. The only 'good' levels are at the very very end of the game after the credits roll. The costumes in these stages are the best in the game also, but by this point you're probably too sick of this game to care a whole lot.

I wouldnt say I necessary regret my time with Balan Wonderworld or that I'm even disappointed really. Im moreso stunned that the game got actively worse every step of the way. I can only recommend this game to people like me who were too curious for their own good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fantastic!

Amazing character designs, great music ,fantastical and whimsical environments! The story has absolutely lovable characters, even if its a little hard to follow. Controls are not the best ever but passable.

Level design is such that each level is like a puzzle box you have to carefully explore and decipher with traditional platforming sprinkled in.

The only slight critique I can offer this game is that some it does a poor job communicating the level of importance of each different objective. I was VERY pleased to find the negative reviews of this game are wrong :)

Some questions I've gotten thus far:

Q: What's your FPS?
A: I have not noticed any drop or lack of FPS. (Nvidia Geforce RTX 2060 and 32 gigs of RAM)

Q: Does the game lock up sometimes?
A: Never. Not even once.

Q: How are the controls?
A: The controls are a tad slippery but above average.

Q: How are the graphics?
A: The graphics are absolutely stellar. Wacky and wonderful. Amazingly whimsical environments, smooth models, adorable and magical character design.

Q: Why is the game so simplistic?
A: It's not simplistic... Or no more simplistic than other 'collect the macguffins' games.

Q: How does it compare to platformers?
A: Less action oriented and more being inside a giant puzzle box. You're meant to out-think the environment.\t\t\t\t\t
