\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAre you in the fence because of the bad reviews? Trust me, I was in the same position and after beating the game I can say with 100% confidence: Just play. Don't let other people tell you what is good or bad, especially when they are just trying to make fun of the game or just being dishonest.

Let's get this out of the way: Yes, the game has flaws and some bizarre design decisions: The characters fading away when you get close, the giant characters staring at you, some weird 3D rigs makes the mocap dance feels weird. But all of this is just superficial flaws IMO, and you get used to them and make the game have its own charm.

But the Balan QTE mini game is definitely the most annoying thing in the game,. it`s way too punitive in a very casual game, and don't let you retry as many as you want. It's so sad because Balan is such a fantastic character, and those animations are amazing, but man... these segments are just long and tedius.

Besides that, some small and specifics problems on the levels here and there, nothing that can't be overlooked by the good parts.

With that said, the game is actually good. After the 4th chapter, things start to open up, and combining different costumes is really fun. The game has a surprising amount of verticality and freedom through the levels, and I enjoyed the collect-a-thon aspect. Note that I didn't try to do 100%, but I collected most of the Balan Statue in the levels. I really enjoyed my time with this game. The levels are not big, so replaying them with new costumes is not a big deal, and you start to look with a new perspective as you unlock more costumes, really nice.

While playing through the level you will collect dozens of the same costume, and you can change at any checkpoint at any time, which gives a lot of freedom to the game. Some of the costume aren't as useful, and some is overlaped by a better version with the same interaction, but overall, most of the costumes has a purpose somewhere in the game, and it`s kind of creative to see the abilites of the costumes and use them to break the game.

The game is clearly targets a young audience, with simple challenges, very expressive and lovable characters, and a Pokemon-ish "gotta cath'em all" aspect with the costumes.

The main quality of the game is the presentation. The character design is amazing, the songs are fantastic, the sound design is cute, the visuals are charming with lots of style, and it has tons of adorable characters that start to grow on you (And the game has a surprisingly emotional ending). Balan is definetely the star of the show, his visuals are just S+, one of the best characters I've ever seen, and I absolutely love the main character (I played with Emma), she's so cute.

Overall, I enjoyed my time with Balan. It`s sad to know that it will be a forgotten mascot because of internet trolls. But if you love 3D platformers and want to give it a try, you may end up lovingit as I did :D\t\t\t\t\t

Not the best game, but Balan Wonderworld is a good one nonetheless.
It's super fun and entertaining, haven't enjoyed a game this much in a very long time. And although I haven't replayed it yet, I can see the replayability being strong seeing that it's sort of a collect-a-thon. It really brings me back to those wonderful games from long ago.
I would say that the weak points is that the gameplay is very, VERY simplistic and quite basic at times, making some parts of the game to be boring, and the QTEs are a bit annoying but it doesn't stop it from being enjoyable.
The sound and music is phenomenal, one of the best I've heard in decades and would leave you stopping playing to just sit back and listen to this magical score.
The graphics are very well done and totally beautiful and vibrant, cutscenes are dynamic and simply stunning.
In Balan Wonderworld, the pros definitely outweigh the cons, and if you're a fan of NiGHTS into Dreams and the style of Naoto Oshima and Yuji Naka, no doubt, this game is for you.\t\t\t\t\t
