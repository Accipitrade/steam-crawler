\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't even know where to begin with this game. To say its a disappointment is an understatement. The only fun I had playing this game was making fun of how bad it was, but that got old soon and I just went back into boredom. This game has no substance and everything blurs together because its the exact same. You go through 2 levels, fight a mini-boss that dies in 3 hits, and then a boss that dies in 3 hits. At the end of each boss you get a ridiculous mocap dance scene with the playable character and the character you save. Balan Wonderworld claims to have 80+ costumes, and they are technically correct, but a good majority of the costumes are the exact same, with a different coat of paint. This game forces you to collect a certain amount of trophies to progress and going back through different worlds would be fine if the costume change system wasn't terrible. Instead of being able to use any costume you've unlocked, you have a finite amount of the costume and if you run out, you have to go back to the level its from and collect more of them. If this is Yuji Naka's last chance to make another video game, its a terrible way to go out, because this proves that he doesn't know how to make good video games anymore. The only praise I have towards this game are the cgi cutscenes and the character/monster designs. Naoto Oshima's artstyle is vibrant and distinct, and is probably what attracted the majority of people to this game. Please do not waste 60 dollars on Balan Wonderworld, there are much better platformers that cost much less.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had some time to give it some thought and I've realized that this game actually isn't that bad. It's colorful, it's fun, and it's actually got some ok songs on the soundtrack. It's not the best game ever but it's not irredeemable garbage as some people might have you believe. Yes, the gameplay is limited and simplistic, but frankly, I don't know why everyone didn't expect that from the get-go. Everyone has been calling this the "New Nights Into Dreams" since the day it was first announced. And Nights has extremely simplistic controls.

I do think that there are quite a few bugs, and that's not great. But none of them got in the way of me finishing the game so it's nothing to get upset about in my personal opinion. And it is true that a lot of the costumes are just redundant, but I think that's actually kind of a good thing since it's more convenient to be able to just grab one from the nearest level that you might need, since anything you replace gets stored away anyways and you can pretty much just change what's in your queue whenever you want.

At the end of the day, I'd say this is more of a game for kids than anything else. The story is simple, the controls are simple, and the artstyle is pretty cartoony and childlike. Not that there's anything wrong with that, though it's a bit of a salt in the wound when this game costs 60 dollars.

I think it's definitely worth your time to try it if you can, but if you seriously can't get over having a very simple control scheme, then I don't think this would be your cup of tea. Overall I'd say this game is pretty inoffensive and not at all worth all the vitriol it gets. Would I pick this up again anytime soon to play it? Probably not. Do I regret playing it all? Heck no. So if you really wanna play it, I'd recommend waiting for the next steam sale.\t\t\t\t\t

An example of this is the jellyfish costume and dolphin one, both of them do a similar function just one is slower and sticks to one path with enemy control (which can just be taken out by any costume of course.) And the other is faster allows jump movement in water and far more utilised.

There is positives here however that being character designs for starters. Mainly our helper through the levels Balan and Lance, these two are at the very least visually interesting to look at but as anyone will tell you style over substance makes for a boring game.

Another positive is some of the minigame like sections that break up the mundane at least a little bit, Balan Bout's do this too but it mostly follows the platform of hit A to do a thing. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's be honest: Balan Wonderworld's selling points are Yuji Naka's fantasy worldbuilding and Naoto Ohshima's art style. The game seeks to appeal to people who miss PS2-era action-adventure games and thus are willing to reexperience both its upsides and downsides. Inevitably, people who've played and enjoyed games like NiGHTS Into Dreams, Billy Hatcher, and Klonoa will find much more amusement in Balan Wonderworld than ones who didn't.

That said, there's still charming depth to the gameplay nonetheless, especially if you intend to collect all statues. For instance, revisiting acts after earning new costumes allows for considerably fresh experiences. Doing so is clearly encouraged by the developers as this is the only way to acquire some collectibles. In other words, Balan Wonderworld challenges you to overcome limitations by making creative use of the costumes you've found.

The Tims (and Tower o' Tims) system is pretty interesting too, working as a side objective to work on while in the lobby. They are heavily reminiscent of NiGHT'S A-life and Sonic Adventure's Chao Breeding, despite working differently from both.

All in all, Balan Wonderworld aims so that 3D retro-platformer fans feel right at home, and it does a great job at that. For the sake of having more games like it in the future, I hope people see it for what it aims to be instead of comparing it to modern 3D platformers.

Edit: If you want an insight on why the game may nonetheless feel incomplete sometimes, be sure to read this Twitter thread. I still think the game is worth playing for anyone who relates to what I've said in the first and last paragraphs of this review. However, be aware the proceedings are more than likely not going to the people who truly wanted this project to thrive.\t\t\t\t\t
