\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a game portrayed by the public to be the worst of its kind, it manages to steal my heart effortlessly by how stylized and fun the game turned out to be, I honestly don't see why It's getting the hate outside of clout chasing and wrong expectations over who directed and designed it.

I loved the freedom you gain later on as you receive more and more powerful costumes, giving you new ways to explore past stages and get the remaining optional statues. While they are for the most part very similar to each other, all of them have a minor difference that makes them feel like an upgrade or good alternative to what you had before.
My biggest pet peeve is the lack of explanation to some of the core mechanics (Tims and Hard mode), had it been delayed we could've seen more of what they've had planned for it.
The repetitiveness of the QTEs did sour up the mood though, had there been more to it rather than reused actions and animations, it could've been a fun one to perfect each time (and for that matter shouldn't require perfecting it for the statues, I feel as though it should've allowed at least two greats or one good). They should've kept them to 1 per stage all the way through, having to do 3 of them in later stages wasn't exactly fun, good thing you don't have to do them unless you wanna grind up Drops.
As for the simplicity of the controls, I think it complements what it was trying to achieve as a Puzzle Platformer, a good majority of the stages seemed to have been built with the lack of jumping (or even the lack of costume switching) in mind where these are to be applied!

I had a blast playing through the entirety of the game despite its minor flukes and I hope to see a patch, DLC or at the very least modding support (official or not) in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

Pros-- Everything about the art direction is tasty, the character designs are lovely, the level music and minigame music is incredibly catchy and memorable IMO. The game has a simplistic enough control scheme and each costume does not have super complex functions, so they're all easy to learn and manage. Some costumes you can get later on make the replayability of this game a lot better than I expected, or are a little game-breaking which is also fun when you backtrack to previous levels. I was playing this game on my custom PC with as high resolution as I could go and the graphics were fine IMO, and I saw no lag nor any framerate drops. If you're a fan of particularly cute things, you would also love the Tims sidequest/pet sim. you can do in-between going through each level.

Cons-- The game has a very simplistic control scheme, which can lead to some annoyances. There's only one button (mapped as both Action and Jump) for everything other than costume switching and pausing, which leads to costumes whose main purpose is Offense also removing your ability to jump until you change costumes. There is also no dialogue/subtitles throughout the game's story and cutscenes so it's difficult to understand the context behind the game's world and chapters. A considerable amount of the actual game's lore is found in a separate novel you can buy for $10, but I don't really think that's a good thing to do for any videogame. Some costumes are also a bit redundant as well. You also can't pause or exit Balan Bouts (QTE minigames that you need to perfect in order to get Trophies, one of the main collectibles in the game)

The game overall is very pretty and has a very fun concept but also a little messy as well. If you don't mind this however, I'd definitely recommend giving the game a go. I would also say the better costumes you unlock as you progress, the more fun the game becomes, IMO. I would def say the whole game picks up after Chapter 3 or 4.\t\t\t\t\t
