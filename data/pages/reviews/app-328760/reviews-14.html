\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had picked up SanctuaryRPG during a Steam sale a few years back. To be real: It was less than a dollar and I was feeling frisky. But after 70+ hours of playing this game, my thought is that the sale price is an insane steal. For the hours of entertainment and enjoyment that I've received from this game, I think the full price tag is worth it and I feel almost guilty for paying so little for a game I love so much. But hey, if you see it for 90% off (like it is RIGHT NOW... at least at the time of writing this) I fully recommend picking it up. That's value.

Gameplay

SanctuaryRPG is a text-based roguelike RPG where you choose from six different classes (each with their own strengths and weaknesses) and grind your way to gain levels and find gear. The premise itself is standard, but there is enough depth to the ways one can create their own character builds that make each new game exciting.

There are four game modes: Classic (permadeath), Softcore (respawns), Lunatic (go ♥♥♥♥ yourself), and Survival which is unlocked through beating the game on the other modes. Most of my experience is playing on Classic, though, I had the unfortunate idea to try playing on Lunatic mode and well… this game is out to kill you. There is no hand holding. You are not safe. Encounters, like most things in this game, are randomized. In the same area that you’ll find a mob that you can one-shot with ease, you will find something that will blind you and force you into a war of attrition before slaughtering you.

That sense of unease and fear can be a lot of the fun of this game though. It sucks to die after thinking up a new build idea and finding the perfect items (which are all RNG drops) to make it work, but there’s some charm in this game that has me throwing more and more attempts at it without ever feeling like it’s pointless. The combat is easy to learn and get into so starting fresh never feels like too much of a drag.

There are definitely some annoyances: because random is well… random, you’re prone to legendary drops that you’re forced to throw away because they have worse stats than some common sword you found at the start of the game. Crafting can sometimes feel pointless because the items you make are just as random as anything you can find from killing mobs (though it is an extra chance at loot). And the inability to hold any armor/weapons besides the ones that you are wearing can feel incredibly frustrating, though, it does make one more mindful of the gear they choose to pick up and forces the player to play and build around the items they find (which can make for some interesting characters).

Graphics

The ASCII-style art of SanctuaryRPG is one of the features that initially stood out to me when I purchased the game and are one of many things that make the game unique. There is a lot of variation in characters, mobs, and environments that is achieved perfectly in this style and are well made. It serves to capture the setting and tone of this game which is very old-school and quirky.

It’s definitely a matter of taste. The screenshots give a good idea of what this game looks like (though, there are some very nice pieces of art that one gets to view as they progress through the story). Some people may be put off, but this game has its own niche that it fills and the ASCII-art continues that theme and charm.

Story

Another fun part of SanctuaryRPG is the writing and the tone and universe it is set within. There is a lot of comedy throughout the game: a lot of silly and random (like the gameplay itself) events that have you milking ghosts, stealing weapons from hobos, or eating flying pancakes. Even in the creation of your character alone you are given an elaborate randomized backstory that sets the fun and humourous tone for things to come.

That said, SanctuaryRPG isn’t devoid of a narrative and it isn’t solely lolsorandom humour (not that I minded the more lighthearted parts of the game anyway). The main plot of the game, while seemingly standard, has your character fighting through places of the world and discovering more about its lore and people.Though, there aren’t many huge plot twists that will keep you awake at night or any moments that will elicit intense emotions.

The story gets the job done and there’s enough intrigue to keep you wanting to get to the end, though the worldbuilding is what I’d consider a stronger element than the plot itself. This game has an interesting setting that feature both dragons and low-technology weaponry alongside robots and portals. It feels like its own unique setting and while it does borrow many classic RPG tropes, it arranges them in fun ways that allows the setting to stand out on its own.

Music

SactuaryRPG’s soundtrack composed by Rafael Langoni Smith consists of 64 chiptune-style tracks. Along with the ASCII-style art, the music helps to give the game both an old-school vibe and its own unique elements. There is a lot of variety in the soundtrack: from the soft and relaxing (Sanctuary Arp II: Title Screen) to dark and ominous (You May Be Experiencing Some Discomfort) to intense and triumphant (Sangue nos Olhos).

There are some really great and memorable melodies in these tracks. The music often serves as another driving force to make one want to continuing playing longer. There’s a thrill in getting into a fight, hearing a particular track and just knowing that you’re ♥♥♥♥♥♥. This is a game soundtrack that I’d actually recommend listening to solely on its own as well!

TL;DR

+Multiple ways to play/build your character
+Easy to get into, but plenty to master
+Unique artstyle
+Comedy-focused writing (which can be good or bad depending on one’s taste, but I’m personally found it entertaining)
+Decent world-building that gives the setting and world intrigue
+Melodic chiptune-style music

+/-RNG adds an element of surprise, but also annoyance
+/-Standard plot that gets the job done, but could have been expanded further (though, I admit that I enjoyed the game regardless of the story)

-Epic/Legendary weapons mean very little since their stats are randomized and often unuseful
-Crafting as well as some other features (such as the Temple or Library) can feel pointless since the same things can be achieved through grinding\t\t\t\t\t
