\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, doing a playthrough with my gf has been great. We havn't finished the game, but it's bloody halarious and I highly suggest picking up this game even though it's well past its time in the current state of games.

Preferably, I had wished there were more classes, but what can one do in 2015 really.
Though late to the table, I hope to see another game like this. Compleatly made of text, filled with out of context humour, and smiling at random sounds in the distance like I know what it is (but honestly I don't, probably just trying to make that sheep over there think i'm cool)

SanctuaryRPG has a ton of little elements that could have been touched on, but for a game of 2015, I see how it could have been quite the feat.
Having played hours upon hours in one of their other games "Card Quest" on my phone every day at work, I was quite overjoyed to find out the two games had both origionated from BlackStudios.

Personally, I'd like to see another SanctuaryRPG game in the future just because of the pure enjoyment of the context and text overhaul. Was quite the breath of fresh air I wanted to take in after having been around so many FPS and RTS games floating around.
Not to forget the beautiful music and artwork.

I plan to finish every piece of this game with every class at hand before moving onto my next trial.

My only regret was being too greedy and accidentally summoning a full on dragon early. To be fair I almost killed it until I became a scortched chew toy.

Know that this game can be fairly hard to master, or to understand fully, but regardless, it's a fun trip all the way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSanctuaryRPG is a fresh take on retro style RPGs. The art style is interesting, the writing is fun and clever, and the gameplay is surprisingly satisfying. It gradually eases you into everything so that all of the text isn't overwhelming, and before you know it, the ASCII presence and text-based commands all feel natural. The combat system is easy to get into while offering quite a bit of complexity. I especially enjoyed the little twists on the art, sound, music, and battles that bring life into this world.

I have no nostalgia for text-based games, and I wouldn't call myself a fan of this genre either, but I found myself hooked on this game. I recommend it to anyone looking for an accessible RPG that's not really like anything else out there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA blast from the past.
+ Art style. The ASCII graphics look great and somehow even detailed. Fun retro art style very well done.
+ Music. The music fits perfectly with the artstyle and retro feeling the game evokes.
+ Story. The story, and the whole game really, is full of quirky paradoic humor that I found pretty funny. Just fire up the game and check out the background story of your character LUL. The story itself is pretty much standard rpg fare, but considering this could've easily been a rogue-like (for real) game with just dungeon crawling and little to nothing in the narrative department, it's cool that the game's got one.
+ Gameplay. You have different classes and builds you can run, combat is turn based but it has a certain rythm to it as you're trying to keep up your combos and dodging/blocking avoiding enemie's strong attacks ("telegraphed" perfectly). Despite the game having artwork for the locations, characters, items, etc. the combat is purely text based. The difficulty curve it's probably a bit steep, at first you'll probably get wrecked by anything but soon enough you'll be breezing through everything (overconfidence is a slow and insidious killer though). Besides combat, there're some small activites you can "do" in town to grind some more XP and gold safely. There's also a crafting system with some RNG and skill worked into it.
+ Complexity. Because of the numerous affixes both on enemies as well as items along with status ailments, special abilities, passive perks and skills there's a deep combat system to be had here, with an emphasis on character build & customization.
+- Combat can get very repetitive when you're several hours into the game. I was tempted to turn on auto-combat some times. Some of the encounters begin to feel like padding a bit.

Overall a fun game that grabbed me surprisingly fast and didn't let go. I think I'll be playing Zork next :D.

I wish there was an android version, seems perfect to play on your phone when you need to kill some time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very charming. I found the ASCII graphics to make me nostalgic for old school DOS RPG's. There is plenty of gameplay and RPG goodness here to tear into for both newcomers to the genre and vetrans alike. All that being said the game has permadeath, which usually isn't something I find to be a turn off, however when you character dies you start all over from the beginning of the story. Normally, not a big deal, but SanctuaryRPG doesn't change playthrough to playthrough. Sure, your item and weapon drops are randomized, but the story, locations, and NPC's are all the same as your last playthrough. With the story being lighlty drizzled on between major bosses and locations, all of which you HAVE to grind for or risk another restart, there is just simply not enough meat on the bone to keep rotating through characters to complete the story. I put about 5 hours into it, and as charming as I found the game, I doubt I will be returning. However for as low of an asking price as they have, I have to give it a recommendation. Its a charming game thats worth a play, but not worth the effort to beat.\t\t\t\t\t
