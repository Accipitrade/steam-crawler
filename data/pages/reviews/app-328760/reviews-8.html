\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wouldn't feel right recommending this game without warning anyone reading the reviews. The creators clearly put a lot of work into the game, but the amount of actual content dries up pretty quickly. The art, humor, and mechanics are thought out, but the RNG deciding the characters, attacks, and dialogue quickly reveals itself.

It starts off as a wild and surprising game. After hours of grinding against random enemies, however, the best parts of the game become repetitive and lose their hard fought appeal. To be clear, it isn't the grinding itself that feels overly repetitive, it is the design, names, jokes, and attacks. Once a joke is played for laughs the second time, or a seemingly unique enemy reappears as if cloned, everything that felt fresh becomes disappointing.

Nevertheless the gameplay and design are definitely worth checking out for the price. The amount of time to dollar spent is tremendous, and this will serve anyone gaming on a budget (or a budget build) superbly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really liked the simple text-based visual aesthetic of the game, but the game itself is not simple. I bought the game as something I could sit down and play for 10-20 minutes at a time whenever I was in the mood. Hey, I got a job and kids and don't have as much time as I used to. I got my first character killed after an hour or so and thought, 'man I'd better be a bit more careful.' However, the game requires that you figure out and then maintain a lot of contextual knowledge that is not always clear. Which 'side' am I on again? Which skills work best with which weapons? Should I proceed on to the next area of the game or go search for some more skeletons to kill in hopes of a good random drop first? These are all questions I had trouble answering properly because of my (too?) casual approach to gameplay. Somehow after I managed to level my second character up quite a bit higher than the first, I didn't play for a few weeks and then couldn't quite remember which abilities were best used in which order to counter certain enemy attacks. Spring some random elite monster on me and...well, the death of my second character was more upsetting than the first. Overall (and especially for the price), this game is worth the price if the screenshots look appealing. I wish I had more time to really focusing on mastering it.\t\t\t\t\t
