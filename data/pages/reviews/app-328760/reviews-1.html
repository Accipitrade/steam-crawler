\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSanctuaryRPG is a neat idea, but it falls down some in the execution. (NOTE: I accidentally left this up in the background for a few days so my playtime is inflated. I still put some good time into it, but nothing like 140 hours.)

In the first place, it's far too linear for a permadeath roguelike. I die the first time, "okay fine, I've learned some things," go back. Cruise through the game for a while then hit a sudden difficulty spike and die. "Well, ♥♥♥♥. I guess I should grind more before moving on to the next stage." Play again. There's one moment early on where you're given a seemingly plot-relevant choice. I made a different choice this time. Everything played out effectively the same.

Then the repetition really sets in. There's no new randomly generated map to explore. There's no map at all, just your current objective, previous objectives (other than the first one in a new Act) to go back and grind more, and various town interfaces. The monsters are pretty interchangeable. Some have distinct traits but they rarely change that much about what you do once you really get the combat down. You get different random equipment but unlike e.g. FTL where building around missiles vs. beam weapons vs. teleporters etc. changes your playstyle, the different items in Sanctuary just change your numbers.

So getting back to where I was after dying was just a boring slog. Moving on from there was somewhat more enjoyable, but the grinding at each stage to make sure I didn't get difficulty spiked again added to the monotony. But the only alternative was just to just press on, likely die, and do the whole thing again. The game at one point gets preachy about "save scumming," but if I could just go back and grind when I knew I had to it would've taken a lot of the tedium out of the game.

I actually found the all-ASCII and even all-keyboard approach kind of neat. Part nostalgia and part just being different from other things out there I guess. The art was okay, but disappointing. I've seen much better ASCII art. I think they were hampered by their choice of chunky font. I'm assuming they went with the 40-character per line font from early PC days for the nostalgia effect ('Member CGA? 'Member Wasteland? I 'member!) but it's not great for drawing with.

So ... I know humor is subjective, but I'm still surprised so many reviewers liked it. There are a few good lines in there, but most of it relies on nonsense and completely fell flat for me. Here's a typical example: you pick the option to entertain patrons at your bar, and get "You strip naked and rub a potato over yourself. One of the patrons leaves." Lulz? The game seems to think if it repeats random things about potatoes often enough anything about potatoes becomes funny.

The most mystifying thing though is the praise for the story. There is *barely* a story there, and most of it doesn't make sense. There are a few NPCs to talk to, but their conversation trees are short and pointless. Eventually you learn a couple more scraps of information about them, but the conversation options stay the same, even though some of them don't make sense anymore. There are a couple times when the game makes you question whether you're doing the right thing. At this point I thought, "wait, why *am* I doing this anyway? I'm just following whatever's put into the [QUEST] line on the screen and come to think of it I don't even know where that's coming from. No one has even told me to do these things most of the time, it's just some disembodied voice telling me to do things. Plus! I'd better be doing the right thing, since the game sure as Hell isn't giving me the option to do anything else."

That's the closest thing to an interesting story I found in the game, and it mostly served to remind me that the game has no real motivation or background. I mean, I guess the intro said something about a threat to the world, but why am I following this path? I think maybe the PC is "the chosen one" or some such, but I legit can't remember and I watched the intro twice.\t\t\t\t\t

At-A-Glance:
Game type: Turn-based RPG (with roguelite elements)
Genre(s): Fantasy
Art: ASCII
Length: 3-5 hours for the main game (assuming you don't die too much)
Pace: Medium-paced
Tags: Amazing art, quirky, great soundtrack
Computer requirements: Very low-end


Notes:
  1. I am firmly split down the middle on this game. Parts of it are awesome, parts of it are not. I would give this a mixed recommendation if possible, but given the price and the indie nature of the developer, I edge out ever so slightly into positive.
  2. As you can see by the thumbnails, this game is entirely - and I mean entirely - in ASCII art. That's extremely impressive - even more so because the art is really damn good! This is easily some of the most well-rendered ASCII art I've seen in a game at all, and there's a ton of different enemies, weapons, and encounters that are rendered for you to see.
  3. The soundtrack is excellent 8-bit tunes, especially the battle and menu themes. The only one that's a little annoying is the Tavern theme, which clangs along in a way meant to sound like mugs but just sounds tinny.
  4. Storyline is forgettable, and it seems way more like an afterthought than anything else. There's something about a Brotherhood and a Resistance, you at one point fall into a portal and awaken near a cyborg city, there's a line at the end about how demons and monsters have families... but it's all window-dressing. You don't do anything that makes these things feel like they matter. Interestingly, the writing is generally good - the third act definitely has a little horror in it.
  5. The humor is... quirky, to say the least. It's very much taking the air out of normal RPG fanfare. Some "lol random" jokes about potatoes, mostly useless talk with villagers, and some recurring bosses that are trying so hard to make you laugh.
  6. A lot of the quests and progression are simply repeating the same little chores. Blacksmith has a quest for you? Just bring him a mithril ore! Your current progress? Go through the highest level dungeon you can! It kinda makes me feel like this game is more like a rough sketch of an old-school RPG experience than something a bit deeper, which belies just how intricate some of the combat is. There are tons of different status effects, weapon enhancements, etc., but they don't really feel like they mean a lot when you can just focus on your 1-2-3-3 combo.
    [*[ Classic mode has permadeath. This can be frustrating, but there's a non-permadeath and easier mode if that bothers you too much.
  7. Come for the RPG, but stay for the art. The game has deep mechanics but are inefficiently realized, yet I really enjoyed seeing the ASCII spritework and enjoying the music. I beat the third act (which I got the impression was the main game) while rolling a barbarian who focused on STR/DEX, and that's good enough for me to feel satisfied.
