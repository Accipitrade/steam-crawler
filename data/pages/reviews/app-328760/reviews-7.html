\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot this game for less than 1$ and got over 25 hours at the time of this writing.
I recommend this game for people that like RPG and are not feared by old school game play.
I which that the game would offer more forgiving mechanics if you do more risky tasks.
Bosses fights are not rewarding enough compared to the tavern/housing/library/blacksmith rotation.


Pro:
Great retro indie/Gameboy/nes music
Game have story mode that can be replayed at higher difficulties
I like the fact that you can grind for items with random stats and skills points and customizable.

Cons:
Extremely punishing game mechanics. (Pretty much, you die you lose everything or encourage the player to start a new character)
Bosses stats are scaled by you current stats and sometimes with random perks. This could lead you to an expected fight that completely counter your character build and it can be frustrating to lose a character after more than 10 hours or just because a boss mechanic do a dps check or health check without any warning. So expect dying alot on your firsts story runs.
Game resolution is fixed by an artificial 720p resolution to be faithful with ascii aspect ratio. (Not a big issue for me since I can rescale the window)
The game is playable exclusively in console commands, so you must press enter after each choice. This is fun for the first 3 hours but after that it become an hassle. Had to use a software like joytokey to bind scripts with a joystick, which help ALOT.
For Example: In the tavern if you want to serve 180 beers you must press then enter every single time.\t\t\t\t\t
