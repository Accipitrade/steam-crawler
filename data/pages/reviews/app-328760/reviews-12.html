\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this clever twist on a nostalgia trip. It's fun, especially in short bursts, as the free-to-play-inspired repetitive bits can get boring if you sit too long in one session.

Like some other reviewers, I felt there was some unfairness, and not just with bosses, but so far I have found that if I grind enough, I can absorb that. I haven't yet finished the game, so I don't yet know how bad it may possibly get. We'll see! EDIT: finished first run, and last boss was annoying, but manageable. I didn't get to finish all the colosseum/dungeon fights, though.

It would be nice if the artwork was a bit more consistent with location and item type, if there were more enemy varieties, and especially if there was more flavour text in the game, such as location descriptions (reminiscent of old text adventure games), and more updated dialogue with NPCs as you get stronger or progress. Ultimately, the heavy randomness prevents it from being all it could be: a more cohesive and living world.

My extended wish list includes:
- Non-hostile towns to explore
- More professions/ownable locations, and more NPCs to converse with
- More distinct special exploration areas
- More unique artifacts (not necessarily weapons) with unique flavour text / stories attached to them
- More guilds and guild quests

In short, I want a fuller RPG experience. That said, I think this is a great little game just as it is, and recommend supporting it. Perhaps we'll get something even more ambitious the next time round, which I would welcome!\t\t\t\t\t

There's a ton of text to read in regards to your character, perks, stats, and what have you, but that's part of the charm of this kind of game. It's kind of like an offline MUD, but it's easy enough to get into without any prior experience playing a game like this.

The music is amazing, and tunes continually end up stuck in my head (though in a good way). There's a lot of mechanics packed into this little game, including stat allocation, a combo system in combat, various status effects and enemy affixes, crafting, gathering, arena battles, secret areas, and more. There always feels like there's something new to find every playthrough, and there's enough in the way of races and classes to keep you coming back and trying new things.

Definitely worth picking up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOMG YES!!! This was bought way back when because of all the positive reviews. I just started playing it a week ago and it just zapped me on how fun this was. I started feeling like a little kid looking at a text RPG again. However after a couple of hours I felt like I was in the movie The Matrix looking at real time battles through the code.

So this is a rogue-like game in that you can die and stay dead with the character you just had. However there are settings to adjust that so death isn't permanent.

It is stategic in that most battles require you to pay attention and actually watch what the enemy is doing. Figure them out though and you'll be stomping monster butt quick!

It's got mining, salvaging, owning a bar, questing, etc... it's an absolutely amazing game. It's got humor as well which is very welcome after sweating through a few battles. It doesn't have anti-alaising settings or HDR bloom effects... (lol)
but that's some of the charm. You are playing a good RPG with the most basic text effects and it just works so nicely.

Great job to the developer. Kudos on the art, the storyline, and that Matron.... the effect you used, so cool! K, no spoilers!

Grab it if you are into text RPG's, rogue-like RPG's, or games that require full attention in one-on-one combat.

Cheers!\t\t\t\t\t
