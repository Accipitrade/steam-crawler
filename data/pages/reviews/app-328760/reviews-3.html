\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've tried to get into this game a couple times but always end up uninstalling it in the end. Granted I haven't gone through the game at length, but at no point throughout my several brief playthroughs was I ever engaged and wanted to continue playing after a half hour or so of playtime. And this is coming from someone who loves roguelikes. When I first installed TBoI, I was in high school and stayed up past midnight on my first runs because I was so enraptured.

I won't and cannot deny the charm behind the art and the music, it's remarkable, engaging and even refreshing to go back and look at a form of art that requires almost more creativity in its application than many other popular titles in the same genre.

At the end of the day, though, SanctuaryRPG: Black Edition offers gameplay that boils down to pressing keys sequentially and occasionally pressing the same button twice when the enemy begins charging at you. It's numbing to go through, especially given the bloated amount of stat effects and abilities that you're presented with on level or with new equipment. Trying to keep everything down is pointless when you inevitably encounter a combat in which the dice rolls poorly in your favor and you end up dying to a critical shot, despite your calculations. Disregarding that, the game offers very little beyond using your numpad. Sometimes things on screen will allow you to press a button prompt for a special event, but they're always colored and obvious whenever you have the opportunity to do so.

At the end of the day, I find this game to be at best a mindless distraction for up to an hour every few days or so given you don't uninstall it and forget that it lays dormant in your inventory, and at worst a game that throws out "funi haha randum potato" jokes whenever actual creativity nearly every other sentence with a bland and forgettable combat loop that gets old after the first few encounters. Even $8 is too much an asking price for a game with so little to offer as this.\t\t\t\t\t
