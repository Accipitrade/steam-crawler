SUMMARY
It's a rogue-like turn-based RPG with a combo-based combat system. You pick a class, you pick a character face, you're given a back-story, and you start with next to nothing. There's a good amount of content, and because everything is randomized it will give you plenty of replayability, although the combat becomes a bit dry after a few hours, as weapons and armor lack identity, becoming just a raw numbers game rather than having unique modifiers and effects other than the base damage and stat bonuses. I didn't really notice much of a difference between most of the classes, and the way combat works it doesn't matter if you're using sword, axe, polearm, or your fists.

Despite this, there's a decent amount of enemies, a large number of events to unfold, and a modicum of exploration.

OVERALL SCORE
Somewhat recommend

Overall the art is amazing, the music and sound design is phenomenal, the character creation is fun, the humor is intriguing, the leveling and progression is slow but solid, and the items, interactions, quests kept me playing. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, so this game is addictive. Very addictive. Not easy either. You will die. A lot. That said, it's a pretty basic game, in every aspect. It has some interesting humor attached. Definitely worth $8.

EDIT:

Ok, so I'm seeing a lot of the top negative reviews talking about perma-death and not getting anything... And about HP not being relevant....

As for the perma death issue:
You unlock races to create your character from as you beat chapters/sections of the game. You can find upgrade items that you can choose to bring with you into the next playthrough.
If you are wise, you store all your excess silver keys in the tavern, and those stored ones make it to your next playthrough as well.
Also, for survivability, running through areas fast is a good way to die. If you want to survive more, in each area, make use of the difficulty setting in the temple, just don't forget about it. The tools are there to increase survivability, you just need to make use of everything they give you. Strategy maybe? Who'd of thunk it.

Now for tactics, and relevance of the stats..
For those who said HP isn't relevant, apparently they didn't try a barbarian... HP is king stat for them. They get an ability that deals scaling dmg based on their hp. They also heal themselves in % with one of their normal attack sequences..

In all, the game is deep enough and involved enough to make everything in it relevant, given you make the right choices for your class, race, and general build. The complexity of this game runs deep, requires thought/planning, and realization that, while it might be a LOT of "1,2,3,4,1,2,3,4", you have to pay attention and explore all the abililties and options in your character and buildings.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL,DR: 8 dollars much money, wait until daily, but i also have over 100 hours on a purely ASCII game, which is saying a lot

One of my most played games in existence. I bought this game for 99 cents. If you believe in the general enjoyment hour-dollar ratio, then I've certainly gotten my money's worth.

Positives:
- Unique classes
- Customization of runs are endless
- Abundant with potatoes and typical teenage humor
- Sacrifice altar to sacrifice your abundant potatoes
- Story is kind of interesting
- The mad lad developed the images in ASCII art (obviously)
- Run a pub
- Forging system is creative and rewarding
- So much replay-ability
- Hardest mode is quite hard

Negatives:
- Optimum builds are frequently hard to find
- Grinding and progression gets mechanical and predictable
- Abundant with potatoes and teenage humor
- Hardest mode is quite hard

Conclusion: Yeah, I enjoyed every second of it.

There is so much to this game and the developers put a lot of love into this game. It's one of the hidden gems in the Steam market. As a personal bias, I enjoyed a lot of this game and feel like this was the best dollar I've every spent on anything in my life. $8 is a decent amount of money for the content though, so I would wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t
