\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game feels like it got abandoned halfway through early access. The underlying ideas are solid, but the execution is almost uniformly poor across the board. About 80% of the game time is just spent grinding levels/equipment in one way or another (there are quite a few ways to do so). The plot certainly creates intrigue and curiosity in multiple ways but fails provide anything on any of those avenues. A lot of the combat abilities are useless; there are so many abilities that the game has to give you *another ability* to switch between sets (since you would be running out of keyboard buttons), but almost the entire combat system is just standard attacks/repositioning (mapped to 1,2,3) and healing (mapped to 4). A lot of the equipment is also useless, because you don't control what equipment you make/buy (which makes no sense but I understand the rougelite-ish appeal I guess), so if you are a character that benefits specifically from using a sword with dexterity buffs, every other weapon is just gonna get thrown in the garbage. Combining these with the first thing, a huge chunk of the game is just mashing the standard 1-2-3-3 combo until a death sound plays, and mashing Enter and S to salvage all the worthless equipment you didn't want anyway.

If you look in the discussions for this game, you'll see that the devs have in fact abandoned it - or at least, the story updates that were mentioned in the pinned thread from three years ago have not been announced or released. It's a shame, because if the writing was fleshed out a bit and the randomization toned down, it would actually be a pretty solid indie RPG.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery fun, easy to learn, and hard to master. I went through about a half-dozen characters and ~40 hours of my life in order to complete a full run of the story. Having said that, I can see people having more difficulty if they start on a higher difficulty (I played Classic) or have less experience with ARPGs/JRPGs.

The ASCII artwork and the musical scores throughout the game really make it welcoming. It reminds me a bit of majula in dark souls 2.

Storywise and writing, the game doesn't take itself seriously. It has a loose story, but mostly it's here to have fun. There are some excellent bits between the humor, however. It can also be a bit darker than one might expect.

Mechanically, it's in a catagory all it's own. If someone somehow mixed JRPG combat and ARPG combat, this is what I would imagine comes out. It's AWESOME! And deep, and challenging. It CAN be cheesed if you really pay attention, but it's much more rewarding to come up with your own stuff.

Crafting is VERY important the farther in-game you get. Toward the end it almost feels mandatory. It's an interesting minigame and very enjoyable once you unlock all the extra options as your smithing levels up. If you have any sort of competency with the system, crafting will produce 99.9% of all gear worth wearing past mid-way through the story.

It doesn't just have wild-story driven combat, either. There are also the Arena(Gladiator -like), with two modes. One is ranked, one is unranked. My understanding is that in ranked you fight automated (and somewhat empowered) player-characters made by other players. In the ranked, anyway.

It also has randomized dungeons (which essenially means X monsters and a Boss fight for exclusive rewards), as well as running your own tavern. You can solve a puzzle to join a secretive guild which rewards you with.... something. ;)

When it comes to the classes and items.... well, let's just say that if you played Diablo 2, you're going to be recognizing a LOT of item names, and the classes might look a little familiar. At least by theme and name. Even the number, actually. Having said that, it's little more than a nod in that direction, and the creators of this game made the classes (and racial choices! SO MANY!) their own. Very deep, involved, and interesting. You'll spend a lot of time reading the end-game manual before you get settled in with the game.

I have a lot more playing to do myself, but I wanted to shoot this review off as soon as I could accept having 'completed' the game.

A few tidbits -
You cannot reroll character stats or skills. Except Weapon Masteries.
You can get up to level 300. I beat Classic at level 126.
Infinite NG+ cycles with the same character.
Get ready for more potatos than you can handle.
READ. THE. SCREEN.
DON'T. GO. TOO. FAST. IN. COMBAT.
READ. THE. SCREEN.

It's amazing. It's a steal.
