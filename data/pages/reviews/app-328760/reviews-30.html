\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game to experience, despite its shortcomings.

The ASCII theming is beautiful, and well executed in conjunction with the soundtrack.

There is a lot of fine details and complexities built in to the racial/class selection options. Despite all of them having incredibly similar skillsets, all of their nuances lead to significantly different playstyles.

There are so many different options in combat that result in some complex tactical decisions. With the exception of a few mildly brainless builds or if you significantly overwhelm your opponent, rolling over the same combos against each opponent will not work. There are several probability based status effects or opponent skills that you will need to actively respond to or face the consequences.

Now for my biggest gripe. I am a big fan of the rogue-like and rogue-lite genres. Death can be fun, death is a learning experience, etc. However, it's all relative to the amount of grind that it took to take you up to that death. The first dozen hours or so of SanctuaryRPG are full of so many learning opportunities, that all those many hours spent fighting and crafting weren't a grind at all. But after that point, death becomes frustratingly expensive, because of how many dozens of hours the game can span. This in turn, actually encourages even more grind to the point of getting your character over-leveled for a particular area, as a safety barrier to protect from dying all over again. And there will come fights that completely blindside you from where you completely overwhelmed the previous fight, so there's very little available to signal danger to you until it is far too late.

All that said, though, despite my frustrations with the circumstances surrounding death, learning how to play and experiencing SanctuaryRPG for the first time was a great experience, and frankly a steal at $8.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSanctuaryRPG: Black Edition, is a masterpiece in it's own right.

30 minutes into the game and I knew I loved it. It's accessible to anyone, and is a game you can marathon on, or to play if you need to kill a bit of time.

This game takes the text-based rpg genre and revives it through interesting/hilarious dialogue, quick and rewarding combat, INCREDIBLE music, and even some exploration!

A lot of the gripes that people have with this game is that it's a little grindy. I personally don't find that an issue however. The game's quick pace and single button commands make grinding a breeze and even enjoyable at points. Definitely better than any JRPG that you'll ever have to grind for.

10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSanctuary is a throwback to MUDs and Legend of the Green Dragon, you select a "dungeon" location and it rolls an event to happen - 95% of the time it will be a random battle, but there are other events that ask you to interact with something in some way or just finding a random NPC who says something and gives you experience or even finding an extra temporary dungeon to search through or a hidden boss. After you're done with whatever you had to do it will throw you back at the base menu ready to go to town or hit up another dungeon.

The combat system is rather indepth and relies upon constantly paying attention to buffs/debuffs and what your enemy is doing so you can try to set yourself up to counter them, all the while trying to maintain a combo which improves the damage of your ultimate skill. The game rolls out new skills that can allow you to switch up how you fight rather generously over the course of playing your character.

Also, the game will surprise you with it's soundtrack, you just simply aren't expecting it to be as good as it is.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you played any door games in the past, such as Trade Wars, Legend of the Red Dragon, Tele-Arena, and various other games played on BBSes, or later MUDs in the beginning of the internet, then do yourself a favor, and buy this right now, you don't need to read further.

I'm such a person. I played lots of door games, had a BBS myself, and been active on various BBSes for a good number of years, starting with a 2400 baud modem and going all the way up to 56k, then broadband.

I saw SanctuaryRPG suggested to me, and as soon as I saw the store video, saying this was Steam's first ASCII game, and saw the very nice ASCII graphics indeed, I was sold instantly. I didn't looked up the reviews, nor checked the forums, I didn't even expanded the more info on the game. I just bought it.

So when I tried it, I had the biggest surprise of my life... this game is pure genius. Only with a text game you could come up with such a brilliant, deep, very deep, combat system. Yeah, you heard me right, this text game has better combat than all graphic games out there. And yet, you see nothing of the combat itself. I won't spoil it, but it's real good.

Don't be fooled, its no rogue-like in the common sense, you aren't a letter on a grid, and move one square at a time, with enemy moving after you. This is played more like traditional door games. Not MUDs tho. You don't move with directions. The rogue part is the permadeath, tho you can play softcore. But I highly recommend you play the way its intended, classic with permadeath.

The chip tunes soundtrack is very good and totally fit the game.

I'm currently the owner of a tavern, serve drinks to customers, entertain them by smashing potatos on top of my head, amongst various other things, and sometimes they give me a gift, or leave :)

Still reading? Go buy it now!\t\t\t\t\t
