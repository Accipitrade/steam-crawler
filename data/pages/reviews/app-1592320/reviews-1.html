\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe concept alone made the game for me. I'm a die hard lover of Mesoamerican culture and mythology, so being able to fly around and explore a futuristic Tenochtitlan is more than enough to win me over. The mechanics are simple, but fun, and I like to think of Achtli as a half Spiderman - half Hellboy kind of heroine, which is an interesting mix, and it goes beyond the mere aesthetics. The story was very well constructed, and even if the final twist is somewhat obvious to anyone well-versed in Aztec mythology, I still appreciated the delivery and the overall journey.

Achtli is very human, her foundation as a character is clearly established from the beginning and her growth feels authentic. Those who are used to longer, triple A games may say it feels rushed, but in my opinion as a filmmaker, it is well done if you look at it from an Indie perspective, just as with movies. I can't wait to see what Lienzo will develop next. Keep up the good work!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall 7.5/10. TLDR: rough in spots but a fun unique game.

Have you ever wanted to fly around like Genos from One Punch Man, then this is the game for you.

This game is badass but it is tough to describe exactly what this game is... i'll just list the elements: 3D Platformer, Action, Parkour, Flying, Sci-fi+Mystical setting, Mecha, Aztec history, it is all these things. The closest game i can equate the movement to is Cloudbuilt.
This game has you flying around a cyberpunk Aztec city with your mecha arm, punching and exploding enemies mid air and fighting huge bosses. Flying around the city with your Aztec mecha fist is where the game really shines! the Aztec setting is rarely used and this is stylized into a cyberpunk cityscape is a new take ive never seen before. a lot of the flying and fist animations are "DBZ-esque" or Anime inspired, which is cool and adds alot of style to the game. Combat is zelda like but you fight mid-air, with a light attack (x on controller) and a epic heavy fist smash (r-trigger) that can smash through multiple enemies. The huge boss fights have large AOE blasts and some bullet hell elements. The character design is also on point, like the main character. Alot of detail and care given to the controls/flight mechanics. they have a learning curve but are overall good, like you can punch the ground to rebound back into the air. The one "flip" animations while flying could use a little polish but the multitude of badass animations made for flying is crazy and look smooth so im not gonna complain too much. There are a few inside jokes/references to Mulaka (the developer's previous game) like the running animation used before you get the fist is a homage to Mulaka, etc. hair style and cloth customization. there is also a skill tree that improves the mecha fist and story progression unlocks a multitude of abilities like a shield that can slow you down and a ranged slice attack. there is a nice narrative that set up the fights and mecha fist, no real complaints. a decent soundtrack.

My only quibble, if you can call it that, is the game seems to be too optimized for lower end PCs and has fast load times, to achieve this, the city NPCs and textures are economically designed for load speed and are not too detailed, considering you rocket past them at 1000 kph, so they do not need to be extremely detailed so this is totally understandable. However there is one cursed child in green, look out for that guy. lol this is not a game breaking issue whatsoever and does not detract from the gameplay at all, just kinda funny to check out.

Overall 7.5/10. its a stylish game with unique setting and mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this game but with a huge but

A flawed game to its core but not unplayable. The flying, once you get the hang of it is actually pretty fun and liberating. The boss fights, which offer the most engaging gameplay have a bit of a janky zelda quality to them where you have to figure out yoursaelf what to do. It never gets too cryptic but I certainly thought I encountered a glitch during a certein boss fight when I kept going into identical rooms. Turns out I had to look at the signs on the door. The camera sometimes gets a bit in the way while you're flying which can lead to your death when you're trying to evade omnidirectional attacks.

The story is actually not that bad and the character have well written motivatoins for why they do what they do. This isn't where the story falters. Where it does is in the delivery. When you start the game there is like 20 minutes of shot reverse shot dialogue that dumps a lot of exposition on you from the get go. The writing isn't bad but it's not engaging enough to excuse the absense of voice acting or the length.

Speaking of voice acting. The only thing we get is characters grunting. Personally it's a bit confusing because there audiofiles in the game where you hear someone saying no no no or you can hear somenoe cry. It's not like the voice actors seemed inept so why they didn't just go with normal spoken dialogue is beyond me. It also makes the cutscenes drag on way more because half of your senses are left out.

In terms of graphics it's fine. I didn't expect lit 2022 graphics or anything but some models do seem lacking in some areas. Sometimes the light weirdly bounces off someone's skin or surfaces seem untextured. It does havce a bt of a PS2/Gamecube era charm to it but it's not perfect.

All in all if you want to fight some bosses in a semi unique way with a surprisingly decent but poorly delivered narrative this is not the worst way to do that.

Oh and the prog metal soundtrack is a pretty unique choice and is pretty cool so there's that.\t\t\t\t\t

Nope.

In Aztech Forgotten Gods we play as Achtli in a futuristic city with plenty of Aztech artifacts laying around including an ancient energy-powered stone arm that conveniently replaces our prosthetic one and makes us super badass. Bad news, the arm seems to have woken up a couple of old not so friendly Aztech gods that we’re going to have to TKO with big punches.

So, what went wrong? Almost everything.

The story seemed interesting at first and showed signs of potential for a build-up but was hindered by clunky, repetitive immersion-breaking animations. It would have been fine if it was text-only, sadly, with no voice acting at all, characters are simply grunting to show that they are speaking and you can’t help but feel like you were watching a live Tennis game. The premise was interesting, but the writing was disappointing and the dialogues were extremely boring.

The gameplay is also not working. Flying over the top of the city was incredibly fun for a bit until it became slightly boring. The platforming aspect of the game is not necessarily working when you can simply bypass obstacles by flying. I also managed to run through walls, through obstacles, through characters. The flying dynamic was certainly innovative and maybe too much effort was put into developing it rather than building it into a cohesive universe. You will understandably spend most of your time in the air with a depleting energy bar that refills itself whenever you enter into contact with a hard surface.

With that in mind, the game just feels empty, for it is a big city with flying cars, there is barely anyone on the streets. After finding a few NPCs, it is maybe a blessing in disguise as the model used for characters is simply not up to par with 2022 standards. While your own character and graphics, in general, already seem out of place and better fitting an older generation type of games, NPCs are absolutely the very fabric of nightmares (see below).

In-game screenshots of NPCs:
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2776813678

Before managing to get any action in-game, you will have to go through 30 minutes of back and forth quests, with no end in sight that just reinforces all the elements we mentioned above. While we were hoping for fighting to salvage the experience, we were disappointed. The game’s combat is underwhelming, fighting smaller enemies or giant bosses makes no difference, everything is auto-lock and you just have to button mash the X button. There is a skill tree that will allow you to better your character and use more elements in fighting, but it is absolutely useless. The camera also has a mind of its own and will propel you in the wrong direction more often than not.

It is all extremely disappointing as the idea was fresh, the universe is something almost never explored in gaming, the gameplay could have been fantastic but Aztech Forgotten Gods fails at delivering a finished product while asking you for 40$CAD.

PS: While I was streaming the game on Twitch, to help with the review, my really little amount of viewers on top of my Discord convinced me that I should simply refund it. I do not usually do this, but they were right.
I will try the game again, eventually way down the road and hopefully, it will be a better experience at a better price.\t\t\t\t\t
