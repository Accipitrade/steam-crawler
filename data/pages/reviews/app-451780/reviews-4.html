Story ★★★★☆

Main Story
♥
The demon world is being invaded by a giant monster with a trillion hp, and it's up to you to take it down before it destroys your entire kingdom.
♡
Side Characters
♥
You can train, bound with, or gift your overlords who are trying to help you destroy trillion, and will result in different endings depending on who you beat Trillion with.
♡
Trillion: Heart Breaker
♥
Anytime Trillion kills a servant you have bounded with, it's rather depressing, and just further increases the feelings of the stakes every time you face him
♡

Gameplay ★★★★☆

Step Based
♥
Every step you make the enemy also takes one, or gets to use a action, this makes for some rather interesting gameplay where positioning and knowing how to deal most damage before getting out of range is important
♡
Abilities
♥
Every ability has its own area of effect, and big enemies can get hit multiple times by a single ability the more squares you line up on them which is useful to take down very huge hp pool , or you can use abilities that give you movement while attacking so you can dodge out of the way from attacks that would otherwise destroy you
♡

Mechanics ★★★★☆

Bonding Events
♥
There is a variety of different bonding cutscenes per overlord that will greatly increase your affection with them, which can help you unlock one of the several endings
♡
Training
♥
There are several stats that can be increased by how well you do the training
♡
Destruction Progress
♥
The demon realm gets more and more destroyed, for every failure to kill Trillion, which adds a real sense of a threat progressing towards you
♡


Overall - ★★★★☆

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTrillion: God of Destruction is a JRPG with dating sim elements. As Zeabolos it is your duty to train one of the Overlords to fight Trillion in your stead. Failing to do so will lead to the destruction of the underworld and everyone that resides in it.

PROS
- Beautiful art... I mean c'mon you can't get anymore unique than that.
- The game requires you to plan ahead when training an Overlord
- Each of the characters are unique, dialogue is always fresh even tho it can get long winded for longer runs.
- Plenty of endings
- Catchy/beautiful opening
- You feel good for when you defeat Trillion for the first time

CONS
- Is very heavy on Dialogue. You won't get a lot of combat in this game except for the Valley of Swords & Trillion/Mojujin
- With the number of endings, the game can get long winded.
- Final Achievement bugged, fix was doing all endings on a single save rather than trying to cheese by seperating endings.

Overall
I honestly got interested in the game after seeing the artstyle, I didn't care how difficult, how repetitive or how depressing the game was. The moment I got it I was hooked. While the game itself is heavy on dialogue, the characters almost make it unnoticeable because you will either grow to love or dislike certain overlords. While the combat is lacking, I feel as if it actually went with the game as the game wants you to grow on the characters as you are training them. After all that training, all that interacting then you lose that overlord to Trillion. You almost feel that sadnesses ready to hit you like a truck but then you also have a sudden burst of encouragement to not allow the same thing to happen with the next Overlord. This will give you an idea how much I enjoyed this game.
