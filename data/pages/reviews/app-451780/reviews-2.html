This game is focused in resource and time management, so don't come here expecting to be aways fighting. You'll spent most of your time in menus, thinking what will be the best strategy for the inevitable fight that will happen after some weeks pass. It's a small game to finish, but you'll never see everything the game has to offer in just one run, so if you want to see each girl's cutscenes you'll have to replay, let then die or let then win against the big bad. Well, I loved it!

I played it while I was reading it to a friend who translated it for me, and we were fascinated above all by the deep personality of the protagonist, from whom I rescued many of his reflections.

I can say without a doubt that, despite its simple story, once you get attached to the characters you constantly feel the desperation of wanting them to stay alive, and knowing that if you had handled things differently maybe you could have succeeded, this is certainly a great element that makes you want to work harder, to maybe win the next time.

Yes, the mechanics may be a bit repetitive, but this doesn't matter since you just want to improve and improve a little more each time, looking for how to exploit these mechanics to the maximum to try to defeat the God of destruction with the least amount of sacrifices possible.

In summary, this game doesn't offer great gameplay, but it does offer a very interesting story, but only if you let it develop and don't stop playing too soon. If you continue in the story it will start to give you some good plot twists and surprises when you least expect it.

Also the game has an ending for each character as well as 1 good ending, 1 bad ending and 1 for an extra character that I won't go into spoiler ;D

Anyway, if you are willing to play this game I hope you enjoy it as much or more as we did. And who knows, maybe you will get the real ending!

Absolutely loved the character art, Tenpei Sato's music and the overall presentation offering something that I really haven't seen anywhere else. Some top-notch voice acting, the cast of characters was endearing throughout and I ended up loving Levia and Faust the most. Especially when the death dialogue changes depending on if you died with max affection or not. Some of the stuff was down right chilling. The whole game really had an effect on me, I know it has its flaws, but ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ I loved the ride.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt has the charm of Disgaea ... but that's all when it comes to the positives. English voice acting is really good, even though many sections are not voiced-over.

Smaller part of the game are the battle mechanics which are your-step-is-enemy-step scenario found in many roguelikes. There are two battle types you fight in the game: in regular, small and uninspiring dungeons filled with monsters and against the main boss, Trillion. The whole boss fight revolves around avoiding it's wide-range attacks and using range skills to deal as much damage as possible. Movement, camera control and field of vision are atrocious beyond belief.

Bigger part of the game is about no-so-well-hidden grind in form of building relationship between you and your subordinates by reading through looong dialogues. You need to determine how to raise your affection with correct dialogue choices and gift selection. Of course, apart from it you have regular progression via new equipment, items, passive and active abilities - none of these are truly interesting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt feels like CH decided to embrace all of their flaws and, instead of fixing them, develop an entire game around them. Egregious asset recycling? The entire game is one boss fight (though he has multiple forms, so it's really three). A huge variety of skills and stats, 90% of which are utterly useless? The full list is there from the start and you have to figure out which ones are worth grabbing by instinct. Inconsistent tone thanks to gag characters? The story gets darker by removing the more serious characters until the comic relief is all that's left. Hiding the lack of gameplay content beneath enormous amounts of text? You go through an entire girl's route just to get another crack at the one boss fight.

Yet, surprisingly, it actually works pretty well. Only having one enemy whose HP gets worn down with each fight gives a clear sense of progression and considering its remaining health against your remaining forces really makes it feel like you're fighting a war of attrition. Testing out builds to find something that works lets you better your ability to fight with player experience instead of just higher in-game numbers and establishes how big a deal the superboss is. The lengthy training periods full of events get you to become invested in a character even when you know she's destined to be a sacrifice to wear Trillion down, making you want to hold onto the faint glimmer of hope that keeps you from growing dull to the sense of loss. The mood whiplash fits the gameplay well, as you are forced to accept your losses and move on, justifying them with the knowledge that they've put you in a better position and maybe, just maybe you're close enough to finish things this time. Just about every aspect of the game ties into the central idea and brings out what makes it work.

That said, it's still lacking in polish. There are a lot of little things that add to the tedium and the excessive randomness can be frustrating. The combat system is a nice turn-based system where you move and attack on a grid, but Trillion's overwhelming stats mean you need to rely on dodging its telegraphed attacks, so you tend to go straight from almost immediately dying to being nigh untouchable once you've figured the patterns out, at which point victory is just a question of how much damage you can inflict before reaching the time limit. All of your characters have the same options for skills and all of Trillion's forms require the same core set of them, so everyone will end up playing the same once you get things figured out. The story hits its emotional peak two-thirds of the way through before losing steam giving the player more than enough chances. The localization, despite having a more-or-less serious translation attempt, is still of poor quality, wrought with grammatical errors and phrases clunky to the point of nonsense, even in important scenes and skill/item descriptions, and fonts with such extreme kerning problems that they make it hard to tell where words are supposed to end.

If you prefer new ideas to refined ones, this is definitely worth a try. It has plenty of issues with the details, but the concept is solid and it doesn't stray from its vision.\t\t\t\t\t

Starts off interesting enough, put in Compile Heart's standard charming use of generic characters, a few healthy shots of Disgaea, and a twist of Princess Maker, nice.

Then get into the gameplay, Rogue-like + Monster Hunter = Tedious Trash?
THAT IS RIGHT! You too can aimlessly walk around a map as the gratuitously unleashed spongy AF boss monsters that telegraph attacks that will take you several turns to avoid, take 7s each time to finish the animation for, and will unleash obstacles and trash adds that will delay you further and further!
A battle of your spirit - NO, your will -- scratch that, YOUR ABILITY TO STAY AWAKE!
Once you exit these boss battles, be ready to train around as you go through more tedious BS!

Was expecting a comedy game about getting repeatedly squashed by a super-secret-optional-post-game boss that somehow wandered out into the open.
Instead, bought a sleep aid.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is, in principle, a good game:

Pros:
+ vibrantly colored, cute characters
+ standard but good voice acting (in japanese, didn't try english, so I wouldn't know)
+ story is overall fun
+ the central idea of passing on your strengths upon death to the next in line to battle against a main foe is good and adequately realized
+ interesting and simple mix of spreadsheet/raising sim and strategy RPG
+ likeable main character (given the genre, this is a rarity imo and deserves a plus)

Cons:

- If you like a character, you have two choices:
a.) play with a guide from the very beginning (there's a good one called something like "easy mode walkthrough" or somesuch) built around cheesing out the game. This takes away the fun in exploring the strategies in the game though.
b.) be prepared for multiple runs; a single run can take anywhere between 20-40h. If you like one of the three first characters, unless you stumble on the strategy mentioned in the guide (which I won't spoil here), you'll likely need to use aforementioned guide (the guide says that runs like that take 10-15? or so hours, so much faster).
- huge grindfest (this might be a positive for others I realize) unless you only do one run, then it is very reasonable - but taking the above into account, that makes the game rather hit or miss
- even though the story is fun and quirky, each character that dies suffers a horribly painful and slow death (offscreen), while pleading for mercy and losing all hope. Again, this might be a bonus for some people, it's a con for me, especially not one I was expecting given the cute packaging of the game and the overall whimsical exchanges of the characters.

***

Normally I'd give a thumbs up, but it's kind of hit or miss to such a large degree; the quality of the product is definitely very high, but there's too much that can go wrong due to RNG (either in the fight with Trillion or random events) or just lack of player knowledge (which is part of the gameplay: Trillion has many surprises in store). Mitigating these via saving and loading all the time increases the grind levels by a ton.

If you decide to buy the game, don't do what I did: I tried to save my run by employing the strategy in the guide halfway through my run, which was much harder to accomplish and only gave me a small percentage chance to save my favourite of the bunch; if you find yourself in that situation, restart the game from scratch instead: it is *much* faster. Or play your first run to its natural conclusion first.

While the strategy change mid-way did put me in an overall good position in the game, my favourite character ultimately barely didn't make it, and while she did enough damage so that the game would definitely be beaten in ca. 5-10 more hours, I've stopped my (only) run due to the aforementioned cons. I played the game 'cuz of the cute character designs, the whole crying for mercy murderfest just is off-putting (again, all offscreen, but there is voiced dialogue, and its long).

As a disclaimer: I could only play this game during my vacation time, so I've been giving this game a shot for almosr 2 years now. Having to trudge through the game for that long has definitely colored my opinion I feel. On the other hand, since I did play it for that long, that should show that the game does have something to it, so I hope that balances out in fairness.\t\t\t\t\t
