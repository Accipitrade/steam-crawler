\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell worth it. For $10, you can get yourself one of my favorite gaming experiences of 2017, 24 songs, walls, and a short but informative art book.

The OST has some songs that stand out, but I will say I've had it on in the background and I find that as I let the game sit, I find the majority of the tracks fun and relistenable. The most intriguing part of the soundtrack is the second disc, which includes an additional 10 tracks. One of the tracks is an earlier version of a song in the game that was written back in 2010, so it is cool to hear the evolution of it. There is a booklet with information on each of those additional 10 tracks on disc 2.

The walls are fine, but I can't say I would use them.

The artbook is neat, but as I had mentioned, somewhat short. I anticipate there is some history in the development of the game that would be neat to peek into, but you will see early concepts, some neat tricks implemented in the game, and some personal notes from Mr. Salvato himself.

Releasing the game as free-to-play was a bold move, but I think we've seen it has been well-worth the massive exposure for the team. I anxiously await what comes next, but I will say this was what I needed out of a VN. Congrats Team Salvato, you've earned the recognition.\t\t\t\t\t
