\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGames and gaming is one of the art forms which I will never have any qualms about supporting. I own hundreds of games which I have never played, but bought because I wanted to support the developers.

This is not one of those games.

From certain points of view, one may complain that the underlying meaning that the game is trying to convey is a bit simplistic, and could be expanded on even more. Still, the meaning is important enough that the primary goal should be to make the game accessible to as many people as possible without dumbing it down to a point of unrecognizability. From that perspective, I think it strikes a perfect balance (even though I, myself, even after going through every piece of dialogue and ARG I could get hold of, would have liked if it delved even deeper into certain subjects).

From a philosophical standpoint, I hold this game in a very high regard, almost reaching the likes of Planescape: Torment and NieR: Automata. Considering the much smaller scope, length and team of Doki Doki Literature Club, I think this is a tremendous achievement. From that point of view, I cannot in any shape or form motivate why I would NOT give at least some money to the developers in the form of the Fan Pack.

And Dan, if you by chance happen to come across this message, know that there are other people out there who have thought about the exact same things you bring up (not going to spoil anything for anyone else, but you should know what I am talking about), have come to pretty much the same conclusions, and have dedicated their lives to put themselves in a position where they will (hopefully) be able to change the whole world for the better. <3
