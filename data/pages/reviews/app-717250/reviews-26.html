\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was too good of a game to not pay for in some way, shape, or form. This truly was a game that I think no one was expecting. The whole thing was wild from start to finish. The complexity that the developer must've gone through to perfect and capture this game's essence is very apparent and done beautifully.
From the purposeful "glitches" in the game to your very files being corrupted and delted, this game goes above and beyond in terms of entertainment. It doesn't stop there either, the characters themselves are surprisingly deeper than expected (in the end they are still tropes, but I personally appreciated the element of surprise, supposedly, each of them had). The very idea of a game that makes you feel that you aren't in control is something I think everyone has thought about one or twice. "What if there was no savescumming?", "Thank god I can remake my choice.", "Glad that this is only a game." All these thoughts are more or less broken as the player goes through the game, and it's done very smoothly. As gamers, we have full control over our games, we're the players, the ones who control, the gods to these characters. However, we're found powerless in this game and it's wonderful. The dialouge (at least once you reach the parts where the dialouge truly does get interesting, such as the ending where you talk to Monika for an eternity) is actually quite insightful and honest. While this was more personal, it really felt like an eye-opener at times for how the human world can be pereceived.
10/10\t\t\t\t\t
