\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Doki Doki Literature Club Fan Pack is, in essence, a way to "buy" the free game and support Team Salvato. For a bonus content DLC, it includes the standard stuff you'd expect: selections from its soundtrack (15 songs from the game and 10 bonus tracks), a set of wallpapers for your computer or phone, and a PDF file going over the game's conception and development.

The most interesting part of the Fan Pack is the DDLC Concept Art Booklet. Along with images from the visual novel's early development, it covers the process behind making a visual novel and creating its characters. It's very insightful for anyone interested in game development or DDLC's ins and outs. I was surprised at how much I learned about visual novels and their development complexities.

The soundtrack is as good as I remembered it, but the release in the Fan Pack is incomplete. A good chunk of Act 2's distorted songs are absent from this release. It's a strange choice to make, considering the fact that dark songs such as Sayo-nara still made the cut. On the positive side, Disc 2 features some rough versions of songs, variants from Act 1, and other remixes. It isn't a dealbreaker, but it is disappointing to see the official soundtrack missing some songs.

The wallpapers are some CGs from the game along with the main menu's background. Nothing fancy, but they work well. If you wanted to have Monika stare into your soul while you work on a spreadsheet, this will fill that void.

If you greatly enjoyed Doki Doki Literature Club and want to support its developers, then I would recommend buying the Fan Pack. Barring the missing songs, it's a comprehensive and high-quality compilation of DDLC content at a reasonable price.\t\t\t\t\t
