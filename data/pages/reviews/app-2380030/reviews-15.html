\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCons:
The only real downside of this DLC that I see is that while the traits itself are ok the fact that you can stack ship upkeep or ship build cost with multiple leaders leads easily to pretty whack and unbalanced modifiers (got easily to ~ -75% ship build cost as a regular empire).

It takes a bit to get used to the UI changes, but these are not part of this DLC and the update overall. Having or not having this DLC will not fix this if you are annoyed. And some of the new additions here are really helpful in the fleet manager.

I have encountered a few issues yet with a situation not ending properly or weird AI behaviour but these are not tied to this DLC but the game and update itself.

Pros:
Apart from that the DLC brings life into a mechanic that previously was really dull and enhancing your leaders was never something really worth it so I think it very much hits the mark of what it tries to achieve.

The hard leader cap, which is often hated, doesn't feel gamebreaking to me. It encourages planning your sectors and their capitals more which adds some depth to it. Sure the governor traits don't apply on all planets anymore but they previously were largely irrelevant anyway so it isn't like we've lost an important asset here. Having only a limited number of leaders and council positions opens up great immersion and specialisation of your empire, even outside of the empire creation. By having to think about which leaders you do want more of your empire takes an entirely different shape. A shape that you can shift by changing your councillors and leaders later on.
Generals are far more interesting now too, unfortunately ground combat still sucks and is unimportant so they stay an afterthought.

Overall for the game progression there are two big improvements (thanks to the cap) to note:
-Exploration takes longer again for you and the AI, slowing down the early game.
-The leader cap helps smaller empires to stay competetitive and be more viable, previously there never really was a reason to not get that additional colony since its productivity would always outweigh the empire sprawl. Now playing with vassals and focusing inwards with a selected combination of strong leaders might be an actual alternative to outright blobbing.

With that away it certainly wouldn't hurt to have a bit more leader cap as the game progresses. Being forced to get these from ascension perks or traditions makes the other APs and traditions not competitive in comparison. They should all in some extent be viable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA real expansion that adds a lot of solid mechanics changes. Not just a glorified 5 minute mod like some of the others.

I love the addition of the council, shame that democracies don't have full real elections still though, feels a little like a missed opportunity. The DLC also should have overhauled some of the older Origins to better exploit the new mechanics, but the new Origin is interesting enough.

They really need to add a civic that gives you +1 origin now though, as a few of them would combine very nicely. Imagine your heroic ancient leader was also a knight questing for the Toxic God or the Original Clone that your Clone Army was fashioned from?

Most of the complaints seem to come from the changes made in the free patch that came alongside this DLC, not the DLC itself, such as the new leader caps and sector mechanics. The DLC is actually quite solid if you don't mind the new mechanics and really opens up some of the government aspects of the game.

I give this one a 6/10. A couple of missed opportunities, but otherwise good in the context of the new game dynamics. Maybe a smidge overpriced, but a lot of work went into this one, so it is ok.\t\t\t\t\t
