\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo seeing the mixed response to this DLC left me on the fence. Is it worth it, or is it not? I caved and decided to go for it, can't be that bad, can it?

having read through what people like about it, and what people dislike. I was left unsure about if I made the right decision or not. Having now played a few hours of it, one as a Lithoid Empire, another as a Machine, as I was curious how a Gestalt entity played with the new system.

Now I have to say so far in both aspects, I can say this: "It's not bad at all"

When I say this, I mean for me it isn't that bad at all. It'll take some getting used to for sure. But it works for me fine.

Now I will say this, it does change the way you might play a fair bit, and to be frank I like that the game is changing in some aspects, but I also understand why people dislike this DLC, because it has changed in some aspects.

I don't know what it'll be like in the more mid to late game as I haven't gotten that far, there will be aspects that will frustrate me to a degree but at the end of the day, it's just learning to adapt to it. which I feel a lot of people just won't take the time to do.

So TL;DR, Get it if your looking for some new flavour to the current state of Stellaris. If you don't like change, don't get it\t\t\t\t\t

The new leader cap is a bit restrictive, especially if you are used to having 20 science ships from day one, but people are acting like it's unplayable. It's not even a hard cap, you just get a penalty to leader upkeep and experience gain.
And the rest of the features are genuinely good. The new legendary paragons add a level of storytelling to Stellaris I haven't felt since Utopia, and the extra personality all your leaders have is amazing. The council is a great addition as well, and all the new council positions are great to fiddle around with.
The new Emperor of Man inspired origin is fun too. I've only played one game with it so far but it was the first time I have actually induced a civil war in my empire without explicitly intending to do so. My pops aren't just resources anymore: they have opinions! They got mad at me and established a communist society instead. There's definitely a ton of paths to try out with this origin so I'll have to play it more.\t\t\t\t\t
