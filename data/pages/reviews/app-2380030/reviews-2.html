This was always the case; the mechanics simply weren't as defined or visible as they are now. This is a solid update that continues to refine this excellent game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave you ever felt starved for Strategic Resources by the game's steep upkeep on advanced buildings?

Galactic Paragons' Governors fix that.

Have you ever felt that your leaders are somewhat anaemic?

Galactic Paragons fixes that.

Have you ever felt tired of the fact you get two, maybe three agendas that you cycle through non-stop?

Galactic Paragons fixes that.

Are you tired of your 'useless' leaders doing nothing but taking up the leader pool?

Galactic Paragons fixes that.

Why the ♥♥♥♥ does Galactic Paragons 'fix' the problems it helped create or otherwise implement, why does it feel like to be truly efficient at managing your empire now you have to pick Galactic Paragons?

I recommend it, but only because after playing with it from a friend in Multiplayer I suddenly found I couldn't live without those changes. Not having Stealth is an inconvenience afforded to my science ships as they float around and try to do my job, and I can 'manage' without the added envoys of First Contact...

...But the benefits you get from Galactic Paragons is seriously game-changing in an absolutely baffling way. The moment you start stacking up Urbanist or have Councillors with Architectural Interest ranked up sufficiently, it changes the game, it changes the way you play, and it gives you an overhead that previously didn't exist when accounting for strategic resources.

What the ♥♥♥♥, Paradox? I can get living without the Destiny Traits (some of those are absurdist too) and the Legendary Paragons are 'ho-hum' if you stick to particular ethics (All I've had is plant-guy who can terraform planets to gaia worlds at the cost of themselves) but seriously, the ability to pick and choose perks and some of these 'veteran perks' should be BASE GAME.

I recommend it on only one ground; after you've tasted it, you find you cannot live without it. Even Gestalts, who arguably get the worst of the picks, still stand to gain from this DLC from the flexibility it affords their 'leader council' in choosing their picks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good and meaningful expansion and I'd recommend this one alongside Utopia and Federations for newcomers easily. It feels fairly essential, which is a welcome change from their last DLC first contact, which felt like the least essential and most optional DLC yet (outside the species packs).

However as always, a flawed launch has soured people's views on this DLC and that's demonstrated by the mixed reviews. At launch this DLC had a bunch of balance issues and exploits which could make your empire super OP, and the leader cap introduced was too strict for most players. Now that all the post-release updates are out, how does the DLC fare now? Let's go over the features first:

Dynamic leaders: Leaders now gain the option every level up of upgrading a trait they have, or acquiring a new one. There are dozens of new traits to choose from that allow you to make your leaders much more specialized, unique and powerful. A lot of these traits are also very unique, especially for generals. They easily could've just slapped on a bunch more generic modifier bonuses but kudos to the team for giving us both creative and useful bonuses in these traits.

At a certain level you choose one of three classes, which will further specialize the leader (for example for a scientist you can specialize it to become a researcher, surveyor or get better at assist research) and also unlock new unique traits that are specific for that class for you to choose later. Then at level 8 you choose a destiny trait, a very powerful and unique trait.

Overall this system is fantastic and a huge improvement. Upgrading and specializing your leaders is very satisfying and it creates a lot of variety every run because you won't always be presented with the same trait options either. If their goal was to make leaders more impactful, they succeeded.

Unique council roles: The council is a free feature (and a great one) but it's further fleshed out by this expansion. With this expansion, every single civic in the game also unlocks a potential council role that gives a bonus based on the councilors level. This is another fantastic addition that adds further variety to empire customization as every civic is now even more unique and powerful. It also helps differentiate some of the copy-paste megacorp civics because they get unique council role bonuses that are usually focused on trade, which is a nice touch.

Legendary and renowned leaders: I don't have as much experience with this one since I never even ran into a legendary leader, but from what I've seen and heard they're a welcome addition to the game that come with unique mechanics and a surprising amount of story content.

I did run into renowned leaders, of which there are at least one for every single ethic in the game, meaning you'll run into different ones in every run which is great for variety. They are powerful leaders with unique traits and they even occasionally offer cool events as well, which is a nice touch. Overall both of these features are welcome and they seem very fleshed out.

New civics, traditions and origins: These are icing on the cake. Both new tradition trees are great, one focusing on leaders and the other on the council and agendas.

The new civics are also excellent, with many unsurprisingly focusing on leaders but there's some cool ones like crusader spirit and some interesting ones for megacorps like the ability to become a pirate raider. Overall these didn't even have to be included but they clearly put a great deal of effort into them, they are not generic at all.

And finally the new origin is also excellent. It actually had me dealing with interesting decisions and also with internal unrest, which is something Stellaris sorely lacks. Actually caring about happiness, crime and stability for once was so refreshing. There's also multiple different paths and endings so it's very replayable as well. Overall a great story-focused origin and I wish they'd make more like these.

Bugs, balance, and other issues: Overall they've done a decent job patching up exploits and balancing some stuff but if you try really hard or get lucky you can still make some pretty OP leaders and do some other exploit-y things, but it's noticeably improved since launch. I also ran into zero bugs or performance issues.

As for the leader cap, they also made some adjustments. Essentially, if you take all the options that give leader cap you can get 12+ leaders now but you actually have to go this route and choose these things, otherwise you'll still be stuck with a low level cap. Honestly I'm okay with it, because it forces you to choose wisely where you want your leaders. Not every planet can have a governor, and not every fleet can have an admiral, and you might not have as many science ships as before. But again I'm fine with this change, although others may find it frustrating and I get that it's hard to adjust after the way the game has been for so long.

So overall I think the leader cap is just a matter of personal taste.

Overall though great expansion and highly recommended if you're both a new or experienced player.\t\t\t\t\t
