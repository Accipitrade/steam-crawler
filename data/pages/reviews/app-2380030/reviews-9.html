\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGalactic Paragons is, in all honesty, one of the best DLCs Paradox has released for Stellaris. The extra council mechanics, civics features and, most importantly, the new paragons add lots of flavor and make your playthroughs way more interesting.

With Paragons, your leaders can now occupy various different positions in your empire's leading council, based around your government's civics. Leaders themselves have also become substantially more powerful, with the addition of classes, veteran traits and destiny traits. A high-level admiral can now make a huge difference in your fleets' power, while a decent governor can boost your planets' output immensely. It's all about how you use your leader cap, which brings us to what's probably the most important part of the review, the criticism.

Most of the negative reviews are actually regarding the 3.8 "Gemini" update, as opposed to the "Galactic Paragons" DLC. But, even then, I can hardly deem the critics reasonable.

The core concept of both the update and the DLC was to make leaders more important, meaningful and interesting. Yes, you will find yourself unable to procure as many science ships as you used to in previous games. But that's because your new scientists are way better than the ones you're used to. Both the tiered traits and the new classes make leaders extremely powerful if used and built correctly. Hell, the leader cap isn't even a hard cap, and its penalties get less severe the more cap you have.

You've got too many fleets? Just give them a damn admiral with bonuses towards fleet command limit, which can bring it up to 500 . Your scientist isn't surveying fast enough? Lucky you, you can upgrade the surveyor trait, and going down the explorer class increases that scientist's experience gain by 50%.

Looking at it this way, it's like most good Stellaris updates have been, a change to a fundamental system. People just need to get used to it. \t\t\t\t\t

Short Summery. The DLC adds a lot of optional flavor to the game, however people cannot separate the DLC from the 3.8 patch which they have bombarded the review board

Hello, I have been playing the game since launch in 2016; as of time of posting I have over 3,316.6 hours of play. Galactic Paragons itself contains content that can drastically change how people will play the game, I would place it on the same level as Utopia Expansion. Before going forward, I do have to point out that the review section of the DLC on Steam, has had several individuals who cannot separate the DLC from the drastic changes to leaders in the 3.8 patch. Though I can understand their sentiments, I cannot however understand them attacking people through the forums, or review bombing without thinking about the changes is honestly deplorable behavior.

With that out the way, onto the review.

The DLC

Galactic Paragons & the accompanying 3.8 Patch had a singular goal. That goal was to expand the importance of leaders, I.E. make them matter. In the patch a new government system was added, The Empires council. In the game you will have 3 seats to fill, the ruler, minster of defense and the head of research. With the DLC you will gain three attentional seats to your empire, each one accompanying a civic. If you obtain more civic(s) through special circumstances, you will get more council slots. These positions will allow each leader to give the Empire a special bonus depending on the civic. One gripe I have with this is that it kind of makes me want to use portraits that have more then two options. Hopefully the Custodians will add more artwork to these species portraits.

Speaking of civics, the DLC adds several civics. These civics ranges from increasing the rate in which your leaders earn experience to roleplay like civics such as declaring a crusade. More civic options are always nice to have though I feel like were getting to the point in the game that there are so many choices for civics but not enough slots to pick them.

There are two additional tradition trees included in Galactic Paragons. One for improving your leaders and another improving the agendas. Speaking of which, Agendas have been reworked in 3.8. They are now actually useful and a much more pleasant change. Just like civics, I feel like at some point down the road, there may be a system to be implemented to allow us to at least one additional tree.

The heroes, with the rise of the importance of leaders, they have included unique characters and reworked older characters from the other DLCs. They now have unique portraits with set traits. I do have to point that some people are angry over this. I can understand that, but they dont seem to understand the difference between making set characters and having to create literal thousands of art assets to make characters truly unique. Though that would be cool for every species portrait to have that much support, like the Human ones, but it is not possible due to the fact this DLC is sold at $15 and they have to make a return on investment. So, I feel like this is a nice comprise. With this DLC, you will pick what traits your leaders will have with each level up, if you do not have this DLC, the game will act normally and assign random traits to the leaders upon level up.

The Origin, I will not spoil this origin, I will state that if you like Warhammer 40,000, you will apricate this origin. It has several endings and if you have utopia, you can have more decisions. All around a very fun roleplay origin.
All in all, I would recommend this DLC as it adds a lot of flavor and mechanical elements to the game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSeems this DLC has been very divisive, mostly over the leader cap. With my play styles though, I can say I'm very much enjoying this DLC. I think the reason the leader cap exists is to make you have to consider the pros and cons of which you use and you have to make some decisions about which way you want to go and which routes to take. Rather than spamming scientists immediately and allow you to explore every system, study every anomaly, and investigate every archaeology site simultaneously while every research world has an assist research scientist in orbit, you need to make some choices. Developing the leaders is a lot of fun and they get some backstories finally. The leaders are ultimately each more powerful than the old style too, so deciding where to put them to work is half the fun. I haven't even come across any legendary ones yet or tried out the new God Emperor setup, and it's already been very interesting.

You can go over the cap by the way, you just take penalties similar to going over fleet cap, starbase cap, etc. which is another choice you can make.\t\t\t\t\t
