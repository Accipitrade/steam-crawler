\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTitle: A Stellar Evolution of Strategy - Stellaris: Galactic Paragons Review

Rating: ★★★★★

Stellaris: Galactic Paragons is a game-changing expansion that revolutionizes the strategy genre with its innovative leadership and government mechanics. As a devoted player of the Stellaris series, I am thrilled to say that this expansion breathes new life into the game, offering an unprecedented level of depth and immersion.

One of the standout features of Galactic Paragons is the introduction of a robust leadership system. The new mechanics surrounding leaders add a compelling layer of strategy to empire management. Leaders are no longer mere placeholders; they are dynamic individuals with unique skills, traits, and experience levels. Assigning them to specific roles, such as Governors, Scientists, or Admirals, becomes an essential decision that directly impacts your empire's success.

The ability to recruit and manage leaders with specialized abilities opens up a world of strategic possibilities. Whether it's harnessing the expertise of renowned Scientists to advance your technological prowess or deploying skilled Admirals to dominate the stars, the choice of leaders becomes a pivotal aspect of your empire's growth and development.

The leadership system goes hand in hand with the revamped government mechanics in Galactic Paragons. The expansion introduces new government types and policies, allowing for even greater customization and shaping of your empire's identity. Whether you opt for a democratic society, a despotic regime, or something in between, the political landscape becomes a dynamic and engaging aspect of gameplay.

What truly sets Stellaris: Galactic Paragons apart is the intricate interplay between leadership, government, and the galaxy at large. The decisions you make regarding leaders and policies have far-reaching consequences, influencing diplomacy, research, military strength, and overall empire management. The strategic depth and immersive storytelling that arise from these mechanics are truly captivating.

Moreover, the attention to detail in the expansion's implementation of leadership and government mechanics is commendable. The visual representation of leaders, complete with their portraits, traits, and evolving abilities, adds a personal touch and enhances the immersion factor. The depth of the system is matched by an intuitive user interface that makes managing your empire's leadership and governance a seamless and enjoyable experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe idea is nice, but the implementation is extremely poor. In principle, you're now forced to pick where and when you really want a leader instead of just spamming them everywhere possible. Ignore the complaints about not being able to have enough, that's the entire point. It's only a soft cap anyway, and the penalties aren't bad so certainly in the mid to late game you'll be well over it, just as with empire size, navy cap, and others. The problem is that leaders absolutely suck. Instead of having a few, much more impactful leaders, their traits are almost universally much weaker than before. Where you used to be able to find scientists with genius and other useful traits, including specialising in the research you want, now you'll mostly be choosing between +10% experience gain or +4 energy, or other similarly pointless traits. If you really focus on leaders and somehow get your head researcher to max level, they might be about as good as a freshly hired scientist used to be.

Veteran specialisations again sound like a nice idea, but even going all in on leader bonuses experience gain is so slow that you'll hardly see them on anything other than scientists before they die of old age. And even scientists will take something like 35 years to specialise, by which point why would anyone even consider picking the option to get bonus survey speed in a few level's time? Overall, leaders are so weak that having a cap isn't even noticeable; aside from needing a few scientists for early exploring, you can go the whole game never bothering to hire or level up a single leader and won't notice the difference.

One particularly baffling choice is making fleet cap depend on admiral level. The game absolutely cannot handle this. Every time an admiral dies, or if you simply want to move them around, everything falls apart, fleets disintegrate and reinforcements scatter themselves across the galaxy (even more so than usual). It also means you can't ever copy fleet compositions, because any fleet with an admiral must be different from all the rest. Again, it's far easier to just not use admirals at all rather constantly fight the game over them and, again, the bonuses are generally much smaller than before so it rarely matters anyway (multiplayer excepted obviously, where any small edge will be important).

The new origin... exists I guess. Your ruler is supposed to be a bit better but, as with other leaders, there's just not a meaningful difference. And there are a few events, but far less interesting and with very little impact compared to other recent origins. Legendary leaders are OK. The upcoming patch will make them not count to leader cap, so at least you'll be able to try them out without worrying how to fit them in. Their traits are a bit more powerful from the start and come with potentially interesting tradeoffs, so they're generally better than other leaders. You won't see many in a given game though, and they can be very specialised towards a specific playstyle, so you'll often just ignore them anyway. But they are one of the better points of this DLC, and certainly give plenty of scope for modding or expansion in the future.

Aside from leaders, it's not always clear which changes are DLC and which are patch, but they're mostly just poor. There are multiple interface changes that do nothing other than require a couple of extra clicks to get where you want. The menu sidebar didn't fill the height of a screen anyway, and now they've removed a bunch of buttons from it and hidden the menus as tabs in other menus. So every time you want to design a ship or change a policy, you have to remember which different menu to open first. Terrible UI decision that can only have been made by someone who never actually played the game, or really used any mouse interface of any sort. The army builder is almost nice, except that armies constantly turn off the option to send newly built armies to join, and they fly the whole way vulnerable to attack instead of using the existing fleet reinforcement mechanic.

It's a theme that runs through the whole update. The occasional nice idea ruined by poor implementation and annoying interfaces. It's a constant fight to find or do the things you want, and then you wonder why you bothered because none of the new stuff has any meaningful impact on the game at all. Honestly, I wouldn't even bother picking this one up on sale. The patch puts in the leader cap, and you'll be better off with the old traits and passive levelling than faffing around with the DLC additions on top of that. Maybe wait a year or so and see if mods manage to do anything interesting with it.\t\t\t\t\t
