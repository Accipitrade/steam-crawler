\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI own all the DLC, this may be my favorite yet. There is so much more life and personality now.

It seems like the two areas people are objecting to are bugs and the new leader (soft)cap. As far as bugs; I have yet to experience any, and its possible that many others are bug free too and happily playing rather than leaving reviews. As far as leader cap, I think its an excellent exercise in a 'less is more' philosophy.

Yes, I too used to have a leader for everything, you had to; but I can't recall the last time caring when any of them ever died, or ever seriously considered a +leader build. Now? I know their names. I groan when one dies to an anomaly, I declare vendetta when my neighbor kills one before we make first contact. who was there before the Stellaris release, watching the gameplay streams and remembers Mercedes Romero? THAT's what I feel like with Galactic Paragons.

What people forget about the leader cap is that 1) you can still go over it and 2) The game needs less leaders now: You only need 1 for your research (not 3), Admirals increase the fleet cap as they level (so less fleets). and to a lesser degree, fewer governors are needed since you have better control over sectors. True, you may need to wait a little bit for your leader pool to refresh if you've had some bad luck, but honestly, I believe the game is much healthier when you can't just reroll infinitely in order to min/max, and instead are given interesting decisions as you make do with less than optimal leaders. Because leaders matter, I am playing the game with builds I would never have considered before.

In short, its a breath of fresh air for role/single players, and a great direction for the game; and I look forward to what they can do with this new system going forward.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI actually really like this one. The new leader system is amazing and a major improvement over the previous one. Now instead of needing 20+ scientists with different specializations and a ton of governors, you only need one. As for normal leaders, they all have more info tied to them, such as their home world. When they level up you get a choice between slotting a new perk or levelling up an existing one, allowing you to customize all your leaders. it's a really good system and a massive improvement over what came before.

The paragons are probably the best new feature. Basically legendary, established characters that will contact and join you based on your ethics. These guys are INSANE, and the benefits they provide are borderline game breaking. Some of them have stories attached to them, but most just function as normal leaders.

The council is decent, but mainly serves as just extra flavoring. There aren't really too many interesting mechanics tied to it. You can set agendas, which give temporary bonuses once they're done charging up. Your councillors will also level up, and each can be assigned a unique role based on your ethics and civics, with each role giving some kind of bonus. I would have liked some more events/story tied to this feature, but gameplay wise it's alright.

The only major issue with this expansion is the leader cap. It makes sense to have one, since leaders are much stronger now than they were before, but the default leader amount is waaaay too small. Bump the cap up from 6 to 10 and things will be so much nicer.\t\t\t\t\t
