\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start by saying I've only looked at stuff and haven't played (it's been out for only an hour) and I wouldn't recommend AGAINST buying this DLC if I could (Unfortunately there is no neutral review rating...)

But, the description on Steam says "Hundreds of new traits" Sounds great, right? It's a major part of why I decided to buy it.

-I load in, and the DLC description says "140 new traits"
This is straight up false advertisement... "hundreds" is not 140, it's 200+. Not a big deal but... lame.

Now, you might think "But Peratus, 'hundreds' technically means 100-999!" And sure, you aren't wrong. But here are my counter points: 1. If I told you I had "hundreds of dollars" what number would appear in your head? Sure ain't 100 or 140. It's more like 200, 300 or more. 2. Paradox knows this, and that's why they used the exact number, 140, in game, but "hundreds" on this page.

- I load in as robots, they have a whopping 3... 3 agendas at the start. 3. Wow!

- This one may be me not understanding correctly, but there is a position/agenda (Already forgot which) that gives you researched upkeep. But... there is only 1 researcher, in the government. There are no more researches for each tech type. Is this an oversight on Paradox or me? I don't know.

- The namesake of the DLC, the Paragons. There are 4. A whole 4! Come on Paradox. These 'Legendary Leaders' are supposed to be very interesting. Why are there only 4? There should be like 16-20 at least.

Anyway, once again Robots are completely devoid of any attention. Seems like for years now you're just supposed to play organics or not play at all.

As for good stuff:

The new government system is pretty cool, a small bit lacking though. The 'hundreds' of new traits seem cool and interesting. Agendas are cool but also a bit lacking. Especially if you aren't organic.

Overall I'd say the additions of this DLC are quite good. The things missing however, bring it down. I will update this as I play more...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't had time to get too far into a game yet, but I can immediately see a positive difference in how we manage our empire. This DLC influences gameplay mechanics from multiple other DLC and makes leaders more likely to fill a role we want by letting us choose 1 of 3 traits when they level. There are also some pretty cool new leader traits and civics to choose from. So far, this DLC is looking pretty good. Good job to the dev team which worked on this.

There are some negative changes I'm not too thrilled with. Admiral level now affects fleet capacity. At first glance, this seems terrible later in the game if we have to juggle our fleets every time an admiral gets elected or dies. They also added a leader cap, which is very restrictive. A lot of positions will go unfilled unless you're willing to eat the experience and unity penalty. This is exacerbated by governments which elect leaders, as those leaders will leave their previous post empty. Before, we could just juggle leaders, but now the leader cap and the increased importance of our veteran leaders makes this a somewhat aggravating change. It's bad enough that I wouldn't be surprised if a hotfix or the next patch increases the leader cap substantially.

EDIT: There's at least one mod in the steam workshop which increases the leader cap for those who hate it.

I'm also not sure why they made governors per planet instead of per sector now, especially with the extremely limiting leader cap.\t\t\t\t\t
