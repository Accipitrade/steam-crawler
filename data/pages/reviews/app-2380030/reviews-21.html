\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStellaris is one of my favourite all time games, I currently have over 5000+ hours in the game. That being said...

This DLC is currently a buggy mess. Avoid in it's current state until there is a hotfix/update to it.

Current Bugs encountered (And yes, they have been reported to Paradox...)

- Science ship orders cancel themselves randomly so you are unable to queue anything. They will even cancel singular orders. This also happens with the auto-explore feature making it frustrating to do the basic first steps of a new game almost impossible. So much so I have stopped playing until this is fixed.

- Leaders vanishing from the leader pool. I had a scientist vanish from their ship with no pop-up or notification to link it to an in game action (such as the new retirement feature). This happened twice.

Pros:
- The new UI is wonderful. So much love and effort has gone into this that you can see the Devs have really been thinking about saving time, stopping notification spam. Thanks for this Devs!
(No, I do not care that a one planet empire has entered into a open boarders agreement with another random empire - nor that the militarist empire has just cancelled all its agreements with the neighbouring xenophiles for the 12th time in five minutes.)

- The new Leaders. They add a amazing new dimension to the game and I also can't wait for the mods to stop rolling out with the player created content

- Leaders have backstories - Although this is only a short sentence, it makes the galaxy feel more alive. Knowing your admiral was a fighter pilot born on your homeworld before leading your fleet is just so sweet. Sweet, Sweet RPG elements *Chefs Kiss*

- The new council feature is amazing. I love the ability to select the people guiding my Empire through the galaxy and how you are now able to set priorities for them. Allowing you to buff your empire with additional upgrades.

- Art Work - It's pretty!

- Hotfix is already out for some of the issues with this DLC. (yay fixes!)

Cons:
- Hotfix is already out for some of the issues with this DLC. (boo shipped with bugs!)

- The leader cap - lets talk about the galactic sized elephant in the proverbial nebula. The leadership cap is a huge step back in allowing players to expand at the speed they would like. An example; I like to spam out 6-7 science ships in the early game and have them B-line to choke points; while one or two other vessels stay back and research and excavate sites. This is no longer possible with the limit in leaders.

I can see what Paradox was attempt to do here, with the new features and functions leaders are more powerful so, in theory you need less right? Incorrect. This limit is very restricting to differing game play types and also runs RPG elements in the game.

- Buggy DLC (AGAIN?!) - This is not meant as a "bashing" comment to Paradox. But there are only so many times you can get excited for a DLC, purchase it and install to learn that it is virtually unplayable. If it were some more niche bugs which, of course, happen and cannot be weeded out by a QA team. It wouldn't bother me - Such bugs were present in the First Contact DLC but it didn't introduce any huge game play breaking issues. But from the bug reports and my own experiences it does feel like this was released with either full knowledge of some of these issues or they were missed (We are talking basic early game bugs here!) and I'm not sure which is worse?

That said, it would take more than a buggy product and frustration to stop me playing Stellaris. It is a product made and maintained with LOVE by the development team and they have crafted an amazing product.

In short though, I will be rolling back to the previous version and disabling this DLC until it is fixed. I would not recommend this as it is at the moment, wait.

Once the issues listed are addressed in planned upcoming patched and hotfixes I'll provide a deeper review after more play time with this new DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom what little I've been able to see of the new content it looks like it will be solid but my game performance has dropped significantly since installing the new expansion so I haven't actually been able to play much of it at all. To be fair, I'm playing on a 2015 Macbook Pro so I wasn't playing on ultra settings to begin with but it worked well on medium settings and that's all that mattered. Now I can barely open a menu without the game locking up and it's not like they changed the game engine or overhauled the graphics. Something with this expansion clearly wasn't optimized properly.

UPDATE: Dropped everything down to low and was able to actually get some game time in. Overall I like the changes they made to Leaders and the addition of the Council mechanics. Also the Tech menu finally has a hotkey (about ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ time). I'm leaving my recommendation as is since the performance drop was significant and unexpected for what this expansion contains. Honestly, I'd give it a 'meh' review if that was an option.\t\t\t\t\t
