\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIMHO, this is the best DLC released for Stellaris in a very long time. The new leaders system was a much needed upgrade and adds a new layer of depth to the game. It's not perfect, but it's a huge step in the right direction and I can't wait to see what the modding community does with this new system. The possibilities seem pretty exciting to me.

I really don't understand the negative reviews except for the fact that this update and DLC definitely has a huge impact on gameplay. If you just want to play the game the way you always have, then you probably are not going to be happy with this update. Building up your council with powerful leaders will now be a top priority. It would definitely be nice to have a higher cap on the number of leaders you can have, but that just adds to the strategy of managing the few that you do have. No longer can you rush out a fleet of science ships at the start, and not every planet will have a governor, and not every fleet will have an admiral, and not every army will have a general. It will be up to you to prioritize your resources, but if you don't want to deal with that, then there are already plenty of mods out there to increase the leader cap to your liking.

But as much as I like the new leaders, I think the biggest step forward with this update and DLC is the improvement in the early pacing of the game. For me, the beginning of the game is a mad rush to explore the universe and expand my empire to control valuable systems and vital choke points. In the past, I would quickly accomplish this and then be left with little else to do while my technology progressed and I was able to slowly develop my planets and improve my fleets and diplomatic weight. Okay, I still do the exact same thing, but the exploration aspect lasts a little longer into the game and doesn't feel quite as rushed. In other words, because it also takes the AI longer to explore and expand, I can enjoy this aspect of the game without the mad rush in the beginning and then the waiting around for stuff to happen part as the game goes on. It doesn't fix all of the pacing issues with this game, but it's a step in the right direction and shows that the developers are working to fix the problem.

As always, you should probably wait on the sale before buying this. As usual, I didn't take my own advice and with a kick from this new DLC I'm heading for 1000 total hours on Stellaris. Mom would be proud.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are certainly some commendable aspects here, and the initial experiences are enjoyable. However, as the novelty wears off, the game reveals itself to be a rather cumbersome management hell, causing one to question whether it truly enhances the gaming experience.

There's a sense of the game being half-baked. The imposed hero limit, rather than adding to the fun, detracts from the gameplay. The restriction prevents full utilization of the new features, leaving you battling against an unduly restrictive hero cap, which often makes it challenging to afford a general.

As for the hero events, they're largely uninspiring. I ended up with a hero that doubled my fleet power, allowing me to easily overpower some nearby opponents on GA. However, I remain uncertain about the intended balance in this aspect of the game.

End of the day, I am going to disable this, as it simply makes the game less fun.\t\t\t\t\t
