Galactic Paragons
Galactic Paragons is a DLC that focuses on the leaders of our empire, giving them new roles, attributes, and personal stories. Along with the DLC we get the mechanics of managing the Empire by a galactic council, new edicts, policies, one new origin, and some new civics. Along with the DLC, improvements have also been added that significantly improve the logistical nightmare of managing our empire.
Rulers, Paragons and Galactic Council
Finally, our leaders are given more opportunities to achieve something in our empire. They were given brief descriptions of their current profession and a brief description of their past, making it possible to get attached to them. The system of gaining experience by them has also changed, now with each new level we can choose their attributes ourselves, which makes it much easier to organize, and there are now a lot of these attributes, so there is a lot to choose from. Coming to the galactic council. In addition to better segregation of edicts and policies, we can now activate powerful bonuses scaled by the number and skills of the council (we have 5 possible council members, we start the game with 3). Now there's a change where we have less available leaders, but they can do a lot more, so no longer we have to drown ourselves in a sea of boring clones. And as for the Paragons, i.e. special heroes that appear in various places of the galaxy, the concept is simple, each of these heroes has original stories, missions, skills and views, so depending on how we play, there will always be someone new joining us. Not to mention that their original models are great and with a large dose of details. The only thing I don't quite like about all these new mechanics is the fact that there is no special system for authoritarian Empires, where instead of a council we could have had a dynasty/family tree, giving us an experience similar to that of historical Total War games. And I almost forgot, the new origin is an origin in which our devastated race is united by one hero who brings many bonuses to himself and to our empire. Basically a calmer version of the God Emperor of Mankind from Warhammer 40k. I hope in the future we will have complete freedom to create Empires very similar to those from Warhammer, Star Wars, Mass Effect, etc. I think a lot of new people would be interested in the game if it was such an open sanbox. Maybe in the future.
Quality of Life Changes
From what I noticed, some textures have been upgraded and the images have been slightly changed, giving them better quality and more colorful colors. There have been a few changes to the empire editor itself that spells out what we want to do with it a little cleaner, along with a much better leader creation panel. A new co-op mode has been added. During the gameplay itself, we will see a significant improvement to sector management, now we can set the boundaries, borders and affiliation of sectors. There are also significant improvements for exploration ships, now auto-exploration allows us to choose options that make these ships completely independent, doing all possible activities on the way, so we don't have to deal with all this manually anymore. And the land army system has also been slightly updated, now we can set recruitment points on space stations that will start recruiting soldiers from each planet in a given sector.
Summary
Very good DLC overall, and straight to the point. It's a shame that with Warhammer 40k origin we didn't get the Imperial Hierarchy/Dynasty system, but I hope it will be added in the near future. At least with each DLC, our potential for role-playing scenes from the Star Wars Prequels increases exponentially.

Leaders consume a lot of unity and it makes it impossible to take traditions at all if you have just 2 science ships.

Imagine, you have an interstellar empire and you have: 1 emperor, 1 governor, 1 scientist, 1 admiral, 1 general and then you can either build a second fleet or colonize one more planet because you can have only 6 leaders. Total garbage.

It makes no sense at all.

Barely playable. It is the first time I NOT recommend a dlc for Stellaris. Though every lately dlc was garbage, this one is total garbage.\t\t\t\t\t
