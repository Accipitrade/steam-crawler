\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate 3.8, that was released together with Paragons, brought some new game mechanics. While the changes to leaders seems not to be too well received by players who only own the base game, I'd like to write a few words about them from the perspective of someone who bought the Paragons DLC.

The biggest, and looking at the forums, most controversial change with Paragons is the leader cap. It's a soft cap, below which the addition of another leader does not negatively impact the leaders, but above that cap any newly added leader increases the upkeep, and reduces the experience gain of all leaders.
This cap makes the early game far more interesting. Instead of just spamming scientists to explore the galaxy in a more or less planless fashin, one now has to carefully plan the order in which one surveys systems. Technologies that weren't really important previously like hyperlane detection range are now nice to have in the early game, because they make surveying the galaxy less of a gamble.
Similarly, one now needs to plan sectors even more carefully. Where previously one would just hire yet another random faceless governor, one now needs to aim to have as many systems in a single sector as possible. This is true even though the governor's skills usually only apply to the sector capital - the level boni still apply to the whole sector.

Overall, the new mechanics make the decision between building wide or building tall an even more interesting one, with building wide no longer being an automatic "I win" card against the AI.

The paragons themselves are a mixed bag. Some of them are really boring, but others have their own interesting event chains. Some previous events have been reworked to grant you a paragon instead of just a faceless empire bonus, what also makes them feel more alive.

Oh, and last, but by far not least: With the new events, paragons, etc. the galaxy really starts feeling alive. There is no time for boredom during the early- and mid-game any more. To the contrary, one quite often has to pause in order to deal with all the things that happen at once.

I really enjoy the DLC. Ever since it came out I didn't play much else, though I hadn't touched Stellaris in months before... I'm now on my third play-through with Paragons, and already planning the empire I want to play in my fourth.

tl;dr:
Leader cap is awesome, new events are cool, and DLC is worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t
