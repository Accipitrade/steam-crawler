Too bad there's not a "Leader Exchange Agreement". As it is, the only way to hire someone else's leaders is with a Migration Treaty, which not everyone wants. Leader Exchange Agreements would let me roleplay as The Academy from Endless Space, which would be a lot of fun. Having leaders that start out as strong as most nations' leaders that have 20 years of experience is quite powerful, especially when you get traits to stack like Shipwright, Retired Fleet Officer, etc.

I do think that the leader cap is either too low, or leaders aren't strong enough. They're already quite strong now, don't get me wrong, but the cap for how strong they are is quite low for most empires. Basically the only way the leader cap is "just right" is for a leader focused empire who, by definition, has stronger leaders than other nations (and tends to have a higher starting leader cap, anyways).

But overall, I highly recommend this DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty much everything about this DLC is ambitious and high-quality; I'd say more so than any other Stellaris DLC thus far, in fact. The empire specialisation potential -- or build/playthrough variety -- offered by these new leaders is immense. But these changes also introduce a "very significant" shift in the game's meta-balance. To put it succinctly, this leader system and its leader cap inherently favour smaller, "taller" empires. Larger, more spread-out empires, or ones with a lot of empty space to expand into, will find themselves at a minor to moderate relative disadvantage. There are only so many leader slots to go around for your science ships and sectors. But it's actually not a big deal. There are ways to get around it and the costs are easily ignored or circumvented. People in this reviews section are getting way too salty and dramatic about it. Skill issue.

On the face of it, expect exploration to be slower and for some of the sectors of a large empire to lack the (now potentially very substantial) economic bonuses provided by governors. Of course, you can also just go over the cap. It's an option, one which too many people seem to be ignoring. The leader unity cost and xp gain penalties of doing so don't automatically outweigh the potential value of quicker surveying of everything around you early on, and later on there's at least one way to give all your leaders levels irrespective of their xp gain penalties, up to a point.

I don't see the current leader cap limitation to be significant except on galaxies with a low number of empires relative to size/stars. In large-scale multiplayer or otherwise reasonably dense galaxies, the cap will likely never have a meaningful impact. Go one or two over the cap for your extra science ships if you like, it's not a big deal. Fire the excess scientists if you need to once you've mapped out your local space and hit your neighbour's borders. Not enough governors for your sprawling empire? Tragic. I'm sure you'll be able to cry yourself to sleep with the consolation of probably having by far the largest economy and fleet at the point where this becomes an issue. Bluntly speaking, being bigger will still be better, even if smaller empires now have the nicety of keeping governor bonuses on all of their planets. Big competitors have the same leader cap issue, except players are actually capable of working around it and maximising the economic output of their worlds in ways the AI cannot. The "limitations" will be gamed by competent players resettling pops to worlds under governors, obviating most of the lost economic potential of conquered or fringe sectors without governors. It's trivial.

The big standing question is how the multiplayer meta will settle around the inevitable governor limits, and the extent to which the economic performance will vary between a good player on a small empire with full governor/leader coverage and a good player on a large empire with limited governor/leader coverage. I could see a shift back to certain hyper-tall origins and builds happening, depending. Who knows.

Maybe the great shaytan Paradox will throw the oppressed purchasers of this DLC a bone and increase the cap by 2 or something, and restore their ability to feel human. It would be fine. Leaders are cool now.\t\t\t\t\t
