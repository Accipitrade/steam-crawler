\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm around 10 hours into my first playthrough of this expansion and I have to say, I quite like it, and I don't particularly see that changing. The condensing of the leader system into 5-12 individuals rather than upwards of two dozen really ensures that you focus on tending to and nurturing these individual leaders, and makes the process of selecting your priorities a lot more weighty. Sure, you won't be able to do everything at once like you were able to before (e.g. you can't field three fleets and five science vessels and also have governors in every sector *and* have two armies standing in reserve), but I think this is actually a boon.

One, it forces you to actually play to your strengths or shore up your weaknesses, not just go all in on everything. Sure, you 'can' try and do it all at once, but it comes with understandable penalties, as makes sense when you're dividing your attention up so much. I like having to make decisions like which of my planets are worth the cost of hosting a governor, and it made me seriously consider how to balance my leaders throughout the game - for this one, I've only had the one general, and that only because it was a Renowned Paragon who showed up to help out. Otherwise, I'd have probably further optimized my pool, since I wasn't conquering a bunch. In a game before this patch, I'd have an army on standby because the costs were minuscule. I think the pool is rather reasonable in size - it doesn't give you so much freedom that you can ever forget about that ceiling, but I've yet to have any particularly pressing need to go more than one above it. I have no idea if it's easier or harder now to go wide, as some of the bonuses you can get to scientists, for example, can really make you zoom about in your surveying, but you have fewer. My guess is that playing wide would understandably leave you stretched thin in a lot of other ways, which might suck if you're used to being able to field whatever you want, but at the same time, that's what's *supposed* to happen when you go wide. It should never be easy, no problem to do, or that would just be the logically ideal way to play.

Second, I like that my empire has some faces now. Leaders were less personable than planets before this patch, and I had virtually no say in how they developed. I like having control over what traits to prioritize, and I like being able to staff a council full of the best of my best. Leaders are tangible things now, not just a cog I plug in and forget. You never forget about your leaders, what they're doing, and how you can move them around to further optimize your stuff now. That's an improvement, I would say.

And third, I'll definitely be experimenting with different builds now. I went rather generalist this time around, but I have to wonder what it might be like to go full military with the council, or full science, things I've never been inclined to do before, because it's often felt like in order to do so, I'd have to deliberately ignore the other elements of the game. Something that makes me consider new ways of playing the game is a boon in my book.

For criticisms, I do have a few. Thankfully I haven't had many issues with UI or the cursor issues people have reported, but if I did that would probably be a bit of a bugger. I will say that this update REALLY emphasizes how poor the non-human species appearance variety is in Stellaris. Unless you're playing a human race, with its varying skin tones, hair styles, clothing options, phenotypes, etc., you really come to realize how repetitive your aliens look - most have four color variations and like six outfits, if that, meaning you get anywhere from four to around 20 combinations of appearance. This meant that my council, with the exception of the paragons I hired, was basically the same looking individual with maybe some variation in color. I hope that, with leaders becoming so important with this patch, sometime soon in the future we get more art assets to vary up the appearances. It was fine before when you plug-and-forgot your leaders, but now that you're spending so much time looking at them, it's very glaring.

And of course, gestalts were just kinda left behind this patch. We were warned about it every step of the way so I'm not really that mad, it's just disappointing. Hoping they get some love sometime soon - I love my hive minds, but I don't think I'll be playing them much for a fair while.

Solid expansion, worth the money I would say. If you're on the fence, wait for a sale and definitely pick it up then - I can't see a world where you wouldn't want this in some shape or form, it just comes down to how much of a priority it is for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright so I've actually changed my opinion on this expansion since I made this review, so I'm rewriting it. (also because a lot of information was incorrect, sorry!)

Galactic Paragons itself is a perfectly fine DLC, adding some cool features that aren't strictly necessary, but are fun to play around with for a while. Worth the price? Not in my opinion, but that's most of the ♥♥♥♥ Paradox makes, so its nothing you aren't used to by now if you've bought DLC from them before. Some people (myself included) probably are confusing a lot of features added in the 3.8 patch for stuff added in Paragons, seeing as a lot of things that you would think are exclusive to Paragons were just added in 3.8

For instance: the leader cap, governor changes, the council, and all changes to the UI, are from 3.8. Paragons just expands on the council by giving you access to unique council roles, a couple tradition trees, a new origin, a ♥♥♥♥ ton of new traits for leaders, and the ability to level up your leaders and pick traits for them. That (and the special Renowned leaders that the expansion is named for) is just about all that this has to offer for you.

Its kind of a mixed bag for me, since it doesn't offer a great deal of content, but there's also nothing really wrong with it. Most of the issues I thought I had with it were just a part of 3.8 so I guess I recommend it.




Original Review below* (I updated it once)




This DLC introduces a lot of really cool ideas that I think are good, but the execution isn't the greatest.

The council is useful, a cool idea, and is executed relatively well. The fact that a single scientist is able to run all 3 research fields is a change that I honestly didn't know I needed.

Now instead of only needing 1 governor per sector to apply that governor's traits, you need one governor per planet to apply them. Unless that governor is on your council, in which case, that governor's traits apply to ALL your colonies, which is cool imo. It makes more sense that its 1 per planet, since managing an entire planet is hard enough, let alone 6 or 7.

But the big deal breaker is the leader cap (which was added in the 3.8 Gemini update, NOT with this DLC. Although it does still detract from it imo). I can sort of understand it from a balance perspective, but only sort of. If you want to do a bunch of exploration right away like you pretty much had to before, you have to invest all of your leader slots (6 by default) into scientists. Your head of research can operate a ship without stopping research or being removed from your council, but that's just 1 science ship, when usually you would have like 5 or 6 science ships out there finding stuff.

So investing in early exploration basically means that you're investing in Tech for most of the game. If you choose to instead focus on military or economy, then you're left with 1 or 2 science ships to do any exploration, meaning less systems, less resources, and generally being in a worse position than if you just hired a bunch of scientists to staff science ships.

By the end game you might have around ~10 or so leader slots, which isn't much better. You'll likely be done with exploration entirely though, only keeping your head of research in a science ship just in case, so you effectively have more space to do whatever. Honestly, I don't think the leader cap RUINS the game, but I definitely think it needs an adjustment, because you're seriously limited in early game, and even late game if you want to take advantage of governor traits for your colonies. An increase of 3 or 4, or even just increasing the amount of slots you get from certain traditions or ascension perks would be a massive positive.

I would like to think that Paradox will see the discourse about the leader cap and change it accordingly, but I won't get my hopes up. As of right now, I wouldn't recommend this expansion since you can't really do everything that it promises in full,
but I'll change my review if it gets updated.\t\t\t\t\t
