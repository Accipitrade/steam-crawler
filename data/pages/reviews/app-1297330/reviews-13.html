\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been waiting for a game like this a long time.

Movement feels incredible by making every motion create many effects and the leap requires skill to recover from or you'll be laying flat on your back.

Combat is punchy and versatile. You can swap between weapon types mid-combo and fling yourself across the room for a better position. The impacts are weight and super satisfying.

The world design itself incorporates the foreground, background, mid-ground, and characters that react to you in a way I would almost describe as cinematic. There's an immense feeling of presence within the first few areas that I never knew how badly so many games are missing.

The artwork is where I would be most critical. Different sized pixels are occasionally mixed together which never ceases to bother the perfectionist in me and makes pixel art seem more like a limitation than design choice but for some reason I want to forgive it because the smoothness and control of the movement wouldn't be possible without fudging the pixel precision.

Maybe it's the muted palette or color based atmosphere of the areas that bring it all together along with the music and sheer amount of unique effects (Wind blowing, leaves falling, light-rays, clouds, fog, etc.)

You're not allowed to skip anything at all which can make you feel a bit like you're on a leash sometimes but it hasn't forced any unreasonably long cut-scenes or anything unforgivably bad. The font isn't pixelated which starkly contrasts the art-style and breaks the rule but it is far more readable because of that.

The gameplay being the primary focus and having the narrative woven into the world while giving impact to the moments you spend simply walking or avoiding traps by looking ahead makes exploration a joy rather than a chore- which I'm convinced most AAA developers may not be capable of learning at this point!

The sheer and obvious amount of work, love, care, time, and inspiration put into this game is apparent within the first hour. You'd be hard pressed to find another game that feels quite like this one.

Don't pass it up without at least trying the demo first.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I'm admittedly not too far into the game but I'd like to share some initial impressions:

Incredible atmosphere in this game, the top notch sound design in this title caught me by surprise as I didn't have such high expectations on that front. I've been playing this on my TV with a full receiver setup and while this game doesn't offer surround sound (no surprises there), the stereo quality is excellent with a lot of lovely sound effects and music that really pulled me into the game. In my experience, it's somewhat rare for an indie title to flex in this area, so it's a nice treat.

Presentation is also really good, no menu overload here to get lost in. The overall world design appears to contain a central hub area with different merchants etc.. that you can unlock and upgrade via collectibles/currency but I won't dive too far into that to keep the surprises alive for anyone interested in playing. Main takeaway here is that on first glance there appears to be some decent progression paths.

Getting into the gameplay means interacting the boat man at the edge of the hub area, which will bring up the world map, you choose a destination and set sail.

Levels are laid out nicely as well, so far I haven't encountered any mazes which is actually a plus in my books. Combat is really really good, incredibly fluid with lovely animations and a bit more fast paced than I was expecting, which again, is a nice treat. It's giving me a mixed vibe of The Eternal Castle and Hyperlight Drifter, both excellent games to draw inspiration from.

Occasional jank during some of the early cut scenes I've seen so far, nothing earth shattering or game breaking. Hopefully an update at some point will address that but if not, it's not really a big deal. An example of this would be after the intro cut scene, having my character standing in the first level, unable to move and then suddenly a cut scene starts, then finishes and I continue on my way. Maybe I'm nitpicking here but it stood out.

So there you go, my initial impressions so far. I expect I'll be playing it for a decent amount of time this evening and I'm really happy I picked it up.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFULL REVIEW

So I've finished the main part of the game which took around 6 - 7 hours according to my steam playtime, definitely didn't feel like that (which is a good thing). I've still got a few more achievements to go, which should give me an hour or so more of the game.

Story:
I'm gonna be honest here, After the initial intro and such I wasn't really paying attention on what was happening in the story. It's fully subtitled and I missed a few, so I was kinda out of luck on knowing what was fully going on.

That aside, it's still beautifully told with fantastic cutscenes, characters & excellent audio / music which all ooze atmosphere.

Platforming:
So In my initial review I said it was a pretty basic 2D hack n slash platformer at it's core, which isn't entirely wrong. What I mean by that is yes you jump around from platform to platform, but at times as you also use your harpoon to traverse areas (think of it like grappling hook in Sekiro).

There's also some puzzles at time which aren't too hard, but certainly aren't straight forward. I enjoyed these as they made good use of your harpoon and one of your secondary weapons. They even put in one stealth section, which makes sense when you get to the part.

Combat:
The combat goes into more depth that I expected, though not an insane amount or anything. During the game you acquire a harpoon as your main weapon and 4 secondary weapons. I don't want to spoil what they are, as one was a total surprise and I'd like to leave it as that. I will say, they add a bit of depth to the game.

Also during the game you collect money which can be used to buy upgrades for the main hub (a sailor to go and find items for you and an alchemist that can upgrade your max health). As well as items that can be used to unlock hats. They've each got a skill, these can be switched out before each level, instead of having to go back to the main hub and change it, definitely helpful

Overall the combat is very fluid and enjoyable with good animations. There's just a one downside which I think is the worst part of the game. The awful dodge mechanic, at first I completely forgot it was even a thing and wasn't really using it. But the more I used it, the more I realised how bad it is (especially during a boss fight). It completely ruins the fluidity of the combat by making your character have to stop for at least a second and get up. Wasn't a fan of that

Boss Fights:
The boss fights can be fun or can be frustrating. They were a bit of both for me. They aren't hard, but that is once you've actually figured out how to beat it them. Some were straightforward and killed in one go, while others took a bit to finally figure out how to beat it.

Glitch I got in the final boss:
Funnily enough, I'm pretty sure I encountered a bug with the final boss. It's got 3 rounds and after each round the ground breaks and you fall to the next level (once you've taken of enough health). But on my final try, the game glitched and the camera stayed at the first level, whereas the boss fell to the next. I was able to just mash it until it died in one go, rather then doing it the correct way

Verdict:
Pretty much the same as my initial review. I had a blast playing, didn't feel like I played as long as I did. Definitely worth the price, since it's pretty cheap. Easy recommendation if you like action 2D platformers

Fantastic artistic direction, great story (so far), excellent audio / music & enjoyable combat. All of this being made by one person, makes it that much more impressive.

I'm having a blast and I've hardly even started. If you enjoy 2D action platformers, I'd highly suggest giving the demo a go to see if it's for you.\t\t\t\t\t
