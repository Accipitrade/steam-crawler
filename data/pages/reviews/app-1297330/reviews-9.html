\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, so its pretty. Its fun. Its pretty-fun.
BUT what is this game exactly??? It seems to cloak itself in something a bit more pale & sinister...
It starts out as the traditional tale of a lazy colonizer that embarks on a journey to exploit others for profit. But instead of a glossy adventurer of conquest, your savage-hero, Faraday, secures the quick fail when he shipwrecks right off the jump. Faraday subsequently indentures a naive native that ferries him about; syncs up with other marooned-savages; and then mounts a swift take over of their troubled colonial foothold. As you play along, you will find Faraday appropriating local property and items to further metastasize (build out) the colonial village. You will also observe Faraday stealing & robbing graves for weapons, and then use them it against indigenous life. At some point, your little s-hole village gets a racist hat maker, that will make you a green backward baseball cap that grants you thievery and earns you an achievement referencing the Nile (racist american tropes).
That's just like in the first hour. Gonna take a break from this game for a bit.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty good action adventure game. The combat is surprisingly great, the setting and lore are interesting, and the levels are fun to go through, with a design similar to the old Prince of Persia 1 and 2 (with far easier platforming, though). The gameplay is overall very tight and well designed.

The game has two major downsides IMO: The graphics style, with large chunky pixels of inconsistent quality that makes things hard to see in the middle of the combat, and the technical issues, such as the many freezes that required me to restart the game, or a bug that prevented me to use sub-weapons on the final boss fight. If you can get past this though, it's still very much worth it.

I really hope the developer makes a sequel or expanded remake or something, because with a little more polish and better graphics, this has the potential to be a top tier game.\t\t\t\t\t
