Summary:

Olija is one of those games that is solid yet doesn't wow: it's not like the game has major problems, and you're going to get a bit of fun out of it. Yes, the publisher of devolver digital seems a little odd at first as it's not hyper stylized from a neon/colorful/playful perceptive, but again, don't let that turn you away from the game's solid combat mechanics and nice movement system. The harpoon gimmick is a good one despite it being almost a bit overpowered in some cases, and some good enemy variety does help the game take advantage of it's nice animation quality despite being able to count the pixels on things at times. The hat system is a nice reward for collecting in the world, and some of the more subtle storytelling at display here goes a long way in making the game feel like it's made with heart and bone: it's structurally sound and is out to show what it has.

The main problem with the game is mainly it's lack of an over the top feature: the driving force that would seperate it from the pack. Level design is great but like many other elements in the game, nothing truly stands out. Great music to listen to, but not amazing. Sound design is there, but not on part with the best of the genre has to show. For you wrestling fans out there, Olija is the definition of the B+ player: it's great at what it does but it just doesn't have what it takes to stand with the elite among its genre, despite not really having anything too “wrong” with it.

Good for the price. Not something you need right away, but worth your time.


Gameplay Footage and Video Review: https://www.twitch.tv/videos/893372979

Lists:
Positives:
  • Movement with Harpoon and natural platforming is fun and gives you good reason to explore the world.
  • Story/Environment do well to move the tale along without divorcing the gameplay from it: it's a tale that weaves well without being overbearing.
  • Animation work shines with the pixel art styles and there are some great moments where the game catches you off guard.
  • Enemy variety is good and the game can put you in some interesting battle scenarios that can take advantage of it.
  • Level Design has good variety and the gameplay never dips too badly where you want to just shrug through a section.
  • Fun music selection, good sound design overall.

Negatives:

The world building, characters, sound design and stunning backgrounds are incredible. Some of the best work in a 2D video game I've ever seen. (And it was all done by one guy.)

Unfortunately, the game play isn't as impressive. The combat and platforming have their moments, but are stupidly easy and lack variety. You've see everything the game has to offer within the first hour. Minus the boss fights. Each one is a thrill and actually demand you think on your feet.

The best part of the game though for me was the length. It was 5 hours and that was enough. Any longer and I probably would've stopped playing. But because the presentation is top notch and the game play has some really fun moments I'm leaving the review positive.

Wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe day this game became available on Steam, I played the demo, and not even a half hour later bough the game because of how much I liked it. I must say, entirely worth the price.
This is a beautifully made game. I came into it expecting more content than there is, but I'm probably spoiled by the other games I play.
Very minor spoilers ahead.

Lets go over the pros and cons:

+ + + Actually real fun game-play.
+ + + Combat is fluid and overall fun.
+ + + Controls very responsive, no BS.
+ + Story is engaging and intriguing.
+ I feel like the cut-scenes work perfectly, maybe a couple are a bit long, but it's definitely bearable.
+ A fun amount and usage of side-weapons, although I didn't use the ranged ones much.
+ Platforming works perfectly with this game.
+ + Really, really good sound design. Use headphones.
+ + Boss battles are fun and well-made.
(coming from someone with 100% in all 3 Dark Souls games and ER)

- - - Many areas you cannot go back to once already explored, thus locking you out of some missed collectables.
- There is only 1 (main) weapon upgrade, which isn't really useful for combat, but rather used for a few puzzles.
- Not enough uses for the money you get - too few main-base upgrades. I had so much leftover money by the end.
- The health upgrade shop feels a little useless, and it seems like there could have been more shops at main-base, but not sure what for since the game is not very long.


A suggestion based on the last con mentioned I have is to have some kind of weapon upgrade/smith at the main base that requires materials gotten from exploration. A shop you can upgrade and just another place to use money. Would make it possible to amp up difficulty of later game enemies if the game were to be expanded upon.

That all being said, I love the game, it was very fun, but I nearly got 100% in under 3 hours.
Lastly, the Fandom wiki is basically non-existent. I'll be changing that :)
I recommend this game fully. I love it!\t\t\t\t\t
