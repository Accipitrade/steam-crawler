\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm sorry to say that this is by far the worst game I've played in a long time.

I played the Demo when it was made available, and I was quite excited for a new action platformer.

Sadly, this game is simply not good.
- The combat is spammy and extremely easy. There are some interesting concepts with the weapons that you gather, but you never need to use them as you can simply spam your way to victory.
- This game is only 4 hours long, yet it still manages to feel like a slog. You frequently need to backtrack through levels, and you are forced to sit through many nonsensical cutscenes that seem to try their best at hitting every bad trope. In addition to that, this game has no replayability, unlike other similar short games like Katana Zero.
- The story seems to actually be the main focus of this game, but it made me angry by how awful it was. Every cutscene or dialog made me cringe so hard, and there are many of them.


In conclusion, don't buy this game. Buy any other platformer, you'll have a much better experience, and it'll probably last you for longer as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA European ending up in a foreign Asian-inspired land very much mirrors the developer Thomas Olsson's living in Japan, developing this game over 2.5 years at Kyoto-based Skeleton Crew. Hopefully his life is not as fraught with black goo, spikes, hostile clans, haunting legends, and stalking monsters as Lord Faraday's quest is to save his crew who have been marooned on these islands after a whale wrecks through their ship.

As Faraday, you come across a legendary harpoon and later on a magic sword that both focus on impactful platforming mixed with melee at the same time. You can grapple to eyeball tendrils or enemies by throwing your weapon at them and then flinging yourself like a magnet. The effect never gets old despite you only acquiring a further rifle, crossbow, and rapier. The combat feels great thanks to punchy sound effects and stunning animation work. While the Rottenwood Clan makes for mild fodder, the boss fights are explosive and memorable bringing all your grappling skills to bear. I rarely found the need for the crossbow compared to the rifle but the various hats with abilities made for some fun variety. It's tempting to just stick to the crocodile hat that gives you health points after defeating enemies but the weapon-producing hats are no slouch. The platforming sings as you grapple up and down and diagonally, making for some of the most enjoyable traversal in such a game. You can replay the levels to save all the crew members but afterwards there is not much else to do which is fine because I don't care for collectathons just for the sake of it that can seem like filler padding.

Olija, similarly named like Legend Of Zelda after a secondary character, is a swashbuckling 2D action romp with a heavy narrative emphasis much like a Prince of Persia or Another World. However, the difficulty is more forgiving and the combat is fluid with some excellent gamefeel. The story itself reminds more of films like The New World and The Last Samurai with less colonialism and white saving. You do ransack the local jewellery like a Moby D*ck-inspired colonialist and treasures but it's more for crafting hats that give you abilities than to steal and take back to your home country. There is a pleasing meet-cute between Faraday and Olija, who have to meet in secret and their own people don't take kindly to this cultural mixing. With gibberish voice acting over English dialogue, there is still a lot said by just foreboding environments and expressive animations like when Faraday looks more fatigued as the story progresses. The characters can be charming such as the alchemist (that converts your money into health upgrades) who breaks things over her cute goo pet or a poetic flute player who makes for one of the most memorable challenges. The more crew members you save, the bigger your seaside hub gets, populated by chefs, builders, and loot-seeking travellers. It's a short and sweet (well, more bitter-tinged with sometimes the horror and misery) journey that knows exactly where to end. There's a bittersweet and dreamlike quality to the way the story unfolds with its beautiful pixel-y cutscenes, evocative music, and narrative conclusion. One of the early 2021 GOTY contenders, don't miss out on this gem.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOlija is a short, but interesting sidescroller with pretty pixel art and straightforward gameplay. Stranded in a weird world, you are trying to collect keys to open the Shadow Gate and find your way back. On the way, you find a harpoon that you can throw onto specific tiles to then teleport to that tile. The puzzles in the game are pretty easy, but zipping around with the harpoon is fun. There are also a few side weapons that range from occasionally useful to "why would I ever use this"?

Combat consists of mashing the attack button with an occasional harpoon teleport. This sounds much less fun than it actually is though, because the attacks feel weighty and the mobility from the harpoon makes fights a breeze, both literally and figuratively. It does get a bit repetitive after a while, but luckily the game doesn't overstay its welcome.

Overall, it is an enjoyable 4 hour romp through a strange world with a few things to collect. Sadly the game is never really challenging as both puzzles and combat are very easy. The game also attempts to tell a story by using subtitles and visual storytelling - sometimes it works, sometimes it doesn't, but it gives you a pretty good clue of what is happening overall.

I would definitely recommend it, though the asking price of $15 dollars is way too high for what you end up getting. If you can pick it up for less than 10 dollars, go for it if you like sidescrollers - and hey, there is a demo too, so try that out if you want!\t\t\t\t\t
