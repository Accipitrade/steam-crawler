\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got Olija on sale as part of a bundle. It was enjoyable, but left me wanting more. The movement and game-feel are top notch; it's super satisfying to dash around with your spear, whether you're just platforming or closing the gap on an impaled enemy. The controls are tight and responsive, the combat is engaging and weighty, and the visuals and sound design do a lot to pull you into the experience.

All that said, I'm left with a bittersweet feeling as I wrap up the story, since Olija doesn't feel terribly interested in fully exploring these fun, polished mechanics. I've never seen a game nail the game-feel this well, only to fumble on level design. Imagine if Celeste ended on Chapter 3 and had no B-sides. That's Olija, in both length and depth. Lovely to play, but there's just something missing from this 5-hour playthrough.

Most of the challenges in Olija are simple and straightforward; even the secrets I found mostly just consisted of throwing my spear offscreen in a different direction from the beaten path. The most engaging parts were the boss fights, and while those were exciting and did take me a few tries, they ultimately boiled down to learning simple patterns and throwing/dashing to your spear accordingly. It just wasn't a very challenging or deep experience, which is a shame, because it definitely had the potential to be something you could really sink your teeth into.

Olija is a nice little indie game, and while I really wish it offered a bit more to chew on, I enjoyed getting to know it. It was a nice 5 hours. If you enjoyed the demo (play the demo! it's great!), I would recommend picking it up on sale or as part of a bundle. But I don't think it's worth paying full price for, when the free demo is about as deep and challenging as the gameplay ever gets.\t\t\t\t\t

There's also light platforming, exploration, and puzzles, and a reasonable number of secrets and upgrades. Never gets too difficult if you remember your tools.
Sound - Good use of music stings and chunky hit sounds. I'm also a fan of the mumble talking.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA cool little game of about 3 to 5 hours, with a quite unique style. The way the story is told, the gameplay, the music, the graphics, the "mise-en-scène".. All of this is both simple and smartly striking.

Maybe i'm too biased towards this genre, but it feels like dark souls is beginning to be an old reference. This game feels like it might be inspired by it without knowing it, or just borrowing ideas like it's a source of practical designs and that's it. For example, it's far away from the classic narrative of souls like, but it's pulling on the same strings to tell the story. The tone and the story, while told mostly by environment and laconic characters, reminds me of an old hollywood movie, with the main character quickly becoming the brave hefty hero.
You could draw the same kind of parallels regarding combat, sound design, progression..

In short, it's nice and original :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCool Iberian music. A fantasy-Irish protagonist with a torc of bondage clamped about his neck. A Japan-like Lost World culture in a watery land that retains its mystery by dragging in sailors who get too close and not letting them escape. A gooey demon lord with a fanged mask for a head who commands an army of lost souls. General atmosphere, conception is A-1! Would like some fill-in on the finer story points, however -- in the end it's way too misty for my tastes, and I could certainly have done with a few more Out of this World cutscenes like we get at the beginning (I'm not even talking about the exquisite hand-drawn promotional thing, in case you haven't played that ancient game I just mentioned, but the simple pixel graphic cinemas).

Fighting with the cursed interdimensional grapple-spear, though it does manage to be somewhat cool anyway, comes off way too sloppy to ever let you feel like you're a true martial arts master -- mostly the graphics are too crunchy and earth-toned to let you see much of what you're doing when things really heat up, so you'd better just go nuts with your arsenal and hope for the best. And that's not too unlikely an outcome, seeing as how the game is a little on the easy side, ramping up a bit on the boss end of things.

So!

+Great music, interesting world
+Well-conceived combat mechanics (in the rough cut)
+Smooth platforming, responsive
-Narrative too vague
-Not enough crunchy cutscenes
-Battles too often devolve into a spear-flailing, grapple-zipping mess with little room for actual mastery

I enjoyed this, and I do not regret buying it in a bundle of Devolver platformers, but I don't think it's quite worth full price. Needed a few more squirts of juice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Olija" is a visually stunning action-adventure where you play as a downtrodden noble that shipwrecks and finds himself in a mysterious eastern naval-world. He gets his hands on a magical harpoon and fends off tar-like eldritch creatures ultimatively seeking his way home and and socialising with the downtrodden souls and an old dynasty living there. The atmosphere of the game is very dense and the worldbuilding is tragic and mysterious.

The controls and the movement are solid for the most part (some platforming passages can be tricky) and combat feels good. It is ultimatively not very long and not that challenging and the collectibles (which is treasure for the most part) are not that impaortant, because there is not too much to unlock in the main hub area (but is fitting to the story of the game because "Lord Faraday" initially went on his quest to find riches in order to aid his land).

Gameplaywise it is a very solid and aesthetically a sublime experience. Definetly check this out if you are a fan of 2D-Platforming-Action-Adventures with a bold setting!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOlija is a very hand-crafted kind of game that will remind you of Another World but with modern-yet-simple gameplay elements. I love that they kept it so concise and honest because that might just be its main appeal. The game has a both very classic and very nuanced story to tell you, it also wants to awe your senses with its atmosphere, and to thrill you with combat and puzzles that are only "difficult" enough that you feel a brain tickle when you beat them.

Olija succeeds in all it tries to do. It knows there are games out there that could do all that better for you but still hopes that you'll appreciate that it's willing to spoil you just for a little while.
