\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are two things I immediately avoid when choosing a game to play: platforming and pixel graphics. (Or make that three, rogue-like included). I decided to give Olija a go because of a project. The experience was short and worth it. I was drawn in the atmosphere. Even when you know that you're just watching a few moving pixels, the small intricate movements and details can pull you in and even convince you that the characters are conveying their emotions and grief through the screen.

There's no difficulty curve. If there is, it's just minimal. You can get the grasp of exploration and combat fairly easily. The actual platforming aspect of the game isn't that hard either. In fact, it can be considered very balanced and a much appreciated welcome for non-platforming enthusiasts like me. The combat is engaging and fast-paced.

The only thing I don't like about the game is the non-interactive menu. You can only see the icons of the items and equipment you have. You can't highlight any of them or see their individual descriptions or tooltips. That means that every tutorial pop-up you'll see must be read and learned. (or just consult a guide)

Olija is a short but wonderful experience. It's not outstanding and hardcore platforming masochists may even find the game too easy or boring. However, for average platformer fans or non-fans at all, the game offers a great balance of narrative, atmosphere, exploration, puzzle-solving, and combat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDevolver has a finger on the pulse of modern-retro gaming. Olija has a strong reminiscence of older games, all the way down to the audio design. The story manages to be gripping, something that many other Devolver titles lack. The nautical-demonic themes are beyond fun, and makes the whole game play like a sailor's tale.

I managed to beat every boss of the game first attempt save for the last one, which took me 4-5 attempts. I've played Hollow Knight and Salt and Sanctuary through, to give you an idea of the skill level. The game should be a quick play, even for beginners to the metroidvania genre.

Good game! Give it a shot if you've got a couple afternoons to burn and you wanna feel like you're playing an arcade game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOlija is a very tight, intriguing, satisfying, and unfortunately (or perhaps bittersweetly) short experience. Combat feels satisfying with meaty strikes and enough variety of combat style to keep things interesting. Movement options are an obviously emphasized feature and all feel great although somewhat disorienting at first. The art style of the game is an obvious draw - although simple it is very evocative and expressive. The music is also somewhat simple but helps set the mood and feels appropriate to the setting.

The story of Olija is somewhat ambiguous as a deliberate choice. It provides just enough of a sense of intrigue to get the player interested in exploring without bogging down the gameplay. It won't win any awards but it certainly piqued my interest and kept me engaged throughout.

All in all Olija is an excellent experience I would recommend to anyone who likes short but well-crafted games that offer some unique mechanics to play around with.\t\t\t\t\t
