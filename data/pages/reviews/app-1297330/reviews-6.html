\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUuhh. It's fine. The demo did not make this look quite as story-focused as it actually is - several levels exist purely for cutscenes, there's slow loading and then slow boat rides to every new location, even most of the boss fights feel like playable cutscenes to an extent and the game does not really try to challenge you much (I died twice in total, both during boss fights) and on top of that I found the character sprites kind of hard to read at times - a lot of them look really samey especially with the matching outfits. So some of the story didn't quite feel... conveyed, even with for as much emphasis was put on it. The game also deletes your save upon beating the game which means I get to be left without four achievements I'm definitely not replaying the entire game just to get, which, okayyyy whatever then.

There -is- a lot to like about this game - you're given a lot of combat options, and combat is fluid and fast-paced, the platforming and puzzles never get too intense but they are satisfying enough, there's secrets and exploration (for what purpose other than achievements I'll never know - none of the three bonus collectible types appear to do anything), the game looks gorgeous and the animation is top-notch, it -feels- good to play. It's just a shame there isn't really much to play. Basically four quarters of the main map that each have one small cutscene area and one large area, you collect the keys, fight the boss, move onto the next, and a (fairly underwhelming) final boss once you're done. It's... weirdly underdeveloped for how much time was clearly put into making the combat mechanics feel good. The harpoon gets a lot of use in both platforming and combat and it feels great, and while I didn't end up jiving much with the other subweapons besides the rapier I can see them all having uses. There's a number of craftable hats that each give different abilities, you can upgrade your health, the depth in the gameplay and progression system just feels unwarranted for how lacking in challenge and length the game as a whole is, though, and it's a shame really.

It's definitely not a bad game - I enjoyed my time with it, though I don't think I'll be replaying it, and it might be priced a bit high for how little of it there is - but it's also not a game I can highly recommend either. Would be a certifiable "meh" if steam offered that option, but considering the quality of what is here, I did enjoy my time with it if nothing else.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t=Olija is a very beautiful indie game that is well worth it's price point. This game is about a tale of a captain who had been caught in a shipwreck and awakes to his crew vanishing. He stumbles upon a cursed spear which he wields for the rest of the game(to put it simply with no spoilers). While this story tells the basis of its story through its dialogue, I feel as if most of the story is told through its environment, its world building. The environment tells its eerie tone its old broken down buildings, and caves full of corpses. The music in this game is exceptional and adds to this eerie tone. This game's art design and music are all factors that allow for this game to flourish in its story.

=While it may have a simple 8 bit like design, the animations are so well done that you are able to interpret any and every movement, which brings me to my next point... the gameplay! The gameplay in this game is a solid 10/10. The way you are able to move in this game while attacking is fantastic! Being able to pull off incredible combos in a room full of enemies is a very satisfying experience, and that satisfaction is very fun. However, there is some to be desired in the aspect of the wearable "magic hats" in the game. There is one specific magic hat in the game that is clearly stronger than others(which I will not name), where you would rather want to use this hat over the others.

-While you could use the other hats during different runs of the game, I think that brings another issue into the game.. There is no real replay ability for the game in the fact that it is a generally linear experience with set rooms that never change. For most players, this shouldn't be that much of an issue, but it can be for those who hope to replay it for a slightly different experience. For the price of $15, it is worth it, but it is slightly disappointing to see that there isn't that much of a reason to go back and replay it.

=This game is an overall 9/10 with the only main issue being that it struggles to run at times, where it would crash while attempting to load into the map.. which is a shame. Without this issue, this game would be a 9.5/10. The crashing I will say is at a very minimal state, but it still persists which isn't a pleasing situation to deal with. It is still worth it to pick it up and try for yourself!\t\t\t\t\t
