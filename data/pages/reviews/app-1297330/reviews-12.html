\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite a fun little game. Worth the price of a movie, which is great cause it'll take you an afternoon to get through. But that afternoon is gonna have some entertaining platforming and combat.

Olija is a short action platformer where you play Faraday, some guy who gets shipwrecked and then gets cursed by a magic harpoon. And you're trying to get you and your men out of this weird land of Terraphage. That's it. That's the pitch. There are moments of story and character dialogue, which are fine, but most of the story is what you see above plus a love story with Olija. Don't expect riveting twists and turns but also do expect to be entertained by the short stuff that's there.

Amusingly, I'd liken this game to the Adventure Island series. Take your boat to a couple islands, each being a self contained little action platformer section, get new items and increase your ability to fight/explore/puzzle-solve.

The meat of the game is the action. And the main gimmick is the harpoon. You can chuck it at enemies and certain things and it'll get stuck, and you can either call it back or bring yourself to it. A simple mechanic but it allows for some pretty fun movement and combat. The combat felt good and was more fun than I was expecting, and you get a couple subweapons that open up your options as well as hats that open up different powers. Not a huge amount but enough. Likewise, the platforming had a couple puzzles that you need to figure out but none took too long. I'd say the game is...relatively easy outside of a certain boss battle where the difficulty suddenly jumps.

The music is decent, always fitting and never takes you out of the game. It's good. There's...not much to say.

The graphics, for being a certain pixel style, are actually quite good looking in motion. Fun animations, easy character profiles so you can tell who's who. Good spritework for the ambiance of the areas, as each feels very unique from each other.

Any gripes I have are small. Screen transitions can get annoying due to the monsters being on one and you on the other then they hit you in between, it's hard to tell what's a safe place to fall down and what will damage you, the bosses can be cheesed easily with a certain hat. Nothing big.

I bought it, played it, enjoyed it. What else can I say? Worth the money and a solid, fun game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun game, worth your time and money. Pleasing art and animation that never gets in the way, stylized so that I was rarely bothered by the pseudo-pixelated style. Great platforming and puzzle challenges, along with satisfyingly challenging combat that was surprisingly easy to learn. Each boss stands out on its own, with unique challenges and obstacles, as does each region.

The hat system is mostly just fun, I think. Could they have taken it more seriously, perhaps used armor or attachments to the harpoon? Absolutely. Is this game all the more colorful that they didn't? Even moreso. There's myriad little touches that make you feel like you're really progressing, and the story manages to strike a striking balance between Lord Faraday being the protagonist and his being just a single part of the world.

There's a straight romance story woven into the plot, but oddly it never really feels that major, despite several of the beats being required to progress. And the fight-flirting is a nice touch. The plot is simple, and you're immediately pulled into the story of the world itself; it's easy to accept the magic that flows through bones and spears and hats.

I very much enjoyed playing Olija. If you enjoy platformers, beat-em-ups with unique mechanics, and metroidvanias, you're most likely going to enjoy this. I look forward to seeing speedruns of this game.\t\t\t\t\t
