\r\n\t\t\t\t\t3/5
The game is pretty good, hence the positive review, BUT I feel like it doesn't meet it's potential.

The gameplay has a very heavy set focus on the combat, having tons of combat encounters when the fighting is quite bland, and uninteresting. The BEST part of the game, the part I was most excited for, was the platforming via the harpoon.

While it was a lot of good fun, it doesn't go full force into it, like I was hoping it might. It has a few sections of tight platforming, needing to string together throws and teleports, but it is more often than not a very simple puzzle, or just a gap closer in the aforementioned bland combat.

Another nitpick I have with the game, is how it's bogged down by things constantly slowing the game down to a grinding halt, making me roll my eyes every single time I encountered it. Namely "Cutscenes" when still in pixel graphics being nonskippable, such as the boat arriving to the island, EVERY SINGLE TIME. Show it to me once, and then don't show it again-- I get how I got there. Also, if you are going to have that, remove the invisible walls from around the boat, if I wanna be stupid and throw myself into the water and lose some health as a result, you should allow it. Invisible walls are just lazy.

I think the game is a good time, a fun time to just sit back and relax. It'll only take you a few hours to beat, so I recommend getting it when it's on sale. Again, although I was ripping on the game quite a bit, it's still a good time, but I think my above statements would elevate the game from a 3/5 to a 4/5. I'd love to see what the devs are capable of, if they lean off of the combat and more into the platforming.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMore people should be talking about this game.

Combat is very fluid and hits hard. Using the harpoon to warp around and switching to melee combat is seamless. It will make you feel like the John Wick of fishermen.

Puzzles are rather easy but creative. They allow for quick "think on your feet" moments that adds to the the game nicely. Only criticism I have hear is practically all the puzzles rely on the teleport feature of your weapon, a bit more variety would have been enjoyable.

Story is a bit hard to follow and nothing super original, but works well with the art style and voice acting.

It is a short (100% in about 8 hours) but i would buy it again even at full price. Overall very enjoyable experience that enjoys much more attention than what it has received thus far.\t\t\t\t\t

Perhaps a little shortlived in some respects, when the game really starts testing your exploration it seems to bring a level to a close. A latter gadget teases at complexity but is only used for a handful of simple puzzles. The satisfying combat does challenge during boss encounters but otherwise feels trifling; sporadically tossing a handful of weak enemies at the player. Combat also, like the exploration, does not invite much use of the gadgets. A final negative of the combat is the superfluous 'crouch' and the largely useless combat roll, both simply get in the way and maybe should have just been removed. Mild spoilers but a climatic encounter also completely limits your use of anything bar the harpoon which is disappointing and feels like a real misstep, even if the fight is pretty good.

However the presentation is FANTASTIC, the best way i could describe it is that it feels akin to something like Uncharted or any other AAA prestige action adventure title, it's definitely Olija's USP. It goes for a 'minimalist' narrative but unlike say Hyper Light Drifter does not come of as pretentious or totally incoherent and has a handful of deft 'interactive storytelling' gimmick moments that work wonderfully. Basically Olija is Hyper Light Drifter but good.

Art is great, especially the animation, but the pixel art does fall apart slightly when the camera pulls back, though these moments are rare, 2 or 3 in the game.\t\t\t\t\t
