\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun little indie capital ship combat simulator. Still pretty rough around the edges but a lot of fun when you play with a crew of friends. During covid, my regular gaming group switched to online games, and this has made its way into our weekly rotation as a good appetizer before an evening with RWR.
According to the roadmap there are a lot of QOL features that will be added, that will improve the experience dramatically; such as AI crew for your unfilled ship roles and (I hope) a more fleshed out enemy crew when playing against the AI. I would love to see a ship full of lizards like the security officer.
There are unlocks like new skins for the different crew roles and multiple engagement scenarios. We play full on PVE, but I imagine a fully crewed PVP battle would be pretty sweet. The game can be played solo, but for me, the real fun is watching a tight group pull together and deal with the unfolding threats of space combat and boarding actions. Well worth the money now, and looking forward to the new features as they come!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI found this on-sale in some Sci-Fi Steam section, then I googled it, then I got it for my friend and I and we've loved it. You can hardly tell it's an early access game- I have played for almost seven hours without finding a single bug! My recommendations would be to:

1: Add more classes and weapons. Maybe a Medic (TF2 inspired skin?) who could have a slightly increased self healing rate, and an ability to made some sort of mini tether-heal machine, like in the Medical Bay. I think that more weapons would also be cool, especially weapons in favor of one thing, and not the other- like a blowtorch. A blowtorch could be a very short-rage secondary that does high damage to doors and robots, but very low damage to players, or maybe a bomb or C4 that can deal very high damage to door and entities, maybe even the ship, but is a one time use item. Or maybe you can tie in the C4, blowtorch, add a pistol, and you could even make a demolitions expert class!

2: Allow for more in-depth ship customization. If you could control B1/B2 patrol routes, where is the medical bay in your ship, or maybe even if you could choose what ship you commanded from a list of pre-made ships instead of just the one we currently have, one, that would be awesome.

3: This kind of ties into #2, but add a a workshop where people could make a ship and then post it on the workshop for others to use, maybe even weapons, upgrades, or skins too!

4: More gameplay. I play the game with one of my friends often, and without him there is not a lot to do. I think that a campaign may be a good idea, where you have to destroy enemy ships or protect an allied space station, and I think something other than a Ship-Vs-Ship gamemode would also be cool, like a few Frigates-Vs-Capital ship, or an Attacker-Vs-Defender.

Overall I think that DSBS is a great game, and since it's an Early Access game, it has all sorts of community ideas and developer plans that have a ton of potential, and I think that for $6.99 (I got it on sale for $5.59 :P) it is worth your money.\t\t\t\t\t

As it stands today, the game is well worth the full asking price and there is a lot more on the roadmap.
