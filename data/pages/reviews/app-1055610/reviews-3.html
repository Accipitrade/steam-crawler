\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot good enough.

I first played this awhile back in Early Access and it was OK but very rough around the edges, various bugs/glitches with: UI, key settings, and controls. Now after multiple updates, it still feels about the same - very rough around the edges with skiddish controls. It's possible to play with others or solo and let the AI control the bots but it seems broken. Here is the main point: the 100% computer controlled AI crewed ship decimates the one the player is on, even though the ship is fully controlled by the AI crew with the exception of whatever the player wishes to "take over" (shooting turrets, flying, managing power systems, jumping into fighter craft, etc.). Which makes it basically unplayable unless you're cool with watching your side be useless and the other side steadily obliterate your vessel. Kind of pointless? The controls for the ships are still wait off too, very bizarre too in fact as if the ships are tethered to a rail system.

Finally, it lacks optimization. This game has graphics that look out of the mid-to-late 90s, that a 20-25yrs old computer could run. Imagine time-travelling from 2002 to 2022 finding out computers in the future can't run your games without overheating the CPU or some crap.
Maybe cpu and gpu makers went too overboard with their planned obsolescence gimmicks?\t\t\t\t\t
