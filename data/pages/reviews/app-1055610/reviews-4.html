\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, the game looks pretty fun. I like the style and simplicity from the start, but the one thing that got to me was the controls.

First, finding out how to play the game is in a section called "missions", not anywhere labelled "tutorial", which was kind of confusing. Then, after jumping into the first mission, my screen is filled with information I don't need yet and don't know about and it's pretty cluttering when the small dialogue box for learning how to play also changes too fast and without notice. Trying to find my way around the ship, why do I start in the medical bay? And why is one of the entrances to the bridge not labelled? And why can't I sprint with a little laser in my hand?

While walking around, there is no indication of which way to go to find a certain area for the next part of the "tutorial". No markers, no path, just "go find it, but be quick cause we might explode or never get out of here". I have to be honest though, I love the small, yet simple, details of the corridors: how they're not all the same, there's small rooms of crew quarters, a hydroponics centre, and little aesthetic blue info panels on the walls. The devs did good on that.

Flying a fighter was, kind of fun, but also a pain. The controls were... okay-ish... but I couldn't change my fighter's roll to the movement of my mouse, only keybindings, and I can't change the sensitivity of the look-around feature without changing the entirety of the sensitivity. Changing keys are a bit buggy and there's not much distinction between which key changes what. Also, there were no markers for enemy fighters behind me/no radar to give an idea of exactly where they were with relation to my ship, so it was hard to estimate my position from the enemy fighter. You'd think after 47 updates, this wouldn't be a problem. Apparently not.

I'd recommend the concept of the game, but not for the controls and learning progression. It seriously needs to be re-done to be more enjoyable, with more quality of life improvements. So, until the game is fixed, I'll be following it's progression and then the devs can have my money back.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEverything in this game is extremely basic: The sounds, the gunplay, the space battles. It's about as barebones as it gets. Functional yes, but there is no depth, no attention to detail to anything. On foot combat is boring and flat, weapons are static and emit a "pew" noise and a basic laser projectile. Same with the space battles. Systems are equally basic, there's a few fun ideas in there like hacking enemy systems, but it's all just pressing a button, with little to no strategy or skill behind it. Nothing feels satisfying to do. The most fun thing to do is to infiltrate the enemy ship, but thanks to the flat combat even that is interesting just once.

This would not be a problem, were it not for the fact that the developers officially announced that this game is now "fully released". There's the foundation of a fun game, but so much more tender loving care is needed. This is just the bottom bun, and they are calling it a burger.

Maybe you can have some fun with friends, but there are much better implementations of the multiplayer multistation space ship simulator genre available, like Pulsar.\t\t\t\t\t
