\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very pretty looking route, and good equipment, including the U30B and the F7, in A- and B- variants. -Nearly 70 mile route, including the Crescent Mills and Quincy sidings
-Beautiful locos, the U30B and F7
-U30B has movable windows and doors
-Custom made bridges
-Nice rolling stock
-Nice caboose
-The stunning Keddie Wye

Cons:
-The old route is not included
-Reused horn on U30B

Other
-The route is mostly 20mph
-You can use also modern-era engines on the route, and it fits perfectly

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, so this route is very impressive. Even though I'm too lazy to actually run it all the way (I will eventually), I have run some parts of it and IT IS LOVELY. I have never heard of or been to Feather River Canyon, but I can tell this is a perfect representation of how I would have perceived it if I actually knew what the real route looked like. I wasn't born that long ago. I'm a 2002 baby.

Edit 1:
Okay, so looking closer at the route, I've noticed some very funky things, mostly with the locomotives.

The U30B is cool, but it lacks A LOT of accuracy. Firstly, the traditional WP livery you see on the loco wasn't the only freaking livery that existed. In fact, WP used a second livery. Green and orange. You'll notice this when you purchase and use the Western Pacific GP35 loco addon that can be used for the route. How the hell does WP only use one WP livery? They don't. Even the GP20 isn't an exception to this. Some GP20s also had the green and orange livery. The Class lights aren't usable either. If the class lights are nonfunctional, then why put them on the locomotive then (even if they do belong on the loco)? Another thing I have to take a jab at is the fact the horn is literally incorrect on the U30B. U30Bs don't have crappy DTG GP40-2 horns. They have M5 Chime horns which most WP locomotives were fitted with.

Another thing about this route I have to take a jab at is the lack of any other necessary locomotives that should have been in the route. WP had MANY locomotives

S1s
S2s
S4s
SW1s
SW9s
NW2s
GP7s
GP9s
F3s
FTs
F7s
SW1500s
U23Bs
GP35s
GP40s
GP40-2s and so on and a damn lot of Steam locomotives.

Yeah we have a GP40-2, SW1500 and GP9, but those locos are part of separate loco dlcs AND only come with the green and orange livery which is very ironic. There's also a WP GP35 that correctly comes with the two WP liveries, but how accurate the GP35 itself is I'm not sure.

The FP7 only comes with one livery which is highly incorrect as WP also had FP7s that were coated with green and orange WP liveries. The same goes for the U30B.

Additionally, the route comes with petty freight bearing one 50ft Boxcar, one 50ft reefer, and a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ caboose. Of course, the GP35 addon does add a little more freight, but still not really enough to be considered accurate for the time the route takes place. Sure, there's the recent EMD F3 dlc which has well, every predecessor but WP. There isn't even a WP boxcar, just a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ bay window caboose instead of a WP Boxcar. You can't even technically use UP rollingstock because that would just make it look weird, unless you want to relive the last days of WP before it merged with UP, though, Burlington Northern rollingstock is still appropriate since BN and WP shared the route back then.

There's also no BN locomotive. BN and WP shared the route, so no BN loco and rollingstock?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, this route is... frustrating. On one hand, I love it because 70's railroading is awesome, however, on the other hand they almost completely failed to fully represent the allure of the Feather River Canyon.

Pros:
1. The U30B is nice to drive. The horn is reused and inaccurate, however that is to be expected. The F7A is also a decent engine, however you can see that they added two single horns to the top, which would make the tone of the horn very flat. Instead you here this very nice, multiple chime horn, which does not emulate the horn physically modeled.
2. The route is stunning and features many new bridge models.**
3. Lots of variation in scenery. I won't spoil this for those of you who might not have it yet.
4. Good rolling stock sounds.
5. 1970's!
6. Very nice looking cliffs. The canyon's natural terrain was modeled well, which makes this route very nice on the eyes at points.

Cons:
1. The fills and embankments
As a route builder, I need to address a common and extremely glaring mistake that DTG does when making American Railroads; They seem to not fully understand that most fills and embankments, especially on the mountain portion of this route, are literally just straight and sharp drop-offs that can be at times up to 150 feet of almost straight down rock. Instead, DTG tends to make the fills and embankments extremely wide, which means that as a driver you can't really see into the valley below like you would in real life. This completely ruins Keddie Wye's realism, and detracts from much of the rugged portion of this route. In real life, it's fully understandable as to why the speed limit is 25. In fact, 25 MPH always felt absurdly fast due to the extreme nature of the route. However, in game, the 25 MPH speed limit makes no sense due to the pristine nature of the grade and track. It is extremely frustrating to see this done over and over again, and it's to the point where I almost do not want them to create an American mountain pass ever again.
2. Horrendous bridges
Again, as a route builder myself, I need to address the fact that you will almost never see a 50 Ft. wide concrete bridge with zero structural support holding up a mainline track in the wilderness or really anywhere else. This is an extremely common theme on most TS routes, and it remains a problem here as well. This is a simple concept. Just make proper steel or wooden bridges. Don't make them too wide, otherwise it detracts from the immersion. From screenshots of the new Clinchfield Route, I can see that they updated the bridge loft, however it's still almost completely inaccurate for almost the same reasons. **This entire thing is super weird, because all of the bridges that they did model were absolutely spot on. A solution to this problem would be to remove the generic bridge loft and replace it with a narrow steel or wooden bridge.
3. Lack of Rolling Stock Options
This route only comes with only a few rolling stock options. It lacks a tank car, centerbeam flats, low gondolas, 3-Bay hoppers and coal hoppers. This is disappointing because the Feather River Route had an extremely diverse set of operations on it.
4. Lack of engine options
In the 70's and 80's this route was shared with the Burlington Northern. It would've been prudent to add at least one BN engine, however they neglected to do so. They also do not have a road switcher included. They could have easily added a GP9 to this route, but they decided not to for whatever reason.

Overall, if you are even just somewhat familiar with the Feather River Route in real life, this route might be a disappointment to you. Their modeling skills as far as terrain and track goes constantly left much to be desired, and it lacked critical engines and rolling stock. One good takeaway is, if you could ignore the issues with the roadbed and bridges, this route is quite pretty. That being said, I would only buy this on sale. (4/10)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving lived in the Feather River Canyon for about 6 months as a child, and having transistioned through it after moving to Greenville, California (Its the next town beyond Crescent Mills) many times to get to the central valley, I'm quite impressed at how they modeled this. It is very accurate. When I lived in the Canyon I lived near Tobin where the two bridges cross each other in the route.

Now being a resident of Texas I play this route very often.

Even the dams that make up the DeSabla hydro division of PG&E are very accurately depicted. I know people are looking at a review as to how it plays and stuff, but I really bought it for what it was, first. I just hope someday they expand the route up to Portola, which is a major UP crew change area now.

As for playability, I think it works very well. There are a lot of scenarios out there for this route now and I've probably played them all. The add on for the F Unit WP locomotives works very well too, and gives you a nice view as you're going up or down the canyon. The Keddie Wye is top notch as well.

Overall, this is probably my favorite route to drive in this game, and I'm moving up or down the canyon quite often with this expansion now. If you like the game, I'd highly reccomend it. Its a nice 90 minute to a two hour drive depending on what you're doing. Watch your brakes on the downhill though!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe route is awesome. The first part from Oroville is a 45mph line curving through hills, an open landscape with scenic views on to a distant landscape. The second, and largest, part is a 25mph line curving through the canyon. An awesome landscape. Grades are 1% at best, and goes uphill all the way to Keddie.

However, it's a shame that the windows in the U30B are pretty small. Although it's a lovely engine to drive, you get to see little of the amazing landscape. The F7 has bigger windows allowing you to see more, but the F7 doesn't have the horsepower to pull a train uphill. I had to couple 4 units to pull 40 boxcars uphill without going slower than 20mph.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly, this a beautiful route, fabulous scenery and very nicely done throughout.
The U boat locos are fine, the horn is a bit weak, but the physics are fine.
The FP loco included ain't so good, it's a bit dated to be honest, it runs well enough, but the horn and engine sounds are rubbish. You can turn on the cab light, but it makes no diff to the illumination in the cab, it's pointless.
There is only one scenario for the FP loco and it is set in fog and snow, there should be more.
In regards the U boat scenarios, there seem to be a few bugs with the AI, that make at least two of the scenarios unable to be finished, a real drag.
Keep in mind that the entire route is set to an average of 25 mph, so there ain't much excitement apart from trying to keep it as close as you can to the speed limit, on a line of this length it can become tedious, but it is what it is.
Then there are 8 of the useless railfan scenarios that require no skill at all and are a total waste of time.
So a bit of a mix up, a few good scenarios a couple that don't work and a poorly done FP loco.
Yet in spite of its failings, it's a nice route for a long leisurely quick drive. It's worth getting at the sale price, but due to its failings I'd have been well ticked off at the full price.
