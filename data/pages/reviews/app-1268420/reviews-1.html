\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuperb implementation of the board game. Britannia is a very interesting game design, and perhaps the most restrictive historical straight jacket that any designer has every successfully attempted. But given that straight jacket, the requirement to have other "players" was always a little iffy. Like History of the World, which came after, this is about point maximization according to an historical script. So, for all intents and purposes, your turn has a "right answer" that a group of people could puzzle out together. Opponents in a sense are superfluous. Also, this is a late 80s design. So its looooong for contemporaries tastes of a game of this weight. The beauty of this computer version is that it removes all of those pitfalls from the analog game, and gives you a fast clean experience. It allows you to understand the paths to victory with the different factions, and all of the fun parts of Britannia are retained. I don't really understand why a couple of the other games using this system weren't included There was one on India called The Raj, and other on Italy. In any event, this was a superb purchase for me. And if your an historical board gamer, you'll definitely get your money's worth.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe most important thing to know is that this computer game is based on the board game of the same name. So base your expectations from this. Britannia was my favorite board game growing up, so it was great to be able play it again. You get to play various nations as they fight over the island of Britannia. Nations rise and fall, and it is up to you to maximize what they can achieve.
It is pretty easy to learn how to play it. A full game can be completed in about an hour. You can play against other people or against the computer. The AI is competent, though does occasionally make weird decisions, and it can also lack on overall strategic play (ie. failing to recognize that one player is clearly winning and needs to be targeted).
Anyone who enjoyed the original board game I would expect to also love playing this version of it. Also if you like Civilization 4 mods 'Rhye's and Fall of Civilization' you probably will also enjoy this (they have that same feel to them as you watch empire rise and fall over time).
I don't know if it will happen, but I would like to see more expansive versions of this made with more turns (the board game was restricted by time, as it took 4+ hours to complete which is about the max you want for a board game), however this game being computerized is much quicker and can be easily saved and picked up, meaning length is not an issue. Would also be nice if it came with more modding options.
Overall I give the game an enthusiastic 4 out of 5 stars.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tObligatory "I don't review a lot of games" (but I really don't)... anyway, I sat down and started playing this on a Saturday and didn't stop until I had finished the game 7 hours later -- mind you I would've kept playing, but the centuries of frenetic warfare had run their course, with many honorable warriors having met their end, one way or another, skewered on some soggy field or crowned in Valhalla, plus many other fates between. Really, I can't say enough good things about this gem of a game. If you're at all intrigued by the subject matter, get it now, carve out some uninterrupted hours, and summon your best strategic acumen. And in case I haven't recommended it highly enough, consider this: I spent an additional hour or so AFTER winning just to wonder over the final game board, review the manual, and think about the course of history implied by my particular game session.

Pros: engaging history, cool battle mechanics, satisfying ebb and flow based on actual invasions and other twists of history, etc. ... in this game, you will not always be strong, sometimes you have to hold on and work with what's there

Cons: none, I just want more content... either for this game or publish another title using the same mechanics but exploring different geography and history\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has issues both with the game idea and the programme bugs. The game is an odd mix of historical campaigning and a board game but doesn’t do either well. The campaigning part is not real and the game tries to follow too many rules according to history. Unfortunately, the player (in this case, I) just wanted to win. Bring in the bugs and it gets comical. I played the game two times and had problems both times. To give an idea of how things deviate the first time I played it (as Romans/yellow player), Emperor Trajan came good for us, and let the legions stay. The legions did not change to Romano-Britains and Arthur, Offa and some of the others never turned up. Neither did some of the other characters and Nordic clans who decided to stay warm at home. When the Saxons turned up the coastline was covered by the legions. But that didn’t stop the Saxons who flew into Leeds/Bradford airport, bypassing any coastal district. The last bit was also bizarre, William the Conqueror versus the Roman Legions. Again, William and his army couldn’t land on the coast (Hastings was not available) so he flew into the interior. But after the Historic “Battle of Heathrow”, the Roman legions sent him packing (somewhere). I think that gives the idea for some of the game plan bugs. The idea of board-game doesn’t work either. What is the point of the dice when only one number (6) counts in the battle. The battles are done individually, so there is no point in stacking. In another game (Green player) one Welsh soldier defeated two Roman legions and a fort because the fights were not connected (unit strengths do not appear to add up). The stacking, the resources and the scoring are not understandable. Some nations never seem to earn enough points to have any additional units. The game machinery is also problematic. Almost every save resulted in a white screen which went onto (close programme/wait until…). The save is not in order of the time, so if you save a game under a used name, it does not appear at the top of the list when loading. The game can really do with a proper direct load command and ideally a quick-load command. After all, this game is easy to win, so long as you are prepared to load the game a few times and save regularly (after each event). I think a lot is needed before the game is enjoyable and in any way playable. I don’t think the board-game aspect lends itself to players disregarding the order of events as the publishers appear to want these processes to go according to plan (they don’t ). And so the bizzare errors happen. For me two times was enough and I can’t see me playing as a third colour.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally needs online multiplayer for replayability. AI is not the best for strategy and playing other humans online is more fun and challenging. However AI is not completely terrible due to the way the points incentivise strategy for any player - human or AI. Therefore the AI does focus on regions that make sense for points and has a reasonable logic.

The use of leaders by AI is probably the weakest point currently - in many cases they are wasted. It would be great to see some improvements to this. Also the AI wastes the use of some raiding armies by capturing empty regions that don't give any points and then withdrawing to sea. If this was fixed the other attacks it does the same turn would benefit from the extra armies.

It appears the rules quirks have been implemented mostly very well. I have spotted and reported a couple of bugs. One to do with Romano British being able to attack formerly submitted nations before Round 8. Another to do with imposed landing restrictions for the Danes before round 16. However on the whole this version definitely inspires confidence.

Probably needs an undo button for decision to retreat as many times I selected retreat and then found out where I could retreat to was undesirable, or that I could only do a partial retreat. Also would be nice to have undo button for proceeding to next phase as a couple of times I accidentally advanced to next phase - once when doing raider withdrawal and once when I had only set up a few movements I advanced to battle stage by accident.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBritannia is one of my favorite board games, and I'm happy to see it on Steam.

Up to now the game runs well, but there are still some bugs.

However, the main problem is that the AI is too terrible. TOO TERRIBLE.

It's clear that the AI designer wants Blue to win every game. And actually, I have started many games with 4 AIs playing, and Blue wins 100% of those games.

Romans never eliminate Belgaes. In over 95% games Romans never attack Downlands (in the other 5% they attack Downlands only after Turn 4), and in 100% games they let Belgaes live happily in Lindsey. Belgaes sometimes grow very strong in Turn 4-5, and keep alive until the end of the game.
Scots never attack Picts before they kill Brigantes. They stupidly keep attacking Strathclyde again and again. Skye and Dalriada seem to be nothing for them. Therefore Picts always easily control the areas which should belong to Scots, and scores a lot in every game.
Saxons play poorly against Angles. They often lose control of Hwicce. Angles become Bretwalda every turn, and often King in Turn 11.
And Saxons also play poorly against Normans. They never plan a defense, and just simply let Normans take all their land.

All these ridiculous AI behaviors lead to a powerful Blue in every game. And some other ridiculous things also happen on the AI of Red, Yellow and Green.
Romans actually never plays proper moves in Turn 1. They seldom try to force Wales submit, and sometimes even Brigantes don't submit. Romans always score too low.
Wales never try to take back their land taken by Irish. They send every unit they build to England, and obviously these units will die in England. They let Irish easily take their land without resistance.
Saxons and Jutes often attack York in Turn 6-7, and Saxons sometimes even go to Lothian around Turn 7.
Norsemen never play a proper attack on Caledonians. They always send 2 units to each area and 1 staying on sea.
Dubliners often block the way of Norwegians, and Norwegians go to attack Scotland - scoring nothing.
At the same time, all the 4 Blue AIs play well, except that sometimes Belgaes stay in North Mercia.

I am an experienced player of Britannia. When I am playing Green and Yellow against AIs (especially Green), I often feel the game is hard because everyone else is helping Blue to win. Sometimes I only score 230-250 with poor dices, and Blue AI wins with 260-290. However, when I am playing Blue, I always feel the game is too easy, as if the AIs all want me win. Scoring more than 400 is not difficult when playing Blue.

Besides the strategies of nations, AI also plays terribly in basic moves. AI starts many 1vs1, 2vs2 and 2vs3 battles, and even 2vs2 on mountains. When they have 3 units on the sea they often send 2 to battle and 1 just sitting on the sea.

In conclusion, the terrible AI has ruined the game. Half a year has passed, yet multiplayer is still not available. So currently the game is only suitable for 4 players playing on a same computer... but why don't they buy a real board game instead?\t\t\t\t\t
