\r\n\t\t\t\t\tObviously, this game exists as a nostalgia vehicle for gamers old enough to remember ICOM titles like Shadowgate and Deja Vu. "From Beyond" imitates their look and playstyle perfectly, but also sports a little bit of polish and benefits from the intervening 30 years of game design. Puzzles follow logically, items don't need to be used in any wacky or bizarre fashion, and there are even multiple ways to attack some obstacles. The story is a simple and sturdy Lovecraftian pastiche that fits the 8-bit look nicely.

There are a couple of negatives. The music is sparse and on a short loop, so it's guaranteed to drive you to madness before the eldritch cosmic horrors do. And personally, I would have preferred the game avoid instant surprise deaths due to walking through the wrong door or proceeding without completing a puzzle; although they do make the game feel old school, they're as annoying as they were in the 80's. Save often! The game is also rather short and ends without real resolution. Billed as "Prologue" to what one assumes will be a full game, this makes sense, but it's still only a couple hours of play time.

However, with a price tag of only a few bucks, I recommend this for a couple hours of nostalgic fun to anyone who has a soft spot for these old puzzle adventures.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've yet to get into this one, after having started it twice now.

Navigating the game feels haphazard at times. For me, an immersive game world is one where I can piece together the various parts....but I'm having a hard time doing that, with this game.

The interface doesn't do this game any favors. It feels dated. Others are clearly enjoying the nostalgia, but I would rather have some modern polish, like some visual feedback on the things I'm mousing over. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a short little hour or so long game, but it's only 3 bucks. I think the price is right here for what it offers, though only just so.

There's quite a few little bugs with the game that you're almost certain to find. Only one bug I found could potentially be game breaking. But most of them are things like using an axe on something and then using it again and getting the same message in spite of nothing being there.

In terms of gameplay it's very much just a simple graphic adventure game. Your oldschool style of pick everything up and only half of it will be useful and some of it redundantly so. My biggest complaint with the game would be that the art style maybe emulates old games a little too much and sometimes something that seems completely benign and irrelevant is actually something you can pick up or smash.

But I'd say that I'm partially ok with this since you're 100% able to complete the game even if you miss several explore-able areas. So if you're only after game completion then it shouldn't bother you. But if you want to see everything it can be mildly frustrating. I'm not sure if it was intentional to be retro-like or if the art design or scene design is to blame, but I don't really care for it regardless.

I also found it a little annoying that using an item doesn't change the cursor so it's very hard to tell if you're actually using it. The fact that the game won't throw an 'I can't use that with that!' message also makes you wonder if you have the item selected or if that's just not what you're supposed to do so yeah.

Overall I'd say it's a good solid oldschool style of game, but I think that in itself is very flawed since we have lots of modern methods to alleviate oldschool game problems. With that said I'm left wanting more.

Recently I've tried getting into visual novels (I tried out Doki Doki literature club and like nekopara and one other) and I found those to be awful and boring. I was beginning to think that I didn't like reading or stories in general. Well this game certainly changed my mind and now I know why I didn't like those games. It's because with a visual novel you just kinda watch things and read, but with a graphic adventure game you get to explore things closer especially in this style.

I should've suspected as much since I'd played shadowgate on the NES and loved that game when I was a kid. I just thought I was getting jaded or something though. No. That wasn't it at all. I'm really glad to find out that's not the case.


So anyway I really hope to see more of this type of game and if you have tried a visual novel and thought "Ew, this sucks.", but you're still looking for something slower paced and adventure oriented then this might be a cheap way to find out if you like graphic adventure games.\t\t\t\t\t
