\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom Beyond Prologue is one of the best games that I played this year. It’s a really good point and click adventure game with a strong atmosphere, beautiful pixel graphics, and challenging puzzles. The game was developed by one person who put a lot of effort and love into making of this game. The story of the game is about a guy who adventures into mountains in search of lost village and after his arrival things start to go south.

Pros:
-The game cost is really cheap. For $3 you’re getting a lot of value from this game.
-Looks beautiful with the pixelated graphics. The pixelated art style and the pixel palettes look well-thought out.
-Strong atmosphere with Lovecraftian vibe, where your slowly are getting deep into the unknown. Theirs blood and dead bodies around the village which further add up to the atmosphere.
-The music is also great. Their about 4 soundtracks and they all sound great when they play.
-Puzzles feel both challenging and fun when you solve them.
-Theirs multiple endings.
-The game creator William, is interactive and friendly in the discussion.

Cons (mostly surface and preference problems):
-Your inventory gets clustered with items and letters after some time. Theirs, no way to throw out the letters in order to better organize your inventory or at least have a separate inventory for letters so that everything doesn’t get clustered.
-The items in the inventory are represented with letters of their names and don’t have pictures to resemble them. I think it would have been better to see the items with their pictures rather than read them out.
-The language used for the dialogue and texts is taken from English language of 1910. The reason why this type of old dialect was taken was probably to enhance the experience of the setting in which the main protagonist lives in. However, it ends up feeling more confusing to read and consume time to retranslate all the texts in my mind. Just wish everything was written in modern English.

My final score for the game is 9.5/10
From Beyond Prologue is a masterpiece in my eyes, even with some problems there’s a lot of fun to get from this game. I highly recommend anyone to give it a try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a masterpiece and an act of love. It has so much atmosphere, the graphics is so good. The story is interesting. I can't comment on the puzzle quality yet. But what matters is that you really feel like an explorer that is uncovering a mystery and your life is at stake.

Other good points is that you don't have those silly constant deaths like in uninvited or shadowgate. and there does not seem to be a time limit, so you can take your time to explore and relax (so to speak).

Did I say the graphics is good? It is very good, not that sketchy pixel art that you see in low budget games. I suggest to play with Reshade and the HQ4x filter, with which the graphics becomes much better. (Example: https://i.imgur.com/C0FN07h.png). (this is something that you need to install separately though)

The only minor drawbacks are the lack of a map and the annoying slide-in effect you have to wait for each time you look at something.\t\t\t\t\t
