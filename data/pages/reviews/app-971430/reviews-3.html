\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA pretty fun (if not a bit short) retro style point and click game (that gives a lot of the same vibes as those old text based adventures). The story is pretty interesting too and there are various ways your character can die (as well as a couple different endings). I did have some troubles finding all the readable items, but the challenge was pretty fun. A part I really enjoyed is the fact that there are a few key items that you are given multiple chances to get (such as the candles, cutting items, silver ingots, etc), so if you miss it in one area, chances are you'll see it in another area and be able to grab it. That makes it so you can get to the ending of the game pretty easily and without too much trouble. I actually didn't realize I had missed a few locations until I did my second run of the game. Spoilers for a different main ending (it's still the same basic ending but the text is different) Save the game when you get passed the pillar puzzle, and instead of talking to him for a second time, use the Cross on him ;)

Overall, it's a pretty good creepy and weird game. Length-wise, the game felt pretty short but it is price pointed very well, although if you didn't struggle as I did, you could get it done in 1-2 hours, I'd assume (so it might be better to wait for a sale on such a short game, but personally, I liked the weird story and retro feel, so it's worth the $2.99). I definitely recommend this game, and I really hope that the dev does come out with a sequel, since I'd love to know what happens next!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite a fun little point and click adventure. It draws inspiration from the old NES ports of Shadowgate, Deja Vu, and Uninvited. If you remembered those games fondly or like these sorts of point-and-click adventure games, it's a good buy for the $2-3 it sells for.

Two problems I did have but were relatively minor. The map boxes can be difficult to know where they head to which can lead to some confusing moving around in the town. Once you reach the town the exploration is largely open-ended (more like Deja Vu) that can lead to aimless wandering about trying to find out what to do next.

Deaths are entertaining though sadly there aren't too many of them (about a third of them are variants of being killed by wolves before reaching the town).

Overall, I'd recommend it. I had a good bit of fun with it.\t\t\t\t\t
