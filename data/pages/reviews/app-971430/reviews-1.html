\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom what I understand of the unique genre that is the point-and-click adventure games that made their debut back in the mid-80's when the transition from extremely-basic text adventures were given something of an upgrade via the inclusion of actual graphics to further immerse players into the world of said game, this then later gave way to several masters of the video gaming art that specialized in this role including the obvious Sierra studio alongside another, lesser known, company that made a small number of quite fascinating point-and-click adventure games that would latter be fondly called 'Macadventure' games.

From Beyond is a product that seeks to replicate the good old days from way back with a new mystery of sorts to boot, with how the story is at the forefront of gameplay here as you immerse yourself into a new world of many secrets waiting to be found alongside even more dangerous threats, be they natural or otherwise, and is very much reminiscent of ICOM Simulations' oldest titles from over 35 years ago! (Indeed, the Déjà Vu duology remains a personal favourite of mines, even compared to newer entries inspired by it such as Infested and Spectacle, although From Beyond's full potential does come rather close to the originals at that!)

It is why then, as much as it pains me to say this, that this is just a 'demo' of what will, some day, be the entry point to a much bigger, far more ambitious, adventure game that truly lives up to it's name and truly excites the niche gaming audience that really appreciates a good story and world to delve into for a solid dozen hours or so.

Unfortunately, despite having enjoyed the experience for the two and a half hours that I spent (mostly) blindly going through it and seeing things firsthand, with the remaining four hours going back for the usual achievement hunting, I couldn't help but feel somewhat disappointed upon finding out that the full game has, to this day, not been released or even announced as of yet, which is saying something when you look back at From Beyond Prologue's original release date in December of 2018, more than four years having passed since then.

It is perhaps also possible that my experience was soured later on when I came across some trouble with one of the more obscure achievements for this game, Learned Scholar, whereby I had to restart the game multiple times to get every single book and note (including two that you are locked out of past a certain point early into the game!) and it would not register for me despite manually reading them in the inventory myself! Perhaps this was because I had accidentally triggered a death through clicking past the textbox into a map entry point that led to an unexpected demise that I already knew of before (another issue that could be fixed for the full release) but it did leave me frustrated regardless. (And yes, that includes the 'Cellar Note' for those wondering, despite needing to delve a little 'dangerously' to get it!)

To give credit where it is due, I did do my research to see if the game's development process had been been abandoned or not and, from what I can tell, the staff who made this demo are still hard at work on the full title, albeit circumstances relating to the pandemic of past alongside other circumstances that have troubled the main developer have made the process of developing From Beyond proper much more difficult and slower than anticipated.

It is why, having originally going into this review with the mindset of giving this a negative review, that I was convinced just enough to change it into a hesitant positive review, although I must warn those who are currently seeking a full game experience that it is still nowhere to be found in sight currently and I would recommend those people wait until From Beyond has been released fully if they do not wish to be disappointed in any manner. (Those who are happy to wait, however, can safely ignore my personal warning and enjoy what is, in all fairness, a pretty promising experience that is marred with it's short length and that 'hook' that is currently not yet pulling it's fans into the full title as of yet!)

Alas, From Beyond Prologue was worth the time spent, brief as it was, however it would be disappointing to see all the effort and dedication into this indie game that desperately needs that *one* title to bring new eyes into the potential of such a genre that could help to expand more interest over time, now that we have more modern tech to work with developing these games alongside decades of game design experience that's out there for young and aspiring developers to learn from in what makes a game FUN to play, whatever the genre may be and as niche a genre as it may be!

TLDR Version:

• Unique use of old MS-DOS graphics from the mid-80's to encapsulate the memorable experience of old 'Macadventure' point-and-click adventure games (+)
• Immersive world that is quite good at engaging the player with slowly introducing it's lore in both direct and subtle ways (+)
• Small details that are hidden in various ways, be they as large as entire new rooms or as little as nods to other adventure games, nicely presented as secrets for the player to optionally discover (+)
• The horror aspect of this game is nicely balanced in such a manner that it is not too tame so as to come across as boring or censored nor is it too intense and terrifying that it'll scare off less-seasoned gamers (+)

• From Beyond's demo is quite short and can easily be completed in less than two hours for anyone who is remotely experience with such games, with replayability being limited due to the small scale of the demo (-)
• The full game is not out and does not appear to be with a release date anytime soon, limiting the potential of what is currently a rather promising 'Macadventure' inspired game (-)
• A couple of solutions to the game's few puzzles aren't as straightforward as one might like, requiring finding some items that are not so obvious due to blending into the background or hitboxes for using items on being particularly small (-)
• One achievement in particular, Learned Scholar, appears to be bugged and is very difficult to register it being unlocked despite doing it all in one sitting (-)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom beyond prologue is a homage to the classic macventure series known for its point and click style with a distinct unease in all of the games, which from beyond delivers a brand fresh new story!

The gameplay honestly lives up decently well, its a simple point and click with puzzles to solve and lots of areas to explore, the positives of the gameplay style all the macventure games and this has, are that every room feels like there's something to do, and there's enough clues and hinting to find your way around, but this game has a particular issue in that elements sometimes aren't made apparent to the player the graphics feel too cluttered and more emphasis should be made on objects that are able to be picked up, i missed several key items and got stuck for periods at a time, but during the story and actual threats in the game it felt insanely intense, probably gave me the biggest chills out of any point and click i've tried so i give it huge props for that.

The graphics as i mentioned feel too cluttered at times, i think more of the border could be sacrificed to make the game window bigger offering for more detail on each screen, but everything else looks pretty good, its not a problem of the graphics being bad, because everything which is clear and visible looks the part (especially the more eldritch looking things) i just wished everything that is intended to be clear is fully clear beyond any doubt, other than that, the game has a very eerie way of doing graphics, blood, indescribable ooze and rooms with foreboding space to add to the unsettling feel which really makes the game a great homage to the macventure series.

The music has some choppy loops which absolutely needs to be fixed, but for the music itself has that vibe all the previous games do, its cosy sounding when you're safe, absolutely ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ terrifying when you're in danger, the death music is one of my favourites for drilling home that "its all over" feel, i absolutely love the music when its without its glitchy side effects, however. And this is a BIG however, there is something absolutely irredeemable with 1 particular sound effect, its for when an eldritch being is telepathically contacting you, it plays a loud jarring beep and flashes the screen, first off i don't really appreciate the jumpscare and second off, WHY IS IT SO DAMN LOUD!? legit it hurt my ears and made me physically jolt to yank my earphones out, i was less than impressed with it and it comes up pretty frequently throughout the game, in the end i had to simply turn down all the game volume and i think this sound effect is near 10 decibels louder than the rest of the game, genuinely this needs to be removed entirely, the jumpscare graphic of being contacted is fine, but that sound effect on people who seriously blast their music and headphones could cause genuine damage; do not ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ do this in video games, its not funny nor is it safe on peoples ears.

Overall a good nod to the macventure games, it is still the prologue and there's a healthy bit of content although i feel the entire game revolves around a single puzzle with several smaller ones linking into it, the game has its ups and downs, im very very split down the middle for whether i like the game or not but due to it still having some good life to it, i give it a pass, but i will say: that jumpscare sound single handedly put this game astonishingly close to being not recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
