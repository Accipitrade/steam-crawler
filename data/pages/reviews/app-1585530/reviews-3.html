\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really really like the idea of having a massively functional, basically fully intractable weapons system. But the way it was executed needs a lot of work. The way to pick items up is absolutely terrible and its so difficult to reload and chamber weapons. An example would be the mp5 being so difficult to load. The idea of being able to manipulate all objects from all angles is really nice but its a nuisance at times when trying to do the simplest things like reloading or picking something off from the ground.

Pros - MASSIVE WEAPON CHOICES
- MASSIVE ATTACHMENT CHOICES
- Multiple gamemodes keeps the game interesting
- Extremely customizable

Neutral comments
- UI is pretty meh, simple but frequent problems with spawning items bc they clip through the platform.
- Npc battles are pretty tough in the aspect of preparations for them, difficult time spawning guns in, but idea is good.
- Some of the handling on the guns are very finicky, mainly with heavier guns but makes sense. A little too much imo.
- You really have to get down on the ground to pick up things which some ppl may like, some may not.

Cons
- Reloading is very difficult, especially with guns with small charging handles, I.E mp5, ak
- Trying to position your hands on a different part of a weapon is very difficult.
- Its a hassle having to constantly be respawning ammo, maybe an option to have infinite in storage.
- The things you store in the belt/storage area are still rendered as solid objects, it collides with your gun if you have it near that area and havent holstered it.

TLDR: Good idea, back execution. Polish up first, then add new things.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI used an Index headset and controllers while playing this game.
I really wanted to give this game a shot after looking at the store page and finding out that it has multiplayer.. I'm not sure if I'm just spoiled from years of H3VR but this game was just not enjoyable for me. The idea[i/] of a physics based gun sandbox is great and a good contrast to the (as default) cartoon-y physics in H3VR, but the implementation just isn't there for me right now.
The guns feel awkward to hold and use, and while it's nice that you can grip the fore of a gun at any position, it also makes it difficult to quickly manipulate the gun. Using parts of the gun while holding it was a royal pain, grabbing charging handles and mags just did not feel good. The difficulty with manipulating the gun and the way the in game "quick menu" was postioned leads me to believe the developer did not create the game with Index controllers in mind, which is a shame because using the finger tracking could greatly help a game such as this.
I tried the Castle Defense level... it felt like a Unity asset cash grab and left a very poor taste in my mouth (figuratively, not literally).
I cannot reccomend this game at its current price point. Maybe for $5 USD to check in on it after some more updates, but not at its current price of $10.99 USD.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor what is here, this has a lot of promise. This isn't near the polish as H3VR (Lower your expectations people, especially since it's been out for a month!) I wasn't feeling this would be as fun as I was expecting, since this was very much feeling like one of those cash grab asset flips that you usually see. The video on the store page also doesn't give it much of a good impression for what's really available to the player.

Pros: Guns feel punchy, as they should. This isn't like a basic shooter for VR where they simulate the movies. The recoil system is a bit basic, but they aren't terribly off on some of the guns. However, there are a few weapons that seem to kick less than I imagine, especially the shotguns. And yes, you can do an HK slap.

Physics based interactions can be extremely difficult and performance heavy. I remained at a Solid 60 the entire time though doing a couple unintended stress tests, as yes, physics can be jank, and there's quite a bit. Not as much as I was expecting. The only thing that seemed to be a big bug to me was holding the gun with the left head while letting go of the right hand sometimes broke the gun, especially when letting go and watching it fall through the floor. All in all, seems stable enough.

Functionality with firearm attachments are pretty nice. You can physically zero optics, bipods are WONDERFUL, you can grip anywhere on the gun to brace it, and attachment system that is similar to H3, what's not to like?!? Only think we're missing here is a good virtual stock system and making the weapon weights a bit more consistent, or at least make some of the ARs a tiny less heavy as not all of these guns are going to be extremely heavy.

Cons: Oversights are the best thing that comes to mind when we're talking about divers ways to arm a weapon since there's so many. For examble, the FAMAS G2 in the game has the classic F1 rail still present, and the charging handle is where'd you'd expect it. Good luck getting your fingers on it though, as collision with your hands and the weapon hate each other. Guns with similar charging handles are on the same playing field, making them near impossible to use. There needs to be a bigger active field where you can grab the charging handle on these weapons: FAMAS, G36(Every variant, including the SL8) and anything with those charging handles.

I talked about guns bugging out and falling out of the world, so, I'll just say please look into this. Another bugging out thing is the LMG, specifically the belt feds. Sometimes, not often, the box mag will become immune to interaction, including collision, and you cannot reload the weapon no more, breaking it completely. Only happened on the M249, but the rest seemed to be fine.

The weight on these guns seems to be odd at times. I said the shotguns have small kick, but they all have weight like a truck. Including some ARs. The MK18 they have in the main menu are and tutorial seem to be a bit heavy for a 5.56 gun. I own a 6.8mm AR15, and I don't even sway as much with it when shooting it. The smoothing also is a problem, as there's too much smoothing on moving the guns around. Please, change these to be more appropriate so I don't default to one handing a Degale all the time, since all the pistols are extremely light. (That includes the .50AE degale.)

Not enough content (For now) for the player to engage in. There's a war game(capture zone, with only one zone, hostage situation (Haven't tried the rest of these yet... I messed with the guns as they're my favorite thing here X'D), Squid game which could be fun with friends(haven't tested multiplayer), a few ranges, and to be coming sandbox maps. I might have missed one more gamemode, but I can't remember if there was another. This as anyone could imagine could get dry fast, but again, there's more to come and it's only been over a month in early access.

Last con, we need differing sounds for a lot of the guns. There is a lot of differences between the AR/AK sounds, but majority fall under that idea (Being Russian or American rounds that is...) And perhaps some rework on the sounds as there was an attempt to add reverb as to simulate how loud they get, but you can hear the weird lazer like distortion on some automatics.

I'm hoping the devs keep this going, and it seems they are still working strong on this, adding and fixing things every few fridays (Seeing in the news feed) and have a road map that so far as I can tell is being followed. Also, mods would boost this game's life a lot. (Get it now as it's only around $11 USD)

Edit: Also multiplayer is... interesting. I snagged the game for my friend and he became the gun entity. Every gun he would grab just got attached to him. It is very beta multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(Played on a Valve Index with Index controllers and an RTX 3080)...(I'm now broke)

Definitely a tentative recommendation based on first couple hours impressions, but I think this sandbox is worth $10. It is VERY rough early access, but it's undeniable that the gun models are the best I've ever seen in a VR game. I looked inside the Sig M17 and it is identical to the inside of my real one. I was actually kind of shocked it wasn't just smooth in there.

Is the entire ecosystem around these amazing gun models better than H3? No. It's way easier to just pick up a gun and start shooting in H3. Grabbing and collision interactions with hands are difficult at best and unusable at worst. It can be very hard to grab what you want to, and once you put attachments on it's even harder. Also the whole game seems to be designed for Oculus controllers. I could barely figure out some of the controls with my Index controllers.

But the most important thing is shooting feeling great, and it does. Realistic recoil, punchy sound. It really feels like a gun is going off in your hand much more than other shooting sandbox games I've played. And this has humanoid targets. You can get your dream shotgun, kit it out with lasers and sights and grips, and clear procedurally generated rooms. No dumb sausages jumping around in this one. That's a huge selling point.

It's bare bones for sure, but it shows a lot of promise. If this game could combine with Hard Bullet, that would be my absolute perfect VR shooter. Hopefully this game gets better in those areas where it's currently lacking right now. But for the price, I think it's worth the money for an early access title here.\t\t\t\t\t
