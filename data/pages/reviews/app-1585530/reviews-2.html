\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEN:

Although the game has a lot of annoying bugs and needs to be fixed, I will put them aside for now as it is early access. In general, they have accomplished things such as weapon use, gun sounds, weapon variety, hit feeling, bullet recoil really well. I had a difficult time using the weapons, a serious improvement is needed in the game mechanics part. Likewise, after getting information on discord, I heard that there was a bug in the loadout system which where you put your guns at. They told me that they are aware of it and im pretty sure it will be fixed after some updates. GunWorld VR is a sandbox game, you can combat with enemies if you want. There are several different game modes. If you want, you can only target shooting in the arena. As I mentioned before, apart from the difficulties in using weapons, the feeling of shooting and sound effects are really satisfying. The variety of weapons and the number of attachments that can be attached to the weapons are also at a satisfactory level. The developers of the game bring updates frequently, and these updates usually contain new weapons, items, basically content. If the bugs and gameplay problems are fixed, I think this game can reach very good places in the realistic shooting category.

7.5/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game may be better later, but as of right now, this is not where it should be to be a worthwhile pickup for VR gun enthusiasts.

I always wondered why VR games used default grab positions on items (Mags, guns, etc) and it's because without them, it's absolutely horrible to interact with things. Mags will be positioned so that your hand has to contort to load it into the gun, you'll grab the gun by the handle, but it won't count as being "the right grip" to fire, so the trigger will do nothing until you re-grip the handle. Guns feel simultaneously "floaty" and "sticky". I've seen people talk about the weight to the guns, and I agree, but the downside is that everything feels muddy and sluggish, not exactly realism, but it's more realistic than one-handing a massive sniper and having it flick around like it clipped into a wall in Gmod.

The magwell will not receive a mag no matter how precise it looks, you actually have better luck reloading by slapping the mag as fast as your hand will move into the magwell and hope that it sticks this time. These may be the single LEAST satisfying reloads I've ever experienced in a VR shooter. These are actually even worse than when VR games had just begun to come out, and nobody knew how to make reloading satisfying. This is worse than when H3VR had literally just hit steam and everyone was trying to figure out how to reload with vive wands, but even worse.

This game just feels... not well considered. As a small example, the wrist menu that's standard for most VR games, is not on your wrist, it's on the bottom side of your hand. Like, not on your palm... but like pinky-side of your hand. Try it, just try and look at that part of your hand, and keep it just far enough away to read an options menu, and point your other hand at it... and if you're not double jointed, tell me if that's comfortable in the slightest.

After shooting some zombies in the empty box room, I didn't really want to do anything more in the game. The guns are well modeled, but they all sound almost exactly the same. They all feel pretty much... the same. The attachments are cool, but when putting on foregrips is more forgiving than even reloading, there may be an issue. Every gun I tried had this issue, it was so bad that I threw most of my guns on the floor to use the only one that could kill zombies in any reasonable timescale. Only to find out, that I could just walk past all of them and they'd not be able to hit me. But... I guess that's to compensate for them spawning on every side of you, constantly.

Also holsters are wildly inconsistent too. Especially making mags respawn, you'll have one under your hand, and it will toggle the one next to it too, for no reason. It's frustrating, and some guns just kind of can't go in certain holsters? I tried for about 5 minutes to put a deagle in the far left holster, and it wouldn't work. HK mag? That was fine. After playing a bad puzzle game to get all the holsters setup, I realized that it was faster to just one hand weapons, because reloading was such a hassle.

Man, I wanted to like this game. It should've been obvious that almost every clip from the trailer of the game had them one handing ARs, but it just felt so janky and bad that it made me want a VR Physics shooter sandbox that's not H3VR even more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a really cool concept, and If I'm to be honest, I kinda like this game as it is now. It's unique, so I couldn't quite say that H3VR is a 100% direct substitute to this game, because this game is sort of going for a different way. It's like Pavlov vs. Onward, sure, they're both VR multiplayer shooters designed with an objective, but Onward is focused on the more realistic side of war. Pavlov is all about running and gunning as far as something like SnD is concerned, or all about that Hide gamemode and Prop Hunt, basically the goofy side. GunWorld VR is probably more focused towards the Onward side of that spectrum, (if I had to pick one).
That being said, I will refund this game, because I have another game just like it that I play more often... H3VR. I'd think of this game as more of an investment. Sure, this game just isn't quite there, for example, it's really difficult to grab something as simple as the charging handle, but I believe in this game to go far, and for those who got it now to be laughing at those who got it then, since I'm almost confident price will go up. And, when that day comes, I'll gladly take part as the laughingstock! This game just needs a little more content and polish I'd say. Also, there's multiplayer, one of the most interesting aspects of this game! But... the servers seem dead. I know there's AI, but I'm not particularly interested in that.

TL;DR: I like the game, but I'm refunding it because I play H3VR more. The game is cool, but needs a little more polish and content. It also has multiplayer, but the servers seem dead.
Give this game a shot if you'd like, but don't try to expect too much out of it for now. Worst case scenario, refund the game like I did, and see where it goes.\t\t\t\t\t
