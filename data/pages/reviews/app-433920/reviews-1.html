\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the complexity of the game, with numerous side quests that you have to come back to. Some people might not like this, as it does mean a lot of walking back and forth to locations, and while the mist portals do make this easier it can get tedious. There are lots of little gimmicks and things to find or improve on, and each character has things to find for them. I loved the involvement of the Cheekis in particular, I thought it was very clever to hide them in parts of the scenery, some were really well hidden, but not so hidden that it felt impossible.
I found the storyline to be quite funny and fun to play, however it did start to drop in quality as the game went on. The switch from playing a hero to someone who identifies as a villain was a nice departure from the rest of the series. There are a lot of characters and while I do love the options that provides, I feel like there could have been more exploration of the characters, rather than adding more.
I loved the towns, minus the Rawvyn (there's not much there), as each of them felt complex and individual, and were fun to explore. Similar thing with the other areas, sometimes it was really hard to keep track of things, and it felt as though they were really big, which as someone who loves exploring is a plus for me, but others might not be so keen on it. However the world map felt really small and cramped. There weren't really many places to explore, but I guess balancing that out the locations available felt really fleshed out.
I finished the game wanting to play more, I really enjoyed it. It took me about 40hrs to complete fully, although you could do it a lot quicker if you aren't as fussy about not missing anything, and if you aren't someone who is slow like me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis has had a massive facelift from its predecessor and is in many ways vastly better. If you haven't played any Aveyond games before, start here. There is virtually no overlap between the stories so you're missing nothing, and Aveyond 3 was such a drag I never finished it.

For those unfamiliar with the series, this is an RPG that focuses heavily on fetch quests, with a fairly simple combat system. If you love being a master battle tactician, this is not the game for you. There is a really impressive depth of quests here, and most of the characters have their own individual journeys to explore. There is a much greater sense of getting to know the characters. Robin's shop was a particularly good add-on.

A few points to decide whether this game is for you:
- It focuses on fetch quests, not fighting. However, I was impressed with the way that the very simple combat system was expanded with new options, for example being able to equip Hi'beru with books that gave him different spells. Another very clever touch was that Robin's abilities depend on who else is in the active party.
- There is a lot of walking backwards and forwards, so some patience is required. The amount of pointless walking has been cut back massively from Aveyond 3 (thank goodness) but travelling is still lumpy in places. The maze-like quality of maps has also reduced to an extent, but some of the early areas may be frustrating if you don't make a quick sketch.
- It uses a humorous storyline rather than going for the serious take of its predecessor. Personally I enjoyed this; it gave it a fresh angle and plenty of originality. The ending was weak and very abrupt, but much of the story's purpose comes from following the journeys of the characters anyway, so saving the world is an afterthought.
- A very detailed free guide is available online, so there's no getting stuck working out what to do.

Overall, a solid recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t16 hours in and still not done!
This game is very typical of it's genre, and if you've played the other Aveyond games you know what to expect:
Turn based battle, lots of walking back and forth between places, fetch quests galore and protagonists full of quippy one liners. My only gripe is that the fast travel is a bit more annoying than in other Aveyond games, in that it still requires you to physically traverse maps to get to some places, and I haven't been able to find "fast walking boots" like there are in some of the other games. The battle mechanics aren't the most sophisticated I've come across, and even though I play on the easiest setting on purpose I have never really been even close to losing a battle, which maybe means the easy setting is a bit too overpowered.
Other than that the story is fun, the maps are really well done.
A really relaxing way to spend a couple of hours without taxing the ole noggin too hard.\t\t\t\t\t

Robin is a straight up idiot — basically a dumb blonde. Hiberu is pretty wimpy and clueless too. If the devs want to make a game in which women are the winners... fine. Also feels like it was made to sell the strategy guide. Like the swordsinger thing... Very non-obvious that you would backtrack so hard to get it started up.

Only time where I dropped so much time into a game, only to decide that the gameplay at the end was just too tedious to continue.\t\t\t\t\t
