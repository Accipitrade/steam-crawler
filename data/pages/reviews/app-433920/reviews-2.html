\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, Aveyond 4 ended up being a nice, unexpected find for me. If you tend to enjoy humorous, top-down, turn-based RPGs, I'd recommend looking into it.

Boyle and the rest of the cast you gather are enjoyable and all play a little differently--only one character has a traditional MP gauge, and another can swap out their combat skills be equipping different tomes, for example. Each of them bring some value to the story, but they are also given additional side quests that focus more specifically on them, which is something I appreciated. The earliest characters you get naturally have the most time to shine, but even the later ones have interesting subplots and personal quests to build on them. All of them get nice art portraits for a full range of emotions, which benefits the storytelling and comedy delivery.

The world and dungeons are relatively scenic and nice to look at, but several of them felt bigger than they need to be. This emptiness was compounded by the fact that many groups of enemies don't respawn, so there's a lot of open terrain once you clear foes out (admittedly, both a good and bad thing in my book). There are some missions and sidequests that involve backtracking to old areas, but the game does offer additional modes of transportation. It alleviates some of the tedium of returning to areas after you've thoroughly cleaned them out, but not entirely (one mode still involves a decent bit of walking around to get where you need to go).

The music and sound effects are generally solid, though many of the areas are filled with ambient noise tracks instead of music. I personally liked this choice as a change of pace (though I did start noticing the same sounds again and again in some areas, like wolf howls at night). I also ran into some sound glitches where music cut out (often after combat), and the only way to bring it back was to exit the area and return.

I played the game on its hard setting, and I ultimately finished it in about 38 hours. You should consider this mark to be on the -very- high end of playtime, as I tend to take my sweet time going through RPGs and try to do everything I possibly can. Some of that time was due to a number of quests being convoluted; I had to wander for a while until I could find some of the tools that Ingrid the Witch uses to make her potions, for example. There's also a little bit of additional replay value, as you're offered some choices in the game that affect the character's interactions and the battle skills they get.

I realize it's hard to recommend games nowadays when it feels like everyone has a backlog going through the roof. There's a lot of options out there. But I feel that Aveyond 4 showcases a lot of passion from its developers and offers a versatile experience (that also has some fun poking at the common tropes without getting -too- cheeky about it). I definitely enjoyed my time with the game, and I'd give it a thought if you're looking for another RPG to add to your collection.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the Aveyond series! This was a welcome addition to the Aveyond series and was a welcome return to their previous all in one game format (as opposed to breaking out the game into several parts/chapters like they did with Aveyond 3). However the game is far from the best Aveyond game and it has some drawbacks.

This is a pixel graphic RPG and if you've liked other Aveyond games or other similar RPGs you will likely enjoy this one too. It has a pretty good story and lots of humor (much of it's a little corny but in a good way... usually). I love that the militant squirrels made a more prominent return. The story starts off strong but it begins to lag in the middle. The end is a little bit of a let down. It gets pulled in a lot of different directions which gets a little difficult to follow making it sometimes difficult to figure out where you need to go to progress to the next part in the story. There is a vast number of side quests available but one of the drawbacks is that main quests and side quests all involve an excessive amount of back and forth. I don't like these kind of games to be completely linear but keeping a large number of quests relatively close to each other in the same region is generally preferable. It feels like you are walking back and forth for almost everything and you have to do a lot of backtracking. This is made worse by the slow walking pace (which can't be adjusted and unlike other games there are no speed boots that let you walk faster) and lack of quick teleport system. There is an odd array of mist portals and later ship ports but it doesn't feel much shorter to use them... you will walk back and forth a lot. Not quite as bad as Aveyond 3 but definitely more-so than other Aveyond games.

The graphics are good for pixel RPG style. There are great backgrounds with lots of detail and variations for different areas. The sprites are good and there's plenty of variation with little repeat. The animations are about what you'd expect for the style. Most of the Character portraits are good but there are some odd looking parts and it feels like some character portraits were given a lot of time and artistry while others feel lower quality, maybe rushed? But they are still good. The small animations when the characters are happy vs. unhappy are cute.

The sound is pretty good. There's decent background music that's different for the various areas. Some of the battle sounds are really weird but most of them are fine. The squirrels in the castle are super weird sounding.

The menu interface is a bit different compared to other aveyond games. If this is your first Aveyond game you will probably not have an issue but if you played other Aveyond games you might find it confusing at first and it may require a bit of getting used to.

Personally, I like to explore all areas and complete every quest so I found it very frustrating that there appeared to be areas I never could get to. There are two islands that are covered in a bubble and little lands to their sides. I could never find a way to access them even though I'm sure it's insanely easy or something. I could pull my ship up to the little islands but nut not enter the dungeon cells. I'm certain there's more that I could have done but it's difficult to figure out how or where I needed to go to do it and because there's already so much back and forth in non-straightforward ways it gets confusing where you've searched already or not. I saved before the final battle so I might have to go back and try again to access those other areas.

I liked the addition of an item that helps you find all the goodie caves. It saved me a lot of time and clicks testing out every wall for those hidden entrances. I didn't like that you don't really have a character house at any point where you can all go and rest for free. You must rely on inns the entire time throughout the game which is a bit different than other Aveyond games. I also found the store that you get later in the game was somewhat entertaining but also pretty useless... why couldn't we have just sold the items to vendors like all the other Aveyond games instead of having unsaleable junk that you can only get rid of once you get a store.

Though there appears to be more I could have done, I reached made it to the end (exploring everything I could finishing every side quest I could, and buying everything I could find) in about 14 hours. If you are buying it at full price that works out to about $1/hr of gameplay which is pretty good. If you can get it on sale though t's an even better deal.\t\t\t\t\t
