The developers saw Adrift and wanted to add guns, more oxygen, and more boost so that you're not a floating,desperate Steven Hawkings in space. The graphics are good and I like the controls. I did not get nauseous playing the game. When you rotate, the game limits your view angle which is nice. I can't say this for Adrift.

The issue I have is that the tutorial needs to be more clear about the controls somehow. I'm not sure how but I didn't initially understand that the left controller is to move, while the right is for rotation. Also didn't realize that you have to push the Track pad on the right controller. I actually stopped playing this for a while because I wasn't patient enough to figure things out for myself. The game doesn't hold your hand enough. Speaking of that, I also don't know where I'm supposed to go for mission objectives, I'm having to guess which kills the fun.

Still, I like the game despite it's flaws. I'm glad I got this on sale despite the Mixed review rating.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not sure if I should post a positive or negative review for this game. It seems like solid game, just not built for VR experience.

Pros:
This game gives a solid 'man in space' feel (as long as you do not look inside man made structures). Overal atmosphere is solid.

Cons:
1. Movement. You can't push yourself of surfaces, you can't use environment to decelerate, you can't attach yourself to surfaces. I might have a relatively high 'VR in space' movement standards, but I feel like these ones are definition of what movement in space should be like.
2. VR games in general tend to favor interactions with environment, in this game there are no VR-like interactions. You see container with oxygen - you look at it and press 'A', it just changes color to dark and your tank indicates that it got more gas. Items that you pick up with 'A' just disapear. You click on a panel and item appears. There are no on-screen buttons. Lack of interaction is notiecable and immersion breaking.
3. Controls. These are not controls for VR spacesuit, but controls made for keyboard and mouse that were mapped to controllers. While I agree with overal control type (you need to engage thrusters to rotate or move), I think controls (at least for oculus controllers) could have been done better.
4. Environment. Most structures do not give a feel of something made for space and do not add properly to environment. Lots of unused surfaces, bare walls and ceilings, most things are uselesly huge (I understand that spacesuit is large and need large passages, but not everything). Not enough 'fillers' be that toilets, tables, suit locker, equipment or whatewer else. Example: the most defining feature of airlock are two large screen (one with vent below) and how bare it is: no railings, no places to lock cargo, no ports where suits were supposed to connect their safeties or life support, no override ports or notiecable access hatches.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've beaten the game and I cannot recommend it in its current state, but it's close to being quite good.
Pros:
-Good graphics
-Great music!
-Good puzzle solving elements.

Cons:
-Poor controls: I played with Arcade controls (which is supposedly easier) and it's frustrating how loose the controls are. I understand it's suppose to simulate being in space, but when there are timed puzzles where you have to navigate through tight corridors, you end up bumping into objects and overshooting your targets a LOT.
-Lack of checkpoints: There is a part where you have to collect energy cells in space and escort a companion's escape pod, where if you die for whatever reason (i.e. killed by drone or run out of oxygen), then you'll have to repeat the fetch quest from scratch, which is super time consuming. As well, there's a timed reactor core mission where if you die, you have to repeat parts of the fetch quest, which is frustrating. About half of my play time is in this area...
-Lack of objective pointers: It's so easy to get disoriented and miss collecting a mission objective, which leads to a lot of back tracking.

Overall, I cannot recommend this game unless it goes on sale or if the developers fix these issues.\t\t\t\t\t
