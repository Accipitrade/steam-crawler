Admittedly I ended up saving the game with very little oxygen left and ended up having to start the first level from the beginning, but that's a definite possibility for anyone.

This is a space simulation based on Newtonian physics and with 6-DOF. The basic premise is that you have ended up in a wreck in space and you need to carry out various tasks to get yourself out of the situation. These tasks generally involve flying from place to place, gathering fuel and oxygen, and pushing buttons. This doesn't sound all that exciting but when you add in the 6-DOF and control systems it is a very fun and challenging game. The game is not big on hand-holding and one of the big things that is slowing me down is trying to figure out what some of the parts of the game want from me. I found myself at one point screaming "HOW THE ♥♥♥♥ DO I EJECT?" at the game repeatedly until I realised that you press the right trackpad, that's just one example. Just after that I found myself screaming "WHAT THE ♥♥♥♥? THIS IS NOT FUN!" over and over because it wasn't, it was just stressful, but it was short and I got through it. You'll know the bit when you see it.

There is quite a bit of talk about how this game has the potential to be quite vomit-inducing and I would say that if you are prone to motion-sickness this is not the game for you. I have never felt motion sick in VR so the idea that this might be the first to induce it had me buying the game as soon as I saw it. So far I'm fine. It is quite dizzying but I haven't felt the urge to blow chunks just yet.

The controls are interesting. I am playing on Vive with the controllers and in that setup the left controller controls the thrust vector of the suit irrespective of which direction you are actually facing and the right controller controls roll. The right hand controller requires you to squeeze the trigger and then rotate your wrist in the direction you want to roll. This is a very immersive means of control but is pretty hard on the wrist. Mine is absolutely twanging today. Combining the controls to guide yourself through the puzzles (some of which are time limited) is very challenging. I went straight to playing on astronaut mode which I gather is more tricky but I wanted maximum realism.

I have not played this game on multiplayer. I'm just enjoying the single player experience for the moment.

The graphics in this game are excellent and truly convey the scale of the space environment. I quite enjoy travelling from one place to another by just putting myself into a random roll and then just drifting and enjoying the view. The music is find and appropriate for the game. The sound effects in general are very good.

If you have a strong stomach, like a challenge, and don't mind risking some serious RSI, I would highly recommend buying this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA superbly engrossing experience. There has been a lot of talk in reviews regarding nausea. I held off from buying Detached for ages until I read one review that essentially said treat it as a space simulation and play with that in mind. I did and I've had a great time in the game. Not once had nausea. I've rocked on my feet with the changes to motion and perspective but not once felt I'll.

I'm not going to go over all the usual pros and cons but I'll just go over a few of the small parts from the experience that makes it so immersive.

My top effect is the helmet. So simple but hard to pull off. The helmet feels exactly as you would expect, roomy yet enclosed, excellent sound muting. However for me the most immersive part is you cannot put your head "through" the helmet. If you try the helmet just moves with. So simple but excellent.

The controls are very well done. It's very easy to spin out of control but also to regain control. It ďoes feel as it might in space.

Get the experience and find out what it really might feel like in space.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely amazing. While the game dosn't fully make you feel like you are in zero G because you can still sense yourself standing in gravity, it gets pretty damned close. It's impossible to not get fully immersed while you are playing and feel like you are actually there, floating around. Detatched takes the VR experience to the level we all imagine it. Not only do you feel like you are reaching out and can touch things, it does so floating around... Which is at least what I imagined the VR experience to be like.

The controls are excellent and easy to understand. After a little practice it starts to become very second nature navigating with them. The game play so far and story is progressing nicely with interesting puzzles to solve. I'm excited to strap the headset on try the game again, and I can't say the same for a lot of VR games. The fit and finish in the game is on par with what a person expects out of a game nowadays. So many VR games are so bad, or so short that it really leaves me not wanting to try others.

Graphics are great, and the screen door effect becomes almost non noticable once you start playing with how they have done the visuals. Absolutely amazing game, a must have for any VR owner.

A word of caution however! This game is VERY nausiating. I don't get motion sickness very easily and this game pushed my limits, I could only muster close to an hour before I needed a break. Try not to play to the point you puke or you won't want to play again!\t\t\t\t\t
