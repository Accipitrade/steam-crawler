\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Good

  • Sound design on point
  • The world is gorgeous
  • The feeling of solitude has never been more authentic.
The Bad
  • Compared to other VR titles I've experienced, the controls are so horrendously unintutitive that game is unplayable.
  • Motion Sickness is almost inevitable with this title, regardless of how seasoned you are with VR.
TL;DR
If you're a fan of games like Descent and Sublevel Zero, this game may be for you.
If you are prone to motion sickness, do not buy this game.

Devs y'all could learn a thing or two from Lone Echo and Star Shelter movement systems.
Something as simple as adding a body to the player model can reduce motion sickness by a good amount.

Remember, this is just my opinion. If you're really wanting to try it, you can always refund if things don't work out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: This is literally the first (and only) game I've ever refunded on Steam.

I've got quite a bit of experience with VR. I've got my "VR-legs" and don't really have any physical issues with games--even those that can be disorienting. My system is spec'd pretty high--well beyond this game's minimum.

I wanted to like Detatched...I really did. It looks like a title that I'd be into. The graphics are solid, it's got a sci-fi theme, you're floating around in space. And it is all those things, but boy it sure wasn't for me. It really made me feel queasy. I'm not talking a LITTLE queasy--I'm talking "this is NAUSEATING" queasy.

Look, I don't like knocking a title, and I don't lightly give anything a non-recommend. This game does look nice and the gameplay elements I saw were okay--but the VR execution is just harsh. Now, everyone is different, and if you're one of the folks who doesn't get sick in this game, then more power to you. It might very well be a great game for those of you who aren't impacted. It's just impossible for me to recommend because it almost seems purpose-built to make people sick. There's even some bragging about it in the game trailer. If you're on the fence with this title, my recommendation is to buy something else. I certainly don't recommend picking it up at full price, and if you get it on sale (like I did) then just be prepared to have your money tied up for a few days while your refund is processed.\t\t\t\t\t

I DO NOT RECOMMEND FOR --EVERYONE--

The game looks great. If I had a better PC, it would be able to look a lot better than "great."
Good game.
Amazing concept and worthy of praise.

This game, as the video literally say, WILL MAKE YOU SICK. I've had VR for a while and don't normally get motion sickness. This game did a number on me when I set it to "Astronaut" mode. I do not dislike it because that. It warns customers, and it's a really great game at that.

WHY I DON'T RECOMMEND IT:
The helmet. The damned helmet. It feels like your head is a bubble 3 tims the size of what the helmet on the image looks like. It feels uncomfortable, potentially hard to get used to. The fact that moving your head moves the entire game with you is really, really bad (there may be an option to change this, hopefully, I am unsure).

The other issue that really bugged me was that you can't rebind any of the keys. It would be much preferable if you were able to change what axes adjusted tilt, yaw, rotation, etc.

Believe me when I say that this game is not for everyone. I would consider myself to have an above-average-consumer tolerence to VR motion sickness. If anything, get it mostly for the arcade mode (same game, just less nausiating).\t\t\t\t\t
