This dlc are my two favorite Borderlands 1 DLCs ever combined

You get awesome content just like with the Secret Armory of General Knoxx, and the Claptasticness of Claptrap's Robolution

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy opinion about this DLC is:

Graphics/effects: 5/5: This DLC is gorgeous. The glitched weapons have awesome skins, and in general, the whole DLC has jaw-dropping solutions.

Sounds/Music 4/5: The sounds and voice actors performed perfectly, just as usual in the series, and the music good too. It's a bit uncharacteristic but fits the DLC very well. And it's not annoying.

Gameplay/story/humour: 4/5: We can absolutely compare this to the Tiny Tina DLC. Not in just quality, but it uses similar effects and solutions too. Ie: the game area changes dynamically with the story multiple times, and the areas depicted the parts of Clappy's memory in a fragmented HDD very well. The missions are interesting, nothing like "kill x things" cliché. The (un)power of the glitched guns is undiscovered for me at least, I have to test them for a longer time. Saying of weapons: the corrosive weapong finally got point, there's a lot of armored enemies in this DLC - opposed to the original TPS content. The only problem I found gameplay-wise is just that I found the DLC a bit short. Short means here around 4-5 hrs of gameplay, and about 2 lvls of xp if you start it at lvl 60.

So I would totally recommend this DLC, along with the respawnable boss patch, it is capable of breathing new life into the TPS. For those who knows BL2 DLCs better, compared to the Tiny Tina DLC, it's almost as good as TTAODK. I played with a phalanx/maelstorm build Athena, used Vibra-Pulse, Hellfire (my alltime fav smg in all Borderlands) and a corrosive Bullpup (very similar to a Viral Marketer) and I have fun with it. There wasn't any details which distracted or annoyed me during the playthrough, except the length of the DLC. Definitely worths its price.\t\t\t\t\t
