Some Claptrap Bonding like never before.

Some key points that this game add on has to offer:

1.\tNew Glitch Weapons.
2.\tUltimate Vault Hunter Upgrade Pack 2.
3.\tExperience Claptrap’s inner workings like you could not have imagined… EVER!!!
4.\tNew Locations within a new location…

Replayability:

As with all things Borderlands they can be replayed with New Game + and New Game++.
Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel: Claptastic Voyage and Ultimate Vault Hunter Upgrade Pack 2 also contains a unique Arena for you to either take on solo or with your Co-op mates, so there is plenty of replay options here, especially considering that with this DLC there are those fancy new Glitch weapons to farm….

MGRza Gaming’s Conclusions:

Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel: Claptastic Voyage and Ultimate Vault Hunter Upgrade Pack 2 is the first full campaign DLC for Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel, and not only does it add new regions to explore, it also adds 10 extra levels for your characters to grind out and get even more powerful.

There is numerous new Pink Glitch gear for you to find and make use of, each unique and super potent to make use of as you go about your business within Claptrap. Yeah you read it right, for this journey we travel inside Claptrap.

The story for the DLC is well thought out and fun to experience: the story within the story that you experience whilst trying to secure the H-Source truly shows you an in-depth look into who and what Claptrap is. To most he is an annoying robot, but to some of us he really is a must have companion that adds a unique mood and setting to the game experience.
All in all, decent value for money.

Score 8

AND IF NOT, BUY IT!

Score: 10\t
\t
*A new type of gun rarity that is incredibly fun to mess with, along with some cool skins and even loot chests with special effects.
*If you play the main game you will realize that it is not as funny as the 2nd one. Well this DLC fixes that problem. After all it couldn't be a Claptrap based campaign if it didn't include his humor and antics.
*Level design and art direction is incredibly interesting. It has a lot more personality than the main game.
*Claptrap has always been kinda annoying but sympathetic, and now thanks to this story you can feel kinda sad for him in a lot of ways. You'll definitely love him way more after you fully complete this DLC.
*One of the best boss fights I have ever played. Seriously, I'd even say its better than the last boss in the main game and I already like that one.

The downsides:

*Sadly this is the only campaign DLC the game could get, and although it is incredibly good, makes buying the season pass a bad choice; which is why getting a Complete Edition is way better.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHIS is how you make a DLC.

Love or hate Claptrap, this is an excellent story. The Tron/synthwave style is excellent, and the map design is incredible. The enemy design is refreshing and there are actually very few reused enemies from the other games. The boss fights are excellent too. There's cool loot, with a new "glitch" rarity for weapons with rotating special effects. There are some fun legendaries as well, and I found myself using red-text blue guns longer than I normally would due to their interesting effects.

Anything negative I have to say is minimal, there's a bit of unnecessary backstory content on pandora where I would have preferred to see new areas, but they way it's done is different enough from the original that I wasn't bothered. There are chances to get legendaries and other drops you can get in the base game, although again I found that to be rare. Some of the missions can be a little reference-heavy (this is a criticism of the main game as well), and there's some "only programmers will get this" humor that I found a little grating, but overall I'd say this story has more personality than the main game.

I didn't think I would ever think of Claptrap as more than an annoyance, and now I think of him as an annoyance with a heartfelt and emotionally complex backstory. So that's an endorsement in itself.\t\t\t\t\t
