\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBorderlands: The Pre-Sequel has been an enjoyable game in its own right. Maybe it rides too much on the coattails of the second game (it wouldn't exist without it) but I still found it to offer new and in some cases superior experiences to Borderlands 2. Yes, it has its flaws - which I'm very much aware of and agree with on the whole, like lack of new legendary items, cloned Bandit weapons, no end-game to speak of, no loot midgets, questionable classes - but nothing stopped me from playing the heck out of this game when it first came out.

This DLC makes me rethink all of that.

I don't even know what to think of it, except that it's tone-deaf, weird, utterly unconvincing and feels completely out of place in the Borderlands universe. It makes me think that The Pre-Sequel is weird and out of place in the series, which I didn't before. At least, not as much.

Overwhelmingly positive reviews led me to purchase this DLC, and I'd hoped that it would have provided something to make an OK game into a good game. It did not.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t The campaign as a whole is fairly solid. I would probably put it slightly below Tiny Tina's Assault on Dragon Keep from Borderlands 2. The enemy designs are quite good, especially the flying bug variants. I found the areas to be interesting as well.
(SPOILERS, if don't want them just skip this paragraph) The main Nexus was cool, although a bit straight forward. It was fun to re-visit Fyrestone and the music there was probably my favorite song in the entire DLC. Overlook was fun too, and I love the whole floating islands thing it had going. The final area was probably my favorite area though. And then the final battles were intense, but not just bullet sponges or one-sided battles. I liked that the semi-final battle had a lot of extra enemies running around and wasn't just me against the boss by himself. The final battle is easily my favorite battle in the entirety of the Pre-Sequel and BL2. I haven't actually played the original BL, but I'm guessing that it didn't have anything that stood up to this DLC's final boss. It was intense, kept me moving, and had a really cool arena. Oh, and the DLC further explains some of the stuff that happens to CL4P-TP in between games, although I had already figured out most of it.
All in all, very solid DLC. I think it starts out a little slow, but it's definitely worth it. The new weapon type is also fun, and again, enemy designs were suprisingly good (mostly the bugs and glitches). I'd also say that's it's worth the price as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite a surprising and in-depth look at one of the Borderlands series' most recognizeable but infamously annoying characters. Shedding new light on the character and showing the real tragedy and self-loathing resulting of the negative views constantly thrust upon him, while he shallowly tries to mask his turmoil with denial and a thin layer of good humor. I still dislike Claptrap, but he seems more 3-dimensional and relatable now.

Pretty good story arc, prodded by greed and shadowed by distrust. Story can be predictable at times but serves its purpose well enough.
Enemies are all unique with very few reskins from the main game. Locations are extremely fascinating and highly varied when compared to the two primary types of locations from the main game. Plus several humorous gags to keep you entertained.
As well Claptastic Voyage features one of the most intense and relentless boss fights ever, easily surpassing the final boss of the main game.

However, you still have to listen to Claptrap the whole time, and he is still unbearably annoying. Some of the maps can be a bit clumsy to move through, resulting in a lot of falling and dying.

Given the content available, Claptastic Voyage is a very strong first story expansion for the Pre-Sequel, offering a good amount of content, weapons, dialogue and visual gags and plenty of what fans of Borderlands enjoy. Definitely recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
