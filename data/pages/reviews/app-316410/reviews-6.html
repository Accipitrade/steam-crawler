\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, to summarize my experience with this DLC: honestly amazing.

Well... most of it... More below.

Many missions making me laugh out loud (I just love Claptrap), interesting enemies with never seen before mechanics, interesting story, unique area's, great new weapons (legendaries but also the new type of 'glitch' weapons), etc.

The boss sucks. Bad.
The part that kind of ruined the whole end-game experience of this DLC for me was the boss. No worries, not putting any big spoilers here.

The boss takes up a substantial amount of bullets. And by substantial I mean, way too many. Magazine after magazine got emptied on the critical weak spot with my purple weapon of the appropriate itemlevel. All this while every 20 seconds a barrage of rockets, lightning balls and one-shot lasers got fired onto me, making me vision impaired for 70% of the time while even remotely close to the boss.

I couldn't get a second wind twice because I could literally not see anything else than explosions right onto my face. Even worse: after I finally finished killing the boss, (spoiler here) another, even bigger boss appeared taking me even more time to kill, which made me practically beg for it to end already.

All in all, 95% of this DLC is great, this is why I recommend it, even for 10 bucks. You get so much more content with all those side missions. Just be prepared, the boss fight is (in my opinion) way harder than the final boss in the main Pre-Sequel campain and might annoy you to the point of just giving up and trying again later.

But maybe I'm a weak person.\t\t\t\t\t

Seems like the only way for Borderlands' devs to release creative content is after the primal financial lusts of rotten 2K publisher have been satisfied enough for it to lessen the controlling capitalistic grip of death constantly looming around the necks of the unfortunate cubical workers. This could only be achieved with DLCs thus far, from my experience, after the main game sold well enough I reckon.

This is a not-so-short DLC that plays out inside CL4P-TP's consciousness by having your character digitally inserted there for plot reasons. Every element you see in there is how the robot perceives it from its own perspective so that lend itself for a lot of creative freedom. Plot itself is above Borderlands standard but I had difficulty following it due to the way characters talk in childish and meme-like manner, and being padded out with numerous fetch-quest (standard by now). Jack is your main sidekick once again but in guidance capacity, you only get to physically hang out at the beginning and at the end.

As irritating as CL4P can be, its insides are rather kinda interesting while his comms are kept on the down low. Locations are made of huge stylized circuit boards, floating islands, partial recreations of earlier locations (bonus points for this), and rooms filled with staircases as if constructed by M.C. Escher himself (CL4P and stairs don't get along). It was a breath of fresh air to explore I have to say. Got tired of planets, moons, rocks and deserts to be honest.

There are around 10 new areas, not all of which are large, and not all have fast travel stations so get used to running around on few occasions. There are no vehicles but they wouldn't fit either so no issue. One of the new areas is another battle area but they made an effort to make it enjoyable by allowing you to change physic parameters, weapon behavior and difficulty. The harder it gets the better the loot at the end. Apparently up to Legendary. You have to take their word for it though as I am all but done with battle areas for one lifetime. But they tried so that's something.

There is a new weapon/item rarity exclusive to this DLC and it's actually powerful and plentiful. They stand out from regular ones in terms of stats so I preferred the new stuff. Enemies are a mix or re-skins and new ones. Nothing extraordinary but not bad either. Things like viruses and militarized CL4P units. MINAC makes a cameo. End boss is pretty unique but has a ton of health (as if balancing issue) so I actually had to start TVHM to level a bit before paying him a visit later at lvl40. Although the wiki says levels are meant for levels 35-40, my game scaled down to 32 so I had no difficulty related problems starting early. The upper cap is true however.

Level cap is globally increased by further 10 so if you got both such DLCs the max level of the character becomes 70.

* * *

Decent DLC and relatively long if you are looking to collect everything. Kinda a good and bad thing at the same time depending how quickly you get bored of the same 'ol stuff. For me it happened after the end, while trying to collect 5th Vault Symbol in Motherlessboard. You know which one, and if you don't then know I envy you. Overall I enjoyed more parts than I did not so here we are. Once again college math pays off.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Sorry but no. It doesn't quite fit for at least two of the Vault Hunters narratively. The environment is predominately one huge platform shaped level with an end game played on a series of floating islands. If you felt you hadn't fallen to your death enough in the main campaign here's a good chance to up that figure. Minor spoiler - it does the Lord of the Rings thing and "ends" the game about five different times. I didn't like the graphics on the new enemy. No interesting new NPCs that you usually get in a DLC. I actually found because of the multiple endings that the DLC was far longer than average. The problem is that Assault on Dragon's Keep massively raised the bar on expectations for DLC and Claptastic Voyage doesn't come close.

If you've bought it as part of a package - yeah it's just about worth the playthrough - but it's worse than all the main games and half of the other DLC. Borderlands DLC split into the good, the bad, and the arenas (cough, rip off, cough) and Claptastic Voyage is probably the best of the bad.

What makes good Borderlands ? Interesting environments. Interesting NPCs. Innovation in going places and shooting things. Good comic moments. On this I felt Claptastic voyage didn't deliver.

But, spoiler alert, if what really turns you on is facing battles against ultra tough end bosses that can take down a full shield and 93% of your health in a single shot but can soak up 1000+ shots from your best weapon - there Claptastic voyage delivers.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Claptastic Voyage centers around CL4P-TP, but it surprisingly isn't incredibly annoying or stupid. Rather, the unique setting is fleshed out well with the unique enemies, glitch weapons, and the exploration of CL4P-TP's psyche.

The main story is serviceable, albeit simple. The one twist that shows up is incredibly predictable, it turns out the robot locked in Quarantine named 5H4D0W-TP is actually the antagonist! What a shock! . We also get Handsome Jack's trademark brand of humor, but nothing really leaves an impression compared to some of his classic lines from Borderlands 2. Some of the individual missions (main and side) are where this DLC peaks, rather than the main quest as a whole. The ending is also a bit of a downer. 5H4D0W-TP offers CL4P-TP a chance of power to get back humanity, who constantly mocks and berates him. CL4P-TP refuses, and gets rewarded with Handsome Jack shooting him and destroying his entire production line. At least he did the right thing I guess.

The main draw of this DLC then is the gameplay. The new glitch weapons will periodically "glitch" and will alter how the weapon behaves in a positive way. For instance, your shot animation will rapidly loop while consuming only a fraction of your ammo, allowing you to mow down enemies, even with a Jacobs pistol. These glitch weapons offer a unique, fun element to the normal gameplay. The new enemies provide some variety and uniqueness to the main game. It's especially fun to shoot the (In)security robots, who resemble CL4P-TP in appearance. You finally get a chance to shoot CL4P-TP, even if it's merely an identical robot.

Despite its flaws, I have to recomend this DLC for it's unique setting, fun gameplay, and the few individual missions that particularly stand out.\t\t\t\t\t
