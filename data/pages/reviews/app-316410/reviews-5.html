\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC was actually quite a bit longer than I was expecting. I had a lot of fun playing through it though. It may have just been the lack of prominent Aussie VAs in this DLC, but the quest design and writing seemed to be much more akin to a mainline Gearbox Borderlands title than to the writing from the main Pre-Sequel game by 2K Australia. That’s just to say that I thought the writing and humor in the DLC were pretty entertaining.

The DLC also managed to keep combat really interesting with the addition of “glitched guns.” Sometimes, using an SMG, the gun just wouldn’t stop firing until the magazine was empty. Other times, the SMG would turn into a shotgun and spread three bullets at once. We also found this one gun called the "%Cu+ie^_^ki||er", which was ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hilarious when being used.

The music in this DLC was pretty fantastic. I especially liked the “Claptrap's Defragmentation” and “Motherlessboard - Combat” tracks. In my opinion, it’s honestly right up there with Tiny Tina’s Assault on Dragon Keep as far as OSTs in Borderlands go. For reference, “Flamerock Refuge” was my favorite track from Tiny Tina.

And just as the rest of the Pre-Sequel DLC, the game was extremely pretty, especially with the new texture pack that was just released. Here’s my running 1440p ultrawide screenshot album for Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm one of those weird people who a) actually enjoyed The Pre Sequel and b) has never really been blown away by Borderlands DLC in the way many others are, so I genuinely wasn't sure how I'd react to this - many people say its the single greatest DLC in the Borderlands franchise (at least prior to Borderlands 3), but I kept an open mind either way.

The 'Many' were right. It's genuinely brilliant.

Essentially mashing up elements of the Matrix and and Tron, the basic concept of being 'digitised' and sent inside CL4P-TRP's mind is an interesting one, but I wasn't prepared for how well it handled the concept. It's very focused on CL4P-TRP but that doesn't mean loads of annoying BS and forced humour - this actually really gets into what makes him tick, why he is the way he is and what his core personality is. I finished this DLC genuinely feeling sorry for him, and playing Wilhelm, thinking my own character was a bit of an arsehole.

The quest design is *very* clever - it's superficially still comic-book style science fiction, with zany borderlands humour, so it feels entirely in place in the universe, but those with a bit of understanding of how servers and computer architecture works will pick up on a lot dry humour that will likely go over the heads of those who do not (a particular sidequest where you had to take sides between cookie miners and antivirus agents had me in stitches and the in-game BSOD was one of the funniest things I've seen in a computer game). As the game goes on and you go deeper into CL4P-TRP's kernel, things get more and more trippy and you actually get a chance to see Handsome Jack's coding skills :)

Weapons and builds-wise, it introduces some decent gear - a few legendaries plus a new grade of gun called 'Glitched' (essentially pink-rarity with a random effect that can occur when reloaded). It's fine, its enjoyable, nothing setting the world on fire or anything. If you enjoyed Pre Sequel you'll definitely enjoy this, and there's lots of callbacks to earlier plot points in the Borderlands series, so veterans will get a lot of nostalgia.

My only real issue with it is this was one of most annoying final bosses of the series - not because it was tough or anything (it absolutely is, but not unfair), it just was *boring*. There's like 4 stages that just go on waaaaay too long.

As I say, I think Pre Sequel is a criminally underrated entry in the series, but even if you didn't enjoy it, Claptastic Voyage should be required gaming for the discerning Borderlands fan.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI thought that Claptastic Voyage was going to be more of the meh-type story and quests of the Pre-Sequel, but I sure changed my mind quick. It ended up being just as enjoyable as BL2 was for me and it packs a lot of content for a single DLC campaign.

So about the gameplay, quests, or whatever: they were a lot better than I had imagined. The whole Claptrap consciousness thing was interesting and it wasn't super gimmicky. I was also surprised to see a large difficulty jump: the main story will probably leave you at about level 27-28 but this DLC will easily demand a level up into the 40s. Depending on how you play the game, this may put you off, especially with an annoying final boss with repeated regen (and no, you can't do anything to stop it), but I liked how this made me choose my build more carefully and play more defensively.

If BL:TPS ended up being a drag for you, Claptastic Voyage will definitely stand out and I'm sure you'll enjoy the change of pace. Unfortunately, there's no other DLC campaign in this game, so you might feel a little disappointed after you finish it. There's still quite a lot to do anyways, so you can have a lot of fun while it lasts.\t\t\t\t\t
