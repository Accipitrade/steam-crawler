\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Pre-Sequel only has one story DLC, and it's a good one. Visually, there are plenty of new things to see, so it's clear that the developers put a lot of work into the maps and such. There are a few locations that are..kind-of recycled, but I don't want to spoil anything about that. I loved that these locations were there, and what they did with them. This DLC explores more about Claptrap and is a lot of fun. There's also a very catchy song in it. Perhaps too catchy?

I don't want to mislead, though. This DLC does have a single major flaw, in my opinion: its boss is incredibly difficult. I do suck at video games, but I highly recommend you look at other reviews - both positive and negative - the boss is freaking hard. Some douche will claim the boss isn't hard, but I assure you they're full of it. Unless you luck out and find an exploit or something, this boss is awful. The only way to stand a chance is to over-level like mad, and then come back. If you're on Ultimate Vault Hunter, everything levels with you, so you're going to be throwing your mouse out your window.

That said, though, the rest of this DLC pleased me so much that I still recommend it. In my opinion, only the Tiny Tina Borderlands 2 DLC is better, as far as DLC for this franchise goes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to give this a good review. Seriously. I've played all of the Borderlands games... and this DLC actually has some interesting story content dealing with Claptrap's past. The gameplay itself... however... is a different story.

This DLC is riddled with bugs! I've died more times during the DLC then I have throughout the whole campaign... and it's not due to the DLC being difficult, it's primarily because of bugs and cheap bosses. I've fallen throughs solid floors, been stuck in places where I can't move, etc. We're talking about the kind of bugs you would see in 3D Sonic the Hedgehog games. Then you have other kinds of bugs... like quests that are bugged, requiring that everyone completely exit the game in order to fix it.

Sure, I've seen some bugs here and there throughout the Borderlands series... but this DLC seems to be a collection of all of those bugs in one DLC package.

So... for the story and atmosphere, I would give it a thumbs up... actually, in these catagories, this DLC is probably one of the best in the whole Borderlands series. However, for gameplay and frustration factor... I give it a thumbs down... and, for me, the frustration factor kinda killed a lot of the fun for me. I couldn't really enjoy the story and atmosphere encountering so many bugs and restarting the game. So ultimately, I have to give it a thumbs down.

If you're really interested in the DLC, or if (like me) you're a bit OCD when it comes to the story and want to see absolutely everything... I'd suggest getting it on a big sale. Otherwise, I'd avoid it.\t\t\t\t\t
