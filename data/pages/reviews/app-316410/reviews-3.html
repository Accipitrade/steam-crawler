\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreetings as I hope all is well! Below is my Steam Review for Claptastic Voyage and Ultimate Vault Hunter Upgrade Pack 2, a DLC in Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel. Thanks for reading as I wish you well in all your gaming adventures...

STORY/LORE - 10/10

Claptrap is one of my all-time favorite characters in the Borderlands franchise and well, you best believe a DLC that entirely features him will be well worth it. Claptastic Voyage and Ultimate Vault Hunter Upgrade Pack 2 has a great storyline that only lets you go deeper into Claptrap’s character, but it explains how he got to be who he is in the Borderlands franchise. You’ll laugh as you play this as it’s a pretty quick DLC to get through, has some fun, easy Steam Achievements to unlock, and yes, who doesn’t want to see Claptrap dance?!?!? Expect a lot of it...

GAMEPLAY - 10/10

Laser weapons, a fun boss fight, lots of unique enemies that are challenging, and endless amounts of Claptrap. I mean, you’re literally inside Claptrap (insert joke here) trying to save the day. It doesn’t get any better than that and best of all, there are some amazing exclusive weapons you can loot in Claptastic Voyage and Ultimate Vault Hunter Upgrade Pack 2. They glitch out and everything to go with the aesthetic of this DLC, they’re all very cool.

GRAPHICS - 10/10

Claptastic Voyage and Ultimate Vault Hunter Upgrade Pack 2 came out in 2015 and still holds up to this very day. The graphics are nice, level design is good, and all the details to some awesome looking weapons are great. It’s Borderlands and well, the cartoony graphics that is a signature staple of this gaming franchise continues to get the job done.

SOUNDTRACK - 10/10

Yes, there is a Claptrap song and because it exists in this DLC and now forever, it’s a 10/10 just for that alone.

MULTIPLAYER - N/A

Claptastic Voyage and Ultimate Vault Hunter Upgrade Pack 2 is in Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel and is an old video game. Nobody is playing this at all as I’m sure you might see a few people jumping in but the multiplayer functionality is a hot mess. It has a ton of problems trying to connect with others online so it makes playing this co-op that way difficult. If you’re looking to get any of the co-op Steam Achievements or anything involving multiplayer, your best bet is to get some friends to jump in with you that are specifically looking to go through this game/DLC and focus on playing together at the same time progressing. If not, sorry, this will be a single-player experience from beginning to end.

FINAL SCORE - 10/10

Overall, Claptastic Voyage and Ultimate Vault Hunter Upgrade Pack 2 was a fantastic DLC and much better than Ultimate Vault Hunter Upgrade Pack: The Holodome Onslaught. It has super easy Steam Achievements to get and is a ton of fun to play. You’ll learn a lot about Claptrap in more ways than one and it’ll be a great time playing from start to finish as the storyline unfolds. Keep an eye out for that Claptrap song and when you hear it, sit back and experience the greatness hitting your warm ears.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the only DLC for the Pre-Sequel that is worth its price at the moment. "Claptastic Voyage" is also its only campaign DLC, but it's a damn good one. I've personally always found Claptrap to be an endearing staple of the series. I know some people do find him annoying, however, and for those, I'm not entirely sure if I can recommend this DLC. Because even though the humour is heavily Claptrap-y, there is a lot of depth present to the storytelling here, and this turns out to be more of a character exploration piece of content, in a similar vein to "Tiny Tina's Assault on Dragon Keep" from "Borderlands 2".

The story starts in what is only a mildly amusing concept. It takes place after the ending of the main game and sees you enter Claptrap's mind to find a piece of tech Jack needs. Naturally, things turn out to be not so simple. What kept me hooked was the sheer quality of the writing. It's back to those peaks that were seen in "Borderlands 2", no doubt better than what the Pre-Sequel has to offer. The humour is brutal. Claptrap is the punching bag of the series, and while he might not seem to be more than that, you can clearly see that there is more which resides in his metallic shell. The sentimentalies here are hardly ever as simple as "funny" or "sad". In fact, you might see both juxtaposed against each other. It's quite representative of Claptrap himself and establishes him as a character who has just as much as the rest of the larger Borderlands cast. I actually played as him throughout, which lead to some fun (and confusing) interactions along the way.

There are a few amusing additions to gameplay here. Glitch weapons are one such thing. They have a few mechanics unique to them that will give enough variety for the length of the campaign. Traversal is not at all linear and turns out to be really fun once you become familiar with the maps. I should mention the final boss, as I see him being a common talking point among players of the DLC. The boss fight was very intense, and it's obvious that the devs went all-out on it. Every stage stands out, unlike the main game final boss, whose six stages only differed two times. Nonetheless, the fight can drag on for a while, especially because of its difficulty. I was fine with this, except for the sheer amount of effects that went about. I legitimately had trouble finding enemies because of how bright all the explosions and whatnot were. Besides, it's not a huge issue for me.

A huge improvement here are the environments. Unlike the repetitive areas of Elpis, there is not one zone here that I found boring. The level design is top-notch and knows how to make each small part of the larger area feel special. It even goes as far as utilising gravity in a rather funky manner that I'd rather not spoil, I'll just say the player comes across this towards the end.

All in all, this is a must for any fans of the "Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel" or simply Claptrap himself. A lot of passion was placed here and it shows in the sheer creativity the DLC never fails to deliver. It takes roughly 6-8 hours to complete, and it manages to avoid placing filler along the ride. I'll give it an 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t
