This is obviously an crazy premise for a wild adventure. After all, they are journying into the mind of the most insane unit of the most insane line of robots ever created.

Anyone who finds Claptrap HA-LER-IOUS!
Anyone who likes shooting annoying stuff.
Anyone who enjoys some good stories about why claptrap is so sad.
Anyone who enjoys jokes about the human . . . er . . . robot psyche.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike quite a few others, I wasn't that crazy about the base game. Playing through it from start to finish once was enough for me. I've tried new runs with different characters or playing on True Vault HUnter Mode but I can never get too far before I've had enough.

I had a recommendation from a friend to play this DLC. I'd only played the base game on and off in recent months and had all but written off getting any of the DLC, so I wasn't sure what to think.

I read positive reviews of this DLC. They said it was supposed to be more like Borderlands 2 and a significant improvement over the base game. Some even claimed it to be the best DLC in all of the Borderlands games.

When a sale started soon after, I bought the season pass so I could get this DLC (among other things). The DLC itself wasn't on sale. I considered waiting until it was even cheaper but given that the reviews seemed so positive, I figured it was worth getting then and there.

Having played this to the boss battle (including side quests I was able to access), but not having completed it, I feel that I have no choice but to rate this as not recommended because of said boss battle. As others have said, it's terrible for multiple reasons. It casts a shadow over the entire DLC. I could state the issues I had with it, but I think the other reviews did a good enough job that I don't need to repeat what they said.

The DLC is pretty good up until point but I wouldn't call it the best.

b) don't mind leaving the last mission in the game unfinished.
