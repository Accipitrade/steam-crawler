\r\n\t\t\t\t\tClaptastic Voyage is an exceptional piece of downloadable content for Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel. This DLC takes players on a mind-bending journey into the mind of the beloved robot, Claptrap.

The DLC takes place inside Claptrap's consciousness, where players are tasked with battling his inner demons and aiding him in his quest to become the ultimate robot. This premise allows for a wildly creative and unpredictable storyline that keeps players engaged throughout.

The DLC is also jam-packed with new weapons, enemies, and environments. The new "Glitch" weapons are especially fun, as they randomly change form and stats during battle, adding an extra layer of excitement to combat. The new enemies are also well-designed, with unique attacks and behaviors that keep players on their toes.

Perhaps the most impressive aspect of Claptastic Voyage is its level design. The environments inside Claptrap's mind are wildly imaginative, ranging from a cyberpunk city to a twisted carnival. Each area is filled with intricate details and hidden secrets, making exploration a rewarding experience.

Overall, Claptastic Voyage is an outstanding DLC that fans of Borderlands should not miss. Its engaging storyline, creative level design, and plethora of new content make it a must-play for fans of the series.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tClaptastic Voyage is the only DLC for Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel that features a whole new campaign, revolving around, you guessed it, Claptrap, the funny yellow robot who is the icon of the franchise. It takes place after the main story of the base game, and you should play it after you finished it since the level requirement is quite high. In this DLC, Handsome Jack sends his Vault Hunters to Claptrap's mind in search of a powerful code. Also included is the second Ultimate Vault Hunter Upgrade, which adds another 10 levels to your max level cap.

The levels and overall style of this DLC is pretty unique, with some having the hi-tech machinery look, and some others are surreal, glitchy recreations of normal areas such as Fyrestone which you visited in the previous 2 games. There are also glitchy, distorted enemies, and glitch weapons which are unique and rare. It's a pretty refreshing and kinda cool change. Also, there are references to many events Claptrap's been through, including some referencing his robot revolution!

This DLC is all fun and games until you reach the final boss, which I'm not a fan. It's not forced, rushed, or anything, just... surprisingly hard. If you completed the DLCs of previous titles you think you'd know what to expect, but in this case the final boss is more akin to a raid boss, with very high health, but luckily the attacks are not as damaging. It's still doable solo though so don't let that deter you. Overall, its a pretty great DLC, but sadly its the only one for Pre-Sequel with a proper new storyline.\t\t\t\t\t
