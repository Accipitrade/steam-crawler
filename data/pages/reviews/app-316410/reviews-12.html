\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe DLC campaign on itself is very good; the story is much better than that of the main game Borderlands PSQ. I actually remember some of the side quest NPCs and what I did for them, and why. 5H4D0W-TP is a better character than any of the new ones in PSQ. Glitch weapons are a really good addon the the Borderlands games and I really hope they will stay in some form or another.

Slight spoiler alert: my very positive opinion of this DLC was quite a bit watered down during the final boss fight. This multi-staged fight has (in normal mode!) the difficulty of the raid boss fights in other Borderlands games, including but not limited to multiple stages, being one-shot before you even re-enter the area should you die, massive AOE attacks, homing missiles that deal VERY high AOE damage (enough to flatten the whole Vault Hunter party in one salvo) and very few cover? What went wrong Gearbox? Was the fight against 5H4D0W-TP supposed to be the real boss fight (hard but balanced and fair) and everything after that should be am optional raid boss fight like in the other games, and somehow it starts directly after the 5H4D0W-TP fight?

Overall, a really good DLC. Not as good as the Tiny Tina DLC or the General Knoxx DLC, but third place for me. If only someone would balance the end a bit....

Edit: and the final (configurable) arena in this DLC is bugged as hell. First, we got 5H4D0W-TP spawned in one wave but he was unkillable (one of his robot drones glitched under the floor and so was unreachable and unkillable, and without killing the drones you cannot bring his reflective shield down), the second try we did not get any loot chest as reward. So we got ZERO reward for this mission. We also got shot through solid walls. No thanks, this arena will not be played again, there are more bugs than at Klendathu.\t\t\t\t\t
