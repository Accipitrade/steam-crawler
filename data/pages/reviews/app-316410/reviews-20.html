\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC takes place after the main Pre-Sequel story has ended. So you need to finish the main story before starting this DLC. However it will certainly be worth it. Without spoiling the game to much, the story is about that Jack sends the Vault Hunters into Clap-Trap's mind because of SPOILER

While this DLC is not the longest story DLC (IIRC Campaign of Carnage in BL2 was longer than this is), the content in the story does certainly hold a pretty high low-level.

The level design in this DLC is great. In fact, I'm gonna say so far that it is among the better games when it comes to level design. The only real complain I have on that point is that there is one area that is built vertically, which makes it a bit hard to navigate.

The story in this DLC is also a pretty good one. While I can't say to much without spoiling, it does give you some new perspective on some of the characters in the Borderlands Series. And given that you are going into the mind of Clap-Trap, a lot of the humor in this DLC is related to computer science. If you have an interest in that, you'll certainly enjoy it.

So if you are going to get Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel, should also get this DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttldwannar: Good dlc, i hope to see more for bltps. a few shortcomings but nothing too dissappointing.

this is the most anticipated dlc for the pre sequel thus far, and for good reason: it is the first proper campaign add on, and the only one included in the season pass, apparently. was it worth the wait? i think so.
the story is decent enough, judging by the tps standard (mediocre dialogue compared to bl1 or bl2), carrying us from stark tron-esque environment to scrambled versions of a couple of borderlands 2 locations, claptraps personality defects being the glue that ties it all together. i feel the team could have added a couple more of these past games locations, since they are the most colorful environments in the entire game so far. perhaps the next full sized dlc can focus on flashbacks, or some other way to take us back to previous entries... i like the idea of a perpetual universe, and i hope that eventually we can play all the borderlands games together in one giant package. i dont think borderlands 1 skill trees and characters would necessarily need to be used, since the rpg elements have matured considerably in the franchise since then. but i digress...
the matrix type area that makes up the majority of claptraps brain, and the majority of the dlc, is at the same time creative, and also a bit lazy i think. while the foreground areas are fine, i think the developers missed an opportunity with backgrounds. they could have had random, crazy ass memories playing here and there, or cheesy things like lollipops or unicorns or whatever, but as it is the backgrounds are just blurry tron type stuff. as bland as the majority of the pre sequel, so i guess it at least fits in... heh.
there are new weapons, both glitched and legendary, and i am enjoying the new things i have found so far. i wont go into detail since there are better places to find the specifics than in this review, but suffice it to say i think the added content will go along way to making tps more interesting (which it desperately needs).
there are plenty of new enemy types, and a welcome return of some old ones (bandits!). there are surprises in many of the chests, usually either an explosion, or a small flying robot that drops extra loot when u kill it. not as fun as loot midgets, but they are plentiful. of course they only have a normal chance of dropping rare loot, nothing like loot midgets who dropped legendaries quite often. and there are floating blocks in many areas that vassilate between health and what amounts to a corrosive mirv, and these all come in handy during rough battles.
speaking of rough battles, holy sh1t. the final boss battles have caused me to give up trying with my claptrap character, and am in the process of playing through the dlc again with athena, in hope that i might have a chance to win that battle. it is really a zero fun fight where you will die over and over. i play single mostly so i am truly fu(k3d. i have yet to see the end of the dlc for that crappy reason.

pretty good dlc, an acceptable amount of new features with some half a55ed efforts. incredibly g4y boss battle preventing me from finishing the dlc up to now at least. nerf that f4660t.
