\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, I'll admit it, I had a pretty negative view of the other Pre-Sequel mission-based DLC expansions. Both previous games had great story extensions like General Knoxx, and plenty of fun, "out-of-this-world" (in those cases, the world being Pandora) like the zombie and Tiny Tina expansions of old. Claptastic Voyage, being the only major story expansion for Pre-Sequel, manages to hit both of these notes at the same time, striking a delicious balance between lulz and feels, something the main camapaigns of the base game have been great at in the past. As a quick side note, I will say I am disappointed this is the only traditional full-length campaign expansion for Pre-Sequel. On a whole, the post-launch DLC support for the base game really highlighted how this was not a regular entrant into the Borderlands franchise, and more of an Assassin's Creed Brotherhood or Far Cry Primal.
Claptastic Voyage sees you taking on a variety of programming and robot-based enemies inside of Clap-TP. Setting wise, this is the Borderlands universe take on Tron and other classic computer-scifi-fi tropes much in the same way that Tiny Tina's expansion for Borderlands 2 was this universe's answer to classic fantasy RPG shenanigans. The inner workings of Clap-TP have plenty of variety for such a condensed Borderlands experience. You'll get to see locations and charactersfrom both previous games, as well as settings based on fanciful dreamscapes and fancy interpretations of computer hardware interfaces. The enemies have plenty of variety, more than the first Claptrap themed expansion from Borderlands 1. As is customary with more recent developments in the series, there are plenty of sub-types for each new enemy class, with lots of programmer and networking in-jokes.
Mechanics-wise, there is a new flavor of gun, which in keeping with the spirit of the expansion, has an almost bit-mask way of describing weapon stats. These new "Glitch" guns are about on tier with Purple weapons, and I ended up gridning the dozen or so that I found, keeping maybe a handful. On reload there's a chance for the gun to glitch on and take on a different set of stats, which area lal determined by the numerical values in the name of the weapon. If you had fun with some of the more random elements of certain skill trees like Gaige's in Borderlands 2, you will appreciate what this new gun tier has to offer.
In the story department, the narrative does a great job of filling a lot of the missing holes in the story between the three main campaigns. A few bits and pieces that I was left pondering about after Pre-Sequel's campaign concluded were addressed successfully, and managed to fill some of the more minor plot holes in the whole Border-saga from the last few years.
As far as the Ultimate Vault Hunter Upgrade Pack 2 portion of the DLC goes, having only played playthrough 1 of each mission in this game and its DLC, I never actually hit level cap and didn't need it. I suppose if you need to break level cap, grab THIS instead, as you'll actually get a solid seven or eight hours of gameplay out of this in the first playthrough alone.
If Pre-Sequel left you itching for yet more Borderlands story and tom-foolery, or you just want the shot at an extra 10 skill points this should be the one (and sadly, only) expansion you grab for this game.\t\t\t\t\t

They all shout his name when he rolls into town! Hooray! Here comes clap trap can't keep up your frown!
He wouldn't hurt your mom or dad! Won't hurt you or me! The mention of his very name fills us all with glee!

This DLC is amazing. i just finished it and felt like i needed to write a review immediatly. The DLC is definitly worth the price. The levels add new enemies to fight and a few bosses. The final hard was REALLY difficult however, even without dying, still took me over half an hour on lvl 32 Nisha to defeat it. The story is well made and the new levels look really good. They really represent what you would imagine is in Claptraps head.

The 'Glitched' weapons are a really cool addition which can turn the tide at a random time in a fight. You are guaranteed at least 2 legendary weapons for completing the DLC. I believe that the final boss drops a random legendary as well. I finished the DLC picking up 4 legendaries, 3 which were weapons and 1 class mod. Raising the level cap is nice but i have not reached the point that it will affect me.

9/10 - Great Levels and new weapons, difficult final boss (Which i didn't mind too much)
