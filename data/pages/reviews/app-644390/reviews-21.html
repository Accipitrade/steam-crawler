I won't play their games anymore, but if you REALLY need to play LoW and don't mind an annoying UX, at least this game works now.

ULTRA-WIDE MONITORS NOT SUPPORTED
---------------------------------------------
TLDR; The people that worked on this game clearly have no love for their craft, or board gaming. If I had to describe Playdek in a single word it would be "apathetic".

It comes through in so many little ways. The UI/UX is bad, there is zero polish in this game (or their other titles), and they leave them entirely unsupported. Playdek writes a game and runs - only returning once the screams about a broken game reach such a fevered pitch that it can't be avoided any longer. Don't give these people your money, find a developer that cares about what they do and support them instead.

That's basically the review, but I wrote the rest of this for fun while waiting for a new game to download.

They have "DLC" for sale, but you can actually play it for free anyway!! It's possible they aren't even aware of this, and they are reaping the rewards of having zero interest in their own games, and don't test them whatsoever (which is obvious based on the types of issues their games have).

The support page steam directs you to doesn't even exist anymore at Playdek's site - they don't even mention offering support on their website, but you will find a half-assed implementation of zen desk if you click on the FAQ button. ALL of the questions posted are unanswered by Playdek staff. Honestly, these people should be embarrassed, but I suspect they aren't whatsoever. The "developers" are likely little more than a couple offshore commodity coders working at the direction of one or two wanna-be entrepreneurs that are too busy cheating on their wives to bother themselves with the banality of running of a company.

About the game, the UI for this game is pretty clunky. A ton of screen real-estate is wasted. It's clear they wrote their own UI framework, because it's pretty bare-bones. No key bindings for anything - not even the escape key will open the game menu or cancel an action. No UI elements are movable, with the exception of the slide out for your quests/intrigue cards, which when opened will cover critical parts of the board so that you constantly need to open or shut it.

Games like Lords of Waterdeep by Playdek set a precedent for the poor quality of digital editions of board games. Inconvenient UI elements, poor UX design, a limited range of usability (breaking on OS updates, new hardware, and so on - they clearly had a very narrow view of the platforms for which they developed), and absolutely no support to speak of really gave this entire genre a bad image.

Thankfully some newer dev shops are releasing games with far more polish these days! Here's to hoping this dev dies on the vine while up and coming teams that love what they do starve them of opportunity.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a great board game, with a very good digital implementation. The UI is a bit too 'mobile-y' for some, but I find that in-game it works just fine. AI works well and couch multiplayer is in. I haven't played online to speak for that side of things.

DLC's add variance for a very fair price and I find myself returning for a twenty minute game against the AI every so often.

If you enjoy board games in general or are a fan of this one in particular, it is hard to pass it.\t\t\t\t\t
