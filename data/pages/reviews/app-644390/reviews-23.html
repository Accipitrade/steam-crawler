\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMe and my other half both loved this game on tabletop. Its one of our favourite to play with friends over a few glasses of wine. With the lockdown we, along with everyone else, lost the opportunity to have get togethers like that. It was great, then, to discover this on Steam. It's a faithful, intuitive, accessible reproduction of the tabletop game.

As a life long D&D player who enjoys the wider cannon and fiction too, I can say happily that Lords of Waterdeep has captured rather well the overall aesthetic of the D&D experience and still created something new.
Worker placement games are ten a penny, but rarely do they flow quite this well. Its dynamics are balanced so that differing play styles can still win, and offers a challenge that changes every play through as there is more than enough variety in the card draws and buildings to keep it feeling new each time. That even goes for the Corruption addition in Skullport. Its always tricky to blend a new dynamic into an already polished game, but they did it very well, adding depth and more options without compromising the existing game.
I've not played much of the AI as we got this for playing with friends, so I can;t speak as to how it plays in solo mode.

Now, there are a few things that could be improved. The multiplayer is not the easiest thing to navigate to set up games with friends, though certainly not the most complicated out there. But it has a lot of room for improvement. The Game could also do a lot better at letting you know it's your turn, and reminding you to properly end your turn. Likewise it could do better at letting everyone else know whose turn it currently is.
These are minor details though.

In summary, there are technically two reviews here. One of the game itself, and one of the Steam/digital platform.
