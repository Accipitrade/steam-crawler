\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLords of Waterdeep is a great game. This digital version not so much. It has some visual bugs and intolerable sound effects, but I'm willing to happily overlook these. I'm not looking for a perfect game, I'm looking for a reasonably good game. However, some feature designs are so outrageous you have to stop and wonder whether someone in the dev team tried to intentionally sabotage this product. TL;DR version: if you're looking for single player vs AI games or if you're a group of 4-6 friends interested in playing together, you're good. If you have just one or two friends interested in setting up a big game it is a nightmare.

I belong to the second group. My wife and I both bought the game, wanting to play together. First questionable design: you can't add AI players to a multiplayer match. You 100% depend on finding other human players, which is next to impossible because the online is on the verge of death. So if we're 2 people wanting to play a game for 6, we need every f*cking star in the universe to align. Needless to say, we didn't get to play the way we wanted. After joining every open game on the server and spending hours just lurking in the online lobby, we only managed to play one 3 players game.

So after being disappointed with the online option, we thought we might just play an offline "hotseat" game with AI opponents. Enter questionable game design number two: even though we're sitting at the same computer, the game will show "opponent moves" for each human player separately. So basically we had to watch AI moves twice, and my wife had to watch a recap of my moves during her turn and vise versa, as if we weren't sitting in the same ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ room staring at the same screen for the entire time. What it means, other than entirely killing the flow of the game, is that a 2 hours game now takes 4. It's like watching a tv show where 50% of the show is the "previously on..." section. Depressingly frustrating.

That's it. Rant over.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLords of Waterdeep is a very fun and challenging worker placement board game. The gameplay and interface is solid and runs very smooth. The music and sound effects are mediocre, but this isn't really an issue for me.

The A.I. is pretty good while a player is learning the game, but the A.I. is not aggressive enough towards the player. The A.I. will focus on attacking other A.I. instead of the player, even at the highest difficulty.

The default animation speed is too fast for me, even after I learned the game. But there is an option to slow the animation or increase it towards your preferred speed, which is great. There is attacking in this game where players will gain or discard cards and resources. I prefer a slower animation speed, so I spend less time going through the discard to see previous actions.

Some achievements are bugged and you have to complete them multiple times to unlock.

I'm still practicing against the A.I. so I'll update the review later on the multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t
