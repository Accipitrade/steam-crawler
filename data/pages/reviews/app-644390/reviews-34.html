Pro: Easy matchmaking mode, option for play-by-mail style games (play at your own pace), no fiddling with little cubes, no forgetting to score points from some activated plot quest. You can get the game plus both expansions for cheaper than buying the board game by itself.

9 out of 10. Would buy again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat port from a classic board game. Let me be the first to say, I started this game by downloading it on my IPod before a deployment in the military and I played countless hours on it and never got bored. I then decided I loved the game so much I bought the game board and this game when it was on sale.

This game has a lot to offer in terms that the steam version has a great layout, sounds, and visuals. Though it's a DnD game it's not quite what you might expect, you'll be taking on one of the various lords of Waterdeep and you will be spending your time sending your adventurers to various locations to pick up clerics, warriors, wizards, or rogues and completing quests.

If you're looking for a fun board game port to play during quarantine I highly recommend this game and the DLCs. If you can wait for the next sale, it's all the better for a discounted price. Each lord feels unique and there are various different play styles that can help you achieve victory. My question to you is, are you truly ready to be a Lord of Waterdeep?\t\t\t\t\t
