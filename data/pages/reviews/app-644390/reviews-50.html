\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent adaptation of the board game to the PC. It plays well, the controls are intuitive, and there haven't been any breaking issues. I have not tried online yet, but the AI is a reasonable challenge on average difficulty. The board game itself is quite balanced, and so far I've seen that same balance reflected ingame. Scores are very rarely more than 20 points between the winner and loser... and most often the players are within 5-10 points of each other, all using different strategies.

My one complaint is it is sometimes a bit annyoing scrolling through buildings on the side, or fitting all action points on the screen -- gotta do full zoom out when using expansions, and even that isn't quite enough by the endgame.

Regarding other negative reviews (which almost caused me NOT to buy the game -- glad I listened to the positive ones instead), I have no seen any issues with blurry cards (everything is crisp from what I can see), speed (it's togglable) or AI trolling (if you're a threat, it treats you as one just as any real player would). People tend to see negative events only when it affects them, and not watching the AI work each other over too.

Nominated for "Best port of a Board Game"\t\t\t\t\t
