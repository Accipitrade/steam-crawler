\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the concept of this game = love it as little as my father loves me.
I'll discuss what I like last. But let's focus on how disappointed I am that you aren't good at basketball like poppa.

First complaint, how is this on Windows and mac, but you can't seem to have functionality for Linux? Linux is basically the more configurable Mac. You can point at what you want Linux users to run for dependencies and they'll do it. As is we have to rig it to run as if running on PC. One hour of work times the number of Linux users that buy this, or 3 hours of work for you? Request number of Linux users on steam. From your lack of commitment to us, I'm sure it will shock you. But that's fine if you don't like a 1% increase in cash for easily less than 4 hours of work (if you don't know what you're doing). You could get an intern to do it.

Then, the humming of hm hm hm, after most cards are played is awful. It now haunts my dreams and I cry when I click to play a card. Looking into the code to replace that with the hum of the huntress from Dead by Daylight. Because that murder's lullaby is less terrifying at this point.

Next, there isn't great AI. Difficulty settings on the AI are not that different.
Easy is too tough. Hard is too easy. You learn to play and the game is over. Yes, I do enjoy playing against live players, but being turn based should have the advantage of being able to play the game as slowly as I'd like when I want to. Not fair to real life players I play against if I want to chill and watch a movie while I play, etc.

Lastly, and the biggest issue. The game mechanics have some issues. Because of the counting of gold and adventurers happens at the end of the game every resource starts to turn into one resource virtually. I feel this would be more appropriate if there was a variable number of agents or rounds. There isn't. You knew how long you had to finish what you need to. If you are just grabbing at gold and stuff as a last resort you shouldn't be able to get more than a well organized quest by just spamming out stuff.

Ok. I will mention what I like. This thing is priced well for what you get. It's playable and it is worth a couple good games. The DLC I am looking at just because it is so cheap, it looks genuinely different, and I feel like I'll get the beginner feeling again if I buy it. Which is when the game was pretty enjoyable. The problem with this is it starts as a 7/10, but quickly becomes a 5/10. But I still play it a little. Which brings me to...

Not a lot of people make turn based card/board games that you can chill out with. So I have to be appreciative. Is it amazing? No. Is it better than Sentinels of the Multiverse video game? Yes, undoubtedly, without question. Is Lords of Waterdeep good and worth buying? That's a tough question. If you are looking for something to chill with once in a while while doing something else. I'd say probably, but most people will likely not get the same value out of it that I have. And I still have complaints. Just like my drunk father.\t\t\t\t\t
