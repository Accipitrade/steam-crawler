\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like the original D&D Lords of Waterdeep boardgame you will definitely gravitate favorably to this version, it depends whether or not you like the User Interface(UI).

If you re used to (like me) to have everyone's quests and information displayed at the same time like playing the board game in real life, you might be let down, but I really didn't mind.

Every player has a tab to the side of the screen, you pull them to the side and displays their quests, gold, adventurers and cards in hand. You will have to do a little more memorizing playing this version but it is all good if you don't mind, although a lot of people play this game without caring much what their opponents are doing, for people like that I recommend once a while to still pay attention to their opponents, since much of what a player does has consequences and some players like me do play having those consequences in mind.

It is a worker placement/engine building game after all.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game that prompted me to buy a physical board game. It has charm, replayability, good learning curve. If you've played board games like Village or Dominion this one will fall right into the place. You gather resources (adventurers), solve quests, interfere with your opponent's plans until finally you have enough influence to become the new ruler of Waterdeep. Though it can be hard at higher AI, it's still a rewarding experience (yes at times it frustrates me to no end, but then again it makes me cheer out loud when I do the right moves that win me the match). For now I have not found any bugs, glitches or errors. The only thing that I cannot comment on is the multiplayer option since I have not tried it yet. Do I recommend this game to lovers of board games and the Forgotten Realms? Yes I do. Buy it with expansions if able, it's worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

Once you figure out the basic strategy, you should win against the AI 80%+ of the time if you have average or better skill in strategy games.

This game is best suited for those:
a] Who want to play other people but don't want to physically meet up for a game.
b] People who want to play the tabletop game, but lack enough people to make the game interesting.
c] People interested in light, relaxing strategy games.

For everyone else, I would not recommend this game.
