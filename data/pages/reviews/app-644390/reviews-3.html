\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally enjoyed my first couple of plays of the game, enough that I went online and found a shop to buy the physical copy from so I can play with my gaming group next time we meet. Bought the expansion too, cause why not, expansions are fun!

THEN I tried actually playing a game with the expansions active, and found that the tutorial had not prepared me for how corruption worked. I chose the Gray Hand faction for myself, and proceeded to play, and got Xanathar. I immediately noticed that for him, instead of gaining bonus points for certain completed quest types as with other lords, you gain bonus points in the final scoring based on how much corruption you're holding. And I noticed that there were several cards and effects on the board with great bonuses that included taking corruption. I assumed there was some penalty to having corruption but the game never explained what that would be and I glossed right past the idea and began collection corruption and leaving my opponents in the proverbial dust. About half way through the game I notice my score isn't what I thought it was. I seem to be losing points. Perhaps I just hadn't counted correctly? Must be it... Hah! No! Turns out Corruption has powerful penalties that far outstrip Xanathar's bonuses, if you ignore the penalties. One being, if you are required to take more Corruption Tokens and none exist on the track to take, you lose points immediately from your score total. (I estimate that I lost about 50-80 points over the course of the game because of this - /facepalm). Finally in the 8th round of the match, I stopped and searched online for how corruption affects scoring. Found out all the details. Used every scrap of finagling I could imagine to boost my score some more while also putting corruption back on the track to reduce the final scoring penalty. Came close, lost by a mere 9 points.

Hell of a lot of fun. HOWEVER, if you are going to play with the Skullport Expansion, make sure you read up on how corruption affects the game and the final scoring, it can be a kick in the nuts.

Also note that for the physical board game, the Skullport and Undermountain expansions come in a single Scoundrels expansion pack, on Steam they must be bought separately. Totally worth it in my opinion and now I can't wait to play this with friends.

There is an online multiplayer available, I haven't tried it yet. It does require setting up an account to play and confirming the account via email before finalizing. But if you just want to play vs the AI you can do that in Offline Play no problem.

The AI comes in three difficulties, Easy, Average and Cunning. Haven't tried yet with Cunning but Average AI will take your shirt if you aren't paying attention.\t\t\t\t\t
