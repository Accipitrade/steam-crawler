\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes, it's a port. Yes, there are some oddities when it comes to clicking through to end the turn (would advise a measure of deliberateness when you're doing this) and yes, occasionally the game timer is a problem when playing with friends.

However, this is still an excellent game which is straightforward to teach, builds nicely across the DLC (which are not essential first up, but add spice in later games or longer games), and is ultimately a nice balance of take-that competitive gameplay and more internally focused engine building and strategy. I love the physical version of this game and enjoy the implementation digitally for the most part. Its age means it's a little bit clunky in places (having to set up online profiles separately to your steam info, for instance), but ultimately results in fun, enjoyable games with friends.

Please note, I play this exclusively with friends and my girlfriend - and in either case it's very straightforward and pleasant to use as 1up PvP or as local pass and play. Also the AI tuning is pretty ok. Unfortunately you cannot log two online accounts into one instance of the game to reduce the number of copies played, but it's relatively inexpensive on Steam, iOS and Android, and cross-play is enabled. We often play with friends on a mixture of tablets, phones and PCs.

Also, the touch implementation from the phone game carries across to the PC, so if you have a touch screen it's a nice experience too.

Hope that was useful to you, and it's a big thumbs up from me.\t\t\t\t\t
