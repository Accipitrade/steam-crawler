\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSeriously, what is up with these graphics? They're splash screen looks like a holdover from the SSI days of the Gold Boxes. The Infinite Space of a digital plane has been consigned to the physical equivalent of a snack tray table.

Graphical concerns aside this game is a faithful and sensible reproduction of the tabletop game. It's a light Euro worker placement game but I've always found it quick and enjoyable but I'm mostly impressed by how well they did the digital integration. In these times of covid-19, basic and unforgivable flaws are even more apparent on table to digital, I'm looking at you Pathfinder Adventure card game. This one has the sensible "confirmation step" after actions which means you're not locked in if you accidentally clicked something or if you forgot that your opponent had a card in play. The information isn't display quite as obviously as I would like but it's all there and legible. All in all: 8 out of 10\t\t\t\t\t

The AI is a little fast for being able to read other players cards, but it plays well and is true to the tabletop version. I would also recommend changing some timing settings when playing with friends online as sometimes there is a little bit of a lag when quite a few people are on, and every player is on a play clock. It really sucks to forfeit a game because of a lag and your timer run out. Just make sure to change that play timer setting before you play with friends online and you will absolutely enjoy this game.\t\t\t\t\t
