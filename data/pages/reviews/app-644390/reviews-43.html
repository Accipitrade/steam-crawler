\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy "hours played" stat is a little misleading, because I played the iOS version of Playdek's adaptation on the iPad for years.

This version is virtually identical to the iOS version, with a few exceptions. The most obvious is the fact that the game is horizontally oriented, instead of vertically. This is an unfortunate necessity, since monitors can't be oriented vertically. As such, the expansions --- and I strongly recommend getting that DLC -- have to be awkwardly crunched into the board, instead of elegantly just placed below the board. One has to scroll as well, and non-obvious tabs implemented to save space.

Gameplay-wise, it seems like AI opponents are a little less aggressive on 2 dot (normal) difficulty, as I found they attacked me a lot less. They also think longer on their strategy as well. Playing against AI on normal difficulty, however, was a piece of cake, provided I didn't get Xanathar, who is still the hardest Lord to play as.

Finding online players is hard, if you want to play more than a 1v1 game (which I personally don't enjoy. Mandatory quests should be a strategy, not obvious targets. And Open Lord becomes a game breaker.)

Otherwise, the game is a completely faithful adaptation of the board game, especially with the smaller things like the Gallery, which includes all of the flavor text found in the manuals. If you're a fan of the board game, this is a must-buy. Just play on highest difficulty for a challenge.
