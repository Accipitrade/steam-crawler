\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm sad to say it but this game is a poor government simulator. It's little more than a clone of the first Democracy game but with a different design and layout. You control spending and taxes through sliders and work to achieve certain set goals. Such goals include things like lowering the ratio of national debt to GDP, improving the state of infrastructure and cutting crime. It's a turn-based game like Democracy is.

Not once did I get the feeling that my actions were having much impact on the people who the government is meant to be serving. The way the economy performs in this game is just ridiculous at times. It's a long way departed from reality and in one of the game's scenarios, it just enters a major depression that seems to go on for years regardless of what you do to try combatting it. Democracy was poor at simulating economic development too, but at least some things you did, such as cutting the company tax rate significantly or spending money on scientific research, impacted on your economic growth in the long-run. Democracy also offered you the challenge of trying to keep different groups of society happy and you had to balance things carefully or face either being kicked out of office, or worse, assassination. At no point when playing Government Simulator did I feel like I was being kept on my toes. I felt like I couldn't care less about the outcome of what I did, since it was all so ridiculous and unrealistic.

Overall, I do not recommend this game unless you can buy it very cheap and are playing a government simulator for the first time. Turn-based government simulators were never going to be as fun as real-time ones anyway.
