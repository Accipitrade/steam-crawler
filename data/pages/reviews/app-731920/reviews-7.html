\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a simulator? Is that uh.. what we're calling it these days? Because this is like a joke.

Military invasions exist in here which I thought was really cool compared to Democracy 3. Sadly no one cares and it hardly seems to matter. I had invaded; Finland, Sweden, Poland, North Korea, South Korea, and a hell of a lot of China before I decided to quit because it became a 30 year slog for some odd reason, despite having a higher military score. Even after I used nuclear weapons. Like I mean, modern conflicts aren't gonna last that long in the best of situations to force slow invasions. But that beside, no one cared other than the nuclear weapons. I could just declare on anyone. No military alliances. Also I don't... see the purpose of these invasions. They don't seem to do anything other than allow more invasions. I couldn't really tell if it was even adding to my population count. Also for satelites, russia doesn't start out with Belarus as one in game, when it pretty much should.

So this game is like Democracy 3 in how you control stuff is through sliders. Simple enough, except then you realize you basically just want higher sliders, to allow higher sliders on other stuff. Mostly I mean, higher taxes so you can hieghten spending. At least for the most part. You'll also want to put in some laws and none of them seem to go against eachother so it's not even like strategic. It's just the process of implementing most of them since they pretty much all have a net benefit. Except flat tax which I wonder why it's even an option since in both real life and the game it seems to be worthless and stupid.

Oh yeah and some didn't even work very well and the laws aren't very well implemented either. There is one dedicated anti corruption law and having it on high didn't seem to mean very much. It's difficult to tell how much something will impact everything. Dictatorship seemed to heighten corruption for some weird reason despite corruption correlating to bribes and dictatorship only seemed to turn off elections mattering. But no one cared I still had pretty much above 90% approval from everyone, which I guess is like real Russia so there's that.

It's not very difficult, it's not very in depth, the only factor seperating this from another game might be actual stats but I don't actually know the stats so I can't even say if they're legit, and military invasions which factor in very little. Buy it for like $1 or hope it improves severely. Otherwise just get Democracy 3 if you actually want an in depth or replayable experience, even if I don't like Dem 3 much better.\t\t\t\t\t

After 5 minutes of gameplay as Russia (the hardest nation in the game) I had a 90% approval rating. 5 more minutes of gameplay and that sunk to 30% but by then I had established a dictatorship, which is the key to my critique of this ''game'':

==On the core game itself==
It's basically just clicking forward in time (to the next quarter) to get more influence points to be able to implement changes, which 90% of the time will simply be adjusting the budget slider for various things like infrastructure or law enforcement to keep up with the ever-increasing demand. Literally the whole game is just sliding the slider and clicking forward in time. That's it. Literally thats it. The whole ''laws'' system is just a gimmick since once implemented all you'll be left doing is, you guessed it, sliding the budget slider. The effect of change caused by budget adjustments is extremely fast and absurd compared to actual goverment (This game is no more a simulator than a kitten is a tiger)

==On diplomacy, or total lack thereof==
The game started in 2014 with Russia in conflict with ukraine and by 2029 I had invaded and turned into satelites Ukraine, Belarus and Georgia with no negative repercussions whatsoever, no sanctions, no embargoes, nothng. International diplomacy is nonexistant which means that as long as your population and military budget is strong enough you can gobble up small nations with no negative ramifications whatsoever.

==Conclusion==
[Pros]
None whatsoever,

[Cons]
-The gameplay is just you sliding a bar to change things from 'not good' to 'excellent' and waiting turns to get the action points to do so
-The aesthetics of the game are drab and remind me of a toilet seat made in communist eastern europe in the 1980's
-Not at all worth the price I mean are you kidding me, bar sliding simulator for this amount of money?

Don't buy it and spend your money on something else. I know I will.
