\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMuch like its protagonist, despite being chaotic, somewhat vulgar and off-putting in appearance, Dialtown has its heart in the right place. Playing it was a delightful experience and I had more fun than I had perhaps in years. The premise, plot and sense of humor are all wonderfully insane, however, focusing only on the absurd and comedy would do this game a disservice. As surprising as it is, once the mold of cynicism and crassness is scrubbed off, it gives way to an abundance of unexpectedly heartfelt and wholesome themes. I recommend this game to anyone who needs a little laugh to help them get through another tiring day and a little reminder that despite all, it is worth it. As long as you're up for some admittedly off the rails shenanigans, I reckon you will have a good time.

The one thing I suppose is worth pointing out that this felt more like a cryptid babysitting simulator than a dating sim. Not that it's a bad thing in the slightest. I found it immensely entertaining. Just remember to save from time to time, as to not end up with your cryptid dead, high on coughing syrup and in prison.

5/5 game. It knows what it wants to do and delivers on that. Sure, it's pretty deranged while doing so but, hey, aren't we all?
