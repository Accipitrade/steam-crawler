\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthis game accomplishes what it sets out to do, and it does it (relatively) well. it caught my interest enough for me to sink about 15-20 hours or whatever into it to try to get every ending, and not just the bare minimum to 100% it, which means it's worth something. despite that though i do very tentatively and conditionally hit recommending versus not recommending here.

its style is very fun and pretty special to the game, and it manages to pull off the weird look flawlessly. it's obvious theres been a lot of love put into the presentation of the game and it shows. the music and visuals is unique AND well put together. chefs kiss!

a lot of visual novels, especially romance genre types like this, tend to wring out their characters with some bare minimum thought and then throw them aside once the route ending is achieved. this game on the other hand almost relishes in giving romance targets flaws and issues, and treating them like actual people instead of some cgi anime boy on the screen to kiss and sell merch of. despite the hurdles posed by the comedy (explained in a moment), i still found myself getting invested in the stories of the characters and the town here.

my biggest point of critique is that the sense of humor of this game 1. permeates pretty much the entire thing, for the most part, and 2. is a little like. hmm. hyperspecific i guess? unless you're deep in the trenches of tumblr's goblincore tag or something, a lot of the humor will probably fall flat on you, or even annoy you. i'm fortunate enough to be familiar with this kind of joke but not enough to actually laugh about it myself. despite this i think if you were raised on like, flipnote hatena early 2010s smosh humor (i say this lovingly) you'll probably enjoy at least a few bits here and there? it appeals to a really semi-niche audience, but i imagine if i was part of that audience, this would be the funniest game i've played all year, so maybe that's a bonus to you!

another critique point comes moreso from a place of concern that i hope/think? the audience and the creator(s) may be receptive to at least thinking about for the future (and i'm not saying this point makes the creator poopoo trashman or the game totally unplayable lol). this part kind of contains spoilers so i'll cover it up. i found it confusing at first why karen's route is only available if your head is a typewriter, and later learned this is supposed to indicate karen's a lesbian through the world lore of men have phone heads and women have typewriter heads. i think there's no problem with making karen a lesbian but the way this is executed specifically by making her route only available to typewriter-heads, and not women (or nonbinary characters too, i suppose?), does put a weird taste in my mouth. you can be a man with a typewriter head, or a woman with a phone head, and the former allows you to date a lesbian while the latter does not. (i'm using 'date' very loosely here, ykwim). again i'm not saying the creator sucks for doing this since i doubt it was malicious, given how the rest of the game is about LGBT stuff, but you can see the issue here, i hope (the issue being a weird fantasy bio-essentialism going on?)

anyways yeah. quirky little game that's probably a lot more fun to play if you like feral mushroom gremlincore creechur humor, but still has a lot of heart put into it, which is more than i can say for most triple A games nowadays. tentatively recommended!\t\t\t\t\t
