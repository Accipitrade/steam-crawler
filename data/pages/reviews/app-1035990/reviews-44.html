Intro, not the review
I was a fan of this developer since the DsaF2 days.
I went into this game both expecting not much, and a lot.
Honestly? I forgot about this game, and when I did remember the game's existence I saw that it was coming out in a week.
I played it blind, I got to the ending.
Is it a meme game? Yes.
Does it rely on being stupid and meme-y for you to like it? No.
If you see this as a weird game and plan on playing it once and then being done with it, it might not be worth it for you.
However, if you plan on doing everything it's worth the price and beyond.

By the way this is my first review of this type, the template and the things I'm rating were chosen by me.

The review of Dialtown

Primary
Gameplay
The gameplay consists of... "People" talking, and sometimes doing stuff. Yeah that's about it. The enjoyment comes entirely from how you'll enjoy reading the text. Must say the dialogue has evolved a lot from the original DsaF. The charm of Dog Man's games comes from: choices . You have A LOT of choices. Play as a kind soul trying to get the happy ending? Play as a kind soul who shoots Little Billy? Play as Satan himself? Play as a failed bank robber? All are your choices. It's very simple, but I guess that's the point of a Visual Novel is it not? Gameplay is very simple, yet well done. 0.5 pts.
Mechanics
The mechanics are... Clicking on prompts, choosing your name, saving the game... But the most important part are the choices. You can play as your own version of the green monster, and that is gnarly. Simple, very simple, maybe even too simple, however it has a nice charm to it. 0.5 pts
Visuals
The visuals are mostly just the dog's photoshops of environments and the actors for the game. Rarely, you will see artwork. Both look great, in my opinion, but I do think there is a certain charm the artwork version of Mingus has over the photoshop. Not to say that the photoshop (or any) is truly soulless (except for you greg.). It works and I like seeing the funny swan man dance. 1 pts
Audio
The audio effects themselves sound.. like generic copyright free sounds. It works, and sometimes adds to the charm, however I can't exactly boost the score for it. 0.5 pts
Functionality
I myself have never ran into a bug. The only complaint I really have regarding functionality is that you can't go back once you click on new game other than closing the game itself. I'd say a tiny problem such as that shouldn't matter much since it will probably be fixed in the future ( I hope so). 1 pts

Secondary
Story
The story... Exists. It is rather nice to find all the pieces or just ignore it completely. As much as I like it, it's not exactly the most memorable. 0.5 pts
Characters
The characters feel like either actual "people" or jokes (or references to a certain pizza place named bear.). They all have their charm. Karen is bored and wants to do her job in fear of judgment. Randy is Randy. Oliver is a geek of old fashion stuff like ol' cinema and such, aswell as liking gnarly things like 6 nipples on one person. Bigfoot. Little Billy is a demon which is supposed to be a child. The corrupt mayor Mingus that constantly has gestures like her head is in pain, be it story reasons or that you are that much of a pain. The only other character I can mention is God , who is too lazy to make stuff and decides to just be a hobo. Yeah he's my favorite. 1 pts
Difficulty
There... is none. Other than pleasing Karen or trying to get a specific ending. Difficulty, you can't lower it nor make it harder. Yeah it's a no sadly. 0 pts
Soundtrack
This soundtrack surprised me. I did not expect any music, let alone an original soundtrack. I would actually sit and enjoy the music from time to time. They fit the situation they are in, however the sad song does sound a bit generic but that's like one song out of 70. My favorites are like the characters, God's theme and Mingus' theme. 1 pts
