\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI genuinely really enjoy this game. I came from DirectDogman's DSAF / Dayshift at Freddy's series, and I am more than impressed. I can genuinely tell that he has improved within his writing, and I (as a previous phone guy enjoyer) enjoy the fact that there are cameos and references to the other games that I love as well. The characters in Dialtown are all very different from each other, and I feel like not a single one is poorly written. I invest in all of them, and love to learn little things about ALL of them... none of them disinterest me. The dialogue options are, while sometimes horrendous to get through, humorous and gutwrenchingly disgusting at the same time. I wonder a lot of the time how under the influence someone would have to be to make something like this up, but as stated, I enjoy it nonetheless. I've replayed it entirely over 3 times, and I enjoy it each time. It's a very silly game... but the characters also have lore, so it's different from a lot of the things I usually see or play. It stands out in such a good way that I feel like ANYONE would be able to enjoy it, it's a calm game, just has a lot of reading (as any dating simulator would.) I don't dislike any of the characters, either, but my personal favorite would probably be Mingus. I would 100% recommend this game, despite being fearful that my friends will be dragged down the eternal hellpit that is this game 👍👍\t\t\t\t\t
