\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't often leave reviews, so this might not be as descriptive of well-written as some like, but;

I wish I could erase my memories of this game.

Just so that I can play through it all over again and experience it fresh for the first time.

Literally some of the best money I've ever spent on a visual novel, and well-worth it.

Each of the chapters--the characters--are lovable or relatable in some way (for better or worse), the dialogue is always entertaining regardless of who it comes from, and I felt things I didn't expect to feel given the game's tone. I laughed out loud--hard--numerous times throughout, the wit is sharp (though often crude) and the comedic timing (where appropriate) is impeccable.

Some might accuse the game of having a "LOLRANDUM" sense of humour, but it's so much more than that; even if the main-character is a little off-the-wall, it's done so well that you can't help but become engrossed in your new identity as a near-literal goblin.

Please do yourselves a favour and pick this up if you like the more off-beat visual novels, although I'm still not overly interested in dating them because they're phones, I'd be more than willing to date most of these wonderful characters because of what (mostly) wholesome (mostly-)humans they are.

And I had to go back and rewrite this final section multiple times for fear of spoiling too much, but the final chapter is great, and in my opinion, rounds out and finishes the game perfectly. I was worried it would feel tacked-on and rushed, but it was arguably the best chapter of the game (which is saying something as every chapter had fantastic strokes that still stick in my head weeks after finishing).

Amazing job, Dogman. (And thanks again for the Little Billy screech.) Regardless of how long it takes, I'll be eagerly awaiting your next project.\t\t\t\t\t
