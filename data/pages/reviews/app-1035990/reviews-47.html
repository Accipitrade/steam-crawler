\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo be completely honest, I'd usually put some dumb memey review since, y'know, DT comes off as such. But actually? Love-able absurdity aside, Dialtown is a dang good game. I've stuck around since I found the demo about 2 years ago at the beginning of quarantine and *finally* being able to see it come to fruition was really something. Sure, it gets crazy and the some of the endings are out of left field, but the good endings (at least from what I've seen as of posting this!) are incredibly well written and sweet (or bittersweet, depending on the date-able). I've genuinely was moved to tears over this silly little game. Multiple times. And I was so glad that I got to experience it.
You've done good, Dog. You've done good.
EDIT: Coming back after completing the final route. Absolutely doubling down on the "moved to tears bit" while also tacking on a "left me absolutely *speechless*". I'm happy to say that DT is the first game I've played *period* that has made me laugh, cry, and made me sit in 20 minutes of absolute silence out of awe and shock. Worth every cent and more. I also didn't say this in my first rendition of my review but the composer, artists and voice actors did an ABSOLUTELY stellar job on this, which *especially* shines through in the final route. Took my already high expectations I saw from teasers within the discord, BLEW THEM UP into a MILLION pieces and then proceeded to make something a million times better.
Please play Dialtown.\t\t\t\t\t
