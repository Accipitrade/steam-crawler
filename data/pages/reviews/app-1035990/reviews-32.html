\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDirectDogman, the creator of the Dayshift at Freddy's 1, 2 and 3 brings you another hilarious, charming visual novel named Dialtown. Dialtown: Phone Dating Sim is well... a dating sim where you date phones(and also a typewriter and also Bigfoot who is also a phone).

You play as yourself, a Cryptid who is green and wants to lay his/her/they/their/(insert your own pronouns) eggs in the funfair, but you have to get someone to pay you in. You go looking for someone to pay you in, but in the end that's not the only thing you go to look for.

From the characters, writing, music, artsyle and humour, Dialtown oozes charm in every aspect of this experience.
Every character has amazing dialogue(including yourself) and every interaction you have with every character will leave you with a smile on your face or laughing with tears, the writing is just THAT good. I went out of my way to click every available text option to see what happens and I was never disappointed. For a game so funny, it hits hard during emotional scenes and such, with the music changing and the atmosphere getting heavy, you can really FEEL these characters pour their hearts out. Every dateable character has an enchanting story with MULTIPLE ENDINGS EACH.

The music in this game is so simple but amazing, with every place and character having their own unique theme, with the character ones explaining their personality without words at ALL. Every scene is amplified with the music and it really sets the tone for everything. EVEN THE NPCs HAVE THEIR OWN UNIQUE THEMES. My favourite song, Tender stroking, makes EVERY SCENE HIT HARD. It's the first thing that's made me feel emotions for a video game in so long. The entire soundtrack is available on most streaming services but I URGE you to buy it to support the developers.

Every character, from the dateables to the random NPCs, have charm to them through their dialogue, look or both. Everyone, AND I MEAN EVERYONE, in this game is likeable. From Ticket Jerry to Randy to Theoroar Rustlebelt to everyone else, they all have great dialogue exchanges with you and eachother, making you laugh, cry or something in between. Like I said before, each have their OWN THEME to accompany them, and they all suit their intended character perfectly. Each character also has their own font, so that's a nice attention to detail.

The humour in this game is phenomenal, through uses of slightly making the font larger, more spread out and a different colour, makes something kinda funny into hilarious. Every character has something to say, and it's ALWAYS FUNNY. I have never seen such good humour in a video game since well... Dayshift at Freddy's(which reminder, was ALSO made by DirectDoggo). All the comedic timing is on point as well, and when it isn't, IT'S INTENTIONAL.

There is also so many little easter eggs scattered throughout the game and even an Extras menu with stuff when you finish the game. Also this game has achievements with great art, which is an oddity for games.

Some convenient tools the game has is: the ability to save whenever(so when you get an ending you can go back and try a different ending), multiple save files(30 I think) and the ability to see what you have to do to unlock chapter 3(with the final dateable character) with DETAIL. All of this makes the game SO MUCH BETTER, and it's weird to see other visual novels NOT have these things that MAKE LIFE EASIER.

Overall: For being a visual novel, this game has such amazing writing, characters, humour and music that every visual novel should aspire to. There is no gameplay in this VISUAL NOVEL, and yet I had more fun playing this game over many games of recent years. EVERY visual novel should strive to be as charming and funny as this, but that will never happen(unless DirectDogman makes another game).
10/10 I LITERALLY cannot recommend more, an amazing visual novel all around.\t\t\t\t\t
