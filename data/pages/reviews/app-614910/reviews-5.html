\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have just started this game and it is pretty awesome. Not for the quality of graphics, not for the high speed fluid animations. For a few reasons though.

It is a dollar. No game is a real gamble at that price, so let us count that as the first plus.
It has quite fun gameplay - it is a match 3 variant (Oh noes!) but more than tolerable - you link up 6 images to form the next step up.
The cards and badges are, if not inspired, pleasant enough.
For achievement hunting perfect game seekers, it gets that out of the way in about 40 seconds.

So why would I say that a match 3 easy to perfect game is worth a look for anyone other than those seeking easy validation?

Hang onto your hats, boys and girls. 3 times every second day you get free stickers in the game and these are 1) pretty enough that people want them and 2) tradable.

That is what actually made me get the game, I saw that there was a certain flag in it and thought I would have a look. So evidently the business model is that people collect the things for themselves in-game (satisfy the completionists), trade on rare ones and the dev gets the standard Steam cut of sales, have fun with a pleasant little short burst gaming effort or just Get their perfect game for a dollar and move on.

Personally, I intend to play it now and again in the hopes of getting the free sticker I am looking for but the game itself is different from the other things I play so I am sure I will keep enjoying it. 3 times a day every 2 days, haha.

For anyone who wants to know how the actual game works, play it for 30 seconds and you have the idea. It is a 5x5 grid where you are looking to convert the lowest form into the next by matching three, not necessarily straight lines, just not diagonal.

The basic order is Scully (looks like a skull) - Caty (Guess what, a cat) - Zombus (Yep) - Trashy - Deviller - Grand Wolf (from badge name, though I thought it was a bear). When you link 3 or more of the final form it explodes clearing more space and you get a power up to use later, like a smart bomb for those of you old enough to remember when games were awful but we loved them anyway. Object of the game is to get a high score for the leaderboards (where it seems some people have hacked themselves on but I despair) but truly you will keep playing and coming back for the collecting. And maybe the profits.

If you ever forget the order of the progression for tactical placement, just have a look at the cards/emotes - they mirror the same order as in the game.\t\t\t\t\t
