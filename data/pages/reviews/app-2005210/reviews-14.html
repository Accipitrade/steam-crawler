\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother excellent "Swarm Survivor"

Cool graphics, tight controls (plays great with a controller), inventive upgrades, and a sick soundtrack (don't usually buy soundtracks, but will be getting this one for sure).

I only have 90 min in the game so far, but I have several hours in both the Prologue and the Demo. My first run I unlocked 16 items including 3 new pilots. I like how there were weapons and upgrades in the demo that will need to be unlocked in the game, an excellent way to preview what a future run might look like. I had a heck of a time unlocking the 2 pilots in the latest Demo, mostly due to RNG, but shouldn't be too difficult now that I have access to Permanent Upgrades. I also really like how the different pilots have very different playstyles. Hopefully the dev follows the pattern of the other Survivor-likes with lots of new content like weapons, pilots, stages, etc. which will keep things fresh for months to come.
Overall I'd say if you're a fan of 20 Minuets Till Dawn, Gunlocked, Rogue Genesia, Bounty of One, Brotato, and of course Vampire Survivors (among many others), this will fit right in to your Swarm Survival collection. Try the Demo/Prologue and see for yourself.\t\t\t\t\t

Its a solid Survivors-like
The speed and the overall control you have over the ships in this game is REALLY something else. The aesthetic is sparse without being dull or too minimalist. The color pallet is really solid for its overall choice in tone and feel. Survivors likes tend to get over cluttered with their screens and this game has the potential for that, but thanks to the color pallets of the stages and ships, everything reads as crisp and futuristic.
I haven't made it very far into the game yet but its one that I can already tell I'm going to be jumping back into when I get the itch for a quick round or 3.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am happy for all of us that this guy is "just" a dev and not a chemist. Whatever he'd be cooking up I'd snort head over backside if it's anything like this game.

Yes, meaning I find the game addictive and the way its upgrade system works leads to extremely fun combinations. If you liked Risk of Rain one and spent at least some significant time in an arcade, there is a high chance you might like this game.

I really don't want to go into the mechanics, because they are really not that complicated but still fun to find out. Which is another thing this game does really well. Simple, but not uncomplex, it offers a good variety of how to play it and certainly rewards reflexes, persistence and the will to experiment.

This is the game if gradius and asteroids had a child and married that child to risk of rain who then had this game.

It's hard to fit it into a real category, because it's arcady, rogue-like, upgrade bullet hell isn't something that appears very often. And from what I see, it's a very, very good game in that category.

I already loved the demo, but this game is really fun and for the price, more than worth it. Also, the soundtrack is a total banger.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI spent a fair bit of time with the demo of this game, so this review is informed by that as well as having tried out the new features available in the full version for a couple of hours.

I've not played many others in this (sub)genre - vampire survivors being the most notable I imagine - but I definitely enjoy the core loop of upgrading my ship and experimenting with different builds. The demo was already pretty fun to replay and e.g. get different ultimate weapons or use some strategies, and the full game expands on that. The way you unlock upgrades, weapons and characters pretty much guarantees you'll be getting new options every time you play that encourage you to play again. On top of that being able to buy permanent upgrades let you tweak the gameplay to your style.

The second to second gameplay itself might seem quite twitchy at first, but I found once I got the hang of it then I'd have an easier time once I was planning for how I wanted to shepherd the enemies rather than reacting in panic.

FWIW I played this on my steamdeck and the experience was flawless, so if you're wanting a game that you can quickly drop in and out of on the go this is a great choice. A run only takes 20 minutes or so if you go all the way, so it's low commitment, and there are plenty of easy points to pause and resume as needed.
