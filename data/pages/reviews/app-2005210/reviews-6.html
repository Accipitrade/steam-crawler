1. It needs it's own hook. Something that makes it stand out.
2. The progression has to be engaging.
3. The music has to slap.(as the kids say these days)

Void Scrappers wildly succeeds in all three categories in my mind. The space aspect makes controlling the game differ from the competition in a great way. This game is an easy recommend, and has only been growing since it was released within the last 2 months.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMovement feels fantastic! That's the first thing you need to know. I feel like I'm gliding through space, navigating tight, dangerous paths between enemy ships. There' just the right amount of inertia to the ship, but without losing the precise control necessary for a game of this type. I really, really love it.

You have a good number of unique pilots/ships to play as each has unique abilities which heavily lean their play style in a certain direction and create a nice variety of experience.

I feel like the permanent upgrade purchases are, like most of the "Survivor's Like" games, necessary for "growth" in the game, but pretty boring. I honestly feel that 20 Minutes Till Dawn, has some of the most interesting perma-upgrades in their rune system. But this is just your standard, "passive increases to basic stats." Ok, yawn.

The general play is what you'd expect, with ok variety from the available weapon types. Your pilot/ship being the most influential choice to overall gameplay. Again, it feels fantastic flying your ship, to me perhaps one of the strongest aspects of an otherwise, solid (if somewhat vanilla for the genre) game.
The Bosses are disappointing to me. They tend to just be a "its time to kite" event. They're always right on your butt as you drag them across the screen, and most of the "challenge" of a boss comes from them bullying you through the crowd of other enemy ships. So if there's no other ships, it's a yawn. If there's tons it can just be "dead time now." I feel like, with this game there's more potential for an interesting boss fight.

I'd like to see a few more weapon types.
I think it'd be nice to have some more interesting, ship specific permanent upgrades to be unlocked, and which must be chosen from (you can have A, or B, but not both). I like the idea of perma upgrades which actually change gameplay instead of just, "more dmg, more often, more xp..."

All in all, if you like Vampire Survivors and its ilk, I think this is a good game at a good price, with potential to be even better\t\t\t\t\t
