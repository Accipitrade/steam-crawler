\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVoid Scrappers is notable for being both a relatively early addition to the genre and one of the first with a sci-fi theme. The speed and maneuverability of the player ship is exhilarating, especially when using the very generous (invincible) dash. Then we have the impressive sci-fi arsenal, which builds on the formula established by Vampire Survivors by adding orbiting melee drones, laser-refracting prisms, and bullets that boomerang back towards their source, just to name a few. The result is a game that feels very different than almost any other survivior-like/auto-shooter I've played.

Although the game is no longer in Early Access, it doesn't feel fully fleshed out. Enemies are one-dimensional, simply chasing after the player and/or shooting occasional projectiles. The developer also released a patch a few months ago that introduced asteroids, which sounds like a neat idea, but the reality is that this is the only random event that occurs outside of enemy/boss spawns. I'd love to see some more curveballs, such as enemies carrying loot/power-ups or different types of enemy waves. Nonetheless, Void Scrappers is definitely worth checking out if you like going fast and blowing up aliens, even if the runs themselves start to blend together after a while.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a solid game that I can recommend for the current price of 4€ I guess. However, I highly recommend to get Vampire Survivors (VS) instead if you have not played that yet, it's better.

Pros for Void Scappers when compared to VS:
+ moving around is faster and more fun than in VS, and precise movement is an important part of the game.

Cons:
- Void Scrappers has less upgrades, less weapons, less characters
- Void Scrappers has no interesting interactions between weapons (much more important)
- in Void Scrappers, it feels like you are getting more powerful in a rather linear fashion during a run, as opposed to the rather exponential gain in power in VS. The end of a run feels a lot more boring in this game.
- You move very quick compared to enemies, and thus it does not feel like you are attracting a mass that you push back with firepower. Instead, you circle around a ball of enemies and dodge through it as often as possible to deal a small bit of extra damage if you have the upgrade for that. It takes ages to kill stuff as you only hit very few enemies per shot.

7.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

There are 9-10 pilots or so you can unlock via playing the game and doing certain things like "dash 100 times in 1 run" and stuff like that.

The mobs are simply, they just head straight towards you and as you get further some will shoot at you. If you can make it to the 20 min mark and get to threat level 40, the "big" boss will spawn that will most likely end you run unless you have upgraded your ship enough to kill it.

I will say this though, it can be very ease to loose track of yourself if your the kind of person to get tunnel vision due to the amount of stuff on the screen, which just gets more intense the further you get, since you need to backtrack in order to get scrap and doing so will put you in the way of incoming ships.

This is definitely more challenging then VS since there is a skill component, you cant just sit there, get the meta weapons and afk your way to victory like in VS (although you could just fly in a straight line but then you will miss out on a ton of scrap aka exp).

Over all this is a good fun game for the price, and if you can get it on sale then theres a very high chance that you will get at least 1 hour of entertainment out of it for every one dollar you spend on it.\t\t\t\t\t
