\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game but still needs some work in order to keep my interest. Feels like it should still be early access because of lack of a few more mechanics. (that is the short of it, below is the long of it), but what it has, it executes extremely well and with some flair, like messages popping up from base when you upgrade a weapon (more of that with some variation plz).

My issues and recommendations:
Game begs for a twin stick mechanic, even if for only aiming the main weapon. Would be nice to have an option for auto or manual firing. Currently right stick does nothing but my right thumb keeps forgetting this.

Dash move seems more dangerous than helpful most of the time because it lasts too long and the acceleration feels a bit spongy or maybe elastic is a better word, draggy, does not go fast enough initially to be optimally useful (it looks awesome however). I end up feeling like I am careening out of control, when I really just want a fast burst that goes maybe a 2.5-3.5 ship lengths, just to get out of a tight spot, and maybe a faster recharge or a multiple boost ability. (Maybe there are more ships/ugprades in the game that I have not seen with different mechanics like this already. If so I apologize for an ignorant complaint.)

General movement is really smooth and fast, but feels almost too fast, sometimes I want to be able to slow down a bit. (without slowing the entire game down, which is an option in the menus, and that is nice)
I had two ideas in this regard. First idea is just an anti-dash button (inertia dampener) that slows our top speed down by about 1/3, and while it is held, our firing speed increases, maybe bullet velocity too...and maybe it drains your dash stamina about the same speed as it normally recharges (after empty) and then once empty, has to fully recharge before the inertia dampener can be used again. Letting go of the inertia dampener would allow it to snap back the recharge significantly faster than normal, so going from dampened to a dash is not interrupted too terribly.
Second idea involves a twin stick mechanic for firing. Slow down the base speed by about 15%, then when both sticks are pointed in the same direction (within about a 5 wedge) your ship slows down about 15-20% and as the variance between aim and steering grows towards 180 (again with a 5 wedge) your speed increases to a max speed 15%-20% faster than base speed. Let go of right stick, ship returns to base speed. Maybe even add a little bit of an inertial swerve mechanic for shooting sideways...(big ask I know, but it would be SO COOL just had to mention it).

I really like how the weapons are separated from the passive upgrades. You get weapons from bosses and passives from leveling. I do feel though, like an additional upgrade mechanic is missing...like relics or alien tech slots that maybe modify a weapon in exotic ways, or modify the dash, or if there were twin stick mechanics, maybe could add a lock on mechanic when you pass your aim line through an enemy, etc.

For the price, it is a lot of fun, even if you only play it once or twice and ultimately get maybe 30 minutes of joy or at least time passed less insufferable than watching The View or pleasuring the missus... Buy it, even if it's not on sale. Toss a coin to your Developers... It's less than a Big Mac and probably more nutritional.\t\t\t\t\t
