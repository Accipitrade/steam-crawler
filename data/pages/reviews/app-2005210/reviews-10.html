\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a boatload of pixel eye candy roguelike upgrade mania fun and I've super enjoyed it. As a game dev myself it's also given me a bit of insight into a genre I'm not as familiar with and shown me how a small-ish project can still be a fantastic experience.

- the difficulty curve is great
- the "permanent upgrades" aren't so dramatic that the first few runs feel unplayable, while still being meaningful
- the various characters you can play as have definite tangible pros and cons to distinguish between them
- the upgrade pick and ban system provides a nice way to shape your upgrade path without making it totally deterministic
- the different weapon types are really fun to combo and mess around with
- the game is difficult and you die a lot -- well, I do... -- but it's still fun (sort of like Hades)
- the devs seem to listen well to the community and it's gotten better since release!

Cons:
- the game really needs a color theory overhaul, there are so many visibility issues on a screen where your eyes have to visually differentiate between hazardous enemy projectiles/ships and your own lasers or orbitals can take on colors like yellows and reds that make it nearly impossible to dodge hazards -- I would strongly recommend making the super orbitals, for example, maintain more of a muted green color of the less upgraded ones and focus on shape and size to differentiate weapon upgrades instead of color. Going the route of a projectiles-only build, this is a non-issue.

Conclusion: absolutely worth paying for this game -- great job devs!\t\t\t\t\t
