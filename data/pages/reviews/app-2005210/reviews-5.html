\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are better games out there, even in this price range.

This is more like a school project than a professionally made game, which is fine in itself. But not something I would recommend you to spend your money or your time on.

You only have to play for a short while to see everything the game has to offer, and there is no real re-play value here either.

One stage/background only; enemy variety is close to none with there being just small non-bosses, bigger non-bosses, bosses, and one final boss, and they all act the same (homing in you); no high score; no new game plus or endless mode; if you continue to play after you have defeated the final boss the game will eventually glitch out more and more (due to the inability to handle it's own resources) until it finally overloads and freezes/crashes; there is one weapon type that is superior to everything else that makes you win everytime; no really interesting mechanics, just the same old basic tropes done in an uninspired way. Also several odd/bad UI and UX designs.

This is a game I would have expected to find on a shovelware collection for C64/Amiga/MSDOS or in appstore for phones/tablets.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVoid Scrappers gives you one pilot to start with and from there you get to vamp—space alien slaying. Those familiar with the recently popularized genre will immediately recognize many mechanics. Your weapons fire automatically, destroyed enemies drop XP orbs (called scrap here) which you have to collect, and once your XP (scrap) bar is full you get to choose an upgrade.

There are a few differences from the usual formula; your ship has a short speed boost which gives you a brief window of immunity and you only choose new weapons from killing bosses. Bosses for the most part are regular enemies with a larger health pool who will hunt you down and follow you at a greater speed than regular enemies. Once destroyed you collect the item they drop and are given a choice of 1 of 3 weapons. The weapons come in 4 unique types; lasers, projectiles (bullets), orbitals, and explosives. You can mix and match weapons or choose the same one up to 3 times. Upon choosing the same weapon a third time you will get the ULTIMATE version. This combines all three and replaces them with a powered-up version that has a unique way of operating. One of my favorites is a single laser that rotates around your ship 360 degrees and when upgraded to its ultimate version 4 lasers will rotate around your ship instead.

Void Scrappers gets a lot of things right. The music is great, with a lot of changing tracks as you go through wave after wave of enemies. The pixel art is great as well, each pilot has their unique ship, and each run is on a random stage with different colored and designed enemy ships. These are nice touches to keep runs feeling a little different and allow the player to not get burnt out on seeing the same enemy or hearing the same song for the 100th time. The controls are also very tight and make movement feel fun. Allowing you to avoid enemies that pop up from off-screen.

For me, it seems none of the games in this genre are meant to be particularly difficult. Instead, they let the player have fun with a wide cast of characters and unique weapons. The fun comes from seeing how powerful you can get and how easily you can clear each wave of enemies. Unfortunately, this is where Void Scrappers missed the mark for me. I found out over my following runs that a big issue is how weapons and power-ups for those weapons dropped separately. Bosses give you weapons but level-ups give you damage boosts. This means getting a shiny new weapon only to have it hit like a wet noodle. If you don't stick to the weapon type you already invested in or you didn't get damage increases you are out of luck, whether it is in ultimate form or not. This compounds with the fact that you unlock countless power-up options but are only offered 3 at a time. So you can go countless levels without ever getting any stronger if you get unlucky. Which happened far too often than I liked. This led to me circling a boss tickling them for minutes at a time far too often.

Overall Void Scrappers is a fun addition to the genre that just needs some tweaking to increase the consistency of fun. If the developers change a few things and continue to update the game (which they have done with small updates as of writing this review) it can only improve. My suggestion would be to increase the power-up choices to 4 for each level up. Or at least guarantee damage-ups far more often/combine a damage-up with weapon drops. If you are looking for a new game to play then Void Scrappers has plenty of unlocks and fun weapons combinations to sink hours into. And it is only $4.\t\t\t\t\t
