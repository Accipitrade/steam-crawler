\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA solid addition to whatever genre Vampire Survivors is in. Unfortunately, it suffers from two major issues.

First is that the random upgrades you draw are flooded with uninteresting raw boosts (increased damage, duration, reduced cooldown), compounded by the fact that these boosts are per weapon type and there are three weapon types as of the writing of this review. These cards not only have a higher inherent value, but also drown out the drop chance of much more interesting and creative cards. Cards such as ones that encourage you to use your dash offensively instead of defensively or cards that change the way weapons behave. Additionally, these raw cards are not capped in quantity, merely having diminishing returns. The end result is that you are not only rarely given the opportunity to make an impactful choice, but when you are the game discourages you from doing so.

The second problem is that balance is all over the place. For example, "projectile" type weapons have low base damage, no AOE, no inherent pierce, limited range and slow travel speed. The card that increases their damage does so in increments of %10. Explosives on the other hand have much higher base damage, massive base AOE, the same or higher range than "projectiles", similar fire rate, and have no travel time. The card that increases their damage does so in increments of %30. Yeeeaah...

Overall it's a fun game worth your money. The things I mentioned could very well change in the future. If not, then all hail our lord and savior Pyrek, in Hull Deflector Blast we trust.\t\t\t\t\t
