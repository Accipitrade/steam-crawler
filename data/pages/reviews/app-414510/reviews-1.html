\r\n\t\t\t\t\t5089 is a worthy offering. If you enjoy the game play loop associated with looter shooters (think Borderlands) and you don't mind the primitive looking graphics (think Virtua Fighter), go ahead and scoop this one up.

While it can be hard to get into at first, once you've run some quests, skilled up, and found a weapon that suits your play style it becomes a lot of fun.

Your character is highly customizable. Just keep dumping skill points where you want to improve and while you're earning more of those, combine parts for weapons and equipment as you find them. When you don't feel like you're improving, push out to a harder area. The world is procedurally generated and functionally endless so you can keep doing this for as look as you want.

There are some weak points that you may not enjoy, of course. The 3-D graphics are low poly and straight out of the early 90's. There's a tiny bit of a story in there but it isn't much and is finished quickly. If you're looking for cutting edge visuals and masterful storytelling, this isn't the game you're looking for.

If you're into solid game play, telling your own emergent story, and finding your own fun (do you have an imagination?), then this game is definitely worth it.
