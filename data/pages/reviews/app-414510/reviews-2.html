\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had fun with 3089, but this version is a buggy mess (especially in coop) and the gameplay is starting to get really old and boring and the flaws are becoming too glaring to overlook.

Bugs: Enemies often skip all over rather than moving smoothly, making fast moving enemies impossible to hit. I'm playing over LAN and even with that my coop partner also appears to jump around unsmoothly. Quests become unfinishable and unfixable (its so bad they added a way to report unreachable objectives, but even THAT doesn't fix the issue sometimes). Enemies appear inside geometry and are unkillable, making it impossible to convert an area or finish certain quests. You can get stuck inside geometry, such as when taking down a stronghold. In co-op, enemies sometimes "clone", where it looks like you are damaging an enemy but when you destroy it, it shows an animation of the enemy being destroyed but the enemy remains standing back at full health. Just check the bug forum, there's endless bugs.

Combat is hideously boring. The core issue stems from the shooting - shots have travel time AND random bullet deviation. Why someone thought this was a fun thing to do in an FPS is beyond me.

So, it's an FPS, but aiming isn't a thing, the vast majority of weapons you find have abysmal accuracy, if you are lucky enough to find a weapon with high accuracy in a little bit it'll become obsolete and you'll have to hunt for another or accept awful boring "roll the dice" shooting. If you move to avoid enemy shots your accuracy gets even worse, so you may as well just stand still and tank the shots while shooting at the enemy and praying the bullet goes where you want.

For some reason I was fine with this in the previous versions of the game but it's just gotten so tedious and boring. What is even the point of giving shots travel time if you are so heavily punished by moving and there's so much random deviation?

I finally rage quit after I needed to upgrade my weapon and after 30 minutes didn't find any with decent accuracy. I absolutely hate using the low accuracy weapons. It's an FPS for christsakes, shooting is supposed to be responsive and fun and satisfying, random bullet deviation should NOT be this extreme.

Armor / chips are seemingly pointless. You can get an entire piece of gear's worth of stats just from completing one quest. You'll waste more time trying to find better armor than you would just doing a quest.

Combat is feast or famine. Either you are OP and enemies go down in a few shots or you are too weak and each individual enemy is a slog to take down via repetitive, totally thought-free combat. Either way combat is just a boring, unpleasant grind.

Going through inventory is painful, there's no "compare" feature making it really annoying to try to compare 2 weapons. For a game that's all about upgrading gear it's inexcusable.

There is a really obnoxious type of enemy called a "floater" which can oneshot you even when you have so much armor that normal enemies hardly damage you. It suffers from the "jumpy" movement issue making it difficult to hit and it's a small sphere making it already hard to hit. It moves really fast, too, so you have to run away, turn and shoot it, run away further, and repeat until it teleports away and you have to go find it again. Combined with the awful accuracy of most weapons it's THE WORST.

VR is abysmal, the game just does NOT work in VR. It's an afterthought.

In short, it's a glitchy "FPS" game which fails to get the fundamental FPS gameplay right.

EDIT: Found out MANY others have complained about the gunplay being no fun, but the dev INSISTS that they are "satisfied" with the current state of gunplay. So no hope of this ever being fixed.\t\t\t\t\t

As for actual gameplay, it's ok. My main gripe with the game is that there really isn't much of a purpose. The game is effectively a grind loop from beginning to end, and thats pretty much it. I think this game could maybe be fun with friends, but for solo players, this probably isn't a good pick, unless you are looking for a more casual and aimless adventure in a low poly environment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great FPS/RPG hybrid playable both with and without VR. That being said, there is a great deal of depth to the game and some reading may be required to fully understand everything so it's not for everyone. The co-op adds a lot of fun to the game and has crossplay with VR mode. I experienced no issues running it on WMR but the developer has said this about support:

#Discussions_QuoteBlock_Author
Windows Mixed Reality works, but it is finicky, which is why I don't have it listed on the store page. Unfortunately, the engine I used to make this game awhile ago wasn't future proof.

First, make sure the controllers are enabled & shown as green/tracked in SteamVR *before* starting 5089. The controllers won't add themselves during the game -- they need to be ready at the start of the game.

Second, the higher resolution of WMR sets will mess with the UI sizing and position. Information about a file that can adjust the UI is seen here:

https://steamcommunity.com/games/414510/announcements/detail/604993342279375154

There is likely a set of values that will display correctly on the resolution you are playing at.
