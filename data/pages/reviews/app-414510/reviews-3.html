\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is actually really good, far passed my expectations.

5089 is the sequel to 3089 and 4089, which i have not gotten to yet.

Early game is extremely tedious to begin with, but its not terribly tedious, but its enough to turn players with less patience off.

Gunplay is fairly simple but it works, stealth is also very very simple but again very functional.

Gameplay loop is pretty fun and there is a procedurally generated infinite game world which the further you get the stronger and better loot you get along with stronger enemys.

Weapon part system is good but underutilised, so much more could have been done with it, like different barrel types, weapons that could be turned into other weapons, but the procedural weapon stats does sort of make up for it.

Enemy variety is basic for an RPG but most of them have semi unique stats which goes hand and hand with the level scaling system.

Amount of weapons is very limited, but procedural part and weapon generation sort of makes up for it.

Melee is fun but basic.

Movement.... oh my god the movement..... ITS BRILLIANT, everything feels like it has weight to it, but it is a bit slippery and gravity is low but it works fairly well combined with the platformer like jumping system and the many movement enhancing items such as grapple hooks and hoverboards.

Over-all its not for everyone, but some might manage to enjoy it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn short, this game provides what you're looking for, but I feel like it's too long of a grind for the pay off. I have other games to explore.

I played this game for 21 hours before writing a review, and I don't plan to finish it. Spoiler alert, it gets a positive review, but there's good news and bad news:

The good news is that this game delivers on its name. It's an action rpg.

The bad news is that it's an action rpg. That means that it takes the now standard formula of kill/fetch/give/protect quests from role-playing games, and embeds it into a 1st person shooter, complete with boss levels.

Like most computer rpgs out there, you grind for experience, which allows you to go to areas with more dangerous monsters, and bigger rewards. After defeating each boss, it unlocks another paragraph of the story.

Rinse and repeat.

It works flawlessly, but for me, the grind isn't worth the story. If the game was shorter, I would have nothing but praise, but the action isn't all that exciting, and the story moves too slowly to be worth the grind. Again, if this was a short game, I would have been satisfied, but now it feels like work.

I'll keep an eye out for Phr00t's work, because this is flawlessly executed for what it is, but Ive hit my grinding threshold with this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very interesting title, this game is quite the oddball among my collection, but I love it all the more for that.
it provides a very unique atmosphere in this very alien and randomly generated world where you will be pit against pretty much everything.

This game technically shines in its replayability, allowing you to take many different approaches, but considering my playtime I have only experienced very little of that (I don't know if steam counts offline hours, but something tells me I've been playing this for longer than 3 hours)

As solid as this game is, it does have some flaws, the gameplay can be repetetive at times despite the wide variety of things to do, and sometimes you'll be screwed over by the RNG a bit, the generation rarely looks odd in places. But for the most part these issues can be overlooked.\t\t\t\t\t
