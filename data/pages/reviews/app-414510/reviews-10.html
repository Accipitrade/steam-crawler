\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA little bit of a lengthy review, but a good one.

Let me start out by saying this. I have never before seen a game with such devotion put into it. This game is one of those things that you look at and go " huh 8 bucks, indie game, it looks like it could be fun" and boy is that right. This is an Action RPG that really is an Action RPG. You can play the game however you want. weather that be a sword weilding stealing stealthing ninja, or a guns blazin all up in your face dude, or a melee all up in your face dude, or a stealthy gunsman that can kill from 40 mile away, the list goes on and on. I think this may actually be one of the most fun RPG's that i have ever played. The point and quest system is amazing, and as you play the game enemy's get tougher and tougher and grow in level. The crafting is very cool, you can get different leveled parts to your gun that can add different skill points to your charecter build.

LAST VERY IMPORTANT THING, READ THIS>> Phr00t, the games developer, is honestly one of the best devs i have ever even heard of. If there is any problem you might have, he will always try his best to solve it. Back when 4089 came out i tried played it and had some problems actually starting it. I then looked at the community forum to see if there was a fix. ( keep in mind that i am on a mac which can make things a little rough) When nothing worked out from the forum Phr00t himself sent me a friend request so that we can chat personally on how to fix it, And we got it working. Now if ever i have something go wrong in the game i can always shoot him a message and i know he will respond to try to work things out with me. Also if i ever really like something i know that messaging him "hey i like that thing you put in" will be very appreciated by him. You can really tell that he cares a lot about his game, and he cares even more about the people who have play it.

SO IN CONCLUSION>> Great game. Great developer who cares about the community. An actual really fun ARPG. 8 bucks. Get it.\t\t\t\t\t

"10/10 Like Borderlands with mods"

TLDR down below

5089 is an FPS, RPG, open-world, action, adventure, procedurally generated game (i think I got it all) where you play as a robot set on ending war on the planet Xax. Along your way, you will meet some very "unique" robots that will help you complete quests, or grind you into a fine powder and inhale your Defense lvl 12 ass. The combat mechanics work great and the game feels very balanced. Your character has a wide range of skills, from being able to turn invisible and sneak past enemies to knowing how to pilot jets. Every single piece of gear is procedurally generated and can be crafted out of some base parts and reassembled to work however you want to. You can take a few different rifles and mix-match them and make a dual-barreled heat-seeking sniper. This also works with armour and chips. You can install computer chips into your character, and they act sort of like wildcards. They give you skill boosts, and you can reconfigure them if you have the right programming level. There are also random items called "Utilities". There are hoverboards, gravity-guns, teleporters, black hole guns and more. There's also building in this game, and it's pretty much limitless. If you want, you can repair some of the damaged houses or bases, or make your own. You can build teleporters and crates to store your goods, and sensors to detect nearby enemies. There's quite a lot in this game, and I missed a lot of it, so here's a

list of things it has:

FPS, RPG, Open-world, jets, fully customizable weaponry, fully customizeable gear, lasers, robots, factions, secrets, procedural EVERYTHING, hoverboards, gravity guns, building, weapon/gear crafting, large bases/kingdoms, computer parts, ghosts, giant tesla coils, & more

**EDIT**
Recently I've been doing some playing and noticed some bugs. They never stood out to me in my previous world, but now they seem more pronounced. One of them is enemies spawning somewhere in a base section that I cannot find and kill. They're either spawning under ground or in objects, niether of which makes sense because enemies seem to spawn after the world is generated. But they're definately somewhere and they're definately not able to be attacked. Also, the game will sometimes crash if you loose focus and do something in another window.

----TL;DR mark----

Pros:
Fully customizeable gear
Plenty of content
Everything's procedurally generated
Open-world
Balanced
Witty/Snarky robots
Worth the money
It'll run on a potato hooked up to a battery and some RAM

Cons:
Meh graphics
Rain seems to slow the game down significantly with certain settings
Repetitive soundtrack
Tutorial could be better
A little buggy

Amazesauce game, feels like Mercenary Kings X Cube World X Borderlands w/ old graphics
This is one of those games that whatever you think should be in a game is probably in this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like a game that throws you into the enviroment forcing you to look at the controls and figure things self out and expirement on your own, I'm new to Phr00t's 3079-5089 series, but this game took me by surprise, the graphics are not actually bad but very good if you think about how unique they are, it gives a break from the realisism that most games have and gives you a chance to enjoy a chunk of the game's creators mind, a look inside his head.

The beginning of the game was fun as hell, I felt like a child wandering around a imaginary world with unlimited curiosity,
constintally getting myself killed due to experimenting with AI and the enviroment, though after I got the hang of the ropes
it could get frustrating at times when you keep dying to an enemy, especialy in 1 shot, but after completing a dozen
quests I was able to get better weapons and gear and the game's difficulty seemed to be lifted off my shoulders.

This game will keep you going, the war-torn-now-hell planet has been conquered by your sworn enemy, the purple robot thingies, you are the defender of the remaining Gold robots, you help the gold robots by conquesting the large
map, while you bring back the Gold Robot's empire piece by piece, you uncover ancient structures with a hidden secret...

Pros:
1. VERY fun CO-OP expierence
2. RPG mechanics keep you progressing
3. Large map
4. Very long play time, does not get stale (especially with a friend)
5. Conquest/Territory gameplay

Cons:
1. Frequent death can get annoying
2. You can bankrupt yourself, this will make it impossible for you to get any ammo, or defend yourself for a long time
3. Most guns have terribel accuracy, this will make begining players rage

If you look past the minor cons, this is absolutly an amazing game!
I was looking for a long-term, open world, singleplayer/co-op RPG and this game really hooked me, i'm looking forward for the constant updates that will definitely keep bringing me back and hopefully make this game better and better!\t\t\t\t\t
