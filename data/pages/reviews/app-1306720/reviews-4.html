\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played a lot of beat 'em ups in my life. Mayhem Brawler is the best and most innovative beat 'em up I've played in recent times.

Reasons for this:

1) The fighting moves you can do in the game are really numerous, almost the same variety as in competitive fighting games(1 vs 1). You can easily adapt this variety to your game thanks to userfriendly controls.

2) Until now, I have not been able to use the things I picked up from the ground comfortably in any beat 'em up. There was always some ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. That's why I wouldn't waste time picking up and using things like knives or guns on the floor.
It is definitely very easy and enjoyable to use them in Mayhem Brawler. Sometimes I even died because I was going to use a gun, rifle, etc.

3) Everything is polished. Characters, animations, backgrounds, screen designs, cinematics everything is extremely polished.

4) The comic style is very well implemented into the game. I really liked the heroes and the dialogues between them.

5) Although the style of the game is retro, from a narrative perspective, it has caught the day with references to social media. Reading tweets in the game can be more fun than the story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMayhem Brawler is a good old-style beat'em up game and I recommend it and give it a thumbs up.
While not the same level of quality as Streets of Rage 4 in terms of animations and fighting mechanics (it is a bit more simple, yet there are juggle combos here too), this game stands on its own, and some aspects I loved even more than in SOR4.

For me the strongest points were: the characters, the lore, awesome soundtrack and alternative routes with 3 different endings.

All story cutscenes are made comic-style, fully voiced. We play as cops with super powers, although in Mayhem city it is not uncommon: there are werewolves, vampires, magic creatures from another dimension, street mages etc. And it's a normal thing around here. So even the police are all x-men in terms of their abilities.

Gameplay-wise it is similar to SOR. You have special attacks, normal attacks, jump and block. Usual combo with normal attacks launches opponent and you can juggle it with new hits, use special attack for some more damage or jump kick him. In the corner it is sometimes possible to score even more hits for damage.

The only thing I didn't really like is the lack of..."culmination feeling" in the end. All 3 possible final bosses for all endings did not have that wow factor, they were not epic enough for final bosses, as well as difficulty-wise, they were not harder than other bosses from the game. Put any of these in the middle of the game or even as first boss and you won't notice a difference. So if there will be a sequel, I'd like to see some real danger in the end, gameplay-wise and story-wise.

Anyway, I had fun with the game, definitely worth a purchase if you are a beat'em up lover.
VERDICT: 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
