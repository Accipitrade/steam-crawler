\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNowadays with all the available games in the Beat-em-up genre on Steam, it's difficult to stand out above the popular entries like Streets of Rage 4, whose success came due to catering to nostalgia as well as some solid gameplay. Mayhem Brawler is another entry in the genre who also tries to entice players with nostalgia and solid gameplay as well, but in a different way.

Mayhem Brawler is a title that pretty much gives players of today a sample of what was popular back a few decades ago. (Comic books that featured mutant powered super heroes, powered up cops and beat-em-up videogames that were easy to learn but hard to master) You'll find all of that here, and it's a lot of fun. The game features some excellent looking comic book style visuals that definitely capture the style used in comic books of the 90's with a simple yet engaging storyline that matches the comic book feel as well, the soundtrack is no slouch either, the voice work is surprisingly decent, the gameplay is fast paced and engaging, and with 5 different characters, branching paths, 3 different endings and a few additional gameplay modes to choose, this game does feature quite a decent ammount of content. You won't mistake the game's playstyle with that of the SOR games, but that does not make it any less fun.

In fact, the only nitpick I have with the game would be its current title screen and library artwork for the game's banner and grid icon. The developers have stated that the game is pretty much done and they are now focusing their attention on the upcoming sequel, but isn't there any chance to update the title screen to feature the rest of available characters? I know Star is meant to be the game's poster girl and all, but the game as of now features 4 extra characters, which are quite cool in their own way. Give them their chance to shine as well by updating the title screen, game banner and icon!

All in all, the game is another solid entry in the beat-em-up catalog available on steam and a solid purchase as well. It may not be quite up to par with titles like SOR4 and Fight'n Rage, but it is definitely worthy of your time and money!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMayhem Brawler is a fresh breath to the beat 'em up genre.

What makes this game different from other beat 'em ups is it let's you choose your own path. Which is super fun to mess with. Because of your decisions in level select, you may never see some enemies and bosses. Talking about it with my friends and hearing their "What? I never knew such boss exists!" or "I should try a different route to see those enemies." is priceless. It reminds me of the times where internet wasn't as common as today and the only source you can trust is your friends word. For me, fast paced action and combat mechanics of the game made me replay it again and again to see every ending and level. Story is good for a beat 'em up. For those who is interested, I heard the sequel is in development so I believe we will learn much more about Mayhem City and its story. Voice acting is fine, it sells you the characters. This is a minor issue for me but in-game grunts doesn't fit with cut scene voices. Also, I wish we had an online mode. It is not an one to one substitute solution but if you have a good internet connection you can use Steam Remote Play to play with your friends.

For my final words, I can highly recommend this game for three group of people.

Those who look for a really good beat 'em up, those who look for for retro tastes with a modern presentation and those who look for a fun game to play with your friends on the same couch.

Peace!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGraphically a decent clone of the stylish looking Streets of Rage 4, but that's where the positivity unfortunately stops.

- There are much better games in the genre: old and new. Even some fan-made games, or modifications for OpenBoR, absolutely destroy anything that Mayhem Brawler has to offer.
- Levels are also too similar in theme and layout to the Streets of Rage and the Final Fight videogame series: as if they didn't have enough ideas themselves.
- In itself, the basic fighting mechanics are okay, but it is all a bit simplistic.
- The story and writing are downright childish and full of hipster references. For example: absurd behaviour for adults in a professional police job, Twitter feeds, selfies and woke culture.
- About half of the characters' voices sound like someone was forced during the recording.
- Sound effects for the characters seem to be recorded in a cardboard box, with a cheap mono microphone. Highly amateurish.
- The music is nothing special and absolutely does not deserve any awards (as several people suggest). It is also a very strange mix of Hollywood-esque bombastic orchestral music mixed with Dance elements.\t\t\t\t\t
