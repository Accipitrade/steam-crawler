\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's easy to write Mayhem Brawler off at first glance as a Streets Of Rage 4 clone solely based on the art style and play style. But those people probably didn't grow up in the late 80's early 90's when pretty much all beat 'em ups consisted of pixeled, jeans-wearing fighters moving screen to screen beating up gangs in streets, subways and other run-down locations.

Back then nobody cared that the games were similar and not innovative, they just wanted to spend an hour smashing head with a friend at home or the arcade.

It's safe to say that with the success of Streets Of Rage 4, it may very well have become a gateway title to the genre for many, but of course, with that, you have people not grasping that this genre has never really been about innovation and more about presentation.
Yes, Hero Concepts "Mayhem Brawler" has that hand-drawn, thick-lined comic book style just like Streets Of Rage 4, but then unlike SoR4, Mayhem Brawler is actually presented as a comic book, with levels having wonderfully drawn and coloured covers and comic book style cutscenes. The style if anything fits the concept of this game more than it ever did with the Streets of Rage franchise.

So, now that we have bodyslammed the elephant out of the room, let's talk "Mayhem Brawler".

The game takes place in what I can only assume is the modern-day era where social media, hashtags, gender politics and social justice warriors exist.. oh.. and superpowers, werewolves, vampires and other fantasy creatures.
Our 3 playable characters are essentially police officers with a twist.
Star is the young, blonde female with quick attacks and telekinetic powers.
A social media addict, complete with hashtags and spouting the sort of buzzwords you would hear from someone of her age group on Twitter.

Then there is "Trouble", he's the older, grumpy cop, sporting sunglasses and a handlebar mustache.
He is most likely the character that we old school brawler fans can relate to the most and is certainly the flipside of "Star", poking fun of her millennial/zoomer mentality.
He's certainly the "Wolverine" of the group, with his werewolf claws, he plays very similar to "Star".

Then we have "Dolphin" the Mike Haggar type character. He's a big, blue, bandana-wearing guy who grapples and body slams everything and can pick up and throw heavy objects.
I didn't see a blowhole or a fin.. heck.. I don't know what he even is, but he quickly became my go-to character as, despite his size, he's pretty fast as well as strong.

Of the three characters, "Stars" personality feels the most fleshed out.
It's an odd choice as a protagonist,, her dialogue in cut scenes really made me dislike her.
She's the antithesis of everything I hate about the social media generation, but that in itself made her memorable, and the juxtaposition with "Trouble" made them both stronger characters.
But I still like the big blue/grey dude who may or may not be an aquatic mammal.

So, you have selected your character, watched the fully voiced comic strip opening for the level, complete with a Twitter-style feed and you are ready to go.

Each level takes place in a different location, split into multiple parts, and littered with enemies, breakables and pickups and ending with a unique boss fight.
Players will find themselves fighting at the docks, city streets, sewers, cafe's, mansions, rock clubs and many many more, with my personal favourite being the park.
These levels are beautifully drawn with so much detail including background characters. It's honestly worth taking a moment just to admire how much work went into these background pieces.

But you won't see every stage in a single playthrough as the game has branching paths after each stage. There are even 3 different final levels, with their own unique enemies and final boss (which alone is a testament to how much work went into this game).
I won't spoil the final levels with their location and enemies but all I can say is that I really hope for some future updates or DLC where the levels and enemy types are remixed.

There are many enemy types throughout the game, each with multiple skins.
From standard thugs to large ladies who like to slide kick you. Pistol and SMG wielding foes and Blade like vampires, to female half-blood sword wielders. Lots of different were foes from werewolves, weremice and werecrocs. Djinn and other magical type foes who teleport. There is certainly no shortage of enemy types, each with their own tactics and status effects to master.

Each stage has it's own unique boss, including each possible path you can take.
At no point are these bosses repeated (I'm looking at you Estel, Commisioner, Beyo & Riha) .
They are also never made into regular enemies and there is no final boss rush to be found.

There's no shortage of pickups either. Lot's of different consumables that increase your health and power and tons of weapons from knives, bats, pipes and guns.

Music has always been an important part of beat 'em ups and while nothing will have you humming along, the music fits really well with each stage. From atmospheric tracks to jazz and even some great sounding guitar-driven djenty tracks, the soundtrack is done well, as is the sound design.

I don't really know what else to say about this game. If you aren't already a fan of the genre then it's still probably not enough to convert you. But as a lifelong fan myself, I couldn't be happier to see this new golden era of beat 'em ups and would love to see more games of this quality and art style.

Hope the game sells well and we can get some future updates/dlc.

Great work guys!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a 43 years old gamer, I had the opportunity to play many beat'em up games on arcades. Golden Axe, Final Fight, Cadillacs & Dinosaurs, Captain Commando, Punisher and much more. I loved them all. Now, thanks to several game developers we are able to get similar experiences again. Dragon's Crown on PlayStation, Streets of Rage 4, Mayhem Brawler and the upcoming Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Shredder's Revenge are some of the very nice examples.

The art style, graphics, sounds and the general atmosphere of this game is quite good. It's a tad slow for my liking, but still it's good and very well varied. Instead of dozens of same enemies with different colors, Hero Concept made themed enemies per stage. I like it.

Unlike dozens of ugly 8-bit games out there, these guys decided to hire professional 2D artists for their project and I really respect that. I really hate those cheap 8-bit games with "wonderful gameplay" where you can't distinguish your character from the stick your character holds...

For me, each run is around 1 hour long. It's quite fitting for an arcade beat'em up game since you might want to complete it on one go. I haven't tried all alternative routes, however, the one I played was quite good.

From my perspective,

Pros:
Nice HD Graphics
Varied and themed enemies and levels
Responsive controls

Cons:
A little bit slow
Cop characters are boring (I wish we could play as one of those werewolf type enemies)\t\t\t\t\t
