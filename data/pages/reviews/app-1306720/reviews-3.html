\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDISCLAIMER: Dont take my time with this game here very serious. I have around 25hrs on the Switch. Here is a double dip to support the devs.


First of all I must say that beat'em ups are my favorite genre. Period. I've been playing this type of game since 1987 and I played my share of good and bad games.

This one is right at the top with the classics.

The gameplay is perfect, you have complete control over your characters and they are very responsive, the enemies are smart and will use a lot of tactics to take you down, varying scenarios and branching paths (not to mention the excelent sound track that you should buy) and three different endings to a surprisingly good story.

More than that, the game already got 2 free updates and the devs are making another one. Speaking of the devs those guys are great! They are always listen to critics and suggestions!

I know a lot of people will complain that there's no online but come on, people, there's lots of softwares that let you play online couch games.

If you enjoy beat'em ups, even slightly, you should buy this game. If you're really into beat'em ups and don't have this game... I mean... Come on, what are you waiting for?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMaybe I bought this game at the right time cause I'm hearing complaints that I'm not feeling myself. Except for possibly the game being hard. That said, this is a game that has a lot of potential. The game play is good, the combos feel nice when you actually land them, moments actually look cool, the multiplayer is fun. Personally, I don't care much about the story, but that's whatever. And it's not like it's a badly written story in the first place. It's a beat em up that does quite a bit right, but possibly due to it's updates.

The only things I can say is this, I hope more characters get added in the future. But overall, this game doesn't deserve some of the bad reviews it's gotten. It's fun and that's what a game should be. There's quite a bit to do and the game is getting attention from it's developers. Though I have to admit, 2 of the characters feel a little underwhelming compared to others. Star is a good character, but not as well designed as Wolf or Dolphin. And Trouble is designed as his archetype should be, but isn't as interesting imo as the aforementioned.

The only thing I have left to say is, I wonder if some of the other bosses will become playable. Considering how well designed they are, I wouldn't be surprised and really hope so. They are part of the potential I speak of. More could possibly be added to this game as far as what you're capable of, but again, I'm willing to wait for more. I really hope people give this game a second chance. It kind of deserves it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is very fun, an excellent beat em up likely to be overlooked because of SoR4, which is a pity.
Controls feel good, feels nice to hit people.
It looks great, with a comic book style level mode, the story is fine for this genre, though I could honestly live without the twitter stuff, but it's fine.
If you are done with Streets of Rage 4 and are looking for another brawler to scratch your itch, this should do nicely, give it chance.

Also I do actually prefer this games fighting over streets of rage 4. SoR4 sticks closer to classic games with a limited mobility and defensive options. This game everything is a bit quicker, snappier, you can block, run do ground to air back to ground combos.
If the devs do make a second game, which I really really hope they do, I hope they expand it just a bit more so the characters each feel a bit more unique maybe with some characters being better with certain weapons and maybe more diverse specials, stuff like that. I also very much enjoy the world, and I hope they explore more of the races and creatures and the cities gangs. With maybe some of the characters that you are able to play also being more diverse between all these creatures.

If the devs read this, I've had a blast with the game and I hope ya'll are able to continue and expand this game series world, it's characters and the lore, great job guys.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's basically an unapologetic Streets of Rage 4 "clone" with what may as well be a Shadowrun theme. The three biggest gameplay differences from SoR4 are that you can sprint (like SoR3), you have a block button with a counter mechanic now, and your special attacks use a dedicated meter instead. You also have branching paths to pick in the campaign for replayability. Although for some ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ reason the story has Twitter ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ injected into itself the entire way through, and I really don't know who thought that was a good idea. It's by far the worst part of the game and I started skipping the dialogue as a result.

Overall the game is alright and looks nice, and the urban fantasy setting is appreciated and fairly unique. I'd still recommend SoR4 and Fight N Rage first if you haven't played those already, but Mayhem Brawler is a respectable addition if you're looking for more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game perfectly shows the situation when you buy some unknown indie project, which is not a fact that you will launch, but in the end you fanatically spend dozen hours in the game without stopping. Pleasantly surprised by this purchase - I got a lot of pleasure from the game.

Pros:
— Nicely done beat'em up combat system;
— Awesome visual style;
— Easy and responsive controls;
— Non-linear passage, many different endings;
— You can play 4 with friends;
— Great music;
— Voice acting in cutscenes and between them. Wow, that was really unexpectible.;
— Interesting opponents;
— Lots of jokes or references to the pop culture;
— Three characters to choose from, the developers plan to add more. I already love them.

Minuses:
— The game is short. You can beat it in 30-45 minutes.
— The bosses in the gameplay have no difference between each other. Same phases and attacks.
— I don’t know if there is a comic book based on Mayhem Blawlers or something else, but the plot is presented in such a way that the player should know the characters and understand the backstory of the game, but this is not so.
— There are a lot of enemies, but these are mostly reskins with an increased amount of health.

As a result, the minuses of the game look quite insignificant against the background of the pluses and the general impression of the game. Finished the game from start to finish 4 times in a row and want more.
