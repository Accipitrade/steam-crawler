\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCalling this game a retro beat'em up would be quite unfair. Mayhem Brawler was surely inspired by the arcade classics of yore but it's way more than a simple chameleon. There are a lot of modern design elements at work here. All of the combos and diverse status effects could feel like overwhelming at first and perhaps a bit unwelcoming to the new player but once you get past the initial hurdle there's a lot of depth and room for mastery here.

What was more surprising was the well crafted, silver age inspired comic book universe and the story that goes with the game. Combined with the excellent art direction it almost feels like licensed game making me yearn for more games in the Mayhem universe.

And perhaps one day that will happen. We can only hope. Until then Mayhem Brawler is a solid game fans of the genre should certainly try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played A LOT of brawlers over the years. Let me tell you about this one:
Pros:
-Overall tight control.
-Well drawn art in a nice comic style.
-The status symbols over the enemy's heads are incredibly useful.
-The special attack system is perfect. Most brawlers, even modern ones like Streets of Rage 4, still use the old "special attacks cost health" system. This one allows you to actually use your special attacks offensively when you have 2 or 3 bars, as well as defensively when you have only 1 bar. In many other brawlers, you are incentivized to use your specials almost exclusively defensively. The special attack system in Mayhem Brawler gets my highest compliments!
-Neat comic style story segments between levels.
-The comic cover artworks are especially well drawn.
-Unique enemy attack patterns that can be satisfying to learn and counter.
-Fun banter between characters during gameplay.
-Blocking is satisfying.
-Multiple endings.
-Standard difficulty feels fair. Have not tried the others yet.

Cons:
-Some of the animations are a bit stiff, particularly some of the player character's walk animations.
-At times, it feels like double tapping forward to run is less responsive than other games.
-Combat system not as complex as some other modern brawlers, but it is more than enough to keep me interested for multiple playthroughs.

I'd say it's a must play for all brawler fans, and a solid game overall!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove the game, it plays like an old-school streets of rage game (before 4) but has the looks of SoR4. It stands out on it's own with interesting characters, an exciting universe and the usage of guns is always fun to have too.

I always loved supernatural creatures such as Werewolves, Vamps, etc and this game encompasses all of that and makes them all have their place in Mayhem City and fit in such a crazy atmosphere. You got your werewolf gangs, corporate vampires, sewer-dwelling rat people and street wizards that probably got kicked out of Hogwarts. They all add a nice element of variety for us to fight against in this city.

Personally my only gripe with the game at this point...is there's not enough of it!

Beyond the story mode and alternate pathways (which is always good for more playthroughs)...that's really all about it.

Storywise there isn't a bio to learn more about the stronghold officers we play as to understand how they got their individual powers or more about who Valeria was.

Gameplay-wise there's higher difficulties for the story mode and an Arcade mode which is the same thing but with a 1 credit playthough. But when it comes to variety of modes in other beat em ups like Streets of Rage 4 and Fight N Rage I have to say that they stand out better in this category.

I love the game though and want to see it expand some more if the devs decide to do so. I support any additional content or DLC whether it be new characters, modes or story chapters and wish the best for the developers that made this enjoyable game.

If you're a fan of old-school gameplay and up-to-date graphics then you can't go wrong with this beat em' up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game got a lot of hype due to it being similar in art style to streets of rage 4, however after 6 hours of gameplay i can attest that it may look like SOR 4 but it plays and feels very different. If dotemu were to ever re-incarnate SOR 2 it would be akin to looking and feeling similar to this game.
The moves in this game are primitive and to some extent more tactics are required to take down certain enemy types in this game. That doesn't make it a bad thing though as it just gives the game a different perspective of combat and play.
Nevertheless , it is fundamentally the comic art style that catches the eye of the gamer and the reminiscent feeling of the olden days of beat-em games so don't expect a lot of content but more of a re-invention of the heydays.
Overall a very fun and challenging game. It would be interesting to see the developers consider adding more interesting combos to these characters and advancing their fighting strategies because this is what is needed most. overall a very good game and contrary to others, quite different to SOR 4 in terms of combat.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, let me start this review with saying that I consider myself as a veteran SoR fan and of Beatemup genre in general (been playing for more then 20 years).
It's obvious that this game will be compared most to SoR4 due to art style similarity, it's also obvious that this game was inspired by SoR4 success.
And yet, despite obvious similarities the games are very much different. In what way you may ask? I think the developers took notice on one thing that was done in the SoR4 very poorly to say the least. Its' single player campaign. It's absolutely disgraceful and way inferior to SoR3 campaign. *SIGH* Let's cut it short (details I'll explain later) - MB does single player campaign x10 better than SoR4, but SP campaign is all it has. Yeah. There's no bonus content whatsoever (as of yet, but hopefully something will be added later).
The SP campaign is really where MB shines.
Here are some of the highlights:
1) It has stylish comic book panels like cutscenes (and as a comics fan myself I love it!),
2) All cutscenes and character interactions have a voice over.
3) It has actually a branching story line! Yes, there's actually a replayability here! 3 possible endings, so you can at the very least complete it 3 times without getting bored.
4) The Bossess are different for each level and do not repeat (*cough* Estel, Divas, Commish, Mr Y). They also have 3 phases.
5) The overall atmosphere is also better in my opinion. The world feels more alive and the humor is great. Not to mention the amazing soundtrack (which I liked better than one from SoR4!
6) The overall tone of the story. You know, when SoR4 originally came out I was one of the haters of it. One of the reasons is SUGARCOATED story. It's not JUST kid friendly, hell no, it feels like the devs intentionally wanted to make SoR4 into - 3+ kindergarten edition. I couldn't and cannot take that tone seriously. Mayhem Brawler on the other hand does plot, lore and atmosphere GREAT! Sure the dialogues, interactions and stories are sorta corny and cheesy - but I think that's a good thing! It's more like - Adam West Batman corny and cheesy. I absolutely salute the devs for being able to pull this off. Old comic book fans must remember the famous "approved by the comics code authority" which prohibited the publishers from adding some questionable stuff in the stories. It was bypassed by most publishers in the beginning of the 2000s. But, what I wanted to say is, "the comic code era" was my favorite. And "the mayhem universe" still feels like it is "comic code approved", so I really like it.
7) I'd like to exapnd the game world discussion a little more. The game is set in the so called "Mayhem Universe", in short - it's like a DC/Marvel Universe. The 3 characters were inspired by Wolverine, Hulk and Jean Grey. But honestly, in my opinion the game world feels more like a Shadowrun universe, albeit very lightheartedned (which I also think is great! Since I love Shadowrun!). During cutscenes there's a social media from one of the characters and while the scene goes the new comments appear, when I saw it, I immediatly thought about Shadowrun BBS, which I also think is fantastic for proper immersion.
P.S. Some people say that's it's hard to watch the cutscene and reed the SM feed simultaneously. Good news! You don't have to! You can read the feed after watching the cutscene.

Well, I am not gonna lie the game has downsides:
1) I won't be able to describe it in great details, but in short - the controls feel kinda clunky and sluggish. Don't know how to explain it.
2) The character/enemies animation could also use some improvements.
3) As I said earlier - complete absence of bonus content.
4) There's no tutorial (and the gamepley isn't exactly the same as in SoR4). Come to think of it. That's actually some huge missed opportunity over here. Especially considering that the characters are all from a special force, tutorial could've been an atmospheric training level (would be even better if there was a boss).
5) Well, there are some technical and graphical issues. Like the achievements that unlock only when you close the game. Or, when the enemies hit invisible wall and you actually see that wall...
6) No Co-op ai.
7) It is poorlier optimized compared to SoR4. Well, some of us still have potato laptops, d'oh!
8) There's something very wrong and unsatisfying about using the firearms. Can't put it into right words. But it honestly feels like using a peashooter. Though I wasn't satisfied with SoR4 gunplay either. Nothing can be Mother Russia bleeds in this department.
There are some neutral points:
1) the game feels more slow compared to SoR4 (though all characters can run and run pretty fast *SoR4 Axel is crying in shame*).
2) Only 3 characters (though fleshed out).
3) The game is HARDER than SoR4. Some enemies are particularly a pain in the ass like werecaimans or magicians. Armed mercs are also more of a threat here.
4) As developers said themselves the game isn't aimed at combo-manicas (which is great in my opinion); what I am saying is the move set is less variable than in SoR4 (but the devs said that they are working on new moves).
5) SJW enemies are hilarious! It cracks me up whenever I see them.

And that's probably all I can think of for now! Maybe I'll expand the review later! So, to buy or not to buy? Well, if SP campaign experience is enough for you and you were frustrated by SoR 4 kindergarten campaign then pick this one up! I promise you won't be dissapointed! If the story, lore, SP campaign isn't what you care about at all and instead you want polished gameplay, lots of bonus content, modes like survival, boss rush, etc, then, well, the game isn't for you. Or at least not as of yet. Check it out later, hopefully some bonus content will appear.

P.S. As a side note. I personally think that the best beatemup in Steam is Mother Russia Bleeds, not even infamous fan-made SoR Remake has top'ed it for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't expect that you are going to find a SoR4 in this game. It is not a clone and does not seek to resemble itself.

This game has its own fighting mechanism and the fighting techniques are very different from Streets of Rage 4. If you are used to playing SoR4 this game will seem clumsy and sluggish at first.

Fighting against boss enemies is difficult because you have to know the correct time to hit since they have their attack time and it is very difficult to avoid.

Main characters can dash and block incoming attacks from any enemy. You can dash vertically, horizontally or longitudinally across the screen.

This is a very challenging game even in normal mode. The graphic art style, the history and the music is interesting. You can see that the developers put a lot of enthusiasm into the graphic details.

This game doesn't have an online co-op but you can try it through Remote Play Together or Parsec.

Do I recommend this game ?
If you like beat 'em up of course, it's a very challenging and fun game.

Difficulty is not on the Dark Souls level, but I made quite a lot of restarts when learning how to progress. Learning to block, using dash attacks and shaking off the grabs with your special (and not dying in general) rewards you with plenty of weapons and power-ups, but game kinda expects you to learn the basics and plan your approach to different situations.

Also the comic book covers... You can clearly see the love in the details and wonder about what could've happened if you take the other routes. I love the character illustrations in general but executing this level of continuity on visuals is a hard work and the team nailed that down in every panel.
