\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDISCLAIMER: I don't think this game is for everyone. If you like RPG maker horror games, this isn't a typical one. It focuses on abstract concepts that are very VERY subjective. It's hard to explain and recommend the game without some spoilers, so those will be censored.

The general gist of the game follows a boy named Ori who is doing various puzzles in order to pass through different "veils" with hints on how to pass through. After finishing both endings, you can piece together that the hotel is something of a combination of Ori and Rem's mind palaces. Ori is guiding Rem while he's being "purified," and ends up either allowing himself to remember his sister, or represses those memories depending on the ending you go for.

It has very heavy religious themes and points Rem's beliefs (description from the wiki: "He describes how humanity can no longer dream, and as a result have turned to Celestials in order to find faith and to forget their disillusionment.") in a pretty neutral / positive light as opposed to the Church and the Rosary being based on pure facts only with no room for emotion. Kinda switching the normal way philosophy (Rem's beliefs) and religion (Ori / the Church's beliefs) are viewed.

Like etherane's other games, it's based on their opinions and beliefs. It's hard to really understand without them directly saying what they meant by any of it and can be a little hard to follow at times. They did post about it on twitter (archived on the wiki as:

"The definitions of religion and philosophy in this story are switched, in a way.

Some, like Remiel and Mari, seek salvation by depending on higher beings (Celestials), whereas many, similar to Orifiel, seek salvation by depending on themselves alone (and inevitably, on the tools they create).

Paradoxically, The Choir uses religious terminology and depends on rather simplistic methods to achieve the feeling of control, whereas the chaotic Forest, where many wander on their own, symbolizes creativity and stands for freedom of thought.

Both human sides ultimately have the same wish, but neither take into account the third side - the Other, non-human entities that co-exist with them.

Among this cacophony, current Conductor's goals remain in the dark."

It's a weird game, but it's easier to relate to BECAUSE of it's vagueness and the abstract way everything is portrayed. If you don't like games that don't explain everything to you, you probably won't like this (or other etherane games), but that's okay! If you're curious and want to try it out but don't know if you'll like it, it's on sale for $1 (half price) every once in a while. My only complaint is that when I tried to get one of the endings (NOISE end), I had to look on the wiki and in guide's for a while because it just wasn't working.
