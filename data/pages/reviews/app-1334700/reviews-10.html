\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR:
First game from this dev that I've played and I enjoyed what I got despite being a little difficult to follow at times. This could come from trying to get too much information and trying to make a deep narrative in about an hour of gameplay. The endings are pretty straight forward and the art is enjoyable. The surrealism adds to the plot of the story and the seemingly absurdity of parts of the plot. It did make me think however that what I experienced was dealing more with exaggerated real life situations in the form of an extremist group and "aliens".

In-depth:

Story:
The story is good but a bit convoluted making it difficult to follow at times, and ultimately I felt like I understood more from one ending over another but that is likely intentional, so I do rec getting both endings. Most of the gameplay is through the dialogue you'll read as everything else is mostly moving from one location to another or fetching items.

Art:
The art presented in the game is done in the older pixel style. If that's not your cup of tea then this probably isn't for you. The surreal images were quite interesting, although I must admit I've never really understood surrealism but then again that's kinda the point.

Music:
The music within the game enhanced the play experience but nothing specifically stood out to me after playing. I would say the music here is about average.

Overall, this game is good if you have an hour to spare and want something thought provoking to play or are simply a fan of the pixelated type of games. I would say the sale price is what I would pay as I like to get an hour of play per dollar I spend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't typically write reviews, but I genuinely loved playing this game. This is just based off of my experience but I would definitely recommend this to anyone who enjoys:

- Connecting dots and working a little to figure out the plot. The game doesn't just come out with an exposition bomb, which I appreciate though I can understand that isn't for everyone.

- Psychological thriller/horror elements. Creepy vibe? Check. A dash of body horror and existential/cosmic dread? Check.

- Puzzles. There are a few puzzles that stumped me for a bit, but it never felt entirely unfair. (Plus, if you really need help, there is a guide written by luna-terra that will help you through the game and get the alternate ending without spoiling the experience)

- Minimalist art. Seriously, the art is phenomenal and wonderfully unique. The style works VERY well with the overall feel of the game.

It's not really replayable outside of getting the alt ending and trying to piece the plot together, plus it's definitely not for everyone, but it's a short experience very much worth the small cost of the game.\t\t\t\t\t
