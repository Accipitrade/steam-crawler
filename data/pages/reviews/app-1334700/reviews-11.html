tomorrow won't come for those without ██████ is a story about two characters, Remiel and Orifiel, who live in a society based on religious fundamentalism and a hierarchical caste system. Ori is a young choir boy who has a short attention span and forgetful memory, causing him to heavily rely on his rosary ― a device that was given to him upon admission into the choir. Rem is a troubled young boy who is highly suspicious and critical of Ori's faith. Not much is known about him, but it's implied his status is obsolete when Ori makes a remark saying "he didn't know students were still around these days".

The plot is very... existential and abstract, which is a staple of etherane's games. There's a lot to unpack and think about and a lot of metaphors to consider, but from what I understood Ori and Rem live in a society ran by the head of a religious sect referred to as "Conductor" by Ori (pretty on the nose naming there). Their system pretty much boils down to the faithful and faithless, with the faithful being the most influential. I kind of feel like etherane went a bit too abstract with her story and should have focused more on creating a cohesive conclusion. Basically, a point has to be made... or at least a point that's more clear than what was presented.

All in all, I enjoyed it and the puzzles were fun.\t\t\t\t\t

+ Easy game mechanics
+ Nice art
+ Fitting music
+ Interesting story
+ Worth the price for the length (about an hour of gameplay)
~ Convoluted plot

While the story is interesting, the plot is a little convoluted and isn't actually that deep. However, it is still quite enjoyable.
For those that may be confused or need help:
There are 2 endings, Silence and Noise.
In order to get the Noise end, make sure you actually use/interact with/"take" the four Indulgences that you have collected.
You do need all four of them so be sure to grab the Euli in the Sunday Class Lesson: Light room.
Once you have all four Indulgences, "use" them before having Rem follow Ori through the doors with shapes above them.
Follow Ori for the first two (triangle first, circle second) doors but then go through the door that Ori does not use(triangle) the third time.

As for understanding the plot, I will explain what I got from it below.
Ori is a Chorister and has entered a mental landscape, the Hotel, which is inside of Rem's mind.
Choristers serve the leader, the Conductor, of a religious organization called the Choir.
Choristers perform Purification Rituals on people possessed by Celestials, presumably a legion-like/(semi-)hiveminded alien race, by entering their mental landscapes.
Rem is possessed by a Celestial, perhaps even the "last one".

People who are possessed by Celestials lose their sense of self and are able to remember the past, dream, and create "Narratives".
Their minds become loud and the Celestial influence is compared to a cacophonous orchestra.
The Choir has somehow modified humans, through poison (metaphorical or physical, not sure) in the water and conditioning of religious beliefs.
A modified human free of possession has reached "Enlightenment".
The modification renders humans unable to remember the past or to create their own personal narratives.
The goal of the Choir is to prevent conflict and pain, making humanity more likely to survive.
Celestials stand in the way of the Choir's goals so the members of the Choir are tasked with weakening Celestial influence ("Only then tomorrow will come for humanity").
Choristers go into the mental landscapes of the possessed and destroy the Celestial barriers.
Barriers can be things or people inside the mental landscape.

Ori ultimately has two options: destroy the barriers and restore Rem to Enlightenment OR to accept possessed Rem as is and run away into the "Dark".

The Silence ending is the first option.
Ori destroys the barriers of the Celestial possessing Rem and quiets Rem's mind.
The final barrier is Rem's body in the mental landscape.
Rem is purified and wakes up into reality, showing signs of emotional instability.
He does not remember what has happened inside of his mind.
The Conductor gives Rem a "rosary" for guidance are it is said to help humans remember "without bias" (sure).
He also tells Rem to stay "hydrated" (I assume because the water is poisoned).
However, Rem notes feeling "empty" without the Celestial's influence and feels regret.
Ori greets Rem and tells him that people who are empty and unhappy are prone to being possessed by Celestials.
Ori expresses the desire to see Rem again.

The Noise ending is the second option.
The Celestial possessing Rem confronts Ori as Ori is only pretending to not know that the Celestial isn't actually Rem.
Ori tells the Celestial that he doesn't want the "noise" to stop and that he doesn't want tomorrow to come.
Instead, Ori wants to stay in the "Dark" and doesn't want to be Enlightened, saying he will throw away his rosary.
Ori decides to grab Celestial!Rem and runs into the Dark. This is how Ori believes he will find meaning to his life.
Celestial!Rem claims that Celestials don't dream (it's probably a lie) and that his kind doesn't want to be bound to humans, that humans are selfish.
Ori agrees that he is selfish and disappears.
Celestial!Rem is back in the Hotel. He looks over the real Rem's essay reflecting on the current state of humanity.
Rem's essay expresses the real Rem's desire to remember and to return to the past.
Checking various parts of the Hotel also reveals a few facts about the real Rem: Rem was obsessed with hygiene products and was also prescribed medicine to prevent vomiting.
Rem would vomit due to (excessive?) crying but threw the medicine away.
Celestial!Rem remarks how he feels very weak as his barriers have been destroyed. He hides in a closet to stay safe.
However, hearing noises, he leaves the closet in his weakened, and now de-solidifying, state only to find Ori dying in the bathtub.
Ori has some sort of dark substance on his body and claims that he ran too far (perhaps into the Dark).
As he is dying, Ori asks Celestial!Rem to tell him a story. Celestial!Rem tells Ori of the past.
They grieve together for the world of the past.
It seems that Ori and Rem die but death is stated to be liminal.

The game restarts because the cycle of Ori and Rem's two paths is reset.
Ori awakens in the bathtub from what he refers to as a sleep, just like in the start of the game.
You will note that the dead bodies of Rem are from previous cycles: the body on the floor is from Ori breaking the Celestial's barrier and the body in the bathtub is from the two characters entering the tub together.


Some more speculation on my part:
- Rem has a red hair tint, red background, and red eyelids to represent his complete possession by a Celestial*
*Celestial!Mari stated that blue is the color of one [being] and orange with a tint of red is the color of two beings. If Celesitals are basically legion, it would make sense that they are red!

- Mari and her Celestial are represented by purple/violet because they are a mixture of blue (those who only have one being in their body) and red (the legion of the Celestials)

- Ori is obsessed with violet because it reminds him of his possessed sister, who gave his life a Narrative/meaning

- Violet represents finding meaning in others through love/companionship/obsession, but if you give too much of yourself to the other, you lose your sense of self like Mari

- Rem was obsessed with hygiene products due to something like OCD (the hygiene products were colored violet, representing obsession)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttomorrow won't come for those without ██████ is very weird and thorny, but it is excellent. like hello charlotte, it feels like it's open to reader interpretation as to what precisely it is about but it feels pretty legible as well. it loses a lot of the fun kind of brechtian work that hc has, which is perfectly fine given the narrative that has wouldn't really support that, but it is maybe only less ambitious in that small way ig.

it's not as gore-y or fascinated in self-mutilation as hc, either, but it still has this like straight-faced morbidity that bookends it which i find really evocative. rituals requiring blood from bodies that are around and unaccounted for, using a razor to get said blood, etc. no real body horror in the explicit terms hc has it but the noise ending definitely considers embodiment in interesting ways.

writing is a lot better this time, and the way it is evoked on screen is fun and i think effective in simplistic ways. really great score as well, sets the pace of the atmosphere excellently. it is also not a funny game. it has moments of levity or character but it's pretty much got no jokes. it's just a descent. which would be frustrating in a longer piece but works well for the size. but yeah, idk, i am finding this tenor of work very fascinating and inspiring. metaphorizing and especially allegorizing themes is very frustrating and such a rote indie game genre move, and for their faults, etherane avoids that rly well by establishing stakes and conversations surrounded by in game lore and worldbuilding as to distanciate from 1-to-1 real world corollaries. it's not an approach that works for everything, but for something around organization, self identity and conception, faith and rationality, and religious trauma, i think this works rly well.

WRT thematic work: i like how the religious symbolism of the chorister is warped by this empiricist, almost objectivist stance? self sufficiency, rationality, xenophobia, politics and deeds modeled around purity and rejection of faith. organizing against that is the presence of the "alien" or the "celestial," which is gestural and auratic and othered, and their presence in what is sort of the reifying of the forest, which is irrational and full of play, halcyonic and based around faith, but also, no longer really self sufficient? there is recognition and reliance on faith here but one that also ostracizes that same alien self in some ways. this dichotomy is never just that; rem and ori are not just faction mouthpieces, and they're not even representatives, simply two different types of people inured to different existences.

etherane is such a talent.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA beautiful, short, self-contained story for a cold, dark evening. Getting all endings and seeing most of the cards will only take a couple of hours, but thanks to inspiring writing and visuals, the experience is wholesome.

There are a few puzzles, but do not expect much in terms of gameplay: it's basically an RPG Maker visual novel with freedom of exploration. If you are into low-key independent products that are more about personal feelings, ideas and experiences than about being "games" in a traditional sence, then don't miss it and do not wait for a sale - it is 100% worth picking and playing ASAP.

Become a forgetful boy named Ori and try to recollect memories that might be precious to you - or ignore your right for an indulgence and choose to follow strict instructions from your primitive memory device instead. Roam around numerous hotel rooms with weird stuff that makes everything feel like an abstract metaphor (and while it is a metaphor, it's also kind of isn't - the plot is quite straightforward, though some things only become clear on the second run). Befriend a creature that is literally your death or become a friend to a creature you are destined to kill. Fight for the future, or escape for the past. Make all the wrong and all the right choices and hurry to your bittersweet ending.

When the inevitable comes, there are two main ways to "keep yourself", and this little story shows the consequences to both of them. I wonder what you would rather choose.

P.S. One of the strong traits of the game is that it doesn't think players are dumb. All the keys are there, and easy to get, but they are not shoved down your throat. This is indeed a rare treat: something you could expect from a novel or an independent movie, not from a product on Steam Store.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had my fair share of rpgmaker type games and with some exceptions, in my opinion, that style got pretty unoriginal and tiresome with time.

tomorrow won't come for those without ██████ is one of those exceptions. It's worth it for the atmosphere only, but tbh everything from the story to the art is as well and contributes to an ensemble. I'd love to see more of Rem and Ori.

The puzzles are few, short, easy and maybe kinda pointless. I would have liked for them to be either not there, or more present. But it's not really that much of an issue if you want to focus on the story only.

That game clearly made me want to discover more of Etherane's works, it's a tiny gem well worth spending 1~$ on!\t\t\t\t\t
