\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very reminiscent of traditional exploration rpg maker games (mad father/ib/witch's house). The story is really nice, but you have to dig deep to get the whole image. The price is good for the amount of time you can play, there's only two endings and the only reason my time is so high for this game is because to get one of the endings you have to do so many tiny actions I had to have 5 saves in order to get it right (and look up a guide). Sadly I wouldn't super recommend going in completely blind, because in order to get the second ending you have to do the minuscule actions. I think that's a big downfall of the game- you definitely have to either explore EVERY inch, or use a guide- and be prepared to be absolutely frustrated. There's also no way to skip previously seen dialogue, all you can do is speed it up. But besides the things I can see wrong with it, If this is your niche, it's definitely worth it- especially for the price. The story can really suck you in when it gets going.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was fantastic in ways I can never get anywhere else. It's wonderfully written and immensely beautiful. As someone who played Hello Charlotte and have loved it- Tomorrow won't come has very clear improvements in both puzzle making and narrative.

One of the best aspects of this game is definitely the dynamic between the two characters and how they view the world. They both clearly see it in dramatically different ways and it's so interesting that they literally see the same things in completely different contexts due to their background. It is very clear these two care about one another in some way that is unique to these two.

It's so fun to analyze the story line and try to piece clues together. Create theories and work your head around the plot. While some games try too hard to make a plot so vague- it gets frustrating- this game does a great job at giving you enough to not only understand what's going on- but able to sense a great mystery in the setting.

This game is one of my favorite games, for it's themes of clear mental illness and loneliness, isolation and hurt, wonderful artwork, excellent choice of color scheme and for slapping me in the face with its plot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA short and peculiar game. It's very much interesting and kept me engaged until the very end. The gameplay is quite enjoyable, although typical for an RPG-Maker game, with riddles to accompany it. The story will probably confuse you at first, but thinking about the story of the game and figuring out a bit about what is going on is part of the fun. I liked the two main characters, they're both quite interesting.
The graphics are held in typical RPG-Maker game fashion, with nice artwork and an aesthetically pleasing color palette to accompany them.
If you like indie games with interesting (and confusing) stories, give it a go. If you're more of a fan of a clear story and exciting gameplay, this might not be the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t
