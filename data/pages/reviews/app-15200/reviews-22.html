\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not clear on why this game has such mixed reviews - I think it's great, and a bargain at the price. It's not the smoothest, best looking or the most stable gaming experience I've ever had, but it's 10 years old. Given its age, I actually think it's a fantastic looking game. It's fun, challenging and involving. It makes you think. It's highly engaging. It's difficult without being unforgiving.

You'll be put in command of a US submarine just after Pearl Harbour and will get sent on patrols in the Pacific. You'll manage your crew roster, recruiting new men and installing new equipment with the renown you win from sinking enemy shipping. You will upgrade your crewmembers as they become more experienced. Most patrols will be to go somwhere deep in Japanese waters and sink enemy merchant shipping, but there are also missions to do more covert stuff like getting US spies ashore in enemy territory, or photo reconnaissance of enemy harbours.

The game has a variety of difficulty settings, from full-noob mode to ultra-sim mode. I would thoroughly recommend it at the asking price, on sale it would be a steal.

+ Realistic
+ Thoughtful
+ Detailed
+ Long campaign
+ Great price
+ Many difficulty / realism options
+ Full world map - ♥♥♥♥ off to Sri Lanka or Argentina if you get bored
+ >8000x time acceleration (this is a serious + given the map size)

- A bit buggy
- Dated graphics (but seriously not at all off-putting unless you're a graphics snob)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI should note: I tend to play with Steam in Offline mode 97% of the time, so my time shown in game is realistically about 3 times more than what it says I reckon . . .

Very good game. If it is the type of subject matter that interests you, and the sort of game play that you expect, I'd say this one is an absolute must have.

I was surprised by how realistic it is. Missions involve long periods of traversing ocean and nothing happening. Then spotting (or being spotted) by an enemy and launched into action. The variable time compression (all the way to 8192:1) makes this aspect of the game just about as pleasant as it could be made. A scripting system would be neat, to "automate" ship activities during long transits. If I had a control panel that allowed me to assign a time of day (either clock time or relative to sunrise/sunset) to do X, Y, or Z activity I'd say this aspect of the game would be perfect.
-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-
15 min before sunrise-> go to Periscope depth; raise observation scope; 1/3rd speed; stay on plotted course; general quarters.

When batteries 5%: lower periscope; surface (no other changes).

Else if sunset: lower periscope; surface; 2/3 speed (no other changes).

I would say that, minus that little scripting functionality I describe above, this game is 9.5/10.

I honestly cannot think of any other "vanilla" feature or balancing the game needs.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA truly engaging addition to the franchise which encourages, and rewards, creativity and guts. I had the luxury of not playing the game until I'd already played Silent Hunter III and V and I would actually recommend that strategy to others. Playing III beforehand gives you an appreciation for the streamlined management system in place in Wolves of the Pacific. Now, watches are organised for you, orders are easier to issue, and the crew aren't dropping dead of exhaustion all the time.

However it does lack the emotional investment of Silent Hunter V, in which you can walk the boat and get to know the characters which make up your crew, so if that is the experience you really want from a WWII submarine sim, go with V rather than IV (if you don't mind putting up with the Ubisoft launcher's BS)

Overall it's terrific, a welcome addition to the genre for it's historical content, upgraded and streamlined features and additional DLCs.\t\t\t\t\t
