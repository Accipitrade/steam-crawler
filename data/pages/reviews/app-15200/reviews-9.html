\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI keep coming back to this one.
I've always loved the silent hunter series, starting with the original by SSI.
The modding community has made this game the best WW2 sub sim out there.
But that doesn't mean its perfect.

Pros:
- graphics and sound have aged well, especially with mods.
- interface is immersive, especially with mods.
- bugs are few, especially with mods.
- game play experience is easily tailored to your liking (from almost point and shoot arcade to math intensive simulation)
- Crew management is about as advanced as you could expect from a sim this old. It is shallow, but it exists.

cons:
- except at port, I don't think I've ever seen a ship that wasn't enemy. Even with mods, major battles aren't modeled. Don't go to Midway during that major battle thinking you'll see American carriers and their aircraft duke it out with their adversaries or the island getting pummeled... you won't. [Edit, after returning to SH4 again, stock version, I'm seeing friendly convoy's all over the place. My memory of not encountering them before must be a result of flaws in the mods I played previously (or my flawed installation of them)).
- TDC interface makes it difficult to target specific points on a ship the way it's been described in books like "Clear the Bridge"
- Difficulty settings and user interface, while highly adjustable, don't seem to allow the player to call the shots like a skipper.... "Range! Mark! Bearing! Mark! Angle on the bow! Mark!, and your firing team enters the values in the TDC... few seconds later, repeat determine speed, set... fire. it should be that easy for a skipper with a trained crew. But it seems like with this game (and most sub games) you either have to have a live (unrealistic) attack map with boats visibly moving on the map (on the easy setting) or you have to race to click the mouse in all the right spots to send the correct data to the TDC (on the hard setting)... no one has seemed to figure out how to give the player the feel of a skipper making calls to a targeting team.
- Crew management. Yeah, it was a pro, but its also a con. SH4 crew management doesn't hold up in the world of survival/sandbox games. plus, there is a design flaw where crew assigned as "watch" or "deck gun" will sustain wounds due to near misses even when submerged. Mods have found ways to work around this, sort of... but even with those workarounds, its wonky.
- boat management - there really isn't any. if you want to manage resources, ship maintenance, crew morale, learn about and play with the many, many devices on a WW2 fleetboat... you are out of luck... you won't find much of that here.

I've played this game with the Real Fleet Boat (RFB), Trigger Mauru, and Rising Sun mega mods. All are good and players will want to try all three. But the game can also be enjoyed vanilla.

Further, there is no modern comparison if you want to play in the Pacific. I recently gave Uboat a try. And I love the ship and crew management focus (which SH4 lacks). But that game has some fundamental flaws that quickly erode enjoyment.... and its not in the Pacific. So, if you want a fun, well polished (with mods) sub sim, SH4 is really still the only game out there that will keep you coming back for more.\t\t\t\t\t
