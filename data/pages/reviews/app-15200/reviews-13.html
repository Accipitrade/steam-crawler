\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing game and even though SH3 got me into submarines, this is definitely my all time favorite sub game now. U-Boats are cool and all but this game takes the perspective of an American sub captain during WW2 against Japan and the unique situations that came from being a sub captain in the pacific theater.

I prefer it over SH3 because:
-When you get tired of hunting merchants and convoys you can go find yourself full blown Japanese fleets to sink. Trying to ambush a carrier or battleship group with a massive escort adds a whole new layer to the game. It's challenging but very rewarding to finally get those hits on a huge aircraft carrier and watch it explode a dozen times.
-There are some special missions to break up the monotony and force you to go places you wouldn't normally go. Especially when it makes you sneak into the Japanese mainland to land a spec ops team on shore in the cover of darkness.
-A lot of the fighting happens near island chains which give you unique tactical and strategic decisions to make.
-There are large battles in the game that command gives you orders to assist in if you want.

The downside (in the game) is that the US submarine campaign against Japan was extremely successful and towards the end of the war subs had trouble finding targets, since Japan went from having 6 million tons of cargo capacity to having about 300,000 tons total by the time they surrendered. The other downside (in the game) is that you are on the winning team and while SH3 gives you the feeling of becoming more and more outmatched, SH4 gives the feeling of becoming more and more powerful as Japan loses ground and falls behind technologically

P.S There are some awesome mods and editing the config files is easy once you see how. The number of enemy planes really bugged me so I edited their range from 2500 to 100 and now I only see planes when a carrier group is nearby\t\t\t\t\t
