\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI Would Not recommend this game; but that doesn't mean it's a bad game... it just means I didn't like it. It seems very well built... good coding...no crashing... no lag...and great authentic sound. So why not recommend it? Because it's just a little too close to realistic for my taste. As an ex-submariner I can vouch for the realism of this game (even though it was 40 years before my time); but a game shouldn't feel like you've actually enlisted in the Navy (or re-enlisted); it should be fun, move forward, and not expect you to actually learn how to operate a submarine. As well... if you ARE going to go down that road, then, at the very least, include a decent tutorial. The in-game Tutorial gives you instructions on WHAT to do... but gives you nothing on HOW to do it. I spent way too many hours trying to figure out simple things like how to move from point A to point B.... How to adjust my course to follow or catch up to my target, or How to calculate a hit on my target (heck, I finally ended up on YouTube watching someone and having to pause the video every couple of seconds to mimic their mouse clicks; then try to duplicate that just to have an idea of what I was suppose to be doing, that the game didn't feel necessary to tell me. And I'm not just talking about point and click; You had to LOOK at your target, click the Stadimeter, line the image up, click the Red Arrow (for some unknown reason) click the buttom in the middle, then the Red Arrow again, then the Right button, then the Stop Watch, then Red Arrow again. I know that didn't make a lot of sense, but those were the notes I wrote while watching the video. Maybe it's just me, but it seems something that complicated should be a Major and specific part of a Tutorial; What to hit and why you're hitting it.

Anyway... again... it's really not a bad game, if that is your cup of tea; but if you're more of the Realistic (but it's still just a game) Gamer... then you may want to take a pass.\t\t\t\t\t
