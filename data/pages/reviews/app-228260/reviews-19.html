\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game seems like it has a lot of potential. It drew me in when a friend showed it to me. So I bought it while it was on sale with 5 DLC. Sadly, I would not recommend this game. There were things I liked and didn't like. The game does a terrible job of explaining itself. It has a tome of knowledge in the game, but it is useless. The information in the games tome of knowledge is bare bones at best. Most of the time, the information about units or buildings etc is better explained during game than in the tome of knowledge. The database has no search function and all the entries are not very helpful at all. Things like total production needed for buildings and total research needed for specific research isn't available as far as I could tell.

When creating a new character and faction, there is very little explanation for what many of the options do. One example is the scrying pool. One of the starting characters has access to building a scrying pool in her settlements. It is a very helpful feature but I could not figure out why she had access to this. Eventually I figured out it was because her faction had the Enchanters feature. But in the faction creation screen the explanation for the Enchanters feature does not mention the scrying pool. The only time I saw it mentioned was when selecting enemy AI's to play againsts, if you hover over the enchanters feature there, it does mention the scrying pools, but it does not mention them when creating a faction.

Other problems: the AI is simply terrible. I understand that an AI cannot be as creative or smart as a human player, but the AI in this game is very bad. The only way for them to be a challenge is to turn the difficult up very high. When the difficulty is turned up, the AI gets bonuses to things such as their units strength, their settlements production times and growth rate, etc. Also when the AI is turned up in difficulty, their units have very high bonuses to their dodge. I would much prefer to play against a smarter AI that has less numbers bonuses than to play against a bad AI that simply is strong because of their numbers benefits compared to the player.

There were many things I enjoyed about this game, however. I enjoyed the various types of treaties and trades that could be done with the AI factions. You can trade resources such as metal, horses, and research. You could not, however, trade units, settlements, or mana between factions. I enjoyed the quests and the maps. I enjoyed leveling characters, but I found that for the most part, Mage was by far the strongest class. The assasin, defender, commander, and warrior sound interesting and do have some interesting features, but simply do not compare to the strength and utility of the mage. I also enjoyed making custom. You can make costum units that have traits that fit what you like. And you can play to the strength of your current games resources. I mean by that that if you have a lot of metal and horses, for example, you can focus on units that require metal for armor and are mounted on horses. But if you are playing a game where you have little access to horses you could focus on other things, such as using magical items on your units if you have the crystal resourse to pay for their magical equipment.

I really wanted to enjoy this game more. But overall, I found it seemed like a game that was rushed or just needed more time and money to make complete. There were many interesting features, but they often were not implimented well. In addition to this, the game was littered with bugs, oddities, and crashes. I found many bugs in the game. For example, sometimes after defeating an AI opponent, some of their units would remain on the map. There was no boats or water combat in this game, but on one map, one of the AI players was on a boat by the shore. When I tried to interact with that unit, I could start combat but I could only do a auto combat, manual combat was not allowed. Then, when I went to attack this same AI in his city, even though he had many units in the city including his leader, when I initiated combat the combat window showed only his leader and a boat unit and only the option to auto combat them. When I auto faught them and won, I could not on the same turn try to fight his city again to take the city. I would end the turn and on the next turn the same thing would happen where I could only auto combat the leader and a boat (in his city, not by the water).

The game crashed often. Sometimes it would crash and immedietly close to the desktop. Other times it would crash and I would have to use control alt delete to end program which was not responding. On at least one occasion, the game 'soft locked'. I tried to end the turn and it would not end. The sound effect for clicking on the end the turn icon would play but the turn would not end. I found with any of the DLC turned on, the game crashed even more often. Speaking of the DLC, I was very dissapointed by the DLC. I purchased the game with 5 DLC for an additional 50 cents (a dime per dlc). They were not even worth a dime. The new leaders in the leaders DLC had builds that you could not recreate as a custom character. For example, one kingdom leader had access to death magic. Normally, kingdoms have access to life magic and empires have access to death magic. I tried to play as her once because I was curious if she would have access to death or life shards. Normally, kingdoms converts death shards into life shards. The more shards of a particular magic type you control, the stronger the spells of that type are. Because she as a kingdom leader, the death shards turned into life shards. Her death magic spells could never be enchanced because she could never control death shards. It seemed like a very poor decision. There were additonal leader traits added in the DLC, but I found them either useless or to be frank, dumb. One for example, increased damage when fighting male opponents. I found this idea incredibly stupid and the other two traits added very useless.

The DLC did not fix bugs or help prevent crashing. Having the DLC installed caused more crashing, in fact, and overall did not improve the game at all.

I really wanted to enjoy this game. Like I said, I feel that it had a lot of potiential, but was executed very very poorly. I wanted to spend a lot of time with this game, but it is simply not worth the time. If you're interested in another game like this that works much better, I would recommend Age of Wonders 3.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun game defenitely. I came here looking for something similar like Age of Wonders and was partly disappointed.

The thing is the game comes with great ideas and lots of potential.

The problem is many of these ideas are not thought through. For exampel tactical-combats-map and unit starting postions are generated as random as they can be. You can attack the same army on the same world tile 10 times and the tactical map will always look different. Meaning the enemy might spawn right next to your army, able to attack them immediately, while the terrain prevents you from defending yourself properly. The next time the enemies spawn 2 turns of walking away from you with open terrain, giving you more than enough opportunity to attack/defend.

It is this randomness in many ways, which is very annoying. Many technologies, traits and even basically abilities have to be googled, because they are never properly explained (like they would be in most proper games).

The magic is also kind of two egded sword. On the hand hand air and life are super powerful and fun, while on the other side they are also so overpowered compared to any other magic schools. Life got a a spell that lets you produce any unit (a expensive unit in the beginning can take up to 15-20 turns to build) immediately! Air gives you extra gold per essence in a city and yeah once you realize this, there is literally no reason anymore to take any other magic.

The last and worst is the bugs. There are so many and the sad thing is you experience them in the first 15 minutes:

The influence markes of your territory, which are damn important because they connect your cities, allow you to build ressource on mines, warg pits and so on, just disappear once you load a save game. That means you have to restart the whole game EVERY DAMN TIME, when you reload a game.

Unit staying on the same tile sometimes just won't fight as group, so might just face an enemy army with just one of your unit. Randomness? Yes!

It also seems (I am not a 100% sure!) that stray monsters only focus your building and armies and just outright ignore the AI-players.

So yes if you can get over all of this and are willing to put lots of time in understanding the basics of the game and want to explore a world with a LOT and I mean A LOT of try and error, then go ahead and buy it ON SALE. Otherwise better wait for another game.


I still play it, because I am a sucker for games like this. You might not be as irradical as I am ;)

P.S: It also says a lot about the devs, that they first brought out an even more unfinished game just called "Fallen Enchantress" (with only halv the factions, maps and others) and then "released" this better (yet even buggier) version of the game with more content though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE: Found there's a actually a ton of mods and active modding community. Will have to re-explore this game, which is exciting. Also, devs are still updating, so that's really cool.

TL;DR Great game with Civ and RPG elements. Crashes more than I like, and you would think they'd have that fixed at this point. Still I love the way it looks and plays and could lose myself for hours in it when it doesn't crash.

This game has a lot to love. It's not the perfect blend of Civ and Heroes of Might and Magic games, but it's headed in the right direction. Some of my favorite games are from Illwinter, Dominions and Conquest of Elysium, this has better graphics, but less overall moddable than Dom and COE (not sure about this now that I know there's a modding community will update again). I love the ability to create custom heroes and factions as well. Like Civ if you chose slow pacing (like marathon on CIv) it's going to take a long time to get any buildings done or units built, and is a little slower than I prefer, but I know people who dig that so more power to them.

Good
Solid fantasy based strategy game with likeable graphics
Lots of factions and leaders with numerous ways to win
Can get creative with factions, leaders, etc.

Bad
Crashes too often (Using a decent Dell G7 purchased this year, so I don't think my computer's the problem)
If you don't have a faction that can buy quest scrolls (pay 100 to shop to do more quests) it can be tedious trying to find new quests
Heroes based on fame can limit you at times, unless you have a faction with henchmen, and you can't take advantage of the governor/adminitstrative traits/perks. (To be fair this is more of personal preference thing, you may not care.)

When all is said and done, I like this game a lot. I look forward to improvements or sequels to this game because it's a great concept.

I recommend this game, but would highly recommend it when it's on sale ( because who doesn't like a good sale?) especially because of the DLC you'll want to get with it. Enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t

Personally I haven't dealt with many bugs, and I would generally recommend this game if you want to try this style of game. This game publisher has a solid reputation amongst strategy gamers and they seem to be players of games as well as producers of the games. A day before this review came out they released a patch which fixed a small bug. That's noteworthy because this game is six years old and still recieving support.

In general I would say that this game has grown on me over the years - and on sale, it definitely has the potential to satisfy that unique CIV with Magic itch you might be having right now, and with good value.\t\t\t\t\t
