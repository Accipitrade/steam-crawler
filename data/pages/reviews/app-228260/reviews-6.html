\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike most of the people who've looked up Stardock's Elemental: War of Magic, I am a MOM veteran from way way back. I had extremely high hopes for the Elemental series when Stardock started development. They were saying the right things, and showing the right things. It was MOM on steroids. MOM v1.04 plus RPG plus new ideas without the bugginess and balance issues of MOM.

Unfortunately, the first few iterations of the game was buggy and unbalanced. I was confused with the standalone DLCs which were actually really different versions of the base game.

10 years after, looking back at all this, here are my thoughts.

What is Fallen Enchantress: Legendary Heroes:

It is a MOM clone, fantasy 4x.
Good city management aspects, but do not expect Civilization depth.
Spells are good, and I like that spells are based on the heroes you have, expanding your choices as you get more heroes or as they level up
Units can be leveled up and upgraded
Story is bland. I didn't even bother to follow what was going on
Questing system is tedious - the Master Quest involved you moving around just to prolong the fighting portions
Hero items are generic, and not as good as it could be (Heroes of Might and Magic have more interesting hero items)
Still slightly buggy
Graphics are a bit dated (this I didn't mind too much)
Wished there were more races! Or at least, more race distinction
Wished there were more fantasy units, leading to more interesting and distinct battles (end game battles were basically archers vs cavalry)
Poor documentation and explanation of the game mechanics

I played MOM when I was 14, and for the next 3 decades, I was looking for the same excitement and experience. I am sure this is something people are still looking for, given the number of clones and spiritual successors (or even Slitherine's official reboot/remake) there are. But we are looking at it with nostalgia, and our expectations have been altered and glossed by time.

I know this because I bought MOM on Steam, and gave up after 10 minutes. I hated it.

I hope that we will still be able to capture that MOM lightning in a bottle again. But I certainly doubt it will ever come without making fundamental changes to the way Fantasy 4x games are.

As it stands, and despite all the issues of Fallen Enchantress, it is the best Fantasy 4x game of this decade.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFallen Enchantress is one of the best fantasy 4X game you can find out there. It's not too overwhelming to understand and play for the first time. This is also the first ever fully-pledged 4X where everything just clicks and I have won for the first time without resorting to any guide, but maybe that's because I've past experiences with other 4Xs. Either that or the AI is easy, even on Challenging. Many customization options for both heroes-empires and units designs, hence many ways to play and have fun. Dragons are OP, so it's a plus.

Stardock is still updating this game even after almost a decade at this point. They have my full respect, even if other games they created are not as good as this.

The first only gripe that I have with this game at the moment is that there's no multiplayer at all, not even a hotseat, which is a real shame. I hope Stardock can implement a hotseat multiplayer without shenanigans like "auto-resolve only combat". I really want to enjoy this with my friend...

Also, creating a custom character is great and all, but there are some pains that I had to go through. First, there's no edit character or empire option. Once you save them, there's no going back. If you really want to go back and edit your character, you have two options: create a new one with the same name or edit the XML file directly with a text editor. If you want to change your character icon to a half-body shot like default characters, you have to screenshot it yourself, save the picture somewhere, fill the XML with the directory, and restart the game, which are too many steps to do for an icon. But, that one is a nitpick at this point.

Overall, really great game. I give it 9/10. It's not perfect by any means, but it's really fun and enjoyable to play and immerse in.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComing from that neither fish or foul era of PC gaming around the early 2010's (or as some would call it the final days of boxed PC games), Fallen Enchantress (an update of the original Elemental War of Magic from 2010) feels like neato comfy through back to that odd era of mixed strategy games. And in many ways it's turn based game is almost a predecessor to Eternal Legend.

But where this game has a more traditional economy and turn based development, there's a better feeling of pacing in this older turn based game compared to it's later day competitors, there's not as much of that hard long drag on resources before you get up to speed. And best of all it's low end specs and lack of heavy bells and whistles means that this can outright be played on everything from a toaster to many that one or two lucky few that were able to snatch an RTX from bitcorn miner surge. And really half of the fun is just screwing around in the free form mode with it's variety of heroes and rando quests. Though it should be noted that unlike later turn based games, the initial factions don't have that same unique flare from the endless games, but for the fantasy game there's enough subtle differences.

A solid recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
