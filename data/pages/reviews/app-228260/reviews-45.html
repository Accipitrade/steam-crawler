\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere was a previous incarnation or two of this game and this one plays in a similiar vein to the previous versions. Set up a "colonly", build a few buildings and expland your territory. There is a party adventure mode as well that lets you explore your world, upgrade your adventurers, and collect items to equip them with. The campaign could use some work though... a bit more depth, cut scenese, voice overs, ect. But really I bought this game for the map editor and sandbox mode. After awhile (40 hours) it does tend to get repetative. That said I was intrigued enough to play each sandbox custome game to see each individual ending related to that vicotry condition. If there was a way to infuse "more story" into the game it'd be a real gem. The only knock against this game is if you play the "huge" map setting. I have an advanced gaming rig that starts to chug and eventually crash if the world is overly populated with cites.

An 8 out of 10. If the developers would add cutscences and some RPG elements it could easily climb to a 10 out 10. The expansion packs seem focused towards "new loot" and different AI characters to play against. They don't really add very much to the gaming experiance so only buy them if you have a couple extra bucks collecting dust.\t\t\t\t\t

Civilization 5 works perfectly fine and runs a millions times better than this game. Civ5 is a much more beautiful, bigger and CPU/GPU intensive game than Fallen Enchantress.

Gameplay: So is the game fun? Yes, it is. It reminds me of an old Magic game by Microprose back in the old dos days of computers. It is a mix of Civilisation where you create cities, build and manage them while you conquer the world in a way that best fits your style. And then battle enemies with your armies of heroes and units that gain experience and level on a grid battlefield a little like Might and Magic: Heroes, but better since good unit placement is actually important.

Graphics: They are subpar at best by this day and age. I thought the textures looked dull and certain equipment items for your heroes were blurry and I sometimes could barely make out what they were just by looking.

Audio: I thought that the audio was good, nothing stood out here. The music was fine, not excellent.

Can I recommend it? Yes. Although I can't help but think what this game could have been. A classic if the studio would have spent a little more time polishing it.

Would I have played longer than 14 hours if the experience was better? Completely!\t\t\t\t\t
