\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend this game for anyone liking a empire-building game where you rule over land. There are some things I should mention. Let's get over the cons first.

BUGS and CONS (EDIT: ALL THESE HAVE BEEN FIXED)
-If you load another game during a game. the boundaries of people's territory don't show (have to go to main menu every time).

-When your character has all skills/abilities of the basics and their class, you can't move forward. Level up screen pops up and the game doesn't allow you to close out of it (really lame). You'll most likely beat the game before you get that high so it's not a huge deal, but it sucks you can play as a max level monster.

-The enemy AI is garbage. It's pretty much impossible to ally with anyone.

PROS
-Very detailed as far as leading people and managing funds

-Character customization is amazing

-Stats, power-ups, and abilities are easy to follow

-The stats are fairly balanced. Despite it being a turn-based game dependent on someone's speed, you aren't utterly destroyed before moving if your speed isn't really high.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fun game. It is not as well optimized as I would like it to be though. My favorite thing about this game, is that with the element of having your sovereign level up along with your other units, it creates the opportunity for your sovereign (who for all intents and purposes is you, the leader of the faction) to be ridiculously powerful (in a good way).

Additionally being able to create your own faction is a feature I've always liked, and I'm glad it is in this game. I've made several already. I was a little bummed there is no sea traversing (from what I've seen in my many hours anyway). But the game makes up for it with how much fun it is to strategically position your units over the map (there's an option to change the map's appearance, a cloth map option, which makes it look like a board game when you're not in battle).

Another game like this I've played is Age of Wonders III, I have to say I hold both of them in equal standing. There's some things I like and don't like about both of them. Age of Wonders III performs much better on my rig than this game. One is newer, sure, but an i5-7600k with a 1070ti shouldn't struggle at 1080p (in AOW III I play in 4k with no problems). Good game, just be aware of performance issues (they might be remedied by tweaks, but I usually don't settle for anything less than high or ultra).\t\t\t\t\t
