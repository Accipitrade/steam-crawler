\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, this is not a complete, finished review. This is an impression after some initial play:
1) It's the same old strategy game min-max. It's fun, to a degree, but... does no one else play these games to imagine the evolution of a kingdom? To imagine the search for rumored treasures? To quote Damon Knight (with regard to science fiction), "In Search of Wonder"? Because it utterly fails in that regard. "Quests" are just a mechanism to upgrade your monarch or hero. There is no wonder.

2) The whole "weak/medium/strong..." description of foes is worse than useless, since one "weak" foe can wipe out another. Why the hell is there no "run away" option? The whole thing is just irritating, especially sIince "armies" (mostly monsters) litter the damned map. It makes the original Master of Magic look like a peaceable place.

3) The tutorial is... terrible. It covers enough to start, but almost nothing is explained of the significance of the choices you will need to make when upgrading heroes.

I bought this as a Master of Magic replacement. It does a very poor job of that. The heroes lack the romance of "Roland the Paladin" or "Mystic X the Unknown". It feels like a fantasy game constructed by someone with a good grasp of math but no sense of story.

Master of Magic was not a perfect game, by any stretch. But this game, while perhaps mechanically sound, is not at all its successor.

The stupid Steam review system forces me to give thumbs up or down. I gave down because, so far, I don't really enjoy it. I want to like it, and I keep coming back to it, but it irritates me into quitting every time.

But the point is not the thumbs up or down. If you like these games just for the mechanics, you may like it just fine. If you want something with more flavor... not this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt some point I played this game and I must have enjoyed it because I have 70 hours in it though that's hardly a drop in the bucket for games I have repeatedly enjoyed over the years. I recently redowloaded the game in an effort to reconnect with it.

My experience since then has been one of frustration and anger. Frustration because everything seems dull, hard and meant to be annoying. From the UI feeling stiff and unreasonable to the lack of movement points and general weakness of your starting hero. The anger is because I do remember play this game and somehow deriving a sense of fun out of it. It is true that a lot has happened in the lst 5+ years to change my perspective on what makes a good game. But most of the games I've enjoyed over that time have stuck with me. This one feels like it SHOULD be fun but it is designed instead to be *not* fun. I do not understand exactly why. I suppose one reason is the attrition in starting out with a weak hero and weaker still units and no way to build units quickly (the better designs you can make are very expensive to create and most abilities are not great.

Keep in mind this review is an expression of my opinion of the game as it is now. The UI gaffs and weirdnesses are highly forgivable. Designing UI that works elegantly and pleases most people is not an easy task. So I am more than willing to look past that. The losing units right away to RNG swings that make no sense is another story. I expect less RNG and more tactics in a strategy title. I realize that we can't escape the RNG and sometimes it can be quite the difference between boring and highly fun. But here it seems torturous. If you lose a unit, you are snowballed into having no chance against other weak units or you sit on the sidelines and wait, (and wait and wait and wait) for units to be created and then you have to pile them altogether (one of those UI things that I dislike a lot) and some how guide everyone back to where you want to be. (That's fine, walking around *is* a staple of RPGs even if it is quite tedious the way it is done here. )

But another problem is if you don't level up units without losing them, you end up unable to fight enemies worth fighting or doing even the weakest of quests involving combat. So the attrition problem actually matters a great deal. And you can bet the AI (sad fellas that they are) are probably taking as much territory/treasure, etc as they can whilst you sit and twiddle your thumbs or get stomped and then sit and twiddle your thumbs.

So the other thing to do in the beginning aside from clearing out monsters/doing quests is to explore. It is after all a psuedo 4x. Exploration is one of the cornerstones of 4x games. Here you might find some fun goodies (nothing OP as far as I can see or remember, or even anything you'd want to equip for that matter). But you have to walk around in a slow torturous manner that does not inspire hope of getting anywhere soon. (Did I mention the slow movement?)

The city building aspect is a kind of cross between Master of Magic style games and Civ style games. There are buildings you need to be able to grow your "civ" and everything is pretty much as you might expect for such a game, if not very exciting or interesting. Granted since I restarted I have not progressed very far (and this is part of my frustration with the game as it is now. It discourages progression from what I can tell.)

This all seems very negative and I wish it was not. There are some good aspects to the game. In fact one which reminded me to try the game again is the leader customization part. Just as in MOM and other AoW style games you can design a leader. (And an Empire!) Dominions series does this better but FE is not slack in this department. There are 6 points and you can spend them in a wide variety of ways. Magic schools, talents, items and weaknesses (1 per hero though because balance?) And you can customize their look, whether they start with a horse and how they stand. The graphics style is not my favorite. (Think early 00s Morrowind style) but the character generator does give you a number of style choices. So a big ++ for that aspect.

The older version of this game had some glaring balance issues as I recall and I can see why Stardock chose to start over and create this version even if playing it does not sit well with me now. I just wish it was a little more user friendly and a little less annoying in the beginning. I also wish the isometric landscape camera was not set at one (very annoying) angle. It makes the whole visual sense of the game feel "wrong" to me. I might have become spoiled by games with multiple camera views since I last played this.

As per usual, with most of my "NO" votes, I would probably say "Maybe" if such an option existed (but doesn't because thanks Valve!) because honestly it is not the worst game I have ever played and I do remember it being fun at one time. It just isn't to me now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStardock Games I'm pleading with you.
You did decently well here, there's still a very high difficulty curve but that's tolerable. If you made essentially this game with bigger maps (even the largest ones feel small, the wasteland areas take up too much space on maps this size would be better and easier just to have bigger maps. Also restricting how many cities the player can own is a dirty move), and some of the features that were in the Original Elemental (The politicking and marriage alliances, the slightly better faction and city design mechanics etc.) you'd have what is effectively something that could cut the floor out from under some very profitable franchises, You'd basically be moving in on civ, total war and to an extent paradox's territory. But a very similar game Age of Wonders 3 did amazingly well for a crowd funded indie game. you guys being an actual studio could in theory do so much better.
AS it stands your games have been losing features and becoming less unique and more derivative for some time. Which is why the Sorceror king did rather poorly at least as I understand it. You need to put the effort in and make features work not strip out anything that takes some time other devs are doing this and it's sad. I mean you literally have the foundations of a sort of fantasy crusader kings if you were just willing to go with it, you even have a much better battle/combat system than anything paradox has done already. Look around at your competition for a second and see is there something they aren't doing, is there something we can do better. Consumers care way more about quality than brand name right now.
While the game is decent, even with full expansions it lacks in several areas and I can't recommend it in good conscience. A improved version would have my support and money in a heartbeat though.\t\t\t\t\t
