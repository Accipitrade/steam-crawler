\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first glance, this looks like a pretty nifty cross of Civilization and Heroes of Might and Magic. And the idea really has potential, especially with the option to customize one's starting hero/civilization for a freeform game a lot like in Galactic Civilizations.

Unfortunately, a great idea alone does not a great game make. In fact, I can't help but feel the idea is exactly what turns this game into a bit of a mess, because rolling CIV and HoMM into one simply does not work. I mean, the early game in CIV is all super-slow city development and tech research, whereas HoMM is all about having your heroes go out and do stuff from day one. That's a pretty huge gap in concept and Fallen Enchantress completely fails to bridge it. City development and tech start at a CIV-like glacial pace, but you're left feeling you're supposed to do stuff with your hero like in HoMM (being basically told so if you play a campaign).

Admittedly, it almost works. Usually, the first couple of easy fights and mini quests give you a sense of progress. And then, things go downhill in a hurry because you run into your first fight highlighting how poorly balanced combat in this game is. I kid you not, based solely on the random placement of units on the map and luck with initiative, I have been completely wiped out and won without a scratch in the very same fight. Strategy and tactics my ass!

I'm really disappointed because I love the idea, but I'll go back to CIV and HoMM. Even the worst installments of those franchises never managed to drain my enthusiasm the way this game did, mostly because no CIV was this unbalanced (that's saying something!) and no HoMM featured fights this impossible early in campaigns unless impossible difficulty was exactly what it said on the tin.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVerdict: This is the 4x game I always wanted. A grand turn-based strategy game, comparable to Civilization IV but in a gorgeous high-fantasy setting with tactical battles and RPG elements. Most significantly for me, it is fully customisable: design your own faction, leader and units to suit your personal playstyle and role-play. Longer games have stability issues, but I fixed mine by turning off anti-aliasing. 8/10

Story: It may be misleading to speak of story in a game like this, and I have not yet played the campaign scenario. The premise of the world, elaborated here from previous Elemental games, is interesting enough. And the customisation allows you to create your own story as you please. 6/10

Gameplay: Despite some AI and balance issues, the gameplay is solid and very engaging. It’s not long before that classic Civilization “one more turn” syndrome sets in and takes over, and single games can easily steal away 20-30 hours. Though the fashion in 4x strategy is towards hexagons, square-based maps do the job here, and it is fun to explore rich landscapes dense with monsters, treasure and quests to fulfill. City and resource management are interesting, and it’s satisfying to see your metropolises grow and your empire expand. Research is broken into three tech trees (Civic, Warfare, Magic) you advance separately, making it a strategic choice of where your focus will be. Tactical battles are fun, if a bit on the simple side; tactics emerge solely from your unit design and the composition of your armies, without regard for other factors such as terrain. Auto-resolve can be a bit wonky, and can sometimes beat otherwise unwinnable battles, but it is not game-breaking. The UI is a masterpiece and provides all the information you need to manage your empire. Detailed faction and unit customisation are what tie all these factors together and make Fallen Enchantress a superior experience to similar 4x games. 9/10

Graphics: The graphics will appear a bit dated to some, but the art style fits the unique setting nicely resulting in a somewhat cartoony, but still beautiful, dark and broken world. 7/10

Sound/Music: The sound is not exceptional (ambiance is decent), but the music is great with some truly epic themes to conquer to. 7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played the original Fallen Enchtress and then I picked this up. This has the polish the first was missing (managing city expansion, better unit promotion, better hero accumluation, unit weapons feeling different, etc.). It still is lacking a lot of polish though.

I have played the Civ games since Civ 2, and this is reminisent of a Civ light mechanics (tech tree, placing cities, cultural borders, corruption/unhappiness/ unrest, city improvements the list goes on). It reminds me of Lords of Magic (an old game) updated with a Civ like interface.

From a mechanics standpoint and Polish I keep not wanted to reccomend the game - poor graphics, poor AI, no multiplayer, etc. I really should just put in Civ every time to get the polish I am looking for, but I don't. I come back to this game periodically because it is fun to outtech everyone or summon 10 scouting towers on the map in a turn. This is definitely a game to pick up on a steam sale, but if you like RPGs and you like Civ then consider this game.\t\t\t\t\t
