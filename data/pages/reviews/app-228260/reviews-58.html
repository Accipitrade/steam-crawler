\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like Civilization? Do you like RPG's? This is basically Civ where you can level up your leader like an RPG character, give them gear and talents, and let them go forth and do your bidding. Also there's questing, recruiting magical creatures or other heroes.

You control various cities with an area of influence around them, and deal with managing cities by building structures, focusing on what the city produces, and doing research, as well as controlling natural resources on the map. There are wandering monsters (Think Barbarians in Civ) that pop up from time to time and they may attack a city if they are close enough.

You can build armies of general sorts of units, but even this has flavor because you're not just designing them and naming them, you pick the equipment and gear they use, and you do have the ability to auto-upgrade existing units to whatever the newest armor/weapons that your research has unlocked, provided you have the resources.

The real fun comes from heroes, who either are recruited during certain quests or random events, or seek you out to join you when you have accomplished enough to gain enough fame. Your Sovreign (your faction's leader) is a hero with talent trees with plenty of choices, spells, and gear just like any good RPG. They can be skilled up to be good at managing a city, good at leading an army, or good fighters in and of themselves (or anything in between really). The same goes for other heroes you come across.

There is diplomacy with other empires but for the most part you're flying solo and trying to win on your own. There are numerous victory options, and most mirror the ones you'd find in Civ.

The random events and quests are varied and interesting, and the randomly generated maps lead to a lot of replayability.

The weakest aspects of the game are the city building (which, while it has depth, is nowhere near what you'd find in a dedicated city building game), the RPG aspects (while there's a lot to do and the random events are good, eventually you'll get some repeats after you've played a bunch of games). They are both pretty well done but not great - it is better than average at both but exceptional at neither. Additionally, there's not really ANY story or campaign mode. You have scenarios, but this is largely a sandbox with a randomly generated map and any story is going to be what you personally make of it via the random events that happen.

HOWEVER: This game supports mod tools and user-created races, maps, items, quests, etc. Any variety the game lacks out of the box goes away if you poke around the official forums for a bit and find something that changes things you dislike (or make your own).

In sum, this game is quite enjoyable and I find myself equally drawn in by both the empire building and RPG aspects of it. Neither aspect is 100% perfect, but the games can be long (especially if you play on enormous maps with lots of AI as I tend to), and if I get bored with the RPG aspect for a bit, I'll spend time focusing on just my cities for a bit. The game lets me play it however I want and never forces me to do anything, and there's enough to do that I consistently go back to it.

It's been out for awhile now and you can probably pick it up at a decent price. The DLC is fine - it's not must-have and it's not a waste of money. It's mostly small things - new heroes, new quests, new items, which all randomly pop up in your game. The game is fun with them, but nothing stands out as absolute must-haves. Get the DLC if you get the game and enjoy it and want more of it, or if you get the game bundled with it at a decent price.\t\t\t\t\t
