\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInteresting game, and I like a lot of the ideas. The overall concept is solid, and it implements some brilliant improvements over similar games in places. I really want to like this game a lot more than I do.

But... (and we all knew THAT was coming)
The graphics are tolerable until you get to the hero units. Human models are Not Good. The clothing options feel like they must be at several inches thick in places.
Depending on your chosen 'race', you will have resource shortfalls for a good long while. Also, heroes cost a fairly high amount of upkeep in gold... and when one is available to purchase, the game tends to not give you the option of NOT purchasing.
The entire backstory and current story of the game setting feels a bit bland and unrefined.
and the tech and skill trees are... how to put this... short. on. options.

Overall, while the game is not strictly BAD, it is not nearly as good as the somewhat unfinished, glitchy Civilization 4 TCMod that came before it.
I'm still likely to play this once in a while, but it's just going to send me scuttling back to Fall from Heaven 2. I'm recomending it though, since honestly, it's better than a lot of the alternatives OTHER than FfH2, which can be twitchy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFallen Enchantress Legendary Heroes (FELH) is a rock solid game that is filled with a lot of great content (when adding in DLC) and ideas. The problem is much of the content are set pieces in the game and new things don't happen often enough to make the world feel dynamic or lived in. That is a problem. You either steamroll everything by midgame or complete the huge quests late when it doesn't really add much.

Combat at the strategic level is usually fairly easy. The CPU does not really seem to know how to use its armies and will invariably park all of them in cities, unless it can be roused to enter a fight. Which is rare. Even if the declare war on you... they'll probably do nothing. Unfortunately these armies are either not well constructed, updated or used effectively. Like I said even in all out war the AI parks them. So you conquer everything else and then go for the killing blow. On a tactical level combat can become quite challenging and the AI seems to handle this aspect of combat better.

The DLC is a mixed bag. There is some good stuff like the undead, but the items and maps packs feel more like a donation to Stardock than great additions. I did but them all, mostly when on sale (2.50 is a better price point). What is missing is a workshop. This is what makes GalCiv3 so awesome, user created content. Even the ability to make magic items was something Master of Magic had... 25 years ago.

The ability to customize your own race/wizard is really cool and for the most part superior to Master of Magic.... save for one thing, this is humans and undead only. As in everything, save for a few monstrous units from spells. You want elves, dwarved, halflings, gnomes? Best of luck, chump. Units can be customized and upgraded too, which is cool and while there are optimal paths there are also lots of options that can differentiate units. Sadly there are no fun mercenary units to hire like Master of Magic either. Getting back to races a cool thing about MoM was that you could conquer cities and those cities made different units (big difference between a dark eld city and a human one...).

As you progress in FELH you will pick up heroes and they can be customized to some degree (earlier ones more so than later ones). They can get gear and expand their skills/abilities. Here there are a few more racial options, but for the most part its a humans club. The great balance here is that while heroes are awesome and powerful you really need an army to bolster them especially early but even later units of heavy cav and wizards do wonders to help them.

Anyways this is a fun game and while I wish it were more of a challenge, had a workshop and more variety in almost everything it has to offer from quests to races... FELH still is a great game and a worthy inheritor to the bar set by Master of Magic for a 4x game. While it does bring that old game forward with the basics, what is missing is not only noticeable, but glaring. Which is a shame.

-----------------------------------
Modest update, if you go to Nexus and download the mods they have there, FELH becomes a wildly different game. A bit more of everything especially heroes and items. New spells and an adjusted magic system are also an option. It feels like the content in the game has doubled and while I enjoyed going back to FELH for old time's sake there is so much added that I can come back and see something new every time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove this game. Build your own Soveriegn, choose a race, choose the strengths and weaknesses of your Kingdom
/Empire and then take on a world overun by monsters and other factions after a cataclysm. You can use the default unit designs they provide or make your own, name them what you want and rename them on the battlefield. "Atlar Heavy Infantry to Jamie's Besiegers" for example. This game can by as immersive as you want.

The aim of this 4x game is to successfully manage your faction towards one of 5 victory conditions whilst fending off the awakened monsters of the world and other soveriegns.

If you have played the civilation series you will feel right at home here. Diplomacy suffers alot compared to Civ but this game makes up for it in other ways. Customisable heroes and turn-based battles are examples.

Would have liked to see more customisation options to really go to town with your factions own unique identity and it would have been nice to see some more 'Master Quests'\t\t\t\t\t
