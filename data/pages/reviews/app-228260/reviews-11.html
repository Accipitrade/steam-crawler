\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay so I'm now 6 hours into my first playthrough. It is fantastic. It might even possibly be better than all HOMM games, and 3 and 7 are in my top ten of all time. There is quite a bit more depth, and the gameplay isn't necessarily that much longer. I am just about to finish crushing my first AI enemy, and I held off for a very long time before declaring war because my report continually stated I had the weakest kingdom. Turns out I have the strongest army though, by a large amount lol. So I'm probably going to sweep through the other two and finish it soon. The really cool thing I like about it, is how linked all of your progress is. I will explain below by breaking it down.

The research: It's broken down into three categories, you can go for your typical economy/building/growth route, your warfare route which grants you new, stronger units, weapons and armour, and your magic route, which grants you access to magical items, spells, and other stuff.

The Inventory: So it's neat, you can equip any of your heroes with pretty much any weapons and armour, so long as you have either obtained the items from combat, or obtained them by researching their paths and then buying them. You must also level up the required perk for higher level armour and weaponry, such as plate. You can also edit your army loadouts to come with more powerful weapons and armour (and magic items) as you unlock them through research, and the unit simply costs more production time and/or resources to make. But becomes a lot stronger as a result. So this can keep T1 units valid later game. Also you can build some stronger ones, some more defensive ones, and really create the army of your vision. Also doesn't force you to unlock higher level troops to make in order to stay relevant in late-game combat.

The level up system: It's sweet, everything gains XP. Regular dudes in your army usually have perks that scale with their level, such as damage or HP. But your commanders you can fully customize their specialization, which unlocks an entirely unique tech tree, with many paths of specialties. So for example my commander is excellent at cleaving and using axes. He gets extra damage, extra attacks, can dual wield and swing again when he misses, etc. Another commander I built pure tank, he's all defensive, can wear plate, can take a beating, has more HP, etc.

The trade system: It's neat everything you can trade for with the AI, all resources and research. You can even force them to declare war on each other. But everything has a cost to that AI, and you can clearly see it, and gold is always worth 1, other resources you have may be worth more or less if the AI has a lot or little of that resource. So you can essentially make exact trades for exact things you want, deals and treaties, etc. by balancing what resources and gold you want to give them in exchange for those things. It's super clear, and super simple. One of my favourite trade systems in any game I've encountered.

The combat system: It's all initiative based and turn based on a grid. Movement is a big deal, so is ranges. You also get gang bonuses for stacking a bunch of units around enemies. If your numpties have 4 guys in the squad, their damage is actually 4x whatever their weapon is. As they take damage, guys in the squad die and their damage goes down. Single guys such as heroes or big creatures retain all damage throughout the fight. Most things have one or more special abilities, attacks, or spells they can do. Stamina can be built up to do melee special attacks, such as cleave. Some have cool things like maul, which keep attacking until they miss or kill the unit. Spells are super crazy and deep, i've barely touched the surface since I've gone for a pure melee build and tech route on my first playthrough. The only thing I dont like is that you cannot lay your guys out before combat starts in any formation, so sometimes it starts with guys in bad positions, and the enemy goes first and kills them before you get an action.

The spells: There are tons of enchantments you can put on cities which give all sorts of buffs (or curses on enemy cities). You can also enchant your armies to have higher initiative, move faster, have more health, etc. Then im combat there are all kinds I've barely scratched the surface but I've seen the enemy cast a few cool things.

The biggest and pretty much only downside are the dumpster fire graphics. When you watch the videos you're probably thinking "wtf is this garbage". If you can get past that, it is a fantastic game underneath. Also, whatever you equip your heroes and troops with is reflected on the battlefield, so that's at least cool.

All in all highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
