\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis isn't a perfect or great game - but it is a good game and very enjoyable, especially as a solo-only player. Some Civ-type element blended with lite role playing and turn-based combat. Finding a solid starting location for your first city - with plenty of surrounding area and resources to expand into - is key. The diplomacy portion of the game is a little weak, but if you focus on signing treaties and regularly trading, you'll boost how the other "players" feel about you.

The game goes something like this: You build your first city and assign construction and research, just like many other games. While you can train scouts, your hero (or heroes) explore map. You level up, collect magic items and weapons, improve your heroes' equipment and abilities, and eventually build a party of heroes that ranges in power from "Weak" to "Epic." Throw in quests, dungeon-like locations, random events, and the threat of war, and the game can keep you having fun for... well, 1,000+ hours as of this review.

The bad? It can crash, but the game keeps two autosaves on tap. Occasionally the graphics will degrade, but exiting the game and relaunching fixes that. If you have a poor starting location (and you don't continually restart until you get a better one) the game can be pretty tedious.

But you get a solid marriage of Civ and single player D&D - and can add dragons and monsters to your party (along with heroes, hirelings, and soldiers). Where most Civ-type games start becoming monotonous about half-way through, this one remains fun almost to the very end. It's not perfect - but it's fun. I recommend this one.\t\t\t\t\t
