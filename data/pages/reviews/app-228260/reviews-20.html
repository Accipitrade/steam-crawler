Very pleased to see this as FE has become one of my favourite 4x games of all time. The fact they're still supporting it and making it sea-worthy once more even years and years after release is a commendable thing to be doing. Whilst I'm in the camp of preferring a game to be complete on release, FE has had a very chequered development history and most of us had assumed it would be consigned to the garbage pile of broken dreams a long time ago. This turns out not to be the case and I'm really hoping that Stardock continue to give it development time in order to really make it the game it was meant to be.

Took me a little while to get into this one, but really it's a very good game with a lot of depth to it. I've played pretty much all of the major 4X games out there, including Age of Wonders 3 and Endless Legend, and this game is up there with the best of them.

It's different enough from any of the others that it's worthy addition to your collection, and if you're deciding which to go with, it's probably a toss up between this and Age of Wonders 3. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an amazing and amazingly frustrating game (and not because of the situations where you are ambushed by an enemy that nukes your entire army in seconds and have to reload). What I mean is the UI is just terrible. Laggy and unresponsive to mouse clicks, with too much happening at once for a turn-based game.

If one of my cities is presently not producing something or I want to cancel my army's move command because they are needed elsewhere sometimes I can click on them and it works sometimes the game decides to let all the enemies move instead and sends my army to its doom. Why? I have no idea.

Just today while having a blast playing a custom civilization with custom-created units (so cool!) I tried to exit the game only for the new research screen to pop up. After selecting a research while simultaneously returning to the main menu I was trapped in a loop and had to force the game to crash (which it does often, usually when trying to load a save).

I would still recommend this game, but I just wish it had a little more polish and balance when it comes to the UI, a critical aspect of a 4X game.

But custom unit creation and the vast options afforded to city building do make it worth while!\t\t\t\t\t

These Factions will even be controlled by the A.I as your oponents if you arn't playing them yourselves. Magic is powerful and the struggle to get more shards to increase their power can be a challenge. The music is pretty good and the graphics arn't too bad for its age

So why do I NOT recommend this game? The answer is simple. It is one of the most unstable,buggy and crash happy games I have ever encountered, at least with newer systems that is. If you are playing on Windows 10, avoid it. I recently had a crash on this game, resulting in having to reset via the button, simply because the game refused to close or tab out, after which my computer struggled to boot back up. Now, it is very likely that the game isn't the reason that the computer struggled to reboot. However, the crashes happen often enough and normally of enough severity to cause you to have to smash the reset button that it just isn't worth the frustration. Another great game unfortunetly ruined, due to bugs and very little attempt to optimise it for newer systems.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game, I really do, but it's just so buggy. Considering this is suppose to be the improved version of the original improved version which was suppose to be an apology for how bad the original Elemental game was, its kind of insulting to the player base. In that manner Stardock is a little bit like EA in which they say they're sorry for their screw ups but go right back to performing the same mistakes.

Considering they released the sequel, Sorcerer King (Which works, but is just a bad game), it's unlikely this game will ever get the attention it so desperately needs. Stardock, stop pimping Sorcerer King, it sucks. Come back and fix Fallen Enchantress.

Edit 5/15/2019 in response to 5/10/2019 2.5 update: 20+ hours into new game testing new update, same glitches as the old version, same turn lag, oh look! NEW GAME BREAKING BUGS WHICH MAKE THE GAME LITERALLY UNPLAYABLE LOCKING UP THE WHOLE GAME. Well that's new. Some new quests and skill improvements that diversify the classes more, cute stuff really. Stardock really shows that they have lots of neat ideas and visions for this game. IT MEANS NOTHING IF YOU CAN'T GET IT TO WORK THOUGH.

Second Edit 5/31/2019: So, it would seem a short while after my last edit the Devs gave the 2.5 patch several small updates. Couldn't find a list of what was fixed so I just jumped in again and guess what? For the first time EVER I was able to play a full game of Fallen Enchantress from start to end without a single glitch! (Well there were a few random times where I couldn't target an enemy with abilities for no reason, but that's so tiny) I mean no major problems. The random crashes back to desktop, gone; that weird thing where the all water lose it's textures and becomes weird blue lines, gone. Even the dreaded glitch that freezes all your units and locks you out of ending your turn with no way to fix or stop it is gone. Sure maybe they're still there and I just go lucky so I went a second game from start to end and guess what? No glitches again! So after testing the game for some 30 additional hours I think after my last post I'm giving Fallen Enchantress back my vote of confidence. Only took them what, 6 years? Who cares? It seems to be working now and that's great. Thanks Stardock Devs for proving that all those years of being a loyal supporter and fan actually pays off.

I still don't like Sorcerer King, but who knows. At this rate I may buy it anyway as just a thank you for listening to us.

Spoke to soon. The "crash to windows" glitch still exist. I don't know how I went from no glitches to 6 in a row in one day. Might have something to do with map size and the AI difficultly of opponents, but who knows. It was fun while it lasted at least.

Final Edit 6/21: I quit. If the crash was rare I'd be fine, but it happens almost every play through, in every session, almost every 5 turns. I have the autosave set to save every 2 turns and sometimes It doesn't even make it to that. I give up. No amount of work will ever get this game working again though it's admirable that they tried. Who knows, maybe in 3 more years I'll actually be able to play a working copy.

Not so Final Edit: Feb. 10th 2022: 3.0 Update released recently. Tried again. Crashes are about once every five hours now. An improvement, yes, but remember this game has been out for almost 10 years now. I give recognition to Stardocks insistence that they can make this game run..., but they can't and because of that I still don't think it's worth your time or money. Shame too. I was almost starting to have fun.\t\t\t\t\t
