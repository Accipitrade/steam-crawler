\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just have to finally put a review down for this game since I don't even know at the time that I played it there were reviews for games on Steam (maybe, though unsure). What caught my eye to come back to this game and write a review is that I get updated in my news feed that they introduced a new patch.....8 YEARS later! This stands out to me significantly, since it shows that the developer will still support their games years after their initial release. I've played plenty of games that will get you to buy their product at launch, or at first received and offer little to no patches afterwards. Even with people having issues throughout the years of performance, crashes, or whatever the bugs or problems might have been at the time this could lead you to feel possibly feel unsure of making a purchase.

I can only speak for myself and when I played the game all the years ago to what I recall then. However, at that time I played it back in like 2014 I did not really encounter any game breaking bugs, crashes, or anything of the like. Whether this was due to luck, or my system specs at the time; I cannot say.

What I will comment on though is that this was one of those games that I started playing and pretty much continued non-stop until I beat it. This can be a more rare event for me nowadays, since there are so many games out there and it is easy for you to get side-tracked and move on to another. But I believe even if the game came out today it would be a similar situation for me.

If you are into RPG's, strategy games, and city builders than this will fill that void of those two genre's for you with an excellent story and a build your character element to it. It was a perfect mix of the 3 that seamlessly flowed together really well and kept your interest in what was ongoing in the story and game.

The combat itself reminds me of a fire emblem game with strategy components, along with picking a hero similar to Warcraft 3. The heroes have various traits, equipment, and play-styles you can select from.

Pros of the game -

Fantasy RPG
Turn-based strategy similar to fire emblem
Character skill trees
Equipment/customizing your character
Epic Boss Battles
Great story
Engaging combat

Cons -

You haven't played it yet\t\t\t\t\t

Now that I just earned the in-game achievement for playing 40 hours, it says that 16.1% of this game's owners have achieved the same thing. Hmmm...

The game is not bad. Of course, I want a map editor. I look in the game folders - nothing. I go online - 2012 seems to be the last time this game was popular. I go to the game's forums: everything stops in 2014.

The game crashes when I play it, usually around Turn 50-80. Looks like that has been an issue since 2014 or earlier.

Stardock is a weird company. I started with them when Galactic Civilization II came out. They do weird, seemingly random game updates for, like, years... And they mess around with game balancing and whatnot. It's just weird.

Anyway, their games are too unstable; the support for them is shoddy; the development is shoddy; and I have absolutely no idea why Brad Wardell is worshiped - he is to video game development what Chuck Norris or Steven Seagal were to action movies: a poor man's Rambo. I think you should just avoid anything made by Stardock. Or, go online and read all the horrible tales of gamers who buy shiddy games, can't get them to work, give Stardock a chance, get burned by Stardock's incompetence, and then bang their heads against the wall. It always ends with, "I think the game concept is great, but I just can't get it to work!"

That is the legacy of Stardock for everything they attempt to do. Stop banging your head against the wall - Stardock doesn't know how to make solid video games. Literally, spend your money on anything else besides Stardock! Their products are half-baked, though perhaps the programmers are baked every day.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played 185 hours in the earlier game "Elemental: Fallen Enchantress", and by the time I bought Fallen Enchantress: Legendary Heroes I was pretty much pooped out for any more. So I forgot all about the game. I'm not even sure if you can buy the original game on Steam anymore.

Anyways... about Stardock. They play a game called "Milk the customers", so you'll see a ton of content, sometimes worthwhile, but most of the time it's content composed of trivial modifications. They have DLC down to a science, and it's my biggest gripe with them. So if you ever see a new game coming from Stardock, unless you really want to keep throwing money their way (hey there's nothing wrong with that if that's your thing), it's best to wait a while (as in years) before buying any of their games, to let them settle in bugfixes and figure out all the DLC that will go with any new game. Fallen Enchantress shows this pretty clearly with all the various DLCs available. It's a bit comical to see this play out.

The game is similar to Civilization but battles are played out in a battle-field screen (a.la. Master of Magic, Age of Wonders, etc.).

The tech tree has 3 nice "separate" trees for Civilization, Warfare and Magic (they aren't really separate - they could have glued these trees into 1 root and the tree would have been the same, but now you have to click an extra button to select which tab, and each tree is never wider than 4 parallel options for a given "technology depth" level).

Settling new cities requires finding areas that can grow food, which are limited on the map, and usually occupied by monsters, some of them roaming. So the drill becomes build a bigger army and conquer locations to build cities.

Every unit can be hand-designed, but you can just use the canned designs too. The unit designer is enjoyable to use and you can build fairly elite units right from the beginning, but the catch is that some special resources might be needed which your cities might not have access to, so map tactics for city location is a big deal.

The "Next Turn" button can be configured to be pressed automatically after all units have moved as far as they can move. This is a great feature - just be careful if you know you want to change something.

I haven't tried brutally high difficulties (or if I did, it was on the older game and I've just forgotten). Stardock / Draginol (lead developer) were pretty excited that they had added multi-threading to allow the enemies to figure out their next moves while you are making your move. I can't say whether or not the AI is dumb. The battle-field AI seems pretty good actually - so that if you're waiting for the AI to walk stupidly into your trap, it will move slowly and not max out its moves to wait for you to cross onto it's side of the map first.

Cities can grow to really huge proportions, though I've never seen this take place in any of my games, because I worry too much about conquering everything. But I am still tempted to see just how big I could make a city.

Stuff I like:

- The back-story is nice. The world fell into chaos and now you're scraping to rebuild it and be the prime ruler among other pretenders - something like that.

- The music is actually good, and better than the original Fallen Enchantress. Yes I noticed. Thank you Stardock.

- you would never know it by looking at the game marketing, but the game has a sense of humor that comes up here and there, such as in the battlefield voice acting derpiness. Was that by design? Did they have a product requirement document that said "make the game derpy"? People want to know!

- Unit design is fun, and pretty much drives all the game-play decisions. This is a strength of Stardock's and other games like Galactic Civilizations do this really well too.

- The quests are fun. They aren't not always "go fetch me a pail/wolf pelts/spider glands/slinky". No sometimes there's another option to use your skills to resurrect someone or something like that and it's a big time saver from having to run all over the map to fulfill a request.

- Replay value is very high. There are many starting hero options which can change the gameplay significantly.


Stuff Not So Much:

- The artwork / shaders are more cartoon-like and almost amateurish. This is tolerable - but I never found it "inspiring" when compared to other games.

- The color pallette. Because this is a "world is being rebuilt from chaos" theme, everything is brown. Almost everything. Well it's enough to say "wow this is really drab".

- It does play fairly slow. There are artificial waves of enemy units that appear at trigger points, along with how the AI responds to your strength versus theirs. Once you understand a fairly fast path to OP units you can ignore a lot of red-herring technologies to try to maximize army strength. Even when playing this way - it takes a very long time to get to strong units. Every enhancement provided by new technology is only slightly incremental.

- The opposing players play a minor role for most of the game. Instead, this is more of a PVE with local monsters in abundance and at wide ranges of difficulty to beat. The game almost implodes on itself in this regard, as it is supposed to be a 4x - but it almost doesn't play like any other 4x - it plays more like an RPG.

- I find myself leaving cities mostly unguarded due to the ridiculously high costs of building defending units, opposed to leaving it to chance that they'll never be attacked. They don't get attacked either, most of the time, so this becomes a regular part of the strategy.

- A bad map roll is not representative of game-play. Just roll a new map. Sorry - I wish this was more clear for new players.


Stuff you might not like, but I don't care:

- Oh there's no multiplayer? That means we can't play a 10+ hour long game that nobody would have ever finished anyway. This is an interesting point - but to make these sorts of games (Civ, etc) multiplayer and to make them work, would mean removing roughly 75% of the complexity to lower the gameplay time for a single game to about 2 hours tops (i.e. try to mimic board-game game-play), and if you did that players would complain "not enough features!" so you can't win.


I still recommend this - with a dash of hesitation that it isn't for everyone, but worth a try, especially if you are willing to give it a few extra tries on different map rolls that can change the game-play. Lastly, if you aren't sure, I think 2 hours is adequate time to play (ahem... i.e. lose a few times) to get a feel for the UI and game-play elements - and by then you should know if you need a refund or want to keep playing.\t\t\t\t\t
