\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I'll start off by saying the core premise of the game is good, as are the base mechanics. The visuals are nice and lend well to the setting, the customization options are great. My biggest problems with this are:
1) Unit pathing is ridiculously done. Units might take a 10 space longer route than just moving three spaces around a monster.
2) The Autoresolve function in combat is kind of terrible. Two units, plus a champion, against a bear cub and the entire unit gets wiped out. Manually go into combat and I don't take a single point of damage by literally just rushing it.
3) There are a few bugs, but none so frustrating as the quest objectives not updating. I have a game going where I have 6 quests that simply will not update after I complete the objective or return to the questgiver. This wouldn't be TOO terrible except one of the quests is the main quest needed to win the game.

Overall, I'd say the concept is cool, the core mechanics are interesting and fun, but with the bugs making quests impossible to complete it's kind of broken.\t\t\t\t\t

The battles can also be unpredictable and dangerous, and they require much preparation and careful grooming and assembling of the optimum composition of units and heroes to be assured of victory. Customisable units and heroes, with an experience-based level-up system, add to the challenge and satisfaction.
