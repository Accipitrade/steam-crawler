Research is huge ,as there is civilization, warfare, and magic to look after as you explore to reek havoc on the world. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is pretty great, even if it has some annoying performance issues, it can stand on its own rather well.
It feels more like a platform to make your own factions/units/stories in 4X format.
There are campaigns where you play along the game's plot but those are really way less fun than fully custom games.
In this mode you can create your ruler, faction and all units you'll use in battle customizeable with items you unlock in a typical 4X-style science research system. The game does have base units but you can truly make every single one you'll use yourself, give them names, chose if they'll be expensive and strong or cheap and weak in a multitude of ways.
It's backed up by a satisfactory mini-tactical battle system where you fight in a map outside the game's world, with some room to maneuver and several types of abilities to use. Those may cost mana that you generate outside the battle with buildings in your city.
There are heroes you can equip directly and use to complete quests you'll find exploring the map. Heroes can be specialized in several ways and equip items that give them abilities so in the end you can have a vastly different experience from one match to the other, depending on choice.
All of that together makes for a pretty interconnected gameplay loop, but still it's plagued by some strange unoptimized coding which will make turns take AGES to process when you play on bigger maps with more AI opponents. Don't buy if you don't want to play on smaller maps and have shorter games on your 4X.

It also looks really weird in general, extremely ugly but at the same time so consistent you learn to accept it's visual horrendousness.
