\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE: It has been over 2 years since I initially reviewed this title. I have plenty of time in it both here on Steam as well as in the Oculus Quest port. The title has evolved a lot over the years, so my description in the original review doesn't include any of the numerous elements that have been added to VR Sailing.

I just wanted to update my initial review because I remain extremely impressed with this title and especially the developer who is the most committed VR developer I'm aware of. VR Regatta is obviously a passion-project for this team, and they have gone to great lengths to continuously improving the title in order to spread their love and knowledge of sailing. It really is an awesome title, and I do recommend it to anyone who is slightly interested.

ORIGINAL REVIEW FROM FEB 2018:
I've only spent a couple of hours in VR Regatta so far so my review won't be as thorough or in-depth as I'd prefer. I have no experience sailing whatsoever but for whatever reason this title intrigued me. After asking about it on Reddit, one of the developers jumped in and answered some of my questions and seemed like a good dude so I decided to take a chance.

VR Regatta walks a fine line between education and game. The simulation aspects of the game seem fairly realistic, however they are "dumbed-down" a bit. For example, you can't capsize, crash, and there's no bad-weather sailing. But other than that, the developers have made a solid attempt to provide a realistic simulation to bring new people into the (real-world) sport.

The game aspects are limited to racing, and that's generally done in service of subtly trying to teach you more about controlling the boat. It is both fun and challenging.

There is a multiplayer element of the title, but I haven't explored that yet so look to other reviewers for details.

One thing the developer shared with me on Reddit is that much of the feedback that they get regarding this title is that it is "relaxing." This is true. There is definitely something satisfying about just tooling around with no real goal.

I probably should add that I have a Vive and I especially enjoy VR Regatta as a seated game. Almost every game I own is room-scale so I don't have a ton of experience playing VR titles seated--but I've found I REALLY like that aspect of VR Regatta. I suppose this is largely because being seated is the realistic way to sail--it just seems very natural.

Look, I know my review isn't the most in-depth, and I certainly don't have a crap-ton of hours in it yet. But here's what I can tell you...it's really cool. Even if you don't sail (like me) this is a pretty sweet simulation. I find it really interesting to see VR simultaneously used to both educate and entertain, and this title pulls that off really well. My limited interaction with the developers on Reddit was very positive and from looking at the development history of VR Regatta, it sure seems like they keep improving it--they're obviously passionate about it so I don't expect them to abandon development or any such nonsense. There are a lot of mediocre VR titles out there....VR Regatta isn't one of them. This is a really good (and fun) title. The learning curve isn't very hard the tutorial will have you sailing within 5 minutes. If you're at all interested after watching some videos, just buy it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVR Regatta is a wonderful VR sailing sim and, in my opinion, is the most complete and engaging game of its type. When I first started I had no sailing experience whatsoever yet after a minor tutorial I was still able to catch on and get sailing on expansive bodies of water (with sparse object collision) in no time.

The steering is done by gripping a rudder behind you in with one hand and the other hand controls the rigging/rope for the mainsail. Get yourself at the right angle to the wind direction and you're off! It is a pretty slick and engaging system that, somehow, managed to never give me motion sickness.

This game has come quite a ways since it was first out in early access and the developer seems to be adding and improving it on a regular basis. Models have been upgraded, a new beach bungalow to start out in and select races or sailing experiences from (I like the relaxing open sailing mode personally), a random sheep, and a big ol yacht. The yacht did cause me to need to lower my supersampling levels for performance but it was worth it.


I highly suggest VR Regatta, it is an immersive and relaxing experience that is only bound to get better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a professional sailor, and i work as a sailmaker. I'm very impressed with the game. it's not very complex (yet, i hope), but the sailing feels very realistic. controls respond well, and you can shift your body weight around the dinghy to flatten the boat out when you're heeling. the speedometer is nice too, it helps fine-tune the boat.

suggestions for improvement
* wind vane at the top of the mast
* the foresail winch should pull in with the handle, but be released by loosening the tail of the line
* the boats point a little higher than is realistic and still move upwind. Usually this is a deadzone its typically 20-40 degrees off the wind, depending on if the boat is designed for racing or cruising
* electric winch controls could be added to a binnacle in front of the wheel
* gps and compass, or other instruments could also bo added
* similar displays could be added to the mast under the boom
* the puff mechanic is a little extreme compared to the speed of the boat. the acceleration due to the puff was a little much
* spinnakers. but i see the controls for them, so maybe it's already in the works

I haven't tried multiplayer, but i think it would be really cool to do big boat racing with a couple friends, each taking a station

Overall, I have really high hopes and praise for the game in the future\t\t\t\t\t
