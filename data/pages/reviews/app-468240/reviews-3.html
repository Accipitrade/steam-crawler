\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVR Regatta is sailing simulator that is realistic, immersive and fun at the same time.

Intuitive controls and helpful voiced-over hints make the game very approachable. It's really cool that the game considers you position in the boat an make it lurch when you step away from the center.

Water and boat itself look very good, but coastal areas are quite ugly up-close. Trees and rocks have no shadows and lack polygons.

Wind and waves sounds are amazing, they really deliver the sense of speed, when you move faster than 3 knots.

Overall, VR Regatta is a great game for relaxation and learning some basics of sailing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSailing in VR Regatta gave me the feeling of being a teenager again, when I used to sail on a small ~12-foot, fiberglass-foam Sunfish.

This captures the realism of the mechanics of a small sailboat; controlling the mainsheet to adjust the sail, holding on to the tiller to steer, your positioning on the boat distributes weight appropriately & if you lean it shifts, and even the daggerboard can be raised up.
(There is also a larger sailboat with an engine and a wheel that you can switch to from the desktop menu.)

It all adds up to an immersive experience. Sailing along the islands in the freeform mode is relaxing and enjoyable; there are different times of day/night to set the mood.
There are also solo timed races and multiplayer racing for anyone who wants sailing challenges, and they're reasons to get better at the mechanics of sailing.

You're not left in the dark wondering what to do when you start -- there's a voiced mentor who gives you tips in a friendly Australian accent, teaching you what to do and how to do it, and he can be turned off once you get the hang of sailing on your own.

Great simulation that's doesn't overwhelm, and is enjoyable & relaxing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI used to ride a small sunfish sail boat in boy scouts. It was a memorable experience. I figured I would give this sailing simulator a try as a relaxing sort of game.

For me, the controls seemed pretty simple (for the dingy, sunfish type boat). I am using an Oculus DK2 and Razer Hydra motion controllers. Ideally I like to sit on the floor for this experience. Though it works well in my chair, you can adjust height in game which is a nice feature. I have not unlocked any boats besides the dingy, so I am curious to try the yacht.

My son who is 8 picked up the controls pretty quickly, enough to move about but not to race.

The graphics create a simple environment though they achieve the relaxing atmosphere I wanted to get out of this game. I have a modest pc spec and this game ran smoothly for me. The sounds are nice. Spatial and dynamic. All that is missing is the wind.
