\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(Disclaimer: Small play time at time of review is because Steam didn't catalog my play time correctly when I played, for some reason it only included time spent on menu. It probably took 2 or 3 hours total to read all of the paths, including skipping repeated dialogue on subsequent playthroughs)

First of all, let me say that if you got this game expecting lewd monster girls or sexual content, you have come to the wrong place. There is no nudity at all in the game and the most there is for sexual content is the indication that sex had occurred, but there is nothing graphic in the game whatsoever.

That being out of the way, Dimension of Monster Girls is more of a niche short romantic visual novel involving three girls, being a Kraken, Lamia, and Alarune. The three have their own positives and negatives that you learn about over the course of your interactions and depending on your choices that you make throughout the short game, you will end up choosing the one that best matches the choices you made.

For characters, I enjoyed all of the girls to some extent. Beatrice made me feel kind of unwanted but was also someone who was very confident in herself which is a plus, Reina does a good job of feeling kind of stalkery and creepy but is also honest and a good cook, and Iris is somewhat meek and timid but is also very warm and inviting. This is what I enjoyed about this visual novel compared to a lot of other monster girl depictions-- it isn't just the attractive parts of monster girls, it's the whole package deal. Krakens spray ink through their mouths, Lamias have large families, and Alarunes don't have reproductive organs the same as the others. It's a good way of making monster girls more believable and less human than many other depictions of them.

The world building is also surprisingly good. There are rules to this new world that you're put into and while a lot of those rules seem to be there to enforce a dream-like happy ending, it also reinforces the need to pick a desired mate by the end. It removed the possibility of being able to secure a harem, it enabled the importance of finding someone that you are compatible with, and it made sure that you knew there was no going back from your decision. I think the story was bold enough in its approach that it sought to create a world with potential and not just an excuse to show lewd monster girls.

Everything being said and done, Dimension of Monster Girls is short and the content is rather meek. There are nine CGs (three for each girl) to unlock and three routes to go through. The description says there is a bad ending too, but I hadn't found it and I have no desire to attempt to find it, either.

If you just kind of want a cutesy romantic visual novel that isn't too heavy on the heart and doesn't demand much of you, I would recommend picking up this title when it goes on sale. As the price currently is, I can't really justify it with the content that is there, but if you can get it for even just 40% or 50% off, I'd strongly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
