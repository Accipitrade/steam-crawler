\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCultures - Northland is an ancient game from 2002. It's a whopping 21 years old at the time of this review. The game lacks proper widescreen aspect ratio support (it's locked at 4:3) or support for any modern gaming display resolutions, hasn't been modernised or updated to run smoothly on modern gaming PCs. Despite this lack of modernisation, it carries a pretty high price tag for such an ancient game. This seems to have been put on Steam as a nostalgia gouge.

This is the third of the Cultures games and like the previous two, this is an RTS/city builder with a cartoony theme. Gameplay involves managing the individually named units you control, harvesting resources, building your town up, and then building an army to crush your enemies. While it does seem to share some similarities conceptually with 2006's Dwarf Fortress, it's a more casual game and more focused on the action and quick clicking that was the hallmark of the turn-of-the-century RTS leaders.

There's no options to change the resolution for the game or customise the graphics settings. There's no way for gamers to ensure this is running at the native resolution of their displays... there's no guarantee this game will look right on any PC as a result of this. There's no way for gamers to try improve the heavily dated, low quality graphics.

The game only displays in 4:3 pillarboxed aspect ratio. It's possible they developed this using an old CRT they found in a dumpster, or this game has been specifically designed for people gaming on PC's from 1995... either way, this isn't really acceptable in the modern era of PC gaming.

These technical defects push this game below acceptable standards for any modern PC game.

Cultures - Northland is relatively expensive for an abandonware nostalgia gouge, at $5 USD, and it's not worth it. The game is just too old, hasn't been modernised. And as the old saying goes, you can't go home again.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game continues in the same theme as Cultures 2: Gates of Asgard, which is sadly not on Steam. I managed to find my old CD for C2:GoA and started up Cultures-Northland immediately upon beating it.
The mechanics and controls are identical, but the game immediately throws you into combat and against the strongest enemies from the previous game. I bought it I believe on sale bundled with 8th Wonder and am very satisfied with my purchase. My brother had bought it years ago and recommended that I buy and play it when I get the chance.
I'm not the best at content reviews but the graphics are gorgeous, even for something nearly two decades old. It's sandbox in that your village and characters are super customizable but certain quests do require specific buildings, materials and/or character actions.
Unlike the previous game, this one had three difficulty modes with Normal as the default. You start off with two heroes this time and gain a third shortly into the campaign. I have yet to check out the Free Play or the "Editor" but if they're even half as good as the campaign I'll be extremely pleased.
If I could give stars instead of just a thumbsup, it'd be 5 stars, a thrilled 10/10. If you see it on sale, buy it! You won't be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
