\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhost Recon Desert Siege is the first expansion pack for Ghost Recon, developed by same Red Storm and released in 2002, one year after original. Yep, still same tactical shooter. It added new single-player campaign, new weapons and expanded multiplayer.

Well, since multiplayer is kinda dead, you probably not going to care about those two new modes. However, you can play 5 multiplayer maps in custom mission if you choose Firefight or Recon mods.
Also I didn't notice new weapons personally. I mean, how different they can be from whatever realistical weapons Ghost Recon already had.

But it does add a new single-player campaign made up of 8 missions. And this time things are going south, from cold Russia into whatever hot African country, where you will be annoying everybody off by the border. Yep, it's still same Ghost Recon gameplay in different enviroment. Still with frustating AI, with enemies now even more happy to throw grenades that teammates AI doesn't bother to avoid, feeling sometimes unfair. Still quickscamming.
It doesn't add new gameplay elements, the only new thing are enemy jeeps, which have driver and machine gunner. They can screw you up and are nice addition. But it's puzzling that Demolition's anti-armor weapon is useless against jeeps. I mean, direct hit may not even harm anybody in the jeep. It should blow up!

But the levels themselves felt much better designed around Ghost Recon gameplay. They are now much more wide open, consisting of huge spaces. And I feel it makes it more pleasent to deal with teammate AI, as they are much better at providing support and cover over such distances. While you place them around to cover each other and move on slowly, taking baddies out from a far. Unless you once again have to enter building with only one path where you get one-shot instantly. Missions also didn't try to be gimmicky, the only armor escort mission has a very slow-moving convoy.

And well, this expansion is good. At least if you liked the original. Overall I think I had more positive experience with Desert Siege's 8 missions than with original's 15 levels.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhost Recon: Desert Siege expands the world of Ghost Recon from Eastern Europe to the deserts of Africa. Small tweaks to the game make this a worthy addition to the original Ghost Recon. In comparison to the original game, the maps are also larger fitting into the desert theme. The long distance engagements can actually be quite interesting because more often than not, you are spotted in the wide open and there is little or no cover in some areas so approaching the situation is quite important.

This is definitely a worthy addition to the original Ghost Recon game. Anybody who is interested in highly tactical games and already has the core game would adore this expansion. And if you don’t have the original, get on that because you’re definitely missing out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDesert Siege has an uninteresting plot behind a short campaign on boringly similar wide open and desolate maps. There is only one convoy escort mission, and there aren't many engagements with armored units to be had. There are fewer specialists and weapons available.
