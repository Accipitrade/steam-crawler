\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat Concept, But Not Great Execution

Pros:

I found this game very relevant given the recent activity with stocks like Gamestop and AMC. People are really hyped up on stocks but many don't even know what shorting a stock means. This game can provide you a basic education on stocks and some insight on what it is like to be a stock trader which is really pretty cool. You will learn how the world is interconnected such as when lumber prices go up housing prices tend to follow suit or how a war in a country can affect the prices of certain goods. This game also hints that there is some manipulation in the trading world and quite frankly I absolutely 100% agree with that.

Cons:

This game was very short, didn't quite go far enough and parts of it were extremely sloppy. I think it would have really helped if there were maybe some company statistics like long term debt, beta, or other financials that would impact the game. The graphics were mediocre and I really thought the people could use actual voices. I really hated the events that took place like the "hack" where essentially you couldn't play the game for 2 days and just had to drink coffee. Honestly most of the events detracted from the game. Another issue was when you purchased stuff for your houses it would have been nice to see them, I mean how hard is it to program a slightly bigger environment.

Overall: 6/10 - Not Terrible, but could be greatly improved\t\t\t\t\t
