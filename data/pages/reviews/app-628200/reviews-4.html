\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the concept of the game. It gives people a simplified idea of how the stock market works. The graphics are horrible. It takes an unusually long time to load the game when you hit play for such a simple game with bad graphics and a tiny world.

There is no end to the game, but I got it to lag on the eval screen when I made $6T in 1 day (level 11 play). Not horrible lag, but it was there and I can imagine it will get worse if I continued. Once you get to level 3, it takes 2 days to get a new level and all you have to do is short 1 stock to max (adding 1M to your existing 1M), take the 2M and invest it whatever is your highest dividend. The dividend percentage holds over each new progression level and my highest divided was over 500,000%...yes, that is a comma in the middle. It's a ridiculously simple game without the feel of a real stock market. I was turning $2M into $1B in 1 game day.

I believe I bought it for around $5, but I would not recommend anyone buy it for more than .25 cents. I have 10 hours of gameplay and there is no replayability factor. The only reason I have that many hours is due to trying to get a couple more of achievements.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCool game but:

As someone who is in institutional finance, this isn't how modern trading is done at all; Lots of QoL missing. Instead this is very much a simulator for "unsophisticated retail" finance, where you trade based off of gut instinct. This feels more like a simulator for guys who trade from their phone on Robinhood who are on FinTwit.

You can't employ even simple well-known finance trading strategies like mean-reversion or momentum since the longest lookback period is simply 1d. Instead you are simply trading based off of "social media" posts and news information. No institution with a few billion dollars of market cap, like Citi, Blackrock, etc. works like this - only small firms with a few hundred million might (and these are not the firms who comprise "the invisible hand").

The absence of other instruments like derivatives, hedging, etc. but the explicit inclusion of dividends is also strange, considering the market price movement mechanics seem pretty developed - buying stock drives the price up, and selling stocks drives the price down, and they seemingly obey a random walk with some level of covariance where it's logical to have. Derivative pricing would be simple to implement with what is here.

The tools for learning how to implement information arbitrage is here, but slightly lacking. The niche for this game is for people who are interested in market dynamics, but I frankly think this needs more options (no pun intended) for me to recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
