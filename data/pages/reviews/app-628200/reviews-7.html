\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun "Wall Street" kind of game with a dash of "Ruthless.com".

You start the game as an analyst, move up the ranks as you make money against competitors, to your help you have an insider channel telling you what to expect on that day, as well as being able to buy tips. You can also manipulate currency and commodities to boost/sink their value.

You make money by either investing or shorting, there are multiple factors that happen that you get in form of a trade tracker, one factor can be "Coffee shortage" and if that also is in the same region that has an economic boom, then that are two factors that will boost coffee companies. Your insider tip can also be nullified by negative factors so keep an eye on the tracker during the day.

The game story line is somewhat interesting, i wouldn't say it's necessary to play it, but it adds a bit of atmosphere. Every day you arrive at work, you get a bit of conversational pie from the receptionist (or the company solitaire expert, or whatever he is) as well as a boss. If you do something stupid, like ruin a financial sector, you get a mouthful at the end of the day from the boss that hired you in the first place. You also get emails and messages and in the story line there is also some kind of Mr Robot reference popping in.

No bugs, but some annoyances, like once the story line is complete the receptionist keeps yammering on about events you already done, until some line says that "it feels like our story is over", after that he keeps quiet. But there are also emails that repeat, in my case it was "How badly we got hacked" that kept showing up every day before 9 am. Nothing game breaking but annoying.

Is it worth it? Yes, if you like stock market sim games and making money like a wall street sociopath. I just wish the game was a bit more developed and had a few more game play mechanics, it feels unfinished, like a raw diamond that needs a bit more polishing.\t\t\t\t\t
