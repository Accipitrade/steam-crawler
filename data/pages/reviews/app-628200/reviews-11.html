\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow to start describing this game...? I might as well start with the setup for the first day: You're given a quick "ethics test" at the start of the game, with the correct answers already marked. While you do the test, your boss remarks on each question on the test, setting up one fact in particular: None of those rules will apply to you.

Once you start your first day proper, you end up in a situation where the market is on a downward trend, your options for making a profit are limited, and you're trapped in a contest with an expert to decide who gets to stay with the firm. On the other hand, you have insider information telling you that if you wait for just the right time, Coffee's going to have a boom. The tutorial's already forced you to buy coffee anyways, why not just go with what it says and let it all work out...?

As the intro of the game establishes, while this is technically a game about buying and selling stocks, the emphasis on the game is more on market manipulation than truly understanding the market.

Your primary tools to understand the market come in the forms of two things, which I'll class as "Unreliable Information" and "Reliable Information". Unreliable Information primarily comes from short messages from various analysis groups of dubious quality, who may or may not affect the market for a limited period of time. You can guess their accuracy by how many "likes" they've gotten, but by the time they've been verified as correct, you've also missed your best chance to earn a profit, so part of the game is trying to figure out when to try to go with the flow to make a profit and when to completely ignore the tips altogether. Reliable Information on the other hand, comes in two forms: "Insider Information" which generally gives an indication of a massive change in things at a preset time which can be leveraged for profits if you're prepared, and "News Articles" that indicate 3 trends that will persist throughout the day. You only get one of the two each day, with Insider Information mostly being plot-related. My own personal experience of course, is that since Insider Information tends to invoke a quick profit at a single point in the day while the news articles give us trends that continue throughout, you'll generally make more profit with news articles than insider information.

But rather than dealing with all of that, what about if you can just... Change the market however you like? And this is where most of the gameplay is focused: Early on in the game, you're given the ability to make use of Lobbyists, which you can use to drive the stocks up and down to achieve your goals. You can only have up to 16 lobbyists working at a time, and they cost money to hire, but they allow to direct the market as you will, barring the fact that if you do it wrong, it can have disastrous effects on the market, and get people very upset with you personally.

Every product on the market has a limit to how high or low it can go: Once it reaches that point it triggers a rebound, which increases people's distrust in you, and eventually results in fines. To make matters worse, some products on the market use other products on the market as materials, so the prices of products further down on the supply chain can have effects on everything higher up on the chain. The game is thus about deciding about how to manipulate the markets: Are you going to play it safe, or take the risk of collapsing the market to help push your plans along?

Either way, I found the main plot enjoyable, though the main story did end surprisingly quickly; While apparently the game can last until Day 37, I made it to the "ending" by Day 16 on my first run. The better you are at making money, the faster the plot goes by.\t\t\t\t\t
