\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is dull and has no content. Everything the game has to offer gets shown to you within the first hour or so.
The gameplay consists of you looking at the games version of tweets and then buying or shorting stock accordingly.
There is no tactics involved with it at all. Tweet say stock go up? Buy stock, then sell it a couple ingame hours later.
Tweet say stock go down? Short it and buy back the position a few ingame hours later.

You can influence the price of certain goods / materials via lobbying but the mechanic essentially is just you spending cash to make the price of something go up or down. None of your actions have any lasting consequences. Within an ingame day everything is back to normal.

You can't destabilize another ocuntries economy and there are no dynamic events such as forest firest or similar that will impact you in the long term.
At the end of the day you get paid private money that you can use to buy houses that will generate you additional private money whose sole purpose is to... buy more houses.

You can't upgrade your workstation, you can't get your own office. Nothing.

The UI is atrocious. It takes up way too much space. It's a shame since the game gives you 4 monitors. Two of which are useless, the other one you only look at once per day, if at all.
The devs easily could have implemented a system that'd let you buy monitors, view multiple monitors at the same time and put the information you want freely on the monitor to let you customize your workspace but no. None of that is in the game.

After the day ends you can spend cash to increase the friendship level with your co-workers. As far as I can tell, this does nothing. All it does is be a cash sink.

The last goal of becoming managing director is literally not achievable. The goalpost gets moved further and further back whenever you are about to achieve it.
The game isn't even giving you the satisfaction of owning everything. No, when you reach a certain amount of stocks owned the game resets your progress, forcing you to do the dull grind all over again. Your reward are upgrades such as "25% more dividends" or "50% increased trade volume" - The trade volume one feels insulting because really the default trade volume should be much higher than it is. It's just more padding for a game that consists of only padding.

On top of that, the game doesn't follow its own rules. It introduces "Danger Zones" but then prices don't rebound or crash when you purposefully navigate the prices inside of them, the danger zone just "vanishes" and nothing happens.
Sometimes you lose public order despite not even using lobbyists. In my last playthrough I got game overed because somehow the game thought I lowered public order, when in truth, my public order went UP because I DIDN'T USE ANY LOBBYISTS.

So either the game is buggy or it doesn't follow the rules and boundaries it sets. It's frustrating.

This game is one wasted opportunity. It would have been nice if you could have worked your way up into owning the company, paying off politicians, influencing politics and the world at large. Instead what you get is a mindless clicker game that has you achieving absolutely nothing.
