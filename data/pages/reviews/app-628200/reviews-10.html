\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInteresting concept, but flawed execution. The writing should have been done by real writers, as its about as subtle as a sledgehammer driving a tack through a wall, but I'm glad it's there. This is the only stock sim game that is wrapped around any kind of narrative, to my knowledge (aside from Wall Street Kid on OG Nintendo). The game is very, very light on realism, as all you can do is buy at current price and short at current price. The markets are quite reliably predictable, as well. If you want something like this but that feels somewhat more like a sim and is more difficult and complex, check out 'wall street raider' or 'speculator' by roninsoft.

There's some ludo-narrative dissonance here. Even without 'cheating', (which the game 'suggests' to you constantly, gotta love that subtle writing), the core gameplay loop is ridiculously easy. I won't describe it here, but the trade feed is all you need to pay attention to. The problem with this is that even if you try to be a good trader and not intentionally manipulate anything, you will still be treated as if you are Bernie Madoff. Nobody likes illusion of choice. Everyone wants to feel as if their choices matter in a game. I get why they do this though, it's to hammer home their message that the financial industry is cartoonishly evil.. These kinds of messages are far more effective when better written though. I would have enjoyed this game much more from a role playing standpoint if it was nearly impossible to make money the traditional way, but cheating allowed you to win big bucks. I also got wrapped up in an endless mode without realizing it for a while, which was annoying, but they fixed that today according to patch notes.

The most annoying part though is a bug that will sometimes sell and buy your stocks in ridiculously small increments, per 'tick'. Other times, the full amount will post in one tick, as you would expect. This is more apparent later in the game, during the 'vice president' sections (math errors using larger numbers I guess?). If I have 900m, sometimes the game will sell or buy only a few million per 'tick'. This means you're completely helpless if an event occurs that tanks the stock, as you're not able to sell off quickly enough to not lose a fortune. Still, this isn't much of a problem since it's so easy to make money.

In short, I love the concept of a stock trading game wrapped up in a narrative, and it's a topical game given the whole GME/dogecoin etc... thing, but the writing leaves much to be desired. Still, it was worth the money for the novelty of it.\t\t\t\t\t
