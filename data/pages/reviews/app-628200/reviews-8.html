I have lost everything, and I'm not sure how to continue. This summer I invested $17,500 (six months salary and my entire life savings) into ornamental gourd futures, hoping to capitalize on this lucrative emerging industry. After watching a video about Vincent Kosuga and his monopoly on onions, I decided I'd try to do something similar with another vegetable. I did some research and found out many agricultural forecasters expected this year's gourd yield would be far smaller than the past, due to deteriorating soil conditions in central Mexico and a warmer-than-average spring. At first, demand soared around Halloween and prices skyrocketed, but the gourd bubble burst on November 12th. Unfortunately, the coronavirus caused a massive drop-off in demand due to fewer families decorating their tables for thanksgiving, and prices plummeted. I had invested early enough that I thought I would still be fine, but then on the morning of December 2nd, a new email in my inbox caused my stomach to turn into a pretzel. The massive gourd shipment from Argentina, scheduled for early March, had arrived. I was planning on selling off my futures right before this, in February, but this ruined everything. To top it off, the gourds in this shipment were absolutely gargantuan, some topping 4 pounds each, causing the price-per-pound to drop like an anchor into the range of 6 cents per pound. I am ruined.\t\t\t\t\t

There are 2 modes, normal mode and story mode. Normal mode should be named story mode and story mode is like a casual mode, the naming doesn't really make sense to me. Even in the casual "story mode" the game is not endless or still does end if you abuse your power too much (use too many lobbyists in a day etc.), which sort of bothers me.

I wish this game had a sandbox/freeplay mode where you could tinker around with controls or limits yourself and see what settings are most enjoyable to play for yourself, like setting a custom amount of lobbyists, starting capital, enable/disable public opinion, etc. The developer claims after you finish the game "endless mode" starts but there are still too many limitations to what you can do, a fully separate sandbox mode would be a great addition to the game.

Some of the controls aren't very intuitive, the 2 tracker monitors above your workstation only work with up and down clicks, why wouldn't they use like a scroll wheel or drop-down menu here? When you start the game, even if you played before and you want to start a new play-through, there is as far as I know no way to skip the tedious 5 minute long stupid intro before you can select your game mode and actually start the game, would be nice to have an option to skip all that when you want to start playing immediately in stead of watching an unskippable cutscene for the 4th time. Also stuff like coffee/tea takes way to long to brew for no reason (yes, it's too slow even after drinking the 3x coffee boost, there's no reason for it to be so slow), there's just several annoying things like that.

For this game being the first release of the developer Power Struggle Games I have high hopes for their future games and hope they don't leave this game as-is. I do hope the devs release some QoL updates because otherwise this game has 0 replay-ability after you finish it with every possible ending.

- - -
Review update November 2021:
Last game update/patch was 6 months ago and I don't see the devs adding more features or changing things that would provide QoL content for the game. I don't blame them, it's basically a finished game but I was just hoping for something new, maybe even a DLC with new features. Nevertheless, it's still a fun game and worth playing.\t\t\t\t\t
