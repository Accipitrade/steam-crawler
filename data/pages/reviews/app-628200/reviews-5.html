\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImagine Tyler Durden created a game about the stock market.

Not the way the stock market actually works or real issues - you won't find anything like "Liar's Poker" or "The Wolf of Wall Street" here.
Instead you get a game that beats you over the head that the only ways to win are either to cheat the system or not play at all. Insider trading, magically teleport lobbyists, and more. By the end you'll have less idea of how the stock markets (and the game's mechanics) work than when you started.
Mostly, this makes me wish it was a good, realistic, interesting game about being a day trader, or a stock broker, or even a member of the US Congress (who are legally permitted to engage in insider trading.)

But, for all its faults, it was reasonably enjoyable to play, and I'll probably play it more in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
