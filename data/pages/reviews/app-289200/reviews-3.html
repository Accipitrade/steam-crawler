\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI must say this about 1C games--they really scratch my strategy itch.

Ascension to the Throne is very much in the vein of Heroes of Might and Magic, except you only have one hero and the entire game is played from a 3rd-person over-the-shoulder point of view. Like HoMM, you go around to different locations, fighting different groups of monsters, collecting treasure and artifacts, and recruiting various units into your army.

Your main hero can level up with experience, and levels come pretty quickly. I just finished the game at level 52. You also have four different equipment slots--one for armor/weapon (yes, they are all considered a single slot), one amulet, and two rings. There are quite a few different pieces that can be equipped in each slot.

Money is fairly plentiful in this game, so don't worry too much about saving up.

The big difference between AttT and HoMM is that your main hero has an 'Authority' stat, which governs how many units he can command in his army. At many different points in the game, you will be given the choice between increasing a hero stat or increasing Authority. I chose to bump up my authority every chance I had, and I did not regret it. By the time I hit the last battle, I lost less than half of my army before I defeated the final boss and his minions.

Is this game as good as something like HoMM 3? No. But I bought this game on sale for $1, and I spent a good 20 hours playing through it. Totally worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good:

- Game is cheap
- Game has good tactical depth, especially in the beginning
- Battles are fast
- Story is decent
- Models are not bad even if female models are a bit 'Red Sonya in bikini chainmail' - esque
- Some quests are fun

The not so good

- Character customisation is non excistant
- Avaideble equipment options are extremely limited (You can basicly only pick two rings as armors aren't really a choice)
- Stats are limited and perks non avaideble
- Camera turns with the speed of the death star aligning for a shot with rebel fighters attacking.
- Hero running animation has been borrowed from the lovechild between the Ultimate warrior and captain Jack Sparrow.
- Some quests make no sense.
- Hero tries to act like a tough warrior but utterly fails at being convincing to me.

In short, don't expect anything special but if you have a couple of hours of fun with the game it's well worth the price of 3 bucks.\t\t\t\t\t
