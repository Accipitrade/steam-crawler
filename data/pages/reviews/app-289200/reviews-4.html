\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLow budget production, but a fun romp that lasted about 20 hours. Hard to complain for the price. The game progresses at a rapid clip, so you don't get bored or bogged down. The next major event is always just a few minutes away.

There are a couple points in the game where it feels like there should have been a teleporter and there wasn't. For example, getting to Steelburg required a couple long treks back to reenforce my army before getting in. That was probably the only point in the game where the pacing seemed slow.

If you just want to play through the game with relative ease, do yourself a favor and go full mage. Trying to play as a warrior drastically increases the difficulty and tediousness. Don't bother putting a single point into attack power. Just boost HP and Magic power until they're at least 300, then start dropping some points in magic resistance too. ALWAYS CHOOSE AUTHORITY when given a stat boost options. Nothing else really matters. Intelligence doesn't even really seem to matter because I never had a fight last more than two rounds so running out never appeared to be a possibility.

Speed wins. Go first, kill first, don't get hit.

Occassionally, I would loose a battle because I didn't have enough speed/damage to kill a very fast enemy immediately, and it would run up and kill the hero, ending the game. In each case, I just hadn't re-balanced my army in a while and had to go back and make sure I had as many of the fastest/hardest hitting troops available top-front to win.

Speaking of that, the one thing that was a little confusing at first is army placement. The left column is the "back", the right column is the "front". In each column, the action moves top to bottom. Unit Speed-->Placement is the order of initiative. So if you have a Speed 15 unit A in top-right, a Speed 12 unit B in bottom right, and a 15 speed unit C in the bottom-left, they will act in A-->C-->B order.

This is basically a "poor man's King's Bounty", but I found the pacing to be much faster which I enjoyed. I wouldn't have finished this game if it were much longer, but it was an enojoyable quick playthrough. Definitely got more than my $3 worth.\t\t\t\t\t

- No widescreen resolution settings, and the highest setting causes the game to never run again until you find the config file to delete and start over.
- Manually editing the config file with a hex editor (yeah, not even plain text like the rest of the world...) can get you up to 1920x1080, but the graphics remain stretched for some reason. Trying to fix that causes the game to not launch, apparently the game can only do certain resolutions (and again not even the ones included) and regardless can only do a certain aspect ratio which makes no sense.
- Your character runs like he has one broken arm, and the camera wobbles as you do. And as you move the camera view there is a big animated 1920's film camera icon moving around. Distracting to say the least, just poor design.
- Night lasts about 3 minutes, day lasts for about an hour
- The sky texture moves at a distracting pace
- View control gets stuck randomly
- oh, and setting the resolution manually in the config makes the main menu not line up with the clicks anymore
Ok on to content/ experience
- Some conversations auto start when you get in range, awkwardly so
- The writing is terrible
- There is no tutorial of any sort
- There is no map marker for most quests
- Sound in cutscense does not honor your volume settings so it's real loud
- Conversations will auto advance randomly before you're done reading
- Conversations will fail to advance when clicking and you just have to wait a while
- The merchant interface is the worst ever, because it's by one way dialog options
- Open looking world is anything but, invisible walls everwhere
- UI lacks navigation elements, again one way menus

And after all that, the game gets you dead in no time flat. You're given almost zero guidance, discover your journal gets updates with no notice, says you should find a blacksmith to get a weapon but there isn't one anywhere in the "village", your only lead is to help an orc attack the castle, which winds you up dead in one round because 3 guards come over and slay your naked unarmed clueless self. I was also not impressed by the essentially (gratuitously) naked warrior woman on the road right at the start who won't help you until you raise an army which again, no apparent way to do. Heck the one guy in the village that has a dialog option for work has none for you, so no way to get more money to hire enough pitchfork peasants to assault the castle, or any of the other attack areas around you.

Glad I only paid a buck for this disaster, still feel cheated, waste of an hour or so.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere is a bland review,

This game is not pretty, the dialogue is terrible (thanks to bad translation at some parts), and has some bugs that can ruin your experience. The gameplay is simple, like that of King's Bounty. The story is cliche. The combat is can simply be broken down to four things: magic is overpowered, ranged attacks are just as good, melee sucks (as well as the heroes you can recruit, including the main character), and whoever goes first (has the highest speed) will win most of the time.

Even though I just bashed the game hard with the above paragraph, I still enjoyed the game. Therefore I recommend it to those who like turn-based games. In fact, the first paragraph is just a warning to those who play the game on what to expect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot bad, not bad at all.

Graphics are a bit dated but, enjoyable game. Took around 26 hours to complete it. Some folks (myself included) have difficulty launching it on newer systems. Simply going into the game files and deleting the graphic.cfg file fixes that. That said, you will not want to try and adjust settings, it'll freeze the game on you. You'll be stuck with default. You'll get used to it.

A lot of things are based on character level. Each level gives skill points to use to improve your character. Early on, and most fights, your character is largely a 'commander'/'placeholder' for your army while your troops do much of the fighting.

Early levels; each skill point for leveling is worth 2 points for the attribute you assign them to...then 5, then higher as you go on. If you hold off spending the skill points until later on, they will spend at the higher level. Which means a lot of 'run-away' for the commander

Same with looting various treasure chests and, using various shrines...it's based on character level...so those can be gamed a bit, as well. For chests, I chose to take authority kicks every time, to increase the command points for better troops/more troops. Made plenty of coin just from the fights, to buy everything I needed. Think I had 250k coin at the end, even after buying all the rings, and such, that I wanted.

The fights can be annoying at higher levels when trying to cover key characters. Save, fight, see how it goes...reload if key character gets killed, adjust character location within army, try again.

Definitely worth the time to try, and play.

(ETA) The game did crash 3 times on me...each required a CAD out and end process; just FYI\t\t\t\t\t
