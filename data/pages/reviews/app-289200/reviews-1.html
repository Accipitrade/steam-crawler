\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh, this game brings back so many good memories...
... Renovated house, my first personal cabinet, new PC and much more. I already had a bunch of new games, which my older PC could not run at that time. But Ascension to the Throne was the first game I installed on my brand new (at that time) hardware.

Rather humble project, definitely without big budget, but it didn't look cheap. Absolutely not. It had really good visuals, very pleasant BGMs and attention to details. For example, back in the days, I never payed much attention to that fact, that castles and villages of different lords belong to different architectural ensemble. Cool detail. Not impressed, you say? How about small indie studio EA, who can't afford to draw 2 different transport planes for BF5 nowadays, uh?

When it comes to gameplay, for a rather long time AttT was the only representative of it's kind. I mean, a combination of 3rd person view for world exploration/interaction and turn-based combat like in HoMM or earlier Kings bounty games. If you played recent Kings Bounty 2, you know what I'm talking about. But while for KB2 such gameplay model looked weird and clunky - here it plays perfectly.

There is no hardcore gameplay mechanics, no complex story, no deep role-play aspects. Instead, you have simple, I'd even say meditative gameplay, with a simple story, typical for B class fantay movies of late 90th - early 00th, where brutal barbarians with huge axes and half-naked amazons with big assets, fight against demons, necromancers and other spawns of darkness. You deal with local badies, get money and respect authority, communicate with local NPCs, recruit more/better troops, get better armor and skills, beat even bigger badies, get even more money and authority... initially it could be very hard from time to time. But from midgame and later on - very easy.

I played it back then few times and had tons of fun. Out of curiosity decided to check it again not so long ago a-a-and - it's still fun. Sure, visuals are not as impressive anymore and some gameplay mechnics are clearly outdated but it was fun nevertheless. Such a shame that it's add-on Valkyrie is not available on Steam.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game walked so that the Katauri King's Bounty games could run. I'm sort of surprised that, by the time King's Bounty: The Legend came out in 2008, it had already been over a year since this game was released!

There are obvious problems with Ascension to the Throne, but most of them are skin deep. Invisible walls abound in the game, preventing you from progressing beyond the bounds of the game's environments. NPCs continue to have exclamation marks above their heads, even after you've finished their quests. Walking speed is a bit slower than I'd like. Ascension to the Throne is obviously unpolished, but whatever - good core gameplay is the meat and bones of any halfway decent game, anyway.

On that front, I'm happy to say that Ascension to the Throne delivers. Gameplay is divided into two segments: free-form exploration, and battles. During exploration, you'll listen to some real nice medieval fantasy music, find chests, altars, and troops willing to join your cause. Sure, the setting is basically Generic Fantasy World #7214, but it works well enough and gets the job done.

The influence from the original 1990 King's Bounty is obvious, although instead of being a pseudo-roguelite with randomized troop/item locations, this game is linear and plays out the same way, every single playthrough.

Combat all takes place on wide-open, huge hex-based battlefields. You get to control an army of units - whom you recruit during the exploration phase - and do battle by managing your army. Notably, your main character joins the fray, and if he dies, it's game over. For this reason, I recommend pumping up his health. It's the most important stat, really. Combat isn't the deepest affair, but it's complex enough. Many troop types exist. You can also cast magic spells to vary things up.

If there's one big flaw with the core combat system, it's that ranged units are too powerful. Specifically: furies. Once you have access to them, there's no real reason to recruit any other troop. I feel the game could've done well to improve its other troops, provide us with more options, and give us different difficulty settings. In this regard, the Katauri King's Bounty games definitely had a leg-up and surpassed Ascension to the Throne; those games are vastly more replayable, with vastly more content, too.

All in all, not a perfect game, but a very enjoyable one, nonetheless. For $3, it's a steal. I really wish they'd port the sequel, Ascension to the Throne: Valkyrie, to Steam, because I wanna play that one now, too.\t\t\t\t\t
