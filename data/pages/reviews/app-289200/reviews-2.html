I owned this game on disk long before it come to steam so my hours are no indication of my playtime.

Is this game the best thing since sliced bread? No way in hell.
Is this game AAA quality? Nope
Is this game bug/crash free? No
Is this game fun to play? Yep (once you understand how to play).
Is this game worth the asking price? Definitely!!!

For the price of less than a cup of coffee, and when on sale less than $1 this game is worth the price for anyone who has even the remotest interest in Turn Based Tactics games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen first played in 2007, I couldn't believe these detailed creatures, I re-played this a lot, but only years later I noticed the lower detail of the world. This game looks great in many things and unfinished in others:

Great:
-Units detail: Excellent design even for current standards, from the low-mid level units as the Combat Instructor, Shooter or Amazon, to the end game ones as the Fury, Myrmilon or Dragon Slayer. All of them wonderfully detailed for that time, and even for current standards in strategy.
-Tactical combat: The turn based strategy system of Heroes of Might and Magic, but instead 1 representative unit with the number of the quantity, all of them displayed in squadrons of maximum 10 units.
-Music: You really feel the Mid-Northern European mythology in it. Still playing these soundtracks when I write my novel, or make a mod, or another computer task.
-The hero in the army: This is always great in a strategy game.

Moderate:
The plot: This is personal, I know the plot is moderate, but I felt a nice story about friendship. I mean, many 'unfinished' games has ridiculous plots, but this one is pretty acceptable.
Linear: You have certain freedom to do random side quests, but main quest is linear.
No respawn: This is personal, many people love that when they kill something, stay killed, but many other (in which I am), when like a game, want to still enjoying it a bit more, and with no respawn, the only way is to repeat the main story.

Unfinished:
-The world: I noticed this years later, cause the previous points was so well, that I barely payed attention to the world, but yes, it seems unfinished, angled in certain parts, low on details, few assets, simple physics, etc.
-Simple RPG system: Few stats to grow at level up, only your hero gets upgrades, no upgrades for units (you just replace cheap ones with more expensive others), combat has no random damage or misses, the only random variation are critical strikes.
-Dialogues: Basic and meaningless.
-Interface: Very basic, just few icons, at even the reward choice is by dialogue, instead the icon of the items.
-One class. You play with Alexander, the Fighter. You find altars to grow also 2 more, the shooter and the mage. You actually find those classes as companions, which cant benefit with the altars. Seems planned to have 3 playable classes, but unfinished with only 1. (As a side note, there is the stand alone Valkyrie expansion, in which you play the story of Eneya, the shooter girl).

Failures, bugs:
-The chests system: The reward grows with your level. So, you dont want to open a single chest until you will leave the area, returning to pick them all at the maximum possible level before leave.
-There is a way to trigger a bug to repeat forever a high level combat, by choosing always the same dialogue option, you can grow 10 levels easily there, but it's not unbalancing at the end of the game, when you already are very strong.

Dont buy it if you want to a complex combat system, or to have a polished game in the Total War style. But if you like turn based strategy, and story driven RPGs, you probably will be amazed by its simplicity and the love shown in each unit.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI completed the main story, every side quest and found all the hidden treasure chests. But I wish I hadn't come across this game.

I admit that battles are well-made and nice to play. I like the idea that every unit in your army has its own referent on the battlefield. Combats are short, and thus you aren't bored of them. However, at some point they become even too short. Although the game initially doesn't seem to be a piece of cake, you need to consider your every move and try to reduce casualties, after couple of hours it isn't challenge anymore and most of the battles end before your enemies has their own turn.

Character development works the same way. When you level up you receive not only authority which lets you hire more and better untis to your army but also some points you can spend on one of the four attributes. The problem is that, the deeper into the forest, on what you spend them becomes meaningless as your hero is killing machine eliminating multiple enemies at once.

Ascension to the Throne is also extremely linear. Even if we stumble upon an army of definitely hostile creatures, we can't attack them until we receive a quest to do so. Also, travelling across the world is limited by the story development and usually not for the logical reasons.
I'm surprised that, at the same time, there're few side quest we can do in two little different ways and the developers actucally give us choice what to do next. It's a shame that thay didn't design the enitre game like that.

The game has been relased in 2007 but it doesn't look like the game from that time by no means. The grapfic is awful and even if you're the one who doesn't care, then you won't ignore how developers set proportions. The buildings and other elements of environment are unnaturaly big compared to the people. The effect is ridiculous.

Music is decent and songs suit situations during which they're played. On the other side, for the most of the game I thought that there're just few sound effecs in the combat in general, but it turned out that some sounds are already hearbale when I set the volume to 30%, but some of them only when it is set to 100%, and then the formers are obviously just too loud. Shouldn't they be better balanced?
Lack of dubbing is also noticeable and I'm sure its presence would add atmosphere.

The game isn't devoid of several minor issues as well. For example, the mistake in English translation was successfuly preventing me from completing one of the side quests. If I hadn't searched for the solution on the Interent, I probably wouldn't have found what's wrong.

I know that the price is really attractive, but believe me - your time is worth more. We don't have enough time to beat all of the video games, so we should pick only the good ones. Ascension to the Throne isn't one of them.\t\t\t\t\t
