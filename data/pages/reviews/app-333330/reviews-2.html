\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game. It was made very well and it is clear that a lot of effort was put forward. I would recommend this game to anyone. For $5 it is very worth it.

I do have some things I would like the developer to know.

1. You level up to slow. Leveling up takes forever and there is too little of a reward when you do level up. I think it should be that you level up slow but when you do level up it gives you a lot of upgrade points or you level up slow but every time you level up you only get, say, 1 point.

2. Quests are to unclear. I found myself many times walking around blindly because I had no clue what to do. I think quests need to be more clear on where to go.

3. Bigger map with many cities/villages. Right now there is only one village (That I know of) and it is boring to only have 1 town. There should be many towns with many quests and lots of people.

If you are reading this and you are thinking of buying the game, buy it. Even though I have stated what I don't like, it is still a good game! If you are the developer, please take these additions into consideration! Thank you for reading this!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom the time Ive had with the game I believe that overall its a preety good game for 5$. Its a questline based story with multiple side quests along the way. The quests arent that detailed though. So the story isnt that rich but it makes up for it with the humor of the quests. And you do get a sense of accomplishment from doing the quests from the gear that you get along the way with some lvls every here and there.

One of the problemes that somebody would have with it is the quests are not very descriptive. Most quests tell you just to do something and not how to do it. For instance one of the very first quests you get is to find who killed your beloved rabbit wife. Thats all it tells you, is to do just that. Youll have to figure out what to do from there. That alone left me wondering around for about 30 minutes untill I learned I had to go to the village, talk to this guy in the bar, and he will tell you to go to this dungeon and you will have to clear the dungeon. However he doesnt tell you where the dungeon is.. So if your expected to be guided through the story dont. With the amount of time it takes to figure things out like that it adds alot of gametime.

Overall I still enjoyed playing this game. I do come back to it every now and then to see if I can figure out that quest I was stuck on. And if your thinking you might like it and are willing to spend an extra 5$ then go for it. Your welcome!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a zelda-esque RPG with simple but charming pixel graphics. It doesnt look like much at first glance, but its really fun. If you are a fan of pixel art or top-down realtime RPGs, take a look at this one. Overall, its totally worth the completely reasonable asking price, even with a few minor flaws.

In depth
+++++++++++++
You move around with the WASD on a top down screen and aim you guy's facing with the mouse pointer, so you can run backwards while attacking or in circles or what have you. Its actually quite entertaining to run circles around a mob dodging their weapons while you hit them repeatedly. It is controlled simply. RMB is any ranged weapon, LMB is use (the cursor changes to a situational icon when you are pointing at something you can interact with) and melee. A few other keys round out the commands (questlist, char sheet, quickbar, inventory, menu), and all are mappable.

All weapons have a slight cooldown and even melee weapons have a range difference based on type (ie a sword has more range than a dagger) identified by how far away your "attack" animation travels. There is a simple char screen any RPG'er will pick up immediately, where you can add stat points, learn talents and generally make your guy better. It has a niceinventory and item system, and there is even a crafting menu. The enemies respawn, so farming and grinding is an option if you wanted to, but not strictly needed. There is also a lot of tongue in cheek humor, but it never feels like it beating you over the head with "trying too hard to be funny"

It does have a few flaws, some people may consider them major, some people may not care at all. One of the ones I consider major is the M+K only configuration. For people like me with physical disabilities that make it so using WASD is difficult, JoytoKey is a must. But even then JoytoKey never works as good as native controller. Another one is that the game is obviously written by someone without complete fluency in English. Grammar mistakes and odd phrasing abound in nearly all dialogue. It isn't as badly written as many games I've seen, but its definately noticeable. There are still many jokes that are greatly amusing, and some of the stilted odd dialogue adds a certain charm.
If you are fluent enough to play it in French, I suggest you do that.

Another thing I noticed is that walking into the bed at the inn is how you regain health and save. It costs 4 gold every time you touch it. There have been times when I bounced off it accidentally while leaving, causing me to spend 12 gold I didnt want to in 2 seconds. I have also been pinned by NPCS in certain areas (the crafter's bench in the miscellaneous store is horrible for this one), and was unable to get out due to the configuration of the area. It could also stand to be a little longer and have wider variety of items and crafting receipes as well as not have everything you can craft visible from the start (although that is a matter of personal taste, I like to gain/learn receipes instead of seeing everything right away). YOu;ll probably get 3-5 hours of gameplay out of it, more if you explore and mess around.

Even with these flaws - which I would call more of a lack of polish than anything - the game is definately worth it. With a seeming trend of indie developers charging ridiculous prices for games far less polished and fun than this one (especially 99c mobile games being badly ported to the PC and sold for $10), its a breath of fresh air to see a non-greedy dev charging a fair price for a game.

Its worth the $5 for any top-down RPG or oldschool fan. Get it.\t\t\t\t\t

The game will give roughly 3 hours of gameplay which is ill suited for an RPG. It gives you just enough time to figure things out but not enough time to advance any sort of story or bring your character to a reasonably customizable point. I hit Level 6 when I finished the game, which gave me 5 points to distribute. I put two in stamina which ended up being a completely useless stat to me, 2 in health and one in mana... which also ended up being a completely useless stat to me.

The final boss of the game was killed by left clicking it over 10,000 times. My right hand is still suffering from carpal tunnel, but thankfully I'm left handed and can do fine without. But if you have carpal tunnel and are right handed.... you probably should just not buy this game.

The game starts you off in a room where you left click on what appears to be a flower until you look a little closer and find out that it's your dead wife. You find out she's dead and receive your first quest to avenge her. Throughout the game you will pick up 8 quests which will get you to 8 different boss-like entities that are all killed in roughly the same way.

The game advertises crafting... which it does have. However since the game is so short and you don't actually need it, it doesn't make up a very important part of the game. I killed the final boss with a weapon I picked off of an enemy, crafting seems like it is tacked on. It is the way in which you get all higher quality goods, but because you can beat enemies by using a microing technique, it's simply not necessary. It's kind of like how I found out I could block shots with the shift key... and then never used it because I barely ever got hit.

Each story is very rushed. You talk to a person and they tell you two sentences, and then you are on your way. For an RPG it's very lacking in plot development.

The pixel graphics unfortunately hinder the game in such a massive way. Pixel art can be very beautiful. In this case it looks like most of the pixel art was taken from royalty free artists and slapped into the game. Nothing looks like what it should be.

If this was a free to play browser game I'd play it. But I don't think I'd ever pay for something that looks like it was made at a game jam.\t\t\t\t\t
