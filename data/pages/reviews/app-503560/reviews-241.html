\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent game. A bit stressful, especially at first when you don't really know what you're doing or how to handle certain situations. And just when you think you're talking to the same person, dealing with the exact same incident, a curve ball gets thrown at you so you really need to pay attention and select the right actions accordingly. There's a lot of voice actors in the game.

There's a surprising amount of content in this game, a lot more than I expected.

There are 3 really frustrating issues with the game... Icons often get in the way of sending support units to the scene or taking them away from the scene since the icons enlarge as you zoom in which means zooming in doesn't help at all. You often end up with a clusterf**k of icons in the way and you have no idea how to resolve it other than work from the outside inwards. You also often receive audio of "shots fired, need backup!" but you have no idea which incident needs the backup since there's often a lot of situations happening all at once and there's no icon highlighting or anything which is a really dumb flaw this game has. The difficulty curve is very unrealistic in the sense that you're extremely limited in funding, especially when first starting out on the map, but they fill the map with situations in which you couldn't possibly handle correctly which in turn causes deaths and / or staff injuries which forces you to suffer a great deal more in the next shift as your reputation drops and there's very little to no funding for the next day. I'm almost tempted to play the game in easy mode just so I can play the game in a more realistic and completable way.

One more thing, I received a real life phone call, and for some dumb reason, I could not pause the game at all while I was in a call within the game, which forced me to make the obvious decision and cause me to lose the call in the game... setting me back even more.\t\t\t\t\t

Well, I did come back to this game about two years later and ended up changing my review to a positive one. I was able to get the base game and all of its dlc on a ridiculous sale and am happy to report that they added new layers, more equipment, and new stats to the game to make it re-playable. I'm sure I'll get tired of the same calls but it definitely feels like some of the new mechanics makes it feel less repetitive. I'm looking forward to their next entry that comes out in 2020.

Also, This is the police added a sandbox mode and dropped part 2 of their game...still no marriage...maybe someday....
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
It really is a great concept but for me it feels a little shallow from a management aspect. I realize it is called 911 operator but was hoping for more department management mechanics and stats at launch (although I don't know if that was ever in the scope of the game to begin with). I think the call aspect is done well enough but in two hours I felt like I couldn't see myself plodding through other cities for hours on end.

Perhaps I'll pick it up again at a later date, but don't think it's quite ready for launch. A similar concept sans the operator part is "This is the Police" which has a deeper management piece to it but currently doesn't offer sandbox mode. If only they could marry the two games...some day....\t\t\t\t\t
