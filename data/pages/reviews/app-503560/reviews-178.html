911 Operator is a great resource manager, and it's made even greater by the fact that you can play it wherever you want in the world. Literally. Just type in the name of a city, and you're off to the races. There is a slight learning curve for some, as the game doesn't exclusively focus on answering calls and dispatching units. You will have to prepare them - police, fire and ambulance primarily - to the best of your abilities beforehand, and within the allocated funding, before you set out on your journey. This is important to get right too, as an incompetent team and/or lack of proper equipment can mean the difference between a successful turnout, or a devastating shortfall that puts you in the red, and your overall reputation at stake. It certainly suffices to say that the RPG and strategy elements involved throughout the title are pretty decent, and you WILL be put on the spot at times. Cause and effect is a very real thing here, and it's probably best not to ignore the manual at the beginning of the game, or skimp out on the tutorial, if you plan on playing it straight to that end. Some of the stuff mentioned throughout, in fact, is real world knowledge that can/will help in an actual, offline emergency situation, should you ever need it. All the more incentive if you ask me.

The pros and neutrals aside, the two caveats of 911 Operator I've found so far, for starters, are that it's one of those affairs that tend to get repetitive if you play it for more than an hour or two at a time. Some people have the patience for marathoning a game like this, and I applaud them. But for me, that just wasn't the case. Secondly, although you can remedy this via the Steam Workshop and download additional calls, as well as emergencies in general, it still doesn't detract too much from the fact that some of the incidents occuring in-game have a tendency to repeat themselves. Additionally, having initially purchased the Collector's Edition, which includes the Special Resources and Every Life Matters DLCs, and the First Response DLC after the precluding fact, I can certainly say that they do little to remedy the situation. It's nothing to make me regret my purchase, but it's a poignant enough fallibility that will rear its head after some time. Something I hope to see the developers address in their continued support of the title, in tandem with its overall consumer base.

As I bring this to a close, although it's really a minor issue that I don't see the sense in docking the game points over, it bears mentioning nonetheless that the sound sliders in the options menu should seriously be looked at in a future update. They are terribly unbalanced. Turning them down to the center of the slider shouldn't mean that everything goes silent, just as turning them up shouldn't double down on the assault of my ear drums. Especially as it's recommended that you play the game with headphones for the best experience.

Now, as for the TL;DR of it all:

Pros/Neutrals:
  • The ability to play in ANY real city, or town, in the world
  • Moddable, with the ability to add new calls and emergencies
  • Decent Strategy/RPG elements
  • Challenging, or hilarious, depending on your play style
  • Actual advice in the manuals for emergencies that help in-game, as well as offline
  • Slight learning curve, especially for those who don't normally play resource managers

Cons:
  • Not enough variety in the calls/situations presented, even with mods
  • Can only be played in spurts, as it becomes cut and dry after awhile (puns intended)
  • Sound sliders in the options menu are unbalanced, and should be addressed in the future

Overall Score for 911 Operator: 4 out of 5. It might be a little steep at full price when you take the negatives into consideration. On sale, however, you could do way, way worse. For the fun that there is to be had here, even if you're not generally into resource management games (as is the case with me usually), this game certainly exists to provide it. And if you can snag it on sale, for your end of the discount, there's no better time to give it a go.\t\t\t\t\t
