\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend this game, I've spent maybe 20 hours playing it and I keep coming back to it just because I want to play more.

Flaws:
*There are some grammar errors in the text of calls.
*When extinguishing a fire for the 'Kitchen on Fire' event over the phone, the text saying it is done shows up way before the voice returns which is a timing error.
*The text isn't always the same as what is actually said.
*Beginning of general calls get repetitive - there needs to be more variation in how people start a call.
*Some calls are only voiced by one gender and not the other.
*Extremely serious calls occur very infrequently and there don't seem to be that many of them right now, four of them I know I've only heard once while playing.
*Some of the calls don't seem to have variations or don't have a variation where a particular option is relevant yet.
*In some of the larger maps in the last short bit of the clock, sometimes the game freezes. It can come back suddenly a few minutes later but it is inconvenient as I've closed it a few times when it froze.

Positives:
*The variations of the way a call can go mean sometimes a call you resolved the first time by dismissing the person must be taken more seriously the next time.
*Very good voice-acting for pretty much everything. I can believe that guy whose car is stolen is actually annoyed when he calls, I can believe that woman who finds a baby is upset and trying not to cry in her shock.
*Each duty takes around 10 or so minutes which is a good quantity of time - if you fail or it crashes then you never lose a lot of time.
*While it isn't realistic, I like being overlord of the emergency services.
*While on the setting up screen, it saves every change you make.
*You can still tell vehicles where to go when paused.

Things I'd like added:
*More different calls. More frivolous calls, more serious calls, more that can vary between them, just more calls all around and even calls where the caller refuses to give information or doesn't know it. Almost all the time, I can't tell you what street I am on, because I just don't know.
*More natural disasters and the ability for them to happen randomly depending on the city e.g. in UK cities, there is more likely to be flooding or normal storms. The earthquake one was really good, I was on easy and I still felt like I was stretched thin by all the things going on at once and I loved it.
*For there to be separated sections for hiring Firefighters, Paramedics and Police sections than just one whole staff section so that if I need to replace staff or want to build up more units, each one stocks independantly rather than me ending up with 5 spare Paramedics when what I really need is more Firefighters.

Generally I enjoy it and the constant background noises really help me get into the zone of what I'm doing and while there are things that should be corrected and things I wanted added to it, I still really like it as it is.

It is a good management game and while it doesn't portray everything realistically (obviously as a game it has to change some things from reality to be fun and seem more like you are in control), the changes it has made make sense for the medium it is. An ambulance won't turn around to pick up the guy with a broken foot when taking a gunshot victim to the hospital in real life and police officers won't cram in more criminals in the back of a car before stopping at the station and the operator isn't an overlord that says who gets what and you won't get a lot of serious calls each day, but in a game, obviously these things are allowed to be different for entertainment purposes.

Generally I just find it to be a very good game. It is also very difficult as you might expect for such a difficult job, I've been playing on easy and still sometimes find certain vehicles stretched thin when random events are all for one type.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 10 hours of this title in 3 different days. I’m ready to pronounce this game is a bit on the addicting side.


What is it?
In short it’s a worker placement boardgame as a videogame...OR map based resource management sim. Your only view is a map, and markers showing the location of your units and events that occur. You spend your time listening and reading the information provided then send the right unit to the right incident just like an emergency dispatcher. Between days you can spend the funds you’ve earnt to hire more staff, buy more vehicles and organise more units.

What does it do well?
The voice acting does add some variety to it and can really bring the game to life. Earlier the calls can seem a bit dull with the same guy claiming quite often that his car was stolen when it was towed in a clearly marked lane but later in the game you can get a little bit of variety like a man ringing up with a bomb threat.
It’s pleasing to see that the money you earn through doing a good job can be reinvested to make your units fight better with better equipment and vehicles etc. The fact that you can change the starting placement of your units is very handy as well as it allows you spread out unit types (and prevents them from overlapping each other)
About your units, It's endearing to see that there is a stat building element for your staff which affects their pay grade however the pre duty deployment menu makes it very easy to forget this.
The fact that they allow you to replay mission as you wish and even load map data for your own city are both welcome. You will need to ignore the fact that everyone speaks with the same US english no matter what city you are in...sort of like a few films I could name.

What could have being improved?
Between days
The pre deployment management screen can also get quite unwieldy. For example if you add extra units they are automatically dropped to the bottom and easily lost on the list unless you MANUALLY shuffle EVERYTHING else down before you can place in the same group.
The equipment and shopping could also benefit and be optimized a bit in this way.

Also regarding pre deployment It seems rather confusing about which equipment suits each staff and vehicle as well. Why can't all vehicles have medical kits? They don’t take up that much space and i’m sure it is law to carry them. Also wouldn't ambulances logically have them by default? Does adding an extra medical kit and defab to a medical vehicle actually have any effect?It’s really hard to see.
Shouldn't all police have a sidearm as STANDARD issue?
Why do these seemingly normal items take up 1 of the 2 inventory space?
Same questions can be asked about firefighters and their equipment.

During the day
The callers and events get repetitive and you have to try not to preempt your responses. I wish there was more variety of callers although fully understand the problems with getting so many voice actors voicing so many lines. I wouldn’t have minded had they gone the partially voice route to allow for more variety of each call and caller type.

Finally the map screen seems to too easily crowded. This means that it can be very easy to lose units and events ON TOP of each other. This also means there is no way of knowing what each unit contains except memory...or seeing what happens after they arrive. This means if you gave all your police a shotgun except one you can easily forget which one NOT to send.
Conclusion
The main campaign isn’t all that long and inserting your own city as a map although sound interesting doesn’t really add that much content to it.
This game could do with a bit more polish.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith my own bit of experience in the firefighting and security services, I can confirm that the game mechanics and scenarios are quite simplistic, but I still enjoy this wonderful piece of infotainment game.
I like the fact that the game gives you useful tips for real life emergency situations, and it's helping me to improve the knowledge of streets and layout of my hometown.

I'd like to point out that there is quite some room for improvement though, and I sincerely hope that there will be an update to add more scenarios and to remove some rare but annoying bugs I've encountered:
- 'ignore' sometimes is the only option to end a call and continue the game (thereby losing a lot of reputation points)
- At some point I was unable to change the speed of the game (not paused at that time) and realized that the time for my units 'on scene' didn't pass at all, which led me to forfeit that round of duty

I usually don't buy games over 10€ on Steam, but I don't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery enjoyable game. Altough - fair warning - the calls get very repetitive after a while (altough there are some unique and well voice-acted ones you can encounter in career mode). I've also encountered some stability issues (random game crashes). The voice acting is on the whole a bit sub-par and could have been done better - for instance adding different voices to the same calls to add some more variety (aswell as adding a broader variety of calls). There are also some UI omprovements to be made as for instance the only way to close the game is by alt+f4.
A huge plus to the game is that you can literally download every city in the world (even the small town in switzerland I live in).
Nonetheless I enjoy the time I've spent on the game so far and look forward to play more - especially if well-made content is added by the devs in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
