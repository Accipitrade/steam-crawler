\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was pleasantly surprised by this game! At first sight, it looks a bit repetitive, as you do the same thing over and over again, but it is actually neither repetitive nor boring. You send emergency services to different adresses, following messages or 911 calls, but it's far more than just clicking on an address and sending a car there. Cases have different urgencies and can also develop in unexpected ways. A seemingly harmless situation can turn into a shooting and suddenly your police car needs plenty of backup. With growing experience I learned to evaluate the cases better and to send appropriate services there, and this learning curve is one aspect that guarantees that the game stays fresh. There are also different types of vehicles - it is better to send a motorcycle, a car or a helicopter? How to equip staff? When to get new staff, cars or equipment, how to distribute them? Many interesting decisions.
The tutorial is not fully sufficient, I was left with several open questions, which made the first rounds difficult. But the game is intuitive enough that one discovers these aspects and can find solutions, so that worked out well in spite of the somewhat meagre tutorial. I kept raising the difficulty level (there are four levels) and succeeding on first attempt once I understood the different mechanics. That makes the game far less challenging, but still interesting.
The 911 calls are nicely done, the acting is not bad at all and sometimes you have to make quick decisions that can have quite a big impact. It's also a nice way to learn something about handling emergencies. There could have been more calls, they are starting to repeat themselves after a while, but there is an interesting variety. I actually kept being personally interested in how some of the more emotional cases end.
During your shifts you are kept busy, messages and calls come on regularly, sometimes all your vehicles are busy, so you have to keep an eye on open cases and also check why some cases can't be completed (sometimes a second or different vehicle needs to be dispatched there). So there is a good variety of things to do and one is almost always busy.

After playing some more, there are a few smaller complaints I have. In some scenarios/cities the call get very repetitive. I just had the same call three days in a row. It takes a lot of challenge and fun out of the game if it's the same too often, especially when it is so quickly after one another. Something good I found out: clicking in the dialogue field speeds the dialogue up. Very helpful when one knows it by heart after hearing it so often.
The other - bigger - complaint is a bug in Miami. I saw that it has been reported several times in the forums, yet the developer apparently didn' do anything about it. There is a day in Miami where I always get told that I'm fired because if insufficient achievements. Even with high reputation! This is an unfortunate bug that prevents the continuation of Miami for me and therefore takes away a part of the gameplay. Disappointing.

So while I still recommend the game, those aspects take away a bit of the enthusiasm.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tfun, stressful (/pos) management game. nothing quite like starting a new duty in freeplay and having two of your three fully armed cop cars wiped out by a gang war at the first incident. worth grabbing if its on sale, but note that the sequel, 112 operator, improves it in almost every way. career mode takes around five hours to complete + free play is pretty repetitive.

criticisms:
- ui leaves something to be desired. 112 operator improves a lot in that regard. imo 911 has a better prep screen and nicer character portraits, but 112 still has an edge overall.

- calls get repeated a lot. like, lady, this is the fourth time this week your kitchen has been on fire. get a new toaster. #girl.

- this isnt something an indie team would be able to circumvent; regardless of where on the map you're playing, its very us-centric. i dont think of it as a slight against the game, but looking at a map of my hometown and hearing americans talking over it *is* immersion breaking.

- the climax of the career mode is very... questionable, politically. spoilers: on your final duty, you get calls from a terrorist and need to send people over to evacuate certain locations before he blows it up. it's a fun twist to the gameplay loop and very exciting. and then you hear his demands, and find out that he's doing this because he's an immigrant who wants immigrants to be treated better. the rest of the game is relatively mild wrt cop-media-typical propaganda, so it did feel like a slap in the face. it really just didn't feel as well-considered and realistic as other calls.\t\t\t\t\t
