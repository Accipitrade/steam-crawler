\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game. I like management sims, and this checks that box quite well. What I don't like is the utterly terrible English translations/dialogue and that in one of the scenarios, a caller used the N-word to describe someone. Since this game has been in development (and available) for so long, you would think they would take care to make sure translations are correct and that the grammar isn't stunted and awkward, but I guess that's either too expensive or not important.

One of the scenarios mirrors the George Floyd protests over the summer, and a caller uses the N-word to describe the victim. As someone who has lived abroad in non-English-speaking countries, I understand that people might not know the weight that the N-word carries, but know your audience - if you're going to market this to American (or even just native-English-speaking) audiences, maybe don't use such a charged word.

Once again, I like the game, but these two issues detract from the gameplay. If something like that would bother you, I wouldn't purchase the game. If you can look past those issues, it's a fun game with a decent amount of replayability.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gameplay mechanics are not that hard to grasp, At the beginning of each level, you’ll either buy new units, select them to spawn them in the next playthrough or upgrade them with new equipment or personnel. During a level, you’ll need to respond to 911 calls and incidents that periodically pop up on your radar. Sometimes you might get a really silly call, like when someone complains that a music event’s music is too loud and they aren’t even playing real music. If you’re like me, you’d immediately end that call. Other times you’ll get really important, interesting calls. The one that struck me was that the devs incorporated the famous 911 pizza order call, where a woman cleverly called emergency services and disguised it as a pizza order to prevent her abusive boyfriend from realising that police were dispatched. You’ll also need to pay attention, (if possible) wear headphones, and not ignore any phone call that might seem initially stupid. These might include someone initially calling because a house party is too loud, to later revealing that they saw someone under the influence of drugs entering the premises. Paying attention to any audio cues is a good and relevant feature of this game, so don’t expect you can speed through this one.\t\t\t\t\t
