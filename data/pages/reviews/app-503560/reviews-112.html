This game is a nice little surprise. Really it's pretty good, it's not just another cheap cash grab.

The game is simple. You're a 911 operator and your job is to manage the emergency services and answering 911 calls.
You have three main services in your control â police, medics and firefighters. You can choose a unit and send it to the point of the map where the incident happened. Depending on the incident you need to choose which units to dispatch. If there's a robbery it might be smart to send a larger group of police officers with guns and medics to take of the wounded. If there's a red light offense you probably can send a single officer there to give him a fine. Same with all the other incidents. Sometimes you get some serious incidents like drug cartel or prison break which means you better send a lot of units there.
Some incidents are more important than others and you have a limited amount of units. So lets say if there's a cat in a tree and a traffic accident where you need technical truck it's more logical to send it to the traffic accident. Managing all this is not easy, you need to position your units wisely and carefully manage their movements so you would be able to send them to the most important incidents.

There's also 911 calls where you need to talk with the caller. Some calls are serious, some are prank calls or just some stoned dude calling to mess with you. The best non-serious ones are gamer calls in my opinion. And some of the serious calls are really disturbing and greatly acted. Like when father found his daughter dead or a rape victim. These hit me hard in the feelings.

Overall the game is pretty challenging, managing your units might be really hard. You need to get reputation points and different incidents give different amount of reputation points. You have small cars, vans, armored cars, helicopters etc. in your disposal. Smaller ones move faster, bigger ones are slower but have more people for more serious incidents.

One of the most interesting things this game has is that you can use whichever city you like. You just type the city name and it downloads the map overlay for that city, with real street names so anyone can play in his home city.

What is pretty disappointing is lack of different calls. After some time you start getting same calls and it's just isn't fun hearing same calls tens of times. Some calls try to fake it and sound like they're different, but you can clearly hear the incident is the same, the voice is the same and there's only one sentence is changed. I'd like more variety.

Graphically the game is as simple as it can get. You basically have an overlay of the city with moving units and that's it. Can't say i'm disappointed but I hope the next game will be vastly improved.

Sound is nice, you hear a chatter of units and you talk with the callers. Most callers are good but some of them feel like they are overacting. Although some are very believable, feels like its real. There's also some calls with different sound quality, clearly recorded with different hardware or something, but it's no biggie.

Overall a nice challenging game which is quite unique.

  • Fun
  • Some calls are really good
  • Unique
Neutral

Cons
  • Calls start repeating to quick
