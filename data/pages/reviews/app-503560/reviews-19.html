\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is harder than you think. Held back by strange unchangeable controls, it forces you to play in such a weird way to fulfill efficiency quotas. You need to act quick but with the pause button (P) and speed changing buttons (Z & X) being so far away, it makes it difficult to operate the game with a keyboard and mouse efficiently or comfortably.

That being said the features and mechanics of the game do their jobs really well. It's fun, needing to strategise all your units and manage your funds so that you are available to help everyone as soon as possible. There are no guides to teach you how to optimise, only you and yourself. I for one haven't figured it out despite completing a Single City run on Normal difficulty. There were so many things I couldn't afford to purchase because every duty I was spending so much money.

Though the game is repetitive, as per the job of a 911 Operator, which doesn't make for a game you can spend a lot of time on. I see a maximum of 20 hours of gameplay before the game just gets really boring. Maybe mods can help extend that, but the game just doesn't have thaaaat much to offer you.

It's a simple strategy game with a sprinkle of personality in its voice acting and stories. Definitely worth the price but don't see yourself playing the game for more than 15-20 hours unless you really love it.\t\t\t\t\t
