\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty addictive game, if you like games that are mostly about "linking" things - for example, city building or transport tycoon type games. This is actually similar when you get down to the basic gameplay mechanic, but the emergency aspect of it means you're constantly under a time limit with limited resources.

It can get genuinely tense in realistic ways. For example, you might have all but a couple of your police vehicles on calls already when a gang fight pops up. So you dispatch one vehicle thinking you'll keep your last one in reserve. Then shooting breaks out with your cops under fire and requesting backup. You send the last vehicle, but in the meantime you hear "officer down" over the radio. Now you need an ambulance too, and maybe you have none in the area. And you know you're going to need more than one more cop car too, because now you've only got one officer up against five armed gang members, who have already incapacitated another officer. So you sit there literally counting the seconds until another call is cleared and a vehicle is freed up. Meanwhile, other random 911 calls and emergencies keep coming in. Especially at the end of a shift, it can become pretty hectic.

A big part of the game is learning to have good instincts about who to send on what calls from the start, just like in real life. Some calls you'll know from the start are going to require multiple vehicles from different disciplines, while others might only require a single vehicle with no transport spaces (ie. just write a ticket or provide first aid). Sending the right combo of vehicles before the situation even unfolds is key.

It is a budget game, though, and there's not a lot of longevity. Once you've completed a career, which takes maybe a few hours, there's not really much else to see. There are a couple of other modes, but they all have basically the same stuff - same equipment, same cities, same 911 calls and other emergencies. It's still fun for a little while, but I'm at 14 hours and I feel like I'm pretty much done.

There's a sequel called 112 Operator, and maybe it adds more to make it last longer, I'm not sure. To be honest, I feel like a 911 dispatcher's job probably *is* pretty repetitive, so in that way it's pretty realistic.

One thing that's pretty funny are all the "Europeanisms" that creep into the game, despite all the cities being set in the US. Most of the callers seem to have eastern European accents and say stuff like "me and my mates were enjoying beers at the pub when..." Of course there are immigrants in US cities but they're not all from Europe! Anyway it's not a big deal but I just find it amusing sometimes. I'm sure they thought setting the game in the US would result in the most sales but probably couldn't afford all American actors. 112 Operator is probably a little more believable as a result, since it seems like it's set in Europe.\t\t\t\t\t
