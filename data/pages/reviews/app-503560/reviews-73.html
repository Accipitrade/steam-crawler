\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have done this job in real life for 27+ years. When I saw this game I expected it to be something cheesy, but was surprised. They actually got it right, as much as a game can. Some days are slow. Some days the calls pour in on top of each other, and it's all you can do to keep up. This game reflects both aspects very well. The calls you get in this game... I have taken every single one of them in real life, many times. The biggest criticism I see in the reviews is how repetitive the game gets as you play for a while, but that too, is totally realistic. I have taken the same calls, over and over, more than I could possibly count. The names and locations change, but the stories are often very the much the same. So, even the repetition is an accurate reflection of this career. The actual job is considerably harder than the game, with police units checking out on stuff they find, which is not reflected here, but the stress and challenge of getting the right units to the right calls, in a timely manner to get those calls cleared before more calls come in and they pile up, This game has done a very good job at providing a good little taste of what it's like. If you find yourself getting overwhelmed or too stressed here, don't try the real thing. It can be a lot more stressful and overwhelming, but if you want a taste of what the job is like, this game does provide that. It's also very educational about what is and is not an emergency. One thing I hope is that this game will make the people who call 911 a little more understanding of the person they are talking to, and of the time it may take to get some help.\t\t\t\t\t
