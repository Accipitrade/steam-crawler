\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoy this game, it's really fun to be in the shoes of a 911 operator, and I think it helped me gain a new appreciation for just how hard it must be to pick and choose which situations to deal with (yeah, it wouldn't be much fun in real life...) There are a lot of problems holding it back from being really great though. The situations in the calls have a tendency to be repeated over and over, which is a shame because it's genuinely really satisfying and fun whenever you get one you haven't gotten before. The gameplay also has little variation and it can get pretty dull after a few hours. I would love a few more narrative elements to shake things up a bit, there doesn't have to be some big fleshed out story, but at one point in my playthrough there was an earthquake, and I had to solve a bunch of incidents relating to it, it felt fresh and shook things up a little without completely changing the game dynamics, more stuff like that in the career mode would make for a much more satisfying experience in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this is a rather ideal game for the traveler who has only a basic laptop. Its easy to pick up at any time and hammer out a quick cycle or two and close up.

While the campaign is somewhat short and the sandbox can get repetitive, its not really a deal breaker for the above reason. I can see myself playing it off-and-on regularly while I'm in between games or stuck at an airport.

The gameplay itself is rather straightforward yet its requirement to multitask can challenge you every time. Don't be put off by the simplistic gameplay style, its biggest atribute is a teaching aid for the uneducated. It should give you a tiny glimpse at what emergency services goes though and it educates you on the basics of emergency services and first aid along the way.

It is by no means a replacment for any real training courses of course, but its a very good intro into that world!\t\t\t\t\t
