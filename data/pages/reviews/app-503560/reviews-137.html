\r\n\t\t\t\t\t5/10 The game is ok, but could easily be better.

There are only very few emergency calls. 80-90% of the tasks just pop up. So you are basically trying to distribute your units efficiently to respond to the popups with a call once in a while spicing things up...

You can't really see which unit is going where, the UI is pretty messy. Too many units in one place: good luck, picking the right one.

You can't change the key configuration, the changes don't work ingame.

Every officer got two inventory slots.... like in real life.... like in real life he has to choose to bring a gun and body armor, or a gun and laptop to speed up his police work. lol. Why those unrealistic restrictions?

The skill/stat system is a mess too.
The stats of your officers are kind of messed up. I got a firefighter with overall tech skill 80 and one with tech skill 65. Looking at the detailed stats, you can see that the second firefighter surpasses the first in every skill, but still his overall skill rating is worse? On top of that, assignments to vehicles changes the stats again. Bugs? Unexplained game mechanics? Who knows?

Music is mediocre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI purchased 911 operator with all the DLC for $4.80. Make sure you download the one free DLC too; this is not automatically included in the bundle! I made that mistake.

At this price, it's worth it. I got through the campaign in about 6 - 8 hours taking my time on expert mode, which is the hardest setting. It's not a hard game by any means. It's interesting when it gets difficult, but for the most part it's very manageable even on "expert." This makes it boring after about 10+ hours of gameplay especially once you've completed the story. But, for less than $5, that's perfect and about what I would expect.

Great game if you were a fan of 911: First Responders (Emergency 4) back in the day. This game plays like a simpler, easier, less buggy version of that.

They have a sequel coming out soon (currently in Alpha on Kickstarter) called 112 Operator, which seems to be more in line with Emergency 4 by adding additional complexity. I'm looking forward to that.\t\t\t\t\t

Simulator where you play a dispatch operator overseeing a city/district's police, fire and medical services, with some elements of planning, purchasing and outfitting your resources prior to handling the numerous calls.

While the game is not very deep strategy-wise (the maps are just different overlays with routes) the game does have a certain charm to it, and can be quite challenging - memorably so - at numerous times. The best feature by far are the actual 911 calls which you have to handle, the voice acting and decisions you have to make to communicate to the caller are very well voice acted and thoroughly immersing. You'll probably learn a thing or two as well about real-life responses through bitter experience... ;)

The other great feature is the career mode with unique events - with everything from riots, earthquakes and snowstorms being thrown at you to make for a unique and flavourful experience.

Overall, a simple but entertaining game, well worth the asking price!\t\t\t\t\t
