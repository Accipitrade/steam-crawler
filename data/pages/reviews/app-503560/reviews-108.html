\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWasn't sure about this game, but turned out to be be pretty decent. The basic concept is you are a 911 operator. Your screen pops up and you see your units available to you. Pretty soon, you will hear a beep and you will either have to dispatch units to an area on the map or answer a call where after you ask questions, you decide what units (either fire, police, or rescue) to that location. You can click the icon on the screen and get a basic idea of whats going on to help you dispatch.

The scenario is actually pretty challenging. I tried on normal and it was pretty hard. Tried on easy and it was still quite the challenge. Nothing special about the graphics, but I did like the music and the voice actor who played the 911 operator was quite good. This game keeps you on your toes. Playing the campaign, You have to beat 10 chapters which can cover over 20 deployments. The only real complaints I have is there is nothing that tells you what you have to score (as in reputation or efficiency) to move on and it can get quite repetitive after a while. But was quite fun for the almost 20 hours I played.\t\t\t\t\t
