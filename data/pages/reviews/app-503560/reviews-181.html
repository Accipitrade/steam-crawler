\r\n\t\t\t\t\t911 Operator is like being in the shoes of a 911 dispatcher, but simpler. The game takes you through shifts (never actually paid attention to how long each is oops) of answering calls, receving alerts, and directing the emergency crew. A very basic idea, with lots of twists and turns along the way.

Now, to the average gamer, this might just be a button presser game, but i find it to be one of my favorite games on my Steam library. With the helpful education tips (how to correctly preform cpr, what to do in certain emergencies, etc.) and the emersivness you can feel, i find this game all around amazing.

The graphics aren't anything to write home about, but i don't think its a con of the game. The simplistic style of the menus helps you not be as distracted as well. The gameplay all depends on what type of person you are. This game is a simulation of a 911 dispatcher, if this dosnt sound in the slightest intresting, this isn't your type of game. But if you have even the slightest intrest, i'd say check it out. As for the actual control of everything, it was a sligh bit weird at first, but after a shift or two its fine. The audio for this game is mostly voice acting or radio noises (like, the stuff you'd hear on a police officers radio). The voice acting isn't the best voice acting ive ever heard, but i dont think they'res been a point ive had to turn the volume down cause of it. The difficulty of it is varried, as you get calls ranging from a small injury to active bomb threats. I also haven't had any technical issues, the game is pretty polished.

One of my favorite things about this game is also the community. So many people have made their own senerios and events you can add in, and they're all pretty good. It's fun to be able to always add new things.

Overall, i'd say give this game a shot. Watch a video of someone playing it, or buy it right away, its whatever you choose. You won't regret just giving it a chance.\t\t\t\t\t
