\r\n\t\t\t\t\t* Fun and very labor-intensive when placed on Expert for a few towns.
* Can mark your own town, or others, but it's nearly the same gameplay throughout.
* Short for what it's worth in story, but makes for a great introduction to future titles.

* ONLY purchase knowing this game's story can be completed within the same day you purchased it. However, you will have massive fun regardless. You will wish for much more story, as every single town has only 3 tours of duty and that's pretty much it.

Challenge yourself with Washington on Expert, and if you clear the first tour then you should be fine. These are random events for the most part, so keep that in mind.

Mods are a thing, so stack them up if you wish to enjoy more of what the game can give.

I would set this at probably a 7/10 just for the fact the story is so incredibly short. Even at only $15, that's my only major complaint. For someone who loves simulation games and has played some way back from the SNES days, this is really disappointing. It's also very underwhelming when you do complete the story, as it feels like it had lots of potential to be drawn out. You could watch an episode of Burn Notice for longer than what it would take to complete one town's scenario.

For what it's worth though, I loved the game. I even suggest buying it, but only when it goes on sale (and all the DLC as well!).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun and sometimes intense emergency services management game, surprisingly indepth when it comes to emergency protocol and handling. A good variety of scenes and emergency calls. I really liked how intense the earthquake and bomber unique stories was! Put a real test to position, timing and rng.

Though I have a few gripes and that is the volume controls are sometimes a bit too loud or too low even with setting changes. Also I get that the emergency guide/instructions are a nice touch and its to inform the player on civilian emergency response but it kind of gets annoying for a regular player, (if there is a setting for this that I miss disregard previous sentence). Also a small nitpick is that for some emergency calls the caller's voice makes it hard to tell the tone of the emergency.

Fun game regardless, definitely worth a try. Looking forward to the 'sequel' 112 Operator\t\t\t\t\t
