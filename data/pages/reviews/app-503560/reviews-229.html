\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI actually really enjoy playing this. You feel like you're the dispatcher, sending emergency services throughout the city at the first sign of trouble. There's a certain satisfaction to hearing that a hostage situation has been resolved, and you got there in time to save someone who was critically injured.

That being said, there are some scenarios that are quite heartbraking to hear, and will have you understanding what a dispatcher goes through in an ordinary shift. Some, also are quite amusing. They could use some more additions though, I tend to get some of the same scenarios (With some slight differences) coming up all the time. Perhaps future updates (or steam workshop?) might solve this.

There are also some spelling and grammar errors in some of the scenarios. Also, I'm not sure if it's because I'm largely doing US cities right now, but almost every police stop seems to end in a gunfight.

Some of the menu UI elements are quite basic, but functional. The first aid tips are an interesting loading screen mechanic, but feel like a popup ad when you just want to start your shift.

I LOVE the fact that you can literally download any city, since it uses OpenMaps to draw the streets, and dispatch units around it realistically. Though I used my home city (London, UK) and I can tell you that even an Ambulance would struggle to move as quickly through the city as they do in this game.

It's a great game concept, and can be addictive. But it could use some more scenario content. I didn't buy the DLC, and probably won't if it only adds extra vehicles.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust want to clarify some feedback (and share mine) I have seen here which almost make me not buy this game!!

1. DLC from day 1: Devs have explained the reason, go to the discussion tab and find out. Looks like a decent one to me. Game was advertized around 20 bucks early feb when I spotted it, today you can get it at 11$ plus 3$ for the DLC. 200% worth the money invested!

2. Calls get repetitive: Guess what, this game is not about listening to nice and countless stories. I am playing in career mode for now, in Washington DC so far, and still discovering new calls or different end to situations I heard before. So yes, you'll get redundancy in calls, but you learn to pay less attention to the dialogue and to focus more on which unit to deploy, based on their skills, their limitations, the current emergencies you're dealing with, their location and completion status as well as wondering when the next situation might pop up. Plus devs are already talking about more calls in updates and DLC to come. This is version 1 guys..

So all in all a really great game, very promising. I've been waiting for something like this for a long time and it runs super smooth on Mac.

Pretty amazed on how deep you can go, despites initially thinking it was fairly (too) simple. From selecting a police car with 5 cops in it for a stolen vehicle driving almost as fast as me, and 1 minute after having a 2 men patrol riding bikes attending a 4 armed men hostage situation, to destroying my reputation for an old man running naked in the street (yeah it's an emergency, not a comedy show), this game keeps you on your toes to make the best decision in a split second.
Whether it is about team management, deployment, attending emergencies or diversity of situations and how to best protect your reputation without wasting all your cash, it gathers a lot of good stuff for a simulation game.

Shifts are short, about 8 minutes without hitting pause or slow motion (ok it can drag up to 30 if you play it seriously), perfect for a short break right? but careful here, you can easily do 10 shifts in a row without realizing it..

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fun game. It is more than just answering 911 calls. Youalso need to dispatch and manage your emergency assets. There is a management side of things as you are in charge of hiring staff and buying equipment. You simply tell who top go where and let it happen.

Small cons that don't really detract too much: The calls do get repetetive but it seems like just because they start the same doesn't mean they turn out the same..but there seems to be an epidemic of people just falling unconsious on the street...but maybe that is realistic.

There is a career mode but honestly the free play mode functions as a career since you are graded on each day and can upgrade equipment between shifts, The career mode just requires you to reach a certain reputation before moving on to a more complicated city. In free mode you can download a map of any town or city....I tried several places I used to live......and play there as a dispatch. It was fun recognizing streets I used to live on.

In the long run this game could get repetetive but it does what it does really well and I definitely recomend it at it's current prce.\t\t\t\t\t
