\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA nice little resource management game. It's limited in scope, but it's competent at what it does.

The basic mechanics are simple: spend a limited pool of resources to prepare for active duty by hiring staff, buying vehicles and equipment, and during duty manage your available squads to try and resolve the most emergencies, allocating enough personnel to be effective and contain risks, but not overcommitting in case any other major events happen.

The main attraction is the pre-registered emergency calls, where your input is required to understand the situation and determine the right response team. Some calls seems to repeat but details can vary, so the pool of possible emergencies is a bit larger than initially expected, but the novelty wears off around the end of the campaign. A full run through will exhaust most available call options, so if you want to keep playing on free mode the calls start to get repetitive. I liked the care that was put in the recordings, though: for instance, background noise can help determining what's going on how serious the issue is. A noise complaint call could have loud music in the background, and on a call for car theft you could hear the criminals driving away. Voice acting is alright.

Tooltips are not very helpful: descriptions of items and vehicles seem to suggest they have certain effects during emergencies, but you are always unsure whether they actually help. Do tasers actually work? What does the firetruck with ladder do that a regular firetruck doesn't, and if it's better, by how much? The only noticeable stats are speed and carry capacity, the rest seems a bit inconsequential. This is one of the reasons DLCs don't feel like they add a lot: you don't feel compelled to use the special vehicles because you never know if they work any differently and how. The Search and Rescue missions are a fine diversion the first 2-3 times, then you start dreading them. The added cities and special events (festival, blizzard, ...) are actually OK, but all in all I suggest only getting the whole package if deeply discounted as DLC cost is higher than base game but added content is not proportional.\t\t\t\t\t
