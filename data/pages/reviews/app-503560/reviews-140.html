\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's just OK. Wait for a deep discount, as 6 hours is about right for the total game experience.

You manage fire, police, and medical emergency services in a given map.

A lot of the phone call scenarios were interesting, adding variety to the standard game play. After the final map, however, I felt like I had completed and seen all that I needed to.

I found using P to pause the game, followed with Z, X to manipulate time was easiest to deal with the myriad situations on screen. These controls can be customized, but that menu is slightly hidden.

A few areas where the game could improve:
- hotkey customization. This is only available at the menu before the game starts. At the menu where you can specify your graphics, before the game launches, you can also access the hotkey customization. This needs to be included in the actual game settings in-game
- drag and dropping of resources. Before each round on a given map, you can customize your teams and their equipment. Dragging and dropping should be more streamlined in this area - I shouldn't have to drag an item off an officer in order to replace that officer's equipment with a new item.

The audio and dialogue was just OK. There were some translation issues with things like "I'm going to hospital!", which is a very British-English way to say things. Usually, this is expressed as "I'm going to the hospital!".

The caller scenario audios felt a little poorly acted - it was strange for the 911 operator to give these exasperated sighs as though they were judging the caller on the other line. That's.. a little silly.\t\t\t\t\t
