\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was a dispatcher for a number of years. This game replicates well the limited number of resources available, and some of the decision making that results from that. However the information you need to make those decisions isnt as readily available as it should be.

Types and skills of resources could be expanded. There is more nuance around rank that isnt really implemented. I'm not sending a Sgt to every incident, only ones where a supervisor is required.

I like that this is map based. However in reality you don't make resourcing decisions based on information presented on the map. You consider the nature of the incident, and intelligence that you hold on the address and those involved. This game doesn't contain any intel element, despite it being a big part of your real life decision making.

In this game the player is making their decisions in isolation. In reality you draw on colleagues and it systems a lot more. But that's fine, because this is a game and not work.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t911 Operator isn't as much a simulator as it is a time-management game. It does set the proper atmosphere, the 911-calls are very well done and the voice acting is quite good for a low-budget title. The sound effects and visuals really help set the stage.

What you are tasked to do is both answer the calls, select the right response and order your units around. You have to balance the costs for calling in reinforcements, buying new equipment and keeping enough staff in rotation (staff might get hurt during gunfights or fires) during and after each game. You get money for handing out fines (hah! Corrupt much?) and completing calls.

There are several different game modes but they all boil down to the same thing as far as I can tell: you have to complete as many tasks as you possibly can without torpedoeing your reputation. If your reputation tanks, you are simply fired and you'll have to try again.

The game isn't as realistic as one might like. You can for example, drive around with a wounded passenger for the entire night and at some point calls will stop coming in (and you'll go into overtime, which costs extra money) and you're just left to wrap up whatever you were doing.

When a day has ended, you can start one anew with the equipment you already had and money you earned the day before.

The game is fairly difficult on expert but not impossible thanks to being able to slow down time (and pausing, although this is not available in expert mode).

The interface does sometimes fail you by being somewhat difficult to use, there do not seem to be any quick keys worth mentioning (at least I haven't found them yet) and the game has a habit of stacking icons on top of each other making it very difficult to get your units to the right incident (hint: select a unit and right click on the incident under the incident tab in the lower left ... )

I really like EMS shows and I think it's great fun that you get to call in the National Guard on a drug lab or outfit your police as SWAT and send them in with riot shields and shotguns or have your motorcycle medic and police race around town chasing dopefiends. You can even put two cops on a bicycle and outfit them with a k9 unit and riot shields if you'd like and I mean, c'mon, that's just funny!

Yes, it does require a bit of imagination but if you've seen enough cop shows, I'm sure you'll have plenty of material to work with. I was quite amused by the Miami Vice cops making an appearance as wel but I ended up selling their car to be able to actually win the map.\t\t\t\t\t

  • multiple units/missions in the single place are shown on top of each other, no way to select the ones underneath
  • you cannot see the equipment of your units on the map
  • vehicles in the shop have very poor descriptions, their maintenance in not shown anywhere
  • if multiple units have travel paths that cross or overlap each other, there is no way to tell what unit is going where
  • time indicating how long the task will take is useless - it is shown in seconds, but those "seconds" pass at different speeds
  • the colours of the units needed to for the task are often incorrect - e.g. it shows blue for police and white for ambulance to resolve domestic violence, but even after the wounded person is treated, the task is still showing white and blue, even though paramedics cannot do anything more there

  • no keyboard shortcuts

This game could offer tens of hours of fun, but sadly it doesn't. If you want to try it out anyway, I recommend waiting for another 80% sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was really excited when I noticed reviews are positive and I thought the game looked really cool.
But... I don't know, I guess I'm kinda disappointed. The idea is great but the game's lacking in quality. I'm glad I bought it while it was on sale because I wouldn't pay the full price.

Pros
+ Helpful information and/or trivia on loading screen that may be used during emergencies irl, I thought it was perfect for this kind of game
+ fun easter egg in Albuquerque, didn't know if it was intentional but I notived two cases which may have been references to Breaking Bad
+ seems very realistic

Cons
- I've spent about 2.5 h playing this game and it got boring and repetitive really quickly
- No plot or goals, no point in gaining reputation points (except for unlocking new cities which do not add that much fun)
- Interface is very unclear and confusing
- It's impossible to check what team is in a vehicle so it makes carefully placing policemen/firefighters in specific groups kind of pointless
- background noises (officers speaking through radio) are distracting, make little to no sense and get boring quickly, besides that there's no background music for gameplay
- Artwork. Whilst main menu looks fantastic I must say the rest of the game is really lacking in quality. Officer's portraits are TERRIBLE, look cheap and lazy, like low quality photoshoped photos mixed with bad drawings.

By the way... I don't want to accuse anyone of plagiarism or lack of originality but it seems awfuly similar to the game called "This is the Police"... So if you're looking for something like 911 Operator but better quality - you may wanna check TITP instead.\t\t\t\t\t
