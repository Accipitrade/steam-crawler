So lets start.

Pros:

A variety of calls, as well as "911 Calls" that you can accept, and then talk to the caller, making the gameplay quite immersive.
A good designed map, fitting to the game design and the modern technology of dispatchers.
Variety of throughout carreer events, making playing through the game even more fun
A free play mode, where you can play as much as you want, etc.
A carreer mode with carreer events, including negotiations, unique dialogues, etc.
A good game to for one, make your boredom go away, or waste a bit of time with a good and fun game!

Cons (here I would like to say, that I legit only have one point for "cons" for the game):
The ad for other games that you guys made, looks nice and all, however is a bit annoying, it would be great if there would be some sort of "Don't show up on startup" button.

Suggestions: Possibly add a S.W.A.T. vehicle, with SWAT officers to respond to f.e. armed robberies, or hostage situations, would make the gameplay even more immersive.

What can I say in the end? The game, is great, one of my favorite coordinating police, fire department and ems games of all time (after the Emergency games). I can only reccomend buying it, it is perfect to blow away time, or have fun adding modded callouts to the game, that make stuff really immersive. Thanks to the developers of this game! \r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I like this game, and I think it's well worth it if you can pick it up for $10 or so.

The Good:
- Very interesting concept and good execution. I think developers should get a lot of credit for creativity and uniqueness. The interface is pretty simple on the PC. You also don't have to micromanage, which is a problem with some other games of this genre. The calls pop up fast enough, and often enough, to generate some real tension.
- I know there are some complaints on other sites about the voice acting, but I actually think the acting is pretty good, and helps immerse you in the scenarios you're playing.
- The game also makes its point pretty well, namely that emergency services management is hard. You also start to feel for 911 operators: you've got shots fired on one end of the city, you're trying to deploy reinforcements, and meanwhile somebody is complaining about a loud party that might be either a bunch of annoying kids, or a drug deal that's about to burst into violence.
- Each city has some unique challenges, like dealing with pythons in Miami or a blizzard in Anchorage. Your gameplay does vary based on where you are, at least in the career mode.
- The game runs pretty well on my old laptop, which mitigates some things I don't like as much about the game.

The not-so-good:
- I'm not sure how much good equipping my people does for me, though I know it does *something* (SAR with a search dog on a ground unit does in fact speed up the search).
- While it's really cool to have a road map of all kinds of world cities (including my hometown! And the fire and police stations are correctly placed!), you sometimes see units doing silly things like going the wrong way down an expressway entrance ramp. The localization to the US is also slightly off in that the address numbers don't really correspond to the cities, and never go above about 200. Now, in Poland, where the game was developed, address numbers very rarely go above 200, but in the states, addresses like "9250 S. Figueroa" are common. I'm also noticing that the missions seem to be generated randomly, rather than clustering where they would in real life: for example, on the Chicago map, my instinct is to cluster my units on the South and West sides, but this doesn't make sense in-game. It sort of takes away some of the immersion factor.
- The game does feel like it can get a bit repetitive. On the other hand, I'm sure actually being a 911 operator can get a bit repetitive.

Overall, though, I'm very happy with this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood:
- A unique concept to a strategy game
- Educational content, on top of being fun and relaxing
- Audio recordings for each of the incidents

Bad:
- A few annoying design issues on the UI
- Game-play customization is insufficient

Unique strategy game with surprises

As a real-time strategy game, 911 Operator has an unique theme of answering emergency calls and dispatching units. Picking the correct response to a caller, in no more than a few seconds, can be challenging. Not only that, I learnt some knowledge about what to do and what not to do in emergency situations as well. It is rare that a game is both fun and helpful in real life.

The game has audio recordings of all the conversions, and it will play differently depending to how the player chooses to respond. This part is done pretty well.

A ran the "single city mode" as my first career. The game really gives you unexpected scenarios that requires quick decisions on the fly. Just as I thought the events start becoming routine, BAM, unusual caller that makes you hesitate how to respond. BAM major city catastrophe that overwhelms all your units.

Dispatching units, in the simplest sense, is giving orders to units on a 2D map. Units have different capabilities, costs and speeds; fairly standard stuff in 2D strategy games.

Poor-designed UI almost ruin the game
My first impression of the game is great. However I progressed along, the more frustrated I got with the UI. It is not game-breaking, but annoying enough that I considered abandoning the game.

The biggest problem is icons overlap each other. At higher difficulty levels / later chapters, this can be a major problem as incidents are closer to each other on the map, and you need multiple units to respond to each of them. It can be almost impossible to select a unit and issue orders. High-density maps (maps where streets are very short and very close to each other) are even worse.

The background sound of the "music festival" and "riot" missions are freaking annoying. The sounds are supposed to be awful in the game, which is fine; however the player should not be tortured for the entire duration of the game play. It is so bad that I literally turn off my speakers and pray the game is over during these missions. It is overlooked by the developers when a simple "disable background sound" option would do.

There are other minor problems about the UI as well. For example the "exit" button on the main menu is quite hard to notice. The options page cannot be entered while in-game. The game would automatically speed up time compression during a call.

Get it on a Steam sale
Overall I'm really torn between "Recommend" and "Not recommended". For the first 5~8 hours it is a solid yes. Then as the game progresses, my view of it falls more than more into the negative area.

It should also be noted that this game does not have much replay value. Once you've experienced all the calls (took me ~24 hours of game play), you know that's coming. However, given that I bought the entire collection (all DLCs) on a steam sale, it is easily worth what I paid.\t\t\t\t\t
