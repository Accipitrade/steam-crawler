\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a semi-casual, map-based management game. Calls come in, you direct the appropriate emergency service to respond.

Though somewhat "casual" in nature, it's not always easy, and sometimes can be fiendishly hard. Hard, but not ridiculous.

You can play through major US cities, each one with
1) Unique storylines (during one of the four or five duty days some catastrophe will happen that is relevant to that city. Some of these catastrophes are obvious, but I won't give examples because spoilers.
2) Daily routine of a dispatcher (sometimes you get city specific catastrophies, but not usually.)

One of the VERY intesting aspects of the game is the ability to play any may, anywhere in the US. The game downloads from google maps, and usually the locations of hospitals, police stations and fire houses are accuract (to incluse my little hometown in nowhere, Texas.) This is actually a lot of fun.

Their is a fairly large bank of pre-recorded 911 calls; you have to choose the right way to answer the call, and how to deploy your forces in light of the call...some calls are frivilous, some are callers that misuse the 911 service out of ignorance, and many you can talk through the emergency (stove is on fire, what do I do?). Wrong answers result in loss of repuation points. Some of the calls are hilarious. Some of them can be soul-ripping.

Reputation points are "grades" on how well you handled each call, and allow you to move on to bigger cities with their own unique story.

Money is earned through calls answered, fines given (sometimes a call won't do much for your repuation, but it might get you money). Money purchases staff (they level up on their own), equipment to help your staff do the job better, and vehicles (anything from police horses, to paramedic bicycles to helicopters.

I've derived a LOT of fun and recreation from this game. The small Polish developer listens to their fans and occasionally updates the game in response.

It's cheap, it's casual, but doesn't feel like either.

Recommendation: Buy, even if not on sale. It's worth it.
