\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was a 911 operator/dispatcher for 5 years.. here is my take so far:


1. Unless you are a really small agency, you general don't work both the radio and phones. I think they should either do away with the call-taking part (as it is the most unpolished), or allow you to choose one OR the other. However, the call-taking part would need quite a bit of work. Sometimes it takes asking several questions before giving you the ability to ask WHERE they are... the location is always the first and most important question. In fact, most agencies have changed "911 what is your emergency to "911 WHERE is your emergency?" for this very reason. I also don't understand the call tracing thing... the location is either immediately available or it is not. In reality there is no "tracing" calls and waiting.

2. On the GUI there is a disconnect between the incidents and units assigned to them. "On way " is not enough information. I need to know at a glance what units are assigned to what incidents and/or their status. If a police unit calls for help, I shouldn't have to track down which one and where. It should be obvious immediately who needs help, because everyone and their mom will be responding. In real-life I could tell just by the officers voice.. in the game, there isn't this luxury, so it needs to be much more apparent on the GUI.

3. It would be nice to be able to create districts and assign units to them. Emergency units cover zones in real life - not an entire city, aimlessly patrolling. For giggles, I played using the very city I used to dispatch at and the units available were enormously underwhelming, but there was nothing I could do about it except not respond to some calls... which in real life would never ever happen. Even if an agency didn't have enough available units, they would be aided by neighboring jurisdictions.

4. I've never had to assign officers guns... or any emergency unit gear. This aspect of the game is silly in my opinion. If you want to add something in its place, add the ablity for the dispatcher to "code" the calls.. i.e. set a priority that dictates the response (lights and sirens or non-emergency).

5. If someone calls 911 - someone is knocking on their door - period. I don't care if its the kid calling about his math problem... or someone trying to order a pizza. There is a reason for this. Someone could be intentionally concealing their efforts to get help. That doesn't mean they’re coming lights and sirens - but they ARE coming. Even a 911 hang-up is going to be dispatched as an unverified 911 call. You don't know why they had to hang up. In this game however, you get penalized for sending someone.

ANYWAYS... I only played for a few minutes, but that's some of the things that popped out me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun little game. Helps loads determining how Emergency Services function, especially since I can download the town I'm operating in and play there.

Only issue I've had is the game crashes now and then. I get a nice little crash log, dated and filled with all sorts of helpful repair information for the developers. I even get a message asking me to kindly send it in so they can fix issues.
Except I have no idea how to send them the error logs. They neglect to give you this gem of info.

But overall, a fun time killer for when you feel more like saving lives for a change.

Update: With some of the recent changes to the game, it seems as if the Devs have actually broken their own game. The Single City "campaign" no longer works beyond the initial day, and you can't move to the roster screen after reviewing your performance. I have to alt-tab out and shut the game off to do anything at all.

Unfortunately, in this state, despite how much I previously enjoyed this game, I can't recommend it any longer.\t\t\t\t\t
