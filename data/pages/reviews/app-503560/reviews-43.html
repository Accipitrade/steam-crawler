\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got it on sale for under $5 but it's not worth it at its full price. The gameplay gets somewhat repetitive but since you're always managing events it doesnt get too stale. It is pretty cool that you can play on any city in the world but don't expect the addresses for the calls to be real addresses (not really a big deal admittedly) The main problem is the interface and money system.

Events happen two different ways: a 911 call from someone that you have to listen to and ask questions, and random pop up "incidents" that automatically tell you the nature of the emergency. The problem is that while you are "on the phone" with the caller, you cannot view any incidents that pop up or are currently active. Time is slowed down and you are still able to send vehicles to a point on the map but you aren't able to multitask the call with a pop-up incident. It gets overwhelming as games go on and calls start to pile up.

There are some moments where multiple calls (anywhere from 2 to 5-6) stack on the same location. You have to click the icon in order to send units to respond. The problem is that you can only see one icon when they're stacked like that. Incidents will pile up and you won't be able to send units because you aren't able to click on the individual icons because they're overlapping. It's already overwhelming when you're trying to manage a ton of calls but it's exacerbated when you're unable to see if you're actually dispatching units to the call in the first place.

Another problem is that there are two types of fire incidents: fires and technical calls. You can't tell which is which unless you click on the incident and sending the wrong truck to the call causes a slower resolution time. Not a big deal at first but when there's a ton of calls going on you dont want to have to slow down and click each event to check.

The solution to an overwhelming amount of incidents is too buy more vehicles but the money system is very slow. Fire trucks and ambulances are expensive and it takes a little too long to get the money to buy another vehicle.\t\t\t\t\t
