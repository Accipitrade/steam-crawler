\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game, and the idea. You are a 911 dispatcher, you have a map of the city in front of you, and on it you can see the police cars, fire trucks, and ambulances. When 911 call comes in, you send whatever is needed to given location. Sometimes you need only cops (speeding for example), sometimes its cops + ambulance (shooting, injured people), sometimes all 3 need to be at the scene (car crash). You need to buy equipment to your squads, you can employ new people, you can buy them new vehicles, and make more teams present out there on the map. The more of everything you have, the more emergencies you can solve faster.

For every emergency solved you get money. With money you buy additional equipment and cars and so on. So you can buy also helicopters, faster cars, motorcycles, faster ambulances.... There is also a huge amount of different cities you can play in. There is also a part where people will call with some emergency and your instant help is needed on the phone, help them cool down, help them properly handle a grandfather having a stroke, or talk them through how to use a fire extinguisher.

The thing that is missing in this game is Career mode, where you start as a newbie and move through smaller cities to bigger cities and make a name for yourself. Without that you have a number of cities without any order, you jump in between them randomly and finish given tasks. It is good, but it misses something to connect everything into a huge single player campaign that would be this career mode. That would move this game instantly to another level.\t\t\t\t\t
