A fun game

A review of the game with all DLC

This game caught my attention a little while ago, when it went on sale for 1.50 I knew I couldn't miss the chance. Loved it, exactly what I was expecting from a game called 911 operator. For the price you really can't go wrong.

It starts with simple missions but once you get settled things start getting hectic, you answer all kinds of calls, from a cat stuck on a tree to earthquakes (crazy difficulty) and riots. Sometimes a festival or a special event is happening in the city and you're tasked to ensure its safety as well. You can choose to ignore some calls during the day if you think it's not an emergency, waste of time or a prank, specially when your personnel is overwhelmed.

You control the police force, the paramedics and the firefighters, each of them have their own equipments, vehicles, set of skills and specific calls, some calls will require all three on the scene. You use your money to hire more workers, buy extra vehicles and new equipment , with their own particular perks. You start with little money and only a handful of workers so it's pretty tough. When your shift begins you manage your units, what they're gonna be using and where they're gonna be stationed, some maps are massive, you can choose almost any city in the world, so it's wise to position your units well. As the day goes by you start getting calls and it's up to you how you want to proceed, you're given multiple options when answering them. One call can have different endings depending on how you approach it, how many personnel you send and what kind of equipment they're carrying, you can even try to help some callers by giving them first aid instructions or advice, that can go wrong pretty easily though. During your shift you can listen to the comms of your units, giving you hints on how a call is being handled, requesting backup, transport or paramedics.

The only issue here is that after a while it gets super repetitive, the cities in career mode feature unique calls but they're just a few, around 6 per city and they're not that unique or special, usually pretty quick. There are not many equipment options and the vehicles, while there's a considerable variety you won't be needing all of them, most times using the same old combo.

This game can be rough, overwhelming you with stuff to accomplish and in higher difficulties it can be unforgiven but once you get things sorted out, with the exception of disasters(total chaos) and special events, get a decent amount of competent workers and equipment you're settled.

I recommend buying all the dlc with the game, they add more equipments and introduce new mechanics giving it a longer life span.\t\t\t\t\t
