\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought the game because it was a steam sale and I was looking for something easy and relaxing to play that I didn't have to stress to much over. This game did just that, it was relaxing and enjoyable. You choose your city (just about any city in the world and it will download a map for it), you place your emergency services where you want them to be, and then like most games of this style you can pause when an event comes up and decide what to send and how important the event is.

The graphics were basic and that was ok with me, it was non cluttering (I'm guessing as you progress and get more and more vehicles and events it could get a little crazy).

Now, I've only played this game for a little over 2 hours and I'm already writing a review and why is that? Well, the 911 calls get pretty repetitious. There is a limited amount of them and you get them over and over in the same voices, no matter what city or anything. It doesn't take long to know which calls to ignore and which ones to take and what you need to say for each one. This caused it to become stale fairly quickly for me. This doesn't mean I'm done with the game, it just means I can't see myself playing it for 6 or 8 hours in one session.

Overall I can recommend this game if you can get it for under $10 it will be a time filler for me here and there but won't be my go to game.\t\t\t\t\t
