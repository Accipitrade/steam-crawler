\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I purchased this game, the only reason for doing so is because I have family surrounded in dispatching. I was not expecting this to be a good game by any means, and fully intended on playing for ten minutes and never coming back again. 911 Operator took me by surprise though, this game was surprisingly cheap (to me) as well.

Once loading up the game, it's pure chaos. There's a couple of things going on to the right side of you, a couple to the left, and a giant map in the middle with a bunch of different icons. 911 Operator did an amazing job with this though, they clearly organized everything that needed to be organized, and gave you almost complete control over everything.

My first ten minutes in this game was a complete disaster. I ignored a call on accident where a guy fell off of a ladder and impaled himself with a knife, and I accidentally sent a fire truck to a robbery scene. Needless to say, it wasn't pretty. But after understanding the controls, and getting used to the game's mechanics, it all fell into place. Just like that, I was gaining loads of money, and keeping the cities safe. 911 Operator does an amazing job at going from zero calls an hour to chaos in a second. You can be sitting there, sending paramedics to a seizure call, and all of a second, have two robberies and a DUI. My point being, they depict the life of a dispatcher pretty well, for a somewhat small game with a very low price.

The calls can be pretty brutal at times. I answered the phone one time, and a middle aged man, was confessing to murdering his wife. I'm not one for fully throwing myself into these types of games, but this call (and some related) easily gave me goosebumps. You also get the why am I playing this game call here and there, where someone will call and ask for help with homework, or to try and order carryout. But as some may know, this happens in the everyday life of a dispatcher.

I've only noticed a few flaws in the game, within my first few hours of playing 911 Operator. For instance, for whatever reason the most frequent call I'll get, is a cat stuck up in a tree. I've also noticed an overwhelming number of repeat calls, only within the first 30-45 minutes of playing (same voice actor calls in about the same issue). The last key issue I've noticed, is in the menus, where all of the faces, seem to be almost the exact same. Whenever I'm creating a unit, 3/5 times I can have a unit full of the same looking people, but with different names.

To end this review off, I love it. 911 Operator does a fantastic job with immersion, and creating a small look into the day-to-day life of a dispatcher. The game is fun, and also intense at times, but also depends on the type of person you are. Your choices in the game can effect everything, with either positive or negative outcomes. In the paragraph above this, I pointed out my personal flaws with the game. Although these flaws have made me reconsider continuing this game, I'm still planning on spending many more hours in this beautiful game, and I would still strongly suggest others to give it a shot! Thanks for your time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very nice take on the emergency services simulation, simplified enough so no need to do utter micromanagement. And I like the graphics.
But it's a little repetitive as a whole. There aren't that many different situations one has to respond to. It's always the same dialogues over and over again. A little more variety would be nice.
The game does shake it up with some special events which are nice variations.

What I like is that the game does give you an option to play your home city if your location is detected. That's a nice detail.

The equipment selection is little annoying, each rescue service person can have two items so for example a police officer can have a gun and a taser but not body armor equipped at the same time.

Also I ran into a bug where I couldn't finish the mission because for some reason the ambulances wouldn't transport the patient from the last job. I kept assigning all my units to that location but nothing helped.

With a little more variety this would be a really great game. Now it feels a bit tedious when it's the same few jobs that are being repeated. But it's a nice little game especially considering the price.\t\t\t\t\t
