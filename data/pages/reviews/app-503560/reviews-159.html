\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game with a very basic concept, to get units out as a dispatcher. Although simple and at times repeating cases (you can expect what to send after a couple hours of gameplay), I don't find it boring, and getting sort of hooked. Bought all the DLCs in a bundle during the sale, and comparing this to the paid mobile version, gives me a lot more variety and intriguing callouts. Also, being able to play this at my home city where I operate as an EMT gives out a very warm and familiar feel.

However, the interface needs a major rework. If you'd apply what you get onto a mobile device, yes, it would be impressive. But for a PC, this is too simple. Hotkeys are missing, no option to group units together, and the game window looks like it's built out of an emulator, with no full screen option (I use a LG 29" screen with a resolution of 2560x1080, but the highest in game resolution is 1680x1080). Also, when completing certain calls that gives zero Reputation - in game exp thingy- (like getting that annoying cat off the tree), Efficiency percentage is affected, no matter if you completed the call. I get a bit wound up by that because I think I deserve the credit for making that effort to send an engine there. Locations of calls should also be more ramdomized, as I can have a different call at the exact same spot where my fire truck last responded.

Other than that, I've played this game straight for 2 hours after buying it, and I'm pretty sure I will keep on racking more hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rarely write reviews but need to comment on this.

I'm refunding this title because although the concept is excellent, and so much of the implementation has promise, in a twitchy real-time strategy game (which this is) the user interface is CRITICAL. And they've got that completely wrong.

You can't group units to select them via hotkeys later, the descriptions of what each unit is doing soon become unwieldy when you have a lot of units, it's not clear enough (at all) when units are free from their tasks, police officers report "under fire, need backup" even when they're armed with pistols taking on thugs with clubs and outnumber them 2 to 1, it's very hard to select the right unit when multiple ones are at a location, WHICH unit you have selected also isn't clear at all, etc. The list goes on.

It's a real shame as there is the core of a great idea here.

The developers might also like to consider that while the "download your city" idea is good, simply allowing you to do that doesn't localise the game (american accents and "911 what's your emergency" in London just doesn't scan). While I'm not expecting them to localise to every market, the major markets for this game do have different emergency procedures. Police with guns patrolling London? We do have some pretty serious hardware out there in the armed response vehicles (much more serious than that in the average US patrol car), but most police officers here remain unarmed. But this is a non-core compliant and just something to think about for some DLC.

Overall the game was a good concept, well executed in theme but poorly executed in user interface and some of the logic around it. I didn't have the patience to continue - YMMV.\t\t\t\t\t
