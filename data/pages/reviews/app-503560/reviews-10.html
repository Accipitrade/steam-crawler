\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you want this game to be as realistic (*as it can be) you should play it on the harder settings. But I recommend you play easy first to get used to how things work and gain confidence. Then maybe even play around with the call editor.

I got the hang of it pretty quick, it's pretty straight forward, triage is key for efficiency.

It's good training for handling certain pressures, like multitasking but there are real-world factors this game and none have been able to really simulate, for example, emergency line transfers when a console/workstation goes down/slows/needs maintenance (team workflow), strong personalities that have advantages to working well with, typing speeds....

As the game stands, it's worth what I paid. In the future, this game could have a dlc for the police side, they have their own command center irl. Which deals with SWAT, surveillance/tracking via street lightpost cams, gang units etc, more involved with military, homeland etc. It would be cool to see that implemented for MULTIPLAYER, someone who can monitor cams for tracking etc (sort of like in SCP, randomly assigned, or maybe you can choose).
Even an option to initiate investigations on individuals for corruption within agencies by reaching out to certain entities (as side missions for lunch break>?), dispatch moral (not just city rep). Hiring a Database Admin/Fiber Infrastructure for like 50k to expand your network with other districts to unlock certain percs would be neat to see. But at this point you might as well make it an RTS, and move away from realism, which would still be fun to see.
