\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has had a lot of effort put into it. The concept is very interesting, kinda like a weird twist on an RTS. As a fan of strategy games, I was instantly drawn into it.

PROS:
- Unique concept: This game is different compared to other strategy games I have played. Here's the basic gist. Before you start a duty, you'll customize your units a little bit. Each unit has pros and cons, so thinking about which one to use, especially later during the game, is part of the challenge. (Ex. police cars and police motorcycles. Motorcycles are faster and are good for dealing with speeding or other traffic offenses you get notified for. However, they aren't able to transport offenders in cases that your officers arrest the offender. Police cars, like motorcycles, have two officers and are a little slower, however, they are able to transport up to 2 offenders in cases of arrest. This is where the challenge comes in. You must decide which unit to dispatch to certain calls. If you know the situation is going to end in an arrest, such as a DUI, responding with a police car is mandatory.) You can also customize what your units carry as well, with DLC's adding more variety to the choices. When the duty starts, you decide the starting positions of your units and then your shift starts and the madness begins.
- Challenge: The game sometimes feels easy, and then you'll get hit with an earthquake and then you're scrambling your units to respond to all the calls that are now coming in. This is on normal difficulty. It gets noticeably harder as you progress through the difficulties, but there is enough of a challenge to keep the game engaging.
- Scenarios: The scenarios in the career mode are fun in a one-time playthrough. Earthquakes, blizzards, riots, etc. All of them add a bit more chaos to the game. However, once you've played through the career mode, there's no sense in playing through it again, since it's mostly scripted.
- Misc.: The game also has some fun little things thrown in as well, such as being able to play in your hometown (Yes, you can do that) or getting a call from Barney Stinson running a play. Not to mention you can mess around and have a sense of humor. ("My daughter is out of control!" Operator: "What do you want us to do ma'am..... shoot her?")

CONS:
- Repetitiveness: If you read through the reviews, this is something you will see in most reviews, as it is, hands down, the game's biggest problem. Unfortunately, it's not something that will be addressed in this game anymore, as they are now working on a sequel, 112 operator. Because of this, it does get annoying after you've beaten the career and are playing city runs and free play. After a while, you kinda memorize the correct responses to these people and it eliminates the challenge of the phone calls. I've accepted that this won't be fixed in this game, but I look forward to how the sequel handles this, as it has to be on the developers' minds.
- Replayability: Now, this isn't a total con, as the game does have a replayability factor, as calls are in different locations and it is still fun to dispatch your units around and trying to handle the challenge of responding to every incident. However, if you look at hours played by people, very few have over 20 hours of playtime. This goes back to the prior con. While the game's concept and its challenges are fun, after 15 hours of play, it gets really obnoxious to listen to the same woman calling about her kitchen being on fire or the guy who has a stomach ache (I don't care about your hangover dude. Stop calling me!).
- Bugs: Bugs are very minor in this game, I rarely see them but they do happen. It can be annoying when you're trying to get 100% efficiency and then you get a cat in a tree that your firefighters can't get to. Overall, however, not a big problem.

CONCENSUS:
