\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot the "funnest" game in the world, but this doesn't seem built for it. You get so immersed in the game that you start noticing the "normal" emergencies of fighting and heart attacks that at times you almost hope the next call is for a fire because your ambulances and police units are tied up for a while and might not make it in time if another emergency requires medical or police intervention.

You have to manage how many people are on the scene that need medical attention. Should you send an ambulance or a larger unit? Is it important enough to send the helicopter now? Should I send the cops on motorcycles to a scene knowing a chance of them needing heavier backup exists? Do I have enough fire and technical specialty vehicles in case half the city suddenly needs them?

And then the situational calls come in. The calls of someone's daughter being long dead from an overdose, breaking down while talking to you; a man thinking the ambulance won't get to him in time, telling you to tell his fiance he died thinking about her; a hostage who has to be creative when calling, her captors in the room with her; and a nurse at a senior-citizen facility, begging you to send help when the power is out because the people there won't last long without it.

The voice acting at times could be better, but when it hits you, it hits you hard. And then there's the calls from idiots who accidentally butt-dial 911 to break the tension. My favorite is a small child calling for help with homework.

You need the DLC, which is a major con. The price should just be upped more and have the DLC included, but oh well.\t\t\t\t\t
