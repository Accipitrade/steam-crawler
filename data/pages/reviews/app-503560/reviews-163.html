\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI believe 12 hours should definitely suffice to objectively review this game in its whole, because there is not much to experince apart from what you expect. Which is not a negative thing!

The voicing of the callers and of your charakter are pretty good, the calls are authentic and it can get pretty stressful after you invested some time. Graphics really do not matter but what you see is smooth and well-made.

Also, 911 Operator has quite some listening and planning depth in it. However, if you are not the guy who likes planning that much, you should still be fine.
The "story mode" is pretty good, but do not expect a AAA stroy line. Do not expect a story line at all for that matter.
You can download your home town if you are not living in buttcrap nowhere to save some guys in your neighborhood. Naturally the game does not cover the whole city if you are living in a larger town, like me in Berlin. So don't be sad, like I first was when I could just save some guys like a minimum of 5km from my house. But that does not affect the gameplay, after all it is just some streetnames and the map layout.

It is getting kind of repetitve pretty fast. You know how to answer calls as soon as you hear the voice. The cases get mixed-up a little but after some rounds you pretty much know what is going on. However, I dont't think you should expect any more, and after all there are enough cases there is enough to take care of.
A big plus is the Workshop compatibility and the modders are doing a great job! From new vehicles to fully voiced calls, you can find a lot of mods to refresh your experience!
I also recommend playing it with headphones to get more into the zone.

All in all I highly recommend this game if you are somewhat into the "dispatchers life" or into first responders in general.
And even if you are not, the game holds a lot of good content for a small budget.
I loved the first 8 hours playing it straight and still like to come back for a few rounds every other week.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t911 Operator might be considered part of the simulation genre.

You play a dispatcher at an emergency phone line (such as 911, 112, etc.) in a city. Your main view is a map with all the available units (police, medics and fire departement) and the incidents.
Those incidents pop up either randomly and without a phone call, or you actually "receive" a phone call and puzzle yourself through a dialog with either may be more successful or less so. In the end they also result in an incident marked on the map where you can dispatch units to,
At the end of each shift (takes about 15 minutes real time, give or take) you receive a summary of resolved (or not resolved) cases and points...and more money. With this money you can buy more equipment, new vehicules and more staff.
The staff also ranks up which apparently should make them faster and better at their respective job.
There is a career mode, some challenge modes and a free mode.
The city you are dispatcher of can be choosen either from a list (some challenge modes and career modes choose the city for you) or you may try to find your own city by searching (it appears to be working pretty good).

Now, the game is OK.
It is not bad, but also not amazing.
The calls and incidents are getting repetitive rather quickly, only with some occasional "big" event.
In my opinion the base game is not worth more than the 15 Euro I paid, I would actually recommend to wait for a sale.
If you are into these kind of things (dispatching units, trying to organise things even when stressful, dont mind the repetivness) then why not...go ahead.

There are quite a few DLCs available, which suppose to make the game more fun and more diverse. I cannot comment on them as I have not bought them (I personally can't imagine they offer that much more options which will not be repetitive at some point, but then again, I haven't tried).

I have not encountered any game breaking bugs, or actually that many bugs at all (it crashed maybe 3 times in 15h or so and there are some units stuck for a moment at some times).

So overall, I cannot say its a bad game and not recommmend it. However, be warned, it may not be for everyones taste and considering the price I'd suggest to watch a few "Let's Play" and such before buying full price.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved the concept of the game and it could have been great but it is way too short and had lot of in game mechanic issues. It's such a great concept with really good visuals and calls and story line: PLEASE FIX THESE for 112. This has so much potential. RIght now though I would not recommend you buy 911 Operator for more than 10$ (all DLCs included): not worth it.
7hours and you're done. That's including all DLCs.
Career mode should not let you progress to the next city if you fail a mission. Missions should be: you try a level, if you fail you get to do it again. Until you succeed. There you try a level and then whether you win o fail you just move on to another level.
Lots of things are not explained in the tutorial (for ex that the little squares above the vehicles mean = they indicate how many transport slots are available for suspects or victims).
The mechanics are clunky: you should be able to send all units of a particular type (like all police, or all firefighters) with one button.. Instead you can just send all units of everything you have (fire, police medical) or just one by one.
YOu should also be able to select multiple units and send them all at once (so you would gather police and medical together and send them as a unit to someplace)
NOt being able to see who's in your unit or rename units on the game dashboard is a big problem: I might have a K9 unit that the game will name unit 3 and a unit with specialed medical equipment unit 15 etc.. but unless you write down someplace the number of that unit there is nothing on the screen or any of the in game control tabs to let you know which unit is which.
Units should be given a combined score for driving, shooting, medical etc... based on who and what you equip them with. Other wise you are left doing the math looking at each individual and equipement etc...
Finally the whole drag and drop mechanics for equiping your characters is exhausting when you have to do that 15 to 32 times once you get a good sized force. Select the equipement then select the character you want to equip. That's how it should work.
Pro tip: Keep loads of cash on hand and just add equiped reinforcements. Much cheaper than buying stuff in the menu (makes no sense)
So disapointing over all.\t\t\t\t\t
