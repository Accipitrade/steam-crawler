\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis isn't a bad game by any means. It's not a "big"game, meaning that it's not something you can play for hundreds of hours. It's more like a small gem that should rightfully find its way into a gamer's portfolio. For the price of a movie, it provides far more entertainment and interaction. The full price is a bit steep, but on sale it's more than worthwhile.

PROS:
-- Informative and didactic: Teaches you about the challenges of managing emergency services. Having worked as a paramedic myself, I truly appreciate the operator's perspective.
-- Critics said it's basically a color-matching game: Send police to blue, medics to white, and firefighters to red. It's much more than that. You have to use common sense to evaluate the situation and react accordingly. Specifics matter.
-- Chilled: While the cases you're confronted with can be harrowing, it's still a chilled game that helps you relax. You're in the operator's seat, which means you're in charge. It gives you a top-down perspective that can be very gratifying, giving you a sense of control. And that's what we all seek in video games, isn't it? A sense of control.
-- Decent progression: Buying gear, vehicles, and staff is decently matched to rising, ever-increasing challenges.

CONS:
-- Limited replay value
-- Somewhat repetitive after a while (emergency calls)
-- Unfulfilled potential: The game can be fleshed out quite a bit more. But the developers started on a budget of about $40,000, so what miracles can you expect?

All in all, I'd like to commend the Polish developers for a truly engaging game that exceeded my expectations. Keep up the good work. Cheers.\t\t\t\t\t
