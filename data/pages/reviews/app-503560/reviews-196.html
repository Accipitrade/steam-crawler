\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore buying this game I wanted to say this is based on what 911 operators do and what they have to deal with. Otherwise...

Explaination:
You are the sole operator of an entire city, starting out with a couple of units to command. You have 3 types of units: Police, Medical, and Firefighters. Each unit has a specific role and you may customize the speed and ability each unit has. Such as if you want more technical Firefighters you buy a technical firetruck and give them the equipment for faster speed in situations that call for technical assistance ex. cut cable in traffic, car crashes eta.

Pros:
-It's very casual, and based on your judgement.
-The phone calls are sometimes hilarious, and I feel they might have taken actual calls out to 911.
-It's not too traumatizing, if there were a level of extreme phone calls with 10 being the highest I would say the most extreme I have found is a 6.
-The ability to specify teams to certain task in the game, which is really helpful.
-Can play in your own town, I found this part neat!

Cons:
-After sometime the calls become repetitive
-Not for someone who expects alot. Very few objects in the game, very little in certain areas of the game.
-Definitely not for hardcore people or people who expect a progressive storyline. Save you the trouble there is no story, it's just a 911 operator simulator, without the normal game progression of unlocking anything new only earning more cash until you have a productive team for really busy maps.

Conclusion:
It's a 911 operator simulator with some cars, people, and customization ability. It is very casual and just a game you can relax and learn how 911 operators are supposed to deal with situations. Don't expect a whole hell of alot out of this, if it's too little in content maybe wait for a sale one day. But the game is worth it for people who just like non complicated relaxed strategy simulators.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have mixed feelings about this game.

Pros :
_ The tech itself is great. Watching units navigating maps downloaded from the internet is impressive and it must have taken some time to get it to work.
_ The presentation is top of the line, with a great sound track and immersive dialogues.
_ The gameplay is simple and fun.
_ Live calls are thrilling.

Cons :
_ I've found the management aspect quite superficial.
_ The gameplay is repetitive and shallow. You can park units anywhere anytime, so the game is just about spreading your units and compensating busy ones with available units.
_ The live calls lasted 13 hours, tutorial and potty breaks included. After that the game gets super repetitive...
_ The UI is lacking basic quality of life functions. For instance, you can't simply switch 2 people in a vehicle. You have to put one in reserve, which unequips his stuff, move people around using the free vehicle slot and then reassign the reserve guy.
_ sometimes a unit decides to go off-road and it takes ages to get it back on track. I got this bug 3 times in my first playthrough so... QA, anyone ?
_ using checkpoints to save the game actually wasted 3 hours of my life.
_ the operator concept could have been better exploited.
_ a little bit overpriced in my opinion, especially since replayability is low.

Bottom word, I had 13 hours of fun so thumbs up. Don't buy the special ressource DLC, it's a useless cheat mode.\t\t\t\t\t
