\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not a terrible game, but it's just... flat. At first a very exciting and fun sounding game; I thought it was like This is the Police without the somewhat stressful campaign story. It is not. The management part of the game is pretty shallow. You can buy new vehicles, staff and a small amount of equipment, and you get paid for each day of service plus bonuses for catching certain crimes, and you send your staff out to each call or problem as they arise, but that's about it. When staff get injured, you end up having to hire a replacement... and there's no consequences not to. Salaries for the units feel nonexistant compard to your income, and is of no concern, unlike This is the Police. Failure to respond properly to calls has no real consequences. And you'll play maps over.. and over... with the same exact callers and the same exact encounters over and over. You sort of memorize them after an hour or two--that's how little variety there is.

It would be a fun game to play for free, but I wish I hadn't paid for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm glad I came back to check what's new. Good to see new stuff, new DLC.
The game was fun already, but got a little boring, I guess. With this new DLC I reinstalled and am having even more fun with it than before.

The new events, even though I've only encountered the one so far (Festival), are a great addition. It really makes things more difficult, especially early on when you don't have many funds, let alone teams.
Reinforcements are a welcome addition, especially during Festivals, or just those days when everyone's busy and you desperately need something right this very minute.

The only ♥♥♥♥♥♥ I have is the inability to multi-task. If I'm on a call I can't see the details of other events on the map, that pop up afterwards, until the call is finished. If the event pops before I answer the call then I can send units out, but not during a call. So, unless it's a Festival (where it's not toggleable) I just toggle off answering calls altogether.

Calls can get repetitive, there's not many voicetypes overall. A female Operator would be good as an option, though. There's both male and female unit voices, as well as callers, of course. Often the voice coming from a unit doesn't match the occupants of that vehicle, but oh well.

But, overall it's still quite a fun game. Often fast-paced (if answering calls is switched on) but the game can be sped up or slowed down, even paused while you send out units, if you get too overwhelmed.

Apart from my one little gripe, I quite like it, and am happy there's extra content to further extend the life of the game for me :-)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe kind of game that you can get completely addicted to and play through in 1 session and quickly become somber when you realize the game isn't very long.

The game is absolutely amazing, they nailed the resource management and time management, the difficulty starts out quite easy and quickly goes up in difficulty and starts to stress you out in a little bit at first but once you get the hang of it, it is a good challenge that you should be able to deal with, and if not you can turn the difficulty down(or up) in the settings.

The thing that saddened me most about this game is how short it is in terms of main story content, for me it took 6-7 hours to complete the game with all the DLC, however, you can download tons and tons of maps of cities around the world, and at the start the game tries it's best to detect which city you are playing from and prompts you to download that map.

Honestly, when it comes to DLC I would recommend you buy them all, if it's too much money to spend I recommend you wait for a sale. Without the DLC the game is just too short in my opinion.

The voice acting is absolutely amazing and convincing, emotions are definitely conveyed in the phone calls.

Apart from grammar errors there were no annoying bugs or hiccups.

A game I would recommend anyone to buy, or at least put it on your wishlist and perhaps wait for sale.
I typically dislike management games, but this one was a very good exception to that rule for me.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t911 operator is exactly what the title says. You play as a 911 operator and you have to dispatch different units (Fire, EMS, Police) to different scenes. It gets quite immersive, as you have to actually question the 911 caller and get as much information as possible and then decide which unit would be the best for the situation.

The only thing I didn't like about this game is that you do get quite a few repeat calls, there doesn't seem to be enough variety to keep you playing for hours upon hours. Overall though, It's a fun game!\t\t\t\t\t
