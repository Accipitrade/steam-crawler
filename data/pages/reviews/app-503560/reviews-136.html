\r\n\t\t\t\t\t911 Operator succeeds in creating a hectic strategy game that tests your skills and decision-making abilities, a feat that it unquestionably set out to do. You feel like every choice you make when taking a call is a one that could result in life or death, and it's up to you to be efficient, helpful and professional. You direct emergency services to the necessary locations on a map and must manage the influx of incidents and callers requesting your help. It's stressful, fun, and addictive. Where the game fails, however, is through the repetition. You quickly become familiar with the calls, as there is not a great variety as of the time of writing. The appeal, therefore, does not lie in the situations your callers get into, it lies in how you efficiently manage your units to deal with them and learning the best ways to do this. In addition, the very poor voice acting and same calls every time get old extremely fast, even after the first hour of gameplay. In saying that, however, it did not stop me from enjoying the game overall – it did stop me enjoying it to its full potential. You don't fully believe you are a 911 operator because of the same calls, same dialogue and unrealistic voice acting. There's enough variety and solid problem-solving elements to make this game a worthwhile purchase for many puzzle/strategy fans, however, although I'd maybe only pick it up if it's on sale. Everyone else, you may want to give this one a pass if you want a game with long-lasting appeal.\t\t\t\t\t
