\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had heard about this game online and I had heard that it was a decent game that was good to spend a few hours on. My sister is a big fan of shows like 911 Operator and Chicago Fire, so I figured I could buy this and if it was any good I might show it to her. I had a general idea going in what kind of game this would be, but I was very surprised at how immersive this game is. I became wrapped up into the game, often sitting on the edge of my seat as I tried to keep all my resources balenced. I would sometimes have to make tough calls, forcing me to abandon some disasters in order to focus on other, more pressing matters. I had a lot of fun, and the ability to download any map of any city was absolutely fantastic, letting me download different parts of the world and experiencing what it might be like. The variety of calls was fantastic as well, I was concerned I might exhaust the available pool of calls quickly, but I still am discovering new ones, and I've been told you can download more, which is also great.

However, this game is not without fault. Some of the game relies heavily on RNG, and you can occasionally get a set up to just screw you over, giving you no chance to complete the level with a positive rating. I once played a level that was nothing but Police/Ambulance calls and Ambulance calls, and it was impossible for me to complete most of them before they would fail and I was given a horrible rating. However, when I tried again, I got a perfect score, with everything happening at a managable, yet tense, pace. Its possible to just get screwed over early in a level, leading to your team getting massacered and requiring you to use up your resources to keep yourself afloat, only to be destroyed in the second level because you have no money to hire more workers becasue you had to buy more supplies the previous level. However, I really enjoyed the game and I cannot wait to play more. Its a great game, and I encourage you to check it out, just know that its a difficult game and that you won't be able to relax at a comfortable pace.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all , I've finished this game's career mode (campaign), so I can give some good advice. My review is written below, but before that:
A moderator deleted my post in forum and issued me a warning about a joke he couldnt understand. This is not the problem. It begins here: After I wrote negative review about this game, the developer himself intervened and changed the warning to a permanent forum ban. It appears this so called Jutsu Games or whatever they are, they have no stomach for negative reviews and they are taking things personal. Writing a review doesnt break the rules, the developer is simply abusing his powers. So I suggest dont write negative reviews if you want to use 911 forums :)
Anyway here is my game review for those who are thinking to buy this game anyway:
Game is good for some hours. After that its extremely repetitive. Every day, same people call you again and again. A kitchen counter is on fire, someone ate mushrooms, two people fighting in street etc. After a while this gets boring fast. There are not enough variations. There are many design problems too: Police reports they are under fire and requests reinforcements. You know what? There is no one firing! Only a drunk trying to punch a police! There is no satisfaction solving emergencies. UI is terrible, you cant choose the unit you want on the same spot. You want an ambulance? We have police for you! You want to send the police to the fight? No you can only select and send police to fire event! Often you cant even control time, it gets bugged too often. Too many bugs like this breaks the game and ruins the fun. Campaign has no story etc and its plainly boring. I fell asleep while playing.
My final advice: Play the game only if you absolutely get it free, too bad its too late for me to refund it.
Summary:
Jutsu Games: 0/10
Game : 3/10\t\t\t\t\t

Pros
-Coordinate where the police officers, firefighters, and paramedics go.

-Not a very difficult game.
