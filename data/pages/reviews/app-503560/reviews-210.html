This game has potential, but unfortunately it sounds like they stopped updating it. At its current price, I can't really recommend it. Especially for 15 dollars plus its DLC. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to preface this review with the fact that I had to refund it. This is not due to the fact that the game is bad. It is in fact a very well made game. ingenious even. The refund was requested because while playing it, It evoked a very real feeling of sadness and brought up memories of incidents in my life that I would rather not think about.

For many this game is a perfect blend of casual strategy and sim management, two genres I love.

The Workshop DLC as well as Free and Paid DLC for this game is great as well. I'm not going to go too in depth here as I only played an hour of it before it became evident that I couldn't cope with some of the calls emotionally, but the game is very good. So good in fact that it sucker punched me with a case of the feels while maintaining fun gameplay. That's something a lot of triple A games can't even manage nowadays.

(Another note, you CAN turn off the calls and play it in a more management oriented style but that takes away from a very fluid design I feel is imperitive to playing theg amei n it's entirety. )

To the game devs, I apologize for refunding as you've made something phenomenal here. The fact that It hit me so hard, when I didn't expect a game of this type to even be capable of such a feat is evidence alone that you've trapped lightning in a bottle.

This game skillfully shows the wholesale ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ a 911 operator has to deal with on a daily basis, and somehow gamifys that experience. If there is an indie award for this genre, 911 Operator sorely deserves it.

I love the game, but alas I am going to have to love it from afar.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot too bad...

I've been a dispatcher for quite a while so I wanted to see how well this game mimicked what we really do.

I'm actually pretty impressed.

I go through some of the same emotions I get at work... dealing with a huge incident like a house fire, dead child or an officer-involved shooting, having to answer a 911 in the middle of it and hearing "There's a car parked illegally." Just as frustrating here as it is in real life.

I wish there were more calls I can take. I just hung up with the lady ordering the pizza. Why is she calling again? How many times can this lady get in trouble?!?!? And can't she be more creative? Maybe I can be a chinese restaurant once in a while.

Also, I disagree with some of the decisions and results. For example, the game makes you take noise complaints on 911. I would never do that in real life. You call 911 for a noise complaint and you get referred to non-emergency. Do that in the game and you lose a ton of reputation points.

There are a few bugs too. I sent an ambulance to a guy having a baby and I didn't have the ability to call the media and tell them how amazing I am for helping a guy figure out which body part to take that baby out of.

I do like being able to use any map. The maps are realistic and kind of fun. I used one of the cities I dispatch in. The fire departments were in the right spots. That was trippy. It was crazy seeing calls pop up with addresses I'm used to. Kinda fun.

Overall, it's a fun game. And if you've never dispatched before, it's a fun experience. It's not everything a dispatcher does... I don't think you ever could recreate that... but it highlights a lot of what we do and you get to feel a lot of the same frustrations and experiences.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've only played this game for around six hours, but it's definitely one of those "quick to learn" games.
It's not complex at all, but it does seem to have quite a lot of replayability.

You take the role of a 911 Operator - which should be obvious from the title - but really it's a management game.
You respond to 911 calls in two ways... one, by moving your assets around to visit each on-map incident based on priority and type (fire engines to red icons, police to blue, etc) or by making conversation choices during a phone call to direct the call to a certain outcome.

You do this for a 'shift', or a game day. Then you get to spend the money you earned on buying gear or vehicles, or hiring staff. Gear helps your staff survive the work day, or work faster. Staff means you can take more people to help deal with particular issues, and vehicles mean you can direct more units to incidents.

Graphically it's simple. There's no 3D environment. You've got a monochrome map, and different coloured units. It's pretty basic. Audio is nice enough, particularly the fully voiced phone calls.

The phone calls themselves get old quickly. You soon learn what responses will get you the most points, and can pick them without thinking. There need to be about ten times more of these. I'd be happy to lose the full voice-overs in order to get more variety... but the ones that do exist are pretty cool until you learn them all.

One truly cool feature is the ability to play in your own city or town. I was able to call up my city and play on it. It even put the emergency services bases in more or less the right places. This adds a surprising amount to the game.

Some of the pathfinding is a bit iffy, though it's not yet been bad to the point of ruining the game.

There is a career mode, where you play on pre-defined cities but it doesn't really seem to give you much opportunity to expand your services before you get chucked up the food chain into the next map.

This is a fun game, and one that I'll happily say I'll pop back into every now and then. Running a shift is no great hardship from a duration perspective, so it turns into a nice casual game you can jump in and out of. You can boost the difficulty to get a bit of challenge too, if you need it.

I'll recommend it, happily - but would caution that you check out a Let's Play or two on YouTube to see if it's your cup of tea before spending the money on it. If it's on special, I'd say that it's likely well worth the effort.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this game is fun. Especially for the price. I read some of the other reviews, especially noted was the 911 operator that wrote one. I can see that we all need to say thanks for people like him/her. This game really makes you scramble to put out fires...literally. And, you have to use your resources the best you can to save as many people as possible from injury from fire, or bad guys. Fast reaction and thinking is everything. The only thing that brings this game down from a perfect 10, is that some of the calls are repeticious (sp?) And after just a few "shifts" you start to hear the same calls. I could understand this if the if overall there were more types and quantities of calls, but there just aren't enough. As a matter of fact there are probably only about 30-40 or so different calls you get, yes, some variations on how you react to them each time....but still, the same call. So, for this reason only, I'm dropping my review to 8/10. It's not a 20 buck game, not even a 15 buck game, but a fun game and worth the price if lower.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery well done game. Those who say its repetitive is not actually playing the game. Even when you have a call that looks like the same the reason can be totally different. So no matter how many times you got that call you always need to investigate to see what you have to do. Its the same voice, same first dialogue but that doesn't mean its the same thing. Need to pay attention.

You also get new equipment, new types of vehicles while advancing. The game is really well done. Also don't understand the complains about graphics. Graphics are great. You need to setup graphics when you hit play - select Fantastic not Fastest. Need to learn how to read.

A great feature is that you can download your city to play in. And its really any city in the world, I think it downloads data from google maps. When you do that it also get the real places where police, fire department and hospitals are in that city. So very realistic. I downloaded a very small city that didn't have a hospital, and guess what? When I request ambulance it comes from the neighbour city using the highway, just like IRL.

I recomend it a lot. Oh and also there are translations for a lot of languages in the workshop.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t'911 Operator' takes a simple idea and turns it into an incredibly fun game. You're responsible for all of the police, fire and ambulance resources in a city. You progress through a specific sequence of cities in the campaign mode (ending, understandably, in Washington). However, you are also given the option of downloading almost any town or city map and playing it. Fun features like "Random City" and "City of the Day" are also appreciated when the novelty of overseeing your hometown begins to fade.
When your shift begins, you'll be dealing with a variety of phonecalls and on-screen alerts, and your ability to respond in a timely and competent manner is essential, especially in the campaign.
My only real criticism is that there aren't enough unique phonecalls, as after a couple of hours' gameplay you'll start having the same conversations over and over again. The game remains challenging, regardless of how familiar you may become with it; as a result, I wouldn't say that this really spoils the ability to enjoy the game.
I definitely recommend '911 Operator'. I picked it up at a highly reduced price in one of the weekend sales a few weeks back, but I think it's worth the full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really liked the idea of this game and I enjoyed playing it for about 6 hours until it got very repetitive.

First of all, I love the mechanics of sending out the different emergency services. The phone calls, however, become very disruptive as they take the flow out of the gameplay and become increcibly repetetive and predictable. I wish there was an option to turn the phone calls off in the career mode. Also, I feel there's a lack of (visible) goals to work towards and no specific challenges. The only "goal" they give you is "reputation", but that gets very boring. Besides certain fancier vehicles, they offer no new unlockable content as far as I can see.

Like:
- Idea
- Management mechanics of sending out emergency services

Dislike:
- Repetitive and predictable phone calls that slow the game down
- Not enough goals/achievements to go for, limited "unlockable" content
- No map specific challenges or variations\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is actually a pretty fun game if you know what you're going into. There is a lot of repetition in cases, but that's pretty much to be expected. The probability of different types of crime isn't linear in real life, just like it isn't in the game. So, you'll be getting a lot of calls about stolen cars, domestic issues, or medical incidents. But, thankfully, there is a mode where the game will minimize repeating calls.

Your job is, obviously, to be a 911 operator. Taking calls and directing medical, fire, and police units to various random situations. You're also in charge of purchasing staff and equipment for all three departments. Not realistic, but, from a gameplay standpoint, I can respect and do appreciate that choice. Because of the random nature of the game, a shift may pretty tame and boring. Or, you could have an armed bank heist, terrorist group, and SimCity-style catastrophic events occurring at the same time. This can leave you pretty stressed as you try to direct your units, in real time, to whatever event takes priority at that moment. And the priority of calls can, and often does, change. A simple domestic dispute that you sent one policeman to could turn into an armed shootout with severe injuries. So, managing the number, type, and layout of your units is vital (think SimCity or Cities Skylines emergency services, it's all about coverage areas).

There are flaws with the game, and it's definitely not for everyone. The voice acting, while good, is limited, so you will hear the same verbatim calls over and over again. Some common calls are strangely missing, such as hit-and-runs, restraining order/parole violations, requests for assistance from ongoing non-emergency issues (like a welfare check or pull-over that goes bad). That, or I haven't encountered them yet.

The menus where you assign staff and equipment to vehicles is pretty rough. Moving a police offer from a car into your staff 'pool' removes their items. So, if you go to move that offer back to a different unit, you have to re-assign him his gun and vest. Why? Why aren't these items related to the staff member, rather than the vehicle? It's also hard to manage units as the units themselves show up in a single giant list. It'd be nice if each department had it's own list. It'd also be nice for there to be some kind of tooltip or info-bubble for randomly generated events. Currently, they just pop up on the map, and if you're currently in a call, you can't view the details for that event. An armed bank robbery will look the same as a naked man running through town until you end the call. And, finally, the ability for modding would do this game wonders. I'd love to be able to add my own sound assets to the game (specifically to replace the short, looping background radio chatter with something longer).

There are various other small things such as grammar/spelling mistakes and odd speech localization (nobody says 'fire brigade' or 'rubbish' in the US). But, despite these flaws, I'm still having fun with the game. If this game was more open for modding, I'd give this a definite buy. But, if you're into games like Euro Truck Simulator 2 or Offworld Trading Company, you'll probably enjoy this game.\t\t\t\t\t
