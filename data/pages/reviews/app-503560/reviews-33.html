\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate:
Just after writing this review, I pulled the game back up and played a few more times. I think the game heard my comment about it being "repetitive" and decided to put me in my place. If you think you've got the hang of things, just wait until you get hit with your first catastrophe. The entire screen lit up! Calls were coming in back-to-back, fires were breaking out all over the city, and trying to manage everything was insane. Just as my resources were stretched to the limits, I got a "shots fired" over the radio followed by a guy who is about to die because the power outage caused his medical device to stop working - all the way on the other side of town.

You've got one firetruck available. Do you send it to the house fire that possibly has trapped people, or do you send it to help the older lady that's about to drown in a flooding basement? Your call. You're the 911 operator!

If the stress level continues to ramp up even tighter, then this game is definitely worth the full price.

-------------

This game makes for a very interesting time waster, and if you're not careful, you could easily throw more hours into a single game session than you originally intended. The concept is pretty simple. You have three types of units police, medical, and fire, and your job is to send the correct unit to the correct incident.

Ultimately, the game is about resource management, with you constantly shuffling your units so that they can respond to incidents as quickly as possible. It's a pretty addicting game, but if you play for too long in one sitting, things can begin to feel a bit repetitive. I find myself just coming back every so often to do a few shifts. Being able to play on any city in the world is a nice gimmick, and it was neat being able to download and play my local city.

Finally, although I am enjoying the game so far, I would not recommend buying the game at full price. I was able to get the game during a recent sale for less than five dollars, and my enjoyment and rating of the game is definitely tied to how little I had to pay for it. if you catch it on sale, I'd say pick it up; otherwise, you may feel like you've wasted your money.\t\t\t\t\t
