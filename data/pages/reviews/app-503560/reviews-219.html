\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game, and concept. Its an resource optimization game. Directing all resources at the right time to the right place to save people. This includes also some ethical dilemmas, such as how to prioritize the different cases.

Also very well implemented is the stories/case engine, which causes stories to slightly change or to even provide different variants of stories, which therefore require different decissions to be made.

I really like the idea and concept of educating the player of the game based on real world cases. How to get rid of fires in a kitchen, or how to do CPR. Even though those guidelines might be incomplete or summarized to fit in the game, I think the approach is well made.

It gets more challenging in bigger cities, more cases, more vehicles and longer distances to the different cases.

Another nice idea is to allow the player to pick any city available on open-street-map. That is really unique. It would make this game exponentially greater, if users could create own missions or scenarios (maybe even based on true stories).

There are also some issues that I experienced when I played the game, which I will briefly comment.
  • It is true, that some mission appear to often. If the guy with stomache problems calls the fifth time within 20 minutes gameplay, it already gets annoying ;). It would be cool to have 3 or 4 times more cases, or to allow cases created by a community.
  • Sometimes vehicles get stuck in certain roads/streets, which I think might be a bug or a glitch in the game or the open-street-map data that has been downloaded.
  • In some maps (e.g. san francisco), the position for most new cases is very often near twin peaks, even though, not so many people live there. I think this is wrong threshold or something, and could be easily fixed. Maybe the balance is not set correctly. I think the algorithm should regard the population density in those areas.
  • UI problem: drag&drop items from shop or storage to a staff member: the icon is too big, and is hiding the checkbox. It does only work when you hit the center of the checkbox. Better zoom the icons to the size of the checkbox, when the drag starts.
  • UI problem: Vehicles/Units that are close together are not selectable. Maybe a circular menu or re-arranging the icons would help to solve this.
  • UI problem: It would be good to have hot keys to change the speed of the game.
  • It is not possible to sell unrequired items? or I do not see how it works.
  • I played San Francisco 10 times, but the earthquake happend only once. Is that intended? It would be great if those "likelyhoods" and other values (e.g., cases per minute) could be configured in custom scenarios or in the game options in general.

I hope some of the bugs will be fixed and a lot more real world cases will be added.
After all the developers did a good job! Thank you for that!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought 911 Operator today and found it great fun. Over 7 hours already on career mode and still repeating the first map, increasing my units and kitting out my teams.

I enjoyed playing my home city too. As it's a fairly big city I got access to helicopters and decent firepower from the get go.

The scenarios are varied, but quickly become repetitive. It hasn't ruined the experience yet, but I hope to see a lot more added in the future to keep the game fresh.

Great value for money at 10 but the additional 4 for the DLC seems a strange way to do it. They should have included it in the main game and charged a bit more.

My staff units have received promotions but I would like to see this have a real effect in the game. A promotion and staff stats should increase making them more efficient at handling situations.

As I progressed, I found myself swapping out lower skilled staff for more skilled staff, but don't see anyway of firing unused staff or selling kit that's not being used. That would be a welcome added feature.

Overall very impressed with this little indie game. Looking forward to future updates.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery interesting concept. Although simple I really liked this game, specially the way the creators managed to let you play on ANY town in the world using its real map, addresses and Police stations, Fire Departments and Hospitals locations. The phone calls are also very well done and realistic.
Now the cons. Ididn't play too much yet but two things already caught my attention on a negative way. The first is the fact that when you play a small town the incidents tend to occour on the same area too many times. I understand that smaller towns have less spots to incidents occour but, even though, the extention of the map should be used more realistic and not so limited on a few areas. The other issue is the limited number of phone call recordings. As I said I like them a lot but, in just less then an hour I already asnswered the "Hidden pizza help call" three times, the same guy chopped his leg twice and other few cases also repeated. So, a much bigger phone calls database must be recorded and implemented on the game so this can avoid a quick lack of interest on the game because of the fact you are always answering the exact same calls.
Diversity must be a key factor on this, somehow limited, game, otherwise it's playability will be short.
Keep the excellent work and please keep improving the game so it becomes more and more interesting and fun as time goes by.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNice little game that if I want to have fun and kill a little time. Gameplay consists of dispatching your units to various crimes around your chosen city, patrolling to spot certain offenses and recieving 911 calls, which you can choose to respond to, ignore, or in some cases resolve over the phone without the need for emergency assistance. The challenge comes from having limited resources in the beginning and having to prioritize the right calls and sometimes even take the hit on less important ones.

You can play on seemingly any city/town in the world (the smallest I've tried is an 8,000 population town so I don't know what there is in the way of villages) and your reputation and progress is saved individually on each map. Something I found to be a nice little touch is that even on that small map the stations are in the right places (I live here so I know where they all are). I know a lot of games wouldn't have bothered to even reference that and would just have placed stations around generically. As I'm sure you read in other reviews if you managed to get to mine, the calls can end up becoming a little repetitive as there aren't a huge number of them (although don't take it for granted at the beginning because there are variations each call can take). Hopefully they might put out some more as DLC but in any case if you treat the game the way I do and just play it here and there when you have some time to spare and are bored it's not that much of an issue. May be more of one if you intend to play a large number of hours very regularly.

Overall I'd recommend, it's something I definitely consider worth having in my library and it will likely last me a very long time, even if I won't be clocking up massive hour numbers in it.\t\t\t\t\t
