\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is great if you like management games and don't mind being stressed with tough decisions and occasional disasters.

A quick test to see if you'd like this game is think of the Sims. If you can't stand your Sims doing anything on their own, this is the game for you!

The game play is rather simple at the surface. You are acting as a 911 Operator (shocker I know). You are in charge of micromanaging a city's emergency services (firefighters, medical, and police). As the "duty" goes on, you receive hits from other 911 operators that you must direct the city services to, keep in mind the only service that seems to have any self-motivation is the police and they just drive around in small kind of circles doing their beats. All of the other services will be half a block from an emergency and just sit there looking cute. You will also receive occasional phone calls that you have to choose the course of the conversation with (I think easy mode had 3-4 a duty).

And to make it even better and totally more stressful, there is constant background chatter from the units and it's pretty hard to play this game effectively without any sound. So get used to hearing like five different people making random callouts and learning to listen for the critical callouts from your units that might need help.

Stress: 9/10
Micromanage: 11/10
Operator: 911
Rating: Definitely M (the "F" bomb and "n" word fly pretty freely in this game as well as more mild cursing, AND if you mess up a 911 call badly enough, you'll hear the person you're trying to help die)
Trigger warning: This game definitely deals with realistic scenarios and include scenes/conversations about drugs, suicide, rape, and all of the other bad things out there

P.S. The loading screens are awesome and give you helpful tips that correlate to any real life emergencies you might run into like seizures, kitchen fires, etc.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really did enjoy this game, unfortunately after 10 hours of game play I think I've run out of unique calls and situations. It's still a really fun time management game and really gives you an appreciation for first responders and the variety of situations they have to address. However, the repetition of the phone calls gets a little grating. Another issue that I have with the game is that there are a few instances where the character you play jumps in with a response that I wouldn't have made the character select. For example, in one 911 call, a man says his car has been stolen from his apartment complex. I asked him his address, he gave it, and immediately the 911 operator chimes in "this isn't an emergency, go to your local police department" but like I didn't select that response and nothing from my asking the address would indicate that this would be the response. Then later on you get penalized for not responding to the emergency, but I would have if the character hadn't immediately decided that it wasn't an emergency independent of my actions.
Another thing I would like to change about the game is that occasionally emergency vehicles appear on top of each other and it's difficult to select them when they're all in the same place. Lastly, the left and right clicking mechanic is very difficult on a laptop when left and right clicks come from the same place, so lots of unintentional clicking, but it adds to the challenge and it's only a little irritating.
Overall I really enjoyed the game and look forward to other offerings from this studio.\t\t\t\t\t
