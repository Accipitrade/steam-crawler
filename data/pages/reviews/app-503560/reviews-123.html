\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA well-made game that has plenty to offer in terms of strategy and sandbox. The fact that you can procedurally generate almost any city in the world with this game and have it use accurate roads is very entertaining.
The story mode limits the cities but that appears to be so that the developer can craft a more engaging narrative around each mission. I did not expect new and interesting events to occur beyond the usual fires and robberies; at one point an earthquake rocked Boston and I was forced to deal with an overwhelming number of incidents each with their own requirements to resolve, all while the typical stuff was ongoing.
There are absolutely moments of panic and anxiety where you feel at least somewhat how a true emergency responder would feel like.

There are some concerns of whether or not the game has fully taken advantage of some of its content. There are many pieces of equipment and vehicles which appear to be entirely or virtually unused, leading me to believe that there is more content to be added which will widen the diversity of incidents to respond to.

A fine game all-in-all. Check it out!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAre you good at multitasking? You will be. This game comes pretty close to what real world 911 dispatchers contend with on a shift by shift basis. Dispatchers do not manage emergency services budget and staffing as you do in this game but that is one of it's charms. I particularly liked the ability to download the map of any city and be its dispatcher. The maps of the cities and their streets/road are 100% accurate though the locations of the stations and hospitals are not. With all of this in mind I found this game fun and I would recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI discovered this game long time ago when I watch a famous french youtuber (#JDG), and he really appreciated the game.
A couple of months after, I finally bought it, and wow... what a game !

Custom maps based on real datas, useful real life informations about critical situations, simple but great graphics, and what a endless game mode !
It's hard to don't like that, because a simple car crash can finish with wounded officers, medical staff attacked, who require heavy armed police agents, and Medivac (Hello SimCopter players !). But, some days can be simple with cats in tree and speed controls.
It's not Liberty City with explosions all the 3 minutes, it's not too much, it's just real situations where sending the right unit at the right time in the right area can prevent/solve situations.

All situations use your brain, no "I send this unit, it make all the work" or "3 mins and this is the end of the day and I finally can bought my 34 police car" , yeah, because you manage money, but you also manage calls.
Because a simple call can hide a wrong call, a simple word a domestical violence victim, and a simple hang up hidding someone shooted right in the heart. But, you also can solve situations like fire or heart attack, giving right indications to the caller. And If you give the wrong awnser, the game explain why and what, as a 911 member, you need to say/do to make the situations less worst.

Loving this game, funny and useful.\t\t\t\t\t
