\r\n\t\t\t\t\t911 Operator is a a cool little game that aims to reproduce the job of an emergency line operator in a way that would be fun. The game certainly has some amount of realism and I don't know if there is any other game with this thematic that would be more realistic, but you can't expect anything super realistic. The job of a real emergency line operator is a lot more "boring", most of the time you respond to empty calls, because someone didn't lock their phone and it dialed up the emergency line in their pocket, or you have to listen to crank calls or explain to people, that you can't send an ambulance just because their back is sunburned. The gameplay time is fairly short, the main campaign + the single city run with the base game + the free DLC took me ~10 hours on hard difficulty, but you can't expect a lot from an indie title. Sure, you can play essentially forever on the freeplay mode, but due to the low variety of calls and incidents, it gets pretty repetitive. There's also still some bugs and design flaws that can get a bit annoying, like when two accidents appear right next to each other and you just can't send the unit to the one you want it to go to. Overall, if you're looking for a game that has more content, is more polished and is still getting new updates and DLCs, buy the newer 112 Operator.
