I never realized how fun a simple 911 Dispatcher sim could be until I played this game. It is highly addicting and pulls you into that sweet vicious cycle of "just one more shift..". The voice acting is incredible with well written dialogue and many different kinds of replies to every call you receive. And if there's anything a game can give me that I just gobble right up is choices! The different events that pop up into the game are interesting and unique and genuinely stressful. Sadly the game does get very repetitive the more you play it and won't compare to your first playthrough.

If there's anything this game has showed me, is that I'd make a terrible over emotional 911 dispatcher.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA pretty okay game if you're interested in emergency services. If you can't afford 112 Operator, don't worry; this is basically the same thing but without the fancy displays.

That's the good things about this game. Here's the bad things.

The calls get very repetitive, very fast. There are no unique calls for specific locales (I'd love a soccer riot in Lisbon, or a freeway pileup in Toronto), which is a huge missed opportunity. Want different calls? Increase your difficulty to the point that you get five calls for armed fights when you only have four officers and two patrol cars. They're also very Western-centric: people generally have American names, and one call mentions a Senator, so there goes any hope of immersion if you're anywhere outside North America. Also, the calls don't appear to care about your surroundings, so get used to sawmill accidents in downtown Los Angeles, earthquakes in Berlin, and plane accidents in the open fields of Chicago.

The calls you have to actually answer are the worst. Half the time, they're nuisances: the same lady has a fire in her kitchen, and you have to remember that one hidden loading screen tip about what to use to fight an oil fire to save your fire engines any trouble—though it's not like fire calls ever happen anyways. At most, they just give your police and medical units more strain than they already have, so prepare to sacrifice a call to handle one of these. The other half of the time, they try to give you some nonexistent emotional moment, like when you're supposed to calm down the guy whose leg gets cut off in the sawmill. It's sort of tough when you first encounter it (though it's prominently featured on the thumbnail videos, so it's not like it's anything new to you), but every other time that guy asks you to tell his fiancee he loves her or some sh!t, you just want to skip it and tell him to shut up.

Don't expect the Workshop to be any better; the exact same coronavirus call being translated into what seems like every single language on Earth has topped the workshop since the beginning of 2020.

Thankfully, this game and its DLCs become the price of a candy bar when they're on sale. You're far better off buying it on sale, anyways.\t\t\t\t\t
