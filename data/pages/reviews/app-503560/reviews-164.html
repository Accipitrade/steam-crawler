\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI saw a video of this game in 2017 and bought it on sale about a year later and promptly forgot all about it. Just another hit of my steam sale addiction.
Then one day I saw it in my games list shrugged and thought this will be a quick easy game to play after the kids asleep and before my wife bullies me into watching prison break again.

Imagine my surprise when I started it up and found a surprisingly complex and stimulating game. its a really simple premise that gives me tons of fun every time i play.
the game play seems simple but you need to manage the time of your crews to the individual emergency and you never know when a major event is going to kick off and you will need to let that speeder off with a warning this time.

Now the main reason I think this game is so great is that it actually makes you think about real ways to deal with emergencies. The load screen has tons of very easily absorbed information about what to do if some one has a heart attack or if there is a house fire and the game needs you to absorb it so you can deal with the calls that come in.

At one point I was trying to help a women and her kids get out a burning building and I did not know that she needed to close the door behind her before opening a window in a burning building. The call went dead and when the ambulance and firefighters arrived there were 3 little black ribbons.

This game not only gave me some complying story and game play into a time management sim but made me realise that if I am ever stuck in a similar situation then I would have died or been hurt and that is a powerful thing for a video game to be able to tell you in an engaging way.

When my kids are older they are playing this game a lot. I honestly do not think I would be able to get the same information to them in the same way and have them actually understand it.

Its not a perfect game for sure. The various equipment other then the police things do not feel like they make much of a difference and I usually just used the basic vehicles as the extras also do not seem to add much. Also the game play does get very samey after a while and you have gone thought the whole campaign.

I have not started the free-play yet but I am excited that I can download my old home town map to play on. That is a fantastic feature too.

So yes I recommend it. Buy it. Tell your friends. Save lives\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSurprisingly engaging "spinning plate" management game where limited resources must be allocated to different needs, 911 Operate have you as the operator sending police, ambulance, and fire crews to various emergencies in the city, ranging from prank calls reports of robbery and assault, to surprise discovery of terrorists and gang hideouts and major disasters such as an earthquake, and everything in between. You also have to give proper first aid and appropriate responses to irate citizens, from coaching a man through helping his wife's childbirth on side of the road to proper use of tourniquet to save a guy's life. The "fun" part about this game is it uses real city maps and streets, and generate calls using those street names. You'll get a kick out of playing through some of the major cities in the world (mostly US, but there are others). You can also pay up to equip your teams with better equipment, such as rifles and bulletproof vests (in case they run into big trouble), first aid kits for first responders (who may not be paramedics), and more. There are also different grades of vehicles... Motorcyles are fast, but no room to carry prisoners, while vans can carry plenty of prisoners but are slow and not suited for pursuit. As staff gets injured you need to hire replacements, and you need to choose between fielding another team vs. operating within your budget.

The DLCs add additional events (different types of calls) and different vehicles for different scenarios. They are not required to play or enjoy.

I like it so far, and I may want the DLCs later.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t911 Operator is a stressful but fun management game. You control both a 911 phone operator and the person deciding where to send out police, fire, and medical emergency services. As you play missions come in both on the map and on the phone, with the phone missions providing multiple ways for a call to go. These calls are fully voiced, too.
There is a lot of replayability in the missions as even though the same ones pop up time and time again, they will play out differently: the prank call in one session can turn out to be a serious issue in the next.

An extra cool feature is that the game has maps for every single city I've tried, from my hometown Amsterdam to tiny cities on the other side of the world! The maps are pretty accurate and it's a nice touch being able to play in a city I'm actually familiar with.

An amazing game, and well worth the asking price.\t\t\t\t\t
