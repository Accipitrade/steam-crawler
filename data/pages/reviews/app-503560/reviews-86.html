\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is not great, though not terrible. There's nothing that stands out on the positive side. The "strategy" aspect is very light. You're mostly going to make the same calls to send whatever is nearest to the crisis to resolve it. There's not much in terms of resource management beyond what is super obvious. As far as difficulty goes, I may have never gotten far enough in the game to get to a part where there was actual difficulty, but the few maps I played through I felt like I was risking effectively nothing.

The 911 calls you work through are also incredibly simple and almost trivial. You answer one of a few dozen set calls and just tell people to, for example, not use water on an oil fire.

The game suffers from narrative whiplash too. One minute you're sending firemen to get a cat out of a tree and the next minute you sit through the heart-rending screams of a man who found his daughter dead from a drug overdose. Neither of these are used for any consistent emotional effect and just make the game feel odd, and the player uncomfortable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's be honest, a game like this can only get but so in depth. I've only played it for a few hours at this point, but I already bought it on my switch (and I've played it even longer). You are pretty limited in what you can do when you start out, and each level you play grants you additional resources and options (more so if you buy the dlc).

So let's also be real: I love this game, and at it's core, it is realistic. Address first, people! If something were to happen in real life you would need to be able to find these people. Thinking this way while playing pays off, which tells you that the developers really thought about what they were doing.

The only thing that bothers me about this game is how some aspects can be repetitive. For instance, the background noise is the same audio track basically on a loop. So once you stopped being overwhelmed by all the features of the game and start paying attention to the extra bits, you are hearing a loop. Also you will sometimes get the same call from the same person each "shift". Granted, sometimes the outcome is different.

I would HIGHLY recommend this game because not only is it enjoyable, it could potentially give some people a better ***baseline*** understanding of what a 911 operator really does go through.\t\t\t\t\t
