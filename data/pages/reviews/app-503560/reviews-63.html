Decent, but could've done better!
Honestly, it is lacking content. It is a fair trade when it is on sale.

The unique calls are well-made (there's plenty of spelling errors in the subtitles), but they quickly repeat. If they had random outcomes it would be forgivable, but once you've played enough you will now exactly how the conversation will end, so I disabled "answering calls" as often as I could. Yes, the core concept (which I like) is seriously lacking.

The game isn't short of bugs, there's odd pathing (to be expected with auto-generated maps), but vehicles can be stuck sometimes. On expert difficulty (which I primary played at), the game would suddenly throw upwards to 12 calls in a span of seconds. There's definitely a need for some type of check for how many calls can be created per minute. The unpredicableness is the fun of it, but not at that extreme.

Other issues are;
* Not being able to view details of a incident if you are in a active call.
* Not seeing the age of an incident (either mouse-over each one, or check in the bottom-right list and select it). What should have been done is adding a incident-number on the map-icon to make this easier.
* The speed stats on vehicles are incorrect, when viewing vehicles in the store they have a certain speed, once you purchase one and hover over one of them, they display another speed.

There isn't much replayability, as for each successful day, you build up a bigger fleet, but the difficult seems almost unaffected - to the point of getting boring before it ramps up in difficulty. Perhaps it should be possible to start a playthrough with many already complete days? Another thing is the equipment, seeing that each personel-type has 2 equipment slots, it gave the false impression that I could unlock equipment for these... but in reality it is just the police officers that has that progression to them. There are some rarer incidents that requires more resources than usual, making it more interesting, but there should have been more of these. The repetitive calls could've been made with different outcomes, and instead of heavily scripted dialogue, they could have been more branching with a more quanititive approach.

All in all, it was decent for 14 hours of gameplay, but not at full price. I do not have any of the DLC, perhaps they solve some of the issues I mentioned, or introduces new ones?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t911 Operator is a stressful but fun management game. You control both a 911 phone operator and the person deciding where to send out police, fire, and medical emergency services. As you play missions come in both on the map and on the phone, with the phone missions providing multiple ways for a call to go. These calls are fully voiced, too.
There is a lot of replayability in the missions as even though the same ones pop up time and time again, they will play out differently: the prank call in one session can turn out to be a serious issue in the next.

An extra cool feature is that the game has maps for every single city I've tried, from my hometown Amsterdam to tiny cities on the other side of the world! The maps are pretty accurate and it's a nice touch being able to play in a city I'm actually familiar with.

An amazing game, and well worth the asking price.\t\t\t\t\t
