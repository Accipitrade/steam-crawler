\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far I have quite enjoyed this game, after playing it for a bit (as of this review, I have played 5.1 hours.), I figured it was fair enough time to give it a review. Here are the pros and cons I've found with just 5 hours of the main missions.

Pros
-The voice acting is well done.
-The sound effects are good and not out of place.
-Several calls have actually affected me emotionally (this is good because it means I am engaged with the game)
-Pretty decent call variety, and not all calls with the same voice and startup scenario have the same result, and choosing options in different orders can end up with different scenarios entirely.
-Several outside references which are nice. (Notably I have seen 3; a Breaking Bad reference (pretty obvious), what I can assume is a reference to the developer of this game (phone call about developing a game like this), and possibly a GTA reference (Guy getting pulled out of his car at a red light)).
-The vehicle diversity allows for interesting strategies. One strategy I've adopted uses Police bikes (which cannot transport criminals to jail) to intercept smaller missions until my patrol car (which is slower) can arrive and ferry away the criminals.

Cons I've seen.
-Ocassional bug where a mission spawns off-map. The mission is still doable, but you have to use the "incidents" and "Units" tabs to monitor the progress and bring your units back. This is sorta a semi-con, because it actually taught me that sending your units via the menus was actually possible.
-There are a few gramatical hiccups and inconsistancies here and there, most notably between what the operator/caller says and the transcript (where the transcript will have a misspelling or mistake.), but these aren't too terrible and are easily missed. In my opinion, the only line that truly bugs me is when units say "What are the orders." (I think they should be saying "what are your orders?" instead, but that may just be me. As a disclaimer I am a simpel highschool graduate, not a professional.)

Feedback.
-... This is a bit embrrasing, but because the exit sign on the main menu was greyed out compared to the other buttons, I did not realise that that was the "exit game" sign... maybe "game" should be added under exit to help make it more noticable as a button rather than part of the background?
-Maybe an alert or indication that you have staff/vehicles waiting in the wings before you start deployment? It took me a while to figure out I actually had some extra units on the side.
-Maybe clear up some of what the stats do? On the staff list their skills are listed as percentages, but when they are on a vehicle they are listed as a number (seeming to range from 0.00 to I think 4.00? unsure exact numbers but its within that range.)
-Clicking on vehicles from stations during the deployment phase didn't always select the vehicle shown. (For instance, if I had a bike and car at the same station, the car might be what I see, but if I click and make it move, the bike will actually be what I selected.) Maybe have the selection prioritize vehicles shown on the front first during deployment.

Overall, I'm going to give the game a solid 8/10. Its very stable, works smoothly, has difficulty options for both casual and hardcore players, and I'm sure there are many crazy scenarios I just haven't reached yet. I hope to see the developers read feedback and work to keep giving the game quality improvements!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, what should one write about this game after playing it for roughly 3h.

It is fairly nice that you actually can use a real map (that is imported from an outside source) -e.g. your hometown- as a background. Units, POIs (hospitals, police stations, etc.) and calls are generated randomly.

For freeplay the user gets to dispatch the units for calls that are either auto-generated or generated following a short "911 calltaker mini-game". Sadly the calls are fairly repetitive and personally I do not see much replay value once one does generate enough money to unlock all units. If one does that. Because honestly you can easily do almost all calls with the basic units.

A few things tend to get annoying very fast:
- You can not change the unit number once you assigned your unit=> If you want your Police units first you have to reassign all units.
- The 911 calltaker game gets very annoying very fast.
- It is a bit unclear what the items you can buy for your staff do. Espcially as you can only read the description ingame once you bought them
- There are basically no firefighting items.
- Only two slots per person. Means: You can not equip a policeman with a gun, a bulletproof vest and a police info terminal. Or tazer. Or dog.
- Staff gets injured. ALL THE TIME. It's mostly unlogical for me when this occurs and is extremly annoying
- You seem to be only able to play one city at a time. Means it seems like you have to reset your progress if you want to try out another city. Not sure about the career here.

As this is a 1.0 of a game and not a early access, etc. I personally can not recommend it, even for the fairly reduced price.
