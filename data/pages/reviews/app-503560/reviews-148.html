\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have always had an interest in fire and rescue. I have wanted this game and decided to get it from the steam summer sale. I'd say that it is a good game with lots of educational value that can be applied to real life. (Don't pour water on an electrical fire) Even the loading screens give educational tips on what to do in an emergency.

What facinates me is that you can use any city you want to, even small towns. It really makes me think about how people design city layouts for emergency services. I know it's just a simple trick that probably just uses a map database similar to google maps, but it always makes me nerd out every time! It really helps the roleplay aspect of the game

I see a lot of room for improvement in this game but I do enjoy it for what it is. I would really dig it if there was an online version where you could play with friends in neighboring cities and offer them mutual aid when there are big emergencies such as Earthquakes or resource depletion due to so many things going on at once.

I'd also like to see there be more in depth stats and maybe some levels and XP for your units. It's sometimes a little tricky to understand how they are upgraded and improved. I see that from time to time my units get promoted, however does this mean their stats also go up? I'd like to see a marked increase if that is true so I can have the best possible team.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt $3 it might be worth a purchase. It's far too simplistic, even though the little content is has does work well.

You are given a few teams of firemen, policemen, and paramedics. Then you play out a shift in the city, and try to use your resources the most effectively you can. When the shift is over, you can use money to purchase additional vehicles, staff, or equipment, and add or change your teams.

Throughout the shift, most assignments simply show up on the map, and it's a matter of reacting quickly and sending whichever teams needs to be there. You will also get a few 911 calls, which are very well acted, and present a few dialog options for you to pick from.

As you play, you progress from city to city, with each city presenting a harder challenge.

Overall the game is polished, but the gameplay is so limited I got bored quickly and ended up returning it. There's simply not that much to do, and each city does not bring anything new other than a bigger playfield.

I can't recommend this game. However, if you are intrigued by the game's concept, try it out when it's on sale. It's hard to justify paying full price for this.\t\t\t\t\t
