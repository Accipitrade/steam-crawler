\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSurprisingly cute and kind of addictive game. Long story short – you'll manage a group of first responders by using the fixed amount of money and then try to “survive” a number of accidents in different cities (based on the real ones). Which won't be that easy since you'll have a limited amount of people and vehicles, while different accidents will require different specialists and they won't stay opened for you forever. You fail to solve the accident by sending the right team in time – you lose reputation. You finish the day with the negative reputation – it's a game over for you (Game over, man! Game over!). Sounds like a basic idea and, well, it is. Even the visuals are very basic in this game. But the thing is – that's exactly what makes it so addictive. 911 Operator is one of those basic games that just make you keep playing.

Unfortunately, that's also the first problem with this game – it does get boring pretty fast. At your first day, you'll find it very addictive and probably waste the entire evening playing it without even noticing it. I mean, it's simple, it's fun and it's very addictive. Time just flies when you play it. But when you'll wake up the next morning... most likely you'll find yourself looking for something else. Just because 911 Operator doesn't have enough variety. There's a pretty awesome feature where you should answer the calls (I was surprised to find out that this game is actually voiced), ask the victims about their situations, provide them with some important advises (that will actually matter, since the entire outcome of such cases will depend on what you'll say) and send the right team to help, but since the developers decided to go with fully voiced calls, you can easily guess that there's a very limited amount of those. Sure, there's some variety in them, but all in all, it takes only one evening to see the entire thing. And after that... it just won't be as fun, since you'll experience the same exact calls again and again. In other words, 911 Operator fails to achieve so-called “Tetris effect”. It's very fun and addictive, but only until you'll experience the entire thing. Which won't take long. And even different play modes won't help much with that since all in all, those are just different versions of the same exact thing.

Another problem with this game is that it's a bit too greedy. The complete edition will cost you $ 32.96, while the thing is – DLC feels a lot like a scam. Turned out that Jutsu Games is one of those teams that are OK with releasing updates as DLC. The base version of the game feels lacking. Like... Early Access lacking. While if you want some vital features that will balance the game out, you should pay for DLC. For example, the entire team management in the base game was poorly balanced and the entire money thing felt a bit pointless. It was fixed by so-called “reinforcement” option, which allowed you to summon some help by paying from your game wallet, but guess what? You'll need to pay real money for that feature. Because it's a freakin' DLC. And even though DLC does include new maps, don't expect it to feel like a big addition. Those won't be a proper expansions. Those are just that – one map here and one map there. A little additions to already existing campaign. And the guys are OK with asking $ 7.99 for something like that... Honestly, it feels like a robbery. You won't want to play this game without the DLC. But the DLC feel like those were supposed to be free updates and paying for them will feel awful. Especially since you can buy a bunch of games for such money on websites like Fanatical. Let's just say that it's hard to swallow.

What this game does right, though, is paying the tribute to emergency services. To people who risk their lives for us every single day. It's also very good for you, no matter how old are you. See, the game will often give you hints about the emergency first aid. In other words, it'll pretty much teach you about how to help people in different situation. In the game, you'll need to know that to choose the right answers during the calls and get better results. In real life, though, you may learn a lot of important basics that you were always lazy to learn. I mean, come on! Most of us are lazy asses who never really cared to learn and remember about how to provide the first aid. And this game? It makes learning a part of gameplay. And that alone is... kind of awesome, really.

Together with basic but addictive gameplay, it makes it almost impossible to give this game a thumb down. Unfortunately, I can't say it's that awesome either. There's a serious lack of variety here, the game gets boring extremely fast, DLC feels like a scam and the full package (which is pretty much the game in the way it was supposed to be to begin with) totally doesn't worth the full price. You have been warned. With a solid discount on the full package, though, this game will be a pretty cute little addition to your Steam library. Just don't expect much, since after a one, or two evening you, probably, won't want to return to it.\t\t\t\t\t
