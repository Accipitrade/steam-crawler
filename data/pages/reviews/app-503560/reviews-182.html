\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimple and addictive.

The game puts your micromanagement and mental juggling skills to the test as you're pitted against every situation you can think of in a modern day city while answering roleplay style lines of dialogue from desperate and sometimes not so desperate (including misuse calls) needing emergency assistance. You will need to decide where vehicles are to patrol stretgically, what type of specialist vehicle will attend all while trying to instruct people who've never attempted CPR in their life to instructing people who cannot speak during a hostage situation but need help.
You must ask the right questions, because people under stress and in danger will not always respond with rational or useful information - Car jacking? Who were the perpetrators? What colour and plates were your car? Are you injured? He's not going to tell you that stuff straight away, so don't forget to ask them.

The game features a variety of vehicles from ambulances to their counterparts, the patient transport vehicle which is faster and can carry more injured, or the paramedic helicopter which is fast but only carries 1 injured person. Fire trucks that carry water, technical fire rescue who can deal with chemical, toxic, electrical and incidents more physically obstructive than a fire hazard incident. Police can take squad cars, fast responding bikes or riot vans. Beware though that the game does take into account small things like in real life - a biker cop has no safe way to transport a criminal and will request a pick up, but they're ideal for resolving petty crime like vandalism or speeding tickets.

You will have the opportunity to make money in a day's duty, sell vehicles or gear. Responding to more crimes effectively and efficiently will garner more money from the fines laid onto criminals and medical expenses paid. You can use this to equip officers with assault rifles for organised crime response, vests for protection or first aid kits and crime computers to faster heal and treat wounds while resolving crimes faster using the database they now have access to.
Firemen will have access to technical tools to help make the water trucks more modular in being able to deal with a technical issue should a designated technical fire truck be unavailable.

You must select these and sell gear at the start of a round of Duty. Staff will all have stats such as driving skill, their designated team skill such as First Aid, Technical, Firefighting and Marksmanship depending on their role. Choose wisely because this will affect the outcome of situations and the safety of your staff.

Do take note of how to treat injuries because the roleplay aspect will have wrong answers and common misconceptions as playable answers e.g. don't ever try to be a hero, don't re-enter a burning building to help somebody as hard as it may be not to do so. Don't pour water onto a fire in a kitchen straight away without identifying if it's a grease fire. If it is, do you know the correct way to resolve it? These situations are all explained in the tutorial but they are fairly black and white at first with some variation happening as you progress into the game.
You will also recieve more abstract situations to deal with such as wild animals entering people's homes and more emotional ones if your imagination fills in the blanks as much as mine does - A woman called to complain that her neighbour often listened to his TV very loudly during the day but he had been listening all night. I asked if the man had any known visitors or family come over recently, she she was not aware of any. Her tone changed to that of genuine concern, I sent paramedics over to find that sadly, the man had passed away, alone, last night.

You will be hit by many situations at once sometimes, including natural disasters and very specific types of dangerous criminals. You will need to prioritise these scenarios in order of importance and will be rated at the end of the round. It is OK to let some incidents go unresolved but you must make sure you only allow a few of these, if at all, go unsolved.

The game is very intense at times but there is control of what speed the game plays at, from paused to normal, fast and fastest. It's a good way to catch a breath and read, analyse, strategize and respond. The game will always play on normal speed (which is very slow) when you answer the phone.

It's got a few rough edges mostly in user interface which could be better, like swapping gear on staff, overlapped units on the map and hilighting could be better when you do hover over a unit from the list view. Fonts could be easier to read at times and the tutorial is essentially only how to treat injuries or resolve scenarios through the roleplay element when it comes up.

All in all, this game scratches that Public Service itch and is a nice little game that isn't too hard on people who desire strategy but struggle with it as I do. It's fun, it's emotional at times for those with imagination and I would certainly be interested in the next game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game's idea is somewhat cool I guess, however it feels like I'm still playing an Alpha version.

Pros:
- Fun to manage your city
- DLCs (I don't own any however they seem interesting)
- Unique calls
- Custom cities which you can download

Cons:
- Very little UI, bare menu with only three buttons, no settings menu, no way to quit, feel unfinished AF. It just doesn't *feel* like a game, it's just bare bones with pretty much no UI
- Very little calls, as I can recall, there's only about 15 or 10 different calls that I've heard, keeps on repeating and gets boring and repetitive
- Little content, gets boring after 20 minutes of playing and no financing
- Not really any tutorial (probably because it's pretty boring)
- The in-game 2D graphics are horrible and not customer friendly
- Low variety of vehicles and vehicles feel repetitive after 10 minutes

In general, it just doesn't feel like a game, doesn't even feel like an Alpha to be honest. After a couple of minutes you seem to be doing the same thing over again, as 1. pretty much no updates and 2. literally 0 content

It's pretty much a 2D 911: First Responders with 98% less content and fun. Not good for its price, and feels like a game that should be on an internet game website.

Total is about: 2.5/10

NOTE: This is for 1.2 hours of playing, however I feel like everything in the game I have already done.\t\t\t\t\t
