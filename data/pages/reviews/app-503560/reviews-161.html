\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe premise of 911 Operator is rather simple. Left-click on an emergency vehicle and right-click on where you'd like them to go. Emergencies can simply pop up on the map, and you have to see which icon is present at that location before sending the corresponding service. Occasionally, some require more than one – sometimes requiring services from all three.

The mechanics are simple to learn and use, and I found no trouble directing my desired emergency service to the appropriate location.

Sometimes you don't have enough units for all of the incidents you're dealing with, and it becomes a waiting game as you hope little Timmy holds on and doesn't die while you wait for a paramedic to free up.

It's great, in all honesty, and I totally get the vibe they were going for with this.

I love that you can input your own town or city into the game and play it in a local area. That is genius!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's decent, it's a good, cheap game that I'd get on sale for about <$5. The game is lacking content to the point I'm two hours in and I've had pretty identical calls 5+ times. But it's a cool concept, almost played 3 hours in my first session and I'm gonna go back to it in the near future, it's definitely a game that you'll have to play in short bursts or it'll get repetitive.

The game is fairly unique and it's cool to see the different scenarios, it can get pretty challenging at times which is good, it's not overly difficult but it's not easy either. I'd say the game doesn't really punish you for doing badly but rewards you for doing well.

Just be aware this is a game that will genuinely run out of original content within an hour or two, so if you're okay with it occasionally sounding like a broken record, it's a fun sim/management game. There's better games for the full price cost though. The game would have a fairly decent amount of content if they included the DLC packs in the base game. I paid £3.99 for the game and the DLC packs cost around the same, kinda disappointing.

Overall, just go for it if you like management games, it's good fun for a few hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t911 Operator is a casual game about matching colors. You are given 3 colors: blue, red, and white which you need to match against various events with corresponding colors. Sometimes you might need more than one type of color for the event. Once a color has been matched, the corresponding object or unit with that color will be tied up "resolving" that event. This is the gist of 911 Operator.

You can customize your units a little bit, make them more effective at being blue, maybe make it so that they can be a little bit red, or make them move faster on the screen. There's a time element too, of which you have a small degree of control over by positioning your colors near the center of your screen. It takes time for units to carry the right color to the right spot on the map. There's some small bits of 2D art that tries to make you think the game is about managing emergency services, but at the end of the day, 911 Operator essentially boils down to a game of color matching.\t\t\t\t\t
