\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDid not expect this to be quite as much fun as it was. In a weird way, it makes me much more thankful for first responders and the operators that coordinate them. Once you get the hang of it, it can feel almost like an RTS, making sure your units are adequately dispersed through various neighborhoods and listening to the nature of calls or how many suspects are involved (let's say public intoxication), I can keep a unit nearby just in case it gets ugly (thankfully this has saved me doing this). It's a great strategy game that let's you spend and customize on squads, vehicles, and equipment. The 911 calls are great too; sometimes funny, sometimes sad, and they demand your close attention to infer the type of emergency personnel you'll need on scene. Really great job by the devs. P.S. I encountered a bug on the woman talking about the loud TV and child crying next door; upon asking "Do you know them?", all dialogue stopped and I was forced to "ignore" the call, even though I sent a unit there anyway. Still, the only bug so far and it was very minor.\t\t\t\t\t
