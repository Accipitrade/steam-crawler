\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's an interesting concept for a video game, although the implementation is a bit odd.

I was lured in by the branching dialog aspect of the phone calls, although I think after about 5 hours of gameplay you will have done all of them at least once. I was intrigued to see there was also a city management aspect to the game as well, but managing them during duties can feel a lot like RNG luck regarding how fast you can get to them. Units can stack on top of each other making others invisible or unselectable, and similarly some emergency events can end up being missed if you're unlucky.

It's also unclear how missions, campaigns and difficulty modes are laid out in the main game menu, so I'm not even sure if I've experienced all the content the game has to offer at this point. Maybe 112 Operator has improved upon these things. Jutsu Games seems to have stuck to the same formula for several of their games now.

It's worth a look when it's on discount (like it's currently just over £1 during the Summer Sale). I think it's fair to say I got more than £1 worth of entertainment out of it.\t\t\t\t\t
