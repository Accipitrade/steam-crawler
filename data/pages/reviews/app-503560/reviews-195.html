\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my initial take and review after being only a few hours in.
First of all, love the game. I'm a first responder myself and I love this style of management game. Really hits all of the key aspects I would like to see in the game. It takes all the good things about "This is The Police", without an unnecessary storyline and amplifies it!
This being said, there are a few issues, none major so far but some do affect the overall rating.
1- You can tell that the English version wasn't translated by a native English speaker. Some very obvious grammar mistakes at points that are hard to look past.
2- Some Mechanics issues. One that bothers me is that other than transporting patients/criminals, and responder can do any job. I could send 3 Medics and 4 Cops to put out a structure fire and it would probably be faster than sending a firetruck. (This is the same in many scenarios but that seemed like a good, & dramatic example).
3- Whatever grading method is used at the end of a duty, seems almost subjective. I will see a call on the report with a score of -1 and in the info it will say everything went fine.

Other than these things, and I do realise the game is young, I love it. Haven't used all the game modes/DLC/workshop features yet but I will update if any of this changes.

10/10 would make police get cats out of trees again\t\t\t\t\t

[h]Complaints/Problems[/h]

I have noticed that when I give a unit directions, there is a small chance that a random straight path will be drawn
and the unit will follow it [even going "through" the ground or roads] to a point on a road and then continue the route I set, but it takes longer for him to get there. It is also hard to manage units and prioritize them when I can't label them (i.e. Taser police, EMS Police, etc. etc.). It would be nice to have the ability to change Unit No. # to something else. Other than that, I would recommend it if you like 911 themed games or managment/strategy games in general.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAN HONEST REVIEW
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PROS:

-Allows for multiple ways to handle scenarios.

-Mods are making gameplay more challenging but FUN

-Allows for differnt scenarios such as Earthquakes... only earth quakes... FOR NOW

-Funny Scenario's as well as Life or Death Scenarios

Cons:

The game does have small bugs, such as not saving scenarios

Needs more content but is fixed with mods, beware though as the more added, really does make the gameplayed more hectic to beat, but fun.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Overall: This game is fun for those who are strategy based gamers. Positioning units is key to success as well as the kits given to those units, EX.) First aid kit to a Police Officer.

I would prefer anyone to buy this game if it is on Sale. It is a great game, but still new, so scenarios can even be repetitive, which is why I strongly prefer mods. You will LOVE this game for the 1-10 hours of gameplay, but until more scenarios or options are added, 911 Operator can only be enjoyable for only those 10 hours.
