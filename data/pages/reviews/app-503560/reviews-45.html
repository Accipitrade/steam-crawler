\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA lot of fun for strategy/puzzle gamers. The game loop is to pick a city map select your units and try to cover all the incidents and incoming 911 calls. You're ranked on efficiency (how well you handle calls and respond to all the incidents) and reputation (not sure about this one, but it seems to be how helpful you are in the call). Pass a level, get some money, add to your equipment/staff before the next shift. The game scales difficulty as you go up levels and throws random events at you that are challenging (such as earthquakes), but still fun. There is a nice mix of vehicles and equipment allowing for a few different strategies.

I don't think this will be a 1000 hour game, but its a great pick up on sale. Fairly casual, but still challenging.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an unfinished, unpolished game and I think it's laughable that the developers are getting away for charging $15 for a $2 mobile game.

First and worst thing about this game is that there is an ad on startup. YouTube Red charges you less to not see ads, why am I seeing ads in a full game I paid for? This is obviously something leftover for mobile game little leagues.

Gameplay and storymode only lasts you about ~3hrs, well under what you would expect from a standard video game.

Gameplay mechanics - are very two-dimensional and poorly explained. For example, police can only respond to police calls and cannot interact with other department's jobs and vice versa. I think that the different departments should be able to respond to all types of calls with varying degrees of efficiency. This absolutely ruins the immersion of the game as police officers wouldn't just sit there and say "welp, sucks you're bleeding out, I don't know how to use this first aid kit"

Missing content - There's also reason to believe that there is missing content in the game. Descriptions of different equipment/vehicles lead me to believe that there is supposed to be more weaponry available to police officers, and there is also missing persons reports that were never implemented. Aside from the police department, fire/medical are greatly neglected in variety of equipment and calls they respond to.

Story mode - is very boring and cliche. The world they built feels empty. The phone calls you receive carry 100% of the story of each map, and each one is cliche as hell. San Francisco is a centre for hippies and has a major earthquake, Washington DC has a political bomb threat, etc. There's cute little references to millenial pop culture occasionally, such as responding to the cast of How I Met Your Mother, but it is simply not enough to make up for the lack of storytelling.

Lastly, I am disgusted at the lack of trigger warnings for suicide. There's at least two phone calls you have to interact with that deal with suicide calls that are very disturbing. This type of neglect is something I wouldn't expect from Electronic Arts.

Also, racial profiling - what the ♥♥♥♥?
