\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played this game on Android years ago, though upon finding it on Steam and remembering it, I decided to get it.

911 Operator is a great game. It's a good game to play that's not too intense, but it can be, if you go on a higher difficulty or of you're stretched thin on people to respond to a new emergency. Moreover, along with being an engaging game, it does it's part in being an educational tool, as it provides "what to do" information in lots of emergency situations, as well as lessons in the gameplay. For instance, if you handle a call or react to a situation incorrectly, you can look at the log at the end of the day to see what you should've done. The game is a masterclass in preparation as well, as in higher difficulties or busy times, you may have to plan ahead and send units to certain areas in order to respond faster. Personally, I enjoy being in the position of a dispatcher, managing the units as situations arise around the map. Speaking of the map, I like that you can use any city as the map, not just a set few. Overall, it's a engaging experience that I have had fun with, and will continue to enjoy.

Overall, I recommend 911 Operator to those that enjoy management or dispatch-type games, as well as anyone that is interested in the work that the brave 911 Operators do each and every day.\t\t\t\t\t
