\r\n\t\t\t\t\t911 operator is a unique and interesting game where you are the dispatcher behind the scenes.

Armed with a computer, and emergency services that you can customize and control, YOU decide what classifies as an emergency and how to best react to it.
911 operator is a a game of balance and triage as you move your ambulances, firefighers and police services around the city responding to multiple requests for assistant and 911 calls that feature pretty convincing voice acting.

Do you send that ambulance to check out a broken bone, or do you send it along with your police units to respond to a burglary on the other side of the city? Do send all your police units to a bank robbery to resolve it quickly and if so, who will patrol the other side of the city and catch that road rage maniac?

The events add up quickly, and if your not great at balancing your units people can die, officers can get shot due to lack of backup, and firefighters can spend all day taking care of a fire that obviously one engine cant handle alone.

PROS:
* I especially like how the game uses a open-source online map as the source of the city / town maps, you can upload and play practically any city / town on earth, with proper placement of hospitals, police stations and fire stations.
* The Voice acting is a very neat feature which not only aids in immersion but offers valuable clues as to how to respond.
* The game features real emergency first aid instructions and visual aids which are not only vital to solving some of the 911 calls but also invaluable in real life. (you can make a situation so much worse by providing wrong advice during a call)
* The customization of your units allows for different approaches to game play, and adds flexibility to your units.
* The post duty-shift tally system which breaks down each 911 call into a more indepth review and explains where you did a good job or screwed up.

CONS:
* Just as in real life, butt dails are very annoying and tie up the phone line. (the annoying part is actually the lack of variety in butt dails)
* Although the DLC was cheap, i feel that it didnt add enough content (that being said i havent tried the game without the DLC to compair)
* The campaign seems a little short, at only 6 cities there is about 15 -25 hours of campaign play before you win, though of course its still fun.
* some towns simply arent available to be played (if your town doesnt have a hospital, police station, and firestation you likely wont be able to play it).

Overall, i thorougly enjoyed 911 operator and applaud the game developers for not only making this game, which brings attention to the difficult job of being a 911 operator, but for their creative use of voice actors and using the open map website as a source of content.\t\t\t\t\t

The good: This game is a nice change of pace from your FPS and will keep you entertained for quite some time. Gameplay can be slow or fast. You will eventually fail a map finding the need to change your method of dispatch.

The bad: The scenarios are repetative, painfully so. Also when you move on from a map to the next... and you will, right away; The currency starts over. Your leveled up, well equipped police fire and ems will not carry along. That really puts some of the nicer equipment/vehicles out of reach unless you stay on the same map. Flip-side if you do not move on to new maps, you won't be finding NEW equipment.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a real life 911 call taker and dispatcher, I really enjoyed this game. It reminded me a lot of the CritiCall test most agencies do before you get hired. While not completely realistic, it gives lay people an example of what it is we do.

Pros: very simple gameplay that feels like actually dispatching,
It has most cities and calls match fairly accurately to real locations. Had a call just down the street to my actual house.

Cons: anytime a suspect is armed there is a shootout. Officers are involved in shootouts about five times per shift.
Needs more incoming 911 call recordings as even after just a few hours they repeat.
Allow multitasking, such as dispatching while still on a 911 call. Currently you must completely finish the call before checking on other calls.
Needs option to simplify and do just dispatching or call take or even further break down dispatch by agency. Co-op gameplay would be fun with each person controlling one discipline.
