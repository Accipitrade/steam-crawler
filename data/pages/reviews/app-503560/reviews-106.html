INTRO
Take control, maintain the order and save lives!

THE GOOD AND THE BAD
+ Such simple graphics, but serving their purpose perfectly
+ Challenging when you're facing sudden catastrophes like an earthquake
+ The calls feel well thought and don't seem fake
+ Some of the calls are pretty funny
+ A lot of equipment and vehicles to choose from
+ Three variations of career mode
+ You can freely rearrange your vehicles on the map before starting your shift
+ Sensical staff management *1
+ Your staff can die while solving a difficult task without proper backup *2

- Sometimes it's hard to predict how to solve the call in a way the game recognizes fit
- I'm sick and tired of saving cats all the time and wasting the expensive technical truck that is needed for more important stuff
- Not all variations of career mode seem to feature DLC content
- Rare bugs *3

NOTES
*1 More people in a vehicle = faster and more effective task solving; injuries happen and you need to have someone "on the bench" to replace these guys when they get hurt.

*2 Always remember to send out enough people, they will ask for backup if you don't, but it might be late by then, as your stuff gets injured or even die in rare occasions.

*3 I once encountered a call that got stuck and just wouldn't end, forcing me to ignore it and lose points. Since I only played like eight hours as of this writing, I'm expecting a few more bugs of this kind. It's unfortunate, but I'd like to think this happens rarely and it depends on the exact dialogue as you have multiple options to choose from all time. I guess I pressed the wrong answer that the devs didn't expect or something, despite the call being led absolutely

OVERALL
This game is great. I only played it for a cup of coffee so far, but it definitely got me hooked.

RATING
8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI Looked at this a while ago and thought it was a bit too basic, however after it was on offer I bought it. (I assume its on offer because I have since found out there is a newer version called 112 with larger maps and content etc). It's obvious what you have to do but I can't quite explain there is something extremely addictive about this game. As well as saving people, answering phone calls and upgrading/kitting out the rescue teams with the money gained, there is a real feeling of satisfaction. At the end of each day there is a review showing your performance and if you missed or failed any incidents. Its quite frustrating to think you've had a good day and then see you've missed one. However this doesn't effect your money too much and you can continue the game. There is no levels that you will get completely stuck on and can just keep going, which I like, there's nothing more frustrating than getting stuck on a level and eventually giving up a game because of it but this game isn't like that thankfully. I would normally dismiss the newer 112 game because of the price but after playing this and seeing how addictive it is, I might just fork out for it.
