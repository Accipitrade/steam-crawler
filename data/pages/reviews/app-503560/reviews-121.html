\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got introduced to this game via playthroughs on youtube a long time ago. This game is addicting and challenging but dissapointing. I like the game but will not recommend it because it's a whopping 15 euros and therefore not worth the money. Sale price of 2/3 euros was very reasonable.
( bad grammar incoming )

Pros:
You can play on almost every metropolis/capital and all of the stations are ACCURATE
There are 4 difficulties
Tons of levels
Career and endless mode
Challenging even on normal mode

Neutral:
Not many types of calls, but there are tons of outcomes for every call.
Game speed is adjustable, but it affects the speed of calls, therefore you might miss some.

Cons:
Buggy point and click system
Buggy map overlay; sometimes an unit will only *look* as if it is on the crime scene, while it is camping 2 meters away from the scene, but will still be found under the "incident icon" creating the illusion THAT it is resolving the incident.
Almost no tutorial for the complicated staff system.
An equipment shop exists, but it's easily overlooked.
Some cities ( mine is Amsterdam ) are more difficult than their supposed to be, because of their layout.
Places where incidents occur are randomized and can therefore be a bit troublesome. This is mostly a problem when all units have to be called in for a backup when you receive a crucial
call that's on the other side of the city.
When multiple incidents occur really close to each other on the map, it's hard to see which unit is resolving which incident.

I bought this game on sale, and it already feels under developed. It's fun for the first couple of hours ( I have more hours than steam shows ) but after that it starts to feel really blank. Everything will eventually turn out to be the same, as there are no more than 50 something scripts, spread over all 3 units. ( Firefighters, police and ambulances )

I suggest getting this game on sale, and refunding it if the game doesn't satisfy you.
