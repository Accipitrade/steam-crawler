\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a fantastic idea, with some fantastic concepts that somehow was just poorly executed.

People kept saying that it was repetitive without saying how or why.

The how: There's not very many unique calls, and the calls are always 2-3 dialogue options with the voice actor having multiple lines of dialogue so that they could RNG a very slight change to the caller.

The Why: Imagine if you were playing 'Papers, Please' and you just kept getting the same 4 travelers over and over again, and no extra mechanics were ever introduced. If you can see how that would get repetitive after awhile you can understand why this game gets repetitive. I'll get the same caller sometimes multiple times in a single level 'MY CAR WAS STOLEN", "Where at?" response 1: "oh i was parked in a no parking zone, nevermind." response 2: "In this area, send police."

The game tries to keep it interesting by allowing you to download the map of your current location, so you can play your city and also by having random events where a music festival will occur for the next day or two, or a bomber is attacking your city. Truthfully, all they needed to keep this game interesting is a revolving door of calls, since... this game is supposed to be '911 operator.' Even if they cut out the fantastic and very well done voice overs in order to spend more money on unique callers.

I'll probably play it more off and on, but realistically you get maybe like 30 mins of play time before it gets boring after the initial 2 hours of learning the mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t
