Summary

Well, it's a tough one. On one hand this game is really good, and addresses the subject mattter both realistically and in such a way that makes you play "just one more turn" - or in this case one more shift. On the other hand calls become quite repetitive very soon. There are only several voices in the game and most, if not all calls, are dubbed by the same person. Sure, there are some variations meaning that conversation doesn't have to end the same way, but it ruins the immersion unbelieveably. Once I had the same call - when someone dials 911 by accident, three times in a row, and it becomes my most hated one. It goes on exactly the same every single time, the same voice, the same lines.

It is hard for me to recommend this game, but at the same time, despite all these flaws, I still like it. Sure, it's quite repetitive and immersion can be broken quite easily, but it's most certainly the best game available that touches this subject. If you're a fan of emergency services, management sims, or broadly simulations, you might want to try this one.


Pros
1. The setting seems pretty realistic.
2. Management aspect of the game is pretty nice and can be quite challenging at times.
3. Ability to choose from thousands of cities around the world is amazing, but vehicles and staff still look American wherever you're playing. Nevertheless, it is amazing to see real-world addresses. I don't recall ever seeing something quite like that.
4. I like some in-game humor connected to TV series such as Breaking Bad. One of the map is Albuquerque so it was inevitable.

Cons
1. Only a handful of voices that are repeating a lot.
2. I don't like it when there is a DLC immediately available after game's release. It feels like a cheap shot.
3. Some of the available cities are not that great - Carlsbad, California, for example has only one hospital, one police station, and one fire department, while even small towns can have a lot of them spread throughout the map.
4. There are too many known terrorists spotted and shooting if you're playing in a small town. The game doesn't adjust types of crimes and their frequency to account for the size of the city or town, or at least doesn't seem to.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm frankly surprised it's taken someone this long to make a video game about being a 911 Operator. I've always thought the idea of monitoring intense situations, in which your instructions can sometimes mean the difference between life or death would make for a great game if done right. Well I personally think my wait is over.

While not perfect, 911 Operator delivers everything it needs to succeed in its premise. An endless free play mode in which you can play on a map of essentially any city or small town in the world! A campaign mode, in which you play in six different major US cities, with some calls you can't find in free play mode. And a nice mixture of intense and humorous situations from the 911 calls, some of which are based on real 911 calls you may have heard about, or come across online.

I was really impressed with the free play mode mechanic of being able to import a map from any city in the world. The maps are completely accurate to the real life locations, right down to the street names. I really enjoyed playing in the city I live in, and recognizing all these streets I drive through everyday.

While some people might find the game repetitive, I think there's enough variety of calls in free play mode to where I can play a decent amount of time before I either get tired, or have somewhere else to be. And each city in the campaign mode has interesting enough unique calls for me to want to restart the campaign city and play it over again. Like games like "Game Dev Tycoon" and "Cook Serve Delicious" I think this game is a fun time killer more than anything else. It's not trying to be anything huge, just trying to emulate the job of being a 911 dispatcher as a simple strategy game, and I think it does this well. Though maybe a little too well, this game can be just as addictive as those two others I mentioned. There were times when I needed to get some work done after playing, but my mind was telling me "One more game!"

I do have some negatives about the game, though. While this can sort of be seen as a compliment as well as a complaint, the game is really unpredictible, in that it can sometimes decide to completely screw you over. Giving you way more situations to deal with than you can handle, or deciding to spawn a situation at a location way to far away from the nearest police, ambulance, or firemen units. But I would kind of give it a pass considering that's probably pretty accurate to what happens in real life.

It can also be really annoying how often a simple arrest can end with the criminal getting injured after trying to fight the cops. Like I understand when it's a large group of people, and you have just as many, or too few cops, but there are times when I have two armed police officers investigate a suspicious individual, only for them to get into an altercation with them in the middle of the arrest. Then I have to dispatch an ambulance to the scene, and it just kind of needlessly drags the situation out. I wouldn't mind it so much, but it just seems to happen with almost every arrest.

At the time I bought this I have encountered a few bugs, but only two or three, and they didn't significanly hinder my experience. I would also say that I hope they add a few more calls in the future, just to add some more variety, I wouldn't mind some calls involving a scared kid, or a kidnapping. Maybe even a few that are specific to certain parts of the world. I mean any calls from Flordia shouldn't be like calls from anywhere else, if you know what I'm saying?

I would recommend 911 Operator to anyone who is interested in this idea as a game, it's probably exactly what you're expecting it to be. If you enjoy strategy and management games, I also think you'd be interested in this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend this game in its current state, although it could use some improvements here and there, which is as expected for a new game.... but it sounds like the devs know what's needed and plan to put out updates and DLCs.

As it sits now you are able to play the role of a 911 operator by taking calls and choosing which ones need units dispatched and which ones can be ignored, like accidentally calling 911. At the same time, you also have to take into account that someone may be a hostage and can't talk directly about the incident, and if you don't take your questioning far enough you won't figure out they're a hostage and you will miss the incident. At the time of writing this I'm sitting at 14 hours of gameplay and I could see myself playing up to around 25-30 hours before getting bored mostly due to reoccur incidents and lack of some key features to be talked about next.

I think some necessary items the devs should consider working on to allow players to log endless hours of gameplay would be as follows:
-More incidents and a wider variety to each incident
-The need to be able to play smaller more localized towns and suburbs. I can't play where I live because it's just out of reach of two cities.
-Ability to save the starting locations of units for each deployment
-Bigger dispatch areas
-And to drive it over the top they should look into integrating voice commands over a microphone. For ex: "Unit 3, Unit 8, Engine 12, Medic 2, respond to Motor vehicle incident at 123 Some street"
I know that last one is a stretch but I feel it would add that extra feeling of being a dispatcher and drive the game way over the top. And if these features were to come out as paid DLCs reasonably priced for the content in them, then I would definitely buy them.\t\t\t\t\t

I hope they add more missions and build on top of this but for the price its a great purchase!\t\t\t\t\t
