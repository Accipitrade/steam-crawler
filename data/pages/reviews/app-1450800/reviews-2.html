\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a charming and strange ( in the best ways ) survival, and exploration game. The radio is great when you are cruising around in space, and it has a fun open galaxy random exploration thing to it that is a well executed. You pick a planet and family to attempt to travel to Lilith with ( it is on the far side of the galaxy ). There are lots of ways to go about getting there and different ways to prepare for the journey.

The game is pretty easy to speed run if you care to do that, and my first victory was basically a build up on my starting planet and travel directly to the final destination in one trip. Once I understood that, the rest of the game mechanics were easier to get my head around.

The space parts are pretty amazing, the planetary parts less so, but are a nice break. It is great that the sum of its parts. This game can be chill or stressful and it is sort of amazing that it has a bit of everything, including the scattered memorials to the Nacho Wars, and other weird things in space. I am curious to see where this game goes during early access, and will be revisiting it regularly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoy space survival strategy games, and this unique open-world game hits the spot.

Gameplay focuses on a long migration journey to the other side of a vast galaxy, all while managing your resource economy along the way. There is no shortage of unique/weird content, which creates a surprisingly deep and humorous experience. There is a small learning curve to grasp both space sailing/navigation and planet survival mechanics, but clearing that hurdle really opens up the gameplay.

Pros:
- Lots of content (sooo many worlds to explore, interesting items, space stations, ship customization, etc.)
- Technology research is deep/fun
- Good amount of interactivity (combat, trading, investing, boosting stats with items, etc.)
- Actually funny in-game radio and awesome songs
- Devs seem to actively update

Cons:
- Smaller bugs (to be expected in Early Access) but nothing game-breaking
- Unfinished content (seems like game has more in the works, but completely playable still)
- Space Madness can't be cured, I think (wish I could turn this off so I can explore more)
- Needs some end-game difficulty balance (a touch too easy once you get your economy revved up)

Wishes:
- Co-op or multiplayer mode to play with friends
- Incentives/quests to explore more
- More radio content

This feels like a one-of-a-kind game and I'm excited to see where it goes. Recommended and worth the purchase.\t\t\t\t\t

I think this is the game Objects In Space was trying to create before they abandoned the project. Cartoony space aliens traveling an open world galaxy thats constantly trying to wear you down.

If you ever played spore, you can skip all the boring evolution stuff and go straight to the space age, but this time with an objective and direction, without losing the open world feel.

Loved:
- Overall game look, feel, play style
- The talkback radio had me laughing so much Id stop traveling just to listen
- the music is all original and honestly bangs
- Building and customizing spacecraft has a great set of challenges and decisions to be made as to how to optimize fit outs
- Resource management and crafting is relatively easy to work out, but resources are just scarce enough to make all decisions important.
- the tutorial is quick and lets you get straight into the action quickly. Worth spending the 10 minutes on
- Space storms! At first I thought they were game breaking, before I worked out how to effectively avoid them. Theyre a brilliant way to force players to strategize carefully.
- very easy to die, the game actively punishes you for bad decisions

Needs work:
- Game feels 75%-85% ready for beta
- Interactions feel extremely robotic and limited
- The early game feels perfectly challenging and balanced, however the balance (too quickly) shifts in favor of the player as you upgrade your tools and resource gathering items
- I had an issue (maybe a bug) where absolutely no course of action cured my space madness
- there are plenty of small bugs but the game is largely well built from the ground up and issue free
- feels like a lot of content is still being developed, so looking forward to seeing that come out (ie the black hole)

Not a fan of:
- the campaign feels like its way too short and theres so much potential here\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLilith Odyssey is a bold undertaking for a small indie dev team but the concept is well executed although not without some expected bugs (that from what I can tell the dev team is actively working through).

The game play is a mix between the Oregon Trail (get to your destination), flight sim, open world exploration/adventure, with a Fallout/GTA esque radio station thrown in for kicks. Ultimately this is a risk and resource management game where you gather resources to (safely) get from your starting home world to Lilith.

Once you get the hang of the controls, resource uses, and ways to advance the tech the game gets challenging from a survival perspective. Any misstep can get you killed or marooned in space (whether from an aggressive animal, space raiders, or space storms). And that's game over man, game over.

Overall I'm looking forward to spending time learning the quirks of this new mini universe and ultimately settling Lilith. Definitely would recommend. Chay Ho!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is fun, quirky Survival Adventure game, if frustrating at times (Typical for this genre). It's not without some jank, polish, some tutorial issues and the controls are not quite intuitive. But once you know what they are, it hardly becomes an issue. You will die or fail, a lot. Usually through starvation, getting eaten by a planet's wildlife or having your ship ripped apart through solar storms that leave you drifting in space to die.. But you learn from your mistakes, get more resources, find a balanced ship that works, and you go again. What is here is already really enjoyable, and is a solid foundation this early in Early Access.

The Devs are highly responsive to suggestions and are dropping patches according to even my own and others feedback. So when they say they want to work with their community, this is actually one Dev team who really mean it.

Also the humour, music and radio station chatter are brilliant in this game. These elements along with the game as it currently stands make it well worth the price of entry and an easy recommendation!\t\t\t\t\t
