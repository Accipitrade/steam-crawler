\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOriginal review:
I would wait on this one.

There's some really cool stuff in the works. It looks like a cross between Oregon Trail and the Sims, with some space travel thrown in. I really like the spaceship radio, the spaceship system in general shows some promise (there's cool touches, like names for the color on each vehicle), and the party generation is intriguing.

On a neutral note, there seems to be a focus on scoring. For some people this might be attractive. Personally, I dislike such things and I wish I could turn that off.

There's some rough choices in the interface:
Pause is F3. Selecting this kicks you back to the main menu, but doesn't save. I already lost progress because of this once.
Separating a stack involves click-and-drag, then right click on an empty spot. This is only references through the tutorial.
The tutorial dialogue box can't be moved. This might be fine if it weren't covering information such as energy and oxygen.
There's also no way to refer back to the tutorial dialogue that has already passed, and I couldn't find a key map (for things like stack separating, I had to go back to the beginning of the tutorial.)

I also ran into some issues during the tutorial, such as inconsistent information between dialogue boxes, and running out of resources on the starting planet due to a lack of pause and a slow reading pace. I've restarted the game three times now in an hour, and haven't managed to finish the tutorial.

Additionally, it's difficult to differentiate the ships at the original market at a glance. The list displays the model and price, but the features (which create price differences between models) aren't shown until the ship is selected from the list. When four ships in a row have the same name but different prices and stats, it can get confusing to remember which two you were deciding between. I'm not certain if this is an issue with other items, as I couldn't get very far into the game.

With everything said and done, I can't recommend it yet. Hopefully my mind will change in the near future.

Update: I changed the review type at the request of the developer. To their credit, they are very responsive. The tutorial issue mentioned above was fixed overnight.

There are still issues which can prevent the player from completing the tutorial, but given the developer's current desire to update it seems reasonable to believe these issues will be resolved in time.\t\t\t\t\t
