\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I were 10 years old again, this would be my childhood horse game. Other adults who played the likes of Barbie Riding Camp, Imagine Champion Rider and My Horse & Me etc etc know exactly what I'm talking about.

As an adult, it's still very enjoyable even if a touch 'surface-level'. It's clearly made for a younger teen/tween audience. The main story took me around 10 hours and I imagine unlocking all content would take around 20 hours, 30 hours at a push.

There is something special and magical about your favourite childhood game and HCA2 manages to replicate that; it took me a while to identify the emotion I was feeling in game. Cosy, cushy and almost-nostalgic fondness despite never having played the game before.

The first Horse Club Adventures was somewhat mediocre - enjoyable but with a few issues. As per an interview with The Mane Quest, its very refreshing to see a developer(s) that listen to feedback and the community, and as such reiterate on the game to make it even better.

Horse Club Adventures 2 features a redone world map, new NPCs and new features. The 10 minute races that require all-perfect jumps are gone and replaced with more refreshing and varied 1-4 minute races, including the new dressage and western races. The world actually feels alive now (and not freakishly empty) with cars on the road and NPCs walking around. Horse animations (and animations in general) are improved and you get a dedicated button to just walk (I like exploring and riding slowly, okay?). There has 100% been an attempt to move away from the "go here, talk to A, go here, talk to B" quest design and the quests are a lot more varied and another little detail I enjoy is the dialogue choices.

My only tiny, TINY gripe is the controls. The camera always tries to spin around to be behind you, and the horse direction is camera relative; additionally you can't slow down once you're sprinting. It just means you have to time your sprinting properly but it can be annoying sometimes.

Over its competitors & at this price point I would 100% say get it. Give yourself a few cosy evenings being a child again by wrapping up in a blanket and playing this game.

I personally hope we get a 3rd and 4th game in the series, to have one for each season, as the first takes place in summer and this one in autumn. My REAL pipe dream though is a 1st person camera.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought the first one for my 5 year old daughter (who can't read).

It ran like jank, was locked at 60Hz, and there was no voiceovers for the main protagonists, which rendered the game unplayable (for a 5 year old who can't read).

Horse Club 2 however, still runs like jank, but does run at 120Hz (albeit a rather stuttery 120Hz, somehow), AND the game's main protagonists have voiceovers for their dialogue, which makes the game totally playable for a 5 year old who can't read.

Gameplay unlikely to appeal to older 'proper' gamers, who actually want to 'game', but the Horse Club 2 certainly keeps my daughter highly engaged, and that is what it was for.....so 'thumbs up'.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wrote a review for the first games being optimistic about this series and I feel this second game has improved on the original. The accessibility issue I voiced in my review of that game has been resolved, both in having sprinting now be an (upgradeable) burst function (think Ocarina of Time Epona's carrots) and with the removal of 10+ minute races. The map has expanded and updated while maintaining a charming style and generally being a peaceful little horse game on a pretty map that I feel well matches the price of the game. It also expanded on types of gameplay and the use of side quests outside of just unlocking cosmetics, which I feel adds a nice sense of progress if you take the time to do them as they unlock.

While my overall opinion of the game is positive and as a whole, an improvement over the first, I have a handful of nitpicks that I feel should be brought up for a (hopeful) future game in the series. Again, I believe this game was developed for console and ported to PC, but I do still hope there is better menu navigation at least for the map in terms of hotkeys and controls. Having to open the whole menu just for the map is tedious, though at least the menu will now stay on the last tab you had open. Further on the map, I don't understand why personal waypoints are exclusive to a set number of locations (races, cleaning stations, and wardrobes) instead of letting the player just select any location on the map to set a waypoint. Considering there is a decently sized collection in this game with the golden horseshoes, this seemed like it should be a feature. Further, on the point of accessibility, I think the player should be able to change the camera to ride steady behind the rider and not have to bob in the settings. While this didn't bother me, I see someone with motion sickness being bothered by this on any console. Additionally, while I'm excited to see an expanding map, a lack of fast travel will become more of an irritation. There are a number of named locations that show up on the map that would work great for fast travel that just aren't utilized for this.

My main criticism though is the mixed implementation of upgrades through the side quests. Overall, I think this a good addition, but it's mainly focused on cross country (to be fair, the only real discipline in the first game). Collecting apples and doing the Mr. Kramer side quest chains will increase sprint and your "perfect jump" box respectively, but other side quests, even for those in other disciplines, don't have the same support. I feel like Western slowly improving your horse's agility and dressage adding a combo boost to cushion in the harder courses would have been really positive additions for these new race styles, which gameplay wise are fun, if their ribbons tend to scale faster than I think is appropriate for a game marketed to kids. Herding was the worst in this regard and only in a very few of these races was I able to get more than 1 ribbon. Considering a particular companion you make later in the game near the story beat around the herding races, it feels like that companion could have helped as you progressed this side quest.

Now, that's one paragraph of praise and two of criticisms, but the reason all of my criticisms remain largely nitpick is that none of these things block progression of the game or the achievements in game/on Steam. At most is blocks some cosmetics and filling up the little ribbon counter on the map, but that's it. The game still plays well and achieving at least the bronze ribbon is doable in all the races, which is the only requirement to get any of the race related achievements. The new gameplay styles are fun and while the story is a touch cliche and one note, it's still endearing and fun for a short little adventure. I do genuinely hope to get a winter and spring addition to the series, with all indications from the first game to this one being a positive indicator for the direction the devs are headed in.\t\t\t\t\t
