\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as I know, there is no other tool out there like it, which makes this an essential "playground" for SHMUP enthusiasts as well as hobbyists. The price is fair, and as long as you know what to expect, you won't regret delving into the intricacies of the software. Having said that, I would argue that in its current state, SHMUP Creator is in more of a(n) Early Access situation than a fully-featured and complete system. (The dev does not try and make it seem as if this is a complete program either, they keep providing updates and look at this as a long-term, WIP, which is great!) The benefit of all this is the developer is actively working on the software and engaging with users on Discord! The downside is that essential/expected features are not readily available, so you kind of have to wait for them to be implemented. I expect tons of features to be added over the coming months and years. I myself have already made a few suggestions for enhancements and they have been added to the list of future updates. Be aware: you are able to create a complete-ish SHMUP, from start-to-finish, if you want, with what is currently available. And since the software is still so new, nothing is yet established. This means that if you post your game on itch.io or any other site, and you have decent graphics (or use some of the provided assets, which do the job)... the game will be considered functional. Where there are issues, right now, is in terms of customization. Yes, if you spend the time to make your own graphics, and you tweak a lot of parameters, you can have a game that looks uniquely yours. If you go with the cookie-cutter approach and make a game in half an hour, it will work, but then it will play pretty much like the dozen other SHMUP Creator games that were made in a half hour (ie: the game-feel will be the same and people playing the games will notice that there is no difference, other than maybe the graphics... something you might have already experienced when playing amateur RPG Maker games). If you are looking for more specific customization (what really makes the great SHMUPS... great) these are not here yet. Once could argue that these limitations might make for more creative games, and I agree. Already, the current feature set of SHMUP Creator is more robust than what was available to devs in the 70s, 80s and 90s. One could easily emulate or replicate those games. It would obviously take some time (and skill) but it's totally doable. And this is wild/fantastic to think about!

The developer appears to be passionate about SHMUPS and this is a HUGE bonus. Someone who is aware of the niche and what makes certain SHMUPS great is super beneficial. The range of possible games is enormous. From the classics on the TurboGrafx-16/PC Engine and Sega Genesis/Mega Drive, to the arcade/Neo-Geo essentials, danmakus, cute 'em ups, etc. This is as close as we have come to having a tool that allows pretty much anyone to create one (or more) games from any of these genres. It's the best way to prototype an idea if you are coding your own engine. With time, I think this will even function as a perfectly acceptable tool to create SHMUMPs, from start to finish. It's just not totally there... yet. It's not too difficult to learn (the manual and instructions are still a WIP) but it is also not completely user-friendly. If, in the past, you have attempted to create your own game in another engine, this will be a breeze. If this is your first time, it may take a bit to become acclimated.

In the end, I have high hopes, I really do. As long as the developer does not abandon the user base and continues to offer regular updates and communicates directly with the community and user base, this will easily and quickly become something of a popular tool. There's RPG Maker, Construct, Godot, and now SHMUP Creator. It's still the early days and there's a ton of room for improvement. What we have now though, is pretty alright! Why not participate in the early days, so you too can help shape the future of a very promising game-making tool? I know I am!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm gonna be a little harsh with this since there is a lot of reviews already pointing out the good things.

(If you're looking into this for Touhou reasons, please read the very last part.)

First of all, simplicity is a huge upsite, but in this case it gets evened out by the limitations that come with it.
There is no way of using complex ways to do things that aren't possible with this tool's simplicity.

No costum code, no more than the slightest cosmetic changes for the 3 option Main Menu with a singular image for the title and a scrolling background behind it, aswell as similar things applying to the HUD of the game you're making. You can't truely costomize these things, rather only slightly reskin the evisioned preset design of the creator of this engine.

The only places where you have full controll is the content of levels. As the examples show there is a lot of unique and interesting things you can do within these, despite the limited tools. But for me that exitement paled quickly as I realized that outside of these levels, you cannot create any kind of scene that isn't "a level" and have no real control over how different scenes connect. Game mechinacs are also something you can not truely control. There is basically only checkboxes for some features and if these are features are not something you'd like, you're basically sutck with having no interesting gameplay.

With these flaws combined, any product made with this tool I'd come across I'd easily write off as "Oh, just some SHMUP Creator thing" and not look into it since I know, something made in this cannot truely be unique.

My conclusion
is that this is fun to play around with and definitely someones passion project, but if you're looking into creating a serious and legit project, I'd recommend using less restrictive tools.


Now, if you're interested into making any Touhou-related content with this I'd straight up recommend you to not buy.
It basically lacks every single key feature you'd need to create anything coming close to Touhou's original formula. Starting with most basic ones such as speed variations on button press or a normal way to have power based shooting
to set up of the screen. Alternatives are harder but look into them unless this gets big changes.\t\t\t\t\t
