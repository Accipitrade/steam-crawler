\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was really looking forward to this game, I love survival horror and the WW1 setting is a great idea, so I made sure I had an evening free to start playing it. Unfortunately it was massively disappointing. Besides managing to complete the game in 41 minutes, the trailers show about 80% of any environments of note, the rest of it is just the same plain trenches everywhere you go. The game relies heavily on using the same half dozen or so mismatched creative-commons-assets jump scares replayed over and over again ad nauseam.
The worst thing about this game IMO is the complete lack of survival horror elements despite it saying survival horror in the description. 90% of the game is walking around a maze looking for crying dolls then going "over the top" right at the end. You're stalked by a monster who I assume kills you if he gets you but in 40 mins of gameplay he only found me twice, both times I was able to lose him by running in the opposite direction for literally 10 seconds. Despite the trailers showing a gun, I didn't see it in my entire run. There's no inventory management, no combat, no puzzles or anything else you might associate with a survival horror game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will always find myself attracted to games based on the great war and now that I am expanding my horizons into horror I decided to play this game. Returned it after 17 total minutes due to the immense amount of cheap jump scares and for the fact that 99% of the horror of this game could fit in thematically with any other form of generic horror environment is truly disappointing. This game fails to capture some of the raw horrors of trench warfare or maybe even tall tales of horror from the war itself and instead focuses on ideas like "wow spooky baby collectibles" for the game play. Speaking of the game play it is literally slender the 8 pages with a hiding mechanic. The one interesting aspect is the fact that, in order to find the babies, you need to blow a trench whistle which alerts the monster and can theoretically lead to the monster finding you. I say theoretically as I blew that whistle a couple of times and no one ever showed up to murder me like all of the soldiers in the notes. I don't go out of my way to on games but the fact that this game has the groundwork of an interesting horror experience but then ruins it with "WHOA! SPOOKY JUMP SCARE!" every minute or two just makes it unplayable.

TLDR: Jump scare a minute horror game with immense missed potential. The prospect of blowing a trench whistle and bringing a monster is absolutely terrifying until no one shows up to kill me.\t\t\t\t\t
