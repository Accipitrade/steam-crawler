\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy sister loves horror and I love WW1, so I thought we'd try it out. We played for about an hour and a half and completed it with 3 or 4 attempts. The total game could probably be accomplished in around half in hour if you really knew what you were doing.

Pros:

- Awesome sound design. I love the ambiance as well as the spooky sounds that play whenever the monster (my sister calls him "the meat bag") spots you. Something about it triggers the fight or flight response, makes the hair on the back of you neck stand up. Personally I could very clearly pinpoint the position of meat bag with the audio, some reviews say otherwise. I was surprised how good audio is as created by a single dev, especially on the Unity engine ;-)
- Some interesting and fun jump scares. They reminded me of the flashbacks dungeons from the Fallout Games' Dunwich Borers. Personally I didn't like the "The Ring" character--I don't see her place in the Trenches. I suppose it's the protagonist's wife haunting him... still, kind of a trite jump scare. As a suggestion, keep the tall lady that zooms past you as the guy's wife, and perhaps add something like a shell-shocked soldier in a gas mask as a replacement for The Grudge lady. Could be even spookier and would fit the setting and lore.
- Attention to detail and story-telling. The little things really help piece the story together and don't fully make sense until the end. My favorite was when I walked past a slightly sunny spot and just barely noticed the protagonist's spooky shadow. Probably 20% of players will even notice that, so props to the dev. All the backround movement and minute jumpscares add to the tension. The hallucinations and the story all fit well together in retrospect, I am excited to see the final product.

Cons:

- Various frame drops at random times with a higher-tier PC.
- Gameplay can become tedious. A simple checkpoint system would be nice for us casual players. I dont mind hunting around the map for the dolls and getting spooked, but I do mind having to repeat it more than necessary. Maybe you collect 3 dolls, the game saves. Also I might just be a smooth-brain but I seriously could not find the dolls most of the time. I'd go around the entire map and not hear one, even with the glowing-baby whistle mechanic. Lots of annoying back-tracking.
- Confusing features. I never fully understood what the bottles were used for, they seemed to attract the meat bag more than distract. Also the jumping mechanic is either broken or has yet to be implemented. When I hid behind the rag, even if I waited for a while the second I got out meat bag would run after me. I'd have to wait several minutes to finally get him to go far enough away to get to the next doll. The worst one was along that long wooded hallway.
-Abrupt ending. I understand It's WIP and has limited dev time, but I was kinda hoping for a second stage. I look forward to one!
- Some bugs. Especially when in the hiding areas, the camera could easily clip through the side walls and the walls in front of you often would not render. Other world items, often planks of wood on the ground, would randomly de-render. The character's shoes would sometimes clip through the floor. Sometimes the monster would chase without the visual or audio ques. After completing the game, my cursor could not be used in the main menu and I had to alt-shift and close the game from the task bar. Nothing gamebreaking really, but bugs nonetheless.

Overall an enjoyable game that can be fun for any horror or history fan. My favorite part is the statue lady and her epic dubstep sound, makes me chuckle everytime.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHard pass. This commits every cardinal sin of horror games that annoy me. It's not ALL bad, but it's definitely not something I enjoyed. (Update at the end)
Pros:
- The lighting is great. There's a REALLY bright lamp? Sun? Sun lamp? Something overhead and it's a bit distracting, but I suppose that's the point of it.
- The smoke and the water droplets on the camera (more on that in a sec) make it really cool to kind of second guess if you saw movement in the trenches or if it's the atmosphere. Few horror games get this right.
Cons:
- First, there's a disclaimer that this game is a work in progress and it's made by one person. The game isn't listed as Early Access in the Steam Store. I don't know if this used to be in Early Access and it just released, but the message remains a day after launch. I can appreciate one person working on a game and being patient, but the majority of my problems with this game are design choices, not really constraints of time or quality.
- I haaaaaate blinking animations in games. You don't need to simulate blinking in a game. You know why? Because I'm blinking in real life. My eyeballs are already doing what the animation does, but my brain filters it out. When games do it, it's slowed down so you can see it which is kind of dumb because your real blinks are very fast and it's like the blinking is being controlled by someone else. I really wish horror games would knock this off. It's not immersive, it's obnoxious.
- The water droplets on the screen are a little annoying. First we have blink mechanics to make it more "immersive" and then you put water droplets on the screen when your eyes don't actually see water droplets like that on your eyeballs. It's more of an effect for a camera.
- Red flag is when I loaded up the game, the audio was suuuuper crunchy. Usually indicative of loud blown out jumpscares. And that's exactly what you get. Everything else is quiet, but it's just to get you to crank up your volume to accent the jumpscares. Which is fine when your jumpscares aren't blown out audio circa 90's newground/ebaumsworld games. I think I just dated myself a bit there.
- There's static images that randomly pop up of photos of trenches.
- The "Monster" that chases you isn't quite exciting. On that note, something else I'm annoyed by is there's a couple of "fake" monsters that chase you. Like random Grungesque kind of characters standing around, but there's no AI or animation. You just run through them. There's also like a random "doll" or something that attaches to your camera and wiggles around while a super loud jumpscare plays. Pretty sure I just groaned at that.

TLDR:
Really wanted to like this game and it has some cool features, but ultimately the intro screen says it's a work in progress (not listed as Early Access on Steam), jumpscares are just loud overblown audio, eye blinking mechanics don't translate to more fun or immersive (unless you're Before Your Eyes), and the game mechanics can be broken if you're not scared and just walk through to fake threats. This one is a hard pass.

Update: There's an update to the game that allows you to remove the blinking animation AND the water droplets! The game is now appropriately tagged for Early Access too! I still tend to be picky with indie horror so I may not personally still enjoy this game, but legitimately bravo dev for giving options to the players and in a quick response!\t\t\t\t\t
