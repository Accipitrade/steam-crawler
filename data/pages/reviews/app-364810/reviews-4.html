\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn the 90s, Star Trek: Starfleet Academy was heavily anticipated and it was supposed to be best Star Trek game ever. Certainly, it ended being a great game but did not end up meeting the hype and high expectations. Years ago, I bought the physical copy of Starfleet Academy but didn’t realize that a working joystick was essential and all the controls you had to memorize was overwhelming at the time so I gave up on the first mission.

Imagine my excitement when I picked it up on sale recently and now I finally get to give it a second chance. As a big Trek fan, I really enjoyed it. The basic story is you play as cadet on the command track who is working his way through Starfleet Academy by completing a bunch of simulations and at the same time, you must manage all the different personalities of your crew. The game does a good job with its FMV (full motion video) cutscenes which were common in so many 90s games though some of the acting needs improvement. Periodically during these cutscenes, you will have to make several choices which will change the direction of the story and affect your crew’s performance. William Shatner, George Takei and Walter Koenig do a fantastic job reprising their old roles from TOS.

You could tell the simulations/missions were TOS era “let’s blow them up” as they were really heavy on combat with a bit of diplomacy thrown in on the side. Actually too heavy on combat even for Kirk himself as some missions have you destroying several ships to complete your objective. I would have liked to see a better balance of diplomacy and action. The starships feel too fast but I know Bridge Commander refined what this game tried to do. I really enjoyed being able to play simulations of my favorite episodes/movies like Balance of Terror and The Wrath of Khan and even the infamous Kobayashi Maru scenario. Targeting subsystems was done very well. My main complaint with the simulations is that they do not give you a grade if you pass the mission so I have no idea how well I did (barely passed or aced it) and for a game called Starfleet Academy, grading should be really obvious.

Overall I highly recommend Starfleet Academy to Star Trek fans especially if you are in the mood for starship combat in the TOS era. I would love to see Steam release the expansion Chekov’s Lost Missions and Klingon Academy next.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMuch enjoying playing this!

Bad points:
Low resolution (640x480) but this is OK 90% of the time, just the side view of the ship when going to warp looks very stretched and chunky.
Its a bit buggy as in; you complete a mission only to get told you failed because of something which was out of your control. e.g., you fire a volley of photons at an enemy who is about to destroy you, after the first one hits, they decide to surrender, you accept, then the 2nd torpedo (which is already launched) hits them. You carry on with the mission only to get back to starbase and fail. This sucks a bit and on occasion I go play something else.
But after a while you learn exactly what to do and in what order, how to get through the mission quite fast (skip the talking and videos), so it is very much a learning process of "how to keep the game happy and pass the mission", not so much "being so good you can accomplish all missions without retrying"
Also sometimes the NPC ships / AI is very strong (suddenly hit by 2-4 torpedos and your ship is gone), yet sometimes the AI is very weak and you can manoeuvre in their blind spot out of weapons angle and smash their back doors in, so luck or how retarded the AI is can play a part.

Still very enjoyable, great to see the ST:TOS actors in the cut scenes.
The cadet actors are not as good, there is some truly over the top acting but hey low budget actors no doubt.
Only 1 crash (CTD) so far, which is much better than my WIn95 box on which SFA suffered frequent crashes!
About half way through now, 8 hrs played on medium difficulty.
I cant wait for KA if that gets re-released, just prey that have multiplayer for it :)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, this is an optimized version of a 1997 PC game that was an updated and ported version of the Super Nintendo original.

Welcome to the Starfleet Academy. You are a student captain attending classes in hopes of graduating and commanding your own starship. At the beginning of the game, you are given a crew that you have to manage while still accomplishing your missions. Each individual is in charge of a system and their view of you will determine their performance while on the ship.

The game is broken up into two sections the simulator missions and the academy where you interact with teachers and students. The academy is a bunch of videos with dialog choices. The simulator is where you will spend 90% of the game. For certain missions, you get to choose which ship you captain. This will determine the difficulty of that mission.
If you have never played a simulator game, the controls will be overly complicated. There are a lot of things you have control over which requires plenty of buttons. This is one of the many great features about this game. You control navigation, engineering, scans, combat, etc. Say you are in a fight and your shields are being depleted. You can switch to the engineering and increase power to shields by diverting other systems power. The game makes great use of the many features you see in the show and movies.

Since this is an academy, the missions are not all combat and depending on how you deal with some of the scenarios, the outcomes will be different.

The voice acting is quite cheesy for today’s standards. Back then they were a little over the top. Other than that, the sounds for the starships and combat are decent. You can tell that they did not try to update the sounds from the original game. The background music is not intrusive and goes well with the game environment.
Graphics…. Well, like I said, this is an optimized version of a 1997 game. The cut-scenes have been optimized for our larger displays but not updated to look any better (trust me, I am playing this on a 4k 55” TV). The 3D models are quite basic and are not very detailed.

With all that said, once you get used to the controls and are in the missions, you will easily overlook the outdated graphics and be concentrating on finishing of those cloaked Klingons that ambushed you while investigating that colony.

The game has its faults, but they are mostly due to how old the game is. Other than that, this is definitely a great game for any Star Trek fan who wants to feel what it is like to captain a starship. Just wish they also ported the expansion disk " Chekov's Lost Missions" to complete the experiance.

Also: if you kept a copy of your old game saves, they work perfectly in this version. You can load them up and visit your favorite missions or continue where you left off.\t\t\t\t\t
