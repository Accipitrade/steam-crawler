\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShatter tiles and walk thru long corridors in Ten Million! 10,000,000 is a match 3 puzzle game dressed up with RPG elements. The tiles you match correspond to your characters actions as he travels the games "dungeon", i.e. match keys to unlock doors. There are also stone and wood tiles which allow you to upgrade your home base, which allows you to upgrade your character between runs; and a couple of different attacks to help with monsters. It's very subject to the whims of the RNG so some runs can end very quickly whilst others will have everything fall into place with little effort. Pun not intended. The graphics are a little rough around the edges but do their job and the same could be said of the audio, all in all this is a cheap time waster that is sometimes fun, sometimes frustrating. I wouldn’t want to pay more than $2 for it but it has its charm and a nice RPG gimmick to separate it from other match 3's.

Good
- Lots of gameplay
- Fun, when you get on a good run

Bad
- Graphics are simple and unpolished. They do the job, but thats about all they do
- A little too luck dependent for me
- Grindy

Recommended only if you are a huge fan of this type of game
3/5

This is a port of the mobile game of the same name. It is a match 3 game where you either slide a row or a column to make matches for whatever is needed at the time. Locked Doors and Chests require keys, Monsters require Swords and Staves, Shields are used for defense, Stars for items to help, and Stone and Wood for building your home base afterwards.

The objective is to go as far as you can in a dungeon, and upon failure you take all the resources you gathered to upgrade your home base to allow even further runs. The main goal is to have a total score of over 10,000,000 in one run of a dungeon.

It's fun for a few hours, though the game is pretty short. If you can get it cheap, it'll be good value.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMatch 3 style games are mostly about 'randomness' and 'luck' with only a pinch of 'skill'. As such these games will take you on an emotional roller coaster; when things don't go your way you will feel deep low's of frustration. But when things are going your way you feel elation from the accomplishment of beating the odds.
  1. This game can be very hard, you need to be extremely quick in matching tiles. There is a sort of 'continual countdown timer' where if you do not match tiles quickly enough then your 'session' will come to an end. You can increase your session 'time' by creating a match 4 or 5 of certain tiles or by eating food. On the other hand, if enemies hit you then your 'time' will decrease.

  2. The style of 'matching tiles' in this game is similar to 'Puzzle Kingdoms' except in this game you can slide as far as you like along the entire row or column.

  3. You will fail alot in this game especially at the harder difficulties as the odds are stacked against you. But 'winning' every play session is not how this game works. You just take each 'play session' as far as you can; you will earn resources and experience every play session which you keep regardless. You spend this upgrading your dwellings, weapon, armor, spells, equipment, etc. So the idea is that you are playing the game for the match 3 puzzle/challenge and just let the progression happen in the background naturally.
I feel this game is more for an advanced or veteran match 3 puzzle player and will not be friendly to new comers of the genre.\t\t\t\t\t

Match 3 as Action RPG...

What sounds like a weird idea actually plays quite nice. You have to reach 10,000,000 Points in score to escape the dungeon.

To reach this you have to skill up and gain enough resources to upgrade your character and beat up attacking monsters, instead of hitpoints you have a timeout, which is reached when your character touches the left border of the area.

Pros:
- Very easy to learn game, the match 3 genre does not need to be explained.
- Fast and intuitive gameplay.
- Solid programmed, No crash encountered during gameplay.

Cons:
- Soundtrack can get fast enervating... either switch it off or keep it on low volume

Recomended:

If you don't have a match 3 game in your library you might wish to try this one. It's about five bucks for this game, which might look like a bit high. But if you have a chance to get this game during a sell out or otherwise cheap. Take your chance. I am not saying it's too expensive, since the programmers have made it a good experience to play.
