\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10,000,000 is kind of like a mix between a match 3 puzzle game and an endless runner with a dungeon crawling theme. You match swords and staves to attack monsters, keys to unlock doors and chests, and materials to fortify your base between runs. When you're between runs you can also stop to spend gold and experience on leveling up your equipment and player abilities. It's not a bad game and the mixed elements work well with each other. I enjoyed the time I've spent playing 10,000,000.

My one gripe about the game is that it feels totally dependent on RNG whether a run will be the one where you actually complete the game or not. You have to have perfect (or just really really good) luck with tile drops on the play field and food drops from the treasure chests to keep the game going as long as possible. You'll wind up losing through no fault of your own simply because you didn't have enough of the right tiles on the screen to unlock a door or to slay a dragon or something. It's super frustrating as well because maxing out your skills and stats won't bring you to a high enough level to defeat certain enemies with ease. It makes all the grinding feel like a waste. If I lose I want it to be a skill issue, not something up to random chance.

Overall 10,000,000 is a fun way to waste a weekend. It won't change your life or anything but you won't feel ripped off or let down while playing it.\t\t\t\t\t
