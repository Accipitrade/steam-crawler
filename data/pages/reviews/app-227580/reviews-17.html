\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recently finished 10,000,000 and had a great time doing so. You can fire it up quickly to play a couple of rounds or you can get stuck to it for a hour, because it has the "just one more run" feel to it.

Your goal is to accumulate 10,000,000 points by repeatedly entering a dungeon, slaying monsters and grabbing loot. Your run is over once your hero is pushed out of the left side of the screen. The screen keeps moving right as long as you are standing still, frantically trying to spot fitting tiles and you are pushed further back by monster attacks.

Once you reach a door or a chest you want to find and match key tiles to grab gold or items. If there is a monster ahead, you can match sword- or staff tiles to either trigger a physical or a magical attack (some are more vulnerable to magic, some to physical damage). If you match wood- or stone tiles, you gather resources to upgrade the rooms in your hub. They grant access to the blacksmith to upgrade your sword or the alchemist to use powerful potions and many more. This gives a great feel of progression. Your first runs might be over in about 30 seconds, but the more you grind and unlock, the longer you survive in the dungeon.

What makes this game so entertaining are the split second decisions you have to make. You might see an enemy behind a crate: to get to match 3 key tiles you would have to sacrifice 4 sword tiles, but they are necessary to fight the monster ahead. Can you find another solution that opens both the crate and gives you 3 or more tiles to attack the enemy, before the time runs out? Remember: the worst move, is no move!

If you are a fan of match 3 games and (grindy) dungeon crawlers, you should check this one out. They are combined flawlessly here.\t\t\t\t\t

TLDR: Match3 sidescroll brawler hybrid with rock paper scissor combat mechanics. Grind oriented with RNG mechanics and unwieldy controls without Xbox pad support.

It is a match3 game in which you must rotate entire rows around the board to match. A character stuck in a runner sequence will habitually encounter chests, doors, monsters. I found the game not immediately obvious in how the sword and the magic wand really differ and I assume it some monsters soak one or the other. Additionally this game has been criticized for encouraging mass-matching without care or thought for strategy, essentially making the entire RPG half completely redundant and irrelevant.

Furthermore this game works better on mobile with its sequel You Must Build a Boat. But neither of these actually offer proper Xbox360 pad controls and the game becomes a bit unwieldy and subject to RNG and grinding.

Since I am not a huge fan after careful consideration of the execution of this hybrid concept. I will simply hold on to Starlaxis instead due to its better looking engine and gamepad support and same basic concept, you control a spaceship and do spacebattles instead of a 8bit sidescroll indiana jones lookalike.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTen Million is a nice little match 3 puzzlegame in which the end goal is to score ten million points.

Your run ends when you run out of time, with your character slowly moving towards the left of the screen as time goes on with the left edge being the end of you. Taking damage through enemies pushes you even further quickly, getting kills among other things pushes you to the right again.

You play by dragging a line or column around and then having at least 3 of the same tile in a line. There is only a few tiles that dont change either and each has its own use, swords and staffs do physical and magical attacks, keys unlock doors and chests etc. Above the field you can see what you are currently faced with and therefor know if you for example need to match key tiles.

During each run you gain ressources that all serve the purpose to make you stronger and go further, be it by having your attack tiles do more damage or by increasing your defense or increasing critical hit chance etc. While this means you grind for ressources run for run it doesnt really feel like a grind upgrading is more like something on the side to do next to playing the game.

Another distraction is the little side quests that you get, many are luck based or happen sooner or later anyway, like matching 5 of the same tile in a single strike multiple times in one run, others require you to use specific items (that you randomly acquire during runs and since you can only carry 3/4 use quickly again anyway)

While you will gain ressources no matter how you play the game does reward you for playing well, for example if you manage to be so far ahead of time that you end up on the right edge of the screen and can keep that up for a bit it gives you a nice amount of bonus experience or gold and big chain reactions or 5 tiles in a row etc also benefit you.

In the end getting to the 10000000 scorepoint isnt that complicated, especially if you have every single upgrade and think carefully what potions to use, however the steam achievements crank it up a notch requiring you to achieve that same score with a much lower point multiplier, setting an endgoal even people who are good at these games will have to work for to reach.

You can probably beat the game in less than 4 hours though i would say it isnt really made to rush through in one sitting, rather to unwind with a little puzzlegame for a bit every evening, or something like that. And while the pricepoint is higher than many similar games I would say it is almost worth it and totally worth it when on sale for even less.\t\t\t\t\t
