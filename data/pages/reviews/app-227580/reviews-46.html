\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10,000,000. An RPGish, Bejeweled-like game. Well... ok...

While the game does have an interesting concept it annoyingly relies on luck and what tiles the game decides to generate more than anything else, much like Bejeweled or similar puzzle games. If a monster is in your path, you'll have to quickly match sword or staff tiles to do damage. A locked chest requires matching key tiles. And as tiles are random, one might find oneself stuck, matching any tiles at all, hoping to match enough of what is needed before time is up.

At the start of the game, be prepared to lose, quite often, until enough materials are gathered to unlock upgrades. While initially the upgrades that can be purchased come in handy and allow one to make quick work of monsters, the upgrades have a max level which in my opinion is too low. Especially during the latter ranks, and especially when fighting bosses. Even completely maxed out requires, more often than not, way too many tile matches and wastes way too much time. However, there are items to find thru out the dungeon run which can help somewhat. Scrolls that will change wood or stone tiles to keys, swords, or staves. Various weapons and orbs to hurl at monsters. Skeleton keys to easily unlock chests or locks. And food items that will give you a time boost. With that said, the weapons, orbs and skeleton keys must be used rather precisely; if used a split second too early when arriving upon a chest or monster the item does nothing and disappears. The same can be said for tiles too. It would have been nice if there was some leeway as making sure the dungeon runner is close enough to an obstacle before using items can waste valuable time, even if just a split second.

And possibly the biggest time waster in the game, combos. Match some tiles, more fall down and they match. Combo! They are sometimes handy like during a boss fight. Except when the tiles continue to fall and continue to match it becomes a potential death trap. Yes, I've lost more than once awaiting tiles to stop falling and matching others because during this time nothing else can be done. And if the reason I lost wasn't because I ran out of time, it was shortly after because the combo completely used up tiles that would have came in handy just a few seconds later.

Replay value? Prepare to spend several hours upgrading everything before even attempting a run for 10,000,000. But after one does achieve freedom by getting to a score of 10,000,000, the only reason I can think of to come back to this game would be to try for a higher personal score (no Steam leaderboards), or to earn all achievements. Personally, once I complete all the achievements I doubt I will ever play this again.

Is 10,000,000 fun? Sure, it can be. Would I recommend it? Hmm. Not at the full price of $4.99. I picked it up on sale for less than a dollar. At that price it was worth it. While I don't really want to vote thumbs down, I'm going to have to since Steam has no neutral option.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo simple, yet so fun and addicting. This match'em puzzler combines simple 3-in-a-row matching with simple rouge-like rpg elements to create an interesting hybrid. The game is really short, lasting about 4 hours until completion, but I've been able to come back to this game and start from the beginning many times. I even purchased the Android version to play on my tablet, and the portibility and touch screen mechanics work better than PC. Nonetheless, this was a great game and I highly recommend you support these indie devs. I await the sequel!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's really disappointing to see all these negative reviews for the game. It's a match 3 puzzle game (but instead of swapping two tiles, you move an entire row or column to match, so it'll take a bit to get used to), but instead of leaving it at that adds in some RPG and combat elements to keep things interesting. In short, you make runs to escape a dungeon (which happens at 10,000,000 points - spoilers!) by matching tiles; sword and staff tiles do damage to enemies you run into, key tiles open locked chests and doors, backpack tiles give items that aid you along the way, and wood/stone are resources you use to upgrade rooms in the initial area you start in. At the end of the day it's still a match 3 puzzle game, so if you're not into that this probably won't do anything to change your mind, but the system of progression it uses to keep things interesting is great. Relatively short, I guess, considering it has a definitive end, but you can keep playing after the end for more upgrades/higher scores. It kept me entertained long after completion on iOS and the PC version will likely do the same.\t\t\t\t\t
