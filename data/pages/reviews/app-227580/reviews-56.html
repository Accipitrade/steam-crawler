\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI thoroughly enjoyed this fine little time waster.

So, yes. It's kind of like Bejeweled. The matches you make down below push your character through combat and exploration above. Each "run" through the dungeon takes a minute or two. But as you run, you achieve a series of objectives which the game gradually presents to you, which leads to improved skills.

The game's genius is the way it rewards you not for a lifetime score, but instead for pushing your single-run high score higher and higher, until you can reach 10,000,000 in a single run. As your character improves a bit with each run, cresting your previous high score becomes theoretically easier each time you run the dungeon. Die, and you can either quit the game, or try one more run almost instantly.

It's not a deep game, but I can't think of many games that made "just one more turn" so compelling. Like, "Civilization"-style compelling. It's so easy to try again, over and over. A minute here, a minute there, next thing you know, 2 hours have gone by.\t\t\t\t\t
