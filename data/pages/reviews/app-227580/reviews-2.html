\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is, basically, Match 3 meets Action RPG (kinda/sorta, maybe a bit of a stretch to call the fights ARPG-type). Anyway, at its current price point, there's just no way I can recommend it to any sane person, especially in 2022. At this point in time, it's got the price tag of a premium mobile game, but it's got way less content than even free mobile games. And yes, I am aware that this is a PC title that came out in 2013. You'd think they'd have lowered the price, but eh, who knows. Maybe the devs forgot this game of theirs exists, tbh. It's that forgettable.

4.99? Come on. Something like 1.99 would be way more appropriate, for the amount of content there is. With 5 euros you can buy a game that will last you for weeks/months (especially if you get it on sale), depending on your tastes. A fine example would be Vampire Survivors. And again, there are free games out there with much deeper progression systems than this. Hell, you might as well play Windows Solitaire, if you want. That never gets old. But I guess that might be too old-school for today, huh. Well, anyway, here's a bit more;

Is this a "fun" little game you can finish in a single afternoon? Yes, it is, and that's pretty much it. There's not much depth. It's mostly grinding, grinding, grinding (in regular match 3 fashion) for permanent upgrades and trying to time your matches properly for the toughest fights for the last achievements. In short, this is a game to keep you busy for 1-2 lazy afternoons. And it just feels like a Flash game you'd play on sites like Kongregate back in 2010 or so.

You can finish it in a couple of hours. I have registered 11.3 hours because I wanted to get all the achievements and also idled for a long time to get the cards.

FYI, I got this game in a bundle, I'd never pay 5 for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you want dialogue, NPCs, story, and interest, you want You Must Build A Boat. 10MIL is basically the prototype of YMBAB, without any kind of the aforementioned story elements. It's a straightforward puzzler with some RPG-style elements, but ultimately it is a grinding game. There is a lot of tedium built into the core gameplay loop that isn't broken up in the same way as its successor, and I'm not a huge fan of it tbh.

If you want a quick-start puzzle game that doesn't have much in the way of reading or narrative, this is a worthy pickup, but imo the successor is more polished and better in about every way. 10MIL does have some charm and stuff going for it though, so up to you. I'd say the $5 is a pretty good price for it.\t\t\t\t\t
