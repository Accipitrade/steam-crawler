That... that pretty much says it all! Do you like Chuzzle? Then you'll probably like 10,000,000.

Pros:
-Tried and true match-three system.
-Unobtrusive soundtrack.
-Unobtrusive graphics.
-No obnoxious in-game purchases.

Cons:
-Unimpressive soundtrack.
-Unimpressive graphics.
-Gameplay is an endless grind.

Overall: 4.5/10, a hair below average. If you're looking for a great match-three puzzler... eh, you can do better. That said, if you're a parent, I'd bump it up to 6/10 just because Little Johnny and Susie can't rack up ten thousand dollars on power ups.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA pretty decent casual game with a fun 8 bit aesthetic. Essentially it's a 3 match puzzle game much like any shuffle games candy crush etc etc. However in this game you need a bit more strategy on when to match what. You have the options between two types of attack, magical and physical, which effect various monsters in various ways. In addition to this you have key blocks to open doors and chests, all of these pieces are needed to continue the "run" trying to get as many points as you can. In the beginning it'll be tough, but there are ways that you can save up supplies that you earn in each run, by clearing stone and wood blocks and those are used to upgrade each room.
All in all this is a pretty fun game, a quick casual puzzle with aspects of a rpg. Take that as you will.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wouldn't buy it again.

This game can best be described as Tetris-like. Your object is to arrange the tiles in groups of three or more in order to eliminate the tiles, gain a bonus, and have more tiles drop into the field of play.

In my opinion, You Must Build A Boat (YMBAB) -- which is by the same developer -- is exactly like this game and is much better. If I had bought this game before YMBAB I might have liked it more. But, unfortunatley, it suffers from the fact that the two games are essentially alike.

I have read other reviews that describe this game (and its other incarnation) as adventures in grinding. This is true. To be honest, I had enough of grinding when I played WoW for seven years. I'm tired of grinding. It's pointless and those moments of my life are more valuable then to keep edging toward some arbitrary goal -- in this case, ten million points.

I always have a questionable amount of fun playing games that are laregly influenced by luck. Perhaps I am not good at thinking visually in three dimensions and, therefore, I'm not able to plan and strategize well in this type of game.

Even YMBAB suffers from this influence of luck. Ultimately, you can move tiles as quickly as you want or need to, but if you don't get the right tiles dropped into place then it you're eventually doomed. Therefore, it really doesn't matter how well you make decisions, how fast you operate, or what your strategy is. Eventually, you will succeed just because of the forces and fates of luck.

I did enjoy this game -- but to be clear I don't think you need to own both this and YMBAB and YMBAB offers more compelling interest. There just wasn't enough positive happening here when I succeeded to compel me to continue.

Buy either this or YMBAB but not both.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere arent too many puzzle games that do what this one does. While it at first might seem like a simple match-4 game with some fantasy elements thrown in, there is so much more below the surface. 10,000,000 is a puzzle game that focuses on upgradeable equipment, dungeon running, resource collecting, and finishing quests. Now, I dont know about you, but there arent too many puzzle games that come to mind that can check off those same boxes. This game does something very special: it makes you want to solve puzzles, not just to get to the next level, but to progress in every sense of the term. Your character will gain access to better weapons, shields, and even potions which help all help customize the experience. 10,000,000 does all of this while being wrapped in a very basic pixel-art shell with music that is guaranteed to be stuck in your head for days.\t\t\t\t\t
