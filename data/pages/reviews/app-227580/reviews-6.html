\r\n\t\t\t\t\ta match game with a few unique characteristics. Your goal is to escape from imprisonment by collecting 10.000,000 points. You get these points by running mazes. There are creatures to kill, doors to open, loot to gather. All these things are gained by matching. If you match swords, you attack with swords. If you match keys, you open a lock. The loot you gain can be used to upgrade your armor, weapons, and many other things. All this matching is done to an invisible ticking clock that kicks you out of the maze when it counts down to zero.

Mindless fun. Could run on your calculator which means you can run this in the background while doing your homework or writing that project report for you boss. The game is usually available for less than one of those coffees everyone drinks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10,000,000 is solid and all, but there's nothing particularly nice about it on PC and you can get it for $1usd cheaper on mobile. The only time I recommend this for Steam is if you can get it on a sale, which may come more frequently than on the mobile app stores, so that's something to consider.

10,000,000 is a pretty simple match 3 game, but has some nice elements to it. Moving whole rows or columns instead of switching spots is a nice mix-up. Another important distinction to make between 10,000,000 and many match 3 games is that the game has encounters which occur in real-time. As opposed to slower-paced match 3's which limit you in terms of the number of moves you can make, 10,000,000 just pushes you to make matches fast. This definitely makes 10,000,000 have a less prominent strategy element and a bigger speed element. This doesn't mean strategy doesn't exist in 10,000,000 though. The strategy of the game comes into deciding when to clear your board of wasted space (not all matches have good results), and when to use the items you pick up along the way.

There's also small RPG elements with upgrading your base and weapons, but these aren't such strong aspects of the game because they are simply a matter of time, not skill or true decisions to attain. To add on another level to the game though, there are potions which add modifiers and are quite nice. Other than what I've described above, the game doesn't have much else that's interesting or exciting. It's a nice little game, but it's pretty tame.

The small shortcoming of 10,000,000 is that the player hub actually has a really hard to see interface. The reason I have 20+ hours is because I was missing a key upgrade in the player hub which was preventing me from progressing. After I finally noticed the upgrade, I was able to beat the game instantly. In reality 10,000,000 is a 3-5 hour game. Another shortcoming is chain matches, especially huge damage ones, won't deal damage to enemies if you're not yet at them, even though the previous obstacle is cleared. Maybe this is a key element of the balancing, but for me it's dissatisfying. I would rather the game appropriately tune up enemy health, and let chain matches have consequences even when you've yet to reach enemies.

Solid title, just get it on mobile.

6.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is okay, it's a casual puzzle game similar to candy crush or bejewelled where you match 3+ tiles and you get a score. The twist is that you're adventuring through a dungeon while you do this and the tiles specify your action, so match 3 swords and do a physical attack, match 3 keys and open a chest, your character runs along a linear track with random encounters and you have to match to complete the task. It's a race against time if you take to long to beat the monster or open a door you lose and you're back to the start of the dungeon. There is a progression system in which you can use stone, wood, gold, and XP to upgrade your heroes stats. The game feels like a complete time sink, the mechanics are clunky and there is no sense of progress being made between runs, at the end of the day you're playing the exact same game from the minute you start to when you end. I put 9 hours into this in a past life and I'm sure it was okay then but it's nothing special, and has no redeeming qualities\t\t\t\t\t
