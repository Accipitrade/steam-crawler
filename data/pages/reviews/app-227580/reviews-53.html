\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a puzzle game at heart. If you line up 3 or more coloured tiles they disappear from the grid, they disappear. In much the same fashion as Bejeweled, new tiles then fall down from the top of the grid to fill in the now empty space. The only way this differs from Bejeweled is that instead of swapping two tiles, you instead can drag any row left or right, or any column up or down in its entirety to make a match.

However, this is only half of the game, and is frankly the boring bit. Each set of tiles you clear does something different, with the aim of keeping your man running through the dungeon above the board as long as possible. So sword and staff attack monsters, shield defends you, keys open locks, backpack provides items, and wood and stone help you upgrade your base. So this balance of keeping the right tiles on the screen, especially when different monsters are weaker to different attacks, is what keeps this game enthralling.

That said, it does have some problems with artificial difficulty. Your health/stamina is represented by how far along the top of the screen your man is. Once this runs out, you have to restart the level for another try at the high score. However, as your man runs through the dungeon, the time it takes to slide backwards along the track gets shorter and shorter, decreasing your max health no matter how well you are playing. As a result, you often need to start at a later checkpoint to have any chance of progressing to a higher score. I'd have much preferred just a straight run through increasing power/health monsters than a backwards slide down my character's own abilities.

So overall:
Negatives:
The match game is Bejeweled and requires little strategy.
Decreasing power through out a run gets on my nerves.
Positives:
The dungeon combat system is inspired and brings the strategy that Bejeweled lacks.
The power increase due to upgrades between runs is well thought through and well paced.

If this game had an endless mode, that is a mode where monster health/attack power went up constantly until you die, I'd recommend this in an instant.
But without this it lacks replay value as you feel like you're hitting a hard-coded wall instead of the limits of your own ability.

So a reasonable game for 3$, is enjoyable, just don't expect too much playtime from it. 7/10\t\t\t\t\t
