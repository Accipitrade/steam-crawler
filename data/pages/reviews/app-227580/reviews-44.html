\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, for a dollar it was kind of ok, even if I didn't finish it. I think I'd be more disappointed if I'd spent more than that. It's an ok way to kill time, but unless you're really into the core mechanic of matching tiles, it's really just matching lots of tiles while numbers get bigger. You can buy stuff along the way to make the numbers get bigger more quickly, and there are sub-goals and achievements for getting your numbers big enough along the way, but there's not much substance to it. It does do what it says it does for the most part though, so there's that, aside from the mouse occasionally hitching when sliding tiles around, which I've heard works better in the mobile version (which is easier as a result). I got tired of how repetitive/grindy/luck-based it gets toward the end, which is a poor combination with my life-long inability to read ahead beyond the current move in match-three games.\t\t\t\t\t
