First, this game is good. It's ported from mobile devices. And it's ported well. It's more easier to control with your mouse. Also it has Steam Achievements. It's good. And it's bad, but I'll tell you about it later. But besides it, this game is awesome puzzle adventure. I thought that playing puzzle games is very boring. I think some Pokemon casted Confusion on me. Puzzle can be interesting. If you'll bring RPG elements to it. It's cool to repair and upgrade your castle, it's cool to learn perks and to upgrade your skills. It's cool to play puzzle game. I'VE SPENT ABOUT 15 HOURS FOR THIS. 15 HOURS FOR PUZZLE. I HAD NIGHTMARES... STAFF, SWORD, KEY, WOOD, STONE. Damn, if you're fond of 100% things, don't buy it. GO AWAY. YOU SHALL NOT PASS.
BTW, they're making the sequel. You Must Build A Boat. They said, it will be bigger and better. You can even put your saves from 10,000,000 to it. You'll get it for free, if you own 10,000,000. I'll get it, but if they'll make something like The Jose, I'll never play it. That's it. My review. You have a choice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter slaying innumerable dragons and demons during what was definitely the best run I ever had, and within inches of recieving the score of ten million, my run was ended by my lack of ability to open a treasure chest. This wasn't the first time, and each time seemed to be more infuriating than the last. This was after hours of trying, and with every upgrade and perk purchased. This was the final nail of the coffin, and if this doesn't convince you of why you should avoid this game, please, continue reading.

****

The game does shine at first, promising variety and throwing you straight into the action, but the game quickly begins to demonstrate an abject amount of laziness. The graphical style is retro, to say the least, but the game is very modern, feeling like a frankenstein of other popular mobile games crushed into one jagged mess. 90% of the sceen is devoted to a very archaic match 3 game where you have to move entire rows instead of individual tiles, and is where you will be putting your focus, but the top half is where the "action" occurs. Up top is an endless runner directly tied to what you do in the match three. Match up swords, you'll attack the monster. Match up keys, you unlock the chest. However, if you don't address the problem in front of you, you'll be slowly pushed off screen and your game will end. Good in concept, but here's my biggest issue: like every match 3, score is a large component, especially given how the game is literally named after the ideal score. However, to do this, you must compile the best combos and deal with the problem at hand, but you have to do it directly infront of the creature or object. If you matched the correct set a second before you stop in front, be it in anticipation or by accident, it doesn't stack and your move becomes invalid. This is particularly frustrating when you have to match certain sets to move on, only to watch your carefully calculated moves get screwed up by a chance mega combo. Too many runs have been ended that way, and to have treasure chests be a mandatory to unlock is absolute bull. There is absolutely no option to skip anything you encounter, which would've spared the game some frustration, but the game just doesn't care enough. Another mobile trope is the "meet one of the three objectives" thingy that also grinds my gears, as they don't enhance or challenge, but merely infuriate as their increasingly impossible demands completely based on luck (or in my case, rawheaded stubborness) to recieve, solely to unlock a harder difficulty with more objectives.

Note how I said difficulty instead of level. This is because there is only one level in the game: A grey dungeon hallway. A hallway that never ends. Monsters, for the most part, are simply pallet swaps with other monsters, which is uncannily lazy for a game in which even the most complex characters are composed of 20 pixels and are the size of your thumb. The only thing you can change is difficulty, which ramps up quickly and is basically required of the player to grind for hours just to recieve some paltry bonuses to attack just to HAVE THE CHANCE to get by. Quickly you learn that some monsters can only be substantially hurt by magic and others by striking, but the problem, once again, is that you rarely have the right board to fight them. Even during your best games, your best is never good enough, for the board may just vomit out a variety of support tiles that do absolutely nothing to help your awful situation, and then your run ends. Outside of a couple items you might get by chance that are specifically one attack or the other (and even they don't always finish the job), the game never gives you the advantage for long, if ever at all.

Tl;dr: 10,000,000 demands concentration and strategy when it builds itself around luck and reflex. Conflicted even in design, the game never fails to give you the worst possible set up in front of the most dangerous to the most menial tasks, matching frantically until you either lose or move past the obstacle for another few seconds before you're forced to beg again for lady luck to bless you again. Like the trials of Sisyphus, your attempts to push forward will gain you ground, but will very quickly add momentum to your downfall. Unlike our unlucky boulder rolling friend, however, this game is not your punishment for defying the gods, and you can easily skirt this suffering by not buying this game. So don't. Please.
