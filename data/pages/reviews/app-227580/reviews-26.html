\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10,000,000 is a simple straightforward match3 /rpg action game with one goal in mind: score 10,000,000 points in one run to escape the dungeon. It may seem impossible initially within the first ten minutes of the game, however as the game progresses and as your rank increases, you'll find yourself having your goal become tantalizingly closer and closer. Of course, it is a bit of a grind fest; you'll need to earn points and materials for upgrades and to pass certain ranks. I would say this though, if you hate match 3 games or if you aren't that good at them, this game is not for you. Nevertheless, it's a great little addicting game that isn't pretentious and provides lots of hours of fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTiny Review by Alderon

It's a good game. It is... Just not as enjoying as i hoped.

The good parts:
- Retro pixel graphics.
- Dive right in.
- Casual gameplay for lunchbreaks but fun enough to avoid getting stale.

The bad parts:
- Grinding, oh how i hate grinding.
- Shallow gameplay.
- RNG based puzzles and rewards.
- Challenges.

The conclusion:
Casual players will enjoy this title a lot and i'd recommend it to them. Gamers wishing for more depth in their game should look elsewhere. 6/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic puzzle game, 10,000,000 thrives on the ability to hop right back in after a crushing defeat. Your ultimate goal is to secure a score of 10,000,000, finally being able to escape from the clutches of a castle that you've been locked away in. Various upgrades are also available, in which you can boost your various attacks, as well as creating special potions with fantastic, and maybe some not so fantastic perks.
My only complaint is that it still is, at its heart, a mobile game. Nevertheless, the $5 dollar price tag still provides hours of fun, especially if you're aiming at earning all of the achievements the game has to offer, even if some of them are a bit tedious.
If you're a die-hard puzzle game lover, buy this game immediately. If not, check the game out on a sale.

Introduction

10,000,000 is an hybrid RPG/Action/Puzzle game. Steady progress in the game allows you to gain the tools to break out of your prison.

Title: 10,000,000
Genre: Action, Casual, Indie, RPG
Developer: EightyEightGames
Publisher: EightyEightGames
Release Date: 15 Jan, 2013

Main Review

It's not an easy game, but it's addictive in how it works. Your character progresses in a dungeon and you match blocks together in order to keep on moving, matching keys to open a door or chest, swords or shields to fight. The game is a race against time. It's quite fast paced and easy to get into, though hard to keep going and finish it. The RNG can sometimes really screw you up - and you may need to reset a level just because you didn't get what you needed for a long time. It's satisfying to see the big numbers appear, and each shake of the screen when you match blocks is pretty cool.

You need to reach the score 10,000,000 in order to complete the game, and all the runs beforehand allow you to buy upgrades such as potions, weapons, gear, score accumulators in order to improve your score further, get better upgrades - until you're good enough to hit the top score to beat the game.

You can collect four items that stay with you even when a run fails, to make future runs easier. It can get really fraustrating, since even if you have every single upgrade, getting the final score is still difficult. However that also makes it fun! The music also helps a lot to get you more stressed.

Visually the game at times make it hard to see what's going on, especially when managing your position in the game. The character screen is small. On the left of the main "running" screen is a skull, on the right is a finish line and a smily face. If your character is at the right of the screen, it's harder to counter a threat since you don't see it until it's already on you. If your character is on the left of the screen, you'll die a lot faster if you don't get the block combination you need to open a door, since your run ends if the skull catches up with you.

TL'DR

Overall Verdict
I recommend this game to other people. I think it's worth a playthrough, and at this price, it's nothing to complain about. Be prepared to get very addicted ... and possibly shout a bit at the screen.
