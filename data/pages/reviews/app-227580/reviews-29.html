\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was probably one of my worst buy decisions in all of my time here on steam. If you are thinking about buying this game, please at least listen to what I have to say first.

The game is essentially a literal dungeon runner. Your character dashes at high speed through the dungeon, gaining gold, experience, wood, and stone. Experience is used to buy small upgrades for the character that are more like small changes in game mechanics. Wood and stone are used to repair and upgrade buildings.

Basically the game is similar to Puzzle Quest if you've ever played it, basic match three or more. Unfortunately, the game starts to get increasingly more difficult as the levels progress. The entire difficulty curve is broken. I have often sat for a long stretch in front of a chest desperately trying to get a key match to unlock it only to have the game absolutely refuse to generate keys. What started out as a hopeful adventure then quickly turned into needless frustration. Clearly the developers should have spent more time to ensure that these issues wouldn't crop up. Adding these up makes the game incredibly difficult, however this is all "invisible" difficulty, stuff that is a part of the game mechanics itself that doesn't really add any actual challenge based on skill. I was unable to make it very far due to these frustrating issues. This was very disappointing because I have wanted this game for a very long time and when I finally got the chance to play it, I was rewarded for supporting the game with my money, with a completely arduous task that doesn't actually provide a challenge based on the player's skill. For this reason I would suggest you avoid this game, though it looks nice, plays nice, and is fun at first, it quickly turns out to be frustrating and devolves into a state of madness beyond any hope of repair.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn each run, you need to match the right tiles depending on your current objective - e.g, match keys to open a chest. It's very fast-paced and the longer you take to clear an objective, the more your characters gets pushed back until he is finally off the screen.

You do run after run, which take no more than few minutes each. Then you get to spend your accumulated resources on upgrades to improve your attack, defense, etc.

The main issue with the game is that there is artificial difficulty. No matter how well you play, there is always a very strong monster that you simply can't beat. This forces you to play and upgrade over and over until you are strong enough to move on. It forces you to play the game for a long time without giving you any actual content.

Additionally there's no storyline and the graphics are very basic.

If you really think you'd like it then get You Must Build A Boat instead.\t\t\t\t\t
