\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10,000,000 is a jaunty little puzzle/RPG game that is easy to play and addictively enjoyable. The plot is simple, you wake up in a dungeon cell and as the title of the game implies, you escape the dungeon for good when you score 10 million points in a single run. The game plays like bejeweled in that the player shuffles tiles on a grid by dragging a row or column in order to make 3 symbols match. Successful matches yields attacks, magic, keys, items, gold and materials that you can use to improve your cell, converting it into a base of operations which provides better gear, perks and special potions. The gameplay is easily accessable and there is a method to process of running dungeons that might feel hectic and challenging but never really feels frustrating. An average dungeon run takes only a few minutes but you can run the dungeon as many times as you like before returning to your base. The graphics are simple and have a Commodore 64 quality to them and the chiptune music is nicely composed.

10,000,000 is simple, fun, and addictive. Easy to pick up and play, yet challenging when you want it to be. The price is very reasonable and the game is not graphically heavy so you can play it on an older machine. I really enjoyed this game and I think you might too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm surprised to see the negative reviews. I really liked this game, and beat it after about 14 hours.

It's no graphical wonder, and if you don't like match 3 games (where the goal is ALWAYS to just play over and over and get a higher score each time), then this probably won't be for you. But, I have a low tolerance for grinding or frustration, and I thought that this was a neat spin on the classic match 3 formula. The timer mechanic meshed with the need to match certain tiles to make play very lively, and I always felt like I was progressing.

Well worth five bucks, imo.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTerrible name aside, this is a semi-decent time-waster in the classic "match 3" puzzle genre. The twist in this one is that the matching mechanic works in conjunction with the actions your character takes at the top of the screen. The character's goal is to keep moving to the right, clearing obstacles such as monsters, treasure chests and locked doors. In order to clear those obstacles, you need to match specific types of tiles (swords to kill monsters, keys to open chests, etc). Getting hit or not matching tiles causes you to slow down and creep towards the left side of the screen. If you get pushed all the way back, the run is over. After each run, you return to a dungeon where upgrades can be purchased using the resources collected during the run (wood, stone, gold and experience). And that's pretty much all there is to it. Basically, you just keep running through the dungeon and buying upgrades when you die. Reaching certain score milestones causes you to rank up, which allows you to achieve higher scores and travel further into the dungeon. The ultimate goal is to score the titular 10,000,000 points and escape. On the technical front, the graphics are extremely simple, done in a low-res pixel style. It works for what it is, but the visuals are really nothing to write home about. I did really like the music, though. It's pretty catchy and very NES-esque.

In summary, if you are a fan of "match 3" games and don't mind spending a few hours on the mindless grind it takes to complete (I would estimate around 5-6 hours or so), then by all means, give this a shot. However, I really must reiterate that the gameplay is the very definition of a grind - the whole experience from start to finish is extremely repetitive. If you have a short attention span or really don't like doing the same thing over and over for hours, do NOT buy this game. Additionally, I have to say I also think the price is a little too high. For some reason, it's double what it costs on iOS, which doesn't make a whole lot of sense. I'm not saying it's a rip-off, but this might be one of those cases where you may want to wait and see if it goes on sale at some point.\t\t\t\t\t
