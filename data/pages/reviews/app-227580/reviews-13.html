\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10,000,000 is more than just a number-it's a small game with a unique twist on puzzle game that is addicting!

Graphics:
Graphics are pixelated-style with many types of colors used. The menu scheme is based on SNES-styled rpg games and old PC sprite games. To some it may be bland, but to me, it was nostalgia. It was like playing an old pc game all over again...for the first time.

Story:
Not much of a story. You just wake up and in order to gain your freedom, you have to reach a top score of 10,000,000.

Music:
Not many tracks, but I LOVE THIS MUSIC! First time I heard one of the dungeon themes, I couldn't stop bobbing my head to the beat. The music is 8-bit style with some modern flair added into it. While playing the game, I never got tired of the music. It was always something to be enjoyed.

Gameplay:
The gameplay took some time to get used to, but easy to catch on pretty quick. Traditional Match-3 games have you swap symbols. This game, however, you have to drag rows horizontally and vertically in order to match 3 or more symbols. At first, you have to be precise at what you're looking for. Later on, it grants bonuses to time, currency, wood, and stone.

There are two types of attacks: Magical(Staff tile) and Physical(Sword tile). There are tank-type monsters, range-type monsters, attack-type monsters, and boss monsters (being the most difficult). Each of these types have unique ways to defeat them. Sometimes physical works better. Sometimes Magical works better. There is also a Shield tile where you gain some defense to block monster attacks, leaving you more time to play. There are obstacles that block your path, which require a set amount of locks. Using the key tiles, you can unlock walls and chests. Matching 3 will count as one lock unlocked. Matching 4 will count as an instant unlock for the chest or wall.

During each run, you are given objectives. Complete enough objectives in order to rank up. Each rank up provides more resources and more on scoring, but difficult monsters. Using each run, collect stone and wood to repair your area to power up magical and physical attacks, unlock potions (after each rank up), get better defense and shield, and train yourself to get special buffs using the experience you gain for your runs. Using the gold you gain from runs, you can temper your magical attacks, physical attacks, shield, and armor.

You have to be quick on this game however, which will take some time. There are two sides your person is on above the tiles: skull and happy face(you want happy face of course). With damage and time passed, your person will lean towards the skull. If he hits the skull, the run is over. If you run again, you still have the items. However, once you quit to desktop, your items are gone. With defeating monsters and unlocking chests, you can progress further and get a higher score.

One problem I have with this game is there is no hint button. Sometimes I'm stumped and my whole run is ruined. It would be nice if there was an option on training to provide a hint button after 10 seconds or so passes to slide the next tile.

Overall:
This game is a unique gem on Steam, both suitable for quick casual gamers or moderate gamers. If you like puzzles, get it. If you like RPGs, get it. If you like both, well you're in luck. Using a unique way to match 3 and progress in the game, 10,000,000 is more than a number-it's a classic indie game that will stand the test of time, even if it's a small game.

Pros:
-Addicting gameplay.
-Great rpg elements.
-Music.
-Nostalgia-like graphics.

Cons:
-No hint button.\t\t\t\t\t

I am still not used to the sliding mechanism of the game which is it's hallmark. I cut my teeth on both Tetris and Bejeweled, but that is not necessarily a bad thing however. You just have to know how to manipulate the board more than anything else and to use what items you have collected along the way and when, as you can only hold so many and must get rid of what you perceive to be useless now, for something more practical. Although you will find more often than not that what you just got rid of what would of helped you greatly in your next encounter or two.

There is luck involved as well, which I am not the fondest of in games, but as you upgrade your weapons and begin to gain powerful artifacts, lady luck will eventually begin to be by your side more often and you will have a much easier time exploring the dungeons and getting further and further as time goes by. You learn the games in's and out's and you get that one real awesome run where it seems everything was set up by angels and you are unbeatable. ^_^ Of course going up in rank will have you being slapped around like a rookie all over again, but as you gain more experience, so to do you go forth conquering the dungeons.

A little sad by the time you reach the 10 mil mark that there is not much more to do, although a few harder quests you really have to work at to start and complete them, (Read more about them in one of the guides for the game.) but the dungeon in and of itself is always open so you can try to rack up even more points to see how well you can do. So in the end there is no real end to it, the game just keeps on going on forever, only no more upgrading.

If you like puzzle games and RPGs you will most probably love this. Just be aware it is more puzzle than RPG and there is no true exploration, but it sure is a heck of a lot of fun. Try it on sale if nothing else. ^_^ Soon you will find yourself wanting to play the next game in the series as well, You Must Build a Boat and conquering the high seas!\t\t\t\t\t
