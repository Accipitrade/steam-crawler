\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"The forum is getting a bit out of hand, people opening new and new topics for speech." - a direct quote from their forums, which alone should tell you not to give them your money.

The core attraction to the game is its voice recognition. Which is a shame because it is effectively non-functional half the time.

I've played many hours using the same set-up, and now all of a sudden it doesn't work again. When I first played the game I spent 3 hours making it hear me. I have already spent 1 more hour trying to fix the voice recognition. Microsoft can hear me. Discord can hear me. Everything can pick up my microphone except for this game.

For the price, it's absolutely insulting that the core part of gameplay is so tedious to make use of. Typing and clicking commands out is not vaguely like a simulator. It's like buying an FPS and having to spend hours teaching the game how to recognise there's a mouse plugged in.

The DLC is further evidence that this game is primarily about making cash rather than producing a quality product. The price point is about 5 times higher than could ever be worth it for some Google Earth screenshots and some random blocky extruded objects (seriously random which structures get the actual '3D' treatment touted in the title of the game.) It's really quite sad because this is a great concept, executed dreadfully.

EDIT: the snarky nature of the dev's comments in forums implies they have no intention of fixing any issues with the game, and are clearly only focused on milking the game for DLC money. If they cared about the people paying their hard-earned money on their game.

If you want to try and fix it, then here's the post blaming everything but themselves: https://forum.simflight.com/topic/83886-speech-issues/\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven though I recommend this game doesn't mean I won't say anything bad about it. It's a great game, if you're inspired or wanting to try yourself in an ATC facility, however I have to agree with most people on this, the game looks more or less unfinished, the DLC prices are stupidly high and you barely have any of the common terminology. The graphics reminds me of FSX which is embarrasing for a game this new.
BUT! I have spent a couple hours of my life trying it out and I'm willing to say that it's a good game if you're interested in aviation. The game has an alright tutorial which takes you through the basics, once you've fiddled with some settings you're able to use the voice recognition feature as well and that is something I find really amazing, even though it is lacking some features it still works and you're able to figure out how to play the game really quickly. The (alleged) money hungry developers probably doesn't give a skjit about this review and for all we know this is how the game is gonna be forever. If I were to rate the game on a scale from 1 to 10 I'd have to give it a 4 or something like that,
Not good, not great,
