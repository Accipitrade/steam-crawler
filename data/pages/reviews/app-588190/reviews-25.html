\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is addictive. I've been considering buying it after seeing a few videos on YouTube. The sale this week was what it took to get me to make the purchase. Overall, I am extremely happy with the game play and have spent quite a bit of time with it. My only complaints are the following:

1. The speech recognition works pretty well, but the voices used by the AI are pretty limited due to the use of the built in Windows text to speech engine. I use this game on both Windows 7 and 10. With 7, I'm limited to the Microsoft Anna voice, so every pilot is the same voice, which gets tiresome. If there's a reasonable way to get more voices that I'm not aware of, I'd love to find out about it.
2. This isn't huge, but the points system is pretty lopsided. You get 10 points per successful takeoff or landing, but a mistake costs you 500 to 1000 points which makes it pretty difficult to overcome a mistake.
3. The AI "pilots" are stupid. One time I got penalized was when an AI plane drove directly into the back end of another one that was stopped on the taxiway. Obviously, no real life pilot would be staring at the back end of a stopped plane ahead and just keep going.

I would definitely buy this game again. I am having a ball with it, despite the three minor issues noted above. It's a lot of fun and makes you work your brain to keep traffic separated.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I've been looking for a realistic ATC simulator for years. Although not perfect, this delivers the closest experience than any other games. The voice recognition is still a little buggy, and I'm assuming newer sp's will fix the performance speed (occasionally, I'll notice a little jerkiness in moving objects). I added on a realcolor program (not available on steam) to get the "real-life" airlines for each airport as well as the coloring of the planes match the current airlines paint schemes. I reccomend the real-traffic add-in as well (also not available on steam--darn).

Do I think it's a good value for the $ you pay for the game? Yes and no. Yes since there isn't any competition/other simulators as close to the real thing as T3DPro. If you are on the fence, I'd recommend you wait for a special/sale on it. My only gripe is paying $19.99 for each airport. Granted, you get a discount if you buy a bundle of them, but I cant justify paying more for the bundle than the retail price for the game. I did buy an additional airport because I love LAS, but I find it hard to justify coughing up $20 bucks at a time for the equivalent of a new background/scene. There aren't any unique or new features between the airports--just the terminal and runway layouts are different (each are exactly like how the airport is mapped in real life of course).

In a nutshell, I am a fan of the game since it provides simulation like no other for ATC. However I somewhat feel like since the game is one of a kind in its genre, they may feel this justifies forcing you to pay $20 (or between $50-$100 for a bundle) on different scenery.

I'm continually spending a great amount of time playing the game, but in the back of my mind I wonder if the same investment in a different game+its expansions would give me a better value.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile this is a very, very, very, niche game, I greatly enjoy it. Definitely simpler and less gimicy than other ATC games, but just the right amount of gamification for me. Using the variable speech speed and voices makes the game more feel more dynamic even though it can become quite repetitive.

Oddly, this is one of the harder "games" that I've played. Several hours into a busy airport will have you multitasking multiple incoming and outgoing flights while trying to direct planes from terminal to runway or vice versa. The planes AI are not very intelligent as they will crash into other planes crossing them and don't question anything, like the plane heading toward them, but they will know how to stack up in a line, and have decent initial pathing. The planes are merely following your directions to a T so it's your fault if they crash.

tl;dr? I enjoy this game for lazy Sunday's or after I've watched Pushing Tin or listened to Kennedy Steve. 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
