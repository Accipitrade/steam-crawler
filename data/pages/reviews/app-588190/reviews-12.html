\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's an overpriced game for the quality you get but it's not the worst sim you can buy. You need to setup and train Microsoft speech to use voice recog, and most of the complaints about it are probably due to player using the basic setup and not training their speech further. Unless I have a callsign with the same number repeated (223, 4445, 8888 etc) I generally don't have an issue with it. The only real problem I have with the game is after roughly an hour, hour and a half the game freezes and I have to force restart the game.

Pros
Real airports
Voice recog
Basic commands are decent
You can select what runways to use in the menu, then deselect which runway you want to use for departures so no arrivals line up for it

Cons
No free camera, you can only lock onto aircraft
Camera zoom can be better
You have to say numbers indivdually (1547 is said one five four seven, not fifteen forty seven like the real world)
Strips don't show real aircraft types, making sequencing difficult
Points system seems to be broken. I've gotten penalties for an aircraft taking off and another landing on a parallel runway, but never for having a faster aircraft touch down while a slower aircraft has not yet cleared the hold short line.
A lot of commands are missing. You can't tell aircraft to reduce speed on approach nor can you request to expedite off a runway.
Game is overpriced for the content you don't get, and the cost for DLCs is a bit ridiculous. You also have to pay roughly $10 per airport to have realistic liveries and callsigns. Having airport and livery packs would be nice but the price they'd probably charge would make it undesirable.
Some airports don't recognize voice recog (specifically ATL and JFK).

Recommendations for devs
Lower your prices. If this was a licensed game off the cut I could handle the pricing due to royalties, but having fictitious airlines and liveries is not reasonable for the price you charge. I'm sure there are mods out there that people are using to avoid this.
Pricing should be more in line with Airport Madness 3D.
Improve the commands, since many real world commands are not present.
Fix the points system to reflect penalties based on real world violations.

Overall this is not a terrible sim and I've spent 4 hours over the last 2 days messing with it and have a ton of fun, but there are definitely improvements to be made.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought this game on sale, and even then payed what I think is more than acceptable for this game.

Game concept itself is sold. You are an air traffic controller of an airport and your job is to get planes to the right place at the right time without issue. Points are awarded if you get people on the ground or in the air without issue. I think there is bones here for a fantastic game that can be enjoyed by anyone who likes aviation, or anyone who is looking for a bit of a puzzle/management game. It's not what I'd call relaxing, but it's a great way to spend some time away from what your doing in the real world.

However, there are some serious issues with this game. If it wasn't for the game supposedly being made by a small indi shop, you'd say that this is an incomplete product. To begin with, the game's menus appear somewhat haphazard and with little explanation, which for some things may be challenging if you're coming into the game with a lower understanding of aviation. Tutorial for the game is also very low quality and doesn't explain important components of the game, but more just tells you how to get planes in the air and on the ground and little else. The voice activation system wasn't explained as I played through the tutorial, and instead any questions you may have are addressed in the very sizeable instruction manual. Panels in the game don't gel well with each other, making the game UI very clunky. At LAX I had all my planes effectively operating one way because I wasn't able to learn from the tutorial how to direct planes down different taxi ways (something important for one of the world's largest airports) and the AI would occasionally just crash aircraft into each other. Settings cannot be adjusted in game. Graphics aren't that impressive either. That's not to say that you can't have fun here, otherwise I would have returned the game. However you do have to battle through these things and be prepared to forgive quite a bit in order to enjoy the game. A proper AV enthusiast who's watched ATC on Youtube for years now (or been up in a tower) won't find this very realistic and immersive which is sad because that appears to be the main market of the game.

My biggest concern for this game is the DLC and the overall cost attached with playing the game. The Game is expensive to buy in the first place, and comes with three maps, one of which you're going to get sick of in about half an hour. naturally after playing for a few hours you'll want to get another map or two, only to find that these maps cost almost as much as the base game itself, for one map. Not all of the DLC for this game is on steam either, with other airports for sale on the publisher's website, some of which are only ~6 usd. Some of these come with real world airlines as well which I'm sure would make the game much more durable.

I get it, AV games have a very small targeted market and are made and played by people with an interest in the field and that's about it. It makes it hard to get people interested. But a less than stellar game coupled with pricing and DLC layout and pricing that reflects a much better product leaves me with a sour taste and an unwillingness to give them slack for being a small indie developer. Drop the cost, bundle airports together in more affordable DLC and work on improving the actual gameplay that I could see quite an enjoyable game in this. But not now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game, personally. It has a high learning curve, but you'll pick it up quickly if your passion for aviation or ATC (Air Traffic Controlling) is strong enough.

I'm neither a pilot or real ATC, just an enthusiast who spends hours watching aviation channels, and gaming channels that focused on this game. I truly wanted it, If you've watched videos on this topic, if you watch people play 30 minutes or an hour at a certain airport on youtube and wanted more? This game may be for you.

I say may because there are too many issues with game itself. EVEN IF you hit all the benchmarks above I still want you to be weary of this purchase.

There are only 3 airports included in the default game. PHL (Philadelphia), LAX (Los Angeles), and the 3rd, I don't remember, but it's just 1 runway and very small. Great for an introduction but once you get the basics down and move on, you're really only left with 2 airports that can really give you a challenge. For example, multiple runways, taxiways, and aprons/terminals.

There are over 25 individual airports that you need to purchase individually. And right now with the Summer Steam Sale going on it adds up to ~$360.

But wait, there's more. You also need to go on their actual website, to buy EVEN MORE DLC. DLC that you cannot buy here on steam.

If you want to use real callsigns like Delta 504, Quantas 7, Japan Air 306? You need to buy that for about $7-$10. The base game replaces the callsigns with fictitious ones. So to really enjoy the immersion of being an ATC, it's a must buy. (Should be included in the game by default, but moving on...)

So, you bought the game, you bought the callsign DLC off the website. You decide to load up Philadelphia. All the planes are white. Yep. There's a DLC for that. So you want to see the cool liveries on the planes? You need to buy that.

And that's not a one time buy, like the callsigns. Thankfully you buy the real airline names, and it applies to all maps.

If you buy the liveries for the planes in Philadelphia? They stay in Philadelphia. So not only do you need to buy each map individually, you need to purchase the liveries for the planes, for each airport. At that rate just bundle the map and liveries together and just raise the price, at least you could psychologically trick us into buying it, rather than being so blatantly obvious about it.

Ultimately, if you're still sold on hearing all the facts about this game. These are fact, this is not my opinion this is all real, and what you'll have to do and go through if you want the immersion in this game.

Is it a good game? Yes, absolutely in my opinion I love it and always finding myself coming back to do an hour session here and there. It's addicting.

Can I recommend it? No, I honestly can't. Especially if you're just a casual personal passing by thinking this looks cool? First time seeing this game or this page? Absolutely not.

If you've seen game play of this, thoroughly enjoy aviation, especially the ATC side of things, and were already aware of this game prior to coming to this page?

Honestly it's up to you at that point. If you can set aside everything I said, and still don't mind giving these developers money only further incentivizing their practices?

Then go for it.

In conclusion would I recommend this game? No. Not for a casual player and not even for an enthusiast because there is so much other stuff wrapped up in it, you feel like you're only buying half a game

I can't recommend this game even on sale because I've bought more fully fleshed out games at $30 (The current Summer Steam Sale price) Let alone $50.
