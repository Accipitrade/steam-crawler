\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've got about 11 hours logged so far and still only have the default airports, but this is a pretty impressive experience with the voice controls. I'm a fan of time and load management types of games and this definitely hits those notes for sure. Adding the real life colors and airline names is a must in my opinion, the additional cost is worth it.

The bummer is that you're paying $50 for a game and another $10-15 for the real world add ons for the base airports and that's not at all counting the additional DLC airports and real color add ons for each of those as well, so the game can get pricey if you're wanting to try out multiple different styles of airports.

There are also certainly bugs that can be a bit aggravating. Some AI pilots will bug out and not listen to any instructions (causing all sorts of issues), I've had voice commands not register correctly despite multiple slow repeats and even had a couple plans show up on my inbound radar and have no identifying marks and they don't show on my strips. But, all in all, the bugs aren't bad and you can delete a plane that is causing issues to get things back on track.

Overall though, it's not quite like any other "puzzle" game I've played before and I'm finding myself sitting down for a short session only to have a couple hours melt away quickly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would definitely recommend this game. I only bought it yesterday and I am completely HOOKED! I've always been in awe of the way air traffic controllers and pilots talk to each other. It genuinely seems like an incredible skill. I was hoping that this game would give me even some limited experience of doing that. I am happy to report that it does. Using my voice to give a plane detailed instructions in one breath is so much fun. I can't wait to get good at it. There's something so thrilling about saying something like "Magic eight two five, wind is one zero niner at 5 knots, runway two eight, climb to 100. On reaching contact departure, cleared for takeoff" and they understand you and do what you said :D

On the negative side I do think, like so many others, that the DLC's are too expensive but I don't care about made up company names and colours and the base game comes with 3 airports (and I've only been to one so far!) so for me it's fine as it is.

Back to the positives though, I was really glad to see that it supports Ultrawide and this game really benefits from that (Screenshot: https://i.imgur.com/5NVKnxW.png). It also immediately figured out how to use my Cereproc voices which are really good quality text to speech voices and that was awesome. The only thing missing at the moment for me would be a dedicated subreddit and maybe also a Discord server. I haven't looked at multiplayer yet but I would imagine those would really help with setup and finding people to play with.

Overall a massive thumbs up and I can't wait to see what future updates will bring.\t\t\t\t\t
