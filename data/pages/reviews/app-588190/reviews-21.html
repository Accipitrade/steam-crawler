\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is fun if, and only if, you are interested in ATC and proper terminology. The voice recognition commands is the only thing that makes this game a worthy buy.

HOWEVER, there are a lot of issues with this game:

* A lot of commonly used phrases are not usable in this game. For example, "Give way to x" and "Follow x" does not work.
* In the biggest airports, the workload can quickly become unmanagable. This game combines both Tower, Ground AND RAMP control. In bigger airports (KLAX/KJFK for example), you are responsible for the ground control of all aircrafts, and all 4 runways. When it gets busy, it gets VERY busy.
* Very limited amount of airports. Right now, you only have the bigger airports from US in the game plus Munich.
* Cost. In order get other airports, you need to visit their website to get it. This means that your CC is stored elsewhere and it isn't processed by Steam. Then, you need to pay $9.99 for Real Traffic, which is critical for immersion in an ATC game. Each Airport costs $18.99, which you'll need for KSAN (Most busy single runway airport in US) or KJFK (It's KJFK!) and others (KATL/KPHL)

But! There are no colours for buildings OR the planes. To get that, you need to top up ANOTHER $6.99 PER AIRPORT.

So that's $50 for the game + $10 for real traffic + $25 PER AIRPORT, you are paying over $100 easily for a game that doesn't have a lot of content, and whose only strong point is the voice recognition technology.

* And finally, NOT ALL DLCS ARE ON STEAM. Why, just why? Airports are a DLC on Steam, but Real Traffic and Real Colours are not?

Conclusion: Get it if you like listening to liveatc.net. Otherwise, this is just too expensive.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAppended Review

Addendum: The real schedules and realistic liveries add-ons seem to be 3r party products, which is why the pricing is what it is.

While I am enjoying the game, I am still going to stick to a negative review for the following reasons:

#Pricing: Expensive Base Game, Expensive Airports and expensive liveries & callsigns
This game is somewhat old but unique while catering to a specific demographic.
I would not have minded around 40 quid, with all the airports and liveries included.
Instead, each individual airport will cost you about 10 quid extra. When you want Real Life callsigns and liveries, you need to purchase these outside of steam, for each individual airport you own and then install them manually.
Without additional airports, the game is not challenging at all and misses the point, so you are drawn into their pricing structure.

#Tutorial & Learning Curve
The tutorial is very basic. There is a manual you can read.
There should be guidance on managing Go Arounds.
The Strips should also be explained, Spacing should be explained.
It would also be interesting to see some guidance on traffic patterns per airport.
I just know that there are things I am not taking advantage of or paying attention to.
When I allow everybody to land as they appear, I have no issues with spacing.


#Not a realistic simulation
Weather does not matter, weather info is not provided as part of takeoff clearance.
'Continue Taxi' is a catch all and used to issue clearance to cross a runway.

#Voice Control
It kinda works for me now. Unless you Windows 10 is set to English you will have issues with voice recognition.

#Perfomance
Certain weather types require that you turn off reflection.
The game is old and ugly but you don't really look out of the window much.
My machine goes crazy when loading an airport in.

Initial Review:
Got it for 45 Euros and went for a refund.
Was highly disappointed by the voice control system and how I had to mess with my win10 setup to get it working.
Then it was not reliable. If you hve 20 quid to spend, go for Airport Madness 3d Vol2.\t\t\t\t\t
