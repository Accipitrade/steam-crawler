\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI purchased Tower 3D! on Steam some time ago and had since learned it was distributed by someone other than the developer and was informed that the Pro version fixed some bugs in the version I owned. I am a real pilot and an ATC fan so this simulator/game has appeal to me, so when the Pro version went on sale I opted to "upgrade".

On the positive, this version finally has labelled the taxiways. That's it, all the positive I can find.

On the neutral, There's no new airports.

On the negative...
Where's the traffic? I literally have had no air traffic in 3 game hours at LAX at 8am, I'm maxed at 100% density and I'm not seeing anything beyond the initial 3 planes of traffic.

Crosswind penalties? I looked deeply into this as I frequently got winds between 20 and 35 mph which can be a big concern for planes. "Maybe" they're also considering ground taxi concerns with the crosswinds, because changing runways does not change the planes decision to abort a taxi or landing due to crosswinds. Delay penalties rack up quickly & expensively despite the decision to abort was made by the pilot and not you as the ATC. I did find a discussion topic on the crosswind component that was posted some time ago and the response from the developer was that they would consider it in possible future enhancements. With the passage of time, I can only conclude their decision was to ignore this negative characteristic of the game while continually thanking you for giving them money.

Commands have to be given in specific points, no advanced commands. If you've got an aircraft on taxi, you can't give a clear to cross a runway ("Continue Taxi") until the plane actually gets to the intersection and stops.

In summary, I personally find the Pro release a worse version to play with than the non-developer distributed Tower!3D version here on Steam. From my own programming experience, I'd categorize this application as suffering from Piss Poor Programming as well as half-way thought out design and I'm quite certain I'll not be purchasing another title from this developer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to like this sim, but there are just too many problems with it. The action is enjoyable and engaging and often quite challenging. And it has by far the best use of voice recognition I have ever seen in any game or sim. Just so long as you have a decent mic and take the time to properly train Windows Voice Recognition on your computer.

However, there are a lot of cons. Every airport beyond the first three costs extra, near $20 each, which would be fine if it included the real airlines and their liveries and schedules for that airport. But they don't. You have to pay extra for real airlines AT EACH AND EVERY AIRPORT, which is another $6 or $7. BTW, the real airlines add-ons aren't even available in the Steam version. You have to buy them from the vendor website, which isn't mentioned here anywhere on the Steam store page. If you don't feel like shelling out for the add-ons, the base airplanes only have 6 or 7 varieties and the liveries are mostly a very basic white with a bit of tail color and a text airline name down the side. A lot of the time, they all look alike from a distance.

The graphics are pretty good most of the time, but you can only zoom so far and there's no real free-roam camera, so it's hard to tell what's going on at times. There is an "action cam" that moves around to where things are happening, but it's erratic and you can't really control it.

About 1 plane in 15 or so will get stuck and just refuse to do anything. Other planes will literally spin in circles 3 or 4 times until they decide to do what they're supposed to. I like doing donuts as much as the next guy, but I don't wan't my pilot to do them. Planes often don't fit the gates and you can watch wings pass through each other as a plane backs out or pulls in to a gate. Pilot AI is equally dumb and watching a plane run into the back or side of another plane is common, forcing you to frequently issue "hold position" and "continue taxi" commands. Pathfinding AI is equally clueless at times and planes will sometimes choose strange routes to get to where they are going if you don't specify exactly paths for them.

Further, I have yet to get the sim to run entirely correctly and support is slow to respond and has provided no real answers to the problem I'm having. If your answer is to tell me to "turn off my anti-virus" or "run the game in admin mode", which seems to be their standard answer to everything, then you need to re-examine how your write software. These were passably acceptable answers 15-20 years ago. Those are not acceptable answers in modern software development. I write software for a living, so I know a little about that.

Lastly, there are very little instructions available in game. You can find a manual on their website which details a few more commands but still leaves a lot to be desired. However, unless you go searching for it, you won't even know a manual exists, because it isn't even mentioned in the game itself.

I haven't tried the multiplayer yet, so I can't speak to that.

Short answer, if you're ok with the limitations, extra costs for realism, and AI bugs, then this is an enjoyable sim. I do really like the potential it shows and I really hope they can eventually get it sorted out. I will certainly be watching to see if they do. I would love to see this one reach its potential. But until they do, I just can't recommend this to every day users and I certainly won't invest more money in add-ons, Steam sale or not, until they can address many of these issues.\t\t\t\t\t
