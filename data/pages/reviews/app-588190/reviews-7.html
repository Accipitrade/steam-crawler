\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately, I cannot recommend Tower!3D Pro. After having played Tracon, Tracon II and Tower (from Wesson International), Tower Simulator (from Wilco Publishing), others that I can't recall and more recently ATCpro (Flag Mountain Software), I have to say that Tower!3D Pro is a disappointment.
The biggest draw for me in this game was the ability to use voice recognition to control aircraft but, that has never worked for me. That seems to be a commonly reported issue and the guidance on fixing it is literally a page and a half long. To their credit, the guidance is thorough but, the prospect of having to change so many Windows settings to US-English, and mess around with Cortana settings (which prompts me to login and reminds me of their data collection and data sharing policies - which I'm not about to accept) in order to get it to work is a no go. Other ATC programs that support voice don't require any messing around with Windows language settings.
As others have mentioned, there seems to have been very little in the way of development on the core mechanics of the game (like overhauling the voice system, for example). Instead they've focused on pumping out DLC airports at a pretty good pace as well as airport addons (on their website) for aircraft liveries. Given the price of each airport and with very little in the way of core game development evident, those just feel like a cash grab.
If you want a well done and more realistic ATC simulation that will challenge you, try ATCpro by Flag Mountain Software (not available on Steam). It's three years old now and I don't think it is being developed any further but, as an ATC sim, it is by far the best in my opinion. There are some YouTube videos out there if you wanted to check it out before buying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most complex games to review on Steam.

The good: Game functions and is fun to play IF you like puzzle solving. Its also interesting to gain a glimpse into the world of ATC...and I totally get why ATC is a very stressful job.

The bad: The devs had a great concept, then pretty much delivered a half finished product. The only saving grace from a solid "thumbs down" is the current 50% Steam sale. That discount makes purchasing the "add-on" content palpable. Yeah, that's right...to play the game with realistic planes...you gotta BUY MORE CONTENT.

the ugly: GRAPHICS! Some of the airplanes look as if they were assaulted by sugar-high pre-schoolers with markers. AND YES, YOU GOTTA PAY MORE FOR THIS KNOCK OFF PICASSO EFFORT. The ground detail is...wait for it...USGS/Google satellite views! The 3D renderings are...fuzzy...hazy...blury...poorly executed. You get the point. Oh, and funny enough...it uses the Unreal engine!

Conclusion: I have seen better effort out of ground sloths drunk on ripe banana juice. Its like the devs got bored halfway through development (or ran out of money) and said "cut and print." The finished product is warm, watered down diet soda. For God's sake, at least have a little pride.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlmost good, but a lack of attention to detail and improvements from the developer make it mediocre.

This game should be good. It's a good idea. But it's not really a simulator. It's a puzzle game like Mini Metro. In playing it, you'll learn pretty quickly the basics of ATC. You don't need to worry about lots of stuff except for managing the path and timing of many objects. Each airport (which cost $20 USD) is like a new puzzle for you to try out. If you're an aviation enthusiast, it feels pretty cool directing in A380s with real liveries (which is even more money).

But the execution is poor. I've owned this game for 3 or so years now and in that time, there have not been improvements. Lots of weird bugs, mediocre AI and an ever frustrating voice-control system really ruin it quickly. For most people, voice control won't work well. The developer encourages you to train the Speech Recognition API or buy a new microphone, but honestly the optimizations on their end have not been made. The developer continues to churn out $20 USD airports, but not improve the core engine / product.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell first off, I personally think that the game is overpriced. What you get is awesome ATC experience but I think it should cost around $20. Ignoring the price though, I think you get a pretty darn realistic feeling of what it is like to be an ATC. What really amazes me the most is the voice recognition, it works really well. And well you can definitely feel the pressure rise the more aircraft you have to handle especially when youre playing alone. You can change the traffic density before you start a game though, so dont worry about that if youre starting off.
The only thing I dont really like is the quality of it. It is very complex and realistic yes, but the graphics are really bad and should definitely get at least a few overhauls. I also dont like that you can barely zoom in on planes, seen that real ATCs have binoculars, I think that should be changed. I also think that there should be a few more commands, sometimes I want aircraft to speed up or reduce speed which is not possible, but I think that can be worked on, I'd just wish for a bit more complexity than there already is. But other than the price and the graphics quality, Im very impressed and would definitely recommend it to anyone looking for a pretty realistic ATC experience, alone or with other people.\t\t\t\t\t
