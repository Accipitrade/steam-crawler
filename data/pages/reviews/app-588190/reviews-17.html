\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a nice and realistic ATC game, however, I would not recommend purchasing this game at full price. Unfortunately it is very bland. You work with fake airlines that do not have paint schemes. Yes, that is correct, the developers are having you pay $49.99 for a game that has less graphics than their mobile phone competitor "Unmatched Air Traffic Control". This being said, there are third party developers capitalizing on this flaw within the game by offering maps and color packs for an additional charge. So essentially, if you buy this game for $50, you will need to spend an additional $15 to make the game look the way it should. Is this game worth $65, not hardly.

We have established that the devs skipped out on the graphics, how are they doing on the gameplay? Terrible. There's no tutorial whatsoever, so the first couple hours of gameplay is all spent relying on Google to answer you questions about how to properly depart aircrafts, assign specific taxi-routes and change landing runways. There is a HUGE learning curve.

You would imagine that for $50 you would get the ability to change airport viewing angles or maybe even watch the planes land and take off up close. You're wrong. You do not have any additional cameras and the zoom from the tower is horrible at best.

Looking at the steam add-ons, you can probably guess that these devs are relying on you to buy, buy, buy. For $50, you get 3 airports, but anything past that and you gotta fork up some serious cash. Overall, big waste of time and money. If i could get a refund for this game, i would. But considering all of the money i sank into this game to get it to appear like the $50 product that I assumed i was purchasing, a refund would still put me upside down on the game considering $35 of my purchase went to a third party dev to dress up the game.

I would not recommend this game until the devs put some serious effort into it. Very dissatisfied.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI originally gave this a very positive review, however the longer you own the game the more issues you will run into, and it's got to the point that I really cannot bare this any longer.
The game comes with a selection of 4 airports, however the aircraft are very low quality models, and the airlines fictional. If you want real airlines you have to buy a separate DLC from their website, which comes in a very dated installer. Once this is installed you will get real airline callsigns, however the aircraft are now plain white liveries. If you want real liveries you have to buy yet another DLC from their website. This won't however give you real liveries for every airport, far far worse. They make you buy a DLC for each individual airport to get real liveries there which is utterly disgraceful. These installers are a bit hit-and-miss as to whether they actually install the DLC. I've just spent the past 2 hours trying to get Gatwick liveries working, which I still haven't managed to do. The aircraft are either plain white, or textured in completely the wrong way. I'm a fairly tech-savvy person and yet I struggle with this.
For a game that's released in 2017 it is seriously behind in terms of design and features. The graphics look like something out of Microsoft Flight Sim 2004, yet this game was released 13 years after that. Multiplayer is like going back into the late 1990s/early 2000s. If you want to play multiplayer lets hope you're very very knowledgeable because it's not as simple as most games where you simply invite a friend and they can join you, oh no, you have to port forward and modify your firewall, which is completely absurd! This is a really really awful system and has no place in a game released in 2017. Once again, if your friend has some DLCs that you don't (or visa versa), you will either have to buy all the DLCs they own, or they will have to uninstall the DLCs they have, because Tower3DPro can't support multiplayer if someone has a different DLC installed.

I won't even begin to mention all the issues once you've started playing, because the list goes on and on.

I wouldn't mind if this game was a 10 game that included the Real Color and Real Traffic DLCs as standard, but to charge the price it is, and then demand more for things that should be included as standard is completely unjustifiable. If you want to play a game occasionally in between flicking through game files trying to solve errors, or messing with your WIFI just to play multiplayer, this game is for you! If you want a good and reliable Air Traffic Control game, maybe you should make your own, it'll no doubt be a more enjoyable experience that playing this.\t\t\t\t\t
