\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTower!3D Pro is definitely for a specific focus group. I'll be honest, it lacks a polish for its extremely high cost. If you purchase the game with the currently available DLC airports, you'll spend just under $150. After you spend that, the game lacks polish unless you continue to purchase additional content called “Real Traffic”, and “Real Color”, which is just under another $60 complete. So we are talking approximately $210 without a sale price or bundle deal on content if you complete the set as of November 2017. To make things worse, the “real” content isn't available on steam at the time of this review, and there is no technical reason the “real” content couldn't be part of the core game, for it absolutely should be.

I played for quite some time without the “Real Traffic/Real Color” additions. Without them, the game is sub par. The “real” additions make the aircraft look amazing compared to the default game aircraft. So, unless you are a serious ATC enthusiast and money isn't an issue, you should absolutely avoid this game. Even after spending hundreds, the airports are sloppy. Cuts or breaks in the images, photos of trucks, trailers, boxes, in the middle of taxiways which aircraft simply travel over, but could have been cleaned up by the devs. Just detail issues that would be simple to clean up, but they simply didn't bother to do. For the cost of the game, I would expect a better layout cleanup, realistic engine noise, something other than a constant looping of bird sounds, and flat scenery outside the airport boundaries. That's the bad of this game, and they are bad because of the price. The game is horribly expensive for the product you seem to get, but if it weren't so expensive, the detail sloppiness would be easier to forgive.

Now the good. I am an ATC enthusiast, and I enjoy playing the game. Unfortunately, it makes me willing to overlook sloppiness that is rampant in the games core. Without the voice recognition, this game would receive a “thumbs down”. The voice recognition is the game changer for me. Once you learn the word pattern norms, and grasp the phonetic alphabet and numbers, this game can be a blast. Phonetics came easy for me, for I learned them at a very young age (Helps to be a military brat). Learning aircraft company identifiers by heart is mandatory, but comes in time by repetitive use. Once you master the language, you'll begin to have fun. In the beginning, the voice recognition was ugly and seemed flawed. But it turns out that it was me, and not the game. Once you master how to talk to the game, it runs pretty flawlessly. So in short, the game is horribly expensive, but is pretty much the only game in town. I have no regrets spending the money because its of interest to me, but if ATC isn't your “thing”, you will probably regret it big time.

** 5-20-2019 - Well I've been playing for years now. I'm changing to a thumbs down, for the developers are NOT focusing on issues that have been ongoing for YEARS now. Spinning take offs, locking up on older DLC, and assorted problems that have existed for years. Thumbs down.

VERY overpriced.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am an real life ATC so I might be a bit biased. I give this game a positive score although in my opinion it's really overpriced for the content it offers. I would recommend it without doubt if it cost like 15$.

This game can offer quite reallistic feel although of course it is limited. There are no vehicles, flight strips are less than basic, there is no coordination and we have to pay extra to get real airlines and schedules. It could use real weather too... Still it is fun balancing between realism and playability. Voice regognition works quite well and it's fun to give clearances by voice. Basic ATC techniques work as intended. Airports are nice and look realistic, also airplanes behave as they should (except push-back which lasts too short).

I would really like this game to have more smaller airports because they give the most realistic experience. On TIST I can turn off ground radar and work visually just like in reality. All other airport are much larger and we have to rely on our ground movement view (which shows callsigns and cleared routes for every airplane) which looks a bit like a cheat. Not to mention that one controller would not be able to handle whole traffc of LAX or JFK alone especially relying only on his visual judgement. Therefore on big airports the game looks more like a action game (we can learn to talk very fast too) than real simulation. Even though it is still fun. Not to mention that we can play in multiplayer dividing the roles with our friend.

To quickly summarise. I think this game can give a real feeling of how it is to be a tower ATC. If you are interested in this kind of stuff you will definitely find it fun. If you are not into ATC or aviation, don't even bother.\t\t\t\t\t
