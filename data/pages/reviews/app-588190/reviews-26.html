\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, the game is not visual stunning...but when you are really in the thick of things, you aren't really looking at the airport scenery anyway. Tower!3D Pro offers the ability to use your computer's speech recognition to give vocal commands to the planes as you simulate being a frazzled air traffic controller. Most commands can be given using the mouse (and some are easier to give this way, such as assigning taxiway paths), while a few commands are exclusively vocal. I just got done doing the 8:00 AM to 9:00 AM shift at LAX and I'm exhausted, it really gets crazy as you get near 9:00.

Game comes with generic fictional airlines, but a DLC pack provides real traffic schedules for the airports with real airline names and numbers and another DLC provides plane liveries. A little pricy for that stuff, but it is really fun battling the real hectic schedules at LAX or ATL. San Diego provides the challenge of routing traffic over one runway and taxiway, while LAX or ATL offers a really busy schedule to contend with.

Every successful landing or takeoff is worth 10 points. However, a mistake such as not clearing a plane to land, being late to pushout a plane, having planes crash while taxiing, or crash in the air, etc. can cost you 500 points!...as it should!

There are 5 speed options (1x, 2x, 4x, 8x, 16x)...but you'd have to be really good to run at anything faster than regular speed (with the exception of airport slow times). Sadly there is no option to go slower than regular time, so when you get overwhelmed the only recourse is to pause and catch your breath!

If you are into a hardcore sim of air traffic control, this is what you are looking for. Want something more arcady, go with Airport Madness. This is hardcore. You give landing clearance, takeoff clearance, taxi routes, pushbacks, etc. The weather changes, the wind changes and suddenly you need to reroute traffic to take off and land via the opposite direction (something I have YET to master.)

10/10 for Simulation
4/10 for Graphics
7/10 for Performance
10/10 for Adreneline and Stress (ATC is a VERY stressful job)

Addicting. Limit yourself to an hour at a time or you'll get pretty worn out!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGet this right out of the way: DO NOT BUY THIS UNLESS ON SALE!!!!!

My opinion, but the $50 price is not worth it for what you get. Wait for a sale then grab it then.

It's a very addictive game and having the voice features is fantastic. Trying to keep track of everything going on, planes landing, leaving, wondering if you gave the right instructions...It's a great game, but again, not for the price.
One other thing that if you want other airports...$10 per airport....if you want "real world names" that's $5-$10...PER AIRPORT YOU WANT THEM ON.....that's almost worse then EA for the love of pete.

I REALLY wish the devs would have put this at a better price, or given at least more airports in the base...

Again, I REALLY like the game, I just can't justify the extra dollars for th extra airports, real colors, etc. I bought a bundle when on a christmas sale and that will be ALL that I buy of this game.

If you're really into puzzle solving or ATC games, this is great, and make sure to get the pro to get voice commands. For you the extra would be worth it.

***To get the voice to work better use the windows speech app, do a few of the tutorial runs to get your voice down, it helps alot***

Ok, so I said the game was good, but the pricing methods are terrible. The amount of extra pricing is the reason I won't recommend this game. IMO too much is too much, and take a look at all the extras and see what you would pay overall for everything they're offering currently...Yeah, big number.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI refunded this sim. Don't get me wrong, it looks absolutely gorgeous. In terms of quality, The only thing I can compare it to is ATC 4, which is a Japanese ATC sim that can be a bit tricky to acquire, but is far superior to this and has a ton of features not found here. The other ATC Madness series is cartoonish compared to these two sims I've mentioned. I still have this on my wishlist and hope the bugs get squished.

The first reason I refunded this is because there is a well-known adverstising banner bug that prevents you from starting the sim. It advertises DLC for this sim. I reinstalled and did an integrity check of the game files and this fixed that problem initially, but it appeared again the next day.

The second reason I refunded this sim is because I could not pan view around and this was after getting a response from the developer about this problem. I tried the suggestion and it did not work.

The third reason I refunded this sim is because of a well-known ground traffic bug. Some planes that are being pushed back into position so they can begin taxiing to a specific runway simply get stuck. The pushback trolley would just sit there and spin in circles. There is an option to delete airplanes, for whatever reason, but this is laughable. Instead of fixing this problem, it looks as though a quick fix was implemented by allowing a delete feature in game.

That was strike three for me, you're out!

The developer was fast, kind and, more importantly, actually present for customers complaints/questions. Thats a rarity nowadays IMO. I told the developer nevermind and that I was requesting a refund. For $49.99, it just felt like this needed more fine-tuning and these bugs were something I'd expect from an EA title and not a full release.

If you're into ATC or want to learn a bit about this professions experience on a basic level, I'd absolutely keep your eye on this one. I feel bad about the refund and eagerly await patch news. I was really enjoying it and would be more than happy to purchase again after some fixes have been implemented.\t\t\t\t\t
