\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I have looked at this game many times on youtube and read many reviews on Steam. Being an aviation fan, I wanted to try the game out, but waited, and waited, and waited for a sale price. After a long time, no sale appeared, so tonight I took the plunge and paid the (overpriced) amount.

Firstly, many people mentioned the graphics, and yes, they are on the woeful side. But really, this game aint about 'pretty' pictures, but directing air traffic. So as much as I would like better graphics, it is but a secondary consideration.

The voice recognition worked fine straight off for me. I think it really helps to use (good quality) headphones with a mic. Using a desktop microphone can be prone to picking up surrounding noise and interferes with voice recognition, as I have found.

I will agree with many other reviews here on Steam that you have to be an aviation/ATC fan to appreciate and enjoy this game. I have only played for 1.5 hours and no problems. But from what I have read, come the faster traffic and more runways, things sound like they go a bit haywire. By the sounds of it, this seems to be due to an inadequate set of commands.

Overall, my view is yes, buy this if you like the idea of being an air traffic controller and probably overpay for this game. Also the DLC is way too much $$, reminds me of Train Simulator, get the game and then pay outrageous prices for the DLC. Lots of greedy game developers out there, eh!

Some days and hours later, got a new monitor and set up dual monitors for the game. Brilliant! Set up ground control panel on separate monitor at full screen and works really well. Nice to have big picture of ground traffic movement.\t\t\t\t\t

For the small test I did, I noticed that many ATC commands were not available, for example:
"Taxi to rnw 16L via B, cross 16R, B2"
The "cross" part was never understood and I typed it manually because it was not available with the premade sentences.

Additionally:
1. You can't warn aircraft for a late landing clearance.
2. You are not penalized for giving clearance to land on a runway in use.
3. You can't warn aircraft for a go-around if clearance isn't given without losing a lot of points (even though that is standard practice in EU).
4. On the taxiway, you can't order who to follow. It's always "follow company " and never "follow the [insert name here]"
5. Only the controller is able to make mistakes. All aircraft AI never makes mistakes (fixing/handling those mistakes should give points)
etc....

I ended up getting it refunded.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlease read the reviews: It is a
- overpriced
- simulator-not-a-game with
- mediocre-at-best graphics (look at the screenshots and believe them. They're actually quite bad) and
- made up airlines only (the airports are real though)

Once you are clear what you are getting, and get it when it's on sale, though:
+ The commands work great, I have very little trouble. There are a lot of them
- but not quite all. You can not have them give way, or cross a runway when they get there. This causes some micromanagement and some planes that you have to delete in order to fix a traffic jam.
+ It's great fun once you get used to it, and immersive enough to have you lose track of time.

I wish they made a newer version with
- better graphics (especially the render distance. I can only see planes when they are literally at the end of the runway already),
- more commands like cancelling clearances to line up and wait, better routing by "giving way to COMPANY or N PLANES from LEFT/RIGHT" etc (There are like 10 flavours of takeoff clearance with wind and headings and altitudes and what not: That level of depth across the board please),
- and pilots calling you back, e.g. for clearance to cross a runway or if you forget them for a while. Don't just sit there.

Until then: Thumbs up if, but only if you really like ATC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeing the job I always wanted to do, I jumped at the chance to own and play this game as it is as close to tower and ground real air traffic control as you can currently get since it uses speech to control aircraft. You will need to go through Microsoft speech recognition training a few times (it took me 4 times for the game to fully reconise what I was saying).

I bought my game through Stream. The basic game comes with 3 airports and additional airports can be bought. Given the reality of the game it is disappointing to find that the airlines, airline schedules, aircraft and colour schemes are all fictitious.

Despite the control realism, there are some annoying bugs in the game which would never happen in reality:
1. An aircraft entering a taxi way with another aircraft heading towards it will not stop and report the issue it will just continue and plough into it!
2. Much of the time two aircraft at a joining taxi way will not give way to each other and again result in a collision.
3. Some aircraft become stuck - either they will not move off stand when ready to depart or not move to stand after having exited the runway (the latter happens frequently with Munich EDDM on taxi way B7).
4. As stands are auto-allocated by the game, if the target stand for a landing aircraft is in use after exiting the runway the aircraft will refuse to move until the stand is vacated.
5. A couple of times I have accidentally told an aircraft to take off that is behind one that is lined up on the runway ready for take off. I know it's a silly error but instead of reporting that an aircraft is ahead it just ploughs into it!

1,2 and 5 would be considered ATC errors but in reality a pilot would not deliberatly cause these issues!

Real airlines, airline schedules, aircraft and colour schemes have been made available by third parties but there are many posts about the difficulty of installing these, not to mention the significant extra cost. It would be nice to see the original developers incorporate these features. It would be worth paying more to D/L and play the game instead of having to resolve the issue of integrating these add ins.

Despite the bugs and downside it is still an enjoyable game and sometimes quite stressful (try playing Atlanta at 100% traffic as a single player!). I hope these bugs will be fixed in a future release.

Some suggestions for the future
1. Limited hold position. Currently if an aircraft is told to hold postion it will wait forever. In reality the pilot is likely to remind ATC that they are still holding after a few minutes. This has been known to happen at very busy times.
2. Add aircraft towing movements to/from maintenance.
3. Allow the user to allocate gates for landing aircraft.
4. Approach control including multiplayer link to existing tower.\t\t\t\t\t
