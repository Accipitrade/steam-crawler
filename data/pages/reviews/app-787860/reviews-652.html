\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDecent game if you like grinding for things. Essentially that's what this comes down to. You work your fields to make money to buy more fields and bigger equipment to work the fields better. Repeat until you own all the fields and the biggest toys.

The gameplay is pretty simple. There are basic rules and steps to follow for the different types of crops that you grow and the livestock you can keep. The vehicles themselves are all well modelled but I felt a bit more like I was playing with toy farm equipment than actually 'simulating' their driving. Don't expect this to be the 'iRacing' of tractor games, it's more arcade. There is no damage modelling. Collisions do nothing but bump you around and vehicle handling is more like GTA. Still that does allow you to focus more on the farm management and less on learning to drive a harvester. Also wheel support was pretty sparse. My G27 worked but the game offers very little configuration options. Had to do a lot of tweaking outside the game to get it feeling right.

All in all though Farming Simulator was a fun, relaxed experience. Definitely a change of pace from most other games. It does what it does well, that is farm management, and there are a ton of different vehicles to drive, equipment to try, crops to grow and animals to raise.

Not for everyone but definitely check it out if you're interested in farming or just want a relaxed, grindy, time management game to chill out to.\t\t\t\t\t
