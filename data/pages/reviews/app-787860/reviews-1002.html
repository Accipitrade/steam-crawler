\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFarmSim gets a new version every 2 years on the odd years, and with each new version comes new additions. People are quick to complain about this and the other, but fact of the matter is they can't give us EVERYTHING we want up front, from a business point of view. This version has new crops (oats & cotton), new machinery (cotton & John Deere), in-game terrain altering tools, and a new farm animal (horse). The next version will have something new and so on and so on. People are quick to jump at the fact that there is no improvement over the previous (FS15) version, where there clearly has. This game has and always will be "about the mods" so you just have to be patient with that aspect, and FS21 will be no different. I've been playing since FS13 and have been nothing but pleased with the franchise, always waiting for the next installment to publish.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good:
Lots of brands.
They added a store where you can see the equipment before buying it.

The bad:
The gameplay has not changed much from the original 2009 edition, less so from the 2017 version.
Many vehicles have defects in their models, many unacceptable considering they have licences (Valtra N series has both electronic and mechanical controls, since its a reused model of FS17 that was modified).
Maps have horrible defects not present in older Farming Simulators, for example, rail track crossings actually sit over the road instead of in it.

The game calls itself a simulator despite having all models of reduced weight and filtypes that most of the time weight less than half of their actual weight, for example 1000L of water weight less than 600kg.

Game does not posses gearboxes as promised, this combined with the reduced weight of vehicles makes tractors handle like Go-Carts (mods have been built in the past for this, ignored by Giants).

Zero crop simulation other than the amount of times fertilizer is applied and weed control (which happens all the time no matter what). Weather does not affect crops, there is no growing seasons so you can seed and harvest crops at the same time, eliminating any sense of order in the game. "Compaction" is incorrectly fixed by using ploughs, a tool well known to generate compaction (both by reducing the soil structure and the building of plough pans).

Horsepower of any type of harvester does not matter, you will still harvest at the same speed no matter if you combine has 200hp or 500hp and no matter the size of the header. Same with balers.
In any type of work, the only limiting factor is speed, not throughput, not pulling force. Mods simulating this have been build in the past multiple times, but Giants keeps ignoring them.

Ai workers are still not smart enough to drive on the road or do anything other than field work.
Ai drivers still miss spots, get caught on trees, stop unexpectedly, turn in the wrong direction, etcetera.

Manure or any product put on fields can't be cultivated over if the field has already been cultivated.
Dedicated no till drills still remove every residue on the soil.

Graphics;
The ambient in general looks oversaturated with light.
Far away mountains look worse than a 2005 game.
Metal textures lack roughness and many look like base colours in sunlight.
They couldn't even fix the problem of rain falling through roofs of buildings and vehicles, it basically rains inside the cab.
Wipers are just plain moving sticks, there is zero interaction of rain with windshields (a feature present in basically any car or truck simulator in the market a few years ago).
Crops look very spaced, nowhere near the density of a real crop. Same with grass.

A friend of mine who sells Ponsse machinery laughed at the way the Buffalo handles logs, IRL the machine can pick and load multiple logs in one grab with utter precision and force. In game the machine can barely grab one log, and the log tends to slide and glitch out of the machine, the claw has no force at all.
At the end forestry machinery ends up being frustrating and unrewarding to play correctly.


I could keep on going, however all you need to know is this is not a simulator, and don't expect nice graphics anyways.
All of this would maybe be acceptable if this was the third or fourth iteration of the game, however its the seventh, not considering the platinium/gold editions.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, if I could give this one stars, it would only get 4 stars due to technical difficulties. I had the same problem many others seem to be having with the game crashing at start up. Somehow, I figured out that if I unplug my game controller (Wingman Formula Force GP with windows 10 driver) the game will start up every time. I wait until the game is loaded, then plug the controller in. It has a couple other minor glitches, but don't all games have glitches?

I see a lot of people who upgraded from version 17, and they are not impressed. Well, I upgraded from version 15, and it was well worth it. So, I am comparing it to version 15.

It is a bit harder to play than version 15, as you can't just go around mowing every ditch row on the map and make silage out of it, you have to own the land before you can mow it. You also have to work the soil more, fertilizing twice and needing to control the weeds. I like that because it adds realism. I also like being able to lease equipment. It also has a sleep mode, where you can go to your house and get some sleep. I like this because I don't have to wait four or five minutes for night to pass (at 120 x time mode), it passes in about 10 seconds. Being able to work for other farmers is cool, too. (my grandpa used to have an 80 acre farm with more equipment than he needed, so he used to either hire out to other farmers or rent land from other people) And being able to start from scratch (no land or equipment, just money) is very cool. I wish someone would get around to adding the Farming Classics expansion pack, it would go well with starting from scratch.

So if you like FS 15, you will like FS 19 even better.
