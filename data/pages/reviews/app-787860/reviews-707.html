\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving no interest in farming it's strange how compelling this game is to play. The fact that you construct your own world to play in, modify it extensively with external mods and then interact with almost everything you make is, perhaps , what makes it fun to play.

The only let down is the profoundly poor game engine that it runs on. It's poorly optimized with no real atmospherics in the game world and poorly implemented physics and collision detection. Even on a high end system this game struggles to hold 60FPS and frame drops and stuttering are a frequent occurrence.

Rain and water has no bearing on the game at all, which is weird for a farming game, and nothing gets wet, there is no mud simulation or water visible anywhere. Just an annoying rain fall sound and nothing else. The AI traffic is profoundly stupid and just gets in the way all the time. The helpers you can hire are almost as stupid.

It's clear that all of the effort has gone into the machinery design and implementation which is excellent (when the physics aren't flipping your tractor upside down for no reason) but this game desperately needs Unreal 5 and some serious work under the hood to beef up the physics and collision detection and the AI and the atmospherics. This game leans to heavily on a mod community adding things to the experience that should already be there, like Follow Me, innumerable building objects, Course Play and so many more. If an unpaid modder can do it, then why can't a professional game company, however small?\t\t\t\t\t
