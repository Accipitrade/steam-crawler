\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdating as of March '19 - Pre-ordered this game and posted an initial review at release. Lots of positive changes since then. When the game first came out mods were few and far between.
But all that has changed. Here's my breakdown -

Pros:

- Detail. The level of detail put into the equipment in this game is remarkable. I really like the effort Giants puts into these games on that front.
- Mods. Lots of mods
- Tons of different types of maps you can either install from game or get from the internets.
Large fields, small fields, combo of both - whatever you like.
- Tons of different equipment
- Passive income mods are now available too (greenhouses, windmills, beehouse, etc)
- Multiplayer - this game is a lot of fun to play when you have other people to play it with.
Don't get me wrong... single player career mode is perfectly fine in its own right. But it is fun
to play this game with others.
- They have John Deere in this game (although somewhat limited)
- Fendt Ideal - if you've ever had the privilege of seeing one of these beasts in person, you'd appreciate the level of detail in the game on these combines. Easily my favorite.
The Massey Ferguson Activa being a close 2nd.
- Music - you can listen to different genres of music in the game. I like that option.

Cons:

- I am a big fan of Agco, and particularly Fendt brand. They don't (at least not yet) have any
Fendt balers, sprayers, windrower, forge harvester and a few other things. It's entirely
possible maybe someone made one or some of those available on a mods download site
somewhere. Haven't looked there. Would like to see them available in the game mods
menu eventually.
- Hired Workers - If you've played past FS games, you know you can hire workers. And
maybe it's just me, but they just seem pretty dumb unless you're on a perfect square field. I
wished they were more intelligent with working fields because there's a lot fields that are not
going to be perfect squares or rectangles.
- Traffic. Cosmetic of course, but the traffic in the game is completely oblivious to you. I find
that annoying and often times end up turning traffic off. Wished there was a little more logic
to the traffic given how many maps have streets nearby fields.

If you've never played a farm sim game before but the idea perks you're interest, you're not going to go wrong with FS19. This is a good game - if - it's something you are interested in. I have quite a bid of mods installed, mostly directly from the mods menu in the game (some downloaded from websites) and I've never had any crashes or bugs or any crazy crap interrupt my gaming time. Its been pretty stable for me. I should point out that I don't use a wheel or other peripherals... I'm totally keyboard/mouse and perfectly content with that for now.

I would like to see Giants build upon the career mode side of this game in the future. Take sports games for example... there's an actual story your character follows as they build their skills and progress. I'd like to see Giants incorporate something like that (optionally) for career mode. Maybe you inherit a farm from a relative that passed and it's a bit run down, has old equipment, some of it broken or in need of repair. And you start out rebuilding what once was; and eventually work your way up. But part of that would include interaction with other NPCs - perhaps with reps at a farm machinery show or something. They do have different career modes you can start in but from an immersive perspective they are all lacking. Doesn't make it bad per se, but I think it would be a neat addition if Giants were build on those in the future.

Overall 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t

MY ORIGINAL REVIEW \\/
I feel really sad and disappointed to be writing this negative review. I was hyped for the game, pre-ordered it and put over 540 hours into FS17. I also own FS15, FS13, and FS11. I even made videos highlighting the awesome new things coming to this game. I can say I was disappointed. If these things get added or fixed, I will be happy. Until then, not impressed.

11/24/2018 This is my first adjustment to this review based on what has been happening in the last few days.

1. Mods are beginning to come out. These mods should have come with the game but they are slowly trickling in. Not all are there that are needed so you might have to wait to see that essential tool you need to play come through.
2. Still no: Greenhouses, beehouses, windmills or any real placeable that can earn passive income
3. Placing placeables where you want them is tricky and not easy at all. It becomes really frustrating especially a game that is trying to highlight it's ability to create your own farm.
4. You are able to "create" farm but you have very few options to pick up sheds, shelters or halls
5. Hired hands are still bad. In fact, they are worse than 17 to which I thought they were decent with the occassional tree crashing. But now, they will leave the corners and ends of a field not harvested.
6. Raveport is a map full of squiggly roads that seems to just make you have to drive twisty and super thin roads uphill and downhill to artifically make the game more challenging. 2nd map isn't better because it has the same issues. Roads mostly do not directly lead anywhere as you have to take mutiple turns and side roads to get anywhere useful.
7. Animal pallets like egg crates will NOT appear. I was able to get wool pallets to appear only if no equipment is in close proximity to animal pen.
8. You can't buy multiple pallets/bags of seeds. Seriously! You need to buy one by one and confirm you want to buy it instead of typing how many you want or having a drop down menu and select amount.
9. There is now a penalty for resetting/fast traveling items back to store. You fuel will GO TO almost 0 after teleporting/resetting[! You will have to fill up tank AGAIN which costs up to $300 plus.
10. Mission reward amounts are super low vs. work and time you put into them
11. They changed the icons for the crops, hay, grass, straw etc. and they are so similar to one another and hard to decipher and they are not named in the prices window. You have to hunt for the icon legend and GO back to verify which icon is which crop they are. Hay, Grass, Straw, Silage and others are hard to tell apart.
12. The controls for the Forestry Cranes have changed and they are strange to say the least, not intuitive at all. I loved forestry and now, decided to sell all the equipment back.
13. No placeable roads to connect locations


I want to update this review in time if these things get fixed because I want to give this game a good review. But I also want to be transparent.


These are just some of the issues in the game. If these are fixed, I'll be excited to play it again. I can't believe the price is $34 and I have to wait for mods to do CORE things in the game. I understand first day bugs will happen but the game seemed more hyped up than anything. Beware!\t\t\t\t\t
