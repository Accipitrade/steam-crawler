\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI totatally love FS and have spent more hours in it than I care to think about.

If there was an "unsure" button to click when recommending games, this is the one I would use for FS2019. Seeing as there isn't, I am going to go with "Not Recommended".

I have played for a few hours now, and to be honest I am a little underwhelmed. The graphics may be slightly better, and the machinery more varied, but I am struggling to really find anything new that can really justify a whole new game with a fairly hefty price tag.

The game mechanics seem to have stayed fairly much the same as have the physics. The graphics and HUD may have had a slight improvement maybe but nothing earthshattering. Yeah, they added "Horses" and "Weeding" but nothing with the potential to blow my socks off.

I was hoping for a larger improvement on the previous titles. Mud? (thinking Spintires here). Greater weather dynamics (snow or ice?). Different terrain (marshland?). Different aspects to the agricultural industry (reclaiming boggy land, use of excavators to install drains, buying heavier lifting equipment to build sheds?) Some of these things do actually exist in the world of farming.

I know I expected too much - although I would have been happy with just a little bit more. Sorry Giants - I have loved your work up until now.

For newcomers, I recommend this game, and FS diehards will buy it anyway (I did!). But if you owned FS17 and aren't sure where to invest your hard-earned cash, maybe leave this one to the side for now.\t\t\t\t\t

Cons: Workers keep leaving small spots undone, workers won't stop backing up after they get done with a job, machinary resets to shop instead of your farm, too many posts to run into and get stuck, you run out of fuel way too quickly, land is extremely expensive, you get horses but I can't place them anywhere yet, I don't know if it's my computer or not but it started running almost perfectly but now it keeps glitching, It rains a little too much, It's hardly every sunny and clear, I'll run a cultivator that can plant as well but it looks like it just cultivates instead, steering and camera movement are slow, it's really easy to get hit by the train it comes by really fast and you don't get much warning, It's very bumpy, a lot of hills making it harder to turn around, birds flying in and out of your field gets annoying, cones are in the way at the gas station, workers take forever to turn around, and you can't just buy the fields you have to buy the land around the fields as well.

I'm still playing the game, and I haven't gone through everything, but this is what I've noticed so far.

Personally, I like Farming Simulator 17 the best.\t\t\t\t\t
