\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've read a significant amount of the reviews posted and I can honestly understand and see the discrepencies that people have with this game, and I also agree. If you have FS17 this game is not worth it,yet.

I discovered Farming Simulator a few months ago, at that point FS19 was already announced, and FS17 had been out for a significant period of time. Both were the same cost so I decidided to do the not so pleasant thing and download FS17 "for free" to see if the game would even be worth it. After playing FS17 for a few hours I absolutely loved the game and decided to preorder FS19 because i figured there would be minor changes, but the gameplay would probably stay the same.

Now that my little backstory is done, why do I recommend FS19. Honestly because the game is very similar to 17, but with a few crucial added features, and while yes a good portion of those features were mods on FS17, that in itself is why I like Giants Software. They actually listen to their community, and actually support modding and custom creations. Through map contests, or equipment contests, and even tractor contests. The company loves when people mod the game, and that's why FS17 was amazing. I could download hundreds of mods, from small textures, to manufacturer packs that included everything from small cultivators to 600 Horsepower Harvestors from companies that the game did not have in it. And judging from this game the modding, and community improvement will only continue.

I like to see Farming Simulator as a foundation of farming games. It throws all of the mechanics, basic machinery, and general farming aspects of the game, and allows its consumers to bascially do whatever their hearts desire. If you already have FS17 I would wait for a sale, however if you are new to FS19 or just want the game early, i recommend it thourougly.

( The game at 11/20/2018, is relatively buggy, however they are not gamebreaking and Giants Software have already stated that upcoming patches should fix most of them, that's why I did not mention them in the review)\t\t\t\t\t

-Mapping controls to a wheel/joystick is such a nightmare, It's a huge guessing game as to what input does what. Naming the axis for the wheel as "axis x etc" is so confusing to figure out what deadzone I am adjusting.

-the LOD distance is so low, it really ruins the immersion
-the AI is horrible yet again... seriously how hard is it to make decent AI? why do you rely on mods to fix this.
-Vehicles handle so unrealistically
-No gearbox mechanics
-Can't buy things in store and have then delivered to your farm, pretty stupid.
-Everything just feels so "2D" I wish the terrain had real deformation like in mudrunner or other farming sims
-I brought this on release and it was horrible, waited almost 2 years before buying again in hopes things would be fixed, and was pretty gutted to find out the game is almost exactly as bad as it was back then.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed 13, 15 and 17, so this isn't my first Farming Simulator. I'm impressed overall with the improvements they made to the game. I've noticed some peopel saying that the game is buggy - I haven't come across anything yet, doesn't mean it won't happen, but I've been playing this game on my Surface Book 2 running the GTX 1060 with no issues. Haven't tried it on my desktop yet, but I doubt the RTX loaded in that will have any issues.

I'm impressed with the ability to build your farm any way you like. The basic mode of getting a pre-built farm is also still enjoyable as always, and the difficulty can now be changed in game , so any mode that is chosed can be modified later. The new graphics engine is cool - the vehicles react to the terrain underneath a little more, and they put the seat stabilizers in the game as well, making it a little jerky at times just because you're not actually in the tractor. I've been enjoying it so far and I expect I'll be enjoying it for many years to come.\t\t\t\t\t
