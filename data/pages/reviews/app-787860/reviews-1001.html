\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like FS19, it has a lot of potential. Unfortunately the modding community needs to step in to take advantage of that potential. There are some mods out there, but the majority of them are just as broken and clunky as the game itself is. FS19 is visually much prettier than FS17, but its gameplay leaves alot to be desired.

As a note to the Devs (if you are even reading this)......Seriously? This is sad. You bring nothing to the base game thats new except John Deere being available from the in game store, and the pretty graphics. You have improved on nothing from FS17, if anything you've taken a step back.

If you are thinking about it, just wait. Stick to FS17 where the modding community has sorted things out. Give FS19 time to mature and pick it up in a year when its on sale.



I'm leaving my earlier review as a reminder to the FS team to not completely fudge up their next game. The next version of FS should be much more polished than FS19 was upon release. I am talking about incorporating some of the BIG mainstream mods into the actual gameplay up front as standard. Don't rely on the community of gamers so heavily to add the content everyone wants. You know what we want! You sponser most of the mods in game for crying out loud!

I can safely say now that thanks to the modding community, FS19 is now a spectacular game. You can now get all the content you could ever dream of in a sim game. The mods for realism are pretty intense. Seasons, Precision farming, courseplay, global company, its insane. I absolutely love the game this has turned in to thanks to the mods.

Bottom line - vanilla game is blander than watching paint dry, mods make the game truly shine.\t\t\t\t\t
