\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had previously played FS13 and FS17, and enjoyed them mildly, but never felt the hook. They were too gamey, too repetitive, not really simulators at all, and lack many aspects that would make them enjoyable immersive games. FS17 was a baby step in the right direction, but not enough to garner more than a passing bit of play.

Farming Simulator 19 is a giant leap forward in playability and immersiveness. Is it perfect? Not yet. But the addition of placeable buildings, terraforming (still rough), and the beautiful graphics make it so much easier to suspend disbelief and immerse oneself in the game's universe. The out of the box maps are beautiful and while still smallish and with limited "environmental interactions" (no access to most buildings, NPC people and vehicles run in continuous mindless loops, and no real NPC interactions), they take you into the setting and get you feeling like a farmer much more than any other game in the franchise.

I have only played a limited amount of time (42.4 hours at this writing) and I still have many many more things to do in the game before I have exhausted my interests. The learning curve is a little steep out of the box, and the help is really limited, but thankfully there is a huge community to support fellow players and most answers are just a quick search away and a video or two on your favorite video service.

I do believe that GIANT and the modding community will add even more content in the next year that make this farming sim one of the best and most playable sims of this decade.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like driving/simulation games (not racing games, these are mostly slow tractors) with a bit of management/economics thrown in, this is the game for you.
A little tricky to get into at first (many icons which don't have a hover over pop up description :( , so a novice user like me spent lots of time looking things up online when a hover over description would have been far better) but persevere and you will find it's worth it.
Not a quick game where you will play for a few hours and then move on, this one will keep you busy and entertained for hours. Can get a little monotonous with the same tasks over and over but you can always hire helper workers to do these and concentrate on the tasks you enjoy (or sit back completely and let workers do the driving while you do the planning and managing). Game can be tuned to remove some of the harder elements but playing with all options on makes for a better game.
With plenty of mods available and likely future DLCs, pretty sure the game will only get better.
Still learning aspects of the game (plenty to keep me going) but would recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

Bottom line: I can't recommend this game right now if you're looking for realistic farming. Go with FS17 and it's plethora of mods (Seasons/MoreRealistic/Gearbox/etc)

Good:
New contract system
Pretty lighting
Multiplayer lag seems better
Multiplayer farm management
Many mods now part of the base game (hoses, field info, etc)

Bad:
Many, many, many bugs
Controller support broken
Same old broken physics
Base maps very bland and Ravenport is clearly not even finished
No sense of weight to vehicles, they feel like they're made of paper
Weeds/lime are just plowing/fertilizing shuffled around, no new gameplay
Animals eat insane amounts of food (1 horse needs 1000L of oats per day for example)
and much much more, it's kind of depressing\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you enjoy the idea of 3 grueling hours of adjusting the height, position, and angle of a forklift just to properly move a pallet? How about doing that 1000x more? Because that is exactly what this game is. An unrelenting, hellish, frustrating act of futility.

I've played all kinds of simulation games in the past, but this is the worst kind. I knew from reading about the game that it was not going to be your typical "big picture" sort of simulation. I actually looked forward to the likely monotony, thinking it would be calming and zen-like to plow fields and tend to my animals. But NOTHING is streamlined or automated and you are required to do every frustrating minutia. I'd even be okay with the forklifting (as I mentioned above) if the game automated it's placement on the back of a trailer. BUT NO. You have to place everything EXACTLY right or it all falls apart. In fact, I've actually driven a forklift in real life. That was easier than trying to control it in this game.

There is a thousand different tools that attach to only specific machines and no clear indication of what fits what and how to utilized anything properly. No guidance, no clarity, no help (the help files are useless). I did the tutorial level, and it basically just stops helping after a few simple tasks and you are left to fumble around and try and make something happen.

If you are some kind of masochist who likes being put through sessions of agonizing pain and torment, you might like this game. But I'd still recommend you find someone who would be willing to just kick you repeated in the crotch and save yourself the $$$ from buying this awful game.

This game also requires very specific video cards to run. I tried to run this on my laptop with a powerful, yet off-brand, GPU and the game just freezes during load. Nvidia on my desktop worked fine.

This game is definitely not worth the $35.99 I paid. I would get a refund if I hadn't already spent six hours trying to move things with a forklift.\t\t\t\t\t
