\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR - While I say I recommend the game, wait to purchase it. Wait for Giants to fix some basic quality of issues, and/or for mod support to be in full swing. Bugs are minimal, gameplay is always smooth and satisfying.

UPDATES: They have updated the game some, AI is still horrid but atleast now we can paint terrain and terraform. Which definitly helps bring immersion into the game, you can make your farm actually look like a farm. Still some minor irritations from me though, it's almost like Giants is just relying on the modders at this point to really make the game, espesially with placeables. Like come on, give me a proper default hay loft or some variety in tractor storage sheds.

The game looks amazing, a definite step up from previous titles. The lighting is probably my favorite added feature to the game, and the random wildlife, more random outdoors sounds. It really makes the game feel alive to me, which FS17 lacked.

It plays very well, bugs are few and far between, while the number of brands is down this time around, the vanilla equipment availble is much better than FS17 in terms of usability for my play style. The incoming mods are very promising looking, Giants really stepped up their game for supporting modders. Which in my opinon is a gift and a curse, sometimes it feels that Giants kicked their feet up for some features and said "eh, the modders will fix it, leave it broken for now". But in my honest opinion, I think it's time for Giants to move to another Game Engine, on that is more 2018. They are doing great things wtih this older engine, but it's time something new.

The games AI somehow took a giant step back. Which is wildly frustrating, because by default maps now have odd shapped fields, and the AI has ZERO idea how to handle it. They just, in many cases... fall off a cliff. It is my number one gripe in the game at the moment, it makes it very difficult to multi-task. Also still has the issue from FS17 when the worker only uses 3/4 of the actual workign width of the implement it is using, which means wages are higher than they need to be, diesel usage is higher also.

Physics are less than... perfect. Sometimes it feels like they didn't even play test the game. Tractors get floaty steering and will drift pulling empty trailers, skid steer falls on it's face lifting more than 100 pounds, which makes loading flat beds with pallets and such a fight.

Default maps feel slapped together, liek they are a tech demo with a couple good photo spots and LOTS of empty land, which makes it feel barren. Which is another bad point, the game still has the "bubble" for view distance. It's a solid option for people that have low-mid PC's, but for us with high end PC's, it would be nice to increase it even more.. without having to alter an .ini file. Also the whole "build your own farm" thing, what a disgusting mess and waste, it's a hap-hazardly thrown together feature. It's useless in my opinion. The 3 default maps, all only have ONE, yes ONE starting area realistically, unless you want to start your farm in a field. Also note, there is no vanilla way to pave or change the ground to cement or dirt, rocks ect.

Still no dynamic ground... this is 2018... oh well. The painted flat textures look pretty 3D, but having real soil simulation would be such a treat, and open so many more gameplay doors. Especially if seasons was a thing *cough* *cough*

Other than those gripes, I think this is a solid step forward in the series. I enjoy it when I just want to relax and stop chasing Diamond in R6:Siege or wanting to throw my PC out the window playing Escape From Tarkov.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played since FS11, it is really interesting to see all the changes in the game over the years.

In my opinion some of the new features in FS19 makes it a very good new generation of the game:

- The machinery detail and additional suspension and ground response makes the driving feel much more realistic
- The way animal pens are placeable and can be put down in multiples, really opens up the game for focussing and scaling livestock farms in a whole new way.
- The wear and repair system for machinery was previously only available through mods - and seeing this in the base game is great
- Obviously there's been a huge graphics uplift as well
- Finances are more challenging now - things are expensive - but you can blaance it, and there's better access to loans
- Introduction of weeds and herbicides is really great. Sprayers were previously only for fertilising, which frankly was not very realistic.
- New animals opens up the game to a wider style of players - focus on building a successful horse livery where the farming is done to feed and keep the horses - the options are there now!
- Houses that allow players to skip the nights, or some of the darkest hours at least.
- A massive range of vehicles compared to any previous version of the game!
- Additional crop types that adds lots of new options and tools (sugarcane if you didn't play FS17 with DLC expansion, Cotton, and oats)


Having said that - there are of course also things that seems a little unbalanced. There seems to be many people suggesting a huge amount of bugs, but after 38 hours, I am still to come across one. I knw this can depend on systems, and know there has been issuses with steering wheels, but overall, this game runs very, very well on the same system that ran the previous version of the game - you do not need a absolutel beast of a computer to play this.

The things that mainly are drawbacks - which i suspect might get fixed/balanced in patches are:

- Lighting cuts off quite hard at night/in the morning.
- The prices of some of the animal placeables are quite steep - but what really wrecks this part is the terraforming costs, which easily pushes the costs up by several multiples of the base cost. If you don't pay attention while placing a building, it could empty your bank account!

And as with previous versions of Farming simulator - mods will take this game to even better and exciting experiences in time - across all platforms now.

All in all - a very warm recommendation for anyone who wants to play a versatile farming game with a tons of features!\t\t\t\t\t
