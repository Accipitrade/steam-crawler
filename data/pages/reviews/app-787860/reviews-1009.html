\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI came from FS17 with high expectations, but it rapidly became blatantly obvious that Farming Simulator relies upon the good will and efforts of the modding community. Even with the few mods currently available for FS19, I'm essentially playing a vanilla version of the game. No real physics, no real weather, no seasons. Only a small handful of maps available, to bolster the 2 maps that come with the game. For me, with FS17, it was a way to pass the time, thinking about life as I nurtured my crops, but there was a diverse stockpile of mods to make things slightly more realistic.

FS19 suffers because the modding community hasn't been able to transfer their FS17 mods to this latest version. And for me, that demonstrates how little Giant/Focus actually do to create a fantastic game. Farming Simulator definitely relies upon the unsung heroes, the people who don't get paid, to create content for a very basic game. It's as though the devs had a meeting and said, "Real mud? Real physics? Real seasons and weather? Nah, we don't have to do that. That's too much like hard work. Let the modders do it for us."

In truth, FS19 has the potential to become a great game (the graphics are slightly improved over FS17, and there's the option to start from scratch and buy tracts of land as you increase the size of your farm), but without the modders, it's not worth getting if you already have FS17. I stopped playing FS17 because I was hoping FS19 would be a vast improvement. It's not.\t\t\t\t\t
