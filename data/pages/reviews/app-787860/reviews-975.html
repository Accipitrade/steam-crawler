\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFarming doesn't even interest me, and I love this game. It's a game that you can sink hundreds of hours into easily.

Pros:
1. Realistic approach to farming is actually very calming. It's a nice way to chill after a stressful day
2. The game is very immersive, so it creates a very Zen experience
3. Graphics are realistic, and there's the right amount of "shake" to make you feel like you're actually operating the vehicles
4. Multiplayer makes the game lots of fun to play with friends / family
5. Numerous ways that you can play the game, so you're not trapped into just crops if that's not your thing
6. Can be played as either a Tycoon where you try to amass as much wealth as possible, or you can pursue a more Zen experience (my personal approach to the game) by simply immersing yourself in the world. The game is very open world, so you are free to pursue what you would like

Cons:
1. The controls of a couple vehicles (the tree crane thing from the tutorial comes to mind) are beyond ridiculous, and very hard to learn.
2. There's a bit of a learning curve for the game in general. You'll go through an hour or two of tutorials before you feel even mildly comfortable starting. They haven't done anything wrong here. There's just so much that you can do in the game, and to achieve the level of realism they have reached it's necessary.
3. The game needs to explain (preferably with tooltips as your hover your mouse) all of the various icons. Someone who's not familiar with farming isn't going to instinctively know that baler X is only used to create round bales (or that there is no equipment that loads round bales, you have to do it manually), whereas baler Y will create square bales. The shop will just display some cryptic icons that mean nothing to anyone except the developers, and those that have spent hours googling to decipher these strange mostly unintuitive symbols. The text won't usually be helpful either for answering compatibility questions. It will simply say something like ARCO 650, and NONE of the equipment anywhere mentions ARCO 650, so you have no idea what it's compatible with, so you end up wasting many thousands of dollars experimenting. This is my biggest gripe about the game, and it's what turned my wife off to it even though I think it's a fantastic game. It just requires so much research outside the game to understand how to play it.
4. Forestry tools were designed by a sadomasochist with a penchant for "the claw" vending machine games, and thusly decided to provide all of the forestry equipment with similar performance characteristics to his beloved vending machine game.\t\t\t\t\t
