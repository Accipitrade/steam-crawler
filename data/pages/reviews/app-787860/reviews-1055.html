\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeing a reasonably experienced Farm Sim player since 2011 (also own previous versions), I had high hopes for FS19 before release. It seemed as if Giants had done a complete overhaul to the engine and made it look much nicer than previous versions.

And in my opinion, they didn't dissapoint. Of course, my potato bag of a computer isn't quite the machine you'd want to be playing this game, but upon opening the game, I was already impressed by the new GUI/HUD and loading screens. The graphics, after some tweaking, look much nicer and full of colour than FS17, albeit a slight bit more saturated for my liking. Lighting and reflections look much better and bounce of the tractors and implements nicer.

Vehicle handling seems to have improved. From what I remember as a comparison, FS15 was very slidy, tractors would tend to drift everywhere. FS17 fixed this, but hardened it too much and made the tractors grip to the ground like velcro, but I personally prefered it to FS15. FS19 has loosened it up a bit, but the gravity of the game feels lighter, as if you are nearer the moon than anything. As far as detail of vehicles goes, serious improvements. They look much cleaner and nicer, and lighting for screens and dashboards is another nice touch, along with cab suspension and moving parts inside (pedals, levers, etc). Steering is another change to the game, where instead of the steering returning to its normal positioning when the button is let go, it will stay in it's current position, and steer back when the tractor is moving.

The placeable buildings feature is something I'm not very keen on. I preferred it when the sheds and silos were preplaced, like in previous games. This only applies to 2 of the 3 gamemodes, where the first 'Easy' mode already has tractors and sheds laid out for you, where as the other 2 leave you with nothing (one does give you a hefty sum of cash to start out with).

As many reviews say, bugs are a big problem in the game at this stage. Graphics, handling issues, glitches, many things to name, but as of now I shall not mention any because they have been in pretty much 90% of all reviews. The game is in its very early stages, people, give it time, the game is in store for many improvements. Patience.

So if you are keen on FS19, whether it be the new tractors, horses or just want an upgrade, get on now! But if you want to hold out until Christmas or maybe earlier/later, FS17 will do you fine until that time. Instead of naming every single feature in this crappy review of mine, I'll leave it to the other reviewers more serious than me to take care of that, or even better, get in your wallet and buy it yourself. From my point of view, it'll be worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
