\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: I've added some things that I find annoying about this game that really detract from its value. Please take that into consideration.

I've been a "fan" of the farming sim genre for a while now. I got really hooked on Farm Simulator 2013 -- I probably put 200 hours into that game, which was way more than I anticipated. I tried again with '14, '15, '17 and now '19 but so far nothing has recaptured that feel for me yet.

I'm not even into the game that far yet, but I can recognize they've added a bunch of options and made it much more customizable. For one thing, you have a lot of options for crops to raise. Back in '13 it was wheat, barley, canola, corn, potatos, and beets, and oh yeah, you could just let the fields grow grass and cut that for hay bales. This game has a lot more options for crops -- 13 different crops including grain, root crops (potatoes etc), cotton, sugarcane, and sunflowers.

There's also a lumber side-gig you can do where you chop trees down (with machinery) to sell to a lumber yard. I don't know if that's really going to be something I'll want to do since to me the idea of waiting for a tree to grow so I can cut it down and sell it for profit sounds like a boring job. If you're talking about a forest then it's different.

The types of animals you can have has also increased from just "Chickens, sheep, and cows" in '13 to chickens, cows, horses, pigs, and sheep, as well as additions to you can do with them. Before it was just eggs, milk and wool... oh and manure for slurry/fertilizing. I don't yet know if this is a good change, and as I figure it out I'll edit this review.

Of course they've added a bunch of different machinery types, so you can feel all manly driving a multitude of different tractors and other vehicles, but they also gave you options for buying vs leasing (which is actually more like renting). If there's a vehicle you only need rarely, you could just lease it and then return it when you're done instead of spending the large amount of cash for something you barely use.

Another change is that they've added the ability to buy and place your own farmhouse. In the original game you had one farmhouse with a small nearby field, and then you slowly bought up all the other land plots around you to increase your farm's size. Now you could potentially buy/build a new house for every field if you wanted.

There's also mods supported much like every Farm sim game ever (at least as far as I know) and you can play this online with friends. In fact I'd go as far as to say that playing it online is probably the best way to go, since offline single-player feels like you're the only person in town. There are AI cars that drive around, but they really don't do anything more than provide scenery and obstacles for you as you drive from point A to point B.

Unlike older games in the series, there's very little online documentation about this game unless you like watching youtube videos that take 26 minutes to get to the point you're wondering about. I prefer a nice manual or at the bare minimum, a wiki that I can consult for help. The in-game help is just about useless by telling you almost nothing about each category, so without a wiki or a manual you're going to be running to the steam forums to find the answers. The really bad thing is that nobody finds out the answers without a lot of trial and error and in some cases, LOTS of errors. How hard is it to make a dang manual Giants?

The older games also had some kind of extra side-quest type stuff that encouraged you to fully explore the map. In 2013 there was a bunch of horseshoes littering the countryside (but no horses strangely enough) and if you found enough horseshoes it would trigger an event. One of the later games (2015 maybe?) had you looking for actual gold bars lying around. I had an easier time believing that someone might leave horseshoes lying around, but both served the same purpose: find them for an achievement and maybe to trigger some event. So far as I can tell in 2019 this doesn't happen.

It was kind of a pointless easter egg hunt in the earlier games so maybe they decided that few people really cared about it and removed it. I liked it because it gave me a reason to wander around the map on foot while I waited for my crops to grow. I could easily have just cranked the time compression up to max and then back down to real-time to harvest, but that was equally boring since I just wound up staying there watching the crops grow.

Overall though, I don't find this game as interesting as the first game I played. I don't know if that's because I already burned out on the idea of being a video game farmer, or if it's just that the game mechanics are kinda dry and repetitive. This is a game I'd give a "meh" thumb to if I could. It's not worth investing hundreds of hours into without friends who are also into the game, but it's not so terrible that I'd warn someone off of it entirely.

I'm starting to think at this point that you'd be better off getting FS 2017 and then modding it up to 2019 standards. I've had the game crash on me a couple of times out of nowhere (just randomly as far as I can tell. I can't reproduce the error anyway) and I've purchased an item that sells in the store as a planter only to find out it can only be loaded with dry fertilizer).

My advice would be to try one of the earlier incarnations of this game that are much simpler in scope like Farm Simulator 2013, and if you like that one, you might also want this one for the expanded options. The thumbs up is basically because I don't hate it...but I haven't really yet come to love it either. Your mileage may vary. If you enjoy building your own farm and paying back a loan then maybe you'll find it engaging enough -- That was one of the original aspects of the game that I enjoyed.\t\t\t\t\t
