\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat can I say about this game? So far I have logged 273 hours. Shouldn't that be enough? It seems like there is no shortage of 'things to do.' Just as soon as you get one task mastered (be it growing crops, making hay or grass bales, raising animals, whatever) you find a different way to make that task more efficient - or even find a totally different thing to do. You can end up filling up your virtual 'days' with an endless amount of farming, constantly upgrading your farming arsenal to more and more powerful machines. (And there are... I don't even know how many trucks, tractors, trailers, and machines to buy. A newbie will be a little overwhelmed by all the stuff that's available.)

The graphics are fantastic - absolutely stunning, and the attention to detail (ie. tire tracks in a freshly-plowed field) are almost unnecessarily realistic. The ambiance (dog barking in background, birds chirping, etc) is enough to lull you into a sleepy daze as you harvest the fields. (The game is not boring at all - very relaxing and VERY chill - so much so that a couple times as I have been knocking down a massive field, I have caught myself dozing off.) There's no rush at all, and no 'goal' - you plan out your day, what you want to accomplish and when, and just... work your butt off, I guess. Despite this very, very chill approach, you don't find yourself losing interest - quite the opposite, you find yourself sitting there thinking, "Ok, after I get this field, I really need to run some loads over to the grain elevator for some extra cash, then I can get my new seeder so I can..." etc.

Yeah, this is not the kind of game you play for a while and then wind up forgetting about. Trust me. You'll be back.\t\t\t\t\t
