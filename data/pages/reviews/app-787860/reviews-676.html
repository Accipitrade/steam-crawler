\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who has never played any of the Farming Simulator series, and this is my first time playing it, I would like to share my opinions about this game.

The game at first seems really difficult to understand, and frankly, it was kind of frustrating. But with tutorials, searching online for videos with tips or pages with useful information in them, and some playing, I really got the hang of it. Once you learn how Plowing, Cultivation, Sowing, etc. works, you pretty much go on your business with planting your favorite grains, or any of the other types the game has to offer. The graphical aspect of the game is amazing, no complaints there, although if you want, you can also download textures. The way the different machines work is just astonishing and really satisfying. The level of detail in each one of the machines is beautiful and I could not ask for better than that. To be honest, I really like big machines such as Harvesters, because they have that something about them which makes you want to use them. The game is superbly relaxing when you're just sowing or harvesting something on your field/s, or just when you drive around to deliver chaff, for example.
The amount of machines you can work with and use is totally ENOUGH for me (and that might be true for you too), although you can buy the dlcs which would add more options in your machinery sections, depending on what you're working on. You can also download great mods depending on what you need (Certain cultivators for corn for example). I would say one thing though; If you're planning on growing sugarcanes, know that your options are almost NON-EXISTENT, that is because you have only one machine to work with and attaching a trailer to it is useless as the trailers that DO fit are small in their capacity, as harvesting sugarcanes gives huge amounts of it and you'll need a friend that will help you with the trailer part or, just use some mods.
Working on your field and growing grains and crops and getting the reward, in the end, is what makes me keep playing the game.
You have 3 modes which you have to pick one of them, "New farmer" which is for newbies, "Farm manager" which is for amateur+ players who already have learned how to play, and "Start from scratch" which is for the expert players who truly feel they're up for a challenge.
The gameplay part is great, and its really easy to play with a keyboard once you memorize all the buttons for all the controls (The same goes for controller users too). You can buy many fields, and if two maps aren't enough for you, you can download user-made content which will make playing the game even more fun and it will last much, much longer. This game takes time to master and understand, so don't skip the tutorials as they are your starting point.
Before buying the game I was thinking if I would even play it, but I am glad that I purchased the game.
I can write more about the game but for now, I'll let you decide if you want to buy the game or not. I would totally recommend the game to a friend. 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t
