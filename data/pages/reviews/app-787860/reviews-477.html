\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf there's anything you read in this review, let it be this: you don't need the DLC to have the complete experience. With that out of the way, this game is a good one if you like menial seemingly mindless tasks to relax to at the end of a hard day of work, whether physical or mental.
It is also a game of progression through parts of the game. I recommend starting with the base game, then adding the mainstream add-ons (precision farming, Seasons) once you master that. For livestock, the wiki is your friend on how much work you will need to put in to get results. Precision Farming's wiki is in-game, Seasons, give it a google (fs19 seasons, there was a mod for fs17 as well) or settle in for a Youtube video.
Find a task in-game that you think "there's got to be a better way" or want more variety in tools/equipment? Mods are your friend. Not just the ones in the mod tab, not just the ones in the mod tab once you click the "show all mods" button. Go searching on the web for more! You may even find ones that emulate/replace what the DLC has! (be warned, this is addictive, I am sitting on a 50GB mod folder as of writing. Also, be aware that FS19 looks for and stores mods on the Boot Drive in the documents. Small C:\\ drives, be warned!

Overall, game's a framework. Does it have issues? Oh gosh yes, the physics engine will occasionally freak out if you or the object/equipment does something "wrong", and things might not work if you're doing them in the "wrong" way, as interpreted by the game or tool. But the community has your back on making the game as easy, difficult, and somewhere in-between as you want.\t\t\t\t\t
