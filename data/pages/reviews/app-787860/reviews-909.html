\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall:
It's a nice step forward in the Giants Farming Simulator series and it is well worth the money if you're new to the farming simulator genre. Giants is THE top dog in the farming simulator world of gaming. But for long time fans/players I would wait for it to go on sale because, sadly, there just isn't enough new additions to justify paying full price for this release.

TL;DR
At the time of writing this review I've only tinkered with FS19 for around 5 hours but I feel, considering that I'm a long time player that has played every iteration of Giants FS series since FS2009 and I have released maps/mods to the community over the years as well, that 5 hours was enough time to preview all of the new additions to this version.

Sometime between the release of FS15 and FS17 I began to come to the slow realization that Giants was turning their Farming Simulator series into a cash grab. While each version of the game arguably has an impressive, real to life, lineup of machinery to use in the game it just seems that Giants has lost focus on a few things. What I mean by that is while it may be nice to drive around a pretty tractor if that tractor can only drive around there really is no point now is there?

There is so much more to farming that just planting, tilling, fertilizing, harvesting, and raising animals. What about agricultural industry? What about seasons? Mods are filling this void and I hope Giants is taking note of this. They have in the past and I hope they go back to that as they once did when they added the most popular mods like logging, cruise control, vehicle washing, etc, etc.

Is this a bad game? No. Not at all. It's a very good game. It's stable and it plays very nicely. There are quite a few new additions that are really nice and I'm not complaining one bit. It's just that I believe Giants needs to get back to being more mindful of their community going forward is all.\t\t\t\t\t
