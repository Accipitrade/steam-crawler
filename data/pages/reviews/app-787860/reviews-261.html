I had played FS17 but I always buy FS towards their end, because I want them with discount and all dlc and all mods available. I don't really trust this dev team because they had many design aspects of FS17 that were lazy programming despite the success of the series, they just don't reinvest back in this game, it seems.

So this game is mostly about operating vehicles and machinery, while also doing some passive income stuff like animals etc. The manipulation and driving of machines is absolutely horrible. Driving feels totally unnatural. The cars are weightless, except when you are in a 1% slope, then their weight triples. When you turn with a loaded truck with 15km or above you will either overturn or roll over. This can be extremely frustrating if you forget to fasten the cargo belts and have logs rolling in the street. There is no progression to the rolling over, it just happens. No visual or audio feedback that it's about to happen, nothing. Just a roll when you turn. The acceleration in the driving is also linear. The same time it takes from 0-10 it also takes from 70-80. So yea. very lazy programming in a core game mechanic, since 90% of the time in this game you will be driving something.

Having played many driving games and racing sims, and knowing that the know how exists, I find it very irresponsible for a successful company such as the one developing FS series, to deliver such horrible driving.\t\t\t\t\t
