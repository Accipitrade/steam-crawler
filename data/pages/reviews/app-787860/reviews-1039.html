\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith 2000+ hours in FS15 and FS17, I feel comfortable leaving a review on FS19 with 23 hours in. Like all FS games before it, this version is better in all ways than previous versions. Most things you'd like to see improved or added have been. Graphics are better across the board. Not a big change, but better, smoother, and more detailed. General physics are better, especially ground response and crop response (crops move when driven over). In cab especially feels more real. Also now, whether in cab or out, you can see pedals and levers move depending on what your doing. It's not noticable right away, especially if your not a seasoned FS player, but you'll see it. It's not a huge thing, but these little details are what make the FS series great. The new property buying sytem is nice, along with the placaeable aspect, although out of the box, there aren't alot of placeables. The side jobs now seem more like jobs, and not just free money. The nice thing especially is you can use your own equipment or lease it just for the job. You can even put a worker on it if you've got better things to do and have extra equipment to spare. Also, like all Giants games, very well optimized.

As it is now, 8/10 in general. If you're a seasoned FS fan, you probably wished for more, and it ends up 7/10 .

What this game is missing at launch, will be completed with mods through the in game mod manager. A great feature introduced in FS17. With that being said, it's surprising that a few things are not included in this version without mods:

-Animated map triggers/operable doors and gates. Especially surprising they took out the operable garage door from Estancia Lapacho. It was in FS17, and gone in FS19???????
-Seasons (I don't want to play that way all the time, but every now and then it's nice. Mods will take care of this)
-Better physics with ground, ie getting stuck/driving through mud. Both available in early mods in FS17
-Better base maps from launch. The USA map is garbage. As an American from the mid-west (AKA farm country) it's
disapointing the base US map was so bland, and kind of trashy looking. Don't get me wrong, alot of the rural farm
county looks like that, but most large farm communities don't. It just felt like it was laid out very poorly as well. And it's all over the place in general, large fields seperated by cliffs, and ocean, and I'm pretty sure I saw palm trees somewhere. I guess it's California? Maybe the Carolina's? I heard the Pacific Northwest, but unless I was really hammered and imagined those palm trees that can't be right. It's just weird non the less. Can't wait for good map mods or the Platinum edition a year from now. Really, both maps feel almost kind of rushed.
-More placeables from start. Yes, there are already mods for buildings. But with such an emphasis buying land and being able to start your farm from scratch, I expected more available buildings to purchase. Again, mods and official DLC will fill the early void.

All in all, it's better than FS17. I preordered, and am happy I did. It's what I expected and more. The few short comings I know will be addressed in mods and official DLC. Solid game once again. The next two years of mods and DLC will make this game great.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe latest from GIANTS Software, Farming Simulator 19 is here!

I will admit, I was skeptical about purchasing this before I did. But, I'm actually glad I did.

There's a lot of improvements both subtle, and eye catching. Standing out the most for me is the dynamic clouds that have been introduced for FS19. It was something I longed for in previous FS games and it's been worth the wait. No more sudden hail storms, allowing you to prepare for incoming weather in a way you see fit.
There's also the improved handling, much improved physics, better lighting, better sound, and in my eyes - well...better everything. It's hard to get this much improvement out of the same engine used in previous series' games, and I say good job! The AI traffic is even (somewhat) better (something that really toyed with my emotions in FS17)!

FS19 so far has been a joy to play, and I have no plans of discontinuing my gameplay.

There are sadly some things that I'm hopeful will be remedied, and or tools for us to overcome. Namely - terraforming. Placing down objects on property you own will generally raise the terrain to meet the highest point on which your object is placed on, which can create some very unsightly placements if you're like me and purchase a small field for extra sheds to store your fleet of vehicles and implements. Removing that object leaves a foundation and raised terrain with lips that are at times impossible to climb, and also prohibits you from placing another object down on that foundation as it is itself an object. What I hope to see out of this - terrain tools to raise/lower terrain at a cost to the player of course (think of it as landscaping).

I have also experienced a few CTD's early on with no error messages which can be frustrating, but auto save is key.

The pros outweigh the cons for this title and it was a well worth it purchase. I look forward to the future of Farming Simulator from GIANTS!
