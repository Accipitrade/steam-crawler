\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've played Farm Sim 2017 or earlier, you already know what you're getting. Each release is an evolution of the previous title, with enough changes to keep things fresh and interesting.

The base game will give you plenty to do, but as your farm grows and you have more fields and crops to deal with, you're going to need mods. Fortunately, mod downloading is built right into the game. New equipment, scripting engines, tools, entirely new maps - it's all there.

I highly recommend Autodrive and Courseplay, the first allowing you to turn your entire farm into a kind of railroad, with named depots you can send any vehicles to, and the second allowing the recording and replaying of vehicle paths and actions. (That's a basic summary. Look up youtube tutorials on each for more.)

In conclusion, this game is chill and relaxing and capable of providing many, many hours of engaging gameplay. I always get the urge to harvest one more field before I log out, and then I might as well sow and fertilise it, and then this other field is almost ready... and so on.
