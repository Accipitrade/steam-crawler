\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: A lot of the bugs have been ironed out since I wrote this so I do recommend this game now. My only complaint now, is the forrestry, but luckily FDR has taken care of that end of it on pc.

The bugs make this game so not enjoyable at all. I was a huge fan of FS17, and this is such a step back.

Good:

- Graphics are better at max settings, but still the shadow and plant draw distances suffer.
- The character animations are cool and he actually bounces, moves, presses pedals and steers.
- Some cool new machinery
- Horses are a nice addition and work pretty well.

Bad:

BUGS. At this stage of the game it is clear they did minimal QA testing of the game. Like there is no way they did much at all. Or just didn't care enough to fix it before launch. Either way it's unacceptable. People that haven't run into bugs yet have probably not done much because i have run into a TON of them. The Fliegl bale trailer attaches itself to whatever was loaded on it even after you unload it. so whatever you unload FOLLOWS IT AROUND AND DESTROYS EVERYTHING IN ITS PATH. Bales, pallets, bags it doesnt matter. The tractors get extremely dirty after you drive 20 feet after washing them. WHY EVEN WASH THEM? The terrain deforms so badly when you place a shelter that you cant even drive your equipment into the shelter sometimes. Both included map designs are AWFUL. A lot of fields dont have enough space due to terrain or objects around them to even turn around equipment when working the fields. there are numerous other problems and this game needs A LOT of work and I don't see a lot of these things getting fixed any time soon or at all. This really sucks because i loved FS17 but this just is NOT FUN in its current rushed, buggy and unacceptable state. Ill come back in a few months and update my review if things have been fixed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will continue to update my review as the game is tweaked and I get more experience with it. Here goes!

I've been playing FS since FS2009 when bale stackers weren't even a thing and you had to remove rocks from your field. So far, my experience with FS19 has been good, but not exactly what I expected. It's a good game, don't get me wrong, but some things just feel a little off compared to FS17 so far. I didn't get that feeling when going from FS11>FS13>FS17, which is why I feel its worth mentioning. The lighting is new and neat, but sometimes it feels a little too glowy and fuzzy compared to the more neutral and sharp lighting of past games.

One thing I tried tonight that i definitely was NOT a fan of was the reworked chainsaws. The sounds are not configured correctly so going from idle to revving will sound very choppy and weird, and the transition as you begin to cut a log and "move" the saw in front of you feels very broken and awkward. This becomes even more of an issue when you are trying to walk along the felled trees and limb them and your saw goes crazy!

Constructable farms: it's a cool concept, but at this stage still feels kinda awkwardin 19. FS17 offered a good amount of this and might have actually offered a larger variety of different placeable sheds and etc, so it's not as much of an improvement as I was hoping it would be.

Maps: These new maps are pretty highly detailed, but a lot of areas just feel overly crammed and small compared to Westbridge Hills or Sosnovka from FS17. I would have liked to have one map that was more like those, or an updated version of them, and then have another map that's more laid out like these new ones, but that's just my preference.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- Graphics and animations have been noticeably improved. Weather, crop flattening, reflections, vehicles bouncing while working a field and other stuff like that.

- Vehicle handing has also been greatly improved.

Cons:
- Placables now create levelled ground where they're placed and add a new texture (asphalt, concrete etc) around the placement. That's all fine and good, but there's no option to place a placable without the texture. Also, the texture remains there after you remove the placable and you can't get rid of it with the roller.

- No ground modification tool in base game, which would be the greatest asset when creating your own farm. It seems like this feature would be easy to implement with the system mentioned above changing ground textures.

- Joystick binding seems to be very buggy at this point in time. Some axes are not registering and in the menus it seems to not be able to decide whether you're using kb/m or stick and flashes between controls. (May just be my joystick however.)

I guess that's it so far. Overall It's an okay start and I'm sure the modding community will take it to the next level.\t\t\t\t\t
