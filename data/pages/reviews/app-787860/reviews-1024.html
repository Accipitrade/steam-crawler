\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as the machinery side of things goes, it's a good game. The graphics have improved but I have seen much more realistic natural textures in other games so a little dissappointing there. As stated in other reviews, the fact that modders can come up with fully animated tractors inside and out and yet giants haven't is a real disappointment. It seems once again they've gone for quantity over quality. I personally would sooner have 1 tractor for small, med and large classes that is fully animated and interactive over many that are not. As far as the helpers go, they have improved slightly in the sense that most of the time when they encounter an obsticle on turn around at the end of a paddock they will back up and find another way around it. I personally haven't had any issues with odd shaped fields as I usually do the first couple of laps myself and then let the helper take over. But that's just me.

One thing I do like about FS19 is the fact that you can actually start off with nothing, although I do personally believe that it should be optional to start with all the fields planted. I'd prefer to start with everything grass and weedy and just start from scratch. As far as what others have complained about with having to start with nothing, Giants need to include all the relevant paperwork as far as field area, implement usage (seed, fert, etc...), hourly fuel usage per vehicle etc....
Fortunately some people have already done that in the forums so I managed to get a field area chart, seed and fert usage chart and a rough yield chart. Being a farmhand myself I just treat it as I would any other farm and do all the math before I start. Work out machinery hire costs, how much seed, fert and chemicals i need for each paddock and then go for gold. Before I started doing this though, I failed miserably every time so I can see why the city folk would complain about not being able to jump in a tractor an make heaps of money straight up.

My biggest complaint about FS19 would have to be the fact that it's called farming simulator. The game is mostly just another driving sim with a few added extras to keep people happy. If it's going to be called farming simulator it should take into account the animal side of things as well. Not just the super basic stuff that has been included.
I can't speak for europe but very few farms in Australia would have a fully automated dairy system where cows just walk in on their own to get milked. Most are either small scale swingovers or large rotary platforms for the bigger farms.
With how customisable this game has been made for modders, why they haven't made animals and economy more accessable is beyond me.

If any modders feel like implementing the following please feel free to :p
Things I would like to see:
More custom dairy options, nothing over the top. Just simple options rather then the usual lely robotic crap.
To milk cows and shear sheep it costs money to hire someone. Should be the same here. eg: notification comes up saying cows need milking or sheep need shearing, press a button to trigger a simple animation of cows and sheep lining up to enter the building and a worker gets paid to do it.

Which leads me to the next item on the list, there should be an option to set a realistic pay rate. I managed to find some code to set my workers to $30/hr but have to insert it into every hirable machine. On a 1:1 time ratio the speed per acre works out damn close so $30/hr works well.

Roads should be hardwired into helpers like the paddock area to allow helpers to pick up a harvester from the store and drive it to paddick 12 for example or go unload at the mill etc...

As stated in other reviews, GPS needs to be implemented. Have it as an added option when purchasing certain machinery.
Bale counters on the balers would also be a nice touch.

The option of animal husbandry. Put a bull in with a cow and it pops out a calf, ram in with a ewe and out pops a lamb etc... Can't remember, but disregard this if it's already in there for FS19.

The option to fence off paddocks (separate fencing options per paddock that cost money) and allow paddock rotation for livestock. Rather then have to hard feed them all the time, have the option for fenced paddocks. 1.5acres of grass per cow per year, not sure on sheep, and placable water troughs with some sort of auto fill plumbing option. have to maintain paddocks as usual, cows actually eat the grass in the paddock and paddock gets fertilised at the same time.
walk up to the cow paddock, option comes up and select move X amount of cows from this paddock to that paddock etc... allow splitable herds. same with all animals.

The option to input realistic weather data and produce prices for a players' local area. Input the players local weather site and it just updates the weekly forecast every time the player is online.

Irrigation options. plants don't grow without water. Here it rains for half the year, paddocks get closed up to grow and then harvested once the weather warms up. If there's little rain through those 6mths then it's a bad harvest and feed needs to be purchased from elsewhere to get through at full production capacity etc...

I haven't looked into it yet but as grain prices go up and down with demand, so should purchase prices. I think they already do but I'm not yet certain.


All in all it's a good game and quite enjoyable when played right however, if you're someone who simply wants to "play farmer" for a couple of hours and make lots of money I'd suggest go back to an earlier version.\t\t\t\t\t
