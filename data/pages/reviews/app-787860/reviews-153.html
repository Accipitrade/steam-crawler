Please read for my explanation of why i left a negative review. If you know about this game already then carry on, i wont waste your time.

Farming Simulator is a game that i have enjoyed for years now. I originally bought FS17 on console in 2017, and later bought FS15 for PC. I eventually got FS17 for PC and now FS19.

I will split my review into categories so that its more digestible

Graphics

I start off with graphics for a reason. It feels like the overall scenery feels cartoony, rather than the more realistic color scheme of FS17. The skyboxes are a big downgrade i feel. The FS17 skyboxes had beautiful sunsets and was overall great, whereas these skyboxes feel nonexistent. The ground textures seem to have gotten worse as well. Overall, it feels that the coloring of FS17 was overall done better than FS19.

Tractors and Machinery

The machines was the major selling point for me. I have liked John Deere since i was a kid. The name carried a reputation of legendary quality and performance. The quality of a real life machine doesn't mean the game it will be featured in will be quality. The sounds of the tractors feel like it should belong on the model of a Toyota Prius rather than a farm machine made to cultivate miles of field. The interiors do feel somewhat more realistic than FS17 as they have moving joysticks and pedals.

Maps

The Ravenport Map is utter garbage. Goldcrest Valley and predecessors of it are 10x better than this map. I feel that Ravenport exists to make a mockery of America. Americans when they make European Maps would never to the deepest slums of the former Yugoslavian countries to model after. It would be more likely they would model it after farms in France, the UK, or Germany. My point being you generally want to show the better part of a country than showing the absolute worst part of a country. (I do acknowledge that Europe is a continent and not an absolutely unified collective of states like the US)

Felsbrunn still suffered from the same crappy graphics as mentioned in an above section, but it feels like a more idealistic view of a German farm. The fields are bigger than what you might assume an average German field would be and overall it feels like a better map than what us Americas were stuck with.

Modding

Modding is the only saving grace for this game. There has been many great mods, although there has been more buggy mods slipping through the grand firewall of giants software mod hub. Despite this, there was many great mods that added more depth to this game, if it were not for the modding i would've spent way fewer hours in this game.

Continued Rant About Sounds And Overall Vibe Of The Game

The vibe that the tractors give me and the game overall is high efficiency. Tractors as i mentioned before are not a Chevy Volt, they are a machine made to do a job. I expect the sounds and graphics to match this. Sadly, expectations are different than reality. This issue just really annoys me comparing the sounds ingame to the sounds of tractors in real life.

Final Thoughts

FS19 to be brutally honest feels like FS17, except somehow they made it 10x worse with John Deere and Claas machines. This game has made me hesitant to buy FS22 and i still am hesitant to buy it. I simply want a farming game that is worth playing. I simply do not want a worse repaint of the game i had before.

Having bought train simulators before, it feels like Giants Software is going down the process DTG has gone down. Pushing out buggy unfinished games. Its sad to see and i still hope Giants doesn't continue to go down this path.

I know there are white-knights who will defend Giants at every turn and corner, so i have a message to you. You don't have to put up with crap quality to like the game. I love Farming Sim, i just do not like Giants pushing low quality crap.

There is new features, but no amount of bells and whistles will cover up the bad with this game.

TL:DR

This game seems like a crappier clone of FS17 with bells and whistles. Get FS17 if your like me. If you must get it, get it on sale. Ok bye.


I decided to get FS22 on another platform. I will do a full review when i get it for PC. The long story short is, it feels like FS22 has improved from the shortfalls i once criticized this game for. FS22 gives me hope for the future of the game unlike FS19 did.

FS19 still leaves a bad taste in my mouth, but if you feel froggy go check out FS22.
