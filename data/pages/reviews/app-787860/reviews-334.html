\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate #2: 250+ hours. Got meself a steering wheel, joystick and the Saitek panel. Found this amazing 4x map called "Ricci's Hollow" with close to 200 products, mining, multifruit, and just damn addictive. Always something to do. When I finish farming there's animals, when I'm done with animals I gotta sort all the production facilities, then mining, then forestry, check what to buy next, what factory to work on, oh crap time to farm again. And repeat.

------------------------------------------------------------

Update: May I point out that it's been like 6 days since this review and I've already passed 100 hours. I still don't quite grasp why it's addictive, it just is.

-------------------------------------------------------------

I've always had a fondness for farming, but there are truly very few simulator games that hook me. The game is very repetitive, but there's a magic in it, something that keeps calling me back.

Then came the mods. I'm only 25 hours in as of this review, but I already got a custom map (PV County) which comes with 130 or so mods, 16x multifruit and factory map where you have to manually build and supply each factory. It's not anywhere near as beautiful as other maps I've seen, but the gameplay for me trumps graphics every single time. 100+ products, chains of productions, mining, making your own fuel, just endless.

Now I just get on the game and start thinking what to do out of 100s of options. Got some deco mods just to spice things up. That's what's so beautiful about heavy moddable games.

In short, if the theme of the game attracts you, go for it. It's definitely the type of game you either love or hate from the beginning. It has lots to offer and a world beyond with mods.\t\t\t\t\t
