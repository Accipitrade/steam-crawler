\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, if you're look for 4K Ultra HD, go out side and be a real farmer. Second, if you not happy about the fact you have to work to earn money. Then while you slurping your drink, and spilling that sandwich down the front of yourself. Remember, some farmer, done the repeatitive and grinding tasks that people have complained about. Just so you can complain about having to do what the poor farmer did. While eating that sandwich.

Now, lets get down to the good stuff.

Graphics - Marked improvement over previous versions. Again, not 4K Ultra HD, so don't be expecting the heavens to open up and the angles to sing when you power up you GTX what ever Ti.

Game Play - Again, better then previous versions. Ok, the dusting you fields with lime every now and then (can be turned off in the in game menu), takes a bit to get use to. But, and I say this is a good way. When you fill your seeders/planter/fertilizers. You'll get the option to unload. When you choose that, make sure you have room. As it will dump it back in bags/pallets on the ground. Even if you fill up from a lime station. I think that is pretty awesome. You don't have to shovel the stuff back in. Crops, grass , bushes will respond to the player, tractor, harvester, tools etc.. when driving over them. No more do they stay stiff and go through the tractor etc... Adds to the fun. Um, yeah there are also nice little quirks in the cabs of the vehicals. The pedals and/or stick will respond to your back and forth commands. Again realy cool little features. And riding the horses around is fun, and required to help keep their value up. If you looking for riding like Red Dead Redemption 2, then you're going to be disapointed. But that being said, it's still cool. And the detail on the horses is fantastic. The building, unless my IQ is in the top 2%, which I am sure my school teachers will disagree with. It's fine, and is better then previous versions. Instead of just be stuck in the on screen postion and only rotating the object you are placing. You can now rotate the screen as well as object (Q E and left and right arrow). You now also have the option to raise and lower the object to help it level with the ground. I am still at a loss as to why some people have had trouble. Chickens, Horses, Cows and pigs. You will need to purchase the actual Pen/Stable etc... And then fill it with animals. Also, you dont buy fields anymore. You by sections of land, which will have a field or two. Or empty space with trees. So for all the people that complained about the animals being stuck on map, Giants now lets you choose where you want to place them. When you purchase them of course.

There is a lot I could go on about. No, I didn't get the game at discount nor for free. I paid full price (AU). And after about 15 minutes was really happy I did.

If you are sitting on the fence, and really enjoyed the previous versions of the game. Then, grab this, you will not be dispointed. If your looking for high stakes action, with 4K Battle Royal Mode, then keep scrolling. And don't complain.

Just remember, (1) It's not going to go at 144Hz at 4K Ultra HD with GTX Ti's. I have been playing at 1080p 60Hz with no worries what so ever. (2) Yes, its a farming game. Yes, it's fun if you're into farming. (3) NO! That's right, NO! Battle Royal Mode, it's a farming simulator, you know, where your food starts out from. (4) If you don't like working in real life, then I'm guessing you will not want to work in a simulator. (5) If you have enjoyed the previous versions then this great.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWrote a review earlier, so this is a correction.

Giants patched the game today, so; I can actually get into the game once again; at least that game breaking bug was fixed. but with no patch notes avaliable, I don't know what else was fixed.

as far as play-ability, for the most part, its a decent (not great) game. there are still a few bugs and glitches, like sometimes not being able to connect to trailers, or combines/harvesters/bailers still registering a seed type in them even after emptying them, making it difficult to fold the units for transport on the maps.

I do appreciate the more realistic, tight and uneven farms that the maps have, adding in the complexity of having to determine the proper equipment to be used on the field in question.

I do not like the fact that there is no equipment trailers for small equipment like skid-steers, or forklifts that the pickup truck is able to pull; hopefully they fix this before the next edition of FS comes out. driving a forklift/skidsteer across the map at 8-11mph early game when you can't afford the big lowboy trailer and the truck to haul it is a pain. also the low-loader trailer (lowboy) doesn't have a way to strap down the load from what I can tell, it may be there, but it doesn't appear in the tool tips, or appear when next to the trailer; so large equipment tends to shift when using it to haul. there also isn't any crates or pallets to store the chicken eggs in in the default game, there are some player mods that do this, but I guess Giants isn't looking at what the mod community is doing, as including their own variations of the most popular mods would greatly improve the quality of their product.

my previous review stated that I wouldn't recommend this game; however I am clicking Yes, this time simply because its a break away from the normal games I play, and it has actually been fairly relaxing... yes, a bit stressful when dealing with rain, and you need to harvest (can't harvest in the rain/hail) but, growing up on a dairy farm, that's actually fairly accuate; most equipment have difficulty operating in those weather conditions, so most farmers wouldn't even attempt it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Farming Simulator Series is an ever-evolving franchise as well as being continuously refined. Each release brings forth new graphics enhancements, new features, new game mechanics, but overall it's the same game. Each year along with all the new points comes the questions of "why wasn't this added yet" and "did they release this seemingly unfinished" product.

Not unique to the FS series, a lot of games appear to be released falling short of their vision and ambitions probably due to time and budget constraints. Sometimes it's painfully obvious. Other times, it appears as though they rely on the modding community to make up for their shortfalls. It may even be strategic. They may be just focusing on what is needed to get the game out and leaving the rest to the modders.

To properly set the tone, I like FS19 and I expect to put in a many hours as I possibly can, having two jobs and a wife.

So the criticism here isn't simply to bash Giants or this series. It's simply to help push this already great game to an even greater game, where it should be.

Let's start off with addressing the multiple bug issues. This is quality control and quite often the best beta test is the initial release. So, despite all the bugs, some having affected me, they WILL be addressed and therefore, I don't want to talk about them. I want to focus on design and direction.

MAPS:
For the first time, Giants has produced some high-quality maps with textures, details, and an ambiance that really immerse me. The layout appears to be good, but I'll only know for certain once I'm several hours into the map. It's even been said, these new maps are almost GTA quality. That's saying a lot. I have noticed a severe absence of trees, but that's likely to prevent overwhelming weaker machines. For me, I have to customize my map, adding more forest and trees before I'll start. It really enhances the immersion for me.

EQUIPMENT:
Of course the tractors and such look even better than before. Every release, they'll improve the detail and textures as the player's hardware also improves. Are many different types of new equipment but very little of it dramatically changes the players farming strategy.

FEATURES:
New crops, better chicken management, horses are added. Nothing exciting, but they're very much welcomed.

BUILD YOUR OWN FARM:
FINALLY... I've always wanted to start with nothing. No ability to get a loan. Oonly has a chainsaw and a pickup truck. Then go dominate the map. Still can't quite do that but close enough. For now. The different game modes and improved contract work make this far more enjoyable.

PATCH 1.2
Landscaping! About time! This is a feature I will really enjoy. How could they have implemented it better? By attaching the landscaping code/script to the machines. I want to level ground with a machine not a mouse cursor. That would be the ultimate feature to this game.

BEEFS:
Ok. Maybe it's just me, but I guess I compare things to a vision I have. There are things to me that seem obvious and how and why they've been neglected will just give me ulcers if I spend too much time thinking about it.

- Decorative placeable objects: I don't mean the "trees and shrubbery" and such.I mean the buildings and "garages" and sheds that are purely decorative. Why are these decorative and not functional? There are barns, houses, shacks and sheds... and you want me to buy these for what? In fact, one of the barns is completely finished on the inside, with amazing textures, but can't you open the doors to get in and use it. I would really LOVE to see these "decorative" objects be patched with completed and functional versions. Also... why one house?

- Bushes/Shrubs: Sure the landscaping tool can remove them, but why can't I mow them? This is something that should have been added in FS15. Mushrooms... mow them. Or pick them.

- Certain objects: I love the junked car carcasses and other junk lying around. What I would love even more is being able to pick them up and move them or have them taken to the dump. I like the idea cleaning up my yard.

- Logs to Lumber: The modders have done it. Why hasn't this been implemented into the core game yet? So many things can be done with lumber.

I'll add more later.

This is the best release yet and I will be putting hours into it once a few mods kick in and I find a good map.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA lot of things need to be fixed. It also doesnt seem like that much changed from FS17. I like the three different "difficulty" settings they have this time. It adds a bit of a better way to get started farming and the prices for equipment seems a bit better than FS17. But outside of a few new crops and pieces of equipment (mostly for the new crops) there really isnt much here. The mod community will once again be relied upon for added depth to the game and for adding much needed pieces of equipment and placeables that there really isnt ANY excuse why Giants couldnt have figured out were needed and inserted into the game prior to release. The game looks a bit better and has a few more pieces of equipment but my honest opinion is wait till its on sale for no more than 20 bucks and hope they have fixed the bugs and the modders have come out with content because it doesnt look like Giants is going to do it. Oh, and if your relying on contracts to fill in the void and give you good money for real time spent, think again! Unlike on FS17 when you took an hour of real time to harvest a big field of potatoes or corn and you got 20k+ for it, now your lucky if you get 6-8k for it AND you have to trailer it, and sell it for the field owner!? So you have to spend almost TWICE the amount of real time to get less than half the money. Contracting is a complete waste of time! I like that they added the ability to use your own equipment to get a bit more money, but I have yet to do that, as the price of leasing the (usually better) equipment to get the job done, in no way makes it worth me using my own fuel/maintenace/usually smaller equipment. So you get to use leased equpiment (that you have to drag from the store to the field...) which is nice to see new stuff, but you get docked money for it and you already get next to nothing for the work. So yeah, contracting sucks!\t\t\t\t\t
