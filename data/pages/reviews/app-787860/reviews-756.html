\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was unexpectedly surprised with this game, in a good way. I usually play survival games and was looking for a relaxing alternative. Most games I've played with a farming component the farming was one of the more boring parts of the game. After watching a couple videos (and getting a good sale price) I decided to try this. The farming in other games I've played was just part of the game and dumbed down to avoid being too time consuming. In this game the farming is the whole point and the developer tries to make it as realistic as possible within programming and hardware restrictions.

I would strongly urge new players to do the tutorials. They don't cover everything but provide the major controls for the game and there are a lot of things to control. You can drive everything from pickup trucks and small tractors to huge harvesters and large trucks.

This isn't just a farming game, it is also a management game. Hire workers or not? What equipment to buy? What crops to grow? Want animals? Which ones? What do they need to be profitable? Where to sell crops/animal products for the best price? Expand your farm or wait for more cash flow?

IT's not perfect. There are some quirks to watch for but nothing game breaking so far at least. I installed the game did the tutorial and started the simplest game mode to get a feel for how things work in the game. Eight hours later I decided I better get some sleep.

I'm not a big fan of mods in most games. I see it as just a way to cheat to make the game easier. With this game modes are almost a must but most involve different brands or types of equipment, buildings, and other maps to use. Nothing (at least not much) that would make the game easier or remove all the challenge of playing.

There is a bit of a learning curve but if you like farming games or management games you may want to try this one. I was pleasantly surprised.\t\t\t\t\t
