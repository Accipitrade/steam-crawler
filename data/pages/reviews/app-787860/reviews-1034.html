\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first Farming sim that I have played and it will be the last one. Unfortunately I have 6 hours in the game so I was denied a refund. But know that I did try to get one. It alone takes about 45 minutes just to complete the tutorial, which barely shows you any of the mechanics of the game. Then you must spend an hour or two trying to figure out how to even start your farm. It was built to get you past the refund threshold before you figure out the game is garbage. You are left guessing when you start, how do I purchase land/equipment? How do I manage animals? What is the purpose of buildings?

On top of all the remaining questions you have, farms are buggy and won't allow complete planting of crops. I don't know if was due to terrain changes or what, but my farm was very patchy. Corn would only plant on 50% of each row. It would sow for 10 feet then it magically wouldn't for 10 feet, then it would start sowing again and then stop again. Very frustrating trying to research online why this would happen when it turns out its just buggy.

The map is missing quite a few labels, So if you start a contract requires you to do something, which was also not explained anywhere, you are left guessing where to go and what to do. Not Recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPro (Enhancements over 2017):
-Cotton
-Oats
-Dog
-Horses
-Field birds
-Landscape editor
-Slightly better graphics
-Buy land instead of fields
-More realistic steering (overall better. Steering for the wheel loader is superior, but steering for conveyor is bad)

Con:
-Slightly more buggy
-No longer have in game vehicle sales events
-The landscape editor breaks game immersion
-Chainsaw does not work as well as 2017 for cutting branches
-No reputation system. In 2017, if you did tasks for a farmer, then you could get a better price for their land.
-Train is less useful. Train automatically runs around the tracks, thus have to constantly move it back to where it is needed. You must be in the train for loading and unloading -- if you exit, then the operation will stop.
-Still uses magic seeds instead of crop specific seeds.
-Giants editor is more complicated -- may discourage mod creation.

Overall better than Farming Simulator 2017, but you are loosing some 2017 features (vehicle sales, a reputation system, better trains).\t\t\t\t\t
