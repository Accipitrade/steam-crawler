\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs big of a fan as I've become of the Farming Simulator series, I have to say I'm somewhat disappointed with this latest iteration. Giants has made some significant inprovements in the appearance of the game, which I have to say are, at times spectacular (new clouds!).

However, I find it interesting that Giants has, for several installments, ignored the community's most requested features, and added less inspired features that, for me, largely fall flat. It is my opinion that they have become too dependent on the game's extremetly dedicated modders to add what their customers want most.

For years, the likes of GPS, Follow Me, Seasons, CoursePlay, and mouse steering (God, how I miss mouse steering on my laptop) have been left to the modders to supply to the community. If this is the intent, wrong though I believe it to be, then get the tools to the modders prior to release so customers don't have to wait for features that they became reliant upon in the previous release.

The new features that are included are mostly misses for me:
- Horses - Don't really care. They're well-animated, but I'd rather tool around in a pickup. Training is a time sink.
- Dog - A neat touch, but the effort would have been better spend making GPS or mouse steering a standard feature.
- Purchasing "lots" - Now instead a buying a field, you buy a field and a little bit of surrounding area. Not sure what the point was.
- The new difficulty modes - Many problems with this, in my opinion. Everything is overpriced. Placing already expensive structures becomes ridiculous if there's even minor leveleing required. Most of the new lots don't easily allow for setting up a farmhouse/structures, so you're still likely to be in the default starting location. I will say that the recently added (expensive) terraforming tools will help with this. I still think most are going to cheat their funds to get past many of these issues.

I hope that Giants will take more of thier customers' requests to heart (as they did bringing John Deere to the game) for long-awaited standard features. The practice of charging dedicated customers for three quarters of a game and letting modders spend their time finishing it at their expense is exploitative and kind of sleezy.

The core game is still strong and will, of course, only be made better by the modding community. The number of manufacturers and volume of equipment are impressive and model quality is extremely detailed (animated PTO's, etc.). I still, with some reservations, would recommend the game, but maybe wait six months and get it at a discount.
