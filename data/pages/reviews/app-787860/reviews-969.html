\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFarming Simulator 19 is a great game if you enjoy the feeling of accomplishing something while you're doing something else.

When I started playing FS19, I had no idea what I was doing. The tutorials are great, they show you what to do to have a successful harvest, how to do it and in what order, but that's about it. Then it throws hundreds of machines at you and says, "Have fun, make money". It took me a bit to get used to what does what, which is better than what, and so on, but while the learning curve could be a bit high for someone who's only used farming equipment for tobacco on a 1-acre farm (ME!), it was a lot of fun.

I enjoyed the visuals. Graphics are very nice, though sometimes I notice blips here and there that take me away from immersion, especially when I'm plowing a field. Not a big deal though. Every machine looks great. As you use them, they get dirtier and dirtier, and I really enjoyed that. At home, we never cleaned the machines. We just had a mentality that they were gonna get dirty, so why bother cleaning mud off? So you have the option of using a pressure washer to clean your machines if you prefer them to look shiny, but I never bothered. I really enjoyed the look of a dirty machine after plowing or seeding a field. It made it look like I actually did something.

The sounds are great, too! When you get in a tractor and start going, you feel like you're in a machine with lots of HP! You can turn your machine sound down if you prefer, along with a slew of other sound options (nice touch), which I definitely liked. I didn't care for the radio much. I would usually turn on Spotify while I was farming, so didn't bother much with their in-game radio.

The game itself can get a little rote. Some fields, especially modded ones, can be huge, and they can take literal hours to plow, seed, fertilize, etc. Its here that my beginning statement shines. I love audiobooks. I find myself with a lot of time at home were I'm conflicted on what to spend my time on. Reading? Gaming? Why not both? I'll start an audiobook, turn down the sound on FS19, and then get to work. Hours later, I have accomplished two things at once. And who says only women multitask?

Farming Simulator is a great game with a huge number of machines at your disposal. Does it get every aspect of farming right? Probably not. So I'm not sure how accurate the "simulation" part of the game is. But is it fun, and can you see the fruits of your labor rather quickly? Yes, and yes.

10/10, would play again.\t\t\t\t\t
