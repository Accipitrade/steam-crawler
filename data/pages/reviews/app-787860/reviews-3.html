\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a huge fan of Farming Simulator. This review is more geared towards someone who has played the franchise before, but I think newbs can take something out of it, too.

Here is what I think of FS19:

CONS
1. Graphics may have gotten worse from FS17
2. They seemed to focus on improving LITTLE elements of the game that either were bugs/glitches in previous versions or not there at all.. ie, birds landing on the fields and flying off when you get close to them - a cool little addition that is pretty well-done; nighttime is nighttime - not like FS17 where it starts getting dark at 10p and light at 4a; glitches with oilseed radish seem to have been resolved but I've noticed a lot of other ones new to FS19 (when you hire a worker and the last bit of field is like an 1/8 of their implement's width and is next to another field it will go into that field - plowing, cultivating, seeding, harvesting, weeding, fertilizing/lime). It is just surprising that these bugs continue to exist and they put time into little stupid things instead of what matters.
3. The maps aren't good. Not terrible, but not good. Goldcrest had it's flaws but that is the best one IMO. Yes, it is too simple, but it has a little bit of everything and you can tell it's the map they spent the most time on. Sosnovka is a joke and Estancia Lapancha seems unfinished - both of these have comparable layouts but their geography is so different that they don't feel that similar. But Ravensport and Wellsbrun do. Yes, Ravensport is more of a working-class small city and Wellsbrun is a upper-middle-class countryside town. But they both have windy, confining roads; oddly shaped, super-hilly fields (a fully loaded 50,000+ tipper needs at least 500hp to get up some of the hills) and annoying guardrails that can really give you a headache (having to reset a fully-loaded overturned seeder because its wheel caught the guardrail and is wedged against a mountain so you can't ram it). Sosnovka was terrible, but it at least was a polar opposite from Goldcrest Valley that made it a little refreshing. These two are just too similar. Giants has a map mod for Estancia Lapancha that is formatted for FS19 that I haven't checked out yet. Mainly because I know what to expect and I wasn't a fan of it in FS17.
4. The landscape function is unusable if you ask me. Maybe if you really invested in mastering it but it is so hit-or-miss - and EXPENSIVE - that it isn't fun and not worth doing.
5. One thing that may sound petty but irks me big time: the opening scene shows this big, beautiful barn that he pulls his vehicles out of.. I was so excited after watching the intro I went right to the store and the placeable menu and there was no barn!
6. Aside from no big beautiful red barn like every farm in world has yet this SIMULATOR cannot provide (can you tell I'm bitter?), the placeables haven't improved. I wasn't a fan of the ones in FS17, but those were better. I'm fine with no greenhouses, solar panels, windmills, beehouses that earn you money, but they're still cool to have on your farm! At least replace them with something. You get 3 sheds, powerwasher, woodchipper, vehicle configurator station, silos and decorative buildings. That is pretty much it.
7. Mods are still a problem - I don't use them unless they are Giants. Too many bad experiences with them to use on a game like this. When you invest so much time designing your farm and the system running it, the last thing you want is something out of your control messing it up. As cool and useful as some of them are, they're not worth it in the long run.
8. I like the menu setup of 2017 better. I'm sure I will get used to this one more and more but they changed it up a little too much for my liking. Also, the in-game on-screen info that is always displayed is harder to read. I don't know why they'd change it from 17 there was nothing wrong with it and was easier to read.

PROS
1. I really like the way they made it possible to buy land. In FS17, as long as it wasn't a field you didn't own, you could cut down trees and plow a field anywhere. Now, you have to own the land to do that and I think this may be the biggest improvement from 17 to 19. There are several plots you can buy that don't even have fields yet. I love that you can buy a plot of land and spend hours making it just the way you want before doing any farming whatsoever. Very cool.
2. The store setup is much better for customization. You can even match the colors up because they tell you which ones are for what manufacturers.
3. There are more vehicles, trailers & implements than FS17 even though a lot are gone. You can get most of those as mods by Giants, though.
4. Everything drives a little more realistic. In 17, working a field was completely smooth, whether you're in a tractor, harvester, truck or anything. Now they appropriately bump around - probably seems banal but it's a nice touch.
5. In FS17, the only way to transport pallets was the low loaders or bale trailers. In 19 they have a trailer that is perfect for this, it can fit 3-4 pallets and can be towed by the smallest tractor. Again, a pretty simple thing but a gamechanger for me which is why I'm mentioning it. I hated that aspect of 17.
6. It loads quicker than 17 and I have fewer issues in-game and starting it up. In fact, no issues so far in-game as far as freezing up. Also, if I had a ton of vehicles in the same area in FS17 it would often lag there. None of that with this one.
Yet. As far as startup, FS17 would give me a problem probably once every 6 times. I haven't played FS19 nearly as much, but I've only had one startup issue so far. Quitting a game is faster, it brings you RIGHT back to main menu, older versions would take much longer and even sometimes bring me back out to my desktop.
7. Traffic AI is better. Still isn't good, but it's at least better. More variety of vehicles, too, not just passenger cars/trucks.
8. Like I said, the graphics may be worse than 17. But it may seem like that because the scenery is really cool and elaborate. Cascading waterfalls, some really well done bridges, downtown areas are more realistic.. they improved on that but didn't up the graphics any. But it's still a noticeable improvement.

There are many other PROs and CONs but these are the ones I felt would be the most useful to discuss. Hope this was helpful, certainly took me a little bit of time that I could've been using to play so YOU'RE WELCOME! :D
