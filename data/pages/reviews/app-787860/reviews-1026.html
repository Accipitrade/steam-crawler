\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, let me clarify the Steam 1.2 hours on record. I play in offline mode so obviously they do not pull time from game files. Also this does not include the numerous hours in Giants Editor and scripting as I would not review FS without looking researching game as well as tools.

I have played FS for many years and am welll aware that Giants has poor Quality Assurance in their software releases. FS19 has exceeded even my expectations for their lack of proper testing. The list of bugs is quite extensive, the lack of logic in gameplay programming and basic decision making (i.e. changes to tractor sounds) show the product was rushed to market with little testing.

Tractor sounds are unrealistic and mimic that of a 2 cylinder 4 cycle. The change from FS17 sounds to FS19 is dramatic and quite unimpressive.

AI workers have never been a strong point in FS, however, after years in the development cycle it would seem they could get this point right. Now they have implemented illogical gameplay design of trying to have traffic stop as tractor, harvester, etc approaches. The traffic however will stop in path of equipment causing an abrupt course change. Again, poor testing combined this time with illiogical design.

The implementation of landscaping was tossed into game in recent update again with not much design thought and testing. More of an appeasement to the console crowd to allow for their ill-designed placeables system. A subset of GE tools were thrown in-game with no proper instruction to those unfamiliar with GE to allow for very simplistic terraforming.
The biggest problem is no testing or design for the back end of this system such as how to defeat obstacles such as you can't perform that function in this location.

The grand new additions to FS19, dogs and horses are about as simplistic as a mid-level MS-DOS game. The dog does nothing. You can pet or throw a ball, the latter of which disappears into ground and dog remains fixed. No testing. Horses can be ridden when they stop glitching and sliding around long enough so you can press key to get on them. Oh what an experience it is to ride these gems. Not. If you implement new gameplay then at least test and make these experiences your focus.

Oh and cleaning your horses, you press a button and hear a "hairspray" sound. No curry comb, no brush, no apply blanket, no wash just a wonderful whoosh. Cleaning over. The total experience. Awesome.

Giants Editor is still in Beta 8.1 as 8.0 was beyond usable. The 8.1 version is workable but any great new features.....nope.

I understand this review may sound harse, but the fact remains that Giants chose a money grab over quality. I enjoy FS gameplay as well as modding for FS however if something is substandard I would be as critical of myself as this product.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game, can't wait till it's out of Beta. At least, that's what it feels like. Unfinished and unpolished, requiring a second screen so the player can navigate bugs and glitches.
There is a lot of great things to celebrate in FS19, The machines exhibit an attention to detail that is very pleasing. The ability to have your favourite internet radio feeds or mp3s play through the vehicle radios, and the well crafted maps are just a few. However, this is interrupted jarringly by pallets getting stuck on forks, horses that do a little bunny hop before the jump animation, Horses that climb sheer cliff faces (horses in general really).
The economy feels haphazard and random (tautology?). Graphing the changes in the prices of comodities in 12 hour increments heralded no discernable pattern. This feels like a missed opportunity as there is no real gauge on what to plant and when based on the market values, which is especially true because there are no seasons. That's right, no seasons. I was especially suprised to find this glaring omission from something with the word simulator in it's title. True, I'm not a real farmer, but arn't seasons a major part of farming? Here, plant what you want when you want with no consideration to anything. I often find myself inventing reasons to plant one crop instead of another. Once, I accidently planted oat instead of Barley and it is of absolutely no consequence.
There are three main flaws with this title;
The bugs and glitches. Some of these have been fixed and hopefully this process will continue.
Missed opportunities. There are many times in this game when I was left shocked and thinking "why is that not in this game".
Nothing to react to. Except weeds. Vehicles don't break down, animals never need a vet. Everything is linear and predictable. So much so that I was excited when I found out that a horse's final day of training gives less value gain than any other days (oddly, this is due to the linear nature of value gain) Yay, something I can optimise by not following the script.

All that being said, FS19 is a nice experience. The progression is slow so each new purchase feels meaningful. The machinery is detailed and wonderful. The new landscaping feature allows for some beautiful farms to be created. Harvesting a big field listening to music or an audio book is relaxing and satisfying. The biggest upside of all is the modding community, those wonderful people working hard to fill in the missing bits, complimented by the ease of adding mods to the game.

The game has many hiccups, things that are weirdly missed, and has a 'do stuff and you'll progress' feel. But it boldly strides beyond this and presents as a really good experience. Its strengths outweigh its anchors and, as the developers and modding community work to make it more than what it is now, it can only get better.\t\t\t\t\t

As always, the modding scene will really hold this game up for the next two years. If you're looking to go from FS17 to FS19, you might want to wait while courseplay and follow me and the GPS mod are updated.

Overall, great game.\t\t\t\t\t
