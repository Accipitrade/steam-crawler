\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoy this game as a way to wind down, it is low stress (once you learn what to do)

I must warn you that this is heavily mod reliant, you will have much more fun when you load the game with user content.

The downside to this game is much like many other simulators, they have very little driving you.
Once the initial startup is done there is no challenge, nothing is going to come ruin your crops so that the bank loan causes parts of your farm to be repo-ed there are 3 maps that the game provides you with, and each has a few points that you might sell crops at. However the games economic difficulty never truly challenges you. if 1 location sells too cheap the other is normally fair. at the hardest setting the prices leave a small margin for profit but i never find myself selling anything at a loss. Certain crops are always more profitable than others the market has no real demand built in to stop you from over producing the same crop over and over. Do not get too excited for horses they have many problems. first being that you buy full grown horses at 5k$ then raise them by caring for them (very simple) and riding them 5 minutes, doesnt matter how fast or where or if you jump or not, nothing you do matters just 5 minutes on them. then after about 8 days of care they sell for 50k$ 10Xs the money with minor costs and a painful time sink.

repair systems are also lacking go ahead and repair stuff from time to time but its just clicking a button there is no tires flat or blowing an engine simply your repair bar is low pay money.

I still recommend it for a bit of fun if you have some friends willing to try the multiplayer can be enjoyable share a farm or split into teams trying to buy up more of the map race to 100 million dollars or something of the sort.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm new to Farming Simulator, so keep that in mind.

I picked this game up on a whim, and was quite quickly taken by how much I enjoyed it. I've played farming games before, but not *simulators* - usually I'm playing something like Stardew Valley.

Watch a few LPs, and if it looks like something you would enjoy, give it a try.

The modding community is hard at work putting out new content for this game - noticeably faster than the developers, in fact - and they've been bringing back lots of content that was in previous titles as well (including maps). If you want the maximum amount of mod content, play Farming Simulator 15 or 17 - but the graphics update for this game is pretty noticeable to my eyes, so if you'd like it to be prettier to play, get this one and just be patient with modders.\t\t\t\t\t
