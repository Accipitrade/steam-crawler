\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe jury's still out on this one.

It's Farming Simulator sure enough, but it feels rinse-repeated (no change there) and has launched with a lot of technical issues that are rapidly making Giants look like they aren't even testing their new releases before dumping them on Steam.

First off, this is NOT a simulator. It's a game trying (poorly) to simulate. There's an important difference!

It looks nicer, with a new shader running on their ageing game engine, but still falls short of the mark when it comes to graphical technical know-how. It has all the hallmarks of a Giant's designed game. Back in the early days there was a rumor that the dev's were originally engineers that decided to make a game. It shows. Every item is technically oozing with detail, from implements unfolding, to fine details in the vehicle models. But the overall graphical artistic talent is lacking, leaving the landscapes feeling dated and lackluster.

Their use of modern rendering techniques feels messy too, with some details looking excellent, while other distance rendering techiques look like a game from the late 90's. When combined, it delivers a mish-mash that doesn't quite come together properly. For example: Blurry distance textures on boundary areas (to give the impression of Depth of Field), with regular in-game trees that are clearly in sharp focus, with no shadows. It looks cut'n'pasted and honestly, very ugly! Why can't they just use Bokeh like most other games that attempt similar visuals? Only Giants know the answer to that one.

I can't help but think Giants need to employ some proper graphic artists and developers to bring this game into the modern day, before it becomes a laughing stock.

Having played this game for many hours with my wife on a dedicated server, I can confidently report that some of the equipment needed doesn't work as you'd expect either. A simple example is a long trailer that is designed to collapse down to cater for vehicles and implements, so the player can transport several items over long distances. This sounds excellent in theory, but in practice it's a disaster waiting to happen. Often the trailer bottoms-out on invisible bumps in the road along your journey and catapults anything it was carrying into the direction it was travelling. Even inspecting the road that it's stuck on reveals nothing restricting it, leading to the thought that it's a collision box on the terrain that's been left unoptimized. This is a classic example of Giants not even testing the only trailer of this kind available (at the moment), on the roads they themselves designed. Simply raising the trailer height would solve the problem.Very shoddy!

Another example of broken implements are the KUHN items that have been added from what I assume was FS17's DLC pack. It's nice that they added them, but again they are more problematic than anything. The 4 part seed box, plow, harrow, seeder set that all connects together, only adds to complication when using an AI driver to work the field. Sometimes it's hard to even change the seed setup, but the most common problem is the AI randomly changing direction at the end of the field if you've had to refill it part way.

Terrible AI programming! Honestly, if this is the kind of AI our future holds, there's no way Elon Musk need worry about AI taking over the world, because it would probably try to download itself into a kettle and boil itself..

Finally, the physics. There's no change from all previous farming simulator releases, no matter what the fan-boys try to tell you. I picked up a pallet of seed, only to have it glue itself to the tip of the front loader forks, refusing to budge. When I decided to try and lift it, the pallet raised slightly, then catapulted about 20 feet into the air, as my wife and I laughed, awkwardly, trying to hold back our frustration at the stupidity of the physics system.

It's a game with a truly love/hate connection. Which is a shame, because for me at least, it used to be a labor of love to work a farm. Now it's becoming tedious as I work around all the old gripes, 10 years on.

I'd usually recommend this franchise, but not this time, as I feel Giants need to take notice of the unhappy customer base.\t\t\t\t\t
