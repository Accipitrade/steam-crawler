\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was on my wishlist since release, and I bought this during the Summer Sale. Let's just say, i'm glad I didn't pay full price.

I have over 150 hours clocked up in FS17, which was a game I found to be highly addictive. So I thought I was on to a winner when I bought FS19.

Unfortunately not. Don't get me wrong. This is just as addictive as it's predecessor, but..... that's about it. I Only started playing a couple of days ago, and having clocked 10+ hours now, I still haven't really noticed any improvements or advantages over FS17. One of the marketing claims was a massive graphical improvement. Well... where is it? All i've noticed is a horrible and somewhat exaggerated orange peel effect on vehicle bodywork. I haven't seen anything else which has been graphically improved.

The main things that annoyed me in FS17 are still present in FS19:

- Helpers still cannot identify a separation between fields. So if you set up one worker to plow a field, keep an eye on them, otherwise you could be losing an entire neighbouring field of crops as well!
- Helpers still cannot finish a task if the field they are working in is not square/rectangular. I've already had messages popping up saying "Helper X has completed their task" when in fact half of the field has been missed, because it curves one way or the other.

The beacons all appear to have broken lenses. The silver colour of the rotating reflector is always visible, as if a piece of the lens is missing.

The controls are a bit messed up compared with FS17. I have assigned all the same controls between the two games. But in FS19, when I press the button assigned to Rear Work Lights, I get the Pause Menu. On foot, if I walk forward, the chainsaw appears. I ended up having to sell it to solve that problem.

There are various little issues like this which you really wouldn't expect to find in a game which claims to have been massively overhauled and refined since the previous version.


Maybe I just haven't put enough time into the game yet, but the fact the helper issues are still present is incredibly frustrating. Having to go round after them to correct their mistakes or finish the job they were meant to do (just as I had to in FS17), defeats the object of hiring them at all.

I'll continue to play, since it is, in essence, still very addictive. It's just a shame that it's really a re-badged version of FS17.

If I find a good enough reason to change my opinion, I will update this review accordingly. But for now, I remain unimpressed.\t\t\t\t\t
