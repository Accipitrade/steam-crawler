\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI originally started with FS17 on console and really didn't know about available mods for money cheats and didn't quite understand the use of mods so I did it the hard way and worked up from scratch by taking contracts and running those long jobs. Buying land bit by bit. I could make $50,000 on one harvest job. I made like $40 million the hard way before moving on to FS19 Platinum edition. The livestock in FS17 became lucrative as I was making $200,000 a day on milk and regularly selling thousands of pigs at a time for millions of dollars. After playing FS19 on XBox One for awhile I discovered streamers on the channels who were using all these great mods that I didn't have access to. Because they were only on PC. So I bought the PC version. Although I didn't do much with livestock with FS19 on Xbox. I did that when I went to FS19 on PC. And I found it disappointing because it's not that lucrative. Cows don't produce enough manure, slurry, or milk to be worth the time having them. They're not a money maker, they're a decoration. The pigs are also anal-retentive. If you want a lot of manure in FS19 you must construct a mod to buy it. Also the volume of livestock is restricted by the pens available. Many of the Cow Barns, pens, etc., that I experimented with had dump points that didn't work properly or were inside structures that prevented me from dumping my big truck trailers. And the trucks would get stuck and need to be re-set. A lot of the cow corral mods are broken. The pigs were unsatisfying and so were the chickens. You don't even need to water the chickens, they don't eat hardly anything, and their egg production is pathetic no matter how many of them you buy. The sheep produce a pathetically small amount of wool. The horses are for amusement, not production. Although a horse breeding mod is available. I was amazed at how low-maintenance are the livestock. They're no work at all. I kept checking back in, amazed, thinking, I still have no work to do here? In FS17 they were a lot of work for a big reward. In FS19 the livestock are very little work for very little reward. The breeding rates of the livestock in FS19 is also slow. In summary, the livestock in FS19 suck. But I'm not trying to trash a great game. And FS19 is a great game worth the money. There are so many available maps and an incredible amount of mods available. There are huge maps where you can take harvesting numbers to astronomical levels. With Course Play this takes the entire game to a new level. Socking away astronomical amounts of crops in a silo that will take 30 million liters. One a couple maps the given silo was 100 million capacity. Obtaining the mods can be ominous as some sites are full of adware and malware. Right now I'm working on the Mining and Construction Economy map. But I am disappointed by the limitations of the livestock on the other maps. And it's very likely I will soon be buying Farming Simulator 17 Platinum on PC here on Steam and explore the available mods I never saw on console. Because I might want to see what it is like to try to raise 2000 cows and 10,000 pigs. And explore some of the larger maps I never saw on console. Hopefully in the next installment GIANT will get the livestock right. Animals should be a productive element of the farm. Not just a decoration.\t\t\t\t\t
