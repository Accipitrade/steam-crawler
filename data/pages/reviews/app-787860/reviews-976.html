\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLets get this out of the way. I've been playing FS since 2013. Its one of my favorite game franchises. But it seems that after FS13, Giants has never really gave the games a big improvement and supposedly this was supposed to be that big improvement. The graphics are....okay. But I'll get into that later.

Pros:
- Lots of Brands! Great variety (although they insist on keeping the EU SMV warning signs so when you go to an American map it breaks my immersion)
- Purchasing land is a lot more realistic to me and more enjoyable.
- Finally, after years and years of waiting, we have a store where you can see what you're buying.
Cons:
- Hardly any real new improvements. Graphics are virtually the same as FS17 but with a few minor changes.
- Physics, they're laughable. Hit the accelerator in a John Deere 6155M and suddenly immediately you're going warp speed. Steering is still awful and gives no feedback, suspension on vehicles is stiffer than stiff. For a simulator series thats been going on for 10+ years, this is really bad considering I can boot up FS13 and get virtually the same experience.
- Horsepower? HA! That's irrelevant in FS19, doesn't matter what Harvester you get, you are stuck at the speed the game tells you to go.
- The game is optimized TERRIBLY, that reason alone is why I REALLY recommend to stick with one of the older games like FS15 or 17. Its jittery, barely runs at points if you look at more than one piece of equipment. For a game that graphically isn't much better than 17, its terribly thirsty for a really beefy system, and I can run games such as BO4 and GTA V at a solid 60FPS AT LEAST.
- Everything drives like a arcade racer.
- AI workers are still, not useless, but about as close to useless as you can get. Because as long as you keep an eye on them and fix their mistakes they make (like not finishing the edges of a field) you should be fine. But that's just a band-aid for poor design.

Unfortunately, it seems Giants is more interested in adding things like....dogs? To the game instead of fixing existing issues. There's more cons that I haven't bothered to list since I believe they've already been so well documented to the point I think it should be easy to look them up. Its a shame, look at a game like Halo CE and 9 years later in Halo Reach, you can see major graphical improvements. Every iteration of FS doesn't feel like something I'd pay $30 for again but more so a $10 update to a previous game. Lets be honest, everything new in FS19 isn't anything that couldn't have been added to FS17 or FS15, and that's becoming a big problem with this series. I still can have fun with it, but I'm very underwhelmed.\t\t\t\t\t
