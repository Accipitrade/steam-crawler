\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like the previous Farming Simulator games, or this type of low paced peaceful game in general, then you'll probably like Farming Simulator 19 as well. You just have to be aware that the game (just like all the previous FS games) remains very much unfinished and has never been through beta testing, and thanks to Giants (the devs) refusing to fix bugs, it still has numerous bugs and glitches - especially with the physics system - which can result in anything from minor things like crops that can't be harvested to completely breaking the game and making it unplayable.

When the game behaves and everything works as it should, it is however still very playable, and especially with mods even more so (without mods the game is very lacking in content).

Of notable new stuff in FS19 is that the "normal" (aka Farm Manager) and "hard" difficulties now start you off without a pre-made farm (modded maps sometimes work differently); you merely get a bit of money and have to buy all the necessary machinery and place all the farm buildings yourself. This means you no longer have a stable/pen for each animal type that you can't get rid of, but instead have to place them wherever you want them. New is also that you can keep and breed horses (max 16 at a time though), and to feed the horses you can grow oats (or you can just sell the oats as any other crop). Also new is the ability to grow and harvest cotton, but it works just like every other crop in the game and as usual the different machines is the most interesting part of any crop.

The actual game has however not changed much or at all since the previous releases and the majority of the new features (like dynamic hoses, the train driving around the map on its own, or chopped straw from harvesters being visible on the ground) are simply taken from mods for FS17. The additions do make the game more "real" and add to the atmosphere, but they are all minor visuals and have no actual effect on the gameplay.

Most important gameplay change is that the one fertilization stage has been changed to lime, so there's now 2 fertilization stages instead of 3, and weeds will grow in all crops and have to be sprayed with herbicide to improve the yield. Both lime and weeds can however be disabled. The need to plough fields now depend on what you grow, but it still doesn't account for low impact farming methods.

The new 3D preview of machinery in the shop is a long overdue improvement and updates live when selecting different options. The way you actually buy stuff in the shop has however not improved at all and requires a minimum of 3 clicks/button presses to buy anything, AFTER you've decide what to buy. Since you can only buy big bags and pallets one at a time and have to approve the purchase of every single one, this terrible design very quickly becomes frustrating and it is mind blowing that anyone at Giants thought it acceptable.

Maintenance of the equipment has changed in FS19 as well, but not for the better. Although in theory slightly more realistic than the terrible system in FS17 - machines now wear down extremely fast and have to be repaired at a workshop rather than having a recurring incremental cost for upkeep, BUT everything simply wears down way too fast to the point where the machines are worn completely out and become practically unusable after just a few (in-game) hours of use.
Even on small fields this means you have to either use equipment that is much bigger than is realistic, or use multiple machines at a time doing the same job (which the game doesn't handle very well), or you have to stop work multiple times to repair the machines. On slightly realistic size fields you have to repair even the biggest equipment several times or end up working the field at 1 kmh.
Considering how small fields in this game actually are it is ridiculous that things wear out so quickly and I highly doubt the manufacturers appreciate their machinery being depicted as not being able to handle working even a single small field before it has to go through major repair or replacement several times, but just as it was with FS17, Giants refuse to acknowledge that it is an issue and won't do anything to change it. Should be underlined that the idea is fine, but the implementation is awful and everything wears out about 100-1000 times faster than it should.

Very minor engine changes means the lighting and colours are better, but overall performance is still appalling and ground textures have not changed in 10 years. The game has no multitasking ability and thus no multicore support - everything literally runs on a single CPU core - which means the framerate will inevitably tank massively at times even on the most powerful PCs. As the game also offloads very little to the GPU, graphics settings generally make no difference to performance or framerate. In multiplayer this is much worse because you simply cannot run a server efficiently on a single core, so you will inevitably have enormous latency issues.

*Now 2+ years since release and we have a bunch of DLC that don't add anything but more of the same to the game, or stuff modders already did long ago. Meanwhile we're still waiting for Giants to fix the major gamebreaking bugs they keep ignoring (and we know they'll keep ignoring them and then spend 6 months making a "new" game just like they did with FS19).

Most significant bugs to be aware of:
- The game may crash when previewing any equipment in the ingame store (according to Giants this is not caused by their code, even though they're the ones who failed to write any form of error handling).
- When attempting to modify any vehicle at the shop (like you're supposed to be able to) the game will crash 99 of 100 times.
- When downloading anything from the ingame modhub, it is normal for the game to lock up and stop responding.
- Vehicles colliding with other vehicles/objects can cause them to glitch through the ground, causing the vehicle to fall forever. In some cases this can crash the game. (sometimes the game just deletes these machines so you can't recover them).
- Autosave often doesn't work, no matter your hardware. Even when it says it's saving, there's no guarantee it actually does. Manual save usually works every time, but then there's the occasional bug where the game locks up when saving. (Yet another problem Giants refuses to acknowledge).

Each DLC also has its fair share of major bugs. Especially the Claas DLC is "special" in its own way.\t\t\t\t\t

- Great time passer
- Super fun
- Multiplayer is actually decent
- Overall solid fun game, also it's new so expect updates and addins

Con's
- Feels incomplete
