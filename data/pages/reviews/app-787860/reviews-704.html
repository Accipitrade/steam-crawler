\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe perfect game to chill out. I have not played previous FS games so my opinion is from someone completely new to the series.

At the beginning you'll get stablished with a piece of land, and some cheap tools. You have to sow your fields, make grow and take care of them, and harvest your profits. With the money you'll eventually get new equipment, and so on, and so on.

It's the perfect game to relax after an stressful day of work at the office, or to disconnect your mind of that important project and his deadline. You only have to put some music/radio/podcast, relax and start to enjoy by working on your fields.

This game as odd as it sounds, it's extremely addictive even when it's EXTREMELY repetitive. You will always have an objective on mind (buy that impressive tractor, take care of your chickens or horses, expand your farm with a great piece of land or do the favor that neighbor is asking for). No matter that you have to work on the same fields over and over again. You feel your crops, your farm (and even your dog) as "yours", and you really have to look after them.

As for the simulation aspect of the game, the machinery is beatifully recreated. With all his working parts that you have to operate (deploy, start, etc.), it's really amazing. You will use the flashing lights on the road, the beacon lights when working on the crops,... the attention to detail and the interior cockpits are great. The physics of the fields are amazingly modelled, when your tractor/tool gets in contact, it's really nicely done. You have to watch your speed or some maneuvers if you don't want to roll your tractor and damage everything. The level of simulation is fully customizable, from an easy experience to a more demanding game. I play with an Xbox One pad although I would love to try some special farming sim peripherals. It supports TrackIr which works as expected.

Unfortunately the visuals, depiste great things like the tractors and tools models, the beautiful (germany) map , and some lighting conditions where the game looks stunning, the overall is that it has some flaws like the drawing distance (even expanding it in the developer options menu), some textures that doesn't look that great and the fact that is not well optimized, although I play it at 1440p with all maxed out at +100fps (i7-8700k, rtx2070,32gb ram), it feels that it still needs some polish or maybe a major graphics engine update. Anyway this goes to the background the moment you get hooked to it.

If you like games like ETS2, PC Building or Car Mechanic Simulators, etc. this game is for you. You decide your level of challenge and game pace. You can use it to wind down from more demanding games (after a tense session of flying or racing sims, this game fits perfectly).

Sometimes the situation has been inverted and I need to switch to more serious games to stop playing this FS'19.\t\t\t\t\t
