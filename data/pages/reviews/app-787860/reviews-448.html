\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow does that Bible verse go? "What has been will be again, what has been done will be done again; there is nothing new under the sun." Yup. Pretty much sums up Farming Simulator 19, which is pretty much the same as Farming Simulator 17, which is pretty much the same as Farming Simulator 15 (where I started playing this game). Sure, there's a pointless dog. Seriously. It serves zero purpose. You can pet it and feed it (it won't starve if you don't--it's an object, not a real animal), or throw a ball, but it's pretty much tied to its doghouse and runs around in a limited area. And there's horses. And you can modify terrain and place your own buildings. And they finally got licensing for John Deere equipment (Who cares about licensing? Save the money on licensing by using fake brands, and build a better game, instead!) Other than that? Same old, same old that's been around for how long now? Thirteen years? If it wasn't for the modding community, this game would be boring and a grind because they add too few new features in each new iteration. Speaking of mods, the in-game ModHub seems to be down more than it's up and functional. It's also not intuitive. Rather than try to build a better game, it would appear that Giants isn't interested in any more versions of this game, as there has been no mention of what would normally be a Farming Simulator 21 (odd numbers are for PC/console), which would have been released in November, 2020. There's also no mention of a Farming Simulator 22 for mobile, which would be released in 2021. In fact, the last news update on the official Farming Simulator website was posted in July, 2020 for the virtual FarmCon. Sure, Covid happened, but would it kill Giants to put out some news as to whether or not they can develop a new game with workers at home? Other businesses seem to be functioning that way. Other games are also being released. If you have Farming Simulator 17, save your money and stick with that.\t\t\t\t\t
