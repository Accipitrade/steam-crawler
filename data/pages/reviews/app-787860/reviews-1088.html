\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played over a thousand hours on FS15 and FS17, so I think that gives me the ability write an informed review after only one day with FS19.
Good points:
1. The crop care simulation is more realistic. The game now includes herbicides and lime. And the use of weeders and plowing have been improved so that they are actually valuable tools.
2. The graphics are definitely better. Graphics are always a trade off between realism, playability and hardware demand. I think FS19 strikes a great balance between these factors. The graphics are attractive, immersive, and appropriate for the gameplay. I use a mid level gaming laptop, and the game runs smooth as silk.
3. You can now choose starting conditions where you don't own a farm, and then build (mostly) from scratch. This is a huge leap forward in customization and replayability.
4. The savegame files are easy to modify with a word processor. I won't explain how to do this here, but it makes it very easy to design any starting scenario you want.
5. The types of livestock have been increased with new care options.
6. The number of farm machines is just amazing. You could play for hundreds of hours just trying new machines and farming techniques.

Bad points:
1. It took me over 30minutes to set up my game pad. I had the same hassles setting up FS15 and FS17. And reading the discussions pages I see I'm not the only one with this frustration. Giant really needs to invest some work in making their set up system better.
2. The starting options are limited. There are only three scenarios: easy start with a working farm, medium with cash and no farm, hard with less cash and no farm. There are work arounds for creating your own scenario by modding the savegame files, but I think Giants should make some of these options visible at game startup. Maybe have some theme farms like a dairy, or horse ranch, or logging camp.
3. The lack of startup options means you have to grind, grind, grind to get to the point where you can specialize in things like raising livestock, or buying and using those expensive pieces of equipment.

Neutral:
1. The game ships with two new maps that are very similar, and one old map in the mod folder. Lots of great old maps from early FS games are missing. The included maps should give you many hours of gameplay, and hopefully the mod community will add more content.
2. There are a limited number of placeble objects for designing your own farm, and many of them are not interactive. For example, there is only one farmhouse design available for each map. Why could they not put all three farmhouses from all three maps in all three placeble folders? Also the moneymaking placeables like bee hives, greenhouses and windmills are missing. I think Giant is waiting for the mod community to do their work for them.

Overall I think FS19 is a great update to the farming simulator line. It's not a stunning change in the game, but it is a very nice evolutionary improvement. It makes the game feel fresh again, with better graphics and new gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
