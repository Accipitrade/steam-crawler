\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, so this is my first ever review.

First off, let me say that I am a huge fan of FS series to date. Particularly FS 17.

But on reflection I now realise that FS17 was great because it created a platform for modders to really up their game.
Now that I'm playing FS19 without mods, I've come to realise that the game is only good with mods. For me anyway.
For me, I like the dynamic of using Courseplay to effectively manage the farm as oppposed to just drive tractors etc.

Why doesnt Giants take ANY advice from its fans? We have been saying for years to add some kind of courseplay replica
to really enhance gameplay. We have also been asking for better ground working physics, more realism. One of the only games I have seen do this is Agriculture Simulator...yes it was an absolute shambolic mess from the get go, but they had the two things FS is missing. A management function AND better ground dynamics.


So heres a few pros and cons:

Pros
-The machinery looks really good. Particularly on High settings.
-There is a real "weight" to driving the bigger machines. This is helped by the new steering system too.
-Finally added John Deere.
-Really interesting "contracting" model.

Cons
-The gameplay reminds me of the original FS. The monotony of drawing my own grain etc is creeping in.
-The maps are ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. Unimaginative. Badly planned. Again, reminds me of a bygone era of FS.
-There is NO useful infrastructure that you can buy. Couple of sheds and a vehicle workshop.
-4/10 times, my games crashes
-Ground dynamics are literally the exact same as FS17.
-♥♥♥♥ all new machinery.
-WAAAYYY TOOOO DAAYYUMM EXPENSIVE YOOO

My verdict:

This game had real potential. The build up to release alone had me really pumped to get it. But its just a re-skinned version of FS17. I for one, will be going back to FS 17 until the very talented modders out there make this game great.\t\t\t\t\t
