\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played many of the previous versions of the game and I must say, the most playable is still FS 15. FS17 was released way before it was a polished product and to this day still has unresolved issues with crashing and hired workers not doing what they are supposed to do. Now FS 19 is out and the state this game is in is almost unplayable.

You will get the farmboi's claiming the game is fun, but there are so many issues that just break the game, it isn't much fun.

First off lets discuss their shipped maps. These are just stupid. Almost all farms have huge piles of garbage on them that cannot be removed other than editing the map and removing them totally from the game. Who wants a farmyard with huge piles of scrap iron in them? These maps also have the ability to put many "placeable" items in them, (which is required if you are starting from scratch), but WHERE you can place them is severely limited. If you cut down a tree, there is a 90% chance it will block you from placing anything where that tree was. Furthermore, the distance with which you can place these items seems to have an excessively large "no build zone" around each placeable object. This will, under many situations make building placement very awkward and restricted.

The shipped US map in now way shape or form emulates North American farms. This farm looks more like something at the foothills of the rockies, NOT the grain belt of the US or Canada. If you're going to emulate the primary grain production of the US, get it right! When is the last time you have seen mountains in Oklahoma or Texas? Seen any mountains in Kansas or Iowa? The way that US farmland is depicted is almost insulting. They seem to think that the US is still living in the dark ages. I have seen farms in the US that would dwarf anything in this game.

These maps are also pooly designed when it comes to placement of items. For some reason, power poles are placed every 30 feet which makes getting around the map, awful. So many of the poles are placed so close together that you cannot enter the field with the larger combines with an attached header since it doesn't fit between the power poles. Where I live, there are ZERO power poles any more. There are the huge 3 phase lines and the major production lines that connect the substations to the production facilities. The lines that go from the substations to the various farms are all buried and have been that way for over 30 years. The telephones lines have been buried for more like 40 years. Is Europe that assbackwards that they are 40 years behind? I find that hard to believe!

They still have not fixed the woodchippers, since they seem like their ability to grip and pull tree's is very lacking. The least amount of drag causes the tree to stall and not enter the chipper. This has been an issue all the way back to FS 15 and it's still not addressed in ANY of the previous releases. The physics that effect trees is just awful. When pulling a tree with the Ponsse Scorpion, the tip that touches the ground swings around and slides sideways with such ease, it looks very stupid.

Ahh, but the machines themselves are correct right? laughable, they are not even close. Most of the combines have a turning radius less that half that of their real world counterparts. The steering on these vehicles is just horrid. Using the mouse and keyboards with factory settings makes the game unplayable. There are settings in game to lessen these delays somewhat, but they fall horridly short.

Best way, edit the game files (yes the ones they tell you not to), because their settings in the game are nowhere near real life. I have had to edit every vehicle that I use on my farm to bring them into their real world counterpart specifications. I think maybe their game testers are pet monkeys! No quality control managers I know would ever publish anything that is this far away from what they actually are. It's not inventing the items, these items already exist, have known parameters and controls. But Giants seems to think that they can just splat on a bunch of stupid parameters and settings and no one will notice. Uhm, there's a "new" thing out here in the real world called "Google", maybe have the devs check it out?

Hired helpers, oh lord, these guys are so broken! They are constantly stopping their harvest with "Unknown reason" and the only way to get them going again, is to hop in, and driver the combine again for a fair chunk of the field until the "Hire Worker" function will actually work again. Furthermore, they very often will leave strips at the end of every field, and sometimes refuse to harvest the last 6 foot strip along the side of a field. "Helper X has completed their task". Uh, there's still half a mile long 6 feet wide!

Now it is very obvious that these devs have never visited a US or Canadian based farm. Land in Canada at least as well as the US is laid out using latitude and longitude. As such over 90% of the land is laid out in "quarters" . This means that most fields in North America are laid out in half-mile by half-mile fields. Perfect squares. Hence the basic land descriptions "SW XX XX XX WX" (Where XX is the numbers that designate the land location). This is what North America was founded on. The fields may be different shaped due to various hills, valleys or water (Sloughs, lakes or rivers), but the base field is square, always square.

European fields may be helter-skelter and unusual shapes, but North American fields are based on squares. Canada uses the metric system, which is bloody stupid. The country was drawn up and divided up by miles and even takes into account the curve of the earth with "Correction Lines".

Farmland in the US and Canada, for the most part has very few fields with only 40 acres or less, They may have crops at that size by subdividing a quarter section into several fields, but the fact remains, your base "Field" in the US and Canada is 160 acres (quarter section or a half a mile by a half a mile). The US map never reflects this at all, as all fields are hap hazard and chaotic.

On a side note, the game is constantly suffering from micro freezes, crashes, and freeze ups. Hopping between many vehicles using the map eventually starts to disable map commands, such as "escape", "enter vehicle" and "hire worker" which will shortly thereafter cause the game to freeze totally disabling everything and causing the player to be stuck in place unable to move, open the map, or open the purchase interface. These issues seem to get even worse if you install mods such as coursePlay. CoursePlay has it's own issues, but at least it's devs are attempting to fix it and are constantly releasing updates via GitHub. Giants? Not so much.

I have since quit playing FS 19, since it's just frustrating having to constantly hop for one field to the next starting hired workers that have quit working for "unknown reason". Some of the larger maps out there, (mod Maps since the ones that ship with the game are just lousy), have over 60 fields, so as your farm grows, the amount of microfreezes approaches that of totally game breaking. Having vast amounts of machinery working in the field with hirelings means microfreezes about every 5 seconds or so. The game starts to stutter so much, you have to turn it off.

Also having even 30 fields and trying to maintain all the machinery with spraying, cultivating, fertilizing and harvesting, just causes so much hassle with hired workers stopping constantly just causes frustration. Sometimes the hired workers do strange things like driving their machine way off the field in a straight line, veer way off course. These instances are usually followed very shortly by a complete game crash, lockup or parts of the GUI locking solid

There are more issues, but sadly, I have reached the character count limit/post.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this is the first Farming Simulator that I don't recommend. I keep the right to change my opinion once I invest a couple of hundred hours into it... Let's wait and see.

So - why "Not recommended"?

Once again, Giants have just re-packaged their game. This is FS17 with two new maps, a bit re-shaded graphics and minimal gameplay enhancements. Add a few FS17 DLCs into the base game and you get FS19.

Yes, it looks nicer than FS17 if you have the graphics set to max, but my i7 7700/GTX1060 laptop can't handle them. So, to get the smooth FPS, I have to play on mid settings - and they look horrible. Way worse than FS17.

Price is also over the top (for what they are offering). Giants are just milking this cash-cow at the moment. Buy yourself FS17 or some DLCs for it - way better value for money.

I already felt cheated when I bought FS17, but now I'm just dissapointed. This is the last time I buy the actual FS. From now on, I'll just play the one that's a generation old.

Edit 1 @ 33 hours:
I'm not sure what grass Giants were consuming when they wrote the minimum specs - 4GB RAM. ??? It seems this game is a memory hog; I could barely run mid settings with 8GB. I have now upgraded to 16GB, and am delighted to report that I can run the game on High settings. (Still, nothing to write home about... it still feels like FS17 with a bit of a shader applied.) I still think you should save your money and play FS17.

Edit 2 @ 470 hours:
Well, yeah, I'm playing it seeing that I already own it. Since my last edit, there was a couple of DLC releases... Only one worth the money though - the John Deere DLC. The platinum expansion was a fiasco - not even a map included in it. No thanks Giants. I'll pass.\t\t\t\t\t
