\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Farming Simulator is a rewarding game to play while still having some challenging hurdles that you will have to overcome. There is definitely a learning curve if you have not played a Farm Sim game before. With so many different kinds of equipment and attachments, it was somewhat overwhelming at times. Thankfully, there are a lot of great tutorials available to help you learn the ropes. They do take a while to get through all of them, but I would highly recommend that you go through all or most of them. You will thank yourself later!
Depending on which of the three game modes you decide to play, you will have to be careful with the money you start out with to get the right equipment that you will need to grow and harvest specific crops, while also having enough money left over for seed, fertilizer, and other supplies that you will need for your farm. The game can feel every "grindy" at times, especially if you don't have enough farm land to be generating much profit to keep expanding your business. Doing contracts for other farmers is also another very good way to make some extra money while also giving you a break from the monotony of working the same fields all of the time.
A game changer for me as I was starting to get tired of playing on my farm alone was being able to jump into a multiplayer game with my friends. It revitalized many elements of the game for me and working with other people also really sped up the farming process. I've had many hours building camaraderie with my group of friends I play with as we worked together and laughed at each other's mistakes
The modding community behind this game is also incredibly talented so if there's basically anything that you find you don't like about the game, there is almost certainly a mod that can fix it for you.

There's a lot more I could write about Farming Sim., but I'll just end by saying that this is easily one of my top 5 games I have ever played on Steam and I highly recommend FS19 if you are considering picking it up.\t\t\t\t\t
