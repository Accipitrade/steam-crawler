\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to like this game, I really did, but it has so many problems that ruin the experience (for me at least), and left me utterly disappointed so it's just not possible. It has some nice features as well, i can't say it's all bad, but there's definitely more negative than positive here. Okay.

First of all, the graphics. Even with all the settings maxed out this game still looks like a work-in-progress. Some of the machines have nice details though, but the environments, buildings, cars, and pretty much everything else looks just plain unfinished. Not cool. I know that graphics don't make a game, but in this case making the game look even "ok" by 2018 standards, would be nice, especially in a game that's supposed to be very immersive and realistic.


Physics in the game, i have a big problem with. You see, in real life, heavy machines like tractors, logging equipment, harvesters, trucks, and so on... are big and heavy. In the game, some of them handle like a three-wheeled go-kart. Try loading a logging truck full and drive it to the sawmill, you'll see what im talking about, the whole thing wobbles left and right like it's drunk. Controls feel wobbly and too fast, like you're driving something totally different than you actually are.

Another issue is with the logging parts of the game. Like big equipment, also huge logs are heavy, but in the game you can toss them around like tiny sticks, yet despite that, the crane in the forwarder is unable to pick up more than one log at a time, this is highly unrealistic (maybe not if you'd have a smaller forwarder, but with big ones like the Ponsse Buffalo or the Komatsu in the game, you're not supposed to have issues like that) and makes for a seriously annoying gameplay.

I know you're supposed to mod the s*** out of this game, and so I did. Better maps, better equipment, more stuff, this and that. The best mods would be the maps, since the original two maps are just plain boring and uninteresting. I liked the "Grizzly Mountain" logging map, and the "Michigan 2.0" farming map much much more than the originals. I also downloaded a mod to make logging more realistic, and while it definitely took it in the right directions, it wasn't right enough and the physics issues mentioned before were still there. It made some things nicer, sure, but not enough. Bummer.

Surely, there must be something positive as well? Yes. The amount of equipment available and the number of functions they can do, is pretty nice. I like the detail of mud/dirt on your equipment the more you use it on the field, it's pretty much the only nice visual thing in the game. The gameplay can be "ok" at best, it's not bad, but my expectations were so much higher, and no I don't thing they were too high.

I operate forestry/logging machines in real life and I was really looking forward to seeing how it was all created in the game. I was left very disappointed. The farming bits of the game are slightly better done, if that's all you want to do then the game can ALMOST be recommended, with heavy modding of course. Wait for a sale.

All that being said i must add that I really hope Giants improves this game in the future, there sure is potential for a great game. Please put more focus on logging, not just adding more machines, but the physics of the logs and the features of the machines themselves.

My overall score would be a 5/10 (the game in it's current state and without mods), not recommended.
