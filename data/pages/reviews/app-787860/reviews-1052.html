I don't recommend the game untill the Devs can catch up with what the modders always fix

First off, the good stuff. Graphics are beautiful, crisp and detailed. The crops, plants, trees loof like real living things in the enviorment. The new levels of soil care and tools for soil care add new depth to the game. The pre built maps are detailed and fun to explore, also enjoyed that we got the south american styled map as well for free. The new land buying system is great, make it easy to know what land is really yours and how far you can really go. Now this however is the end of what i found good.

The bad. The new build your own farm is cool, but do I always want that concrete pad? NO! I don't want it for half the structures I place, the abilty to toggle and change it would be nice. There is alot of equipment that will leave you scratching your head about what it does do to the most vage descriptions they can do. Now the A.I. is a full blown dumbass when it comes to harvesting, Holy crap it cannot drive the forage harvesters to save their lives. If you are not johhny on the spot with a damn tipper they panic, lock up the breaks and have to catch their breath before they start to move again. This is way apperent when working with sugarcane. Isn't it about time we get better control over our workers? Modders please bring back your advanced A.I. controls as the Devs cannot figure it out. A hint to them on this is the A.I. controls from Construction sim 2, where i can tell the ding-a-ling in the dumptruck to follow the cold planner.

So in short the only issues are the farm building and the A.I. after 9 or so years is still dumber then a potato\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFarming Simulator 19 is almost exactly what we've come to expect from the FS franchise. It's an enjoyably buggy game that gives users full creative expression to build the farm of their dreams. With new features like lime and cotton the game adds more options than it's prededcessor and the developers have obviously listened to the community when it comes to the features that FS17 players wanted to see implimented into the base game.

Pros:
-New Crops and livestock allow a more dynamic game, especially in multiplayer
-Multiplayer is VASTLY improved over FS17 with individual farms and bank accounts in the base game.
-"Building your own farm" gives you a feeling of being more connected to the game than in previous versions.
-Of course... John Deere
-Huuuuge modding community is already hard at work creating new and exciting mods for FS19
-Dev's seem to be receptive to criticism and are currently working to push a patch to fix the known issues.

Cons:
-It's a Giants game, the initial release is always full of bugs and it takes a few weeks to work out.
-The engine, doesn't seem to be that much different than FS17 and the game mechanics are almost identical
-Controller support is still an issue
-Some features in Multiplayer obviously weren't tested (granting contractor status, doing contracts for AI)
-AI system is still unable to cope with basic obstacles.
-Lots of equipment has issues with mirrors, lights, interior lighting, etc.
-Giants didn't include "Seasons" OR a working GPS in the game. I know I don't speak for the entire world, but of the dozen people I have spoken to about FS19 all of them were dissapointed about these features being absent.

Overall I think GIANTS has a solid base for what could have been an AMAZING game, but in it's current state it's only "OK". If you're a fan of the FS/LS franchise you should buy the game, but if you're reading reviews to make your decision you probably are going to be dissapointed, at least until they patch some of the major bugs.\t\t\t\t\t
