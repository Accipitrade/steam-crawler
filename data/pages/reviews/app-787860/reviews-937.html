\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt about 30 hours in, I can tell you honestly that this game is oddly satisfying and insanely addictive. Another aspect that I find alluring, yet unexpected is how relaxing it is. As a pilot, my job is often stressful and I find this simple Farming Sim to be a great place to unwind and enjoy the scenery. I haven't even explored the world of logging or animals yet!

Pros:
-simple gameplay with complex elements, basics are easy but there is a depth to the economics and the machinery that keeps it interesting past the first harvest.
-gorgeous landscapes!
-Try the radio function, I particularly enjoy the live stream of current hit music.

Cons
-Driving gets a bit squirrelly at higher speeds and the physics with the vehicles/terrain/trailers gets a bit wonky. This isn't a driving game so I'm not to bothered by it, but getting a trailer stuck on a stop sign is at times annoying.

Verdict: If you have any interest in simulation style games, this one is a fantastic way to pass the time!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly, for context, I am a 36M, married with 2 kids who also play FS. After playing hundreds of hours in FS15 and FS17, mostly vanilla, after only 8hrs I find FS19 is a complete let-down. I play primarily for the logging experience, which in FS17 wasn't perfect but was far far better than FS19. The logging physics has been completely ruined in FS19. Stock wheel loader has lower reach making loading the log trailer very difficult unless parked lower. The log grabber doesn't tip back hardly more than horizontal making it very difficult (especially on a grade) to roll logs deeper into the jaws. The closing physics of the log grabber is also broken resulting in many logs clipping through even when gripped evenly, firmly, and moving slowly. On average, only 3 logs can be grabbed regardless of size etc. Even the tractor log grabber is worse now than FS17, with sticky and clipping logs happening far more often.

The only truck I have so far is the Warrior, which apparently has only 1 gear, or maybe a CVT? The horn is also absolutely pitiful.

The train horn has also been "upgraded" from FS17, which had a very realistic crescendo and auto-repeated if held. FS19 train horn is just a single note that starts and ends abruptly. Why would this be changed?

The only improvements worth mentioning is the visuals and more realistic hydraulics and steering.

After reading through the reddit sub I see now that all these logging issues have been complained about for up to and over a year now with no improvements. Very slack of the developers not to address these issues. I will be returning to FS17 for the logging experience and will not be recommending FS19 to anyone looking for improved logging.\t\t\t\t\t
