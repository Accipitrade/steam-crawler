\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've given positive reviews for all Farming Sim games up until now. As soon as I loaded the game the first issue I encountered was the mouse sensitivity, I can't remember this being an issue for me in the older versions of the game. But on this one even upping the sensitivity on my mouse was still nowhere near enough, as I still needed 5 swipes of the mouse just to look 360 degrees. You'd think that a game would have a simple mouse sensitivity slider, but no. I had to google it and found out I had to go into the game files and change it manually. Something which really isn't fun to do when you've just bought a game you want to play.

The game is still very 'Clunky' the Vehicles still drive and manoeuvre like some old game made in the 2000's. Something I noticed straight away when driving, is that wheels no longer auto straighten. Making things like reversing trailers way more complicated than it needs to be. I feel that was a move for people who use steering wheels when playing. On mouse and keyboard though it doesn't feel right, as every other game I play with Vehicles the wheels straighten up when letting go of 'A' or 'D'.

Although the game does have a handy help section explaining how most things work. It is very vague, and I tend to find myself resorting to YouTube and Google in search for answers on how to do some of the basic things. I was hoping for a search feature in the shop, as with 102 brands and hundreds of different types of vehicles and equipment. When you see a trailer that requires an 'SB100' to use, I'm expected to go through those 102 brands or the 20 odd vehicles tabs to find it. Something again that isn't fun to do for everything you buy.

It does play and feel like I'm playing the exact same game as FS17. It does have some cool new features. I'm a big fan of the smaller details, like having your character use peddles when driving to break and accelerate and bounce on their seat when driving over rough terrain. Birds on your fields. You can enter your house. These are just small cosmetic things in the game, but I can always appreicate the small details. I do like that now you buy land rather than just fields, having the map cut up in to tons of sections you can buy is a big plus.

I have experienced a few bugs, for example if a field needs sowing or something. I'll sometimes hire a worker to do it then go afk. Then when I come back the worker has for some reason decided to just keep driving and ends up on the other side of the map, all whilst still charging me the workers fee. When I return to my field only half of it is usually done, so the worker just decided to go for a drive. This has happened quite a few times for me.

Contracts have changed. In FS17 you would start a contract, teleport to the field you chose, and have all the equipment there ready to use. Now, you have the option of using your own equipment to save money, or you have to pay to lease the vehicles to do the contract. I paid to lease as I didn't want to drive my harvester all the way to the other side of the map. I expected the Vehicles to spawn at the field like in FS17, instead everything spawned in the shop. So I had to drive a Harvester, header, tractor and trailer across the map. Once the trailer was full I had to empty it at a selling point, again on the other side of the map. I had to do this twice. Point is, I spent about 45 minutes doing a single contract worth just £3,500. Minus the £400 lease fees. So the contracts become a waste of time, unless you pick a field next to the ones you own or next to a shop.

I must be the most unlucky person in the game aswell. Considering I'm on my 24th ingame day. With Wheat, Barley, Oats, Canola, Soybeans and Sunflowers all at, or just under the 100,000l capacity of my Silo. Out of those 24 ingame days not a single one has had a high demand at any of the sell points. I've had to survive on Eggs and Bailing alone for all those days. I don't think I can fault the game on this, I assume I'm just really unlucky with the high demand spawns. Pretty frustrating though sitting on hundreds of thousands of pounds worth of crops which I'm waiting to sell.

Overall the game is still worth a buy if you liked FS17, as it's pretty much just a reskin of FS17 but with some slightly better graphics and a couple of new farming methods (Sugar cane, Oats, Cotton etc). I'll most likely still put hundreds of hours in to it like the older versions. However, my reason for not recommending it is the complicated cluttered menus, the odd bug and having to leave the game to search up things. I just felt disappointed on how little the games changed from FS17. And that basic things in the game that have annoyed many of the players in the previous versions weren't addressed or improved. And the lack of common sense when designing certain features like the pre-mentioned Contracts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFOR NOW I would not recommend this game. Sure, better graphics and all that, but the playability is rather 'limited' before it gets tedious and boring. I have played previous versions for more than a 1000hrs, but what really made those versions interesting for me and many (maybe most) players were some mods like Courseplay (and Seasons) for example to take over the tedious chores and give me more room to organize and enjoy farming. I would have thought that Giants would have understood the importance of these few mods to many FS-fans, and would have given those modders more support for creating new versions of their mods BEFORE the release of FS19. For now I keep on playing FS17 with - amongst others - a good working Courseplay and keep FS19 on the shelf until the modders have upgraded their fantastic mods. Unbelievable Giants does not realize the impact of some mods on the value and playability of their product, rather disappointing... These modders deserve more recognition. A lesson for me before I ever buy a next FS edition: wait a year or so after a new release until modders have written their new scripts.

ADDED 3 Feb 2019: a beta of Courseplay has been released (apparently with - too late -support from Giants), but it is an utter disgrace that Giants managed to mess up the setup of the only controller made specifically for FS, the Logitech/Saitek G Farm Simulator bundle, and in-game command shortcuts appear for some Playstation controller instead. So I stick happily with FS17 for now.\t\t\t\t\t
