\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've never played a Farming Simulator game before, then yes, I recommend this. It's quite enjoyable, and does very well what it set out to do; good graphics, very farm-like atmosphere, great detail on the equipment, fun game mechanics. It's a very good farming feel. (The finances and such are simplified and a bit hand-waived; this is a game about doing and ogling, not micromanaging.)

However, if you already have Farming Simulator 17, I don't think it's worth having to buy the whole damn thing again. There's not a whole lot of point. If they gave you a discount for already owning what is in effect a lot of the same game, I'd feel differently. But I can't recommend spending money on a slightly-refreshed version of the same game over and over and over.

And yeah, the base price isn't too bad, but all the DLC they offer offsets that. It's kind of insulting. If you must have it, at the very least wait until it's on a big sale.

EDIT: Okay, so, there's a built-in mod repository right in the game that's super-easy to use, and the dev Giants is really active in making-or-at-least-curating a bunch of add-ons for the game that are free. I wish the mod selection screen gave more info about the vehicles, but overall it does add a great deal of variety to the game without requiring a bunch of DLC purchases.

Importantly, many of the mods appear balanced with the base game, and of comparable quality.

That, plus the modest base game price, made me switch my recommendation from no to yes despite the frequent release schedule.\t\t\t\t\t
