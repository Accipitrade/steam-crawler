\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs this the most realistic modern farming experience? Yes. Is it fun? Sometimes.

Most of the issues I'm going to talk about can be mitigated with mods, maps, etc. However, I want to provide a point of view for people who don't want to mod the game a bunch to get it to what I'd consider a fun state.

Graphics: They're good and more or less what I'd expect from the genre. They're nothing fancy, but they're hardly bad. Nothing more to say without getting nit-picky.

Sound: The sound is good. It is also nothing amazing or bad.

Gameplay: Oooh boy... This is good and bad. I'm going to split this up into sections below. I'll just say that I'm playing on keyboard and mouse, so bear that in mind. I don't really have the money to buy a setup for a specialized controller. I could also probably get good results from an xbox controller as well.

Maps: I HATE the map design for the default maps. Ravenport isn't bad. I like the layout of the shops and roads, but I hate the fields. I also feel like you're required to back into way too many spots.

General Gameplay: This game is REALLY slow. I knew this going in of course, but I didn't realize that it would be quite this slow. Yes, I realize you can adjust the time multiplier but this doesn't effect your farming machines speed. It only effects the grow speed of crops and the flux of prices.

Modding: The best part is most of this can be fixed with mods. Mods are very easy to install and can make your game into pretty much what you want. For example, because I'm playing with keyboard and mouse, I opted to get a mod to load pallets and bags automatically as running a forklift with keyboard and mouse isn't really intuitive. All in all, I like this game, but think it definitely needs mods to be playable.\t\t\t\t\t
