\r\n\t\t\t\t\t**Ingredients:**
3 pounds mixed apples (such as Granny Smith, Gala and McIntosh)
2/3 cup granulated sugar
2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
6 tablespoons unsalted butter
1 tablespoon all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
3/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
1/8 teaspoon salt
2 disks dough for Basic Crust
1 large egg, beaten
Coarse sugar, for sprinkling

**Method:**
Peel and core the apples; slice 1/4 inch thick. Toss with the granulated sugar and lemon juice. Heat 4 tablespoons butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the apples and cook, stirring, until tender, about 10 minutes. Stir in the flour, cinnamon and salt; cook until thickened, 1 more minute. Let cool.

Roll out 1 disk of dough into a 12-inch round on a floured surface; ease into a 9-inch pie plate. Add the apples, mounding them slightly in the center, and dot with the remaining 2 tablespoons butter. Roll out the remaining dough into a 12-inch round. Lay it over the filling; press the edges of the crusts together, then fold the overhang under itself and crimp to seal. Brush with the beaten egg and sprinkle with coarse sugar. Cut a few slits in the top crust. Chill 1 hour.

Place a baking sheet on the lowest oven rack and preheat to 425 degrees F for 30 minutes. Place the pie on the hot baking sheet; lower the temperature to 375 degrees F. Bake until golden, 1 hour to 1 hour, 10 minutes. (Cover the edges with foil if they brown too quickly.) Cool on a rack.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very addictive game, and that is the problem.

This is a very strange game. It checks a lot of good boxes. Tons of equipment, tons of customization. MODs (OMG!!! The MODs!!!), great community.

Then what is the problem? None, if you’d like to invest hours upon hours in something that is not that different from real work without any goal other than what you set for yourself. I bought this game just as I was starting my yearend holiday vacations. I played over 60 hours in my first scenario, and, since then, I’ve started another seven. And that’s how, suddenly, I’ve over 200 hours played. I was astounded to find that during over 15 days I did nothing but playing this game, with breaks only to eat, and sleeping as little as 4 hours. My dog, poor baby, she never leaves my side, started to get very anxious. I think it was because of the zombie like state I’ve been in the last month.

So, it was during this weekend, when I decided to start my eighth scenario with horses. My seventh scenario was with sheep, and I was doing between 300-400k per day, so what’s the point? Added pigs, that was the start from scratch mode, so I actually bought the property with the 3 little fields that one gets in new farmer mode to put the pigs there. Had tons of money so I bought the equipment for corn besides what is needed for barley and canola. Got everything running. Then, I cannot say I got bored. I simply started to question myself, “ what am I doing?” I’ve been a gamer for 30 years – no kidding, I started playing Prince of Persia, Digger, Chessmaster 2100, etc, in a XT86. I’ve never felt like I was wasting my time. So started this new scenario (horses), tried to use the less mods as possible. I see the path to profit. Then again, that sensation that I’m wasting my time.

And it’s not that I didn’t have a good idea of what I was getting into. I played Simcity for many years, I’ve played many other sandbox games. One of my current favorites is ETS2 (Euro Truck Simulator 2), no end game, but the gameplay is extremely satisfying. You don’t feel the need in ETS2 to restart as often as with FS19, and even when you do restart, you’ll be getting back to your old saves to continue them quite often. It might be that FS19 is just not for me? Probably. But it is, definitively, a very dangerous game, for me.

Or maybe, there is something missing here, a competing AI? More strict rules? A mode to play more strategically? I think the starting phase is nice. Planning your farm, your equipment, your buildings. Depending what you do next it can get grindy, wasn’t an issue at the beginning. Do what you need to do in real time, then speed up the clock to see your results. Nice. If you planned well, you just need to rinse and repeat. Then you start to look for better equipment, and you need to upgrade your tractor or get a new one. Nice. Not even getting the biggest tractor, just the right one. Rinse and repeat several times again. Then you get big enough. Specially in my first saves, I started to dread that time of harvesting 4 – 6 HAs, then spreading lime, then seeding ( and I already had discovered seeders that cultivate and fertilize at the same time). Even with helpers, those things are real time. Speeding up the clock will not make your helpers go faster. I guess, that would be difficult to implement with how often the helpers have problems finding the right path or even blocking each other. I think it would be great if you could just instruct a helper with something like “I want this field prepared and seeded with soybeans, with the option to order two fertilizer stages, with lime, herbicide, etc”, then hit go, and, at the cost of the money and game time required, just get the result. The helper wouldn’t need to be animated in this mode. You could then select a different field and provide instructions as well. Then go about your business, feed your cows, your pigs. Whether you decide to speed up the clock or not, you’d get the results in the game time required. That’ll be awesome because then, owning a whole map would be an achievable objective. (Now someone is going to come and tell me that there is a mod for that already, and I’m going to feel very dumb, I’ll be happy to feel that dumb and change my rating). However, I also strongly believe that a competing AI should be an option at some point in the future.

Another topic. MODs. This game would hardly be what it is without mods. So much that when you look at DLCs you are disappointed. There is no reason to buy them, unless you are a collector. But the mods are what really make this game awesome. Who could even think of not having the rep trailer? Or having a replacement for your House, so expensive, you cannot avoid but to sell it to get a cheaper one and use the funds for your precious start up budget? Moving all those bales and pallets with your tractor and front loader, fun at the beginning, a boredom later. Use an autoload trailer better. Though, there are some mods that are plain cheats. Short on money? Look for placeables that give you an hourly income. Though, there are only a few in the official mod site, fairly balanced, there are others out there that are really broken, that’ll recover the price in a few hours but they’ll also basically nullify the need for you to do anything else in game. However, is not that you could even challenge yourself to play without mods, because again, some like the rep trailer, that basically saves you trips to the shop to get seed, fertilizer, herbicide, etc, are basically indispensable.

But the MOD community is awesome, real love is being put by them. The effort and level of detail is stunning in the majority of the mods you see everywhere. Not only in machinery and buildings, but also in very useful gameplay modifications. Maps, OMG!! So many awesome maps, some of them way better than the ones in the base game. From the research I did, the gameplay in this game has hardly changed since about 10 years ago when the first version came out. Just better graphics and some refinements. It is the mods that make it a complete game, in my opinion.

TLDR: The game has a lot of good mechanics. Very well implemented. A lot of different stuff to do. The community, the one dedicated to modding and the youtube creators are awesome. However, the game is extremely addictive, it is almost like real work gameplay. A key element, in my opinion, is missing to remove that sensation that you’re only wasting your time. Though, I’m trying to play other titles, I’m likely still trapped. You’ve been warned.\t\t\t\t\t
