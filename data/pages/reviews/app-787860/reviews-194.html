\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter a hundred hours of gameplay, I decided to write a review. Yes, the game is not perfect, but I don’t really want to scold her. Let's start with the positive aspects of the simulator:

Many little things have been worked out - the land is plowed, the grain visually sets, trees are sawn, combines and tractors straighten their equipment and really interact with the surface;
Large map - there is something to do on it, from shopping and traveling along the roads to selling goods at the appropriate points;
A variety of vehicles, tools and buildings - everyone will find for themselves the perfect combination of technology and buildings to build a dream farm;
Graphics - compared to the previous part, it has become much prettier, both textures and lighting;
Pleasant little things like playing with a dog, riding horses, walking along the promenade.

And now the objective negative:

The detail work is excellent, but I didn’t notice banal things like the character’s legs, his hands on the steering wheel, adequate rear-view mirrors;
Many important processes occur automatically, in a second, without visual support. For example, they brought horses from the store - they instantly teleport to the paddock. We decided to draw water from the water tower - we rolled up a cistern, stood for a couple of seconds, and that's it. And so in most cases, with the exception of the central mechanics like tilling the land, feeding animals, etc.
There is no immersion in the atmosphere, all the time you understand that there is only a simulation around, and not an independent world. You can pass through people on the road, meet head-to-head cars rushing towards you without consequences, drive over the rocks on horseback. How does a good open world game differ from a mediocre one? You believe in what is happening. Here, in addition to admiration for some mechanics, there is only an understanding: "This is a simulator."
Physics in the game at the level of projects from the early 2000s. The character runs at a speed of more than 40 km/h, combines and tractors are extremely clumsy, horses stick their heads through the fence – there are too many such moments.

But! I can't bring myself to call this game bad. But it's expensive - almost one and a half thousand rubles, although there are not so many differences from version 17, and the performance is too "turkey".

Otherwise, it is quite possible to spend more than one hundred happy hours here, setting up a household and observing the results of your painstaking work. If you have played similar simulators before, this project is a must-have. If you hesitate, I advise you to study the video walkthrough and make a final decision. And for those who have never encountered farms, but wanted to try their hand, I advise you to give the project a chance - just an hour and a half, and you will feel a surge of strength for further passage. And be sure to go through training, otherwise the first minutes of the gameplay will be filled with complete confusion.\t\t\t\t\t
