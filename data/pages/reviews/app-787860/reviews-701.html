\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI heard a lot of negative things about FS19 but in the end i just had to see for myself and frankly its all true. There is not much to offer besides the John Deer integration and a graphics overhaul. Most of which you could acheive give or take with mods in previous installments.

If FS19 is going to be your first Farm Simulator, then yes I can recommend it.

However if you're coming out of FS17, I have yet to see anything new for returning players --- in fact it plays feels and operates like FS17 with enhancement mods.

I was hoping there would be more vehicular simulation; traction simulation, load bearing limits....

The only improvement I've seen is the level of detail on things like motors and parts.

...this installment is not innovative in any way.

The default maps seem to leave a lot to be desired particularly Ravenport, where your starting farm is cradled by mountains and the farm hands literally cannot navigate your fields because of it. My first 4 minutes in I told a guy to plow the field and another to harvest the starting field while I went and sold some unneeded equipment. I come back and find both the harvester and the tractor parked up a cliff side.

This game is Farming Simulator 17 repainted.

Yes the graphics are good, yes the machinery is still authentic enough but for what you get its not really worth it if you already own Farming Simulator 17.

Truth be told though my first few minutes in I got a little nauseous at the soft shadows everywhere while my seat and camera was bouncing like mad in the cab.

I think I'll go back to FS17 where the gameplay meant more than the graphics.

With games like Euro Truck Simulator 2, Spintires, and previous installments of Farming Simulator out there, I admit expected a lot to compete, but did not expect this little...
