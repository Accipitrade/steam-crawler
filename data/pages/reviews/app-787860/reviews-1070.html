\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is edited from my original posted on 11/22/18. Today is 12/17/18, and this review is meant to be current. First, some improvements have been made since my last post. I will list pros and cons, but first I have to say this: When released, this game was tatered and torn, and for many (including myself) largely, if not totally unplayable unless you want to steer with a mouse/keyboard. IMO totally a polar opposite of what I have come to expect fro Giants. Then, it took them WAY to long to get a patch out, and that patch didn't address many of my concerns. The Steam forum was full of people very upset with the status quo of the game at tha time. And when we voiced those concerns (complaints) in the forum, many others shunned us/shamed us labeling us as whiners, or worse. I realize that the forum - good or bad isn't part of this review, but I want you to get the full picture before investing your money. As it stands now:
Pros - 1) This is an improvement over 17 0 lots of new toys! The "mission" system has been completely overhauled and way better. Many new jobs, the option to use your own equipment to do the job - or lease from the shop for a SMALL price. Much better!
2) The maintenance fee for owning equipment that was previously collected at midnight has also been made much better. Now over time, your equipments needs repair. There is an option (I took it) to buy a maintenance shop and put it on my land. Then to repair equipment, you just drive up and press a button and boom! It's repaired. If you own this shop the repair is cheaper than at the shop, and you can alter vehicles (for example changint ries from single to wide or whatever) - often for no cost - over and over. really nice.
3) There is now a sleep option when it gets dark. Just set the clock and presto, it's morning again - VERY nice for anyone (like me) who never enjoyed working in the dark.
4) The included maps - there are many varyin gopinions on the maps which are included. Some like em, some love em, some hate em, and some like one but not the other. My opinion - I don't particularly like the maps - too hilly for my taste, and the fields are also mostly very hilly. But this is personal taste - nothing wrong with them. You also get the Sugar cane map from 17 as a mod. I didn't like it (the red soil) in 17, and haven't tried it in 19.
5) New options to start your game in terms of what you start with. I like the options, but many others have indicated the need for more sub-options. For example, if you choose to start with a little cash, but 2 fields and equipment, you don't get to choose easy - med - hard modes - they say that this mode is easy - better prices, etc. Overall, I give the options exisitng now a thumbs up.


CONS 1) Even today, nearly one month after release, the whole "controller/wheels" bugs are not fixed. They have made some bugs gone, but not all. THings that are buggy here include wheel settings vor snsitivity conastantly resetting.
2) The default controller settings interfere with "my settings". For example, I use the Saitek Farm Sim wheel and side panel. So I map - say just for example "V" to my side panel 1 button. So if it is "defalut assigned" by Giants, then there is a conflic and it will not work unitl the conflict is resolved. Why did they assign it for me in the first place? It has caused me more problems and time than I care to admit. I finally just deleted ALL default asignments - and it seemed to work as I have very little problem now. But figuring this out was a nighmare. UNFORGIVABLE. And the worst "bug" for me still exists - a month later - and that is the choice to display assigned functions on the screen while I farm is STILL Bugged. They say they are working on it - but - I've given up on them.

The game is playable, and much improved over its release state. But I think Giants released it way too early, and IMO their Quality Control people either don't exist, were not allowed to do their jobs - or were on vacation. Others say in today's age, no one should expect a totally bug-free release - They MAY be right - but I offer two things to that: 1) These bugs were way too big, and 2) it seems to me that games like ETS 2, and ATS have no bugs when they release - just saying. So I have learned one thing from this - I vow to not buy the next FS (likely 21) until it's been out for at least 2 - maybe 3 or 4 months. Giants let me (and a whole lot of peolpe) down on this one, and I quite frankly cannot forgive them.. I am playing it - because I did pay for it and am thus stuck with it. But can I recommend it - No I cannot - still bugs, way too long to fix, and really no apology from Gaints. They do acknowledge there are issues, but no apology - no offer for refunds - etc.\t\t\t\t\t
