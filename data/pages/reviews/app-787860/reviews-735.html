\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall good game, has provided me over 1000 hours of entertainment both on solo play and multi with friends. You will get your moneys worth out of it if you enjoy agriculture at all or always wanted to drive tractors and harvest corn but never could IRL. The game has it's flaws, but what game doesn't. For me, the modding community makes the game, and that's the reason I continue to play. Would it be fun without all the fancy John Deere, Case, and other brands that have been modeled in 3rd party? Probably. But with this being the 6th installment in the series, and something like GPS in tractors and combines still missing from the vanilla game lineup, it comes up a bit disappointing. I shouldn't have to rely on the modding community to provide things a farming Simulator should include. Not to mention the game engine is in need of an overhaul. The bales act the same way they did when 09 was released. And while logging was a nice touch, better be careful and don't close the grapple too tight, or you'll have 6 meter logs flying everywhere. Those are just a few negatives, there are a whole lot of positives. I play realistically, not arcade style, and some of the physics make it frustrating for me and others to really get into at times. Finesse is the key with stacking pallets or bales. And good luck having the bales stay there and not fidget about. I would REALLY like to see a toggle installed in game, or more customization for Realistic and Arcade. Tractors are not able to go up the side of a cliff, be leaning more than 45 degrees, and not roll over. Granted, it's hard for me to write a review off of "vanilla game," as I haven't played that in years. As stated, the modding community keeps the game alive for me, your results may vary. I have more than gotten my money's worth out of FS19. Will I buy the next version of the FS franchise and continue to play? Sure, we all will. Anyone who tells you they won't and has played this game in previous versions (FS17, FS 15...) is lying to you. I will however, be a little slow to slap my money on the table if the next version doesn't bring something ground breaking (no pun intended) to the table.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a base game, it's very fun and relaxing... and quite OCD friendly.

The game certainly has it's limitations on machinery/maps and stuff. But that can be fixed by mods. It's just unfortunate that mods are so heavily dependant on with this game.

Overall I would still say if you're a "Vanilla" enthusiast give the game a try and if you want you can always add mods.

The only two majorly bad things I have to say about the game is the AI that you can hire to help you is... buggy at the best of times. Often leaving massive areas of fields unworked.

And the fact that this game is so heavily dependant on mods and it doesn't have a proper steam workshop like other games do. (They do have a main menu mod area but it only shows mods the developers like not all the new or "cheaty" or OP ones etc. )

Overall Proabably a 6.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to give a comprehensive review about this game now that i have 600+ hours on FS19, and have been playing the FS series since 2011 (logging closer to 1500 hours across all FS titles).

System specs:

Intel i9-9900k overclocked to 5Ghz
ASRock Z390
GSkill Trident Z 32GB
Zotac GeForce RTX 2080 Ti

I think FS19 is a great game, it can provide you many hours of just average fun. Most of my hours were spent on single player saves, while I did try a bit of multiplayer it wasn't for me (details to follow). The more I play this 'game', the more I find things that are glaringly wrong with it or need massive improvements. It is clear that Giants has enjoyed years of relatively uninterupted "farming simulator" gaming market, while there are a few other competitors working on their own farming games, none have really been able to create a dent on Giant's undisputable control over this niche market.

Let me start with the positives.

1) The modding community is what makes this series successful. For some reason Giants decided to spend a lot of resources on acquiring licenses and modeling more in game content, but why? The amount of time and energy players have spent bringing their own equipment into this game is insane. Sure there are some mods out there that... look like they were textured in MS paint and are really just off putting, others are extremely well done. If it weren't for mods, FS wouldn't be as nearly successful as it is.

2) There is a decent amount of economics in the game, mostly with the help of the seasons mod. It's cool that there are a plethora of crops in the game, some more unique than others. I don't think many people do cotton, but its neat that its there. Animal management is meh, it's fun to be able to have a relatively self-sustainable operation by using manure / slurry instead of buying fertilizers.

3) The community for the most part is pretty solid. I mostly spend my time on the FS subreddit where people exchange ideas and ask for tips / help, and I would say most people are really friendly and encouraging to new players.

4) Visual graphics have been improved in FS19 and it shows. I am able to play FS19 on maxed settings, 4k resolution at 120Hz. It isn't a ground breaking graphics improvement, but it at least feels somewhat up to date. Nothing to write home about however.

Here are the negatives.

1) Giants obsession with adding their own licensed content to the game kills other meaningful improvements that the franchise needs. As I mentioned above, modding is this games biggest strength. Thanks to mods like Seasons, vehicle realism, and global company, we're able to have more realistic aspects to FS19. I feel like the "simulator" part of "Farming Simulator" doesn't do any justice - as this feels more like an arcade mobile game now than an actual simulator. Reasons to follow.

2) Lack of soil health! If you play without seasons mod, you can plant soybeans every 'season' and enjoy absurd profit margins, rinse and repeat and you will soon find yourself burned out and not wanting to play. The implementation of weeds in FS19 was a nice intro into this area, but there still needs to be more work. Applying three layers of fertilizers or herbicide and calling your fields protected is hardly realistic and doesn't imply "simulator".

3) No dynamic texturing or geometry. This might be might biggest complaint about the game. The only time fields look good is when crop is growing, but if you look at a field that you recently work (whether that is plowed, cultivated, seeded, or freshly harvested) you will notice repeated textures every few in-game units. This is very immersion breaking to me. It is just an eye sore especially with big fields! Why not at least let the textures rotate every few units so that they don't all look the same? (Easier said than done, I know).

In addition to the horrible texturing, ground terrain needs to be able to mold and change based on a variety of impacts. I know that Giants has mentioned that they've tried experiementing with this system but if i recall correctly, they mentioned "it doesn't work for them" or something along those lines. I call . There are smaller studios with much smaller budgets who got dynamic ground geometry to work for them. It's the single most painful part of this franchise. What is the point of changing tires if the game doesn't take into consideration soil compaction?

4) Finances & economics, also need drastic overhauls. Forget about drawing on loans in this game, you might as well edit your farms.xml and just give yourself money there. Using the ingame loan function, you get a fixed 300% interest rate (i could be wrong about that exact percentage) and you will forever pay principal without ever paying down the loan itself. There is also no such thing as buying used equipment, and leasing is seldom worth it for large equipment (this can be dependent on what type of equipment, how long you use it for, and what you are using it for). Also, the game's price fluctuations are just ambiguous and you don't feel like you have to compete on a market. The other 'farmers' that are on the map make zero impact on prices. Seasons mod helps address this quite a bit.


Synopsis; i enjoy FS. I would probably be farming on my cousins' farm in Denmark if I wasn't allergic to everything. But I feel like this is more of an arcade game than an actual simulator.
