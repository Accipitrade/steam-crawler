\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Exact same gameplay mechanics as previous Farming Simulator series titles. Just a much prettier wrapper, additional crop, animals, and a handful of popular previous mod offerings built into the game such as crop destruction.

It's definitely worth it if you're an FS enthusiast. The game world seems much more real and alive. You can see storms approaching in the distance, have crows following your seeders/harvesters, hear the blaring horn of passing trains, watch your crops sway in the wind, ect. The game world is truly alive now.

Other than that though, in all honesty, it's pretty much the same game just with a prettier wrapper.

It comes with two built-in maps and one optional map that was previously a paid DLC from FS 17; Estancia Lapacho. Of the two built-in maps, Ravenport supposedly represents the USA but for some reason the devs turned it into a giant junkyard. Even the farmhouse looks like an abandoned trash heap, almost as if the dev's were deliberately giving a middle finger salute to their American players.

The game has many features that can be toggled on or off depending on just how much you want the game to feel like a struggle; crop withering, vehicle grime, crop destruction when driving over it, required periodic plowing, lime enrichment, weed control, ect.

The job system was revamped so that it's not an instant ATM anymore. Now you have to use or lease your own equipment to do jobs for others.

You can now build your own farming home base from the ground up, placing various buildings, sheds, and silo's in whatever layout you want, but the very limited amount of flat land will still pretty much dictate choices for you.

A lot of things were made harder in the game; you still have to dump certain crops on the ground instead of silo's or storage bins like in previous FS games, you have to buy and transport seed and fertilizer from the store, vehicle maintainance is a non-disableable requirement now, and you can't store silage at biogas plants anymore unless you own them.

I'm sure that mod authors will bring in a lot of quality of life improvement mods to make life easier for us such as placeable seed and fertilizer depots, all-inclusive silo's, ect, but, and probably the biggie, there probably won't be a GPS mod for this version since the author is now employed by a rival game development house.

So it's a hit and miss recommendation. The world is very beautiful and alive, but more difficult to justify as a casual gaming experience anymore due to the increased difficulty and demands on your time and resources to get anything done. Your mileage may vary compared to earlier versions of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThought I’d just put my thoughts out there amongst these very mixed reviews. I’ve been playing FS since the very first one, almost a decade ago now, and I don’t agree with ‘the biggest advancement in the series’ - I think FS15 takes that one, although there are a lot of very welcome changes here. Anyway;

Visuals, Models & Environment
Graphics are the best they’ve ever been and the lighting is just as good. The vehicle models also look excellent, and details like moving pedals and seat suspension are very nice. The new maps are very detailed, if a bit smaller than usual. I haven’t tried many of the larger harvesters yet, but I suspect navigating some of the small roads to and from my farm will be challenging. I like the added touches such as crops bending under machinery and birds landing in fields.

Physics and Vehicles
The physics engine seems to have been overhauled and seems more realistic than before - something which is especially noticeable in suspension. I think acceleration and power has been greatly improved - the vehicles are much less jerky than before. FS19 has the largest vehicle roster of the series so far, and it’s great to see so much faithfully reproduced machinery. I know many fans will be very pleased about the addition of John Deere - I’m glad it’s in the game now but I wasn’t crying out for it before.

Gameplay
There are a few new gameplay mechanics and a few new smaller things too. I like the new starting modes - Beginner, Farm Manager, and Start from scratch, giving a bit more than just Easy, Medium, Hard. I like the new land purchasing system that gives more customisation options with buildings etc. However, I do think a lot of the placeables are way overpriced, such as the farm house (which doesn’t serve much more than a decorative purpose), and especially animal enclosures. Tens of thousands for a chicken coop, which is effectively a small shed and some fencing is too much. It’s good to see even more crops being added, such as cotton (quite a high-end crop as only the £350k+ module plus can harvest it) and oats. Weeds and lime are nice as they seem to make the crop cycle a little less rinse-and-repeat, and can be disabled if you like. Horses are a nice addition, but I’m not sure I’ll get much use out of them. I’d prefer to travel around by pickup or Gator, and I’d rather farm cows or sheep. I think the new menus look much slicker, and I like that the vast amount of machinery has finally been split into more sub-categories (particularly tractors).

Frustrations, Issues & Bugs
- There seems to be an issue with the ambient light mechanic where it will not fade smoothly between times of day - instead just jumping to different light levels.
- I have experienced some vehicle bugs, specifically where vehicles are jerky at low speeds (more so in reverse) and their wheels seem to lock up occasionally.
- As I mentioned above, some of the placeables are unreasonably expensive
- I use the Saitek wheel and side panel, and for some reason all of the default button mapppings are different in FS19 to what they were in FS17, and FS19 seems to not save my deadzone settings...

To conclude, yes, there are issues, as with every launch, but I’m sure GIANTS will iron those out in due course. I have thoroughly enjoyed my time in FS19 so far, and would definitely recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
