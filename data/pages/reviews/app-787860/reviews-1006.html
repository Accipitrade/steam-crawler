\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFarming Simulator 19, another title in the decade old Farming Simulator franchise. Does this one meet expectations?

UPSIDES
The game looks beautiful, and has a lot of potential with the mod support.

The new customizable farms are fantastic, same with being able to see the customizations on your vehicle before buying it.

Giants finally got John Deere in the game, though they still haven't gotten any car brands for the pickups. Cotton and oats join the party of crops, along with horses and birds making their debut. The vehicles also lose paint over time, which is a nice touch. I also love the new mission system and land purchasing.

DOWNSIDES
And then there's the issues. First of all, the vehicle handling. This is a big one. Turning is not much different from Farming Simulator 2009. Driving a truck, which should feel like driving a two-ton vehicle, instead feels like driving a Hot Wheels toy car. A truck filled with 39 tons of grain drives like a car towing a shopping cart. This is dissappointing, as Euro Truck Simulator 2, a 7 year old game, handles like the real thing.

Glitches are a big problem in this game. Apart from glitches other reviewers mention, there's the problem where trailers wil run off if not hooked up to a tractor, while FS17, a two year old game, doesn't have that problem. The wool harvester will suddenly have the mechanical part inside the chamber clip through the roof. Will Giants fix these glitches? Well, not after one announcement where they said they will focus on DLC and only fix game breaking glitches.

With that being said, they can improve all this by improving the physics and keeping vehicles from FS17 in the basegame, and fixing the bugs plaguing the game. If they do this, I can finally reccommend this game.

+ Beautiful graphics
+ Massive selection of vehicles
+ 3D customization
+ Customizable farms
+ Vehicles now rust
+ John Deere is now in the game
+ Works on most computers
+ Mod support


- Game feels bland
- New maps are boring
- No gearbox
- Vehicle handling is still awful
- Horse handling is as awful as vehicle handling
- Background is overly blurred
- Unrealistic physics
- Glitches and bugs
- Barely any improvements on realism
- Traffic is still slow
- Old vehicles have been downgraded to mods
- Expensive DLC
- Some mods on the Farming Simulator website aren't on the ingame ModHub

This game was a major dissappointment for me. If anything can save this game, it's the modding community.

3/10, wait for sale or buy the downpriced FS17.

Edit 1: I found some mods on the website's ModHub that enhances gameplay. But when I look for them on the ingame ModHub, they're nowhere. You can't even use the search function to find them, either.
