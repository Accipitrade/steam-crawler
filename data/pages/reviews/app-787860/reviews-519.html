Firstly, for some reason Steam only has 11, 13, 15, 17, and 19. (All/Most of the odd numbered games.) Looking at 2011, the graphics seemed veeeeeeeery outdated, and washed out. I really didn't like the majority of tractors in it, and it's base-game lacked in variety of vehicles, attachments, etc...

2013 had similar graphics to 2011, but much more base-game vehicle variety. Other than that, it didn't really have that much more to show that future versions didn't have. The only reason I greatly considered it was mainly due to nostalgia. This version and 2014 (which Steam doesn't have) are the two that I fully remember, and I had become more interested in Minecraft by the time 2015 came out. Speaking of which:

2015 had a huuuge improvement on graphics, and there were much more features including the ability to wash your vehicles (which for some reason I was greatly excited about when it was released, I know, I'm a nerd). I was almost completely sold on getting 15 (money-wise, it was cheaper at the time of purchase), but the one thing that stopped me was the tractors. (The following is mainly a preference thing, but is worth stating nonetheless.) I personally disliked almost all of the tractors in 2015, because they were just a little too different. Considering all of the previous games up to this point, I would've picked 13, because they are the most familiar to me. I personally have never seen any farming equipment like the ones seen in 2015, but I'm sure somewhere in the world they exist.

2017 was probably the most disappointing to me (however at this point I had already looked at 2019, so I might have been biased here.). There was seemingly no difference in graphics, which I would expect considering it is two years newer than 2015. In fact, I'm inclined to say that the graphics might have gotten a bit worse, but I may be wrong about that, because I only looked at the Steam pics for 2017. However, I personally think the tractors were improved style-wise in 2017.

Now, for why I settled with 2019. Graphics-wise, 2019 was the best (which makes sense, it's the 2nd newest in the series at the time I'm writing this). Ordinarily graphics wouldn't have meant much to me since I am a mostly simplistic person, but I got a new computer with a much better graphics card, so I thought that if it met my expectations then it would be worth it. I purchased this game on sale for 10 US dollars (which is the only reason I even considered it) and boy was that a huuuuge bargain! This game runs great on my old laptop as well as my own computer even with the graphics set so high. The tractors in the game are nothing but satisfactory (although it still shocks me that this is the 1st one featuring John Deere)! However like the previous versions there are flaws, the biggest one being the main menu. I personally don't remember the old ones, but this one isn't really helpful. I still don't know how to get into the graphics/volume settings (which is one of the biggest things you'd want in a main menu), and the tutorials inside the main menu don't really help at all (especially with controls). Whenever I came across something I didn't know how to do, I ALWAYS had to look it up (which wasn't too annoying to find the answers). The other really big problem is the controls, which are some of the weirdest I've ever seen. For example, Numpad 1 and 3 are the signals for the tractors, and the HOME button (not Windows, etc...) is the amber lights. 0 is the horn, but other than those the controls mostly make sense. I like the idea of being able to create your own property and all (I don't know if this was in previous games or not), but when you place something down, it is impossible to manipulate the terrain around it, which can create ridiculous bumps in front of your vehicle sheds. It would also be quite nice if there were other properties other than the starting one with room for things like farmhouses and sheds, etc..

That was my review for all of the Farming Simulator games on Steam, and why I picked 2019, but some of my preferances likely won't apply to you, so do what you wish, but they all go on sale during Christmas/New Years, so if you're getting an older game, wait and you'll be able to get Gold/Titanium edition for only 10ish bucks! I hope this was helpful if anyone decided to waste 10 minutes reading this.\t\t\t\t\t
