\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought Farming Simulator 19 on a whim during a midweek sale; I wanted a relaxing simulator and there were few games in my vast library that I knew would fit the criteria. I have little prior experience with farming sims; I played Farming Simulator 13 for around twenty hours, became frustrated with its lack of direction, and ended up dumping it because I had a technical issue that the developers didn't help with on the forum. Thus, I was a little leery of supporting this one.

78 hours of gametime later, I'm in love. Farming Simulator 19 offers you many tutorials and aids to help newcomers learn how to grow crops (of which there are different types of harvesters, sometimes extra steps, etc.) and raise animals. Sometimes it still shows lack of direction--I spent five hours on a starter map looking for the next step in the tutorial. The game wouldn't let me freeplay until finishing it, but it was missing. So I restarted on a freeplay map and figured out everything from scratch. Still, this wasn't overly stressful; the game advances at a relaxing pace to where you have plenty of time to prepare for the next venture (new field, new equipment, new crop/animal, etc.) and research its requirements online. Trial and error is to be expected. Early on, I almost ran out of money because a new field I bought already had sugar beets planted, so I decided to lease all corresponding equipment to harvest it since I couldn't afford to buy it. Leasing everything cost me $23K when the harvest granted $26K in profit. Thankfully all was well, but had I any idea how complicated it would be beforehand, I would have waited for the AI to harvest it before purchasing the lot. The game doesn't warn you when you're about to make stupid decisions, so with each major purchase, you need to do a lot of research!

In fact, Farming Simulator 19 never tells you what to do. It is a true simulation that allows you every freedom. Everything is customizable, even a lot of crucial game options. You can choose whether or not your equipment gets visibly dirty, weeds grow with every harvest, lime and/or periodic plowing is required to maintain yield, and you can even quicken or slow down time. This last point is my favorite because it allows you to play the game at any pace you want, from where 24 hours passes in 12 minutes (120x speed) to where it's based on real-time, so each in-game minute is one real-life minute. I'd often play in real-time until all my chores on my land were done, then do missions from other farmers to make money until there were no more jobs I wanted to do (Hint: harvesting jobs make the best profit, for you get the job money and a percentage of the harvest income). Then, "fast forward" until other fields develop, do more missions, etc. In this way, you can manipulate the world to where you can maximize the amount of money you make in a day to further protect you from bankruptcy. Because you can hire helpers to do many jobs for you, you can focus on doing those you like best (say, harvesting) while getting the AI to do the others. All of these jobs can be happening simultaneously. As long as you have the equipment and time, you can be working the land all over the map at once with aid from workers.

I grew up on a ranch, and out of all the pets I've ever had (more than two dozen species), chickens were by far the most fun. Farming Simulator 19 is one of the few games that allows me to experience just a bit of that joy as an adult living in the suburbs, and I'm grateful to it. You can also raise horses, sheep, cows, and pigs, though you can only name horses. I would have loved the option to name all animals to give it a more personal touch. Additionally, I hope in the future Giants Software considers building upon the helper system. It's great to have helpers, but they don't have names and aren't hired for good. I think it'd be nice for Farming Simulator to go the way of SCS Software's Truck Simulator series (Euro and American), to where you could hire and manage actual employees, even assigning certain jobs for them to do automatically. The only other request I'd have is for more crops and corresponding equipment. I'd love to tend to orchards or even just grow something more exciting than the roster of grains (and few others like potatoes, sugar beets).

In summary, Farming Simulator 19 is a perfect gateway into farming sims for players looking to start. You can play your way: actively, by tending to every task yourself and fast-forwarding through growth phases, or passively, by assigning AI to tasks and slowing time down to a crawl. If you love tending to and building a business in a relaxing atmosphere, Farming Simulator 19 is one of the few games that captures that unique feeling. This game sits next to those in the Truck Simulator franchise as one of my go-to relaxing simulators. I look forward to seeing Giants Software continue to innovate and grow the series.\t\t\t\t\t
