\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou know what, this game is a lot like Euro Truck Simluator in a lot of ways. I love both games and have spent hours on them, but you get to a certain point, and start to feel like it's a massive missed opportunity. That 'is that it?' feeling. The UI feels rushed and an afterthought (would be nice if you could mouse-over the random symbols to find out what crop they are, at a minimum), stats are perfunctory (for example a proper balance sheet detailing assets as well as cash money, historical data, whatever), the world is limiting (when you plough a new field it's invisible on the map, you can't remove junk on your land, you can't really store or stack anything, half the buildings promise much but don't actually do anything) and so many aspects of developing and managing your farm are ignored when they could easily be incorporated into the game just by building a slightly more complex database. It's lazy. Why can't we see average yield, revenue per crop, revenue / yield per harvest /whatever? Different fertilisers with different properties. I'm not expecting Dwarf Fortress but a little more attention to detail would make everything so much better.

Presumably they could have continued to develop it or even, perish the thought, release a finished product in the first place but (a) all resource is dedicated to producing re-skinned version of the existing vehicles and charging 15 quid a pop for it, (b) a new verion is out soon so why bother making this one any good? There are obvious well known bugs that have been ignored since launch, but here, buy a different brand of tractor. And it's not really a simulator as a result. It's drive a tractor, put various stuff in fields, harvest it all, rinse, repeat, possibly with larger tractors. That's it And the physics is terrible. The traffic AI is dumb and massive farm machinery pinballs off family saloons like they are made of breeze blocks. Would be great to see other farmers actually working their fields rather than.. well, magic. The graphics are average, the fields don't render properly in the distance even on max settings, to the point where it's worse looking beyond about 20 feet than Minecraft. It's miles behind the curve in almost every way.

But still, it somehow works. Basically it's farm ASMR. I'm bought it because sale and Clarkson. I've spent hours on it because OCD. It's great. But I still feel sad for what it could have been with just a little more care.\t\t\t\t\t

The mods are a big part of the game and I was happy the producers embraced it.

+ Mods are easily downloaded from INSIDE the game
+ Graphics are very realistic
+ Tons of stuff to do all the time ( missions , contracts , your farm , animals, ... )
+ You can have AI workers at the farm to help you and give some more immersive gameplay.

- AI works okay. But nothing great, they seem to very clumsy and miss spots in the field and stop If theres a tree in front of them.
- I had a few bugs such as crop not growing and itens bugged in the floor.
- Gravity is not your friend in this game.\t\t\t\t\t
