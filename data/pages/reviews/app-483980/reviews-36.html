\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhenever people think of a masterpiece of a video game people would usually think of games like The Last Of Us, Detroit Become Human, or Dark Souls. I agree, however, I also would include games like Mad Father. Games that gained an audience and set forth an entire generation of gamers, Youtubers, and..just video games. I remember back when I was a kid, playing this game, watching others play this game, memorizing every detail this game had to offer. From it's soundtrack, to it's story telling, it's character designs, everything about this game brings me a wave of nostalgia that makes me want to go back and play it all again. Seeing it remastered brings me to back to when I first played it, and I couldn't say there are games more deserving of a remaster than these ones.

If you have not played Mad Father you are missing out on an incredible piece of gaming history, along with missing out on an ageless story that continues to impress me, and many other gamers, almost 10 years later.\t\t\t\t\t

Mad Father (MF) is brought to you by the developer behind the occult hit classic Misao. Like its predecessor, MF shares a similar fate by being first a freeware but forges its own path by being remastered in 2016 and then remade in 2020. It is precisely the latter in which this review will be based on.

If you've played Misao, you'll be right at home by the almost merciless game design and storytelling centered on expounding everyday relationships but in very moving ways. In fact, you may have even seen the connection between the two games! But if you're totally new- worry not, it's two separate narratives with a shared universe of sorts, so prior lore isn't a must! Now then with introductions out the way, welcome to the family~


"Blood's Thicker Than Water..."
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2344903547

The Gist:
Since she was but a little girl, Aya Drevis has caught wind of his father's freak-of-nature experiments in the basement. She may not know the exact gory details or rationale, but decides it's for the best or so she thought... On the anniversary of her mother's death, however, all hell breaks loose and she puts her love to the test!

! This way, I'll always be your girl!!

[+] PROS;

(a) Papa Don't Please!
The devil-may-care attitude to exploration persists as once again you are tasked with finding out what the fuss is about while putting some spirits to rest, only this time it's set in a mansion! There are diaries, letters to peruse and gems to collect- which curiosity just might get the best of ya you after being kept in the dark about things but would it kill you to continue ignoring? If fetch quests and puzzles don't really appeal to you, there's a chainsaw to clear boards and generally break stuff to release all the pent-up frustration! Still listening? Great!!

But the greatest thrill, I mean terror- comes from the wayward spirits that will prey on your imagination by coming up with a plethora of ways to rattle you. It could be a sudden noise caused by you bumping into the environment, the camera zooming in or things being moved around.

If that's not enough, there are also (avoidable) heart-pumping, grave encounters where you have to enter QTE prompts to survive being assaulted out of nowhere. You also must keep an eye on the health gauge that mysteriously appears at the top during those sequences. Seriously, backtracking can grow quite stressful... Don't you dare avert those big beautiful eyes!!

(b) Can't Put A Price on...
My first run took 4 hours. So, for a $10 game- 20% off now, but its worth it hands down for the "blood" mode alone. Unlocked after the true ending where in it, you play as an adult Aya revisiting the mansion nightmare and learn even more truths!! It technically is a 2nd run of the campaign only with a new character model but like her newfound insight and perspective on life, the scares were modified as well!

As for the additional content, there are beautifully rendered backgrounds and illustration art to feast your eyes on or a OST player that's well, music to your ears~ Don't forget to check out the character models. Perhaps create a doll of your choosing as an icon for you to drool over every time you pause the game?

(c) Playing House
The story it tells and how it is told is easy to follow- being something everyone in the past and in the distant future can relate to. I loved its emotional appeal and how it elicits a sense of empathy from us through the use of flashbacks which never gets old. You can never expect what is literally tossed your way and that is startling in itself. I kinda wish my dad was this messed up but no, his is just plain old disappointment and thinking he can lie through his teeth.

There isn't much in the way of character development but Aya demonstrates the most progress from being strong, kind and smart for her young age to a sensible adult. Also, it's nice to see the recurring role of the enigmatic Ogre, who seems like he's on your side but his motives are questionable at best.

(d) The Things We Do For-
The game is actually already frightening with some creepy setups and jumpscare-like adrenaline rushes. But I think the game's tackling of the "family always look out for one another, no matter what" is undeniably hard-hitting in a few ways.

One, it made me consider that of my own household and how bonkers unconditional love operates by! While Aya's case is a bit of a tall tale (or is it) the point is resounding- everyone would probably withhold evidence that can implicate a loved one, if given the chance. And can I just add that willful ignorance is devastating enough but the fact something much worse goes on under the same roof and has lasted for so long, only rubs salt in the festering wound.

Two, I like the subversion of expectations in the title because going by the synopsis and literal interpretation of the phrase, it should be clear who the baddie is right? Oops, there I go running my mouth off again~

Three, profundity of "blood" mode illustrates how one event can linger and screw us up for our remaining lifetime. Or how the introspection of said deep-seated trauma is gained only as one matures. There may be nothing that can be done by then except give us some kind of closure, whatever that ends up being.

Finally, the idea that blood links people forever comes up quite a bit but there are many different families out there, with their various expression of love- the way they've always known from their own predisposition. Are we in any position to judge?


[-] CONS;

(i) Everyone Has A Limit.
What bothered me the most was its user interface Specifically how interacting with the same objects mid-objective will bring up the "mission" reminder as it's so called each time, even when there's already a tab allocated for active missions in the pause menu. Moreover, the enemy AI appear as though they have a short in their circuit by mindlessly charging at you even if it means running into that wall to no avail.

Notes that have been read lose their shimmer but then when you exit and enter again, the effect returns. Therefore, the game comes off amateurish in those regard and kills the immersive atmosphere it's got going on. Oh and I also got a bug (I think) for "Kaitai Shinsho". But since it doesn't get in the way of anything substantial, no points have been docked!


"Now, Who's A Good Girl?!"
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2347312309

The title makes you think its shock value is lost right off the bat but it's in fact amplified by a most familiar dilemma and traumatic events well before the eventual showdown with the titular antagonist. Better still, the remake's price is justified by to-die-for extra goodies~ Though not nearly as torturous or trigger happy with its instant kills as Misao, the plot nonetheless deals a fatal blow by letting you see how feigning ignorance is just as guilty as the oppressor! That, and of course the twist that comes with the unlockable mode make this adventure one you can't live down.

There's practically no cons to this game too, 'cept for a minor gripe of mine involving the unintuitive AI, nagging HUD and objectives. It should be clear by now that Sen's signature of blending in the paranormal with genuine relations make him a force to be reckoned with! His continued dedication and love in updating each iteration of his work, so as to keep up with the time and in accessible formats truly add layered depth to the term "classic".

10/10

What could possibly come next in Sen's bizarre and twisted horror anthology!? I feel squeamish already!\t\t\t\t\t
