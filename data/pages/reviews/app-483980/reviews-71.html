1/7/21 UPDATE

In light of the refinements made with the latest update, I'm changing my review slightly.

Update Pros
+The updated graphics look spectacular
+New story beats in the Blood scenario make good on foreshadowing in the original game that never got any resolution, and are not retconned
+The game now has a "Mission" list that keeps track of any tasks you haven't completed yet, which prevents you from having to write anything down
+The game now has arrows that point to places on the map that are accessible rooms, helping the player to not overlook anything


Update Cons
-Even if you found all the gems and beat the original game, once you install the update, you'll have to beat the game AND get all gems once more to access the Blood scenario
-The Blood scenario is a rehash of the same puzzles as the original game. Even the gems are hidden in the same locations
-The Blood scenario fleshes out Aya's backstory, but overlooks another main character who I would have expected to get more attention considering the end of the If scenario

All in all, everything I say below is still true (although I think the stairs glitch has been removed.) The game is still worth full price, increased or no. And if you're a returning fan looking forward to playing the update, temper your expectations about the Blood scenario and be prepared to replay the game to get to it.

=======================================================================
Mad Father is a horror puzzle RPG Maker game in the vein of Ib and The Witch's House. It tasks you with saving your deranged father from the horrors he himself created.

Pros
+A surprisingly in-depth and gripping storyline. Characters are varied and well developed
+Both the mandatory puzzles and the optional puzzles to obtain collectables reward the player for quick thinking and paying attention to their surroundings. Fair and sensible solutions keep the game from feeling cheap
+Multiple endings, and each ending has branching paths on top of that
+Frequent save points; not once did I ever have to backtrack when I wanted to save
+Many CGs
+Varied soundtrack with many memorable pieces
+Great price for the amount of content, even when full priced

Cons
-The game is not scary. Those who are looking for a mortifying horror experience will not find it here. The game is, however, as disturbing as it should be, and does not disappoint in that category.
-The later parts of the game have many instant death events that can easily become frustrating
-One of the beginning areas has two enemies that chase you, which should be trivial to avoid, but tile glitching on the stairs makes it extremely easy to get pinned into the wall and die

Full price or no, any horror game or RPG Maker game fan owes it to themselves to give Mad Father a try. Its creativity in storytelling, characters, and puzzles is both memorable and highly enjoyable! Should there ever be a sequel, I'll be one of the first in line to pick it up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Mad Father" is brilliant RPG Maker horror game. It's a story about Aya - young girl, daughter of a... well "mad" scientist. One night, due to certain curse, all of his experiments come to life, and Aya is the one who need to not only survive this madness, but also try to save her father.

This is one of the best known RPG Maker horror game - it is (or was?) available for free and I've played it then. Steam version has slightly different art, there's also "save yourself" mini game added. I don't remember all the story details well, so I'm not sure if anything had changed in that regard. Still, I think this game is well worth the money and it's nice that the creators of "Mad Father" are getting more recognition and hopefully, that they are making well deserved money.

"Mad Father" has amazing atmosphere, intriguing storyline and impressive attention to details. It captures all the best qualities of RPG Maker horror games, so if you're a fan of that type of games, you definitely don't want to miss out on that one.\t\t\t\t\t

Very briefly:

"Papa! Papa! Mommy .... .... Mommy noooo! Papa... KYA! KYA! KYA! Papa~ .... .... DINKLEBURG"


WHAT I ENJOYED ABOUT THIS GAME

Other than the low cost and fantastic storyline?

The play style is simple yet effective. Achievements are relatively easy to obtain as well.

I loved the playstyle and how very subtle decisions could affect the overall turn of the game. The puzzles aren't difficult and if you're paranoid like me, its okay, there's save points around most corners!

I love that this game encorporates so much 'horror' into itself. Since it's RPG we all know a lot of it won't be scary, but it has it's fair share of jump scares to get the heart attacks coming.

This game has so much potential, it literally blows my mind.


WHAT THE GAME LACKED

There is very little lacking in this game. My only wish is that it could be longer with a longer and more defined storyline, a little polishing here and there, and it'd be perfect!


CONCLUSION

I highly recommend this game. 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t
