Mad Father isn't just a scary game, it's a tale of twisted love

Since it's the Halloween season and the remake is coming up, I figured it was finally time to review the RPG Maker horror classic that made my childhood.

Mad Father is about a little girl named Aya Drevis, who knows that her father, "the Doctor"'s, experiment aren't exactly humane, which she keeps quiet about because she loves her father, and that he has a relationship with his assistant, Maria, which she isn't happy about because she still loves her late mother. Things get crazy quickly once her mother's spirit comes to drag her father down to the underworld with her, and Aya sets out on a journey to save her father.

It sounds pretty simple, just a short, scary game to take up a bit of your time, but it's so much more than that. It's a tale of twisted love that will tug at your heartstrings.

Aya loves her father. She knows he hurts people, kills people, but she doesn't do anything about it because it's her father who she loves. And why wouldn't she love her father? She's a ten year old girl and he's her parent, and a very loving one at that, so why shouldn't she love him? That's why she does everything to save him, because despite his monstrous actions, Aya loves her father.

Maria loves the Doctor. She willingly assists the Doctor with his "experiments" and helps him cover it all despite seeming good-natured, because she loves him. And why wouldn't she? He took her in off the streets when she was on the brink of death and made her part of the family. He saved her, and gave her a home, so why shouldn't she love him? That's why she helps him with his horrible deeds, despite deriving no pleasure from them, because despite it all, Maria loves the doctor.

And Aya's mother...we're lead to believe she dragged her husband into the depths of hell because she was jealous. If she can't have him no one can. Because she loves her husband.

And ultimately, despite doing everything in their power to help and save the Doctor, when he turns against them (because there's no way he wouldn't) Aya and Maria have to find love in each other, despite their past differences.

It's tragic, it's sad, and it makes you feel for them because this is what happens when love is used as a weapon to manipulate and harm.

It's also worth noting it's not a very difficult game. Controls are simple, and the puzzles aren't too difficult to figure out.

Now, about the ending, A lot of people feel the ending ruins it, but personally, I think it fits well. Aya never condemns her father's actions. She knows about them, and she knows that they're bad, but she doesn't condemn them, only running once she realizes she's about to become a victim herself. She also agrees to burn down the mansion ONLY because it's what her mother wanted, not because Dio said her father's actions shouldn't continue. Also, if you look in the right places, you can find that she inherited the trait of killing small animals from her father. The foreshadowing is there, so the ending checks out.

In the end, YES you should play Mad Father. It's a classic that shaped the RPG Maker horror genre, and tells a really compelling story with not too difficult but still engaging gameplay. Definitely check this one out.\t\t\t\t\t

As of this review, I have gotten all achievements, all endings, all gallery pictures. I have completed the game 100%

Mad Father is a special game to me, for it was the first indie horror game I played, along with Misao, back in the early 2010s.
I would absolutely say this version is the best among them, having more content and replay value.

MF is a blast to play, even if you're completely new to these types of games. As someone whos played this game to Hell and back (almost quite literally), I can safely say that this game is worthy of your $5.
After I beat the game the first time, I played GAME AGAIN mode and beat it in an hour, it's quite a thrill!

The characters are just as complex and amazing as I remember, the story is largely the same, with little twists I don't seem to remember from the original, but I absolutely love that.

All in all, I absolutely highly recommend this game to those who enjoyed and loved the original!
