\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMad father is a chilling 8 bit game. It follows Ann as she explores her mansion in search for her father, while avoiding ghouls, and malformed monsters along the way. The story is intriguing as the developments keep you motivated to solve puzzles, and find out what lies beneath the basement.

The gameplay is similar to most horror 8 bit games, but this adventure feels more like a point and click adventure as players must explore the mansion, interact with characters, discover and interact with clues to solve puzzles. Mad Father might look rather like a retro game with its pixel art aesthetic, but that hardly stops the experience from getting dark and twisted. Your decisions through the game matter depending on optional actions to determine your ending towards the good, or bad end.

The dark storyline manages to entice players immediately, which makes every threat against Aya feel even more troubling. Interestingly enough, her childish manner is even used as a gameplay element itself. After beating this game to a 98% completion I would say it is very similar to Corpse party, is an expansive thriller that heavily encapsulates you into the mysteries of the mansions, and your established link to the events of Aya.
The story is short, but enticing I would recommend it to anyone who is open to thrillers and 8 bit adventures. I personally rate Mad Father as a 9/10, for its replayability, interacting story, character establishment and mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I'm from a kind of horror bizarro world...

Before touching this game, I played a newer title Viviette. I hated it. It had great art direction, great ambiance, and the scary house had a nice mystery behind it. Then, it feel apart hard by characters I can hardly remember and puzzles you needed a guide for at times.

This game was more what I wanted.

While Mad Father wasn't as colorful as Viviette, or have as good ambiance, it more than made up for with characters I could remember. Characters with story I could follow and feel something for when they died. Instead of people that hardly got any clear story beats until the ending. That brother and sister of Viviette story really could have been told so much better with Mad Father's style of direction.

I could dare say if Viviette's and Mad Father's developers got together, they might make the perfect horror game! But what do I know on horror bizarro world...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMad Father is A Great game for a RPGMaker I streamed this game on twitch a few days ago recommended from a friend.

First off I have to say the story line is well done in this game graphically for a RPG MAKER title most RPGM games are really blurry on the pixels and are completed in a matter of a couple of hours. This game however was different, with great character visuals I found myself blown away with the puzzle design and the amount of effort put in from the developer(s) obviously this game has had a re haul from the original version I played a few years back which makes me believe that they wanted the people who bought this game on steam to have the best possible Experience they could offer using the Engine they were using.

Storyline was Rather interesting for sure however there is a few downfalls for players that Aren't used to the whole "Reading" withing game types. There are a lot of scenes where you have to read a lot in this game and that is where most the game goes I'm afraid however if you read comics or manga's or even watch anime I'm sure this won't be a issue for you and you can enjoy the game. However if you're tired and just want to complete the game or just don't want to be sitting through all the text, You can easily skip this by just mashing you're mouse button. However I wouldn't advise this as the storyline is one of the best things about RPG MAKER titles and you would be missing out on a lot if you didn't give the story a chance.

Pros-
Graphically Impressive
Story Line is very good
A lot of unique puzzles that are fun
Different Enemies to avoid
Easter Egg available within the game.

Cons-
A lot of Filler Within the story which could have been avoided.
Some glitch spots when encountering enemies
sometimes like's to hold you're hand when doing puzzles (easy puzzle)
not enough antagonist involvement.

Rating 3.8/5

Even though I enjoyed this game and it is one of the best RPG MAKER Games I have played I just can't give it that high of a rating. it's just a lot of filler hold's you back from discovering the main plot and the game just doesn't dive to much into the antagonist's role that often just triggering flash backs and happy moments. I was hoping for more dark and horrific moments from this game. as a horror I have to say it isn't that effective but as an adventure it done a very good job
