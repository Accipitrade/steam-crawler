\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSince a new version has been released on November 5th, 2020, I guess I got to redo this review. I will be comparing this to the old original free to play game from 2012 as I review this game.

First, let's start off with the game's plot. Our protagonist is Aya Drevis who lives in a mansion in Germany with her father who is a mad scientist researching for eternal beauty alongside with his assistant Maria, a freeloader. Despite the fact that many innocent people and animals were taken in and killed, Aya still loves her mad father.
On the night before the anniversary of the death of Aya's mother, a curse befalls the mansion and Aya's father gets taken hostage by the spirit of her deceased mother. Aya sets off on an adventure to save her father while encountering the undead corpses of his test subjects and killer dolls.

As for gameplay, there are a lot of changes that make this 2020 remake almost a completely new experience from the original 2012 game. While the many puzzles are the same, they've been altered making the process of solving them different. One detail I always like is that the game takes the playable character's knowledge into account. Even if the player knows what the code for a safe is, the game won't let them input it until Aya finds the hint to it.
When it comes to enemy encounters, the original 2012 game is so ret@rdedly easy that it barely offered any challenge. In moments where Aya has to escape a baddie's clutches, all the player needed to do was button mashing. In the remake, that has been changed. Now, the player has to input sequences of directions correctly.
And that's not all; The 2020 remake has also added Quick Time Events and it has implemented them correctly. The original 2012 game has traps that lead to instant death. You fall in them and it's game over. Now, you have a chance to save yourself with a good reflex should that ever happen in this 2020 remake.

The 2020 remake has some other neat additions. There is a missions list on the pause menu which is there in case you took a long break and you forgot where you left off. There is also a new Blood Mode after clearing the main story which provides new lore that has eluded us for all these years. Now don't be misled by the claim that you play as a new character in Blood Mode. It's actually just Aya again, but now as an adult.

When encountering enemies, you got to be very careful because some of them take place in narrow spaces. It is so easy to get cornered and go from having full health to losing all of it within two seconds. The game tempts you to collect gems and some of them trigger enemy encounters and they are in said narrow spaces.
Other than that, Mad Father is truly a fun game to experience and this is a well done remake. The game is well balanced in being adorable and being terrifying. Mad Father is one of my fond memories of my days in high school. While I have been jump scared quite a few times, the adorable moments made me happy. The 2020 remake has recaptured that and for that, I give this game a grade of 90/100.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow this game was good.
I'm prejudiced against RPG games because they tend to be grindy, boring, and generic, but Mad Father completely won me over. The drawings, especially of Aya and her range of expression, are well done. Aya's emotion and expression felt highly real, and the experience of a small child coming into contact with horrors but still longing for the love of her parents really resonates. There is also a part of her that is dead inside, that has known all along.
Spooky and atmospheric is just the right way, mostly puzzle driven but some dodging and escaping. The puzzles are highly well constructed and intuitive, and don't require a guide, unless you wish to gather all the gems and get the 'true end.'
It was great fun being a 'good samaratin' and helping creepy disgusting ghosts in exchange for gems. Occasionally these ways of helping can be outright funny, while others are touching.
I absolutely loved the noises made by walking on broken glass, bones, and of course, gooey corpses. *SPLOINK*\t\t\t\t\t
