\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absoutely love Mad Father. This RPG horror game is a beautiful remaster of the free version that was floating around the internet as a popular way to spend an afternoon with cheesy fun. The story is engaging and holds your attention, and the pixel graphics are quite wonderful as well. The main theme will stick in your head long after you finish playing this game and Aya, I believe, is a character that will stick with anyone that plays Mad Father. This gothic piece set in Germany reminds me greatly of The Island of Dr. Moreau, and is a definite game to pick up for the Halloween season or for a good afternoon of casual horror. I remember playing this as a young teenager, and I enjoy it just as much now as I did then.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo i love the mechanics and gameplay that is Mad Father because i was genuinely engrossed in the entire experience from the beginning to the end of play one and for that reason this game will always at least be a good memory for me. However the next section explains what prevents me from explaining what i do not like, and it is a big SPOILER so don't read forward otherwise.

In the end of the game you find out you are just like the mad father you ran away from and that irked me because what was the point of escaping the mansion in the first place and the story if you are the psychopath you successfully escape from? The story is great, but the ending/endings are too depressing to me and make little justification for a 2nd playthrough.

Overall i would give this game an 85.\t\t\t\t\t
