\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is said that good things come in small packages and this game definitely proves it. Mad father is an excellent indie survival horror adventure game with humble roots (an RpgMaker game) that expanded in a polished, truly great and terrifying endeavor.

You control Aya, a cute and innocent young girl with a perfectfamily, a loving mother and father. But The mother has since passed away and Father seems more and more occupied with his mysterious and possibly sinister experiments in the basement. And what is the housemaid's role in all this? When the mansion where Aya lives is suddenly infested by ghosts, ghouls and other morbid monstrosities that kidnap Father, it is up to her to solve the mysteries of the mansion, discover ugly truths and save him.

You navigate the through the creepy locations and intypical horror/adventure fashion, you collect items and solve fun puzzles to progress while also trying to stay alive. There is no combat, you simply need to avoid anything that poses a threat or engage in intense QTEs to escape their clutches. The game isn't overtly difficult but tehre are a few times where a wrong decision can lead to a swift death so saving constantly is adviced through the numerous save points.

Graphics are doing an amazing job at presenting you a dark and gloomy mansion with some very effectic scares, and beautifully animated and lively sprites. The music is perhaps the highlight of Mad Father, featuring some mesmerizing tracks that manage to fully immerse you into this dark adventure and add to the already creepy atmosphere.
I haven't played the previous version of the game, but watching comparisons, it is obvious that a lot of care went into the Remake with a significant graphical overhaul and a few extra additions, maps and altered puzzles among other things, to deliver the definitive version of the game. However, it is great that the dev allows you to choose and play the old version if you wish through the game launcher, so you can experience an alternate take of the game.

Apart from the main course, the game offers a few extra modes although they aren't especially deep, they are very welcome and only add to the fun experience. Blood Mode is a 2nd run/playthrough of the game with a few altered events (especially towards the end) but the differences are not that big in terms of gameplay, so it does feel like repeating it from the start. But in terms of story i found it quite intriguing and it also works as some sort of small epilog/expansion. It's not very essential and perhaps doesn't add much but I found it neat.
Same can be said for the other small (and quite short) bonus episode featuring a supporting character that expands on the lore a tiny bit.
Other fun unlockable extras include the image and music gallery where you can freely enjoy the beautiful and scary art while listening to your favorite tracks and the cute "Doll Maker" where you get to create your own fun avatar, shoosing from various parts, hairstyles etc.

Mad Father deserves all the high praise and it offers a very enjoyable and scary adventure that ans of the genre should definitely check it out.\t\t\t\t\t

I believe this game came out originally in 2012 but it wasn't until 2016 when it got released on Steam with an updated version that included new graphics. In 2020, another graphical upgrade was made to the game (and provided free of charge to owners of the 2016 version).

I completed this game entirely by playing the 2016 version viewing all endings and playing the "if" scenario that adds more to the story. I only put a few minutes into the 2020 version but it seems like besides the updated visuals, the game also features new menus and perhaps slight new room arrangements in the Drevis residence (location where the game takes place).

I really enjoyed the atmosphere, characters and story of the game. The true ending was not of my liking but you win some and lose some. The game itself is not really scary but it does a great job of creating this horror atmosphere through the creepy setup and rooms at the Drevis residence combined with the twisted background of the characters and their actions.

Gameplay is focused on exploration, puzzle solving, some stealth segments and some quick-time events. Puzzles are interesting and for the most part fairly straightforward. Here's hoping that the developer at one point decides to expand upon the story and lore of this game (as hinted by endings and if scenario this game can easily be expanded).

If you enjoyed this game make sure to check out Misao which is a game by the same developer that provides some loose references/connections to this game.
