\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI forgot to review this game but I'll do it now, bc it's been months since I played.
Anyways, here's my review!

Mad Father is a good RPG Maker game, from what I've heard it's a classic among the RPG Maker Horror games!
It's story is kind of simple but with some small twists I personally didn't expect, since I strayed away from spoilers.

The story is (imo) interesting! I didn't expect the Mother to also be a bad person, like the crazy Father. I also didn't expect the way Aya turned or was in her past, really shows the apple doesn't fall from the tree! The whole family tree from the mothers side was unexpected. Really thought the Mom was just a good mom yk.

The game play is simple, but that's just how RPG Maker works, I guess!
I will say the music, ESPECIALLY, when you're getting chased personally scared me a bit! I actually had to turn the music all the way down because I would panic and hit the wrong button or run the wrong way.

It was fun getting all the achievements, although they are pretty easy to get lol.

I believe it was a good purchase for how nice it was to play with the twists and turns!
If you end up liking this game definitely check out other RPG Maker games! I heard a bunch of the classics, like Ib and missao were good! I plan on eventually getting those games due to these experiences!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI came to Mad Father shortly after finishing the main line of Corpse Party and, really, was feeling about ready to drop RPG Maker horror games entirely.

When I left Mad Father, I immediately installed The Witch House.

What changed? And why, good god, have I ended up 100%ing the achievements in this game, a state that normally and justifiably arouses derision, eye rolls, snears and contemptuous middle fingers?

...Turns out, Mad Father is pretty good.

It's campy, it's schlocky... the story is never going to win any awards. However, it's a game with a meaningful, relatable topic and subtext, wrapped in a consistently ghoulish, oppressive and downright creepy atmosphere. Characterisation is broad but effective and the leads remain sympathetic and interesting throughout.

You play a little girl, dwarfed by her monstrously large home and buffeted by the conflicting passions and dark desires of her histrionic parents - one of whom is definitely up to something unsavory behind the bolted door of his basement lab. What could be causing those screams and moans, heard dimly emanating from that dire portal...?

As a grim curse begins to grip the mansion and the remnants of crimes past claw their way to the surface, the player must seek out their missing father, solve the riddle of her family history and work out just exactly why there are so, so many dolls lining the halls...

It won't take you long to work out what's going on... but the game is absolutely bursting with small animations and audio touches to put you at your unease. High-pressure encounters ratchet the tension and the occasional jumpscare keeps you gripping your controller. Indeed, the game is packed with extra content including an entertaining narrative twist on 'new game plus' that puts the events of the story in a new light.

Inputs are straightforward and responsive, with the player free to move in any direction. The game presents as a largely linear axonometric adventure, though the path is dotted with optional sidequests that contribute to unlocking a new ending. Puzzles and encounters are inventive, entertaining and very occasionally slightly challenging. You're in for a fun ride here, rather than a bitter, hard-fought mountain ascent.

The game looks the part - vestibules are gloomy, underground passageways are claustrophobic and lighting is suitably dim and moody. Graphic gore and mutilation are present, though it's rarely gratuitous - and is comfortably balanced out by the gentle super-deformed stylisation and... overall cuteness. It's really quite a cute game, sometimes even WHILE it's running at you with a bloody knife.

Mad Father is a creepy little gem. It's perfect Halloween fodder, and an easy recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
