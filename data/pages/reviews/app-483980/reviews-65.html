\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMad Father is a classic rpgmaker game that everyone should play. It's definitely worth the $5.00 in my opinion.

In this game you play as the protagonist Aya Drevis. A young girl who lost her mother and who also lives in a mansion with her father and his assistant Maria. The plot starts with Aya in the mansion of course, who is about to sleep but suddenly she hears a strange noise and wants to find out what was the cause for the noise. So like basically the whole mansion gets haunted by some sort of spirits that have obviously had something to do with Aya's father.

Here are some heads up before playing this game
+ It is gory - So I wouldn't recommend to anyone who doesn't like stuff like that
+ Jumpscares do happen (etc screaming, characters chasing you and etc )
+ ALWAYS SAVE no matter what. It's always good to save whenever possible. So you don't lose your progress.
+ Quick-Time-Events happen in this game. so be prepared to jam those arrow keys whenever you're in these predicaments

Pros:
+ Spoopy ass CGS
+ Game has a beautiful story
+ Fun Gameplay
+ Revamped art - Sprites look beautiful
+ Puzzles aren't too hard but is fun to figure out
+ Beautiful Soundtrack
+ Exclusive Achievements + Steam Badges if you're a collector\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMad Father is a great rpg maker game (wolf rpg editor?) that's an old classic, along with other games like Misao, Witch's House, and Ib. The same people who made Mad Father also made Misao; Misao originally came before this game, but it was remade so I recommend playing this game first, and then the definitive edition of Misao after. They aren't connected in story, but some elements do carry over.

The story is about a girl named Aya whose mother died a year ago, and on the anniversary of her death, the mansion became cursed. Aya was determined to save her father, who was a crazy scientist who experimented on humans and animals. On this wonderful horror journey, Aya encounters many of her father's test subjects as she tries to find her father and end the curse.

The game has plenty of horror elements, but honestly it isn't really the scariest one out there, nor is it the most offensive. The only element I'd warn people about is that there are dead people and animals, but nothing is explicit. It's a Little-Girl-Gets-Herself-In-Intense-Horror-Situations simulator to the T.

There are 2 bad endings, a true ending, and a last ending that unlocks after getting the true end. You don't need to play through the game more than once, as it branches off at the end.

Overall, I really enjoyed this game. I watched a playthrough of it ages ago and I played it myself years later, when I forgot pretty much everything. There are plenty of secrets to unravel and, while the plot isn't all that unpredictable, it still gives some interesting twists that make you think for a while.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's both impressive and a little sad that this game was more enjoyable than the other games I bought during the summer sale, even though the other games were more expensive and had bigger budgets, better graphics, etc.

The other horror games, I felt, had problems with pacing and often tried too hard to make things atmospheric or artsy, often resorting to trite tricks like typical "scary" music, jump scares, overly dark areas, and so on.

But with Mad Father, you get what you see: it's a story told with 2D sprite graphics and simple puzzles. Despite the simplicity, it still manages to create some reasonably tense and creepy moments, but is still mild enough that people who normally dislike horror can deal with it.

There are a few quick-death "bad end" puzzles that will probably require a few reloads, but just keep in mind that a save point is almost always 1-2 screens away, so you never really lose any progress. On top of that, there are numerous secrets and multiple endings to discover.

Even if I disliked this game, it would've been hard to feel ripped off, because the game is so cheap. The other games I bought, however, were double-digit prices...\t\t\t\t\t
