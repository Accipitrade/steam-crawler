\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlay the game for the charming plot and the visuals, and you'll be pleased. Don't play it looking for clever puzzles or jumpscares, though. That's right, it's one of the classic RPG Maker horror games.
Compared to the free version, this paid version has better-looking maps and artwork. It's also been made much easier to locate objects and events. I got it on sale for $3.50, which is really just a steal, I would've bought it at full price too.
Progressing in the game is kind of non-intuitive and you may have to look at a guide here and there, or just interact with every thing until something happens. There are good scares throughout, just not in-your-face all the time, which is a good thing.
Keep in mind that this game is more "corny" than say, Ib or Witch's House, but that's what makes it so charming. This is definitely one of my favorites, and I'm sure it will be yours, too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMad Father, an indie horror game which is a story about a girl named Aya who has a father with quite a horrible way of treating other humans and animals. The further the story goes the stranger and darker the things get.

In my opinion, this game is beautifully made. I really liked the art style of the game, both the gameplay art and cut scene art. The RPG stylish gameplay fit the game really well. The soundtrack brought creepy vibes and kept the creepy atmosphere going. The game had a little bit voice acting and it was ok I guess. Multiple endings were to be unlocked and experienced and I enjoyed following them all.

If you like anime stylish horror games with little puzzles involved, I'm sure you will like or even love this. I think the RPG style was a cherry on top of this cake, too. The game doesn't cost that much and it is totally worth it. I'd rate this piece of art 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

Summary:
Mad Father by sen is the story of a girl named Aya from Northern Germany, whos father has had a curse placed on him due to his human experimentation. In this game, you play as Aya and solve puzzles to try and save your father from the curse he brought onto himself.

Gameplay:
So since I played the original, free version, I have noticed some differences in gameplay. A notable difference is instead of button-mashing one key to escape from the corpses in some scenes, you actually type out sequences of arrow keys; this adds a sense of urgency to the attacks against her, since it is now a lot easier to die.

I've also noticed minor changes done to the puzzles, which made me have to re-figure out parts in it, which I enjoyed. Getting gems was harder though, but I still managed to unlock the secret ending and 'If' again like I had in the older version. These endings did not appear to have any changes, as did the True Ending (the one I got). Bad Ending 1 or 2 may have changes I am unaware of, as I did not play those.

Graphics and sounds:
There have been some notable graphic improvements since the release of the Steam version. First of these being the improved talksprites. Some don't seem to have much change, but others have been polished to look much cleaner tha the originals did (which were already well-made to start with). The tilesets have had MAJOR improvements. The pixelwork in them are exquisitely made, and it's very easy on the eyes.

So one thing I was hoping they would improve on were the scream sounds, because they were ear-piercing in the original. Well, they didn't, but that didn't take away any enjoyment for me, since I adored the original even with those horrible sound effects. The other sound effects are nice, though. In terms of music, I've always loved the music used in this game, and listen to it often in my freetime for nostalgia sake, because I do truly enjoy this game.

PROs:
  • Captivating story.
  • Improved graphics from the free version.
  • Music that sets the mood.
  • Improved game mechanics from the free version.
  • More details to the story added on.
CONs:
  • Still has the annoying screams

Conclusion:
I really enjoyed replaying this game, and kudos to the developers for reworking it and selling it, they deserve all the money they make from it and all the love they can get. Aya forever holds a place in my heart. I give this game 5 uncanny dolls out of 5.

Score: 5/5\t\t\t\t\t
