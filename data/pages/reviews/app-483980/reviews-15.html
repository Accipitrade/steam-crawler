\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an absolute classic—part of a wave of excellent RPG Maker engine-based horror games created in Japan about ten or so years ago that included The Witch's House and Ib—and this version is the definitive edition, with an expanded narrative and a lot of other fun additions. It's also, for a somewhat old game in a classic format, extremely accessible; anyone can pick it up and play through to the end at a brisk pace without getting stuck. (There are also guides in the Steam community if you are.)

This game no longer uses a lot, if any, of the stock RPG Maker assets that came with the original doujin release, and the custom graphics look great, and the music and ambience is excellent. Narratively, this game is incredibly rich, and the initial mystery seems somewhat straightforward at start but turns out to be deliciously twisty in ways that are shocking but never seem completely undeserved. None of the characters in this game are as simple as they appear, and all of them turn out to be flawed in complicated ways that keep you second-guessing yourself as you explore all the game's content.

It's because of this character complexity—which I think is deeply artistically successful—that I have to caution that this game's narrative might jerk around the player's sympathies until they can no longer muster sympathy for anyone. I started to feel that way initially, but it's only on reflection that I've changed my mind and realized that this game is fundamentally a narrative about tragedy begetting abuse. The game is undeniably edgy, but not crassly or cheaply so. This is not a monotonously grimdark game.

Ultimately, I do think Mad Father should be played if you can handle horror. This is a microgenre that more people should become literate in, and this game makes an excellent entry point. You'll get about five hours of gameplay out of it if you take your time to enjoy yourself, but the world-record speedrun is about twenty-eight minutes (an hour is more realistic for most people rushing the game.) Give it a try.\t\t\t\t\t
