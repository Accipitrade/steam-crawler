\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMad Father was one of the first Horror RPG's I had ever experienced, and left a pretty big impact. It sparked my interest in more than a few ways.
-Good, almost completely smooth controls
-Fast character, got used to the speed (and complained when there was anything slower in any other game)
-A defense mechanism! First time I had ever seen one of those in an RPG (that is almost quick-time-event-like)
-Good story:
Little girl's mother dies, father is a [mad] scientist. He becomes endangered from his own doing, and his daughter has to save him (with some help from Snowball!), she finds out more and more about him, and has to fight her way through a few disturbances.

This game left me wanting more stories and games like it (so I bought more) and inspired me to start writing again.

I watched someone play this a very long time ago on YouTube, and remembered it a few weeks ago. I saved up my money just so that I could buy this and enjoy playing it myself.
This game inspired my sarcastic-scared side (where I get sarcastic when scared), amusing friends who watch me play, or try scaring me themselves.

I love this game. I strongly recommend it.

The story of a mad scientist or priest trying to resurrect a loved one has been told in better, more intriguing ways. It's also been done to death.

Anyway, do yourself a favor and play it instead of this weaboo's version of a "horror" story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow. Just... very much wow. This is definitely the best game, story-wise, I have played in a long time, and it's certainly the best indie I've played for as long as I can remember.

I'm a bit of a horror aficionado, so this came under my radar on Steam eventually. I picked it up recently on sale, and I have been captivated by this little gem's depth, story, and emotion. And it's power to induce fear and empathy.

It's a little gem from what I've come to understand is the RPGMaker genre, and used to be a lower-quality -- mostly graphics -- freeware title released in 2012. It was since enhanced and edited for the Steam release. So it's someone's -- "Sen" -- project, and it does feel very... cared about.

The story is a beautiful blend of anime-reminiscent psychological horror and mystery, and it weaves its creeping, creepy tale in a manner that would make Silent Hill 2 gaze on in curiosity, with Higurashi no Naku Koro ni and Shiki making eyes from the corner.

Yes, psychological depths plumbed a la Silent Hill's golden years, and gore-filled shocks and plot twists such that made Higurashi and Shiki beautiful, sad, and insanely entertaining and gripping anime series.

This game, basically, is like the charming indie cousin of 07th expansion and Konami's love child (whatever that may be...), waiting for you to take notice.


PROS
+A wonderful blink-and-you'll-miss-it Silent Hill 3 (and 2, technically) reference
+Beautiful yet simple music
+Story feels ripped straight from a Japanese horror light novel/manga
+Charming protagonist and compelling antagonists
+Endings worth all the pennies you paid for this game
+Will make you think with its puzzles, even just a little

CONS
-If I think on it a little, there's a bit of deus ex machina in the form of one particular character, without whom the ending would not be possible. But hey, nothing's perfect.
(Also, little backstory is given to this character, which makes it more difficult to understand/explain his presence other than: He's Necessary to The Story)
-Watching YouTube videos of the original RPGMaker freeware version, Aya's father was voiced in Japanese. This changed to a rather awkward "Engrish" dubbing for Steam's release, which was corny and detracted from the impact of the scene a little.


Will be recommending this to all my horror-fancying friends for the plot and the feels.\t\t\t\t\t
