\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter her mother’s death by an illness, young girl Aya lives together with his scientist father and their maid in a big mansion in northern Germany. Out of love for her father, Aya chooses to ignore his evil side of killing and experimenting on humans and animals. But when the anniversary of her mother’s death comes, Aya is awoken by the screams of her father. Horrified she finds out that their home is full of corpses and ghosts wandering around. She sets out to look out for her dear father and to uncover the mystery of the walking horrors.

Gameplay

Mad Father 2016 is an enhanced version of the original freeware released back in 2012. It’s developed by a Japanese developer and created with an RPG Maker software. The Steam release has some differences, like improved graphics and new events, but the core game has stayed the same. It is a survival horror game where you control the little girl Aya trying to find her father while exploring the huge mansion, solving puzzles, finding useful items and helping and avoiding the dead. The game is around 2-4 hours long and there are three endings, plus two bonus endings, which you unlock by making different choices in the story and by collecting gems hidden in the game.

The Good
  • Being a major horror genre fan, I was primarily interested in that aspect of the game and there’s plenty of it. Corpses, ghosts, monsters, blood, gore and more. The game is laced with heavy tones of creepiness in the story. You will feel uneasiness, shock and disgust. It’s a messed-up a story within a messed-up game. But it’s also intriguing for a horror fan.
  • Exploring around the huge mansion is fun, uncovering secrets and items you can use on your journey. Sometimes coming across puzzles like the two almost identical rooms where you have to match everything or trapping a monster in a tunnel to pass by it unharmed. Sometimes experiencing creepy events: a ghostly face on a window, a living doll walking past, bloody handprints appearing on glass or a body emerging from a toilet. Basically, something new always happens when you reach new places.
  • The graphics look good, both the sprites and character portraits, and compared to the original they are simply better looking. The game manages to look somewhat cute and morbid at the same time.
  • The soundtrack really fits the atmosphere of the game, some tragic and some eerie sounding, with a couple of tracks I really love. They are available to listen through the menu.
  • The different endings bring a bit of variety to this short game.
The Bad
  • There are a couple of instances where you can be killed very easily and usually they take a few tries to get through. It is a little frustrating having to die again and again to repeat the part. Thankfully these are few and far between and they are generous with the save points.
  • There isn’t any voice acting other than some laughs, yells, grunts or other such sounds in some parts. But towards the end of the game there is a character who says a few lines in English. They used to be in Japanese but got changed for some reason. The original lines actually fit the psychopath character that says them. The current ones not only sound stupid but one line even sounds grammatically incorrect. I know it’s just a couple of lines in the last minutes of the game but given the situation they’re said in it just takes away from the intense atmosphere.
In conclusion

Mad Father is a gem of a game (pun intended) which managed to keep me thoroughly enjoyed and that’s enough for me to positively review it. The few flaws I found are so minor that I can easily overlook them. I was honestly so content with playing it that I wouldn’t have minded for some more, although I think that the length is perfect. The free version still exists somewhere in the internet, and even though I’m often very stingy I was happy to buy it on Steam when it first came out.

Personal Rating: 8/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA small horror-adventure about an 11 year-old girl trying to survive a curse-ridden mansion that is her own home and uncovering some of the mysteries of the ghosts and corpses that are suddenly creeping about.
This is a revamp of a free version that you can find elsewhere online, but you'll also have to apply an English patch since there are no English versions of that around as far as I know.

The game can be played with a keyboard or a controller, it's all up to preference.
There's apparently no way to quit out of the game except with ALT + F4 or from the main menu, and you can't get there when you're playing the actual game unless you die as there isn't even a pause menu in-game.
There are some bugs with the configurations of the game, but nothing I found serious enough that makes the game downright unplayable.

There's little gameplay all throughout and most of it consists of very light puzzle solving that don't take long to figure out if you know how to put two and two together. There was only one side-puzzle in the game that was way too vague for me to figure out on my own, but everything else didn't take much thought.
There's almost no combat in the game, and when you're up against something that can kill you, you're usually running, playing a small minigame, finding a way around it, or just watching the story unfold; it's all about the exploration, atmosphere, and story anyway.

The game's real heavy in its story and horror theme; the gameplay is just so it immerses you a little more into it.
You're a young girl of 11 years who loves her father very much despite already knowing he may be doing questionable acts in the basement. On the eve of the anniversary of her mother's death, she reminisces about her for a while until she hears her father's scream and decides to go check out what happened. This is where the game starts and almost immediately as you step out of the room, the horror begins.
Practically all of the characters—save for the maid and the father—are ghosts of some kind, each with their own very small backstory, making it pretty obvious that the father has done horrific experiments to them as if the title of the game wasn't enough of an indication. The hook of that is trying to find out what he's done to them and why.
The horror isn't too bad, though there are loads of cliché "haunted house" moments like dishware falling randomly and some corpses moving sometimes as you exit the room; there's so much that it becomes expected almost, and it's made less scary with the RPG Maker look of the game. All that said, it's still pretty atmospheric with its very dark but still visible setting, minimal use of music, and proper use of sound cues, making exploration often unsettling; there's also some attention to detail in the environment around you and sometimes little changes to them when you exit and re-enter an area for that extra touch of uneasiness. There's only one real jump scare in the game with loud audio, but it's heavily telegraphed that you can't really help but prepare for it anyway.

The game doesn't take more than 3, 2 hours to beat, and only a little more when going for all the achievements and unlockables like the various endings and side quests in case you missed some. It's all even faster if you're a quick reader even though there's a little voice acting, although they're only limited to anime grunts and the like anyway.
All-in-all, it's a nice little horror story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was a delightfully creepy game, I ended up staying up all night to finish it because it drew me in.

Story. It's not too long--I did a completionist run in around 4 hours--but the story is interesting while also having just enough horror factor to draw you in.

Gameplay. Definitely casual friendly. A few puzzle components, but nothing too difficult to figure out, and the enemies you do have to fight aren't too tough (I definitely died a few times, but it's not hard to figure out what you're supposed to do).

Graphics and aesthetics. Not recommended for the squeamish (lots of gore). Graphics looked great, and I dig the aesthetic, though I love creepy horror games.

Sound. The music is really amazing! Sound effects are good and used with care, which I think adds to the atmosphere. Voice acting (when used) is also good.

Overall recommendation. If you like horror and visual novel/puzzle type games, this is definitely a recommend. Full price is totally reasonable and worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

I found that this game is a great change of pace from the typicall shooter or the popular MMORPG games of late. Mad father is a game that forces you to think quickly whilst also forcing you the player to make decisions that effect the overall ending, each action in certain areas effect how you progress towrads each of the 3 endings. I found Mad father to be a game that anyone who is a fan of horror can really pick up and enjoy. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this game twice when there was a free version on the Internet.
I loved it then and I love it now. I bought it because I wanted to have it in steam and I wanted to get the achievements.

I love the puzzles, the history and the art. The puzzles aren't hard and the history is easy to follow. There are two bad endings and a true ending and 21 gems to gather.

It's a cute easy game. It has scary moments, and some parts can get your heart racing (looking at you, Mad Father).
