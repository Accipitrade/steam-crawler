\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Mad Father" is a survival horror adventure game made in the style of a JRPG, although with many of the elements associated with the genre, like turn-based combat. Instead, it focuses much more on the puzzles and the survival which comes with the horror.
*TL;DR* -> It's pretty damn great and I would recommend it to fans of horror in general as its story is quite intriguing filled with twists and turns (especially if you decide to go for another playthrough after finishing it once, and unlocking all the endings), and the gameplay is fluid and fun, although there are some tricky "trial and error" sequences.

The story follows an 11-year old daughter of a mad scientist who, one night, hears her father's screams coming from the basement and she sets out to save her father, while also trying to survive herself in a big mansion filled with threats on every corner. I expected the story to be a lot more mysterious regarding what her father does as a scientist, but it's revealed at the very start that it's something sinister, so I was intrigued what aspect of the story they would make a mystery out of. To my surprise, they handled the mystery side of it very well and I'll not go into spoilers, but if you pay attention to subtle dialogue cues, a lot of it is foreshadowed and the ending itself ("The Promise", true ending) is very chilling.

I dug the pixel art style a lot. I think these games don't get enough credit for their look as much as photorealistic games do, but there's a lot of effort put into making these various sprites and eerie locations. There isn't any voice over present, until the very end, but you'll constantly be hearing blood-curdling screams and sound effects which elevate the atmosphere. I liked the music, thought it fit the themes well, but I would've appreciated more variety and distinction between the tracks as a couple of them sound very similar in nature.

The gameplay is very simple consisting of arrow keys to move your character and two more buttons, one for your inventory and one for using items, or examining them, which makes for a very casual playthrough and the game's puzzles aren't hard at all, so there shouldn't be a need for you to look at a walkthrough while playing it. However, there are a couple of "trial and error" sequences where you can instantly die if you fail, so you'll find yourself restarting at the last save quite a lot until you get it right. Fortunately, save points are greatly placed and you'll never have to backtrack a lot. There are four endings in total, one of which is an extension to the true ending, and they're all disturbing in their own way, but I liked the true ending the most. There's a reason that's the one they encourage you to get. You can also collect all 21 gems which are placed all over the mansion to unlock the aforementioned extension of the true ending.

All in all, "Mad Father" is exactly what I hoped it would be. An effective, short (3hrs for the first playthrough + 1hr30min more for getting the other endings and all the achievements = 4hr30min), well designed and well written horror story that I can wholeheartedly recommend to fans of the genre, but also to those who are looking to get into the genre as this is a fairly simple and easy-to-get-into game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMad Father is a great little indie horror game which revolves around puzzle solving and running and hiding. The story is great and draws you in and the RPG elements really change the story and the events which will happen later on in the game. Where you will go, what would you rather do and who will you help. These options will affect what story you get and what ending you will get. There are good endings, bad endings and after a few collectables and walk-through's, the True ending.

A nice touch from this game is that it does not rely on Jump scares to scare the player. There a few when you least suspect it but fits in with the scenario but the majority of the scares will be from entering rooms, exploring and the disturbing events and people you will meet. You cannot attack so running away and hiding are you only form of surviving.

Without spoiling the story, it takes place in a mansion late at night where you wake up to disturbing events and your goal is to find your father throughout the madness of the mansion.

The pixel art is nice and clean. Objects and characters can be distinguished and the colours are dark, which fits the theme. The portraits of the characters are detailed and have various expressions. The music is nice and creepy at times which add to the tense atmosphere. You will need to think outside the box to complete some puzzles.

Overall this game is great, creepy and makes always wonder what could happen next. A great indie game which can be completed in a couple hours.\t\t\t\t\t
