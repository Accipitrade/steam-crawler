\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got hooked on these games in high school after playing IB. I've always enjoyed Mad Father, such a beautiful art style. ( )

In this wonderful and lavish house, what could go wrong? You play as absolutely adorable 11 year old, Aya. She wakes up at night and hears her father scream in terror. Instead of calling 911 or attempting to escape, she instead goes to investigate the basement where he is always working.

It's a cute but spook spook puzzle game. It's not going to give you nightmares but the atmosphere is incredibly chilling. I found myself questioning humanity after this.

One of my favourite things is collectables, and I love that you are rewarded with a bonus scene after the credits if you collect all the gems. That made me so happy games nowadays just get you to collect worthless junk and only reward you with achievements. I'm looking at you Shadow Of Mordor, Uncharted series, Horizon Zero Dawn and almost every other mainstream title ever.

One of the staples of these 2D horror puzzle games is the multiple endings. There's 3 and they each require a SEPARATE playthrough. Not just reloading a save before the point of no return and picking one different option, separate playthroughs. I really appreciated the devs giving proper thought to the alternate endings most other games trick you into thinking your choices matter but when you replay and pick different paths you end up with the same outcomes anyway.

It will take a 2-4 hours to get through all the endings, depending on your familiarity with the game you may want to follow a guide. An easy enough 100% if you are into that. ()

I got this on sale and bundled with Misao which I'm excited about. They are inexpensive and I get a lot of joy out of them. Even then I think the $2.99 price tag is worth every penny. If you get it on sale please try it!

I hope they answer all the questions I have with a second game. Please Devs! ~()\t\t\t\t\t
