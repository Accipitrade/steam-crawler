\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this game a LOT of times, but I personally think it was better before all these changes.
I get the feeling it kind of lost it's charm, if I may say?

I hate now that every time you are next to something you can interact with, the game tells you with a sound that gets annoying very fast.
sen removed some challenges, and made it easier for the player, but that was exactly what was fun with these kind of games : on your first run, you died a few times before getting it right and it was normal...
Now, at some point in the game where you really need to react fast, the game points you what you need to do and that sucks..

But some changes are great, such as the bathroom and other visual modifications. (The kitchen is far more logical now lol)
But I think the halls are too much, like it attacks your eyes? It's just too full of stuff, and the choice of floor and carpet is...strange.
sen also added more information on the characters, such as Aya's mother, which fills some holes that always left me speculating.
But I wish sen decided to leave some corpses loithering around, and not just female dolls. :/

((That doesn't have anything to do with the gameplay but I think the new artworks are kinda weird :
The eyes are kinda off, and Aya's head is too big for her small face.
Sometimes Aya looks really funny, like when she's in despair, but that's just my personnal artistic opinion))

I decided to buy it to support sen, but I REALLY prefer the old Mad Father.
It's still a fun game, but not as good as it used to be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMad Father is one of hundreds of avaible RPG Maker-esc, stylized horror adventure games you can find from that programming platform. It tells the story of a young girl, attempting to save her Father from supernatural forces, while learning the turth behind her Mother and Father along the way.

As far as game mechanics go, this game really doesn't break any new ground. It's mostly like a visual novel with a few puzzles and chase scenes scattered about the game. The puzzles generally make sense, but there was one or two that were fairly vauge that required me to look up help from a guide.

The writing is alright, but honestly, you can see the story from a mile away, almost from the start of when you first begin playing. There's multiple endings, but they aren't very satisfying. There's questions the game ultimately don't answer. Some would say this leaves the players "something to chew on". I just find it lazy writing and a refusal to answer all the necessary questions of the story.

I think with better, more polished game mechanics, better vocal work and a better laid out story, I'd have been more happy with it. As it were, it's not a bad game and I'm not mad I bought it, but I kind of feel like this is a $0.99 game at best.

Can I recommend this game? If this kind of game is your jam, you'll love it. For people looking for a more indepth game, it's likely you may end up disappointed. I really wish STEAM had a more neutral option besides "YES" or "NO". For all my complaints, I still mostly enjoyed the game, so I'll go with a yes.\t\t\t\t\t
