\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike so many people on here before me, I played this when it first came out (the English version, at least). It came out at a time when these types of RPGMaker (in this case Wolf RPG) horror games were plentiful. This was one of the few that really stood out.

This game does a fantastic job building an atmosphere that holds your attention and unnerves you while you play. While there are very few moments that scare the crap out of you, there is always just a bit of tension to keep you on the edge of your seat. So many of the art assets seem like they could come to life at the drop of a hat and attack you, making exploration a bit more tense than it otherwise should be.

If you have never played this game before, and are not using a guide, it should take you about 5-10 hours to explore this game to completion. The extra content is not terribly difficult to access, as there are only a handful of things that need to be done aside from the main story in order to gain access to them. I would say that the extra content is definitely worth it if you enjoyed the main story, as it builds on the story in a pretty meaningful way.

Definitely worth the price, especially compared to a lot of what's out there at the moment. And if you are really strapped for cash, it seems to go on sale quite often. Hopefully, this game gets enough support that the developer continues to build upon this world that includes a spiritual sequel, Misao, which is also available on Steam.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played very many games in this style, but Mad Father is fantastic. It sucks you in and maintains an eerie atmosphere throughout. I was so intrigued by the story that I ended up finishing it in one sitting, and I plan to go back to get all of the achievements and see all there is to see.

The pixel art is great, fairly simple yet surprisingly expressive. The Character portraits and cutscene art is also great. It manages very well at being scary. There aren't too many jump scares, and there are plenty of unsettling little things that happen, sometimes only after you've revisited a room a few times, or interacted with certain things. There's a fair bit of body horror, which I love. The implications of the story are also quite disturbing.

I was also pleasantly surprised by the sound design. There's some great music, plenty of creepy sounds, and even some voice acting thrown in sparsely but to great effect.

Highly recommended <3\t\t\t\t\t
