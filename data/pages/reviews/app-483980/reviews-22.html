\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I was younger, I had pirated a version of this game online for free and I played it endlessly. Recently I heard that a new ending was added, though, so I realized it was time to look into buying this game that I knew so well -- and it was well worth the money. If you're an old fan hoping for hours and hours of additional content, unfortunately you may be disappointed. The newer content, however short, does provide a lot more context and lore to the story. The graphics and some character cards have been updated - which part of me has mixed feelings about, but in the end I came to love the new visuals. Most of the audio seems to be exactly the same with some new lines added (such as in the final chase scene), which eased the nostalgia that was unsure of the new look. I do wish there was an option to revert to the previous graphics for old time's sake, but, again, the new graphics are just lovely. There have been minor changes to the layout of the mansion and to some of the gem puzzles, but they honestly make the game flow better in my opinion, especially if you're new to the game. As someone who already knows the story and the puzzles well, it took less than 5 hours in total to complete all three game modes - and some of that was time the game was idly open while I did other things. I remember my first playthroughs years ago taking around 8 hours, though I was also maybe 11 or 12. I also ended up with all 21 achievements in this time, which was a little unfortunate since I was hoping for an excuse to play it through again to do ridiculously specific things. Overall I 100% recommend this game to new and old fans of this game, just don't expect much of a challenge if you're well versed with the original version.\t\t\t\t\t
