SHORT REVIEW
--> Story: 4/5
--> Graphics: 4/5
--> Audio: 4/5
--> Bug-Free: 5/5

--> Verdict: 4.25/5

SPOILER-FREE REVIEW
Mad Father is a Mystery/Horror game leaning more towards the former rather than the latter. Whereas I felt that the element of mystery was present at all times, there were very few truly scary moments throughout the entire game. Nonetheless, I was constantly kept on edge due to the intricate story and excellent atmosphere. In my opinion, there was nothing wrong about that; I love good jump scares and heart-pounding moments, however, I have come to respect games which do not necessarily rely on these mechanics in order to draw you in. The Story remained interesting from start to finish, although it does leave a few stuff unclear by the time it wraps up. To this end, I recommend playing Misao after finishing this one since, although not a sequel, it does appear to take place in the same universe as Mad Father, hence expanding the story in a way or two. Moving on to the Graphics, I have to say that this is a pretty good looking game using the WOLF RPG Editor. Everything was nicely detailed, from the mansion that it takes place in, to the animation of the characters. Same goes for the Audio throughout the entire game which does a very subtle yet important thing; managing its various soundtracks based on the situation at hand. In other words, there were moments where the game was more engaging due to its musical background and others where the complete lack of it contributed to the whole atmosphere build-up even further. Last, but not least, the game was entirely Bug-Free, playing smoothly from start to finish; something which I believe is always nice to state.\t\t\t\t\t
