\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I grew up on games like "Mad Father" and "Witch's House", the RPG Maker Horror era of YouTube in the 2010's did not evade me. The Steam version of "Mad Father" is a remake of the free version, the only changes being graphics, typo's fixed, and an extra ending scene to clear up the cliffhanger of the original. This version also has two other "modes", but they serve as clarification of the story as well. The true ending of the original game was not changed in any way.

And the story held up! Don't expect too much from the game going into it, I completed it in 4 hours using a guide only to find a single gem I missed, but nothing in the story is truly too scary. This isn't a dis at the game, some parts genuinely freaked me (the dead cat in the drawer; it has no visual but the concept was pretty disturbing), but at some point the small changes of the house around you stop phasing you. The story was pretty good, the puzzles were enjoyable and decently easy, and the QTE and chase scenes didn't feel unfair. I don't want to say too much and ruin the story because admittedly it's a very simple concept, but I think only a few parts felt "unnatural" (I did not like the bald guy.)

I do wanna talk about the added modes though! Spoilers a bit for them, just vaguely though.

"If" mode is less than 10 minutes long, and the payoff was an "oh !" moment for sure! But the scenes leading up to it felt weird and kinda unnatural, it was a good idea though.

"Blood mode" is the same exact thing as the normal game, with minimal dialogue and game-play changes. I wish they had leaned a bit more into Aya reliving the night, it felt more lonely when she didn't stop and commentate on everything she was doing. The game didn't really know if it wanted to lean into the changes or into keeping it the same, so it didn't really do anything. Subtle things like not having to use the tongs to fish the key out of the toilet were fun, Aya has grown and is more willing to get her hands dirty than when she was a child. But the game doesn't remember that this Aya isn't a child anymore after that. I did like that the house felt a bit more playful than murderous on this run. This mode is step by step the same puzzles as the first one, and probably took me around an hour to do. The ending cut scene for completing it wasn't "worth it" per say, but it wasn't NOT worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been told to play this game for many years now, but I finally got around to playing it. For me, this was one of those games that I just couldn't stop playing until I was finished. I thoroughly had a blast.

The atmosphere is creepy and fun. Sometimes I find these rpgmaker horror games have frustratingly difficult puzzles that require the aid of a walkthrough, but it's not the case here. The soundtrack is amazing. If I had any complaints, it would only be that I find Aya's voice to be kind of annoying when she screams, but what can you expect from an anime girl? (yes, I know it's not an anime, but you get what I mean.) However, I loved the creepy voice acting for Aya's father.

Not only is this now my favorite rpgmaker game, but one of my favorite games in general. I'm only sad that I'm finished with it. Do you ever wish you could un-play a game so that you can experience it again for the first time? That's how I feel about this game.\t\t\t\t\t
