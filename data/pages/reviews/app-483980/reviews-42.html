Updating my review for the remake. It did not appear as though very much was changed aside from some alterations to the mansion layout in the beginning and the killer doll segments. The newly enhanced sprites are fantastic and are much more detailed compared to their older counterparts. I normally hate QTEs, but the new ones introduced give an ample amount of reaction time as well as allow you to skip having to do more if you don't screw up the first one (this is not always consistent). It was great playing through this game again with better visuals, although I do have a few nitpicks. While Blood mode was a neat addition, I don't feel it actually added very much in terms of gameplay. It's nearly identical to the normal mode except the events are slightly different with new input from the older Aya and there is new information about the relationship between Monika and Alfred. The new stuff is great, but playing through everything the exact same way is a little boring. I also noticed that the ending in Blood mode did not change depending on whether I had collected all the gems or not. Why not just remove the gem collecting from Blood mode if it has no impact? Overall, this is a really solid remake and I would still recommend it. Besides, I got more for free. Thanks guys.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThough I’ve watched let’s plays of this game before and have had it in my library for a while, I didn’t get around to finally playing it for myself until now (mostly in anticipation for the remake). And man, do I regret not picking this up sooner. What I love about many of these RPG Maker horror games is what you lose in graphics, you more than make up for in story and characters. Mad Father is no exception. I wouldn’t say it’s super scary, but you will probably jump a few times. As for the other points about this game I have to make…

Pros:
•\tMemorable characters, music, and twists
•\tFantastic atmosphere and sound effects
•\tArt that manages to be gruesome and creepy, but not gory (blood is a common sight, but it doesn’t go much beyond that)
•\tWell worth the full price (I managed to catch it on sale for about $3, but considering my first playthrough took me about 4 hours including the times I needed to look a couple parts up, I’d say $5 is a fair price)
•\tAll 27 achievements can easily be earned within 2 playthroughs (not counting the IF part as its own thing since it’s so short)

Cons:
•\tSometimes the controls felt a little imprecise, but that might have been the fault of the controller I was using
•\tSome of the chase sequences and parts where you have to fight off things via pressing the arrow keys got annoying, but there weren’t many of them, so they weren’t that bad

Overall, Mad Father is a great game and worth your attention. In my opinion, it’s the best RPG Maker horror game. I’m excited to see what the developer does with the remake.
