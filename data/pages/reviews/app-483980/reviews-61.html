\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMad Father is a great horror game. Even though it looks like an SNES game, it is still very effective. The reason for this is, that there are a lot of spooky events happening everywhere. Even in areas that you thought you cleared ages ago. This makes the whole game very eerie and you never know what is going to happen. Seeing all the different events is a fun game in itself and some are subtle enough to make you question if you really saw it, or are you just going mad. There are a few frustrating sequences, where there are very little margin for error. But those sequences are too brief to be actually bothersome.

Fans of horror games should all play Mad Father. It is simply a great time, even if it is a bit short. 5/5 would piss my pants again.\t\t\t\t\t

However, I did have an issue with the information regarding the controls (especially in the beginning). There was virtually no tutorial on how to move and interact or how to get to the items menu. Now, granted, it wasn't difficult to figure out and maybe I'm just clueless (a very real possibility) and "use the arrow keys" is supposed to be a given... but still, it would have been nice to include it in-game or make it more obvious earlier on.

But like I said, this is the only issue I have with the game and it was fairly easy to work out and move on to enjoying my playthrough.\t\t\t\t\t
