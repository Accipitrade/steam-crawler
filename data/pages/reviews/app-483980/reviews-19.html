\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an exceptional horror adventure game if you don't mind the 2D JRPG-style controls and a handful of timed sequences. It reminds me of how surprising it was to see a good emotional adventure in To the Moon, except this game is for lovers of the macabre. There are lots of creepy details and effective artwork, and the story is compelling enough.

As a game, if you intentionally explore everything, it will take about four hours. Add another hour to go back for a couple of alternate scenes and endings, and you still might not find quite everything, but I'm done with it at 5-6 hours. According to the gem count and the art gallery, I missed one gem and five event images. The achievements give you some idea what there is to do, and I didn't find one "if" ending or a secret event, and I didn't finish blood mode. Blood mode unlocks when you finish the main game, and it is essentially a reframing of the same game as if you have reason to go through it again. As far as I can tell, the progression is the same, with a little different dialogue to explain why you're repeating things.

So in summary, it's an adventure game based on inventory and environmental puzzles with quick-time events and the ability to die but immediately continue. There are save points, but they are plentiful, and there are reasons to replay, but they are a bit contrived. If none of that bothers you, it's a fantastic horror game for those who like a combination of cute and shockingly dark.\t\t\t\t\t
