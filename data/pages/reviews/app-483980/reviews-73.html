\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMad Father is like Misao: Definitive Edition a new great look for a classic rpg maker horror game. But unlike Misao: Definitive Edition witch was all most a remake.

Mad Father is more of a remastered version then a remake like Misao. With update art, newly added events, and some new story. But anyway time for the story.

You play as Aya, a little girl who lost her mother about year ago. As she reflects on the past in her nursery she hears her father, who is a doctor screaming, so Aya gets up to see what is wrong but soon she sees what her father has been doing, so it’s up to her to save him.

Now what I like about this game/the remastered version. I liked the new redesigns, not just for the undead monsters but the Drevis mansion as well.

I mean the Drevis mansion looks more put together. Like in the old game Aya or her parents room looked a bit empty and looked the same, but now in the new game her room is more detailed. It just feels more real.

I liked that we got some new story for two characters in the game. Liked the creepy atmosphere. And I liked the music it could be vary creepy. Characters were good too.

Now for something I did not like changing the quick time event, you see in the old game you hit the spacebar but in the new game you hit the arrow keys, I don’t get it I liked the old way.

Now with all that done. Mad Father is one of the classic rpg maker horror game, that just got even better with this new remastered version. Great art, good scares, and a nice story. I recommend it wholeheartedly, for old and new fans alike.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely love this game. I'm not exactly a reviewer with good words to say, but Mad Father is a great game. Before it was on Steam and was just a game I found on vgperson's site, I would play it constantly and never got tired of replaying it. I enjoy the art style, the story, the mechanics...I dislike only one thing about this game, and it's that oooone part where you have to flip the switch to open the door near the end and you're trying your best to not get chainsawed. Maybe it's me but I always take a while to not get, yknow, murdered because I had to flip a switch then juke you know who so I could actually get to the door. Maybe I just suck, I digress.
But I love this game. It'll break your heart, it'll make you happy, and personally for me, it left me wanting more after the true end because I just gotta know what happens after iiiit.
The true end - the endgame, if you will - is quite good. It doesn't peaches and cream you in the future.
Basically this game is great and I'm no reviewer but I will love it forever please buy this game!\t\t\t\t\t

The graphics for the background were very different from what I saw back then. When I bought the game I discovered so much more than the youtuber did, making me enjoy the game even more. The fact that so much detail was waiting for me to discover around the corner thrilled me. The only problems I faced with the game was the size of the window for the screen, it didn't fill the whole computer screen for me but, still I loved the gameplay. The art work was beautiful yet, I was a little disappointed that they didn't have full voice acting for the characters. Overall I enjoyed this spooky RPG horror game. Please consider making a sequel to the "If" part of the game, I would definitely buy it.\t\t\t\t\t
