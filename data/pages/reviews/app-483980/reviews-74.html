\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*SPOILER FREE REVIEW*

As a huge fan of pixel horror RPGs, I was very happy to see Mad Father on here; I've watched people play it before and enjoyed it enough that I wanted to have a go myself, and it did not disappoint!

Technically speaking, the controls are very simple and the game is easy to navigate. I do have to say, however, that I wouldn't recommend expanding the screen size despite the relatively small window it opens in, as the visuals get VERY fuzzy and distorted. It simply wasn't made for the fullscreen experience. However, unless you're overly finicky about something like that, I can promise you that the smaller game screen does not affect your enjoyment of the game. It's a minor detail that is very easily forgotten about once you start playing.

Concerning the plot, I think it's a great story that isn't too complicated, but it doesn't stop the revelations from being exciting and the intentions of the villain from being disturbing. The fact that I'd already seen the entire game before and knew how it ended made no difference to how much I enjoyed it playing it myself, and I really did. I like the level of challenge in that it was relatively easy to figure out what to do next without getting stuck and having to look it up. The horror aspect of the game is well done in that it doesn't rely on sudden jumpscares or loud noises, but is more subtle in the way it creeps you out. It's in the way something looks or moves. There were some great moments that made me gasp that were simply something in the background moving for a second, or something appearing that wasn't there before. This isn't a plot spoiler, but as an example of such, I love the moment you leave the kitchen while the rabid dog is still in it for the first time, and just as you activate the door, the dog suddenly flies across the room running so fast and is right behind you as the visual cuts to black. There wasn't even a noise that came with it, but I genuinely freaked out for a second cos it was unexpected and very sneaky! There are a lot of great moments like that that keep you on your toes. I also really appreciate the game's very sparse use of voices and speech, as pretty much the only voice you will hear throughout the game is the protagonist's little gasps, unsure noises and screams, and then the odd monster groan. Hardly any other characters are voiced, which means that when you do suddenly hear a voice out of the blue, it has that much more impact. Again, I'm not going to spoil, because this one does involve a MASSIVE plot point, but there's a hugely important character that never speaks aloud until a certain moment, and when they do, it's the most creepy, malicious sounding voice of the whole game, yet almost an intimate murmur-in-your-ear in the way it's delivered... it gave me a full body shiver because it was so creepy!

The plot was decently paced and the game felt like the right length for the story, not too long or short. The twists are, while potentially easy to spot in advance, very enjoyable and you really get a feel of being that protagonist trapped in a horrible situation where you just want the people you love to be alive and happy and all get along, but it just doesn't seem like it's able to work out that way, forcing you to make very tough decisions and choose between people you'd rather not. There are several endings to achieve, and the 'true' ending definitely has a sinister twist to it that you really don't expect after going through what you went through. There is also a very cleverly written and very mysterious character that I still don't believe anyone truly understands. Once you play it, you'll know exactly who I'm talking about.
He appears friendly and helps the protagonist, yet he then goes on to do certain things behind her back that contradict that, and his true intentions are never revealed, leaving him to perpetually walk the line between good and evil without you ever understanding who he is and what he's trying to achieve. I felt loyal to him one moment and scared of him the next, and I love his character because he infuriates me! I want to know!

One last thing that's important to say; Mad Father was being sold at Halloween in the Bloodsoaked Bundle with the game Misao. They might not be bundled anymore, but once you've played Mad Father, I still highly recommend you play Misao because... in the least spoilery way possible, there's a continuation. The actual main games seem completely unrelated to each other, but let's just say there are characters and themes that are related to Mad Father that appear unexpectedly with no warning in Misao. If you weren't familiar with Mad Father, you wouldn't even realise. In the small moments that they do appear, they give you extra information on the more mysterious and unresolved elements of Mad Father that, while not providing a definitive answer, set a lot of possibilities running through your head. I very much like that sneaky little extra. Do try it for yourself.

Right, I've rambled enough! Short version: Mad Father is one of my very favourite games of this genre and you should definitely give it a go. Interesting plot, creepy villain, drama and deceit, running for your life, and mysteries that never quite get an answer... also, who doesn't love a good chainsaw?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed the original back in the day, along with Misao, Witch's House, Ib and others.
The remake made everything even better for me, as far as i remember the old one.

This game is a japanese horror story centered around a girl whose father is a mad scientist, her mother is dead and they live in a mansion. Sounds cheesy, but trust me, it's not. It's almost like a visual novel, but with actual exploration, puzzles and hidden secrets.

The story have a lot of twists and while i think it's amazingly well written, i do not recommend it to children or anyone really bothered by dark toned stories. It's not about the jumpscares. There are almost little to none on this one. (I only remember a single one that was actually frightening) It's the tone of the story. A bit too cruel.

If you're fine with that then give it a go, it's gonna keep you busy for at least 3 hours (maybe less).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo... I am actually pretty scared of horror games, but I love this style of pixel games, so I decided to buy it and try it out
Funny thing is, I actually got really scared after playing it for 10mins, so I went into a friend's dorm, and made her sat next to me to play it (<w<;; But it was also to pressure myself to play it so that it won't be a waste of money)
Anyway!! I'm sure that for some people this is actually NOT scary at all, but that's not the reason of why you should avoid or just buy the game for, it actually has a reaaaaaally nice story. Like I would give it a 9, and 0.5+ for the after story, leaving 0.5 for the errors, bugs, to not to be as biased, blah blah blah.
The game mechanics are really simple, aaaand *epic music* collecting things in this game is easy, fun and important, so you don't feel like you are wasting time looking for the orbs, looking through every room, and clicking everywhere to find something (I have quite the OCD when it comes to collecting things ingame)
The logic/puzzle is pretty simple once you get the grasp, and even if you get murdered, burned, bitten, killed, chased to death by your loving father (that part did scare me REALLY bad) , you can aaaalways find a way out. Oh oh, however there are this few places where its all about speed reaction, so good luck!!
Uhm... So overall, yes, I would recommend this game!! If you are scared, just drag someone, tie her/him in a chair to force him/her to play instead of you while you watch ;3\t\t\t\t\t
