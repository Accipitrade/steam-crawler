\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay:
The gameplay is relitively easy, though it does involve a lot of problem solving and searching for items. In the remake, the map is a lot larger in scale, thus it takes quite a bit to find what you need and figure out where you need to go on a first play through. This experience enriches the idea of thinking outside of the box (even though hints are thrown at you,) and a requirement of trial and error. Combat isn't in this, but there are some thrills, especially (SPOILERS) when Father chases you down or out of nowhere the corpses or dolls appear.


Visuals:
Both original and remake versions of Mad Father provide a classic style of pixel third person RPG games, and still yet inculdes the horror aspects to it and the thrill when escaping enemies. Arguably, low graphic games are just as good as high graphic games in the horror genre. Even a pixelated doll made me jump in surprise in the crying girl mission.


Design:
Beautiful and basic designs that are yet so iconic to this game. We have the innocent "Alice in Wonderland" style our protagonist Aya has, along with the corpses with chilling designs either friend or foe. Father's design is very simple and straight to the point symbolizing thats all that he is: a psychopathic scientist. Maria's design gives a mesh of a maid and nurse look; very old school and shows the time period this game could take place in (Late 1700's to 1800's perhaps,) and signifies her role as one who serves. Monika's (Mother) design is elegant and brings a warm presence (contrasting her role in the story and her past.) The dolls appearances are well, doll-like, unsettling stares of "lifelessness" only to be proven wrong with their sentience.


Soundtrack:
Absolutely iconic and atmospheric. The title screen theme says everything and nothing about the game; a sad tone, but piano keys in which representing a child's view. The floor s and rooms have different themes, thus creating a new experience as you dwell deeper, and deeper into the Drevis Household. The opening credits playing Old Doll is absolutely iconic and sets the tone for what kind of game you are about to play; an epic entrance similar to that of a movie.


Story:
The story is rich in lore and mystery. On the surface, it seems like a family feud with a paranormal twist, but in reality there is so much more to discover by reading diaries, paying attention to the little details in rooms and articles, talking to NPCs, etc. The story behind the surface is very twisted and morbid and is the foundation to the lore behind the Drevis family line. The pacing is slow due to the majority of getting through the missions and puzzles, but it is indeed an emotional and grotesque conflict that our "hero," Aya must face and come with a final decision for herself. The multiple endings provide different realities the player can choose for themselves. What really is the solution to all of this? Grant mother's wish? Save father? Save myself? Become a doll? Save Maria? Continue the family research? There are many realities this game touches, and it gives many options instead of just one final conclusion.


Characters:
The characters in this game are very well written. They appear straight forward when really they all have depth and backstories to follow. In the remakes, they provided more about Dio, the corpses, Monika, etc. These backstories tell many things about the character's motives and why they are the way they are, or even divert expectation from what you thought you knew from the Main Story.


World:
As said before, there is a ton of lore explained in the new Blood Mode version in the remake about the Drevis family and what led up to this very moment in time, when the curse uprooted. Their history is a twisted one, and shocks the players and making them piece together what they thought they knew to what Blood Mode explains. Even views on the main character change, along with Monika and Father. These stem theories, especially tying them to IF Mode, and only leaves you to piecing the puzzle to your own interpretation.



CRITIQUE:
I think this game was perfect in its own way, but to some people, they would find the pacing difficult to sit though. I myself am an impatient gamer, but when given a rich story, I take my time; but that isn't everyone. The game is about 3 hours in total long due to extensive dialogue, many cut-scenes, and puzzles the player has to get through. My record for this game is about 45 minutes of gameplay, includng all 20 gems. Its relatively short, but I can understand certain areas would leave people uninterested.\t\t\t\t\t
