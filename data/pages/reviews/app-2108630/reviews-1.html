This one just feels forced.

Sure, we got a bit of new scenario, and some background story involved.

New mechanics to dig out cave? Sure...but that means you spend more time just getting from point A to point B.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've got REALLY mixed feelings about this one. The new towers are great, but the campaign in this DLC is absolute ♥♥♥♥. I don't know how big of an impact my hunt for bioanomalies has had on my opinion as I fully uncovered every zone in search for said anomalies, but in general it felt more like a painful chore than a joyful experience.

At first we're being introduced to sci-fi necromancers (quite literally) who stay away and keep spawning new creatures right under your feet for no ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ reason. It's like the giant broodmother-like bugs that send swarms of dragonflies at you, except they are highly aggressive, target you off-screen and can spawn their meatbags through ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ walls. And especially in the later parts of the DLC it gets to the point where you have to face up to 8 of them at the same freaking time.

If that wasn't enough, say hello to ye olde diggy diggy hole program... Don't get me wrong, I do like me a nice cave (especially with bioluminescent flora, I'm looking at you, Aberration...), but you're literally forced to dig out HALF OF THE FREAKING AREA in order to get to the mission-related waypoints, find an additional location and track down anomalies. Sure it goes smoothly with the built-in bore (hold space) but it slows you down *a lot* compared to your regular pace in a full combat rig and adds additional time before you can utilize your weapons and shoot ♥♥♥♥ down which is annoying.

Speaking of which, don't you love when random crap that's either off-screen or behind 50000 mobs goes kabloowie and causes a chain reaction that blows your ass up because you couldn't even see the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ thing? Yeah, this absurdly obnoxious game design found its followers among the developers because they decided to added a buttload of that stuff in certain zones and this ♥♥♥♥ got me killed 11 times within 3 hours, and for reference: I haven't died a single time during the main campaign and Metal Terror DLC (which is far superior and actually good fun).

The last nail to this underground coffin is this horrible abomination they wanted to call a "boss fight". Not only are the dialogues long and boring (outside of the one moment where we decide to touch the core instead of blowing it up, the dialogue becomes quite funny at that point but the choice itself has literally no meaning for the plot), the boss'es animations are really quite bad and the fight itself doesn't fit into the game at all, it's more like The Binding of Isaac type of fight (shown in the preview video, like pretty much all things I mentioned here) coming out of nowhere, it would've been much, much better to just have a HUGE, like an actually gargantuan horde of creatures to fend off at the same time and just have the giant-ass worm be a commander that gets killed in a cliche way.

All in all I don't think this DLC is worth it unless you really fall in love with one of the new features or are a huge fan who wants additional content to sink some hours into (rough estimation on my end oscillates at around 8 hours to clear literal 100% of the DLC's content).

4/10, annoying and disappointing, saved by dark-cave-with-bioluminescent-flora vibes\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is marked Recommend, but...

If you like the base game, and have already experienced Metal Terror, then this DLC will feel like a... "development", for lack of a better term. The base game is all about tower defense, and Metal Terror had that too, but Metal Terror also gave you missions where you were on the offense. When you were going up against giant cannons and breaking down walls in your way (in MT), you realized that it was a different experience than the base game.

Into The Dark has it's own unique feel, where you are doing more digging and objective based work, as opposed to tower defense or offense. Of the (I think 6??) maps in this DLC, only one is actually going to stick around if you have an Outpost on it. The others all go "This map is going to no longer be accessible because of REASON". As part of the plot, there's an actual Boss Fight (actually, there's two of them, but it's basically the same boss). And by boss fight, I mean like a big dangerous enemy like there is in the Devs' game X-Morph Defense. So that's cool.

But after you complete the plotline, well, there's not really much reason to come back to the outpost you did make. There's nothing unique of any real value in the biome, and because it's underground, none of your normal plant cultivation options are possible.

As a reminder though, the Survival version of the Crystal Cavern biome is freely accessible with the base game, so definitely try that survival out first.

TLDR: This DLC is a different feel from both base game and the other DLC, but once you complete the admittedly interesting plotline, there's no reason to come back to this biome. Also, the difficulty spike of this DLC is quite a bit, so make sure to only actually start it once you are in mid-to-late game in the normal campaign.

If you want to try something new and want to support the Devs (you should, they are awesome. Join the Discord!), then give this a shot. If you aren't certain, try the Crystal Cavern survival in the base game, and wait for a discount.\t\t\t\t\t
