\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love 2D/3D platformers (mainly those that would fall into the category of precision platformers); I went into this game hoping to find another great platformer, and Germinal did not disappoint. The jetpack gimmick feels nice, and jumping is pretty floaty to complement the amount of aerial movement required in the game. There are collectibles hidden throughout each stage, and they each provide engaging challenges. Each stage of the game explores different mechanics with level design, and they all work quite well. The time trial mode was rewarding and enjoyable. The harder versions of each stage were difficult but still fair. The art is wonderful, and the game sounds amazing. I do have a few nitpicks from my experience, though.
  • While the art style is great and pleasant to look at, a few of the areas look extremely similar aside from a change in the background color.
  • The ending of the final stage can easily be cheesed by avoiding the germs that start the chase sequence
  • There is a boost that can be performed by holding the hover button on the ground and jumping, but in my experience, it never felt practical to use it often since the stages were not designed around it. Trying to boost immediately after landing will often result in hovering just above the ground.
  • During the time trial mode (in the first stage), I respawned inside the ground collider, and I was forced to reset the run (this was the only bug I had encountered, though).
  • While the level design is mostly great, I noticed many curved or diagonal walls. To me Attempting to jump off of those walls will promptly result in hitting the bottom of a tile. In a game that often utilizes wall jumps to keep the player airborne, the curved walls felt pretty uncomfortable.
  • You are not able to pause during dialog.
Germinal is an underrated game. if you enjoy precision platformers like Celeste, you should definitely try this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe simplest description for this is Celeste meets mario sunshine. That's ultimately why I got it so if that's what you want then it is definitely that.

Though with that being said, this game really is inspired by celeste. You can tell looking at it, but the level design and obstacles also feel really similar. Not inherently a negative, but depends on your feelings about that. Personally I think there are a couple design ideas from celeste they borrow that don't particularly work with the system they have. The main one I feel shows this off is they have a refill system like celeste, except this game doesn't have a dash button. it has a double jump and hover. It is used fine here but sometimes you are expected to use it to refresh a double jump quickly and it can get really finicky trying to chain double jumps between normal jumps and movement.

Also some collisions can be annoying and sometimes I think it prioritizes the wrong actions. It almost feels like wall jumps and wall slides are considered the most important action you can take and it will make you mess up a lot. This actually also lead to a really weird issue where the game seems to want you to go over a wall with spikes on top but it's inconsistent on if you will go over it or not. And I had issues at the last level where a jump was on a spiky "stair" shape and I managed to get stuck on the edge. And in this game you can't recover on the same wall like in celeste.

Also the story is the other major part of this game. I didn't particularly care for it. It isn't bad or anything but I wasn't super interested in a pandemic story. Maybe when I'm farther away from the actual pandemic.

Ultimately this is a neat game with some quirks that I wish was a little less similar to celeste.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tParts of this game are excellent, particularly the basic moveset and the controls. Having the double jump and hover work off of the same resource makes for really interesting and challenging platforming. Other parts of this game though are pretty flawed. To address the elephant in the room, yes this game is a bit of a Celeste clone. Both games have similar movement mechanics and physics, effectively the same art style, similar level design, particle effects, etc. Not to mention that both are precision platformers with an introspective story centered on mental health featuring LGBTQ characters and themes. As long as none of that bothers you it doesn't really get in the way of the game. Maybe with the exception of the art style, like in both games the primary environmental danger are the blobs of black mixed with a colour and they look nearly identical in Germinal as in Celeste. My main complaint about the game unfortunately is the story, and while that is never the part of a game I gravitate to I couldn't help but feel that Germinal's story was far too much of a focus for how uninterested I was in it. The characters all aggravated me in some way, so the game pushing me to relate to them at different times was a struggle as I found them (primarily Val and the player character) standoffish and annoying. Overall though this is a good game, and while it is very short (if you're familiar with Celeste you will probably finish this game in 5-10 hours) I think considering how limited the market is for precision platformers that scratch the itch at all like Celeste or Super Meat Boy it's worth it. Maybe wait for a sale.

EDIT EDIT EDIT: I forgot to mention some quirks with the controls and movement mechanics that can be pretty infuriating. First off (unlike Celeste), Germinal doesn't have much in the way of lee-way or Coyote-time, you double jumped while 1/100 of that platform was behind a jump-activated wall? Try again. Also fairly often the game will have you using particle physics to destroy obstacles which (similar to Spelunky 2's liquid physics) introduces too much indeterminacy into what should be a very cut-and-dry physics system. Finally I would like to point out one of the largest sources of aggravation from this game, the fact that even when facing away from a wall if you press jump mid-air if you are close enough to a wall you will do a wall jump instead of a double jump. This really felt unintuitive to me and felt limiting to how you could approach the levels and even how the developers could have designed them, though really it is something you should just get used to.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is quite fun. Lots of unique mechanics, inventive level design, good music, satisfying particle fx, and neat speedrunning mechanics and skips. Carrying the cat through the campaign has been a satisfying and fair challenge, even in the hard mode levels. The story resonated with me in a big way because I was very germaphobic in the first year of COVID. I had never been germaphobic before, and it impacted my relationship with my ex significantly.

Room for improvement:
-Mainly the spawns, a lot of rooms have poor respawns with unnecessary walkbacks. There's also a room in the final checkpoint of the final gameplay chapter with several midway points that feel like checkpoints but are not. I'd love to see the respawns overall improved.

-Corner correction when there is a hazard on a surface. There are lots of times in the game where you are jumping over a pillar which has germs to kill you on top of it. It's very easy when attempting to jump over these pillars to clip the corner on your way up, and it would have been nice to have more leniency here overall.

-The speed of the hover particles. The fall speed of the particles, specifically when you hover, is very very slow, and this led to some frustrating deaths (especially with the cat) when trying to hover over some germs and then land on the floor upon clearing them. I think these particles could use a small increase in speed.

-A tutorial for the secret boost mechanic. Especially to explain what the difference between the two is. I still haven't figured out precisely how it works, or what the difference is, as I unlocked both achievements at the same time.

Overall, I would say this game deserves a lot more praise and recognition than it received. I genuinely think fans of hard platformers would enjoy the game. If you don't care for the story, turn on auto-cutscene skip (this is incredible to have as a feature by the way).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGerminal is a good platformer with precise controls, an okay story and mostly good level design. The game is however held back by poor readability, somewhat janky fluid physics and suspicious hitboxes. If I could, I would give this a "sideways thumb". The game itself is decent, but there are many smaller frustrations piling up throughout the game.

The core gameplay itself is solid, and does not overstay its welcome. You have a very high jump, a fast-fall, wallslide and walljump. What makes this game unique is your stamina based abilities to hover and double jump.

That said, the game suffers from some frustrating moments which come from either janky gimmicks or visibility issues.

First of all, the biggest offender, the double jump. This shared the same button bind as your jump and wall-jump. With no option to re-bind the boost/double jump, you may find yourself wall-jumping when you mean to boost, and boosting when you mean to wall-jump. The walljump is very sensitive, almost like there is a magnet pulling you to the wall, which is good in most cases, but mixing these items will get you killed as stage gimmicks react differently to your boost and wall jump.

Secondly, the ceiling-climb. The game may require you to climb on the ceiling. The game will often have you climb the ceiling and jump off, often around a corner and on top of the ceiling you were just climbing on. This would be okay if it weren't from the fact that there is no "swing" mechanic to let you gain distance from the ceiling you were just climbing on. You will often find that when attempting to do this action, especially in succession, you will often time "bonk" on the ceiling and fall to your death, or you will climb too far and just fall off. I tried different distances while in a safe area, and it felt rough.

The third problem which sticks out throughout the game is poor visibility. Now, I play many different games, and I have never considered using color or visibility accessibility features in any game before this one. In the end, I decided I wanted to play the game with the default settings, but man it gets rough. There is a lvl where you have a thick, purple outline covering the border of your screen, so you can only see the middle. This "fades" into the lvl. Germs which are green look purple in this mode, and I died many times to thinking I could clean something, but they were a different kind of germ. The lvl design is also not made with this tick outline in mind, as it completely covers and hides important gameplay tiles you will need to finish the lvl. You have to do multiple leaps of faith "off screen" and randomly press the jump button hoping you found a wall, or jump off into the distance hoping the trail of germs the game is showing you will end when you reach the end of the screen.
While the purple screen smoke is only there for a small part of the game, the game also suffers from boxes in the background sometimes looking like solid platforms, but when you try to land on them they're not, the "trash" in the party lvl sometimes blended so well into the background I could not see them while rushing through a lvl, the "pillows" that jump at you when you use water are sometimes not telegraphed, they will just hit you in the face the first time you try to complete that room like it's a "gotcha" moment. Also, the trails of flying trash have transparent smaller trash flying after it, this also looks like it is just a visual effect, but it is not. Some levels are so dark you just have to guess where to go and hope you land correctly.

You will often have to clean areas where you are landing, this sounds fine, but the jetback water feels inconsistent, especially when cleaning vertical pillars of germs.

Making a game like this, in the artstyle they chose, it cannot avoid getting compared to Celeste. Other than the graphics, the story, and it being a room-based precision platformer, it does not try to be celeste. However, with the comparisons the devs are welcoming making a game like this, I am not sure they do themselves any favours. While the game is good, it simply cannot compete with what it is undeniably going to get compared to.

So, should you play this game? Maybe. If you are looking for a decent B-grade platformer to play over a weekend and you already played the great ones, this is for you. I enjoyed my time with it, and I am sure you will too. That said, I do not think this is for everyone. There are some rough edges, I found that the music was a mixed bag containing everything from "please turn this annoying music off" to "okay, this is quite pleasant to listen to", I did not care for any of the characters, and I found myself actively disliking the main character.

This game is made by a small teams of devs, and they should get all the praise in the world for releasing their game in the vision they had. I hope they are successful enough so they get to continue making new experiences for others to enjoy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGenuine review for once because I respect the devs a lot as people and probably haven't looked forward to a game as much as this one upon hearing its announcement a year or so back.

Anyway, you can tell right from the get-go the passion that went into this. It had a simple story to tell and it told it more subtly, which I find fascinating.

From a visual standpoint, the elements say more than enough. Polish was a priority and it shows. I wasn't following the development process itself all that closely, but I am very impressed with how the looks of the game came out, especially as a first (and indie) project. Nothing looks rushed and everything blends together extremely well. The atmosphere that is being portrayed hits hard, as I'm sure it does with most of us if the premise wasn't already obvious.

Now for the gameplay. I don't usually seek out platformers, especially given that I'm quite bad at them, but this was a great first experience out of my comfort zone. Every stage adds a new mechanic or a new layer on an already existing mechanic, and I find that type of progression very intuitive. My personal favorite mechanic that I've seen (so far) is the reactive objects, where if you jump then an object moves. It was very fun and challenging to learn the path to either avoid them, or use them to progress. Nothing got stale in this aspect and I loved experiencing a new challenge every time. I also love how the devs instinctively made speedrunning as viable as possible, giving it it's own mode and also designing the stages around that (little waiting sections). Essentially, the way the stages were designed can appeal to everyone.

I'm still not done with the game at the time of writing this (I accidentally left the game on when I was away from home for 3 days lol), and will probably update this more when I get further, but this well exceeded my expectations. Despite my lack of experience in the platformer genre, I feel confident in saying that this is a game worth looking up to from a development standpoint. This was a great entry into my platforming experience and an excellent first project from the studio. If you take on anymore projects in the future, I very much look forward to seeing them through. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I have very mixed feelings about this game. I love it, but I hate it as well. These two worlds kept colliding for the whole duration of my playthrough. I played all of this game in one single sitting. It took 5 hours.

So, let's start by why I hate it. This game is basically a Celeste clone, with only different graphics, and some new things. I, unfortunately, couldn't get past this reality while playing Germinal. Next up, the controls. Given that Germinal is a precision platformer, good controls are necessary. The controls are clunky and sometimes don't react that well, walljumping off of sticky surfaces while holding onto them either triggers the double jump or just hover ability by accident, so game over. The movement feels off, it's like playing Super Mario on Nintendo Switch, the character needs a lot of "steering" to control them properly. This issue isn't present in Celeste, however. Madeline moves exactly as you press the keys on your keyboard/tilt your Joy-Con. This is by the way one of the reasons why I love Celeste so much, because the controls are 100% accurate.

I feel like this game was not playtested properly. There are tons of spaces with incredibly strange timing or bad camera. By "incredibly strange timing" I mean those frame perfect cycles that are everywhere in the fifth chapter. And by "bad camera" I mean places where you usually drop down and you see nothing beneath you until you actually hit an obstacle off-screen. The camera certainly needs a lot of improving.

Achievements were, for some weird reason, malfunctioning. I did not earn the achievements for comleting "Crossing the Bridge", chapter one and chapter two. While we are at it, I would also like to note that there are, according to some other player, more bugged achievements like this, to which the developer openly admitted. There is no way one could release their game if they knew major features were broken.

Last, but certainly not least, the collectible exclusive rooms has one massive flaw - usually, you only have to grab the collectible, it gets collected and then, you just are stuck there. The only way out is to either get lucky with leap of faith blind falling down, hoping you don't hit anything or just die. These really mess up the death score. I think there should be something that would return the player to the room start upon grabbing the collectibles in certain rooms.

To summarize things I like, I really liked the story, it was really dark and creepy at times, it was not like Celeste whose story was sad with funny moments at times, Germinal was incredibly dark and it was talking about a very serious problem that certainly needed some attention. One thing I like as well are the notifications when you are about to enter a room that does not allow you to go back. I wished this was a feature in the original Celeste, as I very often found the correct path by accident and missed the oppurtunity to collect the collectibles, meaning I had to go back later. I also liked the Super Mario 64 like thing in fifth chapter (jumping into paintings).

To summarize it, I had fun while playing Germinal, but, like I said, I can't get over the fact that this is basically a clone of Celeste, along with a lot of bugs and inconsistent controls. The soundtrack wasn't anything extraordinary either, it was pretty much average. The only really praisable things are the level design, which is very decent and the story, that's basically everything I can think of. I honestly think that this is a very controversial game. I think you should play it, but I think you shouldn't play it too. I don't know, but I didn't feel like I was playing a new game at all. I felt like I was playing a Celeste mod instead. A paid Celeste mod. Which is absolute , because all Celeste mods are free.\t\t\t\t\t

the game seems to assume you have a fair amount of experience with Celeste, as the system mechanics as well as your control of your character feel very similar. it almost feels toooo Celeste, in that the mechanics are very obviously similar which is fine, but the art direction is also extremely Celeste. again, this is fine, but i sort of wish the visuals at least had a bit more of their own identity? the cutscenes/character dialogue/texts all add their own sense of flavour, and at the end of the day it feels like its' own thing, and is presented extremely well.

while it might put you off that the game is so Celeste-like, it is very good so far, and i think can stand in the upper echelon of 2D precision platformers.\t\t\t\t\t
