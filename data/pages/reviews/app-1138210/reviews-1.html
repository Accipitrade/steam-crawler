\r\n\t\t\t\t\t6.5/10

Good pick if you exhausted all Fire Emblem games and want more.
Caution is advised otherwise.

It is made using the same game engine as Vestaria Saga, which is both a good and a bad thing.

Good includes a generally robust game system, with AI that exploits correctly terrain and counterattack ranges, and a variety of item mechanics to play with.

Bad includes visual, audio bugs, as well as generally subpar presentation and production values:
- Music no longer playing after a while with error dialog popping up (restart game to fix)
- Badly looping music for some music tracks
- Combat character sprites disappearing after a while (restart game to fix)
- Some oversights regarding text/typos
- Generic text font instead of a dedicated stylized game font
- Transition to combat screen is not very clean
- Lack of UI flair with everything appearing instantly (no cool animations)
- Subpar map graphics and sprites especially on full screen
- Somewhat cheap combat character graphics with ackward movement glitches, some of which are stock assets from SRPG studios (I noticed because I remember them from Vestaria Saga)

No bug was critical enough to cause the game to crash, thankfully.

My run clocked in at roughtly 20 hours, I missed some optional chapters and characters.
While I steamrolled the game in normal difficulty by mercilessly milking EXP and building broken characters (lol Nina), I think the game has nasty difficulty spikes in some chapters that will catch offguard more laid back players. It is very easy to get yourself in a situation that requires save scumming (thankfully the RNG seed is changed when loading a save).

Plot and characters are OK, but developpers really should get an editor. Some dialogs should be cleaned up. Some sexual allusions should IHMO be toned down (even if the point was to portray that character as a dirty pig).
"By all the dragons!" repeated ad nauseum and showing excitement in VN scenes with "!!!!!!!!!" lines look amateurish.

Anyway it's a fun game overall, FE fans should consider playing it !\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate: I played a bit more, and this game is more mature that I thought when bought it. So far it's tasteful and doesn't beat you over the head with it, but if a parent is reading this, think more towards Game of Thrones than Lord of the Rings. I wouldn't want to explain to a middle schooler some of whats going on between the lines.

This is just indie Fire Emblem. The mechanics are exactly the same. From reading the store page I thought it was an RPG closer to Dragon Quest or Pokemon, but I'm not complaining.

There are hints of this being made by one, or very few people. Little things like character portraits not overlapping correctly or being cut off at the edges, and the music transitions never cross-fade.

There's also all the UI lack-of-clarity problems that come with the Fire Emblem games, namely never being quite sure if you're about to pass a unit's turn when doing anything besides attacking.

The story is very medieval and fantasy. It's somewhat basic and I think I see the plot twist coming while I'm on chapter 2. That's not necessarily bad either. The character interactions also follow the Fire Emblem formula.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely loved this game. if you liked the GBA Fire Emblem games, you will like the gameplay and the game will feel immediately intuitive.

Fire Emblem is my favorite series and this is clearly heavily inspired by that. Not just in obvious ways, like a weapon triangle or durability, but in other ways, like extra recruitable characters where the recruitment method is hinted at in the pre-map dialogue, or the map itself. Or supports between characters, which is very reminiscent of GBA Fire Emblem.

However this game is not a Fire Emblem game - it is not a AAA game, it is not polished, and it is a little janky. It feels like what would happen if kaga got really into anime and also had to translate the game to English himself. It has bugs (although nothing game breaking or really bothersome - there were some odd bugs that felt like the result of a memory leak and resolved upon reset). Some non playable characters don't have portraits. The music varies in quality from fantastic to completely forgettable. It has a lot of grammar mistakes. Sometimes the dialogue between chapters takes 45 minutes to read if you read every word (they are not kidding when they say it is a visual novel).

If you're frustrated by a lack of polish in games I would stay away from this game. Otherwise, if any of the package interests you, I highly recommend it. Try out the demo if you're on the fence. I put in 28 hours on my first play through and will undoubtedly play a second time and likely more, so the value of it for me at full price was more than fair.

Lastly, the developer is very active in the discussion pages in the community hub, answering questions and responding to/fixing bug reports, so although I didn't experience anything serious or gamebreaking, I feel they would address it in a timely manner if you did.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Esperia is a fun game! If you're a fan of SRPGs I encourage you to check it out.

Things I liked:
• The support system has lots of potential for making units broken. When two characters get a B support, they exchange one skill.

• The story was alright. It's nothing super surprising, but can get pretty emotional at later parts. The characters themselves are neatly written; I like how they'll sometimes appear past their intro chapters.

• The gameplay variety is very nice. There's varying objectives in there, and some maps have side objectives to them.

• I like that class progression is linear. When playing the newer FE's, for example, it can sometimes get overwhelming to have a bunch of different class options to consider. Here, the biggest choices are just, who do you want to use.

• Of what I saw, the supports were really nice! They add a lot of details to the characters that you wouldn't see otherwise.

Things I didn't like:
• Money economy is pretty tight. It's kinda nice in terms of difficulty, but I felt like there wasn't much room to experiment with the more expensive charms, since promoting costs quite a bit of gold.

• It was pretty hard to get supports unless I was specifically placing the units nearby each other. though i think the updates have added some new maps to help with that.

• The cutscene's music is fine, but the map music is pretty repetitive. Crucial chapters / sidequests have different music, but the majority of the main chapters have the same map theme. It's a catchy song lol, but would've been nice to have a second one at the halfway mark

Despite that though, I totally recommend it. There's a demo you could try out too\t\t\t\t\t

If you're looking for a FE inspired game with a bit more story and less means, go for it !
I'm honestly surprised by the quality of this game. Though keep in mind it's still a very small indie game and it has some issues inherent to lack of funds !

I'm a huge Fire Emblem gamer since my childhood and for games in this category, I'm looking for 2 things :
- A big tactical challenge
- Heavy focus on characters interactions in order to get invested in my favorites units.

Esperia manages to get both of those crucial elements right. And I mean REALLY right + a story that's compelling (but not groundbreaking) for now. If you want a more detailed review, check below.

Writing : 4/5 (so far)
The main story so far is pretty classic (on par with FE games such as FE 6 and 7), but it has potential to evolve. The intro is a bit too complicated to follow, but after that the pace is just about right. The main story is not where the writing shines though. The main accomplishment is in the characters interactions. They feel organic and not forced. Details like the orphan trios, Lysanna and Melvis, or the teacher with Sara and Elphin are REALLY well made.
Supports are far better than what you would expect from any small indie games, let alone main Fire Emblem games !

One of the issues is that a lot of ennemies do not have 2D sprites for dialogues so it's a bit hard to grasp them and they lack presence.


Gameplay : (4/5)
Gameplay is really similar to old FE games. Weapon triangle, bonds, durability, stats, everything is there. They are a few twists to the formula though. Buffs and Debuffs, special abilities (like FE9 10), possibility to add stats manually sometimes.
I don't have a lot to say about maps, they are like any FE game out there, and a lot of them have small gimmick which keep them engaging. I feel like there could have been a little mechanic added to the mix (like dragon veins) but I might be picky.

I'm playing in hard difficulty, which, to be fair, is REALLY tough. I feel like the first few maps would gain from having a bit more option or a bit less difficulty overall. Especially those with time limited objectives, they tend to be REALLY close. It may be a biiiit too close for my taste though.
Thankfully, you can save mid map (I tend not to abuse it), so this difficulty is not that frustrating. If the dev removes this option in the future, maybe you should lower the hard difficulty a bit


Balance : (2.5/5)
Overall, I feel like there should be a bit more balance testing to the game so far. Elphin is too useful and broken. Some early bosses are way too tough (Chapter 4 and 5). There should be a bit more money added (I'm always broke even though I optimize and buy the cheapest things) and a lot of things need fine tuning in my opinion.
It's not making the game unplayable but few easy changes can be made !


Graphics: (2.5/5)
Big Illustrations look REALLY good. Portraits look good but a bit below FE standard, they feel a bit off sometime. The main issue is with battle animations and UI !
Battle animations are great for a lot of them, but movements are stiff and they lack clarity sometimes (like dodges). This is especially true for walking animations for slow classes. They lack in betweens I guess.
Second big issue is UI. The text is really bland, especially for decision making and menues.
UX should be improved. Switching from characters in the menu is a nightmare and with a little fix it should be resolved.

I'm sad there is a lack of portraits sometimes but that's due to lack of funds I guess.


Music : (??/5)
Overall, I'm not a music guy. I admit there are some tracks that are defintely good (The one when Lysanna cries or the one on chapter 8x) but overall I don't really pay attention to them to be honest.
One thing though ! The main battle music has a loop too short + it restarts every time a battle goes on. It gets obnoxious really fast !



Overall the game is really enjoyable so far, and I compare it to the best FE Fangame (GBA) out there. The writing is overall good with characters interactions being above the rest ! The gameplay is classic but challenging in a good way.

Main issues are due to the scale of the team I'm guessing. I can see the game being way better with a few extra things here and there such as UI, more portraits and more testing.

They are a lot of minor issues (which I'll detail in a steam post later) but they can be fixed pretty easily for the main part ! I hope that will be done.


Thanks again for the game. Congrats on the team. I'll get that updated when finished !

P.S : Sylvie is the best archer i've ever dreamed of. Elphin is broken as hell. My Alex is cursed and I freaking love Sara/Elphin supports !\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't beat the game yet, but my experience until now has been a blast. I'll expand on what are the main points of interest of the game.

Writing

The game mainly uses first person narrative from the heroine's point of view. Some other characters get narration from their side, and the dead aren't forgotten either. Outside of the battles, the game makes a true effort to adopt a visual novel style, with some nice CGs at some points in the story. The visual novel part of the game goes with a pretty interesting plot up to the point where I played.
The typos are pretty unfortunate, but given the sheer amount of text, it was pretty much forseeable for an indie game.

Soundtrack

This one is the strongest point of the game in my opinion. There is still some stock music in the game, but most of it is original, and the composer has got talent.

Strategy

The game doesn't use any fancy mechanics compared to other SRPG Studio games. The watchtowers are probably the most original feature. That being said, the battles are very interesting strategically. They would be challenging if savescumming wasn't so easy: the ability to freely save mid-battle isn't disabled contrarily to what most games of the same vein do.
What is interesting is that some strategic choices have story consequences. The dev probably gave a lot of thought there.

If you feel like my description of the game puts you in its target audience, I warmly recommend it to you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tForgive my half hour of playtime, I played the demo for a few hours and, after a bit of time, I can say that if you play the demo, your save won't carry over and you have to restart the whole thing. That also means I have not finished the game and this review might be outdated once I do, but reviews are important, and I do like the game, so I want to help out.

Let me start by saying if visual presentation is important to you, you won't find it here. Every character has good artwork and the backgrounds are well made, but the presentation is weak. Character poses pop in and out without any transition, enemy commanders look like basic units on the field, the textboxes have no style, and the battle animations are clearly based on SRPG Maker's style. Basically what I'm saying, on the surface, this game isn't going to win any awards for graphics and art direction. Bare minimum to a fault and clearly not being bankrolled by a major company.

That being said, the story and gameplay are great. Sure, every character basically has one pose and 3 facial expressions, but the dialogue and character writing is strong enough to make me care about the characters. Diana is likable but clearly spoiled. Hardworking, but thinks she's better than commoners. It's not an easy balance to do, but the game does it well. The supporting cast feels just as well thought out. Not original, but grounded, likable, and with clear motivations.

Gameplay is basically vanilla Fire Emblem with none of the experimenting that modern games have. Archers can shoot from 3 range instead of 2... and that's all. If you played one Fire Emblem, nothing new is here. But Gimmicks don't matter if the gameplay is fun and this game certainly does that. It's challenging without being unfair. The map design in the early half of the game is neat. You have a Dragon Knight Jagen which is cooler than any horse-riding Jagen. Not original gameplay in the slightest, but it's fun.

Also, the music is great. Like, the composer did a great job on basically every track and I hope they were paid well.\t\t\t\t\t
