Now the cons:
- Soundtrack lacks diversity (Same songs over and over again, which is terrible, not to mention the fact that it doesn't even match the type of subject it tackles)

The part where you get into repairing is nice.
The part where you get out of the workshop is bad.

Worth a try for going chill.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was really hoping this would be a fun, highly technical tutorial on how to maintain locomotives, the kinds of things mechanics have to do in order to keep them in working order. But there are no detailed or guided instructions. You either understand what is going on or seek outside help on websites like YouTube because in-game explanations are lackluster.

The camera movement is very difficult to stomach. The extra weight and bounce when you move the mouse left me nauseated. This is very barebones.

It is CERTAINLY worth the ~$1 I paid. I've paid a dollar for awful arcade games and they are not nearly worth it. This game is more than objectively worth that $1. However, it's clear that this was a passion project. There are some areas like UI, gameplay flow, and camera movement that need a lot of work. Seeing a short ~1-2 min video on what the parts are for a work project and how they work on a locomotive would be a great way to LEARN how to repair them in the game. Otherwise, players have no idea what's going on. I am a huge rail fan that wanted to learn more about the mechanical side of locomotive maintenance.

Unfortunately this seems like an unfinished product.\t\t\t\t\t
