\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHad this thing sitting in my library and gave it another shot recently. Came to the store expecting some 10+$ price tag but seeing how cheap this game is just barely landed it a recommended from me.

At it's core the main gameplay of this game just finding the rusted parts and then disassembling them from the engine, repair and reattach. And that's basically it. Parts are never properly broken other than being rusted and repairing them is as simple as a roll of the dice whether it turns out good or not but what can you expect from a 5$ title.
The "open world" segments this game advertises are rather bland and quite frankly just get annoying when you wanna repair some engines and the game tasks you with finding that one lone locomotive in square X of the map for the 500th time. The developers also clearly have no experience in creating railway maps. The turns are way too sharp for train engines to make at any speed higher than 10kmh, although that's only speaking realistically. In-game you can never actually derail, making the whole towing a locomotive a chore that you just speed through with the controls being at mobile-game-tier simplicity, having only a single slider to determine forward and backward momentum. And don't even get me started on the huge time wasters that is picking up derailed trains with the crane.

But despite everything the dismantling of individual train components may still prove fun, especially for train enthusiasts like myself and when its on sale for under a dollar.
5.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
