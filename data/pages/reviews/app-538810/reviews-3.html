\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis.... This is a mess. Seriously. Every part of this is an awful trainwreck, somewhat ironically. Let's break it down.

For starts, a LOT of effort has been made to make this look Pretty. Which is neat, except a huge amount of it has been skipped in terms of optimization. So sometimes you'll get weird acceleration when you shouldn't, sometimes motion blur will happen when you didn't ask it to, and sometimes post-processing effects will happen when you've turned them off. But that's not the worst of it.

See, these games live and die on their process. One part leads to another part, etc. And there's a lot of this one that's set up properly. Except when it's not. For instance, if you take the bearings off of the crank shaft of an engine... you cannot reinstall them. Period. The Piston and Piston Handle will be able to be installed in their place, but you can never get the bearings back on. But why would I know this?

Well, because I'm a mad lad who likes pushing games beyond their expressed "do this" instructions. QA is my specialty. Anyhoo, doing this lead me to another fun bug. But how did I get there? I was hoping you'd ask that.

By completely and totally disassembling an engine on a train. Piece by piece. Every single bit of it.

But surely that wouldn't create a bug on its own? I mean, aside from the aforementioned reassembly bug where parts aren't even set up so that you CAN reinstall them fully...

Oh, no, it does. For instance, the selective inventory that normally pops when you install a part? Starts not being so selective from time to time. And when it does this, you have to open your inventory and close it again. But why on earth would that be a huge bug? That seems like a mild inconvenience, after all!

Because when your inventory has all of the parts of an engine in it? The game absolutely cacks itself trying to load it each time. Which unfortunately means it cacks itself when it tries to load the repair menus, and takes forever to do so. And also becomes a jittery mess when you try to scroll through that repair menu. And since you can't set up batch repairs, or batch sales of items... yeah. They goofed. And they goofed hard, on this one.

Fix the game's inventory management system, fix the parts installation order thing, fix it so that when a part doesn't exist in the space I'm looking at, I can place the part behind it, and fix the widths on the fuel and ignition lines so they're not so stupidly pain-in-the-butt to click on. And then maybe you'll have the start of an early access title that might be good one day.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, 6 hours in, I think I get the point. It is a little bit of a "rinse/repeat" type game, but so far I'm still having fun. I mean, not like edge-of-my-seat, "oh my god when will work ever end so I can go home and play" type fun... more like a relaxing, satisfying type of fun.

The idea is pretty simple - fix trains. You're given jobs - uh oh, the train is doing this or that, and we think it might be the X system - go in there, find the problem, and get it fixed. Seems like most jobs, the train is wheeled into the repair shop.. in a few of the jobs, you have to go "rescue" the train. (More on that later.) You go in, you inspect parts, find faulty parts, and remove them. Once you've removed them, you can either attempt to repair them, or just order brand new parts to replace, and sell the old for scrap.

One of the things I REALLY like about this is that just like repairing real things, you often don't just "yank out the old part." No, that part will be connected to several other parts, or under layers of other parts - just like a real repair (like working on a car engine or something), you've got to tear your way down to the part, removing perfectly good parts to gain access to the faulty part. This involves a lot of bolt removing, a lot of digging, etc. Some of the parts are dissapointingly simple - for example, on the electric trains, there are a bunch of great big boxes for the engine that you CAN just "yank out." Or maybe remove a part or two, then yank out the giant box. It almost feels like the devs poured a ton of thought into the diesel engines, then got to the electric ones and thought, gee whiz, I don't want to program all that again! There's still plenty of stuff to tinker with, but I mean come on - we all want to work on complicated engines. I have not yet got to the steam engines so no comment as of now.

The gameplay is surprisingly fluid. Given the ad videos, I thought this would be one of those games that once you're playing, the controls are horribly frustrating or janky, but everything is pretty easy to control. The graphics are very fluid and pretty - I enjoy every aspect of it, and it looks beautiful (and it doesn't bog down my graphics card like some of the higher end games can). The sounds are meh (and like some reviews have stated, the train horn DOES sound like TrainHornSound.wav)... and the music was annoying so that was turned off fairly quickly. I really do like the visuals - it's so much fun to tear into the components.

There are a few things I would wish for.

First of all, the "repair parts" mechanics are woefully disappointing. (As stated when you remove a defective part, you can either attempt a repair or just buy a new one.) You would think you'd get some kind of mini game to attempt the repair, but instead you're given a percentage chance of success, and then you click a button. It then tells you if the repair was successful. Whoopie.

There are a few wonky things other reviews will mention as well, that are kinda nit-picky but still wonky. For example, when you go out to "rescue" a train, you can barrel down the tracks at top speed, taking ultra-tight corners without derailing, so it just looks weird. I get it, the "driving" isn't the point, it's the rescue that's the point... it just looks bizarre. Yes, the trainhorn.wav sound effects are disappointing.. Also the route planning is a little dumb. You should be able to look at the map and click track splits to pick the route. Instead, you've got to keep going into and out of the map, clicking route splits from the driving view. Kinda annoying.

I will also say the "tutorial" is woefully inadequate. I mean, it's not the most complicated game ever... you'll figure it out... but it's that kind of game where you're dropped into the world, and then pop-up boxes pop up occasionally to drop a hint if you click something. I really hate aggressive tutorials (No, DON'T do THAT, do THIS!!!!!), but I also don't care for the kind that are like "do whatever, and I'll drop a gem if you click the right thing." That being said, you're probably not going to have a tough time figuring out what to do.

The "crane" mini game is kinda fun, if a little unrealistic (no way one little crane, attached to train car sitting on a track, will pick up an entire locomotive and swivel it around onto the track. lol). Derailed train? Put it back on the track.

Overall? I don't know if it's worth the full eight bucks. It's a hard call - maybe it is, maybe it isn't. I paid a buck on sale, which is a more than fair price. I can already tell this isn't a game I'll be pouring all my free time into - this will be more like one of those games where I'll nerd out for a while, and move on, and maybe come back to visit from time to time. It IS fun, and it IS relaxing, and that satisfying feeling of "fixing the problem" is ever-present, it's just not the most engaging game I've ever played. So if it's on sale, why not go ahead and give it a try? (that's why I put "recommend") It's decent for that "from time to time" type of playing!!\t\t\t\t\t
