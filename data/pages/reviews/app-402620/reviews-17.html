Okujou no Yurireisan~
Kindred Spirit on the Roof is a Yuri VN Game that tells a story about achieving a romance relationship between girls from various perspective. It started when the protagonist, Yuna, discover the "kindred spirits" on the roof despite her lack of spiritual sense and then help them creating a yuritopia in her school by act as a yuri cupid for the other couples. This game also the first uncensored game on steam.

Pros :
+ Really good story
+ Heartwarming and cute
+ 7 couples to ships
+ Great character development and relationship
+ Great soundtrack and art
+ Some major events are fully voiced
+ Uncensored Beewbs (I swear I didn't play for this one)

Cons :
- Not really compatible with steam (No steam overlay, no achievement, can't screenshot, etc.)
- After 10-15 minutes of playing, the text become kinda lag, it doesn't disturb the game itself though, and after sometime It didn't bother me anymore (This issue can be solved by launching the exe directly from the game folder, but I want to save the playing hours)
- Fixed 800x600 resolution, but despite that, it didn't look weird when strectched to 16:9 resolution

Overall it's a really good game and I really enjoyed every second of it. Really recommended for yuri lover out there. I already cleared 100% of the game and now I'm starting to miss playing this game...

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKindred Spirits on the Roof benefits from a rather uncommon (in VNs at least) narrative structure, which positions the protagonist as a facilitator of other couples' relationships rather than one striving for her own. This allows players to get into the character's head as she tries to understand the intricacies and nuances of lesbian romance.

While the VN definitely delivers on feel-good high school yuri, it also reveals the difficulties it poses, especially in a conservative, collectivist society where presenting oneself as normal is paramount. It doesn't do so heavy-handedly, however, allowing the more intimate nature of the different stories to shine throug.

All in all, the different romances are quite engaging, and though some initially left me underwhelmed, I was rooting for all of them by the time the game drew to a close. With the vulnerabilities of first love compounded by what is frowned-upon, if not quite forbidden love, the story comes with emotional weight that can be surprising given its lighthearted mood.

All of this is supported by excellent sprites and a great soundtrack.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst and foremost; I genuinely enjoyed all aspects of this game. The art is nice and consistent throughout, the music is pleasant and I actually really like some of the songs, the story is nonsense at times but such is the nature of fiction so I think that the story actually culminates very well, and I really like some of the characters and the development of these characters throughout the story. It's a cute, yuri romance that I honestly feel could make a great anime. It has drama CDs and a spin-off manga, so why not?

Full disclosure; I am a fan of anime and I am a guy. A guilty pleasure of mine is Nekopara and I have Steins Gate for the Vita. I am a fan of visual novels because I watch a lot of anime that derives from them and I enjoy video games with a healthy amount of good narrative or visual novel-esque mechanics.

I heard about this game because I saw an article online about it being an "uncensored" visual novel on Steam. So I looked up some promotional images for the game and liked the art style. I'm no expert on visual novels, but I've seen images from visual novels where the art isn't consistent. This game has a very consistent manga-like art style that looks good against the game's backgrounds as well.

I went into the game thinking this would be another guilty pleasure like Nekopara but found that I was actually enjoying it for multiple reasons without the need of "ecchi" distractions. Right from the beginning the game grabs me with its openning song. I'm a sucker for some anime songs and there are a few songs with vocals in this game that I really enjoy. The lyrics are nice too and the vocalized songs are sung by one of the character's voice actors, which I think is nice. It's all a bit romantic in a shoujo-esque way, though I guess the game is yuri.

A major reason I was enjoying the game before the ecchi parts, and all-around regardless of anything ecchi; I really like the main protagonist. The protag's name is Toomi Yuna. She has a bit of a tomboy-ish, kuudere-ish vibe in that she is very self dependent and solemn with a "cool" or "cold" look to her character, which is why I found it adorable when she gets caught off guard occasionally and gets spooked or embarrassed, which shows that she is a complex character capable of feeling/showing natural emotions other than those typical of her apparant character. A fault that I hear about in many visual novels, or anime inspired by visual novels, or just anime in general is that the protagonist is unrelateable because they're a blank slate for you to imagine yourself in their shoes, or that the protag is just a terrible one-dimensional character whose involvement in the story boils down to plot for "PLOT"; as in they have all sorts of plot armor, plot hole inducing nonsense to make them important, and for some reason everyone then likes them or rivals them for some reason or another, so they are justified in all the "ecchi" stuff they get coming their way. But no. Kindred Spirits is way different from that nonsense. Toomi Yuna is a good protagonist. She has her own character which is developed and interesting and is part of a story within the game which is sort of seperate yet ties into her involvement with the other characters. What I mean by separate is that she isn't the focus of the game's ecchi story lines. Her own development stems more from her overcoming her own personal problems to become a stronger character that others rely on. Basically in a bad harem anime the protag gets unexplained plot power so that everyone is drawn to them. But in Yuna's case she isn't a harem protagonist, she's just trying to help other girls fall in love. This is difficult for Yuna because of her personal problems making her a bit of an introvert. But at her core she is a good person so she wants to help people when she knows she can. So she gets out of her comfort zone to help people. This makes her analyze herself and others and she comes to terms with why she was introverted in the first place so that she overcomes her problems. Basically she becomes a better character worthy of being relied on, capable of helping others, and therefore a justified protagonist.

So the focus of the plot is that Yuna helps girls who are lesbian find love. These girls have their own personalities told through their own narrative which explains how they feel about things, mainly their love interests. All the couples have their own quirks to them and whether or not you like the couples probably depends on the type of characters you like to see in a romance. People love shipping but tend to hate couples that don't meet their personal preferences. So obviously there were couples that I liked more than others for various reasons; some were cuter, some were more fun, some made more sense. But I liked the way the characters' narratives explained who they were and how they felt as characters. Regardless of what makes sense in the real world, I feel like all the couples are justified, more or less, by the characters' backstories, inner feelings, and interactions.

So this is an adult game in that it depicts relationships in a sexual way and shows implicit sex scenes that are more explained through narrative and vocals than the art itself. They do show boobs, but by todays anime standards it's fairly innocent. Even though it shows sex, it's not oversexualized. Some, if not most, anime today tends to be oversexualized even when it's not adult. Anime also tends to portray risque relationships. This game only really has one risque relationship. The entire game is "safe" in a vanilla sense. That's probably one of the reasons it wasn't censored by Steam. But I enjoy the art in general. I also just enjoy the development of the characters in the story. The sex scenes were "nice" but they were more like icing on the cake of the couples, sort of like a justification to the "ships" or a culmination of the romances naturally leading to intamacy.

So obviously I didn't explain the story too well as to avoid major spoilers. I just want to add that the voice acting helps define the characters along with the art and the narrative. Not everything is voiced, but you can sort of imagine how a character might say something in their voice as you're reading. The game also presents you with "choices" but they're very superficial and don't affect more than a sentence in the next line of dialogue. There is only one route that you simply read through, but visual novels offer more than manga and books because they have art, music, vocals, etc. The ghosts or "kindred spirits" are sort of a reason for the plot and a source for everything nonsensical that keeps reminding you this game is leading towards an ecchi finish line, but I still think that the writing in the lesbian relationships is done fairly well and keeps the relationships realistic or even relateable in most cases. I'm a guy, but I like romance too, so I don't even consider this a guilty pleasure; it's just a good romance story to me. So in all honesty you probably wont like all the characters but I'd say you should play it for the ones you will. Again, regardless of all the yuri romances that the other characters present you with, my favorite character is the protagonist Toomi Yuna. I'd play a non-ecchi game starring her, or watch a spin-off anime about her cooking (because she likes to cook). If you're not sure whether you'll like the characters, just go to the game's official website, it has the rough bios on the characters. But if you're an anime fan who likes a bit of ecchi, likes a bit of romance, and likes "ships", this is the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review will be separated into three parts: TECHNICAL, WRITING, and OVERALL.

TECHNICAL
On a surface level, this visual novel is surprisingly well-constructed. The artwork is crisp and well-drawn (artist is peg and you can find more of their work here!), the score is pleasing to the ears and never feels worn out or tiring, and the sound direction is very solid. The dialogue text display is easy on the eyes by default (you can change the opacity of the dialogue text box). I would've chosen a slightly better font, but that's minor and entirely preferential. The voice actors chosen for this game (side note: not all the dialogue is voiced) are very skilled as is apparent by their work here. Not a single character felt like the VA behind them was mismatched, and a few of them absolutely shone out above the rest. The localization of the actual dialogue is also an extreme selling point. The folks at MangaGamer show genuine care for the product they are localizing for stateside release and it shows in the (verified!) high translation quality and stellar editing of the dialogue which helped give the visual novel life.

WRITING
Once you get past the fact that the game revolves around a pair of lesbian ghosts, the visual novel is...surprisingly grounded in reality. The characters, for the most part, are realistic without being uninteresting (a few of the characters, specifically Koba Youka, I've grown to be very fond of and am astonished at how especially good their characterization was), and the interpersonal relationships are plausible without being lackluster. I haven't read the original Japanese text, so I can't verify if it was originally written as such or if it was the (high-grade) localization that makes it seem this way, but engaging with the dialogue in this visual novel was very rewarding throughout. This all being said, there was one relationship in particular that drew some mixed reception; the teacher-student relationship. In all honesty, it was somewhat awkward and felt a bit rushed, but it was nowhere close to a dealbreaker for me.

OVERALL
I don't read many visual novels, but I do engage with plenty of yuri media. And Kindred Spirits on the Roof is one of the finest entries in both of those categories. Do I consider it worth the $34.99? Honestly, I don't know. It does seem to be a bit on the pricier side (especially since STEINS;GATE is the same price and that's longer and more comprehensive), so on the occasional chance that this visual novel goes on sale, please do get it. It's a truly fantastic work.\t\t\t\t\t
