\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing through this game for the second time with the Full Chorus DLC, I thought it best to update my previous lukewarm review. If you plan on playing Kindred Spirits on the Roof, I wholeheartedly recommend the DLC that adds full voice acting to the game. That said, Kindred Spirits is a Yuri visual novel that has you playing cupid to 5 female couples struggling to get their feelings across to one another. It is a rather linear game, but it does give you some freedom in choosing which couple's story you want to read first after completing the main story for each month. The chapters are broken down into days on a calandar and make it for some nice light reading if you don't have much time to play. It's very easy to put down and pick up again, which I appreciate given my work schedule.

I really love the characters in this game. Out of all the couples in this game, I only disliked one, which in my opinion is quite the accomplishment of the writing team. Each couple is unique and has thier own challenges. I think the clearcut winner is the pairing of Ariu Aki and Koba Youka. A A Ai's lyrics made me cry, it was so awesome and romantic. While the OST does have one AMAZING track, I still think the OST is not very good. For a game this long I think the main 10 tracks are just too repetitive.

Also worth mentioning, the game does have H Scenes towards the end, but they aren't that long and no genitals are shown. It's very tasteful and showcases the appeal of sweet yuri romances. So if you are in it for the sex, I think you will be disappointed.

While going through the extra scenes a second time is kind of a chore, I still love this game and recommend it to any Girl's Love fan.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't let my play time fool you, I definitely got over 20-30 hours of play time out of this visual novel and that's not counting the extra scenes you unlock after finishing the game.

Every 15-20 minutes the game would freeze and become unresponsive when I opened it through steam, so I had to use a work around. I had to go to my game library, right click the game, click “properties,” click the “local files” tab, click “browse local files,” and finally open the game directly from the folder. It was only after doing all that the game worked without any problems. The only thing about opening the game like that is that steam won't register it which is why I only have five hours on record even though I played it for far longer than that.

I had this game on my wishlist for a while now waiting to see if it'd get a better sale, but after a while it seemed 25% would be the best sale I'd get so I decided to finally pull the trigger and I wasn't disappointed. I'd say this is probably the ultimate yuri visual novel experience just based on the amount of content you get and the fact that you have five different yuri couples to get that you learn about aside from the main ones each with their own fleshed out storyline that runs parallel to the main one.

In conclusion, if you're a fan of yuri than this is a must buy especially if it's on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
