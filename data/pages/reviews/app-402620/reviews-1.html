\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKSOTR is a visual novel with a whole lot of different relationships going on at the same time. All-girl cast. Voice acting is perfect. Music is amazing. All the love stories are great and it's just the best game to start up on those chilly, lonely nights when you want to be by yourself with a cozy story.

Sad that I'm just about done with the game. I actually finished the game itself (takes over 40 hours if you have the Full Chorus DLC and listen to the audio as you play), but I have to listen to all the audio drama DLCs (there's 6.5 hours of it, all subtitled in English) and I do have the follow-up manga to read. If you start the audio drama DLCs through Steam, it will count towards your time played. The Full Chorus DLC itself has a handful of short audio dramas, and sadly these are not accessable through the Steam launcher. You'll have to dig those up manually. But the other DLCs actually show up in the launcher and you can log time played with those.

Hopefully I'll come back to this game again soon and replay it from the beginning! Love is all there is, and this game exemplifies that. I will definitely be going through this again! For what it's worth, I took screenshots left and right, and they are the wallpapers on my desktop and my phone, and also the background on my Steam profile (bought with points). I hope this tells you something about how much I liked this game.

----------------

Some of the technical stuff:

- IMPORTANT! Turn the 'Planner Save Prompt' to 'ON'. It's the last setting on the bottom. This causes the game to pause at the end of each scene and gives you a chance to save the game before moving on to the next scene. If this option is left at the default (not enabled), you will have to save at awkward points in the game - it won't let you save on the Planner page if you don't change this setting. Save everytime a scene ends. There's enough save spaces for this, and you will enjoy the game more if you can start it up everytime and have a clean position to continue.

- IMPORTANT! I'm using Windows 11, and for some reason, when I first boot up Steam on my PC and start this game by clicking the '> PLAY' button, it flickers to the 'X Stop' button, but before the game starts, it goes back to the '> Play' button. If it does, the game will play, but it won't log the time you played. You need to exit the game (just keep clicking your mouse until you hit the menu, then exit the game) and start it again. Next time you click the '> Play' button, it will not go back to the '> Play' button, and it will log your time. Something to watch if you want to log your time correctly.

- Cloud saving doesn't work between Windows PC to Steam Deck. So if you decide to play this game on both, you'll have to manually transfer your save files.

- The game isn't widescreen. It's in 4:3 aspect ratio. Just wanted to mention it.

- You can't use the Steam Overlay with this game, so you can't take the usual screenshots in-game, or any of the other usual Steam conveniences you may be used to. You can take screenshots with your videocard's keyboard shortcut, if that's enabled.

- If you buy the 'Full Chorus' DLC to add full voices to the game (which you should), slide the 'Music Volume' slider to about 33%. The center of the circle indicator should be in line with the right edge of the 'Full Screen' button above it. Some of the voices, especially Yuna's, will be hard to hear if you don't turn the music down this much.

- Once you finish the game the first time - and you don't need any guides or tips to do this, just follow your instincts and enjoy yourself - you'll be told you have new 'extra' scenes to play. At this point, look up the Steam guide to unlocking all the extra scenes. Very helpful, and you're not cheating. Newer VNs typically have a story map, and this game didn't have one. The guide will help you unlock the extra scenes. Don't torture yourself.

- If you decide you want to buy all of the DLCs for this game like I did, wait for a sale and it'll cost you just under $50.00 US. The Full Chorus DLC (which to me is mandatory) will add full voices to the dialogue in the game, and it should satisfy you. Getting all the other audio drama DLCs will add little voices here and there to the game, but not necessary. You might not want to listen to all the audio dramas after playing the game. Trust me, once you finish the game the first time, the extra scenes that get unlocked will totally exhaust you. When you finally get the 'Congratulations you finished the whole game' screen, you will be totally satisfied. You might not even want to go through the audio dramas. But if you do, the other thing you might want to find outside of Steam is the follow-up manga, which you can find in a complete volume, that continues the story after the game. I personally went this route. Still have to do the audio dramas and the manga, which I have already acquired. Once done, you can go to the Steam points shop and get yourself some nifty wallpapers and emoticons.

Fingers crossed for an anime adaption someday. This game would blow Bloom Into You and Citrus out of the water if it happened. We need this!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLovely story. The character are all extremely charming in both their visual design and personalities - even those who start out looking like tropes quickly become real people with depth, and I got very invested in all of them. This is helped by the main narrative device which allows it to explore a variety of stories without any of them being cheapened by something like multiple routes.

The first 80% of the game were a pure delight. I cried a lot. The final stretch, while still solid, felt hampered to me by the fact that every sub-story needs to culminate in a Mandatory Sex Scene. For some of them it's a great emotional payoff. For others it feels extremely gratuitous. For a lot of them, the fact that the characters are clearly minors makes the whole thing very awkward and I think the same emotional beats could have been hit in a less graphic way.

Like I'm not saying to hide away an important aspect of young love. Just that they're sixteen and I didn't need to see their nipples.

At any rate, the way the last moments of the game are spent rapid-firing through a bunch of sometimes gratuitous sex scenes really feels like a concession a different genre's expectations and kind of took me out of the experience. I think the game would have benefited from a less formulaic approach that paid more attention to the global pacing as well as each story's needs.

With that being said, the main story ended up going to a very surprising and probably kind of controversial place, and I'll count the fact that it managed to keep me on board as a definite win.

All in all a great experience which I warmly recommend, and I really wish I could keep spending more time with these characters.\t\t\t\t\t
