\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat can I say about this game that will truly capture just how amazing it is? Long have I been wanting a detailed, imaginative yuri visual novel with multiple hours of playtime and a realistic take on romance between women. This is exactly that. As a queer girl, I find that games centered around lesbians and yuri often only focus on the sexual aspects, never accurately representing the emotional, loving parts of the relationship. While this game certainly delivers on some risqué scenes, it really takes your breath away with the cute and heartwarming encounters between characters; particularly of those between love interests.

First off, the soundtrack is really nice to listen to. I wouldn't call it mindblowingly amazing or anything, but the few songs that you do hear are really nice and fit the casual, relaxed tone of the game. There are a few sound effects too, which fit in well with the environment, although I thought some of them like the cicadas could be a bit too loud and distracting. The voice actors are amazing! I must commend all of these lovely ladies for putting their hearts and souls into their performances - no matter the situation, they seemed to nail their lines, and all of their voices were very unique and recognizable from each other. And don't even get me started on the artwork, absolutely stunning. It's not top of the line stuff to be sure, but I LOVE the simplicity of each CG and character sprite, while still being adorable and drawn with so much care. I'd fall in love with any of these girls, honestly lol.

There weren't too many bad things about this game. I would say that it is actually worth its normal price, as I got nearly 30 hours of playtime out of the game fully completed, and there is also extra content at the end (although I am the type of person to listen to every single voice line and read at a moderate pace, so take that as you will). Some scenes did seem a bit dragged on more than they needed to be, and it kind of annoyed me that these HIGH SCHOOL AGE girls seemed to know NOTHING about sex, and that the erotic scenes had them moaning like crazy over a freaking boob squeeze, but overall my complaints are few and far between. The characters were lovable, and their personalities were so different and brought out the best in each other. Their humor and quirks had me smirking and laughing many times throughout the game. One or two scenes even got me a bit teary-eyed. The worst thing about Kindred Spirits on the Roof was that it had to end, but I will keep the memories of it with me for a long time to come.
P.S. SasaUmi and AkiYouka 4ever! (HinaYuna too)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this on a whim during the Steam Summer Sale, thinking it might be something to while away a couple of hours when i was bored.
Instead, I bashed through it in a day, unable to stop playing for any amount of time. Heck, I nearly gave it a standing f***ing ovation when the credits rolled.
An unexpectedly good VN with interesting, realistic characters who feel like real people, both in looks (human rather than photoshopped supermodels) and personality (you won't find anyone here who sits in a trope), and a story which, whilst not intense, is certainly sufficient to make you want to keep going.
The soundtrack, whilst (very) limited (~10 songs are used for the actual novel content), but there are two hidden gems among the music tracks (A A AI is way too catchy).
I think the only issue I have is a moral one with the whole encouragement of teacher-student romance, which isn't something I can particularly support.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been wanting to buy Kindred Spirits since it was announced because it was about girls loving girls. The art was beautiful, the song was catchy, and I loved the trailer. I expected it to be good, of course, but I never expected it to be as good as it actually is.

Kindred Spirits on the Roof follows Yuna, an extremely considerate girl who, due to some circumstances, started avoiding people. This changes completely, however, after she starts seeing two ghosts - or, as they prefer being called, kindred spirits - on the rooftop of her school. Since she's the only one capable of seeing them, they quickly ask for her help to pass on: They want her to help the girls in her school who love others girls to be able to confess their love and start a relationship! - A yuritopia. So, Yuna embarks on this not so easy task.

This game is so good it's actually pretty hard to point out all good things in it. I mean, I can't just say "everything", can I? But, yes, it's pretty much that. There are plenty of characters and romance for you to see developing, and ALL the characters are completely unique. Their personalities and hobbies are different, and you feel like they are all real people. Something that really caught my attention was also that, besides seeing Yuna version of things, you also get the opportunity to see these other girls sides on things. This make such a big difference - and also make you fall in love with absolutely all of them.

Kindred Spirits has only a few choices - which don't matter, they only unlock some secret scenes. What does it mean, then? That it is basically a kinectic visual novel. Now, this is something that might've put me off if I knew it before playing the game, but man, they do a kinectic visual novel WELL. Since the story is super interesting, you get caught in it fast, and the calendar let's you follow different girls in different days, in the order you want, which makes it feels like you're actually making choices and actively playing. I basically pay no mind that this is a kinectic visual novel. I don't even REALIZE it is. This is how great this game is.

The romance is also the forte in the game. We have many couples and each of them have different chemistry, way to deal with things and all that. It feels very real to see their love blossoming. Of course, I have some couples that I love way more than others - how could I not? - but, in general, I pretty much love all of them. The game makes a good job on making some mysterious stuff for you to slowly find out during the storyline, which makes you crave for more and makes it hard to close the game.

Simply put, Kindred Spirits is a really, really good game. Romance and light novel fans will love it, and everyone who likes a good story will too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely one of my favorite yuri VNs. Would recommend if you're into that stuff, much better than other yuri VNs like A Kiss For The Petals series that I've read. I really liked the character design and the variety of couple types explored. The plot is pretty silly but it fits in with the personality of the characters and the overall tone. I will say that some plot threads felt tedious and unnecessary but the overall flow of the story was fine and perfectly enjoyable. The trepidations of the main character, Yuna, get a little annoying after a while but I understand why she would have the struggles that she does with her sexuality, it just gets a little repetitive.

I have mixed feelings about the functionality of the 'planner' set up, which isn't something I've seen in other VNs. I like that you can easily rewatch scenes or jump around to specific characters that you're interested in but it is a bit annoying to be pulled back to the planner after each and every scene, kind of makes the overall experience feel kind of disjointed. I do like the concept but I think the execution could've been a bit smoother.\t\t\t\t\t
