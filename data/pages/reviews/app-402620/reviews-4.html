\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great visual-novel game for yuri lovers.

I don't know how to describe this vn properly, but I can assure you, just try to read it for a few hours, maybe you'll find something interesting about the characters and the stories, not just the couples that Yuna's helping with, but also Sachi, Megumi and Yuna herself too.

Ngl, I don't like reading, and I also have trouble at reading too, which would make me feel sleepy or maybe fall asleep easily. I suggest try to play and read this with your friends, it's easier to stay awake since you can also discuss and chat with them while reading it.

With the Full Chorus DLC, it's even more fun while listening to the great voice acting of the talented VAs too. And the OST, this game doesn't have much, but they're memorable for a lot moments despite most of it just a repeated soundtracks like in other games.

Overall, one of my favorite game of all time. I bought this because I know there're yuri 18+ scenes in here, but the story amazed me more than that. Sex is just temporary, pure love is eternal.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne day, eating her lunch in the break area on the roof of her school as she always does, second year student Yuna encounters two ghosts, or kindred spirits as they like to be referred. These kindred spirits are filled with a regret that prevents them from moving on to the next life and recruit Yuna to help fulfill their desires to finally become intimate with each other. In order to do so, Yuna must subtly steer various relationships into romance such that these two spirits can eventually observe them and learn how to become intimate with each other.

While there is a highly exaggerated and unrealistic innocence associated with virtually all of the characters in the story, it hardly diminishes the emotion evoked throughout. The story is told through a number of different perspectives with a fairly unique chapter structure. There is the main storyline where Yuna and the spirits are often discussing their next plan to help the next girl confess her love followed by the same scenes played out from other perspectives. The story is always told through the current character through their thoughts and dialog and I found it be an extremely effective and enjoyable way to experience the narrative. The story spans roughly six months and the calendar-style chapter select screens have dozens of automatic saves, and after the story is completed, dozens more open up providing short little snippets of extra context or perspective of previous scenes.

The story does contain some adult scenes with mild nudity (nothing you wouldn't see on pay-cable) and while I'm normally a huge fan of sex scenes in visual novels, I actually found the ones here to be a bit unnecessary. They don't occur until very far into the story and since Kindred Spirits on the Roof is much more about the journey than the destination, I ended up finding the romance scenes to be a bit more awkward than sexy. Nevertheless, they certainly aren't distasteful or raunchy, but I didn't really find them very erotic either.

As a full priced visual novel, I do think it's a little skimpy in the CG scene department; there aren't a ton of them (maybe 60-70 + minor variations like an expression change) and the graphics, while nice, are certainly dated being only 800x600 and as there is no Steam overlay support, you need to manually create your own screenshots; which is almost always an annoyance.

And while I can't speak a lick of Japanese, I absolutely recommend the Full Chorus DLC, which provides complete voice over and really brings to life each of the characters. I did find it has one fairly significant problem however, and that is many of the voices are at a very low decibel level and many of them at quite a high decibel level such that playing at a single volume was simply impossible for me. This was true throughout most conversations; one character nearly inaudible with the other practically yelling. And without individual character volumes that you often find in other games, there wasn't much of a solution short of constantly changing my speaker volume.

I really enjoyed Kindred Spirits on the Roof and I highly recommend it to anybody with any sort of soft spot for tales of love but if you came here looking for hot girl-on-girl action, then I think you'll be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
