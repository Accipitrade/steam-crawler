\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a huge Yuri fan and played quite a few VN in that category. I'm sorry to say it's the worst I ever played, and I really tried hard. Here is why :
- THE DAMN MUSIC. Oh god, the main 'cheerful' music is terrible, a 10 seconds loop that goes over and over, really cheap and annoying. This music pop out all the time and just simply doesn't fit the mood quite often. I ended up playing without music at all.
- Too much useless filler dialog. A bit of everyday chat is fine to give a realistic touch to the story and get to empathize more with the characters, but the balance is broken here. I'm patient and a good reader, but I just don't see the point of reading 5 minutes about cooking. I fast forwarded many scenes without reading, which never happened to me. I would say 2/3rd of the game are just filler to make it longer, but doesn't bring anything to the story.
- Characters are unrealistic, thus you won't easily empathize with any of the pairings, let aside identify yourself with any of the girls. One girl sleep through all day and speak like a sloth, another one is a teacher that looks like a 10 year old, etc. come on...
- Harem effect, where every damn girl is gay which is unrealistic too
- The gameplay makes the overall story impersonal and hard to connect emotionally with. Playing cupid with various couples just isn't my thing. There is at least one couple (for me all but one) that you won't have interest in / won't like and you will have to read hours about them.

I wish I could go back in time and don't spend 40$ on this. I played so many yuri VNs that were 10 times better and 4 times cheaper...\t\t\t\t\t

It may sound a little silly based on the summary, but it is really a deeply sweet, emotionally vulnerable, exuberantly joyful, bittersweet game about various lesbian couples at a girls' high school finding their love for each other and exploring their relationships, all thanks to the meddling of some gay ghosts who live on the roof. You get to have a lot of time with each of the couples, all of which are profoundly memorable (seriously, even the couple that I thought was gonna be lame ended up being literally the best among a lineup of absolute 10/10 yuri romances), as well as follow a main story that features the drama of trying to get certain couples together, plus some fantastic development of the main character's feelings, all capped off with a bittersweet, highly emotional finale that wrecked me for weeks. If you like yuri even a little, you have to play this game. Its a blockbuster, a five-star full-course meal of shojo yuri romance, set in a world of dreamy-eyed youth so sweet it made me literally want to die and become a high school anime lesbian in a world so much purer than ours. That will never happen, but at least we have this game. And that is a beautiful thing.\t\t\t\t\t

EXPLAIN! EXPLAIN!
~Dalek

I'm writing eroge reviews now! Eroge are cool! … Only they're not. At least, in my opinion. And that's the funniest thing here. See, I had all reasons to dislike this game. Not only it can (and should) be considered as eroge, it's also one of those visual novels, in which there are no flags. Well, technically, there are flags here, but those don't really matter (I'll return to it a bit later). In other words, on paper, this game is everything I dislike. And yet... here's a thumb up from me. Now here's the question - what the heck? Well, let me explain.

First of all, I kinda dig the title. I mean, the original one, not the one they've made up for an English version. See, this game is all about gay girls. And the original title was Okujō no Yurirei-san (屋上の百合霊さん). Which means “Lesbian ghosts from the roof”. Or “Lesbighosts from the roof”. And I don't know, there's something really funny in the original title. Something catchy. And the story? It's exactly that. Lesbian ghosts. On the roof.

The game follows the Toomi Yuna, a schoolgirl who just met two ghosts who love each other but... can't pass on. The ghosts ask Yuna to help them by helping some other young girls who love each other but have serious troubles admitting their feelings and desires. You can see where all this is going and, of course, you're correct. Only don't expect much gameplay. Because Yuna here is more like a bystander. All she (and therefore – you) can do here is to watch. In other words – you click the left mouse button enough times – you win the game and see the best possible ending for everybody. No way to loose, no real reason for the second run. The choices are there, but first – there are only fifteen of them and second – the only reason for them to exist is to unlock some extra scenes that will become available after you'll finish the main story. In other words, choices are here, but at the same exact time... they're not.

Sounds pretty bad and, well, it is. Personally? I'll never consider this kind of visual novels to be a real deal. But. This game comes with a set of really awesome characters. It's really, really hard not to love the girls and seeing them opening to each other? It's a really heartwarming experience. This game is so cute that Ryūgū Rena would totally want to take it home and characters are very believable. While artwork? It's absolutely brilliant. Not only it fits the atmosphere perfectly, it really makes the game to stand out. It helps the game to keep things cute but not too sugary. Perfect for a yuri game. UI is also very comfortable and you can set up the game to fit your personal play style.

The most arguable thing here is the eroge element. Like I said at the beginning of this review, I'm not really a fan of eroge genre. And most of the time? This game doesn't feel like eroge. Like... all. It's a surprisingly pure story about five (technically – six, but I don't want to spoil it to you) couples and their feelings. It's just that – feelings. Nothing else. At the end of every story, though (the stories take place at the same exact time, but thanks to a very useful UI you won't get confused), there'll be a sex scene for every couple. And personally? I can't say I liked that. Those kind of ruined the mood for me and after hours of a pure and innocent content having this kind of stuff is... arguable. Can't say those scenes are bad, though. Quite the opposite. Surprisingly, developers were able to keep even sex scenes pure. Believe it or not.

So, even though technically, Okujō no Yurirei-san is just another eroge with no real choices, there's something very special about it. The game has a soul and even though it's eroge, there's a certain purity in it. As the result, it's totally worth checking even for people like me who usually don't like that kind of stuff. And if you'll like the characters, there's also quite a lot of drama CDs for you to pick up. Fully translated to English (subtitles). What's not to like?\t\t\t\t\t
