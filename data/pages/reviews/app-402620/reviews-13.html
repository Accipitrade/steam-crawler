\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very nice, well written, refreshing Slice of life VN about romance between girls in high school.

What I loved the most is the charming characters, the different dynamics for each characters and how each had a different wat of dealing with their romantic feelings for one another. I especially loved the personal story and growth we see for Yuna (Or MC, respectively) as the story progresses.

The mechanics of this game is rather nice. It follows a journal system where we get a mark/sticker/icon for a date where something significant happens that follows the plot. The main story follows mainly Yuna's POV and sometimes her new ghost friends (Sachi and Megumi). Once we complete all "main story" plot points for a month we then get to see the POV of the other characters for that month afterwards, mostly from the respective couples focused on that month. It makes this game more engaging despite the story being linear. And while it does have choices those choices are only used to unlock more extra plot points after you complete the game.

Do I recommend this game? Yes I do. Especially if you love slice of life, with some awkward high school romance and lovely characters.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYAAAASSS!!! A YURITOPIA THAT WILL GIVE YOU CUTE FUZZY FEELS!!!

As my introduction into the VN realm, Kindred Spirits on the Roof's "calendar" format wasn't that hard to jump into. Or... at least I didn't think it was. The pacing could be seen as a little chopping, considering that you often jump back a few days when subplots are opened. If I hadn't played for a while, sometimes I had to backtrack and skim the last interaction the pairing had as a refresher.

The only thing I have negative to say about Kindred Spirits is that it would often freeze on me. Not sure why. My computer was way over the minimum specs needed to play. But the game would freeze on me after, maybe, 30 minutes of game-play. I was able to find a round-about way to play it by opening it through the in-Stream game Properties menu, instead of the desktop icon that I usually use to launch my games. Not that big of a deal, though, it did affect my "screen-time hours". I've played this game for about 30+ hours, instead of the 6+ that it's suggesting.

Otherwise, I think that this is a really cute, fluffy wlw game. I bought it on sale, so the 40+ hours or whatever I've spent on it (not counting the extra scenes that were opened when I finished the game because I haven't gotten to those yet), is totally worth it. Some of the couples weren't really my cup of tea/thought were dull, so I pretty much skimmed over the dialogue of their scenes. But overall, the story plot was decent, engaging enough.

Wasn't a fan of a few of the pairings, and because of my Western upbringing, there were a few moments that felt kinda yicky to me due because of the fetishizing of some of the characters due to their physical traits. SPOILERS: Kiri's fetishization of Tsukuyo's small and "kawaii" stature was cringe-worthy to me, to say the least. Don't get me wrong, I love me some smol and tol pairings. But, it felt like one of the biggest reasons Kiri was into Tsukuyu was because Tsukuyu was short and petite. And I don't really want to get into the way that Yuna and Hina got together. It felt borderline incest-ey because they'd been portrayed like sisters for the first half of the VN. Yuna felt like a motherly big sister, and Hina treated her like such. Made me cringe when I got to those parts.

Totes recommend this game. If anything, to support the Yuri game genre. We needs more lady-love in the saturated male-centric game world.\t\t\t\t\t

As a gay dude who likes anime, I often stray into yuri territory because yaoi is - almost always - absolutely repugnant garbage. This time, so is the yuri.
There is a really fantastic, gay, VN in here somewhere, with interesting, cute, and fleshed out characters. However, the couple whose entire dynamic seems to be relentless sexual assault and sexual-pressuring is exactly the reason why this sub-genre is viewed so negatively. What's more, the game seems to want to frame this routine assault as "well both girls are wrong in their own ways!" when in reality one girl is being sexually assaulted bordering on partner-rape on one occasion, and the game seems totally fine with portraying this dynamic as a quirk of the characters. Excise this one couple, and the game is wonderful, but as it stands it represents a disturbing and mishandled take on sexuality between gay women.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf what you're looking for is extra-fluffy yuri visual novel goodness with a side of drama, then is this the VN for you. Kindred Spirits on the Roof, also known as Okujou no Yurirei-san, is an excellently written, as well as translated, visual novel with great character development and a satisfying payoff in the ending, that I would personally highly recommend.

First off, the story is pretty good. The base premise is fairly interesting and creates a whole slew of pretty hilarious and also lots of really sweet moments when reading, what with the protagonist, Toomi Yuna, being the 'yuri cupid' of the story. The drama that occurs between the characters is definitely one of the strong points of the VN, and the occasional friction between the cast members makes you really feel for them. Conflicts between the characters and their resolutions are all very reasonable, and god they are seriously well-written.

The characters, their development, and their writing are for sure the best aspect of KSotR, at least in my opinion. Every single main couple has their own unique little chemistry going on, and the dialogue is super fun to read, not to mention fluffy. This being a fairly long visual novel, taking me over 40 hours to finish the main story and all the extra scenes, allows the characters to develop at a natural pace, and it's really nice to see the interactions between specific characters changing over time. As a high schooler myself, some these characters are also reaaaally relatable. Especially Futano Sasa.

As for the art and sound, the artstyle for the VN fits perfectly, and the CGs look awesome. The illustrations in this VN are really good at expressing the characters' personalities, the character designs are great(ly cute), and the background art's nice as well, the BGs do a great job at setting the scene. Speaking of which, the music in Kindred Spirits also does a great job at setting the mood of the scene. The soundtrack only includes 15 different tracks, but that's not really a bad thing. They really grow on you, and they're one of the reasons why the slice of life type scenes in this game are so relaxing. The music is cute and fitting as well.

TL;DR, the writing for the story and characters are really good. You get ultra-fluffy yuri romance and intense yuri drama, a lot of the dialogue is absolutely hilarious too, and really, every character has a ton of depth to them. The art is great and so is the music, and really, if you're a VN person or like yuri, I'd definitely recommend this game. Thanks for reading this review, by the way. Have great day!\t\t\t\t\t
