For Yuritopia!!

Pros:
  • This is a great story about an uber-mature girl, Yuna, helping two "kindred spirits" (girl ghosts in love with each other) to play yuri cupid at their school. This story has a few minor choices included which do not affect the final outcome at all, but the overall story is told both from multiple points of view (various characters) and also non-linearly, jumping around from date to date as new content is progressively unlocked. The game uses a calendar system so it's not confusing at all where you're at in the story.
  • All the characters are unique and well-developed.
  • Lots of content, easily 50+ hours.
  • Good soundtrack that somehow doesn't get tiring to listen to even after 50+ hours.
  • Lots of Awwww! moments.
  • Oh and some adult moments too, though the game is PG-rated until the in-game month of August.
  • Lots of Japanese culture references. By the end of this story you should know your -san from your -chan and your senpai from your sensei.

Cons:
  • Measly 800x600 resolution. What year is this, 1995? The artwork was still well-done though.
  • Seriously, does everyone in Japan have a stuttering problem? You'd think so from this VN. Everyone seems to be easily surprised, unnerved, caught off-guard, or startled too.
  • Ok not really a con per se, but I really didn't like Miyu & Matsuri for their clearly inappropriate treatment of Hina. I guess not all the characters can be likable.
  • Parts of this VN (maybe a quarter?) are voice-acted, but the majority of it is not. While the parts selected to be voice-acted seemed like the parts that should be if you're not going to do the whole thing, it would've been better if they had either done the whole thing, or nothing. (Rumor has it there will be complete voice-acting in the future, so this point may go away.)

Overall, this was a really fun VN, and the cons listed above were minor annoyances at best. I picked up this title on sale but I would say it's still worth it even at full price. Pick up this VN and treat yourself to some happy yuri feelz.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTl;dr
Kindred Spirits on the Roof is a yuri visual novel. It is the best visual novel that I’ve ever read. If you’re into yuri or just looking for a good romantic visual novel, I highly recommend this game.

Full Review
(Note: Spoilers and some of these points are my opinions)

Story- The story revolves around the game’s protagonist finding out about and meeting two female ghosts or kindred spirits in a relationship on the roof of Kokonotsuboshi Girls' Academy, an all girls school. The Kindred Spirits, seeing that they have finally been heard and acknowledged, ask the protagonist to help them become one in body because they don’t know how to do “it.” But, neither does the main protagonist know how to do it. So, to learn how to do it, the three of them attempt to get girls who have hidden feelings for other girls in their school together to make a “yuritopia” and observe them do it. This is the main gist of the story and this makes for a really interesting and creative plot in my opinion. I also love the pacing of the story, although a bit slow and repetitive at times. The story is really memorable and heartwarming, and I doubt that I will forget this story.

Characters- In my opinion, each of the characters are unique and it was really fun and enjoyable to see the changes in their person and in their relationship with other people.

Gameplay/Controls- It’s a visual novel so it’s just your standard press a button to advance the story. However, I really appreciate the ease of the controls and the variety of controls as well. It’s really easy to use the scroll wheel (down to progress, and up to replay text) and there are so many customizable options for a visual novel. You have an option to the change how transparent the text box is, remove the text box, skip to a certain point, replay voice acting for a certain line, and more. I also appreciate the use of a planner or calendar to advance to the next part of the story. Because of this planner, it is easy to review artworks or cg that you have already seen for a particular month. It also allows you to replay to certain points in the story with ease. You can also access the music and play different tracks in the planner.

Graphics and Art Style- The game has an unchangeable standard 800x600 resolution but personally, this issue doesn’t really bother me. The game still has beautiful art direction and the art is what attracted me to buy this game in the first place.

Music- There are a only a few songs that are played in the game but I don’t really mind that since the music in the game is very catchy and most of them are upbeat. I’d find myself playing the songs in my head or even saying or singing them. There are also other songs that fit the mood of the scenes in the game very well. Like I said earlier, you have the options to all of the the music played in the game in the planner.

Voice Acting- This game has good voice actors. Each of them suit their characters really well and I didn’t dislike any voice for each of the characters. Not all parts of the game are voice acted but I think there's no problem there.\t\t\t\t\t
