\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery enjoyable VN.
The art style is very much on the "classic" side. Looks hand drawn and not as saturated as
modern digital anime looks these days.
But that style totally works here.
Steam for some reason does not track my time, it seems. But should be around 40 hrs.
So it's rather long. But I never felt bored.
Had to force myself to stop each evening.

The voice acting is top notch.
But only like hmmm, 30% or so of this novel is actually voiced.
Most of it in the last third of the novel.
I don't understand why they decided to not voice over a lot of scenes, because those
didn't seem to be less important than others.
So I consider this a minus.

The story is very sweet. All of the characters are likable. Some more, some less though.
But in the end, I felt for every couple.

The lewd scenes are all pushed pretty far back towards the finale, which kinda makes sense here.
And you only get "ecchi" level of lewdness. The most you will see are nipples. But the primary areas are
tactically hidden under blankets or behind other body parts.
Well. I didn't watch this one for the sex scenes tbh.
The different story routes of the couples are just sweet and touching.
Every couple has it's own unique set of challenges to overcome.
And the last couple that appears can be surprising, if you don't watch the into scenes too closely.
(My suggestion: Don't!)

Another little complaint: Some of the small "cherry" scenes only offer redundant conversations from another persons perspective with virtually no additional info for the reader.
So those should have be cut. Luckily, you can skip those in one click if you want.
Oh, and don't just skip all of them. Because some of them actually have important scenes
and one lewd one is also hidden there...

So overall a more soft, more grounded VN.
(even when there is an unrealistic number of female couples. But yeah.)

Absolutely worth it and (if it matters to you), I think this one is 100% enjoyable by both genders.
There is no mindless fan service or other silly stuff.
(not judging, just telling you)\t\t\t\t\t

A story about first loves and the ups and downs of friendship and highschool life.

Characters
The focus of this VN, the characters all have their own stories and lives, which interact and weave together to form unique stories of their own. Multiple couples, each with their own charms and issues, and they're also friends with their own adventures and stories. It's all really well put together, full credit to the writers.

Plot
Highschool life, discovering yourself, and discovering love and friendship. We're not breaking new ground here, but it's one of the best done examples of the genre.

Gameplay

Everyone gets their own plots and subplots, because there are both couples and friendships, and it's all interestingly done and paced with a calendar type interface. Some hidden scenes to unlock as well.

Replayability
There are some hidden scenes, as well as multiple plots and couples, all with side stories, so a lot to explore here.

Summary

The stories here are about yuri relationships, but even if you're straight, there's nothing alien about trying to figure out your feelings for that person you first fell hard for. Kindred Spirits explores yuri relationships, but beyond that it also looks at adorable friendships and first loves, as well as the trials of a highschool life well lived. Definitely one of the best done VNs out there, and worth a look even if you're not big on the yuri.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is possibly one of the best yuri visual novels I've read so far, and I'd love to see more from LiarSoft. There are numerous reason to recommend "Kindred spirits on the roof".
1) The characters: multi-dimensional characters. It is very difficult to elaborate on this without spoilers. Each character is shown is more than one kind of situation and they all reveal more than one side of themselves. They also display varying amounts of growth. As for character development, not everyone gets the same amount of it, but maybe one or two get somewhat neglected. Overall however, no major issues.
2) The visuals: baring the facial features, the characters don't fit into standard anime-style tropes. No spiky hair in weird colours, no aquarium-sized eyes, no exaggerated body proportions.Some CGs lack a bit of depth and appear a little flat, but are still quite pleasant to look at, particularly the colours, but the characters and backgrounds as well.
3) The sound: It gets a little repetitive over time, which given the length of the game can diminish the enjoyment some, but the tunes are overall pleasant, so it's not something to go to war over. Also, it makes Youka's song stand out all the more.
4) The most important, the writing: The situations in which the characters find themselves vary between relaxing, sweet, lively, hectic, dramatic and sometimes even a bit dangerous (by the standards of a slice-of-life visual novel). The focus does remain on the relationship building though, as it's the core element of this game. Each relationship gets sufficient spotlight and they all get their time to develop. None of them feels particularly rushed or dragged out. The multiple POV approach might not be everyone's cup of tea, but it's done pretty well.

Closing note: If you like yuri, you'll love this visual novel. If not, this one might change your mind!
The verdict: 9.2/10 (One couple doesn't have a kiss CG, that's - 0.5 points)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this game did a pretty decent job of tackling various perspectives on what it's like to be gay, from not giving a crap that you're different to "the world isn't always happy-go-lucky and we need to be really careful about ourselves," without acting like Lesbian Romance Is So Pure And Carefree or focusing on Being Gay Is So Awful Angsty Angst.

I think good attention and development were given to most of the couples, though I myself was biased to only two of them. I think the porn wasn't necessary. I appreciate the message of "lesbian love is normal love, that includes up to sexual relations," but I feel like an implication and a fade to black would have sufficed just fine. Not to mention they were pretty drawn out and I got bored fast, especially when there's like three sex scenes in a row. Anime girl squealing slaughters my ears too, so that wasn't very pleasant.

The amount of extra scenes is a little crazy. I have to admit I didn't finish them. It's just too much, and at that point it doesn't feel like I'm working towards anything. The main story, though, was very good. I liked the calendar system a lot, and I enjoyed seeing the relationships develop through all the hurdles and setbacks. Makes me wish I could have a girlfriend. It's definitely the best yuri VN I've played, and one of the best yuri stories I've read/seen in general, and I can only hope that in the future we can get more of this quality.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played, read, and watched a few yuri stories in the past (Highway Blossoms, Girl Friends, Citrus anime, and this game). It isn't my favorite genre, and this game doesn't exactly break that mold except for having supernatural elements involved, but I'd say it's the best one I've played. In terms of story, there's the main story and five subplots that all connect to the main. They're character driven, so if you don't like a certain character, you're probably not gonna love their story. Of the five subplots, I'd say one was great, two were good, and the other two were all right but kinda dragged down by one of the two partners involved. The main branch of Kindred Spirits is very good.

This visual novel is pretty kinetic. There's a few choices to make, but none of them affect the story outside a bit of dialogue here and there. The art is good, nothing amazing, but stylized as you'd expect for a yuri story (i.e., soft and rounded). All of the tracks in this are pretty catchy, and there's one song that only plays twice that is awesome (both for what it means in the story and the song itself). Parts of the game can seem repetitive since you go back to certain days to fill in the blanks by looking at the perspective of a different character.

Overall, if you're a fan of yuri, definitely get this game. It tells six stories all at once and does a great job at tying them together. If you can afford it, shell out the cash for the Full Chorus DLC as well. If you're not a fan of yuri but like romance stories, I'd say maybe buy this, but you'll probably only like the main story and a couple of the subplots. For those who don't like romance stories whatsoever, this visual novel is not for you. It's a wholesome story with a fun cast and good characters, definitely one of the best yuri games you'll get on Steam or anywhere else.\t\t\t\t\t
