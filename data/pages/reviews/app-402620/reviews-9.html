\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUsually i don't write that many reviews, but if a game really leaves an impression on you, then it deserves a proper review.

Kindred Spirits on the roof praises itself for being the first yuri-based visual novel on Steam that is completely uncensored. I'm sure that this is the reason most people bought this game, which is good, because while it totally caters to and fills all your yuri needs, this game provides you with a whole lot more.

The characters in the game are all hugely diversified. After you've discovered all of the girls and their respective love interest, helping them getting together, you really get attached to every single one of them.
Every character has their own traits, quirks and loveabale characteristics.
Every couple has really wholesome, loveable scenes, you get a lot of laughs from certain characters, some really valuable life lessons that you can apply to yourself irl, as well.

I don't wanna spoiler too much, but i can wholeheartedly say that if you are a fan of visual novels and you want a truely "wholesome" experience that is all about girl / girl romance, grab this game, it is totally worth it, it is not an all-out f-fest, it is "wholesome" (that is truely the best word for this game) experience with lots of loveable characters and loveable interactions between the couples.
And yes, there are some hardfelt moments and some moments that might make you shed a tear or two, so be prepared for some truely deep storylines.
I haven't completed the game entirely yet, but i think i'm about 75% done and i can definitely say: One of the best games i've ever played.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKindred Spirits on the Roof has some excellent moments but they're not worth all the drudge in between. The story focuses on too many characters and is told from too many perspectives and gets cut up into too many mixed up pieces that are then presented non-linearly. Not only is it a mess but most of the desperately needed characterisation is locked away in extra scenes you can only unlock AFTER completing the story.

It's not just the story and it's presentation that's at fault though; if you bought the full chorus DLC you'll be treated to perhaps the worst audio mixing I've ever seen! It's not unbearable but many characters speak at different volumes and across way too wide volume ranges. Combined with a music volume that NEEDS to be lowered in the options before you can play results in a decidedly unprofessional product. A sentiment only furthered by the mediocre unlooped music which will completely cut out leaving you in silence for more than a few seconds before blaring back in all of a sudden in a way that you probably couldn't make more distracting if you tried.

Ultimately the game is not terrible. This is mostly because of just how much game there is. If you see all of it there will absolutely be parts that you enjoy or even love but I guarantee that they won't be worth the rest of the game to experience.\t\t\t\t\t
