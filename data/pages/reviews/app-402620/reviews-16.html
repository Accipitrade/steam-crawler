\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKindred Spirits on the Roof is a fun read.

The story is about playing yuri cupid to girls around an all girls school. There no males to be seen or heard from whatsoever. Characters in the game even use the word "yuritopia". This game knows exactly what it is about. All of the couples are unique and cute together in their own way, with a total of sixteen characters divided into seven couples and two supporting characters. While the plot does have conflicts, everything ends well with a nice bow over it and is a bit more slice of life than drama. Overall a fun read if you’re a fan of yuri or just silly, cute romance stories in general.

Something else I feel I should mention. This game has gained a bit of attention for being the first Steam game with “sex” scenes. However, you should NOT use that as a reason to buy and play this game. The “sex” scenes aren’t really what one would normally consider a sex scene, they are not very in depth and are there for the purpose of being romantic, NOT sexual. Some of them don’t really go beyond heavy making out. If you think you’re getting an “adult” game, you will be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually play VN's, but this one caught my eye. I wasn't expecting much when I bought it, but what I got out of it was a good 30 hours of surprisingly complex character development, adorableness, and quite a few "squee soo cute" moments. It's a bit pricy, but considering how much I got out of it, I'd say it's totally worth it.

There were a few s❤x scenes, but nothing that didn't feel deserved and natural in the flow of the character's relationships. They definitely weren't the focus of the game, more of just a culmination of hours of watching the character's develop their relationships. If you're looking for something s❤xy, then you'll be disappointed, but if you come into this looking for an adorable and surprisingly complex (despite the tropes) VN about various girls falling in love you'll be pleasantly surprised.

(I cut out most of the negatives from below as the recent port to RenPy and the new DLC resolved pretty much everything.)

The soundtrack could have used more variety.

Highly recommended!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like yuri it won't let you down.

That said it has a lot of drawbacks:

- It is repetitive ad-nauseam. Aside from recapping recent events constantly you get to experience some scenes twice from another perspective that doesn't really add anything. I just clicked through them without any regrets.
- The story is bland for every single couple.
- The music starts to get annoying after the 1st hour. You're in for at least 20 hours so don't mind turning it off.
- Only 800*600 resolution.
- Art is lacking but that is pretty common when dealing with this genre. I swear there is less drawing than in your typical manga chapter.

Not that I didn't enjoy it but I expected much more after all the hyping.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKindred Spirits on the Roof is a fairly long (30+ hours) and high quality shoujo-ai visual novel. It's all about the romance and connecting with that other special person instead of focusing on more "titillating" aspects of new relationships.

Art style is akin to a clean and pleasant manga here. Everyone has distinct looks as well, which is a plus in a sea of almost identical moe characters of today.

Soundtrack is competent, but its easily the least remarkable thing here. It works as a background for all the romantic mayhem constantly going on here.

Character cast is large, but for once almost everyone gets a decent slice of the story instead of being reduced to comedic window dressing the whole time.

Novel is also voiced very competently and you should have fun listening to the main characters going off with all sorts of puns and "yuri conspiracy plots" as they try to lead as many potential lovers together as they can. There's also 4 after story stories in voiced form available in Steam should you like to know how the characters fare after their school year.

Recommended for anyone who wants a little bit different romance and comedy story. It has a dash of supernatural, melancholy and drama just to spice up things, but I'd say general disposition of this novel is very sunny and hopeful.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKindred Spirits on the Roof is a game that will leave you with a good feeling in your heart.

Don't worry about the price tag either because there is a ton of content to this game. It took me 32 hours to finish (SPOILER:) completely and get the "Full Clear" message, and even if you don't go for 100% completion the main story is still the large majority of those hours.

While I could leverage some criticism at the game, its flaws are few and easily excused and it's easy to see the game had a high level of effort, talent, and enthusiasm put into it. These folks really cared about what they were making and it shows. The writing is excellent, the voicework is top notch, the music is catchy, background and character art and UI design are all well done, and really the whole experience is a cut above most of this game's contemporaries.

Kindred Spirits has quickly become my favorite yuri visual novel game, and is really a contender for the most well put together visual novel that I have ever played to be honest. It's right up there with the best of them.

I think the most important thing that this game does "right" that so many others fail to understand the importance of is (SPOILER:) everyone gets a happy ending. This is so important and so needed because, while more and more VNs these days feature same sex romantic couples, which is fantastic to see, it's still very uncommon for a game to have some/all lesbian content and have it all end up with happy endings. So commonly do you see ones with really messed up scenes or just depressing results to their romances and it makes it hard to even enjoy playing games like that. Not so with Kindred Spirits, and that's so very important. We need more games like this one that leave you feeling good. We need more movies and TV shows with happy endings for same sex couples too for that matter.

There was one part in the game that actually did bother me personally. It was maybe 6 or 8 hours in to the game so not very far, and the way that the events played out seemed to run contrary to the tone and precedent of the game up until that point. Words were said by one of the characters to another that I felt were not healthy or "okay" at all, and actually rather cruel. Specifically I am speaking of (SPOILER:) the start of Kiri and Tsukuyo-sensei's relationship when Kiri confesses and Tsukuyo-sensei dismissed Kiri's feelings, telling her they were just a "misunderstanding" and a phase that younger girls often go through. Thankfully though, as the game progressed, this issue that I had with it was actually addressed as part of the story, much to my pleasant surprise, and handled well enough overall that I felt okay about it after that turning point. I was impressed that the staff and writers went so far as to examine a scenario like that, and just knowing that they took that on only added to my respect for the game that Kindred Spirits became.

Another thing worth noting is that this game does feature some adult content scenes without even requiring a separate uncensor patch file to be downloaded. I think that's good progress on Valve's part for finally allowing this kind of content on Steam directly without having to have workarounds like we've seen up till now.

If you're concerned about the adult content, I was really surprised and pleased to see it was very tastefully handled and innocent. A lot of 18+ content games don't really know how to show loving relationships or innocence very well, and I believe it takes a skilled writer to be able to pull off tenderness in a lovemaking scene. Kindred Spirits succeeds wonderfully at this. If you were worried about the content being too graphic or vulgar, you've got nothing to fear here. The relationships take time to build before the intimacy happens, (SPOILER:) and the game's story takes place over the course of 8 months, so if you're going into this game expecting things to move quickly you'll be disappointed. I don't mean that in a bad way though. The character development and pacing is great, and by the time the scenes of intimacy come about, you really feel like you know the characters and the intricacies of their relationships well.

I know that no matter how good it is, Kindred Spirits on the Roof will be considered a "niche" title, because for some reason a lot of us humans are still not comfortable with the idea of women loving women even when represented as an artform, and on top of that, many people aren't even comfortable enough with their own bodies or sexuality to be okay with seeing sexual content in the games that they play.

If you ask me, it's unfortunate really that as a culture we're more okay with depictions of shocking violence, pain, and distress, than we are with sexuality and feeling good, but I digress. Chances are good most folks who fall into these categories aren't sitting here reading this review in the first place.

If you are the kind of person who is open minded enough to not let the fact that this game contains some scenes of an intimate and loving sexual nature between two women bother you, and you enjoy visual novel games, Kindred Spirits on the Roof is one VN you will really be able to appreciate, and its heartwarming story and characters will stay with you for a good while, and leave you feeling glad that you took the chance on it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played every scene from this game and I must say:

This is my favorite visual novel.

But to make this brief, I'll give you my pros and cons:

Pros:

  • Art. Oh my god the art is beautiful. Cute and sweet and fits this story so well.

  • Many different stories. If there is a couple you are 100% for, I can guarentee that another one will be just your cup of tea.

  • Great writing. The stories are sweet and so full of love. Nothing feels that forced, and all of the relationships are very genuine.

  • Sound design. The SFX and music fit the mood and are so pleasing. The music doesn't get annoying.

  • Voice acting. The whole thing isn't voiced, just certain scenes. That way the scenes voice acted are so much more poignant that way.

  • A REAL STORY. The attention to detail in this VN is just so amazing.

    Cons:

  • Not many choices. If you like choice heavy games, this might not be for you. The choices you do make unlock extra scenes from the girl's POV's later.

  • Cultural differences. Some Western cultures have different customs and beliefs which could result in a bit of confusion. Ex. There is a romance between a student and a teacher. The female age of consent in Japan is 16, so both of them are within the age of consent. It makes 100% sense if that makes you uncomfortable, but you need to understand that before playing.


    All around, this game is stellar and I will replay it many times!!
