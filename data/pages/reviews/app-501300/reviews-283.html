\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a dark, mysterious, heartfelt and somewhat morbid game that keeps talking to you frequently (so if you like that about Firewatch you'd probably like this, and vice versa) so you never feel alone, or wonder if you're walking in the right direction. I tend to quickly get sick of walking in games, so this was perfect for me. I also was worried there might be jump scares or really creepy scenes that'd just scare the hell out of me when playing at night, but it became clear quite early on that this wasn't that sort of game. Exploring the house feels comfortable, familiar, and cosy, even, except for the weirdass stuff that you'll come across in it, which just feeds into the mystery.

This is basically an exploration game with no instructions (just WASD + Ctrl/Q, and mouseclicks, if any) and a linear structure, and as the narrator unravels the story during your explorations, you will play scenes from each of the characters backstories to understand their history. Each backstory is a mini puzzle adventure with a different theme and different mechanics (i can't say more or it'll be spoilers, and I was frankly amazed at every point and very very engaged. I would marvel at the mechanics and theme for the new backstory, or how the creators utilised that particular format to have you feel like you have a stake in the history.

For the most part you'll never lose the game, you just have to figure out the puzzle to move the story along. It sounds dry, but with the really powerful voice acting and the dark, sometimes fantastical twists and turns to the story, I was pretty much taking time to babble on the side about how awesome this game was to my friends.

It's not too long a game, I'd say you can complete it comfortably in a day, but one function I feel is sorely lacking is the save function. It only autosaves at checkpoints, so instead of quitting the game when I had to take a break (i'm really bad with motion sickness), I'd just hit Esc to pause and let the game run in the background. Generally the game isn't time-sensitive, you can take your time with it and not even hit Esc to "pause", actually.

While simple, I loved that aspect of it, mixed with the complexity of the storytelling, and would 100% recommend it to anyone who's looking for a good modern story. Excuse me while I now go and binge-read articles and easter eggs about this fantastic game.\t\t\t\t\t
