\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is entertaining. However, it is very, very short. I beat it in about 3 hours. The story is very intricate. You learn about the tragedy of a lot of members of the Finch family. Even subtle things in the environment give you clues about the story and about the characters' personalities. No character in this game feels 1-dimensional; they are all very fleshed out. The main character you play walking around alone in the huge mansion gives you the feeling that there's a lot of history and that the main character feels loneliness.

If I have anything else to complain about with this game besides its short length is the replay-ability of it and the lack of a run button. You walk like a turtle at a snail's pace. This game is a linear walking simulator with a story being told as you play and explore. If you're looking for a game with heavy emphasis on gameplay, look elsewhere.

Also, if you play on controller, you're going to have to play with keyboard-mouse on this one. While this game is advertised as having full controller support, you actually can't select anything like you can with a mouse click. Not to mention that the sensitivity with using a controller is laughably terrible. There aren't very many controls, anyway. This game is a WASD game with the occasional looking around and clicking stuff with your mouse.

If you're a completionist, I highly recommend keeping an achievement guide right next to you and collecting the achievements as you go because the game is designed to be played in one run. The slow walking paired with how big the house is and a lack of simple quick travel would make this game a pain to play a second time just for achievements. I will not be replaying this for achievements. They seem pointless in this game, just expecting you to do obscure stuff that you may or may not do in your first run. There's no accomplishment in achieving them.

The house and the stories are varied and huge, yet the game does a good job of letting you know what to do. The gameplay varies from section to section with new mechanics that fit well with the story being told. There is some freedom of where you can go explore as well. The ending to the game was alright. It just felt like the whole game was just a telling of tragedies with not much of a clear goal.

Buy this game at a steep discount somewhere. It felt way too short to recommend at full price. However, on sale, this is definitely a good story game to check out some time. The lack of interesting gameplay is made up for by the sheer amount of quality story-telling.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch- 100 out of 100

Who is it for?
1. Folks looking for an emotional roller-coaster of a trip.
2. Those looking for short and sweet story.
3. Those who don't mind indulging in conversation about death.
4. Those who love somber music.

Who is it not for?
1. If death doesn't scare you, anyone and everyone should give game a try,

Review-

Video Games are art. They can convey messages that conventional media can't, they can express emotions by means of action, they can evoke a response that one maybe completely unaware of. What Remains of Edith Finch is a proof of that.

I'd kiss the hands who wrote the story, and those who made this game. With a playtime of not more than 90 mins, What Remains of Edith Finch is absolutely packed with creativity, game play mechanics, moments of pure awe.

I can't rate this game objectively because that is simply impossible for me. Telling a story of death of family members doesn't seem like a wholesome premise, however this game absolutely nails it. Crafting a world with amazing world details, well written lore delivered with equally commendable voice acting was a more of pensive to look into the lives of the Finch family.

At the end of it, I couldn't help calling up my mum and telling her how much I love her for everything she did. I see my siblings differently, I am glad to have them in my life. That was good art does, I guess, it changes you as a person.\t\t\t\t\t
