\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely loved this game! I have played a few 'walking simlator' games but this one is by far the best.

The story is told from the viewpoint of a young woman's diary as she tries to discover what happened to her entire family in this huge house - and it is told in an outstanding way. It is so interesting to follow her on the journey and try to uncover the mysteries of the family curse.

The artistic visuals and graphics in this game are amazing and there is a great variety used to tell the story and it keeps it fresh. The voice acting in the same is fantastic. It helped give each character their own identity and made them all unique. The music in the game is also wonderful, it matches the game perfectly.

I think most people would not see any replay value within this game but I do. I might perhaps play it again in the future, just to experience it again. I suggest going into this game as blindly as possible. I did and I'm very glad I did. The game is a little pricey, especially considering the length, but it is definitely worth it.

I highly recommend this game, to everyone. I cannot praise it enough. The story is great, the writing is great, the graphics are great, the voice-acting is great. It's just great!

Go play it!\t\t\t\t\t

Rating
8/10

Worth it?
It's very short and a bit pricey, but yes.

Playtime
It took me less than 2.5 hours to get 100% completion.

Comparison
It's a walking simulator, like The Vanishing of Ethan Carter, but it's also very different.

Story
Told from a perspective of a young woman's diary, the game tells a captivating story of a multi-generational family that used to live in a house on the coast of Washington.
It's been said that there's a curse following the Finch family and you discover what has happened to them.

Graphics
The main part of the game is very pretty. The number of details in the environment is just incredible, both outside and indoors.
But what's really great about it is the variety - some of the stories of family members are presented in completely different artistic styles. Each one is unique and makes the game feel like more than just the sum of its parts.

WARNING: SOME MINOR SPOILERS AHEAD!

One is in a form of slides from a really old news reel where you have to manually switch between the black & white images. Another one is in a form of a comic book with cel shading. Another is shown from behind a camera, looking through the lens. My favourite one was presented as a video game taking place in a person's imagination. Top down at first, then evolving through isometric to 3D TPP to first perspective in the end.

SPOILERS OVER!

Audio
Also very well done, with each part mentioned above having its own matching music.
But the most important element was the narrator - this young woman's voice, reading her diary, describing everything that was going on and offering additional information or context for all the events.

Mechanics
Very simplified, as you could expect from a walking simulator. You can walk around, look around and interact with selected elements of the environment.
There were some areas where I was looking for a sprint button, but then again I understand the slow walking is a part of the game and is required for the pacing of the story. And since this game is all about the story, I recommend you play with your controller so you can comfortably sit back and enjoy the ride.

Gameplay
There's not much here, but that's specific to this genre. It's not about the gameplay, it's about the story. You walk around and interact with items, trying to learn more about your family's history.
But just like the graphics is presented differently in some of the stories, the gameplay also varies.

WARNING: SOME MINOR SPOILERS AHEAD!

Most of the time you play as a human, but there's a bit where you're different kinds of animals, including a monster with tentacles. Another part you play as an imaginary video game character. One more is playing as a rubber frog in a child's bath. The one I enjoyed the most was working in the fish cannery.

SPOILERS OVER!

Conclusion (TL;DR)
It's a fantastic experience! I wish I could forget it and enjoy it again for the first time.
It might seem a bit expensive for 2 hours of play and I understand that. If that's the case, wait for a -50% discount and buy it then. But do buy it and play it, if you enjoy a good story or if you want to see where video games can go as a form of art.

Pros
- beautiful
- good story
- art style variety

Cons
- short
- pricey
- I wish I could enjoy it again for the first time

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't be discouraged by the slow introduction, and the equally slow walking pace. This game is a real treasure of creativity and story-telling. Just when you begin to get bored by the exploration, the game will surprise you with very creative story-telling and even gameplay, which might sound paradoxical for a so-called walking simulator.

The game's environment is full of symbolism: the house is tall and contrived like Edith's family line and the contrived and tragic destinies each of its characters bear, it is filled with hidden passages, secrets and stories untold.
Edith's visit is a maieutic journey, both as a way to rebuild herself unearthing the truth, and as the maieutic of Maia, the greek goddess of childbirth. It is perhaps the only game where you play a pregnant woman?

The game, of course, has its faults. Part through it I found myself frustrated by how it did everything to immerse yourself in its universe and its family tree, with realistic details and flashbacks where you control the characters, but nevertheless the game killed this immersion with overdesigned, unrealistic settings (the house from the exterior, the family myth).
Some of the storylines are treated too swiftly, or are not as interesting as some others, and the game is unequal in this aspect.

However, it is still a very good and innovative game, with fewer tiring clichs than in others. It is some kind of art, different from movies, novels and paintings, bringing them all together in an unexpected and novel way. And perhaps judging it as one would judge art, from the way it enriches you, grants you aesthetic satisfaction and expands in some way your mind, it might be a bit lagging behind. But it is still beyond most of what you'll find in nowadays games in that aspect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately, I think the game is just a great delusion where hidden behind the beautiful settings, art directing, stylish look, etc- there is actually nothing. I admit that the game looks really great, I also admit that the way the story is delivered is very innovative and really good. However... there is no story! I played the whole game expecting the pieces to fall together finally but it never does. From what was supposed to be a very emotional game, I departed completely indifferent.
Then, I tried to approach it as more of an interactive film/book simply because I want to think positively of this game but even like that: I wouldn't have watched this game as a film and I wouldn't have read it as a book so why should I play it as a game?
It really makes me sad to say these things because the developers certainly deserve praise for their attention, care and creativity which makes this game unique in its own way but I just wish they had spent a bit more time finding a real story rather than a placeholder. They had everything else, 100%. Mechanics, story telling, graphics, etc. However, without the core or whatever it should be called, the game ends up as nothing.
Not worth the time or the money unless you are a developer looking for inspiration.\t\t\t\t\t
