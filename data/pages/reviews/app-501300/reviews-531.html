\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the finest example of its genre to date, I would reccomend it to anyone with an interest in narrative games as it establishes a benchmark by which others should be judged.

For an excessively long critque: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qo48HmqrcII Although avoid this if you want to play the game yourself as it is spoilerific (and an hour long)

What remains, tells the story of Edith Finch the last member of a cursed family returning home to make sense of the past and decide the course of her future. You explore the Finch family home experiencing their stories via a mechanic that lets you play out there final moments, each story and the house itself is rich in detail and unlike so many other titles the details lead to actual revelations for the observant rather than leaving a vague lore shaped hole that can filled by whatever you want.

If like story driven games you really should give it a try, like I said it sets the bar to future games in the genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is an adventure/walking simulator with a very immersive story.

Pros:
+ Gfx Design, with the emphasis on DESIGN, really, developer shows what design means. It was really fresh to see something meaningful is created from scratch.
+ Sound design, music is great, narrators are awesome
+ Story. It is immersive. Some scenes and lines sticks with you for a period.
+ Innovation: Developers created a piece of art when it comes to storytelling. They used and transformed 'gaming' as a medium to tell a great story.

Neutral:
~ It is short, i agree on that. But with that kind of quality storytelling i would have find it short even if it was 500hours long.

Cons:
- Not me, but some people might find the price a bit high for the gameplay time.

10/10 It was innovative. It tells a great story. It has good art and dedication on every department. You shouldnt miss it if you are wise enough to know what to expect from it.\t\t\t\t\t
