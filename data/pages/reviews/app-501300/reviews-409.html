\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a story where you find yourself in a dozen of stories. Confused? Don't be.
You start in your families house and start exploring through it. There you find the stories your family has left behind and you start learning where you came from.
Every story is different, some are tragic, most are heartfelt, but all are very gripping. To me at least. There are stories of the old and stories of the young and that in between. I especially liked the story of the little baby: you sit in a bathtube, your imagination takes over and suddenly you are the conductor of your bath toys (this is one of the stories that has a tragic ending).
Conclusion: Everything looks beautiful, the story is fantastic and when I finished the game I really had to sit still a bit and think about it all.
I do have to say that I usually enjoy these kinds of games, where the story is more important than gameplay. Again this is a walking simulator, so you basically walk through a story, but if you enjoy that.....THIS GAME IS A MUST HAVE.

What Remains of Edith Finch; however, is more of a visual novel. It's where movie and games cross path to give you something else. I highly recommend playing this if you understand what you're getting into from the start and not expecting a traditional game.

This game can be completed in 2-3 hours, and you will complete it in 2-3 hours as you will not stop playing it since you'll be throughly engrossed and want to know where it ends.

I admit, the ending wasn't as satisfying as I had hoped, but overall, it was a great experience that I highly suggest giving a shot.\t\t\t\t\t

A Beautifully Dark Tale of Loss and Acceptance
As with many of the games in my library, I picked up What Remains of Edith Finch in a Steam sale and never bothered to play the damn thing. Yet finally, after weeks, I did. Launching this title, I wasn’t entirely sure what to expect – but I was not disappointed, and you won’t be either. What Remains of Edith Finch spins a gripping tale of fate, loss, and mystery that will glue you to your seat throughout its entire two-hour span.

Style – 5/5
Holy f*ck*ng sh*t are this game’s aesthetics jaw-dropping. From the first few moments of the game, What Remains of Edith Finch floods you with visual and auditory sensations of a dead family’s ancestral home lost to time. Everything is slightly wrong. There are faint noises (footsteps?) emanating from the locked attic. Entire sections of the house are cordoned off. Every bedroom has been sealed shut, with only a single peephole in each door serving as the sole way to view their former occupants’ domain. The world Edith is returning to is both familiar and alien, and the slight-to-noticeable art deviation from character to character greatly enhances this beautiful dichotomy.

Gameplay – 3.5/5
Even in such a short game, the walking simulator genre can still feel dull and repetitive. This phenomenon Is combated by a series of minigames that break up Edith’s exploration. Although these minigames can be nice departures from the landscape-ogling status quo, many of them feel like clunky, half-realized gimmicks. That isn’t to say they fail narratively, however. Reminiscent of Limbo, What Remains of Edith Finch capitalizes on the deeply disturbing device of being forced to initiate your own demise in order to proceed in the story. This is used to fantastic effect to give the player a visceral, horrendously dark experience that tapers off as the game goes on (which in and of itself is sort of the moral of the story).

Story – 5/5
Explaining the details of What Remains of Edith Finch’s masterfully-crafted story would be a disservice to say the least. You have to play it to understand why it is so resonating. I spent 2 hours inside the Finch house. The team behind this game spent thousands, and it shows. The gameplay and style are so intrinsically connected to the story that you can’t remove it. This game is not a puzzler, “your choices matter" Telltale-style game (RIP) or anything of the like. It’s a story about stories, which gives the player the experience of uncovering the mystery of the Finch family fate right alongside Edith. The ending is bittersweet... but there is no other way this particular story can end.

What Remains of Edith Finch is a game that seeks to answer its protagonist’s lifelong childhood questions, and in doing so paints a macabre and beautiful picture of a history of continuous, inexplicable family tragedy. For those experienced with loss or any kind of significant familial strife, this game will hit all the right places. Emerging from this game you will find yourself both uplifted and heartbroken. As the game is so keen to point out, life does go on. Buy this game.\t\t\t\t\t
