\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are plenty of reviews out there explaining what is so great about this game. I'm going to offer something different for people who have already played it and feel like they either don't understand why it is touted as being so good or simply don't like it. If you haven't already finished the game please don't carry on reading, although I wont be spoiling anything major, there will be language that might hint towards themes and spoilers inadvertently.

Personally I felt like I must have been missing something as I didn't understand the importance of the Finch family and the details and dynamics that make the story so interesting and poignant. I was correct. I missed the entire theme of the game which ironically is the point. As a member of the Finch family, you are also subject to the curse and only given the information that a family member would have. I felt dragged in by the story and disappointed by the abrupt and lackluster ending. That's the point. The ending is perfect because of those aspects but only if you know why. The issue is that the "why" is sprinkled into the game through details and things that would be seen as padding in a different game. Everything in this game is relevant and not only that, you have to use real world logic to piece together the entire story. For example, why would someone have photos framed of a loved one falling to their death? Why would they live in a bizarre, precarious home? How did a schlocky comic book publisher know intimate details about a family members death, layout of the house and secrets that even you, a Finch didn't know?

I made the mistake of playing this like a game. I thought that these details were for me as a player to see, absorb and add to my knowledge of the game to help me progress. For example; the comic book explaining explaining where a secret key was, to me this was solely information to progress, I never questioned how strange it was within the universe of the game. When I started thinking of these details like they were real, like I was the family member going back to the house, the story really started making sense.

If you had these same feelings and want to know if you missed anything or didn't get the intended experience, I would recommend watching the below video.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6bMn4CoyUkM

After completing What Remains of Edith Finch I felt confused and disappointed. Not only in the game, but it's critical acclaim. After watching that video, I felt relieved to be wrong. After years of playing games that feel like games, that don't expect players to think for themselves and create worlds that would only exist in games; with convenient letters, scripted sequences and unrealistic item placement that is there purely for the player to discover no matter how out of place they would be in real life; I was lulled into a critical mistake of treating this game like every other game and without watching that video, my mindset wouldn't have let me see the true meaning behind this game.

I also wouldn't feel alone in this unhelpful mindset either. It would seem that a lot of people that have positively reviewed this don't fully understand the story. Which is disappointing yet expected for reviewers and Steam reviews. I personally found the story to be simple and largely pointless without the new perspective that I was given and it looks like a lot of reviewers are content with the diluted narrative that they believe is complete and will never know that they missed the entire point. It is like reviewing the movie Inception positively yet fundamentally not understanding the the concept of dreams within the film's universe. Although positively, the review is a misrepresentation the film. In fact after a little bit of research, I found that most people posting online have actually gotten the exact opposite of the point the game was trying to make, then are going online and spreading their misunderstanding as fact. This is why I don't trust the reviews or general online discussions for this game (or many games at all), but I'm glad that they shine a light on it that will see more people hopefully experiencing what this game truly has to offer.

Play it and and don't think of it like a game, apply real world logic. Start asking why, even to the tiniest details. Although the game's premise is bizarre and unbelievable, that's the point. It is bizarre and unbelievable in it's own world.

The only people that would find the absurdity in this game to be normal, would be a Finch.\t\t\t\t\t

As it leads you through this families struggles you feel connected to them in a personal sense.
