\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: This game is story-driven in the sense that TripAdvisor is story-driven.

Pros:
- Very unique interactive gameplay. Each section is like a mini-game where you need to figure out what to do, or even how to control the game.
- Unique way of storytelling where you experience other people's last memories.
- Good atmosphere, nice visuals and music.
- Intriguing story build-up.

Cons:
- It's a very linear walking simulator (i.e. no puzzle or story reacting to your choices or anything like that)
- I got motion sickness from this walking simulator. And I play DOOM with no issue.
- The mini-games are sometimes weird and abrupt, and it is left for you to figure out what is the control scheme in some of them, which may break the flow of the game.
- This game is story-driven in the sense that TripAdvisor is story-driven. You get a number of short stories that are roughly under the same topic and each of them can be quite interesting, but when you clump them up, you just get a clump of short stories that are roughly under the same topic.
- Despite the intriguing build-up, the ending is thrown at you abruptly and it's up to you to interpret everything, including the meaning of this game and the two hours you have wasted. There are people who can get emotional and cry to the story, but I find everything anticlimactic.

The bottom line is, if you buy this game with the expectation that it's a nice assembly of art pieces and there is nothing more to it, then you will be satisfied and would no doubt give the game a thumbs-up. But if you are like me, having read the game description, watched the promo material, and expect a good story or a satisfying ending (not necessarily a good ending, but something that all the build-up leads to), then you will feel being played. It's like watching a crime drama movie that, instead of solving the crime, ends with aliens. And you don't get to beat up the aliens; you only get to pass the case to another detective, and that's the end of the story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, just wow. Got the urge to do some gaming today, so I sat down to try What Remains of Edith Finch. I didn't stop until I'd finished. After getting some food and resting my eyes, I opened the game again to go back and get all the achievements I'd missed on the first run. I'm definitely in love with this game.

I loved the story, which is good, because this is a story-driven game. You play as Edith Finch, who returns to her childhood home after the death of her mother. She is determined to find out the history of her family, which was kept from her as a child. You go through the house, exploring various rooms that belonged to past family members and filling out your family tree as you learn more information about your family's past. It's a simple enough premise, but the creators executed it brilliantly.

FIrst off, while the controls are fairly simple (move around, open doors, pick up objects to examine, etc.), the game never bored me. Each time I found something left by a Finch, I jumped into their story. For example, when Edith finds Molly Finch's diary, the player acts out what is written in it, with Molly narrating sections. I found this a fun way to engage with the story as it was being told. I understand some may prefer having more agency / control in the story, but I was fine with seeing where the story took me, because it was so well told and paced. Even better, each Finch's story is told in a different way. I already mentioned Molly's diary, but there is also a story in comic form and another as a flip book. Each story comes with its own objectives and controls, so gameplay doesn't get stale. Though the game sounds simple on the surface, the creators did a great job of keeping the gameplay fresh and the story compelling throughout.

I was a little heartbroken when I finished the game, and I fell into that good old sequel vs stand alone debate I sometimes have. The game was so good, I wanted more and more, but I didn't want to cheapen what I'd already experienced by dragging it out. I felt like there was more to learn about the Finchs (the game doesn't tie up all the loose ends), but wasn't the mystery part of the allure? I love when a story leaves me like that, both satisfied and wanting more all at once. It's the mark of a great story, in my book at least.

Overall, I really enjoyed playing What Remains of Edith Finch. I had some frustration adjusting to the controls, but that had more to do with me learning to use the Steam controller than the game itself. In fact, this may be a good starting game for getting the hang of the controller, since the controls are simple and the pace makes it easy to adjust to using a controller instead of a mouse for first-person perspective gameplay. This game isn't the type to drive a player insane with difficulty, and it's not a sandbox adventure where you create your own story. However, if you enjoy story-driven games, if you love peeling back the layers of a mystery one-by-one as you try to piece the past together, then this is the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t
