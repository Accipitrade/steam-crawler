\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA linear walking simulator that is all about story and atmosphere. The gameplay is very simple, with just walking around to find things that can be interacted with, but that is not what the game is about. It is all about exploring the house and the Finch family and their weird history. It is all presented through story segments that are usually unlocked from personal items. These segments are mostly from the perspective of the owner with some being just some short text, or being a fairly long journey. And these segments are usually presented in a very unusual or fanciful way (like controlling a rubber ducky) making them all unique and interesting. My words can't do it justice. The presentation is really great throughout, even the credits.

But if you aren't up for a weird and interesting, non-interactive journey though, the game won't be for you. It is short and sweet though so it is worth playing for anyone who is even slightly interested (like me, not really my normal kind of game). The big negative for me would be the lack of concrete answers on what exactly happened. It is up to the player to interpret and try to make sense of all the stories. Personally I prefer real closure, I have no interest in replaying or looking through every little detail to maybe understand things, if that's even possible.

Still the journey is worthwhile in my opinion. It has got to be one of the best walking simulators out there.\t\t\t\t\t

TL;DR
This is the best emotional walking simulator. Full stop. If you have the chance to play it for the first time, do so without reading any further about the game. Please have tissues ready! I play this game once every year and own it on every platform it's available on.

Story / Character
Please stop reading and play the game first! It will greatly enhance your experience!
This game lets you relive the lives of different members of the Finch family. As the player explores the family home. Each story is told in a completely different way. Every time I showed the game to friends, everyone liked a different story the most.
I feel that there is always at least one story that someone can identify with or feel emotionally touched by.
The story that touched me the most personally was Sam's story. On my first playthrough, I had to put the controller down for ten minutes before I could continue the game.

Gameplay
Each story offers a slightly different kind of running simulation. The game never feels tiring or complicated, but is always fresh enough not to get bored of.

Artstyle / Music
Again, the artstyle and music changes for each story. The screenshots give a pretty good idea of the styles you get to see. The music always felt fitting.

Bugs
I didn't notice any bugs in my numerous playthoughs on different devices.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a game that I've struggled to write a review for a while now. It's really hard just to put into words what this game means and how well it tells its story. I'll try my best though. Naturally, every person is going to have a different experience with this game, but that's what makes this game beautiful. It is an experience. That is the best way I can describe it, and a very immersive beautiful one at that. This game tells the story of a family in a completely neutral way which makes it excellent. It has such a profound message when it comes to family and mortality, and how your attitude towards death can shape your relationships around you currently. As I get older many of the themes and ideas of this game resonate more and more. This game is very interpretive and symbolic. The game is very satisfying both mechanically and technically as well. The environments and music are lovely and complement the game well. It is a very artistic game but it works so well with this story. It is easily one of the best first-person games I've ever played. I really cannot recommend this game enough without spoilers, so please try it for yourself and decide how you feel about it <3 And thank you developers!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA beautiful, serene and mysterious game that drew me in with a fantastical and emotional story. The graphics are truly amazing, vibrant yet soft, evocative and dreamy. Level design is interesting, albeit almost fully linear - there's very little choice in which way to take and no opportunity to wander. The ambient soundscape and voice acting are extremely well executed and "just right" - there's never too much of anything. This game looks and feels a notch better than my other two favourite walking simulators: Firewatch and Tacoma. This one is considerably shorter, and I'll admit I enjoyed the story of the other two a bit more, so there's no clear winner. All three are masterful works of art.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor such high praise in the reviews i was expecting more tbh. It's not that the game is bad, not at all, but if you see 95% positive you expect a masterpiece. Something like journey, the stanley parable or INSIDE but this game didn't live up to those highs. It's not groundbreaking enough gameplay wise, visually or narratively to deserve this high a score. Odd as it may sound i'm giving this a thumbs down, not because it's bad, but because the ratings need to go down several percentages. If this game was around 80% i wouldn't feel as let down, because that better reflects where the game stands among it's peers. So in general i still recommend you pick this up, but don't have monumental expectations because you'll come out feeling dissapointed.\t\t\t\t\t
