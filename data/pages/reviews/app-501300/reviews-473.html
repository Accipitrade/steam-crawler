\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"My disappointment is immeasurable, and my day is ruined."
These words were true when they were used to describe popeyes cheddar biscuit butterfly shrimp, and they are true now.

What Remains of Edith Finch is, however, one of those rare examples of a game that is good, but ruins its potential. A game that pretends to offer something more, something deeper, but once the outer layers are peeled back the resolution that is found is both unsatisfying and lazy. For a game that wants to be a movie, it ends up failing at both. Let along price for amount of gameplay, this game only right around 2 hours, and for 20 dollars that is steep. Im not expecting Witcher 3 or anything but even for an indie game it felt very expensive. Indie games like SOMA or Firewatch are similarly priced, but offer significantly more gameplay without sacrificing narrative quality.

At the end of the day, I didnt hate the game. The gameplay is varied and tells some interesting stories in an immersive way, the art direction is phenominal, and and the soundtrack is nice. Unfortunately the short length of the game, the steep price, and the lack of a satisfying story outweigh the pros.

TL;DR - Honestly there are SO many games out there right now that are more deserving of your time and money, dont waste either on this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn the first 15 minutes you learn exactly what is happening/going to happen for the next 2 hours. Go to a room, pick up some form of a note, play something similar to a mini game, mark it down in your notebook, and then repeat 12 more times. Finish a few rooms and the mystery is gone. There is no exploration. It drags you by the nose to each location and goes "CLICK AND READ". It spends almost no time on the characters, so I found it impossible to care about any of them. Besides the artstyle and voiceovers, I found it to be shallow and dull.

My biggest gripe is the price. I TRIED to explore constantly, so my playtime is longer than it needed to be. I would not be surprised if you could beat this in less than 2 hours. For $19.99 that is absurd to me.\t\t\t\t\t

What Remains of Edith Finch Review


Story
The story of the game is centered primarily around the 18 year old Edith Finch exploring her abandoned family ancestral home. Throughout the game, Edith takes the player on a first-person tour of her old family home. The home in itself it maze of sealed off rooms and open rooms that date back old the way to the early 20th Century. The cozy, yet mysterious interior layout and architecture of the house provides a interesting environment and setting for the game. Throughout the story, the backstory of Ediths family and its curse is slowly revealed via Ediths tour of the house. Each past family members tragic story is revealed not through dialogue or present day events, but the rooms and items they left behind that Edith uncovers. Such rooms and items cause a flashback to the life of said family member which is often creative and surprising at the same time. Ediths own inner thoughts on the lives of her ancestors are shown to the player via a unique dialogue system which displays her thoughts real time. The best part of the game, is arguably the climatic ending which involves a surprising, yet simple twist that catches me off guard.

Gameplay
The gameplay primarily centers around Edith exploring her family home for the entire game. The maze like layout of the house and its individually personally oriented rooms create a cozy, yet mysterious atmosphere for the player that gives each individual room its own unique identity. Moreover, backstory into Ediths ancestors is revealed through creative and unique flashbacks that are triggered when Edith explores their rooms and belongings. Each backstory is tailored unique to said family member. Ediths own thoughts and reflections are revealed to the player via a real-time dialogue display that appears on screen in a simple, yet effective manner. My only gripes with the gameplay is that on some occasions, wandering around the house can be repetitive and get boring sometimes when looking for the next room to advance the story, yet this is countered by the fact that the secret entrances and correct paths are fairly obvious to detect.
Controls
The controls are simple, movement is WASD and the interact with items is mouse.
DLC
There is no DLC.
Performance
The game does not require a powerful PC to run.
TL;DR
The game has an creative, yet simple and short story that manages to convey a powerful message until the player in less than 2 hours of gameplay. The setting and atmosphere created by the Finch House makes the player feel relaxed, yet alert when playing the game due to its cozy payment, yet myriad of secrets to uncover.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGames which touches you emotionally are very rare, rarer than the movies . I will not lie I felt very sad when the game ended. It was a 2 hour or max 2.5 hours emotional ride. A roller coaster of weird & sad tales of every member from the finch family.

Story & Presentation
Story is simple it tells the story of each finch member's final moment of their life & the way it tells it is fantastic (in the form of allegories). The menu & intro is something which got me from the beginning. I like it when you pause the game it opens up the family tree. There is no on screen notification about controls. You are left on your own to figure it out which in fact is very simple.

Gameplay
The gameplay is stylish & a bit different than other games of the same genre. Also there are few small puzzles which can be solved easily. I will leave it at that.

Art & Design
This is where the game shines bright. Each person's room does not looks like a game level instead it really looks like a room where a person once lived. Every room in the house looks like someone was really there & they have left their mark.If you observe each room carefully it tells something about the character of its former owner.

Music
Another thing which will keep you captivated. Its beautiful & totally suits the theme of the game.

Is it worth the price?
I would say its better to play a 2-3 hours long game which you enjoyed rather than spending 20+ hours on a grind fest which you get bored of after sometime.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdith Finch is a magical game.

It takes a story that revolves around family, death, curses, and identity and instead of simply allowing the exploration of the Finch manor to tell the story, plunges the player into vignettes that feel surprising yet emotionally true.

The game uses writing and text masterfully, relying on journals to relive memories while also populating the world with words, showing that the written word can inject itself into the living world of the Finches.

The level/environment design of the Finch house is perfect, providing a single narrative path while somehow fooling the player into feeling free. Playing through Edith Finch is like living life pushing to understand, finding meaning in chaos, deciding what pieces of the past youre going to carry into the future.

Like I said, magic.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a very short and atmospheric walking simulator with great deal of mysteries behind it. You take the role of the last remaing member of the Finch family and explore the house and surroundings in order to uncover the truth behind the mysterious deaths of other family members. The game is very creative in every aspect and it makes you very surprised in some scenarios. At the end of course the mystery is up to your opinion and what you deduce to be the cause of their deaths.

The achievements are very easy to get and trading cards are a big bonus. The music is ok and the graphics are stunning. I wished the game was longer and if there were more characters present in the game- it would have been enjoyable. On the side note Milton is supposed to be an easter egg of another game which I should play and find out what's that about. The each character story is different as each family member has different mentalities and hobbies. The deaths are assumed to be a curse but is it? That's what you need to ponder.\t\t\t\t\t
