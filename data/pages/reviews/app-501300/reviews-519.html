\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo first: it is short. Very short. BUT it is entertaining from start to finish. A very interessting way of storytelling with a lot of diffrent jumps of animation and gameplay (between the walking of course).

Personally, I enjoyed Giant Sparrow "A Black Swan" a little bit more, since I felt it had a better idea for the gameplay. But that game was smaller in scope. This feels like a natural evolution from black swan (and i really enjoyed the reference).

It is great to see a developer trying something new and refining their skills and games.
So, I recommend it of course.

Still i do have some critisicm. Specially for such a story-rich game, I felt kinda rushed through the sections. Everytime I learn about a new character, it is always immidiatly their death story. I do understand that the whole point was to find out who they all died. But I feel, if I had more time to find out how they lived first, their deaths would have been more impactful. Some of the characters get lost this way, which I find really unfortunate since all of them are so interesting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is basically a series of mini games. You explore the house of your family, and in each room you find one of their stories and experience it from their point of view.

The game is beautiful for its wild imagination and mixture of video game, graphic novel and storybook. Sometimes, the world transforms around you and you're left baffled, trying to figure out what's going on and what's expected of you. Sometimes, you end up playing a game in a game in a game, interacting with the boundaries of the game (subtitles, frames) itself. Also, the dark and dreamlike atmosphere combined with the charming, gentle humor is amazing. You can really feel the developers put a lot of love into this fictional family and their surreal microcosm.

What I found disappointing is that they didn't put too much effort into the interconnecting framework, though, i.e. when you move as Edith through the house. The lack of running/fast walking is incredibly annoying at times, especially with the weirdly quick footstep sounds. You can't duck or crouch, there's no proper key binding menu (because there are so few keys that you need, I guess) and you have to rely on auto save until you have completed the game, at which point you'll be able to load individual chapters. But there's no way to play any differently no matter how often you revisit the chapters, since you can only move from A to B, and sometimes all you can do in a room is open the letter/diary that will fire up the minigame. When you miss the "right" direction to go, you can get stuck until you do what's expected. I wish you had more kinds of interaction and more freedom of movement in this world.

Edited to add that during my second playthrough, the game grew even more on me. Already knowing the family members and their stories, and moving through the house more quickly, the voice-overs blend into each other almost seamlessly, turning the game into a kind of interactive poem about the beauty and brevity of life that leaves you in tears. Superb voice-acting all along.\t\t\t\t\t

Great story, really engaging (especially after a while), some are fun, some are sad. The best thing about this game is how you collect the memories: in other walking sims, you usually just get a diary entry or a piece of paper or narration, whatever. This game makes you live the particular memory it is about. And the different ways you do so are great. My favourites were her grandpa's (the one with the photos) and her brother's (the one with all the fish :))

It's fairly short, so for those on a budget I would recommend picking it up on sales, but it's really worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
