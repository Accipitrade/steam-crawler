\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a creative delivery of a tragic short story.
It's less of a game and more of an interactive movie, as you essentially just have to do certain things like move around or open things to progress the story. I think involving the player makes them all the more immersed in the story and its characters.
You get to know each family member and what became of them as you tour your own house. Your character narrates each story and thought in-between in an incredibly poetic way (ex: "Nothing in the house looked abnormal, there was just too much of it, like a smile with too many teeth.")
This is a game that I make most of my friends play. As a creative writer, I look up to this game. It really grips you unexpectedly, and I think it's something everyone should play at least once.
My only critique would be that you move painfully slow, but at the same time it creates eerie suspense and gives you time to internalize the previous story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game, a lot of people saying "buy it in a sale" as its only 2-3 hours, but price isn't indicative of value, was worth every penny to me for what i got out of it.

Yes the gameplay isn't going to win any awards, but that's not what this game is about. The beautiful settings, the house, the graphics, the voice acting, all there to help you immerse yourself into the story, and help you understand the characters.

I originally brought this game after reading a few good reviews and wanting a pallet cleanser from the endless questing, combat, and adventure games i normally sink hundreds of hours into, i wasn't disappointed, and it does open your eyes to a new genre of game.

would highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
