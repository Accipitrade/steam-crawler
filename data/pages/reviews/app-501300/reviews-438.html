\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game quite removed from anything else I have played. That may speak more to what I play than what is out there, but regardless, it is an atmospheric and layered narrative experience.

This game is all story; fortunately it is strong in that department. I was under the impression from the description that this was in some sense a mystery game; rather its is a contemplation on lives lived, death, the power of narrative, and the relationship the members of the Finch family have to their own family history. The game is divided into vignettes allowing you to experience the death of each Finch in a different way; the sound, graphic style, and controls can all change significantly, and part of the joy is experimenting with each new scene to figure out how the controls work and what the goals are. While there are no particularly memorable tracks, the soundtrack and sound design are perfect for establishing world and enhancing atmosphere. The game is visually captivating with many small details that can provide clues or further mysteries about members of the family. Every element of this game seems of exceptional quality; my only con was that I had to turn up my mouse sensitivity, as a couple of the commonly used gestures saw me lifting up and replacing my mouse halfway in order to complete them.

In terms of value for money, I think the care and quality that went in justify the price, even for such a short game (at 3.5 hours, I took a leisurely stroll through, and replayed a few scenes). If you find yourself hesitating, buy it on sale. 100% I would buy it again.\t\t\t\t\t
