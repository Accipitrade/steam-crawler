\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGone Home and Dear Esther walked so What Remains of Edith Finch could run (figuratively speaking, of course). Your mileage may vary with this particular type of story-driven walking simulator subgenre, but for those whom that game type itches a particular scratch, Edith Finch is probably the best of them yet. The story is perfectly paced and is able to gently guide the player along its narrative path without discouraging exploration, and it does a wonderful job sustaining its melancholic atmosphere from start to finish. The story is surprisingly large in scope given the modest size of the game - a modern fall of House of Usher detailing the gradual erosion of the Finch family, falling victim to an alleged "family curse" our playable protagonist, Edith, tries to understand and come to terms with. It is deeply tragic, sometimes in shocking ways (one scene in a bathtub is worth a gasp), but it makes use of its limited gameplay in surprisingly beautiful and elegiac ways.

Pick it up on a sale if you are in the mood for a short, tragic but resonant story-driven experience. Also, for you achievement hunters out there, it's very easy to 100% in one go.\t\t\t\t\t

If it's your first time playing the game, don't read anything else about it and just play it. The controls were a little awkward in some of the playable scenes, with a lot of repetition that made the gameplay itself a little subpar (I never thought I'd be so tired of opening peach cans, chopping heads off of fish that are constantly slipping out of my grip, or pumping on that swing).
