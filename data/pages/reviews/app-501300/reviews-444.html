When I first began playing this game I was concerned that it might buy into the whole obscure indie storytelling cliche, luckily I was proven wrong. I was left as confused as Edith at times by the gaps in storytelling, by the denial nor confirmation of a family curse, and also by the ambiguity of Edith's own passing. I think the game deliberately left such ambiguities to intrigue its players though.
Ultimately though the controls for some of the stories were a little strange, the overall "oddness" and in-depthness of a family surrounded in tragedy meant I just HAD to sit down and finish this game in one sitting. Would recommend for the unusual storytelling experience if for nothing else.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWARNING THIS CONTAINS SPOILERS I really thought I would like this game. I've played a number of games like this before that I have enjoyed but I just didn't connect with the story or the characters. It ultimately left me feeling unsatisfied because there were so many things I wanted to know. What really happened to Barbara? Why was the house abandoned fully furnished and full of everyone's possesions. Why was there no dust or dirt anywhere. What did Edie want to tell Edith, is the curse real or just coincidence? Some events in the game seemed like whimsical fantasy and other times serious grounded reality. I'm not saying a narrative can't be both but in this case it seemed like it never went far enough in either direction. A lot of the gameplay was inventive and unique but I couldn't get past the idea I was basically playing the game with the objective of killing all of the characters, and that caused me to feel somewhate disconnected from them and also kinda depressed that death just seemed like the inevitable conclusion to everything. Just as many of the the Finchs led unfulfilling lives often cut short so too did the game leave me unfulfilled, wanting more and wishing I could have found the same enjoyment and wonder that so many others seem to have with this.\t\t\t\t\t
