\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is tied for my favorite video game of all time next to Just Cause 3 and Outer Wilds. Upon completing it for the 3rd or 4th time at around 2:35 A.M., January 27, 2023, I think I can now claim it indisputably takes the number one spot. This game is as close to perfect as I think any game will ever get. It was created over the span of 5 years by a small development team, to fantastic results. This game's environment and ambiance are hitherto unmatched. The house itself is this whimsical, incredible place brimming with small details and stories. The stories, however few (glancing over more details than ever, doing everything available, it took me 109 minutes to beat the game) are endlessly creative and a few of them make me curse my recent inability to be moved to tears by media for some awful reason. This is the perfect game for a good cry but I suppose I can't be granted that. Above all else, this game provides some incredible sense of despondent nostalgia in me that makes me feel so helpless yet so alive. In creating this small experience they have done the world, especially people keen on walking simulators, an enormous service for which i can't thank them enough. Annapurna Interactive, their publishing team, also created Outer Wilds, one of my other top favorites. I'm going to keep a closer eye on them from now on because they have immaculate talent in spotting worthwhile endeavors I'm 80% sure it was my piece of ♥♥♥♥ headphones but by the end of the game the volume kept cutting in higher and lower back and forth which is my only physical complaint, but the game runs smooth as silk otherwise. This game, from somebody who has spent most of their waking, free hours thus far in life playing video games, comes more highly recommended than anything else I've ever played before. I literally beg you to play this game if you think you'll like it, if only to support Giant Sparrow.\t\t\t\t\t
