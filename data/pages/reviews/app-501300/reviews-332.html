\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have a bad habit of avoiding games I end up really loving and, wow. Just wow. I've never really played a game like this before. The game play is so well thought out and i felt like I was genuinely interacting with the environment.

The story though is where the heart of the game lies. It is so genuine and there were moments I found myself so lost in the game that I didn't realize I was doing anything until my hand started to cramp a bit. I think every story left me with something but kudos to who ever came up with Lewis's because just wow. My only complaint is price but I bought it on sale for 8 ish USD and even then I would pay probably up to 10-15 for it. I just have a weird thing about 20 USD because I expect a lot more content at that price tag. The game is short but it feels perfect for the story and I really have no complaint there other than the original price tag but hey, game devs gotta eat too.

My only warning going into this game is that for people who tend to get motion sick easy playing video games (like me) that you run things at a lower res, it worked for me but tbh I just tend to play with things till I feel comfortable. If you're really worried about this though I will say that the game gives you spots to rest your eyes from it and keep you going. As someone who has to clock out of most standard setting first person games after 30 minutes, I was able to sit through the whole thing with only minor sickness (granted I did play with things a bit). I would say its worth it though for the story but remember to take breaks if you need too :)!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's well worth it on sale. I don't remember what I paid, but likely around $5. I'd says it's worth it at 50% or more off. With that said...

The story is excellent, and keeps you interested, this game is basically an interactive story, you have little in the way of choices, very linear. While certainly a bit dark, it is also somewhat funny and has a good pace and tone. Sometimes you may not be certain what it wants you to do, but just staying with it often gets you there.

The graphics are by no means cutting-edge, but were still very good, warm and comforting. I think they fit the setting and pace of the game.

As long as you understand this is not exactly a game and more an interactive story and will only last you 4-5 hours (I took a few breaks in that time, so closer to 4 hours), then you will likely enjoy this offering. Knowing I got it on sale (why pay full price on Steam?), I feel I got my money's worth. If I had paid $20, I may not have been as happy. It is good quality all around, but knowing what I know now, I feel $20 is too much. I expect to be entertained longer than 4 hours when buying a video game. Those are my feelings, decide for yourself what you think of the game. I would recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
