\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are rarely time in which a peice of media compels me to immediatly start writing about it the second I finish it; and What Remains of Edith Finch was one of those times.

Sometimes we often forget as to why stories are told; why we share them with others.

It's always about trying to help each other.

Stories have always been a coping mechanism for problems we have in our lives. They help us forget about them or even remember them and look at them another way.

This story is a grim one; a tragedy is what you'd normally classify it as. It's one which does not have a happy ending, rather a bitter sweet one.

Sometimes those are the ones we need the most; they ground us to our world which never gets a fairytale ending. The level of emotion I felt throughout was the perfect mix of watching a life unfold before you. In fact, you get to see several do so.

I feel as if every story has a point to it; a moral, an underlying meaning, whatever you call it.

I feel as if this story teels us that things can happen; more often than not bad things can happen.

But the point is not to dwell on the past, the past is unchanging; today and tommorow are always your choice.
the past can eat you alive, it can be your downfall even though it's but a fleeting thing.
All the charachters in this story learn that by always thinking the past was their fault leads to their own end.

As much as we wish to change history; history is done.
Time to make your own.\t\t\t\t\t

It is what Interactive Fiction was meant to be, a series of beautiful vingettes that never do the same thing twice, and an emotional story that almost left me in tears. You need to play this.\t\t\t\t\t
