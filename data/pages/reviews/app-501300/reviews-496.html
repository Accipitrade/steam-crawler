\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor me, What Remains of Edith Finch is possibly one of the best games I've played on Steam. You can see and feel how much love and passion Giant Sparrow has put into this walking simulator masterpiece.

Pros:
- Graphics are awesome! It is a really beautiful game with tons of details
- Its music / sound track are just great. In fact, I think it's very enjoyable to listen to its music while the game itself is paused
- Atmosphere: Little action, no pew pew, just a really great atmosphere to enjoy. Fans of walking simulators will know what I mean
- Innovation: I was expecting a great walking simulator but I got flashed by its innovational elements. The story is being told using mini games which let you experience former events as if they were happening to you. The realization probably couldn't be done much better.
- Variety: The game plays very smoothly due to its broad variety. Walking simulators can sometimes get boring if they are too monotonuous. Unlike What Remains of Edith Finch.
- The game is also great for couples who want to spend some relaxing time together and alternate with one another (or just watch the other one play it). It's so beautiful that just watching it being played feels awesome.
- Of course the story. Get your tissues ready.

Cons:
- In my opinion, with about 4hrs of playing time, the game was somewhat short. (I have a feeling that we played it quite thoroughly and didn't miss much.)

Summary:
If you enjoy playing games like Life is Strange or Firewatch, chances are you will like this too. It's not too long (which makes it easy to finish) but is worth every penny/cent/whatever your currency.\t\t\t\t\t
