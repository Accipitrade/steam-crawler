\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort, sweet, mesmorizing and poetic. This is a calm, sometimes tragic, but always beautiful story. The final pay off is a peaceful sense of finality that, for a moment, makes you feel fine with just being. It is one of those stories where the end was the story, yet the beauty is in getting there. Don't expect complex gameplay, gimicks, or much in the way of challenge. This is an adventure of history and discover. This was an amzing story told in a maner that justifies the format, so sit back with a cup of coffee and be prepared to let subtle but powerful emotions wash over. If I was given the option to watch any modern 2 hour hollywood film, or spend twice as much money on this, I would choose the latter for it's novelity. This is not the most impactful story ever told, and I am not sure it was meant to be. What it is, it does well. 4/5\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is all at once intriguing, creepy, beautiful and severely ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up. It's a series of stories within a story with themes of death, loss, grief and moving on. As you move through the world you feel less like a singular autonomous human than the latest in an endless chain of people living out the same events over and over. The house which most of the game takes place in feels like a sinister character with masked motivations, and you can never escape the feeling that all of your actions have been pre-ordained by forces beyond your control or understanding. It's been a long time since a game was able to make me feel so isolated and small.

It's a 9/10 for me. Keep in mind that I'm a complete hipster♥♥♥♥ who's predisposed to these types of games anyway, but I highly recommend that you give this game your much-deserved attention.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn the surface, this is a game that is closest tied to the genre "walking simulator";

Might sound quite boring, if you think about simply walking around. But compared to games like "Dear Esther" (which was quite poetic, if a little copious), "Gone Home" (which told a very human story) and "Everybody's Gone To The Rupture" (which mixed Sci-Fi and Believe themes - but lost me quickly), this game manages to capture the spirit of a good book and mixes it with the possibilities of the gaming genre.

The controller recommendation is due to better immersion and I second it, although you can easily enjoy the game with classic mouse + keyboard setting.

From a technical perspective this game is impressive, as graphics and sound mix together very harmonically and create immersion easily. There is a keen eye to detail and if you enjoy it, you can easily get lost in the small rooms, discovering things that add up to the total story.

See this software more as an interactive experience, a story that shifts your perception and plays with memories.
For me, this is the gaming equivalent to the movie "Big Fish", only that you are right in the middle.

Even though the time spent is only about 2-3 hours to finish the experience I gladly spent the money for it, as the time spent was full of surprises, emotions and made me disconnect from my sometimes hectic life quite well.

If you have to be careful about every cent you spent, you might want to consider watching a "let's play" on youtube, but you will miss on the experience. Better wait for a discount on the software.

I hope these thoughts were helpful in making a decision if you should buy "What Remains Of Edith Finch". Leave a comment if you like.\t\t\t\t\t
