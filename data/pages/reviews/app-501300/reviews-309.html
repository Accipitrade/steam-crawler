This game is amazing! the story, the voice acting (my favorite part), the soundtrack, visuals, diversity in mini games, etc. It's all alot to take in. There are too sides to this coin, on one end you have a game that is short and sweet, but also has this beautiful and haunting story that kinda speaks for life its self. Without spoiling anything, if i had to pick an overarching theme for this games narrative, it would be everything in it is perishable, family, memories, life. Even though this game is based on the story of a fictional family it still manages to hit very close to home. Alot of messages can be recognized throughout the game such as: living on borrowed time, seeking closure to the plot holes in our lives, reflecting on the past, or treading on dead water in hopes you'll discover something you messed previously. In summary, i highly, highly recommend this game, its very unique and i'm eager to see what project(s) is/are next for the developers of this title.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is proof to me that video games have the capacity to be the best storytelling medium ever. I was never much into books, and the story in this game is far from the most riveting, but with the innovative gameplay, I was glued to the screen the entire time. It's difficult to explain this game since you only understand it's creativity if you play it yourself, so I will try to get you to do so.
You play as many different members of the Finch family, but primarily Edith Finch. She is going back to her old house to discover the history of her more-or-less cursed family. As you explore, you get to see through the eyes of those family members in some of their, last moments. Not exactly a happy game.
Each of these "stories" you play through are only interpretations of what happened, which makes things even cooler. The gameplay always goes above and beyond, each segment being so radically different from the next that you could forget what game you're playing.
This is no walking simulator, but it is relaxing and takes no skill to complete. Dialogue being all beautifully voiced, along with the unbelievable visuals, makes the experience perfect all the way through. It took me about 2 hours to finish, and it's so worth it. Just watching someone play it will not do it justice. Beautiful masterpiece.
