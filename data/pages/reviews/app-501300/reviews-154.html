\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's hard for me to develop empathy (or any kind of sympathy) for a family full of idiots. It's not a walking simulator, it's an audio-book about child neglect. You don't get to "search for stories", as the developers allude to in the description. In fact, the game doesn't let you explore the nooks and crannies of this (admittedly) interesting house at all - it forces you to follow a specific trail or sequence of events. It tries so hard to be deep and special, but it just left a stale taste in my mouth. Most characters are unlikeable and obtuse, which made it difficult to keep me engaged. I just didn't really care about finding out more about the bizarre and abusing circumstances leading up to this - unsurprising - genetic dead end. Someone should have called Child Protection and/or Social Services.

I really don't get the hype, but then again, I started the game having high expectations due to the raving reviews. It's just a short visual novel with beautiful visuals and a lukewarm story. Definitely not worth the full price in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a walking simulator, but that's not why I'm down-voting it. I'm down-voting it because this is the third walking simulator I've played with the EXACT SAME BORING PLOT AND THEME. Ethan Carter, Gone Home, and this - all basically the same boring theme.

Well, maybe you'd say that it's unfair to downvote the third one. Well, life is pointless and tragic, right. And, unfair. Right? Isn't that your core philosophy? Well, enjoy this unfairness:P

It's sad too, because sometimes I just want a quick little story. Sometimes, I want a game to be a bit of a palate cleanser between more traditional games. But, it's not fun when the story is this completely predictable. You aren't "subverting expectations" for having a story about the pointlessness of existence in a world where 99.99% of everyone is a self-absorbed solipsist. Like, this is what I would expect more normies to write. And, that's what happened here, probably. The devs of this put down their substance abuse addiction on which they rely on their pointless lives just long enough to make a walking simulator that was either a clone of or a predecessor of every walking simulator out there. (Except for the Amnesia stories...because they include some limited mechanics and a story that's actually worth reading).

OK, I'm done now. What a waste of time, and, to be fair, I wanted to waste some time, but, holy cow...I do have my limits.

Also, if you pay the full $20 for this, that's $10 per hour of playtime:D

PS: Have you ever considered that your life sucks because life is actually existentially fair? Just saying. Give it some time. Mull it over.\t\t\t\t\t
