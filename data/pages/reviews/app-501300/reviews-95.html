\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPERSONALLY this game did not do it for me :<

I got this as it was recommended to me while i was taking about Before Your Eyes to someone else and they spoke very highly of this games story and messages. Upon reading a few non spoiler reviews i was excited to dive in and enjoy however as you can tell it did not work out for me. I should say that its not like i do not think it is a good game however it does not live up to the hype. Without getting into spoilers all i can say is i definitely expected more from such a critically acclaimed title, i even went online after to watch a review/synopsis of the game and its short stories to make sure that i just was not being super dense.

TLDR: Was expecting great things but it only gave me short stories that were forgettable :<

______ SPOILER ALERT________


It was just a bunch of short stories on how each member dies except how can you get attached to any one of them when you only experience them in either the short sequence or have them be talked about to you. I am not sure if you are supposed to feel a connection to the characters at all but none of the deaths felt sad because you get no time to connect with any of the characters IMO. The only exception was Gregory and that was because he was just a baby and THAT was due to the fact that ♥♥♥♥ like that is terrifying for parents (no i am not one so no bias) and a very real feeling issue. On that subject lets go down the line of #relatable..

Molly- Not relatable, i understand that she could eat something poisonous but its a little extreme
Odin- Not relatable but its origin story for tree so it kinda gets a pass
Calvin- So yes and no? you have to throw physics out the window but it can work i guess
Barbra- Not relatable, none of us are stars
Walter- Not relatable
Sam- Not relatable, maybe if you are a hunter? no bonds to papa either
Gregory- God rest his beautiful soul, also was the most fun I had in any sequence of the game
Gus- Not relatable BUT i can see it happening, like dead ass what parent just is like "yea fly your kite in the storm kekw"
Milton- Not relatable AT ALL and unexplained
Lewis- The most relatable one but still no empathy or connection felt :/ definitely the most interesting part TBH
Edie- Realistic
Dawn- Realistic
Edith- Realistic

Like i said, I am not sure if you are supposed to connect with them but i genuinely could not get invested with any of them or their silly deaths. Even IF the curse was real, which they leave up to interpretation, all the death is meaningless and is just a story of how someone passed, embellished or not it is not interesting. The twist ending did not work for me because during the game you really do not connect with Edith as most of her time is spent narrating other peoples stories/lives/conversations and not much on her as a character. I can see how it is SUPPOSED to be sad or a reveal that the whole time it was her son reading her stories about the family but to be a broken record I was not emotionally invested with ANY of the cast.



-----Closing thoughts------

I really wished i got what others got out of this game. I left comments on for those of you that want to share your experience with it or maybe something you feel i missed or maybe i looked too hard into it or you want to send death threats because i did not like your game. Personally if you have not played or heard of Before Your Eyes you should go give it a try I bawled my eyes out playing it haha
