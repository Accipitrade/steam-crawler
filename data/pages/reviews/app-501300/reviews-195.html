After replaying What Remains of Edith Finch for this review over 3 years after first playing, I am reminded of what makes the game special. The game is a perfectly flowing story about 4 generations of the Finch family. Each section of the game tells the story of a family member of the Finch family tree which ultimately led to their death. While the tone of all of these stories can be a little bit inconsistent at times (especially the story of Barbara Finch), a feeling of impending doom begins to come over the Finch family. The later Finch members begin to believe they have a curse and that their deaths are a self fulfilling prophecy. The game has a certain fantastical and mystical element to it. You can tell these stories have been widely misinterpreted by the family members to fit the narrative of the family curse. I think the way that they tell each story is super interesting. They dont just say how each character died, they connect it to their personality and life. For example, Calvins death reflects his desire to take risks and do the impossible, ultimately to a fault. Another strong story is Walters. After the death of his brother and especially his sister Barbara, Walter becomes paranoid with PTSD and lives in a bunker below the house. After losing his sister due to an unknown entity, he sought comfort inthe certainty of his life in the bunker. Not every character was given the same emotional respect as others. Characters like Molly, Barbara, and Milton were not given the same emotional depth and motivations as Lewis, Walter, or Dawn. Despite this, I still felt all stories were very strong, it's just those three stuck at as being particularly interesting. The best aspect of the story is just how it showed how each person reacted to the reality of their family. Some tried to remove themselves from it completely (Dawn), some decided to just live on (Sam), and some people locked themselves out completely from the outside world (Walter and Lewis). It is ultimately a story about dealing with trauma and PTSD that is told with a mixture of realism and fantasy. The game is complemented with fantastic voice acting, music, and beautiful graphics. It is.a short game, but a perfect length for the story it is trying to tell. The game is certainly not for everyone as it is a walking simulator, but there is plenty of intrigue here to keep anyone who is willing to give it a shot interested. The ending is not bad by any means, but it doesnt end with a bang. The ending is quite subtle and understated, but fits what the story has been trying to tell all along. Overall this is a near perfect walking simulator. The story is super unique with a great variety of personalities. You can definitely find someone in the story that you relate to or resonate with. Most importantly, it is a game that respects your time. It has ZERO fluff, which is amazing. I hesitate to give walking simulators 10s because they lack a major component of video games, being gameplay, but this is a must play experience in my opinion. 9/10
