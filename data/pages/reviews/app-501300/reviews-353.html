\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeautifully sad game with some of the best environmental design I have ever seen. The bedrooms of each deceased Finch felt like the former occupant reincarnated as a setpiece, breathtaking in their personality and detail. The stories and gameplay elements had enough variety to keep things fresh and interesting, and the devs somehow managed to craft each vignette in such a way that even the stories of children's deaths never felt morose. Hell, I have suicidal ideations sometimes, and the vignette in which I literally walked one of the Finches into their suicide was handled with such care and tact that I felt only touched by it, not triggered into feeling suicidal myself.

Representation-wise, I was surprised by the fact that the player character and her siblings were half Indian, and by the subtle revelation that one of her brothers was bisexual. Very cool to see in a game!

The biggest issue I had here was with the text. At the lowest graphical settings, text becomes practically impossible to read. Additionally, the way subtitles are displayed as part of the environment may be a cool stylistic choice, but it's not good for people like me who need subtitles as an accessibility feature. I would have liked an option to swap to standard subtitles along the bottom of the screen.

Overall, a gorgeous and plaintive experience. I'll probably replay this once I have a more powerful machine to run it at better graphics settings.\t\t\t\t\t

However.

The game sets you up with wonderfull narrative experiences, beautiful sense of art and execution, gentle promises of deep character and narrative development, alas, just as all the lovely quirky characters and their fates, the game experience ends prematurely, leaving me with a sense of emptinens, unfulfilled promises and longing for a closure of loose ends.
