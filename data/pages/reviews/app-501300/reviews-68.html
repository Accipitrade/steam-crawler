\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCool, but neither fun, nor fulfilling.

I was all hyped up about playing this game once it was on sale. Two hours later after finishing it, I can tell you that the concept, art, and execution is cool, but that doesn't automatically translate to fun or fulfilling.

That is, the scenes where it cuts to you playing family members is imaginative, but at the end of the game, I didn't care for how the story stayed unnecessarily ambiguous or how the game lured you to the goal rather than challenged you. At least in the game Gone Home, you have inventory items and are able to interact with light switches. In Finch, you interact with hardly anything except doors and the storybooks.

I'd only recommend this game who enjoy pure walking simulators, but if you are expecting a light puzzler or adventure, you'll be as disappointed as I was.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is ok. Just ok. Its finished in about 2 hours if you take your time and there isn't really a reason to replay the game. The Story is pretty interesting but way too shallow. Great concepts and ideas but bad execution. I wished for more of a challenge but the game doesnt let you figure out how to get through the mysterious house with its numerous hidden passages and doors. No, your held by your hand and walk a predetermined path without making any decisions yourself. So much untapped potential. The characters are all interesting but way to superficial. You get about 5 Minutes of information for most characters and thats it. Besides that, all of them just act completly irrational:
A storm is raging outside and your son is playing out there with a kite? ah, forget it, turn the music up louder and be surprised if you find his corpse later.
The baby is in the bathtub and you get a call? leave for the call and let the baby drown. No it was not your fault, it was probably the curse of the house or family or something.
Oh your son is working the most depressing job ever and has mental problem that get worse and worse every day? Just do nothing! Oh no, he killed himself, who would have thought?! Its the curse again, i couldnt do anything.

And the game keeps going like this. All of the characters act completly irrational and they die left and right because of that. But its never anyone's fault, of course. I felt like all these characters were either breathing in fumes that made them dumb from the swamp next to the house or they were all just ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. But this makes it really hard to feel bad for any terrible fate you witness.

And lets not talk about the 2 hours of gameplay that are mostly very very slowly walking with no way to sprint and unnecessarily annoying controls where you have to out of nowhere move your characters hand seperatly to open books or doors which just makes up more time. You effectivly get about 1 hour of story and that will cost you 20 bucks. Nah, thank you, I'd rather not.

TL;DR: I normaly love story games but this just wasn't it. Too shallow, too short, too one-dimensional, too clunky and full of unlikable characters.\t\t\t\t\t

Mysterious story around Finch family consumes you completely for 2 hours and releases you only after the credits scene. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tA short game that is honestly more of an interactive movie as it is quite a linear game with a great story covering the various lives of members of the Finch family and the main character's adventure through the family home to uncover them. What Remains of Edith Finch is a beautiful presentation filled with great art and map design, creating a world that feels lived in and filled with character in terms of both environment and the characters presented in the story. Even though we spend only a short amount of time with each character we learn a great deal about them not only from their perspective but also through the environmental story telling of their rooms within the house the game takes place in. The sombre and calm music that plays throughout the game only further adds to the presentation of the game.
Overall, if you enjoy playing games for storylines and don't mind walking simulators that are fairly linear this game is excellent and I would highly recommend it, especially if you find it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
