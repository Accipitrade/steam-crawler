\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall
This game is interesting and aesthetically gorgeous. The gameplay isn't exactly 'fun' as it is more like a walking simulator, but the minigame memories are unique and the plot produces enough intrigue that I genuinely enjoyed it. The characters who are detailed are very human feeling, and the ones who aren't are a bit disappointing. I consider some left out character details to be the game's largest and most frustrating weakness and go into more detail below. Overall I'd say despite the issues I have with the things left unaddressed, the game is worth playing through and the storytelling is still great. The only pain is knowing it *could have been* even better with some extra information.

I got this during a steam sale and recommend it... with the caveat that you do the same. I don't believe this game's 2.5h story/3h 100% achievement completion playtime is worth $20usd. The 'how much is this game worth to me' question can only truly be decided by you, but for me it's reasonable in the $10-13usd range. I got it for $8usd and am grateful I did because my frustration with the writing would escalate proportionally to how much cash I sunk in it.

Positives
++++ The story is phenomenal with some significant holes imo (see below).
++ Gameplay during Lewis's factory scene was unique and very well thought out.
++ Visuals are fantastic, lighting and colour choices remind me of how moved I was with the bright reds and golds in the sky in Bioshock Infinite when it first came out.
++ Diversity of the different stories is beyond my expectations. You get to play as an owl and a sea creature in just one.
+ Soundtrack was pretty nice.
+ I like that you can see your character's body when you look down. My partner and I were debating whether Edith was one of the first fat characters we had played as in first person, or if she was pregnant.
+ Ending itself is cute/sad and felt like a decent cap to the story. All of my anger came at the fact it signified I would never receive more info on some of the characters.

Neutral
  • It's a walking simulator with fun spices sprinkled throughout. I don't mind this at all and got invested into the story, but it's definitely not for everyone.
  • Gameplay mouse movements are quite active, think Amnesia click-dragging to open doors and chests except in this game you're also doing things like operating a flip-book your dead/missing bro left in his cool AF gazebo-like room.
  • As mentioned in opener, the value at the full listed price point is up to the individual, and for me it's in a slightly lower range. Glad I waited for a sale.
  • It's SHORT. I left it paused for a food break and still reached 100% achievement completion at 2.9 hours.
  • During my playthrough it was 99% glitch free, but I would recommend trying not to mess around too much on the Lewis minigame because I did get the Adventure Lewis stuck on the docks for about 2-3 minutes.
  • FOV is limited and locked which bothers some people. In the game's community page people have figured out how to change it to help with motion sickness. I usually prefer my FOV pretty high but this game didn't make me feel queasy so I left it unchanged.

Negatives
I only have one true negative but to me it was large enough to greatly harm my overall enjoyment of the game.

The story was so strong in the beginning and throughout that the sheer amount of character details they left on purpose at the end was a huge letdown. I can't even fully put into words how disappointed I am they didn't provide any more insights into a few of the characters - not even necessarily solid answers on their deaths, just more details or information about who they were. We are told during play that Edie has tried to document all family stories and the reason Edith knows so little is because her mother Dawn is trying to shield her from the family history and escalates further to running away to try to keep Edith from learning more. That's all well and good, but if all books and memorabilia were left in the home undisturbed as Edith claims, there is no excuse that the returning Edith cannot find more information, eg in the family's library where her mother tore the book from Edie out of her hands, and where Edie claims the stories of the family dwell.

It was so dissatisfying I found myself a little angry when the credits started rolling; to know just how many questions would be left unanswered. Of the ones barely addressed, Barbara, Molly, Calvin, and Sam are the characters I feel were served unjustly. Gus, Milton, Edie, Dawn, Lewis, and Edith especially had felt natural with their levels of coverage which spanned from almost nothing known at all (Milton and Gus) to an abundance (Edith, Edie). The writing cleared it for Milton; I don't expect to know more about the death of someone whose body was never found, and it seems Dawn did not want to keep much information around about her child since she was so unlike Edie in how she viewed the past. Edith gave us some information on Lewis from her time spent with him, and his story was poignant and interesting. The writing for the more fleshed out characters contrasting so hard with the absolute dearth of info we're given for those like Barbara and Calvin makes it all the more irritating knowing how interesting they could've been if they had even a quarter of the time the other characters received.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat visuals and story telling devices, but the ending fell pretty flat for me. The game consists around a central mystery of a vague curse which is said to affect the Finch family of the game. If you expect this mystery to be resolved, you are in for a disappointment. There is certainly an elegiac and wistful core to the narrative, but it is left ambiguous how much of what you're seeing in the flashbacks is actually reality. What happened to Barbara who mysteriously disappeared one night? What became of Milton? What did Edie find in the old house? After playing the game you are left none the wiser.

I really appreciate the hard work and attention to detail that the developers put into this game, but this is the type of game that lives and dies with its plot, and the plot never moves much beyond the premise of an insular family with an abnormally high morality rate, which is a real missed opportunity imo.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne word, WOW. It began as a journey of delight and childhood wonder and ended in feelings of sadness and respect. This game took me on a emotional rollercoaster i DID NOT expect to take today. I had just bought this game on a whim, expecting to kill some time diving into a new narrative but boy was i wrong. I felt a great connection to every character in the storyline from beginning to end. It took me through all human emotions from happines to sadness and everything in between. Discovering new rooms and people was amazing, never quite knowing what to expect and being suprised everytime. And at the end, as the credits began to roll, i burst out into tears. i have never played a game this intense before. it pulled every string i had and played the most beautiful song i have ever heard. The song of life.

I do not recommend this game to people who are suffering from mental health issues as the story can get very intense. I myself am struggling with depression and it took me a few days to get back to normal. Though i did find it very therapeutic in dealing with feelings and emotions i had bottled up inside.

I am still glad i experienced and played the game because it helped me get over thing and in the end it made me stronger. life is a journey of wonder and excitement, sadness and hate, happiness and love.

There is a valuable lesson to be learned for those who are ready to embrace it. It's best summarized in the games ending narrative "If we lived forever, maybe we'd have time to understand things. But as it is, i think the best we can do is try to open our eyes and appreciate how strange and brief all of this is."

This is, in my opinion, a true masterpiece of storytelling. Well done and thank you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR Money for value. Lackluster game lasts 2 hours. Unless it costs you 2 bucks or less, don't bother with this.

Honestly disappointed with this "game", barely feels like one at all. Trusting the reviews, bought it on sale and I still feel it's too expensive for what it is. It truly deserves the walking simulator name, since there's barely anything that could be considered gameplay. In fact, the walking is so slow it can become incredibly frustrating at times. Personally I didn't enjoy the story in the least, you're thrown into all these individual stories of each member of the family, which don't feel connected at all and don't last enough to make you feel any sense of attachment—each family member's story is explained on a single sheet of paper, that should give you an indication of how incredibly short these are—and simply make you wonder how much bad luck (so to speak) one can have. Goes without saying the game is 100% linear. You play as Edith, the last member of the family, simply to become the narrator of these stories, because her character is extremely one-dimensional. There is nothing remarkable about it, no mystery to be solved at the end of the game, no "big reveal", nothing. You simply play each of the stories and that's it. That's all the game offers you. There is no reward, no acomplishment at all for finishing the game. It's like you visited (and ofc paid to enter) a museum and they explained to you the life of a dozen people from the same family. At the very least, when you go to a museum, you get to learn about historical figures, not a randomly invented family, which features pathetically one-dimensional characters to boot.

I am incredibly apalled that some people can state this is their favorite game. To each their own, but if they consider this lackluster game with 2 hours of "gameplay" a favorite, I don't really think there is a high standard there. Don't believe this should get a free pass for having "beautiful graphics" (which it really doesn't have, they're honestly acceptable graphics) or great voice acting, since if we removed the voice acting it wouldn't have anything going for it, it has no other notable features.

In conclusion. I am a firm believer in money for value. As such, I cannot recommend this game if they're charging you more than 2 bucks.\t\t\t\t\t
