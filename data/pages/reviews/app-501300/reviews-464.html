\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy review of What Remains of Edith Finch contains no spoilers.

If you were to only ever play a single "walking simulator" game, let it be this one.

Pros:

* Riveting, touching story. It never drags or starts to feel tired, it draws the player in from start to finish. Even more importantly, it manages to be emotionally charged (and at times rather upsetting) without resorting to typical tragedy cliches or making the player feel like they're playing a tear-jerker. Its observations are thoughtful without trying to get overly philosophical and its themes are meaningful without being melodramatic. The story of this game plays out very much like watching a very good, rather sad film.

* The environment is genuinely interesting to explore. The vast majority of the game is played in the Finch family's home, and it is massive, winding, and detailed with care. Walking around and looking at the place and everything in it is at times as intriguing as the main character's narration. If you're interested in old, oddly designed places, then exploring the house and navigating its hidden corridors will please you.

* The main pieces of story unfold in a series of vignettes each focused around a Finch family member. The vignettes bring additional mechanics in the form of what are essentially mini-games: there are entire sequences of playing the game in a different way than just walking around. This provides an interactive way to connect to the story and periods of respite from the usual gameplay sequence.

* Edith Finch is both likable and relatable. This is central to the experience - being able to sympathize with her and feel as though you're in her shoes make the game that much more compelling.

Neutral:

* The game is rather short for the amount it costs. I finished the game and its achievements in 2.5 hours, which is not a great amount of time for $20. That said, the game's quality is top-notch and since you could easily spend $20 on a movie ticket and concessions, I'd still say it's not a bad buy at full price. I bought it on sale for $12, and at that price I'd call it a must-have.

Cons:

* You walk rather slowly, and there is no way to speed up. I suppose this makes sense to preserve the pacing of the game and make sure Edith's narration never runs over itself, but there are a few sparse points where the slow movement can feel tedious.

Overall:

Seriously, buy the game and play it. This game has a ton of positive aspects and the only negatives to it are nitpicks. I'll reiterate the second thing I said in this review: if you play any one walking simulator out of all of them: choose this one!\t\t\t\t\t

I picked it up during the Lunar Sale and feel it's worth the sale price, not sure about full price as it's relatively short and likely minimal replay. I had no issues with the game, ran perfect on my GTX 960m with Windows 10. The game design/quality is top-notch; runs on Unreal 4. It's similar to Layers Of Fear and Firewatch. So if you enjoyed those, give this a try.\t\t\t\t\t
