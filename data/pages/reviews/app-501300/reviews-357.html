What Remains of Edith Finch, is a beautiful and elaborate game that shows walking simulation from its best side. In the game you play as the main character Edith Finch who has revisited her fantastic ancestral home in search of answers regarding the destiny of her relatives, and the supposed curse that watches over the family.

GAMEPLAY & STORY
As you play the game, you will be drawn back by the beautiful scenery, the brillian voice-acting and the unique and fun gameplay which continue to drastically vary throughout the game. In truth, it is also this varying gameplay, that will keep you engaged as it supports the story experience to a sublime degree. Not only will it keep you entertained, it also won't distract you as you experience the story.

The story itself is creative, original and contains a fair bit of afterthought, that will leave you not only wanting for more but also with a slight different view on life than you had before.

PRICE
The pricetag of 19.99 is a tad steep as the game isn't longer than 3 hours max (including all achivements and sitting and looking on the ocean in awe). I would still recommend the game though. This is an original experience created by a small indie team with a burning drive for making good gaming experiences. They deserve all the support they can get.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA real masterpiece!
I expected a slow-paced exploration walking simulator, but what I got was a linear point-and click story-driven game. Not disappointed though! The pacing is great, the art style(s) are beautiful, the music complements the scenes and the whole story and design is just so quirky and unique.
The main story is only about 2 hours (and that's without rushing), and I'd estimate another 0.5-1 hour to collect all the achievements. Quite a short game for the price, but I'd treat it more as a movie than a game.\t\t\t\t\t
