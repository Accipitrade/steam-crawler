\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA true innovation connects the storytelling and the interactive design, which enables the experience that a film or a novel can never provide.

Pros:
- So many detailed things that are telling the story, even the stuff considered as junk in most other games, and yes, you can still play through the game if you don't notice them.
- The way that the captions in and out is perfect.
- The narrative design generates an illusion that you have the choices.
- Most first-person characters can see part of their own bodies when looking down.
- Works on my years-old computer even everything in Graphics set to Ultra.
- The control is simple.

Cons:
- Some story scenes stand out while some others not as much as at the same level, for example, my favorite one is the Lewis' fish-head cutting part , but the storytelling of Odin, WoHer, Dawn, Milton and Edith's death is relatively not as much emotional hitting.
- You may want to play the game twice when you finish it once, and the Unlockable Achievements may lead you to that, but it's quite easy to get the achievements and just that boring, no additional stories, easter-eggs, new discovery or secret endings for a second play. But this is not a must-have for a game, maybe I'm just asking too much or I didn't find any. Please comment below and let me know if there is.

Overall:
A masterpiece that worths the money and I highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
