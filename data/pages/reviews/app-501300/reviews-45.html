\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIndeed a masterpiece of interactive storytelling. Not being a big fan of narrative-driven walking simulator, I found this game so impressive and atmospheric because of engaging design of narration and mysterious ambience throughout the game.

I've never played a game that can capture feelings of touching moments like this one did. I really enjoy wandering around those well-furnished rooms with shafts of light through the windows, crawling through strange secret passages, and swinging on a swing with cold wind blowing into the ears...I like those bizzare stories from the perpective of each family member that delivers topics on life and death in such an imaginative way. I wish it could be longer as some stories I want to explore more end unexpectedly.

Approaching the endgame I get a bit melancholy but actually in a way I enjoy a lot.

“If we lived forever, maybe we'd have time to understand things. But as it is, I think the best we can do is try to open our eyes, and appreciate how strange and brief all of this is.”
