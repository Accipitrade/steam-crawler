\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs spectacularly bleak as it is beautiful. This product answers almost no questions, but seems like a vehicle for a programmer in love with tragedy. Other games have shown that pathos can be a powerful emotional tool, but all pathos and no resolution leaves the viewer feeling depressed and disappointed.
MASSIVE SPOILERS AHEAD
A story in which every single character dies, including the narrator, all in pointless, cheap, sad ways, seems like something an Emo teenager would write. Yes the narrative is well written, the production is mystical and poetic, the music is gentle and beautiful, but ultimately what is resolved? Is there a curse? Why is it that even having decided that the "curse" is more self made, that Edie herself must die? Why so many dead children - was it just to amp up the sadness? Ultimately, this story felt manipulative, vaccuous and pointless. There was no gameplay element of any real note, and as a story, it was deeply unsatisfying. Can nothing be changed? Is this really an excuse to kill 8 or 9 kids and a few adults? Clearly this is about more than mere mortality, and any family that grew up in such a loving, mystical place would have the very best of chances, not the worst. I'm a happy ending sort of guy but I get that not every story must end happily but this tale was the epitome of self-indulgent tragedy for its own sake. It was nothing but mental masturbation with sad music. I imagine the author standing in front of a mirror, mascara smeared with tears, looking with satisfaction at how sad she looks.
This game has masses of likes, but ultimately, regretfully I can't be one of them. I desperately want to reward the story-telling, the graphics, the sheer uniqueness of this work, but I can't get past its overwhelming nihilism. The binary choice of recommend or not do not do it justice but if I must choose one, its unfulfilling lack of resolution earns it a thumbs down. But it is a unique, incredible production. I don't regret the £8 I paid for it, but it will haunt me.\t\t\t\t\t

Game play mechanics are very intuitive and unique from what I'm used to.

It is relatively short, me completing it in two hours, but the quality more than made up for that.
I REALLY suggest giving it a go, worth every cent and second.
Highly recomended!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm saying I recommend this game, because I really wanted to like this game more than I did, and I'm sure there are lots of people out there who will like it better than I did. And because overall, the pros outweighed the cons.

PROS:
-Interesting storytelling mechanic, having the main character pick up stories as she makes her way through the old house.
-EXCELLENT depiction of each character. Where appropriate, the art style changed to reflect that character's perspective. This was probably my favorite aspect of this game, and I cannot say enough about it! (But most of what I say would be spoilers, so I won't. :P )
-There wasn't a character that I found to be unrelatable on some level. Each character was well-written and easy to understand.
-The control scheme for how you interact with things was genius.

CONS:
-The controls, while genius, were awkward or janky a number of times for me, which made a few sections (like the flipbook) more difficult than they needed to be. When they weren't awkward or janky, they were...not...right. There were a couple of instances where I had to move my mouse up in order to interact with something in a way that didn't make any sense for that particular item. I'm not sure if those were bugs or just mistakes, but it was enough that I had to put the controls here, too. :c
-The name of the game is a spoiler, and I knew from the moment I picked up the game that the ending was a foregone conclusion. I would have personally found it more interesting if the title had referred to her mental state after having returned to the house, or--well, see the next two points.
-The ending was abrupt. The story flowed well overall, and then, with very little fanfare or preparation, it was just...over.
-The main character's story feels left out. By the end of the game, I had a general idea of how every character met their demise EXCEPT THE TITLE CHARACTER. I wouldn't have even minded that a lot of them were a little vague, but I at least would have liked to know what actually happened at the end. I know there's something to be said for leaving an audience wanting more, but this isn't a game series or a movie or even a trilogy. It's a standalone story and the main character just had so much more to explore. That was ALMOST was enough, by itself, to make me not recommend this game.

I put some things in spoiler tags, but I don't really feel--if anyone viewed the trailers for the game--that they were spoilers. All the same, I put them in spoiler tags just in case.

So yeah. I loved the game overall, except for the controls and the ending. I'm sure there are a lot of folks out there who'll feel that the ending was some great artistic choice, and even if they don't, the rest of the game is still VERY MUCH WORTH PLAYING THROUGH.

I just won't be replaying this one to get the acheevos. :c\t\t\t\t\t
