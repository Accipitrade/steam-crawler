\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI know saying that this game is a work of art and definitely a masterpiece won't add a thing to whatever amount of reviews you can read, but it's true as can be.

Years ago I bought The Unfinished Swan on a whim, and I was beyond captivated by how engaging it's storytelling was, which at the time, I hadn't played something like it before. I 100% the game more than once I liked it so much.

So come a year ago and I heard their next project had a close release. I think I saw a few screenshots, and somehow became turned off. It probably had to do with not having as strong as a focused mechanic as The Unfinished Swan did, which was painting your way through blank worlds and solving simple puzzles. When I looked at this I foolishly thought there wasn't much to it than similar concepts as Gone Home and especially The Vanishing of Ethan Carter.

This game floored me. Through my skeptical first 20 minutes I was grabbed by the creativity of the sequences. It's very linear, but understand this is the best interactive "movie" you could ever play.

The best thing you could possibly do going into this game is please, PLEASE do not watch or look at many of the trailers. I skipped over everything related to the game besides the praise it recieved, and after finishing it I notice how a lot of sequences are included in screenshots and trailers. Trust me, you'll want to be playing them for the first time you see them.

It's short but much more than sweet.\t\t\t\t\t

I buy a lot of adventure games and walking simulators, but my attention span is abysmal, so I tend to pause them in the beginning never to return to them. This game didn't allow me to do that, because it seriously contains one magical gameplay revelation after another, which all build toward a memorable story as well. There is a 10 minute section in the beginning where the game seems "kind of like a walking simulator", but once you get past that point there is no turning back. The game contains zero percent useless filler to pad out the experience artificially. I don't want to spoil the brilliant interactive sections that the game has to offer, but I would recommend this game for anyone who is interested in games period, irrespective of if you are a fan of adventure games or walking simulators.\t\t\t\t\t
