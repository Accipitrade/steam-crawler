\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm sure everyone else has already said similar to what I will say here. This game is... strange, it's intriguing and it is somehow simultaneously cold, dark, and warm and inviting at the same time. I do highly recommend you play it for yourself to form your own opinions and conclusions. It's rather short and offers a... maybe not life changing but certainly thought provoking experience. The bizarre nature of the events that occur and the way you have to sort of... filter through what happens is incredibly confusing, but so wonderful at the same time. Even if theorizing and attempting to decipher the truth from fiction of the retold events, it still comes with an enjoyable tale and a truly meaningful message. At least in my opinion. Regardless of my experience with What remains of Edith Finch, and regardless of what I may have derived from the stories within, when I look back on what was without a doubt there... I see one core message. The time that we have on this world, is short. Incredibly short for that matter, and none of us know how short our time may be, and yes that sounds scary. Admittedly it is, but that isn't the point of the message, it's not trying to scare you, but remind you that, when someone close to you passes, don't mourn them for too long. Don't get caught up in the heartbreak that the person is gone. Wrap yourself in the warmth that at least for a small frame of time, a few tender moments, that person got to be with you. Even when your time comes, don't be fearful, focus on how amazing it is that you got the chance to be here and experience the wonders you did. Rather it be the smile you get whenever your child is happy, or the warmth you feel inside when your loved ones are close. Focus on the joys in life that you would never trade for anything. The feeling you get when you see someone you hold close truly light up with joy for the first time in days, maybe even longer. Being part of this short time we all have is the truly greatest thing you can experience. Maybe I'm reading too deeply, maybe you'll take away something different, although... that is the point of this game being so vague. We all can get something different out of the same piece of art, because it is subjective. Cherish every moment of the short time we have here, and be grateful that you got the chance to be involved. That is what I believe to be the message of What remains of Edith Finch.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10/10
One of the most amazing experiences a person can have through interactive story-telling.

It's not so much of a game than it is a surrealistic walking simulation, and a short lived one at that. It only took me three hours from start to finish to complete the game. It doesn't have the same depth of puzzle solving as Gone Home, or the same supernatural saga as in Life is Strange, but it doesn't need to. The imagination built into the house alone is coupled well with it's character stories. A mixture beholding of something more grand.

SPOILER LITE:
I wish the idea behind the plot was flushed out a bit more, perhaps in the end through a monologue. I had a few questions to ask that most were left to fan theories in meta content. How characters actually died, what was the real curse, etc. Some of these questions are safe to assume with further inspection of the house and its details, but others are left unknown. Perhaps this is for the best, as I tend to enjoy stories the most when I have "some" questions post witness. In the end it's a story about life and the odd ways it will unfold, some mysterious and other times tragic.\t\t\t\t\t
