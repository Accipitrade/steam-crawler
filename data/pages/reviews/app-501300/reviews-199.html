\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's mostly good. some of the stories are kind of dumb though. who showers a baby in a regular bathtub, let alone answers the phone and leaves him unattended? the only curse this Finch family seems to suffer from is abject stupidity. It's made up though by some of the better stories like Barbara's or Lewis'. Overall though it follows that cookie cutter "everyone is gone, figure out why" template that for some reason every walking sim since The Stanley Parable has to follow. The problem with these games is there is usually no coherent overarching story, and Edith Finch is no different; it's just a collection of short stories about people that are usually based around when the person dies. Edith Finch is probably the crown jewel of this genre though, since the way all the family members are interconnected and referenced shows they put a lot of effort into making the Finch family believable; the manor you explore truly feels lived-in. The way you learn so much about their lives through flashbacks, each family member's room, and Edith's own recollection is impressive. Not to mention the flashbacks are all in different genres; flip books, comic books, symphonies, there's a big variety here. It's only around 3 hours so I would buy it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
