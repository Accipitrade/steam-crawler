\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCan't wrap my head around the love for this game. The game wants to tell an impactful story, but has way too many characters and way too little time to develop any of them meaningfully or tell a compelling story. Similar stories have been told far better in much more appropriate mediums than a game. As a short story, Edith Finch is average at best. As a game, Edith Finch is painfully tedious.

Here's how the gameplay goes:

Slink forward at 0.1 mph.
Fight for control of the camera against floating text that appears.
Find a glowing symbol somewhere.
Hit RT on it.
Move the control stick in some direction to "interact" with them. (Immersively push the control stick to operate a door!)
Hear about how some random uncle died.
If you're lucky there's a a cool flashback.
Usually you aren't lucky.
Repeat for 2.5 hours.
