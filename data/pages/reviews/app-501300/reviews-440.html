\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed this game. I was particularly impressed by the variation in the different stories - you're never just playing the same thing over and over with each member of the Finch family, but rather it's an array of very different experiences each time.

Be aware that it's not a puzzle game, and you can't really get stuck wondering what to do next or how to solve something. But, if you're like me and love this sort of game for the story (but still want a bit more control than "Dear Esther"...), this will be a great game for you.

The length of it could be good or bad depending on how you look at it. At 2.5 hours' play from start to finish (inluding pausing for the loo or to make a cuppa), I'm not sure it's worth the full price, but if you can get it on sale as I did then I'd say go for it. In many ways you wouldn't want the game to be much longer, because the beauty of it is in the variation it has and I would say it's definitely best played through in one sitting.

All in all, a beautifully-made game with an immersive storyline, which kept me interested all the way through.\t\t\t\t\t
