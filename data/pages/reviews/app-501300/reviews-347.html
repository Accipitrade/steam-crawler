\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood info to know before buying: this is basically an interactive story, not really a game, so you are on rails and your "performance" has very little impact. While this game does touch on eerie, horrific, and unsettling topics, it's not a horror game. Don't worry about jump scares. All in all, this is a really gorgeous experience, and I definitely recommend it to folks who like walking simulators/exploration/interactive stories.

Pros: gripping story, beautiful visuals, very novel sub-level storytelling/gameplay styles built into the overarching game.

Cons: it's fairly short, so don't expect anything overwhelming long. Because of this, the full price might feel like a bit too much for some folks. Buy on sale if game length and/or replayability matter to you. The ending unfortunately felt weak and abrupt compared to the rest of the game. I would have liked a little more information, background, closure in general. (see update- I've changed my mind)

Will it cause motion sickness? Yes it will. There are no options to modify field of view, head bob, etc to minimize this. I had to set it down and walk away a few times while playing. Is it worth struggling through if you get motion sick? YES. The game is short enough and good enough that it's worth it.

UPDATE 12/2020: I never really did stop thinking about this game, even long after I finished it, and with time and retrospection I've changed my opinion on the ending- it's SUPPOSED to be that way, and I think it would be much less powerful if they had a different ending. The way the game ends really just highlights and epitomizes the point of the whole game. With that realization, I'm much more satisfied with it.\t\t\t\t\t
