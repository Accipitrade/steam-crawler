\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a quick game to play, but a captivating story. I love the entire idea of digging for answers and knowing you'll never get them all. Of battling with the difference between your past as you know it and as it's told to you. Of facing your own ghosts. I enjoy slower games sometimes, and being able to play at my own pace, and it was nice to wander around and just SEE everything. And the voiceovers were perfect. Every object in every room brings the stories to life and tells you who the characters were. The things the dialogue DOESN'T say are just as powerful as what it implies. Some of the individual stories are so mysterious...but honestly I love the open-endedness of some of it. The gaps in the history. The way the characters remembered something and experienced something, and as the player I'm left with just enough space in my own head to work out what actually happened and come to my own conclusions and opinions. Some of them made me want to cry. There are a few twists that I definitely didn't see coming, and a few answers I never got that left me wanting more. (But in a good way.) It's one of those games where, when you're done, you just sit there in awe. Definitely an amazing story that I'm sure I'll replay again soon. :)\t\t\t\t\t

This game is not what I expected it to be, but then again, I didn't have many expectations, other than I thought I was getting into a horror walking simulator. What I ended up playing was a powerfully emotional experience that got me (middle-aged dude) crying like a child.

This is one of those games that you show your skeptical friends to demonstrate that video games can be effective and engaging forms of narrative to rival books and film.

If you hate walking simulators, at least give this one a try before writing off the whole genre. This game only took me 2 hours to complete, so it's like watching a film where you finish and go, "what exactly did I just watch?" and "that was really quite an experience" at the same time.\t\t\t\t\t
