\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a game that does not require much from you as a player - in the traditional sense that is. Mentally and emotionally however, the game will require your full attention.
I have never played a game quite like this. The game basically plays like what I would call a "very interactive first-person story". If that description scares you away, please don’t let it.
The characters and the narration is beautifully done, and the game tells one of the best stories I have ever been told in a game.
What Remains of Edith Finch is a dark game that deals with tough and painful themes, most notably death, suffering and mental illness. Again, don’t let that scare you away, there is room for laughs in there as well.
On a lighter note, the game is also about family, strength and care.
As a final comment I would advise that you go in as blind as you can. Don't read anything, don't watch trailers or reviews. If you think this could be something for you, just play it.
It is a good game. Trust me on that.
