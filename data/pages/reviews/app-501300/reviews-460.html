\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cried like a little girl at the end. 10/10 would feel the feels again.

No, but seriously joking aside, this game is an amazing experience. When I first saw and heard about this game, I just shrugged it off as a walking simulator, and while that is not entirely incorrect, it's far from only that.
The game is very similar to Gone Home in the sense that you are visiting your old family home and finding about the stories of the people who lived there, but instead of just reading them about them or hearing them, you get to play and take part in the events, and the ways this game tells those tales are very cool and never repeat the same gameplay mechanics. Not only that, but the stories themselves are very interesting and while they do tackle relatable issues, each family member is a distinct and odd character.
I would say that sometimes the controls feel like they've sacrificed responsiveness for realism and smooth animations, and at times it is simply hard to tell which action leads me to the next part of the game and results in me missing out content. These however are nitpicks.
The real problem is that the game is a tad bit overpriced for the amount of content it offers, but I bought it during a sale of -50%, which made the price around the same as a movie ticket and I'd say 2 hours of quality content was definitely worth it. So if the game is ever for sale or the price drops, give it a go if you enjoy good stories and feeling things.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was on the fence on whether or not to buy this game for some time as I wasn't convinced I would find it interesting even though there were many, many positive reviews and game play clips. Which the clips won't do any justice for the game, it's definitely one of those "you need to see it for yourself" titles. I let it stew for weeks and when I was alerted that it was on sale I decided to pull the trigger and purchase it; I'm really glad that I did.

Many tags list this game in the horror directory but it definitely is not a horror game by definition, although the overall tone of the stories is pretty dark. However the way the game tells the story is extremely immersive and interesting. It's almost like you're walking through the story. There were many unique mechanics that broke up the "walking simulator" feel though I wasn't bothered by the walking as the game constantly fed you dialog to keep you progressing, like a trail of bread crumbs.

If you're unsure on this title, just buy it. I would say that I guarantee that you will enjoy it but there are ALWAYS those that can't be pleased by anything. I bought it while on sale but I would have no issue with the $20 full price tag. This is my first review on Steam but this game encouraged me to review it as I'm sure there are many others that are well aware of this game's existence but unsure if they would enjoy it. I definitely recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
