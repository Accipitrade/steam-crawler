\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeautiful game and a very good story with decent gameplay.

Gameplay

The gameplay is mostly a walking simulator but there is also a surprising diversity between the stories. It does the walking very well though making each section unique and pretty weird. There are also some parts that involve more than walking with jumps or even flying and catching things part. One part is set up a bit like a slasher film with some very light combat. The gameplay elements are tied to the story so it is difficult to speak on all the elements without spoiling them. Overall I would say the gameplay greatly enhances the stories and keeps them engaging, it feels necessary and not gimmicky in order to make it interactive.

Story

The story is exceptional and very well written. It follows Edith as she journeys back to her childhood home to get answers about her strange family, all of whom seem to die tragically and many at a young age. The story takes us through each character's death with many of the stories being fantastical, leaving up for interpretation how much if any of the stories told are real. Each one is also narrated beautifully and in a different gameplay setting that is unique and adds greatly to the story. I won’t say much more about the story as it’s best to just unpack it but it deals with death and grief in a smart and creative way. I was thoroughly engaged and invested in the story the entire time and eager to continue. I was sad to see it end.


Graphics

The art style is gorgeous very beautiful. The graphics look great not insanely realistic but it has its own unique style. It also fits the whole mood of the game well.

Soundtrack

The soundtrack is nice, mainly soothing, and not overbearing at all.

Conclusion

What Remains of Edith Finch is an exceptional game due to its incredible story, it is best experienced knowing nothing and letting it envelop you and unfolding it as you go along.

Score: 9/10
