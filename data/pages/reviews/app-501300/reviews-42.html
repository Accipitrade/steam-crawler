The story is very original and the area in which the game most excels, the stories of Lewis and Molly being the best in my opinion, the game is only around 2 hours long but the story is better than some £50 titles i can think of.
The game play is nothing to complain about either, because even though it is simple to understand it varies within each level greatly so every story is a fresh experience.
The visuals are great too with many stylised levels and lighting which is truly beautiful most of the time. As a setting I love the Finch house because despite it's smallness I get lost a lot of the time in it's many rooms and passageways, you can really sense the history that the place holds. Throughout the game there is a constant narration that appears on the screen in the best and most innovative subtitles I have ever seen and I love the way they seem to really be there with the way they are sometimes able to be interacted with.
Overall i think this is a great game definitely worth the price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA gorgeous game with a rather enthralling setting and story. Take your time with this one, it could be easy to make the game feel rushed if you push right through. Any walking simulator like this walks a fine line of "Should this have just been a movie", but this story and your ability to feel the world around you and the bits of life you live through plants this firmly in a separate category for me. While I may find a more choice based and existentialist narrative like The Stanley Parable a bit more impactful, this game carries its tone of loss and sentimentality in a way that makes you want to follow the story exactly as it is presented. I don't think this game necessarily HAD to railroad you as hard as it does, I can't say that the story or experience suffered for it at all. On a cloudy day, with a drink poured and a longing for the company of people who used to mean the world, this game lets you appreciate what was and what could have been.\t\t\t\t\t
