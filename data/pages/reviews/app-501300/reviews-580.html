\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is short, but perfectly formed. While some disparagingly call this sort of game "walking simulators" (including the likes of Dear Esther, Everybody's Gone To The Rapture, and The Vanishing of Ethan Carter) , I prefer to think of them as "Story Games" because your involvement is in going around and uncovering what's happened (which is pretty fun). As it is there is a bit of interaction and a few 'mini-game' type things in here too though.

But that aside, it's the tale of a young woman who goes back to her (very odd) family home in the Pacific Northwest, and discovers the stories of how all the other members of her family met their demise. It's really well done - at times sad, at times funny, at times whimsical, and at other times tragic. The graphics are beautiful, the soundtrack is really good, and the characters are very well done - all in all it's well worth the purchase and a playthrough to experience it yourself!\t\t\t\t\t
