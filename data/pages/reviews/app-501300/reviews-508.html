\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a game I think that all people should play. I would recommend it to my mother if only she knew anything about playing games and she had the time. Heck, I would recommend it to my neighbor's dog if I could.

This game tops everything else in its genre, though I am left wondering if it has not developed a unique genre of its own. The entire experience was so good from the moment you step into the house to the moment the credits end, that it was nearly painful to play due to the unrelenting force of sheer brilliance.

Indeed, it was painful at times, but only due to the tragic, masterfully crafted narrative. However, the writers understood something that many similar games fail to recognize, and that is that fully dark, miserable, deary stories are uninspiring and unengaging, and must be accompanied by some light, (and in the case of Edith Finch), whimsy.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like the idea of exploring a mysterious secluded house full of secret passages, discovering the secrets of your familys alleged curse and unraveling one of the most well-presented stories in video game history? Then youll probably enjoy this game. Its a game driven entirely by story, which is usually a format that I quite dislike or even hate, and yet, I ended up loving What Remains of Edith Finch, which really surprised me.

This game has the most clever storytelling Ive ever seen, as well as excellent sound design, and wonderfully crafted environments with unbelievable amounts of detail. The game adds clever ways to interact with things that would normally just have been played out automatically, like opening a book, turning a knob, or opening a mailbox. This added a layer of interactivity to the presentation and made this exploratory game much more engaging.

This game also uses interactivity to convey ideas and metaphors that could not have been expressed through anything other than a video game. This is something I highly value in games driven only by story. I think its tedious to have to physically play out a story that could have been told more elegantly as a movie or book, especially when the story is the only aspect of the game that it's really pushing, so games like What Remains of Edith Finch are incredibly refreshing. A lot of meaning and understanding behind the stories presented here would have been lost if this story were told as anything other than a video game. Not every section does this but its inclusion made the experience very worthwhile.

The game subverted my expectations multiple times throughout my playthrough, and has several moments of shocking realization that made me stop dead in my tracks just to soak in what was happening.

Some parts of this game arent as great as others, but the overall experience was amazing in my opinion. This game can be finished in about two hours, which might seem short for the asking price, but I assure you this is quite possibly the pinnacle of video game storytelling, at least as far as Ive experienced.

If the price seems too steep, then wait for a sale but dont let this game pass you by if you think youll be interested.\t\t\t\t\t
