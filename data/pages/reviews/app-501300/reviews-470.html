\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThough many people will call it a "walking simulator", Edith Finch subverts the genre cliches by actually being interactive. Between dream segments and flashbacks, the monotony of just walking around a house is broken up by little novelty gameplay segments every couple of minutes. This is all in service of a pretty excellent story about the loss of loved ones, superstition, and persecution complexes.

+ all-star story which puts most video game writing to shame

+ a game which tells its story in a way only games could

+/- pretty much no replayability outside of seeing the story with a new perspective. No collectibles or secrets (which I actually liked)

+/- about 2-2.5 hours long. Can be played in one sitting, about the time span of a long movie (which I recommend)

- gives off kinda pretentious waif PNW hipster vibes at beginning, these go away eventually

- felt like the tiniest bit of content was missing from the final game
