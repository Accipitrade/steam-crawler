\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTotal gem of a game, if the BAFTA & awards galore don't convince you enough. Another walking-sim-esque experience that doesn't disappoint and involves the player in ways that I have never seen while playing similar games. Maybe Road 96, but that's a bit different, and newer :P
Nothing about the story really feels pretentious or long-winded, it's quite a short game overall but there is heaps packed in and a few cute (and kinda sad) achievements that may warrant a level replay, or even a full replay due to the satisfaction of just experiencing the game again (also to make sure it wasn't a fever dream..). I've got to mention, the hallucination/dream of Lewis' at the cannery is probably my favourite thing I've seen in a game. The part in the procession where you can see Molly the cat cheering you on had me in a right state. There was a tear or two shed. Just the fragility of his state of mind and seeing how letting go made him so happy and free, then his end. Abrupt, and wrapped up into another chapter of the family history.
I love this story, there are so many things I'd love to share, but you really need to play it yourself. It's not a wasted purchase. Ten out of ten my dudes!!!
