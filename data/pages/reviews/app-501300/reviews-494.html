\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch's title is a bit misleading as it's actually about the entire family, which is a somewhat interesting premise - going through the history of a family to see what happened to everyone.

And this game delivers on that premise by having each one be a bit more unique than "walking simulator" like so many other indie games with a serious story end up being.

You play through each member's story and find out how all of them died early, with each one being attributed to a "family curse".

But, this game has little gameplay and is all about the story - so if you aren't interested in the family's backstory, you're not gonna like this game at all. It's only a few hours long, and has solid writing & voice acting (as well as making you kinda question the "family curse").

I'd recommend it if you're interested in the story - but if you only have a casual interest (e.g. you've heard it mentioned in video essays), grab it on sale.

8/10 - very finicky controls, but the story, level design, writing, and voice acting can help you overlook that.

I love how the text kinda hovers in-world too - really neat effect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed the game. It has some interesting mechanics intertwined with the narrative layer and if you play as it's meant to be played it becomes very immersive. I was very curious what will happen next and that feeling lasted through the whole playthrough. It is very short but I feel like there's a lot of details in the environment that I could explore and learn more about the house and the Finch family. The parts where you don't get what the designers want you to do are rare, but when they occur, they can be a bit frustrating. For example, if you decide to leave the swing because you hear your mom calling you out - tough luck - you have to keep swinging. Still, if you do as you are supposed to, you're going to enjoy it.

To sum it up: if you like immersive, interacting story-telling games and don't mind a linear story, then you will definitely enjoy this game. A bit short for the $, but the overall quality more then makes up for it, in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t2017 Is almost over, and what a year it’s been for us gamers! With so many great games coming, there’s been a lot I’ve missed. I’ve decided to play every game from this year I’ve been wanting to play, so let’s start with Edith Finch!

What Remains Of Edith Finch

I’m not the biggest fan of walking simulators, so I was pleasantly surprised with how much I liked this game. It is by far the best walking simulator I've played, and it is an experience I won’t forget anytime soon.

Story
You play as Edith Finch, the last Finch alive, exploring her old family house. The Finch family has a curse, which makes almost all family members die at a young age. The game is a collection of short stories where you get to relive the last minutes of each family members life. Every family member has an unique and interesting story to tell, and discovering what happened to them is the real meat of this game.

Gameplay
One of the reasons I like this game better than other walking sims, is that it isn’t just walking. When You read the stories your family has left behind, you get to play a little minigame. They aren’t really minigames, but they aren’t walking either. Some examples: there’s one where you ride a swing, and one were you take pictures. They aren’t anything special on their own, but it stops the game from getting repetitive, and I quite enjoyed them.

Verdict
Edith Finch is a great game. The story is the most important part of the game, so if you only want gameplay in your games, you won’t enjoy it. for the rest of us, I can happily recommend this game.

8/10

For anyone who is turned of by it’s length, I can say that this is some of the best two hours of a game I can think of.
