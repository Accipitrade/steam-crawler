\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA short, tight and emotionally charged game that really elevates what “walking simulators” can be. Having unique interactive elements that all complement and elevate the narrative is just the thing the genre needs to stop it from being a museum exhibit or a visual novel.

What Remains of Edith Finch is a series of vignettes telling the tale of the Finch family. I found it to be deeply engrossing and something of an emotional gut punch. Maybe its due to me having kids in my life now, but there were parts that left me a devastated emotional wreck. Few games actually make me cry to the point where I can’t stop the tears, but boy did this game push all the right buttons.

Some of the vignettes rang less true or had less of an impact, but on the whole the game is beautifully crafted and wonderfully told.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeautifully narrated story about a peculiar family with a tragic history. Though most of the gameplay consits of walking between cues of narration the game also provides some variation in gameplay, introducing different elements and tasks that keeps things interesting.

Most of the stories are consistently good (the long-winded story about Lewis being the only one that was plain boring) and in it's genre, it's easily on par with Firewatch. It does however feel cheap in some regards; For instance, multiple times throughout the story a deer appears. This is fine, however, they've paired the deer with a sampled sound of a horse walking on gravel. A small thing that gives the impression of a €1 shovel-ware game.

Another problem is the build-up to some mysteries, some questions are asked and hinted about that the game just drops and never picks up again. This makes it feel somewhat incomplete, even though the excuse could be made that the game is driven by narration in a book and that the reader doesn't have all these answers it's not presented in that way. It feels rushed in this aspect.

I loved the story and most of the gameplay and would absolutetely recommend this to anyone who liked Firewatch, just not at €20. Buy it at €10 at most.\t\t\t\t\t
