\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can’t capture the sense of wonder this game creates. It is such an imaginative and playful, yet dark, adventure that might trigger an existential crisis if you’re easily swayed into having those. There’s one particular point where I was experiencing such childlike joy that I knew in my heart something terrible was about to happen (and sure enough it did). Each character’s story unfolds in stylistically unique ways that will leave you captivated after the short 3 - 4 hours of play. I 100% think it is worth owning and experiencing despite the relatively short gameplay. At 3 hours, it may be tempting to simply watch a play-through, but I think something essential is lost if you become just an observer. As a player, you become immersed in the lore of this family, exploring the quirky architecture to bypass sealed doorways. Even the Finch house has character.

I am forever searching for another game that might capture my heart the way What Remains of Edith Finch did.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game, whilst excecuted perfectly. is not your traditional kind of game, and therefore not for everybody.
I would say this is the most "movie-like" game that I have ever played, in so far as you are there along for the ride and have very little input. You are there to push the buttons to drive the story onwards and no thought or dexterity is required. It is also quite short (sub three hours) and with not all that much re-playability.
With that out the way, I can say that it is a wonderfully bizarre world you are pulled into. With intrigue and mystery, and some brilliantly designed locations and some ingenously odd gameplay mechanics.
All in all, I enjoyed this little wild ride and its worth picking up in a sale if you want an interesting evening playing a beautifully constructed game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a really good walking simulator but 90% of the game feels like a straight path. You don't really get to explore, and you can't even backtrack past certain spots. The set design is very purposeful and rich, but lacking in important details-- for instance, almost all of the books lying around are copies of the same dozen or so books, and the only bathrooms in the house are behind sealed doors. I cannot imagine anyone actually living there. It's also very short. Those things are enough to not recommend it at full price, regardless of how polished the ride is. If it's on sale for over half off (like it was when I bought it) then maybe.

Further detail:
The overall story is... actually kindof boring once you get it all together. The game starts off with the weirdest stuff to make you think something strange is happening but that never pays off. It's actually almost entirely mundane stories about how people died, embellished through presentation to seem magical. Sometimes the story you're interacting with is outright fabrication compared to the facts of how the person died.
The presentation is definitely top notch though. Each story has its own "gameplay theme" and special control scheme to go along with it, and the controls are intuitive for the most part. The visual design is good, so is the sound design. I felt that the game leaned too heavily into its presentation, however. That's really the only reason to play this game.
"Game". Yes, this is a walking simulator. There's no failure state, anywhere. There are no possible setbacks. Thankfully, the game doesn't imply that any exist. No choices (when you can make them at all) are "wrong" in any way. I guess that's fine, for what it's intended to be.\t\t\t\t\t
