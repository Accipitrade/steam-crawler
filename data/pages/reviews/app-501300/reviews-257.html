\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an interactive story. Mostly a walking simulator, with some interaction from the player. The interaction is not used as a skill check for the player, but as a way to immerse the player into the story.

The controls are basic, and if you have played video games before, they come naturally: WASD, mouse, and Escape are all you need. That being said, they don't explain the controls, so if you're unfamiliar, or haven't played many video games, it can take a small bit to understand how to interact.

The telling and display of the story within the world is very beautiful and captivating. The story itself was interesting all the way through to the end, as you slowly reveal more and more of the history of the family.

As a video game, there is little variation in the play through. As a story to tell, it is very captivating and memorable, like a high quality movie.
I recommend experiencing this story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will be honest this is an exremely overhyped game. I have avoided it for a while as I was never much into walking simulators. I recently played the Vanishing of Ethan Carter and thought that was a really cool game and I want to find more like it.
This game popped up, I decided to buy it and I got to say I am regretting it.
The gameplay is not bad, it has a lot of style and interesting ideas I got to admit but the story itself is so nonsensical, simple and plain stupid it ruined the entire experience for me.
I'm not an expert but I thought these walking simulator games were supposed to have some sort of interesting story on behalf of not having much in terms of gameplay.
This one has a lot of promise at the start. Family mystery, wierd house with secret passages, that sort of thing, but the further you go the more you realise its just a story about a family of mentally ill people and idiots. Incredibly disappointing if you were looking for something with an engaging story.
This game is also very short. I finished it in 2h and I was not in a hurry.
The very narrow FOV does not help with the enjoyment of the game if, like me, you get a bit nauseous with small FOV settings. This setting can not be changed.
My advice, go play Gone Home and give this one a pass.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf there's one game i consistently suggest to others, it's this one.

Pros: Story. Face it, it's what this game centers around. Sure some of the gameplay sequences are innovative (fish factory), but without the depth of story and it's ability to pull you in and keep you there, none of that would matter.

Music. I loved the soundtrack for this game. Despite it being on a loop i never notice, or tire, of the background ambiance and mood set by the score.

Graphics. It's a beautiful game despite it's age and able to be run on fairly low end machines by todays standards. A few of the movement mechanics can feel clunky but nothing that will pull your attention away from what's happening.

Cons: Playtime. The length of this game can feel a little underwhelming. Think of it as a highly interactive movie.

............ That's really my only critique. I love this game so much i repurchased it for ps4 when my pc was down. I've played it about 4 - 5 times on steam and about the same amount on ps4. I love it and i await giant sparrows next game with baited breath!

9.5/10

p.s. If you can catch "The unfinished swan" on sale or part of a package, pick it up. Another fun little title with a bittersweet story.

Could be that I've fallen for the hype of critical acclaim, but I found the experience overall underwhelming. The visuals are fine, and are implemented well. The voice acting is also good, passable. The plot is fanciful, but it's not the typical three act setup. It's just a story.

To get to the heart of the matter - the "Story Rich" tag really rubs me the wrong way. Yes, this walking simulator does have a story, but that is the fundamental difference between a walking simulator and a... tech demo.

I love this genre of games, but that doesn't mean I don't expect more from it.\t\t\t\t\t
