\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVideo games provide such a unique way of drawing a person in. Unlike books, TV, or movies, playing a video game provides that interactivity that pulls a person so much deeper into the story. It allows the player to connect with that story in a way that a movies or books simply cannot match.

Never in my life have I played a game and been reduced to a blubbering mess. This is far and away the most touching, emotional game I have ever played, and I applaud it everyone who worked on it. People still to this day do not think of video games as an art form. People like that NEED to play this game. What Remains of Edith Finch is without a doubt the best story I have ever experienced. And not just video games, but in ANY form of media. I cannot recommend this game enough! Play this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a COOL game. It's so simple in 1st person, but the graphics and game play are stunning. Some of the secrets are challenging, and some are very easy. The developers made a very simple device to keep you moving and knowing which direction to go and that's the words on the screen. The game mechanics are easy enough to figure out on the fly, too, which was great.
While I wish you could interact with a bit more stuff (I would have loved a flashlight at some points - they're scattered all over, but you can't use them), the lighting is actually enough to see by... mostly. But that lends to the overall creepiness, too, and there is plenty of creepy stuff, let me tell you. The most creepy are the portraits of everyone who's dead, including the room of dead birds.
Overall, this is an excellent game and I will definitely be playing more of it, since I haven't finished yet!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm 43yo. I spent dozens of thousands of hours playing videogames of all genre and generations. If you look at my reviews, you'll need I leave 85 to 95% thumbs down, that's how picky I am. This one, this game, this gem, gets a MASSIVE THUMB UP.

TLDR If you liked "the vanishing of ethan carter", "|the beginner's guide", "firewatch", "kona", "the stanley parable", "dear esther", "gone home" or "everyone's gone to the raputre", dont' read further, and go buy this NOW.

More details: It's Stunning from end to end. The game is _nothing_ like it appears to be. At times, I was wondering "how are they going to pull that concept off technically?" "how can they possibly transform this concept in a playable interface?" and yet, here it was. The theoretically impossible made into a playable, enjoyable, intuitive game with ONE control button and the directional keys.

"What remains of Edith Finch" is now the standard to beat in the 'walking simulator' (an amusing , tongue in cheek descriptor of course). It's a game that will stay with me for a long time, it's an 10/10, it's pure gaming bliss, genuinely refreshing, and conceptually radical. It's what video games should be. Kudos to the team who executed perfectly on this marvel. I will make sure to check out all their other games.

PS: is it perfect? no. There is one 'mini game' for which the interaction is rather poor. The story doens't end with any answer, and begs for a sequel. There is certain pathways you can take where you don't get to see 100% of the story the first time around (compensated by A GENIUS mechanism where you can explore these loose ends at the very end of the game). So, yes, there is room to grow for the genre, and that's a good thing.\t\t\t\t\t
