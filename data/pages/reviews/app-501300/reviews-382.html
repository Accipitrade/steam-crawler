\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"What Remains of Edith Finch" is one of these games that left me with a huge impression but also with a pit in my stomach. It's hard to describe the game with words, but I hope my review is going to help you understand why this game is probaby the best 'walking simulator' out there.

Story
Edith Finch is the last of her family left alive - a result of what seems to be a curse haunting her family. After six years of absence and 22 weeks of pregnancy, she returns to her old house in order to find some explanation of why she's the last of her kind. As we arrive at the extraordinary Finch residence, we slowly reveal the tales of the past: in various short stories, we discover the unique fates of each Finch family member. Or more specifally: how they lost their lives.
Characters
We originally play as Edith Finch's son, as he is reading his mother's notes. Later in the game, we find out that Edith had left these notes in case she didn't survive the childbirth. But for the majority of time, we play as Edith herself, walking around her childhood home. From time to time, we switch to other members of the Finch family to experience their stories first hand, which gives the game a very unique touch, because we can actually feel and see the world as they see it. This is all accompanied with commentaries from various people who had a special bond to the affected character.
What I really appreciated about Edith in the game were the often very self-reflective and melancholic comments she made while going through her past. Without being over-dramatic or over-detailed, she gives us short insights into her mind's eye, her emotions and feelings, so that the player can easily empathise with her.
Visual style and music
Rarely have I seen a game with such a lovely and overwhelming art and sound design. Just the obscure and abstract house design - with all its secret passageways - is very memorable. Every person's room has a unique theme and gives a brilliant view on what the affected character was like. The music intonates the rather gloomy and melancholic atmosphere while the voice acting compliments the narrative. The audio was of a high quality and the voices sounded soothing and authentic.
Conclusion
"What Remains of Edith Finch" is an outstanding indie-adventure. Even for a walking simulator - renowned for their story richness - this game came as a huge surprise. The interplay of sound, visuals and narrative make this game stick out of the huge game masses we see everyday. In such volatile and fast paced times - when one can easily get the impression that nothing lasts - games like this give hope that some things are truly timeless and everlasting. More than that, it's a good example of the fact that games should be ackknowledged as another form of art just like literature, painting or acting. The only shortcoming that I want to bring up is the brevity and the price. 20 quid is not cheap for poor students like me and also a bit much if you consider the length (It took me a bit more than two hours to finish it). Despite that, I don't see a reason why a true gamer-soul shouldn't try this game at least once.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn’t know what to expect when I started playing this game, but it has been awesome so far. Gameplaywise, it is both fun and easy to play; the controls are intuitively interactive, which helps to make you feel part of your surroundings. Expect no tedious tutorials or onscreen prompts, even in the action scenes. Storytelling is also top notch: believable voice acting, paired with awesome text display styles. I loved the fact that there’s no need to open menus and read diaries and stuff like that, since everything that’s being narrated is constantly unfolding just in front of you. The transitions between scenes are done greatly and keep you engaged at all times, and the pacing of the game is really up to the player. The stories are engaging and emotionally appealing, having different styles but all leaving you feeling something. Overall, It’s more an experience than a game per se. I’m playing it with my girlfriend, you should do it too!\t\t\t\t\t

Now, calling this a game sounds too superficial. Sure, the player has some control over the interaction but make no mistake, this is no normal game. And for me, it was more than an experience.

Like a great book or a great movie, this experience transcended my expectations, my perceptions, and transformed me like only true art can. TOUCHED...deeply touched was the feeling I left with. Open your heart, mind and soul to this one...and you will not regret it.

While the playtime skews on the short side (even novice gamers can easily navigate the complete story in one sitting), the beauty is that it pulls you in so that you don't want to stop until it's over. Giant Sparrow has, quite literally, created a masterpiece. A masterpiece that can be enjoyed by gamers and non-gamers alike. I can't wait to see what they come up with next.\t\t\t\t\t
