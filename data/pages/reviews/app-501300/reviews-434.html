\r\n\t\t\t\t\t'What Remains of Edith Finch' is a eerie storybased mystery that weaves snippets of the Finch ansectrial home together through family shrines, like a piece of a larger puzzle. The texturized details and first person POV of Edith Finch allows the player to experience an openworld feel, but on a single path of discovery that use twists-and-turns to keep the heart of this short two (to three) hour game pumping.

Where the story gives this game pulse, the setting of the Orca Islands in the Pacific Northwest showcases the mood; that of dramatic irony, a family curse, and theme of an everpresent death with whimsical interpretation. The Finch Home holds many secrets and resembles that of the uncanny Weasley house, but with spook-taculiar hidden passages, peeping eye holes, and quality use of narrative interactive text and voiceover that feeds into the overall tone and mystery.

Without any spoilers, the progression of the story allows the player to feed on the unexpected. Although, I wouldn't categorize it as horror, it has a dark overature and otherworldly quality, like that of a Neil Gaiman story, a less gorey Poe, or even an auteurist Wes Anderson kitchy feel. The story design and structure, along with the voice acting, musical score, various types of narration, and symplistic game mechanics gives a taste of storytelling in a crafty way.

Pros.
The use of gameplay for a unique storytelling experince along with the beauty of the game.
There were so many pros, that of which I have already detailed above, but also the strong emotion that went into this game definatley shows in every crevase.

Cons.
There were a few times when the game mechaincs were a bit glitchy. Without being too spoilery, the simple ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ sometimes detracted from the various intricate storytelling devices by not working as expected.
Also there is sometimes an illusion of 'openworld,' or various paths to the story, but it really is very linear; and those 'choices' were actually limiting by not giving much variation to the outcome of the story.

Overall.
I liked this game, it far exceed my expectations. The flow of the story and reveals made it thoughtprovoking and created a unique space for the imagination and characters to thrive. Althought the game mechaincs needed some work, the use of storytelling (and methods this game uses to achive this) gave an ambiance that allows the player to become emersed into the world. Despite how short of a game it is, the quality is worth the cost,time and money-wise.
It plays like a very advanced visual novel, allowing the player to fold into it like a dreamscape and unravel the secrets of Edith Finch's family in layers, that leaves a very rich impression on many levels.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished the game and it hit me like a truck. As someone who thoroughly enjoys games with a rich story, this one did not disappoint. Make sure you have about two to three hours of uninterrupted time before playing this because you aren’t going to want to stop mid way.

The basic premise of the game is:
You are the last remaining member of the Finch bloodline and after your mother dies, she leaves you a key to the families house. Once you return, you go through many areas of the house you weren’t allowed to enter as a child and learn about your families history.

Note about the ending:
This game has a ending that is similar to the way Firewatch handled their ending. As a result, it leaves some people disappointed but I personally love it when a game does this since it makes everything feel more real and in tune with reality

A few other things to keep in mind is:
  • The game is a walking simulator but unlike Firewatch, the path is very clear. There is also things constantly happening to keep you engaged.
  • The game is very short. I was able to finish it in a little over 2 hours. If money is a concern, I would recommend waiting for a sale ($9.99 when I purchased it). Despite the short time and price, I think the story and message the game leaves you with was well worth it.
