\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really struggled with what I was going to rate this one.

It's a solid game. The story is decently interesting, the graphics are gorgeous, and the mechanics are more engaging than your typical walking simulator. I really liked how well the house was set up because I would intuitively move towards wherever I needed to be without even thinking about it. This showed really intelligent game design, and I appreciated it after playing many games that leave me to wonder aimlessly in search of wherever I'm supposed to be.

All that being said, good gosh, $20 is an insane asking price. I'm a strong advocate against the "$1, 1 hour" mentality, but it took less than an hour for me to play through everything. I bought this on sale for $13, and I still feel like I got gouged. It was a cool game, and I enjoyed what time I did spend in it, but the story didn't do anything super special to the point where I was left thinking about it afterwards. I enjoyed the story while I was in it, but it is, at its essence, "here's how each family member died with Edith's commentary." If the story had impacted me more, I'd be more forgiving of the insane length to price ratio, but while being enjoyable, the story didn't do anything special. The storytelling itself was special, but not the story. Because of this, I can't in any capacity justify the price, as much as I enjoyed the game.\t\t\t\t\t
