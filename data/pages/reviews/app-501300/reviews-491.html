\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is difficult for me to recommend this game. Even though I have just under two hours played, I can't bring myself to refund it. What Remains of Edith Finch kept me immersed in an exciting narrative filled with moments of wonder and sorrow at the fate of the Finch family. The visual detailing was excellent; the audio was fantastic and jumping between various game-play styles was enjoyable. The most lauded aspect of this game, the story, fell completely flat. It hints and teases you then never provides any satisfying answers. It's lessons concern self-fulfilling prophecies and not mystifying the past, yet it tries to remain a mystery. The two biggest mysteries remain unresolved. It's the opposite issue many had with the similarly styled game Firewatch. As I understand it, you have to play the studio's previous game to understand what happened one of the major characters, which I only learned through searching the web. Did I have fun with this game? Ultimately, I did.\t\t\t\t\t
