\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed the game, and it had a brilliant story and soundtrack revolving around this one girl, her family, and the mystery of the house she grew up in. The suspense was enough to keep me engaged.

But, I did have some issues with it.
1. It's short.
The game itself takes about maybe five to six hours max, even if you get all achievements. It costs 20 dollars at full price. I got it on sale for 7, but I like to take about 1 dollar per hour worth, and this game did not cut it.

2. Motion sickness.
This doesn't affect everyone, but like most walking simulators which are 3D-modeled and first-person POV, it can cause motion sickness. But, the game does have the ability for you to change mouse sensitivity to help with that a little bit.

3. Lack of controls.
This game doesn't tell you what controls to use. I get that figuring out what to do is part of the game, but it eventually evolved into me smashing random buttons hoping for something to work, because those buttons had worked in a previous part of the game before. It became kind of annoying.

4. Graphics.
This game has impressive, highly detailed graphics and objects, and fluid animation and character movement. But be prepared to set fire to your computer if you hope to enjoy them to the highest setting. My computer isn't great, and I still enjoyed it with less intense settings, so this one isn't really that big a deal if you have a decent setup.

Conclusion:
I think the biggest dealbreaker for me is that it's a bit too expensive for only a few hours of gameplay for a story you'll only experience once. If there was perhaps a bit more, or some reason for me to replay it, then that might change. Overall it's a good game with a great story and is the epitome of a walking simulator, so if you're willing to buy it then you'd probably enjoy it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is going to be the most positive negative review I have ever written. I also want to be quite clear: I did not dislike this game. But, the types of games I normally play, are not games like this.

When I first finished the game, I was left confused. I thought to myself, "That's it? Where's the closure? Where's the winning condition?" I thought about it for a little bit, and I realized the problem lay not in the game itself, but in what I consider to be the definition of a 'videogame.'

I grew up in the 90s, what I consider the Golden Age of PC gaming. This was the era of DOOM, Duke Nukem 3D, Unreal/UT, and Half-Life. This was also the era of adventure games like Myst, Full Throttle, and Monkey Island. Two different types of genres, but still very enjoyable games. Nowadays, a videogame can exist in many different genres at once; but, not all games can satisfy all audiences.

My point, however, is that this is okay. What you enjoy may not necessarily be something I enjoy, and vice-versa. But you enjoy it; that's really all that matters.

So why am I still not recommending this game?

Look, I'm not stupid; not every gamer-buddy of mine is going to be able to play a game like this and enjoy it. Not all my of my gamer-buddies will be able to allow this game to take them on the journey it wishes to show them. I could, and that's great. But, if I want this review to be helpful, I ought to be honest.

Will I continue to play games like this? Certainly.

But would I play it over DOOM? Nah.\t\t\t\t\t
