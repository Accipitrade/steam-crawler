A masterclass in level design

What can be said about this game that has not been said before?

To put it bluntly, there is no game like it on the market- not only does it pull at the heartstrings (Especially at the end!) without being preachy, utilize strong voice acting and look stunning to boot, but what impressed me the most is how surprising it is at every turn.

When did games stop being this creative and fancyful, without the constraints of "realistic graphics" "physics" and "grittiness"?
The medium is so concerned with aping movies and one-upping the last next-gen title in the technical department that they forget key elements and what sets games apart from other media:

Level design and simulation.
I just love how the game changes up perspectives, scenery, characters or gameplay elements out of the blue.
When was the last time a game surprised you?

My advice: If you miss levels transforming after a button press a la Doom, buy this game.

Enough said.

10/10\t\t\t\t\t

The game looks attractive, but was so tedious to play. Quite a few of the stories about her family members were interesting, but the overarching story was just bland and the ending left me feeling "meh, I could see that one coming". If this game was free or significantly cheaper, I would be satisfied. However, I have played games with far more in-depth stories that are cheaper than this.

To compare games of a similar genre, Firewatch was far more interesting to play than this one. Sure, I'd argue that the game wasn't worth full price, but it was twice as long as this one and far more interesting. Then you have telltale games like The Walking Dead which offer 10+ hours of content which offer a far more detailed and believable story than this one.

I bought this game on half price, and I do not feel like I got my moneys worth.

To end on a positive note, I thought the artwork in the game was very nice. The voice acting was superb and some of the stories were very well done.\t\t\t\t\t

Anyway this game is an outstanding example why video game can be a media on par with the likes of movie and book. It allows a seamless transition between narratives which, coupled with moody music and timely voice over, is pretty great at presenting a story.

Praise aside, some of the mini stories, or chapters, whatever you call them, in the game feels a bit rushed or at least inconsistent. Several people just striaght up disappeared, which does seem rather lazy to me. But no matter this is a game I do not regret spending $10 on and would definitely recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

I thoroughly enjoyed exploring each of the precarious and eclectic rooms of the house's previous inhabitants, just as I enjoyed hearing their stories in the bizarre episodic gameplay and somber narration which accompanied each room.
