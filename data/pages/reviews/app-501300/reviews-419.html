\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch was a touching experience that will haunt me for a long time and I can only describe it as a cute astonishing game with an extremely emotional story. It’s fantastic how a depressing issue like death is delivered in such an emotional manner. The whole thing feels like someone reading you a fairytale with you being able to experience the events firsthand. On top of that, the game looks absolutely gorgeous and thanks to the unique design of the environments you feel the distinct characteristics of each former resident upon entering their room. Each of the stories is unique and so epically told that I actually burst into tears at a certain point of the narrative. All things considered, the game gets a 10 out of 10 in the presentation department.

Gameplay is relatively simple and consists typically of moving around and interacting with objects. What amazes me the most is how one can play the game without it actually showing a prompt as to which key should be pressed. The gameplay itself is fluid and seamless. Throughout playing the game, not once did I feel lost or wondering what I should do next. All in all, playing the game feels more like reading a book or watching a movie.

Finally, if you are looking for replay value or engaging gameplay, you will be disappointed; however, If "playing" a marvelous story is what you seek then don’t hesitate for a second and get the game. I absolutely enjoyed every second of the game. It should be pointed out that although the game itself is extremely enjoyable, the price is unjustified for its roughly two-hour playtime. So you might want to wait for a sale or get it in a bundle.\t\t\t\t\t
