\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCreative Narrative holds attention throughout


Overall
Returning to her childhood home, Edith Finch Jr explores the history of her family in the hopes of better understanding her own.

Narrative/Story
This is the core and strength of the game. As players learn about the other family members, the interactions are unique and memorable. Mixing wonder and delight with tragedy and pain, the various methods of storytelling and game mechanics make it easy for players to invest themselves in the experience.

Gameplay
While it is predominantly a story based game, no fail states, there game does a decent job chauffeuring the player through the hub of the house as well as the individual histories. There was a few times that I wasn't sure exactly what the game wanted me to do, but ultimately I wasn't stuck for very log which is important in allowing the player to stay in the world of the game.

Visuals
Pretty spot on in giving an immersive experience. It's not an action game, so few moving parts and limited interaction, but the game does have a nice, welcoming style.

Overall
What Remains of Edith Finch is as strong as a narrative given game that I have played, almost to the level of Brothers: A tale of two sons. While not a long experience (about 3 hours), it was a memorable one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as "walking simulator" style games go, What Remains of Edith Finch is top notch on quality. The graphics, attention to detail, music, and voice acting are all some of the best I've seen in this sort of game. And the story certainly gets its hooks in you, making you want to find out more about the world these characters inhabit.

On the other hand, this really is the sort of game that got this sort of game the title of "walking simulator"; It is very light on the gameplay, and there aren't a lot of secrets or puzzles to get caught up on. And with an already short story, it means the game goes really quick, and felt like there wasn't much of a reason to replay it. At 2 hours or so, the experience was like sitting down and watching a movie.

Ultimately, if you're up for that, and you don't mind "walking sim" style games, your enjoyment will boil down to how much you like the story. Like I said earlier, it certainly had some good hooks and suspense to it, but I also found it a bit melancholy and artsy to my tastes. But I didn't mind playing through to the end, even if it wasn't really my cup of tea, because the very fact it's suspenseful and short, not overstaying its welcome.

In the end, I would definitely recommend the game, with the above caveats. I would personally make sure you get it on sale though, since it's short and I don't see a lot of replay value; So it felt a lot like renting a movie for the evening. A pretty good movie, though, even if I wouldn't watch it more than once myself.\t\t\t\t\t
