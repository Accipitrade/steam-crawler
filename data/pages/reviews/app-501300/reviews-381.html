\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed through this over a late night and a morning. It bills itself as an adventure game, but it's really just a visual novel. It's fairly linear in how you progress through the story, but that's not a bad thing. This story is talked about as fantastical and the content does come through as such. Each story being played from the perspective of a deceased member of the Finch family, but told sometimes by a surviving family member really lends itself to creating a canvas to bend reality to the imagination of whomever is the source of the particular story you're playing through at the time. Most of the novellas have their own unique playstyle and theme, ranging from simple mechanics to well thought out ones. This only adds to the story because the gameplay for each story is a part of the story itself. It's a pretty impressive way to absorb a game and I'm really glad I picked it up. I'd love to talk more on how things play out and what I feel about the story, but I don't want to spoil anything. If you're into games that can be considered art and not just play, then give this one a go.\t\t\t\t\t
