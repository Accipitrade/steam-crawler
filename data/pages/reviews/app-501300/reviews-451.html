What Remains of Edith Finch Review


One thing i can truly say is:This game surprised me.The core gameplay is utterly different and strange,at the same time.You litteraly play a memory within a memory.Which is amazing.The ending was also unpredictable in a way because you would instantly expect a vastly different one.And also,please don't compare or think that this is a similar game to Life is Strange.It is NOT the same.A million times better from LiS.Lastly,i bought the game during a sale.


MAIN REVIEW


Storyline
8/10
Characters
9/10
Graphics
9/10
Gameplay
10/10



Pros:

  • Calm-paced,interesting storyline with a surprising ending.
  • Very interesting,captivating characters,each one with their own elements,goals and different perspectives about life.
  • Average Graphics,making this game an easy grab for low/medium-end pc's that will surely run it.
  • The Gameplay is ultra simplistic,requiring only the space and the right mouse button/mouse movement only and can support controller gameplay as well.
  • Has only 9 achievements and steam trading cards.
  • This game doesn't have a main soundtrack/ost,which suits it perfectly.After all,you really need to be paying attentionto the protagonist's narrative and feel the characters emotions/understand the events and not get distracted by the music.


Cons:

  • A rather dissapointing ending,in my opinion.The game shows you so many tragedies/sorrrow events that you would expect a happy/neutral ending.Instead,you get something else that isn't explained in a matter of sorts.
  • I am now getting an error when exiting,under 10 hours of gametime,nothing game-breaking just annoying.
  • This game is a bit expensive for a not-well known game.But it offers a lot to justify the given price.


Final Verdict


In Conclusion,always buy the game for yourself first and play it for a while if you can.Interact with the game and if you don't seem to cope with its offerings,you can submit a request ticket,on condition that you won't have passed the 2-hour gametime.Overall,a good storytelling game,offering you a different perspective about somebody else's life.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this game because I loved Hellblade so much and someone recommended I should pick up WRoEF.

They were quite right, Edith Finch is a great game and Im glad I played it, but it felt a bit underwhelming aswell. I hoped for something as emotionally exhausting as Hellblade, but Finch didnt get close. I wish I had played Finch before I played Hellblade, because then my expectations wouldve lower and I mightve been able to appretiate it more.

But I can totally recommend WRoEF. The game has some very interesting concepts about how to tell stories, and some of the gameplay elements are fantastic. Its a 2 1/2 hour interactive movie, and thats by no means a bad thing.
I recommend WRoEF for a relaxed evening where you dont feel like playing something tiring and you dont want to watch a movie but do something a bit more interactive.\t\t\t\t\t
