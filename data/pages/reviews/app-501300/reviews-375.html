\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had this game on my wishlist forever as it had overwhelmingly positive ratings and a high critic rating. The description sounded interesting and the graphics looked amazing. That is about all that was great, the images for sure are incredible. The story however will be great for those who want to feel depressed and sad and want to focus on death. Not very uplifting to play a walking simulator about children dying. The ending was a complete disappointment also as you really don't know how it ends, I had to search it after. Bought this on a daily deal for $8 and wish I had those $8 back. Would have been even more upset if I had paid full price as taking my time playing still only took 3 hours to complete. There is zero to no replayability unless you are an achievement hunter which wouldn't even been worth it. I rarely write reviews but this one needed one. I cannot recommend this "game", having great images does not make up for the disappointing and depressing hours wasted.\t\t\t\t\t

The Malted Review

Edith Finch is a walking simulator through-and-through. Meaning you'll be spending your time walking, exploring and sucking in the world that the game presents to you.

The substance in Edith Finch is the intrigue of the various narratives, framed within an overarching plot centring around the rather unfortunate Finch family.

It's short, it's deep, and it's very much slow-paced.

If you're interested in an experience along those lines, this is one of the best the industry has to offer.

If you're looking for engaging mechanical gameplay, stay well away.

If you want to get a deeper taste for What Remains of Edith FInch, check out my 2019 video review below.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like adventure games and/or walking simulators you can stop reading this and just go play What Remains of Edith Finch. Seriously. It's some of the best those genres have to offer.

While the premise is essentially Gone Home - you, the player character, go back to your childhood home and experience the story by walking around and remembering and discovering things - the execution feels closer to The Vanishing of Ethan Carter (though with, admittedly, fewer frustrating puzzles). There's a significant amount of content to explore here, with phases of the adventure marked by getting the backstory on each family member, in turn.

The word for this game, if I had to only pick one, is imagination. And that's a huge compliment. The sense of wonder and adventure you get just from walking around an old house is incredible. The gameplay is far more varied than it first appears.

The story is moving, and appropriately funny or dark or poignant as the situation merits. I really don't want to go into too many details because experiencing this game without spoilers is by far the best way. I was...awed, and surprised at points. Few games have the audacity to pull that off.

What Remains of Edith Finch is the game equivalent of an indie movie. A good one. Go experience it for yourself.\t\t\t\t\t
