\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch made me feel like crying in the first five minutes of play. The abandoned Finch house reminded me all too much of places I've left behind. The whole game was like that. I could barely follow some of the narrative for all the uncanny reminders buzzing through my own mind. Reminders of a relationship to my past. And how sometimes our past weaves a spell over the future too.

2021 was an unthinkably difficult year for me. It felt nice to spend a couple of hours with What Remains of Edith Finch, even though I cried a few times. I'm glad I decided to try it.

There's probably plenty of stuff about the game I missed. Themes and parallels and images. But that's okay. Edith herself tells us, "Maybe if we lived forever, there'd be time to understand it all. As it is, we just have to open our eyes and appreciate how strange and brief this life is."\t\t\t\t\t
