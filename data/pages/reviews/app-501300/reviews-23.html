\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is probably a great… something. Obviously lots of effort and creativity went into it. But it is at war with itself and works neither as a story nor as a game.

Player's agency is generally pretty limited in walking simulators as they follow a predetermined story. The gaming elements do not really determine the outcome. So the question is whether they enhance the story (and vice versa) or the two go against each other. Here the interactive elements are various “minigames” with odd controls (beside the walking). It can take some time to figure out what is one supposed to do in the first place, but then it is just going through the motions. Even if it was not often actively distracting from the story, it would be still a repetitive and pointless chore.

What Remains of Edith Finch is an interesting attempt at combining storytelling with a videogame, but ultimately a failed one, demonstrating this is not the way to do it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a textbook example of how to do a "walking simulator" game right. In the game, you'll explore the Finch house and surrounding area where you'll engage with the stories of the various Finch family members and how they all came to their tragic end. You'll relive their memories as they are best remembered, in stylistic and imaginative recreations of the scenarios leading up to their deaths. Chase after a bird through trees as a cat, imagine a fantastical scenario of being royalty to get away from your factory job, play with bath toys as they conduct a ballroom dance, and so much more.

What Remains of Edith Finch makes fantastic use of environmental storytelling. All dialogue throughout the game is written out in subtitles on the environment as you see it, but in a way that feels natural and part of the environment itself. The Finch house, while labrynthine in natural, has a innate flow to it that you'll organically progress through while you encounter each new story. It is a captivating experience visually, aurally, mechanically, and while I wasn't the biggest fan of the ending, I would recommend this game to literally anybody. It is a worthwhile experience and truly a unique story.\t\t\t\t\t
