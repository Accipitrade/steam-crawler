\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't want to give too much away so I will keep it brief. "What Remains of Edith Finch" is a short, but fantastic game nonetheless. The story and characters are both well written and expertly expressed through great voice acting, storytelling, and visual rendering of both. The game immerses you in the fantastical yet tragic tale of the Finch family and tells each story with a unique twist that feels authentic to the characters. It takes difficult concepts and expresses them in a beautifully sad way that makes you care about the characters just before it takes them away. The emotional impact does not feel flat or undeserved however and the stories are about more than just death; much like in in real life, death is the certainty, but life is the surprise.

I have minor complaints with the mechanics at certain points, but the game does a fantastic job of pushing you forward and rewarding you for it. The pacing is excellent and it provides just enough mystery to keep you engaged but is not overtly vague. The world design is phenomenal as well, the house alone has so much character and life that though it stands abandoned, sealed, and empty - it feels like a home.

You can not only see how much love and care was put into this game, you can feel it. Excellent game, bring your tissues.\t\t\t\t\t
