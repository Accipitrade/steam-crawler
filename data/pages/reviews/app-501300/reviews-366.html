\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOur legacy is what we make it.

A melancholy walking sim. Sad game that has a lot of bleak moments, but brings up interesting notions of how we think of our lives as stories, how narratives shapw the way we act, and why we can let the belief that something is bad overwhelm all the good in our minds and lives. Not much in terms of gameplay, but very interesting presentation with subtitles appearing as part of the world. No blood or gore, no lengthy cutscenes, just you and a narrator going through memory lane.

10/10 would cry again\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTop notch voice acting, great score, the production values on this from a theatrical perspective are strong (no wonder, since Annapurna Productions - run by Megan Ellison, funded by her dad's, the Oracle founder's fortunes - is a major force in indie movie productions these days). Also sweet - the childhood photos of the team that are part of the end scroll. I image how much joy it must have been for proud mothers and fathers to dig deep into their family treasure troves to unearth some of those. As to story - meh. It often comes across as forced, is strictly linear, and it is - of course - quite short. I got through it in three hours. Which were enjoyable, but I've been touched much more by other entries into this kind of genre. Which means: wait for a sale, to get best value for your purchase.
