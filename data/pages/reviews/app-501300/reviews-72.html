\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is definitely the best walking sim I have ever played.

The art was amazing, the map design was terrific, the mechanics were fleshed-out, the story never gets boring, the sense of progression is always there, and the achievements can be completed with a touch of challenge. It also has all of the most appropriate components that a walking sim game could ever offer, and it delivers them extremely well.

Yes, the entire thing was short (3 hours for 10 bucks), but it was probably one of the most memorable and unique 3-hours walking experience I have ever played in a game.

I really recommend it. Please do try it out if you have a chance.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game. The direction of this game is amazing.

the game starts of pretty normal but it gets pretty dark pretty fast. It definitely isn't a feel good story. The premise of the game: You get to hear the tragedies of the Flinch family in the abandoned family home through letters left behind by them. It's a real tearjerker on some occasions, making you question how it could have come so far.

The artstyle gorgeous. The attention to detail really gets through. At the right times they change it a bit to fit the context, giving some insight into the worldview of the characters and the medium through which it is told.

Don't expect some complex mechanics though. It's basically a walking simulator with a good story. I suggest that, even if the path is pretty linear, you take your time to look at everything in the room.

Jesus Christ the stories of Lewis and Gregory are sad.
