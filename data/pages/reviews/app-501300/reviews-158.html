\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHIS REVIEW CONTAINS SPOILERS.

I got this game on a whim, and I'm so absurdly glad I did. The game mechanics are wonderful, especially as they change up as the story progresses. Even at the points where my awful hand-eye coordination got in the way of completing a puzzle in (anywhere close to) a timely manner, it never felt like the game was being frustrating on purpose.

The narrative is on a whole other level, and I mean that in the most complimentary sense possible. I'm normally not a huge fan of stories caked in so many layers of sadness and depression and grief, but that's not anywhere near all that this game is. For every horrifying or traumatic event the Finch family faces, there's still this absurd sense of hope shot through. Despite many of them believing that they're marked for an early death, they keep living their lives: finding love, raising children, enjoying the parts of life that they do have. That hope makes the sad moments all the sadder, but it makes the moments of joy all the brighter, too.

(It must be said: if your computer can't handle heavy graphics, this might not be the game for you. My mid-range gaming computer WHEEZED at some of the scenes, which was really the only negative experience I had throughout the game.)\t\t\t\t\t
