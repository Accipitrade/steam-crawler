What Remains of Edith Finch tells a story that will stick with you long after your first, second, or even third playthrough. I've played this game multiple times both on switch, and on steam, and every playthrough is better than the last due to how much time and effort went into cramming the house and sequences with tiny details so that you notice something new every single time you play it. The game has you going through your old house and playing through sequences that allow you to learn about your families past. These sequences are all unique and absolutely spectacular at what they look to achieve. The story telling in this game is spectacular and the unique use of words and subtitles throughout the game really help to elevate the experience. The soundtrack does a great job of setting the atmosphere as well but the main reason you play this game is for the story. The story that unfolds you throughout the game is one of pain, loss, and death. Parts of this game hit you like a ton of bricks. The more you explore the house, the more you will get out of the story, and multiple playthroughs only enhance the experience. If you love narratives and stories, I highly recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat remains of Edith Finch...

Even the name of this game gives you some ideas about philosophical theme of this masterpiece. What have left of once a big family? The main protagonist, Edith, is going to find it out.

The game itself is the best walking simulator i have ever played in my life (and i have played many games, trust me). It has wonderful plot, soundtrack and atmosphere. But the best thing in this game, for me, it's the graphical design. It just blows your mind when you see it.

The game can be separated on two parts: the first one is exploration of the big Finch's family house, and second is the re-living of family's member last moments of life (yes, all family members, expect Edith are dead, and it is not a spoiler). And this all is made wonderfully.

The main gameplay is walking which during memories of the Finch's family members changes on some special activity, connected to the memory. This greatly brings atmosphere, thoughts and drama of every family member's last moments.

I definitely recommend this game to everyone, who likes atmospheric games and who is ready to enjoy the story, drama and the philosophy of the game. It lengths for 2 hours, but believe me, this 2 hours will forever stay in your heart...\t\t\t\t\t
