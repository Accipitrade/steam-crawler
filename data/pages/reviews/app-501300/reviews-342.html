\r\n\t\t\t\t\tContent warnings, because even for a magical realism game about death, there are some deaths in this one that are a bit emotionally intense to have to play through! Deaths you will need to experience to play through this game include an infant's death due to parental negligence, several deaths of children, elder suicide using alcohol and medication, and the gruesome suicide of a young man who chooses to die because his real life can't live up to his fantasy live--which is a 'moral' I hate in stories, as someone with a mental illness and a vivid fantasy life. It's quite possible to stay alive with both of those things :| All of the sections are dealt with surreally and you don't ever *see* deaths in the game--they're usually suggested in beautiful, metaphorical ways. But still good to know what you're getting into.

Before playing this walking sim, I played my way through Gone Home, Tacoma, and Firewatch (in that order) and I'd still put Gone Home at at the top, followed by Tacoma, *then* followed by this one, with Firewatch at the bottom. While Edith Finch is absolutely lovely, in terms of design and aesthetics, it just...doesn't stick the landing, for me. It's a meditation on death and how we tell stories about our lives (and how those stories in turn *define* our lives, so we need to think hard about what stories we choose to tell...), but I feel like the game bit off more than it could chew and didn't end up doing those themes justice. Too many of the characters' stories and the story of the family overall are left shallowly explored and unfinished-feeling--which, you could argue, is the *point,* but still...

It reminds me of Firewatch, in that sense. Where somehow the story feeling unfinished and anticlimactic is meant to somehow *help* it have an impact on you. It didn't work for me in Firewatch and it doesn't work for me here, either.

Still worth playing, if the content warnings don't put you off, because the way it uses changing control schemes and mechanics to comment on the story and themes *is* amazing and worth experiencing. But I ended up it feeling very 'so, yep, that's it, then, okay' and I wouldn't go out of my way to recommend it to anyone. (Oh, the music is lovely, though. Even if you don't play the game, I *would* recommend the soundtrack!)\t\t\t\t\t
