\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVerdict: Get if you enjoy subtly told (but beautifully presented) stories and are good at observing your surroundings.

What Remains of Edith Finch is definitely not meant for everyone. It's less of a game and more of an interactive movie and walking simulator. The story unfolds through exploration and by looking at your surroundings. The entire game is told through a series of stories which make up 1 big story. It's more show and less tell but some people do enjoy a more indirect approach instead of direct spoon-feeding. I'm rather slow with these things but thankfully I played with my sister who's faster at picking up subtle hints. With that said, the stories are all very good and I absolutely love how they are presented. Seriously, the way the game tells the stories are amazing. It might seem slow at the start but keep going and you'll find yourself wanting to know more. There will be at least 1 or 2 stories which you'll find memorable.

It's a rather short experience, I myself finished the entire thing in a little more than 3 hours with all achievements. You'll most likely be able to complete the game in one sitting. Probably best played on a nice evening when you've got some free time and can fully focus on the game. Then you can think about it later. I must say it holds little to no replayability as when you've finished figuring out the story there's really nothing much left to do. Perhaps you can come back to it after a long time and play again just to enjoy the experience.

Music was good for what it was meant for with a few memorable tunes but that's about it. I played with a controller so I'm not sure how well it works with a keyboard and mouse though there are a few parts I noticed would be much easier with a controller. Nothing major though, keyboard and mouse should play just fine.

Overall a solid 8/10. Story game with stunning presentation. I recommend getting it on sale of you're really hesitant.\t\t\t\t\t

This has a feel of many good Story-driven walking simulators like Path to Thalamus, but without all those annoying puzzles, Virginia without the uncertainty (ergo play thru a 2nd time to understand it), and Dear Esther without the constant gloom. And of course the 1st Life is Strange but being only 1st person and nil interactions. And slight joy ride of motion sickness some 1PS games give.
I was half expecting the game to be a creepy and spook sensation suspense game. But in turn, turned out to be a Goonies adventure of secret paths, secret stories, and secret lives coming to light. Even if there is a curse on the family... I still can't stop laughing at the karma of the Stag.
Sure there is a mystery surrounding the family, and you never quite find out exactly what the grandmother is trying to tell but you can't stop thinking that there is something supernatural about the house. Kind of like the 80's trilogy House where things supernatural happen. Even though they try to escape it, it always brings them back to their deaths, or takes their souls from afar.
It is as if as the house is growing, it provides the fantasy of the victim of how they wish to die, so they die "cough" vanish in peace. The house feeds on their souls... like a McDonalds drivethrough hungry for nuggets with Mulan Szechuan sauce

Anyway I was slightly dissappointed at the ending as to how the boy came to be and the mother not around. Did she die giving birth? If so, who raise her son, and how did he came into the possession of the house and her journal...
The theme music is nice, and might consider buying it on special for a few dollars.\t\t\t\t\t
