\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSPOILERS in this review (at the end)

The good: Beautiful scenery, graphics, and story. I was engrossed and actually quit playing about five minutes in so that I could start again when I could play in one sitting. The only other game, to date, that I felt that way about was Firewatch (and that was about double the length of this one).

I loved the story and the interactive bits here and there. Yes, you walked quite slowly everywhere, but it gave you a chance to really take in the visuals and think about the story as it unfolded before you. You did not have to do any backtracking, which made the snail's pace tolerable.

The bad: Story was much too short. Each snippet made you want just a bit more detail. I wanted to know just a little bit more of each person's story and their fate. It was like a half-developed polaroid, not quite enough to really grasp.

The heartbreaking SPOILERS: There's no real ending. All those loose threads are left that way. You never find out what's in Edie's journal or the story of the house. You don't find out what *really* happened to some of the family, including Edith herself. It totally leaves you hanging. I was really disappointed that there was no resolution to all the mysteries I'd spent three hours exploring.

Overall: I am glad I played this game. It was very interesting and I'd like to support developers who make story-driven games instead of mindless shooters (although those have a place too). I like this game and I recommend it, inspite of the lack of proper ending.\t\t\t\t\t

  • put me on edge,
  • surprised me,
  • reminded me of someone I love,
  • gave me childhood nostalgia,
  • acknowledged one of my greatest fears,
  • made me grateful for what I have,
  • displayed the consequences of chasing dreams,
  • question the dangers of fame and popularity,
  • despair on the frailty of life,
  • come to terms with not having all the answers,
  • think of the impact I have on others,
  • caused my to mentally fortify myself to a point where I was firmly confident in continuing,

  • and many more that I have trouble even explaining

Joseph Anderson revealed my mistake in one of his many incredible long-form reviews. I HIGHLY recommend playing the game before watching: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6bMn4CoyUkM

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as walking simulator games go, this is probably up there with the best of them. The rich but morbid story is told in a very stylish way. It plays out a bit like an interactive short novel more than a video game and the more of the Finch family history you uncover, the more immersed in the secluded world that Giant Sparrow has created you'll be. Some parts are quite emotional too and provoked unexpected responses from me which is to the game's credit.

The character stories are all told in different ways, keeping the game fresh and successfully avoiding excessive repetition. Barbara Finch's story is of particular note! Further to this, if you know your horror movies, this game is filled with cool references/easter eggs to many of them that you should spot. This Island Earth, anyone?

It won't take you too long to finish the game even with thorough exploration and because of this, I'd recommend that you try to play through to the end in one sitting if you're able to. Only up to about 3 - 3.5 hours should cover it. This way, you will get the best experience that the game has to offer in my opinion. For £14.99 though (I'm in the UK) that is a lot to ask for for such a short game and is not a fair price. I got it in a Steam sale and you should too if you're thinking of buying.

I enjoy walking simulator games because of the stories they tell and Edith Finch's story is one you should not miss out on if you love this genre too. But the game really is only for fans of the genre. If you're not big on them, I doubt you'll get much enjoyment from it at all.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is difficult to select the "Not Recommend" button for this game. It is a beautiful game with gorgeous graphics, a nice atmosphere, and an eclectic story. I know the designers and writers must have put their heart and soul into the development of this "game," but I have to say that the end credits were more engaging and interesting to me than the game itself. I was disappointed in "What Remains of Edith Finch." I'm not sure what I expected, I've played games like "Firewatch" and "Life is Strange," and found them to be much more interactive and "fun." I'm not a huge fan of walking simulators, but as an exploration junkie who doesn't like FPS or combat games, I find my options are sadly lacking. The hype about this game was enormous, and I was stoked, but it really, really fell short. I found myself eager to be done with it. I hate that feeling with a game. The "interactive" bits were minimal, tedious when they did occur, and outright irritating. The environment would have been great fun to explore, to feel more a part of the place, but this was simply a not-that-interesting-tale told to me while I pretty much just sat back and watched. Seriously, I hate to give it such a big thumbs down, but by the time I finally got to a point in the "game" that felt like "ok, now we're getting somewhere," the game stopped there, didn't allow me any interaction, and ended without answering the things it made me most curious about. I sense the developeers wanted to do exactly that for artistic purposes, but it felt cheap and intentionally annoying. Glad many liked it. I, however, am glad it's over.\t\t\t\t\t
