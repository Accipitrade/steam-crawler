\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is what walking simulators all want to be: simple, casual mechanics with an impact. You are on a singular path throughout Edith's childhood home, guided by her heart-felt commentary. You both are just trying to come to terms with and understand the sheer mountain of tragedy she has to scale. You are on this journey together. This is one of the things the game does incredibly well. You connect with each and every member of the family as you explore their deaths. Experiencing these intimate, last moments gives a reality and a finality to each of their short lives that you can't help but cry for.

If you ever need to pause and reflect on mortality, family, human connection, or what it means to live with grief, Edith will not disappoint.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is mostly a 2 hour long movie with user interaction but it's a good movie!
It most definitely enjoyable and the stories really drags you along (in the fish part I was completely lost in the chopping rhythm).
The music (even though I usually don't pay that much attention to it) was pretty accurate for the whole mood throughout the game.
Don't bother for the achievements too, if you know where you get them you're able to snatch em in one run.
At the end of the game I felt a sense of relief, not that it was over but because I reached the end of a beautiful story.
Would I recommend this game? if it's on sale go for it, otherwise just watch someone play it on Youtube (preferably without too much commentary).\t\t\t\t\t
