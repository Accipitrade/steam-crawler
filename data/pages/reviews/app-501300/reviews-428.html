\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a Capital-W Walking Simulator, so bear that in mind. It's also a pretty strange one.

It's the story of the last member of a cursed family going back to an incredibly haunted house to look, what one imagines, are answer. But she doesn't seem too terribly interested in trying to unravel the curse or find explanations, more content to just read bizzare and often horrific last testaments from her family line, all killed in freak accidents or mysterious circumstances.

There are no answers in Edith Finch, and depending on how much credit you want to give the game that's either it's theme or a budgetary constraint. In some cases it works incredibly well: a picture book, a door, and absolutely no explanation is easily the best 'scare' in the game; drawing it's horror from silence and a complete lack of commentary or even investigation. In some cases it works less well; the ending of the game comes across as more of a shrug and a "Well, whatever then" then an actual conclusion.

Edith Finch is a compotent game, but it's also a very narrow one. It's a great Walking Simulator, as these things go, but it's very much one of them. If you're into it, you're into it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I added up the time I was actually playing a game during this I'd probably come to around 20 minutes. Some people have gotten a lot of meaning out of this game, and that's cool, and when you look at pictures and videos of the game it looks pretty cool too, but when you actually play this game (and probably finish it in the same sitting) you are just dragged around by a lifeless protagonist walking through a path watching brief, trivial stories that anyone could have thought up. The presentation of these stories are sometimes really interesting and creative, and other stories are explained and explored in about 5 minutes, through a few hastily-made photos.

They left a lot of room for a great soundtrack, but the soundtrack didn't take up much room at all and in my opinion was unimpressive.

The visuals were pretty cool and made the game feel very atmospheric, but if a game has only great visuals going for it than you might as well watch a youtube video.

I'm not sure what you'd say if you had to summarize the story of this game. It tells the story of Edith's families deaths (some of the stories were simply "he vanished" and "that was the last time I saw her."), but she isn't really solving anything or finding anything new. Most of everything we learned she already knew about, and anyone could find by just asking for a public death record. Anyway, the game enters by cutting to the chase by giving us a lot of information and accounts but just leaves us with them, while the player doesn't know what to do with them. I'm saying it all doesn't amount to anything in the end. You might as well watch two episodes of Unsolved Mysteries and save yourselves 20$.

20$ is way too much money for this game.\t\t\t\t\t
