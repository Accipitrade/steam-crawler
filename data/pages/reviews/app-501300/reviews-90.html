\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"An emotional rollercoaster of thrills & sorrow."

I know I'm a little late to this classic. Before I begin, I won't be talking much about the story & I don't want to be the a-hole who spoilers it for the uninformed. There will be some light spoilers in this review.

-Premise

What Remains of Edith Finch is a walking sim about a pregnant 17yr old girl who revisits her old family home to uncover the past of the unfortunate events that have bestowed her family. It is one of the best in its genre.

-Gameplay

The game is a walking sim. In most walking sims, you venture about listening to the narrator & every once in a while. You click to do something with an object, being the nature of the beast. That stuff is still in this game until you get to the character stories. In the character stories, the gameplay diversifies. One moment you'll be soaring through the sky as an eagle, and the next, chopping off fish heads while exploring uncharted waters.

Every encounter is crafted brilliantly, uniquely, and incredibly memorable. Not during one of these was I bored or uninterested was actively looking forward to each one. Secrets that offer more insight into the family's history; are simple and a joy to discover.

-Graphics

"What Remains of Edith Finch" is a pretty-looking game with phenomenal art direction. The game doesn't have a singular art style. It switches between a few different ones with every encounter. You can a segment that looks like an oil painting, then be transported into a pulp horror comic with cel-shading. Many other surprises a wait.

I didn't have any performance issues or bugs throughout my playtime.

-Story

As I mentioned above, I won't be talking much about the story. That is the meat of this game. I will be talking about the themes of the game. Skip to the final verdict if you don't want to be spoiled.

What Remains of Edith Finch is about grief & acceptance of life. Mortality is constant present & the frivolous actions the characters take throughout the story lead to their demise. The vast majority of deaths could've been preventable, but because they believed in this family curse & couldn't evolve past that, it consumed them.

The stupid decisions they made that led them to their demise are believable.

The acting is superb, the dialogue is immaculately written, and I have zero complaints about the writing.

This brings us to the end of the review. I don't like to use the word perfect nothing is truly perfect. That is the only way to describe this game. "What Remains of Edith Finch" crafted a beautiful although short story with memorable gameplay segments.

Final Verdict
10/10-Must Buy

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's no way to review this game without telling a personal story. So my grandma had just died, the one I was amazingly close with growing up. In that moment, life felt cursed. I couldn't rationally process how one of my main caretakers had passed on. Was I unlucky, was there a dark force that followed me and took from me the people I loved? It wasn't the first loss I had experienced, and after enough tragedy you begin to ask yourself questions that may seem unreasonable to most. They all lead back to the central question of whether or not those who experience tragedy are cursed.

SPOILERS AHEAD:

Playing Edith Finch, which is a story based game that isn't long or difficult at all, directly addresses this feeling through a narrative that is far deeper than one will first give it credit for. In this game, you explore the childhood home of an Edith Finch. It is made up of rooms that were once occupied by the deceased family members of Edith. And every single member, save for her, has passed. It's a dark cloud that hangs over the family, one that the grandmother claims is the result of a dark curse. As you explore the house, you experience the stories of the members who passed away. And experiencing these tragic tales, you can't help but feel like you are part of a dark force consuming everything in its path. By the end, you may even be confused. "Was there a curse or not?"

Upon much reflection, I realize that the curse that everyone is experiencing isn't some ghostly spirit or animated force. The curse you are experiencing is the curse of life itself. And that is to say, life ends. There isn't always a perfect explanation, no matter how desperately we search for one, because death is inevitable and will take us all. And this idea REALLY hit home for me. I found myself blaming fate, blaming God, blaming whoever for the pain I felt during loss. But this pain isn't malicious, it isn't personal. it's the cost of experiencing this gift of life.

Finishing this game made me reflect on loss in a brand new way, and I can honestly say it changed me. It made me reflect on the stories I would tell myself to cope with the inevitability of death. In a strange way, I became more at peace with it because of the reflections in this story. If this sounds relevant to you and your experiences, I can't recommend this game enough. If you're open to the experience, it will show and teach incredible things.

Oh, the gameplay and graphics are both great as well. As are the environments and presentation. Amazing game. Buy it, but only if you're open to the themes I've described.\t\t\t\t\t
