\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a very talented story. The game tells small stories of the protagonist Edith's family. Some are touching, some are disturbing, and all create a compelling story about a broken family. Within the frame narrative, Edith re-evaluates and remembers the details of her childhood and family, in a process very familiar to anyone who's experienced re-evaluating their (in retrospect) abusive childhood. The game is, at times, a walking simulator- but it never fails to use the interactive medium to tell its story, to great effect.

As a note, some of these stories are very dark. Particularly for groups sensitive to exploration of suicidal ideation and abusive themes, make sure you're in a safe place and state of mind before playing.

I'd recommend going into What Remains of Edith Finch as unspoiled as possible. Best when experienced with little knowledge.\t\t\t\t\t
