\r\n\t\t\t\t\t‘What Remains of Edith Finch’ is a perfect balance between high quality and simplicity. It’s an amazing aesthetic experience with astonishing graphics, beautiful music, strange but highly immersive story, and extremely simple and incredibly intuitive controls.

The game sets new standards for quality gaming experience. Balance is the keyword here. The game is short, but just short enough to keep you engaged until the very end, and not short enough to leave you hungry for more.

The mechanics are simple and intuitive, but not repetitive enough to become irritating, the patterns are repetitive and you know what to expect next, but not enough to be bored or make further predictions.

The setting is quite artistic, but only to an extent that it is still believable as a real place. Gameplay seems to be the same, but is diverse enough to keep you excited. It’s all just one perfectly balanced product.

Everything just hits the right spot – there amount and extent of any aspect of the game is neither vast enough to overwhelm the player or narrow enough to make them feel unsatisfied. Even the credits roll has such an interesting design that I watched it willingly until the very end.

The game has elements of my favourite franchises but it cannot be compared to any other games – it’s just one of a kind.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm still processing how I feel about this game, so I'm not entirely sure what to say in my review. One things for sure, I believe this was a worthwhile experience. Ignoring the story for a second, this game has some of the best game mechanics I've ever experienced. I often felt like I was truly in characters shoes, seeing the world from their eyes or doing the things they did. For that alone I would call this an amazing game.

The story itself made me feel a lot of different things: sadness, humor, fear, and an appreciation for what I have in life. In the end, I'm still not sure what to make of it. I definitely think it made me feel a lot more than I was expecting. In the end, making you connect with each of the Finches and their stories is where this game excels. (Or at least, it did for me).

The only small problem I had with this game is that certain parts made me very motion sick and I had to stop playing for a while. Specifically, Molly and Calvin's stories were the worst. Certain abrupt movements walking around as Edith also contributed somewhat. I still greatly enjoyed the game and would highly recommend playing it. However, if you sometimes have issues with motion sickness while playing games, be aware that this one may affect you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[NO SPOILER REVIEW]
What remains of Edith Finch is purely a story driven game where you as the player must uncover the mysteries of your old house, and it will keep you speculating and sometimes in a state of emotional shock after you uncover something new. As Edith arrives in the morning and time lapses as you explore new stuff inside the house.
Its full with emotion, passion, and love from the developers especially in the writing.
This game will still keep you asking questions and watching theory videos on youtube and exploring the subreddit as I did, even after the credits roll.
If you're very busy with work or studying this game can easily be done In one day in 2 hours or less.
Only flaw is that there is no sprint, and 4K support in the settings wasn't working unless in fullscreen.
Overall [] with the story writing being the strongest aspect while gameplay design could be a lot better just from sprinting. (This isn't like Halo or Doom where no sprint is good)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot really a game, it's an interactive story. And not a very good story at that. It's certainly novel in many ways, but it's not really entertaining or thought-provoking. As a story, it's bland because it doesn't really evoke any feelings, and as a game it's boring because you don't get to do much. There's really very little impetus to keep you going on and wanting more, and once you finish it - within the two hours it takes - there's simply no reason to ever go back, as you have seen everything it has to offer. There's no variety, no alternatives, nothing you might have missed - nothing worth going back for.\t\t\t\t\t
