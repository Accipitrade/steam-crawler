It comes at you regardless of your level of engagement and doesn't require anything from you for the story to unfold.


This is, of course, not to say that movies are bad. We've all been terrified, moved to tears, and broken down laughing from them but when you consider the pros and cons of both mediums, you realize video games bring both sets of those pros together.

Playing a video game is an active process.
You could easily argue even more so than reading.
You more or less control what happens when and how.
And even if a plot in a video game takes one linear path, the fact that you're the one pushing it forward tends to make it far more compelling and immersive.

That being said - If you haven't played What Remains of Edith Finch,
I highly recommend it if you're into narrative-driven indie games.
On a very basic level, it's about a supposedly cursed family called the Finches;
you play as Edith Finch, the last living member as she walks around the old, bulbous family estate looking for clues as to how each family member passed away.
When you enter each family member's room, you find out how they died and experience their final moments in a flashback.
So it's a pretty dark game. One of the flashbacks is straight-up a baby drowning in a bathtub and yes, you play as the baby.
And another one is about a guy who's lived with agoraphobia his whole life and one day he finally gets the courage to go outside and is immediately hit by a train.
