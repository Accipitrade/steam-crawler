I was reluctant to play it at first, I mostly threw it into a larger purchase I made, but when I eventually played it this is the game I enjoyed the most.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t “If we lived forever, maybe we’d have time to understand things.”

As the last remaining descendant of the Finch family, Edith travels to their old abandoned home and finds out more about her family and their history by reliving their experiences.
Throughout this journey, the game asks you to contemplate the nature of death, coping with grief, and the legacy each of us leaves behind.

Through the use of this storytelling technique, Edith Finch's gameplay manages to vary quite a bit in its short playtime. One moment, you could be a child on a swing, trying to climb higher and higher, and the next you could be a toddler playing in a bath, letting your imagination run wild.
However, one section involving Edith’s brother Jack is unquestionably the game’s highlight. Told only in the way an interactive medium can, through the merging of storytelling and gameplay, it features a truly beautiful and nuanced perspective of depression. This is gameplay that cannot be put into words; only experienced.
While not every member of the Finch family’s story reaches these same heights, their own narratives are inventive and creative nevertheless, and thus worth praise.

The setting for this story, the Finch house, is as much of a character as the ones who lived in it. From the moment you see it, with its rickety exterior of rooms stacked on top of one another like a game of jenga, the house promises to be full of mystery and intrigue. Similarly, its interior is painstakingly stuffed chock-full of detailed rooms and secret passageways. Simply looking around the rooms of each of the Finches tells you just as much about them as playing through their past.

What Remains of Edith Finch’s ability to understand the ethos of gameplay as narrative allows it to tell one of the most mature and interesting stories in the gaming medium. This combined with its short playtime make it an easy recommendation to those looking for interesting and unique narrative experiences in games. I would also recommend that this game be completed in one sitting, similar to watching a movie.\t\t\t\t\t

Story

What Remains of Edith Finch is undoubtedly one of the more interesting and creatively unique games I have ever played. However, despite its aesthetic appeal, I believe the high rating is a bit misguided. Unfortunately, there are those who see something unconventional and immediately proclaim it as a masterpiece.

The game is quite short, only lasting around 2 hours and the gameplay itself is quite limited. In fact other than walking through the labyrinth and secret passageways of the Finch household there isn't much to offer from this stand point. Indeed this a highly story motivated game. The story is refreshing and essentially a compilation of several short tales regarding how each member of the Finch family died.

Each story is different and told in a separate style from the others. In this sense, the game never feels repetitive yet at the same time, lacks fluidity. The structure of the story is not too smooth and in this sense, the big picture feels much smaller than it should be. For example, in the beginning, the game feels expansive and boundless, but by the end, you get a sense that something is missing.

It's not great value in terms of hours for your money but it's quite special in its own way and will stick with you longer than the time you spend playing it.\t\t\t\t\t
