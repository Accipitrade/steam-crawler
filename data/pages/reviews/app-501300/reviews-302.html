\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA perfect example of video games as art, Edith Finch delivers a thought-provoking, unique experience in a short but sweet bundle of tales focused around one very unlucky family. It's the kind of game that can be appreciated by almost anyone, especially non-gamers, as a shining example of story-telling through gaming.

Multiple sequences in Edith Finch are singularly unique, and the whole thing shines in varying a lot across different chapters, giving experiences you never thought you'd want to have.
Have you ever wondered what it's like to be an owl? Be trapped in a bomb shelter for 50 years? Have a dissociated personality disorder?

The story is compelling and intriguing. The only downside is that the ending does feel somewhat cut short, almost like there was clearly one more chapter that was meant to be included but then was suddenly cut at the end, specifically regarding the fate of one critical character, the grandmother . Nevertheless, despite its brevity the game doesn't feel "too short". Absolutely recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
