\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: A disappointingly short game that fails to tell any meaningful story. No replayability. Alright art direction. No deeper meaning or secrets. Not worth the price tag.
First off let me say that I enjoy "walking simulators", many people find them tedious and boring, but I've always found them relaxing. As long as they have an engaging story and a strong art direction I will become fully immersed. That being said, Edith Finch fails not only as a walking simulator, but also as a video game. The art direction is fine, nothing special such as games like The Witness or Firewatch, but not bad either. It was mediocre, even good. So that is not why I am leaving a negative review. The game completing fails when it comes about to tell a compelling narrative, because wow this story is bad. I don't just mean cliched and bland, which it is don't get me wrong, but it is just plain bad.
There is no originality to be found in this game, every story it tells has been done elsewhere either in other videogames or just literature in general. The game has about 15 or so characters, but with a playtime of only 2-3 hours how can you possibly expect to devote enough time to each character so that the player knows them well? You can't, or maybe you can but this game definitely does not do that. Most of the characters can be summed up in a single sentence. "This is blank and he likes to blank". In fact the game does this itself, When talking about Barbara. Molly, Sam, and others the game explicitly says this character liked this thing, this character is said because of this thing, etc. It's derivative and feels like a child tried to write a compelling story. Not a story made by a medium sized team of artists that spent 5 years making a game. So you never really get to know any of the characters. There are a few exceptions to this of course, you get to know Edith, Edie, Lewis, and Dawn pretty well but most of the cast is not elaborated on.
Well that could be fine, story driven games exist, there doesn't need to be a focus on characters. But this game has no story. I mean that in the most literal sense. There is no narrative. There is an impression of a narrative with a common theme linking each family member's demise together, but it is just that, an impression of a narrative. It is never expanded upon. Quite frankly after my first playthrough of the game I felt empty, "Is that it? Maybe I missed something." So I took to the internet, perhaps this was one of those games where a narrative exists hidden from the viewer, but still there in the background. Nothing. There was no deeper meaning to the game. The only thing that is elaborated on that I found was that one of the character's disappearances is linked to another video game by the same company, that's it. No secret story, no explanation, no characterization, the game is all face value. And while true, there is stuff I noticed while searching the house for clues after beating the game that I didn't notice before, these things were just that. They didn't add any meaning to the story. It feels strange too as the game seems to invite you to look deeper, secret passages and rooms full of old musty books, but when you actually dig deeper, you find nothing. Maybe I'm just too dumb to understand this game, maybe I just don't get it, or I am missing something obvious, but even after watching a ton of theories my understanding wasn't added to at all.
It baffles me that this game took as long to make as it did, because honestly it feels like the "story" took two months to write, and then they spent the rest of the time making assets.
Then there is the gameplay. Let me reiterate. I like walking simulators, you don't need great gameplay to have a good game. This game doesn't even have good gameplay. As in the controls are horrible, Every action you emulate with your mouse, and is so mind-numbing. I would have preferred if you just could click your mouse to perform the action. Why make me rotate my mouse in a circle ten times? The controls are also jittery. Which just added to the frustration. I would be performing an action and suddenly it would stop or go backwards for no apparent reason. At least play-test your controls to make sure they actually function.
It is a shame too, after the first flashback the game told me about Molly it seemed like I would be in for a very fun time, as that first flashback is amazing, but the quality of the flashbacks just drop one after another after that initial first impression. There are a few winners in here, Molly, Lewis, and Sam were all very fun to play as. But most of them felt stitched together and uncreative. In a way I feel bad about writing this review because I can tell that the studio put a lot of work into this game, I can see where they tried to make it fun, they just fail at it, from literally every front. It's almost as if they knew they had a bad game on their hands because the game is justtttt long enough to make it past that 2 hour mark. I cannot in good-conscious recommend this game. If you are really that interested in the story just go watch a YouTube playthrough, you will get the exact same experience. At least other walking simulators give you somewhat of a choice in the actions you take, at least other walking simulators have character that I feel bad for or who I want to succeed, at least other walking simulators have a price that feels justified. But even on sale for 7$ I still think this game isn't worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a self described atmospheric, story rich, walking simulator and it truly is an artistic experience that could only be told through this medium. Beautiful and bittersweet, this is one of the few games that has ever brought me to tears.

You play a story within a story within a story as Edith Finch explores her old family home and learns about the family curse that has supposedly killed every member of her family save for her in a series of unfortunate events.

Somehow despite the stream of tragedy and constant emotional punches I never felt desensitised to the events in the game. An utter masterpiece in terms of design and storytelling, Giant Sparrow has created an unforgettable and thought provoking experience that will have many enthralled from beginning to end.

I strongly recommend that you complete this game in one sitting for the full effect, it’s about three hours and absolutely worth your money.\t\t\t\t\t
