\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game defies standard game conventions, there is no combat, only basic puzzles and is a type that has become known as a walking simulator. With no action and only the vaguest notions of suspense if you are a player that plays games to shoot stuff up this probably isn't for you. But if you want a game that you can play in the evening and for lack of a better term experience then this is the best I have found so far. Individually the components aren't anything special, the story is decent, the visuals are passable and the music has some amazing pieces but is overall not going to win any awards. But the sheer imaginative world that Giant Sparrow manages to build with all these different parts is breathtaking. This game can be completed in a single evening, but I couldntt help but feel that every moment of it was unique and left an imprint. While not for everyone, if you are looking for a world to lose yourself in for a few hours and get told not just a story but an experience this is a good one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuch an incredible game. Definitely one of the top-tier 'walking sims' out there. The story is so engaging and is creatively told through visuals, gameplay and voice-overs. Unlike many other games, you don't need to read any of the reading material since it's all being read out for you like an audiobook paired with interactive gameplay to keep your hands busy. The music is great and fits so well in the scenes as well as the amazing voice acting. The pacing is very well done in my opinion, the game kept me interested and intrigued the whole time until the end and just kept me wanting to know more about the Finch family. Definitely recommend this game and it's only around 2 hours so it's not long enough to bore you and not too short. It has much more interactivity than a usual walking sim.\t\t\t\t\t
