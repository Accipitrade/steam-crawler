One Sentence
A narrative driven game where game mechanics and exceptional writing are meshed wonderfully.

A Bit More
First of all, the game looks great. The design of the house and each and every room is really a work of art, the game is aesthetically pleasing on all fronts. The sound design is wonderful and the variety of things you will experience really make this worthwhile.
Try to get past the awkwardness of walking really slowly and realize you won’t be needing to behave as you normally do when given control of an FPS avatar. Once you are past that initial awkwardness the game really does a good job of getting you into the story, the story is just that good.

Bottom Line
If you are into the medium of video games and want to experience everything they have to offer, I believe “What Remains” is probably the best FPS narrative driven game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a beautiful and haunting game that shows what a "walking simulator" done right can be. The protagonist strolls through an old family home, their return triggered by a family event. The story unwinds as a series of events and minor puzzles -- really more interactions -- that drive the narrative along. The stories of the past members of the house play out in different genres and styles -- some campy, some joyous, some surreal -- in a way that in the end makes the stories meditative and beautiful reflections of life and death.

I finished in 2 and a half hours, and they were exquisite hours of content. Now that the game is older and frequently on sale, do yourself a favor and visit the Finch family estate.\t\t\t\t\t
