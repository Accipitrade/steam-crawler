\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a strange one. I played and finished (pretty much in one session) this game last night and I'm still not sure whether to love it or hate it. Probably a bit of both. What I can say, is that it was definitely a unique experience. It took me about 2 hours to finish, so I'd definitely say the non-sale price is too high. Definitely pick up on sale.

Now as for the gameplay, I've seen this described as a "walking simulator" and even compared to "Gone Home", which is a ridiculous comparison imo. While on the surface, yes, the main part of the game plays like a "walking simulator", throughout the game you discover different characters' stories with very unique gameplay mechanics, some of which are some of the most creative and imaginative uses of gameplay to tell a story, I've seen in my 20+ years of gaming. They also have different art-styles, which again are very imaginative.

As for the story, I can't say too much without spoilers, so let me just say it is a very strange tale about a family, life and death. Even after finishing it, I'm not quite sure what to make of it and more importantly what the developers were going for. The "message" feels very nihilistic to be honest, which is probably my biggest complaint about the game, but that may be up to interpretation.
I will not tell you how this game will make you feel (I hate when reviewers do this "this game will make you cry"), but it certainly triggered a lot of different emotions in me. Parts made me laugh or smile and parts made me cry, but your mileage may vary depending on your sensibilities.
And speaking of, be warned, the game deals A LOT with death and dark themes and personally I felt pretty empty and depressed after finishing it, so maybe don't play it when you're already in a bad place.

That being said, despite that, I still recommend it for the unique experience it offers and the creative game design. And maybe you will feel completely different about its message/meaning, who knows. I will probably replay it at some point in the future to see if I will interpret it differently on a second run.\t\t\t\t\t
