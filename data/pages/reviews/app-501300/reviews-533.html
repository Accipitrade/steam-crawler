\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is really an experience you have to take all in. It is more a piece of art than an actual game so it is not for everyone.

You take the role of Edith Finch and wander through the very strange and mysterious Finch house to unravel all of the family members stories in their respective rooms.
Getting to these rooms is not a straight forward walk to each room tho, but a very well designed route through all the lovely crafted details of the house including all of its secrets.

The graphics and the soundtrack of the game are very nice and the voice acting is also top-notch.
Everything is filled with so many detailed objects that by themselves already tell little stories.
Each family members story is totally different and after each one I found myself looking forward to the next one and all the creative ideas the developer put into them.

I would totally recommend this game to anyone that just wants to have a relaxing and adventurous time and is wondering with what stories and creative ideas developers can come up with.

- Great storytelling
- Mysterious house
- Creative ideas
- Nice graphics
- Good soundtrack
- Top-notch voice acting
- Very short (about 3 hours)
- Walking simulator\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow. I'm amazed. This game is incredible. What is obviously a labor of love for the developers, it was a truly enjoyable experience for me. The story is great, but where the game really shines is in the high level of environmental detail, from the lush surrounding forest, to the incredibly detailed interiors of the house.. I am awed.

Is it short? Yes. Took me about 2 & 1/2 hours to complete. But, is that a concern? Not for me it wasn't. If a game is designed well and keeps me entertained, I'm happy to pay the price for it. The game is just right in length, I think, and it is engrossing and well-crafted throughout. I kept trying to take everything in along the way, but I also wanted to find out what was going to happen next.

Is it exciting? In a way. But it's more like an incredibly detailed hidden-object/point-and-click type adventure, just realized in full 3D. It was a joy from beginning to end to explore the detailed landscapes and poke into every nook and cranny of the mansion. If you like an extremely detailed area to explore, driven by an engrossing narrative, then this game is for you!!

Cons: not many; sometimes the character would get stuck on some of the terrain in an odd place, or a transition area would grab my character's control and I would have to wait until she made it through whatever crawlspace entrance or whatever, to be able to turn back in the direction I wanted to actually go. And there might be a way to fix the non-existent Vsync control issue, but the game is short enough to not really matter.

None of that really mattered, as the game is full of great stuff and nothing that felt like it was just filler or out of place.

Highly recommended for anyone loving story-rich and visually wonderful masterpieces of art. Because this game felt like just that, art. A wonderful journey from beginning to end.\t\t\t\t\t
