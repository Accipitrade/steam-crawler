\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisappointing. All I really want from interactive fiction is a lot of opportunities to explore, but this offered almost none. The story was very linear, which can be fine, but I found myself very bored with only one or two choices to click on at any given time. I also had issues with the controller mapping. You can't pull up past text that you find, so I missed a couple things and didn't know how to view it again.

I have a lot of patience for visual novel games and I don't get bored when there's a good story to latch onto or a lot of stuff to click on, but this game has neither. The graphics were nice and I liked the concept but the execution is poor. This wasn't worth the $12 sale price, so I returned it. Had it been priced at $3-4 then I probably wouldn't have had an issue, but they have no business charging this much for what it is. If you want a great interactive story game, check out The Invisible Hours. I paid full price for that ($30) and it was worth every penny.\t\t\t\t\t
