\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very enjoyable walking sim with a very sad story. It runs smoothly, and the graphics are great.
It's not really an unpredictable story. I, and I'm sure other players could see the ending coming, and sometimes, confirmation that you saw what was to come can ruin otherwise brilliant work, but the art of this game wasn't in trying to stop you from knowing what was to come, but providing a fantastic delivery of the story that they wanted to tell.

Some things to note if you're considering this game. It's a walking sim, buy the game expecting it to be a walking sim.
Also Edith moves kind of slowly (though there is a justfiable reason for this in the story), so if slow walking bothers you, this might not be the game for you. I should point out however, that while I noticed how slow she walked in the beginning, I got used to the pace pretty quickly.

If however, you're okay with a slower movement and you're looking for a walking sim, I could not recommend this enough, and am very happy to add my recommendation into the sea of all of the other recommendations put out by others for this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPLOT (no spoilers):
Without spoiling anything: you play as a girl coming back to her family house to unveil stories about her relatives. As you progress through the rooms, you'll find snippets from those stories, which are interactive (you play each story as a different character). As you go, you'll start making sense of the family's history.

POSITIVE POINTS:
- Short enough to keep you entertained; long enough to be satisfactory.
- Varied gameplay: as most walking simulator, there's very little gameplay in WRoEF. However, every time you get in one the the character's story, you interact with it in a different way. This helps to keep the narrative interesting and varied.
- Brilliant storytelling: one of my problems with walking simulators is that most of them are merely text-based stories presented in a 3D environment. WRoEF, however, breaks this convention is in brilliantly able to use gameplay and environmental elements to convey the story, feelings, etc.
- Good voice acting: it's convincing and one of the less cringy ones I've seen (heard?) in a walking simulator.
- Moving plot and scenes: the stories are told in a way that makes you feel the feels. Even when you know what's coming, the build-up is excellent.

NEGATIVE POINT:
- To my personal taste, the overall plot leaves a little too much up for interpretation. I thought the ending was going to wrap things up properly and provide closure to some unsolved mysteries, but this is taken away from you when you're almost there! It feels more like a cliffhanger for a sequel than an open-to-interpretation thing, but it would be much more satisfactory if we'd got some more concrete answers to some of the mysteries.

VEREDICT:
This is what every walking simulator should be like. The story is compelling and told in very, very interesting and varying ways. It's a great use of the game medium for storytelling. 9.5/10 would relive the deaths of my relatives again.\t\t\t\t\t
