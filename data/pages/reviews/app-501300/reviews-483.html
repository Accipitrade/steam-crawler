\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is hands down one of the best games of 2017. The story is told as a series of vignettes set in a mysterious, secret-passage filled mansion on Orcas Island in northwestern Washington, exploring the mysterious and ironic misfortunes of the Finch family. Each story plays with the game's structure in fun and interesting ways you have to play to see- and one story in particular brought me to tears. It also avoids the pitfalls of many similar walk-around-and-look-at-stuff games in that you're an active participant in the game and its story instead of just wandering around having a story told to you.

All in all, a fantastic game. Buy it!\t\t\t\t\t

Short Lived\t
\t
Edith Finch is a collection of short stories about short lives, and plays like you read a book, each life a new chapter: different in style, setting, graphics, epoch, and game mechanics, but having in common childhood's innocence and imagination mixed with fear and sadness. Every chapter will end too soon, and leave you eager to open the next.

The binding between each story is this impossible house, enormous yet claustrophobic, inspiring both nostalgia and dread.

Edith Finch's storytelling is clever and subtle; no forceful introductions, conclusions, or opinions. And despite the very colorful and varied environments, it still manages to leave a lot to the player's imagination.

At 3 hours of gameplay, it's also very short, but is an experience worth feeling.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt pains me to have to not recommend this game as it is entertaining and the premise is appealing, however in regards to the story and the misfortunes of the family the game feels extremely hollow and for me offered nothing of substance, I feel somewhat empty and dissatisfied where I was hoping it would give me something really emotive, crushing or moving in some way. A few stories stand out, Gregory and Barbara however overall the minigame sequences felt lacking and unimaginative and do not reveal as much as you would like about the situation and why the family is as unfortunate as they are.

Overall this game offers much and provides you with such high hopes but sadly does not deliver. I'm disappointed and don't feel like I got the payoff the developers presume a player will from playing their game. If you want to see something somewhat innovative gameplay wise (in reference to the story based minigames, not the walking) then buy it on offer.

However if you are after the masterpiece that people are dubbing this game as, I fear you will be disappointed, like me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith "Game of the Year" award nominations and overwhelming positivity around this game, I had to experience it for myself. After finishing the game, I find the positivity to be extremely overstated.

I think the deciding factor for whether this game is recommended for a player or not is how impactful these stories will be for them. For myself, the stories didn't offer anything new or any lasting impact. Although tragic and presented with pretty set-pieces, the stories just felt uninspiring and middle-of-the-road. I feel as though I could get a stronger emotional response out of reading a short current events article than what I felt throughout this game. Experiencing the game is like eating a bland and unseasoned meal on a very pretty plate atop an elaborately decorated dinner table.

People suggest watching a 53 minute video that elaborates on the story as if you'd get something more out of the game, but after watching it, it's very much like reading a standard essay analysis of someone outlining the details of the story that are very much on-the-surface or near to it.

For those new to the genre and its story/narrative techniques, this is a competently crafted game. However, if you're looking for something that's "Game of the Year" worthy, it's a bit unimpressive.\t\t\t\t\t
