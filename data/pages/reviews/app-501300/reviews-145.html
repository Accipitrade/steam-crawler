\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort but amazing. I spent a lot of time just poking around and still finished the game in under 3 hours. But what an amazing 3 hours!

Every story segment is presented in a different, and surprising, way. On one level, you can say that this "game" is just someone telling you a story... that it's just a fancy audiobook. But every step of the way, you are _doing_ something to move the story forward. It's highly interactive and unique. And every story is tinged with sadness.

The attention to detail in the house is amazing. There are so many little clues about who the characters were. And so many questions about what really happened to them. And the house just feels so _real_.

The details are what really make the game. Early on, look down at yourself... just looking at yourself gives you clues about Edith's situation. Take your time, don't rush. Look at the knickknacks and mementos and book titles. Soak it in and really feel what Edith is feeling as she explores this history of her family that she never knew.\t\t\t\t\t
