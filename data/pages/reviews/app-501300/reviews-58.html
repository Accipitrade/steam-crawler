\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not sure I can add much to what has already been said, so I just write a few brief comments:

Some people complain that the stories are too short or that the characters aren't very sympathetic (i.e., they were irrational in their actions). It's hard to disagree in reality with the latter part, but, I mean, it's a fictional story; you have to give way to some elements of irrationality.

The "game" is pretty depressing. I didn't particularly like the ending, although I totally expected it. But overall, I still enjoyed it.

One thing that bothers me is that near the end, they begin to tell the story of the "old house", but that isn't finished. Why bother to bring it up and then abruptly shut it down? DId I miss something?

Anyway, I give this game a mild recommend. I bought it on sale over a year before I played it, and finally got around to it today. It took about 2 hours. If you pay the price of a cinema ticket or a DVD, that seems reasonable, since you are basically getting the video-game equivalent of that.\t\t\t\t\t
