\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is, simply put, one of the most beautiful, creative, and heart-wrenching gaming experiences ever made.
To say much about it would be to spoil the impact of experiencing it and realizing where the plot is going, but suffice it to say that, through our protagonist's journal and her reading of several written accounts from her family members, the narrative takes us through many characters and their most unique points of view, and each is illustrated in the most gorgeous and imaginative way, not only with splendid visuals but also a brilliant use of player interface (who knew flying a kite could look SO good and tell SO much?) and absolutely lovely, haunting music, to create an immersive, deeply emotional experience.
No sequence in this game is as the one before or after it, but each leaves a lasting impression and could be a short game in and of itself, and all of them come together to weave a carefully told story that WILL tug at your heartstrings. If there is, in fact, anything to criticize about this game, it is that it leaves you craving for more (it is a relatively short experience, no more than 2/3 hours long)...but, on the other hand, it goes on precisely as it needs to, it tells precisely the story it wants to, and, as all things do, it ends precisely when it has to....which is, as you'll find out, the very essence of this game: nothing lasts forever, so enjoy it while it does.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(8.5/10)

Very creative exploration game. There is an overall story that you are uncovering as you walk through a most improbable house, and then within each room, there will be a story of its inhabitant that you will work your way through.

Obviously no spoilers but each story is very different not only in its focus but in its presentation, graphics, sound, etc. Some are short, some are a bit more fully padded out, all of them tie back in and give you perspective of what happened to this family.

I would be remiss to not touch back on the house, it is almost a fully fledged character and story in to itself. I mentioned at the top it is most improbable, but i wouldn't go quite as far as to say it is impossible. Believe it or not when looking for houses up in the mountains in Colorado, we came across a house eerily like this one, 4 improbable stories built on the edge of a sheer cliff. We never found out if it was full of secret passages, but there were areas where you could use a climbing wall to get between floors etc.

Anyway back to the game. If you like these kind of story walks, you will love this game, if you do not, this isn't the game for you, go play some call of battle duty royale strike 5\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo... What Remains of Edith Finch?

Perhaps you are a gamer who used to read more and would sometimes find a book that you just had to share with a friend so you could talk about it together. Perhaps you climbed an island mountain and, alone, watched the sun set into the sea on one side and then rise from the waves on the other the next morning. Perhaps you have children to whom you relate tales from your own childhood, pulling back the veil of mystery about their parent's origins, while teasing with hyperbole and irony. Or perhaps you once saw a photogragh of your grandfather, young and fit and tanned, leaning against a gate, and you wondered what he was thinking about the path that lay beyond the gate before he would need to lean on you.

Perhaps none of these things... or perhaps not yet. But some readers of this review might understand what I am trying to say... and those of you who do will play What Remains of Edith Finch, and find it impossible not to share what a wonderful experience this game is. Worth every minute. Every page. Every step. Each sunrise and sunset. No hyperbole or irony. A gate to fling open and bound through.

What Remains of Edith Finch is simply amazing.\t\t\t\t\t
