\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is an excellent artistic game, featuring supreme art atmosphere immersion, fantastic story telling, and exquisite medium for the two stated purposes, say, animation making, music involvement, gameplay mode.
I state the positive sides in separate paragraphs.
Edith Finch paved the way for first-person-exploring plot pushing method, which could be an example for other like games. It is an original gameplay mode for the new-rising game kinds, like, Life is Strange, etc. They are not rated 3A, but worthy of equally high artistic achievements.
Great topic indication is also Edith Finch's good point. It features the topic that one could not hide from what he or she fears, the thing he or she is afraid of doesn't vanish just because you run away from it. Vivid topic indication is something that's often seen in movie work, but seeing it in a game is really great experience.
Great plot planning is one of its breathtaking advantages. Personally speaking, I came to my tears near the end of this game, when it came to the part of Edith's great-grandma. Edith Finch is one game work that tends to be more like an art piece, thus this point weighs certainly more than any other game kind.
Edith Finch also has perfect screen performance, near-perfect animation work, diverse story-telling medium, together they create a fantasy world you could not easily get rid of.
Let me say this, this game has no disadvantage. Short plots is not poor. The story and the overall game atmosphere cannot contain longer stories, it'll make the players tired of all the materials, because they are so beautiful that we want to look at and remember everything.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn the beginning it looks like just another walking sim, at the end you will be left with your mouth wide open while watching the credits till the very end amazed at what you just experienced.


It is a walking simulator, obviously, with an unpredictable, curious, amusing and sad story.
The game is linear, it has many unique aspects, from the great and different art-styles, to the way you actually progress through the game.

You explore your family's abandoned house while on a quest to figure out the different fragments of the history of your family, your very... very unlucky family tree.


This is one of the few games that I call interactive art.
Mesmerizing soundtrack. Talented and convincing voice acting. Amazingly written story, it progresses in a good way, it is told in the perfect manner and ends even better.

There's so much attention to detail in this game, whether you're looking at the giant Finch house or a bottle of detergent on a random shelf you'll notice how talented the developers and art directors are.

When a game manages to leave you watching the credits till the very end while you reflect on what just happened, you know you just finished an amazing game.

This is a game every one can enjoy.
