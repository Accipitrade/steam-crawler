\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA unique game. A unique tale.
A gorgeous cinematic experience inside a fantasy world to remember.
If only it was more challenging.
If only it was longer.


Manometer: 8/10


The good
- Gorgeous graphics. Well designed levels (especially the Finch's house).
- Nice music and sound design.
- Great dark atmosphere. You can easily immerse yourself in its world.
- Interesting storytelling. Some of the characters' sub-stories are really unique
- A cinematic experience to remember. Great direction and cinematography.
- Excellent camera movement and smooth transitions form scene to scene.
- It packs some clever ideas at the gameplay mechanics.
- Clever decision not to include a run button. You must go by the game's flow.

The bad
- There is no challenge at all. Not a single puzzle to solve. It is more a first person interactive 3d movie than a real game. This is no bad at all if only you know it before buying.
- I felt that the comic story did not fit well to the aesthetics of the rest game.
- Although it packs some high quality stuff, it is too short for this price.\t\t\t\t\t

This is a dark, twisted and artistically beautiful game. It's very easy (basically a 'walking simulator'), but think of it as an interactive story rather than a game with particularly difficult gameplay. The way that the developers have incorporated keyboard and mouse controls in the game is frankly genius - they really know how to use the medium of gaming to its full potential when telling a story. The shifting first-person perspective further increases the immersion and makes you feel like you're a part of the story. The graphics are gorgeous, and the story is disturbing, mysterious and often just downright sad. You'll love it.\t\t\t\t\t
