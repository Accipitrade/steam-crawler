\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredible. I was hooked within the first few moments, and finished the main story in one sitting.

Some games are meant to be grand and epic. "Edith Finch" is clearly short-- but it is art. This is video game concentrate, and it is flavorful.

"Edith Finch" plays like interactive visual novel. And piece by piece, it introduces its fascinating characters and their haunting stories. With moments of joy, and mystery, and tragedy, and psychosis-- and some where you realize what is about to happen... and the game makes you follow through with it. And you do, because you have more questions whose answers you need to uncover.

Yes, it plays like a "walking simulator"-- but there's never a dull moment. Every scene and aesthetic and design choice felt astounding. The graphics are beautiful, and the level design deserves particular commendation-- along with the music, which was perfectly atmospheric.

The writing and dialogue never felt out of character. The voice actress for Edith delivered a particularly amazing performance. But for most characters you can give the exact same praise. Molly entranced and terrified me.

If I had to point out some kind of flaw, it would be cherry picking lines or scenes that felt poorly delivered, as if the voice actor had received the wrong direction. I'm specifically thinking of Rich and Barbara-- but perhaps it was paying homage to old Hollywood and it went over my head.

Edith's story will stick with me. I'd recommend anyone try it out, especially you find it on sale.

I have thoughts. This game is *incredibly* high quality, the story is very, very good, about a young girl who's inherited a house and there's lots of mystery to uncover in the past and present. Each of the protagonist's family members has a full life to explore and interpret, with distinct art and background story.

Despite... narrative and art of the highest quality, and well-developed *side* stories and art, the lack of gameplay feels unbalanced. I'm an old school adventure gamer and I want more puzzles and non-linearity. If anyone's still reading, I'm torn to give an upvote or downvote. This was a really beautiful 3-D walk through a complex and interesting but sad story. There were some "walking" puzzles (think Myst, not sure how else to explain it, but that's the only kind of puzzle at all here and not much of it).

I'll follow AnnaPurna, while I felt this game probably isn't my preferred genre, I highly recommend it for the interactive storybook that it really nailed, honestly. I liked certain mechanics (I WAS A MURDEROUS SNAKE MONSTER AND IT WAS AWESOME) and artistic choices a whole heck of a lot. On the other hand, I want to struggle to figure things out and there was very little of that here.

My score: 8/10 - Linear interactive fiction is NOT my genre; this game is incredibly well done. If you see a thumb up or thumb down, know that I was uncertain. I tentatively recommend, but didn't enjoy the gameplay decisions at all. My gripes are personal preference. Go play Lucy Dreaming to know what's missing here.\t\t\t\t\t
