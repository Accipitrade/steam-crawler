\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch takes what made Gone Home a memorable and enjoyable experience and dials it up to 11. A lot of the structure of the game is taken directly from Gone Home and is even told through a frame story in the form of a journal.
However, where Gone Home is mostly a walking simulator with narrated journal entries sprucing up the exploration from time to time, Edith Finch has fully narrated and animated sections of the game where not only the narratological perspective changes, but also the gamepplay.
Thus, it is more of a cohesive game where each sub-narrative is supported and enforced by unique gameplay segements.
Also worth noting is how the narrative itself is a lot more engaging as it is both longer and more nuanced.
Well worth the asking price if you're into these types of games, otherwise wait for a sale.
