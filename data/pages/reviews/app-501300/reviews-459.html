\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSometimes you read an enthralling book, and sometimes you watch a deep movie, and sometimes you play What Remains of Edith Finch.
The game is too diverse to really comment on mechanics, but it runs okay and looks good. I found I had to move my mouse a bit too much at times. Now, the narrative is pretty damn amazing, but I wouldn't say it's perfect. What I really loved was the way the two, gameplay and narrative, are combined. The minigames are satisfying and relevant to the story.
Over the years, books have been written that push the limits of how one can string words after one another. Paintings that use colour, curve and chiaroscuro. But I never really notice an equivalent videogame until I played through this game.
Reccomended to anyone who likes well-made videogames.

P.S.
Technically, you even get to shoot people. There are guns too! There's also a bit of romance, although it's imaginary. And a pun or two. All the genres!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a short, yet hauntingly beautiful experience. It's hard to review the game without any spoilers, but I'm going to try and keep the review spoiler-free.

You play as Edith, the last alive member of the Finch family. Edith returns to her inherited childhood home after many years to find answers and learn about her family history. Each member's story is very well thought out and is beautifully narrated by Valerie Rose Lohman (Edith). You get to play as each member of the family and live their stories, each story being unique and intriguing in it's own right. Some events that occur in the story are quite thought-provoking and the soundtrack does a perfect job complimenting the story. Each family member's room is designed with a great level of detail which makes you get to know what the character's persona was like before you live their story.

The game takes you on a roller-coaster of emotions. Each element of the game is beautifully crafted, making it the most immersive game I've played yet. I've seen some reviews bashing the game for being too short or depressing, but it's a matter of perspective and I'd suggest you play through the game and decide for yourself. Not all good stories are long. The game took me 4 hours to finish, which includes me replaying through some of the stories. Calvin's story was my favorite because I related to it a lot, and I'm sure that if you play through the game, you'll relate to one of the stories as well.

It's like playing a game, watching a movie, and reading a book at the same time. I would highly recommend this masterpiece and I'll conclude my review with one of my favorite quotes from the game:

"If we lived forever, maybe we'd have time to understand things. But as it is, I think the best we can do is try to open our eyes, and appreciate how strange and brief all of this is."\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe short review: This game. Oh, my goodness. I played through it last night and I am still thinking about it. It is easily one of the most unique, visually stunning games I've ever played, with gameplay that serves to heighten the story in a way that is not possible in any other medium. Do yourself a favor and buy it, because it is the most emotionally packed two and a half hours a game has ever put me through.

The longer review:
What I loved so much about this game was how seamlessly it blended the uncanny and the real. Every story you hear seems plausible enough to be true, beautifully narrated to guide you through the emotions and misfortunes of the Finch family...yet you are left with enough questions that you have no way to tell what is "true"--and it makes you wonder if what is "true" really matters in this case. Imagination and reality are seamlessly blended into one gorgeous story. But in spite of (or perhaps as a result of) the uncanny mindset the game puts you in, the stories resonate and pack a powerful emotional punch. I gasped after every story. My breath was taken away and my heart ached for these beautifully rendered characters given only moments of gameplay.

This game would not succeed in being as emotionally powerful and moving as it is without its unique gameplay. Each story yet again blends the real and the imagined through forcing YOU to experience the fantastical and bizarre lives and deaths of the Finch family. It is compelling, unique gameplay. What left me particularly breathless was playing as Edith's older brother, simultaneously working at the cannery while imagining himself into a beautiful, complex world. Barbara's story, too, was incredible to play.

The visuals of this game are incredible and, for me, are on par with games like The Witness. Each room is so carefully rendered, and the entire world of the Finches makes sense with the small, fantastical attention to detail the designers had.\t\t\t\t\t
