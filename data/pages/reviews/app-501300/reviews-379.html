I don't know how to tell you how great this one was. This is a masterpiece, it's true art.
I loved everything in the game: first of all the beautiful, sad and touching story, the graphics, the art style, the music, the synchro, the characters, the fact that the game is so detailed. Really I am not one of those "emotional" guys, but I was clutched during the whole game and at the end I could only cry.
I even enjoyed the gameplay and that it is so diversified and creative. The game is "only" 2 hours long, but really that's just perfect. I couldn't stop playing the game till the end and when I finished it I didn't thought that it was too short. If you are one of the those guys who thinks "Nah, I payed for the game and I want a lot of playtime out of it, I don't care about story, I want grind." Then this is definitly not for you. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tA rather beautiful, unique, and enjoyable experience, even if short. It should be said this is really more of an interactive story than a game.

Some of these reviews would have you believing this is the game/interactive equivalent of Boyhood or something and, listen, it ain’t; games just aren’t at that level of character and emotional storytelling yet. Even as games go, I’d give the emotional engagement here a distant back seat to, say, Night in the Woods—which is at least partially due to the difference in length between the two.

That said, the story and character-building are still quite damn good for media of this kind. The individual vignettes are artful, surprising, and surreal and leave you wanting more. Lewis Finch is probably one of the most tragically relatable characters in gaming, despite the fact we never hear him speak. Though the narrative is really linear and the environment low on interactability, lovely visuals, attention-to-detail, well-designed controls, and novel touches make WREF a satisfyingly immersive experience.

Probably better bought on sale, and don’t let the hype set outsized expectations if you have a particularly high standard for character and story, but this was an impressive, original, stimulating experience that I absolutely recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
