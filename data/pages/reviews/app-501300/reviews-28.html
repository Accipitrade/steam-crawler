\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a masterpiece of storytelling that will leave you with a sense of wonder and awe. This game is a beautifully crafted narrative that tells the story of the Finch family, a cursed family whose members all died in strange and mysterious ways.

The game is essentially a collection of short stories, each one detailing the life and death of a different member of the Finch family. The stories are brilliantly written and expertly crafted, with each one drawing you in and immersing you in the world of the Finches.

One of the standout features of this game is the way it uses gameplay mechanics to enhance the storytelling. Each story is presented in a different way, with the player taking on the role of the character whose story is being told. This creates a sense of intimacy and connection with each character that is rare in video games.

The game also features stunning visuals and a hauntingly beautiful soundtrack that perfectly complements the melancholic and mysterious tone of the story.

What Remains of Edith Finch is a must-play for anyone who loves great storytelling and immersive gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t

It’s a tad boring, but that’s only because I’m not a fan of this type of game. If you like this genre, it is one of the best. I must admit it was refreshing to experience a rich and powerful story in just the length of a long movie, rather than having to trudge through 30 hours of dialogue and getting distracted by side quests.

The game sets out to do one thing and it does it well in a unique and provocative way. It’s definitely heartwrenching, so grab some tissues if you’re a crier.

Even as someone who dislikes playing these story-focused walking simulator type games, im glad I did. It was a nice evening at half the cost of the cinema. I think I paid about 5USD for it, but go ahead and pay full price to toss the devs some acknowledgement for their great writing and detail. They clearly poured their hearts into this title.\t\t\t\t\t
