\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI adore this game. Everything about it.
The graphics are beautiful and engaging. You can really see the difference in the perspective of the character's in their journals.
I love the way the developers allow you to interpret the story. There is no definite answer at the end, no exact conclusion. You are allowed to believe what you want about the family and their stories.
Subtitles are built into the game which is so refreshing to see a game not only provide accessibility features, but make them a part of the experience. There is voice acting as well in case you don't like to read and all of the actors do a lovely job of making an immersive world that you can fall into.
The story telling is done in a way that makes you feel as though you are living a novel through the screen. They mastered the art of showing rather than telling and that allows you to understand what is happening naturally.
I don't have any complaints other than I can never play this game for the first time again. I highly recommend this game to anyone and everyone.
This game is a piece of artwork.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is what some people consider a walking simulator, which I think is a negative term, but may help with getting an idea of the type of game you would be getting into.

The game is entirely story-driven and is short, having taken me 2.5 hours to go through the story. The replayability is very low as well. The length of the story and the low replayability is the only con I can think of. The story, visuals, sound, voice acting and gameplay are all top notch.

In this game you control the protagonist, Edith, who is trying to put together the tragic story of her family, the Finchs. While going through her family house, you get to see the story of each family member through their own eyes. By doing this, the game creators change the gameplay experience from story to story. Some of these changes are very minor, but some others are big enough that it may seem that you are playing a different game for the duration of each particular story. This is a good surprise for a game of this type.

Progression of the story is very linear and streamlined but the narrative is very well woven together, even presenting some twists that at least I wasn't expecting, which added to my investment in the story. I didn't feel very touched by the story as some other reviewers did, but this is something very personal and even hard to explain. For example, I wept at some points during Life is Strange 1 and especially Life is Strange: Before the Storm and I'm not entirely sure what triggered it. Probably some connections to my teenage year experiences I would guess, but I digress. What Remains of Edith Finch's story is very well put together, interesting until the end and worth going through it.

I would rate this game a solid 8/10, as the gameplay (especially as it changes from story to story), the sound, the voices and the graphics are all superb. The only thing holding this game from a 10 is it's short length. However, if we compare this game to a movie, which is normally not as long, playing this game is probably more rewarding than any moving you can watch.

I totally recommend this game to any gamer that enjoys story-driven games. I got the game on a discount, I think $5, and for that price it is a steal. I am not sure if I would pay the full 20-dollar asking price, but in the end that decision depends on many factors and is highly individual. In the end, I think It's hard to go wrong by playing this game.\t\t\t\t\t

3. I love the voice acting and Gregory. yes Gregory
4. Not everyone cries; don't expect a lot on that aspect
