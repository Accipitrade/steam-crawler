\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is an emotional and charming game that delivers a poetic and creative storytelling experience. The pacing is calm and perfectly balanced, allowing the player to fully immerse themselves in the story.

The graphics are detailed and well-crafted, although some objects repeat frequently, and the dark atmosphere can be a bit overwhelming at times. However, the different styles used for each sub-story add depth and variety to the visuals.

The sound design is adequate, but the music is the standout feature here, with a lovely score that perfectly complements the game's emotional tone. The voice acting is also top-notch, with convincing performances that bring the characters to life.

Gameplay in What Remains of Edith Finch is extremely simplified, with the player only required to walk and interact with the environment. While this lack of complexity may put some players off, the game makes up for it with its occasional creative use of controls to follow the on-screen action. However, playing with a controller can feel a bit unresponsive at times.

Replay value is hurt by the fact that achievements are easy to get, and there's no incentive to play through the game again once it's completed.

Overall, What Remains of Edith Finch is a beautiful and emotional experience that is well worth playing for fans of poetic and creative storytelling. While the gameplay is simplified, the story, graphics, and sound design are all top-notch, creating a memorable and engaging experience that will stay with you long after the credits roll.\t\t\t\t\t
