\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is an adventure, walking simulator story that I find powerful and comforting all at once. The mechanics of the game are simple, the story makes you both laugh and cry, and the ending and the takeaway is so unbelievably powerful.

The game play is probably one of my favorite styles of game play. I personally love games where I don't have to solve some complicated, over the top puzzle or any puzzle at all, I am simply a character who is walking around the area of the game and learning the story as I go, looking and examining objects or learning about the characters involved. This game is exactly that, it is not complicated, it is simply just a walk around and find out style. It is cozy in art style, the main narrators voice is calming, the whole game is just perfect for if you need something to sit and play after a long day or if you want something quick and easy, not much thought.

The story, characters, and ending takeaway, however, is the opposite of simple. Each character is so different from each other and learning about their lives and their ends are something that brings me to tears. Each of these characters has their own takeaway that each play a huge part in the overall takeaway. Would I recommend this story to young children? No, this game looks deeply into death and grief, but I do recommend this game for people who are teens and young adults. I feel that, especially those who are finally finding their place in the world, everyone can find some part of this story that they can relate to, even as an adult. I recommend this game, a perfect 10/10 that no walking simulator game can compare to. For those who want a game more recommended for children, I recommend Giant Sparrows other game, The Unfinished Swan.\t\t\t\t\t
