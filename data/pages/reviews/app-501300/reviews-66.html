\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow.
This is...I'm not even sure how to describe this game. I cried several times while playing it, but I am also a sensitive person with siblings so that may just be projection on my part.
Learning about the history of the house and the family that had lived inside is both haunting and interesting. Despite the main character walking slowly, I was immersed enough in the story, mystery, and beautiful scenery of this game that I didn't really mind it.
I'm leaving this game saddened and curious, which I think is exactly what the developers intended. There is enough threads to piece together your own narrative about what is happening there, but not enough to be concrete. That ambiguity is difficult to do well and I really applaud the devs for it. It's ambiguous enough for different theories to appear.
Gameplay-wise, this is less of a game and more of an interactive story, if that makes sense. There's not really any choices to make that can impact the world, nor any puzzles to solve (gameplay-wise, not story-wise!) in order for the plot to continue.
If you're here for the story, the mystery, and for some emotions, this game is going to play well for you!\t\t\t\t\t
