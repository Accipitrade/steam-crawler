\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I know I will try to better understand this game, in fact, I'm probably gonna go search it up on Youtube right after writing this review, but in this moment I just want to leave here my brief, unorganized feelings immediately after finishing it.
We've been learning about magical realism in English class recently, and all of us were trying to figure out what such stories looked like. Well, after playing this game, I think I understand it now. The marriage of the mystical and the realistic, of raw human emotion which dazzling visuals, of characters who live on in spite of (because of?) the chaotic, unknowable world they inhabit, this game is magical realism if I've ever seen it. And it's a story told so beautifully, one that is so immersive due to its interactive nature that I felt all the glorious moments and violent ends. It is visceral, unflinching in the face of great tragedy, yet it is also quiet, allowing plenty of time for reflection and contemplation.
I am certain I will understand What Remains of Edith Finch better in the future, its story and its themes. But this, my emotions, I must leave here immediately. Cause these things are fleeting, they live in the moment, unless written down, for then they have form.
Thank you for reading, I hope you experience this story for yourself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt this point there is very little that can be said about this game that hasn't been already, but here goes.....

What Remains of Edith Finch is a walking simulator with a decent amount of actual game-play in it, rather than just wandering around passively observing everything (there are many "minigames" within the larger story).

The graphics are decent (not awe-inspiring, but quite sufficient for the task), the music is lovely, and the story-line (exploring your child-hood home where numerous members of your family have met their mysterious demise and exploring their stories) is awesome.

A minor quibble mind you in regards to the story....this falls into the category of "magic realism", where strange, sometimes ominous/eerie things occur and are just taken as a given. Nobody questions the fantastical and borderline grotesque things that happen to/in this family. This can be a bit of a turn off from time to time, and it meant that while I still enjoyed the game (very much so), I wasn't in love with it. Ultimately the uneven atmospheric tone (is it funny, is it ominous, is it tragic?) resulted in too much inconsistency in mood and overall ambience for me to truly love it. Instead I just really like it :)

Recommended, but beware the not-quite realistic, not-quite magical, not-quite horrific story segments. As I said, it's a good game, but if it is at all possible, it may actually add up to less than the sum of its parts.\t\t\t\t\t
