\r\n\t\t\t\t\tConstantly fresh, charming, clever, and touching, this is interactive fiction as good as I've seen. It's not a walking simulator anymore than Bioshock is. It's not "more of a movie than a game." This is a game mechanically constructed to tell the story it houses. Uncovering the curious tales behind the deaths of the Finch family in all of their bittersweet magicality is like taking an introspective stroll through a quiet corner of Disney World while pondering the last time that you and your ex lover were happy together. But like, in the best pixie dust gut punch way possible.

Strongly recommended if you liked:
Firewatch
The Beginner's Guide
The Stanley Parable
Event[0]
Tacoma
Or if you felt like you WOULD'VE liked Gone Home if there was just a little more "game" to it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a short, atmospheric walking sim without much actual gameplay. It's essentially an Edward Gorey-style tale of how a bunch of people died (I thought it was rather a homage to Gorey's Alphabet book--"A is for Amy who fell down...."). The stories are very artistic and beautiful visually, although I hoped for a more cohesive narrative. All the loose threads of story never really tied together. It took me only about 2 hours to finish the game, so I recommend picking it up on sale. It's definitely not worth $20, given that it's a linear narrative with nothing to do at all but move forward and learn more about the family's gruesome history.\t\t\t\t\t

Even in dark moments this game feels whimsical, like a story book. I really like this game's Pacific Northwest setting which gives it a similar aesthetic to other games I enjoyed like Life is Strange, Oxenfree, and the previopusly mentioned Gone Home. I only have 2.4 hours on record so I technically could return this game and get my money back but I don't think I will, this is a really great game, I really enjoyed it.

I've revisited this game after buying it a year ago and my god, it's still amazing, I'm speechless\t\t\t\t\t
