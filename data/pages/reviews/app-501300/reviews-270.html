\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVisually very pretty, great storytelling, fantastic world building, great voice acting, mixes familial love and melancholy and nostalgia and tragedy and loyalty and impending doom and -somehow- makes it all OK

*possible cons to note - this is not an action or thriller title, super linear, story driven, not terribly long (3.5 hours with some individual story replays), you can't be too terribly picky about things like the laws of physics or medical professionalism - just roll with it

*here there be mild spoilers*
The story told is about the *almost* last member of the Finch family, Edith. Edith was taken away from her family home as a child, so in the game, she returns to learn about the seemingly cursed family members who lived and died in the house, and to come to terms with her own history there. We tag along on Edith's journey, being treated to the surreal adventures-into-the-great-beyond of each member of the family, and playing charming mini-games as she goes. My favorite was probably Lewis - his everyday interests and the worlds he built for himself out of the mundane and his tragic sadness just made him kind of relatable.

I wish Edith's journal had told us a bit more about what her own illustration would have looked like and what her own mini-game would have been, but other than that, I very much enjoyed the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm very torn. This is a hard recommendation, because there are some really cool moments in this game. The element of the mundane day job combined with controlling a kind of RPG was particularly memorable, as were some of the child stories (the kite flying one is very charming.... to a point of course).

But ultimately? This game feels like it's 3/4 done. There's not really an ending. And the curse, which is mentioned a lot in the narrative, is very unclear. Sometimes, the curse takes someone's life via accident. But much of the time, the "curse" is just the human condition. The challenge there is that the game doesn't do a good job of stating that. The ending in particular is very weak, suggesting that you're about to learn something very potent but ultimately snuffing the candle too soon.

But the gameplay? Fascinating and charming. At times, very captivating. And the sound is a big part of the enjoyment.

Ultimately, I think I have to recommend it. I do so begrudgingly, but I feel that I'd be dishonest to say this game isn't enjoyable.\t\t\t\t\t
