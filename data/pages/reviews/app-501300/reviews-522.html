\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my first review for any game I have owned on Steam. I just finished What Remains of Edith Finch and have to say it is the worst game I have played in a very long time. To call it a "game" is a complete joke. There are zero choices to make and it takes zero skill to finish. You would get the exact same value from this game by watching someone walk (the move speed is painfully slowly) through the game on Youtube and treating it like a movie. On top of that, the story is very bad and leaves a lot of open ended questions at the end. It took me 116 minutes to finish and although the scenes are "pretty" to watch, I did not enjoy any of that time. Save yourself the money, this one is VERY over priced and over rated.

Pros
-Great graphics
-Interesting dream like scenery

Cons
-Everything else\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is amazing! Top production quality for the game of this type. Very imaginative, has some really, really great ideas.

I woldn't say this game falls under category of "walking simulators". I'd say it's a full-blown adventure, because it has varying gameplay elements, although it's entirely on rails.

I give it 10 dead relatives out of 10.

Now, I'd like to share a bit of critique, but this will require spoilers, so please beware




[SPOILERS]

The only issue I have with this game is this "curse" thing which never got explained. At first I thougt that the whole family had some kind of inherited down syndrome, because they all died under stupid circumstances, from stupid mistakes, or generally doing really stupid and dangerous things, without having enough brain cells to consider the consequences.

The game tries to build a mystery around this curse, related to the old sunken house, but never explains or even hints about it. I think the opportunity of visiting the old house and finding out about the truth is the biggest missed opportunity for this game. There was a really nice build-up for this, and then it ends abruptly.

Other than this, as I've said, brilliant! I really loved the idea of building new rooms atop of the base house for the new family members, which forms a tower going higher and higher. I'd love to live in the house like this, surrounded by the beautiful scenery... mmm... magnificent!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdith Finch is the only one left alive in her family. Why did everybody die? What's the deal with the house? Who are all the Finches? Get ready to walk around and find out, never closing the doors behind you as you progress!

Cowards die many times before their deaths; the valiant never taste of death but once. -William Shakespeare

Pros
+ Cool story. That's the main attraction here, everything else is supporting.
+ Good voice acting.
+ Storytelling is done through the environment. The important bits are called out, but there's things you can catch on to before they are explicitly mentioned.
+ Graphics. Separate from being able to use the graphics to tell a story, it's just cool to look at the art.
+ Music was good. I don't think it's something I'd listen to outside of the game, but it's fine if the music keeps to the background and sets the mood like it's supposed to.
+ Achievements and cards.

Meh
? Not a pure walking simulator, in the sense that there are several sequences where you do things besides walking around. If you're expecting a walking simulator, sorry to tell you but you're going to have to figure out the controls and objectives of a few minigames in order to progress. Nothing difficult, and these do help keep the story engaging because some of them are such that walking simply won't cut it. Did it break the flow? Not really, since each minigame is for a different story so it's like a nice framing mechanism. Most people would say this is a net positive, but I'm not so sure I'm sold on the idea.
? Hard to miss the important bits. You might miss a little bit of the side narration, but this game is very linear in terms of how you discover it. With the exception of a single branching path near the start, there's always only one way to go to move forward. This is neither good nor bad, because it's a story game and this is how it chooses to tell the story.

Cons
- I got some bad motion sickness during one of the sequences. Thankfully, it was over pretty quickly.
- Predictable. I'm usually gullible and slow to pick up on obvious clues, but it felt obvious to me what was going to happen in some of the sequences. You know how when you see a bed you know exactly what's going to happen on it? It was kind of like that.
- I got stuck in one of the walking simulator parts because the character would not progress down a particular path. I must have been standing in the wrong place. It was obvious where I should be going, but after attempting to go that route and finding I couldn't, I ended up wandering aimlessly until I came back to the path and went down it.
- Part of the interactivity in this game is that you have to follow through with the motion of the interactive object once you start interacting with it. For example, you need to swing the mouse over in order to open a door. I'm not sure if it was my settings or what, but it felt like I've have to swing my mouse WAY more than I should need to in order to complete some tasks. Maybe you will disagree and think that it helps immersion, but I don't think this added anything to the game. There are two times when this matters (winding the music box and reading the pop-up book), and in all other times I know exactly how I'm going to interact with the object because there is only one way to interact with this object, and making me slide the door open isn't very compelling gameplay.
-The price. If you dither a lot, this might take you four hours. If you don't dither, you can see all of the content in three hours. If you go kind of quickly, you can get done in two and still see 90% of the content. Now, a lot of work went into polishing this game, and I'm happy to support the developers, but to be clear, you're paying for quality and not quantity.

Verdict
Oh man, I don't even know. Obviously the review is marked as a positive because it delivered on the premise and I couldn't really find anything fundamentally wrong with it, but this one is kind of a hard sell. Recommended, but only if you really like this kind of storytelling, and if you either really want to support the developers or can grab it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
