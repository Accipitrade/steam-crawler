\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a masterpiece. The music is outstanding and establishes the tone and intensity of any part of the game perfectly. The art style is original but I personally loved it. From the beginning, the game hits you with great voice acting and striking visuals. In the beginning of the game, barely anything is explained to you why things are the way they are. You get to come up with your own theories of what happened, but the way the story is revealed to you is purely genius. At the end of the game, one more twist is thrown your way that is slightly expected but still impactful to the story by proving a central idea and giving you more understanding to the game as a whole. This game has a major focus on family, bonds, trust, and loss. I would recommend this game to anyone who enjoys atmospheric and story driven game. I would recommend playing this game in one sitting, it is only 2 hours long and is quite relaxing to play. I would give What Remains of Edith Finch 9/10.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow. WRoEF is an amazing game! The storytelling is really good and easy to follow along with, the music is beautifully chosen, and graphically the game is gorgeous! Perhaps my favorite thing about this game is that you always know where you need to go. One thing I hate most about games is when they expect you to find your own way to advance the story, which just leads to confusion. My only gripe with this game, Edith is 17 and pregnant. Really? You couldn't make her at least one or two years older? Underage pregnant girls really rubs me the wrong way. I did want to thank the developers for letting me experience being a baby and traveling through the vagina to be born. That's not something I ever thought I'd experience in a game. LOL.

I highly, highly recommend this game if you're into storytelling games. This was so good!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not sure if it's appropriate to call What Remains of Edith Finch a game, it is rather an interactive story. That said, the story is so well crafted that it is worth the full asking price. (Lewis's section alone is a gem that would justify buying this title, and the others are not much weaker.)
If you like piecing together the bits of narrative in order to find rational answers (and I do believe that there are rational answers to almost all questions raised in the story), then you'll surely like What Remains of Edith Finch.\t\t\t\t\t
