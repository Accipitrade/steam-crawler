\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was awesome. It's a really fun narrative story game with a really well thought-out story. First and foremost, it's a story game based on a lovingly crafted linear path, so it has no action. Make note of that before you buy if you've never played a "movie game" as I call them. It's about a girl who has returned to her childhood home after years of being gone, and she explores her old house and the rooms of her family members, learning about how, and why, she is the only person in her family left alive. The game flows in a specific way in that you control Edith, the main character, as she wanders the house. You then take control over each of her family members as she learns about them and you play out their final moments on the day of their death. It can be fairly depressing, but in a very beatiful way.

With a game like this, it relies almost entirely on the narrative, so it's imperative that it does good in that regard, and it does very, very well in that regard. It's very creative and isn't the kind of story you'd expect from either a game or movie. You're guaranteed to be throroughly entertained the entire time.

One thing I like about it that sets it apart from other movie games is that it has legit gameplay in it, but it isn't the kind of gameplay you'd expect. Each family member that you take control of has a different gameplay or storytelling mechanic that keeps the game fresh as you play through each small chapter. From being a small child playing with the toys in the bathtub, to her older brother who works at a fish canning factory where you use one joystick to control his arm as he does his job, and the other joystick to control his "self" and walk around in his imagination. All-in-all, like...pretty much everyone else, I'd consider this game a masterpiece.

Also, btw, the game runs perfectly and looks really good.\t\t\t\t\t
