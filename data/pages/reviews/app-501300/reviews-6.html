An anthology of demise

The best stories are daintily told with a bittersweet tone. Edith Finch expresses with a solemn phrase when passing through the dining room, paraphrasing, everyone fled as if a bomb fell, killing everyone and leaving the furniture safe. The house is abandoned and she returns to discover about her family's past. They all depart this life tragically because of a curse. Many of them are young (and some are old) and perish in ways never thought of.

As she exclaims at the beginning with morose air, everything I tell you it seems to make no sense. Believe it or not, in that family the obsession with death and the acceptance of its destiny were born. This is how an adventure begins through each room, each family member, which compose the story of the Finch as an anthology: a collection of short stories. Edith wants to uncover the family secrets kept with fear and prohibition.

Tree house

The protagonist received a key and with it an advice note. The way the story takes us by the hand is surprising: a preamble in atmospheric sceneries while a colourful and outlandishly shaped house steals our gaze. Upon entering, we notice the simple but well-ordered details of things in each space and room of the house. There are many books and family photographs, which gives that satisfactory impression of visiting someone else's house. I was a visitor. This is how I felt immersed, participating in the adventure, while lines of dialogue about what ensued there as memories emerge without interrupting my contemplation. Her words are displayed visually in the scene to get the player's attention.

As I go further, the rooms have their intimate style. As I climb, such places also change shape until they become smaller and unimaginable. A surreal house wants to break the rules of logic, sense and gravity. The house has underhand passages, but it was always growing. As for lighting, it’s between light and dark. But it’s always oriented to a colour palette with meanings of magical realism. Each room is a world with a story to tell. Things speak of their owner. This is akin to living and interacting with short stories that are quite enjoyable once inside the room locked off by Edith's mother. It undoubtedly offers an experience where each player will partake their own involvements and feelings in the game. But never leaving its story aside.

When rooms tell their own story

An indie game presents us that regardless of resources, imagination and creativity play an essential role. We realize this through its art, design of the house and narrative. So, in terms of the plot structure, I noticed that it’s in a shape of a tree. It takes the family tree as a simile, whose journey begins with some ancestors until it reaches Edith's brother. The story moves almost chronologically. What bonds everyone in common is the tragedy, where step by step, room by room its story it’s revealed. The story oscillates between reality, fantasy but at the same time a bit of something supernatural that embroils the family.

However, the mode of telling the story has been brilliantly amalgamated with the gameplay. The gameplay is modest and unassuming, since it’s based on the walking simulator and the interaction with some objects. As each room wants to tell its story, at the same time a personal gameplay flourishes in each of them. Nevertheless, I embodied in each character one by one of the rooms as the story progresses and its surprise was that took me with a multifaceted gameplay. Thus, monotony or repeating the same formulas was avoided. Also each voice is different, there’re many narrators, so it’s polyphonic. One room offers a mini game. I don't want to go into details, as the experience of that gameplay also becomes personal.

In the end I’m discovering the plot in the form of pieces that come together to make up the whole of the story. The game allows to reach a diversity of interpretations and conclusions. As I said, the game is also a personal journey. Each will suffer some greater impact than another in some part of the story. An anthology with stories that invite to wonder, muse and feel in a unique and incomparable way. It’s a sober and modest work, influenced by magical realism literature. This is how it expresses the unreal and unheard of a cursed family.

The melody for an eerie family

Despite the tragic theme, the game doesn't really turn into horror. Jeff Russo was the composer for the game's soundtrack. The music experience concedes a feeling of being inside a movie. Many of the musical pieces are played in the form of a loop. But it doesn’t repeat itself regarding the use of score music, but it tries to make such melodies sound continuously. That way the music is part of the narrative and doesn't just accentuate the drama of the game, like the Lewis story or the Halloween-like setting of Barbara's story.

Silence, however, also plays an important role. Thus, music is presented to accentuate the expressions of family members but it’s always born from silence. Since the soundtrack tries to lean into the narrative of magical realism. The music always underlines each character, but it also expresses the tragedy of each one with sounds in the form of emotional streams. While the silence gives way to the organic noises of our footsteps. It tries to be as natural as possible. I could say that there’re a diversity of musical pieces for each character. Some are atmospheric performed on piano or strings, and other times festive through wind instruments. It’s always moving in adagio, andante or larghetto. The entire soundtrack is powerful, beautiful and impressive.

What remains

The story is quite short. Although each story as a mini game is pretty enjoyable. I liked how sometimes words can make things. Or how some stories can cause shock or astonishment. The game tries to express the emotions of the characters. That comes to us with which it causes us an emotional impact by living them (through interaction) as well as listening to them. It’s an adventure with a wonderful story, which takes influence from some writers like Kafka, García Márquez, Saramago, Rushdie, Kundera or Murakami. It’s a good extrapolation between game and literature, game and cinema, and reality with the unreal that the Finchs lived every day after the death of a brother, sister or relative.

Recommendation: 9/10

What Remains of Edith Finch? Answer: A Lot

Time to write another review, but I'll make it short this time.

Let's get the main point out of the way, which is the story.

Story

The starting leaves you dumbfounded as the narrative drops you straight into the story without much context - as you progress through the game however, everything eventually starts to make sense. I could maybe just be dumb and such details just flew over my head, but that is the whole theme of the game. It's a story with a heavy dash of mystery. It should also be noted that the story is very heavy in emotion, in particular heavy in mental issues, so its probably good to know your limits and sensitivity when jumping into this game. As for me, there was a few parts in the story where it really got emotional, but not enough for me to cry, fortunately. The ending does leave sort of a cliffhanger though, but maybe the point of it was to leave it up to interpretation.

Visuals + Music (because there isn't much to mention)

Visuals are very stunning even at the lowest settings. (I obviously played at higher settings - I wanted a full experience.) The visuals play a key role in carrying the story. All I have to say about it is that it's absolutely beautiful. As for the music, it's very fitting and is also musically very beautiful.

Conclusion
What Remains of Edith Finch is an almost perfect game. Like most users, I'd say the only cons to it is the length of gameplay - two hours, in which I personally think should have been expanded more to give a more complete structure to the story - and the price of the game. I got this during the Summer Sale, but still RM11 is a little much (in my country at least) for a game this short.

Still, this is probably one of the best purchases I've made this Summer Sale. If you don't get this during a sale, you're honestly missing out on a lot.

8/10 \t\t\t\t\t
