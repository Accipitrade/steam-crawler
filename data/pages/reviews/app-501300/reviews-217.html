\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDamn.

For $20, is it worth *buying*? Hard to say. The game only has 2 hours of gameplay for your first run, and maybe more if you want to replay the same stories over and over. The game is also very linear, further decreasing replayability. Its hard to judge it like you would any other game, because it really isn't, but I'd say that what it lacks in time consumption, it makes up for in a gripping story that leaves you wanting more. Its more similar to a movie than a video game, but does utilize gameplay in some inventive ways to draw people in, such as Lewis' sequence. I think that above all, this game is a monument and idol for what other narrative-driven games should try to put into their stories. Not a lot of cliches here, just intense and emphatic writing. The story leaves you with some hard-hitting thoughts and feelings about life, childhood, and the nature of bliss, and was an experience I think I can safely put up alongside other major hits like Undertale. Its an amazing tale, but maybe not an amazing game.

Regardless, I recommend What Remains of Edith Finch. Set aside 3 hours and 20 dollars, sit down, and ride this short but sweet rollercoaster. I don't think you will regret it.\t\t\t\t\t
