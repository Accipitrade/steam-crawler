It is quite a sad tale, and pulled me emotionally into some fairly dark, unexpected directions. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell... you cannot know whether to buy it or not without a spoiler. So this review does contain a bit of one.

This interactive story certainly has style. I tells a couple of (very) short stories of people who have died. The stories are told in interesting ways and they do not overstay their welcome. Their connection via a metaphorical and physically impossible house works nicely as well.

So far so good. Unfortunately, the stories do not "hit you" particularly hard because they share no common elements and do not seem to take place in the same universe. Some are more or less realistic (an accident, freak accident, suicide and some sicknesses) and some are meant to be mysterious. Some of the latter are meant to sound lovecraftian and some go for more of a magical feeling. This disconnect robs the the individual stories of their emotional impact because they all feel very artifical. What keeps the whole story afloat is the narrator telling you that there is a common theme that you cannot see just yet. So you keep walking through the house-metaphor and listen to the sub standard mystery stories (that are brought to life beautifully) and wait for the reveal.

Unfortunately, the game ends without connecting the stories AT ALL. At the end, the writers even pretend to start an explanation and then they just do not. Instead, the story ends without it ever being a story. Looking back it is just a collection of non-sensical short stories about people dying under rather unclear circumstances connected by beautiful interludes. The spoiler is, that there is no spoiler. There is no twist. There is no story.

The metaphoric house is done beautifully and so is the voice-over. It feels harsh to call this interactive novel out on the shallowness of its staginess. Still, that is what it is.\t\t\t\t\t
