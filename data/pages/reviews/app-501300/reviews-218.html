What Remains of Edith Finch is short, sweet and full of interesting mini-stories that tie together. At it's core, it's a walking simulator and you'll spend most of your time in a generally linear fashion being engrossed on the story of the Finches. Where it really shines are the mini-game like moments involving some of the family members that have you exploring the world from their perspective. Without spoiling anything, it's hard to describe and something I think that needs to be experienced first hand without prior knowledge of what's coming, but I'll say my two favorites were Lewis and Gregory, with Molly and Barbara being close runners up.

You'll spent about 2-3 hours playing through it. Everyone has their own idea of value, but $20 seems a bit high for such a short experience. For me, I felt the environment created, the story it told and then enjoyment I got out of it was worth it the price I paid, $9 on a Steam sale. To me it's a one time through and done experience, but others might find value in playing through a second time.

All in all, I recommend it if you like interactive stories. It's just a well make, pleasant experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best narrative first person walking sims of all time, and one that leaves a lasting impression even after the game has switched off. Never before has the idea of revisiting the deaths of relatives been presented in such a creative way in a game - not only with the ways you control each relative and how they interact with the world in the moments leading up to their demise, but also with the immense amount of visual story details to uncover that are never addressed or explained. Obviously, it's still a walking sim with not much else going for it with replayability and choices in HOW you play; but in how it tells a fascinating story in an interactive medium, this is one not to miss.
