\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch wants to be an interactive visual novel. An emotional story with interesting characters that uses interactivity to pull the player much more deeply into the plot than a novel or a movie ever could. And it has much of that: There are heartbreaking moments, and genuinely funny ones (and some that are actually both). There are memorable characters and at least a few scenes that show how interactivity can greatly enrich a story. But unfortunately, that's where the developers stopped. The lack of a strong overarching storyline, the lack of a conclusion and a proper ending make this feel more like a collection of short stories than a novel. Like a set of beautifully crafted puzzle pieces that just won't fit together.

So what remains of Edith Finch? Some memorable moments and characters, an idea of what the future of the genre might hold for us, and the disappointing feeling that this game could have been so much more.

It's still good at what it is though. As long as you keep in mind that it's just a loose collection of story snippets, you'll have a great time. Just don't expect it to be the next To The Moon.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate:
Ok, finished the game. Well, the first impression (see the original review below) was hinting at something truly great. I was convinced this will be better than The Vanishing of Ethan Carter (closest comparison, imho). It was not. Carter is longer and more varied in terms of gameplay and imagination. I wasn't impressed by the conclusion of Edith Finch either. It's a (slightly over the top) family drama, but not as wildly creative as that of Ethan Carter. The game is constructed of a series of personal stories and although some are truly mindblowing, others are completely uninspired or plain lazy. So play this, sure, but if you haven't played Ethan Carter, then consider it an absolute must. Carter is the best game of its kind as of January 2018, imho. It looks much better too.

Original review:
If this is a walking simulator, then Grim Fandango or Myst are clicking simulators while a bartender's job at a small neighbourhood pub is best described as "standing". Forget the genre pioneers a'la Dear Esther. This next to them is like Portal 2 next to Tetris. Ok, I'm exaggerating a bit, but you get the point....\t\t\t\t\t
