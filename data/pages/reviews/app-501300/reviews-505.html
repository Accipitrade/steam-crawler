\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow did a team of developers extract all the poignant wonder, imagination, adventure and tragedy from different stages of my childhood and stuff it into a video game? Looks like someone has been poking around in places they shouldn't be.

I recommend you play this game in one sitting. Easy to do because it's short and easy to absorb. And yet it leaves you with a lot to think about regarding life -and death- in general.

The detail of the world is tremendous. From the tennis ball hanging in the garage to the crappy insulation-board you encounter in the attic, the essence of a big old eccentric family house filled with childhood memories is captured perfectly.

If you're younger than 20, you probably won't get the most out of this game. But if you think you've grown old and responsible, you need to play it. It's beautiful to look at, intriguing, sad, hopeful all at the same time. I feel that I've recaptured a sense of magic that I had lost.

Thank you, Giant Sparrow, and well done.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBe wary of this one. It has the potential to hit you like a freight train. There's a chance you'll have a tough time telling whether or not you're enjoying it at first--the story is great from the get-go, but maybe the gameplay is lacking? Maybe something else is missing? It won't matter. You'll walk away from it and realize that it followed you whether you wanted it to or not. It is less a "game" or even an "interactive story" . . . it is simply an experience, and, as such, needs to be experienced.

I was in love with this game from the moment I laid eyes upon the bookshelves in the Finch family's living area: Infinite Jest, The King in Yellow, Gravity's Rainbow, The Metamorphosis, even my all-time favorite House of Leaves was in attendance. The atmosphere is beyond stunning. Each character you explore gets his/her own world with a distinctly different feel (and font!) to it, all of which are unique, charming, and believable (though the stories you hear may not be). Some are more engrossing than others, but they all deliver in their own way. You will probably laugh at a few bits. Possibly you'll cry. Most definitely you'll have some moments of crippling numbness. But that's life for you.

I don't think I can pick this game apart enough to come up with anything negative about it. Sure there may have been a missed opportunity here or there or a segment with wonky controls, but honestly it feels irrelevant. It's just a beautiful world to explore, and everything seems to blend together seamlessly. I've seen some negative feedback about the ending, that it's "predictable" and the like. I made the mistake of reading a plot synopsis before playing, so I already knew what to expect. The effect was not lessened. The voyage made it real. Every single story has already been told in some way or another; the secret now is finding a new voice in which to tell it. The voice of Edith Finch is one you'll be happy to hear, even with tears in your eyes.\t\t\t\t\t
