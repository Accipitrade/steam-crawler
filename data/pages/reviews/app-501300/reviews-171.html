Better experienced by watching a walkthrough

Technical
Doesn't run well through Proton, lots of stuttering and freezes.

Gameplay
Controls are awkward. I assume they are calibrated for the completely over the top mouse sensitivity most people foolishly tend towards.
This gets very tiring as soon as the gimmick of "open door by moving your mouse up (and similar gesture driven events)" ceases being novel. And let's be honest, that's basically after the first such event.
They are annoying as is, but having to traverse my mousepad thrice, for something that could have been resolved with a single click, is an inexcusably dumb design decision.
Another annoyance is the "game" wrestling away your controls because it thinks it needs to shove some text into your face.
Movement speed is absolutely laughable and is probably responsible for artificially padding the games length by 1/3.
Gotta stretch the average completion time over 2 hours so people can't refund or something.

Stroy
Story seems pretty nice actually, sadly completely wasted as a game.
Would have made for a phenomenal mini-series or similar. I can imagine it working well with different animation styles for each of the sub-stories.
One thing that was annoying is getting the opinion of the main character shoved into your face all the time. Little room left to draw your own conclusions, despite there being plenty of environmental clues enabling you to do so.

Conclusion
Watch a "no commentary walkthrough", or something similar. As with most walking simulators there is absolutely no value gained by playing it yourself. Watching a playthrough will also allow you to fast forward through all the painfully slow walking.\t\t\t\t\t
