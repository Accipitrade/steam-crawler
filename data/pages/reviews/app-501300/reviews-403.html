\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is the kind of game any fan of walking simulators hopes to find. The game has an engaging story that makes it hard to put the game down until it's finished. You play the titular character Edith Finch, who has returned to her childhood home to learn how all the members in her family died. This sounds like an odd place to go to explore such a thing until you learn of a tradition her family has. Each time someone died their room was turned into a shrine. Forever trapped in the day each individual died. This allows the player to assume the role of each dead family member and play out how each one of them died.

Now an engaging story is just one thing that makes a good walking simulator. The other important part is having a beautiful environment. One that makes you want to explore every inch of the game you can possibly reach. What Remains of Edith Finch has just such an environment. Every room in the house is filled little tidbits of information that tells you even more about the people who once lived in the house. The environment and story come together to make sure each family member’s death is told in a unique way. In one you assume different animal forms as you explore the nightmare of a dying child. Another is told in a horror story comic book format.

To me the only negative of the game is how short it is. On average this game only takes about 2 hours to complete. Yet the story and environment will leave you wishing Edith Finch had a bigger family tree to explore. Still the stories that are told make What Remains of Edith Finch worth the money spent to play it.\t\t\t\t\t
