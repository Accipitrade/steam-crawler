\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverrated game.

I've played better walking simulators. Like Gone Home, in which you had some real investment in the characters and in the mystery. In this game however if you scratch the surface of good graphics and some cool effects, there isn't much worthy your attention. There are too many characters for a short playing time, so you can imagine none of them had a real personality, each one was just a different death in the family tree. After 2 or three deaths, it get repetitive. Why so much tragedy though? You get no explanation, the game seems to tell you "despite how strange life can be, be glad that you were even alive in the first place", then a shot of the next Finch wandering in the cemetery. By the logic of the history he's gonna end up dying in a terrible way if he even lives past childhood (the trickiest part of a Finch life), so happy ending I guess?

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hate to be the spoilsport, because so many people seem to love this game. I didn’t – And I was very surprised that I didn’t, because normally I’m fairly easy to impress.

First off, the positive things:
+ I liked the design. The house is very beautifully designed and decorated. It looks mysterious and cozy at once.
+ The setting is intriguing. The overall *vibe* is very cool, mysterious and also a little creepy. It was actually a lot creepier than I expected. I didn’t know what exactly I did expect, but it wasn’t this.
+ I loved the soundtrack. That I’ll admit. The soundtrack is beautiful, relaxing and eerie and it very much fits the game.
+ Every part of the story (every family member’s death narration) came with a different kind of “mini game”. That bit was okay. I liked it, actually.

But that’s about it. Let’s come to the negatives:
- The story. It *really* didn’t feel complete. I feel like it was trying very hard to be deep and meaningful but it wasn’t. It was missing the golden thread. There were too many ambiguous things left open for the players interpretation. I was waiting for an explanation for at least *some* of the mysterious phenomena that have been introduced to us – that we have been teased with! But there were none. I couldn’t find a deep, hidden message or some sort of “moral lesson”. I don’t understand what the game was trying to tell us, really. Was there really some sort of curse on the family? Or are they just a bunch of unlucky and irresponsible people? I don’t know, and unfortunately the game didn’t manage to make me care about it either ://
- The final twist was the most predictable thing ever
- The key-binding, oh my god, it was horrible! I don’t know about controllers, but with mouse and keyboard, it was really one of the worst I’ve ever seen. There was a moment in a mini game where I pressed literally every key on my keyboard because I couldn’t find out what to do. It ended up being one of the arrow keys, which should never ever even be assigned in a video game…

I feel bad because so many people love this game? I find something enjoyable in almost ALL the games I ever played and I barely ever leave negative reviews. I really don’t understand the hype over this one though. The whole game feels very vague and like it’s trying to be something that it doesn’t end up being. The pretty design and atmosphere serve as a shell for a meaningless story ☹

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those games where you wish you could replay it with a blank slate so you can get that full emotional punch to the gut all over again, as if it's brand new.

I was emotionally affected while playing it, and then even more so afterwards when I sat there and just thought about this family and the events of the game. I looked up the theories fans have come up with. I read people discuss what specific events affected them the most, from teenagers to parents and grandparents. This game is absolutely one of the most emotionally impactful games I've played in a very long time.

To add onto that, it's a beautiful game in itself.
The house is gorgeous, yet haunting. It's empty and lonely, yet still full of life.

Of course, there's the age-old complaint of a fairly short game (approximately 2 hours) being priced at $20. However, you're buying a 'walking simulator' that's entirely focused on telling its story. The story, gameplay, gorgeous art, and enduring emotional impact are all definitely worth the full price.\t\t\t\t\t
