\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a tragic and at times disturbing tale about family with its extended members and how sometimes despite best efforts to escape fate; it’s already written.
Throughout the 2 to 3 hours of exploration, you’ll uncover the singular stories of the Finch family members portrayed by unique gameplay methods and art directions. This refreshing style keeps what can sometimes be a one trick pony genre, to be special amongst its walking sim peers.
The diverse range of vocal narration telling each story, is impressive coupled with intuitive interaction controls around the mansion solving the quirky area transition puzzles are fun. The sheer amount of painstaking artistic effort that must have gone into the house is stunning, although the character models are slightly lacking detail. The haunting coastal sounds and overarching piano score adds to an immersive experience, recommended to use a headset.
We all know what to expect when you buy/play a game of this genre, you won’t stand up afterwards and feel happy about the world but complete it in one focused sitting and you may take away a special personal message about family. Surely, a good contender a film adaptation and showcases the argument that games can be true art.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI won't talk about the story or the mechanics at all, I'll just give you the premise and the impression the game left on me, then the decision's all yours.

What Remains of Edith Finch tells the story of a family. One that seems to be followed by death for a reason no one knows. As a young woman, you visit the old family's house aiming to discover the story of each of its members. From there on, the game presents itself as a collection of short stories with you being the main thread that sews it all up together.

I played this game some years ago, and I really enjoyed it. For some reason, I had a hunch that it was time to play it again. I couldn't be more happy.

This is a true masterpiece, a game that pushes the medium forward, that's able to commit to its message and design all of its mechanics around it. It doesn't care about how to make the game more sellable or monetizable, its only focus is to convey a story. Its mechanics are daring, its plot is intriguing and it's wonderfully made in terms of graphics, sound and music.

I won't tell anything else, because this is a game about discovery, and I don't want to spoil anything. If you like narrative games, this is a must. It's not perfect, but nothing is, I can only applaud its courage and quality.\t\t\t\t\t
