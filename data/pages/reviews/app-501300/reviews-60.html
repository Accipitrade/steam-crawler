\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was pretty impressed with the game overall. In spite of its very short play time, (I finished it in less than 2 hours) the game fits in a very rich and full story. The writing is done in such a way where each individual persons story fills in a small piece of the overarching story and it does so very well. Many of these stories tend to be very personal and wholesome and even comforting at times, but obviously leave a very bittersweet taste in your mouth after. The voice acting is very good overall, much of it sounding as if that person is telling their own story to you in person. The graphics are pretty decent, but the color pallet choice for each segment is chosen immaculately for each individual story, speaking as to the mood and mental state of each character as you go through their story. Most of the individual's stories are very good, filled with great little details, Lewis' especially. However, there were one or two that felt kind of rushed or thrown in as an afterthought, which brings you out of the overall story pretty harshly. I would say that my two major hits on the game are the play time, which I don't know if everyone will be willing to pay the 20 dollar price tag. The other complaint I have is my previous statement of the one or two afterthought stories. Overall, I'd give it an 8/10 and would definitely give it a go.\t\t\t\t\t
