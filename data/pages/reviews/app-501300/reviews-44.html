\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr:
Absolutely phenomenal experience, more art than a "proper" game but makes excellent use of the medium; about 2-3 hours to complete, but it's top quality 2 hours; depicts artistic renditions/interpretations of deaths of several people, including a baby, children and teens, so it may upset some players.


In more detail:
This (along with the original Stanley Parable) is one of the games that saved me from disregarding all "walking simulators" as pretentious non-games that don't understand how to use their own medium. It tells the story of a family that fell apart over romanticization of untimely deaths of most of its members. Through varied and creative depiction of the circumstances of their passing, you'll get to consider whether there is a curse or whether most of them died as a result of recklessness and parental neglect.

The game is very short, at 2-3 hours, depending on your pace and thoroughness of exploration. You might decide to replay the whole game or some of its memorable moments. There are also missable achievements to try for, but at its core the game is short and linear. On the other hand, it left me with more feels and memories in those 2 hours than any of those 50-100+ hours games with colossal open worlds of copy-pasted assets and throwaway characters and quests. The price-to-length ratio isn't as bad as it might seem, considering the quality of the time spent with this game.

Just a word of warning in case you're sensitive to these things: though largely methaporical and artistic, the sequences do depict deaths of teens, children and even a baby, so bear that in mind if you think it might upset you.\t\t\t\t\t
