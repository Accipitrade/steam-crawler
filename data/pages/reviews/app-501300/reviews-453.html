\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot a game, so much as an story told in game form. But what a story it is! This game demonstrates beautifully the rich storytelling potential of games, as a medium distinct from books and movies. It makes you a participant and in brilliant ways (for example Lewis's story) encourages you to empathize with the characters involved. A good part of the storytelling lies in noticing and piecing together environmental details, so if you play take the time to go slowly and absorb it all in. The history of the Finch family extends over several generations, and working out the timeline -- and seeing how personal effects and memorabilia change with the decades -- provide another layer. Overall play time is about 3 hours if you go slowly, and there is some potential for replay. In my view this game is a masterpiece.\t\t\t\t\t
