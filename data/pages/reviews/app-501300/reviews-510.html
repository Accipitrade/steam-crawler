\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Finch family is cursed. Or maybe they're not. It may just be horrible luck, and the family has found a special way to cope and find meaning in tragedy. Either way, in What Remains of Edith Finch, players take control of Edith while she explores her families abandoned home in search of answers. It plays similar to something like Gone Home, while tonally feeling similar to something like the Tim Burton film Big Fish. This can be seen in the games vignettes, in which you take control of one the other Finch family members, and learn about their untimely deaths. This usually involves magical or fantastical elements. Each vignette also has its own simple but unique gameplay mechanics. There are some other nice touches, like how text from the narration will appear on surfaces like walls, or perhaps the top of a table. Then there's the addition of the Finch family tree on the pause menu that gets filled out as the game progresses. It's obvious that Giant Sparrow put a lot of love into this game, and it’s an easy recommendation for fans of this game genre.\t\t\t\t\t
