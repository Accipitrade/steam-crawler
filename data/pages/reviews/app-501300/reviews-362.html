\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI went into this game not having read a single review or any backstory on it. At first I thought I was in for a walking simulator but then you here a narrator telling you her thoughts as you move around the environment and look at different things. What kept me playing was when I first saw the house on the hill, with all it's extra additions it reminded me of the adventure time tree house and I wanted to explore it.

The house looks like a museum as if the owner had suddenly disappeared. You walk around looking at all the attention to detail when you start to notice rooms are sealed shut with little peep holes to look inside, each one a piece of history like looking into a time bubble of the past. Each room is filled with a story of how it's owner met his/her end but in a roundabout way where it's not so obvious at first.

Towards the end you've pretty much gone through a range of human emotion if you're paying attention to the story and don't have a heart of stone. This game will make you laugh, cry, angry, happy, etc. all within just about 2 hours of game time. It's one of the few game's i'd definitely revisit. All in all, 5 out of 7\t\t\t\t\t
