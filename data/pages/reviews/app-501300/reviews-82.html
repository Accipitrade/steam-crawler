Second up - It is NOT a puzzle game.

Hello champions,
After just finishing 'The eyes of Ara' and "Haven Moon" I was looking for another 'Myst' inspired game and came across this one.

It seemed to fit the bill: overwhelmingly positive reviews, inference to 'discovering what happened to the residents', etc.

But it is in reality: walk, click on the provided light spot, read, go on to the next light spot.
There is nothing to solve.

However, there's fun to be had when interacting with the various videos. You're a shark, now flying a kite, now on a swing, and these and other small things are fun and have a little bit of skill in them.

Many have enjoyed the melancholy of the story, others have despaired at the light treatment of challenging issues and mental stability of some characters and I'll leave that to those others to debate.

It is a 2-5 hour 'walk-through' and some won't like the $20+ price tag for that. I understand, but I also don't mind as these games don't get created in an afternoon and I think all developers should be encouraged otherwise we're left to a big few telling us what they think we need to enjoy.

Why did I pay $20 if review after review said it was a short game? I hear you ask. Good question champions.
Compared to most of you I'm an old old person, older than steam and in all that time people keep saying how quick they've raced through games until I felt it wasn't believable anymore.
"I did myst in 12 hours."
"Yeah, I did it in eight!"
"Well I did it in 15 seconds!"

So I guess I've become 'blind' to the claim of game run through times. (My stats say I've played it for 4.4 hours).
