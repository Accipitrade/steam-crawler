\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat remains of Edith Finch is not a game... it is art. That's what I feel I should be saying, but I felt like a pretentious moustache twirling ♥♥♥♥ when I pumped out that line. Of course it's a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ game. It's a light First Person puzzle game with a fantastically portrayed story.

This border line walking sims, 'puzzles' are pretty easy for the experienced gamer. Most of 'em are just figuring out the control scheme and having a basic understanding of your objectives.

The story outlines a teenage girl's adventure back to her old family homestead. I'm not going to say anymore than that as it's a story best experienced rather than read about in a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ steam review.

Sound design is brilliant, graphics and attention to small details are really good and there's a good variety in gameplay to keep you going. The price is steep, but that's because it's a well crafted game by a small company, not a triple A mega budget piece of crap that's usually churned out by the dozens these days.

I bought this game on sale. I feel guilty. I think the devs should have been given the full price. Buy this game. Do it now. You won't regret it. I promise you that. (Unless you hate stories)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdith Finch is an experience, and it's one I'm glad I had, but I can't say it's a "game", in any sense. There is no failing, no challenge, no way to not move forward. And all of this might be fine if it instead rewarded exploration the way a game like "Gone Home" does. But instead, it is the equivalent of a rollercoaster - exciting in moments, but also brief, and on the rails, and the same experience. And while a few of the Finch stories were exciting to play though, a few of them were very dry.

What really kills the game, though, is the lack of any real final act or "bow" on the end of it. For a moment, you're teased with the prospect of entering a second location, getting a whole new set of narratives and some answers.... and then it's grabbed away from you, and five minutes later you're at the end of the game.

Even on sale, I'd have trouble reccomending this. There are better examples of this genre, ones that don't leave you feeling like you've just watched a pilot episode of a series that never got picked up.\t\t\t\t\t

Thank you devs!
One of the most wonderful gaming experience i had in my whole life. Bravo. i can recommend this game to basically everyone even to people who are not really into games. It's just a masterpiece. Few games came even close to What remains of Edith Finch. It basically lacks any drawbacks. Only it's just too short. I would love to have more of this
Gameplay
It's no typical walking simulator as some might think. Actually the gameplay itself is one of the most unique features of the game. I barely can remember games with such versatile gameplay.
Graphic
Really great use of Unreal in indie game. Can't say too much. It's too good for its genre.
Plot
It's perfect. At first i expected something not really depressing. But soon after Sam's story i was starting to feel more and more depressed as the story was going on. Sentimental persons might drop quite a lot of tears.
Music
It's absolutelly wonderful. Not really much sense to write about that you listen to it by yourself. But at one moment Milton story i cried. I am not really sentimental person and that was reallly surprising. Maybe because i felt i was like him.
Overall it's just pure masterpiece and i was in pure awe after seeing credits. I was happy. Happy for gaming industry i love so much. Happy for it's being more than alive. Keep it up indie developers. You are the reason people can respect games as much as cinema or literature.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's short but sweet. It's been noted by many as "just another walking simulator" but this had some actual diversity in its gameplay. The story it tells of a family haunted by a curse of dying in strange ways was mainly good. A decent chunk of the story is told from perspectives other than Edith's, which was probably the best thing going for this game. Each different member of the Finch family played differently and cleverly so. I think in the end my main issue was the story is short, as I completed it in the span of two hours, and had several sections that were notably smaller than others. In some cases this short span was perfect and in others it just felt missing (Gregory and Milton, respectfully). Verdict: average.

I kinda have to bring up the developer's debut work though, The Unfinished Swan, which I enjoyed quite a bit more than this but is exclusive to Sony's platforms. Full recommendation.\t\t\t\t\t
