\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI felt compelled to write a review to this game. It genuinely moved me and had a touching narrative that was well drawn and aptly interactive as a walking simulator game. I've played a few within this genre and this is by far my favourite.

You traverse this interesting home that has been deserted, yet as you walk though its hallways and rooms, you see the personalities of people long gone shine through, the narrative weaving its way seamlessly through these corridors, tunnels, and passageways that unlock your path further and further into the depths (and heights) of the home.

Without spoiling anything - there is also a connection to another game I played nearly 10 years ago that caught me by delightful surprise - see if you catch it. If you are lucky like me and have played that game back then but didn't know of the overarching connection - this will genuinely arrest you and give you that "holy sh*t" moment!

What Remains... will run for 2-3 hours, you won't want to rush through when it starts and you'll be holding back tears when it ends. I suggest walking into it as blindly as possible and let it take you on a journey to which you are the answer to its title.\t\t\t\t\t
