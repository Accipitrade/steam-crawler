\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch can be best described as a walking simulator game developed by Giant Sparrow. The videogame, as the title would imply, is centered around Edith Finch, a 17 years old girl returning to her family's house after a long absence. The Finch family seems to suffer from a terrible curse which kills off all its members one by one in the most unusual circumstances and, as you'll slowly explore the Finch household along with Edith, you'll discover all her relatives' bizzarre and interesting stories.

What sets this game apart from other walking simulators is the peculiar way the gameplay changes regularly to better fit each Finch family member's story. Basically every time you get to read something about one of them you'll be tasked to complete a certain sequence by performing a certain task or behave in a specific way. No Finch family's story is the same and each narration spots its own art style and, shall we say, theme. The presence of these stories makes What Remains of Edith Finch far more engaging than your usual walking simulator, as you'll do spend your time doing something else than just walk from place to place. These sudden gameplay changes help the game feel fresh and engaging for the whole ride.

Add to all of the above Giant Sparrow's attention to detail, masterful environment crafting and excellent sound and music choice and you'll understand why What Remains of Edith Finch is a game you should try out not only if you like the walking simulator genre, but also if you are looking for an unusual and emotional videogame experience\t\t\t\t\t

Wow.. Just wow. This game is a marvelous masterpiece in storytelling. The whole idea behind words and using them to affect the player's emotions is not only well thought-out here, it's also well-executed. The design is immaculate, the music is breathtaking, the story is gripping; I could go on. It's a short experience, yes, but it's so emotionally gripping, I felt like I played for days when it had only been a few hours. There is so much exploration to be done here, and the designers' attention to detail really shines here. Sparrow is a talented studio; I hope that they develop similar experiences to this. You won't regret this purchase if you're looking for a wonderful story game. I have used and continue to use it as an example in my own game development.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMuch better, much more, and totally different from what I was expecting!

First of all, let me be clear - I avoid "interactive movies" like the plague. I find them pointless and boring. I bought this game hoping to find a classic puzzler, so at first I was slightly disappointed with how linear the story was and how little there was for me to do besides walking and occasionally performing straight-forward tasks. However, I was soon mesmerized by the absolutely stunning graphics, the brilliant voice over and fantastic story telling. Also, as the game progresses, the tasks you need to perform get more creative, more unusual and more interesting, until you just sit there with a smile on your face, enjoying the beauty of it all.

It was not the puzzler I was looking for, but a totally unique and immersive experience I had not expected. I'm really happy to have stumbled upon this gem. It's easy to tell it was made with love and tons and tons of effort.

Kudos to the studio behind this, as a bonus I now know what my dream house looks like!\t\t\t\t\t

If you're looking for action and lots of free exploration this is not the thing for you... but if you want to be immersed in an intricate story with lots of guided exploration then this will do the trick!
