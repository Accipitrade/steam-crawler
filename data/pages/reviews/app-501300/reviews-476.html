\r\n\t\t\t\t\t“What Remains of Edith Finch” was on a huge number of video game top ten lists at the end of 2017, and last night—having done a complete play through in one sitting—I learned why. It’s not a long game (about 3 hours) but in that short time it leaves a tremendous impact. It’s quite certainly one of the most hauntingly beautiful and emotionally challenging games I’ve had the honor of playing, and it's undeniably a stunning work of art. I cannot praise it enough, and if you have the means, I strongly encourage you to get it, set aside the time, and complete it in one go. It’s a first-person perspective narrative game that’s more like an interactive short story, so it’s accessible to anyone—you don’t need to be good at games to finish it.

Warning: it’s a game involving death and all that that entails. I’ve read articles from people that had recently dealt with loss who praised the game as being cathartic, but its difficult themes are something to keep in mind. I feel like it's well worth it though. At the very least, have some tissues ready.\t\t\t\t\t
