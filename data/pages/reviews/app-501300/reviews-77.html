Beautiful - yet underwhelming.
Maybe I expected it to be more subtle? More complex and branched?
The game is a large old box from your attic, and the gameplay is a contemplation. There is really not more and not less than this. No hidden details, no layers, no biases, no judgement, synthesis or analysis - just pure contemplation. It is an okay-ish novel though: if you like this type of stories (at least the world's classic literature is full of such, take Edgar Poe or Ray Bradbury) and the visual design (you've seen the trailer) - go for it.

I admit that some storytelling solutions are indeed elegant (story of Lewis, for example).

The ending is very much expected too .\t\t\t\t\t
