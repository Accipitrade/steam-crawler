\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh. My. God.
Did you perhaps play "The Vanishing of Ethan Carter" or "Dear Esther“?
You might not believe it, but this game is even better. Super creative, spooky and touching too!

On the other hand, it's a really sad game. So: if you suffer from depression, you might want to stay clear of this game, since the game addresses the deaths and last living hours of Edith's family members and also herself.

I still wish it was possible to explore the creepy old house in a non-linear way! Instead you can replay each part individually only after you've completed the game.
Each family member has their own story with its unique atmosphere, actions and art style.
The flipbook one is certainly on of the highlights of creativity btw.

Some of these stories even brought me to tears, since they're so well written and imagined...
Poor little Molly; poor crazy old Walter, poor baby Gregory, poor Lewis (at least you're together with your prince/ess now).
I am sure I will always remember your stories for as long as I live.\t\t\t\t\t
