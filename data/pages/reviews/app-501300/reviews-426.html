\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike everyone else who reads sites such as RockPaperShotgun regularly, it was hard not to be aware of the immense amount of gushing praise heaped upon this title by all sorts of writers. 'Cool' I thought, 'Looks rather neat' I thought, before once again moving onto the type of games I feel I should like... sims, shooters, strategy... you know, stuff with action and movement and driving and flying and fighting and...

Boy howdy, was I ever wrong to pass over this title for as long as I did.

While it does have some game-ish elements, it is true that it's more of a visual novel. And while I never considered myself to really hold much interest in that 'gaming' genre, all I can say is that on one hand I now desperately wish there were more titles like this, and on the other, perhaps it's good that there isn't. Because the level of storytelling is so earnest, so perfectly executed, that it makes the attempts at story telling in so many other games in my library seem clumsy by comparision.

Sure, it might pull at the heartstrings a bit in a manner that could come off as a bit cliche or forced, but the manner in which it is narrated makes it seem so honest, that you can't help but be enchanted by it all.

To top it off, the imagery and surreal nature of some of the sequences left me absolutely speechless. I'm not sure if it's a game that I'll replay any time soon, even with the itch to finish some of those easy to grab achievments, but it will surely be a game that will remain in my memory for years to come.

If you're waffling on this still (and let's face it, if you're down here reading reviews you kinda are), do yourself a favour and give it a chance. This is one of the first games where I feel the need to introduce it to certain non-gamer people in my life and tell them "Here, spend a few hours playing this instead of watching a movie. Please. Just... do it."\t\t\t\t\t
