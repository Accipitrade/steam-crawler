\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChrist it's 5 in the morning and yet this game infuriated me so much that I had to stay up and spew my guts about it. The TL:DR version is that this game is a very pretentious con that actually diminishes rather than expands what video games are capable of. Like the Dodo and Nu-metal, it's an evolutionary dead end baby.

For starters, Giant Sparrow have crafted controls for the different sections that initially mystify before settling into sheer tedium once you finally grasp them (I don't know how, but they managed to make playing as a man eating octopus one of the more boring parts of the game). Even stuff like opening drawers or closing books is a pain in the *ss, because they ask you to do such odd inputs on the mouse that you end up stuttering, half closing a book only for it to spread open again. And the movement speed! This is a walking simulator with the emphasis on walking since you move at an absolute snails pace. No doubt the galaxy brain devs would describe this as deliberate and meditative.

And I think that gets to the larger, harder to articulate problems at the core of Edith Finch. It's nauseatingly precious about itself and what it wants to impart at the expensive of the player. This is less an interactive experience and more of a labored story that the developers railroad you into continuing. There are no substantial mechanics here, no unique gameplay, just a series of badly implemented mini games filled with cloying magical realism and the aforementioned awkward controls. And the comic story that takes control away from me for a solid 3 minutes? That's some baaaaad video games bro.

Even the exploration of a place, one of the most important aspects of 1st Person Narrative Games, and the one that primarily cements them as interactive experiences, is diluted here by the fact that Edith Finch is an incredibly linear. It's a ghost train, except you're assaulted with trite tales of love and death instead of a mannequin with a rubber zombie mask. A lot is written about how impressive the presentation is, and yes there are some great effects to transition between stories and locations, but who the hell cares when you're just pressing the W key and orienting yourself with the occasional flick on the mouse.

And the writing? What people consider the absolute crown jewel in this sterling example of interactive art? It honestly bugged me. No, it infuriated me. Its metaphors, its moments of abstraction, its whimsy, they reek of trying to be clever and literate instead of meaningful. Metaphor and abstraction are supposed to illustrate higher truths but these tales just alienated me from their main characters, made me understand them less. I know nothing of Barbra, except that she's a cheesy slasher protagonist, scant about Molly except that she's REALLY hungry, f*ck all about Gus except that he's a sulky lad who has a very destructive kite, I don't mourn these peoples' demises because they're barely people, just quirky, semi-interactive sequences.

It really does makes me mad that this is considered a great example of videos as art. Not because I need my games to have 'splosions or even that I'm against 'walking simulators' - on the contrary I've really enjoyed the likes of Gone Home and Firewatch in the past. Edith Finch on the other hand, is a fraud, something that discards many of the things that make video games a fascinating medium while getting laurels for it.

F*ck this, I'm going to bed\t\t\t\t\t
