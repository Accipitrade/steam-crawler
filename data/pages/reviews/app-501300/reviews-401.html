\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinch is one of those rare gems where you really shouldn't look up any videos or critiques (even the scrollbar at the top of the page gives some surprises away), as the whole game should be experienced without outside influence. It has been described as a "walking simulator", but like The Vanishing of Ethan Carter or even Firewatch there is much more to it. It's true that the time you spend in its world is more about exploration (both of the environment and the people who lived in it) than what you see in typical video games, but there are plenty of variations in gameplay throughout its 2-3 hour length. The story follows Edith's journal as she describes visiting her abandoned family home and learning about the tragedies that have fallen on all of her relatives. As you explore the house and all of its secret passageways, each family member's room you come across offers a different presentation into that person's life. Nothing taxing but always engaging, and I am usually one who gives up on games within the first hour if I'm not engaged with them. There are moments that bring about tremendous joy and others that will make you wallow in sorrow, but if you're the type to get lost in a story (play it through in one sitting if you can), it will be more than worth the effort.\t\t\t\t\t
