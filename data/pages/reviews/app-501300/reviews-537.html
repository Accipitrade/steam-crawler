\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a unique game, I will give it that, but that does not save it from ultimately failing to entertain in a meaningful way. First off if you liked (or loved) Firewatch stop reading this and go buy the game as I am certain you will indeed love this game. However, be warned there are ZERO options in this game, meaning you cannot alter the character's experience in ANY way whatsoever. Also exploring is not possible, yes sometimes there are two routes to continue the story but only 10 seconds or so of different scenery transpire with nothing else to be gained. So this is a movie with small amounts of brilliant interaction.
That interaction can indeed be beautiful and is where the unique quality of the game surfaces, but after the second 'story' you see the formula that will be the entire game which weould have been fine except something is lacking which I will speak to below as it is a spoiler. My problem with this game lies heavily on the fact that the story telling style, characters, visuals and even music are so obvioulsy ripped off from the various Tim Burton films. By the end of this game, I was certain they would give credit to that, but no such thing transpired. The developers left one thing out though which is Burton's humor, so we get the heart aching tragedy and... more tragedy without a single morale to any of the stories. The only morale I can imagine to be had from this movie is don't be a crazy and often suicidal shut-in family.
So while the graphics are beautifu and the small amount of controls you will use are intuitive and never take you out of the story and the story TELLING is so well done, in the end the story itself is bleak, unimaginative and simply depressing as no closure, morales or point is left at the end of it to redeem what you saw. Here ends my non spoiler review and what follows are some thoughts on the stories themselves.

***** SPOILERS AHEAD *****

So this game revolves around a family (The Finches) who believe themselves cursed by some demonic plague which causes only one child to ever survive a generation. No detail is ever given why this is believed other than idiotic superstition. Even your main character shows gullability in possibly lending some of the recounted tales credence such as kids turning into monsters or cats. My assumption is that mental illness runs in the family as some of the deaths come from doing some very silly things like sailing a house across the sea or building a rickety metal slide many stories high. Yet suicide also runs in the family and the game makes no quams about at least two the Finches having offed themselves. That is some curse right there, of course this is all glorified in the Burtonesque story telling to look artsy, but in the end feels a bit disgusting as most of the family's lives were wasted.
By the time you are to the second room you realize you will be leading each character to their various deaths, which is fine I guess, but I was absolutely disgusted to have to purposely drown a baby by swimming down a drain in a tub all the while with the game making it into something 'beautiful', again if there was a point aside from enjoy your life while you have it maybe I could overlook this, probably not for that story at least.
The remainder of the stories are indeed interesting, but they all end with pointless deaths including the main character who I assume dies in child birth and then sends her child also back to the house for the uplifting experience of reviewing the suicidal and dimwitted relatives and their collection of untimely / disturbing demises. Why would anyone do that? Then again why would someone make this game without any redemption built in, if you are going to steal from Burton wholesale why leave out the humor and / or morales surrounding his original work. If you don't agree with me on how much Burton-borrowing is happening here go review his work, after all the Finch house itself which is the main character really is almost identicle to Jack Skellington's house in the Nightmare Before Christmas.
Ultimately what makes me give this a thumbs down though is how short it is - at two hours you end up paying ten dollars an hour for watching a movie as again you cannot alter the story in ANY way so there is no replayability. Did I like it as a story on its own? No it was too dark without any point for MY taste. Did I think it was brilliant story telling? Yeah it is as far as video games go, it got close to genius somewhere in ther invoking emotion and turmoil, but it fails in the end to escape its own weight and deliver something which transcends simply a shut-in suicidal sad family's story.\t\t\t\t\t

Edith... Oh Edith
I sit here now having just finished "What Remains of Edith Finch" and I'm still, I don't know... touched by what I just witnessed.

This is a charming story. And it is a story. This is not a game and It can't be a game for reasons that are apparent when you play this not game.

Edith Finch "plays" linearly, predictably and simply but your emotions, guided by an almost absurd tale about Ediths' rather unlucky family, are taken on a fascinating journey, into nonsensical directions that will one minute leave you grinning with utter delight and the next, anticipating some dreadful outcome. And yet, somehow, it all made sense.. as any good story might

Very few games will leave you watching even the credits with eyes wide open, an enchanted smile and a feeling of sheer and utter delight. This is short but deliciously so. A glimpse of brilliance in a world of mediocrity. Bravo dev team. Bravo...

9.5/10

PS: If you lack the ability to wonder, don't bother, you'd never hope to understand.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is a beautiful introspection on the meaning of life and loss.

If you can't stand walking simulators, avoid this game. However, if that doesn't deter you, you will find yourself absorbed in a story that haunts you long after you've stepped away from your PC.

Without getting into spoilers, you play as Edith Finch, a young woman who has returned to her ancestral island home. As you explore the house, you experience stories of her family members.

The stories take the form of minigames and serve as a mechanical departure from the rest of the game. Each minigame is relatively short and I found none lasted long enough to become stale.

The game is paced very well and I didn't find myself bored or struggling to find the next room.

The game performed well on my PC and I didn't experience any noticeable technical problems.

Jeff Russo's soundtrack transitions well between the main story and the smaller stories and succeeds in creating a unique sound for the different tones of each.

Lastly, the most common complaint regarding walking simulators is the length. A good walking sim, like a good film or a good novel, will succeed in captivating you and causing time to blur. I'm happy to say that I lost myself in exploring Edith's world and found myself truly immersed.

What Remains of Edith Finch is a masterpiece and I highly recommend you play this game.\t\t\t\t\t
