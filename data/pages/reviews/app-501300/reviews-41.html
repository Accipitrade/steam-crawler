\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA creative walking simulator, the most intriguing parts were the unique and vivid methods of telling each family member's story. The game is atmospheric, and is excellent in the way it unravels the poignant legacy of the Finch clan. The dialogue, simple and delivered with a sincere gentleness, was well done, too. All the elements melted together in such a perfect fashion that I felt entranced at each new room and excited when I saw a new piece to read.

I think, though, that the medium of game was not the best choice. Movement is slow and tedious, and the hand controls are often unresponsive or awkward. Many times I had to move my mouse off the mouse pad, or repeatedly fumble with which button, or direction, I was meant to go. This was especially noticeable when the game forced my pov towards whatever floating line of text the narrator was poetically waxing.

As a final note, the I found there to be somewhat of an awkwardness to a few of the situations. For the most part, the glimpses into each member of the family were wonderful, exciting, frightening, etc. But, there were a few peculiarities, such as the psychiatrist writing in the letter to the victim's mom that he lopped his head off for a crown (a bit hamfisted in conjunction w/ the already clear visuals, tbh) and the absolutely horrendous mini game where you play as a baby drowning himself and enjoying it, which reached David Cage level's of tastelessness. I think that in the attempt to portray things artistically the team might have forgotten that some things sound really odd in the real world (like the dude who heard trains his whole life, leaves his bunker, and immediately gets run over by a train with absolutely perfect comedic timing ).

With all this in mind, I think the game is worth playing. If you like walking simulators, and especially if it is on sale, I think it is a worthwhile experience.\t\t\t\t\t
