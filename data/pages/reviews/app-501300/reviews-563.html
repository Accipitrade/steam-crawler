\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most brilliant methods of telling a story I've ever experienced in a game. Most would put this in the walking simulator genre, but considering how much I don't like those games, the fact that this one had me glued all the way through tells you something. I think it's because of the very strong narrative that literally spells everything right on your screen as your walking and looking around.

What surprised me right off the get go was the installation was only around like 4gb, and I was thinking hmmm, for games these days, that's pretty small, just how much content is there? Well surprisingly, they managed to do some serious awesome compression because the graphics, audio, and music is phenomenal. The house looks beautiful and well designed. It's loaded with details and feels very real. I loved just staring at every little object to catch all the little details. I will say however some portions outside I saw some what looked like unfinished textures. It only appeared maybe one area so it doesn't take away anything overall.

With that said, there isn't much to say about the game without giving away all the awesome moments that you will encounter and need to experience on your own through playing it. Talking about it would just ruin the initial experience wow factor of seeing it with your own two eyes and playing through it on your own. I might say it's quite short, just look at my overall game time that it took to complete it with nearly 100% completion (2 hours), but it feels like the perfect time overall. Maybe wait for it to drop in price or pay whatever it's at now to support the developers because overall it was worth it.

Kudos to the team that put this together because it oozes with passion and well thought out design that goes to show they took their time on it and told the story the way they wanted to tell it. Highly recommended to walking simulator fans or ANYONE that loves a good story that plays out like a book you don't want to put down.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is deeply frustrating. The storytelling is simply superb, as are many of the small episodes contained within, each with their own unique mechanical and presentation styles. Unfortunately the actual story being told is lacklustre. It intrigues by presenting itself as a mystery, and although many of the best mysteries remain unsolved, this one goes out of it's way to leave you with so little resolution that you may resent it. The magical realism of some of the self-contained stories can be easy to dismis, or even enjoy, as narrative devices, but the unadressed implausabilites and lack of any real explanation of the framing world that is supposedly real is a real failure. The final sentiment of the story, that stories are important, seems especially ironic in this regard.

I still reccomend it, though only grudgingly, as a collection of small set-pieces that are a joy to experience.

Ultimately this is a story about nothing, beautifully told.\t\t\t\t\t
