\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUsually not into Walking Simulators, but this one had great reviews so I gave it a try. I do not consider this genre as a game per se, for me it is closer to an interactive movie, and as long as I know that beforehand, I am OK with it. If you are not expecting that, then make sure to adjust your expectatives.

What Remains of Edith Finch stand out in two areas, storytelling and art. They are both outstanding and probably the reason of why this title got so much praise. This was probably a dream job for Graphics Designers that worked on this, something that will forever shine in their career resumes. The storytelling is putting the bar very high for other Walking Simulators.

As for the story itself, it is way above average but not top notch. Except for one of the pieces, almost at the end, that without spoiling anything, got me in awe, like a child going into a popular Disneyland ride for the first time.

I recommend the game, but only if you know what a Walking Simulator is.

I will say that there's an annoying glitch where you can't progress further as a character unless you put the graphics settings down all the way. I just left them like that for the rest of the game and encountered no further problems. Presumably higher specs will mean no problems... but this is a problem that's existed for three years, and nothing has been done about it.

Regardless, it's still an interesting story, and some of the games require good coordination, so it's not tedious. There are few choices to be made - only in one story, in fact - and they don't change the direction that the game takes. So if you prefer games which have choices that actually make a difference, this isn't for you. If, however, that's not a deal-breaker, you should check out this game.\t\t\t\t\t
