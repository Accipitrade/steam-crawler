\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuggested pairings: Wildflower mead, bagels and lox, that one book that takes you back to a specific time in your life.

Use as a text for college classes discussing adolescence, suicide, trauma, haunted-house narratives, magical realism.

This game was ... beautiful. I wish I could recommend it multiple times. A lovely story, a thoughtful setting. The voice acting is absolutely EXCEPTIONAL ... as is the sound design in general, for that matter. And that's only one of the things that makes it so atmospheric and haunting.

Discoveries feel natural, and where to go next is usually fairly apparent without seeming railroaded (though a little sequence-breaking is possible -- and doesn't break anything). The process of figuring out the controls for each new story is just-frustrating-enough, in a way that replicates the experience of trying to put together old memories and records into an adult understanding. (Everything is WASD and mouse, but what that does in each scene can change in unexpected ways.) It's got an ambiguous, but exquisitely hopeful ending -- I'm normally allergic to ambiguous endings, because they feel a bit lazy to me, but this ending (these endings, really) feel natural, appropriate, and thoroughly earned.

My caveats are exclusively physiological ones! 1.) Some of the scenes are quite claustrophobic. Play in an open area and be prepared for lots of breaks if you're prone! 2.) I've never had trouble with motion sickness in games, but this one got me a bit. I think it was either the lower FPS in outdoor areas (stick with it, that gets tons better after the opening!) or the perspective shifts between stories.

I finished this in one absorbing three-hour setting. Yesterday. I'm already planning my next play-through.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finally got around to playing this, having purchased it, I'm sure, some outrageously long time ago. It took me about 2 hours to play through the full game, I missed most of the achievements but none would've greatly impacted playtime. It is a really enjoyable game very similar in mood and feel to Gone Home. It's an immersive story game, centered around young Edith Finch roaming the somewhat bizarre house she grew up in (partly pictured below) to uncover the truth behind the untimely and unusual deaths of her entire family tree, going back three generations (and including her own). You spend your time exploring the house (including secret passages and locked rooms) and experiencing vignettes from the perspective of the family members. I really enjoyed it although the ending was a bit abrupt and a couple storylines are frustratingly incomplete (intentional, but still argh). Recommended, would not spend more than $5 though, due to short playtime and lack of replayability.\t\t\t\t\t
