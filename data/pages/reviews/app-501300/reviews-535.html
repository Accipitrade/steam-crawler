\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFIrst off, yes, it's pretty much a walking simulator. With that out of the way, it's a damn good example of how good a walking simulator can be. It concerns Edith Finch, the last in a family line whose members are "Cursed" or, at very least, have a tedency to die tragic deaths, often at young ages. Edith returns to her family home to discover the secrets about her family she barely knew.

It's short and is pretty much divided into two sections. The main part of the game is the walking tour of the home, where the bedrooms of dead family members have been preserved the way it was when they died and sealed to prevent entry. Finding a way into the rooms is the first "Challenge"(finding secret passages isn't really hard though). The more interesting part is when you find a journal or some such relating to the deceased, which starts a gameplay sequence based on their lives, often ending in their deaths. Some of the sequences are quite varied and creative and all of them are tragic(Poor Gregory....).

That's pretty much what there is. An in depth look at a family marked by tragedy for generations. The gameplay is limited but on an emotional and character level, it suceeds. If this appeals to you, buy it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're into walking simulators, you should totally buy this game. If not, consider buying it too.

This is a very very beautiful and sad story. Not in that kind of way, when narrator tearily beg you for compassion, no. It's very intimate, deep, thoughtful tale full of light moments and dark ones, blended in a very smart way. You will be fully drawned in detailed level design and strong art direction, surprised by twists game designers so creatively came up with, taken a deep breath of both scriptwriter's work and actors voicing. And music, that's so accurate and touching.

I'm writing this review under fresh impression, but I really believe that I won't change my mind in any foreseeable future.

This game is a masterpiece, and I'm really thankful to you all the guys from the titles section (which was so impossible to skip) who made this game come true.\t\t\t\t\t
