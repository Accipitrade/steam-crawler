The worst Walking Simulator I have ever played.

The game's story consists of a girl going back to her childhood home as a last farewell to her family. Most of the exposition is done entirely through the girl's dialogue and flashbacks.
The problem is that you very soon discover that her family consists entirely of unrelatable idiots.

So when I inevitably saw them die in their own flashbacks I couldn't help but think
"Yep they died. It was their fault and I am happy they died because they were idiots".

Examples of stupid deaths include:
Little girl eating poisonous berries, because her mom said it was too late for her to eat.
Boy falls off swing that his father built right over a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ cliff.
Child drowns in bathtub because his idiot mother is talking on the phone.
Boy dies because he flies a kite during a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ thunderstorm.

And in the last hour of the game we also discover that the main protagonist has come to visit this crumbling old house while 22 weeks pregnant. So you can add her to the list of idiots as well.

What Remains of Edith Finch is a pretentious game that thinks it is really clever with all it's themes of mortality and family. While in reality it is a game with a few neat ideas shoved in a terrible story.

Do not buy this game, do not support it. I don't want to see more of this kind of garbage getting undeserved attention.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI find it hard to recommend this game. It wasn't bad or anything, but after completing the entire thing in 2 hours even taking my time and exploring everything, even at half-off I feel it's not worth the price. This is maybe worth $3-5. There's no challenge or skill or really any way to fail at the game. It's just a walking sim with a (bizarre) story about a cursed family and the various ways each of them died.

Personally I didn't find it emotionally moving, just strange. I didn't end up relating to any of the characters in any meaningful way and the lack of any kind of challenge left me unfulfilled. 2 hrs and $11 later I just felt "that's it"?

I don't want to be entirely negative though. As a piece of art it was worthwhile, but it's not something I am going to remember or ever come back to. If simple walking sims with moderately interesting stories are your thing, you might love this game. It seems a lot of people do, but I wasn't one of them.\t\t\t\t\t
