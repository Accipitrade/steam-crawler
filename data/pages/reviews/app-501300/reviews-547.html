The controls were intuitive and very easy to learn and use, thus allowing me to explore and absorb the environment without distraction.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had a lot of cynicism going into Edith FInch. There are other similar games of this type which I thought were dreadful (to name them, Gone Home and Dear Esther specifically). Edith Finch managed to remain compelling and captivating throughout its 2 hour duration.

I must pre-face this by saying that the game is not worth the full $20 price tag. I am glad I supported the authors at that price point, but what is currently here is probably worth $10 at the most where I don't feel the dent in my wallet after completing it. We live in a digital age where you can rent movies online for $3 and get a similar but non-interactive experience for your time investment.

To keep things brief and without spoilers - Edith Finch focuses entirely on narrative and art, and makes them compelling in a way I will not spoil, but traditional text dumps have another level of interaction which makes the game far more compelling than others of its type. There is a lot of creativity on display, and I think that Edith Finch has elements which makes it a game that anyone can play and enjoy. That has its own merits. That being said, you will not miss out on much if you just look up a walk-through of the game itself, but the quirks of the different interactive elements are worth experiencing, just perhaps not at the price point of $20.\t\t\t\t\t
