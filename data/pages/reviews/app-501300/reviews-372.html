\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere were so many things that surprised me about this game. It's not that I was expecting garbage, but I had no idea there'd be so much detail. Photos, newspapers, toys are scattered throughout the house and provide insight on the type of people each Finch was. The house feels lived in and real because of minute details that are included in the background (a great example is a tennis ball hanging by a string in the garage).

I loved this game. The characters felt so real. They were flawed and brilliant. I really love how the game made the characters so lovable that I even found myself supporting some really awful decisions they'd made.

Each character's story is told in such a unique, thoughtful way (can't say much more w/o spoilers). It felt like I played a series of short games or watched several short movies in a row. Despite this, the overall story isn't jumbled or confused. The developers and writers had a clear motivation behind each cut scene and game play sequence that dovetailed really well into the overarching plot.

Because of the amount of detail, thoughtful pacing, and intricate story I think this game is worth the asking price. And I'm someone who reuses napkins and dries teabags. I'd punch a kid $20 tbh

I wouldn't recommend this game to people who are looking for a turn-off-your-brain game. Parts of Edith Finch are (in a sense) calming, but it's a game that demands attention from the player. I think it'd be a waste if you played the game on autopilot. There's a lot being offered and a good portion of it is easy to ignore if you're not paying attention (I say this as someone who plays most games pretty haphazardly). I actually want to play this game again because I think I missed stuff the first time around.

In short, please play this game if you like an immersive universe and realistic, weird characters. This is such a quirky, precious game. You can tell so much love went into it and I want everyone to experience this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst up, this is really NOT A VIDEO GAME. Instead, it's some kind of narrative art piece that uses game tech to retell a rambling and very unreliable family history. But what it does, it does really well.

Still interested?

The Finch family is both very creative and very unlucky. You get to steer about 12 of them through their final moments towards inevitable doom. Seen through their eyes. this can be quite funny, or tragic, even psychedelic, or all of the above

There's a YA angle here, with the family revisited through the eyes a young woman. But this is deceptive, as the themes run a fair bit deeper than young adult fiction usually allows. On the other hand, it's probably quite OK just to enjoy a few macabre lolz.

It's only a few hours long. It's short, quirky, and deftly executed. But you'll be tempted to replay it enough times to define just exactly what the malign influence is at work lopping off great boughs of the Finch family tree.

But in the end this plays less a like a good game, and more like a good book. You know, the kind you keep it turning over in your mind long after you've put it away.\t\t\t\t\t
