\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA short but very sweet game. You play as Edith going back to visit her family's old house. Along the way you find out more about the Finchs and Edith herself. The story is top notch, lots of mystery, tons of sadness, and a few funny moments as well. That being said, there is gameplay too! There are all sorts of minigames you can encounter and each one is incredibly different from the other. Not that the house exploration isn't fun. Each time I went through a door or passage I was actually excited to see what was on the other side! That's something I have never experienced before in ANY game. So if you're tired of your everyday FPS, MMO, RTS, or RPG, then take a break and give What Remains of Edith Finch a try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe What Remains of Edith Finch is one of the best walking simulators out there. For me it is up there with The Stanley Parable and Firewatch. If you like the genre, you must try it.

About the game: you are playing as Edith Finch exploring the old family house looking for "diaries". You can play out the stories contained in those diaries to understand how one of your relatives passed away. Once you are done with all of them, you are done with the game.

Each diary is a different beast - with its own rules and "mini-games". In that regard it reminded me of Psychonauts where each person's mind is different. But here, a diary would only be around 2-10 minutes long, so don't expect to spend long times wandering. And that stands true for all of the game. Took me 2 hours to finish it, while looking around a lot. But I got a lot of enjoyment out of it; the environment and the moody stories are really worth experiencing.

When it is in a sale, there is no reason not to buy it (unless you just hate all walking simulators)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI watched as this game got multiple awards in The Game Awards 2017. I watched as people praised it above all games released in 2017. I watched as people played it on YouTube. I watched as it was on my wishlist for a long time.

But What Remains of Edith Finch is something you just have to experience yourself. Get a comfortable pair of headphones, a dark room and some water and prepare to not leave the room for 2 hours.

This game is more like an interactive story. It tells many stories of multiple characters. You explore a huge family home and hear the thoughts of an 18-year-old woman and how the characters tell their stories. Just know that these stories aren't happy ones, so bring that tissue box in, too.

Yes, it's shorter than triple-A games, but there is no dull moment. The story is as it is and there isn't more to tell and that's fine. It might be short but it will stay with you for a long time.\t\t\t\t\t
