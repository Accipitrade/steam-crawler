\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI suppose it's mostly my fault for not reading more reviews beforehand, I just didn't expect to be so disappointed in a game that had such "overwhelmingly positive" reviews. My biggest problem was the fact that I don't even know if you could call this a "game" so much as an "interactive story", and even that is giving it more credit than I think is due. The so-called interacting is predominantly changing the camera view, and you can't interact with anything that is not blatantly marked as something to interact with. There are no meaningful choices, problem solving, or inferences to be drawn. That being said, it was beautiful and completely original, and an interesting way to tell [part of] a story. I accepted it for what it was about an hour in, only to find more disappointment upon finishing, as there is absolutely no conclusion or hint to possible conclusions. The most satisfying end to this tale was a fan theory I read afterwards in the discussions.
So, neat for what it is, and at least it only wasted a few hours, but extremely overpriced for what you're getting - not worth more than a few bucks.\t\t\t\t\t
