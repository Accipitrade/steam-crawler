\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch does not have any game elements and is a minimalistic interactive story. Even the little you can interact with is not consistent and sometimes clumsy. That said, if you enjoy games with stories you will probably enjoy this. The stories are creative yet short and the whole experience will land you between 2 and 3 hours of playtime.

However, I feel the ending fell flat and failed to deliver a satisfying closure to justify the excellent story leading up. There was a small twist that didn't really address any of the questions you will have. Unfortunanely that leaves a disastisfied feeling for me. The graphics were beautiful, the storytelling wonderful, but it just didn't amount to much at the end.

Catch this game on sale and give it your own thoughts. The journey I feel is still worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTook me a while to pick this game up, but I'm glad I did. It may be short, but it's definitely one of the best game stories that I've had the pleasure of experiencing. The various ways that the story is told is brilliant, and I think it makes it easier to get gripped by it. There was never a moment of me playing this game where I wasn't paying attention. I never felt bored because the game would find a new way of portraying something.

The story is very emotional as well. I feel that some people may be able to relate to various aspects of it. I won't talk about it in this review because I honestly think you're better off not knowing anything about it before playing, as I did. The music is great, and the atmosphere really fits the story telling as well. The graphics are stunning and really help set up the mood that the story is based on.

I see why the game won a BAFTA. I think it's well deserved. It's inspirational. As someone who is interested in being a games designer, this game is an accurate representation of the types of games I would like to make. My only problem is the price tag. Despite that, I highly recommend this game to everybody, especially aspiring story writers and game designers.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUltimately I am left with a want for more. The story ends with many questions left unanswered, but I suppose that is an intentional decision. It's a very linear experience, which isn't quite a bad thing, especially with such an intriguing and mysterious environment to explore. On top of that, the amount of unique and varied ways you interact with it, coupled with the brilliant story-telling certainly makes up for the strict, one-way path level design the game has. As someone who sees games as art and wants games to be recognized as art, (in more than just a legal sense), this game gives me hope that people will see these things as more than toys or drugs that turn us violent. I know my review seems somewhat harsh, (or maybe too gushy depending on how you look at it), but I would sincerely recommend this game if you enjoy artsy fartsy deep experiences that go beyond just having a good time. Just be warned that it's somewhat short, taking only about 3-4 hours to complete, and again, it leaves many questions unanswered.

In short: the game is excellent, I would give it a 10/10 if I were a games journalist. Then again I think games like Undertale and Minecraft are 10/10 experiences, so maybe my opinion on games isn't quite sound.\t\t\t\t\t
