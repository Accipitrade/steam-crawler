\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay: Walking simulator at its core but with many addons and pretty nice gameplay segments. Really good.
Music: Appropriate for each scene and pat of the game, nothing that blew my mind. Its fine.
Graphics: More than the graphics, I found the aesthetics particularly nice and contrasting between segments. Really good.
Plot: I play this kind of games for the story, since these, for me, are more of an "interactive" tale than a video game. I don't want to spoil the game, I will just say that gameplay + aesthetics played a bigger role than the characters, story and its closure. Good.
Length: Extremely Short. 2-3h, and I don't think there's any replay value in the game (be it new segments or hidden plot hints).

I expected a good walking simulator, seeing the overwhelming in the reviews, but I got to play a extremely polished game with pretty cool gameplay sections and aesthetics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA lovely game that's really more of a movie but in the best ways. If this were a movie, it would be missing a lot of the interactivity that really makes the game more personal. The cutting of the fish, the kite interacting with the words, this is the PEAK of what interactive stories should be.This is not a difficult game, it is a story with game elements, and that's great! All of the stories are interesting, almost like a twilight zone or scary stories to tell in the dark feel, obviously less paranormal and spooky, but still fantastic and most importantly, clever stories for each character. The vastly different stories and gameplay all connected by one narrative was what kept me playing till the end. Everything from the cartoony towered house, to how lovely the interior of it was, to solving the mysteries of each member of the Finch family was truly captivating. Don't watch gameplay, don't read synopsese, if you like this kind of game you probably don't want yourself spoiled.

The biggest strength this game has is that it doesn't throw the endings in your face, they are implied and give you the tools to figure out how that persons story ends, it treats the player like they are intelligent. I love how also they combine both reality and exaggerated storytelling to let you decide what really happened.

I would say my biggest complaint about this game is that you walk REALLY SLOW, but that's not a huge deal since the game isn't too long.

All in all, super cool short stories with a ton of meaningful creative gameplay additions to them that add instead of subtract to the experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith a concrete and interesting story that thankfully avoids the pitfall of explaining too much, What Remains of Edith Finch greatly succeeds at fulfilling the most core objective of a walking sim. This game has a number of unique mechanics that are best enjoyed without spoilers, but rest assured that there's a truly novel approach here, and in a genre where near everything is the same except for the writing, that's surprising to see.

The environment design is also well done, and the music, while subdued and not near as core as compared to something like Everybody's Gone to the Rapture, is still memorable. Detail is abundant and there's a great blend of the somewhat fantastical and the lifelike.

My single complaint about the game lies in its ending. The experience throughout raised the game to remarkably high levels, but the last act abandons previously established rules and changes the game's tempo. It's not a bad ending by any means - narrative-wise I actually wouldn't change a thing - but the execution could have done with a little bit more help.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually play walking simulators. I also don't usually play games that seem to be more about being art than being a game. But also, this is the first game I felt like I needed to review.

At about 2 hours of playtime, it may be difficult to justify paying $20 for this. But those two hours are... I'm not even sure how to put it. I'd say "deeply moving" but that sounds almost sarcastic. Let's just say, if What Remains of Edith Finch was a movie, it would be one of my favorite movies.

With relatively little real gameplay, it may, in fact, be best to compare it to a movie. A somewhat interactive film where you control the camera, but basically everything is linear and there's not really much in the way of extras to find. You'll get the most out of this game if you treat it like a "memory" as you go through it. Some details are absolutely beautiful, while others can mess with your suspension of disbelief if you look at them too closely (I'm looking at you, book titles). But again, treat it like a memory and that all makes more sense. The game, after all, really is about memories.

I'd give this like a 9/10. 2 hours seems short but I honestly feel like it was worth it. I didn't love the fully linear nature of the game and like I said, some details weren't quite right, but overall it's a masterpiece of storytelling. Play it, buy it, remember it.\t\t\t\t\t
