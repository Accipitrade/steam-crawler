SPOILERS!

If I could give this game a "mixed" review, it would be such one.

People praised it to be the best storybased game they've ever experienced. A walking simulator worthy of being played so to say, even for people who don't like them. Even crying at then end.

The story didn't reach me as well, the ending left me with question marks instead of tears.

Maybe I don't get the ending? I think I have a good understanding of it, unless I am indeed missing something?
Maybe I was expecting too much?
Maybe it wasn't relatable enough for me?

Some parts of the game are super creative, probably one of the best examples how to use gameplay as story telling.
Some parts are too convinient, can be pretty much cut out entirely and are fluff like a music video is to music because they feel so bland compared to the good ones.

I guess this fluff didn't really work on me. I guess I don't have that admiration of a child listening to a story like this anymore unfortunately. Let me explain:

The story builds upon the deliberation of the author, Edith Finch herself. She tells stories about stories, which are blurring out more and more the stories are told and therefore might be inconsistent. Still, even with those factors in mind, often it feels like it's more spectacular for the sake of elevating the gameplay into certain scenarios, molding the story convienently into place for the sake of leaving those gameplay parts in. It felt disingenuous to me, this big contrast of ideas in this mixed bag of nuts and raisins.

It's admiring to see that every single Finch Family Member gave their best to strive for their ultimate goal but leaving them to die early. And the premise of Edith's goal being delivering the story to her child (and ultimately us as the player) even though her mother wanted to protect her from those stories is wonderful. A teacher keeping from teaching stories about their own family and yet for the first time in the history of the Finchs' giving up on her goal.

Sadly, I could not sympathize with the game because of its execution and it's okay. But I would not recommend this game to people based on my experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy sister recommended this game to me and I'm very happy that I trusted her taste and picked it up. "What Remains of Edith Finch" is a highly emotional, shocking and upsetting journey through a family history filled with secrets, lies, and magic.
Each family member you encounter in your journey through Edith's family history is given space and time to become a fully realized character. This strengthens the bonds that you, the player, feel when Edith talks about the stories she's heard about them. Additonally, each minigame that tells the story of another character's death is uniquely designed and makes use of its time to either let the person themselves or someone else narrate their relationship to them and, consequently, to their ending.
I cannot begin to describe the magic of this stunningly looking game without spoiling everything (I couldn't stop talking about it). So, I'll keep it brief and just say: "Edith Finch" is a masterpiece, a brilliantly written and performed game that will certainly take your breath away at some points. It sure took mine away, more than once.

Apart from some minor audio glitches in Lewis' segment of the game, I experienced no technical difficulties during my playthrough.\t\t\t\t\t
