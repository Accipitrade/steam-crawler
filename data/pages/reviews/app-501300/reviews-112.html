\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great narrative game. Visuals are nice and voices are well done. The overall story is good, though could use some work. The Soundtrack is amazing and well done. If you like narrative story games, this is a good pick it does an amazing job of showing individual perspective in a way I haven't gotten to experience yet and I loved it for that. But I cannot give any constructive feedback, however, without giving spoilers so be wary of what's below.

SPOILERS SPOILERS SPOILERS SPOILERS
This game was great but one of the big things that bugged me and is a glaring flaw was, the story of the little brother, Milton. It was a nagging thread for me that seemed so important to have it left up in the air actually made me upset, where did he go? Did he just... die in the house? Why did he go? I feel like, he really deserved some closure that you get with every other character but perhaps I missed it. I feel the same about Sanjay, he was just so... seemingly last minute compared to the others who got this fully fleshed story.
Edie getting to the house is another frustrating thing because you never get to see it but it felt so important, like the clue to the mysterious bad luck was housed within.
And that would be my other complaint was, the reason for the bad luck it was just never explained. Sure you could argue that there never was any, and it was all just happenstance/superstition but when everyone is dead but one... it kind of paints the picture that something more is happening, especially in a house that seemingly defies gravity. With rooms boarded up and sealed, it felt kind of awful to have it lead to nothing.

However, all that aside, there are some absolutely fantastic and heart wrenching moments. Lewis' story resonated with me and I knew where it was going for the get go and it hurt to proceed through it. I think that was fantastic story telling. There are many moments like that and the different character perspectives on the events were amazing and worth deep delving.

The game leads you through the story, step by step, and you cannot really deviate from the path the creators have staked out. What Remains of Edith Finch - 9/10
Playtime - 2.3 hours

Plot and Dialogue: 9/10, 9/10
Setting and Art Design: 10/10
Soundtrack: 8/10
Graphics: 8/10
Gameplay and Sound Design: 5/10, 8/10

Plot and Dialogue:
The plot of this game initially comes off as simple, a woman who is attempting to learn more about her family before she left her house. We return to said house and this is where the game takes place. It is incredibly interesting as the plot lines open, seeing the various relationships that the character has had with other members of the family and what events led to the current situation she is in. The dialogue is spot on, is delivered excellently, and feels genuine. Great job in both regards here.

Setting and Art Design:
The game takes on multiple different art designs throughout, but each one is incredibly well done and reflects on the skill of the artists of this game. Great job on their part.

Soundtrack:
The soundtrack is a much more emotional one, attempting to help deliver emotional weight throughout the game. It does accomplish this goal very effectively and is a very nice soundtrack to listen to.

Graphics:
The game's graphics and graphical fidelity are both done very well here. In all aspects and with all the various art designs, it remains to look very good. Great job on the developers for that.

Gameplay and Sound Design:
The gameplay is very simplistic, as it's a walking simulator. This doesn't necessarily bad, but it wasn't extremely captivating. The sound design is extremely well-done, and really helps to immerse you in the game that you are playing.

Final Notes:
This game is definitely a very good game, providing an experience and a movie like experienced instead of a game. I would pick this game up on sale though, not at the retail price of 20 dollars.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is a bit unfair that I came into this game, with the color of such overwhelmingly positive reviews. This likely biased my expectations unfairly. That being said, the game certainly delivered in terms of creativity, suspense, surreal imagery and erudite writing. "Like a mouth with too many teeth" will remain in my memory for quite some time. Gothic in nature, at times flirting with horror, I never quite figured out what the game was trying to be. BUT I believe that might have been the point.

I can't describe exactly, how I felt after playing this game. Disturbed, to a degree, for sure. But also introspective and contemplative in a way few games leave me. So much so I did some reading on how others felt after finishing the game (something I have only done once or twice before). I wanted to know how the game affected *them* and compare their reactions to my own. That is a pretty incredible thing for a game to do. This game about the transitory condition called life, so tenuous, so fleeting. yet interjected with beauty, and hope and resilience and so often horrifyingly tragic.\t\t\t\t\t
