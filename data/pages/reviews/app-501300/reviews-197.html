\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHm.

I don't know.

I don't think I actually recommend this game, but it also doesn't deserve a thumbs down. It's beautiful, the voice overs are some of the best I've heard in a while, and a lot of the devices are very clever.

The game is a bit too dark in a lot of places - visually, I mean. I had trouble seeing. And the controls are VERY unkind if you have any wrist pain or trouble. That's one thing I wish I'd realized going in. Opening doors, turning latches, winding handles, etc. all have to be done manually and are rather finicky. The first thing I did was set the mouse sensitivity to max and it was still a chore to get through all the movements.

You know from the very start how all the stories will end, so the only twists and surprises lie in how they're are told. Which is not a bad thing, but if you're here for intrigue and mystery, look elsewhere. Most questions you might end up having won't ever get answered.

One of the stories hit a little too close to home. The rest had little effect on me. The characters are all one-dimensional cutouts that are hard to care about with their singular personality trait, but I was never bored so I'll give it a pass.

It's a decent and decidedly artful game. Just don't jump in with too high of expectations.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t3D Visual novel, innovative way of narration, immersive gameplay. However, if you take a step back, you realize you can summarize most character's background story with only five or six lines. You're paying 20 dollars for at most 5 hours of gameplay. (I am a fast reader, so it took me way less than that.) Even worse, about half of the characters' story can be concluded with one sentence: they have bad luck, which the family considers to be a curse. You're left with many questions, for example, is the curse real? If you're into the mystery genre, then this is not a good game for you, because it provides only questions, and no answers, not even enough evidence to suggest answers. The questions are way too open and inconclusive imo. Apart from the immersive storytelling, the characters and their stories fail to have a philosophical or emotional depth that you will find in other visual novels with similar price.

In conclusion, if you just want to experience a 3D visual novel, just want to have 5 hour of novel experience, then maybe buy it. But within the visual novels genre, with 20 dollars you can buy games and stories that will take you way more than 50 hours and leave you with a greater shock.

Edit: I have to admit that I missed a lot of details, and only realized many of them after watching Joseph Anderson's video on youtube. I will say I now want to give the game a neutral grade instead of an yes or no. Here is what I think now: 1. It's still on the relatively expensive side for a game with its play time and age. 2. Unlike what I thought, I believe now there IS some depth in the narration, there is something to be found in the death of the children, BUT the story telling doesn't encourage you to find it, UNLESS you start playing the game with that particular mindset. This is why I thought a lot of details were trivial and added nothing to the game, until now that I have watched other people's analysis. 3. Many of the deaths still feel empty, even with great details, even after knowing what "most likely" the devs were going for. Calvin's story is good, but nothing really builds up to it. I will have no complaint if every story is at least half as long as that of Molly. Sure. The people are either superstitious or negligent , which causes many death, but then what? It doesn't leave me in reflection. There is no moral ambiguity. It's a bunch of stupid people doing stupid things, and causing tragedies within the family. Sure. It feels satisfying to uncover the details (by following Joseph's video), but really it's the kind of satisfaction that doesn't last long.

In short, if I am to rate it in terms of creativity and immersive-ness alone, it's a 9/10. If I am to rate it as a tragedy from a literal point of view, it is mediocre, maybe a 5 or 6/10. Note that it doesn't mean it's not worth reading. Most movies, most novels, most of everything are mediocre. The problem really is about the price.\t\t\t\t\t
