\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most unique games I've ever had the pleasure of coming across.

This is less a game and more a visual novel with light gameplay elements thrown in. The art styles and environments are beautifully detailed, and convey the sadness that permeates the entirety of the Finch family. Edith herself is a compelling protagonist with tons of personality, and her voice actress did a stellar job of portraying just what sort of person she is.

The gameplay comes in the form of experiencing each member of the Finch family's personal story, and each story has its own separate gameplay style which is where this game really shines. Every single story, especially the one about the actress, was enjoyable and never felt forced, and the superb writing did its job on every level of making me actually care about each and every member of this bizarre and tragic family. Most of the story is told through Edith's narration (which is excellent, by the way) and the bits of wandering around the house are filled with her talking to herself, and to you. The way the house is designed (and consequently every character's room) goes above and beyond in visual storytelling. Every little aspect of the rooms is important, and tells you something about its former occupant. Not enough games take environmental storytelling into account, and this game is a prime example of how amazing it can be.

Overall, a heartache of a game that will make you fall in love with its characters, and leave you wishing you could experience it for the first time all over again.

9/10 (Excellent)\t\t\t\t\t
