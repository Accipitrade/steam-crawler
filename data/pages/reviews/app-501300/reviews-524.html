\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a bit surprised, that I still didn't write anything about Edit FInch. Maybe, the reason is that i was completely shocked and under impressions after this powerful masterpiece. It took me almost a week to get rid of thinking about what I saw and what I experienced. Completely unique, atmospheric, terrifying, breathtaking, overwhelming and definitely unforgettable!

First to say, I guess all of use understood already that games became a powerful storytelling tool, even stronger than movies or any TV shows. The explanation is kinda simple - games let you become a part of it, thanks to an interactive elements and possibility to dive into the story, that lets you feel like you are the main part of it, not just a watcher/viewer.

So, after playing a bunch of walking simulator games I was like "Yeah, it's ok game. Oh,another one, yeah, it's not bad.." All my impressions was just ok, but nothing more. And then I purchased What Remains Of Edit Finch. Which I thought, would be another mediocre walking simulator game. I was So.Damn.Wrong.

What Remains Of Edit FInch tells you a creepy story about family's curse. The whole game is divided on a few stories about every Finch's family member. You are playing as and Edith, the only remaining member of the cursed family, trying to figure out what happened to her family and discover the family members secrets, which leaded to the disaster.

All of these stories/tales are very unique and they never repeat themselves. Some of them was so shocking, creepy and thrilling, that I couldn't help myself, I felt shaking all over my body watching this. The whole attention to the details and atmosphere and surroundings is really something, that modern gamedevs should take as an example. You can explore the Finch's house for hours and discover new elements and details again and again. You can replay some of the tales time to time just to remind yourself the whole message of the game. Which is another reason why I am so in love with this game.

The philosophy of this game is that we are all here on this planet Earth, living our lives for a reason. And we all should be really thankful for it. If you ever feel abandoned, or down, or you are just lost in your life..You should play this game. I mean, seriously.. It will definitely helps you to understand that human's life is a miracle. There is nothing more valuable than human's life.

Edit Finch wants to tell you a story. Listen to it. You will not be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game isn't perfect, but it's quite an experience. The game looks beautiful and is extremely detailed, and therein lies the story: a history of a family, and of life. If you don't care for walking simulators, there's a bit of variation here and there, but it remains a walking simulator in essence. Nevertheless, I found the gameplay interesting and somewhat relaxing (albeit often dark). The music is great, too. Overall, you might be disappointed to get only an hour or two of gameplay for the money, but for some like me, it just might be worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
