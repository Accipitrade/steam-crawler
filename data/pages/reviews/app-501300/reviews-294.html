\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch is weird. Very, very weird. But not in a bad way at all. The game has some great lighting and while some areas may look a little dated, my overall consensus on the graphics are that they are good enough to be very immersive. The story itself is gripping and is constantly interesting, with each chapter feeling like an entirely different game. In one part I found myself playing two games at once, which I found really quite unique. The voice acting is also very strong, and strong emotion is displayed throughout the game.

While the game itself is short in length, around 2 hours long, the story is rich and compelling as each stage of the game feels new and refreshing. I was gripped for the entirety of the playthrough. I highly recommend this game to anyone who wants a incredibly unique gaming experience which I will never forget. This game is the complete package. If you haven't played it yet, you are missing out on an incredible experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just wanted a quick palette cleanser in between XCOM2 and XCOM: Chimera Squad. I didn't expect a walking sim to break me mentally and emotionally. Okay, no, let's not call it a walking sim, it feels like a derogatory term. I'd prefer the term Interactive Sim/Story.

Anyways! So! What Remains of Edith Finch is an interactive story where you control Edith Finch, a pregnant women in her early 20's revisiting and exploring her childhood home. Almost everybody's room is locked up, but you do some really easy puzzles, or well, navigation to unlock rooms and learn about different members of the Finch family.

The Finch family is cursed and you learn about who each member is through exploring their rooms and reading their journal entries. Upon reading their journal entries, every mini-story becomes its own mini-game with its own mechanics that suit the style and structure of that family member's particular story.

I think the curse is that each member dies early in very unique ways, some of which are shocking, sad, and some being plain out confusing asking you WHY?! Just WHY?!The whole time I played this game, I got goosebumps getting to the end of each family member's story. Not to mention of course how engaged I felt with how the game changed it up per family member.

This is a very short game that I beat within 2 hours and while it is $20, I do think it is worth the full price because think about it: You watch movies that are now 2-3 hours long for how much? Like $10-15 depending on standard vs 3D, and depending on your age? THIS is a game where you interact with things of your own volition all at your own pace. If you really wanted to extend your play time you could stop and smell the roses, admiring the detail in each part of the Finch house.

Now that said, even if you breeze through it going from story point to story point, this game has something a lot of games just don't have anymore: concision. You are barely ever lost with nothing to do, it moves along greatly. And even if you don't stop to smell the roses, a quick glimpse of any Finch family member's room gives you the idea of who they were and what they were like just as much as it is when you get to play their section in their POV.

It's a very poignant game that excels in narration and subtle detail to drive a very bone chilling narrative. Full price or on sale, this is a game that is perfect for a jam packed 2 hours that you would want to revisit another time later to catch and understand more details of it that you didn't catch the first time around. I may not go ahead and replay it any time soon, but I've got a feeling that I will be reflecting on some of the Finch family members' individual stories in the next few days to come...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll make this review brief.

The premise of What Remains of Edith Finch is fairly simple. You have to figure out the meaning of the "curse" surrounding the Finch family that causes only one member per generation to survive. What Remains of Edith Finch is one of those games that come along once in a decade. It's an artistic marvel that proves that games can be used for more than a sole time waster. Once you finish it you may even consider it to be an example of video games as an art form.

It's thought-provoking and it gets you thinking about the true meaning of the "curse" of the Finch family. The game is basically a walking simulator but that nevertheless has many moments that change the gameplay up to offer a variety of ways that you interact with the game. However, the fact that it's a walking simulator doesn't mean it doesn't have any enjoyment to offer. It's very much a story-driven game which can be thought of as an interactive book. As someone who puts story first, gameplay second in a game I think its story a very compelling one with moments that can make you laugh, cry, smile, and shock you.

In any case, my final statement is that if you enjoy narrative-driven games you'll find that What Remains of Edith Finch is one that you'll heavily enjoy and come out of thinking that it was very much worth your time and money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is not really a game but more a walking adventure that does not really require any skill but does introduce some novel and enjoyable ways to interact with the story as you progress. The graphics are adequate and the feeling of the game is real and sincere. The narration however is 1st class and really makes the atmosphere grow during your play. The one thing I still don't understand is why so many reviews gave it such a high score, I agree that it is a very polished piece of entertainment but I also think a lot more could have been done. After completing it I went back to complete all the goals, and I could not help but think that they has missed the opportunity to make it either darker and more foreboding or alternatively lighter and more fun. This game sits somewhere in between and does not for me, really answer the core question of even why Edith really bothered to go back to the house.

With that all said, I recommend that if this is on sale you buy if for the experience alone. I will continue to buy and support games like this just because they dare to do something different.\t\t\t\t\t
