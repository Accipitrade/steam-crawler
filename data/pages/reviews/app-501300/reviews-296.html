\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMore of an interactive story than a game. That being said'

What Remains Of Edith Finch is an incredibly enjoyable and immersive experience that I'd recommend to anyone who enjoys story led games.

The visuals, soundtrack and atmosphere all gel together perfectly and it's clear that so much passion and creativity was put into making this game.

For those saying the length of the game (Roughly 2 hours, give or take) is not long enough, I have to disagree.
Although What Remains of Edith Finch is what you could say a 'short' experience, it compliments it nicely as it's best to play in one sitting.

Purely for the experience that stays with you. This is 9/10, easy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never played a non-competitive game before so this was a brand new experience for me. I loved the art, voice acting, and the gradually uncovered secrets of the Finch family. I noticed countless 'easter egg' type background details that added a lot of interest and depth to the story--and I feel certain there were plenty more that I did not notice in this first play-through. The layers of psychological and mythological storytelling/creation of a single family's misfortunes are fascinating and twisted and supernatural and totally realistic...if that sounds contradictory...well that's life. And I'm trying not to give any spoilers. LSS: really loved this strange game and will likely play it at least once more, beginning to end.\t\t\t\t\t
