\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is well crafted and made with passion, but massively overrated by the community.

A 2 hour visual, narrated journey, you follow the story of Edith Finch exploring her ancestral history in her family home. A seemingly cursed family, one that has made its way into the news several times, you learn about each family member and live out their history. You do this until the conclusion, which is meant to take you by surprise.

You learn about your family history and... that's it. It is the journey that is supposed to matter, the visual telling of each family member's history. Sure, those are all well done, but ultimately there is not enough substance here. No more substance than Gone Home, which succeeds more at capturing nostalgic feelings. Less substance than The Vanishing of Ethan Carter which seems to be a primary inspiration for this.

The game is emotionally effective, it does what it sets out to do and does it right, but the people calling this a story driven masterpiece don't know the first thing about writing in any medium. Something this lacking of substance can never claim that title, especially when real masterpieces like Planescape: Torment exist. This game is also technically a bit of a mess, only supporting 2 or 3 pre-set resolutions (all 16:9 only).

But Giant Sparrow shows talent in narrative design. I would like to see them attempt something greater.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely beautiful game.

I did not know what I was going into when I bought it. I blindly trusted the reviews, which were Overwhelmingly positives, and I was not wrong. This is game full of mysteries, but you never need to search for answers, they just come at the right pace, you feel just like in a movie, at every second of it.

it is poetic, words are carefully chosen, not a word above another, not a single one missing. You discover all the drama behind this familly, without ever knowing your own fate.

Gameplay wise, the only fact that you control the doors with the mouse and keyboard and you dictate the pace of interaction with the environment, is enough for you to make you feel like you have control on the story. Which in the end, you don't. Although the game is mainly a First Pers. , you will experiment with lots of minigames that switch the game play a little, or entierely.

If you are looking for a relaxing yet stressful game at time. A game where you are a young women, but also a cat, a couragous boy, a frog and an andventurer. This is a game you need. Just to get time off from a intense CS or DOTA round.

This wonder, is 2 hours of pure perfect Game design.\t\t\t\t\t
