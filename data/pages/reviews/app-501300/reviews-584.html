\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's kind of crazy that I'm saying this again considering how I felt after playing The Beginner's Guide (and that game came out 2 years ago) but this is really one of those 'interactive movie' kind of stories that is much better when going in blind, which makes writing a review, again, pretty difficult. Literally what I said in my review for that game absolutely applies for 'What Remains of Edith Finch' but for different reasons. Where as the former looked at game development in a more human and somber way, Edith Finch is a tale about a family that falls apart due to fantastical yet believable circumstances once you see the deeper meanings behind them. Mild spoilers for things I felt were worth mentioning: The story of Lewis is kind of amazing, considering how accurate this game manages to convey what daydreaming is like, considering I'm heavily guilty of it myself. Also, the ending, not to spoil what happens too much, but wow, so tragic yet so perfect at the same time. I really don't know how this could have ended any other way.

If you like these kinds of 'interactive movie' games or ever wanted to give one a try, look at this one. This is probably going to end up being the best of this whole sub-genre of games in 2017.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat an amazing and heartfelt story. This is what walking simulators are meant to be. Don't get me wrong, I enjoyed Dear Esther and Old City Leviathan, I just wish they made more sense. Vanishing of Ethan Carter was better in terms of story, but Edith Finch is the best out of all of them so far for me. What it lacks in length (about 2.5 hrs) it more than makes up for in emotional heft and the way it portrays character. Seriously the characters discussed are almost never phsyically present, yet just by walking through their rooms in the house you get an intimate sense of who they are. Just look at the book names on their shelves from room to room and you get the sense that these are real people with real lives living in a real home. The ending definitely pulls on the heartstrings. Games like this deserve your time, money, and attention. This is the future of storytelling right here.\t\t\t\t\t

An interactive movie that you should get when it's on sale. 7/10

Ps & Cs
+ Beautiful graphics
+ Well designed world
+ Creative vignettes
+ Fully voice acted by good actors
- Not a ton for the player to do
- Not much is interactive
- Very short (2.5 hours)

Nitty Gritty
This game is a walking simulator through a beautifully designed mansion. You walk around discovering the fate of various members of your family. Overall, there's not a whole hell of a lot to do, as you just follow a path, hallway, tunnel, etc to the next location and vignette. As a $60 game, I would say don't bother. As a $20 game, I can't be too hard on it. That said, you should still get it on sale. 7/10\t\t\t\t\t

From the makers of the magnificent game "The Unfinished Swan" comes another weird tale where everything isn't quite what it seems. Boasting some gorgeous visuals and a fantastic narrative, What Remains manages to add some much appretiated gameplay to the now tried and tested "walking simulator", as many like to call them.
