\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUghh...these kind of games can be so frustrating....


Its a clever premise and certainly creative throughout, but, a lot like Giant Sparrow's previous game (Unfinished Swan) It clearly doesn't know where to go with it, and in the end just kind of peters out. Opting for an abrubt, ambgious conclusion that essentially claims that it was the ride that mattered, not the meaning. And, like the Unfinished swan, the ride is indeed fascinating, but, like that game, I ended feeling irritated, not inspired. Like so many so called "walking simulators" my final thought is "Thats it?"

I suppose its worth a playthrough as its interesting and unique, but be aware, it is indeed a walking simulator, with very limited interaction in favor of controlling the narrative, I don't mind that, but if you do, its probably not for you, also be aware that its quite short, taking me just about 2 hours exactly, though that felt right from a pacing standpoint.

If you like creative, ambigous, artsy nonsense you'll have fun, but thats all it really is. Get it cheap.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch......Be amazed that any of us ever had a chance to be here at all.


If you enjoy walking simulators you will love this game, of all the walking simulators I had the pleassure or displeassure to play this one is King.

From the art style,graphics,music,voice acting, writing,story it is all excellent.

The moment I started playing the game I could not let go, the different stories the game has are engaging and some even thoughtprovoking.

You walk to the house and inside you try to get in the different rooms of the Finch family with in each of them you play a story that varies in gamelay, out of all of them there is only one story that I did not like and that is the first one ( mollys) for gameplay reasons.
The rest are great.

It is a very linear game and it is capable of making you feel a good variety of emotions.

So what did I NOT like of this game ?
You cant run in this game.
Mollys story.
Game is quite on the short side with no real replayability.
The games price is $19 which do seems a little too much for what it offers.

But to be honest these are more nitpicks than anything else from my part. The game has a great story and art it is a must play for anyone looking for a very good story and for those who enjoy walking simulators.

What Remains of Editch Finsh gets an 9 out of 10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's be honest. This is not a fresh take on a new game. This one's been out for a while, and I've only just gotten around to beating it because of a Steam event.

There. That's been said.

So. This game does something that many art games only dream of achieving. It uses a minium of control and actual game elements to tell an engaging story. And I do mean this, it's an honestly engaging story, strange and wonderful, with too few explanations but with quite a few moments of beauty.

In the end, this IS a walking simulator, you're only just moving through the environment as the story tells itself. But it tells itself through actual interaction, through discovery and puzzle solving. And even if the puzzles are VERY simple, they're also all interesting and more than worth your time.

That's about it. Big thumbs up.\t\t\t\t\t

this is one of the better walking sims I've played. I've been on a bit of a kick toward this style of game and i'm really happy with this one. for one the story is compelling. you know just enough to motivate you but they keep just enough a mystery to keep you intrigued. the voice acting is really solid and makes the game in allot of ways. the writing too. the world feels very lived in. very organic. tells allot of the story by itself. the game also does enough to feel like a game. it keeps the player involved enough to keep you occupied while you drink in the story and the world. my one complaint is i would have liked to walk a little faster. you don't notice it once the game gets going but at first its really slow.

if you're down for a walking sim i really recommend this one.\t\t\t\t\t
