The game lasted me about 2.5 hours for me, so it's like paying a ticket to see a long, interactive movie which made the price pretty reasonable.

However, *possible minor spoilers* I was disappointed by the plot holes and how the ending felt incomplete. Playing this was a unique and creative experience, but I wouldn't really qualify this as a mystery since not much is solved. I still recommend playing this game if you enjoy immersive, narrative heavy games with a lot of love and detail put into it, but I would recommend buying it on sale if you aren't a fan of open endings or lack of answers like me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWords are not enough as to fully describe the experience that is What Remains of Edith Finch.

This is an intricately crafted game, centered on the exploration of an old house as eclectic as the people who once lived there, and most certainly not up to any modern building code.

Each room brings with its own story, with its own existential crisis, creating a polyglot story that builds up to an insurmountable ending slowly hinted at over the progression of the game.

With such a compelling story and wonderfully gorgeous presentation, What Remains of Edith Finch is a game one certainly cannot afford to miss.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, umm, WOW! This game was surprisingly incredible! I got in on PS4, saw it on the Steam sale and just picked it up on PC (if my hrs are low)

I usually am bored with games of the so-called "walking simulator" variety, but this game was just fantastic. It was a beautiful, great graphics, solid acting, and most-importantly, great writing with a mystery that really gets you wondering. The house is this beautiful, creepy mess of normal that just feels "off" a little too. You'll see. The mystery gives you breadcrumbs with perfect pacing.

Seriously, PERFECT PACING!

Many will complain about how this is something you can finish in 4 or 5 hours, and they are 100% right, but there is so much content crammed into those few hours it is just so impressive. Rather than follow many typical gaming tropes where you artificially drive up playtime with backtracking, tedious puzzles, or whatever, the game just takes you from one page of this story to the next with little delay. It really is phenomenal.

I sat on my couch with 2 of my daughters and they were just glued to the screen, mesmorized with the house. We ended up spending the next several days talking about cool things if we built our own house, from secret passages, to hidden doors. The fact that this is a game story built around the imaginations of children in their bedrooms, only to see it inspire my own kids to dream and imagine and so on just goes to show you how great of a storytelling immersive experience this is.

Really phenomenal. Honestly, while I only paid 10 bucks for the Steam summer sale price, this is worth 20 bucks. Truly remarkable game.

Thank you Giant Sparrow. Amazing creative talent you have. I wish you all the best in your professional careers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would not pay more than $10 for this game. I got it on sale, thus the thumbs up.

****SPOILERS**** ****SPOILERS**** ****SPOILERS**** ****SPOILERS**** ****SPOILERS****





It's a very interesting story with some really great gameplay and creativity. It's less than 2 hours of play time, though, which makes this game kind of a stretch. I like story-rich walking simulators, but this was especially short and especially unfulfilling in the end.

Basically, you find out this family has the generic "family curse" trope where everyone dies too young or in especially gruesome/sad ways. Honestly, I was hoping for some kind of delving into the cause of it, or maybe some deeper meaning in it all, but it has this very generic "cherish every day like its your last" theme. The idea is that the family is so concerned with death and the past and their curse that they don't live their lives to the fullest.

This being the entire sort of point of the story makes it seem a bit empty. It's nothing all that new and it really doesn't have much of a point in the end. It's interesting, but it surely isn't interesting enough to be worth +$10 for less than two hours of entertainment. I had more entertainment and thought in Painscreek Killings than this game.

What it lacks in lack of unique or fruitful story, it makes up for in very unique gameplay. I appreciate the meshes of game design and game mechanics for each person you learn about. I have a feeling this is where most of the positive feedback comes from. I think most people would find this game enjoyable and intersting. I just don't think it's worth the pricetag it has whatsoever.\t\t\t\t\t
