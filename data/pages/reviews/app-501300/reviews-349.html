\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch bewilders me even after I've played it. How can an artistic story of loss and visions of loved one's memories not be talked about more? Everything in What Remains of Edith Finch has a feeling of love and craftsmanship that many triple AAA titles lack. The home itself feels cozy and lived in and in an almost surreal type way. It was hard to believe that I finished this game and didn't notice how 2hrs went by. What Remains of Edith Finch's story, musical atmosphere, and world construction has a strange but sad beauty. I found myself often times getting entranced by each story to a degree I didn't want them to end. Even though this game is what most people would call a walking simulator, it's probably the strongest point. Most likely because they could emphasize world creation and storytelling. This being easily the strongest aspect of the game I think if they went in another direction the story wouldn't have been so poignant. Along with the masterful inclusion of musical landscapes that only work to elevate the narrative to ever greater heights. I would definitely recommend this to anyone who doesn't play video games. Its direction of storytelling is definitely made for any movie fan out there. I would totally recommend this to any person, but the only downside to this game is that it's only 2hrs long.\t\t\t\t\t
