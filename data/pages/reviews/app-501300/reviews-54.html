\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTremendous storytelling. One of the few examples of how to do an emotional and gripping walking sim. It doesn't get much better, so the hype is justified. A perfect encapsulation of a game you can just sit down and play no matter who you are. There isn't much else to say but to remind you to play this.

Giant Sparrow is at their best when telling stories we can relate with. Edith as a game uses a detailed family tree that trails off in all kinds of interesting directions via the people that inhabit it, narrating each person through incredibly varied perspectives that all come back to meld in a cohesive whole. And that family house. Its secrets are so joyful and sad and warm. Discovering them is like unearthing your past. At best this is a game that resides in nostalgia and memories.

The spiritually uplifting music is a gorgeous fit with the cozy nature of the story. On a 4K OLED, I would say Edith does not have top-end graphics, but they for sure do the job. Walking outside at night above the sea is so peaceful. And all the delicately designed rooms and areas remind you this is a dev that cares about small details. A lot of love and small details. And that's what counts here.\t\t\t\t\t
