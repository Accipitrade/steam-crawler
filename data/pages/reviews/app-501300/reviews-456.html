\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvery once in a while a game comes along that totally redefines your expectations. As far as narrative storytelling goes, we have seen a wide range of genres attempt to take on interactive stories, from the early text adventures Dungeon and Zork to Sierra's timeless adventure games, and through to modern day visual novels. However, none can compare to What Remains of Edith Finch in terms of both story and artistry.

Throughout the game, you play as Edith, exploring your family home and unravelling the stories your mother tried to hide from you. Every sealed room puts you in the shoes of another ill-fated relative as you learn the the tragic tales that have left Edith as the last Finch. The game itself isn't overly difficult, but there is a lot to see and experience.

One of the cornerstones of the game is in its artistry. The text floats in mid-air and often interacts with you. Some stories are told from the perspective of that family member, where others are told in multiple perspectives or even as a comic book adaptation. Every nook and cranny of the house is filled with detail, and the narration takes on different tones that match the individual, as well as the overarching narratives of Edith herself.

There are a few mysteries left to interpretation, leaving room for a prequel or sequel, although the game itself is self-contained and spans several generations of Finch. Upon completion, you can go back and revisit specific family members or replay the entire game if you so choose. Considering how many little details there are from the first moments to the end of the credits and how deep the narrative can go, the Finches are a family you will want to revisit time and again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat remains of Edith Finch is a sequence of stories told in a singular non-linear narrative in a video game format. It offers practically no challenge, has invisible walls restricting your access and next to no player choice. It is also worth every penny you pay for it (and probably more if you got it on discount as I did).

Astonishingly beautiful and downright heart-wrenching, Edith Finch transports you to its world like few others have managed. It is a game about death that celebrates the lives of its characters. No two of the eclectic Finches have similar stories, and each's is special in their own way. No two stories are similar, each depicted with such creativity and panache that you can't help but smile (till the game wipes it away with the inevitable conclusion you knew it was building towards). It converts routine activities to tense, magical moments - dreading it at the same time as you are looking forward to the wonderous places you knew the story would take you.

If you like good stories, seek out the stories of the Finches and you won't be disappointed. One may end up heartbroken and disconsolate, but few heartbreaks are as worth it as the tragedy of the Finches.

10/10\t\t\t\t\t
