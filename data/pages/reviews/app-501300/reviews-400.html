\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst things first, this isn't a game, it's a collection of interactive visual short stories that are tied together by a central plot. You can control your character to some extent, but your only option is to progress forward through a linear storyline, with no opportunities for variation or player choice. That isn't necessarily a bad thing, but it's important to keep in mind if you're considering getting this "game".

The reason I don't recommend Edith Finch is that it feels more like a showcase of a storytelling medium than a story in and of itself. The way the character plays through some of the short stories is really creative, and it demonstrates the huge untapped potential of this medium. However, the stories (and the central plot) just aren't that good. They're very short and very predictable. The game just doesn't compare to good-quality paper literature; it's clear that the developers had to sacrifice both length and substance to put their story into this format.

I can't wait for major companies to start using this medium. With a bigger budget, some really amazing stories could be brought to life. As it is though, this game feels like an abridged knockoff of One Hundred Years of Solitude, and I'd rather spend two weeks reading that book than two hours playing this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe most important thing to understand before playing this game is that you need to have the right mindset. Drop the hope for intense gunfights, tedious level grinding, and the likes. Open your mind for a different experience. Enjoy what is presented before you. Drink your beer, light your weed: you need to relax, you won't need your reflex.

Some people will actually benefit from a little spoiler. If you're into that type, read on. Otherwise stop here.

This game tells the story of dead people. Stories about death is never easy, yet this game uniquely delivered it in a lively (pun intended) manner.

Each story is delivered in different narrative style. Trough first person account of someone who had been in realization of her coming death; through third person account of someone witnessing the incoming tragic death of her sibling; and many more.
Not only that, each also has different artistic style.
Each story, each died person has real character, details you can really relate.

Truly awesome writing and production.\t\t\t\t\t
