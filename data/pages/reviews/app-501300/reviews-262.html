\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYikes, man. This game is three hours of heart-wrenching story-telling of the darkest kind. I have never before -- and likely never will again -- be so moved by childhood poisonings, infant drownings, and falls off cliffs.

The game is NOT horror, although it largely takes place in a darkened and twisted house that exudes a heady mix of mental discomfort and cozy reading nooks. There's a brief trip through a cheesy horror comic book, but even that isn't really done with jump scares or anything like that.

No, this game's weapon is your own empathy. This game makes you take part in your own end, repeatedly. It does so in a lush, beautifully rendered environment, filled with perfect music cues, and constantly shifting techniques.

Despite its under-three hour finish and nigh-zero replayability, it is quite simply one of the finest games ever. It is worth its full inexpensive price of $20, and participates in Steam sales that make it a damned STEAL.

Seriously. This is a Good Game.\t\t\t\t\t
