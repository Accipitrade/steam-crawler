It's a story about a "cursed" family, and the weird and tragic things that have happened to them over the years. Each story is unique both in style, and in the kind of thing you have to do to get through it. Most of the gameplay other than the "mini-games" is just walking through the house and discovering snippets about the FInch's lives, but nothing overstayed its welcome - the scenes are gorgeous to look at and Edith's little comments about her previous life were lovely to find.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10/10 Masterpiece

The house is amazingly decorated and designed. What a cool house! And the entire thing is adorned with items related to the family story (old and new pictures, instruments, books, themed bedrooms: everything you learn about the family can be found in the house). This adds a layer of depth and believability. Often when I play a 'house' game it's assets are underwhelming and the house seems plain and boring. This house, is very very impressively decorated.

What can I say, this is one of the best games I've played. That 'overwhelmingly positive' rated game slot on Steam is actually a masterpiece. It's a walk simulator that delivers. The story line is perfect and the protagonist story-line is impeccable. Often times we gamers try to find the perfect game to explain how games are done. This is an example of that. It's a walk sim so there's a lot of ground to cover here, as this is as close as we'll come to perfection.

The graphics and sound are brilliant, the ramp up to the ending is great, the characters are perfect, every thing is on point. The game is very short (4-5 hours) so watch the price before you buy. If you're are looking for a game to break up the monotony of shooter after shooter than this game is for you.

I could type 2 hundred words about this game and never come close to the feeling I got after finishing it. Buy this game (on sale cause it's short) and enjoy an incredible game that has filled a spot than up til now no games has.\t\t\t\t\t

The Remains of Unfortunate Events

What Remains of Edith Finch is a wonderful walking sim/story driven game. A game which shows that games are art.

It's an extremely short and simple game yet the story is very good and is presented beautifully.
You'll be walking through most of the game. You arrive at the old family house of yours and go through the memories of your dead family members and rummage through their past.

Each family member has his story to tell and each story is presented in a different manner. This is when gameplay changes from simply walking to something else. Like crawling, flying or some side-scrolling mini-game. This is why this walking sim is so different. There's not a second you are bored. Even when you're just walking around admiring the beautiful scenery there's always either some voiced lines telling the story or you just look around at all the rooms and things around which tells a little about the background of those characters without any words.

The story is really good although sad (which I love) and many scenes and gameplay sequences could be a little depressing but they are so very well told and presented that I can't stress this enough. I won't spoil anything because any little detail for such a short game could be important.
There are many rooms to explore and each of them is unique. Some areas can be a bit creepy, some adorable and cute but mostly it's pretty depressing.
That's pretty much it when it comes to gameplay and story.

From the technical side the game is great. Graphics are wonderful with lots of details and constantly changing scenes and scenery. There are some minor negatives like pictures being low resolution or same rows of book patterns thrown all over the room. I love to look around in such games so I decided to look what these people loved to read and was a bit disappointed seeing same books scattered around. But as I mentioned that's so minor that it's not important. Overall the game looks beautiful.

Performance is perfect, game ran on 90-100fps for me on max settings. Played on 5800x, 2070s, 32gb @3440x1440.

Unfortunately there's no ultrawide support. Don't know why developers of such games keep ignoring it since it's extremely easy to implement. Making ultrawide to work is possible but it's an annoying process which must be done each time launching the game. And it looks amazing in ultrawide, so much better than 16:9.

The game has chapter select so collecting achievements isn't a pain and you don't need to spend another 3 hours collecting them.

Voice acting is great too. Main character sounds really nice and believable, calm voice telling the story, I felt really connected to her.
Music plays a part too and again like nearly everything in this game it's great and music reflects what you see on the screen very well.

Overall a wonderful walking sim with great presentation, some quite original gameplay ideas and great story. Although it's very short and the asking price is kinda high so get it on sale. But if your financial situation is great and your money doesn't fit into your pockets, I suggest supporting these developers.

Pros
  • Good story
  • Nice innovative presentation which is a nice change from constant walking
  • Sound design is good
  • Looks beautiful
  • Runs great
  • Chapter select

Cons
  • No ultrawide support
