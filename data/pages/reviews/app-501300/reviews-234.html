\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeautiful. Simply beautiful. What Remains of Edith Finch is mere poetry. It has the subtlety, love, and beauty to tell you a deep story, tremendously heartbreaking and sad but exciting. It has ups and downs like a roller coaster.
Her entire existence is the purest and most complex way of embellishing the misfortune of death.
It is a romanticism that lives in the art of metaphors.
Talking about death as something that you should not be afraid of because it is that closure that gives meaning to everything.
However, it is a fear that must be faced and overcome, otherwise living in fear of dying is the same as being dead wanting to live.
I had never played a video game like this.
The visual setting, that is to say: the landscape, the architecture, the "passageways", the little puzzles, among other things, are something that makes you fall in love at first sight.
Its playability is so satisfying. Simply simple and easy to handle. There are even mechanical changes at specific moments in history.
Camera movements, gameplay; everything is perfectly well done.
Its soundtrack is relaxing. It connects you with what is happening, with the tranquility that is inside and outside the story. Makes the whole a good audiovisual setting.
I fully recommend it. It leaves the daily in search of telling you a story of what loss is, the battle against denial, and acceptance of what happened and unstoppable.
It shows you the life itself but not as something negative, but as something beautiful and joyful to live and remember, it gives you the ability to enjoy everything, even after one last breath.\t\t\t\t\t
