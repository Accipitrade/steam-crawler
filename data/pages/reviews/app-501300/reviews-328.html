\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first thing they did when they moved to America was build a cemetery, a wise move for a family whose members rarely reached old age. What Remains of Edith Finch is what Edward Gorey would look like as a video game. When i bought it i expected a game about someone walking through a house uncovering a story about a family. i did not expect a serial killer, a shark, a magic door, a fantasy kingdom and catching rabbits. i did not expect part of the story to be told through an interactive comic book, an old school video game and a flip book. i did not expect the many, many ways people died. And i definitely did not expect a dozen dancing rubber duckies.

What Remains is a set of unrelated stories told in different ways from the perspective of the people whose last minutes are chronicled. It's not scary or gory or action oriented. i think there's only one scene of blood, and that from when grandpa cuts himself making wooden garden gnomes in his workshop. There aren't good guys or bad guys or quests to go on. It's like Spoon River Anthology, a group of people long dead telling their stories. And it's absolutely fantastic.\t\t\t\t\t
