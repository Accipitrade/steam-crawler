\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat remains of Edith Finch? Nothing. You don't get to know what happened to her and her family. All you get is her tombstone at the end with her son holding her diary, which explained nothing. I don't think it's the curse. No monsters and ghosts either. Most of the Finches died of either neglect or stupidity, or both.

The story I didn't like the most is about GREGORY. You get to play Gregory's bath toys, and kinda made it look like that the baby is the one responsible for his own death by filling the tub with water and choosing the drain, thus drowning. But it's actually parental neglect that killed the baby. The father's storytelling (when you get to read the divorce papers) made it look like that the baby is way too imaginative.

The Finches are a bunch of neglectful parents with a huge ancestral home where everyone gets their own room, doesn't matter which part of the house they'd build it or if would make the family members feel disconnected, hence LEWIS.

The worst part? The game makes you nauseous! It's a very short story/game that can be finished in one sitting, but it took me 4.

Also, Edith's surname is Kumar. Her mother is married to Sanjay Kumar. So why is she using the surname Finch? And who knocked her up?

Great graphics though!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI understand this review is going to seem quite contrary to the majority of reviews here but I feel like it must be said. The approach for showing each of the stories through little mini-games and changes in point of view are unique, well done, and seamless. This is probably the highlight and main draw of the game. However, the mystery and overarching plot are rather non-existent and surface level. After the ending you are left wondering is that it? There is no sense of fulfillment or strong theme that ties the narrative together well and you are left with a sense of emptiness. Is it a bad game? No not at all. Unique, fun, and mysterious but lacks the feeling of being solid and fleshed out which, in my opinion, the interesting narrative approach does not make up for. In addition, for the price, the game is only 2-3 hours. If you buy I suggest doing it when it is on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

You, the player, play people about to die on several occasions, and it feels that it is your actions that lead them to their deaths.
The following trigger warnings should had been mentioned beforehand:
Child neglect, bordering to abuse
Death of a baby, accompanied by a joyous music
Gruesome suicide (albeit not graphically displayed)
Joseph Anderson explains it better than I do.

Also, not giving the possibility of changing the controls and remapping the keys is unprofessional. I do not use a QWERTY keyboard, I am not an exception: this should now be good practice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInteresting exploration game where you learn about your family tree. You explore a cool house with lots of hidden rooms and hallways and in each room you learn more about a family member from your family tree. Overall the stories are interesting since they are short and to the point, but provide an escape from the real game too by providing an imaginative way of storytelling.

The graphics are incredible for this type of game. By not having to move quickly or have action on the screen, this allows for ample room to enhance the scenery with details and "What Remains of Edith Finch" does not hold back on detail. The voice over is good too, both adults and children sound really good and come across believably and add well to the storyline and plot.

It is a short game, but I prefer it to be this way as it's meant to be a quick experience not a rinse and repeat type of experience that most games provide. Overall, I felt that the storyline and gaming experience provided something somewhat unique and that I would recommend everyone give this one a shot as it is something that will stand out and be remembered.

The achievements are easy, and they forced me to replay a couple of stories since I had missed them. They are all possible to achieve on the first run through. I do admit that I felt the movement was a bit slow at times, but then again it's not a long game, and it's not an action game so it only bothered for a moment where it was obvious you needed to walk further than usual. The game makes up for this though by continuing the narration while you are walking and at times providing interruption to your experience to distract you from the fact that you are walking. But, this only happened once or twice, it was never something that occurred throughout the game.\t\t\t\t\t
