\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's an experience I cannot describe and I am not gonna try to. Just play it.

Level Design
it subverts all the expectations i expect of a studio this size. the one word to describe the level design in this game is "personal". Every level has it's own unique asset. So unlike most games with repeated models, every room in this game is modeled that is not just detailed, but personal. like every part of the room tells you something about that person.

Writing
Oh god the writing is so robust. The setups and pay-offs, the pacing and foreshadowing are all so well done. There are many things that you can inspect but would only make sense later in the game after you know the stories.
If I describe to you the characters in this game you'd think they are whimsical. But holy cow this ♥♥♥♥ actually works. And it works wonders. Every person feels like a person to a point that you just couldn't care less about the stupid technical loopholes on the larger scheme. And I think the intimacy of the level design really aids this as well. it feels like a real place where people have lived. it's so full of footprints of people living there that you'd just forget about the stupid logic that they extended 3 buildings on top of the roof with super fragile structure and in a region that has strong winds.

Graphics
The game doesn't have red dead level graphic. But playing this game, you can really feel that the developers doesn't use it as an excuse to let go of the graphics aspect. The graphic level is enough that it's serviceable and I sometimes forgot that I am in a game and just think I am in real life (again, the detailed and personal level design really helped this, too)

Mini games
The mini games are all very creative. God, we should be embarrassed as a specie that AAA games now can only follow the GTA template of game structure.(a.k.a. open world where you can enter vehicles and drive to a mission) This and It Takes Two are one of the few games nowadays that are still trying to utilize the full potential of video games, of what a video game can be. Also, the minigame physics is very good. Like the tentacle physics or the swing physics, they actually modeled the swing physics and you can even swing your leg seperately. It's a mastapiece. Mwah

I accidentally launched this game before bed and the ambiance, the mood and the story just immediately drew me in and I ended up just finishing that game, damn.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStunningly gorgeous. 100% worth full price; even if it's only a couple hours long, if you have even a mild interest in walking simulators, minigames, and/or story-driven gaming, this title will be one of the richest experiences you'll have. The tone is perfectly crafted, balancing the quirky and labyrinthine humor of a clustered family tree with a startingly grim and tragic reflection on how the fear of danger often leads to its own damage.

You'll grow to love this family. Some reviewers may say that the plot feels jumpy and groundless at times, but I think that's due to a difference in perspectives. This is supposed to feel less like an overarching drama or mystery plot and more like an anthology chronicling the slow, bittersweet disintegration of a family, with one member providing her reflections on the people she's lost.

Even having only just recently finished the title, I personally would rank this within my top ten favorite games of all time. I will revisit this family soon; they damage the comfortable and comfort the damaged, and this game helps me to feel both ways.\t\t\t\t\t
