\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's difficult to say if I truly liked this game, but it's also far from the truth to say I disliked it. I initially started the game imagining that it'd be somewhat of a horror story (which I generally dislike, btw) but I was intrigued by the visuals and the reviews and decided to just give it a go.

What met me was not just an interactive game, but an interactive story within a story. Aside from the multiple sub-characters you get to play aside from your own, each character comes with their own unique story and gameplay to completing their storyline. I was amazed at the level of detail put into each character's story, of how it stood well alone but still fit like a puzzle to the overall flow. You come into the whole thing thinking "this is just it" but come out surprised at how developers created so many levels to it; there is so much depth to each character's storyline as it tackles some real life (psychological/mental) issues that give players a look into what life is like from someone else's POV. I think that's one of the best things to the game - what you get out of learning how to put yourself in other's shoes, or seeing things from other people's perspectives.

The animation is also just, amazing - I was blown away by the realistic character movement, the various styles of gameplay rolled into one, and the level of design put into every aspect of the game. Developers did not skimp on making certain areas of the game more detailed than the rest - everything was equally detail-oriented.

If there was any one issue that I had, it was with the slight bugs in some of the gameplay. Most of the game is a trial and error basis so it doesn't really tell you how you can complete the storylines or what to do next; the story and narration gives hints but most of it is left up to the player to figure out. But with the bugs, sometimes it's hard to tell if you're doing that part of the game right - unless you look it up online or read a walkthrough/guide.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA walking simulator with beautiful sceneries, seasoning with intriguing storytelling.

I enjoy a good story, and I enjoy a good storytelling even more. What Remain Of Edith Finch mixes up its stories with several bizarre yet fitting visual narration. A fantasy of a child, a crazy yet cheerful imagination of a baby, an unexpected american classic comic transition for a teenage star, a clayman style of sea-sailing for a depressed yound adult, and the list goes on. By doing so, the game presents the personality of each characters colorfully in the Finch family. What they all have in common, is the dark twist at the end. Every story coats itself with wholesome images and ambience, then slowly, gradually envelops the true color with breezing ease. This method really deepens the sadness. Just when we begin to like the character, the poor truth reveals to us.

When I see through the whole Finch family, something comes to my mind. Edith came back to the house because the death of her mother and a special key she got. Every room she visited she recollected the tragedy of a family member. Even though she had to remember them in such sorrowful way, Edith had a better understanding of the family. We don't need to wait to that extend. It's just like our family. Everyone has their own story to tell. I believe we all have elders who like to share their early life adventure to the children. No matter how trivial they might sound to you, they are your family, your very own legacy to deliver, a memory to remember them when they are gone.\t\t\t\t\t
