\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would give this game a "meh" review if I could, however like others, I am rating it overall negative to balance the overwhelmingly positive reviews and praise for this "game".

This game feels like a $20 dollar interactive movie experience. Think a Series of Unfortunate Events-like narrative but of an unfortunate family with incredibly disjointed narratives. The characters are so over-the-top fictational that it becomes difficult to feel impacted or affected emotionally by any of the stories and characters (besides one that relates to a man working a mundane job). It's absolutely beautiful and the environments are wonderful. It's a pretty and artistic game, however I can't stand these artistic games that get overwhelmingly positive reviews just for being quirky and pretty without anything inventive or deep in them.

TL;DR: WRoEF is like a $20 dollar whimsical but dark movie, of which, isn't even a particularly good movie.\t\t\t\t\t
