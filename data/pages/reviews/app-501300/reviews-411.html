\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been fairly lukewarm about narrative-focused games in the past (e.g. Dear Esther, Gone Home, The Stanley Parable, Brothers), but this one is special. Exploring the Finch family estate never loses its charm, even as the manifold tragedies of Edith's kin are revealed. The setting, story, and gameplay work together in perfect harmony, with each room and its associated character vignette presenting a poignant yet uniquely whimsical tale of life and death.

I bought this game on sale for $12, and while I don't forsee myself replaying the game in its entirety anytime soon, I will definitely be revisiting some of the individual chapters so that I can pick them apart. Whether $12 or $20, What Remains of Edith Finch may be a tough sell for those who turn up their noses at "walking simulators". This is a 2-3 hour narrative video game after all. However, the game succeeds where some of its contemporaries fall short: the narrative is actually enhanced by its interactivity, often in truly stunning ways.

tl;dr What Remains of Edith Finch may not be for everybody, but it deserves to be experienced first-hand.\t\t\t\t\t
