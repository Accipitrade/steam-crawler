What Remains of Edith Finch is truly captivating from start to finish and inspires an uncommonly wide range of emotions over a brief but compelling journey. I laughed, I cried, I wondered.

This wonderfully designed house is a charm to explore, filled with ingenious secret passages and backrooms that constantly brought back childhood fantasies of discovery and adventure. The game looks and sounds impeccable, with a unique personality and flair that completely differentiate it from other titles of the same genre.

Relive dreams and memories and play as animals and other family members. Each has their own special stories to tell from a very personal standpoint, such as comic book panels or as seen through the eyes of an imaginative toddler (or pothead factory worker).

Most of these stories are very touching in one way or another, while smooth controls make every different type of experience feel entirely natural. This diversity ensures that all your time spent in this carefully crafted world feels meaningful and valuable.

Positive
  • Masterful storytelling and writing
  • Unique and inspired environments, characters and stories
  • Beautiful, beautiful places
  • Deep melancholy and emotionally diverse
  • Unbelievably fitting voice overs
  • Illustrated notebook menu and unobtrusive text overlays directly in scenes subtly guide the player

Negative
  • Frankly, none

This is a truly remarkable narrative experience that sets a whole new benchmark in quality. Some people consider the length of a title as an important characteristic for their appreciation, but do not let its short duration fool you into missing out on what is an otherwise nearly perfect product. Highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLook, whatever the hell your favorite walking simulator is, unless they're named SOMA, just throw them into trash can. It would take an incredibly creative and talented crew to surpass what Giant Sparrow had achieved with What Remains of Edith Finch.

The game begins as your usual first person exploration experience that takes place at a Pacific Northwesthen house - a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ bizarre yet believably structured house at best. Hallway and stair are filled with stacks of books, doors have eyehole that allows you to peek right in, and the higher floors look like jigsaw pieces clumsily step on each other. Every room in this house tells a story about each deceased member of the Finch family, and the further you go, the more surreal the deaths become. What Remains of Edith Finch's tone dangles between a very thin line of reality and fantasy, where a lot of deaths are disturbingly told in a cheerful or vague manner. It's dark but not edgy, it's melancholic but also warm and heartfelt, it's strangely distant but humane at the same time.

Do me a favor and play it in one sitting (which only takes 2 hours), will ya?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t- MILD SPOILERS -

A young woman, Edith, explores her abandoned, childhood, family home. While exploring the creepy house, she learns about the disappearances or untimely deaths of her ancestors and immediate family. This is not really a game - it's more like a series of stories with some interactivity and exploring. Each family member has their own tale to tell and their story is usually revealed in a mini interactive experience.

The individual stories are the most successful part of the game in my opinion. Most of the stories are told very creatively with excellent use of the environment. The family's tales are all told in a different manner, ranging from an interactive, paranormal comic to just a simple animated fable. Some of the stories are very touching and it's genuinely intriguing to unravel each family member's tragic past. One tale in particular is beautifully made amd terribly sad - it should have been the core of the 'game'. You'll know it when you see it.

Sadly though, the central character that you play seems to have a lack of any real narrative except for wandering from room to room, unlocking rooms and stories. The ending was especially disappointing as it looked like the writers didn't really have a central story to tell, other than building a series of separate vignettes for you to experience. The reason why so many family members died prematurely seem to be unconnected and it's left up to the player to determine whether these deaths were coincidental or part of a family curse. It's never explained. If you're after a big reveal, you will be left wanting when the final credits roll.

Overall the mini stories make this a worthwhile experience and judging by the positive reviews, perhaps my expectations were too high. In my opinion, if you're after a satisfying story to go with your experience then I recommend Firewatch and The Vanishing of Ethan Carter.\t\t\t\t\t
