\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNever imagined I would be one who plays walking simulators but I've taken to them in recent years. It is nice to sometimes play a game with no combat, minimal puzzle solving, zero platforming, etc... In What Remains of Edith Finch you are given the simple task of returning and exploring the old family home.

The game is similar to previous walking simulators such as Gone Home or Dear Esther, but the difference in graphics, artistic design, voice acting and dialogue are much better developed in Finch. The creativity in this title comes from the inclusion of story-driven and episodic segments commemorating the strange and unusual demise of each family member.

We learn about the unlucky and seemingly cursed Finch family as Edith does, reliving their lives through memories, artifacts, letters, and memorials found in their now abandoned home. Though the various members of the family are interesting in their own right it's the home itself that takes center stage; the artistic design and exploration of each room and floor truly gives the feeling that it's a structure that has been built, rebuilt, and expanded many times throughout the years.

If you enjoy coming of age, reflective, nostalgic and solemn tales this game is sure to appeal to you.....unless of course you're a robot and lack an emotion engine.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a weird review to write, but I've never hated a game more, yet recommended it.
My issue is that this is a game about the characters that make up a family, but I find every member of that family to be insufferable. They're all that person you meet at a party sometimes who try too hard to seem quirky and interesting, yet the game seems to expect you to be fascinated by them. The game also hit an exact balance between real and surreal that I couldn't stand, where I just wanted the damn thing to decide whether it was supposed to be taking place in the real world or not. That's probably just me.
What I recommend is the presentation. The characters' stories are often told in interesting ways, with enough original twists to the presentation that I would recommend the game on that basis alone. You'll probably be more tolerant of the Finches than I was, so please give it a try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was never a big fan of walking sims before, but this game really changed my mind about them.

What Remains of Edith Finch has an eerie and alluring atmosphere, surprisingly engaging mechanics and a unique, emotional narrative. The game is very short and sweet, able to achieve everything it needs to in about 2hrs. As an added bonus, each Steam achievement is very easily obtainable.

Overall an excellent experience that I would highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
