\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow! Had my sights set on this game for sometime and must admit the expectations were overwhelming. This game did not just live up to them, it surpassed them in every way.
Now I've been playing games since my father walked in the door with a C64 back in the mid 80's so I've experienced pretty much every type of game, genre, bad to awful, brilliant or passable and inbewtween. Edith Finch pretty much surpasses anything I've played until now.
It must be said at this point that I am a fan of the new wave of so called Walking Sims. I mean I did grow up on a diet of text only adventure games so I rate a good story game and I'm comfortable with that story being drip fed to me on tracks as long as it stays good. It's what makes even this stunning game hard to reccomend as I understand that for some people they just will not get this type of game, if indeed they accept it's a game to begin with.
I'm less concenred with that argument in general given the breadth of what gaming has to offer though I have always been intrigued by a similar debate about whether games can ever be considered Art. I think that argument in recent years has started to be put to bed and in my view Edith Finch knocks it out of the park. This is firstly masterful storytelling, as good as any book I could not put down but secondly I feel it goes along way to portraying a piece of art, maybe not high art but as worthy of the monkier as any film at least.
There are way too many reviews here already which do a better job of explaining the game and why you should or should not play it so I won't touch on that all except to say that I was left moved as much as any great story has moved me.
Catching my breath at the end, I barely noticed I had started afresh and played through a second time. Seriously what was to have been a few minutes checking out some stuff from the start just became a second playthrough. There will be more of those I expect......\t\t\t\t\t

It is exclusively narrative, there is no real gameplay, you only walk in and around a big abadonned family house to replay its former inhabitants' final moments.
