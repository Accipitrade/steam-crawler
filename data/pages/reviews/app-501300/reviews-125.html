\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is so. incredibly. slow. The story might have been good if I wasn't constantly frustrated with the character movement speed. Edith herself isn't too bad, the world is interesting so walking slow works, and it makes sense for reasons you find out later, but in each story mini game the characters move at comically slow paces. I was so frustrated by the movement speed it stopped me from feeling any connection to the characters because I was just begging the narration to move along. The final straw was the cannery story where the boat moves at 0.0004 mph no matter how much you button mash, which causes the narration to have minute long gaps in the middle of sentences.
Also, some of the stories are... underwhelming to say the least. It opened with this big mystery of Milton disappearing, and when it comes time for his story, it's a 30 second flip book of him going through a door and doesn't really help with your understanding at all.
The design is beautiful, the characters are interesting, but the pacing made everything get old and incredibly frustrating very fast, and it felt like the game kind of just withered out once you leave the main house, like they had a lot of initial concept and then couldn't figure out how to wrap it up, or what to put in the stories.
I wish I had just watched a video essay on it or read the wiki

This is what I call an epic walking sim

Why is this game so positively rated? It looks kinda bland, I said but decided to buy it anyway. And now I understand what makes it so good. What Remains of Edith Finch is a wonderful, touching, and imaginative tale of the Finch family. In many creative and novel ways, you will uncover the tragic life stories of each family member when you explore the well-furnished house and its numerous secret passages.

Yes, this is a walking simulator, a game type that has gotten a great deal of hate. Not being the biggest fan of the genre either, I found this one quite engaging because of its gripping narrative and mind-boggling ideas of storytelling. What Remains of Edith Finch is the result of letting a creative mind roam free. But what I personally loved in it were the subtle horror-ish hints scattered throughout the game that is not scary per se, but touches upon some dark topics that made me uncomfortable when I started to reflect on them.

Note that this is not a game that should be rushed - you will want to pay a lot of attention to the environmental cues because they just add to the mysterious, strange ambience. Id say that if theres one walking sim you should play regardless of your usual preferences, its this one - good story, good aesthetics, and good gameplay!
