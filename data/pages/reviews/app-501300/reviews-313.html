\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEnglish:
I played a few "walking simulators" and this one is (by far) the best in the list.
Like the others, it tells the player a story (or several in this case) and therefore has no real replayability. The gameplay is almost nonexistent (even if there is a clear effort here), the environnements are beautiful (and there are LOTS of things everywhere, piles of books, various "styles", etc, often giving clues about the personnality or interests of the characters).
I never felt blocked by invisible walls like in others games in the genre. Everything, from what you can see of the character you play, the environnements, how the house is organized (even the credits) show a real attention to detail.
So, to conclude: It's like a XXL walking simulator which present you with several (13?) stories, each one having their (small/simple) gameplay mechanism. If you don't like that type of game it probably won't be for you (or who knows? It's pretty good, it might work.) but if you want to give it a try, It has a lot to offer for a couple of hours (maybe a little expensive, especially considering I just finished The Vagrant which cost $4).
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6bMn4CoyUkM

Français:
J'ai joué à quelques "simulateurs de marche" et celui-ci est (de loin) le meilleur de la liste.
Comme les autres, il raconte au joueur une histoire (ou plusieurs dans ce cas) et ne présente donc pas de réelle valeur en terme de rejouabilité. Le gameplay est quasi inexistant (même s'il y a un clair effort ici), les environnements sont magnifiques (et il y a BEAUCOUP de choses partout, des tas de livres, divers "styles", etc., donnant souvent des indices sur la personnalité ou les intérêts des personnages).
Je ne me suis jamais senti bloqué par des murs invisibles comme dans d'autres jeux du genre. Tout, de ce que vous pouvez voir du personnage que vous jouez, des environnements, de l'organisation de la maison (même les crédits) montre une réelle attention aux détails.
Donc, pour conclure: c'est comme un simulateur de marche XXL qui vous présente plusieurs (13?) histoires, chacune ayant ses (petits/simples) mécanismes de jeu et atmosphère. Si vous n'aimez pas ce type de jeu, ce ne sera probablement pas pour vous (ou qui sait? C'est plutôt bien, ça pourrait fonctionner.) Mais si vous voulez l'essayer, il a beaucoup à offrir pour à peu près deux heures (peut-être un peu cher, surtout si je viens de terminer The Vagrant qui coûte 4 $).

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI should start by saying, I picked this up because I wanted to try something new and there were so many awards and recommendations but . . . I don't think this type of game is really for me. And to be clear, my 'no' recommendation is not because there is anything actually WRONG with this game, it was enjoyable enough and I'm sure it's a good example of what it is trying to be, but for me, even considering I got this on a 50% off sale, I could never justify paying that amount of money for what this game turned out to be -- something with virtually no actual gameplay, with absolutely no replayability, and which is over in barely two hours (and I really took my time trying to make sure I didn't miss a single thing). I've watched many movies that lasted longer than this game, and enjoyed the experience more, and would never pay the asking price of this game for that kind of experience.

As I said, I did in fact enjoy the game, what little of it there is. Had it been priced at $2 instead of $20, I would be recommending it. I've played an occasional free walking sim and visual novel off Steam before, but I just figured, for a $20 price tag, surely there would be a more substantial difference between those free things and this high-priced professionally produced game. But the while the difference is there (this is certainly a bit better written and more polished than the free story-type games I've seen previously), for me it just isn't enough to justify the price, not even close.

I'm glad that so many people seemed to enjoy this game and think it was worth the price enough for it to be successful. It clearly was made by people who care about what they are doing and of course they deserve to be paid for their hard work. I hope at some point they make something that is more my style, a game that I can sink hundreds of hours into, but with the kind of story and depth I see here. But as for me, I think I'll stick to the free examples of this genre from now on, and save my money for games that are actually, well, GAMES, and aren't just a short linear story, completely finished in the blink of an eye. But, if that's your sort of thing, well, my experience with the genre is limited but if the price isn't an issue for you then the game was enjoyable enough for the few moments it lasted, so give it a try.\t\t\t\t\t
