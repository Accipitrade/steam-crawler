\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is incredible. This is my third time finishing it, and it was beautiful each time. With the game being an anthology that covers the death of each Finch family member, each story is uniquely interesting and believable- despite being so unbelievable. The varying styling, themes, and music makes each new story a distinct, immersive treat. This is not a fast-paced action game, instead it is a slow burn of an enchanting story. The tie-in with Unfinished Swan is also is also very satisfying :)

My only complaints are the slow walking pace, especially in the beginning of the game. The slow pace however gives you more time to look around and appreciate the environment. The other complaint would be the awkward controls for mouse+keyboard. I would recommend a controller for most players.

Overall, would definitely recommend for anyone who would enjoy a short (2-3hr) journey exploring the lives and deaths of the Finch family.\t\t\t\t\t

There is some portion of the game that has interaction but you don't really require to master any skills for this title. It is a soothing trip in Edith's shoes and you try to uncover her family's story.

The only "CON" I can point out would be the unsupported true 21:9 3440 x 1440p UltraWide format.

I do recommend this game for any fans of good storytelling and especially, when the story is quite tragic.\t\t\t\t\t

Genre: Adventure/Walking Simulator

What Remains of Edith Finch has a pretty unique way of storytelling and that's what I really liked about this game. You play as Edith Finch, the teen telling the story of her family, and the story is very depressing. You have a book with a family tree, and everyone there is dead, except you and what's going through the game is you visiting your old house where you lived and uncovering family stories. Every time you finish the story of some family member, Edith will draw that character in her book, so you know which ones you finished. These stories are told in a methaphorical way through fiction to make them more interesting and less dark. One example is while you are reading the story about the woman called Molly, she will turn into the shark cause she is so hungry, and you can hunt seals, or you can fly like an owl etc. I just love the storytelling here. These stories are very sad, and most of these people died in some random accidents. Story is pretty good overall, it really made me appreciate my own family more.

I usually write longer reviews, but this is a walking simulator and I don't know what else to say here except that the gameplay is mostly just walking, you can't even sprint, but there is no need for that. Graphics are pretty and house looks beautiful. Game is pretty short, took me 2 hours to finish it. You can replay the stories so it's easy to get all achievements if that's your thing.

Pros:
- Amazing storytelling
- Pretty good, but depressing story
- Beautiful house
- Nice graphics

Neutral:
- Short

Verdict:
Really unique game that tells very depressing family story, but through such wonderful way to make it a bit less dark than it actually is. Since it's pretty short I would recommend waiting for a sale. Pretty good game overall.

Personal rating: 8/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEssentially a playable short story, and a good one. There's some clever use of different media and different control schemes, but no real difficulty -- the point is to watch the stories unfold. Some of them are fun, and some are haunting. It's less a solve-the-puzzle mystery and more of an exploration. The writing and voice acting are both well done.

One of the best aspects of this game is how much character information it puts into set design, and how well it evokes a particular place. The game is set on Orcas Island, and the look and feel of the Pacific Northwest is spot on. It took me about two hours to get through the whole game, and that included a lot of stopping to look at details of each room, read the book titles, etc.

Non-metaphorical advice: if you're stuck in a UI element, try moving backwards, even if it doesn't make sense in context. That was the only UI issue I had in the entire game.\t\t\t\t\t
