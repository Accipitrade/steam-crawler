\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat Remains of Edith Finch tells the story of a young woman, the last one alive in her family, who returns to her family's ancestral home to discover her family's past that her mother hid from her. Legend has it that all of the Finch bloodline is cursed to die premature and strange deaths, often before they reach adulthood. The late eccentric matriarch of the family, Edie Finch, built memorials all over the Finch house that speak stories of all of the family members who had died, all turned into unbelievable stories mythologized beyond recognition. Interacting with these memorials causes Edith to imagine what these family members must have experienced in their last moments, filtered through grandma Edie's highly fantastical interpretations.

As someone who plays Walking Simulators often, this one is quite unlike any its genre family, with a variety of modes of interaction, and plenty of motion that most definitely aren't just walking, that the trailer highlights well. There are many different interpretations behind the highly abstract story of What Remains of Edith Finch, but it is undeniably questioning how people handle (usually not well) the absurdness and inevitability of death and the grief of losing family members. Despite these heavy themes, there are many points where the game takes itself casually or even outright comedicaly, showing death as the commonplace, absurd, and sometimes-ironic thing it actually is. The nature of the Finch family curse, or even if there actually is one at all, is still a subject of great debate among analysts of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat an amazing game! One of the best narrative-driven games I've ever played; it accomplishes so many things successfully in its short playtime, with so few flaws. The writing and narration is top-notch, the central mystery and the story accompanying it is intriguing, and the ending is sublime.

I dislike the term "walking simulator" but the core gameplay here is essentially that, plus something extra. Not going to spoil what that 'extra' encompasses, as that would be spoiling the game. The same goes for the story, the less you know about it going in, the better. You start the game as Edith Finch, who arrives at her old family home and starts to explore it to learn more about her family members. Discovering the mysteries of that family tree is the bulk of the game. Let's just say that it has some fascinating twists and turns, some of which were so interesting that I even replayed some bits. It's an entirely linear experience, not unlike watching a movie with a bit more interactivity, but that's completely fine. And like I mentioned before, the ending is nearly perfect in its execution, and the final moments of the game left me crying. It's best to finish the game in one go, so you get the full impact of the story.

It's one of the games that will stay in your mind long after you finish it. If you are the slightest bit interested in the game, give it a try. What Remains of Edith Finch is absolutely worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

------------------
The game has an intriguing plot as well as cool graphics and animations accompanying all of that.

The soundtracks are magnificent, I bet you would like to hear them again.

The saddest part is that the game itself is short (i spent exploring the surroundings a bit but completed it in less than 3 hours), but pleasure from those hours can be compared to a reading of a good book or memorable film/serial.

Overall, I am more than satisfied with this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think the attention to detail in the rooms is done with love and care. I think the story is decent and the art is interesting (varied art). I would love to see a slightly longer game or slightly longer stories. There is some beautiful imagery and storytelling for some of the members but not all of them.

It didn't really pull at the heart strings like other games in the genre and the story didn't exactly give me the sense of amazement that I was hoping for.

Nonetheless, a game with a lot of love and care with good story telling execution. There's nothing wrong with it necessarily. I am just not ready to say it's a master piece.

6/10\t\t\t\t\t
