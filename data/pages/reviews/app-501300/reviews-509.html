\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best and most memorable 5 hours I've spent in a video game in recent years.

I cannot recommend this game enough. There's no action, no open world, no collectables, no leveling up and I am perfectly fine with that. It's an interactive story that you explore at your own pace. It's a shinning example of how excellent stroy telling still exists in video games.

Think of it like... paying to go see a movie (a drama), except you control the pacing of the movie and how much of the "little things" you want to see.

Video game are not always about shooting people or doing "X, Y, Z" and/or saving the world. The best stories come from life experiences. From the people and family that come and go.

I know I am sounding melodramatic here but this game, if you allow it, will suck you right in and take you on an emotional ride you will be talking about for years to come.

9.5/10

My only issue is, I wish it was longer. Some character stories were a little short. You could complete this game in one sitting easily (2-3 hours). But I suggest taking your time to explore the house.\t\t\t\t\t

this game is a "roller coaster" regarding emotions and feelings..!
I finished this game (I didn't get all the success of the game...) and the story is really...poignant.

Graphics are really nice, the music is good and the controls (I used my xbox one controller) are good.

The main subject of this game is "tough" : death inside a family.
Strangely, the way Giant Sparrow is able to "tale this story" is really nice and well written.
The action takes place in a huge house, near the sea.. and really you need to visit a lot of rooms here.

This is more a "walking simulator" than a real adventure game, but the atmosphere is really amazing.
You need to like to read and play at a slow pace... or else, you won't enjoy this game.
If call of duty is your cup of tea, then this game is not for you.

This game reminds me of the movie "Forrest Gump" : you will explore different period of the American history.

Each room was preserved after the death of the family member (and the story of this strange family begins in the first pat of the 20th century...).

really, for me it was a nice experience !
Thank you Giant Sparrow !
I'm waiting now your next game! :-)
