\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt took some time for me to get it out of my head that this was a Gone Home clone (not just because it was a similar looking walking sim, but a similarly themed walking sim at that) but I distinctly remember the moment that I did. I was almost taken aback at just how trippy this game got and it put me on edge a bit at first. This quickly became the games strength as it delightfully cuts the searching for interactive notes and trinkets with transporting new scenes and playable sections. Some were more fun than others, but the fact that they were there in the first place was enjoyable enough for me. The house is designed in such a way that it felt very open and that I'd be jumping around from room to room in no particular order but only later on realized that it was more linear than I thought. I thought this was well done in a game that doesn't frontload a lot of story behind my being there, but still managed to get me through the house in a particular order (one end of the family tree to the other). I wouldn't replay this game from the beginning, but the developers allow the player to jump into their favorite parts or revisit areas the player might not have fully explored after finishing the game by breaking each family member's room into a chapter that one can revisit. I recommend this game to anyone new or familiar with the walking sim genre looking for something quick in playtime but also fresh and interesting.\t\t\t\t\t
