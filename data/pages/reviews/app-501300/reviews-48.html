\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter nearly six years, I finally played this game. I went in completely blind, knowing almost nothing about the premise and unaware of its themes. All I knew is that it was critically acclaimed and that I should try it out.

Within the first 5-10 minutes I was completely hooked. Though this game is not long, it had me entranced from start to end, dumbfounded by how visually stunning it is. I was immersed in the world and lost myself in its immaculate narration and phenomenal soundtrack. I cannot say when I last had an experience just like this from a game; I felt like I wasn't just watching an expertly-crafted film, but rather, I was in the film. Playing this game felt truly unique and excellently demonstrates the capabilities of video games to push the boundaries of visual media (even though the game is getting quite old now).

Side note: this game is depressing. I will not spoil why, but I recommend that some players might want to take a break between the various stories. I didn't end up crying, but the game certainly tugged at my heart and caused me to stop playing for a few moments.

Though the year just started, What Remains of Edith Finch is, without a doubt, one of the best gaming experiences I've had in 2023.

Do yourself a favor and play this game.\t\t\t\t\t
