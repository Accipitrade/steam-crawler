\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I was a boy, my mother asked me, "What is so special about a game rather than a book, or a movie?" I responded that a game allowed a way to show a story in a much more intimate manner than any other. Ever since, whenever I play a "Story" game, I have wondered the same: Why could this not be a book? Why not a movie? And almost every time that game could have been either.

Not this one.

What Remains of Edith Finch is a story that could only exist as a game. Every part of it's beautiful narrative could not be told through any way other than an intimate one. It truly lets you experience all of the events inside of it as if you were right there, and in a way that is both intriguing and touching. I won't spoil what happens, but I will say this: No matter who you are, you need to play What Remains of Edith Finch.

This game gets a solid 10/10 from me.\t\t\t\t\t
