\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is great in theory, and I love the gameplay when it works. However, a surprisingly large part of it is incredibly glitchy.

The first two levels (monkeys and agatha) were fine, though I did have to turn down my graphics settings a good bit to get it to work properly.

The third level (gold watchers) was also fine during gameplay, but the bossfight at the end was pretty laggy when I ran. (which I had to do pretty much all the time)

The fourth level (duckies) was fine during gameplay, if a bit repetitive. However, the bossfight was terrible. It was extremely laggy, and barely anything in it worked. The boss's attacks would glitch through pillars to stun me while I was hidden, and when I was stunned there was no way I could break free, the screen would turn completely red or yellow, leaving me with a blank screen until I eventually died. The boss's head would also glitch through the pillar when it was supposed to get stunned, not killing me, but making me do it again and again for every cycle. Once the smaller duckies started to chase me, it just became less fun and more tedious.

The fifth level (clowns) was absolutely my favorite, and had zero glitches, which was surprising considering the quality of the last level.

The sixth level (nurses) was also very buggy, though it's mostly minor and excusable because its more new. The receptionist became giant and started T-posing during a cutscene, and the level was kind of laggy, even though I lowered my graphics even more. The first part of it was super fun gameplay-wise, but my map wasn't fully loading and was super blurry for most of it. This also affected the stealth section later, because it wouldn't load the view cycles of the nurses on the map. The game also became even more laggy at that point, and my computer was completely unable to run the last half of the level, with the game becoming so laggy I couldn't move. This could just be an issue with my computer, as I'm playing on a laptop, but if so then they need to make this game more accessible to people with worse devices, as it wouldn't work even with every setting turned down as low as possible.

All in all, the idea and execution for the game is phenomenal, but glitches stop it from being fun or even playable at too many points. Getting up to the unplayable part took more than two hours, so now I can't refund the game either. It's sad I have to give this a negative review, but spending $20 dollars on a game that's completely unplayable past a certain point and glitchy and laggy before then just isn't worth it. I guess you can only play this game on a really powerful pc, which would've been nice to know before I bought it. Hope this can help someone else...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter watching FusionZGamer and 8-Bit Ryan play up to Chapter 4, I was interested in getting this game. After playing and beating all the chapters that are currently out, being 1-4, I very much did enjoy the game. Here are some good and bad things I find in this game.
Good things:
1: Very different from any horror game I've either played or have seen others play before.
2: There's a great challenge throughout the game and it's Chapters, while not to difficult (for the most part), and has an Easy difficulty in the settings, though I have yet to understand what is made easier that being on Normal mode.
3: Each level is unique in what you do, what new obstacles you will face, and that no level can really be beaten using the same strategy as the other levels. For example, you can't complete Deadly Decadence by simply running and collecting shards, without having to keep your eyes on the Golden Watchers, as otherwise they will outrun you, and kill you.
4: There are lots of secrets that are in throughout the levels, making it nice to go back and try to find the ones that were missed the first time.
5: The story behind the game is interesting, and is enjoyable to find more about it, as there is lore for each level, the main character, and Bierce to find.
Issues:
1: One I had encountered was replaying the first level in Chapter 1, Monkey Business, as I wanted to go for S rank. After grabbing the 2 secrets, and collecting the soul shards, I made my way to the ring piece. After breaking a few of the boards, my game proceeds to crash.
2: Another issue for me was playing Chapter 4 on Crazy Carnevil. Zone one refused to have the clown cars move around, making no enemies able to pop out from the cars. Only when I got close enough to where they spawn in the level did they pop out enemies, only to die from hitting the roof of the tunnel it was in.
3: In Mascot Mayhem, I had a couple times gotten stuck in some walls trying to use the Teleport ability to get away from enemies.
4: Another issue, in Bearly Buried, in Act 3 (I think), after losing all my lives, it sometimes was simply stuck at that screen, forcing me to ALT+F4.
5: In Zone 3 of Bearly Buried, there seems to be no enemies, yet soul shards to collect. However, this could be just me not possibly realizing that the area could be connected to Zone 2, but I don't believe that is the case.
6: Finally, in terms of issues with levels go, in Torment Therapy, during the boss fight, I had encountered a crash at some point, thought I don't know what specifically caused it.
7: Lastly, I noticed anytime trying to open the game through desktop shortcut, and not through your Steam library instead, the game seems to think that you don't own any of the DLC, and resuming a level beyond Monkey Business, yields a screen stating, "DLC not installed", and takes you back to the main menu. And viewing the chapters via the chapter button in the main menu shows that every chapter except Chapter one is coming soon. Not the worst issue, bug it's definitely annoying for those who may be used to simply opening up games via desktop shortcuts.

Conclusion:
Honestly, I find that this game is very much worth it, even with the large amount of bugs, issues, and couple of crashes of my game that I have had. Very excited for the arrival of Chapter 5, whenever that does happen. I hope the developers can patch up and fix what issues are currently present in the game, preferably even before the release of Chapter 5, to reduce the amount of fixes that might be within that chapter. I suggest getting the game, as it's still enjoyable even with the bugs and whatnot.\t\t\t\t\t
