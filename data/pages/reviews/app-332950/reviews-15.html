\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I buy a game, I'm looking for a few things:

(1) A story that captures me.
(2) Entertaining gameplay.
(3) A fair (and reasonable) challenge.
And (4) Creators who care about about what they create. Some might call this "Passion".

(1) At first glance, It looks like your standard Pac-Man maze runner game. You run around collecting these dark crystals and pieces of a magic ring all whilst being chased by monstrous AI's. And That's really it. The story is very lackluster at best, where the character is being this witch's errand boy, a demon and the witch having their "he said, she said" They don't touch on ANY of the characters backstories and it makes it difficult to love or hate them at all. Even the voice actors sounded like they had to come in to the office on their day off and poorly sold what little story there is. It's a real shame.

(2) As I've said before, the gameplay is much like Pac-Man. You collect the things while being chased by other things. Each chapter that has come out so far had decent map layouts and monsters to accompany them. I won't spoil too much for people who haven't played it, but you'll be guaranteed to have your hands full. Especially in the mansion of chapter 2.

(3) Speaking of which, it took literal hours to beat the mansion and I still have yet to get rank S. With how hard the level actually is, who KNOWS how long getting rank S would take. Some of the levels are also broken, and the developers still haven't fixed the bugs. Some of them caused the game to freeze and I had to start all over again, monsters randomly yeet themselves out of existence, and sometimes it just outright crashes, making all the work you have done worthless.

(4) People who've played this game and have been following it up to this point might have noticed that the other chapters have been spreading further and further apart. It's been over a year since the release of chapter 3 and we STILL have yet to even get a release date for the (very long) anticipated chapter 4. I don't know if they are having trouble with schedules amongst the development team, (understandable with Covid-19,) or if they just dropped the project all together. I just hope that we get updated news soon.

Do I recommend this game? Well, that depends on the person. Take this review with a grain of salt, because the playing experience is different for everyone. This is by no means a bad game. It's the way of the world when not all games are perfect. I just think it could use more love and attention than the devs are able to give at this time, and it hurts to say that. It's obvious that there's effort put into this game and I want more then anything to see development continue on it soon.
(If it improves a fair amount, I'll edit this review.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhew okay. This games a mixed bag. On the one hand, SO much is executed very well. The idea of horror pacman might seem silly, but its played out pretty well. It can be quite frightening and intense. The monster designs are pretty cool, with the Joy Joy Gang being the only ones I'm not too big on. The powers are useful and fun to use (at least until late game.) The soundtrack kicks ass and is definitely the most consistently fantastic part of the game.

But no game is without blemish and HOO BOY. Does this game have blemishes.

One major issue I've become aware of since I made my way through Chapter 4 is the power system. I love the powers we have, but actually using them later in the game feels pretty clunky. Each level after the first one introduces a new power. You can equip two powers at a time, and you cycle through them by pressing 1 or 2, depending on which slot you want to change. Each level that introduces a power has certain portions or mechanics that are SPECIFICALLY designed for that power. That means that you'll always want to spend one of your two power slots to make sure you always have that one on deck. The problem is that only leaves you with one slot to play around with. This is fine in early game, but in late game, you're juggling 5+ powers. In a life or death situation, it feels supremely awkward to spam the 1 key until you get to the power you need to survive. Its not fun challenge. Its frustrating, unnecessary challenge. Especially since its a control issue and not an actual aspect of the game.

Then theres the bugs. They are RAMPANT. Which especially sucks, because they didn't used to be. When i got into the game, only chapters 1-3 were out and i encountered no issues. But all of a sudden as soon as chapter 4 comes out, bugs abound. The most common one for me, is the game simply freezing on me whenever I game over. Despite the fact that I have the game set to normal difficulty, replaying any level automatically sets it to easy and I can't change it back. Changing the resolution of the game often times does nothing. Read the other reviews. Theyre are tons of 'em. Take your pick.

Then there are the dev's... questionable priorities. While this might not be an aspect of the game itself, I feel its worth mentioning anyways as it can CERTAINLY affect one's decision on whether or not to buy the game. Basically, Glowstick is funneling a lot of resources into side projects and spin-offs while the game itself sits unfinished. Chapter 4 was delayed multiple times. Which was understandable at first. A rushed game is forever bad and all that, its a small team. But then we learned that the reason it was delayed was not due to standard development issues, but instead due to time and resources being diverted to a multiplayer spin-off. I don't mind spin-offs, but i do feel kinda gipped that they failed to uphold a promised release date on multiple occasions just because they decided to put time into an entirely different project instead of finishing the one thats already due. And then of course, once the update actually DID come out after waiting TWO YEARS FOR IT, its absolutely riddled with bugs. So not only have these out of wack business decisions affected the release dates, it affected the quality of the end product as well. Glowstick. You have something special here. I think it absolutely deserves spin-offs and merch and stuff. But please god, don't squander what you have by thinly spreading resources across a bunch other projects before the base one is finished. Don't half-ass multiple things. Whole-ass one thing. Then move on to side projects.

Look. The game is fun, its scary, it looks great, its got a great soundtrack, monster designs are sick as hell, the story its telling is interesting. I absolutely think the complete edition with all 5 chapters is well worth the price of admission. Hell i thought it was worth the price even before Chapter 4 came out. But that worth comes with a lot of caveats that by all accounts shouldn't be there if better decisions were made.\t\t\t\t\t
