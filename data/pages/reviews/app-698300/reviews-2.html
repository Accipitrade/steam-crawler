\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game for kids that achievement hunters will be interested in picking up. Although this title doesn't have many achievements, it can be completed fairly quickly. You have a screen where you have to search for items in a mess. The game can get difficult especially on higher difficulties as items get so small you will have to scan the screen quickly to try to find the suggested items. The song can get a bit repetitive.

While this game is an easy achievement hunter the real audience is children. Children will find this game entertaining enough however may have difficulties after a few levels depending on their age. It would be great if they added a four kids casual mode where the game plays at the regular pace without the time. They can even have no achievements in this mode for just a relaxing or learning experience for children.

Pros
+Variety of items
+Easy Achievements
+Kid friendly

Cons
-Repetitive soundtrack
-Might be difficult for the intended audience at times

For achievement hunters or kids both this is a fairly easy to recommend title. I do recommend getting it during a sale because you can pick it up fairly cheap. There's no reason to buy it at full price because there isn't any long gripping gameplay here. Still a decent enough game to pick up though.

This makes Hard difficulty impossible after a certain point. It's exactly what it seems to be on the store trailer.

For Adults\t
The achievements are easy and fast to get for any hunters out there. However I doubt the game will keep your interest or even have it at all. It's very simplistic since like I mentioned before it's for kids not adults.

For Kids\t
Could be used as a teaching tool or to keep them busy. Either way for $2 it could be a decent investment, or you could get it on sale to make it even more worth it I'm sure.

-Only one song in the game so it might get old after a while (It's the song in the trailer)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, I know what you're thinking but no, I won't complain about the translation here. xD
Here we have a little cute hidden object game, very nice and relaxing if you wanna kill some time :)
The soundtrack and the effects too are minimalistic and soothing, and the graphics are really OK, cute, well-designed, easy to spot or, at least, easy to recognize! 'Cos in the later levels, even if you stay in easy difficulty well, there will be literally hundreds of items of any kind (fruits, work tools, animals, stickers, food of any kind, weapons, everything xD)!
The game comes for 1€/$ at its full price, so it's super easy to get this on discount without many problems and it's worth it to me!
And as you can imagine from the title well, the core of the game is to find the required objects, who are depicted in a "large" standard size on the top of the screen, inside literally an ocean of other things!
Of course you've got a timer in the upper left part of the screen, and again, as per the standard of these games, every wrong click will net you -2 seconds (more or less!) of time so no autoclickers or random clicks guys ahah, more thinking! :)
You can also choose from the first opening config page to play it on windowed mode (I always play these casual games in windowed mode, my laptop's screen is quite wide xD) and to adjust the graphics settings although as I said, you can play it on low settings and it doesn't change much the game experience!
So, why I'm not ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ and complaining about the language once in a lifetime? Being aware of course that there are 12 languages available but, since I'm Northern Italian, my Steam client is in Italian and it puts on default Italian to every game.. you can choose from Japanese to Russian, Portuguese, Spanish or whatever you like! I'm not complaining 'cos on one side, the translation is extremely bad (I don't think a computer can make a translation like this one, to me it's been done on purpose ahahah just joking xD) but on the other side, well, there's no text and no need of any kind of text everywhere! The game's all casual and all based on graphics, finding the objects! :)
So, you'll be a little bit puzzled on the first screen and on the game store 'cos even though I hold two academical degrees and I should be a little bit smart and active for what concerns my native tongue well, I didn't manage to understand the first page itself.
The good news? There's only one page! XD
The other written things you'll see are the difficulty setting - easy, medium or hard - and, at the end of the game (when your timer hits 0, it's all an "infinite" mode), you'll see your score. Nothing more than this!
Of course, the difficulty settings don't need explanations; you'll earn points and some more seconds for every right object found and obviously the harder you go, the more objects you'll have both to find and on the screen (just try to start it on hard and you'll see by yourself! XD).
So, summarizing, this game lives up at its premises - it tells you that its only goal is to give you levels filled with objects, a score, a timer, and you've got to find them according to the example shown and of course to the difficulty settings.
Oh, there's another thing: by default in the main manu and also when you pause it (everything's mouse-controlled of course) there's a lamp button that's turned off; if you turn it on, of course, it will activate hints! Some achievements are related to finding items without hints of course; this though could be a little bit confusing since all the levels consist of the items you need to find put on a line above the game field, right after a magnifying glass.. which is the universal simbol for hints!
Along with lamps.. xD
But the problem is that well, that lens tells you: "you've got to click on the items on my right!".. so if you click on the magnifying glass it's like you clicked on a "wrong object" and you'll get a penalty xD
So, where's the deal? Well, if you turn on the hint option, after some seconds of "inactivity" the item you have to click on will kinda "shake" automatically so that you can get it right on :)
So, don't touch the magnifying glass at all! XD It's only there to tell you that you've got to click on the objects on the right side, but the hints are automatic if you turn the light bulb on in the menu and they only shake :)
This is a good game for its cost and its premises, it's for kids but trust me, the hard difficulty is REALLY hard and gets hard very quickly, you'll find yourself pretty soon into a maze of hundreds of objects and it won't be easy even if you don't have kids and you're playing as an adult, like me xD
If you're into these games or if you've got kids to give them the easy kind, I think it's a nice game and it can be replayed from time to time since it isn't hard or frustrating at all, and the hint system makes it feel even more enjoyable.. of course it isn't a masterpiece, but it does what it's supposed to to :)
Oh, and the last thing: in November, 2017 the developers added this hint system (before it was automatic and could not be disabled, while now it's disabled by default and you've got to turn it on if you want) and they also said that there will be more updates in the future, so if you read opinions before November, 2017 read also the small updates posted by the developers, hoping that there will be other options in the future! This studio makes lots of nice games enjoyable for families, kids and players of all ages so I'm pretty sure they will keep on caring about their games :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Find This" is a hidden object game that's as simple as it gets: you have to look for certain items in the clutter and click on them before time runs out. There's no backstory or depth whatsoever but it doesn't really matter.

The difficulty scales well with levels that get more and more cluttered as you go on. The timer doesn't reset on each level, you start with 30 seconds and gain or lose time as the game progresses.

It would be nice if this game had scenes, like Hidden Folks. Instead of throwing a bunch of random unrelated items together they could make a level with animals, one with sea items, food, etc.

There should be an option to disable suggestions to make it more challenging.

Technically it's fine, it runs perfectly on my potato laptop with slight FPS drops when levels get really really cluttered.

Overall it's a sweet 99 cents game both for kids and adults with some time to waste.\t\t\t\t\t
