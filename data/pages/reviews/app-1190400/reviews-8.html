\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic game, straight up. Deep combat, fun characters, some of the best action sequences you've ever seen and a silly yet enthusiastic story with some occasional seriousness to break up the levity.

However it seems because of certain gameplay elements, people end up quitting early, which is understandable. The game could do a better job of explaining, and although the weapon drawing mechanic may seem a bit odd and frustrating at first, it's not at all as unreliable as you might think, it just takes some getting used to. It's honestly the biggest reason the combat is as interesting as it is. It can get pretty sophisticated when you're allowed to switch to any weapon almost immediately rather than cycling through a list of them every time you want another. And the game is almost entirely built around this mechanic. To great effect.

With that said, go for it, it really is good, And obviously don't feel bad about the low scores on the missions you're inevitably going to get. Just try to learn and have fun. It's a little confusing at first, but It becomes truly great once you finally get a hang of the combat.

(Game also might have the greatest end sequence of any game I've ever played)\t\t\t\t\t
