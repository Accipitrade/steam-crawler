\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was such a wonderful ride. Fantastic soundtrack, everything just shines with charm and style, the combat may feel a little strange at first, but when it clicks, you'll go nuts with combos and juggling enemies mid-air, feels great. The story had me entertained and enthralled the whole way through.

I've had some issues though, KB/M controls feels really stiff, rather than a PS4 controller in my case. There was another issue that, for some reason, if you press the "ESC" button while the game's in fullscreen mode, it changes to windowed mode. Fortunately, there's a mod that fixes this and a fps issue as well, you can find it in the Steam Guides, a guide with the name "SilentPatch: Frame Pace and Timing Fix".

If you're like me, who didn't have the chance to play this on a Wii U, give this game a chance, it feels rewarding once you learn how to flow through it, and there's really no games like this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really fun and charming beat 'm up, that doesn't lack in challenge.

It may be hard to tell from the screenshots and gameplay videos but: the way combat works and feels, the encounters, boss fights, shops where you can buy and upgrade moves, etc, in The Wonderful 101, is really similar to Bayonetta. So much so even that I can safely say that if you enjoyed the gameplay in Bayonetta, you'll have a blast in this one without a doubt.

There is one aspect of The Wonderful 101 that I can see being a turn-off for some people. The game is a bit overwhelming in the beginning. The main reason for this being, that there is a lot being thrown at you mechanic wise, at the start and the game only briefly explains everything. So you're a bit forced to experiment and figure out things on your own. That being said, once your past the initial learning-curve, it's smooth sailing.

In short. The Wonderful 101 is a really good game that can be a bit rough at first. Would highly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShame, I thought I'd enjoy this but I didn't. This has to be the least fun I've had in a P* game.

It's hard to identify what's what in this game because everything's tiny as hell and there's no good camera setting so far that's right for me. There are times when the game can't distinguish unite morphs from each other(sometimes can't differentiate gun from whip or hammer from whip). It also will prioritize the rainbow morphs over unite morphs that I actually want; for example, I'm fighting a boss and I want to form a unite hand but if there's a civilian, flowerbed, or whatever bullxit wherein it changes the color of the morph to a rainbow then that's what's gonna come out. The worst camera so far, I've bitcehd about the camera from Bayonetta but the camera there is actually manageable just not all that helpful because it moves a lot, here it's actually worse because of how tiny you are so then it zooms in sometimes to fights then I'm like "what the I can't see where the other enemies are" so I zoom out manually which is frustrating since it happens a lot of times. And sometimes the camera isn't centered to where you're character is which is honestly frustrating in this genre. Also you don't have a lot of control of the camera in this game, if you are given control.

Oh well, time to wait for the next P* game that's designed for proper gaming devices unlike that xit Wii U. Too bad this is the first game that P* actually published though, not just developed. If you're gonna buy it, then fine, gives them money for their next project. I'm not sure if I'm gonna continue playing this... Maybe when I'm feeling masochistic.\t\t\t\t\t
