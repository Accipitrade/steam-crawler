\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverview
The Wonderful 101 is a highly unique action game from the masters of the genre.

This is a great port. It runs at a consistent framerate and the visuals are crisp. The controls work perfectly on a controller. You are not missing anything from the lack of a Wii U gamepad.

Story and Graphics
The story is very silly and never takes itself seriously. The humour is entertaining, although it drags on a bit at times. In fact, there is a great deal of dialogue. Despite this, the story ends up being very entertaining and satisfying, with some epic cutscenes.
I love the art, which is bright and colourful. The characters are made to look tiny, and the buildings look like toys. This enhances the gameâ€™s light-hearted feel.

Gameplay
The Wonderful 101 is a style action game, like Bayonetta or Devil May Cry. However, it is full of unique and creative ideas. Some of these work, while others can be a nuisance.

Instead of selecting weapons, you draw them. This enables you to switch weapon mid combo by drawing a different one. With the right upgrade, you can pull off some neat tricks, like doing different types of attacks simultaneously. Another unique mechanic is that you can make different sizes of weapons. Larger weapons hit harder but require a big commitment.

Once you unlock more options, the combat becomes both stylish and crazy. To add to this, there are many epic boss fights.

I had a couple of issues with the combat, however. My biggest gripe with the game is how it handles getting hit. You control a group of heroes, rather than just one. When you get hit, some or all of these get sent flying. It often takes quite a bit of time to get them all back. While they are scattered, your options are heavily reduced.

Another think I dislike is how the game handles large enemies. As you would expect from Platinum Games, the enemy design is solid. The problem is that it is difficult or impossible to combo large enemies, unless you have found all the heroes and can make huge weapons (which will not be the case on your first playthrough). When fighting larger enemies, you are forced to take an excessively defensive approach which feels out of place in a game like this.

The Wonderful 101 also mixes things up with unconventional gameplay. There are many flying stages, for example. While I appreciate the variety in principle, many of these just are not fun in practice. I also found several to have poor controls and to be confusing.

Conclusion
I enjoyed The Wonderful 101 for its zany creativity, despite some issues which hurt the experience a bit. It has a learning curve to it, and I would not recommend it as an entry to the genre. If you are interested in something a bit different, and have experience with this kind of game, it is worth a playthrough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first this game can seem very inaccessible due to its abnormal controls and original home on the Wii U. Hopefully this review will clear up some misconceptions as well as highlight, in short, what makes this game so incredible.

TLDR: PLAY THE DEMO, it will give you a basic feel for the gameplay as well as a showcase of the game's spectacle. Any fan of Bayonetta, DMC and the likes should play this game right now. This game is incredible and my favourite of all time. The controls are responsive (when played with a controller) and the combat is incredibly mechanically deep, combined with an immense level of spectacle through its 20 hr story. It is just Wonderful.

The Controls

First lets address what I see as the most common criticism, the controls. The game uses a 'drawing mechanic' where, using the right stick you draw a shape on the floor to switch weapons. Each weapon has its own unique basic shape, e.g. the fist is selected by drawing a circle and the sword is selected by drawing a line. On the original Wii U version you could draw these shapes directly onto that systems touch pad. This leaves many with the idea that this was how the game has, or at least is intended, to be played. This is incredibly false, the analogue stick is honestly the preferred control as it allows for quicker inputs and removes the need to move you hand away from the face buttons each time. The drawing mechanic works more like a fighting game input, and boils down to quick and distinct motions that are made to switch weapons. However I don't want to diminish the complexity of the system, which give the game incredible mechanical depth with how your drawing interacts with enemies, where you have to consider your spacing and surroundings to decide which weapons are best suited for the scenario.

Now the question is, why do many people consider these controls to be unresponsive and lacklustre? Personally i can't speak for the keyboard and mouse controls having played the entire game on a 360 controller, so if you are concerned about that then play the demo and see for yourself, sorry that I cant help with this one.
However the second more prevalent issue is with people not giving the controls enough time and investment to learn them properly. I don't mean that in an elitist 'git gud' way, personally I struggled with the drawing controls myself, finding them at best a moderate gimmick, and at worst an unresponsive mess. Well I was very wrong and after a couple of hours it just clicked. Not only did I figure out how to much more easily draw the shapes, but also the mechanic's place in the combat, which changed weapon swapping from an option in a menu to another layer of depth which endlessly rewards high-level play with an infinitely high skill ceiling, whilst being one of the most recently innovative additions to the genre. It something that WILL take some getting use to, but the result is such a rewarding experience with a combat system with hidden tricks and secrets waiting to be discovered, and when you do the satisfaction of intuitively discovering, then regularly using it is incredible. Having played [SEE ABOVE] hours of this game I am still excited to find a new strategy to taking out an enemy.

The Camera

For me a camera can make or break an entire game, and luckily we are very much in the make category here. Having to sacrifice the right stick for drawing, the camera is a top down view of your little heroes. The only input you can give to the camera is a simple zoom in and out, which you will always want to be zoomed out. The lack of a movable camera may alert some but the camera will always place you in the middle of the screen, allowing you (most of the time) full view of all the enemies in the battle. You don't have to worry about babysitting a camera and instead keeps the focus on the action. Apart from that I should mention a distracting auto zoom when fighting very few enemies, which fortunately doesn't impact the enjoyment for the game but is a flaw nonetheless. Bear in mind the only character in your team that can take damage is the leader, and they will be circled with a big icon.

The Story

Didn't expect much from the story, it's not really why I play action games. Going this and similar games I expect shallow characters and overall cheesiness in service to the core gameplay. Keep this is mind when I say I was pleasantly surprised by this game's story, in that is wasn't abysmal. What you get is a charming romp with a likeable team fighting comically evil villains. It is not entirely without depth but only about a couple inches deeper than a puddle.

The Port

Many make with rightfully criticise the issues with the port, the slightly changed music is a minus, but I really must clarify that this game feels absolutely fine to play, and while you can definitely tell it was a Wii U game, it has no major impact on the PC version. The controls did get some unnecessary minor tweaks it still plays perfectly fine and unless you own a Wii U and can bear some painful loading times, this port is what we have and it is still an incredible game.

In Conclusion

I will probably add and change this review over time to better illustrate my love for this game. Right now I am going for 100% and can confidently say it's by favourite game of all time. Whilst absolutely not a perfect game (looking at you Tetris), this game just feels so passionate and creative. Exploring so many mechanics and executing them all with a level of perfection and finesse unseen in the genre, I just want to congratulate Platinum Games. Before I go I want to give a few tips to better aid the experience and get you sailing smooth asap

  • Don't expect good ranking and your first play-through. EXPECT CONSOLATION PRIZES, consider your first run a warm up act for the real thing. If you finish the game, boot it up for a second go at least for the first mission and see how you improved over the play-through.
  • Buy Guts and Spring immediately after the prologue, you'll thank me later.
  • Don't hesitate to experiment. Use your brain and try to intuitively tackle each enemy, you will be rewarded for doing so.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best action games I've ever played. The premise of controlling a Pikmin-esque group of superheroes and drawing shapes to form giant constructs is executed flawlessly, with each construct being easy to learn and the combat satisfying enough for anyone, even hardcore action fans. Because, there's a lot of depth to the game in the form of combo-stringing with the various weapons you can draw, and each enemy has patterns, strengths, and weaknesses, to learn and master. Without a doubt this is one of the most unique action games you can buy, and just for that alone I think it's worth buying. On the face of it, the game is a just superhero/Power Rangers spoof, but the light-hearted aesthetic of the game is just serious enough at times to elevate the game from simple parody to an earnest, heart-felt tale about saving the day.
Buy it on sale or at full price, this game is worth it either way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun and innovative game concept, but feels really dated and controls feel awful.

Pros:
  • Hero collector (as long as it's not a gacha, that's a positive for me)
  • Cool concept: draw to select weapon

Cons
  • Most important controls feel awful, both on keyboard+mouse and gamepad. To use weapons you have to draw shapes. You have to do it quite often, because right after drawing it's powered up and it loses power shortly. While drawing circles to get fist I got fist, deltaplane, drill, bomb(?) and something else I can't quite get. There's has to be trick to it....
  • Stage ratings. You PROBABLY want to explore, but various stage hazzards with wonky collision boxes cost you hp, everything costs you time (including you trying to find new weapons by drawing), which just really feels like it's going against the best things the game has to offer. Don't get me wrong, I tried to aim for platinum in first few stages and it's possible, but you kinda have to know the stage and play it safe, which makes the game feel like a chore.
  • Screen clutter. It's hard to keep track of everything at first. In most fights you can only clearly see larger enemies, while small ones just blend in with your own crew.
  • Game design is seriously dated. For example: I've played for about and hour and I've been getting bombarded with popups and various dialogs literally every minute. Only about 10% of those somewhat matter as introductory dialogs. The rest is like "YOU PICKED UP AN ITEM! Press X to continue...". Small issue, but it added to my frustration.

I lost patience, so I wouldn't recommend the steam version. I could recommend trying this on WiiU with native controls.\t\t\t\t\t
