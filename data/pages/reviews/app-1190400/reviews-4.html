\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who is a fan of almost anything platinum puts out, I'll be the first admit there is some bias in my review.

That said, I do think that Wonderful 101 is underrated gem that thankfully got a second chance by being decently ported over to the PC from the Wii U. Anybody who ever played a platinum game would know what to expect. High octane, over the top, but super fun gameplay with bunch quirkiness in between and this game is no exception.

The port is pretty solid, though they have had to make some compromises when having to fit in what was the Wii U gamepad functionality into the game. It's not perfect, but it is still very much playable.

The gameplay does take some getting use to and is something that may put off a lot of people due to how "different" it may be to control at first. But it can be rewarding if you put in the time to learn and practice it.

Overall this is one of my personal favorite platinum games that is very near to my heart, alongside Bayonetta and Metal Gear Rising. I personally think this game is worth $40, but I would not blame anybody for wanting to wait for a sale.


Side note: I recommend playing with a controller instead of mouse and keyboard.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR play Bayonetta, Vanquish, MGR or anything else by Platinum instead.

Probably the worst Platinum game - well, the worst one with a budget at least. Everything the game tries to differentiate itself from it's vastly superior siblings ends up either failing or being a doomed gimmick.
Instead of using a button to swap weapons, you need to draw a shape with your analog and mostly mash one button, which slows combat and feels bad in general; Instead of a simple counter button, like a dodge or block, the game relies a lot on QTEs and encounter/context specific gimmicks for your defensive options.
What encounter/context specific gimmicks? Well, whatever the game feels like doing, I guess. In some encounters you need to have a specific hero out and be in a certain position, or attack with a specific hero, or jump at the right time to trigger a QTE, or block having a certain amount of heroes in your party. None of this is explained to you, mainly because it can't be, it's just random nonsense.
The game has a serious hard on for not explaining anything and mistakes obscure, poorly explained mechanics for depth - your block only works on certain attacks, and you need energy to use it, and actually it doesn't work on all attacks it should and that can be for different reasons and many bosses are just a gimmick fight with several QTEs where you need to guess what you're supposed to do and Jesus Christ, did Platinum make this? They seem to have completely forgotten that the combat in their game is cool because you, the player, is making those crazy combos and perfect dodges/counters happen, not the developers.
The game also has some severe camera problems; for some reason - I'm guessing the Wii u hardware couldn't take it - the camera is REALLY zoomed in a lot of sections (even with the "zoomed out" camera) and the map designers were particularly inspired in this game, creating lots of moving and twisting set pieces... with a zoomed in camera... when you control a dude with a hundred other dudes nearby... while you're trying to draw stuff on the screen. Ok.

A few extra bullet points: the port is pretty bad and looks terrible on high resolutions (in general, to be honest); The Wii-u screen, which you need to use for some parts, is just shoved on the lower-right corner of your screen - by the way, remember the whole "gimmick and QTEs" complaint? Yeah, it's this bad; the game loves on-rails and "different control-scheme" sections where you pilot ships and stuff like that, so I hope you like them too. They're always the best part of every Platinum game, right?; the menu's are an affront to God Himself.

I'm certain the game gets better after a first playthrough, since the player will know the crazy gimmicks - at least most of them, certainly (hopefully?) - and possess the obscure knowledge needed to play this like any other of Platinum's games, which they could play right now, with no extra effort or having to deal with non-orthogonal mechanics, instead of... instead of this game. Oh. Ooooh.
Still probably worth it if it's on sale and you're also a fan - just don't feel bad for dropping it midway through because it's boring and frustrating.

5/10\t\t\t\t\t
