\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game somehow manages to find ways to be unique, in a genre already laden with unique games.

Even if you’re a veteran of Platinum’s or Kamiya’s other works, I think you’ll find a lot to enjoy and some fun surprises here, it employs the same staples as other Character Action games: style points for manoeuvres such as late dodges and taunts, multiple actions which can combo into each other and flashy, over-the-top set pieces. To supplement this, it borrows the spectacle from the likes of Power Rangers/Kamen Rider and the recent Avengers movies.

To get it out of the way first, I found the story and characters to be arguably more involved compared to other Platinum games, each Wonderful One’s not so subtle gimmick making them more endearing than a standard gruff action man. However, this is in service to what I believe is the least important aspect of the game, I doubt many people flocked to Devil May Cry or Bayonetta for the narrative.

I like how the game looks and the style it’s going for, it’s not a graphical powerhouse by any means but the vibrant colours of the environment and toy-like features of the characters reinforce the playful nature of the game. Performance is obviously going to vary depending on your setup, but I found it to be not very demanding. Unfortunately, the game locks to 60fps by default with no option to change it aside from modding.

Onto the meat of the game, the core loop of the gameplay is going to feel like familiar grounds for Platinum fans, build up your combo by using a variety of actions, dodge, parry, etc. there are however a few changes to mix up the formula. The first of which is how weapon switching works, rather than assigned to one or two buttons, you’re given the Unite Morph ability, which ties nicely into the theme of the game in that you’re using every member of the Wonderful 100 to physically create your arsenal. Assigned to the right stick (replacing the Wii U Gamepad’s touch controls), you draw a rough representation of the weapon you want. I believe the touch controls are the superior method for this feature, but the game is generous enough that I could wing it most of the time, only finding issues with Unite Bomb and Glider which I didn’t use often. Whilst I’ll always prefer simply hitting a button to switch my weapon as I feel it provides an instant satisfaction of continuing your combo and styling on your enemies, the Unite Morph ability adds to the spirit of the game despite some of the annoyances it can cause.

Adding to this, Platinum opted for an isometric camera which I feel is another standout difference compared to their other games. I also feel this is a necessary change because of the Unite Morph ability and the way it interacts with the environment, there were plenty of times where I drew a bridge, ladder or a simple line to grab some out of reach items. Also, a lot of the enemies are massive in comparison to the Wonderful 100, so the isometric view also helps here to keep track of all the action.

Keeping with tradition, there are also temporary genre changes throughout the game, Shmup stages make a return taking up a decent chunk towards the finale, a couple of bosses also mix up the gameplay with a fun twist which I won’t spoil.

Overall if you’ve tried other Platinum games or are looking for something different, I think this game is worth your time, the controls may take some time to get used to, but mastering them and the abilities combined with the escalation of the game make for a fun and satisfying playthrough.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game pretty much exemplifies why using the right stick for camera control in 3D action games is the standard, automatic camera for something like this simply doesnt work well enough. Past the first mission almost every single fight is going to be in arenas too big for even the most zoomed out level of view, which the game won't keep to anyway as it constantly zooms in and out on its own, or full of clutter and giant enemies that obscure your position or even their own attacks from view with their bulk. There's also loads of gimmicky platforming segments where the floor tilts in all sorts of directions and your position and the position of platforms you need to jump to become impossible to accurately determine, or segments where your characters go into an enclosed area and it switches to an awful imitation of regular camera controls anyway. These segments are frequently meant to utilize the dual screen gimmick of the original console the game was on, the WiiU, and though I havent finished the game yet to see every implemenation of this gimmick I can say from the ones I have seen that it's never been worth the headache that it is to try and navigate those segments. The second screen for all of them has basically just been a stationary view of something simple that could have just been put in a little window on the original screen anyway, which is how it's implemented in this version, and that I guess just goes to show how ill-conceived the WiiU was in general.

The line drawing gimmick that takes the place of proper camera control is in no way a worthy exchange, past the first two weapons the shapes become far too similar for clear tracing in-combat and the quirk of deciding how big a weapon you get by how big you draw the line is pointless as not only is bigger always better but due to the imprecise nature of drawing with an analog stick you basically can't get the shapes properly differentiated at small sizes anyway.

At least the cutscenes are funny I guess\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t This game is the gene of Hideki Kamiya style.

The battle mechanics involved Bayonetta, Okami and MGR: Revengeance's pattern. Deeper mechanism like dodge slow down, flick left stick to parry can be unlocked in the late game.

Very intense too, you'll keep spamming buttons and change battle mode, there is no room for idling during battle. As you gather more hero, it'll hinder up your vision as they covers most of the screen space. Very often you'll lose track of what's happening and the small sized enemy will do a sneak attack on you.

The game itself is creative and innovative. Sometimes you'll need to use your own imagination to find hidden stuff. This applies to battle as well, you'll need to find enemy weakness by yourself. Howewer, slight inconsistance may occur but rarely happens. Ratio about 1 out of 10. Few parts made me scream due to inconvenience.

The pattern drawing sometimes work, sometimes don't. If you have a lot of experience in fighting game, I think will be fine for you.

PC port wise is good. Encounter no crash nor bug throughout my gameplay. The only problem for me is the controller UI setting always reset back to KBM. I gotta change setting everything I boot the game.

This is one of the game I'll recommend to try. Thank you Platinum Game for releasing this on the PC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo this is the first time I got around playing this game, as I unfortunately never could afford a Wii-U.
I am not a fan of many of the things this game does. The constant genre shifts feel unnecessary and often last far toooo long...
And even if I prefer the combat system of Devil May Cry, I can't help but admire this game in it's creativity and stubbornness. It's one of the most unique and over the top and surprisingly touching games I have ever played, and the combat has plenty of depth (but has a GIANT learning curve).
I was frustrated plenty of times during this game, but I am still giving it my recommendation. I want more Japanese games on the PC, and I am very happy knowing this game was ported here.
The Port is OK, performance is great, but forget playing with KB and mouse. I can't even find the control scheme for it and when I press escape, the game switches to windowed mode for some reason??????
I know the chance is low, but hoping for Bayonetta 2. Would gladly back that one on kickstarter, if it ever gets the chance to be ported to non-Nintendo platforms.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this game when it came out on the Wii-U, So where do I begin with this? Well I guess I start with the good points then the bad.

"GOOD"
The gameplay while a bit tricky is pretty solid and fun, Using the wonder liner to make the unite morphs is smooth (apart from a few like hammer, bomb, glider and a special one that I can't say for spoilers). Plus you have use other morphs to deal with some types of bad guys so you can't just use hand or sword. Sometimes the gameplay changes but they never overstay their welcome and a nice change of pace. Also load time are super fast and framerate is solid.

The story is a fun cheesy love letter to tokusatsu shows like Super Sentai & Kamen Rider while still be solid story with good writing and barely any dull parts. The characters are great for the most part and I'm sure they will win you over with their fun lines, banters and charm.

The music is simply wonderful, Do I have to say more?

The graphics look nice and colourful thou some models and some textures (mainly with normal characters) look somewhat dated due to it was a 2013 Wii-U game and I know it could turn so people off.

A single normal play through last roughly 15 hours which is good length and have great amount of replayable in a form of finding collectables, other wonders and getting better ranks. for the normal price of the game it's worth it.
-----------------------------
Ok on to the...
"BAD"
Since it was on the Wii-U and used the gamepad quite a bit, In some parts you have to look at the pad's screen which make those parts more tougher in this version. While they have made a button to make the second screen open and close but TBH I would like it if they redo those parts without the second screen.

The "remaster" (I'll get to that in a sec) haven't fixed the game's big issue and that is helping the player to fully understand the controls and mechanics. While they did made the guts and spring morphs free, it still doesn't cut it. even worse that the "Loading arena" can't be used since the load time are very quick now, they should of made the arena it's own mode to help new players.

There's no way to properly rebind controls which sucks.

This is for people who didn't had the OG game on the Wii-U, so those who did can maybe skip this. The remaster is more like a somewhat touched up port of the OG game, If you hope some new things then don't be surprised that it's almost the same as the OG which kind makes it's normal price point a bit hard to swallow. I still the game does enough to worth that price point but if you still own the game on the Wii-U then maybe wait for it to go on sale.
-----------------
oh yea, also a
"OTHER THING"
I heard that some people say the game have issues with some graphical and frame rate issues but I don't want put it in the BAD in case a patch fix those issues.
--------------------
The Wonderful 101: Remastered still have the issues of the OG Wii-U release and not sure if I can call it a remaster but I think the good outshine the bad and I highly recommend this game to anyone who's up for toku styled game with unique gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright I know this game may seem weird at first and it does have a learning curve, but it is a really unique and quite charming spectacle fighter by the people over at PlatinumGames. If you like these kind of games, I can't recommend this title enough.

The whole wonderline drawing mechanic and controlling the 100 mini super heroes at screen come to fruition and present one of the most complex yet gratifying combat systems I've experienced yet in a game of this type, it's full of little intricacies and ways to explore the plethora of weapons and options you've got at your disposal to dispatch the different kind of enemies you will face throughout the campaign, which by my surprise is quite long compared to other titles of the genre. Also beware there are tons of minigames offered in here, but I don't feel they outwear their welcome.

In conclusion, the game is fun as hell, it's deep on the gameplay department and it's over the top with its presentation and story. By the time of writing this, I've beaten the game once, but can not wait to dive in again and learn more about the combat and the cool ways to solve each "puzzle" presented in every encounter. And as a PSA, I have only played the game with the Frame Pacing Fix shared by Silent.
