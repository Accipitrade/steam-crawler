\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI originally played this on the Wii U and definitely had a lot of trouble getting the hang of things and never finished it. I think a lot of people expected the drawing mechanic to be exclusively used with a touchscreen but the game plays much better with an analog stick. The moment where it clicked was when I realized that the drawing mechanic is essentially weapon switching in other action games, but much deeper and skill-intensive. I think it's easy to start playing on auto-pilot and run into things that will frustrate you, but if you just take a step back and rethink how you are approaching the game it suddenly becomes more rewarding. It was a struggle unlearning habits and hand-holding that I'm used to in other games. The only parts that don't hold up so well are the genre shifts that require you to look at the gamepad screen. These show up in non-Wii U versions as a picture off to the corner of the HUD and are a little janky. There is also a shooter level late in the game that feels brutally difficult.

Besides that, the narrative in this game is really great! There's some excellent voice acting, great jokes and dialogue, and the story moves at a brisk pace, constantly upping the stakes at every turn. The level design gets crazier and crazier as time goes on. The soundtrack is also extremely memorable - I go back to listen to it every once in a while because its so awesome. I think that if the gameplay wasn't so inaccessible to some, the story and characters would've easily propelled the game's popularity.

I've heard that the first playthrough is the tutorial, but I've only beat it on Normal. I might try a hard playthrough after getting through all the other platinum games I haven't finished.\t\t\t\t\t

During its amazing parts (and there are many, many of them), TW101 is an over-the-top, adrenaline-in-your-eyeballs Super Sentai anime come to life. The cinematography, voice acting, art design, humor, and incredible set pieces all make you feel like you're watching a Saturday morning cartoon show on steroids. It's funny in a lot of places, stirs you up into a heroic frenzy in others (especially with the fantastic orchestral soundtrack), and it's also one of the most stylish games I've ever played this side of Persona 5.

If you're watching someone play through it and seeing the set pieces and bombastic boss fights and all the hilarious and awesome callbacks to other games and genres, it's easy to get caught up in the action and think that it's equally fun to play, but the truth is, it really isn't.

I get that Platinum Games want you to git gud so that you can play their games in the most stylish and optimal ways humanly possible. But TW101 definitely feels like a game stuck in 2013 with its mechanics, UI, and pacing. OK, here I go.

I'm not even going to complain about the disco fireworks explosion vomit that the combat is because there are ways around it when you understand the systems, but why in Kamiya's sake does everything grind to a halt when you acquire new members? They couldn't be introduced in the sidebar like all the temps, or at the end of operations since you're forced to watch the score screen anyway with no way out? Or how about un-telegraphed enemy moves, random team scatter, or hell, random moments of getting stuck or stunned without any indication of what's happening?

And speaking of sidebars, it really ground my gears to have the UI take over 2/3 (or more) of the screen when events happened, switch between zoom distances arbitrarily, and the ultimate worst, when the endgame narration started happening while in the midst of combat. What the actual F. Let me fight, or give me a cutscene, stop trying to do both TERRIBLY.

***INCOMING SPOILERS***

As for the endgame, just...wow. This is the first time I wanted to throw my controller at the wall for having finished a game. The repetition is absolutely mind-boggling. HA HA RED IS BEING EXTRA. HA HA BLUE BEING A DUM DUM. HMM WHY DON'T WE PIVOT THE ENTIRE PLOT AROUND AN ANNOYING LITTLE ♥♥♥♥ OF A KID?

How about that last boss, eh? Like that fight? How about 15 million final forms? Oh you want final forms for your team, its robot, AND its ship as well? Got you fam. Oh wait, you wanted credits? Nah, you don't, how about grinding even more for a pointless score while it rolls instead of being able to kick back and relax after breaking your pad or keyboard from mashing? Now how about replaying the intro, unskippable, but now YOU'RE the annoying little ♥♥♥♥? Good times! Oh wait, the credits wasn't done, how about 35 billion different endings before you get sent back to the menu to finish (because you bet your ass the huge "THANKS FOR PLAYING" banner was only ending 5 of 509345)?

It's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ incredible, is what it is. You should see and hear how much I wanted to Wonder Punch my own face playing through the last 30 minutes. I want to recommend this game, I really do, but I just can't. In my overwhelmingly frustrated opinion, the most wonderful move you can do is watch someone else play it.\t\t\t\t\t

Team! Unite up!

Wonderful 101, a game filled with mechanics, secrets, collectibles, nods to other games, humor, excitement, difficulty, creativity and love, or is it sadism? Maybe both

Story
Earth is under attack by an alien armada called GEATHJERK, a very long acronym that they explain early on but doesn't quite stick, it is up to the Earth's own defense force, the Wonderful 100 to travel all over the world and protect the super reactors that power the energy shield protecting the planet. And yet, everyone except their fearless leader acts dumb in the face of danger

Gameplay

An interesting blend of Pikmin, Viewtiful Joe, Okami and Bayonetta, you control the Wonderful 100 by moving a character designated as leader, who changes depending on your weapon, you change your weapon by using the mouse/right stick to use the Wonderliner, to move all the characters except your leader to draw shapes (like in Okami), the more people in your team the longer you can draw the shapes which gives the weapon a bigger size.
You can also recruit other people by drawing a circle around them, most of the people you recruit are civilians who will leave the team at the end of the mission, but there are also members of the W100 that stick with the team permanently, allowing you to start each mission with more people.
This principle of being able to give power to others also applies when recovering team members, when they get hit you don't lose health but they become stunned for a few seconds, which means you have less people to form weapons, you can get them back if you run over to them or use the Wonderliner to touch the unconscious people to immediately revive them, this is VERY important because everytime the leader gets hit, the entire party will get stunned, it will help get the party back together faster because the enemies are not gonna sit around and wait

Each weapon has its own basic attacks but also properties that make them useful in order to solve puzzles or counter enemy attacks, i.e. the fist can grab valves or containers or the sword can be used like a key or to reflect lasers, unfortunately, the game doesn't have much in the way of explaining things so you'll have to pay attention to cutscenes that introduce characters, keep an eye out for counter attack cues or experiment with things yourself.

Boss battles are very reminiscent of the ones in Bayonetta, where you fight giant enemies and must run on top of them to hit their weak points, and then finish them off with over the top cutscenes while button mashing, there is also the obligatory rival character that can use the same weapons as you, except that he can block your stuff and you can't block 95% of his, might be even harder than anything else in the game but that's what rivals are for, right?

Also, obligatory mention of many long shoot em up sections.

PC Port opinions
It runs well I guess? I had no problems with it, graphically it's nothing special but it works, just don't pay attention to the characters in the continue screen, or segments where you're in cramped areas as their quality is greatly reduced outside of cutscenes.
They also managed to introduce the second screen from the Wii U version as a toggleable screen with the tab/select button that acts as a map or as a menu to select your leader manually, not sure how it works with KB+M but in controller you cannot directly select options inside that second screen unless you're standing still, so it's not very practical in combat

Final verdict

Overall, the game is pretty intense but also very difficult, to the point i felt compelled to put down the game after every chapter or rival character battle (the last one made me quit the game for a few days even), it was a good ride, but when it's not going well it can be quite annoying, I still recommend it, as long as you're aware that this is not as lenient as other Platinum games, still beatable if you persevere, just that it will be a bumpy road. (no but really, that rival boss battle though, what the heck)
