\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never thought I’d see the day, but The Wonderful 101 finally got an encore. It was like the one reason why I hadn’t boxed up my Wii U yet. I even went as far as to buy new pieces of furniture to actually put that gamepad somewhere so wouldn’t neglect it as much. Tough luck though, The Wonderful 101: Remastered is all I ever wanted.

The Wonderful 101 is one of my favourite games ever. Heck, it might just be my favourite. Wonder Red has been my Steam Avatar for over 7 years now. The soundtrack never left my music player and the box art serves as my lock screen on my work computer so my students have to admire it whenever I hit that Win+L.
What makes The Wonderful 101 so amazing then? Let me tell you. This is quintessential video games. Colourful graphics, upbeat music, gameplay that is easy to learn but infinitely rewarding to master, crazy story, mad set-pieces and nearly infinitely replayable. It is the gift that keeps on giving.

The Wonderful 101 is mad crossbreed between Pikmin, Okami and Bayonetta and Power Rangers. You take control of a squad of up to 100 super heroes who can combine into giant technicolor weapons based on the symbols you draw with the right analog stick. Circle gets you a giant fist; a straight line makes for a sword, and a straight ‘L’ makes a gun and then some. The bigger the symbol, the bigger the weapon. You’re limited by your energy, so draw conservatively. You need certain weapons to avoid damage. The fist can absorb fire, the sword can reflect lasers and nullify lightning, while the whip can be used to rip spikes off your enemies. Drawing these symbols requires some practice, but once you get the hang of it you’ll be able to unlock the wild combo potential. Before you know it, you’ll go from having trouble with drawing the hammer, to unleashing 3 different attacks at once while continuously pounding the smaller enemies with your spare forces. And there are enemies to spare. Countless unique enemies and magnificent boss fights are a treat every time. Combat is so involving it becomes an art form onto itself. The game is therefore not shy to demand absolute perfection from you. This is a Platinum game after all. After every battle you’re weighed and measured, and when found wanting, you’ll go home with less than Pure Platinum. This game will condition you to hate the colour gold, but in the end, the reward is pure satisfaction.

In The Wonderful 101 you need to protect the Earth against and alien invasion. You start off with Wonder Red, who is a school teacher by day and a textbook superman in the face of danger. You’re joined by Blue, a swarthy police officer; Green, a portly sniper; Pink, a vivacious socialite; Yellow, shy Russian; White, public weeb and Black, hacker supreme in an effort to repel the unyielding torrent of alien invaders. The story is told through amazing cutscenes with dialog lifted straight out of snappily written Saturday morning cartoons. It is a bit pedantic at times, but amusing all the way through. I can sit through it without skipping every time. The dialog, characters and enemies are also full of Platinium Games references and Easter eggs, so if you’re into their work, you’re in for a treat.
What sets The Wonderful 101 apart from other games is the way it handles its set pieces and the pay off will always make you smile from ear to ear. After a long and intense boss fight where it is all hands on deck, the music rises to the heroic tunes of a newly orchestrated main theme that sets the mood for something incredible and it does not disappoint. Although you may frown on quicktime events, The Wonderful 101 does them tastefully, with ample time to react, incredible payoff when you pass and hilarious consolation if you fail.

Visually, the game is seven years old now. The PC version upscales cleanly to 1080p (I can’t do 4K on this PC) and a heavy dose of anti-aliasing makes it all look smooth. The framerate is also smooth at 60fps on a GeForce 970. A much needed boost from the 30fps on the Wii U. The game is violently colourful and used high-gloss models to give that action figure look. Facial animation is a bit ‘8-bit’, but that’s the trade off for having 100 of them on screen at any time. However, it’s the total picture that counts, the comedic timing and amazing screenwriting really put everything together. Don’t dismiss it for looking cartoony until you have beaten your first real boss.
The soundtrack was composed to scream heroism at every note. Brass, choirs, marching drums, it has it all. The principal voice cast does an incredible job selling the insane story credibly. It gets a bit over the top, but at this point, over the top is the new normal.

The Wonderful 101 will humble you the first time through. Don’t feel bad, this is normal. It will take time to come to grips with the controls, the camera, the combat system, how to draw a hammer, dodging, counters and crowd control. It’s a lot to take in. However, do not let that ‘plastic’ trophy get you down. Just get to the end, smile and all, and then go right back to the beginning with all your powers already unlocked and ready to fire. Suddenly you’re playing a new game. No longer are you trying to beat it; you’re trying to own it. Additionally, the game has so many secrets and things to unlock, you’ll be occupied for a while. Take into account that it also includes Platinium Games’ general quality of life care and you won’t need another game for the rest of this pandemic.

All in all, I cannot recommend this game enough. Its insanely cool set pieces are worth playing, the music is worth enjoying and the combat is so cool, it is prize to master. The PC version is a simple port, but it did what it needed to do, 60fps, smooth textures and little to no load times. You’ll be doing yourself a disservice if you don’t try it. For me, I’m just happy I can now get a higher definition avatar. It has been a long time coming.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA wonderful, albeit unconventional action game, with amazing voice-acting, music, and a surprisingly good story to go along with it. The Wonderful 101 doesn't hold the player's hand, perhaps to a fault at times since it expects the player to pick up on things they may not think about, such as Unite Guts only working on "blunt" attacks because it's symbolized as jello pudding or the Charge attack stunning most enemies once they've been hit enough times, with the stun state being the only way to juggle foes. It's combat starts as a frustrating snowball of constantly losing your Unite Morphs as you learn each enemy's quirks, and turns into a euphoric display of skill as you combine multiple Unite Morphs to quickly dispatch enemies that gave you so much trouble when you first encountered them. Action game prowess is not enough for The Wonderful 101 either, as it tasks the player to master a multitude of different encounters such as shoot-em-up sections or those that utilize the dual-screen function, on top of it's standard action encounters. Not all these sections are winners, but the design of requiring players to demonstrate skills outside enemy control and juggling is enough for me to appreciate them.

As a port, however, it does not seem it was handled in the best way possible. There are some bugs, the worst of which require a reset of combat encounters. A lot of assets look like they were just upscaled from the Wii U version, leading to things like text found in the enemy introductions and certain images looking blurry or jagged. Texture effects, like the "energy" beams found on the tentacles of Vijounne's mech, look very bad and distracting when they're a certain distance away from the camera, but these are thankfully few and far between.

Technical issues aside, I recommend The Wonderful 101 to action fans vets and newcomers alike. It may not be for everyone, but you'll never know until you try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you have played the game, and are just wondering if it is a good port it is, ran perfetly on my machine 950gtx + amd 6300X + 8gb of ram at high settings 1080p. i never noticed a graphical glitch or frame drop load times are extremelly short, i haven't tried playing it with kb and mouse (doesn't sound like a good idea) so if you don't have a controller you may want to wait till you have one.

Side note, i played the game with a nintendo switch pro controller using the steam controller thing to remap a bunch of the face buttons so they matched the place on the xbox 360 controller, i didn't run with any issues using this configuration.

The review will be spoiler free untill i warning you about it, it will also contain a couple gameplay tips you may want to find out by yourself, but id say they barely spoil the experience.
(THE FIRST TIME YOU CAN GO TO THE WONDER MART, BUY UNITE GUTS AND SPRING, i don't get why they aren't part of your initial moveset since they are basically free and are esential to defense)

anyways

The defining feature of TW101 is the wonder liner which lets you change weapons, it may seem like it was intended for the wii u game pad, trust me the analog stick is way better than that, people on the wii u were using the right stick too, the morph shapes you have to draw are pretty forgiving, what i mean is that you don't really need to draw them perfectly, it will be a little bit hard to get used to drawing them but after a while you will be drawing multiple of them in no time to string combos, id say the key to it is thinking of them as fighting game inputs, Hand is a C motion, gun is an L motion whip is an S motion etc.

There are a whole lot of enemy's, id say around 14 each one with plenty attacks and ways to defeat them, a lot of them seem like they can only be defeated by using a specific weapon, however besides ONE i can always think of other strategy's to circumvent the "intended" one, and this is one of the best things the game has, because im not even sure myself if the enemy im thinking of has an alternate strategy to it, because of all of the options the game has i may be missing something, there is always something new to learn on the game, don't get me wrong the game explains all of the mechanics you need to beat it with quick and easy to understand tutorials.

Each one of the bosses is extremely diferent from the others, some changing the game to a shootemup or a punch out clone, there are plenty of these gameplay variations each one is executed but personaly i feel like a lot of the shootemup segments could be shorter, because they lack a lot of the depth the real game has, even when you are fighting the bosses normally a lot of them can have pretty specific ways of fighting them so on your first time going through the game they will probably beat you up a whole lot, but after you understand all there attacks and they ways you can beat them they are really satisfying to beat and add a lot of variety to the game which will make repeated playthroughs way more enjoyable as a result.

If you've played bayoneta you will know that a whole lot of the QTE's on it were pretty unfair, with barely any time to react, here they are really fair, with plenty of time to do them and even if you don't manage to do them they won't instakill you (afaik i failed almost none on the whole game) coupled with a funny and unique fail state animation for all of them.

Also small thing don't be intimidated by being 100 characters, basically the only one that can take damage is the leader (you) if the others get hit they will get put in a dizzy state which will run out after a bit or if you touch them like pikmin.

All of this is wraped with stelar graphics highlighting important stuff, like incoming enemy attacks, and giving all of the actions the player will perform a whole lot of impact and flare, a exelent soundtrack elevating a lot of moments in the game to unforgettable heights and writing and VA that will make you genuinely laugh with the game.

Minor negative points, and i remark these are minor.
I found a couple of bugs on the time ive played, one related to a bridge on operation 1A, and another that makes it so frozen enemy's can still damage you if they started an attack even when they are frozen. Both hopefully will be patched.

When there is no ground on platforming sections my depth perception goes to hell.

Minorest one, the very first time you boot up the game it may use keyboard prompts even if you are using the gamepad, if that happens go to options and change it.


SPOILERY STUFF BELLOW

A couple of gameplay tips.
If the camera seems too zoomed in when exploring you can do a small line (not enough to make a morph) to zoom the camera a whole lot.

Get the speed liner as quick as you can, you will end up getting it at some point, and id say you are better starting getting the muscle memory when drawing with it.

Ukemi, hero counter, hero time and speed charge are the best blocks. don't @me

"This just keeps getting better and better"

The best action game.\t\t\t\t\t
