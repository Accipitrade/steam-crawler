\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Wondeful 101 is a fantastic game whose developers put a lot of heart and soul into...but who unfortunately didn't really put a whole lot of thought into the making of the PC Port.

Performance-wise the game runs perfectly fine - with my various much average rig still able to chug along at 60 FPS - but there are currently a few issues on some of the more technical / mechanical things that ruin the experience.

The game does not like dual monitors at all - with mine for example no matter if I went for a locked fullscreen it'd default to borderless, with my mouse flying off into my second monitor and every click sending the game into a minimized state.

The Wonder-Liner feature also works incredibly awfully with Mouse and Keyboard - the Wonder Liner is controlled entirely by the mouse, which in theory SHOULD work even better than an analog stick, yet it has it's own heaps of problems.

Primarily the first issue is that Wonder Liner has a mind of it's own, with the line itself starting or turning however it wants to without player input.

A simple line to Unite Sword for example will almost always start off going into the wrong direction from your character, and in the process somehow form up for something like Unite Gun.

In Post - Game is fun but hampered by technical aspects, and until these issues are addressed I cannot really recommend the game to anyone - or at least anyone without a controller.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRuns at perfect 60fps, set to high, at 900p. On my i5-2500K and HD7850.
This Wii-U original has been ported "emulator-style". No functions or sequences requiring the gamepad screen have been removed. Instead, you are given an OSD of the gamepad screen that you can toggle with SELECT button, which is necessary for certain functions and sequences in the game.
Just like the original release, I really feel that Chapters 1 and 2 (operations 001 and 002) are very clumsy in both writing and overall design. I beg of you to be forgiving for janky sequences that get you stuck and lots of long and awkward cut scenes in these chapters. Not to mention, you can't even block OP-002 bosses punches till you gather enough fighters, which you are without for the first half of the fight (without NG+ atleast).
The game gets more exciting and has better flow from Operation 003 and onwards. The main campaign is my absolute favorite campaign of all Director Kamiya's games. I recommend it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanna call it Hideki Kamiya's magnum opus, man who is most capable to push Platinum Games and action games.
This game's aproach to combat is different and it creates new type of experience and retains everything that makes games like this fun. In a same way Viewtiful Joe was different from Devil May Cry, its different from Bayonetta. Funny how The Wonderful 101 and Viewtiful Joe share superhero theme, change camera perspective from previous Kamiya's game and some game design ideas (heavy dependence on energy bar and how its maximum capacity resets at the start of every stage and player must collect things to grow it again). Actually all those games share DNA in some way, its really cool to see for example Okami's drawing gimmick be reinterpreted in different context, yet serving to kinda same goals. It makes trivial act of switching weapons its own gameplay mechanic that makes player think in a new way.
I can talk all day how it also shares DNA with works of Shinji Mikami, who is Kamiya's sensei, but its not in depth piece about auteur theory and Hideki Kamiya, nor it is in depth review on The Wonderful 101 actualy. Its a love letter..

And this game is also a love letter, to video games and Tokusatsu, to Platinum Games own legacy and beyond (for example one of its failed QTE animation is a homage to Devil May Cry 3 short video you see when booting the game). Yet it creates identity of its own.
This game has character, creativity and burning guts! Uncompromising and unapologetic in its drive to break up its core gameplay with as many distractions and gimmick stages, it has like 5 shoot em up sections with different gameplay styles. You may not like it, but I think it deserves a respect.

This is not by any means abstract perfect game and I have my own problems with some choices and execution, nor is it perfect conversion (well depends on what you looking for I guess), BUT it gives me insane amount of joy.
Very glad to be playing it again 6 years later!
May Platinum maintains its luster forever!\t\t\t\t\t
