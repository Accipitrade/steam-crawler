\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is another horror game I love and had lots of fun with. The game's art is adorable as well gruesome. The story is a take on humans being pets/livestock but it's not the preachy "be kind to animals" story. The game gives you simple yet effective scary for you to piece on your own as you progress. I even like the protagonist Lisa's "conflict" and what motivates her journey. The game plays like a stealth rouglike but you retry the stage when you die instead of starting from the beginning which I appreciate you only lose what you've collected in previous stages. The developers offer 5 difficulty settings from very easy to h*ll so it helps whether you're a coward or a fearless gamer. The game is around 2 hours or longer depending on your skill or if you're missing some achievements.
There are only two faults in the game I wish the developers could. The option to choose stages. The other is that the pause button doesn't pause the game because enemies and traps can still kill me even when I need to stop the game for a minute.
Before the Night is definitely a great game for fans of the cute horror genre\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs best I can describe it, Before the Night is a horror-themed puzzle game with stealth and top-down shooter elements. Here's a breakdown of that description, for sake of clarity:

Puzzle - your typical goal is to navigate obstacles and dodge hostiles to get to the exit. The first tutorial levels are straightforward with a single solution. As you progress, the stages become larger and more populated. You're presented with multiple paths to reach the same goal, some riskier than others. Running around without a plan becomes deadly, especially at night: it's easy to get lost, make mistakes, and get run down by a monster or killed in a trap. Getting to the exit is one challenge; getting there safely is the other.

Stealth - while there is combat in this game, not all mobs are killable. Stealth isn't mandatory for all but a handful of levels, but it's a common feature in most and you'll probably be using it quite a bit.

Top-down shooter - Lisa doesn't have a gun, but she can throw hammers at lethal velocity in any direction. Close enough ¯\\_(ツ)_/¯ (Additionally, later levels will provide a variety of new weapons. Some are a direct upgrade to her hammers, others are mechanically unique and in limited supply)

Let's take an aside to discuss combat, since that's something you'll encounter on-and-off. Before the Night's combat is sporadic, brief, and intense. One level will have you taking potshots at a single patrolling hostile while another level will spawn a whole group to hunt you down. Keep in mind that you aren't the only one with projectiles. Some of these hostile NPCs have ✨magic✨

I've had my say on gameplay, so onto the story: it's wild. I can't say much without getting into spoilers. What I can tell you is that every time I tried to guess what was going to happen next, I was wrong. If you've watched the animated trailer, you've probably picked up on the primary horror trope, but there is so much more going on. While the general premise isn't new, the presentation is fresh in its oddity and ability to stand on its own without the crutch of horror clichés.

I'm going to wrap this up with my humble opinion: I wasn't expecting the game to be this good. It is shockingly polished for a one-person project. It's also short, but that's because the goodness is concentrated. The game can be completed in 6 to 7 hours.
