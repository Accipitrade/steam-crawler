\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTl;dr cool devs and alright game

A bit short for the price, clocking at roughly 4-5 hours if you're not rushing, and pacing is a bit all over the place, but I still enjoyed my experience. The game works on a series of levels, where you need to reach the exit of each level, trying to get as far as you can before things start turning into monsters. About halfway through the game, it becomes less "run and hide" and more super meat boy with you having to teleport through hordes of saw blades and naruto run away from monsters because hiding usually ends with them camping on you with a hiding spot that slowly disappears.

Pros:
* Puzzles have a nice amount of non-linearity and being able to use items from old levels rewards staying alive by sometimes letting you bypass puzzles entirely
* The devs have already made a number of quality of life improvements and are adding more to work out some balancing issues
* The art style and sound track are really good and nail the atmosphere of cutesy villages and bad trip nightmares

Cons:
* Once you get a feel for it the game is pretty short and only lasts longer if you die a lot
* Not much replayability factor, no level select, no secrets to find, and a lot of levels exist just for exposition
* Nothing in the animated trailer is really present in the game, so if you're expecting something like a Promised Neverland kind of story, it's not quite that

Overall, I'd probably recommend getting when its on sale, or full price if you like supporting devs who care about feedback and listen\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I bought this game on a whim because I saw it was a horror game and the animated trailer was REALLY good. I liked pretty much everything about this game, from the music to the puzzle elements to the combat.

In terms of the music I like that it wasnt too over the top and allowed the game to keep my focus while beating the later half of the game. the music perfectly portrayed a simple cute tune that could switch to a creepy feeling instantly.

the puzzles were really nice imo they werent too complicated, but when you factor in the enemy placements i found myself taking a couple minutes each level to understand the layout of where the flowers, buttons and the enemies was to make sure i can pass the level quickly. I also enjoyed while it was a puzzle it didnt force you to find a single solution to beat the level. the order you can press the buttons and destroy the flowers was not fixed so I enjoyed figuring out what was the optimal order.

The combat wasnt too complicated either, you had several different weapons throught the game each with different play styles, the only complaint I can think of with the weapons is that since the weapons were also used to complete certain puzzles that once you were past those puzzles they stopped giving you those weapons. other than that all the weapons were fun. At the beginning of the game the enemies were weak and you can outrun them if you were quick with your reactions. but later in the game the enemy variety really put my shift button under some pressure. I enjoyed the teleporting dash, it made dodging the enemies attacks enjoyable.

If I had to mention some complaints I have only a few. when I mentioned that late game it was fun to dodge the enemy, it would be hard at times because when on my first attempt at a level it can be hard to gauge where the walls were at. sometimes it would look like a wall i cant pass, when I can pass through it, and sometimes it would look like I can pass through it but couldn't, because of this I died more than I should've lol. but to be honest this is only a small complaint since once i figured out where the walls were it wasn't a issue.(also the shift dash definitely saved my life many times when figuring out where the walls were) and looking at the developers comments they realized this was a issue and fixing the walls in some levels in a update coming in a day or two. The only other complaint that I have is the dialogue was a little bland at times. I only ever thought the dialogue was bad when lisa's friends were talking to her. (the dialogue seemed a little cliche).

Other than those two points I have no issues with the game and I really enjoyed it enough to play through the whole game in one sitting. and im looking forward to more games from this developer, keep up the great work ^.^

TLDR: The game is about 4-5 hours long, the music is good, the vibes the game gave off were really good, the combat was really fun, and the puzzles were really fun paired with the combat. The dialogue could use a tad bit work, but over all it is pretty good. Id rate this game a 4/5 and worth the money!\t\t\t\t\t
