\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game demonstrates the overlooked importance of simplicity.

The game uses a lot of dirty methods to make you want to progress in the game. EVERYWHERE is an opportunity to level up or complete an achievement. This game would be a dangerous free to play game, it was apparently at one point was one. With the massive variety of weapon unlocks you need to grind for, there is ALWAYS something to look forward to that gets you practically addicted to the game.

It's a fun, relaxing, game with a great variety of color... something lacking in modern games, color. You have a vairety of modes, such as trying to shoot targets, trying to shoot through rings before hitting other players, a mode where everyone versus a single player with a massive amount of health, etc. It's great it has variety, but the modes basically don't deliver the same amount of fun as do the normal free for all or team battle modes.

It also has a variety of maps you can play on, a good number of them feeling unfair to some of the players balance wise, and they're so terribly simple I just don't understand why they don't just give players some form of map editor. Over time, these maps rebalnce themselves within battle, giving players otherwise potentially stuck in a hole some wiggle room. You cannot create tunnels in the game, which arguably is a good thing due to most weapons not being designed for them. So if you ever are in a hole and did not upgrade your tank to drive up steep hills, you're probably stuck until you make yourself a ramp which can, at anytime, be sabotaged by other players.

You can upgrade your tank in a variety of ways. It's not super diversed in exactly how you can upgrade it, some trees feel lacking in fact as if the developer ran out of ideas. Despite that, it does the job right and makes you really feel you're designing the tank based on your playstyle more than how the game wants you to play. You even have a "luck" tree which increases your odds of getting good weapons, even if you have not unlocked these weapons yet.

Each weapon has its very own skill tree that can unlock an improved version for you to fire at some point. Even if you unlocked the best variant of a weapon, it doesn't mean you will get it and can very well still obtain an inferior version of the weapon. For most weapons, labeling the earlier variants of the weapon "inferior" can be inaccurate for it's more about trade offs on some. This is a game where you'd have to spend a long time to completely finish and you'll likely stop playing by the time that comes for a moment.

This game doesn't have a whole lot of gameplay, so you will likely play it a lot for a moment and then slowly take breaks and coming back until you stop all together. However, you will definitely most likely play it again at some point. Despite the simplicity and the grindy design of the game, it's a good game. For the price, it isn't too bad of an investment as long you have other friends on board.

There is some problems, however. Though few, some players use various exploits that is impossible to detect in game, such as rulers. Because of this, it's hard to know if players are good or cheating which causes a lot of drama. This game has, multiple times, attempted to fix this with various methods and all of them were more toxic than the cheaters in my honest opinion. One example was nudging, which only hurts the players playing honestly since what it did was adjust shots slightly, causing it so the good players no longer have the feel of the game and the cheaters to simply adjust their measurments method. Another example was like nudging, but randomized, meaning ALL players are going to have inconsistent shots, except the players already bad at the game will be even worse and the cheaters will still be dominate. There is a wind option, which is honestly a good mechanic despite it not being a good tool against cheaters since it makes the game fresh and more challenging.

Another problem is that the game implements tank customization, but you literally have like five options for each piece. This might be an exaggeration, it could be less. I feel that it could give more variety, some way. I believe it's difficult because of how simple the tanks are, the developer probably has no clue what else to really implement other than what is already available.

This problem exists in a lot of areas in the game. Maps and weapons have an overwhelmingly huge variety, but other areas do not most likely due to, as mentioned, lack of ideas. This doesn't hurt the game in anyway, but it's worth noting that the game does sometimes feel empty in some areas.

Pros-
Great weapon variety
Lots of progression opportunities to casually introduce other parts of the game
Solid gameplay

Cons-
Some bad maps for fair play
VERY grindy
cheating is very easy
lacking in content in some areas, such as tank customization.


Overall, I believe 7/10 is a deserved score. It's a great game and I think every gamer that likes laid back gaming sessions should consider this. However, it's not by anymeans revolutionary but it never really was meant to be. It's just fun.\t\t\t\t\t
