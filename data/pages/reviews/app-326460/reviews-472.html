My gun angles upwards by one, by two, by ten, good enough.
I aim a little more forward, my gun practically full force.
I have only one shrapnel bomb left. It is a risky play. My tank is on its last legs – I can hear the spluttering of the engine and a roaring sound that shouldn’t be coming from a healthy tank.
The shrapnel bomb could quickly explode and catch my tank as it releases the little pieces of sharp metal.
It’s a risk I have to take.
I press fire, and the shrapnel bomb fires forward.
It hits the wall, rebounding back towards me.
But my last card hasn’t been played yet.
Above me, a pink bounder.
The shrapnel bomb smashes into the pink bounder, bouncing the bomb back to the other side of the field, landing directly onto to enemy tank.
A smile lights up on my face as the shrapnel bomb explodes, every piece of shrapnel drilling directly into the opposing tank.
A sigh of relief as the tank explodes, and I win the match.
My tires, barely holding on, pull me out of the battlefield and back into safety.

That is just one divine moment I have endured playing this game – The game is a host for moments like these.
Edge-of-seat gameplay, being that one shot away from victory or defeat.
Shellshock Live is one of my favourite games.
It can be so tense, so utterly pressured that you can barely keep your eyes off the screen.
But it can also be the most chill experience.
I have met and talked to plenty of people on Shellshock.
Nearly every one of them are great people and make great conversation.
After a hiatus, signing back onto the game is like attending a school reunion.
You see people you haven’t seen in ages, you ask them about their life, they ask you about yours, and you play a game that brings people together.
Be it for a tense game or a chill one, the game has an attraction beyond gameplay.
It is the people that genuinely make Shellshock for me.
When I load up a game, I want to play a game (obviously), but I also want some social aspect, some sort of commonality between players.
Other games can mimic this feeling, but for me, Shellshock gets it right.

I wish I had discovered Shellshock earlier in life, I may not play it very often anymore, but whenever I do hop on it, I know I am in for a treat.\t\t\t\t\t
