\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA strangely good game for the level of simplicity, mixing, what I'll call simple physics with tactical decisions on how to blow up your enemies (and occasionally a teammate if you aren't careful).

The game has an insane variety of different types of shots to fire, I believe there are about 60 (or more) individual weapon trees, from shooting basic bullets, to using flame throwers, to shooting a tidal wave, and even some more nonsense things like the "cat cannon", or even the nuke!

The game is progression based. Initially you only have access to around 30 different types of weapons (and their upgradeable trees). They are the more basic of all weapons, which really are not exceptionally powerful, but generally have good tactical ability, since most of them can be used in any situation if you can aim well.

As you gather experience points, which are accumulated as you play the game, you get access to newer weapons, which behave differently. Like the Dead Weight shot. Which once it reaches it's highest point in it's arc, will fall straight down.

The only real problems I have with the game is the level discrepancy between a person who has played for a year, and people who play new today is the fact that as you go up a level, you upgrade your tank. Certain trees directly improve the amount of damage output, this is usually offset if a person of the same level uses the defensive tree. The problem lies where when one person is maximum level, and the other is at a minimal level. The person with upgrades will have a significantly higher chance of defeating the lower level player. Partially due to having superior weapons, and partially due to getting boosts to the tank, such as higher starting HP, and higher critical ratios. However, it is easily possible to beat a "superior" player with base weapons. That and low level players now get a small XP boost for "playing out of their league".

Other than the above statement the game is excellent. This is a game that deserves more attention, there are just enough players to get a few servers running, but usually never more than 3 8 man servers. Hopefully this review would bring attention to it. Apparently Twitch brought more attention to it, not it's hard to NOT find a game.

Another plus side, I just noticed. The game is being constantly improved. There are updates nearly every day, and the developers actively play the game to get feedback from players as the changes are made.

I think it's definitely worth the 15 dollar price (it was when I got it, and still is now!)\t\t\t\t\t

Its still the same principle. You set power and angle and click fire and chaos ensues. This game adds a lot of single player missions, some of which are very hard to complete. With those you should be able to have a decent selection of weapons without ever having done a multiplayer match thanks to large XP rewards.

The update frequency is insane and it isnt mundane bug fixes either but rather constant small batches of extra content. (and the price got nerfed in one update so thats also cool from the developer).

At this point it outshines its 2 predeccesors in every way imaginable. It has an insane amount of weapons to just keep on unlocking, a lot of which are actually unique. There is even pop culture reference weapons like a mario turtle shell or a portal gun.

So far there is 8 unique gamemodes of which 4 focus on killing one another. and there are many more to come.

The biggest disadvantage is that high levels eat low levels. I'm not nearly the best at this game but I will crush you every time in a 1 vs 1 should you just start and then play against me, because of having a much larger damage potential thanks to a much larger weapon selection. Though the weapons you will have are chosen at random every ten turns. The best way around this is either playing a non killing mode and accepting losses, playing in a team or only joining rooms with people around your level, which is something you can set.

And yes it is turnbased but that doesnt mean one after another it can also mean that everyone waits till everyone clicked shoot and then everybody fires at the same time. And now imagine 8 players at the same firing weapons that on their own can already destroy half a map....absolutely beautiful.

Also there is a hot seat mode so you can use it as a local party game.

The community has grown so much compared to when this game first came out. This means rooms always fill up easy AND it means far too many cancerous players that make you thank the dev for adding a mute button. Currently a reporting mechanic is in the testing phase.

Awright chumps lets sum it up:
You got friends and yall looking for an online multiplayer or local game to play together. Or you like the concept of games like worms in general? You want to nuke things and have mindless fun? This and much more awaits you, worth it even without a sale.

And it is NOT a dead early access project. It is already a full game, you could release it like it is and it would be considered a good game that gets a lot of updates.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly I'm going to preface this review by noting my experience with this game has taken place in the beta stage of development and things are, I assume, going to change with the full release.

ShellShock Live is a fun, pick-up-and-play tank game a la Worms, Gunbound, and my childhood favorite, Amiga Tanx--but you already knew that. While there's obvious advantages to higher-leveled players (greater weapon selection, tank buffs, etc) the game is still strictly skill based and nailing cool shots is fun whether or not you win the round. ShellShock Live is the kind of game you can pick up with your buddies and play one or fifty matches in an evening without so much as blinking.

As far as I can tell there is only one music track which, as epic as it is, gets annoying after the first five minutes--this is easily solved on my end by cutting the music off, but the inclusion of a few more audio tracks as the game approaches release would be a welcome asset.


Additionally, I would like to see the eventual incorporation of custom match modifiers / mutators where the host can select / ban certain weapons. A Sniper only match, for instance, would be a heck of a lot of fun.

I've enjoyed my experience with ShellShock Live so far, and the few technical issues I've had were quickly and professionally rectified by the devs (specifically kChamp, thanks!). I think the current pricepoint is completely reasonable, assuming that the game will continue to evolve and update without incorporating any pay-to-win elements. Like I said before, the game is currently in beta and I hope to see many additions in the future as this game approaches official release.\t\t\t\t\t
