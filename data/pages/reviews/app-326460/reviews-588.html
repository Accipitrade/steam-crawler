\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rate the game ShellShock Live 4.5/5. I play it on a computer. ShellShock Live is Excellent and a worthy successor to the game on which it is clearly based; Scorched Earth. I have been searching and waiting for a modern, multiplayer replacement for Scorched Earth for years and in ShellShock Live I have finally found a worthy replacement.

In short, ShellShock Live is an excellent, easy-to-learn, hard-to-master, stable, multiplayer, 2-dimensional, turn-based, tactical, artillery game featuring a wide variety of wacky weapons and difficult scenarios wherein the terrain (and thus the tactical situation) is radically changed by explosions as each player takes their turn.

+Adequate graphics which provide a good sense of style (with a nifty neon aesthetic) while providing excellent clarity even if a screen is extremely small.
+I have chatted with some players who said they were playing this on their phones and if this is true then this may be one of the few games that may be fun on either a computer or a phone and may allow players to compete with one another in a fair manner regardless of what type of device they are using.
+This is a fairly popular game and players from around the world can be found playing at nearly all hours of the day/night.
+The community is moderately friendly and fairly helpful. Oddly, an unusually high number of players seem eager to chat about politics/economics during the course of each match.
+The sound is loud features nifty explosions but some explosion sounds may be too loud for many types of music if you are listening to music on media player or whatever in the background. The sound volume can be easily decreased however.
+Lots of steam achievements.
+Very easy to learn and amazingly intuitive controls which, on a computer make extensive use of basic mouse controls to aim and fire using an easy point and click method. Tank movement is handled entirely with the A and D keys to reposition your tank to either the left or right.
+Although I have witnessed a few allegations of hacking (once in a great, great while), I seriously doubt that any hacking is actually taking place in this game. On occasion I've seen even the highest level players miss their shots completely.
+This game features an amazingly wide assortment of wacky and interesting weapons to choose from, each with its own tactical benefits/downsides which the game leaves you to figure out for yourself as you play.
+Extremely easy to get into and play for a while and then get out again. Connects fast, lobbies work fairly well.
+This is not a pay to win game. Items in its in-game shop don't give a great deal of benefit but merely add some minor variety.
+Cussing seems fairly permissible but it is not extremely common in this game. Indeed, saying just about anything in the chat appears to be permissible. There is a curse filter option in the main menu for those who want it.
+The match-making lobbies for individual matches feature your steam name and the picture/avatar you chose for steam (if you've made it public). This makes it very easy to look up people who you've played/chatted with in steam and send friend requests. As a result, this game may be a great way to meet new people on steam. Just don't get so caught up in chatting that you forget to fire before the timer runs down. (Not firing during a team match is generally frowned upon because by not firing you are not helping your team to win).

+/-Leveling up and upgrading your tank feels rewarding and fun (though tank upgrades really only make much of a difference after you get a whole lot of upgrades).
+/-There is still a lot more that could be done with this game but the game's developers must strike a very fine line between fun and frustration while ensuring there are always a lot of players for each game mode.

-The music gets boring fast and consists of various player victory songs (which are all pretty good and fun) but only 2-3 background songs. Before long I turned down the game's background music and started listening to music on media player instead. Fortunately, the game can be minimized (by pressing the windows key) and maximized again (allowing one to use a music player during the game) without causing your game to freeze up.
-The singleplayer/main campaign features many training missions but are not nearly as fun, interesting or enlightening as playing against other people. This game is first and foremost, a multiplayer game.
-This game can get boring and repetitive if you play it for more than a couple hours at a time.
-Players are not adequately penalized for doing nasty stuff to other players (like destroying tanks on their own team). Damage to allied tanks should affect your own hit points, not the hitpoints of the allied tanks you hit (or an option for this should exist) or at least friendly fire should be capable of being turned off. Players are not penalized for working together even when they are officially on opposite teams (like if they know each other outside of the game). There are apparently very few things a player can't do to their supposed allies but of course, doing nasty things to your allies will usually cause people to quit out of the match in a hurry. Fortunately, this game does not feature very many trolling or irritating players, for the time being. Players can be kicked from individual game room lobbies but not from ongoing matches. If a player is behaving badly your only recourse is to quit out of the match yourself though this avoids the terribly irritating difficulty of potentially good players getting unjustly kicked mid-match for incorrect reasons.
-There are no options governing how turns should be taken. There are no options for round-robin, least health goes first or other styles of turn-taking.
-The leveling and weapon/tank upgrade systems are all a lot of fun and kept me wanting to come back for more but higher level players can usually defeat lower level players regardless. There should be an option in the match-making screen which potentially limits the level of the weapons available to players rather than just their hit points. This way low level players might actually want to play with high level players more often. Or perhaps some sort of handicap could exist to give low level players increased health/armor when fighting against high level players. As it stands, the higher your level the more you win and it's basically that simple, though the higher your level the longer it takes to level up further. Currently most lower level players won't ever want to be in the same match as a higher level player.
-There are no AI characters or bots which you can add to multiplayer matches to increase the size of a match (for instance if you have just two humans and want to play a big match) and there is no human versus AI mode which multiple players can have access to at the same time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking for a review you've come to the wrong place, but if your looking for an outstanding pie recipe you've come to the right place.

Ingredients:
1. 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
2. 1/2 cup white sugar
3. 1/2 cup brown sugar
4. 1/4 cup water
5. 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
6. 1 pinch salt
7. 5 apples - peeled, cored and sliced
8. 1 pastry for double-crust pie

Directions:
1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).
2. Combine butter, white sugar, brown sugar, water, cinnamon, and salt in a saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil, remove from heat and set aside.
3. Roll out half the pastry to fit a 9-inch pie plate. Place bottom crust in pie plate; pour in apple slices.
4. Roll out top crust into a 10-inch circle. Cut into 8 (1-inch) wide strips with a sharp paring knife or pastry wheel. Weave the pastry strips, one at a time, into a lattice pattern. Fold the ends of the lattice strips under the edge of the bottom crust and crimp to seal.
5. Pour butter-sugar mixture over top of pie, coating the lattice, and allowing any remaining sauce to drizzle through the crust.
6. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes. Reduce heat to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C), and bake until the crust is golden brown, the caramel on the top crust is set, and the apple filling is bubbling, 35 to 40 more minutes. Allow to cool completely before slicing.\t\t\t\t\t
