\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt a play-time of almost 2 hours (level 11 I believe), I've barely scratched the surface of this game, but one thing is for sure: I CAN'T stop thinking about the damn thing. The thrill of battle, the satisfaction of the progression, and the god-like feeling of landing a perfect snipe is addictive and so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ worth it. This is the definition of BANG for your buck.

In all seriousness:

This game consists of a single player with a set of 80+ missions that act as a tutorial and challenges, and a multiplayer mode against AI (but no xp gain) and human players. While the singleplayer is a nice way to hone your skill, the meat of the game is clearly in multiplayer.

There are quite a few multiplayer modes, but the most popular seems to be deathmatch, though I feel like the Assassination mode could be interesting as well. Players vary in skill level, which affects how many types of guns are available to them, and might affect their passive tank bonus. If you want to steer clear of the disadvantage of playing against high level players, you can either host your own lobby with a low rank difference, or seek out lobbies with similarly ranked players. You gain xp and level up regardless pf whether you win or lose (though more xp goes to the winner of course). I haven't had any problems with the multiplayer so far.

The game has a very satisfying sound and aesthetic. Guns appear in your roster with a flashy animation, and the presentation is very sleek overall. The only problem I have with the UI is the inability to click through the level-up or xp animations. These just take way too long, and I often have to click Ready while within the animation. This isn't hard, guys. Come on.

CONCLUSION: Costs as much as a sub. Provides endless hours of entertainment. Grab it now if you're a Worms fan, grab it anyway even if you're not.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game itself is great. Even when you lose you get XP and feel that you "progress" and become stronger.

The autobalance in matchmaking seems to be solely based on Rating, not Account level, but Account level is a much more significant predictor for the strength of a player. Higher level = more Armor, more criticals, more movement, MUCH better weapons. If you have a high rating as a low level player you will not have fun in 4v4 matches and when you have a low rating with a high level you will freewin 4v4 games.

The aftergame screen is very lackluster, all the interesting information (total damage done, Damage per turn, turns survived, maybe MVP, most successful weapon/hit) are non-existant though very easy to implement. On the bright side there are frequent updates, I jsut wish they would be more meaningful.

Overall a good game with many possible improvements in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
