When I first entered it, I thought it would be similar to a Tanks game I had played previously. I was mistaken. Both games did share some things though:
- Tanks
- Different Weapons
- Different Terrains
- Shooting Mechanic

Differences:
- Shellshock had TOO MANY weapons to keep track of
- Customizability
- HUD
- Colors and Design
- Upgradable features

My original intent was to have a game that was fun and could be played with friends, but I bought it at a rough time and ended up refunding it. It was also very hard for me to figure out how to do anything in the game, like switch weapons, choose a game mode, and even "Why can one customize their tanks?" (I didn't really say that).

Would I buy it again? If I had the money.\t\t\t\t\t

Pros:
- Cool retro, plastic like graphics
- Huge arsenal of unique weapons
- Semi-large community
- Level progression and a game store without microtransactions

Cons:
- Most of the players in the community are toxic, xp farming degererates
- Limited plain levels
- Not much to do once you've reached max level other than wage your own gamestore credits

Overall, the game is okay. I don't have many hours and I don't plan to put in anymore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tControls/Mechanics: Ok, The controls are decent, the physics are very bizarre and you really cant get use to them which the game is fully based on. There are over 300 weapons in the game and a large majority of them are not good weapons, a lot of these weapons do not have good damage, you cannot truly aim them and its luck if they hit the target. You cannot choose what weapons you would like to use and its luck if you get decent weapons to fight enemies. 6/10


Sounds/Music: Ok, The music is nothing special and is quite boring after a time of listening to limited tracks that are not behind a in game pay wall. 6/10


Graphics: Ok, Simple graphics that most computers can run which is nice but nothing special and boring to look at after a time. 6/10


Difficultly: Bad, There is a leveling system from 1-100 and can be very unfair for the reasons being that higher leveled players have better weapons then lower level players, they also get more perk tokens which farther increases the difficultly fighting against higher leveled players. These tokens can give them extra armor, more random critical damage, extra items, fuel to move a larger distance, etc. 4/10


Replay Value: Ok, The community in games is what brings people into the game but a decent amount of the community here is not to friendly and you will not have fun unless you have friends to play with. 6/10

Originality: Ok, There have been games in the past that have done the same style of game and have done it better. 6/10


Enjoyment: Ok, Like what was previously said, you will have a tough time enjoying the game without friends to play with because of the unfriendly community and the large issues with the game. 6/10


Total: 40/70, 4/7, or 57%\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI WROTE THIS REVIEW ORIGINALLY ON MARCH 8TH, 2017. I DELETED THAT ONE AND REUPLOADED IT ON 10/22/2019 SO IT COULD RESURFACE.

I've played this game a bit more than when I originally made this review. After this review was made, many of the things from my "Cons" section were patched. I will now edit this review to make it more suited towards the current version of ShellShock Live. Any edits I've made will be surrounded by parentheses.

So I've played this game for a while, as you can tell. Maybe not enough to make me an expert, but I can say this:

Pros of ShellShock:
Multiplayer:
Not very toxic community (This still holds true even as of 10/22/2019)
Fun to play with friends (My friends don't play this with me anymore, but I know it'd still be fun regardless)
Single Player:
You get cool weapons and experience, i guess (You can also grind for gears, although it takes a very long time to accumulate a few)

Cons of ShellShock:
Multiplayer:
Called a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ if you kill people with an Earthquake, Dead Weight, Dead Riser, or any of the three Bounder types. Basically any weapon that is a guaranteed hit. (I haven't seen this sort of toxicity in the game in a while. Sure, it is very possibly still there, but it has considerably shrunken since this was originally posted)
(Something I want to mention is that the toxicity is very much still there if you use items. I don't know why, since they are a part of the game, but oh well)

Single Player:
Where the hell do i begin?
Enemy AI that can attack you during the campaign, no matter what level it says they are, they have complete access to every weapon in the game at its highest tier. So basically, you get ♥♥♥♥ed by each and every AI in the campaign. (This has been changed for a while now. Each tank's weapons are limited to what their respective level has access to.)
Any sort of aiming or shot accuracy game in the campaign will be damn near impossible. (I didn't see this part of the review. I was just bad at the game lol)

So basically, unless you don't want to know what it feels like to want to kill yourself, go play the campaign.

(A New Section) Updates:
Despite ShellShock's bio claiming that there are updates daily, that is a lie. Updates used to be quite frequent, maybe once every other week. Now, you're lucky if you get one once a month. Or even every *other* month.
The updates are mediocre at best. Sure, the launch into the PS4 market was good, but what about actual content for the game? The last thing you've added that was prominent was 2 new weapons.

I know. Its an old game now. The developers have probably moved on, or have almost moved on, from this game. But it still needs love and attention, much like every other game out there. There is still so much potential that could be had with this game, from new weapons, maps, gamemodes, or even new Single Player campaign stuff.

The game is still good and holds up well, and is still fun to play. (My example being my play time in this game). I really hope that the devs add further more content into this game. Its good, but it can be better.

Thank you for reading my edited ShellShock Live review. As of 10/22/2019, I still recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

Unlocking new weapons and other bits doesn't feel like a grind at all. You earn them by leveling up, using other weapons until they get better, or by sheer luck of the draw. The in-game store has a bunch of different addons you can buy for your tank, but THERE ARE ABSOLUTELY NO IN-GAME MICRO-TRANSACTIONS. Period. Everything you can buy in the in-game store is bought using "Gears" that you earn by actually playing the game. In an age where aggressive micro-transactions are seen as a legit business model, it's refreshing to see a game that does things right.\t\t\t\t\t
