\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust came back to playing this game after a good 6 month break. Decided to make a review.

TL;DR Summary: Fun game but progression is super unbalanced, almost as if the devs intended for a pay-to-progress type system then never did.

Pros:
  • Too many weapons to list, and they all have at least one upgraded version.

Cons:
There are a lot of these, because I like the game enough to see where there could be improvements. I hope the developer(s) will spend the time to read and consider them.
  • Progression is fun but flawed. People make lobbies for farming XP but even that takes a long time to level up and is really boring. (Currently writing this while farming)
  • There are some (But not all!) weapons that are inherently stronger than others, and you can only get access to them by leveling up or buying them.
  • A gear has a chance to drop when someone takes damage. You don't automatically get said gear however. You have to hover over it with your mouse. These gears are effected by explosion forces and can go offscreen before you can collect them. They also fade away after a few seconds. If you're like me and are doing other things in between turns, all it does is make you feel like ♥♥♥♥. In my opinion this really does not fit the turn based gameplay.
  • Everything that is purchasable costs at minimum 70 gears, with most things worth buying cost around 200. Things cost too damn much! I've only ever bought two items with my ~24hrs gameplay.
  • You do not gain gears/xp from a game if you leave. Even if you are dead! This is one of the most frustrating things possible about progression. I can understand if you leave a game prematurely, but if you are dead there is no coming back. Zero reason to force you to wait.

Annoyances:
Little things that would be very easy for the developer to fix.
  • When trying to find a game to join, you frequently get errors joining because the list of lobbies doesn't update unless you tell it to.
  • Having chat open blocks your ability to use arrow keys to aim. This does not close after sending a message.

1. Community:

Before I had this game, i was looking for reviews to guide my opinion. I thought it looks pretty cool, but I saw many reviews that talked about the poisonous community in the game. I was afraid to dive into it, for I feared people would be constantly cursing at each other, calling others cheaters, and so on. When i get into the first match, those people were very nice. They gave me a warm welcome to the game, and even some tips on how to shoot. As a progressed, people still remained kind, fun, and encouraging, except for a few who I believed got a bit frustrated at the game. Very good community; I suggests interacting more with those people :)


2. Graphics, features, etc: I watched several (many) videos about SSL before I got it; I felt like I needed more guidelines as to whether I should get the game or not. In my opinion, the graphics are amazing 10/10 neon colors. I am also the type of guy that gets bored really quickly (within 25 minutes maybe?) of something. This game featured amazing features for such a low price. I love the upgrade, customization, and shop system in this game. In addition to all of these amazing "upgrade" features, my feeling towards this game is that the devs only create this for money partly. The other fraction is for pure entertainment for others. One extra thing to mention is the extra AI campaign and skirmishes, where if the community really doesn't like you, you still have an option to enjoy the game.


3. Yes, this is getting long, so here's my last point: Based on my opinion, this game is perfect for me. I like tanks. I like ships. I like planes. This has all of those, or at least things relating to each of them. I should re-state the fact that this game offers bright, colorful neon graphics! :)) Finally, just to show how 11/10 this game actually is, it is the first ever game (not just on Steam) that I've bought for actual money. For a person that is mostly used to World-War type game scenarios and such, this game really does entertain me when I have free time on my hands.


CONCLUSION, IN CASE YOU DON'T LIKE READING: 15/10 good game, worth the money, at least >20 hours of gameplay if you don't purposely choose wrong :D\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt would be nice if there was a 'sort of' recommendation option. Because No is pretty harsh for this, but a Yes it just too dishonest for me to say. This is definitely a 'if you want to, but I probably wouldn't pay for it again' sort of recommendation.

Cons:
This is not Scorched Earth. It looks sort of like it and follows many of the physics based game mechanics, but the heart of Scorched Earth is not here. If you don't care one way or the other, then by all means, buy the game. If you are like me and were under the hope and impression it would be a Scorched Earth of yester year, know that it isn't.

AI is terrible. That is the singular reason I have no desire to play this game any more. As other people have commented, they are way overpowered and really sucks any fun out of the game.

Campaign is terrible. Don't even bother to keep going past the first couple levels (mainly due to how terrible the AI is).

Pros:
Looks pretty. The graphics are very nice.

Multiplayer looks like it could be fun, but I only tried it once. And it was an ok experience. Other people seem to enjoy it.

There are a lot of upgrades. A lot. Some don't seem to do anything though (but again, I spent most of my time in the Campaign, which may not utilize the tank upgrades...I'm not entirely sure I understood when upgrades were applied, if only for multiplayer or not).

A lot of weapons. Which also might be a con, but I could just be saying that because I'm still wanting to compare it to Scorched Earth, which did not have this many.

The developers seem really open to the community and are committed to improving this game, which is great.

Overall: Yeah, if you have some extra money and don't really mind that it is not Scorched Earth and have friends to play multiplayer with, go ahead and get it. You will probably enjoy it. If, however, you have other things to do or have any reservations about it or have no friends to play with and can only do single player, I'd just wait until they patch the AI or something. Actually I'm not even sure patching the AI would bring me back to the game right now.\t\t\t\t\t
