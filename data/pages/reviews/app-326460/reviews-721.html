\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNOTE: THIS IS A GAME THAT I DO RECOMMEND, BUT I HAVE TO GIVE IT A NEGATIVE REVIEW DUE TO THE DECLINING QUALITY WITH EACH SUCCESSIVE UPDATE.

Having accumulated more hours in this game, to the point where it is now my most played game in my entire Steam library, I feel like I can give a more informed opinion (and thus, an updated review).

I bought this game in the Summer of 2016, it was a fun experience that was well worth the £2.49 it cost me (at sale price) but having played the game for so long I've come accross a number of things that are simply unbearable.

The community, if you can even call it that, is worse than some of the bile I've seen in CS:GO. You'll be playing with whiney high level players who have sunk far too many hours into this who will be easily annoyed either when they're beaten at "their game" or when you decided to have fun. Oh fun? Apparently the game is only fun when you win... I was of the opinion that the two were mutually exclusive, but what do I know? If you try to converse with any other players then one of the following will happen: you'll have a pleasant conversation, they'll have a go at you for being annoying, or alternatively they'll turn into some rabid nuisance that plagues the chat with pointless nonsense. Oh and if you try to explain something logical, the host will kick you (because yes, this game is played by people who apparently have some weird sense of pride and superiority).

As for the actual game itself, it still plays fine... most of the time. Sometimes the game just completely crashes, sometimes your turn is skipped, sometimes the game works against you due to some interesting map generation that puts you at a grave disadvantage, sometimes after you move your tank it will (for no apparent reason) lag back to your starting point. The game honestly feels like it gets more buggy as the updates continue, how is that even possible?! (Oh and as a side note, the damage radius is inconsistent.)

On one occasion the game decided to kill me with my own weapon. Interestingly enough I had killed myself before pressing the 'FIRE' button... and with a weapon I hadn't even unlocked... (arguably the most annoying bug I've come across, though it's only occured once fortunately).

It's a shame, really it is. Because at the end of the day this is a fun game, but the ridiculously over-competitive nature and unpleasant behaviour of certain players ruins the experience. I'll still recommend this game... but with this added note: be wary, if you have fun, they will hate you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShellShock Live: An amazing online tank-battle game that entertains and is generally fun to play.

What is this game? It is a game where your goal is to kill tanks in either multiplayer or singleplayer battles. You can achieve this in multiple ways, with the use of weapons, power-ups, and more. This requires more strategy than anything, as the battles are turn based, like those you find in some RPG games.

Pros:
+Multiplayer is well done, haven't encountered many bugs but since this is early access I know they'll get them fixed soon.

+Multiple game modes provide different forms of intense battles.

+The weapons in this game are probably the best part. The sheer variety of attacks you can pull off in this game are what keep this game fresh. And they get even more crazy as you use them more often.

Netrual:
=Experience system seems balanced. It does take a few games to level up, but when playing a long session of SSL the time (and levels) will pass by fast.

=Except for possibly the "Team" gamemodes, there isn't much of a co-op option. This doesn't really matter as the competitiveness of the main game doesn't get very stale.

Cons:
-Singleplayer is a bit sub-par. It's nice that it's there, but the AI tanks are a bit too powerful for true enjoyment. (come on! top tier weapons at level ~30?)

Final Opinion: Overall, check this game out if you like destructive, awesome, skill-based online battles!

9 of 10

thank you kChamp, an "online" game with no microtransactions!\t\t\t\t\t
