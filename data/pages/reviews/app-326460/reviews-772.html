\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI decided to try this game out, despite seeing numerous people complaining about it, and turns out I made the correct decision. A lot of thing must've changed, since most of those complaints seems not to apply anymore.

-The game is NOT multiplayer only. There are a LOT of single player missions/challenges that allow you to level up very fast, and unlock stuff quickly. You can also practice versus bots if you wish. I got to level 16 by playing an hour of SP, then trying out MP for the next hour. That got me enough weapons to have a very nice varriety in my loudout, and a couple of tank upgrade points for some minor bonuses.
-The progression system doesn't feel like it's making you so overpowered you can't live without it, and there are also a lot of ways to make it less relevant. Sure, you won't ever win vs a lv 60 guy with your 5 level tank, but you'd probably lose anyway since he'd be way better as well. I got no problem playing vs level 20,30 players with my 15 level account, I still had tons of fun, and didn't feel like i'm at a disadvantage. Plus there are game modes that eliminate the disadventage of not having that many weapons unlocked (random weapon each turn), or not being high level (FFA- pure chaos and fun).
-There is a decent number of people playing the game, I was able to find people on my level (+-5) in less than a minute, sometimes I even got into a very good lobby instantly, thanks to quick join function.
-This is not supposed to be played as a 1v1 game. If that's what you're after, forget about it. it's way too much random for a 1v1 game: you can get worse weapons, you can get worse starting position, you can get blocked by some random shield spawn on your trun etc. This is a game about diving into 3v3, 4v4 or FFA games and having a total blast blowing stuff up!


My advice: play some single player missions first, get the understanding of how shooting and weapons work, get some levels that way, and then jump into MP. I guarantee you'll have a whale of a time that way!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, it is a great game. It entertains you for a pretty decent amount of time with the overload of explosions; but there may be a few annoying issues.

Due to the small player base, it's inevitable to be matched up with players that are 30-70 levels higher than you. Even if they have the most experience, these high level tanks get perks such as high armour, damage and other nuclear weapons.

The obstacles/buffs are too random. This destroys the skill part in a strategy game like Shellshock Live. For instance, x2 damage buffs sometimes favors one side and obstacles that deflect projectiles mostly block lucky players from getting damage.

Buy the game if you don't mind RNG and unfair battles.\t\t\t\t\t
