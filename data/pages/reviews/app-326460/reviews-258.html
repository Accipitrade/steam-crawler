A great 2d artillery strategy game!

This game is your basic 2D shooter game, similar to the Worms series, or if you are sufficiently retro, QBASIC Gorillas. It's your usual "choose the angle and velocity" style of gameplay and try to kill your opponent before they kill you. That's the basic jist of the game, but they've wrapped in a ton of different game modes and weapons to make things interesting, fun, and replayable.

The basic game mode is Deathmatch, which is the usual "make your opponent's HP go down to zero before they do." But there are a ton of different mode types, such as Points (get the highest score after so many rounds) or Assassin (you are forced to target a specific player each round). There are also modes that force you to make skill shots, such as Rebound (where only hits that use a bumper count), and Vortex (where only shots that use portals count). Each game mode keeps the gameplay fresh and interesting.

There's other gameplay settings that keep things fun as well, such as allowing for teams, selecting wind, and limiting your use of weapons. Settings can make the game easier or harder, and change the XP you get per round. You can also select a ton of different maps which can range from being super easy to the most annoying map in existence. I usually play with maps on random (so you get a different one each round), and that works out well for gameplay (most of them, anyway).

There are a ton of weapons you can earn by playing the game. As you gain XP and level up, you gain more weapons. Each weapon also has its own XP, which you can then use to level up weapons to gain more powerful versions. As you gain levels, you also gain tank upgrades and visual upgrades as well that can give your tank a bit of flair. This of course means that the higher your level, you are considerably more OP than lower levels, but there are game modes that can equalize things if you want to play with someone that's a lower level.

Visuals and sounds are very attractive, and are very much based on florescent neon colors. Everything is bright and easy to look at. In addition, the variety of weapons adds a lot of flavor to the game. From festive "fiesta" weapons that play a song as they attack your opponent to a Portal Gun weapon with an obvious homage to Portal, there's no shortage of fun sounds and graphics to keep the game fun, but not get in the way of gameplay.

Finally, there is a premium currency called gears that allow you to buy special upgrades, such as victory songs, more visual upgrades, and special weapons. Unlike most games, you cannot purchase gears for real world money, meaning that gears are purely gained by gameplay. You can spin for free gears after your first victory of the day, and gears will occasionally pop up after a successful attack that you can grab by mousing over the gear. This adds some extra gameplay elements, gives you an incentive to play more, and keeps the community free of "pay to win" mechanics.

I definitely give this game a recommend, as it is inexpensive, has zero microtransactions, and is just plain good gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
