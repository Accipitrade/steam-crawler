\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite being in early access during the time of this review, ShellShock Live is really fun. This is a tank game focused on multiplayer but there are a good number of single player levels that emphasize unique weapons and help you hone some of the more nuanced part of the game down. There's also a firing range for practice, which i found really nice.

The game is focused entirely around a core gameplay similar to bowman games. Your goal largely is to use the angle and power of your cannon to guess where your opponents are. You start the game with a load of weapons that get better based on how much you use them, whenever you fire a shot it dissapears and you're constantly forced to re-evaluate your turn based on the weapons you have available. Combine this with the fact that everyone maxes movement abilities first and you have a game full of calculations and decisions, and every minor mistake has huge implications for the game. The competitive feel fo the game can sometimes be ruined by starting the game with really bad weapons (RNG WHY), but overall i think the core multiplayer is really fun.

A game like this does tend to get really boring if the experience is the same from levels 1 to 100, but the upgradable weapons plus new weapons on level ups means you have a constantly changing experience, especially if you go online and play with people only around your level. I did find that once you get to the level 50s and 60s it becomes really hard to find an even game, but i suspect when the game fully releases it'll see a huge boost in popularity and i think the game is more than ready for that point.

It has a solid core, and it built a system to support that core while keeping the game simple, hope other indiemultiplayer games *cough teeworld *cough take notice.\t\t\t\t\t
