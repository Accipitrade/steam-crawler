\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHighly recommand if you're looking for a party game / something to boot for a few minutes up to one hour a day. Especially if you intend to play with friends on the regular. I wanna underline the PARTY game aspect because its NOT a balanced game by design. Not only because you unlock (better) weapons as you level, but also because you dont choose your loadout, its picked at random within the pool of everything you unlocked.

It's also a decent game to dive in for a while, as its mechanics are simple to learn but hard to master. A noob can miss a nuke, a pro can land a sniper (1 pixel big bullet) despite wind, vortexes, bumpers and portals changing your shots trajectory.

However - the more you try to master the game, the more aggravating it becomes. You have to keep practicing to remember how each weapon works. Levels become exponentially longer to grind - to the point there's barely a few thousands players that reached maxed level (100), despite this game having almost a million of copies sold. And the higher level you are, the harder it becomes to find a lobby to play in as people fear the OP weapon you might roll

I still overall recommand it. Unless you're the type to compulsively 100% the games you own. I'm 900 hours in as I'm writing this, and it'd take me probably 300 more to get there.
Also don't forget to go in options > turn victory music volume to 0%. These jingles get irritating reaaal quick.\t\t\t\t\t
