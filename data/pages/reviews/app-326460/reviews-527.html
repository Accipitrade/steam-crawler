\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who played alot of the flash game Tanks online when I was younger, Shellshock holds onto alot of what made the flash game great but improved many visuals and expanded weapons to make the game more exciting. My pros and cons will be mostly based on comparisons between Shellshock and Tanks online.

Pros:
-Varied and interesting weapons.
-Easy to find or host a game, no long queues.
-Friendly and helpful player base.
-Consistent levelling and unlockables.
-A high level player can be beaten by a new player.
-Skill-based mostly, not pay to win.
-Addictive.

Cons:
-Can't break through the bottom of the map with weapons (this makes it fairer but imo less fun)
-Wind could be visually improved so that there are clouds/leaves/birds to indicate speed and direction instead of a tiny indicator.
-Some weapons are counter-intuitive and take some practise to learn. (not really a con but frustrating when you blow yourself up in an online game because your shot goes up, then through the ground then comes back and somehow hits you)

Overall a fun, fairly simple, friendly game.
