\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm sure you know the feeling that you get when you pay money for a game and after playing it you feel as satisfied as if you have done one of the best purchases ever. I'm not even sure why this game is in Early Access. I cannot possibly imagine how cooler they can make it. Can you devs?

Now, I've played a game called Artillery more than 20 years ago on a 8-bit computer. Believe or not the magic is still here in ShellShock Live. The biggest difference is that my buddy was next to me and we were able to shout at, mock, laugh at, annoy ourselves in our faces while doing another round and here you're doing the same at the computer screen (unless probably you're talking with your buddy over the wire). The common thing is that it was and is a lot of fun.

Other differences are:
* The colors (Artillery was green on a black background ;). The game is very colorful as seen in the screenshots and it's super cool at the same time. Nothing negative here in my opinion and I don't like colorful graphics. There are some effects here that I guess make it work.
* The sound is great. Music could be better but still it's pretty good.
* There are LOTS of weapons. LOTS of weapons. LOTS. Each base weapon you get has one, two or up to 4 levels. The best part? You upgrade them by ... using them and scoring damage with them. That's a pretty solid progression mechanic if you ask me. Sure, some weapons are better than others and they can be balanced further but it doesn't matter in the long run. This is what I believe is the luck factor in this game because you don't choose with which weapons to play. A set of your current loadout is rng-chosen at the start of each round and at certain points in the game.
* There are tank upgrades. Each level you get gives you a point for upgrade. This mechanic is nice because it allows for a build but I guess (haven't done the math yet) you can level up everything by the max level. Still, until then you have to play LOTS of games and during that time your build is quite important. This also allows for very diverse and interesting games because no two builds are the same.
* There is a shop where you can buy stuff with tokens. Still here? Oh, there are no microtransactions, you must get those tokens by making your enemies blossom in awesome explosions. Also there's a daily reward roulette that gives you tokens. Unfortunately I fear that the devs are experimenting before introducing in-game purchases one day. If they don't do it, then this will remain an awesome incentive to make you play more because all those items are purely cosmetic but very awesome. Also when you see someone with them you know they are badass.
* There are single player missions. Lots of them by the way and they are very hard. They put your skills to the test and make you learn new stuff. A great addition to a great multiplayer game.
* Multiplayer is top-notch. There are some problems here and probably that's the only area I've had most issues with, all concerned with connectivity. You get into a game immediately but too often it has either started or been filled up to the max. There is a superb chat system that functions as normal chat in-game and in the lobby but in-game each message pops up above tanks. It's super cute, I love it. It's as if the tanks are talking among themselves, it's super fun.
* Last but not least, and this is the most important aspect of the game to me, is the amazing community. Seriously I rarely enjoy such a positive and generally playful community. I've been in many toxic ones and this one ain't like this. Sure, there are exceptions here and there but usually everyone gets along with each other nicely. Once my teammate started dissing on me at the start of the round because I missed my first shots while our enemies hit us hard, then he/she quit but until then I had scored more damage than him/her. My enemies were suprised and were very friendly pointing out the irony in my teammates attitude. Wow.
* The game is lots of fun and it is a skill-based game, make no mistake. You'll suck in the beginning unless your curve geometry is top-notch.
* Oh, there are game modes. I forgot about that mainly because I'm playing only deathmatch. There are 6 or 7 off the top of my head. Look them up if you need info. One is extremely hard, putting your skills to their ultimate test - Rebound. The rest are quite different than each other and the gameplay in each one is different.
* Oh, how could I forget? There are teams, yes. You can play 1v1, 2v2, 3v3 and 4v4. How amazing is that?

Honestly there's not a single major con in this game for me. I think I've layed it all out but if you're still making up your mind, first ask yourself whether this kind of gameplay is for you and then watch a few videos (gampelya or/and reviews) to get the hang of its visual style and mechanics.

One of the best multiplayer games that deliver lots of fun and skill-based progression.

Please devs, only make the game better. Community, stay awesome!

9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
