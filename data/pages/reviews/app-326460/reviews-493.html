\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow I'll preface with the fact that the many hours I've played this it isn't the game itself I'm giving a negative review for, it's the playerbase and the servers that makes this game so unfun to play.

Since this is a negative review I'll begin with why I'm giving it such a review:

- 90% of the games people make are 'XP farms' which involve all the players moving to the centre of the map and shooting themselves in one big group, and the rest are '4.08x XP' Rebound games that take almost 40 minutes to finish if you're lucky. Any attempt I make to make other games such as Juggernaut or the other 4 unexplored game modes results in literally nobody joining, thus making no games possible.

- Since there's no punishment for leaving other than losing the XP you would've gained in that battle, people will simply leave early if they think they aren't going to win, giving the other team an advantage and the rest of the team a much more difficult fight.

- For whatever reason, the servers have a tendency to randomly disconnect you from games, simply removing you from them for literally no reason. You can't control it, it just happens whenever it wants to. I can't have fun in actually good battles because I'm constantly afraid of just being booted from them, like I was on the last one before I got DC'd, and before that the entire enemy team left.

Better put, the game would be brilliant if it had an actually functioning playerbase and servers.

NOW, the reason I've put so many hours into this despite this is how many problems I have with the playerbase is how fantastically designed the game is.

- The visuals are brilliant. The tanks, land and sky are simple and smooth, and everything is easy to tell apart. The tank accessories are stylish, the UIs are clean, and especially the weapons' effects are stellar. It's clear a lot of work went into the weapons, as I'll be about to explain.

- The titanic array of weapons makes earning weapons and playing with them such a fun experience. The game has easily 200+ weapons that can be earned in all kinds of ways: via achievements, the gear shop, levelling up, challenges. There's so many weapons and so many ways to get them that it almost feels neverending, and that's a great thing in a game like this. The weapons are all so different too that you almost feel like a god when you learn what they do, and learn how to use them. They all have different kinds of uses too, so you'll rarely be out of options. Some are risk-reward-based, some are purely for utility, and some are amazing when you know how they work.

- The single player challenges are somewhat fun, but some of them aren't very fun, especially the AI-based ones since making challenging yet fun AIs is always a challenge. Of course, you get some nice weapons from them, so it's always worth doing just to expand your arsenal.

- The selection of 8 creatively-made gamemodes makes it difficult to be bored in this game, even if no one plays them. I always like setting up Juggernaut games because I like the idea of everyone taking down a superpowered player.

All in all, I think ShellShock is a GREAT game, but it's really the half-brained playerbase and the bad servers that make it hard to enjoy, even with everything that I like about it. Now, if you were playing this with a group of friends in a lobby together this would be perfect, but that's really the only circumstance I can think of that someone could legitimately enjoy this game now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTruly wonderful game. Interesting gamemodes make for decent substitute if regular deathmatch gets stale. Great use of mathematics put into using power and angle, with the addition of wind makes it a mind powering stimulation. A few issues with lag and not enough people online, having only a couple games open, but the ability to create a new game fixes this issue. With a new weapon and upgrade each level keeps the game interesting as you can also level up each weapon with their own xp. The leveling up system set up perfectly so that you need more xp the higher you achieve, letting other players know whos a pro compared to new players. Interactive costomationable options weather it be in the game options, map choice or simply a victory tune. It's hard to remember that this game is still early access, but that just makes it all the better, as there is still more to add(hopefully). There is so much flexibility in the gameplay that a game can turn from a loss to a win just with a carefully spawned map object, may it be a health box or a 2x damage. Also with your artillery of items to use at wilm, you can gain advantages against the other players. There is a current problem with players leaving the round mid-game, thus making it incredibly unfair. The skill rating doesn't effect the gameplay but rather informing players of your estimated skill. An allround wonderful game, no regrets, decently priced, average time of a match is reasonable so that you could play a quick round or go on for hours. Highly underrated for the physics put into the whole experience. I overwhelmingly recommend this if you enjoy turn based calculated skill games. I hope you enjoy it as much as I do.\t\t\t\t\t
