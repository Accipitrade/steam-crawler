\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a really well done turn based game that you can sink a lot of time into, but even if everything else was perfect there's a big flaw in the core of the game play. If you've played worms then you know how this works, you have access to a bunch of weapons that can do any number of crazy or really unique things. The problem here being that in order to unlock all of the weapons in this game you have to level up, which starts out really fast but eventually slows to an unbelievable grind as you get further. On top of that every weapon has it's own leveling system where the more damage you deal it'll upgrade two or three times getting stronger versions, which can give a greater advantage over using more powerful higher level weapons. There's also tank upgrades that you get each level with the most important two being how much armor you get at the start of each game, and how far you can move in each turn. I convinced a few friends to get this game at some point after I'd already played a lot and it was great for a bit, but they didn't have access to anywhere close to the upgrades and fun weapons I did and lost interest.

I understand the desire to have some mechanic in the game to have repeated players coming back but in this case it can also easily be off putting, as it was for them. Even for me with over three hundred hours in this game I'm seriously not even close to the cap, being at 93 of the 100 levels. Previously when I first bought this they didn't have a weapon for each level, they've since consistently updated it and I believe they've now got something for every single one. So there's some incentive to it, and even booting it up now to check my level they recently updated the game two weeks ago in June adding a lot more stuff which is truly promising. The game itself is honestly really good and I like almost everything else about it. But as this isn't a free to play game, if you're looking to just buy this and hop in, you should be well aware that the majority of its unlocks are gated behind extensive playtime.

They do have an option that does allow everyone to play on an even playing field, dropping all players to the same level and using the weapons of the lowest person in that lobby. However there's no opposite to this feature that would've allowed my friends to be equal to my level and use all the epic weapons I've already unlocked. The simplest solution would be to add such a feature to the game, but that doesn't eliminate the problem for any new groups of players either. Since they've settled on this system I'd suggest a game type or a mode that rewards no EXP at all, where everyone would have full tank upgrades and weapon unlocks without having to spend literally over hundreds of hours, that could exist purely for fun. Although I don't see them ever doing something like this as I figure the devoted players would be outraged at the sudden lack of exclusivity to the weapons they already grinded for, despite everyone having equally payed for the same game.

TLDR: As good as this game is, if you don't put in the time which does total over hundreds of hours, there WILL be content/weapons in this game you will never be able to unlock. So if that's not what you're looking for, you might prefer a game like worms instead.\t\t\t\t\t
