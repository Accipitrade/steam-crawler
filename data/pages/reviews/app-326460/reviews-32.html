\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love ShellShock, it's a game I have some pretty fond memories of with some very cool trickshots. However, the community is not one of the strongest selling points. I've noticed a large portion of toxic people while playing, including but not limited to: high level players only joining lower level lobbies to get easy wins, people complaining when you miss any shot, being kicked from lobbies for being too close to a person's level, and more. If you can find a group of friends to play this with, solid game. Otherwise, it might not be as fun.

While the game requires skill and luck, I think it actually has a good bit of balance between the two. You'll receive a random assortment of weapons every 10 turns and whether they hit or not is based off of your understanding of the weapon and how well you can aim. Wind increases the skill required, though you may be hard pressed to find a game that uses wind that isn't just exp farming with rebound mode.

Overall, a solid game, just make sure to not let the community get to you sometimes\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's nice artillery game with many cool, interesting and whacky weapons.
Unlike it's ancestor "Scorched Earth", it has RPG elements... you gain experience from battling other tanks and this can be invested in a sort of skill tree that affects how far you can drive and up (or down) how steep hills, the luck you have when collecting weapon crates, your critical hit chance and multiplier and your armour.

Levelling up your tank also grants new weapons and using a weapon gains you experience with this weapon which will sooner or later upgrade that weapon to a new, stronger version.

There are also many interesting game modes, such as rebound, where each shot first has to hit a bumper before it can damage opponents, traditional deathmatch, assassination (where you have a specific target instead of just killing everyone) and many more.

The only thing I don't like about the game is that you often have to wait. When you create a new game, you'll have to wait for enough players to join, since you can't add an AI if not all player slots are filled. During a round, if you don't set shots to simultaneous, you'll have to wait until everyone made their shot, which can take a while in an 8 player round. However, simultaneous shots make the screen very cluttered and so it's hard to plan.\t\t\t\t\t
