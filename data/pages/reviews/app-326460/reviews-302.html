\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShellShock Live is a fun way to pass the time, and reminds me of worms with a different aesthetic. The maps are destructible, which is entertaining and can be used to your advantage. The art design is clean and satisfying, if a little simple. As you play you get access to different skins and weapons, which are fun to test out in-game. The sound design is simple, nothing more than what is needed is given. You get the booms and the bangs and a background score, and that's all you need. Gameplay is easy to grasp, and makes you think about trajectory and land destruction. It's a pretty basic formula that keeps itself engaging via the offering of certain goals in the form of skins and weaponry. Not to mention it's also online multiplayer, so you are given an extra dimension to prove yourself to your opponent. Overall there's only so much to ShellShock, and in the end that's ok. For the reasons discussed above, ShellShock Live gets a 6.5/10 from me.\t\t\t\t\t
