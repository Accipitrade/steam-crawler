This was not the game I was waiting for.
[bi]I WANTED to like this game. After all the hours I've ploughed into Tank Wars, then Scorched Earth, then Worms bnack in the old days. After all the years I'd watched it grow through Early Access, waiting for the full-release before purchase, I WANTED this game to be top notch. Sadly it was not to be. It manages to look and sound like the games of old but it is very much a product of the modern era, the questionable parts of the modern era.[/b]

The Good
  • It certainly manages the look and feel of Classic Scorched Earth.
  • The physics are spot on.
  • Very Flashy
  • Enough Dakka to make an Ork giddy.
  • Active multi-player community

The Bad
  • Grind. Lots, and Lots, and Lots of GRIND.
  • The lowest level game you can find online is 24.
  • Trades Skill for Spectacle.
  • Less Variety than Scorched Earth.

The Disappointment
  • Did I mention the grind?
  • No control customization.
  • Scorched Earth & Tank Wars are both more enjoyable.

As I said. I really wanted to like this game, but I cannot in good conscience recommend it.
Don't get me wrong. It's not a bad game, it just fails to clear the bar set the games that came before it on every level but spectacle. There are fewer mods,. There is less focus on skill., and lest interesting match progression. The weapons also seem a little...meh-is. So to start let me say this to all potential buyers: NO. The game doesn't 'get better' What you see in the first skirmish you play or the first handful of tutorial missions is pretty much it. So you're not missing much if you refund. That said the game's main issue is the inescapable grind.

You see levels, for both your tank and your weapons, carry-over into multi-player. The result is that you can easily find yourself up against someone with significantly better weapons, and more hit points just because they are a higher level, and at this point a good chunk of the remaining community is pretty high-tier. I suppose thats why this game gets sold in a four-pack since it seems the only way to have even matches is to be part of a group just starting out. This kinda undermines the skill appeal of the game, which is unfortunately one of the main appeals of artillery games. In scorched earth and similar older games, you could, even with the piddliest weapon, score a 1-hit kill---if you had the aim. But here, no. You can nail nail someone 10 levels higher than you dead on and it will barely scratch the paint. Of course their leveled will more or less sodomize your tank in colourful and explosive ways with grazing shots.

So to sum up, you have to grind to level up your tank, and grind again to level up your weapons, also to unlock them, But the kicker is...

There is actually less healthy engagement and customization than the old Scorched Earth, and Tank Wars games it borrows so heavily from. Those games rewarded skillful shots and between rounds allowed you to purchase weapons with money earned from your destructive rampages, further rewarding skill. This game, not so much.

And then there are the technicallly issues...

Now. We;'re not talking crashes or game breaking bugs. No. We're talking about little things. The inability to rebind directions keys and most annoyingly, the in ability to turn off controller vibration. Seriously WTF?! And the vibration gets triggered by explosions, firing shots, getting hit by shots. WHat makes this worse is the fact that this is a problem thats been reported in the game for 2 years. This is what tipped the game froma weak recommend to a not recommend. That the devs could leave such things unaddressed for so long....just kills any optimism of things getting fixed in the future.. Maybe it'll get patched but I'm kinda doubting it from what I'm seeing,

Conclusion
Its still a decent game. It's well made, It's pretty . And it is a perfectly serviceable artillery game. Your enjoyment will basically depend on how comfortably you can fit yourself into the Skinner-Box the game sets out for you. But if you ask me... you're better off grabbing a copy of Scorched Earth, Tank Wars, or one of the WORMS games. This is where the let down comes. The devs went to admirable lengths to replicate the look and feel of those older games but did not take the time to bring over the things that made them fun and compelling. My inner cynic suspects that the devs deliberately aped the aesthetics of those older games to trigger a nostalgia fog that would hopefully hide the game's shortcomings

As is. I wound up refunding this game. but I will keep an eye on the updates to see how things Pan(zer) Out.\t\t\t\t\t
