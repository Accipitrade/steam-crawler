\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh man, the nostalgia feeling with an absolute modern twist to it.
I remember when I was a little kid back in the 90s, I would go to addictinggames to play the then in-browser variant of this game. It was fun then, especially with the mechanics and you having to figure out yourself how you're going to hit the enemy with what amount of power.
Now, there's ShellShock Live. I love it! It brings back the feeling of that game, but with SO MUCH MORE! Over 200 weapons to choose from, so many different maps, and so much customization and upgrade options. This game is a true successor to the legacy of the in-browser game Tanks!
I love all the different weapons you get to choose from...and I think my favorite one is the Cat. Meow!
Honestly, there's not much about this game that I cannot say anything bad about. The spawning of the deflector rings and the deflector barriers is a little wonky sometimes, but that's easily overlooked with the simple fact that you're having quite a bit of fun trying to figure out the right tragectory of your shot while also figuring out either how to avoid the deflectors, or use them to your advantage.
And it's thanks to a certain favorite YouTuber of mine that I even found this game. Were it not for them, I would have never found this beautiful nostalgic gem of a game.
Man, kChamp Games did a brilliant job recreating a childhood favorite of mine. Kudos to the kChamps!\t\t\t\t\t

You sank my battleshi-tank, my battle tank\t

So i've played a little, and i bought this because i saw a popular youtuber playing this and i used to play this as a kid. With that being said here are a few cons and pros.

Pros\t
  • Alot of different weapons(over 200 )
  • Easy to play with friends and host a game, or a tournament
  • Rewarding XP\t system.

Cons\t
  • There are too many weapons, sure it brings variety but there's alot of weapons
  • Takes a long time to level up weapons and it get's a bit grindy, there is a weapon farm but it takes time.
  • Not enough customisation.

Summary\t
I do realise it's an early access game and still in development, and some of this will be fixed, my hat's off to the developers for making a fantastic game and i can't wait to see what they will add. So with that said i give this game 7/10 .\t\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game reminds me heavily of those Tank Games I played when I was younger. You were a effectively immobile tank, took aim at a rival tank, shot and hoped it hit. If it didn't, the enemy repeated the action of trying to hit your tank before you hit them more.

This expands on those principles- There are Jetpacks, Shield and random map effects. Flashy weapons and such, too. It's pretty too! I like the simplistic artstyle. However, I disagree with the more "grindy" aspects of the game. Progression is SLOW. One weapon per level sounds good, right? Frankly, it takes an age to level up (I had it easy for myself, reaching level 6- used a grinding server. As your level gets higher the grind gets worse.), and I get a kill each round. There are also upgrades, that are bought by assigning levels to them. The categories are basically- luck, guns, armor and mobility. I like myself a good skill tree, but in the grand scheme of things I like everyone at a fair playing field. While it is possible to defeat someone stronger than you level wise, the weapons unlocked and upgraded give them greater flexibilty and firepower. While one may argue that these upgrades may not be too powerful- the Armor tree gives a single point of armor per level, and the Mobility tree gives a bit more fuel, these do stack up to be important- starting with 20 points more armor is a big deal.

I suppose one could argue that I am a impatient person, and that is a true thing. The other thing that annoys me is the Mac OS version. Being the one person who has a Mac in a sea of Windows users, I understand that developing for the minority isn't really a priority. It does exist, but there are frequent startup crashes that lead to an eventual refund. When it worked, it was pretty good.

FInal Conclusion:
+The game is fun.
+A solid expansion to the tank games I once played.
-Grinding needed to unlock new weapons and level them up
-Seriously, lots of grinding.
-A non equal playing field.
-Crashes on startup, or at least for me.
-200 weapons? More like 15 at the start.

Not recommended personally, though I'm sure you can enjoy it if you can ignore the weapons.
