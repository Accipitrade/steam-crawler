First, some background:
Played the shellshock live series since the first back in highschool. Made the switch from 2 to steam awhile back; I'm level 70ish in this installment (I dont really keep track anymore since level doesnt mean much after you've maxed out damage and traction...)

Take my advice, don't waste your money on this game. If you do buy this game, kChamp's gonna be all, "Well, I have your money. I really don't care what happens now." His coding skills are garbage when it comes to games outside of flash. The ONLY reason this game is still early acces after being out for well over a year now is that he simply doesn't care. He is inactive on the forums and any attempt at contacting him will result in you getting banned from the forums or blocked on steam. (I HEARD he was moving on to work on another game a few months ago, even though this game is still early access. Really shows how much time he put into this, eh?)

Why just recently I discovered that he's gone and censored all curse words period... But the option for the profanity filter is still there. The game crashes half the players on the servers regularly (as in, every couple of hours regularly); whether you're in a game or just sitting in the lobby, the game will crash you out to the previouse menu from wherever you were before. I've been in games where there'll be a small lagspike and then 3-4 players in the game will dc all at once. Sometimes I'm one of those players.

For the sake of hooking more suckerscustomers he occasionally releases new weapons. Some of them are half-decent, and some of them are blatant reskins of older weapons. For example, the bow&arrow weapon may as well be a sniper that does DoT instead of outright base damage (which doesn't really matter anyways given it sticks in you. It does slightly more damage at upgraded levels and takes slightly longer to fire than a sniper, but is still basically a sniper).

There is also the issue with players cheating by using either an aim program or a real-world ruler (yes, a ruler), making it virtually impossible for them to miss. Programs at least provide some evidence that the player is cheating, and the flaw with rulers is that most players only calculate the end point and not the path and arc the shot takes. Wind is also a problem for most ruler users but I've read posts on the forums by people who claim to use rulers and say it's just an extra step to figure in wind... All things considered though, the game isn't moderated enough(if at all) to actually catch and punish cheaters, so you may as well go play tf2.


Backing off from my ranting now, If you are a returning shellshock live player making the jump from an older version be warned, the firing and angle mechanics are different from older games. The same angles and power levels (save for the 45deg angle rule) no longer apply here. If you do buy this game I highly recommend going to the firing range or the singleplayer missions and getting a feel for the new mechanics. Also, there is no such link between accounts to transfer experience, etc. over from a previous game as with ssl1 to ssl2. There is also no buyable content like prestige or weapon packs in this game (via real-world money anyways; you can buy individual weapons, backgrounds, songs, etc. in the shop with Gears, which can be picked up in-game or won via the first win roulette)

1 YEAR LATER EDIT:
Most of the above is still true, for the most part. The game is still early access, kChump is still inactive (not as much as back then, but still impossible to contact), the seemingly random crashes have been mostly fixed (mostly...), few weapons are being released every now and again, some better than others, and yes, there are still unpunished cheaters present (you can usually tell by their ridiculously high skill ratings before a game starts. There's also a report function now, but it only serves to auto-mute players in the chat (which is configurable in settings) and does nothing to actually stop the players from playing/cheating more).

There've been some updates in the past regarding Twitch compatibility and tons of localization, but if you're just here to play the game like I am, this probably won't affect you.

I'm changing my review to positive because the game is actually fun to play now, provided you don't get screwed by the bad players (those who are new, those who think they're good but aren't, those who grief outright), and stay out of the rooms with cheaters (again, check their skill rates and auto-mutes if you've set that up).\t\t\t\t\t
