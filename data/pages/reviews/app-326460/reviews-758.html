\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is an amazing game but, and as it's intended to be, it's a 100% multiplayer focused game. If you intend to play solo and/or don't plan on accessing the random battle finder system, or create games with personal friends, you're going to have a bad time after you reach Mt. Olympus in the single player campaign.

I can hardly blame them for this, as I bought the game knowing this (it's right there in the description for Christ's sake) and still enjoyed the little time I got out of it. If you have any social anxiety like I do, or lack friends to play with, this is not for you (but you may enjoy the single player up to said above point). But if you enjoy a very solid battle game that has an enormous amount of weapons and skill-based gameplay to provide, and like playing with or against others, you'll love this.

Even still, I feel like I got my money's worth. If you're looking for something to kill time and even don't intend on playing the multiplayer, you should still be pleasantly surprised with the experience. 23 hours for me, but I'm not that great at it, so it could take you less to reach a point you cannot progress past without leveling up in multiplayer. But still. It may be worth picking up.

So that said, yes, I definitely recommend it. But be aware knowing that this is as multiplayer as the Jackbox Party Pack or similar out there. The single player campaigns are NOT an afterthought, however, and designed incredibly well, with a great amount of challenge to some of them.\t\t\t\t\t
