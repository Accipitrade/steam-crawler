\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood turn based strategy game to waste your time with. There is enough depth and variation to keep you interested in the game for hours. The campaign in the game is a little challenging and becomes an rng madness train at certain points but overall its a fun 2-5 hour experience depending on how good you are. The real gem in this game is the multiplayer which allows you to join or create custom games or tournaments where you and other players are able to face off in various fun game modes like juggarnuat, assassin, marksman, just to name a few. The game modes themselves are very interesting and different from one another but most of the time you are gonna find yourself sticking to regular team death match type modes. The real shining star is the shear amount of weapons that you can get in the game (eg. black hole, nuke, breaker madness, so on and so on). The weapons are also upgradable as well(eg. nuke turns into mega nuke) and there are easily over 100 weapons which each are VERY different. The only bad part about this game comes in the rng that sometimes makes it unbearable to play. A few examples of rng are as follows: You spawn with a random assortment of weapons(sometimes you get good stuff, other times not so much), random obstacles like black holes, bumbers, etc spawn randomly every turn(sometimes directly in front of your face making it literally impossible to shoot), and more. Usually the rng is balanced enough to make everything seem fair but there are gonna be times when you just want to uninstall the game or something like that. In all, I say that the game is worth a buy especially if you have the spare money or a couple of friends to play it with.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is the spiritual successor of a genre that has been largely ignored but has come back in a big way, capturing the old fans, drawing in new fans, and everyone in between. I am ChocolatierGames with Computerfiguur’s Network and today I will be taking a look at Shell Shock Live.

Cannon Fodder

Shell Shock Live is a 2d tank game, similar to the likes of Scorched Earth, or if we want to go even earlier… Gorillas. You play as a tank trying to destroy other tanks and sometimes targets. Simple right? Not exactly… SSL is chock full of customization, with items, new bullets, and when playing the multiplayer, various obstacles that appear on each turn. This variation ensures that no two games will be the same, and each fight will be a challenge. The first thing that grabbed me was after each level up you unlock a new weapon, and upon my first unlock it shows you the SHEET of weapons you can unlock, each with multiple stages of power. This was at first a bit overwhelming, but I soon came to look forward to each upgrade.

Whimsy

SSL doesn’t take itself too seriously, in the weapons department, I can launch my standard bullets, or I can launch cats, or I can launch bananas. This is a bit silly at first but then falls a bit flat, as the rest of this game doesn’t match the humor, my biggest suggestion on what they can do is add in the little sort of “damn you” that the tanks give off before they die in Scorched Earth. That would help keep the humor consistent throughout the game, instead of just being an afterthought.

HD neon

SSL steps away from the pixel art style of its predecessors, using smooth edges and semi high definition art, that isn’t on its own too substantial, but there is one factor that makes it look amazing. The lighting in SSL is some of the best lightings I have seen in a 2d game, and it is on everything. The bullets, the tanks, the barriers, the powerups, the supply crates EVERYTHING has this superb glow that makes everything pop, and stand out from other objects. This distinct look is awesome and really shows off how lighting can make a game great.

Ready, Aim, Fire

The game controls fairly well, and intuitively instilling total noobs like me with false confidence (until you get to the multiplayer that is). You control with a hybrid of A and D for your movement and the mouse for aiming. I was concerned that SSL would rely on the arrow keys for aiming, and I am glad that isn’t the case. The early turns will employ a lot of trial and error, as you get your shots dialed in against your partner, and then you will have to account for their and your movement as staying in one place is not the best idea. Some weapons work with this and help you get some damage in early without requiring a ton of skill, and some will reward your diligent aiming with high damage outputs. All of this put together gives a gameplay scheme that feels and looks good to play with.

Wham Bam Alakazam

The Sound effects and music are both good. Wouldn’t say it is anything to write home about necessarily in the SFX department, but the music does do a good job of maintaining the energy. SSL uses metal and rock tracks instead of your standard electronic music, which is a nice break from the norm. And while I can’t see myself downloading the soundtrack, it served its purpose well and was pleasant to listen to.

Better Find Some Friends

The multiplayer for SSL is the main component, and while the single player missions can be fun, you will want to play against others. The multiplayer is very extensive, and multiple modes are available. It is super easy to set up your own match and then you have swaths of customizable options. Things like whether or not you want the wind, how much HP you want all the players to have, and choose the map. The latter one is important, as the default option is a random map, which can end up with you being screwed in a hole for the majority of the game. This is helped after the first 10 turns when, provided you don’t die, the terrain levels a bit and each player get supply crates of weapons. The multiplayer is fun, and the community is decent, considering how other gaming communities are (a cough).

Overall

Shell Shock Live is an entertaining experience. Revitalizing a long dead genre with just the right improvements. The humor fell a bit flat, and the SFX were boring at times, but the art style, lighting, and gameplay were superb. Pack that in with loads of customization and replayability and you get yourself an 8 out of 10 games with a label of “hectic”.\t\t\t\t\t
