\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShellShock Live for Steam already feels more complete than the first 2 pay 2 win games. The singleplayer really seems like it's geared towards teaching you how the weapons work without explaining it, rather it lets you experiment around to see how each weapon works. Some of the missions are hard for the wrong reasons (Lookin' at you, Get Lucky.), but generally you're in for a good time.

Now for the multiplayer aspect of the game (which let's face it, you'll probably get this for the multiplayer), it's about the same as ShellShock Live 1 and 2. You shoot other tanks until they die. There's no Points mode yet, but they make up for it in the currency called Gears. Gears are the equivalent of the Tank Coins in SSL 1 and 2. You get Gears when they pop out of tanks after they've been hit, and you mouse over them. Here's my only complaint so far: THE GEARS CAN OFFSCREEN THEMSELVES BEFORE YOU CAN REACT! If there's a tank to the very side of the screen, the gear will bounce offscreen and you won't be able to get it.

Now, this game isn't Pay 2 win like SSL1 and SSL2, but rather it's based on how much you level up, making it somewhat of a meritocracy. I like that. The more you play, the more experience (both in-game and actual experience with the game) you get, the better weapons you get and the more Tank Points you get. Tank Points are your stats, of which you can improve 4: your gun (attack power), your luck, your armor (defense), and your mobility. Each of this has a different advantage in battle, and provides for very unique builds.

I would recommend this to anybody who loves the idea of a 2D turn-based Tank MMO.\t\t\t\t\t

Shellshock live is a MMO tanks game with tons of weapons and other customizable features. Cool. But you probably want to know more.

In case you did not know, Shellshock Live (SSL) is a steam adaptation to 2 free-to-play browser games, reasonably titled Shellshock Live 1, and Shellshock Live 2. Shellshock Live 2 is the newer and more active browser game, but the steam version (the newest adaptation) of this franchise has many differences than SSL2.

Q: if those browser games are free, why buy this game for $7?

A: It's based off what you want. All the weapons are found in both games, but here's the kicker: for $7, you get ALL the weapons. With Shellshock Live 2, lots of those weapons (and other things, but weapons are the main point imo) are locked behind paywalls (you can get 8 weapons for >$7. There are 8 of these weapon packs, and 2 retro weapon packs (from SSL1) that are twice the size and cost more). However, with Shellshock live Steam, it takes lots of GRIINNDDDIINNNGGGGG to get these weapons and upgrade certain weapons. (Im level 58/100 as of this review and i still dont have big shot) Thus, it's if you want to pay, or grind. If you pick SSL2, you chose pay. If you pick SSLS, you chose grind.
Now that that's out of the way, here are pros and cons for this game compared to SSL2.

PROS:

-No IAP (In-game-purchases)
-8 Player Battles (if you can get one going)
-Graphics! Graphics! Graphics!
-Early Access game...new features are being added more often.
-Clans
-Single Player mode (80 missions useful for newbies)
-NO IAP!!!!!@#$*!@*#%!@#*$%^&!#$TRQ@#111111 (sry)

CONS:

-Active people pale in comparison to SSL2
-The game isn't gaining many players currently
-S00 grindy
-Has weapon balancing issues (may be fixed, but we must wait.....)

All in all, this game is arguably in improvement on the flash version. I, myself, am really against In-game-purchases and find this to be a MUCH better value. However, it is much harder to create games than SSL2 due to the lack of people playing this game. For the price tag of $7, though, i find it to be well worth it. Plus, you're helping the community grow, right? :)\t\t\t\t\t

I've followed the franchise since its inception, but I will try to be as unbiased as possible. All reviews are at its current state; I firmly believe they can be 10/10s by the official release.

Single Player (7/10)

Single Player was a nice addition to this version. It is a great for first time players as it introduces a large selection of weapons and strategies. Players are forced to learn about and take advantage of essential aspects of the game such as portals, bumpers, items, etc. As an introductory tool, it is fantastic. My two only two concerns are something that can and likely will be fixed, but currently need to be addressed: opponent AI and mission amount/replayability. At first the opponent AI was something that just was sort of funny at first, but by the later missions people can easily win by taking advantage of it. Quite simply, if you put the computer opponent in a hole, they will shoot themselves over and over. I do not think this is a pressing issue, but it is something that would be nice if it were fixed. Secondly, it would be nice if there were more missions (or replayable ones). Again, this isnt extremely pressing and is likely to be added in the future. Perhaps have a daily challenge (ex: beat mission 8-6 in 5 tries or less for 10 gears!)

Weapons (7/10)

The weapons look great. They are fun and unique. They are built for different situations which makes strategy more interesting. However, there are two somewhat pressing issues. First, is the leveling system. The current system is fun, but once you level a weapon it is unlikely you will ever use the old version again. Thus, the endgame weapon pool isnt that large. I'm not sure exactly how this can be fixed. One idea is once a weapon is unlocked it becomes independent from its older version (they are separate, you can get both in one game for an example). The second issue is weapon balancing. Particularly, the close range weapons.
EDIT: So I took about the part about the OP close range weapons. In the recent update, many were nerfed to do about half of what they use to do, which is a step in the right direction. It also shows the developers are listening to the community. There is still more balancing to be done, but these weapons were the main ones that needed to be changed, so balancing is not super urgent anymore.

Multiplayer (8/10)

Like I said, I love the portals, bumpers, items, maps, all that kind of stuff. Multiplayer is pretty nice in its condition. 8 player games were added which is extra fun. The only thing I dont get is what happened to points mode. It was a good mode if you wanted to play a longer game that worked just fine in the online versions, not sure why it was removed.
Just as a side note, I disagree with everyone complaining about the slow leveling system. The game has been out for a few weeks and you're pissed that you're only 15% of the way to max? If the leveling system was made faster, then you all will be complaining that more levels will need to be added after max. Be patient young jedis!

Other upgrades (9/10)

Skill tree is awesome. Maybe add a little more juice to luck (gears in boxes?). Gear shop is brand new but looking great. Not too many tank customizations, but that can be easily added.

Overall (8/10) (remember, this is for current state!)

I just want to add something, especially for people who have never played. This game is not some action intensive adventure. In fact, it can get quite boring if it is all you do for a few hours. Whats great about this game is that it does not demand so much attention. Its the perfect distraction. Once of my favorite things to do is to watch a sports game on tv and play this at the same time. You dont even really have to think to play this; I played this during the 7 hour redsox-yankees game and was like "I wasnt really even paying attention to my computer, yet I leveled up a few times and did just fine!" Thats what I love about this game.
As for the flaws, they are certainly fixable. I believe that all these things can and will be fixed eventually. For now, we will just have to wait. The updates come pretty fast so it might not be long. So I am going to keep playing and enjoying this game as it gets better and better!

ps: I would love to recommend which weapons should be balanced (and to what extent) if there is any interest\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are familiar with the version on Kongregate.com and love it already then you won't be let down by this one.
The game may be in Early Acces, but it doesn't take away from the fun at all and has a lot to offer to its fans.

If you are new to the series but enjoy the genre of turn-based artillery shooters, then I can't recommend this game enough. It has enough to keep you trying to reach a higher level for new weapons and power ups.
The single player missions are nice, some offering a fair challenge and even occasionally cause some dickripping to occur.



I haven't encountered any bugs and haven't heard of any gamebreaking ones either, so far.

PRO's:

Online Multiplayer AND Singleplayer puzzles
Free For All, Team-based (Everyone shoots at once, team shoots at once, each player shoots on his/her turn)
Very polished (Kchamp has considerable experience, this being his third installment of the game)
CLANS for the competetively minded people out there
Easy to pick up and very casual. Great for a quick 15 minutes of fun!
Trickshots with portals and shields ;D
Plottwists, so many plottwists .. Turned a lost game around by hitting a 2X buff with a snipershot and have it critically hit on target after bouncing on a shield.
I feel the price is right.
Chat function in game lobbies and game itself, but no toxicity.

° New features are being added frequently °

CON's:

Game is in Early Acces, so everything stated and mentioned, including personal opinions, are prone to change
Due to Early Acces there is some waiting time for filling a multiplayer lobby, but this is barely an issue.
It's hard to think of any other cons, the game is really polished.

CONCLUSION: If you are a big fan of the last 2 games, don't hesitate to pick this one up! It's worth it!
Maybe even convince some friends to come and play together :D Or make some new ones like me.
