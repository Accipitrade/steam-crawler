\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best cheap little purchases I've made on Steam.

Shellshock Live is a very simple concept. You are a tank, you shoot at the enemy tanks. That's the game. Take into account distance, power, angle, gravity, and then land your shots on your enemies.

Oh, then there comes the 400+ weapons. We got your standard shots. We got your grenades. We even got shanks. But how about a giant laser that goes through land and other tanks? Yep, got that too. Well how about a giant pigeon that poops all over the place? Yea, got that too. How about a giant next full of angry wasps?! Oh yes, we have plenty of wasp nests you can shoot at people. The sheer variety of weapons is almost always entertaining, and fun.

Go from 1-100, gaining a new weapon every level, in addition to leveling your weapons by using them. You will also gain skill points used to give your tank more mobility, more damage, shields, or bump your luck up so those health boxes give more armor/weapons.

A fair variety of game modes as well. Standard DM or TDM sure. But also Rebound, for the trick shot enthusiast. Or Assassin if you want to play with multiple people, but don't want anyone teaming. Juggernaut where 1 tank gets a substantial health boost, and the rest of the players are against him.

Add in that the creator is still updating the game with new weapons and you get a lot of bang for your buck with this game. If your familiar with an old 90's game called 'Scorched Earth' or any of the Worms games, the gameplay is very similar, but different enough to have it's own unique little corner.
