\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who played the browser games (ShellShock Live and ShellShock Live 2), this is not only an improvement to visuals and gameplay, but also is not "free-to-play." Not only is the XP rate and everything like that much more satisfying, but the game itself is not that expensive to begin with.

For those who haven't played those browser games, but have played tank games of a similar type, I have to say that this is hands down the best tank game out there with the variety of weapons and the sense of gradual progression. Despite the wacky weapons (which are half the fun), the power and angle indicators are intuitive and realistic (not always a guarantee in tank games).

This game also includes the addition of a singleplayer challenge mode, which has some interesting scenarios, not all of which are about killing enemy tanks - it's more about taking advantage of the peculiarities of a weapon, or practicing skill shots. And the occasional deathmatch, of course.

There is a local multiplayer, which is great if you have family, but don't want to buy multiple copies of the game (warning: does not give you XP, if that is your only reason for buying).

And the online multiplayer is the main focus. It's what the browser games were all about, and generally what made ShellShock live unique at the time. It's good. Of course, people of a higher level than you are generally going to win more often, but it's not entirely ludicrous, and it is still very possible to win.\t\t\t\t\t
