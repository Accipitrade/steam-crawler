\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview last updated: 5/18/2015 (mm/dd/yyyy), Shell Shock Live Early Access v0.9.1.2

Shellshock Live is the much anticipated reboot of the free to play flash game Shellshock live 1 and shellshock live 2 that personally I been having a lot of fun with in recent times.

Gameplay

To those coming from the previous flash versions of the game you will find that Aiming weapons is much more difficult to do in this version as all weapons have a different drop-off weight and how much power you put in your shot is much different compared to free to play versions of the game so it will take a bit of getting used too. One issue I been having is the traction as it seems very weird in this version as of the game, in the free to play version of the game even if you do have a low traction level you were able to go down any hills that aren’t at a 90 degree angle but you were unable to go back up that same hill without a higher traction level, where in this version you cannot go up or down most hills without the proper traction level which gets frustrating at times when you find yourself stuck on top of a hill as you are basically a sitting duck until the hill is destroyed enough for you to escape from. The portal mechanic is a welcome new feature that gives a whole new meaning to trick shots as they often enable shots that are usually impossible, possible if you aim well which is always impressive to look at.

The single player mission mode are a nice and very welcome addition to the game that enables new players to the franchise to slowly learn the basics of the game without jumping straight into multiplayer games unprepared, but I feel the difficultly curb goes up by huge spikes every set if you plan to go through them all at once with plenty of missions relying on perfect aim on a single attempt to complete without the assistance of the angle and power meter. Personally I feel this can be improved on by giving players more than a single attempt or at least give some tracers to assist the player as it can get frustrating with the lengthy trick shot missions. On the flip side if you put the missions to the side and level up your weapons a bit you can completely trivialize the difficulty of a lot of the missions. There should be a form of weapon lock system that only allows you to use the tier of a weapon that was originally meant for the mission or later mission additions won’t pose much of a challenge.

Shell shock live currently offers two different weapon system to choose from, one of them is the classic randomized weapon system that veterans of the series have come to know and love and the second is a selectable tiered weapon system each with its own set of advantages and disadvantages. Leveling up your weapons has also changed drastically compared to SSL2, instead of leveling up a single weapon multiple times for more power with that shot you now level weapons to upgrade them to superior versions of the weapons. The game also offers tank upgrading system, each time you level up you get an upgrade point for your tank which you can spend on either improving your traction, adding armor to your tank, adding higher critical chance to your tank, or just increasing your luck with certain aspects of the game.

Currently the multiplayer needs a bit of work because as of right now there are only a small handful of game modes to choose from which can cause the game to get stale after a while playing each of the modes. Finding a game can also hard at times due to the low but slowly growing population of the game, this is especially true if your one of the higher leveled players as a majority of the lower leveled players will tend to avoid you for good reason. Overall though the multiplayer is very enjoyable to play though it could use a couple of new game modes to mix up gameplay.

Training used to be very sluggish, but recently the exp required to level up was greatly adjusted to take out a large portion of the grind required to see everything the game has to offer which makes the game much more accessible to the casual audience while at the same time giving those more hardcore players that want to go beyond the traditional max level of 80 to grind the 20 additional prestige levels that will require tons of time and effort to achieve the real max level of 100. Though right now you might want to hold yourself back on grinding those levels as currently unlocks behind those levels are in development so there is not much reward currently for achieving the real max level aside from maxing out your tank.

The achievements can also be improved upon, for the most part I love the achievement system and I hope to see it expanded upon but I feel right now some achievements requires unrealistic amount of time and effort to obtain to unlock lack luster rewards if compared to the work required to get them. Usually I have no problem with achievements that require a lot of work to get but I feel they should be achievements for achievements sake with no rewards behind them and the more doable achievements having unlocks behind them.

Graphics and performance

The game looks very nice with all weapons and various effects having their own unique look and charm to them and also runs very smoothly on my computer, but I feel some weapons generate too much needless smoke sometimes. Another problem I have is the bloom, every shot and the background have a kind of bloom effect I feel is much too bright for comfort. Personally I feel there should be more options for adjusting certain graphical functions to optimize the game for computers that do not have the best specs because as of right now you don’t have many options and if you do not have the best pc to play this game with you will have no choice but to put up with the lag.

Community

The community is a very competitive one, but with competition comes toxicity so expect drama both in game and on the official forums. For the most part you can ignore these people by simply muting them or staying as far away from the drama as humanly possible, but I feel there should be a way to blacklist players from your games as there will be many times where people cause a lot of trouble for you by either quitting midgame because things are not well going for them or being generally disruptive in your game to the point where it ruins others fun. You are able to kick them from your room but that does not stop them from simply rejoining the room and the process repeating itself until either you finally let that individual in or they get bored and go elsewhere.


Conclusion

To new players of the franchise I would recommend playing the free to play flash version of the game first to get an idea what to expect with this game before buying it so you don’t come in with high expectations. If you are a veteran of shellshock live 2 give the game a shot but be aware currently shell shock live does have less weapons to choose from currently compared to the flash version of the game which will slowly be fixed over time but still don’t expect a large amount of new stuff. With the games recent price cut to the cheap price of $6.99 and the overall improvement to the game, community and developer involvement with the community I am much more prepared to recommend others to try out the game then I was previously. There is still lots of work to be done, I feel the single player mode should be expanded on with options other than missions and the addition of a local multiplayer mode with everything unlocked to play from the start so you can just grab a friend and experience everything the game has to offer without buying the game a second time and putting in the work required but of course that does not carry over to your actual multiplayer profile. Kchamp keep up the good work with the games development and continue to interact with your community as much as you have been lately to ensure the quality of your game only continues to only up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBasically, if you have played Scorched Earth, Dome Wars, or the like, and enjoyed those, then you'll love this.
If you are a Kongregate user, you are well aware of this.
Game used to be in Flash, but now is in Unity. A big jump in game engine, and it's ever so good. I'm not fond of Unity, but I'll grant an exception to this.
No need for a membership to enjoy everything this has to offer, but since this is Early Access, you never know when a VIP membership might arise. A license alone never suffices the cost of servers these days.
With the amount of weapons, maps, and customizable tanks, it is well worth the $15.
Runs great in Ultra setting, 1080, with a 2GB GTX 750 Ti. Windows 8.1 x64.\t\t\t\t\t
