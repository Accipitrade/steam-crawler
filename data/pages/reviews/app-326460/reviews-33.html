\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're here expecting a modern take on the classic 'POCKET TANKS' like i was, don't even bother with this.
it feels like it's trying soo incredibly hard to be like Pocket Tanks, yet at the same time be it's own unique system, and it just sucks at doing both..

it's a minor slog to unlock weapons, since there's deceptively not as many actual weapons existing as it says there is, due to most of the weapons having "level upgrades" i.e. use a weapon WHILE GETTING HITS enough to level it up to a new version that you can dynamically swap to if you pick up that base version as one of your weapon choices in a match.

There's nowhere to view descriptions of what any of the weapons do, you just have to either run to the 'weapon range' and shoot it off a few times to see how exactly it works, or take a guess off of what its picture is.

Tanks have a health system instead of "who scores the most points" along with an RPG style upgrade system that can improve your tank via upgrade points earned from leveling up put into 4 basic trees - health, damage, movement, luck
>health does the obvious, gives you more health to work with so you can handle more damage before blowing up, but also has points that give chances at regeneration
>Damage increases chances at critical hits happening [1.5 damage] among adding some crit damage increases [because random crits was totally needed]
>Movement changes how long you're able to move left or right, since it runs off of a fuel system, and it can have a regenerating fuel amount as well as changing how fast you move and how steep of an angle you can move on
>Luck adds a chance at upgraded weapons showing up in loot boxes, lootboxes are random "supply crates" that drop on all parties when weapon amounts get low, or after a certain amount of turns have passed, they give more weapons and sometimes items

there is a "Campaign" of sorts, that would give off the impression that it would help you learn the different types of weapons and how they work.
instead after the first section of missions they seem to have an obsession with forcing you to do "trick shots"
however, in these trick-shot missions, they disable the numbers from showing up on your angle-power meter-thing, ON TOP OF completely randomizing what angle and power you start at, every single time you re-try the mission, making it even harder to try and zero-in on the right angle and power you need to get past this unneeded challenge that's more frustrating rather than actual challenge.

tl:dr
3/10 it's an incredibly disappointing [seemingly] spiritual successor to PocketTanks with things it doesn't need and frustrations that shouldn't have existed
this would be better put out and gotten as free-ware instead of even it's on-sale cost.\t\t\t\t\t
