\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent strategic shooting game that harkens back to DOS Shareware classics Desert Tank Wars and Scorched Earth. With colorful graphics and an impossible number of weapons to unlock, this game is never boring. It's simple and straightforward, easy to pick up, and just plain fun.

Really, my only complaint could very well be an impossible one to address, and that is the curious case of humanity. In my 48 hours with the game, you either see constructive encouragement in the in-game chat or some of the most abusive, immature comments I've ever read in a chat. You miss and you're a noob who should have been aborted, or you make a shot and are accused of cheating. Racism is rampant. Insults fly easily. Now, I'm not new to this, but I've honestly never seen such flat hatred and overreactions from players over such a fun and simple game, and it makes me not want to play with other people. I suppose a filter for the chat could make it more tolerable, but that just makes people more creative in how they insult. Alternately, I suppose their parents could have hugged them more as children, but I don't think that feature can be added to the game.

My only other complaints are somewhat minor, but I'm often wary about developer-centric achievements, especially when linked to unlocks (with all my time in-game, I've not run across Kchamp once). Just the same, though the game has been addictively fun, I don't think I have the patience nor tolerance to stick around for 10,000 games to gain access to some of the unlockables. Even at 48 hours of gameplay, I've only played around 200 games. If I were to even try to play that many games, I'd have to play for around 2,400 hours, and that's just not going to happen.

So, despite the sometimes infuriating nature of some players and some frankly unachieveable achievements, I highly recommend this game and just hope that it continues to improve.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll I have to say to get a lot of 'helpful' ratings is say 'I really like this game' apparently.

ShellShock Live is a game that initially started as a flash game on Kongregate, however the developers evolved it into much more by having it be a paid game with a new coat of paint and a lot more features and improvements over the flash game. However the one thing I do miss is the ability to play pong in-game when you're dead. It's sort've like Worms where it's either a free-for-all, where one player lines up his shot and fires, and then the next. Or one entire team lines up their shot and fires, then the next team. There's special shots, such as 'earthquake' and 'backroller' (which rolls backwards if you shoot behind an enemy, hitting them anyways) or the 'tunneler' which is self-explanatory. There is, in-fact, at least a few hundred different kinds of shots that you can unlock as you level up and finish more matches. As you level up you can also customize your tank and upgrade its abilities which are not by any means gamebreaking, as the matches are recommended by your level.

All in all, for seven dollars, it's a pretty solid game especially if you're poor and bored.\t\t\t\t\t
