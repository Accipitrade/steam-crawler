\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShellshock live is a PvP Strategy game in the same vein as the worms series

Matchmaking/Community~ The bad thing about it is that players with no connection to the internet are a bit out of luck. The good news is that this game can run on pings somewhere in the stratosphere. Matchmaking is easy to figure out, and you'll almost always have a match to join. The community is pretty good as well, most people are generally nice, though a grand majority of them aren't particularly talkative.

Gameplay~ The gameplay is essentially a turn based volley of (occasionally) flashy attacks. The game's physics engine doesn't allow for the floating islands or death-by water of worms, nor does it allow you to take control of an entire team, but rather be part of a team of other players (or go for free for all).

Variety~ The variety is wonderful on all fronts, from the hundreds of weapons, to the dozens of maps, to even a decent amount of varied and fun gamemodes. The weapons, for the most part, feel very different, and all do differnt things. The only problem is that some are undoubtedly less powerful than others, so RNG can make the game slightly infuriating at times. The maps, unlike the Gamemodes and Weapons, can feel rather same-y, which is more a fault of the game's physics engine then a lack of creativity.

Character Customization~ Shellshock has a couple of interesting ideas in the customization department. Not only do aesthetic options for your tank open up as you level up more, but it has a full fledged skill tree. You can upgrade four different parts of your tank, those being: crit chance, movement speed and climbing ability, extra health in the form of armor, or luck, which lets you get better weapons in a few different ways. There are three pronounced skills within each tree, all of which help out in a bigger way.

Story~ There really isn't any story to speak of. If you're looking story, look elsewhere.

Sound~ The sound design is nice. The sound effects for firing and landing attacks are satisfying, and the music is fittingly epic, etc. Another thing the game has is tracks that play after you or your team wins, and they're mostly passable, though some can get a bit annoying.

Why wouldn't you want this game? ~
~ If you need a good story in a game. (1)
~ If your wi-fi is non existant. (2)
~ You don't like the map variety (3)
~ It sounds interesting, but not 7.00 USD interesting.

What to get instead ~
~ As strange is this sounds, there are a good number of flash games that have a decent story and similar gameplay, check out ArmorGames or MaxGames for something like that. (1)
~ Worms Armageddon has a passable single-player mode. (2)
~ The Worms series has a physics engine that better lends itself to varied and interesting maps. (3)
~ I'd again suggest the free flash games (4)\t\t\t\t\t
