ShellShock Live is a remake of sorts to that. I say 'of sorts' since I don't think its the same dev. ShellShock Live has great graphics, roughly 100 main weapons that each can be leveled/upgraded to have 3 or 4 variants/modes to it. You can level up and spend your skill points into a skill tree of sorts, which upgrades your critical damage, movement speed and ability to go up steep hills, luck so that weapon/item crates have better/more stuff in them, and have better/more armor.

ShellShock Live does have a Campaign mode with some basic missions/challenges, and it also has a skirmish mode so that you can set up fights the way you want it.

ShellShock Live's Multiplayer is also nice and active, having the expected game modes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are a multiplayer-centric gamer, by all means give this game a try. I had hoped that it would replicate the frenzy and explosive insanity of "Scorched Earth" from 1991 and for the most part it does.

Being unable to progress your "character" in strictly offline matches against the AI means that you are unable to experience the full range of customization and weapon options. There have been multiple requests for this feature since the game was in early access. This and the lack of local co-op when similar reboots of old franchises (such as Road Redemption) have included it suggests to me that the developers are more interested in unit sales than the experience.

It's not bad by any means, but I am not going to give it my recommendation either.


<edit> : According to the Store page, the original length of time that SSL was planned to be in early access was 3-4 months. Writing this 12 months after the initial release, with development on SSL2 seemingly well underway, would suggest that this game also be placed in the growing list of cash farming perpetual early access cash cows. My first return ever. </edit>\t\t\t\t\t
