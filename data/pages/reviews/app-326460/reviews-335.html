\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs told by how many hours I have at the time of this review, I thoroughly enjoy ShellShock Live and recommend it if you enjoy(ed) any of the early 2000's 2D tank shooter games. You have a huge and growing arsenal of weapons to unlock and upgrade, you level up and upgrade weapons very quickly when you begin the game but it gets more intense once you get past the level of everyone having the same few weapons. After 150 hours I am almost level 79, but I also don't regularly play those pesky "XP Farm" games.

[in my opinion]
Top Pro's:
-awesome visuals
-multiple fun game modes
-massive arsenal of weapons to unlock and upgrade
-fun and rewarding achievements to work towards
-everything is able to be unlocked through game play, no pay 2 win
-super customizable games
-very fun to play, even better with your friends

Top Neutrals:
-single player missions are mostly fun and challenging, but some are almost impossibly hard to complete, they do reward you well though.
-almost an obnoxious amount of games you see are "XP Farm everybody mid!!!!". super annoying, but it doesn't directly hinder gameplay cause all they really do is making slightly harder to find fun games.
-some weapons take a very long time to upgrade, especially since you have to use them in a game to get XP for them, and the weapon loadouts are randomly generated. BUT that does make it incredibly satisfying and worthwhile when you do upgrade them.

Top Con's:
-as mentioned in the neutrals, there are so many games where all players want to do is go to the middle of the map and shoot each other pointblank to farm XP. this means that if you want to make a game that's anything but that, it can sometimes take 15-20 minutes to get a full lobby of players, and you'd better not even think about trying to get a 16 player tournament running.
-same as it says in 99% of the negative reviews, the playerbase is beyond toxic sometimes, its not as bad as some make it out to be, but it's still bad. there are 0 penalties for leaving a game early aside from you losing any XP you earned that game, so people will often abandon their teammates if they get the slightest scent of them possibly losing the game. and there are quite a few really bad hosts that get saltier than the dead sea if you do better than them, they will kick you from the lobby after the game ends if you land more shots than them, snipe somebody, have better weapons, don't team chat, etc. you get the point. you can often find lobbies that do not have these kinds of hosts, but it can take a while.
-there is an achievement called "dev destroyer" that awards you a very powerful weapon if you beat kChamp in a game, OR beat a lvl. 100 who has the achievement. I am not lvl. 100 yet, but I've been in games with them and if they even mention that they have G-rays [the weapon], any person below level 50 becomes an actual constant stream of "give G-rays PLZ" in the chat. On the flip side, if a lvl. 100 has the achievement and are going to lose/do lose the game, they will very, VERY often leave at the last minute to prevent any of their opponents from getting the weapon, I was lucky enough to not have that happen, but after learning about it I began to notice that a lot of max lvl. people were leaving games. basically, that achievement makes the leaving-early-problem even worse.

all in all, its a 8.7/10 rating for me, quality game :]\t\t\t\t\t
