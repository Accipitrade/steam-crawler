\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShellShock is a perfect example of a game that is easy to pick up but takes time to master. It's very simple: you have a tank that can move left and right, and fire a variety of ammo types that can have crazy, random, funny or epic results; likewise, your opponent has the same capabilities, and the first person or team to achieve the goal of the match wins. It's fun to play both for the strategy, the epic fails and epic wins, and has a wide variety of challenges for players of all skill levels.

You can also customize the matches you create, and select various modes of play, anything from a regular death match to the hilariously random 'shoccer' (basically soccer, but you have to shoot the ball to move it).

There are of course a few downsides, but they don't cause enough of a problem to really turn anyone off from the game. The progression of upgrading your tank can slow down once you reach higher player levels, but you're level is not tied to your ammo upgrades, so even if you're still waiting to upgrade something like your tank's defence you can still make yourself more powerful by buffing your different shot types in the meantime by just playing more matches and getting direct hits. There are also some challenge levels that do seem to rely a bit too much on luck so it can get frustrating when you're just trying to unlock certain weapons, but by the laws of probability you can usually overcome this by playing the level enough times and throwing in what you've learned from previous challenge levels to increase your odds. I like to think of this as the game just giving you more opportunity to practice and get to know the mechanics better.

All in all, probably my most played game on Steam. The replay value is truly worthwhile, and an excellent party game when you want to play with friends. Not to mention, super affordable for such a well-made game.\t\t\t\t\t
