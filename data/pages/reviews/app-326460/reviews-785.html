\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis type of game has existed for a very long time now, and many variations of it have come up on every platform. I've enjoyed every single one thus far, but did eventually get bored of them.

This game, however, seems to perfect the long line. This game has very nice and clean graphics while still being able to run on pretty much every system, it has enough weapon variations to keep one entertained, a sytem to keep players coming, and excellent customization in every area of the game.

The game customization and level system are what surprised me the most. When a player sets up a game, they are able to choose the amount of players, map, game mode (which there are a surprising amount of), health, and other various items. Then, as one plays the game, they see that they get experience. Players level up as they play, which not only unlocks new weapons and maps, but allso allows allows the player to upgrade and customize their tank. A player's tank can be customized to their liking, specializing in four different categories which boost different areas of their vehicle. This feature pleasantly surprised me the most, and is exactly what keeps players coming back. Even the weapons a players use upgrade as they use them. The progression system adds so much to the game, and is a key feature.

The in-game chat system is excellent as well, with text bubbles of what a player says appearing above a tank, which can make for some funny conversations.

When I first saw the video before I bought the 4-pack I was worried that there would be a weapon or destruction overload. It appearned that the game would be over in two or three turns with such destructive power, but it turns out that is not the case. The weapons do get strong, but not game breaking especially due to the option to raise the amount of health that the players can have, which goes up to six times the standard.

Overall, this game is much more than I expected, with a good price tag, and lots of content to keep one entertained. Playing with friends is an absolute must, and even when one can't play with them they can go single player or campaign. The game feels almost complete and is still developing, and would recommend it to anyone, especially if they have friends to lpay with and/or they enjoyed the Worms series.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE: Kchamp has made the EXP curve better twice, and I am extremely satisifed with it now. Level 46 now with ~20 hours played, it's still long enough to keep me hooked and unlocking stuff but is fast enough at the beginning to allow new players to fit in easily.

I originally wrote a review for ShellShock Live when I first bought it during the sale, but I wasn't satisfied with it. I've been playing the crap out of this game, and now want to go back with better knowledge and give it a more in-depth look.

The game is in early access, but I'm going to be mostly reviewing it as a full game. The game has been on Kongregate since 2010, also gaining a sequel in 2012, and has slowly grown and gotten better over those years. While I'm sure there are some features kChamp wants to implement before calling it a full release, as it stands the game is pretty much completed minus a few details that are small enough to look past. So keep that in mind while reading, and keep that in mind if you're thinking of buying the game.

There's two main modes to this game: Singleplayer and Multiplayer. Singleplayer has skirmish mode (just multiplayer against bots) and Campaign Mode (We'll get to it later). The multiplayer is online only, which is kind of disappointing since this seems like a hotseat kind of game. The developer has stated that it would take a re-build of the game to add hotseat, so don't expect it any time soon- if at all.

Going to start off with the multiplayer to explain how the game works. You're a tank, you shoot other tanks, your shots are affected by gravity, it's turn based. Sound familiar? Yes, it's like every other physics based fighter like worms, only the gimmick here is the sheer amount of weapons you have at your "disposal". 200+ apparently? You might think that's a bit much- sounds like quantity over quality. But most of the weapons are rather unique, and the ones that aren't are still upgraded versions of other weapons, meaning that there are only a handful that are "bad" or "useless". The sheer amount of situations that you can take based on your loadout each match is amplified greatly by this arsenal. Why the airquotes around "disposal"? You don't have all 200+ weapons unlocked to begin with, maybe only something like 15. Through levelling up your tank and your weapons, you unlock more weapons, subweapons, maps, parts for your tank, and skill points for your tank. This means that somebody who is level 80 is literally going to wipe the floor with a level 1. So my personal recommendation for new players is to buy the game during a sale or during some event that will draw more new players to the game for a while. But how is the levelling system? To start, fun. Later on? Long. The maximum level is around 80 or something, and apparently there's prestiges or some system similar that adds even more weapons to unlock. I've been playing this game at time of writing for 13.2 hours, and I'm only level 20. I doubt I'll ever hit anywhere even close to 80, and I doubt most players won't either. So there's going to be some weapons, maps and abilities that you'll just never see unless you're hardcore about it. The levelling up system for weapons is even LONGER, since they only get the exp that you damage people with. The only weapon i've upgraded currently is the sniper, and that's just because it deals crazy damage when you hit things with it, raking in exp. I have no idea how you're supposed to level up the basic shot 4 times, even if the threshold for exp is lower.

The campaign is like the grinding system: at first fun, then slowly becomes tedious. It's a series of skill and sometimes luck based challenges that net you crazy amounts of EXP the first time you beat them, and weapon unlocks for beating chapters. They start out fair, giving you difficult target practices and skirmishes with limited weapons, but then become absolutely ridiculous with no margin for error unless you've grinded in multiplayer to a crazy high level. About halfway through the target practices required so much accuracy and the bots became so hard that I just looked up a co-ordinate guide and restarted the mission until I got snipers. However, I still sort of enjoyed the campaign even though the difficulty curve was a bit steep, and think that it adds to the variety of an easily stale game otherwise.

Other than that, the graphics are alright for what they are, the music is generic and there's only like 2 tracks (turn it off and put on your own) and the sound design is satisfying and chunky sounding. If the EXP rate gets fixed and the game retains at least a portion of it's playerbase, I'd definitely say this might be one of my favourite pick up and play multiplayer steam games. As it stands right now, it's just simply alright. The balance of having to grind forever to get something new and a too split playerbase just makes the game boring after a short time playing. It's definitely worth the price if you like the genre even right now though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played a version of this game back in the early 90's and loved it then. We had a single cannon shot to obliterate the landscape and attempt to destroy our opponent. Shellshock Live includes over 250 weapons, I had hesitations that this would work. Sometimes too much variety can be overload.

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=624332808

Gladly this game has a massive grind to open up all the weapons. I hate grinding in games, especially in PVP games. So why do I think it is good in this case? Well, the basic weapons are enough to enjoy the game to its full and recently the playerbase got a MASSIVE influx due to popular streamers publicising the game. Meaning you are pitched against other players of same experience with same weapon loadouts. I can imagine it could've been a problem with a small playersbase but not now.

Maybe preferably I'd like to have access to all weapons without a grind and be able to custom select which to include on the server I create or join a server that has a selection I like to play with. 250+ weapons is a LOT to get to grips with so maybe the devs have implemented their introductions in the best way. Time will tell...

One thing I didn't realise with Shellshock Live was that the most popular way of playing isn't turn-based but actually phase-based. So every player is setting their power and trajectory and deciding which weapon to launch at the same time. The result is very little waiting time for other players!! It keeps the game and the fun flowing extremely effectively.

Touraments have been added recently, I can't wait to try those!

The game is simple looking in design but the graphics are clean and effective.

I'll be spending a lot fo time in Shellshock for sure, highly recommmended!!\t\t\t\t\t
