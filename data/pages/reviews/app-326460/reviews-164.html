\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBring a group or don't buy.

Solo content is limited. There's a campaign of ~80 missions where you can earn some progression, a mix of bot matches and "kill X stationary targets in Y shots" challenges. Otherwise, you'll need to play multiplayer to interact with the vast majority of unlockable content.

If your idea of fun is any of the following:

* Taking turns in an 8-player trick shot match (Rebound) because high level players make the most exp there.
* Moving 8 tanks to the middle of a flat map so that everyone can shoot everyone freely for exp gains.
* Playing on a team of randoms against a premade team.
* Not playing half the available game modes because they don't give as much exp according to the community (even if they actually do), because if you do host them, half the players joining won't even know the rules!
* The players who want to play an "actual" match but are not capable of playing Rebound will host no wind rooms and use set shots (X angle Y power goes Z% across the screen, usually 100 power upward regardless of how easy the shot would be to make "normally").

Then sure, you may have fun playing with the community. The core problem, if it isn't obvious, is that most active players grind for the sake of grind, and are taking the path of least resistance to do it. You want an actual variety of games that aren't farms? Better have your own group.

When playing within my own group, I have fun. When engaging with randoms, 80% of the time its a miserable experience. But if you want to get anywhere with the extreme progression system in place (which used to be 3x slower!), you'll need a very dedicated group or you'll need to tolerate randoms.\t\t\t\t\t

Especially if you know your angles, or you are proficient at making predictions, then you'll surely be good at this game! ShellShock Live is an excellent game to play with friends as well. You get to meet some awesome people on there, but just like every other online game, there is the occasional toxic bunch of players. One thing I like about the game is that it doesn't get boring, unlike most multiplayer games. You have tons of different generated maps, various game modes, and not to mention there are literally hundreds of different weapons (including the upgrades) you can use to absolutely stomp your foes. So the game can be unpredictable, if someone fires a nuke, that will turn the tide for the entire round. ShellShock Live is a game I feel everyone knows about, but not enough people have played the game themselves. I originally got this game in 2018, and I remember in the past, every morning for a while, I would get up extra early and play a couple of matches while eating breakfast before I had to go. And here I am, still playing it with much time spent, hopefully, more in the future till I finish it. So, if you haven't played it, you really should! It's an amazing game I would strongly recommend to numerous others.\t\t\t\t\t
