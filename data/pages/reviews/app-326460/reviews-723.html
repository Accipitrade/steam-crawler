\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShellShock Live is a really fun game, in which you brutally destroy your opponents' tanks with many different weapons, and by many weapons, I mean many weapons. Have you ever imagined a grenade that just broke into a bunch of smaller grenades? How about launching a rainbow? How about a cat? There's all that and more.

When you start, you're able to get right into the action in multiplayer, or play through singleplayer missions, starting from a simple and quick tutorial. The singleplayer missions are mostly fun from what I have played, albeit infuriating sometimes. For example, one of the missions further in pits you against an opponent that teleports almost every turn. Another one I am currently stuck on pits you against 2 enemies, each with slightly less starting HP, but they have more powerful weapons than you, being able to drain over half of your health in one use of one of their weapons, meanwhile you can barely take down ~20% of their health with 1 GOOD shot of one of your weapons. As you progress some of the missions begin to feel slightly unbalanced. However, they do yield XP when completed, which can be used to level up. More on that in a sec.

The multiplayer mode has a wide variety of settings you can play in, having a quick join feature, which sometimes likes to apparently join me into games that are already in progress, and allowing players to create their own rooms or tournaments with their own rules or join other rooms or tournaments. In Multiplayer mode, there are several modes, my favorites being Juggernaut(which seems to not be played much, as in big rooms, the Juggernaut is OP) and the classic Deathmatch. Juggernaut is essentially a 1 vs the rest of the players in the room, and has more starting health than everyone else, which is dependant on the HP setting and the amount of players, not sure exactly how though. The Juggernaut wins if they defeat everyone else, but everyone else wins if the Juggernaut is destroyed. Deathmatch is the most popular mode, and is usually played in teams, but can also be played in FFA. Regardless, the objective is to use the various weapons you are given to reduce your enemies' HP to 0. The last team/player remaining is the victor.

As you play, you will gain 2 things over time, XP and Gears. XP is collected by completing singleplayer missions, dealing damage in games, skill shots in games, winning games, being alive at the end of games, and being the MVP(Dealing the most damage in a game). After getting enough XP, you'll level up and gain an Upgrade Token, along with a weapon and/or map. Upgrade Tokens can be used to upgrade your tank in 4 categories: Tracks, Chassis, Gun, and Luck. Upgrading Tracks will let you climb steeper surfaces, and get some extra fuel per turn for movement. Upgrading Chassis will increase the amount of armor you start with, and get a chance of restoring some armor and/or health at the start of each turn. Upgrading Gun will increase your chances of a Critical Hit, which is normally 1.5x the normal damage, but that can also be increased. Lastly, upgrading Luck yields more weapons in crates, and can let you get more armor and health per crate or even be able to use a grappling hook to automatically grab nearby crates without having to move to them. I really like the rate at which you can level up, as it keeps things interesting, not to mention the weapons have their own XP as well, which can let them be upgraded.

Gears can also be found as you play in a few different ways. Your most common way of picking up gears is when they occasionally blast out of a tank that's been hit. Mousing over these gears will pick them up, adding a gear to your balance. There is also a power-up crate ingame that can occasionally spawn, that when you shoot it, it will drop several gears, letting you mouse over them all. Another way is the Daily Win Bonus. All you have to do is win a single game per day, and you'll be able to spin a wheel that can yield no gears to a whopping 100 gears. The last, but risky method is the Wager Gears option in multiplayer rooms. This option can make players wager up to 3 gears per game, in which the winner(s) have the gears split amongst them. This can be a goo way of grinding gears if you play big FFA games and you're good at the game. Gears can be spent on a few weapons, maps, and other cool cosmetics including tank parts or victory music. Again, I really like the rate at which you can get these gears, and unlike the Flash version of this game, Gears can't bought with money, and are actually obtainable in game.

Overall, this game is really fun, and it's worth it. You don't even need to wait for a sale like I did, just buy it! It also runs well on lower end computers, so there's another plus.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShellShock Live is a fantastic multiplayer tank game that brings back fond memories of Pocket Tanks Deluxe and, of course, SSL2. It is a fantastic game already, and relatively cheap as well. I personally bought the game for a few friends, and we've played a lot together, but it's a testament to the game that I really enjoy playing it alone as well.

The Pros:
Matchmaking often works very well, and the internal chat system makes games far less boring.
Wind and atmospheric nudge as well as the one weapon mod make for challenging, skilliful play.
6 game modes means a lot more time until you get bored.
Tons of weapons to unlock is a great incentive to just play one more game over and over.
The campaign is great, and challenging.

The Cons:
Quick Join is just broken, as of this review. It without fail adds me back to the last lobby I was in, or tells me the lobby I joined was full.
Tournaments. You cannot invite steam friends to join your tournament, nor can you kick anyone. As of right now, tournaments are solely 1v1 games in a bracket, and in the future I'd like to see 2v2 games as well.

Overall, this is an excellent game that I would recommend to anybody. It's especially impressive that the game is so polished in early access.\t\t\t\t\t
