\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the easiest to pick up and play, casual pvp games out there.

If you're familiar with artillery games such as Worms, Tanks or Artillery its self, then you'll pretty much know exactly how this game works. You are a tank, you estimate your power and trajectory for your shot and hope for the best that it hits. Mostly multiplayer (which has latency issues) but can also be played in single player.

Why should you choose this over other tank games with some of them being free? Well it all really depends on how much you like this genre. This game is in a way, one the most content rich out of all the artillery games out there with the amount of weapons it has, however it comes at the cost of being very grindy, especially for a casual game. This game has you grind for levels and in turn, weapon unlocks (which can also be leveled) and skills. This is also the reason why I consider this game more casual than competitive, since this game is far from being a balanced game, since not only do players have level advantages/disadvantages but each match is randomized.

Pretty much everything from the weapons you will recieve (based on what you've unlocked) to the layout of the level is entirely random, in a way this can balance out level differences, but the game still heavily favours higher levels since they straight up have access to better and more exotic weapons that would otherwise need to be obtained via a random crate containing the weapon, which once again favours higher levels since the contents are based on the players luck level and are easier to obtain if you have high tracks (speed, traction and fuel).

On paper, this game sounds like it would be terrible but to be honest, the games weaknesses are also it's strengths. The game gives you inncentive to keep playing so you can be in a position of superiority. It's also really fun when you're the underdog and you come out on top. This is on top of all the games other strengths such as weapon variety, the satisfaction of hitting an enemy and what I consider its biggest strength, an excellent game to play while multi tasking such as watching tv, movies, youtube etc (it's why it is my second most played game).

The community is kind of decent. Definatly better than most mainstream multiplayer games but still has a lot of bad apples. Your experience should be fine for the most part but do proceed with some caution.

As for it's early access state, it's basically a complete experience. New content still gets added but for the most part it feels like a full game. Some issues here and there but nothing super game breaking.

I would recommend this to anyone that enjoys artillery games and also would like a game to play while multi tasking. Easy to pick up and play and overall an enjoyable casual game.\t\t\t\t\t
