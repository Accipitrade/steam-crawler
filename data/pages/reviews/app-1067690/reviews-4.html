\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI waited a few weeks to process this DLC before writing a review. I wanted to give a thumbs up as I'd like to support the devs, but ultimately I'm just not so sure about the content in this one.

I had to give this a thumbs down review after coming to realize that I have been avoiding the new barbarian faction on account of there being no reason to interact with them, as reasoned below:

The newly added weapons and armor from barbarians
Pretty much all vendor trash. There's only one exception: the heavy throwing axes, which is a god send for making throwing builds pretty viable now. All the other weapons and armor are inferior in basically every way to normal gear. The only reason to use it is if you literally have nothing else to use, or you're just very much into roleplaying I guess. The devs should really find some way to make their weapons and armor as more of a sidegrade instead.

Their gear being inferior, though, makes sense and I get that. But there ought to be some alternative reward for fighting them, if not for their loot. Think outside the box. Maybe post-battle, your performance impressed them enough that some want to join you, giving you some recruitment options (I think there's an event similar to this, but it's so rare you can't count on it). Or work in some sort of trophy->crafting system instead.

Difficulty
The difficulty of the barbarian faction seems like it was geared towards this game's seasoned players with hundreds of hours of experience and looking for a challenge. It's a challenge for the sake of a challenge. This faction's tier 3 enemies are far and beyond the difficulty of other faction's tier 3's. I couldn't reliability deal with them until around the time the second crisis was starting or ending. Many players in the community have delcared that even the black monolith battle is easier than taking on a large barbarian camp. I wouldn't really mind this challenge if there was some rewarding reason for doing so, but this goes back to the loot issue from above.

Other things
You sort miss out on getting to play around with the new barbarian background if you're not doing a barbarian origin start, which is a little annoying. It's basically just up to RNG on getting the events to acquire one. I only ever got one so far, and he of course rolled with some rather undesirable stars.

The new legendary location and loot associated with it is good, though, I'll give it that. I enjoyed that boss fight.

The new non-barbarian weapons and armor is all pretty good. Whips are great. New named weapons are a plus. The new helmets look good.

No new crisis really seems like a miss I think, and adds to the underwhelming feeling. The community have come up with some creative ideas on what it could have been.\t\t\t\t\t
