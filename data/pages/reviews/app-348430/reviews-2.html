\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really love the whole Quell series and Reflect might be my favorite one of them all. It offers the same sort of puzzles as the first one and adds in just enough new mechanics to keep things interesting, but not so many that things would turn overly gimmicky. Just playing through the game and collecting hidden gems is very relaxing and fun, but once again there is also the option of trying to complete every level in perfect moves - and that can get really dang difficult from time to time! Whether that's just a nice challenge or a frustrating feature depends on the player, but fortunately the game also has a really good hint system in case you end up needing some help. All in all this is one of my favorite puzzle games that you can easily just pick up and play no matter where you were when you last saved the game!\t\t\t\t\t

TLDR: Series of sliding floor puzzles with a leaderboard and saved results. Fun for a bit but the finnicky feeling.

This is an entire game which is centered around one thing, shoving blocks over multiple rows until they hit a wall, then bounce them off that position with the same constraints over and over until you swallow pellets. It is on par with a flash game in terms of audiovisual quality, The puzzles themselves are fine but are annoying and lack entertainment value.

So unless you are willing to replay that annoying ice floor in a platformer over and over I would steer clear of this series.\t\t\t\t\t
