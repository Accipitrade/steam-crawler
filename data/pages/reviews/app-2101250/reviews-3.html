The game feels great to play, allowing for manual aiming mode on your primary armament and auto aim as a option. Very little performance issues even on low spec PC's, very little slow downs or hiccups creating a seamless experience. Wonderful art style bringing me back to the old Space Invaders days.

Progression is pretty standard for this style of games, more you play the more you earn to spend on globel buffs such as more health or more damage. Additionally you can spend your hard earned Gears (in game money) on weapon buffs. You also get more weapons unlocked as you compete achievements.

Check out the Demo if you are on the fence, worth the 3.99$ so cant go wrong with the price.

Con's
I feel like their is a lack of diversity for enemy's, standard units that touch to do damage and range units of maybe 5 different types.
Honestly cant think of any other down sides.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExciting, colorful, arcade like gameplay. Dodging enemies and enemy fire while targeting with your ship's weapon. You can pick up a lot of additional weapons and other helpful items during each run. Collect scrap to upgrade them. Complete the level by picking up a certain required amount of gears.

A good buy for $4 which provided 6 hours of entertainment. Be aware this game has pixel graphics and doesn't support widescreen. Black bars on both sides.

There are 6 levels. These provide different graphics for the enemies and environments. Difficulty increases with each level. All the enemies have the same basic behavior. Bigger ones fire weapons.

Lots of weapons and ships to choose from. Some obviously better than others. You collect gears to spend upgrading weapons and stats. Not much point in continuing after you complete the hardest level. Things only get easier with more unlocks.\t\t\t\t\t
