\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNebula is another decent "bullet heaven" survival rogue lite with the added benefit of most playable characters (or ships, in this case) having a manually mouse-aimed starting weapon.

Going for a space shooter theme like Void Scrappers but with less demanding gameplay, Nebula lets you choose from a variety of circular spacecraft, each with a different starting weapon and different scaling bonus. Some player ships and most weapons and permanent upgrades are locked behind achievements, as with other games in the genre.

Compared to Vampire Survivors, you can carry more weapons (eight total) but fewer passive upgrades (five total). There are no weapon evolutions but many more types of temporary powerups- virtually every ship stat has a bonus drop save additional projectiles.

As with any game of this type there is a marked difference in effectiveness between weapons. With Nebula the weapons to go for seem to be the lasers, which cause continuous damage in a line and can sweep through entire hordes of smaller enemies in single activations like a mech in a Gainax anime.

While it doesn't approach the sheer chaos that is an endgame VS run, Nebula is one of the few games of this type I have played that can actually cause frame lag.

Overall I enjoyed this game more then Void Scrappers, Magic Survival or that one freebie Hololive survivors game on itch.io, but less then the demo for Star Survivor, Vampires Survivors and especially Army of Ruin. Nebula is worth a look if you are into bullet heaven games but I wouldn't say its the standout example of the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
