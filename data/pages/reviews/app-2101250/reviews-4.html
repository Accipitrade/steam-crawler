\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's an okay VS-like. It has some performance problems as the game progresses. Even on a very high end PC I could see the projectile speeds slowing down greatly over the course of a map. The graphics are rudimentary, but have some charm. The menus could use some love. One significant flaw, is that it's very difficult to see the tiny white crosshair. In general the colors and shapes of things blend together too much. Nothing really stands out in the morass of a busy screen, so identifying what's important becomes difficult. Including your crosshair.

Generally the enemies are too dumb and not very memorable. The game becomes far too easy, and the more difficult maps seem to only increase the health of enemies. I haven't lost a map, I played each of the 6 maps once. Maybe there are hidden unlockable levels, or modes which increase difficulty.

The progression system is really interesting, but I'm not sure it's actually a good idea. Combined with the steep cost of rerolls, it actually makes the game less fun overall if RNG is against you. Part of the fun of the genre is trying new combinations, but the progression makes it feel really bad when you don't get the weapons you spent points in. Unleveled weapons are borderline useless. Maybe if they tinker with the numbers and RNG options it could improve. But I fear the basic formula might be flawed. It's a shame, because initially I really loved the progression idea. After playing with it for a bit I'm not so sure.

I'm torn between recommending and not recommending, but I think I'll say no until some updates to performance. If the performance problem didn't exist (and the crosshair issue) I could recommend. Definitely fun and chill.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far, it's a decent little bullet heaven game. So far, I"m not going to say it's "ground breaking" for the genre, per se, but, it's it's own game though gameplay is familiar to other bullet heavens in the genre.

Why do I call this stuff "bullet heaven, as opposed to, say "Vampire survivor-like game"? It's because the genre existed before Vampire Survivor, and will exist after it. I'd even argue this is one of the OG genre's from the 80's arcade days, just, without a name.

At any rate, it's also because Vampire survivor has one critical flaw: No directionality. Half the weapons in that game are useless because they require you to walk into the bullet sponges if you actually want to use it. This game doesn't have that issue. Want to shoot in a given direction with your main gun? Mouse or right thumbstick to the rescue.

Sorry, that last bit may be a bit of a rant, but, seriously, so many bullet heavens have that stupid mechanic, and, I'm sorry, it's not the 80's, we've had the mouse and dual thumbsticks for decades now, it's best that we're able to use these "new fangled" inventions in games that absolutely require them... Thumbs up. Game is cheap, but that doesn't mean it's not good. Don't expect to dash, though, doesn't have that.\t\t\t\t\t
