\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good superhero/supervillian text based choose your own adventure style game. It is everything The Best of Us tried to be and do but couldn't quite pull off. There is a huge amount of character customization possible and despite a huge amount of super powers I felt like the creator pulled off letting you use your powers very well. Id probably like more content but that said there is a good amount. I've played this game on the app as well so I've probably logged about 15 play throughs over all that steam isn't tracking and each time I play I usually discover something I didn't know about before. The game world is well built. I thought the job section seemed a bit thin and repetetive and doesn't seem to change enough depending on career path, but once more this game was a huge undertaking so this is nit picking. So in short if you like the super life style and you want to live as a vigalant, hero, or villian, buy this game and start your super adventure!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game isn’t the best, but it’s certainly not the worst Choice of Games/Hosted Games has to offer. I usually love all these superhero/villain CYOA games, but this one left me feeling a little disappointed.


Positives:

-A lot of customisation. I spent a fair amount of time picking and choosing things.
-Choice of being a hero or a villain (who would've guessed), and actually getting to do bad things.
-The world updates on the news were cool.
-3/4 hours long, so not too bad in length.
-The idea of having a secret identity and juggling your real life/superhero life was interesting.
-Having different difficulty settings is a welcome addition, although I had 0 issues with the default one.

Negatives:

-Fight scenes can get stuck in this weird loop of repeating the same attacks/dialogue. This feels like it shouldn’t have gotten past beta testing.
-Pretty much all the characters are bland and one dimensional. My “romance” character had 2 scenes and then we were in love. My mother only existed to beg me for money.
-There’s a heavy focus on the job aspect and it was... incredibly boring and almost outweighed the action parts.
-The epilogue is awful. I’m not sure what is with superhero CYOA games ending with getting abducted to an alien planet, but there you go.



Overall I’d give it 4/10. I think the sale price is somewhat reasonable, but since there are much better titles for the same price, I don't think I can recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe extensive customizations are just renamed abilities that give the same outcome in game no matter what you choose. For instance, whether I am hitting an enemy with a stream of water, hot plasma or shadow bolt, they simply fall unconscious, start running away or my favorite-- brush it off and keep fighting. Really? Some thugs can be sprayed with searing hot plasma and keep fighting, Run away, Fall unconscious? How about no. They would be dead or extensively injured if they're lucky to escape the reaper. That's just lazy. If you can't deliver the extensive list of abilities advertised, don't try to implement them as silly illusion choices.

And don't try to save everybody or low key dictate the players moral limit with plot armor and deus ex machinas. It's a very very bad look. If I'm a villain, why do I want to save kids? Why do I want to keep up the image of a hero? Why do I want to keep living the life of a pleb when I can elevate myself above the rank & file with my powers? Why is it whenever I attempt to kill someone, they're just stunned? Why do other villains merely stun cops rather than kill them? I hardly think a villain who took the time to break into a museum to steal a precious power source is merely going to leave incapacitated witnesses in their wake. It's silly and unnatural. Like for instance, I went to visit the mutant cockroach I supposedly fried with my plasma in his homeless alley and instead of being given the choice to finish this known criminal, I (Super flying, plasma spewing, sword & gun toting super being) was scared off by some unwashed homeless savages banging on trashcans? Seriously? Given the choice I would've humbled each and everyone of those wretches without losing a blink of sleep. If you have an issue with death & gore, that's fine but then stay away from genres where its a reality. I don't need some soft heart author projecting their personal scruples onto me.

The author got on my near last nerve with their fascination with Japan/Japanese/Asian culture. In the story the author has Americans using the Japanese language to code name threat levels. It's highly awkward and unnatural. No matter if the Japanese were the first to use a classification system, Americans would translate it into English for our personal usage. You would never ever hear American people walking around going "There's a yon-kyu! A Ni-kyu! A Gon-kyu!" They would say "There a Threat level 4! Threat level 2! Threat level 5!' ect & so forth. Furthermore, we wouldn't be focusing on advertising Japanese heroes in the paper or on the news when we have our own, never mind every other nation across the globe having them as well. And why are Japanese assassins coming half way across the world to meddle in American real estate crime? And why must my choice of food joint be an Asian fusion restaurant? The weeb ain't that strong with me, Bro.

Another annoying thing about this story is most of if is spent primarily on office politics and petty social squabbles. So many times I was like "Can we get to the power toting action already?" but was left trying to navigate the filler politics of trying to ingratiate myself to my coworkers and boss for a promotion. *eye roll*

I wholeheartedly do not recommend this. It is deceptive, boring and weeb heavy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished first play through. I liked it overall, most of the psychic powers felt useful, early game is pretty nice, a lot of what I was looking for, mid game was kinda eh, picked up for the last arc, which was great...right up until the
epilogue which I absolutely hated and wish was not included. Overall I would say it has good replayability at least. I do wish there was an option for checkpoint saves, to avoid redoing the entire early game again each time, if you want to keep the same character concept.

Positives:
A lot of options for powers and difficulty
Plenty of choices for how to approach fights
Early hero/villain dynamics seemed pretty interesting
Plot seems to branch out significantly later on, I expect the other ending sections of the game to be completely different than the one I took.

Downsides:
Most of the characters feel a bit bland. They have names, and say and do things, but don't really show much depth, and none of them ever quite feel like actual people to get attached to.
Unsatisfying epilogue, pretty much just set up for a sequel, but I was quite unhappy with it, and felt it invalidated everything before it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother super hero game. Okay, yeah I got it. Who doesnt want to spend a few hours being impossibly cool and bad ass? Saddly this game doesnt deliver that feeling well at all.

You do get to pick from alot of different things to make your super hero, well super! And I mean alot. Then you get to flesh it all out with how your costume looks, and even a bit of pre shadowing what kind of super hero (or villian) you are! And thats where the coolness ends.

Saddly it seems the main game play doesnt care at all what kind of powers you have, or let you use them in any fun way. There is a section when choosing your "powers" that arnt powers at all, but normal things like, athletics, hunting, fighting. having more money, and it seems like 90 percent of the game revolves around your skill (or lack of) with these mundanes. In my play through I never once got to leap across and take an enemy by surprise, despite this being my main super hero skill granting me extreme agility. Not only that but apparently super heros, even ones with super human agility, cant dodge every bullet. Most of them but not all, so every time some one shot at me, i got hit. And you get shot at alot. at the beginning of the game this is not fatal, as you can go get your wounds healed, at the cost of money of course. Later in the game though, you literaly get shot at every time you turn a corner... 4 corners later my super fast marksman ninja bunny is dead from unavoidable bullets. Oh and i died broke too, on account of my own, and someone elses medical bills.

So basicly, lots of choices, but it looks like if you dont have super health and super stamina, it doesnt matter. Because the other skills grant no in game choice advantages. Also apparently your going to need to take all the first aide choices you can to avoid going broke at the doctors.

In a word, its unbalanced and not fun. Ninja Rabbits should avoid at all costs, for others your mileage may vary.\t\t\t\t\t
