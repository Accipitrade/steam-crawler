\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first, I was kinda Sad, but after a while, Rad is not that Bad, once you get past all the Mad.

RAD is very difficult in the beginning. Your hand will not be held. Many things will not be explained immediately. Expect to die many times in order to get a feel for and really learn the game. At times RAD is almost too difficult; I find myself really getting into the game but end up playing the same content over and over. This is due to RAD being more rogue-like than rogue-lite. You aren't getting progressively more powerful after each run. Your XP from each game only unlocks new player skins and small modifiers (ie more of a particular pickup). Everything costs either tape cassettes or floppy disks. Spend your resources wisely, you won't have enough currency to buy everything. You will need every advantage you can get. You can buy some Endo (passive) and Exo (active) Mutations (skills) from some merchants, but most come from shrines and xp, and will always be RANDOM... The Ranged Exo-mutations help a great deal considering combat is slow, limited, and deliberate. Make sure you have a decent controller for the best experience; you want your actions to be precise.

Pros:
+ Superb Music Soundtrack (ready player one feel)
+ Excellent Sound Design
+ Trippy Radioactive Art

Cons:
- Extremely difficult, and hard to progress
- Mutations are very random, sometimes useless\t\t\t\t\t
