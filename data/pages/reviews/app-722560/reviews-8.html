\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSometimes I wish steam had numerical rating system instead of simply recommend/do not recommend.

The graphics and sounds are really good, the gameplay is rather interesting, mutations system feels great and everything goes pretty good until you go to journal (tome of the ancients or w/e it's called) and realize that you've seen 90% of any possible modifications. Initially I thought I just was unlucky to get the same mutations in a few consecutive runs, then I understood that the pool is just very low. Sigh...

Speaking of these, most of so-called mutations (that are active and passive skills) do not do anything for gameplay, they're just there and your best bet is to just ignore them and keep crushing enemies with a bat cause if you try and adapt your playstyle for specific skill, you'll end up wasting tons of time for common foes and, optionally, quite a bit oh health too because monsters do not care what you do to them, they attack you regardless of your skill with seeming knockdown, their attacks are not interrupted while yours are.

6 levels are exactly the same all the time with the only difference that the map is rotated (doesn't apply to 3-1 and 3-2 I guess, at least gates seem to be at the same island; the difference is in "portals" between islands though) so it doesn't refresh you after the last run, you can barely tell runs apart.

Overall I'd say it is 5/9 and you may get a fair amount of fun playing it for some time. Then you just hit the wall and see that you've basically seen everything the game has to offer in a few successful runs and it's replayability is rather low, despite that it is roguelite. At first I thought that it was early access with more diversity to come, checked here and got lost of what to think of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, you can see how long I've played this game (give or take maybe 5 hours left on pause). I loved it, I really did, and I'm not usually a rogue-like/lite fan.

Aesthetically the game's stunning if you like 80's sound and visuals. The plot is bare-bones, but the action is really not. O.K., once you've played one run through to level 3-2 (there are 6 in total) you've seen all enemies there are to see in the game, but the procedural levels, the RNG on items and mutations (which quite frankly make or break the game) mean you'll have different ability combinations and luck-imposed difficulty with every playthrough. Just when you think you've got what it takes to make it all the way to the end, you get killed by an easy mob you managed to miss like the idiot you are. You will be frustrated, but that just makes winning all the more awesome when you eventually do.

I must've played about 25-30 runs before I finally defeated the game. It was totally worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI purchased this game on sale for approximately $12. I am a big fan of rogue-likes and the aesthetic was appealing to me so I went ahead and made a purchase.

The two biggest issues that kill this game for me are the extremely ineffective powerups and clunky movement and controls. The powerups acquired in rogue-likes are typically suppose to improve your character in some way. However, most of the powerups barely improved my character. The controls also feel extremely unresponsive and the movement itself felt slow. This is particularly bad since you must fight using melee attacks in the beginning of the game. This means that enemies can drain your health rapidly, especially if you are surrounded.

I recommend another rogue-like, such as binding of isaac, enter the gungeon, or rogue legacy. Stay away from this game; the only interesting thing is has going for it is the aesthetic.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed RAD.
BUT as many people say - you shouldn't come into this with the expectations of a traditional rogue-like. The runs are long (I probably averaged an hour per run), the world doesn't change that fundamentally between runs, and I didn't feel much need to replay once i won (about 9 hours in). I would almost think about this as a linear game where you die a lot and get to try out a lot of fun combat builds.
The positive trade off for this linearity is that everything you come across has had a lot of love put in it. The world is stunning and full of funny/interesting details. The music, characters, and level design is awesome.
The mutations are also (for the most part) really well done - I was surprised by how different they were and how viable it was to approach combat from lots of different angles. I won the game by running around dropping eggs, turrets and bombs, but i also had some good runs with agile builds, range builds and combat builds. The abilities often synergise really well, if you are lucky.
If you are unlucky, you can sometimes end up with non synergistic jank. But then you die, and try something new!
I'd say suspend your 'rogue like' expectations and you'll have a great time.\t\t\t\t\t
