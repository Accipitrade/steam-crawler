Mixed review.
The game is not terrible but it's not good either.

TL;DR The gameplay is pretty mediocre. If you're used to Spiral Knights or Bastion, you're not going to like RAD.

* The mutations I got so far weren't playable. Throwing an explosive head or your arm at enemies.
The game is throwing toward exactly where you are looking at (left/right/up/down/diagonals), but enemies are NEVER standing at an exact angle with you, so you simply cannot hit them.

* Attacking, dodging and jumping feels sluggish and awkward.
Well, they suffer from the above problems as well. Good luck playing a fast-faced hack-and-slash with poor movements and attack.
By the way, was the game intended to be played on a XBox with a gamepad? The controls are listed A B X Y and L1 R1. (**added warning after comment, this is definitely a game only intended for controller**).
The camera cannot be moved with the mouse either.

* Don't have the slightest clue where to go or what to do.
The whole world is bright colors and randomly generated.
Nothing stands out visually. Enemies are not particularly visible and often only pop when you are nearby. Pickable items are barely distinguishable at all.
Maps have multiple paths opened in arbitrary directions with seemingly no structure. There is little indication or sense of directions. The camera cannot be rotated so you can't look around.

* Liked the artistic direction and the theme.\t\t\t\t\t

- the idea of leveling up and gaining a mutation from the radiation you absorb

- you can save money from runs to fuel future runs

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game ALMOST feels great... but it's not there yet.

I love a ton about it, especially the presentation - the art style, music, the mutations - it's all great, but the pace feels off - there's a lot of downtime where all you do is run around slowly looking for stuff, which is probably due to the maps being so big, especially the underground lairs which consist of bland, endless corridors connecting predictable enemy rooms, topped with teleporters that send you around randomly (why would I ever use those?)
Games like Isaac and Nuclear Throne got this right with the levels being compact and snappy.

Hitting stuff with your bat is not as satisfying as it should be, considering it's your main weapon for most of the game.
It feels like leveling up takes forever, with the main source of mutations being those underground statues... which is kinda weird.

There are different characters you can choose and unlock - what's the point if they're purely cosmetic? just make it randomized instead, or make the characters unique.

I wish everything was faster and more condensed in this game, as consecutive runs feel more tedious than addictive after a few hours. It could definitely use a few patches.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAssuming you haven't grown tired of the 80's faux-punk aesthetic, this game looks great, and has an inoffensive though not particularly memorable soundtrack. The problem with this game is that it is utterly uninspired.

It makes it thorough the basic rogue-lite bingo card, but it doesn't really do anything with the genre outside of this. The mutations could have been fun, but you only get 4 "exo-mutations" (these change your character's appearance and give you attacks) per run, and they are completely random, which means that any synergies or combos that you gain access to, are purely by chance. Your basic melee attack and dodge ability are short and sluggish. Success requires a lot of dull hit and run tactics that exploit enemies simple behaviors. Gain a good ranged attack with an early mutation and the game becomes easy, get a couple of garbage mutations and you will be spending a lot of time chipping at boss health with your pitiful basic attack.

Binding of Isaac handled the whole "lets stack lots of weird power-ups together" shtick so much better than Rad, that I cannot recommend spending time with this game in its present state. If you really like the aesthetic you might enjoy Rad, but with the Rogue-lite genre as crowded as it is with excellent titles right now, Rad fails to stand out.\t\t\t\t\t

Yup you guessed it this is another Rogue-lite game live and die and live and die never-ending loop of hell but I do love that for some twisted reason especially when you get new mutant powers on each run love that (Rating 9/10)

I love the mutant powers you get on each run they have unique styles and perks to them but its sucks when your getting the sucky mutant power on some runs you will know its sh!ty when you lose a lot quicker than your previous run and the game will tell you that in a way but that's the life of a Rouge-lite game (Rating 9/10)

I totally love the environment of this game with its vibrant colors that make you feel like you're on drugs all the time and I love all those wired looking mutants you encounter roaming around the world trying to kill you just because you're you, great graphical design (Rating 9/10)

Beautiful easy and a little annoying to use mechanics but they are great and it all depends on the mutations you get on each run I like that concept (Rating 8/10)

Each run you unlock new mutations and items that are pretty cool keeps you wanting to play it again and again and again very high replay factor in that but you do not keep or use any mutations on your next run you just unlock them from a huge list of mutations and that's okay I guess. (Rating 8/10)

Its 100% a challenging game difficulty increases on each level/map its a good gradual increase and I like that it does not punch you in the balls from the beginning it does that later on which is great (Rating 9/10)

It has amazing Boss's fights each run I get different Boss's that was a nice touch and a great challenge to it will not let you get bored that easy. (Rating 9/10)

I do recommend this game to all gamers who love (Rogue-lite) challenging games and to those who want to try it for the first time it's a fun adventure and I loved it (Final Rating 9/10)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you enjoy games like Binding of Isaac, Enter the Gungeon, Nuclear Throne, Full Mojo Rampage, or Streets of Rogue, chances are good you'll enjoy this as well.

It plays mostly like a mix between Full Mojo Rampage and Streets of Rogue; less open-ended than the latter, though. I'm loathe to comment too much on synergies and combos, as it may be mostly due to lack of progress on my end, rather than a lack of content on the games end, but what I've seen so far seems promising, although not the level of synergy you might see in Isaac.
So far, I haven't come across that many combos, although strangely, I have used one (apparently) that didn't trigger the associated achievement. But I imagine it'll come later on - at the time of writing, I have 7 hours playtime, managed to get to 3-2, and unlocked ~25% of the items, although far from every Exo and Endo Mutation, and only 1 additional Bat.

Takes a bit getting used to, perhaps, but once I got into the rhythm of it, I quite enjoy both the gameplay and aesthetic of it.

Just remember that Tab enables the mouse-pointer for those using KB/M, which isn't enabled by default, and for the moment being, needs to be enabled after every level-change.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: It may be a slow paced game, but for me that slow pace and the 80's themed post-apocalypse theme make it a great and fun game for when your other games get to be too much.

I don't play many rouge-like games, so take this with a grain of salt, but I genuinely enjoy this game. As many of the negative reviews say, it is kind of slow. However, that is what I find most appealing about this game. It isn't your typical rouge-like with fireballs everywhere and a hailstorm of mobs ready to kill you at every corner, it is a nice slow paced rouge-like that is a great time killer that doesn't feel overly repetitive to the point of mind melt. This is just what I need when games like Apex make me want to smash my face on the table, and Overwatch make me feel like I need to take a scalding hot shower to was the toxic grime off my of me.

Above all, the theme is what keeps me coming back! The background music sets a nice tone, the art style of the game is nice and cartoonish, and the 80's style Post-Apocalypse give it a refreshing feeling.

Closing Note: I have been playing an hour or so a day and don't see it stopping any time soon. Well worth the useless PUBG Twitch skins and 2 dollars spent. If I could go back, I would spend the full 20 on it.\t\t\t\t\t
