\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to pre-face this review by saying I like Roguelikes and Roguelites. One of my favourite genres, being able to have unique experiences and runs each time you play has always been appealing to me.

Rad has some great stuff going for it but ultimately, this game feels half finished. Something just feels off.
By no means is it broken or unplayable, in fact it runs pretty damn well. The whole 80s retro theme has been done to death recently and it feels shoehorned in. The narrative isn't compelling and doesn't grip you, the world is dull and bland - apocalyptic sure, but also very samey. I had a hard time for the first few runs understanding what things were, whether i hit them to get something, to make it explode etc. Nothing really feels intuitive or explained. And by that I'm not saying hold my hand through an unbearable tutorial. Chests sometimes look like parts of the scenery and are easily missed unless you know what to look out for, you're lopped into a hub area which is confusing as it has items for sale but no currency is retained between runs, yet things are for sale here. It's only 3 runs in i discovered you return here mid run through portals.

And whilst I'm on the topic of those, defeating a level and coming to a portal choice has no explanation. I chose one. Where did the other one go? Damned if I know now.

Ultimately, Rad brings nothing new to the table. It's not a bad game per say and I will be playing some more as I;m hoping the mutations I've seen so far are the less exciting ones (So far all mutations I've encountered have been 'turrets' that i slap down and wait until they've done a small bit of damage, respawn, repeat), but right now nothing has gripped m,e to play more.
If you want a great roguelike game with unique runs, give Hades a go. It does all Rad does but 10x smoother, better and a great focus on story driven content.

Final verdict:
Graphics - 5/10
The graphics aren't bad. there's definitely a style in there somewhere, but it's not the worst I've seen. The problem is the style is pretty standard.
Sound - 3/10
Generic synth-wave, pseudo 80s that is jarringly noticeable mid game.
Gameplay - 5/10
Solid but can feel a little slow and clunky. The higher speed running mechanic is good for backtracking your previous routes but this has a couple of glaring flaws:
Why not just make that the default running speed? At least out of combat after a few seconds, not just where you've run.
It discourages exploration as you feel sluggish when doing so.
Difficulty - 6/10
Admittedly with the little I've played, it seemed challenging at first but once a random loading screen told me I could dodge with the LB button, it got easier. But nothing the roguelike/lite genre hasn't had before. it gets easier the more you mutate, you mutate more by trial and error and skill.

Overall - 5/10
Painfully generic, offers nothing new and fails to set it itself apart from the myriad other roguelike/lites out right now. Get this on sale for under Â£10 if you can.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRAD is a game that frustrated me beyond words when I first started playing it. I could not get past the first two levels and I almost gave up on it. As I kept grinding on I started to unlock some mutations and items that really help. In the options screen there are game perks you can turn on such as stronger attacks and more health I suggest turning some of these on when you first start playing. This will help you get through some of the early frustrations that I had with the game. There are three different biomes in this game that will always be in the same order. The levels inside of the biomes are randomly generated but it seems that there are only a few different terrain pieces that are swapped around each run.

Pros
- I Like the visuals
- There are some neat mutations and combinations you can do
- I find the combat satisfying even though it is not very complex

Cons
- The music gets old real quick
- Sometimes the random nature of the game does not give you much of a chance

While this game may not be for everyone it has defiantly scratched that itch for one more unlock or just one more run for me. If you do not mind the early grind and are a fan of this style game I would recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

- Slightly repetitive level design. Even with certain random factors, after the 5th try it looks the same. Turning the level 90 degrees, doesn't make it a new level.

- Various characters to choose from that are visually different, but everybody is the same. Everybody is melee.

- The most unique part of this game are the mutations
You acquire them by leveling up, ie. defeat enemies or finding machines throughout the


What made me quit:
I can't recommend another Roguelike that does NOT tell you which upgrades you are getting.
There are better Roguelikes out there, that clearly tells you what you are going to get or allows you to choose from certain upgrades/items and what that upgrade/item does.

I was very disappointed. If the game would have told me, then this wouldn't have happened.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI should've read other folks' reviews before I bought it...

I was impatient and didn't, and I literally paid for it. My main issue: NO REAL FORWARD PROGRESS. If you die, you lose everything and start all over again. Yeah, yeah, yeah, I know... "it's a rogue-like, stupid!" The only way to save in this game--if you can even call it that--is to finish a level (including killing the boss), pick the right portal back to the village, and then Save and Quit... Yep, you've gotta quit the game to save your progress. If you don't, and keep going, and then you die, it's all the way back to the beginning... not the beginning of the new level, back to the beginning of the entire game! Like the mutations you've found? You'd better make sure you finish the level, and then save and quit 'cause they're gone if you die. It gets more than a little tedious doing the same thing over and over again, 'cause you died. I will say though, when you re-do a level it's randomized, so it's not the exact same map... but just a new map (to try to achieve the same goal) isn't enough.

Keyboard controls are pretty rough. You use WASD, so you're locked into a sticky compass directional system, forced to only go N, NE, E, SE, S, SW, W, and NW... no true 360 degree movement. Makes ranged attacks a pain in the @ss to line up when you need to kite an enemy... and you definitely need to kite. You're not meant to go toe-to-toe with a lot of the enemies. There is a "dodge," but it throws you forward... into the monster you're facing. What kind of dodge is that?

The music, sound effects, and (minimal) voice-over stuff are all well-done. I like the environment, and the overall theme of the game, too. But, there's simply not enough content in this game to make me want to constantly rinse and repeat.

Game's not for me. I wish it had a demo... I would've played it, realized this, and not wasted my money on it. Too bad... I typically dig Double Fine's games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have two main issues with this game:
It's slow
The mutations don't have enough variety.

Combat is slow. Dodging is slow. Exploration is slow (though a bit less of a slog if you're going over your own trail). Leveling is slow. Aiming is slow. PROJECTILES are slow. Every run becomes a slog within minutes.

The 80's aesthetic isn't... bad but hearing "WE GOT THE MEAT" and "DOSH" every minute or two gets obnoxious fast. The soundtrack is fine but uninspired and not varied enough.

There aren't a lot of external mutations from what I can tell. They're fun to play with, but if you end up with one that doesn't jive with your playstyle, you're out of luck. Internal/endo mutations help in some regards, but not enough.\t\t\t\t\t
