\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer: I have played this game for more than 100 hours during the closed beta. I'm unaffiliated with the developers and did not receive anything for free.

RAD is a roguelite heavily influenced by The Binding of Isaac and to a smaller degree by other similar games. It contains many elements that are the same as in BoI: More and more things will unlock as you play. Shops are upgradable (persistently). Pairs of procedurally generated levels for each biome which contain their own set of enemies, with alternative possibilities for boss fights. Mutations (which provide attack options and other effects), when paired, can have synergies, just like Isaac's tear upgrades. There are keys (floppy disks) which open doors and chests. There are different types of hitpoints. Multiple ending cutscenes. There is even a timed daily challenge mode reminiscent of Dead Cells.

Some things are different, however. In Isaac, you almost always fight with a single ranged attack option or a straightforward replacement, in an enclosed space. You learn enemy patterns, dispatch them quickly and breeze into the next room. Combat in RAD is more complex; it's based on melee (short range) and there are five distinct base attack moves in addition to whatever is provided by your mutations (allowing for as many as eight attack options in a run, not including synergies), plus combos and dodge rolls. The environment is 3D and melds together better than the single rooms/hallways of Isaac or Dead Cells, but it's also more extensive and can be more hazardous. As a result, the pace of progress in RAD can often be more deliberate, yielding longer sessions than you'd see in those other games. This is compounded by a slower base running speed (it can be upgraded, but not as easily as in Isaac). That is to say, if you like these other roguelites, as I do, you are likely to enjoy this game, but you have to approach it with a different mindset: Be more wary of enemies, who are tankier, faster attacking and less pattern based than most in Isaac. You are not going to finish a run in less than 20 minutes, as you can in Isaac.

The aesthetic of the game is good, with an 80s-inspired art direction and a lot of little visual rewards that may not be immediately obvious when attempting to rush through a level, but will make themselves known in time. I've seen the soundtrack get almost universal praise; it's not my cup of tea, but even for me it's perfectly adequate. I admire how apparently it has been designed to incorporate lots of little callbacks to music from that time.

RAD contains a lot of little secrets and minor features that are not immediately obvious. Despite how long I have played, I'm still learning new things about the game. Every time the (notoriously tight-lipped!) developers stream or talk about it in public, they mention something that I managed to fail to discover after dozens and dozens of hours, and I love that (I already learned three new things about the game this week, after the game's official release).

In summary: If you're a patient and invested player who wants a solid tribute to the best roguelites on the market, with great replay value, and enjoy the combat and core gameplay mechanics, and its unique aesthetic, RAD is definitely the game for you. If you're uncertain about one or two of these aspects, you should still give it a try. If all of this sounds like a pain, you should try something else.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like roguelites. I'm not a huge fan but i like them, i love the idea on paper. It's basically a game you can play forever. It establishes a dialogue with the player. Each time you die the game says "now try this!". The game feels alive. It feels like it's playing with you and making an effort to keep your company. I've had a very irregular history with them though. I played quite a bit of the original Binding of Isaac and some of the remake, but not that much. I loved Abyss Odyssey's graphics but hated the combat and found the dialogue very random and stupid, what i mean is that there wasn't any room to mix maxing and tactical playing. Lastly i hated Enter the gungeon, that is my fault i guess, because i suck at bullethell games. This one, surprised me for good. I love the good old press "square" to attack formula of zelda-like games. And the roguelite aspect it's not as punishing nor random as other games in the genre. In fact i found it kinda too easy. But that is ok with me. Maybe RAD, with its accessible yet engaging gameplay and it's beautiful artstyle and graphics can appeal to a bigger audience and succeed in breaking the small cage of being a "niche" game.\t\t\t\t\t

Movement speed is slow, but you can be boosted by following your old trail; which is a neat idea until you realize there's little point in doing so. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tRAD is a seriously fun game. The environments are beautiful to look at, the music is (excuse me, but I have to say it) rad, but most importantly the game play is great!

Throughout the game, you will die alot, not for nothing though. As you progress, you'll unlock new weapons, new mutations and all the little goodies that will have you starting a new run just to get the new stuff to try out for yourself. Almost want to die sometimes because I feel like I might've unlocked something great.
As for combat, I think it was perfectly designed. Every enemy has their own attack patterns and you'll learn them pretty quick since they're pretty basic, but where the fun comes from is figuring out how to use the mutations you've been given at random to counter the enemies whether one on one or in a large group. Because the mutations are so varied, there's a ridiculous number of ways to fight. For example; my personal favorite is using Toxic Dump to damage anything dumb enough to step in it or herd the smart ones toward something not so healthy for them like turrets.

There is something I should mention. I've run into a serious bug, just the one though. One of the towers didn't spawn. At first I thought I just missed a bridge or an alt exit from a tunnel, but I checked everywhere and covered close to the entire map in grass but no final tower. I was forced to give up and jump off a cliff to off myself and get my points (on the bright side I unlocked a weapon). I only found this one bug and only once so far. Still, be wary of it.

All in all though, Id say just one issue in all the time I've played is reasonable enough, even if it did strike when I was on a hot streak... The game is fun though and I'd like to see more people giving it a fair shot.\t\t\t\t\t
