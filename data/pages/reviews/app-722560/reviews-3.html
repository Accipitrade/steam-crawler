\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was really sorry to not like this game more. I love the aesthetic, from the color palette to the sound and music, but my praise ends there as all the complaints in other reviews are too true.
Most rogue-likes end up being described as difficult, but fun because we accept what RNG gives us and get smacked into a game over when we get bum start. The real shortcoming of RAD is that RNG gives so much bad gear, character abilities, and repetitive level design so uniformly that any fun gets drained away in short order; not because it makes the game hard, but because you can slog so far through the same levels over and over even with the worst roll of the dice, since the game doesn't even have the decency to kill you off quickly.

Out of my 8 hours playing, I spent:
- at least 2 trying to learn how I could interact with the environment and finding out that the hub world contained nothing;
- maybe 2 hours settling into a pace for level exploration and completion (FYI: it's not worth crossing a level again to get every last bit of currency);
- I doubt even 2 hours trying to see how far I could get;
and the last 2 hours futilely hoping that some fun or variety would Randomly Generate.
It did not.\t\t\t\t\t
