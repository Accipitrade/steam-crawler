\r\n\t\t\t\t\t RAD is fantastic! It is pretty much a pure rogue-like, so if you find the genre tedious or difficult i would steer clear. I myself am a massive fan of Binding of Isaac, Enter the Gungeon, Flame in the Flood, Slay the Spire etc etc, the list goes on. As a fan and critic of these games, I can say comfortably that RAD sits with the best rogues out there, even at launch. That being said, more content never hurt any of the games listed above, and I hope this game gets the support it deserves. Being that Double Fine created it I have faith this will happen.
Now to the game itself, it bears as strong an 80's vibe as you might expect, with a neon color palette and retro techno and synth pop sound track. If you enjoyed the style of sunset overdrive or splatoon you will be right at home here (very punk vibe think mad max + pychonauts). All of the rogue staples are here; multiple currencies, permadeath, random upgrades, with meta experience gained between rounds to unlock permanent additions to your upgrade pool. It all works as well as it should and each round feels compelling and brand new. Minute to minute, the game starts as a well tuned melee brawler with a top down camera, as you defeat enemies and level up, you gain random, game-changing powers which often give you new forms of ranged attack or mobility options, most of which operate on a cooldown timer. The game is very difficult and death is right around the corner, but it is meant to be played many times, and like most rogue-likes, really opens up after several plays. A great game for fans of challenge, unique style, and rogues in general! thank you for reading, and please give the game a shot\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate: If you don't like the flow of the gameplay itself within 2 hours, get a refund.
After having basically completed everything in the game, it needs to be said that the whole premise of the story - restoring power to unlock something (seems like a giant pink door on the edge of the home map), is NOT possible. Maybe that's future DLC, but it seemed very misleading. In the beginning, I was largely playing because of this mystery, but after many hours, you also unlock new items and "quirks" - basically difficulty modes with different rules, like my favorite - half health, but double bat damage, which add to the fun and challenge of the game.

Original review:
Played 10 hours straight today...haven't done that since maybe when I was a kid finding Halo 1 for the first time XD Love the visual style and music. The mutations and upgrades go on for 1-2 hours and then they get stronger and stronger with more passive abilities and tweaks while the game throws bigger crowds of enemies at you. I've only gotten to maybe 3-1 or 3-2, but I still have many mysteries, like how to turn on the power in the hub world and what that might do. I imagine I'll put another 10 hours in minimum for $1/hour of quality entertainment. I don't see the grind element - level 1 is SUPER small and quick. Love the innovation of getting 100s of "cassette tapes" aka currency deposited in your own bank after each round, choosing the white door, and then being able to draw from your total of 100s or 1000s of stored value at a small premium of a price. Definitely beats games like Enter the Gungeon where you have almost no hope of ever being able to buy anything in the first level or two unless you get some magical money item at 5% chance or less. Enter the Gungeon is MUCH harder than this and I have about 140 hours in that and I've only beaten the game maybe 2x ever, haha.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm writing my 2nd review on Steam in order to get the record straight about RAD

I'm a big Enter the Gungeon fan, with over 1040 hours in the game. I'm also a big fan of Isaac, Hades, and Planetary Dustoff
If you like Enter the Gungeon/Binding of Isaac/Any game remotely similar, you will enjoy RAD. It's a solid and addictive entry into the genre.

Things I love:
-Mutations are enjoyable, varied, and keep your interest. For example, there's a mutation that turns your head into a flaming skull which you can then throw and detonate by pressing R2 again, or when it contacts an enemy. This can be upgraded through further mutations, giving the skull homing capabilities
-Environments are gorgeous, vibrant, and fun to explore, with landscapes full of textures and depth. Gaining a mutation like wings or double jump allows you to reach secret areas, and finding cracks in walls and floors reveal secrets as well
-Various different moves/attacks to perform, each of which is useful and effective. Reminds me of Link's movesets in the Zelda series. There's a charge up spin attack, lunging forward slash, 3 attack combo, and more
-Unlocking new items/mutations keeps dying and restarting interesting. Being able to bank your tapes (RAD's in-game currency) for later runs allows for a sense of progression without making you too overpowered
-Each floor has various different bosses to face, randomly chosen each time the boss room is reached, so bosses stay interesting
-Soundtrack is full of absolute bangers

Things that could improve:
-Enemies could be a bit more varied/have more varied move sets, although I have only reached the 2nd level so far so I have not seen all the enemies in the game
-Could possibly be more optimized, as I lose some frame rate here and there
-That's all so far

I'm posting this review to try and counter the negative reviews from users who have only played for little over a half an hour, only to refund and leave a negative review stating "I don't like rogue-likes."
Your review doesn't help the devs improve the game
Your review doesn't help people make an informed decision on whether to get the game
Give the game a fair chance and if you really don't like it, leave REAL reasons in your review so that maybe those things will be improved
If you don't wanna do that, and just want to leave negative reviews on indie games to be a troll and an ***hole, well......you're a troll and an ***hole
Thanks and enjoy RAD!\t\t\t\t\t
