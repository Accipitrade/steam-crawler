\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNEUTRAL REVIEW ******

Before judging me by my playtime, hear me out.

I've been anticipating this game RAD for ages ( exaggerating ), few hours ago I've finally decided I'm gonna buy this game despite some of the negative reviews regarding some major problems in the game. This might not sound convincing but, many people have mentioned that the game will begin to drop in framerates the longer you play, however I did not encounter this problem for as long as I was playing. I think this is still a really really great game nonetheless. I just think it's not for me, of course it does boil down to personal preference.

Pros :

+ good visuals ( retro vibe )
+ good audio ( music and dialogue )
+ creative approach ( gameplay )
++++ the sound effect when you press pause, or enter a game, that's really special and retrospective
+ varieties in mutations, really really fun
+ challenging ( might differ if using with a controller )

Cons :
- really bad control system for Keyboard and Mouse ( was hoping for similiar control system as Dota )


Overall : very solid game, definitely worth at full price in my opinion. I just couldn't get over the clunky and awkward controlling for Keyboard and Mouse, sorry. Unless they rework it, I'll never touch it again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRAD is a game with a lot of character, plenty of style, and a lot of potential. Structured in a similar fashion to other all-star rogue-lites like Binding of Isaac, it carries the player through short levels rife with secrets, threats, arenas, events, and shops. As something to run through, it's a blast, and I'm having a great time running through it.

Here's the rub though: A few runs into Isaac, it takes off the mask and cackles at you, saying "Hah, now there are three NEW floors! And two-to-three more even after that!"

Thus far, RAD has not done that- runs are much shorter than they could be, and by the time you really feel like you've got your character figured out, the game just sort of.. ends. That's not to say these things won't come in the future, and comparing it to what could be considered the iterative (over years and years) magnum opus of this style of rogue-lite just after it has been unboxed would be pretty unfair.

It has potential. It's fun, even if the runs are a little short. Once you've figured out how to find secrets and started to get a feel for items and abilities you want, it's a blast. I'll absolutely be keeping an eye on it to see if it expands in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRAD is a game I really wanted to like more than I ultimately did. It's not a bad game by any means but for a roguelite it's extremely shallow and you'll see pretty much everything there is to see in three complete runs. Yes there are some powers to unlock and different bats and player skins but many of the powers are either underwhelming or straight up bad, there's one bat obviously better than the rest (the meat), and player skins don't actually change how you play like Binding of Isaac characters.

There are only three biomes and every run plays out exactly the same. Money is easy to stockpile so you can easily acquire more of the passive mutations every run. And lord help you if you don't get a damage mutation right away as some bosses are terrible to fight melee. On top of that many mutations straight up don't work on bosses so what's the point?

I wish I could give this a neutral recommendation because it has a cool theme, looks nice, has some neat ideas, and plays pretty well. It just doesn't have any staying power and like many Double Fine games completely peters out towards the end. Roguelikes and roguelites live and die by their variety and that just isn't here. Also massive pro tip: don't fight anything in the final biome. You can only get so many active mutations so there's just no point which makes every ending a mad dash through jump pad hell.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was sceptical that this would just be an over simplified hack and slash rogue-like given that this 'bat' you're given seems to be portrayed as your primary weapon. In reality, the mutations you acquire for killing enemies feel like your primary source of damage as they are safer to use from distance, then you weave in melee attacks when you've fully understood the enemy's attack patterns as your main resource to conserve is your health.

The game just feels, looks and sounds nice. Hitting enemies with your bat feels and sounds meaty, the 80s soundtrack is a nice accompaniment (I swear I could hear sound bites from 'Beat It' and the art style, whilst subjective, has a lot of detail to it.

So beat enemies with a combination of your melee bat and up to 3 mutations per run, beat a boss, progress to the next world until you complete a run...or die (but at least you'll get some unlockables such as new shop items or augmented melee bats to help on your next runs).

9/10.\t\t\t\t\t
