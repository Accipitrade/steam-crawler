A mix of love and hate

RAD is a top-down action roguelite game set in a radioactive wasteland. As the youthful teenagers, players must battle through various dangerous terrains and numerous lethal mutants in order to help the last of humanity survive the apocalypse. But beware, each time you descend down the road, the radiation will drastically mutate your body, for better or worse. Learn to live in harmony with these newly-found "benefits" is the key of survival. Adorned with 80s style and some whacky humor, RAD delivers a decent roguelike experience. Exploring the toxic dust bowl is fun and challenging, but some enemies really need some adjustment; the mutations you get from radiation can offer you different approaches to combat efficiency, but to unlock them all you need playthrough the game several time. Even though some aspects of the game in my opinion need to be tweaked, I still recommend this game to those are looking for a taste of both roguelike and top-down experience.

Things I Like About RAD:

1. Mutation
The core mechanic and the best aspect of the game. During your excavation throughout the badlands, defeating enemies will reward you progress of the mutation gauge. Once full, you will be rewarded with a mutation toward any body parts of yours. Hideous they may be, disturbing they may look, the mutations are your powerful allies. From the immunity to environmental hazards to additional physique boost; from a boomerang made of flesh and bone to your head literally on fire, there are many ways the mutation can assist you in battle. You need to adapt their playstyle, and mix them up with other "modifications" to create deadly combo. My favorite combo so far is Withering(Emit gas to slow enemies down. Can also be ignited.) pairs up with Warhead(Throw your head as an incendiary grenade.). The massive crowd control you gain from this combo is invaluable. It helps you clean up weaker enemies, and heavily damage the stronger foes with fire DOT. Find the right synergy, and the fortune will always be on your side in battles.

2. Unlockable Characters And Bats
There are 9 unlockable characters in game (11 if you have the DLC.) Though they don't give you any tactical advantages whatsoever, it is still nice to see a change of face rather than keep playing the same green spike hair punk kid every time. There are also unlockable bats, relatively speaking, offering you some small side effects when using, like Death Gobbler give you more rad points when killing enemies, and Blast Wand which let's out a little shockwave each time execute the special attacks.

3. Faster Pace On The Visited Road
This is just a small feature but impactful one in general. When your character walks, tiny bushes and flowers will grow behind your trail. The character will pick up the pace if he/she walks on the them. This feature is wonderful because it not only speeds up the return of exploration, it can also be utilize as evasive maneuver in combat. Walk around the mutants, keeping them in range of your grass trail, then you can easily run in and out to the enemies' attack. One thing you need to look out is that fire can burn along trail, so be wary of any firey presence.

Things I Dislike About RAD:

1. Some Enemies Need To Be Toned Down
Some elite mutants have some nasty, fast attacks that also give them stagger immunity. This design often leads to infinite hit and run tactic, which is acceptable to a certain degree. Once the number picks up, it is really hard to engage close-range combat let along the sheer number of enemies exist late game. Some bosses, as well, are entirely non-friendly to melee combat. They often possess massive shockwave and short attack cool down, meaning they can attack you in rapid succession. These enemies' behaviors in my opinion need to be toned a little, or give us some attack that interrupt them, with a longer cool down timer of course.

2. Reward System
Whether you lose or complete the runs, players will be rewarded with meter progression, which when full, will unlock abilities, artifacts, and customization in return. My main issue about this feature is that it takes away some interesting parts of randomness. Some mutations are locked behind reward meter. You will not receive this mutation during the run until you unlock it post game. Some people may like this feature simply because they have a higher chance of getting some mutation they like. I myself am one of those people before. I too love to receive Armarang mutation five times in a row, but that just takes away the charm of "adapt the randomness" in roguelite game.

3. More than Just Bats?
A nitpick. Whacking some mutants with bat is fun, yet the fun can only last so long after a couple of playthroughs. I appreciate the dev designs some alternative bats with different pros and cons, but the moveset is still the same. I would love to see new weapons besides bats.

4. "Empty" Town
The town is full of funny characters and not much else. The only useful and meaningful people are shop owner who sells you keys and consumables, a goat-head farmer who gives you better consumables but takes longer time, and an ATM for you cash in and out currency before and during the run. That's it. The other town folks are just there to criticize your mutated and distorted body. Nothing else. I really hope the dev can implement more side quests for folks to ask us players to collect and help during runs, something like slowly rebuilding the small colony. Even the kids' playground should be used as a character selection menu of some sort. You can just go there and choose a different one instead of quitting to the main menu and start a new one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a showcase of truly incompetent game design. The art style is amazing and the game is bug free, but every design choice made in this game fights against the player.
I rarely give a game the negative but i really think no one should play this.

Close combat roguelikes play one of 2 ways. Either health comes easy so its a balance between how many hits you take vs how fast you kill.

Other way it goes is you dodge\\block gracefully to avoid most hits because health is scares.

Rad will force you down melee animations without possibility to cancel or block. Its a problem because its also very stingy with its health pickups.

This means that running around till its safe for a swing is your only option. You end up with stupid 50 minute runs of mostly running circles to use your clunky melee animation. Not very fun.

Best option is to get a range weapon early which do even less damage than melee, locking you into those great hour long runs of mostly cheesing every fight.

A potential for faster runs comes in the form of passive buffs. But the design also falters there by giving you a coin flip chance to get a debuff instead. A massive game ending debuff like the one making you unable to heal through half of the scarce pickups.

These runs are not very hard either. Its why I accuse RAD of poor design, clearly the "intented" way to play is this slow as molasses 90% run away and 10% fight style of play. If you think you are in for a fast, action heavy roguelike or even a slow and strategic one you are in for a bad time.

I love DF but they tend to favour unique ideas and leave the gameplay loop to suffer.
I imagine a team of amazing artists with one of them drawing the short straw and gets stuck with designing the gameplay mechanics. Unfortunately roguelikes are 100% mechanics.

So in short here's how you play this.
Just reset until you get a usable active mutation then run circles around enemies loosing no health this way. Avoid all buff altars to avoid rotten luck as even possitive ones are useless half of the time. Stack any HP up you can find from stores and an hour later you get a crazy cool looking boss with fantastic aesthetics and art style. Stomp him and you win.

I hate that I can point at a hundreds better roguelikes from rather small and possibly newer game devs that do a far better job than Double Fine...a seasoned development house.\t\t\t\t\t

My third run got a horrifying but useful mutation and I almost managed to get through the second stage before being silly and dying. To those who choose to pick this up, have fun with it. I know I have.\t\t\t\t\t
