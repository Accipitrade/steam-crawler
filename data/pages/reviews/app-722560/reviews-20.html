\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far the game is hella enjoyable. I'm still early into the progression but so far they is a great diversity with the mutations and abilities. The gameplay is fun, but if you're using keyboard I recommend changing some of the bindings such as making the abilities different buttons and making dodge right-click. I also recommend increasing the camera distance in the settings to max. The settings in this game have some very useful features, so I would definitely ignore the monkeys complaining about controls and camera and instead just optimize them to your preferences.

The visuals and combat are incredible and give a refreshing experience from most other rogue-likes, having close-up 3D combat with different abilities every playthrough. I also haven't had any performance issues or bugs so far so it seems like the game is very cleanly built.
I highly recommend it to anyone who enjoys rogue-likes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYet another rogue-like with procedurally generated levels that draws inspiration from titles like Binding of Isaac, that sadly falls short in many ways.

One of the main issues I have with this game is the enemy "variety"; sure, there's a handful of different enemies, but most of them are just recolors or elemental variants of the enemies from the first level. The few bosses I fought were unique comparatively, but that isn't enough to justify a lack of enemy variety. Another issue is the visuals; some of the visuals do pop (as shown in the screenshots), but at times the textures look unfinished and unpolished, even with everything at the highest graphical settings.

While there's a few more issues I have with the game, playing made me realize that I could be playing Hades, an infinitely better dungeon crawler with procedurally generated floors/levels that's in early access on the Epic Games Launcher (and Hades is the only good thing to come out of that cursed launcher imo) and is being developed by Supergiant games.

Overall, the game is mediocre at best with some well needed work on the textures and some actual enemy variety.\t\t\t\t\t

First Impressions

Played through a couple of short runs and wasn't particularly impressed with what I've seen. For the $25.99 CAD price tag, I was expecting a little more than a relatively uninspired cookie cutter rogue like. I think if a 3D rogue like is a game you are itching for, there are better options out there.

Pros:

- The Landscape and setting style is pretty interesting.

- Mutations, which are like new items in Isaac, come from killing enemies rather than just finding them.


Cons:

- The enemies and characters in this game just look like huge contrast blobs of color.

- The camera in the game is not controlled by the player, it follows at a variable height, changing its zoom level depending on where you move to. The issue with this is that it slightly changed my feeling of where the range is with the baseball bat, leading to a lot of swing and misses if I was paying more attention to the enemies than to how far out the camera has zoomed.

- The gameplay mechanics seem very generic to roguelikes. Floppy disks are keys, money is money. There are shops around. there are unlockable chests around.

- Some incredibly annoying voice overs keep telling me some nonsense about the back story that sounds entirely like gibberish. Nothing about the backstory captures my interest whatsoever.

- Biggest con of course is the pricetag. I don't think that you will get the value from this game to fulfil its asking price. From what I've heard from people who played the beta, there isn't much depth to create replayability. Beware of micro DLC's to add content.



Generally speaking this game is seemingly just bleh at a high asking price. The gameplay feels like a bad ARPG and the mechanics are generic and uninspiring. The style is just a spam of high contrast cartoony stuff.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is pretty fun. Unlocking mutations as you play the game killing creatures... These mutations are similar to the upgrades from Binding of isaac and change how you attack throughout the game.

Unfortunately, my playthrough was cut short due to after playing for a certain amount of time... The game starts to lag as if there was a memory leak or something. (Was even getting this stuttering at the start so lowered the settings).

People will be like don't buy this game because of the past mistakes of Double Fine not finishing some games... But who cares about that. All that matters is if the game is good. I'd say buy the game. If the game is as unstable as it is for me, probably get a refund and when the game becomes more optimised then choose to purchase it. So at this time i wouldn't recommend it.

Extra Info: Followed some of the comments of that people did as a reply. Such as restarted my PC to see if this issue would be fixed. This didn't work. The game works perfectly on the first level... Until the second level then the frame rate starts to drop over time.

Jynx also gave some vital information as there was an incompatibility with certain graphics cards that were also chewing up GPU graphics power.\t\t\t\t\t
