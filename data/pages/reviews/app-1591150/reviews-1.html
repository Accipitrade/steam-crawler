\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn balance this game just wasn't worth the price of admission personally. Whilst it was billed as being a nostalgia trip to a 90's arcade, everything fell just too short of the mark. The whole arcade itself just feels lifeless and empty with most of the machines seeming to be there just as space filler rather than actually doing anything. I'm not sure if that changes later on but I felt it wasn't worth continuing past the refund window to find out. What did work in the roughly an hour I spent in the game was just dull and uninspiring. Essentially all that I managed to play with was a skeetball machine, a basketball hoop, a claw machine, a pinball and a single arcade machine. There's also an air hockey table but I didn't get to try that. You can see all of these in the first video for the game on the store page. Beyond that the escape room element held some appeal but not enough that I could see myself coming back to the game at all.

Overall it's really not worth it and feels like a good idea poorly executed. There are some good elements but unfortunately none of them help to detract from everything that's missing.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had a good time. I do recommend, of course, to play Escape from the 80s. Now I will say, i did prefer the first one, but i played them both in a row. They do go together well. Like the first one, you go from clue to clue, trying to follow the tracks left by someone who has been abducted. Like the first one it is not a too serious game, it is lighthearted, but this time, the setting is more arcade oriented (as in, this is literally set at an old arcade). The game can be finished quickly, but if you played the first one, you have to play this now.

About the end of the game, I will say that I kinda breezed through the end sequence without any trouble. I was confused by what was going on for a second, but it was funny, and that is good. However, i was expecting a bit more. Not more fighting, but maybe a puzzle element tied into the fight. Something to figure out. But maybe the ending is more meant as a reward than a final step, i don't know, I'm not a game developer.\t\t\t\t\t
