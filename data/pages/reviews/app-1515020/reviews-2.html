\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game will be a lot of fun once it is optimized. There are a few bugs, which the Dev is really active in Steam threads fixing quickly. I have had one lock up, no crashes in 7+ hours. The story is interesting. The characters are likable. The artwork is good. The voice acting is good. The combat is quick (some times too quick, I don't even have time to click on their healing potions before they die, but there is a slider in Options to adjust it). There is no perma-death for your mercs. I think even after the first nerf the game is a little too hard on Easy, as I am lossing to the 4th invasion of my base camp. If you like rogue-like death games that are difficult, by all means, pick this up now. I think this game will be good after some more optimization, but if, like me, you prefer to have an easy mode or a story mode, then you might want to wait awhile. Maybe the Dev can add a Story Mode easier than Easy? LOL. I am not a huge fan of autobattlers and prefer to manage my troops fighting individually, so I am not greatly experienced in the combat of this game. I can do little to "fix" my team so far.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHavent played a lot yet, but so far the game seems pretty fun.

The story so far is fully voiced, though the quality of the actors and the audio varies as you'd expect from a small production team. It didn't bother me, but it was noticable.

The combat plays out similar to Riot Games' Team-Fight Tactics. Each hero has some traits that can overlap with another hero, and stacking the same traits grants exceedingly powerful team bonuses. Each Hero also has an element, which makes them weak or strong versus certain other elements, rock-paper-scissors style. This combination of factors makes it fun to find the "best" team for each fight.

Out of combat and story, the questing and exploration system seem pretty straight-forward. Do quests to gain points, spend points to unlock areas, areas grant more quests.

Finally, the guild is a place you can improve over time to empower your team in different ways. So far, I've not really dove deep enough to give a full rundown, but it looks like a fine, if somewhat basic, support structure to help you shape the details of your team.\t\t\t\t\t
