\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn obvious title reference to the Konami Code from NES' "Contra", 99Vidas takes a lot of inspiration from classic 16-bit 2-D fighting games and rolls it into a Streets of Rage wannabe game. I say "wannabe" because this games wants to emulate everything that was fun about Streets of Rage, but falls short at almost every turn.

99 Vidas can be summarized in the following phrase: "Hey, you remember that really great game Streets of Rage 2? Am I reminding you of that great game? Glad I could remind you! Now here, play me while you're constantly reminded of a better game."

The fighting isn't fun, the music feels uninspired, and the gameplay is a little lacking in nuance. I really wanted to like this game, as I'm subscribed to Izzy Nobre and I'm a big fan of nostalgic gaming. I'm a HUGE Streets of Rage fan, so this should've been a homerun for me.

However, this game seemed to miss a few things, which I will go into detail.

Pacing

The game falls short in terms of pacing the dialogue, intro, and game play. Initially, you are mostly forced to watch the introduction which has a bizarre nature in how it displays the dialogue. It might have only been my experience, but I couldn't skip through or fast-forward how the dialogue printed out on the screen during the intro, so I had to sit there and wait for a lot of boring, uninspired dialogue on the screen.

I felt like I was reading dialogue that was written to be funny for a few friends, and not a wide audience. That's all fine and good, but I was mostly bored.

Fighting

While the game itself ran smoothly, I felt like the fighting was really boring. The enemies would randomly render my character unmovable and it didn't seem like, unless I wanted to do a lot of running around, there wasn't much of a strategy that fostered any fun.

Music/Sound design

Some of the music tracks were nearly lifted straight from Streets of Rage. I know they were inspired by Streets of Rage 2, but this is where I started really not having a fun time. The tracks just weren't really fun to listen to. I got a little smirk here and there, like "Oh, they're doing a thing where they're reminding me of another SoR level. Heh." and then it would repeat.

Also, did no one do any quality checks before publishing this game? Was was with that insanely loud boss sounds? The first boss with his footsteps "TINK TINK TINK TINK" abso-freaking-lutly BLARING out of my speakers while everything else was at a modest volume level. Same goes for the second boss. Every time he flipped around, only that sound was absolutely blaring through my speakers.

The 2nd Boss Fight

What am I supposed to do? Wait for him to get tired of throwing barrels at me? Spend 20 minutes using chip damaged from afar while he constantly flips around? I can't get close to him. I can't fight him either, as he uses a move to push me back. So I have to spend ~5 lives just to kill him and now I have nothing left. I was convinced that I'm not going to have fun playing this game after that boss fight.

Either the developer's friends/testers didn't tell the dev to fix his game or whoever this game went through before publishing it were incompetent or ignorant. I might go back and try this game again with another character, but I have a feeling I still won't like it.

I do not recommend this game to anyone. Go buy/download Streets of Rage 2 and play that instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's pretty fun. Good art, and music.
The enemies get a little repetitive though, you'll be seeing a lot of the same throught the levels. The bosses are decent, but can have little window to attack them, and even when you do attack, sometimes they don't get stunned. Co-op is fun, but double the enemies can make for a bit of a hassle, and can be hard to track.

One problem that was very punishing for me is the fact that you can't get any lives once you start a new level. I got stuck on the final level with only 2 lives and couldn't get any more, resulting in me having to start over.

However, I appreciate the Sega Genesis soundfont being used for the music, and the pixel art is very high quality. Overall, a fairly good game. Start with easy mode though. It gets tough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t99Vidas bring us back to a classic game genre, the amazing beat em up! Despite the fact that some of the new games of this genre come in a 3D platform, 99Vidas throw us back to the classical arcade. We have a good amount of characters to choose, and each has their own element, like fire, lightning, water and rock. The side scrolling movement, urban fights and pixel style sound like a good game to remember the classics. But it is not only a rescue of the past; it has its own features like upgrade purchases, characters to unlock and 3 types of game mode. 99Vidas
is a great game for anyone who wants to remember the good old beat em up.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a total waste of time
The mechanics is so poorly design that it manages to give a frustrating experience without giving a challenge. Misalign the character to the enemy by a couple of pixel and you can't hit them. Grabbing is so random that whenver you run into an enemy, you don't know who will grab who. Some attacks just do not allow you to do anything, no block or dodging possible, and if you're unlucky then you might get rushed by one after another. Health points are placed thoughtlessly without any real value, often so late that it costs a life before the health pickup is reached, making it useless. Different combos are just useless as none of them deal as much damage, easy and safer to use as just mashing the attack button. There is no strategy whatsoever apart from just spamming buying lives.
