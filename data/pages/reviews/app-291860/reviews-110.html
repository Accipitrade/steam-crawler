\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike all Behemoth games, the story is absolutely ridiculous, and the, um, 'narration' and dialogue are both zany and... jerk-y. As opposed to a side-scroller (Castle Crashers) and a platformer (BattleBlock Theater), this game is a fairly quick, turn-based strategy game with a highly explorable overworld (an a fun feature in which one can avoid random encounters, thus lowering any consequential grinding). The controls are incredibly simple in their mechanics, making it easy to learn and fun to play, and the customisation options are truly a masterpiece of conglomerated bodily components to create uniquely designed characters. There is still challenging aspects to the game that do require a full consideration of enemy movements in regard to one's own pawns- I mean "players"- actions, and also do your side quests as well as your primary quests. Cough. Anyways, thus far, I am incredibly pleased with this game and am excited for its completion.

EDIT: So upon the completion of the game, my opinion hasn't really changed. It's still very fun and the story (yes, shockingly, there is a story with a real ending) is actually pretty fulfilling in the end. The replay value feels a little low but I think it's still there with its side quests and stuff, and it feels like a game to look back on like, oh this was fun, I'll play this again. Definitely still recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is brilliant. This is definitely your go to game if you are looking for something to quench your thirst for dark humor, sarcasm, and the absurd. Even though in terms of game play all it has to offer ultimately is repetitive fast paced role play, the kind that you would eventually try as hard as you can to avoid (If you've played games like final fantasy IX, you will know what I am talking about) what it lacks in game play depth or story depth it makes up for in the ingeniously hilarious sense of humor and diversity of collectable items, that again, are well thought of to come off as unexpected, absurd, and greatly reflective of the wit of the game creators. It keeps you playing because you want to experience more of its humor and you are left wondering where the game will take you, because anything goes and anything can happen. If you are into products like rick and morty YOU WILL ENJOY THIS. If, like me, you have enjoyed previous games by Behemoth, especially for their fun, cute and funny art style and witty sense of humor you will enjoy this because it is an upgrade of both. And these 2 things coupled with the outstanding job of the voice actor definitely make it stand out as a game. These Behemoth games should even be classified as a whole distinctive genre on their own. Worth every singly penny. Obviously many aspects can be improved, many things cam be integrated over time nevertheless even in its crude form it is still a gem.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHilarious strategy game made by The Behemoth.
Likes
-Various races of units made to fill different roles. (Healer, buffer, saboteur, dps, etc)
-You get to pick the equipment of the Humans/Demi-clops/Cyclops so you can customize the role they play to your liking
-Tons of cosmetic choices make your team actually feel like YOUR team.
-Some characters can be found with rare text animations and colors for their name.
-No options to rename characters making the hunt for character names you like yet another thing to hunt for.

Dislikes
-Some races of creatures (Zombies, Gnomes and Kobolds) come in pairs. Currently there isn't a way to trade members of those pairs with the same creatures. For instance, lets say you have a Zombie pair - Hearts(in special red text)/Peanut. If you wanted to switch "Hearts" into another zombie pair, you can't.
-There are tons of equipment but very few of them have elemental properties.
-There's a gold cap of 19,999\t\t\t\t\t
