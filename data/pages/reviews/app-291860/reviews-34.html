\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a hard one to review, whille i love the art and humor of behemoth, this game also has a lot of problems on it's core design, let's go on parts.

Story and side quests, honestly the saving grace of this game for me, some missions are boring but those are few, mostly of them have that humor from behemoth the we know and love.

The units, after about 1 hour gameplay or even less you probally have seen all that has to be seen about units, it get's even worse when you realize that running a team of full humans is better than mixing all of the already small roster of units.

loot doesn't get much better, you do have stuff that changes combat for an extent but also falls short since when you got one of those the rest are pure cosmetic so when you get a new sword "oh a new sword that do the exact same amount of damage that the one i already have but it looks better, neat"

Exploration, gets repetitive real fast, after a while you just stop looking for stuff and head straight to quests.

NOW the combat, oh boy THE COMBAT, THE STRATEGY that this game lacks. I can't really even express how BORING combat is, it was fun for the frist 1 hour of gameplay with the narrator going nuts when you got multiple kills etc, but when you realize it's going to be like that all the time with no new mechanics being introduced as you progress the magic ends. it's so simple, there's no real strategy other than making sure your guys don't get in a position where they would have to chose targets, but at the same making sure that your opponent have to choose between two or more of your units. For those who don't know, you move your units but you can't choose who to attack, the unit does that.

Difficulty: there's basicly none, and that's why most ''hard'' missions are just a bunch of waves rushing at you, and this aggravates the gameplay since combat is already so simplist.

Final point, if you don't mind using the same units over and over again, the super casual aproach to an stratagy game, you will have a great time on the pit, otherwise avoid it.

Oh and yeah this game has online and local co-op so that's always a plus.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoy games made by this game dev but THIS game has some annoying aspects that remind me of mobile games, namely the very repetitive nature of combat, the pushing people to play daily, and lots of upgrades that barely affect game play.

The lack of skill or talent trees make this less of an RPG and more of bashing your skull against the wall to level up a character you want to use because you got them at level 1 and you are level 40...

Some of the character interactions are interesting, namely archers riding spiders, but the battle system seems very shallow and small (if you could keep recruiting teammates and have big battles with 8+ toons it'd be significantly more palatable) instead you play the same 5 toons, pretty much the whole time, and use them the same way because its the way that works without you having to go back to town after every fight to top off your health. Also the game is pretty funny, but the narrator kinda stops being funny after a while.

I'm not MAD i bought this game, and I'm not upset enough to ask for a refund or anything. This is because I hope the devs will invest some time to make it better, but its been out for a while, so I'm not exceptionally hopeful of this possibility.\t\t\t\t\t
