The Behemoth Keeps Impressing

The Early Access title scared me a bit, I've been let down with other Early Access titles.

However, The Behemoth seem able to even get Early Access down right. The game has good content, classic to the dev's unique personality and humor, and the game is a blast to play. I admit I wish there was more to the main story at present, but I also have faith in these developers that more quality content is to be expected.

For now, the strategy element is engaging while maintaining a casual, zany atmosphere. The game is hilarious and fun to play, obviously very similar to Castle Crashers and the previous titles as far as animation and artistic design goes. The game plays well, keeping up the pace while still paying respects to turn based strategy. The customization options for fighters are plenty entertaining, and playing co-op and versus with friends is fantastic.

I'm going to keep playing this game more than enough time to warrant fifteen bucks.
Highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing it for a while, dropping it, and then going back to play it again, allow me to provide an updated review.
For the record, I do know its an early access game. Ok.

Take the art style and humor of Castle Crashers with 100% less poop jokes thank the heavens. Weave said elements into Fire Emblem; and you have Pit People.

The game wastes no time thrusting you into the plot; which is fairly straightforward, and cliched in nature but hey. You're not actually here for the plot, lets be alarmingly real with ourselves.

You're here for the sense of humor, and it delievers.
The gameplay is b a s i c a l l y older Fire Emblem. Grid based chess with everyone usually having a weakness; but unlike FE; they don't throw a bunch of numbers and stats at you (To take words from Konjin; attacks don't really enter double digits much so when you get slapped down the map for 15, it has a legit impact, so yeah you're not hit with tons of numbers) and more importantly; planning isn't really needed.
You don't know how much damage any shot is gonna do, nor if it'll even hit (if you're running an archer or similar for example)
You don't know how far each dude can move.
You don't really know what the items dude have can do until you get hit by it and see you're poisoned suddenly.
You don't even know how many life points you have. You just kinda exist in a limbo of not being too sure.

The AI isn't great here. If you're running a pegasus knigh in FE; you main focus is gonna be where the archers are; and all that, ranges, and the best way to stab them. Here; you don't know how far dudes can move, you can see the archers but as for their range; you really don't know. You can only guess. And even if you guess wrong there is no risk. Here, Vampiress' are damaged more by arrows but they don't get targeted?
At least it doesn't seem like they do? Maybe I'm wrong. I likely am.

Nonetheless; in FE if you threw a flying horse anywhere close to the range of an archer; the thing will get shot right out of the sky.
the AI I guess doesn't want to target weaknesses; and it barely targets more hurt dudes. Heck, fellows with swords will go after helmeted protags despite the fact that they're at a disadvantage whilst doing so.

I think the thing I dislike the most about this entire game is the fact that the fighters you control will choose who to fight.
Yes. That not-good AI basically rolls a dice and smacks around how it wishes. This was done, I know, so that the squids will have an actual purpose, but that gimmick is only ever ok. Its hilarious to see it work, but its only ok. The AI needs fixed for this effect to mean anything more really, and its only useful against the AI, since all you need to do is force the afflicted dude away. So; I have two ideas: remove squids, and let us have full control over our fighters. Let us decide who dies.


Another bothersome flaw with this here game is relocating the city. Why, exactly? So that you don't get used to the map? To present the illusion of having a limited amount of time to captures dudes to your cause? I haven't the foggiest. (inspired by another review)


Each mission is super FE. Its all the same and thats only a bad thing if you see it that way. Let me channel my innter Yahtzee: You effectively go to the place and stab the lads.
Lets be clear. 3/4ths of the game is exactly that.
Each quest provides a funny reason as to why you went to the place and stab the lads, but in the end, you're still just going to the place to stab the lads.
You can save one lad to live to be captured to your side if you have a cage and you will because they're cheap.
This mechanic is awesome and I really enjoy it.
The lads that you can play as, are a varied as they come. From Gorons, to vampiress, to your run of the mill swordsmen and archers, to cyclops with big ol mallets, to wraths (a personal favorite) that offer debuffs at a range; to cupcakes that fling themselves to heal others.

The cosmetics they have are staggering an amusing in their own right, the weapons you get are in their own classes and are wildly creative and also funny, and each dude you kill has a random name.
Generally you choose weapons based off of what they look like. No joke. Execpt that i s a joke.
I can say whatever I want about anything, right?

But time, effort, and love was pouring into each line of code of this game.
Some of the items and cosmetics are named after the long gone memes of yesteryear but irony is alive and well.
I will wear the hat that looks like a Shiba Inu, which flashes the word 'wow' around itself and dang nabbit I will stab the lads.

early access/10\t\t\t\t\t

pros:
-the narrator is simply excellent, the monologue(or dialogue) is well-thought and perfect
-the turn-based combat is very good and easy to learn
-the tutorials are very clear and simple
-strategy is really the key to victory
-loyalty rewards (such as battleblock theater skins)
-classic behemoth humor is still there as always and very appreciated

cons:
-the UI seems not adapted for PC
-character stats are a bit hard to understand at first
-does not autosave often enough
-some elements seems pixelated (low quality graphics) even if running ultra settings (I know it's still early access)

conclusion:
Buy it, it is worth your money
awesome game made by awesome people.
looking forward to future updates and additional content.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People is XCOM, Fire Emblem, and Pokemon thrown into the minds of Behemoth and what they made is a funny and good looking game. The meat of the game is doing battle with teams of bad guys with your very own squad that you find through the story mode until you find Sophia the Recruitador (a lot of characters in this game have funny names). Then, the game branches into another way of playing it. You can capture any unit you fight on the battlefield when you leave it as the last one alive. There are several character classes from the human fighters to the penguins to the beefy giants to the cupcake healer class. Every character has their own name from Gluten to Pipistrella.

This is an early access title as of January, and it shows only in the story mode. The rest of the game is polished and balanced with each weapon type and class, but the story mode is farly short. What it lacks in length it makes up in character and gameplay. The narrator Stamper was present in their previous game Battleblock Theater as well but he takes the role of a godlike bear that overlooks the story as a sort of meta-narrator, talking to the characters and talking to the player. Apart from the short story quests, there are a few side quests that are all five parts incrasing in difficulty. These serve the same purpose as side quests in different games, to give you some more gold and flesh out the characters. These side quests take a bit longer than the story.

Apart from the insanely fun singleplayer, there exists an in-universe multiplayer PvP called the Pit, which is pretty much the only reference to the title of the game. You and a partner control your squads to defeat two other people's squads. It's a very satisfying game mode as you can learn from the other people's squad composition to increase chances of success. Think XCOM PvP. You get story rewards as well depending on how many games you win per day. As it is now, Pit People is very fun and well balanced even though the story mode was short and as sweet as the cupcake healer class.\t\t\t\t\t
