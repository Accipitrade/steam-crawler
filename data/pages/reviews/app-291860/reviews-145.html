\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had little interest in a strategy game that didn't allow you to control WHAT your character does and to WHO. However, it's the streamlined movement and flow of this game that really started to make sense when you begin to macro-manage rather then expect to Micro-manage.

In XCOM you gamble on each unit's interaction with the enemy, with this game it's 100% all the time, but the gamble is confined to player positioning. The depth in this game is less conventional and prone to be overlooked based on it's hands off approach to it's combat, but it allows players to use their decision making skills in other interesting and meaningful ways rather than being bogged down by busy work.

Anyone can access this game, even players who are not fans of strategy games can hypethetically start doing well almost immediately... as the rules required to learn this game aren't clouded with trivialities and unecessary factors or UI elements. The better you are at making decisions, the better you'll stand playing this game.\t\t\t\t\t
