\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People isn't even completed yet, and it fills me with all kinds of happy! And blueberries.

I normally avoid Early Access and just wait for a finished product where I can plainly see if it's reliable and entertaining or not, but I have a lot of confidence in The Behemoth, and watching TotalBiscuit & Jesse Cox's co-op playthrough footage convinced me that the game is indeed in a good enough state for Early Access.

It is absolutely my kind of humor, especially with Stamper's quality narration, but I am also adoring the game on a mechanical level. It's simple enough to where you're not bogged down with tons of tedious information, but still challenging enough to where you need to be aware of positioning and planning (before and during battle), or a Pixie may just burn off the faces of your entire team.

Conducting battles is mentally stimulating, WINNING battles is often deeply satisfying, and the humor is the sweet, delicious frosting on the cupcake that just makes it that much better in the end!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWithout a doubt, this is one of the best RPGs I've played in a long while – the art and animation is hilarious and distinctive, the combat is simple but incredibly addictive, the writing is absolutely brilliant, and the music is just really damn funky. To top it all off, it's got a hilarious narrator in the form of StamperTV as a giant space bear, who I think has done some great voice-over in other Behemoth games.

There are just so many little details crammed in, like the rainbow unicorn creatures leaving a poo trail as they drag their arses along the ground, to the cord of the giant space phone that you use for multiplayer co-op trailing off into space on the world map, to the fact that the giant space bear actually picks up the whole hub city and moves it to a completely different world map at random now and then just for ♥♥♥♥♥ and giggles. Yes, none of that will make sense until you play it. Please do.

Worth noting is the fact that, as an early-access game, the actual main story is barely there, but Behemoth made up for this by packing in a goldmine of delightfully random side-quests that should keep you going for around 10-20 hours, more if you go all-out on finding all the hidden secrets and recruiting all the potential characters.

All in all, if they can make me adore an early-access game this much, then I await the finished product with fear and awe.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is awesome! The story and humor are what you would expect from a The Behemoth game (only played Battleblock, not Castle Crashers). Game play is relatively simplistic on the surface but there's a lot of potential for thought and optimisation during play and I assume especially for PvP.

I likened the game to friends as Final Fantasy Tactics mashed with Pokemon with the art and humor of Battleblock.

Worth a try even during early access as the game is very polished. If you can appreciate The Behemoth storytelling, this is for you, and if you like the idea of turn-based strategy games, this will be right up your alley as well.

Also, the music is just great and so fitting in the game. If that's something that you like in a game, then the music just adds another reason to give it a go!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game! Oh boy!
Behemoth has done it again, this time they hit what could quite possibly be one of their most engrossing ands fluid experiences. I've owned and played all of their games (excluding Alien Hominid) and have to say, this is easily tied for my favorite of their games.
Strategic, fast-paced fun, that's all there is to it. Behemoth's signature quirky style and humor gives this game an edge on other TRPG wannabes on the steam store.

A few notes for the Devs though, if they're reading!
As of Home Improvement 1, there is no skip turn button in combat. While it's nothing major, it does severely effect the flow of the game for TRPG veterans, or people who understand how the turn will play out. Since movement is fairly.. Slow, due to low movement ranges and large maps, this makes a very tedious experience during overworld encounters, and while most will be fine with it, it'd definitely be a welcome change.
