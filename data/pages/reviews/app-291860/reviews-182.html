\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust what you'd expect from The Behemoth. This game is amazing! Despite it being early access and in reality there is very little to it right now, I've had a lot of fun with it.

IF YOU BUY THIS EARLY, DO KNOW THAT THERE IS ONLY THE GENERAL SET OF TUTORIAL QUESTS AND WHAT FEELS LIKE ONLY 1 REAL STORY QUEST.

Most of my time playing has been spent just grinding out battles in order to level up my team, otherwise there really isn't much else to do other than the Unfair Pit and repeatable bonus missions. The Unfair Pit seems quite fun, but even with a full team of above level 20's I barely made it past round 1 and it could also be noted that I'm probably not amazing at the game anyway.

As far as random encounters go, I feel like it would be nice to see even more variety in some of the enemies. There are a few different races you encounter throughout the game, but the only difference between characters of the same race are there helmets mostly and even then it really isn't all that crazy. Each race of character has their own style of attacking and special attributes, and there are actually quite a few different races, but after playing for awhile I would either get a lack of variety in the enemies I was facing or cool races just wouldn't show up at all.

This game is early access so I wasn't expecting it to be all together or anywhere near a complete game. I've had a lot of fun with the game so far, but until its full releases I don't think I'll be playing too much now.

I absolutely recommend getting this game now while it's cheaper though. It is an insane amount of fun and it's only in early access so it can only get better from here.\t\t\t\t\t

I played the game during closed Beta and loved it. It is a marriage of the following things:
Tactical Combat, RPG character progression,forming the best party with oodles of different charatcers, loot gathering mechanics, Pokemon-like creature gathering (which also puts a twist on the tactical combat because you can only capture the last enemy left alive), and it reminds me a lot of the Adventure Time cartoon for some reason (the art style) and It has fantastic humor. On top of all of this, it is multiplayer.
If any combination of those things above sound appealing to you, then i would highly recommend this game even in its current EA state. There is a LOT of game to be had here already and it will only grow. Truly a gem of a game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not one for many turn-based combat games. Xcom and Civ are my only exceptions honestly, and I'm not very good at either of them. I do enjoy them nonethless. Pit People was hanging on the edge for me; I love Behemoth and their games and humour, but the whole turn-taking thing was a bit of a step away from their usual slash-fest style. However, I had some money and time to spare so I thought I'd give their new game a real chance, and man, am I thankful I did. Pit People is a wonder (even in early access) to play. The usual from Behemoth can be expected: a crazy and cartoonish art style, over-the-top humour and stupidity, but the new direction this game takes is suprisingly well done and very simple yet fun to play. It doesn't require a whole lot of planning and thought like other turn-based games do. Simply move your characters to where you want them to go and they will attack who they think is the best, provided that they can. You can't target specific enemies, which I thought was awful at first, until I realised that all-in-all, it works very well. Depending on your weapon depends on how you attack. For example, melee weapons such as swords will be just one tile away for an attack to occur, but if a character say throws axes, you need to position them two tiles away instead so they can throw the axes towards an enemy. If they can target multiple enemies at once, they may split the damage if the AI deems it necessary to, or just attack one enemy for more damage. I like this simple formula that avoids the player themselves setting up positioning as well as attacks, and it's very easy once you get the hang of it. The combat isn't the most in-depth ever seen in a turn-based game, but in my opinion, it succeeds because of this. It creates a fast-paced action game where good positioning will win you the game, sort of like chess in a way. The other section is a pokemon styled run-around a map and find things game. You can seek out enemies that then take you into a more focused battle, and the best part is the quests, which allow for a lot of freedom to do what you want to do instead of sectioned levels. The story is crazy just like all of Behemoth's games, and I won't spoil it (even though there is only a certain amount of these story quests available currently) but there's a giant teddy bear that has crashed into the Earth. The narrator as always is ridiculous and funny. Customisation is plentiful and once again, outrageous. The hub town holds a market place for new fighters and weapons, an arena named the pit sort of like a colosseum as well as a home for you to mess with your characters and team.

Overall, awesome game and a real treat to play. Simple, elegant, funny and over-the-top turn-based action with what seems to be a mind-boggling but intriguing story that never lets up on quick remarks, twists and plot. Support this game and Behemoth! They deserve it :D\t\t\t\t\t
