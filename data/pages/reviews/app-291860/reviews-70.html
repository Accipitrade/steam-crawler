\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a good game. Maybe even great. Not amazing.

Vintage Behemoth style and charm. If you liked it before, you still will with this.

Turn-based strategy on a hex map that emphasizes positioning, thoughtful planning, and build creation. The game definitely has a theory crafting element to the build creation; this adds to the play time and enjoyment a lot.

Fun little overworld with decent replayability, but... it can get old.

Simple enough mechanics to not be intimidating, yet it contains adequate unlocks, depth, and complexity to last for quite a while imo. The diversity of unit types is pretty good and interesting. Ofc some are better than others, but each one has its purpose on the right team. The different types synergize well, and keep things interesting with the resistances, strengths, and powerful connections between certain unit types. For example, a classic powerful build is 2 x Kobold, 2 x Pixie, 2 x Vampiress. That is a synergized glass cannon, squishy but deadly sort of team setup. If you plan to get the game or already have it, try that out.

There is challenge to the single player, but it is rarely too burdensome. It is enough that you usually need to think and play strategically, but it won't destroy you mercilessly if you make errors. If a mission is destroying you, your team build itself is likely the main issue.

The party system and characters of different classes, that can use many different gear setups, have a simple, addictive charm. A bit reminiscent of Pokemon collecting/leveling/battling.

The autobattler is quite nice for afking, if you're into that sort of thing. It is certainly suboptimal to knowledgeable, active human play (as it should be), but the AI performs well enough to make it worth using. You can even manually add target priorities.

Co-op adds a lot of fun and strategy to the story/questing/overworld/arena combat.

Overall I would say 7.9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite the light-hearted look (and sound) of the game, I found myself intensely focusing on my screen until the wee hours of the morning. The story is fun. The battles are intense. There's not a lot of fiddly stuff to worry about during battle; just position your guys to achieve the maximum effect. There's a good ramp up in difficulty. The challenge always seems about right. My only complaint is that I found myself Google-ing a ton of basic info that should have been included in the tutorial. When does the game save? How do you leave the pit once you've started a match (left click for OK...welcome to infinite battles mode- please...must...sleep). With the current sale, if you like turn based tactical games and you have a sense of humor...this is for you. Also...I find myself always turning off music in games. In this one, I let the funky beat continue...it's fitting.\t\t\t\t\t
