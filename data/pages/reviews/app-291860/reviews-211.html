\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs expected, "The Behemoth" did another nice work.

PRO:

- There is an open world with quests, and many monsters that you can recruit by using a cage. Your home is always visible on the world map; it's where you find the online/single-player arena, and a market to buy even more characters, and weapons.

- Characters of your team are customizable: the appearance changes by equipping different items, and different items may have passive bonus (like a chance to freeze your enemy, for example); also, items are divided in something like "light", "medium", "hard", so if you choose an item that increase your defense, you are slower because of weight and you can't move as much as another character with a light armor. The same is for weapons.

- You level up every single character, so it's already enough to play thousands of hours.

- Friendly fire based on positioning or area damage.

CONS:

- Personally, i find that graphics is horrible, and not because it's minimal, but because i don't like the style. It's well done and full of humor though.

- The UI is quiet confusing, especially at a first look.

- You can't choose abilities in combat (it's very simple), or who you want to attack: basically you just move characters, and they decide their targets.


It's a solid game overall. You won't regret of its gameplay, contents and quality.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNever thought The Behemoth of all companies would pull an Early Access "GOTCHA!", but here we are.

Sure, I saw the usual Early Access disclaimers, but I've been following this game for 3 years, I thought for sure I'd get 5-10 hours worth of my $15.

NOPE. 3 Years of development has apparently allowed them to finally establish the fundamentals of Pit People, but the game has ZERO CONTENT. You've got your basic intro quest, and ONE STORY QUEST (combined these two things take less than a single hour). Aside from that, you've only got repeatable filler quests, and repeatable arena skirmishes.

Don't get me wrong:
-The dialogue and story is hilarious
-The gameplay is excellent
-The sound and visuals are The Behemoth at its absolute best

But there's only a fart's worth of content so far. If you want to support the company (they WILL finish it eventually I'm sure, unlike other Early Access scams), go right ahead, but don't expect more than a tiny, tiny sneak peak at the game itself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes.

I already loved the style, humor and battle gameplay of this game, but the "capture new recruits" mechanic of the game sealed the deal for me.

The game can be played almost entirely with a regular mouse and this is a huge plus for me. You do have to tweak the controls a bit though and I would have prefered if the mouse 3 button could be used to end the turn by default (instead of just space) but this could still change of course if there is enough demand for it.

It's still a little rough around the edges (eg. menu control feels a bit clunky to me with mouse) but this is to be expected for an early access title and is subject to change. Still, it already plays like a finished product and the fact that things will still improve is very, very promising.

Overall I think Pit People is a really fun game with loads of more potential. I recommend it to anyone who isn't afraid of early access.\t\t\t\t\t
