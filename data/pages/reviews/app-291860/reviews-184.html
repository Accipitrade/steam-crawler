\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Behemoth is back!

I've been a huge fan of this studio since Newgrounds, have played everything they have developed up to this point, and I have to say that Pit People is turning out to be one of the best efforts they have ever made. Castle Crashers was amazing for unlocks and playing through multiple times to find all of the nuances and secrets lying underneath, but after 30-40 hours you have done and seen most of what the game has to offer. Pit People is so different in that aspect, as there are literally 1,000+ items to unlock, a multitude of different characters and species to "recruit", and a billion reasons to sink your time into this game.

As Early Access, the game gives you a very limited view of what is in store for the main story experience, but it vastly makes up for this with an addictive but accessible PVP and super-hard Co-Op pit mode. Not to mention the huge open world that seems to have a lot to discover, and plenty of creatures to add to your team as mentioned before.

There are a few noticable and jarring UI problems with this game. For one, a lot of the stat management and overviews for characters are incredibly subtle. At first, I had no idea that leveling your recruits mattered. There's no representation of your stats growing or what the growth per individual species would be. You'll especially notice this once you recruit a Mascot species type, as their attack damage on the stats page reads a huge number above all other species, but they merely buff other characters and don't do the damage stated on their page. There needs to be more ways to differenciate between the different Clops or Humans you recruit, as well as stat comparison to someone you're wanting to swap in on your team before taking on PvP or the World Map.

The UI is also very difficult to manage with a mouse + keyboard. I have been playing most of this game with the Steam Controller, and I find that method of play to be a lot more enjoyable. Playing on my laptop with a mouse and keyboard, or even my main PC, was a challenge. There's no visual feedback when you're nearing the edge of the screen in the main hub for bulding selections other than a "click your mouse" prompt, and you usually find yourself running between buildings often trying to make a simple selection. There are plenty of other examples of the UI being incredibly clunky and vague, like the Quest Selection page before you depart to the world. I had to take a few moments to grasp what they wanted me to do.

I've put a good amount of hours into this game so far, and I cannot wait to see what they bring to the table with it. Definitely one of the better Early Access titles I have ever played. I see this game having such potential for updates, maybe little DLC packs for new character stories or mission packs. Hell, maybe even release these quarterly for free. I can also see a potential competitive scene emerging from this game once all the balancing has been ironed out, and most of the gameplay has been tweaked a bit.

I will update this review when the game goes live, but I would highly recommend you buy this if you are on the fence about it. I would say 80% of the game is there, and what is included is well worth the $15 asking price.

To the Pits!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm reviewing this game in it's current state, and so far, I've had alot of fun with Pit People. The humor is perfect, and the art is fantastic. There are so many different weapons and units to delve into and figure out; However, this is part of the problem. The game throws you in with a minimal tutorial and trying to figure out the game can be quite overwhelming.

My first complaint is the way the team customization menu is set up. When trying to choose new swords or helmets for my characters, I often feel its very clunky and uninformative, and the lack of individual statistics for each piece of equipment, trying to figure out the balance of the game is hell. There is also no point in really equiping a sword over a mace because mace's can bash helmets. Swords cant and they both deal around the same damage.

My second complaint is the different races and what they're good against and weak against. It simply gives symbols without telling me what exactly those symbols mean. I wish there was more direction in what these mean and more explanation in the Surival Journal. Traveling around the map seems like a mess because I see all these monuments that I just discovered, but it's just very confusing and nothing is truly recognizable. Unlike in Castle Crashers where there were different and recognizable districts with different enemies. Here, the map is just a cluster of different biomes clashed together in no recognizable structure and generic monuments that I cant really tell apart.

Also, what even does leveling up do to my character other than refill their health?

Sorry if this review seems like a mess, I wrote it quickly. Other than these complaints though, I have no big problems. I have loved all of behemoth's previous games and they are amazing. I trust they'll improve this game and make it even better than it is right now in Early Access.\t\t\t\t\t
