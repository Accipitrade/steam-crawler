Disclaimer\t

To start off I would like to first highlight that despite having 17 hours in the game as of the time of writing this review, I have yet to experience some of the major aspects of this game.

A. These being first Co-op element of the game which typically increases the fun factor of most games by upwards of 100% and I feel in this games case would make for a far far far superior experience.

B. The pit, an arena in the games city hub area thingy that features arena battles against both npcs and AI with coop also being an option.

C. The "hard core mode" or whatever it is called, while less important as not many people will be hinging their purchase decision on an additional difficulty setting, it still bears mention.
(Okay not entirely true, I did switch it on at one point but there were loud noises, flashing light, smoke and fire... long story short I got scared an immediately turned it off again ;-;)

With that out of the way, Let Us Begin!

The Pros\t

This game. Is. Bonkers.
I never expected in my life that I would be setting the personal objective to go out of my way in a video game in order to..

Get a prettier pair of leggings.. that are purely cosmetic.. for my spider lady.. so that they would match the style of my companion.. who rides on her back.. and is also a sentient cupcake.. that flings portions of its body into teammates mouths.. so that it can heal them.. while slowly killing itself in the process.

Now in case you aren’t sold quite yet, here are some more general pros in my opinion.

- It can be described as "babies first strategy game" and NOT and a derogatory sense.
Even if you have never played a strategy game or plain suck at any of those that you have tried, rest assured that you will be able to perform competently in this game. Simple and Satisfying is the motto I use for the gameplay.

**AND YET! The system cannot be called shallow so easily given the wealth of weapon types, unit types, team combinations and hazards/status effects that all serve to enrich the base combat experience.

Additional likeable features of the game that come to mind include the games vast degree of customization items (well over 1000 individual items) that give little advantage over each other in reguards to stats.
Sure some might have certain resistances or attributes such +dodge%chance etc.

However as a whole this does not subtract from the game experience, it helps avoid a balancing nightmare and constant player worrying "are the stats good enough? Do I have anything optimal? Omg heavens forbid I am not min/maxing to the fullest extent" ad infimum."

Instead this lets the player simply... have fun! New hat? Like the hat? Stick that sucker on!
Fashion souls to your hearts content, better to kick butt in style am I right! ^-^

A final attractive feature I will mention before moving along to the next portion of this review (which I can already tell is going to be long af so settle in kiddies) is the games full co-op (both local and online) functionality. Worked seamlessly into the game, for example;

  • Want to go to the shops in the city while your partner edits his team build? Sure! You can both do as you please without causing any inconvenience to the other party.

  • Over world map travel? There’s only one carriage with one driver... so one of you gets to be the gunner on top, allowing for some additional team interactions and coordination to stun enemies that you would rather not start an encounter with.
    (Can be done as a solo player as you are given control over movement and guns in that instance so don't worry)

  • Combat? You both take you full teams into the battles and.. well it's a turn based strategy game so it's not like much can really go wrong or inconvenience either party mechanically.

The Cons\t

Combat can get very repetitive and once you capture all the creatures you want, unless you spot an over world mob with an item you really really want, you may find yourself in the same position as me.
Actively avoiding combat outside of quests and even if you get sucked into one... just fleeing because scccreeww that.

This however is not a con within the context of said quests, this being due to the simple fact that a lot of tender love and care is put into quests. The maps which these encounters take place in are typically hand crafted and narrated, not only adding charm and character to both the enemies and the environment but also changing the gameplay.

Through adding in map objectives (camps to destroy, buttons to press, key targets, etc.) you can complete the mission without having to actually kill everything in the level!

My favourite example (will be vague to avoid spoilers) of this is during a mission where most of the gimmick is to throw tons more foes at you than you initially expected.
This still works. Why? The way the enemies are introduced is entertaining and each new enemy adds a new map objective making it easier beat the level beyond brute forcing i.t
(aka surround the mofo's one by one and dog pile them for a quick 1/2 round kill.)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Another issue with the game to some might be the early access status of the game, however with Behemoth working on the game given their track record with past titles and the update work on this game over the years you can stand to feel a little less anxious.

The main storyline is still underdeveloped in the sense that there are only a handful of mission (albeit recently paced, narrated and designed with interesting cut scenes and objectives) that you can be done within a few hours easily.
This will however continue to be expanded over time and is more forgivable given both the quality level and the games vast array of side quests.

These aforementioned side quests will keep you running all over the world map always excited for the next to see how the stories unfold. For example (spoilers) One mission leads you to Travel to the FRIGGEN SUN to retrieve some sorta solar great sword of massive damage and aoe fire starting nonsense!\t

A final note before we wrap up for the cons is… hard core strategy players...
This might not be the game for you, the game is quite simplistic compared to other strategy games due to features such as the fact that if you move a unit to a positon. Whereby it *can* target more than one opponent... you have no control over which foe it will actually attack so... soooo yeah, that might cheese your onions if you know what I mean.

Conclusion\t

The game is only going to get better with time and the price is said to be getting hiked up about five bucks or so whenever the game leaves early access. So all around I would say it is a fantastic monetary and time investment.

!Hours of fun and so much more potential to be tapped into as development continues! \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t So far the game is very funny and charming. The art style is very nice to the eyes, cartoon fits the tone of the game very well. The writing is hilarious and Stamper (the narrator) nails it with this performance. The game hosts a wide variety of characters to "recruit" and a plethora of weapons, armour, and shields to equip your people with. The interactions between characters are fun. The story is very fun and is added upon by the interesting characters within the main story and the side story characters are also very well written. Did I mention this game is funny as hell?
There are so many more ways I can praise this game, but I'll get to a few problems the game has. 1) I have encountered a few bugs (obviously this is easily fixable) 2) The combat is a bit unusual to me, still. I understand how the combat works it's just I wish I could select who to attack instead of it being decided for me. 3) The overworld, whilst fun, is kinda boring to go across. Now I get it, all games need to have down time to pace things out; but the overworld in this game has some weird hitboxes that makes going across it somewhat annoying. You'll occasionally get stuck on a water tile that you're no where near, for example. One case that I encountered was I couldn't go through a mountain pass (it was a 1 tile wide "road" with mountains on both sides) because of the weird hitboxes on the mountains. Though there are several other ways to get to where you're going this still provides a "*sigh* really?" moment.
These are the problems I've encountered with the game. Now, don't get me wrong, there are SOOOOO many more things to like about this game(I've listed a few in the first paragraph.) Overall I recommend this game to pretty much anyone looking for a fun game or a good strategy game. The equipment system in this game isn't innovative by any means, but they've mastered it and it has some depth to it. I don't like giving ratings but it's a good way to communicate the quality of a game.
8.5/10 I would buy it again\t\t\t\t\t
