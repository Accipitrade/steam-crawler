\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll write this review from the point of view of a dedicated RTS and RTT player.

First of all, don't let the cartoonish style fool you, that's simply the 'brand' of the studio behind Pit People, and if you've played any Behemoth game before you should know. I highlight that, because the game is considerably deep when it comes to the mechanics, and I believe it is streamlined, but a moderate extent. The items and features are many, and that allows you to deploy a wide array of tactics, depending on your taste and personal strategy.

For instance, there are not just shields, swords, maces or raw damage: There are currently 3 types of shields which work differently from each other. The way items work is somewhat based on real life combat (i.e. The massive pavise-like ones provide no protection in melee but great coverage from ranged damage), but there are also illogical details added for the sake of balance: small and medium shields offer no melee protection, bucklers especially were actually good for melee defence and not for small protection from projectiles.
However, overall the positive connotations largely outweigh the negative or unpleasant ones: There is also the concept of pierce and blunt damage, accuracy, and light and heavy armour with their related bonuses and maluses. About that, I don't appreciate that heavy armour decreases so much the attack of a fighter, but that is another story.

Apart from unit customisation, there are also different races you can pick from. Aside from humans you get: arachno-humanoids called spidaurs which are tanky and can be mounted by some fighter types, robots, vampires, trolls, cyclops, mushrooms (yes lol), small jawa like lads that come in 2 for each slot*, fire-shooting fairies called pixies, cupcakes (yeah), wraiths and zombies.

*Slots are what determines how many fighters you can have in your team. You start with 4 open slots and can unlock 2 more easily enough early in the game. Not all races take the same amount of slots. Cyclops and trolls take 2 slots each, the jawa-like gnomes take 1 but come in a couple. Of course, that means that the unit is as strong as the number of slots it takes.

My general interest with gaming is confined to a few genres, the same to which the following titles belong: Total War's, Heroes of Might and Magic, Age of Empires, etc... Compared to HoMM especially, I can't say there is an abyss in-between. I'm actually very satisfied of the diversity that they put into the game.

The humour is very likeable, and so is the story. I put these two last because I care much more about the playability of a title and how many hours of entertainment and variety it might offer.

Review updated between 08.01 and 09.01.18, nearing the launch of the game. Little actually changed in the mechanics, so my review is still valid. If I had to say something 'bad' about the game, it would be the following:
-I would have liked a mode to allow a larger team composition. This may allow for units which have little use in a small team to fill their niche in larger 'armies'. A 18 large squad would be very interesting to arm and deploy.
-Some more character and weapon types would be nice for some extra variety, i.e. spears which can hit from 1 tile away. Also allowing for more customisation in non-human units, not to their same extent, but greater than now. Like I suggested during the development, a type of body armor for monsters which can't carry a shield would have been very interesting
-Modding tools in order to allow easy modification of the game values. This would potentially interest me because it would be possible to make a "Realism Mod".

Overall vote: 7.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People is an amazing, sarcastic turned based strategy game from the creators of Castle Crashers and Battleblock Theater. Quirky characters, hilarious dialogue, and the game play itself will keep you entertained for hours on end. Plus, the amount of characters you can recruit in game and the countless ways you can style them through in game items is endless. The fights are challenging enough to ensure you are properly building teams to suit your play style, but easy enough where you aren't fleeing each match out of frustration or fear.

Pit People is a must - and I am excited to see it come out of Early Access in the future!

The only 'critiques' is that some of the abilities in game aren't always tutorialized (how to drop poop to find your way home, using the gun on the wagon, various other in game abilities that you just kind of 'learn' over time), and another concern with co-op. In co-op mode, it makes it difficult for both players to choose their actions at the same time as the map auto zooms or allows your mouse to zoom to other locations. This has resulted in my co-op buddies and I just taking turns, and once they confirm their strategy then I make my moves and end the turn. There should be the ability to zoom out the map entirely.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game ticks so many boxes:

A "gotta catch-em-all"approach to party/team formation.
Customisable characters.
Online pvp.
Brilliantly wacky single player/co-op story.

However, your enjoyment of this game will hinge upon how much you like games focused on turn based combat. The combat is undeniably the core of the game. As easily accessible as it is, turn based games are not everyone's cup of tea. If you're already a fan of any kind of turn based game, you're sure to find pleasure here. If not, while there's still a lot to love, it may take some adjusting to.

Pit People is a wonderful game all in all. The level of customisation and depth is outstanding considering how easy to access it is for a game of this type. The story content is lacking, but that's why the game isn't full price yet. There's more to come in coming months from a reputable studio.

One of the safest 'Early Access' bets out there.\t\t\t\t\t

Get ready for the Pit, People!

Ich habe die Closed Beta von Pit People ca 20 Stunden gespielt und mir jetzt, kurz nach Early Access release auch das eigentliche Spiel geholt, und ich muss echt sagen: Es hat sich gelohnt!

Pit People ist ein Strategiespiel/RPG mit vielen Fassetten, voller krudem Humor und Anspielungen auf die Realität. Es knüpft unmittelbar an die Handlung von BattleBlock Theatre an, aber es ist nicht nötig das vorgegangene Spiel gespielt zu haben um die Handlung zu verstehen:

Die Welt von Pit People befindet sich in einem komplett abgedrehten postapokalyptischen Zustand! Nachdem ein gottähnlicher Weltraumbär ne bessere Beschreibung hab ich für das Vieh nicht! auf die Erde stürzte sind überall Monster am rumlaufen und der Großteil der Zivilisation ist zerfallen... Du schlüpfst in die Rolle von Horatio, dem bescheidenen Blaubeerfarmer - und verstrickst dich Hals über Kopf in eine extrem lustige Hauptquest, die ich nicht weiter spoilern will.

Ich finde das der Comiclook gut zum Spiel passt, Cutszenes sind flüssig gezeichnet, und die Dialogbilder können einen auch öfter mal zum lachen bringen weil sie einfach so stimmig gezeichnet sind! Die Musik ist wie in den anderen Behemoth-Games auch sehr greifend und passend!

Zum Gameplay gibt es noch zu sagen, dass sich das Spiel ein bisschen wie Pokemon mit "Dress Up- Funktion" spielt - Man fängt Monster die über die Map laufen ein und kann ihnen dann gefundene Ausrüstung anziehen um Stärken und schwächen zu beeinflussen. Im aktuellen Stand des Spiels gibt es bereits eine ansehnliche Anzahl Monster (zB Zyklopen, Einhörner oder Vampire, nur alle in nem eigenen Stil und etwas "trippy"), aus denen man sich sehr coole taktische Teams kombinieren kann - Ich hoffe jedoch, dass die Menge der Monstersorten noch weiter wachsen wird!
Die Kämpfe fühlen sich für mich immer sehr fair und ausgewogen an, gerade bei späteren und härteren Quests, und das taktische Element greift wirklich tief! - Und für Leute dies übel mögen gibt es den knallharten "Insane-Mode", der die Gegner nochmals um einiges bufft!)

Co-optechnisch glänzt das Spiel auch durch einen reibungslos funktionierenden local- sowie Online-Partnermodus, es ist bisher alles gut ausgewogen und spaßig - und in Onlineduellen kann man sein taktisches Können gegen Freunde oder Fremde prüfen und coole Belohnugen sammeln!

Alles in allem kann ich Pit People also ohne schlechtes Gewissen Liebhabern von Taktik, Co-Op und abgedrehtem Humor/Stories die sich selbst nicht so 100%ig ernst nehmen vollstens ans Herz legen - 15€ ist wie ich finde ein wirklich guter Preis für ein so gutes, noch wachsendes Spiel!

Wenn euch das Gelesene gefallen hat, dann hört doch mal bei unserem Podcast-Channel rein! Wir sind zu finden unter: http://dergamerscast.podomatic.com/ , wo wir über Spiele diskutieren und reviewen.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first few minutes of this game says it all. Pit People is a hilarious game with great artwork, which is fun to play and does not take itself very seriously. It is very self aware while still delivering an, this far interesting story (I say this far since the Early Access does not contain much of the story, but the little it contains is great).

The gameplay itself is great. It is a fun, casual strategy game. You do not have to be particularly skilled to enjoy it and play it, but you can still apply quite a bit of strategy into it if you are more competetive.

The world map is fun and interesting, and it is great to see all the creative places that the developers have made on said map.

You have loads of customisation for your characters which is also great. It is also worth to note that this is probably the first Behemoth game where playing with controller is not better than playing with mouse and keyboard. You can still play with controller if you prefer that, which is fantastic. It just means more options for the player.

I am looking forward to see the finished product. However this game is definetly worth it buying already, even if it lacks story. It is fantastic and fun both gameplay wise and aestethically. Not only that, but Will Stamper shows once again that he is the best voice actor ever. I cannot get enough of his voice in this game.

Get this game NOW!\t\t\t\t\t
