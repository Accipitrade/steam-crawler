\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's fun, and has that awesome 'The Behemoth' vibe. The art direction is perfect, the music is outstanding (just the best).

The turn-based gameplay is a blast. It makes sense after a bit, it doesn't take long.

The UI is a train-wreck at the moment. I'm having a hard time trying to figure out what the hell it is I need to do to... just DO things sometimes. It makes it very frustrating. I feel like this should be as effortless to understand as other Behemoth games, but sometimes when I thought I could do something, I end up pressing every key, controller, or mouse button to try to find out what it was.

Also, sometimes you can accidentally get trapped between conflicts you don't want to. Repeatedly. There is no "flee" command, or if there is, it's a real ♥♥♥♥♥ to find. Your only option is to be trapped between fights until you kill off your party. Yes, there is a "stun" cannon on your wagon, fairly early on, but it's not great, and there is so much pomp involved with restarting after you accidentally got trapped between a couple of overpowered enemies. Yes, I know, you should avoid those situations, but when you do get into one, I'd rather not have to reload the game (I keep on fighting them to the bitter end, despite my knowing they will stomp me). In some ways, this is worse that random encounters. In games with random encounters, I usually can run away.

Overall, though, it's great so far. The basic, underlying game is amazing. But, please, "The Behemoth," do a bit to help us understand how it works.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL: DR- If you like turn-based gaming AND humor AND funky addictive music you can't go wrong!


Anyone that has used the coveted Rivscore (TM) system for buying games will be perhaps shocked at the score I gave this game. I'm only 5 hours in but the time played ( as I have said to naysayers in the past ) is really irrelevant; the whole point of gaming for me is does the game capture you from the start!??

Pit People is from developer Behemoth and I have not played any of thier other games; more's the pity it seems. Perhaps PP is their Opus?? Pit People, from the opening quirky scene, hooked me like no game has in perhaps 20+ years.

- Quirky graphics that can run on a potato: check

- Humor that is LOL'able: check

- A combat system that is easy to learn / hard to master: check

-Music ( now bear in mind i'm an 80's Metalhead) that is foot-swinging good-check

And......the game is still in early access. Amazing game that I can see myself playing at least 40+ hours.


Rivscore- 100 / 100\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother classic Behemoth game. Like the others, Pit People is an amazing co-op game and can also be played singleplayer. Will Stamper from Battleblock Theater returns as the narrator in this game again. The game is full of Behemoth humor, along with delivering a nice challenge. The strategy behind the game is what makes it so interesting. You don't choose who your units attack, so if a unit is in range of multiple enemies it'll select which one to attack randomly. Some units are more effective, and weaker against others. This makes the positioning of your units are important and is what makes the game fun. There's a ton of different customization options for different types of helmets and weapons as well.

All in all, a great game. 10/10 - hard to pass up for $15.\t\t\t\t\t
