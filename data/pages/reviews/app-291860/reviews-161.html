\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you want a chess like strategy game that has a natural feeling of progression along with weight for losing and joy for getting that lucky hit, then this game is for you. It has a booming soundtrack, with colorful graphics and they both play along side each other very well, if the style is for you. Weapons and armor are both neat and enjoyable to collect from random enemies and quests. Quests are also fun, and out in the world map the side quests that exist are repeatable after a move for extra gold and loot.

Probably, the best part about this game, aside from the developers just being neat people from newgrounds, is the lack of pressure from doing "wrong stuff". Want to play the game, sure it's single player, don't have internet, don't worry. Want to fight a random troll momma and lose possibly, don't worry you just get sent back to town if you die with full hp so you can try again. Don't want to login to get "daily rewards" don't have to, they don't exist.

This game reminds me of a game that came out back in the N64 or PS1-2 era. You want to play the game, sure, play it, no pressure or incentives other than a good game. Want to play with your friends, sure go for it. It's a game that wants to be played, it's not a "triple A" game that wants to show you how to use a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ store to purchase microtransactions.

A good game like this only comes around every blue moon when you have developers who want to make a game that is legit fun.

+1 Devs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf your looking for a review on this game you've come to the wrong place, but if your looking for an outstanding cheese cake recipe you've come to the right place.

Ingredients:
1 cup digestive plain sweet biscuit crumbs
3 tablespoons sugar
3 tablespoons melted butter
40 ounces softened cream cheese
1 cup sugar
3 tablespoons flour
1 tablespoon vanilla
1 cup sour cream
4 eggs

Directions:
For The base:.
If you're using a dark pan, preheat the oven to 350. If you are using a shiny pan, 375.
Combine the crumbs, melted butter and 3 tbsp sugar well and pat onto the bottom of a 9 inch springform pan. I also like to very very lightly butter the sides of the pan so that the cheesecake will not stick later on.
Bake for 10 mins in your preheated oven and remove. Let it cool while you prepare your batter.
For the batter:.
Make sure everything is at room temperature.
Combine sugar, cheese, flour and vanilla in a big bowl and mix on medium speed till well combined. Don't forget to scrape the bowl as you're doing this.
Mix in the sour cream till its incorporated well. Then, add the eggs one by one, mixing well after each addition.
Pour the batter into the springform and let it bake for 1 hour and 10 minutes After the baking time, turn off the oven and leave the door ajar. Let the cake cool in there for about 1 1/2 hours.
Remove the cake and let it cool thoroughly. Refrigerate overnight.
Release the sides of the springform.
Eat.\t\t\t\t\t
