https://youtu.be/rVbyRKzUYLw
As a turn-based adventure game, Pit People greatly differs from The Behemoth’s beat-em ups Alien Hominid and Castle Crashers and puzzle platformer BattleBlock Theater. As with these games, though, the patented wacky Behemoth humor is definitely present and possibly at its best, with silliness around every corner, craziness that will leave you confused yet smiling like an idiot, and references to past games hidden (or not so much) in every nook and cranny. Stamper as the voice of an omnipresent narrator is particularly on cue with his side-splitting spiteful commentary and nefarious interactions with the game characters and world that can happen at seemingly unexpected moments as he looks to kill his boredom at your expense.

For being in Early Access, the game looks and plays great, with solid controls and crisp, styled 2D sprites, though some of the UI could perhaps be cleaned up a bit or better explained. Your house in town where you can change out your team initially felt a bit overwhelming, requiring a lot of clicks to change out equipment and lack of button cues leading to a team member almost getting sold. Likewise, we received no warning that a quest would receive an F for Failure by returning back to town before we completed it, leading to an awkward visit with the local principal. Battles, meanwhile, could begin to get cluttered and claustrophobic, especially with the extra bodies and input in co-op, with allies and enemies having similar color and designs bunched up together on screen at once; there was often confusion on which character was currently selected and who was moving where. Our gripes are minor compared to the refined edges, though, and once you get things down, battles can feel intuitive while still retaining strategic complexity, allowing fights to be as fast-paced or as slow and methodical as you wish them to be. With a discount while in Early Access, Pit People appears to be another great game by The Behemoth that is worth a pick up and play, and we are excited to see the rest of the crazy content and wacky adventure that will be in the finished game.

Catalyst Players/Reviewers: Jacob and Murphy
Version: Early Access - Jan. 18, 2017

The game has all sorts of content and there will actually be more of it since it is in Early Access at the time of this review. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet met start by saying I reccomend this game. The laughs itself is worth the $15. The behemoth always makes pretty good games, and I've known about several of the people involved in this game back in their Newgrounds days over a decade ago. But, I do believe this game has some issues - not counting the incomplete story campaign, which is mentioned in-game by the developers as a work in progress. At the time of writing this, it will only last you about an hour if you try to complete it quickly. But the game also generates random encounters with rewards and ratcheting difficulty, as well as co-op and multiplayer vs.

The good:
- The game is funny. Really funny.
- The strategy is easy to pick up and accessible.
- Play with a friend!
- Turn-based strategy without the premadeath and nessescary save-scumming that *cough* X-Com still seems to have a hard time getting rid of.
- A high degree of customization and collectibles seems to provide a lot of replay value.
- A giant world with tons of sidequests and rewards.

The bad:
- This game has a wierd habit of making the world full of color and life, but making the characters washed out and a little bland. For a capture-the-pokemon style of collection and recruitment, it's weird that most of the combatants are stark white humans in off-white clothes.
- The strategy is flat, without elevation, without a variety of terrain bonuses or a huge variety in abilities or defences. Most fights turn into bottlenecking and musical hexes.
- Health bars are so huge that often the fights turn into attrition slugfests.\t\t\t\t\t

The second I found out about game number four (now Pit People) I ♥♥♥♥ bricks. I had just played through games like XCOM and Fire emblem (WaifuWars:Awakening) and really started to like the turn based strategy genre so I felt like fate had delivered the dream game. Little did I know that this dream game would also be early access. Mow lets get into the pros and cons (so far).

PROS
+The signiture Behemoth artstyle, the very reason I love the brush tool in Adobe Flash (By the time I typed Flash it
crashed)
+Wonderful soundtrack I walready wish I could buy.
+Lots of customization.
+Instead of moving units one by one they all move at once.
+A unique system of instead targetting an any, a charater will instead attack one enemy withen thier range prioritizing
melee attacks over ranged attacks.
+A simple armor system.
+The randomly generated enemies and names (as far as I know though the character types and positions do not change
for scenarios which is awesome)
+The "class/races" as far as I can tell play pretty differently and uniquely. I couldn't imainge what game would have a
cupcake that mutilates itself to heal others, though its probably hidden somewhere in some DnD story.
+The humor. I'm always one for a good fart joke, but when voiced by Will Stamper, I love a good fart joke,
+Will stamper is the one voice that isn't in the parody of simish that the characters speak in. I love it.

CONS
-No name customization, though I do like the random names I wanna name a party after a few friends and I then place
bets on who dies in what order in the pit.
-The game explains how to play it, but not how the character building works which you kinda just learn on your own.
-Catchin- errr "recruiting" a unit always seems to give me two stars instead of three after each battle, but it makes sense
considering Will Stamper praises you for as murdering as many people in a turn as you can.
-Never seem to get a toon gold, but not too much of a con unless you really want to buy a 7K gold unit.
-Not enough content... yet.

For dedicated Behemoth fans you should buy this after reading this, it has a fairly short download time too so you won't die from getting hyped.
For people who have played a Behemoth game I would wait a week or so then check on the updates.
For people who are interested in this you should wait a month, then check on the updates.

If Dan Palidan and Tom Fulp are reading this, thank you for work and time. I really hope you enjoy your work as much as I do. I really hope you guys flesh this game out. You already have 1,300+ ITEMS, each one unique and quirky! I don't know how you manage to do it but you guys really buckle some pants when it comes down to making video games. Keep at it!\t\t\t\t\t
