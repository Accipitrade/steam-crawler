\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People is off to a good start, with a few issues that will hopefully be worked on in the future. A warning: the Story content is limited at the moment, with most of the gameplay coming from building your team, working out strong team compositions and side questing/exploring. There's a good few hours of gameplay here and battling in the pit is very addictive.

The game works great on a keyboard and mouse, but there's a confusing mix of KB/M specific interface icons and Controller Icons currently. In some cases it's unclear which button I need to be pressing. Additionally, combat can also feel clunky when it comes to selecting the correct character and it would be nice if the game could be played without needing to use the keyboard - a clickable end turn button etc would probably do the trick.

The game's also rather obtuse in a number of areas. There doesn't appear to be an in-game explanation of a number of the attribute icons as yet. Many are obvious (breaks helmets, inflicts fire damage, blocks ranged attacks) yet some aren't clear at all. The equip menu itself is also pretty clunky and unintuitive initially.

Worth checking out now if you're a fan of the Behemoth, otherwise perhaps wait a little while.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an amazing game right now, but there are some major areas that could use improvment.
This review will only be listing the flaws, but rest assured, it earns that positive rating.

-No Zoom in or out
This is vital to nearly all SRPGS, being able to zoom out to see most, if not all of the battle field at all times. This allows for the best and most tactical disicions possible, and give the player all the info they need without hassle.
In this game, your constantly looking through bonoculars magnetically taped to your forehead by Stamper with no way of taking them off because of that metal plate you goit in your head.
Seriosuly, why did you think that was a good idea?

-Not many Varried objectives
Most maps boil down to "Kill the enemy". While this is a fine, morally Upstanding goal, it can get a tad boring at times. The few defend and seiges maps work greatat showing the strengths and weaknesses of each race, class, unit, and otherwise.
However, when the Maps are almost entirly "Kill these guys", it makes some units undeniably better then others, such as Fredrick.
Speaking of which...

-Fredrick is too Powerful
I though I was done with him when I was finshed with Awakening for the 7th time.
I though I had found a game without a ludicrusly Overpowered unit.
I was wrong.
Fredrick the Cyclops, with his long flowing Magenta hair, Tiny turtle shell sheild, and Nucular Bomb Club, was far too powerful for the rest of my units too keep up. Even taking up two slots wasn't enough to stop this deminsiopn hopping monstrosity. His two move seemd like a reasonable nerf.
HA.
That was only so the enemies could pickl a god and pray one last time before their inevitable demise.
The only map he ever struggled with was the Lumberjack one, and that's 'cuse he hate's lumberjacks and long distances.
Nothing could stand up to him, not even other cyclopses.
When the undead came crawling, he put them back in the grave. He wasn't afraid to kil. Again and again.
It didn't matter that he took damage from his bomb.
It didn't even mater that we lacked a healer for most of the game.
Fredrick could not be killed.
Fredrick could not be stopped.
Fredrick was a force of nature.

And it wasn't enough he was that strong, he was ALWAYS the one talking too. He was the leader of this rag tag group he called a team. Sure, his ego was about as through the roof as his strength, but he had twice as much bite for every bark you'd even THINK he had.

But, as amazing as he is, all good things must end. Fredrick is too overpowered. I'd be happy to see him nerfed, in exchange for seeing him more in the story, with his FLowing purple, deep, muscly voice and suprisingly sensable values.

-The terrain sometimes black the veiw of the units
Despite how massive Fredick is even compared to other cyclopses, there were times where the terrain even obscured HIM. Just making the terrain a unit is standing behind less opaque would solve this issue immediately.

-The healers don't heal enough
'nuff said. You need two to make them even think about being useful, and even then, that's a slot that could be used to bring ANOTHER cyclops to use with Fredrick.

-More unique weapons
I want more weapons that DO things.
I love re-skins as much as the next guy, but it can make it hard to tell what some enemies are capable of, and that can SUCK. Besides, it would make getting a new weapon exciting rathen then "Oh, what's this reskin?"

-Sopfia Sucks
I understand that the "Capture" unit shouldn't be a godess. But COME ON, she is SO WEAK compared to everyone else. If she had visable utility outside of capturing, like maybe some abilities to help with defend of seige maps, that might make her useful. But, since every map is "Kil, Kill, KILL" she's an active detriment.
Besides, once you capture Fredrick, there is no need to capture anyone else, so she becomes useless.

-Status effects Suck
Theres no point to avaoiding them because they suck so much.

-More unique characters
I like how Gluten got his own side quest. I want more units like him, who get some soft of quest to go along with them.
I would say restrict the quests so you HAVE to have them in your party, though. For story reasons.

-Show more of the other main characters in the story
Horatio and Pimpstella are suprisingly engaging characters, but I felt myself wondering during some of these scenes "Where's Yslove? Where's Sofia? Where's Glutan? Where's Fredrick?"
These are all characters introduced in the main story, but never get any appearences in the main story, and rarely appear in sidequests (Aside from Yslove). Even if they are not present, they should have at least a little more of an impact.


-PVP's kinda crap
When your losing, there's not many ways to "Win". Though, this is more of an SRPG problem then just this game, so that's why it's last.

-Tell us what our team is strong and weak aginst when we build it
This is more of a request, but I want to see what our team is most effective against and what their weakest to, both Map-wise and Enemy wise. I want to see the game itself acknowledge Fredrick is overpowered. Those purple locks just make him too good.

All in all, great game, but I clearly see why it's in early acess.
I understand Fredrick must be nerfed, But I'd prefer it done in more clever ways, and to see him intereact more with the story.
Good luck on your game development, Gentlemen, And I shall be off!
Fredrick, Pull our cart away!\t\t\t\t\t
