\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll play almost any local co-op game. Turn-based strategy is a great genre for co-op, something I didn't know because I've never seen a turn-based local co-op game before.

I tried playing some of the developers' other games but never quite enjoyed them. They're kinda funny, kinda stupid, kinda janky. This game has a lot of charm, but if you don't care for the story you can skip it. The interface is over-complicated and confusing. It feels like there could be half as many buildings and features without changing the gameplay too much. The equipment system is particularly arcane and annoying.

While my partner and I really enjoyed this game at first, I only ended up putting eight hours into it. The battles became increasingly drawn out. The difficulty level is such that it can go from easy to enraging in a heartbeat. It is incredibly frustrating to come just shy of winning a battle only to realize that you wasted 45-60 minutes with nothing to show for it. You end up running like the dickens from any random opponents on the map because it's tedious and just plain not fun to fight battles that don't move the story along. I like difficult games and play a lot of roguelikes and roguelites, but after a couple of losses our interest in continuing to play was nonexistent. At normal difficulty the enemies simply have too much HP, so fights are drawn out and repetitive without becoming tactically interesting. Playing the same battle twice is mind-numbingly boring.

I would recommend this game only if you really like local co-op games and want to try something new. I'm happy I played it for the time I did, but I can't imagine picking it up again.\t\t\t\t\t
