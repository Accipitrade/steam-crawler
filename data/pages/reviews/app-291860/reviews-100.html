-The secret achievement isn't worth getting leaving a 94% on the progress.
-Lots of frustrating RNG
-Jerkimedes gets on your nerves (I know he's supposed to but I almost stopped playing one night because he was being such a ♥♥♥♥ in the story)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game best described as hilarious madness.

The main “story” is absurd and kept me laughing the entire time, the villain is just the sort of deliciously, over the top, evil that he is cionsistantly entertaining, and I have yet to run into a side quest that was not worth going through.

The combat is rather basic, especially compared to other turn-based games I have played but I found the gameplay engaging enough to keep me going, though I would not suggest marathoning this. I also find it annoying that almost everything that is not human only has cosmetic things to equip but it’s more about who you pick in your team than what you equip them with.

If your looking for a complicated turn based challenge or a serious storyline, then a game like X-COM would be a better pick for you. If you are looking for something funny and completely ridiculous, then you need to pick this up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are already some longer Negative reviews here which essentially echo how I feel about Pit People.

I bought in EA around a year ago and waited until launch to play.

It starts out with the charm offensive set to Ludicrous. The first hour or so contains some amazing animation and acting that rivals the best cartoons of today. Unfortately this outstanding presentation is paired with some of the shallowest and dullest combat I've encountered. It bored me to tears as each fight can last upwards of 10 minutes and it never really gets more complicated than your first battle. Other than some boss fights or quest mobs which may require you to break through a door or a wall, or face a swarm of spawning enemies, it's the same. You simply try to position your guys and click end turn and hope that the round goes well. You're bombarded with EXP gains and LVL UP fanfare in combat yet your heroes don't seem to develop or progress. They don't unlock interesting abilities as far as I'm aware, at least not in my time with the game. The map appears to be packed with interesting locations yet they remain useless window dressing. You gather a metric butt-tonne of loot from completing quests and fighting map mobs, and while it seems like there are many items, there aren't many different items other than the visual gag of it being a fence post or a lolly pop. There are plenty of entertaining cosmetics which have no effect and I believe you need to sacrifice valuable armor in order to wear them.

I was bored to death after around 5 hours. The combat is so shallow and directionless, and is presented in a frustratingly clunky manner making it confusing when shuffling around 6 heroes. An auto-battler is available in options allowing the cpu to take your turns for you. It seems more efficient to just allow the AI to do it's thing unless you need to take over to perform a specific activity such as capturing an enemy. You can enable or disable it whenever at will, so that's a good thing? It's the only reason I played more than two hours.

If there were a tonne more quests and a... I was gonna say a fleshed out combat system, but a combat system would be better than this. I'm reminded of how Endless Legend played out it's manual battles, except there you could actually direct which unit to try to attack, and I still ended up automising them because of how shallow it felt.

Perhaps it's more enjoyable in co-op or pvp. I could see this working well on the Switch or on the sofa with a partner for some mindless banter. If they can vibe with the at-times crass humor, then I'd particularly recommend for players completely new or unfamiliar to gaming. But if you were hoping for an evenly weighted, hilarious strategy game, then Pit People feels like it's missing a leg.

Was it blown off in to space by a giant space bear? Possibly.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start by saying that I highly recommend this game even though it has a few glaring flaws.

It's an insanely tactical turn-based strategy game that doesn't always rely on being tactical. Many combat scenarios don't fully ultilize the main mechanic of the game. For instance, the RNG of the overworld enemies may have you up against four Mascots (Bards) and an Archer.

The extreme poop humor that often edges its way into Behemoth games is also here, but you may like that kind of thing.

But the loot system is great, and the unit diversity is great. Many units are not pure combat units, and are going to hurt you if you do not have the right party combo. Party composition is a game of chess. There are 18 "classes" total, and you can Pokemon Capture up to 500 party members.

The variety of loot, even when it doesn't affect any of your stats, is great.\t\t\t\t\t
