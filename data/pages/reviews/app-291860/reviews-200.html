\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, this is one of the first reviews I've written, so if you disagree with what I say that's fine!

To start off with I will just list some of the basic pros and cons

-Pros
By The Behemoth
No microtransactions
f2p, f2w.
A vast variety of customizations, from characters to weapons to... to... HATS!!!
Easy to learn, easy to master (but hard to battle other people who have mastered the game 20/10)
Very addictive and fun.

~Cons
Paid (although this isn't very much an issue, most games are paid and it is quite cheap compared to other games which are lesser of Pit People... im talking to you, Overwatch)
There is a bit of grinding (not too much though)
It is in early access still, so there will be some unwelcome glitches and, unfortunately, exploits (although i have not heard of any exploits)
It is addictive (yes this can be a bad thing)

Now to describe what the game is like!

The game itself is very simple, a team crafting, (slightly) strategized turn-based 2d game. The graphics are top-notch (3D isn't necessary for every game that exists). You can have at maximum 6 fighters in your team, although you may find that you have to have less, as some characters take up more than 1 slot due to sheer awesomeness. There is a wide variety of different species that you can collect, and lots of items to customize them with. If there is something you dont want, ring up a friend in the oversized phonebox to the far left of the city, and you can easily swap items instantly. You can hold up to 500 recruits in this game. Also, some of the species would be the demi-clops, rainbow unicorn and pixie.

Now to the actual game mechanics!

As you are in a battle, you take it in turns to move your team to your desired positons. But, beware, all species have weaknesses. While crafting your team, you can see their health, move points, defense, dodge chance, heal, special abilities and defenses (like freeze resistance). All characters have up to 5 move points. Characters can become the hero to have stat boosts, like health, damage, and usually move points. Now, back to fighting itself! In a battle, each tile is a hexagon. Each hexagon you move costs 1 move point. Some hexagons are different, and damage your team or cost 2 move points, or just can't be moved onto. Some flying creatures, like the pixie, can fly over gaps and water no problem (water is what costs 2 move points). You can have 2 main types of attackers - melee and ranged. But, if you choose so, you can have a troop with melee and range! But the range will not be very powerful, or, if you are a mortar or ranger, the melee will not be very powerful. If your troops can target multiple enemies, they decide which they will attack. This means you really need to make sure you move your character to the exact spot you want them to be, so that you attack the enemy you need to be dead.

If you are lazy, or not exactly the best at the game, or have a team that is too big to control (some characters come with 2 people in only 1 space, so the max amount of characters you can have is 12) you can turn on the Auto Battler! This will move your characters instantly, although it may not always be very reliable. Only use it if you know that it will work well with your team.

That's all for now...

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHilarious and fun. It's a more violent, slightly depraved Pokemon. There's only the tutorial segment and a single story mission as of this review, but htere are multiple sidequests and an astonishing number of in-world 'bonus quests' to see and do.

I'm at 10 hours played the day after I bought it. I still want to play it. 8 of those hours were myself and a co-op buddy just wandering around, essentially grinding. We never noticed - the soundtrack, enemies, names, and just good gameplay held our attention.

Overall, I can't recommend this game enough. The only points against it would be the lack of hard numbers or detailed tooltips on some status effects. I'd like to know my chance to stun, what exactly a buff increases or a debuff decreases. I learned that the debuff decreases a target's defense, accuracy, and damage. I don't know by how much, and I only know what I do from a loading screen tip several hours into the game.

It's still one of the best turn-based strategy games made; doubly so if you have a friend to play with. Show me the numbers to sate my desire for detail and it'll go from 9.5 to 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t
