>> tl;dr:
Same old but very new fantastic Behemoth game, but missing something important that will hopefully come in the development process. 8.5/10

----
>> For ape creatures who read:
Although there are many, many things about this game that make it just as good as any other Behemoth-- given, better than most other games out there right now-- I'm going to start this review with my one main criticism that I hope the Devs were already considering, which is that:

The battle dynamics, although at first really fun and definitely well-thought through by the devs, feels like it lacks an extra important layer of depth to keep the replayability of the game and the challenge of the battles exciting. To better explain this, the depth of the game's battle strategy can be broken down into:

- Organize and modify your team at base [making a compatible party suitable to your playstyle].
- Strategically place your units on the field of battle for optimum damage, tank-ability and utility.
- [Sometimes] manage which enemies you target to save the last enemy to be recruited.

These two and a half only layers of the battle scenarios are strangely enough to keep you playing the game for a long time. But, I do admit, it eventually can become redundant, and maybe even boring. There are only so many combinations of your "team set-up vs. enemy team set-up"s you can go through before it starts to become repetitive routine strategy.

What I can only hope for is that the Devs add in at least one sort of extra layer of depth to the battle-dynamic which they add in through the development process; A layer like:

- In-battle item use.
- Added character-type activatable or auto-activated abilities (ex: leap for archers, charge for cyclops, etc).
- Talent tree sort-of-thing for personalization of each character you own.
- Something more brilliant that only a Behemoth dev can come up with.

A layer that can take you out of your routine comfort in a battle to yell "F***, I hate it when that f***er does that" or "F***, I love it when my little character dude does that".

Anyway, despite that one main criticism, the game really does nail everything you can love about a Behemoth game and more: Comedy, re-playability, well-constructed world, killer music, the always loved animation style, the unpredictability of every story-quest. Other reviews capture the best parts of the game in more depth.

All in all, 8.5/10. I just think it needs that one bit of spice.\t\t\t\t\t
