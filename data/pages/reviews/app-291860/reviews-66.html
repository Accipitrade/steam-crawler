\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite a fall from the usual Behemoth level.
While some game aspects are still awesome (style, humor, music, characters are fun, cupcakes are cute, etc.), the gameplay just isn't fun.
It is a 'tactical' game, where you cannot tactically control your fighters (since you only control movements, not attacks), and where the best 'tactic' is just gang up on the thing you want dead with as many fighters as possible.
Sure, there's some tactical 'depth' it in, like 'swords are weak against helmets, maces are good', 'catch dangerous melee mobs with net to get rid of them for a round', 'avoid clustering against AOE-attacking mobs like fairies, mushrooms and robots', etc., but it is still superficial and dull.
Most battles last for a loooong time, since attacks deal weaksauce damage here.
It's just a cute little game that fails to be a nice game. Guess the Behemoth wanted to take a break from genres like 'beat'em up Castle Crushers' and 'platformer challenge Battleblock Theater' (both of which are totally awesome BTW) and wanted to experiment a bit, but this experiment ended poorly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh, another slice of Behemoth goodness. What can I honestly say? Alien was a good game, Castle Crashers was an excellent game, Battleblock Theater kinda sucked imo

But Pit People is so drastically different from all of the other games they've made, and that's the amazing part about it. They've somehow completely nailed the formula right on the head.

If you enjoy games like Fire Emblem, XCOM, you'll love this. Enjoy a game filled to the brim with the Behemoth's classic comedy, along with PLENTY of content.

Like, seriously, all Battleblock had was... Completing each level flawlessly? And pointless hat collecting. This game has is filled to the brim with side quests, each with their own little quirks and stories. I quite like the one about the black hair monster who speaks in third person and is also a detective. Harry Hardboiled may be the best character in Behemoth's line of games.

As an added plus, if you own Castle Crashers or Battleblock Theater, you get additional equipment. The Castle Crashers gear isn't actually all that bad. Pay to win much?

Currently playing through the game on a separate save with my brother. It's honestly a blast, I never expected a game like this to work with local co-op... As a side note, there's *plenty* of save files! No need to worry about that.

Jokes aside, play at your own caution, because it's pretty easy to get sucked in. It's like my old 2012 days... Playing nothing but Castle Crashers all day on my Xbox 360. Thanks for putting out another game for me to get addicted to, Behemoth. You guys are seriously the best indie company out there.\t\t\t\t\t
