\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh, Pit People...


Pit People is a great game, and with it being early access, has huge potential. I am a huge fan of The Behemoth, and, as with all Behemoth games it has great humor in it, the narrator (who is Stamper again, by the way) is pretty much god and will do whatever he wants, moving the main city randomly around the map whenever he wants, and just messing with the characters. The story so far is about 2 hours, maybe 2 and a half (although, maybe a little shorter if they explained you needed cages to properly recruit people, god, that took me like, three whole fights to realise you needed a cage to "recruit" someone) you create your own team, which can consist of whoever you want, each character can have a different weapon, helmet and shield, all cracters have there own eqipment and a team can have up to six members. There are 6 base "classes" for a team, Assault, Ranger, Healer, Big Dude, Recruiter and Mid-Range person

Assaulters are your main attack people, Rangers sit at the back and shoot, Healers do just that, heal damdged team members, Big Dudes do a lot of damdge, have a lot of health but con only move a few Hexes, Recruiters are not super powerful, but can get you new team members and Mid-Range People can throw, uh, stuff to stun enemies. The game lets you choose your team pretty much how you want it and, if you want you can go six rangers. Personally, i go for two Assaulters, a Healer, a Ranger and a Big Dude, which unfortunately means i only have five due to Big Dudes taking up 2 slots on your team,

The gameplay is simple but nice, you tell your team members to go here, if they can they attack the enemy or heal your team they do, if not they just kinda, stand there. Then the enemy do the same thing. Ten you do it. Then the enemy.Then you, ect. The gameplay might not be super complex, but when you get a kill it feels super satysfying.

A great game with good humor, satisfying gameplay and lovely strategy. It has great potential, and will probably become a 10/10 when fully released, though due to it not having tons to do currently, i would say about 8/10, but i still frigging love it.

The gameplay is simple yet fun, but I do think the characters can be a little buffed up when you get them around high levels. I have a team that are all around level 60 but they are still doing the same damage and taking the same damage from enemies I fought at level 1. Aside from the free health from leveling up there needs to be a little bif more of a reward there.

The map is nice and open, with the idea of constanly changing the area you're in makes it feel some what fresh each time. The enemy placement is nice and diverse and there are enemies where you'd expect them to be, like robots in the techno zones and monsters in the gloomy areas. A few things could be explained the how the turbo effects the enemies and also shooting the sparkly ground gives you plenty of coins. I have yet to figure out what pushing all the buttons around the world map does but I'm working on that.

The sound track so far is catchy, that's the best way to describe it. A few more tunes would be nice if those songs were given to specific battles. Like say you're fighting mainly humans you'd play X song, then if you go over to the undead area there would be a chance to play a uniqe undead song out of the main soundtrack for the rest of the world. But that's a personal idea.

Another thing I would like to see is a little more interaction with the speaker, like a in combat modifier so if you're doing well he suddenly feels like you should die, special units the narator summons spawns on the enemy's team, like wise if you're losing. Just something more than just a voice to spice things up.

Over all I really enjoy this game for what it is, and considering I can play this with a friend makes it that much better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very entertaining game with a wide variety of units and items to play around with in an amusing setting.

The fact that you get to pick where your pieces go, but not what they do does make some scenarios a little more of a hassle than necessary - such as those where you have to destroy an item but the character in question is adjacent to one or more enemies and decides to attack them instead of the scenario-advancing widget.

There are also a few scenarios where additional enemies are rapidly and repeatedly added to an already ugly situation that are a bit obnoxious, but perhaps those are intended for teams of very high level characters or co-op groups. I've only hit about three that give me that impression - and early access is probably a good enviroment for a company to work out how players manage the scenarious they have in mind.

But those are relatively quibbly issues. Aside from the story missions, there are a great many other missions scattered around the landscape - and it's easy enough to try out team ideas on wandering monsters in the countryside. Early access games can be a bit of a risk, but Pit People seems like a very entertaining game even in its early access state.\t\t\t\t\t
