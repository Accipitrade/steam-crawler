\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Pit People is another great turn-based charming strategy game on Steam. I didn't finish the main story line yet because I don't usually do that during Early Access phase or I wouldn't play the main story after its full release. But despite that, I've finished necessary main quests to enjoy the game's full aspect and some few sub-quests.

STORY
So having played only intro missions, so far the story is funny yet very random! So far, I have no desire to finish the main story during early access phase, I cannot say much about it at this moment. Sub-quests are also great as situations often go hilariously unexpected. So far people are saying main story is quite short so I hope main story becomes longer than it is and maybe more sub-quests!

GAMEPLAY
There are huge customization options in Pit People. Every units can fit into different roles depending on which weapons, shields and helmet you desire to put them on. As you explore the overworld, there are different unit types you can "recruit" by capturing them like Pokemon. Many of these units carry different avatars of item types, so capturing them becomes addictive. Although there are currently 19 types of units in this game, I wish for more types of units to capture.
Having to level them up during progression makes your units personally attached. Unlike XCOM, there is no permadeath so it's okay for them to die. Permadeath would be another good addition if there will be a sandbox mode.

As turn-based strategy game, the positioning is extremely important. Unlike other games, you only command your units to move in specific positions while they will do anything on their own to their possible attackable enemies. But don't worry, they will do their best to win the battle.

The overworld is huge! There are 125 POIs you can explore and features many different biomes to sink your foot in. While it's very fun to explore these places, I wish for randomized sandbox mode, which would increase higher replayability in exploring, randomized quests.
Overworld is procedurally randomized, so hopefully there will be more than 125 POIs on full release as I am already reaching near 100%

Co-op campaign and pvp are great options to enjoy with friends and strangers over Internet. These make perfect reason why capturing units outside of the city is more enjoyable. Seriously, Pit People needs more interesting unit types to "recruit"! There are many different things to try like Insane mode, the pit with AI vs Players and etc which increases replayability.

AESTHETICS
Art style is very consistent across Behemoth games. Having played their older games back in Newgrounds, they still retains that funny and funky art style. Avatars for each units are usually random and hilarious, having 1357 of them are excellent! Only thing I would point out is that some vector graphics are pixelated when zoomed in, maybe this should be eradicated when leaving Early Access. Excellent sound design, love the vicious narrator, overall very good!

OVERALL
Even though this is early access title, it's well polished, very fun, highly replayable. Pokemon aspect truly shines as well as team-building aspect of this game. The game is very well balanced and it is the wonderful game to collect in your collection!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat I've seen so far is great. The game is really well made.

Pit people is an turn based strategy game with a world map that seems to be randomly generated.

You recruit people to your crew and fight battles with them. You find items, gold and all the usual things to get more powerful and then find even more stuff.

For me this formula works and I'm fine with it. I've seen the same ideas used a lot in the past though in Pit people they've made it feel somewhat new.

What makes this game great is the gameplay, the graphics, the music. Frankly it couldn't be better. I absolutely love the funky characters and the edgy music.

The only bad thing I've found so far is the UI that's downright confusing.\t\t\t\t\t
