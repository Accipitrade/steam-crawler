\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBehemoth, you did it again. And this time you really did it!
When I first saw the game in the store, I quickly got the feeling where the game origins come from. Hearing the same narator from the previous titles, I lost it at the very spot.

But my personal question as a life long rts/4x player was: How convincing could a "strategy" (Round based strategy with rpg elements) game be from such a studio?

Well, I was oddly surprised and genuinly impressed when I watched the very beginning in a Let's play video and considered a journey with the game afterwards. The way too familiar characteristics as the artstyle, sound and music (Also the same guy from BBT) never lost their charm, and while I played around 25h in solo I'm looking forward to play it in coop as I rarely had that much actual fun in most games recently.

Taking a closer look the graphics and animations, they surely are nothing comparable to other games like Battlefield, but then again, what would you expect from a 2D strategy game? I wouldn't even dare to compare it to any games other than perhaps Don't starve, if that even. With this kinda pointless statement being said, the graphics are gorgeous and yet simple with clear lines and single color tones, nothing to complain there. The animations are doing their job, but I see in this category why the game is in early acess state. The game holds a large amount of different characters which require different animations (i.e. attack [with various weapon types], take damage, special moves etc.), but some are simply not done (yet?). This problem simply was solved with no animation other than idling, at least you see the feedback from dealt damage by showing numbers of dealt / received damage and mostly the victim animating from taking a hit.

This topic might be a rather personal thing, but I mostly prefer to mute any ingame music and play something else in the background. This game actually managed to keep me from doing so. While it's quite difficult for me to name the direction the music the game is following, I'm really enjoying the it, as well as the electro swing music played in town. However, I was a bit surprised when I figured out it's actually from a different artist: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mSBzvhud3Io . Well, they surely picked a song matching this rather crazy ambiente of a city.

Overall, This game might become my personal favorite game released (in EA state) this year, though there are a few points someone might complain about:

-The character control turns out a bit confusing in multiple aspects, as well as the camera control trying to center the screen but messing up sometimes
-After finnishing the current existing story, I understand people recommending to wait until the full development of the game. You can finnish the existing main story really quick and it suddenly just stops. That was a little turn off when I reached this point.
-I hope that some items will experience various stat modifications in the future. As of now, most items have the same stats and are basically just reskins. A much higher complexity would allow more strategy coming from the equipment.

Overall, I certainly do like this game. Keep it up, Behemoth!<3
