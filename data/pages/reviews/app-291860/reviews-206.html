\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game, if you have a laptop with some beef or any PC you should be able to run this game. Sadly it does not work on my laptop which has no beef so I have to use my PC.

Pros:
+Soundtrack - enjoyable and pleasing, like that of Castle Crashers and BattleBlock Theater
+Humor - the sense of humor you can expect from The Behemoth, it's just overall sillyness
+Gameplay - unlike other turn-based tactical games like XCOM, the game's a bit faster and less complicated, but the depth certainly is there
+Story - the world has been plunged into an apocalypse after the bear crashes into Earth and turns the landscape into hexagons, with bear blood raining from the sky... this game's story is ridiculous, but it's surprisingly good
+Characters - you can play as a blueberry farmer, a princess, a "Demi-clops", a Spanish conquistador, a cupcake, a unicorn, a cactus fly, the possibilites are truly endless, and then there's the costumes...
+Graphics - the beautiful hand-drawn and cartoony graphics straight outta Newgrounds will never get old

Mehs:
Length - the game is short, with not many missions, but this is an Early Access game, and there's always The Pit and online multiplayer to keep you satisfied, and plenty of characters to collect

Cons:
-Early Access - as of now, the game is in Early Access and is as of now unfinished, so the game has a few bugs and no cloud save

Overall, Pit People is a wonderful turn-based tactical adventure game that brings a sense of humor and great gameplay. I'd give this a 9.3/10 at the point it's at, but if it improves, I'll review this better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMild spoiler warning, but honestly there is not much to spoil.
***
The mechanics work perfectly, but for now, everything is... basic. Everything works, yes, but it's just the bones with basic strats and counters. There's nothing really special put in yet. You can feel the artwork's funk, that Behemoth style of doing things. Whoever's been working with this has put heart into it.


The world is appealing, it calls for adventure. I could vision theories of what I'm about to face in this game. will there be random or conditionally spawning legendary/unique monsters, can I recruit them as well, will there be hunt quests for them, can I get fat loot out of some rare or secret quests, what will happen in the main storyline as it gets done and so forth. It's a vast land in there and I want to search it's nooks 'n crannies for every conceivable piece of equipment and lore I can find. Or, you know... that's just how it feels in the game's open world.

Ah, the story... the story is left undone. There's literally one main story quest after you get to free exploring. Not only that, so far it took some odd turns before it's unprecedented end. It was a disappointment, to say the least. "Hey the main story ends here but you can still grind for exp... right?" There WERE big warning signs about it though, so it's not like it came as a surprise. It just surprised how little there was.

I get the feel there was multiple ways to do this and none was clearly chosen, every road that could've been taken was as much fun and rewarding for the producer and the consumer so almost none was scrapped. It's still too open to say anything else. I can imagine though. I would very much like an endgame battle where I could slap the antagonist's space nipples with swords and maces. I would like to see the characters grow a bit, y'know a little backstory for them so they won't feel like they're just slapped in. I'd love to see some stuff even after the main story is finished, that's like giving diamonds to me. (or you can just give the 'mondz, man. Give 'em. Come on, gib. Gib!)

Yes, much is to be done. I'm still recommending it, but don't get your hopes up. Buy it now if you feel like supporting Behemoth, but for those who don't care about that: it's not worth yet. At all.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor me personally this might be Behemoth's best game so far. Granted they've only had 4 games, Alien Hominid, Castle Crashers, Battleblock Theater, and now Pit People. The humor is like those games but at its best. The narrator brings so much to the game surprisingly. The art style is the typical Behemoth artstyle and looks great as usual. Now, this is Early Access as of this review and the main story is not complete. It'll take you only about an hour an a half, maybe 2, to complete. However it takes a lot of different gameplay mechanics from other games that greatly increase the game's life outside of the story. The combat is like Fire Emblem or Xcom (more like Fire Emblem) where it's turnbased strategy, a sort of rock-paper-scissors mechanic of maces good against helmets, etc. Similar to many classic JRPG's there is a world map to roam and explore, where you can encounter many different types of enemy parties. Like Darkest Dungeon and Xcom, there is a base and like Darkest Dungeon, you have a choice of different, random missions that you can buy provisions for before setting out on the mission. And lastly, like Pokemon, you can capture and collect enemies and use them in your party. Now, many of these mechanics are like those of some Rogue-likes however this is not a rogue-like. If a character dies, they go back to town instead of perma-death. Along the early access path it would be nice to see a hardcore mode with permadeath in it. So far it is a great game and fifteen bucks is more than worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
