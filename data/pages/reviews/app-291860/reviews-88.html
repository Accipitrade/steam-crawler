\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first heard about this game being a strategy game, I was worried I'd end up being unable to finish it. My strategies in games often consist of rushing in and beating up my enemies while hoping I'm stronger than they are, so I thought I'd fail mission after mission. It's not as difficult as I thought it would be, thankfully, and after so long I've found that I enjoy planning out my fights, at least a little bit.

In stories, I like watching characters and how they interact with each other. Pit People is full of charming and funny characters, and in my opinion, reading the dialogue between them is one of my favorite parts of the game. I love it so much that I'm actually feeling encouraged to complete every side mission so I can see it all. I enjoy the humor that The Behemoth put in this game as well.

The closest thing to a complaint that I have about this game is that I feel that the main story is kind of short. As stated before, though, the game's full of side missions, both ones that stand on their own and ones that have a connected story, so it doesn't really feel like it's lacking content. There's also the whole entire 5 maps to explore (and some secrets in each map as well), competitive online gameplay (which, I have to admit, I've never touched because I'm sure I'd get destroyed), and multiplayer (which I also haven't touched, but I'd like to play multiplayer and see how it works).

All in all, I'd say this game is worth the price tag. If you like strategy games or The Behemoth, I recommend playing this. Even if you've never played a strategy game before, Pit People feels like a good place to start.

And it really does all make sense now... \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game isn't for everyone. The tl;dr version of the below is that if you're looking for a more in-depth, complex roster management game, then move along. This is more of a casual, goofy party game that goes to the extreme of not taking itself seriously. Its whole design seems to strongly revolve around being as non-complicated as possible, so as to make itself a rare case of both fast paced *and* turned based.

I was expecting a lot more involvement in my characters' development and customization. Leveling up isn't very rewarding or engaging, it's just a flat stat boost, and takes quite a few levels just to gain one extra point of damage, for example. I really would have expecting some branching paths in their growth, maybe new abilities or anything like that.

And equipment-wise, it's simuatanously under and overwhelming. There's countless items in the game, but you'll find many are simply reskins and there's not much progression here. After doing a bunch of quests over about 8 hours, I was still mostly just using the same gear I started out with aside from a couple that had a tiny lil' proc chance. Made me lose interest in doing quests for the sake of loot. The only real armor is shields and helmets, no other equipment slots aside from that.

Most of the stat variety between your team is going to boil down to their species, however you can get one of each species in a short span of time and there is no difference between two members of the same species. All level 99 humans have the same stats, for example. I think it might of been nice if they at least game with some randomized traits to give them some more personality or something...

I will say the story and narrative is somewhat amusing though. But with so many whimsical and silly characters in the world it takes place in, it woulda been nice if the main story cast was a bit more colorful. I found the human crew a bit dull alongside all the other interesting creatures in the world.\t\t\t\t\t
