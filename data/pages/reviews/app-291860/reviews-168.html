\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun and challenging, without being punishing. A lighthearted glee is evident in every aspect of this turn based, hex-map strategy and collection game. Music is some of the best, and the Narrator is great.

Unfortunately there seems to be very little of said Narrator. With his the only voice of the game, other than gibberish played over text, Pit People can rely too heavily on its otherwise excellent sound design. I found that with no actual dialogue to draw me in I just skipped past more and more of the admittedly clever and funny story as soon as I read it.

Plays well with a good strategic options base. Tons of mostly cosmetic items to collect. Wide and shallow with equipped items dictating character class for humans while creatures you recruit can only use their default skills. Since you can't predict the type of fight you'll face this specialization limits their utility and use.

Too much fun with co-op, can't wait for more.\t\t\t\t\t
