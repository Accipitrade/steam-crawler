\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a game that's early access, it really doesn't feel like it. I'm going to highlight what I feel is wrong/needs to be improved, in hopes the devs see it and consider it. For a review, I'll keep it short. A very interesting world, full of LOTS of stuff, like LOTS, complete with music, great sound, and mostly good controls The music is amazing, I'll have it stuck in my head for days. Behemoth has yet another wonderful game out on the market. On to some gripes about the game.

The PC controls are mostly good, but could use some fine tuning to make them not so wonky when selecting loadouts, the default controls are questionable, but rebindable so that's not that big of a deal. Also, in the intro, the load out screen and differences between "tiers" of items (i.e. cosmetics, light helmets, heavy helmets) isn't at all explained. It was super super confusing because I had no clue what I was doing. So the tutorial could use some more fine tuning as well.

There are other things not very well explained, like what's on the HUD, I didn't know what I was looking at, or what it meant for a while. The items, such as campfires, and how to buy them isn't at all explained. I think my #1 glaring problem was the lack of explanation, so if you're reading this, Behemoth, it would be good to look into that.

These are the most notable issues I saw, but damn this game is freaking fun. Doing Coop is sooooo much fun, but online is a mess. The biggest thing this game could benefit from is more flushed out tutorials, really explaining what everythng is. Keep it up Behemoth, it's a damn good game. Also, put people queued together on the same team. Or at least have an option to.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm excited to see the limited amount of content grow.
Behemoth has always done a great job putting together unique games with their own sense of humor and style. As of early access, they still deliever with their signature style.
The art of Dan Paladin is ever present. Simple, humorous, delightful. There were a few times the art was pixelated but I'm sure that'll be fixed. Some of it (art, map, UI) feels a bit overly busy.
As far as content, UI, and fighting system, alot needs to be worked on. The fighting system is simple but shallow and there's two story missions along with a few side quests. I imagine over the next year (in which Behemoth works towards release) mechanics and story will be built upon.
So if you're buying it now, know you're only going to get a few hours of enjoyment at first. But if you believe the track record of the Behemoth team and their wonderfully unique spirit, it's worth to get the game now and support the team!\t\t\t\t\t

The lineage for the company is one worth reading about, and If you're familiar with Newgrounds, you probably know the long history there,but these guys have been at their unique blend of game-spices since I was a wee lad, and it was a bootstraps effort to put the dream in motion and it wound them here on release of a hilarious fourth incarnation of a game only they can make and would be a pity to miss. Unlike anything the Behemoth has done yet, the gameplay is layered, but easy to pick up, about as time consuming as you'd like it to be. The trailers speak for themselves, but the voicework from Stamper, the animation from Dan, the coding of a team that wrangles the madness together know what they are doing-if the early access tag has you 2spook'd. Cmon. It's the Behemoth, so for what I was anticipating, I was not at all let down. I say fifteen is a steal, and with all the art and animation and style and humor in here, you're not likely to find a game that has the kind of charm and wit with gameplay to match. Unique In short spurts or hours of competing for leaderboard rank, this hexa-brawly-turn-based strategy has more to offer than many out there of it's nature. As far as early access goes, this is tip top fare and well worth your $15.\t\t\t\t\t
