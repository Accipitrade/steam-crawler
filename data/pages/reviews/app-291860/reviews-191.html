Land shattered on hexagons, time starts to be experienced by turns.

If you here for a narrated story campaign it's not here yet. Only introductional part and single quest. With damn good cutscenes.

But there several dozens of one-stop side quests on a world map, 4 bigger side quest-lines, nineteen species to catch/recruit and more than thousand of collectible accessories and weapons to collect. Exploration, adventuring, quest dialogs and stories, secrets and rarities, battles in a pit versus other players which beside being showcase of tactical genius also works as showcase of collected rarities and oddities.

I'd say it feels like fusion of Shining Force 2 with adult version of Adventure Time cooked on Behemoth humour.
Oh and music is perfect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh yes, Pit People, my new favorite! Even on its unfinished state.

THE GAME

The game is a turn-based RPG strategy with the witty humor and a beautiful cartoonish art style that made me fell in love with The Behemoth's games. Its a simple, yet clear take to Turn-Based Strategies, a genre that I was completely unfamilliar of. You can explore the world to kidnap recruit other fighters not only because of their stats, but their names too, find more gold MONEYYYYYY and loot, and undertake a LOT of other sidequests other than story mode.(more on that later) The game also has a multikill indicator and its the most satisfying thing ive ever heard.

THE STORY

Although short, it was hilarious! And honestly the very antagonistic narrator (who's VA also voiced the narrator from BBT) that is also a giant space bear is slowly becoming my new favorite bad guy and might even kick Bill Cipher from the TV show Gravity Falls (which is kinda hard tbh) from the "Best Villan" pedastal of my heart. The first snippet of the story already features fourth wall-beraking antics, and a really meta narrator that proclamed himself God of this post-apocalyptic hex-patterned earth. Plus the fully animated cutscenes are really cool!

MUSIC

The. Soundtrack. Is. Gud. Pls. Listen. 11/10
A portion of the soundtrack (AKA Patric Catani's contribution) is available on Patric's Bandcamp for purchase and Spotify for streaming!

THINGS I WAS slightly BOTHERED ATSuggestions

>It was kinda hard to know what fighter you are currently selecting right now even with my 23-inch monitor on my PC well then, its been updated to a yellow outline for the selected fighter! It's now eaiser to see what fighter are you currently selecting right now.
>the lack of a mac version aaaaaaaa (im aware that a mac verison would come soon tho)
>more compatibility with touch screened laptops, so I was able to try the game in a laptop with a touch screen although with a option to include a end turn button on screen (and shaking btw) was a bit helpful, I personally think that it needs a bit more essential controls like cancel placement and player switch for tablet PCs (Surface Pro, Wacom Mobile Studio Pro, and the likes) and 2-n-1 convertables (Lenovo Yoga)

Update:
R I S E
THE DAWN OF THE FULL VERSION IS N E A R

and over the course of several months, we've seen mutiple improvents in gameplay, visuals, STOOOORRYYYYYYY, and Quality of life things. Including the new permadeath mode which is introduced recently. Honestly i just can't wait for update 6. Oh and the game will be $19.99 on full release so, while the autumn steam sale is going on rn and winter sale coming soon, BUY THE GAME!!!! :D

OH SWEET BUTCHEECKS ITS HERE
With the story its completed glory, its a wild ride from begining to end. Im not saying any spoilers, but there's a song you can sing along to i guess? Shorter than expected, its still a great one, lots of laughable, unique (and questionable) moments in the game that made it worth its short time. If you feel that the story is quite short, try picking up some of the game's sidequests and questlines spanned from the game's 5 worldmaps! Also new meeting pip cutscene is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ beautiful.\t\t\t\t\t
