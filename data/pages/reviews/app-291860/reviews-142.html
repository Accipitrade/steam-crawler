\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOMG where do I start? Okay first of all if you are a fan of turn based strategy and must play all of them stop reading this and buy the game now. If you are new to the genre but are interested in tactical games I can truly recommend this for you. If you are seasoned turn based strategy fan this is also for you. For starters I want to say that the combat is very well streamlined. In most TBS games you move your character, choose a direction and then choose a type of attack. If you are a healer you choose your character, move him into position, choose your spell and finally heal who you want to heal. In Pit Poeple this ihs simplified. The healer character will simply heal whoever it can within it's range while dealing damage to itself. This may seem oversimplified but it tends to work and speed up the healing process.

Combat is also streamlined and faced paced. You simple move your character into postion and he will choose to attack whomever he wants. It sounds dumb but it works well in the game. There area good amount of weapons, armor and charactrer classes to keep you guys.

In terms of audio the music is just simply fantastic, You can tell they had alot of fun coming up with great lively muslc that will tickle your funny bone. Sounds effects and the narration is through the roof. I did find some of the jokes totally lame but In the spirit of the game it was perfect.

To sum it up, I'm an avid TBS gamer and this was a complete and total surprise!

+Excellent animation and graphics
+Psuedo open world gameplay
+Excellent memorable soundtrack and voice acting
+Simplified TBS controls that are streamlined and keeps it fast paced

-Main quest super short
-still in early access

Once this game is complete I can recommend it to anyone who has ever liked a TBS tacitcal game. Kudos to behemoth

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a review from playing a fair amount in Early Acces. Note that my experience is from update 2/3 era so if you are reading this at a later time it might not be matching what the game currently is.

The Feel:
This is a perfect game for people that like silly things. There is weird humor and references going on all over the place. The one story mission and the few side missions that are available for play at the moment are really fun. There is also enough going on the world map to keep you busy.

Gameplay:
As this is a sort of tactical positioning game mixed with random elements, it does pretty much what it says on the box. You set up your team of silly fighters, you place them where you want them to go and chaos happens. This is not a bad thing. Even when I am defeated I can sigh with a smile. Just the sheer amount of weaponised silly items (giant banana, crazy hats, throwing a gun at someone called "self defense") just makes me laugh every time i see a new item. At the time of writing there are about a 1000 items to collect in the game to customize your fighters with, and every time I run into a new pack of fighters with new hats/weapons I get excited. The fact that you can only take the last standing opponent with you makes it so you have to choose which of the shiny new things you want NOW :).

Regarding the price:
Worth every penny. You buy it once, and then you have hours worth of gameplay, especially when you consider there is a great amount of "gotta catch 'em all" involved.

Regarding replayability:
In update 3 they added some daily tasks to do co-op with a buddy. Also you get an "Emperor's order" to give you some extra items the moment you complete a specific task. As these orders are infinite its a fine extra reward for stuff you'd be doing anyway. I think the biggest thing going for this game is definitely the collection of custom appearances and items that you can acquire.

Any cons?:
Like I said, it's still under developtment. I didn't notice anything that would interrupt the gameplay experience in terms of bugs or so.
That being said I'd like to see less daily tournament points awarded for people using the auto battler (that's right, you can even play your own fights as a spectator, while the computer dukes it out). At the moment I believe the ratio in the unfair challenge is that you get half points if auto battler was used during a fight. Since I use auto-battler a couple of times during the day for a few fights and still finished high up, I think the rate should be lower so that people that actually fight their own battles can feel more achievement. But then again I think this will be less of an issue when the player pool increases.

Final thoughts?
A great game for its price, especially when you are a completionist. As there is plenty of auto-save going on, it can also be played in short spans for when you only have a little time to relax. Everyone should have a 15 min. pit people break at work!
