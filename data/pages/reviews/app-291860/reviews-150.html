\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPRO: The first 10 hours are GREAT. I loved it.
Lots of fun. Unique writing, entertaining, fun to learn the mechanics.

CONS: After the first 10 hours, things start to feel repetitive and grindy.
You are battling for rep and to unlock cosmetic changes to your characters.
Every once in awhile you might unlock something that makes your character stronger.

OBSERVATIONS: The cosmetic content unlocks, purchasing quests, and posting REP for all to see feel like necessary add-ons to keep people returning to the game once the original content and new discovery feeling wears off. I haven't tried out the multiplayer, but its not a draw for me.

Although I love the game , Behemoth, and I'm happy to support them with my purchase, my 9 year old who LOVES all of the other Behemoth games doesn't even want to try the game out. I fear they missed the biggest consumer age group that drives their sales with this game. Here's hoping they are working on Castle Crashers 2 or Battle Block Theater 2 because this title might cause a huge problem for the studio if they don't come up with some way to draw in the younger players.

I don't feel drawn to play the game repeatedly like I was with Castle Crashers or Battle Block Theater. Most of the replay value of those games were the easter eggs, leveling, and my son pushing me to play with him. The fact that he doesn't want to play this game at all means it won't get much use, which bothers me because I REALLY want this studio to succeed.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game based on the fact that it had an incredible 97% positive score from over 2300 reviews and Stamper voice acting. For the first hour or so I was very entertained by Stamper, but I noticed the combat felt extremely simplistic. By the last couple of hours I played it for I was half-convinced I was just not understanding the greatness that clearly must be there based on the reviews. After reading the negative reviews I think maybe I'm not crazy and the gameplay in general is very shallow.

I get that some people probably really like the collectathon aspect of the game where you capture all these different creatures, but when it comes right down to it all you're really doing is moving characters to appropriate hexes and ending your turn while hoping they crit with their one attack which you don't even get to decide who it's used on in many cases.
Even the gear you equip your characters with has no real effect besides a few preset options like sword with shield or sword with bigger shield. The sword might be a candy cane or a golf club and the shield might be a cake or a playing card, but that has absolutely no effect other than cosmetic customization.

It seems like a lot of people are very loyal to Behemoth and I get that maybe they're also investing in the potential greatness of the game down the road, but as it stands this game is absolutely not one of the Best games on Steam in my opinion like the ratings would have you believe. I hardly ever review anything unless I think the reviews are very skewed in a way I don't agree with and this is one of those cases. I do not at all believe this game is worth $15 in its current state, but I understand why some people might buy it in good faith that it will be in the future and then some. To anyone else reading this please do more research on this game rather than relying on the review system if that $15 matters to you.

Edit: Just a recommendation. I found a game called Battle Brothers that is a turn based tactics game somewhat similar to this one that I would highly recommend you save your money for over this game. The actual replay value of this other game is much higher than this one. There are far more options to customize your team beyond aesthetics and that game is highly recommended without the need for fanboys to automatically give it a positive review since it's by a studio I haven't heard of and I don't know if they even have any other games.

I tried to play Pit People a few weeks ago to see if anything had improved, but the mind numbing repetitive nature of the gameplay instantly turned me off. I again highly recommend you do more research through gameplay videos rather than listen to reviews on Steam. This game can be summed up as kind of a silly chess with very little player agency. The style and vo is again very good, but the actual gameplay is garbage in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t
