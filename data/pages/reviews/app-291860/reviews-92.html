\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn summary, I've rated this positive but have significant warnings for other players.

The core gameplay, the battles, are shallow. There are different classes of unit and equipment, but really you can stick almost any combination together and win any battle by the same sort of margin. There's amazing customisation in terms of the weapons, armour, etc... except the vast majority is cosmetic. You might get bored of the gameplay quite quickly. Much of the world map is a jumble with stuff going on that seems to mean very littlle bar some wandering encounters and extra side quests on the way to your targets. There are multiplayer options (which may be fine, but I don't personally have the time and inclination for them).

What you are getting is non-stop cartoon humour (although also some surprisingly effective pathos occasionally), cutscenes with superb voice acting, and a general sense of fun. I really liked some of this, so I've enjoyed Pit People. But this means it is a sort of triumph of style over substance. I would recommend any potential player, especially one lookng for deep gameplay, considers this carefully.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People is an RPG with a turn based combat system. The story is completely ridiculous in a good way, filled with unusual creatures, wacky humour and jokey one-liners. You are Horatio, a simple blueberry farmer who the narrator tells us is the most boring person in the world, and you're on a quest to get revenge (I won't spoil why) on a huge bear who floats in space. Things only get stranger from there.

Battles are fun, but tactics are very simplistic, with the focus on making them flow quickly. Most characters only have one kind of attack, so you don’t have any interesting decisions to make about when to use a special ability. The only real control you have during a battle is the positioning of your squad of 6 characters on the hex grid. After you tell your people where to stand, they will decide which enemy to attack. You can influence this slightly by placing them next to a single enemy, denying them the choice. But as there are usually 6+ enemies in a battle, this isn’t always possible.

When they stand next to 2 or more enemies, they will often attack the “wrong” target, for example I saw so many times when they had a choice between an enemy on full health, and an enemy close to death, and they attacked the full health guy instead of taking the kill. Its not always possible to focus fire on what you consider to be the main threat, like a healer or a summoner. Trying to avoid friendly fire with AoE attacks, without precise control over the target area, is problematic.

You can recruit enemies and make them fight for you, by throwing a cage onto the last living enemy during a battle. There are a wide range of characters to choose from. Cupcakes heal allies by throwing their frosting, sacrificing a little of their own health. Vampires can grow wings to move further, and regain health after a successful attack. Pixies have a ranged AoE attack. Octoclops can confuse enemies, resulting in them attacking their friends. Choosing which characters are a good fit for your party is the most tactical thing you can do.

The narrator's voice acting is great and he has some funny, sarcastic lines. But no other character has proper voice acting, instead you hear a nonsensical “hurble burble blah”, which gets annoying after about an hour. Especially when you realise that they only have a couple of variations of their nonsense that they repeat constantly. The music is fantastic and I haven’t suffered from any bugs or crashes.

The main story is quite short, but there are lots of side quests scattered around the world map. After 26 hours I still haven’t done everything, which means a full price purchase is good value. The simple combat feels a bit repetitive after a while, but I was motivated to keep playing because I wanted to see what strange scenario I’d encounter next. In one side quest, a motivational speaker gives his students tips on how to be a better cannibal, while another is set in an arcade where the current champion kills new players before they can beat his high score.

Overall Pit People is a good game, worth checking out, but I just wish the developers had added more tactical depth to the combat to elevate it to being a great game.\t\t\t\t\t
