\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPure excellence as is the usual with The Behemoth. The animation is wonderful, the art style awesome, the humor hilarious and off the wall, the gameplay is super solid, the story is so far, priceless. I love the narrator. The game does kind of fail to explain its self very well, luckily I'm familiar with turn based, grid based, rpg games. The fact that it IS LOCAL CO-OP, makes it awesome for me and the wife. To be to the point, thus far it's a 8/10 easy. It could very well be as good as castle crashers in a lot of ways. We could have an award winner here.

PROS
-Great Behemoth style game just like Castle Crasher and Battle Block Theater.
-Wonderful humor/art style
-Solid turn based RPG gameplay, great animations
-Local Co-Op (as well as online, so far though it seems to be invite co-op only)
-Online Competitive play, if you're into that sorta... thing...
-Absolutely mind blowing soundtrack


CONS
-The tutorial is rather haphazard. While I slid right into playing, I didn't feel like how the game worked was very well explained, it just felt familiar enough to pick up and play.
-The quest system didn't seem to be explained at all, I didn't even realize I had "quests" outside of what the story narrative covered as the general 'story' quest. I didn't realize there were quests in a sort of quest log format that could be turned in for rewards until I saw my first quest complete pop up.
- Your "Home base" i.e the town you return to during your adventures to rest and upgrade party members, gets randomly moved about every 3 returns to it or so. It is only ever moved when you are in it, so you don't have to worry about it moving while you're gone, but you can't really make any exploration progress this way because you're continously being moved to new places you don't know.
-You have a limit to the amount of loot you can carry, you must return to town to deposit your booty and then head back out.
-Trading is not explained in co-op, at least not in local. When you return home in co-op you'll get a screen of loot between you and your partner, the left and right represent players 1 and 2 respectively. From the start nothing is 'selected', anything selected is automatically given to the other player. I say given, instead of trade, because if the other player doesn't select anything, they still get everything you clicked anyway for free. This isn't a problem, especially in co-op where maybe someone got something they don't need but the other does. The point is, what this screen even meant let alone how it worked, was not explained at all.
-So far no luck match making. I'm not saying it doesn't work, I just am saying I've tried to find someone to do the Pit Co-Op with and gotten nothing three times over two days.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought Pit People the first week it was released in Early Access. I have been hooked for the entirety of the few days in between the game releasing, and this current moment while writing this review. Its dialogue is incredible, and showcases the brilliance of the writers at the Behemoth. The gameplay is not too simple, but not too complex. Needless to say, I love it.

Pros:
-Amazing story
-Extremely immersive world
-Great sense of humor
-Well balanced gameplay
-Fun in general
-Stamper as narrator again (Stamper = Battleblock Theater narrator)
-Balanced Co-op
Cons:
-There isn't enough stuff in the game because it is a week after release in Early access
-That makes me sad

I recommend this game to people who like strategy games, RPGs, turn-based combat, great writing, and having fun with friends. Anyone who doesn't like any of those has an excuse not to buy the game.\t\t\t\t\t
