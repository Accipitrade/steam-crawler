The Behemoth once again bring their charming artstyle and unceasably comedic dialogue to the strategy realm, with hints of the team member catching of pokemon, thousands of unit cosmetics with varying functionality seen in every other jrpg, and the afforementioned charm from Battleblock & Castle Crashers.

Quests and Characters are spread accross the world that inexplicably want to kill you just for 'almost running them over with your wagon.' Reference after reference is hidden everywhere from the NPCs, to the Areas, and even the occasional hairstyle. You wrap yourself into this world of crazy people as a gang of fanged beasts attempt to destroy all our precious cow painted ten-gallon hats, just to give an example of some of the thing you can encounter (and no I did not make that up.)

With the 20 different races to catch (one of them is a clone of another) of varying pros, cons, & strategies entailing them. Each one brings a new set of rules to follow and it's up to you how you want to utilize them in battle as a few are flexible enough to fit different roles. With synergies galore and a new weapon/hat/shield earned after every battle, no one team ever feels the same (unless of course you made it be the same as another team.)

One of it's prime features is the thing that the title speaks of, THE PIT, a multiplayer battle arena where you can duke it out 1v1, 2v2, or face 3 waves back-to-back alone or with a friend by your side. With gold cosmetics able to win via the tournament, you'll be able to show off your champions in their new beautiful outfits in no time!

The biggest problem with the game atm is that its story is missing, something clearly stated by The Behemoth. Although the multiplayer has been the star of their last two games, the story shed light onto the land's lore and brought it's own crowd of players there for a singleplayer experience. I can't feel truly satisfied with this greatness until I see an end to the story, HOWEVER, the core gameplay mechanics are still enough to bring you to enjoy and play it while still in it's early access. If you want to go through the story in one fell swoop then you should wait til full release

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo any person who may find this message floating across the endless oceans,

I am writing this from a vastly remote location, a place far from home. Not sure how it happened. One second I was waiting on the cashier at the dollar store, on the way back from work, then the next moment an enormous wormhole or something, a huge black hole you only see in overly ambitious sci-fi movies sucked us all up, then plopped us out in this....this strange, strange world. All of us, everybody at the store, including the store itself. Immediately, we were in the hands of the gods. Their screwed up gods, I tell ya. Colorless fellas with sadistically twisted, morbid and disgusting heads or headpieces, I don't know what to call them, were chanting to a mass-proclaimed deity or something, shouting "Chog, chog!", rooting for the enormous beast to devour us whole in a sort of sacrificial ritual, but all I could think of was how similar their blindly devout chants sounded to the "Chug, chug!" you may hear in a 2005 frat party home video. All of them died, one by one, like mindless sheep in a particularly explicit slaughterhouse. It chills my bones to just think about all the blood and entrails, all the suffering I've witnessed. Some enforcers of the deity, coming at us with loaded machineguns, gigantic baseball bats, rocket launchers that shot cruelly sharp emerald swords and table forks the size of my leg. Huge, smirking bats, furry spiders who all for some reason looked like my mom's girlfriends and one eyed moster people. They all died before my own two eyes, all of them from the store. The naggy, slowpoke clerk, the smelly dude behind me in line, the old lady browsing the kitchen utensils, even her dog who was just patiently waiting, leashed at the entrance. Shot to a bloody damn pulp, that puppy. There were some other people, too. Lots of people, actually. Some of them looked normal, like us, confused and scared. Others looked like them, like the strange people from the crowd, eyes glazed at the arena. Some of those seemed to be joyed by the commotion. Religious followers of the deity, readily prepared to sacrifice themselves, as I found out later. Freakish. Death, death. A resounding thought. I closed my eyes. I felt a thunk, then heard a bang. When I woke up, I was surrounded by maybe 5-6 people, with a mixed variety of strange deformations and dressing habits. Silly hats, ridiculous weapons, fish bowls, cookies, all sorts of weird crap turned into make-shift shileds and weapons. I couldn't understand their gibberish language, but I felt that they had good intentions, towards me at the least. I got handed a few tools to make myself useful. Weapons, terribly unfashionable hats, et cetera. I've been roaming these murderous fields in their company every since. It's been fine so far, I took it better than expected. I was always used to changes, even before, when pops died. However, I am starting to be afraid. Even with all the gibberish, I am beginning to understand the words they are saying. And there is one, just one, even these battle-hardened warriors are deathly afriad to speak out loud. A name, only ever spoken of in whispers, from the shadows. A name that has given them, now us, something to be afraid. A massacrist vagabond, murderous fiend they seem to be....gibbering? Deep in the night, guarding our nomadic tents, they stutter his name. The killer. The murder machine, Horatio. We're all starting to be terribly afriad. I don't wanna die. I'm still just a boy. I put this message in a bottle, hoping it would reach someone. Please, tell my mom I love her very much. Tell her I stole the money from the stash, it wasn't Ellie. Sorry, Ellie, I owe ya one! Please, give Birdcrack, my sweet sweet dog a treat in my name! That is all, world! kthxbyenow\t\t\t\t\t
