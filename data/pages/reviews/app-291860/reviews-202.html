\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game so far is good. I mean, really, so far the combat is fun.

However...

- This game's controls are ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ infuriating. It was clearly made for consoles. The responsiveness and way the mouse works in this game is just bonkers.

- Related to controls: specifically, navigating the fighter inventory area is clumsy and cumbersome.

- Related to controls: the whole game is built upon "Left Mouse Button selects, RMB cancels". It's just not user-friendly for PC.

- The amount of feedback/information about stats, items, etc. is killing me. The number of times I stared at the screen asking myself "what does this mean?" tells me that they did not get enough feedback on the useability/UX side.

- During combat: the art style, colors, animations during the planning phase makes it really hard to see what you're doing. I've made a number of mistakes moving my players around and stuff because the way they display the planning phase is confusing. There needs to be more of a clear delineation between characters, and a better way to give you a "high-level" overview of the battle during this phase.

- The size of everything is like 300% too big. It was clearly made for a TV sitting 8 feet away from you rather than a monitor 2 feet away.

TL;DR it feels like a bad port. The way that everyting is presented to you on screen is clearly indicative of a console game and that really hurts the PC experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst thing to note is that game being early access just means the story is not complete. Gameplay is finished, and there's a lot a of quests (which vary a lot) and stuff to do, including PvP mode, and extra difficulty. However, expect that after 2 hours, you'll have finished the story in it, which doesn't mean the game ends.

I loved the presentation of the game. classic Behemoth style, with a lot of humor, the same hilarious narrator from Battleblock Theater, and a lot personality behind it. Great music, with a lot of variety. For me, one of the selling points.

The gameplay is also great, however, is much simpler than your average turn based grid strategy game. Your characters attack on their own if they are on range of an enemy, for example. If you're looking for "hardcore gameplay", you might be disappointed with it. But I think that it goes in the right direction and it fits the game, while being very accesible for people not so into the genre. This is neat because the game has local and online co-op, so you can play this with a lot of people, not just hardcore turn based gamers.

Overall, i really liked the game. I would recommend it if you like the presentation, and you want to be playing it just to explore the world that the Behemoth people have created. If you're looking for a story driven campaign already finished, wait for the full release.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer: I don't think this game is bad. I don't think this game is good. I think this game is OK and got too many positive reviews, which try to push nonsence that if devs made 2 good games all their games will be good. Also I felt like those reviews aren't helpful, so this is my attempt to write helpful review. Next, this is early access, and I think criticism will help devs to create better game. And finally dev said that he not promises to add new units. So here what I think:

Good:
+A lot of customization options
+Music
+Artstyle could be better, but I like it
+Interesting recruiting mechanic, where you should kill everyone except your target and put him in cage.

Bad:
-You can't choose target for attacking. You just come in attack range and when turn ends your troops choose targets from those that they have in attack reach. In theory this is interesting, but in some places it just becomes annoying. For example on quest to kill Necroduke your troops will attack anyone except of Necroduke itself.
-Next, it lacks tactic. Your troops can: attack, heal, summon allies and throw nets. I believe that's all. And you can't even chose target, you just move them and they act by themselves.
-And to previous point, it lacks variety as whole. You can see all units in first 1-2 hours. It could be interesting pokemon-like game if it had more variety and dangerous monsters, but there like 18 monsters. And developer said that they won't promise to add new monsters.
-And so it quickly becomes boring. Battles are mostly the same where you dance around trying to cover ranged units and healers.
-I felt like battle result depends from party composition too much. And you can't see enemy party composition before actual battle.
-It has difficulty settings, but no actual reward for playing on insane difficulty. I expected special hats, monsters or at least achievements. I get it, older games had no rewards for hard difficulty, but now I'm so spoiled that want rewards for playing harder difficulty than everyone.
-No actual explanation what soldier stats mean, just some confusing graphs. Same for attack types and moves.
-Level up just refills your HP. I haven't noticed anything else. So low level troops with fast levelling much better than useless veterans with long levelling.
-Sometimes scenery blocks view, for example, again, castle of Necroduke has walls that won't let you see what happens in hexes behind them.

Ugly:
*Humour is not for everyone. I had a giggle in the beginning, but that's all. That's fine I guess, not everyone has same sence of humour.
*Narrator is not that good. I found him not funny, just plain annoying. But again, this is subjective.



Feel free to add your points in comments, but be constructive.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEarly access, so absolutely do not get it if you are expecting a full game with fleshed out story.

That being said, I would still recommend this game to fans of Behemoth and people who like to play games in jolly co-operation. Gameplay is dynamic, though at the moment certain balancing could be (and should be happening) done. Units with high HP like the troll just steamrolls through even if pit against pixies. Support units like mascots and gnomes do not really have a felt impact, you'd rather bring something else of more worth than what is basically chip damage fodders. I have confidence that I can expect more from Behemoth, and do not regret my first ever early access purchase.

Would like to add that the music choice is superb and very "Behemoth", reminiscent of their past games.

TL:DR
Get if Behemoth fan, wait for full version if hesitant.\t\t\t\t\t
