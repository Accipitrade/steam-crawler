\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: In my opinion, I would just watch the cutscenes on a YouTube Let's Play and go get real tactics game like Invisible, Inc.

Pit people is a strategy (tactics) game with a comedic twist only Behemoth can bring and a lot of appearance customization options. Unfortunately that's where the good parts end, as the game does not satisfy my itch for a tactics game.

Strategizing is definitely the meat and bones in a good Tactics game. For Pit People, positioning is very important in the game as it determined who each unit might attack (you can't select exactly who to attack unless you position very well), the battles are overly simplified to the point of sucking down all the enjoyment out of it! I'm amazed how all the elements that makes a good strategy game were dismissed in lieu of some comedy you already get on Castle Crashers or BattleBlock Theater.

Like I mentioned, there are a lot of fun options to dress up your characters which add a SOME depth to choosing your units and what they can do, but the variety for recruits are disappointedly limited and there isn't much to do in the game more than read out the hit-and-miss "funny" dialogue for the story line. At first I thought it was just because of the lack of content (Started playing during Vanilla or patch 1), but after trying again these last few weeks with their last update (and plans on leaving Early Access very soon), I noticed that that is it for the game and I saw no notable differences in terms of gameplay or substance.

In short, I would look away from this game. Hopefully at some point the game would get interesting enough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first heard of this game, it was in a yogscast video, a long, LONG time ago... (I'll link it; https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BFl4FHNTJx0 ) This is practicly why I bought the game, because of the artstyle, the voice acting and the gameplay...

Pros:
-amazing story
-incredible voice acting by the great William Stamper himself
-the battles are fun
-loads of funny little easter eggs on the map
-fun and challenging gameplay
-over 21 different monsters to capture
-over 3500 different cosmetics
-a lot of really fun sidequests, ranging from beating up cupcakes to following a detective troll
-8 save slots
-Pupondely hondely hopley hobba, peshnoki tooki, pesh toodidoo, antonki whonki donkahong
-you can stun enemies from your cart, so that you don't have to fight them
-all characters do something different
-the storyline intertwines with their other titles, like BattleBlock Theater

Cons:
-the battles are sometimes infuriating
-the battles take a long LONG time to finish
-Elite enemies exist... (these are stronger, and will relentlessly focus you on the world map untill you inevitebly get in a battle with them, they can't be stunned either)
-sometimes you don't know what tiles you can walk on and which ones you can't in battles
-bosses sometimes are ridiculously strong... (Like a certain boss in story mode which got nerfed a month after I beat it and got my ass whooped at the same time
-The prices in the store are ridiculously high... 7000G for a merc? you can LITTERALLY go out into the field with a cage, and capture like, 2... (the item shop is good though, you can buy items you never came accross before)

Overall: I extremely recommend this game, there are a few bugs and glitches here and there, but overall it's a great game!\t\t\t\t\t

The overall game feels like it has no direction and is just a mashup of ideas. Overall mechanics are also deeply flawed e.g. you cannot choose who to attack. This is a huge issue considering it plays on a hexagonal grid, allowing your character to be adjacent to two enemies simultaneously. The AI chooses who you attack and it can be random each round so the whole strategy aspect of the game is hamstringed. I absolutely loved the art style, incredible voice acting, and overall idea behind the game but I can't bring myself to play it any further because it's....boring. If I can't choose who to attack, can't get gear that's really worth anything, and don't get a solid idea of what's actually occuring in combat situations I can't continue playing. I was hoping for a Final Fantasy Tactics or Ogre Battle style battle mechanic and you get nothing remotely close to that degree of polish. I'd avoid it unless it's dirt cheap.\t\t\t\t\t
