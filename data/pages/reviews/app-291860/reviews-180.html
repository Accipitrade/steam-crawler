\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHighly recommended, especially for fans of The Behemoth.

At the time of this review the game is in early access and only contains about 1-2 hours of campaign, but there is much more to do after you've finished what's been released so far.

Pit People is an easily accessible, turn-based, tactical/strategy game.

In the campaign you start out with a small party of weird-o heroes who traverse an open world searching for quests, loot, revenge, and fighters. As you progress you are able to capture enemies and collect gear to add to your armory, giving you the chance to fine tune your party into a ruthless quad-killing machine.

After completing what's available of the main story line you and your party are given free reign of the world. As you grab more fighters and gear you're encouraged to put them to the test in the pit where your party is pitted against "unfair AI" or other players online.

The campaign and side quests are brimming with moxie. This whole game is just full of it, even the environment dances to the upbeat soundtrack.

PROS:
-Local and online CO-OP
-Online multiplayer VS
-Fantastic hex turn-based combat
-AI brawling
-Easy to access, difficult to master
-HIlarious
-RPG-ish elements that get the "ooo! numbers going up!" part of your brain tingling
-Lots of stuff to collect
-Lots of stuff to explore
-Insane difficult mode
-Ability to refuse space-bear's commands and not die

CONS:
-It's in early access, but some minor UI issues.
-At this point in time the campaign is very short, but you're getting it for cheap and there is so much more to do than the campaign.

In conclusion:

If you love Behemoth games and want to play it now, get it, it's worth it and in a completely playable state. If you don't like the idea of a short campaign maybe purchase it now at the lowered price and leave it in your backlog until the whole game is released.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People is the newest nugget of raw charm from our friends at The Behemoth. It plays like a sort of manager-slash-isometric strategy game, with various units dressed in silly attire. The game doesn't by any means feel "Complete" but it's still in early access, so the amount of story that's out can be forgiven. However, I do have a few gripes, such as levelling up, aside from filling your health bar at a critical moment, feels inconsequential. However, such a gripe can be overlooked for me. Given Pit People's current price I will cautiously reccomend it, you'll love this game if you come to Behemoth for the charm and cartoony gore, however if you're looking for a strategy game, maybe look at a video first, instead of my poorly-written review.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat could I say - I fall in Love. I feel unconditional, pure, raw Love for Pit People

This game was on my radar since fellow named TotalBiscuit made a first impression video on it from PAX. Since then I was patiently waiting for time to come so I could play another game from The Behemoth - studio so different with their vision, with exciting and ridiculous sense of humor.

Day of release has come, but I wasn't entirely sure to dip my toes into this fun loving, colourful water from get go, and I wanted to wait for full/finished product.
Everything changed when I watched video by another fellow named Jesse Cox. Him and gentleman I already mentioned few sentences before, started series together. Watching them play, and having fun encourage me to jump right in without any hesitation.

Oh boy! Oh boy! This game delivered.
Five hours in so far and I'm having so much fun. Too much fun. Insane amount of fun; and what I say now, might be a stretch, but I feel Pit People is fun loving cousin of Darkest Dungeon and XCOM in terms of excitement that I have in me while playing.

Gameplay is just too damn addictive.
Writing and humor so ridiculously charming.
Possibilities with customisation are endless, same as number of different characters or creatures.
Music is absolutely awesome, I can't wait for official soundtrack to come out.
Visuals as always nice to look at, with many, many, many, many, many, many bits and pieces that makes the world feel vibrant and alive.
Everything this shows how much though was put into this game, and you can see it from start thru multiple playthroughs.

Besides unfinished story mode this game is polished to perfection ... well, I guess not perfection because inventory management bugs me quite a bit, more than I would like - but with this exception it is perfection in my eyes in terms of gaming experience.

Well done oh mighty The Behemoth. You guys delivered another instant classic.

I know, I know I'm just gushing no much saying praises, but it is just because I am filled with satisfaction and overwhelmed by how good this game actually is, and I need to get out of my system and spread it around.
If you liked previous efforts from this devs, I don't know what are you waiting for; don't be like me and just do it! - BUY IT!!!
