\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI originally reviewed this game during Update 4, of Early Access. (Update 6 is the one as of now, AKA launch.) I gave it a negative review, based on a multitude of reasons, which I'll explain later. However, as of launch, the only changes made that I felt addressed the complaints I had were: the addition of permadeath mode, and the stat changes based on levels became more frequent. Even with these changes, some very major problems still stand with the game.

When I first started playing this game, I was really impressed by the general atmosphere of the game, as it was a big step up from the last Behemoth game, Battleblock Theater. The art was impressive, the music was fitting, and the humor was great as usual. I felt like the gameplay was lacking substance, but figured that it'd pick up later after the sort of "prologue" section of the game. This was a problem I saw in the reviews of the game for a while. A lot of positive reviews only had 5 or 6 hours into the game, which was what it took for me to even realize the game wasn't holding up.

The best point to probably start with is that the gameplay is repetitive. In the game, there are basically 3 different ways to play through a mission. There are missions that can be found in the worldmap, questlines that can be accessed before leaving the "home base" sort of area, and story missions, which I'd say are the most unique. The problem is, all of these missions are more or less reskins of each other. The task is always to A.) Fight a battle, or B.) Go somewhere and fight a battle there. Occasionally, there may be an environmental twist in the battles, like a jump pad, or a structure that has to be broken. Either way, every mission is just the same gameplay, over and over again. Even the story missions, which there are only 6 (maybe 7?) of, essentially are the same thing. These usually have a few more twists in store, but ultimately are not any different from any other battle that you'll fight in the game. (With ONE exception, that I've yet to see anywhere else in the game.)

This might not sound so bad, as long as the gameplay is enjoyable. But again, after fighting a few battles, they're more of a hassle than anything. Battles don't take any shorter than about 10 minutes, usually about 15 or more. Again, if the gameplay wasn't so simplistic, this might not be a problem, but it is. Most classes end up doing the same things repeatedly throughout the whole battle. Shields go to the front, healers and ranged units stay back, etc. While there are several classes, their gameplay mechanics rarely ever vary in battles. A lot of people I've talked to have argued that the real strategy comes into the game in team composition. While I'd partially agree this is true, it's not enjoyable fighting a drawn out battle when more than half the strategy has already been decided beforehand. In most cases as well, you won't know what kind of team you're going up against, in PvE and PvP, so you can't plan to try and counter a certain composition.

Team composition isn't without its flaws either. In the game, there are 19 species you can obtain. Of these species, humans, cyclops, and ranged (technically just humans) are the only ones that can use at least two out of the 3: shield/secondary, melee/ranged, and helmet. A few can equip just a helmet. (Zombies, spidaurs, and pixies.) And the gorgon can equip a sword. So 7 out of 19 different units can even use an item to minimally differentiate their stats. The items for the other 12 species are purely cosmetic. Even so, of the items that do actually affect statistics of a unit, most of them are identical as well. These items are under ~15 different subsets (eg, no helmet, small helmet, big helmet) with most items under a subset having the exact same effects as the others. The items under the four melee subsets are generally a bit more variable. However, the ranged subsets, secondary/shield subsets, and helmet subsets, are more or less exact reskins, past subset choice.

Levelling is another feature that I'll touch on, but not go on for too long about because it has been improved from before. Every 10 or so level, the damage/healing stat of your unit will increase by one. This does make a significant difference, but I feel like when unit's loadout variation is so little, (and in more than 50% of cases, nonexistent) levels should have a bigger effect in order to compensate for this.

So, since there's only 19 units, and they're all virtually the same, minus level difference, eventually there's not really a point to encounters anymore. Once you have 1 of every unit, maybe an extra or two, there's not much purpose to the capturing mechanic. All you essentially get from battles then is gold and items, which 9 times out of 10 are effectively cosmetic. Gold helps you, you guessed it, buy more items, or buy supplies to help you win battles (and get items.) After you leave the town.

People I've talked to in the community who really like the game claim that the fun is centered in collecting all of the items, but honestly, this is a terrible way to give the game any replay value. After the main (~6 hour) story mode is completed, this pretty much becomes the main focus, with maybe completing all side missions being there as well. Gameplay is tedious and tiresome, and the reward of items that aren't going to make gameplay any more exciting are equally underwhelming.

There's a few other things. The PvP is probably the game's strongest point, but it's more drawn out than PvE battles, and usually end up around 20-30 minutes. These are gruelingly long, not much more enjoyable than PvE, and not rewarding. There is an insane mode, but it only really makes encounters more tiresome, similar to the permadeath mode in the game. If gameplay was more enjoyable and not so simplistic to begin with, then maybe these two modes would be less of a hassle to play.

The bottom line is that playing a game with stale gameplay and staler rewards, is a terrible experience. I gave this game a better rating when it was in Early Access, but in 2 updates and around 6 months, the MAJOR problems with the game remain practically unchanged.

TL;DR - The game lacks substance and boils down into repetition, boring gameplay, and no true goal past the underwhelmingly short story mode.

3/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had a friend tell me to check this out because it was done by the same people that made Castle Crashers and Battleblock Theatre. I was initially a bit hesitant because of the early access status and the turn based combat which I generally haven't cared for in games since the early 2000's.

The first hour or two may be rough in figuring out the quirks and getting your butt kicked, but once I got the hang of it, this game's charm and absolute craziness that made me love the previously mentioned two games finally hooked me in here. At first combat feels slow with the turn based movement, but a couple of hours it turned out to be fine with the given need to position units carefully and plan out strategies ahead of time. This game gives lots of customization options, lots of different classes you can recruit for your squad, and quite a bit of depth with each class and equipment having their own ups and downs.

Playing solo gives quite a bit of content, but if you have a buddy to play co-op with, this game's content increases exponentially as you two can coordinate your own squads to take into battles. Mixing and matching our teams has been an absolute blast. The music in this game is pretty damn catchy too, with no themes really feeling like they overstay their welcome or becoming too repetitive. If you loved the artwork from Behemoth's other titles, and you loved the narrator from Battleblock Theatre, you absolutely need to get this game while you still can at a lower price in it's early access status.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPrevious readers of my reviews have listed my reviews as Non-helpful, so I will try to clarify information about this game to help you make an informed decision on whether or not to buy this game.

I am one of the few original fans of Newgrounds.com and their amazing selections of humorous animations, underground soundtracks and colorful art galleries; I grew up on that site. If any of that sounds familiar to yourself, then this is one of the best purchases you can come across, and possibly the best waste of time you'll ever invest in. (In a good way.)

This game focuses on immature humor, fun challenging gameplay and collecting stuff in game (There is over 1400 items to collect). If none of that tickles your fickle, then this absolutely not for you.

The gameplay is a little bit similar to Xcom, turn based stradegy combat. The story is more focused on the journey you make for yourself across the game's world map, much like castle crashers: Good hunting down Evil without much character development in a more comedic fashion. The in game collectables are pretty to look at and are easy to unlock, half of them are weapons and the other half are cosmetics for your units. The sidequests are cleverly written and very witty, the massive amount of content in this game glistens with charm from Developers who clearly care about what they make.


In conclusion, love you guys at Behemoth.\t\t\t\t\t
