\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People is a game that's basically a mix of a little bit of chess, a little bit of Pokemon, and some questionable humor. Overall, it's pretty good, but it doesn't really excel compared to its competition.

PROS:
- Very solid turn-based combat system.
- Good jokes here and there.
- 18 different customizable fighters, some more so than others.
- Better art than Battleblock Theater and Castle Crashers, though it's still stuck in the cesspool that is the Newgrounds flash animation style.
- Lots and lots of collectable items to equip your team with.
- 6 team slots allows for a lot of customization and experimenting.
- World is visually and conceptually interesting.

CONS:
- A bit too many jokes are only funny to people who haven't hit puberty yet, see: http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=842617351
- The soundtrack is pretty iffy. Some times it's good, other times it's like "what am I even listening to?"
- If there's more than one target that your character can hit, you can't choose who you're going to hit. This can be extremely irritating in scenarios where you can either hit someone who's weak to your attacks or someone who's strong to it.
- Aforementioned Newgrounds artstyle. It's alright, but comes off as a little... unprofessional?

In short, Pit People is worth your time if you're looking for a creative, albeit somewhat generic turn-based combat game. I give it a 7/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People is great fun! A few things definately need to be worked on and tweaked, but hey - for an Early Access game from a well-trusted developer, this is looking real good.

Essentially, this plays like a modernized quirky version of Final Fantasy Tactics. Everything you want from a tactical turn-based party battle management game is pretty much here. Lots of collectibles, both functional and cosmetic. Definately has a good chunk of different kinds of units (the deep customization really helps here in making it feel even more extensive). The online play is off to a great start - they are doing it right.

One thing I'll say right up front is that I'm thoroughly confused about people complaining about the short 'main story line' right now. First off, that's the tutorial folks - there is more coming. Secondly, there are over 100 missions WITH storyline dialogue that will certainly take you over a dozen hours to complete. Thats a conservative estimate, too.

You can read about all the good stuff in, well, like every other review. What are the negative points?

First off, the camera desperately needs work with local co-op. They already said they would fix this as a high priority, so fine. Secondly, while the map is huge, it sort of, well, lacks a strong flavor. I think it feels very flat, and simply uniformly dotted with either small buildings or landscape chunks. While the map artwork is good and that is set, I'd like to see a bit of TLC go into making the terrain feel better in general. I hear keyboard and mouse controls are abismal, so be warned (I didn't try them because of this warning). Lastly, I think the music is fantastic, although it is not very artistically cohesive. One minute it is high fantasy classical-esque, then it's western cowboy music, and yet another it is techno-ish. Don't get me wrong, it is all very good, but sometimes this difference made me wonder about how it was all chosen and put together.

Another thing I'd like to mention is the difficulty. In five hours, I had never lost. Mind you, I didn't play on 'insane' difficulty, but still. I finished the 'main story', which is just a tutorial or prequal to the actual story (this version ends where it appears the real story begins). I finished 11 quests and a few other things. After a while, I got lazy and stopped really strategizing and just kept throwing units in haphazardly - and I still won. With only a few hours in, I'm going to chalk this up to early game build-up. Plus, I think online play will likely be a different story. I did play online, but only very briefly.

Lastly, I felt that single player mode felt much better than local co-op. Local co-op was fun, but the screen was almost too busy, and I felt there was even less strategy. It was like 'just send the units wherever they are next to an enemy' kind of thing. It worked, which is the questionable part.

Pros:
+Fantastically addictive combat; everything is done really really right
+Although I didn't like Battle Block or Castle Crashers as much as everyone else (I felt they were shallow), this game has really hit the mark with the depth it brings, respectively
+Controller support is great
+Don't be fooled - there is a ton of content right now; yes the 'main story' (ie. tutorial) is less than 2 hours - I assure you this will change; further, there are over 100 side quests and some 1,300 collectibles right now (I could see 15-20 hours easy - as an estimate)
+There are various parts of this game to enjoy, and it smartly lets you choose - you can turn on AI to actually do all the battling for you, which is great if you just like the larger aspects of the game

Cons:
-Difficulty is questionable - I can't seem to ever lose
-Local co-op is just too busy to be enjoyable; it hardly felt strategic, either
-While the music is great, there is no artistic cohesion; this may be fine, albeit weird
-Overall, I'd like it to feel like there is more strategy involved than what I've felt so far

I think this title is great, and definately worth full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're here reading my review you must be wondering if this game is worth buying or not. Well, I'll go ahead and let you sort out your own opinion and you can decide for yourself. PLEASE NOTE: This game is in Early Access, so don't let this review effect your views on the actual game when it comes out. Thanks!

Pros:

Great Team-Builder type game. If you love the type of game where it's critical to build your own team, this one is for you The units I've discovered thus far are as follows: Tank, short ranged but fast archers, long ranged but slow morters, healers, heavy hitter, DPS, and self-sufficient. All characters spare Healer and self-sufficent are interchangable. You may equip them with items varying from a rainbow.. bow to a chalk board with Einstine's "E=MC2". Though beware, as weight limits may prohibit you from reaching your characters full potential!

In the world, you may fight to get loot. Beware though! If you lose a fight or flee you may lose your items! You must make the difficult decision of staying outside and battling for more loot as you complete quests, or you can venture back home and attempt to drop off your loot. Very good system!

Wonderful balance. The game does a great job of making you feel challenged but not overwhelmed. Never before in this game have I desired to quit the game because a level was brutally unfair to me. It gives me a great challenge, even if playing with a friend, but if it gets too easy for you INSANE MODE is already implimented in the game and ready to use. Turning it on is as easy as completing the tutorial level.

The game becomes self-aware.. mostly. The game only makes you complete its lengthy tutorial once, and after that you can jump right into your 5 out of 6 starting group and play away the day. It also holds nice polishes as to changes in the HUB city. Incuding little changes you might see after completing a quest..

The characters and personalitys are diverse. Going into this I was scared that all of the characters might be static and comedy-esq where I know they only joke. While yes this game has an AMAZING sense of humor, it is very careful to pick and choose when to be full on comedy and when to dawn the garb of light hearted seriousness.

Many refrences. If you're a proud owner of BattleBlock or Castle Crashers like I am, you are rewarded with headwear for your characters that have.. actually pretty reasonable stats. Long live Hatty Hattington, The Tearful Warrior!

Cons:

The interface for equiping weapons and armor is... foreign to say the least. Things don't add up. There are three kinds of swords: Short, Medium, and Large (Forgive me if the names are wrong, but that's the jist). Good mechanic overall, but the swords vary in damage only slightly while other stats such as DODGE and DEF. may suffer or benefit greatly. I found myself unable to equip items that may have looked amazing, or that I got from the games Early Access finale. Worst of all is that the swords add to your weight, so I find myself using almost only the starting weapons since they have the most balance.

Leveling is broken. A big part of the appeal to this game was the Leveling system.. only to find out it does nothing. Amidst the wilds of the map you might discover a wild band of raiders you wish to attack. You kill one and a beam of purple erupts from your character while the letters, "LVL UP!" appear briefly. Is it noticable? No, the ATK, DEF, Ect buff is so small you'd have to level up quite a few times. I'm talking 20+ times, and leveling up does get progressively harder.

Armor eye appeal. There is said to be 1300+ items in the game to equip, and the majority look pretty cool. Be it a cup of joe to a Doge refrence. However... don't expect your people to look cool if you favor stats. As unbalanced as the items are currently, you are ALWAYS stuck with the default "T-shirt" so to speak. Cool helmet? Great. B.A. Sword? Even Better. I just hope those things look cool on your plain white shirt or furry black hair troll.


Overall:

This game is a wonderful game in my eyes, and I hate turn based combat spare Pokemon. It will take you on a few laughs and possibly make you wonder if Stamper's voice hurts after hearing his strange diologue where he occasionally yells. I feel this game is a wonderful addition to the Behemoth family and is on the forefront of breaking the bad trend of video game companies pumping out reskin sequels.

For only the price of 15$ I would reccomend this game if it had a few more fixes onto it. It has a nice touch to it and great replay value to those who enjoy playing with team building options.


What could be fixed/changed:

Wagon upgrades, something to spend the money you'll accumulate as you progress. Something that's not needed nessesaraly, but something that would make the journey more enjoyable if you could customize your ride with cool looking cannons or an alternate horse type.

Biome types. The game is very scattered and geographically makes utterly no sense. While yes, it is the Bear-apocalypse that doesn't mean the world should be as mixed as it is. I feel as if the map should have started with plains at least, expanding into a mountain zone to the west and a lake/island region to the east. Something to help pinpoint where the player is at. Something where the player could say: "To get to the Desert, go North onto the flaming fields, once you've reached the volcano zone stop and head east towards the island region. Follow along this route North-east until the islands begin to clump together and you'll see the desert. Enjoy!"

Biome types. The characters are diverse in this game, but you take no advantage of it. Keep the little bandits in the fields, and the massive cyclopse men in the farther out areas so the player FEELs threatened. Am I saying exclude them from the lower levels? No, but I would suggest making them more frequent and powerful the farther they are from the current location of your Town is.

Town/HUB Movement. It's a good idea, but if you went off of the Biome type idea I mentioned previously you might see an oppertinuty. This town movement instills adventure out of your comfort zone, but having the biome types could help the player navigate the landscape and perhaps even feel a bit more comfortable dodging the raining bear blood on their next quest.

The Pit. Give the player the choice to "Descend" the pit. It would eliminate part of the feel of grinding enemies outside, but here's the twist. The pit only gives EXP and little gold. The real incentive is if you can make it to X floor then you get a reward. However, to make it a challenge for your team you have to get down to X floor in one go. Perhaps a checkpoint at every 5th, 20th, or 50th floor depending on difficulty of individual floors? This pit idea would make the overworld seem less comforting as you'd be spending less time there, and the game would seem bigger as there's another objective for the player to do.

ADD TORSO EQUIPMENT. I've already said this in my review, but PLEASE add this. My team looks really sad-sack. I know the game is in the apocalypse, but the fact they never change shirts shouldn't hold in that situation either..

Leveling. Perhaps if you got your character to say... LVL 100. There should be some kind of choice you can make. Here's what I think that choice should be. Getting to LVL 100 unlocks a quest where you BLAH BLAH BLAH you're left with the option to keep them at 100, split the 100 with the other team members on your team, or spend the levels on unlockables NOT NESSESARY TO THE GAME. Ex) Rare music disc's, unused Stamper audio, paints to re-color your items to make them match. Things a perfectionist, most people who reach 100 would be this, would crave.



That's about it for now, as I'd hate to waste anyones time who's still reading this. I hope this review helped! Have a nice day!\t\t\t\t\t

-The game is very fun, even in its early development days.
-This game is comedic and fun, as all the Behemoth games are.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy playtime in this so far has felt like I experienced a twisted hybrid of Fire Emblem meets Adventure Time, and I'm having a blast with the hours I've put into it at the time of writing this.


Pit People sports a pretty wacky lineup of playable characters, music and art style which I feel have really tied in well in its current released state, of course if you aren't a fan of that certain style or Turn-Based Combat than this one might completely go over your head with little notice.


As of writing this the game comes with a reasonable roster of characters, bunch of unlocks - purely cosmetic and some that add different elements, solo/co-cop/pvp, a fairly large world to explore which I think cycles through a few variations, heaps of side quests and a main story line which isn't complete yet as of right now, I think i spent about half an hour/an hour to complete whats available of it, the story line that is.


All-in-all if you're a fan of Turn-Based Combat I think you should definitely give this one a try, even if you aren't and have the time, fire up a video on the interwebs and have a see if this might tickle your fancy. I feel like it's a pretty solid title, especially for an Early Access, probably the best $15 I spent in quite some time. Oh and the narration is awesome too, if only I had it for my daily life routine.\t\t\t\t\t

Wonderful Game

It has a lot of character and it looks as though a great deal of thought was put into every aspect of it. Even the beastiary leaves you with a smirk after seing the illustrations.

I will say though that the game feels built more for controllers. M+kb navigation of menues can be irritating at times.

Given its current state I still can boast that it is more than worth the 15 USD price tag and honestly in hindsight I would have paid twice that. It already has over 1000 items and 100 quests leaving so much to be discovered beneath the surface. The one main drawback is the fact is only has a few story missions currently .

Items

Currently items of the same type act uniformly aside from elemental damage and resistance. There are a couple items that have interesting effects like slow and knockback but generally you are choosing between lightwight with high damage/movement/dodge or heavy that are more survivable. The non human characters have less customization but I have yet to see an item that I felt was not thoughtfully made and memorable.

Quests

The quests are absolutely bonkers. There are some delivery missions and find this missions but even something you would intitially dismiss in other games turns out to be comedic. For instance in one side quest you find a lizard and are tasked with taking it to its owner. The owner immediatly says he is going to eat it when you find him. You find out the lizard is sentient and blows up his owner telepathically.

Characters

All of the characters names are procedurally generated with a huge pool to choose from. There are a decent amount of types of enemies and even the worst of them is still viable with the right team. There is some balanceing that needs to be done still. One of the ways they balance currently is by making larger units take up more space on your roster. For example you have 6 slots after unlocking the final ones with in game currency. These can be filled in the following ways.

Huge Unit = 3 slots
Large Unit = 2 slots
Regular Unit = 1 slots
Pair of Small Units = 1 slot
Some units can spawn up to 6 spawn units during each fight.

In this way a 2v2 arena fight could theoretically have 8 entities on the field or 84.
8 massive troll moms without thier spawn/ 12 gorgons and up to 72 spawn

Difficulty

Overall the game is not incredibly difficult after learning the basics but with the inclusion of Insane mode and elite enemies you will be pulling your hair out.

Conclusion

Remembering that this is an early access game and everything is subject to change, I give it a perfect 5/7 stars.

Look forward to seeing you in the PIT.
