\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo a friend of mine wanted to play a game together with me, and having exhausted many options already he caught sight of Pit People while Strippin was playing it. Excited he picked it up and chucked me a free copy, and then we tackled the game at the same time.

To put it simply as the game stands now I definitely recommend it. When it's a finished product I can only expect it to be significantly more fulfilling than the current rendition, which is already a good time to be had. Now for a bit more detail...

First and foremost this game is in Early Access and as of this review it has very little story missions to progress through. There are plentiful sidequests on the maps, and a few side missions attached to the Quest Board in town, but Story Missions? Almost none. So if you're looking for the full game...wait a bit?

Technically speaking this game is solid. I've had zero crashes and almost no glitches to speak of. I'm currently running Update 1(Beta) and that hasn't even generated any instability. All in all a good bunch of programming.
That isn't to say that the game is without bugs. I've seen a few others complain about some that have popped up here and there. However...

Behemoth seems to actually pay attention to the players. I've seen an actual attempt by the staff to try and help those who are having problems with the game. That's quite refreshing compared to what feels like cold, mechanical assistance from most bigger named and bigger budgeted companies.

So the game is pretty good overall in terms of stability. But how is it as a GAME?
It's a turn-based strategy game with a plethora of various units and equipments for said units. Each type of unit you can use has a weakness you can exploit, but you won't always face off against what you might want to which means you need flexibility in your loadout and playstyle.

In terms of actual difficulty you'll find it to be sitting close to that sweet spot. You can beat the opponents but they can get quite hard to handle if you're not paying attention. My favorite thing, however, is that even if you're against something that is designed to beat you you won't automatically lose. This isn't Rock/Paper/Scissors, and so weaknesses don't cripple you so much as merely hurt you.

If you like turn-based stategy games you'll probably get a kick out of this. If you hate them because they're too slow Pit People has a slightly faster pace than you might expect, so maybe check a video of a fight from the game. If you simply hate strategy games even then this game might interest you due to the humor and art style.

Also, yes, the music is awesome.\t\t\t\t\t
