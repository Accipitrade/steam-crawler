\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI grabbed this sight unseen because I hugely enjoyed Behemoth's previous games, Castle Crashers and BattleBlock Theater.

Unfortunately, while Pit People shares the excellent music, anarchic humor and undeniable charm of its predecessors, when it comes down to the core mechanics of the game Pit People is a brutal, unrelenting slog.

Both your units and the enemies have tons of hit points and attacks do little damage, so many turns are nothing but the endlessly repeated animations of your units hacking tiny chunks off of massive health bars. There is a leveling and equipment system, but units gain only health and damage as they level up and the majority of items seem to be merely cosmetic. A level 20 unit fights exactly the same as a level 1 unit (and seems to do about as much damage).

The fact that you only control unit movement, and the AI randomly chooses between available targets isn't initially a weakness, except that it tends to drag fights out even longer as it makes concentrating fire on weakened enemies needlessly difficult. It is a huge problem, however, for area of attack units, simply because the inability to target squares adjacent to an enemy makes it nearly impossible to bring these units along to a fight without inevitably including friendly units in the majority of their attacks.

I reserve special hatred for the section of the game where you equipment and manage your team of units. It is hard to express just how clunky, infuriating and uninformative this part of the UI is.

Most galling of all is that Pit People was launched from early access a mere 3 days after Into The Breach was released. By contrast, Into The Breach is a master class of elegant design and deceptively clever tactics crammed into agonisingly tense, fast battles. ITB doesn't have nearly as much story as Pit People but it is substantially more satisfying to play.

Look, if you want a brilliant tactics game, the $15 asking price for Into The Breach is the best deal in town. If you want a great Behemoth game, Castle Crashers and BattleBlock Theater are also only $15 each and are utterly fantastic.

Pit People, not so much.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother great co-op game from The Behemoth! It has all the absurdities, anti-jokes, poop jokes, and absolute chaos you would expect, along with a ton of quests, fights, and costumes. The combat is simple enough for anyone to pick up, but engaging enough for fans of the genre.

I feel like instead of having a repertoire of skills and status effects, all your characters are chess pieces. And every fight is just a different board with the pieces all jumbled up at the start. There's some level of strategy there... not the same level as something like Divinity Original Sin 1 or 2, but it's there. If you're on the fence, I'd recommend checking out Behemoth's other games: Castle Crashers and Battleblock Theater. They're cheaper on sale and great co-op games. If you have a partner and you're still looking, get Pit People.\t\t\t\t\t
