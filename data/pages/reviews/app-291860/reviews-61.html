\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People is quite the game! I grew up Castle Crashers and BattleBlock Theater, and I love all the references to them in Pit People. (You also get exclusive rewards for owning these games on your steam account). Pit People is a turn-based strategy action game filled with humor, 1500+ items to collect, and many creatures to recruit.

You can play through the story and many side missions. Almost each mission has an exclusive item or recruit you can get as a reward. You can fight Elite teams, which are stronger than the normal enemies. Upon recruiting an Elite enemy, they will retain their red tint, but not their buffed stats.

Very rarely, you can find an enemy with a moving or colored name. You can recruit these enemies, and show off your cool rare new recruit! Enemies also have a chance to have a tint, which I believe is more rare than a moving name.

Some downsides to the game:

The turn-based gameplay can get boring, fast. I personally don't get bored of it, but many people don't like it.

Immature humor - Honestly, I love the humor, but apparently some people don't like it.

1500+ items to collect - I love the diversity, but I am also a completionist. Grabbing all these items will take time. Especially due to certain exclusive items that can only be found in certain missions under certain circumstance.

Short story - The story can be completed within a few hours. I suppose the large amount of side missions make up for it.\t\t\t\t\t
