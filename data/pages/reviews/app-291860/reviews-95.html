\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo glad I waited for spring break to launch this title on a friday night, I played it until I got sick, which was sunday at noon.
What's it like, is basically just Castle Crashers as a turn based rts. In that regard, I think you could say there's nothing new here for strategy games or even just The Behemoth, the exact same elements of humour and storytelling, weapon and skill balance, etc. But it's not that I can complain about that.
Something I can complain about, however, is the lack of versatility for the magical races, it's kinda like they made humans that can be virtually anything (except healers), cyclops that can either function as either walking walls or explosive tanks (they even have their own special weapon category) and then ran out of ideas; every other race only serves one purpose and at best can equip some armor. Sure, it makes sense for wraiths to only carry a scythe or for robots to not being able to carry weapons. But it doesn't make sense to me that only cupcakes can heal or that kobolds cannot carry throwable weapons. Maybe it would be too hard to balance? Maybe if humans weren't also versatile to the point that every race but cupcakes are almost redundant it wouldn't be.
My other complain is that when so many characters use the same VA and most of them use the exact same 6 or so dialogues, even Stamper's humorous gibberish can get tedious after some hours. In that regard I think the senseless yapping should be done in shorter but more varied "sentences" and have them randomly mix up when a character speaks.
Also, I wish more quests were on the secondary quests board.

But yeah, it's very fun.\t\t\t\t\t

- I like how all of the equipment is silly and over the top. I mean, going into battle with a toilet scrubber and a bear shield is something I thought I'd never do.

- The side quests give you a good reason to explore the area. I hated the idea of going through the main quest so fast, so I was thankful to learn that there was over 100 side-quests in here, and plenty more characters to find.

- The battles are very simple and easy to understand, though there are certain issues with stats, but I'll talk about that in the CON section.

- Though I'm not a fan of multiplayer, it's nice to know that their's an options for you to fight random people online while you're in the pit. So if you wanna try some random battles between a Kobold army vs Cup cakes, then by all means, join and go crazy.

Cons:

- I am not a fan of games where the enemy matches you in levels. I know this was done so that the game wouldn't be a cake walk for the average player, but for a "filthy' casual like me, it does grate my nerves to see minimal progress be made. Watching my beefed up level 40 tank Horitao be taken out by a bunch of kobolds is not amusing.

- While its fun to see multiple types of weapons and hats I can put on my character. It is annoying that a lot of them are pretty much the same stats wise. The only ones with any stats on them are either useless when it comes to damage OR they make my character too heavy to be used properly, so I'm stuck with a specific set of armor/weapons until something better comes along the way. ( Though I do love my shiney new swordfish.)

- Is there a way to see an HP points rather than the bar itself ? I always make the mistake of spotting a weak opponent with almost no HP and leaving my weaker ally to finish him off, only to find them surviving another turn or two, while damaging or even killing my ally. Actual number would really fix this.

- I REALLY hate the idea of allies hitting each other. I guess this was to help promote better planning, but because of that, I find myself relunctant to use any monsters other than kobolds or zombies, since they only attack the enemy without getting an ally caught in the crossfire. I still use bows of course, but it irks me to see Yosef accidentally shoot Pip in the back of the head.


It's hard to think of anything else at this point. But these are my main points that I find fun/unfun about the game. Is it a perfect game? No, but I still find myself going back to play it, so it's certainly doing something right.\t\t\t\t\t
