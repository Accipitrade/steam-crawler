\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHas the utmost potential, depending on how things are executed upon full release.

Pros:
-Hilariously chaotic
-Pretty decent co-op
-Awesome, if not slightly frustrating, combat
-It's got William Stamper, what more do you want?

Cons:
-Units act of their own volition after you move them, causing problems with focusing single enemies
-Driving in the overworld is borderline painful at the moment
-Abusable mechanics

The game is very fun, that much is certain. Sure, the story is far from complete, but the side-quest lines take a while and are balanced to your level (at least from my personal experience). Essentially, the game is Fire Emblem meets Pokemon meets The Behemoth. As prior stated, moving units is the extent of your turn, which makes strategizing both a breeze and maddening at once. Because the battles take place on hexes, if a single enemy unit has enemies on two opposite sides, you only have about a 33% chance of hitting them, as the other two enemies to either side will also be in range. This is both good and bad, as you can also exploit this strategy. The Pit is a gladiatorial arena, which has an "Unfair" game mode. It's three back to back fights with unit death carrying over between rounds. This at first seems like a fun challenge for late game, but as it turns out, it's a totally exploitable experience farm. The day the early access started, I was at the top of the leaderboard, not because I deserved it, but because I was grinding experience and in the process got unique loot that can only be acquired by placing well in The Pit. The game needs some polishing in some areas, but I was happy to take the dive, and The Behemoth delivers once again.

On the funny scale:
Battleblock Theatre > Pit People > Castle Crashers\t\t\t\t\t
