\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is purely a review based on release.

The game itself is extremely well put together. It's a Behemoth game, after all. The humor is on point, the artstyle is extremely pleasing, the pure amount of character and party customization is almost overwhelming, and the combat system (which I at first thought was as convoluted as the Undertale path minigame) turned out to be pretty well understood.

Sadly, this is also an Early Access game. And while I should not be expecting THAT much from an Early Access game directly on release, I find myself doing so anyways.

You will almost always be outnumbered, outdamaged, and outplayed in every scenario "3 skulls and above". Now in another RPG this would be fine. But due to just how much damage and random chance (not just crits but location of hits, location of AoEs, who gets healed, etc.) there is, it can be extraordinarily frustrating to set up a team around a point. As there is no permadeath in the game, this is simply a setback and puts you back at the city to do it again. But the fact that most encounters are very focused on luck can be frustrating when you start taking longer routes.

The game is also extremely lacking right now in main content if you don't want to either A. Explore and complete all the mini-quests, B. Complete your customization collection, or C. Fight your friends in the PvP arena. Now I know that the non-story portion is almost the complete focus of the game, but the thing I like most about Behemoth games is that I can run through the story (acquiring collectibles along the way) and basically play through everything. I don't have any incentive to go out and collect customizations (because most customizations are exactly the same stat-wise, with a select few being either elemental or slightly more powerful). I don't have any incentive to go out and capture more recruits (because this is just used to get more customizations and all characters of a class have the same stats). And while I played the single story mission (which you can clear in about 10 minutes) and enjoyed it, I do wish the focus of the game was a bit more linear as the open-world nature of the game let the monotony set in once I realized the above.

This should be a game I enjoy, as "turn-based hex-RPGs" are a favorite of mine (Civ, Fire Emblem, etc.). But the freeform nature of it makes it it hard to recommend right now as anything more than a collectathon. If a friend has is, by all means take a swing. It doesn't take long to get caught up to another person gear-wise, and it's enjoyable for the time I spent on it. But if you're looking for an extreme amount of substance ala Battleblock or Castle Crashers, you won't find that here (yet).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEarly Access Watcher Public Service Announcement

Game: Pit People

Update Version: January 13th EA Release

Disclosure: This game copy was bought by the reviewer.

Gameplay Footage: WARNING! This includes spoilers for the available story missions.

https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=O31WGApcyVY

I understand this is Early Access and the final product is not representative of the current state, but this PSA covers the state of the game as it is now. There are some things that stand out in a good way, but the content provided in terms of story and complexity is not worth the cost of the game at this time. Thankfully, this game will get made regardless of what happens, so this warning is just for those on the fence or looking for a single player experience over the multiplayer experience.

Visuals and music are going to be amazing for some, off putting to others. If you played Castle Crashers or Battleblock Theater (I didn't, though I do own them), then the style will look familiar in terms of the shading and design. There is a massive amount of character customization in terms of weapons, shields and helmets, but armour isn't a factor yet. Music ranges from blasting techno to mild synth that I surprisingly found enjoyable. Voice acting is left to a few announcers and the narrator with the rest being incoherent gibberish that requires the use of subtitles to understand. For the setting and the bizarre nature of the world, it fits perfectly and adds to the zany dialog.

The story is incredibly short right now, consisting of a couple of introductory quests that took me about an hour and a half to finish. Once it is over, the Pit (1-2 vs AI, 2 vs 2 players matches) and a collection of side-quests are all that lengthen the gameplay. This is unfortunate, mostly due to the narrator's amazing performance that was indeed the highlight of the game. He eats up the scenery with his voice over accompanied by some beautiful cutscenes and dialog that did get a chuckle in the back of my mind. It left me wanting more, which is why I was surprised by how little there was to the story considering the price tag.

Gameplay is mechanically sound, but lacks depth in terms of character management and improvement. All stats are contributed by the characters level with weapons and armor filling in the rest. Swords are good for smaller foes, longer swords hit harder, maces are required to deal decent damage against heavy helmet wearers and the ranged weapons can deal splash damage or direct damage. Shields also range from small to huge, allowing for more projectiles to be blocked as the size increases. Weapons can also have special perks that can root characters to a spot, freeze opponents or stun targets. Otherworldly creatures also exist, providing attacks that can chain lighting across multiple targets, create minions after being hit or float to provide avoidance of land based traps.

These creatures are mainly acquired through the process of recruitment (kill all but the creature, capture the creature in a cage) and add some life to a combat system boiled down to moving to a space and letting the character attack. You only choose the movement, leaving the attack to be determined by the position and hoping your character hits the guy you want if they are standing next to two enemies. It has gotten to the point that I let the computer handle combat for me while I just maneuver across the world map in search of new locations and enemies during my last hour of play. The best way to describe it would be if you simply moved units and used auto-attacks.

From what I was reading up, this game has been in development for awhile and has a standing deal over on Xbox One Game Preview as well. While I commend the developer for providing a functional core, the lack of a longer story and minimalistic combat style make battles tedious after awhile. The party system (can have up to six slots with some creatures taking up two or more) at least provides countless creature/character combinations that helps keep the multiplayer fresh for those who will focus on the 2 vs 2 battles. For anyone looking for a decent single player experience, however, might want to wait for the story to expand further. For now, not recommended is the best I can give it when comparing the price to the available main story content.
