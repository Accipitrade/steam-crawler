\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't normally care for tactics games. I found Fire Emblem to be rather dull, whereas XCOM was far too difficult. Despite that, I can't seem to get enough of Pit People. It not only achieves a stellar balance between being easy to learn and challenging to master, its charm and depth constantly keep me coming back for more.

This game's use of random elements may put off some pplayers, but I find that it adds to the experience immensely. There's enough tangible RNG to keep things from being entirely within the player's control, but it does so in a way that makes fights feel more tense and not at all unfair. Furthermore, it adds more layers of strategy as the player is forced to plan around these random elements.

The game's style is unmistakeably Behemoth's M.O., but it seems to have gone even farther this time. The world is full of genuinely weird and entertaining characters and creatures, many of which are found within some of the most imaginatively ridiculous quests I've ever seen. The creativity and sense of humor extend to the game's 1500+ collectible items and equipment. As a result, I constantly find myself compelled to keep coming back and grinding for whatever bizarre new items I might get.

I cannot recommend this game enough.\t\t\t\t\t

The story and dialogue are very Behemoth style; goofy and original, however the key problem to this game lies in the lack of depth in combat. All combat feels the same and repetitive. There are slight class advantages against one another, but not enough greatly affect each battle. It never feels like you are getting any better. There are different weapons and armor per class, but within each class seem cosmetic. All combat just boils down to the melee fighters clustered in a circle. It never feels I could be doing something different that greatly changes the battle. Perhaps given Behemoth’s style of gaming they were not going for a game with a lot of depth but just a simple battler, in any case not for me.\t\t\t\t\t
