\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[EARLY ACCESS REVIEW, 12 HOURS IN, SUBJECT TO CHANGE]

After doing some research, I finally decided to buy Pit People while it was in Early Access. To be clear the game is cheaper now than it will be at full launch price according to the Early Access description. I must say, I seriously think it's worth considering. This is easily the most fun I've had with an Early Access title ever and it has enough staying power to the point where I think one could get their money's worth in its current state if they are interested in its concepts.

Pit People is an easy to play/learn strategy RPG, with complicated customisable units and simple execution. You control up to six units (although some units allow you to break this rule) in a match by simply moving them in the general vicinity of their target. Potential targets/enemies on the map are highlighted and your selected unit automatically chooses how, depending on the distance between itself and its target, and who to attack. You can play the entire game smoothly on a controller and the AI is pretty decent, ensuring both allies and enemies offer some unique and interesting tactics. Your units can only heal outside of battle via campfires or the central hub, by levelling up during battle, or by a healing unit which sacrifices some of its own life, like a selfless and sweet prince. Battles are paced in such a way where you can finish a match easily in under half an hour (in some cases much less than that even) or marathon a chain of battles for several hours. The rewards for victory get extremely funky and allow you to try out new strategies or run different parties the next time you battle. They can range from equipment, to swords, to bows, to funny hats and hairstyles, to records, to memes, and... JUST GOOD STUFF WORTH HAVING AROUND OKAY?!

You are capable of capturing additional units, which can live peacefully outside of battle in the barracks. The satisfaction of meeting the capturing requirement, leave the wanted unit the last thing standing and utilize a capture unit with a cage, is a fun twist on the regular battle and can leave some interesting results during and after each encounter. Wanting to capture a stronger unit means you have to keep them alive on the battlefield, a threat to your team, same goes for a unit that may walk straight into your sword to protect a friend. This DOES include story driven bosses, which is quite satisfying alongside their COOL NAMES and COOL EQUIPMENT. Oh, and I guess the fact that you have the strength of someone relevant to the story, or something...

Speaking of story, what's there is pretty good! Admittedly the main story seems to be restrained prior to the full release, but it's still worth playing it through! There are many sidequests, scattered amongst the easy to traverse and diverse overworld as well as inside the castle hub thingamajig with a variety of objectives and peculiar battle scenarios. As someone who has played most, if not all of Behemoth's games, this is by far the funniest and my favorite in terms of story. You also get loyalty loot you might actually like for owning their past games! Stamper is in FULL FORCE as a narrator* and the dialogue between characters and the names of wondrous locales, is charming and delightful! Also pretty messed up sometimes! It doesn't overstay its welcome and is amiable in itself!

The game features some appealing multiplayer modes as well! You can compete with others on a leaderboard for daily special loot dependant on your ranking in THE PIT (filled with people), it's something which I've been extremely enjoying quite A BIT (not with people). Unfortunately I have been unable to test out the PVP and local/online co-op modes with others at the time of this review. That said THE PIT RULES (vs. AI) and the game is still fun, even running solo. Pit People is compatible with two players for the every mode, local and online, same goes for the four player battle mode. I do dream of the day I can try these modes. Hopefully I will...

Pit People as far as I can tell, is a game definitely worth playing, even in Early Access. With a lot of interesting and varied characters, a bundle of laughs, low pc spec requirements, multiple multiplayer modes, a new easy to learn/difficult to master spin on the SRPG genre, flexible play sessions, player driven initiative, policeman cupcakes called copcakes and MORE, I can, at this time, wholeheartedly recommend this game. It's by far my favorite Behemoth game. I do hope you consider playing it at some point. It's pretty good. There's a lot of stuff to do in it. It's a good time. Either way, Love & Respect!

TL;DR - Good Early Access, easy to play SRPG game with new ideas and intriguing charm. A great entry point for the genre as well. If you think you're interested now, yeah, buying it is a good idea. It's supposedly cheaper for the time being too. Also CUTE CUPCAKES WITH POLICE HATS CALLED COPCAKES, 10/10!!! TEN OUT OF FLIPPING TEN!!!!!\t\t\t\t\t
