\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe issues I had with this game might just be a personal thing, but I found this game to be disappointing. Too be fair, I went in with high expectations because I love Behemoth and their games, I also love tactile type games. However, this game just was not fun. The world map is very unfun to navigate. I actually think that was the worst part about the game. It's not a big map by any means, but the territories are littered with garbage and there are a lot of tiles that you have to find a way around. There are also encounters, which is't bad in itself, especially because they give you a way to avoid them, but since the combat is so slow, it makes these small encounters such a time sink. You can flee if you do not want to fight, I'm just stuck on the fact that I wanted nothing to do with them, not even participating in some here or there for fun. The combat is painfully slow. Positioning is the whole crux of the combat. You cannot "choose" which target your characters hit, you have to position them in such a way so that they will attack who you want on their own. There were several times where I needed an enemy killed, but my characters would attack the wrong one because they have "a mind of their own". This gets incredibly frustrating on the final stage. I also found the humor to get very old very fast. All in all, I found that I was too impatient to really enjoy the game. It is so painfully slow. Luckily, the story is short.

Some positive things: The music absolutely slaps. The story is good and funny. The story is short and sweet. The cutscenes are very fun to watch. I like how it pulled the greater universe of the Behemoth games together at the end. The music is so good, easily my favorite part about the game.\t\t\t\t\t
