\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am really enjoying the game!

So I watched gameplay videos etc to get a sense of wether Pit People would be for me. I took a leap (with my credit card that's no unheard of) and I do not regret my decision for one second! The game isn't perfect but it's great for me. I'll list pros and cons and depending on your taste in games you might have a different experience.

Pros
- Simple to learn system. You move your character to where you want and if they can do an action in that space they will.
- Character customization: You have your stock plot characters but you can also recruit any of the denizens of the world. You can't change their name or "class" if they're a monster, but you can collect literally 1000 different helmets, (which are somtimes hair styles) weapons, armor etc. Getting a character with a funny name is many a player's goal.
- No bugs for me. I haven't found any bugs yet, or graphical or sound problems.
- I love the music, it wavers between this weird techno to silly haphazard metal.
- The animation is smooth and I like the simple style.

Cons
-The game itself is not complete and that's ok for me, it tells you and you can keep playing after that part in the plot. Technically a con but it's out there in plain site for us to see befor we purchase.
-Atmosphere. I enjoy the abstract and comedic design of the game's world, some people might not. This isn't a dark and brooding tactical rpg clearly.
- Interface! I DID have trouble figuring out what buttons do what in some cases because they are simpily a graphic, not actually labeled. I don't like how if you hover to another character by accident or another equipment (like from helmets to weapons tab) you go to that field as if you clicked. BUT you can also go there by clicking so it creates some confusion but it's not game breaking or anything. I suggest one interface method or the other when it comes to menu screens. Everywhere else in the game is great.


Watch videos, ask the message boards, check out screenshots, whatever you wanna do. As always see how you feel befor you buy this game and research. I made a good purchase.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs anyone who is an old fan of The Behemoth, especially 'Castle Crashers', you know what you're getting with 'Pit People'. A deceptively simple turn-based strategy game with all the cartoonish charm and humour of Behemoth's other games.

Whilst it is by no means flawless in the current state, you can feel a sense of polish and care that gives hope for future development. The game centers around you taking control of a small team of heroes/monsters/mushrooms/cupcakes and taking on other teams of either AI or player controlled enemies in beautiful hex-tile maps. Despite the depth of the damage and weapon types, the healing, debuffing and buffing, counters and weaknesses; your only function as a player is to move your characters. That's it. Whilst that seems uninteresting at first, you quickly find how complex it really is.

You start your game with three characters and must capture individual enemies in order to recruit them to your hero locker-room to be mixed and matched in order to form an ideal team.

Pros:
- Recognisable and enjoyable art style
- Fantastic soundtrack and SFX
- Simple yet deep combat
- MASSIVE array of cosmetics
- Hilarious writing

Cons:
- Lacking a real vision in what the game wants you to achieve
- Not much character development (Levels seem arbitrary, all gear of the same type have the same stats)
- Will end up being repetitive if turn based strats aren't your genre

My hope for the future is in the developers implementing some kind of (possibly Castle Crashers-esque) character development system. Right now, once you capture a character to use on your team - there is no sense of growth or power. Stat increases from levelling are apparently negligible. As someone who enjoys the 'grind' and progress in my characters, it will eventually be the determining factor on whether I spend 30 hours in the game or 300.\t\t\t\t\t
