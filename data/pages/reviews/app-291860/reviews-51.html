\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People is fun. But clearly; there's a lot of wrong in it. To sum it up; the game likely is meant to be played once and then move on to something else.

There's a fun story with glorious naration and jokes, a genious art style and bunch of references.

Then there's the gameplay. And oh boy. The combat is fine. For the most part. Simply place your bois and they'll do everything like smart people by themselves.
Then you'll notice your fighter was stopped midswing because a loose arrow from your archer hit him and entirely cancelled his attack. Or that fiendly fire can be much more dangerous than your actual opponents. And since it's all automated, you can't tell them to shoot after your melee bois did their things.

The balance is alright, there's some really weird odd choices here and there which begs for the game to be modded to fix it outright.
You are limited in how many of certain """cosmetics""" you can get, so what gives ? Can't give my shiny hat to everyone, okay. But that's also applied to weapons. And some weapons are straight up upgrades over the stock stats, why ? I can't make everyone wear that because it does an extra effect ? Can't I just remove the bonuses just for cosmetics? The AI has entire teams made out of that. Yet not available for the player.

Extra of an item are converted for a really low sum of gold. Which gold is only used to buy other gear for cheap or fighters for EXTRA pricy. And they're 90% of the time lowly basic hoomans.

The different creatures are nice, but some are really rare or hard to capture. I don't think i ever caught a gnome more than once.
They usually have more focused strenghts but with rather bad weaknesses. And some have technical jank that ruins them in specific circumstances; which happen way more than it should.
And forget about using zombies. If they're dead at the end of the combat; they won't rise back up in the post screen. They're so weak. Even as distractions, kobolds are arguably better.

Complaints aside; here comes the main course; the gameplay is tediously repetitive. The combat is just a matter of positioning, it gets stale quickly with little differences every creature brings. You'll likely play the same way no matter what you use.
There are some awesome terrain and battlefield that you'll have the chance to play once then good luck doing the quest all over again just to see the exact same thing. You never see thoses places anywhere else. It's just a thing for that mission only and away it goes (not really since it can be replayed, not instantly, but it can be). The PVP arena is only its own arena with little variations. You don't get fancy fields or some good arena you've fought in during quests. Why ? I so want to play theses with my own rules/teams.

There's a coop which is kinda neat; and every missions gets cranked up with more to balance things out. Can make some really crazy encounters.
Capturing troops is easy and if you use the auto battle tool and mark an enemy as "capture" target, it will do it's best to not kill it. You just need to have a net or a spidtaur. Which both have their drawbacks.

Tactically the combat is pretty simple. Just blob your enemies before they blob you. don't get surrounded and it should be fine.then swap around to avoid damage on one unity if it ends being a solid line of battler.
Maybe way too simple. There's not much depths except when using ranged units because of their ranges and movement penalities and whatnot.

I really have to go over the damage types and gear again.
Since gear are limited in number; you can't just have a specially dedicated type or funny team like the game throws at you. Bows are weird. They differenciate between blunt and spiked heads. The spikes do extra damage against bats; why wouldn't you have them ? Or the acid that does extra to robots ? Why take basic ? Why is that a choice ?
I really like the weapons that explode on hitting stuff. It's funny. But it gives the question, why is everything else weaker in that category ? Why would I use anything else over that ? There's very little drawbacks for some of the op equipment that gives too big of a bonus. And some aren't even restricted to a single copy!

The colliseum is competitive. What I mean is that you fight to be higher in the leaderboard to have more loot, or just loot at all. It resets daily. Or every 12 hours.

Highly repetitive game, little to no depth or tactic. Once you've done the story and quests the only thing left is collecting. Collecting every tidbit of gear and making a team that you like, with some luck they'll even have a different name colour or effect, or a tint of color. That's all there is left.\t\t\t\t\t
