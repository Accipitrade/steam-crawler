\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People is a fun isometric turn-based combat-strategy game made by the lovely folks who created Battleblock Theatre and Castle Crashers. Pit People features many recruitable characters, including (but not limited to) Pixies, Spiderpeople, Unicorns, Cannibals, Zombies, Gnomes, Cupcakes, Mushrooms, Robots, and a pesky moustachioed man named Horatio.

In Pit People, William Andrew Stamper is the incredibly charismatic and not-at-all-psychotic Narrator, just as he was in Battleblock Theatre.

Now, onto the gameplay itself.

While it is early access, the combat is still quite sound, and I've yet to encounter any bugs. One of the only issues that I've encountered is the fact that the main storyline is incredibly short (which is understandable, given the fact that the game is still in Early Access). Despite this, there are still dozens of side-quests to occupy your time, as well as multi-player combat in The Pit.

Just like Battleblock Theatre and Castles Crashers, you can play the majority of the game with another player, in Co-Op Mode. Unlike BT and CC, you can only play multiplayer Co-Op with ONE other player (which is understandable, it would be rather difficult to balance a four-player Co-Op combat system, unless every player was restricted to 3 fighters instead of the usual 6.)


I highly recommend this game for people who enjoy turn-based combat and comedy similar to Battleblock Theatre and Castle Crashers. \t\t\t\t\t

The only thing you can control during combat is the movement of your characters. This is important because you can't control who your characters attack. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I have not been given anything free, and it's rare that I write reviews.
This is a wonderful start to a very fun game! As it is early access, it is unfinished in the plot/storyline, but so much fun that it is worth the current price for a few laughs and a fresh spin on positioning based combat systems. Here are my Pros and Cons. Some of the Cons list will be the kind of things that are almost always an issue in early access, but a Pro is a Pro and a Con is a Con.

Pros:
1. Excellent animation in a captivating style. It turns brutal and nearly meaningless violence into a humorous romp with silly characters that throw strawberries and beer bottles as shuriken and use fences and beach umbrellas for shields. Despite the 2D style it has a visual depth and variety that keeps it interesting.
2. Outrageous and hilarious narrator that is apparently quite evil and argues with the main character about how the story is supposed to go.
3. The world changes constantly, with the heroes home moving from place to place in the world. Not clear if it's procedurally generated each time, but don't focus too much on scouting with this game at present. You'll always be somewhere new soon. It forces a different style of planning and play in that way.
4. Currently playable in a way that the combat system can be learned and understood in it's current form. Fun fights.
5. Unkillable allies. Allies get wasted left and right in some fights. If you lose somebody(0 health) they will be waiting when you return home. No cheesy paying gold for resurrection mechanics. You are penalized by not having them in the field for that time, but you can spring for a resurrection potion if you know you might lose someone before completing a quest. They are pricey.
6. No leveled items as yet. Some items seem more common than others in each game area, but there is not a "sword of a thousand truths" to completely destroy the game balance. All weapons and items have a direct weight/damage/special ratio, so a big sword with high damage and a flame attack may be all a character can carry, while a guy with a light sword might carry the heaviest shield, or forgo armor for greatly improved movement speed.

Cons:
1. Unpolished combat mechanics. The controller system could use a few improvements as some of the controls seem unintuitive especially around interacting with the home and markets. Have not played keyboard controls, but the controller(xbox) works well enough to play, I just make a lot of mistakes due to buttons functioning differently from menu to menu. Dumb mistakes in this combat system can be costly since combat is accomplished solely by moving to take advantage of the strengths and weaknesses of each character/enemy. It is hard to tell if the system of strengths/weaknesses/elements is rudimentary or just complex and not fully applied. The Devs seem to be aiming for a wide variety of playstyles with a complex and intuitive turn based combat system. I think they can do that, and will eventually. This whole game centeres around strategic combat encounters.
2. Weapons and armor don't have much variety in function, despite many many different appearances. Some weapons have special properties, but most are standard in function with a different appearance. I prefer this to a game with progressive overpowering of items and player groups, but more variety of weapon/armor functions would be nice. Ex. A mace beats in a helm but does less damage than a sword of equal weight. Helms deflect swords for greatly reduced damage, and light armor or no armor may offer a slight dodge chance. That's about it for armor effects. Many more ways of making fights dynamic at the equipment level through unique abilities(rather than leveled items and such) is how I hope they improve this. Bows and other ranged weapons are not very useful yet, in that they create a more unrealiable team when it comes to combat, though they are not useless, just uninteresting.
3. Grindy. Unfinished story, yes, but if you don't do story quests then all that is left is to fight and collect fighters at the moment. The characters level up over time, but why is currently unclear. It seems to make them stronger slowly, but so what? This is probably a mechanic not yet well developed. As the game is, I have no reason to care if the characters level up, and kind of hope that aspect of the game is removed for some alternative.

Overall this is a whole lot of fun and laughs, and mentally stimulating to play. My 11 yr. old Nephew thinks it's funny, and I would consider it generally appropriate for modern kids. Needs a lot of work to add more than one dimension of play. I don't play "collect them all" games, so I hope Devs continue to focus on refining and expanding combat mechanics first and foremost. I hope this has been helpful.\t\t\t\t\t
