\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was rather a slow burn, it was rather intimidating (and for some reason slightly alienating for me (that's just me, don't worry about it)) but as time went on everything fell into place and I learned to really enjoy things. Finding and capturing new creatures and seeing what they can do became really enjoyable and now I have multiple teams of fighters all ready to go and fight people and solve quests. Some of my favourite things to do include mounting Cupcakes onto Spidaurs, Using a Gnome's chair to catapult a mushroom into a crowd of enemies right as the round starts and using a Momma hair troll to body-block a whole bunch of fighters while she spawns hair troll babies to surround them. And if any of that bizarre description intrigues you I would recommend checking this game out.

Tips: You don't need to move every unit each turn, if they are already in a good position, you can leave them there. If a ranged human moves in a turn, they get half the normal shots fired, you can get around this by mounting them on a Spidaur, as they can move freely without reducing the amount of shots. You cannot mount a unit on the same turn that they move, so you need to keep the Spidaur or gnome still if you want to make use of them that turn.

It also has multiplayer, so if you have a friend that plays, maybe you could team up or have a match in the arena.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinally decided to try playing this, and was left a bit... Disappointed? Confused? Underwhelmed?

As per usual, Behemoth's art, animation, and comedy is excellent. They can't go wrong there.
But, like many people before me have echoed, the gameplay is boring and repetitive. Too simple. In addition, the game has a slew of issues for people with visual problems.

There's so much information and movement happening on screen that it's difficult to track when something on screen is relevant or not- As far as I've seen there's no "reduced animations" option in the graphics settings that can make some of the ridiculous bouncyness of some of the areas less difficult to look at.
Though, nearly any information on screen doesn't really matter- the constant flashing of the lvlups and the "hero" aura is straining to the eye. It feels like the values of the game are too low and it becomes a muddied shade of grey as everything kind of blends together.

Menu's feel clunky to navigate and the equipment area gives so little information on what does what that I have no idea what's going on. It took me a good while to figure out how to remove a helmet from a character that i accidentally put a helmet on, for example. In addition, the provided information is meaningless. There's no tutorial, barely any information on hover, etc. You're left on your own in the constantly bouncy visuals.

Combat is. Meh. It took me a while to realise that I had no way of selecting what unit I wanted to be attacked. This made actually strategising my party to focus on one problematic enemy a complete pain in the ass, as enemies would keep stepping into my ranges and would distract my units. It's not fun to have combat be decided on randomness. In addition, selecting what a unit should do is confusing. Everything is based on visuals and not proper text on screen telling me what's happening and how much it'll happen. An example being the healing unit having to be very specifically positioned to heal a fellow unit- This indicated by a heart appearing over the unit that will be healed. This is very easy to miss as it does not stand out visually at all.

Moving units also feels like a clunky mess. I can't select a unit by clicking on it- I have to scroll with the mouse to select that specific unit and then click on the space it wants to go to- units only move once you ready your round. I'd prefer if there'd at least be a more visible "ghost" unit standing in the future position to be able to actually properly overview where units are going to move to- or color coding. or any better indicators, really.

All in all,
Not really fun?
Kind of disappointed.
