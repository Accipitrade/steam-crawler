\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not a fan of RPGs. I feel like they're too repetitive and grindy. With that being said, Pit People is one of the best RPGS I've played. Pit People takes place after a giant space bear crashed into the earth and caused the apocalypse. You play as a man named Horatio who's son was kidnapped by the narrator, who is also a giant bear in the sky. You must go and recruit people to get your son back from the narrator. The gameplay is a mix between Pokemon and Into the Breach. You must move choose where to move your character on the small area you are given and then choose which enemy to attack. There are different classes you can have for each of your characters. There are melee characters who have to be right in front of their opponent to attack, characters that will heal their allies, characters who can attack at ranged, and many more. You can also catch enemies in a net and recruit them to join your team. The game is still a turn based game though, and still has a lot of the flaws I feel like turn based games have. The combat can feel grindy as you travel from enemy to enemy farming for levels. It's also hard to add new characters to your team because most of the time they will be a much lower level than the rest of your team and you have to go back to grinding enemies but the new character will die most of the time because he's such a low level and receive little xp. The art and the character design is really good. The characters all look cartoonish and I like that. The Behemoth also brings their signature charm to these characters and I feel like that's what keeps me from closing the game during all the grindy sections. Overall, if you're a fan of turn based RPGs then I highly suggest you get Pit People.

Score: 7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People seems like a cute, quirky and funny game at first, but turns out to be nothing but a bloated browser mini-game in practice. It seems that the map is busy, that there's so much to do, while in fact all you can do is fight and maybe gather them useless coins and items, which are mostly cosmetics. Everything in the game comes to items and sometimes recruits (also, not many different types, mostly different names), and they're 90% cosmetic, which means the game's 90% fluff. To top it off, the equipment UI is lax and unpleasant to use.

In the beginning I thought that the combat system would open up, maybe they'd turn things around somehow, but nope - it is very limited in terms of how much control a player has, and in the end no gimmicks can save these battles from being a total drag. You just show these minuscule things where to go and then they do their minuscule damage to one another, that's it. There's nothing more to it and it limps on forever. Since your input has little to no tactical value anyway, why not just drop the pretense, cut the crap and make it a real-time autobattler to speed up the process?

Yes, to cut a ton of superfluous crap is what this game truly needs and fears, for there won't be anything left afterwards. When the devs actually try to do something semi-interesting with their shallow combat system, it's always at the expense of your time. Came to a quest battle and found out that your squad doesn't suit a new gimmick? Well, too bad, now you'll have to run back there all the dull, uneventful way from the castle with a new squad once again. These jokes aren't as funny the second time around.

You can fight in the Pit, you can do side quests, you can wear hats, there's multi-player... blah blah blah. It's all the same! There's nothing to it. The game is a giglet, a mock, a dummy and a waste of time. And I've got a feeling that it was built this way intentionally, maybe even ironically? What a bunch of nothing. I also absolutely hated the music.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far, so good.

The main point of the game, for me, it's the humor and silliness, which is what attracted me to Castle Crushers and Battleblock Theater years ago.

On that, they definitely over-delivered, I really needed this.

The gameplay is nothing too complex, a tad bit repetitive (this is helped by the "auto-battle" mode - allows for testing new teams while doing something else, can be enabled and disabled at almost any time). The tactic part is heavily based on team composition and positioning. Character customization is very basic, but team setups can be very different and make up for it on a RPG level. On a appareance level, variety is definitely there, however this means hundreds of items which do pretty much the same thing.

Music is just fantastic, it fits the tone and style of the game perfectly.

Graphic design is in their own style, which I always appreciate. That is very subjective of course, but objectively the care was put into it.

All around this is a good purchase even at full price, in my opinion. Very polished and entertaining game, not too difficult (mileage will vary, of course), and definitely out of the usual schemes like Behemoth does.\t\t\t\t\t
