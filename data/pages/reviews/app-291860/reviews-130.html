\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People, one of the games I was looking forward to for some time, is, at its core, a fun, inventive and hilarious game with some real depth to it's gameplay. The graphics are bright and appealing, the music, sound and voice acting (take a bow Stamper) are excellent, crisp and fitting and the atmosphere and humour is highly amusing, resulting in one of those rare instances where I find myself laughing out loud whilst I play

The negatives, few as they are, barely upset this high trend. The game can be a little confusing at times, lack of information or a tutorial outside of basic combat can be a tad frustrating and online battles tend to be extremely one sided as some players who are a higher level, and having farmed the best weapons and characters, will crush those that barely know anything about the game. The biggest offender of this was when I came up against someone three times my level with the best gear and enemies in his roster. Turned out, they had clocked in a couple hundred hours of the game where as I had clocked in a miserable five.

That point aside, this game is still highly enjoyable and fun and, a must for those of Stamper especially.
88/100

The above review was made during early access. Since then, the game has received it's final update and been released.. All things considered, and having played the game to its completion, I can now say that my original 88/100 was generous. Make no doubt about it, I still recomend the game, but there was a very real sense of dissapointment in the stories sudden and unfulfilling conclussion. I still love Pit People for its atmosphere, humour, art and music, but their final work had me sighing more in disapointment than the feeling of fulfillment I was expecting.

Final score is 73/100\t\t\t\t\t
