Fast-paced Turn-based Fun Place!

What's this? The Behemoth, the creators of my favorite beat-em-up and one of my favorite platformers, made a turn-based game? Sign me up!

I'm a fan of turn-based games with RPG elements: the Fallout games by Interplay, Wasteland, Fire Emblem, the works. Seeing this game annouced, I was a little skeptical. Specifically, about the co-op features. Many turn-based games with co-op have each player move individually. But I'll be beaten down with a rubber hose filled with frozen custard if The Behemoth didn't make it work.

I can easily compare the gameplay to Fire Emblem, but boiled down to what's important. No faffing about with the weapon triangle for Pit People! Bigger weapons do more damage but weigh more. Archers aren't effective at close range. Maces work better on armored foes. Ultimately, the secret to success is how you position your soldiers on the field. Different squads can mesh together in different ways, allowing for interesting stratigies that work for you.

The humor is nothing new if you've played Behemoth's previous, with the glorious glorious voice of Stamper, the narrator in Battleblock Theatre, making a return as the narrator. This time around he's made out to be super sarcastic and passive-aggressive towards the protagonists. It's truly a joy to behold the dialouge. But as they say, the devil's in the details. Pit People has a lot of tiny details that make it much more enjoyable. For example, each unit type has a special entry corralating to them. And the entries are just an absolute joy.

This game is a-okay in my book!\t\t\t\t\t
