\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPit People is a fairly fast-paced turn-based co-op strategy game, where you control a wide variety of characters with different strengths and weaknesses. The entire campaign can be played with a friend, either locally or online. With two players, each player has their own characters and unlocks, which can be traded with the other player. You can also play head to head in the 2v2 PvP-gamemodes. (4 local players, or 4 online players)

In Pit People each player controls a team of six freely chosen characters. In most of the missions you can recruit the last enemy standing, and use them in your following team compositions. The game has over a thousand unlocks in the humorous Behemoth-style, which really keeps unlocking new stuff exciting. Talk about arrows, which shoot bows or just a pizza slice that can be used as a shield.

The storyline is funny and entertaining, yet if the main quest ends too soon for your personal taste, the game offers over 100 different side quests, with the addition of fighting encounters from the world map.

Pros:
  • Plays well with either a mouse or a controller.
  • Behemoth humour.
  • Visually pleasing universal art-style.
  • A lot of stuff to unlock.
  • Tons of fun with a friend!
Cons:
  • The base gameplay is fairly simple, which could get a little repetitive in a longer run. However the amount of different characters and quests really keep you going.
  • On the world map it is sometimes hard to distinguish which tiles you can walk on and which ones are walls.

Overall rating [8 / 10]

I haven't personally tried to play the game solo, but as a co-op strategy game you should definitely give it a shot. Especially if you have grown fond of the Behemoth style in the past.\t\t\t\t\t
