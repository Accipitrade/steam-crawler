\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a big fan of BattleBlock Theater and Castle Crashers I was really looking forward to trying Pit People out. It’s quite amazing how they can make a game which shares a lot of the good things with the previous games even though its gameplay is completely different. You can definitely tell that it was made by The Behemoth when you see the art style, hear the music and experience the humor in Pit People.

I really like turn based games so being able to play one in co-op with another player was an amazing experience. The amount of story and conversations was absolutely satisfying. It was mostly something we could laugh about. No story heavy "you must pay attention all the time" kind of game. Those are better for single player games anyway.

The turn based combat is very special compared to other turn based games I have played before. Mostly because it’s a lot about your movement, rather than % chance to hit or miss. That makes the gameplay very simple and easy to learn, but at the same time getting really good takes some knowledge of units and practice.

I made a spoiler-free StingeReview video of Pit People and you can check it out here:

https://youtu.be/Uz28lqdVNMU

If you are searching for a funny co-op game to play with your friend, you can never go wrong with a game from The Behemoth. I would recommend you to get all of their games. It’s really worth it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"This game is kind of like others, but slightly worse" That is a quote from this games loading screen, and I find it the best way to describe the game. At first the game will seem unique, and that will be a refreshing change of pace. But like a bent fork, being different does not mean it will be practical. The game then grows stale rather quickly and suffers from critical design choices.

The most glaring issue is that all of the characters are not actually unique or developed in any way besides their names in the menu and their preset race ability. All the unique character designs are nothing more than gear that you can swap out. No characters have a special abilities, no skill tree as they level up, no branching paths or specializations are possible. There is no way to make them stand out. All humans, regardless if they are a hero, random bandit, hired Merc, or a captured villain, play and can look the exact same as one another.

One very popular request from players is the ability to name your characters, but I understand why you cant do custom names now. They are the single thing that makes a character different. But unique names like "Volume", "Not Good", or the mighty "PVP" are the least important thing gameplay wise.

Another issue is the resetting fog of war on the world map. Fog of war can lead to a sense of discovery and serve as an indicator of places you have already been. The Civilization series does this well, revealing the map but hiding enemys when you are far away.

But this game likes to swap your world maps at random, and when you go back to a map that you have ALREADY been at, the fog of war is completely reset. Its annoying to get stuck by the terrain, it covers up your previous progress and makes it easy to forget where you explored on the map already. I lost all interest in exploring after this.

The third issue is an over all lack of intuitive menus and relaying information to the player. The map screen is clear yet does not show the location of your current quest objective. All you get is a pulsing directional arrow, that is leading into the ever resetting fog. You have no inventory menu or ways to dump items from your wagon to make room. When you level up it does not show what it effects. You cannot enter other citys, there are no other shops. The list goes on.

I ended up having to google about the games mechanics because its not relayed well. But most of the time, the features are non-exisitant. There is very little online presence from this game as well, showing the lack of interest and depth. The wiki has dead or missing links all over the place.

I will end with the good: Sound design, character design, artwork, voice acting, writing, comedy timing, and music. All of it is very very well done and I would love to see a Pit People mini series or Net-flix show. And thats how I would recommend experiencing this game. Treat it like a show and not a game. Blast through the main quest and laugh. Hopefully you will finish before boredom makes you want to sacrifice yourself to the Chog.\t\t\t\t\t
