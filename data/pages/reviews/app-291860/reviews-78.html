\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a weird game to review. I love the writing, the map, and the characters but I hate the combat. It is so slow that I swear to god I fell asleep a few times going through the game. The secondary gripe I have with this game is that it heavily encourages the tactic of ganging up on one person with all 6 of your people if possible. The reason for this is because your units don't actually attack who you want them to. They attack one thing in the radious of thier attack range. A bit weird I know. I think (not actually sure) they do prioritize people that they have strengths against but that random value creates these irritating "Woulda won if X had hit when I needed it to". As I said though the writing of this game kept me going because I wanted to see the next funny thing that they came up with. I did not touch multiplayer so that is something I can't really comment on to say if it's balanced or not. I would say that if this was not a Behemoth game I probably would not have touched it. I think $15 is probably what I would buy it for if you are a Behemoth fan, $10 if you are not.\t\t\t\t\t
