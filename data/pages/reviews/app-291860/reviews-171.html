\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, what does this game have going for it?

Behemoth's sense of humor. Anyone who has played any of their games will recognize and appreciate it immediately.

The turn-based combat system is well done.

The level of unit and squad customization is quite impressive, even though not every unit has the same level of customization.

Cooperative and competitive play is solid. I've yet to have any issue either joining, hosting or finding games.


But are there any concerns?

While the combat system is well done, the player has very basic options available to them.
It seemingly comes down to three things;
1) Place unit next to one enemy and attack,
2) Place unit next to 2+ enemies and attack with AoE or rng single target, or
3) Keep unit x tiles away from hostile units and rng range attack single target.

You, the player, have no further imput into the units actions, so apart from testing out team comps and looking for synergetic pairings, the game practically plays itself. There's even an autopilot mode that you can enable in combat to allow the computer to play on your behalf.

I would like to stress, however, that the "autonomous" nature of the game doesn't necessarily make the game less fun.

If you are looking for a game in which you have really indepth control over every action that your units take (ex. X-COM) then this game probably shouldn't be at the top of your list. Should you still consider it though, of course.

If, however, you are looking for a fun, quirky, simple, co-op, turn-based tile adventure/strategy game, then you can't really go wrong with this title.\t\t\t\t\t

The gameplay is fun (albeit, slightly repetitive), the soundtrack is catchy (also, sort of repetitive), and the variety in the different types of weapons and races you can chose from is astounding.
I will admit, most of the weapons feel like reskins of each other (and in fact, are), but the game is still in early access, and The Behemoth has announced that they will be changing up weapon and item stats to give the game more variety.
Overall, this game is an 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t
