There's also a World Map with randomized encounters and you collect every unique enemy (from my understanding) if you so chose. The combat is simplified, but it isn't bad. Think Fallout 2 minus being able to use aimed attacks.

What it isn't: Divinity 2.

Update: Ended up getting pretty burnt-out with the game, but I think that's predominately due to the Gearbox-esque whimsical gratuitous nonsense. It isn't a bad thing, but the ♥♥♥♥ and fart RAWR xD jokes just don't hit the same way when you're in your mid 30s. If you're a younger person? I think you'll have a higher tolerance for it than me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt took me about an hour of playtime before I really started to enjoy Pit People. As far as turn-based strategy exploration games go, it's lovely. Combat is smooth, enemy AI is frighteningly good (they'll flank frontliners to target weak/isolated allies and healers, and will try to escape if they're low on health, so every encounter takes some proper thinking). Music is great, art style is classic Behemoth (if you liked Castle or Battleblock, you'll love this).

It suffers from a few things that took me a little while to get over though.

For one, the game does a very poor job of explaining combat. Behemoth loves minimalism and previous games used signs or quick pop-ups but frequently you'll see a symbol or effect with no cue as to what it is or what it did. It desperately needs to give the player more information early game. But it's never bad enough that you want to quit. It favors aggressiveness like Castle did and you bonk your way through the first few encounters, and eventually you'll get more information that would have honestly been a lot better at the start.

"Why is this happening or not happening"
Later: "Oh it was because of this detail I wasn't told about, okay."

The other, more irritating thing is that Will Stamper did all the narration. At face value that sounds awesome because Stamper as the Narrator in Battleblock is phenomenal. The problem is, if you came from Battleblock like I did, it just sounds like "the Battleblock narrator but he's trying really hard to be a bad guy now" and it really doesn't work if you're used to the hilarity and bizarre humor from before. Instead of a neutral observer, he's more like "haha I am in control and I like making life hard because I am bad" and it really screws with immersion.

I still recommend it as a big fan of The Behemoth and a bigger fan of the genre. I usually play XCOM and Civ, and this is much quicker and less punishing than those games.

8/10.\t\t\t\t\t
