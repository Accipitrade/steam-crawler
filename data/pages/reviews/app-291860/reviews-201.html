\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, let's begin, shall we?

So, to start off, the fact that it's a Behemoth game does bring multiple hopes and anticipations, no doubt. But it also ensures that this game, even in Early Access, has some bars to reach, or at least start showing the work it's wanting to make to get there. It's an interesting situation, with high potential, but high needs and desires.

Which is why, after the first hour of the game, I can honestly tell you that the promise it shows is magnificent, and the game itself so far is quite enjoyable. Between the artstyle coming together quite well with this sort of genre and gameplay, giving the game a life and enjoyability that's present even when wandering the plains of the game, to the gameplay being simple enough to pick up after the initial tutorial, yet intricate enough to ensure it isn't a "Click to win" situation.

Of course, it does have a few issues. In the beginning, there wasn't a large amount of explanation as to how certain gear works and what some of those stats change, or increase or decrease the chance thereof, and will little explanation as to the nature of whether or not weapons were simply reskins, or have their own stats. I found myself struggling through this sort of issue in the beginning, but had it settle out quite well when you find drops that do infact have stats of their own, albeit rather small and specific, but nonetheless. So while it DOES try to enlighten the player through the tips given while traversing, I found the character loadout designing to be a bit of a beast to understand at first, but as above stated, this is easily remedied with a bit of time in the game. Just a slight warning for anyone who may be turned off because of such.

Ultimately, I'd recommend the game. While it is a bit lacking in content outside of the story (this being in Early Access, that should be obvious), I do feel that it brings enough entertainment and interest in it's state right now, even if you don't find an interest in the online/offline multiplayer. And with the Pit, the playability does infact come in greater and greater amounts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, its a bit like heroes of might and magic meets pokemon but a little simplified.

You start with your small band of fighters, and over time you can capture just about any enemy you fight. You can build your squad and mix and match to your hearts desire... this is where the game really shines.

It also offers co-op which to my knowledge is a unique feature in any tactical turn based game... and one that I would love to see other games adopt/emulate.

Overall, its a very good game for the price and id recommend it to anyone who enjoys turn based strategy games.

The cons for me were, its a little too simple... the game does advertise that its a strategical position game... and that pretty much sums it up. And then also, the interface feels clunky in that it is obviously geared towards controller use.

I want to use my mouse, and i'd like there to be the option to make it feel more natural when doing so.

That all being said, its still a great game and I'll probably hit the hundred hour mark.\t\t\t\t\t

whoops, BUTTERFINGERS

Behemoth's stab at a turn-based strategy game is certainly worth a shot, but not without a handful of issues that aren't what you'd expect in EA. So far in my playthrough, the game itself is bug-free and performs without any hitches, much to Behemoth's credit as a respectable developer. However, the controls in menus are just a step above abysmal, clearly being designed to be navigated with a controller. Selections stick and feel imprecise, and at times I feel a need to be extra cautious should I accidentally sell or buy something I don't intend to. The gameplay handles well enough, but when panning the camera via WASD or arrow key the scroll speed is sluggish, and input icons switch to Xbox inputs for whatever reason.

Gameplay itself is deceptively deep, with an easy learning curve and intuitive mechanics leading into a variety of new weapon variants, effects and troop selections once you get a handle on the basics. However, issues again arise in the menus, with visual cues for stats and weapon status effect icons being at best a vague suggestion on how they work. I shouldn't have to play 20 questions worth of guesswork to figure out what exactly something does or how it's implemented, and a lack of a clear-cut glossary or tooltips only serve to frustrate when introduced to what up to that point was a very intuitive and enjoyable experience. Experimenting with different combinations is fun, but not as fun as it could be when I'm left wondering things such as "can I do anything to unfreeze friendly units, or do I have to wait out the effect?"

The story and atmosphere is enjoyable, with the antagonistic narrator that I personally find much more entertaining than Battleblock Theatre, and an experience more in line with what we've seen in the past with Castle Crashers and Alien Hominid. Behemoth's simple, charming signature style works to the game's benefit, with the game boasting a variety of weapons, enemies and recruitable troops that I've only begun to scratch the surface of.

I have yet to touch the MP, but I'll probably write about it later on. Who knows.

Overall a very solid early access title, but not without some glaring issues that I hope to see resolved in the future.
