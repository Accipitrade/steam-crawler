\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKnee jerk super early reaction: This is wonderful.

There is tons of potential here, and it looks like The Behemoth will meet it. The art is great, the soundtrack is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ magnificent (!!), the writing is just as quirky, irreverent, and genuinely funny as one would come to expect from Behemoth. The actual gameplay is where a few ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ come in. The combat system is simplified in a fairly clever manner, and the way the game is able to make absurd, impossible units manageable is commendable, but the UI isn't the best. The controls were clearly designed with controllers in mind, producing some occasional jankiness when it comes to movement and onscreen prompts. The equipment screen in particular is VERY cumbersome (so I left click the character I want to select, left click the space I want them to occupy, then I have to left click again to confirm, despite the lack of any dialogue box... for some reason?).
Apparently the story isn't complete as of writing, but I haven't gone nearly far enough to have anything to say about that, besides the observation that there is a ton of non story content here. You have co-op modes, competitive arena battles, side quests, and free roaming encounters set in what I assume to be a randomly generated overworld (at one point I triggered the all holy bear deity, making him pluck the city/hub off of the map, and drop it somewhere completely different). There is already an insane amount of loot, characters, classes.
I hope some of the UI problems get sorted out, but otherwise, I am having a great time. Definitely worth a look!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first the game seems of, the combat is wierd, the world seems limited (but the comedy is on point). But once you settle into it becomes really fun. The combat becomes strategic (especially if you play on insane mode), its not that strategic that you need to take ages to cotemplate strategies each turn, it just forces you to be smart with every move (perfect for PvP). The world expands into a difficult and large open world, jam packed with content (very strange content). After getting through a weak start the game becomes amazing.

My only dissapointment are the choices of weapons and armour, I was expecting a more RPG type weapon system how every individual item has their own stats, but the game only has set 5 weapons with different stats for each type of weapon (e.g. sheild, sword, ranged). However, this is redeemed by the wide array of characters that you can have in your party. This can vary your strategies depending on the set of character you captured.

I'm honetly suprised how this game isn't on the "top seller" or "popular new releases" section on steam. It might be early access but I havent come across any bugs.

I think this game is a 9/10, would be 10/10 if the beggining was more engaging.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeen loving the game so much so far. I love the game. Some things that I think would make the gamplay better are:
- Add some upgrades for your caravan (Permanent speed upgrade, faster reload, a spray that attracts and repels monsters, larger caravan to be able to store more items)
- Need more late game items or things that you can buy (Call in the bear to bring you home, xp booster)
- I would also love to see a monster tracker, something to locate certain monsters
- I love the way when you collect coins the sound goes higher, but it would be cool if there was a bonus for getting a bunch of coins in a row. Maybe double coins for 30 secs or something like that
- Customizable tournament against AI and alone
- A quest to find a bunch of pieces, and it ends up creating a portal that brings you to a 1 time dimension ( doesn't have to be 1 time use, maybe you need to charge it everytime) and has lots of monsters and/or loot, but has very difficult monsters
- Random events that can go on in your world (Monster Frenzy, Monster Horde, Gold Rush, Raining Somthing)
- Random traps that you can fall in and bring you to a cavern with lots of monsters
- Random healing/reviving fountains around the map
- A way to explore the map and reveal locations and then create a map
I know this game is in Early Access, but it has a lot of potential and I love how it is going so far. I've made it to the top 10 in the tournament (I know I'll lose it soon). I wasn't sure where to add this so I just made it a review. If you guys are taking recommendations, sorry for posting this. Hope you guys read this and maybe add some things!
