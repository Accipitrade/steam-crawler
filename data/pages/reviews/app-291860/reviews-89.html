\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe entire game's budget was unfortunately put into Will Stamper and all the audio work. All the cutscenes that were added during Early Access were rapidly decreasing in quality, and the whole story feels rushed. The main story only has maybe around 10 quests, and the game is FLOODED with sidequests which aren't really that interesting. The gameplay itself is pretty fun for a while, but it's all you'll be doing for hours with the sidequests.

The main story offers some pretty interesting battles, most of which require completing a certain objective rather than killing all the enemies. The majority of them are "break down the gate(s)", and there is one especially interesting level where you have(?) to go through in stealth, and there's an opportunity to pick up some extra loot.

However, equipment is pretty barebones and nothing provides anything special compared to other weapons of the same type. It's just that each type of equipment offers stat changes and advantages against another type of equipment, and what specific item you choose doesn't matter except for visual effect, save for few items that offer elemental effects. I don't consider this a huge downside, but I personally would rather be able to find stronger equipment from harder missions.
Leveling up your character doesn't even do much, just a tiny damage boost.

The major plus this game has going for it, obviously, is that it is a Behemoth game.
That, of course, means the usual pleasant Behemoth visual aesthetic, absurdist humor, and BANGER soundtrack.

Overall, I expected WAY more when I bought this game in EA, and was once again, disappointed due to lack of content in the long run. If you plan on buying this game, get it on sale, and play it with a friend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm honestly upset I cannot re-live my playthrough of this game. I thought it was going to be a boring game to play with my coop partner, but I had plenty of epic moments and wanted to play more and more.

The campaign is only like 6 missions total (I'm not really counting, but that's what it feels like).


Here's my recommendation if you decide to purchase this:

- Play with a friend
- Play on Hardcore (if you lose your story character, only one person needs to begin a new save and you will start right back where you left off)
- You'll probably want to quit the game until you finally get a feel for capturing units, but give it a try if you like strategy games.


Just a few notes (spoilers?):

- Elites (the red units) are tough to fight against and they'll become regular units once they've been captured (nothing special about them aside from the red coloring).
- You can capture literally any unit you go up against, aside from the big stage bosses.\t\t\t\t\t
