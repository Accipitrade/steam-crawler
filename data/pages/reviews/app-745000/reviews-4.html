Card Crawl is a strategy based solitaire-like card game. Card crawl relies heavy on the RNG.

A deck in this game has 54 cards. You have the following cards: Potions, shields, swords, monsters, coins and each draw you get 5 randomly selected ability cards.
You can unlock more and better ability cards in this game if you earn enough money.
You have 13 health points. Besides both sides of your portrait you have two hands and on the right there is a backpack slot for items. There is a box where you can discard cards. You cannot discard monster cards.
Each turn four cards are drawn. Three of those cards must be played before new cards will be drawn.
The goal is to have zero cards left. This means you have to survive for 54 cards. There is some strategy involved, but also a lot of luck.
The high score is based on how much money you have at the end of a game. This score can be used to buy new ability cards.

The game has a normal mode and a constructed mode. In the normal mode the 5 ability cards are randomly selected for you. In the constructed mode, you can choose 5 ability cards you want in your deck.
You also have your daily challenges.
And you have the Deck merchant mode: custom games made by other players. (you need to win 20 games to unlock it)
The game has a tutorial, which is a very good one.
Your progress is automatically saved.

Card crawl can be really addictive. If you like it, that is. I have played it for many hours on the iPad years ago. Now it's on the PC and I still like this game.
It is one of those games you play during a coffee break. One round of cards takes up to a maximum of 10 minutes.
There are many things you can unlock, for me that is and was a reason to keep playing it,
It has, in my opinion a good price-tag.
I would definitely recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCard Crawl is a really good casual, card based video game. It’s easy to learn and enjoyable to play. There are two things that make Card Crawl great: First, playing a full game doesn’t take long. A full game averages maybe five minutes. Second, this is a game that is a lot more tame when it comes to the difficulty. This is not another frustrating/rage inducing game. Success in Card Crawl depends on what cards you are dealt, and how you choose to play them. There is some strategy involved and you do have to think about how you want to play your cards. There are also a lot of special ability cards that you can unlock as you continue to play and get better. The game’s randomness with regards to what cards you are dealt, and which special ability cards are also randomly chosen for you, provide tremendous replay value for Card Crawl. There are also extra gamemodes and characters that can be unlocked as well. This is a game that can be enjoyed in both short play sessions and long play sessions. There’s also a mobile version available for Android (on Google Play), and for iOS (on the App Store). Card Crawl is definitely one of the very best card based video games that there is. For a game that costs so little, you get so much in return. Card Crawl is great if you like casual games and/or card games. I highly recommend it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a simple card game with allot of RNG. None of the mechanics really get too deep. If you like to flex your strategic muscles a little, while not having to worry about being screwed over by unfair advantages. Then you might like Card Crawl.

Though people use to RPG combat mechanics will have a easy time with this. I do not expect anyone new to the genre to pick it right up, and enjoy it because the RNG can be quite frustrating.

It is easier to win in this game, and is allot more fair than Solitaire. Though the two games are almost entirely different in how you play them. Card Crawl has some mechanics, and rules that are more advanced than what players of solitaire are use to, and that could throw them off.

I enjoy it for what it does, but I often get bored after so many matches. I start thinking about what could have been a really well developed card game, with deep enough mechanics to keep me engaged.

The game does feel repetitive, and has nothing all that special about it to remember it by. Apparently the game has other modes of play, but there was nothing here to compell me to play enough matches to get to 20 wins to unlock anything else. That is one it's most annoying features.

It is not a overly serious game, and for that I liked it more. It is also extremely easy to learn, and atleast for normal play, not that hard to master. You will win just by following logic, but you may fumble a few times like I did. If you don't pay enough attention to the cards you are dealt.

Do I recommend it? Well I think for the price it is worth giving it a try, but do not expect it to keep you engaged. I have been playing it casually from, time to time. I consider the game to be a good way to spend some time just having a bit of fun. Unfortunately this title is not going to stand out from the crowd. There are better games on steam, but for the low price you will be hard pressed to find something better. Still it is a fun romp for when you want to get into a quick game.\t\t\t\t\t

The game is not bad, and probably a 6 or 7 out of 10. If you intend to play for a few hours and never play again. If you are going for progression and completion, then Card Crawl is more like a 3 out of 10. Read Dare Evans review on this forum. I completely agree with EVERYTHING he posted and will not be redundant here. It is all true, and not in an opinionated way, but in the literal sense. Essensially you play perfect/count the cards and expert the math out. Yet Card crawl simply boils down to Luck at the higher levels.

Also, just to earn the right to play the "endless delve mode', you must replay the sdame stock deck DOZENS upon dozens of times, till you grind enough to make your own deck. Than grind through a couple hundred more rounds with your deck ( you only pick 5 cards out of 54 mind you) just to earn the right to play the delve mode. Once there you will hit a wall fast at around level 7 or so. I made it to 13, but once there lost my way back to level 6 and am now back to 12. This took 5 hours of win a couple games, lose a couple games. rinse and repeat. You will NEVER win the 4-5 games in a row required to beat level 13/14/15. Just to beat levels 8-12 you must play perfect, and usually barely win on last draw even IF you recieved highly favorable random cards.

Let me pick my cards please, and create a ratio that will NOT allow for such high concentrations of enemies so early in the 58 card draw.

Do not even get me started on the collect 250$$$ gold achievement. I have mathed out scenarios to obtain 250 gold. Even taking all pure gold multiplying drop cards. It still requires The PERFECT drop order, and you still have to survive( and none of the best gold gaining cards will help you survive. The math required to win this scenario is lightning in a bottle.
5 chosen special cards required to drop give or take in exact sequence, placement of where your cards drop in the 4 card order and you still have to survive. And with no mistakes and no special card support, i suspect your odds of winning is around 1 in 3 . Odds of winning this with perfect strategy and perfect play and perfect math and counting, is likely in the 2000-1 range. Have fun with that\t\t\t\t\t
