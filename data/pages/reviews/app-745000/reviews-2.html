\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA small and fun card game, perfect for short burst sessions.

There is no storyline. You start as a hero with 13 hit points, having 2 hand slots and 1 backpack slot to hold cards. You goal is to remove 54 cards without allowing yourself to hit 0 hit points. You are dealt 4 cards initially, and only dealt another round of 3 cards when the deck is left with 1 card (either using the cards, selling the cards, or keeping some in the hand / backpack slots). The variety of cards include enemies (damage cards, can't be sold), gold, potions, shields, swords, and the most interesting part - ability cards.

However, it's not as simple as that of course. This is really a game to test your mental arithmetics, solving calculations in your head as you balance keeping yourself alive by healing, hitting with sword or defending with shield, versus getting as much gold as possible in each match because the cards (potions, shields and swords) can all be potentially sold for gold. At the end of the match, your record will be based on the amount of gold you obtained. Gold is needed to unlock more ability cards (total of 35 to collect) and also to complete the few achievements and quests (allowing you to unlock new avatars and dealers, which are purely cosmetics). To help with winning, ability cards have different powers, such as swapping health with enemy cards, duplicating any cards, doubling the value of the cards and more. Ability cards when used correctly can be game changing, allowing extra survivability, freeing up cards to be sold for gold, and allowing more golds to be gained from synergy between the cards. I have not counted but the number of cards are fixed in each category (so you will only get 5 ability cards in 1 match for example).

Besides the normal mode, there are also other modes for you to check out, such as a constructed mode which you can choose which 5 ability cards you want in a match. They add much needed replayability. I will have to mention that this game is super grindy, you need patience to collect enough gold to unlock all the ability cards, and even more time to complete the difficult quests and achievements. However, you can do so at your own pace, and it is pretty fun figuring out a strategy to win by mental calculations. In conclusion, if you are a fan of card based game, this will be a fun addition to your game library.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCard Crawl is a RPG card game where you try to clear cards. The objective is to clear all 54 cards in a deck consisting of items and monsters.

Gameplay-wise, use a mouse. You have 4 slots; 1 hero, 2 hands and 1 backpack. Each turn 4 cards are flipped over from the 54 card deck. You can only use 3 out of the 4 cards. Using a Monster will damage you. Using Items will reduce the damage or heal you. Try to survive by pitting Items vs Monsters and ensuring your life points do not hit 0.

Combat follows rudimentary mathematics where you just add and minus. You start with a 13-life Knight. You basically ensure the damage does not kill you and your lifepoints do not hit 0. Monsters damage you according to their power. E.g. 10 power Soulstealer deals 10 damage to you. You can reduce the damage to you by attacking the monster with a weapon, healing with a potion after damage is dealt or blocking with a shield. Basically, item cards are add, monster cards are minus. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was going to recommend this game because it is so cheap and for a couple of hours it is a bit of fun.

But unless you like a game with almost no strategy and massive grinding, it isn't worth anything. I dislike this type of game and regret the time I put into the game far more than the money I spent buying it.

Winning a round in the game isn't the point, it would be hard to lose if you don't pay attention to your gold/score. The game is all about maximising your score. You do this buy cashing in as many of your items, swords, shields and potions, as you think you can spare. The strategy is obvious but whether it will work is pure luck based on how the monster cards are distributed.

The only point of getting a high score/gold is to unlock special ability cards. Some of these are interesting but it is completely random which cards you get in a game, and I believe you always get the same number of these cards regardless of how many you have unlocked. So you grind a massive number of games to get cards that mean very little, not fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a damn simple card game, for a damn cheap price, but damn did I "waste" a lot of time on this addicting game. ^.^
I had a lot of fun just playing the normal, constructed and daily deck mode to collect enough in-game credits to unlock all ability cards. The normal game is luck-based, but it's rarely impossible to win a game as long as you get a few decent ability cards. In constructed mode you can choose which 5 ability cards you want to use, which makes it a bit easier to earn credits. Only the monsters can feel a bit monotone, since they're the same throughout all the game modes. I didn't try delve mode yet (you need to have all ability cards to unlock it), so maybe that will fresh up the game experience.
The quests are quite challenging but allow you to unlock new heroes, which gives the game a new feeling. So worth it. ^.^
If you like community made decks that's also possible at the deck merchant.

In short: this game is underpriced :b\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA tentative thumbs up on this one. It is fun enough, amazingly well balanced, but there simply isn't a lot of game here. That said, what there is seems to have addicted a lot of people, from the leader board, so maybe there is something there for you, that doesn't work for me.
The basics are a simply card game, played in a tavern, to simulate a dungeon run.
The gameplay is basic, requiring a little thought and some basic math skills, nothing heavy duty but enough to engage.
The art is delightful, the artist should be doing children's books and board games for big bucks.
The animation is cute, what little of it there is, but it is a bit hurky-jerky at points.
The sounds are good, suiting the game well, without getting too repetitive, and the music is pleasant, in the background where it belongs, and simple.
The controls work fine, though not much is asked of them. Sometimes you have to be a little careful where the mouse pointer is, but nothing extreme.
The game builds in several minor ways. You can unlock special cards that give you nice abilities. These really only serve as a goal though, and aren't really exciting. In fact this can work against you in the normal game, as the more you unlock the less your know what to expect from the cards, only five of them go into the deck, no matter how many you play.
There are a few other areas where you can play, one where you decide the five cards you want, one where you play against decks other players (I think) have made, a daily game which doesn't seem to offer anything special that I can see, and that's about it.
Wait, there is one more style of play, the dungeon delve. You go into successive dungeons, trying to win as many times in a row as you can. While challenging, it is a bit lackluster, you bring nothing forward from game to game, and before you can even play you've made money obsolete, as you have to have purchased all the ability cards to unlock delve mode.
The game has a few quests, which unlock different players and dealers from what I have gathered. The second player is exactly the same as the first, with different art. Not sure why more effort wasn't put into this, hardly a motivation to grind your way thru, just for different pictures, no matter how cute.
If this were a mini-game in a larger role-playing or dungeon diving game I would think it was awesome, but as a stand alone, yeah, I paid three bucks, more than got my moneys worth, but it isn't anything special.\t\t\t\t\t
