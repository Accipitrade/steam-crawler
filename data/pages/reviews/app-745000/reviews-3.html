\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCard Crawl is an ingenious take on solitaire. You play with a set of playing cards and 5 ability cards. Each turn, you are dealt up to four cards, which can be treasure, monsters, weapons, potions, shields or ability cards. Your hero has a set amount of hit points, and the object is to have all the cards dealt out of the deck, and still be alive.

There are 35 ability cards, and you start with a handful at the beginning of the game. The rest are unlocked as you win the game and use in game currency to unlock them. The better the card, the more expensive it is.

As you progress, you also unlock additional features to the game, as in choosing your ability cards beforehand (normally they are taken from your collection randomly); unlocking additional hero cards; and creating and playing other players' decks. I've played a handful of them. They're all over the place, from challenging to WTF were you thinking?!

It's well worth the cost, either on sale or no. For a relaxing, casual card game, it's aces in my book.\t\t\t\t\t

TL;DR

Fun card game with a blend of strategy and RNG, but needs a bit more depth. Inexpensive and offers potential for much more through DLC.

Full Review

In a similar vein to games like Solitairica, Card Crawl offers a lite fantasy setting, amusing visuals, and some replay value. Without going into too much detail, strategy fans may find things a bit lacking in terms of complexity and replayability, and too much reliance upon RNG. This could easily be solved by DLC that adds new challenges, character abilities and/or gameplay modes.

In particular, the inclusion of character abilities (both the player and the enemy) would add a whole lot to what is otherwise an amusing, lite CCG. This lack of depth is my main complaint, and while it may result in me spending few hours in the future playing. With that said, I play countless hours of Freecell and Spider Solitaire, so I may yet prove myself wrong.

Pros

  • Cheap, especially if you catch a sale
  • Lite strategy for when you like solitaire, but want something different
  • Great illustration and design work
  • Does allow for some customization of decks
  • Offers a few modes of play, including a daily challenge
  • Steam Leaderboard for those that enjoy competitive play

Cons

  • Too much grind to unlock certain features, particularly with the lack of strategy available
  • Character quests offer visual differences only; potential for much more
  • Characters themselves are different in image only; compare with Solitairica where each has different abilities
  • Some minor bugs (e.g., Trophy room hangs as of this writing)

Scores

  • Graphics: 9/10
  • Audio: 8/10
  • Gameplay: 7/10
  • Replayability: 6/10
  • Overall: 7.5/10

Overall, if you're a fan of CCG and/or solitaire-style games, give this one a go. At less than $5 CDN, it's good bang for the buck. And hopefully more purchases will encourage the devs to add new functionality to a game that has lots of potential.\t\t\t\t\t
