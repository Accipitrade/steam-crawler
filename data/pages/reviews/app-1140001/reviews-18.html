\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far my experience with this DLC has been enjoyable and while I wouldn't say it's as good as Utopia it certainly comes close.

What I most enjoyed from this DLC and the 2.6 update overall are the origins as they make your empire feel much more unique and make your empire, as well as other empires feel a bit more alive.

Federations are so much more detailed and improved now with lots of benefits and some downsides depending on the type of federation and it actually makes you want to engage in diplomacy with other empires. The galactic community is also fun and interesting and makes the galaxy feel a lot more alive and gives you a new way to cripple your enemies without actually having attack them directly and I think it's a fantastic addition.

I haven't played around with the juggernaut too much but it's an alright Megastructure and if used right it can be a very powerful tool to take down your enemies.

As for the performance, it's certainly better than it was before but it can still be pretty bad. It can still slow down between mid and late game (though not as bad as before) and can stutter quite a bit as well as some big FPS hits towards the late game. I have a somewhat mid-tier computer so your performance could be better or worse.

Overall for all the content this DLC and the free update adds i'd say it was worth 20 bucks but if you can get it on sale I definitely would.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you buy this, you're basically buying it for 1. Origins and/or 2. To support the devs, because the best stuff came with the free update.

What you get in the DLC-

Origins: a big pile of modifiers you can apply to your species when you create them. These can be anything from minor buffs to completely changing your species' playstyle. By far the most interesting part of the DLC, as without it you are limited only to the most vanilla 2.

Megastructures: there are 2, a Kuat Drive Yards-esque supershipyard and the Juggernaut, which is a massive ship you can repair your other ships with. I can see the appeal, but I feel like the resources will be better spent elsewhere.

New Federation types/mechanics: I don't bother with federations so I don't have much to comment on these, but basically a federation type that should suit most empire types. They have levels that provide buffs as well, again I don't have much interest in these.

What you get for free:

The Galactic community: The real meat & potatoes of this update is free! At some point during the game a Space UN will form, that can begin passing resolutions that can affect the galactic balance of power. Finally you can bludgeon your enemies into oblivion without firing a shot! Some resolutions can play to your strengths, or your enemies', and through sanctions a nation that breaks the laws can be crippled. There's even the possibility of forming a galactic council made up of the 3 most powerful nations (a clear nod to the Citadel Council in Mass Effect) that has special power over what can be voted on. All this really spices up the stalemate that ends up occurring in the mid-late game, and again this is free!

Envoys: These are your diplomats, now represented by a character for the first time. They can be used to improve or destroy your relations with particular empires, or curry favor in the overall Galactic community. You'll need these to get your diplomatic ball rolling, and though a simple concept are incredibly important.

Conclusion: Yes, what is actually included in the DLC seems quite small. The Federation stuff might be more interesting to other people. Don't underestimate the importance of Origins though, the level of customization and replayability they add is massive. In the end I give a thumbs up due to the incredible quality of this update as a whole, which the devs definitely deserve a pile of credit for.\t\t\t\t\t
