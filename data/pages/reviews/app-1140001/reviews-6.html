\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(Short version) -- This DLC is a huge improvement for the game! I really like it, and I definitely recommend getting it if you're into the more political aspects of Stellaris like I am. However, I think $20 is a bit much for only making a couple of changes (see below), so I suggest waiting for something like 50% sale or so. Either way, it's a great catch.

(Long version) -- Envoys: The most important change it adds is a new envoy system, where each empire gets a limited number of envoys they can use to improve or hurt relations other empires. It can also be used to boost your power in the galactic congress (I'll cover congress in a moment) if you wish. However, do be aware that envoys have a fairly brief reassignment cooldown after you give them tasks, so be careful when deciding what you use them for. The envoy mechanic does mean that you can no longer improve relations freely with anyone, but I think it is a neat mechanic anyway.
- Congress: The other big change is the addition of a sort of galactic congress. How it works is that there's a list of rules to enforce, and you and the AI can choose from the choices given. After options are chosen, empires periodically vote on them over time. Success is based on a new "diplomatic weight" mechanic, where each empire gets a certain amount of voting power. This power is based on a number of factors, such as military power, population, economic power, and technological edge. Unless your empire is a ridiculous superpower, you may want to pick things other people will agree with or at least abstain from. The congress is neat, but it's a bit complicated for newcomers, so try and get used to it after getting the DLC.
- Overall: The DLC makes the diplomatic parts of the game more comprehensive, which can be fun for some people and painful for others. Be careful about how you decide your position, because if you hate complicated things and you'd rather just sit and relax while empires destroy each other, this is probably not the DLC for you. If you like taking part in politics on every level, however, you might find this mod to be a real treasure.

If you want to know anything else, feel free to leave a comment. I play the game modded, but I don't think I have anything that specifically changes this DLC. Just UI changes and stuff. Anyway, thanks for reading. :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHey! Do you love conquering the galaxy? Do you enjoy massive fleet battles between alien empires? Does the idea of fighting off an extra galactic invader excite you? Awesome! Well forget all that because that was back in the glory years of 2016-2018, now this game is *click* *click* *click* Stellaris has fallen so far from grace its unbelievable.

All the cool stuff that was part of what made Stellaris fun has been replaced with pointless micro management or outright ruined. Fleets have to be split up now because reasons, some people don't like the doomstack. The result? 6 different fleets, 5 of them all set to follow the first one... Yeah really sorted that doomstack problem.

Late game you will be migrating people as your planets become over populated too. That's right YOU will do that. They don't migrate themselves anymore like they did in the past.

Your massive empire that had sectors to manage it for you in the past now still has sectors... but they're largely useless. You can set individual planets to auto build or the entire sector. Neither will give anything close to satisfactory results. And enabling both at the same time? Who knows what happens then....

Your 2 resource types you had to manage in the past is now like 10 different types. Yay. Fun.

Also the AI which struggled a bit with 2 resources but generally muddled through is now entirely incompetent with the added complexity of more resources to juggle.

Stellaris always struggled a bit with late game performance. This hasn't changed. They take a step forward with optimisations then take two steps back by adding complexity nobody asked for.

What nobody asked for but were given anyway:
- More micro
- Pointless clicking
- Different types of resources, alloys are cool, the other 6? Gtfo.
- Ruining sectors
- All this BS indirectly making the AI worse

I realise I have not talked about federations at all really but that's because I don't care about it. It's been what... 2 years since the big 2.0 change to the game and it's still a shambles? Who is steering this ship? Who is in charge of these terrible design decisions they keep making? Time to hire somebody competent and do some actual usability testing with your game.

I haven't seen an end game crisis since 2018. I get fed up with the boring micro and bail before I get the chance to fight one. Also yeah the performance still sucks on Ryzen 3700X, Vega 56 and 32GB of RAM.\t\t\t\t\t
