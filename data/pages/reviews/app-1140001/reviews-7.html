\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's pretty good, I would say. Makes the game a lot more interesting and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ IS THE NEW DIPLOMACY SYSTEM so much better than before. It's as simple as assigning an envoy to harm or improve relations. Before the only way to harm relations was to insult, and I had no clue on how to improve relations BEFORE the new update. Anyways, basically Federations basically adds on more stuff to the 2.6 update.
So, some things to note: Federations adds some new federation types if I remember. Aside from the Galactic Union, there's the Trade League, Martial Alliance, Research Cooperative, and Hegemony. All of these have unique perks that can be upgraded with Federation Level, which is slowly gained with Federation XP. Another thing to mention is that YOU can use federation laws! Federation laws can basically be used to unlock new perks or enact certain restrictions on the federation. New laws can be unlocked as your federation gains new levels. These laws range from changing how centralized your federation is to having the President of the federation be the only one allowed to vote for wars. The more centralized your federation is, the more powerful the President is, and this allows you to basically become an ALMIGHTY GOD.
Or you could just be ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ lame and have a regular federation.
Next off, we have the Galactic Community. The Galactic Community is basically the place where you can push your agenda and become the masters of the galaxy. The Galactic Community can vote on "galactic laws" which affect every single player (or AI) in the galaxy, ranging from ecological protection to allowing research sanctions. The Galactic Community will vote on a certain law, yes or no. Whether that law passes or not is based purely on diplomatic weight, which means that if you have a massive amount of diplomatic weight compared to everybody else, it doesn't even matter how many people vote AGAINST your vote. The Galactic Community also has a galactic council, which is basically super important. The Galactic Council's abilities range from being able to force a new proposal into the voting area immediately to being able to veto proposals all-together, meaning that if you are on the Galactic Council, down the line you are likely going to be a major bully to anybody who opposes you. There's also sanctions. Sanctions range from economic to research to administrative, and there's three tiers. The higher the tier, the more severe the sanctions get. You can vote to sanction members that are breaching galactic law, so guess what? MORE BULLYING!
Also, Origins.
I like them, that's all there is to say. It's a fun new addition to the game and offers us some cool, interesting gameplay styles we've never seen before. Federations adds some new ones, but it doesn't add all of them.
Finally, Juggernauts and Mega-Shipyards. Juggernauts are basically giant, portable shipyards with laser guns n' everything else. Theoretically, you could take out enemies with a giant shipyard-of-doom while it's creating more ships to take out enemies. It's a really cool concept, but damn do I wish I could afford it in game ;(
Mega Shipyards are in the name, it speeds up ship construction (i think construction in general?) by 100% when you upgrade to max and it basically makes a lot of ships, really really quickly.

Anyways, all I can say about Federations is that if you want more origins, new federation types and a new way to play the game, pick up Federations.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t4 issues I see with the new 2.6.3 patch and the federation system:
1.\tThe AI now stacks their fleets and makes it almost impossible to take them on.
Especially when the Khan event happens with a Mercenary empire.
Example: I had 1 mono fleet vette with 6.2k strength. The AI had 2 fleets each at 3.4k. The AI chewed through me in a heartbeat. I lost half my fleet and the AI only lost about 4 or 5 vettes from both fleets. I have never noticed the AI doing this before this last patch. Because of this and Fleet command and naval capacity are somehow nonexistent as an option to research anymore I have never survived passed my first war with an AI.
2.\tThis is a bug: When I enter and system that has an enemy fleet, science vessel, construction ship, or transport my fleet attacks them and ignores the starport. When enemy ship(s) warps out of the system my fleet flies to the edge of the system like it is still engaging the fleet. No damage is given or taken from or to the starport. But if another enemy fleet enter the system they automatically attack my fleet but my fleet does not engage and gets wiped because it also doesn’t retreat.
3.\tI have noticed now that since the new patch every AI I first meet closes their borders and relations are always bad. This often leads to war within 10 years of first meeting and because of the number 1 issue at the top I have to start a new game.
4.\tTo make Federation work you have to create agreements and pacts with the AI (if you meet one that doesn’t automatically go straight to hostile) which uses Influence. I have created 2 research agreements and a commercial pact and my influences was dropped to 1 per month. This halts your expansion and cripples your empire. You can’t use rivalry to get more influence because they go to war pretty quickly, and this leads back to issue number 1 and have to restart. They should add an edict that gives you a boost to influence or reduce the penalty of influence when making agreements or pacts.

I have been playing Stellaris since it first came out and with the gaming industry making one and done games I have bee very impressed with how Paradox has continuously improved the game. I will play this game until the end of time. But right now it is impossible to survive war with multiple Empires which is happening more often now. Also I have never been able to survive a war with a federation, again due to issue 1. Taking a break from the game until some of these issues get worked out.
