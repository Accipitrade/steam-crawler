\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDlc is alright

-Federation rework is amazing, but federations pre-rework were garbage, so not a great comparison. Hands down the best part of the dlc, but the rest is a disappointment. DLC is worth it on the federation rework alone if you enjoy vassals/political
intrigue [still recommend it on sale though].

-The galactic community saps most of the joy out of the dlc: forced bureaucracy with AI that votes for things on a whim. There are hundreds of examples in the community that show how bad the AI is, often voting for things that actively would be against their interests, or wanting to repealing a law *they voted to enact less than a year ago*. Certain resolutions can completely cripple your empire, and since there are so many resolutions going through the pipe it is constant micromanagement.

-New origins are neat, not much to say besides giving different starting scenarios.

-The juggernaut is cool, but barely the be-all-end-all that the trailer would have you think it is. It is the strongest ship in the game but it is also;
~~Slow as hell, you can't customize it with speed boosters so it lags behind, plus the ship AI breaks if you have anything follow the juggernaut
~~Can't join a fleet, so it can be picked off if rng says that is the thing the AI wants to target
~~Worth only about the same power as that many alloys in battleships/titans
~~Mobile base would be cool, if you couldn't cap enemy star bases to heal mid-war to begin with\t\t\t\t\t
