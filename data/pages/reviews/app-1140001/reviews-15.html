\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish there was a "Middle of the road" recommendation. With the changes and DLCs that have been released for this game, This is probably one of the more important ones in terms of structure to the mid-game gameplay. Ancient Relics and Distant stars for the early game padding, which combined with trying to secure territory in the early game, give you plenty to do, Utopia and Apocalypse help the late game with megastructures and planet crackers / titan class ships to increase the depth of combat with new ways to overpower your adversary along with the planet/sector managing you'll be doing by this point as well.

Federations is essentially a massive early mid-game boon, and with the addition of the council, and galactic community, may make two runs play out extremely differently even with the exact same empires in the map, One run may be heavily biased towards pacifism, another making not having a massive fleet a violation of galactic law. It's pretty good content. For what it actually adds for payed content is a little on the light side, the free update that rolled out along side it doing a majority of the changes.

If you're a powergamer, you're not going to have much trouble swaying the federations or galactic community/council, and the AI, while improved massively (As in, they have the faintest understanding that ships shoot now, and moving a 30k into a 220k deathstack is not a grand idea) still -woefully- lacks in the diplomacy department, and I'm not sure there's going to be much ol' paradox can do about it. I just don't think they'll ever have an AI that can see the player's nation having a ton of sway and saying "We should put a stop to this." while also believably letting the player "pull one over" on them. They're either omni-present, or artificial idiots.. and that probably won't change without heavy development (and CPU intensive) work. It's probably balanced around large PVPVE environments with +10 humans and AI mixed in.

If you play RP style, limiting yourself personally to not "abuse" certain things, playing a faction from a series or tv show, yeah, I think this is 100% worth your time, the AI, While ... acceptable at best, Is very much capable of shaping the galaxy on their own, and some people are going to love that. If you only play this game to go against the AI, make a cool nation and see how it all works out in the end, You'll get some real enjoyment out of it.

TL;DR:

If you play this game to push metas and attempt to find the most rediculously powered combinations, potentially for pure pvp, this'll fall flat fast, and you would be better suited getting other DLCs with much more content to 'play' around with. Skip it. Your best features came for free with 2.6.0.

If you play or plan to play with a large group of friends/players (around 5-15) Every empire will want different things out of the galactic community, and it'll very, very rapidly fill up your midgame with busywork and things to do, and the diplomacy changes and favor system added in this game will shine brightly. There's nothing wrong with any of the mechanics, It's just impossible for a computer to do it well enough.

If you play by yourself and go on campaigns that are mostly casual/RP based, This is -definitely- worth your time, and will easily boost the content density from 'somewhat lacking' around midgame, to action packed, right up until you start getting near the victory years.. And probably past them, But by then, you've got more things to worry about. And I think it does it's purpose well for getting you to it.\t\t\t\t\t

It's a lot of fun but the bugs are very frustrating and also sometimes hilarious.

In my federation the new leader is chosen by psionic battle, i am a psionic empire along with 1 other big psionic empire so i assumed one of us 2 was going to win. I spent 1000 zro on boosting my psykers. A popup said all kinds of disturbances where happening in my empire so i assumed i might have overdone it.
I read the results and burst out laughing at 2 am.

Somehow my one planet vassel, who's species had no psionic capabilities, won.

I love it and hate it.

(edit)

As promised, i have changed my review to positive because most of the bugs have been fixed, stellaris federations adds a massive amount of depth to the game and made engaging in diplomacy a lot more fun. The roleplaying that comes with forming a federation and leading that federation to war against a big empire that has been terrorizing you and your neighbors for the last 50 years feels great. Seeing that big empire lose half of it's influence in the galactic senate due too losing the war feels satisfying.

You can now be properly diplomatic, sending your envoys to suspicious neighbors and forming trade relations with them. I now play on a higher difficulty (admiral) because through being diplomatic i can survive and expand easier\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm fairly harsh with my Stellaris reviews. I took about a 3 year break from stellaris after 2.0 began, but the trailers for federations looped me back in slightly. I honestly was expecting to get robbed for $20 like usual, but I'm happy to say I absolutely love this DLC and it has brought me back into the game.

The new Origins features are super cool, though I've noticed the federation origins kind of put you in a weak position. The "On the Shoulders of Giants" origins is really cool to have, though I didn't get very far in so I'm not sure how it ends. I'd rate Origins 9/10. Now, on to my favorite part.

I love diplomacy in games. For example, the Jedi Consular story in SWTOR is really fun to me (yes, I know that's an unpopular opinion). So when I launched into the federations aspect of the DLC, I'm pleased to say it's really great. Some have mentioned this, but the Galactic Community is more or a less a senate where you can pass or down-vote different resolutions. It's similar to the United Planets Council (I think that's the name?) in Galactic Civ II. It's a good distraction mid-game. I love the various military resolutions you can pass and I imagine sanctioning can get fun in multiplayer. I can see the groundwork for resolutions to be passed about endgame crises and I cannot wait to get there. I like the concept of diplomatic weight & that it actually impacts voting in the Community resolutions.

The new federation aspects aren't too bad either, though I feel like something is missing. Still, the ability to change centralization of the federation, the size of fleet contributions, and many other things is really cool and I'm happy to see these features were added. I haven't noticed any bugs so far which is a huge plus given most releases these days are half-baked.

Overall, I'd say it's definitely worth $20, but of course buy it on sale if that happens. I'd have to rate it a solid 9/10 I think, though if you don't care for diplomacy, I'd say to definitely wait until it's at least 50% off. It won't be worth the price for you. If you love diplomacy as much as I do, definitely buy this DLC as soon as you can!!!\t\t\t\t\t

I've had more fun with empires that aren't just about bullying their neighbors. The bureaucracy change also bumped up the viability of playing wide while maintaining high research, assuming that you maintain enough consumer goods production. In general, I think the patch made peace much more interesting. I haven't gotten to late game in anything as of yet, as I've ended up jumping around different origins to see what kind of playstyle differences there are.
I quite enjoy the Hegemony style federations - you establish yourself as a big entity, and then force everyone else into your sphere of influence. You get to play a super power that isn't just eating everyone near them, but rather integrating them into useful allies. And you can do this either through diplomacy or warfare! As an added bonus, assuming that you don't lose your diplomatic relevance, you get to be the federation leader indefinitely long, and thus control the new and improved federation fleets.
The game also runs much better on my system - I've not detected significant slowdown around the mid-game with a Large galaxy with the maximum amount of initial empire spawns.
My current game has me as the leader of a level 3 Hegemonic Federation made up of me, my 2 vassals, and 2 actual allies. The federation fleet is matching my personal fleet's power one to one, and I've not put a single unit of alloy into building it myself.
The difficulty's on scaling up to commodore difficulty, but I probably should've put it to either no scaling, or bumped to admiral.
Oh, and choosing either one of the federation founding origins is probably playing on easy mode, as you effectively have three times the economic base that you're supposed to in the early game. Of course two thirds of that is managed by an allied AI, but since they're in your federation they're more or less yours to use in early game rush warfare.
Bump that advantage even higher if you usually play without the 2 guaranteed habitable planet spawns nearby, since your allies start on those assuming you have them on.
I might do a non-fanatic pacifist run at one point or another, and see if that can be as entertaining as the military hegemony branch. Tearing down enemy governments and setting them back up as politically aligned federation members might be fun.\t\t\t\t\t
