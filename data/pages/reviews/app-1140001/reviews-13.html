\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just want to preface this review which is mostly me praising this by saying at the time of writing there are some pretty bad bugs concerning multiplayer desync and the juggernaut disappearing after reloading saves, which is really annoying, but I wouldn't say is game-breaking. PDX worked hard for months to jam pack this with as many new features as possible and its understandable some bugs pop up here or there, but when they are fixed the experience will be even better.

I've been playing Stellaris since around when it came out, and this is, along with Utopia, probably a competitor for the best DLC. The new federations system and the different types of them, as well as the origins introduced (especially the ones locked behind federations such as shattered ring) are incredibly fun, and I had multiple late night sessions with friends conquering the galaxy and sheev palpatining our way to the top of the senate in our new research pacts, martial alliances, and of course our glorious trade leagues.
The sheer joy and experiences alone were enough to make Stellaris even more so one of my favorites of all time, and I just love this DLC so much.
The new diplomacy system was urgently needed, and I stopped playing this game before the release of Federations, which has revitalized my interest. I have a hard time separating whats DLC content, and whats 2.6 content, so I'm just going to group them in.
The Senate
This is, by far, the best part of this new update. Being able to basically control the galaxy with 'soft power' as ASpec put it, is amazing. At a certain point, if you have a big enough fleet, big enough economy, or good enough tech, or a big enough alliance, you can just pass whatever you want, sanction who you want, benefit yourself how you want, and more. This is both in the senate and in your federation. In my game, my friends and I had fun giving ourselves permanent seats on the council and reducing the size to only us until our third friend could join the council, after which we promptly expanded it to three seats and granted him a permanent seat. We would all just veto resolutions we didn't like, emergency measure whatever needed to happen quickly, and sanction anyone who opposed us, all while snowballing thanks to all the bonuses the resolutions gave us and our shiny new 'Galactic Bank' lvl 5 federation which was absolutely exploding our economies. It was so fun. We achieved complete galactic dominance through our branch offices and senate power and our position in our federation, we had no real need to wage war. But, of course, we did anyways!
We used the new juggernaut ship which was a load of fun and an entirely new dynamic to be able to repair and replace my fleet on the go. We made the assembly megastructure to further solidify our position in the senate, had fun as shattered rings and voidborne and so on, and used diplomacy to do a whole slew of things.
Seeing Stellaris kind of go on a decline in the last year and not really enjoying my favorite game as much, I was beginning to become sad about it and lose hope, but reading all the notes on this upcoming patch and DLC really got the fanbase excited, and when it finally arrived it did not dissapoint. PDX knew they had to hit the nail on the head here if they wanted Stellaris to prosper and become a more enjoyable video game, and thats exactly what they did. It took a few extra months from the original release date, and it was entirely worth it and I wouldn't change a thing. I hope they continue in the future to make DLC of this caliber and patches as well, even if it takes a few extra months or weeks because that's the right way to do it and it clearly paid off in this example.
Overall, I have to recommend this DLC, it is so, so great, and the many experiences I made in both single and multiplayer in only three days of having it really is a testament to that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is easily the best expansion for Stellaris so far. It finally makes the galaxy feel more alive with intrigue and interactions among the galactic community. The new diplomacy options are my favorite of any 4x game I've played. Just the tension that exists in your own Federation is enough to make you think more deeply about your relationships with the other empires and species, evoking that feeling of emergent gameplay we get from the character interactions in crusader kings 2.

The problems with this DLC are fairly minor. As always, piecemeal DLCs to make a game playable is a dubious model. At least in this case, the price is good for what you get, and if you were having trouble enjoying the mid-game of stellaris before, it fixes that. Additionally, as others have said, there are numerous bugs that need to be worked out. Because of the transparent development cycle and constant improvements to Stellaris in the past, I feel confidant that they will be worked out. It's a bummer in the meantime, though.

Federations seem to be very powerful. I haven't decided if I think this is OP yet, but I'm leaning that way. The reason I say that is because in my game so far, empires that are not federation members are basically being crushed. It still has interesting conflicts between federations, but the threat of an exterminator or enslaving autocracy is not very high, and I think that takes something away from the experience.

And, although the AI's interaction with the diplomacy mechanics seems to pretty savvy, they still aren't that good at playing the game overall. The gradual difficulty setting does alleviate that a bit, but it's hard to tell exactly how to set it to see if you're going to have an enjoyable experience 100-400 years into the game. Even if it were easier to tell, there's something about giving AI empires arbitrary resource buffs that just chaffs me.

For me, this was the DLC that finally made Stellaris what I expected it to be the first time around, but it still has some things it can improve upon. Once those bugs are ironed out, I recommend it to anyone who likes grand strategy games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty solid DLC. The origins feature is very interesting, and I like the new anomalies and dig-sites. The galactic community is kinda meh, it's weird to get a hold of, and they don't give you any sort of tutorial for you to understand what exactly is going on. I still haven't figured out how to denounce another empire.

Since it's a Paradox game, there are, of course, a few bugs. But I expect these to be fixed by the time you read this review.

The one I noticed the most is that when I was playing with the Tree of Life origin, I conquered a primitive world, then realized that the primitive world didn't have a tree of life since I didn't technically colonize it. So, I shuffled everyone off the planet, then recolonized it. I got the tree of life sapling, but the modifier for no tree of life persisted. So, be warned if you're ever playing with the tree of life origin. I will remove this part of the review once it's fixed.\t\t\t\t\t
