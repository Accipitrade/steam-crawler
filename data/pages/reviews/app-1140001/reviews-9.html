\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter doing one playthrough of vanilla I figure its time to do my steam community service and write a review for Stellaris: Federations


***PROS***

-Origins are a blast and offer a more customized start and setup. Some of them really change the dynamic of the game like the Void dwellers or the federation / Hegomony start. Origins is a very welcome feature that helps to shake up the early game.

- Diplomacy revamp is a great improvement from before. Envoys are highly useful and in limited supply so you must choose where to use them. Whether it's improving or harming relations with other empires or using them to improve your federation or boosting your clout in the senate. You will constantly wish you had more of them!

- Federations, the namesake of the expansion, got a MAJOR overhaul. Love the new mechanics as well as the origins that let you start as a federation on day 1, by giving up your guarenteed habital worlds in return for some stalwart allies with atleast 1 shared ethic. The new federation law system reminds me of crown laws from CK2 where over time you can change it to fit your desired playstyle.

- Vanilla AI economy is GREATLY improved compared to 2.5. Not as good as some of the mods like Star Net, but a huge leap forward compared to 2.5.

- The Juggernaught and mega shipyard are VERY welcome additions. Mega shipyard in particular having 20 shipyard slots and a massive speed bonus to just pump out ships during late game when your resource stockpiles are overflowing.

***Cons***

-Performance has been greatly improved, but the late late game still slows down quite a bit on larger maps. its 2020 can we get a game that doesn't lag right when gets really awesome near the end with the crisis and galactic wars??

- BUGS! There are some MAJOR bugs in there right now that can really take the fun out of a campaign. Crisis AI seems to be very passive, which sucks the fun and danger out of an endgame campaign. Also there is some fleet AI bugs, and issues with federation members refusing to follow the player into battle when using the take point command, things like this while not gamebreaking, are highly frusterating and dampen the fun on what would otherwise be a perfect expansion. Hopefully these will be fixed with the upcoming 2.6.3 beta patch.

***TLDR***
Overall, if you don't mind a bit of the usual PDX bug and follow up patches, I would recommend Federations for any serious stellaris fan.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI own every expansion for EU4, HoI4, and Stellaris. Unfortunately, most of said expansions are either extremely buggy on release or generally lackluster for the price and hard to recommend. Not this one though. This is -- by far -- the best expansion Paradox has ever released for any of those games.

Very few bugs (I did notice three or four, but they were all minor and not frustrating), great concept, executed very well -- there's only a handful of small quality-of-life changes I feel could've been added, but aside from that, this is practically flawless. Must-buy for any Stellaris fan. It dramatically improves the game; even for genocidal empires it makes things more fun.\t\t\t\t\t
