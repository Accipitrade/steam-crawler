\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR It's great. If you're a Stellaris fanboy it's an instabuy, if not wait for a sale. Whatever works.

This expansion gives the game a huge breathe of new life. HUGE.

Diplomacy in Stellaris was so limited previously: war, cease fire, non-aggression pact, defensive pact, federation. Federations were mostly about hoarding together large shared fleets. That was it.

Now, it's so much more. SO MUCH. Federations themselves expanded in role tremendously; the Galactic Community can fundamentally change how you play the game; envoys change how you do everything. The Origins are a long desired icing on the cake.

THE GOOD:
-Galactic Community is a (mostly) well thought out and adaptable system. You get diplomatic weight from a number of factors: tech, fleet power, population, economy. Your diplomatic weight is essentially your "say" in how things are done. Almost all of the bills either add to or detract from one of these factors. The early bills are positive leaning mixed blessings, and the later ones are hyper-specialized and punishing. There is a lot of room to play. Is someone breaking the law? Sanction them and wreck their economy. Buy favors from other factions to push through your agenda. You can walk out if you feel the need, but I've never had any desire to.
-Federations are so much better now. There are multiple types with different benefits and requirements. The federations level over time, providing additional bonuses (and an incentive to maintain them).
-The envoy system is great. Off the bat, they can help you stall a war, or avoid it entirely with your neighbors. They can increase your Diplomatic weight. They can make your federation level faster. Previously you had to throw money at people and hope they'd reciprocate. Now you send envoys. It's perfect.
-The Origins provide a lot of challenge and variety to the game. Now, many of them are pulled from mods to a lesser or greater extent, and others used to be civics that you couldn't repeal. Now they're origins. Want to visit an old challenge, the Life Seeded world? Now it's an Origin, and you get to pick two real civics! Want to start in a habitat? Go for it. Want a basic vanilla start? Go for it.
-The Juggernaut, a powerful mobile shipyard is cool (if a bit niche in application) and Shipyard megastructure is great. I found the Juggernaut to be highly useful against crises, but not that strong against other empires.
-Carrier ships are actually useful now.

The Bad:
-Stellaris is already a rather complex grand strategy game, where you have multiple factors going on at the same time. Research, labor, expansion, militarization, diplomacy. This makes it more complicated. The systems added were relatively simple and straightforward, but it's something else to manage.
-There is a high degree of interconnectedness with previous DLC. As in, you will need the older DLC to get the full value out of this expansion. It's just the way it is.
-Stellaris still lacks a good set of victory objectives. Either conquer the galaxy wholesale, or defeat the endgame crisis before it kills everyone. That's it, and that's still it. I wish Stellaris had something like Civ with numerous victory types.
-Violations of Galactic law are a pain in the ass. Which of your 34 citizen species has the wrong living standard? Search that list! You bought a pop off the market and haven't set up their living standard yet? Criminal!
-There is an edict available through the Galactic Council for bum resettlement, but best I can figure it doesn't do anything.
-Some of the origins are wildly OP right now. The variety is cool, but not if it remove's the game's challenge. Stellaris was never particularly balanced, but these Origins. Holy crap.

All things considered, a great expansion. One of the best. There's no reason not to get it if you like Stellaris.\t\t\t\t\t

7. Determined exterminators when beaten get "purged", which then makes the galaxy hate you +1000, which now actually means something (there are huge penalties now for people hating you) and isn't preventable, AND they will only "forgive you" 1 single point PER DAMN YEAR. So yes, Stellaris is a brilliant game, when it works. And right now, it's not working and Paradox need to either admit it can't fix it and let the community sort Multiplayer support, or actually fix the damn game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComparatively, this is one of the best DLC by far. Most of the ones after Utopia have been plagued by bugs and sometimes to the point where Stellaris has been unplayable. But the content's pretty good too. The galactic community's design is solid, allowing you to vote on tons of measures before they're even on the senate floor. Although the AI vote for idiotic stuff and have a fetish for sanctions. Council members will yank random sanctions onto the senate floor mid-crisis. Plus some laws seem poorly balanced or plain idiotic for any empire to vote for but it's largely a coherent and unintrusive addition outside of the typical stupidity of the AI.

Federations remind me of CK2, with the autonomy and the like. Variations to choose from make federations more worthwhile and give a genocidal madman like me an incentive to play nice with anybody else. Diplomacy makes more sense and the envoy system's pretty good. Juggernauts are alright, they're basically weaponized shipyards.

In all, a solid addition and for once it didn't break the game. The AI's still dumb though.\t\t\t\t\t
