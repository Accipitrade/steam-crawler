\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have mixed views on federations; it improves the game significantly, but falls short of achieving some of its most ambitious goals. I would give this DLC a middling rating if steam had an option between “ YES or NO ” when selecting if you recommend a game.

Pros:
Many of the features of this DLC are quite enjoyable and add interesting elements to the game. The new origins increase replayability for a game that had quite a lot of it already as more customisation options are always nice. In addition, the new federation mechanics are certainly an improvement over the previous system as the new federation types, laws and levels make membership much more interesting than before. I also enjoy the new galactic community and the improved diplomatic system that adds envoys to improve or harm relations instead of spamming gifts or insults as before. Finally, the Juggernaut and Mega shipyard are fun additions to late game warfare. Once again, more options are always nice.

Cons:
My main issue with Federations is that Paradox got my hopes up when it comes to AI improvements. Some reviewers seem to see the difference, but in my games, their behaviour is either just as bad or worse than before. For example, while at war to defend its own territory from a neighbour, my ally chose to go through 3 or 4 AI empires which he had truces with (taking 2876 days) to move his fleet from one side of his empire to the other, instead of taking the fastest and safest path that would have taken around a year or two. In a previous defensive war, he just moved his fleets between two systems in the middle of his empire in an apparent (failed) attempt to merge them, until finally losing multiple systems without at fight as I was too busy elsewhere to save him. I could provide many more examples of this type, but my space is limited. Based on my experience, my theory (possibly wrong) is that the AI is now better at moving its forces in offensive wars, no longer declaring wars with fleets 1000 days away from their enemy. However, those improvements do not seem to apply in defensive wars and the AI is even dumber than before. For a DLC focused on alliances (federations) AI improvement needed to be much better.

If Paradox had said nothing about improving the AI, this DLC would have received a positive review, as poor AI was something I expected from Stellaris at this point. However, after being told improvements were ahead, I expected the AI to be slightly better or at least for the status quo to be maintained. What I did not expect was what seems like worse performance and for this reason I feel the need to warn potential buyers to reduce their expectations. This is unfortunate because federations is a very good DLC in seemingly every other way. I can only hope that Paradox will spend more time on AI improvements as they are still sorely needed.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: buggy, lack of content for price, and of course seems like most of this stuff should have been in the base game when launched. Classic paradox style.

The BAD:
The origins aren't balanced, so if playing multiple player you're at a huge disadvantage if you don't take a specific OP origin. Some of the origins are bugged and you can't finish the final parts of it to get the final bonus. The federation part seems like it's so simple that it should have been in the base game. I feel like Stellaris was literally done years ago, but the developer just broke it up into as many DLC as possible to get the most money out of it. Some of the best updates I've seen are from mods, some of which were stolen and put into this DLC looking at you ring world start. The dev doesn't seem to be bothered by the low quality of DLC, lack of content, or literally any concerns of their player base. They just want the money, and know there isn't anything else out there that can do better.

The other thing that bothers me is that they destroyed tall empire game play. They basically added in a way to get more admin cap, and made it so that wide empires don't have any disadvantage to tech. Now it's wide or suck. Biggest issue with this DLC. The main issue I say this isn't a recommended DLC.

No competition, is the best competition.

The good:
The added music is wonderful. The added origins are an interesting spin to the game. Finally fixed the long game lag issue. Finally added in a way to improve relations with others via diplomatic envoy, which was needed in the base game needed for peaceful play styles. Literally gifting away all your resources so people would stop attacking you was the worst mechanic ever. This literally opens up a new play style moving forward. They added in a few extra races models. They made it so I can now have a machine world start (should have been in the machine DLC to be honest). The galactic senate seems fun so far. Will see what limitations there are after a few plays.

Overall I fill like this just isn't enough content for a full $20. Wait for it on sale for 50% off.\t\t\t\t\t
