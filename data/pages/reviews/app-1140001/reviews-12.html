\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe recent development moved Stellaris into the direction of EU4 and the likes. It's not a game about the vastness of space where different civilization rise and fall, but instead it's a small-minded game of risk with +10% modifiers and artificial rules like "claims" and "choke points" - words from medieval warfare. After playing for about an hour I am locked in between three nations: 1 friendly ally and 2 hostile enemies who allied themselves. All systems I have access to are surveyed, all solar system improvements built, all colonies founded. Nothing to do anymore other than building a district once a year. +3 game speed here we go. Don't believe these fancy images and cinematics you can watch on steam - that's not how your game looks like while you play it. It's just some progress bars ticking up. The only way for me to "expand" is to conquer my neighbors by force. As territorial expansion and war is the only way to play this game there is no other way to proceed. The only thing which remains now is waiting until my fleet is stronger than theirs. In that war I would of course also be all on my own because my AI "ally" still doesn't understand how to help in a war. Good thing that I can now send "emissaries" to tick some number up by +2 every month to make them like me. You can of course also tick it down by -2 because it makes complete sense to send an "emissary" to a foreign nation to make them angry. Stupid me thought that foreign nations get angry because you would sabotage their military (not in the game), stir up the population of a planet to rebel (not in this game), steal their technology (in another game, but not this one), change the "factions" of pops (no, not in this game), or maybe try to assassinate their leaders (leaders are characterless stat sticks anyway so why would you want to - but if you would want to you could not in this game). For all of this glorious boringness and absence of meaningful game mechanics you can now pay $20 and waste hours of your life away.\t\t\t\t\t

This DLC comes with major improvements of the engine. You can enjoy the game even in the endgame, without major lag. In multiplayer, I've only seen 1 desync over the course of a very long session on a huge galaxy.

The DLC itself is brilliant. You can now do so much more than just fighting. Politics and diplomacy now actually are fun. Plus, the new Origins you have to pick during Empire creation are really spicing things up and add a lot of replayability to the game.

This is the first Stellaris DLC that I can seriously recommend. Well done, Paradox!\t\t\t\t\t
