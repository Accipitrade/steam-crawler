\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnce again this expansion falls into the "recommend if it's on sale" category since it's overpriced. The stuff here is mostly good though.

The galactic community wasn't the best at launch but now it's greatly expanded and is a good feature. However a big part of it (galactic custodian) is only available if you buy the Nemesis expansion, so i'd recommend getting that along with this. I'd also say it's a pretty impactful feature but it's also not essential; games function just fine without it. Overall though a really good feature.

The expanded federations again aren't essential but if you like playing xenophile empires it's a good feature and the expanded laws especially are actually a great feature.

Origins are nice but again a few origins are added by other expansions, and while this one has some great options I feel like they could've added more. As it stands again, these aren't essential but most are fun to at least try once.

The juggernaut and mega shipyard are good if you like more militarist empires, but again (noticing a pattern here?) they aren't essential.

That's what this DLC is, a collection of decent features that aren't anything amazing and don't exactly justify the price. So get it on sale and if the features interest you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tbest Stellaris DLC after Utopia (Utopia being basically mandatory to even come close to enjoying the game). The galactic community is an excellent representation of the inefficiencies of bureaucracy and democracy on a galactic scale. The vast majority of the reforms are nearly pointless/useless and only by taking the reigns yourself to pass the things you want can you ever get anywhere. It's exactly how I would've imagined it and is incredibly immersive. The Federations themselves are also fun to create and mess around with, and they're just different enough between the types to make each one play out a little bit differently without losing core mechanics. The Void Dwellers origin is excellent as well, being both powerful and a very different way to play the game. Still not worth $20 though lol, buy it on sale like sane people\t\t\t\t\t
