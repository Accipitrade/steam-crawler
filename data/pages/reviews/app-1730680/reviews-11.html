\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen this remaster was announced, I was a little ecstatic. I wasn't expecting the Klonoa series to return after it sort of fell into obscurity even after the first game's Wii remake, but to see both Klonoa 1 and 2 being finally released on modern platforms was something I was definitely happy to see.

It's especially a good thing since, after I played the Wii version of the first game I was hoping to get around to playing Klonoa 2: Lunatea's Veil, but that game ended up getting pretty expensive as well and in all honesty I was sort of too lazy to set up a PS2 emulator and acquire a ROM of the second game, so it is nice to see both games being available again to a potentially much wider audience.

And as a remaster, it's solid. The game takes after the graphical fidelity of the Wii version, and although that still means some of the original art style does get lost in translation, I ultimately thought this game looked decent visually and still did an okay job of carrying over said art style of the original games even if it could've definitely been better. And as a way to play Klonoa 1 and 2, again, it's solid - controls work fine and of course the gameplay and level design is still very much there.

That being said in a similar case to Sonic Origins I sort of feel like there could've definitely been... more here, especially for the asking price of $40. Sure, $40 is still less than what you can find an original copy of either of these games for, but on the same token, all you're really getting is two fairly short (albeit really good) titles with a more HD version of Klonoa Wii's graphics applied to the second game as well - which is fine enough as a change, but doesn't come close to Crash or Spyro's visual overhaul in those respective remakes, and the compilation doesn't seem to really offer any bonus content in addition to the two games.

Another criticism I have is that they didn't bother remastering or re-recording the 'Phantomilian' voice acting of the first game and basically just took it straight from the PS1 game, bitcrunching and all. I don't know if they lost the original files for the voice acting or something, but it definitely sticks out like a sore thumb when playing the first game in this compilation combined with the remastered visuals. The least they could've done was to add an option for the Wii version's voice acting, even if it isn't really popular.

Still, it's a fine enough remaster, especially if you haven't played these games, and even if $40 is a bit steep for what you're getting, that's still less than what you would find a used copy of either game for.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving completed both games, I highly recommend this collection. It is a wonderful way to play these titles. This remaster treats both games nicely, with Klonoa 2 in particular getting the most graphical benefits. You don't realize how much work is being done until you look at side by side screenshots.

Here is a full comprehensive video about all Klonoa ports.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rqwiWyILr6s

That being said make sure to install this game on a solid-state drive (SSD) and not a usb Hard Drive, otherwise it will stutter. Also I recommend going into options and turning the tutorial off pop ups, the game flows much better without it.

Also, Klonoa 2 was originally a cell shaded game on the PS2. If you would like a celshaded look, I recommend downloading Rehsade with the comic filter.

1. Before you begin watch this video to learn how Reshade functions
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N2RosUxcqFE

2. Download Reshade from here
https://reshade.me/

3. Launch Reshade and set it to Direct X11, then install the comic filter from the included list.

4. Learn Reshade hotkey's to turn on and off filters at a moments notice. in Klonoa's case Reshade has a tendency to crash on the game selection title screen right after the Bandai Namco and Monkeycraft Logos.

Once you actually load Klonoa 1 or 2 it won't crash. Just make sure to turn off the filters before the game selection screen loads and turn them back on after Klonoa 1 or 2 has started.

5. If you need to uninstall Reshade, launch the Reshade software you downloaded again, and find the Klonoa exe again and follow the instructions Reshade provides.\t\t\t\t\t
