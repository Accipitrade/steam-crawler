\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
Lovely crafted worlds where nothing looks out of place and everything pulls you in, perfectly designed platforming gameplay and level design where every game mechanic gets its due without getting stale, immersive and very emotive score, lovable characters, surprisingly touching and meaningful story that reveals an important life lesson.

Cons:
I think the graphics should've been polished a bit more, it looks like a Noughties' game. It was a bit sad comparing the way the game looks to the way it could've looked with current technological capabilities.
The complete neglect of retouching the audio in any way really bummed me out when I first heard Ghadius' scratchy voice sounding just as awfully compressed as it did in the PSX original. Spyro and Crash showed how to do it right.
I noticed a handful of localisation issues, but the fact that I'm even bringing this up shows that I've run out of serious complaints (the combined presence of these latter two issues points towards budget constraints though - the Klonoa franchise isn't on the same level of recognition as GTA, so at least they have an excuse for a flawed remaster, and it's still a fantasticatrillion times more polished than the GTA Turdogy).

Comparison between the games:
Klonoa 1 starts off easy but the difficulty ramps up quickly towards the end. Klonoa 2 starts more challenging than 1, but seems to have a milder difficulty curve.
Klonoa 1 has more polished gameplay, whereas Klonoa 2's is more varied.
Klonoa 1's dialogue is efficient and to the point, whereas Klonoa 2 sometimes gets lost in exposition and banter - in those situations it's like an anime, but the mediocre kind that lives off of goofy clichees and fan service. I doubt the writing direction was the same. Luckily it can be sped up
In general I think Klonoa 1 is a more well-rounded and pleasing package. Klonoa 2 sometimes tries very hard to expand upon its predecessor's mechanics, and it doesn't always work too well.

All in all highly a very recommendable purchase, not the least because they're using it as a gauge for the future of the franchise. I really look forward to Klonoa 3!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic art, beautiful/catchy music, tight platforming and excellent level design - Klonoa has it all and I can highly recommend it!

As a first time player, these 2 games have been a blast to play (fresh off playing Kirby and the Crystal Shards). I love 2d and 3d platformers and these games did not disappoint - Klonoa may be simple in its premise and controls, but the platforming is so natural and the difficulty/design curve from level-to-level is smooth and progressive, with each new stage introducing harder challenges for already-learned concepts. The art is gorgeous and I love the character designs and voices. The music is seriously good and I've heard several that are definitely up there with some of my favourite VG tracks during my playtime. A 2.5d platformer that genuinely uses 3d elements for both visual pleasure and gameplay novelties that really work well.\t\t\t\t\t
