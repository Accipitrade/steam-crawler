\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust to preface, this is not a game I grew up with. This experience has me reflecting on how it should have been.

The mark of a great puzzle platformer is when knowing how to solve a puzzle often times doesn't make it any easier. It requires a lot of precise timing, but is much more forgiving than something like Cuphead. I found myself crying during the ending of what I originally thought was a very surface level story.

I have only finished the first of these two games at the time of writing this (will update after completing Lunatea's Veil), yet I'm already satisfied having spent 40$ on the first game alone. Klonoa is going to be on my radar going forward, and I recommend you put it on yours, as well.

The following passage was written directly after the completion of Lunatea's Veil. Do NOT begin playing the series through Lunatea's Veil. While not much of Door to Phantomile is in the sequel at all, the themes of the stories become stronger when experienced as a sequential duology.

From a gameplay standpoint, Lunatea's Veil is definitely the more forgiving of the two, in spite of having your 6 HP cut down to 3. Very cool puzzles were added, like the creature you make larger by feeding other enemies to it to smash a color-coded crystal, or the boss fights. I didn't mention the boss fights from Klonoa 1, because aside from Joka and the true final boss, they were a bit plain. Each boss in Klonoa 2 has two forms, which are easy enough to understand. Yet, as I said before, knowing exactly how to solve these puzzles doesn't make them any easier.

Coming out of the Klonoa Duology, I have a higher appreciation for the medium as a whole. The world wasn't ready for this series in the mid 1990s, as it was still hotly debated on whether or not video games were an art form. As a medium, video games did not get the respect they deserved from critics until Metal Gear Solid 4. (Which, admittedly, deserves most of if not all the praise it gets.)

This is an intricate, existential adventure hidden behind the wrapper of an unassuming children's platformer. I will never forget the experiences I've had in either of these worlds. I look forward to whatever may lay ahead for the series. Given Bandai Namco's promise to revive the series, and the feedback it's received, the future looks bright for the Cabbit of Everyone's Dreams. Of course, not everyone is into 2.5D Puzzle Platformers, but especially for the bargain price these games come with, definitely give it a shot if you have a chance.

10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKlonoa has finally returned from the ashes right on time for his 25th Anniversery and now has never been a better time to be a fan. It has been months since the announcement from that Nintendo Direct back in Feburary and now it's finally here~

Klonoa 1 is pretty much a glowed up remake of the Nintendo Wii remake released back in 2008 but with major improvements such as remodeling the characters to fit closer to their Ps1 counterparts, a few facial and animation fixes, and best of all, reusing the original ps1 script where in the Wii Remake, Klonoa's dialogue was pretty much hammed down to him just being silent which doesn't work for his character being a happy-go-lucky child itching for adventure.
Animation may have been slightly improved but it still uses the robotic and stiff ones from the Wii Remake sadly.
Even so, the first game still plays decently well and is pretty much an accessible way to play DtP as opposed to the Ps1 version which is insultingly expensive as well as it's Wii counterpart.

Klonoa 2 looks fantastic. This was my first actually playing the sequel so I sadly have no memories of playing the original version on Ps2. But looking at it graphically compared to the original, it stands out and looks incredibly colorful and bright, much like a lot of classic platformers of the past. Klonoa 2 is even more challenging than the first at least to me because I lost too many lives to count, but still managed to push myself to the finish line with a few left intact. Gameplay is smooth and incredibly fun to replay over and over.

Overall, this is a solid product. I know a lot of people seem to harp on it being $40 (at least here in the US) but I think it's a fair price for 2 solid remasters of an IP that needs to come back. And come back he did.

This should also be solid proof to Bandai Namco that we don't want the same generic anime fighting game released by them over and over and over again. We would like to have fresh new ideas, possible new IP's, and... maybe... just mabye... a well deserved third entry in Klonoa's future?
if kingdom hearts 3 could happen, so could this. Here us well, Bamco! Klonoa deserves a second chance!!\t\t\t\t\t

I recommend this game to keep te franchise alive, but it needs work. tl;dr; bring back ps1 cutscenes of Door to Phantomile


Oh man, I've been waiting for this game for a long time. I've played Klonoa DtP alot when i was younger and it was able to make me feel a certain type of way no other game has done. That being said, this review of both games is from a perspective of someone who has only played DtP back in the day, it could come across as negative and nitpicky, but that's becouse I do really love these games and i want it to succeed, i bought the game on the switch and pc. If I refer to the "original" game, i'm talking about the Ps1 version, not the wii version.

Door to Phantomile
Gameplaywise it's still as fun as the original version. There are a few changes compared to the original game, but other than that, faithfull to the original. Through the journey I've stumbled upon a few audio bugs, where in 1 level the volume was to low of the background music, and sometimes there were sfx sounds not playing when peforming an action. They should also fix the dialogue boxes, it takes wayyy to long per sentence.

The biggest problem I have with this remaster, is the lack of effort in the new cutscenes. The story is something that made the original game great, it needed the beautifull cutscenes and music to really deliver the story and the feel of the game, so i think the cutscenes are a BIG part of what made the game great. The cutscenes of the remaster is just, liveless and not well made, Klonoa smiling when talking when his grandpa get's murdered, the ending, everything is still green before the song of rebirth like nothing happend. and the animations were just bad and i could go on. I really hope there will be mods that could include the original cutscenes, then i'll actually will recommend it to people. otherwise they'll just get the wrong impression of what the game is in my opinion. Playing with the pixel mode on kinda fixed the bad facial expressions becouse they were harder to see. So that made it bearable. I saw there was a original sfx mod on steam aswell, will take a look at that too and play the game again.

I don't want to ♥♥♥♥ all over the game and make it look bad, so here are the possitives. I LOVE the music of the original, i really resonate with that and i'm glad they haven't changed it. I still had a ton of fun playing this game I think the worlds and characters looked good, it still had the charm the original game has imo. The ending still made me cry, probably becouse of nostalgia, but I hope it delivers to new players aswell. I like the newly added features for replayability.

Once again, it might come across as really negative, But i LOVE the game that's why i'm going hard on it. If you want it to succeed, it needs to deliver.

Lunatea's veil
I've never played this game in the past so i was really looking forward to it. I'll use DtP as a reference for this review.

I start with the negatives again.
Through the beginning of game, I didn't feel the magic the first game had. the music was less unique and it didn't feld like Klonoa music. Also the music didn't reflect the situation really, a reactor was about to blow up, and the game just played some chill jazzy music, which i think wasn't Klonoa-like at all, If you compare it to DtP that had different types of music for everything. I know this was never meant to be a DtP version 2, becouse LV takes place in a whole different world. I didn't like the dialogues and story as much as DtP's, to me it feels like this was more focussed to children, where DtP has some more serious ♥♥♥♥ going on with more scarier looking villains. I think that makes it more interessting for adults aswell.

So i thought that was it and i'll just continue to play and enjoy what the game is, but halfway through, when i arrived at Ishras ark, the feeling of DtP came back. That ark looked awesome and is exactly that style that i like about Klonoa. Everything from that point was going uphill for me. The music started to get those lovely wacky panflutes again, the levels looked more fantasy-like and i just really enjoyed playing through that. I love the new creatures and mechanics in the game ,It was fun to play through.

Conclusion (not tl;dr)
I loved playing through the series, and it made me appreciate the unique art-style even more, DtP needs some work as mentioned above. I really hope this series get the attention it deserves, I would love to see a modern Klonoa game with the technology of today. I don't know what direcion the devs would want to go. I feel like DtP is more child-ish looking, except for the villains. The story feels more grown-up.
LV Looks less child-ish except for some characters, but the dialogues feel more child-ish.

IF there is ever going to be a Klonoa 3, i hope it will have the feeling and music like DtP has, more fairytale-like and a more hard hitting story like DtP with the gameplay from LV. I'm biased becouse i've only played DtP in the past, so I understand that there are different standpoints and preferences and i respect that, please respect mine aswell.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI... love these games, ever since I played Door to Phantomile a few years back. They're some of my favourites in the entire medium of videogames, so expect this to be a fairly biased review.

I had an incredible experience playing through these games in this collection, and - whether this is an unpopular opinion or not, I'm not sure, but I really liked the new graphical improvements and additions to the visuals, especially in Klonoa 2.
The gameplay is as tight and enjoyable as ever, and every level is as memorable as the last.
The storytelling in these games is just phenomenal, and easily some of the best in it's genre. The endings for both games will tug at your heartstrings every time - though maybe for different reasons to eachother.
If you couldn't tell already... I cannot recommend this collection enough.

There are a couple gripes I have however, my main one being some of the presentation of Door to Phantomile's remaster. It combines both the additional cutscene overhauls and gameplay mechanics of the Wii remake, as well as the original Ps1 art style and aesthetics. I like this idea a lot, and it works great for the most part. However, in doing so, we did lose the incredible FMV movies for the opening and ending of the Ps1 version of Klonoa. The way they convey these scenes is perfectly fine in my honest opinion, but it's a bit of a shame to see the original FMVs excluded.
Plus the excessive bloom is... certainly a bit of an eyesore at points, but thankfully you can turn it off in the options (god knows why they had it enabled from the start though) .

These are just minor blemishes on an otherwise fantastic package though. I adored my time with this collection, and I beg that anyone interested in these games buys this, in order to show support for this little guy and the potential for a third mainline instalment.\t\t\t\t\t
