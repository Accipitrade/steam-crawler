\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI certainly recommend this game, however there are a few things worth noting before you buy:
1. These games are very short. Door to Phantomile can be completed 100% within 4 hours or so, and Lunatea's Veil can take around 7 hours to complete. They're perfect games to play through when you have an afternoon to kill, however if you're more of a "one-and-done" type of person I'd recommend waiting and getting the game when it's on sale.
2. While the games are HD remasters and have pretty decent graphics, the game audio is ripped directly from the original PS1/PS2 games, so be aware that there will be some instances of compressed sound effects and voice audio (especially with Door to Phantomile's cutscenes).
3. While I love this game, do NOT purchase the DLC. As far as I know it only includes a few bonus costumes for Klonoa to wear, plus maybe a couple extras. Whatever is included, it is certainly not worth the asking price.

That being said, Klonoa has always been a series that holds good memories from when I was a kid, and Phantasy Reverie Series is perhaps the best and easiest way to experience two of the best 2D platformers of the PS1 and early PS2. Of course, the stories of these games are the most noteworthy aspect. Very simple and straightforward, but with very charming characters and unforgettably emotional endings. I absolutely hope this game's success gives Bandai-Namco the incentive to keep Klonoa around as a series and not be left to be forgotten again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis collection is overall great. I've been a fan of Klonoa ever since I got into the series when I was a teen. Both are fun in there own ways, but there are flaws to be had. Klonoa 1 (my favorite) I still do like in terms of the gameplay, visuals and story. Thank god for the devs to use the original scripted for the game and also making characters emote more which is what made it touching. Yet looking at this and the wii version I'm more astonished with how this game looks in alot of areas. For some flaws, I'm mad that some things aren't fixed and some new problems are put in. Some of the visuals fail to do there job. Mostly in world 6 where the background is static. The model for the final boss looks like a Sonic adventure DX model and the ending is still the same as the wii. I have found issues with trying to grab enemies (This goes for both games). Not all the time, but it does mean I have to be a bit more percise. I'd also say if you're new to the game or you're not so good at platformers, take my advice and play the game on easy. The game is challenging and your result may differ. So just to save the trouble I'd say start on easy and work your way up from there (Not a flaw on the game, but something to just keep in mind).
For Klonoa 2 I can tell ALOT of work was put into the game. The visuals are over the top great and I feel are what stand out the most. Gameplay is still the same. Not too complex as Klonoa 1 is, though it can be sometimes. I'm amazed by how the time took there time to go ahead and recreated all the artwork in the gallery. An amazing step up from the original. Would say I am disappointed that Klonoa and other characters don't emote much in this game. A big downgrade considering they emoted alot in the orignal, but I'd say it's ok. Some visuals I'm not a fan of. Pacifically the fun park level. Stuff like the lighting, the sky and the mansion part look dull. Also not a game I tend to come back to much. I do love this game, but alot of levels tend to go on longer then they need too and bosses aren't as challenging as they were in one. All in all, I had alot of fun with both games and it's SO good seeing Klonoa be alive again after so many years. On top of that if this game gets alot of love we might be seeing Klonoa 3 get it's wish. So I ask you to please buy this game and save Klonoa from sudden death again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis collection has helped me to experience two great games at a somewhat fair price. As a first time player, I really liked my time with both games.

Klonoa: Door to Phantomile is a charming PS1 game. Using the Wii remake as a base, the game has been wonderfully remastered with assets and aesthetics closely matching that of the original PS1 version: a best of both worlds. Charming presentation and characters give a long way to make this game the classic it is as well as the tight platforming which can get tricky by the end of the game. Some tearjerking moments are to be expected when divulging in with the story. You might not be ready for that ending.

Klonoa 2: Lunatea's Veil is, in my opinion, a more polished sequel to the first game. Dropping into a new world with new themes gives this game it's own boost of identity, despite the gameplay remaining largely similar. Some neat gimmicks like a board are a welcome addition to the gameplay as well. The story this time can feel more thought-provoking and meaningful, which I think some people could really relate to. Presentation in this game is top-notch otherwise. The removal of the original iteration's cel-shading might take away from its personality, but this new look to match with the first game suits it pretty well.

Overall, my experience with these versions of the game have been flawless with minimal to no bugs during my playthroughs of each game. As a first time player, it was a joy to experience these classic games for myself and see what all the hype was about. I cannot recommend this collection enough, just for the fact that it might potentially spark interest for more games in the Klonoa series in the future. Heck, I'd go as far as saying that Klonoa absolutely deserves a third mainline game. But please, enjoy these classics here in their near definitive forms.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to play the second game for years, but discovered that PS2 emulation sucks. It sucks in general and for this game it literally didn't work for decades.

And finally this year they've released both games on PC. This is a very good package. It features both games and adds an easy difficulty. It removes the lives system which was a terrible coin-munching idea to begin with. Also it increases the range of the attack from point blank to almost point blank. Doesn't improve attack angles, though.
The first game uses sprites with few frames of animation. The second game uses cell-shading. This release unifies the art-style but it has a generic 3D render. Some of the personality is lost but that's a justified sacrifice to have both games natively run at modern resolutions and modern aspect ratios with proper graphics.
Klonoa's ears are connected in an awkward way. His textures are tiled so he looks a bit weird. And some of the background textures are a bit weak. The second ark was of noticeably lower resolution.
Loading times are weird. The games run fine but sometimes it takes a lot of time to load into one or the other game from the main menu.
The cutscenes can be sped up or skipped, which is a very good addition especially for the sequel.

A good platformer should be so tight that it feels more like a rhythm game. Being a 2.5D game but with 3D levels there are weird alignments, wonky hitboxes and no way to aim. In this game the tolerances are so loose that you constantly overshoot every target. (There are two places in the first game where the placement makes it a 95% chance of undershoot. But those are static and somewhat safe.) The game asks you to underperform instead of giving it your all. Which is not the most pleasant feeling.
Playing both games in this package or the original was fine in the story mode. It's very simple and it rarely asks of you to perform a long chain of actions with accumulation of tolerances. But there are three bonus levels which do exactly that. And all three are required for the achievements.

They are a damn nightmare and the first game's bonus is a ten times nestled nightmare. They require more than the absolute perfect timing and you have to work against the wonky acceleration arks. The level design amplifies every slight miscalculation. And in the first game the level still spend your lives. If i went for it on the original hardware even after accumulating the maximum allowed number of lives i would still run out. Two of the rooms are absolutely insane. I had to switch to keyboard controls fro the Chamber o'Horrors and because i smashed my thumb for it i had to learn how to play with a mouse to finish EXTRA VISION. To prolong the torture there is no instant respawn/reset and every failed attempt has a loading time to it.

Well, at least all of the achievements are in working order. Nothing exciting — the typical story progress trackers and collectibles trackers. Good thing there are no achievements for the stupid gems.

風のクロノア2 〜世界が望んだ忘れもの〜 [Kaze no Kuronoa 2 〜sekai ga nozonda wasuremono〜]

So i thought that the first game is inferior because it lacks puzzles. But it turned out that in the sequel they are barely present. The first one appears past the middle of the game and in total there are probably less than ten of them. Even the double armour is used only in one level.

I liked the first game but this doesn't do it for me. It still has Klonoa's own set of mechanics, the sound is great and the designs are cool. But the soundtrack has no memorable tunes outside of one track. The character was redesigned and instead of looking absolutely great now it looks like a sanik OC. Ghadius is a great character; the sequel instead has some weeaboo drama set in a jumbled world. The cutscenes are extremely long and slow and aren't entertaining much. The levels are very linear and the spring-jumping and the snowboarding ones are kinda bad. Several levels are outright recycled.
This game turned out to be just a diet version of more of the same. It's enjoyable but not great.

So Klonoa traveled to a new world. But it still has moos, and everyone knows about the ring and how to use it. The gems are the same too.
Instead of a straight plot of the first game the sequel offers a convoluted nonsense. Sorrow is too hard to handle for them, but the eternal-civil-war-kingdom is just right? The game is referring to some things which are never explained or appear again. Who's Claire? Who's Lunatea? It's all stupid weeaboo drama about nothing.

The game is very simple (and easier than the first one) but some levels are a nightmare. Without the infinite lives they would be very irritating. The level design is just not very good, especially when hunting for the bells. And if you even think about collecting all 150 gems that already is unsettling every time you see a gem. Without quicksaves or even an ability to restart at the checkpoint, even with infinite lives it would be a torture. The game is made of sawtooths and stupid sliding / riding levels. There are many timed sequences that are already tight, and each one of them have gems in difficult to access areas, while the walls of death never contemplate slowing down. Or even allowing to be outrun. They reset the distance when you get to far. It's not about just collecting them either, every x2 bonus have to be executed perfectly with sub-second tolerances while all the hitboxes and alignments are still whack. And in the second game it doesn't even re-colour the gems. The GUI doesn't even track the collected gems on the level, so you have to manually count them.

The second game is quite good but i don't feel greatness from it. Without the collection of gems with quicksaves, it feels like i do not engage with content. It can't compete with the enjoyment of re-Spyro 2 and 3. But also being less tight than Crash 4 it doesn't feel as fulfilling. I waited for it for about 20 years and it is expensive so i expected more from it.

風のクロノア door to phantomile [Kaze no Kuronoa]

The re-made version loses in several areas — new Ghadius is weak and generic; the re-made music is not as impactful and sounds blurred; there's lens dirt retarded effect; the map screen is weaker. The original looks more cartoonier and uses a more aggressive and fun palette. But with the new engine they have close-ups on Klonoa during the cut-scenes, highlighting his top notch original design. Even if with the weird ears and tiling textures. But all cutscenes needed another QA pass on clipping solids — these are very noticeable and very distracting.
The voices are kinda quiet too, even with everything else being 35% quieter the are still sometimes drown in music.
The new version is undeniably a better game better technology, modern render better readability and light GUI. But there is less greatness and character in it.

So replaying the first game that i considered to be really great after finishing the sequel, it's easier to see the difference. The variety of mobs is bigger. The gameplay is snappier and less slow. The complexity is much higher and the layouts are more enjoyable. At times this feels like a high-budget version of Nebulus. There are more voiced character and the voices are the best feature of this franchise.

The first game is hard as all ass. I thought that having that much HP was excessive on easy difficulty in the sequel, but here it was just right. With loose mechanics and weird hitboxes this was the amount of HP needed for the boss-fights. And the Extra Vision. I died on every phase of the last boss and died on the boss on the swing even with the boosted HP.

The core of the story is simple enough — go defeat the bad guy. But the ending is weird forced drama and it creates and envelope of weirdness and conspiracies that make you question what the hell was even happening in the game.\t\t\t\t\t
