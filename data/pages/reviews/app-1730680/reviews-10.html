\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember playing, but never beating Klonoa 2 on my PS2 as a child. I happened upon it and embarked on my redemption ark. After playing and beating both games, I must say it was great fun. I beat both games in 8 hours, but there is still a lot of things to do if going for full completion. I do not generally like platfromers as a genre, but I found a lot of the mechanics neat, and the level design is usually quite good. The graphics are dated but fun and colorful.

Only complaint is sections of levels seem like they are purposefully set up to be annoying, and some of the later levels can be stingy with checkpoints. I played on easy, as you get infinite lives and reduced damage modifier. Health was never really an issue but the I could see lives becoming in short supply with the amount of fall deaths I had. Worth checking out on a sale, especially if you are fan of platformers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you don't mind a bit of port/remaster jank/inconsistencies, and like a good 2.5D platformer, and have never tried Klonoa before, absolutely get this.

I'm only 20 something minutes into each game respectively, and I can already tell I mostly prefer the originals, but the remasters are still pretty decent. You can do A LOT worse in terms of remasters. Hell, even the file size is decent. Klonoa Wii + Klonoa 2 is about 3.1 GB combined. This is about 8GB, what with enhanced textures and all, not too bloated.

Klonoa 1 remaster is basically a modified version of the Wii remake from 2008, swapped Klonoa's model for a more faithful recreation, yeeted the English voiceovers in favor of the much better, although very pixely PS1 voiceovers, and then enhanced them textures and other misc stuff. Klonoa 2 remaster is just a remaster of the OG PS2 version.

Make sure to turn the tutorials OFF before playing each game, or you will get annoyed quickly. I'm also not a fan of that visual novel skip/fastforward button prompt that's permanently welded to the screen with each cutscene, how about GIVING THE OPTION TO TURN THAT OFF?!

Klonoa veteran here... well, sort of. Played the Wii remake in 2017, liked it, then played the rest of the series, including the PS1 original, and fell in love with the series. Then eventually played the fan translated Japanese exclusive games, also good.

The remasters certainly look better than the originals... in certain areas. Then sometimes the characters are in cl_fullbright 1 mode and aren't lit correctly... like at all.

Yo Namco, surely you'll port the rest of the games, yeah? Moonlight Museum, Empire of Dreams, Dream Champ Tournament, Heroes, and Beach Volleyball (Yes, really.) As for the Japanese exclusive games (Moonlight Museum and Heroes) Just work with the fan translators, the translations are FANTASTIC. All you have to do, translation wise, is proofread and make small corrections. Then port all them games (or just stick them in an official emulator, I don't really care, just run well and at least mostly bug free)

Then maybe after that, you could do a Moonlight Museum remake for the 3DS- oh wait missed your chance on a lowish budget MM 3DS remake, woops. I mean you could still make a similar budget remake for whatever system, add a bunch of new elements, and powers, and stuff... and BOSSES. It'd be cool.

While you're at it, put Klonoa in Smash- OH WAIT, YOU BLEW THAT TOO! WHEN YOU WERE WORKING ON SMASH 4! You couldn't convince Sakurai to move 1 of his twelve thousand Fire Emblem sword fighters aside? Seriously.

Anyway... Wahoo, Wahoo!

Fish Tacos.
