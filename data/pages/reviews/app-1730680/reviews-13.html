\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo start with, I do recommend everyone get this, since the series deserves all the love and attention it can get. However, there's some huge caveats.

Put simply: This is NOT a good remaster.

On the Door to Phantomile side:
The voice quality is horrible, since they used the compressed PS1 audio instead of sticking to the Wiimake script or getting the voice actors back for a clean recording. There's also visual errors such as Nahatomb's body looking like they forgot to smooth the normals, and visible seams in some models. Reverse mode is also absent, as are all the Wiimake alternate outfits.

On Lunatea's Veil:
Shadows are missing in parts, and there's this odd clockwork-esque sound that happens after inflating enemies that wasn't there in the original. Overall though, it's clear this side of the collection had the most attention paid to it.

I will say, I don't blame the developers at Monkeycraft; it's clear they did their best, and it definitely seems like this was a case of them not being given enough time/budget by Bandai Namco. As of this writing, there have been no patches or updates to the game, which is sad since many issues would be simple to fix. But again, this is clearly the fault of the publisher; they've treated this series horribly since the very start, and this is no exception.

However, I still encourage as many as people to get this, especially if you've never experienced these games before. I just believe this deserved to be so much more than what we were given.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWahoo! If you didn't play these games before, you are missing out!

Wonderful to see both of these games come to Steam! I played some of the Phantomile about 6 months ago, and completed Lunatea as a kid. It was nice to play these both again, but in a HD version so to speak.

Contrary to some, I enjoyed having the original audio in both of these, regardless of audio quality.
I noticed some issues during both of these games, such as the english language having differences in wording of sentences compared to their original counterparts. Also music in cutscenes, where it stopped in the original counterparts, kept playing on this version. This release does feel somewhat lacking in polish to me (like this was heavily rushed), but it is still welcoming to see it here. I do hope that more releases of this series are to come, though with a little more polish! :)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally deeply love the PS1 original, and this is a better remake than the Wii version (though I'd still argue that the PS1 original is still marginally superior, mainly due to better story presentation). I've never played the original version of Klonoa 2, and I know that some of the more subtle facial animations are improperly interpolated, which apparently there's a mod to fix, but I think that this is a perfectly fun way to play the second game. I think that Klonoa 1 is one of the cleanest, most concise and confident platformers of its era, and is a must play. I think Klonoa 2 is also phenomenal, but its levels tend to go on for just a bit too long for my tastes, which keeps it from reaching the heights of the first.

Either way, I don't think that 40 dollars is too steep a price, especially if you're the kind of person to replay games like I am. Largely competent PC port of an all around good remaster effort. If you're hesitant, then definitely wait for a sale, but I think this is a must own for any fan of 2D platformers, especially for people who enjoy a more involved narrative in their games.

Also Klonoa's design is GOAT'ed, especially in Klonoa 1.\t\t\t\t\t
