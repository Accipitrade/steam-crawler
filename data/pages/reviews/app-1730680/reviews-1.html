\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty good remasters/remakes. Perfectly suitable for a first playthrough although I enjoy the art direction of the originals much more personally, but thats a preference thing. I had one really weird/frustrating bug/moment of jank in the first game but other than that it was a smooth experience.

As for the games themselves theyre incredible. They got surprisingly effective stories considering both the timeframe they came out and platformers being a genre thats notably often lacking in such department. Klonoa 2 particularly is a very strong stand out in that regard.

The mechanics themselves are very enjoyable but nothing super standout, you'll mostly be doing a lot of puzzle solving and tricky platforming using the main gimmick of grabbing and using your enemies in a variety of ways. The level design is very solid as well, with collectibles that aren't annoying in the slightest to collect with good completion awards for doing so. You get a bonus level (or two bonus levels in the case of the sequel) and theyre some of the most fun and challenging levels in the games so i highly recommend doing so.

These are definitely classics and if you haven't played them before, give them a shot!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst I'd like to say I didn't play the original games on PS1 and 2. If you feel like that should invalidate my review go ahead and hit that thumbs down, steam wouldn't put that there if they didn't want you to use it.

Klonoa is cute and colorful although it does that current trend of having whiplash from the dull late 2000s era of gaming where now where the colors are too white for both games. Stages are challenging but not to the point where you're grinding for 30 minutes beat or collect everything. The soundtrack feels like one of those dreams where everything is pristine and calm but still mysterious. The gameplay is unique for a 3D 2D platformer. I like how enemies have different contextual meanings to the player. One could be helicopter, the other times you have to precisely chain together jumps with enemies to access certain parts of the level. Despite levels also being "course clear" objectives many of them are nonlinear in design and are quite fun to navigate. It's got that vibe that kirby has with it's atmosphere and character designs. I don't want to say that it's "so dark guys lol" but it's got a neat story for both games that lightly messes with you. I 100% achievement it and as you can tell by my hours it's quite a short amount of time to do that.

I played it all on my Steam Deck and it runs flawlessly if you're wondering. I had a certified "WAHOO!" moment with this game!\t\t\t\t\t
