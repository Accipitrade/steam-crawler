\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo as far as being remakes; they're pretty alright. Visually, they definitely upped the graphics. ESPECIALLY in Klonoa 2 (Klonoa 1 feels a bit weaker compared to the Wii-Remake, but still solid nonetheless).
But there is some issues; Some audio bugs with specific (normally louder) SFX not playing properly, or visual bugs with animations not working properly, but relatively minor issues in most cases.


As far as individual games are concerned; Some of the best Puzzle Platformers (though I use this term VERY loosely) in my experience.
Klonoa 1 definitely defines the formula, both with a story that is easy to follow, yet hits some fairly heavy themes later on. Great levels that make great use of the "pick up and throw" mechanics, and level design that intuitively guides you on how you can fully use these to equip you with the tools for later.

Klonoa 2 improves on it with both new level styles, more options, and much more vibrant level design, without relying on some of the labrynthian design Klonoa 1 used for secrets and such (side routes for goodies in levels are a lot easier to tell). The story is definitely a lot heavier, than the first one, but it plays off of the first one regardless (It also tells some life lessons that I think many people could stand to learn; Everybody will have some kind of grief, but it's okay to accept it, and understand it's part of your life. Don't try to ignore it or hate it, simply... be okay with crying. ).

Visually, both games are a treat for the senses. They are very beautiful worlds with unique scenery (especially the second one) that really drive the "Unusual and unnatural" world vibe, without relying on some tried tropes such as simply using weird buildings.

Audio; The music is fantastic, and the dialogue being in their own "Phantomillian" language really adds a nice layer of whimsy to the game.

Challenge-wise, aside from devious bonus levels and 100% completion (and not even for achievements; I mean the TRUE 100% of getting all gemstones in all level), the games are fairly easy. You might take a few tries but they were made on the more forgiving side.


Overall, it's easy for me to say Klonoa 2 specifically is one of if not my single all-time favourite games, and you get the first game alongside it which is a really good title in its own right. A VERY strong recommendation from me for those who haven't played it, and even for those who have and want to revisit these phantasmical lands.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI couldn't tell you how happy I was when I saw they were making a remaster of the first two games-- I very nearly cried. I grew up with Klonoa, though the second game was actually my introduction to the series so I ended up playing them in reverse! I have such fond memories, and have been looking everywhere to possibly own the game again at a price that was affordable, so this bundle gave me everything I could have hoped for and more. Thank you, Bandai Namco.

The games hold up just as well as they ever did, the platforming still being magnificent and the controls tight and well done. Many of the mistakes made as I played were often player-side, as I'm awful at platformers, but the difficulty was in that perfect sweet spot to make things feel rewarding once accomplished.

Downside so far would be some of the animations and shaders, that I've seen some others mention. It gets the job done and compared to what I grew up with, it looks fantastic. But with the popularity Klonoa has finally received, I really hope that Bandai Namco will look at this series and give it the proper, polished look it rightly deserves.

Give this game a chance!! Don't let Klonoa flop!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t...I hate having to write reviews like this...Minor spoilers I guess. This review is purely geared towards Klona: Door to Phantomile (1).

---
TLDR: If you want to support Klonoa and buy this game, great. But if you want to see why Klonoa is such a beloved and fun game, just play the original for PS1. I'm giving this a thumbs up just so people don't think this is a bad game. It's just a bad VERSION of the game. I used to play this, Tomba 2, and Jumping Flash 2 almost nonstop as a kid, so this review is probably biased af. But that's just how I see things.
---

Graphically speaking I feel like a lot of the charm of the original game was stripped. I noticed certain boss animations were completely gutted for reasons I'm not sure, enemies just look different or were changed completely (the rat for example), level designs almost look flatter than they did on the PS1 which is odd so that whole 3D effect goes right out the window, and things like dust falling down from the platforms in 6-2 were missing as well. The missing loading animations between boss fights was also a sad loss.

Sound wise, I hate it. I love that they used the original sound file for dialogue and such, it was perfect then and it's perfect now. But my god, for the stuff they decided to update they sure did butcher the ever living life out of it. It just doesn't have any life or character, stale static. Bosses seemed less interesting because the original sound effects apparently no longer met their standards. What happened to that nice crisp ring after you defeated one of the bosses? Nah, just silence now. A good example is the King Seadoph fight. It just sounds and feels so...empty. Speaking of which, why did they change the layout of the boss arena itself? Was that gap in the middle really considered too difficult? Just, what?

I feel like this is more of a revision than it is a remake. This isn't the same game I played when I was a kid. Some levels are WAY different and have been dumbed down, the constant barrage of tutorial boxes is ridiculous, if you lose a boss fight ONCE they just flat out tell you what to do. Much like the new oddworld, crash, spyro, I quickly learned that "remake" just means "scambait". Klonoa had its time in the sun, and if this is what they consider a fleshed out Klonoa 2022 game should look like, I'd rather it live on in history via the PS1.

Not even gonna touch on the "DLC"...just...

RIP Klonoa, sorry they old yeller'd you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a pretty good compilation overall. there's a few issues. Door to Phantomile kind of got the worst treatment, since it's based on the Wii Remake with some new visuals to be more like the ps1 original, and it doesn't really work sometimes, Klonoa's new model still uses the old Wii model animation rig so his arms constantly clip through his collor and it doesn't look great, the model is also a bit lifeless in some instances. The Bloom makes the game an eye Sore so i'd suggest turning it off. The colors are also kind of to saturated. There's also a lack of the mirror mode in Door to Phamtomile, but no one will really miss that. The audio is also a mixed bag for Door to Phantomile. The cutscene audio mostly uses the og ps1 recording which sound like crap and are not consistent with the fact that they use SOME Wii audio. the music is also compressed, i believe it's in Mono. Klonoa 2: Lunetea's Veil on the other hand was definitly given the better treatment. Only because it only needed higher quality models and cleaner texture work, the one downside is the lighting isn't as good as the ps2 original. But overall i must say... $40 is an absolute steal to get these hidden gems so that you don't have to pay $700 for the ps1 version of Door to Phantomile. or over $70 for the Wii remake or Klonoa 2 alone. IF this sells well enough, there is potential that Namco could see that there IS interest in this series. These games are very near and dear to many people's hearts, including mine. is I highly recommend it, despite it's flaws.\t\t\t\t\t

The story of Door to Phantomile and Lunatea's Veil are what really put the icing on the cake for the two games. Door to Phantomile presents itself as a very happy-go-lucky platformer, where you must save the world from 'darkness'. We learn later that the game is much more than this. In Lunatea's Veil, Klonoa is more aware of himself and what he must do to help his friends. The second game hints at what our hero must do to rescue the world, and this task goes far beyond the, 'save the world by defeating the evil' trope.

I highly recommend Klonoa Phantasy Reverie Series, especially if you are someone that wants a fine introduction to the Klonoa games.\t\t\t\t\t
