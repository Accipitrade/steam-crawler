\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been a hot minute since Klonoa has had... well, anything. We're talking the last entry being a Wii remake of the first game in 2009. Here we are again with both the first and second games remastered for modern consoles!

Having played the originals repeatedly, I am a bit familiar with what was changed or done differently. This remaster seems to have overall weaker animations for cutscenes, and some scenes appear to be quite different like the first scene with Grandpa which now takes place outside instead of indoors like with the Wiimake. There also appears to be some minor audio balancing issues, and Balue appears to be less hilariously loud. While this can be a bummer to some, I find that some of the more positive aspects this remaster bundle brings to be nice. For one, Klonoa 2 is finally in more hands, which seemed like it'd be impossible. Secondly, Door to Phantomile now has level select from the beginning, no longer do you need to beat the game to get that. The levels are faithful to the original, with only minor changes like preventing the camera from drunkenly swinging around. Additionally, Klonoa no longer slides to a standstill in Door to Phantomile leading to a more responsive feeling flow to his movement.

Overall, I'd say the remaster is fine. It's not a *perfect* one, but I'm overall just happy to have the boy back. I do kinda hope they update the cutscene animations at some point, however, as some do not capture the mood nearly as well as the originals.\t\t\t\t\t
