\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is based off Klonoa 1 so far

The Good:

- Environments look spectacular

- A lot of the character models fixed from the ugly Wii designs

-If you die after rescuing a villager, they'll still be rescued when you respawn

The Bad:

- Wind Bullet has extremely short range from what I remember and sometimes simply doesn't hit/grab things even if you're right on top of them. Also if you're on a slope it hits the ground as opposed to the enemy literally 2 inches from you.

- The double-jump is a little inconsistent sometimes

- The knockback from enemies is way too much. In the original game you'd go back the length of made 1/2-2/3 of Klonoa's width at most. Here, you go ROCKETING back sometimes to like 2-3 TIMES his width leaving you to either miss a platform or fall into pits

- Inconsistent dubbing: Most lines are clearly ripped straight from the PS1 game without so much as a decompression or touching up (I'm not an audio engineer so I'm not sure if there was much that could be done about it) whiile other lines are re-dubbed by the new voice actors. Why not just redub the whole thing instead of this weird halfway point? I'd gladly take a full redubbing in Phantomilan to fit the more modern graphics than hearing crunchy PS1 voices coming out of HD character models.

- Unlike the PS1 game where cutscenes were like a visual novel where you push a button to go to the next line of dialogue, here, they're on a sort of auto-pilot leaving a LOT of pausing in between nearly every single line of dialogue. This is where, again, a full redub would have beneffited them as recycling the old PS1 lines while using the Wiis timing for the cutscenes leaves empty space as one was recorded with the VN style word progression in mind while the other was animated with an entirely different dub in mind

- Klonoa's facial animation in a certain scene is utterly dissonant from the mood. He's supposed to be super sad but when not talking he merely looks tired and when he is talking....HE SMILES!!! Sure an argument could be made that he's trying to force a smile but in the context of the scene, he's at his lowest point in the story so far. He shouldn't be smiling!

Other minor nitpicks:

-Sometime sounds like boss grunts and bubbles popping don't play

-Joka and Ghadius don't taunt you when you get hurt in their fights which takes a bit of the charm away

Overall while it is dodgy in some ways, it's still one of of not my favorite platformers ever and I'm so excited to have it back again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe following is only a criticism of the first Klonoa game from this pack and do note that despite everything I will be saying, the game is still fantastic and worth buying to support the franchise.



The shading looks good at places but most of the time the game actually looks worse than the Wii remake as well as models (worlds and enemies) just not featuring basic shading at times (it feels like I'm watching them trough a model viewer).

I've also noticed performance issues even on my 3050RTX I7 PC where some cutscenes would break and make the game run at 25fps and had to restart my whole computer.
(I don't know if it's the game or my graphics card crashing on me so that's up for debate, but this is the first time I ever see something like this from every game I ever played on it.)

And speaking of cutscenes the use of the original compressed audio is very jarring to the point where there's some scene you can't take seriously because the actors sounds like they're from a call of duty lobby while all FX and music has perfect quality.
I mostly think of grandpa's death cutscene where I actually laughed because of how comedic the timing is).

I also think they straight up re-used models from the Wii remake, you can notice it because the model is not adapted for the shading. Ex : Nahatomb

This is overall very disappointing that I wish I was playing the Wii remake rather than this because this should have been the superior version.

If it wasn't for the fact that Klonoa 1 is just an amazing game overall I would have been much harsher than that.

I don't know if this will carry on to Klonoa 2, but if it does I'd also be disappointed, but probably less too since there's really nothing to compare it to.

Praying for mods to pop up for this game and fix these issues. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as remasters go, the changes overall are few and average with nothing too drastic. It might lack additions wanted by old fans and new players alike, but it also avoids major upsets and most complaints will probably be nitpicks. The core gameplay remains intact and faithful, so your version of preference in original vs remaster might largely come down to nitpicks in aesthetic and atmosphere being that the biggest changes are in the visual style and there isn't much you can do you change how the game looks or sounds.

While these ports themselves seem to have done the bare minimum in bringing these games to the modern age, these are still fantastic games regardless. The gameplay is comfy and relaxed compared to most 2D platformers, but the mechanics themselves fall into the "easy to learn, hard to master" category with how creative some of the platforming, puzzles, and exploration becomes if you decide to go for 100%. I'd probably compare them to Kirby 64.

A good experience, even if I still prefer the ethereal and dreamlike charm of the spritework in the PS1 original. But, I also enjoy the new lighting and colors in Klonoa 2's remaster more than it's PS2 counterpart. These are by no means bad ways to experience these games, but there is still room for more potential in this series. More quality to this remaster and support overall would be appreciated considering how long this series has been on hiatus. My only real gripe with this remaster is some of cutscenes feel a bit stiff and could use more polish to bring it up to modern standards.\t\t\t\t\t
