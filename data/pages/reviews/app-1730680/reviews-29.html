\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot even through Klonoa 1: Door to Phantomile.
And yeah, the price is steep. I consider it quite much for what you get.
Basically a port with redone graphics of a game that's 25 years old.

But, I'll still recommend it as it is one of my favorite games.
The design is just adorable, while the story is quite grim at times. It's a touching story though, very akin to fairytales.
The gameplay seems unchanged, the feel of running and jumping is still there, so that's a huge plus.
Namco of the nineties had tight controls to offer, and this one does as well.
There aren't many 3D jump'n'runs that actually work in 2D (think Pandemonium) and Klonoa does it really well.
The soundtrack is one of the finest game soundtracks that make you groove to each level.
The bosses are fun, later on quite hard (although the first 4 got nerfed quite a bit, which is hard to understand as there is an easy mode as well).
Some stages are harder later on, specially with timing of jumps, monster throwing and switch throwing. Dunno why they lowered the difficulty of the bosses while keeping the ruthless timing on some puzzles. A little bit of leniency wouldn't have been that bad, I think. On second thought: The harsh timing is needed to force you to come to the solution that works. So some leniency would defeat the purpose of the puzzle. Not sure if it's different on easy mode.

A word on easy mode: The description is done well on the changes and there your wind blast has a higher range, so it's making the gameplay easier too. If one is looking for just a fun thing for a child, that might be a good thing.

Overall though I will recommend it as my favorite jump'n'run even if it seems overpriced for what it is. 25eur each game is quite something. Totally worth it for me as it brings back the best game of the genre and glorious music and such a loveable design. After some more time with it I can see that there was much care for the original and much effort to make a really good game. I think they succeeded.\t\t\t\t\t

2 utterly fantastic puzzle platformers, given new life

KPRS is a beautiful remaster of two of my favorite platform games ever. They sport gameplay that rewards both tight reflexes and smart thinking, wonderful music, and surprisingly interesting storylines, now with a fresh coat of paint. All of the content in the original versions is here and accounted for, and they've been given new difficulty options for accessibility.

* Door to Phantomile is unfortunately marred with a quite a few presentational and sound problems, but the gameplay itself is still quite solid despite a few changes in the physics.
* Lunatea's Veil, a game stuck on the PS2 for over 20 years, is the crowning piece of the collection, playing every bit as well as the original, but retaining the same style of cutscenes and a myriad of small details that veterans will appreciate. I still have a few nitpicks, but I'd say KPRS is worth getting just for this one game.

It is unfortunate that games like these were overlooked for so long, but they've been given a second chance to shine for a reason. If they seem interesting, please give them a shot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe two games that defined one of Namco's most overlooked franchises.

Klonoa is a 2.5D platformer where you can capture enemies and throw them as a projectile in five possible directions : down (for a double jump), left, right, front or back. The fact that you can interact with the foreground or background was innovative when the first game came out, and even today, it's still an unique way to handle combat and puzzle in a side-scroller.

Apart from the gameplay, the games are known for their colorful aesthetic, memorable soundtrack, lovable protagonist, imaginative levels, fun bosses and bittersweet stories. They're a bit short and easy, especially the first one, but I prefer when games don't overstay their welcome. Both games are among my favorite plateformers of their era and I can't recommend them enough.

To elaborate about this specific version:

- Door to Phantomile is very similar to the Wii remake in terms of visuals, but the more contested changes are either optional (like the easier difficulty) or absent (like the redesign and the redub). I still think the PS1 classic had more charm, but that one is hard to find nowadays. This new version looks nice, plays well, is faithful to the original and is the most convenient to play.

- Lunatea's Veil is pretty much the PS2 classic with cleaner graphics, a smoother frame rate and faster load times. I have no complaints whatsoever, this is my new preferred way to play this game.

All in all, this is a great package and I'm happy these two games are finally available on PC and modern consoles.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh where do I begin.
Those of you who grew up in the 90's might remember this adorable cutesy game on Playstation called "Klonoa: Door to Phantomile." A revolutionary 2.5D platformer that punches you in the gut with it's story. Bandai Namco trademarked this back in 2020 under the name "Klonoa 1+2 Encore" and then in 2022 they unveiled the official title after some trademarks showed up in December 2021 under the name "Klonoa : Phantasy Reverie Series" which of course is the final name of this package. If you have any desire to relive or experience a true platformer that has an enriching story and endearing characters and development, sit back as I review, Klonoa : Phantasy Reverie Series!

What is Klonoa?
Klonoa is a cat-rabbit hybrid or a cabbit if you prefer, he has huge ears that allow him to hover briefly for a moment, he has one source of attack, using the spirit in the ring to fire wind bullets that when impacting with enemies cause them to inflate, this can then be used as a projectile but also as a means of utilising Klonoa's strongest feat, his double jump.
You'll come to realize how useful the double jump is by the time you get to Vision 3-2.

This is a game that combines two of Klonoa's previous escapades into a neat little nostalgic package.
Klonoa : Phantasy Reverie Series is a revitilisation of the I.P. by remastering the first two games in his series, the first being "Klonoa: Door to Phantomile" and the second being "Klonoa 2 : Lunatea's Veil".

Klonoa is a dream traveller, and I mean he travels to different dimensions to solve their problems before eventually returning to his own dimension from which he came. And if you're like me, you probably bawled your eyes out at the ending of the first game or even the second game.
He has a friend called Huepow in the first game and they bond more and more over their adventure as they take off in the land of Phantomile to stop an evil sorcerer known as Ghadius from plunging the world into a never ending nightmare, to do this he plots against the fabled moon kingdom only heard of in legends so people didn't know for sure if it existed and kidnapped the diva Lephise who is the only one who can restore a dying world with her beautiful yet sombre "Song of Rebirth". You'll utilise all of Klonoa's trademark moves as you traverse gorgeous environments, from windy green hills to a literal temple in the sky filled with lava traps and loads of hazards that want you dead. The game clocks in at about 3-4 hours if you go through in one run and collect everything, going for completion may take longer as you need to collect 150 dream stones in each level and free each of the 6 phantomillians trapped in each stage referred to as Visions when you keep to the dream aspect.

Klonoa 2 has a much more driven narrative as it has much more diverse characters and not one but THREE main characters who accompany you, Lolo a priestess in training wants to save the world with her courage and desire for a better world, but she can come across as cumbersome as she's quite naive which you'll see in the game as the story unfolds.
Popka which I have no idea what he actually is but he is quite quirky as he will not hesitate to take a stab at Klonoa when he fails a simple task. Speaking of Klonoa, he got a redesign in this game, sporting all blue outfit, his cap is now reversed so the front is at the back and the back is at the front, like a baseball cap, and he sports some vibrant Red and White shoes similar in design to Sonic The Hedgehog which in turn is why his design was changed, to look cooler.
The villains this time.....well....that's the thing, there isn't really a main villain as Leorina and her subordinate known as Tat who is a white and black cat who can split into two halves that act independently but share the same mind so they are never unaware of their goal as you pursue them during play.
Your main task here is to ring the bells of harmony and acquire the necessary power to traverse to the ruined kingdom of sorrow and "Save" the King of Sorrow. This game has another gut punching ending that I won't spoil so please experience it for yourself!

Can I recommend this game?
Absolutely, this game series has lay dormant for 14 years since the last game released which was Klonoa on the Nintendo Wii. Bandai Namco express interest in this series once again, please support this series and buy "Klonoa: Phantasy Reverie Series" today and show them that we want more Klonoa!

Thank you for reading!
