\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as I love and recommend this collection, I think you should wait until it goes on sale, unless you're already a die hard fan of this series and don't care. It's absolutely not a bad remaster but it does feel kinda cheap in places, and Â£40 is pretty pricey for what it is IMO.

Anyways, it's pretty damn good. I'm not a massive platformer guy but Klonoa has very quickly become one of my all time favorites. Gameplay, writing, music, environments, it's all great. If you're interested then just go for it, and go in blind. It's a simple game with simple abilities, but the complexity comes from level design and enemy placement, forcing you to gradually master Klonoa's moves. It's certainly not among the hardest platformers of course, but you will still be challenged in fun ways.

I can't really talk about what I love without spoiling the game (also I'd need a full damn essay) so I'll just list a couple of things I think this remaster messed up.

My main issues really just boil down to aspects of the game being serviceable, but they should have been better. Largely the visuals. Textures are kinda meh in many places, and I feel like more could have been done with the cutscenes. Character animations are just straight up reused in Lunatea's Veil, which isn't a terrible thing, but imagine how cool it would have been to have a truly modern remaster of the same game instead of just reusing stuff from the PS2. Animations in general are just kinda low effort here. More care in these places would really help to justify the price. I understand not wanting to go all out on a game franchise that historically has been a financial failure, but if you're not going the extra mile then drop the price a little.

Gameplay wise, there's just a few minor bugs I ran into. Collision near the edge of platforms feels pretty jank, and I had a weird collision bug that completely blocked me from completing Door to Phantomile's Extra Vision, I couldn't go through an open passageway on flat ground. No idea how they messed that up but OK.

Overall, I feel like this remaster is lacking a lot of polish and love. As cool as it is to see a beloved but dead franchise come back, I do wish they put in more effort to actually make the game feel like it was actually made in 2022 by a Triple-A company. Like I said before, it just feels kinda cheap in many places, and part of me can't shake the feeling that this is nothing more than a quick cash grab, with no real intentions of showing Klonoa any real care. I really hope I'm wrong.

Regardless of the issues, it's a solid remaster of some pretty old games. I'm glad I played them and I'm sure I'll replay them again and again in the future. This collection is a great way for newcomers to play these games on a wide range of modern platforms, without having to spend stupid amounts of cash on original copies and hardware, or just pirating them. I just wish they did a little more. Or brought the price down.

Edit time: I found out what caused that weird collision bug in DtP's Extra Vision. The collision in some areas bug out if your FPS is too high. Enabling VSync fixed it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter completing Klonoa 1 and 2 on Easy and then Normal, I finally have the words to give the game an overall rating:
5/5
Give Bandai Namco your money, buy the damn game, you won't regret it, until I bought the remake I never played any Klonoa games till this point. I wanted to see if the hype and love for these games are sincere as the fans make it out, and well, I am safe to report, that everything they say is 100% true. Klonoa truly holds his grounds as his own, about the only thing I can compare him to is maybe Kirby 64 but even then its vague. Are the games difficult? Not as much, except for some levels in the sequel but hey sequels are meant to be harder than their predecessors. Even with all the little hiccups throughout my journey of playing Klonoa Phantasy Reverie Series, it still doesn't affect how I rate this game. I mean, hey, its not like playing through any of the Crash Bandicoot games or the early 2D Sonic games or anything, if you want hard, give those a try. Klonoa is rather balanced, its not an easy game but it isn't super hard either (unless going on Hard mode). Even if you struggle, the game will show mercy and give you plenty of chances before truly getting a Game Over screen.

In the end, I have a wonderful time playing through these games, I can see why the fans like them, it revolutionized 2.5D platformers and platformers as a whole. Superb gameplay, a bright colorful, well written cast of characters, and an absolutely damn good story on top. Seriously, these games are absolute masterpieces. If you haven't played this game yet, I highly recommend it. You will not be disappointed.

With that, I once again state that I give Klonoa Phantasy Reverie Series a 5/5. Please support the game if you haven't already, I'd love to see them do more of these.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll admit, I was skeptical about this collection at first because I heard that the physics were off in Door to Phantomile, and I experienced some technical hiccups that were caused more by my weird Linux setup than anything else, but I'm glad I bought it.

So far I can only comment on DtP, since I want to finish it before moving onto Lunatea's Veil, but the visuals and music are fantastic, and the characters are cuter than ever. The platforming can be frustrating at times, like in the original PS1 version of DtP, but sidescrollers are also not my best genre. The game's also quite generous with health and lives on the "normal" difficulty, so that helps soften the blow a bit.

Overall, I'd say this collection is worth buying. The Klonoa franchise absolutely deserves more recognition, and I'm glad that the mainline games are finally available in a more accessible form than ever before.

One BIG thing I have to note; if you're using Linux, this game does NOT like being alt-tabbed out of when you run it in fullscreen! I don't know if this is affected by my dual monitor setup, or the fact that I'm using KDE with X11, but I have to run it in a window in order to keep it from crashing when I'm doing other stuff at the same time. A custom resolution option for windowed mode would be nice to have, but I understand that Linux isn't a platform the devs officially support. :V\t\t\t\t\t
