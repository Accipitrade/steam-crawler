\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite Bandai Namco US trying as hard as they could to mess up the release of this game (No pre-orders, physical releases, and paltry marketing), it's heartwarming to see how the community surrounding these games are all coming together to celebrate the return of Klonoa.

If you're looking for some simple yet fun side-scrolling platformers with cute aesthetics, head bop worthy soundtracks, heartwarming and endearing characters, and gut wrenching sad endings, don't even think of passing on this collection.

The ports aren't entirely perfect with some odd audio issues and frame pacing on framerates higher than 60FPS. But considering Bandai Namco's history of PC releases, these run and play beautifully, and have enough graphical options to make sure even the most potato of modern PCs can handle them. Both games also work great on Steam Deck!

With the possible future of the franchise hanging in the balance of this collection, I implore you: Pick it up, and get Stepping Wind stuck in your head for the rest of time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTrue to form, Klonoa continues to be an underrated series of platformers, now brought to modern consoles and PC in the Phantasy Reverie Series. The original games released in 1997 and 2001 respectively, failed to sell well outside of Japan for a number of reasons not limited to issues like poor marketing, making physical copies of the games extremely expensive and rare. Not only does this collection give players new and old the opportunity to legally play the games for cheap, but also with modernized graphics that look brighter and more detailed than ever, especially Door to Phantomile. The games themselves both hold up extremely well in both presentation and gameplay, and I really hope this game does well enough to warrant a true sequel in the future.

This port both runs very well and has a good number of options for video, audio, and control. Bandai Namco made the smart decision to maintain the original voices, music, and sound effects of most everything while touching up many of the cutscenes, balancing the gameplay (including a two-player mode and difficulty options), and overall polishing that the original PS1/PS2 games in a way they couldn't originally due to hardware limitations. I highly recommend you pick this collection up if you enjoy great platformers with fun gimmicks and amazing story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving finished Klonoa 1 so far and having played a bit of Klonoa 2 i can confirm that this is a worthy collection made by two amazing remasters.

Klonoa 1 has some jank however, it really shows that they just grabbed the wii version, changed some things without proper adjustments and called it a day. Still the wii version is truly the game that needed a remake considering how dry and dark that one felt compared to the original.

Phantasy Reverie gives the game the color it needed, changes klonoa's model to be more similar to the ps1 model and removes the weird dub they made for the wii (not gonna miss it tbh), although the voice lines are a mix of compressed and uncompressed files from the original, which is the weirdest choice i've ever witnessed. That aside the level design remains unchanged meaning that its still the amazing game i played as a child a long time ago.

And from what little i've played of klonoa 2 i really like it, klonoa feels strangely slow and there are some visual glitches, but nothing terrible so far. Since i don't have much experience with klonoa 2 (because of its imprisonment on the ps2) i will be very happy to finally be able to play it properly without game breaking bugs or compatibility issues!.

I wholeheartedly recommend this collection, please save my boy Klonoa!!!\t\t\t\t\t
