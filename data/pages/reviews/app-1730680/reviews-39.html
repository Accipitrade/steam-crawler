Either way, this is an excellent platformer (one of the best ever); this remake is leagues better than the Wii remake. It even includes the sequel Lunatea's Veil originally released on PS2! Both of these titles run very smoothly and you probably won't experience any issues unless you're running it on a toaster.
The pricing at $40 USD is a bit steep, but I've heard Bandai Namco completely butchered the regional pricing for this. I feel for my brothers and sisters around the world staring at a price that is just plain unreasonable. Hopefully they lower the price.
I knew this was going to be a day one purchase for me, regardless of price. I'm just glad to have Klonoa back, and on PC! Hopefully this release sees enough positive feedback that Bandai Namco look into developing a third game, or maybe porting over the still-excellent GBA spin-off titles.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThese new versions are good for normies who don't know how to use emulators but for fans, just stick with the originals. Door To Phantomile is based on the godawful Wii remake, and while Lunatea's Veil is mostly faithful to the PS2 version despite the graphical overhaul, several facial animations are missing. This is still a great purchase for fans to see the unique differences, and LV is definitely a good alternative if you don't want to deal with the headache of making a PS2 function properly on a modern TV without having to deal with Composite AV, but the originals are still better, ESPECIALLY Door To Phantomile. It's downright criminal in the way the story was butchered on the Wii remake, and even though the dialogue and voice acting from the PS1 version were mostly restored, the cutscene animations are ported directly from the Wii and they still look terrible. They even swap to the Wii voices sometimes in certain cinematics and it just comes off as a heavily modded Wii version.

I still recommend this collection for players new and old, because the games themselves are still excellent in their own right, with Lunatea's Veil specifically being one of the best sidescrollers ever made in my opinion. Not to mention we MUST convince Namco to make more Klonoa content. This franchise is really special to us fans for multiple reasons and it just gets treated so dirty by Namco. We were lucky to even get something like this at all, and more official content to expand on the lore set by the first two games would be a godsend. I still think you should play the originals first but Phantasy Reverie Series is definitely worth your time, if not for the interesting differences and a surprisingly good version of Lunatea's Veil.\t\t\t\t\t

Klonoa is a game series that's gotten it's fair share of games/remakes, (one of those remakes was on wii). this is the first time that a remaster has been put on all consoles including pc.

A quick little personal tidbit. I remember when i was 5 years old playing klonoa on my wii, whenever i'd get back from school i'd be super happy and excited to play more of it. countless times i replayed it and super mario galaxy 2. back then, everything just seemed perfect,

playing this now, i can safely say: I feel like i'm reliving/revisiting a part of my childhood, and that makes me happy beyond belief. i'm glad they didn't mess this remake up, i'm glad it has the charm and appeal it did for me when i was younger.

The bosses are charming, I love the bright visuals, the cheery atmosphere. and also; the story is absolutely fun and charming, (ive used the word charming alot this review lol)

Anyways; in total. you won't make a mistake by buying this game. As these two games are the pinnacle of 2d platforming.\t\t\t\t\t
