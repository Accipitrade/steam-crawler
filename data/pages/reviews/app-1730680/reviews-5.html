Rupurudu, wahoo!

PROS: Klonoa Phantasy Reverie Series is a two part collection of the original two Klonoa games. The two are fun & well designed platformers, utilizing the mechanic of being able to grab all enemies above Klonoa's head, & either toss them or use them as a double jump, tossing them down. Though the flutter jump Klonoa uses to temporarily float takes a bit to get used to it's odd arc, the ability to chain double jumps gives the platforming a unique puzzle feel, especially in Lunatea's Veil, which noticeably ramps up the difficulty. Door to Phantomile is mostly based of the Wii remastering, while Lunatea's Veil is the first ported rerelease of the original PS2 title. There are small differences, such as changing Klonoa's design in the original to more closely match the look of the original PS1 title. The story is rather simple in both titles, keeping itself mostly light-hearted, minus a few moments in both games. The visuals pop in a colorful & wonderful manner, & the soundtrack is catchy & quite enjoyable.

CONS: Sadly, this collection does have a few problems. Let's get the potentially patchable one out of the way first. There's a bit of input lag when it comes to the attacking in the game. This means in the latter stages when platforming over bottomless pits, it becomes even harder than the original release to cross. It seems the attack, the wind bullet, doesn't move the same way it did in the original games. This will only be noticeable to those who played the original releases, but it can get fairly annoying. The real hiccup this title has is the audio of Door to Phantomile. For some reason, they ignored the updated speaking audio from the Wii release. This doesn't mean the translated track, this means they ignored the audio in the Phantomilian language. In turn, it seems like they replaced it with the audio from the original PS1 release. This means the audio sharply drops in quality, becoming harder to hear, & much more crunchy.

ACHIEVEMENTS: Klonoa's list is nice & straightforward, requiring a completion of the games while collecting all residents of Phantomile or Momett Dolls. The only ones of slight challenge require completion of the extra stages, though this merely requires heavy use of double jump chaining. Thankfully, one doesn't need to collect all 150 dream stones in every stage, & that reward is so negligible it's barely worth it.

FINAL: Klonoa Phantasy Reverie Series is a wonderful collection that comes so very close to being the absolute definitive way to play these titles. For the first, I still believe the Wii version is better, if only slightly. Regardless of that, this is still a must own two pack for lovers of platformers, & when it comes to the second, those who enjoy an increase of difficulty & length compared to the first. To fans of Klonoa, this is a much welcomed addition to a long since dormant franchise.\t\t\t\t\t
