\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKlonoa is a series that has been very obscure for a long time, but there is not many other communities that are as passionate for the games as the fanbase of this one. Klonoa's last game was in 2008 with the release of a remake of Door to Phantomile on the Wii, which had limited success. This game exists solely because of the passion and heart of the developers and the community who have always kept Klonoa close to their hearts.

This game isn't going to be perfect, and as of right now there is some issues with things like changing your screen resolution. Nothing I've encountered so far has been remotely gamebreaking though. Both games that are included are faithfully remastered and I can tell that they've considered input and criticisms from the fanbase. The first game uses the voice lines from the original game, which can be a little jarring when contrasted with the vibrant, HD textures and models that are used. However, I got used to them after the first few levels.

I have noticed a lot of people complaining about the graphics and saying that the art direction is inferior to the original. Personally I haven't had any issue with any of the graphics, and there is also an option to disable bloom in the game settings if it's something you don't like. I highly recommend this game if you're a fan who's played all the original games, or even if you're someone who's never gotten into the series before. This is a perfect entry point for people who have an interested in the series or like puzzle-platformers in general. On one last note, I hope there's an option to disable tutorials in the future. The pop-up screens are really intrusive and disrupt the experience.

TLDR; Phantasy Reverie Series is a faithful recreation of the original and anyone who buys this is directly contributing to the possibility seeing Klonoa in a brand new adventure.\t\t\t\t\t

While it looks and plays good, there were some weird audio and visual issues. The game uses the voice lines from the PS1 version of the game, but has the cutscenes framed in the manner of the Wii remake which was jarring sometimes. Plus there are certain moments where it uses the Wii voice lines instead of the PS1 making at bit inconsistent. Plus certain models didn't express as much emotion as the sprites from the PS1 version. Not being able to Pause scenes was a bit of a downer as well but not the end of the world.

These two games are damn good and are well worth your time. Hopfully Bandai-Namco will notice people like these games and do more with this IP.\t\t\t\t\t

My favorite is Lunatea's Veil (the 2nd one). But both are so lovely. It's a very meditative game. The gameplay is relaxing. The visuals and art are gorgeous. The music is one of my favorite soundtracks ever.

I will say, the animations/textures don't seem faithful in some parts, and are sometimes very pretty, but other times seem rushed or as if the remaster team overlooked something. There are a few glitches here and there, and timing of bosses or movements that most people wouldn't notice, but are a little off-putting towards big fans of the series. There is occasional lag. Also, tutorial slides keep popping up even after the tutorial is turned off? And maybe they could hide the skip cut-scene option, it doesn't need to be shown the whole time.

I hope they fix these little glitches. This is still the best way to experience the series today, since emulating it is not very stable.\t\t\t\t\t
