\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Had never played the games before these remasters, ended up loving them to the point that i 100%ed them both.


Never played these games as a kid, most i saw was a picture of the main character and the title in magazines but I'd always heard good things. Finally gave the series a try and man I'm so glad I did.

First game was a fun time, some really cool level designs that must've been super ahead of their time when they came out. the hitboxes for grabbing enemies can sometimes be a little finicky but then again i chose to play on "normal" which doesn't extend the range of the air shot like the "easy" mode does. Game was pretty chill and easy until the last portions of the game when they threw in a bit of challenge which was a nice change of pace, and saving all the people gets you a neat challenging little time trial course.

Second game was a fantastic time, it really took the mechanics of the first game and polished them to be much more precise and fast. Had some challenging bits (mostly towards the end) but it was a blast throughout. I like the music a lot in the first game but the music in this one is even better in my opinion. Pretty much everything is better in this version from the character design, to the story, to the platforming variety and thanks to the always enjoyable snowboarding sections that make quite a few appearances in the story.

I would've loved both of these games as a kid if I'd given them a chance, I just honestly barely saw either of these games in stores or at friends' houses and they completely passed me by. If you like platformers like Kirby but with a little more emphasis on precision then you'll definitely have a good time with either of these games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR version: Klonoa just like you remember it, difficulty levels in case you need assistance, a optional 2P helper mode to give you a extra jump. Easy to start off before the final levels crank the difficulty up alot. Easy achievements to earn and perfect. Quality single-player platformer game with no added BS. As for price, think of it as £20 per game rather than a full £40.

Personal note: We need more fun single-player games without live-services and micro-transactions, and these games are one of the few that i hope will prove just that, because no matter what corporate companies say, if a game is treated with love and care without greed in the forefront of their mind, if they stay true and pure to a games route, that's what makes a game feel like magic and a lot of fun. Bring on the rise to single-player games~

Full version: I knew about Klonoa for a long time since the PS1 days, yet i never played the game fully, only the demo which i played multiple times, since then it had a second game on PS2 and even a remaster(?) on Wii/Wii U for the original. This game bundle comes with the first two games, the first one being a port of the Wii version i believe, and a upgraded graphic version of the second. Both games play near enouth the same, which is fine, you move, jump and briefly hover, and shoot a wind bullet, that's the entirety of your controls, simple and effective. The gimmick of the game is your wind bullet, shoot a Moo-Moo *I believe that's one of the basic enemy names* with a wind bullet, and you'll inflate it and carry it around with you, with it you can throw it as a projectile that does damage to enemies or used to break items around your throwing range, that's not just limited to left and right, you can even throw it towards and away from the screen as well, you can also use it as a double jump or to break/attack underneath you. Most stages will have gems to collect, which do carry other levels, with a max to earn being 150, 100 collected gems reward you with an extra life, aside from that there are 6 hidden *or clearly seeable but tricky to find* collectables, collect every single one and at the end you can unlock a hidden stage that'll put your abilities to the test. And honestly that's pretty much everything to the game.
The ending for the first game, without going into detail, if you are weak of heart, just have some tissues on hand just in case, the second game isn't as emotional but it still has its moments.
Overall i had a blast with the games, achievements were easy to earn, which i don't really care about, all i care about is a good game that is treated with respect, which thankfully is done with this, the game is just pure to its roots, a single player platformer game, nothing more, nothing less, no live-services or microtransactions *if you ignore the soundtrack, digital artbook and cosmetic hats since they give no effects whatsoever*, what most games needs to be nowadays in my opinion.
I honestly say the price is just right for the games since i think of it as £20 each for both games, and its a happy nostalgia trip for me. I just hope and pray that wherever Klonoa ends up next, if ever, he'll be able to save the world from what its slowly becoming and make everyone happy. Wahoo~! *And if you want to go even further back, remember that Klonoa had a beach volleyball game on the PS1 after Klonoa 2, plus i think there was a gameboy game also but i cannot speak on that.*\t\t\t\t\t

It's great to see elements of the first game (that had been modified in the Wiimake) be restored to the way it'd been originally intended (Ex. Klonoa's age/height, Huepow's hands, Joka's floating hands/feet, etc.).

My only criticism would have to be some missing sounds and face morphs in cut-scenes (Mainly in Klonoa 2), also imperfected textures; Additionally, some of the modified dialogue is inconsistent or off-putting ("Bell of Anger" as opposed to "Bell of Discord")
I have high hopes a patch comes out for these errors down the line, it would be great to see more care for that since there's been so much care put into its good reception from everyone!

I do highly recommend playing this game despite these things — they're small and don't take away too much, but for someone who's familiar with the originals, it may be a bit off-putting at first is all!\t\t\t\t\t
