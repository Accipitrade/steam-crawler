\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh brother... i have mixed feelings here, i will mostly focus on Klonoa 1 here since it's the one i finished and to be honest, the more special game to me.

First things first, it's an ok way to experience these games, hell i'd argue it's a great way to experience ONE of these games.

Don't expect a Crash Bandicoot N. Sane Trilogy level of remake here, you are getting at best two PS2 games with some spit shine applied to 'em, this works well for Klonoa 2, since that was a PS2 game originally and i'd argue it mostly looks better than the original even tough i've played very little of it.

Klonoa 1 on the other hand, oh boy, this was a PS1 game and to be honest, the PS1 version is still the best way to experience this game, this part of the remake fumbles. The original game might've been a PS1 game but it wasn't a pushover when it comes to graphics at the time and it had a consistent look, this part of the remake honestly wouldn't look impressive even on the PS2, and its so inconsistent, some levels look very good, some look bland, and animations during cutscenes are consistently stiff, more on that later. I will however compliment the character designs, they do capture the style of the original very well and probably save Klonoa 1 from being a total disaster graphically in this remake.

The classic Klonoa gameplay is mostly intact, 2.5 platformer, you interact with elements in the background and foreground, however the new graphics i feel hurt Klonoa 1 here, unlike Klonoa 2, the first game was not designed with full 3D graphics in mind, in the original, your character and the enemies were 2D sprites, only the levels were 3D, as a result, it was very easy to tell when enemies were in front of you, in the background or the foreground, with the new graphics is not so easy and i sometimes tried to grab enemies that were actually in the background, that being said the gameplay is 95% intact, its not really a deal breaker on this aspect, and the game is super easy anyways, mistakes like this wont generate too much frustration.

Now here's the thing, if you've heard of Klonoa 1 before, you probably know it as "that cute platformer that gets surprisingly sad towards the end", i'd argue the story of Klonoa 1 is crucial for the experience and the remake drops the ball HARD here, nearly every single dramatic cutscene is sabotaged by stiff character animations, SPECIALLY stiff facial animations, something very sad might be happening on screen and the characters might have a smile on their faces because animation is hard and someone was very lazy during development, this issue is compounded by the audio which was taken directly from the original, and while that's a plus for the music, which is as great as ever, its been horrible for the character voices which are bit-crunched to hell and back, everyone sounds as if they recorded their lines in a basement and underwater, the fact character talk is gibberish prevents this issue from getting worse, atleast you know you are not supposed to understand anything.

Here's the thing tough, i recognize few people will care as much as i do, this is a game that made me feel sad as a kid and that was rare, it stuck with me, someone who didn't grow up with this game isn't going to give a damn, they will still be getting a decent platformer out of Klonoa 1, albeit a short and easy one, Klonoa 2 while not as emotionally impactful to me, is the better game in terms of gameplay, longer, harder and looks much better overall in this remake.

i suppose the package as a whole is worth it so i recommend it, even tough i believe this is not the best way to play Klonoa 1.\t\t\t\t\t
