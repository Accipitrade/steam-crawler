\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhantasy Reverie Series is an ok remake, but there's unfortunately a noticeable lack of polish for $40.

Here's a list of the (mostly minor) problems:
- The game's core mechanic (the wind bullet) has been altered to be slower and smaller, making it mildly more difficult to use. This affects both games.
- The above point is compounded by the high friction Klonoa has in this game. He can no longer slide as far after shooting the wind bullet.
- The title screen for Door to Phantomile is out of sync with the music jingle.
- Several cutscenes in both games have missing or worse facial animation (even compared to Door to Phantomile's sprites).
- All of the FMVs in Door to Phantomile have been replaced with vastly inferior in-game cutscenes.
- The art style for Lunatea’s Veil has been changed to be much brighter and more saturated. While it might be down to preference which look you prefer, I think the original look of the game should have been preserved.
- A couple of sections in Door to Phantomile are made a little bit more challenging due to Klonoa having a pill-shaped hitbox that can slide off ledges

I don't think this collection is bad way to play these games, but I also don't think it's the *best* way to play them, especially for Door to Phantomile.

Play these remakes, but play the originals too. They're all worth playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI randomly came across this game for the very first time during the Bandai-Namco sale about two months ago and it was the best game discovery I’ve made since seeing my brother’s Half Life 2 case got me back into PC gaming 10 years ago. I’ve been completely obsessed with the series since then, and I’ve been playing both this package and also all the original versions and spin-offs I can find (Klonoa Beach Volleyball being a surprise favourite of mine; trust me, it’s much better than it sounds). I’ve listened to some of the soundtracks so much that I’m now bored of them.

I’d recommend this package to essentially everyone, but there are one or two things to consider, especially if you’ve played the original versions:

- The package is quite expensive (£40), but its sale price (£24) is perfectly reasonable. Bear in mind however that original copies of Klonoa 1 and 2 go for £130 and £40 respectively, and that’s assuming you still have a PS2 and preferably a CRT TV. For some reason, it’s currently (Christmas 2022) on sale on the Humble Bundle but not Steam, and if I have no idea if this is a mistake or not.

- This package is almost hands-down the definitive way to play Klonoa 2 (but make sure you disable bloom in settings to tone down the over-brightness and white objects' glowing), but the remake of Klonoa 1 is much more controversial. The remake of Klonoa 1 here is the Wii remake made to look more faithful to the original, resulting in a mish-mash of the original and Wii remake which both keeps and removes good and bad things from both versions. Thus you could make a case for all three versions being the best. In fact, I’d recommend also trying to play the original version of Klonoa 1 in some way. Unfortunately, there is no way to do this in Europe which is both cheap and legal, but in America and Japan you can get it on the PS3 store, I think for $7.

- The wind ring (the weapon Klonoa uses) works slightly differently and requires more precision in the remakes, and the jumping can be a bit awkward in Klonoa 1 (I’ve run off the edge of platforms earlier than expected multiple times).

Of course, none of these things are too much of a concern if you’re new to the series. Play all the versions you feel like and come to your own conclusion. But please buy this remake; it’s the second time Bandai-Namco have tried to bring Klonoa back from the dead, and if this fails he will never be coming back.

P.S. When playing Klonoa 1, I’d strongly consider pausing the game at the following points and watching the original CGI cutscenes on Youtube instead: the opening cutscene, after Vision 5-2, and the ending cutscene after Final Vision (NOT Vision 6-2). The original’s three CGI cutscenes are much better and more impactful than the remake's in-engine replacements.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKlonoa finally makes its way to steam and modern hardware after many years of silence. I have previously played the original Klonoa: Door to Phantomile and did not get the chance to play Klonoa 2 so this was a fresh experience.

Gameplay: This is a platformer with puzzle elements to it, the main mechanic is grabbing enemies and finding out what to do next with them. There is a lot of items to collect within the game in order to 100% each of them and a lot of replay-ability as a result, the levels are fun that this is not a chore.

Story: The story is pretty wholesome throughout with some harder subjects thrown in but nothing too obscene without going into spoilers, certainly better than most game's storylines in this rating.

Difficulty: Played both games on Normal, not hard by any stretch, plenty of challenges and particular levels to keep you on your toes. Hard difficulty is able to be unlocked which I have not tried which may remedy the difficulty being too easy.

Overall you're getting two games for the price of one, both excellent in quality and shine. I recommend this remaster of two of the main Klonoa games, very underrated franchise and I'd love to see Klonoa 3.\t\t\t\t\t
