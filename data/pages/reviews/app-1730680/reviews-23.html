\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Klonoa games are special to me. Klonoa 2 in particular is one of my favorite games. And it saddened me knowing that in the twenty one years it had been out, the game had no re-release in any form. So seeing the game, along with the original game being re-released for every modern console and the PC made me pretty happy.

I booted up 2 immediately and while I thought the game looked really nice in terms of the environments and updated character designs, all the little details seemed to have fallen to the wayside.

Facial expressions have generally become worse looking and are outright missing in some cases. You have a couple scenes where a certain character is supposed to be smiling, but isn't. Part of what made the game so special to me is how expressive the characters are, and seeing that taken away almost ruins a few scenes. Apparently, a lot of the files that were ported over didn't transfer correctly and were overlooked.

Music cues for certain cutscenes have been broken, so music plays over moments where it's supposed to stop in order to emphasize certain moments in the cutscenes.

Background elements like the crowds in Joilant are incredibly low-poly creating a jarring disconnect with everything else.

Random parts of the script got changed which leads to weird moments where characters repeat what they've just said and certain lines got replaced by ones that just sound awkward.

As for DtP, the game is essentially a shinier port of the Wii remake from back in 2008, but with updated character models to keep in line with the original PS1 game's aesthetic. It tries elements of both via using the PS1's audio files in conjunction with the Wii's cutscenes, which creates a rather jarring disconnect. Still better than the Wii's terrible English dub, but not by much.

Overall, this is not a very good remake and frankly I feel the Klonoa games deserved far better than this. It's frankly a sloppily made product. I would hold off on buying the game until Namco Bandai polishes the game some more via updates or until somebody decides to mod the games to try and fix the issues these games have.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the greatest games of the PS1 is finally here on Steam.

This port, much like the Wii port about a decade ago has its pros and cons which other reviews here have summed up nicely. There is clearly a strong love for the original games from both the fan base and the developers (ignoring their odd DLC choices). Sometimes the controls do not feel as tight as they did on the PS1, but I think it might personally just take some getting used to as I am so familiar with the original.

If you have never played Klonoa Door to Phantomile it is a fantastic example of '2.5D' platforming with lovable characters, cute enemies, an unusually interesting and mysterious plot (especially later in the game) with unique and challening game mechanics (the wind bullet) that still hold up to this day.

As for Klonoa 2, this is my first time playing it as I was never able to get the chance back in the day. This was a big selling point for me, having both games in one!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe remaster didn't do good for Klonoa, and it mostly comes down to the controls and gamefeel. (Though I also think the visuals are just plain bad and if anything the original pixelated PS1 game has a better style, definitely better pre-rendered cutscenes, but I could get past all that.) I feel like this game wasn't tested compared to the original game. And I mean THE original PS1 title, not the Wii remake. I love Klonoa and I hope the series returns, but I also hope that the people who worked on this won't be the creators of the upcoming titles. I've cleared 4-2 and I decided it's not worth it, even though I finished the original game dozens of times and never felt bothered by anything.
With that out of the way here are some things that bothered me through the whole time:

1) Klonoa turns on a dime, but inching forward makes him slide forward like ~3 steps. In the original game Klonoa's direction was irrelevant, he just moved immediately in the direction you pressed. This is bad because the game has some tight platforms, making you unintentionally slide off them, what makes it even worse is, like I said, Klonoa turns on a dime in place, which means that if you wanted to compensate for this slide, you will not do anything because Klonoa simply turns around while continuing sliding.

2) Jumping feels bad. Your input stays for too long, you start gliding way too fast after pressing X, and if you accidentally press X before landing you will do another jump. Klonoa has set jump height no matter how long you hold down X (which is exactly like the original) so having to accidentally jump is not something that should ever happen.

3) Air control has momentum now. In neither of the original games there was any sort of preserved momentum of Klonoa while he was in the air. This was great because it made landing on the tiny platforms precise. Now you have to account for the added momentum, and once having landed on the platforms, you basically cannot correct your position because of the issue listed in 1)

4) The wind-bullet is tiny, it needs to be around twice as chonky. There are SLOPES in the game where you can't grab enemies because the hitbox is so small. Flying enemies feel just about the same.

5) So when you turn around you basically just turn in place, which makes jumping right after turning build up speed very slowly. You basically jump forward at half the speed. (Again these small things are bad because this is a precision platformer)

6) Just when landing and turning around at the same time makes it so that you CANNOT fire a wind-bullet. This is inconsistent at best, but there were cases when my input just didn't register at all. I jump into the air, turn around, land, and my attack never comes out.

7) I really wish they copied from the original game and not the Wii version (it has changed some things, most notably the 2-2 boss, the fish and king boss, where the middle part is now connected just so there is less challenge)

In summary, I'd rather get the original games on an emulator because they feel so much better. Even though the original Klonoa 1 was much slower to grab enemies, actually it was so slow that you could jump, grab an enemy, have like 1-2 frames to preform a glide to stop yourself from descending (this was really good for the helicopter enemies), and then do a double jump. Klonoa 2 got rid of this, probably for the better, the game feels snappier and more responsive, however non of the problems I listed above had appeared in neither Klonoa 1 or 2. I'm hesitant to recommend the remasters and not an emulator. If you can by any means get the original PS1 and PS2 games, play those instead.
