\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKlonoa's deeply melancholic tale fits into the mascot platformer craze of the 90s the same way Doki Doki Literature Club fits into Steam's Romantic Visual Novel library. Combining puzzle solving elements with action platforming, Klonoa frankly has more in common with Celeste than anything of its time, and the fact that I just used two games from the past five years to describe these 20+ year old games should give you an idea of how ahead of their time these titles were.

Are there nitpicks with this remaster? Sure, but nothing that dramatically inhibits the experience outside from a couple moments (which would be easy to patch if Namco gives this package the love it deserves). But at the end of the day, I'd still recommend this as the ideal way to play both these absolutely perfect games with impeccably lean level design, fantastic and distinct music, and some of the best writing that exists for a "children's game." 100% worth the price at $40, but if it's currently on sale, don't even think about it, just get it.\t\t\t\t\t
