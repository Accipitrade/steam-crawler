Two timeless games, one great collection. The Klonoa series is super underrated, and I hope lots of people buy this game so we could possibly get more Klonoa games. That would be awesome! Both of these games are fantastic and still hold up today, and while these remakes feel a bit low budget, the fun is completely intact, and you can feel the love and care put into this compilation. There are some things the originals do better (such as the cutscenes), and some things the remakes do better (such as the graphics), but you can't go wrong either way. The graphics are good, the music is great, the gameplay is simple bliss, the stories are immaculate, and the endings are emotional. I won't spoil anything, but you might want a box of tissues on hand. These games aren't that difficult, but in turn they are quite relaxing.

Klonoa 1 is a remaster of the Wii remake, while Klonoa 2 is a brand new remake. New difficulty options have been added, graphics and character models have been updated, the English voice acting has been removed, and new side modes are included. The only omissions are the Reverse Mode, Character Viewer and alternate costumes from the Wii version. Please play these games, you won't regret it! And unless Namco remakes them as well, emulate the other Klonoa games. I'll give you a list of every other Klonoa game:

- Klonoa: Empire of Dreams (GBA)
- Klonoa Beach Volleyball (PS1/PSX)
- Klonoa 2: Dream Champ Tournament (GBA)
- Klonoa Heroes: Densetsu no Star Medal (GBA)
- Kaze no Klonoa: Moonlight Museum (Wonderswan)

Klonoa Heroes and Moonlight Museum are in Japanese only, but you can easily find fan-made English patches online. I would suggest that you only get the DLC if you're a big Klonoa fan though. I know I sure am, since I bought it. Klonoa is definitely one of my favourite series out there! Here's hoping for a Klonoa 3 in the future!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoth Klonoa games included in this are nice platformers.
Responsible, not too hard, more on the easy side of things, but not too easy either, nice platforming puzzles and so on.

The graphics are.. Well, they're ok. Nothing to write home about. Looks like a mid 2010 game, despite coming out last year. It's a bit inconsistent at times, with some stuff being very low poly and something next to it not.
The first cutscene of the first game, where his collar already clips through his body despite being the first thing the player sees, exemplifies the graphics. Not bad but kinda lazy.

But the cutscenes. Holy hell. You either have the patience of a nice old grandma or you will miss the story. Those are some of the most awful cutscenes I ever witnessed. The cutscenes progress mostly on their own, only occasionally you have to press a button to continue, but you can't "skip this dialog window" either. They speak so slowly in their fantasy language that you could read it 3 times, and to add insult to injury there's a 3-second pause after every bit of dialogue, as if they wanted to add a laugh track but forgot it.
So, what do you do? You skip. There are even two ways to skip, a 5x speed boost and a "skip all". The problem, every now and then the game actually has a few lines of dialogue that are not so painfully slow. So you 5x over that way too long one, and accidentlly over the next 5, just to then see them standing around saying nothing for seconds even at 5x speed before they continue.
A simple "skip to next dialoge", quasi-standard in most games that even have dialog boxes, would have fixed that.

Sorry for spending so much time on this but those damn cutscenes man.

Now the pricing. This is a remake of two 1997 games. Putting this out at 60 bucks is just an insult. It should be half that, possibly less. Do not buy outside of a massive sale\t\t\t\t\t
