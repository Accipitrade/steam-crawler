\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of my favorite franchises of all time; given a stylish and buttery-smooth modern touch-up. Klonoa 1 and 2 are still some of the finest platformers ever made even after 20 years have passed, and these remakes are a testament to just how timeless they are.
While some may prefer their original console counterparts for one reason or another— namely Klonoa 1's original artstyle on the PlayStation 1, the lack of legal avenues to acquire those original releases makes it hard to play them without emulation or spending hundreds of dollars for a good condition physical copy. The Phantasy Reverie Series is the best way to experience them bar none, if you simply want to experience the games for yourself.

Klonoa 1 is just as gripping and fun as ever; compared to the original Wii remake, everything is brighter, more colorful, and far more accurate to the original PS1 release. It is weird how they decided to use the original, heavily compressed voice acting from the original version, though. The Wii remake had rerecorded voice lines for the Phantomilian language, and even in this remake, there are a couple lines with rerecorded lines that stick out from the rest of the compressed PS1 audio. It's jarring, though it doesn't ruin the pacing or the cutscenes.

The new remake of Klonoa 2, on the other hand, is as ideal as you can get for a remake. There are a couple minor nitpicks that I could chastise, but they're so irrelevant when they don't impact the gameplay itself, which remains as stellar as ever. If you love platformers, you owe it to yourself to play these games. Klonoa is my friend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was so lovely. The first game was a bit of a slog due to the controls being a little funky. But the second one improved on it quite a bit and added a few new fun mechanics (tho the snowboard. that did not spark joy.) The mechanics are simple but innovative! Makes em stand out a little.
I love the level designs, all the levels are so lovely to look at. And the music! Gosh! It is so whimsical and fun and nostalgic. Definitely be listening to some of these after the fact.
The stories in both of the games, while simple, were lovely. I like the message and story better in the second game overall. But the ending of the first game really stuck out to me, gah it still hurts to think about.
Overall I'm really happy I did get to experience these games!! Please buy them to show bandai that we want more of this franchise!!\t\t\t\t\t
