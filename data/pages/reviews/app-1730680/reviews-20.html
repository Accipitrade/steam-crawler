Everyone I know loves this series AND these remasters, and I would love to understand why.
I played both games until full completion and I absolutely hated every single minute of it.
The presentation, visuals, music, level design, everything is so mediocre it hurts.
On top of that, the gameplay is straight up broken; Klonoa shoots his lightbeam about a toenail away from his body and it lingers less than a microsecond; and the levels DO NOT accomodate for this kind of limitation. I would not recommend this game, ESPECIALLY for its high pricetag........... but it has its fans for a reason. People clearly see something in this series that I am blind to or something, so maybe this game could also be for you (the reader) and I am not the best judge for this for you. I adore 2D mascot platformers, DKC being one of my favorite video game series of all time.... so it's not like I dont like the genre either.

Total respect if you dig this game, but just because I respect it, does not mean I understand it in the slightest. Nonetheless, I am glad this remaster is doing well and its pleasing the target audience it was meant for. I really do hope Klonoa 3 becomes a thing in the future, and makes you guys happy!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKlonoa's resurgence into the mainstream conversation is an inspiring one. What were once critically adored yet super hard-to-find games are now revamped in an easily accessible collection. As far as the games themselves, they still hold up very well (though the $40-$60 price tag is a bit much).

Door to Phantomile's remake in this collection is now my preferred way to experience the first game. While there are technical aspects that it lacks in comparison to the 1997 original, the graphical overhaul and character models are greatly appreciated. Plus, they removed Klonoa's sliding problem from the first game, which makes the tighter platforming sections significantly more manageable.

Lunatea's Veil's remake on the other hand is a handled really well. There are a few awkward moments here and there, since it's more of a graphical touch-up than a full on remake, but having the game escape PS2 purgatory in a vibrant new light is extremely welcomed.

This collection is well worth a platformer fan's time. They are simple to understand, fun, and pack a significant narrative punch for any first-time player!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a wonderful collection of two "retro" platformers. I never played the originals but these remasters/remakes were great. While certain level design choices show the age of these games, overall the mechanics hold up very well and are surprisingly satisfying. In addition, getting all achievements isn't very much of a hassle except for maybe the last few levels of the second game (which can be made easier by the fact that changing your costume in the options menu resets you back to the last checkpoint). Overall a definte buy for fans of 2D platformers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBetween the two titles in this remaster, I only have experience with Door to Phantomile (though through the original ps1 release, and not the wii remake), but I can say so far being a quarter of the way through of Door to Phantomile on this release, it's a solid remaster. Some things like the momentum and movement (even the placement of some dream stones) are a bit different, though I will chock this up to DtP being based heavily on the wii remake for this port. From what I've seen elsewhere online, the version of 2 included in this remaster is extremely good with certain hiccups at points, and it seems to rival 2's original release. Overall a very solid bundle of two great ps1 and ps2 platformers, and it's probably the best way to play them aside from just owning or emulating the original copies.\t\t\t\t\t
