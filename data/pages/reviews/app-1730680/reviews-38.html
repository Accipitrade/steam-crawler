\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I was never able to pick up the originals and only played the Klonoa 2 demo 100 times over, I'm really happy that this HD remaster was released. It's a cute game with a lot of charm and a game you probably want to play as a die-hard platformer fan. I do have to say that I remember the game being more fun than it is. It's still fun, but it's definitely not the best out there. If the team decides to make a sequel, I really hope they make it to modern platformer standards and update/remove a lot of the outdated and not so fun mechanics.

The characters are cute and the story is alright. I feel like the graphics are fine, but they're a bit over-saturated.

Though I did hit recommend, I really with that Steam had a "maybe" button because I don't recommend against it, but I also wouldn't recommend it to my friends unless they could pick it up for 25 euros. I's a nostalgia cash grab. For a steep 50 euros you get 2 HD remasters, not remakes. This while beautifully remade games like Spyro & Crash were cheaper on release and have 3 games each. Added to that a "DLC" with costumes, an artbook and soundtrack (you don't even get the mp3/midi files) for 20 euros.

What I also find incredibly annoying is the big "speed up" and "skip" buttons on the cutscenes. I'm happy you can skip them when you rerun a level but for Pete's sake at least hide these hideous buttons.

If you're a die-hard fan of the series you've probably bought it, but if not I'd really wait for a sale. Also please don't buy the dlc, it's just a cash grab.\t\t\t\t\t

The game feels polished and the platforming is fun as usual, Klonoa 1 being a fun little romp even if it's short. Cutscenes definitely look strange without some of the prerendered cutscene effects that really add the oomph you'd need. Still, super solid.

But the biggest one for me is Lunatea's Veil. The fact that it hasn't had any support since its release back in 2001 is astounding, but finally it can be played here on PC and almost every modern console. It's such a relief to see it rise from the dead. That aside, 2 is my favorite and It looks absolutely gorgeous. I can't wait to bite into the rest of it and 100% this game. Hope everyone can give this franchise a shot, not just for the possibility of Klonoa returning in future titles, but just to play these 2 amazing games and have a good time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSome things I've noticed from 2 hours in and exclusively for Lunatea's Veil, so far.

The Negative:

I played Lunatea's Veil as a kid on my PS2, and while it's nice it's almost like I'm reliving my childhood, I couldn't help but notice the almost reused textures from the PS2 era. Many things seem old-looking and static. Popka's animations during cut scenes look very out of place and the background textures are crusty. I was really hoping for some more TLC to be put into my all time favourite game for the 25th anniversary, but so far it's just feeling like a slightly polished port.

The Positive:

You can finally own a working copy of Lunatea's Veil because PCSX2 does not have one. 40 bucks well spent, I suppose. And also, it's great that Klonoa is no longer a one console wonder (PS1, PS2, WII)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDead honest, I think the original 1997 PS1 Door to Phantomile is still the best version. Since this is a remaster of the Wii version, some of the charm is lost by not having those incredibly beautiful pre-rendered PS1 cutscenes, especially the one when Cress rises and the one at the end. I will also warn you that the hitboxes in Door to Phantomile can be a bit... imperfect, shall we say.

HOWEVER, they made the chad choice to use the OG PS1 audio, and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ does it sound great. And the music on my modern headphones is an absolute treat. The music in DtP was always bangin' from start to finish, but I love hearing it on a pair of quality headphones instead of from the TV. And while I think some of the charm is lost, I still adore this game.

As for Lunatea's Veil, there's really nothing changed here save for the buttery frame rate and upscaled resolution. But it already had a pretty timeless look, and it's still just as fun to play here as it was on PS2. I was stoked when they announced this remaster, and I'm glad I was not disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
