\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is absolutely worth the money, ESPECIALLY if you get it on sale. You get so much great content for such a low price compared to individual modules. Granted, each plane included is a low fidelity plane which means no fully clickable environment but that isn't really a big deal at all to me. I just want to enjoy flying a plane in a simulation environment and this is exactly what you get to do.

EDIT: I want to add some more positive gloating about this module. When I first got it, I mostly just played around with the Su-27 since the majority of the training missions surround it, and I also flew the Su-25 some since it was fun to compare it with the base game's Su-25T. With that said, it wasn't until recently, a year later, that I finally got into that glorious A10 and I have to say...it is worth it for that plane alone. It has almost as much hard hitting power as the Su-25T but with the maneuverability of the Su-25, if not better...AND, it has that legendary GAU-8 built into the center of it. TOO MUCH FUN.

So in essence, if you are either new to simming or just new to DCS, I highly recommend you learn the Su-25T from the base game to get a feel for how the game works and to get a feel for the editor. Then get Flaming Cliffs which gives you so much to learn:
Su-25 - Less tech than the T model which means to TV guided goodies and no Vikhrs but far less weight and more agility.
A-10A - TERRIFIC Air-Ground plane with massive capability and surprising maneuverability.
Su-27 - Excellent Fighter plane with modern features and surprising handling for such a large plane while retaining ground attack capabilities to add to its depth.
J-11A - Chinese version of the Su-27 which has Fox 3 missile capability.
Su-33 - Carrier launched version of the Su-27 with some pros and cons but more importantly gives you a chance to practice carrier launches and landings.
MiG-29A - Terrific lightweight interceptor with so much power, it will literally tail-strike if you punch it to full throttle from a stand-still.
MiG-29G - German version of the A model.
MiG-29S - (referred to as the C model in game controls) More advanced version of the A model with built-in ECM and Fox 3 missile capability.
F-15C - The only "Air only" fighter with zero ground attack capability but widely accepted to be the very best Air-Air dog fighter in the DCS game World plus it has Fox 3 missile capability.

That's 9 planes for a total of 10 when you include the base game's Su-25T.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile DCS is generally a platform for "study sims" with an almost ridiculous level of detail and authenticity, this package includes several fairly modern aircraft that are more "survey" style with very accurate, high-fidelity flight models, but have some of their system simplified. You won't be able to learn cold-start procedures or the finer nuances of radio operations in these aircraft, but they will still fly and fight much like their real-world counterparts. The biggest downside to these aircraft (which are each sold separately if you choose) is that the cockpits are not interactive... you can't just use the mouse (or VR controllers) to "click" on the Master Arm switch, you have to map that function to your controls. For some of the lesser-used controls, that probably means mapping them to the keyboard, which is hard to use for Virtual Reality users.

These limitations aside, these are probably the most realistic consumer-level simulators of these aircraft out there. These aren't arcade sims. Just learning to take off and land some of these powerful birds will take a bit of practice, and then learning to employ them effectively in combat will keep you busy for a long time. While some missions and training lessons come with the package, to get the most out of it you'll want to get familiar with the mission editor, download missions and campaigns online, buy premium high-quality campaigns, or venture online into multiplayer.

This package of aircraft is a great deal for any DCS player, but they are perfect for new players wanting to dip their toe into the DCS waters. You get some extremely capable aircraft, and the most advanced Russian (and, as a recent bonus, the Chinese J-11A) aircraft in the game. So that's a grand total of 8 aircraft for the price (although you can argue that three of them are variants of others): The F-15C, the A-10A, the Su-27 / Su-33 / J-11A, the MiG-29A / MiG-29s, and the Su-25. The F-15C is a dedicated air-to-air fighter, the Su-25 and A-10A are dedicated air-to-ground attack aircraft with extremely limited air-to-air defensive capabilities, and the other aircraft are primarily air-to-air with some simple air-to-ground systems and weaponry.

Anyway, this is a fantastic package for the price. Just be aware that these aircraft are "DCS Lite." They are great to learn DCS with, fully compatible with all other DCS modules / aircraft, and in truth may be all you ever need. But when and if you are ready to take it to the next level, there are plenty more modules out there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fantastic this pack of aircraft. There's some great variety in roles and missions for these aircraft, ranging from ground attack with the SU-25 and A-10A (an older version of the A-10C), to supersonic BVR duels and perilous dogfights with all of the other aircraft. My only complaint is that the tutorials are really lacking for these modules, and you are better off going to Youtube for help. I don't think a HOTAS is even required for any aircraft besisdes the A-10, since there are so many sensors to play with.

If you are interested after playing some of the free content which is provided on your original download of the game, this is a great way to experience a large range of game modes and play styles available in DCS World. And FC3 can let you play on the fun multiplayer modes, such as PVE/PVP air battles, and even formation flying. Sure, you can't click around the cockpits, but honestly I think it lowers the fustration factor for new players. If you are interested in trying out the clicky cockpits, give the trainer P-51 a shot and see how you fancy it, it's free.

If this is ever on sale, pick it up if you have tried the free aircraft, but I would not suggest it until you give the free experience a try.\t\t\t\t\t
