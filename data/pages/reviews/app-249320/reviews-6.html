\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy old-school favorite when it comes to planes in DCS simulator.

Firstly started up as Lock On and then LockOn: Flaming Cliffs 2, and then here in FC3 using DCS: World engine.

When I thought of DCS:W, my first thought of this is FC3, where I can use it as my base for mix-and-match available and non-playable modules. And yet, this is what I like. I've been porting several missions from Flaming Cliffs 2 to support FC3 and DCS: World engine, and there are minor drawbacks due to the new engine and physics that introduced.

Had to be frank, the StarForce protection system is very sensitive to hardware changes. I've tried that myself on older games like FC2 and Black Shark; they are sensitive to hardware changes and require new activation.

Overall, this set of module provides everything you need to experience the old-school style of Eagle Dynamics' glory, with more features and possibilites that the older games do not have.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is THE pack to get if you start flying DCS, i highly reccomend this both for beginners and more experienced flyers.
Do NOT listen to the people calling it simple, yes it is simple in the sense that not every single thing that are on the aircrafts real life counterparts is modelled and that these modules do not have clickable cockpits. Instead they work the same way as the free included Su-25T wich by the way is one of the very best air to ground aircraft DCS can muster.

Instead of 500+ page manuals and years of ground school that you MUST go trough with the high fidelity modules this gets you in the air in a few days, and you can master most of these planes within a few months of purchase. The manuals for each module ranges from 130 to 200 pages.

The flight models are ED's PFM(professional flight model)and is fantastic, doing high G maneouvers in the Mig-29 looking out the canopy at your wings and watching them flex, twist, and bend from the forces applied to the airframe is just stunning!
You can not go wrong with this pack it gets you in the air, fighting and flying and most importantly having fun much much faster and with a lot less head scratching than anything else, an excellent place to start your DCS adventure.

This review is written by someone who currently owns 70% of ALL available modules and have 1000+ hours with DCS World, so you can take my word for it:)

Happy landings, and welcome to the skies of DCS World!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFC3 (Flaming Cliffs 3, not Far Cry 3) is containing multiple planes and some missions compared to many single vehicle modules.
Although, this package differs from single plane modules quite a bit since these planes are way simpler to use rather than those single vehicle modules.

That means though that these planes are incredibly more user friendly, allowing new users to get the hang of the flight physics and stuff currently in DCS World. See this module as a bridge to DCS World modules before dedicating yourself to a single plane which is way more complicated than all of these included planes all together.

The roster are the following:
A-10A [Close air support/bomber]
You should know what plane this is. BRRRT and whatnot.
+The A-10 loves you.
+Mavericks are awesome A2G missiles. Remember to use the right variant for your mission. Some come with IR, some don't.
+GAU-8 - Accept no substitute.
+BRRRT!
-Not enough rockets. C variant has more pylons for rockets.
-Not very quick and not so agile, don't do any crazy manuevers with this one.
-No laze/Combined Arms support. You can't map in laser coordinated strikes like the A-10C module.
-Does not use metric system, silly americans. You will need to convert feet into meters and stuff, although this may not be a problem for you.
*The A-10A and the A-10C are quite different. The FC3 version is much easier to get up and running while the C variant is a full scale module with clickable cockpit, available weapons may differ for the A and C variants.

F-15C [Fighter]
The classic, no-nonsense air superiority fighter. Kinda boring imo since that is all it does, it is not the E variant which is more multirole.
If you like shooting down planes- this is your plane.
+You can cosplay as Starscream from Transformers.
+Comes with search radar. Can track down airborne enemies quite easily.
-Limited A2G ability.
-Does not use metric system, silly americans. You will need to convert feet into meters and stuff, although this may not be a problem for you.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing package, includes a wide array of planes from both the russian and american side that cover many roles, from CAS to air superiority, well enough to get a very good feel for each.
One of them is even carrier based, what more can you ask for?

Don't expect a study level simulation, however, since the systems and the human-machine interface is very much simplified, and all complex interactions are substituted by keyboard shortcuts. This has its plus side too however since you can learn to use a plane and its systems much quicker, getting much sooner into action without sacrificing days to go trough a simulated flight school.
Learning to operate all systems proficiently however still does take a respectable amount of knowledge and practice, so don't expect a simcade experience, either! Some particular instruments, especially radars and weapon systems, still require a 40ish minutes tutorial to explain fully.

Also, the fact that the systems are simplified does not mean that these planes are not competitive in multiplayer: the F15C is one of the most capable A2A platforms in the game currently, although it lacks datalink. The J11 is also a competitive fighter with decent FOX3s which is included in the package.

Easiest comparison to these planes is the Su25T thats included in the free copy of DCS world. That's basically the general feel they will have (just on a systems interaction level; flight dynamics and such, and the systems themselves, obviously are completely different from plane to plane), so if that's not your thing this pack is not for you. I do reccommend giving that a try before considering whether to buy this or not.

So who's this for? Definitively not for the hardcore simmer that demands study level clickable cockpits, but definitively for those who want a realistic simulator that caters to a slightly more casual approach, and want a broad array of planes to try without shelling out hundreds of dollars for each. Ideal for novices, but if you're a first time flyer do learn the free Su25T included in DCS world before you commit to buying any DLC plane.

Should also be mentioned that these are the only modern-ish REDFOR planes you're ever going to get in DCS, so tough luck if you wanted those in high-fidelity (thank the russian government for that).

All in all, greatly reccommended, great deal, especially when on sale.

Note: the Su25, Su27, Su33, MiG29, F15, A-10A plane DLCs sold individually are completely identical to the ones in this pack, make sure you don't buy them twice.\t\t\t\t\t
