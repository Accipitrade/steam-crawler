\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPeople found my review of the F/A-18C Hornet module helpful so I thought I'd add a couple more reviews of some of the modules I own.
I first want to say that Steam says I have put around 330 hours into DCS but there is probably an additional 500 to 600 hours added after I switched over to standalone so I hope that helps my credibility.

Overall Opinion:
If you are brand new to Digital Combat Simulator this IS the module you are looking for.

Reasoning:
Flaming Cliffs 3 is a bundle of aircraft from a former Eagle Dynamics game known as Locked On: Modern Air Combat, essentially making this bundle a port from an older game. However, Eagle Dynamics has done a fantastic job of updating these aircraft to seamlessly work in DCS including adding new features, so its really more of a remaster. FC3 (which I will know refer to as for brevity) was my first DCS World module and the one that REALLY sucked me into this simulator.

Unlike the other modules that you will purchase for DCS, these are not clickable cockpit modules meaning that you will be manipulating the cockpit with keybinds, if you are familiar with IL-2 Sturmovik you are familiar with this flight model. BUT PLEASE hear me out before you knock it.

If you don't know what RWR is, BVR, IFF or FCS is, you might want to start here. Here with simple keybinds you don't have to strain your brain to remember which switches to turn on and off and in what order to power your aircraft and its avionics systems. This will give you much more time up in the air learning about how fighter jets operate and how different fighters perform and feel differently much like cars do. On top of this you are getting 7 different aircraft and even more if you include the Chinese J-11 and two East German MiG fighters that were built under licensing from the Soviets. All of this in one package which if you can wait for one of ED frequent sales, can pick this up for 25 USD. To my knowledge all of the aircraft are simulated and portrayed accurately just as their real life counterparts meaning that their radars have blind spots, some of the Russian aircraft have emergency afterburners that can only be used for so long etc. So the only true thing you are missing out on is (of course the biggest and most fun part of DCS) clicking the cockpits.

This is your gateway module into DCS through and through. You will learn about BVR (Beyond Visual Range) IFF (Identify Friend or Foe) so you don't blast your wingman out of the sky. and RWR (Radar Warning Receiver) which helps to give the pilot situational awareness on his surroundings while out of visual range. You will also learn about the different types of guided missiles (Fox 1, 2 & 3) and how to properly employ counter measures. Even onto of all this you'll also learn how your radar, which will pick up your targets long before you can visually see them.

You will learn all of this and more without the hassle of going down a full flight checklist to start up the aircraft and then have to fiddle around in your cockpit to find the right switches and buttons to activate your weapons systems while you're already engaged in a dogfight (I learned from experience).

This is the best value module Eagle Dynamics has to offer and will set you up for great success in your future models once you understand what kind of equipment and avionics you are dealing with. I highly highly doubt you will regret this purchase especially on sale for 25.00 USD.

On a final note that I just remembered, there are many mods for DCS World for free such as the F-22 Raptor which requires FC:3 to function. So if you are into modding DCS, this is another plus in purchasing this stellar module.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFlaming Cliffs gives you 5 simplified planes for DCS for a lower price than the professional models. The set is comparable to the SU-25T that comes free with DCS and the way of operating them is very similar. (Most functions use the same key combinations)

SU-25: getting back to basics
The early version of the SU-25T, in terms of gamplay it is more limited and you lose out on the precision strike capabilities of the Free version. Slightly faster and more maneuverable due to lighter weight. There is no heads-up display, meaning the navigation and targeting of enemies is now more difficult.
Think of it as a more primitive, more challenging SU-25T. NOT recommended if you want/can only afford 1 of the FC3 planes.

SU-27: Plane is fine comrade
Soviet air superiority fighter. It takes some time getting used to and handles in a very peculiar way, but with enough training this thing becomes a monster in dogfights. Compared to the F-15 this plane is far more hands-on, firing a missile at long range requires you to use quite a few buttons and the SU is far easier to push over the limit and crash.
More challenging than the F-15, but in my opinion, also more fun. If you can only afford or want 1 Flaming Cliffs airplane, I recommend getting the SU-33 over of this one.

SU-33: HIGHWAAAAY TO THE KUZNETSOV
Take one SU-27.
- Add the ability to take-off and land on an aircraft carrier
- Add the ability to refuel in mid-air
- Add 2 more weapon pylons
- Add canards to make it look even more menacing
Tada, SU-33! Getting the F-15 or SU-33 is a matter of personal preference.

Mig-29: here for a good time, not a long one
The MIG is the smallest fighter in Flaming Cliffs. It has fewer weapons than the others, is trickier to fly, and it has a far more limited range.
What do you get in return? It goes like a bat out of hell on afterburner, in the right hands it will turn circles around the other options, and you will learn to conserve your fuel!
Basically an SU-27 on meth. If you think more difficult = more rewarding then this plane is for you.

F-15: how to SPAMRAAM 101
The American Fighter option. Very easy to fly, requires very little in terms of airplane management so you can focus on shooting loads of missiles at your enemy. Add in mid-air refueling and you've got a solid package.
Compared to the SU-27/33 the F-15 is easier to handle and more effective at medium to long range. Get this if you want the easiest step into air combat, get the SU or MIG if you want more of a challenge.

A-10, BRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRT
The American counterpart to the SU-25T, with mostly the same capabilities. The A-10 is even more focused on ground attack with lower speed than the SU, whilst being slightly easier to handle in my experience.
Unfortunately the above also means that I cannot recommend this if you can only afford or want a single Flaming Cliffs airplane; its gameplay is too similar to the airplane you get for free that your money is probably better spent on one of the fighter jets. Only get this is you absolutely, positively feel the need to BRRRRRRRT every single tank in the AO. (Accept no substitutes)\t\t\t\t\t
