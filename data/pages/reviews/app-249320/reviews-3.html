\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is very much a first impression by someone who never played DCS before but has put quite some time into other flight games, the most comparable of which is probably VTOL VR. Everything you read happened in VR.

At first, I tried to set up the controls for the SU-27, failed miserably, watched Grim Reapers video on the control setup of the SU-27, found it just detailed enough to be annoyed with the lack of detail without outright quitting (because I use a different stick than he does, it's quite hard to know which binding you will need how much) and eventually managed to set up a mapping that I was confident enough with.

Went into instant action, immediately learnt that I actually need the trim controls quite a lot. Rebound my keys.
Went into the first mission, got shot down by an F5 because I was stupid enough to actually go for head on (partially because I knew ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ about how to use the radar and BVR mode), watched another bunch of Grim Reapers videos (which were a lot better).

Again went for the mission, actually managed to shoot down most things only to eat an AIM-120C from some F-15 Eagle. Well, at least I knew how the missile warning system sounded after that. Also, I remembered that I actually know how to evade Fox-3s and felt kinda stupid for not doing anything.

After another two tries, managed to finish the mission, went for the first campaign mission. Had to look up basic stuff like "How do I turn on the f***ing plane?" or "It's quite breezy, how can I close the canopy?" because I ran out of space for those on my HOTAS.

Did my rounds in the mission, was quite impressed by the graphical fidelity of the cockpit. After 30 minutes of basically nothing noteworthy happening went for the landing. My wingman was so eager to get to the ground before me, he glitched into the ground, was catapulted out of it at stupid speeds and crashed into my jet, making the mission fail.

Frustrated that I just wasted 30 minutes of my life, it closed the game.
10/10, would Su-27-glitch-catapult-die again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVisual and flight models are really good, but actual control systems are so overly simplified it is at the point of some systems not working correctly in favor of simplicity. For example, the ILS on the F-15C guides for where it says a player should turn the aircraft not the actual localizer and glidescope. This oversimplification makes it hard to use if you know how to fly. The only plus is for using use made mods, which the required base aircraft can be purchased at much lower price.
Some say this is a good starting point for people to try out DCS or just getting into it. I disagree. To me the oversimplification of these aircraft would have turns me off to DCS and I never would have purchased anything again for it. The shining point of DCS is the realism of the aircraft, systems, and weapons. Here you do not get any realism in operating the systems. You can't even tell light settings without looking at different views as the switches for the lights don't change when using the keyboard commands. If you're just starting in DCS get a 3rd party standalone high fidelity aircraft to get a feel without risk as they operate better than those included in FC3. If you like it then get a regular full fidelity aircraft on sale. That will give a FAR better feel for DCS than FC3 will provide.\t\t\t\t\t
