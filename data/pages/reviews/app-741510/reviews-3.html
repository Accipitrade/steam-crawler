\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember being amazed at the one-take top-down shootout in John Wick 4 and learned that the director used inspiration from this game for that scene, so naturally I had to check this out.

This game is basically Hotline Miami but set in Hong Kong and with 3d graphics. If you enjoy those games I would recommend getting this at a discounted price. I bought it for Â£4, and whilst it only took me just under 2 hrs to beat, I had a blast playing it. I mean, shooting Triad members in slow motion whilst listening to a badass pulsing soundtrack, what is there not to like? It's like John Woo, John Wick and Max Payne all in one.

There are some negatives. The story is bare bones with a few nitpicky typos in the dialogue text and the boss fights repeat the same loop of running whilst shooting parallel to each other across buildings. As mentioned before, the game is very short so definitely do not buy at full price. However, these minor gripes I have didn't detract from the experience as the gameplay and great soundtrack kept me engaged.

If I had to pick between this game and the Hotline Miami games, I would choose Hotline Miami without a doubt, but if you're after a top-down twinstick shooter you can have fun with for few hours then this is a must buy.

7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall Score: 4/10
Consensus:
Somehow "The Hong Kong Massacre," despite having a promising premise, simple to understand controls, and the set for what should be and could have been the popular successor to the Hotline Miami series,fails to live up to the expectations of that which inspired it, and that which follows its influence

Gameplay:
Obviously having been inspired mainly by the Hotline Miami series, developer Vreski had set out to create a 3D rendition of the games. Ignoring all else, The Hong Kong Massacre could have been enjoyable just through level design, slick graphics, and its frankly awesome shoot-dodge mechanic, as well as its most stylish mechanic: time-slowing. However, while the time-slow option is cool to use when in tight situations (and the game addresses this by punishing your score if you use it in excess), you shouldn't have to use such a mechanic really at all if the levels are designed in such a way where you can plan a course of time-sensitive action (similar to the hotline miami series). This of course what makes an enjoyable mechanic: one you can use for fun, but should not be necessary to complete the game.
However the problem with the mechanic doesn't actually come from the mechanic itself, but how it MUST be used to complete the game. Instead of creating levels that are appropriately hard to match the pacing of the game as well as the environment the player finds themselves in, some levels are made so unreasonably hard (mainly by throwing in just as many enemies as possible) that you would have to develop Shaolin Monk reflexes in order to beat the game without the time-slow option. This of course detracts from the game; as what is considered a "mechanic" ends up becoming just as necessary as the guns.

Story/Writing:
Terrible. The plot is just a rehash of every revenge thriller/action film ever made, and doesn't even do it to any emotional or resonating impact. It would be better if they had just simply put the story as a minimal part of the game, however because of the unskippable dialogue portions you are not only forced to come face to face with unself-aware bad writing, but also with frequent grammatical errors and misspellings that plague the game with an unforgivable stench of both cringe and (an even more unforgivable stench) visible amateur development.

Bugs:
Some of the enemies, when shot, begin to shake violently and clip around like someone were click-and-dragging them around, breaking immersion in the game. There are also incorrectly labeled button-commands (the game will give you the option to press "X" to skip, but you actually have to press "A"). The game also offers you the opportunity to purchase upgrades as time goes on. However, the game will frequently fail to save the upgrades and you will have to repurchase them.

Pros:
The graphics themselves are alright, but what really sells the visuals is the gunplay. All the bullets are highlighted in a neon-esque fashion. When you find yourself in an intense firefight, and you have the thought to time-slow in the middle of it, you won't be disappointed.

TLDR: could have been better, but a combination of visibly amateur programming, bad story, bugs, poor level design, and lazy programming combine to deliver less of a Hong Kong Massacre and more of a Hong Kong Mess.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as this game can be enjoyable, I can't recommend it.
It has some decent surface level writing with forgettable characters you don't really care about, but you're not here for that, you're here for the game play, so my opinion will be primarily based on that.

+ Can be a lot of fun, a decent attempt at a "hotline miami" kinda game
+ Soundtrack isn't bad, one you can grove to and fits the mood of the game
- Movement feels clunky at times, makes dodging and getting around around painful
- Artificial difficulty implemented later in through the use of body armour on enemies
- Game doesn't necessarily get "harder", it mainly only throws more enemies with less cover
- With the point above, the level design is mid, it's pretty uninspired
- Limited weapons, with only 4 firearms available, game play can feel repetitive

So on that note, just play Hotline Miami. You'll get a better story with memorable characters along with a smooth game play loop that feels both rewarding and doesn't get old. I will not be finishing this game as it ran it's course by the "Today" chapter. Maybe pick up on a really big sale if that.\t\t\t\t\t
