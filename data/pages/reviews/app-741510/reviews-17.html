\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"The Hong Kong massacre" is the indie tribute to the 80's-90's John Woo movies like "Hard-Boiled" or "Bullet in the Head", made in the form of a top-down shooter.

To those who are TLTR in their heads: It's a cheap-made indie game. Not bad visually, but very limited by the chosen topic and production costs. Buy it if you really love top-down shooters and 80's Hong Kong-style action movies. In all other cases, there are good chances that you'll turn it down after only a little while.

The story is quite plain: you play for the ex-copper who was "erased" from the life by the local triads but somehow survived and set on the bloody revenge course. And now he can't stop until he clears at least part of the Hong Kong slums from the bad guys, by splattering their blood and lives broad wide and destroying their nests, with the help of some automatic weaponry.

John Woo known trademarks were slow-motion or freeze-frame sequences, as well as some tricky acrobatic stunts during the shootouts when the heroes and their foes alike are constantly in motion, performing bends, rolls, somersaults, and other evasion maneuvers while shooting at each other. We've seen it many times in games and movies after that. "Equilibrium", "Max Payne" and even the action game from John Woo himself, the "Stranglehold". However, when we speak for the top-down shooters, well, all the recent gamers think not farther than "Hotline Miami". Whereas rolling mechanics in the "Hong Kong massacre" make it closer to the old Sega title "True Lies". Get me right there: we have both rolls and slow-mo mechanics in many action games of late, including top-down shooters. Like "Enter the Gungeon" for instance. But you don't rely on those mechanics exclusively, and your enemies in those other games don't seem to perform such tricks as well. Whereas survival and good-being in the "HK Massacre", especially on the latest stages, lies almost entirely on the player's skill with dives, slides and other dodges, while the slow-mo works only as of the additional benefit which can be found useful only from time to time.

Well, since not that many players seen the early '90s games and have no idea what "True Lies" was in action, let us go the trivial path and say that the "HK Massacre" is the game where you see things from the upside like in "Hotline Miami" but move like in "Max Payne" games. There are 5x6 levels, with the additional boss level after each 6 in line. Most of those levels are based on the closed and fixed areas, like several chained rooms, like it was with "Hotline Miami". However, there will be just as many semi-open stages, like the roofs where you are supposed to jump from one building to another, like backstreets and even typical HK local market (except there are no civilians on that market in line of events). Each level you can basically storm in 1-2 minutes... but you'll die many times if you like to rush. So, on the major scale, the "HK Massacre" can be considered even as the slow-paced game if the player takes his time to approach every corner providently. In that line, the boss levels are the most boring. All 5 are built in exactly the same way: the screen is divided into two, you are on the left, the boss is on the right, and you cannot jump to the other half, and the same goes for the boss. So you just shoot each other, moving forward (up) until the boss runs out of health, or until he gets you. Enemy infantrymen appear in your half of the arena as an additional irritant as you progress with the boss ... but this is just one more reason not to rush forward headlong, but to try to hold the boss in place, shooting him from one spot while it is possible. And that's all. There are no original patterns, strong feelings, at least something of notion in those boss fights. You simply stuff 5 thick mobs with lead. This is really even worse than the bosses-helicopters that have long been the bummer in the shooter games.

Same feeling the player can gather for all the lack of variety that can be traced in this game, where there are slight differences only in the layout of the locations that you and the bandits will smash into chips within a couple of minutes to a level ... where the bandits are actually distinguished only by the chosen weapon (one of 4 available) and the presence or absence of armor (armored mobs needs 2 hits).

The game is very narrow in its positives, and it lacks some deep hooks to get the player attached. There are no actual characters to hold player's emotions, the line of levels can be actually rearranged in every way, as there are no story-based events behind them (like it was with Max Payne, for example...). Except for one level, maybe, the first on the last day. But it hardly counts as the neglect of my point. The variety of situations in the game is based on the layout of the levels only: you can dodge to the left or right, sweep the levels clockwise or counter-clockwise (or you'll start in one way, then turn back and go for the rest of mob from other direction). But that's it. That's all that game has. The author tried to add some variety with the 3 difficulty levels... But the "medium" and "hard" are working exactly the same way (wee-bit tweaked timers for the speed of enemies don't really count). Except you'll not the good half of achievements for playing on "hard", as those achievements are only fixed to the "medium" difficulty.

All in all, the game's not utterly bad: if you want to blow off some steam, shooting bad guys in the flips and slides - you'll get it. But even John Woo's movies were much deeper than just gunfights. And "Hong Kong Massacre" only provides some shooting time. So I'd give it 5.9 out of 10 points, and suggest to buy it with the discount or in the bundle. It's not bad, it's not too good... Can't really recommend it, but can't turn it down, since it's fair in what it does: the gunplay there is profound as we see in the trailers and I got what I was paying for. Except I still prefer the old good "True Lies" more, for the diversity. But that's just me.

All you need to know as the bottom line is that you should not get your hopes up with this game.
