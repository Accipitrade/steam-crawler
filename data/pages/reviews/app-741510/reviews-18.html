\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat Game kinda the Hotline Miami feel but not exactly the same.

Pros:
-Weapon system and difficulty are a bit slow but pretty well balanced
-Combat feels smooth except for some minor things.
-Cutscenes are a bit blurry but for the most part the effects strenghten the cinematic cutscenes even more.

Cons:
-Sound adjustment is Point perfection ingame Sound, Cutscenes and level and Gun unlocks are all at a different height level which can be very unpleasant to the ears sometimes.
-Cutscene Characters are Recycled its pretty much always the same sprite with glasses or minor changes.
-The enemies feel smooth for the most part unless they are offscreen where u are not able to hit them and once they enter the screen they will target you which outside of slowmo means death. Sometimes The KI tracks you trough walls even if u havent even shot in the level yet and will preaim you trough walls which also without slow mo means certain death 9/10 times. (this just happens in certain levels or certain times so its excusable)

Result:
All in all id give this game a 5/10 for the Minor KI Malfunctions and sometimes stupidly precise aims trough walls. And since im an Achivement hunter and Perfectionist i have to include a -2 for "everyone is sleeping" (Kill 10k enemies) and -1 for bad graphics, -2 for the lack of story.

It could have been a good game all in all but these little things really make it seem kinda put together for the whopping price of 16,78€ out of sale.
Honestly i got the game on sale for about 7-8€ and it really isn´t worth more in my opinion there has been so many little things left unchecked that just in the end ruin the experience that other titles (that are far older) like hotline miami used to achive.

Edit: Turns out the Achivement "everyone is sleeping" (10k Kills) only counts per playthrough and not crossplaythrough as i have 7,2k on medium and 3,2k on hard and i dont have it (+1 deleted playthrough medium) that kinda really is just lazy programing to base the achivement of an ingame enemy counter per playthrough ... .\t\t\t\t\t
