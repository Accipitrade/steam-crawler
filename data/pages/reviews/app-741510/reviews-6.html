\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a love letter to all the greatest Hong Kong action films of the 90's such as Hard Boiled, The Killer, Bullet in the Head, A Better Tomorrow, and more. Fans of John Woo will recognize his signature slow motion and Chow Yun Fat's stylish dive-and-slide gun play in this game. There are different paths you can take in completing each level and when the bullets start flying everywhere, you will feel like you're actually in a Hong Kong triad shoot out. Playing just a few levels of The Hong Kong Massacre got my adrenaline pumping so much that I had to re-watched Hard Boiled again and relive that HK movie magic of the 90's.

Pros:
1. You start with the classic TWO GUNS but you can unlock new firearms such a rifle or semi-automatic
2. The firearms you obtained are upgradable after completing each level
3. When you dive or slide, you're almost impossible to get hit so the game encourage you to do so often
4. You can drop you weapons and always pick up the enemy's gun after you took them out.

Cons:
Boss character took many hits to kill so it gets repetitive as you retry to boss level over and over again.
Price is a bit high for this type of game so I would wait to buy it on sale

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen it works it's some of the most cinematic gun fights that I've ever had in a top down shooter. When it bugs out it's some of the most frustrating ♥♥♥♥ you can imagine.

Things you need to know before you buy this:
-Enemies are deaf, but have 360 eyes, the reaction time of a ♥♥♥♥♥ up house fly and the precision of a marksman
-Enemy placement is unreliable and paths are pretty unpredictable
-Weapons have no recoil and shoot like laser beams (Except the shotgun which is all over the place)
-Reloading an empty weapon is instantly done, a not empty weapon should NEVER be reloaded
-Upgrades for weapons only apply to the player wielding them, not the enemies
-A fully upgraded SMG is basically a dual minigun, a fully upgraded shotgun still a piece of ♥♥♥♥
-NEVER dodge backwards in the line of fire, you will eat a bullet 4 out of 5 times
-Take a tranquilizer before you do a roof mission (Especially the last one)

There's no story and what it has should've been told top down (The 3D scenes look like crap). The "music" consists of maybe 2-3 very similar and monotone tracks and gets muted after 5 minutes.

You can ace it in about 20 hours. Clearing levels without slowmotion is IMO a lot easier than with it. Out of the 20 hours 5 were spent achieving the precision goal of each level (No missed shots).

A nice time killer, but I won't play it again. Hotline Miami is in another league with an interesting story, intriguing art style and banging soundtrack.\t\t\t\t\t
