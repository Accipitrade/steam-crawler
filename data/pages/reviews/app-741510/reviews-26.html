\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHIS REVIEW IS FOR ANYONE WHO PLAYED JOHN WOO'S STRANGLEHOLD! PLEASE READ.

Tl;DR: Not a bad game, but not what I was expecting and I'm left rather disappointed.

Wanna know my top 5 favorite games of all time? In no particular order?

Ratchet and Clank
Alien: Isolation
Spartan: Total Warrior
Metro 2033
John Woo's Stranglehold

Pushing Hotline Miami to the side for a moment, because this game, despite playing like it, has almost no representation of a game like Hotline Miami as it does a game like John Woo's Stranglehold.

Getting the obvious out of the way, this is a clear inspiration of John Woo's Stranglehold, but with a different story. There is absolutely NO shame in it. In fact, there's a lot of respect to be given here. It takes a particular taste to not only like Hard Boiled as a movie, but recognizing the genius of choreography and theatrics that the video game, John Woo's Stranglehold, posesses.

When I first stumbled on this game two years ago, it immediately re-ignited my old memories of Stranglehold and even prompted a re-download and playthrough.

Admittedly, Stranglehold doesn't hold up in today's market, unfortunately. It's a sub par shooter, but with AMAZING mechanics and stunning theatrics to create a smoothe, seamless, episodic catastrophe of every room you went into... and it was beautiful.

I will NEVER forget the time, during the Jazz Level, I jumped backwards, in slow motion of course, without realizing there was a beverage cart behind me. I rolled backwards on that cart unloading my pistols into the four enemies and finishing with a crescnedo of rolling RIGHT into a fishtank, catapulting myself though it, and immediately backing up against the cover the fish tank stand provided me as bullets whizzed overhead.

While there is a certain amount of nostalgia that comes from games like that, the reality is that I can go back and RELIABLY relive that moment... as many times as I want, and it'll never get old.

So... The Hong Kong Massacre: A heartfelt, but over-all dull, attempt to rekinder the experience of John Woo's Stranglehold. I can't so in full honesty that I didn't WANT this game to be a top-down Stranglehold, but I also didn't expect it to be. That said, there is SOME expectation to land that quintessential John Woo flair... and it simply isn't there.

Graphics: Not bad. Not great, but where it needs to be as a baseline. I'm not sure why, but the trailers seemed a whole lot flashier and... sparkier? Unfortunately, the game feels incredibly unstylized... which is a MAJOR detriment to any game with John Woo inspiration.

Sound: As an audiophile, I'm not a fan of this game's sound AT ALL. It's very tinny, repetitive, and the music is very lack-luster, uninspired, and frankly, unfitting.

Gameplay: Again, not bad. Not great, but where it needs to be as a baseline. I can't criticze how it plays all too much because there's only so much a twin stick shooter can do. That said, how it plays OUT? Very bland. It's a game of cube rooms, and the gameplay loop got tiresome very fast. You jump through a door everytime, turn on slowmo (in hopes of feeling like a bad ass), and one-tapping whichever enemy is in that room. On that note, the controls are stiff as a board with botched acceleration. I found myself dragging my mouse way more than I expected to.

In summary: This game was an attempt. A inspired one, but an unfruitful and unergetic one at that.

Who should buy this?

If you like games like Hotline: Miami, this is a great game for you to notch on the bed post.

If you loved John Woo's Stranglehold, like I did, and are capable of recounting very similar memories like that of mine on the Jazz Level, I'd steer far away from this game as it's NOT what you're expecting or hoping for it to be.

I wish Vreski the best. I really do, and I also give them my utmost respect for the valiant effort at an homage. It's not unseen, and it's very much appreciated.

I CANNOT recommend this game, and I'm very disappointed to report that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRight now I have a very mixed feeling about the game. I've been waiting for it since the first trailer. I wanted to like it. But something just doesn't click.

You play as a character who kills enemies in one shot and dies in one hit. That is cool. But the game offers you almost infinite slomo and the weapons that shoot without any spread. Out of 4 weapons in total, the starting pistols are the best choice since they shoot fast, bullets fly straight and kill with one hit, so why bother with anything else? The core gameplay starts to feel simple.

It looks like the devs thought about it and decided to complicate things but in a very irritating way. When you dive and dodge bullets in slowmo, your mouse sensitivity drops so it becomes harder to aim. That mechanic is really frustrating because it feels like you are loosing control of your character.

Minor details:
When you die you have to point and click with the mouse the restart button instead of, say, "press spacebar to restart".
Right now there is no way to rebind controls or change the graphic options. I understand the artstyle but I would love to kill that chromatic aberrations and bind the slowmo to left shift, Max Payne style.
The soundtrack is good overall but some songs are too uplifting and remind me of the SIms. Which is an odd choice for the dark and bloody game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat you see in the trailers is what you get. Top down shooters, in the spirit of Max Payne and John Woo action movies like Hard Boiled.

The game is very challenging (everyone dies from 1 shot, you and the enemies).

Good things

- Good graphics
- Great particle effects, ragdolls and blood
- Some destructible environment. Overall shooting guns feels powerful, because of all this
- AI isn't just running at you like idiots.. they are surprisingly smart, and try to avoid dying
- Good music, although this is subjective (possible to switch off)
- Replay value, in form of challenges (no slow motion, no missed shots, complete under par time)
- Challenging, prepare to die often. It's not as easy as it looks in gameplay videos, those are players who know what they're doing

Criticism

- Apparently the game supports gamepad, but I haven't gotten mine working. Maybe because I also have a wheel in my system... sometimes it causes games to not understand that gamepad is connected
- Keybinds can't be changed. They are good though (WASD + R, E, space, mouse)
- Some typos in menus. Easy to fix though (for example calling magazines "clips")
- Enemies start in same places every time, so it will limit maybe replay value. They move around though, and once engaged in firefight, they move smarter than in most games, reacting dynamically into what you do

The game has 4 guns (pistol, shotgun, SMG, assault rifle). Each can be upgraded in many different ways. But since all guns kill in 1 bullet anyway, I don't know what extra more guns would bring.. so wouldn't say it's criticism. Now they have meaningful differences

Highly recommended. If you're unsure, just see some gameplay videos. If you like what you see, you'll like the game, because that's basically what you get.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been waiting for this game since I first saw the trailer a couple of years ago. It has a lot of elements I love and I am happy to say it seems to be living up to what I was hoping for.

This reminds me a lot of Stranglehold, in a John Woo style environment, with over the top action and Hotline Miami style gameplay. The additions of slow motion and dodging make it a little less stressful than HM and more cinematic. The particle effects, physics based death animations and destructible environment make everything feel very visceral. The controls are surprisingly simple and while initially I struggled with them, after about 5 minutes of dying over and over again on the 2nd level, I started to get the hang of it and that's where the fun really kicks in.

So far I've played through about 9 levels including a boss battle, which I found to be a fun challenge and mild departure from the previous levels. The levels are getting more interesting and I'm hoping there are plenty more. The enemy AI is challenging and can dodge bullets just like you can. Expect to die a lot, particularly early on. Fortunately, just like Hotline Miami, you can reset and start over almost immediately.

As for negatives -- the story is not exceptionally well written (may be some translation issues -- however, for me, I really don't care about the story in a game like this, I just want to shoot stuff), there are some typos in the menus, and I wish there were more graphics options so I could really crank up the fidelity. Each level has 3 challenges to complete, but unfortunately, they are the same for every level, which makes it slightly uninteresting to chase.

WIth that said, I love over the top action and games with lots of physics and stylized violence so this game is everything I had hoped for (as long as there are plenty of levels). Honestly, if you like what you see in the trailer... that's pretty much what the game is. It's well marketed and lives up to what they are advertising -- although it is also very challenging and so it may take a while to get to the point of controlling the game as fluidly as you see in the trailers.

Depending on the overall length of the game, it may or may not be worth the asking price. For me, it definitely is, and if it goes on sale for 40-50% off it would be a steal in my opinion - assuming you like these kind of games.

Finally, if I recall correctly, I believe this game was only made by 2 people. Definitely a very small team, which only makes the game that much more impressive.\t\t\t\t\t
