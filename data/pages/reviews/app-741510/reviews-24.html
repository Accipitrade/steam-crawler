The Hong Kong Massacre is a fast paced top-down shooter that honors cult John Woo movies of the Hong Kong period, which is set in the late 80s - early 90s. In this game, the player gets a chance to take a role of the former police detective who will not stop until he brings an act of revenge for the murder of his partner.


Pros:

+ Pretty satisfying gameplay
+ Ability to upgrade your weapons and make them more powerful
+ Stunning visuals with tons of colorful effects and explosions
+ A slow motion effect looks amazing and feels really like in its own place
+ Nonstop action all the time
+ Online Leaderboards
+ Good amount of a challenge

Cons:

- Only four types of weapons, where two of them are kinda useless
- No skills or perks to purchasing for your character
- Completely boring and dull boss levels
- Cutscenes have a very low quality
- Copy pasted level design
- Becomes repetitive pretty fast
- Slow motion and dashing mechanics make this game super easy
- Pretty shallow story
- Weird animation, especially when dashing forward

This is just another game, that I would be really happy to put a neutral score. You know, something that will help people to understand, that I don’t either love it or I hate it, I just stay neutral and I can only recommend to grab this kind of a game during the sale. And since we don’t have such an option on Steam, I will just explain it here thorough about its highs and lows.

First of all, it’s very important to state that this game has absolutely nothing to do with Hotline Miami. I really hate when people try to compare different games when they have just a small similarity. In general, these games are completely different and the only thing they have in common is that fact they are both top-down shooters with one-shot kill enemies. And that’s it. What is really much more important to state is that this game can be a perfect choice for someone, who is a big fan of John Woo action movies and Hong Kong vibes.

Story

The Hong Kong Massacre doesn’t try to focus that much on its story. It shows us a scene where the protagonist is surrounded by police sirens and his face is all covered in blood. This cutscene shows a dramatic feel of the game and tries to set the right mood by showing us how concerned that guy is about something. The story of our protagonist will unfold eventually by showing us these short cutscenes between the missions and telling us what happened on the previous few days until the present day.

Gameplay

In terms of gameplay, The Hong Kong Massacre is a game, where you have to clean every level without being shot. Someone who already tried the games that follow the same approach will know, that it’s not that easy as it may seem at first glance. However, it is much easier simply because the player is allowed to use special moves that greatly help him during his playthrough. These abilities work as an homage to classical Joh Woo movies, where the protagonists dash all the time during the gunfights and the slow motion turns on to make everything looks more effectively at the most intense scenes.

There is a twist, of course. You can use these abilities to make your life easier and enjoy your game to the fullest and then it will become just a nice small casual run that will last no longer than a few hours. Dashing makes you invincible and slow motion helps you to perfectly aim at your enemies. But the thing is that every single level has a score and if you really love a challenge and want to get three stars on every level, then you will have to do your best and spend some time on it. These challenges require you to never use slow motions, hit enemies only and complete the levels for a certain amount of time. And trust me, this is very difficult and it will take some time for you to finish them all perfectly.

You will need those stars to upgrade your weapons, so this is something that will motivate you to complete at least some of them on the highest score. These starts can be spent to increase the weapon’s fire rate, ammo size or movement speed, etc. Also, these weapon upgrades are the only thing that can be improved in this game, no skills or special perks for the protagonist at all.

Speaking of weapons, I can say that I was a bit disappointed that this game can only offer four types of weapons and two of them never really felt like something useful. Maybe, it’s a matter of taste, but still, they really felt a bit underpowered. I was also expecting to see some grenades and maybe steel arms but it never happened.

The coolest thing about The Hong Kong Massacre is that this game has very addictive and satisfying gameplay. Developers were able to create a good high-quality product, where the player is protected from the long loading screens or watching some continuous boring scenes. Everything happens very fast in this game and you can always feel like that action will never stop at all. It’s very hard to stop playing until you get to the end of the level and you always promise yourself that it’s going to be “one last run”. All these shooting effects and destructible environments perfectly fit the game's atmosphere and definitely add some extra charm to this game as well.

However, there is a problem with the level design. All the levels feel like they were copy-pasted or changed just a little bit to make them look different. I even noticed that some of the levels have absolutely similar rooms as they used to be on the previous ones and that really made me sad, felt like developers were just too lazy to create something more. And this is why the game becomes pretty repetitive after the first hour - you just start to notice that all the levels are pretty much the same and the only difference is the position and the number of the enemies that you have to take down. So the challenge becomes the only thing that keeps you in the game.

The Hong Kong Massacre has a boss fight levels at the end of each chapter and they look completely dull and once again - they are all the same, repeating the idea of each other all the time. Your task is to run along the long narrow corridors and chase the boss, who is trying to cover from you under the different boxes, walls, and metallic constructions. That was completely ridiculous and when I saw that they did this with all the bosses in each chapter, I was very disappointed.

Graphics

This game looks very good visually, a lot of great minor details on the levels and all these special effects and destructible environments are mindblowing. Sometimes, it can be too much and it’s hard to see flying bullets with all these neverending fireworks. Also, the game cutscenes were really bad, this is something that has been absolutely ignored by the developers and they have a completely low quality and poor execution.

Sound

I really enjoyed the music presented in The Hong Kong Massacre, these tunes definitely fit the atmosphere. They can become a bit repetitive if you are stuck on the level for a long time, especially if you restart the same level again and again. I guess there were only two levels where the music was kinda weird to me with these funky motives that felt odd with this Chinese thematics.

Conclusion

The Hong Kong Massacre is a good game for someone who loves fast paced shooters with a challenge and John Woo movies. I would recommend to grab it during the sale only because of its repetitive feel.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the price, it's not worth it. You'd be better off playing RUINER or Hotline miami if you want the frantic action experience. This is maybe a 5$ game at best. Maybe

The controls are poor. It's a brilliant idea, but flawed in execution. Everytime you want to do the dodge jump, good luck actually controlling it. It'll launch you over cars, through windows you weren't near, into enemies that you were trying to get away from. The input lag on the controls doesn't do this problem any favors.

Perhaps the biggest issue I ran into, was just how boring it was. In my limited playtime, I beat the first "batch" of missions, consisting of stiff cutscenes animated in game, random renderings of the character driving places, the WORST boss battle experience I've had in recent memory, and rapidly becoming stale gameplay.

There are only 4 guns in the game: The pistol, the AR, the shotgun, and the SMG. Once you finish two or three levels, you might find yourself with the points to buy the unlimited ammo upgrade for your pistol. This makes the game extremely trivial as you can just gun down everything in the general direction, because there are "helpful" red triangles at the border of your screen telling you where enemies are coming from. The first couple missions took me a few tries, but I found it was more due to the controls messing everything up, than the game being difficult.

For four years of development I expected more from this title. The game and menus are riddled with spelling errors, the dialogue is stale, the gunplay would be so much better if there wasn't lag, and the story was generic, poorly written, and most of all boring. These things wouldn't be a problem if the gameplay held it up, but unfortunately, it doesn't.

Unless it gets majorly fixed and re-developed, avoid this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter waiting for a long time for this game, I finally got to beat it. Granted, I would've been able to beat this in one day if it didn't come out so late at night. Would I replay this game? Perhaps, if more support came to this game by the developers.

Despite it coming out riddled with one or two bugs, it scratched the itch for a top-down Max Payne/Hotline Miami-esque shooter that I needed. Is it worth the $20? I don't think it does, so it would be best to get this game during a sale at about half price. If the devs somehow added a workshop to this, I wouldn't stop playing what people could come up with. Something to ponder down the line.

The story is somewhat lackluster, but the gameplay shines in its gunplay. However, the weapon types serve very little difference aside from movement speed, bullet capacity, and personal preference. You could beat the whole game with just akimbo pistols with enough effort—but a little variety in the weapons' differences (such as damage, but they all do the same way against all enemies including ones with vests) wouldn't hurt.

I may be biased thanks to my love for Hong Kong action films, so I would definitely recommend this game. On sale.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are having INPUT LAG, TURN OFF VSYNC.

I'm seeing a lot of reviews that are complaining about the input lag, but it is completely non-existent if you turn off vsync.


ALSO, NEVER USE THE SLOW-MO BUTTON IF YOU WANT THIS GAME TO BE FUN

---------------------------------------


Let's start by adjusting our expectations. This isn't a AAA game. It is two guys who spent 6ish years developing this game. It also <$20.

The Drawsbacks

-The story is an excuse to justify the gameplay, which, for a two-person team without a writing staff, makes a good deal of sense.

-The AI is limited, but so was Hotline Miami. This game is meant to be played fast, so it doesn't really cause any issues for me.

-Not a lot of variety in weapon choice. You can get dual-wielding handguns, a shotgun, an SMG, or a rifle and there are upgrades you can purchase for each that are identical. No knives, swords, bats, anything melee, or anything else. That's because it is a two-person team. If this game takes off, I hope these would be added in the sequel.

-The slowtime ability is OP and virtually infinite.


-The mission scoring system is a huge stepdown from Hotline Miami and does restrict replayability. I wish it was a lot more complex, but that is, again, a problem resulting from the small dev team that could be fixed in a sequel.


The Draws

-Graphics are surprisingly fantastic.

-The combat is like a top-down Max Payne. Visceral, intense, and rewarding.

-The limited mission scoring does add replayability in that it is annoying to only receive 1 star and so I might replay a level to make it a shorter run, do a no-slowmo run, or a perfect shooting run. Getting all 3 stars at the same time is pretty thrilling, although the game doesn't acknowledge it.


The Conclusion:

Got $20 burning a whole in your pocket? Did you like Max Payne, Hotline Miami, and/or Ruiner? Want to support a small dev team and still get a fun, fast paced visceral shooter?

Then, this game is for you! Don't go in expecting Hotline Miami or you will probably be a bit disappointed. But, if you enter the game with an open-mind and acknowledge how small the dev team is, then I think you will find it to be very enjoyable! What excites me most is the potential for this dev team. If this game is good enough to warrant a sequel, the dev team should be able to hire more staff. There is a lot that could be improved in this game and with a solid basis, the sequel will undoubtably be a fantastic game.\t\t\t\t\t
