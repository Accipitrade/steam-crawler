\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very sparse, direct experience that strains the boundaries of how much content, story, variety, and gameplay depth are required to prop up a half-dozen-ish hours of play. You should go in understanding that. Understanding that there are only 4 weapons and about that many varieties of enemy grunt to shoot, and that the context for levels will be as boilerplate as you could possibly imagine, and that it becomes difficult to tell whether or not environments are being wholesale reused or if everything just looks that samey, and that once you master the simplistic rhythm of dodging and activating bullet time there's little else you'll have to learn or adapt to.

If you understand all that, damned if The Hong Kong Massacre isn't a fun little game. Because there is one thing that it does really well, and that one thing is the glorious GLORIOUS particle effects. Shootouts are, put simply, a spectacle. A spectacle on par with Hard Boiled, and yes a spectacle that surpasses that of Stranglehold and even FEAR.

What you are getting here is a simple Hotline Miami-pattern top down shooter, with basic but acceptable combat and decent flow, and some of the most jaw dropping particle effects and environmental destruction on the market.

Does that sound worth the price to you?

Well it was worth the price to me.\t\t\t\t\t

Gameplay is quite repetitive, Upgrades to weapons don't really feel that important (so why bother doing it), Challenges are the same for every level and basically take away the "fun" mechanics (don't use slowmo, only do oneshot kills) of the game; Bosses are the same encounter again and again. And last but not least gunplay also feels weird because of collision problems - Walking through a door/jumping through a window etc. and gunning down people simply doesnt quite work because your bulltes get stuck somewhere (that happend several times for me).
Overall I'd say it's "meh" even though I really hoped this was nice.\t\t\t\t\t
