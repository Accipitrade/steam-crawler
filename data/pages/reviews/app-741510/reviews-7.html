\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe reason to play this game is for the dual pistols with the unlimited ammo upgrade, which can be obtained early by saving up stars instead of spending them on other weapons and upgrades. It's great fun blasting away as you go, at destructible doors and windows and the enemies behind them, without having to watch the ammo counter apart from reloading.

Otherwise, it plays a bit like Hotline Miami, but focused on guns. A bit less forgiving in some ways, like enemy accuracy and the lack of a lock-on system. A bit more forgiving in others, like the boss fights with a fair amount of flexibility. And the trouble enemies often have tracking your character if you pick a direction and strafe in it. But do pick a direction, you'll get lit up if you double back, unless you're ducking behind cover.

Going back to run the missions again for all stars adds some replay value. It's here that you'll have to care about making every shot count, and beating par times. On a first run through, I don't use the slow-motion button, so that I can use it for these remaining challenges.

Verdict - Eight doves out of ten.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly, I'm only rating this as a non-recommended title, as there's no 'neutral' option. So I'm rating it factoring in its standard/non-discounted price. (Which is $30AUD at the time of writing this)

In any case, overall this is a pretty unremarkable top-down shooter. The graphics are decent and the core gameplay (notably the bullet-time) is fun. However, it's not a particularly long game - I finished it in under four hours, then reran some levels a bunch of times.

Additionally, the AI isn't particularly noteworthy. It's pretty accurate in gunfights, so you'll have a nice challenge if you're trying to get bonus points for not using bullet-time, however it's also dumb as bricks. So for instance, if there are two people chatting in a room at the other end of a hallway, if you fire and take one out, the surviving guy will keep standing there for a bit, before deciding to step over his mate's corpse to walk over to a window. No reaction whatsoever, unless you get close enough and they spot you through a window, or if you enter the room they're in.

So overall, there isn't really a lot to say about the game. I actually had a bit of fun with it, however there are definitely better options in the genre. If you're considering getting this at full price, I strongly discourage you from doing so. Simply do what I did and wishlist the game, then wait until it's 75% off before jumping in. It's good to spend some time blowing through, however it's not at all worth it at its current price tag.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game shouldn't be "Mostly positive". It's at least a "Very positive" one.

The game brings you the hotline miami gameplay into a very nice non-pixelated graphics version. Maps are really well designed, levels get progressively more difficult as you play and gunplay feels nice.

Don't get me wrong, it is a hard game ... but a balanced hard game. It's not being unfair with you, and with every level you pass, it only slightly pushes your limits to become bette. The game has replay value since you can improve time, perform speedruns etc.

Overall a really good game, and I wish we see more of this in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDid you ever want to play the gun levels of "Hotline Miami" but with a Hong Kong action movie scenario? Then this game is exactly what you want, though it's not very good. You will always die in 1 hit no matter what unless you abuse the slow motion combined with the dive dodge which makes you briefly immune to bullets, and a lot of fights can be won by shooting at off angles through doors and windows. For a twin stick shooter, it controls remarkably poorly with a controller.

TBH if you want this style of game just get "Hotline Miami" or "12 is Better Than 6" and if you want HK inspired games, there is always the "Max Payne" series or you can pick up "Stranglehold" on GOG. All of these options are much cheaper.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I had to sum up this game I'd say it's Hotline Miami meets the movie "Hardboiled." Or, "Hotline Miami but way more guns, slightly more forgiving, and with invulnerability frames and slow-motion." It's a lot of fun, though relatively easy to blaze through if you're a veteran of Hotline. Worth £10-15 for a good violent romp.

Now to the more nuanced part: A lot of negative reviews seem to be concerning the bizarre rating system this game uses. I agree with them on that point, but it's not as bad as they make out. Basically, each level has three things you can do to earn up to 3 stars: Complete the level under the par time; Complete the level without using bullet time; Complete the level without missing a single shot.

Completing levels quickly gels well with the spirit of the game; the other two do not. While not using bullet time or missing shots are good, fun challenges to come back to, they don't capture the chaotic "Hardboiled" cinematic feel that makes this game shine. The devs messed up putting those challenges front-and-centre. Fortunately, they can be safely ignored. You can quickly focus on one gun (I'd recommend the SMG) and get it fully upgraded within a few levels. Make good use of the bullet time and get into a flow state, and you should find yourself completing most levels under par time; you will be swimming in stars and there's no pressure to grab the other level achievements if you don't want to.\t\t\t\t\t
