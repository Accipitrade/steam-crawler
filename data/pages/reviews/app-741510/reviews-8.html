\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the idea and concept of this game. This is a one man army top down shooter.
The story is simple: you are a former Hong Kong detective whose partner was killed by the mob, so you go on a revenge killing spree to take out every gangster in Hong Kong. From the botton to the top of the mob piramid.
The graphics are very good and also the soundtrack. The game implements the slow motion ability like in Max Payne games and Matrix, which is always fun and adrenalinic.

But man, this is one of the hardest and most punishing game I ever played.
Fact 1: All enemies have perfect aim.
Fact 2: You have 1 health point in each mission.
So the very first time you get shot, BUM - DEAD. Replay the level again ad nauseam.
Also, there is a very frustrating modality of getting points in order to unlock weapon upgrades.
When you complete a mission ( there are 35 in total, 7 x 5 weeks ) you will NOT get any points for completing it.
So how do you get your upgrade points? You get them by completing incredibly hard challenges. And the challenges are the same for every mission. 1) Complete the mission under the specific time. 2) Complete the mission without using the slow motion. ( why implementing an interesting ability only to punish the player for using it ? ), 3) Complete the mission by not wasting a single bullet.
You can imagine how hard this challenges are. Of course, at the beginning those are doable, but as you progress they get brutally hard. Because the maps get bigger and filled with more enemies. So often you are surrounded and the game feels more like a bullet hell then a top down shooter.
The margin of error is incredibly small.
This modality took away all the fun, because if you want to get as many stars/upgrade points as yiz cab, you must repeat each of the missions countless times and you get mad and frustrated.
The game would be much better withouth this challenges. The developers should have invented another way of getting upgrade points and should let the game flow graciously. It should let the gamer enjoy the John Wick-like action withouth having to think: "Oh ♥♥♥♥, I missed a bullet!" "O ♥♥♥♥, the time is up!", etc.
Ok, the fact is, if you don't care about challenges, you can beat the game withouth many problems using slow motion all the time and not caring to land headshots. So, it is not a problem to beat the game ( however, that is also debatable - getting upgrade points to upgrade the best weapon - the Uzi, is preferable ), but if you are a completionist like me, then you are in for a very though ride.
Also, there are balance issues: 1) There are 4 weapons at your disposal, some are way more effective then others. 2) I was being killed 90% of the times by shotgun enemies, because they would spread the bullets far and wide and it is difficult to evade them.
The developers should have clearly stated in the marketing of the game that this is INTENTIONALLY A DIFFICULT GAME. The 1 health point fact should be in the game description, or at least in all those "objective" Steam reviews out there.
I don't like when the truth about games, the very basic premises are left out from the marketing, so that's why I'm recommending against this game.\t\t\t\t\t

PROS:
-A tried and true formula, this is Max Payne meets Hotline Miami. Another similar game is My Friend Pedro. Bullet time, diving and dodging, dual handguns Hong Kong Blood Opera goodness.
-Great aesthetics. It captures the 90's Hong Kong vibe very well. Chow Yun Fat would enjoy this game. John Woo probably has a great nephew who helped design this game, who knows?
-It's a game where you can play quickly and advance quickly. Great for short bouts of gaming.
-There is a challenge and unlock system, which adds a little bit more depth to the game.


CONS:
-Levels are short. Not too big of a deal, when you got to do challenges such as never miss an enemy so I get that design choice. Just wish there were some longer (Hotline Miami length) levels
-Lacks a bit of depth. Could have more guns. More unlocks. More characters. More abilities. Things like that
-Music is 70/30 hit or miss. Some of the songs are great, others seem a little out of place

Other than that, it's worth it to buy on sale. I got it for $4.99 and think it's definitely worth the money. I'm not sure about full price though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed this game for a while. I played through on Easy as I knew how difficult the game was going to be and just wanted a casual experience to enjoy the visuals and gunplay. I finished that and moved to Medium difficulty, and then I started noticing bugs and/or odd design choices. They were always present - I had learned to live with the quirks on Easy difficulty but with the increased difficulty came increased frustration.

Enemies have this ability where they can do rolls the instant you pull the trigger, letting them dodge your bullets. Fair enough, I guess. But they can also instantly get back up at any point in the roll, meaning they will sometimes just appear to twitch slightly or do a handstand and then nail you with a shotgun blast. Certain enemies take two hits to kill, and in between the two hits they will roll, but they have the same ability to instantly jump back up.

Shotguns have a "random" scatter pattern which is impossible to predict.

One of the most infuriating design choices is the rooftop jump. To jump across gaps, you need to use a valuable roll/slide, which prevents you from dodging when you land due to the cooldown. This means that enemies will often just blast you as soon as you get across. And you can't camp at the edge of the rooftop to kill them, because the range of your gun is just short of the enemy position.

Enemies on higher difficulties roll faster than Sonic and do 180-degree pirouettes better than a prima ballerina.

Enemies have the ability to shoot through glass. No, not like that - they can literally stick the barrels of their guns through glass panes and shoot at you without breaking the glass. Unlike other games this has a number of quite severe negative effects on gameplay beyond being simply immersion breaking.

Markers which indicate living enemies will sometimes attach themselves to dead enemies, making you loop around levels needlessly and waste time.

Those are the issues I've faced, but the game isn't all bad. As noted the slow-motion is really good and you aren't really held back by a long recharge time. The whole game is very cinematic despite being top-down, which definitely adds to the "coolness". Challenges do limit the game somewhat, forcing you to play in a particular way. Honestly I didn't think this was too bad because it increases replayability. There are certain issues with the "no miss" challenge where it isn't clear if you've missed a shot until you finish a level - so it would have been nice to include some way of instantly restarting if you miss once.

If you are interested in this game I do still recommend it despite the issues I had, but only if it is on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot a bad game. In fact, at its best moments, it's quite addictive and feels really cool to play - like Hotline Miami meets the Matrix. It's a shame that those moments get less and less frequent. Though the mechanics all work quite well most of the time (stressing *most*, random enemy dodge rolls and clipping are quite big problems) and there's nothing capital w "Wrong" with the way this is put together, I found the game was getting really tedious toward the end. This is partially due to the reuse of the same environments again and again, partially due to the lack of much musical variation at all (the game has about 4, serviceable but awful after you've heard em for a while, tracks in total), partially due to the goddawful story (not what anyone is here for, I know, but it doesn't help), and partially due to the seemingly random difficulty of levels. Although only running to 5 hours, this combined made me really not like the last 2 of those hours.

The "progression" systems aside from just finishing levels break this game, requiring at least two playthroughs of every level (one where you don't miss a single shot, and one where you don't use slow mo - you're never going to manage both at once), and fundamentally making the game not that fun to play. Everything is too fast to comprehend without slow-motion, and shooting entirely accurately removes all the spectacle from the game entirely. Very bad design decision.

All in all, there is a really good core game here, that can be really satisfying to play. Everything wrapped around it is either really badly thought through, or really frustrating after a while (though this is probably a budgetary constraint). As I had such a frustrating end to my playthrough, I can't recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

Be the Quiet Asian Hitman
I really like the whole atmosphere and setup in "The Hong Kong Massacre". I've enjoy this journey of one man vs the whole crime syndicate story. It feels like I've taken part in a John Woo's movie.
The art department really done a great job on setting up a Hong Kong location background. And the music and sound effects are good, not super outstanding but good enough.
For the game play it's tough in difficulties and very tense gun fight. You might need a faster than ninja reflex to survive without using the slow motion due to the challenge reward. I got this on heavy discount and it's totally worthy for me. 8/10 Recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
