\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game works fine, nice combat system, cool visuals and effects.
The gameplay inherits the level progression from Hotline Miami, so you have to go through plenty of different stages while not letting anybody alive. But unlike HM you only have fire weapons, you can go bullet time and enemies are not that unforgiving. It's also I would not say gory but PULP instead.

Everything cool, right?

Yes, as long as you don't go deeper in the game, you don't try mission challenges and you just treat the game like a mindless time killer more than an actual game (which it is).

Once you start doing the challenges (so you can unlock new weapons and upgrades) the game stops being fun and starts being frustrating, also showing bugs and flaws you wouldn't even notice while casually playing.

First challenge is a time trial, you have to compolete the level within a certain time. It's just ok.

In the second challenge you need to go through the levels without bullet time. Here problems start. Like any other bullet time game, it's just designed to be played WITH bullet time, otherwise it will tend to be too difficult, frustrating and will make you try finding cheaty solutions to break the game, instead of enjoying it. This is what happens here.

Not to talk about the third challenge, where you have to reach 100% accuracy AND the game is clarly not designed for this.
Excluding auto weapons cause you'll end up shooting twice by mistake, you can use the pistol or the shotgun.
If not upgraded the shotgun's fire rate is way too slow, so you go for the pistol, just to realize that hitboxes are very poorly made, so 25% of the time you'll miss your target for no reason.
So you'll be forced to play these amusing challenges to upgrade and go back to the shotgun, at least you don't need to be too accurate, just to realize that for some reason the red shirt guy (one of the enemy type, you find at least 3 per stage) doesn't take damage (or doesn't get hit) more than half of the time.

I love modern top-down, isometric, and "these kind of" shooters, that's probably why I have several hours of gameplay and still, leaving a bad review. But I find that ironically, this game is just the opposite of what Hotline Miami is, as it shows a pefectly designed exterior looking and an extremely poorly designed game concept.\t\t\t\t\t
