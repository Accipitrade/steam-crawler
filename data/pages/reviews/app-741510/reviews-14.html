\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEverything you'd expect from a mash-up of "Hotline Miami" and John Woo's "Hard Boiled." A true distillation of the spirit of both works, married together in a wonderfully complimentary way. For certain values of success, this game sets the standard for how to take popular movies and adapt them to certain game types/genres. Possibly quite by accident, "The Hong Kong Massacre" is one of the best game adaptations of a film ever made.

While I'm an unrepentant fan of Mr. Woo's work from the late 80s and 90s, I'm less a fan of top-down shooters from nearly any era. I had hoped "The Hong Kong Massacre" might provide a friendly segue into the genre, thus helping to more favorably dispose me to the genre. The way I saw it, since the struggle of learning a new game type would be bear the trappings of "A Better Tomorrow" and other masterworks of slick action movies, the addition of so much lead in my diet might be more palatable (as I would be inclined to eat many, many bullets until such time as I had 'got gud').

Sadly, this did not prove to be the case. That outcome will not prevent me from offering a more objective take, however: for fans of top-down shooters, "Massacre" should be right up your alley. A good soundtrack will set the pace for navigating some well-realized settings, all while you jump, roll, slide, and dive your way to victory. And victory, "Massacre" tells me, smells like cordite. Regardless of what Colonel Kilgore says.

Numeric rating is hard to offer, since this type of game is so unfamiliar to me. It feels fair to say 7/10, though I could see a case for 8 depending on what criteria genre-connoisseurs tend to weigh the heaviest. Recommended for: fans of "Hotline Miami" and other top-down shooters. Those like myself, seeking an introductory course in the video game equivalent of being Neo from the Matrix, be advised to look elsewhere.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile not the best game I've ever played, The Hong Kong Massacre is definitely fun in its best moments as it melds the grimy, gritty crime themes and slow-motion bullet time gameplay of Max Payne with modern top-down mayhem ala Redeemer and Ruiner alongside a dash of old school Smash TV herbs n' spices. If you like Hotline Miami and Crimsonland , there's also a chance you'll have some fun here. It's not as good as the excellent Ruiner (to compare it to one of its modern peers...or really any of the above-mentioned games when compared overall) but when you are slow-mo diving, chaining together ruthless bullet combos and wrecking everything in sight it's a blast. There's not a lot of weapons but they're fun to use and at their best, the mechanics are a real helluva time. I was satisfied once I'd get the lay of the levels down and execute my moves to the point where I'd feel unstoppable.

Unfortunately, when the collision detection gets you stuck on objects/furniture/etc., a dive goes strangely in the wrong direction from what you are pushing on the keys or when you have to dive from rooftop to rooftop in slow-motion where instant death awaits if you fail some of the warts start showing. You'll die enough without the game working against you but some of the choppy gameplay quirks take away from the actual challenge offered. The roof to roof jumping is really, really horrible, buggy and random. It is awesome in theory but lousy in execution. The upgrade system also felt a bit undercooked with really only clip size increases making any sort of positive difference when the challenge ramps up. What storytelling there was didn't come through in an interesting way either. Offering very little in terms of world-building as the environments you throttle your way through start getting same-y in some stretches. This isn't the kind of game that needs tons of exposition but they tried latching onto a bleak tale of revenge here and the elements of it don't come together at all...not really even in a good B-movie/exploitation film way. Oddly though, despite the boss battles all using the same format (more or less), they were fun once you caught onto the rhythm needed to come out on top. Some of the sameyness works, some of it doesn't in my opinion. Like I said, it's not a perfect game but there's some tough fights and solid challenge n' thrills all throughout if you like this kind of thing.

I'll definitely watch out for more titles from this dev, as there's plenty to like here and some improvements could make a sequel or similar action style game that they release pack a real wallop. As it is though, THKM would get a solid B from me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI came across this hearing it was a Hotline Miami clone, which drew my attention to it. I love Hotline Miami, the way it approached storytelling, the pace of gameplay. So does Hong Kong Massacre match up, maybe innovate on the ideas? No. The movement feels sluggish, the "dodge" roll cursed with input delay, only 4 weapons, with the shotgun being largely worthless. The challenges in the game handicap how you play so much that if you want to upgrade any weapon meaningfully, you don't get to use the core mechanic of the game - slow motion.

The game punishes you for not using slow-mo. It's essentially infinite, draining on use until killing an enemy, which refills it. However, the game almost forces you to use it. Enemy density is very high at times, with enemies having excellent aim and reaction times. There's no melee at all, so running out of ammo forces you to walk slowly to retrieve a weapon from the ground. Enemies also don't react to the sound of gunshots - at all. So the "strategy" of this game is to move slowly, pick off enemies with slow-mo, rinse and repeat. Not high-octane thriller material like it was marketed to me.

Music is nice, at least. Same with visuals. Boss fights are literal copy-and-paste off each other, with no identity to them. Levels feel that way too. Story is poorly presented, and written. There's very little cohesion to it. It might as well not have existed, I was so barely invested in it. And this game is buggy too, and collision will frequently kill you when you get stuck on objects.

Get this on sale, maybe, if you are desperate for some Hotline Miami-esque gameplay, without the soul of Hotline itself. Otherwise, skip this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHong Kong Massacre takes Direct inspiration from Hotline Miami in it's gameplay and influence with it's theme being centred around John Woo's movies.


The "Boss" fights in the game after every end of chapters can be repetitive and "copy paste" fights and the "Story" is really nothing special at all and not the highlight of the game. What matters and what's more important is the gameplay. You're buying this game just purely to shoot bad guys and have fun. The game offers a selection of "generic" weapons "Pistol", "Shotgun", "Smg" and "Rifle" that may sound bad but you can upgrade these weapons so the shooting is a little bit more enjoyable. Since gunfights are fast paced with bullet time action. Visually the game is great! enjoyable shooting and levels of detail for an indie shooter. There is a decent variety of levels/maps to play and a good challenge to be had when aiming for challenges enough content to keep you playing every now and then.

I only wished this game had a steam workshop and an "endless" mode feature like in Max Payne 2 where you could survive waves after waves of enemies in custom made levels, that way you'd sink more hours into the game and enjoy endless carnage of shooting! If you've played Hotline Miami and you enjoy that game's gameplay, you may like this too it's definitely a favourite of mine, a little bit shorter than Hotline Miami and doesn't have an interesting lore but next to "Maximum Action" I would say I did enjoy playing this game and it's a good time waster. However I will say full price for the game is asking a bit too much for what your getting and it isn't worth it. Go ahead and buy this on the sale you'll enjoy it a lot more.\t\t\t\t\t
