Give a guy a gun, he thinks he's Superman. Give him two and he thinks he's God.
A reasonably polished Early Access title with a whole lot of repeat playability.

Buy this if you're a fan of: John Woo action films, The Hard-Boiled/Tequila series, Bullet-Time, Superhot, Enter the Matrix, Path of Neo, Max Payne, Hotline Miami.

Pros:
- This is a *lot* of fun. I'm sure I'm not in the minority here when I say bullet dodging, trick-shooting and slow-motion acrobatics *always* are welcome in single player. I find it odd that so many open world games don't offer even half the fun of what's found here in terms of actually just operating your character. Games like this, Spiderman, Max Payne, they just have nigh-on-infinite replayability because the moment to moment action is so damn addictive.

-The difficulty level is perfect, cripplingly ruthless if you fail for a second with just enough leeway for your gorgeous, perfect run to feel as rewarding as it should do.

-One of the main contributing factors to this difficulty curve balance is the weight and impact of bullets. It's one bullet one kill on everything except bosses, including you and this makes for a very Rainbow Six: Siege style play where every bullet feels like it counts. Although I am yet to play Ruiner I dare say this is the most satisfying I've found gunplay to be in the top-down genre.

-Price tag is very reasonable for what you're getting and I feel as though this is one instance of Early Access where I'm already quite happy with the product I've got and further imrpovements are just gravy.

-The challenges provided for each level provide the currency for you to upgrade your weapons. This encourages the player to repeat levels and try various different styles of run, timed, no slow-motion, perfect aim gun ballet. It's a rewarding system that gets punishing towards the better upgrades.

With that being said there is still a lot of room for this to grow into something much more impressive. I would normally list cons next but like I say, there's nothing glaringly obvious that isn't a quick fix away from no longer being an issue and the price tag is fair for what you're getting. With that in mind..

Suggested Improvements

-The game script, in fact every single piece of writing, needs completely overhauling. Even things like the options menu has 'Music Volmume' next to 'Sound Volume' which is just maddening. The dialogue has frequent inconsistencies and misspellings. The game seems focused on breaking up the action with elements of story but they're so wooden and badly translated it's just offputting and ultimately unnessecary. Hire someone to write this better, in English at least it's a real let down that could be very easily fixed.

-Add friendly fire to enemies. It would be immensely satisfying to be able to flip between two enemies and have them shoot eachother. Pacifist run?

-When in cover behind an object but poking your gun round the game will act as though your gun is in cover. Ensure the gun shot is coming from the end of the barrel not the character model.

-More weapons. More exotic weapons. Katana only mode in Superhot. This one is a must.

-A melee/disarm maneuvre for when you run out of bullets. This one is a must. Melee only run?

-More varied or complex challenges from level to level, the existant ones are good but it becomes a bit harrowing doing the same three for every level. I've already mentioned pacifist runs and melee only runs and katana only runs but there's so much that can be done here.

-There needs to be a second dive move for if you need to get out of a dive before the end. At present there is a gap between diving and being invulnerable to bullets and the end of your dive. Meaning you can be stuck in your dive and get hit with the last bullet without any chance of evasion. This feels imbalanced. Perhaps if you double click dive again at the end of your dive he could throw himself to the ground or throw himself into a roll?

-Character models, specifically the player character model needs work.

-More variation in level design.

-Lastly may I say that this is absolutely built for film and other media tie-ins and you should be working to secure as many deals as possible in that regard. One idea (which is being made into a movie) would be the graphic novel series '100 Bullets'. People get given semi-magical, untraceable guns by some shady organization and with the gun comes 100 bullets for them to go seek their revenge. You should contact the creators because they would jump at the chance to get their product into this sort of game, It's been very profitable for the Payday 2 devs and there are limitless other options with other franchises.

Overall Score
8/10
This game has literally just released, I can see it being a 9/10 or possibly even 10/10 with a few improvements here and there. Ultimately I would encourage you to buy this if it sounds like something you'll enjoy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rarely leave reviews unless its something i hate and want to rake over the coals.. but i have to leave one here to give these people props.

I've been following this game since i stumbled over it on Facebook for many many years ago when all there was were just gifs of tech demos. I loved the style and the potential that was shown and i was hooked at the idea.

It is inevitable that reviews will bring up Hotline Miami but that is not a negative. The games look similar but they operate in a different way. There is no score here, take that as a positive or negative but the only thing that influence your score at the end is the challenges and if you completed them or not as well as a your time being logged.
There are only 4 weapons but all weapons have an ability to be upgraded. There are no melee weapons and no throw empty weapon ability. But people note this as a negative as they come in expecting Hotline Miami. Its not the same game.
4 guns are limiting but there isn't much more you need. Pistols, Sub-machine guns, Assault Rifle and Shotgun. The only thing i can think of would be grenades which would be overpowered, a hunting rifle or sniper that would be slow to fire but go through multiple enemies etc. But it doesn't really fit with the theme. You are supposed to shotgun a door into sawdust and turn the room into nothing but smoke and abstract art with an obsession for the color red.

I feel the game wanted to focus on style and let you experiment with the sliding and flipping over tables and through windows, diving over rooftops or shooting down a door and watch everything in the room on the other side fly up in the air like confetti. Smoke, paper, glass, wood, blood, bullets. And in the middle is you, dancing through the mess in slow-motion.

There controls feel clunky at first but after getting used to them its barely noticeable, and the game is being patched and has already fixed such issues as being able to keep the same mouse DPI in slow-motion and change the reticle color.

The game can be very short if you rush through it and do not focus on the challenges, experimenting and upgrading all the weapons, etc. With completing all challenges i think i clocked in around 12 hours. (The achievement for completing all challenges is currently bugged but they have stated that they are working on it)
So be warned that if you don't care about the challenges it will be a very short game. 2-3 hours maybe, depending on your skill level.

As for difficulty.. its hard to say really. Again it comes to the challenges. If you don't do the challenges it is fairly easy. You can use slow-motion all the time, not care about how many shots you miss and just spray and pray. Its fun. Its stylish. It gives you a kick. But its pretty easy. However if you go for the challenges then be ready for a.. well.. challenge. There is a time limit, a no shots missed challenge and a no slow-motion challenge. All challenges give you one star which you can use to upgrade weapons if you have enough stars. As said, without going for challenges you might spend 2-3 hours with the game. If you like challenges and don't mind repeating levels until you pull off the perfect run then you will probably spend around 10-13 hours, which IMO is well worth the money.

There are some faults with enemy AI and animations. You can see the enemies bug out every once in a while and the enemy dodge roll invincibility frames is something i will bang on about until the end of time. It seems the invincibility frames start right before or at the exact moment they start the dodge animation, which means if you bullet is on its way in slow motion and they start the animation before the bullet reaches? Then it counts as a miss. Which is a pain in the a♥♥ if you are going for the "No Miss" challenge. It ended my runs so many times i was about to go insane. Especially with the bullet-proof vest dodge-rollers who dodge roll at every hit so you have to wait for 4 seconds before being able to shoot them again, and its never clear when their dodge is over. I've been able to kill some in the middle of a dodge, and some not before after they have shot back. Its infuriating.
But its a nitpick.

I am nitpicking. Cause i love this game. Its a top down version of Stranglehold. A love letter to old John Woo movies. Calling it a Hotline Miami clone is insulting to both that and this game respectively. Its not the same concept even though some elements will be similar.

This is a heavily biased review though. Cause i clearly love the game.
I was afraid this game would never be released but i am happy now that it has.
I wish the developers nothing but success.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust an excellent game. If you like Miami Hotline, if you like the idea of Miamio Hotline and John Woo having a baby, this is the game for you.

On a pure gameplay level everything feels very polished, responsive and just well done.

Your ability to slown down time and glide through a door only to headshot a couple of dudes standing in the room is just an amazing feeling. You totally feel like a badass.

The game is by modern standards quite difficult though, you will instantly die if you're hit by bullets, but slowing down time and using the dodge and slide mechanic can keep you safe.

I just don't have enough praise for this game really, everything feels just right. Sure the length could be a little longer, but I and the community hope the developers will make DLC or make modding possible somehow in the future. This is the perfect platform for a map editor.

Negatives? Well I've had some crashes, the storyline is sort of just there as an excuse to blast away a bunch of people, but honestly playing this game, negative emotions were not a part of the experience, I fully enjoyed it throughout.

Thanks VRESKI, you've really made a quality game you wouldn't expect in this day and age.

If not already completely obviousy I highly, HIGHLY recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

It also has some other bugs (you can check the steam forums to see what I mean) the dev has replied to some of these bugs so hopefully they get worked out. EDIT: some of the bugs have been fixed.

I do like how the enemies try to react to what you do and are generally pretty smart and will even try to dodge bullets (like you do) the AI feels a lot smarter than it does in Hotline Miami.

If you're looking for a spiritual successor to Hotline Miami I wouldn't look here. But if you want a similar game that's pretty fun (pretty short though) I think you'll like this although the $20 price tag is pretty steep for a 4-5 hour game.



This game also seems to run really hot on my GPU like 75-80c. I'm using a GTX970, i'm not really sure what's causing this game to use so many of my GPU resources but it is.



The game is overall pretty fun although I do wish there were more variety in the weapon selection, you also can't drop your empty gun(you can pick up another enemies gun though) and as far as I know(correct me if i'm wrong) there's no way to melee anyone or attack enemies at close range which would be helpful if you ran out of ammo.

Overall a decent fun game but i'd maybe wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe trailers make this game look amazing, but don't let that fool you.

Having previously enjoyed the Hotline Miami series, I purchased this game looking for some fast paced top-down action. Simply put, I was let down. The biggest problem is that it's slow in every conceivable way. Your character is forced to walk at snail speeds, and even after upgrading the walkspeed it's still a slog. Get used to it, pal, because you're going to be walking your way through the entire game. On top of that, all of your inputs are severely delayed, and activating the bullet time ability slows how fast you can aim. Actually, I'm not even sure why there's a button to slow the game down- at normal speed it's slower than watching your grandma participate in the 100 meter women's freestyle at the Summer Olympics. Except the pool is filled with chunky peanut butter, and Michael Phelps is sitting at the top of the bleachers boning your mom.

This game suffers from bad gunplay, poor English, and a story that's impossible to follow. Maybe if the shotgun was satisfying to use, I might give the Hong Kong Massacre some grace. I cannot recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
