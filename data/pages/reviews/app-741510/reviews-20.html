\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredibly stylish shooter. Fun to play, but not much substance. The whole story feels like only the introduction for a larger story. I'd like to see an expansion, with a little customisation and new areas. While Hong Kong looks wonderfully gritty, the reds, yellows and blacks eventually become boring.

I didn't think of Hotline Miami much at all, despite my first impressions. It's far more influenced by the noir of Max Payne and John Woo's Stranglehold. However, I missed the bright colours and neon of Hotline Miami, the vibrancy allowing me to recall entire stages in my head, over a year since I last played it, whereas I cannot recall a single level I've just played.

The soundtrack is fine, but quickly become ambient. I don't think I'll be listening to any tracks on the regular, but perhaps that's for the best. Imitating the driving bass of Hotline Miami in this case would be inappropriate.

Regardless, the game is fun.

I paid roughly $30 AUD, which is very steep. I would advise waiting until it's $15-$20 AUD for a fairer price.

Definitely makes me excited for future works from Vreski.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t8.5/10

I had an absolute blast with the Hong Kong Massacre, despite a few irritations. By the end, I found myself taking stock of enemy walking patterns and positions, so I could ensure they'd all be in my line of sight when I activated slo-mo and dived in, dodging beneath shotgun blasts and evading rifle fire. It's exhilarating to play, watching the final enemy of each level sail backwards in a blood-splattered mess, coupled with the solid and mood-enhancing soundtrack.

Developer VRESKI makes many smart decisions here: red arrows appear on the sides of the screen to direct you to enemy locations (double red arrows indicate an enemy in close proximity), and enemies typically refrain from shooting until they are visible on your screen. These eliminate some of the biggest irritations common to the genre (here's looking at you, Hotline Miami 2).

However, there were a few aspects of the game I can't praise as highly. First of all, the subtitled dialogue is riddled with errors, which dampened the professional-looking production of the package as a whole. Secondly, I can't say I enjoyed any of the boss fights. They were a very small percentage of the game, but each boss was easily dispatched by mostly remaining stationary and peppering them repeatedly in slo-mo.

The only other issue was that the combination of slo-mo and your dive is perhaps a bit TOO powerful, at least on the standard difficulty. However, the game at its core is a power fantasy, so I can't say this bothered me too much. Although I haven't played the hard difficulty yet, I'd imagine that a slower recharge rate on your slo-mo meter could easily balance things out.

If you enjoy top-down shooters or games featuring "bullet time" (I'm an enormous fan of both), I think you'll find this to be a special game. Although many found the game to be too short, I've been pursuing the "perfect aim" challenges for each level and may go back and play the game on a higher difficulty as well. I bought the game during the recent sale for $15, and absolutely felt that I got my money's worth.\t\t\t\t\t
