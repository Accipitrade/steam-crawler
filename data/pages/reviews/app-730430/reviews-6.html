\r\n\t\t\t\t\tfirstly, i'd like to apologise for my grammer, i have issues but i do my best. now then on to the review.
secondly this review uses the term 'player killer' which is a term i use for a segment of the game so boring or painful that i think a normal player would just quit at that point.

to put this short, there was potential for a good game here, but be it by bitterness or dislike, its become a bad one for having no good options and not understanding how important balancing the selections is for a game like this. bad balancing with everything from the amount of missions and the money and overloading the player plague the game as well.
speeches are a bit too realistic in that they are long, boring and without end or point, you don't get anything off them than improved or reduced voting score, which isn't that important, and honestly the speeches really are a player killer. the game is also great for listening to youtube, in that it could just be me, but there doesn't seem to be any significant music or sounds, just papers shuffling and music snippets.
when you become president as well the game overloads you, you get a full sidebar of missions where at most you had two before, as well as overloading you with tasks to do. there doesn't feel to be enough staff as well, so stress becomes a major concern.
the speeches are not even the first player killer, that would be the play, which, despite everything i tried, had no good option to compromise or bribe. the only option was to kill your former freind, which is a three point stress option and promises trouble later.
PRos:
the game can be quite fun
theres a lot of modern day like twitter and stuff in this, rather than staying historical
the UI is designed pretty well.
Graphics are decent too, well except cutscenes which don't seem to work properly currently.

cons-
MAJOR CON- this game only has THE ILLUSION OF CHOICE. i have played nearly every option and most of the time, only one works, and you better hope you selected the right one that has the right option, even then though sometimes none of them work whatosever as apposed to a good of this style game where it would be one good option to two bad options per person selected for the task.
this is major downer for a game like this since you can pick the right option and get punished anyway.
-the money goal is far too high for everything and the chances to get money far too rare. top this off with other goals that require sums also too high, heck the second one that showed up was just 1 million under the required mission goal!
-not enough chances to make money to balance this out either.
-no explaination and so far no use or option for bribary items. you can sell them but your supposed to use them for conversations? except it never comes up.
employee stress does not feel managed very well.
realistic speeches IE long, boring and feeling pointless and without end. the speeches are a game killer i think at the moment.
is it just me or is there no music or sound?
too many 'player killer' events where a normal player trying to play this would become bored. the speeches are too long and certain events such as the play feel just pure punishment as they have no good or compromising options. not even to get help or come clean.
for now this game just isn't worth it, but hopefully one day they will balance this stuff and a good game can come from this.
i didn't want to put this as part of the review, but anyone whos tried to start up yugioh duel links for a quick duel or two will understand how utterly aggravating every month of this game is.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWho would have ever thought being president could be so fun? After playing this game we now know why some presidents liked being president so much they tried to destroy democracy to keep their presidency going-okay, not really, but seriously this is a great game! The politics are at once deep enough and vague enough that your president could be from either party and while the characters are sometimes vaguely reminiscent of real politicians and the situations are also somewhat similiar to real life in 2021, they are also ethereal enough so that the politicians and situations are also unique to the game-which is probably for the best with the way politics are these days. There is a huge cast of characters and content, all championed by fairly simple control and game play with an interesting and often amusing, humorous story. While I do have a small wish list of things I wish the creators had done-let us customize our president more, give them choice of ethnicity and gender, maybe a chance to be non-corrupt?-but overall it is obvious that this team knew what they were doing. If your looking for a fun political game that is at once immersive and serious yet light I recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll keep playing the game, and I recommend it for a bunch of reasons. The concept is great, there should be more political games which put you in the decision role. The length of the game is impressive, and there are enough things going on that it'll make it good to replay.

Now for the criticisms...

I think it is telling that it's called 'THIS IS the President'. I know its a reference to the 'This is the Police' series, but I do also find it telling, because the game never reaches a point where it feels like 'YOU ARE the President'. The game lacks atmosphere, an issue which is compounded by playing as someone who purposefully has no backstory, name, or wider details about their character. Sure, I get it, they're a blank canvas that we can interpret however we want, but it leaves the game fundamentally lacking in making it seem like you are the President.

The game is also sometimes frustratingly gimmicky, which undermines the concept. It could perhaps do well to be less of a parody, and more like games like Suzerain. The political aspects of the game could also be improved - for example, a screen showing whatever Congress is doing at the time, and giving you bills to sign (which may be wild and wacky, if desired). The political side being enhanced would go some way to making the game seem more atmospheric and immersive.

My two most fundamental criticisms are as follows: first, lack of character background makes the whole 'presidency' itself feel less realistic and less immersive. Second, lack of depth on the political side of the game also makes the game less immersive. I praise the game for the concept, and for the sheer length of time it seems to take to complete (I've only played a year in-game so far - 12 turns). Based on that, I would recommend the game, but it could have been a lot more compelling.

6/10?\t\t\t\t\t

The only questionable part is the background story of the president, which at times seems too far-fetched, and in some cases odd and too forced (Gang relations, Gay Couple Murder?!?!, Immunity as the ultimate Goal). For a second playthrough, an easier entry would be beneficial i guess, perhaps a kind of sandbox game mode or a completely alternative background of the president.
All in all, it is a very well-done game that is able to keep the player hooked.\t\t\t\t\t
