\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNOTE: I have not completed the game yet.

The game is random with interesting but poorly fleshed out mechanics, barely saved by a good narrative.

Your choices really don't matter all that much--I could probably use some dice to choose which actions I take and have a better time than actually trying to make the better decision. Create a pottery studio for the first lady after she asked for one? The press: "A sexless marriage filled with bickering". Display miltary strength after Chinese missiles threaten japan? The pacifists didn't like that! Most of the game is spent clicking through this never-ending stream of useless decisions.

Offered to build a home for "pardoned turkeys" to survive thanksgiving? It's a coinflip between being depicted as stupid or a savior. (pro tip: build the turkey rehab)

The problem (as I see it) is the "popularity rating", which doesn't actually represent any differing viewpoints. If you do one thing and it upsets the pacifist crowd, you lose points--There's no recovery from the militant or pro-<country-being-threatened> crowd. It makes actually trying at the game mildly infuriating. At every click I have to wonder "Oh, will I lose popularity points because this upsets the amish?!" Who the popularity points represents changes every five seconds, too: There's no consistency you can learn to follow.

Several of the interesting mechanics are both criminally underused and neglected in terms of consequences and benefits. You tweet like, once a year, and the content of your tweets has zero impact on the midgame. Speeches are too few, too far apart, etc. Early in the game you get options to do press releases, etc. which quickly drop off to nothing during the midgame.

The early game is ROUGH. Which I can understand, but it's almost ridiculous. Ollie, your techie and most valuable character, gets burned out almost every month because you can either use him or fail the task at hand. Sending your security to kill a dude costs 1 stress point, while having ollie hack into some IoT cameras takes three! He's sent to vacation almost every month because of this balancing. Spoiler: He kills himself later in the game if you overuse him!

Overall, I think the narrative is great, but it's just an infuriating game to try and play seriously. I believe this game would be far more enjoyable if the power to turn a bad situation good (and a good situation bad) was actually entrusted to the players. Worth the non-sale price.


Also, the game has a LOT of typos, most of them are pretty obvious. I'm amazed they didn't at least run it through a spell checker given how text-heavy the game is.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 35 hours of suffering through the hardest difficulty, i will probably say that i definitely wouldn't recommend playing the game on the hardest difficulty your first time playing, I liked the story and felt the writing was okay at times, but im only saying that because i don't care or understand politics at all.

I kinda wish they gave you more background on your character and Archie Wister, all you're given is that Wister wants to put you in jail because you're corrupt and you want to be president so you can get legal immunity.

The gameplay was very brutal starting from 2023, even endgame where you HAVE to lose approval on purpose was a pain. But the worst of it was the constant asking for raises, and worse if you refuse to give them a raise they would demand for more and then leave if you refuse again. Another problem i have is the ending, kinda wish that they put in more choices that would directly affect the ending, though it's understandable since i felt like they wrote themselves into a corner by giving you legal immunity no matter what. I also don't like the very random events that simply is a yes or no question, relying completely on luck and guess that would affect your approval rating.

But the biggest reason for this negative review is because of a bug, but not just any bug.

A game-breaking bug that straight up refuses to continue the game, and where did this bug happen? At the end of the game, rolling back a few months or so changed nothing, thus, forcing me to restart the entire game. This game-breaking bug seems to only happen at the end of the game, possibly rendering all the effort and time you put into this game for nothing.

If you want to buy the game, get it on sale, or if you're worried about playing the game until it eventually doesn't wanna even let you finish the game, then recommend don't buy it at all. It was still fun overall but also detracted a lot of enjoyment, especially since i was forced to restart the entire game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe only organized crime, where you dont get punished for... politics

In "This is the president" you have an interesting mixture of visual novel and management game, which is heavily leaning towards story telling.
As the newly chosen Potus, you and your wife, as well as crazy best friend are doing everything they can.... for the people? for the country? naaah.. to protect yourself and cash in as much as you can on the way, of course.
For this you have to keep the money flowing as well as your lackeys happy, who work for you (as long as you pay them and dont stress them out too much).

The main goal of a politician to get his way is to manipulate the dumb mob into believing all his lies. This is mostly done by big and small activities. Depending if you set the right staff onto a problem, the result can cost or bring you voters. Realistically though, many problems you get to solve have positive and negative side effects. Some even long term .. and some are story wise, which you have to take as they come.

The writing goes from fun and hilarious to some quite realistic concerns, but mostly keeps its tongue in cheek humor. I especially liked the many different staff options you can have, and that they effectively leave (or worse) if you dont keep them happy. Adds some roleplay to it. Also as a non american you can even learn a bit about the basic political system.

The graphics and music fit to the atmospheric story of this hopeless view into politics.

Conclusio: For me, as a fan of story based visual novels i really liked this mixture.. and of course this - sometimes too realistic - view on todays politics was also fitting. Add some light management and rp parts and it makes one of my favourite games of this kind in the last years.

8/10\t\t\t\t\t
