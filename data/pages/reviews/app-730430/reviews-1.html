The only issue I have with the game is that it's too bright, especially on the white background that pops up from time to time. \r\n\t\t\t\t\t“Dramatic, but it's way too dramatic.”

The concept of the game and the graphics themselves are good. The story of this game was enough to remind me of the lesson: "It's very hard to be a moral and ideal leader." Before I started this game, obviously I was determined to reform and advance the United States as a politician with a people-conscious, progressive view of politics. But after playing the game, I assassinated 4 characters and generated budget revenue through corruption. I played dirty for my own sake. Political enemies, fake news, and distorted rumors somewhat changed my mind and made me realize how hard it was to stay in power. This experience itself is definitely the beauty and advantage of this game.

BUT!! This game was a bit disappointing for me who expected a political simulation. Unlike other games of the same genre, this game focuses on stories and events. But the problem is that the story itself is not interesting. The whole story feels like watching a low-quality third-rate political drama that's not interesting, and the events are too dramatic. Of course, that is not to say that such events are unrealistic. Rather, repeating 'too dramatic' events made me lose interest in the game, and the combination of some ‘intended’ positive and negative events rather negatively affected the game.

Also, there is a lack of probability. According to the storyline, the president is described as a somewhat less recognizable but inevitably elected because there are no candidates in the party, and the first lady makes a "false" plan to revise the constitution to immune their actions during his presidency.
"Then why on earth did he become POTUS?“
"How did such a person become president?"

In addition, the biggest enemies of this game are White House secretaries. They will be likely to make unfair requests randomly, and if you don't listen to them, they’ll quit their job and disappear. Even, the first lady constantly asks for embezzlement of public funds, and the weirdo vice president repeats actions that only harm the government reputation like a psychopath. Maybe the vice president needs more attention?

In conclusion, I don't really want to recommend this game. Only buy it when it's more than 70% off.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is (almost) the perfect game of this archetype -- political simulations where you really do feel like you are making the choices as president. This game is very refined in its interface and storytelling. My only criticism is some of the storylines take away from the realism of the game. Some level of exaggeration is expected for any video game, even those that are meant to be realistic, but some of it does basically go a bit too far and breaks the immersion sometimes. For example, I don't think a tech guy you find off the internet randomly would be so willing to drone and kill someone. Also, frankly, that guard in the opening scene is too much of an idiot. Hopefully no one actually has that idea about how an American president works in real life.

But I'm sure this isn't supposed to be a completely realistic political simulator. Some of it clearly is supposed to be satirical or a hyperbolic commentary on the real world, as it has every right to be. Ultimately, if you are into this general idea of wanting to roleplay a political figure, this is the greatest game I've ever seen like that at least.\t\t\t\t\t
