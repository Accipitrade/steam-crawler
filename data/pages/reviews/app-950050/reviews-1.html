\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWasn't what I expected.

I expected more space/flight combat. Granted I only have 3 hours in the game, but I expected after that time (or less) I'd be up in space but no, I'm on the ground. The ship hovers just above the ground, most of my game was played on the ground. The closest I got to flying was making the ship "jump"... might as well have made my vehicle a mech.

I expected "Far Cry crossed with No Man's Sky". Now, I haven't played NMS, but I have played Far Cry titles and this ain't it. Far Cry had many more interesting POI's, you couldn't re-customize your perks in mid-combat, and it had more content such as vehicles, different weapons to choose between, and interaction with wild life. Here, all the POI's are combat points that feel same-same, but different in the sense that they give trash rewards, which drove me to ignore the bad ones. I already talked about re-customizing mid-combat. And finally, there isn't much content to keep me interested in the game play loop. Wild life interaction is basically scanning an animal, just because. No perceivable reward, just filling your pokedex.

All in all, its a boring game, might be fun for a kid.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn search of high-quality open-world space exploration games since I first played Freelancer several years ago, Starlink, in my opinion, has perfected many of the elements an ideal Freelancer successor would have. In terms of open-world exploration, it achieves it differently than Freelancer did: rather than travelling between systems, in Starlink the main characters travel between planets within just one system; Atlas. While I appreciate the scenic variety the planetary focus brings to Starlink, I wish it drew more inspiration from the space station trading mechanics of Freelancer, with fast travel within a system, and to additional systems, achieved via gate networks, rather than immersion-breaking teleportation. Starlink's modernity gives it improvements over Freelancer, however, including built-in controller support, more immersive graphics, and multi-platform ports, with Proton making playing it easier than an HD-patched Freelancer on the Steam Deck. To avoid spoiling gameplay, I won't discuss the planets in-depth, but I do wish there was a greater variety of structures, characters, and unique side missions to separate other, somewhat repetitive tasks within the game.

In short, I would definitely recommend Starlink to those looking for a new space exploration or open-world game to play. While its a shame that the failure of the toys-to-life component of Starlink discouraged further investment into the franchise on behalf of Ubisoft, the game remains entirely playable without the physical ship attachments, with their digital DLC equivalents optionally providing identical functionality within the game.\t\t\t\t\t

this game is priced at least 4x its actual value.

This is a slightly above average but otherwise generic "open world" third person shooter. You remember that daft game a couple years ago where you are permanently tethered to a stupid hover bike no one ever wanted? Well it's like that, except there are actually positives in this game. You can fly at will, up into the stars and to another planet seamlessly like in no man's sky. Given that the shooting doesn't suck and there is actual action here, we are off to a good start.
There are missions & npcs to talk to, various enemies to learn how to fight, elemental damages/resistances and upgrades - there is definitely fun here for the maths freak. Even if you have to have ubisoft's garbage unnecessary pile of puke launcher on your system for no reason.

What made me nearly thumb it down outright is that this game is hilariously, comically, unforgivably, insanely overpriced. It is worth $15AUD tops on a good day, not $60+. It's old, it's outdated, it has a stupid unnecessary bloatware launcher for no reason, it's simple and it's to promote a toy line.
With all dlc worth AUD$20 in 2022 if you love this kind of thing. Otherwise give it a miss. Remember, No Mans Sky is better in every way except shooting, isn't abandoned, and costs less. Why buy this? Good question, can't answer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably one of the most under rated games I've ever had the pleasure of playing.

Starlink is a pure joy from start to finish, It's brimming with detail and love every where you look. The audio is great and the voice acting is actually very good.

The graphics are nothing short of amazing. Again the detail in the environments is just porn to me and the way your little ship interacts with the grass and trees on each planet is nothing short of excellent.

Alot of reviews are trying to say the game is gated with DLC. This is false. Yes there is DLC for additional characters and weapons they aren't required and are mostly just reskins of weapons you already get.

Progress is a good loop of exploration, story beats, finding mods for your ship, guns and also levelling up said ship and also your pilot - with their abilities and cooldowns.

A special mention needs to go to the way each planet is completely different in its aesthetic and colour palette. Its simply gorgeous and mesmerising.

This game dunks on No Mans Sky in alot of ways - and I love No Mans Sky. This is just alot tighter, way more focused romp with just as much love and care poured into it.

Makes me wonder how Bethesda will go with Starfield.

I highly recommend this game, So much so that I also own it on the Switch. Get it on sale or full price its still worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
