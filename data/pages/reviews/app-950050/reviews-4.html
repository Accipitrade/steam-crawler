\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall : Very good looking game, interesting gimmic considering Skylanders, bad control scheme.

I've not been playing this long, and overall I like the aesthetics of this game. It reminds me of all the good things of Star Control 2, but better and more modern. The customization of your ship is great, the dialogue is fun, and there is a lot to do. That said, it has some key flaws that prevent me from wanting to do some of the things I really was looking forward to doing.

The Game Gimmick, which is a control you can add to your controller to mount your Space Ship, and Change it's Weapons would be cool if I was younger, and I had the access to the toys. I don't, and I'm not, and while I'm mildly interested, you don't need them to play this game. That's a big bonus, and thank you Ubisoft for thinking that far ahead.

The control scheme makes me tear my hair out. There are times in this game where they will want you manipulating the right joystick, pulling up to two of the bumper triggers on that side, and pressing a button on the right side atop of that. It's just insane, and it doesn't help that if you press to hard on your right joystick, that it'll zoom in and slow your turning down, when you need to have that speed up. It's just insane and unfun and doesn't always come up, but when it does, it just makes you want to walk away.

Playstyle is typical Open World Ubisoft, so there is a lot to do, but it's a lot of repetition. You'll be mining for resources, taking out control zones of the enemy, doing races, fighting monsters, pulling things off of other things, so on and so on. It can be fun, and outside of the missions, it's pretty much do what you want. With the customization of your starships, this seems to be enough for a first play through. Although there are over six characters after add ons to play with, you might never go back to play the rest of them.

Overall, it's a gorgeous game that suffers a little from the concept, and a lot in the control scheme. It's got joys to it, and if you can get it on sale, grab and experience it. It's not completely bad, but it's unfriendly in the controls, and that's just a deal breaker for me. I'll be playing it until it just reaches the point where proceeding isn't worthwhile, and move on.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just purchased during a sale, well worth it. I just installed it and played for almost five hours in a single sitting. Now although the game play is simple, there are a lot of places to visit and a lot of different quests to complete. Now as some said this game can become a bit repetitive, but this was not an issue for me as this is a great game to sort of chill with (as it is not complex and I am able to multitask and do other things while playing).

So for this specific experience I have to say it has been worth what I spent on it. That being said I plan on finishing the game and once I do, I'll probably update this further with the full experience. This game was fully worth it, especially if you get it on a sale, and you enjoy Far Cry/No Man's Sky style game play and obviously the space setting.

Tip: When you are on the second planet (Eli's Quest) make sure not to pick up random minerals you don't need, initially you are limited on inventory slots and if you pick up materials you don't need you will not be able to properly finish the quest until you upgrade your ship to hold more (which is what I had to do). There is no traditional inventory tab in this game, and it is sort of self managed, but you can increase the capacity of what you carry through the upgrades provided. I was a bit suck for a while because I was randomly looting material no really needed during the initial section of the game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving bought this game on both the console(switch) and PC, I will say it's fun. I bought it on pc because of the existence of steam remote-play and Parsec(a third party remote play software). First I will say, I only think its worth it on sale and for the best experience to buy all the weapons and ships available. While you could use one or two weapons or one ship for the entire game, what generally ends up happening, at least for me is that you max out a weapon and rotate a new one in. Every weapon or ship maxed gives one level to maxing it for all characters. This means maxing out a ship or weapon with Mason and getting the pilot xp boost skill with the points not only gives every other pilot 1 level but also means they are one step closer to maxing it. The more weapons and pilots you have, the better this works and the less tedious it feels. That said I definitely think the switch console version is the better deal because it has added pilots which mean added skills and those skill really make a difference. It doesn't look pretty on console, but the extra skills are significant.
Moving on from there, the meat of this game isn't its story. Its playing with a sandbox of systems. Namely, there are space battleships that drop primes, primes drop extractors, extractors send out units to capture points on the planet. When you defeat the lower rungs on the ladder it weakens the next higher rungs. While this goes on, you can build defenses, mines, workshops and so on.
You can play the whole game in local co op which is nice. You are tethered to each other but the distance is pretty generous and you gain bonuses for playing together.
Overall its a good game, that is overpriced considering similar games on the market. For the same price you could pick up 3 to 6 really high quality Indie games that are more enjoyable. As such, I can't really recommend it despite the fact that it is fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a game that definetly has a "toy" feel it is surprisingly addictive. There is a fair amount to do, the story is better then what it could have been, and overall is just a good "pick up and play" kind of game that you can spend hours or just do a quick few activities.

In terms of DLC you don't need any of it...but...it does give more options you wouldn't otherwise have. For example some "puzzles" take different elements. But in terms of elements even with the "deluxe" version (which essentially is the starter + DLC 1 pack) I only have one "stasis" element weapon. The rest are mainly behind the second DLC pack. Which honestly hasn't hurt me much but is a thing.

The second possible issue is that the ships also act as "lives". Meaning if you go down you can switch ship cores to keep fighting normally you only would have 4. Again with the deluxe package I have 5 ships, and if you have it all you essentially have 8 lives. So far, again, this isn't really that much of a deal since each ship also has different stats and mods so you won't exactly be efficient playing this way...but again it is a thing.

Overall it's a decent gem of a game, and far better quality and fun wise then you might expect given it's gimmick. If nothing else I recommend it on sale, but not regretting buying it for the full $80 dollars I spent.\t\t\t\t\t
