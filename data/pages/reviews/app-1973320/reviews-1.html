\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's got a really solid foundation, 2D isometric twin stick shooter but with tower defence added in, topped off with an aesthetically pleasing retro CRT art style.

I would love to see this develop further with more content if the dev wanted to that is, plus with better performance (as best as unity will allow anyway)

The price reflects the content included, that's to say, not a lot, it will only take some 5 hrs to complete the 5 levels including the grinding that needs to be done to power up in order to handle later levels.

Performance isn't fantastic when there's a lot of enemies coming onto the screen, and it has nothing to do with hardware. My Ryzen 7 3800X and RTX 3080 10GB was only being utilised between 30% and 50% in parts with the most dropped frames dropping from 165fps to as low as 30fps still very playable you adapt to it. I originally however got this to play on the steam deck which works perfectly fine despite being untested, until you get to the 4th level, where the most enemies are then you're playing through a slide show which to my surprise didn't crash somehow but this was a bad experience so i switched to my PC Tower to finish. This game also is much easier on a mouse and keyboard btw.

The 4th level is the only one that really presents a challenge and is the only level i find fun and satisfying to repeat over and over trying out different strategies. I 100% the game by getting all the achievements in around 7hr which required a bit of grinding. By which point the cheese strat is to just sit between two atom bomb towers upgraded to the max going off every 2 seconds, then nothing can touch you or your vehicles and the game plays itself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe core gameplay and idea is really good for tower defense and shooter(2 in 1!); limited colour-palette is cool too and feels like something genuine - which i can't say about every single indie title nowadays(good job!), but it needs some balancing and maybe some kind of revamp in mechanics.

1. From get-go it requires too much of grind, to acomplish pretty much anything.
2. Starting gun is way too weak.
3. Wouldn't it be better to get coins from defeated enemies and some kind of bonus for getting whole vehicle to other side?
4. It's pretty weird that i actually need to pull through at least one car to buy one tower - and with the 500$ the options area pretty much limited too.
6. The wave that starts with shooting enemies have maybe a little too much of those.
7. Maybe a bit more of a exp pulling range(for some reason sometimes it feels like i'm not getting those right away, while running pass through it?)
8. Other guns for player might be something to consider too, just food for your thought.

I feel like you put a lot of love in this small game(and no, it's not cashgrab by any means), but maybe didn't get enough of proper advice regarding how the things should work or how you should keep player engaged from the start.

As for now it's just acceptable, but there's a lot of room for improvement :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; A fun arena shooter with VERY light TD elements. You can build towers, but they are pretty much useless, so it's pretty much you vs a horde of enemies.. over and over again. A lot of people have given the developer good feedback, so it'll be upto him to improve the game further. Good news is that the developer has been actively updating the game bit by bit (from release till this review was posted at-least).

Get this game if you want an arena shooter, NOT a Tower Defense game.


Slightly longer review..


PROS:

- Good art style.

- Fun combat with few upgrades for the PC.




CONS:

- Art style is good, but the world itself is static. Levels are boring, never change, have nothing the player can interact with.. unless you count like 2-3 barrels places around that can be destroyed (I don't). Art style also has a big con, where every enemy pretty much looks the same in the heat of the battle.

- Fun combat, until it becomes a chore. You fight hordes of enemies by yourself, since towers are useless, and pretty much are doing nothing but holding down the left mouse button and doing WASD dance to easily dodge every enemy with zero threats by the time you're done upgrading your character. Since there are only 4 levels, you'll upgrade to the max by playing small easy levels and will be OP in no time.

The worst part is the weapon the PC uses. It's the same crappy pea-shooter but shoots faster. It isn't fun to shoot, doesn't get changed, doesn't get improved visually. Just feels so unsatisfying.

- Dumb AI, same-ish enemies. Enemies are also only interested in the "supply van" in the beginning, which is now pretty much unkillable (before it was too easily destroyed) with max armor upgrades and you hovering around it. After the van leaves, it poops out 500 bucks for you and you're.. then left to just kill the same enemies for 10 rounds which is not fun, trust me.

Not only do the enemies pretty much look the same thanks to the art style, they are just designed to home in on you from every corner of the map. They don't attack the tower, which isn't a surprise since towers are useless I suppose, but only you. I think there are scorpions too, which feel out of place but then the game doesn't really have any lore so it's hard to make sense of anything that comes at you.

- No Tower Defense. Towers are a joke in this game. Even after current v1.009 update, towers might as well not exist. Player character is doing more damage than anything else. The fact that last map only has place for one tower (and you can't place them anywhere you want), feels like a joke, considering how weak they are. If you're looking for a TD game, this is NOT it.

- No world/character development. I guess this is asking for too much since most indie games don't bother with it. But since it bothers me, it's in the list.

- UI/UX issues. Dialog boxes that obscure player view in the middle of the battle, getting them attacked or killed. Level ups that pause the game to give you one same award when you're done upgrading character to max. 500 gold reward you get in the beginning, which isn't enough to upgrade your useless towers fully in one map (you get occasional RNG gold drops from 2-3 barrels/boxes on map sometimes).


==

There are more issues with this game, and it definitely needs more content, more updates. It's good entertainment for like $1-$2, so you can safely buy it. Though I'd recommend adding it to the wishlist, then wait and watch for content updates.
