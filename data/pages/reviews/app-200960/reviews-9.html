\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI could go into all sorts of details about what this game is about, but I legitimately feel like the following statement is all you need to know:

You literally use magic to create your own party members.

WHY IS THIS NOT A THING IN MORE MODERN GAMES?


On a slightly more serious note...

This game's story has within it an interesting moral question. You've arrived -- by accident -- on an island filled with shaper creations called serviles. They're more or less the same in physical capability and appearance as humans, but they were created by shapers to do the bidding of said shapers. The island has since been deserted of all shapers for hundreds of years, leaving the serviles with no masters. They've split into three sects based on their views of shapers. There are the Obeyers, who believe that, despite their absence, shapers are still, for all practical purposes, their gods, and should be worshipped as such. Next are the Awakened, who believe that their self-sustinance has severed their ties with shapers, but are also humble enough not to ignore them entirely. Thus, the Awakened view themselves as equal to shapers. Lastly, there are the Takers, who believe that, because they were abandoned, their survival has granted them superiority over shapers. In short, Obeyers < shapers, Awakened = shapers, and Takers > shapers. As a shaper yourself...who will you join?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLoving this game! Only about 50% through, I'm guessing, but wanted to post this review for anyone considering purchasing this while it's still on sale. Can't stop playing, and my 360 and PS3 are getting dusty -- and I've got Skyrim, Dragon's Dogma, Dark Souls, Demon Souls, etc., all of which are great but are now on hold!

Pros:
* Well-written story, and location, item, and character descriptions are compelling and not too long or short.
* Turn-based combat, assuming you like that.
* No loading screen more than 3 seconds -- lightning fast, which means area transitions and other activities are never discouraged. This seems like a minor point, but it isn't. It makes the game feel quick and responsive like driving a Porsche (even though the game looks like a Kia).
* Some (although not consistent) decent challenge here on occasions, especially based on what creations you choose to bring with you on your adventure. Strategy is rewarded nicely.
* Love the graphical interface -- almost all information is on the playing screen, which means most (but not all) commands are one mouse-click away.
* Very low computer demands, playable on old machines.

Cons:
* Minimal graphical treats, very basic map and character and monster animations. However, in my opinion, this adds to the charm of the game, and also makes the game easier on the eyes. After a few hours, it becomes the new normal.
* Minimal audio treats, no voice-acting (so far) that I can remember. The audio for combat, door slides, and other events is adequate but limited.
* No cut scenes (so far) that I can remember.
* Interface could be improved by allowing the same key that opens a pop-up menu to close the same menu when pressed again. Other fans have mentioned this but Spiderweb for some reason has resisted making this improvement.
* Would be nice if clicking anywhere on screen other than a pop-up closed the pop-up. Would be nice to have the right mouse button select player character and all creatures, instead of forcing player to find and punch the "0" on the keyboard every time -- a minor but constant irritation.
* Would be nice if the hotkeys were customizable.

Note on how to enjoy:
* Read everything! The material is enjoyable, short, and well written. If you get tired, play something else until you are ready again to read everything. In this game you will create your own mental image and interpretation of the game world, based on what you read, unlike most games in which you will be shown a more explicit graphical representation of everything.
* Don't read a guide, stay away from forums. They're not necessary (so far). Use trial and error. You can save anywhere.
* Don't worry about trying to make the perfect build or to select every optimal decision in dialog choices. Immerse yourself in the world and let consequences just happen. The game is not so tough (so far) that you will get stuck.
* Even if you don't finish this game, or for some reason don't like it much, it should be at least interesting to see what a very small but talented development team can accomplish with limited resources.

Enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, this is my first review I have ever done, being a person that tends to hide and not let the world know my opinions, that should show how much I appreciate these games. I have been a long time fan of Spiderweb's games for the last 6 or 7 years, I grew up playing these games, given that they were the demos from the Spiderweb website, but I never got bored of the limited length of them. having seen the 45$ 13 game bundle, I jumped on it. I have already beaten most of these games, and am thrilled to have them all now.
As far as having an opinion of this game, first and foremost you should remember that this is one of their earlier games, and that it is an indie game.
Now for the highlights of the games, though the artstyle might have that dated feel to it, it is a charming and unique style the developers have not strayed too far from, each of their different series have slightly different but similar art stlye, that, while simple, are quite intricate.

The combat is, as others have said, turn based and based on speed stats which can be altered based on skills, items, or gear. The wide variety of companions you can summon makes for a large part of the tactical aspect of the game. You can choose between an average strength army, or a couple strong warrior creatures to assist you. I noticed some people didn't like the game based on all these different areas that have 'monsters so strong you can't beat them' or 'monsters killing me in one hit' but that is the fun of this game, the idea of it is, if you can't win, look for a different path, or try later, or use a different tactic. As long as you don't stick to running in with the same strategy 30 times in a row, dying each time, it shouldn't be too rough.

Now, I might be placing this a little far down the list, but from the get go, you can pick several different characters based on your overall desires of game play, they are all rather diverse and skilled in their own special ways, but as such their skills might cost more or less skill points to learn.

The depth of the story is all narrated in text, which in my opinion, I found to be more in depth then a cutscene, depending on how fast you read, it can be agonizing or delightful, the choices are almost always going to influence people and potentially anger them, or make them approve of you more.

Dispite all my praise I am willing to give the game, no true review would be complete without admitting some flaws with the game (Personal opinion I feel) and so I will list several of the downsides to the game.

First and foremost is, that if you ever learn of the little hidden cheat console you can access, it will remove all the fun of the game if you overuse it, I used it once and managed to clear most of the game in about an hour.

Some of the area's do get rather tough, I will admit, I have managed to get a bit stuck in several areas. But there are generally about 4 or 5 areas you can go to at that time, or quests you can finish. With that said, all you need is a little side trip to finish some and you will manage to gear or level up to reach your needs for that challenging area. It is very easily a frustrating game with it's scaling, but worth every moment.

The noises and sounds, while short and sweet, memorable and appealing, do tend to grate on the nerves after listening to them through the hours of play.

In conclusion, if you have made it this far, you are either an avid reader of reviews, or you are truly interested in this game, and if you haven't purchased it, I would strongly reccommend it, that is, if you enjoy a nice text based turn based rpg with hundreds of choices and many outcomes. This game has a beautiful art style for it's time, and a long well thought out story that I can only imagine took many months to think out and many more to fine tune and decide upon. A truly unique gem to have among your collection.\t\t\t\t\t
