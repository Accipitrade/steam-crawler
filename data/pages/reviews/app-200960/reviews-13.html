\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGeneforge (part 1) places you on a mysterious island dwelling in a fantasy world that is run by Shapers, the masters of creation. You, yourself a Shaper (or one of the two other classes), don't know anything about the island except it's declared barren, i.e. forbidden to go to. An accident of sorts forces you to try and discover the mystery that is the Sukia Island.

For those of you who love a good roleplaying game, here's one. The graphics and sounds are simple but the mechanics works and Fogel's script feels magical and creates a thrilling atmosphere that keeps in touch throughout the game. Why, the game even has replayability value with 3 different professions and 3 different distinct factions to ally with. This is rather lot for such a simple-looking yet deep game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGeneforge 1. Feel the retro! The game is relatively good, if you can take the old school. The graphics feel old, the play control definitetly feels old, and the dungeons and difficulty will kick your butt if you don't play carefully, even on Normal difficulty. Which can be good and bad. Further, the strategy elements are actually fairly well done. I started out with a pair of fire spitters, and using the whole "move and cover" thing actually works out just about right.

As a note - if you go for minions, do not let the AI control them. The AI isn't bad at it, but it will drop the ball occassionally with pathfinding and enemy choices, and it tends to go looking for trouble if there isn't an immediate threat.

If your in the mood for it, few Steam games can scratch that old school rpg/strategy itch better than Geneforge. If that isn't what your after, try Geneforge 3 or 4 for an upgraded version instead.\t\t\t\t\t
